# Question

A senior team member regularly ignores agreed ground rules, saying their experience earns them latitude. Junior members are starting to question why they should bother with the rules. The project manager has already raised it directly with the senior member without effect. What should the project manager do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Stop enforcing the rules with the rest of the team to avoid appearing inconsistent.
- **B**: Maintain two informal tiers: stricter expectations for juniors, more flexibility for seniors.
- **C**: Engage the senior member's functional manager and frame the issue around its impact on the team.
- **D**: Reopen the ground rules and let the team craft new ones the senior member will support.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI escalates as a last resort, but a last resort is what this is: direct conversation has already failed and the team is being affected. (C) brings in the right authority and frames the issue around team impact rather than personal fault. (A) and (B) institutionalise unfair double standards and damage team cohesion. (D) lets one person veto rules the rest of the team has already agreed to.
