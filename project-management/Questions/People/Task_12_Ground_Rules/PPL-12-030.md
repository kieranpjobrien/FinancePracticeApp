# Question

A senior team member consistently ignores ground rules, claiming their experience makes them exempt. Junior team members are starting to question why they should follow rules that senior members don't. The PM has raised this with the senior member without success. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Create different rules for senior and junior team members
- **B**: Escalate to the senior member's functional manager and address the behaviour as a team issue
- **C**: Let the situation continue to avoid conflict with a senior person
- **D**: Remove ground rules since they're not being followed equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ground rules must apply equally to be credible. If direct conversation hasn't worked, escalation is appropriate. The functional manager may have influence the PM doesn't. Addressing it as a team issue (not a personal attack) frames it around the impact on team effectiveness.

Option A creates unfair double standards and undermines team cohesion.

Option C allows the erosion of all ground rules and damages the PM's credibility.

Option D punishes the whole team for one person's behaviour.
