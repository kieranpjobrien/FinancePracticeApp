# Question

Two project teams with strong but different ground rules have been merged following a portfolio restructure. Each team believes their own rules are better. The PM now leads the combined team of fourteen. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the ground rules of the larger former team as the starting position and let the smaller former team adapt into them over the first few sprints.
- **B**: Facilitate a joint session where both sets of rules are compared, underlying intents are surfaced, and a shared single set is co-created by the whole new team.
- **C**: Keep both sets of rules active in parallel for the first three months and let the team members choose which rules apply to their own interactions.
- **D**: Write a new combined ground-rules document drawing from both sets, circulate it for comment, and finalise it once the merged team has had a chance to review.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats a merged team as a new team — co-creating a single shared set surfaces the underlying intents and builds joint ownership of the result. (A) privileges one former team and creates two-class feeling in the merge. (C) leaves norms inconsistent in a team that needs to become one. (D) is PM-drafted with comment attached, which rarely produces the ownership a live co-design does.
