# Question

In a retrospective, an agile team cannot agree on a proposed pair-programming ground rule. The Scrum Master suggests adopting it as a one-sprint experiment. What is the PRIMARY benefit of framing it as an experiment?

## Options

- **A**: It removes the need for the team to reach agreement on the rule.
- **B**: It produces real evidence on which the team can base a more informed decision.
- **C**: It signals that the Scrum Master has the final say on team practices.
- **D**: It allows the team to keep the rule indefinitely without revisiting it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Time-boxed experiments convert a hypothetical debate into observable data, which is core to agile's empirical approach. (B) captures this. (A) is wrong; the team still needs to agree to run the experiment and review it. (C) misunderstands the Scrum Master's facilitative role. (D) describes the opposite of an experiment, which by definition has a defined end and review point.
