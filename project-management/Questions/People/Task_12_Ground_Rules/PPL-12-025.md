# Question

An agile team uses retrospectives to review and adjust their working agreements. During the latest retrospective, the team can't agree on a new ground rule about pair programming. The Scrum Master suggests trying the rule for one sprint as an experiment. What is the benefit of this approach?

## Options

- **A**: It avoids the need for team consensus
- **B**: It allows the team to gather data and make an informed decision based on experience
- **C**: It shows the Scrum Master's authority
- **D**: It postpones the decision indefinitely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Time-boxed experiments are a powerful way to test ground rules. The team can gather real data about whether the rule works for them rather than debating hypothetically. After the experiment, they can decide to adopt, modify, or abandon the rule based on actual experience.

Option A - consensus is still valuable; the experiment helps build it through shared experience.

Option C - the Scrum Master facilitates, not dictates.

Option D - the experiment has a clear end date and decision point.
