# Question

During retrospectives, the same ground rule violations are raised repeatedly despite discussions and commitments to improve. The team seems unable to change the behaviour. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop discussing it since nothing changes
- **B**: Escalate to management to enforce compliance
- **C**: Explore the root cause - perhaps the rule is unrealistic, or systemic factors prevent compliance
- **D**: Increase consequences for violations

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Repeated violations despite commitment suggest the issue isn't willpower. Either the rule doesn't fit reality (too strict, conflicting priorities) or systemic factors make compliance difficult (workload, tools, dependencies). Understanding root causes leads to real solutions.

Option A accepts failure without solving it.

Option B treats symptoms rather than causes and may damage trust.

Option D assumes the problem is motivation when it may be capability or context.
