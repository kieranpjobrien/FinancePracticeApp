# Question

A team has a ground rule about keeping meetings to scheduled end times. The PM notices that meetings frequently run over because discussions are productive and team members don't want to stop. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Strictly enforce the rule and cut off discussions at the scheduled time
- **B**: Eliminate the time rule since productive discussions are valuable
- **C**: Discuss with the team whether to adjust meeting lengths or improve time management within meetings
- **D**: Add buffer time after every meeting in case it runs over

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. If a rule consistently causes friction, it's worth re-examining. Perhaps meetings need to be longer, or perhaps better facilitation could cover the same ground faster. The team should discuss and decide together whether to adjust the rule or their behaviour.

Option A may cut off valuable discussions rigidly.

Option B abandons time management entirely.

Option D is a workaround rather than a solution.
