# Question

A hybrid delivery team includes four employees and three vendor contractors on a fixed-price engagement. The PM is co-designing team ground rules and a vendor manager argues their contractors should not be bound by team rules because they report through the vendor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's position and have two sets of rules — one for employees and a parallel set the vendor manages separately within their own delivery team.
- **B**: Include vendor members in the co-design session so the rules are genuinely shared, and resolve any real contractual conflicts with the vendor manager directly.
- **C**: Defer the whole ground-rules effort until contractors have rotated off the engagement so the remaining employee team can agree a single unified set.
- **D**: Impose the ground rules on everyone regardless of reporting line, making clear that project ground rules override vendor arrangements while they are on this team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats contractors as part of the project team — shared ground rules work best when co-designed with them, and any real contractual edges are handled through the vendor manager. (A) creates a split team culture and two classes of member. (C) postpones a foundational team practice. (D) imposes rules in a way that likely breaches the vendor contract and damages trust.
