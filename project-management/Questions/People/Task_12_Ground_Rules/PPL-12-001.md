# Question

In a sprint retrospective, a team member raises that two colleagues regularly arrive late and interrupt others during meetings. The team has no documented ground rules. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Speak privately with the two named individuals after the retrospective and ask them to change the behaviour.
- **B**: Draft a set of meeting ground rules and send them to the team for adoption.
- **C**: Remind the team during the retrospective that professional behaviour is expected of everyone.
- **D**: Facilitate the team to create their own ground rules together as part of the retrospective, including expectations on meeting behaviour and communication.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role is facilitative, and team-created ground rules carry far more weight than imposed ones. The retrospective is the natural forum for this. (D) does it. (A) addresses the two individuals before there is a shared standard for them to violate, which puts personal coaching before norm-setting. (B) drafts rules without the team and tends to produce a list nobody owns. (C) is vague and doesn't translate into specific, actionable expectations.
