# Question

A new project team is forming. The PM wants to establish team ground rules. What is the MOST effective approach to creating these rules?

## Options

- **A**: The PM writes the rules and presents them to the team
- **B**: Adopt the ground rules from a previous successful project
- **C**: Facilitate a team discussion where members collaboratively create and commit to the rules
- **D**: Let ground rules emerge naturally over time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules are most effective when team members create them collaboratively. This creates ownership - people are more likely to follow rules they helped create. The discussion itself builds shared understanding about expectations and values. The PM facilitates but doesn't dictate.

Option A (PM dictates) creates rules without buy-in. Team may comply superficially but not embrace them.

Option B (copy from other project) may not fit this team's specific context and lacks ownership.

Option D (let emerge) may result in inconsistent or dysfunctional norms forming by default.
