# Question

A new project team is forming and the PM wants to establish ground rules in week one. What is the MOST effective way to create them?

## Options

- **A**: Reuse the ground rules from the PM's previous successful project as a starting point and have the team confirm them.
- **B**: Have the PM draft the rules and present them to the team for adoption at the kickoff.
- **C**: Allow team norms to emerge organically over the first few sprints and codify the ones that prove useful.
- **D**: Facilitate a team session where members propose, debate, and commit to the rules together as a group.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Ground rules stick when the people they govern create them; PMI's team-formation practice and basic group dynamics both support collaborative drafting. (D) does this. (A) imports another team's context and may not fit; "confirming" rarely produces real ownership. (B) skips the buy-in conversation and tends to produce compliance rather than commitment. (C) leaves a gap during which dysfunctional norms can take root and become hard to dislodge later.
