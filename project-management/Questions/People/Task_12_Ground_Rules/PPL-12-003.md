# Question

A team agreed ground rules at project start. Six months in, several rules are routinely ignored and there has been no consequence. The Scrum Master notices the rules are quietly becoming dead letters. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin enforcing the existing rules consistently and apply small consequences for violations.
- **B**: Use the next retrospective to review the rules with the team — keep what still serves them, retire or update what doesn't, and recommit to the rest.
- **C**: Draft a tighter, more specific set of rules to replace the original ones.
- **D**: Drop ground rules as a practice; the team has demonstrated they don't see value in them.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ground rules need to be living agreements; a retrospective lets the team prune and renew them rather than keep enforcing what no longer fits. (B) does this. (A) enforces rules that may have lost relevance and breeds resentment without restoring meaning. (C) layers more rules on top of the ones being ignored — likely to suffer the same fate. (D) discards a practice that works when ground rules are owned and current.
