# Question

A team created ground rules at project start, but several are now routinely ignored without consequence. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Strictly enforce all rules with formal consequences
- **B**: Facilitate a retrospective discussion to review which rules still serve the team, update or retire outdated ones, and recommit to those kept
- **C**: Create new, stricter ground rules
- **D**: Abandon ground rules since the team clearly doesn't want them

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Rules ignored without consequence suggests they may no longer fit the team's reality or weren't truly agreed upon. A retrospective allows the team to revisit their agreements, retire rules that don't serve them, update others, and recommit to what they keep. This restores meaning and ownership.

Option A enforces rules that may no longer be relevant, creating resentment without improving function.

Option C adds more rules when existing ones aren't followed - more likely to be ignored.

Option D abandons a useful practice rather than fixing it. Ground rules can serve teams well when they're owned and relevant.
