# Question

The team has a ground rule: "be on time and stay for the full meeting." A senior team member consistently arrives 10 minutes late and leaves 15 minutes early, citing other commitments. Two more junior members have started doing the same. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Speak privately with the senior team member, raise the specific behaviour and its visible effect on the team, and ask them to honour the agreed rule.
- **B**: Restate the ground rule at the start of the next meeting without singling anyone out by name.
- **C**: Update the ground rule to acknowledge that some senior members will have legitimate scheduling conflicts they can't avoid.
- **D**: Make a quiet exception for the senior member while keeping the rule in place for everyone else on the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ground rules only function if they apply to everyone, and the PMI-aligned response is a direct, private, factual conversation with the person who is breaking the rule and influencing others. (A) does this. (B) is indirect and rarely changes the behaviour of someone who knows it isn't aimed at them. (C) rewards the non-compliance by changing the rule rather than the behaviour. (D) creates two classes of team member, which destroys the credibility of every other ground rule too.
