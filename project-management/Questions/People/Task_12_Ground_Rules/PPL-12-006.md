# Question

A team has established ground rules including "respect meeting times." One senior team member consistently arrives late and leaves early, citing other commitments. Other team members are starting to follow this example. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Create an exception for the senior member given their status
- **B**: Remind the entire team about the ground rule without singling anyone out
- **C**: Address the behaviour directly with the senior member, explaining the impact on the team
- **D**: Change the ground rule to accommodate varying schedules

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules only work if consistently applied. The senior member's behaviour is undermining the rule and setting a negative example. A direct, private conversation should address the specific behaviour, its impact (others copying, meetings less effective), and the expectation that all team members follow agreed rules regardless of seniority.

Option A (exception) creates two classes of team members and destroys the ground rule's credibility.

Option B (general reminder) is indirect and unlikely to change behaviour if the person doesn't see it as directed at them.

Option D (change rule) rewards non-compliance and may not be what the team actually wants.
