# Question

A team's ground rules include "all decisions by consensus." This worked with a small team but is now causing delays as the team has grown to 12 people. What should the PM facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Continue requiring consensus to honour the original agreement
- **B**: Unilaterally change the rule to majority voting
- **C**: Facilitate discussion about decision-making approaches that work at the new team size, potentially using different methods for different decision types
- **D**: Split the team into smaller groups

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. What worked at one size may not work at another. The PM should facilitate a conversation about decision-making: What types of decisions truly need consensus? Where is "consent" (no objections) sufficient? Where can a smaller group decide on behalf of the team? This adapts the rule to current reality while maintaining collaborative spirit.

Option A prioritises original agreement over current effectiveness.

Option B changes the rule without team involvement.

Option D addresses a symptom rather than the decision-making process.
