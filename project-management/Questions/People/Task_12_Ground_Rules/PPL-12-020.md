# Question

A project team's ground rules state that all decisions require unanimous agreement. As the project progresses, this is causing significant delays because one team member frequently disagrees. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Override the dissenting team member to keep the project moving
- **B**: Remove the dissenting team member from the decision-making process
- **C**: Facilitate a team discussion to revisit and adjust the decision-making ground rule
- **D**: Escalate decisions to management when unanimity cannot be reached

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules should serve the team, not hinder it. If a rule isn't working, the team should revisit and adjust it. Perhaps consensus (can live with) rather than unanimity is more practical, or perhaps certain types of decisions need different processes. The team should decide this together.

Option A violates the agreed ground rule and undermines trust.

Option B doesn't address the systemic issue and may exclude valuable perspectives.

Option D adds delays and removes team autonomy for an internal matter.
