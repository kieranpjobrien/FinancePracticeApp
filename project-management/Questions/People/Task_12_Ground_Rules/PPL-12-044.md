# Question

A team member argues that a proposed ground rule about limiting work-in-progress is unnecessary micromanagement. Other team members support the rule. How should the PM facilitate this disagreement?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the majority and implement the rule
- **B**: Remove the proposed rule to avoid conflict
- **C**: Facilitate dialogue to understand the concern, explain the rationale, and find a version that addresses both perspectives
- **D**: Implement the rule on a trial basis without further discussion

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The objection may have valid concerns (autonomy, trust) that deserve exploration. Understanding both sides - why some want the rule and why one opposes it - may reveal a modified version that addresses the underlying needs without feeling like micromanagement.

Option A dismisses a potentially valid concern.

Option B avoids helpful conflict.

Option D doesn't address the underlying disagreement.
