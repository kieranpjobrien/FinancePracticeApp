# Question

Two team members had a heated exchange in a sprint review that clearly broke the team's agreed "disagree respectfully" ground rule. Both have since cooled down. Other team members are watching to see what the PM will do. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the matter drop since both parties have cooled down and drawing further attention to the exchange will only amplify the awkwardness.
- **B**: Issue a written reminder to the full team about the ground rule so the standard is reinforced without singling out the two individuals.
- **C**: Counsel the two members privately and, separately, invite the team to briefly revisit the ground rule so the breach is acknowledged openly.
- **D**: Open the next retrospective with a detailed replay of the exchange and facilitate a team conversation about what should have happened differently.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's approach to breach is to address it with the individuals and restore the norm with the team — the team saw it happen, so silence on the norm reads as tolerance. (A) lets the unspoken signal undermine the rule. (B) is a depersonalised memo that the team will recognise as avoidance. (D) publicly re-litigates a resolved flare-up and risks re-igniting it.
