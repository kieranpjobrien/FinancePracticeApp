# Question

In a sprint retrospective, the team becomes heated about the rule for code-review turnaround. Some want a strict 24-hour SLA; others want flexibility. The Scrum Master sees the discussion stalling. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Decide the rule themselves to end the conflict and let the team move on.
- **B**: Park the topic and bring it back to the next retrospective when emotions have cooled.
- **C**: Take a quick majority vote so the team can adopt a rule and end the debate.
- **D**: Help the team surface the underlying needs behind each position and craft an option that addresses both.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role is to facilitate the team to a workable agreement, not to impose one. (D) treats the disagreement as a signal of competing legitimate needs (predictability vs. autonomy) and helps the team design something both sides can commit to, such as a default SLA with a documented exception path. (A) removes team ownership. (B) leaves the team frustrated and the issue unresolved. (C) is fast but leaves the minority uncommitted, which often re-erupts later.
