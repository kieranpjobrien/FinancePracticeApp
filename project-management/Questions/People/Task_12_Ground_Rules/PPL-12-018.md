# Question

An agile team's retrospective reveals frustration about ground rules. Some team members want strict rules about code review turnaround, while others prefer flexibility. The discussion becomes heated. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision for the team to end the conflict
- **B**: Table the discussion until emotions cool down
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion to find the underlying needs and help the team reach a consensus that addresses both perspectives
- **D**: Let the majority vote decide

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The conflict reveals different underlying needs (predictability vs autonomy). Understanding these needs allows the team to craft a solution that addresses both - perhaps a standard turnaround with a process for exceptions. Facilitation helps the team work through the conflict productively.

Option A removes team ownership and doesn't build the team's conflict resolution capability.

Option B delays without resolving and may allow frustration to build.

Option D may leave a significant minority unhappy and uncommitted to the rule.
