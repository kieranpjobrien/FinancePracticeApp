# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of having documented team ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: They provide documentation for HR if discipline is needed
- **B**: They create shared expectations that reduce conflict and improve collaboration
- **C**: They demonstrate professional project management to stakeholders
- **D**: They ensure compliance with organizational policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ground rules establish shared expectations about how team members interact and work together. When everyone understands and agrees to the same norms, there's less ambiguity and fewer conflicts about appropriate behavior. This improves collaboration and team effectiveness.

Option A focuses on punitive use, which isn't the primary purpose. Ground rules are about positive team function, not discipline.

Option C focuses on external perception rather than team function. While good practices may impress stakeholders, that's not the primary purpose.

Option D describes policy compliance, which is separate from team ground rules. Ground rules are team-specific working agreements, not organizational policy enforcement.
