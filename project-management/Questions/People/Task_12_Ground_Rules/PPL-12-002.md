# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of having documented team ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: They provide formal documentation HR can refer to if disciplinary action becomes necessary.
- **B**: They demonstrate professional project management practice to senior stakeholders.
- **C**: They make implicit expectations explicit, reducing avoidable conflict and supporting effective collaboration.
- **D**: They ensure the team is operating in line with the organisation's mandatory policies.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Ground rules exist primarily to make how-we-work agreements explicit, so the team can collaborate without re-litigating norms or getting blindsided by unspoken expectations. (A) frames ground rules as disciplinary tooling, which they aren't. (B) is about external perception rather than team function. (D) describes policy compliance, which is a separate mechanism — ground rules are team-level working agreements, not policy enforcement.
