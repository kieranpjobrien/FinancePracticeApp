# Question

A project team agreed comprehensive ground rules at kickoff three months ago. The PM realises no one has looked at the document since. Several recent minor conflicts could have been prevented if people had remembered specific rules the team had actually agreed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the ground rules visible in the team's working surfaces — retro templates, Definition of Done, meeting agendas — so they are encountered in the flow of work.
- **B**: Send the ground-rules document to everyone on the team by email each week so members are regularly reminded of the agreements they had previously signed up to.
- **C**: Leave the document where it is and rely on the PM to reference specific rules from memory in the moments when they become directly relevant to team work.
- **D**: Turn the ground rules into a compulsory quarterly quiz every team member must complete to prove they have recent knowledge of each rule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI-aligned practice is to weave norms into the artefacts the team already uses so they are encountered naturally rather than as a separate compliance exercise. (B) is a reminder that becomes email noise. (C) relies on the PM's recall and does not help peer-to-peer interactions. (D) is heavyweight process theatre that teams resent and still does not put rules into the flow of work.
