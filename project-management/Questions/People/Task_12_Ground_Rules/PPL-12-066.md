# Question

A hybrid team includes a vice president working day-to-day as a subject-matter expert alongside mid-level staff. When co-designing ground rules, mid-level members defer on every contentious point to the VP, producing rules that mostly reflect the VP's preferences. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the session, name the pattern, and run the next round using small groups and written input so seniority no longer dominates the conversation.
- **B**: Finalise the rules as agreed since no one explicitly objected and raising power dynamics might embarrass the VP in front of the rest of the team.
- **C**: Send out the draft rules after the session for anonymous comment, then adjust based on feedback once the VP has logged off from the shared working file.
- **D**: Ask the VP to leave the room for the rest of the ground-rules co-design so the mid-level staff can express their views without the senior presence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's inclusion and facilitation principles expect the facilitator to name and redesign when a process is being distorted by power — small groups and written input redistribute voice without excluding anyone. (B) hides a known design flaw. (C) runs a shadow process rather than repairing the real one. (D) excludes a legitimate team member, which is neither necessary nor respectful.
