# Question

What is a "working agreement" in an agile team context?

## Options

- **A**: A binding contract between team members and the organisation.
- **B**: A team-created set of ground rules describing how the team will work together.
- **C**: A formal record of working hours and on-call rotations.
- **D**: An agreement with stakeholders defining the deliverables and acceptance criteria.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Working agreements are the agile equivalent of team ground rules: a self-created set of norms covering communication, collaboration, decision-making, and conflict. (B) captures this. (A) overstates the artefact; it is a social contract, not a legal one. (C) is too narrow. (D) describes a stakeholder agreement or acceptance contract, not a working agreement.
