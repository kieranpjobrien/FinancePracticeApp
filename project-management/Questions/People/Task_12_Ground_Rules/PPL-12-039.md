# Question

What is a "working agreement" in agile methodology?

## Options

- **A**: A legal contract between team members
- **B**: A set of ground rules the team creates and agrees to follow
- **C**: A document specifying work hours
- **D**: An agreement with stakeholders about deliverables

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In agile, working agreements are the team's self-created ground rules for how they'll work together. They cover communication, collaboration, decision-making, and other team norms. Teams typically create these during team formation and revisit them during retrospectives.

Option A - working agreements are social contracts, not legal ones.

Option C - work hours might be part of it, but it covers much more.

Option D - stakeholder agreements are separate documents.
