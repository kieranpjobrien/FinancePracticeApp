# Question

A team has a ground rule that says "No blame - focus on solutions." During a project review, a significant error is discovered that was caused by one team member's oversight. Other team members want to understand what happened to prevent recurrence. How should this be handled?

## Options

- **A**: Skip the discussion to avoid blame
- **B**: Discuss what happened and who was responsible so it doesn't happen again
- **C**: Analyse the process failure without personal blame, focusing on systemic improvements
- **D**: Privately reprimand the team member so the team doesn't see it

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. "No blame" doesn't mean avoiding accountability or analysis. It means focusing on systemic factors rather than personal punishment. Examining what process gaps allowed the error helps the team improve without creating a culture of fear. The individual can learn without being publicly shamed.

Option A prevents valuable learning.

Option B may create a blame culture that discourages reporting problems.

Option D is punitive rather than developmental and doesn't help the team learn.
