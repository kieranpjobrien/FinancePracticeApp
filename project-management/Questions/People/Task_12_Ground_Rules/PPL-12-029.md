# Question

A team ground rule states "no blame, focus on solutions." During a project review, an error caused by one team member's oversight is discovered. Other team members want to understand what happened. How should the project manager handle the discussion?

## Options

- **A**: Defer detailed analysis to a private one-on-one with the team member involved.
- **B**: Skip the discussion entirely to honour the no-blame rule.
- **C**: Identify what process gaps allowed the error and discuss systemic improvements.
- **D**: Ask the team member to walk the team through what they did and what they would do differently.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A "no blame" rule is about avoiding personal punishment, not about avoiding analysis. (C) is the blameless post-mortem approach: focus on the system, not the individual, so the team can learn and improve. (A) prevents the team from learning. (B) misinterprets the rule as a ban on examination. (D) puts an individual on the spot to publicly recount their mistake, which is the opposite of a blameless culture.
