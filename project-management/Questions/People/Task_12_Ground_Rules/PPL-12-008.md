# Question

A team created ground rules at the start of the project, but over time they've become outdated and no one follows them. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce the original rules strictly
- **B**: Facilitate a retrospective to revisit ground rules, updating or removing ones that no longer serve the team
- **C**: Abandon ground rules entirely since they didn't work
- **D**: Create new rules without team input

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ground rules should evolve with the team. A retrospective provides structured time to examine what rules are working, what's outdated, and what new agreements are needed. This renews team commitment to relevant rules and removes the cognitive load of obsolete ones.

Option A enforces rules that may no longer fit team needs.

Option C loses the value of shared agreements entirely.

Option D creates rules without the buy-in that comes from collaborative creation.
