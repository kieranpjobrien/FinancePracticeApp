# Question

Ground rules created at project start are no longer being followed. The team has changed shape and the rules feel stale. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Strictly enforce the existing rules to re-establish the discipline they were designed to create.
- **B**: Drop ground rules altogether and rely on the team's professionalism going forward.
- **C**: Rewrite the rules personally based on the issues the PM has observed and circulate the new version.
- **D**: Use the next retrospective to revisit the rules with the team — refresh what still helps, retire what doesn't, and add what's now needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Ground rules are living agreements; the PMI-aligned response is a team-led refresh in a regular reflection forum like a retrospective. (D) does this. (A) enforces rules that may no longer make sense and damages trust. (B) discards a practice that works when the rules are current and owned. (C) writes the rules unilaterally and skips the discussion that produces commitment.
