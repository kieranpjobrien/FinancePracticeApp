# Question

Two team members disagree about whether working from home should be permitted. One values the flexibility, the other believes remote work erodes collaboration. The team's existing ground rules do not address location. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the organisation's published remote work policy and treat the matter as settled.
- **B**: Defer to the functional manager of each team member to decide their work location.
- **C**: Facilitate a team conversation to surface the underlying needs and agree on a working norm.
- **D**: Pilot a hybrid arrangement for two sprints and review results in the retrospective.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Ground rules belong to the team, and gaps should be closed by the team. (C) addresses the underlying tension by exposing the needs behind each position (autonomy vs. collaboration) so the team can craft a norm both can commit to. (A) skips the team conversation and treats a team-norm question as a policy question. (B) fragments decision-making and avoids building a shared agreement. (D) is a reasonable later step, but jumping to a pilot without first understanding the needs risks solving the wrong problem.
