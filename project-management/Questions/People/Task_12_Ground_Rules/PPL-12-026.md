# Question

A global team has members from cultures with very different norms about hierarchy, directness, and conflict. The PM needs to establish ground rules that work for everyone. What approach would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Use ground rules from the headquarters location since that's the organisational culture
- **B**: Avoid discussing cultural differences to prevent uncomfortable conversations
- **C**: Explicitly discuss cultural differences and co-create rules that accommodate or bridge different norms
- **D**: Let each team member follow their own cultural norms

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Acknowledging cultural differences creates psychological safety and shows respect. Discussing them openly helps the team understand each other and create rules that work across cultures. This might include explicit norms about how to disagree, when to escalate, or how to show respect in ways all understand.

Option A imposes one culture and alienates others.

Option B ignores real differences that will cause friction.

Option D doesn't create shared norms and leads to confusion.
