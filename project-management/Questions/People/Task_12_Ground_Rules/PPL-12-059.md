# Question

A PM facilitated the creation of strong ground rules with their team six weeks ago. In a recent review, two team members noted that the PM themselves regularly breaks the "end meetings on time" rule they championed. The PM had not realised. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the breach openly, apologise, commit publicly to the rule, and ask the team to call it out directly if it happens again.
- **B**: Remove the end-on-time rule from the team's ground rules since the PM's calendar load makes it unrealistic to hold in practice for them personally.
- **C**: Explain to the team that the PM's schedule makes the rule harder for them and ask for a PM-specific exemption within the team's ground rules.
- **D**: Make smaller adjustments to the PM's meeting scheduling quietly, without a public acknowledgement that might undermine the PM's authority on the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects leaders to model the rules they ask others to follow — acknowledging the breach and inviting direct feedback builds, rather than damages, credibility. (B) removes a rule the team genuinely values because the PM has failed to keep it. (C) creates a two-tier rule set with the leader on the easier tier. (D) hides the issue and the team already knows, which makes it worse.
