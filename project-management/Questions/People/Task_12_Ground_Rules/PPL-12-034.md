# Question

A project team includes both internal staff and a vendor's developers. The vendor requires all communications to flow through their team lead, while the project's existing ground rule encourages direct peer-to-peer communication. How should the project manager resolve this?

## Options

- **A**: Require the vendor team to follow the project's ground rules since they are working on the project.
- **B**: Let each group operate under its own communications norms to avoid contractual issues.
- **C**: Work with vendor leadership to define an integrated communications agreement that meets both needs.
- **D**: Refer the conflict to procurement to resolve under the contract.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cross-organisational teams need negotiated norms, not imposed ones. (C) addresses the underlying needs (vendor visibility and oversight, project agility) and produces a workable integrated agreement. (A) ignores the vendor's legitimate constraints. (B) creates two parallel sets of norms and predictable confusion. (D) escalates a working-level relationship issue into a contract dispute prematurely.
