# Question

A project team includes members from both the organisation and an external vendor. The vendor team has their own ground rules that conflict with those the PM has established. For example, the vendor requires all communications to go through their team lead, while the PM's rules encourage direct peer-to-peer communication. How should this be resolved?

## Options

- **A**: Require the vendor to follow the project's ground rules since they're working on the project
- **B**: Let each group follow their own rules
- **C**: Collaborate with vendor leadership to create integrated ground rules that work for both organisations
- **D**: Escalate to procurement to enforce contractual terms

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When teams from different organisations work together, ground rules need to accommodate both contexts. Collaboration between leadership can identify the underlying concerns (vendor may need visibility, project needs efficiency) and create rules that address both.

Option A may violate the vendor's internal requirements and create conflict.

Option B leads to confusion and different expectations.

Option D escalates a working relationship issue to a contractual dispute.
