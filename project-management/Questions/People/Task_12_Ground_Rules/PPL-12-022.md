# Question

A team has a ground rule about "respecting different opinions." However, two team members have very different interpretations - one thinks it means never challenging ideas, while the other thinks it means debating ideas vigorously. Tensions arise during technical discussions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add more specific rules about how to discuss technical topics
- **B**: Facilitate a team discussion to develop a shared understanding of what "respect" means in practice
- **C**: Tell both team members they're correct and to find middle ground
- **D**: Remove the ambiguous rule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Vague rules need shared interpretation. Facilitating a discussion helps the team develop concrete examples of respectful behaviour. They might agree that challenging ideas is welcome while personal attacks are not, or establish specific norms for technical debates.

Option A adds rules without addressing the interpretation gap.

Option C doesn't provide clarity and continues the tension.

Option D loses the value of the rule rather than clarifying it.
