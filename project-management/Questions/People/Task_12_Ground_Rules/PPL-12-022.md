# Question

The team's ground rule says "respect different opinions." Two engineers interpret it differently: one believes it forbids challenging others' technical proposals, the other believes vigorous debate is part of respect. Tension is rising in design discussions. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a team conversation to agree on what "respect" looks like in practice.
- **B**: Replace the rule with a more detailed code-of-conduct document.
- **C**: Side privately with the engineer who favours debate, since technical scrutiny is needed.
- **D**: Drop the rule because it is too ambiguous to enforce.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The rule is fine; the team's shared interpretation of it is the missing piece. (A) helps the team translate a value into observable behaviours, for example "challenge the idea, not the person" or "ask before pushing back." (B) replaces a values statement with a procedural document and avoids the discussion. (C) takes sides and damages trust. (D) discards a useful norm rather than clarifying it.
