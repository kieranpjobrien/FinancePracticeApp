# Question

A team has a ground rule that meetings should have agendas shared 24 hours in advance. A team member calls an urgent meeting without an agenda. Other team members complain about the rule violation. How should the PM handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Require cancellation of the meeting until an agenda is provided
- **B**: Add an exception to the rule for urgent meetings and clarify what qualifies as urgent
- **C**: Remove the agenda rule since it's causing problems
- **D**: Reprimand the team member who called the meeting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Most rules need reasonable exceptions. Adding clarity about urgent situations (what qualifies, what minimal preparation is needed) addresses the legitimate concern while maintaining the rule's value for normal meetings. This prevents the rule from becoming a barrier to necessary flexibility.

Option A prioritises process over pragmatism in a situation that was genuinely urgent.

Option C removes a useful rule entirely.

Option D focuses on blame rather than improving the rule.
