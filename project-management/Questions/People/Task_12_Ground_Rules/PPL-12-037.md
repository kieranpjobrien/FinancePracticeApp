# Question

The team's ground rule requires meeting agendas to be circulated 24 hours in advance. A team member calls a genuinely urgent meeting without one, and other members raise the rule violation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add an explicit exception for urgent meetings and define what qualifies as urgent.
- **B**: Ask the team member who called the meeting to apologise to the team for the breach.
- **C**: Cancel the meeting and reschedule it once a proper agenda has been distributed.
- **D**: Drop the agenda rule because it is creating more friction than value.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A useful rule that lacks an exception path will eventually be broken; the right response is to refine it. (A) preserves the rule's value for normal meetings while making the urgent path legitimate and defined. (B) treats a process gap as a personal failing. (C) is rigid and harms the project when the urgency was real. (D) discards a useful norm rather than improving it.
