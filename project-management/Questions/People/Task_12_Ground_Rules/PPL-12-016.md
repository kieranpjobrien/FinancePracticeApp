# Question

During a project kickoff, the PM proposes a set of ground rules they developed based on their experience with previous teams. Some team members seem hesitant but don't object. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the rules since no one objected
- **B**: Explain why each rule is important until the team agrees
- **C**: Invite the team to discuss, modify, and co-create the ground rules together
- **D**: Tell the team they can revisit the rules after a month if there are problems

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules are most effective when the team has ownership of them. Co-creation ensures the rules reflect the team's actual needs, builds buy-in, and makes team members more likely to follow and enforce them. The PM's experience provides a starting point, not a final answer.

Option A mistakes silence for agreement and misses the buy-in that comes from participation.

Option B is persuasion, not collaboration, and may create compliance without commitment.

Option D delays addressing the lack of buy-in and starts the project with uncommitted norms.
