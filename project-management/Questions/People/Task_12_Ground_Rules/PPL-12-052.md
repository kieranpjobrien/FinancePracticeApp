# Question

An agile team's ground rule says important technical decisions will be reached by consensus. In practice, several decisions have stalled for over a week because one team member will not agree to any option. The sprint is at risk. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the consensus rule and use a professional facilitator to break the pattern, since abandoning consensus would damage the team's psychological safety.
- **B**: Override the rule for this decision and use majority vote, returning to consensus for future decisions once the current blocker has been cleared out of the way.
- **C**: Refresh the rule with the team — keep consensus-seeking as the default but add an explicit tiebreaker after a defined time or escalation threshold is reached.
- **D**: Replace the consensus rule entirely with a RACI-style decision matrix that names a single accountable decider for each category of technical decision.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's inspect-and-adapt principle applied to ground rules: keep the intent, fix the failure mode — consensus-seeking with a defined tiebreaker preserves collaboration without the hostage problem. (A) doubles down on a rule with a structural flaw. (B) breaks the rule without changing it, which erodes the agreement. (D) over-corrects by removing collaborative decision-making entirely.
