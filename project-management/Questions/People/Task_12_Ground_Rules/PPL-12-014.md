# Question

A team creates ground rules including "no side conversations during meetings." During a meeting, the PM notices two senior developers whispering together. They may be discussing something relevant to the topic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it since they're senior team members
- **B**: Interrupt harshly to enforce the rule
- **C**: Acknowledge the conversation neutrally and invite them to share with the group if relevant
- **D**: Address it privately after the meeting

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Enforce ground rules consistently but gracefully. A neutral acknowledgment ("It looks like you're discussing something - would you like to share with the group?") maintains the rule without creating conflict. If it's relevant, the group benefits; if not, they're reminded of the norm. This is respectful enforcement.

Option A creates inconsistent application and double standards.

Option B is unnecessarily harsh.

Option D misses the opportunity for in-the-moment course correction.
