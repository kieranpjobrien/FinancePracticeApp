# Question

The team has a ground rule against side conversations during meetings. Mid-discussion, the project manager notices two senior developers whispering. They appear to be discussing the agenda topic. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the conversation since it appears to be about the topic at hand.
- **B**: Pause the meeting and remind the whole team of the side-conversation rule.
- **C**: Invite the two developers to share whatever they are discussing with the group.
- **D**: Speak with the two developers privately after the meeting ends.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Consistent, low-friction enforcement keeps ground rules credible. (C) acknowledges the side conversation, applies the rule fairly, and lets the group benefit if the discussion is relevant. (A) creates a double standard for senior members and erodes the rule. (B) is heavier-handed than necessary and embarrasses the developers in front of the team. (D) misses the in-the-moment teaching opportunity and lets the meeting continue with the rule violated.
