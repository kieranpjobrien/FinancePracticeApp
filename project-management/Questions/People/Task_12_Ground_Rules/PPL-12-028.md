# Question

The team's ground-rules document has grown to over 30 entries, and members admit they cannot remember most of them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a team session to prioritise and consolidate the list down to a small set of essential rules.
- **B**: Print the rules as a one-page poster and display it in the team workspace.
- **C**: Cover three rules per stand-up on a rotating basis until the team has memorised them.
- **D**: Keep the full list but only enforce the rules that the project manager judges critical.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A ground-rule list nobody can recall is functionally no list at all. (A) addresses the root cause by trimming to a memorable, meaningful set the team will actually live by. (B) makes the unwieldy list more visible without solving its size. (C) treats memorisation as the problem when the real problem is excess. (D) creates inconsistent enforcement and erodes trust.
