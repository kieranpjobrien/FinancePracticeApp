# Question

The PM notices that the team has created too many ground rules - the list has grown to over 30 items. Team members are having trouble remembering them all. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Create a poster with all the rules and display it prominently
- **B**: Facilitate a team exercise to prioritise and consolidate to the most essential rules
- **C**: Quiz team members regularly to help them memorise the rules
- **D**: Keep all the rules but only enforce the most important ones

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Too many rules become unmanageable. Prioritising and consolidating helps the team focus on what truly matters. A smaller set of essential rules is easier to remember, follow, and reinforce. Some rules may be combined, others may be dropped if they're not critical.

Option A doesn't address the fundamental problem of having too many rules.

Option C treats symptoms, not the cause, and may feel punitive.

Option D creates inconsistency and confusion about which rules matter.
