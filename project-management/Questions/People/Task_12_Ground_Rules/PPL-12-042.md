# Question

A PM inherits a team that has no documented ground rules but has been working together effectively for years. They have implicit norms that everyone seems to understand. Should the PM establish formal ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: Yes, immediately document formal rules for consistency
- **B**: No, if it's working don't change it
- **C**: Observe and understand existing norms, then work with the team to make them explicit if beneficial
- **D**: Replace existing norms with standard organisational ground rules

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Implicit norms can work well for stable teams but may cause problems when new members join or when conflicts arise. Observing first shows respect for what works. Making norms explicit (if the team sees value) creates clarity without disrupting effective patterns.

Option A imposes change without understanding what's working.

Option B misses the value of explicit norms for onboarding and conflict resolution.

Option D ignores team-specific context.
