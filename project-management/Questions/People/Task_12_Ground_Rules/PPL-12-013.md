# Question

A team's ground rule is "disagree and commit" - debate decisions thoroughly, but once decided, everyone commits fully. A team member continues advocating against a decision after it was made. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let them continue since they feel strongly
- **B**: Have a private conversation reminding them of the ground rule and the impact of continued dissent on team unity
- **C**: Publicly call them out in a team meeting
- **D**: Revisit the decision since someone still disagrees

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ground rules only work if consistently applied. A private conversation addresses the behaviour without public embarrassment. Explain the impact: continued dissent undermines the team's ability to move forward and erodes the value of the decision-making process. Reinforce commitment to the process they agreed to.

Option A allows ground rules to be ignored.

Option C creates public conflict unnecessarily.

Option D rewards continued dissent and undermines the "commit" part of the process.
