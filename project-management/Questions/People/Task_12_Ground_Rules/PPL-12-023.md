# Question

When should a project team revisit and potentially update their ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: Only at the end of the project during lessons learned
- **B**: Only when there's a major conflict
- **C**: Regularly throughout the project and when circumstances change
- **D**: Never - ground rules should remain consistent

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules should evolve with the team. Regular reviews (such as during retrospectives) ensure rules remain relevant. Changes in circumstances (new members, remote work transition, project phase changes) may require adjustments. Static rules may become outdated or counterproductive.

Option A misses opportunities to improve during the project.

Option B is reactive rather than proactive.

Option D treats ground rules as fixed rather than living agreements.
