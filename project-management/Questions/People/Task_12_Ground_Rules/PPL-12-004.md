# Question

When should team ground rules be established?

## Options

- **A**: After the team has worked together long enough to identify the specific behaviours that need agreements.
- **B**: Only when a behavioural problem arises that requires the team to set a clear boundary.
- **C**: They should be set by the organisation rather than created by individual project teams.
- **D**: Early in team formation, before issues develop, and revisited as the team evolves.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's team-development guidance places ground-rule setting early — during forming — and treats them as living agreements that evolve with the team. (D) does this. (A) waits until the team has already established norms by default, often unhelpful ones. (B) is reactive and forces the conversation in a moment of friction. (C) imposes top-down rules and removes the ownership that makes them stick.
