# Question

When should a team's ground rules be established?

## Options

- **A**: Only when problems arise that need rules to address
- **B**: Early in team formation, before issues develop, and revisited as the team evolves
- **C**: After the team has worked together long enough to identify needs
- **D**: Ground rules should be provided by the organization, not created by teams

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Establishing ground rules early creates shared expectations before conflicts occur. This is preventive rather than reactive. Revisiting them as the team evolves ensures they remain relevant - what works for a forming team may need adjustment for a performing team.

Option A is reactive - waiting for problems means dealing with conflict without agreed norms.

Option C delays establishment, missing the opportunity to prevent early friction.

Option D removes team ownership, reducing buy-in. Teams should create their own working agreements.
