# Question

How do team ground rules typically affect psychological safety?

## Options

- **A**: They have no meaningful relationship to psychological safety.
- **B**: They reduce psychological safety because they restrict what people can do.
- **C**: They increase psychological safety through more rules and tighter enforcement.
- **D**: Clear, fairly applied rules support psychological safety by setting predictable expectations.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Psychological safety is built on predictability and fairness. (D) reflects the PMI view that well-crafted team norms reduce ambiguity, signal mutual respect, and make it safer to speak up. (A) ignores the documented connection. (B) misframes ground rules as restriction rather than as a shared agreement. (C) is wrong because volume and rigour are not what produces safety; clarity and fairness are.
