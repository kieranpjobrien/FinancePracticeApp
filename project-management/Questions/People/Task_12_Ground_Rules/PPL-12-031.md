# Question

What is the relationship between ground rules and team psychological safety?

## Options

- **A**: Ground rules are unrelated to psychological safety
- **B**: More ground rules always create more psychological safety
- **C**: Clear, fair ground rules can create safety by establishing predictable expectations
- **D**: Ground rules reduce psychological safety by restricting behaviour

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Psychological safety comes partly from knowing what to expect. When team members understand the norms and know they'll be applied fairly, they feel safer to contribute. Rules about respectful disagreement, for example, can make it safer to voice concerns.

Option A ignores the connection between norms and safety.

Option B - quantity doesn't equal quality; too many rules can feel restrictive.

Option D - well-designed ground rules enable rather than restrict by creating shared understanding.
