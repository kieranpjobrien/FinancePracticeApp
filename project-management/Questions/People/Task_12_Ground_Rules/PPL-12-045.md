# Question

The PMO has issued an organisational ground rule that the PMO must be CCed on every project communication. The team finds this floods inboxes and slows responses. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team appear compliant in major channels and route working chat through informal tools.
- **B**: Enforce the PMO rule fully on the team and absorb the slower response time.
- **C**: Encourage the team to ignore the rule because it harms productivity.
- **D**: Approach the PMO to understand the rule's intent and propose an alternative that meets it without the side effects.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Organisational rules usually exist for a reason; the right response is to engage the rule's owner. (D) treats the PMO as a partner, surfaces the underlying intent (visibility, governance), and proposes an alternative such as weekly summaries or shared dashboards. (A) is dishonest and erodes trust. (B) accepts the dysfunction without trying to improve it. (C) violates organisational policy and exposes the project to consequences.
