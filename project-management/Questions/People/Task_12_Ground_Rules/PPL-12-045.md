# Question

An organisation mandates certain ground rules for all project teams (e.g., all project communications must CC the PMO). The PM's team finds this rule counterproductive because it floods inboxes and slows communication. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the organisational rule and let the team communicate efficiently
- **B**: Enforce the rule strictly since it's organisational policy
- **C**: Work with the PMO to understand the rule's intent and propose an alternative that meets their needs without hampering the team
- **D**: Have the team comply publicly but use workarounds privately

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PMO rule likely has a legitimate purpose (visibility, governance). Understanding this allows proposing alternatives (summary updates, shared folders, exception lists) that meet the underlying need without the negative side effects. This is more sustainable than compliance or resistance.

Option A violates organisational policy and may create problems.

Option B accepts dysfunction without trying to improve it.

Option D creates a culture of workarounds and undermines trust.
