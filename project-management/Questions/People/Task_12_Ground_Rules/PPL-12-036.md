# Question

During a project crisis requiring rapid response, the team finds that some ground rules (like requiring consensus for decisions and 24-hour review periods) are slowing things down. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend all ground rules during the crisis
- **B**: Continue following all rules despite the delay
- **C**: Work with the team to define temporary crisis protocols that modify specific rules while maintaining core values
- **D**: Make unilateral decisions to speed things up

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Crisis situations may require temporary adjustments. Working with the team to define these explicitly maintains transparency and trust. The team can agree on what changes for crisis mode (faster decisions) while preserving what doesn't (respectful communication).

Option A abandons all structure when some rules remain important.

Option B may not respond to legitimate urgency.

Option D undermines team ownership and may damage trust.
