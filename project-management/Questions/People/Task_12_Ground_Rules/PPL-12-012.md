# Question

A team ground rule states "cameras on during video meetings." A new team member from a different cultural background feels uncomfortable with this and has raised the concern. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce the rule since it was agreed before they joined
- **B**: Excuse this one person from the rule
- **C**: Revisit the ground rule with the full team, exploring the purpose behind it and whether adjustments can accommodate different comfort levels while achieving the goal
- **D**: Remove all ground rules to avoid similar issues

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ground rules should be revisited when new perspectives arise. The underlying goal (engagement, connection, attention) can be discussed along with different comfort levels. The team might find the original rule still works, or might adjust (cameras on for some meetings, optional for others, other ways to signal engagement).

Option A prioritises precedent over inclusion.

Option B creates two classes of team members.

Option D overreacts by abandoning all structure.
