# Question

The team has an agreed ground rule: "no changes enter a sprint mid-sprint except for production incidents." The sponsor frequently asks the PM to add high-priority work mid-sprint anyway, arguing their requests are always urgent and important. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Break the ground rule whenever the sponsor asks and explain each exception to the team on a case-by-case basis as it happens.
- **B**: Meet with the sponsor, explain the ground rule, the cost of interruption, and agree a clear path for genuine emergencies versus planned work.
- **C**: Keep the ground rule in place and tell the sponsor that mid-sprint change requests will simply not be accepted by the team regardless of their urgency.
- **D**: Present each mid-sprint request to the team for a vote and accept the request only if the team unanimously agrees it is worth the disruption cost.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder-engagement lens says to educate the sponsor on cost and co-design a realistic path for genuine emergencies — this protects the rule and meets the sponsor's real needs. (A) trains the sponsor that the rule does not exist. (C) refuses engagement rather than reshaping the sponsor's behaviour. (D) puts the team in the position of repeatedly saying no to the sponsor, which is not their role.
