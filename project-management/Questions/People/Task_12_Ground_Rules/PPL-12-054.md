# Question

A new developer joins a mature Scrum team that has well-embedded ground rules co-created two years ago. The new joiner begins breaking several rules in their first fortnight — not out of malice, but simply unaware of them. The team is starting to resent it. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Walk the new developer through the team's ground rules in a 1:1 and explain the reasoning the team originally had for each of them.
- **B**: Raise the pattern in the next retrospective and ask the team to remind the new developer collectively of the rules they are breaching in practice.
- **C**: Revise the team's ground rules wholesale because a new member has joined and the team's previous agreement no longer reflects the current composition.
- **D**: Send the new developer a link to the ground-rules document and expect them to work through it on their own before their next sprint commitment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's onboarding and servant-leadership principles align: a 1:1 walk-through with reasoning is the fastest way to get a new member genuinely on board with norms they did not help create. (B) invites a public group correction before a private clarifying conversation. (C) throws away a working agreement because one person is new. (D) assumes a document alone conveys the reasoning the rules are built on.
