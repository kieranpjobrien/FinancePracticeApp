# Question

What is the PRIMARY purpose of team ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: To punish team members who break rules
- **B**: To establish shared expectations for behaviour and interaction that support effective teamwork
- **C**: To document task assignments
- **D**: To satisfy organisational compliance requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ground rules establish shared behavioural expectations - how the team will communicate, make decisions, handle conflict, and work together. They create a foundation for effective collaboration by making implicit expectations explicit. Everyone knows what's expected and can hold each other accountable.

Option A focuses on punishment, but ground rules are about enabling collaboration, not penalising violations.

Option C describes responsibility assignment, not ground rules.

Option D - while rules may align with compliance, their primary purpose is effective teamwork.
