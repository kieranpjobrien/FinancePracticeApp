# Question

A newly formed team includes members who previously worked on teams with very different cultures - one highly structured with many rules, another very informal with few explicit norms. These different expectations are causing friction. What should the PM do during ground rule establishment?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt whichever team's rules the majority of members are familiar with
- **B**: Avoid establishing ground rules and let norms emerge naturally
- **C**: Acknowledge the different backgrounds and facilitate discussion about what combination of structure and flexibility will work for this new team
- **D**: Impose a completely new set of rules unrelated to either previous team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Acknowledging different backgrounds validates everyone's experience. Discussing what will work for this team allows combining the best of both approaches and ensures new norms are intentionally chosen rather than defaulting to one group's preferences.

Option A may alienate those whose previous norms are dismissed.

Option B risks extended friction as informal norms emerge haphazardly.

Option D ignores useful experience and may seem arbitrary.
