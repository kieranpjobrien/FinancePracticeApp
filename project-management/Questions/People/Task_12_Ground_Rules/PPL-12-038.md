# Question

A newly formed team includes members from two predecessor teams: one was highly structured with many explicit rules, the other operated almost entirely on informal norms. Friction is appearing as the new team begins to work. What should the project manager do during ground-rules establishment?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the structured team's rule set since explicit rules reduce ambiguity.
- **B**: Avoid setting rules and let norms emerge as the team works together.
- **C**: Surface the difference openly and facilitate the team to agree on the level of structure that suits this team.
- **D**: Draft a fresh set of rules unrelated to either predecessor and present it for adoption.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The friction is between two reasonable working styles, both familiar to roughly half the team. (C) names the difference, validates both backgrounds, and lets the team intentionally choose what works for them now. (A) privileges one half of the team. (B) leaves friction to harden into resentment. (D) ignores valuable lived experience and feels arbitrary.
