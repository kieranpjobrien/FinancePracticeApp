# Question

A team has developed strong internal shared understanding but struggles to communicate their work to external stakeholders who lack technical background. How should the PM bridge this gap?

## Options

- **A**: Train stakeholders on technical details
- **B**: Simplify communication so much that important nuances are lost
- **C**: Help the team develop communication skills for non-technical audiences, use analogies and visualisations, and focus on business impact rather than technical implementation
- **D**: Serve as translator for all team-stakeholder communications

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Technical teams benefit from developing external communication skills. Analogies make complex concepts accessible. Visualisations show relationships and flows. Focusing on business impact (what it means for users, value delivered) rather than technical how connects to stakeholder interests. Building this capability benefits the team long-term.

Option A may not be practical or efficient.

Option B loses important information.

Option D creates bottleneck and doesn't build team capability.
