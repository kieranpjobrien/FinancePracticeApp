# Question

A high-performing engineering team understands its own work deeply but consistently loses non-technical stakeholders during showcases. Stakeholders nod along but afterwards ask the PM to explain what was actually demonstrated. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the team to lead with business impact, use analogies and visuals, and structure showcases around what users can now do rather than how it was built.
- **B**: Take over the showcase narration personally so the team can focus on the technical content and the PM can translate.
- **C**: Replace the live showcase with a written report that stakeholders can read at their own pace and refer back to.
- **D**: Provide a short technical primer for stakeholders before each showcase so they can follow the team's natural style.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to develop the team's ability to engage stakeholders directly rather than insulating them from one another. (A) builds that capability and changes the structure of the showcase to suit the audience. (B) makes the PM a permanent translator and prevents the team from ever closing the gap themselves. (C) sacrifices the live, interactive part of the showcase that lets stakeholders react to working software. (D) trains stakeholders to listen to engineering content rather than asking engineering to communicate to its audience — the harder direction.
