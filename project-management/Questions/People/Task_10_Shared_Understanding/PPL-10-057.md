# Question

The PM's status report is green on RAG indicators, but the PM privately believes the programme is in trouble because three critical risks are escalating together. The sponsor has praised the "healthy reporting." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain the current RAG assessment since no single risk has yet crystallised and the sponsor has explicitly affirmed the current reporting pattern from the PM.
- **B**: Quietly soften the RAG to amber in the next report without emphasising it, so the signal gradually shifts without creating a sharp contrast with earlier reports.
- **C**: Add a narrative caveat to the next report noting "things to watch" while keeping the overall rating green so the sponsor is gently prepared for a possible change.
- **D**: Update the report to reflect the PM's honest assessment, brief the sponsor verbally beforehand with the evidence and options, and accept the implications for perception.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's ethics code on honesty and reporting is unambiguous: the report should reflect reality, with the accountable stakeholder briefed directly ahead of a material change. (A) continues a green rating the PM does not believe, which is an integrity failure. (B) obfuscates a real signal through a slow drip, which is an honesty failure. (C) mixes a green rating with contradicting narrative, which creates ambiguity rather than reporting clarity.
