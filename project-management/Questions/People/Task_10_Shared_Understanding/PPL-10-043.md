# Question

A project involves integrating two teams that previously worked independently. Each team has developed their own practices, terminology, and assumptions. How should the PM build shared understanding across the merged team?

## Options

- **A**: Impose one team's practices on the other
- **B**: Let both teams continue their separate practices
- **C**: Facilitate sessions where both teams share their approaches, identify best practices from each, and collaboratively develop unified ways of working
- **D**: Document both sets of practices and expect team members to learn both

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Team integration requires building new shared understanding rather than imposing or continuing separate approaches. Facilitated sharing surfaces the "why" behind each practice. Identifying best practices from each validates both teams' contributions. Collaborative development of unified practices creates buy-in and genuine shared understanding.

Option A creates winners/losers and loses good practices.

Option B maintains silos within the merged team.

Option D doubles the learning burden without creating unity.
