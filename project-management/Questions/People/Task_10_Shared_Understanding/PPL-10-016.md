# Question

The PM has discovered that the development team and business stakeholders hold fundamentally different mental models of how the product should behave end-to-end. Each side is convinced the other "just doesn't get it" but neither has tried to draw out the other's view. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a joint workshop using user-journey maps, prototypes, or whiteboard models to make each side's mental model visible and converge on a shared one.
- **B**: Ask each side to write a short narrative of how they expect the product to work and circulate both for asynchronous comment.
- **C**: Defer to the business stakeholders' model since they own the requirements and let the team adapt their understanding.
- **D**: Have a senior architect produce a definitive system model and require both groups to use it as the reference.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When two groups hold incompatible mental models, the way through is to externalise both — visualise them so they can be inspected, compared, and merged. (A) does this collaboratively and ends with a shared artefact. (B) keeps the models in narrative form and may compound misunderstanding rather than resolving it; comments on writing are slower and shallower than working a model in the room. (C) imposes one side's view and forfeits the engineering insights that often improve the business model. (D) elevates one expert's interpretation and bypasses the dialogue that produces genuine alignment.
