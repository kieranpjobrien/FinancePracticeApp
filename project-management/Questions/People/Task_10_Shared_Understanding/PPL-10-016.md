# Question

A PM discovers that the development team and business stakeholders have fundamentally different mental models of how the product should work. Both sides believe their understanding is correct. How should the PM bridge this gap?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the business stakeholders since they define requirements
- **B**: Side with the development team since they build the product
- **C**: Create visualisations (user journeys, prototypes, diagrams) that make mental models explicit, facilitate dialogue to surface assumptions, and collaboratively develop a shared model
- **D**: Document both models and let each side work according to theirs

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Different mental models often exist because assumptions aren't made explicit. Visualisations make abstract understanding concrete and discussable. Facilitated dialogue surfaces the "why" behind each model. Collaborative model development creates shared understanding that neither side had alone—often better than either original model.

Option A and B take sides rather than integrating perspectives.

Option D perpetuates misalignment and guarantees conflict.
