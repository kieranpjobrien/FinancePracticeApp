# Question

A PM sends detailed meeting minutes after every session, but team members still frequently ask about decisions that were already documented. What might be causing this disconnect?

## Options

- **A**: Team members are being lazy
- **B**: The meeting minutes are too detailed
- **C**: Documented understanding may differ from perceived understanding; consider more interactive methods to confirm alignment during meetings rather than relying solely on written documentation
- **D**: Stop sending meeting minutes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Documentation doesn't guarantee shared understanding. People may not read documents carefully, may interpret them differently, or may have missed context. Interactive confirmation during meetings (summarising decisions, asking for objections, restating commitments) builds real-time alignment. Documentation then reinforces rather than creates understanding.

Option A blames without analysing root cause.

Option B may or may not be the issue.

Option D removes a useful reference without solving the underlying problem.
