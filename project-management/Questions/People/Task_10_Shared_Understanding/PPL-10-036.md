# Question

A project has significant regulatory compliance requirements. Two compliance specialists are available, but the team rarely consults them and most of the team can't articulate why specific controls are needed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Have all compliance work routed exclusively through the two specialists to ensure correctness.
- **B**: Distribute the compliance reference materials to the team and require everyone to confirm they have read them.
- **C**: Wait for compliance issues to arise in delivery and use them as on-the-job learning opportunities for the team.
- **D**: Run sessions where the specialists explain compliance requirements in practical terms and embed compliance checkpoints into the team's normal workflow.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's compliance and ethics principle is non-negotiable, and the PM's job is to make sure the team understands compliance well enough to act on it day to day, with experts as guides rather than gatekeepers. (D) builds capability and embeds the controls. (A) creates a bottleneck and key-person dependency, and the team never internalises why the rules matter. (B) treats reading as understanding and ignores that the team isn't engaging with the material. (C) is risky on a regulatory project — waiting for issues means accepting violations as the teaching moment.
