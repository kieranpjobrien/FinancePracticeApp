# Question

A project involves significant regulatory compliance requirements that most team members don't fully understand. Compliance experts are available but rarely consulted. How should the PM build team understanding of compliance needs?

## Options

- **A**: Let the compliance experts handle all compliance aspects
- **B**: Send compliance documentation to the team
- **C**: Arrange knowledge-sharing sessions where compliance experts explain requirements in practical terms, create reference materials, and embed compliance checkpoints in the team's workflow
- **D**: Assume team members will learn as compliance issues arise

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Compliance understanding should be proactive, not reactive. Expert sessions translate regulatory language into practical guidance. Reference materials enable self-service. Embedded checkpoints make compliance part of normal workflow rather than afterthought. The goal is sufficient understanding to work effectively, not making everyone an expert.

Option A creates bottleneck and doesn't build team capability.

Option B assumes documents create understanding.

Option D risks costly compliance failures.
