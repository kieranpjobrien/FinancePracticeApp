# Question

A team is working on a project where different members have varying interpretations of key requirements. This has led to inconsistent implementation approaches. What should the PM do to build shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Send everyone the requirements document again
- **B**: Let each team member interpret requirements their own way
- **C**: Facilitate a requirements workshop where the team discusses interpretations, clarifies ambiguities, and establishes a shared understanding documented for future reference
- **D**: Have one person decide the correct interpretation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Shared understanding comes from dialogue, not documents. A workshop enables team members to surface different interpretations, discuss underlying assumptions, and reach consensus. Documenting the agreed understanding provides a reference point. This collaborative approach builds commitment along with understanding.

Option A assumes reading equals understanding.

Option B leads to inconsistent delivery.

Option D creates dependency on one person and may miss valuable perspectives.
