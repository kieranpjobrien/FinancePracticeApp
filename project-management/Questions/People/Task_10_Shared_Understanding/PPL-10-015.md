# Question

A development team is implementing a feature inconsistently — different developers are interpreting the same requirements in different ways and producing incompatible results. The PM is approached during a sprint by a frustrated tech lead. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Have the tech lead choose the correct interpretation and direct the rest of the team to follow it.
- **B**: Send the requirements document to the team again with the key sections highlighted and ask everyone to re-read it.
- **C**: Convene the developers to walk through the requirements together, surface the differing interpretations, and agree a single approach captured in writing.
- **D**: Allow the inconsistencies to continue this sprint and address them in the next refinement cycle.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Inconsistent implementations almost always indicate ambiguous requirements rather than careless developers; bringing the team together to surface and resolve the differing readings is the PMI-aligned response. (C) does this and produces a written agreement to anchor future work. (A) creates one person's view as gospel and bypasses the team's input — which usually misses the legitimate concerns that drove the divergence. (B) repeats the medium that already failed to produce alignment. (D) lets defects accumulate for another two weeks; PMI prefers acting on a known issue while the cost to fix is still small.
