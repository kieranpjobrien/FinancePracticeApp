# Question

After a project kickoff meeting, the PM notices that different team members have different interpretations of the project goals. Marketing thinks the goal is brand awareness; Engineering thinks it's technical innovation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept diverse interpretations as healthy
- **B**: Side with whichever interpretation the sponsor supports
- **C**: Facilitate a session to align on shared goals, documenting agreements in the project charter or vision statement
- **D**: Let each team pursue their own interpretation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Divergent understanding of goals leads to misaligned effort. The PM should bring stakeholders together to discuss, clarify, and document the shared goals. This isn't about choosing one interpretation over another but finding the integrating goal that accommodates legitimate perspectives.

Option A (accept divergence) leads to teams working at cross-purposes.

Option B (sponsor's view) may miss valuable input and doesn't build shared ownership.

Option D (separate interpretations) guarantees fragmented outcomes that don't serve anyone.
