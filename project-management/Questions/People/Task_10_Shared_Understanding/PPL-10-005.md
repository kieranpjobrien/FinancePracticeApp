# Question

Two weeks after the kickoff for a product launch project, the PM realises Marketing believes the goal is "build brand awareness in a new segment" while Engineering believes it is "demonstrate the new platform's technical capabilities." Both groups can quote the kickoff slides to support their reading. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the project sponsor which interpretation is correct and communicate that decision to the team.
- **B**: Allow each function to pursue the interpretation that fits their mandate, since both outcomes are valuable.
- **C**: Convene the key stakeholders to agree a single primary objective and update the project charter to remove the ambiguity.
- **D**: Treat both interpretations as valid working hypotheses and revisit alignment at the next major milestone.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to drive shared understanding around the project charter and vision; when the charter language allows two readings, the fix is to align stakeholders and update the document. (C) addresses both the misalignment and its source. (A) shortcuts the alignment conversation and may produce a decision the affected groups don't own. (B) lets two functions invest in different outcomes, which fragments effort and almost guarantees a hard reconciliation later. (D) defers a problem the PM has already detected; waiting for a milestone wastes weeks of misdirected work.
