# Question

A PM inherits a project mid-flight and needs to quickly build their own understanding while also ensuring they have accurate shared understanding with the team. The team claims the PM's predecessor had certain agreements, but documentation is incomplete. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Assume the team's verbal accounts are accurate
- **B**: Disregard previous agreements and start fresh
- **C**: Facilitate sessions to document current state, validate understanding with all parties, and establish clear agreements going forward—acknowledging gaps where they exist
- **D**: Spend months researching history before making any decisions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mid-project transitions require rapid but rigorous understanding building. Facilitated sessions capture current state through multiple perspectives. Validating with all parties catches misremembered or one-sided accounts. Establishing clear agreements going forward creates foundation for the PM's tenure. Acknowledging gaps prevents false certainty.

Option A may perpetuate misunderstandings.

Option B loses potentially valuable context and agreements.

Option D delays necessary leadership and decision-making.
