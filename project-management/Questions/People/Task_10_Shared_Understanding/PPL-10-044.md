# Question

A PM takes over a project mid-flight from a predecessor who left abruptly. The team refers to "agreements with the previous PM" that aren't recorded anywhere, and accounts vary depending on who the new PM speaks to. What should the new PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Treat all undocumented agreements as void and reset expectations from the current charter and plan baseline.
- **B**: Hold individual conversations with each team member to capture their version and merge the accounts afterwards.
- **C**: Run a joint session to capture current commitments, validate them across the room, and document the agreed position.
- **D**: Pause active work for two weeks while the new PM reads all artefacts and reconstructs the project history.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A handover situation needs rapid alignment that everyone is in the room for; doing it as a group lets discrepancies surface and be resolved openly rather than mediated by the PM. (C) does this. (A) is unilateral and discards potentially valid commitments the team is operating against, breaking trust on day one. (B) collects accounts in private and forces the new PM to be the sole reconciler, with no shared moment of agreement. (D) stops the project for the new PM's onboarding and damages momentum and the team's confidence in the new leader.
