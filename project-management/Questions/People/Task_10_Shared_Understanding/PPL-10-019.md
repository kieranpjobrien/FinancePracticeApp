# Question

A new team member has joined mid-project and is struggling to understand the project context. Documentation exists but they find it overwhelming and difficult to navigate. How should the PM help build their understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Give them all documentation and tell them to read it
- **B**: Wait for them to ask questions
- **C**: Pair them with an experienced team member, provide a structured onboarding that highlights key context, and create opportunities for them to ask questions and validate understanding
- **D**: Assign them simple tasks that don't require context

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. New team members need structured knowledge transfer, not just document access. Pairing provides contextual learning and relationship building. Structured onboarding highlights what matters most. Question opportunities validate understanding and surface gaps. This accelerates productive contribution while building genuine understanding.

Option A assumes documents transfer understanding.

Option B is passive and may leave gaps unfilled.

Option D limits their contribution and delays integration.
