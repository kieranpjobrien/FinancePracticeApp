# Question

The PM has noticed that the same questions about scope and decision rights keep coming up in stakeholder meetings, even though the answers are documented in the project's shared site and were covered in the kickoff. Which is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Pin the relevant pages of the project site to the homepage so they are easier to find when stakeholders look.
- **B**: Resend the kickoff deck to all stakeholders and ask for written acknowledgement that they have re-read it carefully.
- **C**: Identify which questions recur and introduce shorter contextual reminders where stakeholders actually engage.
- **D**: Restrict who can attend stakeholder meetings to those who have demonstrated understanding of the existing material.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Repeated questions show that the existing channels aren't producing lasting understanding for this audience; the PMI-aligned response is to change the medium and the timing, not to push more documentation. (C) does this. (A) is a small discoverability fix that doesn't address why the existing material isn't sticking. (B) repeats the kickoff with extra paperwork attached and assumes the issue is exposure, not format. (D) is gatekeeping that damages relationships and shrinks the engagement the PM should be widening.
