# Question

A distributed team across four countries keeps discovering misunderstandings late — usually when a deliverable is being integrated. The team uses a shared language but cultural and time-zone differences mean people often say "yes, understood" without truly being aligned. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Standardise on one country's working hours so the team can hold synchronous reviews together.
- **B**: Add interim validation checkpoints, use visual artefacts in handovers, and adopt paraphrasing protocols where the receiver restates the request.
- **C**: Co-locate the team for the start of each major phase to build the shared context that's currently missing.
- **D**: Document every decision in detail and require written acknowledgement from each affected team before work proceeds.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The defect is in how understanding is being confirmed, not in the volume of communication; PMI's distributed-team practice favours redundancy of medium and explicit comprehension checks. (B) treats the cause directly. (A) burdens three of the four locations and still doesn't change the comprehension habit. (C) is expensive, infrequent, and doesn't help with the day-to-day handovers where misunderstandings are forming. (D) adds documentation overhead and signed acknowledgements, neither of which prevents someone signing while still misunderstanding.
