# Question

A distributed team across multiple countries has chronic misunderstandings that only surface late in delivery cycles. Language and cultural differences contribute to the problem. How should the PM improve shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that everyone use English only
- **B**: Accept misunderstandings as inevitable with distributed teams
- **C**: Implement multiple validation checkpoints, use visual documentation, encourage paraphrasing to confirm understanding, and create psychological safety for asking clarifying questions
- **D**: Co-locate all team members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Distributed, cross-cultural teams require extra attention to shared understanding. Multiple checkpoints catch misunderstandings early. Visual documentation transcends language barriers. Paraphrasing ("what I understand is...") validates interpretation. Psychological safety enables questions that surface confusion before it becomes costly rework.

Option A doesn't solve understanding problems and may exclude valuable contributors.

Option B accepts preventable dysfunction.

Option D may not be practical or desirable.
