# Question

A project charter was created at initiation but team members who joined later haven't internalised its contents. The PM notices decisions being made that conflict with the charter's stated objectives. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Point out each charter violation as it occurs
- **B**: Re-circulate the charter document
- **C**: Facilitate a session to review the charter's key elements, discuss how they apply to current work, and ensure all team members understand and can apply the project's fundamental direction
- **D**: Update the charter to reflect the decisions being made

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Living documents require ongoing engagement, not just initial distribution. A facilitated session creates interactive understanding where team members discuss what the charter means for their daily decisions. This builds genuine internalisation rather than theoretical awareness.

Option A is reactive and may seem pedantic.

Option B assumes reading creates understanding.

Option D changes direction without proper governance.
