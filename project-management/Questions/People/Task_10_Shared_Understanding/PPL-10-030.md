# Question

A project charter was approved at initiation. Six months in, half the team has rotated. The PM notices recent decisions that quietly contradict the charter's stated objectives — particularly around target customer segment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Re-circulate the charter and ask new team members to confirm in writing they have read it carefully.
- **B**: Run a short working session to walk the team through the charter and discuss today's decisions.
- **C**: Update the charter through change control to reflect the direction the team has actually been taking.
- **D**: Raise the charter conflict at the next steering committee meeting and ask the sponsor to reaffirm direction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The charter is a living anchor for shared understanding, and the most effective intervention is a facilitated discussion that connects it to today's decisions — particularly with new joiners. (B) does this. (A) treats the charter as a document to be acknowledged rather than understood and won't change behaviour. (C) bakes potentially wrong decisions into the baseline before checking whether the team has drifted by accident or by design. (D) escalates before the PM has done the lower-cost work of re-aligning the team — escalation should follow attempted resolution, not precede it.
