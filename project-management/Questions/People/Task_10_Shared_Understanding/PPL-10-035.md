# Question

A PM is facilitating a workshop to align stakeholders on project priorities. Some stakeholders are quiet while others dominate. The PM suspects quiet stakeholders may have different views but aren't voicing them. How should the PM ensure genuine shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Assume silence means agreement
- **B**: Move faster to cover all agenda items
- **C**: Use techniques to surface all views—silent writing before discussion, explicitly asking quiet participants, or anonymous polling—then address differences openly
- **D**: Follow up with quiet stakeholders privately and implement their views without group discussion

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. True shared understanding requires all perspectives to surface. Silent writing first prevents anchoring on early speakers. Direct invitations ("Sarah, what's your perspective?") create space for quieter participants. Anonymous polling reveals honest views without social pressure. Differences should be discussed openly rather than ignored.

Option A may mask significant disagreement.

Option B prioritises speed over quality alignment.

Option D creates separate understandings rather than shared alignment.
