# Question

A product owner writes a user story that the three developers who pick it up interpret three different ways, each defensible from the wording. The sprint is planned and work has started. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop work on the story, facilitate a three-way conversation with the product owner to agree a single interpretation, update the story together, and resume.
- **B**: Let each developer continue with their own interpretation and converge at code review, since that will quickly reveal which reading is closest to intent.
- **C**: Ask the product owner to rewrite the story with much more detail so that every possible developer interpretation is eliminated before anyone resumes work.
- **D**: Split the story three ways so each developer's interpretation becomes its own story and can be evaluated by the product owner at review separately.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared understanding is a commitment the team makes at planning; when it fails, the correct move is to stop and rebuild it with the product owner in the room. (B) produces three implementations and wastes two, which is not empirical — it is wasteful. (C) treats ambiguity as a documentation problem, but understanding is built through conversation, not text length. (D) fragments the backlog to paper over a conversation that needs to happen.
