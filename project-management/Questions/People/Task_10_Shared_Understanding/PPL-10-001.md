# Question

During sprint planning, the development team interprets a user story as a simple text input field, while the product owner describes an auto-complete field with server-side validation. Both believe the acceptance criteria support their view. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Park the story for this sprint and add a refinement session before the next planning meeting to resolve the ambiguity.
- **B**: Pause the planning conversation and facilitate the team and product owner to talk through the story until they agree, then re-estimate.
- **C**: Accept the team's simpler interpretation so the sprint commitment can be made, and let the product owner raise concerns at the review.
- **D**: Split the story into two — a basic text field this sprint and the validation behaviour next sprint — to satisfy both interpretations.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role in sprint planning is to facilitate shared understanding between the team and product owner before commitments are made. (B) addresses the root cause — ambiguous criteria — in the moment, while everyone is in the room, and ensures the estimate reflects the agreed scope. (A) defers a problem that can be solved now and may push the story unnecessarily, slowing value delivery. (C) commits to building the wrong thing, which guarantees rework or a failed review. (D) invents structure to mask the disagreement rather than resolving it; splitting should reflect product reality, not papering over a misunderstanding.
