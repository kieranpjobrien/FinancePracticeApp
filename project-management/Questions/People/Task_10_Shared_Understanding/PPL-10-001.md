# Question

During sprint planning, the team and product owner have different interpretations of a user story's acceptance criteria. The team believes it requires a simple text field; the product owner envisioned an auto-complete feature with validation. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Go with the simpler interpretation to meet the sprint commitment
- **B**: Implement the more complex interpretation since the product owner knows the business need
- **C**: Facilitate a conversation to align understanding, clarify the acceptance criteria, and if needed, re-estimate the story
- **D**: Split the story into two separate items for the different interpretations

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The different interpretations reveal unclear acceptance criteria that need resolution before development. Facilitating immediate discussion ensures shared understanding, prevents rework, and allows accurate estimation. This is exactly the kind of alignment conversation that sprint planning should include.

Option A risks building the wrong thing, leading to rework or stakeholder dissatisfaction. Choosing the simpler path without alignment isn't a solution.

Option B assumes complexity without validation. The PM shouldn't interpret requirements - the team and PO should align on what's actually needed.

Option D creates artificial separation based on a misunderstanding rather than genuine product structure. The core issue is unclear requirements, not story size.
