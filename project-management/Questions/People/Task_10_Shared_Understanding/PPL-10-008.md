# Question

In a requirements workshop, the PM realises stakeholders are using the term "customer" to mean three different things: end consumer, paying account-holder, and internal account manager. Discussion is becoming circular. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the workshop, build a shared glossary of contested terms, and only resume once everyone agrees on the definitions.
- **B**: Note each speaker's intended meaning in the minutes and reconcile the terminology offline after the workshop.
- **C**: Pick the most common definition for the workshop's purposes and ask others to use that term going forward.
- **D**: Continue the workshop and let the requirements documentation make each meaning explicit later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared vocabulary is a foundation of shared understanding, and circular discussion is a strong signal to stop and align before more requirements get captured on shaky ground. (A) addresses the root cause in the moment. (B) stores up a reconciliation problem for later, by which time decisions made in the workshop may already be wrong. (C) imposes one stakeholder group's meaning on others without agreement and is likely to be ignored. (D) lets the confusion compound through the rest of the workshop and into the requirements artefacts themselves.
