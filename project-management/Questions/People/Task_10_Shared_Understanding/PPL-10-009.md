# Question

A project has implicit assumptions that team members aren't aware they're making. These assumptions conflict across individuals, but no one realises it because they've never been surfaced. What technique can help build shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Assume alignment and proceed
- **B**: Use assumption mapping or pre-mortem exercises to surface and validate hidden assumptions
- **C**: Wait for conflicts to emerge and address them then
- **D**: Have the PM document assumptions for the team

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Hidden assumptions are a major cause of project problems. Techniques like assumption mapping (explicitly stating what we're assuming) or pre-mortems (imagining failure and working backward) surface implicit beliefs. Once visible, assumptions can be validated, aligned, or explicitly acknowledged as risks.

Option A perpetuates hidden misalignment.

Option C allows problems to develop before addressing causes.

Option D relies on the PM's assumptions without team input.
