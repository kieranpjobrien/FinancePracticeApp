# Question

A team adopted pair programming six months ago to spread knowledge of the codebase, but the same two or three developers still own each major area, and the rest of the team is afraid to touch those areas. What should the PM (or Scrum Master) do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a deliberate pair-rotation policy so every developer pairs with every other across all areas of the codebase over a defined period.
- **B**: Replace pair programming with mandatory code reviews on every change to spread visibility more efficiently.
- **C**: Ask the strong owners of each area to write detailed documentation for the rest of the team.
- **D**: Accept that some specialisation is inevitable and assign work to whoever knows each area best.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pair programming spreads understanding only between the two people in the pair; without intentional rotation, knowledge stays within the same small clusters that already had it. (A) treats the cause. (B) drops a practice that works in favour of one that's lower-bandwidth for knowledge transfer. (C) creates documentation that ages badly and still doesn't give the rest of the team the hands-on experience that builds confidence. (D) institutionalises the silos the practice was meant to break and increases key-person risk.
