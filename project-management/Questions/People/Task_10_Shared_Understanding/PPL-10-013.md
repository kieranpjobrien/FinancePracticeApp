# Question

A complex feature has been developed based on documented requirements. During the sprint review, stakeholders say it's "not quite right" but struggle to articulate what's wrong. How should the team build better shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Ask stakeholders to provide written clarification
- **B**: Show alternative approaches, use the working software as a discussion tool, and iterate based on concrete feedback
- **C**: Tell stakeholders the feature matches their requirements
- **D**: Start over with new requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Not quite right but can't say why" often means the abstract requirements didn't capture the full intent. Use working software as a concrete discussion tool. Show alternatives: "Would this be better? Or this?" Concrete options are easier to respond to than abstract questions. Iterate based on what emerges.

Option A - they've already struggled to articulate in writing once.

Option C is defensive and doesn't solve the problem.

Option D is wasteful when iteration can refine understanding.
