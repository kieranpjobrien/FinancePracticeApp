# Question

In Scrum, what is the primary purpose of the sprint goal?

## Options

- **A**: To list every user story committed to the sprint backlog.
- **B**: To record the velocity the team plans to deliver in the sprint.
- **C**: To assign individual sprint backlog items to specific team members.
- **D**: To express a single shared objective that gives the sprint coherence and guides trade-offs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The sprint goal is a single, concise statement of what the sprint is trying to achieve; it gives the team a shared purpose and lets them negotiate scope mid-sprint while still delivering the goal. (A) describes the sprint backlog, which is the list of items, not the goal. (B) describes velocity, an empirical measurement, not the goal. (C) describes task assignment, which the team self-organises around — Scrum doesn't pre-assign work via the sprint goal.
