# Question

A PM is working with stakeholders who have very different levels of technical literacy. The same communication approach doesn't work for all. How should the PM manage this?

## Options

- **A**: Communicate at the lowest common denominator level
- **B**: Provide only one communication format and let stakeholders adapt
- **C**: Tailor communication to different audiences, layering information from executive summary to technical detail, and providing multiple formats for different needs
- **D**: Segregate stakeholders by technical level and never have them interact

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Different audiences need different communication. Layered information (executive summary for quick understanding, detailed sections for those who need more) serves multiple levels simultaneously. Multiple formats (verbal briefings for some, written documents for others, visual dashboards for executives) match different preferences and needs.

Option A loses important nuance for sophisticated audiences.

Option B doesn't meet diverse needs.

Option D prevents valuable cross-functional interaction.
