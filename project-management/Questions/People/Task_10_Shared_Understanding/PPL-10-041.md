# Question

The PM communicates with stakeholders ranging from a deeply technical CTO to a board director who has never used the product. Today the PM sends one weekly update to everyone, and feedback shows that nobody is fully satisfied. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send the most detailed possible update so every audience finds what they need within it.
- **B**: Pitch the update at the lowest technical level so all stakeholders can follow it.
- **C**: Tailor the update by audience — a layered structure with an executive summary on top, optional technical detail beneath, and the format adapted to how each group prefers to consume information.
- **D**: Stop sending the update and replace it with a self-service dashboard that each stakeholder can interpret.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's communication management plan emphasises tailoring the format and content of communication to each stakeholder group. (C) does this without abandoning the channel. (A) buries the executive audience in detail and trains them to ignore the update. (B) strips out the substance the technical audience needs and signals that the update isn't serious. (D) drops a push channel that lets the PM frame information actively in favour of a pull channel that depends on stakeholders interpreting raw data — usually a regression in shared understanding.
