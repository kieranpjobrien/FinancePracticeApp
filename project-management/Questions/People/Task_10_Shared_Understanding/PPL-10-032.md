# Question

A product owner describes a feature in a refinement session, the team builds it, and at the review the product owner says "that wasn't what I meant." The team can show they built exactly what was described. What should the team adopt to prevent this?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner write more detailed user stories with full acceptance criteria before refinement.
- **B**: Use Three Amigos sessions, BDD scenarios, or quick prototypes during refinement to validate shared understanding before the team commits.
- **C**: Have a senior developer attend every product-owner conversation to capture intent in technical terms.
- **D**: Add a mid-sprint demo so the product owner can flag concerns before final delivery.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The gap is between intent and interpretation; PMI-aligned agile practice closes that gap by validating understanding with concrete artefacts (examples, scenarios, prototypes) before development starts. (B) does this. (A) puts more weight on the same medium that didn't work; written stories rarely capture the intent the PO can't articulate. (C) inserts a translator and never builds direct shared understanding inside the wider team. (D) is helpful as a backstop but treats the symptom — it catches misunderstanding mid-sprint instead of preventing it before commitment.
