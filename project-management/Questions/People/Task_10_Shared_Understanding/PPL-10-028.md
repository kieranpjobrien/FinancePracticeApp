# Question

Developers and testers on the same Scrum team frequently disagree about whether acceptance criteria have been met. Each sprint several stories bounce back from the test column to in-progress. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Have testers and developers sit together when criteria are written, and use Given-When-Then examples to make expected behaviour concrete.
- **B**: Add a tester pre-review of every developer's work-in-progress so issues are caught before the formal test step.
- **C**: Have the product owner adjudicate disputed criteria and direct whether a story should be accepted.
- **D**: Lower the bar for acceptance to reduce the volume of bounce-backs and speed up sprint completion.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The bounce-backs are a shared-understanding problem; criteria written by one role and consumed by another almost always carry interpretation gaps that example-driven, jointly written criteria close. (A) addresses the cause and is what specification by example exists for. (B) adds an inspection step but doesn't change the underlying ambiguity, so disputes will keep happening earlier instead of later. (C) treats the product owner as a referee and does nothing to prevent the next dispute. (D) sacrifices the team's quality bar to make a process problem disappear and quietly accumulates technical debt.
