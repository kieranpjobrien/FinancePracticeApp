# Question

During execution, the PM realises the team can recite features they are building but cannot link those features to the business outcomes in the business case. Trade-off decisions are being made purely on technical grounds. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Brief the team on the business case in the next team forum and circulate the relevant sections so everyone has the same written reference document.
- **B**: Leave the team focused on technical execution and make business-outcome trade-offs as PM decisions, since the team's strength is technical delivery capability only.
- **C**: Map each major backlog item to business outcomes with the team and the product owner, and use that mapping in future refinement and trade-off conversations.
- **D**: Invite the sponsor to speak to the team at the next sprint review about why the project matters so that motivation and awareness are strengthened together.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Shared understanding of value comes from the team doing the mapping work themselves against the live backlog — that is what changes day-to-day trade-off decisions. (A) gives the team text, but trade-off behaviour does not change because the book was read. (B) keeps the team insulated from business context, which is exactly the gap. (D) inspires but does not equip; motivation without explicit linkage leaves trade-off behaviour unchanged.
