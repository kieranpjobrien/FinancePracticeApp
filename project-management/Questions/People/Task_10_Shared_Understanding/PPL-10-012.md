# Question

Stakeholders approved requirements months ago, but now claim the delivered features "aren't what they asked for." The PM has documentation showing the requirements were met as written. What went wrong and how can it be prevented?

## Options

- **A**: The stakeholders are being unreasonable
- **B**: The documented requirements didn't capture the stakeholders' true intent; future requirements should include examples, prototypes, or early feedback loops to validate understanding
- **C**: Written requirements are sufficient; stakeholders need to read more carefully
- **D**: Stop taking requirements from stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Requirements met but intent missed" indicates documentation captured words but not meaning. Prevention requires validation beyond documents: examples and scenarios, prototypes and mockups, early deliveries for feedback, and acceptance criteria reviews. Shared understanding requires confirming understanding, not just documenting words.

Option A dismisses a legitimate problem.

Option C assumes documentation alone creates understanding.

Option D eliminates necessary stakeholder input.
