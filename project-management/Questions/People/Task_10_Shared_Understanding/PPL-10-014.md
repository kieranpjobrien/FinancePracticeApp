# Question

A project team and stakeholders have different mental models of success. The team focuses on technical excellence; stakeholders focus on time-to-market. This causes friction when the team wants more time for quality. How can the PM build shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team to focus on what stakeholders want
- **B**: Tell stakeholders that technical quality is essential
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion about the relationship between quality and speed, helping both sides understand the other's perspective and find shared definitions of success
- **D**: Keep the groups separate to avoid conflict

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Both perspectives have validity. Facilitate dialogue: help the team understand why speed matters (market windows, competitive pressure). Help stakeholders understand quality impacts (technical debt slows future delivery, defects cost more than prevention). Find shared understanding: what level of quality at what speed achieves the best outcome?

Option A dismisses legitimate technical concerns.

Option B dismisses legitimate business concerns.

Option D avoids necessary alignment.
