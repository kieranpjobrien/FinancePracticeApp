# Question

What is the PRIMARY purpose of a project kickoff meeting?

## Options

- **A**: To formally start project execution and assign the first set of work packages to the team.
- **B**: To present the approved schedule and budget baseline to the steering committee for confirmation.
- **C**: To align the team and key stakeholders on the project's objectives, approach, roles, and success criteria.
- **D**: To collect outstanding requirements that were not fully captured during planning.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A kickoff meeting exists to build shared understanding before work begins — what the project is delivering, how the team will work together, who owns which decisions, and what "done" looks like. (A) confuses kickoff with the start of execution; assignments and detailed work allocation happen separately. (B) is one possible agenda item for a steering-committee briefing, not the kickoff's primary purpose, which is broader and team-facing. (D) describes requirements elicitation, which should be substantially complete before kickoff; treating kickoff as a requirements workshop indicates planning gaps.
