# Question

An agile team has new members who weren't part of the original product discovery. They're building features but don't understand the user problems being solved. Velocity is good but customer satisfaction is declining. What should the product owner do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide more detailed user stories with explicit requirements
- **B**: Hold sessions where new members experience the user's perspective through demos, interviews, or observation
- **C**: Remove the new members and hire people who understand the domain
- **D**: Accept that some team members won't understand the big picture

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Understanding user problems creates empathy and better decision-making. When developers understand "why," they make better "how" choices. Experiential learning (watching users, hearing their frustrations, seeing their context) builds deeper understanding than documentation alone.

Option A (detailed requirements) treats symptoms. More detail doesn't build understanding of underlying problems.

Option C (replace people) loses team investment and doesn't address the knowledge transfer problem.

Option D (accept lack of understanding) perpetuates declining satisfaction and missed opportunities.
