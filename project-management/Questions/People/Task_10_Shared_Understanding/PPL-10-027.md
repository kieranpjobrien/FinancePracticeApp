# Question

A team has inherited a large legacy system whose original developers all left two years ago. Documentation is sparse and the team is afraid to make changes. A regulatory deadline in nine months requires significant modifications to this system. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Plan a phased rewrite of the legacy system to remove the dependency on tribal knowledge.
- **B**: Engage former employees as paid consultants to reconstruct the missing knowledge for the team.
- **C**: Launch structured knowledge-rebuilding work — code walkthroughs, documentation sprints, reverse-engineering sessions — and capture the findings as living artefacts.
- **D**: Scope the regulatory work to the smallest possible change so the team can avoid most of the legacy code.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The team needs working understanding of the system before any major change is safe; deliberately rebuilding that understanding is the only response that addresses the cause. (C) does this and creates lasting documentation. (A) is a large undertaking that almost certainly cannot be delivered before the deadline and skips the understanding step the team needs anyway. (B) buys knowledge but doesn't transfer it into the team and creates an ongoing dependency on people who no longer work there. (D) avoids the problem by minimising scope and assumes the regulatory change can in fact be that small — likely a wishful constraint that creates new compliance risk.
