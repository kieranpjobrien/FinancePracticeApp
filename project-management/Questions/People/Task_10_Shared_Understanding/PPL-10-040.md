# Question

A sprint retrospective reveals that team members had different understandings of the sprint goal, which led to some misaligned effort. How should this be addressed for future sprints?

## Options

- **A**: Write longer sprint goals
- **B**: Assign the sprint goal to one person to define
- **C**: Collaboratively refine sprint goals with the full team, confirm understanding through discussion, and reference the goal throughout the sprint
- **D**: Eliminate sprint goals as they cause confusion

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sprint goal misalignment indicates insufficient collaborative development. Full-team participation in goal refinement surfaces different interpretations. Confirming understanding ensures alignment before work begins. Referencing the goal throughout (daily standups, when prioritising) keeps it relevant and provides ongoing validation.

Option A more words doesn't equal better understanding.

Option B loses the benefit of collaborative understanding.

Option D removes a valuable focusing mechanism.
