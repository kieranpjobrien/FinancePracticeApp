# Question

A retrospective surfaces that, in the just-finished sprint, three developers thought the goal was to "ship the new search experience" while two thought it was to "validate the new search ranking with users." Effort was misaligned. What should the team do for future sprints?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner write the sprint goal alone and circulate it for the team to confirm before sprint planning starts.
- **B**: Drop sprint goals as a practice and rely on the sprint backlog to communicate intent.
- **C**: Make the sprint goal a longer paragraph that explicitly addresses every interpretation the team might have.
- **D**: Co-write the sprint goal as a team during planning, confirm it back in plain language, and reference it in each daily Scrum.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The Scrum Guide treats the sprint goal as a team commitment; collaborative drafting and ongoing reference are what produce shared meaning, not longer wording. (D) does this. (A) gives the PO sole authorship and turns confirmation into rubber-stamping rather than alignment. (B) discards the focusing artefact that the team needed in the first place. (C) tries to fix understanding by adding words; long goals tend to be ignored, not internalised, and rarely catch every reading anyway.
