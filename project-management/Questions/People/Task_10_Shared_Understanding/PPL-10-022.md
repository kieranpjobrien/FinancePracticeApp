# Question

During planning poker, team members' estimates for a user story vary significantly—some estimate 3 points, others estimate 13 points. What does this variance indicate, and what should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Average the estimates and move on
- **B**: Use the highest estimate for safety
- **C**: The variance indicates lack of shared understanding; facilitate discussion to surface different assumptions and reach consensus
- **D**: Remove the story as too uncertain

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Large estimation variance signals different mental models of the work. This is valuable information—the discussion that follows often reveals assumptions about scope, approach, or complexity that weren't explicit. After dialogue, re-estimation usually shows convergence. Planning poker is as much about building shared understanding as producing numbers.

Option A ignores valuable information the variance provides.

Option B adds margin without addressing understanding gap.

Option D discards work that may be achievable once understood.
