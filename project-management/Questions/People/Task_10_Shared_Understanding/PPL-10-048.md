# Question

A complex integration project is stalling because teams keep discovering conflicting assumptions about how systems will exchange data. Each discovery costs days of rework. Teams are operating off separate design documents. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that every design document include an explicit assumptions section and be circulated fortnightly for cross-team comment before work proceeds.
- **B**: Have each team present its assumptions in turn at the weekly programme meeting so others can spot conflicts before they become rework.
- **C**: Run a cross-team assumption-mapping workshop, build a single integrated assumption register, validate contested items, and update designs against the agreed register.
- **D**: Appoint a solution architect to read all designs and flag conflicting assumptions to the relevant teams before the conflicts produce integration failures.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Shared understanding across interfaces requires a shared artefact — one assumption register the teams build and own together — not parallel documents with coordination layered over them. (A) keeps parallel documents and adds a review burden, but assumption conflicts are missed precisely because they sit across documents. (B) adds a slow serial forum rather than an integrated one. (D) funnels the problem through one role instead of building team-to-team alignment.
