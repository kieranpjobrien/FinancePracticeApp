# Question

A global project includes stakeholders from cultures with very different communication norms — some interpret direct questions as confrontation, while others read silence as agreement. Misunderstandings keep surfacing late, after decisions are already in motion. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt a single project communication standard and require all stakeholders to follow it for consistency.
- **B**: Route all cross-cultural communication through the PM, who can adjust tone for each audience.
- **C**: Commission cultural awareness training for the team and key stakeholders before continuing the project.
- **D**: Make communication norms explicit, use multiple confirmation methods, and create space for different styles to coexist.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Cross-cultural communication is improved by making implicit norms explicit and by adding redundancy (written summary plus verbal confirmation, for example) so misunderstandings surface early. (D) respects the diversity of styles rather than overriding it. (A) imposes one culture's norms on everyone, alienating stakeholders and rarely sticking in practice. (B) creates a single point of failure and removes the direct relationships PMI expects stakeholders to develop with each other. (C) may be valuable later but is a slow, indirect response to an active communication problem; the PM should adjust the operating model now rather than waiting for training to take effect.
