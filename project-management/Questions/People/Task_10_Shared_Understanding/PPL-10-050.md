# Question

The PM must brief a sponsor who has never worked on an agile project about why the burndown looks flat for three sprints despite real progress. The sponsor is starting to suspect the team is not performing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Explain the agile metrics the team uses, link them to business outcomes the sponsor actually cares about, and show the cumulative flow and delivered-value evidence.
- **B**: Replace the burndown with a traditional Gantt-based view so the sponsor sees progress in a format that matches his existing expectations and methodology experience.
- **C**: Send the sponsor articles on agile metrics and suggest he upskill on the methodology so future reports no longer require this type of explanatory briefing.
- **D**: Brief the sponsor that burndown is a team-internal tool and reassure him that delivery is on track based on the PM's own professional judgement and team view.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared understanding with a stakeholder is built by meeting them where they are — link their language (business outcomes) to the team's evidence (metrics), using real data. (B) changes the instrument to match the expectation rather than building understanding of the real one. (C) puts the burden on the sponsor rather than doing the communication work. (D) asks the sponsor to take the PM's word for it, which is the opposite of shared understanding.
