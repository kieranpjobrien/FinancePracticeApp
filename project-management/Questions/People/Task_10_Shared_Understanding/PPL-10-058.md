# Question

A team has a set of values printed on the wall — "respect, collaboration, transparency" — but retrospectives regularly surface behaviour that contradicts them. The team dismisses the values as "the poster." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team to translate each value into observable behaviours — what it looks like when we do it, when we don't — referenced in retrospectives.
- **B**: Remove the values from the wall since they have clearly lost meaning and are now actively undermining the team's credibility in how it operates day-to-day together.
- **C**: Have the sponsor remind the team at the next all-hands that the values are expected behaviour and that serious departures will have performance consequences for individuals.
- **D**: Run a separate values workshop to rewrite the three words into a more inspiring set so the team feels more connected to what they represent going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared understanding of values requires translating abstract words into observable team behaviours that can actually be enforced in retros — the poster is not the problem, the lack of specificity is. (B) discards an asset instead of activating it. (C) wields authority where coaching is needed. (D) rewrites words rather than specifying behaviour, so the new words will likely also be ignored.
