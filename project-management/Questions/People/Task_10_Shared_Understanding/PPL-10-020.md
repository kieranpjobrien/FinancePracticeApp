# Question

Technical and non-technical stakeholders are struggling to communicate effectively. Technical stakeholders use jargon that confuses business stakeholders, while business stakeholders' requirements seem vague to technical stakeholders. How should the PM bridge this communication gap?

## Options

- **A**: Have technical and business stakeholders communicate only through the PM
- **B**: Tell technical stakeholders to stop using jargon
- **C**: Create shared vocabulary through glossaries, use visual communication methods, facilitate dialogue sessions where both sides translate their concepts for each other
- **D**: Accept that technical and business stakeholders will never fully understand each other

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-functional communication requires intentional bridges. Shared vocabulary (agreed definitions of key terms) reduces misunderstanding. Visual methods (diagrams, mockups, flowcharts) transcend language barriers. Facilitated dialogue builds mutual understanding and empathy. Over time, both sides develop ability to translate between worlds.

Option A creates a bottleneck and doesn't build capability.

Option B addresses one symptom without building understanding.

Option D accepts unnecessary dysfunction.
