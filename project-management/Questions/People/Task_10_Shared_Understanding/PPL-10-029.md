# Question

What communication technique involves restating what someone said in your own words to confirm understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Active listening
- **B**: Paraphrasing
- **C**: Summarising
- **D**: Clarifying questions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Paraphrasing restates the speaker's message in the listener's own words, allowing the speaker to confirm the interpretation is correct. This validates understanding before proceeding. "So what you're saying is..." or "If I understand correctly..." are common paraphrasing openings.

Option A is broader—includes attention and non-verbal cues.

Option C condenses key points rather than restating.

Option D seeks additional information rather than confirming existing understanding.
