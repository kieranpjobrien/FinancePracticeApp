# Question

During backlog refinement, the team and product owner have different understandings of a complex user story. The product owner uses business terminology; the team responds with technical constraints. Neither seems to fully understand the other. What technique would BEST build shared understanding?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner write more detailed acceptance criteria
- **B**: Use collaborative modeling techniques like user story mapping, examples, or visual diagrams to create a shared mental model
- **C**: Schedule a longer meeting to allow more discussion time
- **D**: Have a business analyst translate between business and technical perspectives

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When verbal communication creates misunderstanding, visual and collaborative techniques help. User story mapping creates shared visual context, concrete examples clarify expected behavior, and diagrams illustrate technical constraints visibly. These techniques create a shared artifact that both parties can point to and refine.

Option A keeps the same medium (written words) that's already causing confusion.

Option C gives more time but may just be more of the same unproductive discussion.

Option D adds a translator but doesn't build direct shared understanding between the parties.
