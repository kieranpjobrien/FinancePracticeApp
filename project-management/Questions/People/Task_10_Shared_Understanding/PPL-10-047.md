# Question

Six months into a transformation programme, a newly joined team member asks at standup "what are we actually trying to achieve?" The PM realises no one answers confidently — each person gives a slightly different version. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send a well-written written restatement of the vision in the next all-hands email so everyone has the same reference text going forward for future onboarding.
- **B**: Facilitate a short team session to re-articulate the project vision and success measures in the team's own words, and embed the statement in regular rituals.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to attend the next team forum and restate the vision personally so the team hears the authoritative version from the accountable executive.
- **D**: Produce a glossy vision poster and distribute it to the team so the statement becomes visually consistent across all workstations and meeting rooms.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Shared understanding is built, not broadcast: when a team cannot collectively articulate the vision, they need to co-write it and live it in rituals, not receive a better version of it. (A) delivers text rather than meaning. (C) delegates the PM's facilitation role to the sponsor and still leaves the team as passive recipients. (D) treats alignment as a visual identity exercise.
