# Question

A stakeholder insists on receiving detailed weekly status reports, but the PM believes this frequency is excessive and creates significant overhead. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the reports as requested without question
- **B**: Refuse to provide weekly reports
- **C**: Understand why they need weekly information, explore whether their underlying needs could be met through alternative means, and negotiate a sustainable communication approach
- **D**: Automate the reports even if the information isn't meaningful

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Excessive reporting requests often mask underlying concerns—anxiety about progress, pressure from their stakeholders, past project failures. Understanding the "why" enables creative solutions. Perhaps a dashboard they can check anytime, brief async updates, or exception-based reporting would satisfy their needs with less overhead.

Option A may create unsustainable workload.

Option B may damage the relationship and leave needs unmet.

Option D provides volume without necessarily addressing underlying concerns.
