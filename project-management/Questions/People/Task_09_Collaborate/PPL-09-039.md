# Question

A stakeholder insists on detailed weekly status reports, which the PM believes consume disproportionate effort given the value delivered. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce the detailed reports as requested to keep the stakeholder happy and avoid conflict.
- **B**: Decline to produce weekly reports and direct the stakeholder to the standard monthly summaries.
- **C**: Generate the requested reports automatically from the project tools regardless of meaningfulness.
- **D**: Ask what underlying need the reports serve and propose a lighter alternative that meets it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's communication tailoring principle starts with the stakeholder's information need and designs the channel to fit it; weekly detailed reports are usually masking an underlying concern that a different format will serve better. (A) accepts a costly approach without testing whether it is necessary. (B) refuses without offering an alternative and damages the relationship. (C) produces volume without value and wastes effort.
