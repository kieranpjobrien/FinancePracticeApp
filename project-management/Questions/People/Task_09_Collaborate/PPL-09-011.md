# Question

A technical team and business stakeholders have difficulty communicating. Technical explanations go over stakeholders' heads; business requirements seem vague to the team. What collaboration approach should the PM implement?

## Options

- **A**: Have the PM translate all communication between groups
- **B**: Create joint sessions with visual aids, examples, and glossary of terms; encourage questions and dialogue
- **C**: Keep the groups separated and mediate through written documents
- **D**: Train stakeholders to understand technical concepts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Communication barriers require bridges, not barriers. Joint sessions with visual aids make concepts accessible. Real examples ground abstract discussions. A shared glossary reduces terminology confusion. Encouraging questions creates dialogue rather than one-way communication. This builds mutual understanding over time.

Option A makes the PM a bottleneck and doesn't build direct relationships.

Option C reduces interaction when more is needed.

Option D puts all burden on one side.
