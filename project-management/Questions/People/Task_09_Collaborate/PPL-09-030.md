# Question

The PM has noticed that a small group of stakeholders is informally aligning against the project and influencing others negatively, although none has raised a formal concern. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the coalition members individually, listen to their concerns, address what is addressable, and provide accurate information to neutralise misinformation.
- **B**: Brief the sponsor and ask the sponsor to address the group directly.
- **C**: Document the dynamic in the issues log and continue with the existing engagement plan.
- **D**: Restrict the coalition members' access to project information to limit their influence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's playbook for organised resistance is direct, individual engagement that surfaces real concerns and neutralises misinformation with facts. (B) escalates before the PM has had the conversations they own. (C) logs the issue but does nothing to address it, so the coalition continues to grow. (D) restricts information, which is unethical, often counterproductive, and tends to amplify rather than reduce the opposition.
