# Question

A PM identifies that certain stakeholders are forming an informal coalition opposed to the project. They haven't raised concerns formally but are influencing others negatively. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore informal dynamics and focus on formal governance
- **B**: Report the coalition to management
- **C**: Engage directly with coalition members to understand their concerns, address legitimate issues, provide information to counter misinformation, and work to convert opponents to at least neutral
- **D**: Isolate the coalition members from other stakeholders

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Informal opposition often signals unaddressed concerns. Engaging directly shows respect and may surface legitimate issues. Addressing concerns removes reasons for opposition. Countering misinformation with facts reduces influence. Even converting opponents to neutral significantly reduces negative impact. Ignoring informal dynamics allows opposition to grow.

Option A ignores real influence channels.

Option B may seem punitive and doesn't address underlying issues.

Option D may backfire and increase opposition activity.
