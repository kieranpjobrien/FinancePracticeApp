# Question

The PM has just learned that a critical dependency will slip by six weeks, putting the milestone at material risk. The sponsor is presenting to the board the day after tomorrow. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the board presentation is over to raise the slippage so the sponsor is not distracted during a high-visibility moment with senior leadership.
- **B**: Brief the sponsor today with the facts, impact, options and the PM's recommendation so the sponsor can present the board a current, accurate position.
- **C**: Attempt to recover the six weeks through team overtime and compression techniques first, and only escalate if recovery is unsuccessful within the window.
- **D**: Send a short written update to the sponsor and the steering committee together so everyone gets the same information at the same point in time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's reporting principle is fast, accurate and direct to the accountable decision-maker — especially before they stand in front of senior governance. (A) lets the sponsor present a board-level commitment that has already moved, which is an ethics and trust failure. (C) tries to fix a problem silently and delays the sponsor's ability to decide. (D) uses the wrong channel (broadcast) for a sponsor conversation and may put information into the board before the sponsor has had a chance to frame it.
