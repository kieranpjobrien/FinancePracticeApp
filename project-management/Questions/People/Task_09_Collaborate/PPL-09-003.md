# Question

An important stakeholder provides valuable feedback but only after the sprint review, driving rework. He cannot attend sprint reviews due to his own schedule. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop incorporating late feedback so the stakeholder learns to attend.
- **B**: Offer asynchronous alternatives — a short recorded demo, an earlier private preview, or a 15-minute check-in — that fit his schedule.
- **C**: Add a contingency buffer to each sprint to absorb his late feedback.
- **D**: Escalate the non-attendance to his manager and request he prioritise the reviews.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle says engagement channels should fit the stakeholder, not the other way around; async demos and private previews capture his input when he is actually available. (A) sacrifices genuinely valuable feedback to make a procedural point. (C) institutionalises the waste rather than fixing the timing. (D) turns a scheduling and format issue into a people issue via escalation.
