# Question

A stakeholder consistently provides feedback late in sprints that requires rework. They claim they're too busy to attend sprint reviews. Their feedback is valuable but the timing causes problems. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop incorporating their late feedback to enforce the process
- **B**: Find alternative engagement methods that fit their schedule, such as async demos, brief check-ins, or early previews
- **C**: Escalate their non-participation to their manager
- **D**: Add buffer time to sprints to accommodate late feedback

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The stakeholder has valuable input but a schedule constraint. Rather than forcing them into a format that doesn't work, find alternatives: async video demos they can watch on their time, brief targeted check-ins, or previews earlier in the sprint. The goal is their input, not their attendance at specific meetings.

Option A loses valuable feedback and potentially damages the relationship. The stakeholder isn't being difficult - they have constraints.

Option C escalates what's a scheduling/format problem, not a performance issue.

Option D accepts inefficiency rather than solving the root problem.
