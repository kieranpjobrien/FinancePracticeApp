# Question

In a sprint review, stakeholders pile in significant feedback that would meaningfully change the backlog. The Product Owner is visibly overwhelmed. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Help capture and structure the feedback, facilitate a brief prioritisation discussion, and support the PO in turning the input into ranked backlog items afterwards.
- **B**: Cut off further stakeholder feedback to give the PO breathing room.
- **C**: Tell stakeholders the feedback will be incorporated as-is in the next sprint to maintain goodwill.
- **D**: Take over the backlog refinement temporarily until the PO recovers.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role is to enable the team and the PO to do their jobs better, not to suppress input or take over. (B) loses valuable feedback and damages stakeholder engagement. (C) commits to scope without proper prioritisation, which the PO alone owns. (D) usurps the PO's product authority and is a clear role violation in Scrum.
