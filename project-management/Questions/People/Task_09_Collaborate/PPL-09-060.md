# Question

Twelve months into a programme, the PM realises the stakeholder register has not been updated since the business case and several stakeholders have since changed roles, left or arrived. Engagement is drifting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the existing register and update it at the next major milestone review when there is natural space in the schedule to do the work.
- **B**: Ask each workstream lead to refresh their own section of the register and send updates to the PM for consolidation at the next monthly forum.
- **C**: Schedule a dedicated refresh — identify and classify current stakeholders, update the engagement plan, and rebalance the PM's time against actual current salience.
- **D**: Add new stakeholders to the register as they come to the PM's attention and leave the broader structure until the register is formally reviewed at closure.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats the stakeholder register and engagement plan as living artefacts; refreshing them is a specific activity, not a byproduct of other work. (A) defers an already overdue activity behind a milestone. (B) delegates an analytic task that needs integrated judgement across workstreams. (D) is incremental patching that preserves a stale structural view of the stakeholder landscape.
