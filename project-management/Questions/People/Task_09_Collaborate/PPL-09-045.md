# Question

A project manager leading a regulatory programme has 47 identified stakeholders across legal, IT, operations, and three external regulators. The PM has roughly six hours per week available for direct stakeholder engagement. What should the PM do FIRST to allocate this time effectively?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a recurring weekly 15-minute check-in with each stakeholder to ensure consistent contact across the group.
- **B**: Assess each stakeholder's power, interest, and influence, then match engagement intensity to their position.
- **C**: Concentrate the PM's time on the three external regulators, since regulatory sign-off is the critical dependency.
- **D**: Delegate routine stakeholder contact to team leads and reserve the PM's hours for ad-hoc escalations from any source.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement process begins with analysis (typically a power/interest grid or salience model) and uses that analysis to drive a tailored engagement plan — high-power/high-interest stakeholders get heavy engagement, low-power/low-interest stakeholders get monitored. (A) treats all stakeholders identically, which is exactly what the analysis is meant to prevent and will exhaust the PM's hours on low-priority contacts. (C) prioritises by gut feel rather than analysis; regulators may indeed be high-priority, but that should fall out of the assessment alongside other key players who might otherwise be missed. (D) is a reasonable later step but skips the analysis that should determine which contacts can safely be delegated and which need PM attention.
