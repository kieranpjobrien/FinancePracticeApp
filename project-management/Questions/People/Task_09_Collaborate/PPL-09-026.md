# Question

A PM learns that a stakeholder has been expressing concerns about the project to senior leadership without raising them with the PM first. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the stakeholder about going around you
- **B**: Ignore it since stakeholders can communicate with whomever they want
- **C**: Reach out to understand their concerns, address issues directly, and work to improve the relationship so they're comfortable raising concerns with you first
- **D**: Report the behaviour to their manager

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Stakeholders going around the PM often indicates a breakdown in communication or trust. Understanding their concerns directly addresses the immediate issues. Improving the relationship creates conditions where they'll come to you first in future. This is more effective than confrontation or escalation, which may worsen the dynamic.

Option A may damage the relationship further.

Option B ignores a warning sign about stakeholder relations.

Option D escalates without attempting direct resolution.
