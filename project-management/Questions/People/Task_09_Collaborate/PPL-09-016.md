# Question

A PM discovers that two powerful stakeholders have been communicating separately with the project team, giving conflicting direction. Team members are confused about priorities. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Tell team members to follow only your direction
- **B**: Let the stakeholders sort out their differences
- **C**: Bring the conflicting direction to light, facilitate a conversation between stakeholders to align on priorities, and establish clear communication channels and decision rights
- **D**: Comply with whichever stakeholder has more authority

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Conflicting direction indicates a governance gap. Bringing it into the open enables resolution. Facilitating alignment between stakeholders creates clarity. Establishing clear communication channels (direction flows through PM or defined roles) and decision rights (who decides what) prevents future conflicts. This protects the team while respecting stakeholder authority.

Option A may put you in conflict with powerful stakeholders.

Option B leaves the team confused and may not resolve naturally.

Option D doesn't address the underlying governance issue.
