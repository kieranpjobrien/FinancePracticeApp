# Question

The PM discovers that two influential stakeholders have been giving the team conflicting direction in side conversations, leaving the team confused about priorities. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct the team to take direction only from the PM until the situation is resolved.
- **B**: Stay out of it and let the two stakeholders resolve the conflict between themselves.
- **C**: Side with whichever stakeholder holds the more senior role and cascade that direction to the team.
- **D**: Surface the conflicting direction openly, facilitate alignment between the two stakeholders, and re-establish clear decision rights and channels.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

This is a governance problem and PMI requires the PM to bring it into the open and re-establish decision rights, not to absorb or sidestep it. (A) puts the PM into open conflict with both powerful stakeholders without resolving the underlying issue. (B) leaves the team paralysed and the conflict festering. (C) introduces seniority as the tiebreaker and undermines the project's formal governance.
