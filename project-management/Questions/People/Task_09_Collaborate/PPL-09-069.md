# Question

A friendly but demanding stakeholder keeps asking the PM for "small favours" outside the project's scope — ad-hoc data pulls, informal reports, access to the team's calendar. Each is trivial alone; together they now consume five team hours a week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to absorb the requests because the stakeholder is influential, and declining each small favour risks damaging the relationship the project depends on for delivery.
- **B**: Tell the stakeholder firmly that the out-of-scope requests must stop immediately, because they are distracting the team from the agreed project objectives and milestones.
- **C**: Have the team log every favour quietly and raise the cumulative cost at the next steering committee, so that governance can instruct the stakeholder to stop them.
- **D**: Meet the stakeholder, share the aggregated cost and delivery impact, agree which asks are legitimate work, and redirect the rest to the proper channel.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's scope and stakeholder-engagement principles require the PM to make the invisible visible and renegotiate the arrangement with the stakeholder directly, preserving the relationship while restoring focus. (A) lets scope creep continue invisibly until delivery suffers. (B) is confrontational without data and damages the relationship the PM has just acknowledged matters. (C) is passive-aggressive — using governance to do a conversation the PM should have.
