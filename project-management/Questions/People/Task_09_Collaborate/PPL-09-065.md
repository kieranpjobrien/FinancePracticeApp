# Question

An internal business unit has nominated itself as the primary customer for an internal platform. Three other business units — also legitimate users — are raising concerns that their requirements are being systematically deprioritised at the expense of the first unit's asks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain the current arrangement because the nominated primary customer was agreed at business case and changing it now would be destabilising governance.
- **B**: Rebalance the backlog so each business unit gets equal attention in each increment, regardless of the original primary-customer designation at project start.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor and suggest dropping the platform entirely since serving four customers with one backlog is inherently unlikely to succeed in practice.
- **D**: Bring the four business units and the sponsor to a portfolio-level conversation to re-examine business value, reclarify prioritisation, and update the governance accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's response to multi-customer tension is to reframe it at the right level: business value across all customers, re-clarified with the accountable sponsor, not managed bilaterally by the PM. (A) invokes historical governance to ignore current legitimate concerns. (B) imposes equality where business value may not be equal. (C) proposes abandoning a valuable shared platform rather than improving its governance.
