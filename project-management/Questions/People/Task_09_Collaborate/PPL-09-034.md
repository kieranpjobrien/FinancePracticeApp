# Question

A PM is leading a regulatory compliance project where external regulators are key stakeholders. The regulators have specific requirements but limited availability for collaboration. How should the PM approach this relationship?

## Options

- **A**: Treat regulators like any other stakeholder
- **B**: Avoid regulator contact to prevent drawing attention
- **C**: Maximise value from limited interactions by preparing thoroughly, documenting requirements precisely, seeking clarity on ambiguities, and building a professional relationship that enables future consultation
- **D**: Interpret requirements independently to avoid bothering regulators

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory stakeholders require special handling—their requirements are often non-negotiable and their time is limited. Thorough preparation ensures productive use of limited interaction time. Precise documentation prevents misunderstanding. Seeking clarity prevents costly rework. Professional relationships enable informal guidance that prevents compliance failures.

Option A ignores the unique nature of regulatory relationships.

Option B risks proceeding without necessary clarity.

Option D may result in non-compliance due to misinterpretation.
