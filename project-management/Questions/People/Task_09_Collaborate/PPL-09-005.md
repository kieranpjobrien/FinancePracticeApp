# Question

A project manager needs input from a subject matter expert who is notoriously difficult to work with and has delayed other projects. How should the PM approach this collaboration?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the SME and find alternative sources of expertise
- **B**: Escalate to management to mandate the SME's cooperation
- **C**: Build rapport by understanding the SME's priorities and constraints, then work within their schedule
- **D**: Assign a junior team member to interact with the SME instead

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Difficult stakeholders often have reasons for their behaviour - heavy workload, past negative experiences, unclear expectations. Building rapport and understanding their constraints shows respect and creates a foundation for collaboration. Working within their schedule rather than demanding immediate attention improves cooperation.

Option A (avoid) may not be possible if the SME has unique knowledge, and doesn't build PM capability.

Option B (escalate first) damages the relationship before attempting to build it.

Option D (send junior person) may frustrate both the SME (feels disrespected) and the junior (lacks authority).
