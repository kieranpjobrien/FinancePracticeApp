# Question

A project involves multiple departments with historically poor relationships. Past projects failed due to lack of cooperation. What should the PM do to improve collaboration for this project?

## Options

- **A**: Keep departments isolated to prevent conflict
- **B**: Establish shared goals, create cross-functional working sessions, and celebrate joint wins
- **C**: Report any non-cooperation to senior management immediately
- **D**: Let the departments work out their differences without PM involvement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Breaking down silos requires intentional effort. Shared goals align incentives - success requires cooperation. Cross-functional sessions build relationships and mutual understanding. Celebrating joint wins reinforces collaborative behaviour. The PM actively shapes a collaborative environment rather than accepting historical dysfunction.

Option A (isolation) perpetuates the problem and creates integration challenges later.

Option C (immediate escalation) doesn't give collaboration a chance and positions the PM as adversarial.

Option D (hands-off) ignores the PM's responsibility to create conditions for project success.
