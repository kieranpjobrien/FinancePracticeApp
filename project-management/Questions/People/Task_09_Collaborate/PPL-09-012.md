# Question

A subject-matter expert who would normally be a project stakeholder has been excluded from project decisions by his own line manager. He is unhappy and is creating friction for the team. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat him as outside the engagement plan since his management has formally excluded him.
- **B**: Find informal ways to incorporate his expertise — review requests, FYI updates, optional working sessions — without challenging his manager's decision.
- **C**: Escalate the exclusion decision to senior management and request his formal inclusion.
- **D**: Ask him directly to take his concerns up with his own line manager.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI tailoring guidance allows for informal engagement channels when formal participation is constrained, and respecting the line manager's prerogative is essential. (A) creates an active opponent without trying to recover the relationship. (C) inserts the PM into another department's politics inappropriately. (D) is dismissive and does not address the friction the team is currently experiencing.
