# Question

A key stakeholder has been excluded from project decisions by their own management. They're unhappy and making difficulties for the project team. The PM has no authority over the stakeholder's management. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the stakeholder since they have no official role
- **B**: Find appropriate ways to include the stakeholder's input while respecting their management's decision
- **C**: Escalate to force their inclusion
- **D**: Tell the stakeholder to resolve it with their own management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The stakeholder may have legitimate expertise and concerns even without an official role. The PM can find informal ways to gather their input (coffee chats, FYI communications, asking for feedback) without challenging the management decision. This values their contribution while respecting organisational politics.

Option A creates an opponent who may actively obstruct.

Option C involves the PM in another department's politics inappropriately.

Option D is dismissive and doesn't solve the PM's problem.
