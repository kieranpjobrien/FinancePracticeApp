# Question

A project manager discovers that two stakeholder groups have conflicting requirements that cannot both be fully satisfied. Group A wants maximum security controls that would significantly impact usability. Group B represents end users who prioritize ease of use. Both groups report to different executives with equal authority. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to both executives for a decision on priorities
- **B**: Implement the security requirements since security is non-negotiable
- **C**: Facilitate a joint session with both groups to understand underlying needs and explore solutions that balance security and usability
- **D**: Propose a compromise that partially satisfies both requirements

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before escalating or compromising, bring the groups together to understand each other's perspectives and underlying needs. Security concerns and usability needs aren't necessarily opposites - modern security design can be user-friendly. A facilitated discussion may reveal solutions that address core needs without the perceived trade-off.

Option A escalates before attempting resolution. The PM should try to facilitate alignment first; executives shouldn't be the first resort for stakeholder conflicts.

Option B makes an assumption about priorities (security over usability) that isn't the PM's call. Both requirements may be legitimate business needs.

Option D (compromise) may be the eventual outcome, but premature compromise without exploring creative solutions may leave both groups unsatisfied when a better solution existed.
