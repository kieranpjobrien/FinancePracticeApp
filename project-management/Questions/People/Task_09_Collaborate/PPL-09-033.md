# Question

What is the primary benefit of stakeholder collaboration compared to stakeholder management?

## Options

- **A**: Collaboration is less time-consuming
- **B**: Collaboration positions stakeholders as partners who co-create solutions rather than subjects to be managed
- **C**: Collaboration requires less PM skill
- **D**: Collaboration eliminates stakeholder conflicts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Management" implies one-way control; "collaboration" implies partnership. Collaborative approaches engage stakeholders as contributors who help shape solutions, leading to better outcomes and stronger commitment. This shift in mindset—from managing stakeholders to working with them—is fundamental to modern project management.

Option A may be opposite—collaboration can require more engagement time.

Option C is incorrect—collaboration requires significant interpersonal skills.

Option D is unrealistic—collaboration helps address but doesn't eliminate conflicts.
