# Question

What is the principal difference in mindset between "stakeholder management" and "stakeholder collaboration"?

## Options

- **A**: Collaboration treats stakeholders as partners who help shape decisions, while management treats them as recipients of project outputs.
- **B**: Collaboration is faster than management because it removes formal communication.
- **C**: Collaboration removes all stakeholder conflict, while management does not.
- **D**: Collaboration is required only for agile projects, while management is required only for predictive projects.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The shift from "manage" to "collaborate" reflects PMI's modern stance that stakeholders are partners who co-create value, not passive subjects of communication. (B) is wrong; collaboration usually requires more engagement, not less. (C) overstates the result; collaboration helps surface and resolve conflict but does not eliminate it. (D) is a false dichotomy; both predictive and agile work depend on collaboration.
