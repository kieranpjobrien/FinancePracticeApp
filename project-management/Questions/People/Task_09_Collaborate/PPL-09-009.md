# Question

A project requires collaboration with an external partner company. Both organisations have different working cultures - your company uses agile with daily standups; theirs uses waterfall with weekly status meetings. How should the PM facilitate effective collaboration?

## Options

- **A**: Force the partner to adopt your agile practices
- **B**: Abandon agile and adopt their waterfall approach
- **C**: Establish agreed interface points that respect both cultures - perhaps weekly sync meetings while each team works internally in their preferred method
- **D**: Minimise interaction and work independently as much as possible

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-organisational collaboration requires respecting different working cultures. Neither company can force the other to change. Find interface points that work for both - perhaps weekly coordination meetings, agreed handoff protocols, and clear communication channels. Each team can work internally as they prefer while meeting shared commitments.

Option A creates friction by imposing your culture.

Option B abandons practices that work for your team.

Option D reduces necessary coordination and creates integration problems.
