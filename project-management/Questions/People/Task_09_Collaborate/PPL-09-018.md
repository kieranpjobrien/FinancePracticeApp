# Question

During stakeholder analysis, the PM identifies a stakeholder who is highly influential but currently unaware of the project. The project may significantly impact their department. What engagement level should the PM target?

## Options

- **A**: Keep them unaware to avoid complications
- **B**: Move them to "informed" level only
- **C**: Proactively engage them to raise awareness and ideally move them to "supportive" before they discover the project through other channels
- **D**: Wait until they notice the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. High-influence stakeholders who are unaware represent a risk—they may become resistant if they discover the project unexpectedly. Proactive engagement allows the PM to shape their understanding, address concerns early, and potentially gain an ally. Discovering a significant project impacting their area through other channels often creates negative reactions.

Option A creates risk of negative surprise.

Option B may not be sufficient engagement for high influence.

Option D allows others to shape their perception first.
