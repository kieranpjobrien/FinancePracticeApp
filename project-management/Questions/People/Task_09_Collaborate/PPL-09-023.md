# Question

The project's steering committee meets only monthly, and the PM is losing time waiting for routine decisions between meetings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make routine decisions without the committee and report them at the next meeting.
- **B**: Accept the monthly cadence as the cost of having a governance body.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor and request that the committee be replaced with a more responsive structure.
- **D**: Propose a tiered decision framework — async approvals for routine items, delegated authority for defined categories, on-demand sessions for urgent items — for the committee's agreement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring guidance lets governance be redesigned for effectiveness as long as the committee owns and approves the redesign. (A) bypasses the committee unilaterally and breaches governance. (B) accepts an avoidable delay and lets the project drift. (C) is disproportionate and damages the relationship with a body the project still needs.
