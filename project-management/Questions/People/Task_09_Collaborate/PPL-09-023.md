# Question

A PM is struggling to get timely decisions from a stakeholder committee that only meets monthly. Waiting for decisions is delaying the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make decisions without the committee
- **B**: Accept monthly delays as necessary governance
- **C**: Propose alternative decision mechanisms such as email votes for routine matters, delegation for certain decision types, or ad-hoc sessions for urgent items
- **D**: Escalate to dissolve the committee

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Governance structures should enable rather than impede progress. Most committees recognise that not every decision requires full-committee discussion. Proposing tiered decision rights (routine vs. significant), async approval mechanisms, or emergency provisions addresses the legitimate need for oversight while improving responsiveness.

Option A bypasses legitimate governance.

Option B accepts unnecessary delay.

Option D is extreme and may not address the underlying issue.
