# Question

At a steering committee meeting, two members disagree sharply on a design direction, with the sponsor absent. The committee is unwilling to decide without him and the next scheduled meeting is in six weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the committee to defer the decision formally to the next scheduled sitting and replan any dependent work backwards from that new target date accordingly.
- **B**: Make the call as PM, document the rationale, and ask the committee to ratify it at the next sitting since PM decisions in sponsor absence are common.
- **C**: Brief the sponsor separately on the disagreement and recommendation, secure an out-of-cycle written decision, and notify the committee of the outcome promptly in writing.
- **D**: Ask the two disagreeing members to work it out bilaterally and circulate whichever consensus position they eventually reach to the broader committee for awareness.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to keep decisions flowing at the right authority level — here, that means getting a sponsor decision via an appropriate out-of-cycle mechanism, not either inventing authority or waiting six weeks. (A) is six weeks of idle dependent work. (B) exceeds the PM's authority on a steering-level decision and risks an after-the-fact reversal. (D) delegates a governance decision to two members, which bypasses the committee itself.
