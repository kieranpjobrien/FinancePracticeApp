# Question

A required stakeholder is genuinely overloaded; previous attempts to schedule his input have failed and decisions are stuck. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adapt the engagement to his constraints — short focused sessions, async review of a tight document, or naming a deputy he trusts to act on his behalf.
- **B**: Proceed without his input and document the assumptions used in his absence.
- **C**: Escalate to his line manager to demand a slot on his calendar.
- **D**: Hold the decisions open until he becomes available, regardless of how long.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tailoring engagement to the stakeholder's available bandwidth is a core PMI principle, and a trusted deputy is a recognised pattern for unblocking decisions. (B) drives forward on assumptions about a person whose input is needed and creates rework risk. (C) escalates a workload issue and damages a relationship the PM will need throughout. (D) accepts indefinite delay as a strategy and lets the project slip.
