# Question

A PM is working with stakeholders across multiple time zones. Scheduling meetings that work for everyone is nearly impossible. How should the PM facilitate collaboration?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule meetings convenient for the most important stakeholders only
- **B**: Require everyone to attend regardless of time zone inconvenience
- **C**: Use a mix of synchronous and asynchronous collaboration, rotate meeting times to share inconvenience, and ensure decisions and discussions are documented for those who can't attend
- **D**: Cancel stakeholder meetings and rely only on email

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Distributed stakeholder collaboration requires intentional practices. Async tools (shared documents, recorded updates, discussion boards) enable participation regardless of time zone. Rotating meeting times shares the burden equitably. Thorough documentation ensures everyone can contribute and stay informed. Some critical discussions may still need synchronous time, but balance is key.

Option A alienates important stakeholders in inconvenient time zones.

Option B creates unsustainable burden on some stakeholders.

Option D loses the value of real-time dialogue entirely.
