# Question

A project stakeholder has been very helpful throughout the project but is now making requests that would compromise the project's quality standards. They seem to be under pressure from their own management. How should the PM maintain collaboration while protecting quality?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to all requests to maintain the good relationship
- **B**: Refuse all requests to protect quality
- **C**: Acknowledge their pressure, explore the underlying need, and find ways to help that don't compromise quality
- **D**: Escalate their behaviour as a project risk

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The stakeholder isn't suddenly being difficult - they're under pressure. Acknowledge this ("I can see you're in a tough spot"). Understand the underlying need (what are they trying to achieve?). Explore alternatives that might address their need without compromising quality. This maintains collaboration while protecting project integrity.

Option A compromises quality for relationship.

Option B protects quality but may damage a valuable relationship.

Option D treats an ally as a threat without understanding their situation.
