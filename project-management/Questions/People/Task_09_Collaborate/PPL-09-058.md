# Question

A fintech PM discovers a defect in a beta release that may breach a regulatory notification threshold but is not yet certain. Legal is still analysing. The PM's ops lead wants to wait for the legal view before telling anyone. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Brief the regulatory-affairs team and sponsor immediately with the facts known and the pending legal view, so they can manage notification decisions proactively.
- **B**: Wait for the legal determination and, if it confirms a breach, brief the regulatory-affairs team and sponsor at that point with a single consolidated position.
- **C**: Contact the regulator directly and pre-notify on a no-names basis to preserve the relationship in case a formal notification later becomes required.
- **D**: Instruct the team to remediate the defect quickly and reassess whether notification is required once the remediation has been completed and confirmed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's reporting and ethics principles align: the accountable internal parties need the facts as they emerge so that regulatory decisions are made by the right people at the right time. (B) delays internal awareness of a potential regulatory breach, which narrows the organisation's response options. (C) bypasses the organisation's own governance by contacting an external regulator unilaterally. (D) prioritises remediation over disclosure, which can itself be a reportable failure depending on jurisdiction.
