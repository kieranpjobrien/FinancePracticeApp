# Question

An agile project's Product Owner is overwhelmed by stakeholder requests coming from multiple channels. Some stakeholders bypass the PO to request features directly from the development team. How should this be addressed?

## Options

- **A**: Let stakeholders communicate however they prefer
- **B**: Ban all stakeholder communication with the development team
- **C**: Reinforce the PO role as the single point of backlog authority, educate stakeholders on proper channels, and support the PO in managing stakeholder expectations
- **D**: Distribute PO responsibilities among multiple people

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The Product Owner role exists to manage stakeholder input effectively. Bypassing the PO creates confusion and undermines prioritisation. Educating stakeholders on the proper channel (through the PO) maintains order while ensuring input is captured. Supporting the PO includes helping them manage volume and expectations.

Option A creates chaos and undermines the PO role.

Option B may be too restrictive and limit valuable informal communication.

Option D fragments accountability for backlog prioritisation.
