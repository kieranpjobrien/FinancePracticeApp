# Question

A divisional executive has been bypassing the PM and approaching team members directly for updates, then acting on partial information. The team finds it disruptive and the messaging is becoming inconsistent. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct the team to decline all direct requests from the executive and redirect every approach back through the PM for a consistent channel.
- **B**: Meet the executive, understand what information need is driving the behaviour, and agree a reporting cadence and channel that genuinely serves it.
- **C**: Escalate the behaviour to the sponsor as an interference issue and request that the executive be formally told to respect the governance structure.
- **D**: Copy the executive into every internal update email so she has full visibility and no longer needs to approach individual team members for information.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats bypass behaviour as a symptom of an unmet information need; the PM's job is to diagnose and meet the need rather than police the channel. (A) rebuilds a wall the executive has already shown she'll go around, and puts team members in an awkward position. (C) escalates a peer-level issue before a direct conversation has been attempted. (D) floods the executive with volume rather than the specific information she actually needs.
