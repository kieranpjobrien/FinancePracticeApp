# Question

A project affects multiple business units, each with different and sometimes competing interests. How should the PM facilitate collaboration among these diverse stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Favour the business unit with the most budget contribution
- **B**: Let business units work out conflicts among themselves
- **C**: Identify shared goals that benefit all units, create forums for cross-unit dialogue, facilitate trade-off discussions with transparency, and escalate to sponsors when alignment isn't achievable
- **D**: Separate work streams so business units don't need to interact

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Multi-stakeholder collaboration requires finding common ground. Shared goals (successful project benefits all) create alignment. Forums for dialogue build relationships and understanding. Transparent trade-off discussions enable informed compromise. Escalation to sponsors is appropriate when PM-level facilitation isn't sufficient to resolve competing interests.

Option A damages trust with other business units.

Option B may result in conflict or stalemate.

Option D may not be feasible and loses integration benefits.
