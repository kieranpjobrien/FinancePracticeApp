# Question

What is a salience model used for in stakeholder analysis?

## Options

- **A**: Measuring stakeholder satisfaction
- **B**: Classifying stakeholders based on power, urgency, and legitimacy
- **C**: Tracking stakeholder communications
- **D**: Calculating stakeholder influence scores

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The salience model classifies stakeholders based on three attributes: power (ability to influence), urgency (need for immediate attention), and legitimacy (appropriate involvement). Stakeholders with all three attributes are most salient and require highest attention. This helps prioritise engagement efforts across many stakeholders.

Option A relates to satisfaction measurement, not classification.

Option C relates to communications tracking.

Option D is related but the salience model uses three specific criteria.
