# Question

A stakeholder who was previously supportive has become resistant after a project decision went against their preferences. Their continued support is important. How should the PM re-engage them?

## Options

- **A**: Reverse the decision to regain their support
- **B**: Ignore their resistance and proceed
- **C**: Acknowledge their disappointment, explain the rationale for the decision, explore how to address their underlying concerns within current constraints, and demonstrate ongoing value of their input
- **D**: Reduce their involvement since they're now resistant

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Resistance often stems from feeling unheard or undervalued. Acknowledging disappointment validates their feelings. Explaining rationale (even if they disagree) shows respect. Finding ways to address underlying concerns—even partially—demonstrates good faith. Reinforcing the value of their input rebuilds engagement. Most stakeholders can move past disagreements with proper handling.

Option A undermines decision integrity and sets problematic precedent.

Option B risks continued resistance and potential opposition.

Option D loses a potentially valuable ally.
