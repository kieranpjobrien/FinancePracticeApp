# Question

A previously supportive stakeholder has turned resistant after a project decision went against his preferred option. His ongoing support matters to delivery. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reopen the decision through change control to restore his support.
- **B**: Reduce his exposure to project communications so the resistance has less surface area.
- **C**: Acknowledge his disappointment, walk through the rationale for the decision, and explore how to address his underlying concerns within the current constraints.
- **D**: Brief the sponsor on his shift and ask the sponsor to speak to him.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI handles stakeholder resistance by restoring the relationship through acknowledgement and explanation, then surfacing what can still be done within constraints. (A) reverses a properly made decision purely to manage feelings and undermines governance. (B) hides from the resistance instead of addressing it. (D) escalates before the PM has done the conversation themselves.
