# Question

A PM is working on a project that will result in job losses in certain departments. Employees in those departments are project stakeholders whose cooperation is needed for successful implementation. How should the PM balance transparency with maintaining cooperation?

## Options

- **A**: Hide the job impact until after the project completes
- **B**: Be completely transparent immediately regardless of consequences
- **C**: Work with HR and leadership to develop an appropriate communication plan that balances transparency with sensitivity, provides support for affected employees, and maintains necessary cooperation
- **D**: Refuse to work on projects with negative workforce impacts

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Workforce impact requires careful handling that balances multiple concerns. Communication timing and approach should be coordinated with HR and leadership—they may have legal and ethical obligations about disclosure. Support for affected employees (outplacement, retraining) should be part of the approach. Honest communication, even about difficult topics, typically generates better cooperation than deception.

Option A damages trust when discovered and may violate policies.

Option B without coordination may create legal/HR issues.

Option D is unrealistic in many organisational contexts.
