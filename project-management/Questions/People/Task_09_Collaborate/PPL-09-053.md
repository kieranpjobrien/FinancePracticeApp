# Question

A product team is designing a new mobile app for field technicians. The sponsor insists on specific features based on his prior experience in the role; the technicians' team leaders say those features miss how work is actually done now. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Build the sponsor's feature set first, since he is the accountable decision-maker and funder, then iterate on the basis of technician feedback after release.
- **B**: Accept the team leaders' views over the sponsor's and build what the current field force says they need, since they are the direct end users today.
- **C**: Split the backlog between the two viewpoints and build features from each in alternating sprints, so that neither group feels unheard during development.
- **D**: Run structured user research — shadowing, interviews, prototypes with technicians — present findings alongside the sponsor's hypotheses, and let evidence drive the backlog.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement and product principles converge on user research when intent and reality disagree; evidence, not seniority or proximity, should shape the backlog. (A) builds the wrong product efficiently and defers learning to after money is spent. (B) swaps one unverified set of opinions for another. (C) splits development time between two hypotheses rather than testing which is correct.
