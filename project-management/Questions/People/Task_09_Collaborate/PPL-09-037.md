# Question

A PM realises that stakeholder expectations for the project have drifted significantly from what will be delivered. The gap wasn't apparent initially but has grown over time. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver as planned and let stakeholders adjust their expectations
- **B**: Change the deliverables to match expectations regardless of feasibility
- **C**: Transparently surface the expectation gap, facilitate discussion to understand priorities, and work toward realistic alignment on what will be delivered and when
- **D**: Blame the original requirements gathering for the gap

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Expectation gaps addressed early cause less damage than surprises at delivery. Transparent surfacing may be uncomfortable but enables course correction. Understanding priorities helps determine whether to adjust delivery or expectations. Working toward alignment ensures stakeholders know what to expect and can plan accordingly.

Option A creates significant dissatisfaction at delivery.

Option B may commit to infeasible outcomes.

Option D deflects responsibility without solving the problem.
