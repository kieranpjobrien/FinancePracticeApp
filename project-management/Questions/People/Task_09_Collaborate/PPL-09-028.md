# Question

A vendor is a key stakeholder who has been difficult to collaborate with—slow responses, missed deadlines, and defensive when issues are raised. The contract requires their involvement. How should the PM improve collaboration?

## Options

- **A**: Document all failures for contract disputes
- **B**: Reduce interaction to the minimum contractually required
- **C**: Attempt to understand the root cause of difficulties, establish clearer expectations and communication channels, escalate to vendor relationship owners if needed, and focus on shared success
- **D**: Terminate the contract and find another vendor

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Poor vendor collaboration often has addressable causes—unclear expectations, wrong contacts, internal vendor issues. Understanding root causes enables targeted improvement. Clearer communication and expectations help both parties. Escalating to relationship owners (account managers, partnership leads) engages people with incentive to improve. Focusing on shared success reframes the dynamic.

Option A prepares for conflict rather than improvement.

Option B doesn't improve the situation.

Option D may be costly and may not be feasible mid-project.
