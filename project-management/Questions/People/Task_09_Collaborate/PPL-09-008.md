# Question

A stakeholder keeps submitting changes that the team consider low value. The team is increasingly frustrated by the disruption. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Route all of this stakeholder's requests through the change control board with a priority score attached.
- **B**: Bring the stakeholder and the team together to share the stakeholder's underlying goals and the team's delivery constraints.
- **C**: Ask the team to silently absorb the requests to protect the stakeholder relationship.
- **D**: Start declining the stakeholder's requests by default to reset expectations.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root cause is usually misalignment — the stakeholder has unmet needs that aren't visible to the team, and the team's constraints aren't visible to the stakeholder. A joint conversation addresses both sides and produces better, fewer requests. (A) routes the symptom through a governance body without fixing the alignment problem. (C) protects the relationship at the cost of team trust and morale. (D) picks a unilateral position and damages the stakeholder relationship.
