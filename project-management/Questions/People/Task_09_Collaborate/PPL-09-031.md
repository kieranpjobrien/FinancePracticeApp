# Question

A stakeholder consistently dominates discussions, preventing others from contributing. This stakeholder has valuable input but their behaviour is affecting collaboration quality. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let them dominate since their input is valuable
- **B**: Exclude them from meetings
- **C**: Use facilitation techniques to manage participation, have a private conversation about the impact of their behaviour, and establish meeting norms that ensure all voices are heard
- **D**: Publicly call out the behaviour in meetings

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Balancing participation requires both structural solutions (facilitation techniques like round-robin, time-boxing, written input first) and individual conversation. A private discussion allows addressing the behaviour without embarrassment. Meeting norms create shared expectations. The goal is capturing their value while enabling others to contribute.

Option A loses input from other stakeholders.

Option B loses valuable contributions.

Option D may embarrass and alienate them.
