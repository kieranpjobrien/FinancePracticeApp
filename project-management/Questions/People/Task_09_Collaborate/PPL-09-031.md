# Question

One stakeholder consistently dominates discussions, drowning out other contributors. His input is genuinely valuable but the dynamic is degrading the team's collaboration. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop inviting him to working sessions and gather his input separately by email.
- **B**: Have a private conversation with the stakeholder about the impact of his behaviour and apply structured facilitation techniques (round-robin, time-boxing, written input first) in meetings.
- **C**: Address the behaviour publicly in the next meeting so other stakeholders see it being managed.
- **D**: Allow the dynamic to continue because his contributions add real project value.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's facilitation guidance pairs a private direct conversation with structural meeting changes that protect every voice — addressing both the relationship and the room dynamic. (A) excludes a valuable contributor and signals avoidance rather than facilitation. (C) embarrasses him publicly and damages the relationship. (D) accepts the cost to other stakeholders' input and undermines collaborative quality.
