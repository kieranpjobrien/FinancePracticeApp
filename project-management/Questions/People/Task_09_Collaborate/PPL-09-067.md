# Question

Three months from go-live, the operations team that will run the new service says they have been "kept out of design" and are now unwilling to accept the handover. The design phase has been product-team led throughout. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invoke the governance that made product-team design the approved approach and require operations to accept handover on the agreed schedule as planned.
- **B**: Pause go-live planning, run a joint review of operability requirements with operations, and adjust the product and handover plan where the gaps are material.
- **C**: Offer operations additional post go-live support hours from the product team in exchange for acceptance of the handover on the planned go-live date.
- **D**: Escalate operations' refusal to the sponsor and request that operations be directed to accept the service, since it has been built to the approved specification.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's operability and stakeholder-engagement principles align: operations is a legitimate co-owner of the outcome, and genuine review plus real adjustment is the only path to a sustainable handover. (A) uses governance as a weapon rather than a decision-making aid. (C) buys acceptance with labour rather than fixing the operability gap, and the service will still fail in production. (D) escalates a legitimate concern out of existence rather than responding to it.
