# Question

A key stakeholder has been disengaged from the project, rarely attending meetings or responding to communications. Their input is critical for an upcoming phase. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed without their input
- **B**: Escalate their lack of engagement to their manager
- **C**: Reach out directly to understand their barriers to engagement, adapt communication approaches to fit their preferences, and emphasise the value of their input to project success
- **D**: Remove them from the stakeholder register

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Disengagement often has underlying causes—competing priorities, unclear value of participation, communication method mismatch, or feeling unheard. Direct outreach shows you value their involvement. Understanding barriers enables targeted solutions. Adapting to their preferences (brief updates vs. long meetings, async vs. sync) may improve engagement significantly.

Option A risks decisions without critical input.

Option B escalates before understanding the situation.

Option D loses an important stakeholder.
