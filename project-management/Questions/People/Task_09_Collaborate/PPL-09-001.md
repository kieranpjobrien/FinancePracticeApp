# Question

A key stakeholder has stopped attending scheduled project reviews and no longer responds to status updates. Their input is critical for upcoming decisions. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the stakeholder's non-participation to their manager
- **B**: Proceed without their input since they've chosen not to participate
- **C**: Reach out personally to understand their situation and explore what would make engagement easier or more valuable
- **D**: Send more frequent and detailed status updates to ensure they're informed

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The change in behavior suggests something has changed - perhaps their priorities, their perception of project value, or their availability. Understanding the root cause allows for an appropriate response. Direct, personal outreach shows the PM values their input and creates opportunity for honest dialogue about barriers to participation.

Option A escalates without understanding the situation. The stakeholder may have legitimate reasons, and escalation without conversation may damage the relationship.

Option B accepts disengagement without investigating. Their input is critical, so proceeding without it creates project risk.

Option D assumes the problem is communication volume or detail. If they're overwhelmed or disengaged for other reasons, more updates may worsen the situation.
