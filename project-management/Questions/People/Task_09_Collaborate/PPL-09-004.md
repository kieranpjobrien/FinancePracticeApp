# Question

A key stakeholder consistently changes their requirements after work has begun, causing rework. The changes are often legitimate refinements as they see the product evolving. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement a strict change freeze after requirements are approved
- **B**: Increase stakeholder involvement earlier in development through prototypes, mockups, or early demos to surface refinements before work is done
- **C**: Accept rework as a cost of keeping stakeholders happy
- **D**: Require the stakeholder to pay for changes with schedule or budget adjustments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The stakeholder's changes are legitimate learning as they see the product. Rather than restricting their input, help them learn earlier through prototypes, mockups, or incremental delivery. This surfaces refinements when changes are cheap (before work is done) rather than expensive (after).

Option A fights against legitimate learning, which may result in a product that technically meets spec but doesn't meet needs.

Option C accepts waste without addressing root cause.

Option D penalizes the stakeholder for normal discovery, which may damage the relationship.
