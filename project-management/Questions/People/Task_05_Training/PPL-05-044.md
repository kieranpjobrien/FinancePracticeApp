# Question

A key team member has confirmed they will be on parental leave for four months starting in eight weeks. They own several specialised areas. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Plan a structured knowledge transfer that identifies critical knowledge, documents key processes, includes shadowing for backups, and runs trial handovers before they leave.
- **B**: Ask them to record video walkthroughs of the main systems for the team to refer to once they are gone.
- **C**: Wait until closer to their leave date so the documentation is as current as possible.
- **D**: Hire a contractor to cover the role during the leave so no internal handover is needed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's resource and risk management practices push for planned, multi-modal knowledge transfer well before the person becomes unavailable. (A) addresses the right scope and verifies transfer through trial handovers. (B) is part of the answer but documentation alone rarely produces capability. (C) sacrifices runway and turns a managed transition into a rushed one. (D) is a contingency that does not build internal capability and may be hard to procure quickly.
