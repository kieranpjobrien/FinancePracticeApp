# Question

A key team member is going on extended leave. You need to ensure their responsibilities can be covered. What training approach should you implement before they leave?

## Options

- **A**: Wait and figure it out when they're gone
- **B**: Have them create documentation only
- **C**: Plan formal knowledge transfer: identify critical knowledge, document key processes, conduct shadowing and hands-on training for backups, and do trial runs before they leave
- **D**: Assume others already know enough to cover

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Planned knowledge transfer ensures continuity. Identify what's critical: prioritise the most important knowledge to transfer. Document processes: create reference materials. Conduct active training: shadowing, hands-on practice under their guidance. Trial runs: have backups perform responsibilities while the person is still available to support. This ensures genuine capability transfer.

Option A creates crisis when they leave without preparation.

Option B creates references but doesn't ensure people can actually do the work.

Option D risks gaps when assumptions prove incorrect.
