# Question

A new ERP system goes live in three weeks. End-user training is scheduled for the week before go-live. During UAT this week, users struggle with basic transactions and several express anxiety about the change. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the schedule and rely on the planned training week to close the gap before go-live.
- **B**: Move training earlier, add hands-on labs, and pair end-users with super-users in the run-up to go-live.
- **C**: Push go-live back by a month and run the same training plan with more time.
- **D**: Cut features from the initial release so less training is required.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

UAT has shown that one week of training before go-live will not be enough, and the right move is to redesign the training experience now while there is still time, which is what (B) does. (A) ignores a clear warning signal. (C) buys time but repeats an approach that has already failed. (D) reduces the project's value to avoid solving the training problem.
