# Question

A project is implementing a new system that will significantly change how end users perform their work. User training is scheduled for the week before go-live. During UAT, users struggle with basic functions and express anxiety about the change. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the current plan since formal training will address the issues
- **B**: Delay go-live until users are more comfortable with the system
- **C**: Revise the training approach to begin earlier with progressive learning, provide hands-on practice opportunities, and address change resistance alongside technical skills
- **D**: Simplify the system to reduce the training burden

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The UAT struggles reveal that one week of training before go-live is insufficient for this level of change. Users need more time with progressive exposure, and the anxiety suggests change management needs alongside technical training. Revising the approach addresses both the skill gap and the emotional resistance.

Option A ignores clear warning signs. The same approach that caused problems in UAT won't magically work in formal training. The plan needs adjustment.

Option B may be necessary but doesn't address the underlying training approach problem. A delay without improving the training plan just postpones the same issues.

Option D undermines project value to avoid training challenges. The system was designed to meet business needs - reducing functionality reduces benefits. Better training is usually preferable.
