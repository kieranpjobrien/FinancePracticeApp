# Question

A project requires the team to learn a technology that will be obsolete in two years. The training is expensive. Management questions the ROI of training for soon-to-be-obsolete technology. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree training isn't worth it and proceed without proper preparation
- **B**: Present the cost of inadequate training (defects, delays, support issues) versus training cost, and propose targeted training focused only on what's needed for this project
- **C**: Insist on full comprehensive training regardless of context
- **D**: Delay the project until a newer technology can be used

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Make a business case. What's the cost of poor performance without training? What level of proficiency is actually needed? Propose targeted training that addresses project needs without over-investing in obsolete technology. This shows financial thinking while advocating for necessary preparation.

Option A accepts avoidable project risk.

Option C may over-invest given the limited technology lifespan.

Option D may not be feasible given business constraints.
