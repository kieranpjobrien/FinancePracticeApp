# Question

A project requires the team to learn a vendor framework that the vendor has confirmed will be deprecated within two years. Management has questioned the ROI of training on a soon-to-be-obsolete technology. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Propose training scoped to project needs with a cost comparison against rework and defect risk.
- **B**: Drop formal training and rely on online articles and the vendor's documentation for the team.
- **C**: Push to switch the project onto the vendor's replacement framework so training value is preserved longer.
- **D**: Insist on a full vendor certification course so the team has a recognised credential afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM's job is to make a defensible business case that scopes training to project need, which is what (A) does. (B) treats unstructured self-study as equivalent to targeted training and accepts avoidable risk. (C) is an architecture-level change that may not be feasible and goes well beyond the training question. (D) over-invests in a credential when the framework is being deprecated and is harder to defend than focused training.
