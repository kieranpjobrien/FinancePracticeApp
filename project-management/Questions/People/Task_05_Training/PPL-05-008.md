# Question

When should training needs for a project be identified?

## Options

- **A**: Only after problems occur
- **B**: During planning, when resource requirements and skill gaps are assessed
- **C**: After the project is complete
- **D**: Training is not the project manager's responsibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Training needs should be identified during planning when the PM assesses what skills are needed versus what's available. This allows time to arrange training, budget for it, and schedule it appropriately. Waiting until problems occur is reactive and causes delays.

Option A (after problems) is too late - problems could have been prevented.

Option C (after project) is for lessons learned, not for this project's training needs.

Option D incorrectly absolves the PM - ensuring the team can do the work is part of resource management.
