# Question

You have identified ten distinct training needs across your team but the available training budget will only fund about three. How should the project manager decide what to fund?

## Options

- **A**: Fund training for the most senior people first because they will get the highest application leverage.
- **B**: Allocate the budget on a first-come, first-served basis from team members' development plans.
- **C**: Split the budget evenly across all ten needs to be fair, accepting that no need is fully addressed.
- **D**: Prioritise based on impact on project success, severity of the skill gap, and potential to multiply through internal sharing.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects scarce resources to be allocated by impact and risk rather than seniority, fairness, or arrival order. (D) frames the decision around delivery and capability multiplication. (A) confuses seniority with leverage and ignores where the actual gaps are. (B) optimises for queue position, not value. (C) treats fairness as the goal but produces shallow outcomes everywhere.
