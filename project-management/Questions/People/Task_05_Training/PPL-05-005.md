# Question

A project manager identifies that end users will need significant training on the new system. The functional manager of those users says their department has no budget for training and their staff is too busy for time away from work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with minimal training and address issues as they arise
- **B**: Include training costs and time in the project budget and schedule, since training is essential for benefits realization
- **C**: Make the system simpler so less training is needed
- **D**: Create self-service training materials to minimize time and cost impact

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. If training is essential for the project to deliver value, it's a project responsibility. The PM should plan for training as part of the project scope, budget, and schedule - not treat it as an afterthought the functional area must absorb. Projects succeed through adoption, not just technical completion.

Option A accepts foreseeable failure - users who can't use the system don't generate benefits.

Option C sacrifices functionality to avoid training, which may reduce project value.

Option D might be part of a training strategy but shouldn't be the only approach if the training need is significant.
