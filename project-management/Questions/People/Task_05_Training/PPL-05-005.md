# Question

End-users will need significant training on the new system. The functional manager of those users says her department has neither budget nor time to train her staff. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Move ahead with minimal training and address adoption issues as they appear after go-live.
- **B**: Reduce the system's functionality so that training requirements are smaller.
- **C**: Treat training as part of project scope: include the cost and time in the project's plan and present the case to the sponsor.
- **D**: Build self-service guides only and rely on users to learn at their own pace.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

If training is required for benefits realisation then it belongs in the project plan, and the PM's first move is to fund and schedule it, which is what (C) does. (A) accepts foreseeable failure. (B) sacrifices project value to avoid training. (D) might be one element of a wider plan but is not sufficient on its own when significant change is involved.
