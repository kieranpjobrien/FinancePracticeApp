# Question

The project requires compliance with new regulations that will take effect mid-project. The team needs training, but the regulations are still being finalised and official training doesn't exist yet. How should you approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until official training is available
- **B**: Ignore the regulations until they're fully defined
- **C**: Start with available guidance (draft regulations, industry interpretation), plan for update training when finalised, and build in buffer for learning and potential rework
- **D**: Hire consultants to do all compliance work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory uncertainty requires adaptive approach. Start with available information: draft regulations, industry analysis, similar regulations. Plan for updates: expect to refresh training when final rules emerge. Build buffer: regulatory compliance work may need adjustment as rules finalise. This makes progress while acknowledging uncertainty.

Option A delays progress when preliminary work is possible.

Option B risks major rework or non-compliance.

Option D may be cost-prohibitive and doesn't build internal capability.
