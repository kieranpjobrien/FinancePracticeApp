# Question

A new data privacy regulation takes effect in six months and applies to mid-project deliverables. The regulator has published draft text but the final rules and the official accredited training course are not expected for another four months. The compliance officer is asking how the team will be ready. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Defer all compliance-related design work until the final regulations and accredited training are released.
- **B**: Engage the compliance officer and a subject-matter expert to brief the team on the draft regulation and document the assumptions in the risk register.
- **C**: Replace the affected scope with an external compliance vendor who already holds accreditation under the draft framework.
- **D**: Submit a change request to extend the schedule by four months so accredited training can be completed before any related work begins.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to make progress under uncertainty by working with the best information available and managing the residual risk explicitly. (B) gets the team functionally trained on the draft now, captures the regulatory uncertainty in the risk register, and positions them to update easily once the final rules drop. (A) wastes four months of capacity and exposes downstream activities to compression. (C) outsources capability the team will still need internally and may not be cost-justified. (D) jumps to a baseline change before exploring lower-impact options like interim briefings and assumption-based planning.
