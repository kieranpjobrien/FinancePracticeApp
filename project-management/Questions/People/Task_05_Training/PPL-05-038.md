# Question

Half your team are experienced practitioners with the chosen framework and half are new to it. You need to upskill the new members without disengaging the experienced ones. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Run all training at the experienced level so newer members are pushed to learn the framework quickly.
- **B**: Skip formal training entirely and let experienced members coach the new ones informally on project work.
- **C**: Deliver one general session at an introductory level so the whole team has a shared starting point.
- **D**: Run differentiated tracks with foundational and advanced sessions, using experienced staff as mentors.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI recommends tailoring training to learner needs and using internal expertise to multiply capability. (D) engages both groups appropriately and converts experienced members into a resource. (A) sets newer members up to fail and overwhelms them. (B) leaves learning unstructured and may not produce consistent capability. (C) wastes the experienced cohort's time and underserves both groups.
