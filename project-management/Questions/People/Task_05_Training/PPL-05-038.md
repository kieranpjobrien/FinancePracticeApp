# Question

Team members have varying levels of experience - some are experts who find basic training boring, while others need foundational content. How should you address this diversity?

## Options

- **A**: Provide training at the lowest common denominator
- **B**: Provide training at the expert level and let beginners catch up
- **C**: Differentiate training by skill level, offer advanced tracks for experts, and use experienced members as mentors for those building skills
- **D**: Avoid training to prevent highlighting skill differences

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Differentiated training respects varying skill levels. Beginners get foundational content they need. Experts get advanced material that challenges them (or skip basics entirely). Use experts as resources: mentoring, code review, pairing. This engages everyone at appropriate levels and leverages expertise within the team.

Option A wastes experts' time and may disengage them.

Option B leaves beginners struggling without proper foundation.

Option D avoids the issue rather than addressing it constructively.
