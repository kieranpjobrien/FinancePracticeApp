# Question

A new team member joins mid-project and needs to be brought up to speed quickly. The team is busy with delivery work. How should you handle their onboarding and training?

## Options

- **A**: Let them figure it out by reading documentation
- **B**: Pause project work for comprehensive new-member training
- **C**: Create a structured onboarding plan with prioritised learning, assign a buddy for questions, and ramp them up through increasingly complex work while protecting the team's delivery capacity
- **D**: Wait until the project ends to properly train them

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Structured onboarding balances new member development with team productivity. Prioritised learning: what do they need first to contribute? Buddy system: provides a go-to resource for questions without disrupting everyone. Progressive complexity: start with tasks they can handle, increasing as they learn. This gets them productive quickly while protecting team capacity.

Option A leaves them struggling and slows productivity longer-term.

Option B significantly impacts delivery commitments.

Option D wastes their potential contribution and is demoralising for them.
