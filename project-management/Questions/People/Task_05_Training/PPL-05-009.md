# Question

A new system will change the daily workflow of around 500 employees, but the project budget includes only a token training allocation. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver the minimal training planned and absorb adoption issues post-launch.
- **B**: Build a business case showing the cost of poor adoption versus proper training and present it to the sponsor.
- **C**: Defer the project until a separate training programme can be funded by the business.
- **D**: Restrict initial rollout to a small pilot group so training cost stays inside the current allocation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM's first move is to make the trade-off visible to the sponsor by quantifying the risk of inadequate training, which is what (B) does. (A) accepts foreseeable failure. (C) treats training as someone else's project and stalls delivery. (D) reshapes the rollout to fit a budget constraint without first testing whether the constraint can be revisited.
