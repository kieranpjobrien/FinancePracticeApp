# Question

A project will deploy new software that changes how 500 employees do their daily work. The project budget includes minimal training allocation. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with minimal training to stay within budget
- **B**: Present a business case for adequate training investment, showing the cost of poor adoption versus training costs
- **C**: Cancel the project due to inadequate budget
- **D**: Train only managers and let them train their teams

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Inadequate training is a common cause of failed technology adoption. The PM should present the risk: projects with insufficient training often see low adoption, workarounds, errors, and support costs that exceed training savings. Quantifying this makes the business case for proper training investment.

Option A accepts preventable failure.

Option C is premature - the budget issue may be resolvable.

Option D (train the trainers) can work but needs to be properly planned and resourced, not used as a shortcut.
