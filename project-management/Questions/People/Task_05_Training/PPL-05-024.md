# Question

A team member returns from training enthusiastic but struggles to apply what they learned to actual project work. How should you support the transition from training to application?

## Options

- **A**: Send them for more training
- **B**: Conclude they're not capable of learning new skills
- **C**: Provide opportunities to apply skills immediately, offer coaching during initial application, and allow time for practice while being patient with early mistakes
- **D**: Assign the work to someone else

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The gap between training and application is common. Bridge it: provide immediate opportunities to use new skills before they fade. Offer coaching: help them connect theory to practice. Allow practice time: proficiency requires repetition. Be patient with early mistakes - they're part of learning. This investment yields long-term capability.

Option A provides more theory when practice is needed.

Option B judges prematurely - most people need application support.

Option D wastes the training investment and doesn't develop the team member.
