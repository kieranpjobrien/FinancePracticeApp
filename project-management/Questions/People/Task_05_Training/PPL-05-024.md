# Question

A team member returned from training enthusiastic about the new skills but is struggling to apply them on real project work. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the work to a colleague who already has the skills so delivery is not placed at risk.
- **B**: Send him on a follow-up advanced course covering the same topic in more depth.
- **C**: Pair him with an experienced practitioner on real work and check in early to coach the application.
- **D**: Wait several weeks to see whether he becomes comfortable on his own before intervening at all.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The classroom-to-practice gap is closed by applied work with coaching, not by reassigning the task or by adding more theory, which is what (C) provides. (A) wastes the training investment and removes the development opportunity. (B) adds more knowledge when the gap is application. (D) leaves the team member to struggle and risks both delivery and confidence.
