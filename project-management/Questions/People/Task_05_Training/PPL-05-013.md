# Question

A project introduces an agile methodology to a team accustomed to waterfall. Initial agile training is completed, but the team keeps reverting to waterfall behaviours - detailed upfront planning, sequential phases, change resistance. What additional support is needed?

## Options

- **A**: More classroom training on agile
- **B**: Strict enforcement of agile practices
- **C**: Ongoing coaching, retrospectives focused on the transition, and patience as mindset shifts take time
- **D**: Abandon agile since the team isn't adopting it

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile adoption is a mindset change, not just process change. People revert to familiar patterns under pressure. Ongoing coaching provides support during the uncomfortable transition. Retrospectives create space to discuss what's working and what's hard. Mindset change takes time - instant adoption is unrealistic.

Option A addresses knowledge, but knowledge isn't the problem - application and mindset are.

Option B creates resentment and compliance without understanding.

Option D abandons a valuable approach because transition is challenging.
