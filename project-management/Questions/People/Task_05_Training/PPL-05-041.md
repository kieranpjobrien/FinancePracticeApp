# Question

You've identified that a team member needs significant skill development to perform their project role, but they resist training, believing they already know enough. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Force them into training regardless of their objections
- **B**: Accept their self-assessment and don't require training
- **C**: Have a factual conversation with specific examples of skill gaps, explain the impact on project and their career, listen to their perspective, and collaboratively plan appropriate development
- **D**: Remove them from the project for being difficult

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When there's a gap between self-perception and observed capability, address it directly but constructively. Provide specific examples: where have you observed the skill gap? Explain impact: how does it affect their work, the project, their growth? Listen: they may have valid perspectives or be unaware of issues. Collaborate on development rather than impose it. This approach respects them while addressing real needs.

Option A creates resistance and may not produce genuine learning.

Option B accepts an inaccurate assessment with real project implications.

Option D is a drastic response when the situation may be coachable.
