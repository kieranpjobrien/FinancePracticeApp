# Question

The project team includes a mix of experienced and junior members. Formal training covers the basics, but junior members need more support while experienced members are bored by basic content. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the same training to everyone for consistency
- **B**: Skip training for experienced members
- **C**: Create a blended approach - baseline training for all, advanced content for experienced members, and peer mentoring where experienced members help juniors
- **D**: Only hire experienced members to avoid training complexity

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Different skill levels need different development approaches. Baseline training ensures everyone has fundamentals. Advanced content engages experienced members. Peer mentoring benefits both parties - juniors get personalised support, seniors reinforce knowledge by teaching. This maximises development across skill levels.

Option A bores experienced members and may not support juniors enough.

Option B misses opportunities to develop senior members further.

Option D is unrealistic and loses the value of diverse experience levels.
