# Question

A team mixes senior and junior engineers. A vendor course covers the basics. Junior engineers will benefit but senior engineers say the course will be a waste of their time. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send everyone to the vendor course because shared learning helps team cohesion.
- **B**: Send only the junior engineers to the vendor course.
- **C**: Send junior engineers to the vendor course, give senior engineers an advanced module, and pair seniors with juniors during early sprints to embed the learning.
- **D**: Skip the vendor course and rely entirely on senior engineers mentoring juniors on the job.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Different skill levels need different development paths, and pairing turns the senior engineers into part of the learning system, which is what (C) does. (A) wastes senior engineers' time in basic content. (B) misses the chance to deepen senior engineers' skills and to embed knowledge through pairing. (D) over-relies on informal mentoring and risks inconsistent foundations for the juniors.
