# Question

What is "just-in-time" training?

## Options

- **A**: Training delivered at the last possible moment before it's needed
- **B**: Training that takes exactly the right amount of time
- **C**: Training delivered right when the learner needs to apply the skill, maximising retention and relevance
- **D**: Training delivered at any time convenient for the schedule

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Just-in-time training is delivered immediately before or as the learner needs to apply the knowledge. This approach maximises retention (the skill is used immediately), relevance (the learner sees the application), and efficiency (training is targeted to immediate needs rather than abstract future possibilities).

Option A focuses on timing but misses the purpose - relevance and retention.

Option B confuses "just-in-time" with duration.

Option D describes convenient scheduling, not the just-in-time concept.
