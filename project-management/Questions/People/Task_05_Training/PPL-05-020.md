# Question

What does "just-in-time" training mean?

## Options

- **A**: Training delivered immediately before the learner needs to apply the skill, so retention and relevance are highest.
- **B**: Training scheduled at the most convenient point in the project calendar.
- **C**: The shortest possible training course that still covers the required content.
- **D**: Training delivered in the final week before go-live, regardless of topic.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Just-in-time training is timed to immediate application so retention is high and the skill is used while still fresh, which is what (A) describes. (B) describes scheduling convenience, not learning design. (C) describes content compression, not timing. (D) ties training to a milestone rather than to the moment of application, which is what just-in-time is actually about.
