# Question

Budget constraints prevent sending team members to the training they need. The project requires skills they don't currently have. What alternatives should the PM explore?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that the project cannot be done without the budget
- **B**: Proceed and hope the team can figure it out
- **C**: Explore alternatives: internal knowledge transfer, online resources, vendor-provided training, mentoring from experts in other teams, or phased skill development
- **D**: Remove the features requiring the unfamiliar skills

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When budget constrains formal training, explore alternatives. Internal knowledge transfer: does anyone in the organisation have the skills? Online resources: many technical skills have free or low-cost learning materials. Vendor support: vendors may provide training as part of engagements. Cross-team mentoring: borrow expertise. Phased development: learn incrementally. Creative approaches often address skill gaps within budget constraints.

Option A gives up without exploring alternatives.

Option B sets the team up for failure.

Option D may remove essential functionality without considering training alternatives.
