# Question

Your sponsor has enrolled two apprentices on your engineering project as part of a corporate programme. Each apprentice needs a dedicated mentor and one protected training day a week. Your delivery plan did not assume this. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Dilute the apprentice commitments by cutting the protected weekly training day in half and asking the mentors to support them only when project delivery permits.
- **B**: Reforecast team capacity with the apprentices and mentor time included, rebalance the backlog, and raise any residual gap as a schedule change through change control.
- **C**: Reject the apprentice placements outright on the grounds that the existing project plan simply cannot accommodate the additional training and mentoring overhead required.
- **D**: Accept the apprentices and the mentor overhead silently, absorb the slip inside the team's normal working pattern, and hope the agreed schedule still holds as planned.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects new resource commitments to be modelled transparently and reconciled with scope, schedule, and cost through change control. (B) does that. (A) compromises the apprentice programme and is not the PM's call to make. (C) rejects a sponsor-aligned initiative before attempting to plan it properly. (D) hides the impact and stores a likely overrun in undisclosed team effort.
