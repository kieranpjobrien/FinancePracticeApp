# Question

After completing formal training, team members are still struggling to apply new skills on the job. Training evaluations showed high satisfaction, but practical application is poor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Repeat the same training
- **B**: Supplement with on-the-job coaching, pairing, and practical exercises that bridge classroom learning to real work
- **C**: Accept that training doesn't work and abandon it
- **D**: Blame the training provider

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The gap between knowing and doing is common. Formal training provides knowledge; application requires practice in context. Supplement with coaching (experts helping during real work), pairing (learning alongside experienced practitioners), and practical exercises (safe opportunities to practice). This bridges the transfer gap.

Option A repeats what already didn't achieve practical application.

Option C gives up on development.

Option D blames without solving - training satisfaction was high, so the training itself worked.
