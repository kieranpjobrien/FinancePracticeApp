# Question

After a two-day formal training course, the team's evaluation scores were high but on-the-job application of the new skills is poor. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Re-run the same course in case the content was not absorbed the first time.
- **B**: Add on-the-job coaching, pairing, and short practice exercises that bridge classroom learning to real work.
- **C**: Move to a different training vendor whose materials may transfer better.
- **D**: Accept that classroom training rarely transfers and stop the training programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The training-to-practice gap is closed by application support, not by more classroom time, which is what (B) addresses. (A) repeats what already failed to land. (C) treats vendor choice as the cause without evidence. (D) abandons training when the issue is the lack of application support around it.
