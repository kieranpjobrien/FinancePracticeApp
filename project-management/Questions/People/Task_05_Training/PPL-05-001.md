# Question

A project requires the team to use a new technology that no team members have experience with. The project timeline is aggressive, and stakeholders are concerned about learning curve delays. What approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Hire contractors with the required expertise to minimize the learning curve
- **B**: Build training time into the project schedule, use a combination of formal training and learning-by-doing, and set realistic expectations with stakeholders
- **C**: Let team members learn on their own time to avoid project delays
- **D**: Reduce scope to minimize use of the new technology

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This proactively addresses the skill gap through a realistic plan. Combining formal training (efficient knowledge transfer) with practical application (reinforcement) is effective. Building training into the schedule and managing stakeholder expectations acknowledges reality rather than hoping the challenge will disappear.

Option A might be faster initially but doesn't develop internal capability and may be expensive. It's appropriate for truly specialized needs but shouldn't be the default for new technology adoption.

Option C unfairly burdens team members and likely won't produce effective results. Professional development is a project investment, not an unpaid personal obligation.

Option D avoids the challenge rather than solving it. If the technology is needed, reducing scope undermines project value. Training is usually a better investment than scope reduction.
