# Question

Your project is implementing an organisational change that will affect how hundreds of employees work. Change management training is needed at scale, but individual training is not feasible. What approach should you recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Provide documentation only and expect people to adapt
- **B**: Develop a train-the-trainer program where selected individuals receive deep training and then cascade it to their teams, supplemented by self-service resources
- **C**: Require all employees to attend a single large training session
- **D**: Skip training and rely on the new system to be intuitive

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Train-the-trainer scales training effectively. Select individuals who can effectively teach others. Train them deeply. They then cascade training to their teams, customising for their context. Supplement with self-service resources (videos, guides, FAQs) for ongoing reference. This approach scales while maintaining quality through knowledgeable local trainers.

Option A doesn't provide sufficient support for significant change.

Option C is logistically difficult and one-size-fits-all training often isn't effective.

Option D assumes intuitive design replaces the need for training on changed processes.
