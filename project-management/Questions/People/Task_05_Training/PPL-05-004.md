# Question

A team member returning from agile training is enthusiastic about new practices but the rest of the team is skeptical. How should the project manager support knowledge transfer from the trained individual to the team?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that the team adopt the new practices immediately
- **B**: Have the trained member present to the team and facilitate discussion about which practices to try
- **C**: Let the trained member implement new practices and others can observe
- **D**: Send the entire team to the same training

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This creates structured knowledge sharing while respecting team autonomy. Presentation transfers knowledge; discussion allows the team to evaluate fit and builds buy-in for practices they choose to try. This leverages the training investment without forcing adoption.

Option A mandates without buy-in, which often leads to resistance and superficial adoption.

Option C isolates the learning with one person and may create resentment ("why are they doing things differently?").

Option D is expensive and may not be necessary if knowledge can transfer effectively within the team.
