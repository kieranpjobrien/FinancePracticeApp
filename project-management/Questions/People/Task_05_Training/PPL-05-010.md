# Question

The PM has three options to upskill the team in a specialised technology: vendor classroom training at $50K, free online self-study, or a $30K consultant who trains and mentors over three months. Team members have varied schedules and learning styles. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run a short skills-and-needs assessment with the team to match the option to the team's context, timeline, and learning preferences.
- **B**: Choose self-study because it preserves the budget for delivery work.
- **C**: Choose the vendor classroom course because structured training is the lowest-risk option.
- **D**: Choose the consultant because mentoring is the most effective for sustained adoption.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right first move is to match the training approach to the team's context rather than picking based on price or general principle, which is what (A) does. (B), (C), and (D) all jump to a choice without the assessment that should drive it; even though each option has merit in some contexts, the question asks what to do first.
