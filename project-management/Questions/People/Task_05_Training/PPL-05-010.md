# Question

A project team needs training on a specialised technology. Options include: expensive vendor training ($50K), self-study with online resources (free but unstructured), or hiring a consultant to train and mentor ($30K over 3 months). The team has mixed learning styles and busy schedules. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Choose the cheapest option - self-study
- **B**: Choose the most expensive option - vendor training must be best
- **C**: Assess team needs, timeline constraints, and learning style preferences to recommend the most appropriate option
- **D**: Skip training and learn on the job

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Training decisions should match the situation. Vendor training is structured but intensive. Self-study is flexible but requires discipline. Consultant mentoring provides ongoing support. The PM should assess: How quickly do we need proficiency? What's the team's capacity for self-directed learning? Is ongoing support valuable? The best choice depends on context.

Option A optimises for cost alone, ignoring effectiveness.

Option B assumes price equals quality without analysis.

Option D creates project risk from skill gaps.
