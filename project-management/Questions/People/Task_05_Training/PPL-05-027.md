# Question

What is the purpose of including training activities in the project schedule?

## Options

- **A**: To add buffer time to the schedule
- **B**: To ensure training is resourced, visible, and accounted for in project planning rather than treated as an afterthought
- **C**: To penalise team members who need training
- **D**: To satisfy HR requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Including training in the schedule makes it a legitimate project activity with allocated time and resources. It ensures training doesn't get squeezed out by "real work," makes the time investment visible to stakeholders, and allows realistic planning that accounts for the team's learning curve.

Option A misunderstands training as padding rather than necessary investment.

Option C takes a punitive view of a development activity.

Option D treats training as administrative compliance rather than project necessity.
