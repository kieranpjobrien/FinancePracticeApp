# Question

Your team runs weekly brown-bag sessions led by rotating team members. Quality has become uneven: some are excellent, others are unstructured and poorly attended, and the same few people present each month. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: End the brown-bag format and replace it with a paid external training subscription so that quality becomes consistent and no longer depends on internal presenters.
- **B**: Force every team member to present at least once a quarter regardless of topic so that presenter distribution evens out across the team over time.
- **C**: Leave the format as-is and let the sessions continue to self-regulate, trusting that the team will course-correct without any project manager intervention.
- **D**: Co-create lightweight standards with the team (topic pipeline, short preparation template, pairing of new presenters with a coach), and review outcomes at each retrospective.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats uneven quality in a team-owned practice as a facilitation problem that is solved by lightweight standards, coaching, and inspect-and-adapt, not by substitution, coercion, or passivity. (D) builds presenter capability and preserves ownership. (A) replaces a team practice with external consumption. (B) forces participation without building skill and creates poor sessions by design. (C) accepts a known decline without acting.
