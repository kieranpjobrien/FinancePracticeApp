# Question

A critical team member is the only one with certain specialised skills. They're reluctant to share knowledge, possibly feeling it makes them indispensable. How should you address this training/knowledge sharing challenge?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the situation since they're a valued team member
- **B**: Threaten their job unless they share knowledge
- **C**: Recognise and reward knowledge sharing, frame it as leadership development rather than making themselves dispensable, and create formal knowledge transfer opportunities
- **D**: Replace them with someone more cooperative

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Address the underlying concern. Knowledge hoarding often comes from job insecurity fears. Reframe: knowledge sharing demonstrates leadership and is recognised (promotion criterion, performance reviews). Reward sharing behaviours visibly. Create formal opportunities: mentoring, documentation, training delivery. Show that sharing makes them more valuable, not less.

Option A accepts risk and doesn't develop team capability.

Option B creates adversarial dynamics and may not produce genuine transfer.

Option D loses expertise and may not solve the underlying knowledge transfer issue.
