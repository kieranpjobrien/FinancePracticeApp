# Question

A senior engineer is the only person on the project who understands a legacy integration. They consistently decline to document it or train anyone, citing time pressure, but you suspect they see the knowledge as job security. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the functional manager and request that knowledge transfer be added to their performance objectives.
- **B**: Reassign the integration work to another team member and let the senior engineer focus on other stories.
- **C**: Recognise knowledge sharing as contribution, allocate time for mentoring, and pair them with a backup.
- **D**: Bring in a contractor to reverse-engineer the integration so the team is no longer dependent on this engineer.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI prefers addressing the root cause through servant leadership and team development rather than coercion or workarounds. (C) makes sharing a valued behaviour, removes the time-pressure excuse, and uses pairing to transfer knowledge in the flow of work. (A) escalates before trying to resolve at the team level and may harden resistance. (B) avoids the dependency rather than reducing it. (D) duplicates effort, raises cost, and does not change the underlying behaviour.
