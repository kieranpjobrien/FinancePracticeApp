# Question

The project involves a vendor product that requires certification for team members to configure it properly. The certification is expensive and time-consuming. How should you plan for this?

## Options

- **A**: Skip certification and learn on the job
- **B**: Include certification costs and time in the project budget and schedule, potentially starting certifications before the main project work begins
- **C**: Require team members to get certified on their own time and expense
- **D**: Use only already-certified external consultants

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Required certifications are a legitimate project cost and schedule factor. Plan proactively: include in budget, schedule certification before relevant work begins, identify who needs which certifications. This ensures the team can do the work properly without delays. Failing to plan for required training leads to schedule pressure or quality issues.

Option A risks improper configuration and may void vendor support.

Option C shifts project costs to employees inappropriately.

Option D may be cost-prohibitive long-term and doesn't build internal capability.
