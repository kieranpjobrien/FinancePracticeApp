# Question

After a merger, your project team is made up of people from the acquiring and the acquired companies, each fluent in a different CI/CD toolchain. Leadership has decided the acquiring company's toolchain will be the standard. The project is underway. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow each subgroup to keep using the toolchain they know and plan a full unified migration only at the end of the current project for minimum disruption.
- **B**: Require everyone to learn both toolchains in parallel so the team has maximum flexibility, regardless of what leadership has chosen as the standard.
- **C**: Run targeted applied training for the acquired-company engineers on the chosen toolchain, pair them with experienced users, and retire the second toolchain on an agreed date.
- **D**: Escalate to leadership to reverse the decision because the acquired company's toolchain is technically superior and less disruptive to the current project work.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI aligns team capability with strategic direction through targeted learning and sunset planning, not parallel operation, contrarian escalation, or deferral. (C) builds capability and retires the redundant tool cleanly. (A) preserves divergence and accumulates integration cost. (B) doubles ongoing cognitive load for no real benefit. (D) challenges a settled strategic decision on technical grounds that are not the PM's to override.
