# Question

A project will use a new technology that most team members haven't worked with before. The project has a tight timeline. How should the PM address the training need?

## Options

- **A**: Skip training and let team members learn on the job
- **B**: Delay project start until everyone is fully trained
- **C**: Assess specific skill gaps, provide targeted training, and pair experienced members with those learning
- **D**: Replace team members with people who already know the technology

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Training should be targeted and practical. Assess what skills are actually needed for this project (not comprehensive training), provide focused learning, and use peer learning (pairing) to build skills while working. This balances skill development with project progress.

Option A (no training) leads to mistakes, rework, and frustration.

Option B (full training first) may delay unnecessarily - people can learn while doing with proper support.

Option D (replace team) loses institutional knowledge and may not be practical given timelines or availability.
