# Question

An experienced analyst on your regulatory reporting project has accepted an internal transfer to another business unit, with a four-week notice period. She owns the core reconciliation models and has trained no backup. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Agree a transition plan with her and the receiving manager that pairs her with two successors, documents the models, and validates transfer through trial runs before she leaves.
- **B**: Ask the receiving business unit to delay her transfer by three full months so she can deliver the remaining regulatory reporting cycles without needing a handover.
- **C**: Escalate the transfer to the executive sponsors and frame it as a serious delivery risk that should be reversed immediately to protect the agreed project outcomes.
- **D**: Begin recruiting an external contractor to take over the models once she has left, rather than transferring knowledge internally to existing team members on the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects structured, multi-modal knowledge transfer in the available window, verified through practice, rather than blocking legitimate moves or externalising the risk. (A) uses pairing, documentation, and trial handovers in the four weeks available. (B) overrides a confirmed internal career decision and damages trust for a short-term convenience. (C) escalates before the PM has attempted resolution within their span of control. (D) avoids capability-building inside the existing team and adds procurement lead time with no transfer benefit.
