# Question

Your global team has members whose working English is strong in writing but less confident in real-time spoken sessions. The vendor's standard training is a full-day live lecture in English with limited interaction. Early participants report they struggled to follow. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the vendor for slower delivery, session recordings, written materials alongside the lecture, and follow-up Q&A, and run small-group application sessions after each topic.
- **B**: Require all non-native English speakers to complete an intensive business-English programme before attending the technical training sessions.
- **C**: Accept the standard format as delivered and trust that team members will fill gaps by rewatching the recording and raising any questions when ready.
- **D**: Replace the live training entirely with a written self-paced course so that language-confidence issues do not affect learning outcomes in the sessions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle adapts delivery to the learners without losing the trainer's expertise or the learning outcome. (A) layers pace adjustments, materials, recordings, and applied follow-up to support comprehension. (B) confuses a delivery problem with a language-proficiency issue and delays project-critical training. (C) relies on self-directed catch-up that the feedback already shows is not working. (D) removes the live expert interaction that is the main value of vendor training.
