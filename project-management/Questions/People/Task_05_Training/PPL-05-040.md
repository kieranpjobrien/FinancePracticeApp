# Question

The organisation is implementing a new project management tool. Some team members are enthusiastic, but others are resistant because they prefer the old tool. What training approach would support adoption?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate training and threaten consequences for non-adoption
- **B**: Skip training for those who don't want it
- **C**: Provide comprehensive training that emphasises benefits, addresses concerns, allows hands-on practice, and includes support during the transition period
- **D**: Let enthusiastic adopters convince the resistors

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Tool adoption requires addressing both capability and willingness. Training must cover how to use the tool (capability) and why it's beneficial (motivation). Address specific concerns - why do people prefer the old tool? Can the new tool address those needs? Hands-on practice builds comfort. Transition support helps through the learning curve.

Option A creates resentment without addressing underlying concerns.

Option B creates a divided team with inconsistent tool usage.

Option D puts burden on peers without formal support.
