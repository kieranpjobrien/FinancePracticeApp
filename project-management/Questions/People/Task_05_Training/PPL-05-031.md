# Question

What is cross-training in a project context?

## Options

- **A**: Training that happens across multiple countries
- **B**: Training team members in skills outside their primary expertise to increase team flexibility and reduce single points of failure
- **C**: Training that is delivered by multiple trainers
- **D**: Training on competitor products

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cross-training develops team members in areas outside their primary skill set. This increases team flexibility (more people can do critical tasks), reduces single points of failure (the team isn't dependent on one person for a skill), and develops individuals (broader skills enhance their capabilities). It's a form of risk mitigation and capability building.

Option A confuses cross-training with international training.

Option C relates to trainer arrangements, not the cross-training concept.

Option D is competitive analysis, not cross-training.
