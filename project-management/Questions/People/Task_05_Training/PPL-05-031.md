# Question

In a project context, what is the primary purpose of cross-training team members?

## Options

- **A**: To reward high performers with additional learning opportunities.
- **B**: To increase team flexibility and reduce single points of failure on critical skills.
- **C**: To prepare team members for promotion into management roles.
- **D**: To benchmark team capability against external market rates.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats cross-training as a resource and risk management technique that broadens coverage of critical skills. (B) captures both the flexibility benefit and the risk-mitigation purpose. (A) frames training as a reward, not as capability planning. (C) is career development, not the project rationale for cross-training. (D) confuses cross-training with skills assessment or benchmarking.
