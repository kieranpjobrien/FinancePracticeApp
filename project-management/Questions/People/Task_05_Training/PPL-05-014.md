# Question

A vendor provides a critical system, and the contract includes training for the project team. The vendor's standard training is generic and doesn't address the specific ways the team will use the system. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the standard training since it's included in the contract
- **B**: Negotiate customised training that addresses the team's specific use cases and workflows
- **C**: Skip vendor training entirely and figure it out internally
- **D**: Delay the project until the vendor offers better training

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Generic training often doesn't prepare people for their specific context. The PM should negotiate with the vendor for customised content - either modifying the standard training or adding sessions focused on the team's workflows. This may have cost implications but produces more effective training.

Option A accepts suboptimal training when better is achievable.

Option C loses vendor expertise entirely.

Option D is an extreme response to a negotiable issue.
