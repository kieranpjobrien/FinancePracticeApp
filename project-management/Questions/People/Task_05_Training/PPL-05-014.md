# Question

A vendor's contract includes generic product training, but the team will use the product in a specific way that the standard training does not cover. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the standard training because it is already paid for and bridge the remaining gaps with self-study.
- **B**: Ask the vendor to tailor or supplement training to the team's actual workflows and adjust the contract.
- **C**: Decline the vendor training and run an internal session built entirely around the team's actual workflows.
- **D**: Wait until after go-live and add targeted training sessions in response to the issues that actually arise.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The right move is to negotiate tailored content with the vendor while the training opportunity is still in front of the team, which is what (B) does. (A) accepts a known shortfall and pushes the cost onto the team. (C) loses vendor expertise that may be hard to replicate internally. (D) is a reactive plan that pays for the gap in support tickets and rework after go-live.
