# Question

Half the team has prior agile experience and the other half is new to agile. What is the most effective training approach?

## Options

- **A**: Provide foundational agile training to those without experience, run a team workshop on the specific practices the team will use, and pair experienced members with newcomers during early sprints.
- **B**: Send only the inexperienced half to a vendor agile course.
- **C**: Send everyone to the same vendor agile course so the team has a shared baseline.
- **D**: Skip formal training and rely on the experienced half to coach the rest during real work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Differentiated training plus a team-level alignment workshop respects existing knowledge, closes the gap for newcomers, and gets everyone on the same playbook, which is what (A) does. (B) closes the foundational gap but leaves the team without alignment on which practices it will adopt. (C) wastes the experienced members' time and may not address local practice differences. (D) over-relies on informal coaching and risks foundational gaps for newcomers.
