# Question

The project is transitioning from waterfall to agile. Half the team has agile experience; half has none. What training approach would be most effective?

## Options

- **A**: Send only the inexperienced half to formal agile training
- **B**: Train everyone identically regardless of experience
- **C**: Provide foundational training to those without experience, then use pairing and coaching from experienced members, supplemented by team-level workshops on practices you'll use together
- **D**: Skip training and let people learn through doing

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Differentiated training: those without agile experience need foundational concepts. Those with experience don't need basics but can serve as coaches and mentors. Team-level workshops ensure everyone aligns on specific practices you'll adopt together. This approach respects existing knowledge while addressing gaps.

Option A doesn't leverage experienced members for knowledge transfer or align the team.

Option B wastes time for experienced members and may not address specific gaps.

Option D leaves half the team struggling with unfamiliar concepts and practices.
