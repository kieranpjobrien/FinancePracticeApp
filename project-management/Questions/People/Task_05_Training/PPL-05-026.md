# Question

Several team members have requested training that would benefit their careers but isn't directly required for current project work. How should you handle these requests?

## Options

- **A**: Deny all requests not directly related to project needs
- **B**: Approve all requests regardless of project relevance
- **C**: Evaluate requests considering career development value, future project applicability, team retention benefits, and available budget - balance project needs with professional growth
- **D**: Tell them to pursue training on their own time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Training decisions should balance multiple factors. Immediate project need is important, but so is: future skill development (builds capability for upcoming work), retention (development opportunities keep people engaged), career progression (good for the individual and the team). Evaluate thoughtfully rather than applying rigid rules.

Option A ignores retention and long-term development benefits.

Option B may not be feasible and doesn't prioritise effectively.

Option D shifts development burden to employees and may affect retention.
