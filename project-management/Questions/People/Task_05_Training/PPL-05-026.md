# Question

Three developers on your project have asked to attend a cloud-architecture certification course. The skill is not required for the current sprint backlog but is on the organisation's strategic roadmap for the next 12 months. The training budget has capacity. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the requests because team members should generally receive the development opportunities they ask for.
- **B**: Decline the requests because the training is not tied to current project deliverables.
- **C**: Refer the developers to their functional managers since career development sits outside the project's remit.
- **D**: Assess each request against current capacity, future skill needs, and retention impact, then approve those that align and document the rationale.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats team development as a legitimate PM responsibility that balances project delivery, organisational benefit, and people growth. (D) applies a deliberate trade-off rather than a blanket rule, which is consistent with tailoring and resource management. (A) ignores capacity and project priorities. (B) takes too narrow a view of "project need" and risks disengagement and attrition. (C) abdicates the PM's role in developing the team and supporting the organisation's resource pool.
