# Question

A new system passed UAT but the operations team is generating very high support ticket volume in the first month after go-live. Most tickets are about how to perform routine workflows, not actual defects. What is the most likely root cause?

## Options

- **A**: User training was scoped to system features rather than to the workflows users actually need to perform.
- **B**: The system's user interface needs to be redesigned to be more intuitive.
- **C**: The operations team is resistant to change and the volume will fall on its own.
- **D**: The support team's documentation is incomplete and needs to be expanded.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

UAT passing means the system works as specified, while high "how do I" ticket volume points to a training scope that taught features but not workflows, which is what (A) describes. (B) is possible in some cases but is contradicted by UAT passing. (C) blames users for predictable adoption pain. (D) addresses the symptom by adding support content rather than fixing the upstream training gap.
