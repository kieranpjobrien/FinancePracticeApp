# Question

The project will use a technology that most of the team has never worked with, and the timeline is tight. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver just-in-time training on the skills needed in the next two sprints and pair learners with experienced members.
- **B**: Hold the project for two weeks while everyone completes a comprehensive vendor course on the new technology.
- **C**: Skip training entirely and let the team learn through trial and error during the first few sprints.
- **D**: Replace the current team with external engineers who already know the technology in detail.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right balance is targeted, just-in-time training paired with peer learning during real work, which is what (A) does. (B) under-uses available time and front-loads training that may not all be needed. (C) sets the team up for expensive rework. (D) is rarely feasible and breaks team continuity.
