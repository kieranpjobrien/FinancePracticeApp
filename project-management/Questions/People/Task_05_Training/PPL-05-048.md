# Question

A signed vendor contract included ten days of onsite training for your integration team, but the vendor has now emailed saying they will only deliver four days, citing internal capacity. Training was scheduled to begin in three weeks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the reduced four days, reshape the learning plan around the shorter format, and rely on self-study for the remainder of the content.
- **B**: Escalate to the sponsor immediately so legal action can be initiated against the vendor for failing to honour the original agreement.
- **C**: Cancel the vendor engagement entirely, source a different training provider, and absorb the resulting schedule slip into the baseline.
- **D**: Formally notify the vendor of the contracted obligation, request a written plan to deliver the full ten days, and engage procurement if the vendor cannot comply.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to enforce contract terms through proper procurement channels before accepting reduced delivery or switching vendors. (D) treats the shortfall as a contract issue, gives the vendor a chance to comply, and uses procurement as the accountable function. (A) silently absorbs a contract breach and sets a precedent. (B) jumps to legal action and sponsor escalation before working the issue at the right level. (C) discards a contract without attempting enforcement and creates avoidable schedule impact.
