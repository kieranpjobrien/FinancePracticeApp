# Question

Midway through the project, a significant scope change introduces technology the team has never worked with. The change is approved, and the timeline is not extended. How should you address the training need?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team to figure it out while maintaining the timeline
- **B**: Request timeline extension for training before work begins
- **C**: Identify minimum viable training, bring in expert support (consultant or contractor with the skill), and plan for parallel learning while delivering, with explicit risk acknowledgment to stakeholders
- **D**: Recommend reverting the scope change

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When changes create training needs within fixed timelines, use a pragmatic approach. Minimum viable training: what's essential for immediate work? Expert support: bring in someone who can do the work and transfer knowledge. Parallel learning: the team learns while delivering with expert support. Be explicit about risks: stakeholders should understand the implications.

Option A sets the team up to struggle without acknowledging the challenge.

Option B may not be acceptable given the timeline commitment.

Option D avoids the challenge without considering if it can be addressed.
