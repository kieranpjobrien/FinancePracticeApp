# Question

The project is using pair programming both for productivity and as a training mechanism. Senior developers are complaining that pairing with juniors slows them down. How should you address this?

## Options

- **A**: Stop pair programming to maximise senior productivity
- **B**: Only pair juniors with each other
- **C**: Acknowledge the short-term cost, explain the investment value (junior development, knowledge sharing, reduced bus factor), and find ways to balance productive pairing with focused solo time for seniors
- **D**: Ignore the complaints since pairing is the rule

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Balance is needed. Acknowledge the reality: pairing with juniors does require more from seniors. Explain the investment: junior development, knowledge transfer, reduced single points of failure. Find balance: perhaps not all work requires pairing, or rotate pairing assignments so no one is overburdened. Show that their investment is valued and managed thoughtfully.

Option A loses the development and knowledge-sharing benefits.

Option B limits junior learning to peer level and doesn't distribute expertise.

Option D dismisses legitimate concerns without addressing them.
