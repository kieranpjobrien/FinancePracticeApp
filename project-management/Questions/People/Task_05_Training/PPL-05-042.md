# Question

Your team uses pair programming both for delivery and for upskilling junior developers. Two senior developers report that pairing with juniors is reducing their throughput on complex stories. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pair juniors with each other only, so the seniors can focus on complex work uninterrupted.
- **B**: Suspend pair programming entirely and rely on code review for knowledge sharing.
- **C**: Tell the seniors that pairing is the rule and the team will adapt over time.
- **D**: Acknowledge the trade-off, vary pairing intensity by task, and protect some focused solo time for seniors while keeping the upskilling benefit.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI rewards balanced trade-offs that preserve people development without ignoring legitimate productivity concerns. (D) keeps the long-term capability benefits while addressing the seniors' real constraint. (A) sacrifices the upskilling outcome and concentrates risk. (B) loses both knowledge transfer and the early defect-detection benefits of pairing. (C) dismisses valid feedback and erodes trust.
