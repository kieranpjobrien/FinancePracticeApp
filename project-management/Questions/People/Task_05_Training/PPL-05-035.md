# Question

How does coaching primarily differ from training in a project setting?

## Options

- **A**: Coaching is for soft skills only, while training is for technical skills only.
- **B**: Coaching is informal while training is always conducted by external vendors.
- **C**: Coaching develops capability through guided practice and feedback; training transfers knowledge or skills through structured content.
- **D**: Coaching is performed by the project manager, whereas training is delivered by HR.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes the two by purpose and method, not by topic or who delivers them. (C) captures the difference: training is structured knowledge transfer; coaching is contextual development through practice and feedback. (A) is too narrow on both sides. (B) is incidental and not a defining difference. (D) is an organisational arrangement, not a definition.
