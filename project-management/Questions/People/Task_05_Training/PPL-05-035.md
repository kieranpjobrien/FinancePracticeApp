# Question

What is the difference between training and coaching?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: Training transfers knowledge and skills through structured learning; coaching develops ability through guided practice, feedback, and reflection
- **C**: Training is for technical skills; coaching is for soft skills
- **D**: Training is formal; coaching is informal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Training typically involves transferring specific knowledge or skills through structured content (courses, workshops, materials). Coaching develops ability through one-on-one guidance, practice feedback, questioning, and reflection. Both are valuable and often complementary - training provides the knowledge, coaching helps apply it.

Option A misses meaningful distinctions between the approaches.

Option C is too narrow - both can address various skill types.

Option D oversimplifies - both can be formal or informal depending on context.
