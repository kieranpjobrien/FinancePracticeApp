# Question

A project manager leads a virtual team where some members work from home while others work from a company office in another city. The office-based group has formed strong bonds and often makes decisions during in-person discussions that remote workers learn about later. What structural change would BEST address this?

## Options

- **A**: Require office-based team members to work from home
- **B**: Relocate remote workers to the office location
- **C**: Implement a "remote-first" policy where all key discussions happen in virtual channels accessible to everyone
- **D**: Accept the difference and document decisions for remote workers

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A "remote-first" policy means that even when people are co-located, they behave as if everyone is remote. Key discussions happen in virtual channels, decisions are documented where remote workers can see them, and important meetings include video links. This creates equity without eliminating the benefits of co-location.

Option A removes benefits of co-location unnecessarily.

Option B may not be feasible and doesn't build needed virtual collaboration capability.

Option D accepts inequity and may lead to disengagement from remote workers.
