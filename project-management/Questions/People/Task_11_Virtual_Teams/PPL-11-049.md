# Question

On a virtual predictive project, three of the eight team members consistently keep cameras off, mute themselves, and rarely contribute in meetings. The PM suspects they have disengaged but the team's ground rules do not require cameras on. Delivery has not yet been affected. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the concern in the next retrospective and ask the whole team whether the current meeting norms are still serving everyone effectively.
- **B**: Add a camera-on ground rule for all project meetings, framed as a step to strengthen connection and participation across the distributed team.
- **C**: Continue to monitor delivery metrics, since outcomes are fine and cameras and participation styles are personal preferences the PM should not police.
- **D**: Speak privately with each of the three members to understand what is behind the pattern before deciding what, if anything, should change.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder-engagement principle says to understand before you act — individual 1:1s surface context (caring responsibilities, burnout, feeling unheard) that a group discussion cannot. (A) risks exposing people publicly before the PM knows the cause. (B) imposes a rule on a symptom without diagnosing it. (C) ignores a known early signal of disengagement, which usually surfaces in outcomes later.
