# Question

A virtual team member's home situation has changed (young children now at home during work hours). Their video background shows distractions and their focus seems compromised. How should the PM support them?

## Options

- **A**: Demand they find a quiet workspace
- **B**: Lower performance expectations indefinitely
- **C**: Have a supportive conversation to understand their situation and explore accommodations like flexible hours, reduced meeting load, or adjusted responsibilities
- **D**: Ignore the situation and treat them the same as before

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Virtual team management requires empathy for home situations. Have a private conversation to understand their constraints. Explore accommodations: Can they work different hours? Can some meetings be optional? Can responsibilities be temporarily adjusted? The goal is maintaining productivity while supporting the person through a challenging period.

Option A may not be possible and is unsupportive.

Option B accepts reduced performance without exploring solutions.

Option D ignores a situation affecting performance and team member wellbeing.
