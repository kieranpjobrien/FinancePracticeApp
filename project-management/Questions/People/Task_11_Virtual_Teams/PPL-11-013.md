# Question

A remote team member's home situation has changed — young children are now home during work hours. Their focus has visibly suffered and they've mentioned in passing that they're "just getting through the day." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Explain that the project timeline cannot flex and ask them to find a quieter workspace for core hours.
- **B**: Formally reduce their commitments for the rest of the project and reallocate the work to other team members.
- **C**: Privately ask how they are, understand the constraints, and co-design accommodations — flexible hours, reduced meeting load, or temporarily adjusted responsibilities.
- **D**: Leave the situation alone; commenting on someone's home circumstances risks being intrusive.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Servant leadership expects the PM to engage with the person, understand the constraint, and find accommodations that support both them and the project. (C) does exactly that. (A) doubles down on the project's needs without acknowledging the person's and damages trust. (B) removes them unilaterally without their input, which can feel like a demotion and isn't necessarily what they need. (D) respects privacy at the cost of not offering support the person may very much want; PMI's people-first principle favours checking in.
