# Question

A distributed agile team is repeatedly marking stories as done that later get reopened in the next sprint. Different developers clearly hold different bars for what "done" means. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner personally review every completed story before it leaves the in-progress column.
- **B**: Add more detailed acceptance criteria to every story so done is unambiguous at the story level.
- **C**: Facilitate the team to write a shared Definition of Done together and pin it visibly in the team's online workspace.
- **D**: Add a peer code-review step for every change before any story can be moved to done.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A team-owned Definition of Done is the standard mechanism for resolving "done means different things" across a Scrum team, and the visibility supports it as a living agreement. (C) does this. (A) makes the product owner a permanent gatekeeper, which doesn't scale and removes team ownership of quality. (B) treats every story individually but never gives the team a baseline standard, so the per-story interpretations will drift again. (D) adds a useful technical practice but it's narrower than the issue — done covers more than code review.
