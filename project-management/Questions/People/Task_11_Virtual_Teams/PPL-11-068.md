# Question

The PM has noticed that retrospectives on a remote team have become superficial — the same mild items keep appearing and no one raises anything substantive. Delivery is fine but the PM suspects deeper issues are being avoided. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep running the standard retro format and trust that the team will raise important items once they feel ready to surface them voluntarily.
- **B**: Attend fewer retrospectives and let the team run them among themselves so they can speak more freely without the PM present in the room.
- **C**: Vary the retrospective format — anonymous written input, 1:1 pre-reads, external facilitators — and directly name the superficiality pattern openly with the team.
- **D**: Use a one-off team survey to collect anonymous concerns and bring the aggregated themes into the next retrospective for discussion in the usual format.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's retrospective principles say to vary the format and directly name patterns that are limiting learning — this invites depth and models the psychological safety needed. (A) waits passively while the pattern hardens. (B) removes the PM but leaves the same format and dynamic. (D) is a useful supplementary tactic but alone it does not address why the live conversation is shallow.
