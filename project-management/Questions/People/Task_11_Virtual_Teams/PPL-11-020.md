# Question

A PM has just inherited a virtual team with a six-month history of missed deadlines, blame between members, and minimal interaction outside of escalations. There are six months left to deliver. What approach should the PM take FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a daily individual status report so each team member's progress and obstacles are visible to the PM.
- **B**: Reduce committed scope to give the team a more achievable target and rebuild credibility from a smaller base.
- **C**: Focus on rebuilding trust and psychological safety through transparent communication and visible small wins.
- **D**: Replace the team members with the worst track record to break the established blame culture on the team.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The symptoms describe a trust problem; tightening process or swapping people without first changing the relational base usually deepens the dysfunction. (C) is the PMI-aligned response and the precondition for the other interventions to work. (A) feels like surveillance to a low-trust team and tends to harden defensive behaviour. (B) treats the symptom (missed deadlines) without changing why the team is missing them. (D) destroys institutional knowledge, hits morale, and leaves the new joiners walking into the same culture.
