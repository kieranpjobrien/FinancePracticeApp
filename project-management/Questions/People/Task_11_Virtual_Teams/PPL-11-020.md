# Question

A project manager inherits a virtual team with a history of missed deadlines and blame culture. Team members work in isolation and only communicate when there are problems. The PM has 6 months to deliver a critical project. What approach should the PM prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Implement strict monitoring and daily status reports to ensure accountability
- **B**: Replace team members who have been involved in missed deadlines
- **C**: Focus on rebuilding trust through transparent communication, collaborative problem-solving, and celebrating small wins
- **D**: Reduce scope to account for expected performance issues

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The symptoms (isolation, blame, reactive communication) indicate a trust problem that monitoring won't solve. Rebuilding psychological safety through transparency ("here's what I know and don't know"), collaborative approaches ("let's solve this together"), and positive reinforcement (celebrating progress) changes team dynamics and ultimately improves performance.

Option A may reinforce the blame culture and reduce trust further. Monitoring without trust feels like surveillance.

Option B doesn't address the systemic issues and destroys institutional knowledge. New members would inherit the same culture.

Option D accepts poor performance rather than addressing root causes.
