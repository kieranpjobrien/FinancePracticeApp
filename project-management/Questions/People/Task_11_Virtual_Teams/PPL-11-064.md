# Question

A virtual team is managing a live production incident. Members across three time zones are trying to coordinate through a noisy chat channel and an ad-hoc video call that keeps dropping. Confusion is slowing response and the sponsor is asking for a status update every few minutes. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Establish one dedicated incident bridge with clearly assigned roles, a separate stakeholder update channel, and a single person owning all updates to the sponsor.
- **B**: Continue on the current call and chat but ask everyone to be more disciplined about using threads and muting when they are not actively speaking.
- **C**: Escalate the incident to the central incident management team and hand over coordination so the project team can focus purely on technical remediation.
- **D**: Move all coordination to asynchronous chat with a running timeline document, and hold hourly video syncs to align on next actions across time zones.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a live distributed incident the PMI-aligned first move is to restore structure — a dedicated bridge, clear roles and a single stakeholder voice cut through the noise that is slowing response. (B) preserves the broken format and relies on individual discipline under pressure. (C) outsources leadership the PM can provide now and loses project context during handover. (D) is too slow for a live incident where minutes matter.
