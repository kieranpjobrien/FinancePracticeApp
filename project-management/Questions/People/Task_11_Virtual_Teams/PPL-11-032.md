# Question

An organisation is transitioning a previously co-located team to permanent remote work. Some team members are excited while others are resistant. The PM notices productivity dropping and morale declining among the resistant members. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Focus support on the resistant members while letting enthusiastic members work independently
- **B**: Mandate participation in virtual team-building activities
- **C**: Acknowledge concerns, co-create new ways of working, and provide training and support for virtual collaboration
- **D**: Give the resistant members the option to find other roles in the organisation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Successful transitions require acknowledging that resistance often comes from legitimate concerns (isolation, work-life boundaries, collaboration challenges). Co-creating new ways of working gives everyone agency. Training addresses skill gaps in virtual collaboration. Support helps with the emotional adjustment.

Option A neglects that even enthusiastic members may struggle and creates a divided team.

Option B may increase resistance by forcing participation without addressing concerns.

Option D avoids the leadership challenge and loses valuable team members.
