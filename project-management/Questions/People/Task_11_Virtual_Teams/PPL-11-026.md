# Question

During a virtual project kickoff, the PM wants to help team members who have never worked together build initial rapport. Which activity would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately dive into project requirements to demonstrate focus
- **B**: Share personal introductions including non-work interests and use interactive activities
- **C**: Send team member biographies via email for people to read later
- **D**: Schedule a separate optional social call

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The kickoff is a prime opportunity for relationship building when attention is high. Personal introductions that go beyond job titles (hobbies, fun facts, photos of pets) humanise team members. Interactive activities (virtual icebreakers, collaborative exercises) create shared experiences that form the foundation for working relationships.

Option A misses the relationship-building opportunity; task focus can come after connection is established.

Option C is passive and doesn't create the interactive experience that builds rapport.

Option D may have low attendance since relationships haven't been established yet, and separates relationship-building from project work.
