# Question

A hybrid team's key design meetings have six people in a conference room and four joining on video. The room dynamic dominates, remote voices are interrupted, and decisions effectively land before remote members weigh in. The office group does not see a problem. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ban the use of the conference room for design meetings so everyone must join from their own laptop and participation is structurally equalised across the team.
- **B**: Run design meetings as laptop-first for everyone, each person on their own camera and mic, even when several are physically in the same building.
- **C**: Ask the office attendees to pause, summarise, and explicitly invite remote input every ten minutes during design meetings to balance participation across locations.
- **D**: Assign a remote champion in the room whose job is to notice when remote voices are being missed and intervene on their behalf each time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The structural fix for hybrid meeting inequity is laptop-first — everyone on an equal footing removes the room-dynamic dominance at its source. (A) reaches the same outcome but frames it as a prohibition rather than a design choice. (C) relies on manual discipline under pressure and does not change the underlying geometry. (D) makes one person responsible for fighting the format instead of fixing it.
