# Question

The PM is preparing the very first meeting for a new virtual team. Some members have worked together before, others are new to the organisation, and nobody has met the full group. What should the PM put FIRST on the agenda?

## Options

- **A**: A walkthrough of the project timeline and major deliverables so the team has shared context on the work.
- **B**: An immediate confirmation of roles and responsibilities so members know what they are accountable for.
- **C**: Personal introductions, a light icebreaker, and an initial conversation to establish working agreements before moving into project content.
- **D**: A briefing on the technical architecture so the team can start thinking about how the work will be built.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

For a brand-new virtual team, the first meeting's biggest leverage is building human connection and shared norms; project content lands much better once that exists. (C) does this. (A) jumps to content before the team is a team. (B) pins responsibilities to people the PM doesn't yet understand and locks decisions in too early. (D) prioritises technical context over the relational base that the rest of the project will rely on.
