# Question

In a virtual sprint retrospective, the Scrum Master notices that two team members from cultures that value collective harmony over individual challenge are silent, while three more direct members dominate the conversation. What technique would BEST level participation?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the silent members by name to share their views in turn.
- **B**: Open the retrospective with anonymous digital input — sticky notes or polls — before any open discussion.
- **C**: Extend the retrospective by 30 minutes to give the quieter members more time to speak up.
- **D**: Speak with the silent members individually after the retrospective to capture their feedback.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Anonymous input collected before discussion lets every member contribute without the social cost of speaking against the dominant voices, and gives the team a richer set of inputs to discuss. (B) does this. (A) puts cultural-minority members on the spot publicly, which can be deeply uncomfortable and rarely produces honest input. (C) gives more time to the same dynamic, which doesn't help. (D) collects the silent members' views privately and never lets the team hear them — sustaining the imbalance instead of correcting it.
