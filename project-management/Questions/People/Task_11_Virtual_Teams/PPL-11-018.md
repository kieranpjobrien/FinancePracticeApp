# Question

During a virtual sprint retrospective, the Scrum Master notices that team members from certain cultures remain silent while others dominate the discussion. What technique would BEST encourage balanced participation?

## Options

- **A**: Call on silent members directly to share their thoughts
- **B**: Use anonymous digital tools for input before open discussion
- **C**: Extend the meeting time to give everyone more opportunity
- **D**: Email the silent members afterward to get their input

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Anonymous input tools (digital sticky notes, polls, written responses) allow all team members to contribute without cultural barriers around speaking up, interrupting, or publicly disagreeing. This creates psychological safety and ensures ideas are heard before groupthink sets in.

Option A may cause discomfort and put individuals on the spot, which can be particularly challenging for team members from cultures that value collective harmony over individual expression.

Option C doesn't address the participation dynamic and may just extend the imbalance.

Option D excludes those members from the real-time discussion and doesn't help the team learn to collaborate better.
