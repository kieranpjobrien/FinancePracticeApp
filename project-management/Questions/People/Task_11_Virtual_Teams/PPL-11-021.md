# Question

A new developer joins a distributed agile team in the middle of a sprint. The team works mostly asynchronously with one daily standup. What should the PM (or Scrum Master) do to set them up for effective contribution?

## Options

- **A**: Pair them with a buddy, share recorded context from recent ceremonies, and bring them into events progressively.
- **B**: Hold them out of ceremonies until the next sprint so the current sprint is not disrupted by the onboarding work.
- **C**: Provision their accounts and tools, point them at the wiki, and let them learn the team through their first commits.
- **D**: Ask them to spend the first two weeks observing meetings without contributing, then start work in the next sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first approach to virtual onboarding combines a human guide, async-friendly catch-up material, and graduated participation. (A) does all three. (B) defers the joiner's involvement and signals they are an inconvenience to current work. (C) gives them tools without context or relationships, which leaves them isolated and likely to make low-quality first contributions. (D) is too passive for a virtual setting where the joiner needs proactive contact and early collaboration to feel part of the team.
