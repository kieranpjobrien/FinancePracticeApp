# Question

A new team member joins a distributed agile team mid-sprint. The team uses daily standups and works primarily asynchronously. What should the project manager do to support effective onboarding?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the next sprint to onboard them properly
- **B**: Assign a team buddy, provide recorded context, and include them gradually in ceremonies
- **C**: Give them full access to all systems and let them learn by doing
- **D**: Have them observe for two weeks before participating

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective virtual onboarding combines multiple approaches: a buddy provides human connection and a safe person to ask questions, recorded context (past meetings, documentation) helps them catch up asynchronously, and gradual inclusion in ceremonies helps them integrate without being overwhelmed.

Option A loses productivity and may make the new person feel unwelcome.

Option C provides tools but not context or relationships, leading to confusion and potential mistakes.

Option D is too passive and delays the integration that virtual teams need to be intentional about.
