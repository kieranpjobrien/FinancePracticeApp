# Question

The PM's virtual team has grown from 5 to 15 members in three months. The casual, ad-hoc communication that worked at five people is now causing missed updates and duplicated work. What should the PM put in place?

## Options

- **A**: A structured communication plan that defines which channel is used for what, the cadence of each, and what gets documented where.
- **B**: A weekly all-hands video call so everyone hears the same updates at the same time.
- **C**: A new team chat platform with channels per workstream so conversations are organised by topic.
- **D**: A single communication coordinator who collects updates and distributes them to the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The fix for scaled-up virtual communication is structure: clear channel purposes, cadences, and ownership. (A) does that. (B) is one possible mechanism but doesn't tell anyone where async information should live or how decisions are recorded. (C) buys a tool that without rules of use will simply move the noise from email into chat channels. (D) creates a single point of failure and does not scale beyond the next growth jump.
