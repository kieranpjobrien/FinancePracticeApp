# Question

A PM is starting a new project with a fully virtual team who have never met in person. The project is complex and will last 18 months. Budget allows for one in-person meeting during the project. When should the PM schedule it?

## Options

- **A**: At the end to celebrate completion
- **B**: At the kickoff to build relationships that will sustain virtual collaboration
- **C**: When problems arise to resolve conflict
- **D**: Never - virtual teams shouldn't need in-person meetings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Initial in-person time builds the relationship foundation that makes virtual collaboration work. Face-to-face kickoff creates personal connections, shared context, and rapport that sustain through 18 months of remote interaction. Once relationships are established, virtual communication becomes more effective.

Option A - by end, benefits are limited to celebration.

Option C - using the meeting reactively wastes its relationship-building potential.

Option D - while not strictly necessary, in-person time is highly valuable when available.
