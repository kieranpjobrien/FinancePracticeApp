# Question

A virtual team has been working together for six months and has strong working relationships. A major team restructure adds five new members and changes reporting lines. The PM notices the original team becoming cliquish and new members struggling to integrate. What should the PM prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Let the team self-organise and integrate naturally over time
- **B**: Replace the informal team structures with new formal processes
- **C**: Intentionally mix original and new members in work assignments and create structured integration activities
- **D**: Hold separate meetings for original and new team members initially

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Integration requires intentional action - mixed work assignments force collaboration and relationship building between groups. Structured integration activities (buddy systems, collaborative projects, team-building) accelerate what might otherwise take months. The goal is creating one team, not two subgroups.

Option A allows cliques to solidify and extends the integration period.

Option B may disrupt what's working without addressing the integration challenge.

Option D reinforces the division rather than bridging it.
