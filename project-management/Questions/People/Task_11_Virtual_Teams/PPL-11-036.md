# Question

After six months of strong working relationships, a virtual team has just been restructured: five new members have joined and reporting lines have changed. The original ten are visibly cliquish in meetings, and the new five are struggling to integrate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Deliberately mix original and new members in upcoming work, set up cross-cohort buddies, and run structured integration activities to accelerate one-team behaviour.
- **B**: Allow the team to find its own equilibrium over time; integration usually settles within a couple of months.
- **C**: Replace the informal practices the original team built with new formal processes that everyone is starting from at the same point.
- **D**: Hold separate orientation meetings for the new members for the first few weeks so they aren't overwhelmed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cohort integration is accelerated most reliably by mixing people in real work and adding lightweight social structures; PMI's people-first practice is to make this happen, not to wait for it. (A) does both. (B) lets cliques harden over the very weeks they're easiest to break. (C) discards practices that are working and risks alienating the original team without addressing the integration problem. (D) cements the divide it should be closing by keeping the new members in their own bubble.
