# Question

A virtual team has excellent individual contributors but poor collaboration. People complete their own work well but don't help each other or share knowledge. What should the PM do to improve virtual team collaboration?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that virtual teams are less collaborative by nature
- **B**: Create collaboration structures - pair programming, cross-training sessions, shared problem-solving forums, and team goals alongside individual goals
- **C**: Force collaboration by only assigning work to pairs
- **D**: Add collaboration as a performance metric and penalise those who don't collaborate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Collaboration doesn't happen automatically in virtual environments - it needs to be structured. Pairing creates natural collaboration. Cross-training builds shared knowledge. Forums for shared problem-solving create helping norms. Team goals alongside individual goals balance collaboration with individual contribution.

Option A accepts avoidable dysfunction.

Option C may be resisted and feels forced.

Option D creates compliance rather than genuine collaboration.
