# Question

A project manager notices that a virtual team member's communication style (very direct and brief) is being perceived as rude by other team members from cultures that value more relationship-oriented communication. The team member is unaware of the impact. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team member to change their communication style
- **B**: Explain to other team members that this is just a cultural difference
- **C**: Have a coaching conversation with the individual about cultural awareness while also helping the team understand different communication styles
- **D**: Assign the team member to work independently to avoid further issues

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This requires working both sides: helping the direct communicator understand impact and develop cultural awareness, while also helping the team interpret different styles charitably. The goal is mutual adaptation, not changing one person to match everyone else.

Option A places all burden on one person and implies their style is wrong.

Option B places all burden on the team without helping the individual grow.

Option D avoids the development opportunity and limits collaboration.
