# Question

A virtual team using Scrum finds that their daily standups are becoming status reports to the Scrum Master rather than team coordination discussions. Team members seem disengaged. What should be done?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel daily standups since they're not working
- **B**: Have the Scrum Master ask more engaging questions
- **C**: Refocus standups on team collaboration: what help is needed, what blockers exist, and who is working together today
- **D**: Reduce standups to twice per week

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The purpose of daily standups is team coordination, not status reporting. Refocusing on collaboration elements (dependencies, blockers, pairing opportunities) restores the value. Team members reporting to each other rather than the Scrum Master increases engagement and ownership.

Option A removes a valuable coordination mechanism rather than fixing it.

Option B keeps the dynamic of reporting to the Scrum Master.

Option D reduces frequency without addressing the fundamental problem.
