# Question

A virtual Scrum team's daily standup has become a series of one-by-one updates directed at the Scrum Master, with no real coordination among the team members. People are visibly switching off. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the Scrum Master ask better, more open questions of each team member to draw richer updates.
- **B**: Drop the daily standup to a twice-weekly cadence and use the time for deeper sessions.
- **C**: Cancel the standup and replace it with an asynchronous written update in the team's chat channel.
- **D**: Refocus the standup on coordination — what help is needed, what's blocking, who is pairing today — with team members talking to each other rather than the Scrum Master.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The standup is for the team's own coordination; the dynamic described has converted it into a status-report ritual. (D) restores the original purpose. (A) keeps the Scrum Master at the centre and treats the symptom (poor updates) without changing the dynamic. (B) reduces frequency without fixing the format and weakens day-to-day coordination. (C) eliminates a key sync moment that, when run well, is one of the team's most useful tools.
