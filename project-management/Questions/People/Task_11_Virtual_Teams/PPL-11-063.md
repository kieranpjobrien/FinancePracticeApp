# Question

The remote team has just delivered a major release after six months of hard work. In the previous co-located organisation the PM led, the team would have gone out for dinner together. The PM wants to genuinely recognise the achievement in a remote-appropriate way. What is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Send everyone an individual gift card and a personal thank-you email from the PM acknowledging each member's specific contribution on the release.
- **B**: Book an expensive in-person team offsite in a central city, even though travel will disrupt some members' personal lives over a weekend.
- **C**: Ask the team what kind of recognition would feel meaningful to them, and co-design the celebration around whatever they collectively propose.
- **D**: Host a surprise virtual party with catering delivered to each home, planned entirely by the PM and sponsor as a reward for the team.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's motivation and engagement principles say recognition is most meaningful when it is perceived as such by the recipients — asking the team honours that and fits remote contexts. (A) is a generic gesture that does not involve the team. (B) imposes a format that may be unwelcome and burdensome. (D) is kind but still a PM-driven guess rather than a team-driven one.
