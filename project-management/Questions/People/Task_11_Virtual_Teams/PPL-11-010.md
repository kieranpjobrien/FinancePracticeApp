# Question

What is the PRIMARY reason virtual teams typically need more structured communication than co-located teams?

## Options

- **A**: Virtual team members usually work in more specialised roles and require more formal coordination.
- **B**: The informal, spontaneous interactions that happen naturally in a shared office do not occur in a virtual setting.
- **C**: Virtual teams tend to rely on less reliable technology and need redundant communication channels.
- **D**: Virtual teams are typically larger than co-located teams and require clearer governance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The defining feature of virtual teams is the absence of unplanned contact — hallway conversations, overheard updates, whiteboard moments — and that's what structured communication has to consciously replace. (A) conflates role specialisation with team topology; a co-located team of specialists faces the same challenge. (C) makes an assumption about reliability that isn't generally true and isn't why PMI stresses structure. (D) conflates team size with working model; a small virtual team still needs structure, and a large co-located team often needs less.
