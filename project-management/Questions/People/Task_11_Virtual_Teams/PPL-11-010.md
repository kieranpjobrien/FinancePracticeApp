# Question

What is the PRIMARY reason virtual teams need more structured communication than co-located teams?

## Options

- **A**: Virtual team members are less capable
- **B**: Informal hallway conversations and spontaneous interactions don't happen naturally in virtual environments
- **C**: Virtual teams work fewer hours
- **D**: Technology is unreliable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Co-located teams benefit from spontaneous interactions - hallway conversations, overhearing discussions, lunch chats - that share information and build relationships without formal structure. Virtual teams miss these interactions entirely. Structure must replace what happens naturally in person: scheduled check-ins, explicit information sharing, dedicated relationship-building time.

Option A is incorrect and offensive.

Option C isn't generally true.

Option D isn't the primary reason for structured communication.
