# Question

A project manager leads a virtual team where two subgroups have formed: co-located team members in headquarters who have informal discussions, and remote workers who feel excluded from decisions. The PM notices decisions are often made before remote members can contribute. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Move all communication to asynchronous channels so everyone has equal access
- **B**: Tell headquarters staff to stop having informal discussions
- **C**: Establish that decisions require documented input from all relevant parties and create virtual spaces for informal connection
- **D**: Have remote workers relocate to headquarters

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This addresses both the formal decision-making process (requiring documented input ensures remote voices are heard) and the informal relationship gap (virtual spaces for casual connection). The goal is equity of participation, not elimination of informal interaction.

Option A is too restrictive and eliminates valuable informal communication rather than extending its benefits to remote workers.

Option B is unenforceable and removes beneficial collaboration rather than extending it.

Option D may not be feasible and doesn't build virtual team management capability the organisation likely needs.
