# Question

A hybrid project team has eight people in headquarters and six fully remote. The HQ group makes most of its decisions in informal corridor and lunch conversations, then announces them to the remote members. Remote members feel excluded and their contributions are visibly declining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that all project communication move to async channels so HQ and remote members start from equal footing.
- **B**: Ask HQ members to stop holding any work conversations outside scheduled meetings.
- **C**: Require that decisions of consequence be captured in a shared channel with input from all relevant members before being final, and create virtual spaces where informal conversation can happen across the whole team.
- **D**: Reassign one or two HQ members to remote work so the balance shifts and the HQ group is less dominant.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The fix needs to address both the formal decision flow (so remote members get a real say) and the informal channel that HQ enjoys (so the same connective tissue exists across the whole team). (C) does both. (A) bans synchronous discussion entirely, which is a heavy-handed loss of useful collaboration. (B) is unenforceable and tries to stop HQ from doing something good rather than extending the same benefit to others. (D) reshuffles people without changing the underlying behaviours and creates new disruption.
