# Question

A virtual team member is regularly unreachable for two- to three-hour blocks during their stated working hours. Their work output is on track, but other team members are frustrated by the slow responses. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Open a private conversation with the team member, then facilitate the team agreeing on shared response-time norms (e.g., "respond within four hours during working hours, signal deep-work blocks").
- **B**: Document the unresponsiveness as a performance issue and reflect it in the next performance review.
- **C**: Require all team members to be online and responsive throughout their full stated working hours.
- **D**: Tell the rest of the team to be more patient since the work itself is being delivered.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI-aligned response respects the individual's context, addresses the team's legitimate concern, and produces a clear shared norm. (A) does all three. (B) escalates to formal performance management when output is fine and the issue is collaboration norms — disproportionate. (C) removes the focus time most knowledge work needs and is rarely sustainable. (D) dismisses the team's valid concern and lets the friction continue rather than resolving it.
