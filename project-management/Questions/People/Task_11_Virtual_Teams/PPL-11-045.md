# Question

A virtual team has been together for two years and has developed strong trust and informal communication patterns. A new project manager takes over and wants to implement more formal communication processes used in their previous organisation. Team members are resistant. What should the new PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the new processes immediately since they're proven effective
- **B**: Abandon the idea and adapt to the existing team culture
- **C**: Understand what's working well, identify any gaps, and co-create improvements with the team
- **D**: Gradually introduce changes without discussing them with the team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Established teams have developed practices that work for them. A new leader should first understand what's working before changing things. If there are gaps (documentation, stakeholder communication, etc.), co-creating solutions with the team respects their experience while addressing real needs.

Option A ignores team context and likely creates resistance.

Option B misses the opportunity to bring valuable experience and address potential gaps.

Option D undermines trust with the new team.
