# Question

A new PM takes over a virtual team that has worked together for two years. The team has strong trust and effective informal practices. The new PM wants to introduce more formal communication and reporting processes from their previous organisation. The team is openly resistant. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Spend the first few weeks understanding what's working, identify any genuine gaps with the team, then co-create improvements rather than imposing the previous organisation's processes.
- **B**: Introduce the new processes immediately; they have a strong track record and the team will adapt once they see the results.
- **C**: Defer to the existing team practices and abandon the planned process changes.
- **D**: Introduce the new processes one at a time without explicit discussion so they become normal before resistance can build.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle and good change management both say to start with understanding the existing context, then co-design changes with the team. (A) does this. (B) ignores why the team is resistant and treats imported practices as universal — they often aren't. (C) gives up on improvements that may genuinely be needed. (D) introduces change by stealth, which damages trust the moment the team notices, and they will.
