# Question

A sponsor regularly asks the PM of a remote Scrum team to provide real-time answers on what each individual team member is currently working on. The PM finds themselves constantly pulling developers out of focus time to check. The team has started to resent it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push back firmly and tell the sponsor that this level of real-time individual visibility is inappropriate and inconsistent with the principles of agile team autonomy.
- **B**: Continue providing the real-time updates as requested and ask the team to accept the ongoing interruptions as part of working on a high-visibility strategic programme.
- **C**: Set up a personal tracker the PM maintains from standup notes so the sponsor can be answered quickly without any further direct interruption of the team.
- **D**: Make the team's board, standup notes and release burndown self-serve for the sponsor, and agree a cadence of summary updates that meets their real need.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI-aligned transparency provides self-serve artefacts plus a clear reporting cadence — this meets the sponsor's underlying need without taxing the team. (A) refuses engagement rather than reshaping it. (B) preserves a practice that damages focus and morale. (C) fabricates a separate tracker the PM must maintain, creating a single point of failure and a second source of truth.
