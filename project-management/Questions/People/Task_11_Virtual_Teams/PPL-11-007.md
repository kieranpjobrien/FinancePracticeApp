# Question

What is a key challenge that is unique to virtual teams and that the project manager must deliberately address?

## Options

- **A**: The need to specify clear scope and acceptance criteria for every project deliverable at the outset.
- **B**: The loss of informal interactions and tacit information-sharing that happen naturally in a shared office.
- **C**: The pressure to deliver projects within fixed budgets and approved schedules set by the sponsor.
- **D**: The need to manage stakeholder expectations across multiple business units and functional areas.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Virtual teams lose the hallway conversations, overheard discussions, and lunch interactions that quietly transmit context and build relationships in co-located teams; PMs have to design replacements for that. (A), (C), and (D) are all real PM challenges, but they apply just as much to co-located teams; they aren't unique to a virtual setting. The distinguishing feature of virtual work is the absence of unstructured contact.
