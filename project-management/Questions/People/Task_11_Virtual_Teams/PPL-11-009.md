# Question

A technical disagreement on a virtual team has spilled into increasingly hostile email exchanges over four days. Other team members have been copied in but are staying out of it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pull the conversation off email and set up a facilitated video call to address the issue and reset norms.
- **B**: Reply on the email thread reminding both parties of the team's communication standards and asking them to take it offline.
- **C**: Speak with each party separately to understand their perspective, then convey the other's position back to them.
- **D**: Wait for the dispute to resolve itself; intervening too early may inflame the situation further and damage trust.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Email magnifies conflict because it strips out tone and gives time to compose escalating responses; PMI expects the PM to actively manage team conflict rather than route around it. (A) does both jobs at once. (B) treats the medium issue but doesn't address the substance or the damage already done. (C) keeps the parties apart, which is a temporary calm but never restores the working relationship. (D) hopes the situation will improve while real harm continues to compound — exactly the conflict-management failure PMI warns against.
