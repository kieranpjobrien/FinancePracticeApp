# Question

A team member based in Singapore is being routinely left out of decisions because the relevant discussions happen in synchronous meetings during European business hours. The PM has only just realised the pattern. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate meeting times across time zones and require important decisions to include async input from anyone unable to attend live.
- **B**: Ask the Singapore team member to dial in to the European meetings, even outside business hours, so they can participate.
- **C**: Have the Singapore team member's Europe-based manager represent their views in the meetings going forward.
- **D**: Accept that distributed teams have unavoidable participation gaps and focus on improving the quality of post-meeting notes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's virtual-team practice expects the burden of inclusion to be shared and supported by both rotating live time and async participation paths. (A) does this. (B) places the burden permanently on one person, which is unsustainable and signals their input is optional. (C) creates a proxy who speaks for the absent member rather than enabling their direct contribution. (D) accepts a preventable exclusion problem as if it were a fixed constraint.
