# Question

A virtual team has lost two long-tenured members in a quarter, and much of what they knew about the product existed only in chat threads and their heads. The remaining team is anxious the same will happen again. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a lightweight knowledge-capture practice — living runbooks and decision records — and weave it into the team's regular working rhythm.
- **B**: Require departing members to spend their final two weeks writing comprehensive documentation on every area of the product they personally touched.
- **C**: Hire a dedicated technical writer to shadow the team continuously and externally document everything the team produces on an ongoing basis.
- **D**: Record every virtual meeting by default and archive transcripts indefinitely so future members can search back through the team's historical conversations.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's approach to knowledge management is to make it part of the flow of work, not a last-minute or external activity — living runbooks and ADRs build institutional memory as work happens. (B) captures only what leavers remember to write and under time pressure. (C) separates knowing from doing and rarely keeps pace with a working team. (D) produces a haystack rather than searchable, curated knowledge.
