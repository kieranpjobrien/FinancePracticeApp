# Question

A project team spans three countries with no overlapping business hours. Team members have never met in person and primarily communicate asynchronously. Team cohesion is weak, and there are frequent misunderstandings. What should the project manager prioritize?

## Options

- **A**: Require at least some team members to work outside normal hours for synchronous meetings
- **B**: Document all communications in writing to create clear records and reduce misunderstandings
- **C**: Invest in relationship-building through video calls during the limited overlap time, clear communication protocols, and occasional virtual social events
- **D**: Reorganize the team to have fewer time zones involved

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Virtual teams need intentional relationship-building since informal connection opportunities don't happen naturally. Using available overlap time for video (not just audio) helps build human connection. Clear protocols reduce misunderstandings in async work. Virtual social events create non-work interaction that builds trust.

Option A places unfair burden on team members and may cause resentment and burnout. While some flexibility may be needed, regular off-hours work isn't sustainable.

Option B addresses documentation but doesn't build relationships or team cohesion. Written communication alone can increase distance and misunderstanding without the relationship foundation.

Option D may not be feasible and doesn't address the skills needed to work virtually. The underlying challenge of virtual team management remains.
