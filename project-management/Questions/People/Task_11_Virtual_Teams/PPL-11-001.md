# Question

A project team spans three countries with no overlapping business hours. Members have never met in person, communicate almost entirely asynchronously, and the team feels low on cohesion. Misunderstandings are surfacing weekly. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Require team members in two of the three time zones to overlap working hours so the team can hold daily synchronous calls.
- **B**: Mandate written-only communication so every interaction has a permanent, unambiguous record.
- **C**: Use the small overlap window for short video stand-ups, agree clear async protocols, and add occasional virtual social time to build relationships.
- **D**: Reorganise the work so each country owns end-to-end pieces and depends on the other countries as little as possible.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's virtual-team guidance favours intentional relationship-building plus crisp async protocols, with synchronous time used sparingly and well. (C) does all of that without overburdening any one location. (A) places an unfair burden on specific time zones and tends to breed resentment and burnout. (B) deepens the cohesion problem by stripping the warmth out of every interaction. (D) reduces collaboration friction by reducing collaboration itself, which sacrifices the cross-pollination that distributed teams exist to enable.
