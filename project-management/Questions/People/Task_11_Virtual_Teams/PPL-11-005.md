# Question

A virtual team has members in Australia, India, and the UK. Team cohesion is suffering because members don't know each other personally and only interact in formal meetings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that virtual teams can't have strong relationships
- **B**: Require all team members to relocate to one office
- **C**: Create opportunities for informal interaction, such as virtual coffee chats, team-building activities, or shared social channels
- **D**: Add more formal meetings to increase contact time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Relationships form through informal interaction, not just work discussions. Virtual teams need intentional opportunities to connect as people - virtual coffee breaks, non-work chat channels, online team activities. These build the trust and rapport that support effective collaboration.

Option A gives up on something achievable with intentional effort.

Option B is often impractical and eliminates the benefits of distributed teams.

Option D adds more formal interaction but doesn't create the informal connections that build relationships.
