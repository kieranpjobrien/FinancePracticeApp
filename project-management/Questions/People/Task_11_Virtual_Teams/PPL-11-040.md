# Question

A virtual team is hitting all its delivery targets, but the latest engagement survey shows several team members feel isolated and disconnected from the wider organisation. The sponsor is worried about retention. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run intentional connection activities — virtual social moments, cross-team exposure, and career-focused 1:1s — alongside delivery.
- **B**: Focus on sustaining the strong delivery and treat the engagement scores as an organisation-wide problem, not a project one.
- **C**: Require the team to spend a fixed number of days each month in a company office to rebuild a sense of belonging.
- **D**: Recommend a compensation increase for team members who appear most at risk of leaving the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Isolation in high-performing remote teams is a real and well-known risk; the response is deliberate connection that sits alongside delivery, not at its expense. (A) does that. (B) ignores a clear early signal until it converts into a delivery problem via attrition. (C) is heavy-handed, may not be feasible for everyone, and treats office time as a substitute for actual connection. (D) addresses pay rather than belonging and rarely fixes engagement issues at their root.
