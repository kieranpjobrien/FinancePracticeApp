# Question

A virtual team is highly productive but several team members report feeling isolated and disconnected from the organisation. Their work is excellent, but engagement survey scores are low. The organisation is concerned about retention. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Focus on the high productivity and accept some isolation as a trade-off of remote work
- **B**: Require the team to come into an office periodically
- **C**: Implement intentional connection activities: virtual social events, cross-team projects, and one-on-ones focused on career and wellbeing
- **D**: Increase compensation to improve retention

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Isolation is a risk for virtual teams even when productivity is high. Intentional connection through social events, exposure to other teams, and meaningful one-on-ones addresses the human need for belonging. This sustains long-term engagement and retention without sacrificing the benefits of virtual work.

Option A ignores a real problem that will likely affect future productivity and retention.

Option B may help but isn't necessary if virtual connection is done well, and may not be possible for all team members.

Option D treats symptoms rather than causes; money doesn't address the need for connection.
