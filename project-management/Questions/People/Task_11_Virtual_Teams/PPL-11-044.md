# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of recording virtual team meetings?

## Options

- **A**: Proving who said what in case of disputes
- **B**: Monitoring team member participation
- **C**: Allowing team members in different time zones or who missed the meeting to catch up asynchronously
- **D**: Creating documentation for project audits

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Recording enables asynchronous participation, which is essential for global virtual teams. Team members who couldn't attend due to time zones, conflicts, or illness can watch later and still understand discussions and decisions. This supports inclusion without requiring everyone to be present.

Option A creates a defensive rather than collaborative culture.

Option B implies surveillance rather than support.

Option D is a secondary benefit, not the primary purpose.
