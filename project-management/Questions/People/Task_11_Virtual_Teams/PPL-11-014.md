# Question

A virtual team has developed "us vs them" dynamics between two geographic locations. Each group socialises internally and views the other with suspicion. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that geographic separation naturally creates sub-groups
- **B**: Create cross-location pairs for work assignments, mixed-location social events, and shared objectives that require collaboration across locations
- **C**: Have the locations compete to drive performance
- **D**: Centralise all decision-making to remove location-based power dynamics

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Geographic tribalism is common but manageable. Break down barriers through structure: pair people across locations for assignments, host virtual events that mix locations, create goals that require cross-location collaboration. Increased positive interaction builds relationships that counter "us vs them" thinking.

Option A accepts harmful dynamics that reduce effectiveness.

Option C intensifies the division.

Option D removes autonomy without building relationship.
