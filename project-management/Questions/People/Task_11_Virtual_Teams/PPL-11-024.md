# Question

A project manager notices that a virtual team member in a different country has been working excessive hours, including nights and weekends, to attend meetings scheduled during the PM's business hours. The team member hasn't complained but seems increasingly tired. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing - the team member hasn't raised a concern
- **B**: Praise their dedication and commitment to the project
- **C**: Rotate meeting times to share the burden and check on the team member's wellbeing
- **D**: Tell them they don't need to attend all meetings

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM has a responsibility for team wellbeing, even when team members don't complain. Rotating meeting times distributes inconvenience fairly across the team. Checking on wellbeing shows care and opens dialogue. This also models that sustainability matters more than presenteeism.

Option A ignores visible signs of a problem and potential burnout.

Option B reinforces unsustainable behaviour and may discourage them from setting boundaries.

Option D may be seen as exclusion from important discussions without addressing the underlying fairness issue.
