# Question

A virtual team member has been keeping their camera off in every meeting for the last two weeks, citing "bandwidth issues." Other team members have started doing the same and the PM senses meetings are becoming less engaged. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Update the team's working agreement to require cameras on for all meetings under 60 minutes.
- **B**: Hold a private conversation with the team member to understand the real reason and discuss what would help.
- **C**: Treat camera use as personal preference and accept the new norm without intervention.
- **D**: Arrange for the team member to be issued a higher-bandwidth internet connection at the project's cost.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The stated reason may be technical, may be cover for something else (meeting fatigue, home environment, mental health), and PMI's people-first stance is to engage privately before changing rules or making assumptions. (B) does that. (A) imposes a team-wide rule without diagnosis and may make the underlying problem worse. (C) accepts a clear engagement signal as fixed without ever asking why. (D) jumps to a technical solution before knowing whether the problem is technical, and the cost may be wasted.
