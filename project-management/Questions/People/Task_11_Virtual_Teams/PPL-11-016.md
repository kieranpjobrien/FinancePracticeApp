# Question

A virtual team member consistently keeps their camera off during video meetings, claiming bandwidth issues. Other team members have started doing the same, and the PM notices reduced engagement and connection. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that all cameras must be on during meetings
- **B**: Have a private conversation to understand the real reason and explore solutions
- **C**: Accept camera-off as the new team norm
- **D**: Require the team member to upgrade their internet connection

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Before taking action, the PM should understand the situation. The stated reason may be genuine (bandwidth) or may mask other issues (meeting fatigue, home environment concerns, cultural preferences). A private conversation allows exploration of solutions like partial camera use, alternative internet options, or understanding if there are other concerns.

Option A may be counterproductive without understanding the root cause and could feel punitive.

Option C accepts reduced engagement without trying to improve the situation.

Option D assumes the problem is technical and may not be feasible or address the real issue.
