# Question

A junior team member who used to be confident in person now goes silent in video meetings with senior stakeholders. They tell the PM in a 1:1 that they "freeze up" once the meeting starts. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Speak on the junior member's behalf in stakeholder meetings to make sure their input gets heard.
- **B**: Excuse them from senior stakeholder meetings until they've built more experience and confidence.
- **C**: Tell them that speaking up is a core expectation of the role and that they need to push through the discomfort.
- **D**: Create lower-stakes ways for them to contribute — written input ahead of meetings, chat comments during them, smaller break-outs — and grow their confidence over time.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leadership stance is to remove impediments and develop people, not to speak for them or push them off the field. (D) does both at once. (A) takes their voice away and prevents them ever building the skill. (B) protects them from the situation but blocks the development they need to grow. (C) ignores the real barrier and risks coming across as dismissive of a problem the team member has been candid about.
