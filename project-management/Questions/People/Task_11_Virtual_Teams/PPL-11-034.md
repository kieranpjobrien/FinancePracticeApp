# Question

A virtual team member in a junior role hesitates to speak up in video meetings with senior stakeholders, even when they have valuable input. In person, they were more confident. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Speak on their behalf in meetings
- **B**: Remove them from meetings with senior stakeholders
- **C**: Create opportunities for them to contribute in ways that feel safer, and build their confidence gradually
- **D**: Tell them they need to speak up to be successful

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Virtual meetings can amplify existing power dynamics because interruption is harder and non-verbal encouragement is less visible. Creating safer contribution methods (written input before meetings, chat comments during meetings, smaller group discussions) while gradually building confidence addresses the real barrier.

Option A removes their voice entirely and doesn't build their capability.

Option B excludes them from important discussions and limits their development.

Option D ignores the real challenge they're facing and may feel dismissive.
