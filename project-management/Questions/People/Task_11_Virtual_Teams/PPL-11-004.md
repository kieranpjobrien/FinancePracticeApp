# Question

A virtual team's meetings tend to converge on the views of two confident senior members. After meetings, two quieter members frequently DM the PM with strong, well-reasoned objections that never made it into the room. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Require every team member to verbalise an opinion in the meeting before any decision is finalised.
- **B**: Coach the quieter members on speaking up under pressure and following up assertively after meetings.
- **C**: Restructure facilitation: gather written input ahead, run round-robin questioning, and add an async window for dissent.
- **D**: Treat decisions made in the meeting as final and ask DM senders to raise their views in the next meeting instead.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to design facilitation that surfaces all perspectives, particularly in virtual settings where participation imbalances are easy to mask. (C) does this on both sides of the meeting. (A) forces synchronous speaking and tends to produce shallow agreement rather than considered input. (B) puts the burden on the quieter members to adapt to a flawed process. (D) ratifies the existing imbalance and silences valuable input by procedure.
