# Question

A virtual team has been working together for several months. The team generally functions well but decisions made in meetings often don't reflect input from certain quieter members, who later express different views in chat. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Require all opinions to be stated in meetings
- **B**: Adjust meeting facilitation to gather input from all members, and supplement with async input mechanisms that give quieter members time to formulate and share thoughts
- **C**: Coach the quieter members on speaking up in meetings
- **D**: Weight decisions toward the opinions expressed in meetings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Different people have different communication preferences. Some think out loud; others need time to process. Effective virtual facilitation includes both synchronous techniques (round-robin, explicit calls for input) and asynchronous mechanisms (pre-meeting input, post-meeting comments). This gets all perspectives, not just the loudest.

Option A forces participation in a format that may not work for everyone.

Option C puts the burden on quieter members to change rather than the system to accommodate.

Option D systematically underweights certain team members' input, losing valuable perspectives.
