# Question

A project manager discovers that two virtual team members have been in conflict for weeks but neither reported it. The conflict has started affecting deliverables as they avoid collaborating. Both are in different countries with different cultural approaches to conflict. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both parties together on a video call to resolve the conflict immediately
- **B**: Speak with each party individually first, then facilitate a resolution considering cultural preferences
- **C**: Assign their work to other team members to eliminate the need for collaboration
- **D**: Escalate to HR since the conflict has affected work

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Individual conversations first allow the PM to understand each perspective without the pressure of the other party present. Cultural awareness matters: some cultures prefer direct confrontation while others find it deeply uncomfortable. The PM can then facilitate resolution in a culturally appropriate way (direct mediation, shuttle diplomacy, or a combination).

Option A assumes direct confrontation works for all cultures and may escalate rather than resolve the conflict.

Option C avoids the issue and may limit project options; it also doesn't resolve the underlying problem.

Option D may be premature and could damage trust with both team members.
