# Question

A virtual team member consistently delivers work late and blames technical issues (internet problems, software crashes). The PM suspects the real issue is something else. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Provide better technical equipment
- **B**: Document the late deliveries and issue a formal warning
- **C**: Have a supportive conversation to understand what's really happening and offer help
- **D**: Assign their work to more reliable team members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Technical excuses often mask other issues: unclear requirements, skill gaps, personal challenges, workload problems, or disengagement. A supportive conversation that seeks to understand rather than blame creates safety for honest discussion and may reveal addressable root causes.

Option A addresses the stated problem, not the likely real problem.

Option B may be premature and doesn't address root causes.

Option D doesn't solve the underlying issue and reduces the team member's value.
