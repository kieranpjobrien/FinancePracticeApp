# Question

A virtual team member has missed three deadlines in the last two months and each time blames a technical issue — internet outage, software crash, login problem. The PM doubts technology is really the cause. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Order them an upgraded laptop and a higher-grade internet connection at the project's cost.
- **B**: Hold a private supportive conversation to understand what's actually going on and offer help.
- **C**: Issue a documented warning that the next missed deadline will trigger formal performance management.
- **D**: Quietly move their deliverables onto more reliable team members so the schedule is protected.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Repeated technical excuses often mask other issues — workload, unclear requirements, personal problems, or disengagement — and PMI's people-first stance is to seek understanding before applying consequences. (B) does that. (A) treats the stated problem and may be a wasted spend. (C) skips understanding and applies a sanction that may completely miss the real cause. (D) shields the team member at the cost of the rest of the team and leaves the underlying issue untouched.
