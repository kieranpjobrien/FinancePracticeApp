# Question

A project manager discovers that two key stakeholders - the CFO and CTO - have fundamentally different visions for the project outcome. The CFO wants maximum cost reduction through automation, while the CTO wants to build a scalable platform for future innovation. Both have equal organizational authority and strong opinions. The project charter doesn't clearly prioritize between these objectives. What is the BEST course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Present both visions to the project sponsor and request a decision on priority
- **B**: Design a solution that partially addresses both visions through compromise
- **C**: Facilitate a session with both stakeholders to develop shared success criteria and explore if both objectives can be achieved
- **D**: Proceed with the CFO's vision since cost reduction provides more immediate business value

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before escalating or compromising, the PM should attempt to find alignment through facilitated dialogue. The stakeholders may discover their visions aren't mutually exclusive - automation could reduce costs AND provide a foundation for innovation. Developing shared success criteria creates common ground and may reveal a path forward that satisfies both. This demonstrates servant leadership and problem-solving skills.

Option A (escalation) is premature. The PM should first attempt to facilitate resolution. Also, escalating an unresolved conflict puts the sponsor in a difficult position and may damage stakeholder relationships.

Option B (compromise) may result in a solution that doesn't fully achieve either objective - a lose-lose outcome. Before compromising, explore whether a win-win solution exists.

Option D makes an assumption about business value and overrides stakeholder authority. The PM shouldn't unilaterally decide between equal stakeholders' priorities based on personal judgment.
