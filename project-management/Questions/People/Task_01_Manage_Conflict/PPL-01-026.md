# Question

Two senior developers disagree on whether to build with microservices or extend the monolith. Both have credible technical arguments, the team is divided and the decision is significant. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the architecture call themselves to end the debate.
- **B**: Run a structured evaluation against project requirements, surface trade-offs, and guide the team to consensus.
- **C**: Defer the decision indefinitely until one side gives way.
- **D**: Have the technical lead make the call and inform the team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is collaborating tied to objective criteria — the PMI default for legitimate technical disagreements. (A) is forcing and discounts technical expertise the team owns. (C) lets the conflict simmer and blocks delivery. (D) is escalation that bypasses the people doing the work and can be appropriate only after a structured evaluation has failed.
