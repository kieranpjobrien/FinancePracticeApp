# Question

Two senior developers disagree on whether to use microservices or a monolithic architecture. Both have valid technical arguments and the decision will significantly impact the project. The team is divided. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision themselves to end the debate
- **B**: Facilitate a structured evaluation session comparing both approaches against project requirements, then guide the team to consensus
- **C**: Flip a coin to appear impartial
- **D**: Delay the decision indefinitely until one developer gives up

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a legitimate technical disagreement requiring a collaborative approach. Facilitate objective evaluation against requirements (scalability needs, team expertise, timeline constraints). This respects both experts, involves the team, and results in a defensible decision based on project needs rather than personalities.

Option A undermines technical expertise and may cause resentment.

Option C is not a professional decision-making method for significant technical choices.

Option D causes project delays and frustration.
