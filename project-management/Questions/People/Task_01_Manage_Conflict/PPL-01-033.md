# Question

An influential stakeholder is demanding changes that conflict with the agreed project scope and the project sponsor's direction. The stakeholder has a history of going around the PM directly to executives. How should the PM handle this conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Give in to avoid political problems
- **B**: Ignore the stakeholder since they're outside the governance structure
- **C**: Document the request, align with the sponsor on response, then facilitate a meeting with all parties to discuss the request through proper change control
- **D**: Confront the stakeholder about their inappropriate behaviour

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This requires both political awareness and process discipline. Document to create a clear record. Align with sponsor to ensure unified front. Facilitate a proper governance discussion where the request can be evaluated on merit through change control. This respects the stakeholder while maintaining process integrity.

Option A undermines governance and rewards inappropriate behaviour.

Option B ignores stakeholder influence and creates an adversary.

Option D is confrontational and may escalate the political situation.
