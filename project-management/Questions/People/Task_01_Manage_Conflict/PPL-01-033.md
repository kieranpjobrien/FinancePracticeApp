# Question

An influential stakeholder is demanding changes that conflict with the approved scope and the sponsor's direction, and has a history of going over the PM's head to executives. How should the PM handle this conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the changes to keep the politics manageable.
- **B**: Ignore the stakeholder since they sit outside the governance structure.
- **C**: Document the request, align with the sponsor on the response, then facilitate a meeting where the request goes through formal change control.
- **D**: Confront the stakeholder about going around governance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) combines political awareness with process discipline — the PMI position that changes always go through change control regardless of stakeholder seniority. (A) rewards bypassing governance and erodes the baseline. (B) makes an adversary of an influential stakeholder. (D) is confrontational and likely to escalate the politics rather than resolve them.
