# Question

During a project status meeting, a team member publicly blames another team member for a missed deadline. The accused team member becomes defensive and the discussion escalates. What should the project manager do IMMEDIATELY?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the discussion to continue so team members can express their frustrations
- **B**: Calmly intervene, redirect the conversation to problem-solving, and address the interpersonal issue privately after the meeting
- **C**: End the meeting immediately and reschedule when emotions have cooled
- **D**: Defend the accused team member to show support

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM should immediately de-escalate by calmly redirecting the discussion from blame to solutions. Public blame sessions are unproductive and damage team dynamics. However, completely avoiding the issue isn't appropriate either - the interpersonal conflict should be addressed privately where both parties can speak openly without an audience. This approach maintains meeting productivity while acknowledging the conflict needs resolution.

Option A allows destructive behavior to continue, setting a poor precedent and potentially damaging trust and psychological safety within the team.

Option C (withdrawing/avoiding) delays the meeting unnecessarily. The PM can de-escalate and continue productively. Completely stopping signals loss of control and doesn't address the immediate situation.

Option D takes sides publicly, which undermines the PM's role as a neutral facilitator and may escalate the conflict further. Even if the accusation seems unfair, defending one party publicly damages the PM's credibility with the other.
