# Question

During a status meeting, one team member publicly blames another for a missed deadline. The accused becomes defensive and the exchange escalates in front of stakeholders. What should the project manager do IMMEDIATELY?

## Options

- **A**: Let the discussion continue so the frustration is aired.
- **B**: Defend the accused in front of the room to signal support.
- **C**: Calmly intervene, steer the conversation back to solutions, and address the interpersonal issue privately afterwards.
- **D**: End the meeting on the spot and reschedule when tempers have cooled.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) de-escalates in public, preserves meeting productivity, and reserves the interpersonal conversation for a private setting — the PMI pattern for managing conflict live. (A) allows destructive behaviour and harms psychological safety. (B) takes sides publicly and compromises the PM's neutrality. (D) is an avoidance move; the situation can be managed without walking away, and ending the meeting signals loss of control.
