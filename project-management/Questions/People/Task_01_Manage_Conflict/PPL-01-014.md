# Question

A new team member regularly questions decisions and approaches that the established team takes for granted. The original members are getting frustrated with what feels like constant pushback. How should the project manager view this situation?

## Options

- **A**: The new member is being disruptive and needs to learn how the team operates.
- **B**: The original team should simply be more open to new ideas regardless of merit.
- **C**: This is potentially healthy conflict; facilitate an open discussion to evaluate which challenges have substance.
- **D**: The new member should observe quietly for a few months before offering opinions.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) treats task-level conflict as a source of value when handled well — fresh eyes often spot assumptions the team has normalised, and structured discussion separates the useful challenges from the noise. (A) and (D) suppress potentially valuable input and harm psychological safety. (B) takes sides without evaluating the substance of the challenges.
