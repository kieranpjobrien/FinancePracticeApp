# Question

A newly joined team member frequently challenges decisions made by the established team, questioning approaches that have worked well. The original team members are becoming frustrated with the constant questioning. What should the project manager recognize about this situation?

## Options

- **A**: The new member is being disruptive and should adapt to existing practices
- **B**: This may be healthy conflict that brings fresh perspective; facilitate constructive dialogue to evaluate which challenges have merit
- **C**: The original team should be more welcoming to new ideas
- **D**: The new member should wait longer before offering opinions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Fresh perspectives can identify issues that established teams have normalized. Rather than suppressing or forcing acceptance, facilitate constructive evaluation of the challenges. Some may reveal genuine improvements; others may confirm existing approaches have good reasons. Both outcomes add value.

Option A dismisses potentially valuable input because it creates discomfort.

Option C takes sides without evaluating the actual challenges on merit.

Option D suppresses input that might be valuable because of tenure, which discourages psychological safety for newer members.
