# Question

A long-standing conflict between two team members has escalated to the point where they refuse to work together. Both are high performers critical to the project. Previous mediation attempts have failed. The project deadline is approaching. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove one team member from the project
- **B**: Restructure work assignments to minimise direct interaction while pursuing formal conflict resolution through HR, and document the impact on project performance
- **C**: Force them to work together and let them sort it out
- **D**: Ignore the conflict and hope professionalism prevails

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When informal mediation fails, a dual approach is needed. Short-term: restructure work to enable project progress while minimising friction. Long-term: escalate to HR for formal intervention (this is beyond PM's authority to resolve). Document impact for stakeholder awareness. This protects the project while addressing the root issue through appropriate channels.

Option A loses valuable resources without addressing the underlying issue.

Option C has already failed and may create a hostile work environment.

Option D allows the situation to continue deteriorating.
