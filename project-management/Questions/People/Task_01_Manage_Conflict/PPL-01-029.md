# Question

A long-running conflict between two team members has reached the point where they refuse to work together. Both are high performers critical to delivery and previous mediation has failed. The deadline is close. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove one of them from the project to break the deadlock.
- **B**: Force them to keep working together and let them sort it out.
- **C**: Ignore the conflict and rely on professionalism to carry the deadline.
- **D**: Restructure work to minimise direct interaction in the short term while engaging HR for formal resolution and documenting project impact.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the dual-track PMI response when team-level mediation has failed: protect short-term delivery and engage the right authority for the underlying issue. (A) loses a critical performer without addressing the underlying problem. (B) repeats the failed approach. (C) lets the situation continue to deteriorate, which is not leadership.
