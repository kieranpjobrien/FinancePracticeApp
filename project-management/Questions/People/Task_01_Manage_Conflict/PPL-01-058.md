# Question

Your SAFe release train has three teams quietly blaming each other for an integration failure. No one will name the issue directly but stand-ups are strained and the program increment objectives are at risk. The next PI planning is in two weeks. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run a blameless root-cause review with representatives from all three teams before the next PI planning, focused on system behaviour rather than individual fault.
- **B**: Use the next PI planning session itself to surface the issue in front of all train participants so that the discomfort forces honest conversation across the teams.
- **C**: Speak with each team lead individually and ask them to own the part of the integration failure that sits clearly inside their own team boundary.
- **D**: Leave the dynamic alone and focus on the next PI objectives, trusting that a fresh start will dilute the tension between the three teams naturally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) surfaces a silent conflict using a blameless structured method that separates system from person — the PMI root-cause pattern applied to team dynamics. (B) turns PI planning into a conflict forum it was not designed for and risks escalating rather than resolving. (C) asks each lead to accept partial blame before the issue has been diagnosed and reinforces the very blame pattern at play. (D) is avoidance; the PI objectives are already at risk, so hoping for dilution is not a plan.
