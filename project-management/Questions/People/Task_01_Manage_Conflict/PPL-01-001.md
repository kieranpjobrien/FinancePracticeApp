# Question

During sprint planning, two senior developers disagree heatedly about whether to build a new feature as a microservice or extend the existing monolith. Other team members are visibly uncomfortable and the meeting has stalled. As project manager, what should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend the meeting and ask the technical lead to make the architectural call offline.
- **B**: Pause the debate and ask both developers to summarise their positions against the project's non-functional requirements.
- **C**: Take a vote among the development team to settle the disagreement quickly.
- **D**: Defer the decision to the next sprint so the team can continue with other planned work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI-aligned move: refocus the conflict on objective project criteria so the team collaborates on a problem-solving outcome rather than positions. (A) outsources a decision the team owns and bypasses people doing the work. (C) reduces a technical trade-off to popularity rather than analysis against requirements. (D) avoids the conflict and lets it resurface, eroding psychological safety. PMI favours collaborating/problem-solving over forcing, smoothing, or withdrawing.
