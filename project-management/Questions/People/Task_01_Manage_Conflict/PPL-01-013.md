# Question

Two team members have a personal conflict that originated outside work. It's now affecting their professional collaboration - they avoid working together and communicate only through intermediaries. Their skills are complementary and both are needed on critical work. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign work so they never need to collaborate directly
- **B**: Have a direct conversation with both, acknowledging the personal nature while establishing that professional collaboration is required, and facilitate agreement on working norms
- **C**: Report the situation to HR for resolution
- **D**: Ignore the personal conflict and focus only on work deliverables

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM cannot solve personal conflicts but can establish professional expectations. A direct conversation acknowledges reality (there's tension), sets clear expectations (collaboration is required), and creates practical agreements (how they'll work together professionally despite personal feelings).

Option A accommodates the conflict at the cost of team effectiveness and sets precedent that personal conflicts excuse professional duties.

Option C may be appropriate for serious issues (harassment, etc.) but escalates what might be manageable at the team level. Try resolution first.

Option D ignores a problem that's actively impacting work. The conflict is already affecting deliverables through avoidance and communication breakdown.
