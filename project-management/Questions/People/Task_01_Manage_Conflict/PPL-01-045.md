# Question

A serious conflict has emerged between your project team and the client's team over interpretation of contract terms. Legal teams have engaged on both sides. The working relationship has broken down, but the project must continue. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend all project work completely until the legal dispute between the parties is fully resolved.
- **B**: Continue uncontested work, use neutral channels, document carefully, and protect relationships.
- **C**: Side openly with your organisation's legal position in every team interaction to show solidarity.
- **D**: Try to resolve the contract dispute yourself directly with the client to keep the lawyers out of it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) ring-fences the legal dispute from project execution, preserves what work can continue, and protects the human relationship at the team level — the PMI pattern for contract conflicts. (A) halts uncontested work unnecessarily. (C) damages the working relationship and the PM is not qualified to take a legal position. (D) exceeds the PM's authority and expertise.
