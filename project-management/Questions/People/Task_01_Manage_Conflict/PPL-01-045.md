# Question

A major conflict has emerged between the project team and the client's team over interpretation of contract terms. Legal teams are being brought in. The working relationship has broken down, but the project must continue. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Stop all work until the legal dispute is resolved
- **B**: Continue work on uncontested items, establish neutral communication channels, document all interactions, and work with both project sponsors to maintain professional working relationships separate from the legal process
- **C**: Side with your organisation's legal position in all team interactions
- **D**: Try to resolve the legal issues yourself to avoid lawyers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Separate the legal dispute from project execution where possible. Continue work on uncontested deliverables. Maintain professional working relationships at the team level - legal disputes don't require personal animosity. Document carefully. Engage sponsors to help preserve working relationships. Let legal teams handle the contract interpretation.

Option A may not be practical and causes unnecessary delays on uncontested work.

Option C can damage working relationships and the PM isn't qualified to determine legal positions.

Option D exceeds PM authority and expertise.
