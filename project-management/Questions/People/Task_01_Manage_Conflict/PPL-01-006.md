# Question

A distributed agile team spans three time zones. In retrospectives, team members in Asia consistently stay silent while the North American members dominate. Privately, the Asian members say their ideas are dismissed or interrupted. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate retrospective times so each region takes turns at convenient local hours.
- **B**: Run separate retrospectives for each region so everyone has space to speak.
- **C**: Introduce facilitation techniques such as round-robin input, silent written reflection, and explicit turn-taking for everyone.
- **D**: Privately coach the North American members on cultural communication styles.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) addresses the structural cause — a meeting format that favours extroverted, interruption-tolerant communication — with techniques that give every voice space. (A) improves scheduling fairness but does nothing about the dynamic. (B) fragments the team and prevents shared learning, which is the point of a retrospective. (D) singles out one group and assumes fault rather than redesigning the process that would help everyone.
