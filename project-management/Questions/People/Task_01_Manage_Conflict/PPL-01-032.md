# Question

A vendor is consistently delivering late, causing friction with your internal team who must work overtime to compensate. The vendor blames unclear requirements; your team blames vendor incompetence. How should the PM address this multi-party conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Terminate the vendor contract immediately
- **B**: Bring both parties together to jointly review the requirements process, identify gaps, establish clearer communication protocols, and agree on measurable expectations
- **C**: Accept the vendor's excuse and tell the team to expect overtime
- **D**: Assign blame to determine who is at fault before proceeding

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a collaborative approach to a systemic issue. Rather than assigning blame, focus on process improvement. Joint review sessions build shared understanding, clearer protocols prevent future issues, and measurable expectations create accountability. This addresses root causes rather than symptoms.

Option A may be premature and doesn't address potential internal process issues.

Option C accepts ongoing dysfunction without addressing causes.

Option D focuses on blame rather than solutions, damaging relationships.
