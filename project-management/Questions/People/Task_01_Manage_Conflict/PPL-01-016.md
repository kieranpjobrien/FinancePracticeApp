# Question

A conflict between two team members has escalated to the point where they refuse to work together. Other team members are taking sides, and productivity is suffering. Previous attempts at mediation have failed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Separate them permanently by assigning them to different work streams with no interaction
- **B**: Escalate to HR and their functional managers while documenting the business impact
- **C**: Terminate one of them to restore team harmony
- **D**: Ignore it and hope it resolves naturally over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When conflict has escalated beyond the PM's ability to resolve and is affecting team performance, escalation to HR and management is appropriate. Document the impact (productivity loss, team morale, project risk) to support the business case for intervention. This brings in resources with authority and expertise for serious interpersonal issues.

Option A treats symptoms but doesn't resolve the underlying conflict, which may resurface.

Option C is a drastic action the PM likely doesn't have authority for, and removes potentially valuable team members.

Option D allows continued deterioration of team performance.
