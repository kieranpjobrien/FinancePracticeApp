# Question

A stakeholder from another department is angry because the project timeline conflicts with their department's needs. They've sent several aggressive emails demanding changes. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Respond with equally firm emails defending the project timeline
- **B**: Ignore the emails until the stakeholder calms down
- **C**: Acknowledge their concerns, request a meeting to understand their needs, and explore options together
- **D**: Forward the emails to senior management to handle

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Angry stakeholders often feel unheard. Acknowledging concerns (not necessarily agreeing) shows respect. A meeting allows deeper understanding of their needs and constraints. Collaborative exploration may find solutions that address both the project timeline and departmental needs.

Option A escalates the conflict into adversarial communication.

Option B allows frustration to build and doesn't address legitimate concerns.

Option D passes the problem upward without attempting resolution.
