# Question

A stakeholder from another department is angry that the project schedule clashes with their team's needs and has sent several aggressive emails demanding changes. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Reply with an equally firm written email defending the current project schedule.
- **B**: Acknowledge the concerns in writing and request a meeting to explore options together.
- **C**: Wait for the stakeholder to calm down on their own before responding to the emails.
- **D**: Forward the thread to senior management to handle directly on the PM's behalf.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI engagement pattern: acknowledge to defuse, then move from email to a conversation where interests and constraints can be explored together. (A) escalates into adversarial channels. (C) leaves a stakeholder feeling unheard, which usually deepens the conflict. (D) escalates without first attempting direct resolution.
