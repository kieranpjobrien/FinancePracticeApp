# Question

A project manager notices increasing tension between the development team and the business analyst. Developers complain that requirements are unclear, while the BA feels the developers don't read documentation properly. What approach would BEST resolve this ongoing conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Create more detailed documentation templates for the BA to use
- **B**: Facilitate joint requirements sessions where developers and the BA collaborate directly on refining requirements together
- **C**: Assign a technical lead to review requirements before they reach developers
- **D**: Establish formal defect tracking for requirements issues

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The conflict stems from poor communication and different perspectives. Joint sessions create direct dialogue, build mutual understanding of constraints, and allow real-time clarification. This collaborative approach addresses the relationship and the work product simultaneously.

Option A assumes the BA's documentation is the problem, which may not be true. More documentation doesn't solve communication issues.

Option C adds a middleman rather than improving direct communication. This may help short-term but doesn't build the BA-developer relationship.

Option D formalizes blame tracking without improving the underlying collaboration.
