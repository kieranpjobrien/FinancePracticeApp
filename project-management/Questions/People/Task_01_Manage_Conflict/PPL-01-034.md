# Question

In a retrospective, the team voices frustration about one colleague who frequently misses deadlines. The colleague becomes defensive. How should the Scrum Master handle this?

## Options

- **A**: End the retrospective early to stop the conflict from escalating further in the room.
- **B**: Defend the colleague publicly to keep overall team harmony and protect the retrospective.
- **C**: Refocus on systemic improvements and follow up privately with the colleague on support.
- **D**: Let the team keep airing frustrations openly to clear the air and vent the built-up tension.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) keeps the retrospective on processes and norms (its purpose) while handling the personal element privately, which is what PMI's psychological-safety guidance and Scrum's retrospective design call for. (A) avoids a legitimate concern. (B) dismisses the team. (D) creates a public pile-on that wrecks safety and rarely produces improvement.
