# Question

During a retrospective, team members express frustration about one colleague who frequently misses deadlines, affecting everyone's work. The colleague becomes defensive. How should the Scrum Master handle this?

## Options

- **A**: End the retrospective to avoid further conflict
- **B**: Redirect discussion from personal criticism to systemic improvements, then follow up privately with the individual about support needed
- **C**: Allow the team to continue criticising to get frustrations out
- **D**: Defend the colleague to maintain team harmony

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Retrospectives should focus on process, not personal attacks. Redirect to actionable improvements: "What process changes could help everyone meet deadlines?" This addresses the systemic issue without public shaming. Private follow-up allows for supportive coaching to understand root causes (skill gaps, overcommitment, personal issues).

Option A avoids addressing a legitimate concern.

Option C creates a hostile environment and damages psychological safety.

Option D dismisses valid team concerns and doesn't solve the problem.
