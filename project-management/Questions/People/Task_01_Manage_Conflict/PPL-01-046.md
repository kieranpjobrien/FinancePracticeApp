# Question

On a hybrid SAP rollout, an onshore business analyst publicly corrected an offshore developer during a review, and the offshore engineer has since stopped contributing in calls. The onshore analyst insists her comment was purely technical and that the silence is overreaction. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the offshore engineer privately, understand her experience, then coach the analyst on feedback norms before realigning the whole team.
- **B**: Raise the incident in the next team retrospective so the group can debate norms about public feedback openly together.
- **C**: Ask the offshore lead to pass a message to the engineer reassuring her that the analyst meant nothing personal at all.
- **D**: Move technical critique to written channels so future disagreements are depersonalised and the cultural friction is avoided going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) follows the PMI pattern for cross-cultural conflict: hear the affected party privately first, then coach the behaviour, then address the team — restoring psychological safety before process. (B) drags a personal incident into a group forum before either party has been heard, which often worsens withdrawal. (C) uses a third party to carry feedback the PM should deliver and minimises the engineer's experience. (D) changes the medium without addressing the behaviour and tells the team that disagreement cannot be handled face-to-face.
