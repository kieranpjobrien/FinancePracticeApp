# Question

A vendor and internal team disagree about who is responsible for a problem that's delaying the project. Both sides blame each other. The PM needs to resolve this quickly to get the project back on track. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Assign blame to whoever seems more at fault
- **B**: Focus on solving the immediate problem first, then analyse root cause together without blame to prevent recurrence
- **C**: Let them continue arguing until they reach agreement
- **D**: Escalate to legal to determine contractual responsibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In time-critical situations, separate problem-solving from blame assignment. First, collaborate on fixing the immediate issue - this often builds cooperation. Then conduct a blameless root cause analysis focused on "how do we prevent this?" rather than "whose fault was it?" This gets the project moving while learning for the future.

Option A creates winners/losers and damages ongoing relationship.

Option C wastes time when the project is delayed.

Option D is appropriate only if resolution fails and creates adversarial dynamic.
