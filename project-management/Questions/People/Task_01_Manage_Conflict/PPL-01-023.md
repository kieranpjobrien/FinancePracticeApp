# Question

A vendor and the internal team blame each other for a defect that is delaying the project. The PM needs the project moving again quickly. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Decide which side seems more at fault and assign responsibility there.
- **B**: Focus first on fixing the immediate problem together, then run a blameless root-cause review.
- **C**: Let them keep arguing until one side concedes.
- **D**: Refer the matter to legal to determine contractual responsibility.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) separates problem-solving from blame: collaboration on the fix builds cooperation, and a blameless RCA learns the lesson without poisoning the relationship — this is PMI's preferred approach for time-pressured incidents. (A) creates winners and losers and damages the ongoing relationship. (C) wastes time the project doesn't have. (D) is appropriate only after direct resolution fails and turns the relationship adversarial.
