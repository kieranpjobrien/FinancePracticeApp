# Question

A team member comes to you complaining about another colleague's behaviour. They want you to address it but don't want you to reveal they complained. What is the most appropriate approach?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the accused team member with the anonymous complaint
- **B**: Observe the behaviour yourself, then address any issues you directly witness while coaching the complainant on direct communication
- **C**: Tell the complainant you cannot act on anonymous complaints
- **D**: Share the complaint with HR without investigating

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Balance confidentiality with fairness. You cannot act on unverified anonymous complaints, but you can increase your observation. If you witness issues, address them on your own authority. Meanwhile, coach the complainant: "I'll pay attention, but the most effective resolution usually involves direct conversation. Would you like help preparing for that?"

Option A acts on unverified information and breaks confidence.

Option C dismisses a potentially valid concern entirely.

Option D escalates without investigation or attempt at resolution.
