# Question

A contractor and an employee are in conflict over code ownership and credit. The contractor feels their work isn't being acknowledged; the employee feels the contractor is overstepping. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the contractor they're not a real employee and should accept the situation
- **B**: Give all credit to the employee to maintain internal relationships
- **C**: Clarify roles and acknowledgement practices, establish fair attribution standards that recognise all contributors appropriately
- **D**: Avoid the discussion as it's about feelings, not work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Recognition conflicts affect motivation and collaboration regardless of employment status. Clarify expectations: what does the contract say about IP and credit? Establish fair attribution practices that acknowledge all meaningful contributors. Both parties need to feel valued to maintain productive working relationships.

Option A dismisses legitimate concerns and creates a two-tier culture.

Option B is unfair and demotivates the contractor.

Option D ignores that feelings about recognition directly affect work quality and relationships.
