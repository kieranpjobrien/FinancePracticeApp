# Question

A team is in Tuckman's Storming stage, with frequent disagreements about process and approach. The PM is tempted to step in and shut down each conflict. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Allow constructive conflict, facilitate resolution and recognise that storming is a normal developmental stage.
- **B**: Resolve every conflict quickly to keep velocity up.
- **C**: Make all process decisions yourself to bypass the disagreement.
- **D**: Disband the team and form a new one.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the developmental view PMI takes: conflict in storming is normal and necessary for the team to reach norming. The PM facilitates and coaches rather than suppressing it. (B) creates dependency on the PM and skips team development. (C) is forcing and prevents the team from learning to self-resolve. (D) abandons the team at a critical growth stage.
