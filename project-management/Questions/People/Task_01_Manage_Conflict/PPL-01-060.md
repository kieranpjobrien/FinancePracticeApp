# Question

Your PMO has mandated a standard change-control process that the agile team you lead finds heavyweight and slow. The team lead has been approving small changes informally and the PMO has now raised a governance finding against your project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct the team lead to route every change through the full PMO process and close out the governance finding with a formal remediation plan.
- **B**: Back the team's informal approach with the PMO and argue that PMI's tailoring principle allows the team to design its own change-control process for agile work.
- **C**: Propose a tailored lightweight change path to the PMO for low-impact agile changes, agree it formally, then retrain the team on when each path applies.
- **D**: Keep the informal approvals in place but document them more rigorously so that the PMO audit trail is complete even though the process itself has not really changed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) uses PMI's tailoring principle properly: negotiate the lightweight process through governance, get it formally agreed, then comply — you get both agility and compliance. (A) forces full heavyweight control on work it does not fit and damages the team. (B) claims tailoring without going through governance, which is what created the finding in the first place. (D) dresses up non-compliance in better paperwork and will fail at the next audit.
