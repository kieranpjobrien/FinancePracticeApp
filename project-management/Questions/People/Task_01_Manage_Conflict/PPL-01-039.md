# Question

In agile environments, when should a PM or Scrum Master intervene in team conflicts?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately at the first sign of any disagreement
- **B**: Only when the conflict is affecting team performance or psychological safety
- **C**: Never - teams should always self-manage
- **D**: Only when asked by senior management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Agile teams are expected to self-organise, including resolving their own conflicts. The Scrum Master or PM should observe but allow the team space to work through disagreements. Intervention is appropriate when conflicts escalate to affect performance, when psychological safety is threatened, or when the team is stuck and requests help.

Option A over-intervenes and prevents team maturity development.

Option C under-intervenes even when serious issues arise.

Option D is reactive to management rather than responsive to team needs.
