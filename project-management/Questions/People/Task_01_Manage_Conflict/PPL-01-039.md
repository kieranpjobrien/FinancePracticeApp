# Question

In an agile environment, when should a Scrum Master intervene in team conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately, at the first sign of any disagreement.
- **B**: Never — agile teams must always self-manage every conflict.
- **C**: When the conflict is affecting performance or psychological safety, or when the team asks for help.
- **D**: Only when senior management asks them to.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) reflects the servant-leader stance: give the team space to self-resolve, and intervene only when the conflict damages performance or safety, or the team requests help. (A) over-intervenes and prevents team maturity. (B) under-intervenes when serious issues emerge. (D) is reactive to management rather than responsive to the team.
