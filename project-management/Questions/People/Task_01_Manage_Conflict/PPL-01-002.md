# Question

Which conflict resolution technique involves finding solutions that bring some degree of satisfaction to all parties, often through give and take?

## Options

- **A**: Forcing/Directing
- **B**: Collaborating/Problem Solving
- **C**: Compromising/Reconciling
- **D**: Withdrawing/Avoiding

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Compromising/Reconciling involves finding solutions that bring some degree of satisfaction to all parties. This approach requires that each party give up something (give and take). While it may not result in the best overall solution, it can be effective when time is limited or when maintaining relationships is important.

Option A (Forcing/Directing) is a win-lose approach where one party's viewpoint is pushed at the expense of others.

Option B (Collaborating/Problem Solving) seeks a win-win solution by incorporating multiple viewpoints to reach consensus. This differs from compromise because it aims to fully satisfy all parties rather than partial satisfaction through trade-offs.

Option D (Withdrawing/Avoiding) means retreating from the conflict situation, which does not resolve the underlying issue.
