# Question

A developer on your 8-person Kanban team is consistently late delivering and the rest of the team is now openly frustrated in retrospectives. His functional manager has told you privately he is dealing with a serious health issue he has not disclosed to you. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team the developer is going through a confidential personal issue so they understand and extend their patience towards him for the coming weeks.
- **B**: Reallocate the developer's critical-path work to other team members immediately and reassure the team that the pace issue is being actively handled now.
- **C**: Meet the developer privately, express care, ask what support would help, then agree realistic commitments together and rebalance team workload accordingly.
- **D**: Raise the delivery impact formally with his functional manager and request that a replacement developer be assigned to the team from next sprint onwards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) protects confidentiality, shows care, and solves the delivery problem through adjusted commitments — the PMI pattern balancing the individual and the team. (A) breaches personal information the developer has not chosen to share. (B) makes a visible change without the developer's involvement, stripping agency at a fragile moment. (D) jumps to replacement before any supportive conversation has happened and hands a coachable situation to someone else.
