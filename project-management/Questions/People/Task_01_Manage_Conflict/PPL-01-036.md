# Question

A cross-functional team includes members from departments with conflicting priorities. The marketing representative wants features that will delay the engineering timeline. Engineering wants to cut features marketing considers essential. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Decide which department's priorities are more important
- **B**: Escalate to both department heads to resolve
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion focused on project objectives and customer value to find solutions that address both groups' core concerns
- **D**: Implement both requests and extend the timeline

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-functional conflict often stems from different departmental lenses on the same problem. Refocusing on shared project objectives and customer value helps find common ground. This collaborative approach may reveal creative solutions (phased releases, MVP scope) that address core concerns from both sides.

Option A alienates one department and may not serve project objectives.

Option B abdicates PM responsibility for team facilitation.

Option D may not be feasible and avoids the underlying prioritisation discussion.
