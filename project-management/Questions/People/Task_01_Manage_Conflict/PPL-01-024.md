# Question

During a heated team debate about architecture decisions, one developer makes a comment that others interpret as personally insulting another developer's competence. The targeted developer looks hurt but says nothing. The discussion continues. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let it go since work continued
- **B**: Pause to address the comment, acknowledge impact, and reset expectations for respectful disagreement
- **C**: Speak to the offender privately after the meeting only
- **D**: Take over the technical discussion to redirect

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Personal attacks, even if unintentional, damage psychological safety and must be addressed in the moment. Pause the discussion: "Let's step back - that comment landed as personal rather than about the idea. Let's keep debate on the technical merits." This sets expectations while allowing the discussion to continue constructively.

Option A normalises personal attacks.

Option C misses the opportunity to address damage in front of witnesses.

Option D avoids the interpersonal issue.
