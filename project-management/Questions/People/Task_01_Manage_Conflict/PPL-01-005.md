# Question

According to project management best practices, what is a common source of conflict in projects?

## Options

- **A**: Clear role definitions
- **B**: Scarce resources
- **C**: Detailed project plans
- **D**: Strong executive sponsorship

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Scarce resources are one of the most common sources of conflict in projects. When multiple projects or team members compete for limited resources (people, budget, equipment, time), conflict naturally arises. Other common sources include scheduling priorities, technical opinions, administrative procedures, cost estimates, and personality clashes.

Option A (clear role definitions) actually reduces conflict by eliminating ambiguity about responsibilities. Unclear roles are a source of conflict; clear roles are a preventive measure.

Option C (detailed project plans) helps prevent conflict by setting clear expectations. Lack of planning or unclear plans can cause conflict.

Option D (strong executive sponsorship) typically reduces conflict by providing authority to resolve disputes and clear direction. Weak sponsorship is more likely to lead to conflict.
