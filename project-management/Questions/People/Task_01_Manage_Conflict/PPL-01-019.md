# Question

A project manager inherits a team with a history of unresolved conflicts. Team members are polite in meetings but undermine each other's work privately. Trust is low. What approach should the PM take to address this systemic issue?

## Options

- **A**: Focus only on new conflicts and let historical issues fade
- **B**: Immediately confront all historical conflicts in a team meeting
- **C**: Build trust gradually through consistent behaviour, transparent communication, and addressing conflicts as they arise using fair processes
- **D**: Replace team members who seem to be sources of conflict

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Systemic trust issues require patient, consistent rebuilding. The PM should model the behaviour they want - transparency, fairness, follow-through. Address new conflicts promptly and fairly to demonstrate that issues will be handled constructively. Over time, this creates psychological safety for more honest communication.

Option A ignores root causes that will continue affecting team performance.

Option B risks explosive confrontation that damages trust further.

Option D loses institutional knowledge and may not solve underlying cultural issues.
