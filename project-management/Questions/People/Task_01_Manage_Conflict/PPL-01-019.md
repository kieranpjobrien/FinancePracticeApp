# Question

A project manager inherits a team with a long history of unresolved conflicts. Members are polite in meetings but undermine each other's work behind the scenes. Trust is low. What approach should the PM take to address this systemic issue?

## Options

- **A**: Focus only on new conflicts going forward and let the historical issues fade on their own.
- **B**: Convene a single team-wide meeting to confront all the historical grievances at once.
- **C**: Replace the team members who most appear to be the sources of recurring conflict.
- **D**: Build trust through consistent transparent behaviour and address each conflict fairly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Trust is rebuilt through repeated, observable consistency — that is what (D) describes and is the PMI servant-leadership approach. (A) ignores root causes that will keep affecting performance. (B) risks an explosive session that damages trust further. (C) loses domain knowledge and likely doesn't address the underlying culture.
