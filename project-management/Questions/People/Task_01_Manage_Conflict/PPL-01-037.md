# Question

Cultural differences between team members from different countries are causing misunderstandings. Direct communication from one culture is perceived as rude by another. Indirect communication from another culture is perceived as evasive. Tensions are rising. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Establish one communication standard and require everyone to follow it
- **B**: Facilitate team discussions about cultural communication styles, establish shared norms that respect differences, and provide coaching on cross-cultural awareness
- **C**: Separate team members by culture to avoid friction
- **D**: Ignore cultural factors as they're personal matters

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cultural conflicts require awareness and adaptation, not assimilation. Facilitate open discussion to build understanding of different styles. Establish shared norms that acknowledge differences (e.g., "assume positive intent," "ask clarifying questions before reacting"). Cross-cultural coaching helps everyone adapt. This builds a stronger, more inclusive team.

Option A forces one culture to adapt while others don't, creating resentment.

Option C loses the benefits of diversity and creates silos.

Option D allows misunderstandings to continue escalating.
