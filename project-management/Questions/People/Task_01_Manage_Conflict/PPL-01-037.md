# Question

A multinational team is hitting cultural friction: direct communication from one group lands as rude with another, and indirect communication lands as evasive. Tensions are rising. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a single communication standard and require everyone to follow it.
- **B**: Surface the cultural styles in a facilitated team conversation, agree shared norms that respect differences, and offer cross-cultural coaching.
- **C**: Separate the team along cultural lines to reduce friction.
- **D**: Treat cultural style as a personal matter and stay out of it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the inclusive PMI response: build awareness, agree shared norms ("assume positive intent", "ask before reacting"), and coach the team — none of the cultures has to surrender its identity. (A) is assimilation by another name and creates resentment. (C) creates silos and loses the value of diversity. (D) lets misunderstandings escalate.
