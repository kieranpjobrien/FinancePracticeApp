# Question

On a state-government health portal, two departmental directors each insist their module must launch first and have escalated to the sponsor separately. The sponsor has asked you to "just sort it out." Both modules share a common identity service that cannot support both launches simultaneously. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run your own benefits analysis, pick the higher-value module, and present the decision with evidence to the sponsor for confirmation before informing the directors.
- **B**: Schedule a joint session with both directors, surface the shared identity constraint, and co-design a staged release sequence that both can support publicly.
- **C**: Split the identity service team so that each module can launch independently, even though this increases cost and duplication across the portal delivery programme.
- **D**: Go back to the sponsor and request a direct ruling on which module launches first, noting that peer negotiation between the two directors will not resolve the conflict in time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) brings the conflicting parties together with the shared constraint made visible and moves them from positions (which module wins) to interests (what each actually needs) — the PMI collaborating pattern. (A) pre-decides before either director has been engaged and converts a stakeholder negotiation into a PM ruling. (C) throws money and complexity at a coordination problem. (D) escalates back before facilitation has been attempted and abandons the sponsor's explicit request.
