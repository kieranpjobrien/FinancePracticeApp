# Question

Which conflict resolution technique involves finding a solution that partially satisfies both parties but doesn't fully meet either's needs?

## Options

- **A**: Collaborating
- **B**: Compromising
- **C**: Forcing
- **D**: Avoiding

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compromising is a "lose-lose" or "split the difference" approach where both parties give up something to reach middle ground. It's useful when time is limited or when a temporary solution is needed, but neither party is fully satisfied.

Option A (Collaborating) seeks a "win-win" solution that fully satisfies both parties.

Option C (Forcing) is "win-lose" where one party gets their way at the other's expense.

Option D (Avoiding) withdraws from the conflict without resolution.
