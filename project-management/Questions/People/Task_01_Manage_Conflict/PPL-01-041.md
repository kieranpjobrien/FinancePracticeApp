# Question

A conflict between two team members has been formally resolved, but one party continues to undermine the other through passive-aggressive behaviour. The agreed terms are technically being followed, yet the relationship remains toxic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Address the ongoing behaviour directly, set the expectation of genuine cooperation, and facilitate rebuilding the working relationship.
- **B**: Treat the matter as resolved since the agreed terms are being followed.
- **C**: Permanently separate the parties so they never need to interact.
- **D**: File a note with HR but take no further direct action.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) reflects PMI's view that conflict resolution is incomplete until the working relationship is functional — the PM needs to name the behaviour and rebuild collaboration. (B) confuses compliance with resolution. (C) avoids the issue and may not be practical anyway. (D) is too passive when direct intervention can help.
