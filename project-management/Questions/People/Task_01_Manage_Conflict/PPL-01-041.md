# Question

A conflict has been resolved, but one party continues to hold a grudge and subtly undermines the other through passive-aggressive behaviour. The agreed solution is being followed technically, but the relationship remains toxic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Consider the conflict resolved since the agreement is being followed
- **B**: Address the ongoing behaviour directly, explaining that resolution requires genuine cooperation, and facilitate rebuilding of the working relationship
- **C**: Separate the parties permanently
- **D**: Document for HR but take no direct action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Conflict resolution isn't complete until the working relationship is functional. Address the passive-aggressive behaviour directly: "I notice tension is continuing. The technical agreement isn't enough - we need genuine cooperation." Work on rebuilding the relationship through structured interactions, acknowledging difficulty while maintaining expectations.

Option A ignores ongoing dysfunction that will affect team performance.

Option C may not be practical and doesn't address the behaviour pattern.

Option D is too passive when direct intervention could help.
