# Question

Your lead data scientist on an insurance analytics project received a public award from the CEO last month. Since then, three peer data scientists have grown visibly withdrawn in reviews and one has told you privately the award "felt political." The lead is now isolated. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the CEO's office to issue a follow-up note acknowledging the contributions of the whole analytics team rather than only the award recipient.
- **B**: Speak to the lead data scientist and suggest she deflect attention by publicly crediting her peers for the work that led directly to her receiving the CEO award.
- **C**: Meet the withdrawn peers individually to surface what the award signalled to them, then facilitate a team conversation about recognition and contribution against the project outcomes.
- **D**: Nominate each of the other data scientists for an equivalent recognition at the next award cycle to restore parity between the team members before resentment worsens further.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) diagnoses before treating: the award is the trigger, but the withdrawal is about something deeper the PM cannot assume — facilitation surfaces it and restores shared recognition norms. (A) treats a team-dynamics issue with a communications fix and does not address the interpersonal withdrawal at all. (B) asks the award recipient to perform humility rather than addressing the peers' actual concerns. (D) chases parity through awards, which reduces recognition to a quota and does not deal with the underlying issue.
