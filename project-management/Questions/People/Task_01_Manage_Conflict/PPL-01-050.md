# Question

On a university-industry machine-learning project, a principal researcher wants to publish a paper describing the model architecture; the industry sponsor insists the architecture is trade secret. Both cite the joint agreement and neither will move. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Convene both parties with the signed joint agreement, walk through the IP clauses line by line, and facilitate agreement on what can be published and when.
- **B**: Advise the researcher to delay publication until the sponsor relationship has ended so that the architecture is no longer commercially sensitive at all.
- **C**: Propose the researcher publishes without the architectural details and the sponsor agrees to acknowledge the research contribution in any future product release.
- **D**: Escalate to the university's legal office and the sponsor's general counsel to issue a binding interpretation of the joint agreement text.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) returns both parties to the governing artifact and facilitates interest-based agreement — exactly the PMI approach for contract-anchored disputes between peers. (B) indefinitely delays a legitimate academic interest and damages the researcher relationship. (C) is a reasonable compromise but is proposed before either party has been heard and before the PM has read the governing clauses with them. (D) escalates to legal before peer-level facilitation has been attempted and frames a professional dispute as an adversarial one.
