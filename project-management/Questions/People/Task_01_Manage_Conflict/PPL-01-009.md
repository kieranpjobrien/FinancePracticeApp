# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of addressing conflicts early in a project?

## Options

- **A**: It demonstrates the project manager's authority
- **B**: It prevents conflicts from escalating and affecting team productivity and relationships
- **C**: It creates documentation for performance reviews
- **D**: It satisfies organizational compliance requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Addressing conflicts early prevents them from escalating into larger issues that can significantly impact team morale, productivity, and working relationships. Small disagreements left unaddressed can grow into major disputes that derail projects. Early intervention allows for easier resolution when positions haven't hardened and emotional investment is lower.

Option A is incorrect because conflict management isn't about demonstrating authority - it's about serving the team and project success. Using conflict resolution to assert authority would likely worsen the situation.

Option C (documentation for performance reviews) is a secondary consideration at best and should not be the primary motivation for addressing conflict. This approach could make team members feel they're being monitored rather than supported.

Option D (compliance) may apply in some organizational contexts but isn't the primary reason. Conflict management is fundamentally about team effectiveness and project success.
