# Question

During sprint planning, a developer publicly criticises the product owner's priorities, saying the backlog order "makes no business sense." The product owner becomes defensive. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Support the product owner since they own the backlog
- **B**: Support the developer since they may have valid concerns
- **C**: De-escalate by acknowledging both perspectives and redirecting to a constructive discussion about prioritisation criteria
- **D**: End the sprint planning and reschedule for another day

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The Scrum Master should de-escalate the emotional exchange first, then redirect to productive dialogue. Both parties may have valid points - the developer may see something the PO missed, and the PO may have context the developer lacks. Facilitating constructive discussion serves the team better than taking sides.

Option A or B (taking sides) creates winners and losers and doesn't resolve the underlying disagreement.

Option D (postpone) avoids the conflict without addressing it and delays sprint planning.
