# Question

Your 20-person cloud-migration team spans Sydney, Bangalore and London. The Sydney engineers are pushing back on a 7am daily call; Bangalore is pushing back on 8pm; London is comfortable. Resentment is building and attendance is dropping on both sides. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session to redesign the cadence — rotating unsociable hours fairly, moving content to async where possible, and agreeing the new plan jointly as a team.
- **B**: Keep the current meeting time but record every session so that people who cannot attend can catch up asynchronously without the need to be on the live call.
- **C**: Remove the daily synchronous meeting entirely and replace it with a written status template that every team member must complete by the end of their local working day.
- **D**: Split the team into regional sub-teams with their own cadences so that no individual has to attend an unsociable meeting for the duration of the cloud-migration programme.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) shares the inconvenience fairly, uses the full tool set (time, async, cadence), and gives the team ownership of the working agreement — the PMI distributed-team pattern. (B) leaves the unfair burden in place and records it, which does not fix resentment. (C) loses valuable synchronous signal that distributed teams often need and makes coordination worse. (D) fragments the team's shared identity and creates silos that will surface as integration issues later in the programme.
