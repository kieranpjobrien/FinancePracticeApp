# Question

On an ERP rollout, the finance manager is actively undermining the project in her team meetings while formally signing off on deliverables. Her team is missing training and refusing UAT. The sponsor is unaware of the behaviour. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Report the undermining formally to the sponsor with evidence from the missed training and UAT, and request that the sponsor speak directly to the finance manager.
- **B**: Meet the finance manager privately, describe what you are observing, ask what is driving it, and work with her to find a way she can support the change visibly.
- **C**: Escalate the UAT non-attendance through the change-control process and replan go-live on the assumption that finance will remain unready for the original planned date.
- **D**: Bypass the finance manager and engage her team directly on training and UAT so that the implementation can continue regardless of her personal stance on the rollout.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI stakeholder-engagement pattern: name the observed behaviour with the stakeholder first, understand the driver, and co-design a path forward — often surfacing a legitimate concern that can be addressed. (A) escalates to the sponsor before the PM has had the direct conversation the role expects of them. (C) treats the symptom (missed UAT) and plans around the stakeholder rather than engaging her. (D) undermines the manager back, which escalates the conflict and damages the change adoption the project actually needs.
