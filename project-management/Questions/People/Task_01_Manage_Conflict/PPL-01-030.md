# Question

During sprint planning, the product owner insists on adding more features than the team believes they can deliver. The development team is frustrated, feeling their estimates are being ignored. How should the Scrum Master address this conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the development team since they know capacity best
- **B**: Side with the product owner since they represent business needs
- **C**: Facilitate discussion using historical velocity data to find a realistic scope that balances business needs with team capacity
- **D**: Cancel the sprint planning and escalate to management

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The Scrum Master should facilitate, not take sides. Use objective data (velocity, capacity) to ground the discussion in facts rather than opinions. Help both parties understand constraints while finding a scope that respects both business priorities and sustainable pace. This builds trust between PO and team.

Option A and B take sides, damaging the Scrum Master's neutrality and one relationship.

Option D escalates unnecessarily - this is a normal planning tension the Scrum Master should be equipped to handle.
