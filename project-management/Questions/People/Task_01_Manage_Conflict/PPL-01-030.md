# Question

In sprint planning, the product owner pushes for more features than the team believes it can deliver. The developers are frustrated that their estimates are being dismissed. How should the Scrum Master address this?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the developers in the dispute since they understand their own delivery capacity best.
- **B**: Facilitate using historical velocity to negotiate a realistic scope that respects sustainable pace.
- **C**: Side with the product owner since they ultimately represent the business value of the product.
- **D**: Cancel sprint planning and escalate the disagreement to management for them to resolve.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the Scrum Master's role: stay neutral, ground the discussion in objective data, and facilitate a sustainable agreement. (A) and (C) take sides and damage the PO-team relationship. (D) escalates a normal planning negotiation that the Scrum Master should facilitate directly.
