# Question

Two project managers have overlapping resource needs. Both projects are high priority, and the shared resource cannot meet both demands. The resource's functional manager has asked the PMs to resolve it themselves. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the PMO to make the decision
- **B**: Meet with the other PM to understand timelines and constraints, then jointly propose a solution that optimises for organisational priorities
- **C**: Claim the resource first and let the other PM find alternatives
- **D**: Split the resource's time 50/50 regardless of actual needs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Resource conflicts between projects require collaboration, not competition. Joint discussion reveals actual constraints - perhaps timelines don't fully overlap, or one project has more flexibility. Propose solutions based on organisational priorities (revenue impact, strategic importance). This builds peer relationships and models collaborative problem-solving.

Option A passes the decision up when it could be resolved at PM level.

Option C creates adversarial relationships and may not serve organisational interests.

Option D ignores actual project needs in favour of artificial fairness.
