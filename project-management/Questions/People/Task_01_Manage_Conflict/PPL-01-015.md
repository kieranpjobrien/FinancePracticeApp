# Question

Two developers disagree about the technical approach for a critical feature. Developer A wants to use a proven but slower method; Developer B wants to try a new technology that could be faster but carries more risk. Both are becoming entrenched in their positions. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision for them to end the conflict quickly
- **B**: Let them work it out themselves without intervention
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion focused on project goals, risk tolerance, and evaluation criteria to reach a collaborative decision
- **D**: Assign the feature to a third developer who will choose their own approach

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should facilitate collaborative problem-solving by refocusing on shared objectives. What matters to the project - speed, reliability, maintainability? What's the risk tolerance? Establishing criteria helps both parties evaluate options objectively rather than defending positions.

Option A removes learning and ownership. The developers won't develop conflict resolution skills.

Option B may allow conflict to escalate or result in a suboptimal decision.

Option D avoids the conflict without resolution and may create resentment.
