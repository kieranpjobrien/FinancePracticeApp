# Question

Two developers disagree on the technical approach for a critical feature. Developer A wants a proven but slower method; Developer B wants a newer technology that could be faster but is riskier. Both are entrenched. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Decide for them so the team can move on.
- **B**: Leave them to settle it without intervention.
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion grounded in project goals, risk tolerance and explicit evaluation criteria.
- **D**: Reassign the feature to a third developer who can pick a different path.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) refocuses an ego conflict on objective project criteria — the collaborating style PMI prefers when there is time and trust. (A) is forcing and removes the team's ownership and learning. (B) lets the conflict escalate or end in a worse-quality decision. (D) avoids the conflict and breeds resentment.
