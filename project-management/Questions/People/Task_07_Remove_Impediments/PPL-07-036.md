# Question

A blocker involves a personality conflict between a team member and someone in a dependent team. Both individuals are technically competent but refuse to work together, impeding progress. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the interpersonal issue and focus on technical work
- **B**: Remove one person from their role
- **C**: Meet with both individuals to understand the conflict, establish professional expectations, create structured communication channels that minimise direct interaction if needed, and escalate if behaviour doesn't improve
- **D**: Assign all coordination work to avoid the conflicting individuals interacting

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Interpersonal conflicts require direct but sensitive intervention. Understanding root causes may reveal resolvable issues. Clear expectations about professional behaviour set boundaries. Structured communication (defined interfaces, written rather than verbal) can enable collaboration despite personal friction. Escalation remains an option if behaviour doesn't change.

Option A allows the conflict to continue impeding work.

Option B may be disproportionate without attempting resolution.

Option D creates unsustainable workarounds and doesn't address behaviour.
