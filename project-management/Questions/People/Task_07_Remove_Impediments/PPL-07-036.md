# Question

Two senior engineers on different teams that share a critical integration interface have a long-standing personal conflict and now refuse to attend joint design sessions. Integration work is slipping. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign one engineer to a different workstream so integration can proceed without the pair.
- **B**: Escalate the behaviour to both functional managers and request formal performance action immediately.
- **C**: Meet with each engineer separately to understand the conflict and what they need going forward.
- **D**: Replace joint design sessions with written specifications exchanged between the two engineering teams.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's servant leadership model says the PM must address interpersonal conflicts directly at the source before escalating or restructuring. Separate one-on-one conversations let each engineer be heard and surface what's actually driving the dispute, which is the prerequisite to any durable resolution. (A) deprives the project of a key skill set and treats the symptom, not the cause. (B) escalates prematurely; the PM hasn't yet attempted the conversation they're paid to have. (D) is a workaround that institutionalises the dysfunction and degrades design quality through poorer communication.
