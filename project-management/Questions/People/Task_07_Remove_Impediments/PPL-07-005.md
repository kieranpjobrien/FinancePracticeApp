# Question

A developer reports that code reviews are taking too long because the designated reviewer is overloaded with other work. This has become a recurring blocker. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the overloaded reviewer to prioritize code reviews
- **B**: Escalate to the reviewer's manager to increase their availability
- **C**: Work with the team to explore solutions such as distributed review responsibility, pairing to reduce review burden, or adjusting workflow to batch reviews efficiently
- **D**: Recommend the team skip code reviews temporarily

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a systemic issue requiring a systemic solution. The team should explore options: Can other team members do reviews? Can pairing reduce separate review needs? Can batching make reviews more efficient? The Scrum Master facilitates finding sustainable solutions rather than just pressuring the overloaded person.

Option A doesn't solve the capacity problem and may create conflict.

Option B escalates what might be solvable within the team and doesn't address the root systemic issue.

Option D removes a quality practice rather than solving the bottleneck.
