# Question

A PM discovers that a significant blocker exists because of conflicting organisational policies—one department's security policy prevents the technical approach required by another department's architecture standards. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Choose one policy to follow and ignore the other
- **B**: Escalate to executive leadership without proposing solutions
- **C**: Document the conflict, analyse potential resolutions, engage both departments in finding a workable approach, and escalate with options if needed
- **D**: Redesign the entire solution to avoid both policies

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Policy conflicts require careful navigation. Understanding why each policy exists helps find common ground. Often policies have exceptions or alternative compliance paths. Engaging both departments (together, ideally) surfaces creative solutions. Escalation with documented options and recommendations is more effective than problem statements alone.

Option A creates compliance issues.

Option B puts the burden on executives without PM analysis.

Option D may be unnecessarily costly and still not avoid policy issues.
