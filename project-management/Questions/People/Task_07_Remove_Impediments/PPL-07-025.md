# Question

The team's chosen technical approach is permitted by the architecture standards owned by the technology group but conflicts with a security policy owned by the security group. Both groups are dug in. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pick the policy with the heavier compliance penalty and accept consequences with the other group.
- **B**: Redesign the entire solution from scratch to avoid touching either of the two policy areas.
- **C**: Escalate the conflict immediately to executive leadership and let them adjudicate between the groups.
- **D**: Document the conflict, bring both groups together to find a workable approach, and escalate only if needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's approach to policy conflicts is to attempt direct, evidence-based resolution at the right level before escalating. (D) does that and prepares decision-makers with options if escalation is needed. (A) is a unilateral compliance breach. (B) is disproportionate and may not resolve the policy clash anyway. (C) escalates without first attempting joint resolution.
