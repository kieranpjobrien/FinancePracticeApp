# Question

A project team consistently raises the same impediment about slow approval processes from another department. The PM has escalated multiple times but approvals still take weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept slow approvals as unchangeable
- **B**: Bypass the approval process
- **C**: Analyse the root cause of slow approvals, propose process improvements or workarounds (batch approvals, pre-approval criteria, delegated authority), and engage department leadership in finding a solution
- **D**: Stop requesting approvals from that department

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Persistent impediments require root cause analysis and systemic solutions. Understanding why approvals are slow (capacity? unclear criteria? lack of priority?) enables targeted interventions. Proposing concrete alternatives shows good faith and makes it easier for others to say yes. Engaging leadership addresses authority to change processes.

Option A accepts an unacceptable status quo.

Option B creates compliance and governance risks.

Option D is likely not feasible and avoids the issue.
