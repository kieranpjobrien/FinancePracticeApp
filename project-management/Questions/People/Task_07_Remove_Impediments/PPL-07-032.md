# Question

A critical blocker requires aligned action from three departments that do not report to you and have no direct stake in your project. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate immediately to an executive who has authority over all three departments and let them direct the response.
- **B**: Issue a formal memo to the three department heads citing the project impact and a required response date.
- **C**: Map the stakeholders, understand each department's priorities and constraints, look for mutual benefits or trade-offs, and build informal alignment before any formal escalation.
- **D**: Restructure the project to remove the dependency on all three departments.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI recommends building stakeholder alignment through engagement before escalation, especially when the relationships are not hierarchical. (C) is the diplomatic, evidence-led path. (A) escalates without giving leadership the analysis they need to act well. (B) is confrontational and likely to harden positions. (D) is disproportionate and may not actually be feasible.
