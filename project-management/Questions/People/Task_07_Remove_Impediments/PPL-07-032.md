# Question

A critical blocker requires coordination across three different organisational silos, none of which report to the PM or have incentive to prioritise the project's needs. How should the PM approach this complex impediment?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to someone with authority over all three areas
- **B**: Accept that cross-silo coordination is impossible
- **C**: Map the stakeholders, understand each silo's concerns and priorities, find mutual benefits or trade-offs, and build a coalition of support before formal escalation
- **D**: Work around the impediment entirely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Complex organisational impediments require political skill. Understanding each silo's perspective reveals common ground or trade-offs. Building informal support before formal escalation increases chances of success. Finding mutual benefits ("if you help with X, we can support your initiative Y") creates allies. Escalation works better when leadership sees collaborative effort has been attempted.

Option A may work but is more effective after groundwork.

Option B gives up without attempting resolution.

Option D may not address the fundamental issue.
