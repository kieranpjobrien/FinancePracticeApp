# Question

A team member quietly tells you their own lack of skill in a new framework is slowing them down, and asks you not to mention it to the team. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with them privately to arrange focused training, pair programming, or short-term support, while preserving their confidentiality.
- **B**: Raise the gap in the next standup so the whole team knows and can rally around them.
- **C**: Tell them to keep practising on their own time and check back in a few weeks.
- **D**: Replace them with a more experienced engineer to remove the impediment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first approach treats skill gaps as legitimate impediments that deserve support and discretion. (A) addresses the impediment while keeping psychological safety intact. (B) breaches the confidence and may humiliate the person. (C) puts the burden back on the individual without help. (D) is disproportionate and discourages people from raising similar issues in future.
