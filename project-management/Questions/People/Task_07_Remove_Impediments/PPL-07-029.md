# Question

A team member identifies that their own lack of skills in a new technology is impeding their work. They're embarrassed and hesitant to raise it publicly. How should the PM handle this impediment?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team member to figure it out on their own
- **B**: Announce the skill gap to the team
- **C**: Work privately with the team member to arrange training, pairing with experienced colleagues, or temporary support while they develop the skill
- **D**: Replace the team member with someone more skilled

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Skill gaps are legitimate impediments that deserve support, not punishment. Respecting the team member's privacy while addressing the gap maintains psychological safety. Pairing with experienced colleagues enables learning while contributing. Training investments pay off over time. Creating an environment where people can admit gaps leads to earlier identification of issues.

Option A ignores a real impediment affecting delivery.

Option B may humiliate the team member and discourage future transparency.

Option D is disproportionate and discourages vulnerability.
