# Question

During a daily standup, a developer mentions they've been blocked for two days waiting for access credentials from another department. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the impediment and review it at the sprint retrospective
- **B**: Immediately work to resolve the blocker by contacting the other department directly
- **C**: Tell the developer to be more proactive in getting their own access
- **D**: Reassign the developer to other work and forget about the blocked item

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A two-day block is significant and should be addressed immediately, not documented for later discussion. The Scrum Master's role includes removing impediments - in this case, actively engaging with the other department to expedite the access request. This unblocks the developer and demonstrates servant leadership.

Option A (wait for retrospective) leaves the developer blocked - retrospectives are for process improvement, not urgent blockers.

Option C (blame developer) doesn't solve the problem and damages psychological safety.

Option D (reassign) doesn't remove the impediment and may still delay the blocked work.
