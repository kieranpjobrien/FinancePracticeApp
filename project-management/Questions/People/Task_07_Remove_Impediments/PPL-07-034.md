# Question

An impediment has been escalated multiple times but remains unresolved. The PM's manager has been unable to get traction with the responsible department. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the impediment as permanent
- **B**: Continue escalating to higher levels indefinitely
- **C**: Document the impact, explore alternative approaches or workarounds, and work with the sponsor to determine if the impediment requires executive intervention or project scope adjustment
- **D**: Halt the project until the impediment is resolved

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When normal escalation fails, involve the project sponsor—they often have different relationships and authority. Document concrete business impact to make the case compelling. Simultaneously explore workarounds that might reduce dependency on the blocked path. Sometimes scope adjustment is the pragmatic answer if the impediment truly cannot be resolved.

Option A may be premature.

Option B without new approaches yields the same result.

Option D may be an overreaction if workarounds exist.
