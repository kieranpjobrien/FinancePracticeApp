# Question

During a daily standup, a developer mentions they've been waiting three days for access to a testing environment. The Scrum Master documented this impediment but took no further action. What should happen next?

## Options

- **A**: Continue documenting and wait for the situation to resolve
- **B**: The Scrum Master should actively work to remove the impediment by contacting the relevant team, escalating if necessary, and tracking progress until resolved
- **C**: The developer should solve their own problems
- **D**: Move the developer to different work permanently

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A core Scrum Master responsibility is actively removing impediments, not just documenting them. This means identifying who can resolve the issue, following up persistently, escalating when needed, and keeping the team informed. Three days waiting indicates insufficient action on a blocking issue.

Option A is passive and doesn't serve the team.

Option C ignores the Scrum Master's role in removing organisational impediments.

Option D avoids addressing the root cause.
