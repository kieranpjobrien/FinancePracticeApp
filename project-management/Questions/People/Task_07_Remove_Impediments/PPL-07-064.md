# Question

A key team member needs to enter the country to attend an in-person integration lab, but their work-visa renewal has been delayed beyond the lab date. The lab cannot be rescheduled; two vendor partners have also flown in. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the lab as planned and record the absence as unavoidable, accepting that the team member will simply catch up on lab outcomes through written materials afterwards.
- **B**: Engage immigration counsel and HR to explore expedited options, design a hybrid lab setup with remote participation for the affected member, and confirm the vendors are comfortable.
- **C**: Ask the team member to travel on a tourist visa given the visa delay, since the work is short and the team has done this kind of thing informally on previous integration labs.
- **D**: Cancel the lab outright and reschedule it several months later when the visa is likely resolved, even though the vendors have already flown in and made travel commitments.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI resolves immigration-caused impediments by engaging the right specialists, designing a hybrid fallback, and validating with affected partners, rather than tolerating exclusion, breaching law, or discarding sunk travel. (B) does that. (A) excludes a key team member from a one-off integration event. (C) knowingly breaches immigration law and puts both the person and the company at serious risk. (D) cancels a lab the vendors have already travelled for, wasting their commitments.
