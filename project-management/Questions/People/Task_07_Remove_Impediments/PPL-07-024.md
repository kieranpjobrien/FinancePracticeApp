# Question

A team is blocked by a production outage that's consuming their operations team, who were supposed to help with the project. This has happened three times in the past month. What should the PM do beyond addressing the immediate blocker?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that operations emergencies always take priority
- **B**: Complain to operations management about the disruptions
- **C**: Work with operations leadership to address the root cause of recurring outages and establish agreements about project support during emergencies
- **D**: Remove the operations dependency from the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Recurring impediments indicate systemic issues. Addressing root causes (why so many outages? understaffing? technical debt?) prevents future blocks. Establishing agreements (backup resources, clear escalation when pulled away) creates predictability. This is proactive impediment management rather than reactive firefighting.

Option A doesn't solve the recurring problem.

Option B is unlikely to create constructive change.

Option D may not be feasible and doesn't help the organisation.
