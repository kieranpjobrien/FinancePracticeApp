# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between an impediment and a blocker?

## Options

- **A**: Impediments are more serious than blockers
- **B**: Blockers completely stop work from progressing, while impediments slow progress but work can continue
- **C**: Blockers are external while impediments are internal
- **D**: There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A blocker is something that completely prevents work from progressing - the team member cannot continue until it's resolved. An impediment is something that slows progress or makes work harder, but work can continue around it. Understanding this distinction helps prioritize which issues to address first - blockers typically need immediate attention.

Options A, C, and D represent common misconceptions. The terms are related but not identical (so D is wrong). Severity isn't the defining characteristic (so A is wrong). Both can be internal or external (so C is wrong).
