# Question

In agile usage, what is the PRIMARY difference between a blocker and an impediment?

## Options

- **A**: A blocker halts work entirely, while an impediment slows progress but allows work to continue.
- **B**: A blocker is internal to the team, while an impediment originates from outside the team.
- **C**: Blockers are tracked formally in tools, while impediments are tracked informally by the SM.
- **D**: The two terms are fully interchangeable in everyday agile practice and tooling.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The standard distinction is whether work can continue at all. (A) reflects how teams use the terms in practice and how prioritisation typically follows. (B) and (C) are commonly held but incorrect. (D) overstates the synonymy; even though usage varies, the impact distinction is meaningful.
