# Question

A team member identifies an impediment but says "it's not worth fixing - we've always worked around it." The workaround costs about 2 hours per week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the workaround since the team is comfortable with it
- **B**: Calculate the cost over the project duration and decide if removal is worth the effort
- **C**: Immediately prioritise removing it regardless of cost-benefit
- **D**: Document it but take no action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Analyse the true cost: 2 hours × weeks remaining × hourly rate. Compare against removal effort. Often "small" workarounds have significant cumulative cost that's normalised. If removal effort is less than ongoing workaround cost, fix it. If not, conscious acceptance is reasonable. Either way, make an informed decision.

Option A accepts cost without analysis.

Option C may not be the best use of limited improvement effort.

Option D creates visibility without action or decision.
