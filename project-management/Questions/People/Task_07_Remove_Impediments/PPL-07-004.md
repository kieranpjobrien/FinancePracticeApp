# Question

A team repeatedly identifies "organizational bureaucracy" as an impediment - approval processes that delay their work. Previous attempts to change these processes have been unsuccessful because they're mandated by compliance. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that some impediments cannot be removed
- **B**: Continue escalating until someone with authority changes the process
- **C**: Work within the constraint by helping the team anticipate approval needs, submit requests early, and parallelize work to minimize blocking
- **D**: Document the impediment and its impact for quarterly business reviews

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Some organizational constraints genuinely cannot be removed (compliance requirements exist for good reasons). The Scrum Master should help the team work effectively within the constraint - anticipating needs, early submission, parallel work. This isn't removing the impediment but reducing its impact.

Option A gives up without exploring mitigation strategies. Even immovable constraints can often be worked around.

Option B wastes energy on unwinnable battles. If compliance mandates the process, escalation won't change it.

Option D documents but doesn't help the team now. Documentation might inform future improvements but doesn't address current sprint needs.
