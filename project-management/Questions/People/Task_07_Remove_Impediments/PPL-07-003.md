# Question

A project team consistently identifies "too many meetings" as an impediment in retrospectives, but when the Scrum Master tries to reduce meetings, various stakeholders resist because they rely on those meetings for updates. What approach should the Scrum Master take?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the meetings to maintain stakeholder relationships
- **B**: Eliminate the meetings and let stakeholders find other ways to get information
- **C**: Analyze meeting purposes, find alternative ways to meet stakeholder information needs, and restructure or eliminate meetings that don't add proportional value
- **D**: Tell the team to accept that some meetings are necessary in an organization

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This takes a systems thinking approach - understanding why meetings exist (stakeholder information needs) and finding better solutions that serve both the team (less meeting overhead) and stakeholders (continued visibility). Some meetings may be necessary; others may be replaceable with async updates or combined agendas.

Option A prioritizes stakeholders over the team's repeatedly-raised concern. This ignores the team's feedback and doesn't address the impediment.

Option B ignores legitimate stakeholder needs and may damage relationships and trust. Unilateral action without addressing underlying needs creates new problems.

Option D dismisses the team's concern without investigation. While some meetings are necessary, the team deserves an honest effort to address their repeated feedback.
