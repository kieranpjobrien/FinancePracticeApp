# Question

The team has flagged "too many meetings" in the last three retrospectives. When you have proposed cutting some, several stakeholders pushed back because they rely on those meetings for visibility. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the existing meetings to maintain stakeholder relationships and tell the team this is a constraint to accept.
- **B**: Map each meeting's purpose against stakeholder information needs, and replace or restructure those that can be served by async updates or combined agendas.
- **C**: Cancel the meetings unilaterally and let stakeholders raise concerns through other channels.
- **D**: Move the meetings to bi-weekly to halve the load on the team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI takes a systems view: address the team's impediment without ignoring the stakeholder need that created the meeting. (B) does both. (A) dismisses repeated team feedback. (C) damages stakeholder trust by changing things unilaterally. (D) is a blunt halving that may still miss the real cause and may degrade the value of the meetings that remain.
