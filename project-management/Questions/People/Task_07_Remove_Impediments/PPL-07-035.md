# Question

What distinguishes an obstacle from a risk in project management?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: An obstacle is a current problem impeding progress; a risk is a potential future event that may impact the project
- **C**: Risks are more severe than obstacles
- **D**: Obstacles require more budget to address

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The key distinction is timing: obstacles exist now and are actively impeding work. Risks are potential future events that may or may not occur. An unmitigated risk can become an obstacle (or issue) if it materialises. This distinction matters because current obstacles require immediate action while risks require planning and monitoring.

Option A conflates two different concepts.

Option C severity varies independently of category.

Option D budget needs vary by specific situation.
