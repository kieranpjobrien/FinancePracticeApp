# Question

In agile project management, what is the primary distinction between an impediment and a risk?

## Options

- **A**: An impediment is currently blocking work, while a risk is a potential future event that may or may not occur.
- **B**: An impediment is technical in nature, while a risk is organisational in nature.
- **C**: An impediment is owned by the Scrum Master, while a risk is owned by the product owner.
- **D**: An impediment is documented in the sprint backlog, while a risk is documented in the product backlog.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Impediments are present-tense problems actively slowing or stopping work; risks are future-tense uncertain events that may impact the project. This timing distinction is central to PMI's framing because it drives different responses: impediments require immediate removal, while risks require analysis and planned responses. Option B is wrong because both impediments and risks can be technical or organisational. Option C confuses ownership conventions; either may be owned by various roles depending on context. Option D misstates artefact use; risks live in the risk register, not the product backlog.
