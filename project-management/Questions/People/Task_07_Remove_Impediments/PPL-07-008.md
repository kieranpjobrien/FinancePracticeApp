# Question

What is the primary distinction between an impediment and a risk?

## Options

- **A**: Impediments only affect project schedule, while risks only affect project budget or cost.
- **B**: Impediments are technical issues for the team, while risks are business or commercial issues.
- **C**: An impediment is owned by the Scrum Master, while a risk is owned by the project sponsor.
- **D**: An impediment is a current blocker affecting the team now, while a risk is a potential future event.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes the two by timing: impediments are present and need removal; risks are potential and need response planning. (D) captures that. (A) is incorrect on both sides. (B) is unrelated to the actual distinction. (C) confuses ownership with definition; both can have multiple owners depending on context.
