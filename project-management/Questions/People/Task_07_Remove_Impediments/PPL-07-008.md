# Question

What is the difference between an impediment and a risk?

## Options

- **A**: Impediments affect schedule; risks affect budget
- **B**: An impediment is a current blocker affecting work; a risk is a potential future event that may affect the project
- **C**: Impediments are technical; risks are business-related
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. An impediment is something that is currently blocking or slowing progress - it's happening now and needs to be removed. A risk is a potential future event that may or may not occur - it's managed proactively through risk responses. A risk that materialises may become an impediment.

Option A incorrectly distinguishes by impact type - both can affect schedule and budget.

Option C incorrectly distinguishes by domain - both can be technical or business-related.

Option D ignores the important temporal distinction (current vs. potential).
