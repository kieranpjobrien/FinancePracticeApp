# Question

A blocker requires budget that wasn't allocated to the project. The needed tools would cost £15,000 but would save an estimated 200 hours of manual work over the project duration. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that unbudgeted items cannot be obtained
- **B**: Purchase the tools using personal funds
- **C**: Build a business case showing ROI (hours saved × loaded labour cost vs. tool cost), present it to the sponsor or change control board, and request budget adjustment
- **D**: Have the team continue with manual workarounds

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Business cases change budgets. If 200 hours × £50/hour (example) = £10,000 of labour, plus quality improvements and team morale, the ROI is clear. Presenting this case to decision-makers enables informed choices. Most sponsors prefer investing in efficiency over paying for waste.

Option A accepts inefficiency without attempting to change it.

Option B is inappropriate use of personal funds.

Option D knowingly accepts lower productivity and potentially quality.
