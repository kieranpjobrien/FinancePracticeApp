# Question

A blocker would be removed by purchasing a £15,000 automation tool that the PM estimates would save 200 hours of manual work over the project. The tool was not in the approved budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the manual workaround for the rest of the project to stay within budget.
- **B**: Approve the purchase and absorb it within management reserve without notifying the sponsor.
- **C**: Build a business case quantifying labour savings versus tool cost and submit a change request to the sponsor.
- **D**: Ask each team member to expense a portion of the tool individually to avoid a single large purchase.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI requires baseline-affecting decisions to go through formal change control with a documented business case; a quantified ROI is exactly what gives the sponsor what they need to decide. (A) accepts a known inefficiency without testing whether the organisation would fund the fix. (B) raids reserves without the disclosure and approval that governance demands. (D) deliberately fragments a procurement to avoid scrutiny, which is an ethics breach.
