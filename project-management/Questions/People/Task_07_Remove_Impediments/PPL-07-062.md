# Question

Your technical architect has flagged that a design choice the team is making now will almost certainly become a serious impediment at integration in four months. The team is comfortable with the choice and wants to proceed. The sprint goal depends on deciding this week. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer the sprint goal indefinitely until the architect and the team agree on the design choice, even if this means the current sprint produces no shippable output whatsoever.
- **B**: Override the architect's concern and proceed with the design choice because the team is more familiar with the code and the architect has not provided a proven alternative.
- **C**: Go with the team's choice this week and simply plan to handle the future impediment at integration time, when the scope of the problem will likely be clearer and easier to address.
- **D**: Time-box a short architecture session to weigh the forecasted impediment against the sprint goal, record the decision and rationale, and add a mitigation item to the risk register.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats foreseen integration impediments as live risks to be decided with evidence and logged for continuous management, not deferred, overridden, or kicked down the road. (D) does that. (A) halts delivery without resolution. (B) overrides a technical expert without considering the forecasted impact. (C) knowingly accepts a likely future impediment with no mitigation plan in place.
