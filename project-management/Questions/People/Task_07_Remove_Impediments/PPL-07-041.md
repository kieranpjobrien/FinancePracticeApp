# Question

A Scrum team's impediment board has accumulated 15 open items, several of them several sprints old with no recent updates. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Archive everything older than two sprints to give the impediment board a clean baseline.
- **B**: Walk the board with the team, close stale items, and confirm owners for the rest.
- **C**: Stop using the impediment board and surface impediments verbally in the daily stand-up instead.
- **D**: Continue capturing new impediments and let the older items resolve organically over time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The impediment board only has value if it reflects reality, and the Scrum Master owns its hygiene. Walking it with the team validates which items are still real, prioritises by impact, and re-establishes ownership without throwing away signal. (A) would silently drop genuinely stuck items along with the dead ones. (C) removes a visibility tool the team and stakeholders need. (D) lets the board decay further, which is exactly the current problem.
