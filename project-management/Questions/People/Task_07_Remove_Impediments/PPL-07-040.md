# Question

A team member raises a new impediment in the daily stand-up. What is the FIRST thing the project manager should do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign the impediment to the team member best suited to resolve it.
- **B**: Clarify what is blocked, the impact, and the apparent root cause.
- **C**: Add the impediment to the impediment log and review it at the retrospective.
- **D**: Escalate to the functional manager whose area is involved.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's first step on any impediment is understanding it; "I'm blocked" is not enough information to act on. Clarifying what is blocked, the impact, and the suspected cause shapes both the urgency and the right response. (A) acts before the problem is properly framed and risks fixing the wrong thing. (C) defers what may be a today-blocker to a retrospective held days or weeks later. (D) escalates without first determining whether the PM and team can resolve it themselves.
