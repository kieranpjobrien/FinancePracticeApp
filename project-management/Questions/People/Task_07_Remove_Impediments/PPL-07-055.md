# Question

A subcontractor delivering a key module has consistently produced code with high defect rates. The team is now blocked cleaning up the last delivery before integration work can resume. The contract does not specify defect thresholds. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the subcontractor lead with the defect evidence, agree corrective actions and acceptance criteria, and engage procurement to formalise quality expectations in the contract.
- **B**: Quietly absorb the rework into the internal team's capacity and avoid raising the issue with the subcontractor to preserve the relationship for later project phases.
- **C**: Terminate the subcontractor with immediate effect on quality grounds and replace them with the existing internal team or another external supplier without notice.
- **D**: Pay the subcontractor to redo the module entirely at the original price so the rework cost falls on your project but the contractual relationship continues unchanged.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI addresses quality issues with direct conversation, evidence, and formal strengthening of the contract through procurement, before termination or silent absorption. (A) does this. (B) absorbs avoidable cost and sets a precedent. (C) terminates before corrective actions have been attempted and without contractual grounds. (D) rewards defective delivery by paying twice without addressing the quality issue.
