# Question

Mid-project, the PMO has introduced a new stage-gate process requiring a full document package at every release. Your team releases every two weeks, and producing the package would consume roughly 30% of team capacity. Other PMs have quietly begun to comply. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Comply fully with the new stage-gate package at every two-week release, absorb the 30% productivity loss, and communicate the resulting schedule slip to the sponsor transparently.
- **B**: Ignore the PMO's new process and continue releasing as before, on the basis that the PMO has no authority to impose process changes mid-project without consulting delivery teams.
- **C**: Quantify the capacity impact, engage the PMO head to negotiate a release-cadence-appropriate tailoring of the package, and align with sponsor and other PMs on a reasonable standard.
- **D**: Produce a reduced version of the package for your own releases without telling the PMO and hope the reduced documents will pass inspection if anyone happens to review them.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI reconciles mandated governance with delivery cadence through tailoring negotiated with the PMO, supported by data and shared alignment, not blind compliance, defiance, or covert under-compliance. (C) is that negotiation. (A) accepts huge overhead without challenging fit. (B) ignores legitimate governance authority. (D) is hidden non-compliance, which is an ethics issue.
