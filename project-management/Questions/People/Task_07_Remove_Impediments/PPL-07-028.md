# Question

A blocker requires a decision from a senior stakeholder who is on annual leave for two weeks. The team cannot proceed with the affected workstream. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Wait the two weeks for the stakeholder to return so the right person makes the decision.
- **B**: Identify a delegated decision-maker, see if the decision can be decomposed, and find adjacent work.
- **C**: Cancel the affected scope and replan the project around it while the stakeholder is on leave.
- **D**: Take the decision yourself on behalf of the stakeholder to keep the team moving this sprint.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI recommends exploring delegation, decomposition, and parallel work before either waiting or overstepping authority. (B) does that. (A) wastes two weeks of capacity. (C) is a major scope action that is disproportionate to the cause. (D) overreaches the PM's authority.
