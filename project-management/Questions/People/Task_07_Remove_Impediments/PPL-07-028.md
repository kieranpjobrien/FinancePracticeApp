# Question

A blocker requires a decision from a senior stakeholder who is travelling and unavailable for two weeks. The team cannot proceed without this decision. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait two weeks for the stakeholder's return
- **B**: Make the decision on behalf of the stakeholder
- **C**: Explore alternatives: identify a delegate with authority, see if the decision can be broken into smaller parts, find interim work for the team, or escalate to get an exception
- **D**: Cancel the affected work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Creative problem-solving minimises impact. Most senior stakeholders have delegates for urgent matters. Some decisions can be staged (e.g., proceed with agreed constraints, finalise details later). The team may have other valuable work. Escalating to the stakeholder's leadership can sometimes enable temporary contact. Passively waiting should be the last resort.

Option A wastes two weeks of team capacity.

Option B may exceed PM authority.

Option D is premature.
