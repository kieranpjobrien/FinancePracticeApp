# Question

A project is repeatedly blocked by slow approval processes in the organisation. The PM has escalated multiple times but the bureaucratic process doesn't change. What long-term approach should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the delays as unchangeable and pad all estimates accordingly
- **B**: Work with the PMO to propose process improvements based on documented impact data
- **C**: Bypass approvals to keep the project moving
- **D**: Complain to team members about organisational dysfunction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Systemic impediments require systemic solutions. The PM should document the impact (delays, costs, missed opportunities), then work through appropriate channels (PMO, process improvement teams) to advocate for change. This addresses root causes rather than symptoms and benefits future projects too.

Option A (accept and pad) doesn't solve the problem and institutionalises waste.

Option C (bypass) creates risk and may violate governance requirements.

Option D (complain) is unprofessional and doesn't lead to improvement.
