# Question

A project has a significant external dependency on a partner company's API delivery. The partner has missed two milestones and communication has been poor. The PM has limited leverage over the external company. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept delays as outside the PM's control
- **B**: Threaten legal action
- **C**: Engage through proper relationship channels (account managers, partnership executives), document impacts, explore contingencies (alternative solutions, parallel development), and ensure the partnership agreement includes appropriate remedies
- **D**: Build the API functionality internally

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. External dependencies require relationship management and contingency planning. Account managers and partnership executives have different leverage than project teams. Documented impacts create accountability. Contingency planning (what if they deliver late? never?) reduces dependency risk. Partnership agreements should include delivery commitments and remedies—if they don't, this is a lesson for future contracts.

Option A accepts project failure without attempting action.

Option B damages relationships and may not be proportionate.

Option D may not be feasible, cost-effective, or legally permissible.
