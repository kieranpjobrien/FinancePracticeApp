# Question

In a sprint retrospective, several team members say the project manager's daily check-ins, parallel reporting demands, and approval bottlenecks are their biggest impediment. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the feedback as a normal impediment, facilitate a focused discussion on specific behaviours and impacts, and follow up privately with the PM to agree changes.
- **B**: Dismiss the feedback because it amounts to inappropriate criticism of leadership.
- **C**: Escalate the feedback directly to the PM's line manager so it gets addressed quickly.
- **D**: Tell the team to adapt to the PM's working style for the rest of the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects feedback to be handled honestly and at the right level, even when the impediment is the PM. (A) preserves psychological safety, surfaces specifics, and addresses the issue directly. (B) silences a legitimate retrospective input. (C) escalates before any attempt at resolution. (D) accepts an avoidable impediment and signals that team feedback is not worth acting on.
