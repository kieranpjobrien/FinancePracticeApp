# Question

The team's biggest impediment is the PM themselves - the PM's micromanagement, excessive reporting requirements, and approval bottlenecks are slowing the team down. A team member raises this in a retrospective. How should the Scrum Master handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Dismiss the feedback as inappropriate criticism of leadership
- **B**: Take the feedback seriously, facilitate a constructive conversation about specific behaviours and impacts, and work with the PM to find better approaches
- **C**: Escalate immediately to the PM's manager
- **D**: Tell the team to adapt to the PM's style

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is legitimate impediment feedback, even though it's uncomfortable. The Scrum Master should facilitate constructive discussion - what specific behaviours are problematic? What's the impact? Then work with the PM (potentially in a private follow-up) to find approaches that meet the PM's needs without impeding the team.

Option A silences valuable feedback.

Option C escalates before attempting direct resolution.

Option D accepts unnecessary impediments.
