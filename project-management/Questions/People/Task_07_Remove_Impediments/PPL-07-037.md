# Question

A team identifies that excessive meetings are an impediment to their productivity. They spend 60% of their time in meetings, leaving insufficient time for focused work. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept meetings as a necessary part of corporate life
- **B**: Cancel all meetings
- **C**: Facilitate a review of meeting necessity, frequency, and attendance requirements; work to consolidate, shorten, or eliminate non-essential meetings; and protect maker time for focused work
- **D**: Tell team members to skip meetings they don't want to attend

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Meeting overload is a common impediment. Auditing meetings (purpose, frequency, attendees) often reveals opportunities for consolidation or elimination. Advocating for "maker schedules" (blocks of uninterrupted time) protects productivity. The Scrum Master may need to negotiate with stakeholders who invite team members to non-essential meetings.

Option A accepts lost productivity.

Option B eliminates valuable collaboration along with waste.

Option D creates coordination problems and may cause conflict.
