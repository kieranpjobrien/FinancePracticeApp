# Question

Mid-sprint, a developer raises that ambiguous acceptance criteria are blocking her current story. The Product Owner is on a flight and unreachable until tomorrow morning. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the developer to record the specific questions and pull a different ready story to work on in the meantime.
- **B**: Have the developer make a reasonable interpretation and continue, flagging assumptions for review later.
- **C**: Remove the story from the sprint and add it to next sprint's planning agenda for re-refinement.
- **D**: Wait for the Product Owner to return tomorrow and use the time for documentation tasks.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI-aligned response protects flow without creating rework risk: capture precise questions for the PO and reassign the developer to other ready work, which keeps the sprint goal achievable. (B) lets the developer build on assumptions that may be wrong, generating waste the team will have to undo. (C) is a heavier intervention than the situation requires; one day's PO unavailability does not justify pulling scope. (D) wastes a productive day when other ready stories almost certainly exist on the sprint backlog.
