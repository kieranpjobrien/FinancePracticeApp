# Question

A team identifies that their biggest impediment is frequent interruptions from stakeholders who bypass formal channels and directly approach developers with urgent requests. The developers feel unable to say no. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell developers to ignore all stakeholder requests
- **B**: Establish clear channels for requests, communicate them to stakeholders, and shield the team while ensuring legitimate needs are addressed
- **C**: Accept interruptions as normal in stakeholder-focused organisations
- **D**: Assign a junior person to field all stakeholder calls

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This requires both structural and communication solutions. Establish proper channels (product owner, PM, service desk). Communicate to stakeholders how to get help. Shield the team from direct interruptions while ensuring stakeholder needs are still met. This balances focus with responsiveness.

Option A damages stakeholder relationships and may miss legitimate urgent needs.

Option C accepts unnecessary disruption.

Option D doesn't give the junior person authority to actually redirect requests.
