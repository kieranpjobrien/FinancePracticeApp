# Question

Across the last four retrospectives, the team has raised the same three impediments and you have logged them each time. Nothing has changed. The team is becoming cynical about retrospectives and participation is dropping. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run the next retrospective but skip the impediments agenda entirely so the team can focus on positive reflections, on the basis that repeating the same list has become counter-productive.
- **B**: Dedicate the next retrospective to the three recurring impediments: identify root causes, agree named owners and dates, and commit publicly to a visible closure plan for each.
- **C**: Replace the retrospective with a survey and summarise the results back to the team without any live discussion, so that participation no longer depends on people showing up in person.
- **D**: Send the list of recurring impediments to the sponsor for executive resolution, and cancel the next two retrospectives until the team sees that something has actually changed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats repeated unresolved retrospective items as a signal to change how the team acts on impediments, with named owners and visible commitments, not to hide them, dilute the retrospective, or escalate away. (B) does that. (A) removes a core retrospective purpose. (C) replaces an inspect-and-adapt conversation with one-way reporting. (D) escalates the PM's own ownership away and cancels the practice that might restore trust.
