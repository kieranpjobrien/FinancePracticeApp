# Question

During sprint retrospective, the team identifies poor internet connectivity in the office as an impediment. The PM has raised this before but IT says budget isn't available for upgrades. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the infrastructure limitation
- **B**: Ask team members to work from home where they have better internet
- **C**: Quantify the productivity impact (hours lost, meeting disruptions, customer impact) to build a business case, explore interim solutions, and escalate with data
- **D**: Have the team reduce their sprint commitments to account for poor connectivity

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Budget objections often change when costs of inaction are quantified. Tracking time lost to connectivity issues, failed customer demos, or workaround costs builds a compelling case. Interim solutions (mobile hotspots, priority bandwidth) might address immediate needs while longer-term fixes are pursued. Data-driven escalation is more effective than repeated complaints.

Option A accepts lost productivity unnecessarily.

Option B may not be practical for all team members.

Option D reduces output rather than solving the problem.
