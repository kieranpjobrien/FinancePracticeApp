# Question

Which statement best captures the difference between an impediment and a blocker?

## Options

- **A**: An impediment is technical in nature, while a blocker is organisational.
- **B**: An impediment slows the team's progress; a blocker stops work on the affected item entirely.
- **C**: A blocker is a less severe form of an impediment.
- **D**: The two terms are interchangeable in standard agile usage.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The everyday distinction is one of impact: blockers halt work, impediments slow it. (B) reflects that. (A) categorises by topic, which is unrelated. (C) reverses the severity. (D) overstates the equivalence; the distinction matters when prioritising removal.
