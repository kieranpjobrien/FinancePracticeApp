# Question

A team member reports that outdated equipment is slowing their work significantly. New equipment would cost $5,000 but would improve productivity by an estimated 20%. The PM has discretion over small expenditures. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Deny the request to control costs
- **B**: Approve the expenditure as an investment in productivity, calculating the payback period
- **C**: Tell the team member to work around the limitation
- **D**: Escalate to senior management for approval

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a straightforward ROI decision. If $5,000 equipment provides 20% productivity improvement, it likely pays for itself quickly. The PM has authority for small expenditures and should remove impediments that have clear business justification. Calculate payback and document the decision.

Option A prioritises cost control over productivity unnecessarily.

Option C accepts preventable inefficiency.

Option D escalates a decision within the PM's authority.
