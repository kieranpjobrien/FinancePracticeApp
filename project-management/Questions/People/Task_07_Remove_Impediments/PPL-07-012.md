# Question

A team member tells you their workstation is so old that running the local build takes twice as long as it should, costing roughly 90 minutes a day. A new workstation costs $4,000 and falls within your discretionary spend. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the workstation, calculate the simple payback, and document the decision.
- **B**: Ask the team member to find software workarounds before incurring spend.
- **C**: Decline the request as a comfort upgrade rather than a blocker.
- **D**: Escalate the decision to senior management for endorsement before approving.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to make impediment-removal decisions within their authority when the business case is clear. (A) does that and documents the rationale. (B) wastes more productive time on a workaround. (C) misclassifies a real productivity loss. (D) escalates a decision that is squarely within the PM's discretion.
