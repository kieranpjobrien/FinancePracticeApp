# Question

A severe storm has closed the office building your team works from for the rest of the week. Half the team uses specialist hardware that is not available offsite. The sprint has two days left. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel the remaining two days of sprint work and ask all team members to take annual leave for the duration of the unexpected office closure period this week.
- **B**: Replan the last two days of the sprint immediately: move people to software-only work from home, reforecast the sprint goal, and flag any residual impact to stakeholders.
- **C**: Require the team members who need specialist hardware to physically enter the closed building regardless of the safety closure to finish the sprint on time as planned.
- **D**: Push all remaining sprint work into the next sprint unchanged and accept the sprint loss in full, so the team has a clean break over the office closure period this week.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI responds to short-term continuity events by reshaping work to what is feasible, rescoping the sprint transparently, and keeping people safe, rather than charging leave, breaching safety rules, or wholesale deferral. (B) does this. (A) compels annual leave for an external event. (C) breaches safety to hit a sprint goal. (D) rolls an entire sprint forward without attempting any partial delivery.
