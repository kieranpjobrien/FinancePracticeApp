# Question

The organisation's change management process is slow, and several PMs across the portfolio are blocked by the same approval cycles. What is the most effective action for the project manager to take?

## Options

- **A**: Submit each blocked change individually as it arises to keep the project moving.
- **B**: Continue under the existing process and treat the delay as an organisational constant.
- **C**: Convene affected PMs, document specific delays and impacts, and propose a tiered, risk-based approval model to the change authority.
- **D**: Implement low-risk changes first and document them in the change log retrospectively.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Systemic process problems need a coordinated, evidence-based proposal — that is how PMI expects PMs to influence enterprise environmental factors. A coalition with concrete examples and a workable alternative is far more likely to succeed than individual complaints. (A) does nothing about the underlying constraint and creates ongoing project drag. (B) accepts an avoidable impediment. (D) bypasses change control and is a serious governance and audit breach, even with after-the-fact documentation.
