# Question

A team identifies that the organisation's change management process is an impediment—even small changes require lengthy approval cycles. Other teams have the same complaint. What approach is most likely to succeed?

## Options

- **A**: Have each team escalate individually
- **B**: Bypass the change management process
- **C**: Form a coalition of affected teams, document specific examples with time and cost impact, and propose risk-based change categories with streamlined approval for low-risk changes
- **D**: Accept the process as unchangeable policy

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Systemic process improvements require collective voice and constructive proposals. Coalition demonstrates widespread impact. Specific examples make abstract complaints concrete. Risk-based categories (some changes get expedited approval while high-risk changes go through full process) address governance concerns while improving efficiency. This collaborative approach is more likely to succeed than complaints.

Option A fragments the message.

Option B creates governance and risk issues.

Option D accepts unnecessary friction.
