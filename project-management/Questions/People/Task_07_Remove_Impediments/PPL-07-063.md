# Question

Several small but real impediments on your project cross multiple teams' domains, and none of the other teams clearly own them. Each team says it is someone else's problem. The impediments continue to slow your flow. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Absorb the impediments into your team's capacity by using your own developers to work around each issue, on the basis that fighting for ownership will take longer than working around.
- **B**: Escalate all the impediments to the executive sponsor at once and request that he assign a formal owner to each one regardless of which team each issue logically belongs to.
- **C**: Convene the other team leads with the impediments documented, propose a simple ownership-mapping approach anchored in who is closest to each cause, and agree short-dated actions.
- **D**: Log the impediments as general organisational risks in your register and focus on delivery, accepting that cross-team issues without clear owners rarely get resolved quickly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI resolves orphan cross-team impediments by facilitating ownership at the peer level with evidence and short-dated actions, not by absorbing, escalating straight up, or passive logging. (C) is that facilitation. (A) hides the cost inside your own team. (B) escalates before peer resolution. (D) treats the issue as unactionable when peer engagement has not yet been tried.
