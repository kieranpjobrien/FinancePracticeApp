# Question

Your team's work depends on small changes from a central platform engineering team whose backlog is six months long. Every request from your project joins the end of that queue, regardless of urgency. The platform team says their process is non-negotiable. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the platform team lead with quantified impact, propose a lightweight intake for genuinely urgent work, and offer to contribute team capacity for small items you would raise.
- **B**: Escalate immediately to the CTO as a cross-portfolio issue because the platform team's process is clearly failing multiple projects across the delivery organisation.
- **C**: Build the required changes inside your own codebase as a shadow copy, despite the governance issues, so your team no longer depends on the platform team's queue.
- **D**: Re-baseline the project around the six-month platform lead time, communicate the extended schedule to stakeholders, and accept the queue as an environmental constraint of delivery.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI tackles cross-team impediments through peer-level dialogue with evidence and a concrete, collaborative proposal before escalating, shadowing, or rebaselining. (A) does that. (B) escalates before peer resolution. (C) creates shadow systems that are both ungoverned and duplicative. (D) absorbs a massive delay without attempting to resolve it.
