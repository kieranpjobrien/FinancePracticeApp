# Question

A Scrum team identifies that technical debt is slowing every sprint. The Product Owner wants to prioritise new features over paying down technical debt. How should the Scrum Master address this impediment?

## Options

- **A**: Override the Product Owner's decision
- **B**: Let the team continue struggling with technical debt
- **C**: Help quantify the cost of technical debt (velocity impact, bug rates, time spent on workarounds) and facilitate a conversation between the PO and team to find a sustainable balance
- **D**: Have developers work on technical debt secretly

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Technical debt is a legitimate impediment that affects team sustainability. Making the cost visible (in terms the PO cares about—delivery speed, quality) enables informed trade-offs. Most solutions involve some balance—perhaps 20% of capacity for tech debt, or focused refactoring before major features. The Scrum Master facilitates this dialogue rather than dictating outcomes.

Option A oversteps the Scrum Master's authority.

Option B ignores a significant team concern.

Option D undermines transparency and trust.
