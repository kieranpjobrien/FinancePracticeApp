# Question

The Scrum team raises that technical debt is slowing every sprint. The Product Owner is reluctant to allocate capacity to remediation, preferring new features. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team work on technical debt in the background without changing the backlog.
- **B**: Side with the team and overrule the Product Owner so the debt is paid down this sprint.
- **C**: Accept the Product Owner's call since prioritisation belongs to them.
- **D**: Help quantify the cost of the debt in terms of velocity, defects, and rework, and facilitate a conversation between the PO and team to agree a sustainable balance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A Scrum Master surfaces information so the Product Owner can make informed prioritisation decisions, rather than overruling, hiding work, or accepting the position uncritically. (D) makes the trade-off explicit. (A) breaches transparency. (B) oversteps the SM's authority. (C) accepts the issue without giving the PO the data to reconsider.
