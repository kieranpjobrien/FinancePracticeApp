# Question

A blocker on the current project stems from a vendor selection decision the previous PM made and the steering committee formally approved 14 months ago. Market conditions have shifted and the original choice now constrains delivery. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document why the prior decision no longer fits, then submit a change request through the steering committee with the impact analysis.
- **B**: Treat the prior decision as a fixed constraint and re-plan the work around it.
- **C**: Implement the change the project needs and inform the steering committee at the next status review.
- **D**: Ask the project sponsor to override the original decision so the team is not blocked further.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI requires that decisions captured under formal governance only change through formal governance, but it equally requires the PM to surface conditions that invalidate prior assumptions. A documented change request with impact analysis is exactly that mechanism. (B) accepts an avoidable constraint and may breach the PM's duty to flag risks to value delivery. (C) bypasses change control on a baselined decision and is a serious governance breach. (D) skips the change control body whose job this is and risks both relationships and process integrity.
