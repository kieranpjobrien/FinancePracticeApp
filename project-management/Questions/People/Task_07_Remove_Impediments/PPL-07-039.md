# Question

A PM discovers that a blocker exists because of a decision made by a previous project manager that no longer makes sense given current circumstances. Changing the decision would require reopening a closed project governance item. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the previous decision as unchangeable
- **B**: Ignore the previous decision and proceed differently
- **C**: Document why the original decision no longer applies, propose the change through proper governance channels, and provide evidence of the impact if the decision remains unchanged
- **D**: Escalate to override governance processes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Governance exists for good reasons, but circumstances change. A well-documented case explaining what's different now, why the change is needed, and what happens without it can successfully reopen decisions. Following proper channels maintains process integrity while enabling necessary adaptation.

Option A treats decisions as permanent regardless of changed circumstances.

Option B undermines governance and may create compliance issues.

Option D may damage relationships and bypass important controls.
