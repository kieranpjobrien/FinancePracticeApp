# Question

An executive who signed off a key design decision two months ago has now told the steering committee informally that he wants it reversed. The team has built substantial work on that decision and would need significant rework. The decision sits on the governance record. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Request a formal change-control item with impact analysis, clarify the executive's concern, and surface reversal cost to the steering committee before any team rework begins.
- **B**: Begin the reversal work immediately based on the executive's informal preference so the team is ahead of the curve if the formal reversal is eventually confirmed by governance.
- **C**: Ignore the informal remark entirely and continue building on the original decision, on the assumption that an informal comment carries no governance weight whatsoever.
- **D**: Escalate to the CEO to resolve the conflict between the executive's current view and the prior governance decision, bypassing the steering committee on the basis of urgency.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI requires changes to governance decisions to go through integrated change control with impact data visible, not to be acted on informally, ignored, or escalated above the steering committee. (A) does that. (B) commits team effort to an unauthorised reversal. (C) dismisses a signal from a decision owner that will almost certainly return formally. (D) bypasses the body that owns the decision.
