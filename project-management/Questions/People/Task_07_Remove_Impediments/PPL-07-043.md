# Question

A remote team member reports that time zone differences are creating blockers—they're waiting hours for responses from colleagues in other regions. What should the PM do to address this impediment?

## Options

- **A**: Require the remote team member to work different hours
- **B**: Accept delays as inherent to distributed teams
- **C**: Establish core overlapping hours for synchronous work, improve asynchronous communication practices, and ensure decisions and context are documented so work can progress independently
- **D**: Remove the team member from the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Distributed team challenges require intentional practices. Core hours ensure some synchronous overlap. Strong async practices (detailed messages, documented decisions, recorded meetings) enable progress without waiting. Clear ownership prevents decisions from stalling. These practices benefit the entire distributed team, not just one person.

Option A places unfair burden on one individual.

Option B accepts unnecessary inefficiency.

Option D loses talent rather than adapting practices.
