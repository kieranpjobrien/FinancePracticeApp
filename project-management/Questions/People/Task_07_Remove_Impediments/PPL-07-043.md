# Question

A team distributed across three time zones reports they often wait half a day for responses, which is impeding flow. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a daily core overlap window and strengthen async practices so decisions are documented and unblockable.
- **B**: Ask the team in the most-out-of-phase time zone to shift their working hours to match the majority.
- **C**: Co-locate the team for the remainder of the project to eliminate the time zone gap.
- **D**: Treat response delay as an inherent property of distributed work and adjust the schedule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI guidance on distributed teams pairs a small synchronous window with strong asynchronous discipline so work is not gated on a single person being awake. (B) places the burden on one group and harms wellbeing without addressing the underlying async gap. (C) is disproportionate, expensive, and often not feasible. (D) accepts an addressable impediment as fate and lets the schedule slip rather than fixing the way of working.
