# Question

A multi-layered approval policy is your team's biggest organisational impediment. Three escalations have not changed it. What should the project manager do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Build a coalition of PMs, present consolidated impact data, and adapt team practice in parallel.
- **B**: Bypass approvals on lower-value items to keep the team moving while pushing for wider change.
- **C**: Stop escalating and absorb the approval delays into estimates as a permanent project constraint.
- **D**: Refuse to commit to any further deliverables until the approval policy is formally changed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI handles entrenched impediments through coordinated, evidence-based advocacy plus operational adaptation. (A) does both. (B) breaches governance and creates audit risk. (C) institutionalises the waste. (D) is a confrontational stance that damages relationships and stalls the project.
