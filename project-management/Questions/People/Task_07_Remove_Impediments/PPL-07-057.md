# Question

Your project team has been pulled into three production incidents in two weeks for a system they will own post-launch. People are exhausted, delivery commitments are slipping, and the team is starting to express frustration in retrospectives. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Push the team through the next sprint by extending working hours, on the basis that the incident volume should settle down over the coming weeks without change.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor for an immediate ten percent budget increase to hire more developers, since more hands will make the incident load more bearable for the team overall.
- **C**: Tell operations leadership that your team is no longer available for any incident support, regardless of severity, until after the current project phase is fully complete.
- **D**: Quantify the incident impact on delivery and wellbeing, engage operations and the sponsor to agree an interim on-call split, and re-plan committed scope with transparent communication.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI protects sustainable pace, surfaces impact with data, and re-plans transparently rather than absorbing, over-spending, or withdrawing cooperation. (D) does all three. (A) relies on unsustainable overtime and hope. (B) adds unfamiliar staff who will not cover the incidents usefully. (C) damages the incoming operational relationship and is rarely acceptable for a production system.
