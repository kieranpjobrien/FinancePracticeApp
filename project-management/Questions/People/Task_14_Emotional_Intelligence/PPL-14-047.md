# Question

In a sprint review, the team is delivering results strongly but the PM senses a flatness and quiet resignation in the group that the numbers do not show. No one has said anything is wrong. The sponsor is very happy with progress so far. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Trust the sponsor's reading, continue as planned, and wait for the team to raise anything specific they want to discuss openly with the PM themselves.
- **B**: Raise the observation with the team gently in the next retrospective, describe what is being sensed, and invite the team to share whatever is behind it.
- **C**: Individually ask each team member in a 1:1 whether something is wrong, to bypass any reluctance to speak up in front of the group during the retrospective.
- **D**: Schedule a short team-building event such as a lunch or virtual social, to reset the team's energy without needing to name what the PM is perceiving.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's social-awareness principle is to name what is being sensed and invite the team into the conversation — retrospectives are the right forum for this. (A) ignores a real signal because the numbers are fine. (C) goes round the team rather than engaging them together, and pre-empts any collective processing. (D) treats a possible underlying issue with an activity rather than a conversation.
