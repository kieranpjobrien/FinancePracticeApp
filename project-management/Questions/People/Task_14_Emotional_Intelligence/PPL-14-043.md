# Question

A new globally distributed team has members from cultures with very different norms around emotional expression. Some openly voice frustration in meetings; others view the same expression as unprofessional and disengage when it happens. Tension is starting to surface. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a clear team norm that all meetings will be conducted in a low-emotion, professional register so the highest common standard is maintained.
- **B**: Coach each team member privately to adapt their style toward the group average and let the dominant cultural norm settle naturally over time.
- **C**: Surface the cultural differences openly in a working session and co-create team norms that explicitly respect different expression styles.
- **D**: Avoid topics in group settings that are likely to provoke strong reactions and handle those discussions in smaller, culturally aligned sub-groups.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cultural humility plus team agency is the EI move on a multicultural team. (C) treats the difference as a team-design question and gives the team ownership of how they will work together. (A) imposes one cultural norm as the default, which marginalises others. (B) implicitly designates a dominant style and pushes the rest of the team to assimilate. (D) restricts the team's ability to have hard conversations and entrenches sub-group divisions rather than building one team.
