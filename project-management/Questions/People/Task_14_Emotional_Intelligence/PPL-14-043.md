# Question

A cross-cultural team includes members whose cultural norms around emotional expression differ significantly. Some are comfortable showing emotion while others consider it unprofessional. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Establish that professional behaviour means not showing emotion
- **B**: Let each person follow their own cultural norms
- **C**: Discuss cultural differences openly and establish team norms that respect different styles
- **D**: Avoid topics that might generate emotional responses

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cultural humility means recognising that "professional" looks different across cultures. Open discussion creates understanding ("In my culture..."). Establishing team norms that respect differences (e.g., "It's okay to express frustration" and "It's also okay to process privately") allows diverse styles to coexist.

Option A imposes one cultural norm as the standard.

Option B may lead to misunderstandings without shared understanding.

Option D restricts discussion unrealistically.
