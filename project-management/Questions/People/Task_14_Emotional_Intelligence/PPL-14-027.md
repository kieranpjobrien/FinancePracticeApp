# Question

During a retrospective, a team member becomes visibly upset when discussing a failed sprint goal. They blame themselves even though multiple factors contributed. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Move quickly past the topic to reduce their discomfort
- **B**: Point out that it wasn't their fault
- **C**: Acknowledge their feelings, gently reframe the situation with a systems perspective, and reinforce team ownership
- **D**: Suggest they discuss it privately after the meeting

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Acknowledgment validates their feelings ("I can see this is frustrating for you"). Reframing with a systems perspective ("Let's look at all the factors") shifts from blame to learning. Reinforcing team ownership ("We succeed and fail together") supports both the individual and team culture.

Option A dismisses their experience.

Option B directly contradicts their feelings which may not feel validating.

Option D may help but misses the opportunity to model healthy team discussion of failure.
