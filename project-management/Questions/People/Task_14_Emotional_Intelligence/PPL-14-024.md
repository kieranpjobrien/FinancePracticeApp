# Question

A PM is aware they have a tendency to react negatively to unexpected changes - they've received feedback about this before. A stakeholder just requested a significant scope change late in the project. The PM feels the familiar frustration rising. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Express their frustration immediately so the stakeholder understands the impact
- **B**: Pause, acknowledge internally what they're feeling, and respond professionally while requesting time to assess
- **C**: Hide their frustration completely and agree to the change
- **D**: Refuse the change citing project constraints

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Self-awareness (recognising the emotional pattern) combined with self-regulation (pausing rather than reacting) enables a professional response. Requesting time to assess is honest and appropriate - it acknowledges the request while preventing a reactive response.

Option A indulges the emotional reaction rather than regulating it.

Option C suppresses rather than manages the emotion and may agree to something inappropriate.

Option D may be the right answer eventually but shouldn't be an emotional reaction.
