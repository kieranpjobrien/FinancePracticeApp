# Question

A PM knows from prior 360-degree feedback that they react poorly to late scope changes. In a sponsor meeting, the sponsor introduces a significant new requirement two weeks before go-live. The PM feels familiar frustration rising. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent immediate response?

## Options

- **A**: Push back firmly in the meeting, citing the baseline and the risk to delivery, so the sponsor understands the real impact.
- **B**: Agree to the change in the room to maintain the relationship, then work with the team afterwards to find a way to absorb it.
- **C**: Pause, recognise the emotion internally, and respond professionally by asking for time to assess the change through the formal process.
- **D**: Acknowledge the request and immediately ask the team lead in the room to commit to a delivery date for the new scope.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Self-awareness (noticing the trigger) plus self-regulation (pausing rather than reacting) is the textbook EI move. (C) buys time for an integrated change-control assessment and prevents an emotionally driven response the PM has been coached against. (A) may be the right substantive answer, but delivered while frustrated it becomes a reactive outburst rather than a measured position. (B) suppresses the emotion and bypasses change control by committing without analysis. (D) commits the team to a date with no impact analysis, which is poor process and likely an emotional over-correction.
