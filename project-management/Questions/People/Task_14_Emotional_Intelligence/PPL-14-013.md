# Question

A PM recognises they have a bias against a particular team member based on a negative first impression. The PM's emotions affect how they interpret this person's work. How can the PM apply emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Trust their instincts since first impressions are usually right
- **B**: Acknowledge the bias, consciously seek disconfirming evidence, and work to evaluate the person's work objectively
- **C**: Remove the person from the team to eliminate the issue
- **D**: Hide their feelings and pretend there's no bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Self-awareness includes recognising biases. Self-regulation means managing their effect. Actively seek evidence that contradicts your impression. Evaluate work against objective criteria. Consider: "Would I interpret this differently if someone else did it?" This is hard work but essential for fair treatment.

Option A confirms bias rather than examining it.

Option C punishes someone for the PM's bias.

Option D doesn't address the bias that will still affect perceptions.
