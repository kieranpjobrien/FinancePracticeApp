# Question

A project manager realises they have a bias against one team member rooted in a poor first impression, and that this bias is colouring how they read the team member's work. How should they apply emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Trust their instincts; first impressions are usually a reliable guide to performance.
- **B**: Act normally and try to suppress the feeling so it does not affect day-to-day decisions.
- **C**: Acknowledge the bias, deliberately seek disconfirming evidence, and evaluate the work against objective criteria.
- **D**: Move the team member off the project to remove the issue altogether.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Bias does not disappear by being ignored or suppressed; it shrinks under deliberate counter-evidence. (C) names self-awareness and self-regulation in action. (A) reinforces the bias. (B) leaves the bias intact while pretending otherwise. (D) punishes the team member for the project manager's blind spot.
