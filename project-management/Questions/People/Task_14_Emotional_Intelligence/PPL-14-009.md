# Question

A PM notices their own frustration building during a difficult meeting with stakeholders. They're tempted to respond sharply to a comment they perceive as unfair. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Express their frustration to be authentic
- **B**: Pause, acknowledge their emotional reaction internally, and choose a measured response rather than reacting impulsively
- **C**: Leave the meeting immediately
- **D**: Agree with everything to avoid conflict

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This demonstrates self-awareness (noticing the frustration) and self-regulation (choosing response rather than reacting). Taking a mental pause - even a breath - creates space between stimulus and response. You can acknowledge the emotion privately while choosing a professional response. This is emotional intelligence in action.

Option A confuses authenticity with unfiltered reaction.

Option C avoids rather than manages the situation.

Option D suppresses rather than manages emotions.
