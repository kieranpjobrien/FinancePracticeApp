# Question

A PM notices they have been avoiding a difficult conversation with a team member about an ongoing performance issue. Three weeks have passed, the issue is affecting the team, and the PM has rationalised the delay four different ways. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule the conversation immediately without further mental preparation, since the biggest risk now is yet more avoidance and continued team harm.
- **B**: Acknowledge to themselves that the pattern is avoidance, examine what they are actually afraid of, and prepare the conversation with that awareness.
- **C**: Reframe the issue as one the team can raise directly with the member in their retrospective, so the PM does not need to have the conversation alone.
- **D**: Write detailed feedback in an email to the team member so the message is delivered, documented and actioned without a difficult live conversation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's self-awareness principle says to name the avoidance and understand what is driving it — that self-knowledge makes the conversation far more effective when it happens. (A) jumps to action from the same emotional state that produced the avoidance. (C) outsources the conversation to the team. (D) replaces a conversation with a memo, which rarely lands.
