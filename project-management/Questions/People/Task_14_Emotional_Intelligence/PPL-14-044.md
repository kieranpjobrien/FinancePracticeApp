# Question

A PM is facilitating a workshop and notices that the group energy is low after lunch. Participants are disengaged and the quality of discussion has dropped. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push through the agenda since time is limited
- **B**: Criticise the group for their lack of engagement
- **C**: Acknowledge the energy drop and shift approach - perhaps an energiser activity, different format, or short break
- **D**: End the workshop early

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Reading group energy and adapting is emotional intelligence in action. Acknowledging the situation ("I notice energy has dropped") validates what everyone's experiencing. Shifting approach (standing activities, small groups, breaks) addresses the real barrier to productive work.

Option A ignores real human factors affecting output.

Option B blames rather than leads.

Option D gives up rather than adapting.
