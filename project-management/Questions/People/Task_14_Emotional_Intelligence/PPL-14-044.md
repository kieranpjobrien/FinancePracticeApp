# Question

A PM is facilitating a full-day requirements workshop. After lunch, energy in the room visibly drops: people are checking phones, contributions are short, and the same point is being re-litigated. There is still substantial agenda remaining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the energy drop, take a short break, and switch to a more interactive format such as small-group work or a stand-up activity.
- **B**: Push through the remaining agenda since the workshop is time-boxed and the outputs are needed for next week's planning session.
- **C**: Cut the lower-priority agenda items and end the workshop early so participants can return to other work while attention is still salvageable.
- **D**: Call out that participation has dropped and ask the group to refocus and contribute more actively for the remainder of the session.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Reading the room and adapting in the moment is EI in facilitation. (A) names what's happening, gives a physiological reset, and changes format to re-engage attention — all without losing the agenda. (B) ignores the human factor and produces low-quality output anyway. (C) abandons content the workshop was called to produce. (D) blames the participants for a facilitation problem and rarely produces the engagement it asks for.
