# Question

A PM works with a stakeholder whose communication style is very different from their own - the stakeholder is expressive and emotional while the PM prefers data-driven, logical discussion. The PM finds these interactions draining. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Try to change the stakeholder's communication style to be more data-focused
- **B**: Limit interactions with this stakeholder to essential matters only
- **C**: Adapt their approach to include emotional acknowledgment while also providing the data they value
- **D**: Have someone else manage this stakeholder relationship

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional intelligence includes adapting to others' styles. The PM can acknowledge emotion ("I understand this is important to you") before transitioning to data. This respects the stakeholder's style while maintaining the PM's strengths. Both parties feel heard.

Option A tries to change the other person rather than adapting.

Option B may damage an important relationship.

Option D avoids the PM's development opportunity.
