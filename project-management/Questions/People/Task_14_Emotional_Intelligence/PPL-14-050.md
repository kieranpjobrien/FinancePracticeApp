# Question

A key team member's parent died suddenly two weeks ago. They returned to work last week and are pushing themselves hard, delivering results. The PM notices flatness and tears in a 1:1. Project deadlines are tight and the team member has not asked for time off. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Respect the team member's apparent choice to keep working and simply continue to manage them as normal, since they have not asked for anything different from the PM.
- **B**: Explicitly offer time off, reduced load and flexible hours, and reassure the team member that the project will absorb the impact either way.
- **C**: Reduce their workload unilaterally by reassigning some of their work to other team members, without asking them first, to remove pressure they may not ask about.
- **D**: Encourage the team member to seek professional grief counselling and continue delivering at their current pace while accessing whatever support is available outside of working hours.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's duty-of-care and empathy principles expect the PM to offer space proactively and absorb project impact — grief is not a capacity the team member should need to advocate for. (A) leaves them with a fake choice driven by assumed expectations. (C) makes decisions about their work without consulting them, which takes agency from someone already without control. (D) routes support outside the workplace and keeps production pressure on them.
