# Question

A project manager notices that a senior developer has become withdrawn after their technical recommendation was rejected in favor of a junior team member's approach. The developer continues doing assigned work but no longer contributes ideas in planning sessions. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for them to work through their feelings naturally
- **B**: Have a private conversation acknowledging their expertise is valued, explore their perspective on the decision, and discuss how they can continue contributing
- **C**: Publicly praise their past contributions in the next team meeting to boost morale
- **D**: Assign them leadership of a technical initiative to demonstrate confidence in their abilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This addresses the situation with emotional intelligence: having a private conversation (appropriate venue), acknowledging their value (validation), exploring their perspective (understanding their experience), and discussing contribution (forward-looking). It treats them as a person with feelings, not just a resource to optimize.

Option A risks the withdrawal becoming permanent. Waiting for natural resolution may work, but proactive engagement shows care and may speed recovery.

Option C may feel performative and doesn't address the underlying feelings privately. Public praise when someone feels privately wounded can feel hollow.

Option D assumes more responsibility is the solution without understanding the actual concern. They may not want new assignments - they may want to feel heard and valued.
