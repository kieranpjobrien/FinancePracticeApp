# Question

A senior developer has become withdrawn since their technical recommendation was rejected in favour of a junior team member's approach. They still complete assigned work but no longer offer ideas in planning sessions. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a private conversation, acknowledge their expertise, explore their view of the decision, and agree next steps.
- **B**: Praise their past contributions publicly in the next team meeting to lift their morale and signal continued respect.
- **C**: Hand them a high-profile technical initiative to demonstrate continued confidence in their abilities and restore engagement.
- **D**: Give them time and space; they will likely re-engage on their own once the initial sting has had time to fade.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right response is private, specific, and rooted in empathy. (A) acknowledges the feeling, explores the perspective, and gives the developer agency over how they re-engage. (B) is performative when the wound is private. (C) assumes more responsibility is the cure when the developer may simply want to feel heard. (D) is passive and risks the withdrawal hardening into permanent disengagement.
