# Question

Which of the following BEST describes active listening as practised by an emotionally intelligent project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Capturing the speaker's key points in writing while they speak so an accurate record can be confirmed later.
- **B**: Filtering for the factual content of the message and politely redirecting the speaker away from emotional tangents.
- **C**: Mentally drafting a clear, well-structured response while the other person is finishing what they want to say.
- **D**: Giving the speaker full attention, attending to both content and emotion, and reflecting understanding before responding.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Active listening means full presence, attending to both what is said and how it is said, and demonstrating understanding (paraphrasing, acknowledging) before responding. (D) captures all three elements. (A) is note-taking, useful but not the same as active listening — it can actually reduce eye contact and connection. (B) strips out the emotional content that often carries the real message. (C) is a classic active-listening anti-pattern: rehearsing your reply means you have stopped listening.
