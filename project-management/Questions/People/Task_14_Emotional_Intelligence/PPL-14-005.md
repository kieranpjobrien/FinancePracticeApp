# Question

A team member receives harsh criticism from a stakeholder in a public meeting. The criticism has some validity but the delivery was unprofessional. The team member is visibly upset. What should the project manager do during the meeting?

## Options

- **A**: Defend the team member by pointing out that the delivery was unprofessional
- **B**: Acknowledge the valid concerns raised, redirect toward constructive next steps, and then privately support the team member after the meeting
- **C**: Move on quickly to avoid dwelling on the uncomfortable moment
- **D**: Ask the team member if they'd like to respond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This balances multiple needs: acknowledging valid concerns shows professionalism and doesn't dismiss stakeholder feedback; redirecting to solutions moves forward productively; private support after respects the team member's dignity and provides space to process. The PM doesn't ignore the criticism or the emotional impact.

Option A creates public confrontation and may not be appropriate in the moment.

Option C ignores both the valid feedback and the team member's emotional state.

Option D puts the upset team member on the spot, which may be uncomfortable.
