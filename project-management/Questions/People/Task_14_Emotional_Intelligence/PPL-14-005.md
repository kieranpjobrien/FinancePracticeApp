# Question

In a public meeting, a stakeholder delivers harsh criticism of a team member's work. The criticism contains some valid points, but the delivery is unprofessional. The team member is visibly upset. What should the project manager do during the meeting?

## Options

- **A**: Push back on the stakeholder's tone in the moment so the team member feels defended.
- **B**: Acknowledge the valid concerns, redirect to constructive next steps, and follow up privately with the team member afterwards.
- **C**: Move the agenda forward quickly so the discomfort does not linger.
- **D**: Ask the team member if they would like to respond to the stakeholder now.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI-aligned response balances stakeholder substance with team dignity. (B) takes the valid points seriously, keeps the meeting forward-moving, and protects the team member through private support after. (A) creates a public confrontation that may not serve anyone. (C) ignores both the substance and the impact. (D) puts the upset team member on the spot.
