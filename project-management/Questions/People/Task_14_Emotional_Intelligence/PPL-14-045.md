# Question

A PM has high technical credibility but feedback indicates they don't connect well with people. Team members describe them as "cold" and "hard to read." The PM wants to improve but genuine emotional expression feels uncomfortable. What development approach would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Focus on their technical strengths and accept people skills as a weakness
- **B**: Force themselves to be more expressive even if it feels inauthentic
- **C**: Start with small authentic steps - genuine expressions of appreciation, asking questions about people's experiences - and gradually expand range
- **D**: Hire a coach to tell them exactly what to say in each situation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Development of emotional expression works best through gradual, authentic expansion. Starting small (genuine thank-you's, asking about someone's weekend, acknowledging effort) builds comfort without requiring dramatic personality change. Over time, emotional range naturally expands with practice.

Option A limits leadership effectiveness.

Option B feels fake and people detect inauthenticity.

Option D creates scripted rather than genuine connection.
