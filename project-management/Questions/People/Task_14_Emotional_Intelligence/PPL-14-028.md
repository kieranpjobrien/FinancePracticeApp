# Question

After six months of sustained overtime to hit aggressive milestones, a 20-person delivery team is showing signs of burnout: snappy stand-ups, two recent resignations, and declining quality metrics. The next major milestone is eight weeks away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Openly acknowledge the strain with the team, negotiate scope or timeline relief with the sponsor, and put concrete workload protections in place.
- **B**: Bring in an external motivational speaker and run a half-day team-building offsite to reset team energy before the next push.
- **C**: Identify the lowest performers and address their output gaps so the rest of the team carries less of the additional load.
- **D**: Hold the line on the existing milestone, then plan a recovery period and team appreciation activities once delivery is achieved.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

EI requires honest acknowledgement combined with tangible action — words alone or activity without acknowledgement both ring hollow. (A) names the reality, addresses the root cause (workload exceeding sustainable capacity), and uses the PM's authority to protect the team. (B) is a superficial intervention for a structural problem. (C) scapegoats individuals for what is plainly a systemic issue and will accelerate disengagement. (D) defers care and assumes the team will hold together, which the resignations suggest is no longer safe to assume.
