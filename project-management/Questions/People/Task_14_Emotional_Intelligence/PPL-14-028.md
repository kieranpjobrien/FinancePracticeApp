# Question

A PM observes that morale has dropped across the team after several challenging months. Team members are less collaborative, more irritable, and showing signs of burnout. What emotionally intelligent approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Push through to project completion and then address morale
- **B**: Bring in motivation speakers to boost morale
- **C**: Acknowledge the strain openly, make adjustments where possible, and demonstrate care through both words and actions
- **D**: Identify and address the lowest-performing team members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Addressing burnout requires emotional honesty (acknowledging the strain openly rather than pretending everything is fine), tangible action (making adjustments to workload, timelines, or support), and sustained care. Empty words without action, or action without acknowledgment, are both insufficient.

Option A risks losing team members and further degrading performance.

Option B is a superficial response to a deeper issue.

Option D scapegoats individuals for a systemic problem.
