# Question

The PM has decided a long-serving team member's role on the project must end due to poor performance that has not improved despite support. The member is proud of their long service and will take the news hard. The PM is dreading the conversation. What is the BEST approach to it?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the member privately, state the decision clearly and early in the conversation, acknowledge their service, listen, and agree practical next steps together.
- **B**: Soften the decision with a long preamble about the member's contributions so the news, when it arrives, is cushioned by recognition of their past good work.
- **C**: Frame the role change as driven by project needs rather than performance, to protect the member's dignity and avoid the emotional weight of the real reason.
- **D**: Have HR deliver the decision in the PM's presence so the emotional weight of the conversation is shared and the HR process is visibly observed throughout.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's respectful-honesty principle says clear, early delivery of the decision, followed by acknowledgement, listening and practical next steps, treats the person with dignity. (B) delays the landing and makes the preamble feel false once the news arrives. (C) misleads the member about the real reason and denies them information they need. (D) abdicates the PM's leadership role in a conversation that is theirs to own.
