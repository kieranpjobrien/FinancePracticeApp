# Question

In emotional intelligence terminology, what is an emotional "trigger"?

## Options

- **A**: A scheduled checkpoint in the project where the team reviews stakeholder sentiment and adjusts engagement strategy accordingly.
- **B**: A motivational technique used by the project manager to elevate team energy and engagement during long or demanding phases.
- **C**: A facilitation prompt used in retrospectives to surface unspoken concerns and emotions that may be affecting the team's performance.
- **D**: A stimulus that provokes a strong emotional reaction, often disproportionate to the situation, frequently rooted in past experience or values.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A trigger is a stimulus that produces an outsized emotional response, usually because it touches a value, prior experience, or sensitivity. Recognising your triggers is part of self-awareness; managing them is part of self-regulation. (A), (B), and (C) describe project management or facilitation tools that have nothing to do with the EI concept of a trigger.
