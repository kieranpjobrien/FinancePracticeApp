# Question

A PM recognises they have a blind spot: they tend to underestimate the emotional impact of their direct communication style. They've received this feedback multiple times. What should they do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that this is their style and others need to adapt
- **B**: Overcorrect by avoiding direct communication entirely
- **C**: Actively develop awareness, seek real-time feedback, and practice adjusting their approach
- **D**: Let others handle communications where tone matters

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Addressing a blind spot requires active effort: heightened awareness in the moment, feedback loops to check impact, and practice. This might include pausing to consider how a message will land, asking trusted colleagues for feedback, and developing alternative approaches.

Option A doesn't address a development area that's causing problems.

Option B loses the value of their direct style rather than refining it.

Option D avoids development rather than engaging with it.
