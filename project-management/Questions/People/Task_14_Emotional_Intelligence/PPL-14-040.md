# Question

A PM has been told by several colleagues that they consistently underestimate how their direct, blunt communication style lands on others. The PM accepts the feedback but has not yet changed the pattern. What is the MOST effective development approach?

## Options

- **A**: Soften every message by adding qualifiers and a positive opener so that the directness is consistently buffered before the substance lands.
- **B**: Ask trusted teammates for permission to be told in the moment when a message has landed badly, so the pattern can be addressed each time.
- **C**: Build awareness in the moment, set up structured feedback loops with trusted colleagues, and deliberately practise alternative framings.
- **D**: Route sensitive communications through a deputy or business partner who is known for a warmer style so tone risk is managed at source.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A persistent blind spot needs three things to shift: heightened in-the-moment awareness, real feedback loops, and deliberate practice. (C) puts all three in place. (A) is a partial tactic — mechanically softening every message can feel inauthentic and doesn't build awareness. (B) is part of (C) but on its own is reactive rather than developmental. (D) preserves the PM's blind spot by routing around it instead of addressing it.
