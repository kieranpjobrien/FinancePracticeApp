# Question

A senior business stakeholder is actively resisting a major project change they publicly agreed to, missing meetings and pushing back on artefacts. The PM has explained the business case repeatedly. The resistance seems disproportionate to the rational arguments involved. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the stakeholder's non-cooperation to the project sponsor and request the sponsor apply pressure to bring them into line with commitments.
- **B**: Restate the business case more forcefully in the next governance forum, adding new evidence and external benchmarks to make the rationale impossible to argue with.
- **C**: Proceed without the stakeholder's active cooperation, on the basis that their formal agreement is enough to keep the change on track regardless of the resistance.
- **D**: Meet the stakeholder 1:1, set the rational case aside, and try to understand what the change actually means for them personally and professionally.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's empathy lens says resistance disproportionate to the rational case almost always has a human root — meeting the stakeholder and understanding their personal stake is the PMI-aligned first move. (A) escalates before understanding. (B) pushes harder with more rational argument against a non-rational objection. (C) assumes formal agreement is sufficient, which it rarely is when resistance is active.
