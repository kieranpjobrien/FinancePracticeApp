# Question

In a heated technical design review, one team member makes a remark that sounds like a personal attack on a colleague's competence. The room falls silent. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Move the discussion back to the technical agenda quickly so the meeting stays productive and the moment passes without further awkwardness.
- **B**: Continue the meeting and follow up privately with both the speaker and the targeted person after the session to address it one-on-one.
- **C**: Pause the meeting, name what just happened, restate the team's behavioural norms, and reset the conversation back to the design issue.
- **D**: Ask the targeted team member how they would like to proceed and whether they want to respond to the comment in the meeting.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Psychological safety is created and lost in moments like this. (C) names the behaviour, reinforces norms, and protects the team in real time — exactly what an emotionally intelligent leader does. (A) lets the comment stand and signals tacit acceptance. (B) addresses it but allows the team to leave the room thinking the behaviour was tolerated. (D) puts the burden of response on the person who was hurt, which is unfair and lets the speaker off the hook.
