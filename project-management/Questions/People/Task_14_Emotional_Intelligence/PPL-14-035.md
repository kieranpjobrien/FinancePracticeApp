# Question

During a heated discussion about technical approach, a team member makes a comment that seems like a personal attack on another team member. The room goes quiet. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it and continue the technical discussion
- **B**: Address it immediately, naming what happened and restating expected behaviour
- **C**: Wait until after the meeting to address it privately
- **D**: Ask the person who was attacked if they're okay

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Personal attacks cross a line that needs immediate response. Addressing it ("Let's pause - that comment felt personal rather than about the technical issue") names what happened, reinforces norms, and shows the team that psychological safety matters. Immediate action prevents normalisation.

Option A allows the behaviour to stand unchallenged.

Option C may be seen as tacit acceptance by the team.

Option D focuses only on the target without addressing the behaviour.
