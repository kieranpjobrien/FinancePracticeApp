# Question

A PM has been working seventy-hour weeks for two months to pull the programme back on track. They are exhausted, their judgement is slipping, and the team is watching. The PM believes stepping back even briefly would signal weakness to the sponsor, who is already nervous. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push through a bit longer, hit the critical milestone first, and take time to recover once the sponsor's immediate confidence has been visibly restored.
- **B**: Resign from the programme and hand over to a fresh PM, since the current PM is no longer able to make good decisions or lead effectively in their current state.
- **C**: Take a genuine break, brief the deputy, communicate with the sponsor honestly about sustainable pace, and model the boundary the team needs to see.
- **D**: Keep the hours unchanged publicly but quietly delegate more to deputies behind the scenes so the PM's visible presence is maintained while they actually reduce load.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's sustainable-pace and authentic-leadership principles expect the PM to model the boundary honestly — the sponsor needs the PM's judgement to be sound, not their hours to be heroic. (A) postpones recovery and compounds the cost. (B) resigns when a reset is what is needed. (D) hides the reduction, which is dishonest and rarely sustainable.
