# Question

The team has just delivered a critical milestone after a tough quarter. At the milestone review the PM expects relief and pride, but the room feels flat — short answers, little eye contact, no spontaneous celebration. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the achievement, name the flatness in the room, and invite the team to share how they're actually feeling about what they just delivered.
- **B**: Move briskly into the next phase planning so the team has clear forward momentum and a fresh focus to lift their energy.
- **C**: Tell the team they should be proud of what they have achieved and remind them how significant this milestone is for the business.
- **D**: Schedule a mandatory team celebration event for the following week to formally mark the milestone and recognise the team's effort.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A flat reaction after a hard push usually signals exhaustion, anxiety about what's next, or feeling under-recognised. (A) shows the team you noticed and creates space for the real story to emerge — the heart of EI. (B) ignores the moment and risks reinforcing the very dynamic causing the flatness. (C) tells people how to feel rather than asking. (D) imposes celebration on a tired team, which usually makes the disconnect worse, not better.
