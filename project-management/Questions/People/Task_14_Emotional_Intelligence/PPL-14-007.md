# Question

A project has just received bad news - a major scope increase with no additional budget or time. The PM is frustrated but knows the team will look to them for direction. How should the PM handle their own emotions while communicating with the team?

## Options

- **A**: Hide all frustration and pretend everything is fine
- **B**: Express frustration openly to show solidarity with the team
- **C**: Acknowledge the challenge honestly while modelling constructive response and focusing on what the team can control
- **D**: Delay communication until feeling better

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional intelligence includes self-awareness (recognising one's emotions) and self-regulation (managing emotional expression appropriately). The PM should acknowledge reality (this is challenging) while modelling constructive response (focusing on solutions, what's controllable). This is authentic leadership - not fake positivity or unchecked venting.

Option A (hide emotions) is inauthentic and team members will sense the disconnect.

Option B (vent openly) may amplify team anxiety without providing constructive direction.

Option D (delay) leaves the team without information and direction when they need it.
