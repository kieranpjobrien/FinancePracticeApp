# Question

During a retrospective, a team member becomes emotional when discussing a failed feature. They feel responsible and are visibly upset. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Move on quickly to spare them embarrassment
- **B**: Acknowledge their feelings, reassure that failure is a learning opportunity, and guide the discussion toward what can be learned rather than blame
- **C**: Point out that it's unprofessional to be emotional at work
- **D**: End the retrospective immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Emotional intelligence means handling emotional moments with grace. Acknowledge the emotion ("It's clear you care deeply about this"). Normalise it ("Failures are hard, especially when we've invested so much"). Redirect productively ("Let's focus on what we can learn"). This maintains psychological safety while keeping the session productive.

Option A signals that emotions should be hidden, damaging psychological safety.

Option C shames natural human responses.

Option D ends a valuable session prematurely.
