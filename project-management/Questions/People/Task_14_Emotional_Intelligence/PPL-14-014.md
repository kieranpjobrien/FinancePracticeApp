# Question

In a sprint retrospective, a team member becomes visibly upset while discussing a feature that failed in production, feeling personally responsible. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the feeling, normalise it, and steer the discussion toward shared learning.
- **B**: Move on swiftly to spare the team member further embarrassment.
- **C**: Pause the retrospective and reschedule it for another day.
- **D**: Note that strong emotional reactions are not appropriate in a working session.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A blameless retrospective rests on validating the emotion and redirecting toward learning. (A) does both. (B) signals that emotion does not belong, eroding psychological safety. (C) is a heavy intervention when the moment can be handled in real time. (D) shames a normal human response and makes future candour less likely.
