# Question

A new finance business partner has started pushing back hard on project forecasts in every meeting. The PM's initial read is that the business partner is inexperienced and overreaching into project territory. The pattern has continued for a month. What is the BEST next step?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly route forecast conversations around the new business partner and engage their more experienced manager directly on anything material going forward from now on for a while longer.
- **B**: Brief the sponsor on the problematic behaviour of the new business partner and ask the sponsor to raise it with the finance director for their intervention quickly.
- **C**: Formally push back on their challenges in meetings, with supporting analysis, to demonstrate the PM's authority on project numbers to the rest of the stakeholders present.
- **D**: Meet the business partner privately, ask what they are seeing, and explore whether there is a pattern or concern in the numbers the PM has not recognised.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's curiosity-first stance treats stubborn pushback as data, not noise — a private conversation often surfaces a legitimate concern the PM has missed or a useful reframe. (A) routes around a colleague who may in fact be raising something important. (B) escalates on an interpretation rather than on a shared understanding of the issue. (C) wins the meeting and loses the insight.
