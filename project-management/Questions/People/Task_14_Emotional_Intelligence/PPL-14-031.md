# Question

During a stand-up, a normally engaged team member gives a clipped "I'm fine, no blockers" but their tone is flat and they avoid eye contact. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take their answer at face value and follow up only if it affects sprint commitments or visible delivery progress.
- **B**: After stand-up, pull the team member aside privately and let them know you noticed they seemed off, and create space for them to share if they want to.
- **C**: Publicly check in during stand-up by asking the team member directly whether everything is genuinely okay so the team can support them.
- **D**: Mention the change in behaviour to the team member's functional manager so HR support can be offered if it's a personal matter.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EI means reading beyond the words while respecting the team member's autonomy. (B) signals attentiveness and offers space without forcing disclosure or making the moment public. (A) ignores a clear non-verbal signal. (C) puts the team member on the spot in front of peers, which usually triggers more guardedness. (D) escalates prematurely and breaches trust before the PM has even attempted a direct conversation.
