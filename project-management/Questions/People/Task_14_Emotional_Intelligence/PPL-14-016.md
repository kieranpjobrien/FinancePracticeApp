# Question

After delivering necessary critical feedback, the project manager notices the team member becomes withdrawn for the rest of the day. The feedback was delivered professionally and was warranted. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Walk back parts of the feedback so the relationship is not damaged.
- **B**: Take no further action; the feedback was already delivered appropriately.
- **C**: Hold off on giving this person feedback in the future to preserve the working relationship.
- **D**: Check in privately later, ask how they are processing the conversation, and reaffirm belief in their ability to grow.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Necessary feedback can still leave a mark, and the follow-up is what preserves both the message and the relationship. (D) attends to the person without retracting the substance. (A) undermines feedback that was warranted. (B) is technically correct but misses the human moment. (C) avoids a tool the project manager will need again.
