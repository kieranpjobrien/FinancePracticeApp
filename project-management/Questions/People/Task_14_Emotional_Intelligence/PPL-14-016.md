# Question

After delivering critical feedback to a team member, the PM notices the person becomes withdrawn for the rest of the day. The feedback was necessary and delivered professionally. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing - they delivered the feedback appropriately
- **B**: Check in later to see how they're processing it and reaffirm belief in their ability to improve
- **C**: Apologise and withdraw the feedback
- **D**: Avoid giving them feedback in the future

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Even professionally delivered feedback can be difficult to receive. Checking in shows care for the person beyond the task. "I wanted to see how you're doing with our earlier conversation" opens dialogue. Reaffirming belief in them ("I know you can address this") maintains the relationship while maintaining the message.

Option A is technically sufficient but misses the human element.

Option C undermines necessary feedback.

Option D avoids necessary development conversations.
