# Question

During a tense project meeting, a normally calm team member becomes visibly frustrated and makes a sarcastic comment that offends a colleague. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the comment and continue the meeting to avoid drawing attention to it
- **B**: Reprimand the team member immediately to establish that such behavior is unacceptable
- **C**: Acknowledge the tension, take a brief break if needed, and follow up with the team member privately to understand what's behind the frustration
- **D**: Ask the offended colleague if they want to respond to the comment

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This demonstrates emotional intelligence - recognizing that unusual behavior signals something underneath. The normally calm person's outburst suggests underlying stress worth understanding. Acknowledging tension shows awareness; a break allows emotions to settle; private follow-up shows care while not embarrassing anyone publicly.

Option A ignores both the immediate incident and the underlying issue. The behavior will affect team dynamics whether acknowledged or not.

Option B addresses behavior without empathy or understanding. Public reprimand may be embarrassing and doesn't explore what's causing the frustration.

Option D invites escalation and puts the colleague in an awkward position. The goal is to de-escalate, not continue the confrontation.
