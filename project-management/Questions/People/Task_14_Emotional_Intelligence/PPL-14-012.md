# Question

A team is celebrating a successful delivery. One team member is sitting apart, not participating. The PM knows this person did significant work that went largely unrecognised. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore them - some people don't like celebrations
- **B**: Publicly call attention to them, forcing them into the spotlight
- **C**: Approach privately to acknowledge their contribution and check in on how they're feeling
- **D**: Assume they're fine and focus on the celebratory team members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional intelligence means noticing social cues (withdrawal) and responding appropriately. A private acknowledgment ("I wanted you to know how much your work on X mattered to this success") validates their contribution. Checking in ("Is everything okay?") shows care. This is empathy and social awareness in action.

Option A ignores potentially important emotional signals.

Option B may embarrass someone who clearly doesn't want attention.

Option D focuses on the easy interactions, missing someone who may need support.
