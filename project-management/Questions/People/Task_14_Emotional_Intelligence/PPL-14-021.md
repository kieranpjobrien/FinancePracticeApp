# Question

In Goleman's model of emotional intelligence, which competency BEST describes a project manager's ability to manage their own emotional responses and impulses under pressure?

## Options

- **A**: Social awareness, which focuses on reading the emotions and dynamics of others.
- **B**: Self-regulation, which involves managing one's own emotional reactions and choosing measured responses.
- **C**: Relationship management, which centres on influencing and developing other people effectively.
- **D**: Self-motivation, which is the internal drive to pursue goals despite obstacles or setbacks.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Self-regulation is the EI competency concerned with managing one's own emotions, controlling disruptive impulses, and adapting to changing circumstances — exactly what the question describes. (A) social awareness is about perceiving others, not managing oneself. (C) relationship management uses EI outwardly to influence others. (D) self-motivation is about drive and persistence, not impulse control. PMI emphasises self-regulation because PMs operate under constant pressure and must model composure for the team.
