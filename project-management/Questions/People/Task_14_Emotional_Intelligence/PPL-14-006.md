# Question

During a team meeting, a PM notices that one team member's proposal is dismissed without consideration. The proposer withdraws and stops participating. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Move on to keep the meeting on schedule
- **B**: Acknowledge the proposal, invite the team to explore it, and later check in with the withdrawn team member
- **C**: Call out the dismissive behaviour in front of the team
- **D**: End the meeting immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Emotional intelligence involves recognising emotional cues and responding appropriately. The PM should address the immediate situation (revisit the dismissed proposal) while also showing care for the withdrawn team member (private follow-up). This demonstrates that contributions are valued and maintains psychological safety.

Option A (move on) signals that the dismissive behaviour is acceptable and ideas can be ignored.

Option C (public callout) may embarrass people and create defensiveness rather than learning.

Option D (end meeting) is overreaction that doesn't address the underlying dynamic.
