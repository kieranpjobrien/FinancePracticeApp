# Question

Two team members are in conflict and both come to the PM separately, each presenting their version of events. Both are emotionally charged. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Determine who is right and resolve the conflict immediately
- **B**: Tell them to work it out themselves since it's not the PM's job
- **C**: Listen to each person fully, acknowledging their feelings, before attempting to address the situation
- **D**: Bring them together immediately to resolve the conflict

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before problem-solving, people need to feel heard. Active listening and acknowledging emotions ("I can see this is frustrating") helps people move from emotional reaction to rational discussion. Once both feel understood, the PM can facilitate resolution.

Option A rushes to judgment without fully understanding either perspective.

Option B abdicates leadership responsibility.

Option D may escalate conflict if both parties are still emotionally charged.
