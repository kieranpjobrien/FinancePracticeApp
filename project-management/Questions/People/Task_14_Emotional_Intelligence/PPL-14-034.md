# Question

A PM notices tension between themselves and a stakeholder but can't identify why. Conversations feel strained even though there's no obvious conflict. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the stakeholder to prevent conflict
- **B**: Confront the stakeholder about the tension
- **C**: Reflect on their own behaviour and perceptions, and consider having an open conversation about the relationship
- **D**: Ask other people what the stakeholder's problem is

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Self-reflection first: "What might I be doing that contributes to this?" "What am I assuming?" Then, an open conversation ("I've noticed our conversations feel a bit strained - am I reading that correctly?") opens dialogue. This demonstrates self-awareness and relationship management.

Option A allows the problem to persist or worsen.

Option B may feel accusatory without self-reflection.

Option D involves others inappropriately and may create gossip.
