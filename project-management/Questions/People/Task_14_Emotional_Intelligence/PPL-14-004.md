# Question

During a project crisis, the project manager notices they're feeling overwhelmed and starting to make snappy responses to team questions. What emotionally intelligent response should they take?

## Options

- **A**: Push through and maintain presence since the team needs leadership
- **B**: Acknowledge their state, take a brief break to reset, and then return with better composure
- **C**: Tell the team they're stressed and ask for patience
- **D**: Delegate all interactions to a team lead until the crisis passes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Self-awareness (recognizing the overwhelmed state) and self-regulation (taking action to manage it) are core emotional intelligence components. A brief reset prevents saying something regrettable and allows return with better presence. This is healthier than pushing through or completely withdrawing.

Option A ignores the self-awareness signal. Pushing through while compromised often leads to poor decisions or damaging interactions.

Option C puts burden on the team to accommodate the PM rather than the PM managing their own state.

Option D withdraws entirely when the team likely needs visible leadership. The right answer is managed presence, not absence.
