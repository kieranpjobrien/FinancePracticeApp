# Question

A PM tends to take project setbacks personally and ruminate on them, affecting their mood and interactions with the team. This pattern has been pointed out before. What should the PM work on?

## Options

- **A**: Caring less about the project outcomes
- **B**: Hiding their emotional reactions from the team
- **C**: Developing emotional resilience - the ability to process setbacks and recover
- **D**: Delegating responsibility so setbacks feel less personal

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional resilience is the ability to experience setbacks without being overwhelmed by them. It includes processing emotions (not suppressing), maintaining perspective, and recovering to function effectively. This is a developable skill through reflection, mindfulness, and practice.

Option A sacrifices commitment rather than building resilience.

Option B is suppression, not resilience, and isn't sustainable.

Option D avoids the issue rather than addressing it.
