# Question

A PM's emotional reaction to stressful situations is visible to the team - when things go wrong, they become visibly anxious, which spreads to the team. How can the PM develop better emotional regulation?

## Options

- **A**: Hide all emotions completely
- **B**: Develop self-awareness of triggers, practice calming techniques, and model that stress is manageable without eliminating authentic responses
- **C**: Avoid all stressful situations
- **D**: Tell the team to ignore their reactions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Emotional regulation is a developable skill. Identify triggers (what situations cause anxiety?), practice techniques (deep breathing, pausing before responding, reframing), and work on showing that stress is manageable. This doesn't mean hiding emotions - it means modelling healthy response to difficulty. The team learns from how the PM handles challenges.

Option A is inauthentic and likely unsustainable.

Option C is impossible in project management.

Option D doesn't address the PM's impact on team.
