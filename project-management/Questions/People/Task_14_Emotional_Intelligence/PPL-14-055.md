# Question

A PM inherits a team where members regularly defer to a technical lead who dismisses concerns curtly. Junior members have stopped raising risks, and the PM has discovered a significant issue that a junior had noticed weeks earlier but not voiced. What is the BEST first move?

## Options

- **A**: Run a team training on psychological safety and encourage junior members to raise risks more assertively in team forums going forward from now on.
- **B**: Bypass the technical lead and create a direct channel for junior members to raise concerns anonymously to the PM without involving the technical lead.
- **C**: Coach the technical lead privately on how their responses land, model welcoming challenge in meetings, and rebuild risk-raising as a team norm.
- **D**: Have a direct, specific conversation with the technical lead about the behaviour, the cost already incurred, and the behavioural change needed going forward.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's approach is to address the specific behavioural source directly — the technical lead needs to know the concrete cost and what must change before broader norms can shift. (A) trains juniors on a problem caused by one person's behaviour. (B) creates a shadow channel rather than fixing the real one. (C) is a softer step that may eventually be needed but not as the first move on a known concrete harm.
