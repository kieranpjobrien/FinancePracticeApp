# Question

During a team meeting, a PM notices that a usually engaged team member seems withdrawn and isn't participating. Their body language suggests something is wrong, but they haven't said anything. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Call on them directly to participate in the discussion
- **B**: Ignore it since they haven't raised an issue
- **C**: After the meeting, check in privately to see how they're doing
- **D**: Announce to the team that everyone needs to participate

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional intelligence includes reading non-verbal cues and responding appropriately. A private check-in respects their dignity and creates space for them to share if something is wrong. It shows care without putting them on the spot.

Option A may embarrass them and doesn't address the underlying issue.

Option B ignores visible signs that something is wrong.

Option D is impersonal and doesn't address the individual situation.
