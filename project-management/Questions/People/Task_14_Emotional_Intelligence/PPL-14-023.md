# Question

A PM needs to deliver bad news to the team: a popular project has been cancelled due to budget cuts. Many team members have invested significant effort. How should the PM approach this communication using emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver the news quickly and matter-of-factly, then move on
- **B**: Delay telling the team to avoid negative reactions
- **C**: Deliver the news honestly, acknowledge the disappointment, and allow space for reactions
- **D**: Put a positive spin on the cancellation to minimise negative emotions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional intelligence means being honest while also acknowledging human impact. Allowing space for reactions shows respect for people's emotions. Pretending bad news isn't bad undermines trust. People can handle difficult news better when their feelings are acknowledged.

Option A is emotionally tone-deaf and may feel dismissive.

Option B delays the inevitable and erodes trust when the delay is discovered.

Option D feels manipulative and doesn't allow for genuine processing.
