# Question

A PM is mentoring a team member who received critical feedback in a project review. The team member is discouraged and questioning their abilities. What mentoring approach is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Minimise the feedback to make them feel better
- **B**: Review the feedback objectively, help them identify genuine areas for improvement, and reaffirm their strengths
- **C**: Tell them to move on and not dwell on it
- **D**: Criticise the people who gave the feedback

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective mentoring uses setbacks as learning opportunities. Reviewing feedback objectively separates emotion from information. Identifying genuine improvement areas creates actionable development. Reaffirming strengths provides balance and helps rebuild confidence on a realistic foundation.

Option A doesn't help them grow from the experience.

Option C dismisses their emotional response and the learning opportunity.

Option D shifts blame rather than focusing on development.
