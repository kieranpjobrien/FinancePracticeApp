# Question

A company restructure has just moved a PM's mentee into an entirely different part of the organisation. Sessions have been running well for over a year. The mentee's new role is outside the PM's functional area of expertise. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the relationship, acknowledging the change in context, and explore with the mentee what they still want from ongoing mentoring in their new role.
- **B**: End the relationship since the mentor's functional expertise no longer maps onto the mentee's new role and continuing would not add meaningful value.
- **C**: Refer the mentee to a senior leader in their new area of the business and step back once that new mentoring relationship is warmly established for them.
- **D**: Continue as before with no change since the mentoring has been successful so far and re-examining its purpose would be an unnecessary disruption to the pair.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring lens is broader than functional expertise — career, resilience, leadership and navigation all remain relevant across roles, and the mentee's goals for the new context should guide the reshape. (B) underestimates the breadth of mentoring value. (C) cuts a trusted relationship before checking whether it still serves. (D) ignores a major shift in the mentee's context that warrants a recontracting conversation.
