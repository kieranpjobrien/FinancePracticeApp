# Question

A PM is mentoring several team members but notices that one mentee is not making progress despite regular sessions. They complete assignments but don't seem to internalise the learning or apply it independently. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: End the mentoring relationship since it's not working
- **B**: Provide more detailed instructions and closer supervision
- **C**: Have an honest conversation about the mentoring approach, their learning style, and whether adjustments are needed
- **D**: Continue the current approach and hope for eventual improvement

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Lack of progress signals something isn't working. An honest conversation can identify the issue: perhaps the mentee learns differently, has different goals, faces obstacles the PM isn't aware of, or the approach doesn't suit their style. Understanding allows adjustment.

Option A gives up without understanding the issue.

Option B assumes more hand-holding is the answer when it may be the opposite.

Option D continues an ineffective approach.
