# Question

The project manager mentors several team members. One mentee shows up to every session and completes assignments but is not making real progress; they are not applying the lessons in their own work. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: End the mentoring relationship since the time investment is not paying off.
- **B**: Have a candid conversation about progress, the mentee's goals, and whether the approach needs to change.
- **C**: Switch to closer supervision and review every piece of the mentee's work.
- **D**: Set firmer assignments with measurable outcomes and re-evaluate after a month.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When mentoring is not working, the diagnosis comes before the prescription. (B) opens the conversation that might surface a different learning style, unclear goals, or external blockers. (A) gives up before the cause is understood. (C) substitutes supervision for development and may worsen the issue. (D) tightens the existing approach without checking whether the approach itself fits.
