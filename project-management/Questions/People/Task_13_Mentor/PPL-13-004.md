# Question

A junior team member has been given their first significant responsibility on the project. They're making decisions that are within their authority but occasionally choosing suboptimal approaches. How should the project manager mentor them?

## Options

- **A**: Step in to correct suboptimal decisions before they cause problems
- **B**: Allow them to make and learn from mistakes as long as impacts are limited, while being available for guidance when asked
- **C**: Review all their decisions before implementation
- **D**: Wait until the end of the project for a comprehensive feedback session

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring involves supporting growth, which includes allowing safe failures. If decisions are within authority and impacts are manageable, letting them play out provides powerful learning. Being available for guidance (without forcing it) shows support while respecting autonomy. This builds capability and confidence.

Option A prevents learning and signals lack of trust.

Option C removes their decision authority in practice, contradicting the empowerment given.

Option D delays feedback too long - learning is most effective when timely.
