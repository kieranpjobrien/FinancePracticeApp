# Question

A PM has been mentoring a team member on risk management. The team member makes a decision that the PM thinks is wrong, though it's within the team member's authority. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Override the decision to prevent the mistake
- **B**: Say nothing to respect their autonomy
- **C**: Share concerns and perspective privately, but ultimately let them own the decision and learn from the outcome
- **D**: Escalate to management to protect the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mentoring involves sharing wisdom while respecting autonomy. The PM should share their concerns and reasoning ("Here's what I'd consider...") to provide input. However, if it's the team member's decision to make, they should own it. Learning from outcomes - whether the PM was right or wrong - is valuable development.

Option A undermines development and autonomy.

Option B withholds potentially valuable perspective.

Option D escalates what should be a learning opportunity.
