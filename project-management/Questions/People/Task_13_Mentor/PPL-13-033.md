# Question

An agile coach is mentoring multiple Scrum Masters across the organisation. One Scrum Master asks for advice about a conflict on their team but seems to already know what they should do. They just want validation. What should the coach do?

## Options

- **A**: Give them the validation they're seeking
- **B**: Tell them they don't need mentoring if they already know the answer
- **C**: Use questions to help them articulate their own solution and build confidence in their judgment
- **D**: Provide a different solution to demonstrate expertise

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When mentees have good instincts, the mentor's role is to help them trust those instincts. Questions like "What are you thinking?" and "What makes you hesitant to proceed?" help them articulate and own their solution. This builds confidence for future independent decisions.

Option A provides external validation rather than building internal confidence.

Option B dismisses the value of the interaction.

Option D undermines their good thinking.
