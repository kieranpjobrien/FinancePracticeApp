# Question

A junior team member with strong potential lacks confidence in stakeholder meetings and has asked the project manager for help. What mentoring approach would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them what to say next time so they have a script that works.
- **B**: Have them sit in on meetings as observers until they feel ready to speak.
- **C**: Give them gradually larger speaking roles, debrief after each, and share techniques that work.
- **D**: Enrol them in a presentation skills course and revisit progress in three months.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Confidence is built through supported, escalating exposure with quick feedback loops. (C) combines opportunity, reflection, and shared technique. (A) creates dependence on the project manager's words rather than developing the team member's own voice. (B) postpones practice indefinitely. (D) addresses skill in the abstract while leaving the immediate, concrete need unmet.
