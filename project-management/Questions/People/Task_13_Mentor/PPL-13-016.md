# Question

A project manager notices that a junior team member has potential but lacks confidence in stakeholder meetings. The team member has asked for help improving. What mentoring approach would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them exactly what to say in meetings
- **B**: Let them observe meetings until they feel ready
- **C**: Provide opportunities for gradually increasing exposure, offer feedback, and share techniques that have worked
- **D**: Send them to a formal presentation skills course

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective mentoring combines opportunity with support. Gradual exposure builds confidence without overwhelming. Feedback helps them learn from experience. Sharing techniques transfers practical wisdom. This approach develops capability through supported practice.

Option A creates dependence rather than developing independent capability.

Option B may result in extended passivity without building actual experience.

Option D addresses skills but not the confidence issue, and may delay practical experience.
