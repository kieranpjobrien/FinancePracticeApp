# Question

A senior stakeholder asks the PM to mentor their team on agile practices since the PM has extensive experience. The PM is already stretched thin with project responsibilities. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline outright since they don't have time
- **B**: Accept fully despite time constraints to maintain the relationship
- **C**: Propose a structured approach with defined scope, time commitment, and potentially shared responsibility with other agile coaches
- **D**: Delegate the mentoring to a junior team member

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Accepting while setting realistic boundaries maintains the relationship and provides value. Defining scope (which practices, what depth), time commitment (hours per week), and sharing responsibility ensures sustainability. This approach is honest about capacity while still being helpful.

Option A misses an opportunity to build relationships and add value.

Option B risks overcommitment and may affect project delivery.

Option D may not meet the stakeholder's need for experienced guidance.
