# Question

In the context of project management, who can benefit from mentoring?

## Options

- **A**: Only junior team members
- **B**: Only people who report to the project manager
- **C**: Team members, stakeholders, and even other project managers at various levels
- **D**: Only those with formal mentoring agreements

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mentoring can benefit anyone seeking development. Junior team members may need skill development, mid-level staff may want career guidance, stakeholders may benefit from understanding project practices, and peer PMs may value experience sharing. Mentoring relationships can form across various relationships.

Option A limits mentoring's scope unnecessarily.

Option B ignores the value of mentoring across organisational boundaries.

Option D ignores the value of informal mentoring relationships.
