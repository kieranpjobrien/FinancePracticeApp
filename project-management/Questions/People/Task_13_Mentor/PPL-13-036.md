# Question

A PM is asked to mentor a stakeholder from a different function (marketing) on project management basics so they can better collaborate with project teams. The stakeholder has limited time and different priorities. What approach would work best?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct comprehensive PM training covering all knowledge areas
- **B**: Focus on the specific aspects most relevant to their role with practical, immediately applicable content
- **C**: Recommend they take a formal PM certification course instead
- **D**: Decline since cross-functional mentoring is outside normal PM responsibilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective mentoring meets people where they are. For a marketing stakeholder, focus on what they need: how to communicate requirements, how projects make decisions, how to work with project timelines. Practical, role-relevant content respects their time and provides immediate value.

Option A may overwhelm with irrelevant content.

Option C is more than they need and may not happen.

Option D misses an opportunity to improve cross-functional collaboration.
