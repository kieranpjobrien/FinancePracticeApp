# Question

A PM recognises that they have knowledge and experience that would benefit others but has never formally mentored anyone. They want to start. What is the BEST first step?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for someone to ask for mentoring
- **B**: Announce availability broadly and see who responds
- **C**: Identify one or two people who might benefit and offer to help, while also seeking mentoring guidance themselves
- **D**: Take a formal mentoring certification first

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Starting small (one or two people) allows learning the mentor role without overcommitting. Proactive outreach shows initiative. Seeking guidance about mentoring (from experienced mentors, resources, or reflection) helps develop mentoring skills. This approach is practical and developmental.

Option A may result in missed opportunities.

Option B may attract more demand than can be handled effectively.

Option D delays action unnecessarily - mentoring can be learned by doing with reflection.
