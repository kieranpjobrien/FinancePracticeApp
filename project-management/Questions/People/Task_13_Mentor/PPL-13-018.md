# Question

A PM is mentoring a product owner who is struggling with prioritisation. The product owner keeps asking the PM what they should do rather than developing their own decision-making ability. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them the answer since they're asking for help
- **B**: Refuse to answer until they figure it out themselves
- **C**: Use questions to guide their thinking and help them develop their own prioritisation framework
- **D**: Take over the prioritisation to help them

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective mentoring develops capability, not dependence. Using questions ("What factors are most important?", "What would happen if...?", "How have you approached similar decisions?") guides the mentee to develop their own thinking. Helping them create a framework gives them tools for future decisions.

Option A creates dependence and doesn't build capability.

Option B may frustrate without providing useful guidance.

Option D removes learning opportunity and creates dependence.
