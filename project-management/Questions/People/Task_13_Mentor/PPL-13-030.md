# Question

Leadership has asked a project manager to mentor stakeholders across several projects on adopting a new methodology. Some are enthusiastic, others are resistant, and the project manager has limited time. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Concentrate one-on-one mentoring time on the enthusiastic stakeholders for the fastest early impact.
- **B**: Invest the most time in the most resistant stakeholders to win them over to the new methodology.
- **C**: Decline the request because the scope is too large for one person to cover personally and well.
- **D**: Build a tiered model: shared resources, group sessions, and one-on-one mentoring where leverage is highest.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Limited time across many stakeholders is a scaling problem. (D) makes mentoring repeatable and reaches the broadest population while reserving high-touch attention for the highest-leverage cases. (A) abandons the resisters who could become blockers. (B) spends scarce time on the lowest-yield audience. (C) is a missed organisational opportunity that better design could realise.
