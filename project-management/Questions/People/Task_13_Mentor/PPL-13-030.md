# Question

A PM has been asked by leadership to mentor stakeholders across multiple projects on adopting a new project methodology. Some stakeholders are enthusiastic, others are resistant. The PM has limited time. How should they approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Focus mentoring efforts on the enthusiastic stakeholders who will benefit most
- **B**: Spend extra time with resistant stakeholders to bring them around
- **C**: Create a tiered approach with self-service resources, group sessions, and targeted one-on-one mentoring based on need and readiness
- **D**: Decline since this is too broad a scope for one person

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. With limited time and varied needs, a tiered approach maximises impact. Self-service resources (guides, videos) serve many with low effort. Group sessions create community and peer learning. One-on-one mentoring focuses on those who need or can benefit most. This scales mentoring effort effectively.

Option A abandons resistant stakeholders who may become blockers.

Option B may not be the best use of limited time if resistance is deep.

Option D misses the opportunity to have organisational impact.
