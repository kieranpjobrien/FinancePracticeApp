# Question

A PM is mentoring a junior project manager who keeps making the same mistakes despite feedback. The junior PM seems to understand in the moment but then reverts to old patterns. What should the senior PM try?

## Options

- **A**: Give up - some people can't learn
- **B**: Change the mentoring approach - try practice scenarios, real-time observation, or helping them understand the root of the pattern
- **C**: Give more critical feedback
- **D**: Do the work for them to prevent mistakes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Repeated mistakes despite feedback suggest the mentoring approach isn't working. Try alternatives: role-playing scenarios, shadowing/observing their actual work and providing real-time coaching, exploring what drives the pattern (habits, beliefs, environmental factors). Different people learn differently - adapt the approach.

Option A gives up when alternative approaches haven't been tried.

Option C - more of the same ineffective approach won't help.

Option D prevents learning and creates dependency.
