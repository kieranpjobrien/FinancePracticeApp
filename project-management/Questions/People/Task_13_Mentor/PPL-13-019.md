# Question

Why is mentoring considered important for project managers?

## Options

- **A**: It's required by PMI certification
- **B**: It helps develop team capability, succession, and knowledge transfer
- **C**: It reduces the PM's workload
- **D**: It's the primary way to resolve conflicts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring develops people, which builds team capability and organisational strength. It supports succession planning by preparing others for greater responsibility. It transfers knowledge and experience that might otherwise be lost. These benefits serve both the project and the organisation.

Option A - mentoring is valued but not a certification requirement.

Option C - mentoring requires time investment; any workload reduction comes later through developed capability.

Option D - conflict resolution is a separate skill.
