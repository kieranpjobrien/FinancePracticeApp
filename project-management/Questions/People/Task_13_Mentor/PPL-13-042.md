# Question

A project manager who already mentors three people is asked to take on two more. All five would be valuable relationships, but capacity is limited. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept both new mentees because mentoring is a core leadership obligation that should not be refused.
- **B**: Decline the two new requests in order to protect the quality and depth of the existing three relationships.
- **C**: Reduce time evenly across all five mentees so that everyone still gets a fair share of attention.
- **D**: Reassess capacity, consider group formats or referrals to other mentors, and be honest about what they can offer.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Sustainable mentoring requires honest capacity planning. (D) keeps quality intact while extending reach through different formats and partners. (A) overcommits and degrades quality for everyone. (B) closes the door without exploring alternatives. (C) dilutes effectiveness for all five.
