# Question

A PM has limited time for mentoring but believes in its importance. They currently have three mentees and are asked to take on two more. All are valuable relationships. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept all mentees since mentoring is important
- **B**: Decline new mentees to maintain quality with current relationships
- **C**: Assess capacity realistically, possibly transition some relationships to group format or other mentors, and be honest about what can be offered
- **D**: Reduce time with all mentees equally to accommodate everyone

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sustainable mentoring requires honest capacity assessment. Options include: group mentoring for some topics, transitioning mentees to other mentors, varying intensity based on need, or honestly declining. What matters is providing quality support within realistic constraints.

Option A risks overcommitment and reduced quality for all.

Option B may miss valuable opportunities without exploring alternatives.

Option D dilutes effectiveness for everyone.
