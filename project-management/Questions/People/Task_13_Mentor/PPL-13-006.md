# Question

A senior stakeholder unfamiliar with agile is frustrated by the apparent absence of detailed upfront planning and keeps requesting traditional project documents the team does not produce. What mentoring approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Generate the requested documents to maintain a positive relationship.
- **B**: Decline the requests since agile teams do not produce those artefacts.
- **C**: Ask the project sponsor to redirect the stakeholder away from delivery decisions.
- **D**: Walk the stakeholder through agile's planning approach and show what information is genuinely available.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The stakeholder's information needs are valid even if their framing is traditional. (D) bridges the gap by translating their needs into the artefacts agile actually produces (roadmaps, release plans, sprint outcomes). (A) sustains the misframe and creates parallel work. (B) is dismissive and damages the relationship. (C) escalates rather than mentors.
