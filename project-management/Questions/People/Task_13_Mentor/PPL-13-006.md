# Question

A stakeholder who is new to agile methodology is frustrated with the apparent lack of detailed upfront planning. They keep asking for documents that agile teams don't typically produce. What mentoring approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Create the documents to keep the stakeholder happy
- **B**: Ignore the requests since agile doesn't require those documents
- **C**: Explain agile principles, help them understand what information is available and how it differs from traditional planning
- **D**: Suggest they work with a different project team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The stakeholder has legitimate information needs but is framing them in traditional terms. Mentoring means helping them understand agile's approach to planning - emergent, iterative, and based on working software rather than detailed upfront documentation. Show them what artifacts exist and how they can get the visibility they need.

Option A (create documents) undermines agile practices and doesn't address the underlying misunderstanding.

Option B (ignore) fails to help the stakeholder adapt and creates ongoing friction.

Option D (remove from project) avoids the opportunity to build understanding.
