# Question

During a mentoring session, a mentee confides that they have been working sixty-hour weeks for months, are sleeping poorly, and feel they may be unsafe to drive home some nights. The mentee asks the PM to keep this private. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Respect the confidentiality and focus the next few sessions on helping the mentee manage workload, without disclosing anything to anyone else at all.
- **B**: Break the confidentiality immediately by informing HR and the mentee's line manager of the safety concern raised during the mentoring session.
- **C**: Explain the safety limit of confidentiality, support the mentee to raise the issue themselves, and agree a clear timeframe before the mentor escalates.
- **D**: Suggest some self-care resources, note the conversation privately, and continue sessions as normal while monitoring for any further concerning signals over time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's duty-of-care and ethics anchors: confidentiality has a safety limit, and the right move is transparency about that limit, supporting the mentee to act, with a clear timeframe before escalation. (A) accepts unbounded confidentiality even for a clear safety issue. (B) breaches trust with no dialogue. (D) treats a safety issue with generic resources and waiting.
