# Question

A mentoring relationship has been in place for nine months. The mentor realises neither party has ever reviewed whether the relationship is producing what either of them hoped. Sessions have just continued by habit. What should the mentor do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Continue as usual since both parties keep showing up to the sessions, which is the strongest signal that they are finding genuine value in the arrangement.
- **B**: Bring a polished mentor-prepared self-evaluation to the next session and ask the mentee to respond to the mentor's assessment of how well things are going.
- **C**: End the relationship at the one-year mark with no mid-point review since an end-of-cycle retrospective will capture learnings more usefully than an interim check-in will.
- **D**: Schedule a dedicated review session to co-reflect on what is working, what is not, and whether to continue, reshape, or formally close the mentoring relationship.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's inspect-and-adapt principle applies to mentoring — a dedicated co-reflection session keeps the relationship purposeful and the mentee in a learning frame. (A) treats attendance as a proxy for value. (B) reverses the mentee-led stance. (C) waits too long and loses the chance to course-correct.
