# Question

A project sponsor is new to sponsoring agile projects. They frequently request detailed Gantt charts and ask why the team can't commit to exact dates far in advance. How should the project manager mentor them?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the Gantt charts and date commitments they're requesting
- **B**: Refuse their requests and explain that agile doesn't work that way
- **C**: Help them understand agile planning through analogies, demonstrate how roadmaps and release planning provide appropriate visibility, and find formats that meet their underlying needs
- **D**: Suggest they take an agile training course

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The sponsor has legitimate needs (visibility, planning) but is requesting them in waterfall formats. Mentoring means helping them understand how agile meets those needs differently - roadmaps, release planning, velocity-based forecasting. Find formats that serve their actual needs without forcing waterfall artifacts onto agile work.

Option A gives them what they asked for but creates false precision and may set unrealistic expectations.

Option B is confrontational and doesn't help them understand alternatives.

Option D outsources your mentoring responsibility. The sponsor needs practical, contextual guidance.
