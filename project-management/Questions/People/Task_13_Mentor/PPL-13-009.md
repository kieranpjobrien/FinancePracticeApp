# Question

A senior stakeholder asks the project manager for advice on a difficult political situation involving another executive. The matter is outside the project itself. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Decline because the topic falls outside the project manager's remit.
- **B**: Share useful perspective briefly while being clear about the limits of your knowledge and role.
- **C**: Provide detailed advice to deepen the relationship with the senior stakeholder.
- **D**: Recommend the stakeholder consult a professional executive coach for the matter.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mentoring relationships sometimes extend beyond the formal scope, but only carefully. (B) is helpful without overstepping; it acknowledges relationship value while being honest about what you do not know. (A) misses an appropriate moment to add value. (C) overcommits and risks misadvising on partial information. (D) is overly formal for what is being asked and misses an immediate, lower-stakes contribution.
