# Question

A senior stakeholder asks the PM for advice on handling a difficult political situation with another executive. This is outside the project context. Should the PM engage?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse because it's not project-related
- **B**: If you have relevant insight, share it briefly while being clear about the limits of your knowledge and your role
- **C**: Provide extensive advice to build the relationship
- **D**: Gossip about the other executive to bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring relationships sometimes extend beyond formal boundaries. If you have useful perspective, sharing it briefly can strengthen the relationship. However, be clear about limits - you may not have full context, it's not your area of expertise, and you're not advising officially. This balance is helpful without overstepping.

Option A misses a relationship-building opportunity.

Option C overcommits beyond appropriate bounds.

Option D is unprofessional and damages trust.
