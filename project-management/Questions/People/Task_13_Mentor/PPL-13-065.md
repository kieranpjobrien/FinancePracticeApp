# Question

A mentee is struggling to run effective stakeholder workshops and keeps describing the sessions to the mentor in ways that suggest the mentee's self-perception differs from what the stakeholders are experiencing. The mentor wants to understand what is actually happening. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the mentee to run their next workshop as usual and report back afterwards, with both of them reviewing the recording together to identify development areas.
- **B**: Help the mentee role-play a mock workshop so the mentor can give feedback directly based on the mentee's behaviour observed in a controlled, simulated environment.
- **C**: Arrange to observe the mentee's next real workshop directly, with the mentee's prior consent and stakeholders informed, and debrief openly afterwards with the mentee.
- **D**: Interview a few of the mentee's workshop stakeholders privately to gather their views on how the mentee is performing during the sessions and what could improve.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring approach values direct observation with consent — it gives the mentor ground truth and respects the mentee and the stakeholders involved. (A) relies on a recording that misses dynamics. (B) uses a mock that cannot replicate real stakeholder behaviour. (D) goes behind the mentee's back to gather feedback and breaches the mentoring relationship.
