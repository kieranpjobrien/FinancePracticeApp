# Question

How should a mentoring relationship typically end?

## Options

- **A**: When the project ends
- **B**: When the mentor decides the mentee no longer needs support
- **C**: Through mutual agreement when objectives have been met or circumstances change
- **D**: Mentoring relationships should never end

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Healthy mentoring relationships have natural endpoints. When development objectives are achieved, when the mentee no longer needs that specific support, or when circumstances change (job moves, relationship evolution), ending the formal relationship makes sense. This should be a mutual, discussed decision.

Option A ties mentoring to project boundaries unnecessarily.

Option B makes it a unilateral decision.

Option D doesn't recognise that relationships appropriately evolve.
