# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between mentoring and coaching?

## Options

- **A**: Mentoring is for senior staff, coaching is for junior staff
- **B**: Mentoring focuses on long-term development and career guidance, while coaching focuses on specific skills or tasks
- **C**: Mentoring is formal, coaching is informal
- **D**: Mentoring is provided by HR, coaching by the project manager

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring is typically a longer-term relationship focused on overall professional development, career guidance, and sharing wisdom from experience. Coaching is usually more targeted at developing specific skills, improving performance on particular tasks, or achieving defined short-term goals.

Option A - both can apply to any level.

Option C - both can be formal or informal depending on the organisation.

Option D - both can be provided by various people in the organisation.
