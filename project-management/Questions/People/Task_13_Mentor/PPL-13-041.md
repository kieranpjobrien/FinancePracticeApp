# Question

In a mentoring session, a team member shares concerning information about another project that the project manager was unaware of. The information suggests issues that should be raised. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the information in confidence to protect the trust of the mentoring relationship.
- **B**: Escalate the concerns to leadership immediately, given the project risk.
- **C**: Discuss with the mentee how the information should be raised and encourage them to escalate through the right channels.
- **D**: Use the information in their own conversations with leadership without naming the source.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Mentoring trust and organisational responsibility coexist when the mentor coaches the mentee on how to act. (C) preserves the relationship and develops the mentee's professional judgement. (A) lets a real risk persist. (B) breaks confidence and bypasses the mentee. (D) is dishonest and risks misrepresenting facts the mentor heard secondhand.
