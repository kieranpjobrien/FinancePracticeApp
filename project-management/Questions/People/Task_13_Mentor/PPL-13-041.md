# Question

During a mentoring session, a team member shares information about problems on another project that their mentor isn't aware of. The information suggests issues that should be escalated. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the information confidential to maintain trust
- **B**: Immediately escalate the information to leadership
- **C**: Discuss with the mentee how the information should be handled and encourage them to raise it through appropriate channels
- **D**: Use the information informally without revealing the source

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mentoring relationships involve navigating confidentiality and organisational responsibility. Discussing with the mentee respects the relationship while addressing the issue. Encouraging them to raise it appropriately maintains their agency and develops their professional judgment about escalation.

Option A may allow serious issues to go unaddressed.

Option B may damage trust and doesn't involve the mentee in resolution.

Option D uses information without transparency.
