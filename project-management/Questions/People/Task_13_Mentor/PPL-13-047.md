# Question

A new mentee tells the PM in their first session that they "don't really know what they want" and are hoping the mentor will tell them what their career should look like. The mentee has eight years of experience and a strong track record already. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Prepare a tailored career plan based on the mentee's strengths and bring it to the next session for them to review and adopt.
- **B**: Work with the mentee through structured reflection and exploration exercises so they can articulate their own goals over several sessions.
- **C**: Refer the mentee to a career-coaching service within the organisation since goal-setting at their stage is not really a mentoring responsibility.
- **D**: Suggest a few common career paths people at their level typically take and ask the mentee to pick the one that sounds most appealing.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring approach is mentee-led — helping the mentee find their own goals builds ownership and self-knowledge that last beyond the relationship. (A) hands them goals they did not choose, which rarely motivate. (C) outsources a core mentoring activity. (D) narrows thinking to existing paths and still offers choice without reflection.
