# Question

A mentee asks their PM mentor for advice about an ethical dilemma: they've discovered a colleague falsifying time sheets. The mentee isn't sure what to do. What is the mentor's BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them exactly what to do
- **B**: Stay out of it since it's their decision
- **C**: Help them think through the situation, their options, potential consequences, and their values
- **D**: Report it themselves since they now know about it

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ethical dilemmas are valuable development opportunities. Helping the mentee think through the situation (what are the facts?), options (report, confront, ignore), consequences (for each option), and values (what kind of professional do they want to be?) develops their ethical reasoning.

Option A doesn't develop their judgment.

Option B provides no guidance on an important situation.

Option D takes over their responsibility and development opportunity (though the mentor may also have obligations).
