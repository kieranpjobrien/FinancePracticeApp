# Question

A PM is about to start a new formal mentoring relationship. The mentee is enthusiastic and wants to get straight into discussing a current challenge they are facing at work in the very first session. What should the mentor do at the start of that first session?

## Options

- **A**: Spend time establishing a clear mentoring agreement — purpose, boundaries, cadence, confidentiality — before moving on to the mentee's specific current challenge.
- **B**: Start with the mentee's current challenge because engaging immediately with real work builds trust and shows the mentor's commitment to practical support.
- **C**: Send a written mentoring-agreement template for the mentee to sign in their own time, and use the session for whatever the mentee most wants to discuss today.
- **D**: Do a brief introduction and get straight into the challenge, noting that the agreement conversation can happen at the end once rapport is already well established.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI-aligned mentoring is built on an explicit agreement first — purpose, cadence, boundaries and confidentiality set the frame for everything that follows. (B) prioritises engagement now at the cost of the frame that makes it safe and useful. (C) makes agreement a paperwork task rather than a shared conversation. (D) leaves the frame until after the work has already happened under unclear terms.
