# Question

A mentee expresses frustration that they're not advancing as quickly as their peers. They feel they're doing everything right but not getting recognition. What mentoring response would be MOST helpful?

## Options

- **A**: Agree that the organisation is unfair
- **B**: Tell them to be more patient
- **C**: Explore what "advancing" means to them, what they might be missing, and whether their self-perception matches how others see them
- **D**: Recommend they look for opportunities elsewhere

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Frustration about advancement often has multiple factors. Exploring their definition of advancement clarifies goals. Examining what might be missing opens development areas. Checking perception alignment identifies blind spots. This thorough exploration provides a foundation for constructive action.

Option A validates frustration without helping them grow.

Option B dismisses the concern without understanding it.

Option D may be premature before understanding the real issue.
