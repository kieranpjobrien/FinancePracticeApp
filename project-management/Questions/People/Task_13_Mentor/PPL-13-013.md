# Question

A stakeholder asks the PM to help them prepare for a board presentation about the project. This is outside the PM's formal responsibilities. Should the PM help?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse since it's not part of their job
- **B**: Help prepare the presentation, viewing it as relationship-building and ensuring the project is accurately represented
- **C**: Prepare the entire presentation for them
- **D**: Tell them to ask someone else

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is an opportunity for productive mentoring and relationship-building. Helping the stakeholder prepare ensures the project is represented accurately and builds goodwill. The PM has valuable insight into project details. This falls within supporting stakeholders even if not formally required.

Option A misses an opportunity to help a stakeholder succeed.

Option C does the work for them rather than developing their capability.

Option D damages the relationship without good reason.
