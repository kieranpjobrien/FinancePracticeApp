# Question

A PM has been informally mentoring a stakeholder from another department. The stakeholder now asks the PM to advocate for their promotion with their manager. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Advocate strongly since they know the person's capabilities
- **B**: Decline to get involved in another department's personnel decisions
- **C**: Clarify the boundaries of the mentoring relationship while offering to provide a reference or recommendation if appropriate
- **D**: Tell the stakeholder they're not ready for promotion

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mentoring relationships benefit from clear boundaries. The PM can offer appropriate support (a reference, recommendation, or feedback to help them prepare) without overstepping into advocacy that may not be their role. Clarity about boundaries maintains the relationship's integrity.

Option A may overstep and could create awkwardness with the other department.

Option B misses the opportunity to provide legitimate support.

Option D makes a judgment that may not be appropriate for the PM to make.
