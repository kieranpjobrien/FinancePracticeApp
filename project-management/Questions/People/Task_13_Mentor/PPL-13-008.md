# Question

A product owner is struggling with backlog prioritisation. They treat everything as high priority, leading to sprint overcommitment. What mentoring approach should the Scrum Master take?

## Options

- **A**: Prioritise the backlog for them
- **B**: Teach prioritisation frameworks (MoSCoW, value vs effort) and coach through several prioritisation sessions
- **C**: Tell the team to push back on the product owner
- **D**: Ignore it until it causes problems

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring means developing capability, not doing the work for them. Teach frameworks that provide structure for prioritisation decisions. Coach through actual prioritisation sessions, asking questions that help the PO think through trade-offs. This builds lasting capability rather than creating dependency.

Option A solves the immediate problem but doesn't develop the PO's skills.

Option C creates conflict rather than addressing capability.

Option D allows problems to develop before addressing root cause.
