# Question

The product owner consistently treats every backlog item as high priority, leading to chronic sprint overcommitment. What mentoring approach should the Scrum Master take?

## Options

- **A**: Reorder the backlog for the product owner ahead of each sprint planning session.
- **B**: Tell the development team to refuse any sprint scope they consider unrealistic.
- **C**: Wait for a sprint to fail visibly so the product owner experiences the consequences.
- **D**: Walk the product owner through prioritisation frameworks and coach them through several real prioritisation sessions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Mentoring builds capability, it does not substitute for it. (D) gives the product owner tools (MoSCoW, value vs. effort, cost of delay) and reinforces them through guided practice on actual decisions. (A) creates dependency. (B) creates conflict between the team and the product owner without addressing the root cause. (C) is a punitive non-intervention that damages trust.
