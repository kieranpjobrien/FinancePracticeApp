# Question

A formal twelve-month mentoring programme is ending next month. The mentee has grown significantly and the mentor is proud of the relationship. They both feel the relationship could continue informally beyond the programme. What should the mentor do to close the formal phase?

## Options

- **A**: Simply let the last scheduled session happen and continue as usual afterwards, since both parties clearly already want the relationship to carry on.
- **B**: Send a closing email summarising key learnings from the year and offer to remain available for occasional questions on an as-needed basis from now on.
- **C**: Wait until after the last session before discussing anything about ongoing informal contact, so the formal programme's end is cleanly observed before anything new is agreed.
- **D**: Run a dedicated closing session reviewing the year, acknowledging growth, and explicitly re-contracting the future relationship and its new terms with the mentee.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring lifecycle treats closure as a deliberate act — a dedicated session marks the end of the formal relationship, consolidates learning and allows explicit recontracting for any continuation. (A) blurs the boundary and loses the learning opportunity of a proper close. (B) reduces closure to an email and leaves future contact vague. (C) observes the programme's end but misses the closure and recontracting in one integrated move.
