# Question

A PMO director has asked the senior PM to run a regular "office hours" for the ten PMs across the portfolio. The senior PM is worried this might blur into providing ongoing mentoring relationships with every attendee without any formal agreement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the office hours request and offer instead to take on two or three of the PMs as formal mentees with proper agreements and a regular session cadence.
- **B**: Accept the office hours as a standing session, let individual mentoring relationships emerge naturally over time, and formalise any that deepen into proper mentoring later on.
- **C**: Accept the office hours and treat every attendee as an informal mentee without any formal agreement, since the volume makes individual mentoring agreements impractical on this scale.
- **D**: Run the office hours as a scoped consultation service with clear entry, exit and confidentiality rules, and reserve mentoring for separate formal agreements with specific PMs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring frame distinguishes advisory consultation from mentoring — separating the two with clear scope and agreements keeps both credible and protects the PM's time. (A) refuses a useful portfolio-wide service. (B) lets boundaries form by drift rather than by design. (C) treats all interactions as mentoring without the agreements that make mentoring work.
