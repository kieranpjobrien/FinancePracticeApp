# Question

A team lead the project manager mentors is technically strong but consistently avoids difficult conversations with their team. Performance issues are going unaddressed as a result. What mentoring approach should the project manager use?

## Options

- **A**: Step in and deliver the difficult feedback to the team lead's people directly.
- **B**: Advise the team lead to "just be more direct" and trust they will figure it out.
- **C**: Use role-play, debrief on what feels hard, and offer support before and after real conversations.
- **D**: Recommend a leadership development course and revisit the issue in three months.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Difficult conversations are a learnable skill, and skill is built through scaffolded practice. (C) provides safe rehearsal, explores the underlying discomfort, and offers a support loop around real attempts. (A) takes over and prevents learning. (B) is direction without development. (D) defers a problem the team is feeling now in favour of slower formal training.
