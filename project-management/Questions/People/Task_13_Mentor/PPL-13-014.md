# Question

The PM is mentoring a team lead who has good technical skills but struggles with difficult conversations. They avoid giving constructive feedback to their team members, and performance issues go unaddressed. What mentoring approach should the PM use?

## Options

- **A**: Give the feedback for them
- **B**: Role-play difficult conversations, debrief on what made them hard, and be available to support before and after real conversations
- **C**: Tell them to just be more direct
- **D**: Accept that some people aren't good at difficult conversations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Difficult conversations are a skill that can be developed. Role-play provides safe practice. Debriefing explores the fear or discomfort ("What specifically felt hard?"). Offering support before real conversations (planning together) and after (reviewing how it went) provides scaffolding while they build capability.

Option A doesn't develop their skill.

Option C provides direction without development support.

Option D accepts a development need without attempting to address it.
