# Question

A mentoring relationship has been in place for four months. In the last two sessions, the mentee has been noticeably quiet, short with answers, and once cancelled at short notice. Previously they were engaged. The PM is unsure whether to push or pull back. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Name the change openly, share what the mentor has observed, and invite the mentee to say what is going on for them and what they need.
- **B**: Continue the sessions unchanged and wait patiently for the mentee to raise whatever is going on for them in their own time when they are ready.
- **C**: Suggest a three-month break and offer to resume sessions later, giving the mentee space to come back when they feel ready and engaged again.
- **D**: Ask a mutual colleague to check in with the mentee privately and report back on whether something is going on outside the mentoring context at the moment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's direct-engagement principle applies to mentoring — naming the observation gently and inviting the mentee to speak is respectful, honest and invites repair. (B) waits passively while the relationship deteriorates. (C) offers an exit when what is needed is a conversation. (D) triangulates through another person rather than addressing the mentee directly and breaches trust.
