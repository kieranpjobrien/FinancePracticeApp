# Question

A project manager mentors team members from a range of cultural backgrounds. One mentee, from a culture in which directly questioning seniors is considered disrespectful, rarely speaks up in sessions. The project manager cannot gauge their progress. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce the cadence of one-on-one sessions, since the mentee is not engaging with them.
- **B**: Explain that local norms favour direct dialogue and ask the mentee to participate accordingly.
- **C**: End the formal mentoring and refer the mentee to a culturally aligned mentor instead.
- **D**: Adapt the format to include written reflections, indirect prompts, and structured exercises.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's view of inclusive leadership puts the burden of adaptation on the mentor, not the mentee. (D) creates channels through which the mentee can engage authentically. (A) misreads cultural norms as disinterest. (B) demands the mentee abandon their cultural norms. (C) is an exit when adaptation is the right move first.
