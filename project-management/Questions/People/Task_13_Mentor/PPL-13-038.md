# Question

A PM mentors team members from diverse cultural backgrounds. One mentee comes from a culture where directly asking questions of senior people is considered disrespectful. They rarely speak in mentoring sessions, making it hard to gauge their development or needs. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Interpret their silence as disinterest and reduce mentoring frequency
- **B**: Tell them they need to adapt to the local culture and speak up
- **C**: Adapt the mentoring approach to include written exchanges, indirect questions, and structured activities that don't require direct challenging of the mentor
- **D**: End the mentoring since the format doesn't work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cultural awareness requires adapting approaches. Written exchanges allow thoughtful responses without pressure. Indirect questions ("Some people find X challenging - what do you think?") reduce directness. Structured activities provide frameworks for participation that don't require culturally uncomfortable behaviours.

Option A misinterprets cultural difference as disengagement.

Option B imposes one cultural norm and may alienate the mentee.

Option D gives up rather than adapting.
