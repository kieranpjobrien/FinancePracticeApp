# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between mentoring and coaching?

## Options

- **A**: Mentoring is reserved for senior staff; coaching is for junior staff earlier in their career.
- **B**: Mentoring shares experience to develop the person; coaching builds a specific skill or performance area.
- **C**: Mentoring happens in formal organisational programs; coaching is always informal and ad hoc.
- **D**: Mentoring is about giving advice; coaching is about giving critical feedback on performance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The two practices overlap but have a clear distinction in PMI usage. (B) captures it: mentoring is broader and experience-based; coaching is narrower and goal-focused. (A) wrongly distinguishes by seniority. (C) wrongly distinguishes by formality. (D) reduces both to one-way feedback and misses the developmental nature of each.
