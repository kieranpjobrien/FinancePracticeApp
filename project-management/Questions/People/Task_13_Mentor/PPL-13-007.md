# Question

What is the difference between mentoring and coaching?

## Options

- **A**: Mentoring is for senior people; coaching is for juniors
- **B**: Mentoring shares experience and guidance for development; coaching focuses on specific skills or performance improvement
- **C**: Mentoring is formal; coaching is informal
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. While the terms overlap, there are distinctions:

**Mentoring** typically involves:
- Sharing wisdom and experience
- Broader career/development focus
- Longer-term relationship
- "Let me tell you what I've learned"

**Coaching** typically involves:
- Developing specific skills or behaviours
- Performance-focused
- May be shorter-term
- "Let me help you discover your own answers"

Options A and C incorrectly distinguish by seniority or formality. Option D ignores meaningful differences.
