# Question

The PM is mentoring a team member. The PM is also being asked by the team member's line manager to "let me know how they are really going" from the mentoring sessions. The line manager is trying to help but the request crosses the confidentiality line. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide a sanitised summary to the line manager each month without sharing specifics, framed as a helpful high-level update on the mentee's development.
- **B**: Decline the request, explain why confidentiality is structural to mentoring working, and offer to coach the line manager on speaking to the mentee directly.
- **C**: Accept the request quietly, since the line manager is ultimately accountable for the team member's development and has a legitimate interest in their progress.
- **D**: Ask the mentee whether they would be comfortable with the PM sharing select updates with the line manager on their behalf as a trusted intermediary.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's confidentiality principle is structural to mentoring — declining clearly and coaching the line manager on direct engagement is the PMI-aligned move. (A) pretends confidentiality is preserved while routinely breaching it. (C) breaches confidentiality in exchange for comfort with the line manager. (D) puts the mentee in an impossible position given the power dynamic with their own manager.
