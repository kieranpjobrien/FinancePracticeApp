# Question

A team member asks the PM to mentor them on career development. The PM has relevant experience but limited time. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Decline because of time constraints
- **B**: Agree to regular but bounded sessions (e.g., monthly) and be clear about the time commitment
- **C**: Agree to unlimited availability
- **D**: Delegate mentoring to someone else

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Mentoring doesn't require unlimited time. Structured, bounded sessions (monthly 30-minute check-ins, for example) can be highly effective. Being clear about the commitment sets expectations and ensures the time you give is focused and valuable. Quality matters more than quantity.

Option A misses an opportunity when a bounded approach is possible.

Option C is unrealistic and leads to resentment or withdrawal later.

Option D may be appropriate if someone else is better positioned, but the question implies the PM has relevant experience.
