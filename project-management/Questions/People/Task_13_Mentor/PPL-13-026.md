# Question

A PM is mentoring someone who has a very different work style and personality. The PM finds it challenging to connect and wonders if the mentoring relationship is effective. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: End the relationship and suggest they find a mentor with a more similar style
- **B**: Try to change the mentee's work style to match their own
- **C**: Adapt their mentoring approach to suit the mentee's style while being open about the differences
- **D**: Continue the same approach and assume the mentee will adapt

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective mentors adapt to their mentees. Different styles can be valuable - it challenges both parties to grow. Being open about differences ("I notice we approach things differently...") creates dialogue and mutual understanding. The mentor should adjust their approach rather than expecting the mentee to change.

Option A misses the growth opportunity for both parties.

Option B imposes the mentor's style rather than developing the mentee's strengths.

Option D assumes the mentee should adapt when it's the mentor's role to meet them where they are.
