# Question

A project manager is mentoring someone whose work style and personality are very different from their own. The project manager is finding it hard to connect and wonders if the relationship is effective. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: End the relationship and suggest a mentor whose style is more aligned with the mentee's.
- **B**: Adapt the mentoring style to the mentee and openly discuss how they each work best.
- **C**: Hold a longer first session each month to build rapport more quickly.
- **D**: Continue with the current approach, since the mentee will eventually adapt.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective mentors flex to the mentee, not the other way around. (B) is the PMI-aligned response and surfaces difference as a topic rather than a barrier. (A) gives up on a real growth opportunity for both parties. (C) treats time as the missing ingredient when style adaptation is the issue. (D) places the burden of adaptation on the mentee.
