# Question

A PM has successfully mentored a team member who has now been promoted and is doing excellent work. The former mentee rarely contacts the PM anymore. The PM feels a bit disappointed by the reduced contact. What does this situation indicate?

## Options

- **A**: The mentoring relationship failed
- **B**: The former mentee is ungrateful
- **C**: The mentoring was successful - the mentee has developed independence and no longer needs intensive support
- **D**: The PM should reinitiate regular mentoring sessions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Successful mentoring often results in decreased need for the mentor. Independence is the goal. The mentee's excellent performance and reduced contact indicate they've internalised what they learned and can now operate independently. This is success, not failure.

Option A misreads the outcome.

Option B applies an unfair judgment - professional growth includes independence.

Option D may not serve the mentee's current needs.
