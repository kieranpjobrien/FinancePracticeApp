# Question

A PM discovers that a mentee has used advice from their mentoring sessions to undermine a colleague's work and advance their own position. The advice was sound but was misapplied. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue mentoring as before - how advice is used isn't the mentor's responsibility
- **B**: End the mentoring relationship immediately
- **C**: Have a direct conversation about ethics and professional behaviour, and make future mentoring conditional on appropriate conduct
- **D**: Report the behaviour to HR

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Mentoring includes developing professional character, not just skills. A direct conversation addresses the behaviour ("I've noticed... this concerns me because...") and sets expectations. Making future mentoring conditional reinforces that development includes ethics. This is a teachable moment.

Option A ignores a serious character issue that mentoring should address.

Option B doesn't provide the feedback that might help them grow.

Option D may be appropriate eventually but should follow a direct conversation first.
