# Question

A project manager learns that a mentee has used insight from their mentoring sessions to undermine a colleague's work and improve their own positioning. The advice itself was sound but was applied unethically. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a direct conversation about the behaviour, set clear expectations, and make continued mentoring conditional on professional conduct.
- **B**: End the mentoring relationship immediately without further discussion.
- **C**: Continue mentoring; what the mentee does with the advice is not the mentor's concern.
- **D**: Report the behaviour to HR and let the formal process play out.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Mentoring includes character, not just craft. (A) confronts the behaviour directly, gives the mentee an opportunity to reflect, and ties continued investment to professional standards. (B) closes the door without the conversation that might prompt change. (C) abdicates a teachable moment that PMI's ethics framework places squarely in the mentor's lane. (D) escalates before a direct conversation has been attempted.
