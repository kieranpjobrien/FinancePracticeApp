# Question

A functional manager who provides team members to projects has a reputation for micromanaging and frequently pulls team members into department work during sprints. Multiple attempts to explain agile principles have been unsuccessful. What approach should the project manager try next?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the situation since functional managers have authority over their staff
- **B**: Escalate to the PMO or executive sponsor to enforce agile team commitments
- **C**: Invite the functional manager to observe sprint events and see how the team works, helping them understand the impact of disruptions firsthand
- **D**: Request dedicated resources who don't report to this manager

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The manager may be resistant because they don't understand how agile teams work in practice. Observing sprint events provides experiential learning that explanations haven't achieved. Seeing the team's rhythm and how interruptions disrupt flow can be more persuasive than abstract principles. This is mentoring through experience.

Option A accepts a dysfunctional situation without further effort. The PM should continue seeking solutions.

Option B may be necessary eventually, but escalation before exhausting engagement options damages relationships. The manager may become more resistant if they feel forced.

Option D avoids the problem rather than solving it and may not be feasible. It also doesn't help the organization build better understanding of agile.
