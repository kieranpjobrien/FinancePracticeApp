# Question

A junior team member shows strong technical skills but struggles with stakeholder communication. They recently sent an email to executives that was technically accurate but came across as dismissive. What mentoring approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Handle all executive communication for the team member to prevent future issues
- **B**: Share specific feedback about the email, discuss the impact, and coach on adapting communication style for different audiences
- **C**: Send them to a formal communication training course
- **D**: Let them learn from the consequences of their communication style

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective mentoring uses specific examples to provide actionable feedback. Discussing the actual email helps the person understand what didn't land well and why. Coaching on audience awareness (executives vs. peers) builds a skill they can apply broadly. This is supportive development, not just feedback.

Option A (take over) prevents learning and limits the team member's growth.

Option C (formal training) may help but doesn't address the immediate situation or provide personalised feedback.

Option D (let them learn) is abdication. The PM should actively support development, not just observe consequences.
