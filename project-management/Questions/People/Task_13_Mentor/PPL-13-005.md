# Question

A junior team member is technically strong but recently sent an email to executives that, while accurate, came across as dismissive. What is the BEST mentoring approach for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Take over executive communication for the team member to prevent further missteps.
- **B**: Give specific feedback on the email, explore the impact, and coach on adapting tone for different audiences.
- **C**: Enrol the team member in a corporate communications training course.
- **D**: Allow them to face the executive reaction so they learn from natural consequences.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective mentoring is timely, specific, and behavioural. (B) uses the actual artefact, explores why it landed badly, and builds a transferable skill. (A) protects the team member at the cost of growth. (C) is a slower, less personalised intervention and ignores the immediate moment. (D) is abdication; allowing avoidable damage is not the same as building capability.
