# Question

A product owner who is new to agile insists on writing very detailed specifications and approving every technical decision the team makes. This is creating bottlenecks and frustration. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the product owner on the agile boundary between "what" and "why" (their domain) and "how" (the team's domain).
- **B**: Ask the team to be patient with the product owner while they learn the role on the job.
- **C**: Take on some of the product owner's tasks to relieve the bottleneck for the team.
- **D**: Raise the issue with the product owner's manager so they can adjust the behaviour.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The behaviour reflects unfamiliarity with the role, not bad intent, so coaching is the right first response. (A) gives the product owner a clear, agile-specific framing they can apply immediately. (B) leaves the team frustrated and never addresses the cause. (C) blurs accountabilities and creates dependency. (D) escalates a coachable behaviour and skips the project manager's own responsibility to develop stakeholders.
