# Question

A product owner new to agile frequently provides overly detailed specifications and wants to approve all technical decisions. This creates bottlenecks and frustration for the development team. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team to be patient while the product owner learns
- **B**: Take over some of the product owner's duties to reduce bottlenecks
- **C**: Coach the product owner on their role in agile - focusing on the "what" and "why" while trusting the team with the "how"
- **D**: Escalate the issue to the product owner's manager

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The product owner's behavior likely stems from unfamiliarity with agile roles rather than bad intent. Coaching helps them understand the boundary between product (their domain) and technical (team domain) decisions. This is mentoring - helping someone develop new skills and perspectives for their role.

Option A doesn't address the underlying issue and leaves the team frustrated. Patience without coaching doesn't lead to improvement.

Option B undermines the product owner role and doesn't help them develop. It may also overburden the PM and create confusion about responsibilities.

Option D escalates what should be a coaching opportunity. The behavior isn't misconduct - it's inexperience that can be addressed through mentoring.
