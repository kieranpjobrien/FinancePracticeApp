# Question

What is a key characteristic of effective mentoring?

## Options

- **A**: The mentor does the work for the mentee
- **B**: The mentor shares experience, asks guiding questions, and supports the mentee's own growth and decision-making
- **C**: The mentor only gives negative feedback
- **D**: Mentoring requires formal organisational approval

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective mentoring guides rather than directs. Sharing experience provides context. Asking questions helps the mentee think through situations. Supporting their growth means celebrating wins and helping through challenges. The goal is developing the mentee's capability, not creating dependency.

Option A creates dependency rather than development.

Option C - effective mentoring includes both positive and constructive feedback.

Option D - while formal programs exist, mentoring often happens informally.
