# Question

A PM has been formally matched with a mentee through a corporate programme. After three sessions it is clear the chemistry is poor — the mentee's learning style and goals do not align with what the PM can offer. The programme expects matched pairs to complete twelve months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Persist with the matching for the full twelve months as the programme intended, since committing to the structure has value independent of short-term chemistry.
- **B**: Quietly let the sessions become less frequent and wait for the mentee to stop booking them, allowing the relationship to end without a formal conversation.
- **C**: Complete the minimum contact requirement and focus the sessions on generic PM advice rather than the mentee's specific goals so neither side is disappointed.
- **D**: Raise the mismatch openly with the mentee and the programme, and explore rematching so both parties get a more useful and productive mentoring experience.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's honesty and integrity principles mean raising the mismatch openly and working with both parties to find a better arrangement — this respects the mentee's development and the programme's intent. (A) preserves a structure that is not producing value. (B) lets the relationship decay without a conversation. (C) goes through the motions and wastes both parties' time.
