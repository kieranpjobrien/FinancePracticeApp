# Question

During a mentoring session, a team member shares that they're struggling with work-life balance and considering leaving the organisation. This is outside the typical project mentoring scope. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Redirect the conversation back to project-related topics
- **B**: Listen supportively, explore the situation, and consider whether appropriate support or resources can help
- **C**: Immediately inform HR about the retention risk
- **D**: Tell them what they should do based on the PM's own experience

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective mentoring often extends beyond narrow technical topics to whole-person development. Listening supportively shows care and maintains trust. Exploring the situation may reveal addressable issues (workload, flexibility, growth opportunities). Connecting to resources (EAP, HR for career discussions) can help if appropriate.

Option A dismisses a real concern and may damage the relationship.

Option C violates the trust of a mentoring conversation.

Option D may not account for the mentee's different circumstances.
