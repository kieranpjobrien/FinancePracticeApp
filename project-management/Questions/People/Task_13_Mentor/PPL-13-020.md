# Question

In a mentoring conversation, a team member confides that they are struggling with work-life balance and considering leaving. This is outside the usual project mentoring remit. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Steer the conversation back to project topics to maintain professional boundaries.
- **B**: Listen, explore the situation, and help the team member identify next steps and supports.
- **C**: Share that retention is at risk with HR so the organisation can intervene.
- **D**: Tell the team member how the project manager handled a similar dilemma in their own career.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective mentoring sometimes spills beyond narrow project topics, especially when trust and well-being are at stake. (B) respects the team member's autonomy and helps them think through options, including any organisational supports. (A) shuts down a conversation the team member chose to have. (C) breaks confidence and escalates uninvited. (D) projects the project manager's experience onto a different person and situation.
