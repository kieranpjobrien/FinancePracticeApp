# Question

A PM notices that a team member they're mentoring has started to outperform other team members and is ready for more challenging work. However, there are no immediate opportunities on the current project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep them on current work to avoid disrupting the project
- **B**: Discuss their development goals and help identify opportunities, potentially outside the current project
- **C**: Promote them immediately
- **D**: Give them additional responsibilities without additional recognition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A mentor considers the mentee's development beyond the current project. Discussing goals ensures alignment. Identifying opportunities - whether stretch assignments on this project, involvement in other initiatives, or even discussing moves to other roles - shows genuine investment in their growth.

Option A may lead to disengagement and eventual departure.

Option C isn't within the PM's authority and may not be timely.

Option D may feel exploitative without proper recognition.
