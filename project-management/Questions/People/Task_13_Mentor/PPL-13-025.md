# Question

A team member the project manager mentors has begun outperforming their peers and is ready for stretch work, but the current project has no obvious larger role for them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Discuss the team member's development goals and help them find stretch opportunities, including outside the project if needed.
- **B**: Keep them in their current role to avoid disrupting the team.
- **C**: Quietly add responsibilities without changing their title or recognition.
- **D**: Recommend they apply for a promotion in the next cycle.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A mentor's loyalty is to the mentee's growth, not solely to the mentor's own project. (A) puts the team member's development first. (B) risks disengagement and eventual departure. (C) is exploitative and erodes trust. (D) is a process step disconnected from a real conversation about what the team member actually wants.
