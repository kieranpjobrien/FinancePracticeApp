# Question

Which of the following is a core principle of principled negotiation as described in "Getting to Yes"?

## Options

- **A**: Open with an extreme position to anchor the conversation.
- **B**: Conceal your real interests so the other side cannot exploit them.
- **C**: Win as much as possible regardless of the impact on the other party.
- **D**: Insist on outcomes based on objective, mutually agreed criteria.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The four principles of "Getting to Yes" are: separate the people from the problem, focus on interests not positions, invent options for mutual gain, and insist on objective criteria — (D) restates the fourth. (A) is anchoring, a positional-bargaining tactic the book argues against. (B) contradicts the focus-on-interests principle. (C) is the win-lose mindset principled negotiation is designed to replace.
