# Question

Which of the following is a key principle of principled negotiation from "Getting to Yes"?

## Options

- **A**: Focus on winning at all costs
- **B**: Separate the people from the problem
- **C**: Never reveal your true interests
- **D**: Start with an extreme position

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Getting to Yes" by Fisher and Ury outlines principled negotiation with four key elements: separate people from the problem, focus on interests rather than positions, generate options for mutual gain, and insist on objective criteria. Separating people from the problem means addressing issues without damaging relationships.

Option A promotes adversarial approaches.

Option C contradicts focusing on interests.

Option D is a positional bargaining tactic, not principled negotiation.
