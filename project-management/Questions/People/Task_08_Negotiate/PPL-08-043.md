# Question

In sprint planning, the Product Owner wants to commit to 60 story points; the team's recent average is 40 and they consider 60 unrealistic. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Set the commitment at 50 as a midpoint between the two positions.
- **B**: Facilitate a data-led discussion using historical velocity and risks so the team and PO converge on a commitment the team genuinely believes in.
- **C**: Defer to the PO since the PO is accountable for value.
- **D**: Defer to the team since the team performs the work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In Scrum the team owns the sprint commitment, and the Scrum Master's role is to facilitate a conversation grounded in velocity, risk, and the sprint goal until the team self-commits. (A) splits the difference on a number neither side believes. (C) overrides the team's commitment authority and produces a forced commitment. (D) bypasses the value conversation and weakens PO accountability.
