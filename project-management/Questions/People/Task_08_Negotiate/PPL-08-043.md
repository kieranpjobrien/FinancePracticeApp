# Question

During agile project planning, the Product Owner and delivery team disagree on how much work can fit in the next sprint. The PO wants more; the team believes it's unrealistic. How should the Scrum Master facilitate this negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the team since they do the work
- **B**: Side with the PO since they represent the business
- **C**: Facilitate dialogue about capacity, historical velocity, and risks; help both parties understand constraints; and work toward a commitment the team believes in
- **D**: Split the difference between their positions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sprint commitment negotiations require data and dialogue. Historical velocity provides objective grounding. Understanding PO's priorities helps the team focus on highest-value items. Understanding team's concerns (complexity, risks, capacity) helps the PO set realistic expectations. The goal is a commitment the team believes in—forced commitments undermine agile principles.

Option A and B take sides rather than facilitating.

Option D may produce a number neither party believes in.
