# Question

A team lead is negotiating with a functional manager to reduce a team member's allocation to the project from 100% to 50%. The PM needs the resource full-time for the next month during a critical phase. What is the PM's strongest negotiating position?

## Options

- **A**: Appeal to the functional manager's goodwill
- **B**: Threaten to escalate if the allocation isn't maintained
- **C**: Present data on the critical phase, specific deliverables at risk, and propose a compromise - perhaps full allocation for one more month then reduced
- **D**: Accept the reduction without discussion

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective negotiation uses evidence and seeks mutual benefit. Present the business case (why full-time matters now), quantify risk (what happens at 50%), and propose a compromise that addresses both needs. The functional manager has legitimate demands too - acknowledging this while advocating for project needs leads to sustainable agreements.

Option A relies on relationship without substantive argument.

Option B is adversarial and damages ongoing relationships.

Option D doesn't advocate for project needs.
