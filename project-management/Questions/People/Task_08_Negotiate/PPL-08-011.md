# Question

A functional manager has asked to drop a team member from 100% to 50% during the project's critical design phase. What is the PM's strongest negotiating move?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the functional manager of the project's strategic priority to the wider organisation.
- **B**: Offer a personal favour later in exchange for keeping the team member's allocation at 100%.
- **C**: Propose 100% through the design phase with a planned step-down to 50% afterwards.
- **D**: Accept the 50% allocation and absorb the hit with team overtime during the design phase.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The strongest position is an evidence-backed proposal that shows the PM understands both parties' needs and offers a concrete, time-bounded structure. (A) is an appeal to authority that tends to harden the other side's position. (B) creates informal obligations and edges into ethics issues. (D) loads the cost onto the team and quietly increases delivery and burnout risk instead of negotiating.
