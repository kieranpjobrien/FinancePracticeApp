# Question

During contract negotiations, a vendor insists on payment terms that transfer significant risk to your organisation (full payment upfront, no performance guarantees). The vendor has unique capabilities needed for the project. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the terms because the vendor is unique
- **B**: Reject the vendor and find an alternative
- **C**: Negotiate risk mitigation mechanisms such as milestone-based payments, escrow arrangements, performance bonds, or penalty clauses while exploring BATNA (best alternative to negotiated agreement)
- **D**: Accept the terms but plan for vendor failure

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Unique capabilities don't mean accepting any terms. Creative negotiation can address both parties' interests—milestone payments protect the buyer while ensuring vendor cash flow. Escrow or bonds provide security. Understanding your BATNA (what happens if negotiations fail?) determines your leverage and walk-away point. Often, vendors expecting pushback build margin into initial demands.

Option A accepts unnecessary risk.

Option B may not be feasible if the vendor is truly unique.

Option D plans for failure rather than preventing it contractually.
