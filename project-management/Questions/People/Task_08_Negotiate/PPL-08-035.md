# Question

What is a "walk-away point" in negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: The ideal outcome you hope to achieve
- **B**: The point at which you will end negotiations because terms are unacceptable
- **C**: The first offer you make
- **D**: A tactical break during negotiations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The walk-away point (or reservation point) is your bottom line—the least favourable terms you'll accept before abandoning the negotiation. Knowing your walk-away point prevents accepting bad deals under pressure. It should be determined before negotiations begin based on your BATNA and true needs.

Option A describes your target or aspiration.

Option C is your opening position.

Option D is a tactical choice during negotiations.
