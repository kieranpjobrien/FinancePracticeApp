# Question

What is a negotiator's "walk-away point"?

## Options

- **A**: The opening offer made at the start of negotiations.
- **B**: The least-favourable terms beyond which the negotiator will end the negotiation rather than agree.
- **C**: The most-favourable terms the negotiator hopes to secure.
- **D**: A scheduled break used to reset the dynamic during a long session.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The walk-away point (or reservation point) is the limit beyond which it is rational to stop negotiating and pursue the BATNA instead; it should be set in advance, not improvised under pressure. (A) is the opening position. (C) is the target or aspiration. (D) describes a tactical pause, not the walk-away concept.
