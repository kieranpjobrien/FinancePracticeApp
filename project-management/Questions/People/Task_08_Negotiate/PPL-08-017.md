# Question

Which statement best describes the role of BATNA in negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: BATNA is the most aggressive opening position a negotiator should take at the outset.
- **B**: BATNA is the point at which the negotiator must accept the other side's final offer on price.
- **C**: BATNA is a checklist of mandatory contract clauses that must appear in every written agreement.
- **D**: BATNA is the negotiator's best course of action if no agreement is reached, defining walk-away power.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

BATNA stands for Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement and functions as the benchmark against which any proposed deal is measured; a strong BATNA gives real walk-away power, a weak one exposes the negotiator to concessions. (A) describes opening-position tactics, not BATNA. (B) inverts the concept — BATNA is about walking away, not capitulating. (C) conflates BATNA with contract boilerplate, which is unrelated.
