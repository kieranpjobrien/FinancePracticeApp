# Question

The internal IT department has proposed an SLA for the project that includes 24-hour response times. The PM believes the project needs faster response on production-impacting incidents. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose a tiered SLA — same-business-day for severity 1, 24-hour for everything else — backed by the project's incident impact data.
- **B**: Sign the proposed SLA and escalate later if response times become a real problem.
- **C**: Refuse to sign and request the IT director to overrule the proposal.
- **D**: Stand up a parallel managed-service contract to bypass internal IT.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A tiered SLA aligned to severity meets the project's real risk concentration without demanding more than the IT team can sustain across all tickets — the integrative move PMI favours. (B) signs the wrong commitment and queues up an avoidable escalation. (C) escalates without first attempting a peer-level redesign. (D) duplicates capability at significant cost and may breach internal sourcing policy.
