# Question

A PM is negotiating a service level agreement (SLA) with an internal IT department. The IT department proposes response times that the PM believes are too slow for project needs. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept IT's proposed SLAs
- **B**: Demand faster response times and escalate if refused
- **C**: Understand IT's constraints, share specific project requirements and business impact, explore tiered SLAs based on severity, and find a sustainable agreement
- **D**: Build an external IT capability to bypass internal IT

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Internal SLA negotiations require understanding constraints and priorities. IT may have resource limitations or competing demands. Sharing business impact helps IT prioritise. Tiered SLAs (critical issues get fast response, minor issues wait) often satisfy both parties. Sustainable agreements consider what IT can actually deliver consistently.

Option A may not meet project needs.

Option B damages internal relationships.

Option D is costly and may violate organisational policies.
