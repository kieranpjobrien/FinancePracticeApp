# Question

A vendor has proposed a fixed-price contract for a project whose detailed requirements are still emerging. What contract structure should the PM negotiate?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the fixed price to give the sponsor cost certainty.
- **B**: Negotiate a hybrid structure — time-and-materials with a not-to-exceed cap during discovery and design, transitioning to fixed price for well-defined implementation work.
- **C**: Push the vendor to a pure time-and-materials arrangement so all risk sits with the buyer's budget.
- **D**: Delay contract award until requirements are 100% defined.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's procurement guidance is to match contract type to the certainty of the work — emerging requirements suit T&M with a cap, fixed price suits the well-defined parts. (A) sets up significant change-order exposure on work that does not yet exist. (C) removes all price discipline and forfeits the vendor's incentive to manage scope. (D) is unrealistic on most real projects; some requirements only emerge during execution.
