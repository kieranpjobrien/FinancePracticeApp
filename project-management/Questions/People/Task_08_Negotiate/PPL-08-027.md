# Question

During contract negotiations, the other party makes an offer that is significantly below your target but above your walk-away point. How should you respond?

## Options

- **A**: Accept immediately since it's above your minimum
- **B**: Reject outright and restate your original demand
- **C**: Counter with a reasoned proposal that moves toward agreement while capturing more value, and continue negotiating to find the zone of possible agreement
- **D**: Walk away despite it being above your minimum

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Being above your walk-away point means agreement is possible, but accepting the first adequate offer leaves value on the table. A reasoned counter (with justification, not just a higher number) continues the negotiation constructively. The zone of possible agreement (ZOPA) exists between both parties' walk-away points—your goal is to capture as much value as possible within that zone.

Option A settles for less than achievable.

Option B may seem rigid and damage rapport.

Option D abandons a viable deal.
