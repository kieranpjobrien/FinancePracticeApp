# Question

In a contract negotiation, the other party has just offered terms that fall short of the PM's target but are still better than the PM's walk-away position. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the offer immediately to lock in a deal that beats the walk-away position.
- **B**: Walk away from the offer to signal strength and reopen on better terms later.
- **C**: Counter with a justified proposal that captures more value while staying inside the ZOPA.
- **D**: Restate the original opening position without any change to signal firmness.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A reasoned counter that explains the value being requested keeps the negotiation moving toward the upper end of the ZOPA without rupturing the dialogue. (A) leaves value on the table by accepting the first acceptable number. (B) walks away from a deal that already beats the PM's BATNA and may not return on better terms. (D) is positional rigidity that signals no progress and stalls the conversation.
