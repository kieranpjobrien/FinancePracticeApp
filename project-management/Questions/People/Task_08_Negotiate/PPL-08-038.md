# Question

A vendor's sales lead has stated several terms are "non-negotiable, take it or leave it". What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Probe the firmness of the position by asking for the rationale, presenting market benchmarks, and offering trades that may unlock movement.
- **B**: Accept the stated terms and move on to other clauses.
- **C**: Walk away from the vendor and reopen the sourcing process.
- **D**: Mirror the tactic by declaring the PM's own non-negotiable terms in response.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

"Take it or leave it" is almost always a tactic, and the disciplined response is to test it with data and trade options before deciding it is real. (B) capitulates without testing and leaves value on the table. (C) discards a potentially viable supplier without checking whether the position is bluff. (D) escalates positional bargaining and tends to harden the deadlock instead of breaking it.
