# Question

A PM is negotiating with a vendor who uses "take it or leave it" tactics, claiming they never negotiate on certain terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the terms as stated
- **B**: Walk away immediately
- **C**: Test whether the position is truly firm by probing with data, exploring alternatives, or involving other stakeholders who may have more flexibility
- **D**: Match their aggression with aggressive counter-tactics

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. "Take it or leave it" is often a negotiating tactic, not reality. Testing firmness through specific questions, data (industry benchmarks, competitor terms), or involving different stakeholders may reveal flexibility. If truly non-negotiable, you can still negotiate other terms or decide based on overall value. Assuming firmness without testing leaves value on the table.

Option A accepts without testing.

Option B may abandon a good deal.

Option D escalates without information.
