# Question

A project manager is negotiating with a vendor for software licenses. The vendor's initial price exceeds the budget, but the software is the best fit for project requirements. The vendor has indicated some flexibility. What negotiation approach is MOST likely to achieve a successful outcome?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the initial price to maintain a good vendor relationship
- **B**: Demand a lower price and threaten to consider competitors if they don't comply
- **C**: Explore mutual interests such as volume commitments, payment terms, case study opportunities, or phased licensing that could create value for both parties
- **D**: Split the difference between the initial price and budget

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Interest-based (win-win) negotiation seeks solutions that address both parties' underlying interests. By exploring options like volume commitments, extended payment terms, or marketing value, the PM may find ways to close the gap that create value for both parties. This builds a better long-term relationship and often achieves better outcomes than positional bargaining.

Option A gives up value without attempting negotiation. The vendor indicated flexibility - not exploring it leaves value on the table.

Option B uses competitive/adversarial tactics that may damage the relationship and often don't achieve the best outcome. Even if it works short-term, it creates a transactional rather than partnership dynamic.

Option D (compromise) may leave value uncaptured. Splitting the difference is easy but doesn't explore whether better solutions exist.
