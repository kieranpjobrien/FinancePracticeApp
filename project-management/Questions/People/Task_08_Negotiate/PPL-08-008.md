# Question

A PM is negotiating project scope with a stakeholder who wants to add features without extending the timeline. The stakeholder has authority and is becoming frustrated with pushback. What negotiation approach should the PM use?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to everything to avoid conflict
- **B**: Present the trade-off clearly - additional scope requires additional time or resources, or reduced scope elsewhere - and let the stakeholder make an informed choice
- **C**: Refuse categorically and hold firm on the original scope
- **D**: Agree verbally but don't actually add the features

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM's role is to ensure informed decision-making. Present options honestly: add scope with timeline extension, add scope by removing other features, or maintain current scope/timeline. This respects the stakeholder's authority while ensuring they understand consequences. Let them choose with full information.

Option A sets the project up for failure with unrealistic commitments.

Option C is inflexible and doesn't explore options.

Option D is deceptive and destroys trust when discovered.
