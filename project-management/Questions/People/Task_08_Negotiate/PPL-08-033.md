# Question

During negotiations, the other party becomes emotional and accusatory. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Respond emotionally to show strength
- **B**: End the negotiation immediately
- **C**: Remain calm, acknowledge their concerns without accepting blame, take a break if needed, and refocus on interests and problem-solving when ready to continue
- **D**: Concede to avoid conflict

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Emotional escalation rarely leads to good outcomes. Staying calm de-escalates tension. Acknowledging concerns ("I understand this is frustrating") shows respect without accepting blame. A break allows emotions to settle. Refocusing on interests ("we both want a successful project") moves toward solutions. Emotional intelligence is essential in difficult negotiations.

Option A escalates conflict.

Option B may abandon a valuable deal over a temporary emotional moment.

Option D rewards aggressive behaviour.
