# Question

During a negotiation, the other party becomes visibly emotional and starts making personal accusations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Stay calm, acknowledge the other party's concerns without conceding the substance, and propose a short break before continuing.
- **B**: End the meeting immediately and rescheduled only after a written apology.
- **C**: Match the energy to demonstrate the PM is not intimidated and will not be steamrolled.
- **D**: Concede the disputed point to defuse the emotional escalation and move on.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's emotional intelligence guidance is to acknowledge the affect without absorbing the blame, then create space (a break) for the substance to be tackled productively. (B) over-reacts and may end a viable negotiation over a temporary moment. (C) escalates and damages the relationship the project depends on. (D) rewards the aggressive behaviour and trains the counter-party to use it again.
