# Question

During vendor down-select, a supplier's sales lead offers the PM a weekend at a resort "to discuss the proposal informally." The PM's organisation has no written gift policy covering hospitality of this scale. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the invitation but disclose it to procurement afterwards so the formal record shows that the offer was taken in good faith and transparently.
- **B**: Decline politely, record the offer in the procurement file, and propose an on-site presentation at the PM's office as the alternative working session.
- **C**: Accept only if the other shortlisted vendors are invited on equivalent terms, so that no single supplier gains an exclusive informational advantage.
- **D**: Consult the PM's manager privately, and proceed if the manager verbally confirms there is no explicit prohibition in the organisation's existing policies.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI Code of Ethics requires PMs to avoid not just actual but perceived conflicts of interest, and the absence of a written policy does not lower the standard. (A) accepts a benefit that compromises independence regardless of later disclosure. (C) normalises hospitality-based selling and does not remove the appearance problem. (D) relies on a verbal permission that does not protect the PM, the organisation, or the integrity of the procurement process.
