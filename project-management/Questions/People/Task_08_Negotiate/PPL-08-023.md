# Question

Two stakeholders have conflicting requirements that cannot both be fully satisfied. Both stakeholders are essential to project success and have equal organisational authority. How should the PM facilitate negotiation between them?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision for them
- **B**: Escalate to a higher authority to decide
- **C**: Facilitate a dialogue focusing on underlying interests, help both parties understand trade-offs, explore creative options, and work toward a mutually acceptable compromise
- **D**: Satisfy whichever stakeholder complains loudest

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM's role is facilitation, not arbitration. Helping stakeholders understand each other's interests often reveals common ground. Making trade-offs explicit enables informed compromise. Creative options may satisfy both partially or find alternatives neither considered. When stakeholders agree to a compromise they helped create, commitment is stronger.

Option A oversteps PM authority and may damage relationships.

Option B should be a last resort after facilitation fails.

Option D rewards negative behaviour and damages trust.
