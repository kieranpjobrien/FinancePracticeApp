# Question

A PM needs to negotiate a service agreement with an internal department known for being uncooperative on past projects. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge past difficulties openly, frame the conversation around shared interests, and start with small commitments.
- **B**: Open with a draft SLA mirroring industry benchmarks and ask the department to redline it.
- **C**: Brief the sponsor on the history first and ask the sponsor to attend the negotiation as backup.
- **D**: Approach the meeting fresh and avoid mentioning any past project history to set a new tone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI-aligned relationship management says historical friction must be acknowledged, not papered over, and trust is rebuilt through demonstrated small commitments before any large one. (B) skips the relational work and treats a trust problem as a contract problem. (C) brings authority into a conversation that should be peer-to-peer and signals distrust before talks start. (D) ignores relevant context the other party will absolutely remember.
