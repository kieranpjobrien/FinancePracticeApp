# Question

A PM needs to negotiate a service agreement with a department that has historically been uncooperative with projects. Past PMs have had contentious relationships. How should the PM approach this negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Expect the worst and prepare for confrontation
- **B**: Pretend the history doesn't exist
- **C**: Acknowledge past challenges, express genuine interest in a better working relationship, focus on mutual interests, and demonstrate reliability through small commitments before larger agreements
- **D**: Escalate preemptively to get management support

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Past relationship problems don't doom future collaboration. Acknowledging history without assigning blame shows awareness. Focusing on shared interests ("we both want successful projects") reframes the relationship. Building trust through small, reliable commitments creates positive momentum. People often respond to genuine attempts at better relationships.

Option A creates self-fulfilling prophecy.

Option B ignores relevant context.

Option D creates adversarial dynamics before attempting collaboration.
