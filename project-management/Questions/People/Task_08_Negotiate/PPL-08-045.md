# Question

A PM is negotiating a change request with a customer. The customer wants the change at no additional cost, arguing it should have been included originally. The PM believes it's genuinely new scope. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse the change entirely
- **B**: Accept the change at no cost to preserve the relationship
- **C**: Review original requirements together to establish shared understanding, acknowledge ambiguity if it exists, and negotiate a fair resolution that considers both the relationship and appropriate compensation for genuine additional work
- **D**: Escalate to legal to determine who is contractually correct

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Scope disputes often arise from ambiguity. Joint review of original documentation may clarify whether the item was included. If genuinely ambiguous, acknowledging this shows fairness. Fair resolution might involve cost-sharing, trading for future value, or adjusting other terms. The goal is maintaining the relationship while ensuring sustainable project economics.

Option A may damage the relationship if any ambiguity existed.

Option B sets precedent for future disputes.

Option D escalates when resolution may be achievable.
