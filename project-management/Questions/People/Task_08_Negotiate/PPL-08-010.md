# Question

What is BATNA in negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: The best possible outcome from the current negotiation
- **B**: Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement - your best option if negotiation fails
- **C**: A type of contract for procurement
- **D**: A method for calculating project costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. BATNA (Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement) is your best option if the current negotiation doesn't reach agreement. Knowing your BATNA helps you negotiate with confidence - you know when to walk away. A strong BATNA gives negotiating power; a weak BATNA means you may need to accept less favourable terms.

Option A describes the target outcome, not the alternative if negotiation fails.

Options C and D are unrelated concepts.
