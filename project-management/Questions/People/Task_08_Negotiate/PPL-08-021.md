# Question

What is the difference between a position and an interest in negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: A position is what someone says they want; an interest is the underlying need or motivation behind that position
- **C**: An interest is more important than a position
- **D**: Positions are negotiable; interests are not

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Position: "I need the report by Friday." Interest: "I need to prepare for Monday's board meeting." Understanding interests enables creative solutions that may satisfy both parties better than either original position. Perhaps the report can be delivered in stages, or key findings shared early while details follow. Focusing on interests rather than positions is fundamental to principled negotiation.

Option A misses this crucial distinction.

Option C is evaluative rather than definitional.

Option D is backwards—understanding interests enables negotiating positions.
