# Question

A PM discovers during negotiations that the other party has a deadline pressure that wasn't disclosed. This information could be used to extract concessions. What is the ethical approach?

## Options

- **A**: Exploit the deadline pressure for maximum concessions
- **B**: Ignore the information entirely
- **C**: Use the information to understand their interests and constraints, but maintain ethical negotiation practices that support a sustainable long-term relationship
- **D**: Disclose that you know about their deadline pressure

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Information asymmetry is normal in negotiations, but how you use information matters. Understanding their constraints helps find mutually beneficial solutions. Exploitative tactics may win short-term but damage relationships and reputation. Ethical negotiators seek fair outcomes that both parties can commit to, even when they have leverage.

Option A prioritises short-term gain over relationship and ethics.

Option B ignores useful context for problem-solving.

Option D may put them on defensive without adding value.
