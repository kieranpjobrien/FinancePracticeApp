# Question

During contract negotiation with a vendor, the vendor discloses they're having financial difficulties but offers a significantly lower price. The work is important but not critical. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the low price as a great deal
- **B**: Evaluate vendor viability risk against price savings, consider risk mitigation strategies like milestone payments and contingency plans
- **C**: Reject the vendor immediately due to financial risk
- **D**: Ignore the disclosure and proceed normally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The disclosure creates a risk-reward decision. The lower price is attractive, but vendor failure mid-project is costly. The PM should evaluate: How severe is the financial difficulty? What's the probability of failure? What mitigation is possible (milestone payments, performance bonds, identified backup vendors)? Make an informed decision balancing price advantage against viability risk.

Option A ignores significant risk.

Option C may reject a viable option without analysis.

Option D ignores material information.
