# Question

During contract negotiation, a vendor discloses they are under financial pressure and offers a 25% price cut if the PM signs this week. The work is important but not on the critical path. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sign this week to capture the 25% discount before the opportunity disappears entirely.
- **B**: End negotiations and re-run a full competitive sourcing process to avoid the viability risk.
- **C**: Ignore the financial disclosure and negotiate purely on price as before the disclosure.
- **D**: Evaluate vendor viability against the savings and, if proceeding, structure milestone payments.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI requires the PM to weigh total risk-adjusted value, not headline price, and to tailor the contract structure to the specific risk in play — milestone payments, performance bonds, or identified backup vendors address exactly this situation. (A) jumps on the discount without pricing the vendor-failure risk. (B) over-reacts and discards a potentially good option without analysis. (C) ignores material information that changes the risk profile of the deal.
