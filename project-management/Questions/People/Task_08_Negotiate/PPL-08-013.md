# Question

A vendor wants to add a clause limiting their liability to the contract value. The project risk of vendor failure far exceeds the contract value. How should the PM approach this negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the clause since it's standard vendor practice
- **B**: Reject any liability limitation outright
- **C**: Negotiate a compromise that provides reasonable protection for both parties - perhaps higher limits for specific risks or additional insurance requirements
- **D**: Involve lawyers and step back from the negotiation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Both positions are legitimate - vendors need to limit exposure, buyers need protection. Creative solutions might include: higher limits for specific high-risk areas, vendor carrying insurance to cover gaps, capped liability with exceptions for gross negligence. The PM should seek solutions that address underlying concerns of both parties.

Option A accepts unacceptable risk transfer.

Option B may be unrealistic and end the negotiation.

Option D abdicates negotiation responsibility.
