# Question

A vendor wants a liability cap equal to the contract value, but the project's downside exposure if the vendor fails is several times that amount. How should the PM negotiate this clause?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's standard cap since it is common market practice.
- **B**: Refuse any liability cap and insist on unlimited liability.
- **C**: Hand the clause to in-house counsel and accept whatever they negotiate.
- **D**: Work a layered solution with the vendor — a base cap, carve-outs for specific high-risk failures, and vendor-carried insurance to cover the gap.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Liability is a shared-interest problem, and PMI expects the PM to design a structure that protects both sides' legitimate concerns — exactly what carve-outs, higher caps on defined risks, and backing insurance do. (A) silently transfers unacceptable risk onto the buyer. (B) is a positional demand vendors almost never accept and ends the negotiation. (C) abdicates the commercial judgement that only the PM can make; legal drafts, but does not decide risk appetite.
