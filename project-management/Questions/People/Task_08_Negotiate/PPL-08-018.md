# Question

A functional manager is willing to allocate a developer at 50%, but the PM has calculated the project needs the developer at 100%. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to 50% allocation now and raise the gap at the next portfolio review meeting.
- **B**: Ask the PMO for a different developer with full availability to cover the full 100%.
- **C**: Discuss the manager's constraints, share the data on project need, and explore hybrid arrangements.
- **D**: Accept the 50% allocation and absorb the shortfall within the existing project team.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's first move in any resource dispute is an interest-based conversation that surfaces why each side holds its position and searches for creative alternatives. (A) defers the conversation to a forum that may be weeks away while the project takes damage now. (B) starts over with a replacement before testing whether the original can work. (D) quietly accepts a shortfall that will surface later as missed dates or overtime.
