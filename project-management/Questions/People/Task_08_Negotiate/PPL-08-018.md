# Question

A functional manager agrees to provide a resource for the project but wants to limit their allocation to 50%. The PM believes 100% allocation is needed. How should the PM approach this negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Accept 50% to maintain the relationship
- **B**: Demand 100% and escalate if refused
- **C**: Understand the manager's constraints, present data on project needs, explore creative solutions (phased allocation, additional support, adjusted scope), and find a mutually acceptable arrangement
- **D**: Request a different resource entirely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Internal negotiations benefit from understanding interests, not just positions. Why does the manager limit to 50%? (Other commitments, concerns about full reassignment?) Why does the PM need 100%? (Critical path work, coordination overhead?) Creative solutions might include 100% during critical phases, 50% otherwise; additional junior support; or scope adjustment. Finding win-win preserves the relationship.

Option A may compromise project success.

Option B damages relationships without exploring options.

Option D starts over rather than solving the actual problem.
