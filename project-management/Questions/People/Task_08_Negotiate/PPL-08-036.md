# Question

A PM needs to negotiate with a stakeholder from a culture that values relationship-building and indirect communication, while the PM's culture favours direct, transaction-focused negotiations. How should the PM adapt?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain your own cultural style—they should adapt to you
- **B**: Completely abandon your approach and mimic their style
- **C**: Invest time in relationship-building before discussing business, use more indirect communication, and be patient with the pace while finding a style that works for both parties
- **D**: Delegate negotiations to someone from their culture

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-cultural negotiations require adaptation. Relationship-focused cultures may need trust established before business discussions. Indirect communication requires listening for nuance. Patience shows respect. Finding a middle ground—where both parties feel comfortable—leads to better outcomes than imposing one cultural norm.

Option A risks offending and failing to build necessary trust.

Option B may seem inauthentic or uncomfortable.

Option D abdicates responsibility rather than developing cultural competence.
