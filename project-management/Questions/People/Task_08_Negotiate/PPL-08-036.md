# Question

A PM whose own culture favours direct, transactional negotiation is starting talks with a stakeholder whose culture emphasises relationship-building and indirect communication. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct the negotiation in the PM's normal style; substance matters more than ceremony.
- **B**: Adopt the counterpart's style entirely to avoid causing offence.
- **C**: Hand the negotiation to a colleague from the same culture as the counterpart.
- **D**: Allow time for relationship-building, listen for indirect signals, and adjust pace and style while staying authentic to the PM's own approach.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI tailoring guidance and cross-cultural competence both call for the PM to adapt approach without abandoning their core authenticity. (A) ignores cultural context and risks failing to build the trust the deal depends on. (B) is performative mimicry that often reads as inauthentic. (C) abdicates the PM's responsibility and signals disengagement to the counterpart.
