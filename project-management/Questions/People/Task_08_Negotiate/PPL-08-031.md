# Question

A vendor agreed verbally to a discount and an early-delivery clause, but the redlined contract sent for signature reflects neither. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the signature, raise the discrepancies with the vendor, and require the contract to be amended to reflect the agreed terms before signing.
- **B**: Sign the contract and rely on email records of the verbal agreement to enforce the missing terms later.
- **C**: Sign the contract to keep the schedule and renegotiate the missing terms after kick-off.
- **D**: Refuse to do business with the vendor on the basis of the discrepancy.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The signed contract is what governs, and PMI-aligned procurement practice is to reconcile any gap before signature, not after. (B) bets on disputed evidence and weak enforcement instead of fixing the document now. (C) signs an unfavourable agreement and burns negotiating leverage that will not return. (D) is disproportionate; the discrepancy may be a drafting error and is fixable.
