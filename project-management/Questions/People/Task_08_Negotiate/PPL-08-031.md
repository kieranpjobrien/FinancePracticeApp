# Question

A vendor agrees verbally to favourable terms during negotiation but the written contract contains different, less favourable terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sign the contract since verbal agreements don't matter
- **B**: Refuse to work with the vendor
- **C**: Raise the discrepancy immediately, request the contract be updated to match verbal agreements, and ensure all agreed terms are documented in writing before signing
- **D**: Sign the contract and expect verbal terms to be honoured

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Discrepancies between verbal and written terms must be resolved before signing. Verbal agreements may not be enforceable; the written contract governs the relationship. The discrepancy could be an error or an intentional attempt to change terms. Either way, raising it directly and insisting on accurate documentation protects both parties.

Option A ignores potentially material differences.

Option B may be premature—the discrepancy might be an honest mistake.

Option D naively trusts verbal promises over legal documentation.
