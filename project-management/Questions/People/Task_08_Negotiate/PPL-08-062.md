# Question

In a partner negotiation, the other side makes a concession only if the PM agrees on the spot to a warranty clause that exceeds the PM's delegated financial authority. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the session, be transparent that it exceeds the PM's authority, and return after obtaining a sponsor decision and updated mandate for the clause.
- **B**: Decline the concession entirely and continue on the previously agreed terms without surfacing the delegation limit to the counterparty at any point.
- **C**: Accept the clause to lock in the concession and seek retrospective sponsor approval once the deal is signed and the commercial momentum is preserved.
- **D**: Sign conditionally, noting in the minutes that the clause depends on internal ratification, and continue proceeding as though it has already been approved.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's Code requires PMs to operate within their delegated authority and be transparent about it; this is an ethics issue before it is a negotiating tactic. (B) walks away from value without consulting the sponsor who may well authorise it. (C) signs beyond authority, which is a breach regardless of later endorsement. (D) records a condition but behaves as if signed, creating ambiguity the counterparty can later exploit.
