# Question

During contract negotiation, a vendor insists on a clause that limits the buyer's ability to change requirements. The project will be delivered using agile practices that depend on iterative refinement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Walk away from the vendor and seek one comfortable with unlimited change.
- **B**: Accept the clause and absorb the risk; the relationship is more important than the clause.
- **C**: Negotiate a tiered change clause defining process, lead time, and pricing for changes within and beyond a baseline.
- **D**: Hand the clause to legal to redraft without further PM involvement.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A well-designed change clause respects the vendor's need for predictability while preserving the buyer's need for adaptability — exactly the structure agile contracts are built around. (A) treats a negotiable issue as binary and may eliminate the best technical fit unnecessarily. (B) accepts a real constraint on the project's delivery model without trying to shape it. (D) abdicates a substantive PM responsibility; legal supports negotiation but cannot judge the operational impact alone.
