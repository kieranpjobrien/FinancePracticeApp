# Question

During vendor contract negotiations, the vendor insists on a provision that would limit the project's ability to change requirements. The PM knows agile practices require flexibility. How should the PM approach this negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the provision to close the deal quickly
- **B**: Reject any vendor who won't agree to unlimited changes
- **C**: Negotiate a change management clause that allows reasonable flexibility with defined process and cost implications
- **D**: Have legal handle all contract negotiations

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Both parties have legitimate interests - the vendor needs predictability, the project needs flexibility. A well-designed change clause can address both: defined process for requesting changes, clear impact assessment, fair pricing for changes. This creates a workable framework rather than all-or-nothing positions.

Option A (accept limitation) constrains the project unnecessarily without exploring options.

Option B (unlimited changes) is unrealistic - no vendor can accept unlimited scope changes at fixed price.

Option D (delegate to legal) abdicates PM responsibility. Legal supports negotiations but the PM understands project needs.
