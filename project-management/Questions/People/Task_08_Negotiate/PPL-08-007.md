# Question

In principled negotiation, what does "focus on interests, not positions" mean?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate based on what each party publicly demands at the start.
- **B**: Identify the underlying needs each party is trying to meet and design solutions that address them.
- **C**: Make sure the strongest party's position prevails for efficiency.
- **D**: Avoid stating any position until the other side commits to theirs.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Positions are what people say they want; interests are why they want it. Negotiating around interests opens the design space and often reveals options that satisfy both parties better than splitting positional differences. (A) treats opening positions as fixed and forecloses creative solutions. (C) is power play, not principled negotiation. (D) is positional gamesmanship and slows progress without uncovering interests.
