# Question

In principled negotiation, what does "separate the people from the problem" mean?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate only through intermediaries, not directly
- **B**: Address the substantive issues without making it personal; be hard on the problem, soft on the people
- **C**: Replace difficult negotiators with more agreeable ones
- **D**: Focus only on relationships, not on outcomes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Separate the people from the problem" is a key principle from Getting to Yes. It means addressing substantive issues (the problem) while maintaining respectful relationships (the people). Attack the problem vigorously while treating the other party with respect. This prevents negotiations from becoming personal conflicts.

Option A misunderstands the principle - direct negotiation is fine; the issue is how you engage.

Option C takes the phrase too literally - it's about approach, not personnel changes.

Option D goes too far - you should care about both relationships AND outcomes.
