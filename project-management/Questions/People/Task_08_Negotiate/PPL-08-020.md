# Question

The PM learns that the vendor's lead negotiator is compensated primarily on deal value rather than on delivery outcomes. How should this influence the PM's approach?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the information, since the vendor's incentives are the vendor's internal matter.
- **B**: Raise the incentive structure with the vendor's CEO as a formal ethics concern.
- **C**: Anchor on external benchmarks, involve the vendor's delivery leaders, and confirm the PM's BATNA.
- **D**: Match the adversarial posture by focusing the PM's team purely on minimising the price.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Understanding the counter-party's incentives is legitimate information that should shape strategy — using market benchmarks depersonalises price, and engaging delivery-side stakeholders balances a price-focused negotiator. (A) discards a useful piece of information. (B) treats a common and legal compensation structure as an ethics issue and will alienate the vendor. (D) reduces the deal to a price war and forecloses the value-creating options that typically produce better outcomes for both sides.
