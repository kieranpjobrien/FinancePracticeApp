# Question

During vendor negotiations, the PM discovers that the vendor's lead negotiator is personally incentivised on contract value, not project success. How should this knowledge influence the PM's approach?

## Options

- **A**: It shouldn't matter—focus on the deal terms
- **B**: Report unethical behaviour to the vendor's management
- **C**: Recognise this creates pressure to maximise price; focus on objective criteria, engage other vendor stakeholders who care about relationship, and ensure your BATNA is clear
- **D**: Match their approach by focusing only on minimising price

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Understanding the other party's incentives informs negotiation strategy. Commission-based negotiators may have limited authority to discount. Engaging technical or relationship stakeholders who value long-term partnership can balance this. Using objective criteria (market rates, benchmarks) depersonalises price discussions. Knowing your BATNA prevents being pressured beyond fair value.

Option A ignores useful strategic information.

Option B may be premature—commission structures are common.

Option D creates adversarial dynamics that harm both parties.
