# Question

A PM is negotiating a partnership where their organisation has less power than the other party. The power imbalance means the partner could dictate unfavourable terms. What strategies can the PM use?

## Options

- **A**: Accept whatever terms are offered
- **B**: Pretend to have more power than you do
- **C**: Strengthen your BATNA, form coalitions, emphasise unique value you bring, build personal relationships, and use objective criteria to balance power dynamics
- **D**: Delay negotiations until power balance changes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Power imbalances can be addressed through multiple strategies. Strengthening your BATNA (finding alternatives) increases your walk-away power. Coalitions with others add collective leverage. Highlighting unique value you provide changes the calculus. Personal relationships create commitment beyond transaction. Objective criteria (market rates, industry standards) provide neutral anchors.

Option A accepts unfair outcomes.

Option B will be exposed and damage credibility.

Option D may not be feasible and loses current opportunity.
