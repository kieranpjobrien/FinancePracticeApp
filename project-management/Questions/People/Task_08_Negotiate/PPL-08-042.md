# Question

The PM is negotiating with a partner whose organisation is significantly larger and holds more market power. What is the most effective strategy to balance the asymmetry?

## Options

- **A**: Strengthen the BATNA, anchor on objective benchmarks, surface the unique value the PM's organisation brings, and build personal relationships across the partner's team.
- **B**: Bluff about alternative partners to manufacture leverage.
- **C**: Accept the partner's standard terms and capture relationship value over time.
- **D**: Delay the negotiation until the PM's organisation has a stronger position.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Power asymmetry is best addressed through a portfolio of moves — credible alternatives, neutral criteria, distinctive value, and personal relationships — that together raise the negotiating floor. (B) is a bluff that violates the PMI Code of Ethics and collapses if tested. (C) capitulates and locks in unfavourable terms for the life of the partnership. (D) treats waiting as a strategy when waiting may itself be impossible or expensive.
