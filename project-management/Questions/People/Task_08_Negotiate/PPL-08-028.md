# Question

A senior team member is refusing a critical but unglamorous task, calling it "below my skill level". What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the task to a more junior team member who has available capacity.
- **B**: Have a one-on-one conversation to understand the team member's concerns and motivations.
- **C**: Escalate the refusal to the team member's functional manager for performance action.
- **D**: Direct the team member to do the task and formally document the conversation afterwards.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leader stance is to understand motivation before issuing direction; the refusal usually masks something else (fear of typecasting, perceived demotion, lack of context on importance). (A) silently shifts the load and rewards the refusal. (C) escalates before the PM has had the conversation they own. (D) commands compliance and damages the relationship without addressing the cause.
