# Question

A PM needs to negotiate with a team member who refuses to take on a critical but unglamorous task, claiming it's beneath their skill level. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Force the assignment using authority
- **B**: Assign the task to someone else
- **C**: Understand the team member's concerns, explain why the task matters, explore how it connects to their goals, and discuss how to make the assignment worthwhile
- **D**: Escalate to their functional manager

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Internal negotiations benefit from understanding motivations. Perhaps the team member fears being typecast, wants visibility on other work, or doesn't understand the task's importance. Connecting the task to their interests (learning opportunity, visibility with stakeholders, path to preferred work) can change their perspective. Collaborative problem-solving often finds acceptable solutions.

Option A damages motivation and relationship.

Option B may unfairly burden others.

Option D escalates before attempting direct resolution.
