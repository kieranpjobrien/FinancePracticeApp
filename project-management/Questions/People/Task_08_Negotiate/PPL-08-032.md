# Question

A PM is negotiating scope with a customer who has a fixed budget. The customer wants more features than the budget can support. The PM values the long-term relationship. What approach best balances these factors?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver all features at a loss to win the relationship
- **B**: Refuse any scope beyond what the budget supports
- **C**: Collaboratively prioritise features to fit the budget, propose phased delivery with additional features in later releases, and explore value-add options that strengthen the relationship without unsustainable cost
- **D**: Agree to the scope and plan to request more budget later

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Long-term relationships require sustainable agreements. Helping the customer prioritise demonstrates partnership. Phased delivery enables getting core value now with additions later. Creative options (training, documentation, support packages) may strengthen the relationship without significant cost. This approach balances immediate constraints with relationship building.

Option A sets an unsustainable precedent and may harm your organisation.

Option B may seem rigid even if technically correct.

Option D damages trust when the budget request comes.
