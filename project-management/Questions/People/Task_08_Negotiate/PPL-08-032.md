# Question

A long-standing customer is asking for more scope than their fixed budget supports. The PM's organisation values the relationship for future revenue. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver the full scope at a loss and treat it as a relationship investment.
- **B**: Work with the customer to prioritise the must-have features for the current budget and propose a phased roadmap for the rest.
- **C**: Decline anything beyond the budget and stand on the original statement of work.
- **D**: Agree to the scope verbally and submit a budget-increase request to the customer later.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI-aligned negotiation balances relationship and project viability through joint prioritisation and phasing — the customer keeps their value path; the PM keeps the work sustainable. (A) sets a precedent that destroys margin and trains the customer to expect scope creep. (C) is technically correct but rigid and damages the relationship. (D) is dishonest by omission and breaches the PMI Code of Ethics.
