# Question

A project depends on a vendor who is the only qualified provider in the region, and they are pushing for terms the PM finds unfavourable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's terms because the project has no realistic regional alternative available.
- **B**: Tell the vendor a competitor has offered better terms to create some pricing leverage.
- **C**: Hand the negotiation over to procurement to apply more pressure on the vendor.
- **D**: Look for terms the vendor values and trade them for relief on the difficult clauses.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When positional leverage is weak, value-creation is the strongest move available — find what the vendor wants beyond price and trade it for the terms the project needs. (A) gives in without exploring whether trades are possible. (B) is a bluff that violates PMI's ethics standard on honesty and torches the relationship if discovered. (C) outsources the conversation but does not change the underlying leverage and adds friction with a partner the project must work with for years.
