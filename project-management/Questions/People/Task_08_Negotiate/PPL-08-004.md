# Question

A project manager is negotiating a contract with a critical vendor. The vendor has significant leverage because they're the only qualified provider. The vendor is demanding unfavorable terms. What negotiation approach should the PM use?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the terms since there's no alternative
- **B**: Bluff about having alternatives to create artificial leverage
- **C**: Focus on building a long-term relationship, explore ways to add value for the vendor, and find terms that work for both parties
- **D**: Escalate to procurement to negotiate more aggressively

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When leverage is limited, relationship-based negotiation becomes more important. Understanding what the vendor values (long-term partnership, case studies, referrals, simplified processes) may reveal opportunities for mutual benefit. Building relationship also protects you if you must work together through challenges.

Option A surrenders without exploring options.

Option B is dishonest and damages trust if discovered - problematic when you'll have an ongoing relationship.

Option D brings different people but same leverage situation.
