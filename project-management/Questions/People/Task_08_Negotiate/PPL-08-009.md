# Question

Two stakeholders with equal authority want conflicting project outcomes. Stakeholder A wants maximum features; Stakeholder B wants fastest delivery. Neither will compromise. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Side with whoever is more persistent
- **B**: Escalate to a higher authority who can make a binding decision
- **C**: Try to find a creative solution that partially satisfies both, or facilitate a structured discussion to reach agreement
- **D**: Proceed without resolution and hope it works out

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. First, explore whether creative options exist - phased delivery (some features fast, others later), MVP approach (core features quickly, enhance over time). If no creative solution works, facilitate structured negotiation - understand underlying interests (why features? why speed?), explore trade-offs, seek compromise. Escalation is a fallback if negotiation fails.

Option A isn't principled decision-making.

Option B is appropriate only after direct negotiation fails.

Option D creates ongoing conflict and unclear direction.
