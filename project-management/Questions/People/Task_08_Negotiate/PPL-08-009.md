# Question

Two stakeholders with equal authority are at an impasse: one wants the maximum feature set, the other wants the earliest possible go-live. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a joint conversation to surface their underlying interests and explore phased or MVP-style options that partially satisfy both.
- **B**: Escalate immediately to a more senior executive who can make a binding decision.
- **C**: Recommend the position favoured by the stakeholder with the larger budget contribution.
- **D**: Lock in the original scope and timeline and let the stakeholders work it out themselves.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to attempt facilitated, interest-based resolution before escalating, because creative options (MVP, phased rollout, slice-by-segment) often dissolve apparent impasses. (B) escalates prematurely and is a last-resort tool, not a first one. (C) introduces an arbitrary criterion that has nothing to do with project value. (D) abdicates the PM's facilitation role and leaves the conflict to fester at the working level.
