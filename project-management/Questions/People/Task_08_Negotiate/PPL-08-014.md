# Question

In a resource negotiation, the functional manager's final offer falls short of what the project needs but pushing further risks damaging a relationship that matters across future projects. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold out and keep pressing until the manager concedes.
- **B**: Accept the offer, document the resulting gap, and develop mitigation plans (scope, schedule, or supplemental resource).
- **C**: Reject the offer and start looking for a completely different resource.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to force a different outcome.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Knowing when the negotiation has reached its productive limit is a core PM skill; accepting the best available deal while formally recording and mitigating the gap is the disciplined response. (A) trades relationship capital for marginal gains that may not materialise. (C) abandons partial success with no evidence a replacement will do better. (D) escalates a peer negotiation that has reached a reasonable endpoint and damages the relationship the PM wanted to protect.
