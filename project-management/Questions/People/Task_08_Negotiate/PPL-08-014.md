# Question

During resource negotiations, a functional manager makes a final offer that doesn't fully meet project needs but is their absolute limit. Continuing to push risks damaging the relationship for future projects. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue pushing to get what the project needs
- **B**: Accept the offer, document the gap, and develop alternative plans to address the shortfall
- **C**: Escalate immediately
- **D**: Refuse the offer entirely and find different resources

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Recognising when you've reached the best possible outcome is part of negotiation skill. Accept the offer that's available, document the resulting gap clearly (creating visibility), and plan mitigation (adjust scope, timeline, or find supplemental resources). Preserve the relationship for future negotiations.

Option A damages the relationship for marginal gains that may not materialise.

Option C escalates when the negotiation has reached a reasonable conclusion.

Option D may not be feasible and abandons partial success.
