# Question

A newly formed predictive-agile hybrid team of ten has started bickering about what "done" means, who attends which meeting, and how decisions get made. The PM has not yet held a chartering session. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Publish a team charter drafted by the PM based on organisational defaults, and ask the team to flag anything specific they cannot live with.
- **B**: Facilitate a chartering workshop where the team negotiates working agreements, Definition of Done, and decision rules, and commits to revisit them regularly.
- **C**: Resolve the meeting-attendance and decision-making issues individually with each team member so that private conversations stay focused, collegial and non-confrontational throughout.
- **D**: Wait one more sprint so real patterns emerge, then convene the chartering session once the team has a clearer sense of its actual problems.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats team-written working agreements as the foundation of self-direction; the PM's role is to facilitate the team writing its own rules, not hand them down. (A) replaces ownership with compliance and misses the point of chartering. (C) fragments a team-level issue into private conversations, which cannot produce a shared agreement. (D) delays a conversation the team is already signalling it needs, and allows dysfunction to set.
