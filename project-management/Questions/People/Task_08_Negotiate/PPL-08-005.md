# Question

The PM needs a key engineer for six months; the engineer's functional manager will only release her for three. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the three-month allocation and re-plan the second half of the work.
- **B**: Discuss options with the functional manager — part-time after month three, training a backup, or scoping specific deliverables — that meet both needs.
- **C**: Escalate the resource conflict to the PMO for a binding decision.
- **D**: Offer the functional manager a future favour in exchange for the full six months.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest-based negotiation looks for arrangements that satisfy both parties' real needs before either side has to give ground. (A) surrenders the project's position without exploring alternatives. (C) escalates before the PM has done the work themselves and damages a peer relationship. (D) is informal horse-trading that can create real ethics issues and obligations the PM may not be able to honour.
