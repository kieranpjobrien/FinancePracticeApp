# Question

A project manager is negotiating with a functional manager for a key resource. The functional manager wants the resource back after 3 months, but the PM needs them for 6 months. What negotiation approach is MOST likely to succeed?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to senior management to override the functional manager
- **B**: Explore options like part-time allocation, phased involvement, or cross-training another resource
- **C**: Accept the 3-month allocation without discussion
- **D**: Threaten to report poor cooperation to the PMO

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Principled negotiation focuses on interests, not positions. Both parties have legitimate needs. Exploring creative options (part-time after 3 months, training a backup, phased involvement) may satisfy both. This approach builds relationships and creates sustainable agreements.

Option A (escalate) damages the relationship and should be a last resort after negotiation fails.

Option C (accept without discussion) doesn't advocate for project needs and may set a precedent.

Option D (threaten) is adversarial and damages future working relationships.
