# Question

A contract negotiation has stalled on a single issue—liability cap—where both parties have firm positions. What technique might help break the impasse?

## Options

- **A**: Give up on that issue entirely
- **B**: Force the other party to concede through pressure
- **C**: Explore creative options such as tiered liability based on fault severity, insurance requirements, escrow arrangements, or trading concessions on other issues
- **D**: Escalate to lawyers to resolve

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Impasses often result from treating negotiable items as fixed. Creative options can satisfy both parties' underlying interests. Tiered liability acknowledges different risk levels. Insurance transfers risk to third parties. Trading across issues (I'll accept your liability cap if you accept my payment terms) creates value. Expanding the pie often breaks deadlocks.

Option A loses something important.

Option B may end negotiations entirely.

Option D adds cost and adversarial dynamics.
