# Question

A PM is negotiating a contract with a vendor who has provided the lowest bid but also the longest delivery timeline. Another vendor offers faster delivery at a higher price. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose the lowest price
- **B**: Always choose the fastest delivery
- **C**: Evaluate total cost of ownership including schedule impact, negotiate with both vendors to find the best value, and consider which constraints matter most to project success
- **D**: Split the work between both vendors

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Procurement decisions should consider total value, not just price. Schedule delays have costs (delayed benefits, extended overheads). Negotiation may reveal flexibility—can the cheaper vendor accelerate for a premium? Can the faster vendor reduce scope? Understanding project priorities (is time or budget more constrained?) guides the decision.

Option A ignores total cost of ownership.

Option B may waste money when schedule flexibility exists.

Option D may create coordination complexity without clear benefit.
