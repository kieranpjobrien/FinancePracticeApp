# Question

Two qualified vendors have bid on the same scope: Vendor A has the lowest price with the longest lead time; Vendor B is 15% more expensive but delivers six weeks sooner. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Always pick the lowest bid unless it is technically unacceptable.
- **B**: Pick the faster vendor because schedule is almost always the dominant constraint.
- **C**: Split the scope between the two vendors to hedge the decision.
- **D**: Evaluate total cost of ownership including the value of earlier delivery, then use that analysis to negotiate with both before awarding.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's procurement guidance is to decide on total value, not sticker price, and to keep competitive tension live during final negotiations. Quantifying the value of earlier delivery turns a qualitative trade-off into a comparable number. (A) and (B) each pick one constraint as automatically dominant without testing it against the project's priorities. (C) introduces integration risk and overhead that rarely repay the supposed hedging benefit.
