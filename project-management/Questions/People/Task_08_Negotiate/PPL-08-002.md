# Question

In principled negotiation, what does "separating people from the problem" mean?

## Options

- **A**: Removing difficult team members from negotiations
- **B**: Addressing substantive issues separately from relationship concerns and avoiding personal attacks
- **C**: Having different people negotiate different aspects of the agreement
- **D**: Keeping personal feelings out of any business discussion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Separate the people from the problem" is a key principle from the Harvard Negotiation Project (Getting to Yes). It means dealing with relationship issues (trust, communication, emotions) separately from substantive issues (terms, conditions). You can be firm on substance while being respectful of people - this prevents negotiations from becoming personal conflicts.

Option A misinterprets the phrase literally - it's not about removing people but about separating relationship concerns from substantive issues.

Option C describes delegation of negotiation topics, which isn't the concept's meaning.

Option D is too extreme - emotions are part of human interaction. The principle is about managing them constructively, not eliminating them.
