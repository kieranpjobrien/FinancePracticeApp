# Question

In principled negotiation, what does "separate the people from the problem" mean?

## Options

- **A**: Address substantive issues directly while maintaining a respectful relationship with the other party.
- **B**: Use intermediaries so the principals never argue face to face.
- **C**: Replace anyone whose personal style is making the negotiation harder.
- **D**: Keep all emotion out of the room and focus only on numbers.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Harvard "Getting to Yes" principle is to be hard on the problem and soft on the people: dispute the issue vigorously without making it a personal attack. (B) is delegation, not the principle, and often makes things worse by introducing translation loss. (C) reads the phrase too literally; you do not remove people, you change how you engage them. (D) overshoots — emotions are part of the conversation and must be acknowledged, not banished.
