# Question

The PM is leading a four-party negotiation: their own organisation, a customer, and two subcontractors with overlapping scopes and different risk appetites. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate sequentially with each party and assemble the combined agreement at the end of the process.
- **B**: Map each party's interests, identify cross-party trade-offs, and design a coherent agreement architecture.
- **C**: Hand the negotiation to the legal teams and provide high-level commercial guidance only on request.
- **D**: Negotiate primarily for the PM's own organisation and accept the other parties as price-takers.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Multi-party deals require a systemic view because concessions in one bilateral track create obligations in another; the agreement architecture is the PM's job. (A) creates inconsistent terms that often fail to reconcile at the end. (C) misses the commercial and relationship dimensions only the PM holds. (D) ignores that subcontractors who feel coerced under-perform throughout the contract life.
