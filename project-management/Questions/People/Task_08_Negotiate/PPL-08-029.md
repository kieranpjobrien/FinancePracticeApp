# Question

A PM is negotiating a complex multi-party agreement involving their organisation, a customer, and two subcontractors. Each party has different priorities and risk tolerances. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate separately with each party and hope the pieces fit together
- **B**: Let the lawyers handle all negotiations
- **C**: Map each party's interests and constraints, identify potential trade-offs, facilitate joint sessions where appropriate, and build an agreement architecture that addresses all key concerns
- **D**: Focus only on your organisation's interests

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Multi-party negotiations require understanding the full landscape. Mapping interests reveals where conflicts exist and where alignment is possible. Trade-offs between parties (A concedes X to B in exchange for Y from C) can unlock value. Joint sessions build transparency and trust. A holistic agreement architecture ensures all pieces fit together coherently.

Option A creates integration problems and inconsistent terms.

Option B misses commercial and relationship aspects.

Option D may create an agreement others won't commit to.
