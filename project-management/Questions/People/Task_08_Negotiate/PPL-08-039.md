# Question

After a contract is signed, the counterparty claims a key clause means something materially different from the PM's understanding. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a formal notice of breach citing the PM's interpretation of the clause.
- **B**: Accept the counterparty's interpretation in the interest of preserving the relationship.
- **C**: Convene a joint review of the clause, the negotiation history, and the working assumptions to identify where the misunderstanding arose and negotiate a resolution.
- **D**: Pause delivery until lawyers from both sides arbitrate the clause.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Interpretation disputes are usually best resolved by direct, joint reading before legal escalation; that often surfaces a fixable ambiguity rather than bad faith. (A) escalates a possibly genuine misunderstanding into a contractual fight. (B) capitulates and may reward opportunistic re-reading of agreed text. (D) introduces cost and delay before either party has tried to talk it through.
