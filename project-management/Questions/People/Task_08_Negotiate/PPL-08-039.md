# Question

A PM has successfully negotiated an agreement, but during implementation the other party claims the agreement means something different than the PM understood. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on your interpretation and threaten legal action
- **B**: Accept their interpretation to maintain the relationship
- **C**: Review the documented agreement together, seek to understand the source of the misunderstanding, and negotiate a resolution that addresses both parties' legitimate expectations
- **D**: Void the agreement and start over

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Interpretation disputes happen despite careful drafting. Joint review of documentation may clarify intent. Understanding how misunderstanding arose prevents future issues. If the agreement is genuinely ambiguous, negotiating a fair resolution—recognising both parties' reasonable expectations—maintains the relationship while solving the problem.

Option A escalates without attempting resolution.

Option B may reward opportunistic reinterpretation.

Option D is extreme and loses value already created.
