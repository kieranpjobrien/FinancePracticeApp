# Question

A PM successfully negotiates an agreement but suspects the other party agreed under pressure and may not fully commit to implementation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Consider it their problem—they agreed
- **B**: Void the agreement and renegotiate
- **C**: Check in with the other party, address any lingering concerns, ensure they genuinely support the agreement, and adjust if needed to ensure sustainable commitment
- **D**: Document the agreement heavily in case of disputes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agreements made under pressure often fail in implementation. A deal both parties genuinely support is more valuable than one that technically binds an unwilling partner. Checking in shows good faith and may surface issues better addressed now than during implementation. Minor adjustments to ensure commitment are usually worthwhile.

Option A ignores implementation risk.

Option B may be unnecessary if concerns can be addressed.

Option D prepares for failure rather than preventing it.
