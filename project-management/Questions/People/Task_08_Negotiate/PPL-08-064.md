# Question

Two project managers in the same portfolio both need the only qualified cloud architect for the next four weeks. The portfolio manager has told them to sort it out between themselves. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Secure the architect first by placing an immediate booking through the shared-services tool before the other PM has a chance to act.
- **B**: Escalate back to the portfolio manager and argue that this kind of resource conflict is not appropriately resolved at the PM level without authority.
- **C**: Meet the other PM, compare business value, deadlines and risk, and jointly propose a sequence or split to the portfolio manager for governance endorsement.
- **D**: Find an external contractor at short notice so the other PM can have the internal architect and both projects proceed without any interpersonal friction.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs in a matrix to resolve peer-level conflicts through structured conversation grounded in business value, then formalise through governance. (A) is first-come-first-served, which ignores value and damages the peer relationship. (B) refuses an explicit delegation from the portfolio manager. (D) spends money to avoid a conversation without comparing value or consulting the portfolio.
