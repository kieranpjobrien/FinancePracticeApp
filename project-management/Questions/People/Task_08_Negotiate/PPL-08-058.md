# Question

A PM discovers that the vendor's minimum acceptable price is higher than the sponsor's maximum approved budget. There is no overlap in the zone of possible agreement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Return to the sponsor with the analysis, explore whether scope, timing or funding can flex, and only proceed if a genuine ZOPA emerges.
- **B**: Push the vendor harder on price by escalating to their senior account executive and threatening to withdraw the opportunity entirely if needed to force movement.
- **C**: Sign at the budget figure and plan to request a supplementary budget later in delivery, once the vendor has already begun mobilising the resources.
- **D**: Accept the vendor's minimum and find the shortfall by reducing contingency and management reserve on the overall project cost baseline for the programme.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When no ZOPA exists, PMI says the PM must change the problem — scope, schedule, funding or partner — rather than force a deal neither side can honour. (B) pressures a vendor below their reservation, producing either a refusal or a damaged delivery. (C) is a commitment made without funding and a textbook ethics issue. (D) consumes reserves set aside for identified and unidentified risks, quietly degrading the project's risk posture.
