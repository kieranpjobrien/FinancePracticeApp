# Question

At 5pm on a Friday, a vendor calls the PM and says their CEO needs a signed contract by Monday 9am or the pricing goes up 12%. The PM has not yet finished evaluating two clauses. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sign before Monday to lock the current price and address any outstanding clauses via amendment after signing if they prove to be material issues later.
- **B**: Ignore the deadline entirely and continue the evaluation at the original pace, since artificial time pressure is a well-recognised negotiating tactic anyway.
- **C**: Counter with the PM's own artificial deadline — "we decide next Friday, take or leave" — to reset who controls the timing of the discussion.
- **D**: Acknowledge the deadline, quickly finish the clause analysis over the weekend if practical, and only sign if evaluation supports it on the commercial merits.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's negotiation guidance treats manufactured deadlines as tactics to be recognised but not blindly obeyed or defied; the decision must still rest on the commercial merits after due diligence. (A) signs on incomplete evaluation and accepts an unquantified risk. (B) risks a genuine price movement if the deadline is real, without even testing it. (C) matches tactic with tactic and abandons interest-based negotiation.
