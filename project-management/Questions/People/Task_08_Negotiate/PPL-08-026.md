# Question

Three months into delivery, the PM concludes the original deadline was based on a flawed estimating assumption. The sponsor is publicly committed to the date and resistant to any change. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the original date and pull resources from other projects to try to recover.
- **B**: Insist on a deadline extension as the only credible answer and present that recommendation.
- **C**: Bring the sponsor the data on what changed, the impact options (extend, add resources, descope, phase delivery), and ask the sponsor to choose how to re-baseline.
- **D**: Wait until the date slips and then present a re-baselining plan after the fact.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI requires transparent, options-based renegotiation when a baseline is no longer achievable, with the sponsor making the call on which trade-off is acceptable. (A) hides the problem and damages other projects. (B) presents a single-answer ultimatum and removes the sponsor's authority to choose. (D) breaches the duty to disclose known issues and is an ethics failure.
