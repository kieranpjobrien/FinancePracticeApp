# Question

A PM is renegotiating a project deadline with the sponsor after discovering that the original timeline was based on inaccurate assumptions. The sponsor is resistant to any extension. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the original deadline and hope to catch up
- **B**: Insist on the extension without flexibility
- **C**: Present data on the actual situation, acknowledge the impact of extension, offer options (scope reduction, resource addition, phased delivery), and collaborate on finding an acceptable path forward
- **D**: Blame the original estimators for the problem

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Renegotiation requires transparency and options. Data builds credibility—what changed, why, what's the real situation? Acknowledging impact shows you understand sponsor concerns. Options empower the sponsor to choose trade-offs they can accept. Collaborative problem-solving maintains the relationship while addressing reality.

Option A sets the project up for failure.

Option B is confrontational and limits options.

Option D deflects responsibility without solving the problem.
