# Question

An experienced team is approaching a critical milestone when a key team member announces they must take unexpected medical leave for several weeks. The team is concerned about the workload impact. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately request a replacement resource from management
- **B**: Distribute the absent member's work across the team and extend the milestone deadline
- **C**: Facilitate a team discussion to assess impact, identify options, and develop a plan that the team commits to
- **D**: Personally take on the absent member's responsibilities to minimize team disruption

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This involves the team in problem-solving, leverages their knowledge of the work, and creates shared ownership of the solution. The team may identify creative options the PM wouldn't consider. This approach respects team autonomy while providing leadership support.

Option A assumes a replacement is needed without assessing the situation. New resources have ramp-up time and may not help with an imminent milestone. The team might find better solutions.

Option B makes decisions for the team without their input. Extending the deadline may not be necessary and concedes impact before exploring options. The team should be involved in assessing trade-offs.

Option D creates a single point of failure (the PM) and removes the PM from their coordination and leadership role. It also doesn't scale - the PM likely lacks the specialized skills and may do the work poorly. It also signals distrust in the team's ability to handle challenges.
