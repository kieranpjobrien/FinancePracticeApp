# Question

A team member comes to you asking to be excused from a mandatory team event due to a religious observance. Some other team members have expressed that everyone should attend. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them the team event is mandatory for all
- **B**: Grant the accommodation without question, treating religious observance as a legitimate reason for absence
- **C**: Ask them to prove their religious beliefs are sincere
- **D**: Postpone the event to accommodate everyone

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Religious accommodation is both legally required in most jurisdictions and ethically appropriate. Grant the request directly without requiring proof of sincerity. If other team members object, explain that accommodating religious practices is standard organisational policy and the right thing to do. Ensure the absent member receives any critical information.

Option A may violate religious accommodation requirements and is unnecessarily rigid.

Option C is inappropriate - sincerity tests for religious beliefs are problematic.

Option D may be impractical and isn't necessary for granting individual accommodation.
