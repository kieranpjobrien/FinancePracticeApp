# Question

Your sponsor has asked you to report the programme status as "green" in the next steering report even though your evidence shows it is "amber" trending "red." She says she needs a clean report before a board funding decision next week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Report honestly as amber with the supporting data, explain the trajectory to the sponsor before the meeting, and help her prepare a strong case for continued funding regardless.
- **B**: Report as green as the sponsor has asked and add a short written risk appendix so that the underlying status data is still technically available to any board member who searches for it.
- **C**: Report as green this once on the understanding that the board decision is time-critical and escalate your concerns through the PMO or ethics channel after the funding has been secured.
- **D**: Refuse to produce the status report at all and tell the sponsor she must write it herself if she wants the programme reported in a way that differs from the current delivery evidence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is PMI's reporting-integrity principle: tell the truth with the data, and help the sponsor make a credible case — honest amber with a clear plan often lands better with boards than a suspiciously clean green. (B) falsifies the headline and buries the truth in an appendix, which is an ethical breach even if technically defensible. (C) treats ethics as a timing problem and commits a breach now intending to fix it later. (D) avoids the breach but also avoids the conversation, leaving the sponsor to go it alone on board day.
