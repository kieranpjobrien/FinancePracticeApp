# Question

What is servant leadership?

## Options

- **A**: A leadership style where the leader makes all decisions for the team
- **B**: A leadership style focused on serving the team's needs, removing obstacles, and enabling their success
- **C**: A leadership style where the leader avoids all responsibility
- **D**: A leadership style used only in non-profit organisations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Servant leadership prioritises the growth and well-being of team members. The leader's role is to serve the team - remove obstacles, provide resources, shield from distractions, and create conditions for success. This approach is particularly valued in agile environments where the Scrum Master exemplifies servant leadership.

Option A describes authoritarian or directive leadership, the opposite of servant leadership.

Option C confuses serving with abdicating responsibility.

Option D incorrectly limits servant leadership to specific sectors - it's applicable anywhere.
