# Question

A team member makes an honest mistake that causes a minor project setback. They're visibly anxious about the consequences. How should you respond as a leader?

## Options

- **A**: Use this as an opportunity to make an example for the team
- **B**: Acknowledge the mistake without blame, focus on learning and prevention, and publicly protect them from excessive criticism
- **C**: Pretend the mistake didn't happen to avoid embarrassment
- **D**: Privately reassure them but allow others to criticise them publicly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. How leaders respond to honest mistakes defines psychological safety. Acknowledge what happened factually. Focus forward: "What can we learn? How do we prevent this?" Protect them from pile-on criticism. This response encourages future mistake reporting (essential for early problem detection) and demonstrates that learning is valued over blame.

Option A destroys psychological safety and discourages reporting future issues.

Option C denies reality and prevents learning.

Option D is inconsistent and doesn't truly protect the team member.
