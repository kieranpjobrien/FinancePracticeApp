# Question

Your organisation is going through layoffs. Your team is safe, but they're anxious and distracted by rumours. Productivity has dropped. What leadership approach is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Deny the layoffs are happening to maintain morale
- **B**: Be transparent about what you know and don't know, acknowledge the anxiety, provide as much certainty as possible about the team's situation, and refocus on work that gives purpose
- **C**: Threaten that poor productivity could make them targets
- **D**: Ignore the situation as it's outside your control

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership during uncertainty requires transparency and empathy. Share what you know (and admit what you don't). Acknowledge that anxiety is normal. Provide certainty where you can ("this team is not affected by current reductions"). Help the team find purpose and focus in their work. This builds trust and gradually restores productivity.

Option A destroys trust when the truth is obvious.

Option C exploits fear and damages psychological safety.

Option D leaves the team to suffer through uncertainty alone.
