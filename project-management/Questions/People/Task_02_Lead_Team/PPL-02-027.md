# Question

The organisation is going through layoffs. Your team is safe but is anxious and distracted by rumours, and productivity has dropped. What leadership approach is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Be transparent about what you know and what you don't, acknowledge the anxiety, give what certainty you can about the team, and refocus on meaningful work.
- **B**: Deny the layoffs are happening to keep morale up.
- **C**: Warn the team that poor productivity could put them on the next list.
- **D**: Stay out of it because it's outside your control.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI leadership response to ambiguity: transparency about knowns and unknowns, empathy for anxiety, certainty where it exists, and purposeful refocus. (B) destroys trust the moment the news becomes obvious. (C) is fear-based and damages safety. (D) leaves the team to suffer alone.
