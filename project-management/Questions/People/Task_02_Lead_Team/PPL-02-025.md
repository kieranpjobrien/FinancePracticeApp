# Question

A high-performing team member is showing signs of burnout - working long hours but becoming increasingly cynical and less effective. Their work quality remains acceptable but enthusiasm has disappeared. How should the PM lead in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it since work quality is still acceptable
- **B**: Have a supportive conversation to understand what's happening, then work together on sustainable workload adjustments and ensure they take time off
- **C**: Assign them more work to keep them busy and engaged
- **D**: Warn them about their negative attitude affecting the team

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership includes caring for team member wellbeing. Burnout is a serious issue that worsens without intervention. Have a supportive conversation to understand causes (workload, lack of challenge, personal issues). Collaborate on solutions: workload adjustment, delegation, time off, new challenges. Sustainable performance matters more than short-term output.

Option A allows burnout to worsen until performance collapses or they leave.

Option C will accelerate burnout.

Option D attacks the symptom without addressing the cause.
