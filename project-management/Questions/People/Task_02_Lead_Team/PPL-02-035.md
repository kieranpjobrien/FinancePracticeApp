# Question

An executive asks you to assign a specific high-profile task to their preferred team member, bypassing the team member who is most qualified. Doing so would be unfair and demotivating to the qualified team member. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the executive's request to avoid political problems
- **B**: Explain your concerns professionally, advocate for merit-based assignment, and propose a solution that addresses the executive's interests while being fair to the team
- **C**: Refuse outright without explanation
- **D**: Assign the task to the preferred person but complain to your manager

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership requires courage to advocate for your team while navigating organisational politics. Understand the executive's interest (developing their protégé, visibility, relationship). Explain your concern (fairness, qualified team member's morale). Propose alternatives: perhaps the preferred person can shadow or assist, or receive a different high-profile task. Find solutions that address multiple interests.

Option A sacrifices team trust and fairness for political ease.

Option C is confrontational without seeking understanding.

Option D is passive-aggressive and doesn't solve the problem.
