# Question

You need to deliver difficult feedback to a senior, experienced team member whose performance has declined. They have more domain expertise than you and may become defensive. How should you approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the conversation to maintain the relationship
- **B**: Assert your authority as PM to establish who's in charge
- **C**: Prepare specific examples, approach with curiosity rather than judgment, focus on observed behaviours and impact, and listen to their perspective before problem-solving together
- **D**: Deliver feedback via email to avoid confrontation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Difficult feedback requires preparation and humility. Gather specific examples (not generalisations). Approach with curiosity: "I've noticed X, help me understand what's happening." Focus on behaviour and impact, not character. Listen genuinely - there may be factors you're unaware of. Collaborate on solutions. Your authority isn't about hierarchy; it's earned through fairness and competence.

Option A allows performance to continue declining.

Option B is likely to trigger defensiveness and damage the relationship.

Option D is impersonal for a sensitive conversation and doesn't allow dialogue.
