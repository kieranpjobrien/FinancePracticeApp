# Question

You discover that a team member has been taking credit for another team member's work. The victim hasn't complained but you've observed it directly. How should you lead through this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the victim to complain before acting
- **B**: Address the behaviour privately with the credit-taker, correct the record appropriately, and monitor to ensure it doesn't continue
- **C**: Publicly call out the credit-taker to make an example
- **D**: Let it go as office politics are inevitable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leaders must address ethical issues proactively, not wait for formal complaints. Speak privately with the credit-taker: describe what you observed, explain why it's unacceptable, and clarify expectations. Correct the record where appropriate (acknowledge the real contributor). Monitor for recurrence. This maintains fairness and trust without public humiliation.

Option A allows unfairness to continue and may enable repeated behaviour.

Option C is unnecessarily humiliating and may backfire.

Option D normalises unethical behaviour and damages trust.
