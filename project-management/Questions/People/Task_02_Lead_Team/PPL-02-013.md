# Question

A project manager must lead a team through a difficult period where a key product decision was made by executives against the team's recommendation. The team is demoralized, feeling their expertise was ignored. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the team that executives have broader perspective and their decision should be respected
- **B**: Agree with the team that the decision was wrong and sympathize with their frustration
- **C**: Acknowledge the team's disappointment, validate that their input mattered, and refocus on executing well within the decision
- **D**: Request that executives reconsider based on team feedback

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM must navigate between loyalty to leadership decisions and empathy for the team. Acknowledging disappointment shows understanding; validating that input mattered maintains trust; refocusing on execution demonstrates professionalism and moves forward constructively.

Option A dismisses the team's feelings and may feel like the PM is siding with executives against them.

Option B undermines the decision and the PM's role in supporting organizational direction.

Option D may be appropriate in some cases but doesn't address the team's immediate emotional need or help them move forward.
