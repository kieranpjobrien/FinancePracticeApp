# Question

Executives have made a key product decision against the team's recommendation. The team feels their expertise was ignored and morale has dropped. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the team that executives have broader context and the decision should be respected.
- **B**: Agree with the team that the decision was wrong and sympathise openly.
- **C**: Acknowledge their disappointment, validate that their input mattered, and refocus the team on executing the decision well.
- **D**: Ask the executives to reconsider based on team feedback.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) holds both sides: empathy for the team and loyalty to the organisational decision, then constructive forward motion — that is PMI's leadership stance. (A) dismisses the team's experience and casts the PM as enforcer. (B) undermines the decision and the PM's role. (D) may be appropriate later but doesn't address the immediate morale issue or move the team forward.
