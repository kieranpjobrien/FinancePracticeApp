# Question

Your utility-industry programme has 15 internal staff and 22 contractors from three different vendors, all sitting in the same office. Internal and vendor staff eat, meet and socialise separately and trust signals at the team level are poor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the internal and vendor teams working on separable streams so that lower trust between them does not damage delivery on the shared elements of the programme plan.
- **B**: Introduce shared team rituals — joint stand-ups, mixed pairing on critical path work, shared retrospectives — and evaluate vendor work on the same quality bar as internal delivery.
- **C**: Commission a team-building day facilitated by HR so the combined programme team can get to know each other socially and begin breaking down the existing separation patterns.
- **D**: Ask each vendor manager to address the separation with their own contractors so that trust-building happens through the normal vendor management channels rather than directly from the programme PM.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI "one-team" pattern: shared work structures and a single quality bar build trust far faster than team-building days, and they also improve delivery. (A) institutionalises the divide and pushes the problem into the integration work later in the programme. (C) is a one-off event that cannot carry the weight of daily working separation on its own. (D) delegates a programme-level leadership issue to vendor managers whose incentives and reach do not extend to internal-vendor trust.
