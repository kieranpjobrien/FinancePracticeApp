# Question

A project manager must deliver difficult feedback to a team member whose work quality has declined. The team member is going through personal difficulties. How should the PM approach this conversation?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the conversation until the personal situation improves
- **B**: Deliver the feedback harshly to ensure the message is clear
- **C**: Have a private, empathetic conversation that addresses the performance issue while acknowledging the circumstances and exploring support options
- **D**: Document everything without speaking to the team member

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Performance issues need to be addressed even during difficult times, but with empathy. A private conversation allows the PM to acknowledge the circumstances, express care, and still address the work impact. Exploring support options (flexible hours, EAP, temporary workload adjustment) shows leadership that balances compassion with accountability.

Option A lets performance issues compound and doesn't serve the team member.

Option B lacks empathy and may worsen the situation.

Option D avoids the human element and may feel threatening when discovered.
