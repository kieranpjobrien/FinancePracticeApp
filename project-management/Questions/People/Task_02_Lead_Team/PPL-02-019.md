# Question

A PM must give difficult feedback to a team member whose work quality has declined. The team member is going through personal difficulties. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a private, empathetic conversation that addresses performance and explores support options.
- **B**: Postpone the conversation indefinitely until the team member's personal situation clearly improves.
- **C**: Deliver the feedback very bluntly so there is no ambiguity at all about the message being sent.
- **D**: Document the performance issues in writing but skip the face-to-face conversation with the person.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) balances accountability with empathy — privacy, acknowledgement of circumstances, and support options like flexible hours, EAP referrals or temporary load adjustments. (B) lets the issue compound. (C) is harsh without need and likely worsens performance. (D) is documentation without leadership and lands threateningly when discovered.
