# Question

Your organisation announces a new strategic direction that will significantly impact your project. The team is confused about what this means for their work. What leadership action should you take first?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for official guidance before communicating anything
- **B**: Gather information, communicate what you know and don't know to the team, and articulate how you'll work together to adapt
- **C**: Reassure the team that nothing will change
- **D**: Start making major project changes immediately based on your interpretation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership during ambiguity requires proactive communication. Gather what information you can. Communicate to the team: what you know, what you don't know, and when you expect to learn more. Don't pretend you have answers you don't have. Articulate your approach: "We'll learn more together and adapt." This reduces anxiety through transparency.

Option A leaves the team anxious during the information vacuum.

Option C makes promises you may not be able to keep.

Option D risks major rework if your interpretation is wrong.
