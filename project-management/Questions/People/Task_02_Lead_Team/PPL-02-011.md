# Question

A team consistently delivers what's asked but never innovates or suggests improvements. When asked for input, team members defer to the project manager. What leadership behavior might be causing this, and what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: The team lacks capability; provide more training
- **B**: The PM may be inadvertently suppressing initiative; create space for ideas by asking open questions, acting on suggestions, and explicitly encouraging experimentation
- **C**: Some team members are natural leaders and some aren't; accept the current dynamic
- **D**: Implement an innovation incentive program to motivate suggestions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When an entire team defers, it suggests a systemic issue - likely the PM's behavior has taught them that initiative isn't valued or safe. The PM should examine their own patterns (do they dismiss ideas? Always have "the answer"?) and actively create conditions for participation: open questions, acting on input, celebrating experiments.

Option A assumes capability issues when the pattern suggests psychological safety or cultural issues.

Option C accepts a dysfunction rather than addressing it. Healthy teams have members at all levels contributing ideas.

Option D treats symptoms with extrinsic motivation rather than addressing the underlying dynamic.
