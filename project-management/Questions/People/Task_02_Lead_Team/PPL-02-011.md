# Question

A team consistently delivers what is asked but never volunteers improvements or innovations. Asked for input, members defer to the PM. What is the likely cause and the right response?

## Options

- **A**: The PM may be suppressing initiative; create space by asking open questions and acting on ideas.
- **B**: The team likely lacks the capability needed; arrange more training and development for them.
- **C**: Some people are leaders and some aren't; accept the team dynamic as it is and move on.
- **D**: Set up a formal innovation incentive scheme to motivate team members to offer suggestions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) treats the entire-team pattern as the systemic issue it is — usually the PM's own behaviour has taught the team that initiative isn't safe. Self-reflection plus deliberate space-making is the fix. (B) misdiagnoses the root cause as capability. (C) accepts a dysfunction. (D) layers extrinsic motivation on a psychological-safety problem.
