# Question

What characterises situational leadership?

## Options

- **A**: Using the same leadership style regardless of circumstances
- **B**: Adapting leadership style based on the team's development level and the situation
- **C**: Leading only when situations become critical
- **D**: Letting the situation determine who leads

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Situational leadership (Hersey and Blanchard) involves adapting your leadership style to match the development level of team members (competence and commitment) and the specific situation. New team members may need more direction; experienced, committed members may thrive with delegation. Effective leaders flex their style.

Option A describes rigid leadership that doesn't adapt.

Option C describes reactive rather than adaptive leadership.

Option D describes emergent leadership, not situational leadership.
