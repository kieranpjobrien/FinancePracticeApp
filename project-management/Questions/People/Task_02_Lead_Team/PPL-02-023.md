# Question

A project is failing despite the team's best efforts because executive stakeholders have imposed unrealistic constraints. Team morale is dropping as members feel set up for failure. How should the PM lead?

## Options

- **A**: Hide the reality from the team to protect morale.
- **B**: Tell the team publicly that executives are at fault to take pressure off.
- **C**: Tell the team their efforts don't matter given the constraints.
- **D**: Be transparent with the team, advocate to executives for changes, focus the team on what they can control, and shield them from unfair blame.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the four-part PMI leadership stance for impossible situations: honesty, upward advocacy, focus on the controllable and shielding from blame. (A) destroys trust the moment reality becomes obvious. (B) is unprofessional and creates organisational damage. (C) destroys effort and morale.
