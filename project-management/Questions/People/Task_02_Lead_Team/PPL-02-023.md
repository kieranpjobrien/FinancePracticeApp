# Question

A project is failing despite the team's best efforts due to unrealistic constraints imposed by executive stakeholders. Team morale is suffering as they feel set up for failure. How should the PM lead the team through this?

## Options

- **A**: Hide the reality to protect morale
- **B**: Be transparent about constraints, advocate upward for changes, and focus the team on what they can control while protecting them from blame
- **C**: Blame executives publicly to deflect responsibility
- **D**: Let the team know their efforts don't matter given the constraints

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership during impossible situations requires honesty, advocacy, and focus. Be transparent with the team (they usually know anyway). Advocate strongly to executives for realistic constraints. Focus team energy on what's controllable. Shield them from unfair blame. This maintains trust and dignity even if the project struggles.

Option A destroys trust when reality becomes apparent.

Option C is unprofessional and creates organisational conflict.

Option D destroys morale and stops effort entirely.
