# Question

A project manager is leading a team of highly skilled experts who resent being micromanaged. The previous PM gave detailed instructions for every task, and the team is now disengaged. What leadership style should the new PM adopt?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the detailed instruction approach for consistency
- **B**: Adopt a servant leadership approach, removing obstacles and empowering the team to self-organise
- **C**: Provide no direction at all and let the team figure everything out
- **D**: Focus only on administrative tasks and avoid interacting with the team

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Highly skilled experts typically respond best to servant leadership - supporting their work rather than directing it. The PM should focus on removing obstacles, providing resources, shielding the team from distractions, and empowering them to make decisions within their expertise. This rebuilds engagement and leverages their capabilities.

Option A perpetuates the disengagement problem.

Option C provides too little structure - even experts need context, priorities, and support.

Option D abandons leadership responsibility entirely.
