# Question

According to situational leadership theory, what factors determine the most effective leadership style?

## Options

- **A**: The leader's personality and preferences
- **B**: The organisation's culture only
- **C**: The team member's competence and commitment level for the specific task
- **D**: The project's budget and timeline

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Situational leadership (Hersey and Blanchard) suggests leaders should adapt their style based on the follower's readiness - their competence (ability) and commitment (willingness) for the specific task. The same person may need different leadership styles for different tasks depending on their experience with each.

The four styles match readiness levels:
- **Directing**: Low competence, high commitment
- **Coaching**: Some competence, low commitment
- **Supporting**: High competence, variable commitment
- **Delegating**: High competence, high commitment
