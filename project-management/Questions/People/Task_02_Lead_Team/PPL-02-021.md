# Question

A PM notices that decisions made in team meetings are not being followed through. Team members agree in meetings but then continue with their own approaches. What leadership action should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Make all decisions unilaterally to ensure compliance
- **B**: Investigate why commitments aren't being honoured and address root causes - possibly unclear decisions, lack of buy-in, or competing priorities
- **C**: Stop having team meetings since they're ineffective
- **D**: Publicly call out non-compliance to enforce accountability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The pattern suggests something is wrong with either the decision process or follow-through environment. Are decisions clearly captured? Does the team genuinely agree or just appear to? Are there conflicting priorities pulling people away? Understanding root causes enables appropriate action - possibly improving how decisions are made and tracked.

Option A may get compliance but loses engagement and team ownership.

Option C abandons a valuable forum without fixing the issue.

Option D creates fear rather than addressing underlying causes.
