# Question

You are kicking off a fresh cross-functional team of ten people drawn from five different departments for a six-month customer-experience transformation. Most have never worked together and each brings their own team's working norms. What should the PM prioritise at the kick-off?

## Options

- **A**: Review the detailed project plan, roles and milestones so that the team walks out of the kick-off with a precise understanding of who owns what across the six-month programme.
- **B**: Run an energising social activity so that the team bonds personally before getting into the serious work of delivery, since relationships are the foundation for later collaboration.
- **C**: Co-create a lightweight team charter covering purpose, decision rights, working norms and conflict handling, alongside the project overview and an early working agreement retrospective.
- **D**: Delegate each functional area to its existing departmental lead so the team can start working in familiar patterns and resolve inter-team norms naturally once real collaboration begins.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI forming-stage pattern — explicit team charter plus the work overview sets both direction and norms early, reducing storming pain later. A short retrospective cements the habit. (A) covers mechanics but leaves the biggest variable (how this group will work together) to chance. (B) is nice but insufficient on its own for a six-month cross-functional effort. (D) reproduces departmental silos at the heart of a transformation whose purpose is to break them.
