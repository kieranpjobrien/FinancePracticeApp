# Question

What is the primary difference between leadership and management?

## Options

- **A**: Leaders are more senior than managers
- **B**: Leadership focuses on inspiring and directing people toward a vision, while management focuses on organising resources and processes to achieve goals
- **C**: Leaders work on strategy, managers work on tactics
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Leadership involves setting direction, aligning people, and motivating them toward a vision. Management involves planning, organising, staffing, and controlling to achieve specified objectives. While both are necessary, leadership is about people and direction, management is about systems and execution. Project managers need both skills.

Option A confuses title with function.

Option C is an oversimplification - both leaders and managers may work at various levels.

Option D ignores meaningful distinctions in the literature and practice.
