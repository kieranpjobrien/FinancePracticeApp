# Question

What is the primary difference between leadership and management?

## Options

- **A**: Leaders hold more senior titles than managers.
- **B**: Leaders work only on strategy and managers only on tactics.
- **C**: There is no meaningful difference in practice.
- **D**: Leadership focuses on inspiring and aligning people toward a vision; management focuses on organising resources and processes to achieve goals.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the standard Kotter distinction: leadership is about direction and motivation, management is about planning, organising and controlling. PMs need both. (A) conflates title with function. (B) is an oversimplification — both occur at many levels. (C) ignores well-established distinctions.
