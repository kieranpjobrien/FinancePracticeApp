# Question

The COO is regularly contacting individual engineers on your supply-chain programme directly, asking for quick changes outside the prioritised backlog. Engineers comply to avoid conflict and your planned sprint commitments are slipping as a result. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell each engineer firmly that any direct COO requests must be refused until they have been added to the prioritised backlog through the product owner for that specific sprint.
- **B**: Log the ad-hoc requests quietly as additional scope and adjust the sprint forecasts each week so that the COO's requests are reflected transparently in the programme's delivery trend.
- **C**: Raise the pattern with the COO personally, explain the impact on committed delivery, and agree an intake path through the product owner that preserves his access and your commitments.
- **D**: Escalate the COO's behaviour to your sponsor and request that the sponsor instruct the COO to stop contacting engineers directly on the programme as a matter of governance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) handles the issue at the right level — peer conversation with the executive, data-driven, with a proposed path that meets his interest and protects the team. (A) places the burden on engineers to say no to an executive, which is unrealistic and unfair. (B) silently absorbs the impact and normalises the behaviour without fixing it. (D) escalates before the PM has had the direct conversation the situation calls for and risks appearing politically clumsy in the wider stakeholder community.
