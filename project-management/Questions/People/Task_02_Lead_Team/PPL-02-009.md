# Question

Which leadership style is MOST appropriate when team members are highly skilled and motivated?

## Options

- **A**: Directing - providing specific instructions and close supervision
- **B**: Coaching - explaining decisions and providing opportunity for clarification
- **C**: Supporting - facilitating and supporting team efforts
- **D**: Delegating - turning over responsibility for decisions and implementation

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. According to situational leadership theory, when team members are highly skilled (competent) AND highly motivated (committed), the most appropriate style is Delegating. These team members can be trusted to take responsibility and make good decisions with minimal oversight. The leader should empower them and step back.

Option A (Directing) is appropriate for team members who lack both skills and motivation - they need specific instructions and supervision. Using this style with highly skilled, motivated people would be demotivating and inefficient.

Option B (Coaching) suits team members who are motivated but still developing skills - they need guidance and explanation while learning.

Option C (Supporting) is for skilled but less motivated team members - they know how to do the work but need encouragement and support to stay engaged.
