# Question

A new team member is struggling to meet expectations. They have the technical skills but seem unclear about priorities and how their work fits into the bigger picture. What leadership approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Give them more time to figure it out on their own
- **B**: Provide clear direction on priorities and context, with regular check-ins to ensure understanding
- **C**: Assign them only simple tasks until they improve
- **D**: Document performance issues for potential termination

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The description suggests a situational leadership need for more direction (not ability issues). New team members often need explicit guidance on priorities, context, and expectations that experienced members have internalised. Regular check-ins allow course correction and build confidence. This is "directing" style for someone new to the situation.

Option A leaves them struggling without needed support.

Option C underutilises their skills and may demotivate them.

Option D is premature when the issue appears to be onboarding support, not performance.
