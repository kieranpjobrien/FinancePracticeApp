# Question

A project manager inherits a team that previously had excellent results but is now underperforming. Investigation reveals the previous PM had a strong personal connection with each team member and spent significant time on team development. The new PM is more task-focused. What should the new PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Focus on processes and metrics to bring performance back up
- **B**: Try to replicate the previous PM's personal style exactly
- **C**: Invest time in building relationships with the team while being authentic to their own leadership style
- **D**: Accept that performance will be lower without the previous PM

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The performance drop suggests relationships matter to this team. The new PM should invest in relationships (the team's need) while being authentic (not faking a personality). Different leadership styles can build strong relationships - the key is genuine investment in the people, not mimicking someone else.

Option A ignores what made the team successful - relationships. Pure task focus may further demotivate a team that valued personal connection.

Option B asks the PM to be inauthentic, which rarely works long-term. People detect false personas. The PM needs to build relationships in their own way.

Option D accepts decline without attempting solutions.
