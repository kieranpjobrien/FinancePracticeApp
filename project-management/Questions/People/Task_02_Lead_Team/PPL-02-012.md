# Question

A new PM inherits a previously high-performing team that is now underperforming. The previous PM had strong personal relationships and invested heavily in team development; the new PM is more task-focused. What should the new PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Lean into processes and metrics to drive performance back up.
- **B**: Invest deliberate time in building relationships with the team while staying authentic to their own leadership style.
- **C**: Try to copy the previous PM's style as closely as possible.
- **D**: Accept that performance will be permanently lower without the previous PM.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) recognises what made the team work — relationship investment — and meets it without asking the new PM to fake a personality. Different styles can build strong relationships when investment is genuine. (A) doubles down on the gap. (C) is inauthentic and rarely lasts. (D) gives up.
