# Question

On a two-year public-sector records digitisation project, your 22-person team has delivered every milestone but engagement scores have fallen each quarter. Exit interviews from the three recent leavers cite "lack of meaning" rather than pay or conditions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange a modest team celebration and public thank-you from the sponsor at the next all-hands to lift morale and acknowledge the consistent delivery the team has produced so far.
- **B**: Work with HR on a spot-bonus programme so that individual contributions can be recognised financially each quarter in addition to the organisation's normal annual review cycle.
- **C**: Introduce a monthly "star contributor" award and let team members nominate each other so that recognition becomes an ongoing peer-driven rhythm within the team's working week.
- **D**: Connect the team's daily work to citizen outcomes explicitly — bring users in, share before-and-after impact, and re-narrate the project's purpose at milestone moments with the sponsor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) addresses what the exit interviews actually said: meaning. Intrinsic motivators — purpose, impact, mastery — outperform recognition ceremonies for engaged knowledge workers, which is the PMI leadership principle. (A) is a one-off gesture that does not change the lived experience. (B) assumes money fixes a meaning problem, which exit data has already contradicted. (C) adds a ceremony but does not reconnect the work to why it matters to the people doing it.
