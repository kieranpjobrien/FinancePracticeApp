# Question

A project is in crisis - significantly behind schedule with a critical deadline approaching. The team is demoralised after months of overtime. As the PM, you need to push for one final effort. How should you lead the team through this?

## Options

- **A**: Demand more overtime and emphasise the consequences of failure
- **B**: Acknowledge the difficulty, be transparent about the situation, work alongside the team, and commit to addressing sustainable pace after the deadline
- **C**: Promise bonuses you're not authorised to give
- **D**: Hide the severity of the situation to avoid further demoralising the team

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Crisis leadership requires authenticity and presence. Acknowledge the team's sacrifices and the difficulty of the ask. Be transparent about why this matters. Work alongside them (don't just direct from above). Commit to post-crisis recovery - sustainable pace, recognition, time off. This builds trust through shared hardship.

Option A threatens and demands without offering support or recognition.

Option C makes promises you can't keep, destroying credibility when they're not honoured.

Option D is deceptive and treats the team as incapable of handling truth.
