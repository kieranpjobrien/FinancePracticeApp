# Question

A PM takes over a team that was previously led in an authoritarian style. Team members are used to being told exactly what to do and wait for instructions. The PM wants to develop more autonomous team behaviour. What leadership approach should they use?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately stop giving directions and expect the team to self-organise
- **B**: Gradually transition by first explaining decisions, then asking for input, then delegating decisions with support
- **C**: Continue the authoritarian style since it's what the team knows
- **D**: Replace team members with people accustomed to autonomy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Teams accustomed to direction need gradual transition to autonomy. Start by explaining decisions (building understanding), then ask for input before deciding (building participation), then delegate decisions with PM support available (building confidence). This developmental approach builds capability rather than creating anxiety.

Option A creates confusion and anxiety from sudden change.

Option C perpetuates dependency.

Option D loses team knowledge and may not be practical.
