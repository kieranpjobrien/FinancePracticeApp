# Question

Your team has been performing well, and a few members are ready for more challenging work. The current project does not naturally offer that growth. How should you lead?

## Options

- **A**: Identify stretch opportunities within or adjacent to the project, advocate for their involvement in organisational initiatives, and discuss longer-term career development.
- **B**: Tell them to focus on current work and wait for the next project.
- **C**: Recommend they look for jobs elsewhere if they want growth.
- **D**: Take their good performance for granted and do nothing.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the developmental PMI response: stretch within the project, advocate beyond it, and have explicit career conversations — that's how engaged people are retained. (B) lets boredom convert into attrition. (C) abandons retention without trying. (D) takes good performance for granted, which usually erodes it.
