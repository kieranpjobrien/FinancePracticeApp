# Question

Your team has been performing well, and a few members are ready for more challenging work. However, the current project doesn't offer growth opportunities. How should you lead in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Tell them to focus on current work and wait for the next project
- **B**: Identify stretch opportunities within or adjacent to the project, advocate for their involvement in organisational initiatives, and discuss longer-term career development
- **C**: Recommend they look for jobs elsewhere
- **D**: Ignore their development needs since performance is good

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Good leaders invest in team member growth even when it's not immediately convenient. Within the project: can they lead a component, mentor newcomers, or handle stakeholder communication? Outside: are there organisational initiatives they could join? Discuss their career goals and how you can support development. This retains talent and builds loyalty.

Option A risks losing engaged team members to boredom or external opportunities.

Option C gives up on retention without trying development options.

Option D takes good performance for granted and ignores factors that sustain it.
