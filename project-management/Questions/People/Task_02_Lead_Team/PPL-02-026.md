# Question

You've just taken over leadership of a team that was previously micromanaged. Team members wait for explicit instructions before acting and avoid making any decisions themselves. How should you lead the transition to greater autonomy?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately remove all oversight and tell them to figure it out
- **B**: Continue the micromanagement style since it's what they're used to
- **C**: Gradually increase autonomy, starting with small decisions, providing clear boundaries, and supporting them through mistakes as they build confidence
- **D**: Replace the team with people who can work independently

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Teams conditioned to micromanagement need gradual transition to autonomy. Start with small, low-risk decisions. Provide clear boundaries ("you can decide X without asking"). Support them through early mistakes rather than punishing them. As confidence builds, expand their decision-making authority. This builds capability sustainably.

Option A will create anxiety and likely failure - they don't have the practice.

Option B perpetuates learned helplessness and limits team potential.

Option D is wasteful when the current team can be developed.
