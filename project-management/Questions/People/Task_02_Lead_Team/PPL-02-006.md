# Question

According to Tuckman's model of team development, during which stage does the team begin to work together effectively with established norms and mutual trust?

## Options

- **A**: Forming
- **B**: Storming
- **C**: Norming
- **D**: Performing

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. In the Norming stage, the team has worked through initial conflicts (Storming) and begins establishing shared norms, developing trust, and working together more effectively. Team members start to appreciate each other's strengths and accept their roles.

Option A (Forming) is the initial stage where team members are getting to know each other, are often polite but guarded, and look to the leader for direction. Trust and effective collaboration haven't developed yet.

Option B (Storming) is characterized by conflict as team members push boundaries, compete for position, and challenge ideas. While necessary for growth, it's not when effective collaboration with trust occurs.

Option D (Performing) represents peak team performance where the team is highly effective and autonomous. While this also involves trust and collaboration, the question asks specifically about when these BEGIN to emerge, which is Norming.
