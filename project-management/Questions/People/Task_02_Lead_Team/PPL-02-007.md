# Question

A PM leads a cross-functional team in a matrix organisation. Each member is appraised by their functional manager against departmental goals, not project outcomes, and they consistently put departmental work first. What is the MOST effective approach?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor to mandate that project work takes priority over departmental goals.
- **B**: Issue detailed task assignments with deadlines so team members are held individually accountable.
- **C**: Request dedicated resources who report only to the project rather than their functional line.
- **D**: Engage functional managers to include project performance in appraisals and align on goals.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) attacks the root cause: the incentive structure. Once project contribution counts in appraisals, behaviour follows naturally — that is systems thinking. (A) uses authority to override incentives, producing compliance without commitment. (B) tracks symptoms but doesn't change the underlying incentives. (C) is often infeasible in matrix settings and avoids rather than solves the misalignment.
