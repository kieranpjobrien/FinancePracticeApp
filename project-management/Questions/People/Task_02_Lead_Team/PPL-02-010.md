# Question

Decisions made in team meetings are frequently not implemented. Members nod along in the room but don't follow through. Workload is not the constraint. What is the most likely cause and the right response?

## Options

- **A**: The team is being insubordinate; introduce stricter accountability measures.
- **B**: The PM should email a written summary of decisions after each meeting to lock in commitments.
- **C**: It's likely false consensus; improve facilitation to surface disagreement and confirm genuine commitment before closing each item.
- **D**: Assign someone to track action items and report on completion in the next meeting.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) targets the root cause — meeting dynamics where dominant voices or rushed agendas produce surface agreement without buy-in. Better facilitation surfaces real concerns and converts compliance into commitment. (A) assumes bad intent and damages safety further. (B) and (D) add bureaucracy that documents the failure but doesn't change why it happens.
