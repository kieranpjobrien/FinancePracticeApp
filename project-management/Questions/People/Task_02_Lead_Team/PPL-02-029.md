# Question

A project has an extremely tight deadline. The team is working hard but progress is slower than needed. What leadership approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Add more people to speed things up
- **B**: Work with the team to identify obstacles, prioritise ruthlessly, cut non-essential scope, and protect the team from distractions while ensuring sustainable pace
- **C**: Demand longer hours until the deadline passes
- **D**: Accept the project will fail and stop pushing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective leadership during crunch focuses on enabling the team. Identify and remove obstacles. Ruthlessly prioritise - what must be done vs nice to have? Protect the team from distractions (unnecessary meetings, scope creep). Consider scope reduction with stakeholders. Sustainable pace prevents burnout and maintains quality.

Option A often slows progress (Brooks' Law - adding people to a late project makes it later).

Option C burns out the team and typically reduces quality, creating more work.

Option D is premature defeatism.
