# Question

A newly formed agile team is struggling with self-organization. Team members frequently look to the project manager for decisions on task assignments and technical approaches. What leadership approach should the project manager adopt?

## Options

- **A**: Provide clear direction and assign tasks to establish structure during this forming stage
- **B**: Coach the team on self-organization while gradually stepping back as they develop capability
- **C**: Immediately enforce self-organization by refusing to make any decisions for the team
- **D**: Escalate to management that the team lacks the maturity for agile methods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This reflects servant leadership and situational leadership principles. A newly formed team (Tuckman's "forming" stage) needs more support initially. The PM should coach self-organization skills, model desired behaviors, and gradually reduce direction as the team matures. This builds sustainable capability rather than creating dependency or abandoning the team.

Option A provides too much direction for an agile team and creates dependency. While some structure helps initially, the goal is self-organization, not PM-directed work.

Option C is too abrupt and may leave the team struggling and frustrated. Teams need support to develop self-organization skills - it doesn't happen automatically by removing support.

Option D gives up too quickly. Most teams need time and coaching to become self-organizing. Declaring failure after initial struggles doesn't demonstrate leadership.
