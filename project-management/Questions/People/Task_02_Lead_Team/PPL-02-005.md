# Question

During daily standups, a team member consistently reports "no blockers" but their work items remain incomplete for multiple sprints. One-on-one conversations reveal they are struggling but don't want to appear incompetent. How should the Scrum Master address this?

## Options

- **A**: Publicly call out the discrepancy between their standup reports and actual progress
- **B**: Pair them with a senior team member and reinforce that asking for help is valued and expected
- **C**: Assign them simpler tasks that match their capability level
- **D**: Document the performance issues and initiate a formal improvement plan

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The root cause is a psychological safety issue - the team member fears appearing incompetent. The solution addresses both the immediate need (pairing for support) and the cultural issue (normalizing help-seeking). This is servant leadership - identifying the real barrier and removing it while developing the person.

Option A destroys psychological safety and confirms the team member's fear. Public criticism makes the problem worse and damages trust with the entire team.

Option C might help short-term but doesn't develop the team member or address the underlying fear. It may actually reinforce their sense of inadequacy. The goal should be growth, not accommodation of low performance.

Option D jumps to formal consequences without addressing the root cause. The team member isn't refusing to work - they're struggling silently. Formal improvement plans should follow coaching and support, not replace them.
