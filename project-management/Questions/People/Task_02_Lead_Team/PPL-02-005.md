# Question

In daily standups, a team member consistently reports "no blockers" but their work items remain incomplete sprint after sprint. One-on-ones reveal they're struggling and don't want to look incompetent. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Pair them with a senior member and reinforce that asking for help is expected and valued.
- **B**: Call out the discrepancy publicly in the next standup so the rest of the team can support them.
- **C**: Reassign them to simpler tasks that better match their current demonstrated capability level.
- **D**: Document the under-performance formally and start a performance improvement plan straight away.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) treats the root cause — psychological safety — by combining a practical support (pairing) with a cultural signal that help-seeking is normal. PMI treats safety as a leadership responsibility. (B) confirms the fear and damages safety. (C) accommodates rather than develops the person and may reinforce inadequacy. (D) jumps to formal consequences before any coaching has been tried.
