# Question

What is psychological safety in a team context?

## Options

- **A**: Physical security measures in the workplace
- **B**: An environment where team members feel safe to take interpersonal risks, speak up, and make mistakes without fear of punishment
- **C**: Insurance coverage for mental health treatment
- **D**: A guarantee that no one will be fired

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Psychological safety, as defined by researcher Amy Edmondson, is the shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. Team members feel able to speak up with ideas, questions, concerns, or mistakes without fear of embarrassment or punishment. This enables learning, innovation, and collaboration.

Option A refers to physical safety, not psychological safety.

Option C is about healthcare benefits, not team dynamics.

Option D is job security, which is different from psychological safety.
