# Question

You notice that junior team members rarely contribute ideas in workshops when senior architects are present, even when the topic is squarely in their domain. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Restructure facilitation so junior members present their thinking first, use silent ideation before discussion, and explicitly ask seniors to listen before responding.
- **B**: Run separate workshops for junior and senior members and merge the outputs afterwards.
- **C**: Tell the seniors privately to be less assertive in workshops.
- **D**: Accept the dynamic since juniors typically grow into contribution over time.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats participation imbalance as a facilitation problem, addressed through structure rather than personality coaching or segregation. (A) is a structural intervention that lifts contribution without making it personal. (B) loses the value of cross-level discussion and creates two parallel tracks. (C) puts the issue back on individuals and is hard to enforce. (D) accepts an avoidable performance loss.
