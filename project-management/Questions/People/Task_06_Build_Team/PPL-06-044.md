# Question

A team includes both junior and senior members. The PM notices that junior members are reluctant to contribute ideas in the presence of senior members. How can the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that junior members should defer to seniors
- **B**: Separate junior and senior members into different subteams
- **C**: Create structures where all contributions are valued, such as having junior members present first, using anonymous idea submission, or explicitly asking seniors to withhold judgment initially
- **D**: Tell senior members to be less intimidating

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Power dynamics affect participation. Structural changes can level the playing field without diminishing senior expertise. Having junior members speak first prevents anchoring on senior opinions. Anonymous submissions remove status bias. Explicit norms ("let's hear from everyone before evaluating") create permission to contribute.

Option A loses valuable perspectives from junior team members.

Option B eliminates learning opportunities and collaboration benefits.

Option D is vague and may not change the underlying dynamic.
