# Question

A PM is forming a team for a high-risk, innovative project. When reviewing candidates, they notice one highly skilled individual who has a reputation for being difficult to work with but delivers exceptional results. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose the most technically skilled candidate
- **B**: Never hire someone with interpersonal challenges
- **C**: Assess whether the individual's skills are truly irreplaceable, the impact on team dynamics, and whether appropriate support structures can mitigate risks
- **D**: Let the team decide whether to include this person

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This requires balanced judgment. Some situations genuinely require rare skills that outweigh interpersonal challenges. However, toxic behaviour can destroy team effectiveness regardless of individual contribution. The PM should consider: Can the skills be found elsewhere? What specific behaviours are problematic? Can coaching, clear expectations, or team structure mitigate issues?

Option A ignores the team impact of difficult personalities.

Option B may exclude valuable contributors who could succeed with support.

Option D abdicates leadership responsibility for team composition.
