# Question

You are forming a team for a high-risk innovation project. One candidate is exceptional technically but has a documented pattern of dismissive behaviour in past teams. Skills are scarce in this domain. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Assess whether the skills are truly irreplaceable, define expected behaviours and supports, and decide based on the team-impact trade-off rather than reputation alone.
- **B**: Hire the candidate because the technical scarcity outweighs any interpersonal concerns.
- **C**: Reject the candidate to avoid any risk of damaging the new team.
- **D**: Put the decision to the prospective team members and let them vote.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to make team composition decisions based on evidence and clear expectations rather than reputation or risk-aversion. (A) is structured and accountable. (B) underweights team risk on a project that depends on collaboration. (C) discards potential value without testing it. (D) abdicates a PM responsibility and creates an awkward dynamic from day one.
