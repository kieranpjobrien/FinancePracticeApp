# Question

A team that has been Performing for several months has a senior developer join. She challenges several established practices, and previously collaborative members are becoming defensive. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the new developer to keep her concerns to herself for the first three months while she observes quietly.
- **B**: Recognise the regression to Storming and facilitate a structured discussion of the challenged practices with the team.
- **C**: Back the new developer's perspective publicly to signal that the existing team should welcome change from newcomers.
- **D**: Step back and let the team work it out themselves now that they are a mature Performing team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI recognises that team composition changes can cause regression to earlier stages and expects the PM to facilitate the team back to Performing. (B) treats the symptoms as predictable and addresses both the substance and the dynamic. (A) silences potentially valuable input and signals that conformity matters more than improvement. (C) picks winners and alienates the existing team. (D) is hands-off when active facilitation is what is needed.
