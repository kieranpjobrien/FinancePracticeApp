# Question

A project team has been together for several months and was performing well. A new senior developer joins, challenging existing practices and creating tension. Previously collaborative members are now defensive. What is happening and what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the new developer to restore harmony
- **B**: Recognise this as regression to Storming; facilitate healthy conflict resolution while protecting valuable practices
- **C**: Side with the new developer to bring fresh perspectives
- **D**: Ignore the conflict and expect it to resolve naturally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When team composition changes, teams often regress to earlier stages. The new member's challenges and existing members' defensiveness are Storming behaviours. The PM should normalise this as part of team development, facilitate constructive discussion of the challenges raised, and help the team integrate the new member while preserving what works.

Option A (remove new person) loses potentially valuable input and doesn't develop the team's resilience.

Option C (side with new person) alienates existing members and picks winners/losers.

Option D (ignore) lets conflict fester and become destructive rather than productive.
