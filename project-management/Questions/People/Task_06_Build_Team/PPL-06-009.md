# Question

A PM is building a new team for a critical project. They can choose between people who have worked together successfully before but have moderate skills, versus highly skilled individuals who have never worked together. Which consideration should be PRIMARY?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose highest skills regardless of team dynamics
- **B**: Always choose existing teams regardless of skills
- **C**: Assess project needs - complex technical work may favour skills; tight timelines may favour existing team cohesion
- **D**: Let HR decide team composition

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Team selection requires balancing multiple factors. New teams need time to develop (Tuckman's stages). Existing teams bypass forming/storming but may lack needed skills. The choice depends on project context: How complex is the work? How tight is the timeline? Can skills be developed? Is there time for team formation?

Option A ignores the productivity cost of team formation.

Option B ignores potential skill gaps.

Option D abdicates PM responsibility for team composition.
