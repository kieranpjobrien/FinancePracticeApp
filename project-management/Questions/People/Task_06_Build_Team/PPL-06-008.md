# Question

According to Tuckman's model, what are the five stages of team development?

## Options

- **A**: Planning, Executing, Monitoring, Controlling, Closing
- **B**: Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning
- **C**: Initiating, Defining, Designing, Developing, Delivering
- **D**: Starting, Struggling, Settling, Succeeding, Stopping

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Tuckman's model describes how teams develop:

1. **Forming**: Team comes together, polite, testing boundaries
2. **Storming**: Conflict emerges as members assert themselves
3. **Norming**: Team establishes working agreements and finds rhythm
4. **Performing**: High performance, effective collaboration
5. **Adjourning**: Team disbands (added later to the original model)

Option A lists project management process groups, not team development stages.

Options C and D are not standard models of team development.
