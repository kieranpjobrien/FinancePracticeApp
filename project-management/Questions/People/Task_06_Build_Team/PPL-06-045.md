# Question

A PM is forming a team and must choose between co-locating all team members or allowing remote work based on individual preference. What factors should primarily drive this decision?

## Options

- **A**: Always co-locate for maximum collaboration
- **B**: Always allow remote work for maximum flexibility
- **C**: Consider project complexity, need for real-time collaboration, team experience working together, and available collaboration tools before deciding on the optimal arrangement
- **D**: Let each team member decide independently

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Neither co-location nor remote work is universally superior. Complex, innovative projects with new teams may benefit from co-location initially. Experienced teams with established practices may work effectively remotely. Hybrid approaches can balance collaboration needs with flexibility. The decision should be based on project and team characteristics, not ideology.

Option A ignores valid reasons for remote work.

Option B ignores valid benefits of co-location.

Option D may create coordination problems if choices conflict.
