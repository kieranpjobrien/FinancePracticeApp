# Question

Your team of twelve includes four people from a managed-services vendor who report to a vendor delivery lead, not to you. Three months in, vendor staff attend standups but defer to their own lead for decisions and commitments. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with the vendor delivery lead to agree clear decision rights, shared team charter, and a single way of working that binds everyone regardless of which employer pays them.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor to convert the four vendor staff into badged employees of your organisation so that command lines collapse into a single reporting structure.
- **C**: Accept the dual reporting as a fact of managed-services delivery and work around it by making vendor commitments contingent on their lead's later confirmation.
- **D**: Stop inviting the vendor staff to team ceremonies and integrate them only through a weekly status meeting with their lead that you attend on the team's behalf.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's team-building principle in co-managed arrangements is to negotiate a clear working framework with the other employer, not to accept silos, force a restructure, or exclude members. (A) creates one team with explicit decision rights. (B) is an HR/procurement change far beyond what the issue requires. (C) normalises delay and two-tier commitment. (D) formalises exclusion and erodes team cohesion.
