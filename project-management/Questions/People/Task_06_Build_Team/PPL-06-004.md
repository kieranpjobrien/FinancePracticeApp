# Question

A new team has been formed from people who previously worked in different departments with different cultures. In early meetings, members are hesitant to share opinions and defer to perceived senior members. What team development activity would be MOST helpful?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a clear hierarchy so everyone knows their place
- **B**: Conduct team-building activities that help members learn about each other and establish shared norms
- **C**: Jump straight into project work so members can learn each other through collaboration
- **D**: Have each member present their credentials to establish credibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The symptoms (hesitancy, deference) indicate the team is in the Forming stage and needs relationship-building before effective collaboration. Team-building activities help members understand each other as people, reduce uncertainty, and begin establishing shared culture and norms.

Option A reinforces hierarchy over collaboration, which may worsen the hesitancy to speak up.

Option C skips the relationship foundation. Without connection, project work may be awkward and ineffective.

Option D emphasizes credentials and status, which may increase the deference problem rather than reducing it.
