# Question

A cross-functional team has just come together from several departments. In the first two meetings, members are polite, rarely disagree, and defer to whoever is perceived as most senior. What should the project manager do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Move quickly to assign concrete work so members learn about each other through collaboration.
- **B**: Facilitate structured team-building and norming activities to build relationships and agree ways of working.
- **C**: Ask each member to present their experience so credibility is clearly established up front.
- **D**: Define a clear chain of command so members know who to defer to.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The behaviours described are classic Forming-stage signals; PMI recommends investing in relationships and shared norms before expecting productive disagreement. (B) addresses the stage directly. (A) skips the foundation and often leads to shallow collaboration. (C) deepens status-based deference rather than reducing it. (D) formalises hierarchy where psychological safety is what is actually missing.
