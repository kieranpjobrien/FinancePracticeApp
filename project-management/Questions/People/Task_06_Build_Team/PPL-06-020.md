# Question

You are staffing a complex innovation project. HR policy strongly favours internal candidates, but two specialist skills you need are not present in-house and would take six months to develop. The project must deliver in nine months. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hire externally for the two specialist roles without engaging HR, on the grounds that delivery has to come first.
- **B**: Staff exclusively with internal candidates and accept the schedule risk to comply with policy.
- **C**: Escalate the conflict to executive leadership immediately and let them decide.
- **D**: Build a business case showing the specific gap and timeline impact, propose a blended team, and negotiate targeted exceptions with HR.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to work within organisational constraints while advocating for project needs through evidence and dialogue. (D) addresses both. (A) bypasses governance and damages trust. (B) optimises for compliance at the expense of delivery. (C) escalates before attempting resolution at the appropriate level.
