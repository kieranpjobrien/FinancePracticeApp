# Question

A PM is building a team for a complex, innovative product development project. The organisation's HR policy favours internal candidates, but the PM believes external specialists would bring necessary expertise. What approach balances organisational policy with project needs?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore HR policy and hire externally
- **B**: Accept only internal candidates to comply with policy
- **C**: Present a business case showing specific skill gaps that internal candidates cannot fill, propose a blended team, and negotiate exceptions where justified
- **D**: Escalate to senior management to override HR

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective PMs work within organisational constraints while advocating for project needs. A documented business case showing specific gaps, along with evidence that internal training isn't feasible within project timelines, creates a compelling argument. A blended team often satisfies both organisational culture goals and project requirements.

Option A creates organisational conflict and undermines trust.

Option B may doom the project if critical skills are missing.

Option D should be a last resort after attempting negotiation.
