# Question

An organisation is transitioning from traditional project teams to stable, long-lived agile teams. What is the primary advantage of stable teams?

## Options

- **A**: Easier budget tracking
- **B**: Teams develop deep knowledge, established working relationships, and predictable velocity over time
- **C**: Reduced need for management oversight
- **D**: Lower personnel costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Stable teams avoid the productivity loss of repeated forming/storming cycles. They develop shared understanding, tacit knowledge, and refined processes. Their velocity becomes predictable, enabling better planning. Research shows stable teams significantly outperform newly formed teams.

Option A is not a primary benefit of team stability.

Option C may or may not be true depending on team maturity.

Option D is not typically a characteristic of stable teams.
