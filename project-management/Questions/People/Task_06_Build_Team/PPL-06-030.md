# Question

An organisation is moving from project-based teams that disband after each release to stable, long-lived agile teams that take in a flow of work. What is the PRIMARY benefit of stable teams?

## Options

- **A**: Stable teams are easier for finance to track for budget allocation and capitalisation purposes.
- **B**: Stable teams avoid repeated forming cycles and develop predictable capacity and deeper shared knowledge.
- **C**: Stable teams require less management oversight than project-based teams that disband after each release.
- **D**: Stable teams are typically cheaper to staff than project-based teams over comparable time horizons.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and the agile community both highlight that stable teams accumulate capability and predictability that project teams lose every time they reform. (B) names that primary benefit. (A) is incidental, not primary. (C) and (D) are not consistent generalisations and depend on context.
