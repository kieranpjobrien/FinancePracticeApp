# Question

In Tuckman's model, what best describes the Performing stage?

## Options

- **A**: Members are still cautious about boundaries and tend to defer to perceived authority.
- **B**: Open conflict and contests over informal leadership are common.
- **C**: The team is settling into shared norms and beginning to establish a steady rhythm.
- **D**: The team operates with high trust and autonomy, focusing energy on goals rather than internal dynamics.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Performing is the high-functioning stage where the team's effort goes into the work, not into internal negotiation. (D) captures it. (A) is Forming. (B) is Storming. (C) is Norming.
