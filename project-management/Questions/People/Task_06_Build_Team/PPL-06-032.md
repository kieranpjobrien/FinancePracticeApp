# Question

What does "T-shaped skills" mean in the context of agile team building?

## Options

- **A**: Team members who only have deep expertise in one area
- **B**: Team members who have broad knowledge across multiple areas (horizontal bar) plus deep expertise in one specialty (vertical bar)
- **C**: Teams that are structured in a T-formation
- **D**: Technical skills that are transferable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. T-shaped individuals combine depth (vertical bar - their specialty) with breadth (horizontal bar - general knowledge across other areas). This enables collaboration, reduces bottlenecks, and allows team members to help each other. Agile teams benefit from T-shaped skills because work can flow more smoothly.

Option A describes I-shaped skills (specialists only).

Option C misinterprets the concept.

Option D partially captures breadth but misses the depth component.
