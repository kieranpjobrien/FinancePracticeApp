# Question

What does "T-shaped skills" mean in an agile team context?

## Options

- **A**: Members who specialise deeply in one technology and avoid working outside it.
- **B**: Skills that are formally certified and recognised across the industry.
- **C**: Members who combine deep expertise in a primary specialty with broad working knowledge across other areas of the team's work.
- **D**: A team layout where one architect sits across two delivery pods.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

T-shaped is a well-established description of a contributor profile that supports flow in cross-functional teams. (C) captures both axes of the "T". (A) describes I-shaped specialists. (B) confuses the model with certification. (D) misreads the term as a structural metaphor.
