# Question

A team member is technically competent but consistently disrupts sessions with side conversations, dismissive comments about other team members' ideas, and late arrivals. The team's own working agreement covers these behaviours explicitly. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Have a direct, private conversation with the team member that references the working agreement, names the specific behaviours, and agrees a change with clear follow-up.
- **B**: Raise the specific behaviours in the next team retrospective so the wider team can collectively challenge the individual and reinforce the working agreement norms.
- **C**: Escalate to the team member's line manager and request a formal performance process before attempting any direct conversation with the individual yourself.
- **D**: Quietly reassign the team member off your project to another initiative where their behaviour will have less impact on a more senior and seasoned group.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to address behaviour directly with the individual first, anchored in an agreed team norm, before escalation or removal. (A) does that. (B) uses the team as a confrontation device and can damage psychological safety. (C) escalates to performance management without a prior direct conversation. (D) offloads a behavioural issue onto another team rather than addressing it.
