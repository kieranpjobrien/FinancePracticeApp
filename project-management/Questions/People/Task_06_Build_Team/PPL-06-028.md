# Question

You are staffing an 18-month programme. Some roles are needed from day one, others from month six, and a few only from month twelve. Budget rules limit the number of people you can onboard up front. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hire all roles in the first month so the team is settled before delivery accelerates.
- **B**: Defer recruitment for each role until the month it is required, to minimise burn rate.
- **C**: Outsource the entire later-phase scope to a single supplier so internal staffing only covers the first six months.
- **D**: Build a phased resource plan, identify and engage candidates for later roles early, and keep their managers warm so onboarding is smooth when needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI emphasises proactive resource management with lead-time awareness, especially when budget rules limit upfront hiring. (D) reduces both cost and availability risk. (A) wastes budget on idle resources. (B) optimises burn rate at the expense of availability. (C) is a major scope and supplier decision that should not be driven by a staffing question alone.
