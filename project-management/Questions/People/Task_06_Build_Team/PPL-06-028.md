# Question

A PM is building a team for an 18-month programme. Some roles are needed immediately while others won't be needed for 6-12 months. Budget constraints prevent hiring everyone upfront. How should the PM approach team building?

## Options

- **A**: Hire all roles immediately to ensure availability
- **B**: Wait until each role is needed to begin recruitment
- **C**: Develop a resource plan with phased onboarding, begin early identification of future resources, and build relationships with potential candidates or their managers
- **D**: Use only contractors to maintain flexibility

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Strategic team building considers timing and lead times. Early identification allows for negotiation and planning even if onboarding is delayed. Building relationships with functional managers or external candidates ensures resources are available when needed. This balances budget constraints with resource availability risks.

Option A wastes budget and may result in underutilised resources.

Option B creates risk of unavailability when roles become critical.

Option D may compromise team cohesion and knowledge retention.
