# Question

A functional manager will not release one of their senior engineers to your project for the four months you need her, citing departmental priorities. The engineer holds the key skill for an early high-risk workstream. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the resource dispute to the PMO and request the engineer be reassigned by management decision.
- **B**: Accept a less qualified replacement engineer from the pool to keep the project schedule moving forward.
- **C**: Meet the functional manager to understand constraints and explore options like part-time or a phased start.
- **D**: Delay the high-risk workstream entirely until the engineer becomes available from her current assignment.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs in matrix environments to negotiate before escalating, and to look for options that meet both sides' needs. (C) does that. (A) escalates without first attempting resolution at the right level. (B) accepts capability risk without testing whether a better deal is possible. (D) shifts the schedule without first exploring lower-impact options.
