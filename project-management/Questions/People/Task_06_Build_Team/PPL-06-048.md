# Question

A senior architect who founded the original team is returning after eighteen months on another programme. The team has evolved new practices during her absence, and she has publicly said she expects her old role back exactly as before. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign her former role back to her and revert any recent practice changes that conflict with how the team used to operate under her original leadership.
- **B**: Ask her to shadow the team silently for a month with no defined role, then have her propose how she would like to contribute once she has observed everyone.
- **C**: Meet with her one-to-one before her return to agree the shape of her contribution given the team's current state, then introduce her into the team with a clear remit.
- **D**: Keep her out of the team and place her in an advisory role reporting directly to the steering committee, bypassing the team's existing working arrangements entirely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI combines respect for experienced members with protection of the team's evolved norms by setting expectations before the person rejoins. (C) does both. (A) rewinds the team's development to accommodate one individual's assumptions. (B) leaves both her and the team in ambiguity, which breeds friction during reintegration. (D) isolates a returning senior member from the very team she is meant to strengthen.
