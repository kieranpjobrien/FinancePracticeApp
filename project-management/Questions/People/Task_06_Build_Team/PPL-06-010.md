# Question

A cross-functional team includes members from departments with historical tensions. During the kickoff, members are polite but guarded, and some make subtle negative comments about other departments. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it and focus on the work - professionalism will prevail
- **B**: Address the dynamic directly, acknowledge the history, establish shared team identity separate from departmental affiliations
- **C**: Escalate to department heads to resolve the tensions
- **D**: Replace team members from the problematic departments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Unaddressed historical tensions will undermine team performance. The PM should acknowledge the elephant in the room - past tensions exist, but this team is a new entity with shared goals. Establishing team identity, norms, and shared purpose creates space to build new working relationships distinct from departmental baggage.

Option A allows tensions to simmer and poison collaboration.

Option C doesn't address the immediate team dynamic.

Option D loses needed expertise and may not be feasible.
