# Question

A cross-functional team is being formed from two departments that have a history of friction over budget allocations. At the kickoff, members are polite but pointed comments about "the other side" come up several times. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Address the dynamic openly, acknowledge the history, and work with the team to define a shared identity, purpose, and ground rules.
- **B**: Stay focused on the project schedule and trust that delivering together will eventually dissolve the tension.
- **C**: Escalate to the two department heads to settle the historical disputes before continuing.
- **D**: Quietly swap out the most outspoken members to reduce the friction in the room.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to surface and manage interpersonal dynamics that threaten team performance, rather than ignoring or escalating them. (A) is direct, builds psychological safety, and creates a shared identity. (B) lets corrosive dynamics persist into delivery. (C) sends an unresolvable issue upwards instead of addressing what is solvable at the team level. (D) treats people as the problem and sacrifices needed expertise.
