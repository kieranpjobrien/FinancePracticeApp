# Question

You are interviewing a candidate with strong technical skills for a role on a highly collaborative team. When asked about teamwork, the candidate says they "prefer to be left alone to deliver" and dismisses pair programming. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Probe specific examples of past teamwork and conflict, explain the team's expected ways of working, and decide based on whether they can adapt rather than on a single answer.
- **B**: Hire the candidate based on the technical scores and rely on team norms to shape behaviour.
- **C**: Reject the candidate immediately because the answer is incompatible with the team's working style.
- **D**: Hire conditionally with a written warning that collaboration will be assessed at three months.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI emphasises evidence-based hiring decisions and expectation setting up front, not snap judgements or punitive conditions. (A) tests the concern with examples and gives the candidate a fair chance. (B) ignores a clear signal. (C) reaches a conclusion without evidence. (D) starts the relationship with a threat and skips the diagnostic step.
