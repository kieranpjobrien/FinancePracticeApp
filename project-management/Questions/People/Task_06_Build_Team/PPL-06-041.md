# Question

A PM is conducting interviews for a new team member. One candidate has excellent technical skills but gives concerning answers about teamwork, preferring to work alone. The project requires extensive collaboration. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hire based on technical skills and hope collaboration improves
- **B**: Reject the candidate without further exploration
- **C**: Probe deeper to understand the candidate's concerns about teamwork, assess if they can adapt, and consider if there's a role that minimises collaboration requirements
- **D**: Hire conditionally with a warning about expected behaviour

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Initial interview answers may not tell the whole story. Some people prefer independent work but collaborate effectively when needed. Others have had bad team experiences but can thrive in the right environment. Understanding the "why" behind their preference helps assess fit. If collaboration is truly essential and the candidate cannot adapt, that's valuable information.

Option A ignores a significant red flag.

Option B may reject a good candidate without full understanding.

Option D starts the relationship with a threat rather than understanding.
