# Question

You are forming a team while the organisation is in the middle of a publicly announced restructure. People are anxious, rumours are spreading, and you cannot guarantee anyone's job. What should the project manager do FIRST when bringing the team together?

## Options

- **A**: Privately reassure each member that their roles on this project are secure for the duration.
- **B**: Acknowledge the uncertainty openly, share what you do and do not know, and focus the team on what they can control: their work, their support of each other, and the value they deliver.
- **C**: Avoid mentioning the restructure in team forums and keep meetings strictly on project topics.
- **D**: Replace anxious internal members with external contractors who are not affected by the restructure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's leadership principles call for honesty and psychological safety, especially when the environment is volatile. (B) builds trust without overpromising. (A) makes commitments beyond your authority. (C) is read as evasive and erodes trust. (D) abandons internal colleagues and tells the team that loyalty does not matter.
