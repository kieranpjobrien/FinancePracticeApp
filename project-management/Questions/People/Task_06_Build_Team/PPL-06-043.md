# Question

A PM is building a team in an organisation undergoing significant layoffs. Team morale is low and people are anxious about job security. How should the PM approach team building in this environment?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the broader context and focus only on project work
- **B**: Promise team members that their jobs are safe
- **C**: Acknowledge the uncertainty, be transparent about what you know and don't know, and focus on creating a supportive team environment while delivering value
- **D**: Recruit only external contractors who aren't affected by layoffs

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ignoring organisational context damages trust. Making promises you can't keep is worse. Authentic leadership acknowledges reality while focusing on what you can control—creating psychological safety, meaningful work, and demonstrable value. Teams that deliver results are more likely to survive restructuring.

Option A appears tone-deaf and loses trust.

Option B makes commitments beyond the PM's authority.

Option D may be seen as abandoning internal colleagues and damages morale further.
