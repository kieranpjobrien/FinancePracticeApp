# Question

A newly formed Scrum team is struggling to self-organise. Team members keep looking to the Scrum Master for direction on how to accomplish work. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide detailed direction since the team is new
- **B**: Ignore requests and let the team figure it out completely on their own
- **C**: Coach the team on self-organisation by asking guiding questions, facilitating discussions, and gradually reducing direct involvement as the team matures
- **D**: Escalate to management that the team isn't ready for agile

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Self-organisation is learned, not innate. The Scrum Master should use coaching techniques—asking "What do you think?" rather than providing answers. Facilitation helps the team develop problem-solving muscles. As confidence grows, the Scrum Master steps back progressively.

Option A creates dependency rather than building capability.

Option B provides no support during a critical learning period.

Option D judges prematurely without attempting to develop the team.
