# Question

Your team is a mix of full-time members and part-time contributors who split their time across three projects. The part-time members often miss decisions and feel out of the loop. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop part-time members from social and lower-priority sessions entirely to reduce their overall meeting load.
- **B**: Negotiate with their functional managers to pull them off the other projects and onto this one full-time.
- **C**: Design a cadence of reliable async updates, decision logs, and sync points part-time members can attend.
- **D**: Accept the disengagement as an unavoidable consequence of shared resourcing across multiple competing projects.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to design ways of working that fit the resourcing reality, not fight it. (C) closes the information gap and gives part-time members a realistic way to stay connected. (A) deepens the disconnection. (B) may not be feasible and skips the cheaper structural fix. (D) gives up on a solvable problem.
