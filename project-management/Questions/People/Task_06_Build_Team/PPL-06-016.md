# Question

A high-performing team has developed a strong culture that new members find intimidating. They have inside jokes, established patterns, and implicit knowledge that makes newcomers feel like outsiders. How should the PM help integrate new members?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the existing team to be more welcoming
- **B**: Have new members adapt on their own - sink or swim
- **C**: Create explicit onboarding that shares team history, norms, and context; assign buddies; and actively include newcomers until they're integrated
- **D**: Don't add new members to avoid disrupting the team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Strong cultures need explicit onboarding. Document the implicit knowledge (how do we work? what's our history?). Assign buddies who help newcomers navigate the culture. Actively include newcomers in discussions and activities. The goal is reducing time to integration while preserving the positive culture.

Option A is too vague to be actionable.

Option B wastes talent and may lose good people.

Option D prevents necessary team evolution.
