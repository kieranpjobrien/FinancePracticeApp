# Question

A PM has budget to hire one more team member. Two candidates are available: one with 10 years of experience in outdated technologies, another with 2 years of experience in relevant modern technologies. The project uses modern technologies. Who should the PM select?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose more experience
- **B**: Always choose relevant technical skills
- **C**: Evaluate both candidates holistically considering learning ability, cultural fit, and specific project needs, recognising that relevant skills may outweigh years of experience
- **D**: Hire neither and request additional budget for both

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Experience in irrelevant technologies may not transfer. However, experienced professionals often have valuable soft skills, problem-solving abilities, and learning capacity. The PM should assess: How quickly can each candidate contribute? What's the learning curve? How do soft skills compare? The answer depends on specific circumstances, but relevant skills often matter more than years.

Option A ignores relevance of experience.

Option B ignores valuable soft skills from experience.

Option D doesn't address the actual decision.
