# Question

You can make one more hire. Candidate 1 has ten years of experience on legacy technology that is not used on this project. Candidate 2 has two years of experience directly on the modern stack the team will use. How should the project manager decide?

## Options

- **A**: Default to the more experienced candidate because seniority reduces risk on any project.
- **B**: Default to the candidate with direct stack experience because relevance trumps seniority.
- **C**: Assess both against project-specific skill needs, learning capacity, team fit, and time-to-productivity, and choose based on that combined picture.
- **D**: Decline to choose and request that both be hired on reduced contracts.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI favours evidence-based, tailored decisions over universal rules about experience or relevance. (C) weighs the factors that actually predict contribution to this project. (A) and (B) pick a rule before examining the facts. (D) dodges the decision and is unlikely to be funded.
