# Question

A newly formed project team has members who are polite but guarded. No one challenges ideas in meetings, and there's little creative tension. According to Tuckman's model, what stage is this team in, and what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Forming stage; facilitate activities that build trust and help members get to know each other
- **B**: Storming stage; let conflicts play out naturally
- **C**: Performing stage; maintain current dynamics
- **D**: Norming stage; establish team norms

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The correct answer is A. The behaviour described (polite, guarded, avoiding conflict) is characteristic of Forming - team members are testing boundaries and being cautious. The PM should facilitate trust-building, encourage openness, and create psychological safety. The team needs to move through this stage to reach productive conflict (Storming) and eventually high performance.

Option B misidentifies the stage - Storming involves conflict, not polite guardedness.

Option C - Performing teams have open discussion and creative tension, not politeness covering caution.

Option D - Norming has established working patterns; this team hasn't found its rhythm yet.
