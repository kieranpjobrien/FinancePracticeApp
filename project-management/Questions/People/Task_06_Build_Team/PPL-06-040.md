# Question

An organisation uses a resource pool model where team members are shared across multiple projects. A PM finds their team members are spread across 4-5 projects each, leading to constant context-switching and reduced productivity. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept this as organisational reality
- **B**: Demand dedicated resources for your project only
- **C**: Work with the PMO to quantify the cost of context-switching and advocate for reducing multi-tasking through dedicated assignments or time-boxing
- **D**: Hire contractors to supplement the fragmented team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Research shows context-switching can consume 20-40% of productive time. Quantifying this cost in terms the organisation understands (money, time, quality) creates a compelling case for change. Solutions might include dedicated team members, time-boxing (certain days for certain projects), or reducing work in progress. This is a systemic issue requiring systemic advocacy.

Option A accepts an inefficient status quo.

Option B is unlikely to succeed without data and may create conflict.

Option D adds cost without addressing the root cause.
