# Question

In a matrix organisation, your team members are each allocated across four or five concurrent projects. Delivery is slowing and people are visibly fatigued from constant context switching. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify context-switching cost in schedule, cost, and quality, then work with the PMO to reduce concurrency.
- **B**: Ask executive leadership to grant your project dedicated full-time staff pulled off the other parallel projects.
- **C**: Supplement the team with external contractors to absorb the load while current people continue splitting their time.
- **D**: Accept the fragmentation as how the matrix organisation works and manage delivery around the known constraints.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to advocate with data and to address systemic issues at the appropriate level rather than escalating, patching, or accepting. (A) builds the case and proposes change where it is owned. (B) jumps to the top before engaging the PMO and functional managers. (C) adds cost without fixing the root cause. (D) normalises a known drag on delivery.
