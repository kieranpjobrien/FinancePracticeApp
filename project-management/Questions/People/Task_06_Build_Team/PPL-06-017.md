# Question

You are forming a new agile team for a complex product discovery project. The candidate pool you have been offered all share similar backgrounds, education, and prior employer. What should the project manager prioritise in the final selection?

## Options

- **A**: Pick whoever has the strongest individual track record on similar problems.
- **B**: Push back on the candidate pool and request additional candidates with different perspectives and problem-solving styles before finalising the team.
- **C**: Pick those with the most years of experience to maximise senior judgement.
- **D**: Pick people who have already worked together to shortcut the forming stage.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and current research both treat cognitive diversity as a major driver of performance on complex, novel work. (B) addresses the actual gap in the candidate pool. (A), (C) and (D) optimise for one factor while leaving the homogeneity untouched, which is exactly what undermines discovery.
