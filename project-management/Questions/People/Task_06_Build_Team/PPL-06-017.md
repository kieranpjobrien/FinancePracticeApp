# Question

A project manager is forming a new agile team and notices that all candidates have similar technical backgrounds and thinking styles. What should the PM prioritise when finalising team composition?

## Options

- **A**: Select only the candidates with the highest technical skills
- **B**: Ensure cognitive diversity by including people with different perspectives and problem-solving approaches
- **C**: Choose candidates who have worked together before to reduce forming time
- **D**: Select the most experienced candidates regardless of other factors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Research consistently shows that diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones, especially for complex problem-solving. Cognitive diversity brings different perspectives, challenges groupthink, and leads to more innovative solutions. While technical skills matter, they shouldn't be the only criterion.

Option A ignores the value of diverse thinking styles.

Option C may reinforce existing biases and limit fresh perspectives.

Option D overlooks that experience alone doesn't guarantee effective collaboration or innovation.
