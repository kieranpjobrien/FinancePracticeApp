# Question

Leadership has told you that the existing onshore team of ten will be transitioned out over the next six months and replaced by an offshore team of twelve at a different vendor. The onshore team is aware. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the onshore team delivering as usual until the day the offshore team is ready, then switch the entire workload across in a single weekend cutover exercise.
- **B**: Announce the change, ask the onshore team to begin documenting their knowledge, and let the offshore team learn from the documents once they eventually come on board.
- **C**: Replace the onshore team immediately so the schedule and budget benefits of the offshore model are realised from the start of the transition window, not the end.
- **D**: Agree a phased transition with overlapping pairing, knowledge transfer milestones, fair and transparent treatment of the outgoing team, and measured capability checkpoints.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI handles team transitions with structured overlap, measurable capability verification, and fair treatment of the people leaving. (D) combines all three. (A) plans a big-bang cutover with no verified capability on the receiving side. (B) relies on documents alone for a six-month handover and under-invests in people transfer. (C) removes institutional knowledge before the offshore team has absorbed any of it and treats the onshore team as disposable.
