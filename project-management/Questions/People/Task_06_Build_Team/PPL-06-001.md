# Question

A project manager is assembling a team for a new project. They have the option to select team members from different departments. What approach will BEST support building an effective team?

## Options

- **A**: Select only the most technically skilled individuals available
- **B**: Consider both technical skills and team dynamics, looking for complementary skills and collaborative attitudes
- **C**: Allow functional managers to assign whoever is available
- **D**: Select team members who have worked together before to avoid the forming stage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective teams require both technical capability and good team dynamics. Considering complementary skills ensures coverage of needed capabilities, while assessing collaborative attitudes supports team cohesion. The best technical experts who can't work together often underperform compared to a well-balanced team.

Option A focuses only on individual technical skill, ignoring that teams are more than the sum of individual parts. Team dynamics and collaboration significantly impact performance.

Option C abdicates the PM's responsibility for team composition. While functional managers have input, the PM should actively participate in selection to build an effective team.

Option D avoids the forming stage but may create groupthink and miss opportunities for diverse perspectives. Some forming-stage investment is worthwhile for a better long-term team.
