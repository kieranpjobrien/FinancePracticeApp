# Question

During team formation, a PM discovers that two prospective team members have a history of conflict from a previous project. Both have skills essential to the current project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Choose only one and find an alternative for the other role
- **B**: Meet with both individuals separately to understand the history, then facilitate a conversation about working together with clear expectations and ground rules
- **C**: Ignore the history and hope it resolves itself
- **D**: Assign them to work in completely separate workstreams with no interaction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Past conflict doesn't necessarily predict future behaviour, especially with proper facilitation. Understanding the root cause allows the PM to address it. A structured conversation can establish fresh expectations and working agreements. Many professionals can work together effectively despite past difficulties when given the right support.

Option A loses valuable skills without attempting resolution.

Option C ignores a known risk.

Option D may not be feasible and doesn't resolve the underlying issue.
