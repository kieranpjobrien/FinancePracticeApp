# Question

According to Tuckman's model, what typically characterizes the "storming" stage of team development?

## Options

- **A**: Team members are polite and getting to know each other
- **B**: Team members experience conflict as they push boundaries and compete for position
- **C**: Team works together smoothly with established norms
- **D**: Team achieves peak performance with high autonomy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Storming stage is characterized by conflict and competition as team members push against boundaries established in the Forming stage. Disagreements emerge about work approaches, roles, and leadership. While uncomfortable, this stage is necessary for teams to work through differences and establish authentic working relationships.

Option A describes the Forming stage - initial politeness and orientation.

Option C describes the Norming stage - when the team has worked through conflicts and established effective norms.

Option D describes the Performing stage - when the team operates at peak effectiveness.
