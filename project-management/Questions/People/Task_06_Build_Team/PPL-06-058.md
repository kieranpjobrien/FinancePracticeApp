# Question

Your Scrum team has three developers who also hold senior technical leadership roles outside the project. In sprints, they routinely override Product Owner priorities and redirect other developers without consulting the PO. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Recognise the three technical leads as de facto co-product-owners so their priority calls are legitimised and the team has clearer direction than it currently has.
- **B**: Hold a working session with the PO, the three leads, and the team to clarify Scrum roles, decision rights, and the boundary between technical influence and product priority.
- **C**: Replace the current Product Owner with one of the three technical leads who is already making effective priority calls on behalf of the wider team in practice.
- **D**: Escalate the issue to the steering committee as a governance breach and request formal sanctions against the three leads for overstepping their defined roles.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and Scrum both expect role boundaries to be clarified through facilitated team conversation, not through role inflation, replacement, or escalation. (B) addresses the confusion in the room where it sits. (A) dilutes PO accountability and confuses the team further. (C) replaces the PO with someone whose role is fundamentally different. (D) escalates a team dynamics issue to governance before the PM has attempted facilitation.
