# Question

In sprint planning, two team members consistently dominate every discussion while three others rarely contribute, even on stories closest to their expertise. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to run planning as is since the work is still getting committed.
- **B**: Pull the dominant members aside and ask them to limit their contributions.
- **C**: Move planning to a different format such as round-robin estimation or silent brainstorming, then converge on decisions together.
- **D**: Reduce planning attendance to just the dominant members and a Product Owner.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A Scrum Master is responsible for the effectiveness of events and for psychological safety. (C) changes the structure rather than the people, which lifts the quality of input. (A) accepts a clear dysfunction. (B) singles the dominant members out and addresses the symptom. (D) shrinks the team's collective intelligence and undermines shared ownership.
