# Question

During sprint planning, a Scrum Master notices that two team members consistently dominate discussions while others remain silent. According to team-building best practices, what should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept this as natural team dynamics and continue as usual
- **B**: Use facilitation techniques like round-robin or silent brainstorming to ensure all voices are heard
- **C**: Privately tell the dominant members to speak less
- **D**: Remove the quieter members from future planning sessions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective facilitation ensures psychological safety and equal participation. Techniques like round-robin (each person speaks in turn), silent brainstorming (write ideas first), or dot voting help quieter team members contribute. This builds a stronger team by leveraging everyone's insights.

Option A ignores a dysfunction that limits team potential.

Option C addresses symptoms without changing the underlying dynamic.

Option D would lose valuable perspectives and punish people for team facilitation failures.
