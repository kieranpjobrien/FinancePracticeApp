# Question

Your team is split across Australia, Japan, and India. You notice that members from higher power-distance cultures rarely challenge ideas raised by senior members in meetings, even when they clearly disagree. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add written input or anonymous channels for feedback and explicitly invite each member's view in meetings, adapting facilitation to the team you have.
- **B**: Brief all team members that PMI norms require them to disagree openly and expect them to adopt the same style.
- **C**: Limit decision-making forums to senior members and brief everyone else through written summaries afterwards.
- **D**: Reorganise so each region has its own decision-making cell and only escalate joint decisions when essential.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's cultural awareness principle is to adapt facilitation to the team rather than expect everyone to conform to one style. (A) preserves diverse perspectives without forcing public disagreement. (B) imposes a culture and is likely to be ignored. (C) shrinks the decision pool and reinforces the imbalance. (D) fragments the team where the goal is integration.
