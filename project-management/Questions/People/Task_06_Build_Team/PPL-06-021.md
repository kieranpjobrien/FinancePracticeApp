# Question

A project team includes members from three different countries with varying cultural norms around hierarchy and communication. The PM notices that team members from high power-distance cultures rarely challenge ideas from senior team members. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore cultural differences and expect everyone to adapt
- **B**: Create alternative feedback channels (anonymous surveys, written input) and explicitly invite all perspectives during meetings
- **C**: Only assign leadership roles to people from low power-distance cultures
- **D**: Separate the team by cultural background

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cultural intelligence requires adapting team processes to work across different norms. Anonymous or written channels allow people to contribute without challenging hierarchy directly. Explicitly inviting perspectives ("What does everyone think?") gives permission to speak. This leverages cultural diversity rather than suppressing it.

Option A ignores legitimate cultural differences and loses valuable input.

Option C is discriminatory and doesn't solve the underlying issue.

Option D eliminates the benefits of cross-cultural collaboration.
