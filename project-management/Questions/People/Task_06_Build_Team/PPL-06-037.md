# Question

A PM is building a team for a project that will involve significant organisational change. When selecting team members, what additional consideration should guide team composition?

## Options

- **A**: Only technical skills matter for project success
- **B**: Include change champions and influencers from affected areas who can build buy-in and navigate organisational politics
- **C**: Avoid including anyone from the affected business areas
- **D**: Select only external consultants to ensure objectivity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Change projects succeed when affected stakeholders are involved and influential voices advocate for the change. Including respected individuals from impacted areas builds credibility, provides insider knowledge of concerns, and creates channels for communication. These "change champions" are essential for adoption.

Option A ignores the human element critical to change success.

Option C removes essential stakeholder representation.

Option D may lack organisational knowledge and credibility.
