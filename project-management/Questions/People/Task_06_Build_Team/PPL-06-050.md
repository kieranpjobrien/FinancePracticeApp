# Question

A product that started with one eight-person team needs to scale to three teams over the next quarter to meet new demand. Leadership has asked you to design the new structure. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Split the existing team roughly into thirds, assign two rough workstreams per new team, and figure out coordination practices once the new structure is live.
- **B**: Preserve the current team unchanged, make the other two new teams from incoming hires, and keep the three groups working as independently as possible.
- **C**: Consult the existing team, design the split around product areas and skill coverage, seed each new team with experienced members, and establish coordination norms upfront.
- **D**: Recruit three new team leads externally first, have them each propose their own team design, and pick the structure that the leads most broadly agree to adopt.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI scales teams deliberately: product-shaped boundaries, skill coverage in each team, seeded experience, and coordination designed in from the start. (C) reflects this and involves the team. (A) splits without design and defers the coordination decision. (B) isolates new teams from the experienced core and guarantees uneven capability. (D) prioritises unfamiliar leads' preferences over a product- and skills-based structure.
