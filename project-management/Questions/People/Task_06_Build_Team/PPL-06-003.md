# Question

A project manager is forming a team for a complex initiative. One highly skilled candidate has a reputation for brilliant individual work but poor collaboration. Their technical expertise is nearly irreplaceable. Another candidate has strong technical skills (though less exceptional) and excellent collaboration history. The project requires significant teamwork. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Select the brilliant individual since their technical skills are irreplaceable
- **B**: Select the collaborative candidate, recognizing that team dynamics often outweigh individual brilliance for team-based work
- **C**: Select both and hope the collaborative candidate's attitude influences the other
- **D**: Interview the brilliant individual to assess if they can adapt to a collaborative environment

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. Before making assumptions, gather more information. The "reputation" may be outdated, context-dependent, or based on incomplete information. An interview allows direct assessment of their willingness and ability to collaborate. The PM makes a more informed decision with direct input rather than relying solely on secondhand reputation.

Option A prioritizes technical skill over team fit without investigation. For team-based work, poor collaboration can undermine the whole team's performance.

Option B may be the right choice, but it's premature without more information. The PM should verify assumptions before deciding.

Option C creates a risky situation - hoping one person will influence another without addressing the issue directly. This often fails and can poison team dynamics.
