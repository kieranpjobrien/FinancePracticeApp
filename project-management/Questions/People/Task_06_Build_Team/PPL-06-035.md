# Question

You notice that developers, testers, and analysts on your team only collaborate within their own functional groups, even though the team is co-located. Hand-offs are formal and slow. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reseat the co-located team to break up the functional clusters and force more random interaction.
- **B**: Create cross-functional pairing, mixed sub-teams for features, and shared goals requiring all functions to succeed.
- **C**: Mandate that lunch and social activities mix across functional groups inside the team each week.
- **D**: Accept the pattern as a normal working preference and focus on tightening the existing hand-off process.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI builds team cohesion through shared work and goals rather than seating, mandates, or process tightening. (B) creates the interdependence that drives genuine collaboration. (A) treats the symptom and may breed resentment. (C) forces social proximity without changing how work flows. (D) accepts a known performance drag and patches the symptom.
