# Question

A PM notices that team members are forming cliques based on their functional backgrounds (developers socialising only with developers, testers only with testers). What should the PM do to improve team cohesion?

## Options

- **A**: Accept this as natural human behaviour
- **B**: Mandate that people socialise outside their functional groups
- **C**: Create opportunities for cross-functional collaboration through pair work, mixed subteams, team-building activities, and shared goals that require cooperation
- **D**: Reorganise seating to separate functional groups

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Building cohesion requires creating shared experiences and interdependencies. Pair work (developer + tester), mixed feature teams, and collaborative activities create natural connections. Shared goals that require cooperation make cross-functional relationships necessary for success, not just encouraged.

Option A ignores a barrier to team effectiveness.

Option B feels forced and is unlikely to create genuine connections.

Option D may cause resentment without creating positive interactions.
