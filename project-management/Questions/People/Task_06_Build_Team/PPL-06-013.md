# Question

Your agile team has been Performing for a year. The portfolio leadership wants to break the team up and seed members into struggling teams. You believe the disruption will hurt delivery. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Comply with the request and start planning the transitions immediately.
- **B**: Refuse to release any team members and protect the existing team.
- **C**: Bring data on team velocity and the cost of regression, and propose alternatives such as mentoring, communities of practice, or short rotations.
- **D**: Volunteer specific names to leave so leadership can see the team is willing to help.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to advocate for the team and propose alternatives that meet the underlying organisational need. (C) is principled push-back with data and options. (A) capitulates without surfacing the cost. (B) refuses without engaging with the legitimate need. (D) sacrifices the team to look cooperative without exploring better options.
