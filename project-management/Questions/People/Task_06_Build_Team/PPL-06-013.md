# Question

An agile team has achieved a stable, high-performing state. Management decides to "share the wealth" by moving some team members to struggling projects. The PM is concerned about breaking up a working team. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept management's decision without comment
- **B**: Refuse to let any team members leave
- **C**: Present data on team performance and the cost of disruption, propose alternatives like having the team mentor other teams
- **D**: Proactively volunteer team members to leave

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should advocate for their team while proposing alternatives that address management's underlying need. Data on performance and disruption cost makes the case. Alternatives like mentoring, temporary assignments, or sharing practices may achieve the goal of helping other teams without destroying this one.

Option A doesn't advocate for the team or project.

Option B is confrontational and doesn't acknowledge the legitimate organisational need.

Option D undermines the team unnecessarily.
