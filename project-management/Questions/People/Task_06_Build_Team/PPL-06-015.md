# Question

Several contractors on your project are technically strong but are not integrating with the permanent staff. They sit apart in retrospectives, rarely speak in standups, and route questions only through their own lead. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the contractors as a separate delivery pod with their own backlog, ceremonies, and lead for coordination.
- **B**: Deliberately include contractors in team rituals and pairing, and treat them as full members of the team.
- **C**: Accept the dynamic since contractors and employees naturally work in different ways across any project engagement.
- **D**: Replace the contractors with permanent staff to remove the integration problem from the delivery team entirely.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI does not distinguish between employee and contractor when it comes to team building; cohesion is a PM responsibility for everyone delivering the work. (B) addresses the cause directly. (A) formalises the divide and worsens collaboration. (C) accepts a known performance drag. (D) is rarely feasible and discards expertise the project needs.
