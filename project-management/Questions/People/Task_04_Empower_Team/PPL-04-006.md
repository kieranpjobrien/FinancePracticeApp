# Question

An empowered team makes a technical decision that the project manager believes is suboptimal, though not wrong. The team has considered alternatives and chosen based on their assessment. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Override the decision since the PM has more experience
- **B**: Accept the decision, trusting the team's judgment within their domain
- **C**: Require the team to reconsider and present all options for PM approval
- **D**: Document concerns in case the decision causes problems later

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. True empowerment means letting teams make decisions even when you might choose differently. If the decision isn't wrong (just different from what you'd choose), respect the team's judgment. Overriding reasonable decisions destroys empowerment and teaches the team that their authority is illusory.

Option A undermines empowerment. If the PM overrides technical decisions they disagree with, the team isn't actually empowered.

Option C makes the team jump through hoops to justify decisions they're supposedly authorized to make. This is fake empowerment.

Option D is passive-aggressive and sets up "I told you so" later, which damages trust.
