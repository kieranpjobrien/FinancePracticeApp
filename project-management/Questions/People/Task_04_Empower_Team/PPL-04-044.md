# Question

You've been asked by senior management to implement a decision that you believe will negatively impact your team. You have concerns but limited ability to change the decision. How do you maintain team empowerment in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Implement without discussion to avoid undermining management
- **B**: Refuse to implement and encourage team resistance
- **C**: Be transparent about the decision and your perspective, involve the team in how to implement it effectively, and advocate for their concerns upward while supporting the direction
- **D**: Blame management for the decision while implementing it

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Even when decisions come from above, empowerment can exist in how they're implemented. Be transparent: share what you know about why this decision was made and your honest perspective. Involve the team: they may have ideas for effective implementation. Advocate: channel their concerns upward through appropriate channels. Support: once the direction is set, work together to make it as good as possible.

Option A treats the team as pure executors without voice.

Option B is professionally inappropriate and doesn't serve the team long-term.

Option D is toxic and damages trust in leadership generally.
