# Question

Senior leadership has just announced a decision the PM believes will negatively impact the team but has limited ability to change. How can the PM still maintain team empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the decision quietly and avoid discussing it with the team to prevent unrest or delay.
- **B**: Tell the team the PM disagrees and will quietly look for ways to work around the decision.
- **C**: Be transparent about the decision, involve the team in shaping how to implement it, and surface concerns upward.
- **D**: Defer all questions to the leader who made the decision so the PM is not seen as endorsing it.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When the "what" is fixed, the PM can still empower the team on the "how" while honestly carrying their concerns upward, which is what (C) does. (A) treats the team as pure executors and damages trust. (B) sets up insubordination and undermines leadership in front of the team. (D) is a credibility-saving move for the PM that abandons the team's need for context and direction.
