# Question

A team member asks you to make a technical decision that is within their expertise area. They seem to want validation rather than needing your input. How should you respond?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision to show leadership
- **B**: Ask questions to understand their thinking, then empower them to make the decision themselves with your support
- **C**: Refuse to engage with technical matters
- **D**: Defer to senior management for all technical decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When team members have expertise, empower them to use it. Ask questions to help them articulate their reasoning: "What options did you consider? What's your recommendation?" If their thinking is sound, support their decision-making authority: "That sounds right to me - it's your call." This builds confidence and reduces bottlenecks.

Option A creates unnecessary dependency and doesn't leverage their expertise.

Option C is unhelpful and disengaged.

Option D escalates inappropriately and slows decisions.
