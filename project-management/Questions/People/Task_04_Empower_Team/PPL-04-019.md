# Question

What is the primary goal of empowering team members?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce the number of approvals the project manager personally has to sign.
- **B**: Allow the project manager to spend more time on stakeholder engagement.
- **C**: Push accountability down so the PM is not blamed when something goes wrong.
- **D**: Enable team members to take ownership, decide closer to the work, and contribute their best thinking.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Empowerment exists to put decisions and ownership where the work is, which produces faster, better decisions and stronger engagement. (A) and (B) are downstream PM-convenience benefits, not the purpose. (C) misuses empowerment as blame-shifting; accountability for project outcomes still rests with the PM.
