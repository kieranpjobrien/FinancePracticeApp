# Question

Three business stakeholders on your data platform programme each email the team daily asking for slightly different data extracts. The team is spending roughly 20% of capacity on these requests. The stakeholders say they "don't feel trusted" when the team declines. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invest in self-service data access for the three stakeholders — documented catalogue, simple query tooling, training — so they can empower themselves rather than routing through the team.
- **B**: Continue to service every request the stakeholders make because declining any of them would damage stakeholder relationships and undermine the team's reputation across the programme.
- **C**: Appoint a single stakeholder liaison on the team whose job is to handle all incoming data requests so the rest of the engineers are shielded from the 20% capacity drain entirely.
- **D**: Ask the stakeholders to consolidate their requests into a single weekly batch so that the team can handle them more efficiently while still responding to every business request.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI empowerment pattern for stakeholders: give them the tools and access to help themselves, which respects their autonomy and frees the team. "Not trusted" is often code for "waiting too long". (B) normalises a capacity drain and never addresses the underlying dependency. (C) trades engineering capacity for liaison capacity without changing the number of requests. (D) batches the work but leaves the stakeholders waiting longer and still dependent on the team for every data need.
