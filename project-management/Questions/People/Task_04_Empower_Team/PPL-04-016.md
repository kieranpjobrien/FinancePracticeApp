# Question

A team member has been empowered to lead a workstream. They're doing well but constantly seek approval from the PM before making decisions. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Provide approval each time since they're asking
- **B**: Discuss the pattern, reinforce their authority, and encourage them to make decisions within their boundaries without checking first
- **C**: Revoke their leadership since they're not ready
- **D**: Make the decisions for them to save time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This behaviour suggests the team member hasn't fully internalised their authority - possibly due to past experiences, lack of confidence, or unclear boundaries. The PM should explicitly reinforce that they have authority to decide, express confidence in their judgment, and encourage autonomy. The transition from seeking approval to making decisions often requires active encouragement.

Option A enables dependency rather than building confidence.

Option C punishes natural transition behaviour.

Option D undermines the empowerment entirely.
