# Question

Two stakeholder groups have conflicting requirements. You could make the decision yourself, but you want to empower them to resolve it. Both groups expect you to side with them. How should you facilitate empowered resolution?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision yourself to avoid stakeholder conflict
- **B**: Tell them to work it out and come back with an agreement
- **C**: Facilitate a structured discussion where both groups present their needs, explore trade-offs together, and guide them toward a decision they own
- **D**: Escalate to executive leadership to decide

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment in conflict requires facilitation, not abdication. Create structure: both groups present their needs and constraints. Guide exploration of trade-offs: what are the real priorities? Are there creative solutions? Help them reach a decision they own rather than one imposed on them. Your role is facilitator, not decision-maker.

Option A doesn't empower stakeholders and may create resentment toward you.

Option B is abdication - they need facilitation support.

Option D escalates when the issue could be resolved at this level with proper facilitation.
