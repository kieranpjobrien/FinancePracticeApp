# Question

Two stakeholder groups have presented incompatible requirements for the same feature. Both expect the PM to side with them. The PM wants the groups themselves to own the resolution. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pick the requirement that better aligns with the business case and inform both groups.
- **B**: Ask each group to send their must-haves and decide later based on which is shorter.
- **C**: Tell the groups to meet between themselves and bring back an agreed answer.
- **D**: Run a structured workshop where both groups present their needs, the trade-offs are surfaced, and they converge on a decision they jointly own.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Empowering stakeholders to resolve conflict means facilitating, not choosing for them or abandoning them, which is what (D) does. (A) takes the decision the PM was trying not to make. (B) is a procedural shortcut and does not generate alignment. (C) leaves them without facilitation, which is the very reason the conflict has not yet resolved.
