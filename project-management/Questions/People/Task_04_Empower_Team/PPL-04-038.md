# Question

Which of the following is most often the PM's own internal barrier to empowering the team?

## Options

- **A**: A reluctance to give up control of decisions for which the PM is held accountable.
- **B**: A lack of formal training programmes available for team members.
- **C**: An organisational policy that prohibits decision delegation.
- **D**: Limited budget to invest in team development activities.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM's own discomfort with delegating decisions while remaining accountable for outcomes is the most common internal barrier, which is what (A) describes. (B), (C), and (D) are external constraints; they may need to be navigated, but they are not the PM's own mindset issue, which is what the question is asking about.
