# Question

A product owner is frustrated because the development team frequently says "no" to feature requests, citing technical complexity. The product owner feels their authority is being undermined. How should the project manager address this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Support the product owner's authority by directing the team to implement requested features
- **B**: Support the development team's technical judgment by backing their decisions
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion to help both parties understand each other's constraints and find solutions that address business needs within technical realities
- **D**: Establish a formal escalation process for disputed decisions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The conflict stems from different perspectives (business vs. technical) and possibly poor communication. Rather than taking sides, facilitate mutual understanding. The product owner needs to understand technical constraints; the team needs to understand business drivers. With shared understanding, they can collaborate on solutions that work for both.

Option A overrides technical judgment, which can lead to poor decisions and team resentment. The PM shouldn't use authority to resolve what should be a collaborative discussion.

Option B sides with the team without addressing the product owner's legitimate concerns about delivering business value. This also doesn't build the collaborative relationship needed.

Option D adds process but doesn't improve the underlying dynamic. Escalation processes are for resolving disputes, not building collaboration.
