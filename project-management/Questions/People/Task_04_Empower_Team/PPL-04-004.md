# Question

The product owner complains that the development team keeps refusing feature requests on technical grounds and feels her authority is being undermined. The team says her requests routinely ignore architectural constraints. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reinforce the product owner's authority and ask the team to implement requests unless they can prove it is impossible.
- **B**: Defend the team's right to reject any work that compromises the architecture or long-term maintainability.
- **C**: Facilitate a session where both sides surface constraints and agree how trade-offs will be made together.
- **D**: Define a formal escalation path so disputed feature requests can be resolved at the steering committee level.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This is a collaboration breakdown, not a power dispute, and the PM's role is to restore shared decision-making, which is what (C) does. (A) overrides legitimate technical judgment and damages trust. (B) sides with the team and leaves the product owner without a path to deliver business value. (D) creates a heavy escalation route for what should be a routine product-engineering conversation, and routine reliance on escalation is a signal of dysfunction, not a fix.
