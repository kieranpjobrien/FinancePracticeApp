# Question

Your end-customer — a large hospital network — has asked to review every user story before it is built on your clinical portal programme. Your product owner is experienced and fully customer-aligned. The team is frustrated by the slowdown. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Co-design a tiered engagement model with the customer — light-touch for most stories, deeper review for high-risk or high-value ones — so their voice is heard where it matters most.
- **B**: Require the customer to review every story as they have requested because customer empowerment on a clinical programme is always more important than engineering velocity overall.
- **C**: Decline the customer review request and reassure them the product owner already represents their interests faithfully on every story that enters the team's backlog.
- **D**: Have the product owner forward every story to the customer for review after the sprint has started so that team flow is preserved while the customer still sees the work in progress.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI stakeholder-empowerment pattern: segment engagement by risk and value, give the customer real voice where it matters, and preserve the product owner's role and team flow where it does not. (B) accepts the request without tailoring and disempowers the product owner. (C) dismisses a legitimate customer interest and undermines the relationship on a clinical programme. (D) creates review theatre after the fact, which is not empowerment and produces worse outcomes for both the customer and the team.
