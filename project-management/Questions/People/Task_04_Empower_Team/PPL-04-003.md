# Question

An organization is transitioning to agile, but middle managers continue to assign work directly to team members, bypassing the product owner and sprint planning. Team members feel caught between following manager directives and respecting the agile process. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell team members to ignore manager directives and follow only the product backlog
- **B**: Escalate immediately to senior leadership to enforce agile practices
- **C**: Work with middle managers to help them understand the new model and find appropriate ways for them to provide input through the product owner
- **D**: Accept that the organization isn't ready for agile and return to traditional project management

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This addresses the root cause - managers aren't clear on their role in the new model. They likely have legitimate needs (getting work done, meeting their own commitments) but are using old patterns. Helping them understand how to work through the product owner channel gives them a path to influence while respecting agile practices. This is change management - meeting people where they are.

Option A puts team members in an impossible position and creates conflict. It doesn't address the managers' underlying needs or help them adapt.

Option B may be necessary eventually but is premature. First, try to solve through collaboration and education. Escalation before engagement appears adversarial.

Option D gives up on organizational change due to expected resistance. Transformation challenges are normal - they require persistence and change management, not abandonment.
