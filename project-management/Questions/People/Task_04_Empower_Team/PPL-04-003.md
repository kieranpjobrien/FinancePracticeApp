# Question

A company is six months into an agile transformation. Two functional managers continue to assign work directly to engineers on a Scrum team, bypassing the product owner and sprint planning. Engineers feel caught between sprint commitments and manager directives. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the engineers to decline any work that did not come through sprint planning.
- **B**: Raise the behaviour with the transformation sponsor and ask for an organisation-wide directive.
- **C**: Meet with the two managers, understand the underlying needs they are trying to satisfy, and agree how they will channel work through the product owner.
- **D**: Add a policy to the team's working agreement that only the product owner may add work to the sprint.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The managers are using old patterns to meet legitimate needs and the right first move is to engage them directly, surface what they are trying to achieve, and route it through the proper channel, which is what (C) does. (A) puts engineers in the middle of an organisational conflict that is not theirs to resolve. (B) escalates before the PM has tried to influence at the working level. (D) writes a rule that the engineers cannot enforce against their own functional managers.
