# Question

What does effective delegation of authority require from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Step back completely so the team learns by doing without interference.
- **B**: Schedule frequent check-ins so the PM can spot issues early.
- **C**: Provide detailed instructions so the team executes the PM's intent precisely.
- **D**: Define clear decision boundaries, provide support, and remain accountable for outcomes.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Delegation works when authority is bounded, supported, and paired with retained accountability at the PM level, which is what (D) describes. (A) is abandonment, not delegation. (B) describes oversight cadence, not the conditions that make delegation work in the first place. (C) is micromanagement disguised as delegation.
