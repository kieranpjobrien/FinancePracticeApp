# Question

Your team is selecting a message-broker vendor for your trading platform. The team has done the evaluation thoroughly and wants to choose Kafka. Procurement has quietly signalled the CIO prefers another option for strategic vendor reasons you were not briefed on. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team to pick the CIO's preferred option so that procurement is satisfied and the strategic vendor decision is aligned with senior leadership's unstated preferences and concerns.
- **B**: Have the team present their Kafka recommendation to the CIO directly so that they can defend their evaluation and the CIO can overrule them publicly if he chooses to do so.
- **C**: Surface the strategic context back to the team transparently, ask them to refresh their evaluation against it, and let them take the decision with the full picture now visible.
- **D**: Make the vendor decision yourself as PM based on the combined technical and strategic factors so that the team does not get caught between their recommendation and the CIO's preference.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI empowerment-with-context pattern: empowerment requires giving the team the full information, not asking them to decide in the dark. Often the refreshed evaluation still reaches a defensible answer. (A) overrides their evaluation without explaining why and tells them their work was wasted. (B) sets up an ambush where the team will likely lose and feel publicly burned. (D) takes back the decision entirely and demonstrates that team authority evaporates under senior pressure.
