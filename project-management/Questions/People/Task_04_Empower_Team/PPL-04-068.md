# Question

You maintain the project risk register yourself on a nine-month energy-sector rebuild and update it before steering meetings. The team rarely looks at it; when you raise a risk, people treat it as "your worry." Several emerging risks have come from the team informally but never reach the register. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the risk register as a PM-owned artefact since risk management is a core PM responsibility and the team's informal signals can be captured into it by you as they arise.
- **B**: Require each engineer to submit a risk entry every sprint so that the team is forced to engage with the register as part of their regular working rhythm on the programme.
- **C**: Ask the team lead to take over ownership of the risk register so that you are freed up for other programme responsibilities and the register stays within the team's visibility.
- **D**: Make risk a standing team agenda item, co-own the register with the team, rotate who curates it, and use team-sourced signals as a primary input each sprint.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI living-risk-register pattern: shared ownership keeps the register real, surfaces emerging risks early, and builds risk thinking across the team. (A) keeps the PM as the sole owner and entrenches the "your worry" dynamic the team already has. (B) forces output without changing the relationship to risk and produces compliance rather than insight. (C) transfers ownership without designing how the team will engage collectively, so the same dynamic recurs with a different nominal owner.
