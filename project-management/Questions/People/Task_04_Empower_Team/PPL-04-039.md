# Question

A team member is empowered to represent the team in cross-functional meetings. You learn they've been agreeing to commitments the team cannot meet. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Revoke their cross-functional representation role
- **B**: Apologise to other teams and honour all commitments regardless of feasibility
- **C**: Discuss the gap between commitments and capacity, coach on setting realistic expectations, and work together to manage the current situation
- **D**: Let the team fail to meet commitments as a learning experience

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a coaching opportunity, not a reason to revoke empowerment. Discuss: help them understand the gap between what they committed and what's achievable. Coach: how to check with the team before committing, how to express constraints, how to say "I'll confirm with the team." Address current commitments: work together on how to renegotiate or deliver.

Option A removes empowerment rather than developing capability.

Option B creates unsustainable situations and doesn't address the root cause.

Option D creates unnecessary damage to team reputation and cross-functional relationships.
