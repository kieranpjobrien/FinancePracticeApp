# Question

A team has been empowered to manage their daily work but keeps escalating minor decisions to the PM for approval. The PM suspects they lack confidence rather than authority. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decisions quickly since it's faster
- **B**: Push decisions back to the team with coaching questions that help them decide themselves
- **C**: Remove the escalation option entirely
- **D**: Reduce the team's empowerment to match their comfort level

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Building confidence requires practice making decisions. When teams escalate unnecessarily, push back with coaching questions: "What options are you considering?" "What would you recommend?" "What's the worst case if you decide wrong?" This develops decision-making muscle while maintaining support. Over time, escalations decrease.

Option A perpetuates the dependency.

Option C removes needed safety net abruptly.

Option D reduces empowerment rather than building capability.
