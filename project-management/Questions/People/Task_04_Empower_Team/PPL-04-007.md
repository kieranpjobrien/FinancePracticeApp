# Question

A team has been given authority to select their own tools and technologies. They choose an emerging framework that the organization hasn't used before. IT governance raises concerns about support and security. How should the project manager handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Override the team's choice to satisfy IT governance
- **B**: Support the team's choice unconditionally since they were empowered to decide
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion between the team and IT governance to understand concerns, evaluate risks together, and find an acceptable path forward
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to decide between team empowerment and governance requirements

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment operates within organizational context. IT governance has legitimate concerns (support, security). Facilitation brings both parties together to understand each other's perspectives, evaluate actual risks, and find solutions - perhaps the framework can be used with specific safeguards, or alternative choices address both innovation and governance.

Option A immediately overrides empowerment without exploring solutions.

Option B ignores legitimate organizational concerns that could create real problems.

Option D escalates what could be resolved through facilitated dialogue.
