# Question

A senior contractor on the team consistently defers technical calls to permanent staff in stand-ups and design discussions, prefacing answers with "I'm just a contractor." How should the PM empower her contribution?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a one-on-one to confirm the scope includes technical leadership and ask her to make calls.
- **B**: Wait until the contract is converted to a permanent role before pushing for stronger technical participation.
- **C**: Reassign technical calls in her area to a permanent engineer to remove the awkwardness for her entirely.
- **D**: Raise the deferral pattern in the team retrospective and ask the team to invite her in more often.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The first move is to address the misperception directly with the contractor and reset what is expected of her, which is what (A) does. (B) ties empowerment to employment status, which is the very assumption that needs to be broken. (C) confirms her self-narrative and removes contribution opportunities. (D) makes a one-on-one issue a team-level discussion before the PM has talked to her.
