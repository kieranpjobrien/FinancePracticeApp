# Question

A highly skilled contractor on your team keeps deferring decisions to full-time employees, saying "I'm just a contractor." How can you empower them to contribute fully?

## Options

- **A**: Accept their limited participation since they're not permanent
- **B**: Clarify that their contributions are valued equally, explicitly invite their input and decision-making participation, and recognise their contributions publicly
- **C**: Convert them to full-time employment
- **D**: Assign them only execution tasks since they don't want decision responsibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Contractors may feel like second-class team members. Counter this explicitly: their expertise is why they're here. Invite participation: specifically ask for their input in discussions. Empower decision-making in their domain. Recognise contributions publicly. This creates an inclusive environment where all team members, regardless of employment status, feel empowered.

Option A accepts reduced contribution when more is possible.

Option C may not be feasible and doesn't address the immediate inclusion issue.

Option D reinforces the second-class perception.
