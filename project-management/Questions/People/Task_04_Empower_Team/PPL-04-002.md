# Question

What is a key characteristic of empowered teams?

## Options

- **A**: They require detailed direction from the project manager for all decisions
- **B**: They have authority to make decisions within their domain and are accountable for results
- **C**: They work independently without any interaction with stakeholders
- **D**: They are free from any organizational constraints or guidelines

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Empowered teams have decision-making authority within defined boundaries and are accountable for the outcomes of their decisions. This combination of authority and accountability is essential - empowerment without accountability can lead to poor decisions, while accountability without authority is frustrating and ineffective.

Option A describes the opposite of empowerment - teams that need constant direction are dependent, not empowered.

Option C confuses empowerment with isolation. Empowered teams still collaborate with stakeholders - they have authority to make decisions in that collaboration, not to avoid it.

Option D describes chaos, not empowerment. Empowered teams operate within organizational context and constraints - they have decision authority within appropriate boundaries, not unlimited freedom.
