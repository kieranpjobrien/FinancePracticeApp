# Question

In a self-organising agile team, you notice one member is dominating decisions while others have become passive. How should you address this to maintain balanced empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: Do nothing since the team is self-organising
- **B**: Remove the dominant member from the team
- **C**: Facilitate discussion about balanced participation, coach the dominant member on inclusive behaviour, and create structures that ensure diverse voices are heard
- **D**: Assign the dominant member to lead formally

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Self-organisation doesn't mean no facilitation. When power becomes imbalanced, the Scrum Master or PM should intervene. Facilitate team discussion about participation patterns. Coach the dominant member on stepping back to let others contribute. Create structures (rotating facilitation, explicit solicitation of quiet members) that distribute voice.

Option A allows dysfunction to persist under the guise of self-organisation.

Option B is a drastic response to a coachable behaviour.

Option D formalises the imbalance rather than correcting it.
