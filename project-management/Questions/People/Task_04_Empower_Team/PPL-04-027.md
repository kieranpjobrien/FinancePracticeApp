# Question

The PM delegated authority for vendor management to a team member. He has now signed off on contract terms within his delegated authority that the PM personally would not have approved. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Honour the agreement, then debrief privately on the trade-offs and decide whether the delegation boundaries need to change.
- **B**: Reopen the negotiation with the vendor and renegotiate the unfavourable terms.
- **C**: Withdraw vendor management authority from the team member to prevent further unfavourable agreements.
- **D**: Share the PM's concerns with the team in a stand-up so others learn from the example.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM should honour decisions made within delegated authority and use the situation to learn together, which is what (A) does. (B) overrides the agreement and signals that the team member's authority is provisional. (C) revokes empowerment after a single decision the PM disagrees with rather than addressing the boundaries. (D) turns a private coaching moment into a public correction and damages trust.
