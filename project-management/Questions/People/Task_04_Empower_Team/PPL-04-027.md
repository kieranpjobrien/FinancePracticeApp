# Question

You've empowered a team member to manage a vendor relationship. You later learn they agreed to terms you would not have approved, though the terms are within their delegated authority. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Revoke the vendor management authority
- **B**: Override their decision and renegotiate the terms
- **C**: Honour the decision since it was within their authority, then discuss lessons learned and whether authority boundaries need adjustment
- **D**: Criticise them publicly to establish that you're still in charge

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment means accepting decisions made within delegated authority, even ones you might have made differently. Overriding undermines empowerment and trust. Instead, honour the decision. Later, discuss privately: what factors did they consider? Share your perspective for future decisions. Adjust boundaries only if genuinely needed.

Option A punishes them for using the authority you granted.

Option B destroys trust and teaches that empowerment isn't real.

Option D is destructive to both the individual and team dynamics.
