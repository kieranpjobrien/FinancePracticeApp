# Question

At last month's retrospective, your 12-person claims processing team agreed three process improvements. Four weeks later, nothing has been implemented because "no one has time." The team has stopped raising improvements at subsequent retrospectives. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Take ownership of the three improvements yourself and implement them so that the team can see that ideas do lead to action and the improvement loop is restored quickly.
- **B**: Drop formal retrospectives for a quarter and revisit improvement conversations once the team is less pressured on delivery so ideas are not raised in vain during busy cycles.
- **C**: Add the improvements to the sponsor's programme report so that external pressure pushes the team to implement what they agreed rather than letting delivery pressure continue to crowd them out.
- **D**: Work with the team at the next retrospective to name the pattern, cut the number of improvements to one well-owned experiment, and protect explicit sprint capacity for its implementation.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI improvement-empowerment pattern: name the pattern honestly, reduce ambition, assign clear ownership, and protect capacity — improvements fail to land when they have no home. (A) lets the PM own improvements that should be the team's and teaches them that improvement is not their work. (B) abandons the inspect-and-adapt loop rather than fixing it and loses the signal entirely. (C) weaponises improvements through external pressure, which may force delivery but kills the intrinsic motivation that keeps improvement ideas coming.
