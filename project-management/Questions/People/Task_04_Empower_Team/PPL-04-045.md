# Question

A team member suggests changing a process that has been in place for years. Others are resistant because "that's how we've always done it." How should you empower productive change while respecting experience?

## Options

- **A**: Shut down the suggestion to maintain team harmony
- **B**: Force the change over objections since new ideas should be tried
- **C**: Facilitate open discussion of the suggestion, explore concerns from experienced members, consider a pilot or experiment, and let the team reach a collaborative decision
- **D**: Let the experienced members veto any changes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment means all voices are heard - both the innovator and the experienced resistors. Facilitate discussion: what's the rationale for change? What concerns do experienced members have? Often "how we've always done it" has good reasons. Consider experiments: try the new approach in limited scope to gather data. Let the team decide collaboratively rather than imposing either continuity or change.

Option A suppresses potentially valuable innovation.

Option B dismisses legitimate experience and concerns.

Option D gives veto power based on tenure rather than merit of ideas.
