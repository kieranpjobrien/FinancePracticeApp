# Question

A team member proposes changing a long-standing process. Two senior members push back with "we tried something like that years ago and it didn't work." How should the PM enable a productive decision?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the senior members and ask the proposer to drop the idea.
- **B**: Override the senior pushback and run the change as a directive.
- **C**: Hand the decision to the senior members because they have more context.
- **D**: Facilitate a session where the proposer presents the idea, the seniors share what failed last time, and the team agrees a small experiment to test the change.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right move is to harness both the new idea and the historical lesson by running a low-cost experiment together, which is what (D) does. (A) silences innovation. (B) ignores legitimate experience and damages trust. (C) gives veto power based on tenure rather than the merits of the proposal.
