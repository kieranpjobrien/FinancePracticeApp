# Question

A team is distributed across three time zones and the PM is the bottleneck because every meaningful decision waits for her availability. The PM wants to push more decisions down without losing alignment. What is the BEST first step?

## Options

- **A**: Define decision categories with local-decision criteria and a lightweight async record for daily review.
- **B**: Schedule daily 24-hour-rotating decision meetings so each region has equal access to the PM herself.
- **C**: Promote a senior team member in each region to regional decision maker with PM-equivalent authority.
- **D**: Move all material decisions to a single weekly all-hands meeting so everyone hears them at once.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right structure is bounded local decisions backed by an async record, which keeps speed and alignment without recreating the bottleneck, which is what (A) does. (B) keeps the PM in the loop on every decision and just spreads the wait time. (C) creates three new bottlenecks instead of one and risks regional drift. (D) introduces a weekly meeting cadence that is even slower than the current model.
