# Question

You want to empower your distributed team to make local decisions rather than waiting for your approval across time zones. However, you're concerned about consistency and alignment. How should you approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Require all decisions to wait for your approval to ensure consistency
- **B**: Empower local decisions entirely with no coordination mechanism
- **C**: Establish clear decision criteria and boundaries, create lightweight coordination mechanisms (async updates, documented decisions), and trust while verifying through regular reviews
- **D**: Relocate all team members to one time zone

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Distributed empowerment requires structure. Clear criteria: define which decisions can be made locally and what factors to consider. Lightweight coordination: async communication keeps everyone informed without blocking. Trust and verify: regular reviews catch misalignment early without micromanaging. This enables speed while maintaining sufficient consistency.

Option A creates bottlenecks that slow the distributed team significantly.

Option B risks misalignment and inconsistent decisions.

Option D is impractical and doesn't address the empowerment challenge.
