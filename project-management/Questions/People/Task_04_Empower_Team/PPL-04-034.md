# Question

In agile frameworks, what does it mean for a team to be self-organising?

## Options

- **A**: The team works without any management oversight
- **B**: The team decides how to accomplish work and organises themselves to deliver, within given constraints and goals
- **C**: Team members can come and go as they please
- **D**: The team sets their own project goals without stakeholder input

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Self-organising teams have the authority to determine how they will accomplish work - they decide who does what, how tasks are approached, and how they coordinate. This operates within given constraints: the product owner sets priorities, the organisation provides goals, and the team figures out how to deliver.

Option A removes necessary guidance and accountability.

Option C misunderstands self-organisation as lack of commitment.

Option D confuses self-organisation (the how) with goal-setting (the what).
