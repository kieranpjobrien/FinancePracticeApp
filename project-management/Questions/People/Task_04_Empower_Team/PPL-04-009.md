# Question

A PM empowers their team to make technical decisions. A team member makes a decision that later proves wrong, causing a 2-week delay. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the team member's decision-making authority
- **B**: Accept accountability for the outcome while working with the team to learn from the situation
- **C**: Blame the team member in front of stakeholders
- **D**: Take back all decision-making authority from the team

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Empowerment requires accepting that not all decisions will be perfect. The PM remains accountable for project outcomes even when delegating decisions. The correct response is to own the result publicly while privately using it as a learning opportunity. This maintains trust and encourages continued initiative with better judgment.

Option A (remove authority) punishes initiative and discourages future risk-taking.

Option C (public blame) destroys trust and psychological safety.

Option D (take back authority) abandons empowerment over one mistake, creating command-and-control culture.
