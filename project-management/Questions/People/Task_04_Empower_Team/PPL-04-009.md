# Question

A team member made an architectural decision within his delegated authority. It later proved wrong and caused a two-week delay. The sponsor asks the PM in a meeting how this happened. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Take ownership of the outcome, explain the decision was within delegated authority, and outline the lessons.
- **B**: Explain that the team member personally made the decision and that responsibility for the delay lies with him.
- **C**: Acknowledge the delay and propose pulling architectural decisions back to the PM for the rest of the project.
- **D**: Defend the decision as reasonable given the information available at the time and decline to discuss the rework.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM stays accountable for outcomes even when authority is delegated, and (A) owns the result while protecting the team and committing to learning. (B) throws the team member under the bus and destroys empowerment culture. (C) revokes empowerment in front of the sponsor over a single error and is an overcorrection. (D) is defensive and dodges the legitimate question about what the team learned.
