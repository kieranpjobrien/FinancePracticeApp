# Question

You've empowered your team to self-organise their work. One team member is exploiting this freedom - they cherry-pick interesting tasks while leaving difficult work for others. Other team members have noticed but haven't confronted it. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Revoke self-organisation and assign all tasks yourself
- **B**: Wait for the team to resolve it themselves
- **C**: Coach the team on having the difficult conversation, and if needed, address the behaviour with the individual while maintaining the team's overall self-organisation
- **D**: Ignore it since everyone is still getting work done

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Self-organisation requires accountability among team members. First, coach the team on addressing the issue themselves - this builds their capability to manage such situations. If they can't resolve it, address the individual behaviour directly while preserving the team's broader self-organisation. Don't let one person's behaviour undermine empowerment for everyone.

Option A punishes everyone for one person's behaviour.

Option B may allow the situation to fester if the team can't self-correct.

Option D normalises unfair behaviour and breeds resentment.
