# Question

Your organisation has a command-and-control culture, but you want to empower your team. Senior management expects all decisions to be escalated for approval. How should you navigate this tension?

## Options

- **A**: Comply fully with organisational culture and don't empower the team
- **B**: Ignore organisational culture and fully empower the team anyway
- **C**: Find appropriate empowerment opportunities within constraints, advocate upward for changed practices, and protect the team from unnecessary escalation where possible
- **D**: Complain about the culture but take no action

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Navigate the tension pragmatically. Within constraints: identify decisions that don't require escalation and empower those. Protect the team: handle escalations on their behalf when possible. Advocate upward: make the case for empowerment benefits to change the culture over time. This creates positive change without reckless disregard for organisational reality.

Option A accepts constraints without attempting improvement.

Option B creates career risk without addressing root causes.

Option D is passive and accomplishes nothing.
