# Question

A stakeholder wants to be more involved in project decisions but lacks the technical knowledge to contribute meaningfully to detailed discussions. How can you empower their involvement?

## Options

- **A**: Exclude them from decisions since they lack expertise
- **B**: Give them all technical details regardless of relevance
- **C**: Find appropriate ways for them to contribute - clarify business requirements, validate priorities, review user-facing decisions - while keeping technical details with technical experts
- **D**: Have them attend all technical meetings for education

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment means finding appropriate ways for stakeholders to contribute based on their expertise and interest. Non-technical stakeholders can provide valuable input on business requirements, priority validation, user experience, and strategic direction. Match involvement to their strengths rather than excluding or overwhelming them.

Option A loses valuable business perspective and damages the relationship.

Option B overwhelms them with irrelevant detail.

Option D wastes everyone's time without meaningful contribution.
