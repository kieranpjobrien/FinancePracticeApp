# Question

A stakeholder asks the PM to resolve a minor issue that the team could handle. The PM could easily solve it but recognises it's an opportunity for team empowerment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Solve it quickly since it's minor and won't take much time
- **B**: Redirect the stakeholder to the team member who can resolve it, coaching both parties on appropriate channels
- **C**: Ignore the stakeholder's request
- **D**: Escalate to management to establish proper channels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a teaching moment for both the stakeholder and team. Redirecting establishes that the team has authority and capability. Coaching helps the stakeholder understand who to contact in future. This builds team credibility and reduces PM bottleneck for routine issues.

Option A solves the immediate problem but reinforces the PM as single point of contact.

Option C is rude and damages stakeholder relationship.

Option D is overkill for a minor process adjustment.
