# Question

You're transitioning to a new PM role. The outgoing PM was very hands-on and made most decisions personally. The team expects the same approach from you. How should you introduce more empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately stop making decisions and tell the team they're now empowered
- **B**: Continue the previous approach to avoid disrupting the team
- **C**: Gradually shift to empowerment, explaining the change, demonstrating trust, and building the team's decision-making confidence over time
- **D**: Replace team members who prefer the old approach

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cultural change requires thoughtful transition. Explain the shift: why you're moving toward empowerment and what it means for them. Demonstrate trust: start delegating decisions and supporting them. Build confidence: start small, celebrate successes, learn from mistakes together. This gradual approach allows the team to adapt.

Option A creates anxiety and likely failure - the team isn't prepared.

Option B perpetuates learned helplessness and doesn't leverage empowerment benefits.

Option D punishes people for adapting to their previous environment.
