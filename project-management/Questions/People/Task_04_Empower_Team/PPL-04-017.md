# Question

A team made a release decision within delegated authority that introduced a customer-facing bug. Senior stakeholders ask the PM in a town hall who is responsible. How should the PM answer?

## Options

- **A**: Name the team member who made the call so accountability is transparent.
- **B**: Say the decision belonged to the team and that they will own the fix and the lessons.
- **C**: Explain that the PM was busy with other priorities and missed the chance to review the decision.
- **D**: Take public accountability for the outcome and commit to a blameless review with the team.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM stays accountable for outcomes regardless of delegation, and the right public stance is ownership plus a learning commitment, which is what (D) does. (A) destroys empowerment by punishing a single person publicly. (B) sounds like the team is being thrown under the bus and weakens trust. (C) is self-serving and inaccurate; the issue is not whether the PM was busy but how the team learns from it.
