# Question

A team member approaches the project manager seeking approval for a minor process improvement they've identified. The change would affect only their own work and carries minimal risk. What response BEST demonstrates empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change quickly to unblock the team member
- **B**: Tell the team member they have authority to make such decisions themselves and support their ownership of the improvement
- **C**: Ask the team member to document the change and submit it through the change control process
- **D**: Schedule a team meeting to discuss and vote on the proposed improvement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. True empowerment means giving team members authority to make appropriate decisions, not just faster approval from the PM. By explicitly telling them they have this authority, the PM builds their confidence to act independently in the future. This reduces bottlenecks and develops the team member's ownership mentality.

Option A is faster than formal process but still positions the PM as a gatekeeper. The team member learns they need approval rather than learning they have authority.

Option C applies heavy process to a low-risk decision, which discourages initiative. Change control should be reserved for changes with broader impact.

Option D creates unnecessary overhead and delay for a minor improvement. Not every decision needs team consensus - this wastes time and may discourage future initiative.
