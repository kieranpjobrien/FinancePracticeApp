# Question

Your 11-person team feels the product owner is "handing down" the sprint backlog rather than refining it with them. Engineers have good implementation-level suggestions that never make it in. The product owner is protective of the backlog as her artefact. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the backlog with the product owner entirely since it is her artefact and scrum is explicit that prioritisation is the product owner's sole authority on the team.
- **B**: Facilitate a conversation between the product owner and team to co-design the refinement ritual, clarifying what the team influences versus what remains her prioritisation call.
- **C**: Override the product owner's control by asking the team to propose their own sprint backlog next sprint and let her veto items only where there is a genuine business priority conflict.
- **D**: Quietly let the engineers bring their implementation suggestions to you and raise them with the product owner yourself so the dynamic does not become a direct confrontation between parties.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI empowerment-with-role-clarity pattern: refinement is collaborative by design, and co-designing the ritual honours both the product owner's prioritisation authority and the team's implementation influence. (A) misreads Scrum — prioritisation is the product owner's call, but refinement is shared — and blocks legitimate team contribution. (C) inverts the role definition and damages the product owner relationship. (D) uses the PM as a broker between parties who should be speaking directly and embeds the very pattern the team is unhappy about.
