# Question

You're working with a client stakeholder who feels their input is being ignored by the project team. They've escalated their frustration to your manager. How should you empower this stakeholder?

## Options

- **A**: Explain why the team has been ignoring their input
- **B**: Promise to implement all their suggestions going forward
- **C**: Establish regular touchpoints for their input, ensure their concerns reach decision-makers, and close the loop on how their feedback is being addressed
- **D**: Tell your manager to handle the stakeholder relationship

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Stakeholder empowerment means giving them meaningful ways to influence the project. Create structured input opportunities (regular reviews, feedback sessions). Ensure their voice reaches decisions (include their concerns in discussions, even when you can't implement them). Close the loop: explain what was done with their feedback and why. This addresses the root cause of feeling ignored.

Option A is defensive and doesn't address their need for voice.

Option B makes promises you may not be able to keep.

Option D abdicates responsibility for stakeholder management.
