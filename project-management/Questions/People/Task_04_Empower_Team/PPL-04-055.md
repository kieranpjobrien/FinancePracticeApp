# Question

Two weeks ago you empowered your QA lead to choose the team's new test-automation framework. She chose Playwright; the team is now using it. Your CTO has just told you "I would have picked Cypress — can you change it?" The work is already underway. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Change the framework to Cypress as the CTO has requested so that senior leadership preference is honoured and the organisation is aligned on a single strategic tooling choice.
- **B**: Tell the CTO you will review Playwright with the QA lead to compare it against Cypress objectively and let the QA lead decide again with the new CTO preference factored in.
- **C**: Stand behind the QA lead's delegated decision, explain the rationale and cost of undoing it, and work with the CTO on future vendor standardisation rather than reversing this specific call.
- **D**: Escalate the framework question to a joint committee of QA and platform so that the decision is owned collectively and the CTO feels the choice has had appropriate architectural scrutiny.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI empowerment-integrity pattern: once a decision is delegated, you back the decision-maker unless there is a genuine overriding reason — personal preference is not one. (A) overturns her decision on preference alone and tells the whole team that delegated authority is conditional on senior approval. (B) dresses the reversal up as a review but has the same effect on trust as a reversal. (D) escalates to a committee to avoid defending the delegation, which undermines future empowerment across the team.
