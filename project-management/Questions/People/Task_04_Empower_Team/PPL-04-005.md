# Question

A team member makes a decision within their authority that results in a minor setback. A senior stakeholder criticizes the decision publicly and suggests the PM should have been consulted first. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the stakeholder and implement closer oversight of team decisions
- **B**: Support the team member publicly, acknowledge the setback as a learning opportunity, and address the stakeholder's concerns privately
- **C**: Let the team member defend their own decision to learn from the experience
- **D**: Explain that the decision was within acceptable risk parameters and no changes are needed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM should publicly support empowerment principles - team members who make decisions within their authority should be backed, even when outcomes aren't perfect. Publicly undermining them destroys empowerment culture. The setback becomes a learning opportunity, not a reason to revoke authority. Addressing the stakeholder's concerns privately maintains the relationship while protecting the team member.

Option A abandons empowerment based on one setback. If teams can only make risk-free decisions, they aren't empowered. Some mistakes are the cost of moving faster and developing people.

Option C abandons the team member under pressure. While they should learn, the PM shouldn't leave them to face senior stakeholder criticism alone. Leaders protect their teams.

Option D is defensive without acknowledging the legitimate concern about the setback. The PM should acknowledge what happened while defending the principle of empowerment.
