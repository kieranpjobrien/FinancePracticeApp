# Question

A team member made a vendor selection within his delegated authority that turned out to be a poor fit, costing about a week of rework. In the steering committee, a senior stakeholder criticises the choice publicly and says the PM should have signed off first. How should the PM respond in the meeting?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the stakeholder and commit to approving all future vendor selections personally.
- **B**: Invite the team member to the next steering committee to explain the decision himself.
- **C**: Explain that the team member acted within his authority and that no further action is required.
- **D**: Defend the team member's authority, acknowledge the rework as a learning opportunity, and offer to follow up with the stakeholder one-on-one.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM should publicly back delegated authority while taking accountability for the outcome, and (D) does both without picking a fight in the meeting. (A) revokes empowerment in response to a single setback and trains the team to seek approval for everything. (B) puts the team member on the spot in front of senior stakeholders, which is the opposite of leaders protecting their teams. (C) is correct on the facts but reads as defensive and ignores the legitimate concern about the rework.
