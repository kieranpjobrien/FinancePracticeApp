# Question

A team member made an empowered decision that turned out wrong and caused a week of rework. Other team members are now visibly hesitant to make their own decisions. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Lead a blameless review of the decision and re-emphasise that mistakes within delegated authority are expected and supported.
- **B**: Have a private conversation with the team member about what went wrong before doing anything else.
- **C**: Tighten the decision boundaries so future decisions need PM sign-off until confidence returns.
- **D**: Move on without comment so the incident is not amplified.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team is watching how the PM responds, and the right first move is a visible, blameless treatment that protects empowerment for everyone, which is what (A) does. (B) is necessary but insufficient because it does not address what the rest of the team is now feeling. (C) shrinks empowerment in response to a single failure and confirms the team's fear. (D) leaves the chilling effect in place by saying nothing.
