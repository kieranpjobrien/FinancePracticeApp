# Question

A team member made an empowered decision that turned out to be wrong, resulting in rework. Other team members are now reluctant to make decisions themselves. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Revoke decision-making authority from the team
- **B**: Publicly criticise the team member who made the wrong decision as a lesson
- **C**: Treat it as a learning opportunity - discuss what was learned, reinforce that mistakes are part of growth, and encourage continued empowered decision-making
- **D**: Ignore the situation and hope confidence returns

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. How you respond to empowered decisions gone wrong determines whether the team continues taking ownership. Treat it as learning: "What did we learn? How do we improve next time?" Reinforce that mistakes within reasonable risk-taking are acceptable. Don't punish. This maintains psychological safety for continued empowerment.

Option A sends the message that empowerment was a test they failed.

Option B destroys psychological safety and ensures no one takes risks.

Option D leaves the team in a state of uncertainty and fear.
