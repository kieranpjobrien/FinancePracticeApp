# Question

Your 9-person Scrum team has been stable for a year with fixed roles: one tech lead, one deployment owner, one on-call rotation owner. The rest of the team has expressed interest in those responsibilities but the incumbents are comfortable and delivering well. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the roles as they are because the team is delivering well and disrupting stable specialisations may create unnecessary risk across ongoing platform delivery work.
- **B**: Reassign all three roles at the start of next quarter so that the rest of the team gets direct experience and the incumbents move on to other responsibilities on the team.
- **C**: Have the incumbents informally mentor others so that the wider team gradually picks up the skills without disturbing the current role assignments or the team's delivery momentum.
- **D**: Design a co-owner or rotation model with the team — shadowing, paired ownership, then full handover — so new responsibilities are grown deliberately while delivery remains steady.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI empowerment-through-growth pattern: structured rotation and shadowing builds capability without abandoning continuity — deliberate, not random or invisible. (A) optimises for near-term stability and quietly limits growth for most of the team. (B) rotates everyone at once, which loses continuity and punishes incumbents who have not done anything wrong. (C) relies on informal mentoring without explicit design and rarely produces actual skill transfer under delivery pressure.
