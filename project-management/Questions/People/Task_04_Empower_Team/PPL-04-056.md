# Question

On your aerospace certification programme, the compliance subject-matter expert is universally respected technically but routinely waits for you to tell her what position to take in meetings with regulators. Her hesitation is starting to slow regulator interactions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to prepare positions for her since she is evidently more comfortable executing than authoring and the regulator interactions need to keep progressing against the certification timeline.
- **B**: Script each regulator meeting in advance together so that she has clear lines to use and her hesitation is reduced at least during the high-stakes interactions on this programme.
- **C**: Assign a second compliance specialist to attend regulator meetings with her so that she has a peer to lean on and is less likely to freeze on technical questions during sessions.
- **D**: Explicitly confirm she is the technical decision-maker with regulators, coach her on asserting her positions, and attend with her to model backing her publicly in the early meetings.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI empowerment-plus-coaching pattern: name the authority, coach the confidence, and visibly back her so both she and the regulator see the authority as real. (A) reinforces the dependency pattern exactly at the moment the PM should be breaking it. (B) substitutes a script for authority and treats the symptom. (C) adds a second person rather than supporting the one who already holds the role, which can feel like a lack of confidence in her.
