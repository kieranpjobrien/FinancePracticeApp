# Question

You want to delegate more responsibility to your team, but they seem hesitant to take on decision-making authority. What approach should you take?

## Options

- **A**: Force them to make decisions by refusing to answer questions
- **B**: Continue making all decisions yourself since they're reluctant
- **C**: Start with small, low-risk decisions, provide clear boundaries and support, celebrate successes, and gradually increase scope as confidence builds
- **D**: Replace team members with more confident individuals

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Empowerment is built progressively. Start small: delegate decisions with limited consequences where they can succeed. Provide clear boundaries: what decisions can they make autonomously? Offer support during early attempts. Celebrate successes to build confidence. Gradually expand scope as they demonstrate capability.

Option A creates anxiety rather than empowerment.

Option B perpetuates dependency and limits team growth.

Option D doesn't address the underlying development opportunity.
