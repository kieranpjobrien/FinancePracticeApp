# Question

A Scrum team has overcommitted and missed its sprint goal three sprints in a row. Stakeholder confidence is dropping. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Take over sprint planning to ensure realistic commitments are made.
- **B**: Run a deep retrospective focused only on estimation accuracy.
- **C**: Announce to stakeholders that the team will commit to less work while it stabilises.
- **D**: Coach the team to use historical velocity and a defined buffer in planning while keeping the commitment decision with them.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right move is to build the team's planning capability while keeping commitment authority with them, which is what (D) does. (A) revokes empowerment to fix a capability gap and trains the team to wait for the Scrum Master. (B) is useful but only addresses one facet (estimation) when the issue is overall commitment discipline. (C) is a stakeholder communication move but does not address why the team keeps overcommitting.
