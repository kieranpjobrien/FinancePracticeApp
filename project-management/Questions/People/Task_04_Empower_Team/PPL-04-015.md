# Question

A team has been empowered to manage their own sprint commitments. For three consecutive sprints, they've overcommitted and failed to deliver what they promised. Stakeholder confidence is eroding. How should the Scrum Master respond?

## Options

- **A**: Take back control of sprint planning to ensure realistic commitments
- **B**: Coach the team on better estimation techniques and forecasting while maintaining their authority to commit
- **C**: Blame the team publicly to restore stakeholder confidence
- **D**: Continue without intervention as learning takes time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The team needs coaching, not rescuing. Help them understand why estimates are off (optimism bias, not accounting for interruptions, story complexity). Teach better techniques (using historical velocity, breaking down stories, including slack). Maintain their authority while building their capability - this develops sustainable self-management.

Option A undermines empowerment when what's needed is capability development.

Option C destroys team confidence and trust.

Option D ignores a pattern that's causing real stakeholder damage.
