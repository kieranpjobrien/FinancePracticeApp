# Question

A self-organising agile team makes a tooling decision that the project manager would not have made. The choice is within the team's authority and is defensible, though the PM thinks another option would have been stronger. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the decision to stand and ensure the outcomes are reflected in the next retrospective.
- **B**: Add the decision to the project's risk register so the PM's concern is on the record.
- **C**: Ask the team to redo the analysis and brief the PM before any work begins.
- **D**: Bring the decision to the technical advisory group to confirm whether the team's choice should hold.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empowerment requires the PM to accept reasonable decisions they would not personally make, and (A) does that while keeping a feedback loop. (B) uses the risk register as a passive-aggressive way to second-guess the team. (C) is fake empowerment because the team must justify themselves to keep authority they were already granted. (D) routes around the team to a forum that effectively overrides them.
