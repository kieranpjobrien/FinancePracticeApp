# Question

A self-organising agile team makes a decision that the PM disagrees with but that falls within their authority. The PM thinks it's not the best approach but acknowledges it could work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Override the decision since the PM has more experience
- **B**: Allow the decision to stand, monitor outcomes, and use results as a learning opportunity regardless of outcome
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor to reverse the decision
- **D**: Publicly express disagreement to distance themselves from potential failure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Empowerment means accepting that the team may make decisions differently than the PM would. If the decision is within their authority and isn't clearly wrong, the PM should let it stand. Success validates the team's judgment; failure becomes a learning opportunity. Either way, the team develops decision-making capability.

Option A undermines empowerment and team development.

Option C violates the trust and authority granted to the team.

Option D is politically motivated and damages team trust.
