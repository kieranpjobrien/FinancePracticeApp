# Question

On a public-sector grants portal, your three junior analysts never speak in design sessions while your three senior analysts dominate. The juniors have fresh ideas in 1-1s that never make it into the room. The portal launches in three months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the seniors to hold back more in design sessions so that the juniors naturally have more space to contribute their ideas during group discussions going forward.
- **B**: Restructure design sessions — written proposals ahead of time, silent reading, then discussion — so every analyst contributes ideas on equal footing regardless of seniority or confidence.
- **C**: Pair each junior with a senior advocate in design sessions so that their ideas can be raised through the senior's voice and land more easily in the current group dynamic.
- **D**: Accept that seniors typically drive early-stage design and that juniors will grow into the role gradually as they gain more experience over the coming project work cycles.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) changes the system — written-first then discuss — which is the proven empowerment pattern for mixed-seniority groups. Ideas land on merit, not volume. (A) asks seniors to self-restrain without changing the structural reason juniors do not speak. (C) uses seniors as intermediaries, which trains juniors to route ideas through others rather than owning their voice. (D) rationalises the current pattern and loses three months of junior contribution on a launch that would benefit from every perspective.
