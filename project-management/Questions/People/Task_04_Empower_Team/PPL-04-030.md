# Question

What is the key difference between delegation and empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: Delegation hands off a task; empowerment hands off decision authority within agreed boundaries.
- **B**: Delegation is generally permanent by design while empowerment is only ever a temporary arrangement.
- **C**: Delegation is a tool for line managers while empowerment is reserved for executive leaders.
- **D**: They are essentially the same concept and the two terms can be used interchangeably.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Delegation is about handing off a task or responsibility; empowerment goes further by handing off the authority to decide how to achieve it, which is what (A) captures. (B) reverses typical practice and is incorrect. (C) ties the concepts to organisational level rather than to authority. (D) collapses an important distinction.
