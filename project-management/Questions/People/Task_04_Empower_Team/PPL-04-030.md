# Question

What is the difference between delegation and empowerment?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: Delegation assigns tasks; empowerment grants authority and autonomy to make decisions about how work is done
- **C**: Delegation is for managers; empowerment is for executives
- **D**: Delegation is permanent; empowerment is temporary

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Delegation involves assigning specific tasks or responsibilities. Empowerment goes further - it grants authority to make decisions, choose approaches, and act autonomously within defined boundaries. You can delegate tasks without empowering (telling someone exactly how to do it), or you can delegate with empowerment (giving them authority over how to accomplish the goal).

Option A misses the distinction between task assignment and authority granting.

Option C relates to organisational level rather than the concepts themselves.

Option D reverses typical patterns - delegation is often task-specific while empowerment is ongoing.
