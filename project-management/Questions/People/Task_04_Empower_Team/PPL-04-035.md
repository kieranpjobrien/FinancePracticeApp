# Question

A developer proposes using a new open-source library that is not on the organisation's approved list. She believes it will materially speed up a non-critical internal tool. How should the PM handle this empowerment opportunity?

## Options

- **A**: Tell her the library is non-standard and direct her to use an approved alternative.
- **B**: Approve the use within this internal tool only, based on a short review of risks and benefits, with a time-boxed evaluation after two sprints.
- **C**: Let her use the library and monitor outcomes rather than spending time on governance.
- **D**: Route the proposal to the architecture review board and wait for their decision before acting.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The right move is bounded experimentation with clear review points, which is what (B) does and which balances innovation with appropriate risk management. (A) shuts down the idea without evaluating it. (C) skips governance entirely, which is not responsible use of empowerment. (D) uses the heaviest possible governance route for a low-risk internal tool and delays learning.
