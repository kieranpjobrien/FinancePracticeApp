# Question

An agile team's velocity has declined over the past three sprints despite no changes in team composition. Team members report feeling overwhelmed and meetings have become tense. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce the sprint commitment to a more achievable level
- **B**: Facilitate a focused retrospective to understand the root causes of the decline
- **C**: Report the velocity decline to management and request additional resources
- **D**: Implement overtime to catch up on missed commitments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Before taking corrective action, you need to understand the root cause. A focused retrospective gives the team opportunity to identify what's changed - it could be technical debt, unclear requirements, process issues, external interruptions, or team dynamics. The solution should match the actual problem.

Option A might help symptoms but doesn't address root causes. If the problem is technical debt or interruptions, lower commitment just extends the pain without fixing anything.

Option C is premature - escalating without understanding the cause doesn't give management actionable information and may reflect poorly on the team. First understand, then act.

Option D is counterproductive. Overtime typically reduces productivity and morale over time, and the team already reports feeling overwhelmed. This would make the situation worse.
