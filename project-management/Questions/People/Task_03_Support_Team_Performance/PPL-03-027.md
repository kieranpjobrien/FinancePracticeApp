# Question

What is the main purpose of a daily standup in Scrum?

## Options

- **A**: For the Scrum Master to assign tasks
- **B**: To provide a brief synchronisation point for the team to coordinate work and identify blockers
- **C**: To give status reports to management
- **D**: To update project documentation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The daily standup (or daily scrum) is a brief synchronisation meeting for the development team. Team members share what they did, what they plan to do, and any blockers. It's about team coordination, not status reporting to management. The Scrum Master facilitates but doesn't assign work.

Option A misunderstands the Scrum Master role - the team self-organises.

Option C treats standups as management reporting, which undermines their purpose.

Option D confuses synchronisation with documentation activities.
