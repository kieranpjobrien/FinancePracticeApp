# Question

A single senior engineer owns all knowledge of the fraud-detection service on your banking platform. She is going on four weeks of leave next month. The team has been comfortable letting her handle the service because she "just does it" efficiently. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask her to write comprehensive documentation before she leaves so that the rest of the team can read it as needed when any fraud-detection service issues arise during her absence.
- **B**: Schedule structured pair-programming and shadowing with two other engineers for the next four weeks, rotate responsibility, and agree a maintainable ownership model beyond her leave.
- **C**: Put a senior contractor on standby for her leave period so that there is a fallback if anything serious goes wrong with the fraud-detection service while she is on leave.
- **D**: Freeze all fraud-detection service work during her leave so that only operational issues need to be handled and those can be escalated to her personally if absolutely required.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses the underlying bus-factor problem by building shared ownership through pairing — the PMI capability-resilience pattern. Four weeks is ample for meaningful knowledge transfer. (A) relies on documentation alone, which rarely survives contact with real production issues. (C) rents a fallback without building team capability and recreates the same dependency with a different person. (D) paralyses half the platform and breaches the "do not disturb on leave" principle the moment anything serious happens.
