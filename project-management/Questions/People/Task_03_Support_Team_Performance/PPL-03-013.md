# Question

A Scrum team has been working on a complex feature for four sprints. Progress is steady but slower than the product owner expected. In daily scrums the team reports no blockers, yet the Scrum Master notices low energy and short answers. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the slow progress with the product owner and propose descoping the feature.
- **B**: Add a more experienced developer to the team to accelerate delivery on the feature.
- **C**: Use the next retrospective to surface hidden impediments such as unclear requirements or technical complexity.
- **D**: Run a working agreement workshop to reset team norms around transparency in standups.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

"No blockers but low energy" is a classic signal that impediments exist but are not being voiced. The retrospective is the dedicated forum to inspect how the team is working and create the safety to surface them, so (C) addresses the root cause first. (A) jumps to a scope decision without understanding why delivery is slow. (B) adds a person before diagnosing the problem and risks Brooks's Law. (D) is a reasonable later step, but resetting norms before understanding the actual impediments treats the symptom (silent standups) rather than the cause.
