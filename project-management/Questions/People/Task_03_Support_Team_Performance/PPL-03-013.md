# Question

A team has been working on a complex feature for multiple sprints. Progress is steady but slower than stakeholders expected. The team reports no blockers but confidence seems low. What should the Scrum Master investigate?

## Options

- **A**: Whether the team needs additional training
- **B**: Whether there are hidden impediments the team isn't reporting, such as technical complexity, unclear requirements, or reduced confidence due to external pressure
- **C**: Whether individual team members are underperforming
- **D**: Whether the team should be broken into smaller groups

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "No blockers but low confidence" suggests issues the team may not recognize as blockers or feel comfortable reporting. Hidden impediments might include: technical debt making work harder than expected, accumulated ambiguity in requirements, or pressure creating fear of admitting struggles. The Scrum Master should create safe space to surface these.

Option A assumes skill gaps without evidence.

Option C blames individuals when the team collectively shows the pattern.

Option D addresses structure without understanding the underlying issue.
