# Question

A team's velocity has dropped for three consecutive sprints. Team members report feeling burnt out. What should the Scrum Master do to support team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Push the team harder to recover lost velocity
- **B**: Investigate causes of burnout, reduce work in progress, and protect sustainable pace
- **C**: Add more team members to increase capacity
- **D**: Ignore velocity and focus on feature completion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Declining velocity with burnout signals an unsustainable pace. The Scrum Master should investigate causes (too much WIP, unrealistic commitments, technical debt, external interruptions), reduce load to sustainable levels, and protect the team from pressure to overcommit. Sustainable pace is an agile principle - short-term gains from overwork lead to long-term losses.

Option A worsens burnout and will further reduce performance.

Option C doesn't address the root cause and may slow the team further (Brook's Law).

Option D ignores valuable performance signals.
