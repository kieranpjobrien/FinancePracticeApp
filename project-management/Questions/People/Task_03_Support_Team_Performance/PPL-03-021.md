# Question

A project manager reviews delivery metrics and sees one developer closes tickets fastest but has the highest defect rate, while another is the slowest but produces almost defect-free work. How should the PM use this information?

## Options

- **A**: Share the metrics in the next team meeting to encourage healthy competition on both speed and quality.
- **B**: Raise both individuals' patterns with their functional manager so it can be reflected at performance review time.
- **C**: Adjust ticket assignment so the faster developer takes high-volume work and the slower one takes critical work.
- **D**: Have private one-on-ones with each developer to coach on their weaker dimension while reinforcing their strengths.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Individual delivery metrics are a coaching input, not a public scoreboard, and the PM's role is to develop both people. (D) does that privately and respects each person's strengths. (A) creates public comparison from metrics that are rarely like-for-like and damages trust. (B) outsources a coaching opportunity that the PM should handle directly and turns formative feedback into a summative judgement. (C) reinforces the silo and avoids the development conversation.
