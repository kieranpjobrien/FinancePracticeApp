# Question

A PM receives performance metrics showing one team member completes tickets faster than others but has higher defect rates. Another team member is slower but produces nearly defect-free work. How should the PM use this information to support team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Publicly share the metrics to create healthy competition
- **B**: Use private conversations to understand individual working styles and help each person improve in their weaker area while valuing their strengths
- **C**: Penalise the person with defects and reward the faster one
- **D**: Ignore the metrics and treat everyone the same

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Individual metrics should inform private coaching, not public comparison. The faster developer may benefit from quality-focused practices; the thorough developer may learn efficiency techniques. Both have strengths to leverage and areas to develop. The PM's role is to support individual growth, not rank performance.

Option A creates unhealthy competition and may not compare fairly (different task complexity).

Option C penalises without supporting improvement and values speed over quality.

Option D ignores useful information for supporting development.
