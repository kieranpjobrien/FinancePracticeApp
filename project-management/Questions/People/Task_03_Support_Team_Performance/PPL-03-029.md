# Question

A delivery team has hit every release date for the last quarter, but in retrospectives engineers consistently raise that they are skipping tests and deferring refactoring to do it. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Recognise the team for hitting the dates and revisit the technical debt question after the next major release.
- **B**: Add a quality gate to the definition of done that blocks releases until tests are written.
- **C**: Bring the trend to the product owner and rebalance the next release to include explicit capacity for technical debt and quality.
- **D**: Raise the under-investment in quality with the sponsor as a project risk requiring leadership intervention.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The team is signalling that current pace is only sustainable by borrowing against quality, and the right lever is to rebalance the work plan with the product owner, which is what (C) does. (A) accepts the trade-off and lets the debt grow. (B) imposes a process change unilaterally rather than working the trade-off conversation, and may simply cause missed dates without sponsor alignment. (D) escalates before the PM has worked the issue with the product owner, who controls priorities.
