# Question

The team is meeting all deadlines, but retrospectives reveal they're doing so by cutting corners on quality and accumulating technical debt. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Celebrate the deadline achievement and ignore the quality concerns
- **B**: Work with the team and stakeholders to rebalance priorities, allocate time for technical debt reduction, and establish quality standards that are enforced
- **C**: Let the team continue since deadlines are being met
- **D**: Blame the team for poor decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Meeting deadlines by sacrificing quality is not sustainable performance - it's borrowing from the future. Work with stakeholders to adjust expectations: can some deadlines be extended? Should scope be reduced? Allocate explicit time for debt reduction. Establish quality gates. Sustainable velocity matters more than sprinting into a wall.

Option A ignores accumulating risk that will eventually cause bigger problems.

Option C allows unsustainable practices to continue.

Option D blames the team when they may be responding to unrealistic expectations.
