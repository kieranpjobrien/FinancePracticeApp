# Question

A remote team member is consistently quiet during meetings and rarely contributes ideas. Their individual work is good. How should you support their participation?

## Options

- **A**: Call on them frequently in meetings to force participation
- **B**: Accept that they're not collaborative and don't expect input
- **C**: Use varied participation methods (async input, written submissions, smaller groups), privately explore their comfort level, and create safer opportunities for contribution
- **D**: Question whether remote work is appropriate for them

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Some people are uncomfortable speaking in large group video calls but have valuable contributions. Vary methods: collect ideas in advance via written channels, use breakout rooms, try asynchronous discussion tools. Privately understand their perspective - are they uncomfortable, are there technical issues, or do they simply process differently? Create multiple pathways for input.

Option A may increase anxiety without improving contribution quality.

Option B loses valuable perspectives and accepts reduced collaboration.

Option D blames the individual rather than adapting team practices.
