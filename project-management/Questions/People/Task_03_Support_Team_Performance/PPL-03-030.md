# Question

A remote team member rarely speaks in video meetings but produces strong individual work and contributes thoughtfully in the team chat. The PM wants to support better participation. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Call on the team member by name in each meeting so participation becomes habitual.
- **B**: Add structured async input ahead of meetings and reserve time in meetings for written contributions to be discussed.
- **C**: Have a one-on-one to ask what would make verbal participation easier and tailor meeting practices accordingly.
- **D**: Move the team member's role to focus on individual deliverables that do not require meeting attendance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Before changing meeting design, the PM should understand what the person actually needs, which is what (C) does. (A) can increase anxiety and risks performative participation rather than substantive contribution. (B) is a reasonable change but applies a team-wide adjustment before knowing whether it addresses this person's situation. (D) reshapes the role around the symptom and isolates the team member from collaborative work, which limits both contribution and growth.
