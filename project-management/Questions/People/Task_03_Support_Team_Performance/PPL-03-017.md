# Question

A team member is capable of taking on more responsibility but seems reluctant to step up. In one-on-ones, they express fear of failure and imposter syndrome. How should the PM support this person's performance?

## Options

- **A**: Force them into challenging situations to prove they can handle it
- **B**: Accept their self-assessment and keep them in their comfort zone
- **C**: Provide gradually increasing challenges with coaching support, celebrate wins, and normalise that growth involves discomfort
- **D**: Tell them their fears are unfounded and they should be more confident

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Growth requires stretching beyond comfort zones, but safely. Gradually increasing challenges allow the person to build confidence through small wins. Coaching provides support during the stretch. Normalising discomfort ("it's normal to feel uncertain when learning") reduces the shame around struggle.

Option A creates overwhelming pressure that may confirm their fears.

Option B limits their potential and development.

Option D dismisses valid emotions without providing support.
