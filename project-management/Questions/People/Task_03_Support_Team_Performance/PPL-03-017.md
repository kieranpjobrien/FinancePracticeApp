# Question

A senior analyst is technically capable of leading a complex workstream but in one-on-ones tells the project manager she fears failure and feels like an imposter. The PM needs that workstream led within the next month. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign her the workstream now, on the basis that being thrown in the deep end will build her confidence faster.
- **B**: Reassign the workstream to a more confident team member and revisit her development plan after the project closes.
- **C**: Give her a smaller leadership task first with coaching support, then transition her into the full workstream as confidence grows.
- **D**: Tell her her concerns are unfounded given her track record and confirm her as the workstream lead in the next stand-up.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Stretch assignments work when paired with coaching and graduated risk so the person builds confidence through real wins. (C) does that within the timeline. (A) is sink-or-swim and risks confirming the imposter narrative if she struggles publicly. (B) protects the project short-term but stalls her development and reinforces her self-assessment. (D) dismisses a stated concern rather than addressing it, which damages trust and rarely changes how the person feels.
