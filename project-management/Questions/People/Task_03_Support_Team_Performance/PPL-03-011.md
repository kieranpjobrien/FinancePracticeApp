# Question

A team's burndown chart shows consistent work completion, but they never finish all committed stories by sprint end. Each sprint, 1-2 stories carry over. What should the Scrum Master investigate first?

## Options

- **A**: Whether team members are working hard enough
- **B**: Whether sprint planning is producing accurate estimates and appropriate commitment levels
- **C**: Whether the product owner is adding work mid-sprint
- **D**: Whether individual team members are underperforming

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Consistent patterns (always 1-2 stories over) suggest systematic overcommitment rather than random issues. The first investigation should be the planning process - are estimates accurate? Is the team committing to more than their demonstrated velocity? Adjusting commitment to match actual capacity solves this.

Option A and D blame individuals when the pattern suggests a process issue. If it were individual performance, results would be more variable.

Option C (mid-sprint additions) would show as scope increases during sprint, not consistent overcommitment at start.
