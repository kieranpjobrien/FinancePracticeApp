# Question

Team performance has plateaued and retrospectives have become repetitive - the same issues come up, the same actions are proposed, nothing changes. The team seems resigned to current performance. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop holding retrospectives since they're not working
- **B**: Inject new energy - try different retrospective formats, bring external perspective, focus on one improvement and see it through completely
- **C**: Accept the plateau as the team's natural performance level
- **D**: Replace team members to bring new ideas

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Stale retrospectives need reinvigoration. Try new formats (Timeline, Speedboat, appreciation-focused). Bring external perspective (guest observer, cross-team sharing). Focus deeply - instead of many small actions, commit to one improvement and track it relentlessly until complete. Success with one improvement can restore belief that change is possible.

Option A abandons a valuable practice.

Option C accepts underperformance.

Option D loses team knowledge and cohesion.
