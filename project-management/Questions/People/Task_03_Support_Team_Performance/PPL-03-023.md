# Question

A Scrum team's performance has plateaued and the last four retrospectives have produced the same general actions: improve estimation, write better acceptance criteria, reduce interruptions. Few of the actions are ever finished. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the standard retrospective format with a focused session that picks one improvement and runs it to completion before adding any new actions.
- **B**: Skip the next retrospective and use the time for a team-building activity to reset energy.
- **C**: Bring in an external agile coach to run the next two retrospectives.
- **D**: Share the recurring actions with the sponsor so leadership can support the team's improvement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The retrospective is failing because too many actions are taken on and none are completed, which kills belief that change is possible. (A) restores the improvement loop by limiting WIP on improvements, which is exactly what the team is failing to do on its own work. (B) skips the inspection event entirely and treats engagement as the issue rather than the broken loop. (C) outsources facilitation without changing the underlying pattern. (D) escalates an issue the Scrum Master should address through facilitation first.
