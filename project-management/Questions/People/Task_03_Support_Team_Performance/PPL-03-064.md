# Question

Your engineering team has a 28% rework rate this quarter on user stories shipped. Engineers blame "unclear requirements"; the product owner blames "engineers not asking enough questions." Both are frustrated and delivery output is shrinking. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a stricter Definition of Ready that must be met before any story enters a sprint, on the assumption that requirements clarity is where rework is most frequently originating.
- **B**: Run a focused root-cause session with both engineers and the product owner, inspect a sample of recent reworked stories, and agree where the breakdown is actually happening across the flow.
- **C**: Require engineers to ask a minimum number of clarifying questions during refinement so that the product owner's objection about under-questioning is directly addressed from next sprint onwards.
- **D**: Escalate the rework rate to the programme director so that senior leadership can determine whether the engineering team or the product owner is ultimately more accountable for the pattern.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI root-cause pattern applied to a two-sided blame dynamic: inspect actual samples with both parties present and diagnose the specific breakdown before changing any process. (A) guesses the cause and adds policy, which may miss the real issue and hardens the blame pattern. (C) turns clarifying questions into a quota, which undermines the quality of refinement conversations. (D) escalates for a ruling on who is to blame, which is exactly the wrong frame — the issue is a shared process problem, not a who-is-at-fault question.
