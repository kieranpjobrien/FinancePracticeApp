# Question

A team is struggling with too many meetings that fragment their time and reduce productive work hours. What should the PM do to support better performance?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel all meetings to maximise work time
- **B**: Audit meetings for necessity, consolidate where possible, protect blocks of uninterrupted focus time, and ruthlessly decline or delegate non-essential meetings
- **C**: Add a meeting to discuss the meeting problem
- **D**: Accept that meetings are part of corporate culture

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Meeting overload is a common productivity killer. Audit: which meetings provide value? Which can be consolidated, shortened, or eliminated? Protect focus time: establish "no meeting" blocks for deep work. Model discipline: decline or delegate attendance at meetings where you add little value. Continuously prune the meeting calendar.

Option A eliminates necessary collaboration along with waste.

Option C adds to the problem while trying to solve it.

Option D accepts dysfunction rather than actively managing it.
