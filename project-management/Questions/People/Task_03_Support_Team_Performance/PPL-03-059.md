# Question

Your sponsor wants to award a delivery bonus to the "top three" individuals on your 15-person regulatory programme that shipped early. The team worked closely together and the project succeeded as a whole. You are worried about team dynamics if only three people receive recognition. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Advocate with the sponsor for a team-wide bonus or a combined team-plus-individual structure, using the collaborative nature of the delivery as the basis of the recommendation.
- **B**: Let the sponsor choose the three people she wants to reward, given that it is her budget and her decision, and focus your own energy on managing the team's reaction afterwards.
- **C**: Ask the team to nominate their own "top three" so that peer selection legitimises the awards rather than leaving the decision to the sponsor's possibly incomplete view of the delivery.
- **D**: Accept the sponsor's structure but keep the individual recipients confidential so that the rest of the team never learns who received the bonus and so relationships are protected.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI team-reward principle: when delivery was genuinely collective, reward structure should reflect that, and the PM's role is to surface the evidence and advocate. (B) passes on a leadership conversation the PM should have with the sponsor directly. (C) converts a sponsor decision into a popularity contest among peers and can damage trust if the "top three" outcome feels unfair. (D) relies on confidentiality of financial rewards, which almost always breaks and then multiplies the original problem.
