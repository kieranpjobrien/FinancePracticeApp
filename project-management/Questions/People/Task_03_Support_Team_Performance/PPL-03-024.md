# Question

What is the purpose of a burndown chart in agile projects?

## Options

- **A**: To track individual team member productivity
- **B**: To visualise remaining work over time and track progress toward sprint or release goals
- **C**: To document reasons for project failure
- **D**: To compare team performance against industry benchmarks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A burndown chart shows the amount of work remaining (y-axis) over time (x-axis). It helps the team visualise progress toward sprint or release goals, identify if they're on track, and spot trends early. It's a team tool for transparency and self-management, not individual evaluation.

Option A misuses the chart for individual measurement rather than team progress.

Option C confuses burndown with failure analysis.

Option D is about benchmarking, not progress tracking.
