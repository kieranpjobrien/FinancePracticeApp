# Question

Your 10-person platform team's calendars show 28 hours of recurring meetings per engineer per week. Delivery has slowed, deep-work complaints have risen, and three engineers have mentioned that they write most of their code outside working hours. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel all existing recurring meetings for a month and ask the team to reintroduce only those that prove indispensable through the month-long experiment across the entire team calendar.
- **B**: Ask each engineer to push back individually on the meetings they find least useful so that personal autonomy determines how each calendar is shaped going forward each week.
- **C**: Introduce a "no-meeting Wednesday" across the team so engineers have at least one guaranteed day of deep-work time regardless of how the rest of the calendar evolves over the coming weeks.
- **D**: Run an audit of the 28 recurring hours with the team, classify each meeting's purpose, consolidate overlapping sessions, and agree protected focus blocks everyone will honour.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI performance-support pattern: diagnose specifically, act surgically, and agree a new norm — protect focus time without throwing away the useful synchronous signal. (A) is a blunt reset that creates its own disruption and relies on chaos to produce better design. (B) pushes the system change onto each individual, which underrepresented and junior staff rarely feel safe doing. (C) reserves one day but leaves the other four as they are, meaning most of the meeting load continues to crowd out focus.
