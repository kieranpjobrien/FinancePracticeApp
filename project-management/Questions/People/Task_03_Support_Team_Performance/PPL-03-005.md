# Question

A project manager receives feedback that daily standups have become status reports to management rather than team coordination meetings. Team members read prepared updates rather than collaborating. What change would BEST restore the intended purpose of standups?

## Options

- **A**: Invite management to observe but not participate in standups
- **B**: Remove management from standups and refocus the conversation on team coordination, impediments, and collaboration needs
- **C**: Shorten standups to 5 minutes to prevent lengthy status updates
- **D**: Replace verbal updates with a written status board that management can view

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The root cause is that management presence has changed the meeting's nature - team members perform for their audience rather than coordinate with each other. Removing management restores psychological safety for honest discussion about blockers and challenges. The standup should serve the team, not management's information needs. Management can get status through other mechanisms.

Option A doesn't solve the problem - "observing" management still changes behavior. Team members will still perform rather than collaborate honestly.

Option C (shorter meetings) treats symptoms, not causes. Teams will just compress their status reports. The content and dynamic remain wrong.

Option D (written status) might serve management's needs but eliminates the real-time coordination benefit of standups. It's an acceptable alternative for management information, but the team still needs an effective coordination meeting.
