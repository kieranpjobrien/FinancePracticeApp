# Question

A team member excels at their primary responsibilities but shows no interest in helping teammates or participating in team activities beyond their own work. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept it since their individual work is excellent
- **B**: Have a conversation about team expectations, explain how collaboration benefits everyone including them, and set specific expectations for team contribution
- **C**: Force them to participate in everything
- **D**: Give them poor performance reviews for lack of teamwork

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. High individual performance with low team contribution can limit overall team effectiveness. Have an honest conversation: explain that team success requires collaboration beyond individual tasks. Explore their perspective - are they overloaded? unclear on expectations? Help them see benefits of collaboration (learning, backup when needed, career growth). Set specific, reasonable expectations for team participation.

Option A ignores the team dimension of performance.

Option C may be counterproductive and doesn't address underlying attitudes.

Option D uses formal consequences before having a direct conversation.
