# Question

What is a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)?

## Options

- **A**: A network diagram showing the sequence and dependencies of project activities.
- **B**: A hierarchical, deliverable-oriented decomposition of the total project scope into work packages.
- **C**: A matrix mapping each task to the responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed roles.
- **D**: A time-phased budget showing planned cost over the life of the project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The WBS is a deliverable-oriented hierarchical decomposition of total project scope into work packages, and it is the foundation for scheduling, costing, and assignment. (A) describes a network diagram such as a precedence diagram. (C) describes a RACI chart. (D) describes a cost baseline or S-curve, not the WBS.
