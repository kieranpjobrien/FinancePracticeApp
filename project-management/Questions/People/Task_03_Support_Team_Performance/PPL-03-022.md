# Question

A high performer tells her project manager she wants to develop machine learning skills, which are not required by the current project. The team is fully loaded for the next two sprints. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Explain the project cannot fund non-essential development and revisit the request when project capacity opens up.
- **B**: Approve a standing 20% time allocation for ML learning starting next sprint for her to use.
- **C**: Identify a small adjacent task with an ML angle and connect her with an internal community of practice.
- **D**: Suggest she use her evenings and weekends for the learning so it does not impact project delivery.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Supporting development is part of the PM's role and aids retention, but it has to fit project reality. (C) finds a low-cost way to honour her growth need within the current constraints, which is the PMI mindset of tailoring support to context. (A) shuts the door on a motivated team member and risks attrition. (B) commits unilaterally to capacity the team does not have. (D) offloads professional development entirely to personal time, which the PM has no authority to require.
