# Question

A team member asks for opportunities to develop new skills that aren't directly needed for the current project but would advance their career. Project work is fully loaded. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse since it doesn't benefit the project
- **B**: Find creative ways to support development - perhaps through stretch assignments, side projects at 10%, or connections to other opportunities after the project
- **C**: Promise unlimited development time
- **D**: Suggest they pursue development entirely on their own time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Supporting team member development improves retention and engagement, which benefits the project. Be creative: Can any project work provide a stretch? Can small time allocations allow exploration? Can connections be made for post-project opportunities? Balance project needs with career development to keep talent engaged.

Option A risks losing a motivated team member.

Option C makes promises that may be impossible to keep.

Option D provides no PM support for something that matters to the team member.
