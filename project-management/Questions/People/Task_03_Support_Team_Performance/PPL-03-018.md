# Question

For the third retrospective in a row, a Scrum team identifies "unclear requirements causing rework" as a top issue. Action items from the previous two retros were logged but never completed. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the recurring requirements problem directly to the product owner's functional manager.
- **B**: Drop the requirements topic for one sprint and revisit it only if the rework continues next sprint.
- **C**: Re-add the same action items with the team and assign explicit owners and due dates this time.
- **D**: Investigate why previous actions were not completed and design a single, owned action to remove the barrier.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When the same problem recurs, the real issue is usually that the agreed actions were not actionable, not owned, or were blocked. (D) addresses that by diagnosing the failure of the improvement loop itself before adding more items. (A) escalates over the product owner's head before the Scrum Master has worked the issue at the team level. (B) accepts the recurring rework as cost of business. (C) repeats the same actions in a slightly tighter wrapper without understanding why they failed last time.
