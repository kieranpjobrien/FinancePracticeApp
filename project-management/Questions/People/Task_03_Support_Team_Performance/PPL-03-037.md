# Question

A mature agile team has been performing well but recently seems to be going through the motions in ceremonies without the engagement they once had. Retrospectives produce few insights, standups feel mechanical. How should the PM/Scrum Master support revitalisation?

## Options

- **A**: Skip ceremonies since the team has matured past needing them
- **B**: Add more ceremonies to stimulate engagement
- **C**: Introduce variety into ceremonies, revisit their purpose with the team, consider new challenges or experiments, and check if external factors are affecting engagement
- **D**: Replace team members to inject fresh energy

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Ceremony fatigue is common in mature teams. Refresh approaches: try different retrospective formats, change standup structure, rotate facilitation. Revisit purpose - has the team forgotten why these practices matter? Explore whether external factors (boring work, organisational issues) are the real cause. Consider introducing new technical challenges or experiments to re-engage.

Option A loses valuable practices due to format fatigue.

Option B adds burden without addressing root cause.

Option D is a drastic response to a solvable engagement issue.
