# Question

A formerly engaged Scrum team has started going through the motions: standups are mechanical, retrospectives produce no insights, and the team rarely reflects on the sprint goal. Delivery is on track. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce the cadence of team ceremonies so the team only runs them when they clearly see real value.
- **B**: Raise the disengagement in the next retrospective and ask the team what they would change.
- **C**: Bring in a different facilitator to vary the dynamic in standups and retrospectives for a few sprints.
- **D**: Set a stretch sprint goal that forces the team to engage more deeply with planning and execution.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team is the right group to diagnose its own engagement, and the retrospective exists for exactly that, so (B) is the first move. (A) removes the mechanism that surfaces the issue and trades inspection for convenience. (C) treats facilitation as the cause when the symptoms are broader than the Scrum Master's style. (D) imposes a stretch goal as a stimulant, which can backfire if the disengagement is rooted in workload or alignment problems.
