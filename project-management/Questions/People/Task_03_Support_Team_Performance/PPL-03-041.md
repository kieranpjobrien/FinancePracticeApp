# Question

A team is consistently delivering high story-point throughput, but the last two quarterly NPS surveys show user satisfaction is declining and product analytics show low feature adoption. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Set a higher velocity target so more candidate features get shipped and at least some land with users.
- **B**: Bring the output-vs-outcome gap to the product owner and team, and add user-validated outcome measures to the team's working agreement.
- **C**: Run a usability study on the next major feature and apply its findings before further work continues.
- **D**: Replace the product owner with someone who understands the user base better.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team is optimising for output instead of outcomes, and the right first move is to make outcomes a first-class measure that the team owns alongside the product owner, which is what (B) does. (A) doubles down on the wrong metric. (C) is a useful tactic but treats it as a one-off rather than addressing the systemic measurement problem. (D) blames a single role for an outcome problem the whole delivery system is producing.
