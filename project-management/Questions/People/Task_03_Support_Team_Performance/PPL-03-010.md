# Question

A mature agile team has requested to eliminate sprint reviews, arguing they are unnecessary since stakeholders receive continuous demos throughout the sprint via their collaboration tools. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to eliminate sprint reviews since continuous feedback is more agile
- **B**: Insist on keeping sprint reviews exactly as defined in the Scrum Guide
- **C**: Explore what value sprint reviews provide beyond demos and help the team find a format that serves those needs while respecting their feedback about efficiency
- **D**: Let the team decide since they are self-organizing

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sprint reviews serve purposes beyond just demonstrating work - they provide a forum for stakeholder feedback on product direction, enable adaptation of the backlog based on what was learned, and create a natural cadence for planning discussions. The team may have a point about redundant demos, but the other functions need to be addressed. Help them find an efficient format that serves all needs.

Option A eliminates an important ceremony without ensuring its functions are covered elsewhere. Continuous demos are great but don't fully replace the strategic conversation and formal feedback loop.

Option B rigidly adheres to process without considering context. Mature teams should adapt practices to their needs. Insisting without dialogue doesn't respect the team's experience.

Option D abdicates the PM's responsibility to guide the team. Self-organization doesn't mean the PM provides no input - they should coach and challenge the team's thinking.
