# Question

What is the PRIMARY purpose of velocity tracking in agile projects?

## Options

- **A**: To evaluate individual team member performance
- **B**: To help the team forecast how much work they can complete in future sprints
- **C**: To compare productivity across different teams
- **D**: To report progress to management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Velocity (work completed per sprint) is primarily a planning tool for the team - it helps predict what they can realistically commit to in upcoming sprints. It's the team's own metric for sustainable pace and capacity planning.

Option A misuses velocity - it measures team output, not individuals, and shouldn't be used for performance evaluation.

Option C is inappropriate - velocity depends on team context, estimation conventions, and work type. Cross-team comparisons are invalid and harmful.

Option D is a secondary use. Velocity serves the team's planning needs first.
