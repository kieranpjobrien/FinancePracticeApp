# Question

Your product team depends on a shared platform team for environment provisioning. Provisioning takes three weeks on average and your engineers regularly sit idle. The platform team says your requests are "badly formed" and you are "one of many." What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise a formal escalation to the CIO about the platform team's response times so that executive pressure forces them to prioritise your product team's provisioning requests.
- **B**: Ask each of your engineers to start two or three features in parallel so that at least one of them is always unblocked while the others are in the platform team's queue.
- **C**: Bypass the platform team entirely and have your product team provision its own environments against the organisation's baseline security and operational standards where possible.
- **D**: Meet the platform team lead with provisioning data, understand their constraints, agree a standard request format for your team, and co-design an SLA that works for both sides.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI cross-team performance pattern: data, understanding, shared norms and a co-designed SLA — lifting both teams. The "badly formed" feedback is itself a useful signal. (A) escalates before peer work has been attempted and will harden positions on both sides. (B) drives up WIP to mask the blocker, which kills cycle time and quality. (C) goes around a governance function, which usually breaches security standards and creates worse problems later.
