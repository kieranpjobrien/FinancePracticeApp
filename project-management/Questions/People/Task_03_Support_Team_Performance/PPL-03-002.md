# Question

Which of the following is a PRIMARY purpose of conducting regular retrospectives in agile projects?

## Options

- **A**: To assign blame for sprint failures
- **B**: To document lessons learned for organizational archives
- **C**: To enable continuous improvement of team processes and effectiveness
- **D**: To report team performance metrics to stakeholders

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The primary purpose of retrospectives is continuous improvement - the team reflecting on what worked well, what didn't, and what they can do better. This is the "inspect and adapt" principle applied to the team's process and collaboration, enabling the team to become more effective over time.

Option A is destructive and incorrect. Retrospectives should be blame-free environments focused on improvement, not assigning fault. Blame destroys psychological safety and prevents honest discussion.

Option B (documentation) may be a secondary outcome but isn't the primary purpose. Retrospectives focus on actionable improvements for the immediate team, not organizational record-keeping.

Option D (reporting to stakeholders) confuses retrospectives with status reporting. Retrospectives are internal team events for the team's benefit. Performance reporting uses different mechanisms.
