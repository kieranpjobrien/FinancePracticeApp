# Question

A team member is consistently delivering high-quality work but works in isolation, rarely participating in team discussions or helping colleagues. Their behavior is creating resentment among other team members who value collaboration. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept their working style since their individual output is excellent
- **B**: Have a private conversation to understand their perspective, explain the impact on the team, and explore ways they can contribute to team collaboration
- **C**: Publicly recognize their technical excellence to encourage others to follow their example
- **D**: Pair them with struggling team members to force more interaction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This addresses the situation directly and respectfully. Understanding the team member's perspective is important - they may have valid reasons (introversion, focused work needs) or may not realize their impact. Explaining the effect on team dynamics gives them information to act on. Exploring solutions together respects their autonomy while addressing the team need.

Option A ignores the team dynamic issue. Individual excellence at the expense of team collaboration can harm overall team performance and morale. Team success requires more than individual contributions.

Option C would worsen the resentment - publicly praising someone others see as non-collaborative sends the wrong message about team values.

Option D forces interaction without buy-in and may be uncomfortable for both parties. The team member may comply without genuine engagement, and the struggling colleague may feel imposed upon.
