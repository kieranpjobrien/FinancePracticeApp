# Question

A new team member is ramping up slowly, taking longer than expected to become productive. Other team members are spending significant time helping them. How should the PM support the situation?

## Options

- **A**: Set strict deadlines for the new member to be fully productive
- **B**: Acknowledge that onboarding takes time, structure the support load across the team fairly, provide focused learning opportunities, and check in regularly on progress
- **C**: Have them figure things out on their own to speed up learning
- **D**: Question whether the hiring decision was correct

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Onboarding investment pays dividends in long-term productivity. Acknowledge reality to the team - early investment is normal. Spread the support load so no one is overburdened. Provide structured learning: pair programming, documentation, appropriate starter tasks. Regular check-ins track progress and identify where support is most needed.

Option A creates pressure that may impede learning and damage morale.

Option C abandons the new member and may delay productivity further.

Option D judges prematurely - ramp-up time varies and can improve with better support.
