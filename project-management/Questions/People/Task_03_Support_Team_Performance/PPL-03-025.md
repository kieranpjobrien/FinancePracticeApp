# Question

A team's velocity has decreased over the last three sprints. Team members seem engaged, and no one has left. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Demand the team return to previous velocity
- **B**: Investigate root causes through retrospectives and data analysis - examine what's changed in work complexity, dependencies, or processes
- **C**: Add more people to the team to compensate
- **D**: Accept lower velocity as the new normal without investigation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Velocity changes have causes that should be understood. Use retrospectives to explore team perspectives. Analyse the work: has complexity increased? Are there more interruptions, dependencies, or technical debt? Has the team been pulled into unplanned work? Understanding the cause informs whether intervention is needed or the change reflects legitimate factors.

Option A treats velocity as a target to demand rather than a measure to understand.

Option C may slow the team further and doesn't address root causes.

Option D misses an opportunity to address potentially fixable issues.
