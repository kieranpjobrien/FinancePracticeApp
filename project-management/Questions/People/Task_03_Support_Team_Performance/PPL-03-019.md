# Question

A team member consistently delivers quality work but never shares knowledge with others. When they're absent, the team struggles with their components. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept it as their working style
- **B**: Mandate documentation for all work
- **C**: Discuss the importance of knowledge sharing, identify barriers, and create opportunities for collaborative work like pairing or code reviews
- **D**: Reduce their responsibilities to limit the knowledge silo risk

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Knowledge hoarding creates project risk and limits team capability. The PM should have a conversation to understand barriers (introversion? time pressure? unclear expectations?) and create structures that encourage sharing - pairing sessions, code reviews, lunch-and-learns. Make knowledge sharing a valued and expected part of the role.

Option A accepts an ongoing risk without addressing it.

Option B may get compliance without genuine knowledge transfer.

Option D punishes capability rather than addressing the sharing behaviour.
