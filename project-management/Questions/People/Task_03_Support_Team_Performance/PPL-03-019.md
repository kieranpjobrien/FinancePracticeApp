# Question

A senior engineer consistently delivers high-quality work but rarely shares context with the rest of the team. When she takes leave, two release defects trace back to areas only she understood. How should the project manager address this?

## Options

- **A**: Have a one-on-one to understand what is in the way of knowledge sharing, then introduce pairing and code reviews on her components.
- **B**: Add a documentation deliverable to her objectives and require a wiki page for every component she owns.
- **C**: Reassign her most critical components to other engineers so the silo is broken up.
- **D**: Raise the knowledge concentration as a risk in the risk register and accept it given her performance.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The silo is a behavioural and structural issue, so the PM should first understand the cause and then build collaboration into the way work happens, which is what (A) does. (B) often produces compliance documentation without real knowledge transfer and treats the symptom. (C) punishes a high performer and disrupts delivery without resolving the underlying behaviour. (D) names the risk but takes no action to mitigate it, which is not appropriate when a low-cost mitigation is available.
