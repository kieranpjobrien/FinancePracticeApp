# Question

You inherited a 14-person data-engineering team where the previous PM ran annual 1-1 performance conversations only. Your first signals show people are unsure what's expected, underrepresented members report feeling invisible, and delivery predictability is poor. What should the PM change FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a team-wide off-site to reset expectations and performance norms for the next quarter so that everyone hears the same message at the same time from the new PM.
- **B**: Build detailed written role expectations for every position on the team and share them in a single document so that individuals are clear about what is expected of them going forward.
- **C**: Wait until the annual review cycle comes round in six months so that you can propose changes to the performance approach as part of the organisation's existing performance management process.
- **D**: Replace annual performance conversations with a regular monthly 1-1 for each team member focused on goals, obstacles and feedback, and document the cadence publicly as a team norm.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI continuous-feedback pattern: frequent focused 1-1s build the signal, clarity and relationship the team needs, and it's proven to lift engagement for underrepresented members particularly. (A) concentrates leadership into a single event rather than an ongoing rhythm. (B) documents expectations but ignores the conversation channel where expectations actually land. (C) defers a clearly needed change to fit an existing cycle rather than fixing what the evidence shows is broken.
