# Question

A Scrum team has been using story points for estimation for several months. Management now wants to convert story points to hours for resource planning and asks the project manager to create a conversion formula. The team is concerned this will undermine the purpose of relative estimation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Create the conversion formula as requested since management needs the information for planning
- **B**: Explain to management why story points shouldn't be converted to hours, educate them on using velocity for planning, and help them get the information they need through appropriate metrics
- **C**: Have the team switch to hour-based estimation to meet management's needs
- **D**: Refuse the request and escalate the disagreement to the project sponsor

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a coaching opportunity. Story points measure complexity and relative effort, not time - converting them defeats their purpose and creates false precision. However, management has a legitimate need for planning information. The PM should help management understand how to use velocity (points per sprint) for capacity planning, which gives them useful planning data without corrupting the estimation approach.

Option A gives management what they asked for but not what they need. The conversion creates inaccurate data and pressures the team to estimate in hidden hours, reducing the benefits of relative estimation.

Option C abandons an effective practice to satisfy a request based on misunderstanding. Hour estimation has known problems (anchoring, false precision, pressure). The better solution is education.

Option D is unnecessarily confrontational. The PM should first try to solve the underlying need. Escalation is premature when education might resolve the situation.
