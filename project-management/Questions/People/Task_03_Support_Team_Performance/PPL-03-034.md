# Question

A severity-1 production defect is reported on day 4 of a 10-day sprint. The team has already committed to a full sprint backlog. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate sprint scope with the product owner so the defect is taken in and lower-priority work is dropped or deferred.
- **B**: Cancel the sprint, fix the defect, and re-plan once production is stable.
- **C**: Ask two team members to work overtime on the defect so the sprint commitment is still met.
- **D**: Route the defect to a separate support team so the sprint is not disturbed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The product owner controls priority, and the right move is to renegotiate sprint scope so capacity is preserved while the urgent work is taken in, which is what (A) does. (B) is the heaviest possible response and is reserved for cases where the sprint goal becomes obsolete, not for any urgent defect. (C) preserves both the commitment and the defect by overloading the team and breaks sustainable pace. (D) avoids the responsibility instead of integrating production support into the team's normal flow and is rarely the right first move.
