# Question

During a sprint, a critical defect is discovered in production that requires immediate attention. The team is already committed to sprint work. How should the PM support the team in handling this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the defect until the sprint ends
- **B**: Work with the product owner to adjust sprint scope to accommodate the defect fix while protecting sustainable pace
- **C**: Demand the team work extra hours to do both
- **D**: Assign the defect to someone outside the sprint

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Production defects often legitimately interrupt sprints. Work with the PO to prioritise: is this defect more important than committed sprint items? Adjust scope accordingly - remove or defer lower-priority work to create capacity for the defect. Maintain sustainable pace by treating the sprint as a container of fixed capacity, not a commitment plus emergencies.

Option A ignores legitimate production needs.

Option C overloads the team and normalises unsustainable expectations.

Option D may not be practical and doesn't use the team's knowledge of the codebase.
