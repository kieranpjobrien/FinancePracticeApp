# Question

Your quarterly team engagement survey for a 40-person education platform programme has dropped ten points in three months. The specific dimensions that fell hardest are "I can raise concerns safely" and "decisions are explained." The sponsor wants a one-page action plan next week. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Write a comprehensive action plan covering every dimension of the survey and present it to the sponsor next week to demonstrate decisive leadership of the team's engagement issues.
- **B**: Wait for the next survey cycle before acting so that you can confirm whether the drop is a genuine trend or a one-off data point reacting to a recent specific programme event.
- **C**: Run small focused listening sessions across the programme to understand the specific stories behind the two falling dimensions before designing any action plan with the sponsor.
- **D**: Focus the action plan on the two lowest dimensions by announcing new decision-making rhythms and an anonymous concerns channel so the sponsor sees immediate visible response.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is PMI diagnose-before-treat: engagement surveys point to themes, but designing actions without hearing the specific stories produces generic fixes that often miss the real problem. (A) creates a plan on limited data and risks solving the wrong issues under time pressure. (B) delays action on a legitimate signal for no good reason and leaves people feeling unheard. (D) jumps to plausible interventions before listening, which is the quickest way to announce change that lands as performative rather than genuine.
