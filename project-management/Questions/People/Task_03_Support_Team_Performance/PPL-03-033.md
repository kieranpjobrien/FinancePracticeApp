# Question

The team has become dependent on one technical expert who is a single point of failure. Others defer to them on all technical decisions and haven't developed equivalent skills. How should the PM address this risk while supporting team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the dependency since the expert is reliable
- **B**: Remove the expert from the team to force others to learn
- **C**: Implement knowledge sharing through pairing, documentation, cross-training rotations, and gradually distribute technical decision-making responsibilities
- **D**: Hire another expert so there are two single points of failure

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Single points of failure create project risk and limit team growth. Address gradually: pair programming spreads knowledge, documentation captures expertise, rotation through technical responsibilities builds capability. Deliberately include others in technical decisions. This builds team resilience while respecting the expert's contributions.

Option A accepts ongoing risk and stunts team development.

Option B punishes the expert and creates immediate capability gaps.

Option D doesn't address the team's skill development need.
