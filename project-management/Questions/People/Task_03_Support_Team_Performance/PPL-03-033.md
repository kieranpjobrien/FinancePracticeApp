# Question

The team's lead architect has become a bottleneck: every technical decision routes through her, and she is increasingly the only person who understands several core components. She is reliable and willing, but she is also a single point of failure. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the architect to step back from day-to-day technical decisions to force the rest of the team to grow into the gap.
- **B**: Add the key-person dependency to the risk register and accept it given her proven reliability and willingness.
- **C**: Recruit a second senior architect from the market so the current bottleneck is shared across two people.
- **D**: Work with the architect to introduce pairing and a plan to redistribute ownership over the next two sprints.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM needs to mitigate the key-person risk while keeping the architect engaged, which is what (D) does through gradual knowledge transfer and decision delegation that she helps design. (A) creates an immediate capability gap and signals that her contribution is the problem. (B) names the risk but does nothing to mitigate when low-cost mitigations are available. (C) is expensive, slow, and may simply create two bottlenecks rather than a more distributed team.
