# Question

Two months into a 12-month cloud-native rebuild, you have realised that four of eight engineers lack the Kubernetes experience the architecture demands. Delivery is already behind. Your sponsor wants more bodies; your architect wants to replace the four engineers. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the four engineers with more experienced contractors as the architect suggests, since the delivery timeline cannot absorb the ramp-up cost of a large training programme now.
- **B**: Add two senior Kubernetes contractors to accelerate delivery and leave the existing four engineers to pick up the skills gradually through their normal day-to-day exposure on the project.
- **C**: Add one senior Kubernetes contractor as a mentor, invest in focused training for the four engineers, and rebaseline delivery realistically with the sponsor to reflect the ramp-up.
- **D**: Escalate the sponsor's "more bodies" proposal and argue for a larger contractor injection, since the delivery pressure is too high to spend any time on internal capability building right now.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) balances short-term delivery, long-term capability, and honest replanning — the PMI way to handle a skill-gap crisis without abandoning the people involved. (A) throws the four engineers out rather than developing them and loses organisational knowledge in a twelve-month rebuild. (B) adds bodies but leaves skill transfer to chance, which rarely happens under delivery pressure. (D) doubles down on staffing at the expense of the team you already have and the genuine learning the long programme can absorb.
