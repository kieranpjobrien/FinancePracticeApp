# Question

Team members are reluctant to estimate work, fearing they'll be held to their estimates as commitments. This is causing delays in planning. How should the PM support better estimation practices?

## Options

- **A**: Make estimates binding commitments to ensure accountability
- **B**: Educate the team that estimates are forecasts not commitments, create psychological safety around estimation uncertainty, and use ranges rather than single-point estimates
- **C**: Have the PM provide all estimates to remove team burden
- **D**: Stop estimating altogether

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Fear of commitment often stems from past punishment for estimate misses. Reframe estimates as forecasts used for planning, not promises. Create safety: don't punish misses, instead use variance to improve future estimates. Use ranges or confidence levels to acknowledge uncertainty. This improves estimate quality over time.

Option A reinforces the fear that causes poor estimation behaviour.

Option C removes team ownership and typically produces worse estimates.

Option D loses valuable planning information.
