# Question

A team has become reluctant to give estimates after a previous PM treated them as fixed commitments and held people to them publicly. Refinement sessions are now stalling. How should the new project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Reframe estimates explicitly as forecasts, agree no one will be held to them publicly, and use ranges or t-shirt sizes to acknowledge uncertainty.
- **B**: Run estimation only at the team level so individuals are not exposed to scrutiny.
- **C**: Have the PM provide first-pass estimates so the team only has to react, not commit.
- **D**: Move to a no-estimates approach and rely on throughput and cycle time instead.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The fear is the cause of the stall, and the PM has to address it directly by changing how estimates are used and communicated, which is what (A) does. (B) hides individual exposure but does not change the underlying social contract about how estimates are treated. (C) takes ownership away from the team and will produce poorer estimates. (D) is a legitimate model but is a bigger change than the situation calls for and does not address the trust issue with the team.
