# Question

Two high-performing team members have very different working styles - one prefers detailed planning, the other prefers improvisation. Both approaches have merit, but they clash when working together. How should the PM support team performance in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Force them to adopt the same style
- **B**: Always keep them working separately
- **C**: Help them understand each other's strengths, find complementary ways to work together, and use their different approaches strategically based on task needs
- **D**: Let them figure it out without intervention

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Diverse working styles can be a strength when leveraged appropriately. Help them appreciate what each approach brings: planning provides structure and risk mitigation; improvisation enables adaptability and innovation. Find complementary arrangements: perhaps one does initial design, the other handles emerging issues. Match approach to task needs. This turns conflict into capability.

Option A loses the benefits of cognitive diversity.

Option B prevents knowledge sharing and collaboration opportunities.

Option D leaves a resolvable tension to fester.
