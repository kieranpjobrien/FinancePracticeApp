# Question

Two senior engineers on the same team work very differently: one wants detailed plans before coding, the other prefers to spike and iterate. Both deliver well alone but argue every time they pair, and the rest of the team is starting to take sides. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pair them on a low-risk story together so they can resolve the working-style difference themselves.
- **B**: Reorganise work so they only collaborate through pull requests and avoid direct pairing.
- **C**: Facilitate a conversation with both to surface the underlying working-style difference and agree how they will collaborate when paired.
- **D**: Take the issue to their functional manager so a formal mediation can be set up.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM's role here is conflict facilitation, not avoidance or escalation. (C) addresses the root issue by making the implicit working styles explicit and brokering an agreement, which is consistent with PMI's emphasis on direct, respectful conflict resolution. (A) hopes the conflict resolves itself, which it has not so far. (B) avoids the conflict by separating them, but the team is already taking sides, so the underlying issue continues. (D) escalates a conflict the PM can and should handle at their level first.
