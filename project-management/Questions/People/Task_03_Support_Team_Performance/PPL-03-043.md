# Question

What is the primary purpose of WIP limits in Kanban?

## Options

- **A**: Cap the number of items the product owner can place on the backlog at any time.
- **B**: Constrain the amount of work in progress at each stage so flow improves and bottlenecks become visible.
- **C**: Prevent any individual team member from working on more than two items at once.
- **D**: Limit the number of stories that can be released in a single deployment window.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

WIP limits exist to expose bottlenecks and improve end-to-end flow by stopping the team from starting more work than it can finish. (A) is about backlog policy, which is separate from WIP. (C) is sometimes used as a personal heuristic but is not the purpose of column-level WIP limits in Kanban. (D) is a release management constraint, not a flow constraint within the system.
