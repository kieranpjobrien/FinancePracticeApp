# Question

A high-performing agile team has been together for two years. Recently, performance has plateaued despite no obvious problems. The team seems comfortable but not challenged. How should the PM support continued growth?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the team alone since they're performing well
- **B**: Break up the team to prevent complacency
- **C**: Introduce new challenges - stretch goals, learning opportunities, or rotation to new problem domains
- **D**: Add more work to fill any slack time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Plateaued performance in experienced teams often indicates they've mastered their current challenges. To continue growing, they need new stimulation - harder problems, new technologies to learn, different domains to explore. This maintains engagement and develops capabilities without disrupting what works.

Option A accepts stagnation when growth is possible.

Option B destroys valuable team cohesion unnecessarily.

Option D adds volume without challenge - more of the same doesn't promote growth.
