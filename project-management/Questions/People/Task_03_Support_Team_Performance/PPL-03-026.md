# Question

A team member consistently delivers work late, affecting team throughput. You've spoken with them once before without improvement. What should be your next step?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it since you've already addressed it once
- **B**: Have a more specific conversation exploring root causes, set clear expectations with measurable targets, offer support, and establish consequences for continued issues
- **C**: Immediately escalate to HR for disciplinary action
- **D**: Reassign their work to others to compensate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Performance management is progressive. The first conversation may not have been specific enough. Now: explore root causes (skill gap? overcommitment? personal issues?), set SMART targets, offer concrete support (training, workload adjustment, pairing), and clarify what happens if improvement doesn't occur. Document the conversation. This is fair and gives a genuine chance to improve.

Option A allows performance issues to persist and affect the team.

Option C skips progressive steps that might resolve the issue.

Option D doesn't address the underlying problem and burdens others.
