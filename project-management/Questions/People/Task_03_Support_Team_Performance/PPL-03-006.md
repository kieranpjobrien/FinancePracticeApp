# Question

What is psychological safety in the context of team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Physical security measures for the team workspace
- **B**: An environment where team members feel safe to take risks, speak up, and make mistakes without fear of punishment
- **C**: Mental health support services provided by the organization
- **D**: Protection of team members' personal information

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Psychological safety refers to the belief that the team environment is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. Team members feel comfortable speaking up with ideas, questions, concerns, or admitting mistakes without fear of embarrassment, rejection, or punishment. Research shows psychological safety is crucial for team learning and performance.

Option A refers to physical/workplace safety, which is different from psychological safety.

Option C describes employee assistance programs or mental health benefits - important but distinct from the team dynamic concept of psychological safety.

Option D refers to privacy and data protection, not the interpersonal team dynamic.
