# Question

A project is implementing a SaaS solution. The vendor is acquired by a larger company that announces plans to sunset the product in two years. The project is six months from completion. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Complete the implementation since two years of use justifies the investment
- **B**: Cancel the project immediately
- **C**: Assess the situation - confirm sunset timeline, evaluate migration paths, calculate total cost including future migration, and present options to stakeholders
- **D**: Request the vendor guarantee continued support

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Vendor acquisition and product sunset significantly changes the long-term picture. The PM should gather facts (is the sunset confirmed? what's the migration path?), analyze total cost of ownership (including future migration), and help stakeholders decide whether short-term value justifies the investment and future switching costs.

Option A accepts a two-year lifespan without analyzing whether that's adequate value.

Option B abandons the investment without analyzing whether short-term value might still be worthwhile.

Option D is unlikely to succeed and doesn't address the strategic question.
