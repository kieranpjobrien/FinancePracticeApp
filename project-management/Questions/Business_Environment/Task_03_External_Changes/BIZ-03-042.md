# Question

A severe weather event has shut down your construction site for an indeterminate period. The contract includes a force majeure clause. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a force majeure notice in line with the contract and begin impact assessment with the contractor.
- **B**: Continue billing for site costs and wait for the site to reopen.
- **C**: Cancel the contract under force majeure to recover unspent funds.
- **D**: Wait for the contractor to invoke force majeure on their side.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Force majeure clauses typically have notice requirements; the PM follows the contract and gathers the impact data (A). (B) ignores contract obligations and accumulates undue cost. (C) is disproportionate and may not be permitted. (D) allows the clock on notice to run and weakens the contractual position.
