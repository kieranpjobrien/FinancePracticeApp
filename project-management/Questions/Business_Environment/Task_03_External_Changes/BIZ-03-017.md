# Question

The organisation is shifting from global sourcing to regional suppliers in response to ongoing shipping disruption. Your supply-chain optimisation project was designed around the old strategy. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Halt the project until the new sourcing strategy is fully stable.
- **B**: Trim scope to features that work for both global and regional models.
- **C**: Complete current design as-is and add regional support in a follow-on phase.
- **D**: Engage stakeholders to understand the new strategy, assess requirement and design impact, and propose how the project should adapt.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A strategic shift drives a requirements shift (D); the PM facilitates the re-alignment with stakeholders. (A) stops without trying. (B) hedges without understanding feasibility. (C) ships a solution for a strategy the business has just abandoned.
