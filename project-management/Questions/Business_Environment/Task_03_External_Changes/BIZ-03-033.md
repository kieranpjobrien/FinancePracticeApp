# Question

A new data localisation regulation will require your customer data to be hosted in-country before your planned launch. The current architecture uses a single offshore region. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Submit an updated business case and change request reflecting the architecture, cost, and schedule impact.
- **B**: Launch on the original architecture and migrate to in-country hosting after launch.
- **C**: Cancel the project because the new regulation invalidates the architecture.
- **D**: Continue as planned and request an exemption from the regulator.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM responds to a binding external change by formally updating governance artefacts and routing through change control (A). (B) launches in known non-compliance. (C) is disproportionate to a solvable problem. (D) gambles on an exemption that may not be granted.
