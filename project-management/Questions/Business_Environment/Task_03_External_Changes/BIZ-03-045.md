# Question

The central bank has raised interest rates significantly. Your project is funded through a revolving credit facility tied to that rate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Recalculate financing cost, update the business case, and engage finance on alternatives.
- **B**: Continue as planned and let finance worry about the rate change on their own.
- **C**: Pause the project until interest rates fall to a more manageable level.
- **D**: Switch to internal funding sources without any analysis of the trade-offs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A rate change directly affects project economics; the PM updates the financial picture and explores alternatives with finance (A). (B) treats financing as someone else's problem. (C) pauses without analysis. (D) acts unilaterally on a finance decision.
