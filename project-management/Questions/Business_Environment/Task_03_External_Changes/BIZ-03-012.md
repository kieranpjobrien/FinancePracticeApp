# Question

A project developing an electric vehicle charging network discovers that a major automotive manufacturer is about to announce a proprietary charging standard that differs from the standard the project is implementing. This could significantly affect market adoption. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the planned standard since it was approved in the business case
- **B**: Immediately redesign to support the new proprietary standard
- **C**: Gather intelligence on the announcement, assess potential market impact, evaluate technical options (supporting multiple standards, pivoting, waiting), and present analysis to stakeholders
- **D**: Wait until the announcement is made before taking any action

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a significant market development that requires analysis before action. The PM should understand the situation (how definitive is the information?), assess impact (will the proprietary standard dominate or coexist?), and evaluate options. Stakeholders need informed analysis to decide the path forward.

Option A ignores potentially significant market changes.

Option B reacts without understanding whether the change is confirmed or how the market will respond.

Option D waits passively when proactive analysis could inform better decision-making.
