# Question

A project is developing a new retail product. Economic forecasts now predict a recession within the next year, which would significantly reduce consumer spending. The project is 40% complete. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue as planned since economic forecasts are often wrong
- **B**: Immediately cancel the project to preserve capital
- **C**: Assess the impact on the business case, evaluate options (continue, pause, pivot, cancel), and present recommendations to stakeholders
- **D**: Accelerate delivery to launch before the recession hits

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Significant economic changes warrant reassessment of the business case. The PM should analyze: How would recession affect demand? Is the product recession-resilient or recession-sensitive? What are the costs and benefits of different options? Stakeholders should make an informed decision with updated analysis.

Option A dismisses a significant risk without analysis.

Option B makes a major decision without proper analysis or stakeholder input.

Option D assumes faster delivery is the right response without understanding the nature of the economic impact.
