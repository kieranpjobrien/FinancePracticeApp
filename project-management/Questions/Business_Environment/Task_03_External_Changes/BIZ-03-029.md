# Question

A project is building a renewable energy facility. Government policy shifts result in reduced subsidies for the technology the project is implementing. The business case assumed the original subsidy level. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue construction and lobby for subsidy restoration
- **B**: Halt construction until policy stabilizes
- **C**: Recalculate the business case with new subsidy levels, assess project viability, evaluate options (continue, modify, pause, cancel), and present analysis to stakeholders
- **D**: Accept lower returns and complete the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Policy changes affecting project economics require business case reassessment. The project may still be viable with lower returns, might need cost reductions, or might no longer be justified. Stakeholders need updated financial analysis to decide whether to continue investing.

Option A continues spending while hoping for policy reversal, which may not happen.

Option B pauses without understanding whether the project is still viable at current subsidy levels.

Option D accepts reduced returns without stakeholder authorization or analysis of alternatives.
