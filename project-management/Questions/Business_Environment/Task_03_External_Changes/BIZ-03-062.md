# Question

A major supplier prices in USD and your project budget is approved in AUD. The AUD has dropped 12% against the USD since planning, and supplier purchase orders represent 40% of remaining project spend. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Absorb the impact into contingency and update the forecast without raising it formally at the next governance review.
- **B**: Quantify the FX exposure against remaining spend, consult treasury on hedging options, then brief the sponsor.
- **C**: Demand the supplier honour the original USD price at the AUD equivalent on the original approval date of purchase.
- **D**: Renegotiate the supplier contract to AUD at current rates so the project carries no further FX exposure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FX exposure is a finance matter; the PM quantifies, consults treasury on hedging, and briefs the sponsor with options (B). (A) absorbs a material impact quietly. (C) is commercial wishful thinking without contract basis. (D) locks in a weak rate without professional advice.
