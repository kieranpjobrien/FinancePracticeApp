# Question

Geopolitical tension is rising in a country supplying the bulk of your contractor workforce. Visa restrictions are a real possibility, and the project is 60% complete. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately replace all contractors with local resources to remove the workforce exposure completely.
- **B**: Continue with current contractors and hope tensions ease before any restrictions land.
- **C**: Compress the schedule aggressively to finish before any visa changes can take effect.
- **D**: Assess the risk, develop contingencies for staffing, and align mitigations with stakeholders.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right move is to manage the risk explicitly with options sized to likelihood and impact (D). (A) over-corrects without analysis. (B) takes the risk without preparation. (C) is rarely feasible at 60% and ignores quality risk.
