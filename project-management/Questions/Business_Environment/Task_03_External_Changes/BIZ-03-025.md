# Question

A project relies on contractors from a specific country. Geopolitical tensions escalate, and there's risk that visa restrictions could prevent contractor travel. The project is 60% complete. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with current contractors and hope tensions ease
- **B**: Immediately replace all contractors with local resources
- **C**: Assess the geopolitical situation, develop contingency plans for contractor disruption, explore alternative resourcing options, and discuss risk mitigation with stakeholders
- **D**: Accelerate the project to complete before restrictions take effect

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Geopolitical risk affecting resources requires risk management. The PM should assess the likelihood and potential impact, identify alternatives (remote work, local contractors, different countries), develop contingencies, and ensure stakeholders understand the risk and mitigation options.

Option A accepts risk without mitigation.

Option B overreacts without understanding the actual risk level or exploring intermediate options.

Option D may not be feasible and doesn't address the underlying risk if the project continues longer.
