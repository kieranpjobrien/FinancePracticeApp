# Question

A competitor announces a product launch that will occur before your project completes. The competitor's product appears to address similar market needs. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accelerate the project timeline at any cost to beat the competitor
- **B**: Continue as planned since the original business case was approved
- **C**: Analyze the competitive impact, assess differentiation opportunities, and present options to stakeholders for decision
- **D**: Cancel the project since the market opportunity is lost

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. External changes require analysis, not reflexive reactions. The competitor's announcement changes the context, but the right response depends on analysis: How similar are the products? What differentiation exists? Is being second to market acceptable if the product is superior? Stakeholders need options and analysis to make an informed decision.

Option A reacts without analysis. Racing to launch may compromise quality and may not be the right strategy.

Option B ignores relevant market change. The original business case assumed certain market conditions that have now changed.

Option D assumes being second to market means failure, which isn't always true. Many successful products weren't first to market.
