# Question

A technology project is using AI components. New legislation is proposed that would require explainability for AI decisions in the project's domain. The legislation might pass in 12-18 months. The project launches in 3 months. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed as planned since the legislation isn't passed yet
- **B**: Wait until the legislation passes to understand exact requirements
- **C**: Assess the legislative likelihood and requirements, evaluate the effort to add explainability capabilities, and discuss with stakeholders whether to build for potential compliance now or plan for later adaptation
- **D**: Immediately redesign to include full explainability

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Potential future regulation creates uncertainty that requires analysis. Consider: How likely is passage? What might the requirements be? How difficult would it be to add explainability later versus building it in now? What's the cost of being wrong either way? Stakeholders should decide the risk appetite.

Option A ignores a foreseeable compliance risk.

Option B delays decision-making when early preparation might reduce future costs.

Option D overreacts to uncertain legislation, potentially incurring unnecessary costs.
