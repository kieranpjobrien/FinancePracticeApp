# Question

A project is developing a mobile payment solution for a specific country. The country's central bank announces plans for a central bank digital currency (CBDC) that would compete directly with the project's solution. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel the project immediately since government competition is insurmountable
- **B**: Ignore the announcement since government projects typically face delays
- **C**: Assess the CBDC timeline and features, evaluate differentiation opportunities, and present strategic options to stakeholders
- **D**: Accelerate delivery to establish market presence before CBDC launches

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A CBDC announcement is significant but requires analysis, not reflexive reaction. Consider: What's the realistic CBDC timeline? What features will it have? Can the project differentiate (B2B focus, additional features, international reach)? Can it complement rather than compete? Stakeholders need analysis to make strategic decisions.

Option A assumes competition means failure without exploring alternatives.

Option B dismisses a significant competitive threat.

Option D assumes speed is the answer without strategic analysis of whether first-mover advantage matters.
