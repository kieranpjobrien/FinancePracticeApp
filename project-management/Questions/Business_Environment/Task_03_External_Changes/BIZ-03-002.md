# Question

In response to an economic downturn, leadership has mandated a 20% budget cut across all active projects. The original scope cannot be delivered with the reduced budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assess what scope still delivers core value at the new budget and present stakeholder options.
- **B**: Push the team to absorb the cut by working harder and tightening estimates across the board.
- **C**: Apply for an exemption on the basis that this project is too important to be cut.
- **D**: Cancel the project outright because the originally approved scope is no longer feasible to deliver.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tailor scope to match the new constraint and let stakeholders choose (A). (B) is wishful thinking — 20% rarely comes from effort alone. (C) hopes the cut applies to other people. (D) gives up before exploring whether reduced scope still creates value.
