# Question

An economic downturn causes the organization to mandate a 20% budget reduction across all projects. The project's original scope cannot be delivered with the reduced budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Attempt to deliver the original scope within the reduced budget by increasing efficiency
- **B**: Conduct impact analysis, identify options for reduced scope that still deliver core value, and present recommendations to stakeholders
- **C**: Request an exemption from the budget cut due to project importance
- **D**: Cancel the project since it can't be completed as planned

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. External constraints are real - wishing them away doesn't help. The PM should analyze what's possible with the new budget, identify scope reductions that preserve core value, and present options. This enables stakeholders to make informed decisions about priorities.

Option A is unrealistic - 20% efficiency gains through determination alone are unlikely.

Option C may not succeed and doesn't prepare for the likely outcome of living with cuts.

Option D gives up without exploring whether reduced scope still delivers value.
