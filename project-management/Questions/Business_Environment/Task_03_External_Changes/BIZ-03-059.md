# Question

A long-term vendor invokes a contractual price-escalation clause due to inflation, increasing your annual spend by 15%. The clause is valid. The PM did not anticipate the magnitude. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to pay the escalation and force renegotiation.
- **B**: Pay the escalation, update the forecast, and explore reducing scope or finding savings to offset.
- **C**: Switch vendors to remove the escalation risk.
- **D**: Absorb the cost silently to avoid drawing attention.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The contract is binding; the PM honours it, updates the financial picture, and finds offsets (B). (A) breaches the contract. (C) is disproportionate without analysis. (D) hides a material cost change from those who must approve it.
