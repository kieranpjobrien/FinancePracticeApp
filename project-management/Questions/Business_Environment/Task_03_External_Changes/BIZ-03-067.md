# Question

A sudden recessionary signal from the market leads the CFO to require a 20% cut across the portfolio. Your project's benefits are long-dated and material. What should the PM recommend to the portfolio manager?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver a concrete plan to descope or delay lower-value elements while preserving the project's core benefit streams.
- **B**: Argue the project should be fully exempted from cuts because the long-dated benefits outweigh any shorter-term savings.
- **C**: Volunteer the full 20% cut immediately across the board to show alignment with the CFO's portfolio direction.
- **D**: Pause the project until the portfolio manager personally selects which specific elements to descope or delay.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM provides a surgical options paper that protects core value within the constraint (A). (B) asks for exemption rather than engaging with the constraint. (C) applies a blanket cut regardless of value protection. (D) hands decision-making back without doing the analysis the PM is best placed to do.
