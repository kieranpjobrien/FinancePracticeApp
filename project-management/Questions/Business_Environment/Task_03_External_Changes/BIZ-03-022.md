# Question

A project is developing a product for the European market. The United Kingdom has exited the European Union, creating regulatory divergence. The project originally planned a single product version for all of Europe. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with a single product version and hope regulations stay aligned
- **B**: Create separate product versions for UK and EU from the start
- **C**: Assess the regulatory differences, evaluate the cost and complexity of maintaining one versus two product versions, and present options to stakeholders
- **D**: Focus only on the larger EU market and abandon the UK

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory divergence creates complexity that requires analysis. Consider: How different are the regulations? Will they diverge further? What's the cost of maintaining two versions? What market opportunities exist in each? Stakeholders should decide the strategic approach based on comprehensive analysis.

Option A hopes for the best without planning for likely divergence.

Option B commits to complexity before understanding if it's necessary.

Option D abandons a market without understanding if that's the right business decision.
