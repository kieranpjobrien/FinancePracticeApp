# Question

A project developing IoT devices learns that a critical component is being discontinued by its sole manufacturer. The component is central to the design. Alternative components exist but would require design changes. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Stockpile the current component for production
- **B**: Assess the timeline and impact of component discontinuation, evaluate alternative components and required redesign effort, and present options to stakeholders
- **C**: Immediately begin redesigning for the alternative component
- **D**: Find a way to manufacture the component independently

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Component discontinuation is a significant supply chain risk. The PM should understand the timeline (how long is the component available?), evaluate alternatives (what redesign is needed? what's the cost and timeline?), and present options so stakeholders can make an informed decision.

Option A addresses short-term supply but doesn't solve the long-term problem.

Option C commits to redesign without understanding costs, timeline, or whether stockpiling might be viable.

Option D is likely impractical and expensive compared to alternatives.
