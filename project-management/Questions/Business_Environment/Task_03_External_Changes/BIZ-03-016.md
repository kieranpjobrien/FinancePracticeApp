# Question

A project is developing software for a specific industry vertical. A major industry publication reports that the industry is consolidating, with several potential customers being acquired. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue development since the software is still needed
- **B**: Assess how consolidation affects the target market, potential customer base, and competitive landscape, then discuss implications with stakeholders
- **C**: Add features to appeal to acquiring companies
- **D**: Find new industry verticals to target

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Industry consolidation can significantly affect market dynamics - fewer potential customers, different decision-makers, potentially different requirements. The PM should analyze the impact and discuss with stakeholders how this affects the product strategy and go-to-market approach.

Option A ignores market changes that could affect project viability and strategy.

Option C changes product direction without proper analysis or stakeholder input.

Option D pivots markets without understanding whether consolidation actually threatens the project.
