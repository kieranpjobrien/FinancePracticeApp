# Question

An infrastructure project is planned with a 5-year payback period. After project approval but before significant spending, economic forecasts shift to predict a recession within 2 years. The CFO asks the PM to reassess. What analysis should the PM provide?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the original business case is still valid
- **B**: Update financial projections with recession scenarios, assess cash flow impacts, and present options including deferral or phasing
- **C**: Argue that economic forecasts are unreliable and should be ignored
- **D**: Recommend immediate cancellation to conserve cash

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. External economic changes affect project viability. The PM should update analysis with recession scenarios - will the project still provide acceptable returns? Are there cash flow risks during the downturn? Can the project be phased to reduce near-term spending? This gives leadership information to make an informed decision.

Option A (confirm original) doesn't account for changed circumstances.

Option C (dismiss forecasts) doesn't provide useful analysis - forecasts may be uncertain but shouldn't be ignored.

Option D (recommend cancellation) makes a decision that belongs to leadership without providing full analysis.
