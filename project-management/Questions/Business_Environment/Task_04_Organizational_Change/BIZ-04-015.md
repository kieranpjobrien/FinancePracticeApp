# Question

A project is the third attempt to implement a particular business process change in the organization. Previous attempts failed due to user resistance. The project manager wants to avoid the same outcome. What approach should they take?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the same solution with stronger executive mandates
- **B**: Analyze why previous attempts failed, address root causes in the change approach, engage skeptical stakeholders early, and build the change plan around lessons learned
- **C**: Implement the change gradually without announcing it
- **D**: Hire external consultants to drive the change since internal efforts have failed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Third-time implementations carry historical baggage. The PM should understand why previous attempts failed - was it the solution, the approach, the timing, or stakeholder management? Addressing root causes and acknowledging past failures builds credibility. Engaging known skeptics early shows willingness to listen.

Option A applies more force to the same approach, likely getting the same result.

Option C is deceptive and will generate resistance when discovered.

Option D brings external resources without understanding what actually went wrong.
