# Question

What is organizational change management in the context of project management?

## Options

- **A**: Managing changes to project scope through change control
- **B**: The structured approach to transitioning individuals, teams, and organizations from a current state to a desired future state
- **C**: Reorganizing the project team structure
- **D**: Updating the organization chart

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Organizational change management focuses on the people side of change - helping individuals adopt new processes, systems, and behaviors. It addresses how people experience and adapt to change, ensuring that technical implementations are actually adopted and used.

Option A is project change control, not organizational change management.

Option C is team restructuring, not organizational change management.

Option D is an administrative update, not a change management approach.
