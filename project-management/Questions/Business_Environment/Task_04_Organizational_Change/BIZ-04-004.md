# Question

What role do "change champions" play in organizational change management?

## Options

- **A**: They are the executives who approve changes
- **B**: They are advocates within the organization who help promote and support the change among their peers
- **C**: They are external consultants who drive change
- **D**: They are the project team members who implement the technical solution

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Change champions are individuals within the affected groups who believe in the change and advocate for it among their colleagues. Because they're peers rather than management or outsiders, their support is often more influential with other employees. They help spread understanding, address concerns, and model adoption.

Option A describes sponsors or steering committee members.

Option C describes consultants who may facilitate change but aren't embedded peer advocates.

Option D describes implementers rather than advocates within the user community.
