# Question

A project is transforming a department's culture from individual contributor focus to collaborative team-based work. Systems and processes have been changed, but behaviors remain individualistic. Bonuses are still based on individual performance metrics. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Provide more training on collaboration
- **B**: Mandate collaborative behaviors through policy
- **C**: Align incentive structures with desired behaviors by changing performance metrics and rewards to support teamwork
- **D**: Wait for the cultural shift to happen naturally over time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. People respond to incentives. If the reward system encourages individual performance while the project asks for collaboration, the reward system will win. Successful culture change requires aligning all systems - including performance management and incentives - with desired behaviors.

Option A addresses capability but not motivation when the incentive structure actively discourages the desired behavior.

Option B creates conflict between stated policy and actual incentives, leading to cynicism.

Option D ignores a structural barrier that will prevent natural evolution.
