# Question

A project implements a new workflow system that changes how employees do their daily work. During pilot testing, users complain the new system is slower than the old one, even though metrics show it's more efficient overall. What change management approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Show users the efficiency metrics to prove the system is better
- **B**: Acknowledge their experience, explain the learning curve, provide support, and communicate the long-term benefits
- **C**: Force adoption since the metrics prove it's better
- **D**: Revert to the old system to keep users happy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Change resistance often comes from the learning curve - the new system feels slower because it's unfamiliar. Effective change management acknowledges the user's experience (validating their feelings), explains that initial slowness is normal, provides support to accelerate learning, and communicates why the change is worthwhile. This addresses both the emotional and practical aspects.

Option A (show metrics) dismisses user experience in favour of data - users care about their experience, not aggregate metrics.

Option C (force adoption) creates resentment and may lead to workarounds or minimal compliance.

Option D (revert) abandons a beneficial change based on initial learning curve challenges.
