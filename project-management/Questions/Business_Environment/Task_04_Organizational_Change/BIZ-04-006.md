# Question

A digital transformation project requires significant changes to how managers supervise their teams. Middle managers are the most resistant stakeholder group, as the changes reduce their traditional control. What approach is MOST likely to gain their support?

## Options

- **A**: Have senior executives mandate the change
- **B**: Bypass middle managers by training staff directly
- **C**: Involve middle managers in shaping the new management approach and demonstrate how it can enhance their effectiveness
- **D**: Delay the project until resistant managers retire

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. People resist changes done TO them but often support changes done WITH them. Involving middle managers in designing the new approach gives them ownership and influence. Showing how the changes can make them more effective (not just taking away control) reframes the change from threat to opportunity.

Option A (mandate) may force compliance but not genuine adoption. Managers may comply minimally or undermine the change.

Option B (bypass) makes managers opponents. They have significant influence over whether their teams succeed with the new approach.

Option D (wait for retirement) is passive and may never resolve the issue.
