# Question

A project manager needs to communicate an upcoming organizational change that will be unpopular with some stakeholders. What communication approach should they take?

## Options

- **A**: Delay communication until the last possible moment to minimize anxiety
- **B**: Communicate openly and early, explain the rationale, acknowledge impacts, and describe support that will be provided
- **C**: Let rumors spread so people can gradually adjust
- **D**: Focus only on the positive aspects of the change

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Honest, early communication builds trust even when the message is difficult. People deserve to understand what's happening and why. Acknowledging negative impacts and describing support shows respect and helps people prepare. This approach reduces resistance compared to surprises or spin.

Option A creates anxiety through uncertainty and loses opportunity to shape understanding.

Option C is irresponsible and allows misinformation to spread.

Option D is seen as spin and damages credibility when people discover negative impacts.
