# Question

A project delivers a new customer service system. Initial adoption is strong, but after three months, users are reverting to old habits. Metrics show declining system usage. What change management element is likely failing?

## Options

- **A**: Training was inadequate
- **B**: Communication about the change was poor
- **C**: Reinforcement mechanisms to sustain the change are lacking
- **D**: The system wasn't properly tested

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Initial strong adoption followed by reversion indicates reinforcement failure. Users made the change but slipped back because there's insufficient support, recognition, incentives, or accountability to sustain new behaviors. Reinforcement includes feedback, recognition, rewards, and embedding change in processes.

Option A would have shown poor initial adoption, not regression.

Option B would have affected initial adoption more than sustainment.

Option D is a technical quality issue, not a change management issue.
