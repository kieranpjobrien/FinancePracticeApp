# Question

A consolidation project is merging three departments onto a single workflow system. Each department head has agreed in principle, but their teams are actively resisting. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Build the new system to accommodate all three legacy workflows side-by-side.
- **B**: Identify change champions, involve resisters in solution design, and address specific concerns about how their work will change.
- **C**: Defer the project until visible resistance subsides on its own.
- **D**: Escalate to executives and have them mandate adoption from the top.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI response to active resistance is engagement at the source (B): champions to model adoption, involvement to build ownership, and direct response to specific concerns. (A) defeats the consolidation. (C) waits without acting. (D) buys compliance, not commitment.
