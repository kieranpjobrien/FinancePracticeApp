# Question

A project to consolidate three department workflows into one system is facing resistance from all three departments, each wanting to preserve their existing processes. Each department head has agreed to the project in principle but their staff actively resist in practice. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to executives to mandate adoption
- **B**: Design the system to accommodate all three existing workflows
- **C**: Work with department heads to identify change champions, involve resisters in solution design, and address specific concerns about how work will change
- **D**: Delay the project until resistance decreases naturally

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Resistance often stems from fear of change and feeling excluded from decisions. Engaging change champions creates internal advocates. Involving resisters in design gives them voice and often surfaces legitimate concerns while building buy-in. Addressing specific concerns shows their worries are heard.

Option A uses force, which may achieve compliance but not commitment, often creating passive resistance.

Option B defeats the purpose of consolidation - accommodating everything preserves the fragmentation.

Option D hopes resistance fades without action, which rarely happens without engagement.
