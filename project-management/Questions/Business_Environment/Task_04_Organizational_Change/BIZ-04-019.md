# Question

A project is rolling out changes to multiple departments. Department A has embraced the change enthusiastically. Department B is resistant. Both departments received the same communication and training. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply more pressure to Department B
- **B**: Use Department A as proof that the change works and demand Department B comply
- **C**: Investigate what's different between the departments - leadership, culture, specific concerns, or how the change affects them - and tailor the approach for Department B
- **D**: Delay Department B's implementation until they're ready

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Different responses to the same change indicate contextual factors are at play. The PM should understand: Does Department B have different concerns? Is their leadership less supportive? Does the change affect them differently? Does their culture differ? Understanding these factors enables a tailored approach.

Option A applies force without understanding, likely increasing resistance.

Option B uses comparison punitively, which creates resentment and ignores legitimate differences.

Option D waits without addressing root causes, and "ready" may never happen without intervention.
