# Question

A project manager is planning training for a new system rollout. The training plan includes classroom sessions two weeks before go-live. Some experienced team members suggest this approach has failed before. What should the project manager consider?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the plan since classroom training is standard practice
- **B**: Cancel training since experienced team members are skeptical
- **C**: Evaluate alternative training approaches such as just-in-time training, job aids, super-users, sandbox environments, and reinforcement after go-live
- **D**: Extend classroom training to provide more practice time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Training approaches should match user needs and learning context. Classroom training two weeks before go-live often fails because people forget before they apply. Alternatives include training closer to use, job aids for reference, super-users for support, practice environments, and post-go-live reinforcement.

Option A ignores feedback that the approach hasn't worked.

Option B eliminates training without considering alternatives.

Option D provides more of what hasn't worked rather than trying different approaches.
