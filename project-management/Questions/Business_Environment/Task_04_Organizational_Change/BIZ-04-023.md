# Question

Your organisation has a history of "change fatigue" from too many initiatives that started loudly and faded. Employees are sceptical of yet another change programme. How should the project manager approach this one?

## Options

- **A**: Position this initiative as different and more important than previous attempts.
- **B**: Acknowledge the history honestly, demonstrate commitment to follow-through, and start with visible quick wins.
- **C**: Implement quickly and quietly before fatigue and resistance build up.
- **D**: Wait for change fatigue to subside before launching the initiative.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trust is rebuilt through honesty, follow-through, and visible early wins (B). (A) is "this time it's different" and is rarely believed. (C) compounds fatigue. (D) waits indefinitely without action.
