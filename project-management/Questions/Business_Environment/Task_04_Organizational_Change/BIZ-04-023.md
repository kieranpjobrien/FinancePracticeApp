# Question

A project involves significant organizational change. The organization has a history of "change fatigue" from too many initiatives. Employees are skeptical of yet another change program. How should the project manager approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Position this change as different and more important than previous changes
- **B**: Implement the change quickly before resistance builds
- **C**: Acknowledge the history honestly, demonstrate commitment to follow-through, start with visible quick wins, and be realistic about what's being asked
- **D**: Wait for change fatigue to subside before proceeding

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Change fatigue requires acknowledgment and trust-building. The PM should be honest about the organization's history, demonstrate that this initiative has proper commitment and support, deliver early wins to build credibility, and be realistic about demands on people's time and energy.

Option A is likely seen as spin since "this time it's different" is often promised but not delivered.

Option B creates more fatigue and resistance, not less.

Option D may wait indefinitely, and waiting doesn't rebuild trust.
