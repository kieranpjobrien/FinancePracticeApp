# Question

A senior executive who was the primary sponsor for a transformational project has suddenly left the organization. The project is 70% complete. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the current plan since the project was already approved
- **B**: Pause the project until a new sponsor is assigned
- **C**: Quickly identify and engage an interim sponsor, assess whether the change affects project support and direction, and communicate proactively to stakeholders
- **D**: Accelerate completion before anyone questions the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sponsor departure creates a leadership vacuum that must be filled quickly. The PM should work with leadership to identify a new sponsor, assess whether the departure affects organizational commitment to the project, and communicate with stakeholders to maintain confidence and momentum.

Option A ignores the risk that sponsorship loss creates for organizational support and decision-making.

Option B may be necessary but pausing without attempting to find a sponsor first is premature.

Option D avoids addressing the sponsorship gap rather than solving it.
