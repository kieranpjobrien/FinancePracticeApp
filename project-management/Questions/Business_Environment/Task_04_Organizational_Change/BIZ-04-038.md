# Question

The project is replacing a tool that has been in use for 15 years and is regarded with affection by long-tenured staff. Resistance is widespread but largely emotional rather than technical. What should the PM emphasise?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the legacy, involve respected long-tenured staff in the rollout, and make space for a meaningful sunset of the old tool.
- **B**: Move quickly so the resistance has less time to organise.
- **C**: Highlight the technical superiority of the new tool to overcome attachment.
- **D**: Mandate the switch through executive directive.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emotional resistance is met with respect for the past, inclusion, and ritual (A); these are the practices that earn adoption. (B) suppresses rather than addresses. (C) misreads emotional resistance as a logical objection. (D) compels behaviour without commitment.
