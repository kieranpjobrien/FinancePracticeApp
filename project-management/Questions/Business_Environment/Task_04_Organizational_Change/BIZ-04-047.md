# Question

A senior executive is publicly supportive of the change but privately tells their direct reports they "don't have to take it too seriously." The PM has heard this from multiple sources. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Request a private meeting with the executive to share what has been heard and discuss the impact on adoption.
- **B**: Confront the executive in the next steering committee.
- **C**: Inform HR about the executive's behaviour.
- **D**: Ignore it because the public position is supportive.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A direct, private, evidence-based conversation respects the executive and addresses the issue at its source (A). (B) is public confrontation that destroys trust. (C) routes a relationship issue through HR prematurely. (D) lets covert resistance continue.
