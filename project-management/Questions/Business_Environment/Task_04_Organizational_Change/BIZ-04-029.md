# Question

Users of an agile project are complaining about "constant change" and asking for fewer, larger releases. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with frequent releases because that is the agile approach.
- **B**: Switch to infrequent large releases to meet the users' request.
- **C**: Diagnose the real source of frustration (quality, comms, learning time, or cadence) and address the root cause.
- **D**: Add more training between releases so users feel better prepared.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

"Constant change" is a symptom that can have several causes (C); treating the symptom discards the benefits of frequent delivery without fixing the real issue. (A) dismisses legitimate feedback. (B) trades away agile benefits blindly. (D) assumes a training gap without evidence.
