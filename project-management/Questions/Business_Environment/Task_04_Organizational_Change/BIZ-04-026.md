# Question

During change implementation, a vocal critic of the project makes a valid point about an unaddressed user need. The project manager's initial reaction is to dismiss the feedback because of its source. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the feedback since it comes from a known resistor
- **B**: Evaluate the feedback objectively, and if valid, address the concern and acknowledge the contribution
- **C**: Address the concern privately to avoid giving the critic visibility
- **D**: Wait to see if others raise the same concern

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Valid feedback should be addressed regardless of its source. Resistors often have legitimate concerns that, if addressed, can improve the project and even convert them to supporters. Acknowledging good input, even from critics, demonstrates openness and can reduce resistance.

Option A rejects potentially valuable feedback based on its source.

Option C hides the resolution, missing an opportunity to demonstrate responsiveness.

Option D delays addressing a known issue without good reason.
