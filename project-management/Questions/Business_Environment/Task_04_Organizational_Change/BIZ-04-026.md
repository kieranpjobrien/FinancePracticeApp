# Question

A vocal critic of the project raises what looks like a valid concern about an unaddressed user need. Your instinct is to discount it because of the source. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Park the feedback until other, less-critical stakeholders raise the same issue.
- **B**: Address the concern quietly in the background to avoid giving the critic visibility.
- **C**: Ignore it on the basis that the source is a known resistor.
- **D**: Evaluate the feedback on its merits, act on it if valid, and acknowledge the contribution.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Valid input is valid regardless of who gives it (D); acknowledgement can also convert resistors into partners. (A) delays known issues. (B) hides the resolution. (C) filters feedback by identity rather than content.
