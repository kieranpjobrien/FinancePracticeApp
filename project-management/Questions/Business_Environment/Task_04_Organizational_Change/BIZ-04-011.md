# Question

A project is implementing automation that will eliminate some roles while creating new ones requiring different skills. The affected employees are anxious and resistant. Union representatives have expressed concerns. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Minimize communication about job impacts to avoid increasing anxiety
- **B**: Focus only on the technical implementation and leave workforce issues to HR
- **C**: Work with HR, leadership, and union representatives to develop a comprehensive change plan that addresses retraining, redeployment, transition support, and clear communication
- **D**: Accelerate implementation to minimize the period of uncertainty

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Workforce transitions require comprehensive planning and honest communication. The PM should work with HR and leadership to develop retraining programs, redeployment options, and transition support. Engaging union representatives builds trust and may surface practical improvements. Clear, honest communication reduces anxiety better than silence.

Option A creates distrust and allows rumors to fill the communication void.

Option B abdicates responsibility for a major impact of the project.

Option D doesn't address the underlying concerns and may create more problems.
