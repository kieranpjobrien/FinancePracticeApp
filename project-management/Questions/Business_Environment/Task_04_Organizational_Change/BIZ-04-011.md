# Question

An automation project will eliminate some unionised roles and create new ones requiring different skills. Affected staff are anxious and union representatives have raised concerns. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Limit communication about specific job impacts to avoid spreading further anxiety on the floor.
- **B**: Focus on the technical build and leave workforce issues entirely to HR and union liaison teams.
- **C**: Compress the schedule so the period of uncertainty for affected staff is as short as possible.
- **D**: Co-design a workforce transition plan with HR, leadership, and the union covering retraining.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Workforce-impacting projects require open, joint planning with the people affected (D). (A) creates a vacuum that rumours fill. (B) abdicates a major project consequence. (C) shortens uncertainty without addressing fairness or fit.
