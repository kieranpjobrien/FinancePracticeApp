# Question

A project will significantly change workflows for a department. The department head is supportive, but middle managers seem skeptical during meetings. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Rely on the department head to manage their direct reports
- **B**: Bypass the middle managers and communicate directly with staff
- **C**: Engage the middle managers directly to understand their concerns, involve them in implementation planning, and help them become change advocates
- **D**: Replace skeptical middle managers with project champions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Middle managers are critical to change success - they translate strategy to their teams and can either champion or undermine change. The PM should understand their concerns (which may be legitimate), involve them in shaping implementation, and help them see how to succeed in the new environment.

Option A ignores a critical stakeholder group whose buy-in is essential.

Option B undermines middle managers and creates organizational conflict.

Option D is unrealistic and treats symptoms rather than addressing concerns.
