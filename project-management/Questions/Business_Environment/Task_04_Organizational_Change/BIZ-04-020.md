# Question

What is the primary reason organizational change management is important for projects?

## Options

- **A**: To satisfy audit requirements
- **B**: To ensure that project deliverables are actually adopted and used, enabling benefits realization
- **C**: To document the change process
- **D**: To justify the project budget

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Project deliverables only create value when they're actually used. Change management ensures that people adopt new systems, processes, and behaviors. Without adoption, even technically successful projects fail to deliver benefits.

Option A treats change management as compliance rather than value enablement.

Option C focuses on documentation rather than outcomes.

Option D is unrelated to the purpose of change management.
