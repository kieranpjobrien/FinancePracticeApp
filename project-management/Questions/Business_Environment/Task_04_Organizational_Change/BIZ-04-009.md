# Question

A project is implementing a new performance management system. HR reports that managers are quietly continuing to use the old evaluation methods alongside the new system. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Report the non-compliance to senior leadership for enforcement
- **B**: Investigate why managers prefer the old methods, address their concerns, demonstrate the value of the new system, and work with HR to support the transition
- **C**: Allow managers to use whichever system they prefer
- **D**: Add more mandatory training sessions on the new system

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Parallel use of old systems indicates change resistance that needs understanding and addressing. Why do managers prefer the old way? Is the new system harder to use? Are they unconvinced of its value? Understanding the reasons enables targeted intervention - perhaps system improvements, better communication of benefits, or addressing specific concerns.

Option A uses enforcement without addressing underlying issues, likely creating resentment.

Option C abandons the change initiative and wastes the project investment.

Option D assumes the problem is knowledge when it's likely about willingness or system design.
