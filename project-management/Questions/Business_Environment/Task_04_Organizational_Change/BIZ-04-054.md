# Question

Frontline managers are the closest to the staff affected by the change but were not included in early planning. The PM has been told they are now "obstructive." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bypass the frontline managers and work directly with their teams.
- **B**: Bring the frontline managers in now: explain the change, hear their concerns, and co-design the rollout in their teams.
- **C**: Ask senior leadership to direct the managers to comply.
- **D**: Replace the obstructive managers with cooperative ones.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Frontline managers are critical change agents; including them late but meaningfully repairs the gap (B). (A) widens the rift. (C) compels behaviour without buy-in. (D) is disproportionate and avoids the relationship work.
