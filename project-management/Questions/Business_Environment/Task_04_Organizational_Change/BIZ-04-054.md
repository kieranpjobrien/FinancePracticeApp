# Question

Frontline managers are the closest to the staff affected by the change but were not included in early planning. The PM has been told they are now "obstructive." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bypass the frontline managers entirely and work directly with their teams on the rollout.
- **B**: Bring frontline managers in now: explain, hear their concerns, and co-design team rollout.
- **C**: Ask senior leadership to direct the managers to comply with the project's rollout plan.
- **D**: Replace the obstructive managers with more cooperative ones from other parts of the business.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Frontline managers are critical change agents; including them late but meaningfully repairs the gap (B). (A) widens the rift. (C) compels behaviour without buy-in. (D) is disproportionate and avoids the relationship work.
