# Question

A subcontractor in a sanctioned country has been working on non-sensitive components of your project. New sanctions take effect in 30 days that will prohibit the engagement entirely. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue work for the full 30-day window to maximise delivery before the sanctions cutoff.
- **B**: Plan an orderly transition of the work to a permitted supplier and complete it before cutoff.
- **C**: Apply for an export licence to allow the engagement to continue past the sanctions cutoff.
- **D**: Pause all work immediately pending a full legal review of the new sanctions language.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM must end the engagement by the deadline and protect the work; transition is the planning response (B). (A) maximises throughput but does not address what happens at the cutoff and may include work that cannot be transferred legally. (C) is sometimes possible but is not the default plan and may not be granted. (D) halts work without a transition path.
