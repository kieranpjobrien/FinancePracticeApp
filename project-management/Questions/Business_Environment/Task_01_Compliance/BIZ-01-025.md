# Question

A project involves building a medical device. The team has completed development, but during final compliance testing, they discover an edge case that could cause patient harm under rare circumstances. Fixing it would delay market launch by three months. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the issue and include it in user documentation as a known limitation
- **B**: Release the device and issue a patch later
- **C**: Halt the release, fix the safety issue, and accept the schedule impact
- **D**: Assess the probability of the edge case occurring and release if it's sufficiently rare

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Patient safety is non-negotiable in medical devices. An edge case that could cause harm must be fixed before release, regardless of schedule impact. Medical device regulations (FDA, CE marking) require demonstrated safety, and known safety issues cannot be released.

Option A documents the danger but doesn't prevent harm. Users may not read or understand limitations.

Option B is illegal for medical devices. You cannot release known safety issues with a plan to patch later.

Option D attempts risk-based reasoning for safety. Regulatory bodies don't accept "rare enough" as justification for known harm potential.
