# Question

A software composition scan on your product flags a copyleft-licensed library deep in a third-party component. Legal says shipping as-is would require open-sourcing proprietary code. Launch is in four weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ship the product under the current licence because the dependency is indirect and buried deep in components.
- **B**: Release the affected proprietary code under the copyleft licence to satisfy the obligation cleanly and quickly.
- **C**: Engage engineering and legal to identify a compliant alternative, replace the component, and rescan before launch.
- **D**: Request a commercial dual-licence from the upstream maintainer so the existing component can be kept in place.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Copyleft obligations follow the code regardless of depth; the PM removes the dependency with a compliant alternative (C). (A) knowingly ships under a breached licence. (B) is a strategic decision well above the PM and not a default compliance response. (D) may be possible but is a long-shot fallback, not the FIRST action.
