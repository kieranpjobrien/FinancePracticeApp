# Question

A project manager learns that a vendor the project depends on has failed a compliance certification. The vendor claims they will be recertified within two months. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue using the vendor since they expect to be recertified soon
- **B**: Assess the compliance impact, evaluate alternatives, and develop contingency plans while monitoring the vendor's recertification progress
- **C**: Immediately terminate the vendor contract
- **D**: Hide the compliance issue from stakeholders to avoid project disruption

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Vendor compliance issues create risk that needs management. The PM should assess whether continued use creates compliance problems for the project, evaluate alternative vendors, create contingency plans, and monitor progress. Two months of uncertainty requires active risk management, not passive hope.

Option A accepts vendor assurance without verification or contingency. Certifications can take longer than expected.

Option C may be necessary but is premature without assessment. The compliance gap might be manageable, or switching costs might exceed benefits.

Option D is unethical and likely violates the PM's responsibility to stakeholders. Hidden compliance risks can cause major problems later.
