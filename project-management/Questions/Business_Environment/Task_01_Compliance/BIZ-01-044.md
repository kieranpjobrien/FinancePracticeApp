# Question

You are running a Scrum team in a medical device company. Regulatory documentation requires traceability from requirement to test for every release. A developer suggests skipping detailed test traces this sprint to focus on a critical defect. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the trade-off for one sprint and resume traceability the following sprint.
- **B**: Reassign traceability work to the QA team so developers can focus on the defect.
- **C**: Defer the defect to the next sprint to maintain traceability without overload.
- **D**: Reject the trade-off and protect both the defect work and the traceability obligations.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Regulated traceability is a non-negotiable part of done; the PM protects it while replanning the work (D). (A) creates an undocumented release that fails regulatory inspection. (C) is a viable last resort but should not be the default before exploring scope, capacity, or help from QA. (B) without a plan or capacity check is a hopeful reassignment, not a decision.
