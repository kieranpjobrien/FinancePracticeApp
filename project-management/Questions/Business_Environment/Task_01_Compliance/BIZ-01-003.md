# Question

A project team realizes that full compliance with a newly interpreted regulation would require significant rework that would cause major schedule delays. Partial compliance is possible and would address the regulation's primary intent, though technically falling short of full requirements. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement partial compliance since it addresses the intent
- **B**: Present the options (full compliance with delay vs. partial compliance with risk) to stakeholders with clear risk assessment for their decision
- **C**: Implement full compliance regardless of schedule impact
- **D**: Challenge the regulation interpretation with legal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This is a business risk decision that should be made by stakeholders with decision authority, not the PM alone. The PM's role is to present clear options with assessed risks (full compliance: certain delay; partial compliance: potential regulatory risk) and let appropriate decision-makers choose.

Option A makes a compliance risk decision that may be above the PM's authority.

Option C may be right but doesn't engage stakeholders in a decision with significant business impact.

Option D might be appropriate as part of B's analysis but shouldn't be the primary action.
