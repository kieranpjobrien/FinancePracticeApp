# Question

A new interpretation of a financial reporting regulation surfaces late in a banking project. Full compliance would push the launch back four months; a partial implementation would meet the regulation's stated intent but not its literal text. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the partial approach, since it satisfies the underlying intent.
- **B**: Document the two options with risks and impacts and bring them to the steering committee for decision.
- **C**: Implement full compliance and absorb the four-month delay to eliminate risk.
- **D**: Ask legal to formally challenge the new interpretation with the regulator.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A compliance trade-off with material schedule impact is a governance decision, not a PM call. (B) prepares the analysis and routes the decision to those with authority and risk appetite. (A) commits the organisation to regulatory exposure without sponsor sign-off. (C) may be the right call but pre-empts the conversation about cost-benefit. (D) is a possible workstream that can flow from the steering decision but is not the first action.
