# Question

A project is implementing a system in a heavily regulated industry. The team wants to use a new technology that offers significant benefits but hasn't been explicitly approved by regulators. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the new technology since it hasn't been approved
- **B**: Use the technology and seek forgiveness later if questioned
- **C**: Work with compliance and legal to assess whether the technology can be used within current regulations, and if unclear, engage with regulators proactively
- **D**: Use the technology but document the decision thoroughly

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory ambiguity around new technology requires expert analysis. The technology may be permissible under existing rules, may require specific implementation approaches, or may need regulatory clarification. Proactive engagement with regulators demonstrates good faith and reduces risk of adverse findings later.

Option A may unnecessarily forfeit benefits. "Not explicitly approved" doesn't mean prohibited.

Option B accepts regulatory risk without proper assessment. "Forgiveness over permission" is dangerous in regulated industries.

Option D documents the decision but doesn't adequately assess the regulatory risk before proceeding.
