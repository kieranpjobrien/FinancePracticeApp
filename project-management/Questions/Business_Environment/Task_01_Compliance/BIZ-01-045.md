# Question

What is the difference between a control deficiency and a control failure?

## Options

- **A**: A deficiency is a design weakness; a failure is the control not operating as designed.
- **B**: A deficiency applies to financial controls; a failure applies to operational controls.
- **C**: A deficiency is reported internally; a failure is reported to regulators.
- **D**: A deficiency is identified by management; a failure is identified by external auditors.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Control deficiencies relate to design (the control as designed cannot meet its objective), while failures are operating issues (the control did not run as designed) (A). (B), (C), and (D) describe organisational reporting choices, not the conceptual distinction.
