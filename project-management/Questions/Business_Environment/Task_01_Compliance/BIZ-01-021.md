# Question

You are launching a customer platform that processes personal data in the EU (GDPR), California (CCPA), and Brazil (LGPD). The three regimes overlap on most points but differ on consent, data subject rights, and breach notification. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the strictest single requirement from any of the three globally to keep compliance simple.
- **B**: Implement three independent compliance stacks, one per jurisdiction.
- **C**: Standardise on GDPR because it is the most comprehensive and assume the others are covered.
- **D**: Engage privacy counsel to design a unified baseline with jurisdiction-specific overlays where needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Multi-jurisdiction privacy compliance needs expert design (D): a shared baseline with targeted overlays balances coverage and maintainability. (A) sounds neat but can be operationally impractical and may still miss jurisdiction-specific obligations. (B) creates needless complexity where regimes align. (C) is a common myth — GDPR coverage does not satisfy unique CCPA or LGPD provisions.
