# Question

A project is implementing a system that processes personal data across the EU (GDPR), California (CCPA), and Brazil (LGPD). Each regulation has different requirements for data subject rights, consent, and breach notification. What approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the strictest requirement from any regulation and apply it globally
- **B**: Implement separate compliance approaches for each jurisdiction
- **C**: Work with legal and privacy experts to design a unified approach that meets all requirements, with jurisdiction-specific handling where regulations conflict
- **D**: Focus on GDPR compliance since it's the most comprehensive

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Multi-jurisdiction privacy compliance requires expert input and thoughtful design. A unified approach where possible reduces complexity, but jurisdiction-specific handling is needed where requirements differ. This requires legal and privacy expertise to navigate correctly.

Option A sounds simple but may not work when requirements conflict or when applying strict requirements globally is operationally impractical.

Option B creates maintenance burden and complexity. Where regulations align, a unified approach is more sustainable.

Option D assumes GDPR covers everything, which isn't true. CCPA and LGPD have unique requirements that GDPR compliance alone won't satisfy.
