# Question

Your customer requires a passing SOC 2 Type II report before they will deploy your new platform. The audit window requires six months of operational evidence. How should the project manager plan for this?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule the audit immediately after project closure and treat it as a separate downstream engagement.
- **B**: Assume the operations team will own the audit once the system has transitioned to live.
- **C**: Build the evidence period and audit milestones into the schedule with a documented transition.
- **D**: Ask the customer to accept a Type I report initially and upgrade to Type II post go-live.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A SOC 2 Type II audit needs a planned operational period and clear ownership (C), so it belongs in the schedule with explicit transition arrangements. (A) leaves a gating dependency outside the project plan. (B) assumes ownership without confirming or resourcing it. (D) renegotiates a customer requirement instead of planning for it.
