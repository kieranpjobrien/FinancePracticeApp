# Question

A project requires SOC 2 Type II compliance for the delivered system. The compliance audit must be passed before customer deployment. The audit takes 6 months of operational history to complete. How should the project manager plan for this?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule the audit to begin after the project closes
- **B**: Build the 6-month operational period and audit into the project timeline, or establish a transition plan with operations for post-project compliance
- **C**: Request that the audit requirement be waived for the initial release
- **D**: Assume the operations team will handle compliance after project delivery

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. SOC 2 Type II compliance requires operational history before audit can be passed. The PM must either extend the project to include this period or establish clear ownership and transition plans with operations. Either way, this constraint must be planned for explicitly.

Option A leaves compliance completion outside the project without clear ownership or planning.

Option C treats a customer requirement as optional. If SOC 2 is required for deployment, it cannot be waived by the project.

Option D assumes handoff without ensuring operations is prepared, resourced, and committed to completing the compliance work.
