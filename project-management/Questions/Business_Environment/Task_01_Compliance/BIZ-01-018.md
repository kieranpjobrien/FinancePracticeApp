# Question

A project manager discovers that a team member has been bypassing required compliance checkpoints to speed up delivery. The shortcuts haven't caused any known problems yet. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow it to continue since there have been no problems
- **B**: Stop the practice immediately, assess what work was done without proper compliance checks, and determine if remediation is needed
- **C**: Thank the team member for their initiative in improving efficiency
- **D**: Document the practice for future process improvement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compliance checkpoints exist for reasons - skipping them creates unmanaged risk. The PM must stop the practice, assess whether previous work meets compliance requirements, and remediate if necessary. Even without visible problems, the compliance status of work done without checks is unknown.

Option A accepts compliance risk based on luck. The absence of known problems doesn't mean there are no problems.

Option C rewards behavior that undermines compliance controls, setting a dangerous precedent.

Option D documents the problem without fixing it. Documentation doesn't address the compliance risk.
