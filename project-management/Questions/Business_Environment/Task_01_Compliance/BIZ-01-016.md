# Question

A web application project must meet WCAG 2.1 AA, but most of the development team has never delivered an accessible product before. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Build accessibility training and expert design reviews into the plan from the start.
- **B**: Allow the team to learn during development and address gaps in the QA phase.
- **C**: Outsource the entire accessibility workstream to a specialist supplier.
- **D**: Request a temporary waiver from accessibility requirements while the team builds capability.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A known capability gap on a mandatory requirement is a planning problem (A): training and expert reviews lift the team while the work proceeds. (B) defers the issue to test, where defects are far more expensive to fix. (C) removes accountability from the team and builds dependency without internal capability. (D) treats compliance as conditional on team skill, which it isn't.
