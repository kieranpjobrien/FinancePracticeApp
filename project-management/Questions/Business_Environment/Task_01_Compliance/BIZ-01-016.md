# Question

A project must comply with accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA). The development team has limited experience with accessibility. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assume the team will learn as they go and address accessibility issues during testing
- **B**: Plan for accessibility training, incorporate accessibility experts into design reviews, and include accessibility testing throughout development
- **C**: Outsource all accessibility work to specialists
- **D**: Request a waiver from accessibility requirements due to team inexperience

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When the team lacks compliance-critical skills, the PM should proactively address the gap through training, expert support, and appropriate process changes. Building accessibility in from the start is more effective than testing it in later.

Option A accepts a known capability gap without mitigation. This will likely result in significant rework when accessibility issues are found late.

Option C may be partially appropriate but fully outsourcing creates dependency and doesn't build internal capability. Integration of expertise with the team is better.

Option D treats compliance as optional based on capability. Accessibility requirements aren't waived because they're difficult.
