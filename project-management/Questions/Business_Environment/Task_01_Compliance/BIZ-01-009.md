# Question

A construction project is operating in a jurisdiction where environmental regulations are ambiguous about a specific disposal method. The legal team says the method is "probably acceptable" but cannot provide certainty. The compliant alternative would add significant cost. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Use the cheaper method since legal didn't definitively say it was prohibited
- **B**: Document the legal opinion and proceed with the cheaper method to protect the project budget
- **C**: Present both options to the sponsor with risk analysis, including potential penalties, reputational impact, and precedent implications
- **D**: Automatically choose the more expensive compliant method regardless of cost

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory ambiguity creates risk that requires business judgment, not PM decision alone. The sponsor needs to understand both the financial impact of the conservative approach and the regulatory, reputational, and legal risks of the uncertain approach. This is a business decision that should be made with full information.

Option A accepts regulatory risk without proper authority or risk assessment. "Probably acceptable" is not the same as compliant.

Option B attempts to document away the risk rather than address it. Documentation doesn't prevent penalties.

Option D removes business judgment. While conservative, the cost differential might be significant enough to warrant stakeholder input on risk appetite.
