# Question

A project in the healthcare sector must comply with patient data privacy regulations (like HIPAA). Midway through the project, a new regulation is enacted with stricter requirements. The project was designed to the old standards. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the original design since the project started before the new regulation
- **B**: Assess the impact of new requirements, submit a change request, and update the project to comply
- **C**: Ignore the new regulation until the next project
- **D**: Cancel the project since compliance isn't possible

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compliance requirements aren't optional, even when they change mid-project. The PM should assess what changes are needed to meet the new requirements, document the impact (scope, cost, schedule), and follow change control to update the project. The alternative is delivering something that can't legally be used.

Option A (grandfather clause) may not apply to all regulations, and even if legal, may create risk when the system inevitably needs updating.

Option C (ignore) risks legal penalties and may result in unusable deliverables.

Option D (cancel) is premature without assessing whether compliance is achievable within reasonable constraints.
