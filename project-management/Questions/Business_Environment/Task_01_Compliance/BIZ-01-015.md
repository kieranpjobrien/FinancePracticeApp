# Question

When in a project's life cycle should compliance requirements be identified?

## Options

- **A**: During closure, as part of the lessons-learned review.
- **B**: During initiation and planning, as early as practical.
- **C**: Only when an external audit has been formally scheduled.
- **D**: Once stakeholders raise concerns about regulatory exposure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Compliance shapes scope, design, cost, and schedule, so it must be identified up-front (B). (A) is far too late — discovering rules at closure can render the deliverable unusable. (C) and (D) are reactive; PMI expects proactive identification of compliance during planning, not in response to outside pressure.
