# Question

When should compliance requirements be identified in a project?

## Options

- **A**: After the project is complete, during closure
- **B**: As early as possible, ideally during initiation and planning
- **C**: Only when an audit is scheduled
- **D**: When stakeholders raise concerns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compliance requirements should be identified early because they often influence scope, design, budget, and schedule. Discovering compliance requirements late leads to costly rework, delays, or non-compliant deliverables.

Option A is far too late. Discovering compliance requirements at closure means the deliverable may be unusable.

Option C is reactive rather than proactive. Compliance should be built in, not checked after the fact.

Option D waits for problems rather than preventing them. Proactive identification is more effective than reactive response.
