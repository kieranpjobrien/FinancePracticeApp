# Question

A project discovers mid-execution that a new data privacy regulation will affect the solution being built. The project wasn't originally scoped to meet this regulation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue as planned since the regulation wasn't known during planning
- **B**: Assess the compliance gap, document the risk and impact, and work with stakeholders to modify scope and plan to achieve compliance
- **C**: Immediately stop work until legal provides guidance
- **D**: Add compliance activities without changing the timeline or budget

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Regulatory compliance is typically non-negotiable - you can't deliver a non-compliant solution. The PM should assess what changes are needed, document the impact (scope, schedule, cost), and work with stakeholders to modify the project appropriately. This treats the regulation change as a legitimate driver for replanning.

Option A ignores legal requirements, which exposes the organization to penalties and delivers a non-usable solution.

Option C is overly cautious - legal input is valuable, but the PM can begin assessment and impact analysis immediately.

Option D is unrealistic - compliance requirements have real impact on work. Pretending they don't affect timeline or budget sets up failure.
