# Question

A multinational rollout must satisfy data residency rules in two countries that effectively contradict each other for one data set. Legal teams in both regions are firm. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the stricter of the two requirements globally to err on the side of caution.
- **B**: Allow each region to interpret and apply its own requirements independently.
- **C**: Escalate to the steering committee and pause the rollout in both regions.
- **D**: Convene the two legal teams with the architect to design a compliant solution before the next gate.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Conflicting requirements demand a worked solution, not a default (D): the legal teams plus architecture can design segregation, regional instances, or anonymisation that satisfies both. (A) sounds safe but may be impossible if the rules genuinely conflict. (C) escalates without an attempted resolution. (B) creates inconsistent compliance posture and shifts the problem onto regional teams.
