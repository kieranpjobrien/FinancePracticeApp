# Question

A project manager discovers that a recently implemented feature doesn't comply with accessibility regulations (WCAG standards). The feature passed functional testing but accessibility testing wasn't included in the test plan. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document it as a known issue and address in a future release
- **B**: Immediately prioritise remediation and update testing processes to include accessibility compliance
- **C**: Wait for a complaint before taking action
- **D**: Remove the feature entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Accessibility compliance is often legally required and affects real users. The PM should address both the immediate issue (remediate the non-compliant feature) and the systemic issue (add accessibility testing to prevent recurrence). Compliance gaps should be treated seriously.

Option A (defer) may violate legal requirements and excludes users who need accessibility features.

Option C (wait for complaint) is reactive and may result in legal action or reputation damage.

Option D (remove feature) punishes all users for a fixable compliance gap.
