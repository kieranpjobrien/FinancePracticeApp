# Question

What is the primary purpose of compliance management in project management?

## Options

- **A**: To create additional documentation for auditors
- **B**: To ensure the project and its deliverables meet all applicable laws, regulations, standards, and contractual requirements
- **C**: To slow down project execution by adding review gates
- **D**: To give legal departments control over project decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compliance management ensures that the project operates within legal and regulatory boundaries, meets contractual obligations, and adheres to relevant standards. This protects the organization from legal penalties, reputational damage, and delivery of non-usable products.

Option A treats compliance as bureaucracy rather than protection. Documentation supports compliance but isn't its purpose.

Option C mischaracterizes compliance. While compliance activities take time, their purpose is protection, not delay.

Option D confuses compliance oversight with project control. Legal advises on compliance, but PMs manage projects within those boundaries.
