# Question

Your global SaaS rollout must satisfy both EU GDPR data minimisation and a client country law requiring extended retention for audit. Legal says both apply; engineering says the schemas cannot satisfy both cleanly. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the stricter retention rule so the audit law is fully satisfied in every jurisdiction worldwide.
- **B**: Convene legal, privacy, and architecture to design a jurisdiction-aware solution and document the decision rationale.
- **C**: Escalate to the steering committee that the conflicting laws make the project technically infeasible as planned.
- **D**: Proceed with GDPR-compliant defaults and document the audit conflict as an accepted residual project risk.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conflicting regulations are resolved by cross-functional design, not by defaulting to one and hoping (B); jurisdiction-aware data handling is standard practice. (A) over-retains in GDPR jurisdictions and itself breaches privacy law. (C) escalates before the PM has attempted resolution with the right experts. (D) accepts a known legal breach as a risk, which compliance is never traded against.
