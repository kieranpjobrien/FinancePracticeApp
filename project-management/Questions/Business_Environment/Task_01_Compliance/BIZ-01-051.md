# Question

An internal audit identifies a moderate finding on a control your project owns. The auditor's recommended remediation is reasonable but will take six weeks. Your project closes in four. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree the remediation plan with the audit team and the receiving operations owner who will complete it after closure.
- **B**: Compress the remediation into the four weeks and accept the impact on other work.
- **C**: Defer the remediation to a future project so this project can close on time.
- **D**: Request that the audit finding be downgraded to a low-risk observation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Findings can transfer to receiving operations with a documented plan and owner (A); the project closes cleanly and the work continues. (B) absorbs scope late and may compromise quality elsewhere. (C) lacks a concrete owner and timeline. (D) attempts to manage the finding rather than the underlying issue.
