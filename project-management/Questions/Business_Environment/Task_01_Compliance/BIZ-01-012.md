# Question

An external auditor presents the project with several minor non-conformances and one major non-conformance against the relevant industry standard. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Walk through each finding with the auditor to confirm scope, validity, and impact.
- **B**: Push back on the major finding because it relies on an interpretation the team disagrees with.
- **C**: Halt all delivery work until every finding has been remediated and re-tested.
- **D**: Identify which team members produced the non-conformant work for the corrective action plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Before responding, the PM needs a shared understanding of what the auditor actually found and why (A). (B) commits to a defensive posture before the facts are clear. (C) overreacts; minor findings rarely justify a stop-work. (D) targets blame rather than the underlying process gap that PMI emphasises in corrective action.
