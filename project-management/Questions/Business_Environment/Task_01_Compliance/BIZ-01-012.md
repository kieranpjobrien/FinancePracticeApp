# Question

A project is being audited for compliance with industry standards. The auditor identifies several minor non-conformances and one major non-conformance. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Dispute the audit findings to protect the project's reputation
- **B**: Understand the findings, assess their validity and impact, and develop a corrective action plan
- **C**: Immediately halt all project work until issues are resolved
- **D**: Assign blame to team members responsible for the non-conformances

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Audit findings require a professional response: understand what was found, assess whether findings are valid and their impact, then develop appropriate corrective actions. The major non-conformance likely needs priority attention, but all findings deserve a response plan.

Option A is defensive and unproductive. Auditors generally identify legitimate issues; disputing valid findings damages credibility.

Option C overreacts to findings that may not require work stoppage. Address findings appropriately but proportionally.

Option D focuses on blame rather than correction. The goal is compliance, not punishment.
