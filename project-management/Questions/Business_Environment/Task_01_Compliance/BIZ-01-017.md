# Question

Six months before launch, a regulator publishes a draft rule that will require significant redesign of your financial product, taking effect 18 months after the project's planned launch date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pivot the design now to the future rules to avoid rework after launch.
- **B**: Hold the current plan and revisit after the rule is finalised closer to enforcement.
- **C**: Analyse impact and present launch-now-then-modify versus redesign-now options to stakeholders.
- **D**: Continue with the current design because the rule does not bind at launch.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Both launching first and redesigning first are defensible — this is a stakeholder choice (C) about cost, time-to-market, and adaptability. (A) commits to redesign without weighing market opportunity. (B) is passive when direction is already known. (D) ignores a foreseeable obligation that will be material within 18 months of launch.
