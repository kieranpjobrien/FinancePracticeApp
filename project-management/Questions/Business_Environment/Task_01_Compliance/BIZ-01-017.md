# Question

A project is developing a financial product. Midway through development, the regulatory agency announces new rules that will take effect in 18 months. The new rules would require significant changes to the product design. The project is scheduled to complete in 6 months. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Complete the current design since the rules don't take effect until after launch
- **B**: Immediately pivot to the new requirements, abandoning current design work
- **C**: Analyze the impact, assess whether the current design can be adapted later, and present options including launch timing and redesign approaches to stakeholders
- **D**: Wait until the final rules are published before making any decisions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This requires business analysis, not a reflexive decision. Questions to consider: Can the product be launched now and modified later? What's the cost of redesign now versus later? What's the market opportunity of launching earlier? Will early adopters accept a product that needs changes? Stakeholders need options with analysis to decide.

Option A may work but ignores the upcoming compliance requirement. Launching a product that needs significant redesign in 18 months has business implications.

Option B discards work without analysis. The current design might be adaptable, or early market entry might be valuable enough to accept later modification costs.

Option D delays decision-making. While final rules matter, the direction is known and planning should begin.
