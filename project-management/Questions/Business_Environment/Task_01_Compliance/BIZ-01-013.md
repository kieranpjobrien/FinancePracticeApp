# Question

A multinational project must comply with regulations in three different countries. The regulations conflict - what is required in Country A is prohibited in Country B. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the regulations of the country where the organization is headquartered
- **B**: Choose the strictest interpretation across all regulations
- **C**: Work with legal and compliance experts to develop a solution that satisfies requirements in each jurisdiction, potentially with localized implementations
- **D**: Seek exemptions from the conflicting regulations

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Conflicting international regulations require expert navigation and often creative solutions. This might mean different product versions for different markets, architectural approaches that satisfy all requirements, or legitimate business structures that separate regulated activities. This requires legal expertise, not PM judgment alone.

Option A doesn't resolve the conflict - you must comply in each jurisdiction where you operate, not just headquarters.

Option B sounds conservative but may not work when requirements actively conflict (one requires what another prohibits).

Option D assumes exemptions are available. In practice, regulatory exemptions are rarely granted for compliance conflicts.
