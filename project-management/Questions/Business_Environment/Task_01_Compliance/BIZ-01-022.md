# Question

At 60% complete, the compliance officer tells you the project's documentation does not satisfy the audit trail standard required for the regulated industry you operate in. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assess the gap, reconstruct evidence for completed work where possible, and tighten practice for the remaining 40%.
- **B**: Add a single documentation clean-up task to the backlog for the final sprint before closure.
- **C**: Continue as planned and capture the audit standard for the next project's lessons learned.
- **D**: Push back on the compliance officer's interpretation of the audit trail requirement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Address both halves of the project: reconstruct what you can for completed work, and prevent recurrence on the remainder (A). (B) leaves reconstruction to the worst possible time, when memories and artefacts have decayed. (C) accepts a foreseeable audit failure. (D) is adversarial; the compliance officer holds the standard.
