# Question

What is the relationship between compliance requirements and the project scope?

## Options

- **A**: Compliance requirements are separate from scope and managed independently
- **B**: Compliance requirements should be included in the project scope as they define what the deliverable must achieve
- **C**: Compliance requirements only affect testing, not scope
- **D**: Compliance requirements can be traded off against other scope items

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Compliance requirements define constraints and requirements that the deliverable must meet. They should be explicitly included in scope documentation so they are planned for, resourced, and tracked like other requirements.

Option A creates a gap where compliance work isn't planned or tracked, leading to surprises.

Option C underestimates compliance impact. Compliance affects design, development, and operations, not just testing.

Option D treats compliance as optional scope. Unlike nice-to-have features, compliance requirements typically cannot be descoped.
