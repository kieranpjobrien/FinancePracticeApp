# Question

A control owner reports that their quarterly access review was completed, but the supporting evidence shows only one of the three required reviewers signed off. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the partial sign-off given the control owner's verbal confirmation.
- **B**: Wait for the next quarterly cycle and ensure all three reviewers participate then.
- **C**: Treat the control as not operating effectively and require completion before closing the period.
- **D**: Update the policy to require only one reviewer going forward to match practice.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A control with a designed three-reviewer step is not operating if only one signs off; the PM treats it as a deficiency and remediates (C). (A) substitutes verbal assurance for evidence. (B) leaves the period unprotected and accepts the gap. (D) lowers the standard to fit a failure rather than fixing the failure.
