# Question

A project is subject to contractual compliance requirements from the customer. The contract specifies that all code must be reviewed by two developers before merging. The team has been conducting single-reviewer reviews due to time pressure. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with single reviews since the contract requirement is impractical
- **B**: Immediately implement the two-reviewer requirement and assess whether previous code needs re-review
- **C**: Negotiate a contract amendment to reduce the review requirement
- **D**: Document the deviation and inform the customer at project completion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Contractual requirements are binding. The PM should immediately comply with the contract and assess the risk of work done without proper review. Depending on customer requirements and risk, re-review of previous code may be necessary.

Option A unilaterally decides to violate the contract, which creates legal risk.

Option C may be appropriate but doesn't address work already done in violation. The requirement exists now; compliance must start now.

Option D defers disclosure of a known breach, which damages trust and may violate contract terms about notification.
