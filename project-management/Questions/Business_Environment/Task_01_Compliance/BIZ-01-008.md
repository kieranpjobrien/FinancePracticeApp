# Question

A healthcare IT project must comply with HIPAA regulations. During a sprint review, the team demonstrates a feature that stores patient data in a way that doesn't meet encryption requirements. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the issue and address it in a future release after launch
- **B**: Stop the feature from being released and work with the team to implement compliant encryption before deployment
- **C**: Request a waiver from the compliance department
- **D**: Deploy as planned since the feature works correctly from a functional perspective

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. HIPAA compliance is legally required for healthcare data. Deploying non-compliant features exposes the organization to significant legal penalties and patient data risks. The PM must ensure compliance before deployment, even if it impacts the schedule.

Option A treats compliance as optional and deferrable. HIPAA violations can result in substantial fines and criminal penalties - this risk cannot be accepted.

Option C assumes waivers exist for legal requirements. Organizations cannot waive HIPAA compliance.

Option D prioritizes functionality over compliance, which would deliver a legally non-usable feature.
