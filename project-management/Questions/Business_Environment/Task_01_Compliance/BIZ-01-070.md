# Question

A security patch must be deployed within 24 hours to close an actively exploited vulnerability on a regulated payments platform. Full change control normally takes five working days. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invoke the emergency change process, execute under accelerated controls, and complete full documentation immediately afterwards.
- **B**: Bypass change control entirely because the exploit represents a genuine and imminent safety to production.
- **C**: Follow the standard five-day change process to preserve governance integrity even under this pressure.
- **D**: Deploy the patch locally in a sandbox and wait for the standard process to authorise production rollout.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Mature change control includes an emergency path with post-hoc documentation; the PM uses it (A). (B) abandons governance under pressure, which is exactly when it matters most. (C) applies the wrong cadence to an active exploit. (D) leaves production exposed while governance catches up.
