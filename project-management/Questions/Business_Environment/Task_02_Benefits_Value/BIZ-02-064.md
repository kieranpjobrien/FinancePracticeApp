# Question

Your shared-services automation project delivered, and three business units each claim the cost savings toward their own budgets. The combined claim exceeds the actual saving measured. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the business units sort attribution among themselves because it does not affect delivery performance.
- **B**: Escalate the inflated combined claim to finance and propose a transparent attribution method across units.
- **C**: Split the measured saving evenly across the three units in the benefits register without further analysis.
- **D**: Delay reporting benefits until the units agree on their shares, even if that means missing governance deadlines.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inflated benefits harm organisational credibility; the PM escalates and offers a transparent attribution approach (B). (A) allows double-counting to persist. (C) splits evenly without reference to contribution or driver. (D) withholds benefits reporting as leverage, which is not the PM's role.
