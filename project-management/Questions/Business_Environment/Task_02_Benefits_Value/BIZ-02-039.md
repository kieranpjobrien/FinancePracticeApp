# Question

Six months after a CRM rollout, the benefits owner reports that adoption is lower than expected and the projected sales-cycle reduction has not materialised. What should be the next step?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct a post-implementation review to identify why adoption is low and close the gap.
- **B**: Reduce the projected benefit in the benefits register to match observed actual performance.
- **C**: Launch a new project to replace the CRM with a different product from another vendor.
- **D**: Wait another six months for adoption to mature organically before assessing the benefit.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Investigation comes before adjustment or replacement (A): adoption barriers are often fixable. (B) writes off the value without understanding why. (C) is disproportionate before diagnosis. (D) defers learning while the gap continues.
