# Question

A project delivered every planned feature on time and on budget, but six months after go-live the expected business benefits have not materialised — adoption is far below forecast. What is the most accurate diagnosis?

## Options

- **A**: The project succeeded because it met its scope, time, and cost targets.
- **B**: Benefits realisation was not planned or actively managed; outputs were delivered but outcomes were not.
- **C**: The metrics being used to track benefits are likely incorrect or mis-configured.
- **D**: Users are change-resistant and adoption should now be mandated by leadership.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Outputs (features) and outcomes (adoption and benefit) are not the same; PMI explicitly distinguishes them (B). (A) defines success too narrowly to include the original purpose of the investment. (C) blames the instruments rather than the absence of an adoption plan. (D) addresses the symptom with force, not root cause.
