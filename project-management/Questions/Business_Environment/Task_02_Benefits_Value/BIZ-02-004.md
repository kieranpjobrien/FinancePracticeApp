# Question

A project successfully delivers all planned features on time and within budget. However, six months later, business metrics show the expected benefits haven't materialised - users aren't adopting the new system. What went wrong?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing - the project was successful since it met its objectives
- **B**: Benefits realisation wasn't planned or managed; delivery doesn't guarantee value
- **C**: The metrics must be wrong
- **D**: Users are resistant to change and should be forced to adopt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Project success and benefits realisation are different. Delivering outputs (features) doesn't guarantee outcomes (adoption) or benefits (value). Benefits realisation requires planning for adoption, change management, measuring outcomes, and sometimes adjusting after delivery. The project delivered but didn't ensure value.

Option A (project succeeded) uses a narrow definition of success that ignores the purpose of the investment.

Option C (metrics wrong) avoids accountability for the adoption failure.

Option D (force adoption) blames users rather than addressing why the system isn't being adopted.
