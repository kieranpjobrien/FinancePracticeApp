# Question

What is the difference between a project's outputs and its outcomes?

## Options

- **A**: Outputs are everything that was planned in advance; outcomes are things that emerged unexpectedly during delivery.
- **B**: Outputs are the tangible deliverables produced; outcomes are the changes or benefits from using them.
- **C**: Outputs serve external customers of the project; outcomes serve only the internal stakeholder community.
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference between the two; the terms are generally interchangeable in practice.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Outputs are what the project hands over (a system, a building, training); outcomes are what changes when those outputs are used (B). (A) misuses the terms. (C) is a fabricated split. (D) collapses two distinct concepts that PMI keeps separate for benefits realisation.
