# Question

What is the difference between a project's outputs and its outcomes?

## Options

- **A**: Outputs are everything that was planned; outcomes are anything that emerged unexpectedly.
- **B**: Outputs are the tangible deliverables produced; outcomes are the changes or benefits that result from using them.
- **C**: Outputs serve external customers; outcomes serve internal stakeholders only.
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference; the terms are interchangeable.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Outputs are what the project hands over (a system, a building, training); outcomes are what changes when those outputs are used (B). (A) misuses the terms. (C) is a fabricated split. (D) collapses two distinct concepts that PMI keeps separate for benefits realisation.
