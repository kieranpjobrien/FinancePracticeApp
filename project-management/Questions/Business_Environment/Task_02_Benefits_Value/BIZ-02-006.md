# Question

What is the difference between project outputs and project outcomes?

## Options

- **A**: Outputs are planned; outcomes are unplanned
- **B**: Outputs are the tangible deliverables produced; outcomes are the changes or benefits that result from using those deliverables
- **C**: Outputs are for external stakeholders; outcomes are for internal stakeholders
- **D**: There is no difference

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B.

**Outputs** are what the project produces - the tangible deliverables (software, buildings, documents, trained people).

**Outcomes** are what happens when outputs are used - the changes, improvements, or benefits that result (increased sales, reduced costs, improved customer satisfaction).

Example: A CRM system (output) leads to better customer retention (outcome).

This distinction matters because delivering outputs doesn't guarantee realising outcomes.
