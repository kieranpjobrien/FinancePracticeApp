# Question

A portfolio of three projects was approved on combined annual benefits of $10M. Two delivered on plan and are tracking to $7M combined; the third failed. Leadership asks why portfolio benefits are below target. What should the project manager explain?

## Options

- **A**: The $10M target was aspirational and always unlikely to be met.
- **B**: Two out of three succeeding is a strong portfolio delivery rate.
- **C**: Benefits forecasts are inherently uncertain and should be treated as estimates.
- **D**: The shortfall is the cancelled project; the successful two are meeting their projections.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Clear attribution is the PM's duty to leadership (D): the successful projects are on track, and the gap is the failed one. (A) deflects without evidence. (B) changes the question from value to count. (C) is true in general but not the explanation for this specific shortfall.
