# Question

A portfolio of three projects was approved based on combined benefits of $10M annually. Two projects delivered on plan, but the third failed. The two successful projects are achieving $7M in benefits. Leadership asks why benefits are below target. What should the project manager explain?

## Options

- **A**: The target was unrealistic from the start
- **B**: Two out of three projects succeeded, which is a good success rate
- **C**: The benefits shortfall is attributable to the failed third project; the successful projects are delivering their projected benefits and the overall portfolio shortfall reflects the project failure
- **D**: Benefits calculations are inherently uncertain and should be viewed as estimates

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Clear accountability and transparency are important. If the two successful projects are achieving their projected benefits, the shortfall is entirely attributable to the failed project. This accurate attribution helps leadership understand what happened and make informed decisions about future investments.

Option A deflects rather than explaining the actual cause.

Option B focuses on project success rate rather than benefits accountability.

Option D, while true, doesn't explain this specific shortfall when the cause is clearly identifiable.
