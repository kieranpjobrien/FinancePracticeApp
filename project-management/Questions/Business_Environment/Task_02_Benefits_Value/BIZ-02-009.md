# Question

During sprint planning, the product owner wants to prioritize a feature that has high business value but significant technical complexity. The team prefers to work on simpler features first. What should the project manager facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Support the team's preference to build momentum with simpler features
- **B**: Mandate the product owner's priority since they own the backlog
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion where business value is weighed against complexity, risk, and dependencies to make an informed prioritization decision
- **D**: Split the feature into smaller pieces regardless of whether it makes sense

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective prioritization considers multiple factors: business value, complexity, risk, dependencies, and team capacity. The PM should facilitate a conversation where both perspectives are heard and the tradeoffs are understood. The product owner has prioritization authority, but should make informed decisions.

Option A prioritizes team preference over business value without analysis.

Option B imposes prioritization without collaborative discussion, missing the team's valid technical concerns.

Option D applies a solution (splitting) without understanding whether it's appropriate for this feature.
