# Question

In sprint planning, the product owner wants to pull a high-value but technically complex feature to the top of the backlog. The team would prefer to clear two simpler items first. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Side with the team to keep momentum and morale high in the early sprints.
- **B**: Defer to the product owner since they have backlog authority.
- **C**: Facilitate a conversation that weighs business value against complexity, risk, and dependencies before the PO decides.
- **D**: Split the complex feature into smaller stories regardless of whether the slicing makes sense.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PO ultimately decides priority, but the decision should be informed by team perspectives on complexity and risk (C). (A) trades long-term value for short-term comfort. (B) skips the team conversation entirely. (D) imposes a slicing solution without first understanding whether it preserves value.
