# Question

A project manager is reviewing a business case that promises "improved customer satisfaction" as a key benefit. This is the only description of that benefit. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the benefit as stated since it comes from the business
- **B**: Work with stakeholders to define specific, measurable targets for customer satisfaction improvement and how it will be measured
- **C**: Remove the benefit from the business case since it's not specific
- **D**: Assume customer satisfaction will improve if the project delivers its features

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Vague benefits are difficult to track and verify. The PM should work with stakeholders to define what "improved customer satisfaction" means specifically - perhaps a 10-point increase in NPS score, or 15% reduction in complaints - and how it will be measured. This enables accountability and verification.

Option A accepts a benefit that cannot be meaningfully tracked or verified.

Option C discards a potentially important benefit rather than improving its definition.

Option D assumes correlation between delivery and benefit without measurement.
