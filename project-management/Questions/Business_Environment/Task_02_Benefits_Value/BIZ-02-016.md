# Question

A draft business case lists "improved customer satisfaction" as a primary benefit with no further detail. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Strike the benefit from the case because it cannot be quantified.
- **B**: Work with stakeholders to define a measurable target (e.g., NPS uplift) and how it will be tracked.
- **C**: Accept the wording as-is because it came from the business sponsor.
- **D**: Assume satisfaction will rise as a side-effect of delivering the planned features.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A benefit must be measurable to be managed (B): the PM helps the business turn the aspiration into a target with a tracking method. (A) loses a real benefit instead of sharpening it. (C) leaves the project unable to verify success. (D) assumes a causal link without evidence.
