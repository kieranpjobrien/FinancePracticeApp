# Question

Your new self-service portal will shift call volume away from agents, saving money, but it will also slow resolution for a segment of elderly customers. The business case omits this dis-benefit. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the dis-benefit to the benefits register, quantify its customer impact, and propose mitigations to the sponsor.
- **B**: Leave the business case unchanged because adding a negative will risk the project funding being reconsidered.
- **C**: Treat the dis-benefit as an operational matter for the call centre leadership team to fully resolve post-launch.
- **D**: Cancel the self-service portal because any negative customer impact disqualifies the initiative from proceeding.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Dis-benefits are registered, quantified, and mitigated so the net value is transparent to decision-makers (A). (B) hides downside to protect funding. (C) abdicates responsibility for a foreseeable customer impact. (D) overreacts by cancelling when mitigation is possible and the net may still be positive.
