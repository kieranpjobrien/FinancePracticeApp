# Question

A project's business case projected $2M annual savings. At the halfway point, market conditions have changed and the realistic savings estimate is now $500K. The project is on track for original scope, schedule, and budget. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the project since it's performing well against plan
- **B**: Present the revised benefits analysis to the sponsor for a go/no-go decision
- **C**: Hide the revised estimate to avoid project cancellation
- **D**: Accelerate the project to increase savings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The project's justification was its business case. If the expected value has dropped by 75%, stakeholders should make an informed decision about whether to continue, pivot, or cancel. The PM's job is to provide accurate information for decision-making, not to make that decision or hide relevant data.

Option A (continue blindly) ignores that the rationale for the investment has materially changed.

Option C (hide information) violates professional ethics and stakeholder trust.

Option D (accelerate) doesn't address the fundamental value change and may waste more resources.
