# Question

The organisation has committed to ESG goals, including a 20% reduction in supplier carbon intensity. Your supply chain project will reduce cost by selecting cheaper, but higher-carbon, suppliers. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend the cheaper suppliers because cost savings are explicit in the business case.
- **B**: Engage the sponsor and ESG lead to weigh cost against the organisation's ESG commitment before recommending.
- **C**: Pick suppliers that meet ESG criteria regardless of cost impact.
- **D**: Defer the supplier decision until the ESG commitment is formally rescinded.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Value is multi-dimensional; the PM surfaces the trade-off and lets the right decision-makers choose (B). (A) ignores a strategic commitment. (C) overrides the cost objective without weighing it. (D) refuses to act because of competing objectives instead of resolving them.
