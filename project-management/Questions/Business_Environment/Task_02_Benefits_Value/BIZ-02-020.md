# Question

A project's original business case projected benefits based on market conditions that have since changed. Competitors have entered the market, reducing the achievable revenue gains. The project is 50% complete. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue as planned since the project was already approved
- **B**: Reassess the business case with current market conditions and present updated value analysis to stakeholders for a continue/cancel/modify decision
- **C**: Accelerate delivery to beat remaining competitors
- **D**: Reduce project scope to lower costs and restore ROI

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When the business environment changes, the business case should be reassessed. Stakeholders need updated information to decide whether to continue investing. The project might still be worthwhile, might need modification, or might need to be cancelled to avoid further investment in reduced value.

Option A ignores relevant market changes that affect the project's value proposition.

Option C assumes faster delivery is the right response without analysis.

Option D reduces scope without understanding whether it addresses the actual value issue.
