# Question

Who is typically responsible for ensuring that project benefits are realized after project closure?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager
- **B**: The project team
- **C**: A benefits owner, often a business manager or sponsor, supported by operations
- **D**: The PMO

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Benefits realization often occurs after project closure, when the PM and team have moved on. A benefits owner from the business - typically a manager who will use the deliverables or the sponsor - takes responsibility for driving adoption, measuring benefits, and taking corrective action when needed.

Option A places responsibility on someone who typically moves to other projects after closure.

Option B has team members who also typically move on after project completion.

Option D may track benefits across projects but doesn't own specific project benefits realization.
