# Question

Six months into a two-year platform programme, telemetry from an early release shows one use case is driving 70% of customer value and another is unused. The sponsor wants to maintain the original scope. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the agreed scope so the commitment to stakeholders and dependent teams is honoured.
- **B**: Present the telemetry with a reprioritised roadmap proposal focused on the high-value use case to the sponsor.
- **C**: Drop the unused use case from the backlog immediately and reallocate the freed capacity to the high-value one.
- **D**: Wait another six months to gather more data before proposing any material changes to the programme scope.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Evidence-based value management presents the data and proposes a reprioritisation through governance (B). (A) ignores telemetry to honour a plan value has already overtaken. (C) makes a scope change without the sponsor or change control. (D) defers action when the evidence is already actionable.
