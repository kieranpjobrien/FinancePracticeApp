# Question

A project's business case projected annual savings of $2M from process automation. The project has been live for six months, and finance reports savings are tracking to only $800K annually. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Close the benefits tracking since the project is delivered
- **B**: Investigate the benefits gap, identify root causes, and recommend corrective actions to improve benefits realization
- **C**: Revise the business case to reflect actual savings
- **D**: Declare success since the project delivered the automation as specified

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A significant benefits gap deserves investigation. Possible causes include lower than expected adoption, process issues, incorrect baseline measurements, or unrealistic projections. Understanding the gap enables corrective action - perhaps additional training, process adjustments, or feature enhancements to close the gap.

Option A abandons benefits realization responsibility prematurely.

Option C adjusts expectations rather than addressing the gap. While projections may need updating, the goal should be improving actual benefits.

Option D treats project completion as success regardless of business value, ignoring the purpose of the project.
