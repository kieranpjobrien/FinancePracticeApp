# Question

Six months after go-live of a process automation project, finance reports actual savings of $800K against a $2M annual projection. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Investigate the gap, identify root causes, and recommend corrective actions to lift realised benefits.
- **B**: Conclude benefits tracking now that the project is delivered and operational.
- **C**: Update the business case to set the new run-rate as the official baseline.
- **D**: Declare project success because the automation was delivered as specified.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A 60% benefits shortfall warrants investigation, not closure or restatement (A). (B) abdicates the PM's benefits responsibility. (C) hides the gap by moving the goalposts. (D) confuses delivery of the output with realisation of the benefit.
