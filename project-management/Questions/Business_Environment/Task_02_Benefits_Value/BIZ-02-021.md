# Question

A project delivered a manufacturing automation system. Post-implementation review shows 110% of projected cost savings achieved, but quality issues increased by 15%. The quality issues weren't tracked in the original business case. What should the project manager document in lessons learned?

## Options

- **A**: The project exceeded expectations by delivering 110% of projected savings
- **B**: The business case should have included quality metrics as success criteria; exceeding one metric while degrading another doesn't represent full success
- **C**: Quality issues are an operations problem, not a project issue
- **D**: The 15% quality increase should be accepted as a tradeoff for cost savings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The lesson learned is that business cases should include all relevant metrics, not just primary benefits. Achieving cost savings by degrading quality is a pyrrhic victory - the quality costs may exceed savings. Future projects should define comprehensive success criteria including potential negative impacts.

Option A ignores the quality degradation and presents an incomplete picture of project success.

Option C deflects responsibility. The project caused the quality issue; it's a project outcome.

Option D accepts a tradeoff that may not be appropriate without stakeholder input and full analysis.
