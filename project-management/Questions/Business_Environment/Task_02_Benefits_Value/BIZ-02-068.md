# Question

An agile team can release feature A (80% of projected benefit, ships in 3 months) or wait 9 months to release features A and B together (100% of projected benefit). The benefits owner prefers combined release. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Release feature A in three months, measure realised benefit, and use that signal to refine feature B's plan.
- **B**: Wait nine months and release both together so the benefits owner's preference is respected unchanged.
- **C**: Split feature B into smaller pieces and release them incrementally alongside feature A from the third month.
- **D**: Release feature A now and cancel feature B because 80% of projected benefit exceeds the nine-month hurdle rate.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Earlier value plus learning outperforms waiting, and the data refines plans for the remaining scope (A). (B) prioritises preference over measurable earlier value. (C) commits to splitting B without evidence from A; plan then learn. (D) cancels B on a guess rather than on data from an actual release.
