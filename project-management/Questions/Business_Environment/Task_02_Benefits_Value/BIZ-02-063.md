# Question

A capital project is 60% through budget but market conditions have eroded the expected ROI from 22% to 4%, below the hurdle rate of 12%. The team has invested heavily and morale is at stake. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Continue because the project is more than halfway through and stopping would waste committed investment.
- **B**: Re-baseline the business case at the lower ROI so the project can continue against a realistic target.
- **C**: Present a kill-or-pivot recommendation to the sponsor based on remaining spend against remaining expected value.
- **D**: Accelerate delivery to finish sooner and begin extracting whatever reduced value remains as quickly as possible.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Sunk cost is irrelevant; the PM frames the decision on remaining spend against remaining value (C). (A) is sunk-cost fallacy dressed as pragmatism. (B) hides a hurdle-rate failure by redefining success. (D) assumes accelerating is cheaper than stopping without showing the maths.
