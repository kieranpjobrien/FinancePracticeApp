# Question

The product owner proposes shipping a minimum viable product (MVP) that delivers 60% of the planned benefits in 40% of the timeline. The sponsor is sceptical, preferring the full release. What should the PM emphasise?

## Options

- **A**: That the team can probably shorten the full release timeline if pressured.
- **B**: That MVPs always deliver more value than full releases in software projects.
- **C**: The earlier value, faster learning, and reduced risk of an MVP relative to the full-release option.
- **D**: That the product owner has authority to decide release scope.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM frames the trade-off in value terms — earlier benefits, real-world feedback, and lower risk (C). (A) is wishful and avoids the actual question. (B) is an overgeneralisation. (D) shuts down the sponsor instead of helping them decide.
