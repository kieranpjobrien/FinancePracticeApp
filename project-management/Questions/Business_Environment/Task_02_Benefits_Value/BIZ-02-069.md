# Question

A marketing automation project reports "emails sent" as its primary benefit metric and is trending well above target. Conversion and revenue metrics are flat. The sponsor is pleased with the headline. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue reporting the emails sent metric because it is agreed and trending ahead of plan.
- **B**: Replace the benefits register with conversion and revenue metrics, retire emails sent from reporting immediately.
- **C**: Add more vanity activity metrics so the dashboard shows a broader picture of the automation project's output.
- **D**: Raise with the sponsor that emails sent is vanity and propose outcome metrics tied to value.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Activity is not value; the PM raises the measurement problem and proposes outcome-based metrics (D). (A) keeps reporting comfortable numbers that do not represent value. (B) makes a unilateral governance change that the sponsor should be part of. (C) adds more noise of the same kind.
