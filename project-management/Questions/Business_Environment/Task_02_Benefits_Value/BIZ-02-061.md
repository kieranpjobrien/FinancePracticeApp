# Question

Twelve months after a CRM project closed and was formally handed to operations, the benefits owner reports only 40% of the projected revenue uplift has materialised. The original PM has been reassigned. Who should own remedial action and how?

## Options

- **A**: The original PM should reopen the project and lead benefits recovery because the project owns realisation.
- **B**: Commission a new project to redeliver the CRM scope until the full uplift is demonstrably achieved in production.
- **C**: Accept the 40% as the actual benefit realised and formally close benefits tracking to free up governance capacity.
- **D**: The benefits owner leads recovery using operational levers, with PMO governance tracking progress against the benefits plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

After closure, benefits realisation belongs to the benefits owner supported by PMO governance, not the delivery PM (D). (A) confuses delivery with operations; projects do not reopen to operate a benefit. (B) treats realisation shortfall as a delivery gap rather than an adoption or operational one. (C) gives up without investigating the gap.
