# Question

Engineering proposes a value-engineered solution that cuts build cost by 25% but also trims the benefit projection by 10%. The sponsor must decide. What should the PM include in the recommendation package?

## Options

- **A**: Net present value of both options, risk profile differences, and adoption assumptions behind the benefit figures.
- **B**: The cost saving amount only because the benefit reduction is marginal and acceptable relative to cost.
- **C**: A single recommended option with the headline numbers so the sponsor can decide quickly without distractions.
- **D**: Engineering's preference as the recommendation because they understand the technical trade-offs most thoroughly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Good value decisions need comparable NPV, risk, and assumptions for each option, not just headline figures (A). (B) strips the benefit side of a cost-vs-benefit trade-off. (C) hides the trade-off by offering only one option without rationale. (D) outsources a business decision to an engineering preference.
