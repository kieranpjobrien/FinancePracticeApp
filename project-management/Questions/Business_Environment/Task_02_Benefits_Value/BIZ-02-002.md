# Question

Midway through a product development project, market research reveals that customer needs have shifted significantly. The product as currently designed will likely have limited market success, but significant investment has already been made. What recommendation should the project manager make?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the current plan since significant investment has been made (sunk cost)
- **B**: Present the market research to stakeholders and recommend pivoting to better align with customer needs, accepting that some work may be discarded
- **C**: Complete the current product and then start a new project for the changed needs
- **D**: Cancel the project entirely since the original business case is no longer valid

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The sunk cost fallacy (continuing because of past investment) leads to poor decisions. What matters is future value. If the market has shifted, the wise choice is to pivot toward what customers actually need. Some work may be lost, but continuing to build something nobody wants is a larger loss.

Option A falls into the sunk cost fallacy - past investment shouldn't drive future decisions.

Option C delays addressing reality and invests more in a low-value product. If you know the market has shifted, why complete something misaligned with needs?

Option D may be appropriate if no pivot is possible, but the question suggests a pivot could align with changed needs, which preserves some investment and delivers value.
