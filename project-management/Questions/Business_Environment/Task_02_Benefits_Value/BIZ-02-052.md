# Question

A two-year R&D project has clear "kill criteria" defined at the second-year gate. At the gate, two of three criteria are missed. The team argues they are close and one more quarter will fix it. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend continuation because the team is close and morale matters.
- **B**: Recommend that the steering committee enforce the kill criteria and either stop the project or formally re-baseline with new criteria.
- **C**: Recommend an extra quarter and revisit the criteria informally.
- **D**: Recommend reducing the kill criteria to one of three so the project can proceed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Kill criteria are a governance discipline; if they are missed, the project either stops or is formally re-baselined (B). (A) ignores the criteria. (C) erodes the discipline by working around the gate. (D) lowers the bar instead of facing the decision.
