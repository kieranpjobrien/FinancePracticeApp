# Question

When should benefits tracking typically begin?

## Options

- **A**: After project closure when benefits materialize
- **B**: During project initiation when the business case is developed, with baseline data collected before implementation
- **C**: During the final sprint or phase
- **D**: When stakeholders request benefits reports

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Benefits tracking should begin early - establishing baseline measurements before the project changes anything, defining how benefits will be measured, and setting up tracking mechanisms. Without baseline data, you cannot demonstrate improvement.

Option A is too late - without baselines, you cannot measure change.

Option C is too late for establishing baselines and tracking mechanisms.

Option D is reactive rather than proactive and likely too late for proper measurement.
