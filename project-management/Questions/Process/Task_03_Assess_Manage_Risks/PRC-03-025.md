# Question

What is the difference between contingency reserve and management reserve?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: Contingency reserve is for identified risks (known unknowns); management reserve is for unidentified risks (unknown unknowns)
- **C**: Contingency is larger than management reserve
- **D**: Management reserve is controlled by the PM; contingency is not

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Contingency reserve covers identified risks in the risk register (known unknowns) - the PM can use it for planned risk responses. Management reserve covers unidentified risks that may emerge (unknown unknowns) - it requires management approval to use and is typically outside the baseline budget.

Option A ignores distinct purposes.

Option C size varies by project, not by type.

Option D is reversed - PM typically controls contingency, management controls management reserve.
