# Question

What is the purpose of a risk breakdown structure (RBS)?

## Options

- **A**: To assign numeric probability values to each risk in the register.
- **B**: To hierarchically categorise potential sources of risk to support identification and analysis.
- **C**: To prioritise risks by impact on the cost baseline.
- **D**: To track the status of risk responses through the project lifecycle.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An RBS is a hierarchical categorisation of risk sources (technical, external, organisational, management, etc.) that helps ensure comprehensive risk identification and supports analysis. (A) describes probability assessment, not categorisation. (C) describes a prioritisation matrix. (D) describes risk monitoring or a status report, not the RBS.
