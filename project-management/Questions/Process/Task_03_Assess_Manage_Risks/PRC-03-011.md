# Question

What is the difference between a risk and an issue in project management?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: A risk is an uncertain event that may occur in the future; an issue is a problem that has already occurred and requires immediate attention
- **C**: An issue is more important than a risk
- **D**: Risks are technical; issues are business-related

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Risks are potential future events with uncertainty - they may or may not occur. Issues are current problems that have materialised and require resolution. Risk management is proactive (preventing or preparing for potential problems); issue management is reactive (addressing current problems).

Option A ignores the key distinction between potential and actual problems.

Option C and D mischaracterise the difference.
