# Question

A PM identifies that a specific piece of legacy integration, if attempted, has 70% probability of extending the project by two months. The business value of this integration is small relative to the overall project benefits case. What is the best response strategy?

## Options

- **A**: Mitigate by adding architects and test resources to reduce the integration's probability of causing the schedule extension.
- **B**: Transfer the integration work to a specialist supplier under a fixed-price contract so schedule risk sits with the supplier.
- **C**: Accept the risk and build the two-month contingency into the schedule baseline explicitly, with visibility to the sponsor.
- **D**: Avoid by removing the legacy integration from scope, given its small business value relative to the probable schedule impact.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Avoid is the PMI response of choice when a scope element's risk outweighs its value; removing it from scope eliminates the exposure. (D) matches the situation. (A) mitigating a low-value element invests further in something that may not be worth doing. (B) transfer still keeps the schedule dependency on the project's critical path. (C) accepting a two-month extension for a low-value element is poor value management.
