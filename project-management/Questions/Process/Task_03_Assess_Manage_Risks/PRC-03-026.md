# Question

A mitigation action reduces probability of a high-impact risk from 60% to 20%, but costs $25,000. The unmitigated EMV is $90,000. Should the PM recommend the mitigation?

## Options

- **A**: No — mitigation actions should be reserved for risks rated critical in the qualitative matrix.
- **B**: Yes — the mitigation cost is less than 30% of the unmitigated EMV.
- **C**: Yes — residual EMV plus mitigation cost ($30,000 + $25,000 = $55,000) is less than $90,000, a net benefit of $35,000.
- **D**: No — reducing probability to 20% still leaves meaningful exposure that the team cannot accept.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The financial comparison PMI expects is: unmitigated EMV versus (residual EMV + mitigation cost). Original EMV = 0.60 x $150,000 = $90,000 (implied impact $150k). Residual EMV = 0.20 x $150,000 = $30,000, plus $25,000 cost = $55,000 total. Net benefit = $35,000, so mitigation is worthwhile. (A) introduces a false rule; cost-benefit drives the decision, not category. (B) the percentage comparison isn't the correct calculation — it ignores residual EMV. (D) misapplies risk tolerance as a blanket rejection of residual exposure when the economics clearly favour the action.
