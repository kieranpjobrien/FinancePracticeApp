# Question

What is the difference between a risk and an issue?

## Options

- **A**: Risks are more serious than issues
- **B**: A risk is an uncertain event that may occur; an issue is a problem that has already occurred
- **C**: Risks are external; issues are internal
- **D**: Risks require mitigation; issues require escalation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A risk is an uncertain future event that may or may not happen and could positively or negatively affect project objectives. An issue is a current problem that has already materialized and requires immediate attention. When a risk occurs, it becomes an issue.

Option A is incorrect - issues can be very serious (a materialized major risk), and some risks may be minor.

Option C is incorrect - both risks and issues can be internal or external.

Option D describes possible responses but isn't the defining difference. Risks can be accepted (not mitigated), and issues may be resolved without escalation.
