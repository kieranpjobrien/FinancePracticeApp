# Question

A PM is negotiating a fixed-price contract with a supplier for a custom software component. The supplier wants a time-and-materials arrangement, citing scope uncertainty. The project has tight budget constraints and the risk appetite is low. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the T&M terms and manage the scope closely to contain the cost risk to the project's approved budget ceiling.
- **B**: Insist on a fixed-price contract so all scope-uncertainty cost risk sits with the supplier rather than the project.
- **C**: Structure a hybrid contract — fixed price for well-defined scope and a capped T&M envelope for the genuinely uncertain portion.
- **D**: Defer the contract decision until scope uncertainty is resolved through further analysis and the supplier's concerns are addressed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Contract type is a risk allocation choice; PMI-aligned practice matches contract mechanism to scope certainty at component level. (C) does this well. (A) accepts open-ended cost risk the appetite doesn't support. (B) insisting on fixed-price on genuinely uncertain scope leads to padding, disputes, or supplier withdrawal. (D) deferring delays delivery to resolve what hybrid contracting can handle now.
