# Question

A positive risk has been identified: if a pending regulatory approval arrives early, the project could enter market three months ahead of plan, capturing significant first-mover revenue. What is the BEST response strategy?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the potential upside without taking action so the team stays focused on baseline deliverables.
- **B**: Share the opportunity with a partner firm that can co-invest and reduce the organisation's capital exposure.
- **C**: Mitigate by preparing a contingency plan in case early approval causes readiness problems.
- **D**: Exploit by investing in pre-launch marketing and production scaling now to ensure the opportunity is captured if approval arrives early.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

For positive risks (opportunities), the four strategies are exploit, share, enhance, and accept. When the opportunity is high value and the organisation wants to guarantee the benefit, exploit is the active strategy — take action to make sure the opportunity occurs. (A) acceptance leaves value on the table when proactive action could capture it. (B) share applies when another party is better positioned to capture the opportunity; here the organisation itself can benefit. (C) mitigate is a negative-risk strategy and misframes an opportunity as a threat.
