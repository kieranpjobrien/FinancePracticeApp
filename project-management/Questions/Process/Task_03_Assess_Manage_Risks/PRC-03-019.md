# Question

A project stakeholder with significant political influence insists their concern be documented as a high-priority risk, but the PM's assessment indicates it's low probability and low impact. How should the PM handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Inflate the risk rating to keep the stakeholder happy
- **B**: Document the risk as assessed but acknowledge the stakeholder's concern, explain the assessment criteria transparently, and agree on what trigger conditions would warrant re-evaluation
- **C**: Refuse to document the risk at all
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to overrule the stakeholder

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Maintain assessment integrity while respecting stakeholder input. Document: include the risk with your honest assessment. Acknowledge: the stakeholder's concern is heard. Transparency: explain how probability and impact were assessed. Triggers: agree on conditions that would change the assessment. This respects both analytical rigour and stakeholder relationships.

Option A compromises risk management integrity.

Option C dismisses a stakeholder's legitimate input.

Option D escalates when the situation can likely be resolved directly.
