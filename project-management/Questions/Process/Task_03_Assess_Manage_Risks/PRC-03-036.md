# Question

During a risk workshop, a junior team member identifies a risk that the senior engineers dismiss as trivial. The PM's experience suggests the junior may be right. How should the PM handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Log the risk in the register at the junior member's initial rating and commit to a follow-up analysis before downgrading.
- **B**: Let the senior engineers' judgement prevail since they have more domain experience.
- **C**: Override the engineers and set the risk at high priority based on PM judgement.
- **D**: Park the risk for a separate discussion with the engineers without the junior present.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Risk identification should capture ideas without premature filtering. Logging the risk and committing to analysis respects the input while preserving rigour. (B) defers to seniority and discourages future input from less senior voices — an anti-pattern for risk identification. (C) override by PM authority is the opposite error. (D) excluding the junior from the follow-up is disrespectful and signals their input isn't welcome.
