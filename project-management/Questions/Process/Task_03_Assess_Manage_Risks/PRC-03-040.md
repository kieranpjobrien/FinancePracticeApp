# Question

The team has become complacent about risk management - reviews are perfunctory, the register is outdated, and no one takes it seriously. What should the PM do to reinvigorate risk management?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that risk management doesn't work for this team
- **B**: Reconnect risk management to real project outcomes, streamline the process, demonstrate value when risks are successfully managed, and involve the team in process improvement
- **C**: Create more detailed risk procedures
- **D**: Remove risk management from the project

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Complacency often stems from process being disconnected from value. Reconnect: show how risk management helps the project (crises averted, early warning). Streamline: perhaps the process is too bureaucratic - simplify. Demonstrate: when a risk is successfully managed, highlight it. Involve: let the team help improve the process they'll use.

Option A gives up on valuable practices.

Option C may increase bureaucratic burden that caused complacency.

Option D removes important project protection.
