# Question

Post-project review reveals that several risks materialised that weren't on the risk register. The team missed them entirely during identification. What should be done to improve future risk identification?

## Options

- **A**: Punish the team for missing risks
- **B**: Document the missed risks as lessons learned, analyse why they were missed (assumptions, blind spots, lack of expertise), and improve identification techniques for future projects
- **C**: Accept that some risks can't be identified
- **D**: Create longer risk workshops

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Turn failure into learning. Document: what risks materialised that weren't identified? Why?: were there assumptions, blind spots, or missing expertise? Improve: adjust identification techniques - broader participation, assumption analysis, external review, historical data. Some unknown unknowns are inevitable, but patterns in missed risks indicate improvable processes.

Option A discourages future risk identification.

Option C doesn't pursue available improvements.

Option D assumes duration is the issue without diagnosis.
