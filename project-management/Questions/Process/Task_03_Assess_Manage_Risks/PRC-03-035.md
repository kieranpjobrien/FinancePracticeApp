# Question

Halfway through a project, quantitative analysis shows cumulative project EMV has grown from $120,000 to $210,000 due to new risks. The contingency reserve is $150,000 and the risk tolerance threshold is 'reserves must equal or exceed EMV'. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adjust the EMV calculation methodology to bring the number within tolerance.
- **B**: Accept the gap since the project is halfway done and some risks will expire without occurring.
- **C**: Immediately transfer reserve from another project in the portfolio.
- **D**: Raise a change request to either increase reserves by $60,000 or implement additional risk responses that reduce EMV back within tolerance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When a documented tolerance is breached, PMI expects a formal change request — either bring reserves up or bring exposure down. (A) manipulating methodology to match a target is unethical and violates data integrity. (B) passive acceptance ignores a documented threshold that exists specifically to trigger action. (C) inter-project resource moves require portfolio-level change control, not unilateral PM action.
