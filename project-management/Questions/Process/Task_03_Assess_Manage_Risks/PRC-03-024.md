# Question

A team member identifies a potential risk but is reluctant to raise it because they worry it will be seen as negative or that they'll be assigned to manage it. How should the PM create an environment that encourages risk identification?

## Options

- **A**: Make risk identification mandatory and punish those who don't contribute
- **B**: Celebrate risk identification, don't automatically assign identifiers to manage risks, create psychological safety for raising concerns, and demonstrate that early identification is valued
- **C**: Tell the team member to raise it anyway
- **D**: Identify all risks yourself so the team doesn't need to

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Risk identification culture requires psychological safety. Celebrate: recognise people who identify risks early. Don't punish identifiers: assign risk owners based on expertise, not who found the risk. Safety: make it clear that raising risks is valued, not criticism. Model: respond positively when risks are raised. This encourages proactive risk identification.

Option A uses punishment which suppresses identification.

Option C doesn't address the underlying cultural issue.

Option D misses the value of diverse perspectives and creates single point of failure.
