# Question

The PM suspects a team member is hiding a risk because they fear being blamed or assigned more work. The PM has indirect evidence but no proof. How should this be handled?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the team member with accusations
- **B**: Create an environment where raising risks is safe, have a private conversation about any concerns they might have, and reinforce that early identification is valued not punished
- **C**: Ignore the suspicion since there's no proof
- **D**: Report the team member to HR

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Address the suspected behaviour indirectly. Safe environment: reinforce that risk identification is valued, not punished. Private conversation: "Are there any concerns about the project you haven't had a chance to raise?" Opening the door without accusation. Investigate gently: look for evidence through normal project monitoring. This addresses the issue without damaging the relationship.

Option A is confrontational without proof.

Option C ignores potential hidden risk.

Option D is premature and may be unfounded.
