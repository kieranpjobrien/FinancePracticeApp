# Question

A project manager is running a programme-level risk review and notices that two of the three subprojects have identified the same underlying risk but with very different probability and impact ratings. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Average the two sets of ratings to produce a programme-level view.
- **B**: Use the more conservative (higher) rating since it protects the programme.
- **C**: Keep the ratings separate at subproject level since each project has its own context.
- **D**: Bring the two subproject teams together to align on assumptions, data, and context before producing a programme-level rating.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Different ratings for the same risk usually reflect different assumptions or data access. PMI expects alignment through dialogue before rolling up to programme level. (A) averaging without understanding why they differ produces a number neither team trusts. (B) the conservative rating may overstate the risk and misallocate reserves. (C) leaving them separate dodges the programme-level question and prevents consistent response. Alignment first, then roll-up.
