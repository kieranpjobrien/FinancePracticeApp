# Question

The PM of a regulated pharma project is told by the sponsor to remove a low-probability compliance risk from the register because it 'shouldn't worry auditors'. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the risk as requested since the sponsor has authority over register content.
- **B**: Reclassify the risk as an assumption rather than a risk to satisfy the sponsor.
- **C**: Decline to remove the risk, explain that the register must reflect identified compliance exposure, and offer to add context about current mitigation status.
- **D**: Remove the risk from the main register and maintain a private copy for the PM's own tracking.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Compliance risks cannot be removed for optics — that compromises integrity and potentially violates regulatory record-keeping. PMI's ethics standard requires honest reporting. (A) ceding register integrity to sponsor preference. (B) reclassification to hide a risk is the same violation under a different label. (D) a shadow register is deceptive and unprofessional. The correct response is a respectful, clear explanation with a constructive alternative (context on mitigation).
