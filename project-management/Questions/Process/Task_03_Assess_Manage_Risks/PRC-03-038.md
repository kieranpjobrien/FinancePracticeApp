# Question

The project schedule has no slack - every task is on the critical path. From a risk perspective, what concern should the PM raise?

## Options

- **A**: No concern - a tight schedule is efficient
- **B**: The schedule is extremely vulnerable to delays since any task delay directly impacts project completion. Recommend building buffers or identifying tasks where crashing could recover delays.
- **C**: Critical path is unrelated to risk
- **D**: Eliminate the critical path

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A schedule with no slack (zero float on all paths) is highly fragile. Any delay anywhere becomes a project delay. This is a significant risk. Consider: building schedule buffers (contingency), identifying tasks that could be crashed if needed, or accepting the risk with eyes open. The schedule itself creates risk.

Option A ignores the risk of zero slack.

Option C misses the schedule-risk connection.

Option D is conceptually impossible - every schedule has a critical path.
