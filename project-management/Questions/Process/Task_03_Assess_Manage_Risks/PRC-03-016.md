# Question

During qualitative risk analysis, two stakeholders disagree sharply on the probability rating for a supplier-delay risk. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Average the two ratings and document the compromise value in the risk register.
- **B**: Facilitate a discussion to surface the underlying assumptions each stakeholder is using, then agree on a rating with documented rationale.
- **C**: Defer to the stakeholder with more seniority or domain authority.
- **D**: Record both ratings separately and let the risk owner choose during response planning.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Qualitative risk analysis depends on shared understanding of the underlying assumptions. Disagreement usually signals that stakeholders are working from different data, not that one is wrong. (B) resolves the gap and produces a defensible rating. (A) mathematical averaging hides the real disagreement and produces a rating no one believes. (C) defers to authority rather than evidence, undermining the analysis. (D) kicks the problem to response planning, which then starts from ambiguous inputs.
