# Question

During sprint planning, the team identifies that a user story depends on an external API that has been unreliable in the past. How should this risk be handled in an agile context?

## Options

- **A**: Create a detailed risk register entry and wait for approval
- **B**: Discuss the risk in planning, identify contingencies (mock API, alternative approach), and factor the uncertainty into story pointing and sprint commitment
- **C**: Ignore it and hope the API works this time
- **D**: Remove the story from the sprint backlog permanently

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In agile, risk management is lightweight but continuous. Discuss openly: team members raise and address risks in ceremonies. Contingency: what if the API fails? Can we mock it? Is there an alternative? Factor in: perhaps the story needs more points to account for uncertainty, or the team commits to fewer stories to maintain buffer.

Option A is overly formal for agile context.

Option C ignores a known risk.

Option D removes potentially valuable work without exploring solutions.
