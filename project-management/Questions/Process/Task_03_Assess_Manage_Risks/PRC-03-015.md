# Question

A project has identified a high-impact risk related to key personnel leaving mid-project. The identified mitigation is to document their knowledge and cross-train others. However, management won't approve time for cross-training because of deadline pressure. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that the risk cannot be mitigated
- **B**: Quantify the risk in business terms (probability of departure, impact on project cost/schedule if it occurs) and present the business case for cross-training as an investment against potential larger losses
- **C**: Cross-train in secret without management approval
- **D**: Don't mention the risk again

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When mitigation is rejected, help decision-makers understand the trade-off. Quantify: what's the probability of departure? What's the cost if it happens (delays, hiring, knowledge loss)? Compare this to the cost of cross-training. Present: help management make an informed decision by understanding the risk they're accepting.

Option A doesn't advocate for the project.

Option C circumvents management authority.

Option D leaves a significant risk unaddressed.
