# Question

Midway through a 12-month hybrid project, the environment has shifted significantly: a key vendor has been acquired, and two new regulations have been announced. The risk register reflects the original planning assumptions. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a risk reassessment workshop with the team and key stakeholders to identify new risks and reassess existing ones.
- **B**: Ask each team lead to submit individual updates to the risk register by end of sprint.
- **C**: Wait for the next scheduled risk review to incorporate these changes alongside others.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor and request formal guidance on how to handle the environmental changes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats the risk register as a living document that must be updated when the environment changes materially. A reassessment workshop brings diverse perspectives and identifies risks individuals would miss. (B) fragments the analysis and loses the benefit of group risk identification techniques. (C) delays response to known environmental changes — PMI expects continuous risk management, not calendar-driven reviews only. (D) escalates before the PM has done the work; the sponsor expects a proposed response, not an open question.
