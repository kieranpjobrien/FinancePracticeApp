# Question

A PM realises three months in that a critical planning assumption — that the legal team would provide contract templates within two weeks — was never tested and has become a major schedule risk. Two other assumptions may be similarly untested. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise only the contract templates as a risk because it is confirmed material, and continue monitoring the other two informally.
- **B**: Rewrite the project plan with extended timelines to absorb all three assumption risks regardless of actual likelihood or impact.
- **C**: Assign ownership to the sponsor for validating all remaining project assumptions since planning was originally the sponsor's responsibility.
- **D**: Review the full assumption log, test each remaining assumption against reality, convert any failed ones into risks or issues.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Assumption analysis is a core risk identification technique; when one assumption fails, a disciplined PM revisits them all. (D) applies the technique systematically. (A) leaving two known untested assumptions uninspected is precisely the gap that caused the current problem. (B) padding timelines without analysis hides the underlying issue. (C) assumption validation is the PM's responsibility, not a delegation to the sponsor.
