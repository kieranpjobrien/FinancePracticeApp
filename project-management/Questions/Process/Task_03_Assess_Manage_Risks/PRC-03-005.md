# Question

What is a positive risk (opportunity) in project management?

## Options

- **A**: A risk that has a low probability of occurring
- **B**: An uncertain event that, if it occurs, would have a positive effect on project objectives
- **C**: A risk that has been successfully mitigated
- **D**: A risk that stakeholders feel good about

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A positive risk or opportunity is an uncertain future event that would benefit the project if it occurred - perhaps completing early, under budget, or with better quality. Just like negative risks (threats), opportunities should be identified, assessed, and have response strategies.

Option A describes probability level, not risk direction.

Option C describes a mitigated threat, not an opportunity.

Option D confuses stakeholder feelings with the nature of the risk itself.
