# Question

What is a positive risk (opportunity) in PMI risk management?

## Options

- **A**: A risk that has been mitigated to the point where it can no longer harm the project.
- **B**: A risk whose impact is small enough to be ignored at the project's risk tolerance threshold.
- **C**: A risk where stakeholders have a positive emotional reaction to its occurrence.
- **D**: An uncertain future event that, if it occurs, would have a beneficial effect on one or more project objectives.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI defines risk symmetrically: an uncertain event that, if it occurs, has a positive or negative effect. A positive risk is an opportunity. (D) is the standard definition. (A) describes a successfully mitigated threat. (B) describes a low-impact risk regardless of direction. (C) confuses stakeholder feeling with the risk's effect on objectives.
