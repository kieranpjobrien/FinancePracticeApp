# Question

An agile team uses a risk-adjusted backlog where high-risk items are addressed early. The product owner wants to delay a technically risky feature to later sprints because stakeholders prefer seeing business features first. What should the Scrum Master advise?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the product owner's prioritisation
- **B**: Explain that addressing technical risks early reduces overall project risk and allows time for course correction
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor to override the product owner
- **D**: Let the team secretly work on the risky item anyway

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Scrum Master should educate on risk management principles. Addressing high-risk items early ("fail fast") allows the team to discover problems when there's time to respond. Delaying risky work to later sprints means problems surface when options are limited and stakes are higher.

Option A (defer completely) fails to provide coaching on agile risk management practices.

Option C (escalate) undermines the PO and doesn't try to resolve through education first.

Option D (work secretly) violates transparency and the PO's backlog ownership. It's deceptive and damages trust.
