# Question

A critical risk response requires $40,000 that is not currently in the cost baseline. The PM has a $60,000 contingency reserve allocated to the work package affected. What is the correct action?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a change request to the change control board for additional funding.
- **B**: Notify the sponsor that the project cannot absorb the cost and request approval to proceed.
- **C**: Draw the $40,000 from the contingency reserve, document the use, and update the risk register.
- **D**: Defer the response until the next budget review and track the risk as accepted.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Contingency reserves are pre-allocated funds for identified risks within the PM's authority. Using them does not require change control because they are part of the cost baseline specifically for this purpose. (A) is wrong because CCB approval isn't needed for contingency reserve use — that's what the reserve is for. (B) unnecessarily escalates when the reserve exists to cover this. (D) passively accepts a critical risk rather than funding the response that is already resourced.
