# Question

The project risk register has grown to over 200 risks. The team is overwhelmed and risk review meetings take hours. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue reviewing all risks in each meeting
- **B**: Prioritise risks by severity, focus attention on top risks, retire low-priority or expired risks, and use tiered review frequencies based on priority
- **C**: Delete most risks to make the register manageable
- **D**: Stop holding risk review meetings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Risk registers can become unwieldy. Prioritise: focus on high probability/impact risks. Retire: close risks that are no longer relevant or have passed their trigger point. Tiered review: high risks reviewed frequently, low risks less often. This maintains effective risk management without drowning in administrative overhead.

Option A is inefficient and leads to review fatigue.

Option C may delete relevant risks without proper evaluation.

Option D abandons risk management entirely.
