# Question

The project is implementing a secondary risk - a new risk that arises as a result of implementing a risk response. The mitigation for the original risk created a new dependency that itself is risky. How should this be handled?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore secondary risks since they're side effects
- **B**: Document the secondary risk, assess it using the same process, and ensure the combined risk exposure (original residual risk plus secondary risk) is acceptable
- **C**: Remove the original risk mitigation to eliminate the secondary risk
- **D**: Blame whoever implemented the mitigation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Secondary risks are real risks requiring management. Document: add to the risk register. Assess: evaluate probability and impact. Evaluate trade-offs: is the combined exposure (residual original risk + secondary risk) better than the original unmitigated risk? If not, consider alternative mitigations.

Option A ignores a real risk.

Option C may restore worse original exposure.

Option D focuses on blame rather than management.
