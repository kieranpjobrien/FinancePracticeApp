# Question

A project team has identified a risk with high probability, high impact, and a clear trigger condition. The team proposes an aggressive mitigation that requires reallocating two developers from a critical feature. The sponsor is pushing to hold feature scope. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the mitigation immediately because high probability plus high impact requires action regardless of scope.
- **B**: Defer mitigation and escalate the risk to the sponsor to let them decide the trade-off unilaterally.
- **C**: Accept the risk since the sponsor has signalled that scope takes priority over risk reduction.
- **D**: Present the sponsor with the quantified trade-off — residual risk if scope is held versus feature impact if developers are reallocated — and seek a decision.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to frame trade-offs quantitatively and let the sponsor make informed decisions, not default to one side. (A) acts on risk without acknowledging scope impact the sponsor has prioritised. (B) escalates without providing the analysis the sponsor needs to decide well. (C) accepts the risk implicitly by reading the sponsor's preference, without surfacing the actual cost of acceptance. The PM's job is to present options with data.
