# Question

A vendor has submitted a proposal with a fixed price significantly lower than competitors. This creates cost savings opportunity but also raises concerns about their ability to deliver. How should the PM approach this risk-opportunity combination?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the lowest price without due diligence
- **B**: Perform due diligence on vendor capability, understand why their price is lower, include appropriate contract protections, and have contingency if they fail to deliver
- **C**: Automatically reject unusually low bids
- **D**: Award to a more expensive vendor to avoid risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Low price is both opportunity and risk. Due diligence: can they actually deliver at that price? Why is it lower - efficiency, or understaffing/quality cuts? Contract protections: milestones, performance bonds, quality requirements. Contingency: what if they fail? This captures the opportunity while managing the associated risk.

Option A ignores significant delivery risk.

Option C may reject genuinely efficient vendors.

Option D foregoes real opportunity without exploring risk management.
