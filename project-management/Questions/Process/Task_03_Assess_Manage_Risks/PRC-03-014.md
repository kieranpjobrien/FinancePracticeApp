# Question

What are the four common risk response strategies for negative risks (threats)?

## Options

- **A**: Accept, ignore, complain, defer
- **B**: Avoid, transfer, mitigate, accept
- **C**: Eliminate, reduce, share, embrace
- **D**: Plan, do, check, act

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The four strategies for threats are: Avoid (eliminate the threat entirely), Transfer (shift responsibility to a third party, e.g., insurance), Mitigate (reduce probability or impact), Accept (acknowledge the risk without active response). The choice depends on risk severity and cost-effectiveness of responses.

Option A includes inappropriate responses like "ignore" and "complain."

Option C uses non-standard terminology.

Option D is the PDCA cycle, not risk response strategies.
