# Question

A project manager discovers that a vendor's financial health has deteriorated — a credit rating downgrade was reported last week. The vendor is delivering a critical component in three months. What is the BEST action?

## Options

- **A**: Terminate the vendor contract immediately to avoid being caught by a vendor failure.
- **B**: Continue monitoring and wait for an actual delivery problem before acting.
- **C**: Add the risk to the register, develop a contingency plan, and increase monitoring cadence.
- **D**: Notify the sponsor and ask for direction on how to handle the vendor.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A material change in a vendor's financial position is a classic new risk — it must be logged, analysed, and have a response planned, including a viable contingency. (A) immediate termination may breach contract and damages an active delivery without evidence of failure. (B) passive monitoring of a known threat is exactly what PMI warns against. (D) escalates before the PM has done the analysis that would make the escalation useful.
