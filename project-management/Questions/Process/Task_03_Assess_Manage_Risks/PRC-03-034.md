# Question

Stakeholders are demanding a "zero risk" project. The PM knows this is impossible but needs to manage expectations. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Promise zero risk to keep stakeholders happy
- **B**: Explain that zero risk is impossible, describe the risk management approach being used, identify the residual risk level that's being accepted, and help stakeholders understand risk-return trade-offs
- **C**: Refuse to discuss risk with stakeholders
- **D**: Add infinite contingency budget

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Educate stakeholders on risk reality. Zero risk is impossible - even "safe" options have risks. Explain the approach: how risks are identified, assessed, and managed. Residual risk: what level remains after mitigation? Trade-offs: lower risk often means higher cost or longer time. Help stakeholders make informed decisions about acceptable risk levels.

Option A makes impossible promises.

Option C avoids necessary stakeholder engagement.

Option D is impractical and doesn't educate stakeholders.
