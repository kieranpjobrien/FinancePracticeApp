# Question

What is a probability and impact matrix used for in risk management?

## Options

- **A**: To calculate project budget
- **B**: To prioritise risks by plotting their probability of occurrence against their potential impact
- **C**: To assign resources to tasks
- **D**: To create the project schedule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A probability and impact matrix (or risk matrix) is a tool for prioritising risks. Risks are assessed for probability (likelihood of occurrence) and impact (severity if they occur). Plotting these dimensions identifies high-priority risks (high probability and/or high impact) requiring the most attention.

Options A, C, and D describe other project management tools, not the risk matrix.
