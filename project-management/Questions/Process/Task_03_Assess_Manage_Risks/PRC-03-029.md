# Question

What is the difference between contingency reserve and management reserve?

## Options

- **A**: Contingency reserves cover schedule risks; management reserves cover cost risks.
- **B**: Contingency reserves are for known risks and sit within the cost baseline; management reserves are for unknown risks and sit outside it.
- **C**: Contingency reserves require sponsor approval to use; management reserves are controlled by the PM.
- **D**: Contingency reserves are set as a percentage of the budget; management reserves are set as a fixed amount.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI is specific: contingency reserve is allocated for identified (known) risks within the PM's authority and is part of the cost baseline; management reserve is for unforeseen work (unknown unknowns), sits outside the baseline, and requires management approval to access. (A) invents a schedule-vs-cost division. (C) inverts the authority — contingency is PM-controlled, management reserve needs approval. (D) is a funding-method distinction that isn't the definitional difference.
