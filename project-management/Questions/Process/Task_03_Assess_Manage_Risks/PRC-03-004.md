# Question

A risk that was identified and accepted during planning has now occurred. The impact is within the range that was estimated. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor since a risk has materialized
- **B**: Execute the contingency plan that was prepared for this risk
- **C**: Conduct a new risk assessment to determine response
- **D**: Add the impact to the project budget variance report

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A properly accepted risk should have a contingency plan (what to do IF the risk occurs). Now that it has occurred, execute that plan. This is exactly what risk planning prepares for - having responses ready so you can act quickly when risks materialize.

Option A is unnecessary for an expected risk within estimated parameters. Escalation is for unexpected events or impacts beyond planning.

Option C delays action. The assessment was done during planning - now is the time to act, not reassess.

Option D treats the impact as a surprise variance rather than a planned contingency draw-down.
