# Question

A mitigation action for a critical risk has failed, and the risk has materialised. The contingency plan is now being executed, but it's not fully addressing the impact. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the failing contingency plan
- **B**: Escalate immediately, assess if additional actions or resources are needed, consider invoking management reserve, and communicate impact to stakeholders
- **C**: Hide the situation until it's resolved
- **D**: Stop the project immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When planned responses are insufficient, escalate and adapt. Escalate: decision-makers need to know the situation. Assess: what additional actions might help? Management reserve: may be needed to fund additional response. Communicate: stakeholders need to understand the impact. This enables informed decision-making and resource allocation.

Option A continues with an ineffective approach.

Option C hides important information from decision-makers.

Option D may be premature without exploring additional responses.
