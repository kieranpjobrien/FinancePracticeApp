# Question

The project is in an industry with rapidly changing regulations. New rules could significantly affect the project at any time, but the specific changes and timing are unpredictable. How should the PM approach this risk?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore it since it's unpredictable
- **B**: Establish a monitoring process for regulatory changes, build flexibility into the solution architecture where possible, plan for potential pivots, and maintain relationships with regulatory bodies
- **C**: Wait until regulations change to respond
- **D**: Complete the project quickly before regulations change

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Unpredictable risks require resilience rather than specific mitigation. Monitor: track regulatory developments to get early warning. Build flexibility: design systems that can adapt to various regulatory scenarios. Plan for pivots: know how you'll respond to different regulatory directions. Relationships: maintain connections to get early information.

Option A ignores a significant risk.

Option C is purely reactive without preparation.

Option D may not be feasible and rushes quality.
