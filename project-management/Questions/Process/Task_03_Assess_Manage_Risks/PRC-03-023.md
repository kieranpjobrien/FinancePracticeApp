# Question

A project manager realises the organisation's risk appetite, documented at the programme level, conflicts with the risk tolerance a key stakeholder has expressed for this specific project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Default to the programme-level risk appetite since it governs all projects in the programme.
- **B**: Apply the stakeholder's tolerance because they have decision authority on this project.
- **C**: Average the two positions and document the result in the risk management plan.
- **D**: Raise the conflict with the sponsor and seek an agreed position before finalising the risk management plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Conflicts between organisational risk appetite and stakeholder tolerance must be resolved before the risk management plan is finalised, because responses, thresholds, and reserves all flow from that position. (A) ignores legitimate stakeholder input and may produce a plan the stakeholder rejects later. (B) overrides organisational governance; a single stakeholder does not override programme-level appetite unilaterally. (C) arithmetic averaging of qualitative positions produces a plan neither party agrees with.
