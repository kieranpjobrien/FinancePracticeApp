# Question

A vendor contract has been signed that transfers significant project risk to the vendor. The PM is now less concerned about this risk area. What should the PM ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the risk from the register since it's transferred
- **B**: Monitor the vendor's ability to manage the transferred risk, maintain contractual oversight, and have contingency if the vendor fails to manage the risk
- **C**: Completely forget about the risk
- **D**: Assume the contract fully protects the project

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Risk transfer doesn't mean risk disappearance. Monitor: is the vendor actually managing the risk effectively? Oversight: ensure contract terms are being met. Contingency: if the vendor fails to manage the risk, the project may still be impacted. Risk transfer reduces but doesn't eliminate the PM's responsibility for the risk area.

Option A removes oversight when monitoring is still needed.

Option C ignores residual risk.

Option D overestimates contractual protection.
