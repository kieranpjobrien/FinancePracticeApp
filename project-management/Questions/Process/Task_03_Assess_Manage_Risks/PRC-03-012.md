# Question

A risk previously rated as low probability and low impact has materialised and disrupted the current sprint. The team has no pre-defined response. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the entire risk register to identify other underestimated risks before taking action.
- **B**: Convene the team to implement a workaround, then update the risk register and response plan.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor because the risk was unplanned and exceeds the team's authority.
- **D**: Trigger the management reserve to fund a formal risk response development effort.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When an unplanned risk materialises, PMI expects the PM to apply a workaround — an unplanned response to a negative risk event — and then update the risk register. (B) addresses the realised issue quickly while feeding learnings back into the process. (A) delays response to review documentation while the project is actively being disrupted. (C) escalates prematurely; the PM should first attempt to manage it at their level. (D) jumps to funding before a response is even defined, and management reserve requires change control to access.
