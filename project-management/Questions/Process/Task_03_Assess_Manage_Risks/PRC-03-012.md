# Question

A risk that was previously assessed as low probability has just occurred. The team didn't have a detailed response plan because the risk was considered unlikely. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Blame the team for poor risk assessment
- **B**: Quickly develop a response, contain the impact, document lessons learned, and review risk assessment criteria for similar risks
- **C**: Ignore it since it wasn't planned for
- **D**: Stop the project until the risk is fully analysed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When risks materialise, immediate response is needed regardless of planning status. Develop response: address the immediate situation. Contain: limit damage where possible. Lessons learned: why was probability assessment off? Review: should similar risks be reassessed? This addresses both the immediate issue and improves future risk management.

Option A focuses on blame rather than response.

Option C ignores a real problem affecting the project.

Option D may be excessive depending on the risk impact.
