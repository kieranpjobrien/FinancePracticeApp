# Question

A project team identifies a risk that a key vendor might go bankrupt during the project. The probability is low (10%) but the impact would be severe (6-month delay). What risk response strategy is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the risk since probability is low
- **B**: Avoid the risk by eliminating the vendor dependency entirely
- **C**: Transfer the risk by requiring performance bonds and identifying backup vendors
- **D**: Mitigate the risk by reducing the project timeline

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. For low-probability, high-impact risks, transfer and contingency planning are appropriate. Performance bonds provide financial protection if the vendor fails. Identifying backup vendors (contingency) ensures the project can continue if the risk materialises. This manages both the financial and schedule impacts.

Option A (accept) may be inappropriate given the severe impact. Even low-probability catastrophic risks warrant active management.

Option B (avoid) may not be practical if the vendor provides unique capabilities. Avoidance should consider feasibility and cost.

Option D (mitigate by reducing timeline) doesn't address this specific risk. Faster execution doesn't protect against vendor bankruptcy.
