# Question

A specialist vendor on a 12-month project has a 10% probability of becoming insolvent during delivery. The vendor's failure would delay the project by six months. The vendor provides niche capability not available from internal staff. What is the most appropriate risk response?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the risk because the probability is low and active management is unlikely to be cost-effective.
- **B**: Avoid the risk by removing the vendor from the project and developing the capability in-house from scratch.
- **C**: Mitigate and partially transfer: require a performance bond, qualify a backup vendor, and hold escrow of critical IP.
- **D**: Mitigate by compressing the project schedule so the work is finished before the vendor could plausibly fail.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

For a low-probability, high-impact risk on critical-path work, layered mitigation plus partial transfer (bond, backup vendor, escrow) is the standard PMI response. (C) does this. (A) under-manages a catastrophic-impact risk. (B) is impractical when the vendor provides niche capability and abandons partial-transfer options. (D) doesn't address the actual risk — a vendor can fail at any point, including before completion.
