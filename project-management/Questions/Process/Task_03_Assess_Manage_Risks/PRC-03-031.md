# Question

A project risk assessment identifies that the single biggest risk is reliance on a technology the team has never used before. The sponsor wants to proceed despite the risk. What should the PM ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to proceed without eliminating the risk
- **B**: Document the risk clearly, implement robust mitigation (training, experts, proof of concept), establish early indicators of trouble, ensure the sponsor makes an informed decision, and build appropriate contingency
- **C**: Proceed without additional measures since the sponsor approved
- **D**: Guarantee the risk won't materialise

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. If proceeding with significant risk, do so with eyes open and appropriate measures. Document: ensure the decision is recorded. Mitigate: training, hiring expertise, conducting proof of concept to reduce probability. Early indicators: establish triggers that signal trouble early. Informed decision: sponsor should understand what they're accepting. Contingency: plan and budget for potential issues.

Option A may refuse acceptable risk.

Option C proceeds without adequate risk management.

Option D makes impossible promises.
