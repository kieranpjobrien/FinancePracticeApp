# Question

What is a risk audit?

## Options

- **A**: An external financial audit
- **B**: A structured review to evaluate the effectiveness of risk management processes and identify areas for improvement
- **C**: Counting the number of risks in the register
- **D**: A legal review of contract risks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A risk audit examines how well risk management is working. It evaluates: Are risks being identified effectively? Are responses working? Is the process being followed? What can be improved? Risk audits may be conducted by the PM, PMO, or external auditors, depending on project governance.

Option A is financial audit.

Option C is simple counting, not audit.

Option D is specifically about contracts, not general risk management effectiveness.
