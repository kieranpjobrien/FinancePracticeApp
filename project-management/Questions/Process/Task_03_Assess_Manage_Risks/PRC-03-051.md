# Question

A PM is reviewing a risk register with 45 entries. The team's probability-impact scoring has flagged 22 as "high". The sponsor is overwhelmed and says the register has become noise rather than signal. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Recalibrate probability-impact thresholds, reassess the full register, and clearly separate top risks from important-but-monitored risks in reporting to the sponsor going forward.
- **B**: Archive the 35 lowest-rated risks to simplify the register and focus only on the current top-scoring ten risks for reporting now.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor which risks they find most useful and keep only those visible in the summary reports going forward each month.
- **D**: Split the register into one thematic register per workstream so each area has a more manageable number of risks to track actively.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When everything is "high" the scoring itself is broken; PMI-aligned response is to recalibrate thresholds and restore signal. (A) fixes root cause. (B) archiving without recalibrating can hide real risks. (C) sponsor preference isn't a risk-scoring methodology. (D) splitting the register creates coordination overhead without fixing the underlying threshold problem.
