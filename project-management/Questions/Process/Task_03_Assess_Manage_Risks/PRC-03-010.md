# Question

What is a secondary risk?

## Options

- **A**: A risk that has low priority
- **B**: A risk that arises as a direct result of implementing a risk response
- **C**: A risk identified after the risk register is completed
- **D**: A risk that affects only secondary stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A secondary risk is a new risk that emerges from implementing a risk response. For example, if you mitigate a resource risk by hiring contractors, a secondary risk might be knowledge loss when contractors leave. Secondary risks should be identified and managed like any other risk.

Option A (low priority) describes risk ranking, not secondary risks.

Option C (identified later) describes a newly identified risk, which could be primary or secondary depending on its cause.

Option D (secondary stakeholders) conflates unrelated concepts. Risk classification isn't based on stakeholder tiers.
