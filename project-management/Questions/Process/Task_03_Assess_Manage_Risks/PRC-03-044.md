# Question

A risk that was 'accepted' six months ago has now grown in probability due to environmental changes. The original acceptance documentation remains in the register. What is the BEST action?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the risk and, if it now exceeds acceptance criteria, change the response and update the register.
- **B**: Leave the acceptance in place since the response strategy was formally agreed at project outset.
- **C**: Transfer the risk to a vendor via an emergency procurement to shift exposure off the project.
- **D**: Escalate the risk to the sponsor because changing any response always requires formal sponsor approval.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Acceptance is conditional on the risk staying within the accepted range. When conditions change, the response must be reassessed. (B) treats the original decision as permanent despite changed facts. (C) jumps to a specific new response (transfer) without analysing whether it's the best option. (D) overstates the authority needed; the PM can reassess and propose a new response, escalating only if the new response exceeds their authority.
