# Question

The project is using iterative development. How should risk management be adapted for this approach compared to predictive projects?

## Options

- **A**: Risk management isn't needed in iterative projects
- **B**: Integrate risk discussions into iteration ceremonies, use the backlog to capture risk-related work, address highest risks in early iterations, and adjust risk assessments as learning occurs
- **C**: Use exactly the same approach as predictive projects
- **D**: Only identify risks at the project start

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Iterative projects adapt risk management to their cadence. Ceremonies: discuss risks in planning, reviews, retrospectives. Backlog: risk mitigation activities can be backlog items. Early iterations: tackle highest-risk items first while there's time to adapt. Continuous adjustment: reassess risks as you learn each iteration. This integrates risk management with agile rhythm.

Option A ignores the need for risk management.

Option C doesn't leverage iterative benefits.

Option D misses opportunities for ongoing risk identification.
