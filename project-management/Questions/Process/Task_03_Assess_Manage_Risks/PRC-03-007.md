# Question

What is the role of a risk trigger in risk management?

## Options

- **A**: It is the categorisation of a risk by source, such as technical, schedule, or organisational.
- **B**: It is the underlying root cause that gives rise to a particular risk.
- **C**: It is the residual risk that remains after a response has been implemented.
- **D**: It is an early warning indicator that a risk is about to occur or has just occurred, signalling the team to execute the response.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A trigger is the leading indicator that activates a planned response. (D) captures both purpose and use. (A) describes risk categorisation. (B) describes the cause of a risk, not the trigger. (C) describes residual risk, a different concept altogether.
