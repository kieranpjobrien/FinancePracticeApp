# Question

What is the difference between a risk trigger and a risk response?

## Options

- **A**: A trigger is the risk itself; a response is what happens after
- **B**: A trigger is an early warning sign that a risk may occur; a response is the planned action to address the risk
- **C**: A trigger is the probability; a response is the impact
- **D**: There is no difference - they are the same thing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A risk trigger (or warning sign) is an indicator that a risk event is about to occur or has occurred. Identifying triggers helps teams recognise when to implement risk responses. A risk response is the planned action taken to address a risk - such as avoid, mitigate, transfer, or accept.

Option A confuses triggers with risk events. The trigger signals the risk; it's not the risk itself.

Option C confuses triggers with probability and responses with impact. These are separate risk attributes used in assessment.

Option D is incorrect - triggers and responses serve distinct purposes in risk management.
