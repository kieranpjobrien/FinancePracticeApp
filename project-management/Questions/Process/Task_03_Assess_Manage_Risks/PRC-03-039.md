# Question

A Monte Carlo simulation shows the project has only a 40% probability of meeting its committed deadline. Stakeholders were told the deadline was achievable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hide the analysis since it contradicts commitments
- **B**: Present the analysis to stakeholders, explain the methodology and key risk drivers, and discuss options to increase success probability (scope reduction, additional resources, extended timeline)
- **C**: Run the simulation again hoping for better results
- **D**: Ignore quantitative analysis in favour of optimism

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quantitative analysis provides important information for decision-makers. Present findings: 40% success probability is a significant concern. Explain: what risks are driving this? What assumptions were made? Options: what would it take to increase probability - reduce scope? Add resources? Extend deadline? This enables informed decisions rather than discovering problems later.

Option A hides critical decision-making information.

Option C doesn't change underlying reality.

Option D ignores data in favour of wishful thinking.
