# Question

What is the PRIMARY purpose of obtaining formal acceptance at project closure?

## Options

- **A**: To trigger the start of the warranty period for the delivered product.
- **B**: To authorise final payments to the project team and vendors.
- **C**: To free the project manager from any post-closure responsibility for the deliverables.
- **D**: To confirm that the customer or sponsor agrees the deliverables meet acceptance criteria, formally completing the project's scope obligations.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Formal acceptance is the customer or sponsor's confirmation that scope obligations have been met against the agreed acceptance criteria, which is what allows the project to close. (A) is often a downstream effect of acceptance but is not its purpose. (B) payments may be tied to acceptance contractually but are again a consequence, not the underlying reason. (C) is incorrect: the PM may still have transition, warranty, or reporting obligations after acceptance.
