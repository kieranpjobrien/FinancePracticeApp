# Question

At closure of a predictive construction project, the PM finds warranty terms in three vendor contracts extend 24 months past project closure. Operations has no process to track vendor warranty obligations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project on time and trust operations to discover the warranty terms themselves if any in-warranty defect actually occurs during the following two-year window.
- **B**: Extend the project formally for two more years until every warranty has expired so that warranty management remains within the project's accountability.
- **C**: Summarise the warranty terms in an email to the operations director and ask them to handle tracking through whatever internal mechanism they feel is appropriate.
- **D**: Document each warranty's scope, duration, claim process, and contact, transfer the register to an accountable operations owner, and verify their acceptance before closing.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI procurement closure requires that ongoing contractual obligations transfer with clear accountability, not be left implicit. (D) preserves the organisation's rights through a proper handover to a named owner. (A) lets valuable warranty coverage go unclaimed. (B) keeps a project alive beyond its actual work. (C) passes the problem without structure or acceptance.
