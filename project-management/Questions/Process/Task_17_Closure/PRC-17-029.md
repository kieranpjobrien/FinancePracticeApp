# Question

During closure, the project manager discovers that some deliverables were informally accepted by end users but never formally signed off by the designated accepting authority. The deliverables have been in use for months. What should be done?

## Options

- **A**: Consider them accepted since they're in use
- **B**: Halt their use until formal acceptance is obtained
- **C**: Obtain formal acceptance retroactively - document what was delivered, confirm it meets acceptance criteria, and obtain official sign-off to close out properly
- **D**: Leave acceptance incomplete since the project is closing

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Formal acceptance should be obtained even if late. Document the deliverables, verify they meet criteria (their successful use suggests they do), and obtain official sign-off. This completes the formal record while acknowledging the practical reality that the deliverables are already providing value.

Option A leaves formal records incomplete.

Option B is impractical and disruptive when deliverables are working.

Option D leaves the project formally incomplete.
