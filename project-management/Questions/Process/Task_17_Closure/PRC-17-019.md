# Question

A Scrum team has been releasing increments to production every two weeks for nine months. The product owner has decided the backlog is complete and the project should close. How does closure differ from a predictive project of the same size?

## Options

- **A**: Agile projects do not require formal closure because acceptance happens at each sprint review with the product owner.
- **B**: Closure is identical, since formal acceptance, contract closure, and archiving are required regardless of delivery methodology.
- **C**: Incremental acceptance lightens final acceptance, but contracts, consolidated lessons, resource release, and archiving still apply.
- **D**: Only the product owner needs to approve closure, since they accepted each increment as the project progressed over nine months.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI recognises that agile changes the shape of closure but not its existence. (C) is accurate: incremental acceptance reduces the surface area of final acceptance, but formal closure obligations such as contracts, archives, lessons consolidation, and resource release still apply. (A) skips required closure obligations entirely. (B) ignores the legitimate differences agile creates in how acceptance accumulates. (D) limits closure approval inappropriately; sponsors, procurement, and PMO governance still apply.
