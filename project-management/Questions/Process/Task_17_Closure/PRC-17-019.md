# Question

An agile project has been incrementally delivering value throughout the project. How does closure differ from traditional projects?

## Options

- **A**: Agile projects don't need formal closure
- **B**: Closure is identical to traditional projects
- **C**: While incremental delivery may reduce the magnitude of final closure activities, formal closure is still needed: final acceptance of the complete product, contract closure, final lessons learned review, team release, and archiving
- **D**: Only the product owner needs to do closure activities

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile's incremental delivery means some acceptance and transition happens throughout. However, formal project closure is still required: final acceptance of the complete product, closing any contracts, comprehensive lessons learned (combining retrospective insights), releasing resources, and archiving. The activities may be lighter but are still necessary.

Option A omits necessary formal closure.

Option B ignores that incremental delivery changes some closure dynamics.

Option D excludes necessary PM and organizational closure activities.
