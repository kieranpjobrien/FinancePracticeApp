# Question

A hybrid R&D collaboration between three organisations is closing. Intellectual property created during the project has commercial value. The collaboration agreement addresses some IP scenarios but is ambiguous on joint inventions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign IP to the organisation whose staff did the most work on each invention, since contribution level is the fairest practical allocation criterion at project closure.
- **B**: Defer IP decisions to after project closure and let the three organisations' legal teams negotiate ownership separately on whatever timeline works best for them.
- **C**: Engage legal counsel from each organisation to resolve the ambiguity formally, document IP disposition in the closure pack, and obtain tri-party acceptance before closing.
- **D**: Assign all disputed IP to the lead organisation that funded the collaboration, since they carried the greatest financial risk during the research and development period.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

IP disposition with commercial value requires legal resolution, documented disposition, and multi-party acceptance before closure, which the PM must drive. (C) closes the project on solid legal ground. (A) applies a rule of thumb to a legal matter. (B) defers a closure deliverable indefinitely. (D) unilaterally allocates IP without authority or agreement.
