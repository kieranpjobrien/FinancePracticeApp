# Question

What financial activity is typically performed at project closure?

## Options

- **A**: Requesting an additional budget increment to cover any remaining closure work before archives are handed to records management.
- **B**: Reallocating any unspent project budget to the next project the PM will lead without involving the funding committee.
- **C**: Finalising accounts, releasing unused funds back to the funding pool, and recording final actual costs against the baseline.
- **D**: Holding the project's cost accounts open until the warranty period ends so any late adjustments can be charged against the project.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Financial closure includes settling all project costs, releasing remaining funds, and recording final actuals against the budget baseline. (C) captures these standard activities. (A) is a request for resources and is the opposite of closure. (B) reassigns funds without authority and bypasses the funding governance process. (D) confuses warranty obligations, which may sit elsewhere in the organisation, with the project's own financial closeout, which should not be left open.
