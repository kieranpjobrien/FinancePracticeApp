# Question

During closure, the project manager realizes that some project equipment (laptops, testing devices) is no longer needed but has residual value. What should be done?

## Options

- **A**: Let team members keep the equipment
- **B**: Dispose of equipment without consideration of value
- **C**: Follow organizational asset disposition procedures - return to asset pool, transfer to other projects, or dispose according to organizational and accounting policies
- **D**: Store equipment indefinitely in case it's needed later

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Project equipment is an organizational asset. Proper disposition follows organizational procedures: returning to an asset pool for reuse, transferring to other projects that need it, or formal disposal if no longer useful. This maximizes value recovery and complies with accounting and asset management requirements.

Option A inappropriately gives away organizational assets.

Option B may waste valuable resources.

Option D ties up resources that could be used elsewhere.
