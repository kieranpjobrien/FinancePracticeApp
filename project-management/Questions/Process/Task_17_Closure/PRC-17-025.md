# Question

A construction project used five vendors with staggered work scopes. Two vendors finished four months ago, one finished last month, and two are wrapping up now. The PM is planning procurement closure. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until every vendor has completed work, then close all five contracts together for administrative simplicity.
- **B**: Close each vendor contract individually as that vendor's work is verified complete, while the project remains open until the final contract is closed.
- **C**: Hand the open contracts to procurement at project close and let them complete the closeouts on the procurement team's timeline.
- **D**: Close the two early-finishers' contracts now and wait until project closure to handle the remaining three together.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's procurement closure runs per contract: each is closed when the work for that contract is verified, payments are settled, and warranties are addressed. (B) prevents trailing receivables and obligations from sitting open longer than necessary. (A) delays closeout for already-complete work and risks losing context on early vendors. (C) abdicates the PM's procurement closeout responsibility and leaves the PM unable to confirm complete project closure. (D) is half-right but unnecessarily defers the recently-finished vendor's closeout.
