# Question

What should happen to project documents at project closure?

## Options

- **A**: They should be retained on the project team's shared drive in case team members need them later.
- **B**: They should be archived in line with the organisation's records retention policy so they remain available for audits, future projects, and reference.
- **C**: They should be reduced to the final project report and the lessons learned register only, with working documents disposed of.
- **D**: They should be transferred to the operations team that owns the resulting product going forward.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI requires project documentation to be archived as organisational process assets according to the organisation's retention rules. (B) supports audits, knowledge reuse, and lessons learned across future projects. (A) leaves documents under team control without organisational governance and risks loss when the team disperses. (C) discards working documents that may be valuable for audit defence or future similar projects. (D) misallocates ownership: operations needs the runbooks and product artefacts, not the full project record such as plans, registers, and procurement files.
