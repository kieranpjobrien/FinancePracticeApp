# Question

What should happen to project documents at project closure?

## Options

- **A**: Delete them to save storage space
- **B**: Archive them according to organizational policies for future reference, audits, and lessons learned
- **C**: Give them to team members to keep
- **D**: Leave them where they are without any action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Project archives preserve organizational knowledge. Documents may be needed for future reference (similar projects), audits (compliance verification), legal purposes, or lessons learned. Archiving should follow organizational retention policies regarding what to keep, how long, and where.

Option A loses potentially valuable information.

Option C lacks organizational control over potentially sensitive information.

Option D doesn't ensure accessibility or proper retention.
