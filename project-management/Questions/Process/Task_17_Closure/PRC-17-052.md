# Question

A Scrum team has delivered a successful product after a demanding 14-month engagement. The PM is planning closure activities. The sponsor wants a quick disbandment to redeploy people to urgent new work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Disband the team rapidly as the sponsor requested to preserve redeployment velocity and leave recognition for the receiving functions to arrange later.
- **B**: Delay redeployment by two weeks to run extensive celebration, recognition, and career development events before anyone moves to their next assignment.
- **C**: Conduct a proportionate close-out that includes recognition, learning capture, and handover, then support timely redeployment aligned with sponsor priorities.
- **D**: Replace celebration with written thank-you notes from the sponsor and move the team immediately to preserve the organisation's new-work momentum entirely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI balances respect for team effort with organisational priorities; both matter and closure must serve both. (C) honours recognition and learning without blocking redeployment. (A) discards morale and learning for speed. (B) overweights recognition against organisational needs. (D) treats recognition as disposable and damages future engagement.
