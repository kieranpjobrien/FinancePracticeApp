# Question

An agile project has been delivering incrementally to production throughout its lifecycle. At project closure, what is different about acceptance compared to a traditional project?

## Options

- **A**: No acceptance is needed since delivery was incremental
- **B**: Acceptance is identical to traditional projects
- **C**: While incremental deliveries received ongoing acceptance, final project closure should confirm overall product acceptance, verify all agreed scope was delivered, and document the complete delivered capability
- **D**: Only the product owner provides acceptance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile's incremental acceptance doesn't eliminate the need for final acceptance. At closure, confirm: the complete product is accepted (not just individual increments), all agreed scope was delivered (product backlog completed as expected), and the full capability is documented. This provides formal closure even when delivery was incremental.

Option A skips necessary formal closure acceptance.

Option B ignores that incremental delivery changes acceptance dynamics.

Option D may not satisfy organizational or contractual acceptance requirements.
