# Question

A Scrum project has been delivering increments to production for two years and the product owner has accepted each one at the relevant sprint review. The PM is now planning project closure. What should the PM do regarding final acceptance?

## Options

- **A**: Treat acceptance as already complete because every increment was accepted at the time of release.
- **B**: Re-run formal acceptance testing across the entire product backlog to revalidate everything before closure.
- **C**: Ask only the product owner to sign closure, since they have been the accepting authority all along.
- **D**: Confirm formal closure acceptance covering the complete delivered capability, scope completeness against the agreed backlog, and any contractual or governance acceptance that operates at the project rather than sprint level.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI recognises agile acceptance accumulates across increments but does not eliminate the need for project-level closure acceptance, which speaks to the whole and to obligations that sit outside the sprint cadence. (D) closes the gap by addressing what sprint-level acceptance does not cover. (A) skips the closure-level confirmation that contracts and governance typically require. (B) wastes effort revalidating already-accepted work. (C) underweights the sponsor, contracting authority, and governance roles that may also need to sign at project level.
