# Question

A project is being transitioned to a support team in a different country, creating challenges around documentation language, time zones for knowledge transfer, and different working practices. What considerations are critical for successful transition?

## Options

- **A**: Provide documentation and trust the receiving team
- **B**: Keep the project team involved indefinitely
- **C**: Plan extended transition with overlapping support hours, documentation reviewed for cultural and language clarity, video-recorded knowledge sessions that can be viewed asynchronously, and clearly defined escalation paths during the transition period
- **D**: Delay transition until an in-country team is available

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-border transitions require extra planning. Overlapping hours enables real-time interaction despite time zones. Language and cultural review ensures documentation clarity. Recorded sessions allow asynchronous learning. Clear escalation paths provide safety nets. These accommodations address the specific challenges of international transition.

Option A underestimates cross-border challenges.

Option B isn't sustainable.

Option D may not be feasible and delays transition unnecessarily.
