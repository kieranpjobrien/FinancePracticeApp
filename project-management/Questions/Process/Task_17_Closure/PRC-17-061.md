# Question

At closure of a predictive IT project that replaced a legacy system, the PM discovers the legacy system is still running alongside the new platform and draws ongoing licensing cost. Decommissioning was never formally scoped. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project on scope as originally defined, since decommissioning the legacy system was never part of the authorised scope of work at baseline.
- **B**: Scope a decommissioning work package into the project, execute it, and defer closure until the legacy environment has been fully retired from use.
- **C**: Escalate the unaddressed legacy system to the sponsor as a gap and ask them to decide whether it should be handled before closure proceeds formally.
- **D**: Raise the decommissioning gap in the closure report with cost analysis, recommend a named owner and funded follow-on initiative, and close the project.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to surface gaps honestly with data and propose appropriate ownership, while not expanding a closing project beyond its scope. (D) transfers the decommissioning obligation to a suitable vehicle with evidence. (A) ignores a significant cost exposure. (B) stretches scope beyond the project's mandate. (C) escalates without the analysis and recommendation the sponsor needs.
