# Question

When should planning for project closure begin?

## Options

- **A**: Early in the project, with detailed closure planning refined as the end approaches.
- **B**: When the final deliverables have been accepted by the customer.
- **C**: When the sponsor formally requests a closure plan.
- **D**: At the start of the closure phase, after all execution work is complete.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats closure as something to plan for from initiation, because closure activities such as acceptance criteria, contract terms, and transition to operations have implications that must be designed into the project. (A) is the standard guidance: plan early, refine late. (B) is far too late; many closure dependencies need to be in place well before acceptance. (C) makes closure planning reactive to the sponsor instead of an inherent PM responsibility. (D) leaves no time to set up the conditions for orderly closure such as criteria, archives, and transition partners.
