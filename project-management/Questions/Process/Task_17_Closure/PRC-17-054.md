# Question

A hybrid hospital platform is at go-live. Operations readiness reviews show the night-shift team has not completed simulation exercises, though day-shift readiness is strong. The sponsor is pushing to launch. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend a targeted delay or staged go-live aligned to readiness, complete night-shift simulations, and return with an updated go/no-go readiness assessment afterwards.
- **B**: Launch on the planned date and rely on day-shift staff to cover any night-shift incidents during the early weeks of post-launch operations at the hospital.
- **C**: Launch on the planned date and accept higher incident risk during night-shift operations since the platform is otherwise ready to serve patients across the facility.
- **D**: Launch as planned and ask the vendor to provide additional remote night-shift support during the first month to compensate for incomplete simulation coverage.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects go/no-go decisions to weigh operational readiness, especially in patient-safety contexts, and the PM should surface options even under sponsor pressure. (A) aligns transition to readiness and protects patients. (B) places day-shift under impossible coverage load. (C) accepts safety risk for schedule. (D) compensates cosmetically without fixing the readiness gap.
