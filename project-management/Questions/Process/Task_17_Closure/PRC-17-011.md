# Question

A team has just delivered an intense year-long programme. The PM is heads-down on administrative closeout: contracts, archives, and the final report. What important closure activity is the PM most likely to overlook?

## Options

- **A**: Recognising team contributions and providing closure for the team itself before they disperse.
- **B**: Triggering the next planning cycle with the sponsor while the team is still assembled.
- **C**: Conducting a peer review of the final project report before it is published.
- **D**: Re-reading the original business case to compare planned versus delivered benefits.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first orientation includes recognising contribution and closing out the team experience, not just the artefacts. (A) is the activity that most often slips when the PM is buried in paperwork, and it has direct effects on morale, future collaboration, and reputation. (B) is a sponsor activity, not a closure obligation of the current project. (C) is good practice for the report, but it is part of the closeout the PM is already doing. (D) is useful for benefits realisation reporting but does not fill the people-shaped gap the question is highlighting.
