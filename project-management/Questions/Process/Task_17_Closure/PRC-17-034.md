# Question

Why is it important to update organisational process assets at project closure?

## Options

- **A**: To meet a PMO requirement that all closed projects submit closure documentation.
- **B**: To make templates, lessons learned, and historical data from the project available so future projects benefit from improved estimates, methods, and risk awareness.
- **C**: To create an evidence trail in case the project is reviewed by external auditors after the fact.
- **D**: To formalise the PM's reputation by associating their name with the project's closure record.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats organisational process assets as the mechanism by which one project's experience improves the next, including templates, historical data for estimating, and lessons learned. (B) captures this purpose directly. (A) is a compliance framing that misses the underlying value. (C) audit defence is a side benefit, not the primary purpose. (D) treats closure as a personal credential rather than as organisational learning.
