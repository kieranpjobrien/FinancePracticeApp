# Question

A new claims processing system is ready to transition to operations. Operations has identified 14 open defects they want fixed before they accept the system. The project team believes most are cosmetic and do not affect functionality. The project is already past its planned end date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Compare each defect against the agreed acceptance criteria, fix those that breach the criteria, and negotiate conditional acceptance for the rest with documented remediation commitments.
- **B**: Fix all 14 defects regardless of severity to secure operations' acceptance and close the project.
- **C**: Press for unconditional acceptance on the basis that the project is past its end date and the defects are minor.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor and the operations director to settle the dispute on the PM's behalf.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI grounds acceptance disputes in the agreed acceptance criteria, not in either side's preferences. (A) uses the criteria as the objective test, fixes what must be fixed, and uses conditional acceptance, a legitimate mechanism, for the rest. (B) is wasteful and rewards moving the goalposts beyond the criteria. (C) pressures operations to accept against their stated concerns and damages the relationship and the system's reputation. (D) escalates before the PM has used the contractual instrument that already exists, which is the acceptance criteria.
