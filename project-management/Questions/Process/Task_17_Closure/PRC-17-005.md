# Question

A new payments platform is ready to hand over to the operations team. The operations lead complains that her team was first contacted four weeks ago and feels unprepared to support the system. Looking back at this project, what should the project manager have done differently?

## Options

- **A**: Identified operations as a stakeholder during planning and engaged them throughout the project on transition criteria, supportability requirements, and runbooks.
- **B**: Embedded an operations engineer full-time on the build team for the entire project to write the support documentation.
- **C**: Required the operations team to attend every sprint review so they could absorb context as the system was built.
- **D**: Delayed the handover by another quarter to give operations more preparation time before go-live.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats stakeholder identification and engagement as planning activities, and the receiving team for a transition is a stakeholder from day one. (A) addresses the root cause: operations should have been on the stakeholder register and engaged on supportability and transition criteria from planning. (B) overcorrects with a heavyweight allocation that few organisations could sustain and is more than is needed. (C) is engagement, but mandating attendance at all reviews is impractical and substitutes presence for genuine collaboration. (D) treats the symptom by buying more time rather than fixing the late engagement that caused the gap.
