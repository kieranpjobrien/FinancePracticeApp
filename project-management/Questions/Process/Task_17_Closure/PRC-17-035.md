# Question

A defence contract has been completed and the PM is planning closure. The project produced classified materials and used cleared personnel throughout. What should the PM do that goes beyond standard closure activities?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the security classification rules to archiving, retention, and disposal, verify clearances of anyone involved in closure activities, and confirm secure handling of classified materials with the security officer.
- **B**: Treat closure exactly the same as any other project so the closure process is consistent across the portfolio.
- **C**: Destroy all classified material immediately at closure to remove any risk of future leakage.
- **D**: Retain all classified material indefinitely under the project's accounts to avoid making any retention decisions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI requires tailoring closure to the project context, and security-sensitive projects have specific obligations the PM must meet alongside standard closure. (A) follows the classification regime and brings in the security officer, which is the correct tailoring. (B) ignores legal and contractual obligations specific to classified work. (C) likely violates retention requirements that mandate certain materials be kept for defined periods. (D) avoids decisions the PM is responsible for and creates an unmanaged accumulation of sensitive material.
