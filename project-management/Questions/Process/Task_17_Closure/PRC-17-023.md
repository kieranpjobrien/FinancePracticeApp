# Question

A three-year programme is closing. Six of the original ten team members have rotated off and been replaced over the years. The PM is concerned that lessons learned from the early phases will be lost. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Capture lessons only from the current team so the workshop stays grounded in firsthand recall and therefore remains accurate throughout.
- **B**: Skip the lessons learned exercise because too many key participants are no longer available to contribute meaningfully to early-phase insights.
- **C**: Combine current team input with phase reports and retrospective records, and reach out to a few key alumni for early-phase input.
- **D**: Reconstruct early-phase lessons from the project documents alone so no one has to be re-interrupted from their current work elsewhere.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats lessons learned as a continuously accumulated artefact, and closure should consolidate what was captured throughout, not start from scratch. (C) uses three complementary sources to maximise coverage given the constraints. (A) loses three years of insights from earlier phases. (B) abandons the activity rather than tailoring it. (D) ignores the tacit knowledge alumni still hold and that documents alone cannot recover.
