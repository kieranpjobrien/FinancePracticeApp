# Question

A successful product has shipped, but during closure review the PM realises the original business case overstated the expected benefits. Actual benefits are positive but well below what was promised when the project was approved. How should this be handled at closure?

## Options

- **A**: Present only the actual benefits in the closure report without referencing the original projection, since the project itself was successful.
- **B**: Adjust the recorded business case figures to reflect what was actually achieved so the variance does not appear in the historical record.
- **C**: Note the overstatement informally to the sponsor and omit it from the closure report so the project's success is not undermined.
- **D**: Document the variance honestly in the closure report, analyse why the original projections differed from actuals, and capture the analysis as a lesson learned to improve future business case accuracy.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects honest closure reporting because it is the input to organisational learning, including learning about how the organisation estimates benefits. (D) keeps the record accurate, asks why, and feeds the answer back into the next business case. (A) hides the variance and prevents the organisation from improving its forecasting. (B) falsifies historical records, a serious ethical breach. (C) protects the project's image at the cost of organisational learning and stakeholder trust.
