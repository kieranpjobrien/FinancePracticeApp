# Question

A project delivered a successful product, but during closure review, it's discovered that estimated benefits in the original business case were overstated. Actual benefits are positive but lower than projected. How should this be handled at closure?

## Options

- **A**: Adjust the historical record to show lower original projections
- **B**: Ignore the discrepancy since benefits are positive
- **C**: Document the variance honestly in closure materials, analyze why projections differed from actual, and capture this as a lesson learned for improving future business case accuracy
- **D**: Blame the people who created the original business case

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Honest documentation supports organizational learning. The variance should be recorded accurately, analyzed (were assumptions wrong? did conditions change?), and captured as a lesson learned. This improves future business case accuracy. The project still delivered positive benefits, which should also be acknowledged.

Option A falsifies records.

Option B misses a learning opportunity.

Option D focuses on blame rather than learning.
