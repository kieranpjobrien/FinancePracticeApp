# Question

A project involved developing intellectual property (IP) using both internal resources and external contractors. At closure, the ownership and handling of this IP needs to be formalized. What should the project manager ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Assume all IP belongs to the organization
- **B**: Let legal handle it completely without PM input
- **C**: Work with legal to ensure IP ownership is clearly documented according to contracts, that appropriate confidentiality measures are in place, and that IP is properly transferred to whoever will maintain and use it going forward
- **D**: Share IP with all project participants

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. IP requires careful handling at closure. Ownership should be confirmed based on contracts (contractor agreements may address IP). Confidentiality measures protect valuable IP. Transfer to the maintaining party ensures proper custody. The PM should work with legal as this is a specialized area, but shouldn't simply hand it off.

Option A may not reflect contractual reality, especially with contractors.

Option B removes PM involvement from an important closure activity.

Option D may violate ownership and confidentiality requirements.
