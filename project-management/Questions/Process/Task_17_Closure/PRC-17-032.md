# Question

A research project has produced patentable methods using a mix of internal staff and three external contractors. Each contractor signed a different statement of work with different IP clauses. At closure, the PM is responsible for ensuring IP is handled correctly. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat all project IP as belonging to the organisation and transfer the assets to the receiving business unit.
- **B**: Hand the IP question to the legal team and exclude it from the closure report so the PM is not on the record.
- **C**: Work with legal to confirm IP ownership against each contract, ensure confidentiality controls are in place, and document the transfer to the receiving owner as part of closure.
- **D**: Wait for the contractors to confirm in writing that they release their IP claims before recording any disposition.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to coordinate specialist input rather than abdicate or improvise. (C) brings legal into the contractual interpretation, then completes the operational closure tasks of confidentiality and transfer. (A) presumes ownership the contracts may not actually grant and creates legal exposure. (B) abandons the PM's accountability for ensuring closure obligations are met and leaves the closure record incomplete. (D) waits indefinitely on a passive action by external parties, which can stall closure unnecessarily when the contracts already define the answer.
