# Question

A project delivered a product that will require ongoing support from a vendor. The vendor relationship is transitioning from the project team to a support relationship managed by IT operations. What should be transferred?

## Options

- **A**: Just the vendor contact information
- **B**: Let IT operations establish their own vendor relationship
- **C**: Transfer complete vendor relationship information: contracts, SLAs, escalation contacts, history of the relationship, known issues, and any special arrangements - ensuring continuity of vendor management
- **D**: Have the vendor report to both project and operations indefinitely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Vendor relationships involve more than contact information. Contracts define terms. SLAs define service expectations. Relationship history provides context. Known issues prevent surprises. Special arrangements need to be honored. Complete transfer ensures IT operations can manage the relationship effectively.

Option A transfers insufficient information.

Option B wastes effort rebuilding knowledge the project already has.

Option D creates unclear accountability.
