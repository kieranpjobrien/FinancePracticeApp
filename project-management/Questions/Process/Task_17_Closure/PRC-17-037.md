# Question

A delivered product depends on ongoing support from a third-party vendor. The vendor relationship is moving from the project team to IT operations. What should the project manager transfer?

## Options

- **A**: The vendor's primary contact details and the master services agreement, leaving operations to build the rest of the relationship themselves.
- **B**: A summary of the vendor's contractual obligations only, since operations will form their own working relationship over time.
- **C**: The contract and SLA documents, leaving relationship history and informal arrangements with the project team for reference if asked.
- **D**: The full vendor relationship: contracts, SLAs, escalation contacts, performance history, known issues, and any informal arrangements that affect day-to-day support.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats vendor relationships as accumulated organisational knowledge that must transfer fully for support to remain effective. (D) gives operations everything they need to operate the relationship from day one. (A) is incomplete and forces operations to rediscover what the project team already knows. (B) loses the operational context such as performance history and known issues that affect daily support. (C) leaves critical informal knowledge dependent on the project team's continued availability after they have rolled off.
