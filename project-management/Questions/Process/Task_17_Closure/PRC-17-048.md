# Question

A predictive product development project is being cancelled by the sponsor after 10 months because market conditions have shifted. The team is demoralised and expects immediate reassignment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the team immediately to preserve morale and hand the remaining artefacts to the PMO to handle through normal archival processes afterwards.
- **B**: Record the cancellation reason briefly in the closure log and release the team, prioritising the sponsor's stated urgency to redirect resources elsewhere.
- **C**: Run full closure including lessons learned, asset disposition, contract closeout, and an honest retrospective on why the project was terminated early.
- **D**: Preserve all work in progress in case the project is restarted and defer formal closure decisions until the sponsor confirms the cancellation is irreversible.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI requires the same closure rigour for cancelled projects as for successful ones because organisational learning depends on it. (C) completes the obligations and captures the valuable terminated-project lessons. (A) skips closure activities. (B) treats closure as administrative burden. (D) holds open a project the sponsor has cancelled.
