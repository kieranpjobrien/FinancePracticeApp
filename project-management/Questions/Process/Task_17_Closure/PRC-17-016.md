# Question

A trading platform built by an internal development organisation is being transitioned to a managed services provider that will run it in production. The two organisations use different ticketing tools, naming conventions, and on-call practices. What should the project manager do FIRST when planning the handover?

## Options

- **A**: Reformat all internal documentation to match the managed services provider's templates before any handover sessions begin.
- **B**: Agree exit criteria for transition with the receiving organisation, including the artefacts they need, the format they need them in, and how completion will be confirmed.
- **C**: Schedule a single intensive two-day handover workshop and treat any remaining gaps as warranty issues.
- **D**: Embed two project engineers with the managed services provider for the first six months to bridge the differences.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-organisational transitions only succeed when both sides share a definition of done, so PMI starts the planning conversation with exit criteria. (B) makes the receiver's needs explicit before work begins and avoids both over-investment and under-delivery in the handover materials. (A) commits to large reformatting work before knowing whether it is needed or sufficient. (C) is a single-shot transfer that will leave gaps undiscovered until they hit production. (D) is a heavy structural commitment that should follow, not precede, agreement on exit criteria, and may not be necessary at all.
