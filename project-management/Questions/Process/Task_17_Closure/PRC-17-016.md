# Question

A project is transitioning from a development organization to a different organization that will handle deployment and operations. The two organizations have different processes, tools, and cultures. What transition planning is critical?

## Options

- **A**: Hand over documentation and let the receiving organization figure it out
- **B**: Have the development organization stay involved indefinitely
- **C**: Plan a structured handover including documentation in formats useful to the receiver, joint sessions to transfer knowledge, agreed criteria for when transition is complete, and defined support mechanisms during transition
- **D**: Start over in the receiving organization

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-organizational transitions require intentional planning. Documentation should suit the receiver's needs. Joint sessions transfer knowledge that documentation can't capture. Clear completion criteria prevent indefinite transition. Defined support mechanisms provide safety during the transition period.

Option A ignores the challenges of cross-organizational transfer.

Option B never achieves full transition.

Option D wastes work already completed.
