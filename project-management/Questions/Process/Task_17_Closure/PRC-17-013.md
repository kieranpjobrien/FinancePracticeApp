# Question

A predictive engineering project is moving from the design phase to the build phase. The PM wants the transition to be controlled without losing momentum. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Begin build work in parallel with the final remaining design tasks so there is absolutely no idle time between the two phases.
- **B**: Pause the entire project for two weeks while the design artefacts are properly finalised and the build team is fully onboarded.
- **C**: Skip any formal phase transition and simply let the build team adapt as design questions naturally surface during construction activities.
- **D**: Hold a phase gate review to confirm exit criteria, update plans based on learnings, and obtain go-no-go approval for build.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

In a predictive lifecycle, phase gates are the control point that protect the next phase from inheriting unresolved problems. (D) confirms the previous phase actually met its exit criteria, incorporates learnings into the next phase's plan, and obtains the appropriate authorisation. (A) overlaps phases without a control point and risks build rework if design has unresolved issues. (B) wastes time without addressing what the gate review would actually decide. (C) skips the control entirely, leading to discovery and rework during build that the gate would have caught.
