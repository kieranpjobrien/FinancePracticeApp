# Question

During closure of a construction project, the PM finds three vendor contracts still open: one with a final payment pending invoice review, one with materials yet to be returned, and one with a five-year equipment warranty that needs an owner. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the project open until all three contracts are naturally resolved.
- **B**: Close the project on schedule and let procurement chase the open items afterwards.
- **C**: Coordinate with procurement to settle the invoice, return the materials, and transfer the warranty to operations as part of the project's contract closeout.
- **D**: Transfer all three open contracts to a successor project so the current project can close cleanly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Procurement closure is part of project closure: each contract needs a defined disposition before the project itself can close. (C) handles each item appropriately, including the long-tail warranty, which gets a permanent owner rather than ending with the project. (A) leaves the project formally open for years because of a warranty, blocking resource release. (B) abandons the PM's accountability and risks contractual gaps once project oversight is gone. (D) creates an artificial tie to a successor project that may not exist or may not be the right home for these obligations.
