# Question

A project is being cancelled due to a strategic shift, even though deliverables are partially complete. What should the project manager ensure happens during closure?

## Options

- **A**: Delete all project documentation since the project failed
- **B**: Document lessons learned, archive deliverables, release resources, and close contracts
- **C**: Simply notify stakeholders and move team members to other projects
- **D**: Continue working to complete as many deliverables as possible before cancellation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cancelled projects still require proper closure. Lessons learned capture what led to cancellation and what can be salvaged. Partial deliverables may have value for future initiatives. Resources need formal release for proper reassignment. Contracts must be closed per their terms. Proper closure protects the organisation.

Option A (delete documentation) destroys valuable information. The work done may inform future projects or contain reusable components.

Option C (just notify) is incomplete closure. Without proper handoff, resources may be stranded, contracts may have penalties, and knowledge is lost.

Option D (keep working) contradicts the strategic decision. Once cancellation is decided, continuing work wastes resources that should be redirected.
