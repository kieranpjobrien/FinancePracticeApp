# Question

In the context of project delivery, what is a warranty period?

## Options

- **A**: A reserve of budget retained after closure to cover enhancements requested by the customer.
- **B**: A grace period during which the customer may decline acceptance and require re-delivery.
- **C**: A defined period after delivery during which the project team or vendor is obligated to remediate defects found in the delivered product at no additional cost.
- **D**: A window during which the project team continues to develop new features based on customer feedback.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A warranty covers the remediation of defects, not enhancements, for a defined period after delivery, typically at no additional cost to the customer. (C) is the standard definition. (A) confuses warranty with a budget reserve for future work. (B) is a rejection mechanism, not a warranty. (D) describes post-delivery enhancement, which is separate work, not warranty coverage.
