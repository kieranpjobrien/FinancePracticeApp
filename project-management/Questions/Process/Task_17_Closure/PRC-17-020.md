# Question

A project delivered successfully, but post-implementation review after three months reveals that expected business benefits have not materialized. The project met all its scope, schedule, and cost objectives. Whose responsibility is the unrealized benefits?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, since they led the project
- **B**: The project team, since they built it
- **C**: Benefits realization typically extends beyond project closure and is often the responsibility of the business/operations owner or sponsor, though the project should have set up measurement mechanisms
- **D**: No one - if the project met its objectives, the project was successful

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Project success (delivering scope on time and budget) and benefits realization (achieving intended business outcomes) are related but distinct. Benefits often materialize after project closure in the operations phase. Responsibility typically lies with business owners or sponsors who requested the project. The project should establish measurement mechanisms to track benefits.

Option A holds the PM responsible for post-project operational outcomes.

Option B similarly misassigns responsibility for business outcomes.

Option D ignores that ultimate project value depends on benefits realization.
