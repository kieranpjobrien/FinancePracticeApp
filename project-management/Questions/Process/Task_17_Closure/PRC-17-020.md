# Question

A project delivered on scope, schedule, and budget, but a post-implementation review three months later finds the projected business benefits have not materialised. Whose responsibility is the unrealised benefits?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, because they led the project and the benefits were part of the business case.
- **B**: The project sponsor alone, because they approved the business case.
- **C**: The project team, because the product they built is not driving adoption.
- **D**: Benefits realisation typically extends beyond closure and sits with the business owner or sponsor, while the PM is responsible for setting up benefit measurement during the project.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes project success (delivering the agreed scope, schedule, cost, and quality) from benefits realisation (achieving the business outcomes), and the latter is owned by the business once the product is in operation. (D) reflects this split accurately and notes the PM's contribution: instrumenting the measurement. (A) misallocates ongoing operational accountability to the PM after the project has closed. (B) is too narrow because business owners and operations also have responsibility, not the sponsor alone. (C) confuses delivery quality with adoption and value, which depend on operational decisions outside the team's control.
