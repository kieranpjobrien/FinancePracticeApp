# Question

A platform project has met every documented requirement, but two senior stakeholders are withholding acceptance because of concerns they say should have been addressed during the project. The PM is under pressure from the sponsor to close. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the concerns as out-of-scope items and request the sponsor formally close the project despite the senior stakeholders' objections.
- **B**: Meet with the stakeholders, classify each concern as in-scope delivery or new requirement, and drive resolution through the appropriate path.
- **C**: Reopen the requirements baseline and address the stakeholders' concerns as additional scope before closing the project and releasing the team.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to require the stakeholders to provide acceptance because all documented requirements have been formally met and evidenced.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI starts with understanding before deciding the response, especially when stakeholder concerns surface late. (B) separates concerns by their actual nature: some may turn out to be misunderstandings, some legitimate scope issues, some new requests, each with a different appropriate path. (A) shortcuts the conversation and risks closing with stakeholders who may obstruct adoption later. (C) commits to reopening scope before knowing whether the concerns are even in-scope items. (D) escalates before the PM has attempted resolution at their level and treats the stakeholders as obstacles rather than as people with concerns worth understanding.
