# Question

A project is complete, but several stakeholders have unresolved concerns they believe should have been addressed during the project. They are reluctant to provide formal acceptance. The project met all documented requirements. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the stakeholders and close the project
- **B**: Reopen the project to address all concerns
- **C**: Meet with concerned stakeholders to understand their issues, document whether concerns relate to original requirements (if so, verify delivery) or new/changed requirements (if so, handle through separate process), and work toward closure while acknowledging concerns
- **D**: Escalate immediately to force acceptance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Unresolved stakeholder concerns at closure need exploration. Some may be misunderstandings about what was delivered (can be clarified). Some may be requirements that were in scope but stakeholders feel aren't met (need verification). Some may be new requirements that emerged (should be handled separately). Understanding enables appropriate resolution path.

Option A ignores legitimate stakeholder concerns.

Option B reopens the project without understanding if the concerns are in scope.

Option D is confrontational and doesn't address underlying concerns.
