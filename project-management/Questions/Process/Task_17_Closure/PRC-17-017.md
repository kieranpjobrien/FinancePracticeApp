# Question

The closure phase is in week two and several team members are visibly distracted because they have not been told what their next assignments will be. Closure tasks are slipping. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Refocus the team on closure tasks by reminding them that completing this work professionally will reflect well on their next role and reputation.
- **B**: Tell each person which assignment they are likely to receive, based on the PM's best guess from conversations with the various functional managers.
- **C**: Release the team early so they can pursue their own next assignments and pick up the remaining closure tasks personally through to completion.
- **D**: Engage functional managers to clarify next assignments where possible, then communicate transparently about what is known and pending.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leadership stance treats team welfare as the PM's responsibility, including resolving the anxiety that is blocking work. (D) acts on the cause by going to the functional managers who actually decide assignments and then communicating honestly, even when some answers are still pending. (A) acknowledges the issue but offers no concrete information and asks the team to suppress a legitimate concern. (B) creates expectations the PM cannot guarantee and risks damaging credibility when guesses prove wrong. (C) abandons closure obligations and shifts the PM's load onto themselves, which is unsustainable.
