# Question

Team members are anxious about closure because they don't know what assignments they'll have next. This anxiety is affecting their focus on completing closure activities. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore their concerns and focus only on closure tasks
- **B**: Promise them specific assignments even if uncertain
- **C**: Communicate transparently about the transition process, work with functional managers to clarify next assignments where possible, and help team members understand that completing closure well supports their professional reputation
- **D**: Release team members early to find their own next assignments

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Team anxiety at closure is normal and should be addressed. Transparent communication about the process, even if details are uncertain, reduces anxiety. Working with functional managers to clarify assignments where possible provides concrete information. Framing good closure as professionally valuable motivates completion.

Option A ignores legitimate concerns affecting work quality.

Option B creates false expectations.

Option D leaves closure activities incomplete and may harm team members.
