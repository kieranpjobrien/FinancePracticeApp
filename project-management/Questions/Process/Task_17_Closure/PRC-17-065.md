# Question

At closure of a predictive product development project, the PM finds the product works but post-release metrics show it is unlikely to recover its investment. The sponsor wants the closure report to recommend aggressive marketing to push adoption. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Include the sponsor's recommended aggressive marketing strategy in the closure report to align with their strategic view of how to respond to the result.
- **B**: Omit benefits projections entirely from the closure report and focus instead on the project's scope and schedule achievements against the delivery baseline.
- **C**: Reframe the disappointing metrics as early signals and project optimistic recovery scenarios to support the sponsor's preferred narrative for future investment.
- **D**: Report the metrics honestly, analyse the shortfall, and separate the closure from any future marketing investment decision governed by a different process.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI requires honest closure reporting and clear scope boundaries between closure and follow-on investment decisions. (D) preserves integrity and lets the right body decide on marketing. (A) mixes closure reporting with a marketing pitch. (B) hides the most important finding. (C) falsifies outlook to flatter a sponsor, a serious ethics breach.
