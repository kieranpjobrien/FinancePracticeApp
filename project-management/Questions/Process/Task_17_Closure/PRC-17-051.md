# Question

At closure of a predictive government IT project, the PM is preparing the final report. Delivery came in on time but 15 percent over budget due to a clearly documented scope change. The sponsor suggests omitting the overrun. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Omit the budget variance as the sponsor requested since the scope change justification would complicate the report for senior executive readers.
- **B**: Report the overrun with the linked approved scope change, the variance analysis, and approved change records, presenting the picture transparently.
- **C**: Report the overrun as a summary figure without linking it to the scope change so executives form their view from the delivery outcome.
- **D**: Ask finance to rebaseline the budget retrospectively to include the scope change so the final report shows no variance against the baseline.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI requires honest closure reporting with full context, and linking variance to approved changes is both transparent and defensible. (B) preserves the record and explains the outcome. (A) conceals a material variance. (C) presents data without context, inviting misinterpretation. (D) falsifies historical baseline records, a serious ethics breach.
