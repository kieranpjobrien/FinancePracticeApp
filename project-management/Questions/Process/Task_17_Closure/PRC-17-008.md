# Question

A project is being terminated early due to organizational restructuring. The project has completed about 60% of planned work and has produced some valuable outputs. What closure activities are MOST important in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Close immediately without additional activities since the project is being cancelled
- **B**: Continue as if completing normally and document all remaining planned work
- **C**: Document what was completed, capture lessons learned, archive artifacts, transition any usable deliverables to appropriate parties, and complete contract and resource release activities
- **D**: Assign blame for the early termination

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Early termination requires proper closure to preserve value. Documenting completed work captures what's available. Lessons learned captures experience. Archiving preserves artifacts for potential future use. Transitioning usable deliverables extracts value from what was done. Contract and resource activities fulfill obligations.

Option A loses value from work completed and leaves loose ends.

Option B wastes effort on work that won't be completed.

Option D focuses on blame rather than value preservation.
