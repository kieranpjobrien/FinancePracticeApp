# Question

A merger has triggered the early termination of a 60%-complete data warehouse project. Some completed components are reusable for the merged organisation. The PM has two weeks to wind the project down. What should the PM prioritise FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Identify completed components that have value to the merged organisation, confirm a receiving owner for each, and capture what is needed to transition them.
- **B**: Run a comprehensive lessons learned workshop covering every aspect of the cancelled project before the team disperses.
- **C**: Begin the formal contract closeout process with all vendors before assessing reusable components.
- **D**: Archive the full set of project artefacts so nothing is lost when the team is reassigned.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When a project terminates early, PMI prioritises preserving business value before purely administrative closure activities. (A) is the highest-value first move because reusable components have a short window before the team disperses and tacit knowledge is lost. (B) is necessary but lessons can be captured even after partial reassignment, and pure introspection comes after value preservation. (C) is also required but does not need to precede salvaging value, and contract closeout often runs in parallel. (D) is administrative and can proceed alongside the salvage activity rather than ahead of it.
