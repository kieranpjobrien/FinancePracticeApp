# Question

The PM is one month into a one-year project. CPI = 0.82 and SPI = 0.75. The sponsor asks whether the project can still be delivered on budget and on time. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Share the current performance data, explain that without intervention the trend projects over-budget and late delivery, and propose corrective actions with quantified impact.
- **B**: Reassure the sponsor that one-month data is too early to draw conclusions and commit to monitoring.
- **C**: Rebaseline the project immediately to align with current performance.
- **D**: Commit to bringing the project back on track without specifying how.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects honest, data-backed communication with sponsors. (A) presents facts and offers a plan. (B) dismisses clear early-warning indicators with a 'too early' argument the data doesn't support. (C) rebaselining hides the problem rather than addressing it; baselines should change through change control when circumstances warrant, not to mask performance. (D) commits to an outcome without a plan — the sponsor can't assess feasibility.
