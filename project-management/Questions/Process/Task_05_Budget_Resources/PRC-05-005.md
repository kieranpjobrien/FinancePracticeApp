# Question

A project is 60% complete with a CPI of 0.85 and an SPI of 1.10. The sponsor asks if the project will meet its original budget. Based on EVM analysis, what should the project manager report?

## Options

- **A**: Yes, because the project is ahead of schedule
- **B**: No, the project will likely exceed budget by approximately 18%
- **C**: Cannot determine without knowing the original budget
- **D**: Yes, because the cost overrun will be recovered by early completion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. CPI of 0.85 indicates persistent cost inefficiency. Using the EVM forecasting formula:

**Estimate at Completion (EAC) = BAC / CPI**
**EAC = BAC / 0.85 = 1.176 × BAC**

This means the project is forecast to cost approximately 118% of budget - an 18% overrun. Being ahead of schedule (SPI > 1) doesn't recover cost overruns unless finishing early actually reduces costs (e.g., less rental time).

Option A confuses schedule and cost performance. They're independent metrics.

Option C is incorrect - CPI alone is sufficient to forecast final cost relative to budget.

Option D assumes schedule efficiency translates to cost recovery, which isn't automatic. Faster completion may reduce some costs but doesn't address the fundamental cost inefficiency.
