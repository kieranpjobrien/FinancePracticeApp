# Question

An agile team's velocity has been stable, and the product backlog is well-estimated. The sponsor asks for a budget forecast for completing the remaining backlog. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to forecast budgets in agile
- **B**: Use velocity to forecast iterations needed, multiply by known cost per iteration, and provide a range reflecting estimation uncertainty
- **C**: Provide a precise fixed-price estimate
- **D**: Estimate without using velocity data

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Agile can provide budget forecasts using empirical data. Calculate: remaining story points divided by velocity gives iterations needed. Cost: multiply by cost per iteration (team cost). Range: provide a range reflecting uncertainty (optimistic/likely/pessimistic based on velocity variation). This is data-driven forecasting, not anti-agile.

Option A ignores legitimate business needs for forecasting.

Option C overstates certainty.

Option D ignores valuable empirical data.
