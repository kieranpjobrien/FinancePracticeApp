# Question

The sponsor pushes for the PM to commit to a fixed budget before scope is fully defined. The PM knows this will force either scope cuts or budget overruns later. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Present ranges with current confidence levels and propose a staged commitment that tightens as scope matures.
- **B**: Agree to the sponsor's request and hope that scope can be actively managed down to fit the budget.
- **C**: Refuse to provide any estimate at all until the project scope is fully defined and baselined.
- **D**: Provide a conservative estimate with a large reserve to avoid any future budget overrun surprises.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects estimate confidence to reflect scope maturity. (A) is transparent and offers a progressive commitment path. (B) agreeing to a firm number on vague scope sets up the project to fail. (C) refusing to engage leaves the sponsor with no information when they need a planning figure. (D) large reserves hide the estimate rather than communicating honest uncertainty, and usually get trimmed by the sponsor anyway.
