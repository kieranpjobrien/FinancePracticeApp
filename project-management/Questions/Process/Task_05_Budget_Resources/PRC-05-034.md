# Question

Monthly cost reporting shows the project is spending exactly on plan, but milestone tracking shows three deliverables are now late. What does this combination of indicators most likely mean?

## Options

- **A**: The team is producing less work than planned for each dollar spent, which signals poor schedule performance and a likely future cost overrun.
- **B**: The cost baseline is correct but the schedule baseline is unrealistic, so the schedule should be re-baselined.
- **C**: The project is healthy overall because the cost variance is zero, and the late milestones will catch up naturally.
- **D**: Earned value reporting is producing a false signal and the EVM calculations should be reviewed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Spending to plan while delivering behind plan means EV is below PV — SPI is less than 1.0 and CPI is also at risk because the same money is buying less work. (A) correctly diagnoses both the schedule problem and the implied future cost pressure when the team has to catch up. (B) jumps to re-baselining, which is a last resort and avoids understanding the variance. (C) ignores the schedule signal. (D) blames the data rather than acting on it.
