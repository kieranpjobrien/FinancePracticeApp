# Question

The current cost report shows a significant negative Cost Variance (CV). The team lead explains that several large vendor invoices were posted earlier than planned, so the variance is "just timing." What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the cost baseline to reflect the new invoice timing pattern going forward.
- **B**: Accept the team lead's explanation and annotate the variance as timing-related in the next status report.
- **C**: Submit a change request to recover the cost variance through scope reduction in the remaining delivery work.
- **D**: Reconcile invoices against earned value with finance to confirm whether the variance is timing or real overspend.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to verify variance explanations against the data, not just accept them, because the response to a timing variance is very different from the response to a genuine overrun. (D) does the verification work — comparing AC to EV with finance — before deciding what to report and act on. (A) re-baselines without confirming the variance is actually timing. (B) trusts the explanation without verification, which risks reporting a real overrun as benign. (C) treats the variance as real and acts on it without confirmation, the opposite error.
