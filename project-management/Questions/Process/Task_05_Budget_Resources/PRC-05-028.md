# Question

An agile team has been established with a fixed, dedicated composition. Management wants to pull one team member for urgent work on another project for "just a few weeks." How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree since management has authority
- **B**: Explain the impact on velocity and project delivery, propose alternatives (another resource, deferred work), and ensure decision-makers understand the trade-offs before proceeding
- **C**: Refuse absolutely
- **D**: Pretend the team member is still available

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Changes to team composition affect delivery. Explain impact: velocity will likely drop, delivery commitments affected. Propose alternatives: is there someone else? Can the urgent work wait? Trade-offs: if they proceed, what's the cost to this project? This enables informed decision-making rather than assuming impacts are understood.

Option A doesn't advocate for the project.

Option C may not be appropriate given organisational needs.

Option D is dishonest and will create bigger problems.
