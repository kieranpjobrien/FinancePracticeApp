# Question

The project's burn rate is higher than planned due to unexpected technical complexity. The team is working productively, but more effort is required than estimated. The sponsor asks for options. What should the PM present?

## Options

- **A**: Only the request for more money
- **B**: Multiple options with trade-offs: request additional budget, reduce scope to fit budget, extend timeline to spread costs, or hybrid approaches - with analysis of each option's impacts and the PM's recommendation
- **C**: Only the suggestion to reduce scope
- **D**: A demand to reduce expectations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Decision-makers need options, not single solutions. Additional budget: what's the amount? What does it buy? Scope reduction: what could be deferred? What's the impact? Timeline extension: could spreading work reduce total cost? Hybrids: combinations of above? Analysis: impacts of each. Recommendation: PM's professional judgment. This enables informed choice.

Options A, C, and D present single options without alternatives or analysis.
