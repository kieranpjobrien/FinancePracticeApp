# Question

The project has a fixed budget and fixed deadline. During execution, a critical requirement is discovered that wasn't in the original scope. Including it will exceed either the budget or timeline. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Include it without adjusting constraints
- **B**: Present the situation to governance: the requirement, its importance, and the trade-off options (increase budget, extend timeline, reduce other scope, or exclude the requirement)
- **C**: Exclude the requirement without discussion
- **D**: Force the team to work overtime to absorb it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The triple constraint means you can't add scope without impacting cost or schedule (assuming quality is fixed). Present to governance: what's the requirement and why is it critical? Options: what are the trade-offs? Let appropriate decision-makers choose: more money, more time, less other scope, or accept the gap.

Option A pretends the constraint doesn't exist.

Option C makes a significant decision without appropriate authority.

Option D isn't sustainable and doesn't address the constraint.
