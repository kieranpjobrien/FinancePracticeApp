# Question

A 12-month infrastructure rollout has BAC of $2,400,000. At month 6 the team reports EV = $1,000,000, AC = $1,250,000, and PV = $1,200,000. Leadership assumes the cost variance will continue at the current rate. What is the Estimate at Completion using the typical formula?

## Options

- **A**: $2,650,000, calculated as AC plus the remaining budgeted work divided by the schedule performance index so far.
- **B**: $3,000,000, calculated as BAC divided by the cumulative cost performance index from the project to date.
- **C**: $2,880,000, calculated as AC plus the estimate to complete assuming work continues at planned-value efficiency.
- **D**: $2,400,000, calculated as BAC because the team commits to recovering the variance over the remainder.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When cost variance is assumed typical (continuing), EAC = BAC / CPI = 2,400,000 / 0.80 = $3,000,000. (A) blends CPI and SPI incorrectly and uses the wrong base. (C) invents a PV-efficiency formula that does not exist in PMI EV. (D) ignores the stated assumption that variance will persist and reverts to BAC, which is atypical EAC logic.
