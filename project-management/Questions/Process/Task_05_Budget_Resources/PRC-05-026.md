# Question

Your data migration project relies on a single in-house database engineer who is the only person with deep knowledge of the legacy schema. Resource planning notes that this person could be reassigned to a higher-priority initiative at any point. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Sequence the engineer's most critical work as early as possible and identify a backup option in the risk register.
- **B**: Negotiate a written agreement with the functional manager guaranteeing the engineer's full availability for the project duration.
- **C**: Hire an external contractor with the same skills as a permanent backup throughout the project.
- **D**: Reassign the legacy schema work to other team members to remove the single point of failure.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats single points of failure as risks to be planned for, not eliminated through unrealistic guarantees or premature spending. (A) applies two standard risk responses — front-loading critical tasks (mitigation through scheduling) and identifying a fallback option (contingent response) — and records them in the risk register. (B) seeks a guarantee the functional manager cannot realistically give in a matrix organisation. (C) burns budget on a full-time contractor when the risk may never materialise; this is a contingent action, not a default one. (D) is not feasible — the team members do not have the legacy knowledge, which is the whole reason the engineer is critical.
