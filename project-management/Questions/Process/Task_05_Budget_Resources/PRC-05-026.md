# Question

The project requires a specialist with rare skills. Only one person in the organisation has this expertise. They're available but could be reassigned to another project at any time. How should the PM manage this resource risk?

## Options

- **A**: Assume they'll remain available
- **B**: Document the risk, develop contingency plans (cross-training, external backup, prioritising their critical tasks early), and communicate the single-point-of-failure risk to the sponsor
- **C**: Lock them in contractually within the organisation
- **D**: Don't use their skills to avoid the risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Single points of failure are project risks. Document: make the risk visible. Contingency: cross-train others, identify external resources who could help, schedule their most critical work early. Communicate: ensure the sponsor understands the risk. This doesn't eliminate the risk but prepares for it.

Option A ignores a real risk.

Option C may not be possible and doesn't fully mitigate the risk.

Option D avoids critical expertise and likely isn't feasible.
