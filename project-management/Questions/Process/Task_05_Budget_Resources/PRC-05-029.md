# Question

What is the key distinction between analogous estimating and parametric estimating?

## Options

- **A**: Analogous estimating is always more accurate because it draws on real data from similar past projects.
- **B**: Analogous uses overall data from a similar past project; parametric uses a statistical relationship between variables.
- **C**: Analogous estimating is used for cost estimates while parametric estimating is used only for schedule estimates.
- **D**: Analogous estimating is a bottom-up technique while parametric estimating is a top-down technique.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) captures the standard PMI distinction: analogous estimating extrapolates from a similar past project at a high level, while parametric estimating multiplies a quantity by a unit-cost or unit-duration factor (e.g., $/sqm, hours/feature). (A) is wrong — analogous is generally less accurate than parametric or bottom-up. (C) is wrong — both techniques apply to cost and schedule. (D) reverses common terminology: analogous is top-down, not bottom-up, and parametric is calculation-based rather than bottom-up.
