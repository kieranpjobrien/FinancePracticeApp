# Question

A senior developer on your project notifies you they need eight weeks of approved personal leave starting next month, which falls during a critical integration phase. They are difficult to replace at short notice. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with the developer to confirm timing, then assess the schedule impact and identify coverage options before bringing recommendations to the sponsor.
- **B**: Submit a change request to extend the integration phase by eight weeks to absorb the loss of the developer.
- **C**: Ask the functional manager to deny or defer the leave because the project cannot accommodate it.
- **D**: Backfill the role immediately with an external contractor so the integration phase stays on schedule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to gather facts, assess impact, and bring options before invoking changes, escalations, or spending. (A) does that — confirm the dates, model the impact, then present coverage options. (B) submits a change request before any analysis or alternative has been considered. (C) attempts to override an approved personal leave decision the PM does not control and disregards the team member as a person. (D) commits external spend before exploring cross-coverage, re-sequencing, or partial-coverage options.
