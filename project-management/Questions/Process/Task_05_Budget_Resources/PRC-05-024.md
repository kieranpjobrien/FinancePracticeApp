# Question

Near the end of the fiscal year, the finance department asks if the project can spend remaining budget quickly to avoid losing it. However, there's no productive use for the funds. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Spend the money on anything to use it up
- **B**: Explain that spending without productive purpose wastes organisational resources, and suggest carrying funds forward or returning them - project budgets should be managed for value, not fiscal year games
- **C**: Refuse to engage with finance
- **D**: Add scope to use the budget

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Ethical budget management means spending for value, not to preserve budget. Explain: wasteful spending doesn't serve the organisation. Suggest alternatives: can funds be carried forward? Returned? These are better than waste. This demonstrates fiduciary responsibility even when systems create perverse incentives.

Option A wastes organisational resources.

Option C doesn't address the situation constructively.

Option D adds scope that isn't needed for project objectives.
