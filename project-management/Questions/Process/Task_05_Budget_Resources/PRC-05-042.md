# Question

A vendor proposes a time-and-materials contract while the sponsor prefers fixed-price for budget certainty. The scope is not yet well-defined. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose fixed-price for certainty
- **B**: Explain the risk trade-off: fixed-price with unclear scope either gets high bids (vendor risk padding) or change orders later. Consider phased approach: T&M for discovery, then fixed-price for defined work.
- **C**: Always choose time-and-materials
- **D**: Contract type doesn't matter

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Contract type should match the situation. Fixed-price with unclear scope is risky: vendors add contingency (higher price) or the buyer faces change orders. T&M shifts risk to buyer but may be appropriate for uncertain scope. Hybrid: perhaps T&M for early phases to define scope, then fixed-price for implementation. Help the sponsor understand trade-offs.

Options A and C don't consider the specific situation.

Option D ignores significant risk allocation differences.
