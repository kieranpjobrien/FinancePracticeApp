# Question

What does the To-Complete Performance Index (TCPI) measure?

## Options

- **A**: The cost performance that must be achieved on the remaining work to meet a stated target (typically BAC or EAC).
- **B**: The historical cost performance achieved on completed work, expressed as the ratio of EV to AC.
- **C**: The probability that the project will finish within its approved budget given current burn rate.
- **D**: The percentage of remaining budget that has been committed but not yet invoiced.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

TCPI = (BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC) for the BAC target, or (BAC - EV) / (EAC - AC) for the EAC target. (A) is the textbook definition. (B) describes CPI, which is backward-looking. (C) describes a probabilistic forecast, not the deterministic ratio TCPI computes. (D) describes commitment accounting, not a TCPI value.
