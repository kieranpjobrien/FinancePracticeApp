# Question

A procurement-heavy project is over budget on labour but under on materials, netting to on-budget overall. The sponsor reviewing only the net number is satisfied. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Present the decomposed variance (labour over, materials under) with root causes for both and the implications for remaining work.
- **B**: Continue reporting only the net on-budget position since the sponsor is satisfied.
- **C**: Shift funds from the materials line to the labour line to make both look on budget.
- **D**: Raise a change request to rebaseline both categories so variance disappears.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Aggregated numbers can mask serious issues. (A) is honest reporting and helps the sponsor make good decisions about remaining work. (B) hiding the underlying variance is a disservice to the sponsor and likely to fail when labour runs out of material offset. (C) reclassifying actuals to hide variance is unethical. (D) rebaselining to hide actuals misuses the change control process.
