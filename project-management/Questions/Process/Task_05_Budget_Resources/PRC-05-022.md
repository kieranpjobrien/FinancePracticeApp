# Question

The project is tracking under budget, and stakeholders are pleased. However, the PM notices that the team is cutting corners on testing to stay within budget. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Celebrate the budget performance
- **B**: Address the quality concern with the team, explain that budget performance shouldn't come at the expense of quality, and adjust approach to maintain both cost and quality objectives
- **C**: Encourage more corner-cutting to improve budget performance further
- **D**: Report only the budget metric to stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Budget performance that sacrifices quality is false economy. Address: explain that quality issues will cost more to fix later. Balance: find sustainable ways to achieve both cost and quality. Adjust: if budget is genuinely too tight for required quality, that's information for stakeholders. Good performance on one metric shouldn't hide problems in another.

Option A ignores the quality problem.

Option C compounds the problem.

Option D hides material information from stakeholders.
