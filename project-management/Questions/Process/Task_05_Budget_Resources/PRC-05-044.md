# Question

You are staffing a 12-month build phase. Contractor day-rates are roughly 50% higher than fully-loaded employee costs, but contractors carry no benefits, training, or long-term commitment. How should the PM frame the staffing mix decision?

## Options

- **A**: Use contractors for the entire build to maximise scalability and avoid long-term headcount commitments.
- **B**: Use employees for the entire build because the lower fully-loaded cost makes it the more economical option.
- **C**: Use employees for core capability and contractors for peaks, specialist skills, or bounded scopes.
- **D**: Default to whichever option the functional manager prefers, since they ultimately own the resource pool.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) reflects PMI's tailoring principle — the mix depends on the work, the duration, and what capability the organisation wants to retain. (A) ignores the higher day-rate and forfeits institutional knowledge. (B) assumes employees can be allocated at will and ignores skill gaps and demand variability. (D) abdicates a project-level decision to the functional manager, who has different incentives than the project.
