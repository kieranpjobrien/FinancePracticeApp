# Question

Five projects in your portfolio are competing for the same pool of senior cloud engineers. Each project manager is lobbying their sponsor for priority access. As one of those PMs, what is the BEST action you can take?

## Options

- **A**: Accept whatever allocation you receive and re-plan around the available capacity once it is confirmed.
- **B**: Lobby your sponsor harder than the other PMs so your project secures the resources first.
- **C**: Propose a portfolio-level governance forum that prioritises engineer allocation against strategic value, dependencies, and risk.
- **D**: Hire external contractors with similar skills so the in-house pool is not on your project's critical path.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to recognise when an issue belongs at portfolio level and to push the conversation there rather than competing in a way that suboptimises for the organisation. (C) replaces lobbying with governance — a transparent prioritisation against strategic value and dependencies. (A) is passive and forfeits a legitimate role in shaping the decision. (B) is exactly the dysfunctional behaviour the question describes; lobbying intensity is not a good allocation mechanism. (D) is a workaround that adds cost and risk and avoids the underlying portfolio problem.
