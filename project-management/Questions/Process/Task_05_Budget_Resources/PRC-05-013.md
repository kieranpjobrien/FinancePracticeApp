# Question

The project is forecast to exceed its budget. The PM has identified several options: reduce scope, request additional funding, or accept lower quality. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the least expensive option
- **B**: Present all options with their trade-offs to the sponsor for decision-making, including analysis of impacts and recommendations
- **C**: Reduce scope without consultation
- **D**: Request additional funding as the default option

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Budget decisions with significant trade-offs require sponsor involvement. Present options: what are the choices? Trade-offs: what does each option cost in terms of scope, quality, or additional funding? Recommendation: what does the PM advise? This enables informed decision-making at the appropriate authority level.

Option A doesn't involve decision-makers in a significant decision.

Option C changes scope without appropriate authority.

Option D assumes funding is the preferred solution without exploring alternatives.
