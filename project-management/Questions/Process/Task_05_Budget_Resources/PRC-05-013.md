# Question

A team member is chronically over-allocated across this project and two others. Their work quality is starting to slip. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the over-allocation since all three projects are important.
- **B**: Escalate to the sponsor for a ruling on project priority.
- **C**: Reassign all their work to other team members immediately.
- **D**: Discuss the allocation with the team member and their functional manager, map actual commitments, and agree on realistic allocation or transition of work.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Over-allocation is a shared problem best solved through peer-level conversation grounded in real data. (D) involves the right people with the right information. (A) acceptance silently damages quality and the person. (B) escalation without first mapping the actual situation wastes sponsor time. (C) unilateral reassignment disrupts work without understanding priorities or consulting the team member.
