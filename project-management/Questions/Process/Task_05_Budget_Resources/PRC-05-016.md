# Question

A critical specialist on the project has given notice and will leave in three weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Post the role externally immediately and accept a three-week coverage gap before replacement starts.
- **B**: Ask the specialist to stay by offering a retention bonus funded from the project contingency reserve.
- **C**: Identify at-risk knowledge, create a transition plan with the specialist, and explore interim coverage.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to block the departure through HR channels before the notice period ends.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM's focus is continuity of work and knowledge transfer. (C) starts by understanding the exposure and designing a practical response — standard transition-management practice. (A) accepting a gap without mitigation risks project outcomes. (B) offering a retention bonus is outside the PM's authority and doesn't address the underlying reason. (D) trying to block a departure is unethical and usually unsuccessful.
