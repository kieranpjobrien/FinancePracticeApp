# Question

The project budget was approved based on rough estimates. Now that detailed planning is complete, the PM realises the project will cost significantly more than approved. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work within the original budget regardless
- **B**: Present the refined estimate to the sponsor, explain what's driving the increase, and discuss options: additional funding, scope reduction, or project cancellation
- **C**: Start the project and hope costs come in lower
- **D**: Hide the estimate until budget runs out

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Better estimates mean better decisions. Present: share the refined estimate with transparency. Explain: what's driving the difference - more complete understanding, missed requirements, market changes? Options: given the true cost, does the business case still hold? What adjustments are possible? This enables an informed go/no-go decision.

Option A may lead to failure or compromised outcomes.

Option C is wishful thinking with predictable consequences.

Option D is unethical and delays the inevitable.
