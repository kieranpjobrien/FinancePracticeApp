# Question

Your 15-person product team spans Sydney, Bengaluru, and Dublin on a 24-month predictive programme. Retrospective feedback says remote members feel "second-class," decisions happen in the Sydney office, and documentation lags. Two Dublin leads are quietly interviewing elsewhere. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that all key design decisions wait until a weekly all-hands meeting that all three sites must attend live.
- **B**: Arrange quarterly face-to-face workshops in each city to build cohesion and keep the rest of the work distributed as now.
- **C**: Ask site leads to produce written summaries of local decisions and share them to a central wiki within 48 hours.
- **D**: Redesign working agreements, decision logs, and ceremonies for remote-first equity, then co-create them with all sites.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Remote-first practices treat distributed members as default, not as an afterthought, and are best co-created so the team owns them. (D) addresses root causes of the second-class dynamic. (A) punishes all sites with mandatory live meetings across time zones. (B) treats symptoms with occasional travel. (C) is one useful practice but does not address the decision-making culture.
