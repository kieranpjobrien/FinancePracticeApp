# Question

What is Variance at Completion (VAC)?

## Options

- **A**: The difference between EV and AC
- **B**: The difference between BAC and EAC, indicating how much the project is expected to be over or under budget at completion
- **C**: The variance in resource costs
- **D**: The standard deviation of estimates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. VAC = BAC - EAC. It shows the expected variance between the original budget (BAC) and the forecast final cost (EAC). A negative VAC indicates expected overrun; positive indicates expected underrun. It helps stakeholders understand the bottom-line budget impact.

Option A describes Cost Variance (CV).

Option C and D are different concepts.
