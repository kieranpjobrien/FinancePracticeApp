# Question

Midway through a pharma validation project, an identified risk materialises and the project consumes most of its contingency reserve. A new, previously unidentified risk now emerges that threatens a further $250,000 exposure. The cost baseline has no remaining contingency to draw on. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reallocate uncommitted budget from later work packages to fund the response and inform the sponsor of the change.
- **B**: Submit a change request for additional funding from management reserve with an updated risk analysis and response plan.
- **C**: Absorb the cost within the current cost baseline by reducing scope in low-priority deliverables to free capacity.
- **D**: Delay action until the risk becomes an issue, then respond with whatever funds remain in the baseline at the time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Management reserve is held outside the cost baseline for unknown-unknowns and is released by the sponsor through change control. (B) follows the integrated process correctly. (A) raids baselined work packages without change control. (C) cuts scope unilaterally without stakeholder agreement. (D) violates proactive risk management by waiting for an issue to occur.
