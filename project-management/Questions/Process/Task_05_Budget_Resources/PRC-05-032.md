# Question

Your project requires a specialist piece of test equipment for two months only. Purchase price is $100,000; rental is $20,000 per month with no minimum term. Which is the BEST recommendation for the procurement decision?

## Options

- **A**: Purchase the equipment because owning the asset retains residual value for the organisation.
- **B**: Rent the equipment for the two-month usage period unless analysis shows future projects will need it or resale value justifies purchase.
- **C**: Rent the equipment because operating expenditure is always preferable to capital expenditure on projects.
- **D**: Defer the decision to the procurement department since they own equipment-buy-versus-rent policy.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) applies the make-or-buy / lease-or-buy analysis correctly: rent costs $40,000 vs purchase at $100,000 for the project's needs, so renting is cheaper unless there is a documented future use or resale value that closes the $60,000 gap. (A) and (C) apply absolute rules without analysis — neither is universally true. (D) abdicates the analysis; procurement enacts decisions but the PM is responsible for the project-level cost-benefit case before engaging them.
