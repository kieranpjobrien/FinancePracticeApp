# Question

A project team of 6 people is working at capacity. A critical team member announces they're leaving in two weeks. The project has 3 months remaining. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Request the team work overtime to compensate
- **B**: Assess the impact, identify critical knowledge to transfer, and begin recruiting/reassignment
- **C**: Ask the departing member to stay longer
- **D**: Reduce project scope to match remaining capacity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM should first assess the situation: What work does this person own? What knowledge needs transfer? Then take parallel actions: start knowledge transfer immediately (2 weeks is short), and begin sourcing a replacement (internal reassignment or external hire). Understanding impact informs the right response.

Option A (overtime) may be needed short-term but isn't sustainable and doesn't replace the lost resource.

Option C (ask to stay) may be worth trying but isn't the PM's first action - they need to plan for the departure first.

Option D (reduce scope) may be necessary but is premature without assessing whether replacement is possible.
