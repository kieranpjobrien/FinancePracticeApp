# Question

The project team is consistently over-forecasting resource needs. Actuals are running 20% below estimates. What is the BEST PM response?

## Options

- **A**: Refine estimation practices with the team — analyse why estimates are high, use historical data, and recalibrate estimation techniques.
- **B**: Cut the next set of estimates by 20% across the board.
- **C**: Let the over-estimation continue to create a safety buffer.
- **D**: Assign estimation authority to a single senior estimator instead of the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Over-estimation is a process problem best solved by analysing the cause and improving the approach. (A) addresses root cause and builds estimating capability. (B) blanket cuts ignore the actual distribution and may swing to under-estimation. (C) deliberate padding hides performance and misallocates capacity. (D) centralising removes team ownership and may produce worse estimates without their domain knowledge.
