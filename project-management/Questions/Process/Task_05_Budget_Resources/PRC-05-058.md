# Question

Three sprints into a hybrid delivery, two developers have independently complained to the project manager that management's public recognition programme only names tech leads, never contributors, even when junior staff drove the work. Morale is declining. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the leads privately on crediting the team and leave the corporate recognition programme untouched for the time being.
- **B**: Launch a parallel peer-to-peer recognition ritual within the team to balance the formal programme and lift morale quickly.
- **C**: Raise the issue with HR and senior management, proposing changes to how the recognition programme names contributors.
- **D**: Reassure the developers the work is valued and focus the team back on delivery to reduce the distraction from recognition.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The systemic issue is a misaligned recognition programme; the PM advocates for the team by raising it with decision-makers. (C) is the servant-leader action. (A) addresses only part of the problem. (B) is a useful ritual but leaves the systemic inequity untouched. (D) dismisses a legitimate motivation concern and avoids advocacy.
