# Question

You manage a project in a matrix organisation. The functional manager who provides three developers has begun charging your project at senior-rate ($180/hr) instead of the mid-level rate ($120/hr) agreed in the resource plan, claiming the assigned staff are "more senior than originally scoped." Your monthly burn rate is now 30% over plan. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise a budget change request through the change control board to reflect the higher resource costs.
- **B**: Pull the developers from the project and request replacement resources at the originally agreed rate.
- **C**: Meet with the functional manager to review the original resource agreement and clarify the basis for the rate change.
- **D**: Notify the sponsor that the functional manager is overcharging and request intervention to enforce the agreed rates.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to seek understanding before escalating or invoking formal processes. (C) addresses the variance at its source by clarifying whether the rate change reflects a legitimate scope change, a misunderstanding, or an unauthorised charge — without that information no other action is appropriate. (A) processes a baseline change before confirming the change is even justified. (B) is a unilateral, disruptive action that damages the working relationship and may breach the resource agreement. (D) escalates prematurely; the PM should attempt resolution at peer level before involving the sponsor.
