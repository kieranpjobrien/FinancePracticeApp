# Question

What is a fishbone diagram used for in issue management?

## Options

- **A**: Scheduling issue resolution activities
- **B**: Identifying potential causes of an issue by categorizing them into major categories
- **C**: Tracking issue status over time
- **D**: Assigning issues to team members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A fishbone diagram (also called Ishikawa or cause-and-effect diagram) helps identify potential causes of an issue by organizing them into categories (often People, Process, Equipment, Materials, Environment, Management). This structured brainstorming helps ensure comprehensive consideration of possible causes.

Option A describes scheduling activities.

Option C describes status tracking.

Option D describes issue assignment.
