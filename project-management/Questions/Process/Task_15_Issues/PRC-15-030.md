# Question

What is the primary use of a fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram in project issue management?

## Options

- **A**: To assign issue ownership across functional groups based on the most likely root cause of the problem.
- **B**: To plot issue trends over time and detect inflection points in how often similar problems recur.
- **C**: To rank issues by frequency and concentrate effort on the vital few that drive most of the impact.
- **D**: To brainstorm and categorise potential causes of an issue across categories like people, process, and equipment.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A fishbone diagram is a structured cause-and-effect brainstorming tool, organising candidate causes by category. (D) is the textbook description. (A) is an ownership-assignment activity, not what the diagram does. (B) describes a control chart or run chart. (C) describes a Pareto analysis.
