# Question

What should happen when a risk that was being monitored actually occurs?

## Options

- **A**: Remove it from the risk register
- **B**: Convert it to an issue and manage it through issue management processes
- **C**: Continue monitoring it as a risk
- **D**: Immediately escalate to senior management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When a risk occurs, it transitions from potential (risk) to actual (issue). It should be moved from the risk register to the issue log and managed through issue resolution processes. The risk response plans may inform the issue response, but it's now an active problem requiring immediate management.

Option A loses tracking without proper issue management.

Option C continues treating it as uncertain when it's now certain.

Option D may be appropriate for significant issues but isn't the defining characteristic of risk-to-issue transition.
