# Question

A project has accumulated 47 open issues in the issue log over 3 months. Team members complain they can't keep track of what's important. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Close all issues older than 30 days to reduce the backlog
- **B**: Prioritise issues by impact and urgency, then focus team effort on resolving high-priority items
- **C**: Assign each issue to a specific team member regardless of their workload
- **D**: Stop logging new issues until the backlog is cleared

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Issue management requires prioritisation. Not all issues are equally important - some are critical blockers, others are minor inconveniences. The PM should work with the team to assess and rank issues, then focus resolution efforts on high-impact items. A prioritised list is easier to manage than an undifferentiated pile.

Option A (close old issues) loses potentially important information. Old issues aren't necessarily resolved issues - they may be deferred but still relevant.

Option C (assign regardless of workload) creates overload and doesn't address prioritisation. People assigned too much can't effectively resolve anything.

Option D (stop logging) is dangerous. New issues still need to be tracked even if older ones exist. The solution is better management, not information suppression.
