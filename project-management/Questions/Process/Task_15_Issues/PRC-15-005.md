# Question

The issue log on your project has 47 open entries after three months. Team members say they cannot tell what is important and start ignoring the log. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Bulk-close any issue more than 30 days old to bring the active count down to a workable size.
- **B**: Triage the log with the team using impact and urgency, then focus resolution effort on the highest-priority items and set review cadence for the rest.
- **C**: Spread the open issues evenly across team members so every issue has an owner accountable for resolution.
- **D**: Stop accepting new issues into the log until the existing 47 are resolved.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A bloated, unprioritised issue log is a triage problem, not a deletion problem. (B) restores signal by ranking and focusing effort. (A) loses information that may still be relevant. (C) creates artificial ownership without considering capacity or priority. (D) suppresses new information, which is dangerous and never the right move.
