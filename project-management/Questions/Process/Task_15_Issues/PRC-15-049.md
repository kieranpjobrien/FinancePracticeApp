# Question

A Scrum team of eight developers keeps routing minor technical issues directly to the project manager instead of resolving them within the team. The PM is becoming a bottleneck. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the team on self-organisation, clarify which issue types the team owns, and redirect items back to team members.
- **B**: Continue triaging issues personally to maintain visibility and ensure consistent prioritisation across the delivery workstream each week.
- **C**: Create a dedicated issue coordinator role within the team so one person centrally manages all incoming technical items daily.
- **D**: Introduce a formal approval gate that forces developers to justify why each logged issue needs the project manager's input.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Servant leadership in agile contexts removes impediments to team self-organisation rather than inserting the PM as a gatekeeper. (A) develops team capability and respects Scrum accountabilities. (B) reinforces the bottleneck and deskills the team. (C) creates a mini-bottleneck in someone else. (D) uses control rather than coaching and damages psychological safety.
