# Question

A stakeholder repeatedly raises the same issue at every meeting, even though the project manager believes it has been addressed. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop inviting the stakeholder to meetings
- **B**: Ignore the stakeholder since the issue is closed
- **C**: Have a direct conversation to understand why the stakeholder doesn't consider the issue resolved, and either provide more information or reopen the issue if their concern is valid
- **D**: Escalate the stakeholder as a problem

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Persistent concerns deserve investigation. Either the stakeholder doesn't understand the resolution (communication issue), they have information suggesting it's not actually resolved (valid concern), or they have different resolution criteria than the team (expectations gap). Understanding the disconnect enables proper response.

Option A excludes a stakeholder without understanding their concern.

Option B dismisses a stakeholder who may have valid concerns.

Option D treats the stakeholder as a problem rather than seeking understanding.
