# Question

A senior stakeholder keeps raising the same closed issue at every steering meeting. From your view, the issue is fully resolved and documented. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Have a one-on-one with the stakeholder to understand why they do not consider it resolved, then either share the missing context or reopen the issue if their concern is genuinely valid.
- **B**: Stop inviting the stakeholder to steering meetings to keep the agenda moving forward.
- **C**: Reply at the next meeting with a clear public statement that the issue is closed and ask the chair to disallow further mention of it.
- **D**: Escalate the stakeholder's behaviour to the sponsor as an obstacle to project progress.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Repeated raising of a closed issue almost always means a missed expectation, missed information, or a real concern that has not yet been addressed. (A) goes to the source. (B) excludes a stakeholder rather than understanding them. (C) silences a senior voice publicly, which damages the relationship. (D) escalates without first attempting direct resolution.
