# Question

A vendor has failed to deliver a critical component, causing a project issue. The vendor blames their supplier, and the supplier blames the vendor for unclear specifications. The project is delayed while they argue. What should the project manager focus on?

## Options

- **A**: Determine who is at fault before taking action
- **B**: Wait for the vendor and supplier to resolve their dispute
- **C**: Focus on resolving the immediate impact to the project (alternative solutions, revised timeline) while separately addressing the vendor issue through appropriate contractual and relationship channels
- **D**: Terminate the vendor contract immediately

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM's primary responsibility is the project's success. Separate the immediate need (getting the component or alternative) from the contractual/blame resolution. Address project impact through problem-solving (alternatives, timeline adjustment), while contractual matters are handled through proper channels (procurement, legal).

Option A delays project resolution while assigning blame.

Option B puts project success at the mercy of an external dispute.

Option D may have contractual implications and doesn't solve the immediate need.
