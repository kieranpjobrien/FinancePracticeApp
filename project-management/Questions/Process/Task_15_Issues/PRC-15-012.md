# Question

Your prime contractor has missed delivery of a critical hardware component. They blame their sub-supplier; the sub-supplier blames the prime for ambiguous specifications. The project is delayed while the two argue. What should the project manager focus on FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Identify which party is contractually at fault before deciding what to do about the project schedule and downstream tasks.
- **B**: Wait for the contractor and sub-supplier to settle their dispute themselves, since the resolution will determine the next step.
- **C**: Address the immediate project impact by exploring alternative sources while procurement and legal pursue the contractual issue.
- **D**: Issue a notice of contract termination to the prime contractor to force them to resolve the dispute with their sub-supplier now.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM owns project outcome; legal and procurement own the contractual dispute. (C) splits the two cleanly so the project does not stall while the lawyers work. (A) treats blame as a prerequisite to action and burns time. (B) hands the project's schedule to an external dispute. (D) is a unilateral, drastic contractual move that the PM does not generally have authority to issue and that does not address immediate need.
