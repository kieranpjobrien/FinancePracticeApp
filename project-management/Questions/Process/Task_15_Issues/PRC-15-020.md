# Question

An issue's resolution would slip your project by two weeks if not actioned. The proposed fix would, however, force schedule changes on two adjacent projects you do not control. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the fix on this project; defending your timeline is your primary responsibility and the impact on other projects is their PMs' problem to absorb.
- **B**: Accept the two-week slip on this project to avoid disturbing the other two, and note the impact in your status report.
- **C**: Approach the other two project managers directly and try to negotiate an accommodation between yourselves.
- **D**: Escalate to programme or portfolio management with an impact analysis covering all three projects so a cross-project decision can be made by the right authority.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Cross-project trade-offs need authority that spans those projects. (D) takes it to programme or portfolio level with the data needed to decide. (A) imposes consequences on projects the PM has no authority over. (B) accepts a two-week slip without exploring options. (C) is fine for early information sharing but cannot make a binding cross-project commitment.
