# Question

A team member has built a clever workaround for a recurring issue. It works, but it lives entirely in their head. If they leave, the workaround knowledge goes with them. What should the project manager ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Document the workaround in the issue resolution notes and have a second team member learn and own it as a backup.
- **B**: Schedule the permanent fix immediately so the workaround is no longer needed and the knowledge dependency goes away.
- **C**: Ask HR to flag the team member as a retention priority while the workaround remains the only option.
- **D**: Accept the dependency and address it only if the team member announces they are leaving.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Knowledge transfer and documentation are the standard PMI response to single points of failure. (A) handles both. (B) may not be feasible or worth the cost on its own merits and is the wrong reason to schedule the work. (C) treats a knowledge problem as a retention problem. (D) waits for the risk to materialise rather than treating it now.
