# Question

A team member has developed a creative workaround for an issue that works well but isn't documented. If this team member leaves, the workaround knowledge would be lost. What should the project manager ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Hope the team member doesn't leave
- **B**: Prevent the team member from leaving
- **C**: Document the workaround, ensure it's in the issue resolution notes, and have another team member learn it
- **D**: Implement the permanent fix immediately to eliminate the workaround

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Workarounds represent operational knowledge that should be documented and shared. This ensures continuity if the team member is unavailable, enables others to use the workaround, and captures learning. Whether a permanent fix is pursued is a separate decision - the workaround should be documented regardless.

Option A is not a management strategy.

Option B isn't realistic or appropriate.

Option D may not be the best use of resources, and the workaround should be documented anyway.
