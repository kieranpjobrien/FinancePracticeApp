# Question

During a sprint review, the product owner spots what they call a "critical" production bug in the feature being demonstrated. The team has six days left in the sprint with committed work in flight. What should the Scrum Master suggest?

## Options

- **A**: Park the bug in the backlog and address it in the next sprint to protect the existing sprint commitment and team velocity.
- **B**: Stop the sprint immediately so the entire team can focus on the production bug until it is fully resolved and deployed.
- **C**: Have team members work overtime so the bug is fixed without disturbing the sprint backlog or committed delivery dates.
- **D**: Assess actual severity with the product owner, and if it warrants disruption, swap the bug in by removing equivalent work.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) follows the standard Scrum response to mid-sprint scope changes: the PO assesses value, and if the bug enters the sprint, equivalent work comes out to protect sustainable pace. (A) ignores the possibility that the bug really is critical. (B) is an over-reaction without verifying severity. (C) sacrifices sustainable pace, which violates a core agile principle.
