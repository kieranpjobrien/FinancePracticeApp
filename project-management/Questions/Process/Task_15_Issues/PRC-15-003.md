# Question

During a sprint review, a critical production bug is discovered in the feature being demonstrated. The product owner wants it fixed immediately, but the team has sprint commitments to complete. How should the Scrum Master advise the team?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with sprint commitments and address the bug in the next sprint
- **B**: Stop the sprint and focus entirely on the production bug
- **C**: Work with the product owner to assess severity, then decide whether to pull the bug into the current sprint by removing other items
- **D**: Assign the bug to team members as overtime work outside the sprint

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Production bugs vary in severity. A critical bug affecting users may warrant disrupting the sprint; a minor issue may not. The Scrum Master should facilitate a conversation with the PO to assess impact and make an informed trade-off decision. If the bug enters the sprint, other work should be removed to maintain sustainable pace.

Option A (next sprint) may be appropriate for low-severity bugs but ignores the possibility that this is genuinely critical.

Option B (stop everything) may be overreaction for bugs that aren't truly critical. Not all production bugs warrant abandoning sprint goals.

Option D (overtime) is unsustainable and violates agile principles. The team should work at a sustainable pace with protected capacity.
