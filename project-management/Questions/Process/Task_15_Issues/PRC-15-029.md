# Question

A team is working on resolving a complex technical issue. After two days of investigation, they still don't understand the root cause. What should the project manager suggest?

## Options

- **A**: Continue investigation indefinitely until the cause is found
- **B**: Implement any fix that seems to work without understanding the cause
- **C**: Time-box further investigation, consider bringing in additional expertise, and evaluate whether implementing a fix while continuing investigation is appropriate
- **D**: Escalate to management since the team has failed

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Extended investigation needs management. Time-boxing prevents indefinite investigation. Additional expertise may break the deadlock. Sometimes implementing a fix that works while continuing to investigate (especially if impact is ongoing) is appropriate. The PM helps structure this decision rather than leaving it open-ended.

Option A allows indefinite resource consumption.

Option B may fix symptoms while the root cause causes other problems.

Option D frames the situation as failure rather than a difficult problem.
