# Question

A security vulnerability is discovered in a third-party component used by the project. The vendor hasn't released a patch. The vulnerability could potentially be exploited but hasn't been. Business stakeholders want to continue with deployment to meet a critical deadline. Security stakeholders want to delay until the vulnerability is patched. How should this be resolved?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with deployment since the deadline is critical
- **B**: Delay indefinitely until the vendor patches
- **C**: Facilitate a risk-informed decision with appropriate stakeholders, considering mitigating controls, probability of exploitation, business impact of delay, and clearly documenting the risk acceptance if proceeding
- **D**: Let the security team decide since it's a security issue

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a risk-based decision requiring input from multiple perspectives. The PM should facilitate analysis: what's the actual risk (probability x impact)? Are there mitigating controls possible? What's the business impact of delay? The decision should be made by appropriate authority with full information, not by any single perspective dominating.

Option A ignores legitimate security concerns.

Option B ignores legitimate business concerns.

Option D gives one stakeholder group veto power without balancing business needs.
