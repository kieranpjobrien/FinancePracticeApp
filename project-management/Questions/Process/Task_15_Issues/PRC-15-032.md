# Question

A high-severity vulnerability has been disclosed in a third-party library used by your project. The vendor has not released a patch. Business stakeholders want to deploy on schedule; security stakeholders want to delay. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with deployment because the deadline drives business value and there is no confirmed exploit yet in the wild.
- **B**: Delay deployment indefinitely until the vendor releases a patch, on the basis that a disclosed vulnerability cannot be accepted.
- **C**: Defer to the security team because this is a technical security matter and the business should not override their judgement here.
- **D**: Facilitate a risk-informed decision with the appropriate authorities, presenting exploitation likelihood, compensating controls, and documented residual-risk acceptance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is PMI's risk-and-stakeholder approach: bring the right authorities together with full information and document the resulting decision. (A) overrides security without analysis. (B) overrides business without analysis. (C) hands binding authority to one stakeholder group rather than balancing perspectives.
