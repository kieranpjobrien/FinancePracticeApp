# Question

Two team members have different opinions about the cause of a technical issue and therefore recommend different solutions. Each is confident they're right. The project manager has limited technical expertise in this area. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pick the solution from the more senior team member
- **B**: Split the difference and implement part of each solution
- **C**: Facilitate a structured analysis to examine both hypotheses, possibly through prototyping or testing, and bring in additional expertise if needed to reach a fact-based decision
- **D**: Randomly choose one solution to avoid delay

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Technical disputes should be resolved through evidence, not authority or randomness. The PM can facilitate by structuring the analysis: what evidence would support or refute each hypothesis? Can both be tested? Is there an expert who can evaluate? The goal is a fact-based decision, not a political one.

Option A uses seniority rather than evidence to decide.

Option B may create a solution that doesn't address the actual cause.

Option D is arbitrary and may pick the wrong solution.
