# Question

Two engineers disagree on the cause of a tricky technical issue and propose different fixes. Both are confident. The project manager does not have deep technical knowledge in this area. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the more senior engineer's judgement, since seniority is a reasonable tie-breaker for tricky technical debates like this.
- **B**: Implement both fixes in parallel and pick whichever appears to work first based on user feedback in production over time.
- **C**: Facilitate analysis of the two hypotheses with a small spike or test for evidence, bringing a third specialist if inconclusive.
- **D**: Pick one of the two solutions at random to avoid spending time on the dispute and to keep delivery moving forward today.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A PM without deep domain knowledge resolves technical disputes through evidence and facilitation, not authority or coin flips. (C) does that. (A) substitutes seniority for evidence. (B) ships untested code into production as the experiment, which is reckless. (D) is arbitrary and risks the wrong fix.
