# Question

Analysis of the issue log reveals that 60% of issues originate from one particular area of the project. The team working on that area is frustrated that their issues get the most scrutiny. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce scrutiny of that area to improve morale
- **B**: Replace the team working on that area
- **C**: Investigate why that area generates more issues - is it inherently complex, under-resourced, has unclear requirements, or has process problems - and address root causes while reframing issues as process improvement opportunities rather than criticism
- **D**: Stop tracking issues from that area

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Concentration of issues indicates something about that area: it may be inherently complex, under-resourced, working with unclear requirements, or have process problems. Understanding why enables targeted improvement. Reframing as process improvement rather than criticism helps the team engage constructively rather than defensively.

Option A ignores a significant source of project issues.

Option B blames the team without understanding root causes.

Option D hides the problem without solving it.
