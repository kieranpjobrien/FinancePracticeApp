# Question

An issue has been open for several sprints with no resolution. Each retrospective, the team acknowledges it but no one takes ownership. The issue affects team efficiency but isn't critical. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the issue since the team can't seem to resolve it
- **B**: Assign the issue to a specific team member to drive resolution
- **C**: Facilitate a focused discussion to understand blockers, break down the issue into actionable steps, and help the team commit to specific actions
- **D**: Escalate the issue to management for resolution

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Persistent non-resolution often indicates the issue feels too big or unclear. Facilitation helps: understand what's blocking progress, break into smaller actionable steps, and create concrete commitments. The Scrum Master enables the team to resolve it rather than doing it for them or forcing assignment.

Option A gives up on something that's actually affecting efficiency.

Option B imposes ownership on a self-organizing team rather than facilitating their commitment.

Option D escalates what's within the team's ability to resolve with proper facilitation.
