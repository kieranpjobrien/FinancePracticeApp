# Question

An issue affecting team efficiency has appeared in five consecutive Scrum retrospectives. Each time the team agrees it is a problem but no action is taken. It is not critical, but it is corrosive. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a focused retro on this issue, decompose it into small concrete steps, and help the team make a specific commitment with an owner and a sprint deadline.
- **B**: Pick the most experienced team member and assign them to drive the issue to closure.
- **C**: Close the issue in the log, since the team has had repeated chances to act and has not done so.
- **D**: Escalate to the engineering manager and request that they direct the team to resolve it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a self-organising team, the Scrum Master removes impediments to action rather than imposing solutions. (A) attacks the usual cause of stalled retro items: the work feels too big and unclear. (B) overrides team self-organisation. (C) discards a real efficiency issue rather than enabling action. (D) escalates work that is well within the team's authority to fix.
