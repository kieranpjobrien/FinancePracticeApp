# Question

When is it appropriate for a project manager to escalate an issue?

## Options

- **A**: As soon as the issue is logged so leadership has full visibility from the outset.
- **B**: Only at the next scheduled steering committee meeting, regardless of the issue's urgency.
- **C**: When the issue cannot be resolved at the PM's level or requires authority the PM does not hold.
- **D**: Never; escalation undermines the PM's credibility and should be avoided where possible.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats escalation as a tool for unblocking, not a default behaviour. (C) captures that. (A) overloads leadership with operational issues. (B) ignores urgency and time-criticality. (D) refuses a legitimate management tool when authority is genuinely lacking.
