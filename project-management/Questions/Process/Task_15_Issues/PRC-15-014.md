# Question

When should an issue be escalated?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately when identified
- **B**: Only at scheduled status meetings
- **C**: When it cannot be resolved at the current level, exceeds the PM's authority, or requires decisions from higher authority
- **D**: Never - the PM should resolve all issues

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Escalation is appropriate when the issue exceeds the PM's ability to resolve (technically or organizationally), requires authority beyond the PM's level (budget increases, scope changes), or needs decisions that only higher authority can make. Escalation is a tool for getting appropriate help, not a sign of failure.

Option A escalates unnecessarily, overloading higher levels.

Option B may delay time-sensitive escalations.

Option D ignores the reality that some issues require higher authority.
