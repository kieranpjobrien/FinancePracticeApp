# Question

On a hybrid insurance project, four urgent issues land on the same day and the delivery team has capacity to tackle only two in parallel without compromising in-flight work. Stakeholders for each issue are lobbying for priority. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign all four issues to the team immediately and let them self-organise on sequencing as work progresses through the week.
- **B**: Work the two whose stakeholders are politically most influential first, then address the remaining pair during the following week.
- **C**: Apply objective prioritisation criteria against business impact and urgency, sequence work transparently, and communicate the rationale to all four stakeholders.
- **D**: Split the team's capacity across all four issues in parallel so every stakeholder sees their item progressing simultaneously each day.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects prioritisation to be data-driven and transparent, especially under stakeholder pressure. (C) uses documented criteria and explains the sequencing, preserving trust. (A) creates overload and risks in-flight damage. (B) rewards political pressure over impact. (D) splits focus until nothing finishes, violating flow principles.
