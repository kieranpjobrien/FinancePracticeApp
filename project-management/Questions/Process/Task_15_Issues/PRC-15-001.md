# Question

A defect in a payments integration is found mid-sprint. Without a fix the project will slip about two weeks, but the team has identified a workaround that lets development continue. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the issue once the workaround is in place, since development is no longer blocked by the defect.
- **B**: Hold all development until the underlying defect is properly fixed to avoid layering further work on top of a latent flaw.
- **C**: Apply the workaround to keep progress moving, and keep the defect open in the issue log with an owner and target date.
- **D**: Escalate the defect to the sponsor immediately so they can decide whether to keep the project running or pause it now.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A workaround buys time, but the underlying defect must remain visible until properly resolved or it becomes silent technical debt. (C) does both: protect progress and protect the record. (A) closes a real defect prematurely. (B) is heavy-handed when a viable workaround exists. (D) escalates a problem the team already has a solution for, which is not what PMI calls escalation criteria.
