# Question

An issue has been assigned to a team member who, you suspect, lacks the skills to resolve it. They have not asked for help and progress has stalled. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the issue to a more senior team member without further discussion to keep the project moving.
- **B**: Wait until the team member raises a request for help, since pushing in could be seen as a lack of trust.
- **C**: Have a supportive one-on-one to understand what is blocking progress, then provide training, pair them with a stronger team member, or reassign the issue together.
- **D**: Note the lack of progress in the team member's performance record so the situation is captured if it recurs.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the servant-leader response: investigate gently, then choose the right combination of support and reassignment with the team member. (A) takes the work away without conversation, which damages trust. (B) lets the stall continue and assumes the team member will self-advocate, which often does not happen. (D) jumps to performance management before understanding the cause.
