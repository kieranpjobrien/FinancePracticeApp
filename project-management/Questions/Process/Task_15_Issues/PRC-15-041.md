# Question

An issue has been assigned to a team member who lacks the skills to resolve it. The team member hasn't raised this concern but progress has stalled. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the team member to ask for help
- **B**: Reassign the issue to someone else without discussion
- **C**: Have a supportive conversation with the team member to understand the situation, and either provide training/support, pair them with someone who has the skills, or reassign appropriately
- **D**: Document the lack of progress as a performance issue

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should investigate why progress has stalled. If it's a skills gap, the response should be supportive: training, pairing, or reassignment. The team member may have been reluctant to raise the concern. A supportive approach addresses the issue while building trust, making future communication easier.

Option A allows the stall to continue.

Option B may damage morale and miss a learning opportunity.

Option D frames a skills/assignment issue as performance failure.
