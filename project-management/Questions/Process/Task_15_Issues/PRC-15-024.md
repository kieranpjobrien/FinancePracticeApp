# Question

An issue has been escalated three times. The sponsor says it is a team problem; the team says it is organisational; middle management says they lack the authority to act. The project keeps absorbing the impact. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the full issue history, demonstrated impact, escalation attempts, and a specific proposed action with clear ownership, then request a single decision meeting that brings all parties into the room together.
- **B**: Continue escalating to ever higher levels until someone takes the decision off the table.
- **C**: Build a permanent workaround into the project and accept the residual impact as a cost of doing business.
- **D**: Close the issue in the log on the basis that no party has accepted ownership and treat it as out of scope.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Escalation ping-pong is a symptom of unclear ownership. (A) breaks the cycle by consolidating evidence and forcing a single conversation with everyone present. (B) repeats the same pattern that has not worked. (C) bakes in damage rather than resolving it. (D) makes the log lie about a real, ongoing impact.
