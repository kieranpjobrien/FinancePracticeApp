# Question

An issue has been escalated multiple times without resolution. The sponsor says it's a team problem. The team says it's an organizational problem. Middle management says they lack authority to fix it. The issue is causing ongoing project impact. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that the issue cannot be resolved
- **B**: Continue escalating to higher levels
- **C**: Document the full issue history including escalation attempts and impacts, propose specific actions with clear ownership, and request a decision meeting with all relevant parties to break the deadlock
- **D**: Work around the issue permanently

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Escalation ping-pong indicates unclear ownership or accountability. The PM should consolidate the situation: full history, demonstrated impact, specific proposed actions, and request a meeting with all parties to force clarity on who owns resolution. This creates visibility and accountability that prevents continued deflection.

Option A accepts ongoing project harm without resolution.

Option B continues the pattern that hasn't worked.

Option D may be necessary short-term but doesn't resolve the underlying issue.
