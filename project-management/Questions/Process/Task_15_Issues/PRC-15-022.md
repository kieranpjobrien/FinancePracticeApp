# Question

What technique involves asking "why" repeatedly to drill down to the fundamental cause of an issue?

## Options

- **A**: Pareto analysis
- **B**: Five Whys
- **C**: SWOT analysis
- **D**: Monte Carlo simulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Five Whys technique involves asking "why" iteratively (typically around five times) to move from symptoms to root causes. Each answer prompts another "why" until the fundamental cause is uncovered. It's a simple but effective root cause analysis technique.

Option A analyzes the distribution of problem sources (80/20 rule).

Option C analyzes strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.

Option D is a statistical technique for uncertainty analysis.
