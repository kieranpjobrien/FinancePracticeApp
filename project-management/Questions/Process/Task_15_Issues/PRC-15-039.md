# Question

A current issue traces back to a design decision made early in the project. Reversing that decision would require significant rework. Some stakeholders are reluctant to acknowledge the original call may have been wrong. How should the PM approach the conversation?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid linking the current issue to the original decision so stakeholders are not put on the defensive.
- **B**: Name the people responsible for the original decision so accountability is clear before the discussion proceeds.
- **C**: Insist that the original decision be reversed regardless of cost since it is the source of the problem.
- **D**: Present the issue, the linkage to the original decision, and the realistic options with their costs, then facilitate a stakeholder decision on the best path forward.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM's job is to make the trade-off decidable, not to score points or avoid hard truths. (D) does that. (A) hides information stakeholders need. (B) replaces analysis with blame. (C) ignores cost and removes the trade-off entirely.
