# Question

An issue is discovered that appears to stem from a decision made early in the project. Revisiting that decision would require significant rework. Stakeholders are reluctant to acknowledge the original decision may have been wrong. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid raising the connection to the original decision
- **B**: Blame those who made the original decision
- **C**: Present the situation factually - the issue, its apparent cause, and options including their costs - enabling stakeholders to make an informed decision about the best path forward
- **D**: Insist on revisiting the original decision regardless of cost

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM's role is to provide clear information enabling good decisions, not to assign blame or avoid difficult topics. Presenting facts (the issue, its root cause, options, costs) objectively enables stakeholders to decide whether the cost of rework is justified. Sometimes living with consequences is cheaper than fixing root causes.

Option A avoids information stakeholders need.

Option B creates defensiveness that blocks good decision-making.

Option D advocates for a position without considering costs and alternatives.
