# Question

Five interdependent issues have appeared and fixing one tends to make others worse. The team is going in circles. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team work through the issues in the order they were logged and accept the temporary regressions.
- **B**: Map the dependencies between issues, identify whether a common root cause or a particular sequence resolves multiple at once, and bring in additional expertise to design a coordinated response.
- **C**: Pick the easiest two issues, close them quickly to build momentum, and revisit the harder ones in the next iteration.
- **D**: Escalate the entire situation to the sponsor as too complex for the team to resolve alone.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interdependent issues need systems analysis before action. (B) makes the dependencies visible and looks for leverage. (A) ignores the dependencies that caused the loop. (C) trades long-term progress for the appearance of momentum. (D) escalates instead of analysing first.
