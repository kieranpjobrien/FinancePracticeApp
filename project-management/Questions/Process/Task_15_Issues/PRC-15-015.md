# Question

A team member tells you about an issue but asks you not to log it because they think it will make their work look bad. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Respect the request and keep the issue out of the formal log to maintain trust.
- **B**: Log the issue anonymously so it is captured but no individual is named.
- **C**: Report the team member to their line manager for attempting to suppress project information.
- **D**: Reframe the conversation around project health rather than blame, then log the issue normally with appropriate detail and continue building a safe reporting culture.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's psychological safety thread says issue surfacing is a positive behaviour and should be encouraged. (D) addresses both the immediate logging need and the underlying culture concern. (A) lets a real issue drop out of management. (B) hides authorship and reinforces that the log is a blame tool. (C) punishes the very behaviour you want to encourage.
