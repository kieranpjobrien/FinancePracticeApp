# Question

A critical production issue is discovered that originated from work done by the project team. The operations team is handling the immediate incident, but finger-pointing between project and operations teams is escalating. What should the project manager focus on?

## Options

- **A**: Defend the project team against criticism
- **B**: Acknowledge the operations team's criticism fully
- **C**: Focus first on supporting incident resolution, then facilitate a blameless post-incident review to identify root causes and improvements for both teams
- **D**: Deny any project responsibility until fault is proven

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. During incidents, resolution takes priority over blame. The PM should support operations in fixing the immediate problem. Afterward, a blameless post-incident review (focusing on what happened and how to prevent recurrence, not who to blame) identifies improvements. This approach builds relationship and produces better outcomes than defensive posturing.

Option A is defensive and doesn't help resolution or learning.

Option B may not be accurate and doesn't help resolution.

Option D is defensive and damages team relationships.
