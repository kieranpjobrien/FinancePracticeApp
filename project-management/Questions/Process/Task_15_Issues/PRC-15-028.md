# Question

A live production incident has been traced to a recent project change. Operations is on the bridge fixing it; in the chat, project and ops engineers are starting to point fingers. What should the project manager focus on FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Defend the project team in the chat by sharing the design review documentation that approved the change.
- **B**: Publicly accept full responsibility for the project team and apologise to operations to defuse the situation.
- **C**: Privately pull the project engineers off the bridge so operations can resolve the incident without further conflict.
- **D**: Support the operations team in resolving the incident first, then facilitate a blameless post-incident review focused on what happened and what to change.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the standard incident response: stabilise first, then run a blameless review. (A) defends rather than helps and inflames the chat. (B) accepts responsibility without facts and undermines the project team. (C) removes the people who may best understand the change and looks evasive in the moment.
