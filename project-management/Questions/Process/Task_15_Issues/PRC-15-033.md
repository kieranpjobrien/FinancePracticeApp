# Question

During sprint retrospectives, the same issue is raised repeatedly but never seems to get resolved. Team members are becoming cynical about the retrospective process. What should the scrum master and project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop holding retrospectives since they're not working
- **B**: Tell the team to raise new issues instead of repeating old ones
- **C**: Prioritize action on the persistent issue to demonstrate that retrospective feedback leads to real change, rebuilding trust in the process
- **D**: Escalate the team's cynicism to management

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The retrospective's value depends on acting on insights. Persistent unaddressed issues undermine the process. Prioritizing action on this issue - even if difficult - demonstrates that raising concerns leads to improvement. This rebuilds trust in the retrospective and encourages continued honest feedback.

Option A abandons a valuable practice due to execution problems.

Option B ignores legitimate persistent concerns.

Option D treats the symptom (cynicism) rather than the cause (inaction).
