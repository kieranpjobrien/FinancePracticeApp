# Question

A Scrum team on a retail platform has 15 open issues. The product owner insists the newest issue is most urgent because a vocal customer raised it yesterday. Other issues carry larger revenue impact. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the product owner's prioritisation because backlog ordering is their clear accountability under the Scrum framework by design here.
- **B**: Share the business impact analysis with the product owner so prioritisation is informed by value rather than customer volume.
- **C**: Prioritise the vocal customer's issue immediately to preserve the relationship, then tackle the higher-impact issues straight afterwards in sequence.
- **D**: Ask the team to vote on which issue they would like to tackle first so decision ownership stays with them.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The product owner owns prioritisation, but PMI expects the PM to ensure decisions are informed by value and impact data rather than stakeholder volume. (B) respects the role while improving the inputs. (A) abdicates the PM's advisory duty. (C) rewards squeaky-wheel behaviour over value-based ordering. (D) moves an owner-level decision onto the delivery team.
