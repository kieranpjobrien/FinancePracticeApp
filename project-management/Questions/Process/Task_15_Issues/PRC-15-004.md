# Question

What is the difference between a project issue and a project risk?

## Options

- **A**: Issues are potential problems; risks are current problems
- **B**: Issues are current problems affecting the project; risks are potential future events that may impact the project
- **C**: Issues are managed by the team; risks are managed by the project manager
- **D**: Issues require budget; risks do not

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. An issue is something that has already occurred and is currently affecting the project - it needs to be resolved. A risk is a potential future event (positive or negative) that has probability and impact - it needs to be managed proactively. A risk that occurs becomes an issue.

Option A (reversed definitions) is backwards. Risks are potential; issues are current.

Option C (who manages) isn't the distinguishing factor. Both issues and risks can be managed by various team members depending on their nature.

Option D (budget implications) isn't accurate. Both issues and risks may or may not require budget to address.
