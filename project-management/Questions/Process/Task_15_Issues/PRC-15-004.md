# Question

How does PMI distinguish a project issue from a project risk?

## Options

- **A**: An issue is a current condition impacting the project; a risk is an uncertain future event that may or may not occur.
- **B**: An issue is something the team has identified; a risk is something only the project manager has identified.
- **C**: An issue affects schedule and budget; a risk affects quality and scope.
- **D**: An issue is a high-severity event; a risk is the same condition at a lower severity.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's definitions: a risk is an uncertain event with probability and impact, managed proactively in the risk register; an issue is something that has occurred and now needs to be managed in the issue log. A risk that materialises becomes an issue. (A) captures that. (B) confuses ownership with definition. (C) splits the concepts along the wrong dimension. (D) treats severity as the difference, which it is not.
