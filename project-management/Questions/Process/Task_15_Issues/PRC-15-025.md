# Question

Reviewing the issue log, you notice that issues raised by certain team members consistently get lower priority ratings than equivalent issues raised by others. The bias appears unintentional. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Take no action since the bias is unintentional and corrective action could be perceived as accusatory.
- **B**: Compensate by always rating the affected members' issues one priority level higher than the rubric would suggest.
- **C**: Allow affected team members to log issues anonymously through a separate intake channel.
- **D**: Review and standardise the prioritisation criteria so all issues are rated against the same impact and urgency rubric, regardless of source.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The fix for inconsistent prioritisation is an objective rubric applied uniformly. (D) addresses the root cause. (A) lets a real bias keep harming morale. (B) replaces one bias with another. (C) hides authorship rather than fixing the rubric and weakens the social trust the team needs.
