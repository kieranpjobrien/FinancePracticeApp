# Question

A project manager notices that issues reported by certain team members tend to be prioritized lower than similar issues reported by others. This appears unintentional but is causing frustration. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing, since it's unintentional
- **B**: Always prioritize issues from the affected team members higher to compensate
- **C**: Review and standardize prioritization criteria to ensure objective, consistent prioritization regardless of who reports the issue
- **D**: Have the affected team members report issues anonymously

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Even unintentional bias affects project outcomes and team morale. Standardizing prioritization criteria (based on impact, urgency, etc.) removes subjectivity that may be causing the inconsistency. Clear criteria ensure issues are evaluated on their merits, not their source.

Option A allows bias to continue affecting the project.

Option B introduces reverse bias rather than objectivity.

Option D addresses the symptom without fixing the prioritization process.
