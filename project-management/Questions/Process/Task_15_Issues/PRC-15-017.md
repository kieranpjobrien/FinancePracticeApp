# Question

The issue log has grown to over 50 open items and team members are struggling to identify what to focus on. Some entries have not been touched in weeks. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a structured log review with the team to validate relevance, refresh priorities, close resolved or obsolete entries, and add categorisation that supports filtering going forward.
- **B**: Archive the entire log and start a clean one so the team can focus on current issues only.
- **C**: Auto-close every entry that has been inactive for 30 days to bring the active count back to a manageable size.
- **D**: Suspend new issue logging until the team has worked the existing 50+ items down to fewer than 10.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A bloated log is fixed by maintenance, not by deletion. (A) preserves history, restores prioritisation, and adds structure. (B) loses traceability and any useful audit trail. (C) closes potentially live items by age rather than by status. (D) suppresses new information, which is always the wrong move.
