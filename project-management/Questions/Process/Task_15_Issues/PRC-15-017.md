# Question

The issue log has grown to over 50 open items. Team members complain that it's difficult to know what to focus on, and some issues seem to have been forgotten. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Archive all issues and start fresh
- **B**: Close all issues older than 30 days
- **C**: Conduct an issue log review to validate relevance, update priorities, close resolved or obsolete items, and implement better categorization and filtering for manageability
- **D**: Stop logging new issues until the backlog is cleared

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. An unwieldy issue log needs maintenance. Review all items: some may be resolved but not closed, some may be obsolete, some may need reprioritization. Better categorization (by type, priority, owner) and filtering enable focus on what matters. Regular maintenance prevents future accumulation.

Option A loses potentially important issue history.

Option B arbitrarily closes issues that may still be relevant.

Option D stops capturing new issues which may be more important than old ones.
