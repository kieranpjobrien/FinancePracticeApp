# Question

An issue has been open for three weeks with no movement. The assigned owner says they are buried in higher-priority work, and downstream tasks are now slipping because of the unresolved issue. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Compare the issue's priority against the owner's other work and either re-prioritise or reassign with a realistic date.
- **B**: Leave the issue with the current owner and accept the downstream slip until the owner eventually finds capacity to address it.
- **C**: Close the issue out of the log because three weeks of no progress shows the team cannot resolve it with current resources.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor and request that they direct the owner's manager to make the issue a priority in their queue.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A stalled issue blocking downstream work is exactly when the PM intervenes. (A) is the right intervention: re-rank work or move ownership and re-baseline the resolution date. (B) accepts a known schedule impact for no good reason. (C) treats slow progress as cause to discard a real issue. (D) escalates a workload reprioritisation that is well within the PM's authority.
