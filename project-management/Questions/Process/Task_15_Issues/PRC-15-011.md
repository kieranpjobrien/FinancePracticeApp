# Question

An issue has been open for three weeks with no progress. The assigned owner says they're too busy with other priorities. The issue is causing delays in dependent work. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the owner to find time
- **B**: Close the issue since it's taking too long
- **C**: Assess the issue's priority against other work, either reprioritize the owner's tasks or reassign the issue to someone with capacity, and establish a realistic resolution timeline
- **D**: Escalate immediately to senior management

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Stalled issues need intervention. The PM should evaluate whether the issue truly is lower priority than the owner's other work (and should wait) or whether it should be elevated. If it's blocking other work, resolution is urgent. Options include reprioritizing, reassigning, or getting additional resources.

Option A allows the problem to persist indefinitely.

Option B doesn't resolve the underlying problem causing delays.

Option D may be premature if the PM can resolve through resource reallocation.
