# Question

During issue triage, the project manager realises an issue requires deep specialist knowledge the team does not have. What is the BEST next step?

## Options

- **A**: Identify and engage the right expertise, whether that is an internal SME, a consultant, or vendor support, and pair them with the team to resolve the issue.
- **B**: Encourage the team to investigate the issue themselves to build new capability and reduce future dependence on outside experts.
- **C**: Mark the issue as unresolvable in the current iteration and defer it to a later release.
- **D**: Escalate the issue to the sponsor and ask them to assign someone with the necessary expertise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Issues outside the team's knowledge are routed to the right expertise; the PM is the connector. (A) does that. (B) is fine for capability building in slack time but inappropriate when the issue actually needs specialist resolution now. (C) defers a real problem rather than solving it. (D) puts a sourcing problem on the sponsor when the PM can typically resolve it directly.
