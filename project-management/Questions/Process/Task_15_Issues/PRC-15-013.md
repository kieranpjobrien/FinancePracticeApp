# Question

During issue triage, the project manager identifies that an issue requires expertise the team doesn't have. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Close the issue as unresolvable
- **B**: Have team members attempt to resolve it anyway
- **C**: Identify and engage appropriate expertise - either internal subject matter experts, external consultants, or vendor support - to help resolve the issue
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to solve it

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Issues requiring specialized expertise should be routed to those who can help. This might mean engaging internal experts from other teams, bringing in external consultants, or working with vendor technical support. The PM should identify the right expertise and facilitate their involvement.

Option A gives up without exploring options.

Option B may waste time and potentially make things worse.

Option D puts technical problem-solving on the sponsor who may not have the expertise either.
