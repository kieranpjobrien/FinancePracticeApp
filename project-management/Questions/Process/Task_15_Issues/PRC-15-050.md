# Question

On a predictive infrastructure project, a vendor-caused issue is blocking two internal work packages. The vendor insists the defect is outside contractual scope. The PM has reviewed the statement of work and disagrees. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise internal staff to fix the defect now and pursue cost recovery from the vendor through invoicing at a later date.
- **B**: Accept the vendor's position to preserve the relationship, log the schedule slip, and replan the affected work packages accordingly.
- **C**: Escalate directly to the sponsor and request funding for an alternate vendor to replace the incumbent supplier immediately.
- **D**: Engage procurement, present the contractual evidence to the vendor formally, and require the defect to be remediated under the existing agreement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Contract issues require disciplined use of procurement channels backed by evidence, not workarounds or capitulation. (D) enforces the existing agreement through proper authority. (A) performs work the vendor owes and complicates later cost recovery. (B) accepts scope shift without challenge and damages contract integrity. (C) escalates and triggers supplier change before exhausting contractual remedies.
