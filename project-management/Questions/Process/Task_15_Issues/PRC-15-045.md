# Question

At project closure, the issue log shows several issues that were never fully resolved but were worked around. What should the project manager do with these issues?

## Options

- **A**: Delete them since the project is ending
- **B**: Close them as resolved since the project delivered
- **C**: Document the unresolved issues and their workarounds in project closure materials, and transfer relevant issues to operational support or future projects as appropriate
- **D**: Leave them open indefinitely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Unresolved issues with workarounds represent operational knowledge and potentially ongoing technical debt. At closure, these should be documented (what the issue is, what workaround is used) and transferred to whoever will maintain the deliverable (operations, support team). Future projects may also benefit from this knowledge.

Option A loses important information about ongoing workarounds.

Option B misrepresents the resolution status.

Option D leaves issues in project tracking that won't be actively managed.
