# Question

At project closure, several open issues remain in the log. They were never fully resolved but the team has been operating around them. What should the project manager do with these entries?

## Options

- **A**: Mark them resolved, since the project successfully delivered despite their presence.
- **B**: Document each unresolved issue and its workaround in the closure pack and transfer ownership to operations or to a follow-on project, as appropriate.
- **C**: Leave them open in the project's issue log so future questions can be researched in the historical system.
- **D**: Delete them from the log so the closure pack reflects only resolved items and produces a clean handover.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unresolved issues with workarounds are real operational knowledge and potential future work. (B) preserves them and assigns ownership for the post-project world. (A) misrepresents resolution status. (C) leaves entries in a tracker no one is monitoring. (D) destroys information the receiving team needs.
