# Question

What is the primary distinction between a risk and an issue in PMI terminology?

## Options

- **A**: Risks always carry larger schedule impact than issues at the same point in the project lifecycle.
- **B**: Risks are tracked by the project manager; issues are tracked by the team and raised at standups.
- **C**: A risk is an uncertain future event; an issue is a current condition that has already occurred.
- **D**: Risks are documented in the project charter; issues are documented in the schedule and status reports.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Per PMI, risks live in the future with uncertainty about whether they will occur, while issues are present-tense conditions that the team must respond to now. (C) captures that. (A) confuses severity with category. (B) is wrong about ownership. (D) describes the wrong artefacts; risks live in the risk register and issues in the issue log.
