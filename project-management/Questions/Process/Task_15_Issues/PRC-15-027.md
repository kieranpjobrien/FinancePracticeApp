# Question

An issue was resolved with a workaround that has ongoing operational overhead. The permanent fix would require significant effort. Stakeholders are divided on whether to invest in the permanent fix. How should the project manager help resolve this?

## Options

- **A**: Let the workaround continue indefinitely
- **B**: Insist on implementing the permanent fix
- **C**: Provide analysis comparing the ongoing cost of the workaround versus the one-time cost of the permanent fix, and facilitate a decision based on value
- **D**: Close the issue since there's a workaround

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a cost-benefit decision that stakeholders should make with good information. The PM should analyze: what is the ongoing cost of the workaround (time, efficiency, risk)? What is the cost of the permanent fix? At what point does the permanent fix pay for itself? This enables an informed decision.

Option A may not be the economically optimal choice.

Option B doesn't consider whether it's the right investment.

Option D treats the symptom as resolved without addressing the ongoing cost.
