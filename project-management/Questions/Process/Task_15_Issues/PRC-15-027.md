# Question

An issue is resolved with a workaround that has ongoing operational cost. The permanent fix would require significant effort, and stakeholders are split on whether to invest. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the recurring cost of the workaround against the one-off cost of the permanent fix and facilitate a value-based decision with stakeholders.
- **B**: Direct the team to implement the permanent fix because workarounds always carry hidden risk.
- **C**: Leave the workaround in place indefinitely because stakeholders cannot agree on the investment.
- **D**: Close the issue in the log on the basis that there is a working solution in production.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The decision is a cost/value trade-off and the PM's role is to make it decidable. (A) supplies the analysis and facilitates the call. (B) makes the investment decision unilaterally. (C) defaults to the status quo despite an unmade decision. (D) closes the log entry while the cost is still being borne.
