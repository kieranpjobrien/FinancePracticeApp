# Question

A new regulatory ruling is published and your project must respond within ten business days. The required changes will hit scope, schedule, and possibly budget. The steering committee meets in fourteen days. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin implementing the changes under your own authority and brief the steering committee at their next scheduled meeting.
- **B**: Wait for the scheduled steering meeting so the decision is taken with full governance even if the deadline is missed.
- **C**: Treat the ruling as out of scope until the steering committee has formally accepted it as a project obligation.
- **D**: Prepare an impact assessment with options and recommendations, then request an emergency steering session or chair-delegated authority to take the decision inside the regulatory window.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A regulatory deadline that exceeds the standard governance cycle is what expedited approval routes are for. (D) supplies the analysis and asks governance to move at the required speed. (A) exceeds delegated authority on a multi-baseline change. (B) accepts a regulatory miss for the sake of a calendar. (C) treats a binding regulatory obligation as optional.
