# Question

A critical decision must be made today between Option A (lower risk, over budget) and Option B (within budget, higher risk). The decision exceeds the PM's delegated authority, the sponsor is on a flight and unreachable, and delay will materially worsen both options. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the call independently and document the reasoning, since the sponsor is unavailable and delay will only make things worse.
- **B**: Hold off on any decision until the sponsor is reachable, even if both options become significantly worse in the meantime.
- **C**: Default to Option B because it stays within the delegated budget envelope and therefore sits within the PM's authority.
- **D**: Try every documented escalation path (sponsor mobile, designated delegate, governance contacts), and only if no decision authority can be reached in time, take the best decision possible with full written rationale and immediate transparency to the sponsor on return.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the disciplined PMI response to a governance stress test: exhaust the proper authority paths, then if forced to act, do so transparently and document fully. (A) skips the attempt to reach authority entirely. (B) ignores the harm caused by inaction. (C) reads "within budget" as authority to choose Option B unilaterally, which sidesteps the broader risk decision the sponsor needs to own.
