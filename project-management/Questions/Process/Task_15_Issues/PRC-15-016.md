# Question

A critical issue is identified that requires an immediate decision between two options: Option A is lower risk but more expensive, Option B is higher risk but within budget. The sponsor is unavailable, and delay will make both options worse. The project charter gives the PM authority for decisions up to a certain dollar amount, which this exceeds. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision independently since the sponsor is unavailable
- **B**: Wait for the sponsor regardless of consequences
- **C**: Document the situation, make reasonable attempts to reach the sponsor or delegate, and if truly unable to reach decision authority in time, make the best decision possible with documentation of circumstances and rationale
- **D**: Choose Option B since it's within budget

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a governance stress test. The PM should exhaust reasonable options to reach appropriate authority (sponsor, designated delegate, other governance contacts). If truly impossible and delay worsens the situation, making a documented decision with clear rationale is better than paralysis. Full transparency when the sponsor is available is essential.

Option A doesn't attempt to reach proper authority.

Option B may cause worse outcomes through inaction.

Option D makes budget the only criterion without proper authorization.
