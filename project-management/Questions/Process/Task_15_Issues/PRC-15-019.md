# Question

Your agile team uses a physical impediment board in the team room. It works well in standup but there is no historical record, and you want to spot patterns over time. How should the project manager approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the impediment board with a formal enterprise issue-tracking tool to capture every change automatically for the record.
- **B**: Keep the impediment board for daily use and add a lightweight log that captures resolved impediments for trend analysis.
- **C**: Accept that visibility into agile impediments is intentionally local and trend analysis is not appropriate for a self-organising team.
- **D**: Maintain a separate PM-only spreadsheet of impediments observed from standups without telling the team about the tracker.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) preserves the lightweight team artefact and adds a thin layer for pattern analysis, which is the standard hybrid solution. (A) burdens the team with tooling that may slow them down. (C) treats trend analysis as incompatible with agile, which it is not. (D) creates a hidden parallel record, which is poor stewardship and tends to drift from reality.
