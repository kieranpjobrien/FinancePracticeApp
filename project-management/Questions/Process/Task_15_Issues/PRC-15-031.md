# Question

Issues on one work stream are being caused by incomplete handoffs from another work stream. The teams blame each other for unclear requirements and late deliveries. What issue management approach would help?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to have one team declared responsible
- **B**: Add buffer time between the work streams
- **C**: Establish clear interface agreements between the work streams defining handoff criteria, quality expectations, and joint issue resolution processes
- **D**: Merge the two teams into one

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Interface issues require interface management. Clear agreements about what constitutes a complete handoff, what quality standards apply, and how issues at the interface are resolved together creates shared accountability. This is more effective than blame or organizational restructuring.

Option A assigns blame rather than solving the interface problem.

Option B adds time but doesn't address handoff quality.

Option D may not be practical and doesn't address the need for clear handoff criteria.
