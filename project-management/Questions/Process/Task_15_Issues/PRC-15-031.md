# Question

Issues on the integration work stream keep arising because the upstream design work stream delivers incomplete or unclear handoffs. The two teams blame each other. What approach best resolves this?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to leadership so one team is officially declared responsible for fixing the handoff problem.
- **B**: Add a two-week buffer between the two work streams so the integration team can absorb late or incomplete handoffs.
- **C**: Combine the two teams into a single work stream so handoffs are eliminated by design.
- **D**: Establish written interface agreements covering handoff acceptance criteria, quality expectations, and a joint resolution process for issues at the boundary.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Interface issues are solved with explicit interface management. (D) gives both teams shared, written rules that remove ambiguity and replace blame with criteria. (A) assigns blame rather than fixing the interface. (B) adds buffer that hides poor handoff quality. (C) is a heavy organisational change that may not be feasible and does not necessarily produce better handoff discipline.
