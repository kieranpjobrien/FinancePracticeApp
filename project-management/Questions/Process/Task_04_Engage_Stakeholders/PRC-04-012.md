# Question

A key stakeholder who was initially supportive has become increasingly critical of the project. Their engagement level has shifted from supportive to resistant. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore them since they're being difficult
- **B**: Meet privately to understand what caused the change in their perspective and address their underlying concerns
- **C**: Escalate to their manager to control their behaviour
- **D**: Remove them from project communications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Engagement shifts have causes worth understanding. Meet privately: what changed? Did expectations not get met? Did they receive new information? Are there organisational pressures? Understanding the root cause enables targeted response. Often concerns can be addressed once understood.

Option A lets a valuable relationship deteriorate.

Option C escalates before understanding or attempting resolution.

Option D removes an important stakeholder without addressing concerns.
