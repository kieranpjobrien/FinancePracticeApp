# Question

A PM running an agile project notices that end users are consistently absent from sprint reviews, despite being the primary stakeholder group. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel sprint reviews until end-user attendance can be reliably guaranteed going forward.
- **B**: Continue without end users and rely on the product owner to represent the user voice fully.
- **C**: Understand why attendance is low, adjust format to reduce friction, and invite specific users.
- **D**: Require management to mandate end-user attendance at every sprint review from now on.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Low attendance is a symptom; the PM's job is to understand the cause and remove friction. (C) applies servant leadership — make it easier to participate rather than demanding compliance. (A) cancelling reviews loses the feedback loop entirely. (B) relying solely on the product owner loses direct user feedback, which the event is designed to capture. (D) mandated attendance produces physical presence without engagement and damages the spirit of agile collaboration.
