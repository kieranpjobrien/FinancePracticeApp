# Question

A stakeholder analysis reveals that a senior manager has high power and low interest in the project. The manager has shown occasional resistance to providing required approvals. How should the project manager engage this stakeholder?

## Options

- **A**: Keep them minimally informed since they have low interest
- **B**: Provide concise updates focused on what they need to know, make approvals easy, and engage them early on decisions requiring their authority
- **C**: Increase their interest by involving them in detailed project planning
- **D**: Work around them by finding alternative approval paths

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. High power, low interest stakeholders should be "kept satisfied" - not overloaded with information they don't want, but engaged effectively when their authority is needed. Concise updates respect their time; easy approval processes remove friction; early engagement on key decisions prevents surprises that cause resistance.

Option A risks neglecting someone whose approval authority can block the project. Low interest doesn't mean they can be ignored.

Option C tries to change their fundamental interest level, which is difficult and may annoy them with unwanted involvement. Work with their interest level, don't try to change it.

Option D creates political problems and doesn't address the relationship. The stakeholder will likely notice and may become actively resistant.
