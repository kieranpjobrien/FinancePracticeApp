# Question

A senior manager has been classified as high-power, low-interest on the stakeholder grid. Their formal approvals are needed at three gates but they rarely respond to project emails. How should the PM engage them?

## Options

- **A**: Copy them on all project communications so they have full visibility.
- **B**: Schedule weekly one-on-ones to build interest in the project over time.
- **C**: Provide concise, decision-focused briefings ahead of gates and make it easy to approve with pre-prepared material.
- **D**: Route all gate approvals through the sponsor instead to avoid the manager's slow response.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

High-power, low-interest stakeholders should be 'kept satisfied' — respected with minimal, well-targeted engagement around the decisions only they can make. (A) flooding an uninterested senior with information wastes their time and trains them to ignore project comms. (B) forcing interest through weekly meetings is disrespectful of their time and likely to annoy. (D) bypassing a stakeholder with formal authority creates political and governance problems.
