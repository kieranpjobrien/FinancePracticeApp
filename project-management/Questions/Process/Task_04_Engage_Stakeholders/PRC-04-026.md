# Question

A regional delivery lead in a multi-site project feels left out because the main project meetings are held in the head-office time zone. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate meeting times so no single region bears all the off-hours inconvenience and offer recorded sessions with written summaries.
- **B**: Keep the current schedule since the majority of participants are at head office.
- **C**: Hold separate regional meetings and never combine them.
- **D**: Let the regional lead send a delegate so they don't have to attend themselves.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Inclusive engagement requires acknowledging geographic realities. (A) shares the burden and provides async options. (B) treating one region's convenience as the default erodes engagement from the others. (C) permanent separation fragments decision-making and loses cross-regional alignment. (D) delegation without rotation pushes the lead out of strategic conversations over time.
