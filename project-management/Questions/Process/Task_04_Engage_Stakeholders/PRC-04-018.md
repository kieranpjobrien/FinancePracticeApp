# Question

The project requires significant change in how end users work. Users are resistant because they weren't involved in the decision to implement the project. How should the PM approach user engagement now?

## Options

- **A**: Force adoption since the decision was made
- **B**: Engage users in how the change will be implemented, involve them in design decisions where possible, address concerns transparently, and work with change management to support adoption
- **C**: Wait until deployment to engage users
- **D**: Tell users their concerns don't matter

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Even when the "what" is decided, users can influence the "how." Engage: involve them in implementation design, workflow adjustments, training approach. Address concerns: legitimate worries should be heard even if the core decision stands. Change management: support users through the transition. This builds buy-in and improves adoption.

Option A creates resistance without buy-in.

Option C delays engagement when early involvement would help.

Option D dismisses users who will determine adoption success.
