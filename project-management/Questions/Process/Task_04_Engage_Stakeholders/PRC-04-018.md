# Question

A PM is planning stakeholder engagement for a project with 40+ stakeholders. Managing each individually isn't feasible. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Group stakeholders by shared characteristics and design engagement strategies at the group level where appropriate.
- **B**: Engage only the top 10 most powerful stakeholders individually and ignore the rest of the group.
- **C**: Use a single communication channel for all 40 stakeholders to ensure equal and consistent treatment.
- **D**: Delegate stakeholder engagement entirely to the functional managers of each group across the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects scalable engagement via grouping where shared interests make sense, combined with individual engagement for key influencers. (A) matches the principle. (B) ignoring 30+ stakeholders creates gaps the project will suffer from. (C) one-size-fits-all communication violates the tailoring principle and produces irrelevant messages. (D) delegation without coordination loses the PM's ability to manage cross-cutting engagement.
