# Question

The project sponsor has been replaced mid-project due to an organisational restructure. The incoming sponsor has no prior context on the programme and has asked the PM for a week to "get up to speed" before any engagement. Delivery decisions are pending. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Respect the incoming sponsor's stated one-week request and continue within the PM's existing authority for delivery decisions in the interim.
- **B**: Offer to provide a focused one-hour briefing on pending decisions and any current risks so the sponsor is informed without full onboarding.
- **C**: Route the pending decisions through the previous sponsor if they remain with the organisation in a different capacity during the transition.
- **D**: Defer all pending decisions until the incoming sponsor has completed their stated one-week onboarding before any programme engagement occurs.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sponsorship transitions need continuity on pending decisions; PMI-aligned practice offers a focused handover that respects the sponsor's time while keeping delivery moving. (B) balances both. (A) pure autonomy on pending decisions risks later reversal when the sponsor is up to speed. (C) the old sponsor no longer carries authority. (D) waiting a week with pending decisions is a delivery failure.
