# Question

A stakeholder group that was classified as low interest has suddenly become highly engaged and is raising many concerns late in the project. They feel they weren't adequately consulted. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Dismiss their concerns since they didn't engage earlier
- **B**: Acknowledge the consultation gap, engage now to understand their concerns, assess impact on project, and adjust stakeholder classification for future monitoring
- **C**: Accept all their changes without analysis
- **D**: Blame them for not engaging sooner

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Late stakeholder emergence happens, sometimes due to initial misclassification or changed circumstances. Acknowledge: don't be defensive about past engagement approach. Engage now: understand their concerns - are they valid? Impact: can concerns be addressed within constraints? Adjust: update stakeholder analysis to reflect their true interest level.

Option A dismisses potentially valid concerns.

Option C doesn't assess feasibility or impact.

Option D damages the relationship without solving anything.
