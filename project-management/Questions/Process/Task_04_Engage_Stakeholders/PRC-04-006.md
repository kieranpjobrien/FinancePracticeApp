# Question

A stakeholder has started giving contradictory approval signals — verbally supportive in meetings, but blocking decisions in writing afterward. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rely only on written decisions going forward since they are the formal record.
- **B**: Document every verbal comment and hold the stakeholder publicly to their statements.
- **C**: Stop inviting the stakeholder to meetings where they cannot follow through.
- **D**: Meet the stakeholder directly, understand the disconnect, and agree a single decision channel.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Contradictory signals usually reflect real ambivalence or a private concern the stakeholder isn't raising in group settings. (D) addresses the root cause through direct conversation and agrees a clear decision channel. (A) ignoring the verbal channel loses information about what the stakeholder actually thinks. (B) 'holding them publicly to their statements' is confrontational and destroys the relationship. (C) excluding a stakeholder from relevant meetings creates worse problems than the mixed signals.
