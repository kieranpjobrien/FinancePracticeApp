# Question

A PM discovers that a stakeholder on the evaluation panel for a procurement has a family connection to a bidding vendor. The stakeholder hasn't declared it and the evaluation is partway through. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the evaluation, raise the undeclared connection through the procurement and ethics channels, and follow the organisation's declared process.
- **B**: Ask the stakeholder directly whether they want to recuse themselves now that the PM has noticed the family connection.
- **C**: Continue the evaluation and flag the connection in the closeout documentation so the record shows the PM was aware of the issue.
- **D**: Inform the other panel members so they can factor the bias into their scoring as the evaluation continues through its remaining stages.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Conflicts of interest are non-negotiable under PMI's ethics code; once known, the correct action is to pause and use the declared channels. (A) does that properly. (B) an informal ask doesn't satisfy the organisation's declared process and may not even surface full facts. (C) continuing despite a known undeclared conflict is an ethics breach. (D) sharing with peers without formal action still leaves the breach unresolved.
