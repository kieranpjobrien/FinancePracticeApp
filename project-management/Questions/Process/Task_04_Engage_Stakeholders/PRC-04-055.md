# Question

A project is six weeks from go-live and a previously-unknown internal audit group has raised formal concerns about controls. The audit group wasn't in the stakeholder register. Their concerns, if valid, could delay go-live by three weeks. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with the audit group immediately to understand their concerns and any formal authority they hold over go-live readiness.
- **B**: Assess the concerns against existing controls documentation and respond only if a genuine gap is found against approved requirements.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor so governance can clarify whether the audit group has any authority to delay the go-live programme.
- **D**: Add the audit group to the register, run an analysis on their influence, and plan engagement in the coming week ahead.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When a stakeholder surfaces with potential delay authority close to go-live, PMI-aligned practice is to engage them directly and quickly. (A) does that. (B) assessing without engaging leaves the relationship unmanaged and may miss the audit group's actual concerns. (C) escalating before engaging is premature and abdicates the PM's relationship role. (D) register hygiene is important but not the first move with six weeks to go-live.
