# Question

A stakeholder has been on the register for six months but the PM realises they've never actually been engaged beyond being copied on group emails. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the situation unchanged since the stakeholder hasn't complained.
- **B**: Remove the stakeholder from the register since they haven't needed active engagement.
- **C**: Reassess whether the stakeholder still requires engagement, and if so, reach out directly to begin meaningful engagement.
- **D**: Increase the volume of group emails they receive to improve coverage.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Being cc'd on group emails is not engagement. (C) restarts the relationship properly — confirm relevance and engage directly. (A) lack of complaint isn't evidence of effective engagement. (B) removing from the register assumes irrelevance without checking. (D) more email to someone who isn't engaging is noise, not engagement.
