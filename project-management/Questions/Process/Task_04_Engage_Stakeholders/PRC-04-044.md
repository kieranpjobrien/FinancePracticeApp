# Question

A project is being handed off from one PM to another. The outgoing PM has strong stakeholder relationships but limited documentation of engagement history and stakeholder analysis. How should the transition be handled?

## Options

- **A**: Transfer only documented information
- **B**: Schedule dedicated time for relationship introductions, verbal briefings on stakeholder dynamics, key concerns, and engagement history - capturing critical information even if not previously documented
- **C**: Let the new PM start fresh with stakeholders
- **D**: Complete the transition via email

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Relationship knowledge transfer requires personal handoff. Introductions: facilitate meetings between new PM and key stakeholders. Briefings: share tacit knowledge about stakeholder dynamics, history, sensitivities. Capture: document critical insights during transition even if previously undocumented. This sets the new PM up for success and honours existing relationships.

Option A loses valuable tacit knowledge.

Option C wastes established relationships and trust.

Option D is insufficient for relationship transfer.
