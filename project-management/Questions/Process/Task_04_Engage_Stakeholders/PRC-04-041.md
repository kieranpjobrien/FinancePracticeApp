# Question

Stakeholder analysis reveals that a department head is a hidden opponent - publicly neutral but privately working against the project through informal influence. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Confront them publicly about their opposition
- **B**: Seek to understand their concerns through direct or indirect channels, address legitimate objections, build broader coalition support, and escalate to the sponsor if informal opposition continues
- **C**: Match their behaviour with hidden opposition
- **D**: Ignore their influence

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Hidden opposition requires strategic response. Understand: why are they opposed? Are there legitimate concerns? Direct engagement: try to address their concerns openly if possible. Build coalition: ensure other stakeholders are supportive to counter their informal influence. Escalate: if hidden opposition continues to harm the project, involve the sponsor.

Option A may escalate without understanding.

Option C descends to political games.

Option D allows harmful influence to continue.
