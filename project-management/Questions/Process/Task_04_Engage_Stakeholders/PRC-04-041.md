# Question

On a politically sensitive programme, the PM realises the stakeholder register contains private assessment notes that, if leaked, would damage relationships. The PM has been asked to share the full register with a new team member. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Share the full register since the new team member needs full visibility.
- **B**: Refuse to share anything until all notes are removed permanently from the register.
- **C**: Share only a summary without the classification and assessment data the team member needs.
- **D**: Maintain a public-facing version without sensitive assessment notes for broad sharing, while keeping sensitive analysis in a restricted file accessible only to those who need it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Stakeholder classification notes are often candid and not meant for broad distribution. (D) separates confidentiality layers professionally — this is standard practice on politically sensitive work. (A) sharing sensitive assessments broadly risks exactly the leak scenario. (B) removing assessment notes loses analytical value. (C) omitting classification data starves the new team member of the information they need to engage appropriately.
