# Question

A project spans three business units with different cultures (one hierarchical, two collaborative). The PM is planning stakeholder engagement. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Tailor engagement style to each business unit — formal written updates and scheduled reviews for the hierarchical unit, participatory workshops for the collaborative ones.
- **B**: Apply a single standard engagement model so all units receive equal treatment.
- **C**: Let each business unit self-organise their engagement with no central approach.
- **D**: Align everything to the sponsor's home business unit's culture since they have ultimate authority.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI emphasises tailoring: engagement mechanisms should fit stakeholder context, not the PM's convenience. (A) matches style to culture and will produce better engagement across all three units. (B) a uniform model will fit one culture well and the other two poorly. (C) no central approach risks inconsistency and missed information across units. (D) privileging the sponsor's home unit alienates the other two and misreads why cultural tailoring exists.
