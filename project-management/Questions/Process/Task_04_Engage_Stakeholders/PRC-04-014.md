# Question

Two powerful stakeholders have conflicting interests - what benefits one harms the other. Both expect the PM to support their position. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Support the more powerful stakeholder
- **B**: Facilitate transparent discussion of trade-offs, escalate to appropriate governance for decision, and ensure the PM role remains focused on project objectives rather than taking sides
- **C**: Support whoever is nicer
- **D**: Avoid both stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When stakeholder interests conflict, the PM shouldn't pick winners. Facilitate: help both understand the trade-offs and each other's perspective. Escalate: decisions between conflicting stakeholder interests are governance decisions, not PM decisions. Project focus: advocate for what serves project objectives. This maintains PM neutrality while ensuring decisions get made.

Option A takes sides based on power.

Option C takes sides based on personality.

Option D avoids a situation requiring management.
