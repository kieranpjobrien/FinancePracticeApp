# Question

The project is implementing technology that will replace manual processes. The workers who currently perform these processes are nervous about their jobs, while management is enthusiastic about efficiency gains. How should the PM balance engagement with both groups?

## Options

- **A**: Focus on management since they're more powerful
- **B**: Engage both groups authentically - understand worker concerns, explore redeployment/reskilling options, communicate honestly about impacts, while also addressing management's efficiency goals
- **C**: Focus on workers and ignore management
- **D**: Don't acknowledge the job impact to avoid conflict

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Both stakeholder groups have legitimate perspectives. Engage workers: understand their concerns, which are valid. Explore options: can workers be redeployed, retrained, or transitioned? Communicate honestly: don't pretend impacts won't happen. Management goals: efficiency gains are legitimate project objectives. Bridge: perhaps workers can contribute to solution design or transition planning.

Option A ignores stakeholders who will determine adoption and deserve respect.

Option C ignores sponsors whose support is needed.

Option D is dishonest and will backfire.
