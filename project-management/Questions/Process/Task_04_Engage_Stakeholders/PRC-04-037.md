# Question

A PM is managing a project where cultural norms vary across three international sites. A planned all-hands video call produced very different engagement — one site dominated the conversation and another barely spoke. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Call on the quiet site's members by name to force them to participate.
- **B**: Hold separate calls with each site so the dynamics don't clash.
- **C**: Accept the difference as a cultural reality that cannot be changed.
- **D**: Adjust the format — shorter calls, structured turn-taking, chat-based contributions, and pre-submitted questions — to give all sites equal voice.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Engagement format should adapt to reduce culturally-driven participation imbalances. (D) applies multiple practical techniques. (A) cold-calling can humiliate participants from cultures with different meeting norms and won't produce genuine contribution. (B) separate calls fragment the shared context and widen the gap over time. (C) resignation accepts a preventable problem and gives up on inclusion.
