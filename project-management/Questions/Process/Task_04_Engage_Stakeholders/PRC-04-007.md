# Question

A project manager discovers that a stakeholder has been privately lobbying executives against the project, despite expressing support in meetings. The stakeholder has legitimate concerns about how the project affects their department's budget. What approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the stakeholder in the next public meeting to ensure transparency
- **B**: Report the behaviour to the project sponsor and request the stakeholder be removed from the project
- **C**: Meet privately with the stakeholder to acknowledge their concerns and explore options to address the budget impact
- **D**: Ignore the backstage behaviour and focus on the stakeholder's public support

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The stakeholder has a legitimate concern (budget impact) and is using informal channels because formal ones aren't addressing it. A private meeting allows the PM to acknowledge the concern without putting the stakeholder on the defensive, and to explore solutions collaboratively. This is managing stakeholder engagement, not just stakeholder communication.

Option A (public confrontation) damages the relationship and creates an adversarial dynamic. The stakeholder will become more resistant, not less.

Option B (request removal) doesn't address the underlying concern and creates an enemy. The stakeholder still has influence with executives regardless of their formal project role.

Option D (ignore) allows the opposition to grow. Informal influence is real influence, and unaddressed concerns don't disappear - they escalate.
