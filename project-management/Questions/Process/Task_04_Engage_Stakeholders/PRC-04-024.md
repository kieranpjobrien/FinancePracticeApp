# Question

A new executive has joined the organisation and has questions about the project. They haven't been engaged as a stakeholder before. They have significant authority. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for them to request a briefing
- **B**: Proactively reach out to provide a project overview, understand their perspective and concerns, and add them to appropriate stakeholder engagement activities
- **C**: Ignore them since they weren't original stakeholders
- **D**: Only engage if the sponsor directs it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. New executives with authority are important stakeholders to engage proactively. Reach out: don't wait for them to come to you. Brief: provide project context and status. Understand: what's their perspective? Do they have concerns or insights? Integrate: add them to appropriate communication and governance. This builds relationship and prevents surprises.

Option A is passive about an important relationship.

Option C ignores stakeholder changes.

Option D misses an opportunity for proactive relationship building.
