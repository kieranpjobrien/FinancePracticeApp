# Question

The PM has completed a stakeholder analysis and identified that a key external partner is currently 'neutral' but needs to be 'supportive' for a major phase gate. What is the MOST effective action?

## Options

- **A**: Send them the same status updates other supportive stakeholders receive and hope they engage.
- **B**: Add them to more meetings to increase their exposure to the project.
- **C**: Issue a formal request asking them to commit to a supportive position.
- **D**: Identify what the partner values, find ways the project can create value for them, and engage through channels and messages relevant to their priorities.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Moving a stakeholder from neutral to supportive requires understanding their interests and showing how the project serves them — not just adding volume or formality. (A) treating them like already-supportive stakeholders won't change their position. (B) more meetings adds burden without necessarily shifting engagement. (C) a formal request for a position change without earning it typically produces polite refusal or resistance.
