# Question

A stakeholder group is essential for requirements but is known to be notoriously difficult to engage - they're busy, skeptical of IT projects, and have had bad experiences in the past. How should the PM approach initial engagement?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid engaging them
- **B**: Acknowledge their concerns upfront, demonstrate understanding of their work and constraints, propose minimal-burden engagement methods, show early wins that address their needs, and build trust gradually through reliable follow-through
- **C**: Force mandatory participation
- **D**: Engage proxies instead of the actual stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Difficult stakeholder engagement requires patience and credibility building. Acknowledge: "I know past projects haven't gone well..." Understand their world: show you've done homework about their challenges. Minimise burden: respect their time with efficient engagement methods. Early wins: deliver something valuable quickly. Build trust: keep commitments, follow through, earn credibility over time.

Option A avoids essential stakeholders.

Option C creates resentment without addressing underlying concerns.

Option D loses direct access to important perspectives.
