# Question

Stakeholder engagement is tracked and documented, but the team treats it as a checkbox exercise rather than genuine relationship building. Engagement activities happen but don't improve stakeholder satisfaction. What should the PM change?

## Options

- **A**: Add more checkboxes to track
- **B**: Shift focus from activity tracking to outcome measurement - are stakeholders actually satisfied? Are concerns being addressed? Is support growing? Emphasise quality of engagement over quantity
- **C**: Stop tracking engagement
- **D**: Accept that stakeholders can't be satisfied

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Shift from output to outcome focus. Outcome measurement: track stakeholder satisfaction, concern resolution, support levels. Quality over quantity: a single genuine conversation may be more valuable than ten perfunctory check-ins. Continuous improvement: use outcome data to improve engagement approaches. This creates genuine relationships rather than bureaucratic compliance.

Option A adds more bureaucracy without improving effectiveness.

Option C loses visibility without improving engagement.

Option D gives up on achievable improvement.
