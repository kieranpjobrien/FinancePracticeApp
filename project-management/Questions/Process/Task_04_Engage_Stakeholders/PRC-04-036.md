# Question

A stakeholder has been consistently supportive but recently asked the PM for confidential information about another stakeholder's position. The supportive stakeholder claims they need it to help the project. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Share the information to maintain the good relationship
- **B**: Politely decline to share confidential information about other stakeholders, explain why confidentiality matters, and offer alternative ways to address their underlying concern
- **C**: Lie about the information
- **D**: Share the information but ask them to keep it confidential

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Maintain confidentiality even with supportive stakeholders. Decline: "I'm not able to share information about other stakeholders' positions." Explain: confidentiality is important for trust - they benefit from knowing their discussions with you are also protected. Alternative: "What are you trying to accomplish? Let me see if I can help another way."

Option A violates confidentiality and damages trust.

Option C is dishonest.

Option D shares confidential information inappropriately.
