# Question

Two stakeholders from different departments have conflicting requirements that both claim are non-negotiable. The PM cannot deliver both within scope. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a joint meeting where both stakeholders hear each other's rationale and work toward a trade-off or compromise.
- **B**: Escalate the conflict directly to the sponsor for resolution.
- **C**: Pick the requirement that best aligns with the PM's understanding of business value.
- **D**: Defer both requirements to a future phase and deliver the shared middle ground.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to attempt resolution at their level before escalating. (A) the joint meeting often reveals that one 'non-negotiable' has more flexibility than stated, or surfaces a creative solution. (B) escalating first skips the step the sponsor will expect the PM to have tried. (C) unilateral PM choice damages both stakeholder relationships and likely gets overturned. (D) deferral isn't resolution — the conflict resurfaces in the next phase.
