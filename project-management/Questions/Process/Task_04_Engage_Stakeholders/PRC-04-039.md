# Question

What is a "dormant" stakeholder?

## Options

- **A**: A stakeholder who is sleeping
- **B**: A stakeholder who has power but currently lacks legitimacy or urgency - they could become active if triggered by project events
- **C**: A stakeholder who has left the organisation
- **D**: A stakeholder who never responds to communications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In stakeholder salience theory, dormant stakeholders have power but currently lack legitimacy (appropriate claim) or urgency (time-sensitive need). They may seem inactive but could become highly salient if project events trigger their interest or give them legitimate cause for concern. They should be monitored.

Option A is literal, not a stakeholder classification.

Option C describes a former stakeholder.

Option D describes communication behaviour, not salience type.
