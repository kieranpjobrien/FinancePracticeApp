# Question

A stakeholder has emailed to express surprise that a specific capability "isn't in the project scope". Reviewing the records, the PM finds the capability was explicitly excluded in the charter the stakeholder signed. The stakeholder is senior and now visibly frustrated. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Forward the signed charter to the stakeholder with the exclusion highlighted so the scope boundary is clearly documented for them.
- **B**: Raise a change request to add the capability in response to the stakeholder's reaction, since they clearly consider it valuable.
- **C**: Meet with the stakeholder to understand why they expected it, walk through the documented exclusion, and agree a path if needed.
- **D**: Escalate the misalignment to the sponsor so the scope boundary is reinforced at governance level and the stakeholder is addressed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Scope misalignment with a senior stakeholder deserves a conversation, not a document reference or a change request. (C) understands the "why" before deciding any action. (A) a document-and-highlight reply is correct but adversarial and relationship-damaging. (B) raising a CR before understanding the need jumps to a solution prematurely. (D) escalating without a direct conversation skips a level and worsens the dynamic.
