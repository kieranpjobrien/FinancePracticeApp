# Question

In the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix, what does "Leading" engagement level mean?

## Options

- **A**: The stakeholder is the project sponsor
- **B**: The stakeholder is actively engaged and working to ensure project success
- **C**: The stakeholder is in a management position
- **D**: The stakeholder attends all project meetings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Leading" is the highest engagement level in the assessment matrix. A leading stakeholder is actively engaged, advocates for the project, and actively works to ensure its success. They're champions who help influence other stakeholders and remove obstacles.

The five engagement levels are:
- **Unaware**: Doesn't know about the project
- **Resistant**: Aware but opposed
- **Neutral**: Aware but neither supportive nor resistant
- **Supportive**: Aware and supportive
- **Leading**: Actively engaged and championing success

Options A, C, and D describe roles or behaviours, not engagement levels.
