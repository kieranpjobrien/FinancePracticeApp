# Question

The PM discovers that a team member has a personal relationship with a key stakeholder and has been sharing project information informally. This creates information asymmetry. How should this be handled?

## Options

- **A**: Forbid team members from having personal relationships
- **B**: Acknowledge the relationship exists, establish guidelines about what information can be shared informally, ensure formal channels provide the same information, and address any confidentiality concerns
- **C**: Remove the team member from the project
- **D**: Ignore it since personal relationships are private

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Personal relationships exist and can't be forbidden. Acknowledge: the relationship is a fact. Guidelines: clarify what project information can be shared (probably most status information) vs what's confidential. Level the playing field: ensure formal channels provide equivalent information to all stakeholders. Address concerns: if confidential information was shared, address that specifically.

Option A is impractical and controlling.

Option C punishes normal relationships.

Option D ignores potential governance issues.
