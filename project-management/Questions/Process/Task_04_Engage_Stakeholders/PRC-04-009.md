# Question

A project will implement a new system that eliminates several administrative positions. The affected employees' union representative is a stakeholder who is currently "resistant" on the engagement assessment matrix. The desired state is "neutral". What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Exclude the union representative from communications to avoid conflict
- **B**: Focus engagement efforts elsewhere since moving from resistant to neutral is unlikely
- **C**: Engage proactively by understanding concerns, involving in transition planning, and addressing job displacement transparently
- **D**: Wait until the project is complete to address workforce concerns

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Resistant stakeholders need proactive engagement, not avoidance. Understanding concerns (job security, member welfare) and involving the representative in transition planning (retraining, redeployment) demonstrates respect and may shift them toward neutral. Transparency about impacts builds credibility even if the news is unwelcome.

Option A (exclude) increases resistance. Excluded stakeholders often become active opponents through other channels.

Option B (give up) accepts ongoing opposition that could escalate. Even partial improvement in engagement is valuable.

Option D (wait until complete) means facing maximum resistance at implementation when options are limited.
