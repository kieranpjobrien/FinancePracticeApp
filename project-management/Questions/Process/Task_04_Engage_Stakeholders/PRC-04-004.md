# Question

A project manager is working on a regulatory compliance project. The legal department head insists on being consulted for every decision, causing delays. The CFO wants rapid progress to meet a regulatory deadline. Both are high-power stakeholders. How should the project manager manage this conflict?

## Options

- **A**: Prioritise the CFO's needs since timeline is the primary constraint
- **B**: Create a RACI matrix that defines specific decisions requiring legal consultation versus those that can proceed independently
- **C**: Escalate to the CEO to determine which stakeholder takes precedence
- **D**: Satisfy the legal head by copying them on all communications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A RACI matrix clarifies roles and responsibilities, defining which decisions require legal consultation (Consulted or Accountable) and which can proceed with legal simply being Informed. This addresses both stakeholders' legitimate concerns: legal oversight for compliance matters and speed for the overall project.

Option A (prioritise CFO) ignores a legitimate stakeholder concern. Legal consultation on a compliance project exists for good reason - skipping it creates risk.

Option C (escalate to CEO) is a last resort. The PM should first attempt to resolve stakeholder conflicts through negotiation and clear agreements.

Option D (copy on all communications) doesn't solve the problem - it actually makes it worse by overwhelming legal with information while not clarifying decision rights.
