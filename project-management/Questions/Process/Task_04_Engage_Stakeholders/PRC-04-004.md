# Question

The PM learns that two key stakeholders on a cross-functional project have a long-standing personal conflict unrelated to the project. Their disagreement is now affecting project decisions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the personal conflict with HR for mediation since it predates the project.
- **B**: Meet with each stakeholder separately to understand their project-specific concerns, then structure project forums so decisions focus on facts and criteria rather than personalities.
- **C**: Assign a single point of contact between the two so they don't have to interact directly.
- **D**: Defer the affected decisions to the sponsor to remove the two stakeholders from the decision path.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM's remit is project behaviour, not fixing personal history. (B) separates the project concerns from the personal conflict and redesigns the decision process to minimise the impact of the latter. (A) HR mediation of a personal dispute is outside the PM's role and will likely fail. (C) a buffer ignores the underlying dynamic and creates single-point-of-failure communication. (D) escalating every decision to the sponsor undermines stakeholder authority and is not scalable.
