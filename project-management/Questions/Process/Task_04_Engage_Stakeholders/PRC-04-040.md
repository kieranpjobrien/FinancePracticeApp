# Question

The PM needs to present bad news to stakeholders - a significant delay. Some stakeholders will be angry. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Delay informing them as long as possible
- **B**: Communicate proactively with facts, explanation, impact, and proposed actions - be honest, take accountability, and don't make excuses while showing the path forward
- **C**: Blame the team publicly
- **D**: Minimise the delay in the presentation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Bad news delivery requires courage and honesty. Proactive: don't wait to be asked. Facts: what happened? Why? (explanation, not excuses). Impact: what does it mean for stakeholders? Actions: what's being done to respond? Accountability: own it. Path forward: show how you'll proceed. This builds trust even when news is bad.

Option A delays necessary decisions and damages trust when discovered.

Option C damages team relationships and doesn't solve the problem.

Option D is dishonest and will be discovered.
