# Question

A stakeholder who rarely engages suddenly becomes very active — attending meetings, asking detailed questions, raising requests. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enjoy the increased engagement without questioning what has triggered the sudden change.
- **B**: Ask the stakeholder to slow down their requests so the project team isn't overwhelmed.
- **C**: Meet with the stakeholder to understand what has changed and adjust the engagement approach.
- **D**: Assume a political threat is emerging and quietly prepare defensive responses with the sponsor.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A sudden shift in engagement almost always reflects a change — a new role, new pressure, new interest. (C) investigates and adjusts without assuming intent. (A) passive acceptance misses the chance to understand and shape the new engagement. (B) asking them to slow down is tone-deaf if their increased engagement is for a legitimate reason. (D) assuming hostility poisons the relationship when the shift may be positive.
