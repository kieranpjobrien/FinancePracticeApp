# Question

A project manager notices that a key stakeholder who was initially supportive has become disengaged and stopped attending project update meetings. The stakeholder's department will be heavily impacted by the project deliverables. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Document the stakeholder's absence and escalate to the project sponsor
- **B**: Remove the stakeholder from the communication distribution list to reduce noise
- **C**: Schedule a one-on-one meeting to understand the stakeholder's concerns and current priorities
- **D**: Continue with the project and re-engage the stakeholder closer to implementation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When a previously engaged stakeholder becomes disengaged, the project manager should proactively investigate the cause. A one-on-one meeting allows the PM to understand if there are concerns about the project, competing priorities, or other factors affecting engagement. This is active stakeholder management.

Option A (escalate) is premature. The PM should first attempt to understand and resolve the situation before involving the sponsor.

Option B (remove from communications) worsens the problem. This stakeholder's department will be impacted, so keeping them uninformed increases project risk.

Option D (wait until implementation) is risky. Disengaged stakeholders often become resistant stakeholders. Re-engaging late means less time to address concerns and risks late-stage opposition.
