# Question

A stakeholder is spreading negative information about the project within the organisation, damaging its reputation and support. The information is partially accurate but presented misleadingly. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the stakeholder publicly
- **B**: Engage the stakeholder directly to understand their perspective, provide accurate information transparently, build broader communication to counter misinformation, and address legitimate concerns they may have
- **C**: Start spreading negative information about the stakeholder
- **D**: Ignore the situation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Address both the relationship and the misinformation. Engage: understand why they're doing this - unmet needs, genuine concerns, political motivations? Transparency: provide accurate information through appropriate channels. Broader communication: ensure stakeholders have correct information. Address concerns: if there are legitimate issues behind the criticism, address them. This tackles root causes.

Option A escalates publicly without understanding.

Option C is unprofessional and escalates conflict.

Option D allows reputation damage to continue.
