# Question

The sponsor is publicly supportive of the project but the PM observes they often don't champion it in executive committee. Delivery milestones are being missed as a result. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bypass the sponsor by building relationships directly with other executives.
- **B**: Publicly request stronger sponsorship in the next steering committee.
- **C**: Assume the sponsor's level of support is fixed and plan mitigations around it.
- **D**: Meet with the sponsor privately, share specific observations of where championship was needed, and agree on concrete commitments.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Sponsor behaviour can be coached — but only if the PM raises it directly, with specifics, in private. (D) is honest, specific, and respectful. (A) bypassing the sponsor creates political problems and breaks governance. (B) public pressure in steering committee is confrontational and damages the relationship. (C) assuming the gap is permanent accepts a preventable problem and plans workarounds instead of addressing the root cause.
