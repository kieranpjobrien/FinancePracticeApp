# Question

On a public-sector project, a journalist contacts the PM directly to request information on project performance. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Answer the journalist's questions directly based on currently publicly available project data.
- **B**: Decline to comment and forward the journalist's request to the project sponsor for handling.
- **C**: Wait to respond until the next scheduled public status update is released to everyone.
- **D**: Redirect the journalist to the organisation's communications function and follow internal media protocols.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

External media engagement on public-sector projects is almost always governed by communications protocols. (D) respects those protocols and protects both the PM and the organisation. (A) answering directly bypasses governance and may contradict an organisational position. (B) forwarding to the sponsor isn't wrong but skips the communications function whose job this is. (C) silent deferral damages the relationship with media and invites negative framing by default.
