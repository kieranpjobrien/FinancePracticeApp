# Question

The project will have negative impacts on one stakeholder group (job losses in one department) while benefiting others. The affected group is understandably resistant. How should the PM approach engagement with this group?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid engaging since you can't help them
- **B**: Engage honestly about the impact, involve HR and leadership for support services, listen to concerns, explore mitigation where possible, and treat them with dignity throughout
- **C**: Pretend the negative impact won't happen
- **D**: Exclude them from all communications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Honesty and dignity are essential with negatively impacted stakeholders. Engage honestly: don't pretend the impact isn't real. Support: involve HR for transition services, outplacement, etc. Listen: their concerns are legitimate even if you can't eliminate the impact. Explore mitigation: are there alternatives, redeployment options, or ways to soften impact? Dignity: they're people facing difficult changes.

Option A abandons stakeholders who deserve respect.

Option C is dishonest and will damage trust.

Option D excludes people who need information about their situation.
