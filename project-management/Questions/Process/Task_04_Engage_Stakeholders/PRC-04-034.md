# Question

Virtual project stakeholders are distributed globally. Face-to-face engagement isn't possible, but the PM notices that stakeholder relationships are weaker than in co-located projects. How can engagement be improved?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that virtual relationships are weaker
- **B**: Use video calls rather than audio-only, create opportunities for informal interaction, be more deliberate about relationship building, and consider occasional face-to-face meetings for key stakeholders when feasible
- **C**: Increase written communication only
- **D**: Reduce engagement activities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Virtual relationships require more deliberate cultivation. Video: seeing faces builds connection better than voice alone. Informal interaction: virtual coffee chats, team events, personal check-ins. Deliberate: relationship building that happens naturally in-person must be intentional virtually. Face-to-face: if budget allows, periodic in-person meetings strengthen relationships.

Option A accepts suboptimal outcomes without trying to improve.

Option C misses the richer communication of video.

Option D weakens engagement further.
