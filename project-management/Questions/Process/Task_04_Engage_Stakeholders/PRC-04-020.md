# Question

The PM has sent three status reports to a stakeholder group and received no acknowledgement. How should the PM confirm engagement is working?

## Options

- **A**: Assume no news is good news and continue the current reporting cadence without changes.
- **B**: Add more detail and more frequent reports to increase the project's visibility with the group.
- **C**: Reach out directly to confirm the reports are being received and are meeting their needs.
- **D**: Stop the reports entirely to see whether anyone in the group notices their absence.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats communication as a two-way exchange — reports are not engagement; feedback is. (C) verifies the reports are actually landing and being read. (A) silence is ambiguous and may mean reports aren't reaching the intended audience. (B) more volume doesn't fix a problem that may be relevance or delivery. (D) testing by withholding is passive-aggressive and risks leaving stakeholders uninformed at a critical moment.
