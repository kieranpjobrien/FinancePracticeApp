# Question

A project is being delivered to a client. The client's project manager is supportive, but their end users are resistant. The client PM says not to engage directly with their users. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage with client users anyway
- **B**: Respect the client's governance while expressing concern about user resistance, provide materials the client PM can use, and ensure the risk of poor user adoption is documented
- **C**: Ignore user resistance since it's the client's problem
- **D**: Escalate to bypass the client PM

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Respect client governance while managing risk. Don't bypass: honour the client's decision about their organisation. Express concern: help the client PM understand the risk of user resistance. Support: provide materials, talking points, or training they can use. Document: ensure the adoption risk is formally acknowledged. This respects boundaries while managing project success factors.

Option A violates client governance.

Option C ignores a risk to project success.

Option D damages the relationship with the client PM.
