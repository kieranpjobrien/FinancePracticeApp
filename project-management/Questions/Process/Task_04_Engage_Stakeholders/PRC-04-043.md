# Question

What is an engagement strategy of "manage closely" typically used for?

## Options

- **A**: Micromanaging team members
- **B**: High-power, high-interest stakeholders who need active engagement, regular communication, and involvement in key decisions
- **C**: Stakeholders who cause trouble
- **D**: External regulatory bodies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. "Manage closely" is the strategy for stakeholders with both high power (can significantly influence the project) and high interest (care about outcomes). These stakeholders should be actively engaged: frequent communication, involvement in decisions, addressing their concerns promptly. They can be project champions or blockers depending on how well they're engaged.

Option A misunderstands the context.

Option C describes behaviour, not classification.

Option D may or may not fit this category depending on their specific power/interest.
