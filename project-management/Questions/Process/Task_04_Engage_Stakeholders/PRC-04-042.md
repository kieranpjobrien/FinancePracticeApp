# Question

The project team includes people from different departments who have competing priorities with their home departments. Their functional managers (stakeholders) sometimes pull them for non-project work. How should the PM manage this?

## Options

- **A**: Demand full-time dedication from all team members
- **B**: Build relationships with functional managers, establish clear priority agreements, maintain visibility of project impact when resources are pulled, and escalate through the sponsor when conflicts can't be resolved locally
- **C**: Accept that resources will be pulled unpredictably
- **D**: Only use dedicated resources

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Matrix resource conflicts require relationship management. Relationships: functional managers are stakeholders who need engagement. Agreements: establish upfront how priorities are determined and resource conflicts resolved. Visibility: when pulls happen, ensure the project impact is understood. Escalation: when local resolution fails, involve sponsors to arbitrate priorities.

Option A may not be feasible in a matrix organisation.

Option C accepts dysfunction without attempting to manage it.

Option D may not be practical with available resources.
