# Question

A PM needs a specific decision from a CFO within two weeks to keep the project on track. The CFO is hard to reach and has no direct reporting line to the sponsor. Previous attempts to get on the CFO's calendar have failed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue attempting to book the CFO directly via their executive assistant with increasing frequency until the decision can be made.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor to broker the conversation with the CFO since the sponsor's authority will help elevate the request above competing demands.
- **C**: Send the CFO a detailed written paper on the decision so they can respond at their own pace whenever convenient for them.
- **D**: Engage a trusted peer of the CFO to understand preferred routes in, then craft a short, decision-focused brief tailored to their style.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Executive engagement is about access and format; PMI-aligned practice uses peer networks and tailored briefs rather than brute-force booking or delegated asks. (D) works through access and style. (A) repeated booking attempts rarely change the calendar reality. (B) sponsor brokering is useful but abdicates the PM's relationship-building role. (C) a detailed paper is often ignored at exec level without prior context or a decision focus.
