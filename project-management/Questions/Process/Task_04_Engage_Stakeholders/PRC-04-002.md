# Question

A stakeholder analysis reveals that a middle manager is resistant to the project because it automates work their team currently performs. They have been spreading negative information about the project. What engagement strategy should the project manager use?

## Options

- **A**: Exclude them from communications since they're spreading negativity
- **B**: Escalate their behavior to senior management
- **C**: Meet with them to understand their concerns, explore how affected staff might be redeployed or upskilled, and address legitimate fears about their team's future
- **D**: Provide more information about project benefits to counter their negative messaging

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The resistance has a legitimate root cause - fear for their team. Addressing this underlying concern through genuine dialogue (understanding fears, exploring solutions like redeployment) is more effective than information campaigns or exclusion. People resist when they feel threatened; addressing the threat reduces resistance.

Option A excludes an influential stakeholder, which may increase their resistance and underground opposition.

Option B escalates what's a change management issue, not misconduct. The manager is protecting their team.

Option D fights messaging with messaging without addressing the underlying fear. Facts rarely overcome emotional concerns.
