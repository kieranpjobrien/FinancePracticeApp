# Question

A community stakeholder group is raising concerns that the project will affect local services. Their concerns are partially factual and partially based on outdated information. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a press release correcting the inaccuracies so the public record is accurate.
- **B**: Refer them to the organisation's public affairs team and stop engaging directly.
- **C**: Ignore the group since they are not formal stakeholders with decision authority.
- **D**: Meet with the group, listen to their concerns, acknowledge the valid points, clarify the outdated information with evidence, and agree on ongoing dialogue.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Community stakeholders are legitimate even without formal authority — their concerns can still affect delivery. (D) models the PMI approach: listen, acknowledge, correct, commit to dialogue. (A) issuing a press release without a conversation escalates and often entrenches the group. (B) handing off avoids the direct engagement that would build trust. (C) dismissing non-formal stakeholders is a common error that produces public opposition.
