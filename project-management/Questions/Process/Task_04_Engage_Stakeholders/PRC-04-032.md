# Question

A project in a consulting firm has the client as a key stakeholder. The client's representative keeps changing what they want, causing scope creep. The PM needs to maintain the relationship while managing scope. What approach should be taken?

## Options

- **A**: Accept all changes to keep the client happy
- **B**: Establish a change control process that the client agrees to, document changes and their impacts, and have honest conversations about trade-offs while maintaining a collaborative relationship
- **C**: Refuse all client changes
- **D**: Escalate every change request to the client's management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Balance client satisfaction with project viability. Change control: establish a process both parties agree to. Document: ensure changes and impacts (cost, time, scope trade-offs) are visible. Honest conversations: help the client understand implications of changes. Collaborative: maintain relationship - you're partners in project success, not adversaries.

Option A leads to unsustainable scope creep.

Option C damages the client relationship.

Option D escalates when direct engagement could resolve issues.
