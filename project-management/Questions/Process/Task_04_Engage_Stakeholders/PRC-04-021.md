# Question

During stakeholder identification, the team realises they've missed a critical stakeholder group - a union that represents workers who will be affected by the project. The union has significant power. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Hope the union doesn't notice the project
- **B**: Engage immediately with appropriate organisational support (HR, labour relations), be transparent about the oversight, understand their concerns, and integrate them into project governance
- **C**: Continue without engaging the union
- **D**: Cancel the project to avoid union issues

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Late discovery of powerful stakeholders requires immediate attention. Engage: don't delay. Organisational support: work with HR and labour relations who have expertise and established relationships. Transparency: acknowledge they should have been engaged earlier. Understand concerns: what do they need? Integrate: include them appropriately in governance. Better late than never.

Option A is risky and likely to fail.

Option C ignores a powerful stakeholder who could derail the project.

Option D is excessive when engagement could work.
