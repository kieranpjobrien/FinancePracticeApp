# Question

A new stakeholder has joined the programme mid-way through and expects the team to pick up several feature ideas they've been pushing elsewhere in the organisation. The team's backlog is already fully committed for the next two release cycles. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the new stakeholder's items to the backlog so they can be refined and prioritised alongside other business requests going forward.
- **B**: Explain the fully-committed two release cycles to the stakeholder and deflect any further requests until the following planning cycle.
- **C**: Meet with the stakeholder to understand the value behind their items, then have the PO prioritise against existing backlog transparently.
- **D**: Raise the capacity constraint to the sponsor so the sponsor can adjudicate between the new stakeholder and existing commitments.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI and agile practice channel new demand through the value-based prioritisation process rather than accepting or deflecting by default. (C) does that. (A) adding items without prioritisation bloats the backlog. (B) deflecting without understanding value damages the relationship and misses real value. (D) sponsor escalation on routine prioritisation is over-governance.
