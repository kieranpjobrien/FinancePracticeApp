# Question

At sprint review, stakeholders accept a new feature as meeting its acceptance criteria but raise several minor usability concerns that were never part of the criteria. The team confirms the story meets the Definition of Done. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Send the team back to fix the usability concerns before they accept the story as done so the stakeholders' feedback is acted on immediately.
- **B**: Accept the story as done because it meets the agreed Definition of Done, and capture the usability concerns as new backlog items for the product owner to prioritise.
- **C**: Update the Definition of Done to include usability criteria with effect from this sprint and reapply it to the story under review.
- **D**: Defer the decision to the product owner during the next backlog refinement and leave the story in an "accepted-with-conditions" state in the meantime.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI agile practice respects the agreed Definition of Done as the contract for "done" and captures emergent feedback as new backlog items the PO can prioritise. (B) does both. (A) moves the goalposts after the fact and is unfair to the team. (C) retroactively changes the DoD for work already completed. (D) leaves work in an ambiguous state and delays a decision the team can already make at the review.
