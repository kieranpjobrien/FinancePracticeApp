# Question

A control chart for a process has Upper Control Limit (UCL) = 10.5 and Lower Control Limit (LCL) = 9.5. The customer specification limits are USL = 11 and LSL = 9. The latest sample reads 10.8. What is the BEST conclusion?

## Options

- **A**: The product is within the customer specification, but the process is statistically out of control and the assignable cause should be investigated.
- **B**: Both the product and the process are acceptable because 10.8 is below the upper specification limit of 11.
- **C**: The product must be rejected because the measurement exceeds the upper control limit of 10.5 set on the chart.
- **D**: The process is out of control and production should be halted immediately until the cause is corrected.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Control limits describe process stability; specification limits describe product acceptability. The reading 10.8 is inside the spec (9-11) so the product is acceptable, but it exceeds the UCL (10.5) so the process is statistically out of control. (A) is correct. (B) ignores the process signal. (C) treats a process control limit as a product specification. (D) overreacts — out-of-control means investigate, not always halt.
