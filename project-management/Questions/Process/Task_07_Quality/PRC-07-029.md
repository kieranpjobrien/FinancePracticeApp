# Question

A process has upper control limit (UCL) of 10.5 and lower control limit (LCL) of 9.5. The specification limits are USL=11 and LSL=9. A measurement of 10.8 is recorded. What should the PM conclude?

## Options

- **A**: The process is out of control and requires immediate correction
- **B**: The product is within specification but the process is out of statistical control
- **C**: Both the product and process are acceptable
- **D**: The product should be rejected as it fails quality requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Control limits (UCL/LCL) measure process stability; specification limits (USL/LSL) measure product acceptability. The measurement 10.8 exceeds UCL (10.5) indicating the process is out of statistical control, but it's within the specification limit (11), so the product is acceptable.

Option A correctly identifies the process is out of control but "immediate correction" overstates the response. Investigation is needed.

Option C ignores that 10.8 > 10.5 (UCL). The process has an assignable cause that should be investigated.

Option D is incorrect because the product (10.8) is within specification limits (9-11). The product passes; it's the process that needs attention.
