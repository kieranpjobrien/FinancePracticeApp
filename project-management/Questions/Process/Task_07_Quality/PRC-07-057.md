# Question

A project spent $40,000 on training and process design, $25,000 on inspections, $60,000 on rework for pre-release defects, and $150,000 on warranty claims. What is the cost of non-conformance?

## Options

- **A**: $275,000, the total quality spending across all prevention, appraisal, internal, and external failure categories overall.
- **B**: $150,000, because only external failure (warranty) counts as true non-conformance from the customer's perspective specifically.
- **C**: $210,000, the sum of internal failure (rework) and external failure (warranty) costs, which represent non-conformance overall.
- **D**: $65,000, the total of prevention and appraisal costs, which represent the price paid to avoid non-conformance issues.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cost of non-conformance = internal failure + external failure = rework ($60k) + warranty ($150k) = $210,000. (A) sums all quality costs, including conformance. (B) ignores internal failure. (D) sums conformance (prevention + appraisal), the opposite of what the question asks.
