# Question

On a 15-month predictive build, three quality leading indicators have trended amber for two consecutive months: review turnaround, test-coverage movement, and defect-escape rate. No single indicator is red. The sponsor wants to "wait and see." The PM sees a pattern. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the sponsor's wait-and-see guidance so the situation has time to stabilise without premature management action.
- **B**: Treat the pattern as a serial warning, trigger a targeted quality review, and recommend early interventions to the sponsor.
- **C**: Add a fourth indicator to the board to confirm the pattern is genuine before any material action is taken on the project.
- **D**: Escalate the concern to the programme board over the sponsor's head because the pattern is material and multi-factor.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A pattern across multiple leading indicators is an early-warning signal that warrants proactive action; the PM brings evidence and recommends intervention. (B) is correct. (A) defers on weak rationale. (C) delays action by looking for more data rather than acting on what the data already shows. (D) goes over the sponsor's head prematurely before being given the chance to act.
