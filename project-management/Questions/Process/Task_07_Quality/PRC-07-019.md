# Question

A construction project requires inspecting 10,000 concrete blocks. Full inspection would take 3 weeks and delay the project. What quality control approach should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Skip inspection to maintain the schedule since the supplier is reputable
- **B**: Implement statistical sampling to inspect a representative subset
- **C**: Hire additional inspectors to complete full inspection faster
- **D**: Request the supplier provide certification and skip project inspection

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Statistical sampling allows quality verification of a large population by inspecting a representative sample. This provides reasonable confidence in quality while managing time and cost constraints. The sample size is determined by acceptable quality levels.

Option A accepts unquantified risk. Reputation isn't a substitute for verification, especially for structural materials.

Option C might work but is expensive and may still cause delays. Sampling is more efficient when properly designed.

Option D transfers responsibility but doesn't verify quality. Supplier certification is valuable but shouldn't replace receiving inspection entirely for critical materials.
