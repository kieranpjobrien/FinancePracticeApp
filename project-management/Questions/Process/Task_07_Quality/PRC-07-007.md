# Question

A project has a Cost of Quality breakdown showing: Prevention costs = $15,000, Appraisal costs = $10,000, Internal failure costs = $8,000, External failure costs = $12,000. What is the total Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ)?

## Options

- **A**: $45,000
- **B**: $20,000
- **C**: $25,000
- **D**: $35,000

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ) includes only the failure costs - both internal and external.

**Cost of Quality components:**
- **Conformance costs** (doing it right):
  - Prevention: $15,000
  - Appraisal: $10,000
- **Non-conformance costs** (Cost of Poor Quality):
  - Internal failure: $8,000
  - External failure: $12,000

**COPQ = Internal failure + External failure = $8,000 + $12,000 = $20,000**

Option A ($45,000) is the total Cost of Quality (all four categories combined).

Option C ($25,000) adds appraisal to failure costs incorrectly - appraisal is a conformance cost.

Option D ($35,000) incorrectly excludes prevention but includes appraisal with failures.
