# Question

The project sponsor asks the PM to cut testing time in half to accelerate delivery. Testing was budgeted based on industry standards for this type of project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Comply with the sponsor's request since they control the budget
- **B**: Present the quality risks associated with reduced testing and let the sponsor decide
- **C**: Refuse to reduce testing because it would compromise quality
- **D**: Propose risk-based testing to focus efforts on highest-risk areas

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. Risk-based testing prioritises testing efforts on areas with highest probability or impact of failure. This approach can reduce testing time while maintaining focus on what matters most. It's a collaborative solution that addresses both schedule pressure and quality concerns.

Option A abdicates professional responsibility. The PM should inform the sponsor of consequences, not blindly comply.

Option B is passive. While informing stakeholders is important, the PM should also propose solutions, not just present problems.

Option C is confrontational and doesn't offer alternatives. Flat refusal doesn't help find a workable solution.
