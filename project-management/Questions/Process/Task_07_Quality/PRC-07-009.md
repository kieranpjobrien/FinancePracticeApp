# Question

A Pareto chart is used in quality management. What does it help identify?

## Options

- **A**: Whether a process is in statistical control
- **B**: The vital few causes that produce the majority of problems (80/20 rule)
- **C**: Correlation between two variables
- **D**: The sequence of process steps

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A Pareto chart is a bar chart that arranges causes by frequency (descending order) with a cumulative line. It visualises the Pareto Principle (80/20 rule) - typically 80% of problems come from 20% of causes. This helps teams focus improvement efforts on the "vital few" causes that will have the greatest impact.

Option A (statistical control) is shown by control charts, not Pareto charts.

Option C (correlation) is shown by scatter diagrams.

Option D (process sequence) is shown by flowcharts or process maps.
