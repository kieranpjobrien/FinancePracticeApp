# Question

An agile team's velocity has been consistent at 30 story points per sprint for several months. The product owner asks the team to commit to 40 points in the next sprint to meet a deadline. What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Support the increased commitment since the team should stretch to meet business needs
- **B**: Recommend maintaining sustainable pace and exploring scope or deadline adjustments
- **C**: Suggest the team work overtime for one sprint to demonstrate commitment
- **D**: Agree to the higher commitment but plan for reduced quality standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Sustainable pace is an agile principle that protects both quality and team wellbeing. Historical velocity is the best predictor of future performance. The PM should help stakeholders understand this and explore alternatives like adjusting scope or negotiating the deadline.

Option A ignores the evidence of consistent velocity. Arbitrary stretch goals typically result in either burnout or quality problems.

Option C violates sustainable pace principles and sets a dangerous precedent. Overtime should be the exception, not a planning tool.

Option D explicitly compromises quality, which contradicts the PM's responsibility to maintain project quality standards.
