# Question

What is the difference between quality assurance (QA) and quality control (QC)?

## Options

- **A**: QA is performed by testers; QC is performed by developers
- **B**: QA focuses on process and preventing defects; QC focuses on product and detecting defects
- **C**: QA is done before development; QC is done after
- **D**: QA is optional; QC is mandatory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality Assurance is process-focused - it establishes processes and standards to prevent defects from being created. Quality Control is product-focused - it inspects deliverables to detect defects that exist. QA is proactive (prevent); QC is reactive (detect).

Option A incorrectly assigns roles - both QA and QC involve multiple roles.

Option C oversimplifies timing - both occur throughout the project.

Option D incorrectly suggests QA is optional when both are important.
