# Question

Two days before the end of a sprint, a developer asks the Scrum Master if they can skip the code-review step from the team's Definition of Done on one story so the sprint goal can still be met. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the exception this once because the sprint goal is the team's commitment and protecting it is the Scrum Master's primary responsibility.
- **B**: Hold the line on the Definition of Done, help the team find another way to meet the goal (split the story, pull it from the sprint, or pair on the review), and surface the pressure pattern in the next retrospective.
- **C**: Refer the request to the product owner since they own the priority and any quality trade-off should be their call.
- **D**: Allow the exception, document it as a quality deviation in the project's risk register, and add a follow-up story to perform the code review next sprint.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role is to protect team standards and coach the team through pressure, not to negotiate the Definition of Done away under deadline. (B) does that — keep the standard, find a real path to the goal, and address the systemic pressure later. (A) sets a precedent that quality gates are negotiable when timing is tight. (C) misallocates the decision; the Definition of Done is owned by the developers, not the PO. (D) "documents" the shortcut rather than preventing it and creates a deferred risk that may never be paid down.
