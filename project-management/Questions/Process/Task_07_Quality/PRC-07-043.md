# Question

A new project manager inherits a project plagued by rework. Investigation shows the team has been gold-plating — adding small UX features beyond the agreed scope, which then introduce defects. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a written instruction that no work outside scope is to be performed without a change request.
- **B**: Have a candid conversation with the team to understand why they have been adding unrequested features.
- **C**: Strip the unrequested features out of the upcoming release immediately.
- **D**: Prepare a quality issues report for leadership outlining the rework data and propose corrective controls.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gold-plating is a symptom, not a root cause; the team may be filling unclear requirements, responding to side-channel stakeholder requests, or trying to deliver value they don't see in the backlog. PMI's bias is to address root cause first. (A) imposes a control without understanding the driver and likely demoralises the team. (C) acts on output without diagnosing the system, and may remove things users now rely on. (D) escalates symptoms before the PM has done the diagnostic work expected at their level.
