# Question

A project team uses a control chart to monitor a manufacturing process. The chart shows seven consecutive data points above the mean, though all are within control limits. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing - points are within control limits
- **B**: Investigate for an assignable cause since seven consecutive points on one side indicates a trend
- **C**: Stop production immediately
- **D**: Recalculate the control limits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The "Rule of Seven" in statistical process control states that seven consecutive points on one side of the mean indicates a non-random pattern, even if within control limits. This suggests the process has shifted and requires investigation. There may be an assignable (special) cause that, while not yet producing defects, indicates a change in the process.

Option A misunderstands control charts. Being within limits isn't the only signal - patterns matter too.

Option C is overreaction - the process isn't out of control, just showing a trend that needs investigation.

Option D doesn't address the root cause. The limits may be correct; the process may have drifted.
