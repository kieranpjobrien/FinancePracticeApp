# Question

A control chart shows seven consecutive data points above the mean, with all points still within the upper and lower control limits. Which conclusion is BEST supported by Statistical Process Control?

## Options

- **A**: The process is in control and performing on target because all points lie within the control limits.
- **B**: The control limits are too wide and should be tightened to detect the trend earlier in future runs.
- **C**: The pattern indicates a non-random shift in the process that should be investigated for an assignable cause.
- **D**: The points reflect normal random variation and no further action is required at this stage.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

SPC's "Rule of Seven" treats seven consecutive points on one side of the mean as a non-random signal even when control limits are not breached. (C) responds correctly. (A) confuses "within limits" with "in control" — patterns also matter. (B) re-tunes limits based on a likely real shift, which would mask future similar shifts. (D) misclassifies a clear non-random pattern as random.
