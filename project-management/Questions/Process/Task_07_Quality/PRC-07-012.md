# Question

A control chart for a manufacturing process shows seven consecutive data points above the mean but within control limits. What does this indicate?

## Options

- **A**: The process is in control and performing well
- **B**: The process shows a trend that requires investigation
- **C**: The control limits need to be recalculated
- **D**: Normal random variation that requires no action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Rule of Seven states that seven consecutive points on one side of the mean indicates a non-random pattern, even if all points are within control limits. This suggests a systematic cause (assignable cause) that should be investigated.

Option A is incorrect because being within control limits alone doesn't mean the process is performing optimally. The pattern indicates something has shifted.

Option C is premature. Control limits shouldn't be recalculated until the cause of the trend is understood and addressed.

Option D misinterprets the pattern. Random variation would show points distributed on both sides of the mean.
