# Question

A project manager notices that different team members interpret "done" differently. One considers a feature done when it works locally; another only when it's deployed to staging. What should the PM facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a written Definition of Done that the entire team agrees to
- **B**: Let each team member use their own definition based on their expertise
- **C**: Have the product owner decide what "done" means for each feature
- **D**: Use the most rigorous definition to ensure highest quality

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The correct answer is A. Inconsistent understanding of "done" leads to integration problems, false progress reporting, and quality issues. A team-agreed, written Definition of Done creates shared understanding and consistent quality standards across all work.

Option B perpetuates the inconsistency. Individual definitions don't work when team members' work must integrate.

Option C puts burden on one person and may vary feature to feature. Consistency requires a team standard, not case-by-case decisions.

Option D might seem safe but may be unnecessarily burdensome for some types of work. The team should agree on what's appropriate for their context.
