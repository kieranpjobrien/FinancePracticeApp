# Question

In Lean thinking, "build quality in" most directly emphasises which of the following?

## Options

- **A**: Adding more inspection stages downstream to catch defects.
- **B**: Stopping the line when a defect is detected and fixing it at the source.
- **C**: Maintaining a separate quality assurance department independent of the team.
- **D**: Producing detailed quality procedure manuals for every workstation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lean's "build quality in" is operationalised through andon (stop-the-line) and poka-yoke (mistake-proofing): defects are addressed where they occur, not detected later. (A) is exactly the inspection-heavy approach Lean rejects. (C) externalises quality, contradicting "quality is everyone's job." (D) confuses documentation with practice.
