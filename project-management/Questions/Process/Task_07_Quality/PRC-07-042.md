# Question

In Lean methodology, what does the concept of "building quality in" primarily emphasise?

## Options

- **A**: Adding more inspection points throughout the process
- **B**: Stopping work immediately when a defect is detected to fix it at the source
- **C**: Having dedicated quality assurance team members
- **D**: Extensive documentation of quality procedures

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In Lean, "building quality in" means defects are prevented or caught immediately at their source through practices like andon (stopping the line when problems occur), poka-yoke (mistake-proofing), and empowering workers to halt production for quality issues. This prevents defects from flowing downstream.

Option A adds inspection but doesn't prevent defects at the source.

Option C separates quality responsibility from the team. Lean emphasises that quality is everyone's job.

Option D focuses on documentation rather than the practices that actually build quality into the work.
