# Question

A project manager notices that the team is spending significant time fixing defects found during testing. Analysis shows most defects originate from unclear requirements. What quality management approach should the PM prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Increase testing coverage to catch more defects earlier
- **B**: Invest in prevention activities like requirements reviews and prototyping
- **C**: Add more testers to the team to speed up defect identification
- **D**: Implement stricter acceptance criteria for completed work

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The cost of quality framework shows that prevention costs (training, requirements reviews, prototyping) are far less expensive than failure costs. Since defects originate from unclear requirements, addressing the root cause through prevention activities is most effective.

Option A addresses detection, not prevention. Finding defects faster doesn't reduce the cost of fixing them.

Option C adds resources to detection rather than addressing the root cause of unclear requirements.

Option D may catch issues earlier but doesn't prevent the defects from being introduced in the first place.
