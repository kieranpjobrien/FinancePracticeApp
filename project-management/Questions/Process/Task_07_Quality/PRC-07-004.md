# Question

What is the difference between quality assurance and quality control?

## Options

- **A**: Quality assurance focuses on process compliance; quality control focuses on product inspection
- **B**: Quality assurance is performed by management; quality control is performed by the team
- **C**: Quality assurance happens during planning; quality control happens during execution
- **D**: Quality assurance is optional; quality control is mandatory

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The correct answer is A. Quality assurance (QA) is process-oriented - it ensures the processes used to manage and create deliverables are followed and effective. Quality control (QC) is product-oriented - it involves inspecting deliverables to verify they meet requirements (e.g., testing, reviews, inspections).

Option B (who performs) is incorrect. Both QA and QC can be performed by various roles depending on the organisation.

Option C (when performed) is partially true but incomplete. QA activities occur throughout the project lifecycle, not just planning. QC occurs when deliverables exist to inspect.

Option D (optional vs mandatory) is incorrect. Both QA and QC are important components of quality management.
