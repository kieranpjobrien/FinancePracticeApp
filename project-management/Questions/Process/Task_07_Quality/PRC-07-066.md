# Question

A team retrospective reveals developers have been hiding defects from the PM because they fear the sponsor's reaction. Defect counts in reports are artificially low. The PM realises stakeholder trust is at risk and team psychological safety is poor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reprimand the individuals who hid the defects so the team understands that transparency is a serious expectation now.
- **B**: Introduce automated defect reporting so manual suppression becomes impossible and defect numbers surface independently of developers.
- **C**: Raise the issue with the sponsor and request a softer reaction to bad news so developers feel safer reporting defects.
- **D**: Address the safety root cause with the team, commit to transparent reporting, and reset sponsor expectations on honest data.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Defect hiding is a symptom of poor psychological safety and misaligned sponsor behaviour; both need addressing together. (D) is correct. (A) punishes symptoms and worsens the safety problem. (B) is a control but does not restore trust. (C) asks the sponsor to change alone without the team-side reset.
