# Question

A DevOps team wants to move to continuous delivery with deployments multiple times a day. The risk and compliance function insists on a weekly change advisory board for every release. The team argues the CAB is now the bottleneck and blocks quality-improvement deployments. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the weekly CAB intact because governance is non-negotiable and the team must adapt its cadence to compliance.
- **B**: Remove the CAB for this project on the grounds that DevOps practices replace the need for manual governance reviews.
- **C**: Go live with continuous delivery and raise any CAB concerns only if an incident occurs in production post-deployment.
- **D**: Facilitate a joint review with risk and compliance to design automated controls that give equivalent assurance without the bottleneck.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Modern governance designs controls into the pipeline so assurance is continuous and fast, not manual and slow; the PM facilitates that design. (D) is correct. (A) keeps the bottleneck. (B) unilaterally removes a control without an equivalent. (C) is reckless and violates change control.
