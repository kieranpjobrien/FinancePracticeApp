# Question

What distinguishes a quality audit from a quality inspection?

## Options

- **A**: Audits are performed by external parties; inspections are internal
- **B**: Audits evaluate whether quality activities follow the plan; inspections verify product conformance
- **C**: Audits happen at project end; inspections happen throughout
- **D**: Audits are mandatory; inspections are optional

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality audits are structured reviews of quality management activities to identify good practices, non-conformance, and improvement opportunities. Quality inspections examine specific deliverables to verify they meet requirements. Audits focus on process compliance; inspections focus on product conformance.

Option A is incorrect. Both audits and inspections can be performed by internal or external parties.

Option C is incorrect. Audits can occur throughout the project, not just at the end.

Option D is incorrect. Both may be mandatory or optional depending on organisational policies, regulations, and contracts.
