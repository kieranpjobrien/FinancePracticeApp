# Question

A pharmaceutical project must comply with FDA regulations requiring extensive documentation. The team argues this documentation slows them down without adding value. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor to get the documentation requirements waived
- **B**: Explain that regulatory compliance is non-negotiable and help the team find efficient ways to meet requirements
- **C**: Reduce documentation to minimum viable compliance to balance speed and regulation
- **D**: Hire additional resources specifically for documentation so the team can focus on development

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Regulatory compliance isn't optional - it's a constraint. The PM's role is to help the team understand why requirements exist (patient safety, legal liability) and find efficient processes. Perhaps templates, automation, or integrated tooling can reduce burden while maintaining compliance.

Option A is inappropriate. Regulatory requirements can't be waived by internal sponsors. Attempting this shows misunderstanding of the constraint.

Option C risks non-compliance. "Minimum viable" is subjective and dangerous with regulators. Full compliance is required.

Option D might help but doesn't address the team's attitude. Understanding why documentation matters (traceability, patient safety) is important for team buy-in.
