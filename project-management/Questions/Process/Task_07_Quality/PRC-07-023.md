# Question

A pharmaceutical project is subject to FDA Good Manufacturing Practice requirements that mandate extensive process documentation and audit trails. Several team members complain that the documentation effort is non-value-adding overhead and want it cut. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Explain that regulatory compliance is non-negotiable, help the team understand why the documentation matters (patient safety, traceability, audit defence), and work with them to streamline how it is produced — templates, automation, fit-for-purpose detail.
- **B**: Approve a project-specific reduction in documentation to a "minimum viable" level so the team can move faster while still claiming compliance.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor and request a temporary waiver from the FDA documentation requirements until the project's launch milestone passes.
- **D**: Add contract documentation specialists to the team so the engineers can keep building without the administrative burden.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats compliance, especially in regulated industries, as a non-negotiable constraint. (A) addresses both the requirement and the team's concern by explaining the why and removing friction in how. (B) creates compliance risk by reducing controls without authority to do so. (C) requests something the sponsor cannot grant — the FDA, not the sponsor, sets these requirements. (D) hides the burden by hiring around it without addressing the team's misunderstanding of why the documentation matters.
