# Question

Which statement BEST describes the relationship between quality and grade?

## Options

- **A**: A high-grade product is by definition a high-quality product because it offers more features and capabilities.
- **B**: Grade and quality are interchangeable terms that both describe how well a product meets stakeholder expectations.
- **C**: Low quality is always a problem; low grade can be perfectly acceptable if the product still meets requirements for its intended use.
- **D**: Low grade is always a problem; low quality can be acceptable if the project is operating under tight cost constraints.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes the two clearly: quality is conformance to requirements (low quality is never acceptable), while grade is a category of features or characteristics (low grade is acceptable when fit for purpose, e.g., an economy car). (C) captures this. (A) confuses grade with quality. (B) treats them as synonyms. (D) reverses the relationship — low quality cannot be traded away for cost.
