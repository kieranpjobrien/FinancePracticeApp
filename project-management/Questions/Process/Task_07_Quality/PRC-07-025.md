# Question

Which statement BEST describes the relationship between quality and grade?

## Options

- **A**: High quality always means high grade
- **B**: Low grade is always a problem, low quality may be acceptable
- **C**: Low quality is always a problem, low grade may be acceptable
- **D**: Quality and grade are interchangeable terms

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Quality refers to meeting requirements - a product that doesn't meet its requirements is low quality and always problematic. Grade refers to category of features or characteristics. A low-grade product (economy car vs luxury car) can be perfectly acceptable if it meets requirements for its intended purpose.

Option A confuses the concepts. A budget hotel can be high quality (clean, functional, meets expectations) while being low grade (limited amenities).

Option B reverses the relationship. Low grade is often intentional and acceptable; low quality is never acceptable.

Option D is incorrect. They measure different things - fitness for purpose vs feature richness.
