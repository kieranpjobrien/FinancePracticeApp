# Question

An agile team ships working software every two weeks. Individual features pass their acceptance tests, but stakeholders report that the overall user experience is inconsistent — different navigation patterns, mismatched terminology, and uneven visual styling across recent releases. What should the team change?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a hardening sprint at the end of the release to clean up UX inconsistencies in batch.
- **B**: Update the team's Definition of Done to include UX-consistency criteria so each story conforms before it can be marked complete.
- **C**: Extend each sprint by two days to give the team additional polish time before the sprint review.
- **D**: Engage a dedicated UX lead to perform a comprehensive UX review at the end of every quarter.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI agile guidance is to build quality into every increment by tightening the Definition of Done rather than batching fixes or extending durations. (B) does that. (A) accumulates UX debt and delays feedback, the opposite of agile. (C) lengthens sprints without addressing why UX is not being checked. (D) is too late and too coarse — by the time the quarterly review runs, three months of inconsistent work is in production.
