# Question

A fishbone diagram is also known as which of the following?

## Options

- **A**: Pareto diagram
- **B**: Ishikawa diagram
- **C**: Control chart
- **D**: Run chart

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A fishbone diagram is also called an Ishikawa diagram (after its creator Kaoru Ishikawa) or a cause-and-effect diagram. It organises potential causes of a problem into categories branching from a central spine, resembling a fish skeleton.

Option A (Pareto diagram) is a bar chart showing defect frequency in descending order with a cumulative line.

Option C (control chart) monitors process variation over time against upper and lower control limits.

Option D (run chart) displays data points over time to identify trends but without statistical control limits.
