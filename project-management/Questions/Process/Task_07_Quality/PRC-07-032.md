# Question

Which quality concept emphasises building quality into the product rather than inspecting for defects after the fact?

## Options

- **A**: Quality control
- **B**: Quality assurance
- **C**: Prevention over inspection
- **D**: Continuous improvement

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. "Prevention over inspection" is a core quality principle that emphasises designing and building quality into processes and products rather than relying on inspection to catch defects. It's more cost-effective to prevent defects than to find and fix them later.

Option A (quality control) is product-focused inspection - the opposite of prevention.

Option B (quality assurance) focuses on process but includes both prevention and detection activities.

Option D (continuous improvement) is about ongoing enhancement but doesn't specifically capture the prevention vs inspection trade-off.
