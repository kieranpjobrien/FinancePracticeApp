# Question

During a quality audit, the auditor finds that the team is not following the documented testing procedures but their defect rates are actually lower than similar projects. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Require immediate compliance with documented procedures
- **B**: Document the variance and investigate why the alternative approach works better
- **C**: Update the procedures to match what the team is doing
- **D**: Report the non-compliance to the project sponsor

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality audits identify both non-conformance and improvement opportunities. The team's approach is producing better results, which warrants investigation. Understanding why before making changes allows for informed decisions about updating standards.

Option A prioritises compliance over outcomes. While process discipline matters, blindly enforcing procedures that produce worse results isn't good quality management.

Option C is premature. Before updating organisational procedures, you need to understand why the approach works and whether it's repeatable and applicable elsewhere.

Option D escalates unnecessarily. The PM should investigate first. This is a potential improvement opportunity, not necessarily a problem requiring sponsor attention.
