# Question

A quality audit finds that the team is not following the documented testing procedures from the QMS. However, their measured defect rate is materially lower than that of comparable projects following the documented process. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the team to return to the documented procedure immediately because non-compliance with the QMS is itself a quality finding.
- **B**: Investigate why the team's alternative approach is producing better outcomes and document the variance, then engage the QMS owner about updating the standard.
- **C**: Update the project's quality plan to formalise the team's current practice as the local standard.
- **D**: Report the non-compliance to the project sponsor and the quality manager so they can decide whether enforcement action is required.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats audit findings as opportunities for organisational learning, not just compliance issues. (B) investigates the root cause of the better outcome before changing anything, then engages the right authority (the QMS owner) about a possible standard update. (A) enforces compliance without understanding why the alternative works. (C) changes the standard locally without the QMS owner, which fragments organisational practice. (D) escalates before the PM has done the investigation and brought options.
