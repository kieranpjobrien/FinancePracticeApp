# Question

A PMO template requires a 60-page quality management plan for every project. Your three-month, five-person predictive project does not need this level of documentation. The PMO insists all templates must be used in full. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce the full 60-page plan as required by the PMO to remain compliant with organisational process standards overall.
- **B**: Propose a tailored quality plan that meets the PMO's intent in a form proportionate to the project, with justification.
- **C**: Ignore the PMO template and produce a one-page plan that suits the project, raising concerns only if questioned later.
- **D**: Escalate the template dispute to the sponsor and ask the sponsor to intervene with the PMO on the project's behalf.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tailoring is a core PMI practice; the PM proposes a proportional approach that meets intent and negotiates it with the PMO. (B) is correct. (A) wastes effort and creates low-quality documentation that no one uses. (C) violates governance without negotiation. (D) is premature escalation over a tailoring conversation the PM should have directly.
