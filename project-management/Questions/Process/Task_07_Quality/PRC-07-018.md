# Question

What is the primary difference between quality assurance and quality control?

## Options

- **A**: QA is performed by the team; QC is performed by external auditors
- **B**: QA focuses on process; QC focuses on product inspection
- **C**: QA happens during planning; QC happens during execution
- **D**: QA prevents defects; QC documents defects for lessons learned

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality Assurance is process-focused - ensuring the processes used to manage and create deliverables are effective. Quality Control is product-focused - inspecting specific deliverables to verify they meet requirements.

Option A is incorrect. Both QA and QC can be performed by internal or external parties depending on the organisation.

Option C is partially true but incomplete. QA is ongoing throughout the project (not just planning), and QC occurs whenever deliverables are ready for inspection.

Option D oversimplifies. While QA does help prevent defects through process improvement, and QC does identify defects, the fundamental distinction is process vs product focus.
