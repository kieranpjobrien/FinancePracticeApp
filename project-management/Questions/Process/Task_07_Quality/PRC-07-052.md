# Question

A medical-device project is under ISO 13485 compliance. A senior engineer proposes shortcutting a required design-history-file review to hit a launch date. He argues "we've done this dozens of times; it'll pass anyway." The PM has schedule pressure from the sponsor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the shortcut given the engineer's experience and conduct a full review retrospectively after the launch has shipped.
- **B**: Reject the shortcut, keep the mandated review, and manage the schedule impact through proper stakeholder communication steps.
- **C**: Document the shortcut in the quality record as an agreed deviation and proceed to launch with it noted in the record.
- **D**: Delegate the decision to the quality manager and stay neutral so the PM does not appear to be influencing compliance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulatory compliance is non-negotiable in medical-device work; the PM protects the process and manages the schedule consequence transparently. (B) is correct. (A) ships a non-compliant product. (C) treats unilateral skipping as a deviation, which violates the quality management system. (D) abdicates PM accountability for compliance culture.
