# Question

A scatter diagram shows a strong positive correlation between hours of testing and defects found. What should the PM conclude?

## Options

- **A**: More testing always finds more defects, so testing should be maximised
- **B**: The team should investigate why more testing correlates with more defects
- **C**: Testing is effective and should continue at current levels
- **D**: The correlation proves that testing causes defects to be found

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Correlation doesn't imply causation, and the relationship could indicate several things: modules that need more testing have more defects (good targeting), or more complex features naturally have both. Understanding the relationship helps make informed decisions about testing strategy.

Option A oversimplifies. There's a point of diminishing returns, and the correlation doesn't prove causation.

Option C may be correct but accepts the correlation without understanding it. Understanding why helps optimise the approach.

Option D confuses correlation with causation. The scatter diagram shows relationship, not cause and effect.
