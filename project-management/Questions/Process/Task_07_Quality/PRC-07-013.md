# Question

A software team delivers features that pass all tests but users report the application is difficult to use. The product owner is frustrated. What quality concept is the team likely missing?

## Options

- **A**: Grade - the features lack premium characteristics
- **B**: Fitness for purpose - meeting technical specs but not user needs
- **C**: Gold plating - the team added unnecessary features
- **D**: Conformance to requirements - the tests are inadequate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality means fitness for purpose - the product must satisfy the needs for which it was intended. Passing technical tests while failing to meet user needs indicates the team focused on conformance to specifications rather than actual usability.

Option A confuses quality with grade. Grade refers to category of features (economy vs luxury), not usability. A low-grade product can still be high quality for its intended purpose.

Option C is the opposite situation. Gold plating means adding unrequested features, but here the features don't meet user needs.

Option D partially addresses the symptom (tests don't catch usability issues) but doesn't identify the core concept being violated.
