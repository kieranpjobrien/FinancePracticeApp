# Question

A Scrum team has no automated regression tests; every release requires two weeks of manual testing by contractors. Release cadence has slowed from monthly to quarterly. The product owner wants faster releases but resists "non-feature" work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with manual testing for now and raise test automation in the next annual planning cycle for capacity allocation.
- **B**: Introduce a test-automation initiative using a separate capacity pool outside the team so feature velocity stays intact.
- **C**: Present the release-cycle data to the product owner, quantify the value of automation, and negotiate a sustained investment.
- **D**: Unilaterally allocate 30 percent of each sprint to test automation since quality engineering is the PM's technical remit.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM influences through data and negotiation, not unilateral allocation or avoidance; showing the PO the opportunity cost of slow releases is the lever. (C) is correct. (A) kicks the can. (B) creates a parallel team that fragments accountability. (D) overrides the product owner's prioritisation authority.
