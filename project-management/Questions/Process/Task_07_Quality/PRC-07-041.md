# Question

During a retrospective, the team identifies that their testing environment doesn't match production, causing bugs to slip through. The infrastructure team says a matching environment would cost $50,000. How should the PM approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the risk since the cost seems too high
- **B**: Perform a cost-benefit analysis comparing environment cost against cost of production defects
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor requesting additional budget for quality
- **D**: Find workarounds to simulate production conditions without the full environment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Quality decisions should be based on data. Comparing the $50,000 investment against the cost of production defects (support costs, customer impact, rework, reputation) provides an objective basis for decision-making. Prevention costs are often less than failure costs.

Option A accepts risk without quantifying it. The cost might actually be justified if production defects are expensive.

Option C escalates without analysis. The sponsor will want to know the business case, not just the request.

Option D might be appropriate but should follow analysis. Workarounds may not fully address the risk and could create their own problems.
