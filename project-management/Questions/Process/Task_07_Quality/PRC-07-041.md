# Question

In a retrospective, a team identifies that their test environment differs significantly from production, allowing config-related bugs to slip into release. Building a production-parity environment will cost roughly $50,000. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the risk because the spend is large relative to the project budget.
- **B**: Quantify the historical and projected cost of production defects and compare to the $50,000 investment.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor with a request for additional budget for quality.
- **D**: Have the team build temporary scripts that approximate production behaviour at low cost.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A cost-of-quality decision should rest on the comparison between prevention/appraisal cost and failure cost. Without the numbers, neither approval nor rejection is defensible. (A) accepts a risk without quantifying it. (C) escalates without analysis, which sponsors rightly push back on. (D) may be a reasonable interim measure but should follow, not replace, the decision analysis.
