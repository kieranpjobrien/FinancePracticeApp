# Question

What distinguishes an RFP from an RFQ?

## Options

- **A**: RFPs are for government contracts; RFQs are for commercial contracts
- **B**: RFPs request detailed proposals on approach and qualifications; RFQs request price quotes for well-defined items
- **C**: RFPs are binding; RFQs are non-binding
- **D**: RFPs are used for large procurements; RFQs are used for small procurements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. An RFP (Request for Proposal) asks vendors to propose how they would approach the work, including methodology, qualifications, and pricing. An RFQ (Request for Quote/Quotation) asks for pricing on well-defined items or services where the scope is clear and comparison is mainly on price.

Option A incorrectly associates document types with sectors.

Option C mischaracterises the binding nature of both documents.

Option D oversimplifies - both can be used for various procurement sizes.
