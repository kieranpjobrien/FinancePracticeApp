# Question

The lead architect named as key personnel in the vendor's proposal has just resigned from the vendor. The vendor proposes a replacement they say has equivalent qualifications. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the replacement immediately because the qualifications appear similar and the project cannot afford delay.
- **B**: Terminate the vendor for non-compliance with the named-personnel clause.
- **C**: Renegotiate the contract price downward because the original named team is no longer in place.
- **D**: Apply the contract's key-personnel substitution clause: require the vendor's formal request, review the replacement's credentials against the named role, and approve, conditionally approve, or reject.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Most contracts with key-personnel clauses define the substitution process, including notification, qualification review, and buyer approval. The PM should follow it. (A) skips the verification the contract specifically requires. (B) is disproportionate where substitution is permitted with approval. (C) is opportunistic and not supported by contract terms unless renegotiation is explicitly tied to personnel changes.
