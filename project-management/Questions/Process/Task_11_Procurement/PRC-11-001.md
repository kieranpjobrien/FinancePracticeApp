# Question

The project needs a specialised analytics module. The internal data team estimates eight months to build it; two vendors have shipping products that could be configured in eight weeks. The PM needs to recommend a path. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend the vendor option because vendor delivery is faster and reduces project complexity.
- **B**: Recommend in-house build because it preserves long-term internal capability and IP control.
- **C**: Conduct a make-or-buy analysis covering total cost of ownership, time, capability, strategic fit, and risk.
- **D**: Defer to the technical team's preference because they will live with the chosen solution.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI prescribes a structured make-or-buy analysis before recommending a sourcing path; the answer depends on multiple factors, none of which are dominant a priori. (A) and (B) jump to a conclusion without the analysis. (D) substitutes preference for analysis and excludes business factors the team may not see. The make-or-buy analysis is what enables the recommendation in the first place.
