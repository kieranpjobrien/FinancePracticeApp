# Question

A project requires specialized software that could be built in-house with significant effort or purchased from a vendor. The project manager needs to make a recommendation. What analysis should be performed?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose the vendor option to reduce project complexity
- **B**: Always choose in-house to maintain control over quality
- **C**: Perform a make-or-buy analysis comparing total cost, time, capability, and strategic factors
- **D**: Ask the technical team their preference

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Make-or-buy analysis is a structured approach to this common project decision. It considers multiple factors: total cost of ownership (not just purchase/build price), time to deliver, internal capability, strategic value of developing the capability internally, vendor reliability, and long-term support. Neither option is always better - it depends on the specific situation.

Option A assumes vendor is always better, which ignores situations where internal capability is strategically valuable or vendors don't meet needs.

Option B assumes in-house is always better, ignoring cost, speed, and capability advantages vendors may offer.

Option D abddicates analysis to preference, which may not consider business factors the team doesn't see.
