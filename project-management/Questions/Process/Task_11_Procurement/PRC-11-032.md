# Question

What is a teaming agreement?

## Options

- **A**: An agreement between project team members about roles
- **B**: An agreement between potential vendors to bid together on a procurement
- **C**: An agreement between the buyer and seller about team composition
- **D**: An agreement between the PM and functional managers about resources

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A teaming agreement is a legal arrangement between two or more organisations to combine capabilities and bid together on a procurement opportunity. It typically covers how work and revenue will be shared if the bid is successful, and what happens if it isn't.

Option A describes internal team agreements, not procurement teaming.

Option C might be part of a contract but isn't a teaming agreement.

Option D describes resource agreements, not procurement teaming.
