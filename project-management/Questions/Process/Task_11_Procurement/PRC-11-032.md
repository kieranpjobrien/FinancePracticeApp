# Question

What is a teaming agreement in procurement?

## Options

- **A**: A formal agreement between two or more sellers to jointly bid on a buyer's procurement.
- **B**: A document defining roles and responsibilities within the project team.
- **C**: An agreement between the PM and functional managers about resource availability.
- **D**: A clause in the contract that requires the seller to use a specific buyer-provided team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A teaming agreement is a pre-bid pact between two or more organisations that combine capabilities to submit a joint proposal, typically defining work share, revenue split, and contingencies if they don't win. (B) describes a team charter or RACI. (C) describes a resource agreement, internal to the buyer. (D) describes a contract clause about staffing, not a teaming arrangement.
