# Question

Which contract type places the MOST risk on the buyer?

## Options

- **A**: Fixed Price
- **B**: Cost Plus Percentage of Cost
- **C**: Time and Materials
- **D**: Cost Plus Fixed Fee

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cost Plus Percentage of Cost (CPPC) places maximum risk on the buyer because the seller is reimbursed for all costs PLUS a percentage of those costs. This creates perverse incentives - the seller earns more profit by incurring higher costs. The buyer has no cost certainty.

Option A (Fixed Price) places most risk on the seller, who must deliver at the agreed price regardless of actual costs.

Options C and D place moderate risk on the buyer but have some controls (T&M has rates; CPFF has a fixed fee cap).
