# Question

A project requires cloud services. The vendor offers a standard terms-of-service agreement that isn't negotiable. The organisation's legal team has concerns about some terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the standard terms since cloud vendors don't negotiate
- **B**: Find a different vendor that will negotiate terms
- **C**: Work with legal to understand the risks and determine if they're acceptable given business needs
- **D**: Proceed without involving legal since cloud services are standard

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. When standard terms can't be negotiated, the decision becomes whether to accept the risks or not use the service. Legal should assess the risks, and business stakeholders should decide if the service value justifies accepting those risks. This is an informed risk-acceptance decision, not automatic acceptance.

Option A accepts risk without assessment.

Option B may not be practical if all vendors have similar terms.

Option D bypasses necessary risk assessment.
