# Question

The project depends on a SaaS vendor whose terms of service are non-negotiable click-through agreements. Legal has concerns about indemnity and data protection clauses. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the standard terms because the vendor will not negotiate and the project needs the service.
- **B**: Restart the procurement and select an alternative vendor that will negotiate bespoke terms.
- **C**: Engage legal and the affected business owner to assess the residual risks, capture any accepted risks in the risk register, and decide whether to proceed.
- **D**: Sign the agreement on the PM's authority and bring the risk to legal's attention afterwards if a problem occurs.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When terms cannot be negotiated, the decision becomes informed risk acceptance involving legal and the risk owner; the residual risk goes on the register. (A) accepts without explicit analysis or risk ownership. (B) may be impractical and ignores the option of accepting the risk. (D) bypasses governance and is an ethics issue.
