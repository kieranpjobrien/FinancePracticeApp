# Question

A contracted vendor has completed their deliverable but the buyer's team hasn't had time to perform acceptance testing. The contract payment terms are "net 30 days from acceptance." The vendor is pressuring for payment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the vendor since the deliverable was submitted
- **B**: Explain that payment terms are tied to acceptance, and acceptance requires testing
- **C**: Prioritise acceptance testing to enable timely payment
- **D**: Request a contract amendment to change the payment terms

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The vendor has fulfilled their obligation by delivering. The buyer should fulfil their obligation to perform acceptance testing in a reasonable timeframe. Delaying payment indefinitely because of buyer-side delays is unfair and damages the relationship. The PM should prioritise testing to enable payment.

Option A pays without verification that deliverables meet requirements.

Option B is technically correct but doesn't solve the problem.

Option D changes terms rather than addressing the execution issue.
