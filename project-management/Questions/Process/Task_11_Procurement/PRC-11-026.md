# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between a purchase order and a contract?

## Options

- **A**: Purchase orders are for goods; contracts are for services
- **B**: Purchase orders are typically simpler and for straightforward transactions; contracts are more comprehensive for complex arrangements
- **C**: Purchase orders are legally binding; contracts are not
- **D**: Purchase orders don't require approval; contracts do

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Purchase orders are typically used for simpler, more standardised transactions where terms are straightforward. Contracts provide comprehensive terms and conditions for complex arrangements, custom deliverables, or situations requiring detailed specifications of obligations, risks, and remedies.

Option A oversimplifies - both can be used for goods or services.

Option C is incorrect - purchase orders are legally binding agreements.

Option D is incorrect - both require appropriate approvals.
