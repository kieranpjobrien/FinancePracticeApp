# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between a purchase order and a formal contract in procurement?

## Options

- **A**: Purchase orders are not legally binding while contracts are.
- **B**: A purchase order is typically a simpler instrument for routine, well-defined transactions, while a contract is a more comprehensive agreement for complex or custom arrangements.
- **C**: Purchase orders are used only for goods, contracts are used only for services.
- **D**: Purchase orders bypass approval workflows that contracts must go through.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A PO is a legally binding but lightweight instrument used for routine, defined transactions; a contract is a fuller agreement appropriate for complex deliverables, risk allocation, and custom terms. (A) is wrong because POs are binding once accepted. (C) is wrong; both can be used for goods or services. (D) is wrong; both follow internal approval processes.
