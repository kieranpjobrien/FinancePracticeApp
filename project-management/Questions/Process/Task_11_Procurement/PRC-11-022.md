# Question

A vendor on a cost-reimbursable contract is billing for significant overtime hours. The work is progressing, but costs are exceeding estimates. The contract allows reasonable overtime with buyer approval. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to pay the overtime since it wasn't pre-approved
- **B**: Pay the overtime and accept the cost overrun
- **C**: Review whether the overtime is reasonable and necessary, and establish clearer approval processes
- **D**: Convert the contract to fixed-price to cap costs

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should verify the overtime is legitimate (is the work progressing proportionately?) and whether it was properly approved. If approval processes are unclear, clarify them going forward. Cost-reimbursable contracts require active cost monitoring, not passive acceptance of all expenses.

Option A may breach contract if overtime was implicitly approved or approval process was unclear.

Option B doesn't exercise appropriate oversight.

Option D can't unilaterally change contract type mid-engagement.
