# Question

A vendor performing well under their current contract asks the PM to extend their scope to include additional work without going through the standard competitive process. The additional work is within their stated capabilities. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Award the additional work to the vendor since their performance to date is strong.
- **B**: Require a full competitive procurement regardless of incumbent performance.
- **C**: Negotiate the addition directly with the vendor because it is faster and avoids procurement overhead.
- **D**: Check the organisation's procurement policy on sole-source and contract-extension thresholds, then proceed accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to operate inside organisational and regulatory procurement policy, which typically defines when sole-source or non-competitive extensions are permitted. (A) and (C) act before confirming policy compliance. (B) may be unnecessary if policy permits an extension and would waste effort.
