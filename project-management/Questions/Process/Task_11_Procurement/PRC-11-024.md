# Question

A vendor performing well on their current contract asks to be considered for additional work without going through the standard procurement process. The additional work is within their expertise. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Award the additional work since the vendor has proven performance
- **B**: Require competitive bidding regardless of current performance
- **C**: Evaluate whether organisational procurement policies allow sole-source awards and under what conditions
- **D**: Negotiate directly since it's more efficient

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Many organisations have policies about when sole-source or non-competitive procurement is allowed (e.g., below certain thresholds, for contract extensions, for specialised work). The PM should understand and follow these policies. Good vendor performance may be a factor, but policy compliance is required.

Option A awards without considering policy requirements.

Option B may not be required if policies allow other approaches.

Option D ignores procurement policies.
