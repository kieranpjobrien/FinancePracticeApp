# Question

A vendor delivering a critical-path component has been late on the last three milestones. The contract has liquidated damages for late delivery. The vendor blames unclear specifications from the buyer. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a formal cure notice and begin assessing liquidated damages per the contract.
- **B**: Investigate the specification clarity claim with the team and the vendor before deciding whether to enforce penalties.
- **C**: Waive the liquidated damages to preserve the vendor relationship and recover the schedule together.
- **D**: Begin a parallel procurement for an alternate supplier so termination is an option later.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM must establish facts before invoking contractual remedies. If specifications were genuinely unclear, the buyer contributed to the delay and enforcement may be unfair (and unwinnable in dispute). (A) acts on one side of the story without verifying. (C) gives up contractual protection without diagnosing the root cause. (D) is a sensible mitigation but premature; understanding the cause comes first.
