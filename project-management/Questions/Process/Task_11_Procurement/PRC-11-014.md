# Question

A vendor is consistently delivering late on a critical path component. The contract has penalty clauses for late delivery, but the vendor claims delays are due to unclear specifications from the buyer. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce the penalty clauses since the contract is clear
- **B**: Waive the penalties to maintain the vendor relationship
- **C**: Investigate the specification clarity claim and work to resolve the root cause
- **D**: Terminate the contract and find a new vendor

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before enforcing penalties or making other decisions, the PM should investigate the vendor's claim. If specifications were genuinely unclear, that's a buyer-side issue that needs resolution. Understanding the root cause enables the right response - which might be correcting specifications, enforcing penalties, or both.

Option A ignores the vendor's potentially valid claim.

Option B gives up contractual protection without investigation.

Option D is drastic without understanding the actual problem.
