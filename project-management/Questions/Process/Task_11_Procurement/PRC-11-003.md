# Question

During contract execution, a vendor delivers work that technically meets specifications but clearly doesn't meet the project's actual needs. The specifications in the contract were poorly written. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the delivery since it meets the contract specifications
- **B**: Reject the delivery and demand the vendor provide what's actually needed
- **C**: Negotiate with the vendor to modify the deliverable, recognizing this may require a contract change and additional cost
- **D**: Escalate to legal to enforce proper delivery

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The vendor delivered to spec - they fulfilled their obligation. However, the project still needs the right solution. The PM should work collaboratively with the vendor to modify the deliverable, acknowledging that this is a change (not original scope) and may require contract modification and additional payment.

Option A technically protects the PM but doesn't solve the actual problem - the project still needs usable deliverables.

Option B unfairly expects the vendor to absorb costs for the buyer's specification errors.

Option D involves legal in what's a negotiation opportunity, escalating unnecessarily and damaging the vendor relationship.
