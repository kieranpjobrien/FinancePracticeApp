# Question

A vendor delivers a deliverable that conforms to the contract specifications, but it does not solve the project's actual business problem because those specifications were poorly written by the buyer. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the deliverable because it meets the contractual specifications and close the procurement.
- **B**: Reject the deliverable and require the vendor to deliver what the project actually needs.
- **C**: Engage the vendor to scope the modifications, raise a contract change with appropriate compensation, and route through change control.
- **D**: Escalate to legal to compel the vendor to deliver something more useful.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The vendor met its obligation; the buyer's specification was the gap. Fair resolution is to negotiate a contract modification that reflects the additional work and pay accordingly, while routing the change through integrated change control. (A) is contractually clean but leaves the project without a usable deliverable. (B) unfairly forces the vendor to absorb the buyer's specification error. (D) escalates a negotiation matter into a legal one and damages the relationship.
