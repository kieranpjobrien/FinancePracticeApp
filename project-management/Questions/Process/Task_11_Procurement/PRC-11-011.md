# Question

A project needs to procure custom software development, but requirements are not yet fully defined and will emerge during development. What contract type is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Firm Fixed Price
- **B**: Cost Plus Fixed Fee
- **C**: Fixed Price Incentive Fee
- **D**: Time and Materials with a not-to-exceed clause

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. With undefined requirements, fixed price contracts are risky because scope isn't clear. Time and Materials provides flexibility to adapt as requirements emerge. A not-to-exceed clause provides budget protection while allowing for evolving scope. This balances flexibility with cost control.

Option A is inappropriate because scope is undefined; the seller would need to pad the price for risk.

Options B and C might work but may provide less budget control than T&M with a cap.
