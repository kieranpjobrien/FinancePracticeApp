# Question

A project manager is evaluating vendor proposals. One vendor offers the lowest price but has no experience with similar projects. Another vendor costs 20% more but has completed three similar projects successfully. Which factor should weigh most heavily in the decision?

## Options

- **A**: Price, since budget is always the primary constraint
- **B**: Experience and capability, as execution risk with an unproven vendor may exceed the cost savings
- **C**: Neither - select the first vendor to respond
- **D**: Split the work between both vendors to hedge risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Total cost of ownership includes risk. An inexperienced vendor may deliver late, require more oversight, or produce lower quality work - all of which have costs. The price premium for a proven vendor is often justified by reduced execution risk and more predictable outcomes.

Option A (price only) ignores execution risk. The lowest bid isn't the best value if the vendor can't deliver successfully.

Option C (first responder) is not a legitimate selection criterion and likely violates procurement ethics.

Option D (split work) adds coordination complexity and may not be practical for many deliverables. It's not a standard risk response for vendor selection.
