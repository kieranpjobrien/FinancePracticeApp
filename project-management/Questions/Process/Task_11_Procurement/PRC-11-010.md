# Question

Which contract type places the most cost risk on the seller?

## Options

- **A**: Cost Plus Fixed Fee (CPFF)
- **B**: Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF)
- **C**: Firm Fixed Price (FFP)
- **D**: Time and Materials (T&M)

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. In a Firm Fixed Price contract, the seller commits to deliver at a set price regardless of actual costs. If costs exceed the price, the seller absorbs the loss. This places maximum cost risk on the seller and maximum cost certainty on the buyer.

Option A and B are cost-reimbursable contracts where the buyer pays actual costs plus a fee, placing cost risk on the buyer.

Option D pays for time and materials used, with risk shared depending on how it's structured.
