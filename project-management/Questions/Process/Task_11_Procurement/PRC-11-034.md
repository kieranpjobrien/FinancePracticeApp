# Question

Independent estimates prepared by the buyer before receiving proposals are used to:

## Options

- **A**: Set the maximum price the buyer is willing to pay
- **B**: Evaluate whether received proposals are reasonable and competitive
- **C**: Determine which vendor to select
- **D**: Calculate the project budget

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Independent estimates provide a baseline for evaluating proposals. If all proposals significantly exceed the estimate, it may indicate the scope was underestimated or the market is different than expected. If proposals are significantly below, it may indicate vendors don't understand requirements or are lowballing.

Option A may be one use, but reasonableness evaluation is the primary purpose.

Option C oversimplifies - selection involves many factors beyond price comparison to estimate.

Option D is project-level, not procurement-specific.
