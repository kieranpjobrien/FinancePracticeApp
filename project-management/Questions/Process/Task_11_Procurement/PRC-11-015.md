# Question

During contract negotiations, the vendor proposes terms significantly different from the RFP requirements. The vendor's alternative approach would actually work better for the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the alternative since it doesn't match the RFP
- **B**: Accept the alternative approach if it meets project needs better
- **C**: Evaluate the alternative against original requirements and involve appropriate stakeholders in the decision
- **D**: Ask all vendors to rebid based on the new approach

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A better approach is worth considering, but changing requirements mid-procurement has implications. The PM should evaluate whether the alternative truly is better, whether it changes the competitive landscape (would other vendors have bid differently?), and involve stakeholders in deciding whether to accept it. Procurement fairness matters.

Option A misses a potential improvement opportunity.

Option B accepts without proper evaluation or stakeholder involvement.

Option D may be appropriate but should follow evaluation of whether rebidding is necessary.
