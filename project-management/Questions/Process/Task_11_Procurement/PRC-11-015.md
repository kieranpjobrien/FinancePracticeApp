# Question

In contract negotiations, the preferred vendor proposes an alternative technical approach that differs from the RFP requirements but appears to meet project needs better. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the alternative because it serves the project better, and finalise the contract on that basis.
- **B**: Reject the alternative outright because vendors must conform to the RFP requirements as they were issued.
- **C**: Evaluate the alternative with procurement and stakeholders on whether to accept or reissue, then document the decision.
- **D**: Reissue the RFP to all original bidders with the new approach to keep the procurement process clean.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM must balance project value with procurement fairness; an alternative may be better, but accepting it without considering whether competitors would have bid differently can taint the procurement. The right step is structured evaluation with procurement and stakeholders, then a documented decision on whether to proceed or reissue. (A) overlooks fairness concerns. (B) rules out a potentially valuable improvement reflexively. (D) may end up being the right action but should follow evaluation rather than be the default.
