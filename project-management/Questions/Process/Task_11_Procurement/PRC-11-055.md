# Question

Under a firm fixed price contract, the vendor has missed two consecutive milestones and appears to be struggling with the work. The contract includes performance cure provisions. The PM is considering options. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Terminate the contract for cause immediately so a replacement vendor can start rebuilding before further slippage.
- **B**: Issue the formal cure notice per the contract, document the performance gap, and monitor the cure period.
- **C**: Offer the vendor additional payment outside the FFP contract to incentivise them back onto track quickly.
- **D**: Wait silently for the vendor to recover on their own, as most vendors recover from short-term performance issues.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contractual cure provisions exist precisely for this situation; issuing the cure notice and documenting performance preserves both the relationship option and the legal position. (A) terminates before exercising contractual cure rights. (C) pays outside the contract, destroying its commercial logic. (D) accepts continued slippage without contractual action.
