# Question

An agile programme needs to engage an external development partner for an embedded module. Traditional fixed-scope contracts conflict with the team's iterative delivery and emergent requirements. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate a contract structure that pays for capacity or outcomes per iteration with adjustable scope.
- **B**: Use a firm fixed-price contract upfront and manage all emergent change through formal contract amendments.
- **C**: Defer the procurement until the agile team has finished elaborating all requirements and acceptance criteria.
- **D**: Avoid external procurement entirely because agile delivery and third-party contracts are fundamentally incompatible.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Agile-aligned contracts (capacity-based, outcome-based, or iteration-scoped with not-to-exceed) preserve the iterative cadence and adaptive scope that agile relies on. (B) imposes predictive control on adaptive delivery and turns every adjustment into a change order. (C) defeats the point of incremental delivery; requirements never finish elaborating. (D) is simply false and rejects a legitimate option.
