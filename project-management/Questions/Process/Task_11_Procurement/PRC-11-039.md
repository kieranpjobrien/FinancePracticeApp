# Question

A multi-year contract is ending and the incumbent vendor has performed well. The buyer wants to compete the next contract to test the market. The vendor expects a renewal. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Renew the contract automatically given the vendor's strong performance to avoid procurement effort.
- **B**: Run a competitive procurement quietly without notifying the incumbent so they cannot influence the process.
- **C**: Use the threat of competition to extract a price reduction from the incumbent without actually competing the work.
- **D**: Notify the incumbent professionally that the work will be competed, invite them to bid, and run the procurement against fair, pre-defined criteria.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Transparent procurement maintains both market discipline and a professional vendor relationship. The incumbent can compete on demonstrated performance and the buyer gets market signal. (A) forfeits the value the buyer wants from competing. (B) treats the incumbent unprofessionally and damages reputation. (C) is coercive and unethical, using a competition the buyer does not intend to run.
