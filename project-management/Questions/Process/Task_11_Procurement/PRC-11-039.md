# Question

A contract is ending and the vendor has provided good service, but the buyer wants to explore alternatives in the market. The vendor expects the contract to be renewed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Renew the contract since the vendor performed well
- **B**: Conduct a competitive procurement without informing the current vendor
- **C**: Communicate intentions early, conduct a fair procurement, and treat the incumbent professionally
- **D**: Use the threat of competition to negotiate better prices with the current vendor

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Professional procurement involves transparency. The current vendor should be informed that renewal isn't automatic and can participate in the competitive process. They may win based on performance advantages and relationship value, or the market may offer better alternatives. Fair treatment maintains professional relationships.

Option A foregoes potential market benefits.

Option B treats the vendor poorly and damages reputation.

Option D uses competition as a threat rather than conducting genuine evaluation.
