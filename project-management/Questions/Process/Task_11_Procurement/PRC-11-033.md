# Question

A vendor is underperforming, missing deliverables and quality standards. The contract has termination for cause provisions. However, finding a replacement vendor would significantly delay the project. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Terminate immediately since the vendor isn't meeting obligations
- **B**: Continue accepting substandard work to avoid delays
- **C**: Evaluate termination costs and delays against recovery possibilities with current vendor
- **D**: Threaten termination to motivate better performance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Termination decisions require analysis of all factors: cost of termination, delay impact, likelihood of finding better alternatives, and whether current vendor performance can be improved. Sometimes working to improve a troubled relationship is better than starting over; sometimes termination is necessary. Analysis should drive the decision.

Option A terminates without considering alternatives.

Option B accepts continued poor performance.

Option D uses threats rather than analysis.
