# Question

A vendor has missed the last two milestones and quality on accepted work is declining. The contract permits termination for cause. Replacing the vendor would add four months to the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a termination-for-cause notice immediately and begin the replacement procurement.
- **B**: Continue accepting the vendor's deliverables to avoid the four-month delay.
- **C**: Use the threat of termination as leverage in the next vendor meeting to motivate improvement.
- **D**: Analyse the cost and schedule impact of termination versus a documented performance improvement plan with the current vendor, and recommend the option with the better risk-adjusted outcome.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Termination is a major decision that requires a structured trade-off analysis: cost and time of replacement, probability of recovery with the current vendor, and risk-adjusted impact on project objectives. (A) acts before analysis. (B) accepts continued underperformance with no plan to recover. (C) substitutes coercion for management; PIPs are an explicit tool for this situation.
