# Question

A multi-year FFP contract is closing on schedule with clean acceptance. The PM is preparing closure activities. Procurement records are scattered across email, SharePoint, and a few team laptops. What should the PM do before formal contract closure?

## Options

- **A**: Formally close the contract now and reconstruct the procurement record later if an audit ever requires it.
- **B**: Close informally by email and leave procurement records exactly where they are for the team to find.
- **C**: Consolidate procurement records into the archive, capture lessons learned, then execute formal closeout.
- **D**: Archive only the final invoices and acceptance certificate, which are the records most likely to matter later.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Contract closeout requires complete, consolidated procurement records, lessons learned capture, and formal closure documentation for organisational learning and audit readiness. (A) reverses the sequence and makes later reconstruction harder. (B) leaves records ungoverned. (D) archives selectively and discards records that may matter for future disputes or learning.
