# Question

On a cost plus incentive fee contract, the vendor has submitted monthly invoices including hospitality costs for a team offsite. The contract lists allowable and unallowable costs, and hospitality is specifically listed as unallowable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the hospitality costs this time because challenging them will damage a strong vendor relationship.
- **B**: Approve half of the hospitality cost as a compromise between relationship and cost allowability principles.
- **C**: Reject the unallowable costs, inform the vendor with reference to contract language, and continue oversight.
- **D**: Pay the invoice in full and recover the hospitality amount at the final reconciliation at contract closeout.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cost allowability in cost-reimbursable contracts is enforced at invoice review; unallowable costs are rejected with contract reference. (A) pays outside contract terms and sets a damaging precedent. (B) compromises a principle that is not negotiable. (D) defers enforcement until closeout, by which point amounts have compounded.
