# Question

A vendor on a T&M contract is logging 30% more hours than estimated for the current work package. The vendor says the work is more complex than originally scoped. The contract allows T&M billing up to a not-to-exceed cap. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the invoices in full because T&M contracts compensate the vendor for actual hours worked.
- **B**: Withhold payment beyond the original estimate until the vendor justifies the variance.
- **C**: Review the original scope and current performance, determine whether the variance reflects scope underestimation or vendor inefficiency, and route any scope expansion through change control.
- **D**: Terminate the T&M contract and re-procure on a fixed-price basis.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

T&M variances can come from scope underestimation (which may justify a contract change) or from vendor inefficiency (which the buyer should not subsidise). The PM must understand the cause before responding and route any genuine scope expansion through change control. (A) pays without diagnosis. (B) may breach contract terms for legitimate work. (D) is disproportionate and expensive without first establishing the cause.
