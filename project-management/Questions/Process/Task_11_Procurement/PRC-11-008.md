# Question

A vendor on a time-and-materials contract is taking longer than expected to complete work, resulting in higher costs than budgeted. The vendor claims the work is more complex than originally scoped. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the invoices since T&M contracts pay for actual time
- **B**: Refuse to pay beyond the original budget
- **C**: Review the original scope, assess whether complexity was underestimated, and negotiate adjustments or scope changes
- **D**: Terminate the contract immediately

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. T&M contracts do pay for actual time, but the PM should understand why actuals exceed estimates. If the scope was genuinely underestimated, an adjustment may be fair. If the vendor is inefficient, that's a different conversation. If scope increased, formal change control applies. Understanding the cause informs the right response.

Option A (just pay) doesn't investigate whether the charges are justified or whether scope management is needed.

Option B (refuse payment) may breach contract terms if the vendor performed legitimate work.

Option D (terminate) is extreme without first understanding the situation and attempting resolution.
