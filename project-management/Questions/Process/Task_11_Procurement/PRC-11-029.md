# Question

A vendor submits a claim for additional payment, asserting the work was out of scope. The PM believes it was within the contract scope. The two parties read the SOW differently. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Deny the claim in writing and refer the vendor to the dispute resolution clause.
- **B**: Pay the claim to preserve the relationship and avoid an extended dispute.
- **C**: Review the contract language, work order history, correspondence, and actual work performed, then meet with the vendor to walk through both interpretations.
- **D**: Refer the claim to legal for a binding contract interpretation before any further discussion.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Most claims resolve through joint analysis of the facts and the contract; the PM should establish what the contract actually requires and what was actually delivered before taking a position. (A) takes a position without analysis and pushes immediately to dispute. (B) settles without analysis and rewards weak claims. (D) escalates prematurely; legal involvement should support negotiation, not replace it as a first step.
