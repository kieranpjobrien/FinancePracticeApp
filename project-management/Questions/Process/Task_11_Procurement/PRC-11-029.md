# Question

A vendor claims additional payment for work they say was out of scope. The PM believes the work was within the contract scope. Both parties interpret the contract language differently. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Deny the claim since the PM believes the work is in scope
- **B**: Pay the claim to maintain the vendor relationship
- **C**: Review the contract language, gather documentation, and discuss with the vendor to understand both positions
- **D**: Immediately escalate to legal for contract interpretation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before taking a position, the PM should thoroughly understand both interpretations. Review the contract language, relevant correspondence, meeting notes, and the work actually performed. Then discuss with the vendor to understand their reasoning. Many claims are resolved through clarification and negotiation.

Options A and B take positions without full analysis.

Option D escalates prematurely before attempting resolution.
