# Question

The PM discovers the prime vendor has subcontracted 40% of the work to a company that was not disclosed in the proposal. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the arrangement provided the deliverables continue to meet specification.
- **B**: Direct the prime to terminate the subcontractor and complete the work using only their own staff.
- **C**: Issue a termination-for-cause notice based on misrepresentation in the proposal.
- **D**: Review the contract's subcontracting and disclosure clauses, determine whether the non-disclosure is a contract breach, and respond proportionately (consent, formal notification, or remedy).

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right response depends on what the contract says about subcontracting, disclosure, and consent; the PM must read the clauses before taking action. (A) ignores potential breach. (B) may not be enforceable if subcontracting is permitted. (C) is disproportionate before the contract analysis confirms a material breach justifies termination.
