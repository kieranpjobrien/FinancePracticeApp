# Question

During contract execution, the PM discovers the vendor is subcontracting a significant portion of work to a company that wasn't disclosed in the proposal. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the subcontracting if the deliverables meet requirements
- **B**: Terminate the contract for misrepresentation
- **C**: Review contract terms regarding subcontracting and address the non-disclosure appropriately
- **D**: Require the vendor to use only their own staff

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Most contracts have provisions about subcontracting - some require disclosure and approval, others prohibit certain subcontracting. The PM should check what the contract requires, assess whether the non-disclosure was material, and take appropriate action ranging from documentation to formal contract remedies.

Option A ignores potential contract violations.

Option B may be excessive depending on contract terms and circumstances.

Option D may not be enforceable if the contract allows subcontracting.
