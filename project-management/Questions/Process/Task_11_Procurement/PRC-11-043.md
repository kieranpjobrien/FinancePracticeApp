# Question

A project has a critical procurement where only one vendor can meet the technical requirements. The vendor knows this and is negotiating aggressively on price and terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's terms since there's no alternative
- **B**: Cancel the project since fair procurement isn't possible
- **C**: Document the sole-source justification and negotiate best achievable terms while exploring long-term alternatives
- **D**: Wait until another vendor enters the market

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Sole-source situations happen when only one vendor can meet requirements. The PM should document why this is a sole source, negotiate the best terms achievable given the power imbalance, and consider strategies to reduce dependence (developing alternatives, building internal capability, modular architecture) for the future.

Option A accepts without attempting negotiation.

Option B abandons potentially valuable work.

Option D may not be practical within project timelines.
