# Question

Only one vendor in the market can supply a critical component. The vendor knows it and is taking aggressive positions on price and terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the sole-source justification, negotiate the best achievable terms, and start work to reduce future dependency.
- **B**: Accept the vendor's terms because there is no alternative and the project cannot proceed without the critical component.
- **C**: Cancel the project outright because a fair commercial outcome is simply not possible under the current market conditions.
- **D**: Pause the procurement indefinitely and wait until competitors enter the market segment with viable alternatives.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sole-source situations require both immediate negotiation under the constraints and a longer-term strategy to reduce dependency. (B) accepts terms without using available negotiation leverage. (C) discards potentially valuable work. (D) waits indefinitely on market dynamics the buyer cannot control.
