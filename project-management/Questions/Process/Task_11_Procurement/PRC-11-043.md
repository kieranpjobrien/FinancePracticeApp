# Question

Only one vendor in the market can supply a critical component. The vendor knows it and is taking aggressive positions on price and terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the sole-source justification, negotiate the best achievable terms now, and start parallel work to reduce future single-vendor dependency (build internal capability, modular architecture, alternate sourcing).
- **B**: Accept the vendor's terms because there is no alternative and the project cannot proceed without the component.
- **C**: Cancel the project because a fair commercial outcome is not possible.
- **D**: Pause the procurement and wait until competitors enter the market segment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sole-source situations require both immediate negotiation under the constraints and a longer-term strategy to reduce dependency. (B) accepts terms without using available negotiation leverage. (C) discards potentially valuable work. (D) waits indefinitely on market dynamics the buyer cannot control.
