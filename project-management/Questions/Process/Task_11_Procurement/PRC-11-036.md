# Question

A reputable, technically qualified vendor has bid 40% below the next lowest proposal and 35% below the buyer's independent estimate. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Award to the low bidder because they are reputable and the savings are significant.
- **B**: Disqualify the bid because it is so far outside the cost range that it cannot be sustainable.
- **C**: Conduct a price-realism review with the vendor to understand the basis of estimate, validate scope understanding, and identify the risk of buy-in pricing or change-order recovery.
- **D**: Average the low bid with the next two bids to set a fair price for negotiation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A bid materially below benchmarks raises legitimate concerns (calculation error, scope misunderstanding, lowballing for change-order recovery); a price-realism review surfaces the risk before award. (A) takes a low bid at face value and exposes the project. (B) discards a potentially valid bid without analysis. (D) is not how competitive procurement works; you cannot create a price by averaging bids.
