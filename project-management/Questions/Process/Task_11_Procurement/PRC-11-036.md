# Question

A vendor has submitted an unusually low bid that's 40% below other proposals. The vendor is reputable and technically qualified. What risk should the PM be most concerned about?

## Options

- **A**: The vendor made a calculation error and will request price increases later
- **B**: The vendor doesn't understand the requirements
- **C**: The vendor plans to make up the shortfall through change orders
- **D**: All of the above are potential risks worth investigating

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. Significantly low bids warrant investigation. Calculation errors can lead to disputes. Requirement misunderstanding leads to performance problems. "Buy-in" pricing with change order recovery damages the relationship and budget. The PM should investigate the pricing basis before awarding to ensure the bid is sustainable.

Options A, B, and C are each valid concerns but incomplete alone.
