# Question

The project needs a vendor to start work in two weeks, but the standard procurement process takes six weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Skip the procurement process due to time constraints
- **B**: Start the vendor without a contract and formalise later
- **C**: Work with procurement to understand expedited processes or interim arrangements
- **D**: Delay the project until procurement completes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Many organisations have expedited procurement processes for urgent needs, or mechanisms like letter contracts, interim agreements, or emergency procurement authorities. The PM should work with procurement to find legitimate ways to meet the timeline while maintaining appropriate controls.

Option A bypasses necessary process.

Option B creates legal and financial risk.

Option D may not be acceptable if the deadline is important.
