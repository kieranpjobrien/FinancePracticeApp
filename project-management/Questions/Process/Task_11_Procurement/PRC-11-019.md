# Question

A fixed-price contract included a warranty clause. Six months after delivery, a defect is discovered that appears to be from original manufacturing. The vendor claims the warranty period has expired. The contract language is ambiguous about when the warranty starts. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's interpretation since they wrote the contract
- **B**: Insist on the buyer's interpretation since ambiguity should favour the buyer
- **C**: Review the contract with legal counsel and negotiate a resolution
- **D**: Escalate immediately to formal dispute resolution

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Contract ambiguity requires interpretation. The PM should involve legal counsel to understand the contract language, identify relevant precedents or industry standards, and then negotiate with the vendor. Most disputes are better resolved through negotiation than formal proceedings.

Option A accepts without analysis.

Option B assumes a rule that may not apply in this jurisdiction.

Option D escalates prematurely before attempting negotiation.
