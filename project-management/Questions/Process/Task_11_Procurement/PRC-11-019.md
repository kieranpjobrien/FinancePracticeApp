# Question

Six months after delivery on a fixed-price contract, the team finds a defect that appears to be original to the manufactured component. The vendor argues the warranty has expired. The contract language about warranty start date is ambiguous. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's reading because the seller drafted the contract and knows their intent.
- **B**: Insist on the buyer's reading because contract ambiguity is generally construed against the drafter.
- **C**: File a formal dispute under the contract's dispute resolution clause to force a binding decision.
- **D**: Engage legal counsel to interpret the warranty clause, identify applicable precedent, and pursue a negotiated resolution with the vendor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM should not invoke a one-sided interpretation or jump to formal dispute without grounded legal analysis. Engaging counsel surfaces what the contract actually means and supports a negotiated outcome, which is faster and cheaper than dispute proceedings. (A) and (B) take positions without analysis; while (B) reflects a common doctrine, it is not universal. (C) escalates before negotiation, which PMI consistently positions as a last resort.
