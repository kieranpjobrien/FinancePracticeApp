# Question

Under a firm fixed price contract for a predictive data centre fit-out, the client asks the PM to add one extra server rack installation. The vendor has quietly started the work to be helpful. The PM is preparing to pay the next invoice. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Process a contract change order for the added rack before authorising any payment against that scope.
- **B**: Pay the invoice with the extra rack included because the vendor completed the additional work in good faith.
- **C**: Decline the extra rack entirely because any FFP scope change is inherently too risky to accept reliably.
- **D**: Pay the base invoice only and ask the vendor to absorb the extra rack as a goodwill gesture to client.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

FFP contracts protect scope boundaries; additions require a formal contract change order with agreed price before payment and before any further work continues. (B) pays outside the contract and undermines procurement governance. (C) refuses a legitimate change rather than processing it. (D) exploits the vendor's goodwill and damages the commercial relationship.
