# Question

During bid evaluation for an infrastructure rebuild, two proposals are technically close but one contains a pricing line that looks unusually low. The PM suspects a pricing error. The evaluation committee is about to score. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with scoring based on the submitted price to preserve evaluation committee timing and momentum.
- **B**: Disqualify the low-priced bid so that no unfair advantage could come from a pricing anomaly.
- **C**: Seek clarification from the bidder through the formal bid process before scoring final commercial positions.
- **D**: Contact the bidder informally to verify the price and then continue evaluation based on their response.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Formal clarification through the bid process preserves fairness, evaluates the real price, and protects the procurement from later dispute. (A) scores a potentially wrong number without verification. (B) disqualifies for a suspected error without confirming. (D) uses informal channels, which creates unfair access and audit exposure.
