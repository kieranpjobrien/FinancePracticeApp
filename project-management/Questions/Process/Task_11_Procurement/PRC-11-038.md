# Question

A project depends on three vendors whose deliverables integrate. Vendor 1 has slipped a milestone, which is now starving Vendors 2 and 3 of inputs. What is the most useful PM intervention?

## Options

- **A**: Convene a joint integration meeting with the three vendors, replan the integration schedule, and confirm interface ownership going forward.
- **B**: Issue a default notice to Vendor 1 and let the others continue working around the gap.
- **C**: Direct Vendors 2 and 3 to redesign their work to remove the dependency on Vendor 1.
- **D**: Wait for Vendor 1 to recover before involving the other vendors.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Multi-vendor integration is a coordination problem the PM must own; bringing the parties together to replan and clarify interfaces is the most direct intervention. (B) addresses contractual remedy but does not solve the schedule cascade. (C) discards approved scope and shifts the burden inappropriately. (D) lets the cascade worsen and wastes the time of the unaffected vendors.
