# Question

A project is closing, but there's a dispute with a vendor over the final payment amount. The project team is being reassigned. The vendor needs payment to close their books. What should the PM ensure before transitioning?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the disputed amount to enable clean project closure
- **B**: Refuse all payment until the dispute is resolved
- **C**: Document the dispute thoroughly and ensure responsibility for resolution is formally transitioned
- **D**: Escalate to legal and wait for resolution before closing

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Project closure shouldn't leave disputes unresolved or unmanaged. The PM should thoroughly document the dispute (claims, positions, relevant evidence), identify who will continue managing it, and formally transition responsibility. This ensures the dispute is addressed even after the project team disperses.

Option A pays without resolving the disagreement.

Option B may harm the vendor unfairly if some payment is clearly owed.

Option D may delay closure indefinitely; disputes can take time to resolve.
