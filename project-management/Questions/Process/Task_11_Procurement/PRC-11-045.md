# Question

The project is closing but a vendor payment dispute remains unresolved. The project team is being reassigned next week and the vendor is pressing for resolution to close their books. What should the PM ensure FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the disputed payment in full to enable a clean project closure and avoid carrying the issue forward indefinitely.
- **B**: Withhold all further payment to the vendor until the dispute is fully resolved through the contract dispute process.
- **C**: Refer the dispute to legal counsel and pause the formal project closure until a binding resolution is reached.
- **D**: Document the dispute and transition ownership to a named role with the authority and information to resolve it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Project closure cannot leave a live dispute owner-less. The PM's job is to make sure the open item is properly documented and transitioned to a named owner with the context needed to settle it. (A) settles a disputed amount without analysis or authority. (B) is unfair if some portion is clearly owed. (C) holds closure hostage to a dispute that may take months to resolve.
