# Question

Which activity is part of contract administration (after award)?

## Options

- **A**: Selecting the seller from competing bids.
- **B**: Performing the make-or-buy analysis to decide whether to procure.
- **C**: Drafting the procurement statement of work.
- **D**: Monitoring seller performance, processing invoices, and managing contract changes.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Contract administration covers the post-award lifecycle: monitoring performance, managing payments, handling claims and changes, and ensuring both parties meet their obligations. (A) happens during conduct procurements before contract award. (B) is a planning-stage activity. (C) is also planning-stage and informs the bid package, not post-award management.
