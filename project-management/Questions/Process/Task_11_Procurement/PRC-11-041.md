# Question

A vendor performed work that the PM considers an out-of-scope change; the vendor argues it is within the existing SOW. Both sides want to keep working together. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document each side's interpretation, hold a working session to compare against the SOW and evidence, and agree a resolution (which may include partial compensation, scope clarification, or trade-offs).
- **B**: Accept the vendor's position to preserve the relationship and keep the project moving.
- **C**: Insist on the buyer's position because the buyer is paying and bears the procurement risk.
- **D**: Refer the disagreement immediately to the contract's formal dispute resolution clause.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Most contract disagreements are best resolved through documented joint analysis and good-faith negotiation, which is also what PMI's emphasis on collaborative procurement management calls for. (B) and (C) take one-sided positions that erode either commercial discipline or the relationship. (D) escalates to formal dispute before negotiation has been attempted, which is a last resort.
