# Question

A vendor performs work that the PM believes is a contract change, but the vendor argues it's within the original scope. Neither party wants to damage the working relationship. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's interpretation to preserve the relationship
- **B**: Insist on the buyer's interpretation since they're paying
- **C**: Document both positions and work together to find an acceptable resolution
- **D**: Defer to the contract's dispute resolution clause

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Most contract disagreements can be resolved through good-faith discussion. Documenting positions creates a record while working toward resolution shows professionalism. Often a middle ground exists - perhaps partial additional payment, or trade-offs elsewhere. Preserving working relationships while protecting interests requires collaborative problem-solving.

Options A and B take one-sided positions.

Option D escalates beyond what's needed for most disagreements.
