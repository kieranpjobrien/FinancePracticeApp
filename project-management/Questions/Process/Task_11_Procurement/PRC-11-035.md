# Question

A vendor proposes a contract amendment that benefits them (longer payment terms) but offers no obvious benefit to the buyer. The contract permits amendments by mutual agreement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the amendment to maintain a positive vendor relationship and ongoing goodwill on the programme.
- **B**: Reject the amendment outright since it benefits only the vendor and offers nothing to the buyer organisation.
- **C**: Refer the request to legal counsel for a formal interpretation of the contract's amendments clause before responding.
- **D**: Evaluate the project impact, negotiate concessions of comparable value, and proceed only if the exchange is fair.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Contract amendments should produce balanced value for both parties. The PM should treat the request as a negotiation opening and seek something of comparable value before agreeing. (A) gives value away unilaterally. (B) refuses without exploring trade-offs that could benefit both sides. (C) is unnecessary for a straightforward commercial negotiation; legal involvement comes later, if at all.
