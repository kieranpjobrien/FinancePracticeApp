# Question

A project requires specialised equipment. Three vendors submit proposals: Vendor A offers lowest price, Vendor B offers best quality, Vendor C offers fastest delivery. The project has a tight deadline but quality is critical for regulatory compliance. Which selection approach should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Choose Vendor A to minimise cost
- **B**: Use weighted criteria scoring to evaluate all factors based on project priorities
- **C**: Choose Vendor B since quality is always most important
- **D**: Choose the vendor the organisation has used before

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Complex procurement decisions with multiple factors require a structured evaluation approach. Weighted criteria scoring allows the team to define weights based on project priorities (quality high for compliance, delivery high for deadline, cost as constraint), then score each vendor objectively. This produces defensible, traceable decisions.

Option A (lowest price) ignores quality and delivery factors that the question identifies as critical.

Option C (quality always wins) doesn't account for the tight deadline constraint.

Option D (past relationship) isn't a systematic evaluation method and may not serve this project's specific needs.
