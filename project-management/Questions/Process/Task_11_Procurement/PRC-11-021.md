# Question

In a Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF) contract, what motivates the seller to control costs?

## Options

- **A**: Fear of contract termination
- **B**: The sharing ratio that gives the seller a portion of cost savings
- **C**: Fixed profit regardless of costs
- **D**: Buyer auditing of all expenses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. In CPIF contracts, the seller and buyer share cost savings and overruns according to a pre-agreed ratio. If the seller controls costs below target, they receive a portion of the savings as additional fee. If costs exceed target, they share in the overrun. This aligns seller incentives with buyer interests.

Option A is a negative motivation, not the contract's incentive mechanism.

Option C describes CPFF, not CPIF.

Option D is a control mechanism, not an incentive.
