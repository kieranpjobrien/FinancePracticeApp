# Question

Under a time and materials contract for a hybrid platform build, the vendor is billing thirty percent more hours each week than forecast but delivering on schedule. The PM has not been reviewing weekly timesheets in detail. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue paying as invoiced because the vendor is meeting the agreed delivery milestones despite extra hours.
- **B**: Convert the contract to firm fixed price immediately so the overrun problem is contained by the buyer.
- **C**: Institute weekly hour reviews, compare against the work performed, and challenge unjustified billings promptly.
- **D**: Pay the current invoices and schedule a contract renegotiation for six months from now to reset the commercial terms.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

T&M contracts require active hour oversight by the buyer; without review, cost overrun is structural to the contract type. (A) surrenders cost control that T&M specifically demands. (B) unilaterally changes contract type without negotiation. (D) delays oversight by six months, during which overrun continues to compound unchecked.
