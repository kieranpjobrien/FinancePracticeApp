# Question

Mid-delivery, a vendor on a fixed-price contract submits a change order claiming work was implied by the SOW but not explicitly stated. They estimate the additional work at $40,000. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change quickly to keep the vendor relationship strong and avoid formal dispute resolution.
- **B**: Reject the change outright because all defined-scope risk under FFP belongs to the seller by design.
- **C**: Review the SOW to determine whether the work was genuinely implied, then negotiate based on the finding.
- **D**: Terminate the contract for cause and re-procure the remaining work with a clearer, more complete SOW.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Disputed claims need analysis, not a reflexive answer. Reviewing the SOW reveals whether the work was genuinely implied (negotiation territory) or clearly outside scope (the seller's risk). (A) sets a precedent for unsupported claims. (B) is reflexive and unfair if the SOW was genuinely ambiguous. (D) is disproportionate and expensive for a scope dispute that can typically be resolved through negotiation.
