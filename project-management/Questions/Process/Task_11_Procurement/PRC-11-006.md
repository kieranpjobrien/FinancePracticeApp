# Question

A vendor on a fixed-price contract requests a change order for additional work they claim was implied but not explicitly stated in the contract. The work would add significant value to the deliverable. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change to maintain a good vendor relationship
- **B**: Reject the change since it's the vendor's responsibility under fixed-price terms
- **C**: Review the contract scope, assess whether the work was truly implied, and negotiate based on findings
- **D**: Terminate the contract and find a new vendor

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Contract disputes about scope require analysis, not reflexive responses. The PM should review the statement of work and contract terms to determine if the work was genuinely ambiguous or clearly outside scope. Based on this analysis, the PM can negotiate fairly - approving if the work was implied, or clarifying that it's a scope addition requiring formal change.

Option A (approve immediately) may accept invalid claims and set a precedent for future requests.

Option B (reject reflexively) may be unfair if the contract was genuinely ambiguous. It also damages the relationship and may lead to disputes.

Option D (terminate) is disproportionate for a scope dispute that can likely be negotiated.
