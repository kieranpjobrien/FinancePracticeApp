# Question

Under a cost plus incentive fee contract with an 80/20 sharing ratio, the vendor is trending twenty percent over target cost at the midpoint. The vendor says they expect to finish close to target. The PM reviews their own independent cost estimate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's assurance for now and re-assess at the end of the next quarter when more data exists.
- **B**: Invoke the contract's termination clause for cost overrun to protect the buyer from further financial exposure.
- **C**: Warn the vendor that the sharing ratio means they will lose money if they cannot return to target cost.
- **D**: Run an independent estimate to completion, compare with the vendor's forecast, and discuss the variance openly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Independent estimate to completion is standard on cost-reimbursable contracts for early divergence detection; open discussion of variance drives course correction. (A) relies solely on the vendor's forecast without independent data. (B) terminates based on a midpoint trend without analysis. (C) threatens without data or a proposed corrective path.
