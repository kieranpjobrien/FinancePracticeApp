# Question

During a procurement, a vendor's proposal includes a significant exception to a contract term that allocates risk to the buyer. The exception wasn't disclosed in the proposal's executive summary. What should the PM ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the proposal since it doesn't comply with requirements
- **B**: Ensure evaluators carefully review all proposal sections, not just summaries
- **C**: Accept the exception if the proposal is otherwise superior
- **D**: Ask the vendor to remove the exception before evaluation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This highlights the importance of thorough proposal review. Exceptions can be buried in technical or legal sections. Evaluators should review complete proposals, and evaluation processes should specifically look for exceptions and non-compliances that affect risk allocation and contract terms.

Option A may be appropriate but requires evaluation first.

Option C accepts risk without proper assessment.

Option D changes the proposal before evaluation, which may be improper in competitive procurements.
