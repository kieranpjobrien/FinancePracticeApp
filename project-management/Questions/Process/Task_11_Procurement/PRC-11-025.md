# Question

The leading proposal contains a buried exception in the legal section that shifts a major risk from the seller to the buyer; the executive summary makes no mention of it. The team almost missed it. What is the most important corrective action for the PM to take?

## Options

- **A**: Direct evaluators to check every section of every proposal for exceptions and add it as a formal evaluation step.
- **B**: Disqualify the leading proposal outright because it failed to disclose the exception clearly in the executive summary.
- **C**: Accept the exception because the proposal is otherwise the strongest on weighted score and technical fit.
- **D**: Ask the leading vendor to remove the exception so the proposal can be evaluated cleanly against the others.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The systemic issue is that the evaluation process did not surface a material risk-shifting clause; that is the gap to fix and the right next step. (B) penalises the vendor for relying on a permissible disclosure pattern; the gap is in the buyer's evaluation, not the proposal format. (C) accepts a risk shift without analysis. (D) modifies a competing proposal mid-evaluation, which can taint the procurement.
