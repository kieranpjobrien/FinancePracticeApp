# Question

A predictive project has completed the design phase. The PM discovers that requirements gathered earlier have become outdated due to regulatory changes. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the original requirements to stay on schedule
- **B**: Initiate change control to update requirements, assess impact on scope/schedule/cost, and get approval before proceeding
- **C**: Wait until the end of the project to address regulatory compliance
- **D**: Ignore the changes as they occurred after design freeze

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Regulatory changes must be addressed through proper change control. Document the change, assess its impact on the project baselines, and get approval from the appropriate authority (CCB, sponsor). Proceeding with outdated requirements risks delivering a non-compliant product.

Option A delivers a potentially non-compliant product.

Option C risks significant rework and potential legal issues.

Option D ignores real project constraints.
