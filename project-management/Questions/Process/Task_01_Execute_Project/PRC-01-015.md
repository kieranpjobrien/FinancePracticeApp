# Question

Six months into a 12-month product build, a major competitor releases a substitute that has commoditised the market. The PM's analysis shows the original benefits case is no longer achievable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue executing — the team has already invested significant effort and stopping wastes the work done so far.
- **B**: Quietly redirect the team toward an adjacent feature set in the hope the original case can be salvaged later.
- **C**: Bring the updated benefits analysis to the sponsor with options to terminate, pivot, or de-scope, and recommend a path.
- **D**: Continue at current pace but reduce status reporting to avoid alarming the steering committee.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When the business case fails, the PM owes leadership transparent analysis and a recommendation — not a quiet continuation. (C) is the PMI-aligned response: surface facts, present options, recommend. (A) is the sunk cost fallacy. (B) is unilateral scope change without sponsor authority and erodes trust. (D) is unethical concealment and a clear breach of the PMI Code of Ethics.
