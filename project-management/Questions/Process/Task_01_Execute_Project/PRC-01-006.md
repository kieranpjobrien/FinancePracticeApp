# Question

In sprint planning, the product owner ranks a small reporting tweak above a complex customer-facing onboarding flow because the tweak fits in one sprint. The onboarding flow is widely understood to drive new revenue. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the PO on value-based ordering and ask if the onboarding flow can be split into thin vertical slices so high-value work starts now.
- **B**: Defer to the PO's prioritisation authority and let the team commit to the reporting tweak.
- **C**: Privately raise the concern with the head of product so the prioritisation can be reviewed outside the team.
- **D**: Propose that the team work on both items in parallel to satisfy the PO and capture the revenue opportunity.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Scrum Master coaches the PO without overriding them. (A) addresses the underlying issue — confusing "small" with "high value" — and offers a concrete technique (story slicing) that often resolves the tension. (B) avoids the coaching responsibility; the PO owns the backlog but the SM owns process health. (C) goes around the PO and damages trust without first attempting direct coaching. (D) promises capacity the team likely doesn't have and dodges the prioritisation conversation rather than resolving it.
