# Question

A project team is using Scrum. The product owner prioritises a technically simple feature over a complex one that provides more business value, because the simple feature can be completed in one sprint. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Support the product owner's decision as they own the backlog
- **B**: Override the product owner and prioritise the high-value feature
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion about value delivery and help the PO understand the trade-offs
- **D**: Split the complex feature into smaller pieces that fit in one sprint

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The Scrum Master's role includes helping the team and PO make informed decisions. If the PO is prioritising ease over value, a facilitated discussion can highlight this trade-off. The PO may have valid reasons, or may reconsider after understanding the value implications.

Option A (just support) abdicates the SM's responsibility to coach and challenge. The SM should question decisions that may not serve the product well.

Option B (override) violates the PO's authority. The SM doesn't make prioritisation decisions - they facilitate better decision-making.

Option D (split feature) may be appropriate but doesn't address the underlying prioritisation question. The PO should make an informed value decision, then the team can discuss implementation approaches.
