# Question

The team's project depends on integration with a partner system whose team releases monthly. Your team runs two-week sprints. How should the PM manage the integration dependency?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate a service-level agreement that requires the partner team to align with your sprint cadence.
- **B**: Plan integration touchpoints around the partner's monthly cycle, build against mocked interfaces meanwhile, and agree contracts early.
- **C**: Defer all integration work to the final sprint before release so it happens in one consolidated effort.
- **D**: Have the partner team provide a dedicated engineer to your team for the duration of the project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

External cadences must be respected; the dependency is managed by adapting your own work, not by demanding others change. (B) does both — interface mocks let internal work continue, and early contract agreement reduces integration surprises. (A) damages the partnership and is rarely successful. (C) concentrates integration risk at the worst possible time. (D) is a heavy ask the partner is unlikely to agree to and doesn't address cadence misalignment.
