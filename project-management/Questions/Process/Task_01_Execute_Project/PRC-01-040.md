# Question

Production users start hitting issues with a feature deployed in the previous sprint. The product owner wants the team focused on the next planned feature. The support manager wants the team to drop everything and fix the production issues. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the PO's prioritisation since they own the backlog.
- **B**: Drop the new feature work and put the entire team on production support until the issues are resolved.
- **C**: Bring the PO and support manager together to agree severity-based criteria for production work, and adjust the sprint plan accordingly.
- **D**: Have the team work on production issues outside sprint hours so neither workstream is disrupted.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This is a value-prioritisation conflict between two legitimate stakeholders, and the PM's role is to facilitate a shared decision based on impact, not to pick a side. (C) builds the criteria once, so future incidents resolve themselves quickly. (A) treats the PO as the only voice and ignores customer impact. (B) overreacts without severity context. (D) burns the team and disguises what is really a capacity-allocation problem.
