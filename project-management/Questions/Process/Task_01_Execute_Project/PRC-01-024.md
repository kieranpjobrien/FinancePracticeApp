# Question

A waterfall project is in system testing. The defect log is climbing and most defects trace back to ambiguous requirements. The go-live date is in three weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Triage defects by severity, agree with the business which can be deferred to a post-go-live release, and document accepted residual risk.
- **B**: Push the go-live by four weeks so all defects can be properly fixed before release.
- **C**: Direct the test team to close low-severity defects without retest to focus capacity on critical issues.
- **D**: Reopen requirements with the business analysts and rework the affected modules from the design stage.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Triage with the business is the standard PMI response when quality issues meet a deadline — protect what's needed for go-live, defer the rest with formal acceptance. (A) does this. (B) jumps to a schedule slip without first checking whether scope or quality decisions can save the date. (C) compromises quality and audit trail without business agreement. (D) treats the symptom (defects) by going back to requirements, which is the right long-term fix but not the first action with three weeks to go.
