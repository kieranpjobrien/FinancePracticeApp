# Question

What does "Minimum Viable Product" (MVP) mean in project delivery?

## Options

- **A**: The lowest quality product that can be sold
- **B**: A product with the minimum features needed to be usable and provide value to early customers while enabling learning
- **C**: A product that meets minimum regulatory requirements
- **D**: The cheapest possible product to build

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. An MVP includes just enough features to be usable by early customers and provide real value, while enabling the team to learn from actual usage. It's about delivering value early and iterating based on feedback, not about low quality or minimal cost.

Option A confuses MVP with low quality - MVP should still be well-built.

Option C relates to compliance, not MVP concept.

Option D focuses on cost rather than value and learning.
