# Question

A hybrid infrastructure programme is three months from go-live. The sponsor asks the PM to compress the final two milestones into one by removing the pilot phase entirely, citing cost pressure. The PM's own analysis says the pilot is the only way to catch integration defects early. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to the compression since the sponsor has final authority on scope and funding for the programme.
- **B**: Run a structured impact analysis showing defect-discovery risk and cost of rework, then present alternatives to the sponsor.
- **C**: Comply with the directive but document dissent formally in the project log so the record protects the team later.
- **D**: Escalate immediately to the steering committee because the sponsor's direction introduces unacceptable delivery and regulatory risk to the programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to inform sponsor decisions with data before accepting or escalating. (B) gives the sponsor the defect-cost picture and alternatives, which is the PM's accountability. (A) is compliance without stewardship and ignores delivery risk. (C) logging dissent while complying is a CYA move, not project leadership. (D) jumping to steering before engaging the sponsor skips a level and damages trust.
