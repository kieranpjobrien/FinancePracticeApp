# Question

Mid-sprint, a developer realises a story is significantly larger than estimated and won't be completed before sprint end. What should the team do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team work additional hours across the sprint to honour the original story commitment to the PO.
- **B**: Mark the story complete and capture the remaining work as a defect to be fixed in the following sprint.
- **C**: Discuss with the PO, split the story so the deliverable portion completes, and return the rest to the backlog.
- **D**: Cancel the sprint immediately and reset planning from scratch using the corrected story estimate as the basis.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Discovering a story is too big is normal; the response is to slice and recommit, not to compromise quality or the sprint. (C) preserves the definition of done and surfaces the change to the PO so they can re-prioritise. (A) burns the team out and rarely solves estimation errors. (B) violates DoD and creates phantom progress. (D) is an extreme remedy reserved for sprints that have lost their goal entirely, not a single mis-sized story.
