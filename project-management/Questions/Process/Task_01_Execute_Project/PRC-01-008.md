# Question

A project team using Kanban notices their cycle time has doubled over the past month. Work items are taking twice as long to move from "In Progress" to "Done". The team's WIP limits haven't changed. What should the project manager investigate FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Whether the team needs more resources
- **B**: Whether work items have become more complex or blockers are accumulating
- **C**: Whether the WIP limits should be increased
- **D**: Whether the team is not working hard enough

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Increased cycle time with unchanged WIP limits suggests either work complexity has increased or items are being blocked. The PM should investigate the root cause - are items waiting on dependencies, reviews, or external inputs? Are stories larger or more complex? Understanding the cause informs the correct response.

Option A (more resources) may not help if the issue is blockers or dependencies. Adding people to a blocked system doesn't unblock it.

Option C (increase WIP) would likely make things worse. Higher WIP typically increases cycle time further due to context switching and queue buildup.

Option D (blame the team) is not a professional response and doesn't investigate the actual cause of the slowdown.
