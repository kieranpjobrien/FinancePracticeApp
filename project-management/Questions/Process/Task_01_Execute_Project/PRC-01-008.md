# Question

A Kanban team's average cycle time has doubled over the past month. WIP limits are unchanged, the team composition is stable, and there are no obvious environmental issues. What should the PM examine FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Increase WIP limits temporarily to clear the queue and restore throughput.
- **B**: Run a focused review of in-flight items to see whether work has grown larger or whether items are sitting blocked between columns.
- **C**: Add a developer to the team to absorb the apparent extra load.
- **D**: Replace the Kanban board with a sprint-based cadence to enforce timeboxing.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Doubled cycle time at unchanged WIP almost always points to either bigger items or hidden blockers — both visible in a quick board review. (B) is the cheapest, fastest diagnostic and reflects PMI's "address root cause" thinking. (A) raises WIP, which queueing theory predicts will worsen cycle time, not improve it. (C) commits a costly resourcing change before the cause is even known. (D) abandons a working method based on one data point and doesn't address why items are slow.
