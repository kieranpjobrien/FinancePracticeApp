# Question

What does "crashing" mean as a schedule compression technique?

## Options

- **A**: Adding additional resources to critical path activities to reduce their duration, typically at increased cost.
- **B**: Performing previously sequential activities in parallel to shorten the critical path.
- **C**: Reducing project scope by eliminating low-priority deliverables from the schedule.
- **D**: Cancelling activities that have not yet started so dependent activities can begin earlier.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Crashing trades cost for time on the critical path — usually by adding people, paying for overtime, or expediting procurement. (A) is the standard PMBOK definition. (B) describes fast-tracking, a different compression technique. (C) is scope reduction, not compression. (D) is not a recognised technique; cancelling activities removes scope rather than compressing the schedule.
