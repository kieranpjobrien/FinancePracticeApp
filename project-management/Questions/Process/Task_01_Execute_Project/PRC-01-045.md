# Question

What does "crashing" mean in schedule compression?

## Options

- **A**: The project failing
- **B**: Adding resources to critical path activities to reduce duration, typically at increased cost
- **C**: Removing team members
- **D**: Cancelling the project

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Crashing involves adding resources (people, equipment, overtime) to critical path activities to reduce their duration. It typically increases cost - you're trading money for time. Crashing should be applied to critical path tasks with the best cost/time trade-off. Not all tasks can be effectively crashed.

Options A, C, and D misinterpret the term - "crashing" is a technique, not a failure.
