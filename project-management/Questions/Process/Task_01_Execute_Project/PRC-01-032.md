# Question

During execution, the team identifies a more efficient technical approach that would require refactoring existing code but would speed up all future development. The product owner is resistant to "rework." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the PO and continue with the current approach
- **B**: Present the business case for the refactoring - quantify the time savings over remaining development and frame it as an investment with measurable return
- **C**: Have the team refactor secretly
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to override the PO

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Technical improvements need business justification to get PO buy-in. Quantify: how much time will refactoring take? How much time will it save per sprint over remaining development? Calculate the ROI. Frame it as investment, not rework. If the numbers support it, most POs will agree once they understand the value proposition.

Option A accepts slower velocity without exploring the opportunity.

Option C lacks transparency and doesn't involve the PO in decisions.

Option D escalates when persuasion may be sufficient.
