# Question

The development team proposes a refactor that would slow the next two sprints but materially speed every sprint thereafter. The product owner refuses, calling the refactor "rework that doesn't ship features." What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the PO; the PO owns the backlog and refactor decisions are theirs.
- **B**: Help the team build a quantified case — projected velocity uplift, payback sprints, and what features become possible — and facilitate a conversation with the PO using that data.
- **C**: Escalate to engineering leadership to override the PO and get the refactor approved on technical grounds.
- **D**: Encourage the team to absorb the refactor into normal feature work and stop discussing it as a separate item.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

POs prioritise on value, but value-of-refactor is invisible without numbers. (B) is the SM's coaching role — equip the team to make the business case. (A) abandons the team. (C) bypasses the PO and damages the working relationship. (D) is the same as the original problem with less transparency; hidden refactor inflates story estimates and erodes trust.
