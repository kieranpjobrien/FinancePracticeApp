# Question

A business sponsor keeps asking the delivery lead for "small tweaks" directly, bypassing the PM. Over six weeks these tweaks have absorbed roughly 15% of team capacity and aren't reflected in the baseline. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the pattern with the sponsor, quantify the capacity impact, and route future changes through the change-control process.
- **B**: Tell the delivery lead to stop taking direct requests from the sponsor under any circumstances going forward.
- **C**: Absorb the tweaks into the baseline retroactively since the sponsor clearly considers them in-scope project work.
- **D**: Escalate to the programme board so governance is visibly reinforced and the sponsor is reminded of process.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to engage the sponsor first with data and a proposed path, not to police the team or skip levels. (A) addresses root cause and restores control. (B) pushes the problem onto the delivery lead and doesn't engage the actual driver of behaviour. (C) silently legitimises uncontrolled change and damages baseline integrity. (D) premature escalation bypasses the sponsor conversation and damages trust.
