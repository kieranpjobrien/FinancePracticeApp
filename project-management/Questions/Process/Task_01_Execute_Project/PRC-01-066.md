# Question

A healthcare platform team is mid-release when a regulator announces new data-handling rules effective in six weeks. The rules affect three features already built. The PO wants the team to keep shipping while the legal team interprets the rules. What should the PM advocate?

## Options

- **A**: Pause new feature work on the three affected features, engage legal and compliance jointly, and replan remaining release scope accordingly.
- **B**: Keep the team shipping and carve out a separate response stream once legal publishes their formal interpretation of the new rules.
- **C**: Ask the PO to deprioritise the three affected features until the regulator issues detailed implementation guidance on each requirement.
- **D**: Commission a separate compliance taskforce to handle the new rules so the current team's delivery is fully unaffected.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Regulatory change affecting already-built features is a project-wide concern that needs coordinated PM action, not a parallel workaround. (A) integrates the response into the plan properly. (B) shipping while interpretation is pending risks compliance exposure on recent work. (C) deprioritising alone doesn't engage the legal input needed to decide what to change. (D) a separate taskforce ignores that the delivery team owns the affected code.
