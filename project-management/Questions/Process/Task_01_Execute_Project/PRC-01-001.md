# Question

A Scrum team is two sprints away from a launch when the product owner learns a competitor will release a similar feature in three weeks. The PO asks the team to skip user acceptance testing on the next two sprints to ship sooner. The team is uncomfortable with the request. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session between the PO and team to identify which testing activities can be parallelised, automated, or trimmed without compromising the definition of done.
- **B**: Support the PO's decision since they own scope and release timing in Scrum.
- **C**: Escalate the trade-off to the steering committee so leadership can decide whether quality or speed matters more.
- **D**: Decline the request and reassure the team that the definition of done cannot be changed mid-release.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's role is to facilitate, not decide unilaterally or block legitimate business pressure. (A) brings the right people together to find a compromise that respects both competitive urgency and the team's professional standards — this is servant leadership in action. (B) abdicates the SM's coaching duty; the PO doesn't override the definition of done. (C) escalates a problem the team can solve at its level. (D) is correct that DoD shouldn't be casually changed, but flatly refusing without exploring options doesn't help the business outcome the PO is trying to protect.
