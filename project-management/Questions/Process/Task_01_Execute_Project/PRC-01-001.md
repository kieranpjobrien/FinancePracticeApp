# Question

An agile team is working on a product with a competitor launching similar features. The product owner wants to accelerate delivery by reducing testing. The team is concerned about quality. What should the Scrum Master recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Support reduced testing to meet competitive pressure
- **B**: Maintain full testing and accept that competitors may launch first
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion to find ways to accelerate delivery while maintaining adequate quality, such as automated testing, scope reduction, or focused feature prioritization
- **D**: Escalate the decision to senior leadership

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This addresses both the urgency (competitive pressure) and quality concerns by exploring options that don't force an either/or choice. Automation can speed testing; scope reduction focuses effort on the most competitive features; better prioritization delivers value faster. The team should solve this together.

Option A sacrifices quality for speed, which often creates technical debt and defects that slow future development. The short-term gain may cause long-term pain.

Option B ignores legitimate business urgency. Competitive timing can be critical to market success. The team should try to find solutions, not just accept constraints.

Option D abdicates the team's problem-solving responsibility. The team and product owner should attempt resolution before escalating.
