# Question

A project team is consistently delivering features, but stakeholders express frustration that the most valuable functionality keeps getting delayed while less important features are completed. What should the project manager investigate?

## Options

- **A**: Whether the team is working efficiently
- **B**: Whether product backlog prioritization truly reflects business value and whether the team is working on the highest-priority items
- **C**: Whether stakeholders are communicating their priorities clearly
- **D**: Whether the team has the skills for the valuable features

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The symptoms suggest a prioritization problem - valuable features aren't being worked on first. This could mean the backlog isn't ordered by value, or the team is selecting work based on something other than priority (perhaps choosing easier items). Investigating the gap between stated priority and actual execution focuses on the root cause.

Option A focuses on efficiency when the issue is prioritization - working on wrong things efficiently still delivers wrong things.

Option C blames stakeholders when the issue may be internal process.

Option D assumes skill gaps, but the team is delivering features - they may have the skills but aren't applying them to the right work.
