# Question

A regulatory project has a fixed compliance deadline 12 weeks away. Halfway through, the PM realises the original scope cannot be completed in time without major quality compromises. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a change request to extend the project schedule baseline and notify the regulator of the slip.
- **B**: Crash the schedule by adding contractors to the most behind workstreams to recover the original plan.
- **C**: Convene the sponsor and compliance lead to identify the minimum scope required to meet the regulation, and replan around that subset.
- **D**: Accept the risk of partial delivery and document it in the risk register for the next steering committee.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A regulatory deadline is rarely negotiable, so schedule and quality are fixed — scope is the variable lever. (C) follows PMI's approach of working with the right stakeholders to identify the minimum compliant scope and replanning. (A) treats the deadline as flexible when it likely isn't, and notifying the regulator should follow internal alignment, not precede it. (B) risks Brooks' law and addresses symptoms; if the original estimate was significantly off, more bodies won't close the gap. (D) is passive — logging the risk doesn't act on it, and the PM owes leadership a recommended path forward, not just a problem statement.
