# Question

A project to implement a new regulatory requirement has a fixed compliance deadline. Midway through, the team realizes that the original scope cannot be completed by the deadline. What approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Request a deadline extension from the regulatory body
- **B**: Add resources to accelerate work and meet the original scope and deadline
- **C**: Work with stakeholders to identify the minimum viable scope for compliance, deliver that by the deadline, and plan follow-on releases for additional features
- **D**: Report the situation and let senior management decide the path forward

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. With a fixed regulatory deadline, scope flexibility is the appropriate lever. Working with stakeholders to identify minimum compliance needs ensures the organization avoids regulatory penalties. Planning follow-on releases captures remaining scope without risking the deadline. This demonstrates agile thinking applied to a constraint situation.

Option A is often not possible for regulatory deadlines - these are typically non-negotiable.

Option B may not work due to Brooks' Law - adding people late to a project often slows it down due to onboarding overhead. Also, if the estimate was significantly off, more resources may not close the gap.

Option D is passive and delays action. The PM should come with analysis and recommendations, not just problems.
