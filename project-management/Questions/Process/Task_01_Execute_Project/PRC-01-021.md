# Question

Which of the following best describes the product backlog?

## Options

- **A**: A static list of all approved requirements signed off at project initiation.
- **B**: An ordered, evolving list of everything that might be needed in the product, owned by the product owner.
- **C**: A record of features completed each sprint, used for status reporting to executives.
- **D**: A shared library of project documents, including the WBS, schedule, and risk register.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The product backlog is dynamic, ordered, single-source, and owned by the PO — it represents the current best understanding of what the product needs. (B) captures these properties. (A) describes a frozen requirements document, which is the antithesis of a backlog. (C) describes a release notes or done-list — completion record, not the work-to-do. (D) confuses the backlog with the project's document repository.
