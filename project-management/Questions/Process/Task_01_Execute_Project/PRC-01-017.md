# Question

In agile delivery, what is the purpose of timeboxing?

## Options

- **A**: To limit how long team members can work each day
- **B**: To create fixed time periods that drive focus, forcing prioritisation and preventing scope creep
- **C**: To extend deadlines when needed
- **D**: To schedule vacation time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Timeboxing creates fixed time periods (sprints, iterations) that cannot be extended. This forces prioritisation - only the most valuable work fits in the timebox. It prevents scope creep, drives focus, and creates regular delivery cadence. Time is fixed; scope flexes within it.

Option A relates to work hours, not timeboxing concept.

Option C is the opposite - timeboxes are fixed, not extended.

Option D is unrelated to the timeboxing concept.
