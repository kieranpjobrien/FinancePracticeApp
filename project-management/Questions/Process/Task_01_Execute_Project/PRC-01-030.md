# Question

The project sponsor wants a detailed weekly status report with extensive metrics. The agile team complains the prep work is consuming a meaningful share of their sprint capacity. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce the reports the sponsor requested in full so the relationship isn't damaged, and accept the velocity hit.
- **B**: Skip the report once or twice and see whether the sponsor follows up; if they don't, scale it back permanently.
- **C**: Meet with the sponsor to confirm what decisions the report supports, then automate the underlying data and trim the manual narrative to what's actually used.
- **D**: Delegate report production entirely to a junior project coordinator so the team's capacity is fully protected.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PMI move on reporting overhead is to clarify the decision the report supports, then minimise effort to what serves that decision. (C) does both — it respects the sponsor and reduces the cost. (A) accepts unnecessary capacity loss. (B) is passive-aggressive and erodes trust. (D) treats the symptom (effort) without checking that the report content is even right.
