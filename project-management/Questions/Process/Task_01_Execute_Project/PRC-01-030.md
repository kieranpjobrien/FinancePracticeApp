# Question

The project sponsor wants weekly status reports with extensive metrics. The agile team feels this reporting overhead reduces their productive time. How should the PM balance these needs?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the sponsor's request and don't provide reports
- **B**: Automate reporting where possible using existing tools, streamline to essential metrics, and discuss with the sponsor what information they actually need vs want
- **C**: Stop the team's work to produce manual reports
- **D**: Provide false data to satisfy requirements quickly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Balance stakeholder information needs with team productivity. Automate: use tools that generate metrics from normal work (burndown from the board, velocity from completed work). Discuss needs: what decisions is the sponsor making with this data? Focus on essential metrics. Often sponsors want less than they ask for once they understand the overhead.

Option A ignores legitimate stakeholder needs.

Option C over-prioritises reporting at expense of delivery.

Option D is unethical and destroys trust when discovered.
