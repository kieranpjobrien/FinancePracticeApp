# Question

A critical-path activity has slipped two weeks. The end date is locked because of a contractually committed market launch. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Crash the critical path immediately by approving overtime for the affected workstream.
- **B**: Notify stakeholders that the launch will move and begin a rebaseline of the schedule.
- **C**: Develop fast-tracking and crashing options with cost and risk impact, and present them to the sponsor for a decision.
- **D**: Submit a change request to descope the most expensive remaining feature so float can be recovered.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The deadline is fixed, so the PM must recover schedule — but the choice between fast-tracking, crashing, and descoping has very different cost and risk profiles, and that's a sponsor-level decision. (C) prepares the analysis and brings options. (A) commits to a specific recovery technique without analysis. (B) gives up on the contractual deadline before recovery options are explored. (D) jumps to a single-lever solution without cost/benefit comparison to other options.
