# Question

A new product is being built to compete with a known competitor release in six months. The full proposed feature set requires twelve months to deliver. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend a six-month MVP scope focused on features needed to be competitive at launch, with planned increments afterwards.
- **B**: Recommend approving the full twelve-month plan and accept that the competitor will reach the market first by some months.
- **C**: Recommend doubling team size for six months to crash the schedule and ship the full proposed scope by launch.
- **D**: Recommend cancelling the project altogether and reallocating the budget to a different strategic opportunity for the business.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When time-to-market is the binding constraint, MVP-then-iterate is the established pattern. (A) protects the launch window without abandoning the longer-term scope. (B) cedes the market without a fight. (C) is Brooks' law in action — doubling team size rarely halves time, especially over only six months. (D) is too drastic without first evaluating whether scope flexibility could solve the problem.
