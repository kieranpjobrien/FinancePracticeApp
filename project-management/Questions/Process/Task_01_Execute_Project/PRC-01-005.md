# Question

The PM observes that two senior developers have spent most of the sprint polishing a payment screen that already meets the acceptance criteria, while three other accepted stories sit untouched in the backlog. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the developers continue, since their refinement reflects strong professional standards.
- **B**: Raise the observation in the next daily stand-up and ask the team how they want to handle work that meets DoD versus stories still in the queue.
- **C**: Reassign one of the developers to a different story and brief the team lead on the change.
- **D**: Submit a change request to formally remove the polish work from the sprint scope.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gold-plating is real, but the PM doesn't unilaterally redirect a self-organising team. (B) surfaces the trade-off through the team's existing ceremony, respecting team self-management while addressing the value problem. (A) tolerates gold-plating that delays other accepted commitments. (C) bypasses the team's authority over their own work allocation and may damage trust. (D) misuses change control — once acceptance criteria are met, additional polishing isn't a scope change, it's a capacity choice the team should make together.
