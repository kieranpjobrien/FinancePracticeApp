# Question

Technical debt has reached the point that new feature delivery has slowed visibly over the past three sprints. The product owner wants every sprint dedicated to feature work because of competitive pressure. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Allocate 100% of the next two sprints to debt repayment to reset the codebase before resuming features.
- **B**: Quantify the velocity loss attributable to debt, present the cost-benefit to the PO, and propose a steady allocation (for example 20%) for debt reduction each sprint.
- **C**: Have engineers refactor opportunistically inside feature stories without telling the PO, since clean code is everyone's responsibility.
- **D**: Continue feature-only delivery and revisit the trade-off after the next major release.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tech debt is a business decision; the PO needs evidence to make it well, and a steady drip avoids the disruption of a full feature freeze. (B) provides both. (A) is too disruptive and rarely needed. (C) hides effort the PO is responsible for prioritising — that's a transparency violation, even with good intent. (D) accepts a known and worsening problem without surfacing it.
