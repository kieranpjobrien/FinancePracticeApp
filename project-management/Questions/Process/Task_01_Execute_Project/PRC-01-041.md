# Question

What is "fast-tracking" as a schedule compression technique?

## Options

- **A**: Increasing team work hours
- **B**: Performing activities that were planned sequentially in parallel, accepting increased risk
- **C**: Reducing the scope of work
- **D**: Adding more resources to critical tasks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Fast-tracking involves performing activities in parallel that were originally planned to be sequential. This compresses the schedule but increases risk - if the earlier activity has issues, work done in parallel may need to be redone. It's appropriate when activities have minimal real dependencies.

Option A relates to working longer, not differently.

Option C is scope reduction, not schedule compression.

Option D describes crashing, not fast-tracking.
