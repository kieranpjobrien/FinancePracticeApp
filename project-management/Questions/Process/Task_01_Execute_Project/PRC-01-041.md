# Question

What does "fast-tracking" mean as a schedule compression technique?

## Options

- **A**: Approving overtime to deliver work faster within the existing sequence of activities.
- **B**: Adding additional resources to critical path activities to shorten their duration.
- **C**: Performing activities or phases that were planned sequentially in parallel, accepting greater risk of rework.
- **D**: Reducing the project's scope so fewer activities need to be performed before completion.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Fast-tracking specifically means doing in parallel what was planned in series, and the trade-off is increased risk of rework if the upstream activity changes. (C) is the textbook definition. (A) describes overtime, not a technique. (B) describes crashing — adding cost to shorten duration — which is a different compression technique. (D) describes scope reduction, which is also distinct.
