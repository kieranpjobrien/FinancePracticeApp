# Question

An MVP has launched successfully to early customers. The CRO wants to roll it to all 30,000 enterprise customers within four weeks. The architecture was sized for 500 concurrent users. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the four-week rollout to capture momentum; performance issues can be patched as they emerge.
- **B**: Refuse any rollout until the architecture has been redesigned and load-tested for 30,000 users.
- **C**: Propose a phased rollout aligned to architecture milestones — performance hardening, load testing, and graduated cohorts — with explicit go/no-go gates.
- **D**: Decouple the rollout from the project entirely and hand it to the operations team to manage on their own timeline.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM's job is to honour the demand signal while protecting the customer experience that produced it. (C) sequences technical readiness against commercial pressure with clear gates. (A) trades durable success for short-term momentum and risks an outage that damages the very interest the CRO is responding to. (B) is too binary and ignores that partial rollout is feasible. (D) is abdication — the rollout sits squarely in the project's value realisation.
