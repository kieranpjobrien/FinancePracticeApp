# Question

Mid-sprint, a developer demonstrates a feature the PO wants to accept despite a failing automated test covering an edge case. The PO argues the edge case is rare and shipping now captures market window. What should the Scrum Master advise the team?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the story and raise a new backlog item to fix the failing test in a later sprint.
- **B**: Mark the story incomplete against the Definition of Done and keep it open until the failing test passes reliably.
- **C**: Ask the PO to formally waive the Definition of Done for this story and proceed with the release as planned.
- **D**: Refactor the Definition of Done during the next retrospective so this edge case is no longer included.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Definition of Done is the team's agreed quality gate and is not negotiable story-by-story. (B) preserves empirical trust in what "done" means. (A) quietly lowers the bar and creates hidden debt. (C) the PO cannot unilaterally waive the DoD; that is a team agreement. (D) changing the DoD mid-flight to unblock a specific story is gaming the standard, not inspecting and adapting.
