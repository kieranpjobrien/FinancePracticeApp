# Question

A start-up's first MVP has two weeks until launch. The PO wants to add a high-profile feature after seeing a competitor release a similar one. The team estimates the feature would take one full sprint and displace already-committed scope. What should the PM facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Add the competitor-response feature immediately and defer whichever committed items have the lowest delivered value to the next release cycle.
- **B**: Hold the current MVP scope firm, ship as planned, and queue the new feature as the first item in the next release backlog.
- **C**: Bring team, PO, and sponsor together to reassess MVP intent against the competitive shift before committing to either path forward now.
- **D**: Defer the decision to the sponsor since a competitive response feature is a strategic judgement beyond team and PO authority here.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A late MVP scope change driven by external signals is a strategy-level inspect-and-adapt moment; PMI-aligned practice is to make the trade-off explicit before choosing. (C) gets the right voices in the room. (A) assumes the competitor feature is the right response without validation. (B) holds the plan but ignores new market information. (D) abdicates the facilitation role the PM should own in this conversation.
