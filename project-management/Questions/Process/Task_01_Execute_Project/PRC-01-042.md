# Question

A project is 60% complete when the organisation announces a restructuring. Several team members will be reassigned in two months. The project needs four more months to complete as planned. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the restructuring and hope team members stay
- **B**: Reassess the project plan, identify what can be completed with remaining resources, prioritise highest-value items, and communicate revised expectations to stakeholders
- **C**: Cancel the project immediately
- **D**: Demand that team members stay regardless of restructuring

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Resource changes require plan adjustment. Reassess: what's achievable with reduced team in months 3-4? Prioritise: ensure highest-value work is completed while full team is available. Communicate: stakeholders need to understand the impact and revised expectations. Perhaps scope can be adjusted to still deliver core value within new constraints.

Option A ignores a known constraint.

Option C may be premature when partial value can still be delivered.

Option D isn't within PM authority and ignores organisational decisions.
