# Question

A project is 60% complete. The organisation announces a restructure: in two months, three of the team's six engineers will be reassigned. The remaining work, as planned, requires four months of full team capacity. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Replan around the reduced capacity, identify which features can still be delivered, and bring revised scope-and-date options to the sponsor for decision.
- **B**: Approach the resource owner and request the engineers be retained on the project until completion.
- **C**: Crash the next two months by approving overtime so as much as possible is delivered before the team shrinks.
- **D**: Continue executing to the original plan and raise the impact only when the team is actually reduced.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI move is to absorb the constraint, replan, and bring options — not to negotiate against the organisational decision or wait for the cliff. (A) does this. (B) assumes the resource decision is reversible, which it usually isn't, and treats the symptom rather than the consequence. (C) commits to heroics before evaluating whether smarter scoping would suffice. (D) lets a known issue degrade execution and damages credibility when the predictable impact lands.
