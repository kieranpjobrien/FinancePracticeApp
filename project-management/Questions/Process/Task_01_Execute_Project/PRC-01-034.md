# Question

A vendor responsible for a critical-path deliverable has been telling the PM in daily check-ins for two weeks that they're "almost done." The deadline is in three weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the daily check-ins and trust the vendor's professional judgement on completion.
- **B**: Replace daily verbal updates with evidence-based check-ins (working artefacts, test results, completed deliverables) and develop a contingency plan in parallel.
- **C**: Issue a formal default notice to the vendor's account executive to motivate quicker delivery.
- **D**: Accept that the deadline will slip and notify stakeholders the schedule will rebaseline.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Vague vendor reassurances are a red flag — the PM's job is to verify, not to escalate immediately or capitulate. (B) replaces talk with evidence and prepares a fallback. (A) keeps trusting a process that's clearly not producing reliable signals. (C) is heavy-handed and escalates before the PM has confirmed the actual state of work. (D) gives up before doing the diagnostic work.
