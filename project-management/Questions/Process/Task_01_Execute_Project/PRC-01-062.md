# Question

A Scrum team has hit stretch commitments for four consecutive sprints to catch up after early delays. Two team members have taken sick leave this sprint and velocity has dropped sharply. The sponsor is pressing for the original recovery trajectory to be restored. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Commit the team to another stretch sprint to restore the recovery line and protect sponsor confidence in the delivery plan.
- **B**: Recalibrate the forecast using realistic sustainable-pace velocity, share it with the sponsor, and renegotiate the recovery plan jointly.
- **C**: Offer the sponsor a smaller stretch target this sprint as a compromise between sustainable pace and the original recovery trajectory.
- **D**: Bring in contract developers to replace the capacity lost to illness and sustain velocity until the original plan is fully restored.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sustainable pace is a PMI principle; repeated overcommitment eventually produces the collapse being observed here. (B) faces reality with data and renegotiates responsibly. (A) doubles down on the behaviour that caused the problem. (C) partial overcommitment still treats unsustainable pace as a negotiating chip. (D) onboarding contractors mid-crisis rarely adds net capacity quickly and skips addressing the underlying plan mismatch.
