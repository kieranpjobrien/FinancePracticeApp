# Question

A portfolio manager asks the PM to slow down the project to free resources for another initiative. The current project is on track and delivering value. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Immediately slow down without question
- **B**: Present the value being delivered and the impact of slowing down, then support whatever decision is made at the portfolio level
- **C**: Refuse and continue at current pace
- **D**: Speed up to finish before resources are pulled

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Portfolio decisions require visibility into trade-offs. Present the facts: current value delivery, impact of slowing down (delayed benefits, potential resource inefficiency). Support the portfolio manager with information to make a good decision. Ultimately, portfolio-level prioritisation decisions should be made at that level with full information.

Option A doesn't provide decision-makers with necessary information.

Option C doesn't respect portfolio authority.

Option D doesn't address the portfolio manager's request.
