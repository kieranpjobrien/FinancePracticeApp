# Question

The team's velocity has been stable at 40 story points per sprint. The product owner wants to commit to 60 points in the next sprint because a key stakeholder is pressuring for faster delivery. How should the Scrum Master respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to the higher commitment to satisfy the stakeholder
- **B**: Explain that velocity is a measure not a target, committing beyond capacity leads to incomplete work and damaged trust, and help find alternative solutions
- **C**: Tell the stakeholder their request is unreasonable
- **D**: Add more team members immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Velocity reflects actual team capacity based on historical data. Artificially inflating commitments leads to incomplete work, quality issues, and damaged trust when commitments aren't met. Work with PO and stakeholders on alternatives: scope reduction, phased delivery, or process improvements that genuinely increase capacity over time.

Option A sets the team up for failure.

Option C is confrontational and doesn't explore solutions.

Option D won't immediately increase velocity (onboarding takes time).
