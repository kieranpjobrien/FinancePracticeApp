# Question

The team's velocity has been a steady 40 points per sprint for ten sprints. A senior stakeholder is pressuring the product owner to commit to 60 points next sprint. The PO asks the Scrum Master what to do. How should the SM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the PO that velocity reflects sustainable capacity, then facilitate a conversation with the stakeholder about scope or sequencing.
- **B**: Recommend the team stretch to 50 points as a compromise to demonstrate good faith with the senior stakeholder and preserve goodwill.
- **C**: Recommend the PO accept the 60-point commitment and let the team decide which items to drop if they run out of time mid-sprint.
- **D**: Offer to take the conversation away from the PO and handle the stakeholder pressure directly on the product owner's behalf.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Velocity is a measurement, not a lever the PO can pull by negotiation — pretending otherwise damages trust when work isn't completed. (A) coaches the PO and redirects the conversation toward levers that actually exist (scope, sequence, descope). (B) hides the same problem behind a smaller number. (C) loads the team with overcommitment and pushes the cost of failure onto them. (D) takes accountability away from the PO, who owns the relationship with the stakeholder.
