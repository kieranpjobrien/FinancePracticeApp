# Question

A vendor critical to integration testing has slipped their delivery by three weeks on a fixed-price contract. The testing window is also three weeks. The PM's team is idle and the sponsor wants the original go-live held. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Invoke the contract penalty clause and formally demand the vendor compress their remaining work at no extra cost.
- **B**: Move the go-live date out by three weeks to restore the original testing window and protect quality.
- **C**: Repurpose the idle team to begin parallel test-environment preparation and author pre-scripted test cases while awaiting vendor delivery.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor and recommend replacing the vendor with an alternative supplier before any further slippage.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The first move is to preserve optionality by making every hour count; (C) does that without pre-committing to dates or contractual action. (A) a penalty demand is adversarial, slow, and rarely recovers schedule. (B) moving go-live is a last resort, not a first action. (D) replacing a vendor mid-delivery in three weeks will typically make things worse, not better.
