# Question

An agile team's product owner has been unavailable for two sprints due to an unexpected business crisis. Backlog grooming has stalled and the team is running out of well-defined stories. The PO says they can't free any time for another two weeks. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team groom the backlog themselves and ask the PO to approve stories asynchronously via written review comments.
- **B**: Pause delivery for two weeks until the PO returns, using the time for technical debt, learning, and team development work.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to name a short-term delegate PO empowered to prioritise and clarify stories during the current absence.
- **D**: Work from the current refined backlog, flag the grooming gap as a risk, and accept the slowdown as unavoidable reality.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A product owner is a functional role, not a single person; a temporary delegate keeps the value decision-maker role filled. (C) preserves the team's flow without compromising the role. (A) having the team act as PO violates separation of concerns and dilutes value decisions. (B) a two-week pause is a disproportionate response when alternatives exist. (D) accepting the slowdown without asking for a delegate abandons the role that empirical process requires.
