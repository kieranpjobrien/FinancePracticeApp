# Question

A hybrid programme runs predictive workstreams for regulatory artefacts and agile sprints for product features. The regulatory workstream is now blocked waiting on an external authority for at least six weeks. How should the PM keep value flowing?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the agile workstream too, since the programme can't ship without regulatory clearance and idle work risks rework.
- **B**: Reassign the agile team to assist the regulatory workstream while the approval is pending.
- **C**: Continue agile delivery on items independent of the regulatory dependency, document the dependency as a programme-level risk, and maintain regulator communication.
- **D**: Recommend abandoning the hybrid model and converting the entire programme to predictive to simplify governance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Independent workstreams in a hybrid programme are designed precisely so blockers in one don't stall the other. (C) keeps value moving while the dependency is managed. (A) wastes capacity for no gain — the work would still need to be done after the block clears. (B) misallocates skills; agile feature engineers rarely accelerate regulatory approvals. (D) is a structural overreaction to a single dependency event.
