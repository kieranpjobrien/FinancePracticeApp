# Question

The product owner wants to delay a release by two sprints to bundle in additional features. The current increment already meets the agreed MVP and would deliver real value to early customers. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Support the delay; release timing is the PO's call.
- **B**: Quantify the cost of delay (lost revenue, missed feedback, opportunity cost) and bring it to the PO so the trade-off is explicit.
- **C**: Escalate to the steering committee to overrule the PO and force the release.
- **D**: Release the MVP without the PO's sign-off to capture the value.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PO owns release decisions, but the SM is responsible for surfacing data the PO may not be considering. (B) coaches the PO with evidence rather than opinion — exactly the SM's role. (A) is too passive; supporting without challenge isn't coaching. (C) bypasses the PO and damages the working relationship. (D) violates the PO's authority over scope and release; the SM cannot release product unilaterally.
