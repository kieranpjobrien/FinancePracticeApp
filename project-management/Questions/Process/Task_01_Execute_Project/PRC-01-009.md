# Question

A product owner wants to delay a release by two sprints to add more features, even though the current increment meets the minimum viable product requirements and provides value to users. What should the Scrum Master advise?

## Options

- **A**: Support the delay since the product owner owns the backlog
- **B**: Challenge the decision by highlighting the value of early release, user feedback, and the cost of delay
- **C**: Escalate to management to override the product owner
- **D**: Release without the product owner's approval

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Scrum Master should coach the product owner on agile principles - early and frequent delivery enables feedback loops and earlier value realisation. Delaying a viable product to add features risks building the wrong things and delays return on investment. The SM's role is to challenge decisions that may not serve the product well.

Option A (support unconditionally) fails to provide coaching. The PO may not be considering the full cost of delay.

Option C (escalate) undermines the PO's authority. The SM should coach and influence, not go around the PO.

Option D (release without approval) violates the PO's ownership of what gets released. The SM can advocate but not override.
