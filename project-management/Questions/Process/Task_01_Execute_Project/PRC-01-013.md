# Question

Mid-sprint, the product owner asks the team to add a new feature requested by a major customer. The team is already at full sprint capacity. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the request automatically because the sprint backlog is locked once it has been committed by the team.
- **B**: Add the new feature on top of the existing sprint commitment to keep the major customer happy and satisfied.
- **C**: Compare the request's value with in-flight stories and, if higher, swap an equivalent amount out of the sprint.
- **D**: Defer the discussion to the next sprint planning session so the request gets normal prioritisation with the rest.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Sprint scope can change when both team and PO agree it serves the goal — sustainable capacity is the constraint, not commitment rigidity. (C) keeps capacity constant and forces an honest value comparison, which is the Scrum-aligned response. (A) is too rigid and ignores legitimate value. (B) silently overcommits the team and threatens the sprint goal. (D) may be appropriate for low-urgency items but ignores that this customer is signalling real urgency the team can address now.
