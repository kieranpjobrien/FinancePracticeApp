# Question

What is "crashing" as a schedule compression technique?

## Options

- **A**: Removing activities from the critical path
- **B**: Adding resources to critical path activities to reduce duration, typically at increased cost
- **C**: Working overtime without additional pay
- **D**: Reducing the quality of deliverables to save time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Crashing involves adding resources (people, equipment, overtime) to critical path activities to shorten their duration. This typically increases cost but reduces schedule. It's a trade-off of cost for time.

Option A describes scope reduction, not crashing.

Option C describes unpaid overtime, which is an ethical issue, not a legitimate technique.

Option D describes quality reduction, which is not a proper project management technique.
