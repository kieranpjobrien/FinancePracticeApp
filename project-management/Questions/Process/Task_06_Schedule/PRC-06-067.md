# Question

Your organisation's predictive projects consistently finish 25 percent later than their original schedules. A review shows estimators historically ignore historical data and use gut feel. The next project's estimators are proposing the same approach. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the estimators proceed with their usual method and add a 25 percent contingency to the resulting schedule estimate.
- **B**: Replace the estimators with estimators from another team that has a better track record on schedule performance.
- **C**: Require the team to use historical data and analogous or parametric techniques, with gut feel as a supplementary cross-check.
- **D**: Escalate the estimating problem to the PMO for organisation-wide process reform before the current estimates are finalised.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Estimating should be anchored in historical data through analogous or parametric techniques; gut feel supplements but does not replace them. (C) addresses the root cause in this project. (A) bakes in known bias. (B) is heavy-handed and avoids coaching. (D) is valid long-term but blocks the current project on a systemic fix.
