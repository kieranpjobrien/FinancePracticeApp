# Question

A team estimates an activity as 8 days optimistic, 14 days most likely, and 26 days pessimistic. Using a triangular distribution and the PERT (beta) formula, what are the expected durations?

## Options

- **A**: Triangular 14 days, PERT 15 days, since PERT weights the most-likely value four times the other two estimates.
- **B**: Triangular 16 days, PERT 15 days, reflecting simple average for triangular and PERT's weighted beta-distribution formula.
- **C**: Triangular 14 days, PERT 16 days, where triangular uses the mode and PERT is the weighted three-point average.
- **D**: Triangular 16 days, PERT 17 days, since both methods pull the expected value up due to the long pessimistic tail.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Triangular = (O + M + P) / 3 = (8 + 14 + 26) / 3 = 16. PERT beta = (O + 4M + P) / 6 = (8 + 56 + 26) / 6 = 90/6 = 15. (A) misstates the triangular value. (C) swaps the two methods' output. (D) overstates both values.
