# Question

Four Scrum teams in a SAFe train depend on Team A's API for two downstream features. Team A has under-forecast and will miss the API for this program increment. Three other teams are now blocked for the PI. What should the RTE or PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign developers from Team A's backlog items to accelerate API delivery and protect the PI's committed objectives.
- **B**: Cancel the dependent features from the program increment and restart PI planning for the downstream teams immediately.
- **C**: Convene the affected teams, replan the PI around the API slip, and renegotiate objectives with business stakeholders.
- **D**: Escalate the slip to the portfolio sync and request an additional increment to absorb the overflow from this PI.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

At scaled agile level, dependency slips are resolved by replanning the PI with affected teams and renegotiating objectives transparently. (C) reflects SAFe and PMI replan practice. (A) is reactive staffing that ignores team capacity and ownership. (B) is disruptive overreaction. (D) is premature escalation before local replan is tried.
