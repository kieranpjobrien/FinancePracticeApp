# Question

An infrastructure programme is three weeks behind on a 30-week critical path. The sponsor demands the original date. Fast-tracking is possible but increases integration rework risk; crashing is possible but adds $160,000. Resource skill availability is constrained. What should the PM recommend first?

## Options

- **A**: Crash the critical path by adding resources since cost can be recovered later through efficiency gains on downstream work.
- **B**: Fast-track sequential critical activities to recover time because schedule pressure outweighs the modest integration risk.
- **C**: Analyse crashing and fast-tracking options against cost, risk, and resource constraints, then propose a combined response.
- **D**: Accept the three-week slip and communicate a revised baseline to the sponsor, avoiding the risks of either compression technique.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Schedule compression decisions are data-driven trade-offs between cost, risk, and resources; the PM analyses options before committing. (C) is the correct first step. (A) and (B) pre-select a technique without analysis and ignore known constraints. (D) surrenders the recovery conversation before analysis.
