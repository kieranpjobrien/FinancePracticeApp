# Question

The project needs to compress its schedule. The PM has analysed two options: fast-tracking can recover 3 weeks but raises rework risk, while crashing can recover 2 weeks at additional cost with lower risk. The sponsor has not stated a preference between cost and risk. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Choose fast-tracking because it recovers more time and time is the binding constraint that triggered the analysis.
- **B**: Present both options with quantified time, cost, and risk impacts and ask the sponsor to choose based on their cost and risk tolerance.
- **C**: Choose crashing because it is the lower-risk path and risk avoidance is generally preferred over cost savings.
- **D**: Recommend a mixed approach combining partial crashing and partial fast-tracking based on the PM's professional judgement of the trade-off.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The sponsor has not chosen between cost and risk, and PMI expects the PM to surface that decision rather than make it on the sponsor's behalf. (B) does that. (A) and (C) each pre-empt the sponsor's choice in opposite directions. (D) is also a unilateral decision; it may be the right answer once the sponsor weighs in, but the PM should not commit to the trade-off unilaterally before that conversation.
