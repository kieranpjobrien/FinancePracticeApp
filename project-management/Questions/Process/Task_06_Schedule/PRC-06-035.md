# Question

Schedule compression is needed. Fast-tracking would reduce the timeline by 3 weeks but increases rework risk. Crashing would reduce it by 2 weeks with additional cost but lower risk. How should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Always choose the option with greatest time savings
- **B**: Present both options with their trade-offs (time savings, cost, risk) and let decision-makers choose based on their risk tolerance and priorities
- **C**: Always choose the lowest risk option
- **D**: Flip a coin

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Schedule compression decisions involve trade-offs that depend on project context. Present: fast-tracking saves more time but with rework risk; crashing saves less time but is safer. Context matters: is time or cost more critical? What's the organization's risk tolerance? Enable informed decision-making rather than applying rules.

Options A and C apply rules without considering context.

Option D isn't a decision-making approach.
