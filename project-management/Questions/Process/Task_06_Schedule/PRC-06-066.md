# Question

A major customer commits publicly to a product launch date that your Scrum team has not forecast. The date is four weeks earlier than the team's current forecast based on six sprints of velocity data. The customer's marketing is already in market. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the earlier date and ask the team to push harder across the remaining sprints to close the four-week gap.
- **B**: Negotiate scope reduction with the customer and product owner so the earlier date can be met with reduced content.
- **C**: Add two contractors to the Scrum team to increase capacity and recover the four-week gap through sustained crunch.
- **D**: Reframe the conversation with the customer using velocity evidence, propose scope trade-offs, and agree a realistic plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A premature customer commitment does not override empirical data; the PM uses velocity to renegotiate scope or date honestly. (D) is correct. (A) courts burnout and likely missed dates. (B) pre-selects one lever without data-led conversation. (C) adds people late, typically reducing short-term throughput (Brooks's law), and keeps the unrealistic date.
