# Question

Team members are multitasking across multiple projects, causing context-switching overhead and schedule slippage on all projects. What should the PM advocate for?

## Options

- **A**: Accept multitasking as organizational reality
- **B**: Present data on context-switching costs to management, advocate for dedicated resources or at least minimized task-switching, and work with other PMs to coordinate resource usage
- **C**: Demand exclusive resources
- **D**: Ignore the impact on schedule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Multitasking creates hidden costs. Present data: studies show context-switching can consume 20-40% of productive time. Advocate: dedicated or focused resources improve throughput. Coordinate: work with other PMs to minimize switching (perhaps dedicate people to one project for a sprint). This addresses root cause.

Option A accepts inefficiency without attempting improvement.

Option C may not be feasible.

Option D ignores a significant schedule risk.
