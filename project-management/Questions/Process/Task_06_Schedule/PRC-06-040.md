# Question

A project manager inherits a schedule created by a predecessor. The schedule shows the project on track, but team members privately express concern that dates are unrealistic. What should the new PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Trust the schedule since it was approved
- **B**: Validate the schedule with the team - review assumptions, compare estimates to their experience, and assess realism. If concerns are valid, address them transparently with stakeholders.
- **C**: Immediately declare the project late
- **D**: Ignore team concerns since they're not PMs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Inherited schedules need validation. Team input: those doing the work often know if estimates are realistic. Review: check assumptions, compare to experience. If concerns are valid: better to surface early and address than to discover later. Transparency: stakeholders should know if the schedule has issues.

Option A trusts without verifying.

Option C concludes without analysis.

Option D ignores valuable input from those closest to the work.
