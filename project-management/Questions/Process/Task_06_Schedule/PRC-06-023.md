# Question

You are using the Critical Chain Method on a construction project. A feeding buffer protects the critical chain from one feeding chain. The feeding buffer is 50% consumed but only 30% of the work on that feeding chain is complete. What does this signal and what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: The chain is consuming buffer faster than it is producing work; investigate the cause and implement recovery actions before the buffer is exhausted.
- **B**: Buffers are designed to be consumed, so the situation is normal and no specific action is required at this time.
- **C**: The feeding buffer is undersized for this chain; expand the buffer immediately to avoid future overconsumption.
- **D**: Remove the feeding buffer because it is failing to perform its protective function on this chain.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

CCM uses the buffer-consumption-versus-progress relationship as its primary control signal. Consuming 50% of buffer while producing only 30% of work places the chain in the "act" zone. (A) responds correctly: investigate root cause and recover before the buffer is gone. (B) ignores a clear warning signal. (C) treats a control signal as a sizing problem and gives the chain less incentive to recover. (D) removes the early-warning mechanism rather than acting on it.
