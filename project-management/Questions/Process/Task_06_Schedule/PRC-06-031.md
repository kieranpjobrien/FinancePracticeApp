# Question

The PM notices the team is "gold plating" - adding unrequested enhancements to deliverables. While quality is high, this is consuming schedule reserve and threatening the deadline. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Encourage gold plating since it improves quality
- **B**: Address the behaviour - explain that unrequested enhancements consume schedule without corresponding value approval. Focus the team on delivering agreed scope on time.
- **C**: Punish team members for caring about quality
- **D**: Request deadline extension for the enhancements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Gold plating consumes resources (time, budget) on unapproved enhancements. While well-intentioned, it threatens project constraints without stakeholder agreement. Address: explain the issue without discouraging quality focus. Redirect: channel their energy toward agreed scope. If enhancements have value, propose them through change control.

Option A encourages scope creep.

Option C is heavy-handed for well-intentioned behaviour.

Option D seeks approval for work that wasn't requested.
