# Question

A 24-month predictive transformation programme has detailed schedule only for the first three months; the remainder is in high-level milestones. At month two, stakeholders are demanding a fully baselined schedule for the entire 24 months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce a detailed schedule for the full 24 months to satisfy the stakeholder request and baseline the complete plan.
- **B**: Explain rolling-wave planning, show how detail emerges at each wave, and confirm the current baseline stays intact.
- **C**: Defer the request until the programme board meets and let governance rule on whether rolling-wave planning is appropriate.
- **D**: Produce a detailed schedule only for the next six months and keep later months in milestone form as a compromise.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rolling-wave planning is the appropriate PMI technique when later-stage detail is unknown; the PM should educate stakeholders on the method rather than fabricate false precision. (B) is correct. (A) creates a schedule that is detailed but not credible and risks rebaselining later. (C) is unnecessary escalation of a tailoring decision. (D) moves the horizon but applies the same flawed logic.
