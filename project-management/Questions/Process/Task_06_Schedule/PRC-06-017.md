# Question

What is the difference between a milestone and an activity?

## Options

- **A**: They are the same thing
- **B**: A milestone is a significant point or event with zero duration; an activity is work that consumes time and resources
- **C**: Milestones are more important than activities
- **D**: Activities are optional; milestones are mandatory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Activities are work packages that take time and resources to complete. Milestones are significant points in the project (start, completion of phase, key deliverable) with zero duration - they mark achievement rather than work. Both appear in schedules but serve different purposes.

Option A misses the key distinction.

Options C and D mischaracterize the relationship.
