# Question

Stakeholders complain that the project Gantt chart, which has more than 500 activities, is too complex for them to use and they cannot find what is relevant to them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce filtered and summarised views tailored to each audience — milestones for executives, deliverables for sponsors, and detail for the delivery team.
- **B**: Reduce the schedule to its top 50 activities and discontinue maintaining the full detailed plan because stakeholders cannot consume the detail in the current form.
- **C**: Keep the existing single 500-activity view and offer training so that stakeholders can read it themselves and find the items that are relevant to them.
- **D**: Stop sharing the schedule externally and answer schedule questions verbally as they come up to avoid further confusion across the stakeholder group.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's communications planning guidance is to tailor information to audience needs while keeping a single source of truth. (A) does that — the underlying schedule is intact, but each audience sees the slice that is meaningful to them. (B) destroys the detail the team needs. (C) puts the burden on the audience and does not solve the problem. (D) reduces transparency, which is the opposite of what stakeholders are asking for.
