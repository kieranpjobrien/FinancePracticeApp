# Question

Stakeholders complain that the project Gantt chart, which has more than 500 activities, is too complex for them to use and they cannot find what is relevant to them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce filtered and summarised views tailored to each audience — milestone view for executives, deliverable view for sponsors, and detailed view for the delivery team — drawn from the same underlying schedule.
- **B**: Reduce the schedule to its top 50 activities and discontinue maintaining the full detailed plan because stakeholders cannot consume it.
- **C**: Keep the existing single 500-activity view and offer training so stakeholders can read it themselves and find what is relevant to them.
- **D**: Stop sharing the schedule externally and answer schedule questions verbally as they come up to avoid further confusion.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's communications planning guidance is to tailor information to audience needs while keeping a single source of truth. (A) does that — the underlying schedule is intact, but each audience sees the slice that is meaningful to them. (B) destroys the detail the team needs. (C) puts the burden on the audience and does not solve the problem. (D) reduces transparency, which is the opposite of what stakeholders are asking for.
