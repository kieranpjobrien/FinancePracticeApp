# Question

Stakeholders complain that the project schedule is too complex to understand. The Gantt chart has 500+ lines and they can't find what's relevant to them. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Tell stakeholders to learn to read schedules
- **B**: Create summary views for different audiences - executive milestone view, team detailed view, stakeholder-specific views showing what matters to each group
- **C**: Remove detail to simplify
- **D**: Stop sharing the schedule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Different audiences need different views. Executive: milestone summary showing major deliverables and decisions. Teams: detailed activity view for their work. Stakeholders: filtered views showing what impacts them. The detailed schedule exists but isn't forced on everyone. This improves communication without losing detail.

Option A doesn't solve the communication problem.

Option C loses necessary planning detail.

Option D reduces transparency.
