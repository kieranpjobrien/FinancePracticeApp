# Question

A predictive project is two weeks behind plan at the midpoint. The PM estimates that crashing will cost $50,000 and recover one week, while fast-tracking will recover two weeks but materially increase rework risk in two downstream test phases. The sponsor has stated that the deadline is the binding constraint. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Crash only, recovering one week and accepting that the project will still be one week late.
- **B**: Combine partial crashing and partial fast-tracking so the schedule is recovered with the smallest increase in rework risk, and document the residual risks in the risk register.
- **C**: Fast-track only, recovering two weeks and accepting the elevated rework risk in the test phases.
- **D**: Neither — present the sponsor with a re-baselined schedule that reflects the two-week slip.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When the sponsor has prioritised the deadline, PMI expects the PM to use both compression levers in combination so the cost and risk impact of each is minimised, not to apply one extreme. (B) does that and explicitly captures residual risk where it belongs (the risk register). (A) leaves the project late, which the sponsor has already ruled out. (C) hits the date but takes more rework risk than necessary because partial crashing could substitute for some of the fast-tracking. (D) ignores the sponsor's stated priority by re-baselining instead of compressing.
