# Question

A project using predictive methodology is 2 weeks behind schedule at the midpoint. The project manager identifies that crashing by adding resources would cost $50,000 and recover 1 week. Fast-tracking would recover 2 weeks but increases quality risk. The sponsor prioritises the deadline. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Crash only, accepting partial schedule recovery
- **B**: Fast-track only, accepting increased quality risk
- **C**: Combine both approaches with risk mitigation for the fast-tracking quality concerns
- **D**: Accept the 2-week delay as unavoidable

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Given sponsor priority on deadline, the PM should explore all options. Crashing recovers 1 week; fast-tracking recovers 2 weeks. Using both could recover the full 2 weeks, with crashing reducing the amount of fast-tracking needed (reducing risk exposure). Additional quality measures can mitigate fast-tracking risks.

Option A leaves the project 1 week behind - not acceptable if deadline is priority.

Option B recovers schedule but accepts avoidable quality risk without considering combination.

Option D doesn't attempt to meet stakeholder priorities when options exist.
