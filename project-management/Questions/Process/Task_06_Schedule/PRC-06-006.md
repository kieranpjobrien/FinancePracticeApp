# Question

A predictive project is two weeks behind plan. The project manager has been asked to compress the schedule. The team has been working overtime for the past month and morale is visibly declining. Which schedule compression approach should the PM consider FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Continue crashing by adding more overtime hours since schedule is the binding constraint.
- **B**: Submit a change request to extend the deadline by two weeks to align the schedule with reality.
- **C**: Identify activities planned in sequence that can safely overlap and fast-track those, accepting the increased rework risk.
- **D**: Reduce scope by deferring non-essential features so the remaining work fits within the original deadline.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The question asks specifically about a schedule compression technique, and PMI lists two: crashing and fast-tracking. (C) chooses fast-tracking, which adds risk rather than cost or burnout — appropriate when the team is already over-extended. (A) doubles down on crashing through overtime, which is what is causing the morale problem; sustained overtime degrades quality and slows recovery. (B) is acceptance, not compression, so it does not answer the question. (D) is a scope change, not a compression technique, and may also need a change request and stakeholder agreement.
