# Question

A project is behind schedule. The project manager considers crashing by adding resources to critical path activities. However, the team is already working overtime and morale is declining. What alternative schedule compression technique should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Continue crashing since schedule is the priority
- **B**: Fast-tracking by overlapping activities that were planned sequentially
- **C**: Reducing scope to meet the deadline
- **D**: Extending the deadline to match current progress

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Fast-tracking overlaps activities that were originally planned in sequence. Unlike crashing, it doesn't require additional resources - it requires accepting increased risk from parallel work. Given team exhaustion, fast-tracking may be preferable to more overtime.

Option A (continue crashing) ignores human factors. Sustained overtime leads to burnout, errors, and turnover - ultimately slowing the project further.

Option C (reduce scope) may be valid but changes what the project delivers. Schedule compression attempts to meet the deadline with original scope.

Option D (extend deadline) isn't schedule compression - it's accepting the delay. This may be the right answer ultimately, but the question asks about compression techniques.
