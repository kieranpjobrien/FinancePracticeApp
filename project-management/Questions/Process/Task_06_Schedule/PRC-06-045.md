# Question

Which of the following BEST describes schedule compression?

## Options

- **A**: A change-control technique that updates the schedule baseline after approved changes are incorporated into the project plan.
- **B**: A risk-response technique that adds contingency reserve to the schedule to absorb identified risks before they materialise.
- **C**: A communications technique that summarises the detailed Gantt chart into a milestone view for executive reporting.
- **D**: Techniques to shorten the project duration without reducing scope, primarily crashing (adding cost) and fast-tracking (adding risk).

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI definition. The two named techniques are crashing (adding resources to critical-path activities at higher cost) and fast-tracking (overlapping sequential activities at higher rework risk). (A) describes integrated change control. (B) describes contingency reserve. (C) describes a reporting technique, not compression.
