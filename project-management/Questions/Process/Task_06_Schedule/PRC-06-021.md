# Question

What are the four types of logical dependencies between activities?

## Options

- **A**: Start-to-Start, Start-to-Finish, Finish-to-Start, Finish-to-Finish
- **B**: Must-Start, Should-Start, Could-Start, Won't-Start
- **C**: Hard, Soft, External, Internal
- **D**: Critical, Near-Critical, Non-Critical, Float

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The correct answer is A. The four dependency types describe relationships between activity starts and finishes: Finish-to-Start (most common - B can't start until A finishes), Start-to-Start (B can't start until A starts), Finish-to-Finish (B can't finish until A finishes), Start-to-Finish (rare - B can't finish until A starts).

Option C describes dependency categories, not types.

Options B and D are not standard scheduling terminology.
