# Question

A schedule analysis shows three near-critical paths, each with two days of total float. Any small slip on any of those paths could shift the critical path. What is the BEST way to manage this exposure?

## Options

- **A**: Move resources from the current critical path to the near-critical paths so all paths gain some float.
- **B**: Re-baseline the schedule to extend the project end date and increase float across every network path.
- **C**: Reduce monitoring overhead by tracking only the path that is currently critical for this reporting period.
- **D**: Track all near-critical paths in schedule control and document the path-shift exposure as a risk.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats near-critical paths as a known schedule risk and expects the PM to monitor them and prepare contingent responses. (D) does that — broaden monitoring and place the exposure in the risk register with planned responses. (A) moves resources off the genuinely critical path and may extend the project. (B) hides risk by changing the baseline rather than managing the exposure. (C) ignores the very paths the question identifies as risks.
