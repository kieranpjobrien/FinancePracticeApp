# Question

A key integration milestone on a predictive project will slip by 10 days. The downstream team is unaware because the PM expects to recover 7 of those days through fast-tracking next sprint. The monthly status report is due tomorrow. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Report the current 3-day residual slip after planned recovery so the status reflects the latest forecast position.
- **B**: Report the 10-day slip and describe the recovery plan with its probability of success and underlying assumptions.
- **C**: Hold off reporting until the fast-track outcome is known so the monthly report reflects the true end position.
- **D**: Report on-track since the recovery plan will likely restore the milestone within a tolerable window overall.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Transparent status reporting means the current baseline variance is reported alongside the recovery plan, not the netted figure that hides information. (B) preserves reporting integrity. (A) communicates a forecast as fact. (C) delays information stakeholders need now. (D) misrepresents the present position and erodes future reporting trust.
