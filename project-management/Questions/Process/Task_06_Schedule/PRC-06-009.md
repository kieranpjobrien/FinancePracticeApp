# Question

An agile team's velocity has been stable at 30 story points per sprint. The product backlog has 180 story points of work remaining. Stakeholders ask when the project will be complete. What should the project manager communicate?

## Options

- **A**: Exactly 6 sprints from now
- **B**: Approximately 6 sprints, acknowledging that velocity may vary and backlog may change
- **C**: Refuse to provide an estimate since agile doesn't do long-term planning
- **D**: 8 sprints to build in buffer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Using velocity for forecasting is appropriate: 180 points ÷ 30 points/sprint = 6 sprints. However, communicating uncertainty is important - velocity fluctuates, and the backlog may grow or shrink as requirements evolve. The estimate is a forecast, not a commitment.

Option A (exactly 6) overstates certainty. Velocity varies, and treating it as precise misleads stakeholders.

Option C (refuse) misunderstands agile. Agile welcomes change but still provides forecasts based on empirical data.

Option D (arbitrary buffer) adds padding without basis. If uncertainty exists, communicate it rather than hiding it in inflated estimates.
