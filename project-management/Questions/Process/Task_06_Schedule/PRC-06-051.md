# Question

Your data-centre migration depends on a utility provider energising a new feeder by week 22. The utility has just advised week 26 is now realistic. The critical path runs through this dependency. Your sponsor asks, "Can we work around it?" What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the four-week slip, communicate a revised baseline to stakeholders, and adjust downstream milestones proportionally.
- **B**: Re-sequence non-dependent work into the affected weeks and run workstreams that do not require power in parallel.
- **C**: Analyse options with the team, including re-sequencing, interim power sources, and partial cut-overs, then recommend a path.
- **D**: Escalate the utility's change to senior leadership, requesting political intervention to bring the original date back.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

External-dependency slips call for analysis of options with the team before committing to a direction. (C) is the correct first step. (A) accepts the slip without exploring mitigation. (B) pre-selects one option without checking feasibility. (D) is premature escalation before the PM and team have explored internal options.
