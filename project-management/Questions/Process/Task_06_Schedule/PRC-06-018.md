# Question

Across the last four sprints your team has consistently finished work later than its sprint estimates suggested, and the cumulative slip is now a full sprint behind plan. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that all future estimates be doubled to absorb the historical optimism.
- **B**: Stop estimating effort and switch to a flow-based approach that measures only completed throughput.
- **C**: Ask the team to commit to fewer story points each sprint until they consistently finish what they pull in.
- **D**: Use the retrospective to investigate the root causes of the optimism and adjust the team's estimating practice based on the findings.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's agile guidance points to the retrospective as the standard mechanism for inspecting and adapting team practices, including estimating. (D) finds the root cause first — hidden dependencies, unclear stories, interruptions, definition-of-done gaps — and improves the practice from data. (A) applies a blanket multiplier without diagnosis. (B) discards estimating without understanding why it is failing. (C) addresses the symptom (over-commitment) without understanding the cause; the team may still finish late on a smaller commitment.
