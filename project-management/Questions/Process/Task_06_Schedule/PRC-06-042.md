# Question

Your hybrid programme runs infrastructure work in waterfall (firm dates for environment readiness) and application development in Scrum. The Scrum stream depends on environment milestones from the waterfall stream. How should the project manager integrate the two schedules?

## Options

- **A**: Build an integrated programme schedule with the waterfall environment milestones modelled as constraints into the Scrum stream's release plan and roadmap, with explicit handoff criteria.
- **B**: Run the two streams as completely separate schedules and rely on the weekly programme meeting to surface any dependency issues as they arise.
- **C**: Force the waterfall infrastructure stream to adopt the Scrum cadence so a single sprint-based schedule can be maintained for the whole programme.
- **D**: Force the Scrum application stream onto the waterfall milestone plan so the entire programme reports against a single Gantt-based schedule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's hybrid guidance is to integrate at dependency boundaries while preserving each methodology where it is appropriate. (A) does that — milestone constraints, explicit handoff criteria, and a programme-level integrated view. (B) ignores known cross-stream dependencies. (C) and (D) impose one methodology on work that is better suited to the other and lose the benefits the hybrid approach was chosen for.
