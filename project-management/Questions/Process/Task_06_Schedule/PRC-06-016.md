# Question

Senior stakeholders insist on seeing a fully detailed Gantt chart for an 18-month transformation programme. The teams will use Scrum for delivery and many requirements are still being discovered with users. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Produce the detailed Gantt chart anyway because senior stakeholders need a credible commitment to plan around.
- **B**: Decline to produce a schedule and explain that detailed scheduling is incompatible with agile delivery.
- **C**: Provide a release-level roadmap with key milestones, with detail planned only one or two iterations ahead through progressive elaboration.
- **D**: Produce a placeholder Gantt chart and update it sprint by sprint as actual progress is recorded.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's hybrid guidance is to use progressive elaboration — high-level roadmap and milestones for the long term, detailed plans only for the near term — and to explain that approach to stakeholders. (C) does both. (A) creates false precision that will not survive contact with the work. (B) is a dogmatic refusal that ignores legitimate governance needs. (D) maintains an artefact that constantly contradicts reality and confuses stakeholders rather than informing them.
