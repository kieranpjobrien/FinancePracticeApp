# Question

An agile team is having difficulty estimating user stories because they lack detail. The product owner says detailed stories defeat the purpose of agile. What practice should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Require complete specifications before stories enter the backlog
- **B**: Use a Definition of Ready to ensure stories have sufficient detail for estimation before sprint planning
- **C**: Accept vague stories and refine them during the sprint
- **D**: Have developers write the acceptance criteria themselves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The Definition of Ready establishes minimum criteria a story must meet before the team commits to it. This typically includes clear acceptance criteria, estimated size, and no blocking dependencies. It balances agile flexibility with the team's need for enough information to estimate and plan.

Option A contradicts agile principles of emerging requirements but goes too far toward waterfall.

Option C delays clarification until the team is already committed, risking sprint failure.

Option D may create alignment issues. The product owner should own acceptance criteria since they represent business requirements.
