# Question

A predictive consulting engagement is delivering its final report. The client's senior sponsor has reviewed informally and is pleased, but no formal acceptance has been signed and the contract requires written acceptance for final payment. The PM wants to close the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin closure activities because the senior sponsor has verbally confirmed the client is pleased.
- **B**: Obtain formal written acceptance per the contract before treating the deliverable as accepted.
- **C**: Accept the sponsor's verbal confirmation and send the final invoice alongside an acceptance form.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to forward an acceptance email to the PM as a lightweight acceptance record.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Validate Scope is a formal process; contractual acceptance terms govern closure and payment, and must be respected. (A) treats an informal signal as formal acceptance and bypasses the contract. (C) invoices before acceptance is confirmed, exposing payment risk. (D) substitutes a lightweight email for the formal acceptance the contract requires.
