# Question

An agile product owner continuously adds small enhancements to user stories during the sprint, arguing they're clarifications not scope changes. The team's velocity is declining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the enhancements since the product owner owns the product backlog
- **B**: Freeze requirements once a sprint begins and defer changes to future sprints
- **C**: Work with the product owner to distinguish between genuine clarifications and scope additions
- **D**: Increase sprint length to accommodate the additional work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. There's a legitimate distinction between clarifying intent and adding new requirements. The PM should facilitate a discussion to establish criteria for what constitutes a clarification versus a scope change. This preserves agile flexibility while protecting the team's ability to deliver.

Option A allows unchecked scope creep. The product owner manages the backlog but shouldn't change committed sprint work unilaterally.

Option B may be too rigid. True clarifications are normal and necessary; the issue is distinguishing them from additions.

Option D treats the symptom (not finishing work) rather than the cause (mid-sprint scope changes).
