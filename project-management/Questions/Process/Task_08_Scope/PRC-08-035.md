# Question

A requirements workshop reveals that stakeholders have vastly different visions for the product. Some want a simple solution quickly; others want a comprehensive platform. The project budget can't support both. What should the PM facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Let the sponsor decide which vision to pursue
- **B**: Propose building the comprehensive platform in phases
- **C**: Facilitate creation of a shared product vision and MVP definition that stakeholders can align on
- **D**: Recommend cancelling the project due to lack of stakeholder alignment

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before escalating or proposing solutions, the PM should facilitate alignment. A shared product vision establishes what the product should ultimately become. An MVP definition identifies what's needed for initial value. This approach may satisfy both camps - quick initial value with a path to comprehensive features.

Option A abdicates the PM's facilitation role. Getting to alignment is part of the job.

Option B proposes a solution before establishing agreement on what's being built.

Option D is premature. Disagreement at this stage is normal; the PM should work to resolve it.
