# Question

During requirements gathering, two key stakeholders have conflicting requirements. One wants a simple interface for speed; the other wants comprehensive features for power users. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Prioritise the senior stakeholder's requirements
- **B**: Build both interfaces and let users choose
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion to understand underlying needs and find a solution that addresses both
- **D**: Document both requirements and let the sponsor decide

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Conflicting requirements often stem from different underlying needs rather than truly incompatible goals. The PM should facilitate dialogue to understand why each stakeholder wants what they want, then explore solutions that meet both needs (progressive disclosure, user profiles, configurable complexity).

Option A uses hierarchy instead of analysis. Seniority doesn't make requirements more valid.

Option B doubles the work without exploring whether it's necessary. There may be simpler solutions.

Option D escalates without attempting resolution. The PM should try to resolve conflicts before escalating.
