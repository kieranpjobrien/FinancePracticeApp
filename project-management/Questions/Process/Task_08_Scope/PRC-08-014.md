# Question

A project's requirements traceability matrix shows that several requirements have no corresponding test cases. The testing team says those requirements are "obvious" and don't need explicit testing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the testing team's judgement since they're the experts
- **B**: Require test cases for all requirements to maintain traceability
- **C**: Document the gap as an assumption and proceed
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor for a decision on testing coverage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Requirements traceability ensures every requirement has verification. "Obvious" requirements are still requirements that need documented verification. Without explicit test cases, there's no proof requirements were validated, creating audit and quality risks.

Option A accepts an incomplete process. Expert judgement doesn't replace documented verification, especially for regulatory or contractual compliance.

Option C formalises the gap but doesn't address it. Assumptions should be about unknowns, not about skipping process steps.

Option D unnecessarily escalates a methodology question. The PM can enforce proper traceability practices.
