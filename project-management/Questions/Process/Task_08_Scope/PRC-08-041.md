# Question

A customer signs off on requirements but later claims the team misinterpreted them. The team's interpretation is documented and was reviewed with the customer. How should the PM handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Implement what the customer now says they wanted to maintain the relationship
- **B**: Refer to the documented interpretation and signed requirements as the baseline
- **C**: Propose a change request to modify scope based on the customer's clarified intent
- **D**: Escalate to legal to determine contractual obligations

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The baseline exists and the customer approved it. However, if the customer's needs aren't being met, the project may fail regardless of being "technically correct." Proposing a change request acknowledges the baseline while providing a path forward. The change can include discussion of cost and timeline impacts.

Option A ignores the baseline and sets a precedent for scope changes without process.

Option B is factually correct but doesn't solve the problem. Being right about the baseline doesn't help if the customer is unhappy.

Option D escalates prematurely and damages the relationship. Business resolution should be attempted first.
