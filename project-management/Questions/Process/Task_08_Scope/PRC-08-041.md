# Question

A customer signed off on documented requirements four months ago and now asserts the team "misinterpreted" them. The team's interpretation is documented and was confirmed in writing during sign-off. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly rework the deliverable to match the customer's new interpretation so the relationship is preserved.
- **B**: Stand on the signed baseline and decline any change, citing formal sign-off as final.
- **C**: Acknowledge the baseline, raise a change request capturing the clarified intent, and present impact for joint decision.
- **D**: Refer the dispute to the legal team to determine contractual obligations under the current agreement.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The right move respects the baseline (so process integrity is preserved) and the relationship (by offering a path forward). Change control with explicit impact is the PMI mechanism for exactly this situation. (A) sets a precedent that signed scope means nothing. (B) is technically correct but ignores the user value the project is supposed to deliver. (D) escalates to legal before attempting professional resolution.
