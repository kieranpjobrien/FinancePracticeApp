# Question

A Scrum team has carried the same large user story across three sprints; it never finishes inside one. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Lengthen the sprint timebox to give the team enough room to finish.
- **B**: Add more developers to the story to accelerate completion.
- **C**: Move the story into an "epic" tracker and stop tracking it as a sprint commitment.
- **D**: Split the story into smaller, independently deliverable pieces that each fit within a sprint.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A story too large for a sprint should be split, not accommodated. PMI/agile guidance is clear: stories should be small enough to deliver, demo, and learn from inside a sprint. (A) breaks the timebox and hides the underlying sizing problem. (B) ignores Brooks's Law and may not even apply if the work has serial dependencies. (C) just relabels the unfinished work and leaves the team without small increments to deliver.
