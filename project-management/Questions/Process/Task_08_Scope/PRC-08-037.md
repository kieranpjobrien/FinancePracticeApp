# Question

A team using Scrum has a user story that keeps getting carried over sprint to sprint because it's too large. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Extend the sprint length to accommodate larger stories
- **B**: Assign more team members to the story
- **C**: Split the story into smaller, independently deliverable pieces
- **D**: Move the story to a dedicated epic for long-term development

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Stories should be small enough to complete within a sprint. A story that repeatedly carries over is too large and should be split into smaller pieces that can each deliver value independently. This maintains sprint predictability and provides regular feedback.

Option A changes the methodology rather than the problem story.

Option B doesn't address the fundamental issue of story size and may not help if the work has dependencies.

Option D defers the problem rather than solving it. Large stories need decomposition, not relocation.
