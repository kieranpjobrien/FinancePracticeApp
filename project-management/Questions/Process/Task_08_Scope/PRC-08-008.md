# Question

An agile team has completed 4 sprints of a 10-sprint project. The product owner wants to add a major new feature that would require approximately 3 sprints of work. The original scope is still important. What options should the Scrum Master present?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the new feature to protect the original scope
- **B**: Add the feature and extend the project by 3 sprints
- **C**: Present trade-off options: extend timeline, remove equivalent scope, or add resources
- **D**: Let the team work overtime to fit everything in

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile welcomes changing requirements but doesn't ignore constraints. The Scrum Master should help the PO understand the options (the "iron triangle"):
1. **Extend timeline** - add 3 sprints
2. **Remove scope** - deprioritise ~3 sprints of existing backlog
3. **Add resources** - may accelerate but has limits (Brooks's Law)

This empowers the PO to make an informed decision based on business priorities.

Option A (reject) violates agile's embrace of change. The new feature may be more valuable than original scope.

Option B (just extend) doesn't explore alternatives that might be preferable.

Option D (overtime) is unsustainable and often counterproductive.
