# Question

After 4 of 10 planned sprints, the product owner wants to add a major capability that would take roughly 3 sprints to deliver. The original backlog is still considered valuable. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the new capability to protect the existing roadmap and the team's current sprint commitments.
- **B**: Quietly extend the project timeline by three sprints to accommodate the new work without flagging the change.
- **C**: Coach the team to absorb the additional work through overtime and longer days over the remaining sprints.
- **D**: Lay out the trade-offs for the PO — extend timeline, descope equivalent work, or add capacity — for a decision.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Agile welcomes change but does not pretend it is free. The Scrum Master's job is to make the trade-off visible so the PO can make a value-based call. (A) is rigid and may forfeit higher-value work. (B) commits the organisation without explicit decision and may not be the best option. (C) relies on overtime, which violates sustainable pace and usually backfires (Brooks's Law on the "add capacity" lever also has limits).
