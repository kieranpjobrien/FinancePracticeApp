# Question

A project's WBS was created by the PM alone due to time pressure. Now the team disagrees with how work was decomposed and estimates based on the WBS are inaccurate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defend the WBS since revising it would delay the project
- **B**: Have the team adjust estimates to fit the existing WBS structure
- **C**: Facilitate team revision of the WBS to improve accuracy and buy-in
- **D**: Keep the WBS but document team concerns as project risks

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. WBS quality directly affects all downstream planning. A WBS the team disagrees with will produce unreliable estimates and execution problems. Investing time to revise the WBS with team input improves accuracy and ownership. The short-term delay prevents larger problems later.

Option A prioritises schedule over quality. Working with a flawed WBS causes bigger problems than revision would.

Option B forces estimates into an inappropriate structure, perpetuating the problem.

Option D acknowledges the problem but doesn't fix it. Documenting issues without addressing them isn't effective risk management.
