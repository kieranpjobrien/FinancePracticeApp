# Question

The PM built the WBS alone under deadline pressure. Now the team disputes the decomposition and the estimates derived from it are proving inaccurate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defend the WBS to avoid the schedule slip a revision would cause at this stage.
- **B**: Direct the team to adjust their estimates to fit the existing WBS structure and continue.
- **C**: Capture the team's concerns in the risk register and continue executing the current plan unchanged.
- **D**: Facilitate a session to revise the WBS with the team, then re-baseline schedule and cost.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A WBS without team buy-in produces unreliable estimates and execution surprises; the cost of fixing it now is smaller than the cost of executing on bad scope decomposition. (A) doubles down on a known-flawed artefact. (B) forces the data to fit the model. (C) records the problem rather than solving it; assumptions/risks are not a substitute for fixing planning errors.
