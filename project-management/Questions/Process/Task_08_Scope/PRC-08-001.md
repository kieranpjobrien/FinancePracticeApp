# Question

Three days into a two-week sprint, the product owner approaches the Scrum Master with a "must-have" feature from a major customer and asks that it be added to the active sprint, which is already fully loaded. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the new item and let the team de-scope or carry over whatever does not fit.
- **B**: Decline the request because sprint scope is locked once planning concludes.
- **C**: Facilitate a conversation between the PO and team about which committed item would be removed if the new one is added.
- **D**: Extend the sprint by a few days so the original commitment and the new item can both fit.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Scrum allows mid-sprint scope changes only via negotiation between the team and the PO; the principle is "stable but not rigid." A swap conversation respects both the team's capacity and the PO's authority over priority. (A) bypasses the negotiation and effectively breaks commitment. (B) is rigid and ignores legitimate business urgency. (D) violates the timebox, which is foundational to Scrum predictability.
