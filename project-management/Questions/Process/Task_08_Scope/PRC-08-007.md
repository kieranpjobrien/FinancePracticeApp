# Question

During requirements gathering, two stakeholder groups provide conflicting requirements. Marketing wants feature A; Operations wants feature B. Both can't be implemented within budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement whichever feature the more senior stakeholder wants
- **B**: Implement both features and request additional budget
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion to understand the underlying needs and find a solution that addresses both
- **D**: Flip a coin to decide fairly

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Conflicting requirements often stem from different underlying needs that aren't mutually exclusive. The PM should facilitate discussion to understand why each group wants their feature. Understanding the "why" may reveal a third option that satisfies both, or help stakeholders make informed trade-off decisions.

Option A (seniority decides) may overlook the better solution and creates winners/losers without exploring options.

Option B (do both) assumes additional budget is available and doesn't address the root conflict.

Option D (random selection) abdicates responsibility for facilitating a good decision.
