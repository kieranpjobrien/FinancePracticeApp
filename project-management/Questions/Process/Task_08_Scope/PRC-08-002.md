# Question

What is gold plating in project scope management?

## Options

- **A**: Delivering exactly what was requested in the requirements
- **B**: Adding extra functionality or quality beyond what was specified or requested
- **C**: Using premium materials for higher quality
- **D**: Exceeding cost estimates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Gold plating refers to adding features, functionality, or quality improvements that weren't requested and aren't in the approved scope. While well-intentioned (team thinks customer will appreciate extras), it's considered poor practice because it consumes resources without customer validation, may delay delivery, and the extras may not be valued.

Option A describes proper scope management - delivering to requirements.

Option C refers to material choices, not scope management.

Option D describes cost overrun, not scope expansion.
