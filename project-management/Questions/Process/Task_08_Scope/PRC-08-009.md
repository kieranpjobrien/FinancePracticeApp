# Question

What is scope creep?

## Options

- **A**: The formal process of changing project scope
- **B**: Uncontrolled expansion of project scope without adjustments to time, cost, or resources
- **C**: The natural evolution of requirements in agile projects
- **D**: Reducing project scope to meet deadlines

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Scope creep is the uncontrolled addition of features or requirements without corresponding adjustments to schedule, budget, or resources. It often happens gradually through small additions that seem harmless individually but accumulate to significant impact. Proper change control prevents scope creep.

Option A describes change control, which is the opposite of creep - controlled scope changes.

Option C describes legitimate agile adaptation, which includes reprioritisation and trade-offs, not uncontrolled expansion.

Option D describes scope reduction, not expansion.
