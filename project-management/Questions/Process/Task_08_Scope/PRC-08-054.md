# Question

On a predictive construction project, work package estimates are routinely exceeded by thirty to fifty percent. The PM reviews the WBS and finds several work packages span more than eighty hours and combine dissimilar activities. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Increase contingency reserves to absorb the recurring overruns across the remaining work packages.
- **B**: Decompose the oversized packages further so estimates reflect homogeneous work and specific ownership.
- **C**: Instruct estimators to apply a standard thirty percent uplift to all future work package estimates.
- **D**: Document the overruns in the issue log and continue executing against the existing WBS structure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Over-large heterogeneous packages hide estimation error; finer decomposition enables accurate estimates and clear ownership. (A) funds the symptom rather than fixing the decomposition cause. (C) applies a blanket uplift that masks which activities actually vary. (D) records overruns without addressing the underlying structural flaw.
