# Question

A monthly steering report shows scope variance of fourteen percent over plan across three work streams on a predictive government programme. The PM has not yet analysed the cause. The steering committee meets tomorrow and expects an explanation. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct variance analysis now to identify drivers, proposed responses, and residual impact.
- **B**: Present a high-level variance figure at the meeting and promise analysis within the following week.
- **C**: Cancel tomorrow's steering meeting so a full variance root cause analysis can be completed first.
- **D**: Ask each work stream lead to explain their own variance directly to the steering committee.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Control Scope requires analysis of variance drivers before reporting so the steering committee can make informed decisions. (B) reports a number without insight, which is low-value governance. (C) cancels a scheduled oversight forum rather than preparing properly. (D) fragments accountability and denies the committee an integrated view.
