# Question

What is the difference between scope validation and scope control?

## Options

- **A**: Validation happens during planning; control happens during execution
- **B**: Validation gains formal acceptance of deliverables; control manages scope changes
- **C**: Validation is performed by the team; control is performed by the customer
- **D**: Validation prevents scope creep; control verifies deliverables meet requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Scope validation is about obtaining formal acceptance of completed deliverables from the customer or sponsor - confirming they accept what was produced. Scope control monitors scope status, manages changes to the scope baseline, and prevents uncontrolled changes (scope creep).

Option A reverses the timing. Both happen during execution, with validation occurring when deliverables are complete.

Option C reverses the participants. The customer validates (accepts); the team controls (manages).

Option D reverses the definitions. Control prevents scope creep; validation verifies acceptance.
