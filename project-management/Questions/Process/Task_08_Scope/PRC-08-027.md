# Question

A regulator publishes new compliance requirements that must be implemented before launch. The sponsor states there is no additional budget. The compliance work is non-negotiable and there is a hard regulatory date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly use management reserve to cover the compliance work without flagging it.
- **B**: Present trade-off options to the sponsor and CCB — descope lower-priority work, extend timeline, or secure additional funding — and seek a documented decision.
- **C**: Proceed without the compliance features and document the regulatory exposure as a risk.
- **D**: Pause the project until additional funding is approved.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulatory work is mandatory; budget is constrained; therefore something must change and the choice belongs to governance. The PM's job is to surface the levers and seek a documented decision. (A) misuses reserve and hides a baseline change. (C) accepts a known compliance failure, which is unethical and exposes the organisation. (D) is a unilateral pause without offering alternatives.
