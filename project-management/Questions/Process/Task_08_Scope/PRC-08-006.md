# Question

Over the past three sprints, the project manager observes that the team is consistently delivering more than what user stories specify because they "think users will like it." Stakeholders are happy in demos but the project is now two sprints behind. What should the PM address with the team?

## Options

- **A**: Thank the team for their initiative and adjust the schedule baseline to reflect actual throughput.
- **B**: Reduce the testing budget so the team can absorb the extras and stay on track.
- **C**: Coach the team that suggestions belong in the backlog for the PO to prioritise, not in unscheduled implementation.
- **D**: Restrict developer access to stakeholders so unsolicited input cannot reach the team.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This is gold plating — the cumulative cost is real even though each addition feels small and well-intentioned. The right intervention channels the team's good instincts through the backlog, where the PO can compare value against committed work. (A) rewards the behaviour and entrenches the slip. (B) trades quality for unauthorised scope, which is a textbook bad trade. (D) addresses information flow, not the team's discipline, and damages collaboration.
