# Question

In sprint planning, the team and the product owner read a user story and reach different interpretations of what it covers. The PO sees broad behaviour; the team sees a narrow change. What should the Scrum Master facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the product owner because they own the backlog and the intent.
- **B**: Defer to the team because they are accountable for delivery.
- **C**: Pull the story from the sprint and let the PO refine it later in isolation.
- **D**: Run a brief refinement conversation now to reach shared understanding and write explicit acceptance criteria.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The disagreement is a clarity defect in the story; the cure is conversation that ends in concrete acceptance criteria all parties accept. (A) silences the team and risks rework. (B) silences the PO and risks missing intent. (C) defers a problem that can be cleared in minutes when context is fresh and both parties are present.
