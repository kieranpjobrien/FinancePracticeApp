# Question

During sprint planning, the development team and product owner disagree about what a user story means. The product owner interprets it broadly; the team interprets it narrowly. What should the Scrum Master facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the product owner's interpretation since they own the backlog
- **B**: Accept the team's interpretation since they will build it
- **C**: Facilitate discussion to create shared understanding and refine the story with clear acceptance criteria
- **D**: Remove the story from the sprint until agreement is reached later

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Disagreement about story meaning indicates the story isn't ready for sprint commitment. The Scrum Master should facilitate a conversation where both perspectives are heard, clarify requirements through discussion, and ensure the story has clear acceptance criteria that everyone understands and agrees upon.

Option A (PO wins) ignores valid team input. The team's understanding matters because they must build it - unclear requirements lead to rework.

Option B (team wins) ignores the PO's intent. The team might build something that doesn't meet business needs.

Option D (defer) delays the problem rather than solving it. The story will still need clarification eventually - better to do it now while context is fresh.
