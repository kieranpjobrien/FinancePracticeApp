# Question

What is progressive elaboration in project management?

## Options

- **A**: Adding scope throughout the project as requirements become clearer
- **B**: Continuously improving and detailing plans as more information becomes available
- **C**: Progressively involving more stakeholders as the project matures
- **D**: Gradually increasing quality standards throughout the project lifecycle

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Progressive elaboration means developing project plans in iterations, adding detail as more information becomes available and as estimates can be made with greater accuracy. It's about refining understanding and plans, not about adding scope.

Option A confuses progressive elaboration with scope creep. Elaboration refines how work is understood and planned, not what work is done.

Option C describes stakeholder engagement evolution, not progressive elaboration.

Option D describes quality evolution, not progressive elaboration of plans.
