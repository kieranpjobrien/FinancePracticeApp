# Question

What does progressive elaboration mean in PMI terminology?

## Options

- **A**: Continuously refining and adding detail to project plans as more information becomes available.
- **B**: Adding new scope to the project as requirements emerge and clarify.
- **C**: Bringing more stakeholders into the project as it progresses.
- **D**: Raising the quality bar incrementally throughout the project lifecycle.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Progressive elaboration is iterative refinement of plans, not expansion of scope or stakeholders. (B) describes scope creep, which is the opposite of disciplined planning. (C) describes evolving stakeholder engagement. (D) describes a quality strategy, not a planning approach.
