# Question

A telecommunications deployment passes internal verification testing, but the customer refuses to sign off on the deliverable, claiming it does not match their interpretation of the specification. The PM reviews the scope statement and finds genuinely ambiguous wording. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Insist the customer accept the deliverable because it meets the written specification as drafted.
- **B**: Rework the deliverable immediately to match the customer's interpretation to unblock the project.
- **C**: Facilitate a joint walkthrough, document the agreed interpretation, and raise a change request if rework is needed.
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to the sponsor and request a directive on which interpretation should prevail.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Validate Scope is a collaborative acceptance process; ambiguity is resolved by joint clarification with formal change control governing any rework. (A) treats the specification as authoritative despite genuine ambiguity and risks acceptance refusal. (B) commits effort without assessing impact or securing approval. (D) escalates before the PM has attempted structured facilitation.
