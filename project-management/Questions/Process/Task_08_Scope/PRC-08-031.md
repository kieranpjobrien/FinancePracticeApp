# Question

A stakeholder asks why their feature request was rejected after the change control board meeting. The PM wasn't present at the meeting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Explain that the board made the decision and it's final
- **B**: Review the meeting notes to understand the rationale and communicate it to the stakeholder
- **C**: Resubmit the request on behalf of the stakeholder
- **D**: Schedule a follow-up meeting with the board to discuss the rejection

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Stakeholders deserve to understand why their requests are rejected. The PM should review documentation from the meeting to understand the rationale (cost, timing, strategic fit, technical feasibility) and explain it clearly to the stakeholder. This maintains transparency and trust.

Option A provides no value and may damage the stakeholder relationship.

Option C circumvents the process without new information. If the same request is resubmitted without changes, it will likely be rejected again.

Option D wastes the board's time if the decision was sound. Understanding the rationale comes first.
