# Question

A project uses story points for estimation. Stakeholders keep asking for conversion to hours or days for planning purposes. What should the PM explain?

## Options

- **A**: Story points can be converted using the team's average velocity
- **B**: Story points measure relative complexity, not time, and should not be directly converted to hours
- **C**: The team should switch to time-based estimation to meet stakeholder needs
- **D**: Provide an unofficial conversion ratio while explaining its limitations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Story points measure relative size/complexity, not duration. The same story might take different amounts of time depending on who works on it or what else is happening. Velocity (points per sprint) provides planning capability without false precision of hourly estimates.

Option A oversimplifies. Velocity helps with sprint planning but individual stories don't convert cleanly to hours.

Option C abandons a useful technique because stakeholders don't understand it. Education is preferable.

Option D creates the conversion stakeholders want but perpetuates misunderstanding about what story points measure.
