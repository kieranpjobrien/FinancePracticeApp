# Question

A finance director keeps asking the agile team to "convert story points to hours" so she can map sprint estimates to her budget tracker. What should the project manager explain?

## Options

- **A**: Points measure relative size, not duration; use velocity at programme level rather than hour conversions.
- **B**: The team can multiply each story by an average hours-per-point ratio to give finance a working number.
- **C**: The team should switch to hour-based estimates so finance can integrate the data directly into her tracker.
- **D**: Provide an unofficial conversion factor with a note that the mapping is only approximate guidance.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Coaching the stakeholder is the right move; story points and hours measure different things, and the appropriate planning instrument at the team and programme level is velocity. (B) implies a precision points-to-hours mapping that the technique was specifically designed to avoid. (C) abandons a useful estimation approach because of a stakeholder's spreadsheet. (D) gives a misleading number that will be cited as authoritative.
