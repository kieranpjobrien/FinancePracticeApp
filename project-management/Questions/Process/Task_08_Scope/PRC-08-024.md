# Question

During scope validation, a customer refuses to accept a deliverable because it doesn't match their expectations, even though it meets all documented requirements. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on acceptance since the documented requirements were met
- **B**: Rework the deliverable to meet customer expectations at no additional cost
- **C**: Investigate the gap between documented requirements and actual expectations
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to resolve the customer dispute

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A gap between documented requirements and customer expectations indicates a requirements elicitation or documentation problem. The PM should understand where the disconnect occurred - were requirements misunderstood, poorly documented, or did expectations change? This informs both the resolution and process improvements.

Option A may be contractually correct but damages the relationship and doesn't deliver value.

Option B accepts responsibility without understanding the cause. It may also set a precedent for future scope creep.

Option D escalates prematurely. The PM should investigate and propose solutions first.
