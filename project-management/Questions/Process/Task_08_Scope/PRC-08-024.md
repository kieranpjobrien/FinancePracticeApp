# Question

During scope validation, the customer refuses to accept a deliverable, saying "this isn't what we expected" — even though the deliverable meets every documented requirement. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Investigate the gap between documented requirements and the customer's actual expectations to understand where alignment was lost.
- **B**: Hold firm on acceptance, since the documented requirements were objectively met.
- **C**: Authorise the team to rework the deliverable at the project's expense.
- **D**: Escalate the dispute to the sponsor so leadership can settle it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A mismatch between requirements and expectations is a clue, not a verdict. The PM needs to understand whether requirements were misunderstood, whether they evolved, or whether elicitation missed something tacit. (B) is contractually defensible but harms the relationship and outcome. (C) accepts open-ended rework before knowing what to fix. (D) escalates without analysis the sponsor will need anyway.
