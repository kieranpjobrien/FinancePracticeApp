# Question

An agile team is asked to commit to a complex feature involving a technology none of them have used before. The PO needs an estimate for release planning. What should the PM recommend the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide a wide-banded estimate with significant contingency and refine over time.
- **B**: Estimate by analogy from the most similar feature shipped on a previous project.
- **C**: Plan a time-boxed spike to investigate the technology, then estimate the feature with the knowledge gained.
- **D**: Decompose the feature into very small increments and estimate each piece individually.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A spike is the standard agile technique for converting "we don't know yet" into "we now know enough to estimate." The cost of the spike is bounded; the value is a defensible estimate. (A) commits to a number while still ignorant. (B) relies on analogous data that may not transfer when the technology is fundamentally new. (D) cannot meaningfully decompose unknowns; the pieces would be just as uncertain as the whole.
