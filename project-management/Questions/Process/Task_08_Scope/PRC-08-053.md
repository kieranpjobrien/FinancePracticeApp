# Question

An executive sponsor on an agile retail platform insists the first release must include every feature listed in the original business case. The product owner has sequenced a smaller MVP based on user research showing only a subset drives adoption. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Revise the release plan to match the sponsor's list because sponsor alignment matters most here.
- **B**: Support the product owner's MVP privately while publicly promising the sponsor the full list.
- **C**: Let the product owner defend the MVP decision alone since backlog ordering is their accountability.
- **D**: Facilitate a session where research evidence and the MVP rationale are presented to the sponsor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Stakeholder engagement with evidence realigns expectations while protecting the product owner's accountability; transparency builds sponsor trust. (A) overrides product ownership and user research to appease authority. (B) is duplicitous and breaches PMI ethics around transparency. (C) abandons the product owner on a sponsor conversation the PM should be facilitating.
