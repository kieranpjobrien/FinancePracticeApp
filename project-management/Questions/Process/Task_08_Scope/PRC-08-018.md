# Question

A project using predictive methodology is 60% complete when the sponsor requests a fundamental change to the core architecture. The change would require scrapping significant completed work. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change since the sponsor has authority over the project
- **B**: Conduct a thorough impact analysis and present options including continuing as-is, implementing the change, or terminating the project
- **C**: Reject the change due to the sunk cost of completed work
- **D**: Propose a hybrid approach that preserves some completed work while accommodating the new architecture

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Major architectural changes late in a predictive project have significant implications. The PM should analyse the full impact and present options objectively. The sponsor needs to understand the cost of change versus the cost of not changing, including potential project termination if neither makes business sense.

Option A implements without analysis. The sponsor needs information to make a good decision.

Option C falls into the sunk cost fallacy. Past investment shouldn't drive future decisions; future value should.

Option D assumes a hybrid is viable without analysis. It might be, but technical feasibility needs assessment first.
