# Question

While decomposing the WBS, a team lead resists splitting a work package further, saying "it'll lose context." The PM thinks it could go one more level. What is the best basis for the decision?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the team lead because they will be accountable for the work.
- **B**: Push for a smaller package because finer decomposition always improves control.
- **C**: Get a third opinion from another senior team member to break the tie.
- **D**: Test the package against decomposition criteria — can it be reliably estimated, assigned, and progress-measured? — and stop when those tests pass.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Decomposition has a purpose: making work manageable. The right test is functional, not aesthetic. (A) substitutes authority for criteria. (B) over-decomposes and creates administrative noise without proportional benefit. (C) trades one opinion for another rather than applying objective tests.
