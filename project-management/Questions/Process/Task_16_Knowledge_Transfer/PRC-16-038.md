# Question

What term does PMI use for knowledge that lives in people's heads, is grounded in personal experience, and is difficult to articulate or write down?

## Options

- **A**: Explicit knowledge
- **B**: Procedural knowledge
- **C**: Tacit knowledge
- **D**: Codified knowledge

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Tacit knowledge is PMI's term for experience-based, personal knowledge that resists documentation and is typically transferred through observation, mentoring, and conversation. (A) explicit knowledge is the opposite: it can be written down and easily shared. (B) procedural knowledge refers to documented processes and steps, which is a form of explicit knowledge. (D) codified knowledge is also a synonym for explicit, formally documented knowledge.
