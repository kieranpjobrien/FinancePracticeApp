# Question

What is the term for the knowledge that exists in people's minds based on their experience and is difficult to articulate or document?

## Options

- **A**: Explicit knowledge
- **B**: Documented knowledge
- **C**: Tacit knowledge
- **D**: Archived knowledge

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Tacit knowledge is the personal, experience-based knowledge that exists in people's minds. It includes intuition, judgment, know-how, and contextual understanding that is difficult to articulate or write down. It is often transferred through observation, practice, and mentoring rather than documentation.

Option A is the opposite - knowledge that can be easily articulated and documented.

Option B is explicit knowledge in written form.

Option D refers to stored historical information.
