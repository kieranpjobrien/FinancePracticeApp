# Question

Halfway through a hybrid ERP implementation, the PM realises no structured knowledge capture has taken place. Closure is six months away. The team insists they are too busy building to also document. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Integrate lightweight knowledge capture into existing ceremonies and artefacts now, so transfer happens continuously rather than in a late, compressed closure push.
- **B**: Hire a dedicated technical writer to shadow the team and produce documentation without disrupting the team's ongoing delivery commitments across sprints.
- **C**: Accept the team's position and schedule a concentrated documentation sprint during the final month of the project before formal handover begins.
- **D**: Require every team member to spend one hour daily on documentation regardless of their current delivery workload and sprint commitments this week.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats knowledge transfer as continuous throughout the lifecycle, not a closure-only activity, and lightweight integration protects capacity. (A) builds capture into the flow of work. (B) outsources understanding to someone without the tacit context. (C) defers to a moment when memory has degraded and capacity is stretched. (D) imposes a fixed quota disconnected from actual work.
