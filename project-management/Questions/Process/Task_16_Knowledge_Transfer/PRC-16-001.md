# Question

A critical team member with specialized knowledge announces they are leaving mid-project. Their knowledge is essential for several upcoming deliverables. What should the project manager prioritize?

## Options

- **A**: Request that HR extend the team member's notice period
- **B**: Begin immediately documenting their knowledge and pairing them with other team members for knowledge transfer during their remaining time
- **C**: Hire a replacement with similar skills as quickly as possible
- **D**: Reassign their work to other team members who can figure it out

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Time is the limiting factor - the PM must maximize knowledge transfer during remaining time. Documentation captures explicit knowledge; pairing transfers tacit knowledge through working together. This enables remaining team members to continue the work and helps onboard any replacement.

Option A may not be possible and doesn't maximize the remaining time. Even if extended, knowledge transfer still needs active effort.

Option C is important but doesn't address knowledge transfer. A replacement still needs the knowledge that's walking out the door.

Option D assumes others can figure it out, which may be true for some things but risky for specialized knowledge. Active transfer is more reliable than hoping others can reconstruct knowledge.
