# Question

A specialist on your team has just announced she is leaving in three weeks. She owns the bulk of the tacit knowledge for two upcoming deliverables and almost nothing she does is documented. What should the project manager prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Ask HR to negotiate an extension to her notice period so the project has more time before she goes.
- **B**: Begin posting a replacement role and focus on hiring someone with similar skills before her last day.
- **C**: Combine targeted documentation of her current responsibilities with structured pairing sessions so other team members absorb her knowledge while she is still on the project.
- **D**: Reassign her remaining work to other engineers so they will be forced to learn it under pressure.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The constraint is time, and PMI's guidance is to capture both explicit and tacit knowledge through documentation plus active transfer. (C) does both. (A) may fail and even if granted does not by itself transfer knowledge. (B) addresses succession but not knowledge; the new hire still needs the knowledge that is leaving. (D) treats sink-or-swim as a strategy and risks deliverables.
