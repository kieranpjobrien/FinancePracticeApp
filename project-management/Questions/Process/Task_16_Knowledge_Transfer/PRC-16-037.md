# Question

A completed banking integration project is being handed over to a support team in Manila. The support team reads English documentation but has flagged that dense technical specs are slow going for them. Handover sessions are scheduled in two weeks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Translate the full technical documentation set into Tagalog before the handover sessions begin.
- **B**: Meet with the receiving team's lead to confirm which documents they find hardest to use and tailor the handover materials accordingly.
- **C**: Replace the written handover with live walkthroughs so the support team can ask questions in real time.
- **D**: Add a glossary of technical terms to the existing documentation and proceed with the handover as planned.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Knowledge transfer is most effective when it is tailored to the receiver. (B) starts with the receiver's actual experience to identify the specific friction points before investing in solutions, which is the PMI-aligned tailoring approach. (A) is a large investment that may not target the real issue and could delay the handover. (C) abandons durable written artifacts that the support team will need long after the sessions end. (D) is a reasonable enhancement but commits to a solution before understanding the actual gap, and may prove insufficient.
