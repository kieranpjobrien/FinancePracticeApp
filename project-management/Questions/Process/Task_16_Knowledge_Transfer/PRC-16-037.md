# Question

A project is being transferred to a support team in a different country with some language and cultural differences. Technical documentation is in English, which the support team reads but with some difficulty. What should be considered for effective knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Assume English documentation is sufficient
- **B**: Translate everything into the support team's language
- **C**: Supplement written documentation with visual aids, diagrams, and video recordings; use simpler language in documentation; provide extra time for Q&A; and consider translation for critical documents
- **D**: Only assign support to people with perfect English

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cross-cultural knowledge transfer benefits from multiple modes: visual aids and diagrams transcend language barriers, simpler language improves comprehension, video allows tone and demonstration, extra Q&A time addresses misunderstandings, and translation of critical documents ensures accuracy. A multi-modal approach accommodates different language proficiencies.

Option A ignores real comprehension challenges.

Option B may be excessive and time-consuming for all content.

Option D may exclude capable team members.
