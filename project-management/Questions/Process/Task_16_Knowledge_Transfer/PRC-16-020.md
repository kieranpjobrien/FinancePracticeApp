# Question

A project is being cancelled, but similar projects will be undertaken in the future. Team members are demoralized and just want to move on. How should the project manager ensure valuable knowledge isn't lost?

## Options

- **A**: Skip knowledge capture since the project failed
- **B**: Hold a normal lessons learned session
- **C**: Acknowledge the team's feelings while emphasizing the value of their experience, conduct a focused knowledge capture session covering what was learned (both positive and negative), and ensure materials are archived accessibly for future similar projects
- **D**: Let the PMO handle knowledge capture later

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Cancelled projects often contain the most valuable lessons. The PM should acknowledge the difficult situation while encouraging knowledge sharing. Focus on practical learnings (what worked, what didn't, what they wish they'd known) rather than rehashing failure. Proper archiving ensures future projects benefit from this experience.

Option A loses valuable (often expensive) lessons.

Option B may not capture the unique aspects of a cancelled project.

Option D delays capture when memories are freshest and team may disperse.
