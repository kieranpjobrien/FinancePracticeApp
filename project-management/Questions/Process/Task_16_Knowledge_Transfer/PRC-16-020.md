# Question

Your project has been cancelled. The team is demoralised and ready to move on, but the organisation is likely to attempt similar projects in future. What should the project manager do to preserve the value of what was learned?

## Options

- **A**: Skip lessons-learned activity since the team is in no state for it and the project did not succeed anyway.
- **B**: Defer the entire knowledge capture activity to the PMO, who can pick it up when team members are available again.
- **C**: Acknowledge the team's frustration, then run a focused knowledge capture covering what was learned (positive and negative) before people disperse, and archive the materials so future similar projects can find them.
- **D**: Run a standard end-of-project lessons-learned session as if delivery had succeeded, focusing on the activities that were completed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cancelled projects often hold the most expensive lessons; capture them quickly before the team scatters. (C) handles both the human and the knowledge sides. (A) discards highly valuable lessons. (B) defers capture past the point at which memories are still fresh and people are still reachable. (D) ignores the cancellation context, which is itself the most important thing to learn from.
