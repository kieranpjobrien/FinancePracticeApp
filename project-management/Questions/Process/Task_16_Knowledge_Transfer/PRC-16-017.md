# Question

A geographically distributed team has knowledge silos - each location has expertise the others lack. This creates dependencies that slow work when experts in one location are unavailable. What knowledge management approach would help?

## Options

- **A**: Centralize all expertise in one location
- **B**: Accept the silos as a consequence of distribution
- **C**: Implement cross-training, documented knowledge bases, and regular knowledge-sharing sessions to spread critical knowledge across locations
- **D**: Add more people to each location

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Knowledge silos are a common distributed team challenge. Cross-training develops capability in multiple locations, documented knowledge bases make expertise accessible regardless of location, and regular sharing sessions (virtual) spread knowledge actively. This reduces single points of failure and enables work to continue when specific experts are unavailable.

Option A may not be feasible and loses distribution benefits.

Option B accepts inefficiency without improvement.

Option D adds capacity but not capability if knowledge remains siloed.
