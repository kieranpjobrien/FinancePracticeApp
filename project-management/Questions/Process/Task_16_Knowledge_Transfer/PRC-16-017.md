# Question

Your distributed team has knowledge silos by location: each office holds expertise the others lack, and work stalls when local experts are unavailable. What knowledge approach would best help?

## Options

- **A**: Centralise the experts in one office to consolidate expertise and remove cross-location dependencies.
- **B**: Add headcount in each location so there is more capacity to absorb expert unavailability.
- **C**: Cross-train across locations, build accessible knowledge bases, and run regular cross-site knowledge sessions to spread critical knowledge.
- **D**: Accept the silos as a structural feature of distributed teams and route work around expert availability.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) attacks the silo problem directly with cross-training, codification, and regular sharing. (A) gives up the benefits of distribution. (B) adds capacity without spreading capability. (D) institutionalises the problem rather than fixing it.
