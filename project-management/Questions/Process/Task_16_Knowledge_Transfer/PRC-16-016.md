# Question

A project is implementing a complex system that operations will support for years. The operations team needs deep knowledge to troubleshoot and enhance the system. The project budget includes time for documentation but not for extensive training. How should the project manager approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver documentation only since that's what's budgeted
- **B**: Skip documentation to save time for training
- **C**: Raise the concern that the budgeted knowledge transfer approach may be insufficient, propose options (adjusting budget, phased training, self-study with support), and ensure stakeholders make an informed decision about acceptable knowledge transfer
- **D**: Let operations figure it out after go-live

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. If the PM believes the knowledge transfer approach is insufficient for operations to be successful, they should raise this concern. Documentation alone may not be enough for complex systems. Options might include budget adjustment, phased training over time, structured self-study with access to experts, or extended project support. Stakeholders should decide with full information.

Option A accepts potentially inadequate knowledge transfer without escalation.

Option B doesn't address the fundamental budget constraint.

Option D sets operations up to fail.
