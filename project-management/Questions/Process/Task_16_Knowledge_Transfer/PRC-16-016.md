# Question

Your project will hand a complex platform to operations who must support it for several years. The budget covers documentation but not the level of training you believe operations needs. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver against the documentation scope as funded; operations and finance can resolve any training shortfall after handover themselves.
- **B**: Reallocate scope from another project work package to fund operations training, since the operational consequence is severe enough to justify it.
- **C**: Surface the concern with the sponsor and operations leadership, present options, and let the right authority decide on the transfer plan.
- **D**: Have the project team commit to remaining available for ad hoc operations support for six months after go-live to bridge the gap informally.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A funding gap that endangers operational success is exactly what governance is for. (C) brings the trade-off to the right authority with options. (A) accepts a known risk silently. (B) reallocates between work packages without authorisation, which is a baseline change. (D) commits people to post-handover work that they may not be able to deliver and that should be a planned, funded commitment.
