# Question

A project involved novel technology that the organization wants to use in future projects. The project team has gained significant expertise through trial and error. How should this expertise be captured for organizational benefit?

## Options

- **A**: Assume future teams will learn the same way
- **B**: Create detailed technical documentation only
- **C**: Develop multiple knowledge artifacts: technical documentation, common pitfalls and how to avoid them, architectural decision records, training materials, and potentially have team members available as advisors for future projects
- **D**: Hire the same team for all future projects using this technology

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Novel technology expertise is valuable and should be captured comprehensively: technical documentation (how it works), pitfalls and patterns (hard-won experience), decision records (why certain approaches were chosen), and training materials (structured learning). Having experts available to advise future teams transfers tacit knowledge.

Option A wastes the learning investment by making future teams repeat the same trials.

Option B captures explicit knowledge but not the experience-based learning.

Option D isn't practical for scaling the technology across the organization.
