# Question

Each work stream on your project has a single specialist lead. When any one of them is unavailable, that work stream stops. What knowledge approach would best address this single-point-of-knowledge risk?

## Options

- **A**: Combine the work streams under a single team so specialisation is no longer concentrated in individuals.
- **B**: Cross-train at least one backup per work stream to the level needed to continue work or triage issues, supported by documentation and regular cross-stream sharing sessions.
- **C**: Require every lead to take their leave at the same time so the project is paused only once rather than four times a year.
- **D**: Accept the risk on the basis that specialisation is more efficient than redundancy and the impact of any single absence is short-lived.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the standard PMI response to single-point-of-knowledge risk: build redundancy at "good enough to continue" rather than expert level, and back it with documentation and ongoing sharing. (A) restructures the team to avoid the problem rather than solving it. (C) is a scheduling trick that does not address unplanned absences. (D) accepts a known continuity risk that has known affordable mitigations.
