# Question

A senior developer who built several critical microservices is leaving the project in two weeks. None of his design rationale is documented. What is the most effective way to transfer his knowledge?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule daily pairing sessions with two engineers and an architect, supported by lightweight notes captured during the sessions.
- **B**: Block his calendar for two weeks and ask him to produce comprehensive written documentation covering every component he has built.
- **C**: Have him record video walkthroughs of each microservice that engineers can watch on their own time after he has left the team.
- **D**: Ask him to add detailed inline comments to every method in his code so future readers can follow the original logic and decisions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tacit design rationale is best transferred through doing-together and conversation, not via dumping it into a static medium. (A) does that. (B) prioritises explicit-only transfer that is rarely complete in two weeks and loses the tacit layer. (C) loses interactivity and ages quickly. (D) captures the "what" of the code, not the "why" of design choices.
