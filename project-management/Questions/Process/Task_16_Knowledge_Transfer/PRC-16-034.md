# Question

What is a key benefit of conducting knowledge transfer throughout a project rather than only at the end?

## Options

- **A**: It creates more meetings
- **B**: Information is captured while fresh, the current project can benefit from early learnings, and gradual knowledge building is easier than massive end-of-project dumps
- **C**: It satisfies stakeholders who like frequent updates
- **D**: It keeps team members busy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Continuous knowledge transfer has multiple advantages: fresh memory yields better quality capture, early learnings can improve the current project (not just future ones), and gradual accumulation is cognitively easier than trying to capture everything at once. End-of-project capture often suffers from faded memories and rushed execution.

Option A views it as overhead rather than benefit.

Option C focuses on stakeholder satisfaction rather than knowledge quality.

Option D views it as busywork rather than value.
