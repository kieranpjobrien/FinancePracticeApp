# Question

How does PMI distinguish tacit knowledge from explicit knowledge?

## Options

- **A**: Tacit knowledge is held by senior staff while explicit knowledge is held by junior staff regardless of subject matter.
- **B**: Tacit knowledge is personal, experience-based, and difficult to articulate; explicit knowledge is codified and easily shared through documents and procedures.
- **C**: Tacit knowledge is verbal and short-lived; explicit knowledge is anything written down in any project artefact.
- **D**: Tacit knowledge is about people and culture; explicit knowledge is about technology and process.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI uses the standard knowledge-management distinction: tacit is internal know-how built through experience; explicit is codified information that travels in artefacts. (B) captures that. (A) ties knowledge type to seniority, which is wrong. (C) ties tacit to verbal communication, which is incidental. (D) splits the categories along subject lines, which is not the definition.
