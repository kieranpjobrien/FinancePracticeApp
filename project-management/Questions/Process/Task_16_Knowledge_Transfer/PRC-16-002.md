# Question

What is the difference between tacit knowledge and explicit knowledge?

## Options

- **A**: Tacit knowledge is written; explicit knowledge is verbal
- **B**: Tacit knowledge is personal and hard to articulate; explicit knowledge is documented and easily shared
- **C**: Tacit knowledge is about facts; explicit knowledge is about skills
- **D**: Tacit knowledge is false; explicit knowledge is true

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Tacit knowledge is personal know-how gained through experience that's difficult to express in words or documents (like intuition or expertise). Explicit knowledge is codified and can be written down, stored, and transferred through documents, procedures, or databases.

Option A has it backwards - explicit is more easily written, tacit is harder to verbalize.

Option C mischaracterizes both types.

Option D confuses knowledge types with truth values.
