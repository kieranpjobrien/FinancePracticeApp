# Question

What is the difference between tacit and explicit knowledge?

## Options

- **A**: Tacit knowledge is more important than explicit knowledge
- **B**: Tacit knowledge is personal, experience-based, and difficult to articulate; explicit knowledge can be easily documented and shared
- **C**: Explicit knowledge is what experts know; tacit knowledge is for beginners
- **D**: There is no meaningful difference

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Tacit knowledge is the personal, contextual knowledge gained through experience - it's "know-how" that's difficult to write down (like knowing when code "feels" wrong). Explicit knowledge can be clearly articulated, documented, and shared (like procedures, specifications, and guidelines). Both types are valuable.

Option A makes a value judgment that isn't accurate - both types are important.

Option C reverses the relationship - experts have significant tacit knowledge.

Option D ignores an important distinction for knowledge management.
