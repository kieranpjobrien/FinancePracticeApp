# Question

A vendor is developing a custom component for the project. The contract includes delivery of source code and documentation. The project manager is concerned that documentation alone won't enable the internal team to maintain the component long-term. What should be negotiated?

## Options

- **A**: Accept documentation as sufficient since it's in the contract
- **B**: Require the vendor to maintain the component indefinitely
- **C**: Include knowledge transfer sessions, training, and a support period in the contract to ensure the internal team can effectively take over maintenance
- **D**: Plan to reverse-engineer the code if needed

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Contracts should include provisions for effective knowledge transfer, not just deliverables. Knowledge transfer sessions, formal training, and a support period during which the vendor remains available enable the internal team to gain capability. This should be negotiated before work begins rather than assumed.

Option A accepts potentially inadequate knowledge transfer.

Option B may not be cost-effective long-term.

Option D is inefficient when knowledge transfer could be contractually included.
