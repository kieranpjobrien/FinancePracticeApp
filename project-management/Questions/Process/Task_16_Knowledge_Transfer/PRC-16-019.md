# Question

A vendor is building a custom component your team will maintain after handover. The contract already includes source code and documentation. You are concerned the team will not be able to maintain the component effectively from documents alone. What should be negotiated into the contract?

## Options

- **A**: Knowledge transfer sessions, formal training for nominated internal staff, and a defined post-delivery support window during which vendor engineers remain available for questions.
- **B**: A clause requiring the vendor to maintain the component on the project's behalf for the first two years after delivery.
- **C**: Acceptance criteria that include comprehensive documentation only, on the basis that source code plus docs is sufficient.
- **D**: A right for the project team to reverse-engineer the component if any maintenance question cannot be answered from the supplied documentation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the standard PMI procurement provision when in-house maintenance is the goal: explicit knowledge transfer, training, and a support window. (B) keeps the vendor in the critical path long-term, which often is not what the organisation wants. (C) accepts the gap that prompted the question. (D) plans to recover knowledge through reverse-engineering, which is expensive and avoidable.
