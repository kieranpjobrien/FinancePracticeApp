# Question

A team maintains a "knowledge base" but team members rarely contribute because it takes time away from their "real work." How can the project manager increase contributions?

## Options

- **A**: Make contribution mandatory
- **B**: Accept low contribution rates
- **C**: Reduce friction (make it easy to contribute), integrate into workflows (capture during natural work), recognize contributions (acknowledgment), and demonstrate value (show how knowledge base helps)
- **D**: Hire someone to maintain the knowledge base

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Contribution barriers include friction (too hard), separation (not part of normal work), lack of recognition (no reward), and unclear value (why bother?). Addressing all four: make contributing easy, embed in workflows, recognize contributors, and show how the knowledge base helps people. This shifts knowledge contribution from burden to value.

Option A creates reluctant, low-quality contributions.

Option B accepts a poorly maintained knowledge base.

Option D removes team contribution, potentially losing valuable context.
