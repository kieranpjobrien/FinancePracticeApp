# Question

The project team has access to an organisational knowledge base, but contributions are sparse because team members say writing entries pulls them away from sprint work. The PM wants to lift contribution rates without slowing delivery. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make a knowledge base contribution a mandatory exit criterion for every user story.
- **B**: Hire a technical writer to interview the team and author entries on their behalf.
- **C**: Recognise top contributors at sprint reviews and add a contribution metric to the team dashboard.
- **D**: Embed knowledge capture into existing ceremonies such as retrospectives and definition of done so contributions happen during normal work rather than after it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI favours integrating knowledge capture into the natural flow of work rather than bolting it on as overhead. (D) addresses the root cause the team raised, which is that contribution feels like a separate task, by making it part of activities they already do. (A) creates a compliance burden likely to produce low-quality entries written to satisfy a gate. (B) loses the team's voice and context, and the writer becomes a bottleneck. (C) treats the symptom with motivation rather than removing the friction the team identified.
