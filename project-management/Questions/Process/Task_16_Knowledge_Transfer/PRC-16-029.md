# Question

In retrospectives, team members openly share what went well but become quiet whenever the conversation turns to failures. They worry about judgement and career impact. What should the facilitator do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop the failure-focused part of the retro until the team is more comfortable with each other.
- **B**: Establish psychological safety by framing the conversation as systems improvement rather than individual blame, separating retros from performance reviews, using anonymity where helpful, and modelling vulnerability through their own examples.
- **C**: Ask line managers to leave the room so the team feels free to speak honestly about what went wrong.
- **D**: Require each person to share at least one failure to ensure balanced participation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the comprehensive psychological safety response that PMI/agile literature recommends. (A) avoids the most valuable part of the retro. (C) addresses one symptom (presence of managers) without building underlying safety. (D) compels disclosure rather than enabling it, which usually backfires.
