# Question

During a project retrospective, team members are hesitant to share failures because they fear judgment or career impact. How should the facilitator address this to enable honest knowledge capture?

## Options

- **A**: Skip discussing failures
- **B**: Require participation in sharing failures
- **C**: Establish psychological safety by focusing on process improvement rather than blame, using anonymity where helpful, separating learning discussions from performance evaluations, and modeling vulnerability by sharing leadership learnings
- **D**: Have management leave the room

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Honest retrospectives require psychological safety. Focus on systems and processes (what can we improve?) rather than blame (who messed up?). Anonymity can help with sensitive topics. Separating retrospectives from performance reviews reduces fear. Leaders modeling vulnerability (sharing their own learnings) demonstrates it's safe.

Option A loses valuable learning from failures.

Option B creates forced participation without addressing the safety concern.

Option D may help but doesn't comprehensively address psychological safety.
