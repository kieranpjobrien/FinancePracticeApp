# Question

In retrospectives, team members openly share what went well but become quiet whenever the conversation turns to failures. They worry about judgement and career impact. What should the facilitator do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop the failure-focused part of the retro until the team is more comfortable with each other and ready to engage on harder topics.
- **B**: Establish psychological safety by framing retros as systems improvement, separating them from performance reviews, and modelling vulnerability.
- **C**: Ask line managers to leave the room so the team feels free to speak honestly about what went wrong during the sprint just finished.
- **D**: Require each person to share at least one failure every retro to ensure balanced participation and a fair distribution of vulnerability.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the comprehensive psychological safety response that PMI/agile literature recommends. (A) avoids the most valuable part of the retro. (C) addresses one symptom (presence of managers) without building underlying safety. (D) compels disclosure rather than enabling it, which usually backfires.
