# Question

A project completed successfully and comprehensive knowledge transfer was done to operations. A year later, a major incident occurs and the post-mortem reveals the operations team had drifted from documented procedures because they didn't understand why the procedures existed. What knowledge transfer element was missing?

## Options

- **A**: More detailed procedures
- **B**: More frequent training
- **C**: Transfer of the "why" behind procedures - the rationale, risks being mitigated, and consequences of deviation - so operations could maintain procedures intelligently rather than mechanically
- **D**: Stricter enforcement of procedures

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Procedures without rationale become rote processes that people modify without understanding consequences. Knowledge transfer should include why procedures exist, what risks they mitigate, and what happens if they're not followed. This understanding enables intelligent maintenance and prevents well-intentioned "improvements" that introduce risk.

Option A adds more detail but not understanding.

Option B repeats the same training without adding rationale.

Option D treats the symptom (non-compliance) rather than the cause (lack of understanding).
