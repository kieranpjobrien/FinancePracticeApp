# Question

The project is preparing to hand a new platform over to operational support. Operations has the documentation but says they do not understand the "why" behind several key design decisions. What approach best addresses this?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule shadowing sessions where ops engineers work alongside the project team, supported by a walkthrough of the architecture decision log and structured Q&A sessions.
- **B**: Expand and rewrite the documentation pack to include more depth on the design decisions before handover.
- **C**: Defer the question; once operations is running the platform, the rationale will become apparent through experience.
- **D**: Record a series of video tutorials covering each design decision and add them to the operations knowledge base.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The "why" behind decisions is largely tacit and best transferred by working alongside the people who made them and by walking through the recorded rationale. (A) covers both. (B) adds explicit documentation but does not bridge the tacit gap. (C) gambles operational outcomes on rediscovery. (D) is one-way, ages, and loses the chance to ask questions.
