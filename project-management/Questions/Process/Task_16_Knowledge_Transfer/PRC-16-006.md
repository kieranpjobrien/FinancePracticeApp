# Question

A project is entering its final phase and will soon transition to operational support. The support team has received documentation but expresses concern that they don't understand the "why" behind many design decisions. What knowledge transfer approach would address this?

## Options

- **A**: Provide more documentation
- **B**: Let the support team figure it out once they're using the system
- **C**: Arrange shadowing sessions where support team members work alongside the project team, supplemented by decision log reviews and Q&A sessions to transfer tacit knowledge
- **D**: Record video tutorials covering system features

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Understanding the "why" requires tacit knowledge transfer - context, reasoning, and experience that's difficult to document. Shadowing (working alongside experts), reviewing decision logs (which capture rationale), and interactive Q&A sessions enable this deep knowledge transfer that documentation alone cannot provide.

Option A addresses explicit knowledge but not the tacit understanding requested.

Option B risks poor support decisions due to lack of context.

Option D may help with "what" but not deeply with "why."
