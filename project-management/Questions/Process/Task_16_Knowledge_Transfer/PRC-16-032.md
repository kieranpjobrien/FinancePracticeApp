# Question

Your organisation is launching a mentoring programme to transfer knowledge from senior project managers to juniors. Which design choice would most contribute to its effectiveness?

## Options

- **A**: Pair mentors and mentees randomly so all participants experience a wide range of styles and perspectives over the programme's duration.
- **B**: Mandate completion of the programme for any future promotion to keep enrolment high and ensure consistent participation across the cohort.
- **C**: Have senior PMs write down everything they know in a single document that all mentees can read at their own pace without meetings.
- **D**: Set clear objectives, match mentors and mentees thoughtfully, structure interactions flexibly, and support mentors with training.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) sets up the conditions under which mentoring actually works: clarity, fit, structure, support, and scale. (A) ignores fit. (B) creates compliance pressure rather than genuine learning. (C) is a documentation exercise, not mentoring.
