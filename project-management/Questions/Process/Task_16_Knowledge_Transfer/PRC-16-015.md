# Question

A project has excellent technical documentation but new team members still struggle to become productive. Exit interviews reveal that experienced team members have "tribal knowledge" not captured anywhere. How should this be addressed?

## Options

- **A**: Hire only experienced people who don't need onboarding
- **B**: Accept that some knowledge can't be transferred
- **C**: Identify key tribal knowledge areas and capture them through interviews, recorded knowledge-sharing sessions, wikis with informal tips, and mentoring relationships
- **D**: Create more technical documentation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. "Tribal knowledge" is tacit knowledge that exists in people's heads. It can be partially captured through various means: structured interviews to elicit what experts know, recorded sessions sharing tips and context, wikis for informal knowledge, and mentoring to transfer through relationship. The key is recognizing this knowledge exists and actively capturing it.

Option A doesn't solve the knowledge transfer problem.

Option B gives up without trying appropriate approaches.

Option D adds more of what they already have (technical documentation) rather than addressing the gap.
