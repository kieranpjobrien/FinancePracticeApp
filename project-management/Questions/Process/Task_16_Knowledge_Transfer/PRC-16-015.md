# Question

Your project has thorough technical documentation, yet new engineers still struggle to ramp up. Exit interviews keep mentioning "tribal knowledge" that never made it into any document. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Recruit only senior engineers who can be productive without onboarding support.
- **B**: Identify the high-value tribal knowledge areas and capture them through structured expert interviews, recorded knowledge-sharing sessions, an informal tips wiki, and a mentor pairing for each new joiner.
- **C**: Accept that tacit knowledge cannot be fully transferred and adjust ramp-up expectations accordingly in the project plan.
- **D**: Commission an additional round of formal technical documentation focused on the deepest internals of the system.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) tackles the tacit-knowledge gap with a mix of techniques tuned to it. (A) avoids the problem rather than solving it and is rarely feasible at scale. (C) gives up on a problem that has known partial solutions. (D) doubles down on the medium that already exists and clearly is not the gap.
