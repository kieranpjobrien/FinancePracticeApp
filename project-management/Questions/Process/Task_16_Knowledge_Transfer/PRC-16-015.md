# Question

Your project has thorough technical documentation, yet new engineers still struggle to ramp up. Exit interviews keep mentioning "tribal knowledge" that never made it into any document. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Recruit only senior engineers who can be productive without structured onboarding support or extended ramp-up time.
- **B**: Capture high-value tribal knowledge via expert interviews, recorded sessions, an informal tips wiki, and mentor pairing.
- **C**: Accept that tacit knowledge cannot be fully transferred and adjust ramp-up expectations accordingly within the project plan and schedule.
- **D**: Commission an additional round of formal technical documentation focused on the deepest internals of the platform and its core modules.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) tackles the tacit-knowledge gap with a mix of techniques tuned to it. (A) avoids the problem rather than solving it and is rarely feasible at scale. (C) gives up on a problem that has known partial solutions. (D) doubles down on the medium that already exists and clearly is not the gap.
