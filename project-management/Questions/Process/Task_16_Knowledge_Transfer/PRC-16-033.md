# Question

Six months after go-live, operations reports that the runbooks the project produced are out of date because changes have been made to the system without corresponding documentation updates. What process improvement is needed?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a quarterly documentation refresh exercise, owned by operations, to bring the runbooks back into line with reality.
- **B**: Make documentation updates a required part of the change management process, so a change is not considered complete until the related runbook has been updated.
- **C**: Recreate the runbooks from scratch using a reverse-engineering pass against the current production system.
- **D**: Add a disclaimer to the runbooks noting that they may be out of date and advising readers to verify against the live system before acting.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) prevents documentation drift by binding it to the change process, which is the standard PMI/operations pattern. (A) treats the symptom on a lag. (C) is expensive and only resets the problem. (D) shifts trust burden to readers without fixing the underlying decay.
