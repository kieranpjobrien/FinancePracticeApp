# Question

A project is transitioning a product to a different team who will continue development. The receiving team is anxious about taking ownership. What can the project manager do to facilitate effective knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Provide comprehensive documentation and wish them luck
- **B**: Maintain ownership indefinitely to avoid the transition
- **C**: Plan a structured transition with overlap period where teams work together, graduated handover of responsibilities, accessible documentation with context, and clear escalation paths for questions after the transition
- **D**: Have the receiving team shadow for one day

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective transitions address the receiving team's anxiety through: overlap period (working alongside knowledgeable team), graduated responsibility transfer (building confidence), thorough documentation with context (not just what but why), and clear escalation paths (support when questions arise). This builds capability and confidence.

Option A doesn't address the knowledge and confidence gap.

Option B avoids the transition rather than executing it well.

Option D provides insufficient exposure for meaningful knowledge transfer.
