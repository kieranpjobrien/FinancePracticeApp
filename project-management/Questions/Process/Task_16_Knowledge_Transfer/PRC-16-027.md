# Question

A team member has built a small automation script that materially speeds up a recurring task. They have shared it informally with two colleagues. It is undocumented and unowned. What should the project manager facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Hand the script over to central IT for them to rebuild and own under their own standards.
- **B**: Discourage continued use until the script is built into a properly engineered enterprise tool.
- **C**: Document the script, add it to the team's standard toolset, assign maintenance ownership, and share it with adjacent teams that could benefit.
- **D**: Leave the informal sharing in place to avoid bureaucratising a useful grassroots tool.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) preserves the value of grassroots innovation while removing the single-point-of-failure and giving it a proper home. (A) decouples the tool from the people who created it and rarely adds value. (B) loses an existing benefit while waiting for a perfect version that may never arrive. (D) leaves a useful asset undocumented and unowned.
