# Question

The project's wiki has accumulated conflicting information across pages because updates have not been applied consistently. New joiners can't tell which version to trust. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team to default to whichever wiki page has the most recent edit timestamp.
- **B**: Replace the wiki with a brand new tool and migrate only the content the team is confident is accurate.
- **C**: Add a banner on every page directing readers to ask the original author of any content before relying on it.
- **D**: Run a structured wiki cleanup, assign owners to key pages, introduce a lightweight review process for updates, and add "last verified" dates so freshness is visible.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) addresses the conflicts, the lack of ownership, and the lack of freshness signal in one go. (A) treats recency as a proxy for correctness. (B) replaces the tool when the underlying behaviour is the problem. (C) routes every reader to original authors who may no longer be on the project.
