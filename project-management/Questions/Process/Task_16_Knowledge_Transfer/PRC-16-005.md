# Question

A project manager wants to implement continuous knowledge sharing in an agile team rather than waiting for project end. Team members resist, saying they're too busy delivering features. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Make knowledge sharing mandatory and track compliance
- **B**: Wait until project velocity stabilises before introducing knowledge sharing
- **C**: Integrate knowledge sharing into existing ceremonies, such as brief "knowledge moments" in retrospectives or demos
- **D**: Hire a technical writer to document knowledge on behalf of the team

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Integrating knowledge sharing into existing ceremonies minimises additional burden while making it a natural part of how the team works. Retrospectives already involve reflection - adding a knowledge dimension is a small increment. This approach respects the team's delivery focus while building good habits.

Option A (mandate compliance) creates resistance and treats symptoms not causes. Forced documentation often produces low-quality artifacts.

Option B (wait for stability) delays indefinitely - agile projects rarely have "stable" periods. Knowledge is lost while waiting.

Option D (technical writer) can help but doesn't transfer knowledge between team members. The team still lacks shared understanding if only the writer knows things.
