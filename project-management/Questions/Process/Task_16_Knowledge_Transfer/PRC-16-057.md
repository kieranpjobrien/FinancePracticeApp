# Question

After handover of a retail Scrum product to operations, the receiving team keeps phoning original developers whenever issues arise rather than using documented escalation paths. The developers are reassigned and complaining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Forbid the developers from answering operational questions to enforce the escalation path and protect their capacity for assigned new project work.
- **B**: Extend developer availability indefinitely since operations clearly needs the continued access until they build their own capability over time.
- **C**: Update the documentation to include the developers' direct contact details so at least the right developer answers each operational question coming through.
- **D**: Investigate why operations skips the path, strengthen the gaps being bypassed, and coach both sides to follow the escalation process.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats recurring process deviation as a signal that the process or enablement is inadequate and investigates before enforcing. (D) fixes both the gap and the behaviour, with coaching rather than commands. (A) punishes symptom without diagnosis and damages goodwill. (B) perpetuates the dependency the handover was meant to end. (C) formalises the anti-pattern.
