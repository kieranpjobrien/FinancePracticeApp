# Question

A PM wants to measure whether knowledge transfer actually worked on a hybrid retail platform after handover. The sponsor asks for meaningful indicators rather than activity counts. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Report the number of documents produced, training hours delivered, and runbook walkthroughs completed during the handover period as transfer metrics overall.
- **B**: Track how many handover meetings were attended by operations staff and the satisfaction ratings from post-session feedback forms collected at events.
- **C**: Count how many hypercare tickets were raised in the first month compared with the second month, assuming the reduction indicates learning occurred.
- **D**: Measure operations' ability to resolve incidents independently, resolution time trends, and qualitative confidence ratings from operations leadership over time.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI values outcome indicators that reflect capability, not activity or volume. (D) measures what the transfer was meant to achieve. (A) counts inputs without testing impact. (B) counts attendance and feelings rather than ability. (C) confuses a drop in ticket volume with genuine capability, which could equally indicate resignation or avoidance.
