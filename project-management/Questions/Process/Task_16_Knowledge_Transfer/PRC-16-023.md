# Question

A consultant has built deep project-specific knowledge over a six-month engagement and the contract ends in three weeks. What should the project manager ensure?

## Options

- **A**: Extend the consultant's contract on a rolling basis to keep their knowledge available to the team.
- **B**: Plan a structured handover combining documentation, formal training sessions for permanent staff, paired work on representative tasks, and a defined post-engagement window for follow-up questions.
- **C**: Accept the loss of consultant-specific knowledge as a normal part of contract endings and move on.
- **D**: Schedule a single full-day knowledge transfer session in the consultant's last week to cover everything.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the standard PMI approach when knowledge sits with a leaving party: explicit transfer, tacit transfer, and a defined safety net. (A) avoids the transfer rather than executing it. (C) discards knowledge the organisation has paid for. (D) compresses meaningful transfer into too short a window.
