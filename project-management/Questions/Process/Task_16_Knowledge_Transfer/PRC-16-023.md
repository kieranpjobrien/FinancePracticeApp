# Question

A project team includes a consultant who has developed deep expertise in the project's domain during their engagement. The contract is ending. What should the project manager ensure regarding knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Nothing - the consultant's knowledge leaves with them
- **B**: Extend the contract indefinitely to retain the knowledge
- **C**: Before the consultant leaves, ensure their knowledge is transferred through documentation, training sessions, pairing with permanent staff, and establishing a mechanism for limited follow-up questions if needed
- **D**: Ask the consultant to train someone in one day

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Consultants often develop valuable project-specific knowledge. Before they leave, this should be actively transferred to permanent staff through multiple mechanisms: documentation (explicit knowledge), training/pairing (tacit knowledge), and ideally a mechanism for follow-up questions during a transition period. This should be planned before the contract ends.

Option A loses valuable knowledge the organization paid for.

Option B may not be feasible or cost-effective.

Option D is insufficient for meaningful knowledge transfer.
