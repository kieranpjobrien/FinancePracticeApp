# Question

A hybrid banking project is weeks from handover. The receiving operations team claims they are too stretched to engage meaningfully with transfer sessions and has missed three scheduled workshops already. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with operations leadership, surface the capacity issue, co-design realistic scheduling, and negotiate protected time with senior management for transfer sessions.
- **B**: Proceed with written artefacts only, note operations' non-engagement in the closure report, and proceed with formal handover on the planned date.
- **C**: Escalate the missed workshops to the sponsor immediately and request that operations leadership be instructed to attend all remaining transfer sessions.
- **D**: Record every planned workshop as a video and send the full set to operations so individual team members can watch when suits best.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement approach favours direct dialogue with leadership to unblock capacity, recognising the receiving team's constraints as legitimate. (A) solves the constraint collaboratively. (B) accepts failure of the transfer in order to meet a date. (C) escalates before attempting local resolution. (D) replaces engagement with recordings nobody will watch without time to watch them.
