# Question

What is a key benefit of documenting decision rationale (why a decision was made) in addition to the decision itself?

## Options

- **A**: It creates more paperwork
- **B**: It enables future team members to understand the context and reasoning, which helps them make better decisions about whether to change the approach as circumstances evolve
- **C**: It assigns blame for bad decisions
- **D**: It is only useful for audits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Decision rationale (why, not just what) enables future understanding of context. When circumstances change, people can evaluate whether the original reasoning still applies or whether the decision should be revisited. This prevents both blindly maintaining outdated decisions and carelessly changing decisions that remain valid.

Option A views documentation as burden rather than value.

Option C frames rationale as blame rather than knowledge.

Option D understates the operational value.
