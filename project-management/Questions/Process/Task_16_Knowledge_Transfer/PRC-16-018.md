# Question

Why does PMI recommend documenting the rationale behind a decision, not just the decision itself?

## Options

- **A**: It satisfies internal audit requirements that mandate formal decision rationale be documented on every project.
- **B**: It provides a record future team members can use to assign accountability if a decision later turns out to have been wrong.
- **C**: It allows the team to charge time against a dedicated decision-rationale work package included in the baseline project plan.
- **D**: It lets future team members understand the original context, evaluate whether the reasoning still applies, and revisit the decision as circumstances change.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) captures the operational value: rationale travels with the decision and supports better future decisions. (A) overstates audit drivers as the primary reason. (B) reframes documentation as blame, which is the opposite of healthy practice. (C) is not a real PMI practice; rationale is captured in decision logs, not work packages.
