# Question

Why does PMI recommend documenting the rationale behind a decision, not just the decision itself?

## Options

- **A**: It satisfies internal audit requirements that mandate decision rationale on every project.
- **B**: It provides a record that future team members can use to assign accountability if a decision turns out badly.
- **C**: It allows the team to charge time against a decision-rationale work package in the project plan.
- **D**: It allows future team members to understand the original context, evaluate whether the reasoning still applies, and decide whether the decision should change as circumstances evolve.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) captures the operational value: rationale travels with the decision and supports better future decisions. (A) overstates audit drivers as the primary reason. (B) reframes documentation as blame, which is the opposite of healthy practice. (C) is not a real PMI practice; rationale is captured in decision logs, not work packages.
