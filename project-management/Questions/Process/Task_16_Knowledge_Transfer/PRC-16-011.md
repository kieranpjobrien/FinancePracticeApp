# Question

A new team member joins mid-project. The project has complex history and many undocumented decisions. Existing team members are too busy to provide extensive onboarding. What knowledge transfer approach would be MOST efficient?

## Options

- **A**: Give them all project documents to read
- **B**: Have them observe silently until they understand
- **C**: Create a structured onboarding that combines essential documentation review, targeted sessions with key team members on their specific areas, and assignment to a buddy/mentor for ongoing questions
- **D**: Wait until the project slows down for proper onboarding

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Efficient onboarding balances independent learning (documentation) with targeted interaction (short sessions with experts on specific topics) and ongoing support (buddy/mentor). This minimizes demands on busy team members while ensuring the new member gets what they need. Structured approach makes limited time more effective.

Option A is overwhelming and inefficient without guidance on what's important.

Option B delays productivity and may leave gaps.

Option D delays the new member's contribution indefinitely.
