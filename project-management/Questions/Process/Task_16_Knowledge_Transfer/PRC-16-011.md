# Question

A new engineer joins a project with significant history and many undocumented decisions. The existing team is too busy with delivery to provide extended onboarding. What approach is most efficient?

## Options

- **A**: Hand over the full document repository and let the new engineer self-direct through what is most relevant.
- **B**: Have them sit and observe meetings silently for the first two weeks before being given any work.
- **C**: Combine targeted documentation reading, short focused sessions with key team members on their specific areas, and assign a buddy for ongoing questions.
- **D**: Defer onboarding until the next quieter sprint when the team can dedicate time to it properly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) balances minimal demand on the existing team with effective absorption by the new engineer, which is the standard PMI onboarding pattern. (A) is overwhelming with no signal on what matters. (B) keeps a productive resource idle. (D) defers indefinitely; the quieter sprint rarely arrives.
