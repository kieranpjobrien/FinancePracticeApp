# Question

A specialist holding critical integration knowledge is being promoted in four weeks; her replacement starts six weeks from now, leaving a two-week gap. How should the project manager plan knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the two-week gap and have the replacement learn primarily from the documentation that the specialist produces before leaving.
- **B**: Stand up a comprehensive transfer plan: intensive documentation now, identify interim knowledge holders within the team, arrange limited part-time availability from the specialist during the gap, and structured onboarding for the replacement.
- **C**: Ask the specialist's new manager to delay her promotion until the replacement has started and completed onboarding.
- **D**: Outsource integration support to an external vendor for the gap period and resume internal ownership when the replacement is up to speed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The gap must be actively managed across documentation, interim coverage, residual access to the specialist, and structured onboarding for the new hire. (B) covers all of those. (A) accepts the risk without mitigation. (C) is often outside the PM's authority and may not even be possible. (D) is slow to set up and replaces internal capability with an external dependency.
