# Question

A highly specialized team member who holds critical knowledge about a custom integration is being promoted to another department. Their departure is in four weeks. A replacement has been identified but won't start for six weeks. How should the project manager plan knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Accept a two-week knowledge gap and have the replacement learn from documentation
- **B**: Request the departure be delayed until the replacement arrives
- **C**: Create a comprehensive knowledge transfer plan: intensive documentation now, identify interim knowledge holders, arrange overlap via part-time involvement or availability for questions, and structured onboarding for the replacement
- **D**: Outsource the integration support to a vendor

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The knowledge gap must be actively managed. The departing member should document extensively while available. Identifying interim knowledge holders (perhaps partially trained team members) provides coverage during the gap. Arranging for the departing member to be available for questions (even limited) bridges the gap. The replacement gets structured onboarding.

Option A accepts significant risk without mitigation.

Option B may not be possible and depends on organizational constraints.

Option D may not be feasible quickly and loses the opportunity to capture internal knowledge.
