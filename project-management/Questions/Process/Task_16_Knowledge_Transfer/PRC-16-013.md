# Question

Several agile teams across the same programme run their own retrospectives, but insights stay local. The same problems recur on neighbouring teams. What change would best improve organisational learning?

## Options

- **A**: Have every team regularly attend each other's retrospectives so retrospective insights are shared in real time across the whole programme.
- **B**: Periodically consolidate retrospective insights and share the patterns across teams via a community of practice or cross-team forum.
- **C**: Replace per-team retrospectives with a single programme-level retro so that all retrospective insights are captured together in one place.
- **D**: Keep retros confidential to each team to preserve psychological safety and avoid politicising findings across the wider programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the standard scaling pattern: keep team-level retros effective, then aggregate at the programme level for cross-team learning. (A) is impractical and dilutes individual retros. (C) loses team-specific safety and depth. (D) optimises one value (safety) at the cost of another (organisational learning) when both are achievable.
