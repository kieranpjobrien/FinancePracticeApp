# Question

An agile team conducts retrospectives every sprint but the insights aren't shared with other teams working on similar projects. What improvement would increase organizational knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Stop doing retrospectives
- **B**: Consolidate retrospective insights periodically and share relevant learnings across teams through a community of practice, shared documentation, or cross-team forums
- **C**: Have all teams attend each other's retrospectives
- **D**: Let each team keep their learnings private

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Retrospective insights have value beyond the single team. Periodic consolidation identifies patterns and broadly applicable learnings. Sharing through communities of practice, documentation, or forums enables other teams to benefit without requiring attendance at every retrospective. This scales knowledge sharing.

Option A loses the value of retrospectives.

Option C is impractical and time-consuming.

Option D misses opportunities for organizational learning.
