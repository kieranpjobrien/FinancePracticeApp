# Question

A hybrid insurance platform programme depends on a third-party vendor delivering an API by month four. The PM has drafted the integrated plan assuming on-time API delivery. The vendor has a history of slipping by four to six weeks on similar commitments. What should the PM do in planning?

## Options

- **A**: Plan for the API to arrive on the committed date and manage any slippage in execution through standard controls.
- **B**: Plan an earlier internal target date for the API and treat it as the working date across integrated plans.
- **C**: Ignore the history and trust the vendor's current commitment, since past slippage involved different projects.
- **D**: Replace the third-party vendor immediately with one that has a better on-time delivery record.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The risk of slippage belongs in the risk register with a planned response; plans should reflect committed dates while risk responses and reserves handle probability. The trap is treating risk as a planning assumption rather than a risk to monitor. (B) bakes pessimism into the integrated plan, obscuring real variance. (C) discards relevant historical signal. (D) overreacts with a late vendor change before exercising risk response planning first.
