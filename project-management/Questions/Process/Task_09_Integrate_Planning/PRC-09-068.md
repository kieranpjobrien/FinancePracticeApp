# Question

A PM planning a regulated financial services project is told by a senior executive to leave three known compliance activities out of the cost baseline so the business case looks more attractive to the board. The PM knows the activities are unavoidable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the executive's direction this once, log the concern, and raise it after board approval is granted.
- **B**: Leave the activities out of the cost baseline but list them separately as potential future adjustments.
- **C**: Include the activities in the cost baseline, explain the position clearly, and escalate if overruled.
- **D**: Submit the cost baseline without the activities and privately inform an audit partner of the discrepancy.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics requires honesty in plans and reports; omitting known compliance activities from the baseline is misrepresentation regardless of who directed it. (A) participates in misrepresentation before surfacing it. (B) hides the impact through presentation. (D) bypasses the proper escalation path and pre-empts the PM's direct transparency obligation.
