# Question

A PM starting a new customer portal project in a telco recognises that two previous portal projects at the company failed due to integration defects between engineering and operations. Lessons learned exist but have not been reviewed by the team. What should the PM do in planning?

## Options

- **A**: Focus the team on the current plan and let past lessons emerge naturally as the project progresses.
- **B**: Share lessons learned with the sponsor only and adjust the plan where the sponsor explicitly requests.
- **C**: Skip lessons learned because the current team and technology stack differ meaningfully from past portals.
- **D**: Review prior lessons learned with the team and integrate responses into the plan where relevant.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Lessons learned are an OPA input to planning; teams using prior lessons avoid repeating integration defects that have already cost the organisation. (A) discards a known information source and invites repeat failure. (B) limits reflection to sponsor level, bypassing the team that will execute. (C) rejects relevant lessons based on surface differences without examining content.
