# Question

The triple constraint concept suggests that changes to one constraint typically affect:

## Options

- **A**: Only the directly changed constraint
- **B**: One or more of the other constraints
- **C**: The project sponsor's satisfaction
- **D**: Team morale

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The triple constraint (scope, schedule, cost - sometimes including quality) shows that constraints are interrelated. Changing one typically requires adjusting others. Adding scope typically requires more time or cost. Reducing timeline may require more resources (cost) or less scope.

Option A ignores the interrelationships that make integration management necessary.

Options C and D may be affected but aren't what the triple constraint specifically addresses.
