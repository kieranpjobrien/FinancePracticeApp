# Question

The schedule includes a regulatory approval activity at 4 weeks duration; the regulator's published average is 6-8 weeks and the project has no leverage to accelerate it. Which integration issue does this most directly represent?

## Options

- **A**: A planning failure — a known external constraint was not properly reflected in the schedule and must be replanned around it.
- **B**: A risk to be added to the register and monitored as the activity approaches.
- **C**: A schedule contingency issue that can be absorbed into existing buffer.
- **D**: An opportunity to fast-track the regulator through political escalation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The duration of a known external constraint is a planning input, not a risk or contingency item. The plan needs to be updated to reflect the realistic 6-8 week window before any baseline is set. (B) treats a known fact as a probabilistic event. (C) misuses contingency, which is for unknowns. (D) presumes leverage the project does not have.
