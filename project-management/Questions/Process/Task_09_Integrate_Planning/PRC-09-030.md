# Question

A project plan requires a regulatory approval that typically takes 6-8 weeks. The schedule shows only 4 weeks for this activity. What integration issue does this represent?

## Options

- **A**: Optimistic scheduling that should be flagged as a risk
- **B**: A constraint that wasn't properly considered in planning
- **C**: Normal variation that scheduling contingency should cover
- **D**: An opportunity to fast-track the regulatory process

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The plan doesn't reflect a known constraint. Regulatory processes have defined timelines that can't be unilaterally shortened. The schedule should be built around this constraint, not ignore it. This is a planning integration failure.

Option A understates the issue. It's not optimism; it's ignoring reality.

Option C misapplies contingency. Contingency is for unknowns, not for known constraints that weren't planned for.

Option D is generally not possible. Regulatory timelines are typically fixed and outside project control.
