# Question

A hybrid energy transition programme has three workstream plans that contradict each other on resource allocation for Q3. Each workstream lead insists their own plan is correct. The PM is drafting the integrated project management plan. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a joint working session to reconcile conflicts and produce a single coherent integrated plan.
- **B**: Build the integrated plan from the plan with the highest estimated business value of the three.
- **C**: Submit all three plans to the steering committee and ask them to choose a reconciled version.
- **D**: Average the three resource allocations across Q3 so no workstream lead feels particularly overruled.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integration happens through facilitated reconciliation so the integrated plan is coherent and owned by the workstreams it governs. (B) privileges one workstream without attempting integration. (C) escalates before the PM has exercised their integration accountability. (D) produces a mechanical average that nobody believes and that nobody can execute against.
