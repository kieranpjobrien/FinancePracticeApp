# Question

On a hybrid industrial IoT project, the procurement plan requires vendor selection before detailed design begins, but the schedule shows design starting in four weeks and procurement taking twelve. The two subsidiary plans have not been reconciled. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Start design on schedule with a preferred vendor assumption and select formally as design progresses.
- **B**: Delay design until procurement completes, even though it will push the programme milestone by eight weeks.
- **C**: Adjust sequencing and timing across the plans so design inputs arrive when the design phase requires them.
- **D**: Raise the inconsistency as an issue and ask the sponsor which of the two plans should be considered authoritative.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated planning reconciles timing across subsidiary plans so sequencing supports value delivery; this is a core integration responsibility. (A) introduces a hidden assumption that could force rework if the selected vendor differs. (B) imposes a mechanical wait without exploring compression options. (D) escalates a reconciliation the PM should perform before seeking governance input.
