# Question

A project plan shows aggressive timelines that the team privately believes are unrealistic. The sponsor is committed to the dates. The PM is concerned that the plan lacks integrity. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the timeline since the sponsor has authority over dates
- **B**: Document the team's concerns as risks and proceed with the aggressive plan
- **C**: Present data showing why timelines are unrealistic and propose alternatives
- **D**: Pad estimates secretly to create buffer for the unrealistic timeline

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A plan built on unrealistic assumptions will fail. The PM's professional responsibility is to provide honest assessments. Presenting data (historical velocity, resource availability, complexity analysis) gives the sponsor information to make better decisions or accept risks knowingly.

Option A creates a plan the PM doesn't believe in, setting the project up for failure.

Option B documents concerns but proceeds with a known-bad plan. Risks should inform the plan, not just be noted alongside it.

Option D is unethical. Hidden padding obscures real issues and undermines trust.
