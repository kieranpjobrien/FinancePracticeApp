# Question

The team has privately told the PM the dates in the draft schedule are unrealistic. The sponsor is publicly committed to those dates. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Present an evidence-based analysis to the sponsor — historical velocity, capacity data, complexity assessment — and propose alternative scope/time/cost combinations.
- **B**: Accept the dates and document the team's concerns as risks in the register so accountability is clear.
- **C**: Add hidden contingency to internal estimates without changing the public schedule.
- **D**: Defer to the sponsor's authority on dates and proceed as planned.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM's professional duty under the PMI Code of Ethics is honesty in reporting. Bringing data and options to the sponsor respects their authority while honouring the truth. (B) records concerns but baselines a plan no one believes. (C) is unethical; covert padding distorts decisions for everyone downstream. (D) is silence in the face of a known problem.
