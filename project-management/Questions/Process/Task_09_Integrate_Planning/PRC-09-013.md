# Question

The risk management plan identifies need for weekly risk reviews, but the communications plan only schedules monthly stakeholder updates. A risk owner needs to communicate urgent risk status. What integration issue does this reveal?

## Options

- **A**: The risk management plan is too aggressive
- **B**: The communications plan doesn't align with risk management needs
- **C**: Risk owners should communicate independently of the communications plan
- **D**: Monthly updates are sufficient for stakeholder communication

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Subsidiary plans must be integrated. If risk management requires frequent status updates but communications only plans for monthly updates, there's a gap. The communications plan should include provisions for risk-related communications at the frequency risk management requires.

Option A assumes monthly is the right frequency without justification.

Option C creates ad-hoc communications outside the planned approach, which can lead to inconsistency.

Option D ignores the stated risk management requirement. The plans need alignment.
