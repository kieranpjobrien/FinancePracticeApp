# Question

A PM is using historical organisational process assets from three previous projects of similar type. The data shows those projects underestimated effort by 25-30% on average. How should this inform the current plan?

## Options

- **A**: Use the historical estimates unmodified, on the principle that past data is the best baseline available.
- **B**: Discard the historical data because each project is unique and the comparison may not hold.
- **C**: Apply a documented correction factor calibrated to the historical underestimation pattern, and note the assumption explicitly in the plan.
- **D**: Double every estimate to provide an obvious safety buffer.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Historical data is most useful when its biases are known. Applying a calibrated, documented adjustment is evidence-based estimation. (A) reproduces the known error. (B) wastes a real organisational learning. (D) over-corrects without basis and obscures real productivity.
