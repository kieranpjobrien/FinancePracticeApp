# Question

After three sprints, velocity data shows the team is delivering at 60% of the rate the original release plan assumed. The release date is now clearly unachievable at the current scope. When should the PM trigger plan revision?

## Options

- **A**: Now — three sprints is credible empirical data; analyse options and engage stakeholders promptly.
- **B**: After three more sprints, to confirm whether the downward trend persists across more data points.
- **C**: At the next quarterly portfolio review where re-planning is normally performed for the programme.
- **D**: Only when stakeholders or the sponsor raise concerns about the achievability of the release date.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empirical agile planning means acting on the data you have. Three sprints is plenty to expose a structural gap; waiting compounds the cost. (B) delays for confirmation that the data already provides. (C) ties replanning to a calendar event when the issue is real now. (D) waits passively for someone else to notice and erodes trust.
