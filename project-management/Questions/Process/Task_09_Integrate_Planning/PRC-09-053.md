# Question

While reviewing the draft project management plan for a water utility project, the PM notices the team has embedded seven critical assumptions inside schedule notes rather than a dedicated assumption log. Some assumptions contradict each other. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the assumptions where they are, since schedule notes are read by the team that needs them.
- **B**: Delete the assumptions entirely to clean up the schedule before distributing the plan for approval.
- **C**: Ask the team lead to reconcile the contradictions and leave them in schedule notes for convenience.
- **D**: Consolidate assumptions into a proper log, reconcile contradictions, and reflect them in the plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

An assumption log is a core planning artifact that must be complete, reconciled, and integrated with the plan for risk and decision transparency. (A) hides assumptions in notes that governance forums never see. (B) erases risk context without understanding or reconciling it. (C) reconciles the content but leaves it in the wrong artifact, failing integration.
