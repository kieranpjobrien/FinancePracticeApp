# Question

A PM newly assigned to a global payroll consolidation project inherits a project charter and a more detailed scope statement that disagree on key deliverable boundaries. The sponsor has moved on, and the new sponsor is unsure which document governs. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Finalise planning based on the charter, as the charter is the higher-order authorising document by convention.
- **B**: Facilitate reconciliation between the charter and scope statement with the new sponsor before planning proceeds.
- **C**: Choose whichever boundary is simpler to execute against, document the choice, and proceed with planning.
- **D**: Plan to both definitions and let execution reveal which boundary the organisation actually needs and wants.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Planning integration requires a coherent authorising baseline; reconciliation with the current sponsor resolves authority ambiguity before plans deepen. (A) defaults to the charter without confirming the new sponsor's intent, missing the specific clarification step. (C) picks a boundary for convenience without stakeholder alignment. (D) plans against both, guaranteeing rework once reality forces a choice.
