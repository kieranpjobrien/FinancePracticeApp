# Question

A PM has completed individual plans for scope, schedule, cost, and quality. When reviewing them together, inconsistencies emerge - the quality plan requires testing resources the resource plan doesn't include. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the resource plan to include the testing resources
- **B**: Reduce quality activities to match available resources
- **C**: Conduct an integrated review to identify and resolve all cross-plan inconsistencies
- **D**: Escalate the resource gap to the sponsor for additional funding

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Individual plans must work together as an integrated whole. If one inconsistency exists, others likely do too. An integrated review examines how plans interact and resolves conflicts before they become execution problems. This is the essence of integration management.

Option A fixes one symptom without systematic review. Other inconsistencies may remain.

Option B trades off quality without exploring all options or stakeholder input.

Option D escalates prematurely. The PM should understand the full picture before requesting changes.
