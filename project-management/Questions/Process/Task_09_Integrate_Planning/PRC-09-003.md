# Question

A project manager is developing the project management plan. The schedule shows the project completing in 10 months, but the resource plan only allocates team members for 8 months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Submit the project plan and address resource gaps during execution
- **B**: Extend the resource allocation to 10 months without adjusting the budget
- **C**: Reconcile the schedule and resource plans, then update the budget baseline accordingly
- **D**: Reduce scope to fit within the 8-month resource window

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Integrated planning means ensuring all subsidiary plans (schedule, resources, budget, scope) are aligned and consistent. A 2-month gap between schedule and resource availability is a planning conflict that must be resolved before baselining. The PM should determine the correct approach (extend resources, compress schedule, or both) and update all affected plans including budget.

Option A (plan and fix later) creates an unrealistic baseline. Starting execution with known conflicts undermines the purpose of planning.

Option B (extend resources without budget) is wishful thinking. Resources cost money - extending allocation without budget adjustment creates a cost overrun from day one.

Option D (reduce scope) may be valid but shouldn't be the default response without analysing other options first.
