# Question

The cost baseline was set on optimistic assumptions. Mid-execution, actuals are running 20% over baseline. The PM is considering re-baselining. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Re-baseline immediately so reported variance returns to normal against the new cost baseline.
- **B**: Hold the original baseline to maintain accountability and use variance reporting to show the impact transparently.
- **C**: Maintain a public baseline for external reporting and a private working baseline for the team's internal use.
- **D**: Diagnose whether the variance reflects approved scope change (where re-baselining applies) or performance; act accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Re-baselining is a controlled, exception-only action triggered by approved scope or condition changes — not by under-performance against an agreed plan. The PM must distinguish the two before acting. (A) hides poor performance. (B) is sometimes correct but is the answer only after diagnosis. (C) is dual-track reporting and unethical.
