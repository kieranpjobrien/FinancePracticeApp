# Question

A project's cost baseline was set based on optimistic assumptions. Midway through, actual costs are 20% over baseline. The PM wants to re-baseline to reflect reality. What should the PM consider?

## Options

- **A**: Re-baseline to show accurate performance metrics
- **B**: Keep the baseline to maintain accountability for the original commitment
- **C**: Evaluate whether conditions justify re-baselining or whether the variance represents true performance issues
- **D**: Create a second baseline for internal use while keeping the original for stakeholder reporting

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Re-baselining isn't automatic. The PM should determine if the variance is due to planning errors (assumptions were wrong) versus execution issues (work is less efficient than planned). Legitimate baseline changes require approval and should reflect changed scope or circumstances, not just mask poor performance.

Option A uses re-baselining to hide problems rather than address them.

Option B may perpetuate comparison against a known-invalid baseline.

Option D creates dual-track reporting that's confusing and potentially dishonest.
