# Question

The cost baseline was set on optimistic assumptions. Mid-execution, actuals are running 20% over baseline. The PM is considering re-baselining. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Re-baseline immediately so reported variance returns to normal.
- **B**: Hold the original baseline to maintain accountability and use variance reporting to show the impact.
- **C**: Maintain a public baseline for reporting and a private working baseline for the team.
- **D**: Diagnose whether the variance reflects an approved scope/scope-change basis (where re-baselining via change control may be appropriate) or simply poor performance against the agreed plan, and take only the action the diagnosis supports.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Re-baselining is a controlled, exception-only action triggered by approved scope or condition changes — not by under-performance against an agreed plan. The PM must distinguish the two before acting. (A) hides poor performance. (B) is sometimes correct but is the answer only after diagnosis. (C) is dual-track reporting and unethical.
