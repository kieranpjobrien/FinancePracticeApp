# Question

A PM on a health insurance claims modernisation has drafted the project management plan working largely alone to save time. The team will see it at the next meeting. A colleague warns that plans not co-created often fail to gain team commitment. What should the PM do now?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed as planned because redrafting the plan collaboratively will cost time the schedule cannot afford.
- **B**: Circulate the draft for team review and incorporate feedback before finalising and baselining it.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to mandate team adherence to the plan at its introduction to the whole team.
- **D**: Split the plan by discipline and have each specialist review only the section relevant to their discipline.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Plan integration with the team produces commitment and uncovers gaps; PMI emphasises team involvement in planning rather than directive delivery. (A) optimises for speed at the cost of buy-in and plan accuracy. (C) substitutes authority for commitment and signals distrust in the team. (D) fragments review so no one reviews the integrated plan as a whole.
