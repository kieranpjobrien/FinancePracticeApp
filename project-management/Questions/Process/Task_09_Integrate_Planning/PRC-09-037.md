# Question

A government project must comply with three regulatory frameworks whose requirements overlap but are worded differently. Different team members are interpreting the requirements differently in their workstreams. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Build a compliance traceability matrix mapping each requirement to one agreed approach and conformance evidence.
- **B**: Adopt the strictest interpretation across the board to stay safe under any auditor's review.
- **C**: Allow each workstream to maintain its own interpretation and reconcile during the external audit phase.
- **D**: Outsource all compliance interpretation to an external specialist firm with regulatory expertise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A compliance traceability matrix is the canonical integration artefact for overlapping frameworks: one agreed approach, evidence specified, audit-ready. (B) trades clarity for blanket strictness and may be uneconomic. (C) delays the integration problem until it is most expensive. (D) abdicates judgement that the project must own.
