# Question

A PM drafting the project management plan for a customer loyalty initiative cannot find the benefits realisation plan or a current benefits register. The business case is two years old and its benefits estimates look stale. What should the PM do before finalising the plan?

## Options

- **A**: Finalise the plan using the two-year-old benefits estimates rather than delaying the planning phase.
- **B**: Request an updated benefits register and align the plan to current benefit assumptions and targets.
- **C**: Remove benefits references from the plan entirely and focus on scope, schedule, and cost integration.
- **D**: Assume standard industry benefits uplift percentages where the existing data appears to be stale.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Integrated planning requires current benefits context so the plan supports real value delivery, not just output delivery. (A) plans against stale value assumptions that may no longer justify the investment. (C) strips the value thread from the plan, leaving outputs without outcome anchors. (D) fabricates benefits data rather than asking the business for a current view.
