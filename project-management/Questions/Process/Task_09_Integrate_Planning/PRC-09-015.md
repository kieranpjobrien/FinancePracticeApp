# Question

A PM is tailoring the project management approach for a small, low-risk internal project. The organisation's PMO requires all 10 knowledge area plans. The PM believes this is excessive. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Comply with PMO requirements since they are organisational standards
- **B**: Skip unnecessary plans and explain later if questioned
- **C**: Propose tailored, simplified plans that address each knowledge area proportionate to project needs
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to override PMO requirements

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Tailoring applies processes proportionate to project characteristics. Rather than skipping areas or creating full plans for simple projects, propose appropriately scaled documentation. A risk plan for a low-risk project might be one page rather than twenty, still addressing the knowledge area.

Option A applies standards without tailoring, which wastes effort on bureaucracy.

Option B ignores governance without proper justification or approval.

Option D creates conflict without first proposing a reasonable alternative.
