# Question

A PM is launching a small, low-risk internal project. The PMO requires a complete plan covering all 10 knowledge areas. The PM thinks the standard template is excessive for the scale. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tailor the plan: address every knowledge area but at depth proportionate to project size, risk, and complexity, and confirm the tailoring with the PMO.
- **B**: Comply fully with the PMO template to avoid friction with governance.
- **C**: Skip the plans the PM judges unnecessary and explain afterwards if challenged.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to override the PMO requirement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tailoring is a PMI principle; the right move is to adapt depth to context while still covering each knowledge area, and to do so transparently with the PMO. (B) ignores tailoring and wastes effort. (C) sidesteps governance unilaterally. (D) escalates a methodological decision rather than agreeing it through normal governance.
