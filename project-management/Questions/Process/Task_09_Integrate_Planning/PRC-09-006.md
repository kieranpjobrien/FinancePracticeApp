# Question

A project manager discovers that the communications plan specifies weekly stakeholder meetings, but the schedule doesn't allocate time for meeting preparation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel the stakeholder meetings to save time
- **B**: Expect team members to prepare in their own time
- **C**: Reconcile the plans by adding preparation time to the schedule
- **D**: Proceed without preparation and see what happens

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Integrated planning means subsidiary plans align with each other. If communications require preparation effort, the schedule must reflect that work. The PM should update the schedule to include realistic preparation time, ensuring the project plan is internally consistent and executable.

Option A (cancel meetings) solves the conflict by abandoning stakeholder communication, which creates other problems.

Option B (personal time) is unrealistic and unsustainable. Required work should be in the schedule.

Option D (proceed unprepared) produces poor-quality stakeholder communications and reflects badly on the project.
