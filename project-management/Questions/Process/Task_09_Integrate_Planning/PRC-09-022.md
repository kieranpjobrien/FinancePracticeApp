# Question

When is the project management plan typically baselined?

## Options

- **A**: After detailed planning is sufficiently complete and approved, before controlled execution begins.
- **B**: At the moment the project charter is signed.
- **C**: At the end of the first execution phase or sprint.
- **D**: Whenever the sponsor requests a baseline.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The plan is baselined when planning is detailed enough to support measurement and control during execution. (B) is too early — the charter authorises planning, not execution. (C) is too late — there's no baseline against which the first phase was measured. (D) makes baselining ad hoc rather than methodology-driven.
