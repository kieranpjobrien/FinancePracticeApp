# Question

A hybrid project has agile development teams and predictive infrastructure teams. The agile teams plan in sprints; infrastructure teams plan in phases with milestones. How should the PM integrate these approaches?

## Options

- **A**: Require all teams to use the same planning approach for consistency
- **B**: Let each team plan independently and integrate only at major milestones
- **C**: Create a master plan showing dependencies between sprints and infrastructure phases with regular integration points
- **D**: Have the agile teams adapt their sprint planning to infrastructure phase gates

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Hybrid projects require integration planning that respects different methodologies while ensuring coordination. The master plan should show how agile sprints align with infrastructure phases, where dependencies exist, and when integration or handoffs must occur.

Option A forces inappropriate methodology on one or both teams, reducing effectiveness.

Option B risks misalignment. Dependencies need more frequent coordination than major milestones.

Option D one-sidedly adapts agile to predictive without considering whether infrastructure could also flex.
