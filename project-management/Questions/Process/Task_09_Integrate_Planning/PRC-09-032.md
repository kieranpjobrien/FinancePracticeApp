# Question

A PM is creating plans for a complex project with many unknowns. Some stakeholders want detailed plans; others say detailed planning is waste given the uncertainty. What approach balances these views?

## Options

- **A**: Create detailed plans to satisfy stakeholders who want them
- **B**: Keep plans high-level and detail them as execution reveals more information
- **C**: Create detailed near-term plans and progressively detailed longer-term plans as uncertainty reduces
- **D**: Let each stakeholder group have the level of detail they prefer

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Rolling wave planning provides detail where it's meaningful (near-term, lower uncertainty) and avoids false precision where it isn't (long-term, higher uncertainty). This approach respects both the reality of uncertainty and stakeholders' legitimate need for planning.

Option A creates detailed plans that may be wrong, wasting effort and setting false expectations.

Option B may leave stakeholders without sufficient information for near-term decisions.

Option D creates inconsistent planning that's hard to integrate and compare.
