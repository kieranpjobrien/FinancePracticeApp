# Question

An approved scope change on a hybrid transport project adds three weeks of schedule impact and seventy thousand in cost impact. The PM has updated the schedule and cost baselines. The risk register, communications plan, and resource plan have not yet been reviewed. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct integrated impact analysis across all subsidiary plans and update the ones genuinely affected.
- **B**: Update only the schedule and cost baselines, as those are the documents that sponsors actually monitor.
- **C**: Inform the steering committee of the change and request they identify other plans requiring updates.
- **D**: Wait until the next quarterly plan review to assess whether other subsidiary plans need updating now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrated change control requires impact assessment across all subsidiary plans, not just the primary baselines. (B) leaves affected subsidiary plans stale and creates integration defects downstream. (C) offloads the integration assessment the PM is accountable for performing. (D) defers required updates past the point they should inform execution decisions.
