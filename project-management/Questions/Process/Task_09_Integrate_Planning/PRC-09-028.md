# Question

A PM notices that different functional managers have provided resource estimates using different assumptions. Some assume full-time allocation; others assume shared resources. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Use the most conservative estimates to be safe
- **B**: Average the estimates to balance different approaches
- **C**: Establish common assumptions and have managers re-estimate consistently
- **D**: Accept the estimates and manage resource conflicts during execution

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Estimates based on different assumptions can't be meaningfully combined. The PM should establish common planning assumptions (allocation percentages, availability hours, calendar assumptions) and have managers re-estimate on a consistent basis. This creates an integrated, reliable plan.

Option A doesn't resolve the inconsistency and may create an overly pessimistic plan.

Option B creates a mathematically combined but meaningless number from incompatible inputs.

Option D defers resolution of a planning problem to execution, where it becomes harder to fix.
