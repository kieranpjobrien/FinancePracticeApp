# Question

Two stakeholder groups have different communication expectations that are difficult to reconcile - one wants transparency with full details, the other wants streamlined executive summaries only. Creating both is time-consuming. How should the PM resolve this?

## Options

- **A**: Create one format and tell everyone to accept it
- **B**: Continue creating both formats despite the burden
- **C**: Create a tiered communication approach with summary at top and drill-down detail for those who want it, meeting both needs efficiently
- **D**: Stop communicating with one group

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Tiered communication serves multiple audiences efficiently. Executive summary at top: key points, status, decisions needed. Detailed sections below: for those who want depth. Appendices/links: full detail for deep dives. One document, multiple consumption patterns. The detail-oriented get what they need without forcing executives to wade through it.

Option A doesn't meet both groups' legitimate needs.

Option B is inefficient when a combined approach could work.

Option D neglects important stakeholders.
