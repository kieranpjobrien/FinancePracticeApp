# Question

Two stakeholder groups have incompatible appetite for project information. Operations wants exhaustive detail; the executive team wants only a one-page summary. The PM is producing two separate reports each week and finding it unsustainable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Force a single shared format that both audiences must accept to reduce the PM's reporting overhead.
- **B**: Continue producing both reports as they are, since the audiences are too different to meaningfully combine.
- **C**: Survey both groups on which content can be cut and trim each report to the minimum each will accept.
- **D**: Restructure to a single layered document — exec summary up front, detail sections behind, appendices for deep dives.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tiered or layered communication is the standard PMI pattern for serving distinct audiences efficiently — one source of truth, multiple depths of consumption. (D) is the durable fix. (A) ignores legitimate differences and will be rejected. (B) accepts an unsustainable workload that risks the PM's other work. (C) trims volume but keeps the duplicated structure.
