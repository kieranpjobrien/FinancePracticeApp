# Question

A major incident has occurred on a live system built by a previous project the PM managed. The current operations owner is handling response. Several senior stakeholders are messaging the PM directly asking for updates because they know her from the original project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the stakeholders with what the PM can gather from the operations channel to meet their information needs quickly.
- **B**: Decline to comment and redirect stakeholders entirely to the operations owner so a single source of truth is maintained.
- **C**: Offer to help triage stakeholder questions in support of the operations owner, clearly communicating the current ownership.
- **D**: Take over incident communications on behalf of the operations owner since the PM has stronger relationships with the asking stakeholders.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

During a live incident there needs to be a single accountable comms owner, but existing relationships can support them. (C) offers help without taking over. (A) parallel communication from a non-owner creates confusion and conflicting versions. (B) a cold redirect wastes a useful relationship asset during a crisis. (D) taking over undermines the current accountable owner and fragments command-and-control.
