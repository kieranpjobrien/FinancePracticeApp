# Question

The PM must communicate a four-week schedule slip to the steering committee. The cause is over-optimistic original estimates. The committee chair is known to react sharply to bad news. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Lead with what's been delivered, mention the slip briefly toward the end, and move quickly to next steps.
- **B**: Send the news in a written status update before the meeting so the chair has time to process privately, and answer questions live.
- **C**: Open with the slip, the root-cause analysis, the revised forecast, and proposed recovery options, taking ownership of the situation.
- **D**: Defer the conversation by one cycle to first develop a recovery plan that mostly closes the gap, then present a smaller delta.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's communication principle is transparency, ownership, and forward-looking analysis — even when the audience is uncomfortable. (C) does all three and treats the chair as a decision-maker, not a recipient. (A) is bury-the-lede; the chair will sense it. (B) is reasonable on cadence but moves the moment of accountability rather than handling it. (D) delays disclosure of material status, which violates the PMI Code of Ethics on integrity.
