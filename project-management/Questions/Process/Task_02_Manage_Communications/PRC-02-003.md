# Question

A project manager must communicate a significant schedule delay to stakeholders. The delay is due to discovering that original estimates were overly optimistic. Senior stakeholders have previously reacted negatively to bad news. What communication approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Minimize the news by focusing on positive aspects and progress made
- **B**: Present the delay with full transparency, including root cause analysis, revised forecast, and proposed recovery actions
- **C**: Share the news in writing to avoid face-to-face confrontation
- **D**: Wait until there's better news to balance the negative

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Professional integrity requires transparent communication of project status. Presenting root cause analysis shows understanding, revised forecast sets realistic expectations, and recovery actions show proactive management. This builds long-term credibility even if short-term reception is negative.

Option A is dishonest by omission and delays the inevitable. When the truth emerges, trust is damaged.

Option C avoids dialogue that could clarify concerns and appears evasive. Difficult messages warrant direct conversation.

Option D delays addressing reality, which may make the eventual news worse and damages credibility.
