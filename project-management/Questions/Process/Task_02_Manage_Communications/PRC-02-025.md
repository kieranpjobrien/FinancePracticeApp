# Question

A technical team member communicates in highly technical language that business stakeholders don't understand. Important messages are being lost in translation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let stakeholders figure out the technical terms
- **B**: Coach the team member on audience-appropriate communication, offer to help translate technical content for business audiences, or establish yourself as a translation bridge
- **C**: Remove the team member from all stakeholder communication
- **D**: Tell stakeholders to take technical courses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective communication requires audience-appropriate language. Coach the team member: help them understand stakeholder perspective and how to explain technical concepts accessibly. Offer support: perhaps review their communications before sending. Bridge: serve as translator when technical and business worlds need to connect. These approaches develop capability while ensuring effective communication.

Option A lets important information be lost.

Option C isolates technical expertise from stakeholders who need to understand it.

Option D places unreasonable burden on stakeholders.
