# Question

Two stakeholders have received conflicting information about project status from different team members. Both are confused and frustrated. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Blame the team members who gave conflicting information
- **B**: Clarify the correct information with both stakeholders, then establish clear communication channels and single points of contact for key information to prevent recurrence
- **C**: Let stakeholders figure out which information is correct
- **D**: Stop all team communication with stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. First, fix the immediate issue: clarify correct information with affected stakeholders. Then address the systemic cause: conflicting messages typically indicate unclear communication responsibilities. Establish who communicates what to whom - single points of contact for key topics prevent contradictory messages.

Option A focuses on blame rather than resolution.

Option C leaves stakeholders confused and damages credibility.

Option D is excessive and isolates stakeholders from the team.
