# Question

Two stakeholders contact the PM separately, frustrated because they've received contradictory accounts of the project's go-live date from different team members. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a single short call with both stakeholders to confirm the actual date and explain how it was set, then update the comms plan to define a single source of truth and a single voice for status.
- **B**: Issue a formal correction memo to the full distribution list clarifying the date and reaffirming the official channels.
- **C**: Identify which team member gave the incorrect date and ask them to apologise to the affected stakeholders.
- **D**: Restrict all stakeholder communication to the PM personally to prevent further inconsistencies.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Fix the immediate confusion, then fix the system that produced it — that's the PMI sequence. (A) does both with minimal disruption. (B) over-broadcasts a clarification to people who don't need it. (C) focuses on individual blame rather than the structural cause (no single source of truth). (D) is a heavy-handed control that bottlenecks communication and signals distrust of the team.
