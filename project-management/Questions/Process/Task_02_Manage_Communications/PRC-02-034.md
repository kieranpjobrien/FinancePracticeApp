# Question

The agile team is pushing back on regulatory documentation requirements, citing the Agile Manifesto's preference for working software over comprehensive documentation. The regulator requires specific artefacts in specific formats. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the regulatory documentation as non-negotiable, work with the team to integrate the artefacts into normal definition-of-done where possible, and explain the business and legal context.
- **B**: Defer the documentation work to a separate hardening sprint at the end of the release so the team can focus on features now.
- **C**: Create a parallel documentation team to produce the regulatory artefacts so the development team isn't burdened.
- **D**: Engage the regulator to discuss whether the documentation requirements can be relaxed for agile delivery.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Regulatory artefacts are constraints, and the PMI move is to integrate them into the normal flow rather than separate them or try to renegotiate them. (A) does both — making the requirement non-negotiable while reducing friction. (B) defers compliance work, creating end-of-release risk. (C) splits responsibility from the people who produce the underlying work, which causes accuracy gaps. (D) is rarely productive and treats the constraint as flexible when it isn't.
