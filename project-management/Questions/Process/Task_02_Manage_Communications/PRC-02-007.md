# Question

A project manager sends a detailed technical email to a non-technical executive sponsor explaining why a key milestone will be missed. The sponsor responds asking for a simpler explanation. What communication principle did the PM violate?

## Options

- **A**: Frequency of communication
- **B**: Tailoring communication to the audience
- **C**: Using the wrong medium
- **D**: Timing of communication

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective communication requires adapting the message to the audience's needs and knowledge level. Technical details appropriate for engineers may be inappropriate for executives who need business impact and action items. The PM should have translated technical issues into business terms.

Option A (frequency) isn't the issue - a single email about a missed milestone is appropriate frequency.

Option C (medium) is secondary. Email may be fine; the issue is the content, not the channel.

Option D (timing) isn't indicated as a problem. Communicating about a milestone issue proactively is good practice.
