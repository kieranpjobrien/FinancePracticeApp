# Question

The PM emails the executive sponsor a detailed technical write-up explaining why a milestone will slip — including stack traces and architecture references. The sponsor replies asking what it means for the business and what action they need to take. Which communication principle did the PM most clearly miss?

## Options

- **A**: Tailoring the message to the audience's role and information needs.
- **B**: Choosing the right communication frequency for the recipient.
- **C**: Selecting an appropriate communication medium for the topic.
- **D**: Communicating in a timely manner relative to the event.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM produced an accurate message in the wrong language for the audience — the sponsor needs business impact and decisions, not technical detail. (A) is the missed principle. (B) frequency wasn't the issue — a single, timely message is appropriate. (C) email is reasonable for written context with attachments; the medium isn't the problem. (D) the message was timely; the content was wrong for the audience.
