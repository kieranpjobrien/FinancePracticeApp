# Question

Which of the following best describes active listening?

## Options

- **A**: Taking detailed notes during a conversation so the speaker's points can be reviewed later.
- **B**: Giving the speaker your full attention, reflecting back what you heard, and confirming understanding before responding.
- **C**: Mentally preparing your response while the other person is still speaking so you can answer immediately.
- **D**: Allowing the speaker to talk uninterrupted from start to finish without offering any reaction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Active listening is defined by attention, reflection, and confirmation — not just silence or note-taking. (B) captures the technique. (A) is note-taking; valuable but not the same skill. (C) is the opposite of active listening — splitting attention to compose a reply. (D) describes silence, which can mask poor comprehension; active listening checks understanding.
