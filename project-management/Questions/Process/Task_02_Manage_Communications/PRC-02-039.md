# Question

A project decision was made in a meeting, but there's now disagreement about what was actually decided. Different attendees have different recollections. How should the PM prevent this in the future?

## Options

- **A**: Record all meetings for legal review
- **B**: Establish clear practices for documenting decisions: capture them in meeting notes, share promptly, and request confirmation or corrections
- **C**: Only make decisions via email
- **D**: Let whoever remembers loudest determine what was decided

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Decisions need documentation. Capture: note decisions explicitly in meeting minutes with clear wording. Share promptly: distribute notes soon after the meeting while memory is fresh. Confirm: ask attendees to confirm accuracy or flag issues. This creates a shared record and catches disagreements early when they're easier to resolve.

Option A is excessive and doesn't address documentation of decisions.

Option C eliminates valuable real-time discussion.

Option D lets power dynamics override actual decisions.
