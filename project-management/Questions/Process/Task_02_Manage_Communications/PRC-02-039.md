# Question

A decision was made in last week's design meeting, but two attendees are now arguing about what was actually decided. What should the PM do to prevent this in future?

## Options

- **A**: Audio-record all project meetings going forward so disputed decisions can be replayed.
- **B**: Capture each decision explicitly in the meeting minutes (decision, owner, date), distribute the minutes within 24 hours, and ask attendees to confirm or correct within an agreed window.
- **C**: Require all decisions to be made by written email vote rather than in meetings.
- **D**: Have the PM personally send a decision-of-the-day email after every meeting summarising what was decided.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The standard PMI artefact for this is documented minutes with explicit decision capture and confirmation. (B) is the lightest, most durable solution. (A) is heavyweight, has consent issues, and doesn't replace structured decision capture. (C) eliminates the value of synchronous discussion. (D) creates a single bottleneck and doesn't standardise the format.
