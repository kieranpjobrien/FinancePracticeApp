# Question

Through a colleague at a coffee chat, the PM hears that a key business stakeholder has been telling other executives they're frustrated with the project's direction. The stakeholder has not raised any concerns in formal meetings. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the stakeholder to raise their concerns through the proper governance channel before acting on second-hand information.
- **B**: Add the topic to the next steering committee agenda so the concerns are surfaced in front of the right audience.
- **C**: Inform the project sponsor of the rumoured concerns so they can address them at the executive level.
- **D**: Request a brief one-on-one with the stakeholder to check in on the project and listen for their perspective.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PMI default is to engage stakeholders directly and privately first, especially when concerns are still informal. (D) gives the stakeholder a safe space to share, builds the relationship, and verifies whether the rumour is accurate. (A) is passive and lets the issue grow. (B) ambushes the stakeholder publicly with concerns they haven't formally raised. (C) routes around the stakeholder before any direct conversation has been attempted, which usually damages trust.
