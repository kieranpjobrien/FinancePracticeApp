# Question

A project manager learns through informal channels that a key stakeholder is unhappy with project direction, though they haven't raised concerns in formal meetings. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the stakeholder to raise their concerns formally
- **B**: Raise the rumored concerns in the next stakeholder meeting to address them openly
- **C**: Have a private conversation with the stakeholder to understand their perspective and address concerns directly
- **D**: Inform the project sponsor about the stakeholder's potential resistance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Proactive, direct communication is more effective than waiting for formal channels or exposing the stakeholder publicly. A private conversation allows the stakeholder to speak freely about their concerns and demonstrates the PM's genuine interest in their input. This builds trust and may resolve the issue before it escalates.

Option A is passive and allows concerns to fester, potentially leading to larger problems or active resistance later.

Option B may embarrass the stakeholder or put them on the defensive. Concerns they haven't chosen to raise publicly shouldn't be surfaced that way.

Option D involves others before the PM has directly engaged. This may appear to be going over the stakeholder's head and could damage the relationship.
