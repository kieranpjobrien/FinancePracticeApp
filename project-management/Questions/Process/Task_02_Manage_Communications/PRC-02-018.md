# Question

The executive sponsor rarely opens project status reports. After a recent decision was made without their input, they complained that they "had no idea this was happening." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue sending the same reports — the sponsor's failure to engage with information they receive is their own responsibility.
- **B**: Add the sponsor to all working group meetings so they have ample opportunity to engage with project detail.
- **C**: Discuss format with the sponsor, switch to a one-page exec summary, and pre-flag any decision needing their input.
- **D**: Escalate the gap to the sponsor's executive assistant and ask them to flag project communications as priority email.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM owns adapting communication to the audience, even (especially) for executive sponsors. (C) tailors format and creates explicit decision triggers — the structural fix. (A) is technically defensible but accepts a known dysfunction the PM can solve. (B) overwhelms an exec who clearly doesn't want operational depth. (D) outsources a problem the PM should handle directly with the sponsor.
