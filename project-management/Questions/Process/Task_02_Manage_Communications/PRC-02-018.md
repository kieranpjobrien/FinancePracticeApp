# Question

A key executive sponsor rarely engages with project communications but later complains about decisions made without their input. They claim they didn't know. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that it's not possible to engage busy executives
- **B**: Schedule a direct conversation to understand their communication preferences, adjust approach to match their style (executive summaries, brief verbal updates), and establish escalation triggers for decisions requiring their input
- **C**: Include them in every detailed communication
- **D**: Stop including them in any communications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Busy executives need right-sized communication. Understand their preferences: perhaps they want brief verbal updates rather than written reports. Adjust approach: executive summaries, key decisions requiring their input flagged clearly. Establish triggers: what decisions require their input before proceeding? This ensures appropriate engagement without overwhelming them.

Option A accepts dysfunction without attempting to solve it.

Option C will likely be ignored and increases noise.

Option D removes them entirely when adjusted communication could work.
