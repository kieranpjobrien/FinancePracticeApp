# Question

A team distributed across Sydney, London, and San Francisco complains that synchronous meetings consistently fall outside two of the three regions' working hours. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Convert most status touchpoints to asynchronous formats (recorded updates, written summaries) and rotate the few necessary live meetings across time zones.
- **B**: Anchor all meetings to headquarters time zone and treat after-hours attendance as part of the role for non-HQ team members.
- **C**: Hold separate per-region meetings for each major touchpoint with the PM relaying decisions between them.
- **D**: Reduce overall meeting frequency by half so the absolute time spent outside hours drops, even if the inequity remains.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI move on distributed teams is to default to async where it works and rotate the unavoidable synchronous load fairly. (A) does both — minimising and equitably distributing the burden. (B) institutionalises an inequity. (C) triples the PM's coordination load and creates information drift between regions. (D) reduces volume but leaves the structural inequity in place.
