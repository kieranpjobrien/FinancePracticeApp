# Question

A project team is distributed across three time zones. Team members complain that they're attending too many meetings outside their normal working hours. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Require all team members to adjust to headquarters' time zone
- **B**: Rotate meeting times so the inconvenience is shared, and increase use of asynchronous communication
- **C**: Eliminate all synchronous meetings and rely entirely on email
- **D**: Hold separate meetings for each time zone with the PM attending all

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Fair rotation of meeting times shares the burden of inconvenient hours. Increasing asynchronous communication (recorded updates, shared documents, collaboration tools) reduces the need for everyone to be online simultaneously. This balances the need for synchronous discussion with respect for work-life balance.

Option A (force one time zone) creates persistent inequity - some team members always bear the burden of inconvenient hours.

Option C (eliminate meetings) loses the benefits of synchronous discussion - real-time problem-solving, relationship building, and nuanced communication.

Option D (separate meetings) creates significant overhead for the PM and risks information gaps between sub-teams.
