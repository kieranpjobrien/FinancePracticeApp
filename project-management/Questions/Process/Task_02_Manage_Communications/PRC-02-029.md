# Question

A virtual team's written communications are frequently misinterpreted, leading to conflicts. Messages intended as neutral are being read as critical or hostile. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on only face-to-face communication
- **B**: Encourage more video calls for nuanced discussions, establish norms for written communication (assume positive intent), and address misunderstandings promptly before they escalate
- **C**: Add more emojis to all messages
- **D**: Reduce all communication to minimise conflict

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Written communication lacks tone and body language, increasing misinterpretation risk. Video for nuance: use richer channels for sensitive or complex topics. Norms: establish guidelines like "assume positive intent" and "ask before assuming." Quick resolution: address misunderstandings immediately before resentment builds. This reduces friction while maintaining productive communication.

Option A may not be practical for distributed teams.

Option C doesn't address underlying communication challenges.

Option D prevents necessary collaboration.
