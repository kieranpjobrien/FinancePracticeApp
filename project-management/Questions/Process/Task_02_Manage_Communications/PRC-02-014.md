# Question

A significant project issue has occurred. The PM wants to communicate transparently but is concerned about causing panic or damaging stakeholder confidence. How should the PM approach this communication?

## Options

- **A**: Hide the issue until it's resolved
- **B**: Communicate the issue factually, including what happened, impact, actions being taken, and expected resolution - honesty builds long-term trust even when news is bad
- **C**: Exaggerate the issue to lower expectations
- **D**: Only inform the project sponsor and keep it confidential

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Transparent communication builds trust. When communicating issues: state facts clearly (what happened), explain impact (what it means), describe response (what's being done), and provide outlook (when resolution is expected). Stakeholders respect honesty and are more concerned when they discover hidden issues later.

Option A destroys trust when discovered and often the issue is discovered.

Option C is dishonest and damages credibility.

Option D may leave stakeholders blindsided and doesn't allow them to help.
