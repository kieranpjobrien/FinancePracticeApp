# Question

The project has stakeholders in three countries across different time zones. Team meetings have been scheduled for headquarters' convenience, but remote stakeholders are increasingly disengaged. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep meetings at the current time since headquarters is most important
- **B**: Rotate meeting times to share the inconvenience, use asynchronous communication tools, and record meetings for those who can't attend live
- **C**: Exclude remote stakeholders from meetings
- **D**: Move all communication to email only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Distributed stakeholders require thoughtful communication approaches. Rotate times: share the burden of inconvenient hours. Use async tools: allow participation regardless of time zone. Record meetings: ensure information reaches everyone. This demonstrates respect for all stakeholders and maintains engagement across locations.

Option A devalues remote stakeholder contributions.

Option C excludes important perspectives.

Option D eliminates valuable synchronous interaction entirely.
