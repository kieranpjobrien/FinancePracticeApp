# Question

A programme has stakeholders in three time zones. All ceremonies are currently set for HQ business hours, and remote stakeholders have stopped attending or contributing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt a hybrid pattern: rotate the small number of essential live meetings, record them, and shift status sharing to async channels with structured written updates.
- **B**: Keep meetings on the HQ schedule and ask remote stakeholders to designate a delegate who can attend on their behalf.
- **C**: Add second sittings of the same meeting in remote-friendly time slots so attendance lifts without disturbing HQ.
- **D**: Move the entire programme to async-only communication so no one has to attend a live meeting again.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI move on distributed teams is to reduce the live meeting load and equitably rotate what remains. (A) does both. (B) entrenches the inequity and uses delegation as a workaround. (C) doubles the PM's coordination effort and risks information drift between sittings. (D) over-corrects; some live touchpoints (e.g., decisions, complex problem-solving) genuinely need synchronous time.
