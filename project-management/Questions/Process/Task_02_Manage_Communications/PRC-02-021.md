# Question

During a project meeting, a stakeholder raises a concern that isn't on the agenda. Other stakeholders seem interested in the topic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Strictly follow the agenda and refuse to discuss
- **B**: Acknowledge the concern, briefly assess if it can be addressed now or needs separate discussion, and either allocate time or schedule follow-up while capturing the concern
- **C**: Let the discussion go wherever it leads
- **D**: Tell the stakeholder their concern doesn't matter

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Balance agenda discipline with responsiveness to legitimate concerns. Acknowledge the stakeholder. Quick assessment: can this be addressed in 5 minutes? Does it need longer discussion? Either allocate brief time now or schedule follow-up. Capture the concern so it's not lost. This respects both the stakeholder and other attendees' time.

Option A dismisses potentially important issues.

Option C loses meeting focus and may not serve all attendees.

Option D is disrespectful and damages the relationship.
