# Question

In a steering committee meeting, a stakeholder raises a concern not on the agenda. Other members visibly engage with the topic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold to the agenda strictly to keep the meeting on schedule and ask the stakeholder to email the concern after the meeting.
- **B**: Suspend the published agenda and let the new topic run as long as members are engaged with it.
- **C**: Note the topic for the next meeting's agenda so it can be properly prepared and stick with the planned items today.
- **D**: Acknowledge the concern, check whether it can be addressed now, and either timebox a discussion or schedule a follow-up.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PMI move is to honour the stakeholder, protect the meeting's purpose, and capture the issue. (D) does all three. (A) is rigid and risks dismissing a legitimate concern. (B) abandons agenda discipline and lets the meeting drift. (C) is reasonable in form but doesn't acknowledge in the moment, and "wait for next meeting" can frustrate stakeholders if the issue is real.
