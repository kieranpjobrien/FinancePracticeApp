# Question

A programme has three distinct audiences: a monthly exec steering group, a weekly delivery community-of-practice, and a fortnightly vendor-review meeting. The PM currently produces a single report reused for all three. Engagement has dropped in every forum. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce three audience-specific reports aligned with the decisions each forum actually makes, reusing underlying data where sensible.
- **B**: Keep the single report but add audience-specific appendices covering the points each forum typically raises in discussion.
- **C**: Ask each forum to nominate a liaison who translates the single report for their members before every scheduled session.
- **D**: Run a short survey across all three forums to confirm whether the current report genuinely needs audience-specific tailoring.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's communications planning explicitly calls for tailoring content, format, and cadence to audience information needs. (A) does that purposefully. (B) an ever-longer document isn't a substitute for fit-for-purpose reports. (C) liaisons are a workaround that pushes the PM's tailoring responsibility onto volunteers. (D) a survey is delay theatre when the problem and fix are already clear.
