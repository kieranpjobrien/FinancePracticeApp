# Question

The PM receives an angrily worded email from a stakeholder accusing the project of incompetence. Behind the tone, several specific points are factually correct. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Match the stakeholder's tone in the reply so they don't take the PM's restraint as agreement.
- **B**: Acknowledge the valid points professionally, lay out facts and the planned response, and address the tone separately if it repeats.
- **C**: Forward the email to the stakeholder's line manager with a request that the line manager moderate the tone going forward.
- **D**: Reply requesting a meeting before discussing any of the substance of the email so the conversation can happen verbally.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI principle is to respond to substance, not tone, and to address tone separately if it persists. (B) does this. (A) escalates conflict and abandons professional standards. (C) goes around the stakeholder before any direct engagement. (D) defers a substantive response unnecessarily; written acknowledgement of valid points is appropriate now, with a meeting if needed.
