# Question

The PM receives an angry email from a stakeholder criticising the project. The criticism is partially valid but the tone is unprofessional. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Respond with equal anger to establish dominance
- **B**: Acknowledge the valid concerns professionally, address the substance of the criticism constructively, and avoid escalating the emotional tone
- **C**: Ignore the email
- **D**: Forward the email to the stakeholder's manager

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Respond to substance, not tone. Acknowledge: "I understand your concern about X." Address: respond constructively to valid points. Don't escalate: use professional, measured language regardless of their tone. Often, addressing the underlying concern defuses the emotion. If unprofessional behaviour continues, address it separately.

Option A escalates conflict unproductively.

Option C ignores legitimate concerns.

Option D escalates when direct resolution may be possible.
