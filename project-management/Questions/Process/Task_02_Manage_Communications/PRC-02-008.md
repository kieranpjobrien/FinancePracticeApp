# Question

In a retrospective, the team reveals they've been hesitant to raise issues in stand-ups because a senior engineer dismisses their points. Several recent issues weren't surfaced until they became serious. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the behaviour with the senior engineer's line manager so it can be addressed through performance management.
- **B**: Establish written team norms requiring all team members to be heard and have the team commit to them in the next stand-up.
- **C**: Have a private one-on-one with the senior engineer to share the impact, then work with the team to define and reinforce safety-supporting norms.
- **D**: Move stand-ups to a written async format so the senior engineer's verbal dominance no longer affects participation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Psychological safety problems are addressed by combining direct private feedback with team-level norm setting — not by escalation, public process, or routing around the person. (C) does both. (A) escalates a coachable issue and is usually disproportionate as a first step. (B) sets norms but skips the conversation that gives the senior engineer a chance to change. (D) treats a symptom by removing the channel rather than addressing the behaviour.
