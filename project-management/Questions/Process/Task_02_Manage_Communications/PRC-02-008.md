# Question

During a project retrospective, team members reveal they've been hesitant to raise concerns in meetings because a senior team member dismisses their ideas. This has led to issues being hidden until they become critical. How should the project manager address this?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the senior team member from future meetings
- **B**: Privately coach the senior member on inclusive behaviour while establishing team norms for psychological safety
- **C**: Tell the team to be more assertive
- **D**: Switch all communication to written format to avoid confrontation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. This requires addressing both the behaviour and the environment. Private coaching helps the senior member understand their impact without public embarrassment. Establishing team norms for respectful communication creates expectations for everyone. Psychological safety is essential for surfacing issues early.

Option A (remove from meetings) loses the senior member's expertise and doesn't address the underlying behaviour.

Option C (tell team to be assertive) puts the burden on those already feeling unsafe. It's the PM's job to create safe conditions.

Option D (written only) avoids the problem rather than solving it. Written communication has its own challenges and doesn't build team cohesion.
