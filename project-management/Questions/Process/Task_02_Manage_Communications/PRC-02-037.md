# Question

Stakeholders complain they receive so many project updates that important ones are getting missed. Open rates on the weekly status email are below 30%. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Switch the format from email to a dashboard that stakeholders can check at their convenience.
- **B**: Mark every status update with "high priority" and "action required" so urgent items aren't missed.
- **C**: Reduce frequency from weekly to fortnightly to lower the volume of inbound communication.
- **D**: Audit current communications, consolidate overlapping updates, sharpen subject lines to indicate action and urgency, and segment recipients to who actually needs each message.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Communication fatigue is a relevance and signal-to-noise problem; the fix is structural triage, not volume cuts or false-priority flags. (D) addresses all three (audit, sharpen, segment). (A) changes channel without addressing relevance. (B) is the classic anti-pattern that destroys priority signals entirely. (C) reduces volume but doesn't fix the underlying relevance issue.
