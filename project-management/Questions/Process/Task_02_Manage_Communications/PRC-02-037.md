# Question

Stakeholders complain that they receive too many project communications and have started ignoring them, including important ones. How should the PM address communication fatigue?

## Options

- **A**: Stop all communications
- **B**: Audit current communications for necessity and audience appropriateness, consolidate where possible, make subject lines clear about urgency and action needed, and use different channels for different purposes
- **C**: Continue current pattern but expect stakeholders to pay attention
- **D**: Mark everything as high priority

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Communication overload leads to tuning out. Audit: which communications are necessary? Who really needs them? Consolidate: combine related updates into single communications. Clear signalling: subject lines indicating urgency and action needed. Channel discipline: routine updates in one place, urgent matters through another. Less noise means important signals get through.

Option A eliminates necessary information flow.

Option C expects behaviour change without addressing the cause.

Option D makes priority meaningless and worsens the problem.
