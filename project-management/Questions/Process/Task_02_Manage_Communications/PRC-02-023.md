# Question

A project steering committee requests detailed monthly reports but then only discusses high-level issues in meetings. The PM spends significant time creating reports that aren't fully used. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue producing detailed reports as requested
- **B**: Propose a streamlined reporting format focused on high-level information and exceptions, with detail available on request, and validate this meets their actual needs
- **C**: Stop providing any reports
- **D**: Provide less detail without discussing with the committee

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Reports should match actual information consumption. Propose adjustment: "I notice we focus on X, Y, Z. Would a summary format with detail available on request better serve the committee?" This respects their time, reduces your overhead, and may actually improve information consumption. Validate that the new format meets their needs.

Option A continues inefficient use of everyone's time.

Option C stops providing necessary governance information.

Option D changes without agreement, which may backfire.
