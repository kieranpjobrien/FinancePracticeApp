# Question

The steering committee asks for monthly status reports of 15+ pages but only ever discusses the executive summary in meetings. The PM spends two days a month on the report. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue producing the full report as originally commissioned to avoid any governance friction with the committee.
- **B**: Stop producing detailed reports and switch unilaterally to a one-page executive summary from the next cycle onward.
- **C**: Reduce report depth gradually and observe whether the committee notices or asks for more operational detail.
- **D**: Discuss with the chair what decisions the report supports and propose a one-pager with on-demand annexes.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PMI move on reporting is to confirm the decision the report serves, then minimise effort to what serves it — agreed with the audience, not assumed. (D) does this. (A) accepts known waste. (B) makes a unilateral change to a governance artefact without sponsor agreement. (C) is passive-aggressive and erodes trust if discovered.
