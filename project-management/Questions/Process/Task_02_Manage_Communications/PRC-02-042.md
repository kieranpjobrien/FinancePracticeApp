# Question

Cultural differences are affecting communication effectiveness. Some team members prefer direct communication while others find it rude. Some expect formal channels while others prefer informal. How should the PM navigate this?

## Options

- **A**: Impose one communication style for everyone
- **B**: Facilitate team discussion about communication preferences, establish team norms that acknowledge different styles, and help team members understand and adapt to cultural differences
- **C**: Separate the team by communication preference
- **D**: Avoid addressing cultural differences

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Cultural differences are assets when navigated well. Discussion: help the team understand different communication styles exist and none are "wrong." Norms: establish shared agreements that work across cultures (e.g., "when in doubt, ask for clarification"). Adaptation: help team members flex their style when communicating across cultural preferences. This builds a more effective multicultural team.

Option A forces assimilation that may not be possible or desirable.

Option C creates silos based on culture.

Option D allows misunderstandings to continue.
