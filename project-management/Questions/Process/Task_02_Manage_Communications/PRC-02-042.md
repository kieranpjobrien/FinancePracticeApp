# Question

The team includes members from cultures with different communication norms — some prefer direct critique, others find it disrespectful; some expect formal channels, others informal. Misreadings are starting to affect collaboration. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the communication style of the largest cultural group on the team to give the team a single shared norm.
- **B**: Allocate one-on-one coaching sessions to each team member individually so they each receive culturally tailored guidance.
- **C**: Ask the team to default to written formal communication, since text is more easily controlled for tone across cultures.
- **D**: Facilitate an open conversation about communication preferences, co-create team norms that work across the cultural mix, and equip members to flex their style with each other.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Cross-cultural friction is best resolved by surfacing differences openly and co-creating norms that respect them — not by enforcing one style or going underground. (D) is the PMI-aligned approach. (A) marginalises members from other cultures. (B) treats the issue as individual coaching when it is a team-norm problem. (C) loses the value of synchronous discussion and doesn't address how members should treat each other.
