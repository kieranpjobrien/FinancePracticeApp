# Question

The PM learns that a senior business owner — not on the stakeholder register — has been getting weekly verbal updates from one of the developers. The owner just raised concerns based on partial information they received. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Discipline the developer for going outside the formal communication plan and brief the team on proper channels.
- **B**: Update the stakeholder register and add the business owner to the communication plan with the right cadence and content.
- **C**: Tell the developer to stop responding to the business owner's questions and route all future requests through the PM.
- **D**: Treat the situation as informal feedback only and address the business owner's concern without changing the communications plan.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root cause is a stakeholder gap, not a developer problem — someone with legitimate interest wasn't in the plan. (B) fixes the gap and prevents recurrence. (A) punishes a symptom and assumes bad intent. (C) cuts off communication that exists for a reason and may push it underground. (D) treats the immediate concern but leaves the plan defective.
