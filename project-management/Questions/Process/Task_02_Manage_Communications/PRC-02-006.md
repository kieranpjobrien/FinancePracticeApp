# Question

A project manager discovers that a team member has been sharing project status information directly with a senior executive who isn't on the stakeholder list, bypassing the PM. The information shared was accurate but incomplete, causing the executive to raise concerns. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Discipline the team member for unauthorised communication
- **B**: Add the executive to the stakeholder list and establish appropriate communication channels
- **C**: Instruct the team member to stop communicating with executives
- **D**: Ignore the issue since the information was accurate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The root cause is a gap in stakeholder identification and communication planning. If an executive is interested enough to seek project information, they should be a stakeholder with defined communication. Adding them to the plan and establishing appropriate channels prevents future issues while respecting their legitimate interest.

Option A (discipline) treats symptoms, not cause. The team member may have been responding to legitimate executive interest. The PM should fix the communication gap.

Option C (stop communication) may not be appropriate. The executive has shown interest - blocking communication could create resistance or drive information underground.

Option D (ignore) allows the pattern to continue. Incomplete information reaching executives without context is a project risk.
