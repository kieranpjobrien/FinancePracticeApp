# Question

A project involves sensitive organisational changes that will affect employment. Information has been leaking prematurely, causing anxiety and rumours. How should the PM manage communications in this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Stop all project communications
- **B**: Work with HR and leadership to establish a controlled communication plan with clear timing, authorised messaging, and appropriate confidentiality while acknowledging that some information naturally flows informally
- **C**: Investigate and punish anyone who shared information
- **D**: Ignore the leaks and continue as normal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Sensitive organisational changes require careful communication governance. Partner with HR and leadership: they have expertise and authority on such matters. Controlled plan: agree who communicates what, when, and how. Acknowledge reality: some informal communication is inevitable - focus on ensuring official channels are timely and trustworthy.

Option A creates an information vacuum filled by rumours.

Option C may be disproportionate and creates a culture of fear.

Option D allows rumours to become the primary information source.
