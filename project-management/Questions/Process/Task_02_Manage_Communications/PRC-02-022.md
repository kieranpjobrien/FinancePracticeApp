# Question

A restructuring project will affect employment in two business units. Information has begun leaking ahead of the planned announcement and has triggered unhelpful rumours. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend all project communications until the formal announcement date to prevent further leakage from any channel.
- **B**: Initiate a leak investigation through HR and IT to identify and discipline the source before any further communication.
- **C**: Partner with HR, legal, and the sponsor to bring the formal announcement forward and brief leaders to address the rumours.
- **D**: Continue to the original communication timeline and treat the rumours as a separate cultural issue rather than a project communication problem.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When sensitive information leaks, the practical PMI response is to accelerate authorised communication, partner with the right functions, and equip leaders to address the rumour vacuum. (C) does this. (A) creates a vacuum that rumours fill. (B) starts with enforcement before solving the immediate communication failure. (D) lets misinformation harden into perceived truth.
