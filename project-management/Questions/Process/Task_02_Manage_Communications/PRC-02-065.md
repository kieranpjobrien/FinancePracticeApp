# Question

A Scrum team is complaining that their daily stand-up regularly runs 25 minutes, often drifting into problem-solving. The Scrum Master is new and doesn't feel comfortable cutting participants off. Velocity has dropped and team members are losing focus time. What should the PM coach the Scrum Master to do?

## Options

- **A**: Shorten stand-up to ten minutes, have each person answer the three standard questions, and defer problem-solving to follow-ups.
- **B**: Let participants raise whatever they need since cutting them off damages psychological safety and the team's willingness to speak.
- **C**: Change stand-up to twice a week so each session has room for the problem-solving the team clearly values every morning.
- **D**: Skip stand-up on days when there's no new information to share, reducing the meeting burden without changing its format.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Stand-ups are a fixed-length coordination event, not a problem-solving forum; PMI and Scrum-aligned practice is to keep the format and take problem-solving offline. (A) does exactly that. (B) confuses safety with time discipline; a well-run meeting is safer for everyone. (C) reducing cadence breaks the daily coordination loop Scrum depends on. (D) skipping events erodes the ceremony's value and hides the real issue.
