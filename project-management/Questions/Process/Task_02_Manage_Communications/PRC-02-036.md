# Question

In communication theory, what does "noise" refer to?

## Options

- **A**: Background sound that interferes with verbal communication on a phone or video call.
- **B**: Communications sent at the wrong frequency or to the wrong audience.
- **C**: Overly formal language that obscures the message.
- **D**: Anything — physical, semantic, psychological, or environmental — that interferes with the accurate transmission and interpretation of a message.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Communication noise is the broad concept covering all interference sources, not just sound. (D) is the standard PMBOK/communication theory definition. (A) is one type of noise but not the concept. (B) describes communication targeting errors, not noise. (C) is one type of semantic noise but not the full concept.
