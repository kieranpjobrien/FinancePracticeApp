# Question

What are "noise" factors in communication?

## Options

- **A**: Background sounds during calls
- **B**: Anything that interferes with message transmission or understanding, including physical, semantic, or psychological barriers
- **C**: Unnecessary information
- **D**: Loud team members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Communication "noise" includes any interference with effective message transmission and understanding. Physical: actual sound, poor connections. Semantic: jargon, language differences. Psychological: preconceptions, emotional states, distrust. Environmental: distractions, multitasking. Effective communication minimises noise through appropriate channel selection and clear messaging.

Option A is one type of physical noise but not the complete concept.

Option C relates to information overload, a specific type of noise.

Option D is a specific physical noise source.
