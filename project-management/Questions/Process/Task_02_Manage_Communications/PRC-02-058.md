# Question

A new PM is inheriting a 14-month programme and finds no communications management plan. Stakeholders have been emailed status inconsistently and meeting cadences vary wildly. The sponsor wants the PM "focused on delivery, not paperwork". What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Build a lightweight communications plan covering audiences, frequency, channel and owner, and review it with the sponsor briefly.
- **B**: Defer planning for a quarter, observe what communications are actually needed, then formalise a plan based on the observed patterns.
- **C**: Adopt the PMO's standard communications plan template in full so the programme meets organisational governance expectations quickly.
- **D**: Hold a workshop with all key stakeholders to co-design the communications approach before documenting it formally in any plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A communications plan isn't paperwork; it's the design that prevents the inconsistency the PM is inheriting. (A) tailors the artefact to what the programme needs and engages the sponsor briefly. (B) deferring perpetuates the current inconsistency. (C) full adoption of a template without tailoring misses the point of planning. (D) a full co-design workshop is disproportionate for a document the PM should draft and validate quickly.
