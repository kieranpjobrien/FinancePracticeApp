# Question

The PM discovers that a stakeholder has been providing inaccurate information to executives about the project, making the situation seem worse than it is. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the stakeholder publicly
- **B**: Establish direct communication with executives to ensure accurate information flow, address the discrepancy privately with the stakeholder, and understand their concerns that may be driving the behaviour
- **C**: Start providing inaccurate positive information to balance
- **D**: Ignore the situation and hope it resolves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Address both the symptom and potential cause. Direct executive communication: ensure accurate information reaches decision-makers. Private discussion: understand why the stakeholder is presenting this view - do they have concerns not being addressed? Build relationship: perhaps their behaviour reflects unmet needs. This corrects the immediate issue while potentially resolving underlying problems.

Option A escalates conflict publicly without solving the problem.

Option C adds more inaccuracy.

Option D allows misinformation to continue.
