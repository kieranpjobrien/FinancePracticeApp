# Question

The project is running over budget. The PM needs to communicate this to the sponsor. What approach is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Delay informing the sponsor hoping to recover the variance
- **B**: Present the facts clearly: current spend vs budget, reasons for variance, options for recovery, and your recommendation - then facilitate decision-making
- **C**: Blame the team for the overrun
- **D**: Present only good news to maintain a positive relationship

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Bad news should be delivered clearly and constructively. Facts: what's the variance? Why did it occur? Options: what can be done - scope reduction, additional funding, acceptance? Recommendation: what do you advise? Decision support: give the sponsor what they need to make informed decisions. This builds trust even when news is bad.

Option A delays necessary decisions and destroys trust when discovered.

Option C focuses on blame rather than solutions.

Option D is dishonest and prevents necessary decisions.
