# Question

A stakeholder consistently interrupts other speakers during meetings, preventing diverse voices from being heard. Meeting dynamics are suffering. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Let it continue to avoid confrontation
- **B**: Establish meeting ground rules, use facilitation techniques to ensure all voices are heard, and if needed, privately coach the interruptive stakeholder on inclusive behaviour
- **C**: Exclude the stakeholder from meetings
- **D**: Only communicate with this stakeholder in writing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Address both the behaviour and its impact on the group. Ground rules: establish expectations for meeting participation. Facilitation: use techniques like round-robins, time limits, or "parking lots" to manage airtime. Private coaching: address the individual's behaviour directly and privately. This improves meeting effectiveness while respecting the individual.

Option A allows dysfunction to continue.

Option C excludes a potentially important stakeholder.

Option D avoids rather than addresses the behaviour.
