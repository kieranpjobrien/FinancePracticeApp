# Question

A key stakeholder complains they never know what's happening with the project despite receiving weekly status reports. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send reports more frequently
- **B**: Meet with the stakeholder to understand what information they actually need and how they prefer to receive it, then adjust communications accordingly
- **C**: Add them to all project meetings
- **D**: Assume they're not reading the reports and resend them

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The stakeholder's need isn't being met by current communications. Understand their specific information needs: what do they want to know? In what format? At what frequency? Perhaps they need different content, verbal updates, or a dashboard rather than written reports. Tailor communication to recipient needs.

Option A increases quantity without addressing relevance.

Option C may overwhelm them with unnecessary detail.

Option D assumes the problem is delivery rather than content or format.
