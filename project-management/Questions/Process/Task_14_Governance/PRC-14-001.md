# Question

A project spans four departments, each with a different decision style. Decisions are stalling because nobody knows who can approve what or how to escalate when the departments disagree. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Send every contested decision to the project sponsor so a single executive resolves them without further debate.
- **B**: Have each department follow its own decision process within its area and renegotiate at integration points later.
- **C**: Convene the department heads with the sponsor to agree decision rights, approval authorities, and escalation paths.
- **D**: Make the contested decisions yourself to keep the project moving and inform the departments afterwards in writing.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's first move when governance is unclear is to make it clear, with the people who will live with it. (C) does that. (A) overloads the sponsor and leaves the ongoing ambiguity in place. (B) accepts the fragmentation that is causing the delays. (D) exceeds the PM's authority and damages trust with the departments.
