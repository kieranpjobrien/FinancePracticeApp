# Question

A project spans multiple departments with different management styles and approval processes. Project decisions are being delayed because the escalation path and decision authority are unclear. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate all decisions to the project sponsor for resolution
- **B**: Make decisions independently to avoid delays
- **C**: Work with stakeholders to establish a clear governance structure defining decision rights, escalation paths, and approval authorities
- **D**: Let each department follow their own processes

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The root cause is unclear governance. Establishing a clear structure with defined decision rights (who can decide what), escalation paths (how unresolved issues move up), and approval authorities (who signs off on what) removes ambiguity. This should be agreed upon by stakeholders from all involved departments.

Option A overloads the sponsor with decisions that could be made at lower levels, and still doesn't clarify the ongoing governance.

Option B may resolve immediate issues but creates risk of unauthorized decisions and stakeholder conflict.

Option D accepts fragmentation that causes the current delays. The project needs unified governance, not departmental silos.
