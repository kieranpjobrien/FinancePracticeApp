# Question

A self-organizing agile team is told they must follow the organization's traditional governance processes including formal change requests for any scope modification. The team argues this conflicts with agile principles of embracing change. What approach should balance these needs?

## Options

- **A**: Exempt the team from all governance requirements
- **B**: Abandon agile and use traditional methods
- **C**: Tailor governance to distinguish between product backlog refinement (handled within the team) and significant changes affecting budget, timeline, or contract scope (requiring formal change control)
- **D**: Have the product owner handle all governance requirements so the team doesn't know about them

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Governance can be tailored to agile contexts. Normal backlog refinement and priority changes within sprint/release scope are team decisions. Significant changes affecting budget, timeline, or contractual commitments need formal governance. This preserves agile's responsiveness for normal work while maintaining appropriate oversight for significant changes.

Option A ignores legitimate organizational governance needs.

Option B abandons agile benefits unnecessarily.

Option D hides governance rather than integrating it appropriately.
