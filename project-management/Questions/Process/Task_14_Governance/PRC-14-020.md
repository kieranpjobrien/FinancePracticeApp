# Question

External regulators have asked for real-time access to project systems and the right to attend any project meeting. The team worries this will chill open discussion and slow decisions. How should the PM structure governance?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the access request and offer instead a quarterly written compliance report.
- **B**: Grant full real-time access and let the regulator attend any meeting they choose.
- **C**: Direct the team to behave at every meeting as if a regulator were present, even when they are not.
- **D**: Negotiate access that satisfies the regulator's mandate — read access to documented decisions, periodic reviews, attendance at formal governance meetings — while keeping working sessions internal.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI says compliance is non-negotiable but how it is delivered can be tailored. (D) protects the regulator's mandate and the team's ability to think out loud. (A) risks regulatory non-compliance. (B) accepts unnecessary disruption and may chill open discussion. (C) creates performative meetings and does not change the underlying access question.
