# Question

A project is subject to regulatory oversight requiring external auditors to review project decisions and documentation. The external auditors have requested real-time access to project systems and the ability to attend any meeting. The team is concerned this will slow decision-making and inhibit open discussion. How should governance be structured?

## Options

- **A**: Deny auditor requests to protect project productivity
- **B**: Grant all auditor requests without modification
- **C**: Work with auditors to define access and participation that satisfies regulatory requirements while minimizing disruption - perhaps periodic reviews, access to documented decisions, and attendance at formal governance meetings rather than all meetings
- **D**: Conduct all project work in audit-friendly ways even if it slows the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Regulatory requirements are legitimate but can be satisfied in various ways. Negotiating a practical approach - documented decisions rather than meeting attendance, periodic reviews rather than real-time access, formal governance meeting access rather than all meetings - can satisfy regulatory needs while enabling effective project work.

Option A may violate regulatory requirements.

Option B may accept unnecessary disruption.

Option D prioritizes audit convenience over project delivery.
