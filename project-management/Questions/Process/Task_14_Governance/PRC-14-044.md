# Question

A project manager inherits a project that has been running for six months with no documented governance structure. Decisions have been made ad hoc by various stakeholders, some of whom claim authority that others dispute. The project is now facing a major decision where three stakeholders each believe they have final authority. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the stakeholders fight it out
- **B**: Make the decision unilaterally to move the project forward
- **C**: Escalate to the project sponsor to clarify immediate decision authority, then work to establish documented governance going forward to prevent recurrence
- **D**: Delay the decision indefinitely until everyone agrees

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The immediate crisis (who decides) needs resolution through escalation to whoever has authority above the disputing stakeholders. Then the PM should establish proper governance documentation to prevent future disputes. This addresses both the immediate need and the systemic problem.

Option A doesn't resolve the dispute or address the underlying governance gap.

Option B exceeds PM authority when stakeholders dispute who should decide.

Option D allows the governance gap to continue causing problems.
