# Question

You have inherited a six-month-old project with no documented governance. A major decision is needed now, and three different stakeholders each insist they hold final authority. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the authority dispute to the sponsor for an immediate ruling on this decision, then move to formalise governance documentation to prevent a repeat.
- **B**: Convene the three stakeholders and facilitate a consensus decision so the project can keep moving without escalation.
- **C**: Take the decision yourself as the project manager since the dispute is preventing progress and someone has to act.
- **D**: Ask each stakeholder to provide written justification of their authority and decide whose claim is strongest based on the evidence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The immediate need is a clear decision-maker, and only the sponsor sits above the disputing stakeholders. (A) resolves the urgent call and then closes the systemic governance gap. (B) seeks consensus on a decision that may not require it and avoids the underlying authority question. (C) exceeds the PM's authority where senior stakeholders are actively contesting it. (D) puts the PM in the role of adjudicating senior stakeholders' authority claims, which is not the PM's place.
