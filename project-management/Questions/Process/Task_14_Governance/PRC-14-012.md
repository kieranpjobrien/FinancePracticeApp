# Question

A global project has stakeholders in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific regions, each with different regulatory requirements, business practices, and expectations for involvement. Time zone differences make synchronous governance meetings difficult. What governance approach should be implemented?

## Options

- **A**: Hold all governance meetings at times convenient for headquarters
- **B**: Rotate meeting times so each region is occasionally inconvenienced
- **C**: Establish a tiered governance structure with regional representatives who can make region-specific decisions, supported by asynchronous decision processes and periodic full-group sessions at rotating times
- **D**: Give one region authority over all decisions

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Global governance needs tiered decision authority (regional matters decided regionally), asynchronous mechanisms (documented proposals with decision deadlines), and fair practices for synchronous meetings (rotating times). This enables effective governance despite geographic distribution.

Option A creates inequity and may violate local regulations if regional considerations aren't heard.

Option B addresses meeting times but not the decision structure.

Option D ignores legitimate regional needs and regulatory differences.
