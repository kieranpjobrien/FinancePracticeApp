# Question

A team member skipped the PM and complained directly to a steering committee member, who then directed an operational change without consulting the PM. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the violation and ask HR to address the team member's behaviour formally with a written record of the breach.
- **B**: Recommend the steering committee formally remove the member who overstepped to deter any future recurrences of the pattern.
- **C**: Reinforce the escalation rules with both parties so team escalations flow through the PM and steering members route through the PM.
- **D**: Accept that any member of the team can escalate to any steering committee member at any time for transparency.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This is a process clarification, not a discipline issue. (C) addresses both ends of the breakdown without punishing anyone. (A) treats it as misconduct rather than process. (B) is disproportionate and damages the steering relationship. (D) abandons the PM's coordinating role and invites chaos.
