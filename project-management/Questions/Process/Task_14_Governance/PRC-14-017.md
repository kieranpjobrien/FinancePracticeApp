# Question

A team member skipped the PM and complained directly to a steering committee member, who then directed an operational change without consulting the PM. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the violation and ask HR to address the team member's behaviour formally.
- **B**: Recommend the steering committee remove the member who overstepped to deter recurrences.
- **C**: Reinforce the escalation rules with both parties — team escalations flow through the PM, steering members route operational matters back through the PM unless there is a real reason to bypass.
- **D**: Accept that any member of the team can escalate to any steering committee member at any time, since transparency matters.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This is a process clarification, not a discipline issue. (C) addresses both ends of the breakdown without punishing anyone. (A) treats it as misconduct rather than process. (B) is disproportionate and damages the steering relationship. (D) abandons the PM's coordinating role and invites chaos.
