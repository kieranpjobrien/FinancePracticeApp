# Question

During a project, a team member bypasses the project manager and goes directly to the steering committee with complaints about project decisions. The steering committee member then directs changes without consulting the project manager. How should governance be clarified?

## Options

- **A**: Punish the team member for bypassing the PM
- **B**: Accept that team members can escalate whenever they want
- **C**: Reinforce the escalation process with all parties: team members should escalate through the PM first, and steering committee members should route operational matters back through the PM unless there are valid reasons to bypass
- **D**: Remove the steering committee member who overstepped

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This is a governance process breakdown on multiple fronts. The escalation path should be clarified: normal escalation flows through the PM, the steering committee should direct operational matters through proper channels. Both the team member and steering committee member need to understand and follow the process.

Option A focuses on punishment rather than process improvement.

Option B undermines the PM's role and creates chaos.

Option D is excessive - the steering committee member may not have understood proper process.
