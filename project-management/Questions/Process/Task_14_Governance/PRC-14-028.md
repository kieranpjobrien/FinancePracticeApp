# Question

The steering committee has not met in four months, your sponsor stopped responding two weeks ago, and a vendor dispute has emerged that requires a decision well above your delegated authority. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decision yourself based on your best judgement, since governance has effectively collapsed and the project cannot wait.
- **B**: Pause vendor work and put the project on hold until the sponsor responds and governance is reactivated.
- **C**: Document the governance gap and the pending decision, and escalate to the sponsor's manager or PMO leadership to either reactivate governance or appoint an interim decision-maker.
- **D**: Convene the team and use a consensus vote to take the decision so the project can keep moving.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When governance collapses, PMI expects the PM to restore it through proper escalation rather than fill the vacuum personally. (C) escalates above the unresponsive sponsor to re-establish authority, which is the correct response when an issue is genuinely blocked at the PM's level. (A) exceeds the PM's delegated authority and exposes the organisation to risk. (B) may be necessary later but skipping straight to a project halt before attempting to restore governance is premature. (D) gives the team authority it does not have and creates a false governance structure.
