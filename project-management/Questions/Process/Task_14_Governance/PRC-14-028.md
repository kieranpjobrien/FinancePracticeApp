# Question

A project has experienced governance breakdown: the steering committee has stopped meeting, the sponsor is unresponsive, and the PM has been making all decisions alone. The project is now facing a major issue requiring authority beyond the PM's level. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue making decisions alone since governance has failed
- **B**: Halt the project until governance is restored
- **C**: Formally document the governance gap and immediate need, escalate to appropriate organizational leadership to either reactivate governance or establish interim decision authority, and avoid making decisions beyond current authority
- **D**: Resign from the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should avoid exceeding authority even when governance has failed. Formally documenting the situation and escalating to organizational leadership (above the sponsor if necessary) brings appropriate attention to the governance failure. The goal is establishing at least interim authority to address immediate needs while governance is restored.

Option A exceeds authority and creates risk.

Option B may be necessary but escalation should come first.

Option D abandons the project without attempting resolution.
