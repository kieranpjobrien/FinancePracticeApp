# Question

A project manager realizes that key technical decisions are being made informally by senior developers without going through the project's technical governance process. The decisions are generally good, but the lack of documentation and formal approval is concerning. What should the project manager address?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the informal process since the decisions are good
- **B**: Discipline the developers for bypassing governance
- **C**: Work with the technical team to understand why governance is being bypassed and adjust the process to be appropriate for the type of decisions being made while ensuring documentation and accountability
- **D**: Require all technical decisions to be made by management

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The bypass suggests the formal process may be too heavy for these decisions. The PM should understand why it's happening - is governance too slow, too bureaucratic, or at the wrong level? Adjusting to be fit-for-purpose while maintaining documentation and accountability addresses both the efficiency the team wants and the oversight the project needs.

Option A ignores risks from undocumented, unaccountable decisions.

Option B focuses on punishment rather than process improvement.

Option D removes technical expertise from technical decisions.
