# Question

Two functional managers are giving conflicting direction to four team members shared with the project. Rework is mounting. The project charter is silent on shared-resource governance. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor and request that the sponsor instruct one functional manager to step back.
- **B**: Pull the four team members from the project and request dedicated resources instead.
- **C**: Tell the team members to follow project direction whenever it conflicts with functional direction.
- **D**: Convene the two functional managers, agree decision rights for shared resources, document the agreement, and update the charter or governance plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The root cause is unclear governance, which the PM is positioned to fix at peer level before escalating. (D) fixes it through the right conversation. (A) escalates prematurely without a proposed resolution. (B) costs the project capacity and ignores the underlying issue. (C) puts team members in an impossible spot and does not address the conflict.
