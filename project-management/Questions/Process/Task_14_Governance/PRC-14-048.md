# Question

A joint-venture project between two organisations has two co-sponsors. They agree on project goals but disagree on governance cadence: one wants weekly, the other quarterly. The PM is caught in the middle. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Go with whichever sponsor is more senior in their own organisation and ask the other to accept that cadence.
- **B**: Hold separate meetings at each sponsor's preferred cadence so both sponsors get the rhythm they want individually.
- **C**: Propose the midpoint (monthly) and move on, since both sponsors need to compromise for the project to actually progress.
- **D**: Facilitate a joint session with both co-sponsors to design a single shared governance structure that meets both organisations' needs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's joint governance principle asks the PM to facilitate a shared structure rather than split or arbitrate. (D) produces a single jointly owned governance model. (A) picks sides on seniority. (B) creates two governance streams and doubles overhead and divergence. (C) imposes an arbitrary compromise without exploring what each side actually needs.
