# Question

A project has multiple stakeholders with different interests: the business wants features, IT wants technical quality, compliance wants controls, and finance wants cost containment. Decisions often become battlegrounds between these perspectives. What governance structure would help?

## Options

- **A**: Let the strongest personality win each debate
- **B**: Have the PM make all decisions to avoid conflict
- **C**: Establish a governance body that includes representatives from each perspective, with clear decision criteria that balance the different concerns and a defined process for reaching decisions
- **D**: Rotate decision authority among the groups

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Effective governance balances legitimate competing interests through inclusive representation, clear criteria (so decisions aren't arbitrary), and defined processes. Each perspective is heard, but decisions are made systematically rather than through power struggles. This might include weighted criteria or priority frameworks.

Option A leads to inconsistent decisions based on personalities.

Option B may not adequately represent all perspectives.

Option D doesn't provide consistent decision criteria.
