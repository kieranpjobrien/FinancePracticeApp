# Question

A project has competing stakeholder interests: business wants features fast, IT wants technical quality, compliance wants controls, finance wants cost containment. Decisions devolve into battlegrounds. What governance design would help?

## Options

- **A**: Let the PM make all decisions unilaterally to break stalemates quickly and keep the project moving.
- **B**: Convene a governance body including each perspective with explicit decision criteria weighting all four interests.
- **C**: Rotate decision authority among the four groups so each gets equal say across the year and feels heard.
- **D**: Hand authority to whichever stakeholder represents the largest financial commitment to the project going forward.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's governance design balances legitimate stakeholder perspectives through structured representation and clear decision criteria. (B) does both. (A) silences the perspectives that need to be heard. (C) gives inconsistent treatment of similar decisions over time. (D) lets money override the other legitimate interests, which usually produces compliance and quality issues later.
