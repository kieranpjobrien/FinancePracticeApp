# Question

The PMO has flagged that an agile team is making in-sprint adjustments to user stories without raising change requests, arguing these are scope changes requiring CCB approval. The product owner believes they are normal refinement. How should the project manager resolve this?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend story refinement until every adjustment has been logged and approved by the CCB to keep the PMO satisfied.
- **B**: Take the position that agile work is exempt from CCB processes and continue current practice without further engagement.
- **C**: Work with the PMO to define a written threshold separating in-team refinement from changes that affect baseline scope, schedule, or contract.
- **D**: Request that the project be moved out of PMO governance so the team can operate without this kind of friction.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The dispute is definitional: where does refinement end and a baseline change begin? (C) resolves it with a clear, jointly agreed line that respects both agile cadence and enterprise governance. (A) makes agile delivery impossible. (B) sets up a recurring conflict and is not a tailoring decision the PM can make alone. (D) treats governance as the obstacle rather than negotiating sensible tailoring.
