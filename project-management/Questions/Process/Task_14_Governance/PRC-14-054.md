# Question

A project is preparing to close with all deliverables accepted. The business case promised benefits that will only materialise over twelve months of operation. The sponsor wants to disband governance at closure. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Disband governance at closure as the sponsor has requested, since the project itself has officially delivered all outputs.
- **B**: Keep the full steering committee running for twelve months to monitor the benefits that were promised in the business case.
- **C**: Hand off governance entirely to operations so they can measure benefits themselves under their own internal management structures.
- **D**: Transition to a lean benefits-realisation governance with the benefit owner, sponsor, and defined cadence for twelve months.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI and the business-case principle expect benefits realisation to continue after output delivery under appropriate governance. (D) right-sizes governance for that phase. (A) ends accountability before benefits are known. (B) keeps a project-size structure for a different purpose. (C) hands off accountability to a function without shared governance.
