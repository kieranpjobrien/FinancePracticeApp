# Question

The sponsor has asked you to cut monthly steering committee meetings from two hours to thirty minutes. You are concerned that complex strategic decisions cannot be properly debated in that time. What should the project manager propose?

## Options

- **A**: Run the next two meetings in 30 minutes as requested and reassess based on whether decisions slip.
- **B**: Push back firmly and keep the existing two-hour slot since it is the only safe option for complex decisions.
- **C**: Move governance entirely to email so that members can engage when they have time and avoid meeting fatigue.
- **D**: Propose a tiered cadence: a 30-minute monthly status check supported by detailed pre-reads, with longer working sessions called when a complex decision is on the agenda.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) honours the sponsor's efficiency goal while preserving capacity for genuinely complex decisions, which is a typical PMI tailoring response. (A) treats it as an experiment but accepts the risk of decision slippage in the meantime. (B) refuses a reasonable request from the sponsor without offering an alternative. (C) eliminates the discussion that complex decisions need and reduces governance to written exchanges.
