# Question

A project sponsor has requested that governance meetings be shortened from monthly two-hour meetings to monthly 30-minute meetings. The project manager is concerned that complex matters can't be adequately addressed in 30 minutes. What approach should the project manager propose?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the 30-minute format without modification
- **B**: Insist on maintaining two-hour meetings
- **C**: Propose a tiered approach: 30-minute status updates monthly, with longer sessions scheduled when complex decisions are needed, and pre-meeting materials to maximize meeting efficiency
- **D**: Move all governance to email to avoid time constraints

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The tiered approach accommodates the sponsor's desire for efficient regular meetings while ensuring complex matters get adequate time. Pre-meeting materials enable quick meetings when matters are straightforward. Flexibility to schedule longer sessions when needed addresses the PM's concern about complex matters.

Option A may not allow adequate discussion of complex matters.

Option B doesn't address the sponsor's legitimate interest in efficiency.

Option D loses the value of discussion and may not be appropriate for all decisions.
