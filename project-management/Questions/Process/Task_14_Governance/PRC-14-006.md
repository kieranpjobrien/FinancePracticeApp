# Question

A project steering committee meets monthly, but the project manager needs urgent decisions more frequently. Critical path decisions have been delayed waiting for the next scheduled meeting. What governance modification should be proposed?

## Options

- **A**: Bypass the steering committee for all decisions to speed things up
- **B**: Establish decision thresholds and delegation authorities so some decisions can be made without full committee involvement, with expedited escalation procedures for urgent matters
- **C**: Schedule daily steering committee meetings
- **D**: Make all decisions independently and inform the committee later

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Effective governance defines decision rights at different levels. Establishing thresholds (e.g., decisions under $50K or within schedule contingency can be made by PM) and delegation authorities speeds routine decisions while preserving steering committee oversight for significant matters. Expedited escalation handles genuine urgencies between meetings.

Option A abandons governance entirely, creating risk and accountability issues.

Option C is impractical for senior stakeholders' schedules.

Option D undermines the governance structure and may create problems.
