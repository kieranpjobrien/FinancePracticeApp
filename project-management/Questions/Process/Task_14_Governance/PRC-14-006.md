# Question

The steering committee meets monthly, but the project regularly needs decisions inside the month. Three critical-path decisions slipped last month waiting for the next meeting. What governance change should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Add monthly out-of-cycle meetings dedicated to urgent decisions, in addition to the regular monthly steering meeting.
- **B**: Define decision thresholds and delegated authorities so routine decisions happen between meetings, with an escalation route for the rest.
- **C**: Move the steering committee to a weekly cadence so decisions are never more than a week away from being resolved.
- **D**: Make the urgent decisions independently and report them at the next steering meeting for ratification by the committee.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Governance design should match the rhythm of decisions to the people who need to make them. (B) gets routine decisions out of the committee and gives a fast lane for urgent matters. (A) doubles meetings without solving capacity at the right level. (C) is impractical for senior schedules and oversolves the problem. (D) bypasses governance and accumulates accountability risk.
