# Question

A project manager discovers that decisions made in steering committee meetings are frequently overturned by individual executives outside of meetings. This creates confusion and delays. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept that executive decisions override steering committee decisions
- **B**: Escalate to the project sponsor to reinforce governance
- **C**: Work with the sponsor to clarify decision rights and ensure steering committee decisions are binding unless formally reconsidered
- **D**: Stop bringing decisions to the steering committee and go directly to individual executives

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Governance only works if it's respected. The PM should work with the sponsor to reinforce that steering committee decisions are organisationally binding. If executives have concerns, the proper channel is to raise them in the next committee meeting, not override unilaterally.

Option A (accept overrides) renders governance meaningless. If decisions can be casually overturned, there's no point in having a steering committee.

Option B (escalate to sponsor) is part of the solution but incomplete. Escalation without a proposed resolution shifts the problem rather than solving it.

Option D (bypass steering committee) worsens the problem. Ad-hoc decisions with individual executives will create more conflicts, not fewer.
