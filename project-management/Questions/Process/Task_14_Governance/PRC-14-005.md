# Question

The PM finds that decisions made in the project's steering committee are routinely overturned by individual executives in side conversations during the week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop tabling decisions in steering and approach each executive individually instead, since that is how decisions actually get made.
- **B**: Accept the side decisions as the operating reality and adjust the project plan accordingly.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor and request that the sponsor reinforce the steering committee's authority.
- **D**: Work with the sponsor to clarify decision rights, agree that steering decisions are binding unless formally reconsidered, and communicate the rule back to all members.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The fix has to address both the rule and the behaviour. (D) does both — co-designed with the sponsor, and communicated so everyone hears the same thing. (A) institutionalises the bad pattern. (B) abdicates governance entirely. (C) is partway there but escalation alone will not change behaviour without a clear rule and rollout.
