# Question

Why does PMI recommend documenting the project governance structure during initiation?

## Options

- **A**: It is a regulatory requirement under most national project standards.
- **B**: It allows the PMO to benchmark the project against historical performance data.
- **C**: It establishes shared understanding of decision rights and escalation paths so issues are routed to the right authority quickly.
- **D**: It produces an artefact that can be referenced during the lessons-learned session at project close.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Documented governance lets the team and stakeholders agree on who decides what before the first contentious decision arrives. (C) captures that operational benefit. (A) is not generally true; governance documentation is a PMI-recommended practice, not a legal requirement. (B) is a possible PMO use but not the reason to document it. (D) is a downstream side-effect rather than the primary purpose.
