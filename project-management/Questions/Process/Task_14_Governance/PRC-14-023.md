# Question

A project manager is establishing governance for a project with significant financial implications. What financial governance controls would be MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Let the PM approve all expenditures regardless of amount
- **B**: Require steering committee approval for any expense
- **C**: Establish spending authority limits at different levels (PM can approve up to X, sponsor up to Y, steering committee above Y), with regular financial reporting and variance analysis
- **D**: Rely on the finance department to track all spending

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Tiered financial authority enables routine spending to proceed efficiently while ensuring appropriate oversight of significant expenditures. Regular reporting and variance analysis provide visibility without requiring approval of every transaction. This balances control with operational efficiency.

Option A gives too much authority for significant expenditures.

Option B creates bottlenecks for routine spending.

Option D monitors but doesn't control spending decisions.
