# Question

Who typically serves as the primary decision-maker with ultimate authority over a project?

## Options

- **A**: Project manager
- **B**: Team lead
- **C**: Project sponsor
- **D**: Scrum master

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The project sponsor provides executive support and has ultimate authority over the project. They secure funding, resolve issues beyond the PM's authority, make final decisions on major matters, and champion the project within the organization.

Option A (PM) manages the project but operates within authority granted by the sponsor.

Option B (team lead) has technical or team authority, not project authority.

Option D (scrum master) facilitates the team but doesn't have decision authority over the project.
