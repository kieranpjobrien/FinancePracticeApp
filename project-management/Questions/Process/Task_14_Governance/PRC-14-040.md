# Question

A project has both internal stakeholders and external partners who contribute resources. The internal governance structure has clear authority, but decisions affecting external partners require their agreement. This dual structure is causing confusion about what can be decided internally versus what needs partner agreement. How should governance be clarified?

## Options

- **A**: Have internal governance make all decisions and inform partners
- **B**: Require partner agreement for all decisions
- **C**: Define clear boundaries between internal-only decisions (subject to internal governance) and joint decisions (requiring partner participation), with appropriate processes for each type
- **D**: Create completely separate governance for partner-related work

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Clarity comes from defining decision categories: what's internal-only (no partner decision rights), what's joint (requires partner agreement), and what's partner-only (their decision within their scope). Each category has appropriate governance. This prevents both overreaching (making joint decisions unilaterally) and gridlock (seeking agreement when not needed).

Option A ignores legitimate partner decision rights.

Option B creates unnecessary delays for internal matters.

Option D may not be practical when work is interconnected.
