# Question

A project team is geographically distributed and operates across multiple time zones. Governance meetings are scheduled at times convenient for headquarters, causing team members in other regions to attend very early or very late. This is affecting morale and participation. What should be adjusted?

## Options

- **A**: Continue as is since headquarters is the most important location
- **B**: Hold all governance meetings in writing only
- **C**: Implement fair meeting practices such as rotating meeting times, recording sessions for those who can't attend live, and enabling asynchronous input on governance decisions
- **D**: Exclude remote team members from governance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Fair governance for distributed teams requires accommodating different locations. Rotating times shares inconvenience fairly. Recordings enable asynchronous participation. Written input mechanisms ensure voices are heard even without live attendance. This maintains inclusive governance while acknowledging practical constraints.

Option A creates inequity and may reduce engagement from other regions.

Option B loses the value of discussion but may be part of a broader solution.

Option D excludes legitimate stakeholders.
