# Question

Your project team spans four time zones. All governance meetings are currently scheduled in the HQ business day, meaning team members in two regions attend late at night or before sunrise. Engagement and morale in those regions are dropping. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate meeting times across regions, record each session, and create asynchronous channels for written input on governance decisions.
- **B**: Keep the current slot because HQ stakeholders are the primary decision-makers and need prime attention.
- **C**: Move all governance to written-only updates so no one has to attend inconvenient meetings.
- **D**: Split the governance meetings by region so each area runs its own forum during local business hours.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Inclusive, distributed governance shares the inconvenience of off-hours meetings while preserving real-time discussion. (A) balances fairness, recording, and asynchronous input. (B) entrenches inequity and undermines engagement. (C) loses the benefit of real dialogue and weakens decision quality. (D) fragments governance and risks producing divergent decisions by region.
