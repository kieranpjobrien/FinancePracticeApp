# Question

A project is implementing a new system that will require ongoing operational support after go-live. The project's current governance structure doesn't include operations stakeholders because they weren't initially involved. As deployment approaches, the operations team is raising concerns that weren't considered earlier. How should governance be adjusted?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with current governance since operations is a post-project concern
- **B**: Add operations representation to project governance to ensure their concerns are addressed before deployment
- **C**: Let operations create their own governance separate from the project
- **D**: Delay the project until operations is satisfied

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Operations will own the system after go-live, making them legitimate project stakeholders. Including them in governance ensures their operational requirements (supportability, maintainability, operational procedures) are considered before deployment, leading to better outcomes and smoother transition.

Option A ignores stakeholders who will be significantly affected by project outcomes.

Option C creates separate governance that may conflict with project decisions.

Option D is reactive; better to include operations in governance to address concerns constructively.
