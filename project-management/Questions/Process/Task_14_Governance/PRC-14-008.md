# Question

A project spans three business units, each with its own VP. Every VP wants to approve anything affecting their unit. The sponsor sits above all three. Decisions are stalling because every change touches at least two units. How should governance be structured?

## Options

- **A**: Define decision domains for each VP, name the decisions that need joint sign-off and how they get approved, and route only unresolved conflicts to the sponsor.
- **B**: Have every decision approved by all three VPs in sequence so each retains full authority over their area.
- **C**: Push every decision to the sponsor, who can decide based on the VPs' inputs.
- **D**: Replace the three VPs in governance with a single delegate appointed by the sponsor.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects governance to assign decision authority by domain and define how shared decisions get made. (A) does both and reserves escalation for genuine conflicts. (B) is a guaranteed bottleneck and gives every VP a veto. (C) overloads the sponsor with operational decisions. (D) ignores legitimate stakeholder authority and is unlikely to be accepted.
