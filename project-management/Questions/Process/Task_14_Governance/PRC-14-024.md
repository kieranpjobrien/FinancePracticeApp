# Question

A project is being delivered by an external vendor with their own governance processes. The client organization also has governance requirements. The two governance approaches conflict in several areas: different change control processes, different status reporting formats, and different escalation paths. How should this be resolved?

## Options

- **A**: The client's governance always takes precedence
- **B**: The vendor's governance always takes precedence
- **C**: Develop an integrated governance framework that satisfies both parties' needs, mapping processes where possible and establishing clear interfaces where different processes must be maintained
- **D**: Maintain completely separate governance with no integration

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Both parties have legitimate governance needs. An integrated framework identifies where processes can be unified (common status reporting), where one party's process can satisfy both (if one change control process is acceptable to both), and where interfaces are needed (how vendor escalation connects to client escalation). This requires negotiation and documentation.

Option A may not satisfy vendor contractual or organizational requirements.

Option B may not satisfy client oversight needs.

Option D creates confusion and potential gaps or conflicts.
