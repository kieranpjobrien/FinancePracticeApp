# Question

What is an escalation path in project governance?

## Options

- **A**: A predefined route for elevating issues or decisions to higher authority when they cannot be resolved at the current level.
- **B**: A list of executives the project manager may contact directly for urgent operational matters.
- **C**: A formal process for accelerating activities on the schedule's critical path.
- **D**: A workflow for handling team performance issues outside the regular HR process.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

An escalation path defines who an issue or decision moves up to and under what conditions. (A) is the standard definition. (B) describes a contact list, not a path. (C) describes schedule compression, not governance. (D) describes a personnel process, unrelated to governance escalation.
