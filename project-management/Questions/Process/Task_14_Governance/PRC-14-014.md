# Question

What is an escalation path in project governance?

## Options

- **A**: A way to climb the organizational hierarchy quickly
- **B**: A predefined route for elevating issues or decisions to higher authority when they cannot be resolved at the current level
- **C**: A method for firing team members
- **D**: A technique for accelerating the schedule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. An escalation path defines how issues or decisions move up through levels of authority when they cannot be resolved at lower levels. It specifies who to escalate to, what triggers escalation, and expected response times. This ensures issues don't stall while providing appropriate oversight.

Option A mischaracterizes escalation as career advancement.

Option C describes a disciplinary process, not governance.

Option D describes schedule acceleration, not governance.
