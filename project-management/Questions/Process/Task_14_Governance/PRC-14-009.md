# Question

A Scrum delivery team is told the corporate PMO requires stage-gate approvals at the end of each quarter. The team fears governance will smother their cadence. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Map sprint reviews and release planning to the stage-gate evidence the PMO needs, and align quarterly increments with the gates.
- **B**: Negotiate exemption from stage gates because they conflict with agile delivery.
- **C**: Convert the team back to a predictive plan so it cleanly satisfies the gates.
- **D**: Run sprints internally and let the PMO see only quarterly status reports without involving the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring guidance handles this case directly: keep the cadence, design the evidence into existing ceremonies. (A) does that. (B) frames governance as the enemy and is rarely granted. (C) gives up working practice for ceremony alone. (D) hides reality from governance and undermines trust when the next gate review is contested.
