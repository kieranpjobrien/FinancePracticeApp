# Question

An agile project using Scrum needs to satisfy organizational governance requirements including stage-gate approvals. The team is concerned that traditional governance will undermine their agility. What approach should the project manager facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Request exemption from all organizational governance
- **B**: Abandon Scrum and use waterfall to match governance expectations
- **C**: Map Scrum events and artifacts to governance requirements where possible, and design incremental reviews that satisfy stage-gate needs without compromising agility
- **D**: Ignore governance requirements and focus on delivery

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile can work within organizational governance by thoughtfully mapping practices. Sprint reviews can serve as governance checkpoints, potentially several sprints can align with a stage-gate. Release planning provides governance visibility. The key is maintaining agile's delivery cadence while satisfying legitimate oversight needs.

Option A may not be possible and doesn't address legitimate governance needs.

Option B abandons agile benefits unnecessarily.

Option D creates organizational conflict and compliance issues.
