# Question

A project manager is establishing governance for a cross-functional initiative spanning four departments. Each department head wants decision authority over their domain. How should the PM structure governance?

## Options

- **A**: Give each department head full authority over decisions affecting their area
- **B**: Establish a steering committee with all department heads for collective decisions
- **C**: Centralise all authority with the project sponsor
- **D**: Create a RACI matrix defining decision rights by topic, with the steering committee handling cross-departmental conflicts

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. A RACI matrix provides clarity on who decides what, respecting departmental expertise while establishing a mechanism (steering committee) for decisions that span boundaries. This balances efficiency (most decisions made quickly by appropriate authority) with coordination (cross-cutting issues escalated appropriately).

Option A (departmental silos) ignores integration. Many project decisions affect multiple departments and need coordinated decision-making.

Option B (committee for everything) is slow. Requiring all department heads to weigh in on every decision creates bottlenecks and delays.

Option C (sponsor authority) doesn't scale. The sponsor shouldn't be deciding operational details - that's what governance structures delegate.
