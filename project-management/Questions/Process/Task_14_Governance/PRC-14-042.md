# Question

What is the difference between project governance and organizational governance?

## Options

- **A**: There is no difference
- **B**: Project governance applies to a specific project while organizational governance applies to the entire organization including how projects fit within the organization's oversight structure
- **C**: Project governance only applies to large projects
- **D**: Organizational governance doesn't affect projects

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Project governance is the framework for a specific project's decision-making, roles, and oversight. Organizational governance is the broader framework for how the organization operates, including how projects are authorized, prioritized, and overseen. Project governance typically operates within organizational governance.

Option A ignores the different scopes.

Option C is incorrect - governance applies to projects of all sizes.

Option D is incorrect - organizational governance often defines how projects are governed.
