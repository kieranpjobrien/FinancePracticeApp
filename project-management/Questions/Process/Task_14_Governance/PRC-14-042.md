# Question

How does project governance differ from organisational governance?

## Options

- **A**: Project governance addresses a project's decision rights, while organisational governance is the broader framework for all projects.
- **B**: They are interchangeable terms; PMI uses both to describe the same concept at different levels of formality and scope.
- **C**: Project governance only applies to predictive projects, while organisational governance applies across portfolios regardless of methodology used.
- **D**: Project governance covers people decisions, while organisational governance covers financial and strategic decisions across the enterprise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes between governance scoped to a single project (sponsor, steering committee, CCB) and governance scoped to the enterprise (board oversight, portfolio prioritisation, policy). (A) captures that hierarchy. (B) treats two distinct concepts as one. (C) wrongly ties governance to delivery approach. (D) carves governance along functional lines rather than scope.
