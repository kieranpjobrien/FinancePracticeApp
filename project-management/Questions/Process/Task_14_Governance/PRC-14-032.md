# Question

A project is implementing significant organizational change that will affect many departments. Each department head wants decision authority over how the change affects their area. Without clear governance, decisions are either delayed (waiting for consensus) or contradictory (different departments deciding differently). What governance structure is needed?

## Options

- **A**: Let each department head decide independently for their area
- **B**: Have the CEO make all decisions to ensure consistency
- **C**: Establish a change governance board with department representation, clear decision criteria for balancing departmental and organizational interests, and defined processes for department-specific vs. cross-cutting decisions
- **D**: Delay the project until all departments agree

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Organizational change needs governance that balances local and enterprise concerns. A board with department representation ensures voices are heard, clear criteria ensure decisions aren't arbitrary, and categorizing decision types (department-specific vs. cross-cutting) enables appropriate authority levels. This provides structure while respecting departmental interests.

Option A creates inconsistency across the organization.

Option B overloads the CEO and may not account for departmental expertise.

Option D may never achieve universal agreement.
