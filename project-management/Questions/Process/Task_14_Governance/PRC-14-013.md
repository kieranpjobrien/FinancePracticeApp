# Question

A project sponsor is very hands-off and rarely available. Critical decisions requiring sponsor approval are being delayed. The project manager has tried multiple times to get the sponsor's attention. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Make sponsor-level decisions independently
- **B**: Halt the project until the sponsor engages
- **C**: Work with the sponsor to either obtain delegated authority for certain decisions, identify an alternate decision-maker, or establish a communication approach that works with the sponsor's constraints
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor's supervisor immediately

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should address the problem directly with the sponsor, exploring solutions: delegated authority for decisions within certain parameters, designating someone who can act on the sponsor's behalf, or finding communication mechanisms that fit the sponsor's style (brief written summaries, scheduled brief calls). The goal is enabling decisions while respecting the sponsor's constraints.

Option A exceeds authority and creates risk.

Option B is reactive and harms the project.

Option D may be appropriate later but should follow direct engagement.
