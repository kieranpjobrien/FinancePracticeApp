# Question

A PM's project has its own risk register and review cadence. The enterprise risk committee asks for all material project risks to be reported to them in a different format at a different cadence. The team resents the duplication. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push back on enterprise risk and ask them to rely on the project's existing risk register and cadence for reporting.
- **B**: Accept both structures unchanged and have a team member dedicated to producing the enterprise-format report each cycle separately.
- **C**: Send the enterprise risk committee a zero-return each cycle and escalate only when the project itself deems a risk material.
- **D**: Agree with the enterprise risk committee a shared definition of materiality and automate a mapping from project to enterprise format.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI governance integrates project risk with enterprise risk through shared definitions, not duplication or avoidance. (D) solves the structural interface issue. (A) refuses a legitimate enterprise need. (B) burns effort on manual parallel reporting. (C) under-reports to the enterprise committee by design.
