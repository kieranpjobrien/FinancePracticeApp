# Question

A PM notices that workstream leads consistently report status as green when honest assessment would be amber. When challenged, leads say they fear being singled out at steerco. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Override the workstream leads and report honest amber status yourself to the steering committee at the next meeting.
- **B**: Address the psychological-safety issue with the steering committee and the leads to enable honest status reporting going forward.
- **C**: Keep accepting green reports but add a private amber register so the PM retains a truer picture of project health.
- **D**: Change the RAG criteria so more items count as green and the reporting lines match the leads' current assessments anyway.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's transparency principle works only inside psychological safety, and the PM must address the fear driving the behaviour. (B) fixes the root cause. (A) publicly overrides leads without addressing why they were not honest. (C) keeps two sets of books which erodes trust further. (D) changes thresholds to make bad news look acceptable.
