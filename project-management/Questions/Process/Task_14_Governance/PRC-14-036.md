# Question

You are delivering a SaaS product that will be deployed into five regulated client organisations, each with its own governance and compliance requirements, on top of your own internal controls. How should governance be structured?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the governance requirements of the largest client, since meeting those typically satisfies the others by default.
- **B**: Map all internal and client governance requirements, design a core framework that satisfies the common set, and define documented extensions for each client-specific variation.
- **C**: Follow only the internal governance framework during build, then add client-specific controls during the deployment phase at each site.
- **D**: Negotiate with each client to waive their internal governance in favour of your organisation's standard framework.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

With multiple external governance regimes, a union of requirements is usually impractical and a single-client default is risky. (B) is the classic PMI tailoring answer: identify common ground, satisfy it once, then handle variants explicitly. (A) gambles on overlap without verifying it. (C) defers known requirements and will produce expensive rework in deployment. (D) is unlikely to succeed where clients have regulatory mandates and is the wrong first move regardless.
