# Question

An internal audit of the project flags weak evidence for change-control decisions over the past six months. The PM believes decisions were made correctly but documentation did not capture them. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reconstruct missing documentation from memory, emails and chat threads so the audit findings can be cleared before closure.
- **B**: Accept the findings, strengthen documentation going forward, and remediate only the highest-materiality historical decisions with owners.
- **C**: Contest the audit findings on the basis that the decisions themselves were demonstrably correct even without documentation.
- **D**: Escalate the audit report to the sponsor and ask them to instruct the auditors to reconsider their findings fairly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to accept legitimate audit findings, improve going forward, and prioritise remediation by materiality. (B) does all three proportionately. (A) creates retrofit documentation that may not stand up. (C) contests a control finding by pointing at outcomes, which is not how audit works. (D) attempts to overturn an audit by escalation rather than remediation.
