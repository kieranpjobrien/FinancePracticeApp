# Question

A PM identifies a material risk that could breach the organisation's stated risk appetite. The team wants to handle it at PM level; the steering committee is distracted by a separate crisis elsewhere. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Handle the risk at PM level for now given steerco's distraction, and report it formally at the next scheduled meeting.
- **B**: Escalate the risk immediately to the steering committee despite the crisis, making clear it exceeds agreed risk appetite.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor informally whether the risk needs formal escalation or can be handled at PM level given the circumstances.
- **D**: Add the risk to the risk register at high rating and continue mitigating at PM level while logging the decision clearly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats risk appetite thresholds as non-negotiable triggers for escalation regardless of steerco workload. (B) respects the governance rule as designed. (A) defers required escalation to suit circumstances. (C) asks the sponsor to bypass the governance threshold informally. (D) absorbs at PM level a risk that explicitly exceeds delegated authority.
