# Question

You are setting up the Change Control Board for a mid-sized infrastructure project. Which composition is MOST consistent with PMI guidance?

## Options

- **A**: Permanent members with authority over scope, schedule, budget, and quality, with specialists invited as needed.
- **B**: All functional managers from departments touched by the project, regardless of whether they have authority over the baselines.
- **C**: The project manager alone, since any change should reflect the PM's integrated view of the project and its priorities.
- **D**: A rotating group of subject-matter experts so that decisions always benefit from fresh technical perspectives and current expertise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A CCB needs authority to bind the organisation across the constraints a change touches. (A) places authority where it belongs and brings in expertise on demand. (B) confuses being affected by changes with having authority over them, producing an unwieldy group. (C) collapses the separation of duties between proposing and approving changes. (D) prizes expertise over authority and would leave the board unable to commit organisational resources.
