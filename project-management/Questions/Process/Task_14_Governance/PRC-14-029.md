# Question

A project manager needs to establish a Change Control Board (CCB) for a medium-sized project. What composition would be MOST effective?

## Options

- **A**: Include every stakeholder to ensure all voices are heard
- **B**: Include only technical experts who understand the changes
- **C**: Include representatives with authority over scope, schedule, budget, and quality - typically including the sponsor or delegate, key stakeholders, and technical expertise as needed
- **D**: Let the PM serve as the sole CCB member for efficiency

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. An effective CCB needs authority to make binding decisions across the dimensions affected by changes (scope, schedule, cost, quality). Representatives should have appropriate authority, not just opinions. Technical expertise can be brought in as needed for evaluation rather than permanent membership.

Option A creates an unwieldy group and may include people without decision authority.

Option B lacks business perspective and authority over budget/schedule.

Option D concentrates too much authority and may lack needed perspectives.
