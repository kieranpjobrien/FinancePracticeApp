# Question

A highly engaged sponsor unexpectedly leaves the organisation mid-project. The new sponsor takes over but has no context and limited capacity. Governance decisions stall for several weeks while they get up to speed. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run a structured induction with the new sponsor covering context, decisions pending, risks, and agreed governance expectations.
- **B**: Continue operating under the previous sponsor's decisions and assumptions until the new sponsor finds time to engage fully.
- **C**: Escalate the stalled decisions to the steering committee so they can act while the new sponsor settles into the role.
- **D**: Make pending decisions yourself to maintain momentum and update the new sponsor on outcomes when they become available.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement places onboarding new sponsors squarely on the PM and does so proactively. (A) addresses the root cause of the stall. (B) uses assumptions about a predecessor's preferences as cover. (C) bypasses the sponsor role for decisions that sit there. (D) exceeds the PM's authority and presents the new sponsor with faits accomplis.
