# Question

A project involves both internal teams and external vendors. The internal governance structure is well-defined, but decisions involving vendors often stall because authority over vendor relationships is unclear. What should be addressed?

## Options

- **A**: Exclude vendors from governance processes
- **B**: Let vendors make their own decisions about their work
- **C**: Extend the governance framework to clarify decision rights involving vendors, including who has authority to direct vendor work, approve vendor deliverables, and resolve vendor disputes
- **D**: Route all vendor decisions through procurement

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Governance should address all project relationships, including vendors. Clarifying who can direct vendor work, approve deliverables, make contract-related decisions, and resolve disputes prevents the confusion causing stalls. This might involve the PM, procurement, technical leads, and contract managers with defined authorities.

Option A creates an ungoverned area prone to problems.

Option B may result in vendor work that doesn't meet project needs.

Option D may be part of the solution but procurement alone doesn't address all vendor-related decisions.
