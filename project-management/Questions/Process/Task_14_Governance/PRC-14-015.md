# Question

A project mixes internal teams with three vendor partners. Internal governance is well-defined, but vendor-related decisions stall because no one is sure who can direct vendors, approve their deliverables, or resolve disputes. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Route every vendor-related decision through procurement, since they own the contracts.
- **B**: Let each vendor make calls about their own work and only intervene if quality issues arise.
- **C**: Exclude vendors from project governance and manage them entirely through contract terms.
- **D**: Extend the governance framework to define decision rights involving vendors — direction, deliverable approval, dispute resolution, and contract change requests.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Vendor relationships need explicit governance, not an assumption that "the contract" or procurement covers everything. (D) addresses every gap the question names. (A) sends operational decisions to a function that does not have the technical context. (B) creates an ungoverned space. (C) cuts vendors out of conversations they need to be in for the project to succeed.
