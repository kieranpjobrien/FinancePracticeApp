# Question

A project has a governance structure that requires approval from multiple committees for major decisions: the technical review board, the business committee, and the executive steering committee. A time-sensitive decision needs all three approvals, but they meet at different times and the next aligned opportunity is six weeks away. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait six weeks for proper approvals
- **B**: Get approvals sequentially as each committee meets
- **C**: Bypass the governance process due to urgency
- **D**: Request expedited decision procedures such as emergency sessions, out-of-meeting approvals, or delegated authority for urgent matters

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The correct answer is D. Governance should accommodate urgent situations. The PM should request expedited procedures: emergency meetings (even brief virtual ones), email/documented approval processes, or delegation to individuals who can act for their committees on urgent matters. Well-designed governance has mechanisms for urgency.

Option A delays too long for a time-sensitive matter.

Option B still takes weeks if committees meet monthly.

Option C violates governance without attempting proper escalation.
