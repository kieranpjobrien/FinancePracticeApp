# Question

A new executive joins the organization and wants to be added to the project steering committee. This would increase the committee to eight members. The project manager is concerned about decision-making efficiency with a larger group. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to add the new executive
- **B**: Add the executive without any changes
- **C**: Discuss with the sponsor the implications of committee size on decision efficiency, and consider whether a tiered governance structure or different role for the new executive might be more effective
- **D**: Remove another member to make room

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should raise legitimate concerns about governance effectiveness with the sponsor. Options might include: keeping the core committee smaller with the new executive in an advisory or informed role, creating tiers of governance, or proceeding with the larger committee but adjusting meeting structures. The decision rests with appropriate authority but should consider the PM's input.

Option A may not be the PM's decision to make.

Option B ignores legitimate concerns about effectiveness.

Option D may remove needed perspectives without proper consideration.
