# Question

A new vice president has joined the organisation and has asked to be added to your seven-member steering committee. Adding her would push the group to eight members, and you are concerned about decision velocity. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly add the VP to the distribution list but only invite her to quarterly sessions to keep the working group small and fast-moving.
- **B**: Politely decline the request, citing the documented governance charter that caps the committee at seven members for decision velocity.
- **C**: Discuss the request with the sponsor, surface decision-efficiency concerns, and propose a tiered structure or advisory role for the VP.
- **D**: Ask another committee member to step down so the seat count remains stable before the VP joins the group formally.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Membership of the steering committee is the sponsor's call, not the PM's. (C) gives the sponsor the information needed to make a good decision while respecting the VP's interest. (A) hides governance changes from a senior stakeholder, which is an integrity issue. (B) refuses a senior stakeholder unilaterally without consulting the sponsor. (D) reshuffles existing members without addressing the underlying question of optimal committee design.
