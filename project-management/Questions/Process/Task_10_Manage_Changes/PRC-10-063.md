# Question

Eight months into a predictive government transformation, the original sponsor retires and is replaced by an executive with different priorities. The new sponsor proposes substantial changes to direction. The PM is considering the governance path. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the new sponsor's preferences immediately to demonstrate responsiveness to the new leadership.
- **B**: Frame the proposed shifts as formal change requests for impact assessment and governance review.
- **C**: Continue with the original plan until the new sponsor formally signs updated project documents first.
- **D**: Ask the previous sponsor to stay engaged long enough to sign off on any direction changes themselves.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Even sponsor-initiated changes go through integrated change control; framing proposed shifts as formal change requests preserves governance and makes impact visible. (A) implements without impact analysis simply to accommodate new authority. (C) creates a standoff with the current sponsor rather than engaging through process. (D) relies on a retired sponsor who no longer holds accountability.
