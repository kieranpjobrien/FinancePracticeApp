# Question

A change request is rejected by the CCB. The requester is upset and says they'll escalate to the CEO. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reverse the decision to avoid escalation
- **B**: Explain the CCB's reasoning and suggest the requester provide additional information if they believe the decision was wrong
- **C**: Support the requester's escalation since stakeholders have that right
- **D**: Document the rejection and consider the matter closed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The PM should ensure the requester understands why the change was rejected. Perhaps the impact analysis revealed problems, or it conflicted with project objectives. If the requester believes important information was missed, they can resubmit with additional justification. Escalation may be appropriate but should follow proper channels.

Option A undermines the CCB's authority and process integrity.

Option C enables escalation without first ensuring the decision was understood.

Option D ignores a stakeholder's legitimate concerns.
