# Question

A stakeholder has submitted six change requests in the past month; the CCB rejected all of them. The stakeholder is openly frustrated and says the process is rigged against their group. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Approve a low-impact change to demonstrate the process is fair and rebuild goodwill.
- **B**: Schedule a one-on-one to understand the underlying business need behind the requests and help shape future submissions that align with project objectives.
- **C**: Inform the stakeholder that CCB decisions are final and the project cannot reopen them.
- **D**: Restrict the stakeholder's ability to submit change requests until they better understand the project's priorities.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Repeated rejections usually signal that the underlying need is real but the framing or fit is wrong. The PM should engage directly to surface the actual need and help the stakeholder shape requests that the CCB can act on. (A) corrupts change control to manage the relationship. (C) addresses the symptom and damages the relationship. (D) restricts a legitimate stakeholder voice.
