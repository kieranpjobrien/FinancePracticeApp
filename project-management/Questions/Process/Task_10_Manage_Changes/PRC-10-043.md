# Question

A stakeholder frequently submits change requests that are consistently rejected. The stakeholder is frustrated and feels unheard. How should the PM address this?

## Options

- **A**: Approve some changes to keep the stakeholder satisfied
- **B**: Meet with the stakeholder to understand their needs and help them frame requests more effectively
- **C**: Explain that the CCB's decisions are final
- **D**: Limit the stakeholder's ability to submit requests

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Consistent rejections may indicate the stakeholder's underlying needs aren't being addressed, or they're framing requests in ways that don't align with project priorities. Understanding their needs might reveal alternative approaches that would be approved, or help them understand why certain changes don't fit current objectives.

Option A compromises change control to manage relationships.

Option C doesn't address the underlying issue.

Option D restricts legitimate stakeholder participation.
