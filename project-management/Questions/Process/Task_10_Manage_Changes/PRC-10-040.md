# Question

Mid-implementation, the team finds that an approved change is roughly twice as much work as estimated when the CCB approved it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push to completion since the change has CCB authorisation and stopping mid-work creates waste.
- **B**: Pause implementation, prepare an updated impact assessment, and return to the CCB for a decision based on the new information.
- **C**: Continue implementation and capture the variance in lessons learned for the next project.
- **D**: Reduce the scope of the change unilaterally to fit it inside the original effort estimate.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The CCB authorised the change based on a specific impact picture; if reality differs materially, the original decision may no longer hold and the body must be given the chance to reconsider. (A) treats authorisation as immutable regardless of new facts. (C) accepts a significant variance without authority. (D) modifies the approved change without going back through the process.
