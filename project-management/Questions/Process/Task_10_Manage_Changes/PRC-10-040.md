# Question

A team discovers during implementation that an approved change is more complex than estimated. The actual effort will be double the original estimate. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Complete the change since it was approved
- **B**: Stop implementation and return to the CCB with updated impact assessment
- **C**: Complete the change and document the variance in lessons learned
- **D**: Partially implement the change to stay within original estimates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The CCB approved the change based on certain assumptions about impact. When reality differs significantly, the original approval may no longer be valid. The CCB should reassess with accurate information - they may still approve, approve with modifications, or reject given the true cost.

Option A continues based on invalidated assumptions.

Option C accepts significant variance without proper authority.

Option D delivers incomplete work without authorisation.
