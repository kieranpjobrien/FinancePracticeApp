# Question

An approved change on a predictive infrastructure project adds scope. The sponsor has told the PM the delivery date cannot slip. The PM's analysis shows either the date or scope must give. What should the PM present to the CCB?

## Options

- **A**: A single recommendation that the date must slip because the analysis shows date commitment is unachievable.
- **B**: A single recommendation that scope be cut because the analysis shows the date commitment must be preserved.
- **C**: A request for more planning time before the CCB meeting so the PM can find a way to hold both together.
- **D**: Trade-off options with impact analysis across scope, schedule, cost, and quality for the CCB to choose from.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Change control decisions belong to the CCB with informed options; the PM presents analysis and trade-offs rather than pre-deciding. (A) pre-empts the CCB's decision and removes their governance role. (B) pre-empts similarly in the opposite direction. (C) delays rather than surfacing the real trade-off that exists now.
