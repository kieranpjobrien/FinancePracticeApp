# Question

Late on Friday the sponsor calls and verbally instructs the PM to start work on a change immediately, saying the formal approval will follow Monday. The change management plan defines an emergency procedure but it requires an emergency CCB approver, not just sponsor verbal direction. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin work on the sponsor's verbal instruction and ensure written approval is filed Monday.
- **B**: Decline to begin work until the documented emergency procedure has been followed and the emergency approver has signed off.
- **C**: Notify the team to prepare to start Monday after formal CCB approval is in place.
- **D**: Convene the full CCB by phone over the weekend rather than using the emergency procedure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When a documented emergency procedure exists, the PM follows it rather than substituting an informal verbal authorisation, even from the sponsor. (A) bypasses the defined emergency path and treats verbal instruction as authority that the procedure has explicitly delegated elsewhere. (C) ignores the urgency the sponsor is signalling. (D) contradicts the change management plan by replacing the defined emergency mechanism with an ad-hoc full-CCB session.
