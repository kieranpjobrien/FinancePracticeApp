# Question

The project sponsor verbally instructs the PM to implement a change, saying formal approval will come later. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change since the sponsor has authority
- **B**: Refuse to implement without written approval
- **C**: Document the verbal instruction and implement, ensuring formal approval follows
- **D**: Escalate to the CCB to override the sponsor's instruction

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The sponsor has authority, and verbal instructions in urgent situations can be valid. However, the PM should document the instruction (who, what, when) and ensure formal approval follows for the record. This balances responsiveness with proper documentation.

Option A implements without documentation, creating traceability gaps.

Option B may be too rigid in urgent situations where the sponsor has clear authority.

Option D inappropriately escalates - the sponsor may have authority that supersedes normal CCB process.
