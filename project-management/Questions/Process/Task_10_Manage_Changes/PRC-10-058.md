# Question

A Scrum team working on a retail platform tells the PM the product owner keeps declining technical debt stories in favour of new features. The team fears stability will collapse within two releases. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Help the team quantify technical debt impact and present the risk to the product owner for informed prioritisation.
- **B**: Override the product owner and direct the team to allocate twenty percent of each sprint to technical debt work.
- **C**: Let the product owner continue and surface the issue after the predicted stability failure has occurred.
- **D**: Escalate the product owner's behaviour to the sponsor and ask for a replacement with better judgement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Product owner decisions improve with evidence; the PM's job is to surface impact so prioritisation trade-offs are informed. (B) overrides the product owner rather than influencing through evidence. (C) waits for the failure the team is trying to prevent. (D) escalates the person before attempting evidence-based coaching and engagement.
