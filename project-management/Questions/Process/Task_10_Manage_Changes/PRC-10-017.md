# Question

The team is mid-sprint with a clear sprint goal. The product owner brings an urgent customer item and asks to add it to the current sprint. What should the scrum master recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the request to protect the sprint commitment and add the item to the next sprint.
- **B**: Pull the item in and ask the team to absorb the extra work to keep the rest of the sprint intact.
- **C**: Have the product owner and team negotiate: if the item is taken in, an equivalent item is removed and the sprint goal is reassessed.
- **D**: Cancel the sprint, replan, and start a new sprint that includes the urgent item.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Scrum allows mid-sprint scope negotiation between the product owner and the development team, but capacity is finite, so something must give. (C) preserves sustainable pace and an honest commitment. (A) is too rigid for a genuinely urgent customer issue. (B) overloads the team and erodes commitment integrity. (D) is a heavyweight response reserved for cases where the sprint goal becomes obsolete; a single urgent item rarely meets that bar.
