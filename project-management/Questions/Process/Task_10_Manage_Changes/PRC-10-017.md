# Question

An agile team has committed to a sprint goal. The product owner wants to add an urgent item mid-sprint. What should the scrum master recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Reject all mid-sprint changes to protect the sprint commitment
- **B**: Accept the item if the team agrees and adjust the sprint commitment accordingly
- **C**: Add the item without adjusting the sprint commitment since it's urgent
- **D**: Wait until the next sprint planning to add the item

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Agile accommodates change, but mid-sprint changes should be exceptions with team agreement. If the item is truly urgent, the team can accept it but should remove equivalent work to maintain a realistic commitment. The sprint goal may also need adjustment. This maintains sustainable pace while responding to urgent needs.

Option A is too rigid for genuinely urgent situations.

Option C overloads the team without adjusting expectations.

Option D may not be appropriate if the item is genuinely urgent.
