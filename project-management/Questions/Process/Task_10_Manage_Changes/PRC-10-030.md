# Question

A PM has authority to approve changes under $5,000. A change request estimates $4,500 but the PM suspects actual costs will exceed $5,000. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change since the estimate is under the threshold
- **B**: Refine the estimate before making an approval decision
- **C**: Route to the CCB to be safe
- **D**: Approve the change but monitor actual costs closely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Making decisions based on estimates the PM believes are inaccurate isn't appropriate. The PM should refine the estimate to get a more reliable cost projection before deciding whether this falls within their authority or needs CCB review. Decisions should be based on best available information.

Option A uses a suspect estimate to justify approval authority.

Option C may be appropriate but should follow better estimation, not just caution.

Option D approves based on unreliable data with hope for the best.
