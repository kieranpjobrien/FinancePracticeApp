# Question

Eighteen months in, the project has absorbed dozens of approved changes. The product looks substantially different from what the original business case described. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Continue executing because each individual change was already approved by the CCB through due process.
- **B**: Commission a business-case revalidation to confirm the evolved project still delivers the expected benefits.
- **C**: Roll back the accumulated approved changes to restore the original scope so the business case still applies.
- **D**: Capture the cumulative drift in lessons learned so the next similar project can avoid the same outcome.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Individual change approvals do not guarantee that the cumulative result still delivers the strategic value originally sanctioned. A business-case revalidation surfaces whether to continue, adjust, or stop. (A) treats individual approvals as a substitute for strategic assurance. (C) is impractical and discards work that may still create value. (D) records learning but does not address whether continued investment is still justified.
