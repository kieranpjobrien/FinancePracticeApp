# Question

How does gold plating differ from scope creep?

## Options

- **A**: Gold plating is when the team intentionally adds work beyond requirements; scope creep is uncontrolled expansion of scope from any source.
- **B**: Gold plating is approved by the customer; scope creep is not approved by anyone.
- **C**: Gold plating only affects quality attributes; scope creep only affects functional requirements.
- **D**: Gold plating is regarded as positive customer service; scope creep is regarded as negative.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Gold plating is team-initiated extra work delivered without being asked for; scope creep is the broader phenomenon of scope expanding outside controlled change, regardless of source. (B) is wrong because gold plating is exactly what is not asked for. (C) misrepresents gold plating, which adds work, not just quality. (D) is wrong because both are problems when they bypass change control.
