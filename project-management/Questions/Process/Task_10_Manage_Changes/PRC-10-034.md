# Question

How does gold plating differ from scope creep?

## Options

- **A**: Gold plating is team-added work beyond requirements; scope creep is uncontrolled expansion from any source.
- **B**: Gold plating is approved by the customer directly; scope creep is not approved by anyone on the project.
- **C**: Gold plating only affects quality attributes; scope creep only affects functional requirements in delivery.
- **D**: Gold plating is regarded as positive customer service; scope creep is always regarded as a negative outcome.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Gold plating is team-initiated extra work delivered without being asked for; scope creep is the broader phenomenon of scope expanding outside controlled change, regardless of source. (B) is wrong because gold plating is exactly what is not asked for. (C) misrepresents gold plating, which adds work, not just quality. (D) is wrong because both are problems when they bypass change control.
