# Question

Gold plating differs from scope creep in that:

## Options

- **A**: Gold plating is approved; scope creep is not
- **B**: Gold plating is intentional additions by the team; scope creep is uncontrolled expansion from any source
- **C**: Gold plating affects quality; scope creep affects scope
- **D**: Gold plating is positive; scope creep is negative

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Gold plating refers to team members intentionally adding features or capabilities beyond what's required, often with good intentions. Scope creep is uncontrolled expansion of project scope from any source, often through incremental changes that bypass formal control. Neither is desirable without proper change control.

Option A incorrectly suggests gold plating is approved.

Option C mischaracterises gold plating - it adds scope/features, not just quality.

Option D oversimplifies. Neither is inherently positive; both can cause problems.
