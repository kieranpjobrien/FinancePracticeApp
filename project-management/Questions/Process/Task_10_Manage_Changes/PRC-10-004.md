# Question

A project team discovers that a vendor's component doesn't meet the technical specifications documented in the contract. The vendor claims the specifications were ambiguous and their interpretation is valid. Resolving this could delay the project by 4 weeks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to legal to enforce the contract
- **B**: Accept the component as-is to avoid schedule delay
- **C**: Review the original specifications with the vendor to clarify requirements and determine if rework is needed
- **D**: Terminate the vendor contract and find an alternative supplier

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Before escalating or making major decisions, the PM should clarify the actual situation. Reviewing specifications together may reveal genuine ambiguity (requiring negotiation) or clear non-compliance (supporting enforcement). This fact-finding step informs the appropriate next action.

Option A (escalate to legal) may be necessary but is premature. Legal involvement is expensive and adversarial - it should follow failed attempts at resolution, not precede them.

Option B (accept as-is) may compromise project quality. Accepting non-conforming deliverables without understanding the technical implications could create downstream problems.

Option D (terminate contract) is the most extreme response and likely the most expensive. Termination should be a last resort after other resolution attempts fail.
