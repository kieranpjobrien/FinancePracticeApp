# Question

The CCB has rejected a change request submitted by a senior stakeholder who has a strong working relationship with the PM. The stakeholder has not yet been told. The PM is drafting the response. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Delay communicating the rejection until an alternative can be proposed to soften the disappointing outcome.
- **B**: Ask the CCB chair to deliver the rejection so the PM's relationship with the stakeholder is not harmed.
- **C**: Quietly imply the change is still under discussion, to preserve the relationship while awaiting new information.
- **D**: Communicate the rejection promptly with the reasoning and any path to resubmit with stronger justification.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Change control decisions are communicated promptly, transparently, and with reasoning so stakeholders can act accordingly and trust the process. (A) delays bad news in a way that undermines trust when it surfaces. (B) offloads the PM's communication responsibility. (C) misrepresents the status, which breaches ethics and damages future trust.
