# Question

A stakeholder submits a change request that would significantly improve the product but requires extending the deadline by two weeks. The project has schedule contingency remaining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change since contingency covers the schedule impact
- **B**: Reject the change since the original deadline should be maintained
- **C**: Process the change through integrated change control for proper evaluation and approval
- **D**: Implement the change but hide the schedule impact within contingency

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. All changes, regardless of how beneficial they appear, should go through integrated change control. This ensures proper impact analysis, stakeholder awareness, and authorised decision-making. Using contingency for scope changes rather than risk responses is also questionable and should be explicitly approved.

Option A bypasses proper change control and misuses contingency.

Option B rejects without proper evaluation.

Option D is unethical - hiding impacts in contingency lacks transparency.
