# Question

A stakeholder submits a change that would clearly improve the product but would push the deadline by two weeks. The schedule reserve has enough buffer to absorb the slip. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change at the PM's discretion since the schedule reserve covers the impact.
- **B**: Implement the change and consume the reserve quietly to keep the published deadline intact.
- **C**: Submit the change request through integrated change control with full impact analysis, including the proposed reserve consumption.
- **D**: Decline the change because reserves should be preserved for risk responses, not new scope.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Beneficial changes still go through integrated change control so impacts, including the use of management or schedule reserve, are visible and authorised. (A) bypasses the process and unilaterally consumes reserve. (B) hides impact, which is an ethics issue and erodes the integrity of the schedule baseline. (D) is a unilateral rejection without analysis; whether reserve can fund the change is a CCB decision informed by the impact analysis.
