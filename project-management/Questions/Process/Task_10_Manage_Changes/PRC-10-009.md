# Question

What is the PRIMARY purpose of a change control board?

## Options

- **A**: To generate ideas for project improvements during execution.
- **B**: To formally review, evaluate, approve, defer, or reject change requests.
- **C**: To execute approved changes against the project deliverables.
- **D**: To communicate change decisions to all project stakeholders.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A CCB is a chartered group that decides on change requests and records those decisions for traceability. (A) describes brainstorming or continuous improvement, not formal change governance. (C) describes implementation, which the project team performs after CCB approval. (D) describes a communications activity that follows the decision; it is not the CCB's primary function.
