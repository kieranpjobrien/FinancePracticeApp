# Question

A change request on a hybrid SaaS programme is positioned as "cosmetic" by the requester: updating branding colours across the interface. The PM suspects deeper impact because the branding touches shared design system components used by three products. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change under an expedited cosmetic review path so delivery does not slow unnecessarily.
- **B**: Conduct a full impact assessment across scope, schedule, cost, and affected products before CCB review.
- **C**: Reject the change request and ask the requester to resubmit once they have narrowed its stated scope.
- **D**: Treat the request as low risk and bundle it with similar items for a quarterly review at end-of-quarter.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

"Cosmetic" labels are unreliable; integrated change control requires real impact assessment before approval, especially when shared components are touched. (A) trusts the requester's categorisation over actual analysis. (C) dismisses the request without analysis instead of assessing it. (D) defers an assessment that the impact analysis shows is likely non-trivial.
