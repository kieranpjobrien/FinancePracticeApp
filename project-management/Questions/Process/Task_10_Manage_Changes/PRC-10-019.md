# Question

A vendor on a fixed-price contract proposes a design change that would improve quality but raise their cost by 10%. The vendor is asking the buyer to approve a contract change so the increase can be passed through. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse the increase because cost risk on fixed-price work belongs to the seller.
- **B**: Approve the contract modification because the change improves deliverable quality.
- **C**: Review the contract terms and the change request to determine whether the change is buyer-driven or seller-driven before negotiating who bears the cost.
- **D**: Split the cost increase 50/50 with the vendor as a goodwill gesture.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Fixed-price contracts place defined-scope cost risk with the seller, but who pays for a change depends on whether the buyer requested it or the seller proposed it as an improvement. (A) is correct only if the change is seller-initiated, which has not been determined. (B) commits the buyer to a cost increase without analysis. (D) skips the contract analysis and arbitrarily splits costs, which sets a poor precedent and bypasses procurement governance.
