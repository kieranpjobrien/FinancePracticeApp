# Question

A vendor proposes a change that would improve deliverable quality but increase cost by 10%. The contract is fixed-price. Who should absorb the cost increase?

## Options

- **A**: The vendor, since they proposed the change under a fixed-price contract
- **B**: The project, since the change improves quality
- **C**: It depends on contract terms and how the change request is structured
- **D**: The cost should be split equally between vendor and project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Contract terms govern who bears costs for changes. A fixed-price contract typically means the vendor bears cost risk for defined scope, but customer-requested or approved changes may warrant contract modifications. The answer depends on whether this is a vendor suggestion or a mutual decision to improve quality.

Options A, B, and D assume specific outcomes without considering contract terms and negotiation.
