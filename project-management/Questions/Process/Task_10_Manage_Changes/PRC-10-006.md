# Question

The CCB approves a scope addition but rejects the schedule extension and budget increase the PM said the change required. The PM is convinced the change cannot be delivered within the existing baselines. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Document the CCB decision and execute the change as approved, accepting the schedule slip if it occurs.
- **B**: Return to the CCB with the integrated impact analysis, options, and risks of approving scope without time and cost, and request reconsideration.
- **C**: Implement the change but quietly reduce the testing effort to absorb the impact within the baseline.
- **D**: Decline to start work and inform the sponsor that the CCB decision is unworkable.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM has a duty to give decision-makers accurate information. If the CCB authorised scope without the corresponding time and cost, the responsible step is to present the analysis again with options so they can knowingly accept the trade-off. (A) sets the project up to fail silently. (C) is an ethics violation that hides impact in quality. (D) refuses without giving the CCB a chance to reconsider with the right information; the PM should educate the body, not pre-empt it.
