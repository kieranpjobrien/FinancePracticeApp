# Question

A change control board approves a scope change but rejects the associated schedule and cost adjustments. The PM knows the change cannot be implemented without additional time and budget. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change as directed by the CCB
- **B**: Refuse to implement until proper adjustments are approved
- **C**: Present analysis showing the change is impossible without schedule/cost adjustments and request reconsideration
- **D**: Implement partially and accept whatever quality results

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM has a professional obligation to provide accurate information. If the CCB's decision is based on incomplete understanding, the PM should present clear analysis showing why the change requires additional resources. This enables informed decision-making - the CCB may reconsider or may choose not to implement the change.

Option A (implement as directed) sets the project up for failure. The PM shouldn't accept impossible constraints without objection.

Option B (refuse outright) is confrontational. The PM should educate and influence, not simply refuse direction.

Option D (accept poor quality) delivers something nobody wants. Partial implementation with quality issues rarely satisfies anyone.
