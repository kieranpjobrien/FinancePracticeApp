# Question

During execution, the project sponsor requests a significant scope addition that would add 3 weeks to the schedule and $50,000 to the budget. The sponsor says it's critical for business success and expects immediate implementation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change immediately since the sponsor has authority over the project
- **B**: Refuse the change to protect the baseline
- **C**: Document the change request, assess impacts, and present to the change control board for decision
- **D**: Negotiate with the sponsor to reduce the change scope to minimise impact

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. All changes, regardless of who requests them, should follow the change control process. The PM should document the request, analyse schedule/cost/risk impacts, and submit it to the CCB (or appropriate authority) for decision. Even if the sponsor chairs the CCB, the process ensures impacts are understood and documented.

Option A (implement immediately) bypasses change control. The sponsor can advocate for the change, but proper process ensures informed decision-making and baseline integrity.

Option B (refuse) isn't the PM's decision to make. The PM's role is to analyse and present impacts, not to approve or reject changes - that's the CCB's role.

Option D (negotiate scope) may be appropriate but should happen as part of the change control process, not as an informal workaround.
