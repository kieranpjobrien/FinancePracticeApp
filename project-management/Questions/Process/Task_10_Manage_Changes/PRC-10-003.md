# Question

Mid-execution, the sponsor calls the PM and asks for a scope addition that will add three weeks and $50,000. The sponsor stresses it is critical and expects implementation to start tomorrow. The project has a CCB that the sponsor chairs. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin work tomorrow on the sponsor's instruction and document the verbal authorisation in the project record.
- **B**: Negotiate with the sponsor to reduce the change so the impact stays within the PM's delegated tolerance.
- **C**: Document the request, perform an impact assessment, and route it to the CCB for a formal decision in the next session.
- **D**: Reject the request because the impact exceeds the approved cost and schedule baselines.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Even when the sponsor is the requester and chairs the CCB, the change must run through the documented process so impacts are visible, recorded, and decided with proper analysis. (A) treats sponsor pressure as authority to bypass process; the sponsor can decide quickly inside the CCB but the analysis must come first. (B) negotiates around the process informally, which prevents the CCB from seeing the original request. (D) is not the PM's call; the PM analyses, the CCB decides.
