# Question

Which best describes the difference between corrective and preventive actions?

## Options

- **A**: Corrective actions address an existing variance from the plan; preventive actions reduce the likelihood or impact of a potential future variance.
- **B**: Corrective actions are formal change requests; preventive actions can be implemented by the PM without approval.
- **C**: Corrective actions are taken during execution; preventive actions are taken during planning.
- **D**: Corrective actions affect baselines; preventive actions affect the project management plan only.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The textbook distinction is timing relative to the variance: corrective realigns actual to plan after a variance, preventive reduces probability or impact of a variance that has not yet occurred. (B) misstates approval requirements; both can require change control depending on impact. (C) is inaccurate because both can occur during execution. (D) misrepresents what each action affects; both may or may not touch baselines.
