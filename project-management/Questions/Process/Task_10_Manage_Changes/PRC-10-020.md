# Question

What is the difference between a corrective action and a preventive action?

## Options

- **A**: Corrective actions are documented; preventive actions are informal
- **B**: Corrective actions address existing variances; preventive actions address potential future variances
- **C**: Corrective actions are approved by the CCB; preventive actions are approved by the PM
- **D**: Corrective actions affect scope; preventive actions affect schedule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Corrective actions bring project performance back in line with the plan when variances have already occurred. Preventive actions are taken to reduce the probability or impact of potential future variances before they occur. Both are types of changes that may require change control.

Option A mischaracterises documentation requirements.

Option C misattributes approval authority - both may require various approvals.

Option D incorrectly limits the scope of each type of action.
