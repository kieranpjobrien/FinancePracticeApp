# Question

A Kanban team supporting a streaming service has a steady flow of customer enhancement requests arriving daily. WIP limits are being breached because the PM is letting every request enter the "to do" column on arrival. Cycle time is rising. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce WIP limits at the upstream queue and prioritise new requests before they enter the workflow at all.
- **B**: Raise WIP limits to match the arrival rate so nothing gets blocked at the top of the flow at any point.
- **C**: Ignore the WIP limit breach because the team is still delivering items at a reasonable pace in practice.
- **D**: Add two more team members to the team so that increased capacity absorbs the growing arrival rate sustainably.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Kanban depends on WIP limits and upstream triage; ungoverned intake is the classic reason cycle time rises. (B) abandons the discipline that makes Kanban work. (C) disables the signal that WIP limits are designed to give. (D) adds capacity to address a flow discipline problem that more people will not fix.
