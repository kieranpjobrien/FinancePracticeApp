# Question

In agile projects, how is change management typically handled?

## Options

- **A**: The same way as predictive projects with formal CCB approval
- **B**: Through backlog management where the product owner prioritises and reprioritises based on value
- **C**: Without any change control since agile welcomes change
- **D**: By freezing scope for each release and only accepting changes between releases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Agile manages change through the product backlog. The product owner continuously prioritises items based on value, and new items can be added at any time. Sprint commitments provide short-term stability, but overall scope evolves based on learning and changing needs. This is controlled change, not uncontrolled change.

Option A imposes predictive controls on agile.

Option C misinterprets agile - welcoming change doesn't mean no control.

Option D applies phase-gate thinking to agile.
