# Question

A change was implemented without proper approval. The PM discovers this during a review. The change appears beneficial. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the change since it's beneficial
- **B**: Require the change to be reversed
- **C**: Document the change retroactively and address the process violation
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor for guidance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM needs to address both the outcome (document what changed for traceability) and the process (why was change control bypassed and how to prevent recurrence). Accepting without documentation creates gaps in project records; reversing may not be practical or wise if the change is beneficial.

Option A ignores the process violation.

Option B may not be practical and doesn't address why it happened.

Option D escalates unnecessarily for something the PM can handle.
