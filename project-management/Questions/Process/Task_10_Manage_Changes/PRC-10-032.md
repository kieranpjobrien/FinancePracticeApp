# Question

During an internal review the PM discovers a beneficial change was implemented two sprints ago without going through change control. The change appears safe and useful. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the change without action because the outcome is good and reverting wastes effort.
- **B**: Reverse the change immediately and require a fresh change request before any reapplication.
- **C**: Document the change in the change log retroactively, run an after-the-fact impact assessment, and address the process breach with the team.
- **D**: Escalate the process violation to the sponsor for guidance on disciplinary action.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM must restore traceability and address the root cause without overreacting. Logging the change preserves the audit trail, the impact assessment confirms there are no hidden consequences, and team coaching addresses why control was bypassed. (A) ignores the process gap. (B) is disproportionate for a benign change and disruptive to the team. (D) escalates a coaching matter and skips the PM's own responsibility to handle it.
