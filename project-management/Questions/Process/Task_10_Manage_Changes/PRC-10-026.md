# Question

A regulatory body issues a new requirement that affects the project. The requirement is mandatory but wasn't in the original scope. The sponsor refuses to increase the budget. How should the PM handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the requirement anyway and absorb the cost overrun
- **B**: Document that the project cannot meet regulatory requirements without budget increase
- **C**: Present options: reduce other scope, extend timeline, accept risk, or increase budget
- **D**: Proceed without the regulatory requirement and accept the compliance risk

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The PM should present options rather than making unilateral decisions. Mandatory requirements can't be ignored, but there may be ways to accommodate them: reduce other scope to free resources, extend timeline to spread costs, or accept increased risk elsewhere. The sponsor needs options to make an informed decision.

Option A absorbs cost without approval.

Option B presents the problem without solutions.

Option D accepts potentially serious compliance risk without authorisation.
