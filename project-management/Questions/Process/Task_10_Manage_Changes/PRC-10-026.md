# Question

A new regulation lands during execution. Compliance is mandatory and adds an estimated $80,000 of work. The sponsor says no additional budget is available. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Absorb the work into the existing budget by quietly trimming non-regulatory testing and contingency activities.
- **B**: Document that the project cannot meet the regulation under current funding and stop the affected work entirely.
- **C**: Prepare a change request with options (descope, extend, use reserve, increase budget) and route to the CCB.
- **D**: Continue execution without addressing the regulation and capture the compliance gap on the project risk register.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Compliance is non-negotiable, but the PM does not unilaterally decide how to fund it. Presenting structured options through change control lets the CCB and sponsor choose a viable trade-off with full impact understood. (A) compromises quality and bypasses change control. (B) frames the situation as a problem without offering options. (D) accepts a serious compliance risk the PM has no authority to accept.
