# Question

A production incident requires a change to a deliverable being released this evening. The CCB does not meet for another week, and the change management plan defines an emergency change procedure with a designated approver. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the release and wait for the next CCB meeting so all changes follow the standard approval path.
- **B**: Get verbal sponsor approval, implement now, and brief the CCB at their next session.
- **C**: Implement the fix immediately and submit a change request afterwards purely for documentation.
- **D**: Invoke the documented emergency change procedure, obtain the designated approver's decision, and log the change.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A mature change management plan includes an emergency procedure precisely for time-critical situations; the PM should follow it. (A) elevates process over an urgent business need that the procedure was designed to handle. (B) bypasses the documented emergency path in favour of an informal verbal approval, which weakens governance. (C) implements without prior approval at all, which is not authorised even in emergencies.
