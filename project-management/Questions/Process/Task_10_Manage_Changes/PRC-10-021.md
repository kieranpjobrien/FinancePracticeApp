# Question

A change request proposes descoping two features so the project can recover schedule. One business stakeholder considers one of those features essential to their group's adoption. What should the PM do before the CCB session?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the change because it removes a feature a stakeholder considers essential.
- **B**: Approve the change because schedule recovery outweighs feature loss.
- **C**: Engage affected stakeholders, capture their position in the impact analysis, and present it with the change to the CCB.
- **D**: Forward the change to the CCB without further input so they decide unbiased by stakeholder lobbying.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated impact analysis must include stakeholder perspectives so the CCB has the information it needs to make a value-based trade-off. (A) and (B) pre-empt the CCB and miss the analysis step. (D) deprives the decision-makers of relevant context; capturing stakeholder positions is part of analysis, not lobbying. The PM serves the decision by surfacing impact, not by editing input.
