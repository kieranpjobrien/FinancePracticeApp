# Question

The change control board, which includes the sponsor, has approved a scope change the project manager believes will harm long-term product quality. The PM presented their concerns in writing during the CCB review. What should the PM do now?

## Options

- **A**: Decline to implement the change and require the sponsor to formally direct the team in writing.
- **B**: Implement the approved change, record the PM's documented concerns and the CCB's rationale in the change log, and proceed.
- **C**: Escalate above the sponsor to the executive steering group to challenge the CCB's decision.
- **D**: Implement the change but instruct the team to flag any quality issues that arise so the decision can be reversed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM advises; the CCB decides. Once a legitimate governance body approves a change that is neither illegal nor unethical, the PM implements while preserving documentation of the decision and the dissent. (A) refuses legitimate authority. (C) goes around the governance body, damaging trust and undermining the process the PM is meant to uphold. (D) sets the team up to look for failure rather than executing the approved direction; concerns belong in the change log, not as instructions to the team.
