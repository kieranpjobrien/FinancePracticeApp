# Question

At a sprint review, a stakeholder notes that a demonstrated feature behaves differently from what they agreed to in a workshop two months earlier. The backlog item has been refined several times since. What artifact practice would prevent this?

## Options

- **A**: Freeze backlog items once they are accepted into a sprint so they cannot be refined further.
- **B**: Link each backlog item to its source artifact (workshop notes, requirements doc) and review those links during refinement.
- **C**: Require the stakeholder to sign off on every backlog refinement before the team can proceed.
- **D**: Record all requirement workshops so the team can replay them before refining a backlog item.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI emphasises traceability — every backlog item should tie back to the need it came from. (B) lets refinement happen (which is valuable) while giving the team a fast way to check intent. (A) prevents beneficial refinement, which directly contradicts agile practice. (C) is burdensome and slows the team without addressing the underlying traceability gap. (D) produces raw material but not the structured link that makes verification quick.
