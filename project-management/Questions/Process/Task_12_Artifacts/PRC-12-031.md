# Question

During a sprint review, a stakeholder notices that a feature demonstrated doesn't match what was discussed in a meeting two months ago. The team insists they built what was in the product backlog. Investigation reveals the backlog item was refined several times but the original meeting notes weren't referenced. What artifact practice would help prevent this?

## Options

- **A**: Stop refining backlog items once created
- **B**: Link backlog items to source artifacts (meeting notes, requirements documents) and review links during refinement
- **C**: Have the stakeholder approve every backlog change
- **D**: Record all meetings with stakeholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Traceability between backlog items and their sources (original requirements, meeting notes, design documents) enables the team to verify that refinements remain aligned with original intent. During refinement sessions, reviewing these links catches disconnects before development begins.

Option A prevents beneficial refinement that improves clarity and feasibility.

Option C creates excessive overhead and may not be practical for busy stakeholders.

Option D creates content but doesn't establish the linkage needed for efficient verification.
