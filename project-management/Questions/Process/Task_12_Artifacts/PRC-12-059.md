# Question

A distributed Scrum team keeps its task board in a tool that two product stakeholders cannot access due to licensing. Those stakeholders say they feel disconnected from progress and keep requesting ad-hoc status updates. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Send those stakeholders a personalised written status update weekly to replace the information radiator they cannot see.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to verbally brief those stakeholders after each daily stand-up using notes from the board.
- **C**: Request the organisation licences them so everyone accesses the same transparent board rather than duplicate channels.
- **D**: Export a read-only snapshot of the board to a location they can access, refreshed automatically each sprint cadence.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI and agile principles favour shared transparent information over parallel reporting streams. (C) removes the access barrier at source. (A) creates bespoke reporting that drifts from the board. (B) relies on verbal relay that degrades accuracy. (D) is better than bespoke reports but still maintains two records that can diverge.
