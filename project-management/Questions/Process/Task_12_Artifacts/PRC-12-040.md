# Question

The organisation is rolling out a new collaborative artifact platform to replace shared drives. Long-tenured employees prefer the old way; new joiners use the new platform exclusively. The PM has to manage artifacts for a project staffed with both groups. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let each group work in their preferred collaboration environment and federate search capability across both systems.
- **B**: Run a phased migration with training, keeping critical artifacts accessible in both formats during the transition.
- **C**: Mandate the new platform from day one with no fallback so the entire team is forced to adopt it quickly.
- **D**: Maintain dual artifact systems indefinitely so that neither the long-tenured nor the new-joiner group is disrupted.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats this as a change management problem as much as an artifact problem. (B) acknowledges the human side, invests in capability, and time-boxes the dual-running. (A) entrenches the split and undermines a single source of truth. (C) ignores change management and will create errors and resentment. (D) accepts permanent overhead and the same fragmentation the rollout was meant to fix.
