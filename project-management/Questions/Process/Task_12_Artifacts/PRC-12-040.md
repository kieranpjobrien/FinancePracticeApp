# Question

An organization is transitioning from document-centric project management to a more integrated approach using collaborative tools. Long-time employees resist the change, preferring traditional documents. New employees prefer modern tools. The project manager must manage artifacts across both groups. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Allow each group to use their preferred approach
- **B**: Mandate immediate adoption of new tools for everyone
- **C**: Implement a phased transition with training and support, ensuring critical artifacts are accessible in both formats during the transition period
- **D**: Maintain parallel systems permanently

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. A phased transition acknowledges change management realities. Training and support help resistant employees adopt new tools. Maintaining dual accessibility during transition ensures work continuity. The goal is eventual adoption of integrated tools while managing the human element of change.

Option A creates fragmentation and integration challenges between the two groups.

Option B ignores change management principles and may create productivity loss from unprepared users.

Option D creates permanent inefficiency and maintenance overhead for two systems.
