# Question

A project involves sensitive customer data. Regulatory requirements mandate that all project documents containing customer information must be retained for seven years after project completion and must have a complete audit trail of who accessed them. What artifact management approach should the project manager establish?

## Options

- **A**: Store documents on team members' local drives with password protection
- **B**: Implement a document management system with access logging, retention policies, and secure archival capabilities that meet regulatory requirements
- **C**: Print all documents and store them in a locked file cabinet
- **D**: Delete all customer data immediately after project completion for security

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Regulatory compliance requires systematic controls. A proper document management system can enforce access controls, maintain audit trails of who accessed what and when, apply retention policies automatically, and ensure secure long-term archival. This addresses all regulatory requirements systematically.

Option A lacks audit trails and centralized control. Local storage makes compliance verification difficult and creates security risks.

Option C is impractical for modern projects and makes audit trail maintenance extremely difficult. Physical documents also have security and disaster recovery challenges.

Option D directly violates the seven-year retention requirement.
