# Question

A healthcare project handles patient records subject to a seven-year retention rule and a requirement for a complete access audit trail. The PM is deciding how to manage these artifacts. Which approach BEST satisfies the regulatory requirements?

## Options

- **A**: Store documents in the team's SharePoint site and apply a retention label to the library.
- **B**: Use an enterprise document management system with access logging, retention policies, and secure archival.
- **C**: Back up documents nightly to an encrypted NAS and keep the backups for seven years.
- **D**: Keep working copies in the team drive and archive finals to the corporate records management team at closeout.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The requirement combines three controls: long retention, access audit trails, and regulatory defensibility. (B) addresses all three in one system that is designed for compliance. (A) provides retention labels but not always the granular access logging regulators expect, and governance depends on tenant configuration. (C) covers backup but not access auditing — you cannot tell who opened a document. (D) splits custody and introduces a gap where working copies are uncontrolled until archival, breaking the audit trail.
