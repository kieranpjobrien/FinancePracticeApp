# Question

A scrum team generates various artifacts including the product backlog, sprint backlog, and increment. The organization's PMO requires certain documentation for governance purposes. The team views PMO requirements as bureaucratic overhead. How should the scrum master address this?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore PMO requirements since they conflict with agile principles
- **B**: Comply fully with PMO requirements even if it slows the team
- **C**: Work with the PMO to identify which requirements can be satisfied by existing agile artifacts and where lightweight adaptations are needed
- **D**: Ask the product owner to handle all PMO documentation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Many agile artifacts contain information the PMO needs in a different format. Working together, the team and PMO can often find ways to satisfy governance needs without creating redundant documentation. For example, burndown charts may satisfy progress reporting needs with minor adaptation.

Option A creates organizational conflict and may violate legitimate governance requirements.

Option B treats all requirements as non-negotiable when many can be satisfied more efficiently.

Option D inappropriately shifts documentation burden to the product owner.
