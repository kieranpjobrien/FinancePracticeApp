# Question

A strategic pivot cancels a project at the 60% mark. The PM has two weeks to close out artifact management. What is the MOST important action?

## Options

- **A**: Archive all existing artifacts to the PMO library exactly as they stand today.
- **B**: Transfer current artifacts to a sister project that may reuse the work.
- **C**: Produce a close-out pack covering current state, decisions, and lessons learned, then archive all artifacts with that context.
- **D**: Delete work-in-progress artifacts that are no longer relevant and archive only approved deliverables.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cancelled projects carry the most valuable organisational learning, and PMI explicitly requires a structured close-out regardless of success. (C) preserves not just the raw artifacts but the context, which is what future teams actually need. (A) archives content without the context that makes it retrievable. (B) presumes a receiving project and may transfer baggage the other team does not want. (D) destroys context embedded in in-progress work that could inform future efforts.
