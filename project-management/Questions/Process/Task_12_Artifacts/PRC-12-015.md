# Question

A business stakeholder complains they cannot find a stable version of the product backlog to use for their quarterly planning. The product owner refines the backlog daily. What should the product owner do?

## Options

- **A**: Freeze the backlog one week before quarterly planning so stakeholders can review a stable version of it.
- **B**: Export a tagged snapshot of the backlog at each release planning event and share it with the stakeholder.
- **C**: Hand the stakeholder a separate planning document maintained outside the backlog tool for quarterly reference.
- **D**: Walk the stakeholder through the live backlog one-on-one whenever they need information for their planning work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The backlog must keep evolving (refining is a core agile practice), but stakeholders have a legitimate need for a stable reference point. (B) preserves both: the live backlog stays dynamic, and tagged snapshots give the stakeholder a defensible point-in-time view. (A) breaks the agile principle of continuous refinement and may stall important changes. (C) creates a parallel artifact that will drift from the real backlog. (D) is high-touch but does not give the stakeholder anything they can take into their own planning meeting.
