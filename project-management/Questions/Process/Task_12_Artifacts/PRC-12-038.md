# Question

A defence contractor's project produces documents at three classification levels. Some team members are cleared to all levels, others to lower levels only. How should the project manager structure artifact storage?

## Options

- **A**: Apply role-based access controls in the document repository tied to clearance level so each member sees only what they may see.
- **B**: Place all classified documents on a separate offline drive accessed only inside the secure area.
- **C**: Mark documents with classification headers and rely on team members to respect the markings.
- **D**: Limit access to the highest-cleared members and have them brief lower-cleared members verbally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects information governance to enforce need-to-know through controls, not goodwill. (A) implements least privilege automatically and supports collaboration within allowed boundaries. (B) is operationally crippling for a multi-classification team. (C) relies on individual discipline rather than enforced controls — auditors will reject it. (D) creates a bottleneck and bypasses the formal classification system.
