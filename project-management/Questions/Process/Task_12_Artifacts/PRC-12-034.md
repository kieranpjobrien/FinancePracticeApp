# Question

A new PM inherits a project where the prior PM produced thorough documentation but used non-standard terms (e.g. "phase" for sprint, "package" for epic). Three new joiners say they cannot make sense of the existing wiki. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Spend the next two weeks rewriting all existing project documents into standard terminology before onboarding more joiners.
- **B**: Keep the existing local terminology to avoid invalidating the thorough work the previous PM put into the wiki.
- **C**: Tell the new joiners to learn the project's local terminology since the documents are otherwise correct and thorough.
- **D**: Publish a glossary mapping local terms to standards, fix the most-read documents, and require standard terms going forward.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI favours pragmatic, incremental improvement. (D) gives joiners immediate relief through the glossary, focuses rework where it pays off, and prevents the problem getting worse. (A) is a heavy investment that pauses delivery for documents that may not all be high-traffic. (B) leaves the comprehension problem in place. (C) puts the cost on every joiner and is the opposite of servant leadership.
