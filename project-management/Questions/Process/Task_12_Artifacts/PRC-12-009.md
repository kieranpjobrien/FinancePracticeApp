# Question

A Scrum team tracks work on a Jira Kanban board. A newly added steering committee member asks for visibility into project progress but has never used Jira and only looks at project information once a fortnight. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Grant read-only access to the board and offer a short walkthrough of the view they will use most often.
- **B**: Pull a PowerPoint status report from Jira each fortnight and email it to the steering committee member directly.
- **C**: Add the steering committee member to the recurring sprint review invite so they see progress live.
- **D**: Request that the PMO provide the member with formal Jira training before granting any access.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects artifacts to be transparent and accessible to stakeholders with an appropriate orientation to the tool. (A) gives the member self-service access while removing the comprehension barrier with a targeted walkthrough. (B) creates a manual reporting channel that will drift from the live board and adds ongoing PM effort. (C) assumes a cadence mismatch — sprint reviews are weekly or fortnightly team events, and the member may not need that depth. (D) over-engineers a simple access request and delays transparency while waiting on PMO scheduling.
