# Question

A vendor on a fixed-price contract asks for the project's full technical specification document so they can confirm their deliverables align. Parts of the specification reveal proprietary client business processes. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Email the full specification to the vendor under the existing master services agreement.
- **B**: Decline the request and ask the vendor to work from the requirements summary in their statement of work.
- **C**: Have the vendor sign an additional non-disclosure agreement and then share the entire specification.
- **D**: Work with legal and the client to identify the sections the vendor genuinely needs, then share only those.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI applies the principle of least information necessary while still enabling the vendor to deliver. (D) involves the right people (legal and client), shares what the vendor needs to perform, and protects what they do not need. (A) over-shares proprietary content even with an MSA in place. (B) under-shares and creates a real risk that the vendor will misinterpret requirements. (C) treats an NDA as a substitute for information governance — NDAs provide legal recourse, not exposure control.
