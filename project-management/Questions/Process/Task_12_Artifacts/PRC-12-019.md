# Question

During project execution, a vendor requests copies of the project's technical specifications to verify their deliverables will meet requirements. The specifications contain some proprietary information about the client's business processes. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to share any documentation with external parties
- **B**: Share the full specifications immediately to keep the project moving
- **C**: Review the specifications with appropriate stakeholders, redact or exclude proprietary information as needed, and share only what is necessary for the vendor's work
- **D**: Ask the vendor to sign an additional NDA and then share everything

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This balances the vendor's legitimate need for information with protecting proprietary content. The PM should involve appropriate stakeholders (legal, client) in determining what can be shared, redact sensitive information, and provide what's needed for the vendor to do their work effectively.

Option A may prevent the vendor from meeting requirements, causing project issues.

Option B ignores the responsibility to protect proprietary information.

Option D assumes an NDA fully protects proprietary information, but minimizing exposure is still prudent. NDAs provide legal recourse, not prevention.
