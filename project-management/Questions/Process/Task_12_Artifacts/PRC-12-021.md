# Question

A new executive sponsor takes over an 18-month project with inconsistent documentation and asks for a comprehensive project history for next month's board meeting. Compiling a perfect history would take two weeks of dedicated effort. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Block off two weeks for the team to compile a comprehensive history before the board meeting.
- **B**: Assemble what is readily available, transparently flag the gaps, and stand up documentation standards going forward.
- **C**: Reconstruct the missing portions from interviews with current team members and present the result as the project history.
- **D**: Tell the sponsor that a comprehensive history is not feasible and offer the next status report instead.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI values transparency and pragmatic action. (B) gives the sponsor a usable history, is honest about what is missing, and uses the moment to fix the underlying artifact problem for the rest of the project. (A) sacrifices delivery to chase a perfect record of historical work that may not justify the cost. (C) risks fabricating an inaccurate history and undermines the sponsor's trust if reconstructed details are wrong. (D) refuses a reasonable executive request when partial information is clearly available.
