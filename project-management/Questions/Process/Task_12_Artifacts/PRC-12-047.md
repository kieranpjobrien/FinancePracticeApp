# Question

At closure of a complex banking transformation, the PM realises no lessons-learned artifact has been produced and the team disbands in two weeks. The sponsor wants the PM to focus on final benefits reporting instead. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a short lessons-learned session now, capture the outputs formally, and keep working on benefits reporting alongside it.
- **B**: Draft lessons learned alone after the team leaves, using your own memory of what went well and poorly.
- **C**: Skip lessons learned since the team is disbanding and focus fully on the benefits report the sponsor requested.
- **D**: Push lessons learned to the PMO to run independently after closure using whatever records remain available.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats lessons learned as a non-negotiable closure artifact captured while the team is still intact. (A) protects organisational learning without abandoning the benefits work. (C) permanently loses knowledge. (B) produces a weak artifact biased by one person's memory. (D) delegates a duty PMI assigns to the PM and removes the participants who lived the work.
