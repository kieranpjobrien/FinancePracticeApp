# Question

A project manager needs to report weekly status to stakeholders. The team uses Jira for tracking, but executives prefer PowerPoint summaries. Creating the weekly report takes 4 hours each week. How should the PM improve this situation?

## Options

- **A**: Stop creating PowerPoint reports and require executives to view Jira directly
- **B**: Delegate report creation to a junior team member
- **C**: Automate report generation by creating Jira dashboards or export templates that feed into status reports
- **D**: Reduce reporting frequency to monthly to save time

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Automation reduces effort while maintaining stakeholder communication. Jira dashboards can be configured to show relevant metrics, and many tools allow export to formats suitable for executive reporting. This addresses the effort concern without sacrificing stakeholder needs.

Option A (force Jira on executives) doesn't meet stakeholders where they are. Effective communication means adapting to your audience's preferences, not requiring them to adapt to yours.

Option B (delegate) shifts the effort but doesn't reduce it. A junior team member's time is still project time, and they may produce lower-quality reports.

Option D (reduce frequency) may not meet stakeholder needs. Weekly visibility may be important for executive decision-making.
