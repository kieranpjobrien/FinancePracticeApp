# Question

A hybrid project must satisfy a predictive PMO that requires formal phase-gate documents while the agile delivery team wants to keep documentation lean. How should the project manager approach artifact management?

## Options

- **A**: Follow PMO requirements fully, since governance documentation is non-negotiable.
- **B**: Let the team work in their preferred lightweight style and negotiate an exemption from PMO templates.
- **C**: Run two parallel documentation streams so each audience gets what it is used to seeing.
- **D**: Identify the artifacts the PMO actually needs, produce those from the team's lightweight artifacts where possible, and tailor the rest.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle applies directly: the PM maps governance needs to the minimum set of artifacts that satisfy them, and reuses team artifacts where the content overlaps. (D) respects both audiences without duplicating work. (A) over-documents and frustrates the delivery team. (B) is unlikely to succeed and may reflect a PM who has not engaged the PMO on what they really need. (C) doubles the work and creates two versions of the truth that will inevitably diverge.
