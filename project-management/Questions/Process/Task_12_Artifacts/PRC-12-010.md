# Question

What is the primary purpose of a project baseline?

## Options

- **A**: To freeze project scope so no further changes can be introduced.
- **B**: To serve as the approved reference point for measuring project performance.
- **C**: To record the original estimate for lessons-learned purposes at closeout.
- **D**: To document the historical plan as an immutable audit record.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A baseline is the approved version of scope, schedule, or cost used to measure performance through variance analysis. (B) captures this definition exactly. (A) is wrong because baselines are updated when approved changes go through change control — they do not prevent change. (C) is too narrow; baselines are used continuously during execution, not just at closeout. (D) confuses a baseline with an archived record — baselines are living references that can be rebaselined through CCB approval.
