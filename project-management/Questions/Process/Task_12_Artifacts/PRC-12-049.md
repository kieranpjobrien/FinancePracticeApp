# Question

A pharmaceutical project shares a full artifact repository with an external CRO partner. Legal flags that commercial strategy documents sitting beside trial artifacts have been visible to CRO staff for weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Restructure access permissions so each stakeholder group sees only the artifacts their role legitimately requires.
- **B**: Ask the CRO to sign a confidentiality acknowledgement covering any commercial content they may have viewed.
- **C**: Email all CRO staff asking them to confirm they have not opened the commercial strategy documents.
- **D**: Escalate to the legal team for guidance before making any changes to the repository structure or access.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's information-management principle pairs transparency with need-to-know control, and the root cause is an access structure that never matched the stakeholder map. (A) fixes that. (B) papers over the exposure without preventing recurrence. (C) relies on self-reporting and is unenforceable. (D) delays an obvious corrective action the PM has authority to take now.
