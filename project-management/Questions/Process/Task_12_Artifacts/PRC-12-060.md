# Question

Executives complain the weekly status report is too long and they cannot find the decisions they need to make. Operational stakeholders complain the same report lacks enough technical detail for their needs. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce two tailored artifacts: an executive dashboard focused on decisions and a detailed operational report for delivery teams.
- **B**: Ask the executives to flag which sections they want removed, then tailor the single report based on their specific requests.
- **C**: Keep the single report but add a one-page executive summary on top and leave the detailed content unchanged underneath.
- **D**: Keep the existing report and schedule a monthly executive briefing where decisions needing attention can be raised verbally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's communications principle calls for tailored artifacts that match each audience's decision needs. (A) produces fit-for-purpose artifacts for each audience. (C) is a cosmetic fix that still buries decisions. (B) solves for one audience while leaving the other under-served. (D) adds a verbal channel without fixing the written artifact both audiences need.
