# Question

A Scrum team uses a physical whiteboard during daily standups to capture decisions and action items, then photographs the board at the end of the day. Six sprints in, finding any specific decision means scrolling through dozens of photos. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the whiteboard with a digital task board so every change is captured automatically.
- **B**: Ask the scrum master to maintain a searchable decision log in Confluence alongside the whiteboard.
- **C**: Continue with the photos, since agile values working software over comprehensive documentation.
- **D**: Email the daily photo to all team members so each person can index decisions in their own way.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team should keep the high-bandwidth collaboration tool that is working and add a lightweight searchable record of the durable decisions. (B) preserves the whiteboard for daily flow and adds the right-sized documentation that PMI's "minimum viable documentation" concept calls for. (A) throws away a tool the team likes for a problem that is really about searchability. (C) misreads the Agile Manifesto — "over" does not mean "instead of"; key decisions still need to be findable. (D) pushes the indexing burden onto every individual, leading to inconsistent personal records.
