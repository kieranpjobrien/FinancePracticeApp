# Question

A developer needs to understand why the team chose Postgres over DynamoDB six months ago, but the rationale is not recorded anywhere. Similar gaps keep appearing. What artifact practice should the project manager put in place?

## Options

- **A**: Record every team meeting and keep the recordings in the project repository.
- **B**: Maintain a decision log that records each significant decision, the options considered, and the rationale.
- **C**: Require all technical decisions to be emailed to the PM, who forwards them to a distribution list.
- **D**: Schedule a weekly architecture review and capture minutes in the team wiki.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A decision log (or architectural decision records in a technical context) is the purpose-built artifact for this exact problem. (B) captures decision, context, alternatives, and rationale in a searchable form. (A) creates enormous volumes of content with no structure; finding any specific decision becomes harder, not easier. (C) puts decisions in an ephemeral inbox and does not solve searchability. (D) adds a meeting but does not guarantee the decision rationale is captured in a retrievable format.
