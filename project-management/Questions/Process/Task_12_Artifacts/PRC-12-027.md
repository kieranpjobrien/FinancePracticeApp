# Question

A team member needs to reference a design decision made six months ago but cannot find documentation of the rationale. Several similar situations have occurred recently. What artifact management practice would help prevent this?

## Options

- **A**: Require all communications to be in writing
- **B**: Maintain a decision log that captures significant decisions, the options considered, rationale, and who made the decision
- **C**: Record all meetings
- **D**: Increase the frequency of status reports

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. A decision log specifically captures decisions, the context in which they were made, alternatives considered, and reasoning. This makes it easy to find and understand past decisions. It's more efficient than searching through meeting recordings or general correspondence.

Option A creates excessive documentation without organizing it for retrieval.

Option C creates a lot of content to search through without structured organization of decisions.

Option D provides project status but doesn't capture decision rationale.
