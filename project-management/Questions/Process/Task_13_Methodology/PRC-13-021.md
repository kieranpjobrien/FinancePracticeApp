# Question

Three months into a Scrum project, the customer is rarely available, requirements have proven much more stable than expected, and stakeholders keep asking for fuller upfront documentation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue Scrum to avoid disruption and educate stakeholders on agile expectations and ceremonies.
- **B**: Force the customer to participate in every sprint review and demand stricter stakeholder attendance rules.
- **C**: Document the situation in the next status report and continue the current cadence as planned.
- **D**: Reassess the delivery approach against current conditions and propose a transition to a predictive cadence.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI says tailoring is continuous; if the project's reality changes, the approach should be reconsidered. (D) does that through the right channel. (A) prioritises method consistency over fitness for purpose. (B) tries to bend stakeholders to the methodology rather than the other way round. (C) documents pain without acting on it.
