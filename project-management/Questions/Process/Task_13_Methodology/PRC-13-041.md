# Question

A project team is using Scrum but the product owner is frequently unavailable due to other responsibilities. User stories often lack clarity, and the team spends significant time in sprint planning trying to understand requirements. What should be addressed?

## Options

- **A**: Have the scrum master take over product owner duties
- **B**: Extend sprint planning to allow more time for clarification
- **C**: Address the product owner availability issue - either dedicating more PO time, delegating authority, or finding alternative ways to ensure requirements clarity
- **D**: Skip sprint planning and let developers interpret stories themselves

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. An unavailable product owner undermines Scrum's effectiveness. The root cause must be addressed - either by ensuring adequate PO time commitment, empowering proxies to make decisions, or adjusting the process to ensure clarity is achieved before sprint planning (such as through refinement sessions).

Option A combines roles that should be separate and may not solve the domain knowledge gap.

Option B treats symptoms rather than the cause.

Option D will likely result in building the wrong things.
