# Question

A Scrum team's product owner attends only 30% of refinements and is rarely available between ceremonies. Stories arrive in sprint planning unclear, and the team spends hours guessing intent. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Promote a developer to a proxy product owner role so the team has an on-call decision maker.
- **B**: Surface the impact of PO unavailability to the sponsor and agree on either dedicated PO time, a delegated decision-maker, or a different engagement model.
- **C**: Have the scrum master cover product owner duties when the PO is absent.
- **D**: Lengthen sprints to four weeks so the team has more elapsed time to clarify stories.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root cause is product owner capacity, which is a structural issue only the sponsor can resolve. (B) puts the data in front of the right person and forces a decision. (A) blurs roles and may not solve the domain knowledge gap. (C) doubles up the scrum master's role and undermines Scrum's separation of duties. (D) treats the symptom by giving the team more time to compensate for the gap.
