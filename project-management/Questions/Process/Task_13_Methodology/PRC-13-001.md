# Question

A project manager is starting a new project to develop a product in a market with rapidly changing customer preferences. Requirements are uncertain and expected to evolve. The organization has traditionally used waterfall methodology. What approach should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Use waterfall since it's the organization's standard approach
- **B**: Recommend an agile approach that accommodates changing requirements through iterative delivery and frequent customer feedback
- **C**: Create detailed requirements upfront to reduce uncertainty before development begins
- **D**: Hybrid approach using waterfall for planning and agile for development

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The project characteristics (uncertain requirements, rapidly changing market, need for evolution) strongly favor agile approaches. Agile embraces change through short iterations, frequent customer feedback, and adaptive planning. While organizational standards matter, methodology should fit project needs.

Option A prioritizes organizational comfort over project fit. Using waterfall for highly uncertain, change-prone projects typically leads to poor outcomes.

Option C attempts to force predictability onto inherently uncertain work. Detailed upfront requirements in volatile markets often become obsolete before implementation.

Option D might work but adds waterfall planning overhead that may not be needed. Pure agile is simpler when requirements are truly uncertain and volatile.
