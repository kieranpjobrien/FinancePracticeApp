# Question

A project manager is starting a consumer-product project in a market where customer preferences shift quarterly. Requirements are highly uncertain. The organisation has only ever used waterfall and the PMO is sceptical of agile. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Begin with waterfall to respect organisational standards and run a small agile pilot inside one workstream as proof.
- **B**: Run the project as Scrum from day one and brief the PMO afterwards on the results.
- **C**: Lock the requirements early through extensive market research so a predictive plan can be made to work.
- **D**: Engage the PMO and sponsor with the project's volatility profile, propose an adaptive approach with PMO-friendly governance touchpoints, and document the tailoring rationale.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI emphasises tailoring through proper channels and stakeholder engagement before changing a method. (D) brings the PMO and sponsor along with evidence and a governance fit, which is what makes adoption stick. (A) starts with the wrong method for the work and delays the case for change. (B) bypasses governance and breeds distrust. (C) tries to force predictability that the market does not allow.
