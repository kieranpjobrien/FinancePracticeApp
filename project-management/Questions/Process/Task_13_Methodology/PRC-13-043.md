# Question

A finance leadership team wants to adopt agile but is anxious about losing the cost and progress visibility they get from Gantt charts and earned value. How should the PM approach the transition?

## Options

- **A**: Keep traditional Gantt and EV reporting in place and start agile adoption only once leadership is comfortable with it.
- **B**: Replace Gantt and EV reports with burndown charts only and educate leadership on agile metrics through formal training.
- **C**: Reframe the leadership view with a roadmap for timeline, iteration spend reporting, and agile progress metrics.
- **D**: Continue using Gantt and EV reports indefinitely as the team works in sprints underneath the existing structure.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The need is to give leadership the information they actually require in a form that suits the new delivery model. (C) does both — translation rather than substitution. (A) defers benefits and signals reluctance. (B) leaves a visibility gap that fuels exactly the anxiety the question describes. (D) hides the new way of working behind old artifacts and discourages adoption.
