# Question

A predictive project with quarterly phase gates keeps finding that gate reviews surface fundamental issues too late. Each gate currently lasts a day and reviews thousands of pages of documentation. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the gates and supplement them with lightweight monthly risk-and-issue reviews that feed early signals upward to sponsors.
- **B**: Replace the gates entirely with a fully agile approach so quarterly gates are no longer needed on the project.
- **C**: Extend each gate to three days so the steering committee has more time to review documentation carefully at each gate.
- **D**: Reduce the number of gates to only two, at mid-project and at closure, so review effort stays more manageable overall.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring keeps appropriate governance while adding continuous feedback loops to surface issues earlier. (A) does both. (B) discards governance rather than improving its cadence. (C) extends the bottleneck without fixing the underlying cadence problem. (D) reduces oversight at precisely the project stages that benefit most from it.
