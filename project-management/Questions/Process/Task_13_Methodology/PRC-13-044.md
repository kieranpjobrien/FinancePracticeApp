# Question

A project manager is selecting methodology for a project that will deliver a minimum viable product (MVP) in six months, then iterate based on market feedback. The organization requires stage-gate approval for funding at quarterly intervals. How should the project be structured?

## Options

- **A**: Pure waterfall to align with quarterly gates
- **B**: Pure agile ignoring the funding gates
- **C**: Agile delivery within a framework that aligns releases and demonstrations with quarterly funding reviews, enabling iterative development while satisfying governance requirements
- **D**: Request removal of funding gates as they're incompatible with agile

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Agile can work within funding governance by aligning significant increments with review points. Sprints deliver continuously while quarterly reviews serve as checkpoint for budget decisions. This structures agile within organizational governance rather than fighting it.

Option A doesn't support the iterative, feedback-driven approach needed for MVP evolution.

Option B ignores legitimate organizational governance needs.

Option D may not be achievable and frames governance as the enemy rather than working within it.
