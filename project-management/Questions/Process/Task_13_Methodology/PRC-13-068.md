# Question

A Scrum team handling interruption-heavy operations work finds that sprint planning is routinely invalidated within days by urgent tickets. The team is considering Kanban but the organisation wants Scrum maintained. What should the scrum master propose?

## Options

- **A**: Keep Scrum strictly and ask operations to queue all urgent tickets until the next sprint planning session starts.
- **B**: Change to Kanban fully and stop sprint planning, sprint reviews, and other Scrum ceremonies the team currently runs.
- **C**: Abandon sprint commitment but keep sprint length as a review cadence only, blending it with continuous ticket handling flow.
- **D**: Adopt Scrumban: keep retrospectives and reviews, add WIP limits and pull-based flow, and drop sprint commitment as written.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle recognises hybrid approaches like Scrumban that blend cadence with flow. (D) preserves learning loops while matching interruption reality. (A) demands the work pattern fit the method rather than vice versa. (B) loses valuable review and retrospective cadence. (C) discards commitment without introducing the WIP discipline that makes flow work.
