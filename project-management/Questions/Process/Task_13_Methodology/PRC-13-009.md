# Question

A project team experienced with waterfall methodology is assigned to a project where requirements are expected to change frequently. The sponsor insists on agile. The team is resistant due to unfamiliarity with agile practices. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Use waterfall since the team knows it, regardless of project needs
- **B**: Implement full Scrum immediately to meet sponsor expectations
- **C**: Start with a simpler agile framework like Kanban while providing training, then evolve practices as the team gains experience
- **D**: Let the team decide which methodology to use

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. This balances project needs (adapting to changing requirements) with team capabilities. Kanban is often easier for waterfall teams to adopt as it focuses on flow and incremental improvement without the full ceremony of Scrum. Training and gradual evolution builds team capability while meeting project needs.

Option A ignores project characteristics that favor agile approaches.

Option B may overwhelm a team unfamiliar with agile, risking poor implementation.

Option D may not consider project needs and sponsor requirements.
