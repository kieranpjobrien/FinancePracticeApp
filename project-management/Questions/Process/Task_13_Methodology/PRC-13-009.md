# Question

A team experienced only in waterfall is asked to deliver a project with volatile requirements. The sponsor wants agile. The team is anxious about unfamiliar ceremonies. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a focused training programme and then implement Scrum by the book in the first month of delivery.
- **B**: Stay with waterfall since the team is already competent in it, and renegotiate scope stability with the sponsor directly.
- **C**: Let the team independently pick the delivery framework so they feel full ownership of the methodology change.
- **D**: Adopt Kanban first to build comfort with flow and visualisation, then layer in Scrum practices as capability grows.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI emphasises both fitness for purpose and respect for team capability. (D) gets the team into adaptive thinking quickly, with a low ceremony lift, and matures from there. (A) overloads a team unfamiliar with the practices and risks poor adoption. (B) fights the project's actual characteristics. (C) sounds empowering but leaves the team without the structure they need to grow into agile.
