# Question

A research-led product development project has huge technical uncertainty in its first phase (will the science work?) and commodity delivery in its second phase (build and ship at scale). What approach should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Use iterative experimentation for the research phase, then transition to predictive delivery once the science is proven.
- **B**: Use predictive planning for both phases, buying certainty with extensive upfront analysis and a strong contingency reserve.
- **C**: Use Scrum continuously across both phases so the team retains momentum and the same ceremonies throughout the project.
- **D**: Outsource the research phase entirely so the project can start with a predictive build from a proven scientific baseline.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's lifecycle guidance tailors the approach to the uncertainty profile of each phase. (A) matches method to uncertainty. (B) plans precision where discovery is needed. (C) applies product-delivery ceremonies to exploratory research. (D) transfers a strategic capability outside the organisation rather than managing its uncertainty.
