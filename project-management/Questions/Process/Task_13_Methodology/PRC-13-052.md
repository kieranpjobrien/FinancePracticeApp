# Question

A Scrum team is distributed across Sydney, London, and San Francisco. Daily stand-ups never find a time that suits everyone and people are routinely joining at inconvenient hours. What should the scrum master propose?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on a single synchronous stand-up time, rotated weekly, so no one group is always the one joining inconveniently.
- **B**: Skip stand-ups altogether and rely on tool-based status updates that everyone reads asynchronously within each working day.
- **C**: Have each location hold its own stand-up separately, with the scrum master attending all three to bridge information between locations.
- **D**: Use a short asynchronous daily written sync plus targeted synchronous overlap for coordination of the hardest blockers.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle adapts ceremonies to context while preserving their intent of transparent coordination. (D) matches the constraint. (A) spreads pain evenly but keeps the unsuitable format. (B) loses the synchronous unblocking that stand-ups exist for. (C) fragments the team into three sub-teams and makes the scrum master a relay point.
