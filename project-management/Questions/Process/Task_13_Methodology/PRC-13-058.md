# Question

A buyer organisation has only ever used fixed-scope fixed-price contracts with software vendors. The next project has genuinely uncertain requirements. Procurement insists their standard contract must be used unchanged. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the standard contract and manage inevitable scope drift through formal change requests as requirements evolve.
- **B**: Engage procurement and legal on an agile-friendly structure: fixed budget and timeline, scope priorities flexed iteratively.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor immediately to override procurement's insistence and mandate an agile contract for this project.
- **D**: Choose a waterfall delivery approach so the standard contract fits, even though requirements are known to be uncertain.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring extends to contracting and expects the PM to work with procurement early. (B) engages the right stakeholders to design a commercial structure fit for the uncertainty. (A) accepts a structure that is known to generate disputes. (C) is premature escalation before collaborative discussion. (D) forces the project into a method to match the contract rather than the other way around.
