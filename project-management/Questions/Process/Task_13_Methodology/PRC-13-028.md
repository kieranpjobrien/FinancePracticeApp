# Question

An organization is implementing a portfolio of 15 projects, some of which are traditional infrastructure projects, some are software development, and some are organizational change initiatives. Each type has different characteristics. What methodology approach should the PMO recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Standardize on one methodology for all projects for consistency
- **B**: Let each project manager choose their preferred methodology
- **C**: Establish methodology selection criteria that match approaches to project characteristics, with common governance and reporting standards across all projects
- **D**: Outsource methodology selection to consultants

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Portfolio-level management needs common governance and reporting for oversight, but individual projects benefit from methodologies suited to their characteristics. Selection criteria ensure appropriate methodology choice (infrastructure may need predictive, software may suit agile, change initiatives may need hybrid), while common standards enable portfolio oversight.

Option A forces inappropriate methodologies on some projects.

Option B may result in inconsistent governance and reporting.

Option D outsources a core competency without building organizational capability.
