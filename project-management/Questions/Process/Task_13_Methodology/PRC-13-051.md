# Question

An organisation moving from waterfall to agile has run three Scrum pilots that all reverted to waterfall habits within six months. Leadership wants to try a fourth pilot. What should the PMO do differently this time?

## Options

- **A**: Select a bigger project this time so the visibility creates pressure to sustain the agile methodology rigorously.
- **B**: Invest in coaching, structural changes, and leadership habits alongside the pilot, and define measurable adoption indicators.
- **C**: Send the pilot team on intensive certified Scrum training so they have the knowledge to prevent reversion this time.
- **D**: Wait longer before the fourth pilot so lessons from the three reversions mature across the wider organisation first.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's organisational change perspective treats methodology adoption as an ADKAR-style change, not a method install. (B) addresses reinforcement and ability alongside knowledge. (A) amplifies a pattern that has already failed three times. (C) mistakes training for adoption. (D) delays without changing the conditions that caused reversion.
