# Question

An organization has six scrum teams working on related products that need to coordinate their work. Individual teams are doing well, but cross-team dependencies are causing delays and integration issues. What should the organization consider?

## Options

- **A**: Combine all teams into one large team
- **B**: Have each team work more independently to reduce dependencies
- **C**: Implement a scaling framework like SAFe, LeSS, or Nexus that provides coordination mechanisms across teams while preserving team-level agility
- **D**: Return to waterfall for better coordination

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Scaling frameworks are designed to coordinate multiple agile teams working on related work. They provide mechanisms for cross-team planning, dependency management, and integration while preserving the benefits of small, focused teams. The choice of framework depends on organizational context and needs.

Option A creates an oversized team that loses agile's small-team benefits.

Option B doesn't address the reality that the products are related and require coordination.

Option D abandons working team-level agility rather than addressing the coordination challenge.
