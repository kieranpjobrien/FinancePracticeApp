# Question

A six-month project must deliver a mobile app that satisfies a regulatory go-live date, but the user experience requirements are still being discovered. The organisation has only ever delivered with waterfall. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Detailed waterfall with formal requirements sign-off in month one to lock the scope.
- **B**: Pure Kanban so the team pulls work continuously without committing to a fixed scope.
- **C**: Pure Scrum with two-week sprints, letting requirements emerge throughout the six months.
- **D**: A hybrid: fixed compliance milestones planned predictively, with iterative discovery for UX features.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The two pressures (immovable compliance date, evolving UX) call for two methods working together. (D) honours both. (A) freezes UX too early and risks rework when discovery reveals real user needs. (B) gives no commitment to the regulatory date. (C) supports discovery but does not foreground the compliance milestones the way the regulator will expect.
