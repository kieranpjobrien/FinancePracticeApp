# Question

In Scrum, what is the recommended length for a sprint?

## Options

- **A**: One day
- **B**: One to four weeks, with two weeks being common
- **C**: Three months
- **D**: Whatever time is needed to complete all work

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Scrum recommends sprints of one month or less, with two weeks being a common choice. This length provides enough time to complete meaningful work while maintaining frequent feedback cycles. Shorter sprints increase agility, while longer sprints (up to a month) may be needed for complex work.

Option A is too short to complete meaningful increments.

Option C is far too long - it undermines agile's short feedback cycles.

Option D contradicts Scrum's timeboxed approach where sprints are fixed lengths regardless of work remaining.
