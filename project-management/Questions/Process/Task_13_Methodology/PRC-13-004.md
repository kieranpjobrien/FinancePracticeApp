# Question

The organisation mandates waterfall for all projects. A PM is assigned an R&D project to explore three emerging technologies for a problem with no known solution. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document waterfall plans for each phase but run the team iteratively day-to-day to maintain governance optics.
- **B**: Engage the PMO and sponsor with a proposal to tailor the methodology, presenting the R&D characteristics and recommended adaptive approach.
- **C**: Apply the mandated waterfall approach because organisational standards exist for a reason.
- **D**: Decline the assignment and recommend a PM with experience running mandated methodologies on R&D work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle and the PM's responsibility to advocate for project success both apply. (B) uses governance properly: take the case to the people who own the standard. (A) is "agile theatre" — it creates a credibility problem and breaks governance. (C) ignores fitness for purpose; waterfall on R&D usually fails. (D) abdicates the PM role; advocating for appropriate conditions is part of the job.
