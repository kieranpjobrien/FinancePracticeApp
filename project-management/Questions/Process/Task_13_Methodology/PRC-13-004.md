# Question

An organisation mandates that all projects use their enterprise waterfall methodology. A project manager is assigned a research and development project where the end product is unknown - the team will experiment with emerging technologies to discover viable solutions. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the mandated methodology strictly to ensure organisational compliance
- **B**: Request a waiver from the methodology and propose an adaptive approach suited to R&D work
- **C**: Use waterfall documentation templates but run the project iteratively in practice
- **D**: Decline the project assignment due to methodology mismatch

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. PMI emphasises tailoring methodologies to project context. R&D projects with high uncertainty are poor fits for predictive approaches. The PM should advocate for an appropriate methodology through proper channels - requesting a waiver and proposing an alternative that better serves project success.

Option A (strict compliance) prioritises process over outcomes. Following waterfall for exploratory R&D will likely produce poor results and waste resources.

Option C (waterfall theater) creates confusion and mistrust. If the actual approach differs from documented approach, governance and oversight become meaningless.

Option D (decline assignment) abdicates responsibility. Part of the PM role is advocating for appropriate project conditions, not just accepting or rejecting assignments.
