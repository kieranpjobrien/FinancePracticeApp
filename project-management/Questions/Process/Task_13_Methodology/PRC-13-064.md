# Question

Half a new team comes from a waterfall background and finds daily stand-ups pointless; the other half comes from agile teams and wants to preserve them. The scrum master senses tension. What should the scrum master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the waterfall majority view and reduce stand-ups to weekly so the new team finds its collaborative footing first.
- **B**: Facilitate a working-agreement discussion where the team explores the purpose of stand-ups and agrees a format collectively.
- **C**: Impose daily stand-ups on agile grounds and coach the waterfall members individually on why the ceremony adds value.
- **D**: Let each subgroup run its own ceremonies independently and bring them together only at the end of each sprint.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leader and team-development approach builds shared understanding through facilitation, not imposition. (B) lets the team own the norm. (A) picks a side on frequency without exploring purpose. (C) imposes a ceremony without shared buy-in. (D) formalises a split team and undermines collaboration.
