# Question

A Scrum team consistently delivers what was planned. Sprint reviews are positive on output, but stakeholders say the features are not solving their highest-priority problems. What practice should the team add?

## Options

- **A**: Add more detail to acceptance criteria so the team builds exactly what is described in the story.
- **B**: Move to longer sprints so each delivered feature is more complete and polished on arrival at review.
- **C**: Tighten the definition of done so quality issues stop reaching the review and recurring after release.
- **D**: Adopt outcome-focused practices such as impact mapping and value-based metrics to guide backlog priorities.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The team is delivering output but not outcome — a value-prioritisation problem, not a quality or scope problem. (D) connects backlog work to outcomes the business cares about. (A) refines the wrong things faster. (B) does not change what is being prioritised. (C) addresses quality, which is not what stakeholders are complaining about.
