# Question

The PMO mandates a predictive methodology for all projects. The PM is assigned a research initiative whose outcomes are highly uncertain. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the mandated predictive methodology because established organisational governance must be respected by the PM.
- **B**: Run the project with adaptive practices internally and report progress in the standard predictive templates.
- **C**: Decline the assignment and let the PMO assign a PM more comfortable running the organisation's standard approach.
- **D**: Bring evidence of the project's R&D characteristics to the PMO and propose tailored adaptations via the exception process.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to advocate for project success while respecting governance. (D) does both — engaging the PMO using its own process. (A) sets the project up to fail. (B) creates an integrity issue and hides the real approach from oversight. (C) abdicates the PM role; advocating for the right approach is part of the job.
