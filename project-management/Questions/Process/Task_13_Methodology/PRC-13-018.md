# Question

What is the PRIMARY advantage of iterative development approaches?

## Options

- **A**: They require less documentation
- **B**: They allow for early and continuous feedback, enabling course correction throughout the project
- **C**: They are faster than predictive approaches
- **D**: They require fewer team members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Iterative approaches deliver working increments regularly, enabling stakeholders to provide feedback early and often. This feedback allows the team to adjust direction, correct misunderstandings, and improve the product based on real-world input rather than only discovering problems at the end.

Option A is a misconception - documentation levels vary based on needs, not methodology.

Option C isn't necessarily true - speed depends on many factors.

Option D isn't accurate - team size depends on project scope, not methodology.
