# Question

Which approach is characterized by detailed planning at the beginning, sequential phases, and formal stage gates?

## Options

- **A**: Agile
- **B**: Scrum
- **C**: Predictive (waterfall)
- **D**: Kanban

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Predictive (waterfall) methodology is characterized by comprehensive upfront planning, sequential phases (requirements, design, development, testing, deployment), and formal reviews/approvals between phases (stage gates). It works well when requirements are stable and well-understood.

Option A (Agile) emphasizes iterative delivery and adaptation over upfront planning.

Option B (Scrum) is an agile framework with short iterations and continuous refinement.

Option D (Kanban) focuses on continuous flow without fixed phases or iterations.
