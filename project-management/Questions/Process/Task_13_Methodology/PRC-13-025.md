# Question

A team is practicing Scrum but their sprints frequently fail to deliver committed work. Analysis shows that unplanned work and emergencies regularly interrupt the team. What methodology consideration should be explored?

## Options

- **A**: Longer sprints to absorb interruptions
- **B**: Consider Kanban which better accommodates variable flow and unplanned work through WIP limits rather than sprint commitments
- **C**: More detailed sprint planning
- **D**: Ignore interruptions and focus only on sprint work

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. When unplanned work is a constant reality, Kanban's flow-based approach may fit better than Scrum's commitment-based sprints. Kanban uses WIP (Work In Progress) limits to manage capacity without requiring upfront commitment to specific deliverables. This accommodates interruptions while maintaining productivity.

Option A doesn't solve the interruption problem - longer sprints will still be interrupted.

Option C can't predict unpredictable interruptions.

Option D ignores business realities and may damage stakeholder relationships.
