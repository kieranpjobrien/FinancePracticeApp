# Question

A pharma company is starting a new drug development programme. The regulator requires sequential phase gates and formal documentation; inside each phase, the formulation work is exploratory with high uncertainty. What approach should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Predictive governance and phase gates at the programme level, with adaptive practices used for the exploratory work inside each phase.
- **B**: Predictive delivery throughout the programme to maximise traceability for the regulator.
- **C**: Adaptive delivery throughout the programme with the regulator informed of releases as they happen.
- **D**: Ask the regulator to waive the phase-gate requirements for this programme due to its exploratory nature.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's hybrid pattern is governance-on-the-outside, adaptive-on-the-inside. (A) gives the regulator the structured deliverables they require while letting research teams iterate. (B) suppresses the experimentation needed to find a viable formulation. (C) cannot satisfy the regulator's gate requirements. (D) is unrealistic — regulators do not waive phase-gate requirements.
