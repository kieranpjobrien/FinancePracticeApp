# Question

A pharmaceutical company needs to develop a new drug. The regulatory approval process requires extensive documentation, sequential phase gates, and formal sign-offs. However, the actual research and formulation work is exploratory and uncertain. What methodology approach addresses these competing needs?

## Options

- **A**: Pure agile since research is exploratory
- **B**: Pure waterfall since regulatory requirements are strict
- **C**: Hybrid approach with predictive governance and phase gates, but agile practices within each phase for the exploratory research work
- **D**: Skip methodology selection since pharma projects are unique

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. The regulatory environment requires predictive elements (documentation, phase gates, formal approvals) for compliance. However, within each phase, the exploratory nature of research benefits from agile practices (experimentation, iteration, adaptation). This hybrid preserves regulatory compliance while enabling appropriate flexibility for research.

Option A ignores regulatory requirements that demand predictive governance.

Option B doesn't accommodate the exploratory, uncertain nature of research.

Option D fails to provide structure that any complex project needs.
