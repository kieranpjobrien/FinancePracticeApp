# Question

A project manager is assigned to a construction project with a fixed deadline, detailed specifications from the client, and regulatory requirements that mandate specific sequencing of activities. Which methodology approach is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Pure agile with daily iterations
- **B**: Predictive (waterfall) approach with detailed upfront planning
- **C**: Scrum with two-week sprints
- **D**: No specific methodology, just adapt as needed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Construction projects with fixed deadlines, detailed specifications, and regulatory sequencing requirements are well-suited to predictive approaches. These characteristics indicate stable requirements, sequential dependencies, and the need for comprehensive planning. Predictive methods excel when requirements are known and unlikely to change.

Option A is inappropriate for projects requiring sequential activities and stable requirements.

Option C (Scrum) is designed for iterative development, not sequential construction activities.

Option D lacks the structure needed for regulatory compliance and deadline management.
