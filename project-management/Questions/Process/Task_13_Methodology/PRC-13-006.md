# Question

A PM is assigned to a hospital construction project. The contract has fixed milestones, a detailed bill of quantities, and regulatory inspection sequencing. Which delivery approach is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Iterative delivery in three-month increments so design issues can be discovered progressively.
- **B**: Predictive (waterfall) with detailed upfront planning aligned to the inspection sequence.
- **C**: Scrum with a cross-functional delivery team and two-week sprints.
- **D**: Lean construction with a continuous flow board and pull scheduling.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Construction with stable scope, sequential dependencies, and regulatory sequencing is a textbook predictive context. (B) matches the work. (A) breaks construction's natural sequence (foundations before frame, frame before fit-out). (C) imposes a software framework on physical sequencing it cannot honour. (D) has merit on parts of construction but does not fit a single project with rigid inspection gates as the primary delivery method.
