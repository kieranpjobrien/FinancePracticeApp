# Question

A project manager is working with a customer who contractually requires fixed price, fixed scope delivery with predetermined milestones. The development team prefers agile practices. How should the project manager approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate to change the contract to time and materials
- **B**: Use agile practices internally while providing predictive-style reporting and milestone deliveries that satisfy contractual requirements
- **C**: Abandon agile practices to match the contract
- **D**: Explain to the customer why their contract is wrong

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Contract requirements and team practices don't have to be identical. The team can use agile practices internally (iterations, daily standups, continuous integration) while structuring deliverables and reporting to meet contractual milestone and fixed-scope requirements. This gives the team the benefits of agile while satisfying the customer.

Option A may not be possible if the contract is already signed.

Option C loses the benefits of agile practices the team has developed.

Option D is unlikely to be productive and may damage the customer relationship.
