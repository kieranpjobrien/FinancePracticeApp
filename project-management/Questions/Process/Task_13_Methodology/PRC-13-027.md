# Question

A signed customer contract requires fixed-price, fixed-scope delivery against four pre-defined milestones. The internal delivery team prefers agile practices. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Renegotiate the contract before kickoff so the delivery model aligns with the team's preference.
- **B**: Use agile practices internally for delivery while structuring milestones, reporting, and acceptance to satisfy the contract.
- **C**: Have the team adopt the predictive approach the contract implies for the duration of the engagement.
- **D**: Make the customer attend agile ceremonies so they understand the team's working method.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contractual obligations and internal delivery practices can coexist. (B) gives the customer the deliverables and milestones they bought while letting the team work iteratively underneath. (A) is unlikely once the contract is signed and would delay execution. (C) gives up valuable team practices for no real benefit. (D) imposes the team's working style on the customer and does not address the contract.
