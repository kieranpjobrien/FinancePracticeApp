# Question

A startup is developing a new product for a market that doesn't exist yet. They need to test multiple concepts with potential users and pivot quickly based on feedback. Their investor expects regular demonstrations of progress. Which approach is MOST suitable?

## Options

- **A**: Comprehensive upfront planning to reduce risk
- **B**: Lean startup approach with MVP iterations, combined with agile delivery practices
- **C**: Traditional waterfall with extensive market research
- **D**: Detailed requirements documentation before any development

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Lean startup's build-measure-learn cycle combined with agile delivery enables rapid hypothesis testing through MVPs (Minimum Viable Products). This approach acknowledges high uncertainty, prioritizes learning, and allows quick pivots. Regular iterations also satisfy the investor's need for progress demonstrations.

Option A assumes requirements can be known upfront, which isn't possible in an undefined market.

Option C doesn't accommodate the need for rapid pivoting and learning.

Option D delays learning and may produce documentation for concepts that won't survive market testing.
