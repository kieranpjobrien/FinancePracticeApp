# Question

Across an organisation, Scrum teams have drifted: some skip retrospectives, some have month-long sprints, others have no product owner. A new agile coach is hired. What should the coach do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that every team strictly adheres to the official Scrum Guide within one quarter across the organisation.
- **B**: Visit each team to separate intentional tailoring from unintentional drift, then agree on minimum viable practices.
- **C**: Recommend the organisation drop Scrum entirely and standardise on Kanban to remove the ongoing framework disagreements.
- **D**: Let teams continue as they currently are because agile fundamentally values self-organisation above all else in practice.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI/agile pattern is to understand context before standardising. (B) distinguishes deliberate tailoring from drift, which leads to honest conversations about what to keep. (A) is heavy-handed and ignores legitimate context. (C) replaces one inconsistency with another and does not address the underlying lack of shared standards. (D) misuses self-organisation to defend missing fundamentals like retrospectives.
