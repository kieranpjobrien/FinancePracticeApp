# Question

A project manager discovers that the organization's agile transformation has resulted in teams using different interpretations of Scrum - some have abandoned retrospectives, others have very long sprints, and some have no product owner. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Let each team continue their approach since agile is about adaptation
- **B**: Mandate strict adherence to the Scrum Guide
- **C**: Work with teams to understand which practices are intentional adaptations versus drift, and establish minimum viable practices that maintain agile principles
- **D**: Recommend abandoning agile and returning to waterfall

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. Distinguishing between intentional tailoring and unintentional drift is important. Some modifications may be legitimate adaptations to context, while others (like abandoning retrospectives) may undermine agile principles. Establishing minimum viable practices ensures core principles are maintained while allowing appropriate tailoring.

Option A ignores problematic deviations that undermine agile's value.

Option B is overly rigid and doesn't acknowledge legitimate context-specific adaptations.

Option D overreacts to implementation challenges rather than addressing them.
