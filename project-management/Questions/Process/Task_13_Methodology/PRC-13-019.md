# Question

A systems integration project depends on three vendors using waterfall, Scrum, and Kanban respectively. The PM cannot change the vendors' delivery models. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Require all vendors to adopt a common delivery model in a contract amendment so coordination is simpler.
- **B**: Coordinate at integration milestones and interface points, treating each vendor's internal model as their own concern.
- **C**: Run integration testing only at the end, after all vendors have completed their contracted deliverables.
- **D**: Assign a separate sub-PM for each vendor and let them coordinate among themselves.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When you cannot change vendors' internals, you focus on the contract surface. (B) is the right level: integration dates, interface contracts, joint test windows. (A) assumes leverage you do not have and may trigger change requests and cost. (C) defers integration risk to the worst possible moment. (D) fragments leadership and pushes coordination away from the PM who actually owns the integration outcome.
