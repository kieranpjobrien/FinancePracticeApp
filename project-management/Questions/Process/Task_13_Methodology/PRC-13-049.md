# Question

Three Scrum teams building a shared product keep hitting late integration surprises. Each team plans its sprint in isolation and discovers cross-team dependencies mid-sprint. What practice should the scrum masters introduce?

## Options

- **A**: Merge the three teams into one larger team so all work is planned in a single sprint backlog every iteration.
- **B**: Add a shared architect who reviews every story before it enters each team's sprint backlog for the upcoming iteration.
- **C**: Appoint a release manager to schedule each team's work centrally and resolve dependencies outside the teams themselves each sprint.
- **D**: Introduce a joint planning event where teams share upcoming work, surface dependencies, and agree coordination before sprinting starts.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI and scaled-agile practice handle cross-team coordination through shared planning, not centralisation. (D) keeps team autonomy while making dependencies visible early. (A) destroys team size benefits and slows decision-making. (B) creates a gatekeeper and queueing delay. (C) reverts to command-and-control scheduling the agile approach aims to avoid.
