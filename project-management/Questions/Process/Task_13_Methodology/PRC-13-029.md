# Question

A predictive project keeps receiving change requests even though the requirements were signed off four months ago. Each CCB cycle approves new scope. What does this MOST likely indicate?

## Options

- **A**: The change control board is too lenient and should reject more requests.
- **B**: The original requirements were less stable than assumed, suggesting an adaptive approach might fit better.
- **C**: The stakeholders are being unreasonable and the sponsor should restrict their access.
- **D**: The team is not pushing back hard enough on stakeholder requests during planning meetings.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Persistent legitimate change requests are a strong signal that the assumption of stable requirements was wrong, which is the foundation of predictive delivery. (B) names the underlying issue. (A) treats the symptom — rejecting valid changes does not make scope stable. (C) blames stakeholders for delivering business reality. (D) blames the team and ignores the methodology mismatch.
