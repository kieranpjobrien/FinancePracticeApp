# Question

A PM joins a small not-for-profit that has never run a formal project. There is no PMO, no templates, and team members have very different ideas about what "the project" means. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Roll out a full PMI-aligned methodology with charters, plans, RACI, and weekly steerco from week one of the project.
- **B**: Skip formal methodology entirely and let the team define their own working agreements as they go along.
- **C**: Apply a few high-value practices (clear goal, simple plan, regular check-in) and add structure as value is seen.
- **D**: Ask the board to commission a maturity assessment and a multi-year PM uplift programme before starting anything.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle calls for matching the weight of methodology to the organisation's capability. (C) introduces just enough structure to add value and earns trust to add more. (A) overwhelms a team with no PM history and risks rejection. (B) misses the chance to bring real benefit. (D) is a multi-month detour when the project itself is the chance to demonstrate value.
