# Question

A new Scrum team's story-point estimates have drifted so that almost every story is now a 13. Velocity is meaningless and planning is painful. The product owner wants to revert to hour estimates instead. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to switch to hour estimates since the current approach clearly provides little useful planning value anymore for sprints.
- **B**: Facilitate a recalibration workshop using reference stories, smaller slicing, and relative sizing to restore meaningful useful estimates.
- **C**: Stop estimating entirely and move the team to a flow-based approach like Kanban with cycle-time forecasting instead properly.
- **D**: Keep story points but ask the product owner to accept that every story is effectively the same size going forward.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring and coaching stance restores estimation health by treating the root cause: poor story slicing and missing reference points. (B) rebuilds the practice. (A) abandons a technique before coaching it. (C) changes the method to avoid fixing the skill. (D) accepts a meaningless metric and drops any pretence of planning.
