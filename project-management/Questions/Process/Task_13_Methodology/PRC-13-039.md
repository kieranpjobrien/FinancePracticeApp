# Question

A 25-person delivery team is split across Sydney, London, and Bangalore — up to 12 hours of time-zone gap and almost no overlap. What methodology adaptations are MOST important?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate a single overlap window each day and require all team members to attend live ceremonies.
- **B**: Choose asynchronous-first practices (written standups, recorded demos, decision logs) and use a small overlap window only for the conversations that need it.
- **C**: Switch to predictive delivery because daily synchronous standups are not viable across these time zones.
- **D**: Split the team into three regional sub-teams that work independently and only sync at major milestones.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring guidance for distributed teams favours asynchronous flow with selective synchronous moments. (B) keeps everyone moving, respects local hours, and reserves real-time time for what truly needs it. (A) imposes unsocial hours on at least one region. (C) misses that adaptive practices can run async — the standup format is a means, not the goal. (D) breaks the team and pushes integration risk to milestones.
