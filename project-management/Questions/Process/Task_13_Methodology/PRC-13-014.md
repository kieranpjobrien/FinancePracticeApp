# Question

Which factor is LEAST important when selecting a project methodology?

## Options

- **A**: Requirements stability and clarity
- **B**: Stakeholder engagement needs
- **C**: The methodology the project manager is most comfortable with
- **D**: Organizational culture and capability

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. While PM familiarity with a methodology may influence ease of implementation, it should not drive methodology selection. The methodology should fit the project's needs, not the PM's preferences. PMs should be adaptable and willing to learn new approaches when project characteristics warrant them.

Options A, B, and D are all important factors: requirements stability affects whether predictive or adaptive approaches fit better; stakeholder engagement needs influence feedback cycles; organizational culture and capability affect what approaches can be successfully implemented.
