# Question

A finance team is replacing its ERP. The data migration and configuration are well-scoped, but the new business processes and user training will need iteration. The current ERP licence expires in nine months and cannot be renewed. What approach is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Predictive delivery across the whole programme from end to end to protect the immovable nine-month go-live date.
- **B**: Hybrid: predictive plan for migration, agile iterations for process design and user enablement, sequenced to go-live.
- **C**: Agile delivery across the whole programme, treating the hard go-live as a routine release milestone in the plan.
- **D**: Two parallel projects with separate PMs — one waterfall for migration, one agile for adoption — meeting at go-live.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring approach is exactly this: methodology by workstream, integrated under one schedule. (B) gives migration the predictability it needs and gives adoption the feedback loop it needs. (A) leaves no room to learn from training pilots. (C) lacks the rigour required for technical migration. (D) creates governance overhead and a synchronisation risk at go-live.
