# Question

A project team is using Scrum but finding that the two-week sprint cycle doesn't work well because many work items require longer than two weeks to complete. What should the project manager and scrum master consider?

## Options

- **A**: Extend sprints to six weeks to fit the work items
- **B**: Break work items into smaller pieces that can be completed within sprints, and consider if Scrum is the right fit
- **C**: Allow work items to span multiple sprints
- **D**: Switch to waterfall immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. The agile principle of delivering working increments requires breaking work into pieces that fit within iterations. If work items consistently can't be broken down, this may indicate the project isn't well-suited to Scrum's timeboxed approach. The team should examine whether items can be decomposed or whether a different approach (like Kanban's flow-based method) might fit better.

Option A extends sprints too long, losing the benefits of short feedback cycles.

Option C undermines Scrum's principle of completing work within sprints.

Option D is reactive - the team should understand the root cause before changing approaches.
