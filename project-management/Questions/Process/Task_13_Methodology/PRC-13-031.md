# Question

A team adopted Scrum six months ago. They follow all the ceremonies but velocity isn't improving and quality issues persist. Retrospectives consistently identify the same problems but little changes. What should the project manager help the team address?

## Options

- **A**: The team needs more training on Scrum mechanics
- **B**: The team may be following Scrum mechanically without embracing continuous improvement - focus on actually acting on retrospective insights
- **C**: Switch to a different agile framework
- **D**: Return to waterfall since Scrum isn't working

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The correct answer is B. Following Scrum ceremonies without actually improving is "mechanical Scrum" or "fake agile." The retrospective exists to drive continuous improvement, but its value comes from acting on insights. The team needs to focus on actually implementing changes identified in retrospectives, not just discussing them.

Option A assumes lack of knowledge, but the problem is lack of action.

Option C changes frameworks when the issue is how the current one is being used.

Option D abandons agile when the problem is implementation, not the methodology.
