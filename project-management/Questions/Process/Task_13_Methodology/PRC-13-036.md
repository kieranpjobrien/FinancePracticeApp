# Question

An organization is attempting enterprise agility with SAFe but is struggling. Teams report excessive meetings, complex processes, and that they were more productive before SAFe. Middle management feels bypassed. Senior leaders aren't participating as expected. What should be considered?

## Options

- **A**: More training on SAFe processes
- **B**: Enforce stricter adherence to SAFe
- **C**: Evaluate whether SAFe was the right choice for this organization's culture and maturity, consider if a simpler approach or different framework might fit better
- **D**: Abandon agile entirely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The correct answer is C. SAFe is a comprehensive framework that requires significant organizational change and leadership engagement. The symptoms described suggest possible misfit between the framework and organizational readiness. Evaluating whether a simpler approach (starting with team-level agile before scaling) or different framework might work better is appropriate.

Option A assumes the framework is right and only implementation is wrong.

Option B may increase resistance if the framework doesn't fit.

Option D overreacts - team-level agile may work even if SAFe doesn't fit.
