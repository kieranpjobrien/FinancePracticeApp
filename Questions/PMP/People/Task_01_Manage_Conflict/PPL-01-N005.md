# Question

Early in a hybrid product programme, you notice two team members from different sub-teams trading increasingly tense messages in a shared channel about who owns an integration interface. The disagreement is still small and has not affected delivery. What is the most appropriate action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Reach out to the two privately, bring them together to talk through the competing assumptions about ownership, and help them agree where the boundary sits.
- **B**: Update the responsibility assignment matrix to record a clear owner for the interface, and share the change with both sub-teams.
- **C**: Raise the ownership ambiguity at the next programme sync so the boundary can be settled with the wider team that depends on the interface.
- **D**: Ask the two sub-team leads to confirm with their members who is accountable for the interface, and have them resolve it through their own line.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The conflict is still minor, between two named people, and rooted in an ambiguous boundary — so all four responses are reasonable, and the margin is about who resolves it and how directly. (A) is best: conflict is resolved most durably early, directly, and privately between the people involved, and a short joint conversation both clears the ambiguity and repairs the rising tension. (B) fixes the documentation but, applied without talking to them, imposes an owner over a live disagreement and leaves the interpersonal friction unaddressed. (C) escalates a small two-person issue to a larger forum prematurely, which can magnify it and put the pair on the spot publicly. (D) routes a resolvable peer issue up two management lines, slowing it and signalling the two cannot sort a minor boundary themselves.
