# Question

On a core banking modernisation, two tenured COBOL engineers openly dismiss the junior team's Kafka proposal as "fashion" during design reviews, and the juniors have stopped proposing ideas. The architecture decision is still genuinely open. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the two senior engineers from future design reviews to protect the juniors' psychological safety and ability to contribute again.
- **B**: Meet the seniors privately to name the impact of their tone, then run the next review against written evaluation criteria so ideas are judged on merit.
- **C**: Ask the juniors to put their proposal into a written paper with benchmarks so that the seniors cannot interrupt them during the next session.
- **D**: Escalate the behaviour to the enterprise architect so that he can rule on the decision and close down the unproductive design debate once and for all.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses both the behaviour (directly, privately, with the people responsible) and the process (objective criteria that protect every voice) — the PMI pattern for silencing behaviour in a genuinely open decision. (A) removes valuable experience and punishes without coaching. (C) shifts the medium but leaves the dismissive behaviour unaddressed. (D) escalates the decision away from the team that owns it and treats behaviour as someone else's problem.
