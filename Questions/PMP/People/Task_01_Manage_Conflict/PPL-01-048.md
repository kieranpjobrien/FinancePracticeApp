# Question

On a Scrum team, the product owner keeps inserting urgent items mid-sprint and the developers have formally complained to you that the sprint goal is meaningless. The PO insists the requests come from real paying customers and cannot wait. How should the scrum master respond?

## Options

- **A**: Support the PO and ask the developers to accept that customer escalations override the sprint goal until the backlog stabilises.
- **B**: Lock the sprint backlog strictly and require every new customer request to wait until the next sprint planning session.
- **C**: Facilitate a session with the PO and team to agree a written policy for handling urgent items, including cancellation rules and capacity buffers.
- **D**: Extend sprint length from two to four weeks so that mid-sprint changes are less disruptive to the team's delivery cadence.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the servant-leader move: surface a recurring conflict into an explicit, team-owned working agreement that balances empirical process control with responsiveness to real customers. (A) sacrifices the sprint goal and the team's predictability for the PO's framing. (B) imposes a rigid rule that ignores genuine urgency and damages the PO-team relationship. (D) treats a behavioural issue with a ceremony change and makes feedback loops longer rather than fixing the root cause.
