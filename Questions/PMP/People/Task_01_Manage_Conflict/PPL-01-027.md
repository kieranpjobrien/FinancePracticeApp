# Question

A stakeholder sends a hostile email criticising the team's quality and copies senior executives. The PM knows the criticism is rooted in a misunderstanding of scope. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Reply-all with a polite but firm rebuttal pointing out the stakeholder's scope error directly.
- **B**: Stay silent and let the issue blow over on its own without responding to the executives copied.
- **C**: Forward the email to your manager and ask them to handle the stakeholder on your behalf.
- **D**: Call the stakeholder privately, clarify scope, then send a follow-up summary to the recipients.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) handles the misunderstanding directly without a public confrontation, then closes the loop in writing — exactly the PMI pattern for stakeholder conflict. (A) is a public showdown that escalates and damages the relationship. (B) leaves a damaging narrative in front of executives. (C) escalates without first attempting direct resolution.
