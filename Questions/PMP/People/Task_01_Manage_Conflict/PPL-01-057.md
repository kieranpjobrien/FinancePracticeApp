# Question

On a rail upgrade, a union safety representative has called a stop-work citing a scaffolding concern your site engineer insists is compliant. Both are dug in. Every hour of delay costs significant penalty payments to the rail operator. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the site engineer to rectify the scaffolding immediately to whatever standard satisfies the safety representative so that work resumes within the shift.
- **B**: Instruct the site engineer to provide the compliance evidence and resume work while you manage the union representative through formal industrial-relations channels.
- **C**: Continue work on unaffected areas of the site and lift capacity elsewhere while the penalty clock runs so the overall schedule impact stays as low as possible.
- **D**: Keep the stop-work in place, inspect with both parties and an independent safety officer, and resume only once the scaffolding status is jointly and formally agreed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) treats safety as non-negotiable, uses an independent authority to resolve a genuine technical dispute, and protects both the workers and the project from later challenge — PMI's compliance-first stance. (A) capitulates on a genuine technical question and may over- or under-correct without understanding the real issue. (B) overrides a live safety stop-work, which exposes the project to regulatory and moral failure. (C) works around a safety concern rather than resolving it and sets a precedent the PM cannot afford.
