# Question

Six weeks into a data-warehouse implementation, your consulting vendor insists a critical reconciliation module is out of scope; your business lead insists it was discussed in pre-sales and is clearly implied by the statement of work. The relationship is cooling and work has paused. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a formal change request to add the reconciliation module and absorb the additional cost into the contingency reserve so that work can resume this week without further delay.
- **B**: Escalate the dispute to your procurement team and the vendor's account manager so that commercial leadership can settle the interpretation and unlock the stalled project delivery together.
- **C**: Facilitate a session where the vendor lead and business lead walk through the statement of work, capture points of agreement and dispute, and agree the minimal module scope jointly.
- **D**: Direct the vendor to deliver the reconciliation module as part of the original signed contract and withhold the next milestone payment until the module is delivered exactly as specified.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) returns both parties to the governing artifact, separates agreed from disputed scope, and uses facilitation to find the minimal workable scope — the PMI first move for SoW disputes. (A) concedes the commercial point before any negotiation and sets a precedent for future "implied" scope. (B) escalates to commercial before the PM has run the joint walkthrough that often resolves the dispute. (D) weaponises payment before interpretation is agreed and will harden vendor positions rather than unlock the work.
