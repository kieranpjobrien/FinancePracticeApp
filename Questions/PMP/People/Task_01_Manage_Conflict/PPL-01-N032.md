# Question

You run a project in a strong matrix. Your lead analyst's functional manager has, without telling you, redirected her to a competing operational task two days a week, and your analyst is now caught between conflicting instructions and falling behind on the project. She is visibly stressed about whom to obey. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the analyst that project commitments take precedence and that she should prioritise your deliverables until told otherwise.
- **B**: Meet the functional manager to understand the operational demand and jointly agree a workable allocation, then confirm clear priorities to the analyst.
- **C**: Escalate immediately to the sponsor that the functional manager is undermining the project by diverting committed resources.
- **D**: Re-plan the project schedule around two days a week of reduced analyst availability and absorb the impact without raising it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The conflict stems from two managers issuing competing claims on one person, so the resolution must happen between the managers — engage the functional manager directly to understand the driver and agree an allocation, then relieve the analyst of the impossible choice by confirming clear priorities. (A) telling her to favour your work simply pits her against her functional manager and worsens her position. (C) escalating before even speaking to the functional manager is premature and damages a relationship you depend on in a matrix. (D) silently absorbing a 40% availability cut hides a real resourcing conflict and may make the project undeliverable.
