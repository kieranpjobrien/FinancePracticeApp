# Question

Your sponsor verbally directs you to add a significant feature "by month end" outside the approved baseline, framing it as urgent. The change would breach the cost baseline and the technical lead believes it is infeasible in the timeframe. The sponsor is irritated and expects compliance. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin the work immediately as directed, since the sponsor has the authority, and raise a change request in parallel to formalise the baseline impact.
- **B**: Tell the sponsor you will assess feasibility and impact with the team and come back within a day or two with options, before committing to anything.
- **C**: Acknowledge the urgency, then walk the sponsor through the integrated cost and schedule impact and the feasibility constraint, and ask them to authorise the trade-offs through the change control process.
- **D**: Propose dropping or deferring lower-priority committed scope to make room for the new feature, and present that swap to the sponsor as the way to deliver it by month end.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Every option is a defensible move under sponsor pressure; the margin is which both respects the sponsor's authority and protects baseline integrity now. (C) does both: it takes the urgency seriously, surfaces the real cost, schedule, and feasibility consequences, and routes the decision through change control where the sponsor authorises the trade-offs transparently. (B) is reasonable and avoids a rushed commitment, but it only buys time — it defers the decision without yet putting the impact and the change-control path in front of the sponsor, which (C) does. (D) identifies a genuine trade-off but pre-selects scope to cut and presents it as settled, bypassing the impact analysis and the formal approval the descope itself requires. (A) commits work and cost before any authorisation, undermining change control even with a request running in parallel.
