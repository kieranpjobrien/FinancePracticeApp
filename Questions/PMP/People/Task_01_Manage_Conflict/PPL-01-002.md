# Question

In Thomas-Kilmann's conflict-handling model, which approach combines high assertiveness with high cooperation to find a solution that fully satisfies all parties' concerns?

## Options

- **A**: Compromising
- **B**: Collaborating
- **C**: Accommodating
- **D**: Avoiding

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Collaborating (problem-solving) is high assertiveness and high cooperation, seeking a win-win that addresses underlying interests. (A) Compromising is moderate on both axes — a lose-lose split where each side gives something up. (C) Accommodating is low assertiveness, high cooperation; one party yields. (D) Avoiding is low on both — the conflict is sidestepped, not resolved.
