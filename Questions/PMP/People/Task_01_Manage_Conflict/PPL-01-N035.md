# Question

In a scaled agile programme, your team and a partner team share a critical service. At the scrum-of-scrums, the two teams openly accuse each other of breaking the shared API and causing each other's failed builds, and the meeting has become unproductive. As one of the scrum masters, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose that the two teams jointly agree an interface contract for the shared service, with versioning and change-notification rules and a protocol for coordinating breaking changes.
- **B**: Set up a shared integration pipeline with contract tests that fail the moment either team breaks the agreed interface, so breakages are caught automatically before they hit the other team.
- **C**: Ask the release train engineer to facilitate a joint session where the two teams work through the dependency and agree how to coordinate going forward.
- **D**: Arrange a joint debugging session on the most recent failed build so the two teams trace the specific breakage together and rebuild trust through solving a concrete problem.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

This is intentionally close: every option is a constructive, collaborative engineering response, not a throwaway. The recurring failures come from an ungoverned interface, so the root-cause fix is a jointly owned contract that defines versioning, change notification and how breaking changes are coordinated (A) — this removes the underlying ambiguity that keeps generating the blame. (B) contract tests are an excellent enforcement mechanism, but they enforce an agreed interface; without first agreeing what the contract is (A), there is nothing for the tests to assert. (C) escalating to the RTE for facilitation is reasonable, yet it defers to someone else a coordination problem the two teams can and should resolve directly, and a facilitated talk without an agreed contract may not produce a durable rule. (D) a joint debugging session fixes today's build and rebuilds some trust, but it is a one-off that leaves the governance gap open for the next breaking change.
