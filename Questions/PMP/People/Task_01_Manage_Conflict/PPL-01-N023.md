# Question

On an e-commerce replatforming project, marketing wants the promotions engine built first and fulfilment wants the warehouse integration first. Both keep lobbying the product owner directly between sprints, the backlog order has flipped three sprints running, and the developers are now openly frustrated at the churn. The product owner tells you privately she feels caught in the middle and is reordering the backlog to whoever pushed hardest most recently. As the project manager, what should you do first?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the product owner to hold both stakeholders to a single explicit prioritisation basis she already owns, so she can defend her ordering decisions herself rather than yielding to the loudest voice.
- **B**: Convene marketing, fulfilment and the product owner to agree, together, how competing value will be weighed and ordered, and capture that as the standing basis for backlog decisions.
- **C**: Help the product owner sequence the two epics by their measurable business value and dependency order now, so the immediate churn stops and the team gets a stable plan to work to.
- **D**: Establish a regular backlog-refinement forum with both stakeholders present, so priority trade-offs are debated openly at a set cadence instead of through ad hoc lobbying.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

All four are legitimate and would help, so the question turns on attacking the root cause first. The churn exists because there is no agreed, shared basis for weighing the two groups' value, and only the product owner currently arbitrates — so the durable first move is to convene both stakeholders and the product owner to agree that basis jointly (B); this both stops the lobbying at source and gives the product owner a mandate she did not invent alone. (A) coaching her to enforce a basis assumes a shared basis already exists and is accepted — it is not, which is why her decisions keep getting overturned. (C) sequencing the two epics on value fixes this instance but, without an agreed framework, the next contested priority reopens the same fight. (D) a refinement cadence is a good structure, but a forum with no agreed decision basis simply relocates the unresolved argument to a recurring meeting.
