# Question

Six months after an acquisition, legacy and acquired engineers on your integration project openly mock each other's coding standards in stand-ups. Delivery has slowed and two acquired engineers have resigned this quarter. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask HR to run a diversity and inclusion workshop for the whole combined team to reset the cultural tone across the entire integration programme.
- **B**: Quietly replace the most vocal legacy engineers with contractors who have no history with either side so that the team can reset its dynamic.
- **C**: Name the behaviour in a team forum, co-create a single set of coding standards with representatives from both groups, and track adherence as a team metric.
- **D**: Let the informal friction continue on the assumption that time and shared delivery pressure will smooth the cultural difference over the coming sprints.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) addresses the behaviour directly, gives both groups ownership of a shared artifact, and creates a measurable team norm — the PMI integration pattern for post-acquisition teams. (A) delegates a leadership conversation to HR and treats a specific behaviour as a generic training gap. (B) removes people rather than coaches them and signals that one side of the merger has lost. (D) is avoidance; the evidence (attrition, slowed delivery) shows time alone is not resolving it at all.
