# Question

What is the primary benefit of addressing conflict early in a project?

## Options

- **A**: It prevents small disagreements from hardening into entrenched disputes that damage productivity and relationships.
- **B**: It gives the project manager a record of team member behaviour for performance reviews.
- **C**: It demonstrates the project manager's positional authority over the team.
- **D**: It satisfies the organisation's formal dispute-resolution policy.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Early intervention matters because positions are still soft and emotional investment is low — the fix is cheaper and the relationship damage smaller. (B) makes documentation the motive, which erodes trust. (C) misframes the PM as authority rather than servant leader. (D) may be a secondary benefit in some organisations but is not the core purpose.
