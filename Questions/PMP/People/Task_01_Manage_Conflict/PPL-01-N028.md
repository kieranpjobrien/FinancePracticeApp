# Question

On a hybrid data-migration programme, your sponsor and the head of data governance are in open disagreement: the sponsor wants to go live with a known data-quality gap to hit a board-promised date, while governance refuses to sign off on incomplete reconciliation. Both have escalated to you to "make the other see sense". What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the sponsor and the head of governance into one session and facilitate them towards an agreement they can both live with, mediating the trade-off live.
- **B**: Prepare a clear, evidence-based summary of the data-quality gap, the specific risks of going live with it, and the viable options, and put it to both decision-makers so they can resolve the trade-off with full information.
- **C**: Refer the disagreement to the programme's steering or change-control board, since a board-level date versus a governance sign-off is above your authority to settle.
- **D**: Propose a phased go-live on a reduced, fully reconciled data scope, giving the sponsor a date and governance a clean sign-off on what is released.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This is a genuine business trade-off between two accountable authorities, and the most useful thing the PM can do first is make the decision well-informed rather than try to own or arbitrate it — so assembling the gap, the concrete risks, and the real options and putting them to both decision-makers (B) lets them resolve it with eyes open, and naturally feeds whatever forum ends up deciding. (A) facilitating them towards a live agreement casts the PM as mediator of a decision two senior authorities must make on the merits; without the evidence in front of them it risks a brokered fudge rather than an informed call, and may exceed where the PM should insert themselves. (C) escalating to the steering or change board is a legitimate route and may well be where this lands, but referring it cold — before you have framed the gap, risks and options — just relocates an unstructured argument upward; the board needs the same summary (B) provides. (D) a phased reduced-scope go-live is a credible option, but proposing it as the answer pre-selects one outcome before the decision-owners have weighed the trade-off, and it may meet neither the board-promised commitment nor governance's view of an acceptable release.
