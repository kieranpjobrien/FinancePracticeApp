# Question

In planning poker, one developer consistently estimates a story at a 2 while another insists it is an 8, and after several rounds neither will move. They are becoming irritated and the rest of the team is waiting. As scrum master, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask each developer to explain the assumptions behind their estimate, since the gap usually reflects different understandings of the work.
- **B**: Invite the two outliers to discuss their reasoning, then re-vote with the whole team to see whether estimates converge.
- **C**: Suggest the team split the story, on the basis that an estimate this contested often signals the story is too large or poorly understood.
- **D**: Note the disagreement, record the story at the higher estimate to reflect the uncertainty, and flag it for clarification before it is pulled into a sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

These are all recognised planning-poker practices, so the answer turns on what to do first when two estimates are far apart. The immediate value lies in surfacing why they differ — a 2-versus-8 gap almost always means the two developers are picturing different scope or risk — so having each explain their assumptions (A) converts the disagreement into shared understanding, which is the entire purpose of the discussion. (B) re-voting is the right follow-on, but re-voting before the outliers have actually explained their reasoning tends to reproduce the same split; the explanation in (A) must come first for the re-vote to converge. (C) splitting the story may well be the outcome once the assumptions are aired, but deciding to split before understanding the source of the gap pre-empts the diagnosis. (D) defaulting to the high number and deferring treats the disagreement as noise to be parked rather than information about where the real uncertainty lies.
