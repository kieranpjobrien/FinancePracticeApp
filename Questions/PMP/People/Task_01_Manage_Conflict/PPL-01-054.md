# Question

On a hybrid consumer app project, the UX designer and the front-end lead have spent three weeks arguing over whether the checkout should be single-page or multi-step, and analytics from the last release are inconclusive. The sprint that depends on the decision starts on Monday. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a short working session where both propose a minimal A/B experiment with success criteria, then ship whichever design wins on real user behaviour.
- **B**: Ask the head of product to pick a direction so the team can start building and stop wasting time on an argument that cannot be objectively settled.
- **C**: Default to the current design for now and revisit the question once the wider redesign effort delivers more complete analytics several sprints later.
- **D**: Invite both parties to present to the team and have the developers vote on which checkout design they prefer to build for the upcoming sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is empirical process control — resolve an opinion-based conflict by letting real users decide against pre-agreed success criteria, which is the PMI tailoring move for a data-available context. (B) escalates a decision the team can settle with evidence and removes ownership from the people doing the work. (C) is avoidance disguised as pragmatism and leaves the conflict unresolved. (D) replaces a design decision with a popularity contest among people who are not the users.
