# Question

A steering committee member consistently attacks your CRM programme in public forums while privately telling you he supports it. The programme sponsor has noticed the attacks and is losing confidence. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to rely on his private support and counter his public comments with strong status reporting at steering forums going forward.
- **B**: Raise the inconsistency with the sponsor and let the sponsor manage the steering-committee member's behaviour at the executive peer level directly.
- **C**: Publicly challenge his comments in the next steering committee so his criticism is exposed as inconsistent with what he is saying to you privately.
- **D**: Meet him one-on-one, surface the inconsistency you have observed, ask what is driving it, and agree how he will engage visibly going forward.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the direct, respectful PMI move: meet the stakeholder, name the observed behaviour, seek understanding, and recontract the relationship — stakeholder engagement at its most deliberate. (A) leaves a confidence-eroding pattern in place and trusts status reports to fix a relationship issue. (B) skips a conversation the PM can have first and outsources stakeholder management. (C) publicly ambushes a senior stakeholder, which is rarely recoverable politically.
