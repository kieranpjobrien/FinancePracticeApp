# Question

On a predictive construction project, the mechanical subcontractor and electrical subcontractor are both claiming the same ceiling void and blaming each other for rework. The site supervisor has escalated to you after two days of stalled progress. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct both subcontractors in writing that electrical takes precedence and the mechanical team must replan around them.
- **B**: Convene both subcontractor leads with the drawings, walk the void on site together, and agree a coordinated sequence before work resumes.
- **C**: Escalate the clash to the design consultant and pause both trades until a formal design clarification is issued to site.
- **D**: Ask each subcontractor to submit a written claim so commercial can assess who is entitled to the additional rework costs.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses the conflict at the level it belongs — a coordination problem between peers — using the artifact (drawings) and the ground truth (the site) to collaborate on a joint sequence. (A) imposes a ruling the PM may not have authority to make and damages the subcontractor relationship. (C) escalates prematurely and halts progress when peers could resolve it working together. (D) jumps to commercial claims before anyone has even attempted to coordinate the work.
