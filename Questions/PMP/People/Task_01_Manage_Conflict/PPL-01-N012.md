# Question

Two members of your Scrum team have developed a persistent personal friction that now surfaces as sniping in every ceremony. You have facilitated one conversation between them, which improved things briefly, but the behaviour has returned and is dragging down the whole team's mood. What should the Scrum Master do next?

## Options

- **A**: Separate the two by moving one to a different team so the friction no longer affects ceremonies.
- **B**: Hold a focused conversation with the two to understand what is really driving the recurring friction, agree specific behavioural commitments, and define how the team will hold each other accountable.
- **C**: Raise the behaviour in the next retrospective so the whole team can give the two direct feedback about its impact.
- **D**: Document the disruptive conduct and refer both members to their functional managers for performance discussions.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) recognises that one surface-level chat was insufficient and goes deeper into the underlying cause, then converts it into concrete, accountable commitments — escalating the depth of intervention before the level of authority, which is the right progression. (A) is a heavy structural fix that may be unnecessary and avoids resolving the relationship. (C) risks a public pile-on that humiliates the pair and can entrench positions. (D) jumps to formal HR/management channels before a substantive direct attempt has been exhausted.
