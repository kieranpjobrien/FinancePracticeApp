# Question

A contractor and an employee are in conflict over code ownership and credit. The contractor feels their work is going unacknowledged; the employee feels the contractor is overstepping. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the contractor they are not a permanent employee and should accept the current situation.
- **B**: Clarify the roles, review the contract on IP, and agree fair attribution for all contributors.
- **C**: Give all credit to the employee on the work to maintain internal team relationships and morale.
- **D**: Treat it as a feelings issue between two team members and avoid getting directly involved at all.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses both the contractual reality and the human concern about recognition — both parties need to feel valued for the team to keep delivering. (A) creates a two-tier culture. (C) is unfair and demotivates the contractor. (D) ignores that recognition directly affects collaboration and quality.
