# Question

Two team members on a marketing automation project disagree over the campaign data model: one wants a flexible schema for future channels, the other a lean schema for current reporting speed. The disagreement is civil and both have strong technical reasons. The design choice will shape the next quarter of work, and the architecture decision blocks two other team members who cannot start their stories until it is settled. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session where both lay out their reasoning and jointly design a model that serves both the flexibility and the performance goals.
- **B**: Have each member concede part of their requirement to reach a middle-ground schema quickly so the build can start.
- **C**: Ask the member with deeper data-modelling experience to make the call and have the other support it.
- **D**: Adopt the lean schema now to unblock the waiting team members and capture the flexibility concern as a backlog item for a later iteration.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stakes are high, the relationship is intact, and both positions hold genuine merit — the conditions where collaborating to an integrated design (A) is the strongest move, because a schema that is lean where it must be fast and extensible where channels will grow may satisfy both and is exactly what unblocks the waiting members durably rather than storing up rework. The blocking detail is real pressure, but a focused working session resolves the decision in hours, not the weeks that would justify trading the technical merits away. (B) settles for a middle-ground schema neither fully endorses and that may inherit the weaknesses of both when an integrative option is reachable. (C) decides by seniority, which can leave the other member disengaged and the flexibility concern under-weighed on a quarter-shaping choice. (D) looks responsive to the blockers but pre-commits the architecture to one side under time pressure and defers a structural concern to a backlog item that is costly to act on once the schema is built; the unblock it buys is illusory if the lean schema later has to be reworked.
