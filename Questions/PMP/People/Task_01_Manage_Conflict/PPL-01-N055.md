# Question

Two developers on a healthcare-scheduling Scrum team are locked in a tense disagreement during a backlog refinement session, and the discussion has stalled the whole team. The disputed point is a genuine design fork that the developers are best placed to decide, but it needs data neither has to hand right now. As Scrum Master facilitating the session, which actions are appropriate in the moment? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Name the disagreement openly and check that each developer's position has been accurately heard before moving on.
- **B**: Ask the pair what evidence would let them settle it, and arrange to bring that data to a focused follow-up with only the relevant people.
- **C**: Continue the rest of refinement on the other backlog items and return to the contested story once the data is available.
- **D**: Have the team vote on the two design options now so refinement produces a decision rather than ending unresolved.
- **E**: Escalate the design fork to the product owner to choose between the two approaches and unblock the session.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The constraint is that the decision is the developers' to make but cannot be made well without data that is not present, so good in-the-moment facilitation acknowledges the conflict and confirms positions (A), defines what evidence would resolve it and parks it for a focused session (B), and keeps the rest of refinement productive in the meantime (C). (D) is tempting because voting feels like progress and is a legitimate agile technique, but forcing a decision now — before the very data both sides agree is missing — manufactures a poorly-informed outcome and pressures the minority to fall in line. (E) hands a "how" decision the development team owns to the product owner, who owns the "what"; it unblocks the clock at the cost of the team's design ownership. The trap is that D and E both produce a tidy decision, but each resolves the wrong thing — the issue is missing information and rightful ownership, not a stalled vote or an escalation.
