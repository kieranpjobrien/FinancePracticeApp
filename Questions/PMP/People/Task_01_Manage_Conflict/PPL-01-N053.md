# Question

On a data-platform migration, you notice one engineer has stopped responding to another engineer's pull-request reviews and is routing all his work around her. There has been no open argument, but collaboration between them has quietly broken down and delivery on a shared component is slowing. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the shared component to a single owner so the two no longer need to collaborate on it.
- **B**: Meet the avoiding engineer one-on-one to understand what has changed before deciding how to address it.
- **C**: Bring both engineers into a joint session immediately to clear the air about the breakdown.
- **D**: Add a third reviewer to the component so reviews keep flowing despite the friction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Withdrawal hides an unspoken issue, so (B) starts by privately understanding the avoiding party's perspective before choosing an intervention — diagnose then act. (A) and (D) are workarounds that paper over a relationship problem and can entrench the avoidance. (C) forces the two together before the PM knows what is actually wrong, which can backfire if one feels ambushed. Understanding precedes the joint conversation.
