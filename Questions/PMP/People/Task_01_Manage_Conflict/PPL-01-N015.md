# Question

In a scaled agile programme, two feature teams share a platform component. Team A needs an API change urgently for its sprint commitment; the platform team says the change destabilises Team B's in-flight work. At the scrum-of-scrums, the two teams' representatives are blaming each other for poor planning and the dependency is now blocking both. As the programme's agile lead, which TWO actions best address the conflict? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the teams to make the dependency and its impact visible on a shared board and jointly sequence the work to minimise disruption to both.
- **B**: Tell the platform team to prioritise Team A's request because Team A escalated first and has a firm commitment.
- **C**: Bring the affected product owners together to re-rank the competing items against programme-level value and agree the order.
- **D**: Instruct both teams to stop the disputed work until a programme manager rules on whose need is more important.
- **E**: Let each team proceed independently with its own branch of the component and merge later to avoid blocking either now.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

The dispute is a cross-team dependency-and-priority problem, so the fixes are to make the dependency transparent and jointly sequence it (A) and to have the product owners arbitrate priority by programme value (C) — collaboration plus proper prioritisation ownership. (B) rewards escalation speed over value and ignores Team B's risk. (D) freezes both teams awaiting a top-down ruling rather than enabling the teams to coordinate. (E) defers an integration conflict that will likely resurface, harder, at merge time and undermines a shared platform.
