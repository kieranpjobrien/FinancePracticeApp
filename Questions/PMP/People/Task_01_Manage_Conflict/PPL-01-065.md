# Question

Your only female engineer on a 15-person DevOps team has told you privately that two male peers repeatedly interrupt her and restate her ideas as their own in stand-ups. She has asked you not to name her when addressing it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Observe the next several stand-ups closely yourself, then address the interrupting behaviour generally with the team based on what you have directly observed in the sessions.
- **B**: Ask all stand-up attendees to start using a simple turn-taking protocol immediately, framing the change as a general productivity improvement rather than responding to any specific reported behaviour.
- **C**: Meet the two peers privately and tell them you have received feedback that they are interrupting a colleague, preserving confidentiality about which team member raised the concern with you.
- **D**: Encourage the engineer to raise the issue herself at the next retrospective, offering to support her publicly in the room so her concerns are heard by the whole team directly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) honours the confidentiality commitment, changes the team-level dynamic through a structural intervention that lifts everyone, and does not rely on the PM naming individuals based on second-hand reports — the PMI respectful-workplace pattern when the affected party has asked not to be named. (A) relies on the PM seeing the behaviour live, which frequently does not happen when observers are present. (C) identifies the reported team member by implication, breaching the stated confidentiality. (D) places the burden of fixing the behaviour on the person being harmed and contradicts her explicit request.
