# Question

In a heated design review on your aerospace avionics project, a senior systems engineer called a junior colleague "lazy and careless" in front of the full team. Several team members have approached you privately about how uncomfortable the outburst was for everyone present. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the incident in the next retrospective so the team can jointly agree on norms of respectful behaviour and what consequences should apply for breaches.
- **B**: Arrange a meeting with the junior colleague and the senior engineer together so they can work through the comments and restore their working relationship directly.
- **C**: Ask HR to issue a formal written warning to the senior engineer so the breach of workplace standards is recorded on his personnel file for any future pattern.
- **D**: Meet the senior engineer privately today, name the behaviour as unacceptable, then check on the junior colleague and restore the team's standard of respect together.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI immediate-intervention pattern for personal attacks: address the behaviour with the person responsible now, care for the person harmed, then re-establish the standard with the team. (A) is too slow for a personal attack and asks the team to retroactively set norms that should never have been broken. (B) forces the harmed colleague into a meeting on the aggressor's terms before the behaviour has been named. (C) escalates to HR before the PM has done the direct coaching conversation PMI expects of them.
