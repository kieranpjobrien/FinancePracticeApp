# Question

A project manager is leading an agile transformation. The operations manager openly criticises the project in meetings, claiming it will disrupt stable processes. A private conversation reveals he fears losing staff to the new squads. The project has strong executive sponsorship. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the resistance and inform the executive sponsor so they can address the operations manager directly.
- **B**: Present case studies of successful agile transformations to show the operations manager the approach works.
- **C**: Proceed with the transformation on schedule since executive sponsorship gives the PM the authority to do so.
- **D**: Acknowledge the concerns, explore how his team's interests can be protected, and invite him to help shape the transition plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) addresses the root cause — fear of staff loss — by turning a resistor into a collaborator on the transition design. PMI values stakeholder engagement that addresses underlying interests, not just positions. (A) escalates prematurely before the PM has attempted resolution. (B) answers the stated objection but ignores the real concern, so it will not change behaviour. (C) relies on positional power and leaves active resistance in place, which undermines delivery even with executive backing.
