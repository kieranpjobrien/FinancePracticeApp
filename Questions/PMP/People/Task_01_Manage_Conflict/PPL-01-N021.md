# Question

During sprint planning for a mobile banking app, two senior developers disagree sharply about whether to adopt a new state-management library. One argues it will halve future defect rates; the other says the migration cost will sink the next two sprints. The debate has consumed twenty minutes and the rest of the team has gone quiet. As scrum master, what should you do next?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the two developers to each prepare a short spike for the next sprint comparing the approaches against agreed criteria, and timebox today's discussion so planning can continue.
- **B**: Defer to the developer with deeper experience in the existing codebase, since their estimate of migration cost is more likely to be reliable.
- **C**: Put the decision to a team vote so the whole team owns the outcome and the two developers stop dominating the session.
- **D**: Escalate the architectural choice to the engineering manager so an authoritative decision removes the deadlock and protects the sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The disagreement is over an empirical question (defect rates vs migration cost) that neither party can settle by assertion, so converting opinion into evidence via timeboxed spikes is the collaborative, data-driven move while still protecting today's planning. (B) settles a two-sided technical question by seniority alone and discards the defect-rate argument without testing it. (C) a majority vote on a deep technical trade-off the wider team has not analysed produces a poorly informed decision and can entrench division. (D) escalating an in-team architectural choice prematurely undermines the team's self-organisation and is not yet warranted.
