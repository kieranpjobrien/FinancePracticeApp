# Question

Two business units sponsoring a shared CRM rollout each insist their reporting requirements take priority for the first release, and both have escalated to you in writing. The requirements genuinely conflict on data structure, so only one can be delivered first. The combined budget funds the project equally. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Take the two written escalations and your own analysis of business value to the steering committee, and ask them to rule on which requirement is sequenced first.
- **B**: Bring both units together to agree objective prioritisation criteria — such as quantified business value, regulatory deadlines, and number of affected users — and apply them to sequence the requirements transparently.
- **C**: Assess each requirement against the business case and the project's stated benefits, then sequence the one that contributes more to the approved objectives and explain the rationale to both units.
- **D**: Ask each unit to quantify the cost of delay for its requirement, and sequence first the one whose deferral does the most measurable harm to the organisation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Each option is a legitimate way to break a genuine priority tie, so the margin is fine. (B) is best because the parties co-define the criteria before they are applied: shared ownership of the method is what gives the outcome legitimacy and stops the loser re-escalating — the confronting/problem-solving approach. (C) uses sound criteria but the PM decides alone, so a unit that disagrees with the weighting has had no part in it and is likely to escalate again. (D) is a valid single lens (cost of delay) imposed unilaterally, and it reduces a multi-factor decision to one factor each unit will game. (A) escalates to governance before the two units have attempted to resolve it together, surrendering a conflict the PM can facilitate.
