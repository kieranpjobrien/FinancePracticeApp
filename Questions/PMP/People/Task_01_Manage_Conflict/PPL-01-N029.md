# Question

Mid-sprint, the product owner asks the team to pull in an extra high-value story that a major client has just requested. The developers respond that the sprint is already full and that taking it on will force them to cut corners on testing. The exchange in the team channel is turning testy, with each side restating its case more sharply. As scrum master, what is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a short conversation about remaining sprint capacity and the new story's value, so the developers and product owner decide together whether to renegotiate the sprint scope.
- **B**: Explain that the Sprint Backlog can be renegotiated between the product owner and developers as more is learned, and let the two parties settle it on that basis.
- **C**: Remind the team that the Sprint Goal is the fixed commitment, and help them assess whether the new story fits within that goal or threatens it.
- **D**: Ask the product owner whether something already in the sprint can be dropped in exchange, so capacity is freed before the team commits to the new story.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

This is deliberately fine-grained: all four reflect correct Scrum thinking, so the best answer is the one that fits the scrum master's facilitative role most completely. Scope can be renegotiated mid-sprint, but that negotiation belongs to the developers and product owner together and must be grounded in real capacity and value — so facilitating that joint conversation (A) is the fullest response. (B) is true in principle but leaves the two testy parties to "settle it" without the facilitation that is exactly what they need right now to avoid the channel war escalating. (C) anchoring to the Sprint Goal is a sound lens and may well be part of the discussion, but it pre-frames the answer around protecting the goal rather than helping them weigh the trade-off openly. (D) proposing a specific swap is a reasonable option to surface, but the scrum master suggesting the mechanism pre-empts a decision the team and product owner should reach themselves.
