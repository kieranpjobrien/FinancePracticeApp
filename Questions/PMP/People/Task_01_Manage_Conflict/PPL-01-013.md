# Question

Two team members have a personal conflict that started outside work. It is now affecting collaboration — they avoid one another and communicate only through intermediaries. Their skills are complementary and both are needed on a critical workstream. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign work so they no longer have to collaborate on anything inside the critical workstream.
- **B**: Refer the matter to HR for resolution rather than attempting to address it at the project level.
- **C**: Meet them together, acknowledge the personal element, and agree professional working norms.
- **D**: Stay out of the personal issue entirely and only manage to the agreed deliverables and dates.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI position: the PM cannot fix a personal relationship but can and must set professional expectations and facilitate practical working agreements. (A) accommodates the conflict at the project's expense and sets a precedent that personal issues excuse professional duties. (B) escalates to HR before attempting a direct resolution. (D) ignores work that is already being affected through avoidance and bottlenecked communication.
