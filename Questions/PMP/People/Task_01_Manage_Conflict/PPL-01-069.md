# Question

Your programme sponsor is on extended leave for eight weeks and a critical dispute between finance and operations directors over funding allocation has reached an impasse. Neither director will act without the sponsor's call and project burn is continuing unchecked. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue executing within the last approved baseline, pause any work dependent on the disputed funding, and wait for the sponsor to return before seeking a ruling.
- **B**: Escalate directly to the sponsor's manager and request an executive decision on the funding allocation so that work can resume within the current programme baseline immediately.
- **C**: Facilitate a joint session with finance and operations, frame the cost of delay in business terms, and propose options that both can sign off within their delegated authority limits.
- **D**: Request the acting sponsor nominated by the governance plan, brief them on the dispute and burn impact, then bring both directors to them for a time-bounded decision this week.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) follows the governance plan's delegated authority, respects the sponsor's absence, and converts a stalled dispute into a decision within the project's own governance framework — PMI's escalation-path discipline. (A) lets burn continue unchallenged and abdicates the PM's duty to drive resolution. (B) bypasses the agreed governance path and risks undermining the sponsor on return. (C) is useful facilitation but cannot by itself resolve a dispute that both directors have already said needs executive ruling.
