# Question

A vendor supplying critical components has delivered late repeatedly. The PM has documented the issues and held several discussions with the vendor; performance has not improved. Procurement owns the contract, which contains penalty clauses, and a regulatory deadline is now at risk. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invoke the contract's penalty clauses straight away to put pressure on the vendor.
- **B**: Replace the vendor with a different supplier to stop further slippage.
- **C**: Adjust the schedule to absorb the slippage since the vendor relationship sits with procurement.
- **D**: Engage procurement to escalate formally, evaluate contractual remedies, and stand up contingency plans in parallel.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the integrated PMI response: the PM does not own the contract but is accountable for the project, so escalate through the right channel and protect delivery with contingency. (A) bypasses procurement, which the PM cannot do unilaterally. (B) is a major decision that requires procurement and may not even be feasible before the regulatory date. (C) is passive — "outside my control" is not the same as "not my problem".
