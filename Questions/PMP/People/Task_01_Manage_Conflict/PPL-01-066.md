# Question

Your sponsor keeps adding small "just while you're in there" features to the approved scope of a regulatory reporting project. The team is now 15% behind on the committed delivery date and increasingly frustrated that nothing has changed officially. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reframe the conversation with the sponsor around the business case, quantify the cumulative impact of the small additions, and agree either a rebaseline or a clear scope freeze together.
- **B**: Keep absorbing the additions quietly on the assumption that a strong sponsor relationship matters more than the delivery date for a regulatory reporting project of this size.
- **C**: Tell the team they should deliver what the sponsor has asked for without pushback because the sponsor has the final say on what counts as project scope.
- **D**: Raise a formal concern to the steering committee about the sponsor's behaviour and request that scope authority be moved from the sponsor to the steering group.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) uses the business case as the anchor, makes the cumulative impact visible, and forces an honest choice between scope and schedule — the PMI integrated-change-control pattern for creeping scope. (B) lets the baseline drift silently and breaches the PM's transparency duty to the team and the organisation. (C) abdicates the PM's stewardship role and reduces change control to whatever the sponsor asks for. (D) escalates before the PM has had the honest conversation with the sponsor that the situation actually calls for first.
