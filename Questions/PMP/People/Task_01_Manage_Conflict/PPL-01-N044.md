# Question

Early in a logistics analytics project, two analysts have a minor but recurring disagreement about naming conventions in shared notebooks. It has not affected delivery, but you have noticed a slight edge creeping into their stand-up exchanges over the past week. What is the most appropriate PM response?

## Options

- **A**: Raise notebook naming as a topic at the next team retrospective so the whole team can agree a convention together.
- **B**: Speak with the two analysts together, point out the friction you have observed, and ask them to settle and document a convention between themselves.
- **C**: Have a quiet word with each analyst separately to check whether the naming dispute is really about something else before deciding what to do.
- **D**: Add a naming-convention check to the notebook review checklist so the standard is enforced regardless of personal preference.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The issue is low-stakes, work-focused, between two named people, and only just starting to sour, so the proportionate move is to address it directly and promptly with the two of them and have them own a documented convention (B) — early, lightweight, and self-managed. (A) defers a here-and-now friction to a scheduled ceremony and widens a two-person matter to the whole team unnecessarily. (C) treats a visible, trivial naming dispute as a likely proxy for a deeper problem; that diagnostic step fits withdrawal or escalating tension, not a small open disagreement the pair can resolve now. (D) imposes a mechanical control that may stop the symptom but leaves the interpersonal edge unaddressed and signals you do not trust the team to settle a minor standard.
