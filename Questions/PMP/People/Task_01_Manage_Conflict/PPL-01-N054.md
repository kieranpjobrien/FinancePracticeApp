# Question

You are forming a new cross-functional team for a transport-ticketing project. Several members have not worked together before, and two of the departments involved have a history of friction. Work starts next week. You want to reduce the chance of destructive conflict before it takes hold. What is the most effective preventive action?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team in co-creating a charter with agreed ground rules for communication, decisions, and how disagreements get handled.
- **B**: Hold a kickoff that builds shared purpose and clarifies each member's role and decision rights before work begins.
- **C**: Meet members from the two historically clashing departments individually to understand past grievances before they join the team.
- **D**: Schedule a recurring retrospective from sprint one so emerging friction surfaces and gets addressed early and regularly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For a forming team carrying a friction history, the most effective preventive action is to have the team co-create a charter with ground rules covering communication, decision-making, and how disagreement is handled (A); because the team builds and owns the rules, they hold up when tension arises, and they pre-empt destructive conflict rather than waiting to catch it. (B) is genuinely valuable and addresses a real cause of conflict — role and decision-right ambiguity — but it is narrower than a charter and does not itself establish agreed norms for handling disagreement, so it leaves the interpersonal rules unset. (C) gathers useful context and can defuse specific grievances, yet on its own it works one relationship at a time and sets no shared team standard, so it is a complement to a charter, not a substitute. (D) is sound but reactive by design: a retrospective surfaces friction after it appears, whereas the question asks for the action that most reduces the chance of it taking hold in the first place. The fine margin is that all four are legitimate prevention moves, but only the co-created charter sets shared, owned norms up front across the whole team.
