# Question

A matrixed tester is double-booked: your release-critical test cycle overlaps with another PM's regression run. The functional manager says the tester will decide herself, and she has frozen under the pressure, attending neither project's stand-ups. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Take the tester off the decision, agree a split schedule directly with the other PM, and inform the functional manager of the arrangement you have reached.
- **B**: Raise a resource conflict escalation to the PMO immediately so that an independent authority can direct the tester to your release-critical cycle.
- **C**: Check in with the tester privately to understand the pressure she is under, then bring her and the other PM together to co-design a workable schedule.
- **D**: Accept the delay on your side, free the tester for the other project, and rebaseline your release plan with the sponsor on the new delivery date.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) addresses the human issue first (the tester is overwhelmed, not empowered) then solves the allocation with the peers who own it — the servant-leadership and peer-negotiation pattern PMI favours. (A) removes the tester's agency precisely when she needs it restored. (B) escalates a peer-resolvable issue and bypasses the functional manager's stated intent. (D) trades your baseline away before any real negotiation has happened with the other PM involved.
