# Question

On a fully remote 12-person platform team, a pull-request thread has turned into a heated argument between two engineers across 40 comments in 24 hours. The rest of the team is silent and the related deployment is now blocked. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the two engineers to stop commenting, close the PR without merging, and bring the design question to the next sprint planning session as an agenda item.
- **B**: Schedule a brief video call with the two engineers today, acknowledge that the thread has escalated, and facilitate a conversation focused on the underlying design question.
- **C**: Post a calm summary of both positions in the thread and invite the wider team to weigh in so that the argument is no longer between only two people.
- **D**: Allow the thread to continue since engineers should be free to debate technical designs rigorously and merge the PR once one engineer concedes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) moves a stalled written conflict into synchronous dialogue with the parties involved — the PMI pattern for emotion escalating in async channels. (A) avoids both the relationship issue and the unblock decision, punishing the whole team for two people's thread. (C) widens a personal argument to a group audience before it has been de-escalated and rarely helps. (D) mistakes silence for consent and lets psychological safety erode while the deployment stays blocked.
