# Question

In a heated architecture debate, one developer makes a comment that lands as a personal insult about another developer's competence. The targeted developer looks visibly upset but says nothing. The discussion continues. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the comment pass since the technical discussion kept moving along productively.
- **B**: Take over the technical discussion to redirect attention away from the personal comment.
- **C**: Speak to the developer who made the comment privately after the meeting ends.
- **D**: Pause briefly, name that the comment landed as personal, and reset respectful debate.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the right intervention: name the impact in the moment, reset the norm, and let the technical discussion continue with the boundary clear. PMI treats psychological safety as a leadership responsibility. (A) normalises personal attacks. (B) avoids the interpersonal issue. (C) is necessary as a follow-up but missing the live moment fails the targeted developer in front of witnesses.
