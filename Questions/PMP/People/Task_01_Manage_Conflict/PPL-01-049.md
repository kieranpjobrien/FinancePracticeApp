# Question

On a pharmaceutical validation project, the sponsor is pressuring the validation lead to sign off on a protocol deviation she believes is not compliant with GxP. She has come to you visibly distressed and is considering resigning. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassure her that sponsor pressure is normal and coach her on how to have a more diplomatic conversation with the sponsor directly.
- **B**: Offer to sign the deviation yourself so she is not personally exposed and the project can continue on schedule without further delay.
- **C**: Schedule a meeting with the sponsor, QA and the validation lead together to settle the disagreement between the parties on an agreed position.
- **D**: Back her professional judgement, document the concern, and escalate to QA and the sponsor together before any sign-off occurs on the protocol.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the only response consistent with PMI's non-negotiable compliance principle and the PM's ethical duty: support the qualified professional, create a paper trail, and engage the compliance authority before any sign-off. (A) minimises a legitimate compliance concern and frames it as a communication problem. (B) is a serious ethical breach — the PM is not the qualified signatory and substituting signatures to bypass judgement is falsification. (C) treats a regulatory matter as a negotiation among equals, which it is not.
