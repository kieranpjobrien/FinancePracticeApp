# Question

A team member privately complains to you about a colleague's behaviour but asks you to keep their identity confidential. What is the most appropriate approach?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the accused colleague immediately with the anonymous complaint as reported to you.
- **B**: Tell the complainant you cannot act on anonymous information and close the matter there.
- **C**: Forward the complaint to HR without any investigation or first-level attempt at resolution.
- **D**: Increase direct observation, act on what you witness, and coach the complainant on options.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) preserves the complainant's confidence while honouring fairness — you only act on what you can verify, and you support the complainant in addressing it directly, which is PMI's preference. (A) acts on unverified information and burns the confidence. (B) dismisses a potentially real concern. (C) escalates without any first-level investigation or attempted resolution.
