# Question

On a distributed game-development project, the London and Manila teams are in recurring friction: London feels Manila ignores its messages, while Manila feels London schedules every meeting at times that fall late at night for them. Resentment is starting to show in code review comments. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate that all meetings rotate across time zones so no single team always bears the inconvenient hour.
- **B**: Bring both teams together to surface each side's experience and jointly agree communication and meeting norms that work for both.
- **C**: Set up a shared asynchronous channel as the default so fewer live meetings are needed across the time gap.
- **D**: Speak to each team lead separately to calm tempers before the friction spills further into the work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The conflict is rooted in unspoken cross-location expectations, so (B) — getting both teams to hear each other and co-author norms — addresses the real cause and gives both ownership of the fix. (A) and (C) are sensible mechanics but impose a solution before the teams have shared their needs, so they may miss the actual pain. (D) calms symptoms separately without building the shared agreement that prevents recurrence.
