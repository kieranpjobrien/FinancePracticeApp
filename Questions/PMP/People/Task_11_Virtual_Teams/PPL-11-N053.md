# Question

A project manager coaching a distributed team wants members to choose the right communication channel for the situation rather than defaulting to whatever is nearest. Match each scenario on a virtual team to the most appropriate channel.

## Items

- **1**: A heated disagreement about design direction is escalating in a text channel, with tone being misread on both sides.
- **2**: A non-urgent question whose answer should be findable by the whole team later, including members in other time zones who are offline now.
- **3**: A production incident is unfolding and the on-call members need to coordinate and make decisions immediately.
- **4**: A routine status note that several stakeholders need but that requires no discussion.

## Matches

- **A**: A live video call to read tone and resolve it in real time.
- **B**: A written post in the shared knowledge base or team channel where it is searchable and answerable asynchronously.
- **C**: A real-time voice or video "war room" bridge for the duration of the incident.
- **D**: A one-way broadcast update (e.g. email or a status channel post) with no expectation of reply.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Channel choice should match richness and urgency to the need. A conflict where tone is being misread needs the richest synchronous channel (1-A) so people can hear and see each other. A non-urgent, reusable question is best handled asynchronously and searchably (2-B) so time-zone-shifted members can answer and others can reuse it. A live incident demands an immediate real-time bridge (3-C). A no-discussion status note is a one-way broadcast (4-D), which avoids pulling people into unnecessary interaction.
