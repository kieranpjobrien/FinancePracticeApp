# Question

On a distributed agile team, the project manager notices one developer in another region has gone quiet: his camera stays off, he rarely speaks in stand-ups, and his pull-request comments have become terse. His delivery is still on track, so no one else has raised it. Which actions should the project manager take? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Raise the pattern with the team in retrospective so everyone can discuss whether remote engagement norms need to change.
- **B**: Arrange a private, informal one-to-one to check in on how he is doing and whether anything is getting in his way.
- **C**: Observe his collaboration and contribution over the next few iterations to understand the pattern before drawing conclusions.
- **D**: Ask his regional team lead to have a quiet word and report back on whether there is a problem.
- **E**: Make camera-on mandatory for stand-ups so the whole team is visibly present and he is drawn back in.

## Answer

B, C

## Explanation

A possible disengagement signal on a virtual team warrants a private check-in (B) — direct, respectful and the fastest way to learn what is really going on — combined with quiet observation of the pattern (C) so the PM responds to evidence rather than one data point. (A) exposes one person's behaviour to the group and risks embarrassing him over something that may be personal. (D) routes a personal conversation through a third party and can feel like being reported on. (E) treats a symptom with a blanket rule that ignores bandwidth, privacy and the actual cause, and may deepen his withdrawal.
