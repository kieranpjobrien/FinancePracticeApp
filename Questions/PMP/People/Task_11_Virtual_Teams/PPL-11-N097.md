# Question

A project manager is chartering a brand-new, fully virtual team drawn from four organisations, none of whom have worked together. The PM wants the team charter to genuinely de-risk distributed working rather than be a generic document. Which elements would most directly strengthen a virtual team charter? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: A detailed individual work-from-home equipment and ergonomic standard each member must certify they meet before starting.
- **B**: An agreed daily overlap window and the team's primary working time zone reference, so people know when synchronous collaboration can happen.
- **C**: A fixed seating and presence plan specifying which hours each member must appear online to the others.
- **D**: Defined communication norms — which channel for what, expected response times, and where decisions are recorded as the single source of truth.
- **E**: A shared escalation and decision-making path, so members spread across organisations know how choices are made and who to involve when blocked.

## Answer

B, D, E

## Explanation

A virtual charter de-risks the things distribution makes hard. An overlap window and time-zone reference (B) make synchronous work possible across zones; communication norms with a single source of truth (D) prevent the channel confusion and lost decisions that plague distributed teams; and a shared escalation and decision path (E) matters especially when members come from four organisations with no common chain. (A) addresses individual home setups, which is a reasonable hygiene item but does not shape how the team collaborates across distance — it is peripheral to coordinating a multi-organisation virtual team. (C) is the trap: a rigid hours-of-presence mandate contradicts the flexible, overlap-plus-async model that lets a cross-zone team function, and it confuses being online with contributing. The strongest charter elements set up synchronous opportunity, communication discipline and a clear decision path, not mandated presence or individual desk standards.
