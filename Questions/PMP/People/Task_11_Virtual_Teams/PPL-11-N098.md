# Question

On a distributed team, a written code-review comment from one member was read by another, in a different country, as dismissive and rude. A terse reply followed, the thread cooled into clipped one-line exchanges, and the two now avoid each other's work. Both are capable and neither has raised it formally; the rest of the team senses the chill. The PM learns of it second-hand. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the two together in a private synchronous conversation to surface the misunderstanding and let them re-establish intent and rapport directly, then address any norms afterwards.
- **B**: Post a reminder to the whole team about respectful, considerate written communication, so the norm is reinforced without singling anyone out.
- **C**: Review the offending comment thread and privately advise the member whose wording caused offence to phrase future feedback more carefully.
- **D**: Wait and watch, since neither has formally complained and capable professionals will usually repair such frictions themselves once the immediate sting fades.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The conflict is rooted in misread intent over text — a tone that did not survive the leap from one culture and language to another — and it has now hardened into avoidance that the team can feel. The most effective first move restores the lost channel: a private live conversation lets the two hear each other, recover the intended meaning and rebuild rapport, after which any writing norms can be revisited (A). A broadcast reminder to everyone (B) is indirect, addresses nobody's actual relationship, and may feel like a veiled rebuke while leaving the rift between the two untouched. Privately coaching the "offender" (C) presumes one party is at fault when the issue is a two-sided misinterpretation, and risks deepening the divide rather than reconnecting them. Waiting (D) gambles on self-repair while the chill is already spreading to the team and the avoidance is entrenching. The discriminator is whether the action reconnects the two people in a richer medium now or substitutes a general, impersonal gesture for the direct repair the situation needs.
