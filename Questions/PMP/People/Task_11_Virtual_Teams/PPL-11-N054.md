# Question

A globally distributed team runs a follow-the-sun model: work is handed from one region to the next at the end of each region's day. Defects and rework are rising, and post-mortems trace many to misunderstandings at the hand-off — the receiving region misreads the state of the work and assumptions made by the previous region. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Require the outgoing region to write a much more detailed written hand-off document capturing every decision and assumption before they log off.
- **B**: Reorganise the work so each region owns whole features end to end, eliminating most cross-region hand-offs altogether.
- **C**: Add a quality gate so the incoming region reviews and signs off the previous region's work before continuing it.
- **D**: Introduce a brief live overlap at each hand-off where the outgoing and incoming members talk through the state of the work, supported by a standard hand-off note.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Hand-off failures in follow-the-sun usually come from lost tacit context that documents alone cannot fully carry, so a short live overlap plus a standard note (D) lets the incoming region ask questions and confirm understanding at the moment of transfer. (A) improves the artefact but written hand-offs cannot capture every assumption and offer no chance to clarify. (B) may be ideal long term but is a major restructuring that does not help the current cadence and may not fit interdependent work. (C) adds inspection after the fact, catching some defects later but not preventing the misunderstanding that creates them.
