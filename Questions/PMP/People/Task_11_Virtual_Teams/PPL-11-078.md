# Question

A PM is coaching a virtual team to match each communication need to a channel of appropriate richness and immediacy, rather than overusing video calls or burying everything in chat. Match each communication need to the most suitable channel.

## Items

- **1**: Delivering sensitive, difficult performance feedback to one team member.
- **2**: Broadcasting a finalised schedule change that needs no discussion but everyone must know.
- **3**: Collaboratively designing a complex workflow that benefits from real-time sketching and back-and-forth.
- **4**: A quick clarifying question that any teammate could answer when they next look at their messages.

## Matches

- **A**: A one-to-one private video or voice call where tone and empathy come through.
- **B**: A one-way written broadcast — email or an announcements channel — with no expectation of reply.
- **C**: A live shared-screen or whiteboard working session with the relevant people.
- **D**: A short post in the team chat channel, answerable asynchronously.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Matching richness and immediacy to the need avoids both over- and under-communicating. Sensitive feedback demands the richest, most private channel so tone and empathy are present (1-A); a chat message or broadcast would be tone-deaf and exposing. A finalised, discussion-free notice is a one-way broadcast (2-B), which informs everyone without pulling them into interaction. Complex collaborative design needs real-time co-creation with shared visuals (3-C), which async text cannot support well. A low-stakes clarifying question fits a quick async chat post (4-D), reserving synchronous time for things that truly need it. The discriminator is fitting the channel to the interaction's sensitivity, complexity and urgency rather than defaulting to one medium for everything.
