# Question

A project manager is supporting a newly assembled, fully remote team and recognises it is moving through the stages of team development. Each stage shows up differently when the team is distributed and needs a different kind of support. Match each observation on the virtual team to the development stage it most clearly indicates.

## Items

- **1**: Members are polite and tentative in chat, unsure who does what, and wait for the PM to direct nearly everything.
- **2**: Two members are openly clashing over tooling and ways of working, and others are taking sides in side channels.
- **3**: The team has settled into reliable async norms, gives candid feedback freely, and self-organises around the work with little PM intervention.
- **4**: The team coordinates almost wordlessly across time zones, picking up and handing off work fluidly and consistently hitting its goals.

## Matches

- **A**: Forming
- **B**: Storming
- **C**: Norming
- **D**: Performing

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

The polite, dependent, role-unsure behaviour of early remote contact is classic forming (1-A), where members orient and look to the leader. Open conflict over tools and ways of working, amplified by remote side channels, is storming (2-B), as differences surface and positions harden. Settled norms, candid feedback and self-organisation mark norming (3-C), where the team has agreed how it works and trust is established. Fluid, near-wordless cross-zone coordination with consistent results is performing (4-D), the mature stage of high autonomy and effectiveness. Reading these signals correctly matters because each stage needs different support — direction and clarity in forming, conflict facilitation in storming, reinforcement of norms in norming, and largely getting out of the way in performing.
