# Question

The PM notices that one team member, based eight hours behind the rest of the team, has been attending every recurring meeting after midnight local time for the last three months. They haven't complained but look visibly tired and have started declining occasional invites. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Praise the team member's commitment publicly so the rest of the team appreciates the contribution they're making.
- **B**: Tell them they don't have to attend every recurring meeting and rely on the meeting notes to stay informed.
- **C**: Leave the situation alone unless and until they explicitly raise it as a problem with the PM.
- **D**: Rotate the meeting times across time zones so the load is shared, and check in privately on their wellbeing.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's people-first principle expects the PM to act on visible wellbeing signals and to share burdens fairly across a virtual team. (D) addresses both the structural unfairness and the individual. (A) reinforces unsustainable behaviour and frames it as exemplary, which is the opposite of what PMI advocates. (B) excludes the person from real-time discussion as a workaround instead of fixing the time-zone burden. (C) waits for a complaint that may never come and accepts a known wellbeing risk.
