# Question

A virtual team with members in two offices — roughly 10 in each — has drifted into an "us vs them" dynamic. Each office socialises internally, each blames the other for delays, and the PM has started hearing phrases like "that's a Dublin problem" and "that's a Berlin problem." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pair people across offices for work, mix offices in social events, and set shared objectives that require cross-location collaboration to deliver.
- **B**: Centralise decision authority with the PM so neither office feels ownership is slanted to the other.
- **C**: Identify respected individuals in each office and make them the liaison point for all cross-location work.
- **D**: Run a workshop to address the relationship issues directly and have each office commit to specific behaviour changes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cross-group tribalism is reduced most effectively by increasing positive shared work and shared outcomes, not by talking about the relationship in the abstract. (A) does this structurally and durably. (B) makes the PM the bottleneck and doesn't change how the offices feel about each other. (C) routes contact through liaisons and keeps the two offices at arm's length, preserving the silos. (D) addresses the symptom openly but rarely sticks without the structural collaboration that makes new behaviour natural — a conversation without changed working patterns fades quickly.
