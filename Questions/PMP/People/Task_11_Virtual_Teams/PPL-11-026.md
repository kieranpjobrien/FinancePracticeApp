# Question

The PM is preparing the kickoff meeting for a fully virtual team whose members have never worked together. The PM has 90 minutes. What is the MOST effective use of the first part of that time?

## Options

- **A**: Walk straight through the project objectives, scope, and timeline so the team knows the work facing them.
- **B**: Run personal introductions that include something non-work, then a short interactive activity, before moving into project content.
- **C**: Email biographies in advance and use the meeting time entirely on project content.
- **D**: Skip the introductions in the kickoff and book a separate optional virtual social call later in the week.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The kickoff is the moment of highest collective attention; spending some of it on light human contact pays back across the rest of the project. (B) does that and still leaves time for the project content. (A) optimises for content and leaves the team as a set of strangers working on a shared deliverable. (C) treats relationship-building as out-of-band homework that few will do. (D) splits the social and the work into separate optional events, which usually means the social one is poorly attended.
