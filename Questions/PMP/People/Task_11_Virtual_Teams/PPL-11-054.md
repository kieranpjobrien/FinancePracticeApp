# Question

Two senior engineers on a remote team have been trading increasingly terse messages in the project channel over a design disagreement. Others have noticed and are going quiet in the thread. The PM reviewed the messages and sees genuine miscommunication rather than personal hostility. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reply in the channel clarifying the miscommunication, restating both positions fairly so the thread is corrected for the watching audience.
- **B**: Message both engineers privately and get them onto a short video call together to work the design disagreement through face-to-face.
- **C**: Escalate to both engineers' line managers and request they remind each party about professional conduct in shared project spaces.
- **D**: Leave the thread alone because technical experts are entitled to disagree robustly and the PM should not interfere in engineering debate.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Text amplifies tone loss — the PMI-aligned move is to shift the disagreement to a higher-bandwidth medium and resolve it privately before more harm is done publicly. (A) tries to correct the thread but keeps the discussion in the channel that is fuelling the problem. (C) is premature escalation and damages trust. (D) conflates a technical disagreement with its corrosive delivery mechanism.
