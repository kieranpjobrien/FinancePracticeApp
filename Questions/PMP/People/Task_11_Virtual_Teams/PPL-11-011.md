# Question

A virtual team has strong individual performers but weak collaboration. People deliver their own work but don't help each other, don't share knowledge, and avoid taking on cross-cutting items. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add collaboration as a formal performance metric so contributions to others' work are visible and rewarded.
- **B**: Assign every piece of work to a pair to force people to collaborate through the mechanics of the assignment.
- **C**: Establish structured collaboration practices and introduce team objectives alongside individual ones.
- **D**: Treat it as culture and accept that a virtual team of strong individuals may collaborate less than a co-located one.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Virtual collaboration has to be designed in through practices that create regular shared work moments, paired with shared accountability. (C) does this without pushing into artificial compliance. (A) makes the metric the target and produces performative collaboration rather than the real thing. (B) overcorrects by removing individual autonomy on every item, which will be resisted and damages throughput. (D) explains away a problem PMI expects the PM to solve.
