# Question

On a distributed banking project, the project manager observes that members from one region speak up freely and challenge ideas in calls, while equally senior members from another region stay silent and defer, even when the PM senses they disagree. Important risks may be going unvoiced. What is the most effective action?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce structured techniques such as round-robin input and anonymous written contributions before calls, so quieter members can raise concerns through a safe channel.
- **B**: Directly ask the silent members for their views during the call, naming each in turn so their perspective is heard on the spot.
- **C**: Brief the more vocal members privately to leave more space in discussion so the quieter region has room to contribute.
- **D**: Accept that contribution styles differ across cultures and rely on one-to-one conversations to gather concerns from the quieter members.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Differences in how readily people speak up are often cultural and about safety, not lack of opinion; structured formats such as round-robin and pre-call written input give every member a low-risk route to surface concerns and equalise voice without singling anyone out. (B) puts reserved members on the spot publicly, which can heighten discomfort and produce a deferential non-answer. (C) helps create space but does not give quieter members a comfortable mechanism actually to contribute. (D) gathers some input but accepts an unequal forum and keeps risks out of the shared conversation where the team needs them.
