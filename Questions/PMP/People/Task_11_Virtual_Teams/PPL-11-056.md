# Question

In virtual sprint reviews, one capable team member never speaks unless addressed directly. The PM has noticed their written contributions in docs are consistently insightful. Other members have started skipping over them to keep sessions moving. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Call on them by name during each review so they are visibly prompted to contribute and their expertise is drawn out for the team.
- **B**: Leave the pattern alone because written contribution is a legitimate style and forcing verbal input in meetings would undermine psychological safety.
- **C**: Explore their preferences privately, then adapt the review format so written input is surfaced alongside verbal discussion for the whole team.
- **D**: Suggest they take the facilitator role at the next review so they have a structural reason to speak and the team hears their voice.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's inclusion lens is to adapt the process to surface every member's contribution — tailoring the review format honours their style while stopping the team from skipping their insights. (A) forces public verbal performance that may not match their preferred mode. (B) accepts the team skipping over them, which is the actual harm. (D) assigns a role they may not want and assumes the issue is opportunity rather than format.
