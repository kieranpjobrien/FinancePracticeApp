# Question

On a fully remote agile team, a project manager realises that good work is no longer seen the way it was when the team was co-located, where contributions were witnessed in passing and acknowledged on the spot by peers. A couple of members have hinted their efforts go unnoticed. The PM wants the recognition the team is missing to feel timely, genuine and visible to the whole team. Which approach best fits that intent?

## Options

- **A**: Create a lightweight shared "kudos" channel and a standing moment in the review where peers, not only the PM, call out specific contributions as they happen.
- **B**: Make a point of personally and specifically thanking members in the team channel whenever they do strong work, so the recognition is public and tied to real examples.
- **C**: Ask team leads to surface standout contributions to the PM regularly so the PM can acknowledge them with the right context and to the right audience.
- **D**: Build a recognition round into the retrospective where the team reflects on who helped them that sprint and thanks each other.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

What the team lost is specifically peer-to-peer, in-the-moment, visible acknowledgement; a kudos channel plus a peer moment in the review restores exactly that — recognition that is timely, from teammates, and seen by all — best matching the stated intent. (B) is public and specific but routes all recognition through the PM, recreating a top-down channel rather than the peer recognition that was lost. (C) is even more PM-centric and adds latency through leads. (D) is genuine peer recognition but only once per sprint, so it is not the timely, in-the-moment acknowledgement the scenario calls for. A combines peer ownership, immediacy and team-wide visibility, where the others each drop one of those.
