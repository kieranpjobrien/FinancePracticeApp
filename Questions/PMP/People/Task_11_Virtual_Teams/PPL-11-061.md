# Question

A distributed agile team building a logistics platform spans Sydney, Bengaluru and Lisbon. The team has chosen to run a daily stand-up, but the only slot where all three locations are awake and inside reasonable working hours is a narrow forty-minute window each day. New team members keep asking how the team handles work that surfaces outside that window. What should the PM encourage the team to do?

## Options

- **A**: Use the single daily overlap for the stand-up and agree async conventions — a written update in the team channel — for anything that arises outside it.
- **B**: Run three separate regional stand-ups so each location meets at a convenient local time, and have the scrum master reconcile them afterwards.
- **C**: Rotate the stand-up time weekly so the inconvenience of an awkward slot is shared evenly across all three regions over the month.
- **D**: Drop the synchronous stand-up entirely in favour of a purely written daily thread, since a single shared window is too fragile to rely on.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A distributed team should protect its one genuine overlap for the highest-value synchronous interaction and lean on agreed async conventions for everything else (A) — this is the standard pattern for a single-window team. (B) fragments the team into three sub-teams that never actually meet, recreating the silos the stand-up exists to prevent. (C) trades a workable shared slot for a rotating one that is awkward for someone every week, with no gain when a good common window already exists. (D) discards a real, usable synchronous touchpoint the team values; async should supplement that window, not replace the only daily moment the whole team is together.
