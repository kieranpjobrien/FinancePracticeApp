# Question

A remote agile team has slipped into an "always on" pattern — messages fly late into the evening and some members respond instantly at weekends. The PM themselves has contributed to it. Two team members have privately mentioned feeling unable to switch off. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team in the next retrospective whether always-on working is a problem, and let them decide any norm changes they would like to adopt.
- **B**: Introduce a formal company-wide policy through HR that bars any work-related messaging outside core business hours for all project teams.
- **C**: Send a firm note to the team asking everyone to refrain from messaging after hours, while continuing to reply to urgent issues whenever they arise.
- **D**: Co-create explicit working-hours norms with the team, model them personally, and use scheduled-send so the PM's own patterns reinforce the agreement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's sustainable-pace principle expects the PM to model the boundary and build it into team norms — co-creation secures buy-in and scheduled-send removes the "the PM emails me at 10pm" signal. (A) leaves the norm vague and does not address the PM's own role. (B) routes a local problem through global policy. (C) sets a rule the PM visibly breaks, which teams rightly discount.
