# Question

A remote Scrum team in three time zones holds a live daily stand-up at a time that suits two of the three zones; the third zone always joins very early or very late and increasingly feels the daily call is not worth the inconvenience. Velocity is steady, and the team's main use of the stand-up is surfacing blockers — full progress is already visible on the board. The Scrum Master wants every member to engage with the daily sync within their own working day without losing the team's ability to catch and unblock issues fast. What should the project manager propose?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the daily live stand-up but rotate its time each sprint so the burden of the awkward slot is shared evenly across all three zones rather than always falling on one.
- **B**: Replace the daily call with a pure asynchronous written stand-up in the shared channel, and rely on the next post or the board for anyone whose blocker needs follow-up.
- **C**: Move to an asynchronous stand-up where each member posts progress, plans and blockers in a shared channel within their own working hours, with a standing agreement that any blocker needing discussion triggers a quick live huddle among just those involved.
- **D**: Keep the live stand-up at its current time but make it optional for the disadvantaged zone, who instead read the notes and add anything of their own to the channel afterwards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The team's stand-up exists mainly to surface and clear blockers fast, and the goal is engagement within everyone's own hours without losing that. An async stand-up that captures the daily update plus an explicit trigger for a small live huddle when a blocker needs discussion (C) preserves the fast-unblock loop while freeing everyone from a fixed live time. (B) goes async too but drops the live element entirely, so a blocker that genuinely needs real-time discussion waits for the next cycle — losing exactly the rapid resolution the team relies on. (A) keeps everyone on a live call and merely rotates whose day is disrupted; it shares the pain rather than removing it, and still forces members to sync outside their hours. (D) lets the third zone off the call but cuts them out of the daily blocker exchange, eroding the cohesion and shared awareness the stand-up is meant to create. Preserve the ceremony's purpose while making it fit every time zone.
