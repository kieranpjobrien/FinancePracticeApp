# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of recording virtual team meetings?

## Options

- **A**: It provides an authoritative record that can be used in disputes about who said what during a discussion.
- **B**: It allows team members in other time zones, or those who couldn't attend live, to catch up asynchronously.
- **C**: It supplies evidence for project audits and post-implementation reviews carried out by external parties.
- **D**: It makes it easier for the project manager to monitor each team member's level of meeting participation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The point of recording virtual meetings is inclusion across time zones and life — anyone who can't be live can still pick up the discussion and decisions. (A) frames the record as defensive evidence, which corrodes trust and isn't the everyday purpose. (C) is a possible secondary use but not the primary reason teams record. (D) reframes recording as surveillance, which undermines the psychological safety that virtual teams especially need.
