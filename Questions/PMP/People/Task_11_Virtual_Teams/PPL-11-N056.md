# Question

A new contractor is joining a fully remote agile team for a six-month engagement. The project manager wants the contractor productive and connected quickly, having seen previous remote joiners flounder for weeks. Which actions should the PM take to support an effective remote onboarding? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Assign an onboarding buddy from the team with protected time to answer questions and pair on early tasks.
- **B**: Point the contractor at a comprehensive self-paced onboarding portal and the team's written docs so they can ramp up independently without drawing on busy teammates' time.
- **C**: Ensure access to tools, repositories and the team's information radiators is provisioned before day one so the contractor is not blocked waiting for access.
- **D**: Schedule the contractor's first live introduction to the team for the end of the first sprint, once they have something to show.
- **E**: Fill the contractor's first week with back-to-back introductory video calls with every team member so they meet everyone in the team straight away.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

A buddy with protected time gives a remote joiner the "someone to ask" that prevents floundering (A), and provisioning access before day one removes the dead time and frustration of waiting to be unblocked (C). (B) sounds efficient and protects the team's time, but self-paced docs alone leave a remote joiner isolated with no one to resolve the questions that documentation never quite answers — it is the slower-floundering path the PM has seen before. (D) delays the relationship-building that remote joiners most need early. (E) front-loads connection but a week of back-to-back intro calls is overwhelming and video-draining, builds names-and-faces without the working contact that actually integrates someone, and keeps the contractor from starting real work; a buddy gives that connection in the flow of the work instead.
