# Question

A global virtual team works in English but several members are non-native speakers. In fast-paced video meetings, those members contribute far less and the team's decisions are visibly skewed toward native-English voices. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Slow the meeting pace and build in structured turn-taking, written-chat parallel input and summary recaps to level the playing field.
- **B**: Offer intensive English-language coaching to the non-native speakers so they can keep pace with the fast spoken discussions over time.
- **C**: Hold a separate pre-meeting with the non-native speakers so their input can be captured privately and represented by the PM during the main session.
- **D**: Accept that language fluency is a legitimate factor in meeting dynamics and document decisions thoroughly so absent voices can still react afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's inclusion approach adapts the meeting format so every voice can contribute — structured turn-taking and written chat remove the fluency penalty. (B) makes the member adapt to a format that is itself the problem. (C) excludes them from the real decision room and uses a proxy. (D) accepts exclusion as a given and papers over it with documentation.
