# Question

Engagement survey results show a remote team is suffering from "meeting fatigue" — their calendars are full, deep work is squeezed into evenings, and two members have mentioned burnout. The PM's own diary shows eighteen standing meetings per week touch the team. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel every recurring meeting for two weeks and reinstate only those that people actively miss once the experiment ends.
- **B**: Audit each recurring meeting with the team to confirm its purpose, attendees and value, then cut, shorten or async-ify where justified.
- **C**: Reduce all standing meetings to half their current length while keeping existing attendee lists and cadences unchanged across the portfolio.
- **D**: Enforce a company-wide no-meeting day such as Wednesday, keeping the rest of the week's meeting load exactly as it is today.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI favours purposeful meeting design: a collaborative audit against purpose and value addresses fatigue at the root and preserves meetings that genuinely matter. (A) throws away valuable cadence and creates chaos. (C) treats the symptom without questioning whether meetings are needed at all. (D) protects a single day but leaves the underlying overload intact.
