# Question

On a remote product team, decisions made in the small live overlap window keep being relitigated by members who were asleep when they were taken. This causes rework and quiet resentment. The team uses a chat tool, a wiki and weekly video calls. What approach should the project manager establish?

## Options

- **A**: Move the daily decision-making sync to a time that captures all three zones and ask everyone to attend, so decisions are made with the whole team present.
- **B**: Have the product owner, as the accountable decision-maker, settle contested calls during the overlap window and publish the call, so decisions are owned and the team can move on quickly.
- **C**: Adopt a lightweight decision record practice: significant decisions are written up with context and options, given a short asynchronous window for objections, then treated as final.
- **D**: Batch significant decisions to the weekly video call when the whole team is already together, so each is debated and agreed in real time with everyone present.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The root problem is that decisions are taken in a window most of the team is absent from, with no inclusive route to weigh in before they harden — which is exactly what a written decision record with a defined async objection window fixes: absent members get a real chance to contribute, and the team gains a durable, referenceable agreement that removes the basis for relitigating. (A) restores presence but only by pulling people into a sync outside their working hours, trading relitigation for the out-of-hours burden and disengagement the PM wants to avoid. (B) is a legitimate adaptive practice — a single accountable owner is fast and decisive — but speed is not the failure here; routing calls through the PO still shapes them in the overlap window and leaves absent members reacting after the fact, so the resentment persists. (D) keeps everyone synchronous and is genuinely inclusive for those who can attend, but it throttles the team to a weekly decision cadence and crams every call into one debate, which neither scales nor helps members who cannot make that slot. The discriminator is which approach gives absent members input before a decision is final without forcing anyone out of their hours.
