# Question

A virtual team has a recurring weekly decision meeting that genuinely needs everyone live. The only window that catches all four regions falls early-morning for one region and late-evening for another; the two middle regions are fine. The disadvantaged regions have started to resent always drawing the bad slot, and one member there is increasingly disengaged from the meeting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate the meeting time on a published schedule so the inconvenient slots move around and no single region always bears the unsociable hour.
- **B**: Keep the fixed time because it is the only window that works for everyone, and ask the affected members to accept it as an unavoidable cost of a global team.
- **C**: Record the meeting so the disadvantaged members can opt out and catch up asynchronously whenever the time is too inconvenient for them.
- **D**: Split the meeting into two regional sessions at locally convenient times and have the PM carry decisions and context between them.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When a meeting must be synchronous and no slot is convenient for all, fairness comes from sharing the burden, so a rotating, published schedule spreads the unsociable hours across regions instead of fixing them on the same two (A). (B) is "efficient" but entrenches a standing inequity that is already breeding resentment and disengagement, which is a leadership failure even if the logistics are tidy. (C) lets people drop out of a meeting the scenario says genuinely needs everyone present, so decisions get made without the absent voices — the opposite of engaging them. (D) fragments a meeting that needs the whole team together and turns the PM into a lossy relay between two half-conversations.
