# Question

A newly formed fully remote team of twelve has never worked together and has a tight deadline. The PM has one day of kickoff time before delivery work must begin. The sponsor wants most of that day spent on detailed project planning. What is the BEST use of the kickoff day?

## Options

- **A**: Run a full day of detailed planning as the sponsor requested, since the deadline pressure means the team cannot afford any non-delivery time.
- **B**: Split the day roughly between relationship and context building, shared working agreements, and the level of planning needed to start meaningfully.
- **C**: Spend the whole day on team-building exercises and get-to-know-you activities so trust is in place before any real delivery work begins.
- **D**: Skip the live kickoff, circulate detailed plans in writing, and start delivery immediately, using the early sprints themselves to build team relationships.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats virtual-team kickoff as a deliberate investment in both people and process — a balanced day builds the relationships, agreements and shared context that remote teams depend on, while still starting planning. (A) optimises for planning at the cost of the trust remote delivery will require. (C) under-invests in the shared context the team needs to start. (D) skips the single best opportunity to form the team.
