# Question

A team member's communication style is short, factual, and direct. Several other team members from cultures that value warmer, more relational communication have started saying privately that they find this team member rude. The team member is unaware of the impact. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team member to soften their tone in writing so the rest of the team feels more comfortable working with them.
- **B**: Explain to the offended team members that this is a cultural and personality difference they need to look past.
- **C**: Coach the individual on how their style is landing across cultures, and at the same time help the team interpret different styles charitably.
- **D**: Reassign the team member to work that requires less collaboration with the more relational members.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's emphasis on emotional intelligence and inclusion expects work on both sides — the individual develops awareness, and the team builds tolerance for difference. (C) does both. (A) places all the burden on one person and treats their style as wrong. (B) places all the burden on the team and gives the individual no chance to grow. (D) routes around the relationship problem by reducing collaboration, which sacrifices both development and team cohesion.
