# Question

A virtual team has strong synchronous meetings but members in two time zones are frustrated that most decisions are effectively settled before their day begins. The PM is committed to inclusive decision-making but cannot keep adding meetings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Shift most decisions to a documented asynchronous flow with a fixed decision window that every time zone can meaningfully contribute within.
- **B**: Reserve all significant decisions for the one overlap window each day and hold them back until every time zone can be present live.
- **C**: Rotate who acts as the decision-making delegate each week so each time zone has a turn at carrying their region's interests forward.
- **D**: Continue current practice and publish decision logs promptly so members who were absent understand the rationale and context quickly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For distributed teams, a written async-first decision process with an explicit window gives every time zone genuine voice without meeting bloat — this is the PMI-aligned tailoring move. (B) squeezes all decisions into one slot and slows the project down. (C) relies on delegates speaking for others, which erodes ownership. (D) addresses transparency, not inclusion in the decision itself.
