# Question

A company-wide mandate requires all teams to return to the office three days a week. The PM's distributed team includes two members recruited on fully remote terms who live over 400km from the nearest office. Both are strong performers considering resignation. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Comply fully with the mandate and support the two members through the transition, accepting the risk of losing them as a cost of policy alignment.
- **B**: Quietly exempt both members from the mandate without formal approval and hope the policy's enforcement focus sits elsewhere in the organisation.
- **C**: Surface the specific case to HR and senior leadership with the business impact, and formally request a documented exception for the two members.
- **D**: Let the two members make their own choice about compliance or resignation and focus the PM's energy on the rest of the team's transition.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI-aligned PMs engage policy owners with specific business impact and request exceptions through the right channel — this respects governance while advocating for the team. (A) accepts a predictable, material loss without trying to prevent it. (B) breaches organisational policy and exposes the PM to consequences. (D) abdicates an advocacy responsibility the PM holds for their team members.
