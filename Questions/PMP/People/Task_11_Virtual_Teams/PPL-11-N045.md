# Question

During remote sprint retrospectives, two senior engineers dominate the discussion while four junior members rarely speak. The project manager has noticed the quieter members raise good points privately afterwards but stay silent on the call. The team values candid feedback. What is the best way to improve participation?

## Options

- **A**: Use a round-robin in the retrospective where each member in turn, quietest first, gives their input before open discussion, so everyone contributes before the floor opens up.
- **B**: Use an asynchronous or anonymous input method before the call — such as a shared board everyone adds to silently — then discuss the collected themes together.
- **C**: Ask the two senior engineers to hold back and speak last so the quieter members have space to go first.
- **D**: Run a short anonymous pulse survey after each retro to capture what the quiet members did not say live, and fold the results into the next session.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The quieter members already have good points — they raise them privately — so the barrier is the live, judged, dominated floor itself; the fix has to let their input form away from that dynamic. Collecting it asynchronously or anonymously before discussion (B) gives a low-pressure route in, surfaces their themes regardless of who dominates, and then steers the live conversation onto what everyone raised — which suits a remote retro well. (A) is a real facilitation technique and going quietest-first does help, but it still extracts the input live and on the spot, where introverts tend to give thinner answers than they would unpressured, and the seniors still own the discussion that follows. (C) likewise eases the dynamic a little, yet it leaves the same real-time floor in place as the channel for input, so the people it is silencing must still perform to be heard. (D) gathers candid input but only after the fact, so it informs the next retro rather than letting quiet members shape the one they are in. The discriminator is which option lets their contributions form off the dominated floor and still feed the live discussion.
