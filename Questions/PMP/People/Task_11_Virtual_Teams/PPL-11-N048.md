# Question

A project manager is facilitating the team in drafting a working agreement for a newly distributed, cross-time-zone agile team. The team wants the agreement to genuinely improve remote collaboration rather than just list platitudes. Which provisions would most directly support an effective distributed team? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: A defined core-overlap window each day that everyone keeps free for synchronous collaboration, with the rest of the day flexible.
- **B**: Expected response times for asynchronous messages, distinguishing what needs a same-day reply from what can wait.
- **C**: A requirement that all members keep their cameras on for the entire duration of every meeting without exception.
- **D**: Agreed norms for where information lives — which channel is for quick chat, which tool is the single source of truth for decisions and documents.
- **E**: A norm that team-channel messages are acknowledged promptly during the working day so no one is left wondering whether a message was seen.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Effective distributed working agreements protect a shared synchronous window (A), set explicit async expectations so people are not anxious about silence or chained to notifications (B), and define where information lives so the team can find current state (D). (C) is too rigid — a blanket always-on-camera rule causes fatigue and ignores bandwidth, privacy and accessibility; "default on for discussions" is more humane. (E) sounds considerate but is the trap: requiring prompt acknowledgement of messages during the working day pushes a cross-time-zone team toward constant, notification-driven attention and undercuts the very async expectations (B) is designed to set — across zones, "promptly" is unachievable without someone working out of hours. The tiered response-time norm in (B) handles the "was it seen?" worry properly by saying explicitly what needs a fast reply and what can wait.
