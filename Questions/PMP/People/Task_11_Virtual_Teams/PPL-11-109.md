# Question

Three sprints into a fully remote agile delivery, the project manager wants an early read on whether the distributed team is genuinely engaged or quietly drifting, rather than waiting for velocity to dip. Which approach gives the most reliable signal of virtual team engagement?

## Options

- **A**: Combine observation of participation in ceremonies and collaboration patterns with candid retrospective discussion and periodic one-to-one check-ins.
- **B**: Track each member's activity metrics — messages sent, tickets updated, hours of presence online — and review the trends each sprint.
- **C**: Run an anonymous quarterly engagement survey across the organisation and read this team's results against the company benchmark.
- **D**: Rely on the sprint velocity and burndown charts, since a truly disengaged team will show up as declining throughput before long.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Engagement on a virtual team is best read by triangulating qualitative signals — how people actually participate and collaborate, what surfaces in retrospectives, and what one-to-ones reveal — which catches drift early and in context. (B) measures activity, not engagement; busy logging can mask disengagement and quiet high performers can look idle, so it misleads. (C) is too infrequent and too coarse to guide this team now, and an org benchmark hides team-specific issues. (D) is a lagging indicator by the PM's own framing — waiting for velocity to fall is exactly what they are trying to pre-empt.
