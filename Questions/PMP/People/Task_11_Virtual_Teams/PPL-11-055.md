# Question

A hybrid team includes four employees and three contractors from an offshore vendor. The contractors are being excluded from informal virtual chat, social calls and architecture discussions. Their technical work is suffering from missing context. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the arrangement as standard procurement practice and work around the context gap with thorough written specifications, detailed design notes and formal written handovers.
- **B**: Fold contractors into all team forums including social channels, architecture sessions and retrospectives, subject to any specific information restrictions their contract actually imposes.
- **C**: Create a parallel contractor-only channel where the PM briefs the vendor members separately each week on whatever context they may have missed in the wider team.
- **D**: Escalate to procurement and ask them to renegotiate the vendor arrangement so contractors can be treated as full employees for day-to-day integration purposes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats the team as one team regardless of employment type — contractors need the context to do the work, and exclusion usually comes from habit, not contract. (A) accepts a dysfunction that is costing delivery quality. (C) preserves the silo and doubles the PM's communication load. (D) is the wrong tool; integration does not require an employment change, just inclusion in the team's working forums.
