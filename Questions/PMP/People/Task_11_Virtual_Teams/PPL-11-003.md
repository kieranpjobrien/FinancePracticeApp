# Question

A virtual team member who used to be highly engaged has gone quiet in the last month — camera always off, minimal participation, slow chat replies. Their work output is still acceptable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a team-wide "cameras on" expectation in the next meeting to bring everyone back into visible contact.
- **B**: Reach out privately, acknowledge the change, and ask how they are and what support might help.
- **C**: Leave the situation alone since their delivery hasn't dropped, and revisit only if quality slips.
- **D**: Assign them a higher-visibility piece of work to draw them back into the team's daily flow.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A noticeable change in a team member's engagement is a signal worth investigating, and PMI's servant-leadership stance is to engage privately and supportively. (B) does that. (A) imposes a public norm to address what is plausibly a private issue and may worsen whatever is going on. (C) ignores a clear signal until it becomes a delivery problem, by which point the human and project costs are higher. (D) increases pressure on someone who may be struggling, without first understanding why.
