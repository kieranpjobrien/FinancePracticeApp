# Question

A new business analyst joins a fully remote insurance modernisation project on a predictive plan. After two weeks she tells the project manager she still feels unsure who to approach for what, has not spoken to most of the team, and is hesitant to ask questions in the busy group channel. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pair her with an experienced team member as a buddy and set up short one-to-one introductions with the people she will work with most.
- **B**: Reassure her that the uncertainty is normal for remote starters and that she will naturally find her feet within another few weeks.
- **C**: Ask her to post her open questions in the group channel so the whole team can see them and anyone available can respond.
- **D**: Forward her the project's stakeholder register and team directory so she can identify the right contacts for each topic herself.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

On a virtual team, deliberate connection is the PM's job — a buddy plus targeted introductions builds the relationships and psychological safety a remote starter lacks, directly addressing both the "who do I ask" and the reluctance to speak up. (B) leaves a disengaging team member to drift and risks a poor early experience. (C) ignores that she is already uncomfortable posting in the busy channel, so it will not surface her questions. (D) gives names but not relationships, and a register alone will not make her feel safe to reach out.
