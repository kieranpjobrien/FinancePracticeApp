# Question

A hybrid product team spanning four time zones is suffering from meeting fatigue: members complain they spend their overlap window in back-to-back video calls and have no focused time, yet attendance is dropping and decisions still stall. The project manager wants to protect the team's energy without losing alignment. Which actions best address this? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Default to asynchronous decision-making — using written proposals with a clear comment-and-decide deadline — and reserve synchronous time only for discussions that genuinely need it.
- **B**: Shorten every recurring meeting from sixty to thirty minutes so the same cadence fits more easily into the overlap window.
- **C**: Record all meetings and share notes so anyone who skips a call can catch up later without attending live.
- **D**: Audit the recurring meetings with the team and cancel or merge those that no longer earn their place.
- **E**: Make the daily synchronous sync mandatory again to stop the drop in attendance from fragmenting alignment.

## Answer

A, D

## Explanation

The cause is too much synchronous load on a narrow overlap; shifting decisions to well-structured asynchronous channels (A) frees the window and actually helps decisions progress, while auditing and pruning the meeting set with the team (D) removes the low-value calls driving the fatigue. (B) keeps every meeting and the same fragmented day, just compressed, so it does not relieve the load. (C) treats recordings as a substitute for fixing the schedule and quietly accepts disengagement. (E) forces attendance at the very synchronous load people are fleeing, attacking the symptom and worsening the cause.
