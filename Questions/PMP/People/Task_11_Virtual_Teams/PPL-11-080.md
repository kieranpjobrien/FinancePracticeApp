# Question

A PM is leading a hybrid programme where part of the team is co-located and part is fully remote. The PM wants every working practice to be deliberately inclusive of remote members rather than treating them as an afterthought to an office-centred team. Which practices most directly support an equitable hybrid team? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: Default important discussions and decisions to channels and documents accessible to all, so co-located members do not settle things informally where remote members cannot take part.
- **B**: Run meetings so that remote and in-office members have an equivalent experience — shared agendas, equal speaking access, and decisions made in the meeting, not in the corridor afterwards.
- **C**: Distribute high-visibility, career-enhancing work and recognition with conscious attention to balance, so physical presence does not quietly predict who advances.
- **D**: Keep the most strategically important roles with co-located members who can be in the room for fast decisions, and assign remote members the more self-contained execution work.
- **E**: Hold the richest informal relationship-building among the in-office group and let remote members connect mainly through formal scheduled calls, since spontaneous contact is harder for them anyway.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Equitable hybrid working means presence must not confer advantage: defaulting decisions to accessible channels stops the team settling things informally where remote members cannot participate (A); designing meetings for an equivalent experience prevents a two-tier room-versus-screen dynamic (B); and consciously balancing visible work and recognition counteracts the proximity bias that otherwise lets location predict advancement (C). (D) bakes in exactly the inequity to be avoided, reserving strategic, high-growth work for the co-located group and relegating remote members to execution. (E) accepts and reinforces the relational exclusion of remote members rather than working to close it, leaving them with thinner connection and a weaker informal network — the opposite of deliberate inclusion.
