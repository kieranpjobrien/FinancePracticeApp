# Question

A predictive project depends on an offshore vendor team who attend meetings but say little, agree to everything, and later deliver work that misses the intent. The vendor PM brushes off concerns as "cultural communication style." Delivery risk is rising. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the cultural explanation from the vendor PM and compensate by writing much more detailed specifications for every work package issued to them.
- **B**: Escalate the pattern to the vendor's senior management and request replacement of the vendor PM with someone who communicates more directly.
- **C**: Continue the current cadence and hold the vendor contractually responsible for missed intent, using the contract mechanisms to recover on any rework.
- **D**: Co-create a shared working model with the vendor — jointly authored acceptance criteria, walk-throughs before build, and regular checkpoint demos.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's integration lens treats vendor teams as part of the one team — jointly authored acceptance criteria and early walkthroughs surface misalignment before it becomes rework. (A) accepts a label in place of a solution and adds documentation burden. (B) escalates without first addressing the working model the PMs control. (C) optimises for recovery after failure rather than preventing it.
