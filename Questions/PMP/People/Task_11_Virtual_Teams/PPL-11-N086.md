# Question

On a virtual programme, six specialists are seconded part-time from different functional departments and continue to report to their own line managers, who set their priorities and appraisals. The specialists treat the programme's work as secondary, slipping on commitments whenever their home department calls. The project manager has no authority over their appraisals. What is the most effective action?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate each missed commitment to the relevant line manager so they can direct the specialist back onto the programme's work.
- **B**: Build extra buffer into the programme schedule to absorb the specialists' unpredictable availability so their slippage stops affecting milestones.
- **C**: Reach an explicit shared agreement with the line managers and the specialists on the programme's claim on each person's time and how their programme contribution will be recognised in their home appraisal.
- **D**: Request that the specialists be reassigned to the programme full-time so they report through the programme and prioritise its work.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In a matrixed virtual team the specialists rationally serve whoever owns their priorities and appraisal — currently the line managers — so commitments to the programme keep losing. The durable fix aligns the incentives: a negotiated agreement that defines the programme's claim on their time and feeds their programme contribution into the appraisal their line manager controls gives them a real reason to honour it (C). Escalating each slip (A) treats symptoms reactively, casts the PM as a complainer, and depends on line managers re-deciding every time. Adding buffer (B) hides the problem in the schedule and lets the under-commitment continue unaddressed, eventually overwhelming the buffer. Demanding full-time reassignment (D) may be neither feasible nor warranted for part-time specialists and overreaches; it also bypasses the cheaper path of realigning incentives. The discriminator is whether the action changes what the specialists are rewarded for or merely chases the consequences of the existing misalignment.
