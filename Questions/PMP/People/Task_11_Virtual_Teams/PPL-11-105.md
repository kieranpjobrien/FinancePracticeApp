# Question

A project manager leading several distributed teams sees different engagement problems on each. For each situation below, match the most appropriate first response the project manager should take.

## Items

- **1**: A brand-new virtual team has been assembled from five offices; members are polite but cautious and unsure how decisions will be made.
- **2**: An established remote team is delivering well but members say they feel isolated and never interact outside formal work tasks.
- **3**: A team member in a far time zone is repeatedly left out of decisions made during the other members' overlapping working hours.
- **4**: A remote contributor consistently does excellent work but is being overlooked for visible assignments by an office-based sponsor.

## Matches

- **A**: Facilitate the team in agreeing a working agreement covering how they will decide, communicate and collaborate.
- **B**: Create lightweight social connection opportunities such as informal virtual coffees or a non-work channel.
- **C**: Move some decision points to asynchronous channels and shift meeting times so the isolated member can take part.
- **D**: Make the contributor's results visible to the sponsor and advocate for fair access to high-profile work.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

A forming virtual team needs explicit rules of engagement, so a working agreement comes first (1-A). A high-performing but isolated team needs deliberate social connection that remote work strips away (2-B). A member excluded by time-zone overlap needs the decision process itself made asynchronous and inclusive, not just an invitation (3-C). A remote contributor disadvantaged by distance bias needs the PM to surface their results and champion equitable assignment (4-D). Each match pairs the symptom with the intervention that addresses its actual cause rather than a generic "communicate more."
