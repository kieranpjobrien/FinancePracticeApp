# Question

A remote agile team replaced its live daily stand-up with a written async one because of time-zone spread. Months on, the written updates have decayed into vague one-liners ("still working on the API"), blockers surface late, and members admit they barely read each other's posts. The team does not want to return to a daily synchronous call. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reinstate a short daily synchronous stand-up at the team's overlap window, accepting that some members will take it at an awkward hour, because async clearly is not working.
- **B**: Facilitate the team to agree a lightweight structure for the written update — progress toward the sprint goal, today's plan, blockers — and a norm that named blockers get a same-day response.
- **C**: Ask each member to write a more detailed update covering everything they did, so colleagues have the full picture and stop missing things.
- **D**: Have a tool automatically pull each member's completed tasks from the board into the channel so the update reflects real activity without anyone writing it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Async stand-up failed in execution, not in concept: the updates lack a purpose-built structure and nobody acts on what is posted, so the format degraded. Giving the written update a tight, sprint-goal-focused structure and an explicit norm that blockers draw a same-day response restores its two jobs — coordination and unblocking — while preserving async (B). Returning to a daily synchronous call (A) abandons a format that can work and reimposes the time-zone burden the team adopted async to avoid. Demanding longer, exhaustive updates (C) makes the posts heavier to write and read, worsening the very disengagement that produced one-liners. Auto-pulling completed tasks (D) reports activity but cannot capture intent, plan or — critically — blockers, which is precisely what is surfacing late. The best first step fixes the update's structure and the response norm rather than scrapping async or piling on volume.
