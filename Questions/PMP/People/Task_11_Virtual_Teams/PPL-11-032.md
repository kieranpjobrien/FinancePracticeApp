# Question

A previously co-located team is being moved to permanent remote work. Half the team is excited; the other half is openly resistant, and the PM is seeing productivity dip and morale drop in the resistant group. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run the transition as decided and address performance issues with the resistant members through normal performance management.
- **B**: Acknowledge the resistant members' concerns, co-create new ways of working with the whole team, and provide training and support for virtual collaboration.
- **C**: Concentrate the PM's time on the resistant members so they get the help they need to adapt to remote work.
- **D**: Offer the resistant members the option to move into other co-located roles in the organisation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to lead through change with empathy and inclusion: surface concerns, co-design the new operating model, and equip people with the skills they need. (B) does this. (A) treats a change-management problem as a discipline problem and will deepen the morale issue. (C) over-invests in one group at the expense of the team-wide work that should accompany a transition. (D) is a last-resort option offered before the PM has made a serious attempt to support the team through the change.
