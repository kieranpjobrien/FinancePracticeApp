# Question

A distributed Scrum team of nine across four countries uses three overlapping chat tools because each sub-group prefers their own. Cross-thread context is routinely lost and two incidents have already traced back to missed messages. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a short team session to agree on one primary channel and clear rules for when each tool is appropriate.
- **B**: Mandate a single corporate chat platform immediately and disable the others to eliminate any further ambiguity or duplication.
- **C**: Escalate the tooling fragmentation to the PMO and request an enterprise-wide decision that binds this team to one standard tool.
- **D**: Assign one team member to monitor all three channels daily and relay important messages into a shared project log for everyone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leadership and tailoring principles say to involve the team in decisions that affect how they work — a facilitated agreement produces commitment and catches context the PM lacks. (B) is command-and-control and ignores legitimate sub-group preferences. (C) is premature escalation of something the team can resolve locally. (D) treats the symptom by adding coordination overhead instead of fixing the root cause.
