# Question

A sponsor, worried that a newly remote delivery team is not working hard enough, asks the project manager to install activity-tracking software that reports each member's active hours and keystroke counts. The team is currently meeting its sprint commitments and morale is good. The sponsor frames this as non-negotiable visibility. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Roll out the tracking software as directed, then monitor the reports for any drop in effort and raise it with the team if the figures dip.
- **B**: Tell the sponsor you will not install the software and ask them to trust the team's track record of meeting its commitments.
- **C**: Agree to deploy the software but arrange for the reports to come to you alone, and share only a summary with the sponsor.
- **D**: Show the sponsor the delivery evidence the team already produces — completed increments, velocity, burndown — and propose these outcome measures as the visibility mechanism instead of activity surveillance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The sponsor's real need is confidence that the team is delivering; activity metrics are a poor proxy for that, whereas the increments, velocity and burndown the team already produces measure the thing the sponsor actually cares about. Leading with that evidence reframes the conversation from monitoring effort to demonstrating results and usually satisfies the underlying concern (D). Complying and policing the figures (A) imposes surveillance that is known to erode the trust and autonomy a remote team runs on, in service of a metric that does not even show value delivered. Refusing and simply appealing to the team's record (B) names a defensible position but skips the step of meeting the sponsor's legitimate concern with something concrete, and a flat refusal to a sponsor who has framed this as non-negotiable is likely to escalate rather than resolve. Deploying it but routing the reports through yourself (C) still installs surveillance, merely controlling who reads it, and risks worse damage if the team later discovers they were being tracked. The best first move surfaces the outcome data that meets the sponsor's need without paying the trust cost.
