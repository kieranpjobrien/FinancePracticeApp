# Question

A PM is starting a complex 18-month project with a fully remote team of 12 people who have never met in person. The budget allows for exactly one in-person gathering during the project. When should the PM schedule it?

## Options

- **A**: Early in the project so the team can build the relationships and shared context that will sustain remote working throughout the engagement.
- **B**: At the midpoint, once the team has something concrete to show and can use the in-person time for deep technical problem-solving together.
- **C**: Near the end of the project so the team can celebrate delivery together and invest in relationships to support any follow-on work.
- **D**: Hold the budget in reserve and schedule the gathering reactively only if the team hits a serious collaboration or trust problem.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Research and PMI's virtual-team guidance consistently show that early in-person contact builds the trust and shared context that make the rest of the remote project work well. (A) puts the investment where it compounds. (B) waits too long to capture the relationship benefit and uses scarce face-to-face time on problem-solving that can typically be done remotely. (C) is pleasant but has almost no effect on the 18 months that preceded it. (D) gambles that the team will function well enough without the relationship base and uses the gathering as a crisis response rather than a compounding investment.
