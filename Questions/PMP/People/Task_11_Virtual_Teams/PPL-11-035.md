# Question

When scheduling recurring meetings for a virtual team that spans multiple time zones, what principle should guide the approach?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate meeting times so the inconvenience of unsociable hours is shared fairly across the team.
- **B**: Lock the meeting time to the project manager's working hours so the PM can prepare effectively.
- **C**: Choose the time most convenient for the largest group and accept that the minority will have to adapt.
- **D**: Eliminate synchronous meetings altogether and run the team purely asynchronously.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first principle and modern virtual-team practice both stress that the burden of unsociable hours should be shared, not loaded onto one location. (A) does that. (B) places the team's convenience second to the PM's, which is the opposite of servant leadership. (C) consistently disadvantages the same minority and breeds resentment. (D) gives up a valuable communication mode entirely; sync time has its place when scheduled fairly.
