# Question

A senior stakeholder insists the PM install activity-tracking software on every remote team member's laptop, citing concerns about productivity. The team is meeting all commitments and delivery metrics are strong. The PM worries tracking will damage trust. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Install the tool as requested since the stakeholder holds budget authority and their concerns warrant a visible response to reassure them.
- **B**: Decline on principle and explain to the stakeholder that remote monitoring tools are inconsistent with modern virtual-team practice everywhere.
- **C**: Understand the stakeholder's underlying concern and propose outcome-based reporting that addresses it without damaging trust through activity surveillance.
- **D**: Delay the conversation and hope the stakeholder loses interest once the next set of delivery metrics lands and reassures them quietly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder-engagement approach is to understand the underlying concern and propose a solution that meets it — outcome reporting addresses productivity visibility without the trust cost of surveillance. (A) implements a symptom-level fix that damages team trust and undermines performance. (B) refuses without engaging the concern. (D) avoids a conversation the PM should lead.
