# Question

A globally distributed team includes two members in a region with unreliable, low-bandwidth internet. They frequently drop off video calls, cannot use the team's heavy real-time collaborative whiteboard tool, and miss parts of discussions as a result. Their work is good when they can engage. The project manager wants to stop the connectivity gap from sidelining them. Which actions are appropriate? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Ask the two members to relocate temporarily to a co-working space with better connectivity for the duration of the project.
- **B**: Default to lower-bandwidth modes — audio-first calls and asynchronous, lightweight collaboration artefacts — so participation does not hinge on a strong connection.
- **C**: Exempt the two members from real-time collaboration sessions and have a colleague brief them afterwards on what was decided.
- **D**: Ensure key discussions and decisions are captured in durable written form so the two members can engage even when a live session degrades or drops.
- **E**: Schedule the heaviest collaborative sessions for times when their connection is historically most stable, and require them to be present then.

## Answer

B, D

## Explanation

The fix should reduce the team's dependence on bandwidth and preserve a record that survives a dropped connection. Defaulting to low-bandwidth modes (B) lets the two members participate without a strong link, and capturing discussions and decisions in writing (D) means a degraded session no longer costs them the content. (A) offloads the problem onto the members, may be impractical or unwelcome, and treats their circumstances as the fault to be corrected rather than adapting the team's practices. (C) formally sidelines them from real-time collaboration and reduces them to recipients of second-hand briefings — the exact exclusion the PM is trying to prevent. (E) sounds accommodating but "historically most stable" on an unreliable connection is no guarantee, and mandating presence at the heaviest sessions sets them up to be cut off at the worst moment; lightening the bandwidth load (B) is more robust than betting on a fragile link. The appropriate moves lower the bandwidth bar and keep a durable record, rather than excluding the members or relying on their connection holding.
