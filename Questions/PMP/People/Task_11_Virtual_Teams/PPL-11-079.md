# Question

A distributed agile team blends members from cultures where openly challenging a senior person in a group setting is normal with members from cultures where it is strongly discouraged. In retrospectives and planning, the first group dominates and the second stays publicly silent, yet privately the silent members hold sharp, valuable reservations they raise only one-to-one with the PM afterwards. Delivery is fine; the PM is concerned that real dissent never reaches the group. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Encourage the reticent members to speak up more directly in the group, coaching them that candour is valued here and that the team needs to hear their concerns openly.
- **B**: Adapt the team's processes to gather input in ways that do not require public challenge — pre-submitted written input, anonymous retro contributions, structured silent rounds — so reservations reach the group regardless of comfort with open confrontation.
- **C**: Continue collecting the silent members' views privately and represent their concerns to the group yourself, so the substance is heard even if they do not voice it directly.
- **D**: Establish a ground rule that everyone must verbally contribute at least one point in each retrospective, so participation becomes mandatory and evenly distributed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The cause is a mismatch between a process that rewards public challenge and members for whom that is culturally fraught, so the inclusive fix is to change the process to capture input through routes that do not demand open confrontation (B) — written, anonymous or silent-round formats let valuable reservations reach the group on the members' own terms. (A) asks people to override a deep cultural norm to fit a process that could instead be adapted to them, placing the burden on the minority and likely producing little real change. (C) keeps the PM as a permanent proxy, which is fragile, filters the dissent through one person and never lets the group actually hear from its own members. (D) forces speech without making it safe or culturally comfortable, so it tends to elicit token, low-value contributions rather than the candid reservations the PM wants surfaced.
