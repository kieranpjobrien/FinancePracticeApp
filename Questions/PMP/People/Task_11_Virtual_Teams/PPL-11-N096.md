# Question

A project manager leads a virtual team spread across several countries and wants to recognise good work consistently. The PM has been giving effusive public praise in the all-team channel, as worked well with a previous team. Some members from certain locations now seem visibly uncomfortable when singled out this way, and a couple have asked the PM privately to stop highlighting them by name. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Learn how recognition is best received by each member and culture, and vary the approach — public for some, private or team-level for others — rather than applying one praise style to everyone.
- **B**: Switch the whole team to private one-to-one recognition, since public praise has caused discomfort for some members.
- **C**: Continue public recognition as the team standard but quietly exempt the specific individuals who asked not to be named.
- **D**: Replace individual recognition with team-level recognition only, praising collective achievements so no single person is singled out.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The lesson is that recognition is not one-size-fits-all: what motivates and feels respectful to one member or culture can embarrass another, and the PM has direct evidence of that mismatch. Tailoring the form of recognition to how each person and culture prefers to receive it respects those differences while still valuing everyone's contribution (A). Switching everyone to private recognition (B) over-corrects, denying public acknowledgement to members who would value and be motivated by it. Keeping a public default and merely exempting those who complained (C) is reactive and leaves others who are equally uncomfortable but have not spoken up still exposed, and treats the requests as awkward exceptions rather than a signal to adapt. Praising only the team (D) erases individual contribution entirely, which can demotivate members who want their personal effort seen. The discriminator is whether the PM adapts recognition to each person and context or imposes another blanket rule in place of the first.
