# Question

A senior stakeholder, uneasy that the delivery team now works fully remotely, asks the project manager to install activity-monitoring software that tracks keystrokes and active hours "to make sure people are actually working". The team is meeting all its commitments. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Explore what is really worrying the stakeholder, then offer to address that concern through transparent outcomes — visible boards, regular demos of working product and delivery metrics — rather than activity surveillance.
- **B**: Explain that the team is meeting every commitment, share the delivery metrics and demo cadence that already evidence this, and propose holding off on monitoring while results continue to speak for themselves.
- **C**: Acknowledge the request and commit to raising team-level transparency — more frequent demos and clearer progress reporting to the stakeholder — so their need for assurance is met without monitoring individuals.
- **D**: Take the stakeholder's concern to the team, explain monitoring is on the table, and work out together what visibility they are willing to offer to satisfy the stakeholder.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Keystroke surveillance would damage the team's autonomy and trust to satisfy what is really a confidence gap, so the response that both protects the team and durably resolves the request reframes the stakeholder from monitoring activity to seeing outcomes — visible boards, demos of working product, delivery metrics — anchored in what is actually driving their unease. That converts a trust-corrosive demand into a sustainable assurance mechanism. (B) is close and genuinely defensible: it answers with the very evidence that should reassure them. But leading with "you're already wrong to worry" can entrench the position, and "hold off while results speak" defers the request rather than replacing it with something the stakeholder accepts. (C) commits to more reporting but locks in a specific remedy before knowing whether progress reporting is what would actually settle this stakeholder, risking effort that misses the mark. (D) hands a still-live monitoring proposal to the team, which can alarm them and concede the framing before the PM has reshaped the request away from surveillance at all.
