# Question

The PM has discovered that two virtual team members in different countries have been in conflict for several weeks. Neither has raised it. They've stopped collaborating, and a key dependency between their work is now slipping. The two cultures involved have very different conventions around direct conflict. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both team members onto a video call together to address the conflict openly and reach a resolution.
- **B**: Speak with each party privately to understand their perspective, then choose a culturally appropriate way to facilitate resolution.
- **C**: Rearrange the work so the dependency between them is removed and collaboration is no longer required.
- **D**: Refer the matter to HR since the conflict is now affecting deliverables.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to handle interpersonal conflict at their level and to tailor the approach to context — including culture. Private conversations first allow honest perspectives to surface and inform whether direct mediation, shuttle diplomacy, or another approach is appropriate. (B) does this. (A) assumes a direct, in-person-style mediation works for both cultures and risks public escalation. (C) routes around the conflict and leaves it to fester elsewhere. (D) escalates externally before the PM has even attempted resolution.
