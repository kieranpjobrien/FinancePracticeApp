# Question

A hybrid project team has six people in one office and four fully remote in other cities. The office group makes most of its decisions in person, then notifies the remote members. The remote members are increasingly disengaged. What structural change should the PM put in place?

## Options

- **A**: Have the office members work from home one day a week so they experience the remote members' situation.
- **B**: Move the remote members onto co-located teams in their own cities and rebuild the project around regional teams.
- **C**: Document every in-office decision in a shared channel so remote members at least have access to the outcomes.
- **D**: Adopt a remote-first working model where key discussions happen in virtual channels accessible to everyone, even when some attendees are co-located.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Remote-first is the structural fix that PMI-aligned virtual practice recommends for hybrid teams — it removes the asymmetry by treating every meaningful discussion as if everyone is remote. (D) does this. (A) is empathy-building but doesn't change the operating model. (B) reorganises work around the wrong axis and may not be feasible. (C) gives remote members visibility of decisions after the fact but no real ability to participate in shaping them, sustaining the disengagement.
