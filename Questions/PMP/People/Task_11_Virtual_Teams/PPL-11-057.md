# Question

A remote developer's commits have slowed noticeably over six weeks and two peers have privately complained about their responsiveness. The PM has never worked with them in person. HR advises the PM needs documented specifics before any formal performance conversation. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Open a formal performance improvement plan immediately using the peer complaints and the commit data as the initial documented evidence base for the new plan.
- **B**: Raise the concern in the team retrospective so patterns can emerge collectively and the developer hears the feedback from peers, not management, in the first instance.
- **C**: Ask another team member to quietly observe the developer's activity and behaviour for a few weeks to gather further evidence before acting on anything.
- **D**: Have a direct, private 1:1 with the developer that shares the specific observations, asks what is going on, and sets a timeframe to revisit.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leader stance is to engage the individual directly with specifics — a private, evidence-based conversation respects the person and often surfaces context (health, workload, blockers) that changes the frame. (A) skips straight to formal process before any dialogue. (B) weaponises a team forum against one person. (C) asks peers to surveil a colleague, which is unethical and destroys trust.
