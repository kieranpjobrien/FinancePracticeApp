# Question

On a hybrid construction-programme team, the PM regularly thanks people for good work but tends to do it in passing during in-office moments. A fully remote analyst recently delivered an excellent risk model and received no acknowledgement, simply because she was not in the building when the praise was given. What should the PM do going forward?

## Options

- **A**: Deliver recognition through channels every team member can see — such as the shared team channel or all-hands — so acknowledgement does not depend on physical presence.
- **B**: Make a point of remembering to phone or message remote members individually whenever their in-office colleagues are praised in person.
- **C**: Keep in-person recognition as the norm but compile a written summary of praise that is circulated to remote members at the end of each week.
- **D**: Encourage remote members to come into the office occasionally so they are present for the informal recognition that naturally happens there.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Recognition on a hybrid team should reach everyone equally, so routing it through channels every member sees removes presence as a precondition for being valued (A). (B) relies on the PM remembering each time and keeps recognition fundamentally tied to the in-office moment, with the remote member always getting a relayed, second-hand version. (C) delays and bundles acknowledgement into a weekly digest, draining the immediacy that makes recognition motivating, and still treats in-person as the default. (D) puts the onus on remote members to change their working pattern to access recognition that should simply be made accessible to them where they are.
