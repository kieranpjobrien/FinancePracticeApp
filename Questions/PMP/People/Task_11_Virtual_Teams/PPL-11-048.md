# Question

Two new developers have joined a fully remote agile team mid-sprint. After two weeks they are still producing very little and one has privately told the PM they feel lost without anyone to ask. Existing team members are heads-down on sprint commitments. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Extend onboarding to six weeks and reduce their sprint commitments so the pressure eases until they feel confident enough to contribute on their own.
- **B**: Run a weekly PM-led catch-up with both newcomers so the PM can personally answer their questions and track their ramp-up progress each week.
- **C**: Pair each newcomer with a nominated buddy for a defined period, with protected time in the buddy's calendar for questions and walkthroughs.
- **D**: Create a comprehensive written onboarding pack covering architecture, tooling and process so newcomers can self-serve without interrupting the team each day.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Remote onboarding fails most often because there is no one obvious to ask; an assigned buddy with protected time solves that directly and builds relationships. (A) removes pressure but not the cause — without a person to ask, time alone does not help. (B) routes everything through the PM, creating a bottleneck and reducing peer integration. (D) is useful supplementary material but written docs alone cannot replicate the quick clarifying exchange that unblocks new joiners.
