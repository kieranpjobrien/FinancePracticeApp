# Question

A pharmaceutical programme runs hybrid: six team members sit together in a London office and four join remotely from other countries. The remote members tell the project manager that side conversations happen in the room, decisions seem half-made before they can contribute, and they often cannot hear people who turn away from the microphone. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run the meeting as "all remote" — everyone joins from their own device on the call, even those in the office — so every participant has an equal seat.
- **B**: Ask the in-room group to repeat any side conversations aloud and to summarise for the remote members at the end of each agenda item.
- **C**: Rotate the meeting facilitation role to one of the remote members so they control the agenda and can pull discussion back to the call.
- **D**: Schedule a separate follow-up call for the remote members after each meeting to walk them through what was decided in the room.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The "one person, one device" approach removes the structural advantage of the co-located group and gives every participant equal presence, audio and access to the discussion — it fixes the cause rather than the symptoms. (B) depends on the in-room group's discipline to self-police side conversations and still leaves remote members second-class. (C) hands over facilitation but does not change the asymmetry that lets in-room decisions form before remote input. (D) reacts after decisions are already made, so remote members influence nothing and feel even more sidelined.
