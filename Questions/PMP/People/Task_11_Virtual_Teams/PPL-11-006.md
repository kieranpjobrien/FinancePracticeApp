# Question

A virtual team member has missed three deadlines in a row and is unresponsive during agreed core hours. The PM has had two informal conversations and behaviour hasn't changed. The rest of the team is frustrated. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the team member's functional manager and request they be reassigned off the project.
- **B**: Hold a documented performance conversation that sets specific expectations, consequences, and a time-bound improvement plan.
- **C**: Quietly redistribute the missed work to other team members and adjust the schedule to the new reality.
- **D**: Treat the variance as part of working with a virtual team and continue informal coaching.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI escalates only after attempted resolution at the PM's level, and informal coaching has now run its course. (B) is the next step — formal, documented, fair — and gives the individual a clear chance to improve before harder action. (A) escalates without first putting a documented expectation in place, which is unfair and weak. (C) shields the underperformer at the cost of the rest of the team and trains the team to expect the workaround. (D) excuses behaviour the PM has already tried twice to change; informal-only is no longer working.
