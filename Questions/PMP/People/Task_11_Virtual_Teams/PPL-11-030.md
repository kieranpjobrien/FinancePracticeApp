# Question

A virtual team member returns from a six-week medical leave. In their first week back, the PM notices they are quiet in meetings, defer decisions to others, and seem hesitant to commit to deliverables. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat them exactly the same as before the leave so they don't feel singled out.
- **B**: Privately check in to ask how they are and what return-to-work pace works for them.
- **C**: Quietly reduce their workload for the next sprint and reallocate items to others without discussion.
- **D**: Brief the rest of the team to be patient and supportive while the team member readjusts.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's people-first principle and basic respect for the individual say to ask, not assume. (B) opens a private conversation that puts the team member in control of how they return. (A) ignores a clear signal and may push them too hard too soon. (C) makes a unilateral decision about their work, which removes agency and can feel like demotion. (D) talks about the person rather than to them and may breach the privacy they likely want preserved.
