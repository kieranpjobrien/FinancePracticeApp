# Question

On a distributed agile team, two engineers in different countries are locked in an increasingly terse exchange in a pull-request comment thread about an architectural choice. Each reply is being read as more dismissive than it was meant, and other team members are starting to take sides in the thread. Both engineers are competent and usually collaborate well. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Post in the thread to remind both engineers to keep comments professional and assume good intent, then let them continue resolving the technical question in writing.
- **B**: Get the two of them onto a short live call to talk it through directly, where tone is visible and the disagreement can be separated from how it is being read.
- **C**: Make the architectural call as PM to end the argument quickly, since the thread is now disrupting the wider team and consuming attention.
- **D**: Move the discussion to a dedicated decision document where each engineer writes up their position fully, so the debate is structured and on the record.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The dispute has stopped being about architecture and become about misread tone in a lean text channel, so the first move is to switch to a richer synchronous channel where tone is recoverable and the people can be separated from the positions (B). (A) keeps the conversation in the very medium that is generating the misunderstanding and relies on a reminder to undo an escalating dynamic. (C) resolves the technical question by fiat but leaves the interpersonal rupture — and the side-taking — unhealed, and short-circuits two capable engineers who normally work well together. (D) adds rigour to the debate but is still asynchronous and written, so it does nothing about the tone problem and may entrench two now-formalised opposing camps.
