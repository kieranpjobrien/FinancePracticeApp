# Question

A follow-the-sun virtual team has members in three regions who hand work between time zones each day. Quality is suffering because each regional handover drops important context, and defects are repeatedly traced to "the previous shift." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a structured daily handover artefact with a fixed template, a short live overlap call, and clear ownership of in-flight items across regions.
- **B**: Restructure the team so each region owns whole features end-to-end and handovers only happen at feature boundaries rather than on a daily basis.
- **C**: Add a daily three-way synchronous call that overlaps with every region, even if this means two of the three regions meet outside their normal working hours.
- **D**: Accept a small quality cost as the price of follow-the-sun working and absorb the defects through a stronger downstream testing and triage process.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI-aligned first move for a broken handover is a structured artefact plus a short overlap — this captures context where it is being lost without redesigning the team. (B) is a more disruptive restructure that may be right eventually but not as the first intervention. (C) forces inconvenient hours on two regions indefinitely. (D) accepts a quality problem instead of fixing its root cause.
