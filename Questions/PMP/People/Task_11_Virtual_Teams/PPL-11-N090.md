# Question

A project manager is setting up a follow-the-sun support model where a development team in Manila hands off to a team in Dublin, which hands off to a team in Denver, around the clock. The PM wants handoffs to be clean so that no in-flight work stalls or is duplicated across the daily handover boundary. Which practices would most directly support effective follow-the-sun handoffs? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: A standard written handoff record per item — current state, what was tried, what the next team should pick up — kept in one agreed place.
- **B**: A short scheduled overlap at each handoff boundary where the outgoing and incoming leads can talk live about anything ambiguous.
- **C**: A rule that the originating team retains ownership of each item until it is fully complete, with other regions only assisting.
- **D**: Clear, shared definitions of item states and "ready to hand over" so an incoming team can trust what a status means without re-investigating.
- **E**: A requirement that all three regions keep their full teams online together for one hour each day to synchronise.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Clean follow-the-sun handovers depend on a reliable written record so the next region can resume without archaeology (A), a brief live overlap to resolve ambiguity that prose cannot (B), and shared state definitions so a status means the same thing everywhere and is trustworthy (D). (C) defeats the entire model: insisting the originator owns each item until done prevents genuine handoff, so work either waits for that region's next shift or never truly transfers, eliminating the round-the-clock benefit. (E) is the trap — forcing all three full teams online together for an hour daily is impossible without two regions working far outside their hours, which is exactly what follow-the-sun exists to avoid; the targeted lead-to-lead overlap in (B) gets the necessary synchronous contact without dragging whole teams across the clock. The workable set combines a strong async record, definition discipline and a minimal live touch at the boundary.
