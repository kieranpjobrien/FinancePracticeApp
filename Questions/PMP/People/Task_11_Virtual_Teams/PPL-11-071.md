# Question

A distributed agile team adopted a "default to async" working agreement to respect time zones. It worked at first, but lately decisions stall for days as long comment threads loop without converging, and a quietly building frustration is that the team has not actually talked through anything contentious in weeks. The team is proud of being "async-first" and reluctant to add meetings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reinforce the async discipline — better-structured proposals, explicit decision deadlines and a clear owner per thread — so the existing model converges faster without reintroducing meetings.
- **B**: Help the team see that async is a default, not an absolute, and agree a lightweight trigger for escalating genuinely contentious or stalled topics to a short synchronous conversation.
- **C**: Reinstate a daily synchronous decision meeting for the whole team, accepting that some members will have to attend at inconvenient local hours, to break the logjam decisively.
- **D**: Let the threads run their course; async deliberation is inherently slower, and the team values the model, so the delays are an acceptable cost of inclusive, time-zone-friendly working.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team has turned a sensible default into a dogma, and the failure mode is specific: contentious, ambiguous topics need the bandwidth of synchronous conversation, which async cannot supply no matter how disciplined. The right move is to keep async as the default while agreeing an explicit trigger to escalate the hard cases to a short live conversation (B). (A) optimises the async machine but cannot fix problems whose root cause is that some discussions are simply unsuited to text, so the genuinely contentious items keep looping. (C) over-corrects into a blanket daily meeting that imposes bad hours on someone every day and throws away the async benefits the team rightly values. (D) accepts mounting delay and unspoken frustration as the price of an ideology, when a targeted, occasional synchronous escape valve would resolve it cheaply.
