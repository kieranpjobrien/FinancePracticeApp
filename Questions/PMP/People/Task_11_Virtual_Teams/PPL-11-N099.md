# Question

Stakeholders and team members of a remote agile project keep asking the project manager for status — what is in progress, what is blocked, how the sprint is tracking — and the PM spends much of each day answering the same questions individually over chat and email. What is the best way to reduce this?

## Options

- **A**: Send a detailed written status update to all stakeholders and team members at the end of each day so everyone has the latest picture.
- **B**: Hold a daily open call where anyone with a status question can join and have it answered live.
- **C**: Maintain a shared, always-current information radiator — a visible board and burndown the team keeps updated — that anyone can consult at any time for live status.
- **D**: Ask stakeholders to batch their status questions and send them once a day so the PM can answer them in a single pass.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The drain is repeatedly relaying status that could simply be visible. A live, self-serve information radiator that the team keeps current lets anyone — across time zones and without involving the PM — see progress, blockers and sprint health whenever they wish (C). A daily written update (A) helps but is a snapshot that goes stale within hours, prompting fresh ad-hoc questions, and still costs the PM time to compile each day. A daily open call (B) reintroduces a synchronous burden that is awkward across time zones and serves only those who attend at that moment. Batching questions (D) reduces interruptions a little but keeps the PM as the manual conduit for information that should be openly available. A transparent radiator removes the PM as the bottleneck entirely, which the other options do not.
