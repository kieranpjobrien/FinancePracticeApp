# Question

A PM has received feedback in two consecutive performance reviews that they take project setbacks personally and that their visible mood after bad news affects team morale for days. The PM accepts the feedback. What should the PM focus on developing?

## Options

- **A**: Emotional resilience — building the capacity to process setbacks, regulate the visible response, and recover quickly enough to support the team.
- **B**: Detachment from project outcomes so that personal investment in delivery does not translate into visible disappointment in front of the team.
- **C**: A more polished public persona that hides emotional reactions behind composed body language during status meetings and team forums.
- **D**: Delegation of bad-news communications to a deputy so that the PM's mood does not influence how the team receives setbacks.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Resilience is the developable EI capability that fits this feedback: it acknowledges the emotion, processes it, and recovers function — without losing commitment. (A) addresses the root cause. (B) sacrifices the engagement that drives good PM performance. (C) is suppression dressed up as composure; teams notice the gap between body language and reality, and trust erodes. (D) avoids the development opportunity and delegates a core leadership responsibility.
