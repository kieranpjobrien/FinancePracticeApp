# Question

During a daily stand-up, a usually upbeat team member gives a flat, one-word update and looks downcast. The PM senses something is wrong but the stand-up is time-boxed and others are waiting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the stand-up continue as normal and quietly check in with the team member straight afterwards.
- **B**: Gently ask in the stand-up whether the team member is doing okay, in case the team can help.
- **C**: Note the change mentally and observe whether it persists before deciding to act.
- **D**: Send the team member a message during the call asking if they would like to talk later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empathy combined with respect for the individual and the format points to a private follow-up. (A) honours the time-box, avoids putting the person on the spot in front of the group, and still acts promptly on the concern. (B) is well-intentioned but asking a downcast person to disclose in front of the team can be intrusive and embarrassing. (C) is too passive when someone is visibly struggling now. (D) is acceptable but a face-to-face or voice check-in shortly after carries more genuine empathy than a message and avoids the person stewing on a vague text.
