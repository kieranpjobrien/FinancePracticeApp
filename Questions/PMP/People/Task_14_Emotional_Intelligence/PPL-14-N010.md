# Question

A project manager on a renewable-energy programme is working to strengthen their emotional intelligence. Match each behaviour the PM displays to the component of emotional intelligence it best demonstrates.

## Items

- **1**: The PM notices their tone sharpened during a tense call and silently identifies that fatigue is fuelling their reaction.
- **2**: Feeling provoked by a contractor's jibe, the PM takes a breath and chooses a measured reply instead of snapping back.
- **3**: The PM senses unspoken anxiety in a quiet team member and adapts the conversation to draw them out gently.

## Matches

- **A**: Self-regulation
- **B**: Empathy
- **C**: Self-awareness

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Self-awareness is the ability to recognise one's own emotions and their drivers in the moment, which is exactly what item 1 describes (noticing the reaction and naming its cause). Self-regulation is the conscious management of an emotional impulse, shown in item 2 where the provocation is felt but the response is chosen. Empathy is sensing and responding to another person's emotional state, demonstrated in item 3 by reading the unspoken anxiety and adjusting accordingly. Distinguishing recognising one's own state from managing it, and from perceiving others' states, is the core of applying emotional intelligence well.
