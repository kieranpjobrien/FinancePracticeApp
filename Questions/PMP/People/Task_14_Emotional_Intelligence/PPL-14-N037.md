# Question

A developer on a hybrid project repeatedly misses commitments, and each time offers a sympathetic personal reason. The project manager has been understanding, but the slippage now threatens the iteration goal and other team members are quietly carrying the load and growing resentful. What is the most emotionally intelligent course?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to make allowances for the developer's situation, since empathy means supporting people through difficult personal times.
- **B**: Have an honest conversation that acknowledges the developer's circumstances while being clear about the impact on the team and agreeing concrete, realistic commitments going forward.
- **C**: Reassign the developer's critical-path work to more reliable team members to protect the iteration goal.
- **D**: Raise the recurring misses with the team collectively so they can agree how to share the workload more fairly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mature emotional intelligence balances empathy for the individual with fairness to the team; compassion is not the absence of accountability. (B) holds both — genuine acknowledgement of circumstances plus honest naming of the impact and a workable forward agreement. (A) mistakes endless accommodation for empathy and ignores the growing unfairness and resentment, which damages the wider team. (C) quietly works around the person without addressing the issue, entrenching it and signalling the problem is undiscussable. (D) exposes one member's struggles to the group and frames a performance matter as a workload-sharing exercise, which is neither fair to the developer nor a real resolution.
