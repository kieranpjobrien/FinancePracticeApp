# Question

A PM wants to recognise a team member who just led a difficult release. The team member is visibly uncomfortable with public praise in past meetings. The PM's usual practice is a shout-out in the team meeting and mention in the stakeholder update. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the usual public shout-out so the team sees consistency in how successes are recognised, even where an individual finds it uncomfortable personally.
- **B**: Recognise the team member's contribution in the usual public channels, while pairing it with a written private note acknowledging their specific role and thanking them.
- **C**: Choose recognition that fits the individual — a sincere private thank-you, a note to their manager, and a meaningful non-spotlight mention — matched to what they value.
- **D**: Avoid all recognition in this case since the individual does not enjoy being singled out, and assume the accomplishment itself is its own reward for the team member.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's individualised recognition principle says match the form of recognition to the person — sincere private acknowledgement plus a note to the person's manager often means more than a public shout-out for an introverted recipient. (A) optimises for PM consistency over recipient meaningfulness. (B) still runs the public form the member dislikes. (D) withholds recognition entirely and misses a genuine opportunity.
