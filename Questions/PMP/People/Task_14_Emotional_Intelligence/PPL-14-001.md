# Question

In the middle of a tense project meeting, a normally composed team member becomes visibly frustrated and makes a sarcastic comment that lands badly with a colleague. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the tension, suggest a brief break, and follow up privately with the team member afterwards.
- **B**: Continue the meeting without comment so the moment is not enlarged into a bigger issue.
- **C**: Address the sarcastic comment immediately so the team sees the standard being upheld.
- **D**: Ask the colleague who was on the receiving end if they would like to respond now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A normally composed person acting out of character is signalling something underneath. (A) is the emotionally intelligent response: defuse in the moment, give space for emotion to settle, and follow up privately to understand what is actually going on. (B) ignores both the immediate dynamic and the cause. (C) addresses behaviour without empathy and risks public shaming. (D) puts the colleague on the spot and prolongs the confrontation.
