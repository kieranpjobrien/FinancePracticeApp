# Question

A project that the team has worked on intensely for nine months has just been cancelled by the steering committee due to a strategic pivot. The PM must brief the team this afternoon. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent way to deliver the news?

## Options

- **A**: State the decision honestly, acknowledge the team's effort and disappointment, and create space for the team to react and ask questions.
- **B**: Lead with the strategic rationale and the new opportunities the pivot creates, then mention the cancellation in that context.
- **C**: Send a written summary first so the team can process privately, then meet a day later once initial emotions have passed.
- **D**: Brief the team leads first and let them cascade the message to their sub-teams in a way that suits each group.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

EI requires both honesty and acknowledgement of impact. (A) is direct, validates the team's investment, and gives them the room to process — which builds trust even in bad news. (B) reframes the message in a way that can feel manipulative or dismissive of the loss. (C) delays direct contact and leaves people to process alone, which damages trust. (D) outsources a difficult conversation the PM should own and risks inconsistent messaging across the team.
