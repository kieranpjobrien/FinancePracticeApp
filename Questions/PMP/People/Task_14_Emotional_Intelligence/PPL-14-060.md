# Question

The PM is about to give a struggling team member specific, honest feedback about their recent performance. The team member has been defensive in past sessions and tends to shut down when criticised. The issues now clearly need addressing for team and individual reasons. How should the PM deliver the feedback?

## Options

- **A**: Lead with broad praise, bury the developmental feedback in the middle, and close with encouragement, minimising the emotional impact on the team member during delivery.
- **B**: Start by asking the team member how they think they are going, listen carefully, and share the PM's specific feedback grounded in observable examples afterwards.
- **C**: Send the feedback in writing first so the team member can absorb it privately, then hold a follow-up session after they have had some time to sit with it.
- **D**: Deliver the feedback quickly and directly, without preamble, so the key message lands before the team member's defensiveness has time to activate in the session.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's feedback approach is to start with the other person's view, listen, and then layer specific behavioural feedback on top — this reduces defensiveness and produces genuine development dialogue. (A) is a "feedback sandwich" that teams learn to discount quickly. (C) outsources a conversation the PM should have in person. (D) ignores known defensiveness patterns that will shut the conversation down.
