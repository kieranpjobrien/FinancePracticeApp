# Question

You coach an agile delivery team. Match each situation to the EI competency it most directly calls on you to exercise. Each competency is used once.

## Items

- **1**: Reviewing how a tense planning session went, you realise that your sharp tone tends to spike specifically when your own estimates are questioned, and you note this as a pattern worth watching.
- **2**: Part-way through that same kind of session, you feel the heat rising and are about to cut someone off — you catch the impulse and take a breath before you respond.
- **3**: A developer who is usually vocal turns quiet and clipped after their work is reassigned and insists nothing is wrong; you pick up that they are upset and gently adjust how you approach them.
- **4**: Two team members have been frosty since a disagreement last sprint; you bring them together, surface the tension, and help them rebuild a working rapport.

## Matches

- **A**: Self-awareness
- **B**: Self-regulation
- **C**: Social awareness / empathy
- **D**: Relationship management

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

The items are deliberately adjacent so the mapping needs discrimination, not label-matching. (1) is self-awareness: the defining act is recognising a pattern in your own emotional triggers after the fact, with no regulation yet attempted — it is tempting to call it self-regulation because it concerns your own anger, but nothing is being managed in the moment. (2) is self-regulation: here you actively restrain the impulse in real time, which is the step beyond merely noticing it. (3) is social awareness/empathy: you perceive another's unspoken state and attune to it; it can be mistaken for relationship management, but you are reading and adjusting, not yet repairing a relationship. (4) is relationship management: you act on the dynamic between others to rebuild rapport, which goes beyond sensing feeling to mending the connection. The trap pairs are 1↔2 (noticing versus managing your own emotion) and 3↔4 (perceiving another's state versus actively repairing a relationship).
