# Question

A senior stakeholder publicly criticises the PM in a steering committee, using language the PM considers unfair and factually wrong. The PM feels their face flush and their voice tighten. Several team members are watching the exchange unfold in real time. What should the PM do IN THE MEETING?

## Options

- **A**: Defend themselves immediately and robustly in the meeting to protect their credibility and authority in front of the watching team members.
- **B**: Acknowledge the concern calmly at a high level, propose to take the detail offline, and continue with the meeting's substantive agenda and next steps.
- **C**: Name the stakeholder's behaviour as inappropriate, ask them to retract the comment, and pause the meeting until the matter is formally settled.
- **D**: Say nothing in the moment, absorb the criticism publicly, and raise the issue privately with the stakeholder's line manager after the meeting has concluded.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's self-regulation principle says respond, not react — a calm acknowledgement plus taking detail offline protects credibility, the meeting's purpose and the relationship. (A) escalates in public when the PM is still emotionally activated. (C) demands a retraction mid-meeting and makes the situation worse. (D) absorbs it silently and then goes around the stakeholder afterwards.
