# Question

A technically respected PM has received consistent feedback that the team finds them "cold" and "hard to read." The PM wants to improve but says that visibly expressing warmth and emotion feels inauthentic and uncomfortable. What is the BEST development path?

## Options

- **A**: Engage a communications coach to provide a library of phrases and behaviours the PM can use in standard team interactions to project warmth.
- **B**: Begin with small, genuine practices — specific thank-yous, asking about people's work, acknowledging effort — and expand range gradually as comfort grows.
- **C**: Lean into the technical strength as the PM's distinctive contribution and accept that warmth is not a realistic development goal for this individual.
- **D**: Practise more expressive body language and tone in team meetings even when it feels unnatural, so the team experiences the desired warmth quickly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EI development for emotional expression works best when it is gradual and authentic — small genuine behaviours compound and feel real to the team. (B) is the path that actually shifts perception without the PM faking it. (A) produces scripted warmth that teams reliably detect as performative. (C) abandons a development area that is materially affecting the PM's leadership effectiveness. (D) prioritises speed over authenticity and tends to read as inauthentic, which can damage trust further.
