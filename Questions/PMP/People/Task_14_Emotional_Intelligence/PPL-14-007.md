# Question

The project has just been hit with a significant scope increase but no extra time or budget. The project manager is frustrated and the team will look to them when the news lands. How should the project manager handle their own emotion when communicating?

## Options

- **A**: Project optimism and assure the team they will get through it together.
- **B**: Express the frustration openly to show the team they are not alone in feeling it.
- **C**: Postpone the team conversation until the project manager feels more even-keeled.
- **D**: Acknowledge the difficulty honestly while modelling a constructive response and focus on what is in the team's control.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Authentic leadership names reality without amplifying alarm. (D) does this and pivots to action the team can take. (A) is hollow positivity that the team will see through. (B) deepens anxiety without giving direction. (C) leaves the team without information at the moment they need it most.
