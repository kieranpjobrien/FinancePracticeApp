# Question

A PM has been carrying significant personal stress for a month. Their team has quietly become more tense, defensive and cautious over the same period. The team's delivery is still on track but the mood has shifted. The PM had not connected the two until now. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the personal stress fully private, focus on supporting the team, and trust that the team dynamic will settle on its own once delivery pressure eases.
- **B**: Share the personal situation in detail with the team openly so they understand the reason for the change and can extend patience while things settle down.
- **C**: Acknowledge to the team that the PM has been under additional pressure and that it may have affected their presence, without requiring personal-life detail.
- **D**: Take two weeks of personal leave to reset so the PM can return in a state that does not transmit stress to the team working environment any further.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's emotional-contagion awareness says leaders shape team mood — naming the impact briefly without oversharing repairs the dynamic and models self-awareness. (A) keeps the team guessing and does not repair the shift. (B) overshares personal detail the team cannot help with. (D) is avoidance dressed up as self-care and leaves the team exposed.
