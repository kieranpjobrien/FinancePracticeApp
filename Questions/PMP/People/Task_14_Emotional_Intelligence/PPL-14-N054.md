# Question

After a demanding release, a project manager wants to recognise the team in a way that genuinely sustains motivation. The effort was uneven: a couple of people carried long nights on the critical path, while the win was only possible because the whole team held the line together. The PM knows individuals on this team value very different forms of acknowledgement. What is the most emotionally intelligent way to recognise them?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the collective achievement to the whole team, then separately recognise the specific contributions of those who carried the heaviest load, tailored to what each values.
- **B**: Recognise each person individually for their specific contribution, in the form each one values, and skip a collective message to keep it personal.
- **C**: Credit the whole team equally for a shared win, deliberately not singling anyone out, to protect the team's cohesion and sense of fairness.
- **D**: Privately thank the two people who carried the critical path, since they did the work that actually saved the release, and let the wider recognition wait for the next all-hands.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four are real recognition strategies with genuine trade-offs, so the best answer fits this specific situation: a true team win plus a few people who clearly did more, on a team with varied motivators. (A) honours the collective effort and the standout contributions and personalises the latter, capturing both truths without making them compete. (B) personalises well but drops the shared identity that a whole-team win deserves and that many members draw motivation from. (C) is fair in the uniform sense but ignores that real differences in effort went unacknowledged, which can demotivate those who stretched furthest. (D) recognises the heavy lifters but withholds timely acknowledgement from everyone else and defers it to an administrative moment, missing the people who held the line. The fine margin is that A balances collective and individual recognition rather than sacrificing one for the other.
