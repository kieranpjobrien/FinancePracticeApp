# Question

A project has just hit a serious setback that will delay the go-live by several months. Team morale is visibly shaken. The PM's instinct is to project strong optimism in the next team meeting to lift everyone's spirits and reassure the team. What is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Lead with strong optimism and forward-looking language so the team feels the PM is confident and capable, and their own confidence is lifted in turn.
- **B**: Strictly project realism with no emotional framing at all and walk the team through the delay, the recovery plan, and specific numbers in full detail.
- **C**: Ask the team leads to handle the delay communication while the PM works through their own emotional reaction before engaging the wider team in conversation.
- **D**: Acknowledge the setback honestly, name the feelings it raises, and share a clear realistic path forward with genuine belief in the team's capability to deliver.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's authentic-leadership principle combines honesty about reality with genuine belief in the team — teams see through performative optimism and lose confidence in the PM. (A) projects optimism that reads as denial of what the team is feeling. (B) strips out emotional framing the team actually needs. (C) passes responsibility at the moment the PM's presence matters most.
