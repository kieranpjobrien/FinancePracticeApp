# Question

A project manager is about to facilitate a charged retrospective after a failed release where blame is already circulating between developers and QA. The PM wants to bring emotional intelligence to how they run the session. Which of the following are emotionally intelligent preparations and behaviours? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Check their own emotional state beforehand so frustration about the failure does not leak into how they facilitate.
- **B**: Open by reaffirming a blameless framing and reminding the team the goal is to learn, not to find fault.
- **C**: Watch for rising tension during the session and intervene early to keep the conversation on systems and process rather than persons.
- **D**: Steer the discussion away from the failure quickly to keep the mood positive and avoid reopening conflict.
- **E**: Let the developers and QA debate the cause freely so the disagreement burns itself out without the facilitator interfering.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Facilitating with emotional intelligence starts with self-regulation (A), sets a psychologically safe frame (B), and actively reads and manages the room to keep it constructive (C). (D) suppresses the very analysis a retrospective exists for, prioritising comfort over learning and leaving the blame dynamic unresolved. (E) abandons facilitation when the group is already inflamed, allowing the conflict to harden into personal blame. The three correct options together create the conditions for honest, blameless improvement.
