# Question

On a distributed banking programme, the PM notices that team members from one regional office never push back in video calls and always defer, while members from another office debate openly. The quiet office's deliverables have recently contained avoidable errors that someone there likely foresaw. What is the most emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Recognise that directness norms differ by culture and create lower-stakes, asynchronous channels where the quieter members can raise concerns comfortably.
- **B**: Encourage the quieter office in calls to speak up more, explaining that the team values everyone's challenge and input equally.
- **C**: Pair members from the two offices so the quieter colleagues learn to voice concerns by observing their more vocal counterparts.
- **D**: Add a structured round-robin to every call so each person is explicitly asked for their view before decisions are made.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Social awareness here means recognising that silence may reflect cultural norms about hierarchy and face rather than a lack of concerns, and adapting the channel rather than demanding people change their style. (A) meets people where they are by offering a comfortable medium, which is most likely to surface the concerns. (D) is a reasonable supporting tactic but public round-robins can still feel exposing in high-context cultures and put people on the spot. (B) places the burden of change on the quieter group and may be heard as criticism. (C) treats one cultural style as the standard the other must emulate, which is neither respectful nor reliable.
