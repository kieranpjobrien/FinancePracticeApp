# Question

A PM notices they instinctively trust the estimates and judgement of one outgoing, articulate developer far more than those of an equally skilled but reserved teammate, and they have been assigning the more interesting work accordingly. A retrospective comment about uneven opportunities makes the PM uneasy. What is the most emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Reflect honestly on whether an affinity bias is shaping their judgements, then review how work and trust have actually been distributed.
- **B**: Make a point of assigning the next interesting piece of work to the reserved teammate to correct the imbalance immediately.
- **C**: Ask the team for feedback on whether work allocation feels fair, so the matter is addressed transparently and collectively.
- **D**: Explain to the team that allocation reflects current priorities and proven delivery, while committing to watch for fairness going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emotional intelligence at its deepest is the self-awareness to recognise one's own biases, including the comfort-driven pull toward people who communicate like us. (A) confronts the possible affinity bias directly and checks it against evidence, which is the necessary first step before any sound corrective action. (B) jumps to a fix before understanding the pattern and risks tokenism or overcorrection driven by guilt rather than judgement. (C) outsources self-examination to the team prematurely and can put the reserved teammate in an awkward position. (D) rationalises the existing allocation and defends rather than reflects, which sidesteps the uncomfortable self-insight the situation calls for.
