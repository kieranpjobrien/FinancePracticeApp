# Question

A PM leading a high-stakes data-centre migration has just learned that a critical cutover failed overnight and the rollback only partially worked. They feel a wave of panic. The team will join a bridge call in ten minutes looking to the PM for direction, and several members are already anxious. What should the PM do before the call?

## Options

- **A**: Take a few minutes to steady themselves and order the known facts, so they can lead the call calmly and give the anxious team a stable focal point.
- **B**: Join the call immediately to convey urgency, sharing their own concern openly so the team understands the gravity of the situation.
- **C**: Quickly draft a detailed recovery plan alone so they can present a complete solution and reassure the team that it is under control.
- **D**: Delay the call by an hour to gather full diagnostics, so the team is not pulled into uncertainty before the picture is clear.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Self-regulation in a crisis is what allows a leader to become the calm the team borrows from. (A) manages the panic and organises facts so the PM can project steadiness, which is the single most stabilising thing for an anxious team in an incident. (B) transmits the PM's panic to an already anxious team, amplifying it. (C) wastes critical minutes and assumes the PM can solve it alone, when the team's input is likely needed and a premature plan may be wrong. (D) leaves the team in limbo during a live incident where prompt, calm coordination matters more than complete diagnostics.
