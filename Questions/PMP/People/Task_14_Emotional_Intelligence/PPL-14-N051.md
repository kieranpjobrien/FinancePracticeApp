# Question

Ten minutes before a sprint review with executives, the product owner tells the project manager they have unilaterally promised the executives a feature that is not in the increment and that the team has not built. The PM feels blindsided and angry. The review is about to start. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Manage their reaction, run the review honestly on what was actually delivered, and address the product owner's commitment with them directly afterwards.
- **B**: Open the review by correcting the product owner in front of the executives so expectations are accurate from the outset.
- **C**: Quietly go along with the promise during the review and work out afterwards how the team might deliver the feature.
- **D**: Postpone the review on the spot, citing a misalignment that must be resolved before facing the executives.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Self-regulation means not letting justified anger drive a damaging public reaction. (A) contains the emotion, keeps the review truthful, and deals with the real interpersonal issue in the right setting, preserving both credibility and the working relationship. (B) is honest but airs an internal conflict in front of executives and humiliates the product owner. (C) protects appearances by misleading stakeholders and commits the team to an unplanned promise. (D) is a disproportionate, visible escalation that signals dysfunction and wastes the executives' time over something manageable.
