# Question

A scrum team's newest member has gone quiet in refinement after a senior developer publicly corrected them twice in pointed terms. The team genuinely values direct, candid technical debate, and the senior developer is a strong contributor acting in good faith. The project manager wants to rebuild the newcomer's confidence without dulling the team's prized candour. Which two actions should the PM take first? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Speak privately with the newcomer to understand how they are experiencing the sessions and what would help them re-engage.
- **B**: Have a quiet, non-judgemental word with the senior developer about how their delivery is landing, without questioning the substance of their points.
- **C**: Raise the incident in the next retrospective for the whole team to reflect on how it gives and receives feedback.
- **D**: Personally make a point of inviting and building on the newcomer's input in the next few refinements to visibly restore their standing.
- **E**: Pair the newcomer with a supportive teammate for upcoming refinements so they have a sounding board before speaking up.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

All five are reasonable, non-punitive responses, so the question is which two best fix the immediate dynamic at its source first. (A) starts with the affected person, understanding their experience before acting, which is the empathic foundation for anything else. (B) addresses the specific behaviour that caused the withdrawal, privately and constructively, preserving the candour the team values while changing how it lands; together A and B target the actual cause. (C) is worthwhile but is a slower, team-level intervention that risks re-exposing the newcomer publicly and does not address the senior developer directly. (D) helps rebuild standing but treats the symptom and could feel conspicuous before the PM understands what the newcomer needs. (E) is a kind support, yet it works around the problem rather than resolving the interaction that caused it. The fine margin is that A and B repair the root cause, while C, D, and E manage around it.
