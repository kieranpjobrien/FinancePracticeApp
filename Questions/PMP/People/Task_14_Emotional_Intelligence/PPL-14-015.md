# Question

A project manager's anxiety becomes visible to the team whenever something goes wrong, and the team starts to mirror it. How should the project manager improve their emotional regulation?

## Options

- **A**: Mask the anxiety entirely so that the team only ever sees a calm and composed leader.
- **B**: Avoid taking on projects whose risk profile they find personally stressful or difficult to manage.
- **C**: Tell the team upfront that they sometimes look anxious and to simply disregard it when they do.
- **D**: Identify their triggers, practise calming techniques, and model that stress can be worked through visibly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Regulation is not suppression. (D) describes the developmental path: notice the trigger, manage the response, and let the team see what healthy coping looks like. (A) is unsustainable and inauthentic. (B) tries to narrow the world rather than build the skill. (C) shifts the burden of management onto the team.
