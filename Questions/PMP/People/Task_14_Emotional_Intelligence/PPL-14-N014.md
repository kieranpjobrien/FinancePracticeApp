# Question

Two senior members of a Scrum team building a telematics platform have an unspoken rivalry that is now slowing decisions; each subtly undermines the other's proposals in refinement. The PM has good rapport with both. Which actions reflect emotionally intelligent relationship management? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Meet each person separately to understand their perspective and what each feels is at stake before bringing them together.
- **B**: Name the pattern to both privately, without blame, and explore what is driving the friction beneath the proposals.
- **C**: Publicly praise whichever proposals are technically strongest in refinement so the rivalry resolves on merit.
- **D**: Facilitate a conversation between the two that focuses on shared goals and how their strengths complement each other.
- **E**: Separate their areas of responsibility so they rarely have to collaborate on the same decisions.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Relationship management draws on self-awareness, empathy, and deliberate facilitation to repair a strained dynamic. (A) gathers each person's perspective and emotional stake, which is essential groundwork. (B) surfaces the underlying pattern without assigning fault, opening it for honest discussion. (D) reframes the relationship around shared purpose and complementary strengths, which is the constructive resolution. (C) injects competition and a winner-loser dynamic that is likely to deepen the rivalry. (E) avoids the conflict by structurally separating them, which leaves the interpersonal issue unresolved and forfeits the value of their collaboration.
