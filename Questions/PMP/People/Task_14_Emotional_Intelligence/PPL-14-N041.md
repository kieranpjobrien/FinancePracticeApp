# Question

At a daily standup for a mobile banking app, a developer announces that a dependency the project manager personally championed has just broken the build for the third time this week. The PM feels heat rise in their face and an urge to defend the decision in front of the team. The standup is time-boxed and three other people still need to report. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the dependency is causing real pain, note internally that the defensiveness is about their own stake in the choice, and ask the developer to take it to a follow-up so the standup stays on track.
- **B**: Explain the reasoning that led to choosing the dependency so the team understands it was a sound decision given what was known at the time.
- **C**: Agree on the spot to replace the dependency, since three broken builds is clear evidence the original call was wrong.
- **D**: Let the developer finish, then ask the whole team in the standup to debate alternatives so the issue is resolved quickly while everyone is present.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The emotionally intelligent response separates the PM's ego from the problem and respects the team's time-box. (A) names the trigger internally (self-awareness), regulates the urge to defend, validates the pain, and defers the deep dive appropriately. (B) is exactly the ego-driven defence the PM noticed forming and consumes the standup. (C) is an impulsive reversal under emotional pressure without analysis. (D) hijacks a time-boxed event for a problem-solving session that belongs elsewhere.
