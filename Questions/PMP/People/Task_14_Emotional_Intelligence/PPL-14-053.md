# Question

A business stakeholder joins a call visibly angry about a missed deliverable, voice raised and talking over the PM's attempts to explain. The PM knows the miss was partly caused by the stakeholder's late input, but stating that now would escalate further. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the impact of the miss, let the stakeholder finish, and agree to come back with a clear picture of what went wrong once the call has cooled down.
- **B**: Interrupt and calmly explain that the late input was a major contributing factor, so that the PM's team are not unfairly held responsible for the miss.
- **C**: End the call on the basis that no productive conversation is possible while the stakeholder is shouting, and reschedule it for later once things have calmed down.
- **D**: Stay silent for the remainder of the call, let the stakeholder exhaust their anger, and then move to the next agenda item without any further response from the PM.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's de-escalation move is to acknowledge impact and let the person be heard before introducing new information — the root-cause conversation is much more productive once the emotion has settled. (B) introduces contested facts into a live anger spike. (C) shuts the stakeholder down rather than working with them. (D) is passive absorption that leaves the issue unaddressed.
