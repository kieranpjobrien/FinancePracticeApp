# Question

A normally meticulous engineer on a predictive infrastructure project has submitted three late, error-prone deliverables in a fortnight. In a one-on-one, the project manager notices the engineer is subdued, gives clipped answers, and avoids eye contact when schedule is mentioned. The schedule slip is becoming material to the milestone. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a clear recovery plan with daily check-ins on the outstanding deliverables, making expectations explicit so quality returns to its usual standard.
- **B**: Reflect back what they are observing in a non-judgemental way and create space for the engineer to share whatever may be affecting their work.
- **C**: Reassign the engineer's critical-path tasks to a colleague to protect the milestone while the engineer recovers their footing.
- **D**: Document the pattern of late, defective work and reference the performance expectations agreed at the start of the project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The behavioural cues signal that something has changed for a previously reliable person, and empathy precedes correction. (B) uses social awareness to read the signals and opens a safe channel without accusation, which is most likely to surface the real cause and preserve the relationship. (A) and (D) jump to performance management on a symptom and may worsen an underlying personal or workload issue. (C) protects the schedule but removes responsibility without understanding the cause and can read as a vote of no confidence. Diagnosing with empathy first does not ignore the milestone; it is the fastest route to a durable fix.
