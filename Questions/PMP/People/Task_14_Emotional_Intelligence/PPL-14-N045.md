# Question

On a hybrid programme, two team leads, one from the agile delivery squad and one from the predictive integration team, have a history of friction. In a planning workshop their disagreement over sequencing turns sharp, voices rise, and the wider group begins to look uncomfortable. Both leads have valid technical points, the sequencing genuinely affects the next release, and the workshop has the right people in the room to settle it. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge both positions have merit, then keep the pair working the trade-off in the room, steering them to test each sequence against the shared delivery outcome until a jointly owned answer emerges.
- **B**: Step in to settle the sequencing decision yourself there and then, since both technical cases are sound and the team is looking to the PM to end an exchange that has stopped being productive.
- **C**: Pause the item, have the two leads work the sequencing offline as a focused pair, and bring a joint recommendation back to the group for confirmation.
- **D**: Park the sequencing decision and move the workshop on to items the group can progress, scheduling the trade-off for when tempers have cooled.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The discriminator is in-the-moment relationship management versus three reasonable ways of taking the heat out by removing the work from the room. (A) de-escalates while keeping the conflicting parties engaged on the live problem and re-anchored on the shared outcome, which converts the friction into a jointly owned decision now, with the right people present. (B) is defensible, decisive leadership and ends the noise, but it imposes a call the leads could own and deepens a standing rivalry by signalling they cannot work together. (C) produces a joint recommendation but pulls the trade-off out of a workshop convened precisely to make it, and leaves a strained pair to negotiate without facilitation. (D) lowers the temperature but defers a decision the group is positioned to make and lets the unresolved tension carry forward. The fine margin is that A resolves the relationship and the decision together in the moment, whereas B, C, and D each trade one away to relieve the discomfort.
