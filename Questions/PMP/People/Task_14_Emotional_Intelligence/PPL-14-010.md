# Question

A team member's performance has slipped after a recent death in their family. They have not shared this with the wider team, but the project manager is aware. How should the project manager handle this with emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly inform the team so colleagues can adjust their expectations and offer help.
- **B**: Treat the team member exactly the same as before to be fair to everyone.
- **C**: Refer the team member to HR and the EAP without delay.
- **D**: Respect their privacy, offer private support and flexibility, and adjust expectations without explanation.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Empathy in this case requires both compassion and respect for the team member's choice not to share. (D) honours both. (A) discloses private information without consent. (B) ignores human reality and is not actually fair. (C) is a useful follow-up but is not the first or only response; it should not pre-empt a personal check-in from the project manager.
