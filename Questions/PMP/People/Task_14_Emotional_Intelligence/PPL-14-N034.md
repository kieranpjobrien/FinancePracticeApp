# Question

On a multinational pharmaceutical project, the project manager notices that colleagues from one country consistently agree in meetings and then raise concerns privately afterwards, while the PM had read the meeting silence as consensus. A key decision is approaching. What is the most emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the quieter colleagues directly in the meeting to voice any disagreement, so all views are on the table transparently.
- **B**: Recognise that public agreement may not equal real agreement in this context, and build in private or written channels for concerns before the decision is finalised.
- **C**: Proceed on the basis of the meeting consensus, since decisions cannot wait indefinitely for everyone to be individually canvassed.
- **D**: Tell the team that for this project a culture of open challenge is expected and that silence will be taken as agreement.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Social awareness includes sensitivity to cultural and individual differences in how dissent is expressed, and adapting the process to surface real views. (B) reads the pattern accurately and creates a channel that fits how these colleagues actually communicate. (A) demands public disagreement in the very setting where they are reluctant to give it, which can cause loss of face and yield nothing. (C) mistakes silence for consent and risks a decision people quietly oppose. (D) imposes the PM's preferred norm rather than meeting the team where they are, and "silence equals agreement" is precisely the misreading that caused the problem.
