# Question

On a hospital construction project, a normally meticulous site supervisor has missed two inspection sign-offs this fortnight and was short with a subcontractor yesterday. The PM knows the supervisor's spouse was recently diagnosed with a serious illness. The missed sign-offs carry a compliance risk that must be addressed. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Have a private conversation that opens by acknowledging the supervisor seems to be carrying a lot, then move to how the sign-off responsibilities can be supported or temporarily redistributed.
- **B**: Document the two missed sign-offs in the issue log and discuss the compliance gap with the supervisor in the next formal project review.
- **C**: Quietly arrange for another qualified inspector to cover the sign-offs for a few weeks without raising it with the supervisor, to reduce their burden.
- **D**: Reiterate the compliance obligations to the whole site team in a toolbox talk so the supervisor understands the stakes without being singled out.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Social awareness means reading the human situation and responding to the person and the risk together. (A) leads with empathy, preserves the supervisor's dignity, and still resolves the compliance exposure by addressing support and redistribution. (B) treats a struggling person as a performance entry and delays a live compliance risk. (C) is well-meant but removes responsibility without consent, which can feel like a vote of no confidence and leaves the supervisor unaware of how their lapses are being seen. (D) avoids the direct conversation the situation needs and uses a group setting to deliver what is really individual feedback.
