# Question

How does emotional intelligence relate to servant leadership in a project context?

## Options

- **A**: They address different leadership challenges — EI is about individual self-management while servant leadership is about organisational structure.
- **B**: Servant leadership is a more advanced approach that supersedes the need for explicit emotional intelligence work in mature project teams.
- **C**: Emotional intelligence is most relevant for command-and-control leaders, while servant leaders rely primarily on facilitation skills and process tooling.
- **D**: Emotional intelligence is foundational to servant leadership because serving the team requires accurately perceiving and responding to team needs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Servant leadership requires the leader to understand what the team actually needs — which is precisely what EI's self-awareness, empathy, and social-skill competencies enable. (D) captures this dependency. (A) draws an artificial line; both are about how leaders relate to people. (B) reverses the relationship — EI underpins servant leadership, it is not replaced by it. (C) is wrong on both counts: EI matters across leadership styles, and servant leadership in particular leans heavily on it.
