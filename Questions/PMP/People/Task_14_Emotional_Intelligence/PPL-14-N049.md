# Question

On a distributed agile team, a developer who is usually warm and talkative on video calls has had their camera off for a week, gives one-word answers in chat, and missed two stand-ups, yet their stories are still moving on the board. The project manager already has a routine one-on-one scheduled with this developer in four days. What is the most emotionally intelligent way for the PM to respond?

## Options

- **A**: Use the developer's progress as a natural opening and raise the change in demeanour at their scheduled one-on-one in four days, where there is unhurried time to talk.
- **B**: Send a brief, warm private message today, naming that they seem a bit quieter lately and asking, with no mention of tasks, how they are doing.
- **C**: In the next stand-up, gently note to the team that a few people have seemed flat this sprint and invite anyone who wants to talk to reach out, so the developer is not singled out.
- **D**: Quietly ask the teammate who works most closely with the developer whether they have picked up on anything, so the PM approaches the conversation already informed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Every option is a caring, non-punitive attempt to reach the developer, so the discriminator is which best matches empathy to the urgency and the person. The signals form a week-long cluster of withdrawal, which warrants a timely, individual, person-first contact rather than a delayed or indirect one. (B) reaches out now, privately, naming the change without making it about output, which most respects the developer's dignity and is likeliest to surface a real issue early. (A) is genuinely empathic and the existing one-on-one is a comfortable venue, but waiting four days while someone may be struggling lets the concern sit and risks more missed events; a week of signals is enough to act on now. (C) protects the developer from being singled out but is so diffuse it may never reach them, and a struggling person rarely self-identifies to the group. (D) gathers context but routes a personal matter through a third party before the PM has spoken to the developer at all, risking gossip and signalling indirectness. The fine margin is timely, direct, private contact (B) over a kind but slower (A) or less personal (C, D) channel.
