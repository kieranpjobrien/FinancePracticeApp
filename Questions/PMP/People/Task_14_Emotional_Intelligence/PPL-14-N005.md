# Question

A scrum team building an insurance claims system has just had a release pulled at the last minute by a regulator, wasting three sprints of work. At the next stand-up the team is flat, terse, and avoiding eye contact. The PM wants to respond with emotional intelligence. Which actions are appropriate? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge openly that the cancellation is demoralising and that the frustration in the room is understandable.
- **B**: Create space, separate from the stand-up, for the team to voice how they feel before pivoting to next steps.
- **C**: Immediately refocus the team on the new backlog priorities to restore a sense of momentum and purpose.
- **D**: Reframe the cancelled work by identifying what was learned and what can be reused, once feelings have been heard.
- **E**: Reassure the team that the cancellation was entirely outside anyone's control and there is no point dwelling on it.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Emotional intelligence requires naming and validating the team's emotional reality before driving forward. (A) demonstrates empathy and social awareness by acknowledging the mood rather than ignoring it. (B) gives the team a legitimate outlet, which research links to faster recovery and re-engagement. (D) provides constructive reframing, but crucially only after emotions are acknowledged, preserving the sequence. (C) rushes past the team's feelings to task delivery and can read as cold. (E) is dismissive toxic positivity that shuts down the very expression the team needs, even though the facts about control may be true.
