# Question

A quiet but highly capable tester on a medical-device project has just found a critical defect days before release, almost certainly preventing a costly recall. The tester is uncomfortable with public attention. The project manager wants to recognise the contribution in an emotionally intelligent way. What should they do?

## Options

- **A**: Praise the tester prominently in the next all-hands so the whole organisation sees the value of thorough testing.
- **B**: Keep the recognition entirely private, since the tester dislikes attention and forcing it would be uncomfortable for them.
- **C**: Nominate the tester for a formal company award without mentioning it, so the recognition is a pleasant surprise.
- **D**: Thank the tester sincerely in private, ask how they would feel about wider recognition, and acknowledge them publicly only in the form they are comfortable with.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Emotionally intelligent recognition is attuned to how the individual wishes to be acknowledged, not just to the manager's instinct to celebrate. (D) honours the contribution while respecting the tester's preference, checking rather than assuming. (A) ignores the tester's discomfort and imposes the spotlight they dislike, which can feel punishing rather than rewarding. (B) over-corrects by withholding deserved recognition entirely, when a private or scaled acknowledgement may be very welcome. (C) acts on the tester's behalf without consulting them and could spring exactly the public attention they want to avoid.
