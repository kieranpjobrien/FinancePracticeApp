# Question

A team member comes to the PM frustrated about a recurring blocker, speaking quickly and clearly venting rather than seeking an immediate solution. The PM has a fix in mind. What is the most emotionally intelligent response?

## Options

- **A**: Listen fully and reflect back what they are feeling and saying before moving toward solutions.
- **B**: Offer the fix straight away so the team member's frustration is resolved as quickly as possible.
- **C**: Ask the team member to put the blocker in writing so it can be triaged properly later.
- **D**: Reassure the team member that the blocker is common and not worth getting frustrated about.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empathy and active listening mean attending to the person's emotional need to be heard before jumping to problem-solving. (A) demonstrates that the PM understands and validates the frustration, which often matters more to the speaker in that moment than the solution itself. (B) is solution-mode that can leave the person feeling unheard even if the blocker is fixed. (C) is administratively reasonable but emotionally cold to someone who is venting and needs acknowledgement. (D) minimises the person's feelings, which is dismissive and likely to increase rather than reduce the frustration.
