# Question

During a one-on-one, a team member on a pharmaceutical validation project unexpectedly becomes tearful while describing how overwhelmed they feel by the workload. The PM had intended to use the meeting to discuss a missed milestone the team member is responsible for. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Set the milestone discussion aside, give the person space to talk, and listen to understand how overwhelmed they are before anything else.
- **B**: Acknowledge the emotion briefly and gently steer back to the milestone, since the deadline issue still needs to be resolved today.
- **C**: Offer to end the meeting and reschedule for when the team member is feeling more composed and able to discuss performance.
- **D**: Reassure the person that the missed milestone is not a serious problem so they feel less pressure and can calm down.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empathy requires meeting the person's emotional state before any agenda. (A) responds to the distress in front of the PM, builds trust, and will almost certainly surface context that explains the missed milestone anyway. (B) prioritises the task over the person at a moment of vulnerability, which will damage the relationship. (C) avoids the emotional conversation entirely by deferring, leaving the person feeling dismissed when they have just opened up. (D) offers false reassurance that may not be true and skips the chance to genuinely understand the overload driving the performance issue.
