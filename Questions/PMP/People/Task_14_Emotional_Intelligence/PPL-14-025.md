# Question

Why is empathy considered a core emotional intelligence competency for project managers?

## Options

- **A**: It enables the PM to understand stakeholder perspectives and tailor communication, decisions, and engagement to the people involved.
- **B**: It allows the PM to align with whichever stakeholder has the strongest reaction in any given meeting.
- **C**: It reduces the need for the PM to use formal communication channels such as status reports and meetings.
- **D**: It ensures the PM consistently prioritises individual relationships over project objectives and delivery commitments.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empathy is the ability to perceive and understand others' emotions and viewpoints, which lets the PM tailor stakeholder engagement, anticipate resistance, and communicate in ways that land — exactly what (A) describes. (B) confuses empathy with capitulation; understanding a perspective is not the same as agreeing with it. (C) is wrong — empathy improves but does not replace formal communication artefacts. (D) creates a false trade-off; empathy supports delivery by improving collaboration, it does not override project objectives.
