# Question

In a backlog refinement session for a gaming studio, two developers who usually collaborate well have a sharp exchange over a technical approach; one goes silent and folds their arms, the other keeps pressing the point. The room cools. The project manager wants to handle the moment with emotional intelligence and keep the relationship healthy. Which actions are appropriate? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Read the non-verbal cues, gently pause the debate, and reflect back what each developer seems to be valuing so both feel heard.
- **B**: Separate the people from the problem by reframing the disagreement as two valid options to be evaluated against the same criteria.
- **C**: Note privately that the silent developer has withdrawn and follow up with them one-on-one after the session to check they are alright.
- **D**: Decide the technical question yourself on the spot to end the tension and let the team move on.
- **E**: Let the more vocal developer finish making their case fully, since the quieter one has clearly conceded the point.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Emotionally intelligent relationship management combines reading the room (A), depersonalising the conflict so it stays about ideas (B), and tending to the person who has disengaged (C). (D) imposes a resolution that suppresses the conflict rather than resolving it and removes the team's ownership of a technical decision. (E) misreads folded arms and silence as agreement when they more likely signal shutdown, the opposite of social awareness. The three correct options together de-escalate the moment while protecting both the work and the relationship.
