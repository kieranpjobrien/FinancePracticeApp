# Question

A PM senses unexplained tension with a key business stakeholder. There has been no overt conflict, but recent meetings have felt strained and the stakeholder has been less responsive over email. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce direct contact for a few weeks and route project communication through the stakeholder's delegate to let the tension dissipate.
- **B**: Reflect on what may be contributing from their own side, then request a short one-on-one to surface the dynamic and discuss it openly.
- **C**: Confidentially ask peers who work closely with the stakeholder whether they have noticed anything and what may be behind it.
- **D**: Raise the strained relationship with the project sponsor so they can intervene with the stakeholder on the project's behalf.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) combines self-awareness (own contribution) and relationship management (direct, low-key conversation) — the EI playbook for a strained relationship. (A) lets the issue fester and signals avoidance. (C) risks gossip, undermines trust if it gets back to the stakeholder, and skips the obvious step of asking them directly. (D) escalates to the sponsor before the PM has attempted to resolve the issue at their own level.
