# Question

A PM realises that they consistently feel irritated and become curt during one particular recurring meeting with a vendor. They want to manage this better. What is the most emotionally intelligent first step?

## Options

- **A**: Reflect on what specifically about the meeting triggers the irritation, so they can manage their reaction next time.
- **B**: Ask a colleague to co-chair the meeting so the PM's curtness is less likely to surface.
- **C**: Reduce the meeting frequency to limit the number of occasions the irritation arises.
- **D**: Prepare a tighter agenda so the meeting runs efficiently and gives less cause for frustration.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Self-management begins with self-awareness: understanding the trigger before attempting to control the response. (A) identifies the source of the emotion, which is the necessary foundation for regulating it. (B) outsources the problem rather than developing the PM's own capacity to manage it. (C) avoids the situation instead of addressing the reaction, and the irritation will persist. (D) may help marginally but assumes the trigger is inefficiency, which the PM has not yet examined; it treats a symptom without understanding the cause.
