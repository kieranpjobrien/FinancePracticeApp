# Question

Mid-sprint on a media-streaming product, the product owner messages the project manager demanding that a half-finished feature be force-pushed to production tonight for a marketing deadline the PM was never told about. The PM knows this skips the team's testing gate and feels a flash of anger at being put on the spot. The product owner is online and waiting for a reply. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Take a moment to settle, then reply asking to talk for five minutes to understand the deadline and lay out the testing risk before any decision is made.
- **B**: Reply immediately that pushing untested code to production is not acceptable and that the marketing deadline cannot override the team's quality gate.
- **C**: Agree to the push to preserve the relationship with the product owner, and arrange extra monitoring overnight to catch any defects quickly.
- **D**: Tell the product owner this is their call as the owner of priorities, and ask them to confirm in writing that they accept the testing risk.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The competency on test is regulating a hot reaction and converting a demand into a conversation rather than a collision. (A) buys the pause that self-regulation requires, then moves to shared understanding of both the deadline and the risk, which is the only basis for a sound joint decision. (B) is technically defensible but is fired while angry and shuts the door, escalating a relationship the PM will need again. (C) is appeasement that trades a known quality risk for short-term comfort. (D) abdicates the PM's duty to surface risk and uses a paper trail as a substitute for actually managing the situation; the testing concern still needs to be discussed, not just documented.
