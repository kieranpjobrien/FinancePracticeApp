# Question

After a punishing crunch to ship a public-sector grants portal on time, the team is exhausted but quietly proud. At the retrospective, the sponsor sends word they want the next release accelerated. The PM agrees the timeline is feasible. How should the PM open the retrospective with emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Genuinely acknowledge and celebrate what the team achieved and the toll it took, before any discussion of what comes next.
- **B**: Share the sponsor's enthusiasm and the accelerated timeline first, framing it as recognition of how capable the team has proven to be.
- **C**: Run the retrospective as usual on what went well and what to improve, keeping the tone neutral and process-focused.
- **D**: Thank the team briefly and move quickly to planning the next release so momentum from the successful launch is not lost.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emotional intelligence means recognising the team's emotional state, in this case earned pride alongside exhaustion, and honouring it before adding new demands. (A) validates their effort and sacrifice, which replenishes the team and builds the goodwill needed for whatever comes next. (B) leaps straight to more work dressed as a compliment, which a depleted team is likely to experience as the reward for hard work being more hard work. (C) misses the emotional moment by staying coolly procedural. (D) under-acknowledges a significant achievement and prioritises momentum over the people who delivered it.
