# Question

A PM runs a distributed agile team across several time zones in a logistics software company. One member consistently joins ceremonies at the very end of their working day and has mentioned, only in passing, that the schedule means they routinely miss putting their children to bed. Their engagement is starting to slip. The PM wants to respond with genuine empathy while keeping the team effective. Which TWO actions best demonstrate empathy here? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the personal cost the schedule is creating and check in directly on how the timing is affecting the member.
- **B**: Work with the team to review the meeting schedule and find a rotation or timing that shares the inconvenience more fairly.
- **C**: Reassure the member that the dip in their engagement is completely understandable given the hours and tell them not to worry about it.
- **D**: Arrange for the ceremonies to be recorded so the member can watch later and step back from the live sessions.
- **E**: Quietly shift just this member's meeting times to suit them, without raising it with the rest of the team.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Empathy is both perceiving and genuinely caring about another's experience and then acting to address it, so the empathetic pair is to acknowledge the personal impact directly (A) and to act on its cause in a way that is fair to everyone (B). The distractors all sound considerate, which is the point: (C) offers warmth but no action and tells the member not to worry about the very engagement problem that needs addressing, mistaking reassurance for support. (D) appears accommodating yet quietly removes the member from live collaboration, which tends to deepen disengagement and substitutes exclusion for understanding. (E) does act, but solving it privately for one person without involving the team trades a shared, fair fix for a quiet favour that can breed resentment and sidesteps the open conversation empathy calls for. The fine margin is that A and B pair acknowledging the feeling with fair, visible action on its cause, whereas C, D, and E variously avoid acting, exclude, or quietly work around the situation.
