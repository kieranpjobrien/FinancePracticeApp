# Question

A project manager receives 360-degree feedback indicating that team members find them dismissive when they disagree, even though the PM believes they are simply being efficient. The PM is surprised and a little defensive on reading it. What is the most emotionally intelligent first response?

## Options

- **A**: Reflect on specific recent interactions where the perception may have formed before deciding how to act on the feedback.
- **B**: Ask the facilitator which individuals gave the feedback so the comments can be understood in context.
- **C**: Explain to the team in the next meeting that quick responses are meant to keep things efficient, not to dismiss anyone.
- **D**: Note the feedback but set it aside, since one round of comments may not reflect a consistent pattern.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The foundation of emotional intelligence is honest self-awareness, especially when feedback is uncomfortable. (A) resists the defensive reflex and looks inward at concrete behaviour, which is the prerequisite for any genuine change. (B) is a deflection that seeks to discredit or contextualise the source rather than absorb the message. (C) rationalises the behaviour and effectively tells the team their perception is mistaken. (D) dismisses the signal, which is precisely the dismissiveness the feedback describes.
