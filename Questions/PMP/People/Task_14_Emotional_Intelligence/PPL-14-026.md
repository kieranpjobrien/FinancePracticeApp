# Question

A high-performing analyst on a hybrid team consistently volunteers for additional work, then privately complains to the PM about being overloaded. The pattern has continued across two iterations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop assigning the analyst optional work for the next iteration to give them space to recover and reset workload.
- **B**: Praise the analyst's commitment publicly and reassure them that their effort is visible and appreciated by leadership.
- **C**: Reassign some of the analyst's commitments to other team members during the next sprint planning session without discussion.
- **D**: Have a one-on-one conversation to explore the pattern, surface what's driving it, and coach the analyst on setting workload boundaries.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

EI here means addressing the root cause through empathy and development, not managing the symptom. (D) explores why the analyst over-commits and helps them build a transferable skill. (A) treats the symptom and removes the analyst's agency without discussion. (B) reinforces the unsustainable behaviour. (C) silently shields the analyst, which prevents them from learning to manage their own workload and may breed resentment in the team if commitments are reshuffled without explanation.
