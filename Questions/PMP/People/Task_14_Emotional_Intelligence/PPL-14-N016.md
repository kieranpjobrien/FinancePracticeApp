# Question

A newly formed agile team building a transport-ticketing app is in its third sprint. The PM observes that one member's dry humour, intended as banter, is repeatedly landing badly with a quieter colleague who has started withdrawing. Neither has raised it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Speak privately with the humorous member, share the observed impact on the colleague, and help them see how the banter is being received.
- **B**: Raise the importance of inclusive communication with the whole team so norms are reinforced without singling anyone out.
- **C**: Check in privately with the withdrawing colleague first to confirm they are genuinely affected before taking any further action.
- **D**: Wait to see whether the team's forming dynamics settle on their own, as friction is common in early sprints.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Social awareness means reading the situation accurately before acting, and the PM's read of the quieter colleague's experience is an inference that should be confirmed. (C) verifies the impact directly with the person affected and respects their agency over how to proceed, which prevents the PM from acting on a misread or escalating something the colleague would rather handle quietly. (A) intervenes with the humorous member based on an unconfirmed assumption and may overstep if the colleague does not see it as a problem. (B) dilutes a specific interpersonal issue into a generic message unlikely to reach the individual. (D) is passive while a colleague is visibly withdrawing.
