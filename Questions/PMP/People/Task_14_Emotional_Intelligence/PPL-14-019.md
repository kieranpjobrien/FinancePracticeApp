# Question

In a team meeting, the project manager notices that a normally engaged team member is unusually withdrawn and quiet. Their body language signals something is off, but they have not said anything. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Check in with them privately after the meeting to see how they are doing.
- **B**: Direct a question to them in the meeting to draw them back into the discussion.
- **C**: Note that everyone is expected to participate and continue the meeting.
- **D**: Assume their silence is intentional and respect it without follow-up.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Reading non-verbal cues and acting privately is core emotional intelligence. (A) respects dignity while opening space for conversation. (B) puts the team member on the spot publicly. (C) issues a generic instruction that does nothing for the individual. (D) ignores a signal that something specific is going on.
