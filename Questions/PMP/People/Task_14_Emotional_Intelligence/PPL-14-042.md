# Question

A PM realises that under sustained delivery pressure they have become shorter and less patient with the team, and team members have started softening bad news to avoid the reaction. The PM sees the pattern but keeps slipping back into it under stress. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take a week of leave to fully decompress, then return with a clearer head and better capacity to model the leadership the team needs.
- **B**: Open the next team meeting with an apology, acknowledge the recent behaviour, and commit to the team that it will not happen again.
- **C**: Temporarily delegate day-to-day team leadership to a senior team member during the highest-pressure period until delivery stabilises.
- **D**: Build personal stress-management strategies — recognising early warning signs, recovery routines, workload protections — that prevent the behaviour at source.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM has self-awareness but no system for self-regulation under load. (D) addresses the root cause by building the conditions that prevent the behaviour, rather than reacting after the fact. (A) may help short-term but doesn't change the underlying pattern. (B) is a useful one-off but without a systemic change the team will see the apology repeat. (C) sidesteps the leadership development the PM needs and may be read as the PM stepping back at exactly the wrong moment.
