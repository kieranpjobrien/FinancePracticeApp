# Question

A senior stakeholder who strongly opposed the project at initiation now sends terse, sceptical replies and challenges the project manager's figures in every forum. The PM has begun to dread the interactions and notices they are avoiding this stakeholder. The stakeholder controls access to a resource the project needs next month, and the sponsor has offered to broker the resource handover if it becomes difficult. What is the best course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the sponsor to take the lead on securing the resource, using their authority and existing relationship with the stakeholder to get the commitment locked in before next month.
- **B**: Seek a direct one-to-one with the stakeholder to understand the concerns behind the scepticism and what evidence or assurances would build their confidence in the project.
- **C**: Tighten the figures and the reporting so that in each forum the stakeholder's challenges can be answered cleanly and the PM's credibility is steadily re-established in front of peers.
- **D**: Map exactly what the project needs from the stakeholder next month and engage them narrowly on that transaction, keeping the broader relationship at arm's length for now.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

All four are defensible, and each protects the resource by a different route; the discriminator is which most durably addresses the relationship the project depends on. (B) recognises the PM's own avoidance and turns toward the stakeholder to understand the opposition, the only option that can convert a sceptic into a cooperating party rather than merely managing around them. (A) is a legitimate use of the sponsor and may well secure the resource once, but it outsources a relationship the PM will keep needing and can read as the PM being unable to manage it. (C) strengthens credibility on the figures and is worth doing, yet it answers the challenges without addressing the underlying distrust driving them, so the friction persists. (D) is pragmatic and may get the project through next month, but transactional engagement leaves the resentment intact and the next dependency just as fraught. The fine margin is that B repairs the source while A, C, and D each secure the immediate resource without resolving the relationship.
