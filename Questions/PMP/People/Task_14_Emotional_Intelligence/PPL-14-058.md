# Question

A PM has noticed they consistently become defensive and short when the finance director challenges project numbers. The pattern has been observed by colleagues and flagged in informal feedback. The PM respects the finance director professionally. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reflect on what specifically is triggering the reaction, prepare for the next interaction with that awareness, and practise a more regulated response in the moment.
- **B**: Limit direct interactions with the finance director by routing most financial conversations through a team member better suited to handle the particular dynamic.
- **C**: Raise the pattern with the finance director openly and ask them to deliver their challenges more constructively so the PM is less likely to become defensive.
- **D**: Accept the pattern as part of the PM's working style and focus development effort on other areas where behaviour change will return more value to the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's self-awareness principle treats patterns like this as developmental signals — reflection on triggers and rehearsed regulation is the work. (B) avoids the stakeholder the PM needs to engage with. (C) attributes the reaction to the other party's behaviour. (D) concedes a development area with a visible cost to professional credibility.
