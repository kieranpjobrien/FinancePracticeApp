# Question

On a fully remote software project spanning three time zones, the project manager notices that one developer, who recently relocated and works alone, has stopped turning their camera on and gives minimal updates, though their code is fine. In the last team social call they were absent without explanation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign the developer a buddy on the team to pair with regularly so they have a built-in social connection.
- **B**: Raise in the next team meeting that cameras-on and active participation help remote cohesion, framing it as a general expectation for everyone.
- **C**: Leave it be for now, since the developer's deliverables are unaffected and they may simply prefer to keep their camera off.
- **D**: Reach out privately and informally, mention they have seemed a bit quieter lately, and ask how they are finding things since the move.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Social awareness on a virtual team means noticing the weak signals of disengagement or isolation that physical distance hides, and responding to the person. (D) checks in privately and gently, opening space for the developer to share without being put on the spot. (B) addresses a possible individual struggle with a blanket rule, which can feel like a public rebuke aimed at one person. (C) rationalises the withdrawal away because output is fine, missing that isolation often precedes a deeper problem. (A) may help but imposes a solution before understanding whether anything is actually wrong; the empathetic check-in comes first.
