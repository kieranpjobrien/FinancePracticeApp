# Question

A project manager leads a hybrid team across three time zones with little face-to-face contact, and wants to sharpen their social awareness, the ability to read the team's actual emotional state despite the distance. The PM already runs regular all-team ceremonies and reads the shared chat daily. Which two further practices would most directly strengthen social awareness in this context? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Pay close attention to tone, pacing, and what goes unsaid in calls and messages, and follow up privately when something seems off.
- **B**: Rotate short one-on-one check-ins so the PM hears directly from each member and can sense how they are doing over time.
- **C**: After noticing a member seems withdrawn, deliberately invest in rebuilding that relationship and realigning them with the team.
- **D**: When the PM feels their own frustration rising on a difficult call, pause and manage it before it colours how they read others.
- **E**: Share more about the PM's own context and pressures so the team feels safe being open in return.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

All five are sound emotional-intelligence practices for a distributed team, so the discriminator is which two build the capacity to perceive others' emotions. (A) reads the available cues across channels and probes gently when something seems amiss, which is social awareness applied to a remote setting. (B) creates direct, recurring contact that lets the PM sense each person's state and how it shifts over time. (C) is relationship management, acting on a relationship once a problem is perceived, not perceiving it. (D) is self-regulation, managing the PM's own state. (E) is a trust-building and relationship move that helps openness but does not itself sharpen the PM's perception. The trap is that the wrong options are all genuinely good; only A and B are about reading others accurately.
