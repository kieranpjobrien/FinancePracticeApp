# Question

Which statement BEST reflects how a project manager develops their emotional intelligence over time?

## Options

- **A**: Emotional intelligence is largely fixed by adulthood; PMs should play to their existing strengths rather than try to develop new EI capabilities.
- **B**: EI develops through self-reflection, feedback from others, deliberate practice in real situations, and ongoing intentional learning.
- **C**: EI is best developed through formal training programmes followed by certification, similar to other PM technical competencies.
- **D**: EI improves naturally as PMs gain years of experience leading teams; targeted development effort is rarely necessary.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EI is well-evidenced as developable, and the standard development loop is exactly what (B) describes: reflect, get feedback, practise in real settings, repeat. (A) contradicts the research. (C) overweights formal training; classroom learning without applied practice rarely shifts behaviour. (D) assumes experience equals development, which is not reliably true — many experienced PMs plateau without intentional work on EI.
