# Question

A capable developer on a healthcare app team has become visibly disengaged: they deliver competent work but no longer contribute ideas in refinement and decline pairing. Their output is acceptable, so colleagues have not raised it. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Have a one-on-one to understand what has changed for the developer and what would make the work feel meaningful again.
- **B**: Assign the developer a high-visibility component to re-engage them through a more challenging and rewarding piece of work.
- **C**: Raise the importance of active participation in refinement with the whole team to lift collective engagement.
- **D**: Monitor the developer's output over the next two sprints to confirm whether the disengagement is affecting delivery before acting.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emotionally intelligent leadership reads the change in a person and seeks to understand the driver before prescribing a fix. (A) opens a dialogue to surface the actual cause, whether it is boredom, a personal issue, or a grievance, which must be known before any intervention can work. (B) assumes the cause is insufficient challenge and may misfire entirely if the real issue is burnout or a conflict. (C) addresses an individual matter through a generic group message, which the developer is likely to find impersonal. (D) delays engaging with a person who is signalling distress and risks the disengagement deepening.
