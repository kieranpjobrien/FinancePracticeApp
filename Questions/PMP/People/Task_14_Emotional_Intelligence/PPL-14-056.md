# Question

Late one evening, a sponsor messages with an angry, unreasonable demand. The PM feels a strong pull to reply immediately with a sharp defence of the team. Their partner suggests waiting until morning. The sponsor will expect a response overnight. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reply immediately tonight with a sharp well-constructed defence, on the basis that a delay will signal weakness and the sponsor plainly expects overnight responsiveness in urgent situations.
- **B**: Ignore the message until morning entirely, reasoning that a sponsor who sends angry messages late at night is not entitled to a quick response from the PM.
- **C**: Send a brief acknowledgement tonight committing to a considered response by a specific time tomorrow, then address it fully in the morning with a clear head.
- **D**: Forward the message to the sponsor's line manager and ask them to intervene before the PM engages any further with the content of the overnight demand directly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's self-management principle is to use the pause — a brief holding response honours the sponsor's signal while preserving the PM's capacity for a good answer. (A) replies from an activated state and creates damage. (B) ignores a stakeholder signal and will be read as dismissive. (D) escalates prematurely, before the PM has even engaged with the substance of the demand.
