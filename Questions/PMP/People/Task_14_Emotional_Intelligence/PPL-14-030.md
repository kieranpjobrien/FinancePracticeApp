# Question

A PM has been putting off a difficult performance conversation with a team member for two weeks because they feel anxious about the team member's likely emotional reaction. What should the PM do to prepare for the conversation?

## Options

- **A**: Rehearse a tightly scripted opening and stick to it word-for-word so the message lands clearly regardless of reactions.
- **B**: Hold the conversation in the next available slot today, before the anxiety has any more time to build and influence the message.
- **C**: Ask the functional manager to be present in the meeting so the team member receives the feedback from two sources at once.
- **D**: Acknowledge the anxiety, prepare specific examples and a developmental framing, and plan for how to respond to likely reactions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) combines self-awareness (naming the anxiety), preparation (specific behavioural examples), and intent (developmental, not punitive) — the EI recipe for a hard conversation. (A) makes the PM brittle in the moment; rigid scripts struggle when the other person reacts. (B) reacts to the PM's anxiety rather than the team member's needs and skips preparation. (C) escalates and ambushes the team member; the PM should attempt the conversation themselves before involving the line manager.
