# Question

A PM whose preferred style is concise and data-driven works with a senior business stakeholder who is highly expressive and processes decisions through extended discussion. The PM finds the interactions draining and notices their patience slipping. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Adapt their own style to acknowledge the stakeholder's perspective and emotion first, then present the data and recommendations the PM is comfortable with.
- **B**: Have a candid conversation asking the stakeholder to be more concise in working sessions so meetings can focus on decisions and outcomes.
- **C**: Limit live interactions and shift the relationship toward written briefings and decision memos so most communication happens asynchronously.
- **D**: Ask the program manager to take the lead with this stakeholder so the PM can focus their energy on stakeholders whose styles are a closer fit.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

EI requires adapting your style to the person, not requiring them to adapt to you. (A) honours the stakeholder's processing style while still letting the PM make their case — empathy plus self-management. (B) asks the more senior stakeholder to change rather than the PM developing range. (C) preserves comfort but starves the relationship of the rapport it needs. (D) avoids the development opportunity and may damage the relationship if the stakeholder reads the change as disengagement.
