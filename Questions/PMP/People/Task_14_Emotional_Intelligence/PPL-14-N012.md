# Question

A PM is about to deliver difficult news to a manufacturing project team: a planned overtime payment has been cancelled by finance after the team worked weekends expecting it. The PM is angry at finance too. How should the PM prepare to handle the team conversation with emotional intelligence?

## Options

- **A**: Anticipate the team will feel betrayed, plan to acknowledge that openly, own their own disappointment honestly, and come ready to explore what can still be done for the team.
- **B**: Frame the message around the business rationale finance gave, so the team understands the decision was a sound financial call beyond the PM's control.
- **C**: Deliver the facts plainly and quickly to avoid prolonging an uncomfortable conversation, then open the floor to questions.
- **D**: Reassure the team that the PM fought hard for the payment and that the cancellation is entirely finance's doing, not the project's.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emotionally intelligent preparation means anticipating others' emotional responses and planning an authentic, empathetic delivery. (A) predicts the sense of betrayal, validates it, models honest emotion, and shifts to constructive options, which sustains trust. (B) leads with rationale before acknowledging feelings, which the team is likely to experience as the PM siding with finance over them. (C) is efficient but emotionally tone-deaf to a team that has just been let down. (D) deflects blame and positions the PM as the hero, which can come across as self-serving and does nothing to address how the team feels.
