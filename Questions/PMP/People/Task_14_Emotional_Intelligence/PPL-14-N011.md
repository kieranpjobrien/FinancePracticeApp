# Question

In a sprint review for a retail e-commerce platform, the product owner harshly criticises a feature the team is proud of, in front of stakeholders. The PM sees the lead developer flush with anger and start to interrupt. The PM also feels their own protective instinct flare. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Calmly intervene to acknowledge the product owner's concerns, suggest capturing the detailed critique for refinement, and steer the review forward.
- **B**: Let the lead developer respond, since they own the feature and have the right to defend their work to the product owner.
- **C**: Side with the team by noting how much effort went into the feature and that the criticism feels disproportionate to the stakeholders present.
- **D**: Pause the review and ask the product owner to hold feedback until a separate session, to protect the team from public criticism.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM must regulate their own protective impulse and de-escalate before the developer's anger boils over, while still honouring legitimate feedback. (A) manages the moment with composure, redirects the critique to the right forum, and keeps the review on track without taking sides. (B) lets an angry exchange play out publicly, which can damage the team's standing and the PM's credibility. (C) abandons neutrality and turns the review into a defence, escalating in front of stakeholders. (D) shields the team but also signals that the product owner's input is unwelcome, which undermines a key collaborator and can read as overreaction.
