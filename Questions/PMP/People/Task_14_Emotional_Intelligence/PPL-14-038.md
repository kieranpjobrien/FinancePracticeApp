# Question

Late on a Friday, a PM receives an email from a stakeholder that feels critical and unfair, copying the sponsor. The PM's first instinct is to write a defensive reply pointing out the inaccuracies. What is the MOST emotionally intelligent next step?

## Options

- **A**: Reply briefly to the stakeholder and the sponsor noting the inaccuracies and proposing a meeting to discuss the substance further next week.
- **B**: Hold the response until the PM has cooled down, considered the stakeholder's perspective, and drafted a measured reply that addresses the substance.
- **C**: Pick up the phone immediately and call the stakeholder so the issue is dealt with directly rather than escalating further over email.
- **D**: Forward the email to a trusted peer for their read on whether the criticism is fair before deciding how to respond to the stakeholder.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The defensive urge is the signal — the EI move is to wait, regulate, and respond thoughtfully. (B) does exactly that. (A) is reactive: a quick reply with a meeting offer still puts a defensive message in front of the sponsor on a Friday evening. (C) calls the stakeholder while the PM is still emotionally charged, which is likely to escalate. (D) seeks validation rather than resolution and risks turning the issue into office gossip before the PM has even responded.
