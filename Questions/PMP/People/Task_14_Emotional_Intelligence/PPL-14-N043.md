# Question

While presenting a status update to a steering committee, the project manager notices the sponsor's expression harden and arms fold the moment quarterly costs appear, although the sponsor says nothing and the agenda has two more topics to cover. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue through the remaining agenda items as planned and address any cost questions if the sponsor raises them before the meeting closes.
- **B**: Pause and gently check in, noting that the cost figures may warrant discussion and inviting the sponsor to share any concerns now.
- **C**: Pre-empt the reaction by immediately presenting the cost-mitigation options the team has prepared, to reassure the committee.
- **D**: Make a note to follow up with the sponsor privately after the meeting, where a frank conversation is easier than in the committee.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emotional intelligence includes reading non-verbal signals and responding to them in the moment rather than ploughing ahead. (B) surfaces the unspoken concern while the sponsor is engaged, demonstrating attentiveness and preventing a small worry from festering into a meeting-derailing issue. (A) ignores a clear signal and risks the sponsor disengaging or raising it later in a more charged way. (C) reacts to an assumption about what is wrong and may answer a question the sponsor has not asked. (D) defers a concern that is live now and lets it colour the rest of the session.
