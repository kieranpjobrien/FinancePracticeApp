# Question

Two functional leads on a road-construction project — the structures engineer and the traffic-management lead — have escalated a sequencing dispute to the project manager, each having presented a one-sided account. Both are respected, both are frustrated, and the wider team is taking sides. What is the most emotionally intelligent way for the PM to proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Review both accounts and the schedule logic, then issue a sequencing decision with a clear rationale to settle the dispute quickly.
- **B**: Meet each lead separately again to fully understand their concerns before deciding, so neither feels ambushed in a joint meeting.
- **C**: Bring the two leads together, have each restate the other's position to the other's satisfaction, then work from the shared facts toward a sequencing decision.
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to the project sponsor, since two senior leads at an impasse signals a decision above the PM's authority.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Relationship management of a heated peer conflict aims to rebuild understanding between the parties, not just resolve the surface issue. (C) uses the discipline of each restating the other's view to defuse the us-versus-them dynamic and produces a decision both can own. (A) may settle the sequencing but leaves the relationship and the team's factionalism untouched, likely breeding the next dispute. (B) keeps the leads apart and reinforces parallel, competing narratives rather than a shared one. (D) abdicates a conflict squarely within the PM's remit and signals to the team that disagreements get pushed upward rather than worked through.
