# Question

A usually strong team member made a significant mistake in production that required a weekend rollback. Since then they have been quiet, avoiding eye contact in meetings, and declining to take on new high-visibility work. The PM can see shame at play. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Address the shame directly in a 1:1 — name what happened, what was learned, and actively assign them a visible task that signals continued confidence in them.
- **B**: Leave the team member space to recover in their own time, accepting their reduced ambition as a temporary phase, and trust them to come back when they are ready.
- **C**: Assign them only low-visibility tasks for a period so they can rebuild their confidence quietly before being put back into more exposed work again over time.
- **D**: Add an additional layer of peer review to all their future work so the PM and the team member both feel assured that a similar mistake cannot happen again soon.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's coaching approach to recovery is direct and visible — naming what happened, honouring the learning, and publicly reinvesting in the person is what repairs the shame spiral. (B) lets the recovery drift and risks the pattern setting in. (C) signals lowered confidence by reducing their work. (D) solves a perceived technical problem with oversight and compounds the shame.
