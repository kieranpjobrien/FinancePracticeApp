# Question

A delivery lead on an agile e-commerce team realises, mid-sprint, that they have been snapping at the team during stand-ups all week and that morale has visibly dropped. On reflection they trace it to their own anxiety about an executive review at the end of the sprint. The lead wants to stop their state from degrading the team's mood and performance. Which TWO actions best demonstrate emotional self-management here? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Name to themselves the anxiety driving the irritability and consciously choose calmer responses in stand-ups while the pressure persists.
- **B**: Acknowledge to the team that they have been short this week, own it, and reset how they intend to show up.
- **C**: Ask their manager to clarify the scope and stakes of the executive review so the source of the anxiety is better contained.
- **D**: Be transparent with the team about how anxious the executive review is making them, so the team understands the recent edginess.
- **E**: Block focused prep time for the review so the lead feels readier and the underlying worry eases.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Self-management pairs recognising one's emotional state (self-awareness) with deliberately regulating the behaviour it produces, and repairing the impact on others authentically — so (A), the internal regulation move, and (B), the authentic accountability and reset, are the two that directly govern the lead's own conduct toward the team. The distractors are all reasonable things a lead might do, which is the point: (C) is sensible stakeholder management and may reduce the anxiety, but reducing a stressor is not the same as managing one's behaviour, and it leaves this week's impact on the team unaddressed. (D) looks like healthy openness, yet narrating one's anxiety to the team shifts the emotional burden onto them and can spread the tension rather than contain it; accountability for the behaviour (B) differs from offloading the feeling (D). (E) is good personal preparation but directs energy at the review, not at the regulation or repair the situation with the team requires. The fine margin is that A and B have the leader own and regulate their state in relation to the team, whereas C, D, and E manage the stressor or involve others.
