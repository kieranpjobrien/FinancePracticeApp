# Question

Two sub-teams on an agile programme, front-end and platform, have grown openly resentful of each other after weeks of blaming one another for integration failures. Sprint output is suffering and the mood in shared ceremonies is cold. The project manager has kept their own composure throughout and can read that both sides feel unfairly blamed. Given that the PM already has their own emotions in check and an accurate read of both sides, what should the PM do now?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the two sub-teams together to surface the resentment, rebuild trust, and realign them on the shared delivery outcome they both own.
- **B**: Continue to model calm and self-awareness in ceremonies, trusting that a steady example from the PM will gradually settle the tension between the groups.
- **C**: Meet each sub-team separately to listen carefully and acknowledge how unfairly blamed each one feels, deepening the PM's understanding of both perspectives.
- **D**: Reframe the integration failures as a shared process problem and tighten the hand-off definition of done so there is less to blame each other for.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The scenario deliberately states the PM has already done the inward work, self-regulation and self-awareness, and the perceiving work, an accurate empathic read of both sides. What remains is to act on the relationship between the groups, which is relationship management, and that is what the situation now demands. (A) convenes the parties, rebuilds trust, and realigns them on a common goal, the relationship-management move that directly addresses the rift. (B) keeps exercising self-regulation, a competency already in hand, and passively hopes the conflict resolves itself. (C) is more empathy, valuable but already largely done, and stops short of bringing the sides together. (D) is a sensible process fix that may reduce future friction but leaves the existing resentment and broken trust unaddressed. The trap is that B, C, and D each reflect a real EI competency or sound practice, but only A applies the one the situation has been set up to require.
