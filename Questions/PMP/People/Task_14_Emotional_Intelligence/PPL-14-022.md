# Question

Two senior developers on a 12-person agile team have escalated a design disagreement and each comes to the project manager separately, visibly frustrated, asking the PM to side with them. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both developers into a joint meeting immediately so the disagreement can be resolved face-to-face before it spreads.
- **B**: Make a decision on the technical approach so the team can keep moving and avoid further delay to the sprint.
- **C**: Refer the matter to the technical lead or architect so a subject-matter expert decides the design question.
- **D**: Meet with each developer individually, listen actively, and acknowledge their concerns before facilitating any joint discussion.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Emotional intelligence requires that people feel heard before they can engage rationally. Listening individually first (D) lets the PM understand each perspective and lower the emotional temperature so a joint conversation can be productive. (A) brings two emotionally charged people together prematurely, which often escalates conflict. (B) substitutes the PM's judgement for a team decision and bypasses collaboration. (C) may be appropriate later, but escalating to the architect before the PM has even understood the concerns skips the relationship-management step.
