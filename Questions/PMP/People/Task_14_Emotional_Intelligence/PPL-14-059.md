# Question

The PM needs a peer manager in another function to release a resource for two weeks. The peer has previously refused and seemed irritated by the ask. The PM's instinct is to escalate to the peer's director to force the release since the project need is genuine. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate immediately to the peer's director, since the business need is real, the peer has already said no, and further discussion is likely to waste more time.
- **B**: Send a detailed written business case to the peer reiterating why the resource is genuinely required, and follow up with a firm deadline for a formal response.
- **C**: Meet the peer informally, understand their current pressures, and look for a mutually workable arrangement before any escalation path is explored or seriously considered further.
- **D**: Proceed without the resource for now, on the basis that the peer's refusal is final and the PM will have to absorb the cost of that decision personally.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's social-skill lens expects the PM to build understanding and look for mutual value before escalation — the peer's irritation likely reflects pressures the PM has not yet understood. (A) escalates past a peer relationship that can still be worked on. (B) re-pitches against a "no" without changing anything. (D) gives up too soon on something the project needs.
