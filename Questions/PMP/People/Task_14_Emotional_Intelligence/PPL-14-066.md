# Question

Three weeks after a heated design disagreement, two team members are still working around each other — polite in meetings, but avoiding collaboration and refusing to pair. Delivery has started to show the effects. The PM had assumed they would reconcile on their own. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign one of the two members to a different part of the project so their collaboration is no longer required and delivery can resume without the friction.
- **B**: Bring both members together in a facilitated conversation focused on the working relationship they both need, not on re-debating the original design disagreement.
- **C**: Raise the pattern at the next retrospective and let the broader team help reconcile the two members' working relationship over the following sprints.
- **D**: Continue to wait on the basis that adult professionals should be given more time to repair a working relationship without direct PM intervention into the issue.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's relationship-repair principle says to facilitate a direct, focused conversation on the relationship — the design is resolved, the relationship is not. (A) works around the problem and rewards avoidance. (C) exposes a private relationship issue to the whole team. (D) lets delivery keep suffering while hoping time will fix what has already not fixed itself.
