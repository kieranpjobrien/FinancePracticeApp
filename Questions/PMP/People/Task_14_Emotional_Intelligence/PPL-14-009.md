# Question

A project manager feels a flash of frustration during a tense stakeholder meeting and is tempted to fire back at a comment that strikes them as unfair. What should they do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause, notice the reaction internally, and choose a measured response rather than reacting impulsively.
- **B**: Express the frustration plainly to keep the conversation honest and authentic.
- **C**: Step out of the meeting briefly to regain composure before the conversation continues.
- **D**: Defer to whatever the stakeholder is saying to keep the relationship intact.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The hallmark of self-regulation is putting space between stimulus and response. (A) names the internal reaction and then chooses a professional reply. (B) confuses authenticity with unfiltered reaction. (C) is sometimes useful but here is unnecessary and disruptive when a brief internal pause would do. (D) suppresses rather than manages and abandons the project's interest.
