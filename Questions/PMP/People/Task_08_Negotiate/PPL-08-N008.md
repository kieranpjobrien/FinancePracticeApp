# Question

Midway through an agile delivery, the sponsor adds a high-priority regulatory feature but will not move the fixed go-live date or add to the team. The remaining sprints are already fully committed. The regulatory feature is large. The project manager needs an agreement the sponsor will own. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Take the sponsor through the current backlog ranked by value and agree which lower-value items the regulatory feature will displace within the existing capacity.
- **B**: Work with the product owner and sponsor to define a compliant minimum slice of the regulatory feature for the go-live date, scheduling the remainder for after launch.
- **C**: Identify a roughly equal-sized lower-priority item already in the plan and propose swapping it out for the regulatory feature, presenting the trade as the cleanest one-for-one exchange.
- **D**: Add the regulatory feature to the top of the backlog and run a one-sprint spike to size it accurately before deciding with the sponsor what gets displaced.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

With date, cost, and team fixed, scope is the only lever, and the regulatory feature is large, so the sound agreement is a value-based re-prioritisation across the whole backlog that the sponsor consciously owns — this surfaces every trade-off, not just one. (B) is a reasonable technique, but committing to a "minimum compliant slice" pre-judges that the feature can be safely split and that a partial regulatory capability is acceptable at launch, which is a compliance question, not a default. (C) fixes on a single equal-sized swap before anyone has confirmed the feature's true size or that the swapped item is genuinely the lowest-value casualty, so it risks displacing the wrong work. (D) is useful when sizing is unknown, but spending a whole sprint on a spike when capacity is already maxed itself consumes the capacity the new feature needs and defers the decision the sponsor must make.
