# Question

A product owner has secured budget to bring a contractor squad onto an agile programme to lift delivery capacity. The contractor's commercial lead proposes the squad work to its own backlog and its own reporting so it can "move fast independently." The project manager is negotiating the engagement terms. What should the PM insist on in the agreement?

## Options

- **A**: The contractor squad keeps its own backlog drawn from a slice of features the product owner allocates to it each release, but integrates its output and demonstrates it at the shared review.
- **B**: The contractor squad runs its own iterations and backlog for speed, synchronising with the programme through a dedicated liaison who attends programme planning and feeds priorities back to the squad.
- **C**: The contractor squad self-organises around outcomes the product owner specifies per release and reports progress through shared programme metrics, while managing its own day-to-day backlog.
- **D**: The contractor squad pulls from the same prioritised product backlog and joins shared planning and review, with delivery accepted only as increments integrated into the programme's product.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Added capacity only creates value if it serves *one* prioritised backlog and proves itself through *integrated* increments — that keeps a single source of truth, lets the product owner re-prioritise across the whole programme each iteration, and surfaces integration risk continuously rather than late. (A) hands the squad a pre-allocated slice, which fixes scope a release ahead and prevents the product owner from re-prioritising across the combined work as things change. (B) a liaison plus a separate backlog and separate iterations creates a parallel stream that drifts between sync points; the relay is exactly where priorities and integration go stale. (C) shared metrics and per-release outcomes sound aligned, but a separately-managed backlog still lets the squad's day-to-day work diverge from the programme's live priorities. Only D keeps the work genuinely on one backlog and one definition of done.
