# Question

A PM from a direct, task-oriented culture is negotiating a delivery partnership with a supplier whose culture prioritises relationship and indirect communication. Early meetings have been polite but no commitments have emerged. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tighten the meeting agendas to force concrete decisions and list outstanding items at the end of every session with owners.
- **B**: Send a firm written proposal and ask the supplier to respond in writing to each clause so positions are clear.
- **C**: Bring a senior executive from the PM's organisation to signal importance and push the supplier towards faster closure of the agreement.
- **D**: Invest time in relationship-building activities, slow the pace of demands, and let trust develop before pressing for commitments on specific terms.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI emphasises tailoring negotiation style to cultural context; in high-context, relationship-oriented settings, trust precedes commitment and rushing destroys both. (A) imposes one culture's norms on the other and will read as pressure rather than clarity. (B) reduces a relationship-first negotiation to a clause-by-clause contest, which is unlikely to surface the supplier's real interests. (C) escalates visibility rather than building trust and can be read as coercive.
