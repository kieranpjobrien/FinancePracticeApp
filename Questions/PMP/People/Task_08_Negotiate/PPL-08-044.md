# Question

A negotiation has just concluded successfully, but the PM senses the other party agreed under time pressure and may not be fully committed in implementation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the deal as done; commitment risk is the other party's problem from this point on.
- **B**: Tear up the signed agreement entirely and reopen the whole negotiation from scratch.
- **C**: Reach back, surface any lingering concerns, and adjust terms where reasonable to secure commitment.
- **D**: Increase contractual penalties to force performance regardless of the counterparty's genuine commitment.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Agreements made under pressure are at high risk of failure in execution; PMI-aligned practice is to test commitment proactively and adjust where reasonable to convert grudging consent into genuine buy-in. (A) ignores a foreseeable execution risk the PM can mitigate now at low cost. (B) is disproportionate and may surrender hard-won terms unnecessarily. (D) substitutes coercion for commitment and predictably degrades performance.
