# Question

A PM is selecting the contract type for a data-migration package where scope is well-defined but data quality in the legacy system is poorly understood. The vendor has offered either fixed-price or time-and-materials. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take the fixed-price option so cost is locked in and the vendor carries all the delivery risk for the migration work across its duration.
- **B**: Take time-and-materials so the team pays only for hours actually worked on the dataset that emerges as the legacy system is examined properly.
- **C**: Propose a hybrid — fixed-price for the well-understood extract and load components, with a capped T&M envelope for data-quality remediation work.
- **D**: Ask the vendor to choose whichever structure they prefer so the relationship starts on a cooperative footing with the agreed commercial terms.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's guidance on contract selection is to match risk allocation to where the uncertainty actually sits; here, scope is clear but data-quality effort is not, so a hybrid isolates the unknown portion under T&M with a cap. (A) transfers a risk the vendor will simply price as a large contingency, inflating the cost. (B) hands the buyer all the data-quality risk and gives the vendor no incentive to be efficient on the known work. (D) abdicates risk-allocation choice, which is the buyer's responsibility.
