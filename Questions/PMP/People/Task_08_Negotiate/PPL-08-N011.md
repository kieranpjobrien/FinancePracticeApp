# Question

Two Scrum teams in the same release train both depend on a single shared UX designer for the next two sprints, and each product owner insists their stories cannot start without her. The designer can realistically support only one team at full capacity. The two product owners report to different business lines. As release train facilitator, how should the project manager resolve the contention?

## Options

- **A**: Convene both product owners to compare the relative value and downstream dependencies of their stories and jointly agree how the designer's time is allocated across the two sprints.
- **B**: Sequence the designer to the team whose stories unblock the most downstream work this sprint, then to the other team next sprint, and walk both product owners through the reasoning.
- **C**: Escalate the conflict to the release train's portfolio prioritisation forum, since the designer is a train-level constraint and the two business lines may not agree between themselves.
- **D**: Negotiate with a resource manager to bring in a contract designer for the two sprints, removing the contention rather than rationing the one designer between the teams.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cross-team resource contention is first a negotiation of priority between the parties who own the work, and bringing the two product owners together to weigh value and dependencies lets them reach an allocation both will support — the collaborative resolution that should be attempted before escalating or spending money. (B) proposes a specific, defensible sequencing, but the PM selecting the criterion and imposing the order pre-empts the product owners' own priority decision; it is a reasonable outcome reached the wrong way. (C) may be needed if the product owners genuinely deadlock, but jumping to escalation before they have tried to agree abdicates the facilitator's role and slows a decision they could likely make themselves. (D) solves the symptom and is worth exploring, but commits budget and onboarding time and assumes a contractor can be productive immediately, which is a fallback if negotiation and reprioritisation cannot reconcile the need.
