# Question

A construction PM is negotiating revised site hours with the workers' union after a schedule compression request from the client. The union shop steward is openly hostile. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Present the client's business case and the compressed schedule to the union and ask them to agree the new hours on that commercial basis.
- **B**: Escalate to the union regional office so the hostile steward is bypassed and a more commercially oriented negotiator is brought to the table instead.
- **C**: Offer a 10% premium on the compressed hours and let the union decide whether the financial package adequately offsets the disruption to its members.
- **D**: Listen to the steward privately, separate the interests — safety, rostering, overtime pay, respect — from positions, then frame options around those interests.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's first move in a heated negotiation is to separate people from the problem and surface interests underneath the positions, which hostility often masks. (A) advances the client's case before the union has been heard, which hardens opposition. (B) treats the elected representative as an obstacle and will damage trust with the broader workforce. (C) throws money at a position before understanding whether pay is actually the driver.
