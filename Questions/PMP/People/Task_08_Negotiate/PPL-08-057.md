# Question

A project manager is negotiating the terms of a shared services agreement for an automotive plant's new paint shop. Three stakeholders each raise a different underlying interest during the talks. For each stated interest, match the contract feature that most directly addresses it.

## Items

- **1**: The operations director fears being locked in if the supplier's service quality later declines
- **2**: The finance controller needs the annual cost to be predictable for budgeting
- **3**: The plant engineer wants assurance that response times in a line stoppage are guaranteed

## Matches

- **A**: A fixed annual charge with defined, capped indexation over the term
- **B**: Service-level commitments for breakdown response with remedies for failure to meet them
- **C**: A break clause allowing exit at defined review points if performance falls below agreed measures

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Good agreement design maps each stakeholder's real interest to the feature that satisfies it. The fear of lock-in (1) is met by a performance-linked break clause that provides a genuine exit if quality drops (C). The need for budget predictability (2) is met by a fixed charge with capped indexation, which gives a known annual figure (A). The demand for guaranteed stoppage response (3) is met by service-level commitments with remedies, which make the response time contractually enforceable (B). The discrimination lies in matching interest to mechanism: a break clause answers lock-in, not response time; an SLA answers responsiveness, not budgeting; capped pricing answers predictability, not exit.
