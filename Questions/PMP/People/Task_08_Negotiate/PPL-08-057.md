# Question

After six weeks of negotiation, the PM and a key subcontractor are deadlocked on liability caps. Both sides are frustrated and communication has become terse and defensive. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Restate the PM's organisation's position more firmly and set a hard deadline after which the subcontractor will be replaced by an alternative provider.
- **B**: Concede on the liability cap to close the deal quickly and recover the relationship, then document the additional risk in the project's risk register.
- **C**: Escalate the clause to both organisations' legal counsel and have them negotiate the cap directly without any further PM involvement in the discussion.
- **D**: Suggest a short cooling-off period, then return with a neutral facilitator to reset the process, clarify interests, and explore options beyond the clause.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's approach to deadlock is process-first: change the dynamic before changing the terms, often via cooling-off and neutral facilitation, which lets interests re-emerge. (A) hardens positions and may destroy value already built. (B) trades a commercial term to rescue a relationship problem, which misdiagnoses the issue. (C) removes the PM from a commercial conversation the PM is accountable for and lets lawyers negotiate business terms in isolation.
