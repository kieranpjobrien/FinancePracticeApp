# Question

Two stakeholders with equal authority hold incompatible requirements that both believe are non-negotiable. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Decide for the stakeholders and move on so that the project does not stall further.
- **B**: Facilitate a joint conversation on the underlying interests and search for options that satisfy both.
- **C**: Escalate immediately to the project sponsor for a binding ruling on the conflicting requirements.
- **D**: Implement whichever requirement is cheaper to deliver and use cost as the objective tiebreaker.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI casts the PM as facilitator, not arbiter, and most apparent stakeholder impasses dissolve when the conversation moves from positions to interests. (A) usurps a decision the PM does not own. (C) skips the facilitation step the PM is paid to attempt before escalating. (D) introduces an arbitrary criterion that has nothing to do with project value or stakeholder interests.
