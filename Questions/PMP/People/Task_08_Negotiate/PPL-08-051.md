# Question

Mid-way through a fixed-budget Scrum programme, the sponsor adds two new "must-have" features without dropping anything. The team is already at capacity and velocity is stable. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team to absorb the new work through overtime and unpaid sprints so the sponsor's priorities are all met within the existing budget envelope.
- **B**: Facilitate a trade-off conversation with the sponsor using the current backlog to decide what will move, defer or drop to make room.
- **C**: Escalate the capacity problem to the steering committee and request that the sponsor's scope additions be formally overruled by programme governance today.
- **D**: Accept the new items into the next sprint and flag the resulting schedule slippage in the following status report for stakeholder visibility.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's iron-triangle thinking applies inside the organisation too: with fixed budget and capacity, added scope must be traded against existing scope, and the PM's job is to facilitate that trade openly with the sponsor. (A) sacrifices sustainable pace and the team's wellbeing, which PMI explicitly prohibits. (C) escalates before negotiating with the sponsor, bypassing the accountable decision-maker. (D) silently accepts scope creep and lets the schedule slip without informed consent.
