# Question

In a scope negotiation, a key stakeholder states firmly, "the full reporting module must ship in phase one." Delivering it whole in phase one would push out two other committed features. In an earlier hallway remark the stakeholder mentioned an external audit due shortly after the planned phase-one date. The project manager wants a durable agreement, not a concession that unravels later. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose delivering the audit-related reports in phase one and the remainder of the module in phase two, framing it as the way to protect both the audit and the other committed features.
- **B**: Ask the stakeholder to walk through what specifically depends on the reporting module by the phase-one date and what would happen if any part slipped, so the underlying need is clear before any options are weighed.
- **C**: Offer to bring forward only the highest-effort reporting components into phase one, since those carry the most schedule risk, and defer the simpler ones.
- **D**: Ask the stakeholder to rank the individual reports within the module by priority, then commit to delivering as many of the top-ranked ones as phase-one capacity allows.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM has a *hypothesis* about the interest (the audit) but not confirmation, and the stated position is "the whole module." Surfacing exactly what depends on the deadline tests whether the real need is the audit, the full module, or something else — and a durable agreement has to be built on the confirmed interest, not an inferred one. (A) is a strong solution but commits to a specific split built on an assumption about the audit that has not been verified; if the real driver is different, the deal is fragile. (C) optimises for the PM's schedule-risk concern rather than the stakeholder's need, and may deliver exactly the reports the audit does not require. (D) is a reasonable prioritisation move, but ranking the reports presupposes the module is divisible in a way that serves the underlying need — which is unknown until the need itself is surfaced.
