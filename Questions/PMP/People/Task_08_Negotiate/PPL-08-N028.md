# Question

Midway through a fixed-price building-automation project, the client's facilities lead asks the project manager to add occupancy sensors to two floors not in the original contract. The client says the sensors are needed for an energy-reporting deadline three weeks away. The relationship is good and the PM wants to keep it that way. What is the most appropriate first action?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the request with the client's contract owner (not just the facilities lead) to confirm there is authority and budget for additional scope before spending effort estimating it.
- **B**: Start the impact assessment immediately and, given the three-week deadline, begin procuring the sensors in parallel so the work can proceed the moment a change order is signed.
- **C**: Offer to fold the sensors into the existing scope at no extra charge in exchange for the client agreeing to a short extension on the overall completion date.
- **D**: Acknowledge the request, confirm it is outside the agreed scope, and have the team assess the cost and schedule impact so a change order can be put to the client before any work is committed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

New scope on a fixed-price agreement runs through a change order, and the first step is to acknowledge the request, establish it is out of scope, and assess the impact so the client can decide on a properly costed basis — this is responsive without committing the team or the commercials prematurely. (A) is sensible diligence, but verifying the requester's authority is something the PM can do alongside scoping the change; making it a precondition that stalls the assessment delays a time-pressured client unnecessarily and the facilities lead is a reasonable entry point. (B) commits real money on procurement before the change is approved, exposing the project if the client declines. (C) trades away margin and negotiates a schedule concession before the impact is even understood — a deal struck blind, and the deadline pressure makes a free addition a poor precedent.
