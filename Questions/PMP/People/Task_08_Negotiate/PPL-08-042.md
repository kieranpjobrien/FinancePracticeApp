# Question

On an agile data-platform delivery, a specialist database engineer the team relies on is shared with a separate operations group, and her functional manager has just pulled her to 50% availability with no notice, threatening the next two sprints. The product owner wants the project manager to "get her back to full time." The functional manager says operations has a live incident and cannot release her. The project manager is meeting the functional manager to settle the arrangement. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate a time-boxed split tied to the incident — agreed reduced availability until operations closes it, with a defined date to return to full allocation and a named backup for the gap.
- **B**: Escalate immediately to the sponsor to have the engineer's full-time allocation to the project reinstated, since the resource was committed in the project plan.
- **C**: Accept the 50% allocation indefinitely and rescope the next two sprints to whatever the reduced capacity can deliver, protecting the relationship with operations.
- **D**: Ask the functional manager to fund a contractor to cover the operations incident so the engineer can return to the project at full time as originally planned.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The functional manager has a legitimate, bounded reason (a live incident), so the durable agreement ties the reduced availability to that cause and fixes the conditions for return, while covering the immediate gap — addressing both groups' needs. (B) treats a genuine operational emergency as a queue-jumping contest and burns the cross-team relationship the project depends on. (C) over-concedes by accepting an open-ended reduction when the trigger is temporary, giving away capacity the project may not need to lose. (D) dictates how the functional manager should solve their own problem and presumes a budget the PM does not control, which is more likely to harden the other side than reach agreement.
