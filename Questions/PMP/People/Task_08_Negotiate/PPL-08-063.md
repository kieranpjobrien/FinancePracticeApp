# Question

A contractor has submitted a variation claim worth 8% of the contract value, citing client-side delays in approvals. The PM's team believes some of the delay is genuinely client-side and some is the contractor's own mobilisation issue. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the claim in full and require the contractor to proceed on the original contract while continuing to work through the remaining scope items.
- **B**: Reconstruct the approval and mobilisation timelines with both sides, apportion responsibility using the evidence, and negotiate the claim on that factual basis.
- **C**: Pay the claim in full to protect the relationship and avoid a contractual dispute that could stall the project at a critical delivery stage.
- **D**: Refer the claim to external adjudication immediately so an independent body decides liability without consuming more project team time or effort.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's approach to variations is fact-based: reconstruct the timeline, apportion cause using objective evidence, and negotiate from that position. (A) denies a partially legitimate claim and invites escalation. (C) concedes without evidence and sets a precedent for future inflated claims. (D) goes to adjudication before the parties have attempted a fact-based resolution, wasting money and relationship capital.
