# Question

A financial-services regulator has informally signalled that a new control, not yet in the written rulebook, is expected to be in place by go-live. Adding it consumes 15% of remaining budget. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Add the control to the scope immediately and absorb the cost, since regulator expectations effectively function as compliance obligations regardless of written status.
- **B**: Ignore the informal signal until the rulebook is formally amended and the control becomes a written regulatory requirement for the wider industry.
- **C**: Seek written clarification from the regulator on scope and timing, then bring evidence to the sponsor to agree a compliance position and change.
- **D**: Negotiate with the regulator directly for a lighter version of the control that fits within existing budget without requiring any sponsor-level conversation first.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to treat regulatory expectations as serious input but to drive decisions through evidence and accountable governance, not oral hints. (A) commits a material spend on an unconfirmed requirement without sponsor approval. (B) ignores a credible compliance signal and risks a go-live refusal. (D) negotiates with a regulator before the organisation has agreed a position, which is outside the PM's authority and could create an undertaking the organisation cannot keep.
