# Question

A customer has raised a change request and is arguing it should have been included in the original scope, so no additional charge applies. The PM believes it is genuinely new scope. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the change outright and require a new statement of work be raised for it.
- **B**: Absorb the change entirely at no additional cost to preserve the customer relationship.
- **C**: Refer the dispute immediately to legal counsel to determine the contractually correct interpretation.
- **D**: Walk through the original requirements with the customer and negotiate a fair settlement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Most scope disputes turn on ambiguity in the original documents, and PMI's first move is a joint review that grounds the conversation in the agreed text rather than each side's recollection. (A) is a positional refusal that may be wrong on the facts and damages the relationship. (B) sets a precedent that every disputed change is free and undermines change control. (C) escalates to legal before either party has tried to talk it through.
