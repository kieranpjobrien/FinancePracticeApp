# Question

A PM is preparing to negotiate a two-year managed-services contract with a vendor. The sponsor tells the PM to "just close it this week" and the legal team is unavailable until next Monday. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Push the negotiation out a week so the PM, sponsor and legal can align on objectives, BATNA and walk-away criteria before offers.
- **B**: Open talks immediately using the sponsor's directive as the mandate and bring legal in once commercial terms are settled between the parties.
- **C**: Delegate the commercial conversation to procurement and ask them to surface any issues to the PM and the sponsor as they arise.
- **D**: Send the vendor the standard template contract and ask them to mark up anything they cannot accept before the formal meeting begins.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats preparation — interests, BATNA, reservation point, authority — as the largest single determinant of negotiation outcomes; rushing in without it trades a week for years of contract pain. (B) opens without a mandate or legal review, risking unenforceable or misaligned terms. (C) delegates a commercial decision without clarifying what "good" looks like, and still skips preparation. (D) anchors on the PM's template before understanding vendor interests, which is positional rather than interest-based.
