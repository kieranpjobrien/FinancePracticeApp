# Question

After a constructive two-hour session, a project manager and a key materials supplier reach a verbal agreement on revised delivery dates and a small price concession for a school construction project. Everyone leaves the room satisfied. What should the project manager do next to protect the agreement?

## Options

- **A**: Promptly document the agreed terms and circulate them to the supplier for confirmation, so both sides share the same written record.
- **B**: Wait to see whether the supplier honours the first revised delivery before committing anything to writing, to avoid appearing distrustful so early.
- **C**: Rely on the goodwill built during the session, since formalising a friendly understanding risks making the relationship feel adversarial.
- **D**: Ask the supplier to draft and own the written version of the agreement, since they offered the concession and should record their own commitment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A negotiated agreement is only durable once it is captured and confirmed by both parties, so documenting the terms and circulating them for agreement is the immediate next step. (B) leaves the understanding undocumented through the very period it is most likely to be misremembered or disputed. (C) mistakes goodwill for a record; memories diverge, and a written summary protects the relationship rather than souring it. (D) needlessly cedes control of the record and risks a version that omits or reframes terms the PM understood differently.
