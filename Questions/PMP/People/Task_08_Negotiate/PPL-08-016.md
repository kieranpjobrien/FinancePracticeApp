# Question

A vendor with unique technical capabilities insists on full payment upfront with no performance guarantees. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept because there is no qualified alternative vendor and the capability is essential to the project.
- **B**: Reject the vendor and rescope the project around capabilities the wider market can provide on standard terms.
- **C**: Negotiate milestone payments, escrow, or performance bonds that protect the buyer with acceptable cash flow.
- **D**: Accept the terms but quietly build an internal team to replicate the capability as an unstated contingency.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Uniqueness limits leverage but does not eliminate it; structuring the deal around shared interests (cash flow for the vendor, protection for the buyer) is the PMI-aligned move and is usually accepted because vendors expect pushback. (A) transfers the full performance risk onto the buyer with no mitigation. (B) discards the best technical fit without first testing whether better terms can be negotiated. (D) commits significant hidden cost while still accepting the unfavourable terms and creates transparency issues with the vendor.
