# Question

A distributed Scrum team spans Sydney and London. The product owner demands a daily standup at 08:00 Sydney, which is 23:00 London. The London developers are exhausted after four sprints at this cadence. What should the PM negotiate?

## Options

- **A**: Move the standup to a time within both sites' working hours, reshape it as async where overlap isn't possible, and protect sustainable pace.
- **B**: Rotate the meeting pain so each site takes the unsocial slot every second sprint, feeling the burden has been shared equally between the two.
- **C**: Split the team into two standups and have the PM or Scrum Master summarise each site's update to the other team for cross-site visibility.
- **D**: Maintain the 08:00 Sydney slot but compensate London developers with extra annual leave to offset the sustained out-of-hours attendance commitment here.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI and the Scrum Guide both treat sustainable pace as non-negotiable; the PM's job is to negotiate structures that protect it, not distribute the damage. (B) just spreads the harm rather than designing it out. (C) fragments the one-team principle and creates two parallel narratives that the PM then has to reconcile. (D) buys off exhaustion with leave instead of fixing the structural cause.
