# Question

An agile team building a public-sector benefits portal has a legally fixed launch date that cannot move. Partway through, the sponsor adds several "essential" new requirements, and the team's velocity makes clear that the full backlog plus the additions cannot all be delivered by the date. The project manager is meeting the sponsor to agree what launch will actually contain. What is the best basis for that negotiation?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the sponsor to agree the minimum set of features the launch genuinely must contain to be lawful and usable, deferring the rest to a fast follow-on release.
- **B**: Commit the team to the full expanded backlog and protect the date by mandating sustained overtime across the remaining sprints.
- **C**: Tell the sponsor the new requirements cannot be accepted because the backlog was already sized to the fixed date.
- **D**: Hold all the original scope, drop the sponsor's new requirements without discussion, and deliver exactly what was first agreed by the date.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

With the date immovable and capacity finite, the negotiation is about scope, so agreeing the genuinely essential launch set and sequencing the rest into a follow-on release serves both the legal deadline and the sponsor's priorities. (B) protects the date by burning the team through sustained overtime, which erodes quality and sustainability and tends to fail anyway. (C) refuses the sponsor outright and forecloses the chance that the additions are more important than items already in the backlog. (D) unilaterally discards the sponsor's input without weighing it, which is just the mirror error and ignores that priorities may genuinely have changed.
