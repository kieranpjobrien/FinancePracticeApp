# Question

In principled negotiation, what is the difference between a position and an interest?

## Options

- **A**: A position is what someone says they want; an interest is the underlying need behind it.
- **B**: A position is the opening statement; an interest is the final agreed compromise between parties.
- **C**: A position is held by individuals; an interest is held collectively by organisations as a whole.
- **D**: A position is always documented in writing; an interest is only communicated verbally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The position is the surface demand ("the report must be on my desk by Friday"); the interest is the why behind it ("I need to brief the board on Monday"). Working at the level of interests opens up creative solutions that pure positional bargaining cannot reach. (B) confuses sequencing in a negotiation with the position/interest distinction. (C) and (D) invent unrelated criteria that have nothing to do with the concept.
