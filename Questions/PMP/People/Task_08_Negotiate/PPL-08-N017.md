# Question

Partway through a tense negotiation over delivery milestones, the supplier's lead becomes visibly angry, raises their voice, and accuses the firm of repeatedly moving the goalposts. The project manager believes the milestones have in fact been consistent, but still needs a workable agreement and a continuing relationship. What is the best immediate response?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the supplier's frustration, propose a short break to let the heat dissipate, and resume by separating the relationship grievance from the substantive milestone questions.
- **B**: Stay engaged and ask the supplier to point to the specific milestones they feel were changed, working through each one against the record so the substance is addressed directly.
- **C**: Acknowledge the frustration but calmly hold the firm's position, redirecting at once to the documented milestone history to show the goalposts have not actually moved.
- **D**: Suggest pausing the bilateral talks and reconvening with both organisations' sponsors present, so a fresh forum can reset the milestone discussion on a calmer footing.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When emotion spikes, the effective sequence is to acknowledge the feeling, create space for it to cool, and only then separate the people/relationship problem from the substantive issues so bargaining can continue on the merits — addressing the emotion first is what makes the substantive discussion productive. (B) goes straight to the substance while the supplier is still heated; asking them to defend their accusation point by point in that state tends to deepen the confrontation rather than resolve it. (C) couples a brief acknowledgement with an immediate move to prove the supplier wrong using the record, which, however factually correct, reads as rebutting them mid-anger and is likely to escalate. (D) over-engineers the moment by escalating to a sponsor-level forum for an outburst the PM can defuse directly, which can embarrass the supplier's lead and signals the PM cannot manage the room.
