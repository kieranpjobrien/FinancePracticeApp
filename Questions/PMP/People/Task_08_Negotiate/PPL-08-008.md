# Question

A senior stakeholder with budget authority insists on adding three new features without changing the timeline. The PM has analysed the impact and knows the team cannot deliver everything in the original schedule. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the new scope to keep the stakeholder happy and have the team absorb the extra work.
- **B**: Hold firm to the original scope and decline the request for additional features outright.
- **C**: Present quantified trade-off options — timeline, resources, or scope — and let the stakeholder decide.
- **D**: Verbally agree but quietly de-prioritise the new features in the team's actual sprint plan.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PM's job is to enable informed decisions by the people with the authority to make them — the iron triangle does not bend just because someone with authority wishes it. (A) sets the project up to miss commitments and burns out the team. (B) refuses without offering options and surrenders the chance to find a workable answer. (D) is dishonest, breaches the PMI Code of Ethics, and destroys trust the moment it is discovered.
