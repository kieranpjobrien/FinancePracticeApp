# Question

At the opening of a licensing negotiation, the vendor's lead opens with a number 40% above the PM's budget and justifies it with list-price references. The PM's team is visibly rattled by the anchor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the figure without conceding its legitimacy, pivot to interests and objective criteria such as comparable deals, and let those reset the range.
- **B**: Accept the anchor as the starting point and negotiate percentage concessions from there to reach a workable figure for both sides.
- **C**: Counter immediately with a number 40% below budget to offset the anchor and reset the negotiating range around the PM's target.
- **D**: Call a break, brief the team to ignore the number, and return with the PM's originally prepared opening position unchanged from the plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's interest-based approach says a high anchor is defused by shifting the conversation to objective criteria and underlying interests, not by trading counter-anchors. (B) legitimises the anchor and guarantees the final price is dragged upward. (C) matches aggression with aggression and entrenches positional bargaining. (D) ignores the anchor's psychological effect on the team rather than directly addressing it, and misses the chance to reframe the discussion.
