# Question

A project manager is about to enter a high-value negotiation with a key equipment supplier for a water-treatment plant upgrade. The sponsor has said only "get us a good deal." During preparation the project manager realises the likely settlement could exceed the spending authority the PM personally holds, and concessions on liability terms may also be on the table. What should the project manager do before the negotiation begins?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the negotiation and commit to whatever deal looks best, then seek the sponsor's ratification afterwards if the figure exceeds the PM's authority.
- **B**: Agree explicit boundaries with the sponsor — the price ceiling the PM may commit to and which terms require referral back — so the PM negotiates within a clear mandate.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to attend the negotiation personally so that any decision beyond the PM's authority can be taken live in the room.
- **D**: Negotiate only up to the PM's own authority limit and let the deal fall through if a good settlement would require exceeding it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negotiating without knowing the limits of one's own mandate risks committing to terms the PM cannot actually authorise, so agreeing the price ceiling and the referral points with the sponsor first is the essential preparation (B). (A) commits the organisation beyond the PM's authority and treats the sponsor's approval as a rubber stamp, which can collapse the deal or damage credibility if refused. (C) drags the sponsor into the room unnecessarily and may not even be possible; a clear delegated mandate is the normal mechanism. (D) lets a good outcome fail purely because the PM did not establish the authority the situation plainly required.
