# Question

A project manager is negotiating a software licence. The PM's maximum approved budget is £90k. The vendor opens at £120k, and through discussion the PM judges the vendor's true floor is around £85k. The PM's walk-away alternative — building a reduced version in-house — would cost the equivalent of £95k. The PM wants the best deal without losing one that is genuinely available. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Disclose the £90k budget cap to the vendor as a hard constraint and ask them to land the deal at or under it, using the cap to close quickly within the overlap.
- **B**: Treat £85k as the realistic target, open at exactly that figure as a fair, fact-based offer, and decline to move up, since the budget room above it is the PM's to keep.
- **C**: Make a reasoned opening counter below the vendor's assessed floor — around £80k, supported by specific justifications — to anchor low, then settle within the £85k–£90k overlap.
- **D**: Conclude the in-house build at £95k is the better path because it removes vendor dependency, and use that as the reason to walk away rather than settle even within the overlap.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

An overlap exists (roughly £85k–£90k), and the in-house alternative at £95k sits *above* the vendor's likely floor, so it confirms a vendor deal is worth pursuing rather than serving as a tight walk-away. The strongest play is to anchor with a justified counter just below the vendor's floor and then settle inside the zone — anchoring shapes where in the £85k–£90k band the deal lands. (B) opening at the vendor's own floor with no headroom leaves no room to be negotiated upward and surrenders the anchoring advantage; a single fixed number invites deadlock if the vendor's floor is even slightly higher than assessed. (A) revealing the £90k cap hands the vendor a target to converge on from above, forfeiting the lower end of the zone. (D) misreads the alternative: at £95k the in-house build is *worse* than an achievable ~£88k licence, so walking away destroys value that is on the table.
