# Question

A project depends on a partner organisation outside the PM's company to provide test data and a shared environment. There is no commercial contract between the organisations, but the dependency is critical. The PM is drafting a memorandum of understanding to anchor the relationship. All five elements below are reasonable to include. Which TWO are the highest priority to capture? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: The specific deliverables each party will provide, with the timing and the named contacts accountable for each.
- **B**: How the shared test data will be handled, protected, and disposed of, including any confidentiality and privacy obligations.
- **C**: A statement of the shared objective and the mutual benefit each organisation expects from the collaboration.
- **D**: How issues and proposed changes to the arrangement will be raised, reviewed, and resolved between the two organisations.
- **E**: The process and notice period for either party to wind down or exit the arrangement if circumstances change.

## Answer

A, D

## Explanation

A non-contractual MoU works by making *mutual commitments* and the *means to manage them* explicit: defining who delivers what and by when (A) gives the relationship something concrete to run on, and an issue/change mechanism (D) keeps it workable as reality shifts — without these two the MoU is an expression of intent with nothing operable behind it. (B) data handling, (E) exit terms, and (C) the shared-objective statement are all sensible and worth including, but each is secondary: data protection matters yet supports the deliverables rather than defining them, exit terms govern an end-state that may never arise, and the objective statement sets context but creates no actionable commitment. When prioritising what the MoU must contain to function, A and D come first.
