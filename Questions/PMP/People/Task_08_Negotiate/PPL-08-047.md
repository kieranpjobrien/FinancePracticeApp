# Question

On a hybrid programme, the functional manager has pulled two senior engineers off the critical path to cover a production incident. The PM needs them back within 48 hours to protect a regulatory milestone. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor immediately and request that the functional manager be directed to return the engineers to the programme without delay.
- **B**: Meet the functional manager to understand the incident, share the regulatory impact, and jointly agree a return plan that protects both commitments.
- **C**: Backfill the two engineers with contractors from the approved panel and charge the cost to the incident rather than to the programme.
- **D**: Re-sequence the critical path around the absence and accept the schedule impact until the incident team fully releases the engineers back.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to negotiate resource conflicts directly with functional managers first, on the basis of shared interests and facts, before escalation. (A) escalates before the peer-level conversation has even happened. (C) commits spend and changes the delivery model without first trying to resolve at source. (D) silently absorbs a regulatory risk rather than surfacing and negotiating it.
