# Question

After a long three-party negotiation, the parties verbally agree a deal in the room. Two parties are already celebrating and one proposes leaving documentation to "the lawyers next week." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Join the celebration and let legal draft the terms next week while the commercial goodwill from the intense session is still fresh in everyone's memory.
- **B**: Send each party's legal team separately what the PM understands was agreed so their own counsel drafts first, reflecting each party's preferred framing.
- **C**: Wait for the other side to circulate their version first so the PM can respond rather than anchor the written record on the PM's own interpretation.
- **D**: Capture a written summary of the agreed points in the room, confirm each party acknowledges it, and schedule legal drafting before understanding drifts apart.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's rule on negotiation closure is that what is written down immediately becomes the shared memory; gaps of days between verbal and written agreement regularly produce three different versions. (A) delays capture until memories have diverged. (B) runs three separate drafting processes that will almost certainly disagree. (C) cedes framing and is a positional move disguised as caution, not a shared-understanding move.
