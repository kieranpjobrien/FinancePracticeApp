# Question

After a constructive meeting, a project manager and a supplier's account lead have verbally agreed a revised delivery date, a reduced first batch, and a shared weekly checkpoint. Both shook hands and the account lead said he would "make it happen". The changes affect the terms of a signed supply contract. As the meeting breaks up, what should the project manager do first to give the agreement the best chance of holding?

## Options

- **A**: Write up the agreed points in a brief summary, send it to the account lead, and ask him to confirm it captures what was agreed before either side acts on it.
- **B**: Note the agreed points in the project's own decision log and issues register so there is a dated internal record, and proceed on that basis.
- **C**: Pass the agreed points to contracts staff to raise a formal variation to the supply agreement straight away, so the new terms are legally binding before anything else happens.
- **D**: Confirm the substance with the account lead now, then update the schedule and brief the team so the revised date takes effect without losing momentum.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A negotiated agreement should be captured and confirmed with the other party so both sides share one understanding before anyone relies on it — a short summary sent to the account lead for confirmation does exactly that and is the right first move. The distractors are all reasonable follow-on actions, which is what makes the timing the discriminator. (B) creates a record but only a one-sided one; an internal log the supplier has never seen does nothing to align the two parties' understanding. (C) will likely be needed because the agreement changes contract terms, but raising a formal variation before the parties have even confirmed the plain substance is premature and can feel adversarial after a cooperative meeting — confirmation comes first, then the variation. (D) acts on the agreement internally before the other side has confirmed it, committing the schedule to an understanding not yet verified. A precedes and enables B, C, and D.
