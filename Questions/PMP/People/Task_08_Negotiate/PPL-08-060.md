# Question

The PM is negotiating the service-level agreement between project delivery and the operations team that will run the product. Operations is insisting on 99.95% uptime; delivery has designed for 99.5%. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Split the difference at 99.725% uptime so both sides feel acknowledged and an agreement can be reached without any further architectural or costing investigation.
- **B**: Side with operations, since they own the service post go-live and an uptime commitment is ultimately theirs to meet with the production platform anyway.
- **C**: Surface the cost, architecture and business-value implications of each SLA band, let the sponsor choose the tier, and lock it into the agreement.
- **D**: Sign at 99.5% now and raise the target through a post-go-live improvement programme once operations has baselined the actual live system performance levels.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's objective-criteria principle says SLA negotiations should be resolved by evidence about cost, risk and business value, not by splitting or deferring to seniority. (A) invents a middle number with no engineering or cost basis. (B) lets the operating party set architectural requirements after the fact, bypassing sponsor trade-off authority. (D) signs to the lower tier and commits to a future uplift without agreed funding or scope.
