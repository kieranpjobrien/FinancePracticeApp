# Question

A critical supplier has delivered poorly across an 18-month contract, but they remain the best-positioned partner for an upcoming phase two. The PM is planning contract close-out and phase-two sourcing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the performance issues accurately, share the evaluation with the supplier, and negotiate phase-two terms that explicitly address the root causes directly.
- **B**: Close the contract out quietly with a neutral performance record to preserve the option of reappointing the supplier for the phase two activities ahead.
- **C**: Close the contract out with a strongly worded performance record and move directly to open sourcing for phase two, to signal displeasure commercially.
- **D**: Skip formal close-out and roll straight into phase-two negotiations using the existing master agreement to save time and to preserve commercial momentum.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects accurate performance records and honest feedback as both an organisational-learning and ethics requirement; reusing a supplier without addressing the root causes guarantees repeat failure. (B) misrepresents performance to protect an option, which is a record-keeping ethics issue. (C) uses documentation as punishment rather than learning, and forecloses the best-placed option without analysis. (D) skips a required step in the project lifecycle and loses the improvement opportunity.
