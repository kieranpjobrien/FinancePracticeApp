# Question

A project manager is negotiating with a software vendor whose initial price exceeds the project budget by 20%. The product is the best technical fit and the vendor has hinted at flexibility. What approach should the PM take?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the initial price and request a budget increase from the sponsor.
- **B**: Counter at the budget figure and indicate the PM will evaluate alternative vendors if the gap is not closed.
- **C**: Explore mutual-interest options such as multi-year commitment, phased licensing, or referenceability in exchange for a better price.
- **D**: Split the difference between the budget and the vendor's quote and propose that as a final offer.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI favours interest-based negotiation that creates value for both parties rather than positional bargaining. Surfacing what the vendor actually values (volume, term length, reference rights) often produces a better deal than haggling on price alone. (A) leaves money on the table the vendor has signalled they will move on. (B) is a positional, low-trust play that may damage a relationship the project will rely on. (D) is the classic split-the-difference compromise that captures none of the joint value still available.
