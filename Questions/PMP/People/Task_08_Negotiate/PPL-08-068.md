# Question

A project manager is preparing to negotiate a contentious cutover window with a client's IT operations team, who fear the cutover will disrupt their year-end processing. Prior cutover talks with this team have hardened into standoffs, and the project manager wants this one to reach an agreement both sides will stand behind. Which preparations best support that aim? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Identify the operations team's underlying interest — protecting year-end processing — beneath their stated position of refusing the proposed window.
- **B**: Develop two or three cutover options that could each protect year-end processing while still meeting the project's deadline.
- **C**: Agree in advance what objective basis — system load data, processing calendars — will be used to judge which window is genuinely safe.
- **D**: Settle on the window the project needs and marshal the strongest evidence and arguments to win the operations team over to it in the meeting.
- **E**: Work out the project's own fallback if no window is agreed, and prepare to put it on the table early so the operations team grasp the cost of holding out.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Interest-based negotiation rests on separating positions from underlying interests (A), inventing multiple options for mutual gain (B), and grounding the decision in objective criteria both sides accept (C) — these preparations make agreement more likely and the result more durable. The distractors are realistic preparation, which is what makes the choice require judgement. (D) sounds diligent but is positional bargaining dressed as persuasion: fixing on one window and building a case to win the other side over leaves no room for the options in B and tends to reproduce the standoffs the project manager is trying to escape. (E) is legitimate — knowing your fallback is sound preparation — but planning to brandish it early to pressure the other side works against the cooperative, agreement-seeking tone the situation calls for; a fallback is best held in reserve, not led with.
