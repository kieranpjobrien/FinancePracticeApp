# Question

A business stakeholder insists the acceptance criteria for a release include "the system feels responsive." The development team says this cannot be tested objectively. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the stakeholder's wording and have the team use professional judgement to decide when the feel criterion has been met during testing.
- **B**: Reject the wording as untestable and require the stakeholder to submit revised acceptance criteria that the team considers objectively verifiable.
- **C**: Facilitate a working session to translate "feels responsive" into measurable criteria — response-time thresholds and user-perceived latency — that both sides sign.
- **D**: Split the criterion into a testable portion for this release and a qualitative portion to be reassessed at the next steering committee meeting.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's approach to acceptance criteria is to translate stakeholder intent into measurable, testable language through joint facilitation — neither side is wrong, they are speaking different registers. (A) leaves a subjective criterion as a dispute waiting for UAT. (B) bounces the problem back to the stakeholder and loses the PM's facilitation value. (D) preserves a subjective criterion and defers the translation work the project actually needs now.
