# Question

A complex multi-party commercial negotiation must be conducted entirely over video conferencing because travel is not feasible. Previous remote sessions have stalled on misunderstandings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push for an in-person session regardless of the travel constraint so parties can read each other and build trust through shared physical presence on the day.
- **B**: Tailor the process explicitly for remote — smaller sessions, a neutral facilitator, structured agendas, pre-reads, shared document editing, and named roles on each side.
- **C**: Rely on longer, more frequent video sessions and written follow-ups after each call to bridge the gaps the remote format keeps producing between the meetings.
- **D**: Conduct all substantive discussions one-to-one over video and reserve multi-party sessions purely for confirming ceremonially what has already been privately agreed between parties.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle applies to negotiation formats: remote negotiation works when the process is explicitly redesigned for it, not when an in-person process is simply put behind a camera. (A) ignores the stated travel constraint. (C) adds volume to a broken format rather than redesigning it. (D) hides the multi-party dynamic and replaces open negotiation with a series of bilateral agreements, which in multi-party settings creates inconsistency and later disputes.
