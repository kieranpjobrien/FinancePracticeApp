# Question

A project manager is about to negotiate a one-year support renewal. The renewal budget is £100k. The firm could instead build the capability in-house for about £110k per year, all-in. From past dealings the PM judges the vendor will not realistically drop below roughly £95k. The vendor opens the meeting at £125k. For each of the three opening moves below, match it to the assessment that best describes whether the PM should make it.

## Items

- **1**: Signal early that the firm has a viable in-house option costing around £110k
- **2**: State that £100k is the firm's absolute final figure and talks end above it
- **3**: Counter the £125k opening at around £96k and justify it on cost grounds

## Matches

- **A**: Unwise as stated — it draws the firm's own ceiling below where a deal is achievable and risks ending talks inside the zone where agreement exists
- **B**: Sound — it anchors near the vendor's likely floor and keeps the discussion within the realistic settlement range
- **C**: Sound — used to inform, not threaten, it strengthens the firm's position because the alternative is real and roughly priced

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

This turns the BATNA/ZOPA/reservation-point concepts into live tactical choices rather than definitions. Referencing the in-house alternative (1) is sound when used to inform the discussion, because it is a genuine, costed walk-away that legitimately strengthens the firm's hand (C). Declaring £100k as an absolute final figure (2) is unwise: the vendor's floor is around £95k and the firm's own walk-away is about £110k, so a deal almost certainly exists between roughly £95k and £108–110k — fixing the line at £100k threatens to kill an agreement that sits inside the zone of possible agreement and throws away value the firm could capture (A). Countering at about £96k (3) is the strong move: it anchors just above the vendor's likely reservation point and is defensible on cost grounds, pulling the settlement toward the firm's end of the range (B). The discrimination is not "what does BATNA mean" but "given these numbers, which move helps and which one quietly forfeits a deal."
