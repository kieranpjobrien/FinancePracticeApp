# Question

In a weak matrix organisation, a functional manager agreed in a corridor conversation to release a specialist database engineer for a six-week phase, but has since kept assigning her to operational tickets, and the phase starts in two days. The functional manager is dealing with a backlog spike and is not refusing outright — the commitment was simply never made concrete. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the functional manager to convert the understanding into a specific agreement — exact dates, the percentage of the engineer's time, and how operational tickets will be covered — and record it for both to acknowledge.
- **B**: Ask the engineer directly what her actual availability looks like over the next six weeks, then build the phase plan around the time she can realistically give.
- **C**: Bring the sponsor the original commitment and the current ticket assignments, and ask the sponsor to confirm the project's priority against operations for this phase.
- **D**: Offer to fund contractor cover for the operational ticket backlog so the functional manager loses nothing by releasing the engineer, then confirm her availability on that basis.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root problem is that the agreement was never made concrete enough to act on, so the immediate negotiation is to re-engage the functional manager and turn a vague understanding into a specific commitment that also addresses the competing operational load — without which the same erosion continues. (B) gathers useful data but lets the engineer, who does not own the allocation decision, effectively set the agreement, and skips the manager who controls her time. (C) escalates a priority conflict the PM has not yet tried to resolve directly with the one person who can fix it, and in a weak matrix invites a turf fight before negotiation is exhausted. (D) addresses the functional manager's real constraint and may be worth offering, but it commits project budget to solve a problem a concrete agreement might resolve for nothing, so it is a fallback rather than the first move — and buying backfill before even pinning down the terms still leaves the allocation undefined.
