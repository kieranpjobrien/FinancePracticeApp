# Question

On a fully remote 8-person machine-learning team, half the engineers consistently keep their cameras off and rarely speak in ceremonies. Engagement scores are dropping and the two most vocal engineers are now effectively driving every decision. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a mandatory cameras-on policy for team meetings so that engagement can be observed and the quieter engineers are required to participate visually in decisions.
- **B**: Meet each quieter engineer one-on-one to understand what is driving camera-off behaviour and low participation, then adjust the ceremony design with the team based on what you learn.
- **C**: Continue the current meeting format but follow up every session with a written summary that asks for input from each team member so that quieter voices are captured afterwards.
- **D**: Replace the two most vocal engineers on the core decision forum with rotating members from the rest of the team so that decision influence is spread more evenly across the group.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) diagnoses before intervening — camera-off and silence have many different causes (burnout, environment, cultural norm, exclusion) and the fix depends on the cause. This is the PMI listening-first leadership pattern. (A) imposes a rule without understanding and can feel like surveillance, worsening disengagement. (C) routes around the problem rather than fixing it and embeds a two-tier participation norm. (D) punishes vocal engineers for behaviour the PM has allowed to become the default and does not address the silent engineers' own needs.
