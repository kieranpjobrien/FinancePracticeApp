# Question

You've taken over a team that was previously micromanaged. Members wait for explicit instructions before acting and avoid making decisions. How should you lead the transition to greater autonomy?

## Options

- **A**: Increase autonomy gradually, starting with small decisions and backing the team through mistakes.
- **B**: Remove all oversight at once and let them figure it out through trial and error.
- **C**: Continue the previous micromanagement style since it is what the team already knows and expects.
- **D**: Replace the team with people already accustomed to working independently without direction.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) builds capability sustainably and matches situational leadership: scaffold autonomy as confidence grows. (B) creates anxiety and likely failure. (C) entrenches learned helplessness. (D) wastes a developable team.
