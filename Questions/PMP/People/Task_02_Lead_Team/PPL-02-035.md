# Question

An executive asks you to assign a high-profile task to their preferred team member, bypassing the more qualified team member. Doing so would be unfair and demotivating. How should you handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Comply with the executive's request to avoid political friction with a senior stakeholder.
- **B**: Refuse the request outright without giving any explanation to the executive.
- **C**: Assign the task to the preferred person and complain privately to your own manager.
- **D**: Explain your concerns professionally and propose alternatives that meet the executive's interest.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is courageous, solutions-oriented leadership: understand the interest, advocate for fairness, propose options that satisfy both sides — exactly PMI's stakeholder-engagement approach. (A) trades team trust for political ease. (B) is confrontational without seeking understanding. (C) is passive-aggressive and doesn't solve the problem.
