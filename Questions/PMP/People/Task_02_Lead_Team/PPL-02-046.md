# Question

You have just taken over a struggling 18-month transport ticketing programme from a departing PM. The team of 40 is demoralised, the roadmap is unclear, and several senior engineers have told you "we don't know what we're building any more." Your first week starts Monday. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Invest your first week in structured listening — one-on-ones, review of artefacts and delivery signals — then co-create a refreshed north-star narrative with the team and sponsor.
- **B**: Publish a revised programme vision statement in week one so that the team has immediate clarity and can resume forward momentum against a new direction from the top.
- **C**: Commission a delivery audit from an independent firm so you have a neutral baseline of where the programme actually stands before you decide on any leadership interventions.
- **D**: Replace two of the struggling team leads in week one to signal decisive change and demonstrate that the new PM has the authority to remake the programme quickly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the servant-leader entry pattern: listen, understand, then co-create — legitimacy comes from what you learn, not what you announce. (B) imposes a vision before you have earned the right to set one and will be read as yet another reshuffle. (C) outsources the understanding the PM must build personally and delays leadership signal to the team. (D) makes people decisions before the PM has any data and will deepen the demoralisation already present on the programme.
