# Question

You have been appointed to lead a hybrid programme replacing a hospital's patient-records system. The work spans clinical, IT, and administrative groups who have rarely collaborated, and early conversations reveal each group has a different idea of what "success" means. The team is keen but pulling in slightly different directions. As the leader, what is the most valuable thing to establish early?

## Options

- **A**: A detailed RACI matrix clarifying which group is responsible and accountable for each deliverable across the programme.
- **B**: A shared vision of the programme's purpose and the outcome it will deliver for patients and staff, owned across all three groups.
- **C**: A combined governance forum where the three groups' leads meet fortnightly to surface and resolve cross-group issues.
- **D**: A communications plan ensuring each group receives information tailored to its concerns and ways of working.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The defining problem is divergent definitions of success across groups that must work as one, and a leader's primary early task is to forge a unifying vision that aligns effort before structures and channels are layered on top. (B) creates the shared purpose that makes the other mechanisms meaningful, addressing the root misalignment directly. (A) clarifies who does what but not why, and a RACI without an agreed outcome simply formalises three groups still aiming at different targets. (C) provides a venue to resolve issues but, absent a shared vision, those forums become arenas for competing definitions of success rather than alignment. (D) improves information flow yet does nothing to reconcile the underlying disagreement about goals. Governance, accountability, and communication are valuable, but each is more effective once a common vision gives them a single direction to serve.
