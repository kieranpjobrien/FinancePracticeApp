# Question

You have been appointed PM of an established 25-person data platform team after the previous PM was promoted. Several senior engineers have worked together for years and openly refer to the outgoing PM in every meeting. You sense your authority is not landing. What should the PM do in the first month?

## Options

- **A**: Remind the team formally that you are now the PM and that decisions will be made through you going forward so reporting lines and authority are clear to every member.
- **B**: Ask the outgoing PM to stay involved as an advisor for three months so the team has continuity and you can draw on his credibility while you build your own over that period.
- **C**: Focus on delivering quick visible wins in your first month by taking a couple of decisions the team has been stuck on, so that your credibility is established through decisive action.
- **D**: Spend the first month listening and pairing with senior engineers, understanding what they value about how the team works, and contributing visibly in areas where your help is genuinely useful.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI earned-authority pattern: legitimacy in an established team comes from listening, understanding and contributing before changing anything. (A) asserts positional authority before it has been earned and will harden the "outgoing PM" referencing. (B) keeps the outgoing PM on stage and prevents you from building your own relationship with the team. (C) rushes decisions that are not yet yours to take and can come across as the new PM trying to prove something before understanding the team.
