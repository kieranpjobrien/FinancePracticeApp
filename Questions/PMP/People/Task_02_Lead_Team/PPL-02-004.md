# Question

A PM takes over a troubled project. The previous PM ran a command-and-control style and the team is demoralised — members do exactly what they are told and never raise concerns. The new PM wants to shift to a collaborative style. What is the BEST way to begin?

## Options

- **A**: Announce the new leadership philosophy and immediately introduce self-organising practices across the team.
- **B**: Open with a team meeting that criticises the previous command-and-control approach and promises change.
- **C**: Build trust through consistent behaviour — listening, acting on input, and protecting the team.
- **D**: Remove the team members who appear unwilling to adapt to the new collaborative leadership style.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is how trust is rebuilt — repeated, observable consistency. The team has learned that speaking up isn't safe; only sustained behaviour will change that. (A) moves faster than trust will allow. (B) is unprofessional toward the predecessor and produces words without proof. (D) punishes people for adapting to the system they were in and signals the new PM is just as threatening.
