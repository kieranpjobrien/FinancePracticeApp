# Question

A team member makes an honest mistake that causes a minor project setback. They're visibly anxious about the consequences. How should you respond as a leader?

## Options

- **A**: Use the incident as a cautionary example for the rest of the team in the next meeting.
- **B**: Reassure them privately but allow other team members to criticise them publicly.
- **C**: Acknowledge the mistake factually without blame and focus the team on learning and prevention.
- **D**: Treat the mistake as if it did not happen at all to avoid embarrassing them.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) builds psychological safety — the PMI view that how leaders respond to honest mistakes defines whether future issues get surfaced early. (A) destroys safety and deters reporting. (B) is inconsistent and fails to protect the team member. (D) prevents learning and denies reality.
