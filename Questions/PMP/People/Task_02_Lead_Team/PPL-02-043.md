# Question

You need to deliver difficult feedback to a senior, experienced team member whose performance has declined. They have more domain expertise than you and may become defensive. How should you approach this?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the difficult conversation entirely to preserve the working relationship.
- **B**: Assert your authority as PM firmly to establish who is in charge of the project.
- **C**: Prepare specific examples, lead with curiosity, and problem-solve together after listening.
- **D**: Send the feedback by email to avoid an uncomfortable face-to-face confrontation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the prepared, humble, behaviour-focused approach PMI prescribes for difficult feedback — curiosity over judgment, examples over generalisations, dialogue over decree. (A) lets the issue fester. (B) triggers defensiveness and damages the relationship. (D) is impersonal for a sensitive topic and forecloses dialogue.
