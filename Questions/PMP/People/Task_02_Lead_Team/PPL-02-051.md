# Question

Your board has asked you to pause a six-month IoT product initiative while they reconsider the strategy. The team has built strong momentum over eight months and will interpret the pause as a cancellation. You do not yet know whether it is. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Tell the team the board is considering cancelling the initiative so that people can begin exploring other roles inside the organisation before formal announcements are made.
- **B**: Communicate transparently today what you know and do not know, frame the pause honestly, describe what the team should focus on this week, and commit to a cadence of updates.
- **C**: Delay telling the team anything at all until the board has made a definitive call, on the grounds that premature communication will create unnecessary anxiety and speculation.
- **D**: Ask each engineer to identify a backup project they could move to in case the pause becomes a cancellation, so that the transition is smoother if and when it actually happens.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is leadership during ambiguity — say what you know, what you don't, what they should do now, and when they will hear more. (A) projects your own interpretation onto an undecided board and can damage both team and sponsor relationships. (C) leaves the team in an information vacuum that rumour will fill faster than you can control. (D) signals cancellation as a foregone conclusion before the decision is actually made and can become a self-fulfilling prophecy for the team.
