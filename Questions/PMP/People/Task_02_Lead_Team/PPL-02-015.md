# Question

A project is in crisis — significantly behind schedule with a critical deadline approaching. The team is demoralised after months of overtime, but a final push is needed. How should the PM lead through this?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the difficulty, work alongside the team, and commit to restoring sustainable pace.
- **B**: Demand more overtime from the team and make the consequences of failure clear to everyone.
- **C**: Promise bonuses and rewards that you don't actually have the authority to grant anyone.
- **D**: Downplay the severity of the situation to avoid demoralising the tired team any further.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is authentic crisis leadership: name the hardship, share the picture, share the work, and pre-commit to recovery. (B) is forcing without support. (C) destroys credibility the moment the bonuses don't appear. (D) is deception and treats the team as fragile rather than capable.
