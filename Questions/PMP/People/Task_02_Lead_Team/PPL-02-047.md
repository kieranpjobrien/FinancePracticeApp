# Question

At 2am, your payments platform has a production incident with customer impact. The on-call lead is in a heated argument with the security engineer about whether to roll back or patch forward. The CTO is in the bridge call listening. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the CTO since she is the senior person on the bridge and can break the tie between the on-call lead and the security engineer decisively.
- **B**: Pause the argument, restate the current impact and the two options with their risks, and ask the incident commander to make the call against agreed incident criteria.
- **C**: Call a vote between the two engineers and side with whichever of them has the stronger technical argument so that the team can move forward without further delay.
- **D**: Take the decision yourself on the basis that the PM is accountable for the project and therefore owns the choice between rolling back and patching forward in this incident.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is calm leadership in a live incident — restore clarity, use the agreed incident-management roles, and let the designated decision-maker decide against criteria, not volume. (A) pulls the CTO into an operational call she is observing and undermines the agreed incident commander role. (C) reduces a safety-critical decision to a debate score. (D) takes a technical call that is not the PM's to take and bypasses the incident commander agreed for exactly this moment.
