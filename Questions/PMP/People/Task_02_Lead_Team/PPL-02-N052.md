# Question

A PM has moved from a directive style to servant leadership over a scrum team that historically depended on managers for direction. Six weeks in, the team still routes most decisions to the PM and waits for sign-off before acting. The PM wants their day-to-day behaviour to actually grow the team's autonomy, not just carry the servant-leader label. Which actions would most help this particular team become self-managing? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: When the team brings a decision that sits squarely within their remit, hand it back with the context they need and let them decide, rather than deciding for them.
- **B**: Step fully back from impediment removal as well, so the team learns to clear organisational blockers themselves rather than depending on the PM for those too.
- **C**: Shield the team from external interruptions and ad-hoc requests during the sprint so they can focus on the sprint goal.
- **D**: When the team asks the PM to choose between technical options, decide quickly each time so they are never blocked waiting on the PM.
- **E**: Coach the team to make and own decisions in increasing scope, accepting some imperfect calls as the cost of them learning to self-manage.

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

This team's specific problem is learned dependency, so the helpful actions are the ones that transfer ownership while still giving the team the support a servant leader provides. (A) returns in-remit decisions to the team with context, which is exactly how dependency is unwound. (C) protects the team's focus — a core servant-leader behaviour that serves the team without taking over its decisions. (E) coaches the team into a widening decision scope and accepts the imperfect calls that learning requires, which is what actually builds self-management. (B) overcorrects: clearing organisational impediments the team cannot remove on its own is a genuine servant-leader duty, and abandoning it dumps blockers on a team that lacks the authority to shift them — withdrawal is not the same as empowerment. (D) feels supportive and keeps the team unblocked, but answering every decision for them reinforces the very habit of routing choices to the PM that the team needs to break.
