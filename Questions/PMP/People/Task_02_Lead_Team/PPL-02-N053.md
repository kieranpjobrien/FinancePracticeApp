# Question

A PM leads a hybrid team where five members are co-located in an office and four work remotely. Over the past month, decisions have increasingly been formed in informal office conversations and the remote members learn of them after the fact; two of them have grown noticeably quieter in meetings. The PM has a steering review in three days and wants to correct the imbalance properly. What should the PM do to lead the team more effectively?

## Options

- **A**: Establish that decisions of any significance are made and recorded in shared channels everyone can access, and run meetings so remote voices are deliberately drawn in.
- **B**: Move the team to a remote-first default — hold key discussions on video with everyone dialling in individually, even office members, so no one has an in-room advantage.
- **C**: Speak privately with the two quieter remote members to understand how the pattern is affecting them, then use what they say to shape the changes the team adopts.
- **D**: Set up a standing weekly call dedicated to the remote members where pending decisions are talked through with them before anything is settled in the office.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root cause is that information and influence are tracking physical location, so the leadership fix is to change where and how decisions are made and to make participation equal for everyone — not to bolt a remote-handling process onto an office-centric pattern. (A) does this directly: decisions move into channels both locations share and meetings are run to include remote voices, which addresses the cause and re-engages the quieter members. (B) attacks the same root cause and can work, but flipping an otherwise functioning co-located half to fully individual remote-first is a heavier intervention than the situation requires and discards the value of in-person collaboration where it is genuinely useful; equal access, not enforced sameness, is the goal. (C) is a humane and useful input-gathering step, but a private listening conversation is diagnosis, not the structural change that fixes how decisions get made — it informs (A) rather than replacing it. (D) keeps the office as the place decisions are settled and adds a separate forum to brief the remote members, which institutionalises a second tier that is consulted rather than equally present.
