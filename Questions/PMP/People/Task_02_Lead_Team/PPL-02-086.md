# Question

You are about to lead a major change initiative — consolidating four regional teams onto a single shared services platform — and you know much of the affected staff is anxious and sceptical. To lead the people through this well, which actions should you prioritise? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Articulate a clear, compelling vision of why the change matters and what a better future state looks like for those affected.
- **B**: Create channels for two-way dialogue so concerns can be heard and the people can shape how the change is implemented.
- **C**: Move quickly and decisively to implement the change before resistance has time to organise and harden.
- **D**: Reassure everyone that nothing significant about their day-to-day roles will really change once the platform is in place.
- **E**: Delay communicating details until the plan is fully finalised, to avoid spreading uncertainty with incomplete information.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Leading people through change rests on a shared sense of purpose and on genuine engagement with those affected. (A) gives an anxious group a reason to move and a picture of a worthwhile destination, which is foundational to overcoming scepticism. (B) involving people and listening converts resistance into ownership and surfaces issues early, which is the most reliable antidote to fear. The distractors each embody a classic change-leadership error. (C) ramming the change through to outpace resistance treats people as obstacles and almost always deepens opposition. (D) promising that "nothing will really change" is both untrue for a consolidation of this scale and corrosive to trust once reality bites. (E) withholding communication until everything is finalised lets rumour fill the vacuum and signals that the change is being done to people, not with them. A clear vision and authentic two-way engagement are the priorities.
