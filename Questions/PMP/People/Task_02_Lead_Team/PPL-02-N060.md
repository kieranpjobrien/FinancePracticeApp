# Question

A PM acting as scrum master notices the daily stand-up has become a status report directed at them: each developer reports to the PM, makes eye contact only with the PM, and waits for the PM to respond before the next person speaks. Dependencies between developers are surfacing late as a result, because no one is really coordinating with anyone but the PM. The PM wants the stand-up to serve the team's own coordination toward the sprint goal. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team run the stand-up around the sprint board, with each member speaking to how their work moves the board toward the sprint goal rather than reporting personal status.
- **B**: Step back from the circle and stop responding to each update, letting silences prompt the developers to start addressing one another directly.
- **C**: Introduce the three standard questions — what I did, what I will do, and any blockers — so each developer's update is structured and dependencies are stated explicitly.
- **D**: Ask the developers to direct their updates to each other rather than to the PM, and to raise any dependencies between their work as they go.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stand-up is the team's own coordination event toward the sprint goal, and the most reliable way to shift it from leader-centred reporting is to change what the conversation is organised around. (A) re-anchors the event on the work and the goal via the board, which naturally turns attention from the PM to the shared objective and exposes dependencies as part of the flow — it changes the structure, not just the etiquette. (D) corrects who people speak to and is good practice, but on its own it adjusts manners while the meeting can still be a round of individual reports. (B) relies on the PM withdrawing and hoping the team fills the vacuum, which often produces awkward stalling rather than coordination and gives the team no new frame to organise around. (C) imposes a reporting format that, if anything, can reinforce the sequential person-by-person status round the PM is trying to move away from.
