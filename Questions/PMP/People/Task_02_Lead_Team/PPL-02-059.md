# Question

Your Scrum Master's 12-month contract ends in three weeks; procurement has not approved an extension and the team has deep trust in him. The product owner is pressuring you to simply hire a cheaper replacement through a different agency starting the week after. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the product owner's proposal and start the new Scrum Master immediately so that there is no gap in facilitation coverage for the team's sprint ceremonies.
- **B**: Escalate the continuity risk to procurement and the sponsor with data, recommend a short extension for structured handover, and if declined co-design a transition with the team.
- **C**: Ask the outgoing Scrum Master to run the team remotely during his notice period on his own time so that handover is not compressed into the final three weeks of the engagement.
- **D**: Keep the team focused on delivery and deal with facilitation coverage yourself during the gap, avoiding any team conversation about the contract until a replacement is in place.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses the continuity risk through the right channels with data, offers a handover solution, and keeps the team informed — the PMI leadership pattern when a key role is ending. (A) optimises for cost over team and ignores the trust asset that has been built. (C) relies on unpaid goodwill to cover a procurement gap, which is unprofessional and unreliable at best. (D) hides the looming change from the team and piles facilitation onto a PM role that is already fully committed.
