# Question

A failed release last week caused 14 hours of customer outage on your SaaS billing platform. Team morale has cratered, some engineers are blaming QA, QA is blaming engineering, and the CEO has asked you for a "full accountability report" by Friday. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Identify the two people most directly involved in the faulty release, have private conversations with each, and include their names and actions in the accountability report as the CEO requested.
- **B**: Delay the accountability report by a week so that tempers have cooled before the team is required to revisit the details of the outage in any formal organisational forum.
- **C**: Write the accountability report yourself based on the logs and timeline so that no team member is placed under direct scrutiny from the CEO during this emotionally difficult week.
- **D**: Run a blameless post-mortem before Friday, reframe the CEO's request around systemic causes and learning actions, and deliver a report focused on how the team will prevent recurrence.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI blameless post-mortem pattern — systems, not people — and coaches the executive ask into a learning output rather than a punishment output. (A) reduces a systemic failure to a blame story and is likely to drive the two people out and discourage honesty in the future. (B) delays the learning window when emotion is freshest and risks losing evidence as memories fade. (C) keeps the team safe from scrutiny but also blocks the learning the team needs to own together on the platform.
