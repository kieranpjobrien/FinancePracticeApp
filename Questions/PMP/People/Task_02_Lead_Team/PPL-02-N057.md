# Question

A PM has inherited a team that is reluctant to raise problems early; issues tend to surface only once they have become serious. The PM believes the team does not feel safe speaking up and wants to change that. Which leadership behaviours would most help the team start surfacing concerns sooner? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: When someone raises a problem early that turns out to stem from their own mistake, respond with curiosity and focus on the fix rather than the fault.
- **B**: Admit the PM's own errors and uncertainties openly so the team sees that being fallible is acceptable.
- **C**: Set a team target for the number of issues and risks logged each sprint, so raising concerns becomes an explicit, measured expectation everyone is held to.
- **D**: When a concern is raised early and prevents a larger problem, name that contribution specifically so the team sees early disclosure is valued.
- **E**: Promise the team that problems raised early will never carry consequences, so that speaking up is risk-free whatever the situation.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Safety to speak up grows when raising problems is consistently met without personal cost and is modelled from the top. (A) removes the fear of blame at the exact moment it bites — when the problem is the speaker's own mistake. (B) models fallibility from the leader, which gives the team permission to be imperfect. (D) reinforces early disclosure by recognising it specifically, so the behaviour is seen to pay off. (C) looks supportive but turns speaking up into a counted target, which drives people to log trivia to hit the number and makes the team feel measured rather than safe — quantity quotas distort the behaviour they aim to encourage. (E) overreaches: a blanket promise that early disclosure never carries consequences is one the PM cannot honestly keep — some problems have genuine accountability attached regardless of timing — and a promise the team senses is hollow erodes the trust it was meant to build. What safety requires is that the messenger is not punished for being the messenger, not that outcomes never have consequences.
