# Question

A PM leads a team delivering a regulatory reporting system. The business sponsor verbally asks the team to add an analytics dashboard that is not in the approved scope, telling two developers directly during a site visit that it is "a quick win the board wants." The developers, eager to please the sponsor, have started building it. The PM only learns of this two days later. How should the PM lead the team here?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the work to continue since it came from the sponsor, and update the project documentation afterward to reflect the added scope.
- **B**: Stop the work and tell the developers never to act on direction from the sponsor without the PM's prior approval in future.
- **C**: Ask the developers to pause the unapproved work, explain why scope changes must go through the agreed process, and take the request to the sponsor through change control.
- **D**: Let the developers finish the dashboard since it is nearly done, but make clear the team must not start further additions without checking first.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A leader holds the line on process while keeping the team and sponsor aligned, and uses the moment to teach rather than to blame. (C) halts the uncontrolled work, explains the reasoning so the developers understand why, and routes the genuine request through change control where it can be assessed properly. (A) ratifies scope creep retroactively and undermines the change process for everyone. (B) issues a blunt prohibition that overcorrects and damages the team's relationship with the sponsor without explaining the principle. (D) lets unapproved scope through because it is convenient, which signals that the process is optional whenever the work is far enough along.
