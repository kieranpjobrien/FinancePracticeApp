# Question

You have been told in confidence that three of your twelve-person product engineering team will be made redundant at the end of the quarter. You do not yet know who. The team has started sensing something is wrong and you are meeting them today. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Deny that anything is happening so that speculation does not damage delivery further, and wait until the organisation makes the official announcement about the redundancies.
- **B**: Tell the team privately today which three roles are likely at risk based on your own judgement, so that individuals can prepare for what appears to be coming their way.
- **C**: Quietly start reassigning work away from the three people you think are most likely to leave so that delivery continuity is protected when the redundancies are eventually announced.
- **D**: Acknowledge to the team that change is underway, confirm what you can confirm, be explicit about what you cannot say, and commit to telling them promptly when you can.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is PMI leadership in sensitive ambiguity: be honest about the fact of change, respect the confidence, be explicit about what you can't share yet, and commit to a cadence. Trust survives honesty more than it survives denial. (A) lies to the team, which is a direct ethical breach and will unravel badly. (B) breaches a confidence and substitutes the PM's guess for the organisation's formal decision. (C) prejudges individuals and visibly confirms the rumour through your own actions without any conversation.
