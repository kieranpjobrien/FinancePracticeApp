# Question

Your 12-person analytics squad has been working 55-hour weeks for six consecutive sprints to hit a regulatory deadline. The sponsor is asking for one more "final push" of a further four weeks. Two team members have started taking sick leave and quality signals are declining. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to the four-week push but promise the team a two-week recovery period afterwards and communicate the arrangement clearly with the sponsor so everyone understands the trade-off.
- **B**: Reject the additional push and insist the team returns to standard hours from next week, accepting whatever regulatory penalty the organisation receives for missing the delivery deadline.
- **C**: Show the sponsor the quality and sick-leave data, present options that deliver the regulatory minimum at sustainable pace, and agree the path together before committing the team to anything.
- **D**: Quietly reduce the team's stretch commitments without telling the sponsor, on the grounds that the decline signals mean the team cannot sustain the requested push any longer.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) makes the real trade-off transparent using data, respects sustainable pace as a leadership duty, and restores decision-making to the right level — the PMI leadership pattern when pace harms delivery. (A) commits the team to more harm in exchange for future rest that rarely arrives as promised. (B) protects the team but abandons the sponsor relationship and the regulatory stakes without any negotiation. (D) silently rebaselines without transparency, which is a breach of the PM's reporting integrity duty.
