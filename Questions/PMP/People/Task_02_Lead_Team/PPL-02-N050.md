# Question

A PM leads a cross-functional team in a strong matrix organisation, where team members report functionally to department managers who own their assignments and reviews. For the past two weeks one functional manager has been pulling a key tester onto departmental work for several hours a day, and project testing is now slipping toward the critical path. The PM and this functional manager have a constructive working relationship. The tester has privately told the PM she feels caught between the two demands. What is the best leadership approach?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the functional manager, share the specific schedule impact and the tester's position, and work out an allocation of her time that both can commit to.
- **B**: Ask the tester to log the hours she spends on departmental versus project work, then use that record to make the case to the functional manager once a clear pattern is documented.
- **C**: Agree a temporary split of the tester's time directly with her that protects the critical-path testing, and inform the functional manager of the arrangement afterward.
- **D**: Raise the resourcing conflict at the next project steering meeting where the functional manager is present, so the allocation is decided transparently with other stakeholders in the room.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a matrix the PM leads borrowed resources through direct influence and negotiated agreement with the manager who controls them, addressing the conflict at its source and early. (A) goes straight to the functional manager as a partner, brings the concrete impact, and reaches a mutual commitment — and it relieves the tester, who should not be left brokering the conflict between two leaders. (B) delays action while a problem already visible is documented, letting the slippage grow and leaving the tester squeezed in the meantime; evidence helps but is not a reason to wait. (C) strikes the deal with the tester rather than the manager who actually owns her time, which oversteps the PM's authority and exposes her to her manager's displeasure. (D) turns a solvable two-party issue into a public forum item, which can corner the functional manager and strain a relationship that is currently the PM's best asset for resolving it quietly.
