# Question

On your six-month fintech migration, a mid-career developer has started running design sessions informally and the team clearly follows her technical lead. The named tech lead is competent but disengaged. Your architect has asked you whether to "do something." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the informal arrangement in place since it is working and any formal change risks disturbing a team dynamic that is currently delivering strong outcomes.
- **B**: Ask the named tech lead to step aside and formally promote the mid-career developer into the role so that the title reflects the reality everyone already recognises.
- **C**: Coach the named tech lead privately about the disengagement you are observing and agree a development plan that makes his contribution visible again on the project.
- **D**: Meet both developers separately to understand their ambitions, then work with the functional manager on a formal co-lead or mentorship structure that develops both and makes accountability clear.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) invests in both people, uses the functional manager for the formal role question, and converts an informal pattern into a structure that keeps growing talent — the PMI leadership pattern for emerging leaders. (A) normalises shadow leadership and sets up future confusion over accountability. (B) demotes a competent person without coaching first, which is unfair and damages trust. (C) does half the work — coaches the disengaged lead but ignores the emerging leader, whose growth needs an intentional path as well.
