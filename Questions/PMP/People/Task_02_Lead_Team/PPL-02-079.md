# Question

Your agile team is debating two technical approaches for an integration and has been going back and forth for most of an afternoon without converging. Both options are viable; the team is capable and the decision is genuinely theirs to make, but they keep glancing at you for a steer. The clock is ticking on the sprint. What is the most appropriate leadership action?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner which approach better serves the business need and have the team adopt that one.
- **B**: Offer your own recommendation between the two approaches to break the deadlock, since both are viable and time is short.
- **C**: Help the team agree how they will decide — for example a time-box and a tie-break method — so they reach their own decision and move on.
- **D**: Let the discussion continue, trusting that a capable team will resolve a two-option decision without your involvement.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The decision belongs to a capable team that is stuck on process, not on competence, so the leader should equip them to decide rather than decide for them or let them drift. (C) gives them a mechanism — a time-box and tie-breaker — that resolves the stall while keeping ownership with the team, which builds their decision-making muscle. (B) breaking the tie yourself is tempting and fast, but it teaches the team to wait for your steer and erodes the self-organisation you want; it should be a last resort. (A) misroutes a technical decision to the product owner, whose remit is value and priority, not implementation choice, and it still removes the decision from the team. (D) pure non-intervention ignores that they are visibly stuck and the sprint clock is running, so it risks wasting the time-box entirely. Facilitating how they decide, without making the decision, is the balanced action.
