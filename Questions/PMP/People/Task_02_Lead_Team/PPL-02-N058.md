# Question

A capable analyst on the PM's team has the technical skills to run a complex data-quality reconciliation; she has done similar work competently before. After a recent reconciliation of hers was publicly criticised by another department for an error that was later found not to be her fault, she has become hesitant, checks routine decisions with the PM, and has asked to have her work double-checked before it goes out. The reconciliation is on the critical path and due in a week. How should the PM lead her on this task?

## Options

- **A**: Give her detailed step-by-step instructions on the reconciliation method so she has a clear procedure to follow and fewer decisions to second-guess.
- **B**: Encourage her, talk through her reasoning, and affirm the judgement calls she is already making correctly, while leaving the task and its decisions with her.
- **C**: Delegate the task fully with minimal contact, signalling complete confidence in her so she feels trusted to get on with it.
- **D**: Assign a peer to review her output before it is released for this task, removing the risk of another public error while she rebuilds confidence.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Situational leadership matches the style to the person's competence and confidence on this specific task. She is fully competent but her commitment and self-assurance have dropped after the criticism, which calls for a supporting style: encourage, draw out and affirm her own judgement, and keep ownership with her. (B) does exactly this and rebuilds the confidence that is the actual deficit. (A) treats a confidence problem as a skills problem; piling on instruction to someone who already knows how can reinforce the message that she is not trusted to decide. (C) misreads the situation as readiness for full delegation and abandons a shaken team member who is currently seeking reassurance. (D) is the support she literally asked for, but institutionalising a check on someone who is technically competent props up the dependency rather than restoring her self-belief.
