# Question

Your agile team keeps getting pulled off sprint work by ad hoc requests from various stakeholders who drop by desks, send "urgent" messages, and book impromptu calls. Some requests are genuine new work; many are quick questions or favours. The team is willing but increasingly fragmented, and the sprint goal is slipping. What is the most appropriate action?

## Options

- **A**: Add a buffer of capacity into each sprint so the team can absorb the unplanned stakeholder requests without derailing the goal.
- **B**: Coach the team to politely redirect or defer requests themselves so they protect their own focus during the sprint.
- **C**: Ask stakeholders to route requests through the product owner so each is prioritised against the sprint's existing work.
- **D**: Make yourself the single point of contact for incoming requests now, triaging them so the team can hold its focus this sprint.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The disruption is a stream of ad hoc interruptions hitting the team directly mid-sprint, so the immediate need is to put something between the team and the noise while the goal is still salvageable — and only the leader can do that without taxing the team. (D) intercepts the flow at once and triages it, restoring focus today, which is why it is the best first action. (C) routing through the product owner is the right long-term home for genuine backlog items, but on its own it does not stop the desk-side drop-bys and "urgent" pings, and it loads the entire interruption stream onto the product owner; it is a complement to shielding, not a substitute. (B) telling the team to deflect requests itself pushes the burden back onto the people being disrupted and puts junior members in awkward stand-offs with stakeholders. (A) buffering capacity concedes time to the interruptions rather than stemming them, and a buffer is quickly consumed by an unmanaged flow. Intercepting and triaging the requests yourself protects the team now, after which the steadier stream can be channelled through the product owner.
