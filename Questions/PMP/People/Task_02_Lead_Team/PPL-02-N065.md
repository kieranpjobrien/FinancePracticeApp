# Question

You lead a programme to consolidate two merged insurers onto a single claims platform. You have no line authority over the two divisional heads, each of whom directs their own staff to prioritise their division's legacy processes over the shared target design. Both behave reasonably from their own division's standpoint, and both outrank you. Delivery is stalling because their teams pull in opposite directions. What should you do first?

## Options

- **A**: Ask your sponsor to reaffirm the target design as the agreed direction to both divisional heads, so the mandate they are working against is made explicit before you engage them.
- **B**: Meet each divisional head to understand the interests behind their position and find where the shared design already serves those interests, building a case for alignment.
- **C**: Bring the two divisional heads together to surface the conflicting priorities openly and agree between them how their teams will sequence the shared versus legacy work.
- **D**: Convene a joint workshop of both divisions to negotiate a blended design that incorporates the legacy processes each side most wants to keep.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Every option is a legitimate alignment move, so the question is sequence — what to do first when leading without authority. (B) starts by understanding each party's underlying interests and locating the common ground, which builds the influence and the shared rationale every later step depends on; without it, you do not yet know what would actually move either head. (A) reaches for borrowed authority before any direct influence attempt; re-issuing a mandate to two senior peers tends to harden positions and casts you as someone who escalates rather than persuades. (C) puts the two heads in a room to negotiate sequencing before you understand what each truly needs, so the meeting risks becoming a standoff between fixed positions. (D) opens a design negotiation that may be a sound later step, but blending the target design before grasping the real interests risks reopening settled scope and trading away the consolidation's value. Understand interests first, then convene.
