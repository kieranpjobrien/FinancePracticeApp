# Question

A newly formed agile team is struggling with self-organisation. Members regularly come to the project manager for decisions about task assignment and technical direction. What leadership approach should the PM adopt?

## Options

- **A**: Coach self-organisation skills, provide more direction now and step back as the team matures.
- **B**: Provide clear direction and assign tasks for the duration of the forming stage.
- **C**: Refuse to make decisions for the team to force them into self-organisation.
- **D**: Tell management the team isn't mature enough for agile and switch to predictive.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is situational/servant leadership: a forming team needs more support, which is gradually withdrawn as norming and performing emerge. (B) entrenches dependency and never builds the capability the team needs. (C) abandons the team without coaching — that is not self-organisation, it's neglect. (D) gives up without doing the leadership work.
