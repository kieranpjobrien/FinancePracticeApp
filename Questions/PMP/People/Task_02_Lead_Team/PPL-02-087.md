# Question

You lead a distributed agile team across three time zones. One developer has missed the last several commitments he took at sprint planning, his updates in the shared channel have become vague, and other members are quietly picking up his slack while saying nothing openly. You do not yet know the cause. Which actions are appropriate for you to take as the leader? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Have a direct, private conversation with the developer to understand what is behind the missed commitments before drawing conclusions.
- **B**: Review whether something structural — unclear stories, dependencies, time-zone handoffs, or tooling — is impeding him.
- **C**: Raise his missed commitments by name in the next team retrospective so the team can hold him accountable.
- **D**: Reinforce, with the whole team, the shared expectations around transparency and following through on commitments.
- **E**: Quietly reassign his upcoming stories to the members already covering for him, to protect the sprint.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

A pattern of missed commitments with an unknown cause requires understanding the individual, checking the system, and reinforcing shared norms — without shaming the person or papering over the issue. (A) gets to the root cause respectfully and privately; (B) checks whether structural impediments, common in distributed teams, are the real driver rather than the individual; (D) restates the team's transparency and follow-through expectations for everyone, which is fair and non-singling. (C) and (E) are the traps. (C) naming and shaming him in the retrospective before you even know the cause is a public call-out that breaches the safety a healthy team depends on and may punish someone facing a legitimate obstacle. (E) silently reassigning his work entrenches the unhealthy dynamic of others covering for him without addressing why, and removes any chance for him to recover the commitment. A, B, and D address person, system, and norms constructively; C and E damage trust or dodge the cause.
