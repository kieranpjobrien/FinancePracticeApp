# Question

What characterises situational leadership?

## Options

- **A**: Using the same consistent leadership style regardless of any circumstances.
- **B**: Leading actively only when situations become critical or urgent.
- **C**: Letting the situation determine who organically emerges as the leader.
- **D**: Adapting leadership style to the team member's development level.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Situational leadership (Hersey and Blanchard) flexes between directing, coaching, supporting and delegating based on the follower's competence and commitment. (A) is rigid and ineffective. (B) is reactive, not adaptive. (C) describes emergent leadership, a different concept.
