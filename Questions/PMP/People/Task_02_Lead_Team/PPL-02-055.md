# Question

Your organisation has mandated a move from Waterfall to Scrum across all projects. Your current team of twelve has delivered reliably under Waterfall for five years and three senior members have openly called the transition "buzzword theatre." You have been asked to lead the change. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Announce the new Scrum ceremonies and roles in next week's team meeting and require participation from everyone so that the transition begins with clear expectations from the top.
- **B**: Meet the three senior sceptics individually, understand their specific concerns, invite them to co-shape how Scrum will be tailored for this team, and integrate their expertise into the rollout.
- **C**: Bring in an external agile coach to run a two-day kick-off workshop so the team hears the case for change from a respected neutral voice rather than internal leadership.
- **D**: Start the transition with a pilot sub-team of willing volunteers so that early wins can be demonstrated before rolling the new ways of working out across the entire team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) turns the loudest sceptics into co-designers of the change — the PMI pattern for adult change management combined with servant leadership. Their concerns are often legitimate tailoring inputs. (A) imposes change by decree and will harden the sceptical voices. (C) outsources leadership legitimacy to a stranger before the PM has engaged the experienced people on the team. (D) creates a two-tier team and leaves the hardest conversations for later when trust is harder to rebuild.
