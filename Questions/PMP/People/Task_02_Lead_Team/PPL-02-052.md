# Question

A senior principal engineer on your infrastructure project is brilliant but dominates every technical discussion and dismisses junior ideas. Your last team survey shows psychological safety dropping sharply. He is critical to delivery and reports to a different manager. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the survey results and the behaviour pattern to his functional manager so that formal feedback can be delivered through the right reporting line for the role.
- **B**: Raise the psychological-safety signal anonymously at a team all-hands and trust the senior engineer to recognise himself in the feedback and adjust his behaviour accordingly.
- **C**: Meet the principal engineer privately, share the survey signal and specific observed examples, and agree a small set of concrete behaviour changes he will own going forward.
- **D**: Structure technical discussions with a strict turn-taking protocol so the engineer cannot dominate sessions, even if the change feels artificial to the rest of the team at first.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI coaching pattern for senior experts: direct, private, evidence-based feedback with agreed behaviour changes — respect the person and address the behaviour. (A) avoids the conversation the PM should lead and treats it as someone else's people problem. (B) relies on the engineer to diagnose himself from anonymous data, which rarely works with strong expert personalities. (D) treats a behaviour issue with a process workaround and will be felt as a collective punishment by the rest of the team.
