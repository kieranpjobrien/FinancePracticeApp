# Question

You take over a struggling 18-month programme to modernise a council's planning-permission system. The team has lived through two reorganisations and a leadership change, still delivers competent work, and is currently within schedule and budget tolerance — but is visibly flat: people do what is asked, raise little, and show no spark in stand-ups or planning. Your sponsor wants you to "get them moving again." What should you prioritise first as the new leader?

## Options

- **A**: Spend your first weeks one-on-one drawing out how the past disruption affected each member, so people feel genuinely heard before you ask anything more of them.
- **B**: Work with the team to reconnect the programme to the outcome it delivers for residents and to their own part in it, establishing a shared sense of purpose.
- **C**: Run a facilitated lessons-learned on the two reorganisations so the team can close out the disruption together and move forward from a clean line.
- **D**: Set a near-term, visible delivery target the team can rally around and hit quickly, using an early win to rebuild momentum and belief in the programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team is competent and on-track but disengaged and disconnected from purpose, so the leadership deficit is meaning, not capability, process, or capacity — and the first move should supply it directly. (B) rebuilds the shared "why" and each person's stake in it, which is what is actually missing. (D) is a credible motivation tactic and an early win can help, but a target handed to a team that sees no point in the work risks reading as another push without addressing why they have disengaged. (A) and (C) both turn first to the past: (A) listening individually and (C) a collective lessons-learned. Both can have value, but leading with a retrospective focus on two reorganisations and a leadership change risks deepening the resignation and rehearsing grievance rather than redirecting energy toward what the programme is for. Acknowledging the disruption matters, but the priority for a disengaged yet capable team is forward-looking purpose, not processing the past.
