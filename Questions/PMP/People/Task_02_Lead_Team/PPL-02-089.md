# Question

As the leader of an agile delivery team, you face several distinct situations in a single sprint. For each, match the servant-leadership response that best fits it. Each response is used once.

## Items

- **1**: A vendor's slow turnaround on environment access is blocking two developers, and the matter sits outside the team's authority to resolve.
- **2**: The team is debating a design decision that is squarely within its competence and remit, but keeps pausing to look to you for the answer.
- **3**: Two team members are in an escalating personal disagreement that is starting to sour the daily stand-up and pull others in.

## Matches

- **A**: Facilitate how they reach their own decision and hold back from supplying the answer yourself, reinforcing their ownership.
- **B**: Step in to clear the external obstacle on the team's behalf by escalating to the vendor and securing the access.
- **C**: Bring the two together to address the conflict directly and help them agree how they will work through such clashes.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Servant leadership means reading what each situation actually requires — removing obstacles, empowering decisions, or resolving conflict — and not defaulting to a single move. (1) is an impediment beyond the team's reach, so the leader clears it on the team's behalf (B); this is the classic obstacle-removal duty. (2) is a decision within the team's competence, so the leader facilitates rather than answers (A), protecting the self-organisation that would erode if you simply gave the steer. (3) is an interpersonal conflict, which a leader addresses by bringing the parties together to resolve it and to agree how they will handle future friction (C), since unmanaged conflict corrodes the team. The trap is to blur the responses — solving the decision for them, or treating the blocker as something the team should just push through — when each situation calls for a different and specific servant-leadership behaviour.
