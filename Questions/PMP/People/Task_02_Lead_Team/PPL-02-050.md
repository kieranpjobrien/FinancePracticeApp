# Question

A newly hired senior engineer has joined your fully remote 10-person platform team. Two weeks in, she has told you she feels invisible in meetings, no one has offered to pair with her, and she is questioning the decision to accept the role. The team thinks onboarding is going fine. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet her weekly for the first 90 days, deliberately structure pairing rotations that include her, and set a visible expectation with the team that onboarding is a shared responsibility.
- **B**: Tell her that remote onboarding always feels disorienting at first and to give it another month before deciding whether the platform team is actually working out for her.
- **C**: Ask her to present a technical topic at the next team meeting so that the rest of the team is forced to recognise her expertise and begin engaging with her in future sessions.
- **D**: Raise the issue in the next retrospective without naming her so that the team can collectively improve how they onboard new joiners to the platform team going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) addresses the specific person with the specific intervention that remote onboarding actually needs — structured connection, visible expectation, and sustained attention. (B) minimises a legitimate signal and risks losing a senior hire the team needs. (C) treats visibility as her problem to solve and puts a new joiner on the spot before she feels safe. (D) addresses the systemic gap but leaves her in the same situation this week while the team debates generic fixes at the retrospective.
