# Question

A team lead on your education-sector implementation has missed the same architectural deliverable three sprints running. Other leads have started questioning in stand-ups whether they should depend on him at all. He is otherwise capable and has never been coached on the pattern. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the architectural deliverable to a different team lead so that the downstream work is unblocked and the missed pattern does not affect programme commitments any further.
- **B**: Address the pattern in the next leads' meeting so that accountability is seen to be public and every lead knows that missed commitments are taken seriously across the programme.
- **C**: Let the natural peer pressure continue since visible questioning from other leads will push the individual to improve his delivery without the PM needing to intervene directly.
- **D**: Meet the team lead privately, describe the pattern with specific examples, understand what is driving it, agree concrete commitments, and then support him visibly in front of peers.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI leadership pattern for missed commitments — private, specific, supportive feedback with clear re-commitment, followed by visible backing so peers see the issue is being handled. (A) removes the work but not the pattern and teaches the lead nothing about what is expected. (B) publicly shames a person who has never been coached first, which breaches the "praise in public, correct in private" principle. (C) relies on social pressure to do the PM's coaching work and lets the pattern ripple further into the team.
