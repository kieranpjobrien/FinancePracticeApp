# Question

A junior member of your civil-works project team volunteered over a difficult week to untangle a messy supplier-data problem that several others had avoided, and her work saved the schedule. She is quietly pleased but has said nothing about it. You want to reinforce the behaviour and keep her motivated. What is the most effective leadership action?

## Options

- **A**: Give her a more prominent task next, so she sees that good work is rewarded with greater responsibility.
- **B**: Recognise her contribution specifically and sincerely, in a way she would value, so the effort is seen and the behaviour encouraged.
- **C**: Note the achievement in her performance record so it is captured for her formal review.
- **D**: Thank the whole team for pulling together through a hard week so no one feels singled out.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Recognition is one of the most reliable motivators, and it is most effective when it is timely, specific, and matched to how the individual likes to be acknowledged. (B) does exactly this and directly reinforces the initiative you want to see repeated. (A) can be a meaningful reward, but stretching responsibility is not a substitute for acknowledging the effort already made, and may even read as "your reward is more work." (C) is appropriate administratively but a note in a record months before a review does little to motivate now, when the impact of recognition is greatest. (D) generic team thanks erases the individual contribution and misses the chance to reinforce her specific behaviour; it can even feel deflating to the person who actually did the work. Specific, sincere, well-timed recognition is the strongest of the options.
