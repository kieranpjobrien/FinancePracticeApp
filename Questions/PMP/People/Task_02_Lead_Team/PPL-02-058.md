# Question

A high-performing mid-career developer on your year-long platform rebuild has told you confidentially she is thinking about leaving because she can't see her next step inside the organisation. She is critical to delivery and losing her would hurt. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Work with her and her functional manager to co-design a visible growth path on the programme — stretch role, mentoring, or exposure — that she can try within the next quarter.
- **B**: Promise her an immediate retention bonus and a promotion at the end of the programme so that she has a concrete financial reason to stay through the current delivery phase.
- **C**: Ask HR to run a general career conversation with everyone on the team so she receives career attention without being singled out for special treatment over her peers.
- **D**: Accept that her departure may be inevitable and begin quietly identifying a potential replacement who can be onboarded before her eventual exit causes serious disruption.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI retention pattern: address what she actually said she needs (a next step she can see) and use the functional manager who owns formal growth — not bribes, not generic programmes. (B) treats an intrinsic motivator with an extrinsic fix and rarely retains someone who has already started looking. (C) hides her from the attention she needs and fails to engage with the specific concern she raised privately with the PM. (D) gives up on a critical contributor before any real retention move has been attempted on the programme.
