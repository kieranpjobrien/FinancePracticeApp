# Question

On a globally distributed 30-person data platform programme, the Australian team dominates decision-making in meetings while the Indian and Polish teams contribute mostly in writing afterwards. Decisions are being reopened repeatedly because written dissent arrives after calls. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Require everyone to speak up in meetings by going around the virtual room and asking each person directly for their view so that all voices are heard before decisions.
- **B**: Make one of the Indian or Polish team leads the chair of the weekly decision meeting so the centre of gravity shifts away from the dominant Australian voices during sessions.
- **C**: Redesign the decision process so that proposals are shared in writing 24 hours ahead, meetings are used for clarification, and decisions are confirmed after a written comment window.
- **D**: Run a cross-cultural communication workshop so the three groups understand each other's communication norms and can adjust their behaviour in meetings on their own over time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) redesigns the system rather than trying to change individuals across cultures — written-first, synchronous-for-clarification is the structural fix for mixed-culture distributed teams, and it eliminates the rework of reopened decisions. (A) forces verbal participation in a format that disadvantages two of three groups. (B) moves a symbol but not the mechanism that is producing the pattern. (D) is useful but slow, and does not change the structural reason dissent is arriving late.
