# Question

Three months ago you promised your team a post-launch recovery sprint in exchange for a difficult stretch. The sponsor has now allocated that time to a new urgent feature and you could not push back effectively. The team feels the promise has been broken. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the broken commitment openly with the team, explain exactly what happened, take ownership of your part, and co-design together what repair is realistic now.
- **B**: Keep communication light on the broken promise and focus on selling the new urgent feature as an exciting opportunity that came up after the original commitment was made.
- **C**: Go back to the sponsor and insist that either the recovery sprint is restored in full or you will not take the new urgent feature on behalf of the team at all.
- **D**: Offer individual extra leave or other compensation to each team member as a one-off substitute for the group recovery sprint that has now been reallocated by the sponsor.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI integrity pattern when trust is damaged: acknowledge, own, explain, and co-design repair — credibility is rebuilt through honesty, not through cover. (B) minimises a real breach and will be read as spin by a team who already know what happened. (C) is an ultimatum without the transparent conversation the team needs first and may fail without strengthening anything. (D) substitutes individual transactions for the shared rest the team was promised and misses the relational harm.
