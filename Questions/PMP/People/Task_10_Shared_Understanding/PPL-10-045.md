# Question

The team's wiki is large but most of it is outdated. People have stopped checking it and instead message a few "knowledgeable" colleagues, who are now overwhelmed with questions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add documentation maintenance into the Definition of Done, assign owners, and set a review cadence.
- **B**: Block the practice of asking colleagues directly and direct everyone back to the wiki for the next sprint instead.
- **C**: Hire a technical writer to take over the wiki and maintain it on the team's behalf going forward.
- **D**: Replace the wiki with regular knowledge-sharing meetings where the experts present updates to the whole team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The wiki's failure is a maintenance and ownership problem; fixing it means embedding documentation work in the team's normal flow with explicit owners and review cycles. (A) does this. (B) treats the symptom — asking colleagues — and pushes people back to a source they already know is unreliable. (C) outsources ownership and creates a key person who likely won't have the technical depth to keep the content accurate. (D) shifts knowledge transfer to meetings, which is harder to scale and search and doesn't replace persistent reference material.
