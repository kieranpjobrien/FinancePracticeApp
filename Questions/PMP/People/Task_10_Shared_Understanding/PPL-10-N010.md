# Question

A project manager wants to use the right technique for each shared-understanding problem on an agile programme. Match each situation to the technique most directly suited to building common understanding for it.

## Items

- **1**: The team and product owner disagree on what behaviour a feature should exhibit in specific edge cases
- **2**: New team members do not understand how the product creates value or who the users are
- **3**: Stakeholders hold different mental pictures of how a user moves through the whole experience end to end

## Matches

- **A**: Collaboratively build a user story map of the customer journey
- **B**: Run example mapping to capture concrete examples and rules for the feature
- **C**: Facilitate a product vision and personas workshop

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A

## Explanation

Example mapping (B) is purpose-built for resolving disagreements about specific behaviours and edge cases by grounding a feature in concrete examples and rules, matching situation 1. A vision and personas workshop (C) directly addresses newcomers not understanding the product's value or its users, matching situation 2. A user story map (A) lays out the end-to-end journey so stakeholders converge on a single picture of how users move through the experience, matching situation 3. Each technique targets a distinct kind of understanding gap, so swapping any pairing would apply the wrong tool to the problem.
