# Question

The team's Definition of Done is one paragraph long and every sprint review produces an argument about whether a story is actually done. The product owner and team consistently disagree on test coverage and documentation. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the disagreement to the sponsor and ask for a binding decision on exactly what constitutes Done for the remainder of the project deliverables going forward.
- **B**: Use the Scrum Master's authority to set a stricter Definition of Done and require the team and product owner to follow it from the next sprint.
- **C**: Wait for two more sprints to see whether the pattern resolves itself as the team matures and the product owner becomes more familiar with technical realities here.
- **D**: Facilitate a team-and-product-owner session to expand the DoD into specific, testable criteria, include it in the team's working agreement, and reference it in reviews.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI and the Scrum Guide both treat Definition of Done as a team-owned, specific, testable commitment — shared understanding is built by writing it together, not by imposing or escalating it. (A) kicks a craft decision up to a non-expert authority. (B) commands where the Scrum Master should facilitate. (C) watches value leak away for two more sprints.
