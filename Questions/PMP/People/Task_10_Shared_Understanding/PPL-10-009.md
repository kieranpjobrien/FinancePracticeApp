# Question

A PM suspects that team members are operating on different unstated assumptions about scope, sequencing, and external dependencies, but no one has surfaced them. The project is two months in and still in detailed planning. What technique would BEST surface these hidden assumptions?

## Options

- **A**: Ask each team lead to email their assumptions to the PM, who will consolidate and share back.
- **B**: Add an "assumptions" section to the weekly status report so issues surface as they come up.
- **C**: Run an assumption-mapping or pre-mortem workshop with the team to expose and validate implicit beliefs.
- **D**: Document the PM's understanding of the assumptions and ask the team to flag anything they disagree with.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Hidden assumptions are exposed most reliably by structured group techniques such as assumption mapping or pre-mortem ("imagine the project has failed — why?"); these surface beliefs that individuals would never write down because they don't realise they're making them. (C) gets the team into the same room and pulls the implicit into the explicit. (A) relies on people listing things they don't know they assume — the very problem you're trying to solve. (B) reacts to assumptions as they cause issues rather than surfacing them up front. (D) anchors on the PM's mental model and asks for objections, which tends to produce thin validation rather than genuine surfacing.
