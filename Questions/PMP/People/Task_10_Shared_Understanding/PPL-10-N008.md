# Question

Three sprints into building a customer self-service portal, the development team and the product owner are in low-grade but constant conflict: the team keeps building robust, configurable features while the product owner wants the simplest thing that lets customers complete a task. Both believe they are serving the product goal, and both can point to the product vision to justify their stance. Velocity is fine, but rework is high and morale is dropping. As the project manager, what is the most effective intervention?

## Options

- **A**: Re-run a product vision workshop so the team and product owner reconnect with the outcomes the product is meant to achieve
- **B**: Have the product owner articulate the goal of each upcoming story and the minimum that would satisfy it, so the team builds to intent story by story
- **C**: Facilitate the team and product owner in agreeing explicit guiding principles for right-sizing scope — what "good enough to ship" means and how they will decide when to add robustness
- **D**: Introduce a lightweight working agreement that any feature exceeding the agreed minimum requires the product owner's explicit sign-off before build

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Notice that both parties already share and can cite the vision, so the gap is not the vision itself but the absence of an agreed rule for translating it into "how much is enough" — which makes a vision workshop (A) a re-statement of common ground that will not resolve the recurring trade-off. Setting explicit, co-owned right-sizing principles (C) attacks exactly the missing shared understanding and equips the team to self-resolve the conflict on every future story. Clarifying intent story by story (B) helps locally but leaves the underlying disagreement about robustness to be re-fought each time, and it keeps the product owner as the per-story arbiter rather than building shared judgement. A sign-off gate (D) is a reasonable guardrail but manages the symptom through control and adds a bottleneck without the team and product owner actually agreeing where the line sits. C is the durable, jointly-owned fix; the others are narrower or address ground that is already shared.
