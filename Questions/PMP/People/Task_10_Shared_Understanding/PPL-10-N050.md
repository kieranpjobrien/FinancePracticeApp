# Question

Midway through a predictive ERP implementation, a business stakeholder reviews a configured module and says it does not match what she requested, though she had approved a signed requirements document. On discussion it is clear she approved wording whose practical implications for day-to-day use she could not fully picture. The remaining modules are configured the same way: detailed written requirements, signed off, then built. How should the project manager change the approach for the remaining modules?

## Options

- **A**: Before sign-off, walk each stakeholder through an interactive prototype or configured mock-up of the module performing their real tasks, and confirm acceptance against what they have seen working
- **B**: Have a business analyst translate each requirement into plain-language "day in the life" descriptions for stakeholders to review and approve
- **C**: Run a structured requirements walkthrough where the analyst reads each requirement aloud and the stakeholder confirms understanding clause by clause
- **D**: Have stakeholders co-author the requirements for the remaining modules with the analyst rather than reviewing a finished document

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stakeholder approved words she could not translate into real-world behaviour, so the validation has to be against something she can directly experience — an interactive prototype or mock-up of the module doing her actual work — which lets her judge fit before sign-off. Plain-language "day in the life" descriptions (B) help comprehension but still ask her to imagine behaviour from prose, the very step that failed her. A clause-by-clause walkthrough (C) confirms she has heard each requirement, not that her mental model of the resulting system matches what will be built. Co-authoring the requirements (D) gives her more involvement and is genuinely useful, but a non-technical author can still write or agree to wording whose practical consequences she cannot foresee; only seeing the behaviour reliably closes the translate-text-into-use gap.
