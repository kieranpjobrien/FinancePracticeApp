# Question

A co-located agile team building a retail loyalty app already keeps a prominent task board and a sprint-goal display on the wall. Even so, members keep acting on outdated assumptions about the goal and current priorities, leading to misdirected effort. Watching the team, the project manager notices the board is updated only once, at the start of the sprint, and is rarely touched again as priorities shift mid-sprint. What is the best way to make the board actually keep the team aligned?

## Options

- **A**: Build updating the board into the daily standup so the team revises the goal and priorities together as part of an event it already attends
- **B**: Assign one team member as the board owner responsible for keeping it current throughout the sprint
- **C**: Replace the physical board with a digital tool that syncs automatically from the team's work-tracking system
- **D**: Add a mid-sprint checkpoint meeting where the team reviews and resets the goal and priorities

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The board is a sound radiator that fails for one reason, it goes stale because nothing in the team's routine refreshes it, so the fix is to attach updating it to an event the team already holds daily, making the current picture a shared by-product of the standup. Option B keeps the board current but routes alignment through a single person's diligence; if that owner is absent or busy the board lapses, and the rest of the team still passively consumes rather than collectively maintains it. Option C changes the medium, and an auto-syncing tool can show task status, but a sprint goal and shifting priorities are not captured by a work-tracker feed, so the same judgement-based information still ages unless someone tends it. Option D does force a refresh, but a separate standalone meeting adds overhead and only resets alignment at a single point rather than keeping it continuously current the way a daily touch does. All four are real practices; A most directly turns the existing radiator into a living one at the lowest cost.
