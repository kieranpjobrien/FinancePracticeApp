# Question

Three team members each believe they are responsible for integration testing sign-off. When something goes wrong, each assumed one of the others would catch it. When things go well, each claims credit. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pick whichever team member has the strongest profile in testing and designate them sole owner of integration testing going forward, with the others taking supporting roles only.
- **B**: Rotate responsibility for integration testing sign-off across the three team members so each takes ownership by sprint and the role is unambiguous within that assigned window.
- **C**: Run an informal conversation to clarify who does what in testing and trust the team to resolve the overlap based on the discussion without documentation afterwards.
- **D**: Facilitate a RACI clarification for the testing value stream, agree it with the three and adjacent roles, and embed the result in the team's working agreement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Role ambiguity resolves through explicit shared artefacts — RACI or equivalent — that extend across adjacent roles, not through picking winners, rotations or informal understandings that decay. (A) picks an owner without examining why three people thought they owned it, preserving root-cause opacity. (B) dilutes accountability into a roster. (C) solves it in the room for the people in the room, leaving adjacent roles still misaligned on the documented reality.
