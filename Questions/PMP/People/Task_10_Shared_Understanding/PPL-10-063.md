# Question

The engineering team wants to dedicate the next sprint to reducing technical debt. The product owner keeps deferring it because "we need features." The team is frustrated; the product owner is frustrated; neither can articulate the trade-off to the sponsor in shared terms. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Decide as PM to dedicate 20% of every sprint to tech debt, codified in working agreement so neither side re-argues the trade-off each sprint going forward.
- **B**: Facilitate a session to translate tech debt into business consequences — velocity impact, defect risk, operational cost — and agree a sponsor-level trade-off approach together.
- **C**: Escalate the dispute to the sponsor with both positions and ask them to decide which side is correct, so the team can move forward on the next sprint.
- **D**: Take the product owner's view as controlling, since priorities sit with the product owner role, and document the tech debt for consideration when capacity allows it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Shared understanding of tech debt requires translating engineering language into business consequences the sponsor can weigh — the PM's facilitation job is the translation, not the verdict. (A) invents a policy without the business linkage that would make it stick. (C) passes a craft-and-business decision up without the translation work that would let the sponsor decide well. (D) defaults to one role's authority without the conversation that would justify the choice.
