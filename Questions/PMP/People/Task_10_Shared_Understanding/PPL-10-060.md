# Question

The PM has 10 minutes at the next executive committee to update them on a 24-month programme. The previous PM used the slot to walk through detailed Gantt milestones and lost the room within three minutes. The committee wants substance without drowning. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Present the full updated Gantt with more confidence and pace this time, since detailed milestone context is what executives in this committee expect from a PM update.
- **B**: Replace the Gantt with a written pre-read circulated 48 hours before and use the 10 minutes in the room purely for executive questions and clarifying discussion.
- **C**: Lead with outcomes and decisions needed, support with two or three metrics tied to business value, and hold detailed milestone data as backup only if specifically asked.
- **D**: Cut the detail and speak for five minutes at a high level, leaving the remaining five minutes open for committee members to raise anything they see as material.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's communication tailoring at executive level says lead with outcomes and decisions, support with the minimum sufficient evidence, hold detail in reserve — that is how shared understanding actually forms at that level. (A) repeats the failed pattern faster. (B) abandons a live conversation opportunity and relies entirely on the pre-read, which many executives won't have read. (D) leaves the agenda undefined and cedes framing to the committee rather than leading it.
