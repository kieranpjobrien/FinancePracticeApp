# Question

A development team frequently pulls stories into the sprint that turn out to be poorly understood, causing mid-sprint clarification scrambles. Some members blame the product owner for vague stories; the product owner blames the team for not asking questions. The project manager wants a durable fix that both sides genuinely own. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner and a couple of developers draft a story-quality checklist and circulate it to the rest of the team for adoption
- **B**: Introduce example mapping into refinement so each story's readiness is demonstrated through concrete agreed examples before it is pulled in
- **C**: Adopt an industry-standard definition-of-ready template and tailor it with the team in the next retrospective
- **D**: Facilitate the team and product owner in jointly agreeing a definition of ready that specifies what a story must contain before it can enter a sprint

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The blame cycle exists because there is no shared, co-owned standard for when a story is understood well enough to start, so the fix must be both explicit and owned by everyone who is currently pointing fingers. Jointly agreeing a definition of ready with the full team and product owner present (D) builds that shared standard and removes the "not my job" defence for both sides at once. A subset drafting a checklist for others to adopt (A) produces a similar artefact but without whole-team ownership, so the members who did not build it can still disown it — the exact dynamic causing the problem. Example mapping (B) is an excellent technique for understanding individual stories but is a per-story practice, not the agreed entry standard the team is missing. Tailoring a template in retrospective (C) is reasonable and close to D, but starting from an external template anchors the conversation on someone else's criteria rather than the team surfacing and agreeing what readiness means for them. D maximises genuine joint ownership, which is the stated requirement.
