# Question

A distributed agile team spanning three time zones is building an e-commerce checkout. The product owner writes terse one-line stories; developers in each location fill the gaps with their own assumptions, and the divergence only surfaces during integration. The product owner is capable and engaged but genuinely cannot expand every story herself, and the time-zone spread means the three groups rarely overlap live. As the project manager, what is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Have each location post its interpretation of a story before starting, and reconcile any differences before development begins
- **B**: Introduce collaborative refinement where the team co-authors concrete acceptance examples per story and the product owner confirms intent, run asynchronously across time zones
- **C**: Arrange a recurring live refinement at an hour that overlaps all three time zones so the product owner can clarify each story with everyone at once
- **D**: Nominate a lead developer in one location to set the canonical interpretation of each story for the others to follow

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root cause is that intent is never made explicit, so each group fills the gap privately; co-authoring concrete acceptance examples makes intent shared at the source, scales without the product owner writing exhaustive detail, and works asynchronously to fit the time-zone spread. Option A surfaces divergence usefully but only after each group has already formed its interpretation, adding a reconciliation step per story rather than converging on intent up front, and the product owner's authoritative intent may still be absent from that reconciliation. Option C can build genuine shared understanding too, but forcing a single regular overlap slot burdens at least one region with off-hours meetings and is fragile, making it a weaker fit than an async practice for this team. Option D creates one point of interpretation the other locations may not actually share, reproducing the original problem one level up. All four are workable; B best fits a capacity-limited product owner and a distributed team.
