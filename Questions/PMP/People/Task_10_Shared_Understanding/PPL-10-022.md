# Question

In planning poker, estimates for a single user story span from 3 points to 13 points. The Scrum Master sees three developers at the low end and three at the high end. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Average the estimates and adopt that as the team's estimate for the story.
- **B**: Use the higher number to give the team safety margin and protect the sprint.
- **C**: Have the team agree to break the story down into smaller items and re-estimate them individually.
- **D**: Ask the lowest and highest estimators to explain their reasoning, surface the assumptions, and re-estimate.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The point of planning poker is not the number; it is the conversation that the spread forces. A wide spread means the team holds different mental models of the story, and the productive next step is to expose those models. (D) does exactly that. (A) hides the disagreement behind an arithmetic average and ships divergent assumptions into the sprint. (B) protects schedule on paper without solving the comprehension gap. (C) may be the right action eventually, but only after the discussion reveals whether the spread is about size or about understanding — splitting first jumps to a structural fix without the diagnosis.
