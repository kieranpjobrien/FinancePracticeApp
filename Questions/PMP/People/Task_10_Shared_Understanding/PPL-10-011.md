# Question

A distributed team has been working together for four months but is still routinely misreading each other's chat messages — short replies are taken as curt, questions are read as criticism. The PM uses a chat-first communication norm. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Issue written communication guidelines reminding people to be polite and always assume good intent.
- **B**: Ask team leads to mediate any disagreements that arise from misread messages as they come up.
- **C**: Require every chat message to be reviewed before posting and to follow a standard template.
- **D**: Add a regular short video call so the team builds enough context to interpret messages charitably.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The misreadings stem from a shortage of relationship context, not a shortage of words on the page; voice and video carry the tone and rapport that text strips out. (D) treats the cause. (A) tells people to behave better but doesn't add the missing context that makes interpretation reliable. (B) treats each misunderstanding after the fact rather than preventing the next one and uses leads as a permanent translator layer. (C) adds friction and bureaucracy to every message and still won't supply tone.
