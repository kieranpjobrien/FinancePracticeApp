# Question

In retrospectives, the quieter half of the team rarely speaks. The PM has noticed they later raise significant concerns one-to-one. When asked why they don't speak in the group, they say "it's easier this way." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Restructure retrospectives to include a silent-write phase, small-group discussion, and rotating facilitation so that participation is not dependent on volunteering to speak first each round.
- **B**: Accept the pattern because the concerns are still reaching the PM through one-to-ones and intervening might feel coercive to team members who prefer that channel.
- **C**: Name individuals during retrospectives and ask them directly for their view so that their input becomes part of the public record and the team benefits from it.
- **D**: Record retrospectives and require every team member to contribute at least one item per session, so that input from the quieter half is captured consistently overall.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared understanding requires genuine participation, and for quieter voices the path is structural — format design that lowers the cost of speaking — not exhortation or compulsion. (B) leaves valuable input buried and preserves a safety problem in the group. (C) singles out individuals and can destroy the little safety that exists. (D) forces input quantity without lowering the speaking cost, which often produces performative contributions.
