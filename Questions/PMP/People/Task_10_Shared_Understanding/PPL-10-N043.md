# Question

A mobile banking team already has a Definition of Done that the team and product owner agreed at project start. Velocity looks healthy, yet the product owner keeps reopening "completed" stories because they lack accessibility support. The developers point out, correctly, that accessibility is nowhere in the agreed Definition of Done, so they consider the stories finished. The product owner believes accessibility was always obviously implied. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the product owner and team to revisit the Definition of Done together and decide explicitly whether accessibility belongs in it
- **B**: Bring the team and product owner together to write detailed accessibility acceptance criteria into the affected stories
- **C**: Support the product owner in adding accessibility as a non-functional requirement that applies across the whole backlog
- **D**: Facilitate a session for the team and product owner to agree how the existing stories will be reworked to add accessibility and reschedule them into upcoming sprints

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stories met the agreed Definition of Done, so the real gap is that the shared standard itself is silent on accessibility and the two sides hold different views about what it should cover; the durable move is to revisit and update that standard together so it explicitly resolves the question for all future work. Writing criteria into the affected stories (B) is collaborative but fixes only this batch and leaves the general standard ambiguous, so the next stories can be "done" yet inaccessible all over again. Adding accessibility as a blanket non-functional requirement (C) is reasonable but pre-decides the answer rather than letting the team and product owner weigh it, and a requirement held outside the Definition of Done can drift out of the team's working agreement. Agreeing how to rework and reschedule the affected stories (D) addresses the immediate remediation collaboratively, but it treats the symptom — the stories already reopened — without closing the gap in the shared standard that will keep producing reopens; the alignment here depends on making the standard itself explicit.
