# Question

A new analyst joins a project mid-flight. There are three folders of documentation, but they say it's overwhelming and they can't tell what matters. They've been on the team for a week and have produced almost nothing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pair them with an experienced team member and give a structured first-week plan with check-ins.
- **B**: Direct them to read the project charter, WBS, and latest status report, then meet with the PM to confirm.
- **C**: Assign them a small, low-context task so they can contribute immediately while they learn the project in parallel.
- **D**: Ask them to come to the PM with specific questions as they work their way through the documentation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first thinking favours active onboarding — pairing, structured context, and feedback loops — over leaving someone to absorb documentation alone. (A) gives the new joiner a knowledgeable guide, focus, and early signals. (B) is reading-led and places no human alongside them; the volume of documentation is exactly what they said is the problem. (C) keeps them busy but doesn't build the project understanding they will need to be useful. (D) makes the PM reactive and depends on the new joiner knowing what they don't know — the situation they're already in.
