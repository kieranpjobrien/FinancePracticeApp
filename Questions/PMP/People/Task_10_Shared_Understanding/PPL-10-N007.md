# Question

Midway through a predictive construction project, the client's facilities manager states that the building automation system "obviously" should integrate with their existing energy dashboard — something never captured in the requirements. The project manager confirms the contract and requirements make no mention of it; the manager genuinely believed it was always understood. The integration looks technically feasible but non-trivial. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the expectation, walk the manager through the documented scope, and explore together where the understanding diverged before deciding next steps
- **B**: Note the request in the issue log and prepare a change request quantifying the cost and schedule impact of adding the integration for the client to consider
- **C**: Convene the client and sponsor to clarify the original intent and reach a shared decision on whether the integration belongs in scope
- **D**: Confirm with the project team whether the integration is feasible and what it would take, so the conversation with the manager is grounded in facts

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Each option is a reasonable step and most will eventually happen, so the question is sequence. The immediate situation is a single stakeholder holding an expectation that conflicts with the documented baseline — before estimating, escalating, or scoping a solution, the PM needs to re-establish a shared understanding with that person of what was actually agreed and how the gap arose. Acknowledging and jointly tracing the divergence (A) does that and preserves the relationship, and it informs whether B, C, or D is even warranted. Preparing a change request (B) is the likely follow-on but pre-supposes the integration should be added, committing to a path before the misunderstanding is understood. Escalating to the sponsor (C) pulls in authority prematurely over what is so far a two-person interpretation gap. Checking feasibility with the team (D) gathers useful facts but answers "can we?" before the prior question "what did each side believe was promised, and why?" A is the necessary first move the others build on.
