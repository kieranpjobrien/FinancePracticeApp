# Question

A new developer joins a 15-month programme at month eight. After two weeks, retrospectives show she is confused about priorities, uncertain who decides what, and is hesitant in estimating. The team's existing members have stopped noticing how complex their context has become. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Give the new joiner a two-week observation period and expect her to pick up context organically through meetings and informal conversations with team members.
- **B**: Design structured onboarding — briefings, decision-rights walk-through, shadowing, paired work, a week-two checkpoint, and a team-specific glossary of key terms to use daily.
- **C**: Ask a team member to mentor the new joiner informally alongside their usual duties, and check in with her at the end of the first month.
- **D**: Extend the new joiner's probation period so there is more time for her to find her feet without pressure on individual sprint contributions during the ramp-up.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Shared understanding for a new joiner is a deliberately designed process, especially mid-programme when context has become dense; onboarding by osmosis is a privilege of simple contexts. (A) outsources onboarding to chance. (C) adds mentoring without structure, leaving gaps in what is covered. (D) trades time for design effort and frames a ramp-up problem as a performance concern.
