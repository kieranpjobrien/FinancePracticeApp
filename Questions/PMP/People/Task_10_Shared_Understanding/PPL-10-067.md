# Question

A key senior engineer has resigned and will leave in four weeks. She holds deep undocumented knowledge of the integration layer that no one else on the team has. Standard exit interviews and "update the wiki please" are not working in practice. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the engineer to write exhaustive wiki documentation during her final four weeks and review it with the team after her departure when issues arise in production operations.
- **B**: Pair her with two team members in real work on the integration layer, run recorded walkthroughs of key scenarios, and document decisions and rationale as the work proceeds.
- **C**: Offer a retention bonus to delay her departure by six months so the team has more time to naturally absorb the knowledge through the ordinary course of daily work.
- **D**: Hire an external contractor to interview her for two full weeks and produce a handover document that the team can reference when integration-layer questions arise later.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tacit knowledge transfers through shared practice — pairing, scenario-based walkthroughs, in-context decision capture — not through solo documentation or one-off interview extraction. (A) assumes the engineer can see what is tacit in her own head; she usually can't, which is why wikis fail. (C) pays to delay an inevitable problem rather than solving it. (D) captures narrative but not the practice, and the team still hasn't done the work hands-on.
