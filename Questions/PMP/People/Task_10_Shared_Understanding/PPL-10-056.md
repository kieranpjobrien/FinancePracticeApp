# Question

The PM discovers that each workstream maintains its own risk register in a different tool, with different severity scales and review cadences. The programme-level register is a manual rollup that is always two weeks out of date. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the current workstream registers and improve the manual rollup by assigning a PMO analyst to refresh the programme-level view weekly rather than fortnightly instead.
- **B**: Require each workstream to email weekly risk updates in a common template so the PM can consolidate them more quickly and consistently at the programme level.
- **C**: Agree a single tool, a single severity scale and a shared review rhythm with leads, and migrate the registers so one live programme-level view exists.
- **D**: Abandon the programme-level register since workstreams know their own risks best, and rely on a quarterly rollup review for steering-committee-level reporting instead of this.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Shared understanding of risk needs a single source of truth with agreed conventions, not a better manual rollup of incompatible artefacts. (A) automates the wrong process. (B) still produces a lagging consolidation and preserves the scale inconsistencies that make the rollup meaningless. (D) downgrades risk visibility at programme level, where cross-workstream risks live.
