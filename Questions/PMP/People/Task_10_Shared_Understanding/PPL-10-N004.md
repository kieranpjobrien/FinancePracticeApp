# Question

In backlog refinement for a mobile banking app, a developer says she is unsure what "the account screen should load quickly" requires; another developer assumes it means under one second. The product owner is present but is not certain what number the business actually needs either. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree a placeholder target of one second now and flag the story to revisit if performance data later shows it is wrong
- **B**: Have the product owner take an action to confirm the real performance need with the business and bring an agreed figure to finalise the story
- **C**: Capture concrete examples of acceptable and unacceptable load behaviour with the product owner now to define what "quickly" means in testable terms
- **D**: Define the criterion as "load time no worse than the current app" so it is measurable without needing a specific number yet

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Each option produces a measurable criterion, so the discriminator is whether the team agrees on a number that reflects the real need or merely one that is convenient. The product owner has admitted not knowing what the business requires, which means no one in the room holds the actual target — defining examples (C) or benchmarking to the current app (D) would crisply encode an unvalidated guess, and a placeholder (A) bakes an arbitrary figure into the build that is expensive to unwind once code depends on it. The honest gap is that the source of truth is absent, so the product owner taking an action to confirm the genuine need and return with an agreed figure (B) is the move that yields real shared understanding rather than a precise-looking assumption. The other options are sound techniques applied to information the team does not yet have.
