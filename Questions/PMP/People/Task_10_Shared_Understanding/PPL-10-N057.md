# Question

On a scaled agile programme, three Scrum teams each build part of an integrated payments service. Integration keeps failing because each team made different assumptions about a shared data contract between their components. Each team's internal understanding is solid; the gap is strictly between teams. The release integration window is approaching. What should the project manager prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Convene representatives from the three teams to jointly define, agree and document the shared data contract and the assumptions behind it
- **B**: Have a lead architect draft the data contract and facilitate a session with the three teams to challenge and ratify it together
- **C**: Stand up a scrum-of-scrums where the teams' representatives meet regularly to coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **D**: Introduce consumer-driven contract tests so each team's assumptions about the interface are made explicit and verified continuously

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The failures come from the teams holding different unstated assumptions about one shared interface, so the most direct fix is to get the teams themselves to jointly define and agree that contract, producing a single shared understanding they all own. An architect drafting it for the teams to ratify (B) is close and can work, but starting from one person's draft anchors the contract on the architect's assumptions and reduces the teams to reviewers, yielding weaker joint ownership than co-defining it. A scrum-of-scrums (C) is the right standing mechanism for coordinating dependencies generally, but it is a recurring coordination forum, not the focused act of nailing down this specific contract before the imminent window. Consumer-driven contract tests (D) are an excellent way to keep interface assumptions honest over time and surface mismatches automatically, but they verify and detect divergence rather than create the upfront agreement the teams currently lack, and setting them up does not by itself establish what the contract *should* be.
