# Question

A critical incident on a live system occurred during deployment last night. By 9am the rumour mill is ahead of the PM's first official message, and three competing narratives are circulating among stakeholders. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Delay any communication until the technical investigation is complete, since early messaging could introduce inaccuracies that then compete with the rumours already circulating among stakeholders today.
- **B**: Issue a short written note denying the worst of the rumours and commit to a full explanation once the investigation report is finalised later on in the day.
- **C**: Hold one-to-one conversations with the most influential stakeholders to give them an accurate picture individually, and let the message radiate outward informally from there onward.
- **D**: Issue a prompt holding communication — what is known, what is being investigated, next update time — then provide regular, short, accurate updates until it is resolved.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's crisis-communication principle is: own the narrative early with a prompt, accurate, regularly updated message; absence of an official voice is filled by rumour. (A) lets rumours harden while the PM waits for perfection. (B) spends credibility denying rather than informing, and then pauses for hours. (C) creates inconsistency as each private version is repeated with drift, and leaves out stakeholders outside the PM's inner circle.
