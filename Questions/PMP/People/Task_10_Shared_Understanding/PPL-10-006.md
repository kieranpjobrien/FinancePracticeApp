# Question

An agile team's velocity is healthy, but customer satisfaction with recent releases is falling. Several developers joined after the original product discovery and have never spoken to a real user; they are building stories as written without understanding the underlying problems. What should the product owner do?

## Options

- **A**: Add more detailed acceptance criteria and rationale paragraphs to each user story before refinement.
- **B**: Arrange for the new developers to observe customer interviews, sit in on support calls, or shadow users in their workflow.
- **C**: Have the original team members pair with the new developers on every story for the next two sprints.
- **D**: Restructure refinement so the product owner walks through user research findings at the start of each session.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Empathy with users is built through direct exposure, not documentation; PMI's people-first thinking and modern agile both stress that developers make better trade-offs when they understand the user's context. (B) closes the experience gap at its source. (A) adds documentation that the team can already follow procedurally — the deficit is understanding, not instructions. (C) helps with knowledge transfer but burdens the original members and still keeps the new developers one step removed from users. (D) is useful supporting practice but is a summary of someone else's research; it does not produce the same insight as direct contact.
