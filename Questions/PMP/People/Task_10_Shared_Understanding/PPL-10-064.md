# Question

Six months in, the PM notices the team repeatedly re-opens decisions from earlier in the project — not because circumstances changed, but because no one can remember why the original call was made. Each re-opening costs a meeting and a week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rely on meeting minutes — since they already record every decision — and reinforce the discipline of reading them before re-raising any previously discussed items at length.
- **B**: Tell the team that re-opening previously agreed decisions is not acceptable without new information, and document this firm stance in the team's current working agreement.
- **C**: Maintain a lightweight decision log — decision, context, rationale, date, owner — and use it as the first reference whenever a topic resurfaces during team discussions.
- **D**: Ask workstream leads to push back on any re-opening themselves, so the PM doesn't become a gatekeeper and each lead enforces decision stability in their area.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Shared understanding over time needs a lightweight decision log that captures rationale, not just outcome — that is what lets the team check whether new information truly has emerged. (A) relies on meeting minutes, which record decisions but rarely the rationale. (B) sets a rule without the artefact that would make the rule workable. (D) pushes a distributed gatekeeping burden on leads without giving them a shared reference source.
