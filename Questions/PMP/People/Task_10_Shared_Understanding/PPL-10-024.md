# Question

A PM sends thorough meeting minutes within two hours of every meeting, yet team members keep asking about decisions that are clearly recorded in those minutes. What is the PM's BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Restructure the minutes into a shorter "decisions and actions" summary at the top, with the full discussion below.
- **B**: Add a follow-up email asking each attendee to confirm they have read and understood the minutes.
- **C**: Move the meeting minutes from email into a project wiki where they are easier to search and reference.
- **D**: Confirm decisions and owners verbally at the end of each meeting and ask attendees to restate them before closing.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Documentation reinforces shared understanding but rarely creates it; the PMI-aligned response is to build the alignment in the meeting itself, then let the minutes serve as a reference. (D) does this — verbal confirmation in the room is a cheap, reliable check. (A) and (C) are useful improvements to the document but assume the issue is discoverability or length; the questions show people don't have the understanding regardless of format. (B) adds confirmation overhead but doesn't change the moment when alignment is actually formed.
