# Question

The PM and a key vendor disagree on what a particular clause in the signed contract actually commits the vendor to deliver. Each has consulted their own legal team and come back with a different reading. Work is stalled. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Convene a joint session with both legal teams and the two delivery leads, walk through intent and current reading together, and record the agreed interpretation in writing.
- **B**: Suspend the clause temporarily and proceed on the understanding that each side has acted in good faith, returning to the clause in the next formal contract review cycle.
- **C**: Concede the PM's reading and proceed on the vendor's interpretation to preserve the commercial relationship and unblock the stalled work on the current deliverables for now.
- **D**: Escalate the interpretation to external adjudication so an independent party decides the meaning and both sides are formally bound by the adjudicator's written determination going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Shared understanding of a contract clause is built by bringing the two sides' legal and delivery people into one conversation and writing down the agreed reading — adjudication is a last resort, not a first. (B) accumulates risk by proceeding without agreement. (C) concedes without evidence and sets a precedent for future interpretation disputes. (D) treats adjudication as the starting move, which is expensive and relationship-damaging when the lower-effort approach has not been tried.
