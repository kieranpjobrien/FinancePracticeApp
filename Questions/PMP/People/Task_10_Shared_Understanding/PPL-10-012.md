# Question

Stakeholders signed off on requirements four months ago and the team has built precisely what was documented. At the showcase, those same stakeholders say "this isn't what we asked for." The PM has the signed requirements to prove compliance. What is the BEST way to handle this on future projects?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on more thorough sign-off and add stakeholder initials to each requirement line.
- **B**: Replace freeform requirements with a strict template that forces complete specification.
- **C**: Build feedback loops with prototypes, examples, and incremental demos so intent is validated long before delivery.
- **D**: Move to a fixed-bid contracting model that makes scope changes a formal commercial event.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The pattern of "requirements met, intent missed" is exactly what iterative validation, prototyping, and incremental demos are designed to prevent — they let stakeholders react to something concrete while there is still cheap time to adjust. (C) is PMI's preferred response and aligns with both agile and tailored hybrid practice. (A) hardens the paperwork but does nothing to surface misalignment between text and intent. (B) imposes process burden without addressing the comprehension gap. (D) protects the PM commercially but delivers the wrong product on time and in budget — a worse outcome for the business.
