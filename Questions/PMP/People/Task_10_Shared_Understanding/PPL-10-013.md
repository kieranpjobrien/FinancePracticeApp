# Question

At a sprint review, stakeholders look at a complex feature the team has built and say "it's not quite right" but cannot say what would make it right. The team built it exactly to the documented acceptance criteria. What should the team do next?

## Options

- **A**: Use the working software itself as the discussion artefact, walk through alternative approaches, and iterate based on the concrete reactions.
- **B**: Ask the stakeholders to submit written clarifications and re-plan the work into the next sprint.
- **C**: Reopen the original requirements and reconfirm the acceptance criteria line by line with the product owner.
- **D**: Treat the feedback as out of scope for this sprint and add a follow-up story for grooming.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When stakeholders can't articulate what's wrong with a finished feature, abstract requirements work has failed — the way forward is to keep using the concrete artefact as a prompt and let reactions drive iteration. (A) does this and is exactly why sprint reviews exist. (B) sends the stakeholders back to the same medium that failed to capture intent the first time. (C) treats the symptom (criteria) and ignores that the criteria were met; rereading them won't surface the real gap. (D) defers a problem that is cheapest to solve while the work is fresh and the stakeholders are in the room.
