# Question

On a fintech mobile team, a payment story was marked complete and then failed live in the sprint demo. In the post-demo discussion it emerges that the two developers treated "done" as passing unit tests, while the tester assumed "done" meant passing end-to-end against the sandbox gateway. The team is otherwise collaborative and this is the first time the gap has surfaced. Nobody can point to where the threshold for completion was ever actually settled. What is the most effective way to prevent this recurring?

## Options

- **A**: Have the tester and developers walk through this story together to align on what its completion should have required
- **B**: Configure the pipeline so a story cannot move to done until its end-to-end sandbox tests pass
- **C**: Add an end-to-end sandbox check to the acceptance criteria of every payment-related story going forward
- **D**: Facilitate the team to agree and post a shared definition of done that governs all stories, then apply it each sprint

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The disagreement is not about anything specific to the payment story; it is about what "done" means in general, an assumption every story inherits, so the durable fix is one explicit, team-owned standard that governs every story rather than a patch to the one that failed. Option A aligns the team on this single past story but does not establish the general standard, so a differently shaped story reopens the gap. Option B enforces one specific check automatically, which is valuable, but it hard-codes a single criterion for one path and leaves other "done" expectations (code review, documentation, non-payment flows) untouched, so the underlying ambiguity persists elsewhere. Option C embeds the rule only in payment stories, treating a team-wide standard as a per-story attribute and leaving every other type of work exposed. All four are real practices; only D is scoped to the actual problem — a shared understanding of completion across all the team's work.
