# Question

On a hybrid satellite-manufacturing programme, three workstreams run their own tools and report status in their own formats. At each steering meeting the sponsor and workstream leads spend most of the time arguing over whose numbers are right rather than what to do. The figures genuinely differ because each workstream maintains its own snapshot on its own cadence. The project manager wants the recurring confusion gone for good, not just managed each cycle. Which approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate a common status-report template each workstream completes before every steering meeting
- **B**: Stand up one shared, continuously updated progress view that all workstreams feed and everyone reads from the same source
- **C**: Add a pre-meeting reconciliation call where the leads align the figures before the sponsor sees them
- **D**: Define one set of common progress measures and a single reporting cadence all three workstreams adopt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The disputes come from three separately maintained pictures refreshed at different times, so a single continuously updated source that all feed and read from (B) is the only option that makes the numbers consistent by construction and keeps everyone aligned between meetings, not merely inside them. A common template (A) standardises the format but still yields three independent snapshots that can disagree the moment they are taken, so the "whose numbers" argument survives. A pre-meeting reconciliation call (C) does produce one agreed picture for the meeting and is a defensible stopgap, but it negotiates the figures into agreement every cycle and leaves between-meeting decisions exposed — it manages the symptom rather than removing it. Aligning measures and cadence (D) is genuinely close and necessary: shared definitions and timing remove much of the divergence. Yet without a single source the three still report separately, and any drift between their copies reopens the gap — D makes the inputs comparable, whereas B also makes the output one thing everyone literally sees.
