# Question

Two product teams that previously worked independently are being merged for a single programme. Each has its own ceremonies, terminology, and definitions of "done." After the first joint sprint, friction is high and progress is poor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pick the more mature team's practices as the standard and require the other to adopt them quickly.
- **B**: Document both sets of practices side-by-side and ask team members to use whichever suits the work in front of them.
- **C**: Allow each team to keep their existing practices for now and revisit standardisation after a few sprints of running side-by-side.
- **D**: Run a joint working session where both teams share their practices, agree which to keep, and co-design unified ways of working.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When two teams must operate as one, shared practice has to be co-created — that's what produces buy-in and surfaces the legitimate reasons each team works the way it does. (D) does this. (A) saves time on paper but creates resentment, loses good practices from the other team, and doesn't address the underlying friction. (C) defers an integration problem that will only get more entrenched. (B) institutionalises two ways of working under one roof, which is exactly the friction the PM is trying to remove.
