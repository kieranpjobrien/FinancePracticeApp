# Question

On a hybrid programme, a design team hands specifications to a separate build team each phase. Defects repeatedly trace back to the build team interpreting the designs differently from what the design team intended, even though the documents are thorough. The two teams sit in different buildings and interact almost entirely through documents. Which actions would most directly build shared understanding between the teams? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Hold joint design walkthroughs where the build team questions the design team and confirms intent before building
- **B**: Embed a design team member with the build team during the early part of each build phase
- **C**: Have the design team produce annotated worked examples showing how a sample requirement should be built, and review them with the build team
- **D**: Create a shared backlog of open interpretation questions that both teams review together regularly
- **E**: Add a design-team reviewer who inspects the build against the specification and logs deviations for the build team to correct

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

The defects arise from intent that documents alone cannot fully convey between two separated teams, so the effective remedies create direct, two-way human interaction: joint walkthroughs to confirm intent before building (A), an embedded designer answering questions in context (B), and a shared question backlog the teams work through together (D). Annotated worked examples reviewed together (C) are genuinely useful and close to the spirit of the answer, but a single sample worked one-way still relies on the build team correctly generalising from one example, and the option centres on the design team producing artefacts rather than the teams jointly reaching agreement. A design-team reviewer logging deviations (E) is a downstream inspection that catches misinterpretation after the build has gone wrong; it corrects defects but does not build the upfront shared understanding that prevents them, and a one-way deviation log can feel like policing rather than alignment.
