# Question

The PM's current dashboard has 28 metrics. Each stakeholder looks at a different subset; no one looks at the whole thing. Decisions are being made on whichever metric someone happened to see. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the full dashboard intact so every stakeholder has access to anything they might want and let individual stakeholders curate what they personally review.
- **B**: Push for stronger data discipline by reviewing all 28 metrics at the start of every steering committee meeting, so decisions are always based on the complete picture.
- **C**: Run a metric-culling session with stakeholders mapping each metric to a decision it drives, retain only those, and tailor views by role around the surviving ones.
- **D**: Replace the dashboard with a monthly narrative status document summarising the key points, so the team no longer relies on metrics people interpret selectively.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring and reporting principles say metrics exist to drive decisions, so culling happens by linking each metric to the decision it supports and removing the rest. (A) preserves noise and guarantees fragmented attention. (B) reviews noise rather than removing it and destroys committee time. (D) swaps one format for another without fixing the underlying question — which metrics actually matter.
