# Question

Two team leads are in conflict: each genuinely believes the other has broken a prior agreement, and each cites remembered conversations that support their version. Both are normally reasonable. The team is starting to take sides. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both leads together, facilitate a shared reconstruction using any written artefacts, confirm the current position in writing, and address the behaviour going forward together.
- **B**: Meet each lead separately, hear their version, and decide as PM which one is correct, so the conflict can be closed and the team moves forward.
- **C**: Ask the leads to put their disagreement aside for the sake of the team and reset their working relationship without dwelling on who said what previously.
- **D**: Escalate the conflict to the sponsor as a behavioural issue and request that the sponsor speak to both leads about their professional conduct on this project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Memory conflicts are resolved by reconstructing evidence together and agreeing the forward position, not by picking a winner or asking the dispute to evaporate. (B) produces a judgment without shared understanding and preserves the grievance. (C) suppresses the dispute rather than resolving it, and it will resurface. (D) escalates a conflict the PM is equipped to facilitate.
