# Question

A Scrum team's Definition of Done is informal — different developers apply different quality bars, and rework is appearing late in each sprint. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the organisation's standard Definition of Done from another team and brief this team on it.
- **B**: Have the senior developer draft a formal Definition of Done and circulate it for the team to follow.
- **C**: Facilitate a team workshop to write a clear, shared Definition of Done collaboratively, then make it visible to everyone.
- **D**: Add a quality review step to each sprint where the Scrum Master checks completed work before review.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In Scrum, the Definition of Done is owned by the team; collaborative creation is what makes it stick and what surfaces the differing quality assumptions causing the current variance. (C) builds shared understanding and ownership in one move. (A) imports another team's context and is unlikely to fit; the team had no say in it. (B) gives one developer authority over a team artefact and reduces collective ownership. (D) inserts the Scrum Master as a gatekeeper, which contradicts the role and treats the symptom rather than fixing the unclear standard.
