# Question

The design team and the engineering team on a digital product each believe the other doesn't understand their constraints. Designs arrive that engineers say are infeasible; engineering push-backs arrive that designers say misread the intent. Throughput is dropping. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Require every design to include an explicit engineering feasibility review before being considered complete, so infeasible designs are caught earlier in the delivery process overall.
- **B**: Introduce a shared working session where design and engineering co-create upfront — early feasibility dialogue, constraint-mapping and shared criteria — for significant work.
- **C**: Appoint a lead to arbitrate between design and engineering whenever they disagree, so that the dispute has a defined resolution path and throughput is not blocked.
- **D**: Keep the current handover model but add weekly alignment meetings between the two leads so that any points of misunderstanding can be resolved outside the work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Shared understanding between functions is built by doing work together upfront, not by adding reviews or arbitrators that preserve the silos. (A) is late feasibility gating — it catches problems after effort, not before. (C) institutionalises disputes rather than preventing them. (D) adds a meeting layer but leaves the same two-team handover dynamic that produced the disputes.
