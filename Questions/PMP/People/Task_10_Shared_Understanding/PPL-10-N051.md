# Question

A Scrum team delivers steadily but routinely gold-plates features the product owner considers low value while skimping on items the product owner sees as critical. The backlog is clearly prioritised with rankings, yet the team's effort does not follow it. Probing reveals the developers genuinely do not understand *why* the high-priority items matter to the business. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner share the product vision and the business rationale behind the priorities, and keep connecting each upcoming item back to the value it delivers
- **B**: Have the product owner add a short "why this matters / value" note to each high-priority backlog item so the rationale travels with the work
- **C**: Invite the team to sprint reviews with the end customers so they hear directly how the product is used and what hurts when it is weak
- **D**: Ask the product owner to talk the team through the relative value of items during backlog refinement so trade-offs are understood before work starts

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team is misallocating effort because they lack the overarching understanding of what drives value, and the most durable fix gives them the product vision and the reasoning behind priorities and keeps reinforcing it, so they can make value-aligned judgement calls on any item, not just the ones explained to them. Per-item value notes (B) help and are worth doing, but rationale attached item-by-item conveys local justification without the bigger picture that lets the team generalise. Exposure to real customers at reviews (C) is a powerful way to build empathy and will reinforce the why, yet on its own it is indirect and intermittent — it supplements the vision rather than substituting for the product owner explicitly sharing it. Walking through relative value at refinement (D) is good practice and overlaps with the answer, but it operates one refinement at a time and still leaves the team without the standing understanding of the vision that makes their day-to-day choices align with value.
