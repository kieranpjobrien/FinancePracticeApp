# Question

Halfway through an airport baggage-handling upgrade, the project manager discovers the three sponsoring departments hold genuinely different views of what success means — throughput, reliability and cost recovery — and each has been quietly steering decisions toward its own. The departments are peers with no single authority over the others. Which actions are most appropriate to rebuild a shared understanding of success? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Convene the three departments together to surface each one's success criteria openly and see where they conflict
- **B**: Collect each department's criteria separately in advance, then synthesise a proposed ranking for the group to react to
- **C**: Facilitate the three to agree a single prioritised set of success criteria they all commit to
- **D**: Refer the competing definitions to the steering committee for a binding ranking, then brief the departments on the outcome
- **E**: Record the agreed criteria in the project's baseline documents and confirm them back to the group

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

With peer stakeholders holding divergent definitions of success and no one able to overrule the others, rebuilding alignment means getting the differences into the open together (A), facilitating agreement on one prioritised set they jointly own (C), and capturing and confirming it so the shared understanding is durable and visible (E) — surface, reconcile, lock in. Pre-collecting criteria and bringing a ready-made ranking (B) is efficient, but it casts the project manager as author of the answer and asks the departments only to react; it skips the joint reconciliation that creates genuine buy-in among peers and can entrench the divergence behind a tidy proposal. Referring the ranking to a steering committee (D) may eventually be needed if the peers truly deadlock, but reaching for an imposed binding ruling before they have attempted to align manufactures compliance rather than shared understanding — departments briefed on a verdict have been overruled, not aligned.
