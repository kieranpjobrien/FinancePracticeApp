# Question

In backlog refinement, the product owner keeps describing a complex story in business terms while the team keeps responding with technical constraints. After 30 minutes neither side feels understood. What technique should the Scrum Master suggest?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner to draft more detailed acceptance criteria before the next refinement session.
- **B**: Move the story to the next refinement and ask a business analyst to translate between the two perspectives.
- **C**: Use collaborative modelling — story mapping, concrete examples, or whiteboard diagrams — to create a shared visual artefact.
- **D**: Extend the meeting by 30 minutes so the conversation can continue until alignment is reached.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When two parties keep talking past each other in words, switching media — to visuals, examples, or models — usually breaks the deadlock by giving them something concrete to point at and refine together. (C) builds a shared mental model in the room. (A) keeps the same written/verbal medium that is already failing and pushes the problem to the future. (B) introduces a translator but never builds direct understanding between the people who actually need to collaborate. (D) just adds more of the unproductive conversation; longer is not the same as clearer.
