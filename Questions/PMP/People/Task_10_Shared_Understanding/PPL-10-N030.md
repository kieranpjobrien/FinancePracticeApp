# Question

A predictive ERP implementation has a signed charter, but two months in the finance and procurement leads describe the project's scope differently when their teams ask, and end users are now confused about what will actually change. On inspection the two leads are not in open conflict; each has quietly extrapolated the charter's high-level wording in a direction that suits their own function, and neither realises the other has done the same. As the project manager, what should you do first?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a joint working session where the two leads compare how each has been describing the scope, surface where and why their readings diverge, and agree a single more specific statement
- **B**: Produce a detailed scope statement yourself from the charter and the requirements, then have both leads review and endorse it
- **C**: Meet each lead separately to learn how they are interpreting the scope, then reconcile the two readings into one version to bring back to them
- **D**: Publish a clear, plain-language summary of what is changing directly to the end users to stop the confusion spreading while you work the scope out with the leads

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The leads do not yet know their interpretations diverge, so the first move must bring that divergence into the open between them and let them resolve it into one jointly owned statement; alignment the two messengers reach themselves is what makes the cascade consistent. Option B can produce a perfectly good document, but the project manager drafting it first means the leads react to someone else's version rather than confronting and reconciling their own assumptions, and they may endorse it while their underlying readings still differ. Option C gathers each view but keeps the leads apart, leaving the project manager to broker a reconciliation they never witness, so each can walk away with a private gloss intact. Option D addresses the downstream symptom for end users but does nothing to align the two leads, who keep emitting conflicting messages; it is a reasonable parallel step, not the first thing that fixes the cause. All four are legitimate; only A makes the two leads see and close the gap together before anything cascades.
