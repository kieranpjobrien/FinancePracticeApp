# Question

The portfolio director proposes swapping your two highest-performing developers with two developers from another project that is struggling. Both projects have similar delivery pressure and neither team has been consulted. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse the swap outright to protect current team velocity and ask the portfolio director to solve the other project's issue using external contractor capacity instead.
- **B**: Accept the swap and coach the incoming developers intensively so that some of the lost team continuity can be recovered through onboarding over the first two sprints.
- **C**: Engage with the portfolio director, the other PM, and both teams to understand the problem the swap is trying to solve and propose alternatives that preserve team cohesion.
- **D**: Propose a time-limited loan of one developer instead of a permanent swap, with a clear end date, so that the sending team retains a path back to its original composition.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI uses targeted, reversible moves with clear end dates to address a specific problem without permanently disrupting high-performing teams. (D) is that move. (A) protects velocity but refuses to help the portfolio, which is not the PM's full remit. (B) accepts a permanent composition change that damages two teams for one problem. (C) is a reasonable step but on its own it does not converge on an action; (D) is both engaged and concrete.
