# Question

Five sprints into building a hospital scheduling product, the team delivers steadily, but the same two engineers handle every electronic-health-record integration task while the rest gravitate away from it. A release roughly two quarters out will be heavily integration-focused. Current sprints are comfortably met. What is the BEST action now?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the upcoming integration-heavy stories forward into the next few sprints while the two specialists have spare capacity, smoothing the future peak.
- **B**: Run a focused internal workshop where the two specialists teach EHR integration patterns to the rest of the team ahead of the demanding release.
- **C**: Deliberately pair other engineers with the two integration specialists on the EHR stories arriving in current sprints, so the skill spreads through real work before the heavy release.
- **D**: Add the integration-skill concentration to the risk register and revisit resourcing options when the heavy release enters planning.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A known future demand plus a concentrated skill calls for growing capability now, and capability grows most reliably by doing the real work with support. Pairing others onto the EHR stories already flowing builds genuine competence in context before the load arrives (C). (A) smooths the peak but does nothing about the key-person concentration; the same two people simply do the work earlier, and pulling future stories forward can disrupt the current backlog's value order. (B) transfers knowledge but a workshop without hands-on application tends to fade, and it pulls the specialists off delivery for teaching that sticks less well than pairing. (D) is responsible record-keeping yet defers action on a concentration the team has runway to fix now, wasting the lead time.
