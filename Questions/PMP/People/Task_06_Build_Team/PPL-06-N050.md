# Question

A predictive analytics migration needs cloud-security expertise for a phase starting in four months; no current team member has it. The same capability is on the roadmap for two further projects in the portfolio over the next two years. The project manager weighs three paths: train a capable existing analyst, contract a specialist for this phase, or recruit a permanent hire. Training would take most of the four months and carries some risk the analyst is not ready in time. Which recommendation best fits the situation?

## Options

- **A**: Recruit a permanent hire, because the capability is a recurring portfolio need and retained in-house expertise serves this and the later projects.
- **B**: Contract a specialist for this phase, removing the timing risk, while starting to train the analyst so the capability is in-house for the later projects.
- **C**: Train the existing analyst, since growing internal capability is the most durable answer to a recurring organisational need.
- **D**: Contract a specialist for this phase, treating the immediate need as the decision and revisiting the longer-term capability question when the next project is chartered.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The need is both immediate-and-time-constrained and recurring, so the strongest play covers both horizons: contracting removes the four-month timing risk for this phase, while training the analyst builds the retained capability the portfolio will need (B). (A) suits the recurring need but a new hire may not be productive within four months, leaving this phase exposed. (C) addresses the durable need yet stakes a time-critical phase on training that might not finish in time. (D) safely covers this phase but defers a capability question the roadmap has already answered, guaranteeing the same scramble next time.
