# Question

An agile team building a tax-filing platform is two sprints behind a fixed statutory deadline that cannot move. The programme manager offers four experienced engineers from a team that has just disbanded, available to join the eight-person team immediately. The backlog splits into two reasonably coherent areas. The Scrum Master fears that absorbing four people at once could slow delivery before it helps. What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the four and instead work with the product owner to cut scope to the minimum that satisfies the statutory requirement by the deadline.
- **B**: Add the four to the existing team but in a staged way over the remaining sprints, starting with one or two so onboarding load stays manageable.
- **C**: Accept all four immediately and have the original team's members pair with them so the newcomers reach productivity as fast as possible.
- **D**: Stand the four up as a second team owning one coherent area of the backlog, coordinating with the original team through a shared product owner.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Late in a constrained delivery, dropping four newcomers into one team imposes onboarding and communication overhead that can slow it further (Brooks's law). Because the backlog splits cleanly, forming a second team around a coherent area lets the experienced engineers own work with minimal entanglement in the original team's flow (D). (A) is a legitimate and often wise lever — protecting a fixed deadline by cutting scope — but it discards genuinely useful capacity without first considering whether that capacity can be absorbed productively; it is a fallback if structuring the people well is not feasible. (B) staged onboarding into the existing team is sound thinking, yet with a hard near-term deadline even a trickle adds coordination load to the very team under pressure, where a separate team avoids it. (C) pairing is supportive, but pairing all four at once pulls the original team's members off delivery to onboard, slowing the team exactly when it can least afford it.
