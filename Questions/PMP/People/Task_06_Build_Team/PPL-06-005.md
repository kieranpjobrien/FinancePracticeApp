# Question

You are staffing a six-month project and can either secure experienced specialists who are available for only the first four months or less experienced staff available for the whole duration. How should the project manager decide?

## Options

- **A**: Choose the experienced specialists because their early output will dominate the overall delivery.
- **B**: Choose the less experienced staff because continuity across the project always matters more than depth.
- **C**: Assess task complexity, phase boundaries, and knowledge-transfer feasibility, then choose the option that best fits those factors.
- **D**: Split the work so each cohort owns a distinct set of deliverables end to end.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle rejects blanket answers and pushes the PM to decide based on project context. (C) looks at complexity, natural handover points, and transfer feasibility. (A) and (B) absolutise one factor and ignore the other. (D) is a structural choice that may or may not fit; it should follow from the analysis in (C), not replace it.
