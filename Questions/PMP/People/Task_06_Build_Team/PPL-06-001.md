# Question

A project manager is assembling a team for a year-long transformation programme and has some latitude to select members from several departments. What approach should they take to team composition?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate for the most technically skilled individuals the departments can release, since technical capability determines delivery.
- **B**: Accept the first people each functional manager offers, to avoid political friction during resourcing.
- **C**: Prioritise people who have worked together previously so the team can skip through the early stages of Tuckman.
- **D**: Identify the mix of technical and collaborative skills the programme needs, then negotiate specific individuals who together cover that mix.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats team composition as a PM responsibility that balances technical coverage and team dynamics, not a single-factor optimisation. (D) uses a skills profile to drive negotiation, which is what resource planning calls for. (A) ignores how teams actually perform and risks a group that cannot collaborate. (B) abdicates the PM role in shaping the team. (C) is useful but not sufficient on its own and can import groupthink.
