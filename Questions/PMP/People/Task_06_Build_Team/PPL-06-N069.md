# Question

A project manager building a team for a hybrid logistics-platform programme faces four team-formation problems in the same week. Each could be addressed with more than one artifact, so the task is to match each problem to the artifact that targets its actual cause rather than a related one. Match each situation on the left to the most appropriate response on the right.

## Items

- **1**: Two team members each believed the other was going to sign off the integration test deliverable; it slipped because both assumed it was someone else's call. Allocation and calendars are fine — the confusion is purely about who is accountable.
- **2**: A senior engineer is fully committed to the programme on paper, but the team keeps planning work for her in weeks when she is actually allocated to another release, so sprints over-commit and slip.
- **3**: A newly merged squad keeps clashing over how it works — when to escalate, what "done" means, how disagreements get settled — and wants to set its own rules in week one.
- **4**: The team is staffed and roles are clear, but a deliverable is at risk because no one currently on the team can actually do the specialist data-modelling the WBS requires.

## Matches

- **A**: Facilitate the team to co-create a working agreement (team charter).
- **B**: Build a resource calendar showing each person's real allocation across the delivery windows.
- **C**: Develop a RACI responsibility assignment matrix.
- **D**: Run a skills assessment and gap analysis against the WBS.

## Answer

1-C, 2-B, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Each situation is written to be confusable with a neighbouring artifact, so the match turns on the true cause. Situation 1 looks like a ground-rules problem, but the failure is undefined accountability for one deliverable — precisely what a RACI matrix fixes by naming who is Accountable (1-C); a charter sets general norms, not per-deliverable ownership. Situation 2 looks like a commitment dispute, yet the person is committed in principle and the breakdown is planning work into windows when she is unavailable — a resource calendar showing real allocation across delivery windows is what prevents that over-commitment (2-B), where a RACI would clarify roles she is not disputing. Situation 3 is the genuine norms-and-ways-of-working case the team wants to own itself, captured in a co-created working agreement or charter (3-A). Situation 4 is a true capability shortfall against the work, located by a skills assessment and gap analysis against the WBS (4-D), not a roles or scheduling issue. Matching on the surface cue — "two members confused" to a charter, or "fully committed" to RACI — produces the wrong pairing.
