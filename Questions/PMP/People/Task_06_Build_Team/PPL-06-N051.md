# Question

A project manager adapts how she leads a forming agile team to what each situation needs. Match each situation to the leadership response that best fits it.

## Items

- **1**: The team is new and capable but unsure how to start; members keep asking the project manager what to do and in what order.
- **2**: The team now openly disputes technical direction and roles, and the disputes keep recurring because there is no agreed way to settle them.
- **3**: The team consistently meets its commitments and makes sound calls on its own, yet still routes routine decisions through the project manager out of habit.

## Matches

- **A**: Give clear initial direction and structure to get the team moving, while explaining the reasoning so they can take it on themselves.
- **B**: Facilitate the team in agreeing how it will make decisions and handle disagreement, so conflicts resolve through an owned process.
- **C**: Deliberately step back and delegate, encouraging the team to own decisions it is already equipped to make.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C

## Explanation

A new but able team that lacks direction needs the leader to provide structure up front while transferring the reasoning, not to withhold guidance (1-A). Recurring disputes with no resolution mechanism call for facilitating an agreed decision-making process rather than the leader settling each clash (2-B). A team that is demonstrably self-sufficient but over-relies on the leader needs the leader to step back and delegate so the habit gives way to genuine autonomy (3-C). The skill is matching the response to the team's actual need, not labelling the stage.
