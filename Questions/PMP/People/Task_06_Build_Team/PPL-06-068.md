# Question

A small discretionary reward pool has been given to you to recognise team performance. You are weighing between splitting it evenly, rewarding a few top performers, or letting the team decide. Two members are known to push back strongly against both even-split and top-performer approaches. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Split the pool evenly across the whole team to avoid any arguments and rely on non-financial recognition such as public praise to differentiate contribution levels across the individual members.
- **B**: Reward only the top three performers based on your own assessment of delivery impact, on the principle that visible differentiation reinforces high-performance behaviours across the team over time.
- **C**: Give the reward pool to the line managers of each team member, on the basis that the PM should not be making pay-like decisions about people they do not manage directly.
- **D**: Co-design the recognition approach with the team: agree the criteria, use peer input, and combine individual and team-level elements so the result reflects what the team values most.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI favours participative recognition that reflects team-defined value, not pure equality, pure differentiation, or complete outsourcing. (D) builds buy-in and fairness together. (A) flattens differentiation without addressing the members who object. (B) relies entirely on one person's assessment in a team-performance context. (C) defers a team-building opportunity to line managers who may not see the project work at all.
