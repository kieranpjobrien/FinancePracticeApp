# Question

A hybrid programme to modernise a freight company's booking platform is staffing from three offices — London, Bengaluru, and São Paulo — which share only about two hours of overlap each day. The skills are excellent in all three. The first phase is dominated by architecture work needing frequent, fast back-and-forth between the lead architect, who can only be sourced in London, and two senior engineers. Equally strong candidates for those two engineering seats exist in both Bengaluru and São Paulo. The project manager is deciding how to compose the team. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Recruit the two senior engineers in London alongside the architect so the trio that needs constant real-time interaction shares a time zone, and distribute the independent roles across all three offices.
- **B**: Recruit the two engineers in Bengaluru, whose two-hour overlap with London is workable, keeping the architect's office leaner while still placing the trio across only two zones.
- **C**: Source the engineers wherever the strongest individuals are and offset the time-zone split with a documented architecture spec and asynchronous design reviews.
- **D**: Set overlapping core hours across the locations and have the architect and engineers reserve that window each day for the architecture decisions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The architecture phase depends on rapid, frequent exchange, and the candidates for the two engineering seats are equally strong in every location, so there is no capability cost to placing them with the architect — co-locating the trio in one time zone removes the collaboration friction entirely while the independent roles still draw on the wider talent pool (A). Putting the engineers in Bengaluru still straddles two time zones, leaving the trio with only a two-hour daily window for work that needs constant interaction — better than three zones, but it accepts a friction the equal-strength London option eliminates (B). Chasing the strongest individuals and papering over the gap with specs and async reviews sacrifices the fast iteration the architecture work specifically requires, and since the talent is equal everywhere it trades nothing for that loss (C). Reserved core hours help, but they cap the trio's high-bandwidth collaboration at the slim overlap and force a daily constraint that co-locating them would make unnecessary (D). With capability equal across locations, clustering the high-interaction roles in one time zone is the targeted composition choice.
