# Question

A project manager inherits a hybrid satellite-ground-station programme where the previous manager left no record of which engineers hold which competencies. New work packages are being assigned, and the project manager keeps discovering skill mismatches only after assignments go wrong. Several mismatches have already caused rework. What should the project manager do to make assignments reliable going forward?

## Options

- **A**: Build a team skills inventory through structured self-assessment and lead review, then assign and plan development against it.
- **B**: Sit with each engineer individually to review their recent work and assign the next package based on demonstrated, current competence.
- **C**: Ask the two longest-serving engineers to map who on the team is strong in what, and assign from their shared knowledge of the group.
- **D**: Have engineers self-select work packages from the backlog, on the basis that people know their own strengths best.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four could improve matching, but only one creates a durable, transparent basis the PM can use for both assignment and development. A documented skills inventory makes the whole team's capability visible and reusable, supporting deliberate assignment and targeted growth (A). (B) gathers good evidence but produces tacit, point-in-time judgements in the PM's head rather than a shared artefact, so the next assigner inherits the same blind spot. (C) is faster but concentrates team knowledge in two people, is hard to keep current, and embeds their biases. (D) leans on self-knowledge, which helps engagement but can over- or under-state ability and gives the PM no way to plan capability across the programme.
