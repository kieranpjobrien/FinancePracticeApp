# Question

An agile team is being stood up to rebuild a regional bank's mobile-payments app. The charter named one back-end specialist as pre-assigned because she wrote the original payment gateway. When the project manager confirms the rest of the roster with the development manager, he learns she is now allocated 80% to a regulatory remediation programme for the next two sprints and can give the new team only occasional advice. The first two sprints contain the gateway integration the team cannot complete without that knowledge. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reschedule the gateway integration stories into later sprints when the specialist is expected to be released, and start the team on other backlog items.
- **B**: Discuss the conflict with the development manager and the remediation programme's lead to agree how the specialist's time is shared across both efforts for the next two sprints.
- **C**: Accept her occasional advice and have two of the new team's engineers begin the integration now, escalating only if they get stuck.
- **D**: Raise a staffing risk with the sponsor and ask them to direct the specialist's full release to the new team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The specialist was pre-assigned but is now double-booked, so the immediate need is to resolve the competing claim on her time with the two managers who actually control it before resorting to workarounds (B). The conflict is between two real demands, and only the people who own both can agree a workable split; settling that first either frees enough of her time or makes the genuine shortfall visible. (A) reorders the backlog to avoid the problem, but it defers value the bank is expecting and assumes a future release that has not been negotiated, so the conflict simply recurs later. (C) sets two engineers onto critical integration without the tacit knowledge they lack, accepting delivery risk locally and escalating only after damage is done. (D) jumps to the sponsor before the project manager has even attempted to resolve a routine resource conflict with peers, which is premature escalation; the sponsor is the right move only if the managers cannot agree.
