# Question

Your programme is closing in four months and the team of sixteen will need to transition to new roles. Some members are anxious about next assignments, and you have noticed a drop in engagement on late-stage deliverables. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Partner with HR and functional managers now to map transition options, share the process openly with the team, and keep final deliverables visible in every standup.
- **B**: Delay any transition conversations until the last month of the project so that team members focus only on delivery without the distraction of their next role.
- **C**: Let the team self-organise their own onward placements since each person is best placed to manage their own career decisions from this point.
- **D**: Use financial retention bonuses tied to final-month attendance as the primary mechanism to keep engagement up through the remaining deliverables and closure activities.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's approach to adjourning teams is transparent, supported transition combined with continued delivery focus. (A) addresses both the career anxiety and the engagement signal. (B) treats anxiety as a problem to be hidden and usually deepens the disengagement. (C) withdraws PM support at exactly the moment where people need it. (D) uses money to patch a motivation issue whose root cause is uncertainty rather than pay.
