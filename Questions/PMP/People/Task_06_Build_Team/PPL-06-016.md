# Question

A long-standing high-performing team has built a strong internal culture: shared shorthand, inside jokes, and informal norms newcomers find hard to read. Two new members joined six weeks ago and report feeling like outsiders. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Put a structured onboarding in place that documents team norms and history, assign buddies, and deliberately invite the new members into key conversations.
- **B**: Ask the existing team members to be more aware of how they come across and to include the newcomers more.
- **C**: Give the new members more time to settle since this kind of culture takes months to absorb.
- **D**: Pause adding any further members until the current two are fully integrated.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats integration as a process problem, not a personality problem; strong cultures need explicit pathways for newcomers. (A) makes the implicit explicit and accelerates integration. (B) is well-intentioned but vague and unlikely to change behaviour on its own. (C) leaves a known issue to drift. (D) defers a separate decision and does not help the people already in the team.
