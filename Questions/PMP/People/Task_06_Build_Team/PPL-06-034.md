# Question

You discover that a newly assigned engineer does not yet hold the security clearance required for sensitive parts of your defence project. Clearance takes about three months. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Initiate the clearance process immediately, assign non-sensitive backlog in the meantime, and update the plan.
- **B**: Grant temporary access to sensitive materials under a chaperone arrangement to keep the project moving forward.
- **C**: Remove the engineer from the project entirely and request a replacement engineer who already holds clearance.
- **D**: Pause all clearance-restricted work across the project until the engineer has completed her clearance process.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI does not compromise on compliance, but expects the PM to use the constraint productively. (A) preserves capacity, respects the rules, and reflects the constraint in the plan. (B) is a clear compliance breach. (C) discards a usable resource without trying. (D) over-corrects and stalls work that could continue with the existing cleared team.
