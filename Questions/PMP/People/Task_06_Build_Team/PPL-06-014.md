# Question

In team workshops, one engineer dominates discussion while two others rarely speak even when their expertise is most relevant. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Take the dominant member aside and ask them to step back to make room for others.
- **B**: Accept the pattern as a personality difference that the team will eventually balance.
- **C**: Use facilitation techniques such as round-robin, written input first, or direct invitations to draw out quieter members.
- **D**: Move the most affected discussions to written-only channels so everyone can contribute in writing.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI frames participation imbalance as a facilitation issue first, not a personality issue. (C) is a structural intervention that lifts everyone's contribution without making it personal. (A) singles the dominant member out and may damage trust. (B) accepts a known dysfunction. (D) is an over-correction that loses the value of synchronous discussion.
