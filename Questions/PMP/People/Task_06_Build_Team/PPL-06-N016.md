# Question

While building the team for a nine-month telecom rollout, the project manager has confirmed the skills needed and identified suitable internal candidates in other departments. Before finalising assignments and committing the baseline schedule, which TWO things does the project manager most need to confirm next? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Each candidate's availability and competing commitments via their resource calendars.
- **B**: That each candidate's functional manager will release them at the level and on the dates the plan assumes.
- **C**: That each candidate's home department has budgeted to backfill the time they spend on the project.
- **D**: Each candidate's preferred working hours and leave already booked within the rollout window.
- **E**: Whether each candidate has the security clearances and system access the rollout work requires.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

With skills already matched to people, the gates for committing assignments are whether the people are genuinely free and whether their owners will actually release them as assumed: resource-calendar availability (A) and functional-manager commitment (B) are what turn a plan into a real one. (D) is close — booked leave does affect availability — but it is one input *into* the resource-calendar picture in (A) rather than a separate gate, and preferred hours are a softer scheduling detail. (E) clearances and access genuinely matter and would gate a *start*, but they are typically resolved during onboarding once people are assigned, not the thing that determines whether the assignment can be made. (C) backfill budgeting is the home department's concern and rarely the project's gate for assignment.
