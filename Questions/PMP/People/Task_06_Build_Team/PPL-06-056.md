# Question

To hit a deadline, procurement has offered either three six-week freelance contracts or two four-month contracts of comparable skill level at similar total cost. The work is interdependent and the learning curve on the codebase is steep. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Take the three six-week freelancers because the higher headcount gives more parallel coverage and sharper reaction to any short-term surges in backlog volume.
- **B**: Take a mix: one four-month contractor for continuity on hard areas and one or two six-week freelancers for surge capacity on well-bounded work that can be ring-fenced.
- **C**: Take the two four-month contractors to cut ramp-up churn, given the interdependent work and steep learning curve are more important here than raw parallel headcount.
- **D**: Decline both options and try to deliver with the existing team by extending working hours until the deadline is met, avoiding the cost of contractors entirely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI tailors resource decisions to the nature of the work; interdependent work with steep learning curves is far better served by longer contracts than by a series of short ones. (C) fits the context. (A) privileges headcount over continuity in exactly the conditions where continuity matters most. (B) introduces unnecessary variety into an interdependent team. (D) relies on unsustainable overtime, which PMI explicitly discourages.
