# Question

A strong technical candidate has disclosed a neurodivergent condition that makes open-plan stand-ups and noisy war rooms very difficult for them. The existing agile team has a loud, fast-paced ceremony style. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Withdraw the offer and look for another candidate whose working style is a more natural fit with how the team already runs its daily stand-ups.
- **B**: Hire the candidate and ask them to adjust their preferences to the team's current ceremony style, since consistency across the team matters for cohesion.
- **C**: Hire the candidate, discuss reasonable adjustments with them confidentially, and work with the team to adapt ceremonies in ways that benefit everyone.
- **D**: Hire the candidate but route them to a separate asynchronous workflow outside the team's ceremonies so neither party needs to change how they operate.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects inclusive team design: bring in the right capability and co-design working practices that suit a wider range of people, often improving the team's overall effectiveness. (C) does both. (A) discards talent for a condition that reasonable adjustments can address and raises equity concerns. (B) places the full burden of adaptation on the new member. (D) isolates the person from the team and forgoes the psychological-safety benefits inclusion would bring.
