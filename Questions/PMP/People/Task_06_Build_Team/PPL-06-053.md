# Question

A sponsor who fears late risk has pushed you to accept twenty people for a scope a six-person team could comfortably deliver. After three weeks, coordination overhead is heavy and several team members report feeling underutilised. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold on to the full team through mid-project in case scope grows, even though current utilisation is low and the team reports coordination overhead.
- **B**: Ask the twenty to self-select into smaller workstreams of their own choosing and let the team structure emerge without any PM design intervention.
- **C**: Quantify the overhead and underutilisation against the work, propose a right-sized team to the sponsor with data, and negotiate a phased release of surplus capacity.
- **D**: Generate additional discretionary scope to keep the full team busy, even where the new work does not clearly contribute to the agreed benefits case.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to rightsize teams based on evidence and to challenge resourcing decisions through data and dialogue with the sponsor. (C) is that path. (A) keeps known waste on the books indefinitely. (B) abandons structural decisions to chance and does not address the sizing issue. (D) inflates scope to consume people, which is both uneconomic and a governance breach.
