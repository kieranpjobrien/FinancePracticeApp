# Question

Mid-programme you are asked to restructure three feature teams into product-aligned pods to improve end-to-end ownership. Team members are worried about losing working relationships they spent months developing with their current teammates. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Announce the new pod structure immediately, assign people on paper, and trust that relationships will re-form quickly once the pods are working in real sprints together.
- **B**: Postpone the restructure indefinitely to preserve existing relationships, even if that leaves the programme without the end-to-end ownership that leadership has asked you to enable.
- **C**: Split relationships randomly across pods to force everyone to build new connections quickly, on the basis that discomfort accelerates the formation of new team bonds.
- **D**: Involve the teams in designing the pod boundaries, map the product areas and the current relationships, and phase the transition with deliberate pod-forming rituals.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats structural change as a co-designed process that protects product outcomes and team relationships simultaneously. (D) does that. (A) imposes the change without design or ritual and often produces shallow pods. (B) blocks a valid strategic move to preserve comfort. (C) manufactures discomfort on the assumption it speeds formation, which is the opposite of how Tuckman's stages work.
