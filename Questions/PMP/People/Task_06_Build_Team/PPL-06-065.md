# Question

Your team spans Sydney, Berlin, and San Francisco. The only overlap where all three time zones can meet is 08:00 San Francisco, 17:00 Berlin, and 02:00 Sydney. Sydney members have silently tolerated monthly 02:00 calls. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the current slot since it is the only one that works for all three time zones and has been running without explicit complaints from the Sydney members so far.
- **B**: Rotate the inconvenient time around the three locations on a monthly cadence, supplement with asynchronous written updates, and hold full-team live sessions less frequently but more deliberately.
- **C**: Move all synchronous work to the Berlin time zone since it sits in the middle and is the least painful compromise across the three geographies on paper.
- **D**: Split the team into two sub-teams organised by time zone and rely entirely on written handovers to coordinate between them, eliminating any live cross-zone meetings at all.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the burden of inconvenient meeting times to be shared fairly and reduced through asynchronous working, not silently absorbed, centred on one zone, or replaced by pure handover. (B) does both. (A) lets an unsustainable pattern continue because no one has spoken up. (C) quietly offshores the inconvenience to the other two zones rather than rotating. (D) removes live collaboration entirely, which often weakens the team in complex work.
