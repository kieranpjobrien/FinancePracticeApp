# Question

A team delivering a regulatory reporting system has just been assembled from three companies. Each brings different norms for availability, decision rights, and how disagreements are raised, and in the first two days members have already talked past each other in meetings. The project manager has scheduled a two-day kickoff and wants the time to pay off. The first integration milestone is six weeks away. Which approach to the kickoff will best set this mixed group up to work as one team?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team co-create a team charter, then immediately use a real upcoming decision to test and refine the decision-making and conflict norms they just agreed.
- **B**: Have the team co-create a team charter covering shared values, communication norms, and decision-making, and circulate it for sign-off after the session.
- **C**: Run a facilitated session surfacing each company's existing norms and differences, so members understand one another before any agreement is drafted.
- **D**: Have the team build a RACI for the first integration milestone, so decision rights and accountabilities are settled where friction is most likely.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For a group already misfiring across cultural lines, a charter only changes behaviour if it is exercised; co-creating it and then putting it to work on a live decision turns stated norms into practised ones within the kickoff itself (A). (B) produces the right artifact but leaves it untested, and charters that are signed and shelved rarely shift behaviour. (C) is valuable groundwork and would naturally feed (A), but on its own it builds mutual understanding without committing the team to any shared way of operating. (D) clarifies accountabilities for one milestone yet skips the broader behavioural and decision norms that are the actual source of the friction.
