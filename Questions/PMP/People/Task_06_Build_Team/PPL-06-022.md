# Question

An agile team's velocity has been steady for three months. Retrospectives are short, the mood is friendly, and almost no one disagrees in planning. Quality has begun to slip on edge cases. What is the most likely cause?

## Options

- **A**: The team has matured into the Performing stage and the velocity reflects steady output.
- **B**: High cohesion without constructive disagreement may have produced groupthink, reducing the team's willingness to challenge each other.
- **C**: The team needs a new technical lead with stronger expertise.
- **D**: The team is too large and should be split into two streams.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI considers psychological safety and constructive conflict essential to sustained team performance; "too pleasant" is a warning sign, not a sign of health. (B) names the dynamic that fits the symptoms. (A) misreads the symptoms as performance. (C) jumps to a personnel change without evidence. (D) jumps to a structural change without evidence.
