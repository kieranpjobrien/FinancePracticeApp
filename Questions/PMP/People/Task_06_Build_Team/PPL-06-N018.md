# Question

A project manager is assembling a panel of clinical subject-matter experts to validate requirements for a medical-device submission. One SME, nominated personally by the sponsor, is engaged and respected but, on appraisal, lacks current knowledge of the regulatory pathway the submission depends on. The validation must be defensible to regulators. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the nominated SME for their clinical strengths and route the regulatory-pathway validations to a separately engaged regulatory specialist, briefing the sponsor on the split.
- **B**: Retain the nominated SME and arrange for them to be brought up to date on the current pathway through focused training before validation begins.
- **C**: Discuss the specific capability gap with the sponsor and propose adding an SME with current regulatory-pathway expertise to the panel alongside the nominee.
- **D**: Accept the nominee as proposed and strengthen the submission with additional independent documentation reviews to compensate for the gap.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Appraising stakeholder skills against what the project needs is part of building the team, and a gap in a validation-critical role warrants an open conversation with the sponsor and a concrete proposal to strengthen the panel's coverage (C). (A) is close and respectful of the sponsor, but it quietly works around the nominee's gap by handing the critical validations elsewhere; absent an explicit agreement about the nominee's role, this risks confusion over who actually owns the regulatory sign-off — transparency about the gap is cleaner than re-routing around it. (B) could work only if there is time and the nominee can credibly reach current competence, which is a strong assumption for a fast-moving regulatory pathway. (D) accepts a known gap on critical validation and hopes downstream reviews catch the consequences, courting rework on a regulated submission.
