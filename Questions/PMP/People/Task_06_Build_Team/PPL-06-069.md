# Question

You are close to hiring a well-known industry expert for a lead role. In the final interview, she asks for an individual office, opt-out from all team ceremonies, and direct access to the sponsor bypassing the PM. The team operates as a collaborative, flat agile unit. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to her terms to secure the rare capability she brings, trust her to produce results, and adjust the team's current working arrangements to accommodate her preferences.
- **B**: Withdraw the offer politely, explain that the requested conditions are incompatible with how the team works, and continue searching for a candidate whose style fits the team.
- **C**: Discuss the team's working agreement and decision norms openly with her, explore which of her requests reflect real needs, and agree whether a mutual fit can be negotiated.
- **D**: Hire her at a lower seniority that limits her authority and visibility so the requested conditions become moot without needing any conversation about them first.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI protects team cohesion and working norms when a single hire's conditions would fundamentally undermine them, even if the capability is attractive. (B) does this cleanly. (A) trades away the team's operating model for one person. (C) is worth trying in less extreme cases, but her requests are not a misunderstanding of norms; they are a rejection of them, which (B) recognises. (D) manipulates the role level rather than addressing the fit issue directly.
