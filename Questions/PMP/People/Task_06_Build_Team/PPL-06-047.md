# Question

Your project has always used a local team, but a global resourcing decision now requires you to add six offshore developers in the next sprint. The existing team is anxious about quality and communication. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Announce the additions to both groups, rely on existing standups and the definition of done, and let integration happen naturally through normal sprint work going forward.
- **B**: Design a structured integration plan covering joint kick-off, overlapping hours, pairing, shared working agreements, and early risk and expectation conversations with both groups together.
- **C**: Split the backlog into two streams, local and offshore, so the existing team can continue undisrupted while the new team ramps up on its own workstream independently.
- **D**: Escalate to the resourcing lead to reverse or delay the decision, since merging two groups mid-project will inevitably slow both the new and current team too much.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects team extensions to be actively integrated through shared norms and early joint work, especially when the existing team is anxious. (B) addresses integration directly. (A) leaves integration to chance and usually produces two parallel teams with a seam between them. (C) formalises the seam and forgoes the benefits of a unified team. (D) challenges a settled resourcing decision before attempting to make it work.
