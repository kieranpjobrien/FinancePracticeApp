# Question

You are selecting between two candidates for a lead engineer role on a team-heavy project. Candidate A has rare, near-unique technical expertise but is known for dismissive behaviour in previous teams. Candidate B has strong but less exceptional skills and a consistent record of collaboration. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Select Candidate A because the technical skills are irreplaceable and coach them into the team.
- **B**: Select Candidate B, since collaboration-heavy work favours team fit over individual brilliance.
- **C**: Interview Candidate A directly, explore the collaboration concerns with specific examples, and check references before deciding.
- **D**: Hire both so Candidate B's influence can lift Candidate A's team behaviour.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to gather evidence before acting on reputation, particularly when the stakes are high and inputs are secondhand. (C) verifies assumptions and supports a defensible choice. (A) locks in a risk without checking whether it is still current. (B) may be right but is premature without investigation. (D) stacks the team based on hope rather than design and is often expensive and unstable.
