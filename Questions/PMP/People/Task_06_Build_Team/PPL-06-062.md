# Question

Your sponsor has hand-picked three of his preferred staff to be on your new team without consulting you. Two of the three are experienced but not aligned to the skills profile you need; the third is a good fit. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept all three assignments without question, since the sponsor controls the budget and contesting staffing choices is likely to damage the sponsor-PM relationship.
- **B**: Discuss the skills profile with the sponsor, confirm the good fit, and propose alternative placements or development plans for the two who are not aligned.
- **C**: Quietly assign the two poorly-fit members to peripheral work so the sponsor sees them on the team but their limited fit does not affect delivery-critical activities.
- **D**: Reject all three assignments outright on the basis that the sponsor bypassed the agreed resourcing process and must restart the conversation from the skills profile.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to advocate for the right composition with evidence and to negotiate constructively with the sponsor, not to acquiesce, hide the issue, or stonewall. (B) does this. (A) accepts a known skills mismatch without challenge. (C) parks team members on tasks that waste their time and obscures the fit issue. (D) rejects the sponsor's intent wholesale before attempting dialogue on specifics.
