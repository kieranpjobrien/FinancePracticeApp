# Question

Your project needs a subject-matter expert in derivatives pricing, but only one internal candidate has the depth required and she is available for three days a fortnight. The sponsor suggests hiring a full-time junior with her mentoring instead. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the junior-plus-mentoring model proposed by the sponsor so the project has a dedicated full-time resource on the team regardless of the junior's current skill level.
- **B**: Structure the fractional arrangement with predictable days onsite, a defined decision scope, a named backup for urgent issues, and a phased handover to an internal successor.
- **C**: Open an external contractor search for a full-time senior derivatives specialist who can replace the internal expert entirely for the duration of the current project.
- **D**: Proceed without derivatives expertise in the team and rely instead on ad-hoc email queries to the internal expert whenever specific questions come up during the build.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's resource strategy uses fractional deep expertise deliberately, with structure that protects availability and develops successors. (B) does both. (A) creates a hard dependency on mentoring time the expert cannot reliably give. (C) discards available internal expertise in favour of paid external cover. (D) leaves the team operating without access to expertise it clearly needs.
