# Question

A replacement quantity surveyor joins a construction project two months into execution. She is highly experienced in the discipline and has already read the project management plan and the main commercial documents before starting, so she grasps the contract structure well. What she lacks is any feel for this project's live commercial position, its unwritten conventions, and the counterparts she must work with daily. The handover with the departing surveyor is only a half-day before he leaves. The project manager has limited time with her this week. Where should the project manager direct that time for the strongest start?

## Options

- **A**: Spend the half-day on a structured handover from the departing surveyor covering the open commercial items, their history, and the live state of each, before he leaves with that knowledge.
- **B**: Use the time to walk her through the project's objectives and key artifacts and confirm how her role fits, since a shared framework should come before any detail.
- **C**: Personally introduce her to the commercial counterparts and team members she will depend on, so the working relationships form early.
- **D**: Set her onto the most pressing live commercial item with the project manager available for questions, so she learns the current position by working it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

She already has the framework and contract knowledge that onboarding usually supplies; the scarce, perishable resource here is the departing surveyor's grasp of the live commercial position and the history behind each open item, which walks out the door in half a day. Capturing that handover first is the highest-value use of the window (A). (B) would be right for a joiner lacking context, but she has done that reading already, so re-covering objectives and artifacts spends time on what she has and neglects what she is about to lose. (C) relationships matter and are a strong next step, but the counterparts remain available next week whereas the outgoing surveyor does not. (D) learning by doing is reasonable once she knows the current state, but turning her loose on a live commercial item before the handover risks acting on a position she has only read about, not the one that actually obtains.
