# Question

You discover that two prospective team members had a serious conflict on a previous project. Both have skills you need. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet each individual privately to understand the history, then facilitate a structured conversation about working together with explicit ground rules.
- **B**: Decide in favour of one and recruit a substitute for the other to avoid risk.
- **C**: Bring them on with no preamble; old conflict rarely repeats once people are in a new context.
- **D**: Place them in different streams with no shared interface so they never need to interact.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's people-first approach is to address conflict directly with evidence and clear expectations. (A) tests whether they can work together while giving both a fair voice. (B) sacrifices needed expertise without trying. (C) ignores a known risk and leaves the team to absorb the fallout. (D) is rarely possible on a real project and avoids rather than resolves the issue.
