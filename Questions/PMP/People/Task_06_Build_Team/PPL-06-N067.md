# Question

A renewable-energy SaaS company is standing up a product team split across three time zones, working in two-week sprints. The members have never worked together, two of the five are new hires, and the only guaranteed overlap is two hours each afternoon. Leadership wants the team productive within a month. The project manager can act on only a few priorities first. Which TWO actions should the project manager prioritise to build this team into an effective unit? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Spend the two-hour overlap on collaboration, joint planning, and relationship-building, and move status-style updates to asynchronous, written channels.
- **B**: Have each member publish working hours, and shift one or two ceremonies to a fairer rotating slot so the same zone does not always take the inconvenient time.
- **C**: Co-create a team charter covering communication norms, response-time expectations, and how decisions get made across the zones.
- **D**: Invest the first sprint in a shared technical onboarding and a working agreement before pushing for committed delivery velocity.
- **E**: Pair each of the two new hires with an experienced member for their first sprints to accelerate context transfer and connection.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

For a brand-new distributed team, the two highest-leverage moves are protecting the scarce synchronous window for the work only real-time contact can do — collaboration and relationship-building — while pushing routine updates async (A), and establishing explicit norms through a co-created charter so the team operates coherently when apart and the new hires inherit clear expectations (C). The others are all reasonable and would plausibly appear later in the plan, which is what makes the choice hard: (B) is genuinely good distributed-team hygiene, but rotating a ceremony slot is a fairness refinement that matters less, first, than securing the one window the team has and agreeing how it will work; (D) sensibly front-loads onboarding, yet spending a whole first sprint before any delivery overshoots a one-month productivity target and the working agreement it names is the charter already captured in C; (E) accelerates the two new hires specifically, but it strengthens individuals rather than forming the five-person unit, so it ranks below the team-wide foundations of A and C.
