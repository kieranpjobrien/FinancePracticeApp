# Question

A company-wide layoff round is announced publicly, affecting 8% of staff. Your project team is not directly in scope but members are anxious, sharing rumours, and two have quietly started interviewing elsewhere. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Communicate openly with the team: what you know, what you do not, and acknowledge the uncertainty while confirming how this team fits into the organisation's post-layoff plan.
- **B**: Stay silent on the layoffs in team forums and rely on HR's official channels to manage communications, since repeating information could risk spreading rumours.
- **C**: Guarantee the team that none of them will be affected now or in any future round, even though you do not have authority to make that kind of commitment.
- **D**: Require all team members to recommit to the project through signed retention agreements covering the next twelve months in exchange for a modest project bonus.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI values transparent communication and psychological safety in stressful periods, combined with honesty about what the PM does and does not know. (A) fits. (B) treats a live anxiety as a communications policy issue and amplifies rumour. (C) makes promises the PM cannot keep and destroys trust when reality breaks the commitment. (D) coerces commitment rather than earning it and does nothing about the underlying uncertainty.
