# Question

A hybrid data-migration project is adding four contractors mid-flight to hit a fixed regulatory deadline. They are experienced migration engineers but new to the client's domain, tooling, and the team's conventions. The core team is already at capacity and worried that ramping the contractors will slow current delivery, and there is roughly six weeks of work before the deadline. The project manager wants the contractors contributing as fast as possible without derailing the work in flight. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Pair each contractor with a core-team buddy for a structured ramp on domain, tooling, and conventions, and plan for a short, expected dip in core-team throughput.
- **B**: Run a concentrated two-day onboarding workshop covering domain, tooling, and conventions for all four at once, then let them work independently to protect the core team's time.
- **C**: Start the contractors on real but lower-risk work items immediately, with a core-team reviewer on their pull requests, so they learn the conventions by doing.
- **D**: Have the contractors shadow core-team members for the first sprint before taking on any of their own work items.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

With a fixed deadline only weeks out, the fastest sustainable ramp transfers tacit domain and convention knowledge person-to-person and accepts a planned, temporary dip — pairing gives continuous context exactly when each contractor needs it and is the most reliable way to make new joiners productive quickly (A). (B) front-loads the explicit knowledge efficiently but a one-off workshop cannot convey the tacit conventions, and cutting the contractors loose afterward tends to produce rework that costs more time than it saved. (C) gets output sooner but pull-request review is asynchronous and lossy for the unwritten norms, so contractors absorb conventions slowly and reviewers carry a hidden load. (D) protects quality but a full sprint of pure shadowing burns a large share of a six-week runway before the contractors deliver anything, the opposite of why they were hired.
