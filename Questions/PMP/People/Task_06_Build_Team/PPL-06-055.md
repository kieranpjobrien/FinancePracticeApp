# Question

Your organisation has a commitment to a more diverse engineering workforce. The shortlist your recruiter has produced for a senior role is from the usual networks and reflects none of that commitment. The hiring deadline is in two weeks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Hire the strongest candidate on the existing shortlist to meet the deadline, since relaxing the deadline would slow the project and delay the commitment.
- **B**: Pause the shortlist, work with the recruiter to broaden sourcing channels and rewrite the brief, and extend the timeline by the minimum needed to produce a more representative pool.
- **C**: Ask the recruiter to simply add candidates who meet the diversity criteria to the existing shortlist so the pool at interview stage is numerically broader.
- **D**: Withdraw the role entirely for the current project and reopen the hire as part of a separate organisation-wide diversity programme led by HR several months from now.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI aligns team building with organisational values: broaden the pipeline at source and accept a proportionate timeline adjustment, rather than shortcutting, tokenising, or withdrawing. (B) addresses sourcing and brief together. (A) locks in a non-representative pool against a stated commitment. (C) papers over the sourcing problem with late additions and risks tokenism. (D) passes the buck and loses the hire altogether.
