# Question

A new Scrum team is in its third sprint. Members frequently ask the Scrum Master to decide how to split stories, who should pair with whom, and how to handle blockers, rather than working it out as a team. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide direct answers to keep the sprint moving and revisit self-organisation later.
- **B**: Decline to engage with the questions until the team works things out themselves.
- **C**: Coach the team with guiding questions, facilitate the conversations, and step back as their capability grows.
- **D**: Recommend to leadership that the team is not yet ready to operate in Scrum.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A Scrum Master develops the team's capacity to self-organise, which is a coaching activity, not direction or abandonment. (C) builds capability without leaving the team stranded. (A) creates dependency that gets harder to unwind. (B) withdraws support during the most fragile period of team formation. (D) judges prematurely and labels a normal early-stage challenge as a fundamental failure.
