# Question

Your hybrid team has agreed to be in the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays. In practice, two senior members regularly work remotely on those days and join meetings by video, while everyone else attends in person. Juniors feel the rule applies only to them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop the onsite-day agreement entirely and move the team back to a fully remote working model so that no one feels unequally treated by the existing hybrid norm.
- **B**: Privately remind the two senior members of the agreement and trust them to self-correct, without discussing the issue openly with the wider team at all.
- **C**: Raise the issue as a team, revisit the working agreement, understand the senior members' constraints, and renegotiate norms that are fair and that everyone will actually follow.
- **D**: Make the onsite-day rule strictly mandatory with a formal attendance log and escalate any further breaches to the senior members' line managers without further team discussion.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats a broken working agreement as a team conversation that re-establishes norms with full information, not as a policy to abandon, enforce privately, or escalate. (C) does that. (A) over-corrects by discarding a collectively agreed norm. (B) tries to fix a visible fairness issue in private and preserves the perception. (D) swings to enforcement without understanding constraints and damages trust.
