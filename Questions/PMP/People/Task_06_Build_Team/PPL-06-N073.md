# Question

A newly formed Scrum team building a logistics-tracking product sits split across two floors of the same building because that is where desks were free. After two sprints the Scrum Master sees questions that once took a quick word now waiting for scheduled calls, the stand-up is the only time the team is whole, and minor design decisions are stalling. Capacity, skills, and tooling are adequate. A vacant team room on one floor could seat everyone, though three of the eight members work from home two days a week and that is staying. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Move the team into the shared team room so members are colocated whenever they are on site and can collaborate spontaneously through the day.
- **B**: Keep the current desks but introduce core collaboration hours when the whole team is reachable and decisions are made together.
- **C**: Set up a team chat channel and a shared virtual board so questions and design decisions are resolved without anyone moving desks.
- **D**: Bring the design decisions into a second short daily session so the items that are stalling have a dependable point to be settled.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The cause is physical separation draining the high-bandwidth, spontaneous communication a co-located team relies on, and the direct fix is to put everyone in one space; the partial remote pattern does not change that, because colocating on the days people are on site captures most of the benefit and is plainly better than leaving them split across floors every day (A). Core hours improve when the team can reach each other but leave them on separate floors during those hours, so the loss of quick face-to-face exchange — the actual problem — persists (B). A chat channel and virtual board are the right backbone for the remote days and worth having, but adopting them instead of closing the on-site gap accepts low-bandwidth substitutes when proximity is freely available, treating the team as more distributed than it is (C). A second daily session formalises yet more of the communication that proximity would make effortless and still leaves people apart between meetings (D). Colocating when on site is the highest-bandwidth move the situation allows, and the remote days are handled separately with tooling.
