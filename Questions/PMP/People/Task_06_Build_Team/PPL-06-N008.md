# Question

A project manager is determining the resource requirements for a new offshore wind-farm construction project so that staffing requests can be raised with functional managers. Which of the following genuinely help establish what skills and quantities the project needs, as opposed to who can be supplied or who is preferred? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Bottom-up estimating of the resources each decomposed activity in the schedule requires.
- **B**: Expert judgement from people who have delivered comparable offshore scope.
- **C**: Analogous and parametric estimating drawn from similar past projects.
- **D**: The engineering resource pool's current bench-utilisation report.
- **E**: A resource histogram showing how many people the project can realistically have on site each month given accommodation limits.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Resource *requirements* are derived from the work: bottom-up estimating of decomposed activities (A), expert judgement on comparable scope (B), and analogous or parametric estimating (C) all establish the skills and quantities the work demands. (D) describes who is currently available; availability informs acquisition and resource levelling but does not define the requirement, and letting it do so staffs to convenience. (E) is a genuine planning artefact, but a histogram bounded by accommodation capacity expresses a *constraint on supply* over time, not the underlying need; it shapes how the requirement is met and smoothed, not what the requirement is. The trap in both D and E is mistaking constraints on staffing for the determination of what staffing the work requires.
