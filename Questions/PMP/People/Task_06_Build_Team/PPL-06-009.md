# Question

For a critical project, the PM can either form a brand-new team of strong specialists or take an existing team that has worked together well but has only moderate skills in the relevant domain. Which factor should be PRIMARY in the decision?

## Options

- **A**: The complexity and timeline, weighed against the cost of new-team formation versus upskilling.
- **B**: The seniority profile of the existing team, since their general experience generalises across domains.
- **C**: The preferences of the functional managers responsible for releasing the required project resources.
- **D**: The fastest route to a fully signed and approved project resource management plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI tailors team selection to project context: complexity and timeline drive whether team formation cost or skill-development cost is the bigger risk. (A) names the right primary factor. (B) overstates how well experience transfers across domains. (C) outsources a PM decision to functional management. (D) optimises for paperwork rather than outcomes.
