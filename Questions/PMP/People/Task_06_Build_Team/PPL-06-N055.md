# Question

A newly formed Scrum team is co-creating its working agreement during a chartering workshop. Which THREE of the following genuinely belong in the team's own working agreement rather than in another artifact? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The core collaboration hours when everyone agrees to be available for synchronous work.
- **B**: How the team will handle disagreements and reach decisions when members differ.
- **C**: The team's agreed expectations for how members give and receive feedback in retrospectives.
- **D**: The team's definition of done that sets the quality bar for every story.
- **E**: The sprint length and the dates of sprint reviews for the release.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A working agreement captures how the team chooses to operate together: shared availability the members set among themselves (A), how they will resolve disagreement and decide (B), and the interpersonal norms for giving and receiving feedback (C). (D) is a real and important team agreement, but the definition of done is a distinct quality artifact about when work is complete, not a behavioural norm about how the team works together. (E) is a cadence and scheduling decision that lives in the team's calendar and release plan; fixing dates is planning, not a self-set working norm.
