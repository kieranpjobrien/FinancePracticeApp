# Question

A project manager is deciding the composition of a team for a hybrid product-development effort before approaching resource owners. Several inputs are available. Which THREE should genuinely shape what skills and how many people the team needs? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Analysis of the planned work and backlog to deduce the skills and skill levels the deliverables require.
- **B**: An assessment of candidate competencies against those requirements to surface gaps and overlaps.
- **C**: Identification of capabilities held by only one candidate, so redundancy can be designed in against single-point dependencies.
- **D**: The team size used on the organisation's last successful product, applied as a sensible default for this one.
- **E**: The named resources the sponsor has already promised to other initiatives, so this team avoids competing for them.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Composition is derived from the work: infer the required skills and levels from the deliverables and backlog (A), measure candidates against those requirements to see gaps and overlaps (B), and find single-skill dependencies so the team is resilient rather than fragile (C). (D) imports a headcount from a different problem; team size should follow this project's scope and skill mix, not a precedent that may not transfer. (E) is useful intelligence for the later negotiation over availability, but who is promised elsewhere does not determine what capabilities this team actually requires.
