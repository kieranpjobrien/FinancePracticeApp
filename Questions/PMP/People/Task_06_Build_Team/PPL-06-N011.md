# Question

A hybrid project needs specialised geophysical-survey expertise for a single ten-week phase, after which the organisation foresees no further use for the skill. There are no internal geophysicists. A niche survey consultancy is available now and a competent permanent hire is possible but slow. The project manager has decided to engage the consultancy. How should the project manager structure that engagement to best serve the team and the project?

## Options

- **A**: Contract the consultancy to deliver the survey work, and assign an internal engineer alongside them throughout to learn the discipline for possible future reuse.
- **B**: Contract the consultancy to deliver the survey work and a clear, self-contained findings report, accepting that the specialist methods leave with them.
- **C**: Contract the consultancy on a time-and-materials basis so internal staff can direct the day-to-day survey activities and retain ownership of decisions.
- **D**: Contract the consultancy to deliver the survey work, with a defined obligation to hand over methods, data, and key findings to named internal staff during the phase.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

For a short, genuinely non-recurring specialist need, buying the capability is right; the question is how to do so without the team losing the understanding it needs to own the result. Requiring transfer of methods, data, and findings to specific internal staff captures exactly what the project will rely on afterward, at proportionate cost (D). (A) over-invests: embedding an engineer to learn a discipline the organisation does not expect to reuse buys retention the project will not benefit from. (B) is defensible and close, but a findings report alone can leave the team unable to interpret or defend the work later; transferring the underlying methods and data is a modest addition that materially de-risks reliance on it. (C) shifts a delivery model without addressing knowledge at all, and having non-experts direct specialist survey work can undercut the very expertise being bought.
