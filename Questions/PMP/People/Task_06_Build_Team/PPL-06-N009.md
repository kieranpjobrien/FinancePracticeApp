# Question

A successful Scrum team of five building a streaming-media platform faces so much demand that the product owner wants to double its capacity quickly. The backlog is plentiful and splits naturally into two coherent product areas, playback and recommendations. Five experienced engineers are available to bring in. The Scrum Master is wary of disrupting a team that is working well. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Grow the existing team to ten under one product owner and one backlog, keeping the proven team intact and simply adding capacity.
- **B**: Keep the original five on the core product and stand up the five new engineers as a second team owning playback as a component the core team consumes.
- **C**: Split into two right-sized cross-functional feature teams aligned to playback and recommendations, each seeded with experienced members from the original five.
- **D**: Form two teams by playback and recommendations but keep all five original members together on recommendations, staffing playback entirely with the new engineers.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Throughput scales by adding right-sized teams aligned to coherent value streams, and stability is preserved by distributing experienced members so each new team carries forward the original's norms and knowledge (C). (A) keeps the team intact but pushes it past the size where self-organisation and communication stay efficient, so coordination overhead erodes the speed sought. (B) creates a component team and the hand-offs between playback and the rest that feature teams exist to avoid. (D) splits along the right lines but concentrates all the experience on one team and leaves the other entirely new, so playback launches without any embedded history and the two teams ramp very unevenly.
