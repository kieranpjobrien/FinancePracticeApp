# Question

A top-performing senior developer on your e-commerce platform has told you she is bored, considering leaving, and feels there is nothing left to learn on this project. She delivers reliably and mentors others well. You have no budget to promote her and she knows that. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Work with her to identify a stretch assignment on the programme — a complex new domain, a tech-choice she has not led before, or a visible advisory role — that she genuinely chooses.
- **B**: Ask the functional manager to move her to a different project so that she has the novelty she is asking for before she resigns from the organisation entirely over boredom.
- **C**: Give her a small pay rise or spot bonus if budget allows somewhere so that the compensation signal reflects the market value of the experience she is accumulating on the programme.
- **D**: Let her move on naturally given that top performers frequently leave when they plateau and backfilling with a fresh senior hire may bring useful new energy to the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) addresses the actual motivator she named — learning — with a stretch assignment she chooses, which is the PMI intrinsic-motivation pattern for plateaued high performers. (B) solves boredom by handing her to someone else and loses a critical contributor to the programme. (C) reaches for a monetary lever she did not ask for and will not fix the underlying plateau. (D) accepts attrition as a pricing strategy and ignores the clear signal that a retention move now would probably work.
