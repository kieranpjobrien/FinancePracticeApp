# Question

A new programme director asks you to start ranking your delivery team members from best to worst each month using lines of code, tickets closed, and hours logged, so underperformers can be identified early. You are concerned this will harm a team that currently collaborates well. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Propose measuring team-level outcomes such as delivery predictability, defect escape rate, and cycle time, and explain how individual ranking on output volume can drive gaming and damage collaboration.
- **B**: Implement the ranking as requested but keep it confidential to yourself, using it only as private insight rather than sharing it with the team.
- **C**: Comply with the director's instruction since performance measurement is a legitimate management prerogative and resisting it will undermine your standing with leadership.
- **D**: Decline to measure individual performance at all and reassure the director that the team's strong collaboration is sufficient evidence that everyone is performing.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Volume metrics like lines of code are easily gamed and pit collaborators against each other; (A) redirects the director's legitimate goal (early visibility of problems) toward outcome measures that improve the team rather than corrode it. (B) still relies on flawed data to judge people and is no safer for being hidden. (C) treats a harmful measurement scheme as something to obey rather than to inform and improve. (D) refusing any measurement ignores the director's reasonable need for visibility and leaves genuine problems undetected.
