# Question

A mid-level engineer on your six-month insurance claims rebuild has been reopening a disproportionate number of defects and has missed two key deliverables this month. He believes his performance is fine. His functional manager has asked you for input ahead of his annual review. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the engineer before the review with specific defect and delivery evidence, describe the pattern you have observed, hear his perspective, and agree concrete improvement actions together.
- **B**: Send the defect and delivery data directly to his functional manager and let the formal review process handle the feedback in the way the organisation has designed that conversation.
- **C**: Assign him only low-risk work going forward so the team is protected from further impact and his performance pattern becomes less visible to the rest of the engineers.
- **D**: Raise the concerns with him at the next team retrospective so the performance issue is discussed in an open forum where his peers can also contribute their own observations.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI performance-support pattern: direct, private, evidence-based feedback with a chance for the engineer to respond and agree actions before any formal review — feedback should never be a surprise. (B) outsources a conversation the PM should have first and leaves the engineer blindsided. (C) manages around the issue without addressing it and denies him the feedback he needs to improve. (D) publicly surfaces individual performance in a team forum, breaching "correct in private" and damaging psychological safety.
