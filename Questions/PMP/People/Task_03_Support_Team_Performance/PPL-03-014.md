# Question

A development team's velocity has dropped for three consecutive sprints. In one-on-ones, several members say they are exhausted from sustained overtime to meet a fixed release date. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Protect sustainable pace with the team and then renegotiate release scope with the product owner.
- **B**: Coach the team to break stories down further so that velocity recovers in the next sprint cycle.
- **C**: Escalate the burnout concern to the sponsor and request approval to add two contractors to the team.
- **D**: Run a focused retrospective on estimation accuracy to address the velocity decline directly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sustainable pace is an agile principle, and the immediate problem is overload, not estimation or staffing. (A) protects the team and then forces the trade-off conversation with the product owner, which is the root cause of the overcommitment. (B) treats the symptom (velocity number) and ignores the burnout. (C) escalates and adds people before the PM has done what is in their control, and new contractors will reduce velocity further in the short term. (D) misdiagnoses the issue as estimation when the team has clearly told the Scrum Master the issue is workload.
