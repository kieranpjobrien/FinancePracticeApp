# Question

A team has held its first three retrospectives. Each generated a long list of improvement ideas, the team votes on the top few, and the Scrum Master records them in the retrospective notes. Yet sprint after sprint the agreed ideas are not implemented, and the team is beginning to treat the retrospective as a pointless ritual. The Scrum Master already facilitates well and the team engages openly. What should the Scrum Master change to restore the value of the event?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the small set of agreed improvement actions into the team's visible work for the next sprint, each with an owner, and review their status at the start of the next retrospective.
- **B**: Tighten facilitation so that each retrospective converges on a single highest-impact improvement rather than a top few, reducing the load the team is failing to act on.
- **C**: Begin each retrospective by re-deriving the most important problems from current data, so the team always works its biggest issue rather than carrying a stale list forward.
- **D**: Ask the team to agree a working agreement that members will act on retrospective outcomes, reinforcing collective accountability for follow-through.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The gap is not idea generation, facilitation quality, or willingness — those are fine — it is that agreed actions live only in notes and never enter the actual workflow, so they compete with delivery and lose. (A) closes that loop directly: putting the actions into the sprint's visible work with owners and reviewing them makes follow-through structural rather than dependent on goodwill. (B) reducing to one action helps capacity but still leaves even that one action unowned and outside the workflow, so it can fail the same way. (C) re-deriving problems each time abandons commitments mid-flight and can mean nothing is ever finished. (D) a working agreement appeals to accountability but, without making the work visible and tracked, relies on the same intention that has already failed three times. The margin is between operationalising the actions (A) and merely re-deciding or re-committing to them (B, C, D).
