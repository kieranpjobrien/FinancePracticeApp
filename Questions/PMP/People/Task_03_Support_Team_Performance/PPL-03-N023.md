# Question

A development team marks user stories "done" at sprint review, but the product owner keeps finding missing edge-case handling the following sprint, generating defect tickets that consume new capacity. The team's Definition of Done already lists "unit tested and code reviewed", and the team genuinely believes it meets it each time. The product owner is losing trust in the "done" label. As Scrum Master, what is the most effective way to support the team's performance?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team and product owner walking several of the escaped defects back to pinpoint what the current Definition of Done fails to catch, then revising it so those conditions must be verified before "done".
- **B**: Facilitate the team adding edge-case and negative-path testing to the Definition of Done, so the category of defect the product owner keeps finding is covered going forward.
- **C**: Add a story-level acceptance-criteria review to refinement so each story's specific edge cases are agreed and testable before the team starts building it.
- **D**: Have the product owner and team agree explicit acceptance criteria per story and confirm them together at sprint review before the story is accepted.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The DoD already exists and the team believes it meets it, so the problem is that the DoD does not capture what is actually escaping — the fix has to be driven by the real defects, not by guessing a category to bolt on. (A) walks the escaped defects back to find precisely what "done" is missing and revises the DoD against evidence, building quality in at the team-wide level where the leak is. (B) is close and tempting, but "add edge-case testing" presumes the missing condition is edge cases generically; without inspecting the actual escapes it may add ceremony while still missing the real gap. (C) strengthening per-story acceptance criteria in refinement is a legitimate and valuable practice, but it improves how individual stories are specified rather than fixing the shared definition of completeness that is letting work pass — and edge cases discovered in build still need a DoD that forces verifying them. (D) moving acceptance to sprint review shifts detection later and leans on the PO to catch misses at the gate rather than the team owning completeness. The margin is between revising the shared DoD from evidence of what actually escapes (A) and adding a plausible but unvalidated rule or a story-level/inspection step (B, C, D).
