# Question

Your Scrum team's burndown has flatlined halfway through the last four sprints. Engineers consistently report they are "waiting on security review" or "waiting on environment access." The same blockers appear every sprint. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add a 30% buffer to every story estimate so that the team's forecasts absorb the expected blocker time and commitments become more reliable sprint after sprint.
- **B**: Coach engineers to start a backup task immediately whenever a blocker arises so that their individual utilisation stays high while the blocker is being resolved in parallel.
- **C**: Remove security review and environment access from the sprint scope by moving those activities to a dedicated phase before each sprint actually starts.
- **D**: Meet the security and environment leads directly, quantify the delay impact, and negotiate service-level commitments and clear pathways for common requests going forward.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is PMI impediment-removal: address the systemic upstream blocker by engaging the teams who own those services and negotiating SLAs — fix the flow, not the forecast. (A) disguises the problem in padding and gives the upstream teams no incentive to improve. (B) keeps individuals busy but does nothing about throughput; utilisation theatre is not performance. (C) rebrands the blocker as a "phase" but the same dependency still exists and the same delays will recur.
