# Question

Engineers on a 12-person team report that meetings consume more than half their day and they cannot find blocks of focus time. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce two no-meeting days per week and audit the standing meeting calendar for purpose, attendees, and cadence.
- **B**: Cancel the daily stand-up and weekly retrospective so engineers reclaim those blocks.
- **C**: Push back on the engineers and explain that meetings are part of working in a delivery team.
- **D**: Ask each engineer to track their time for two weeks before deciding on any change.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM should both protect focus time and prune the meeting load, and (A) does both. (B) cuts the agile ceremonies that exist precisely to coordinate the team, which is the wrong meetings to remove. (C) dismisses a legitimate concern that the team has raised. (D) delays action by two weeks to gather data the team has effectively already provided through the retrospective signal.
