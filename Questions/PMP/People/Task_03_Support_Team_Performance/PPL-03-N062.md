# Question

On a hospital construction project, a normally reliable site coordinator has missed two consecutive weekly progress submissions and the quality of their reporting has slipped. The project manager has already had one informal conversation in which the coordinator agreed to catch up but did not. The sponsor, who relies on these reports, has begun asking the project manager directly for status. What is the project manager's best next step?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a direct, private conversation with the coordinator to understand what is preventing the agreed catch-up and jointly agree specific, dated commitments with a follow-up point.
- **B**: Escalate the coordinator's missed submissions to their functional manager so the performance issue is handled through the line.
- **C**: Take over compiling the progress reports yourself until the coordinator's situation stabilises, to keep the sponsor satisfied.
- **D**: Document the missed submissions formally as a performance concern in case the pattern continues.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A first informal conversation has already failed, but the project manager has not yet explored the underlying cause, so the next step is a more pointed but still supportive conversation that surfaces the obstacle and sets concrete, dated commitments with accountability (A). Escalating to the functional manager (B) skips a step the project manager can and should take directly and can damage trust before the cause is even understood. Taking over the reports (C) relieves the symptom for the sponsor but lets the performance issue drift and removes the coordinator's ownership. Formal documentation (D) may follow if behaviour does not change, but jumping to a paper trail before a genuine second attempt to support and unblock the person is premature and punitive rather than developmental.
