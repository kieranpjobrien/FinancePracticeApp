# Question

A high-performing team that has worked together for over a year has become complacent — they meet but never exceed commitments, resist trying new practices, and retrospectives produce few improvements. How should the PM re-energise the team?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce new stretch challenges and celebrate innovation attempts regardless of outcome.
- **B**: Increase performance targets significantly to push the team harder toward higher output.
- **C**: Accept that the team has naturally reached its performance peak.
- **D**: Rotate members to other projects in order to inject fresh perspectives.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) addresses the root cause — the team has run out of novelty and growth. New challenges plus safety to fail revives a complacent team. (B) adds pressure without growth. (C) accepts premature stagnation. (D) destroys the team that took a year to build and treats a symptom.
