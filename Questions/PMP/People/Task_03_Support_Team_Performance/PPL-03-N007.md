# Question

Your Scrum team has hit its commitment every sprint for two months, and stakeholders are pleased. During a code walkthrough you notice the team is routinely marking stories "done" while skipping the automated regression tests required by the Definition of Done. Velocity looks healthy and no defects have surfaced yet. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the gap between the team's practice and the Definition of Done at the retrospective and help the team work out why it is happening and how to restore the standard.
- **B**: Reinstate the regression tests as a hard gate immediately, directing that no story be marked done until they pass, so the standard is enforced from the next story onward.
- **C**: Report the skipped tests to the product owner and let them decide whether the accumulating quality risk is acceptable given the strong delivery record.
- **D**: Add a separate hardening sprint after the current release to retrofit the missing regression tests once the committed scope is delivered.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A "done" that ignores the Definition of Done is hidden technical debt the healthy velocity is masking, but the PM does not yet know why a capable team is cutting this corner — schedule pressure, a flaky test suite, unclear ownership, or something else — and the durable fix depends on the cause. (A) surfaces it transparently in the team's own forum and has the team diagnose and own the remedy, which is what makes the standard stick. (B) enforces the right outcome but imposes it top-down without understanding the cause; if the tests are being skipped because the suite is slow or brittle, a hard gate just moves the pain and breeds new workarounds. (C) reframes a team-discipline and engineering-health issue as a product trade-off and hands the decision to someone who does not own the team's practices. (D) defers the debt and tacitly accepts skipping tests under pressure as normal, the very pattern that needs correcting. The discriminator is engaging the team to understand and self-correct versus enforcing, escalating, or deferring around them.
