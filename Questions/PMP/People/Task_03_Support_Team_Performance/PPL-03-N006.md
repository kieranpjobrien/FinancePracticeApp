# Question

You manage a predictive ERP rollout using a matrix structure. A functional manager asks you for input on a team member's annual performance review. The team member has delivered solid project work for eight months, but you have never given him direct feedback on the two areas where he could clearly improve, and his appraisal meeting with the functional manager is in three days. How should you handle the functional manager's request?

## Options

- **A**: Give the functional manager specific, evidence-based input covering both his strengths and the two development areas, drawn from what you have directly observed.
- **B**: Speak with the team member yourself first to share your observations directly, then provide the functional manager with the same balanced input.
- **C**: Provide the functional manager only the strengths you can firmly evidence, and raise the two development areas with the team member yourself after the appraisal so the feedback comes from the right person.
- **D**: Ask the functional manager to hold the development points until you have coached the team member on them, then confirm whether they still warrant inclusion in the formal review.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In a matrix the PM holds the day-to-day visibility the functional manager lacks, so contributing balanced, evidenced input is part of supporting the person — and good feedback practice means an employee should never first hear a development point about themselves in a formal appraisal. (B) honours both: the team member hears the constructive feedback directly from the person who observed it before it lands in his review, and the functional manager still receives complete, fair input in time. (A) supplies fair input but lets him be blindsided in the appraisal by criticism his PM never raised, which damages trust. (C) splits the picture so the functional manager appraises him on incomplete information and defers the development feedback past the cycle where it matters. (D) effectively asks the functional manager to filter the formal record around the PM's coaching timeline, overstepping the PM's role and muddying who owns the appraisal.
