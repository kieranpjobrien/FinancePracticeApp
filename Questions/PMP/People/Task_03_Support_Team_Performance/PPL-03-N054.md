# Question

A previously high-performing Scrum team has slipped over the last three sprints: velocity is down, two stories carried over each sprint, and the retrospectives have gone quiet with little discussion. The team is otherwise unchanged. Which THREE actions best help you understand and address the decline as the Scrum Master?

## Options

- **A**: Hold individual one-to-ones to understand privately whether something has changed for people that they are not raising in the group setting.
- **B**: Examine the flow of work for new external dependencies, larger or less-refined stories, or environmental issues that could explain the slowdown.
- **C**: Change the retrospective format to a different technique to re-energise the conversation and draw out what the team is not saying.
- **D**: Set a recovery velocity target equal to the team's previous average and ask the team to commit to hitting it next sprint.
- **E**: Replace the carried-over stories with fresh ones each sprint so the backlog stays clean and the team starts each sprint with a clear slate.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A quiet retrospective and falling throughput call for diagnosis across people, process, and environment: (A) surfaces personal or interpersonal issues people will not voice in a group, (B) checks whether dependencies, story size, or tooling changed, and (C) revives the team's own reflection so they can name the problem. (D) imposing the old velocity as a target pressures a struggling team without addressing why it slipped and invites padding. (E) discarding carryover hides the symptom and loses the partly-done work, doing nothing to explain or fix the decline.
