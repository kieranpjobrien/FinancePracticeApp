# Question

You are the project manager of an 18-month grid-connection project nearing a major milestone. A functional team member who reports to a line manager elsewhere has performed exceptionally on your project but is invisible to that manager. You want your performance feedback to actually help her career and reinforce the behaviour on your team. Which actions are appropriate? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Provide specific, behaviour-based feedback to her directly, citing concrete contributions rather than general praise.
- **B**: Send a written acknowledgement of her contribution to her line manager so it informs her formal appraisal.
- **C**: Recognise her contribution visibly within the project team in a way she is comfortable with.
- **D**: Recommend to her line manager that she be promoted, since her project performance demonstrates she is ready.
- **E**: Wait until the project closeout report to document her performance so all contributions are captured together.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

(A) direct specific feedback, (B) routing recognition to the person who owns her appraisal, and (C) appropriate team recognition together support both performance and career — the PM's legitimate levers for a functional resource. (D) oversteps: the PM lacks the full performance picture across her role and promotion is the line manager's decision, so recommending it specifically is presumptuous. (E) delays feedback until it can no longer reinforce behaviour or reach her appraisal cycle, undermining its value.
