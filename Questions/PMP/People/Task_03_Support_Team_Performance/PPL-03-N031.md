# Question

A new analyst joined your predictive data-warehouse project three weeks ago. She is technically capable but her output is well below the team's pace, and she keeps interrupting whichever senior colleague is nearest with questions, breaking their concentration on their own deliverables. The team has no onboarding process; people were expected to "pick it up as they go". What is the best way to support overall team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Pair her with one experienced colleague as a buddy with a protected daily question slot and a short ramp-up plan, so her questions have a known channel and the rest of the seniors keep their focus.
- **B**: Build a self-serve onboarding pack — environment setup, data dictionary, runbooks, and an FAQ from her recurring questions — so she can resolve most queries herself and interrupt others far less.
- **C**: Have her log her questions and route them to whichever senior owns the relevant area, so each query reaches the right expert without random interruption.
- **D**: Schedule a short daily team check-in where she can raise her accumulated questions to the group, so answering her is bounded to one predictable slot for everyone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

She needs to ramp quickly *and* the seniors need their focus back, and with no onboarding process at all the gap is a reliable support relationship, not just a document or a routing rule. (A) gives her one accountable guide plus a ramp-up plan — fastest path to competence — while protecting the other seniors by concentrating the interruption into one person's planned time. (B) a self-serve pack is genuinely valuable and worth building, but a brand-new joiner cannot yet tell which questions the pack answers or judge the answers' context; alone it leaves her stuck and likely still interrupting on everything the pack does not cover. (C) routing questions to the area owner spreads the load back across all the seniors she is currently disrupting and gives her no coherent guidance or ramp plan — it organises the interruptions rather than reducing the dependency. (D) a daily group slot bounds the timing but pulls the whole team into answering her and defers urgent blockers up to a day, slowing her further. The margin is between one dedicated buddy who accelerates her while shielding the team (A) and options that document, route, or batch the questions without building the support relationship (B, C, D).
