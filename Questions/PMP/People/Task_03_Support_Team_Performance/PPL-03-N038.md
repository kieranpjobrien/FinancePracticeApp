# Question

You run a hybrid project: a co-located core team works to a predictive plan while an offshore squad delivers in two-week iterations. The offshore squad's throughput has dropped and they have gone quiet on status calls. Speaking with their lead, you learn the cause: the squad's only overlap with the onshore team is a single late-evening call in their time zone, so requirement clarifications take a full day to round-trip, and they have stopped raising questions on calls because it feels futile. The onshore team has begun assuming they are simply underperforming. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a shared overlap window and an asynchronous channel where the offshore squad can raise and get clarifications resolved within hours, and make the real cause of the slowdown visible to the onshore team.
- **B**: Move the daily status call to a time that sits within the offshore squad's working hours, so they stop dropping off and their progress becomes visible again.
- **C**: Embed an onshore product owner proxy in the offshore squad's time zone to answer requirement questions locally without waiting on the round-trip.
- **D**: Shift the offshore squad's work to the most self-contained, low-clarification stories so their throughput recovers without depending on fast access to the onshore team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The cause is now known — a one-day clarification round-trip caused by almost no time-zone overlap, plus a team that has given up asking — so the fix must attack both the latency and the silence, and protect the squad from an unfair "underperforming" label. (A) does all three: a real overlap window plus an async clarification channel collapses the round-trip from a day to hours, restores a path for their questions, and the transparency corrects the onshore team's wrong assumption. (B) moving the call into their hours fixes attendance and visibility but does nothing about the requirement-clarification latency that is actually throttling their throughput. (C) a local PO proxy is a strong, valid option for the clarification bottleneck, but it is a heavier organisational change that may not be staffable quickly and does not by itself fix the broken question-raising culture or the onshore misperception. (D) steering them to self-contained stories is a reasonable interim buffer, but it shrinks their contribution to dodge the dependency rather than fixing the collaboration, and the harder stories still have to be done. The margin is between fixing the actual latency-and-communication mechanism while correcting the perception (A) and options that restore visibility, add a heavier structure, or route around the dependency (B, C, D).
