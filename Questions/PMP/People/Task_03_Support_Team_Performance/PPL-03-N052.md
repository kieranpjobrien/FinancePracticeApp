# Question

Your team's velocity looks healthy, but defects found after each sprint are rising and the product owner is increasingly frustrated at reopening "completed" stories. In the retrospective the team admits they have been skipping automated tests to hit the sprint forecast. What is the best way to restore performance?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team to strengthen and genuinely honour the Definition of Done, and reset the product owner's expectation that a lower but real velocity is healthier than an inflated one carrying hidden defects.
- **B**: Add a dedicated testing sprint after every three delivery sprints to clear the accumulated defect backlog without slowing the regular cadence.
- **C**: Introduce a rule that any story reopened for a defect costs the team double its original points, so the team is incentivised to stop cutting corners.
- **D**: Bring in a separate QA team to test the increments after each sprint so the developers can keep their focus on building features at pace.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root cause is a Definition of Done that the team is bypassing to chase forecast, so (A) repairs the quality bar and corrects the velocity expectation driving the shortcut, which is the durable fix. (B) defers quality into batches, the opposite of building quality in, and lets defects accumulate between testing sprints. (C) a punitive points scheme breeds gaming and hidden defects rather than honest "done" work. (D) bolting QA on at the end reinforces the throw-it-over-the-wall mindset and erodes the developers' own ownership of quality.
