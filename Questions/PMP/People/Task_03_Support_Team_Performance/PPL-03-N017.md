# Question

You are taking over a hybrid logistics-platform project mid-flight. To support the team's performance, you want to understand where individual and collective capability gaps may be putting delivery at risk. Which approaches would genuinely help you assess team performance and development needs? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Review delivery data such as cycle time, rework, and defect trends to see where work is repeatedly getting stuck.
- **B**: Hold one-on-ones with team members to understand their own view of their strengths, gaps, and what they need.
- **C**: Build a skills matrix mapping required competencies against the team to expose single points of failure and gaps.
- **D**: Rank team members from strongest to weakest based on the previous PM's handover notes and focus your attention on the bottom of the list.
- **E**: Ask each team member to estimate their peers' competence so you can triangulate a performance picture quickly.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A sound assessment combines objective signals, self-insight, and a structured capability view. (A) uses real delivery data to locate where performance breaks down. (B) gives each person's own perspective on their development needs, which builds trust and surfaces hidden gaps. (C) makes capability and key-person risk visible across the whole team. (D) anchors on a predecessor's unverified ranking and biases you against people before you have evidence. (E) invites peer judgement that is often political and inaccurate, and can damage trust on a team you have only just joined.
