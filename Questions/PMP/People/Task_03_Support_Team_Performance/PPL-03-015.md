# Question

A high-performing agile team has been together for two years. Velocity is stable, quality is excellent, and stakeholders are satisfied, but the project manager notices retrospective actions have become trivial and engagement in ceremonies is dropping. How should the PM best support continued growth?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate two team members onto a different product to refresh perspectives and bring new skills back to the team.
- **B**: Run a team health check survey and share the results with the sponsor for visibility and potential action.
- **C**: Reduce the cadence of retrospectives so they only happen when the team identifies a real improvement issue to address.
- **D**: Work with the team to set stretch learning goals and pair them with a harder problem domain next quarter.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A plateaued team that has mastered its current work needs harder problems and explicit growth goals to re-engage, which is what (D) provides while keeping the team intact. (A) breaks up a high-performing team based on speculation and may destroy more value than it creates. (B) is measurement without action; sharing results upward does not address the team's need for new challenge. (C) accepts disengagement and removes the very mechanism that surfaces growth opportunities.
