# Question

On a hybrid pharmaceutical compliance project, your strongest analyst tells you privately that she is bored, feels her growth has stalled, and is considering a transfer to another programme. She is currently the only person who understands the validation tooling, and her departure would create a critical single point of failure. Which actions should the project manager take? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Work with her to identify stretch assignments or a more challenging role on the project that aligns with where she wants to grow.
- **B**: Begin deliberately cross-training another team member on the validation tooling to reduce the single point of failure.
- **C**: Tell her that her transfer cannot be approved until a suitable replacement with equivalent tooling knowledge has been recruited and onboarded.
- **D**: Discuss her aspirations with her functional manager so any development or mobility can be supported rather than blocked.
- **E**: Reassign the validation tooling work to a contractor immediately so her engagement is no longer tied to a critical dependency.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Supporting team performance means developing people and protecting delivery at the same time. (A) addresses the actual cause — stalled growth — by finding meaningful challenge. (B) reduces the genuine key-person risk regardless of whether she stays. (D) enlists her functional manager so her development is supported through the proper channel. (C) tries to trap a high performer and breeds resentment, which makes departure more likely. (E) reacts to the risk by stripping her of the very work that could re-engage her and assumes a contractor can absorb undocumented tooling knowledge overnight.
