# Question

The team has just delivered a difficult release after several weeks of overtime. The portfolio manager has assigned the same team to start a similarly demanding project the following Monday. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Brief the team on the new project and start sprint planning so momentum is not lost.
- **B**: Negotiate with the portfolio manager for a structured transition window with a reduced first-sprint commitment.
- **C**: Rotate the most tired team members off the new project and replace them with available staff.
- **D**: Ask the team to vote on whether they want a break before starting the new project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM is the team's advocate at the portfolio level and the right first move is to negotiate realistic conditions, which is what (B) does. (A) ignores burnout signals and is the path to a much worse second project. (C) breaks team continuity and punishes the people who carried the previous release. (D) abdicates a leadership decision to the team and puts them in an awkward position with the portfolio manager.
