# Question

A newly formed agile team building an internal HR portal is delivering steadily, but the project manager notices individuals often seem unsure whether their work is genuinely helping the product. People work hard but ask, in passing, whether what they are doing "really matters." What practice would most directly support the team's performance?

## Options

- **A**: Increase the frequency of status reporting so the project manager has clearer visibility of individual output.
- **B**: Add more detail to each user story's acceptance criteria so the work is fully specified.
- **C**: Make the product goal and the value of each increment visible and revisit it regularly so people can connect their work to the outcome.
- **D**: Introduce individual performance objectives so each person has a personal target to focus on.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

People who cannot see how their effort connects to the outcome lose motivation and direction, so making the product goal and the value of each increment visible and revisiting it regularly (C) gives meaning and supports sustained performance. More frequent status reporting (A) serves the project manager's visibility, not the team's sense of purpose. Individual targets (D) can fragment a team that needs a shared outcome and may pull focus toward personal metrics rather than the product. More detailed acceptance criteria (B) clarifies what to build but not why it matters, which is the actual gap. Connecting work to purpose is a core way leaders support team performance.
