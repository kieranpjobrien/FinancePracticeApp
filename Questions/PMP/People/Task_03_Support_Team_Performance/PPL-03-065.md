# Question

A principal engineer on your telecom OSS project is technically strong but his code reviews consistently block junior engineers on stylistic preferences rather than actual defects. Other seniors have quietly complained. He responds to feedback by saying "this is how we maintain quality." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove him from code review duties for junior engineers' pull requests so his personal style preferences no longer create blockers on the team's otherwise productive working flow.
- **B**: Ask another senior to mentor him on feedback style and allow peer influence rather than PM input to shift his review behaviour over the remainder of the current engagement.
- **C**: Meet him privately, share the specific review examples and their team impact, explore what he is protecting, and agree concrete adjustments to how he provides review feedback.
- **D**: Introduce a standardised style-guide and linter so that stylistic decisions are automated and the principal engineer's review feedback can focus on genuine substantive defect issues only.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI senior-coaching pattern: direct, private, evidence-based feedback that respects his experience while naming the specific behaviour — feedback on how you give feedback. (A) removes his contribution rather than addressing it and feels punitive without a conversation. (B) delegates a PM coaching responsibility to a peer who may not feel comfortable coaching a more senior engineer. (D) is a reasonable supporting move but bypasses the behavioural conversation and does not address what he is actually protecting.
