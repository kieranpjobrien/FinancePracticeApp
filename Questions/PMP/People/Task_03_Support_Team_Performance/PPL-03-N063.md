# Question

A junior data analyst on a retail analytics team has just delivered a dashboard that exceeded the client's expectations after weeks of careful work. The project manager wants to support the analyst's continued growth and sustain their motivation. What is the most effective way to do this?

## Options

- **A**: Note the achievement in the analyst's annual performance review so it is formally captured.
- **B**: Send a thank-you message to the whole team highlighting the project's overall success.
- **C**: Acknowledge the specific contribution and its impact promptly, and discuss what the analyst would like to take on or develop next.
- **D**: Move the analyst onto a larger, more visible piece of work straight away to capitalise on their momentum.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Supporting performance and motivation is most effective when recognition is specific, timely, and forward-looking, so naming the actual contribution and discussing the analyst's development interests (C) both reinforces the behaviour and invests in growth. Waiting for the annual review (A) delays recognition for months, badly weakening its motivational effect. Immediately assigning a larger task (D) may feel like a reward but could overwhelm a junior person and skips the conversation about what they actually want. A general team thank-you (B) is positive but dilutes recognition of the individual's distinctive effort and does not address their development.
