# Question

A team member working remotely on a different continent has fallen behind on two consecutive deliverables. In your video one-to-ones they are upbeat and say everything is fine, but the work does not materialise. Other team members have begun routing around them. You manage a fully distributed team. What should you do first?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team member to break their deliverables into smaller, visible increments with agreed check-in points, so progress and any obstacles surface early instead of at the deadline.
- **B**: Increase oversight by requiring the team member to send you a written status update at the end of every working day until delivery improves.
- **C**: Reassign the team member's current deliverables to the colleagues who have been routing around them, since the work clearly needs to move regardless.
- **D**: Escalate the repeated non-delivery to the team member's local line manager so the performance gap is addressed through their reporting chain.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In distributed work, large opaque deliverables hide trouble until it is too late; (A) creates frequent, low-friction visibility and a supportive structure that reveals the real obstacle and helps the person recover. (B) daily reporting reads as surveillance, can damage trust across the distance, and adds overhead without diagnosing the cause. (C) routing the work away may keep delivery moving once but writes the person off without any attempt to support them. (D) escalating to a line manager before you have tried to understand and help is premature and undermines your direct working relationship.
