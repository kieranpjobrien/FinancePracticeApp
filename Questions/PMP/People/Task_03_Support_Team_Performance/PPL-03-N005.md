# Question

After a demanding release, your agile team delivered a complex payments feature on time and the customer is delighted. The team is visibly tired but proud. As project manager, what is the most appropriate way to support their continued performance?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the team's achievement specifically and publicly, and protect some slack in the next sprint to let them recover before the next push.
- **B**: Move straight into planning the next release while motivation is high, so the team capitalises on its momentum.
- **C**: Give the two engineers who did the most visible work individual bonuses to reinforce the behaviour you want repeated.
- **D**: Thank the team briefly in the next stand-up and remind them that the following release is even more critical to the business.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sustainable performance depends on recognising good work and respecting the team's pace. (A) gives specific, genuine recognition and builds in recovery, which sustains a constant pace over time. (B) ignores visible fatigue and risks burnout and quality slips. (C) singles out two people for what was a team effort, which damages cohesion and the team's shared ownership. (D) pairs a thin acknowledgement with added pressure, undercutting the recognition it offers.
