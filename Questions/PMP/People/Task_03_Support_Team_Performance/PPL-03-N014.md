# Question

Your agile team has delivered five strong sprints, but you notice overtime creeping up, two members have taken sick days, and the quality of pull requests is starting to slip. The product owner keeps adding "just one more" high-priority item to each sprint. The team has not complained. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Discuss the trend with the product owner and the team, and help them protect a sustainable sprint capacity rather than continually expanding the commitment.
- **B**: Tell the team to flag overload themselves in sprint planning, since protecting their own capacity is part of self-management.
- **C**: Ask the product owner to formally reprioritise the backlog so only the single most valuable item is added each sprint.
- **D**: Schedule a recovery sprint with no new features after the current release so the team can address the slipping quality.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The early warning signs point to an unsustainable pace driven by scope creep into each sprint, and the team is not self-correcting. (A) brings the data to both the product owner and the team and re-establishes a realistic, sustainable capacity, addressing the cause. (B) is technically true about self-management but the team is clearly not raising it, so the PM cannot simply wait. (C) limits additions but does not address whether even the agreed scope is sustainable. (D) is a useful recovery step but treats the symptom; without changing the over-commitment pattern, the team will overload again.
