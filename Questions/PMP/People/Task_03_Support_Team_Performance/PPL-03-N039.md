# Question

Six sprints into a machine-learning product build, the team is delivering steadily but every story touching model-deployment infrastructure bottlenecks on the one engineer who understands it, and his queue is growing. The product owner wants to keep feeding deployment stories because they carry the highest business value. What should the Scrum Master advocate to best support sustained team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Sequence deployment stories one at a time behind the single capable engineer so each is completed properly without overloading him.
- **B**: Invest now in pairing and cross-skilling around model deployment so more of the team can take those stories, even at a short-term velocity cost.
- **C**: Recommend the product owner deprioritise deployment stories until the team has spare capacity to handle them without the bottleneck.
- **D**: Ask the organisation to hire a second deployment specialist so the team has redundancy for the high-value work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) builds team capability through cross-skilling, dissolving the single-point bottleneck so the high-value deployment work can flow sustainably — a short velocity dip for durable throughput. (A) keeps the constraint and the growing queue, capping the team to one person's bandwidth. (C) deprioritising the highest-value work to dodge the bottleneck sacrifices business value unnecessarily. (D) hiring may help eventually but is slow, outside the team's control, and skips the faster internal capability-building the team can start now.
