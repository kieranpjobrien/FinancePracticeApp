# Question

For the last four sprints your team has raised the same retrospective complaint: the staging environment is unstable, costing each person several hours a sprint. Action items keep being written but never completed because the platform team that owns staging is outside your project. Morale in the retrospective is visibly flat. What is the best way to support the team's performance?

## Options

- **A**: Take ownership of the staging issue as an impediment, negotiate a concrete commitment from the platform team, and report progress back into the retrospective so the team sees the loop closing.
- **B**: Facilitate the team to brainstorm workarounds they can apply themselves within their own control, since the platform team is outside the project's authority.
- **C**: Stop logging the staging item as a retrospective action since it recurs without resolution, and focus the retrospectives on improvements the team can actually deliver.
- **D**: Capture the lost hours as a quantified cost and present it to your sponsor so leadership can decide whether the platform team should reprioritise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A recurring, unresolved impediment that the team cannot fix themselves is precisely what the PM/Scrum Master must clear, so (A) takes the obstacle off the team and demonstrably closes the loop, which restores faith in retrospectives. (B) pushes the burden back onto the team for something genuinely outside their control. (C) abandons a real, repeatedly-raised problem and teaches the team that retrospectives are pointless. (D) quantifying impact is useful but is a supporting tactic within (A); jumping straight to the sponsor skips the working-level negotiation that often resolves these faster.
