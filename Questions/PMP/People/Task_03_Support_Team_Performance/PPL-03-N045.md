# Question

One developer on your agile team closes nearly half the team's story points each sprint, but always picks up the most complex stories alone and rarely pairs. Other developers have started avoiding those stories entirely, and two have mentioned in one-to-ones that they feel their skills are stagnating. The product owner is delighted with the velocity. How should you best support overall team performance?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team to adopt practices such as pairing on complex stories and a work-in-progress limit, so knowledge spreads and no single person becomes a bottleneck, even if short-term velocity dips.
- **B**: Leave the arrangement in place because the team is meeting its commitments and the high performer is clearly motivated by owning the hard work.
- **C**: Privately ask the high performer to deliberately take simpler stories for a few sprints so the others get exposure to the complex work.
- **D**: Raise the concentration risk with the product owner and recommend recruiting a second senior developer to reduce reliance on the one individual.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A single person carrying the hard work creates a key-person dependency and stalls everyone else's growth; team-level practices like pairing and WIP limits in (A) distribute capability and resilience, which matters more than this sprint's points. (B) optimises a vanity metric while skills atrophy and risk concentrates. (C) artificially throttling your strongest contributor wastes capability and can feel like a punishment, without building a durable practice. (D) adding headcount treats the symptom (capacity) not the cause (no knowledge sharing) and may simply create a second silo.
