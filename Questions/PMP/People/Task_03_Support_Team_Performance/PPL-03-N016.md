# Question

Your development team's throughput has dropped for two sprints. In the retrospective the team is clear about the cause: the product owner is frequently unavailable, so user stories enter the sprint with unresolved questions and stall mid-sprint waiting for answers. The product owner is a busy senior executive. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the product owner to agree how story questions will be answered quickly, such as a delegated proxy or a fixed daily availability window.
- **B**: Have the team make its best assumptions when the product owner is unavailable and proceed, to keep stories moving within the sprint.
- **C**: Pull only fully clarified stories into the sprint and leave capacity unused when too few stories are ready, so nothing stalls.
- **D**: Escalate the product owner's unavailability to the sponsor and request that a more available person be appointed to the role.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The impediment is access to decisions, and the team has correctly diagnosed it, so the PM should remove the obstacle collaboratively. (A) solves the actual constraint by securing a workable availability mechanism without removing the executive's ownership. (B) lets the team guess at requirements, which produces rework and erodes the product owner's authority over the product. (C) protects flow but caps the team's output and wastes capacity rather than fixing the root cause. (D) jumps to escalation before trying a reasonable accommodation, which damages the relationship and may not even be feasible.
