# Question

Your payments platform team has been hitting every sprint commitment for six months, but production defect volume has tripled in the same period. The team argues they are "shipping as agreed." Your customer support lead is complaining. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team to tighten the Definition of Done — mandatory test coverage, peer review on all changes, and production-smoke checks — and track defects as a team-level quality metric.
- **B**: Reduce sprint commitment by a fixed 20% going forward so the team has slack to invest in the quality work they have clearly not been completing inside the current sprint scope.
- **C**: Introduce a separate QA team to catch defects before release, keeping the development team's velocity intact while a dedicated function owns the production quality problem.
- **D**: Accept the trade-off on the grounds that shipping speed and defect volume are always in tension and the customer support load is a normal cost of delivery at this pace.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI quality pattern: strengthen the Definition of Done so quality is built in rather than inspected later, and make quality visible as a team metric. (B) buys slack without committing to how it will be used and is easily reabsorbed by scope pressure. (C) builds a throw-it-over-the-wall structure that degrades shared ownership and rarely improves quality long-term. (D) rationalises a declining-quality trend and abandons the PMI principle that delivery and quality must be held together.
