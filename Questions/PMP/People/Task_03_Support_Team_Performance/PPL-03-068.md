# Question

A junior engineer on her first project is visibly struggling on your 5-month healthcare integration build. Her peers are quietly taking over her tickets to protect delivery. She appears to be withdrawing further with each sprint and has stopped speaking in stand-ups entirely. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ask her peers to stop taking over her tickets so that she has to complete them herself and is forced to ask for help when she genuinely needs it from the team.
- **B**: Meet her privately, acknowledge that starting out is hard, understand how she is experiencing the project, pair her intentionally with a supportive senior, and agree small early wins.
- **C**: Reassign her to a smaller lower-risk project where the learning pace is gentler, so that she can build confidence before returning to the current healthcare integration work later.
- **D**: Raise her struggle with her functional manager so that they can handle the performance and development conversation through the normal line management performance review channel.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI junior-support pattern: private conversation, intentional pairing, and small achievable wins rebuild confidence — this is what first-project struggle actually needs. (A) strips support in the name of growth and may precipitate her resigning rather than learning. (C) removes her from the project without a supportive conversation and can feel like a demotion, not development. (D) delegates a direct coaching responsibility to the functional manager and misses the immediate opportunity the PM has this week.
