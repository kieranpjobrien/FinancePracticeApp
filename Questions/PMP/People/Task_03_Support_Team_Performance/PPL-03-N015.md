# Question

On a predictive telecoms project, you have coached a struggling team member privately twice and adjusted his workload, but his deliverables remain below standard and are now affecting the critical path. His functional manager asks you whether to start a formal performance improvement plan. The team member has been genuinely trying. What is the most appropriate response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Share your documented observations and the support already provided with the functional manager, and agree a clear, fair plan with defined expectations and a review point.
- **B**: Ask for more time, since the team member is trying hard and a formal plan may demoralise him and the wider team.
- **C**: Recommend immediate reassignment off the critical-path work so the project is protected, and leave any formal action entirely to the functional manager.
- **D**: Decline to be involved in a formal process, since coaching is the PM's role and formal performance management belongs solely to the functional manager.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

After genuine, documented coaching has not closed the gap and delivery is now at risk, escalating fairly through the proper channel is the responsible step. (A) provides evidence, credits the support already given, and shapes a fair plan with clear expectations — supporting the person while protecting the project. (B) keeps repeating an approach that has twice failed while the critical path slips. (C) protects the schedule but withholds the PM's first-hand evidence the functional manager needs to act fairly. (D) abdicates the PM's responsibility to contribute observations to a process that directly concerns project performance.
