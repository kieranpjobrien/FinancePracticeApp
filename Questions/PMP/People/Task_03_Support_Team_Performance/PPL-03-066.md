# Question

Your product team is responsible for both new development and production incident response. Incidents have been spiking — team members are spending roughly 40% of their time on ops and planned feature delivery has stalled. The product owner is pushing for more features. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend adding two new engineers to the team so that there is more capacity available for feature work while the remaining members continue to absorb the operational incident load.
- **B**: Ask the team to deprioritise all non-critical incidents during working hours so that feature development can resume while only production-critical incidents continue to interrupt daily work.
- **C**: Set up a rotation system where two engineers at a time handle all incident work for a week so that the rest of the team has predictable focus time on feature delivery commitments.
- **D**: Work with the team and product owner to quantify the incident load, identify the underlying causes driving the spike, and agree a balanced capacity plan between new features and reliability.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI performance-support pattern: quantify, diagnose, and negotiate a balanced plan with the product owner rather than picking one side of the tension. Often the incident spike is itself a reliability-investment signal. (A) throws bodies at a symptom and may hide the underlying reliability issue. (B) deprioritises operational signal, which risks customer impact and erodes trust with operations. (C) is a reasonable tactic but is applied before the root cause of the spike has been understood.
