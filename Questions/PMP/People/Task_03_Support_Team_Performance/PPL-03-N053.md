# Question

A capable mid-level engineer has told you in their development conversation that they want to grow into a technical lead role. An upcoming workstream would stretch them in exactly that direction, but it carries real delivery risk and you have a more senior engineer who could deliver it safely. The project timeline has some, but not unlimited, slack. How should you best support the engineer's performance and growth?

## Options

- **A**: Assign the mid-level engineer the stretch workstream with the senior engineer as a mentor and agreed check-ins, calibrating the support to the delivery risk involved.
- **B**: Give the workstream to the senior engineer to protect delivery, and offer the mid-level engineer a low-risk side task to develop on instead.
- **C**: Assign the workstream fully to the mid-level engineer with no scaffolding, since genuine stretch growth requires the freedom to succeed or fail on their own.
- **D**: Defer the engineer's development goal to a later, lower-pressure project and keep the current staffing focused purely on delivery certainty.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Supporting growth means matching a meaningful stretch to appropriate support, so (A) gives the development opportunity while managing the delivery risk through mentoring and check-ins. (B) plays it safe but denies the engineer the very experience they need and offers only a token alternative. (C) throwing them in unsupported on a risky workstream gambles both their development and the deliverable. (D) postponing growth indefinitely is how capable people stagnate and leave, and the project has slack to invest now.
