# Question

A mature agile team asks to drop sprint reviews, arguing stakeholders already see continuous demos via the team's collaboration tools. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Explore what other purposes the sprint review serves and redesign the format efficiently.
- **B**: Agree to eliminate sprint reviews since continuous feedback is more agile and lean.
- **C**: Insist on keeping the sprint review exactly as defined in the official Scrum Guide.
- **D**: Let the mature team decide on their own since they are fully self-organising now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) honours both the team's experience and the ceremony's other purposes — backlog inspection, stakeholder dialogue on product direction, planning input. PMI's tailoring principle says adapt the form, not abandon the function. (B) drops the function with the form. (C) is rigid adherence without dialogue. (D) abdicates coaching, which is part of the PM's role even with mature teams.
