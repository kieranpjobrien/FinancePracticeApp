# Question

A normally reliable team member on your predictive aerospace project has had three weeks of declining output and missed two commitments. He is usually one of your strongest performers. You do not yet know the cause. Which of the following are appropriate first steps to support him and the project? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Have a private, supportive conversation to understand whether something has changed for him at work or otherwise.
- **B**: Review whether his recent assignments, tools, or dependencies have changed in a way that could be impeding him.
- **C**: Quietly redistribute his critical tasks to other team members so the schedule is protected while you look into it.
- **D**: Document the missed commitments in his performance record so there is an evidence trail if the decline continues.
- **E**: Check whether your own communication or shifting priorities have made his work harder or less clear recently.

## Answer

A, B, E

## Explanation

(A) understanding his situation directly, (B) checking whether assignments or dependencies changed, and (E) examining your own role in the disruption together find the cause before judging the person — the correct support-first sequence. (C) reassigning his critical work pre-emptively can feel like a demotion and may worsen disengagement before you know the cause. (D) building a performance-record trail before you have even talked to him is an escalation step, not a support step, and signals distrust prematurely.
