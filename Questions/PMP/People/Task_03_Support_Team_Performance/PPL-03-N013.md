# Question

A team member on your predictive data-migration project has produced two deliverables with avoidable errors that a peer caught during review. He is clearly capable and seems unaware the errors are recurring. How should the project manager best support his performance?

## Options

- **A**: Give him timely, specific feedback in a private one-on-one, focusing on the pattern and how to catch it, and agree a simple check before submission.
- **B**: Add a mandatory second-reviewer step for all of his deliverables so the errors are caught before they reach the customer.
- **C**: Wait until the project's mid-point review so the feedback can be delivered formally alongside his other performance areas.
- **D**: Mention in the next team meeting that review comments are rising and ask everyone to be more careful with quality.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Feedback is most useful when it is specific, private, and timely, and aimed at helping the person improve. (A) does exactly that and equips him with a practical self-check, addressing the root cause. (B) controls the symptom but does not help him grow and signals distrust. (C) delays feedback for months, during which the pattern continues and the errors compound. (D) is vague, public, and indirect, so the person who needs the message may not even realise it is meant for him.
