# Question

The team has missed its sprint commitment two sprints in a row because a partner team consistently delivers an upstream API a sprint late. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the dependency owner into sprint planning and refinement so both teams are aligned in advance.
- **B**: Stop committing to any stories that depend on the partner team until they become reliable on delivery.
- **C**: Escalate the partner team's repeated delivery performance issues directly to their delivery manager.
- **D**: Plan a one-sprint buffer between the partner team's delivery and the team's commitment to consume it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right first move is to integrate the partner team into the planning process so dependencies are visible and timed, which is what (A) does. (B) protects the team but lets the broader value flow stall. (C) escalates before the PM has improved coordination at the working level. (D) is a reasonable risk response but it builds slack into every sprint without addressing the underlying coordination problem and is better as a fallback than a first move.
