# Question

For the last four sprints, a game-development team has met its commitments only by working evenings and a weekend before each sprint review. Quality is holding, the product owner is delighted with the pace, and the team has not formally complained, though you have observed rising fatigue. The next retrospective is in two days and sprint planning the day after. As Scrum Master, what is the best way to act?

## Options

- **A**: At the retrospective, surface the overtime pattern as data for the team to discuss, and have them set the next sprint's commitment based on their normal-hours capacity.
- **B**: Brief the product owner before sprint planning that the recent pace relied on unpaid overtime, so the PO can adjust expectations and the upcoming scope accordingly.
- **C**: Lower the team's recorded velocity to exclude the overtime hours, so future commitments are automatically sized to a sustainable level without singling anyone out.
- **D**: Raise it privately with the two members who appear most fatigued, checking on their wellbeing before deciding whether the wider pace needs to change.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four take the fatigue seriously, but the question is who owns the decision and where it happens. (A) brings the issue to the whole team in the right forum and lets them recalibrate their own commitment from real capacity — protecting sustainable pace while keeping ownership with the team, which is the Scrum Master's role. (B) is necessary supporting communication but, done alone, sets expectations over the team's head without involving them in the fix. (C) quietly editing the velocity number engineers the outcome without the team's awareness and treats a transparent metric as a lever to pull. (D) a wellbeing check is humane but addresses two individuals when the pattern is team-wide and structural; it is a partial step, not the systemic correction. The margin is between letting the team adjust its own commitment transparently (A) and either deciding for them, hiding the adjustment, or addressing only part of the team (B, C, D).
