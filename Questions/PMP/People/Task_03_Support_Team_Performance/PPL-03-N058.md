# Question

A normally reliable team member's output has dipped over the past fortnight and a couple of small errors have crept into their work. The deliverable they are on is not yet at risk, but the trend is unlike them, and you do not know the cause. What is the most appropriate first step to support their performance?

## Options

- **A**: Have a private, supportive one-to-one to understand what has changed and how you can help, before deciding on any further action.
- **B**: Pair them with a peer on the current work, framing it as collaboration, so they get support and a second set of eyes without a conversation that might feel like criticism.
- **C**: Ask the team to strengthen peer review on the affected work stream generally, so the errors are caught without putting the individual on the spot about their dip.
- **D**: Offer to ease their immediate workload, on the basis that an out-of-character dip usually means something is overloading them and relieving it is the supportive move.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

You have a clear, recent, out-of-character signal but no idea of the cause, so the first step that can actually fix it is to find out what changed: (A) a direct but supportive conversation surfaces whether it is workload, a personal issue, a skills gap, or something else, and lets the right help follow — and it often resolves quickly. (B) pairing is a kind, low-confrontation support, but it manages the symptom and may never reach the cause if the issue is personal or motivational rather than technical. (C) quietly raising review across the stream catches errors but treats the person's dip as a quality-control problem and avoids the conversation that would explain it. (D) easing workload is genuinely supportive *if* overload is the cause — but it assumes a cause you have not confirmed and could be wrong (the issue might be disengagement, a home situation, or a tooling gap), so it should follow the conversation, not precede it. The margin is between diagnosing the cause first (A) and jumping to a plausible-but-unconfirmed support action (B, C, D) that may miss it.
