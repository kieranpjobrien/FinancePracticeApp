# Question

On a year-long predictive product-development project, your team will review a small set of indicators together each month and use them to adjust how they work. The sponsor already receives the formal earned-value status pack separately. You can take a handful of measures into the team's monthly review and want the ones that will most help the team improve their own delivery. Which TWO should you choose?

## Options

- **A**: Defect escape rate per release, trended over time and reviewed with the team to trace where defects originate and change the practices that let them through.
- **B**: Cumulative cost variance against the baseline, so the team can see whether the project is over or under budget to date.
- **C**: The proportion of incoming work items that met the team's entry criteria before they were started, so the team can see and fix upstream readiness problems.
- **D**: Schedule performance index (SPI) at each month-end, so the team knows whether it is ahead of or behind the planned schedule.
- **E**: Earned value accrued to date, so the team can see how much of the planned scope has been completed.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

All five are legitimate, but the team can only *act* on measures that point to a practice they own and change. (A) defect escape rate, traced to root causes, tells the team which of their own practices to adjust; (C) entry-criteria adherence is an upstream readiness signal the team can directly improve by tightening refinement and intake — both convert into a concrete change next sprint. (B), (D), and (E) are lagging, baseline-control metrics: cost variance, SPI, and earned value report aggregate outcome against plan, are heavily influenced by factors outside the team's control (scope, baselines, estimates set elsewhere), and tell the team *that* it is behind without pointing to what to do differently — and the sponsor already gets these in the status pack. The discrimination is not leading-versus-lagging on its face but which measures map to a lever the team can pull (A, C) versus which summarise overall position for governance (B, D, E).
