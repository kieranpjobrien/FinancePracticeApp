# Question

In your last three retrospectives, engineers reported "everything is fine." Your delivery data shows missed commitments, rising defect rates and falling morale on the quarterly survey. You suspect the team is avoiding hard conversations but have not yet addressed it directly. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Share the delivery, defect and survey signals openly with the team, name the gap between those signals and the retrospective messaging, and invite reflection on what is making it hard to speak up.
- **B**: Bring in an external facilitator to run the next retrospective so that the team has a neutral space in which they feel safer to raise the concerns they have been holding back.
- **C**: Start running anonymous retrospective submissions so that engineers can raise issues without attribution and the PM can see the themes that are not coming through in open discussion.
- **D**: Accept the retrospective output as given and work the delivery, defect and morale issues through 1-1s instead since retrospectives are clearly not the right channel for this team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI psychological-safety pattern: name the observation, connect the data, and invite reflection — modelling exactly the behaviour the team needs to practise. (B) outsources a conversation the PM should lead and signals that the team cannot safely talk in front of their PM. (C) patches the symptom without addressing why people do not speak up in person. (D) moves the conversation to a lower-leverage channel and abandons the group learning loop that a retrospective is designed to deliver.
