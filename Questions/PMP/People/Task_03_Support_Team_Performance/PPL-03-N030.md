# Question

A previously high-performing Scrum team's velocity and quality have both quietly declined over the last month. Retrospectives have gone silent, two members have stopped raising blockers, and stand-ups feel perfunctory. You suspect an erosion of psychological safety after a recent incident where a senior stakeholder publicly criticised a developer in a review. Which actions best support restoring the team's performance? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Address the stakeholder's behaviour and re-establish that reviews are for the work, not for personal blame, protecting the team from that exposure.
- **B**: Replace the open retrospective with anonymous written feedback permanently so members never have to speak up in front of others again.
- **C**: Hold one-to-one conversations to understand each member's experience and rebuild trust before expecting candour to return in group settings.
- **D**: Increase the cadence of stand-ups to twice daily to force more frequent communication and surface blockers sooner.
- **E**: Set a velocity recovery target for the next two sprints to refocus the team on getting back to its previous output.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

(A) removes the source of fear by addressing the stakeholder behaviour, and (C) rebuilds trust individually before group candour can return — together they treat the root cause, eroded safety. (B) institutionalises avoidance rather than restoring the ability to speak openly. (D) adds pressure and meetings without addressing why people have gone quiet, likely worsening it. (E) targets the symptom (velocity) and adds performance pressure to a team that is already fearful, deepening the problem.
