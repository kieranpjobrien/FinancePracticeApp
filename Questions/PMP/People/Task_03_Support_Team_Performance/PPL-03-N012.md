# Question

Two new developers joined your stable, high-performing Scrum team three sprints ago to increase capacity. Since then, velocity has fallen, two senior members are spending heavily on rework, and the last retrospective was tense. The sponsor wants to know why adding people slowed the team down. Which actions best support the team's performance now? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Explain to the sponsor that adding members temporarily disrupts an established team and that a dip while the team re-forms is expected.
- **B**: Pair each new developer with a senior member for a defined period so knowledge transfers and rework drops.
- **C**: Adjust forecasts to reflect the team's current, lower velocity rather than the pre-expansion figure.
- **D**: Remove the two new developers to restore the original team's velocity and protect the release.
- **E**: Set a stretch velocity goal above the old baseline to push the enlarged team to integrate faster.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Adding people to a performing team resets it to forming/storming, so a temporary dip is normal and must be managed, not punished. (A) sets a realistic expectation with the sponsor grounded in team dynamics. (B) accelerates integration and cuts the rework directly through deliberate pairing. (C) keeps forecasting honest by using actual current velocity. (D) abandons the capacity investment and disrupts the team yet again. (E) piles pressure on a team still finding its feet, which deepens the storming rather than resolving it.
