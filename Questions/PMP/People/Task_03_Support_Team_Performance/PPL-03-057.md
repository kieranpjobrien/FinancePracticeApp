# Question

Your sponsor praises your mobile app team's high feature-release count and strong velocity trend. Meanwhile the app's weekly active users are flat, conversion is down, and NPS has fallen in the last two quarters. The team feels proud of their delivery cadence. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep celebrating the team's delivery metrics with the sponsor since strong delivery cadence is the PM's primary responsibility and product outcomes sit with the product owner.
- **B**: Reduce the team's feature output significantly so they have slack to investigate why active users and conversion are declining despite all the recent delivery they have completed.
- **C**: Refocus the team's primary review metrics on outcome indicators — active users, conversion, NPS — and discuss feature trade-offs with the product owner against those metrics from now on.
- **D**: Shift the team to a Kanban flow model so that feature delivery becomes continuous rather than sprint-based, improving the chance that outcome metrics will eventually recover over time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI business-value principle: outputs without outcomes is waste, and primary review metrics should be the outcomes the project exists to deliver. Refocusing the conversation with the product owner puts trade-offs where they belong. (A) confuses activity with value and accepts that outcomes are someone else's problem. (B) cuts output as a blunt instrument before the team has even been shown the outcome data they are currently missing. (D) changes the delivery cadence without changing the feedback loop on whether features are actually working.
