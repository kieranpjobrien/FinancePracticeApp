# Question

You lead a hybrid team across three time zones. In a private one-on-one, an offshore developer whose tasks have been slipping tells you the real problem: she is the only team member in her time zone, all design decisions are thrashed out in calls she cannot attend, and by the time she comes online the answers she needs are buried in a long, unsearchable chat thread. She feels cut off and is losing momentum. What should the project manager do to support her performance?

## Options

- **A**: Reschedule the key decision-making calls to a rotating time so that, on a fair share of occasions, she can attend live alongside the rest of the team.
- **B**: Work with the team to change how decisions are captured and shared — recording outcomes in a structured, searchable place — and pair her with a teammate who bridges her into in-flight discussions.
- **C**: Ask the developers in the main time zone to write her a focused daily summary of decisions that affect her work, so she always has the context she needs to proceed.
- **D**: Arrange for her tasks to be sequenced so she works on items whose design has already been settled, removing her dependence on discussions she cannot join.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The cause she has named is a structural information gap, not a personal one: decisions live in a synchronous channel she cannot reach and an unsearchable record. (B) fixes the system for everyone — durable, searchable decision capture plus a human bridge into live discussion — which closes the gap whoever is offline and integrates her back into the team. (A) helps her attend some calls but rotating the inconvenience onto others is a partial, recurring fix and still leaves the poor decision record that buried the answers in the first place. (C) gives her context but loads daily summarising onto teammates and keeps her a passive recipient rather than a participant, doing nothing for the searchability problem. (D) routes around the impediment by curating her work to avoid live decisions, which protects throughput short-term but isolates her further from the team's thinking and caps the work she can take on — the opposite of re-engaging her.
