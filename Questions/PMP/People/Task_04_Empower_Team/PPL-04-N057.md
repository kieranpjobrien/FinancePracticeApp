# Question

A project manager leads a distributed team across three time zones and wants to empower members to make more decisions without waiting for synchronous approval, given the time-zone lag is causing delays. Which approaches genuinely increase the team's empowerment in this distributed context? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Publish clear decision-making guardrails and a shared definition of done so members can act confidently within agreed limits without waiting for the PM's working hours.
- **B**: Give each sub-team authority to resolve issues local to their workstream and only escalate cross-cutting matters, reducing dependence on a single approver.
- **C**: Require all decisions to be logged in a tracker that the PM reviews and confirms at the start of their working day before the team proceeds.
- **D**: Schedule a daily synchronous call at a fixed time so every decision can be made together with the PM present.
- **E**: Route all cross-time-zone decisions through a single senior member who relays them to the PM for sign-off.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

(A) lets members act within explicit boundaries regardless of time zone, directly removing the wait-for-approval delay while keeping decisions safe. (B) pushes authority down to where the work and context live, cutting dependence on one central approver. (C) reinstates PM confirmation before action, which keeps the very delay the PM is trying to remove. (D) forces synchrony onto an inherently asynchronous team and centralises decisions rather than empowering them. (E) funnels decisions through a relay to the PM, adding a bottleneck and a hop rather than distributing authority.
