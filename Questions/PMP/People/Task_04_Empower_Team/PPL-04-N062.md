# Question

On a predictive infrastructure project, you delegated vendor selection for the network hardware to a capable team lead, with authority to make the final call within the approved budget. She has run a thorough evaluation and recommends a technically strong vendor whose lead time would put one non-critical milestone at modest risk; she has logged the lead-time risk in the risk register with a mitigation. She is about to confirm the order and asks whether you are comfortable. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the decision is hers, ask her to walk you through how she weighed the lead-time risk against the technical fit, and back the call she lands on.
- **B**: Tell her the choice is sound and to go ahead, and note that next time a milestone is in play she should loop you in before reaching a recommendation.
- **C**: Confirm it is her call, and ask her to brief the milestone owner on the lead-time risk and agreed mitigation before she places the order.
- **D**: Affirm the decision is hers and tell her she does not need your sign-off on within-budget vendor calls, so she can proceed without checking in next time.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four leave the decision with her — none retracts the delegated authority, so this is not "spot the empowerment option". The discriminator is what best serves her growth and the project at this moment. She has explicitly asked for engagement ("are you comfortable?"), so (A) honours the request by having her articulate the trade-off — confirming her reasoning is sound and reinforcing that the decision is hers. (B) approves but adds a forward instruction that quietly narrows her future autonomy (loop you in *before* recommending), which contradicts the mandate you granted. (C) is a reasonable communication step, but the milestone risk is already logged with a mitigation, and front-loading a stakeholder briefing answers a question she did not ask while sidestepping her request for your read. (D) affirms her authority but waves off the check-in she actively sought; an empowering leader engages when invited rather than treating "you're empowered" as a reason to disengage. The finest margin is A over D — both keep the decision hers, but D declines the coaching moment she asked for.
