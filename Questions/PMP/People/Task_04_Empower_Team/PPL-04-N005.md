# Question

A self-organising development team on a fintech onboarding product has chosen an architecture the PM privately thinks is weaker than an alternative, but it is workable and the team is confident. In the same sprint, the team also decides to skip the agreed security review to hit the demo date. The PM can raise concerns in the retrospective or now. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Stay out of both decisions, since the team is self-organising and intervening would damage their autonomy.
- **B**: Raise both the architecture and the skipped security review now, correcting the team's choices to the stronger options.
- **C**: Let the architecture decision stand but intervene immediately on the skipped security review, as it breaches an agreed quality gate.
- **D**: Defer everything to the retrospective so the team can reflect on both decisions together at the end of the sprint.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Empowering a self-organising team means respecting decisions within their remit while still enforcing non-negotiable boundaries — (C) lets the workable architecture choice stand (theirs to make) but stops the skipped security review (a breach of an agreed gate, not a style preference). (A) treats autonomy as absolute and lets a real quality/compliance risk through. (B) overrides a legitimate team decision on architecture, eroding the autonomy that makes the team effective. (D) delays action on a security gap until after the harm could occur.
