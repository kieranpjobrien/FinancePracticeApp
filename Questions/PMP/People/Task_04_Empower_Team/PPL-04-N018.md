# Question

On a hybrid maritime-logistics platform, the PM delegated a risk to two team members jointly, asking them to "share ownership" of mitigating it. Weeks later the risk has worsened and each believes the other was handling it. The PM needs the mitigation back on track and wants to prevent a repeat. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign a single named owner for the risk mitigation, with the other in a clearly defined supporting role.
- **B**: Hold both members jointly accountable and tell them to coordinate more closely from now on.
- **C**: Take ownership of the risk back to the PM to ensure it is handled.
- **D**: Split the risk into two halves so each member owns one part independently.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empowerment requires unambiguous ownership; diffused "shared" accountability is why the risk slipped through. (A) gives one person clear ownership and authority while keeping the other in a defined support role — the cleanest fix and prevention. (B) repeats the original mistake by leaving accountability split between two people. (C) solves the immediate gap but disempowers both members and burdens the PM. (D) may be artificial for a single coherent risk and can create new seams; a single accountable owner is the standard remedy for the diffusion-of-responsibility trap.
