# Question

In a self-organising agile team, one member has begun dominating decisions in refinement and planning while others have gone quiet. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the dominant member to a different team for a sprint to reset the team dynamic.
- **B**: Speak privately with the dominant member about the impact and adjust facilitation to invite quieter voices.
- **C**: Add a team working agreement that everyone must speak in every single refinement session without exception.
- **D**: Raise the participation imbalance in the next retrospective and let the team work it out together.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The right first move is direct private feedback to the person whose behaviour is causing the imbalance, paired with facilitation changes that create space for others, which is what (B) does. (A) removes a contributor without addressing the behaviour. (C) imposes a procedural rule that produces performative participation rather than real engagement. (D) airs a personal behaviour issue in a group setting before the Scrum Master has tried direct conversation.
