# Question

On your telco field-service app rollout, field technicians have rich ideas about the tool but consistently say "no one asks us" and "we just get what they give us." Their supervisors are the designated stakeholders and have their own views. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue engaging the designated supervisor stakeholders because they are the formal accountable stakeholders and are supposed to represent the technicians' interests to the project team.
- **B**: Replace the supervisor stakeholder group with a field technician forum so that the people who actually use the tool have the formal authority to shape feature design decisions.
- **C**: Run a small field technician co-design workshop for selected features, feed outputs into the supervisor forum, and credit the technicians visibly in decisions that reflect their input.
- **D**: Collect technician feedback through an anonymous survey so that their views are captured without disturbing the existing stakeholder arrangement that has been set up with the supervisors.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI stakeholder-empowerment pattern: give the underrepresented group real voice through co-design, route outputs through the formal forum, and make their contribution visible — honouring both governance and the actual user. (A) accepts supervisor representation uncritically when the technicians themselves say it is not working. (B) overturns the formal governance structure rather than supplementing it and will damage the supervisor relationship. (D) collects input anonymously, which loses accountability and does not give the technicians the visibility or voice they actually want.
