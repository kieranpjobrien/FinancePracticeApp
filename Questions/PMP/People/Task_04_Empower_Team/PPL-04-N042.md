# Question

A project manager delegated full ownership of the data-migration workstream to a senior analyst three weeks ago, including authority to sequence the cut-over. The analyst has just made a sequencing call the PM privately thinks is suboptimal but which is defensible, within budget, and meets the migration acceptance criteria. The sponsor asks the PM in a steering meeting why that particular sequence was chosen. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Explain the rationale the analyst gave, stand behind the delegated decision, and offer to bring the analyst to the next steering meeting to speak to the detail.
- **B**: Tell the sponsor the PM would have sequenced it differently but chose not to override the analyst, so the sponsor understands where the PM stands.
- **C**: Commit to the sponsor that the PM will personally review and re-approve the sequence before cut-over to ensure it is correct.
- **D**: Acknowledge the question and quietly instruct the analyst afterwards to revert to the PM's preferred sequence to avoid further sponsor questions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) preserves the delegation by representing and defending the empowered owner's reasoning, which is essential if delegation is to be real rather than nominal. (B) undermines the analyst in front of the sponsor and signals the PM does not trust the people they empowered. (C) retracts the delegated authority the moment it is questioned, teaching the team that ownership is conditional and fragile. (D) overrides a defensible, in-tolerance decision purely to avoid discomfort, which erodes trust and the value of having delegated at all.
