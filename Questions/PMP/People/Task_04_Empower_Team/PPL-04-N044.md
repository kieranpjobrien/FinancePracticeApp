# Question

A newly formed Scrum team is reluctant to pull work from the sprint backlog on their own. At each daily scrum they wait for the project manager to assign the next task to each person, as they did on previous command-and-control projects. The team is technically capable; what they lack is the habit and the confidence that pulling their own work is genuinely allowed. The PM wants them to begin self-organising. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Keep assigning tasks but, for each one, first ask the team who thinks they should take it and assign accordingly, so they get used to volunteering.
- **B**: Explain that from now on the team self-assigns, and ask the Scrum Master to coach them on pulling work while the PM stays available for impediments.
- **C**: At the next daily scrum, invite the team to decide together who picks up which item and facilitate that conversation, stepping in only if they stall, then repeat daily while gradually saying less.
- **D**: Run a working session to agree how the team will pull and sequence work, including a simple WIP limit and who picks up blocked items, then let them apply it from the next sprint.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The team can do the work; the missing thing is the lived experience of choosing it, in the moment, with the PM visibly not stepping in. (C) gives exactly that, repeated practice at the daily scrum with light facilitation and a deliberate fade, which builds the habit fastest while support is still present. (B) is sound but pushes the coaching onto the Scrum Master and announces a change without giving the team in-the-moment practice, so the old reflex can persist. (A) keeps the PM as the one who formally assigns, so the team is volunteering for the PM's decision rather than owning it. (D) is useful groundwork but agreeing a process in the abstract does not, by itself, overcome a behavioural habit; the team still has to experience pulling work to internalise it. (C) edges the others because it converts intent into practice immediately and with support.
