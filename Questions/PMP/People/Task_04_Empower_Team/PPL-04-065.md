# Question

A new senior hire has joined your cyber incident response team one month ago. He is asking to lead the next live incident end-to-end. He has strong experience from his previous company but you have not yet seen him handle a real incident on your platform. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invite him to lead the next live incident end-to-end, backed by a senior engineer in a shadow role, so he gets genuine ownership while the team has a safety net.
- **B**: Have him shadow three incidents first before taking the lead so he has observed your platform's specific dynamics and the existing team's response patterns before making decisions.
- **C**: Let him lead without shadowing since his previous experience is strong and any hesitation you feel is unfounded caution that might insult an experienced senior hire on the team.
- **D**: Decline his request and keep existing leads in charge of the next several incidents so that his ramp-up does not create risk during the programme's busy incident-response season.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI graduated-empowerment pattern for senior hires: real ownership plus a safety net tests the hire's fit while protecting the platform, and respects the seniority he brings. (B) treats a senior hire as a junior by default and delays empowerment unnecessarily. (C) skips the safety net on a genuinely unknown quantity and exposes the platform to a real risk. (D) declines the request outright and signals that seniority elsewhere does not transfer, which is discouraging and slows his ramp.
