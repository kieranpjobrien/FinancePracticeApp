# Question

A business stakeholder has been assigned as the owner of user-acceptance sign-off for a new claims-processing system. In practice they keep deferring every acceptance decision back to the project manager, saying "you know the system better, you decide." Acceptance is now on the critical path and the stakeholder is reachable this week. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Walk the stakeholder through the acceptance criteria and the test evidence against each one, and support them in making the sign-off call that is theirs.
- **B**: Run a joint working session where the PM and the stakeholder go through the deliverables together and reach the acceptance decision side by side this time, given the schedule pressure.
- **C**: Pair the stakeholder with an experienced business peer who has signed off before, and have that peer guide them through this acceptance round.
- **D**: Ask the stakeholder to confirm in writing that they delegate the technical judgement to the PM, so the PM can clear the gate now and keep the critical path moving.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stakeholder holds the authority and is available; the deferral is a confidence and know-how gap, not a capacity or willingness gap, so the schedule pressure is best answered by getting them able to decide rather than by routing around them. (A) does exactly that, equipping them with the criteria and evidence so they exercise the sign-off they own. (B) looks supportive and quick, but deciding "side by side this time" lets the PM co-own a judgement that is the stakeholder's, and "this time" tends to become every time. (C) builds capability but inserts another person and hands the guiding role to a peer rather than the PM enabling the owner directly, which is slower on a critical path and still keeps the stakeholder one step removed from owning it. (D) formally transfers the decision to the PM, who does not own acceptance, trading the stakeholder's accountability for short-term schedule relief. (A) is best because it removes the actual blocker while leaving ownership and accountability where they belong.
