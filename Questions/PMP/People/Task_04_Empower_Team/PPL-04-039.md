# Question

A team member who represents the team in cross-functional planning has been agreeing to commitments the team cannot meet. The next round of planning is in two days. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Take over cross-functional representation until the team's current representative becomes more reliable over time.
- **B**: Honour the commitments made and ask the team to absorb the extra work across this quarter.
- **C**: Coach the representative on confirming with the team first, review current commitments, and replan together.
- **D**: Email the partner teams that previous commitments are withdrawn and will be re-issued by the PM directly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The right move is to build the capability the representative is missing and clean up the current overcommitments together, which is what (C) does. (A) revokes empowerment over a coachable issue. (B) protects relationships by overloading the team and confirms the bad pattern. (D) goes around the team member publicly and damages trust with both him and the partner teams.
