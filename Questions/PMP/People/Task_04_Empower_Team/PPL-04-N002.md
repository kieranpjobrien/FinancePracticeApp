# Question

On a predictive hospital-systems integration project, the PM delegates ownership of the data-migration workstream to a capable team member who has never led a workstream before. Two weeks in, the team member is making reasonable progress but has quietly avoided two interface decisions that affect another workstream, leaving them open. The PM notices during a routine review. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take the two interface decisions back under the PM's control to protect the dependent workstream, leaving the rest of the migration with the team member.
- **B**: Ask the team member what is holding up the two decisions and what support would let them resolve them, while keeping the ownership with them.
- **C**: Let the decisions sit, since the team member owns the workstream and pushing in would undermine the delegation.
- **D**: Reassign the migration workstream to a more experienced lead before the open decisions cause schedule damage on the dependency.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective delegation includes monitoring and offering support without reclaiming the work — (B) surfaces the blocker and supplies help while leaving accountability intact, which builds the new leader's capability. (A) strips authority at the first stumble and teaches the team member that hard decisions get taken away. (C) confuses empowerment with abandonment; ignoring decisions that affect a dependency is negligent. (D) is a premature escalation that wastes a genuine development opportunity over a coaching-sized gap.
