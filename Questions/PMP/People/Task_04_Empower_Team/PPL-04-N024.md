# Question

On a four-sprint data-migration build, you have a tester who is meticulous but a weak coder, a fast coder who avoids documentation, and an analyst who writes clearly and enjoys running stakeholder demos. The fast coder, keen to learn testing, asks to own the test suite this sprint; the tester is willing to swap onto something else. The work must still ship each sprint. How should you set up ownership?

## Options

- **A**: Give each person primary ownership aligned to their proven strength for now, and build the fast coder's testing growth in as a deliberate, supported stretch rather than a straight swap.
- **B**: Honour the request and let the fast coder own testing this sprint while the tester takes on coding, so both develop their weaker areas.
- **C**: Keep all three on their strengths every sprint and decline the swap, since a short build cannot absorb a learning dip.
- **D**: Let the team decide the allocation among themselves in planning without your steer, so ownership is fully self-selected.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Empowerment means matching ownership to strengths while also growing people, but growth has to be set up to succeed and not put delivery at risk. (A) keeps delivery anchored on proven strengths and treats the coder's interest in testing as a supported, deliberate stretch — empowerment plus development. (B) swaps two people onto their weakest areas at once on a short build that must ship each sprint, risking the increment and the migration's integrity. (C) protects delivery but flatly refuses a motivated growth opportunity, which under-develops the team and ignores genuine willingness. (D) sounds empowering but a self-selection free-for-all could land the hardest work with the least-suited person on a time-boxed build; bounded guidance is the PM's job here.
