# Question

A project manager has delegated a complex reporting workstream to a capable team member and agreed weekly check-ins. After the first week the PM, anxious about a board deadline, starts requesting daily status updates and reviewing the member's working drafts in detail. The member's output quality is fine, but they have begun asking the PM to confirm small choices they previously made on their own. What is the most likely cause, and what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: The member has lost competence under pressure; the PM should keep the daily oversight until the deadline passes.
- **B**: The PM's increased oversight has signalled reduced trust and is eroding the member's ownership; the PM should return to the agreed weekly cadence and let them work.
- **C**: The member is seeking reassurance because the work is genuinely too hard; the PM should reassign part of the workstream to someone else.
- **D**: The check-in cadence was always too infrequent; the PM should formalise daily reviews as the new standard for this workstream.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) correctly reads that the PM's own behaviour, not a drop in the member's ability, has caused the regression to dependence, and restores the conditions for ownership. (A) misattributes the cause to the member and prescribes more of the very behaviour that caused the problem. (C) treats a trust signal as a capability gap and dismantles a workstream that is performing well. (D) institutionalises the micromanagement, permanently undercutting the empowerment that was working before the PM's anxiety intervened.
