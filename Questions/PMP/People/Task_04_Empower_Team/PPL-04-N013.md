# Question

Two senior developers on a self-organising team building a telehealth scheduling app have spent two days deadlocked over which of two valid frameworks to adopt, and the rest of the team is now blocked behind the choice. The two have asked the PM to "just decide" so everyone can move. The sprint goal will slip if the framework is not settled in the next day. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a timeboxed working session today where the two lay out trade-offs against the team's own criteria and the team converges on one framework before the day ends.
- **B**: Decline to be drawn in and ask the team to establish, on their own, a standing decision rule for resolving technical disagreements, applying it to this choice once it exists.
- **C**: Make the call yourself this once to protect the sprint goal, and put a team retrospective item in to agree how they will break such deadlocks next time.
- **D**: Have the two developers each spike their preferred framework over the next day and let the team pick from the working prototypes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team owns this decision, so the aim is to get them to converge without the PM taking the call — but a real deadline constrains how. (A) does both: the PM facilitates rather than decides, the choice stays with the team, and the timeboxed session fits the one-day window — empowerment that also clears the block in time. (B) honours ownership but is too slow and abstract for a same-day deadline; asking a stuck team to first invent a decision rule from scratch leaves them blocked while the goal slips. (C) protects the sprint but does the one thing that erodes a self-organising team — it makes their technical call for them, and deferring the "how we decide" lesson to the retro lets the dependence harden. (D) keeps ownership with the team but burns the remaining day on parallel spikes the schedule cannot absorb, and may simply reproduce the deadlock once both prototypes work. The discrimination is between facilitating convergence inside the deadline (A) and three responses that each sacrifice either ownership or the timeline.
