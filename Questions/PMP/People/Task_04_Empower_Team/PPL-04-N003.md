# Question

Midway through an agile retail-pricing initiative, two business sponsors keep emailing developers directly to push their own pet features into the next sprint, bypassing the product owner. The product owner is frustrated and the backlog ordering no longer reflects agreed value. The developers are unsure who to listen to. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner meet the two sponsors to capture their requests as backlog items and rank them against everything else by value, and ask the team to direct sponsor approaches there.
- **B**: Set up a regular intake session the PM runs where sponsors bring requests, so the developers are shielded from direct approaches and the queue is visible.
- **C**: Have the developers acknowledge sponsor requests but log them for the next backlog refinement so the product owner can weigh them with the team.
- **D**: Ask the sponsors to copy the product owner on their requests to the developers so the product owner stays aware of what is being asked.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The dysfunction is that prioritisation — the product owner's accountability — has migrated to whoever emails the loudest. (A) puts the decision back where it belongs by having the PO engage the sponsors directly and rank their asks by value, then redirecting the team's channel, which both empowers the PO and keeps the sponsors heard. (B) shields the developers but installs the PM as the new intake gatekeeper, displacing the PO just as the sponsors did. (C) stops the immediate disruption but still leaves developers as the first point of contact triaging sponsor input, and only defers — it does not reassert the PO's authority with the sponsors. (D) keeps the sponsor-to-developer channel open and merely informs the PO, so the bypass continues. Both (C) and (D) treat the symptom; only (A) restores the ownership.
