# Question

A senior engineer asks the PM to make a technical choice that is squarely in his own area of expertise. He seems to want validation more than input. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Make the call so the engineer is not blocked and can get on with the technical work.
- **B**: Say "I trust your judgement, this is your call" and ask what is driving the request.
- **C**: Bring the question to the team's tech lead for a tiebreak decision on the matter.
- **D**: Defer the decision until the next scheduled architecture review board meeting for discussion.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The right move is to redirect the authority back to where it belongs and surface why the engineer is seeking validation, which is what (B) does. (A) takes a decision that is not the PM's to take and reinforces a dependency. (C) replaces one bottleneck with another and does not address the engineer's underlying need. (D) adds delay and ceremony to a routine call that has a clear owner.
