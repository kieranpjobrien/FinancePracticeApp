# Question

A project manager wants to delegate ownership of the supplier-integration workstream to free up capacity for stakeholder engagement. Two team members are interested: one has deep integration expertise but has never led a workstream, the other has led workstreams before but is new to integration. The work carries moderate technical risk and a firm regulatory deadline. How should the PM decide who to empower with the workstream?

## Options

- **A**: Choose the experienced integration specialist and pair them with a brief on workstream coordination, while staying close enough to support the leadership aspects they are new to.
- **B**: Choose whichever person has more spare capacity this sprint, since both are capable and availability is the practical constraint.
- **C**: Choose the experienced workstream leader, because leadership skill matters more than technical depth and they can lean on the rest of the team for integration knowledge.
- **D**: Keep the workstream yourself given the regulatory deadline, and revisit delegation once the deadline has passed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) matches the delegate's strongest, hardest-to-replace capability (integration depth) to the work's core risk while scaffolding the gap they have, which is sound delegation under a firm deadline. (C) places the larger competence gap on the riskiest dimension, gambling that borrowed integration knowledge will be enough against a regulatory deadline. (B) ignores fit between person and work entirely and optimises only for short-term availability. (D) defaults to non-delegation, defeating the capacity goal and missing the chance to grow a team member when support is feasible.
