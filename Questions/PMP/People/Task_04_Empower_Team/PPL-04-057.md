# Question

You are hiring a senior engineer for your ten-person infrastructure team. The team wants to run the technical interview and make the recommendation; your HR partner insists only managers should recommend hires. The role has been open for three months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree a formal hybrid process with HR where the team runs the technical interview and recommends, while formal hire sign-off rests with the hiring manager as required by HR policy.
- **B**: Follow the HR policy strictly and have the team provide written feedback only while you, as manager, make the recommendation on your own authority for the infrastructure engineer role.
- **C**: Let the team run and recommend informally in the normal way, then have HR rubber-stamp the decision to keep the process moving without the formal hybrid structure.
- **D**: Bring in a senior colleague from another team to make the hiring recommendation so that HR is satisfied by a manager decision and the team's interview work is still the primary input.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI empowerment-with-governance pattern for hiring: give the team real authority over assessment and recommendation inside the organisation's required process — both can be true. (B) reduces the team's role to commenting and keeps the decision remote from the people who will work with the hire. (C) circumvents HR policy and exposes the hire and the organisation to later challenge. (D) solves the policy problem with a disconnected manager whose recommendation carries no signal about fit.
