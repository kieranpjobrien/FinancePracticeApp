# Question

Your 14-person platform team delivers reliably but engineers have told you in 1-1s they feel they are "only executing" and have no space to explore new ideas. The backlog is relentless. Your product owner is nervous about any time away from committed work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the current focus on the backlog since delivery reliability is what the product owner and sponsor value most on this programme and protects the team's reputation best.
- **B**: Ring-fence 10-20% of team capacity for engineer-chosen exploration work, agree visible outcomes with the product owner, and evaluate after a quarter whether to continue the practice.
- **C**: Tell engineers to explore new ideas in their own time outside working hours so that the backlog is not affected and individuals can pursue whatever interests them personally.
- **D**: Run an occasional one-day hackathon every quarter so that engineers have a token space for exploration while the rest of the team's time stays focused on committed delivery.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI empowerment-by-design pattern: protected exploration time with visible outcomes turns execution into mastery and retention, and the product owner sees the trade-off honestly. (A) dismisses a signal that predicts future attrition. (C) pushes exploration to personal time, which is exploitative and will not fix the ownership problem. (D) is a token gesture that signals exploration is not really valued and tends to fade once delivery pressure rises in the team.
