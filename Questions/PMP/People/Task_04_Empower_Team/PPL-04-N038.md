# Question

A developer on your Scrum team has owned the search functionality for two sprints. She is now missing her own forecasts, going quiet in standups, and her increments are arriving late and incomplete. You want to uphold her accountability without simply pulling the work. Which actions are appropriate? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Have a direct, supportive conversation to understand what is blocking her and what support she needs.
- **B**: Reaffirm that she still owns the search functionality and clarify what "done" looks like for the next increment.
- **C**: Reassign the search functionality to a more reliable developer to protect the sprint.
- **D**: Agree a concrete, lightweight way for her to make progress visible so accountability is evidenced.
- **E**: Assign a senior developer to pair with her and co-sign her search decisions until the increments are back on track.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

(A) uncovers the real cause and offers support; (B) keeps ownership with her rather than removing it; (D) makes accountability visible so progress can be evidenced without micromanaging — together these uphold task accountability while supporting her. (C) abandons the empowerment approach the question asks you to maintain by pulling the work outright. (E) is the subtler trap: pairing for support is sound, but having a senior co-sign her decisions dilutes the ownership you are trying to uphold and signals to the team that her authority is conditional — support without removing decision rights (option A) is the empowering form, so (E) is the inferior of the two support-flavoured options.
