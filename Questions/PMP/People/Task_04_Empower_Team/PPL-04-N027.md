# Question

You delegated three workstreams to three team leads at the start of a predictive ERP rollout, each with clear deliverables. Two leads send proactive status with risks and blockers; the third reports only "on track" with no detail and becomes guarded when you ask follow-up questions. The third workstream is on the critical path. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the third lead a lightweight, specific reporting format and review concrete evidence of progress against the workstream's milestones.
- **B**: Have a direct one-to-one with the lead to understand why the reporting is thin and whether something is being held back, before changing any process.
- **C**: Apply the same proactive status format the other two leads use to all three workstreams, so reporting is consistent across the rollout.
- **D**: Ask the lead to walk you through the workstream's deliverables in your next scheduled review so you can see progress first-hand.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A vague "on track" on the critical path is exactly where empowerment must be paired with evidence of accountability, and the fix should target this workstream without stripping the lead's ownership. (A) does this: agreeing a lightweight, specific format and reviewing evidence against milestones keeps the lead owning the work while making progress verifiable — proportionate and transparent. (B) is a reasonable instinct and the guardedness may signal a hidden problem, but jumping to "is something being held back" reads as suspicion and can put a defensive lead further on the back foot; agreeing how progress is evidenced surfaces the truth either way, with less risk to the relationship. (C) imposes a uniform format on two leads who are already reporting well, which is a heavier, less targeted response to a single lead's thin reporting. (D) gets you first-hand evidence, but a one-off walkthrough is a snapshot rather than an ongoing way to evidence progress, and waiting for the next scheduled review may be slow on the critical path. The margin is between agreeing an evidenced reporting mechanism (A) and probing motive first (B).
