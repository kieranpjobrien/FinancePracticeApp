# Question

Your agile team builds features for a retail banking app. The product owner is technically the decision-maker on backlog priority, but a powerful sponsor routinely overrides her ordering directly with the developers mid-sprint. The product owner has stopped pushing back and now just relays whatever the sponsor wants. Increment quality is fine, but she seems disengaged. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the sponsor's interference to the steering committee as a governance breach so it is formally stopped.
- **B**: Speak with the sponsor about routing priority changes through the product owner, and reaffirm with both that she owns the ordering decision.
- **C**: Coach the product owner privately to be more assertive with the sponsor in future conversations.
- **D**: Accept the sponsor's involvement as a sign of engagement and ask the product owner to document the changes for traceability.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Empowerment fails when the assigned decision-maker's authority is repeatedly bypassed; (B) restores the boundary at the source by realigning the sponsor's behaviour and publicly reaffirming her ownership, which is what re-engages her. (C) puts the whole burden on the product owner to defend authority you have not protected — assertiveness training cannot fix a structural override. (A) escalates to a breach before a direct conversation, which is disproportionate and damages the sponsor relationship. (D) legitimises the bypass and merely makes the disempowerment tidy and auditable.
