# Question

On an agile analytics team, a quiet but strong analyst has the deepest domain knowledge of the customer data, yet she always defers facilitation of refinement sessions to louder peers. You believe she could lead refinement well and it would raise the team's output. She has not volunteered, and in a recent chat she said she is not sure people would listen to her in that setting. What is the best way to empower her?

## Options

- **A**: Speak with her privately, name the specific strength you see, ask whether she would take on facilitating refinement, and agree what support she would want before she does.
- **B**: Ask her to co-facilitate the next few refinement sessions alongside the current facilitator, then take the lead once she has found her feet.
- **C**: Give her ownership of preparing and presenting the data context at the start of each refinement, building toward facilitating the whole session as her confidence grows.
- **D**: Privately encourage the current facilitator to start deferring the data-heavy parts of refinement to her, so she leads those segments naturally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Her hesitation is about whether she will be heard, not lack of capability, and empowerment built on strengths works best when the person actively consents to the stretch and shapes the support. (A) does this directly: it makes her ownership explicit and her choice, names the strength so she sees why she is suited, and lets her define what support she needs — the most genuinely empowering move. (B), (C), and (D) are all sensible confidence-building ramps, but each defers the moment she actually owns the role and, crucially, decides whether to take it: (B) installs her into leading "once she has found her feet" without her having agreed to that endpoint; (C) grows her into it through a sub-task but leaves the facilitation ownership unaddressed for now; (D) engineers it through someone else's deferral, so the authority is conferred sideways rather than offered to and accepted by her. The margin is between offering her real ownership with her consent (A) and easing her toward it without securing that consent first (B)/(C)/(D).
