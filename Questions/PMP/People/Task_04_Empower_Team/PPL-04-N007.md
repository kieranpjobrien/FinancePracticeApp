# Question

A PM is coaching a newly formed agile team on a public-transport ticketing app toward genuine ownership of their work, rather than waiting to be told what to do. The team currently looks to the PM for direction in almost every event. Which actions would meaningfully increase the team's empowerment? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Have the team facilitate their own daily stand-up and decide how they coordinate the day's work.
- **B**: Let the team pull their own work from the sprint backlog rather than the PM assigning each task.
- **C**: Have the PM set the sprint goal and acceptance criteria up front so the team has a clear target to self-organise toward.
- **D**: Invite the team to define and own their definition of done collaboratively.
- **E**: Have the PM screen stakeholder questions and pass on the ones the PM judges relevant, so the team is not pulled in many directions.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Empowerment grows when a team controls how it works and owns its standards: (A) self-facilitated coordination, (B) self-selected work, and (D) a team-owned definition of done all transfer real ownership of process and quality to the team. (C) is reasonable management and gives useful clarity, but the PM unilaterally setting the goal and acceptance criteria is direction-setting that keeps those decisions outside the team — it does not increase the team's ownership, and in an empowered agile team the product owner and team shape acceptance criteria together. (E) puts the PM between the team and its stakeholders as a filter; reducing the team's direct access to context narrows their autonomy rather than building it, even though it is framed as protecting focus.
