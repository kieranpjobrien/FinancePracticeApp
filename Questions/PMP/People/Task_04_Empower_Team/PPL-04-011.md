# Question

A new project manager wants to shift the team from a model where every decision routes through her to one where the team makes more decisions independently. What is the BEST first step?

## Options

- **A**: Announce in the next stand-up that team members should start making decisions themselves rather than asking her.
- **B**: Run a formal empowerment training session for the whole team before changing how decisions are made.
- **C**: Stop attending some recurring meetings so that the team is forced to decide on its own.
- **D**: Co-create a delegation matrix with the team that clarifies which decisions are theirs, shared, or hers.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Empowerment without clarity creates anxiety, not autonomy, so the right first move is to define decision rights together with the team, which is what (D) does. (A) sets a verbal expectation without making the boundaries clear, which usually leads to inconsistent behaviour. (B) treats the issue as a knowledge gap when it is really a structural one about how decisions are routed. (C) creates a vacuum and forces decisions without giving the team the authority or context to make them well.
