# Question

A project manager wants a department head, a key stakeholder, to own communicating an upcoming process change to their own staff, rather than the PM doing it for them, so the message carries the weight of the staff's own leader. The department head is willing but unsure how to start. What is the best way for the PM to empower them?

## Options

- **A**: Draft the full communication for the department head to review, adjust, and then send out under their own name.
- **B**: Brief the department head thoroughly on the change and the rationale, and let them work out how to communicate it to their staff.
- **C**: Help the department head shape the key messages and talking points, then have them deliver the communication to their staff in their own words.
- **D**: Have the department head introduce the change and ask the PM to join the session to answer the detailed questions about the process.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Empowering the stakeholder means lowering the barrier (not knowing how to start) while keeping the message genuinely theirs. (C) does this best: co-shaping the key points removes the blocker, and delivering in their own words keeps full ownership and builds the stakeholder's capability for next time. (A) is supportive but the PM authoring the content means it is the PM's message wearing the stakeholder's name, so ownership is thinner and little capability is built. (B) respects their ownership but leaves a willing-yet-uncertain person without the help they said they need, making a stumble likely. (D) keeps the PM visibly co-delivering, which dilutes the signal that this comes from the staff's own leader and lets the stakeholder lean on the PM rather than grow into the role. (C) is best because it combines real support with undivided ownership.
