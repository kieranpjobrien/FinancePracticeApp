# Question

A PM on a predictive construction-software rollout has made a team member accountable for vendor coordination but routes every vendor email through the PM for approval before it is sent. The team member says the approval lag is making them look powerless to the vendors and they cannot do the job effectively. The PM still wants visibility of what is being agreed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the team member send routine vendor correspondence directly, agreeing a short list of decision types that still need the PM, and copy the PM on the rest for visibility.
- **B**: Keep the approval step but commit to a guaranteed turnaround time so the team member's messages are never delayed more than a few hours.
- **C**: Set up a weekly vendor-coordination review where the PM and team member go through all pending communications together before they are sent.
- **D**: Have the team member draft communications in a shared space the PM monitors, stepping in only when something looks wrong.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team member has been made accountable for an outcome but stripped of the routine authority the role needs, and the vendors can see it — the fix is to match authority to the accountability while preserving the PM's legitimate need for visibility. (A) does exactly that: direct authority over routine correspondence, a defined carve-out for the genuinely significant decisions, and copies for oversight. (B) keeps the PM as a gate on every message; faster approval still signals to vendors that the team member cannot act alone. (C) batches and therefore slows even routine messages and keeps every communication subject to PM sign-off. (D) looks like oversight-only but "stepping in when something looks wrong" on each message is still a pre-send approval gate in practice, leaving the team member unable to commit to anything without the PM's tacit clearance.
