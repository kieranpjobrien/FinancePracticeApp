# Question

Your 10-person research team needs a new specialised testing tool costing roughly 4% of the project budget. The lead researcher has done a rigorous evaluation and recommends a specific product. Your finance contact says you must secure formal programme-board approval. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Override the finance contact's guidance and approve the tool purchase yourself on delegated PM authority, trusting the rigorous evaluation the lead researcher has already completed for the team.
- **B**: Submit the researcher's evaluation to the programme board with a clear recommendation, attend to defend it alongside her, and keep her as the named decision-maker if the board approves the spend.
- **C**: Ask the lead researcher to redo her evaluation using the finance team's standard procurement template so that the programme board has a document structure they are familiar with reviewing.
- **D**: Delay the tool purchase by one quarter to avoid a formal board submission at this point and use the existing tool for the next phase of the team's research work instead.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI empowerment-with-governance pattern: respect the governance path, bring the researcher into the process as the named decision-maker, and keep her authority visible through the approval. (A) bypasses required governance and exposes the PM to a legitimate finding. (C) puts her through additional process work that does not change the substance of the decision and risks appearing as if her evaluation needed correcting. (D) avoids the governance step by dropping a well-evidenced need, which harms delivery and teaches the team that empowerment cannot survive governance.
