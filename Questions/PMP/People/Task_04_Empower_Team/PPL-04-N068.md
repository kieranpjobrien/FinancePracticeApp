# Question

You delegated ownership of the test strategy on a predictive medical-device project to a senior QA engineer, granting her authority to decide the approach within the agreed quality and budget constraints. Two weeks in, she proposes a risk-based testing approach that trims regression coverage on lower-risk modules to hit the timeline. As the project manager who delegated this, which responses are consistent with genuine empowerment? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the decision sits with her, and ask how she has assured herself the trimmed coverage still meets the device's safety and regulatory obligations.
- **B**: Direct her to reinstate full regression coverage and find the timeline savings elsewhere, since cutting test coverage on a medical device is too sensitive to leave to a delegated call.
- **C**: Make sure she has access to the regulatory and risk information she needs, and offer to connect her with the quality assurance lead if useful.
- **D**: Tell her the approach is hers to set, but ask her to walk you through the final test plan and confirm you are comfortable before it is baselined, since you remain accountable for the outcome.
- **E**: Support her decision while being transparent that you remain accountable, and agree how she will keep you informed as the approach plays out.

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

Empowerment means the decision stays with her once delegated; (A) reinforces ownership while prompting due diligence, (C) equips her without taking over, and (E) balances her authority with your retained accountability through transparency and an agreed feedback loop. (B) retracts the authority the moment a sensitive judgement appears — and although the regulatory stakes are real, the delegation was explicitly bounded by "agreed quality constraints", so the right response is to test whether those constraints are met (A), not to seize the decision back. (D) is the subtle trap: it opens by affirming her authority, but requiring you to be "comfortable before it is baselined" converts a delegated decision into a recommendation awaiting your approval — accountability is preserved through visibility and clear constraints, not by inserting a sign-off gate that lets you veto her call.
