# Question

A PM wants to empower a predictive pharmaceutical-LIMS project team to make more decisions, but the sponsoring organisation has a strong command-and-control culture, and a functional manager has just overruled a sound decision the team made within the authority the PM had granted them. The team is demoralised. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Apologise to the team and quietly narrow the authority they are given to avoid future clashes with the functional manager.
- **B**: Discuss the incident with the functional manager to align on the team's agreed decision authority and how overrides should be handled.
- **C**: Escalate the functional manager's interference to the sponsor as a governance breach.
- **D**: Tell the team to proceed on their decision regardless, to demonstrate that their authority is real.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Empowerment in a hierarchical culture only holds if the surrounding managers respect the granted authority, so the PM must first align with the functional manager — (B) addresses the source of the override and seeks a shared understanding of the team's remit. (A) sacrifices the team's empowerment to keep the peace, rewarding the overreach. (C) leaps to formal escalation before a direct conversation, likely hardening positions. (D) provokes an open conflict between the team and a functional manager, putting the team in the firing line over a problem the PM should resolve manager-to-manager.
