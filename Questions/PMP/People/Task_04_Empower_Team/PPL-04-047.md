# Question

On a supply-chain redesign, your warehouse operations manager keeps asking you to "decide" about workflow details she understands far better than you do. She seems genuinely uncertain that it is her call to make. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the workflow calls yourself based on her input so that she is unblocked and the project continues to deliver against the current programme schedule.
- **B**: Establish a steering subgroup of operations representatives so that each workflow decision has a formal sign-off route that does not rely on a single stakeholder making a call.
- **C**: Explicitly confirm she holds the decision rights for workflow choices, ask what is driving her hesitation, and offer whatever support she needs to step into the role.
- **D**: Route each workflow decision through the sponsor so that senior endorsement is attached to every call and the operations manager does not feel personally exposed on any individual item.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI empowerment pattern: name the authority she actually holds, surface the real hesitation, and offer support — empowerment starts with clarity. (A) takes on decisions the PM is unqualified to make and reinforces her dependency. (B) creates a committee where an individual decision-maker is the correct and faster answer. (D) escalates routine operational calls to the sponsor and signals that the manager cannot be trusted with her own domain.
