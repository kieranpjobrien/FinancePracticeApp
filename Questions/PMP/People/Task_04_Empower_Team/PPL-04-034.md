# Question

What does it mean for a Scrum team to be self-organising?

## Options

- **A**: The team selects its own product goals and priorities independently of any stakeholders.
- **B**: The team operates without any interaction with a product owner or Scrum Master at all.
- **C**: The team decides how to deliver the work it has taken on, within goals set for it.
- **D**: The team is free to alter the Scrum framework itself to suit its own preferences at any time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Self-organisation is about the "how" of delivery within externally set goals and constraints, which is what (C) describes. (A) confuses self-organisation with self-direction on product strategy, which sits with the product owner. (B) removes roles that are part of the Scrum framework. (D) conflates self-organisation with changing the framework itself, which is a different decision.
