# Question

A project manager is deciding how much authority to grant in three different situations. Match each situation to the most appropriate level of delegated authority, given the risk and the team member's experience.

## Items

- **1**: A seasoned analyst is asked to choose the format of an internal weekly status report; the choice is low-risk and easily reversible.
- **2**: A mid-level engineer will decide the rollback approach for a production change; the decision is moderately risky and the engineer is competent but new to production rollbacks.
- **3**: A junior team member will recommend whether to accept a contractual change from a vendor; this affects budget and legal terms and is outside their experience.

## Matches

- **A**: Decide and act independently, simply informing the PM afterwards.
- **B**: Recommend an option to the PM, who makes the final call.
- **C**: Decide, but review the approach with the PM before acting.

## Answer

1-A, 2-C, 3-B

## Explanation

(1-A) Low risk and reversible work suits full delegation with after-the-fact information, which avoids needless oversight. (2-C) Moderate risk with a capable but inexperienced owner warrants delegating the decision while reviewing the approach first, balancing empowerment with a safety check. (3-B) A high-stakes contractual decision outside the member's experience is appropriately kept with the PM, while still involving the member by having them recommend, which develops them without exposing the project to undue risk. Matching authority to risk and competence is the essence of effective, graduated empowerment.
