# Question

A high-performing analyst on a hybrid clean-energy reporting programme has started doing only exactly what is asked and no more, after months of the PM reviewing and rewording her every deliverable. She used to volunteer improvements. The PM realises the pattern but a major report is due in two weeks and quality matters. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the close review for the next two weeks given the deadline, then loosen control once the report ships.
- **B**: Agree the report's quality criteria with her up front, then let her own the drafting and review only against those criteria.
- **C**: Pair her with another analyst so two people own the report and the PM can step back from detailed review.
- **D**: Acknowledge to her that the rewording was excessive and ask her to flag any further interference.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The analyst disengaged because authority over her own work was stripped through constant rewording; restoring ownership re-engages her. (B) sets shared quality criteria so the PM's standard is met without taking over the work — it rebuilds autonomy and protects the deliverable. (A) continues the very behaviour that caused the withdrawal, deepening it right when discretionary effort is most needed. (C) dilutes ownership and signals further distrust. (D) is a worthwhile acknowledgement but, on its own, changes nothing about how the work is actually controlled.
