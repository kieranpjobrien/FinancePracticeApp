# Question

How does transparency support empowerment on a project?

## Options

- **A**: It reduces empowerment because mistakes by empowered team members are visible.
- **B**: It is unrelated; empowerment depends only on delegated authority.
- **C**: It enables empowerment by giving people the context they need to make good decisions.
- **D**: It supports empowerment only when the team is mature enough to handle bad news.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Empowered decisions need information, and transparency on goals, constraints, and progress is what makes those decisions good, which is what (C) describes. (A) treats visibility as a downside rather than a feedback mechanism. (B) misses the dependency between information and decision quality. (D) gates transparency on team maturity, which usually creates the very immaturity it is trying to manage around.
