# Question

A capable senior developer on your platform team keeps bringing decisions he could make himself to you with a quick "what do you think?" and then executing your preference. You find yourself making several of his calls each week. The team notices. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue answering his questions since they take only a few minutes each and it seems to produce acceptable outcomes on the work he is delivering for the programme currently.
- **B**: Redirect each decision back to him with "what would you do?" — let him reason aloud, coach where needed, and explicitly confirm his authority on that class of decision going forward.
- **C**: Ask him to document his decision-making criteria in a short written document so that he has a reference he can use rather than checking with you each time a decision comes up.
- **D**: Escalate the pattern to his functional manager so that his leadership development can be addressed through the organisation's formal talent processes and not by you as the project PM.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI anti-reverse-delegation pattern: return the "monkey" through coaching questions and explicit authority confirmation — empowerment is built by the PM refusing to take decisions that are not theirs. (A) solves the PM's comfort but deepens his dependency and tells the watching team the PM is the real decision-maker. (C) treats a behavioural pattern with a documentation fix, which does not change the underlying habit. (D) outsources a direct coaching opportunity the PM is well placed to handle today.
