# Question

On an agile content-management product, the PM has been giving the team task estimates based on past projects, and the team accepts them without comment but consistently misses them. The PM wants more reliable estimates and more genuine team ownership of their commitments. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team estimate the work themselves each sprint, and treat their estimates as the commitment the team plans against.
- **B**: Run a collaborative estimation session each sprint where the PM and team estimate together and agree a number both accept.
- **C**: Keep providing baseline estimates from past data but ask the team to review and adjust each one before it is committed.
- **D**: Use the team's recent actual completion rate to set the next sprint's commitment for them, replacing the past-project figures.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team misses the estimates partly because they are not theirs — handing the team ownership of estimating the work they will do is what improves both accuracy and accountability, because the people doing the work hold the relevant knowledge and now own the commitment. (A) does this fully. (B) is collaborative and better than imposing numbers, but a jointly agreed figure still dilutes ownership and lets the PM anchor the estimate. (C) keeps the PM's figure as the starting point the team merely tweaks, so the anchor and the sense that estimates come from above remain. (D) produces more realistic numbers from real throughput but the commitment is still set for the team rather than owned by them, so it fixes reliability without building ownership. Only (A) does both.
