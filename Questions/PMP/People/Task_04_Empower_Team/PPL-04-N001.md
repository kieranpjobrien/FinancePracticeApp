# Question

A Scrum team building a logistics tracking app keeps stalling because the developers escalate every technical design choice to the project manager, who is also serving as a stakeholder liaison. The increments are slipping and the developers privately say they already know the right answers. The PM has limited time between stakeholder commitments. What is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team to agree which classes of technical decision they own outright and which few genuinely need escalation, then consistently hold to that line.
- **B**: Ask the developers to make their own technical calls and bring only the design decisions they are unsure about to the PM as they arise.
- **C**: Have the team document each escalated decision and the PM's rationale, building a reference the team can use to decide similar cases themselves next time.
- **D**: Move the stakeholder-liaison duties off the PM temporarily so the PM has time to turn the developers' escalations around quickly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The developers already hold the competence; the slowdown is that no one has defined where their authority ends, so they escalate everything to be safe. (A) sets that boundary collaboratively, empowering the team for the calls they should own while keeping the few genuine escalations flowing — it removes the cause. (B) sounds empowering but leaves the boundary undefined: an unsure team will keep escalating to stay safe, so the pattern persists. (C) builds useful precedent but still routes every decision through the PM first and relies on the team inferring boundaries case by case rather than agreeing them. (D) frees PM capacity yet keeps the PM as the decision-maker, reinforcing the dependence instead of transferring authority to where the knowledge sits.
