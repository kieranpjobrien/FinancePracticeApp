# Question

Your executive sponsor has written a detailed vision document for the education-technology programme and brought it to your kick-off as "the direction." Your delivery leads have useful critique but are hesitant to push back on a document the sponsor has already authored. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a structured input session where each lead contributes strengths, concerns and proposals on the draft, and work with the sponsor to integrate feedback into the next version.
- **B**: Accept the sponsor's vision as given since it is her document and her authority, and focus the leads on how to deliver against it rather than on reshaping what she has already written.
- **C**: Ask each lead to submit their critique to you privately so that you can represent the feedback to the sponsor without placing individual leads in an awkward position publicly.
- **D**: Hold a separate meeting with the sponsor to walk through the concerns the leads have raised and agree revisions before the leads see the document again in its updated form.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI stakeholder-empowerment pattern: the leads' expertise is a strength to be made visible, and doing so in a structured session normalises input on executive artefacts. (B) treats the document as fixed and silences legitimate expertise. (C) hides the leads from the sponsor and teaches them that critique happens through intermediaries rather than peer conversation. (D) does the editing work behind the leads' backs and deprives both them and the sponsor of the shared conversation that builds real alignment.
