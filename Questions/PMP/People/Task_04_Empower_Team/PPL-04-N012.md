# Question

On an agile e-commerce checkout project, a key business stakeholder keeps asking the PM what the team is building next and feels their priorities are not landing. The PM wants this stakeholder to have a genuine influence over direction through the proper agile channel rather than through side conversations with the PM. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invite the stakeholder into backlog refinement and sprint reviews so they can put their priorities to the product owner and respond to working increments directly.
- **B**: Arrange a recurring one-to-one between the stakeholder and the product owner so the stakeholder's priorities are heard before each refinement.
- **C**: Ask the product owner to share the ordered backlog with the stakeholder each sprint and invite written feedback on the ranking.
- **D**: Have the stakeholder nominate their top priorities to the PM each sprint, and the PM will represent them to the product owner.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Real influence comes from participating where prioritisation actually happens and from seeing the working product, not from a side channel. (A) puts the stakeholder into refinement and reviews, where they argue their case to the PO and react to real increments — the fullest, most direct empowerment, and it scales to all stakeholders rather than privileging one. (B) gives a genuine voice but in a private channel that can let one stakeholder lobby the PO outside the team's shared forum, and it omits the feedback loop from seeing increments. (C) keeps the stakeholder informed and able to comment, but reacting to a finished ranking is weaker than being in the room while it is set. (D) routes the stakeholder's voice through the PM, reinstating exactly the intermediary the PM is trying to remove. All four give some input; only (A) seats the stakeholder directly in the value conversation.
