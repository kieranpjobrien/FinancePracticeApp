# Question

You are delivering a clinical scheduling system iteratively. The nursing staff who will use it daily attend demos but stay passive, deferring to their unit manager, who is not a hands-on user but controls their time and sign-off. Adoption risk is rising because the people who know the workflow are not shaping the product. What should the PM do first to empower the right stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the unit manager that a few named frontline nurses will be released to sit on a working group with a defined remit to review increments and influence backlog items.
- **B**: Stand up a frontline-nurse working group immediately with authority over the relevant backlog items, and inform the unit manager that their input now flows through it.
- **C**: Ask the product owner to run short workflow-walkthrough sessions with the nurses so their day-to-day knowledge is captured and fed into refinement.
- **D**: Embed a respected senior nurse into the team as a part-time product representative to speak for frontline needs throughout delivery.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The frontline nurses hold the workflow knowledge that drives adoption, so they need a real, defined role in shaping the product — but the unit manager controls their availability and sign-off, so empowering them sustainably means securing that release rather than working around the manager. (A) gives the nurses genuine ownership and locks in the access that makes the group viable, without making an enemy of the gatekeeper. (B) grants the right ownership but bypasses and antagonises the person who controls the nurses' time, so the group may be starved of participation or undermined. (C) surfaces their knowledge but grants no ownership or influence over priorities — capture is not empowerment. (D) gives one proxy a voice, which is better than nothing but re-creates a single filter and leaves the wider frontline still passive. The fine margin is between empowering the group with the gatekeeper's buy-in (A) and doing so over their head (B).
