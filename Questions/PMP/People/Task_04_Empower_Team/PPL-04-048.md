# Question

A mid-level engineer on your data-lake project made an empowered technical call last sprint that failed in production and caused three hours of downtime. She is visibly shaken and asking whether she should "stop making these decisions myself." The team is watching closely. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take back decision authority for this class of technical calls for the rest of the programme so that the risk of another production failure is reduced as delivery continues.
- **B**: Ask her to have her decisions reviewed by a senior engineer first before execution, so that the empowerment is maintained technically while safeguarding against another similar outcome.
- **C**: Reassure her that mistakes in production will not be tolerated again and expect her to be more careful with her decisions going forward now that she has learned this lesson.
- **D**: Run a blameless learning review with the team, publicly confirm she still holds the decision authority, and draw out the systemic lessons that help everyone decide better next time.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI empowerment-with-learning pattern: failure is where empowerment earns its keep, and the PM's public response determines whether the rest of the team will ever take another empowered decision. (A) punishes a single failure by removing empowerment and tells the team that authority is revoked when used imperfectly. (B) dresses review up as safeguarding but is really a soft rollback of her authority. (C) shames the engineer in front of the watching team and guarantees nobody will take an empowered decision again.
