# Question

A self-organising team is mostly working well, but one developer cherry-picks the interesting stories and leaves harder ones for others. The rest of the team has noticed but no one has raised it. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Take back task assignment and hand out stories themselves each sprint until the fairness issue resolves.
- **B**: Raise the cherry-picking behaviour directly with the developer in their next one-on-one meeting.
- **C**: Add a working agreement in the next retrospective that stories must be pulled in strict priority order only.
- **D**: Coach the team to raise the fairness concern in their retrospective and equip them to resolve it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Self-organisation depends on the team's ability to surface and resolve its own issues, and the Scrum Master's first move is to coach them to do that, which is what (D) does. (A) revokes self-organisation and solves the symptom for them. (B) goes around the team and takes ownership of a team-level issue. (C) imposes a rule before the team has had the conversation and may not even address the real concern, which is fairness of contribution.
