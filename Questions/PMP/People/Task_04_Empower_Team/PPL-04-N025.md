# Question

On a hybrid satellite-ground-station project, you delegated vendor change negotiations to a capable procurement lead with authority to approve changes up to £25,000. She has just approved a £24,000 change that, combined with two earlier approvals she made, takes cumulative scope creep on that vendor past £60,000 — a figure that would have triggered sponsor review if presented as one item. Each individual approval was within her limit. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Revoke her approval authority and route all vendor changes through you going forward.
- **B**: Keep her authority but agree a cumulative threshold and a visibility mechanism so related changes are seen together, then review the current total with the sponsor.
- **C**: Let the approvals stand since each was within delegated limits, and note the learning for the next project.
- **D**: Lower her per-change limit to £10,000 so future approvals are smaller and less impactful.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The delegation was sound but the threshold rule had a gap — it controlled single items, not cumulative drift — so (B) fixes the rule and adds aggregate visibility while keeping her empowered, and surfaces the current total for the review it warrants. (A) over-corrects by stripping authority she has not misused. (D) just shrinks the limit, which slows her down without addressing the cumulative blind spot. (C) preserves empowerment but ignores a genuine governance gap and a total that now needs sponsor awareness.
