# Question

A project manager is preparing to hand a team full ownership of release-readiness decisions, which the PM has made until now. The PM wants the delegation to succeed rather than collapse back onto them within a fortnight. Which actions genuinely support empowering the team for this decision? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Make the boundaries explicit: define what "release-ready" means and the conditions under which the team must still escalate.
- **B**: Ensure the team has access to the information and tools needed to judge readiness, such as the test results and quality metrics.
- **C**: Keep a private veto so the PM can quietly reverse any release decision they disagree with, without telling the team in advance.
- **D**: Agree how the team will be supported if a release decision goes wrong, so a single bad outcome does not end the delegation.
- **E**: Require the team to produce a detailed written justification for each release decision and submit it to the PM for review before acting.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

(A) gives the team the clear authority limits that make confident independent decisions possible. (B) ensures empowerment is real by providing the information the decision depends on, since authority without access is hollow. (D) pre-agrees support for failure, which is what stops one bad outcome from triggering a retreat to central control. (C) is a hidden retraction of the very authority being granted and destroys trust when discovered. (E) reintroduces PM pre-approval, converting genuine ownership back into a sign-off process.
