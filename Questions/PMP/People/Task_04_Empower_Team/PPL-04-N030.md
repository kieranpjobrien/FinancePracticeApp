# Question

You handed a capable team member sole ownership of the supplier onboarding workstream a month ago. She has been running it well, but lately she pauses work to email you for a yes/no on small, routine calls — minor wording tweaks, which form to use — that plainly sit inside what you delegated. The pauses are starting to slow onboarding. What is the most empowering response?

## Options

- **A**: Walk through two or three recent examples with her to confirm where her authority already extends, and agree she will decide that class of call herself and flag only genuine exceptions.
- **B**: Write up a detailed decision matrix for the workstream listing every decision type and who owns it, and share it with her to remove all ambiguity.
- **C**: Tell her you trust her judgement completely and she should simply decide these routine calls without checking, then leave her to it.
- **D**: Ask her to keep a running log of the small decisions she is unsure about and review them together in your weekly one-to-one.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

She already holds the authority; the issue is that she is not yet exercising it with confidence, so the most empowering move builds her judgement on concrete cases and resets the expectation that she decides routine calls and escalates only exceptions. (A) does exactly this — it is specific, uses real examples, and hands the routine decisions back to her. (B) is well-intentioned but answers an over-escalation problem with a heavy artefact; an exhaustive matrix can entrench check-the-rule dependence rather than build judgement, and is disproportionate to "which form to use". (C) gives the right message but no anchor — a blanket "just decide" is what she has already shown does not land for her, so she is likely to keep hedging. (D) keeps a routine decision tethered to your weekly review, which slows her further and signals these calls still need your eyes. The fine margin is between coaching her into authority she has (A) and either over-formalising it (B) or under-supporting it (C).
