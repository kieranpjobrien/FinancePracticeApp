# Question

Two developers on a Scrum team disagree on the technical approach for a shared component and ask the project manager to decide between them. The decision is squarely within the team's technical remit, both approaches look viable, and the team has the skills to evaluate them. The developers want a quick ruling so they can get on with the sprint. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline to rule and suggest the team timebox a short spike to compare the two approaches on the evidence, then decide based on what it shows.
- **B**: Ask the two developers to bring the question to the wider team and facilitate a brief design discussion so the team settles on an approach together.
- **C**: Point the developers to the team's existing definition of done and architecture conventions and ask them to use those to choose between the approaches themselves.
- **D**: Give a quick provisional call to unblock them now, on the understanding that the team reviews and can change it at the next design discussion.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The decision belongs to the team, so the PM should keep ownership there while helping them resolve it; the question is which form of help fits best. (B) brings the team's collective judgement to bear quickly and builds the habit of resolving technical disputes themselves, which serves both the ownership and the developers' wish to proceed soon. (A) also keeps ownership with the team and is excellent when the approaches genuinely cannot be separated by reasoning, but a spike costs time the developers are trying not to lose and may be heavier than this disagreement warrants. (C) appropriately pushes the choice back to them, yet if existing conventions actually settled it the two would likely not be deadlocked, so it may not break the tie. (D) unblocks fastest and leaves a review door open, but it still has the PM making a team-owned technical call and trains the team to escalate disputes upward. (B) is best because it resolves the disagreement through the team's own judgement without either taking the decision or imposing disproportionate overhead.
