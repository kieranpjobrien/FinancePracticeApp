# Question

In a single sprint, four empowerment-related situations arise on your agile team. Match each situation to the most appropriate first response by the project manager.

## Items

- **1**: A junior you empowered now routes their next call to you for a yes/no, a fortnight after a decision of theirs led to a half-day of rework that the team absorbed
- **2**: A developer who has run their owned area cleanly all quarter mentions, almost in passing, that the work no longer stretches them
- **3**: A team member forwards you a choice between two equivalent libraries for a decision the working agreement lists as theirs, asking which you would pick
- **4**: A sub-team has, on its own, told a client a feature will ship by a date the contract treats as the sponsor's to set

## Matches

- **A**: Reaffirm ownership, frame the mistake as learning, and agree an earlier check next time
- **B**: Remind them of their decision boundary and redirect the routine decision back to them
- **C**: Offer a stretch by expanding their decision scope or responsibility
- **D**: Address the breached boundary, reset expectations, and manage the client commitment

## Answer

1-A, 2-C, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

Item 1 reads as a confidence wobble after a recoverable, already-absorbed slip, so the response is to reaffirm ownership and treat it as learning with an earlier check, not to withdraw authority. Item 2 is a quietly ready performer signalling under-use, so the fit is a stretch through expanded scope. Item 3 is someone handing back a decision the working agreement already gives them, so the response is to redirect it within their boundary rather than decide for them. Item 4 is a sub-team having reached past its authority into a commitment the contract reserves for the sponsor, which is a genuine breach needing the boundary reset and the client commitment managed, not endorsed. The discrimination is that 1 and 3 both look like someone deferring to you, yet one needs reassurance and the other needs redirection; and 2 and 4 both involve a team taking on more, yet one is invited and one must be reined in.
