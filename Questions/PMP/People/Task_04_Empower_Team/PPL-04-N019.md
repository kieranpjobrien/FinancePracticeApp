# Question

Mid-sprint on an agile travel-booking product, the team discovers a small ambiguity in an acceptance criterion. The product owner is unreachable for two days at a conference, and resolving it one way would keep the sprint moving but makes an assumption about user intent. The team asks the PM how to proceed. What is the best guidance?

## Options

- **A**: Let the team make a reasonable assumption, document it, and confirm with the product owner on their return.
- **B**: Have the team pause that item, pull a ready, unambiguous story from the backlog instead, and settle the criterion when the product owner is back.
- **C**: Have the team reach out to the business analyst who wrote the acceptance criteria to clarify the intent before they build it.
- **D**: Let the team decide their interpretation and proceed, treating it as settled once they have agreed among themselves.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

This is a minor ambiguity, the product owner is back in two days, and an empowered team can keep moving by deciding transparently and validating later. (A) does exactly that — the team owns the call, the assumption is documented, and it is confirmed on the product owner's return, so flow and autonomy are preserved with the risk managed. (B) is a defensible flow-protecting move and avoids building the wrong thing, but swapping in another story stalls the original item for a small ambiguity the team is capable of resolving, and reordering mid-sprint has its own cost; pausing should be reserved for ambiguities too consequential to assume. (C) is reasonable if the analyst genuinely owns the intent, but acceptance-criteria intent rests with the product owner, so the analyst's read is itself a guess the product owner may overturn — and it reintroduces a dependency rather than empowering the team to proceed. (D) gets the autonomy right but drops the documentation and the follow-up confirmation, so a unilateral interpretation could quietly diverge from the product owner's intent with nothing flagged. The margin is between deciding-and-validating (A) and pausing (B).
