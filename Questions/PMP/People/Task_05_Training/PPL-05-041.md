# Question

You have observed a team member struggling with tasks that depend on a skill they list on their CV. When you suggest training, they insist they are already proficient. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the affected work quietly to another team member to avoid a confrontation.
- **B**: Add the training to their development plan and make attendance mandatory.
- **C**: Have a private conversation citing specific examples, listen to their perspective, and jointly agree a development approach.
- **D**: Raise the gap with their functional manager and request that training be assigned through the line.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's people-first approach is to address performance issues directly with the individual, with evidence, before escalating or imposing solutions. (C) respects the person, surfaces the gap with facts, and produces a shared plan. (A) avoids the conversation and lets the issue recur. (B) imposes a solution without dialogue and is likely to be resisted. (D) escalates prematurely before the PM has tried to resolve it.
