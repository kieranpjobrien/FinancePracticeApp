# Question

The project depends on a vendor platform that requires certified administrators to configure it without voiding support. Three team members will need certification, and the exams have a six-week lead time. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the certification cost and lead time to the baseline and book the exams before configuration begins.
- **B**: Begin configuration with uncertified team members and pursue certification afterwards once go-live pressure eases.
- **C**: Ask the three team members to fund and complete the certifications in their own personal time off-hours.
- **D**: Engage the vendor's professional services team to do all configuration so internal certification becomes unnecessary.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats mandatory certifications as planned project costs and schedule items, not personal expenses or afterthoughts. (A) brings them inside the baseline so the work is sequenced correctly and support is preserved. (B) risks voiding vendor support and creating rework. (C) is inappropriate cost-shifting and a retention risk. (D) may be necessary in some cases but does not build the in-house capability needed to operate the platform after delivery.
