# Question

Your agile team supports a logistics platform and has just trained the warehouse operations staff on a new wave-picking module being released this iteration. You want to confirm the training was genuinely effective, not merely delivered, before the module goes into daily use. Which checks give you meaningful evidence that the staff are adequately trained? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Have staff perform representative wave-picking tasks in a realistic environment and observe whether they complete them correctly and unaided.
- **B**: Track the relevant operational metric — such as picking error rate or throughput — after go-live to see whether the expected improvement materialises.
- **C**: Survey staff to ask how confident they feel about using the new module, and treat a high average confidence score as evidence they are ready.
- **D**: Invite staff to raise questions and demonstrate the parts they are unsure about, surfacing residual gaps before live use.
- **E**: Confirm the learning-management system records every member of staff as having completed the wave-picking course.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Effective training is proven by what people can do and what happens in the work, not by how they feel or that delivery occurred. (A) observing real tasks performed correctly and unaided is the most direct evidence of competence. (B) tracking the operational metric the training targeted shows whether the capability translates into the intended outcome once live. (D) inviting questions and demonstrations exposes residual gaps while there is still time to address them before daily use. (C) self-rated confidence is a reaction-level signal and a poor proxy for ability — people routinely feel ready when they are not, and feel unsure when they are competent — so a high confidence score cannot establish readiness. (E) an LMS completion flag records that the course was finished, which like an attendance register speaks to delivery, not to whether anyone can do the work. Verifying capability and outcomes is how you confirm the team is adequately trained.
