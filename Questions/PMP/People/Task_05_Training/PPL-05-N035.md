# Question

On a hybrid project, a specialised modelling skill is needed only for a work package starting in four months. The training provider runs the public course now, then not again for six months. Taking it now costs fee plus three lost days of current capacity, and the skill will partly decay before it is used; waiting risks no course being available in time. The provider will also run a private session for your team on request, at a premium. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Send the team to the public course now and schedule a short refresher with hands-on practice just before the work package starts to counter the decay.
- **B**: Send two team members to the public course now and have them lead internal practice sessions to bring the rest up to speed close to when the work begins.
- **C**: Take the public course now for the whole team, since securing the available slot removes the risk of having no trained team when the work package opens.
- **D**: Book the provider's private session for the team timed to just before the work package begins, accepting the premium to get the skill exactly when it is needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The tension is between a scarce public slot now (with decay) and the skill being needed in four months, but the private-session option changes the calculus by letting you place full training exactly when required. (D) is best: a private session timed to just before the work package delivers the skill at peak readiness with no decay gap, and the premium is justified when the modelling work is specialised and quality-critical. (A) public course now plus a refresher is reasonable and recovers much of the decay, but it pays for training twice over and still relies on reinforcement to undo forgetting that timing could have avoided. (B) sending two then cascading internally is economical but bets quality on those two being able to teach a specialised skill well. (C) the public course now alone leaves a four-month decay gap with no reinforcement, wasting much of its value. Timing the training to the point of need, which the private session makes possible, optimises both availability and retention.
