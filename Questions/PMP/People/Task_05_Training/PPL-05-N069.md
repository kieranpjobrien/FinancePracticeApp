# Question

You are building the competency-development plan for a programme moving five agile teams onto a new cloud-native data platform that the next quarter's roadmap depends on. You have gathered several inputs and want to base the plan on those that actually tell you what training is needed and whether it has worked. Which THREE inputs should most directly shape the plan? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: An assessment of each team's current skills against the specific platform capabilities the upcoming work will require, identifying who has which gaps.
- **B**: The roadmap's phasing, showing when each platform capability is first needed, so training can be timed to the work rather than delivered all at once.
- **C**: Evidence of how people are performing on the platform after training — escalations, defects, and independent delivery — fed back to adjust the plan.
- **D**: The vendor's standard catalogue list price for each certification course, regardless of which courses the gaps call for.
- **E**: A tally of how many staff across the organisation already hold any cloud certification, irrespective of the platform or the teams involved.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A sound competency-development plan is driven by the real gap, the timing of need, and feedback on whether the training landed. (A) defines what to train by sizing actual gaps against required capabilities — without it the plan is guesswork. (B) sets when to train by aligning it to the roadmap's phasing, applying training just in time so skills are fresh when used. (C) closes the loop, using on-the-job performance to confirm effectiveness and adjust where training fell short. (D) is a cost input that matters for budgeting but does not tell you what is needed or whether it worked, and listing prices for courses the gaps may not call for can actively misdirect the plan. (E) counts unrelated certifications across the whole organisation — different platform, different people — so it neither identifies this programme's gaps nor measures its outcomes. Gaps, timing, and effectiveness feedback are the three inputs that shape the plan; price and an organisation-wide tally are not.
