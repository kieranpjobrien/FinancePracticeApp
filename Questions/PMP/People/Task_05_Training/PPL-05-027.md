# Question

Why are training activities included as discrete items in the project schedule and budget?

## Options

- **A**: To make the training visible, resourced, and accounted for in planning rather than absorbed into delivery time.
- **B**: To create a contingency buffer that can be reallocated if delivery work falls behind.
- **C**: To satisfy HR reporting requirements about workforce development hours.
- **D**: To ensure that team members are formally evaluated on their training attendance.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI views training as planned work with cost and duration, not as overhead absorbed by the team in their spare time. (A) reflects this: scheduling makes training a first-class activity that survives delivery pressure. (B) reframes training as buffer, which encourages cutting it. (C) confuses a project planning purpose with an HR compliance one. (D) describes a performance management activity, not the reason for scheduling training.
