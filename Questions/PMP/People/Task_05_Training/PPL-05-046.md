# Question

An internal audit of your healthcare project has just flagged that roughly a third of mandatory privacy training records cannot be located, though team members recall completing the sessions. The auditor wants a response within five business days. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the team to redo training quickly and backdate the completion records to the original session dates to close the audit finding.
- **B**: Work with HR and the training provider to reconstruct attendance evidence, then confirm actual completion and schedule targeted retraining where proof is missing.
- **C**: Push back on the audit by arguing the training happened and shifting the burden of proof onto the auditor to demonstrate real harm.
- **D**: Accept the finding, pause all project work immediately, and retrain the entire team regardless of what records already exist on file.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats compliance as non-negotiable, and the correct first move is evidence-based reconciliation before any retraining decision. (B) verifies what actually happened and targets the remaining gap. (A) is record falsification, which is an ethics violation regardless of the underlying facts. (C) is confrontational, ignores the audit obligation, and wastes the response window. (D) over-reacts and halts delivery without first establishing what is genuinely missing.
