# Question

On a hybrid claims-modernisation programme at an insurer, your sponsor reviews a dashboard showing that 100% of adjusters have completed the new system's training and is about to announce a hard cutover date on that basis. During floor visits you have seen some adjusters handle straightforward claims comfortably but hesitate on complex ones, and the iterative releases keep adjusting the workflow. You want the cutover date to rest on something sound. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose a hands-on proficiency check against representative complex-claim scenarios, and let the results inform when the cutover date is set.
- **B**: Recommend a phased cutover that goes live first with simpler claim types and brings complex claims onto the new system once adjusters have built up real volume.
- **C**: Identify the adjusters who are struggling with complex claims and arrange targeted coaching for them ahead of the announced date.
- **D**: Accept the completion figure as the readiness measure the programme agreed to use, and support the sponsor in announcing the date.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The weakness in the date is that completion proves attendance, not the ability to do the harder work, so the first move is to replace it with a measure of actual proficiency on realistic complex-claim scenarios and let that evidence set the date (A); this is what tells you, and the sponsor, whether the team is genuinely ready. (B) a phased cutover is a reasonable delivery strategy and may well follow, but choosing it now presumes the answer before you have measured where people actually stand; the proficiency evidence should shape whether and how to phase. (C) targeted coaching is sensible once you know who needs it, but it acts on an impression from floor visits rather than a deliberate assessment, and on its own it does not give the sponsor a defensible basis for the date. (D) lets a metric everyone can see is misleading drive a high-stakes commitment, which is the risk you exist to flag. Establishing demonstrated competence first is what makes the date sound.
