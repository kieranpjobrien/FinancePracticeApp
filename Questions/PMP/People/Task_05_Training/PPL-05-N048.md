# Question

You are setting up a new open-pit mine expansion project. New autonomous-haulage technology will be introduced, and you need to establish what competencies the operations team must hold before the technology goes live. You are not a haulage-technology expert yourself. To define the required competencies reliably, who should you primarily work with?

## Options

- **A**: The technology vendor, who can supply their standard operator competency framework for the autonomous-haulage system.
- **B**: The project sponsor, who set the business case and can state the outcomes the expansion must deliver.
- **C**: The functional managers and subject-matter experts who own the operational work and understand what competent performance on the new technology requires in your operation.
- **D**: The HR learning-and-development team, who maintain the organisation's competency frameworks and training standards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Defining the required competencies is a role-specific operational judgement, best made with the functional managers and subject-matter experts who own the work and understand what competent operation of the new technology demands in your own pit and conditions (C). (A) the vendor's standard framework is a valuable input, but it describes generic operation of their system, not how it must be operated safely and effectively in your site's geology, fleet mix, and procedures, so it informs the definition rather than setting it. (B) the sponsor frames outcomes and the business case but is not the source of detailed operational competency requirements. (D) HR owns the format and governance of competency frameworks and helps structure the output, but they do not hold the technical content of what an autonomous-haulage operator must be able to do. The PM coordinates the effort; the substance of the competencies comes from those with the operational expertise.
