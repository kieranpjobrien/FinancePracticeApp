# Question

A skills assessment on your coastal-defence design project shows the team can deliver most of the scope, but the specialist hydrodynamic modelling — needed for roughly four months out of an eighteen-month programme — sits with no one in-house. You have two competent civils who could be trained up to the required standard, a recognised but lengthy external course, contractors available at short notice, and a sponsor watching cost. The modelling outputs feed the structural design on the critical path. How should you close this gap?

## Options

- **A**: Bring in an experienced hydrodynamic-modelling contractor for the four-month modelling window, and have the two in-house civils work alongside them so capability also grows for future phases.
- **B**: Enrol the two in-house civils on the recognised external course now so the capability is owned internally and no contractor cost is incurred.
- **C**: Engage a contractor for the full modelling window with no internal involvement, since they can deliver the outputs fastest and most reliably on the critical path.
- **D**: Split the modelling between the two civils once trained, accepting a slower ramp-up in exchange for keeping the skill and the budget inside the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The modelling is on the critical path and needed soon, so the gap cannot wait for a lengthy course to mature internal capability — yet the skill also has future value, so pure buy-in wastes a development opportunity. (A) resolves both: a contractor secures the critical-path outputs immediately while deliberate side-by-side working transfers capability to the two civils, blending buy and build. (B) and (D) rely on training that will not produce competent modellers in time to protect the critical path, risking the structural design. (C) delivers the outputs but treats this as a pure staffing buy, missing the low-cost chance to build a skill the programme will need again — and leaves the team fully dependent on the contractor. The fine margin is that (A) and (C) both deliver on time; (A) is better because it also closes the longer-term capability gap.
