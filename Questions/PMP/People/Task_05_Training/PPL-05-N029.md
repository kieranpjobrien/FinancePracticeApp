# Question

Leadership has mandated that every developer complete a two-day security training before month-end, which falls partway through your team's current sprint. The team has already committed to a sprint goal the business is expecting, and the product owner wants the commitment kept and the training "fitted in somehow." As the Scrum Master, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Help the team find the lowest-disruption window for the training within the sprint, scheduling it so flow is least affected while keeping the full commitment.
- **B**: Have the product owner take the capacity conflict to the business and agree which committed items can move to the next sprint to make room for the training.
- **C**: Stagger attendance so only part of the team trains at a time, keeping work flowing while everyone completes the mandate before month-end.
- **D**: Bring the lost capacity into the open with the team and product owner now, and have them re-negotiate the sprint scope to what is realistically achievable around the training.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The mandate removes real capacity from a sprint that is already committed, so the honest agile response is to make the reduction visible and let the people who own scope and value reset the forecast together. (D) is best: surfacing the impact and re-negotiating with both team and PO produces a realistic sprint and keeps the commitment meaningful. (A) optimising the timing is sensible and worth doing, but doing it while holding the full commitment denies the capacity loss and sets the team up to miss the goal. (B) having the PO renegotiate with the business is part of a good outcome, yet starting there skips the team's own re-forecast and treats it as purely a stakeholder conversation rather than a planning one. (C) staggering attendance smooths flow but still consumes capacity; presenting it as a way to "keep work flowing" risks implying the commitment is unaffected. Making the impact transparent and re-forecasting with the team and PO is the disciplined move.
