# Question

Your agile team will adopt a new automated-testing framework, but only from the fifth iteration onward when the relevant components are built. The team has spare capacity in iteration one, and a team member suggests running the full framework training now "to get it out of the way" before delivery pressure builds. When should the framework training best be delivered?

## Options

- **A**: Just before iteration five, close to when the team will actually start using the framework on real work.
- **B**: In iteration one while there is spare capacity, so it is done before the busier iterations and does not compete with delivery later.
- **C**: Split it so the team covers the framework's fundamentals in iteration one and does the hands-on, detailed training just before iteration five.
- **D**: Build it into iteration five itself, training the team on each part of the framework as they first need it for the work in hand.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Skills are retained and applied best when training is delivered close to the point of use, so scheduling the framework training just before iteration five (A) means the team learns it when they are about to apply it to real work, with little time for it to fade. (B) using the spare capacity in iteration one is tempting, but four iterations separate the training from its first use, and much of a framework learned that far ahead is forgotten and re-learnt — convenient timing for the calendar, poor timing for retention. (C) a split looks like a hedge, but front-loading fundamentals still detaches that material from its application and adds a second training event; with use not starting until iteration five, the cleaner choice is to teach it once, close to need. (D) learning each part exactly as it is first needed sounds just-in-time, but interleaving first-time training with live delivery means the team works the framework while still learning it, risking poor early practice; readiness should slightly precede use, not coincide with it. Aligning learning with need, just ahead of application, is the aim.
