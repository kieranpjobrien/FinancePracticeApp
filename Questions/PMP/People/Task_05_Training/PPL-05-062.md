# Question

Your organisation has an enterprise agreement that prices training at a flat rate per seat regardless of content. Your project needs four narrow modules, but procurement has paid for full bundles per attendee. Finance wants you to use all bundled content. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Require every attendee to complete the entire bundle so that the paid-for seats are fully consumed and finance's spending can be justified on paper.
- **B**: Have each attendee complete only the four modules genuinely needed now, and document the unused bundle content as an enterprise-level asset for future projects.
- **C**: Ask finance to renegotiate the enterprise agreement immediately to remove the bundle structure, even though the agreement runs for another two years.
- **D**: Send a subset of the team through the full bundle and leave the rest untrained, so that the seat cost is at least partly justified by full consumption.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI allocates team time by value to the project, not by how procurement structured the purchase. (B) meets the actual project need and reframes the unused content as enterprise-level value. (A) consumes team capacity on modules with no project need just to justify spend. (C) attacks a structural procurement issue that is out of scope for a single project. (D) leaves an avoidable capability gap and optimises for invoice-level appearance rather than capability outcomes.
