# Question

Your agile team has identified that nobody besides the departing tech lead understands the legacy billing integration, which several upcoming epics depend on. Spreading this knowledge will require the two remaining senior developers to spend significant time over the next two sprints learning it, which will visibly drop delivered velocity. The product owner is anxious about the burndown. What is the best way to proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Keep velocity up this quarter by having the tech lead handle all billing-integration work during their notice period, and address the knowledge gap afterwards.
- **B**: Make the knowledge-transfer work explicit on the backlog, plan capacity for it across the two sprints, and help the product owner weigh the short velocity dip against the delivery risk of a single point of failure leaving.
- **C**: Ask the tech lead to extend their notice period so knowledge transfer can happen without affecting team velocity.
- **D**: Defer the billing-dependent epics to later sprints so the knowledge gap does not block the current burndown.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) treats capability-building as real, plannable work, makes the trade-off transparent, and lets the product owner make an informed call between a temporary velocity dip and the much larger risk of losing the only person who understands a critical integration. (A) banks on the departing person and leaves the team exposed the moment they go. (C) depends on the leaver agreeing to stay and merely delays the same decision. (D) reshuffles the schedule around the gap without closing it, so the dependency and single-point-of-failure risk persist.
