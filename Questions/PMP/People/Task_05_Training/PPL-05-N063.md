# Question

On a North Sea platform decommissioning project, the diving contractor's crew must each hold a current saturation-diving medical and a renewed competence ticket before the subsea cutting campaign begins. A routine check eight weeks out shows that four of the twelve divers' tickets will lapse partway through the campaign window. Renewal runs through an assessment centre that typically books up several weeks ahead, and the weather window for the campaign is fixed and cannot move. The contractor's project manager, pointing to the crew's long track record, proposes starting on schedule with the full crew and arranging the renewals alongside the work. How should you proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Start on schedule with the full crew as the contractor proposes and progress the four renewals in parallel, keeping the campaign moving within the fixed weather window.
- **B**: Book the four divers' renewals at the assessment centre now and roster the campaign so each of them works only after their ticket is confirmed current, keeping the other eight on the critical early tasks.
- **C**: Replace the four divers with others from the contractor's pool whose tickets are already current, so the planned roster runs unchanged and no renewal is on the critical path.
- **D**: Escalate to the project sponsor that the fixed weather window is now at risk and seek authority and contingency funds to have the assessment centre expedite the four renewals.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A mandatory competence ticket is a hard gate — experience cannot substitute for a current certification the regulator and contract require — so the genuine contest is among the three options that *respect* the gate, and it turns on which acts soonest at the lowest cost to the campaign. (B) wins: it secures the renewals immediately and sequences the roster so lapsed divers simply work later in the window, protecting both compliance and the fixed schedule with the team's own people. (A) is the contractor's framing and the obvious trap — it puts uncertified divers in the water on a track record, exactly the rationalisation the gate exists to defeat. (C) keeps compliance but discards four of the strongest specialists to avoid paperwork that is achievable in the eight weeks available, importing new familiarisation risk for no gain. (D) jumps to sponsor escalation and contingency spend before the straightforward booking has even been attempted — premature, since there is not yet any evidence the routine renewal will miss the window. Book now and sequence around the gate; escalate only if that genuinely cannot be made to fit.
