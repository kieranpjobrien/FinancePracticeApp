# Question

A vendor is implementing a new ERP system for a manufacturer and will hand operational support to your internal team at go-live. The contract includes vendor-led classroom training for your support staff in the final week before cutover. You are concerned the internal team will be able to navigate screens but unable to resolve real production issues once the vendor leaves. How should you strengthen the arrangement?

## Options

- **A**: Embed the internal support team alongside the vendor's consultants on actual configuration and issue-resolution through the build, so they learn to troubleshoot by doing it.
- **B**: Replace the single final-week course with a longer, more thorough classroom programme covering the system's features and common error scenarios in depth.
- **C**: Require the vendor to deliver a comprehensive support manual and issue-resolution runbook the internal team can work from after go-live.
- **D**: Negotiate a paid period of vendor on-call support after go-live during which the internal team leads on issues with the vendor backing them up.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The capability the team needs is hands-on issue resolution, which is largely tacit and built by doing the real work with the experts while they are still there, so embedding the internal team alongside the vendor through the build (A) develops troubleshooting competence that survives the handover. (B) a deeper course still teaches in the classroom; covering more features and example errors raises familiarity but does not produce the diagnostic judgement that comes from working live problems. (C) a manual and runbook capture explicit knowledge worth having, yet rarely convey the reasoning needed to diagnose unfamiliar production issues, so the team is left consulting a document mid-incident. (D) a backed-up on-call period where the team leads is the strongest of the alternatives and does build real experience, but it pushes the learning to after the vendor would otherwise have gone and extends the commercial dependency; capturing the same exposure during the build, when the experts are already on site, is the stronger move. Learning the real system beside the vendor transfers the competence that lasts.
