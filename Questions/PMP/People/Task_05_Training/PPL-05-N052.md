# Question

Midway through a hybrid grid-connection programme, finance signals that the training budget may be cut to protect the overall margin. Your training plan covers safety-critical switching authorisations, optional supplier product seminars, and refresher sessions for staff already competent in the affected tasks. You want to respond in a way that protects the project's real competency needs. Which actions are appropriate? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Rank the planned training by how directly each item closes a genuine, work-required competency gap, prioritising safety-critical authorisations.
- **B**: Identify training that can be delivered more cheaply — for example internal pairing or e-learning in place of external courses — without losing the required competency.
- **C**: Defer the safety-critical switching authorisations to a later phase to ease the immediate budget, then reinstate them if funding is restored.
- **D**: Quantify and surface the schedule, safety, or compliance impact of cutting the safety-critical authorisations so the decision is made with eyes open.
- **E**: Take the figure finance has indicated and trim training items to land on it, so the programme meets the margin target promptly.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Under budget pressure the aim is to preserve the competencies the work genuinely needs, find cheaper ways to meet them, and make any real trade-off visible. (A) prioritises by genuine gap and protects the safety-critical authorisations that cannot lawfully be skipped. (B) seeks lower-cost delivery — pairing, e-learning — that meets the same competency for less, stretching a reduced budget. (D) quantifies and escalates the consequence of cutting safety-critical training so decision-makers own the impact rather than cutting blind. (C) deferring the safety-critical authorisations gambles the one thing that cannot be deferred — staff would otherwise perform switching without current authorisation — on funding that may not return, which is the wrong item to push out. (E) cutting to hit the finance figure without first protecting mandatory competencies fits the plan to the number instead of to the work, and can strip out training the project cannot lawfully proceed without.
