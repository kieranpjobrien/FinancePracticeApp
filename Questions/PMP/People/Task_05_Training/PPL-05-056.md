# Question

A SaaS platform the team relies on has announced a major UI and API redesign that will be rolled forward automatically in four months, during your project's build phase. The team's working knowledge is built on the current version. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Freeze the version used by the project through a vendor-negotiated exception so the team does not need to learn the redesigned platform during build.
- **B**: Carry on as planned and deal with any learning or rework once the redesigned platform is actually live in the team's working environment.
- **C**: Migrate the project to a different SaaS platform whose roadmap is more stable, even though this adds procurement, integration, and learning work now.
- **D**: Plan structured preview training on the redesigned platform using the vendor's beta environment, align builds to the new patterns early, and track the rollout as a risk.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI favours proactive skill building against a known, dated change, combined with risk tracking, over freezing, drifting, or overcorrecting. (D) builds fluency on the target version while the team still has time. (A) delays an inevitable upgrade and assumes the vendor will grant an exception. (B) ignores a known and dated disruption. (C) overcorrects by switching platforms and incurs far greater cost than the learning curve would.
