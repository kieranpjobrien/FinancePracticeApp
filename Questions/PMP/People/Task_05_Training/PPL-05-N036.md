# Question

During a programme you must develop capability in four situations. More than one method could arguably help in each case; match each situation to the method that fits it best.

## Items

- **1**: A new analyst must become productive on the team's bespoke modelling toolchain, which behaves differently from the documentation and whose quirks the team has learned through experience. You need her reliably contributing within a few weeks.
- **2**: Sixty back-office staff in eight offices must all reach the same baseline competence on a replacement finance system in three weeks, and you need a record that each person completed it.
- **3**: A capable team lead is being groomed to run programmes herself in a year or two and needs to grow her judgement and stakeholder skill steadily over that period.
- **4**: Your delivery team must be able to prove to an external auditor that they hold the current, recognised competency standard for the regulated work they sign off.

## Matches

- **A**: Self-paced e-learning modules with completion tracking, rolled out at scale
- **B**: On-the-job training through pairing with an experienced team member
- **C**: Accredited external certification course
- **D**: A sustained mentoring relationship

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-D, 4-C

## Explanation

Each method fits the nature, scale, and goal of its situation better than the alternatives, even though others could partly help. (1→B) The toolchain's value is tacit and team-learned and the documentation is unreliable, so pairing on the real work transfers the quirks fastest — a course or e-learning could not capture them. (2→A) A uniform baseline for many staff across sites in a tight window, with proof of completion, is what tracked self-paced e-learning is built for; pairing or a course would not scale or evidence coverage. (3→D) Growing judgement and stakeholder skill over a year or two is a developmental relationship, not a one-off intervention, so mentoring fits where a course would be too narrow and short. (4→C) Only an accredited external certification yields the recognised, auditable standard the regulator and auditor require; internal methods cannot confer it. The discrimination tested is choosing best fit when several methods look superficially plausible.
