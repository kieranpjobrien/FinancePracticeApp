# Question

Your sponsor questions a proposed £90,000 line item to certify the delivery team on a new cloud data platform, asking why the project should pay to "upskill people the company keeps anyway." The certification is needed to deliver the project's core data-migration workstream safely. How should you respond?

## Options

- **A**: Reframe the cost as a project-delivery requirement by linking the certification to the data-migration scope and the risk of proceeding without verified competence, and present the cost against that exposure.
- **B**: Offer to halve the cost by certifying only the two lead engineers and having them informally upskill the rest of the team during delivery.
- **C**: Agree the concern is fair and look for free vendor training and self-study materials to remove the line item from the project budget.
- **D**: Explain that funding skills development is standard good practice and that withholding it would harm team morale and retention.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) answers the sponsor's actual objection by connecting the spend to deliverable scope and the risk of an uncertified team performing a safety-relevant migration — i.e. it is a project cost, not a generic HR perk. (D) argues on morale and retention, which are exactly the "company keeps them anyway" grounds the sponsor has already rejected. (B) concedes the framing and proposes informal upskilling on a safety-relevant migration, accepting a competence gap to cut cost. (C) abandons a justified requirement to make the line item disappear, risking inadequate preparation for the core workstream.
