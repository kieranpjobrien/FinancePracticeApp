# Question

A hybrid project team of fifteen, spread across four time zones, must become proficient in a newly adopted issue-tracking and workflow tool before the next phase begins in five weeks. Working hours rarely overlap, and individual starting proficiency ranges from never having used such a tool to near-expert. You need everyone to reach a dependable working level, not just be exposed to the tool. Which training approach best fits these constraints?

## Options

- **A**: Run two identical live virtual workshops at times chosen so each pair of time zones can attend one, recording both for anyone who still cannot make them.
- **B**: Group people by starting proficiency and run a short live session for each level at a suitable time, so beginners and advanced users each get pitched-appropriate instruction.
- **C**: Provide self-paced modules with built-in exercises that people complete asynchronously to a deadline, plus an open question channel and short recorded clips for the trickier workflows.
- **D**: Train one proficient champion per location and have each run hands-on local sessions for their colleagues using a shared lesson outline you provide.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The binding constraints are non-overlapping hours, a wide proficiency range, and a need for real proficiency rather than exposure. (C) is best: asynchronous self-paced modules let each person start at the right level and practise at a workable time, while the exercises build actual capability and the channel and clips supply support — it fits all three constraints at once. (A) two live workshops ease the time-zone problem but deliver one pace to a very mixed-ability group, so beginners or experts are poorly served. (B) levelled live sessions handle the proficiency spread well but reintroduce scheduling pain across four time zones and add coordination load. (D) champion-led local sessions give hands-on practice but depend on each champion's teaching skill and a shared outline to avoid drift, risking uneven results. The self-paced blended approach matches the team's real constraints most completely.
