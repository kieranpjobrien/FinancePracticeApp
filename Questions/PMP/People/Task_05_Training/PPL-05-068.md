# Question

Eight team members completed a four-week certification bootcamp last month. Only two have passed the exam, and the pass mark is below average compared to other cohorts. The vendor blames the test takers; team members blame the vendor. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run a structured root cause review covering the curriculum, the preparation time, the test environment, and the learners' prior knowledge before deciding on remediation.
- **B**: Replace the vendor on the assumption that a low pass rate clearly signals poor course design and book a replacement bootcamp at another provider.
- **C**: Require the failed team members to resit the exam at their own expense so that remediation cost falls squarely on the individuals who did not pass.
- **D**: Quietly drop the certification requirement from the project, since the cost of getting everyone certified is now obviously outweighing the practical benefit.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats a performance anomaly as a signal for root cause analysis before any action, not immediate blame assignment or scope retreat. (A) identifies the actual cause before spending on remediation. (B) assumes a single cause without evidence and repeats the investment. (C) punishes individuals for a likely systemic issue and signals unsafe culture. (D) abandons a required capability on the basis of a single cohort's result.
