# Question

You are assembling the competency-development plan for a two-year rail signalling renewal. The team blends seasoned signalling engineers, contractors new to your safety processes, and a graduate cohort. Which actions genuinely strengthen the plan as a basis for ensuring everyone is adequately trained? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Assess required competencies per role against current capability so the plan targets actual gaps rather than blanket training for all.
- **B**: Schedule every team member through the identical full training curriculum to guarantee consistency and simplify records.
- **C**: Define how training effectiveness will be verified on the job, not just by attendance or test completion.
- **D**: Sequence safety-critical training so each person is competent before being assigned to work that requires it.
- **E**: Defer all training decisions until the detailed design is complete so requirements are fully known.

## Answer

A, C, D

## Explanation

(A) grounds the plan in a gap analysis so effort goes where competence is genuinely missing. (C) builds in verification of on-the-job application, the only real measure of "adequately trained". (D) aligns timing with assignment so no one performs safety-critical work before they are competent. (B) wastes effort training experienced engineers in what they already know and treats records convenience as more important than fit-for-need. (E) stalls all development for two years' worth of staff until design completion, by which point safety-critical work would already be under way untrained.
