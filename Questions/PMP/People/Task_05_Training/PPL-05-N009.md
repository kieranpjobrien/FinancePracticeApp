# Question

A scaled-agile programme running five teams keeps stalling because automated-test engineering is concentrated in two specialists who float between teams as a shared service, creating queues and handoffs. The RTE wants a structural fix that improves flow over the next few program increments. Which approach best addresses the root cause?

## Options

- **A**: Formalise the two specialists as a dedicated testing team with a prioritised intake queue so demand from the five teams is managed transparently.
- **B**: Run a deliberate skills-growth effort so each team develops its own test-automation capability through pairing and a visible skills matrix, reducing dependence on the shared specialists.
- **C**: Recruit three additional test-automation engineers, one per remaining team, so every team has its own specialist.
- **D**: Have the two specialists record a library of reusable test patterns and training videos the teams can apply themselves.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The flow problem is caused by a skill concentrated outside the teams; the durable fix is to grow that skill *inside* the teams (more T-shaped members), which is exactly the train/develop response and what removes the handoffs. (B) does this through pairing and a transparent skills matrix. (A) institutionalises the queue rather than removing it. (C) reduces but does not eliminate single-points-of-skill and carries hiring lead time and cost. (D) provides assets that help but, like documentation alone, rarely transfer hands-on testing capability or change the dependency structure.
