# Question

Your new analytics platform is due to launch to the executive team next month. The sponsor has said executives "don't do training" and wants a twenty-minute demo instead. Early pilot feedback shows executives struggle to interpret the dashboards without guidance. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose a tailored executive enablement approach: a short guided demo, a one-page interpretive reference, and optional short one-to-one walkthroughs with their analysts.
- **B**: Override the sponsor and require all executives to attend a standard two-hour classroom session to ensure the dashboards are used correctly from day one.
- **C**: Deliver only the twenty-minute demo as requested and accept that executives will learn the platform by trial and error during their own review cycles.
- **D**: Cancel the executive launch until a full formal training curriculum for senior leaders can be commissioned, piloted, and rolled out through the internal academy.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle adapts learning to the audience without abandoning the goal; executives need short, context-rich enablement tied to how they will actually use the tool. (A) does that and respects the sponsor's constraint. (B) overrides a stakeholder constraint without negotiation and is likely to fail on attendance alone. (C) accepts a known adoption risk that the pilot has already surfaced. (D) delays a launch the business needs for a disproportionate training programme.
