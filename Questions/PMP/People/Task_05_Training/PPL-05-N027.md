# Question

Your organisation is new to agile. Three sprints in, the assigned product owner keeps approving stakeholder requests into the sprint backlog mid-sprint, rarely orders the backlog, and misses most reviews. They are a capable businessperson, want to do the role well, and have had no agile training. The team is frustrated and scope is churning. As the Scrum Master, what is your best course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Work alongside the product owner on backlog ordering and protecting the sprint, coaching them in real time on the role until they can do it unaided.
- **B**: Arrange for the product owner to attend a formal external product-owner certification course at the next available date.
- **C**: Agree a working agreement with the product owner that defines how mid-sprint requests are handled and what the team needs from them, and review it each sprint.
- **D**: Ask an experienced product owner from another team to mentor this one on agile product ownership over the coming months.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The product owner is a willing stakeholder whose role competence is part of your duty to develop, and the problems are concrete, immediate, and recurring each sprint. (A) is best because hands-on coaching in the flow of the actual work closes the visible gaps now and builds the role skill fastest where the person is struggling. (B) a certification course builds knowledge but is a future, generic event that does nothing for the churn happening this sprint and may not transfer to their context. (C) a working agreement is a useful reinforcement and may complement coaching, but on its own it sets expectations without building the capability to meet them. (D) external mentoring develops the role over the long term, yet it is slower and less targeted at the specific behaviours derailing the current sprints. Direct, applied coaching of the role holder is the most effective immediate development response.
