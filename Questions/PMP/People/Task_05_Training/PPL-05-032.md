# Question

Six weeks after a new code review process was rolled out with formal training, you observe that most pull requests are still being merged with the old, lighter checks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a repeat of the original training to refresh the content for everyone.
- **B**: Investigate why adoption has lapsed, then address barriers through coaching, tooling, and reinforced expectations.
- **C**: Add a policy that any PR merged without the new checks will be reverted automatically.
- **D**: Ask each team lead to report monthly on whether their team is following the new process.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI emphasises root-cause analysis when behaviour does not change after training; the gap is rarely solved by repeating the same content. (B) treats reversion as a symptom and investigates barriers (workflow friction, unclear expectations, tooling gaps) before choosing an intervention. (A) assumes more training will fix what is actually an application problem. (C) jumps to enforcement before understanding the cause and may damage trust. (D) creates reporting overhead without addressing the underlying behaviour.
