# Question

On your predictive project, one specialist holds deep, undocumented, hands-on knowledge of a critical legacy interface that the final three milestones depend on. The specialist has given notice and leaves in six weeks. Two of your engineers are capable and available to take it on. You want the team genuinely able to run and troubleshoot the interface after the specialist goes. What is the best way to use the six weeks?

## Options

- **A**: Have the specialist produce a thorough technical guide and runbook for the interface, reviewed by the two engineers before the specialist leaves.
- **B**: Have the specialist record detailed walk-through sessions of the interface's operation and common faults for the engineers to study and keep as a reference.
- **C**: Pair the two engineers with the specialist on the live interface work now, having them do the tasks under supervision while the specialist coaches and corrects.
- **D**: Arrange a contractor experienced in similar legacy interfaces to shadow the specialist for the handover and then support the team through the final milestones.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The knowledge is tacit and hands-on, and the goal is that the team can actually operate and troubleshoot the interface, so the best use of the limited window is supervised practice on the real work. (C) is best: having the engineers do the tasks while the specialist coaches transfers tacit skill and lets you verify, before the specialist leaves, that the team can genuinely do it. (A) a guide and runbook are valuable and worth producing alongside, but documents alone rarely convey complex tacit know-how and never confirm capability. (B) recorded walk-throughs capture explanation passively; studying them is not the same as being able to perform under real conditions. (D) a contractor adds cover but still needs transfer from the same departing specialist and leaves your own team no more capable, perpetuating the dependency. Supervised hands-on transfer to your engineers, with documentation as backup, builds the durable capability the milestones require.
