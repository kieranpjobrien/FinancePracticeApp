# Question

Mid-execution on a fixed-budget predictive project, a newly issued regulation requires your team to hold an updated compliance certification before they may sign off deliverables. The certification was not in the plan and costs about 4% of the remaining budget. Your contingency reserve was set aside for identified delivery risks; there is no management reserve. The sponsor is reachable but expects issues handled within governance. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Fund the certification from the contingency reserve now to avoid delay, and report the draw-down and its rationale to the sponsor afterwards.
- **B**: Identify lower-priority discretionary spend within the current baseline that could be re-prioritised to absorb the cost, and bring that option to the sponsor.
- **C**: Treat the certification as procurement of a required service and process it through the project's normal purchasing authority.
- **D**: Raise a change request documenting the new requirement and its cost impact so the cost baseline is updated and funding is authorised through integrated change control.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

An externally imposed, unplanned, mandatory cost is a genuine change to the cost baseline, and the disciplined route is the one that authorises funding and adjusts the baseline transparently. (D) is best: integrated change control puts the new requirement, its cost, and the baseline impact in front of the authority who can approve funding and reset expectations. (A) contingency reserve is for identified risks within the existing baseline, not for new mandatory scope; drawing it unilaterally and informing afterwards bypasses the governance the sponsor expects. (B) re-prioritising baseline spend is itself a baseline change that must be approved, so surfacing it informally is incomplete — the proper vehicle is still a change request. (C) routing it as routine procurement records the purchase but hides a material baseline change from change control and the sponsor. Putting the change through integrated change control is what keeps the baseline honest and funded.
