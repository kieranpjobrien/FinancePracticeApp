# Question

New accessibility regulations take effect in seven months and will apply to the public-facing portal your team is building for a government client. None of the developers or designers have done accessibility work before. The portal is already in development. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the regulations are finalised to avoid training on an interpretation that later changes, and book training once the final text is available for use.
- **B**: Run a capability assessment against the current regulation draft, schedule targeted accessibility training with applied coaching, and integrate accessibility criteria into the Definition of Done.
- **C**: Outsource the accessibility remediation to a specialist vendor at the end of the project so the current team can focus on feature delivery until go-live is complete.
- **D**: Rely on an accessibility scanner tool to flag defects during the final pre-launch phase so the team can remediate the issues without any formal accessibility training.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects anticipated compliance requirements to be designed in early through capability building plus process integration, not retrofitted. (B) builds the skill and embeds accessibility in the team's delivery flow through the Definition of Done. (A) waits for a cleaner problem while the backlog is already producing non-compliant code. (C) outsources a responsibility that must be built in throughout design and development, and leaves the internal team without the skill. (D) relies on a tool to substitute for knowledge, which catches only a fraction of accessibility issues.
