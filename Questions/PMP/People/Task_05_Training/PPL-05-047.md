# Question

Four engineers on your two-year infrastructure programme hold a vendor certification that expires fifteen months into the project. The vendor has confirmed the recertification curriculum will change significantly before their renewal window. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer the issue until month fourteen so the engineers study the final, most current version of the recertification materials.
- **B**: Require all four engineers to take the existing recertification exam this quarter so the certificates are refreshed for the full remaining project duration.
- **C**: Plan recertification into the schedule with budget, study time, and staggered exam dates, and track the vendor's curriculum changes as a risk on the register.
- **D**: Treat recertification as a personal development matter that sits outside the project and let each engineer choose whether and when to sit the exam.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects foreseeable skill and credential gaps to be planned and tracked, not deferred, rushed, or devolved. (C) integrates recertification into the schedule and keeps the curriculum change visible as a live risk. (A) waits until the window is too tight and raises the chance of lapsed credentials mid-delivery. (B) front-loads unnecessary effort on the current syllabus without reducing the need to re-cover any material the new curriculum adds. (D) removes PM ownership of a delivery-critical credential.
