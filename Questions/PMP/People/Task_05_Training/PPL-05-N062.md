# Question

On an 18-month aircraft maintenance-hangar fit-out, the commissioning crew must be trained and certified on a specialised non-destructive-testing rig that they will operate during the commissioning work package, which begins in month 14. A resource manager, keen to lock the crew's diaries before they are committed to other work, proposes booking them onto the next available course in month 3 while they are between duties. You confirm with the training provider that the certification carries no expiry once earned and that the course runs as an open enrolment every month. How should you schedule the training?

## Options

- **A**: Book the course in month 3 as proposed, securing the crew's diaries early and confirming the team is certified and ready well ahead of the commissioning milestone.
- **B**: Book the course in month 3 to lock the diaries, then add a paid half-day refresher with the provider immediately before commissioning to restore any hands-on sharpness lost in the interval.
- **C**: Reserve a provisional month-3 slot now to protect availability, but plan to run the actual course in the weeks just before month 14 if the slot can still be held open.
- **D**: Schedule the course for the weeks immediately before the commissioning package begins, so the crew apply the skill while it is fresh and diaries are reserved against that window instead.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The discriminator is *when competence is needed* versus *when a diary is convenient*. Because the certificate never expires and the course runs every month, training early buys no protection — the only constant is that hands-on skill decays over an eleven-month gap with no practice, so the timing should follow the work. (D) applies just-in-time training and still reserves the crew's diaries, just against the right window. (A) optimises purely for diary certainty and treats holding a certificate as being competent, ignoring decay over eleven idle months. (B) recognises the decay but accepts it and then pays a second time for a refresher to undo a problem that need never have been created. (C) looks like a compromise but commits to nothing — a provisional early slot adds administrative juggling while the sensible plan is simply to book late, which the freely available monthly course makes easy. When a credential is permanent and readily obtained, train to the point of use.
