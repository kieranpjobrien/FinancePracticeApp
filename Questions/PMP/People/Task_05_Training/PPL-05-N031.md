# Question

You coach a development team that completed test-driven development (TDD) training last month. Adoption is shallow: tests are mostly written after the code. The team agrees TDD is valuable but says writing tests first "slows them down," and several members are genuinely unsure how to drive a design test-first on their kind of work. Which TWO actions are most likely to turn the training into real, lasting practice? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Arrange for an experienced practitioner to pair with team members on their live stories, writing tests first together on the actual work.
- **B**: Make TDD adoption a standing retrospective topic where the team inspects how it is going and adapts how they apply it to their context.
- **C**: Run a hands-on internal workshop using exercises (katas) so the team practises the test-first cycle in a low-stakes setting.
- **D**: Ask the team's strongest TDD practitioner to write and share reference examples of test-first code from the team's own codebase.
- **E**: Schedule a follow-up session with the original trainer focused on the specific situations the team finds hard to test first.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The gap is applying a known technique on real work and making the change stick, so the two strongest actions combine deliberate practice in context with team-owned reinforcement. (A) pairing on live stories transfers the skill exactly where it must be used and demonstrates that test-first need not slow good work — the most direct capability builder. (B) standing retrospective inspection lets the self-organising team adapt and embed the practice on its own terms, which is what sustains it after the coaching ends. (C) katas build the cycle but in an artificial setting that may not transfer to the team's real, messier code. (D) reference examples are a helpful static aid yet passive, and reading is not doing. (E) a targeted trainer session adds knowledge for the hard cases but is still a one-off event without the ongoing practice and ownership that make adoption durable. Practice on real work plus team-owned adaptation is what converts training into lasting practice.
