# Question

Three weeks after a customer-service team completed training on a new CRM, you want to judge whether the training was actually effective rather than merely delivered. Which of the following give meaningful evidence of effectiveness? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Observed on-the-job performance showing agents completing real CRM tasks unaided to the required standard.
- **B**: Attendance records confirming every agent sat the full training session.
- **C**: A measurable drop in CRM-related support tickets and supervisor escalations since go-live.
- **D**: End-of-course satisfaction scores rating the trainer and materials highly.
- **E**: The number of training hours delivered against the hours budgeted in the plan.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

Effectiveness is about whether the trained behaviour now happens correctly in the work, so (A) on-the-job performance and (C) a measurable change in real outcomes (fewer CRM tickets and escalations) both evidence it. (B) attendance and (E) hours delivered measure activity, not result — people can attend and still be unable to do the job. (D) satisfaction (a Kirkpatrick Level 1 reaction) reflects how the course felt, not whether it changed performance; well-liked training can still fail to transfer.
