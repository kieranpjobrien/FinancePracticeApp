# Question

Your offshore development partner rotates people between your project and others on roughly a quarterly cycle. Each cohort receives onboarding training, but after six months the team still seems to restart from scratch every rotation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Demand that the partner freeze the team and stop all rotations for the remaining twelve months of the project, regardless of their internal staffing model.
- **B**: Work with the partner on retention levers: a stable core of longer-tenured members, documented knowledge assets, and buddy pairing that preserves context across each rotation.
- **C**: Accept the model as a feature of offshore engagement and raise prices in contingency to cover the recurring ramp-up cost each quarter.
- **D**: Replace the offshore partner with another vendor who will commit to zero rotation on your account, even if the new contract is materially more expensive.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI favours working within the partner's operating model while shaping it to protect delivery and knowledge continuity. (B) combines a stable core, asset-based retention, and pairing to keep context across rotations. (A) dictates a change the partner is unlikely to accept and damages the relationship. (C) normalises avoidable waste by pricing it in rather than fixing it. (D) is a disproportionate response that incurs full switching cost when relationship-level levers have not yet been tried.
