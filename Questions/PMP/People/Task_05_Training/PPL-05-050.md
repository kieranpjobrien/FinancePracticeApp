# Question

Your medical device project requires all engineers working on validated software to complete documented GxP training before touching any validated code. Two new engineers joined this week and the sprint backlog has validated stories. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Let the new engineers start on the validated stories now while flagging the GxP training as an action for the next available training slot in the team calendar.
- **B**: Pair the new engineers with certified engineers for the sprint so that work can continue while formal GxP training is arranged in parallel with their regular sprint tasks.
- **C**: Hold the new engineers off validated work, allocate them non-validated backlog items, and book the mandatory GxP training before any validated assignment begins on the project.
- **D**: Ask the quality lead to grant a temporary exception so the team can hit the sprint goal and document the training gap as a minor quality deviation for later closure.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI is explicit that compliance is non-negotiable and regulated training must precede regulated work, regardless of backlog pressure. (C) keeps the engineers productive on non-validated items until the mandatory training is complete. (A) is a direct compliance breach. (B) produces work on validated code from untrained engineers, which invalidates the output regardless of pairing. (D) normalises compliance deviations and risks the device submission and patient safety.
