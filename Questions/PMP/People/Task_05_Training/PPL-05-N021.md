# Question

On a predictive infrastructure upgrade, you have already mapped the work packages against your engineers' skills and confirmed that only one of six engineers can configure the network platform that three late work packages depend on. Those packages are critical-path and start in eight weeks. The vendor's certification course runs once, in week six, and takes four engineers per cohort. Your one capable engineer is fully committed to earlier critical-path work until week seven. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Have your experienced engineer prepare and run internal hands-on sessions now, fitting them around their committed work so colleagues build the skill before week eight.
- **B**: Ask the resource manager to second an already-certified engineer from another project to cover the critical work packages while your team is brought up to speed.
- **C**: Send four engineers to the week-six vendor course so a trained pool is certified and available when the critical work packages begin.
- **D**: Re-sequence the schedule so the platform-dependent work packages start later, buying time to train the team without external help.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The gap is already quantified, so the decision now turns on which option reliably has capable people in place when critical-path work starts. (C) is best: the certification course is the one externally available route, its week-six timing lands just before the week-eight need, and a four-engineer cohort gives both capacity and cover for absence — it directly resolves the constraint within the window. (A) relies on your sole expert who is committed to earlier critical-path work until week seven; squeezing in teaching threatens that earlier work and leaves little runway. (B) a secondment helps short term but builds no lasting capability and depends on another project releasing scarce talent. (D) moving critical-path packages out pushes the end date and should follow only if developing the team in time proves impossible. Using the available course that fits the timeline best secures the skill when it is needed.
