# Question

A project will use a new container orchestration platform that most of the team has not worked with. The timeline does not allow weeks of off-the-job training. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Run a one-day jump-start workshop for the whole team and let people learn the rest on the job.
- **B**: Replace half the team with external engineers who already know the orchestration platform in depth.
- **C**: Identify the skills required for early sprints, give targeted training, and pair experienced staff with learners.
- **D**: Postpone the project for two weeks while the entire team completes the vendor's full certification course.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The most effective approach is targeted training scoped to what is actually needed soon, paired with peer learning during real work, which is what (C) does. (A) under-invests at the start and leads to expensive rework. (B) sacrifices team continuity and assumes external talent is readily available. (D) over-invests in upfront comprehensive training when only a subset of skills is needed for the first sprints.
