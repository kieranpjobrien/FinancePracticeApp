# Question

You set up a weekly community-of-practice for a new front-end framework six months ago. Attendance has fallen from eighteen to three, and the sessions have become show-and-tell for the same two enthusiasts. The skill is still broadly needed. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the sessions, interview non-attendees about what would make them valuable, and restart with a format, cadence, and content that matches current team needs.
- **B**: Enforce mandatory attendance for everyone in the team to restore the original eighteen-person audience and maintain knowledge-sharing discipline.
- **C**: Let the community wind down naturally and accept that self-directed learning has replaced the need for any structured knowledge-sharing mechanism.
- **D**: Replace the community with an individual paid external subscription for every team member and discontinue internal sharing on the framework entirely.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's empirical approach is to inspect-and-adapt a capability practice when its outcomes fall short, starting with the people it is meant to serve. (A) diagnoses the decline and redesigns with the team. (B) forces attendance without fixing the fit, producing passive compliance. (C) lets a live capability gap drift on the hope that self-direction will suffice. (D) replaces collective sensemaking with individual consumption, which rarely builds shared practice.
