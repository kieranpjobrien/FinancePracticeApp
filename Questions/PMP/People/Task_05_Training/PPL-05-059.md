# Question

Your operations-handover plan includes shadowing the ops team for four hours a week across three months. The operations manager has pushed back, saying her team cannot spare the time and wants a one-off three-day intensive at go-live instead. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the operations manager's proposal for a three-day intensive at go-live and cancel the shadowing sessions to avoid friction with operations.
- **B**: Override the operations manager by escalating to her director and insisting the twelve-week shadowing plan be honoured exactly as originally scoped.
- **C**: Work with the operations manager to explain why distributed shadowing produces better post-go-live outcomes, then tailor the plan to fit her capacity constraint.
- **D**: Replace shadowing with written runbooks that the ops team reads on their own time, and rely on those documents to cover the operational knowledge transfer.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI rewards negotiated tailoring that preserves the learning outcome while respecting a legitimate stakeholder constraint. (C) explains the rationale and co-designs a workable plan. (A) abandons a known-better practice without attempting to preserve it. (B) escalates past the person who owns the resource and damages the handover relationship. (D) swaps an effective multi-modal method for documentation alone, which rarely produces operational readiness.
