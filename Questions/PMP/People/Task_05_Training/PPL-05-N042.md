# Question

Your Scrum team of six delivers a payments platform. Only one developer understands the fraud-scoring service, and whenever a fraud-related story is in the sprint the team stalls if that person is on leave or pulled into support. The team is otherwise healthy, the Product Owner is happy with throughput, and fraud stories make up a steady but minor part of the backlog. You want to reduce the team's exposure to this single point of knowledge. What is the best way to do it?

## Options

- **A**: Pair other developers with the specialist on the actual fraud-scoring stories as they come up, so the knowledge spreads through doing the real work.
- **B**: Have the specialist run a series of structured workshops walking the team through the fraud-scoring service end to end.
- **C**: Have the specialist write thorough documentation and a runbook for the fraud-scoring service so anyone can pick up a related story.
- **D**: Ask the Product Owner to cluster fraud-scoring stories so the specialist can clear them efficiently while the team focuses elsewhere.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Working competence in a service like fraud-scoring is largely tacit and transfers fastest by doing the real work alongside the person who holds it, so pairing on the actual stories (A) builds genuine capability across the team rather than just awareness of it. (B) workshops convey structure and vocabulary but stepping through the service in a session rarely produces the confidence to deliver a fraud story unaided; it is a useful supplement, not the mechanism that closes the gap. (C) documentation captures explicit knowledge and is worth having, yet a runbook seldom carries the judgement needed to work unfamiliar fraud logic, and the dependency persists in practice. (D) clustering the stories makes the specialist more efficient but deliberately keeps the knowledge with one person, leaving the exposure exactly where it started. Spreading the skill through real work is what removes the single point of knowledge.
