# Question

An offshore oil-and-gas tie-in project relies on a specialist subcontractor to perform a hazardous hot-tapping operation. At the pre-job meeting the subcontractor's supervisor assures you their crew is "fully trained and experienced," and the crew is mobilising to the platform. Your project carries ultimate accountability for safety on the worksite. The operation is days away. What should you do before it proceeds?

## Options

- **A**: Obtain and check documented evidence that each named crew member holds the specific current certifications and competencies the hot-tapping operation requires.
- **B**: Accept the supervisor's assurance, since crew competency sits squarely within the subcontractor's scope and they are the specialists in this work.
- **C**: Have the subcontractor warrant the crew's competence in writing, with an indemnity, so accountability for any competency shortfall rests with them.
- **D**: Ask your own project safety team to observe the crew during the operation and stop it if anyone appears to lack the necessary competence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Where your project holds ultimate safety accountability for a hazardous, regulated operation, ensuring people are adequately trained extends to the subcontractor's crew, and assurance comes from checking documented evidence that each named individual holds the specific current certifications the work requires (A) — verified before the job, while there is still time to act on a gap. (B) accepting a verbal assurance leans on the contractor's specialism but leaves you unable to demonstrate competence if challenged and carrying the consequences if the claim is wrong. (C) a written warranty and indemnity may shift commercial liability, but it does not establish that the crew is actually qualified, and on a safety-critical task transferring blame after a failure is no substitute for preventing one. (D) observing during the operation is a reasonable additional control but comes too late as the primary check: a competency gap discovered mid hot-tap is a gap that has already put the platform at risk. Competency assurance on safety-critical contracted work means verifying records up front, not accepting claims, indemnifying against failure, or catching problems live.
