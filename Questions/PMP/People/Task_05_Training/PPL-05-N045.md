# Question

You are conducting a training needs assessment for the commissioning phase of a new pharmaceutical manufacturing line. The phase brings in operators, quality staff, and maintenance technicians, several of whom are new to this product and to the validated processes the regulator requires. Which inputs and considerations genuinely improve the quality of the needs assessment? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The competency requirements defined for each role in the commissioning and operations work, against which current capability can be compared.
- **B**: The training plan used to commission the company's previous line, adopted as the baseline so a proven programme is not redesigned from scratch.
- **C**: The regulatory and validation requirements that mandate specific qualified competencies for tasks such as aseptic operations.
- **D**: The schedule of when each person is assigned to competency-dependent work, so training is timed to precede it.
- **E**: A count of how many assigned staff already hold a general pharmaceutical-manufacturing certificate, used as the measure of who needs training.

## Answer

A, C, D

## Explanation

A sound needs assessment is built from what this work requires, what mandates apply, and when competence must be in place. (A) supplies the role-by-role competency baseline that lets you find the real gaps for this team and this line. (C) captures the regulatory and validation requirements that make certain competencies non-negotiable in a pharmaceutical setting and must shape the plan. (D) ties training timing to assignment so people are qualified before doing competency-dependent work, which is essential where unqualified operation is a compliance breach. (B) is tempting as reuse, but a prior line's plan reflects that line's equipment, processes, and people; adopting it as the baseline imports assumptions instead of assessing the gap in front of you. (E) a general certificate is a poor proxy for role-specific competence on this validated line, so counting it as the measure of need will both clear people who still have gaps and overlook task-specific requirements.
