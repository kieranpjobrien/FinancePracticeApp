# Question

Your agile team has been delivering a new merchandising platform for a retailer in fortnightly increments. The business-side Product Owner is enthusiastic but repeatedly writes acceptance criteria that conflict with what the platform can do, and the store managers who will use it keep raising questions at the review that show they do not understand the new ordering workflow. Delivery velocity is good but adoption is at risk. What should you focus on?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the Product Owner on writing fit-for-platform criteria and bring the store managers up to speed on the new workflow, developing both so they can play their parts.
- **B**: Concentrate on the Product Owner, since better acceptance criteria are the root of the rework, and let the store managers learn the workflow at the planned go-live training.
- **C**: Get the store managers hands-on with the increments between reviews so they build workflow understanding by using it, and address the criteria issue as it arises in refinement.
- **D**: Bring in a business analyst to translate between the platform's capabilities and the business, writing the acceptance criteria and fielding the store managers' questions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ensuring people are adequately trained extends to stakeholders, not only the delivery team, and here two groups lack what they need: the Product Owner cannot yet write feasible criteria and the store managers do not understand the workflow they will adopt. Developing both (A) closes the actual gap and protects adoption, and on iterative delivery that capability has to grow throughout, not at the end. (B) fixes the more visible problem but parks the store managers until a single pre-cutover event, which leaves adoption at risk and treats training as a one-off when the increments are landing now. (C) usefully builds the managers' understanding through real use, but pairing it with only ad-hoc handling of the criteria leaves the Product Owner's gap to chance rather than deliberately coaching it. (D) a business analyst can bridge in the short term, but doing the Product Owner's job for them entrenches the dependency and develops no one, when the role exists to hold that capability. Building stakeholder competence directly, across both groups, is the durable response.
