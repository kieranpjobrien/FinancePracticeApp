# Question

You are preparing the training plan for a project rolling out a new enterprise procurement system to finance, warehousing, and supplier-facing staff. You have gathered several pieces of information. Which TWO should most directly determine the content and targeting of the training each group receives? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: A breakdown of the specific tasks each role group will perform in the new system, validated with their team leads.
- **B**: Each group's current proficiency with the processes and tools the new system replaces or changes.
- **C**: The change-impact assessment showing which existing workarounds and offline steps the new system eliminates.
- **D**: The go-live date and the cut-over schedule for each department.
- **E**: The system's full functional specification listing every feature the platform offers.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Targeted training is driven by the gap between what each group must be able to do in the new system and what they can already do, so the two inputs that define that gap should shape content and targeting most directly. (A) the role-specific task breakdown defines what each group must learn to do. (B) their current proficiency sets how much and what depth of training closes the distance to that requirement. (C) the change-impact assessment is genuinely useful context for messaging and adoption, but it describes what is being removed rather than the competencies staff must acquire, so it informs more than it targets. (D) the go-live and cut-over schedule drives the timing and sequencing of training, not its content. (E) the full functional specification covers everything the platform can do, far beyond what any role needs, so training built from it would be unfocused. The task requirements and the current-proficiency baseline are what pinpoint the right content for each group.
