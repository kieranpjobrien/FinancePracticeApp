# Question

Midway through a three-year construction programme, the safety officer reports that working-at-height certificates for six crew members have quietly expired and that two are scheduled to do elevated work next week. Recertification takes a full day each. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the two scheduled workers complete next week's task under supervision while the full recertification cohort is booked for the following month.
- **B**: Ask the safety officer to extend the expired certificates informally for two weeks so the schedule can hold and training can be arranged without disruption.
- **C**: Remove all six from elevated work immediately, rebook recertification as soon as providers have slots, and update the schedule to reflect the capacity reduction.
- **D**: Request that the crew self-certify based on prior experience since the previously issued training is essentially the same content being re-delivered.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats safety compliance as absolute and expects visible schedule impact rather than hidden workarounds. (C) removes the uncertified workers from the regulated activity and makes the cost of the lapse transparent. (A) knowingly allows uncertified workers to perform regulated high-risk work. (B) informally extending certificates is a compliance breach that also exposes the safety officer. (D) self-certification is not recognised, the prior credentials are expired, and this bypasses the compliance regime entirely.
