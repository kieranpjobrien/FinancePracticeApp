# Question

Facing a budget squeeze, the sponsor has instructed you to cut all remaining end-user training from the project scope. Training was a core part of the benefits realisation plan and the business-change manager is alarmed. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly comply with the sponsor's instruction to avoid conflict, and pass the risk and cost of untrained end users on to the receiving business units.
- **B**: Refuse to cut training and continue spending as planned, informing the sponsor that the decision would jeopardise the benefits case the project is signed up to.
- **C**: Run training outside the formal project budget by asking functional managers to carry the cost, so the sponsor's instruction is met on paper only.
- **D**: Present the benefits impact with the change manager, explore lower-cost training options, and route the decision through change control for the sponsor to confirm or amend.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI requires scope changes with material benefit impact to go through integrated change control with transparent data, not silent compliance, refusal, or off-book workarounds. (D) gives the sponsor informed choice and preserves governance. (A) hides the benefits risk and breaches PM accountability. (B) refuses a sponsor direction without first raising the impact formally. (C) is off-book accounting that undermines transparency and the governance record.
