# Question

A two-day vendor training has just finished. Feedback scores are mediocre and several attendees say the trainer was unprepared and skipped planned labs. The vendor has invoiced the full contracted amount. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the invoice to preserve the vendor relationship, share the feedback informally, and book a different trainer for any future sessions the project may need.
- **B**: Refuse to pay the invoice entirely, cite the poor feedback as cause, and ask procurement to terminate the vendor's contract with immediate effect.
- **C**: Consolidate the feedback, compare delivery against the contracted scope, raise a formal quality issue with the vendor, and propose remediation such as re-delivery or a credit.
- **D**: Absorb the weak session outcomes, schedule internal catch-up workshops on the skipped lab content, and treat the vendor invoice as a sunk project cost.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats vendor underperformance as a quality and contract management issue that starts with structured feedback and proposed remediation, not silent acceptance or unilateral payment refusal. (C) is the correct first step. (A) quietly validates poor delivery and forgoes recoverable value. (B) jumps past remediation to termination without following procurement process. (D) absorbs avoidable cost and skips the chance to recover value from the existing commitment.
