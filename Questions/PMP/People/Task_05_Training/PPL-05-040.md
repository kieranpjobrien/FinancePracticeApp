# Question

The PMO is rolling out a new project management tool to replace a long-standing spreadsheet-based approach. About a third of your team is openly resistant and prefers the old way of working. What training approach is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Provide hands-on training that explains why the change is happening, addresses specific concerns, and includes coaching support during the transition.
- **B**: Make training optional so only the willing attend, and let the resistant group keep using the spreadsheets in parallel.
- **C**: Mandate training and inform the resistant group that non-adoption will be reflected in their performance reviews.
- **D**: Skip formal training and rely on the enthusiastic adopters to pull the rest of the team along.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI ties training to change adoption by addressing both capability and willingness, especially when resistance is rooted in valid concerns about the old approach. (A) builds skill, surfaces and addresses objections, and supports the transition. (B) creates a fragmented team and undermines the rollout. (C) compels compliance without addressing the cause and damages trust. (D) leaves change management to peer pressure with no formal support.
