# Question

Three sprints in, your team keeps failing its definition of done because every story now needs accessibility compliance the team does not know how to build or verify. Accessibility is a firm requirement for the product. There is a release in six weeks, and the product owner is anxious about throughput. As the Scrum Master, what is the best course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Engage an accessibility specialist to work inside the team for the coming sprints, implementing and verifying compliance while deliberately teaching the team as they go.
- **B**: Keep the definition of done intact and arrange expert pairing or focused training so the team builds the accessibility skill into its everyday work.
- **C**: Keep accessibility in the definition of done but track the unmet items as explicit technical debt to be resolved before the release, so flow continues now.
- **D**: Negotiate with the product owner to thin the accessibility criteria in the definition of done to the legally essential minimum until the team is up to speed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root problem is a team capability gap against a real quality bar, so the most durable response develops the team while preserving "done." (B) is best: building the skill through pairing or training lets the team meet the definition of done itself, every sprint, which is the only option that closes the gap permanently and keeps quality intact. (A) an embedded specialist who also teaches is a close and reasonable choice and may accelerate (B), but if framed as the specialist doing the compliance, ownership and capability stay outside the team and dependency lingers. (C) tracking unmet accessibility as debt keeps flow moving but ships increments that are not truly done and bets the release on clearing a debt the team still cannot clear. (D) relaxing the criteria, even to a minimum, lowers the quality bar and erodes the meaning of done rather than addressing the missing skill. Developing the team to meet the standard is the response that protects both quality and ownership.
