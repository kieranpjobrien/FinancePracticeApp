# Question

A senior backend developer joins your project in the middle of a delivery push to replace someone who left unexpectedly. The team is at capacity finishing a release. What should the project manager do FIRST to bring the new member up to speed?

## Options

- **A**: Ask them to read the project documentation and start picking up tickets once they feel ready to contribute.
- **B**: Pause the rest of the team's sprint commitments to deliver a comprehensive onboarding programme for the new hire.
- **C**: Schedule full classroom training on the project architecture before they touch any production code at all.
- **D**: Pair them with a buddy, give a prioritised onboarding checklist, and start them on small ring-fenced work.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's approach to integrating new team members balances ramp-up with team capacity, using buddying and progressive responsibility. (D) gets the person productive quickly without derailing the release. (A) leaves them isolated and slows time-to-contribution. (B) over-corrects by pausing the release, which is rarely justified. (C) front-loads formal training that is less effective than blended on-the-job learning at this stage.
