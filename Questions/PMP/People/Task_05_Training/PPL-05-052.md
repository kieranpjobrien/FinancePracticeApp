# Question

The sponsor has asked how you will know whether a $200K change-management training investment actually worked. Previous projects have reported only course-completion rates, which the sponsor considers weak evidence. What measurement approach should the project manager propose?

## Options

- **A**: Continue reporting completion rates since they are the most reliable and objective measure available at a reasonable tracking cost.
- **B**: Survey learner satisfaction immediately after each session because high satisfaction is a strong leading indicator of business outcomes later on.
- **C**: Measure only final business metrics at project end, since interim measures are too noisy to tell the sponsor anything meaningful or actionable.
- **D**: Track learner reaction, knowledge acquisition, on-the-job behaviour change, and business results, and agree which combination the sponsor will rely on for decisions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI aligns with Kirkpatrick's four-level model: reaction, learning, behaviour, and results, with business value as the ultimate measure. (D) offers the full view and negotiates which levels inform sponsor decisions. (A) uses completion as a proxy for learning and is exactly what the sponsor has already rejected. (B) stops at reaction, which correlates poorly with behaviour change. (C) dismisses interim indicators that are useful for timely correction and explicitly needed for this sponsor's question.
