# Question

A project manager is launching a hybrid logistics-optimisation project with a team drawn from three departments who have not worked together. Some members will be in the office and others fully remote. The project manager wants to reduce avoidable friction over meetings, response times, and how decisions get made. When is the most appropriate time to establish the team's ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: Early, during team formation at the start of the project, before significant work begins.
- **B**: After the first month, once the team has experienced enough friction to know which rules are needed.
- **C**: At the first major milestone review, alongside the lessons-learned discussion.
- **D**: Only if and when a specific conflict arises that the team cannot resolve on its own.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ground rules are most valuable when set during team formation, as they shape behaviour before unhelpful habits form and give a newly assembled, mixed in-office and remote team a shared basis for working together from day one. (A) reflects this. (B) lets avoidable friction accumulate first, which is the very outcome the project manager is trying to prevent. (C) delays the agreements until well into delivery, by which time misunderstandings will already have cost time and goodwill. (D) treats ground rules as a conflict-resolution tool rather than a preventive one, leaving the team without shared expectations for routine collaboration.
