# Question

When the team co-created their ground rules, two members were noticeably quiet and the final wording heavily reflects the views of the most vocal half of the team. Those two members have since said privately they do not feel the rules represent them. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a second session focused specifically on surfacing quieter voices, then revise the rules to reflect the full team's views fairly.
- **B**: Encourage the two members to raise their specific concerns in the next retrospective so their views are heard by the whole team in context.
- **C**: Accept that the rules reflect the team's majority view and support the two members informally outside the formal ground-rules framework.
- **D**: Add the two members' private concerns to the rules as additional clauses without reopening the original session for debate or discussion.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats participation as a design issue — if the first process missed voices, the PMI-aligned move is to redesign the process (smaller groups, written input, anonymous collection) and revise the product. (B) pushes the burden of speaking up onto those the first format already silenced. (C) accepts a flawed artefact as representing the team when it does not. (D) changes the document but does not repair the trust or the process.
