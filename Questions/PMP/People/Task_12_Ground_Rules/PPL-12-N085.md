# Question

You are facilitating a manufacturing-systems team as it drafts its working agreement. Members propose several candidate rules. You want to keep only those that will function as effective ground rules — describing an observable behaviour the team can hold itself to without depending on someone outside the team or on a state no one can see. Which proposed rules should be kept as-is rather than reworded or reassigned? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: "We start every scheduled meeting within two minutes of the agreed time, regardless of who is present."
- **B**: "Our line manager will follow up with anyone who misses two stand-ups in a row."
- **C**: "Any team member may request a five-minute timeout when a discussion becomes heated."
- **D**: "We stay fully focused and avoid distractions during pairing sessions."
- **E**: "We respond to one another in a timely manner."

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

An effective ground rule names a behaviour the team itself can observe and uphold; the test is whether you could tell from watching the team whether it is being followed and whether the team — not an outsider — owns it. (A) and (C) pass: starting within a stated tolerance and any member invoking a defined timeout are both visible, team-enforced actions. (B) describes a real, observable behaviour but hands enforcement to the line manager, so it is not something the team governs itself — it belongs in an escalation path, not the team's own agreement. (D) sounds concrete but "fully focused" and "distractions" are internal states no one can reliably observe in another person, so the rule cannot be applied consistently. (E) is the classic vague norm: "timely" has no shared meaning and each member will read it differently. Only A and C are observable, team-owned behaviours that need no rewording.
