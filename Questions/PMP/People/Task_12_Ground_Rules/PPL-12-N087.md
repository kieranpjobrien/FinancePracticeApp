# Question

A co-located and remote agile team building an e-learning platform has just finished co-creating its working agreement during a facilitated session. The Scrum Master wants the agreement to actually influence day-to-day behaviour rather than be forgotten. What is the most effective way to handle the finished agreement?

## Options

- **A**: Make it visible and easily accessible to the whole team, for example posted in the shared digital workspace where it can be referenced and revisited.
- **B**: File it with the project documentation so there is a permanent, controlled record of what was agreed.
- **C**: Email it to each member individually so everyone has a personal copy for reference.
- **D**: Keep it with the Scrum Master, who will remind the team of the relevant rule whenever a situation calls for it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A working agreement influences behaviour only when the team can see and refer to it regularly, so the priority is high visibility for everyone, including remote members. (A) keeps the agreement in a shared, accessible place where it stays present in the team's daily work and can be revisited. (B) preserves a record but tucks it out of everyday sight, where it is easily forgotten. (C) scatters copies that quickly become buried in inboxes and out of mind. (D) makes one person the gatekeeper of the agreement, undermining the shared ownership and constant visibility that give a working agreement its force.
