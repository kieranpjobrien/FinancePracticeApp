# Question

A team member has repeatedly broken a ground rule about merging code without peer review. Two peer reminders and one 1:1 from the PM have produced short-term improvement followed by reversion. The behaviour is now affecting quality and team trust. What should the PM do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a further gentle 1:1 focused on why the behaviour keeps returning, and give the member another chance to bring their practice into line.
- **B**: Raise the ongoing issue at the next retrospective so the team collectively confronts the member about the pattern and its effect on their trust.
- **C**: Document the pattern carefully, escalate appropriately through the member's functional manager, and then follow the organisation's standard performance-management process through to outcome.
- **D**: Quietly assign a senior peer to review all of that member's code changes so the ground rule is de facto met without further confrontation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's progressive-discipline approach says once peer and PM interventions have failed, the PMI-aligned move is to document and escalate through the proper channel — this is fair to the individual and the team. (A) repeats an intervention that has already been tried and failed. (B) weaponises a retrospective against one person. (D) hides the problem and quietly burdens another team member to cover it.
