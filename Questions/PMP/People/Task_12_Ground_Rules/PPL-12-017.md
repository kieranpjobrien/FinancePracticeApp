# Question

The team agreed to respond to direct messages within four working hours, but one member is consistently slower, and others have begun complaining. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the response-time issue at the next stand-up so the team can address it together.
- **B**: Have a private conversation with the team member to understand what is driving the lag.
- **C**: Soften the rule because it appears to be unrealistic for the team's workload.
- **D**: Document the pattern and include it in the team member's performance feedback.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A first step in handling a ground-rule breach is to understand it, not to escalate or generalise it. (B) preserves dignity while exploring legitimate causes (workload, tool issues, ambiguity about the rule). (A) airs a personal issue in front of the group before the project manager has the facts. (C) abandons a valid rule without diagnosing the actual problem. (D) is a delayed, formal response that does nothing to fix the immediate frustration.
