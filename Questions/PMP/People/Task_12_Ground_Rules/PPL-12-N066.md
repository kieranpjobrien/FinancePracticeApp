# Question

Six months into a predictive aerospace project, a new engineer joins a team with a mature, well-functioning team charter. Within two weeks she has unknowingly breached two charter norms — copying the whole team on minor updates and pushing changes without the agreed buddy check. The other members are mildly irritated but say nothing to her directly. How should the project manager handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Walk the new engineer through the team charter, explaining each norm and the reasoning the team had for it.
- **B**: Raise the breaches at the next team meeting so the whole team can re-baseline its understanding of the charter together.
- **C**: Ask one of the existing members to mentor the new engineer informally on how the team works.
- **D**: Update the charter onboarding checklist so future joiners are briefed, and let this engineer learn the norms by observation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root cause is simple: the new member was never inducted into the existing agreements, so she cannot follow norms she does not know. The project manager's direct, immediate move is to orient her to the charter and the intent behind each rule. (A) does this and resolves the friction at source. (C) is a reasonable complement and pairing her with a mentor can reinforce the norms, but delegating the basic charter induction risks an incomplete or inconsistent hand-off when a clear walkthrough is needed now. (B) turns one person's onboarding gap into a full-team agenda item, which is disproportionate and risks embarrassing her. (D) fixes the process for the future but leaves this engineer to keep breaching norms by trial and error, prolonging the very friction that needs addressing.
