# Question

The team has a ground rule that meetings end at the scheduled time, but most meetings now run over by 10-15 minutes because the discussions are productive. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: End every meeting precisely on time and capture overflow items in a follow-up note.
- **B**: Drop the rule, since the overruns are creating value rather than waste.
- **C**: Bring it to the team to decide whether to lengthen meetings or improve in-meeting facilitation.
- **D**: Schedule a 15-minute buffer after every meeting so overruns no longer disrupt anyone.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When a rule consistently chafes, the team should adjust either the rule or the practice rather than tolerate the friction. (C) returns the choice to the team. (A) is rigid and ignores that the overruns are valuable. (B) drops a useful norm without considering whether the meetings are simply mis-scoped. (D) is a workaround that hides the underlying issue and consumes more calendar time.
