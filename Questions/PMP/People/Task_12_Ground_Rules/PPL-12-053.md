# Question

A PM is defining ground rules for a new global team with members from four countries. In the co-design session, proposals around directness of feedback produce visible discomfort — some members view direct challenge as healthy, others see it as disrespectful. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the most direct standard any team member has proposed so the team can move quickly and candidly on genuinely difficult issues in the future.
- **B**: Adopt the softest standard any team member has proposed so no one feels culturally uncomfortable with the way feedback lands within the team.
- **C**: Defer the feedback ground rule and let individual pairs work out their own feedback styles as working relationships develop during delivery over time.
- **D**: Facilitate the team through the tension openly, surface what each style is trying to protect, and co-create a shared standard that works for everyone.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's cross-cultural and team-development principles say to name the tension and co-create a shared standard — this produces a rule the team owns and that honours what each style values. (A) privileges directness and alienates members who experience it as disrespect. (B) produces vague feedback the team cannot actually learn from. (C) postpones a foundational norm and lets bilateral friction build.
