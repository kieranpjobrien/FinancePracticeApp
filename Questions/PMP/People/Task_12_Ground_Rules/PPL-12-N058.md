# Question

While facilitating a team's ground-rules workshop, you encounter several situations. For each situation (left), match the facilitation response (right) that best preserves team ownership of the agreements.

## Items

- **1**: One dominant member is dictating most of the proposed rules while quieter members stay silent.
- **2**: The team has produced thirty highly detailed rules and is losing energy.
- **3**: Two members fundamentally disagree on a proposed rule and the group is stuck.

## Matches

- **A**: Introduce a round-robin so every member contributes before any rule is finalised.
- **B**: Help the team consolidate to a short list of the agreements they most value.
- **C**: Surface the underlying interests behind each position and seek an option both can accept.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C

## Explanation

For the dominant voice, a round-robin (A) rebalances participation so the rules reflect the whole team, not one person. For rule overload, consolidating to a valued short list (B) restores focus and makes the agreements usable. For the deadlock, exploring underlying interests to find a mutually acceptable option (C) is the collaborative conflict approach that keeps both members invested. Each pairing protects shared ownership rather than letting the PM impose a fix.
