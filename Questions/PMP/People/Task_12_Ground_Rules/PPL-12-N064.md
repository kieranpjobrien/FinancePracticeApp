# Question

A hybrid programme spans an onshore product team and an offshore engineering team in a culture where openly disagreeing with a senior colleague is uncomfortable. The team's working agreement says "anyone can challenge any idea in the moment." In practice, offshore engineers stay silent in joint meetings but later raise serious concerns one-to-one with the project manager, causing rework. The project manager wants the agreement to work for everyone. What should she do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reaffirm the existing agreement and explicitly invite the offshore engineers by name to voice concerns in the joint meetings.
- **B**: Bring the gap to the whole team and facilitate a revision of how dissent is raised, so the agreement reflects channels that work across both cultures.
- **C**: Add an asynchronous written channel where concerns can be logged before and after meetings, alongside the live challenge norm.
- **D**: Coach the offshore leads privately to model challenging ideas in meetings so their team gains confidence to follow.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The agreement encodes one culturally specific style of dissent (live, public challenge) and is failing because it does not fit half the team; the durable fix is to revise the norm together so it legitimises ways of surfacing concerns that work for everyone. (B) re-establishes shared ownership and is the necessary first step before bolting on mechanisms. (C) is very likely part of the eventual answer and an excellent practice, but adopting a specific channel before the team agrees the principle imposes the project manager's solution and skips the joint sense-making that makes any new norm stick. (A) names individuals to perform a behaviour their cultural context discourages, which can increase discomfort and is unlikely to change the pattern. (D) helps over time but relies on slow behaviour change and still treats public challenge as the only valid form, leaving the immediate rework risk unaddressed.
