# Question

A team ground rule requires "giving each other honest feedback." In practice, feedback given is vague, arrives late, and rarely leads to behaviour change. The PM wants the rule to actually function. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the feedback rule from the team's ground rules since it is not working and formally tracking feedback would add unwanted bureaucracy the team does not actually want.
- **B**: Replace the vague rule with strong sanctions for members who fail to give timely feedback, making accountability explicit and consistently enforced across the whole team every sprint.
- **C**: Raise the pattern in the next retrospective and ask team members individually to commit to giving more feedback in the sprint ahead than they did the last one.
- **D**: Work with the team to translate the rule into specific, observable practice — who, when, how, and worked examples of what good feedback looks like in context.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI-aligned practice is to make norms specific and observable — turning "honest feedback" into concrete practice (pairing format, cadence, examples) is what actually changes behaviour. (A) loses a valuable rule rather than fixing how it is expressed. (B) tries to fix a vagueness problem with a punishment problem. (C) exhorts harder against the same vague standard.
