# Question

The team operates under a "disagree and commit" ground rule. After a decision was made about a technical approach, one team member continues to lobby other members against it in side conversations. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reopen the decision in the next team meeting since dissent persists.
- **B**: Speak to the team member privately about the impact of their behaviour on team commitment.
- **C**: Raise the behaviour in the next stand-up so the whole team sees the rule being enforced.
- **D**: Ask the team's senior engineer to talk to the dissenter informally.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ground rules are upheld by consistent, respectful enforcement. (B) addresses the behaviour at its source, in private, preserving the team member's dignity while reinforcing their commitment to the rule they agreed to. (A) rewards continued lobbying and undermines the "commit" element of the agreement. (C) creates public conflict unnecessarily and may damage trust. (D) delegates a conversation the project manager should hold and risks a confused message.
