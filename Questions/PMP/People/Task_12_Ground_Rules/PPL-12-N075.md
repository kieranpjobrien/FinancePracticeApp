# Question

A delivery team is organising several statements it has been treating loosely as "ground rules." Each statement actually belongs to a distinct artefact or agreement so that it is owned and applied correctly. Match each statement (left) to where it most properly belongs (right).

## Items

- **1**: "A story is not accepted until it is peer-reviewed, unit-tested, and the integration build is green."
- **2**: "We give each other direct feedback in the moment and assume good intent."
- **3**: "Each story must satisfy the specific conditions written on its card before we call it done."

## Matches

- **A**: Team working agreement / ground rules
- **B**: Definition of Done
- **C**: Acceptance criteria

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Item 1 states a uniform completeness bar applied to every story before acceptance — a quality standard that holds across the whole increment, which is the Definition of Done (B). Item 3 looks similar but ties to conditions written for each individual story, which is precisely what acceptance criteria (C) are: story-specific tests of fitness, not a blanket standard. Item 2 is a behavioural norm about how members treat one another, the natural content of a working agreement (A). The discrimination matters because the Definition of Done is general and reused, acceptance criteria are particular to each backlog item, and behavioural norms are owned and enforced differently from either quality artefact.
