# Question

A hybrid software team has co-created a working agreement that includes "we deploy to production on Fridays only after the full regression suite passes." The sponsor, frustrated by what she sees as slow releases, instructs the project manager to drop the Friday restriction and let the team deploy any day. The team values the rule because it concentrates their support coverage. How should the project manager respond to the sponsor?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the sponsor's instruction immediately, since the sponsor has the authority to direct release practices.
- **B**: Decline to change the rule, explaining that working agreements are owned by the team and not subject to sponsor direction.
- **C**: Explain the team's reasoning for the rule and the risk the sponsor's change introduces, then ask the team to reconsider the agreement in light of the sponsor's release-speed concern.
- **D**: Keep the Friday rule but commit to the sponsor that the team will shorten the regression suite to speed up releases.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The sponsor's underlying need is faster releases, and the team's rule reflects a genuine operational reason; the project manager neither simply overrides the team nor stonewalls the sponsor, but brings the legitimate concern back to the team so they can adapt their own agreement. (C) honours both the sponsor's authority over outcomes and the team's ownership of how they work. (A) imposes a change on a team-owned norm without consultation, eroding trust and the rule's support-coverage benefit. (B) is technically protective of team ownership but treats the agreement as untouchable and ignores a valid business concern the team should weigh — agreements adapt to real constraints. (D) makes a unilateral promise to weaken a quality safeguard the team relies on, trading away regression coverage without the team's input and without confirming it actually solves the speed concern.
