# Question

A newly assembled, fully remote agile team spanning four time zones is defining how it will work together. Every option below is a defensible norm, but three most directly pre-empt the dysfunctions that specifically arise from time-zone spread and lack of co-location. Which THREE? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A norm that meeting recordings and written summaries are posted so anyone who could not attend stays informed.
- **B**: Core overlap hours when synchronous collaboration is expected, balanced fairly across the four zones.
- **C**: Expected response times for each communication channel, distinguishing urgent from routine.
- **D**: An agreement that the team uses video-on for its synchronous calls to build rapport across locations.
- **E**: A standard that decisions and their rationale are recorded in a shared place so members who were offline can catch up.

## Answer

B, C, E

## Explanation

(B), (C) and (E) attack the three failure modes most particular to a multi-zone remote team: no fair window for real-time work, messages disappearing into the void with unclear urgency, and decisions being invisible to whoever was asleep when they were made. (A) is closely related and genuinely useful, but it overlaps heavily with (E) and addresses catching up on meetings specifically rather than the broader decision-visibility problem; given a choice of three, the decision-record norm covers more ground. (D) supports rapport and is a sensible agreement for any remote team, yet it does not target a time-zone-driven dysfunction and can even add fatigue across awkward hours, so it is the weakest of the five for this particular goal. The item asks for the three that best fit the stated problem.
