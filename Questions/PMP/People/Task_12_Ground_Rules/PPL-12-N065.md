# Question

A Scrum team's working agreement and its Definition of Done have grown tangled: the working agreement now contains items like "all code is peer-reviewed and unit-tested before a story is accepted," while the Definition of Done contains "we keep our cameras on during planning." Quality slips have started because acceptance criteria are inconsistently applied. As the project manager supporting the team, what guidance is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Help the team move each statement to the artefact that owns it — product completeness criteria into the Definition of Done, behavioural norms into the working agreement.
- **B**: Merge the two documents into a single "team agreements" page so nothing is lost and everything is visible in one place.
- **C**: Focus on rewriting the Definition of Done to be measurable, since that is where the quality slips originate, and leave the working agreement as-is.
- **D**: Ask the Scrum Master to take ownership of both documents and reconcile them before the next sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Definition of Done governs when a product increment is complete and releasable, while the working agreement governs how the team behaves; the quality slips trace directly to completeness criteria sitting in the wrong artefact where they are not enforced at acceptance. (A) restores each item to the artefact that actually applies it. (B) preserves visibility but collapses a meaningful distinction — the two have different purposes, owners, and enforcement points — so the underlying confusion remains. (C) fixes one half but leaves the behavioural items still misfiled and the boundary still blurred. (D) hands a team-owned set of agreements to one person to reconcile, eroding the shared ownership that makes either artefact effective.
