# Question

During a sprint retrospective, the team raises that its ground rule requiring cameras-on for all video meetings is causing fatigue; several members feel it adds little value for routine stand-ups, while a couple still find cameras-on useful for connection during longer workshops. The Scrum Master asks how the team should handle the rule. As the project manager observing, what guidance best supports the team?

## Options

- **A**: Encourage the team to dig into why cameras-on was originally adopted, so any revision preserves the connection benefit the rule was meant to provide.
- **B**: Support the team in revising the rule now to fit the situation — for example, cameras optional for stand-ups, on for workshops — capturing the change as an updated agreement.
- **C**: Suggest the team trial cameras-optional for stand-ups for the next two sprints and review the effect at the following retrospective before changing the agreement for good.
- **D**: Recommend the team keep the rule unchanged for now and instead address the fatigue through shorter or fewer meetings, since the camera norm aids engagement.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ground rules are living agreements and the retrospective is precisely where a team adapts a norm that no longer serves it; the team has already surfaced a workable, nuanced revision that honours both the fatigue concern and the connection benefit. (B) acts on that directly and records the updated agreement, which is the cleanest, most ownership-preserving outcome. (C) is reasonable and the trial-and-review instinct is sound, but the team already has enough signal to make a sensible split now, so a two-sprint trial defers relief from a fatigue everyone agrees on. (A) is a good facilitation prompt and worth a moment, yet on its own it only explores and does not resolve; the team understands the trade-off well enough to decide. (D) keeps the rule and tackles meeting length, which is a legitimate parallel improvement, but it overrides the team's clear feedback that cameras-on specifically is the burden for routine stand-ups.
