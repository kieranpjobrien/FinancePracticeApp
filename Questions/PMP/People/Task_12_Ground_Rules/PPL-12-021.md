# Question

A new engineer joins the team mid-project. The existing members have well-established ground rules that they follow naturally. How should the project manager handle the new member's onboarding to those rules?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the new engineer to observe the team for two weeks before formally introducing the rules.
- **B**: Walk the new engineer through the ground rules and invite them to ask questions or propose changes.
- **C**: Pair the new engineer with a buddy who will model the rules implicitly through behaviour.
- **D**: Hold a brief team meeting where every member explains the rule they care about most.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Explicit onboarding to team norms gives the new member clarity from day one and signals that the rules are open to evolve as the team changes. (B) achieves both. (A) leaves the new engineer to infer norms by trial and error and risks early friction. (C) is useful but insufficient on its own; implicit modelling does not surface the reasoning behind rules. (D) is well-meaning but performative and consumes team time better used for the broader onboarding.
