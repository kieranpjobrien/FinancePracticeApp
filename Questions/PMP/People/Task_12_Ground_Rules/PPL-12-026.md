# Question

A globally distributed team includes members from cultures with very different norms about hierarchy, directness, and conflict. The project manager is preparing the first ground-rules workshop. What approach should they take?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the headquarters location's existing team norms as a baseline so the team starts from a consistent reference.
- **B**: Avoid raising cultural differences in the workshop to keep the conversation focused on practical agreements.
- **C**: Survey each member privately for their preferences and then publish a consolidated rule set.
- **D**: Open the workshop with an explicit conversation about cultural differences and co-create rules that bridge them.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI emphasises cultural awareness on global teams. (D) creates psychological safety, surfaces hidden assumptions, and produces rules everyone can commit to. (A) imposes one culture as the default and silences others. (B) ignores friction that will surface later in worse forms. (C) skips the team conversation that builds shared understanding and produces a top-down artefact instead.
