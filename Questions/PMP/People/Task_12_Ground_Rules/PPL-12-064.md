# Question

The team has a ground rule that peer conflicts should first be addressed directly between the individuals, escalating to the PM only if that has failed. Two team members have come directly to the PM with a dispute without speaking to each other first. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hear the dispute, take a view, and tell both members what the outcome should be so the project can move on from the distraction.
- **B**: Refuse to hear the dispute at all until the two members have clearly attempted to resolve it directly between themselves as the ground rule requires.
- **C**: Acknowledge both members, remind them of the ground rule, and coach them on how to have the direct conversation before any PM involvement becomes appropriate.
- **D**: Treat the direct escalation as a sign the ground rule does not work in practice and drop it, since team members clearly prefer to come straight to the PM.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to coach, not command — redirecting to the agreed rule with support for the conversation respects the rule, the people and the relationship. (A) bypasses the rule and trains the team to escalate faster. (B) is technically consistent with the rule but offers no coaching or support. (D) abandons a valuable rule because it is briefly uncomfortable.
