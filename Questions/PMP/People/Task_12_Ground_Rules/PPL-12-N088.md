# Question

Four months into a hybrid insurance-claims modernisation project, two new analysts join a team that co-created a solid working agreement at kickoff and has followed it well. Within a fortnight the newcomers have broken two norms — posting decisions in a side channel and skipping the agreed pre-read before meetings. Both apologise readily and say they had not realised the team worked that way. What should the project manager do to prevent recurrence?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the two analysts to read the posted working agreement and confirm they will comply, then consider the matter closed.
- **B**: Build a brief walkthrough of the working agreement into the team's onboarding so every new joiner is introduced to the norms and invited to discuss them.
- **C**: Reopen the working agreement for full re-negotiation by the whole team so the newcomers feel equal ownership of every rule.
- **D**: Assign each newcomer an experienced buddy who will correct them informally whenever they deviate from a norm.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The breaches stem from a gap in onboarding — the norms were never conveyed to the new members — so the systemic fix is to make introducing the working agreement a standard part of bringing anyone onto the team. (B) does this and, by inviting discussion, helps newcomers genuinely adopt rather than merely tolerate the rules. (A) addresses these two individuals but institutes no process, so the next joiner hits the same wall. (C) overcorrects: a stable, well-functioning agreement need not be wholly renegotiated for two arrivals, and doing so risks unsettling norms the team values. (D) relies on after-the-fact correction by buddies, which is reactive and inconsistent compared with proactively orienting newcomers to the agreement up front.
