# Question

A live production incident has triggered a project crisis. Some of the team's ground rules, including consensus decision-making and 24-hour review windows, are slowing the response. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Suspend the rules entirely for the duration of the crisis and resume them once the incident is resolved.
- **B**: Continue applying every rule as written and accept the slower response while the crisis plays out.
- **C**: Ask the team to define a temporary crisis protocol that adjusts specific rules but keeps core values.
- **D**: Take unilateral decisions for the duration of the crisis and brief the team after each one is made.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Crises sometimes need different process, but agreed process. (C) preserves trust and clarity by having the team explicitly choose what changes (faster decisions, smaller approval pool) and what stays (respectful communication, transparency). (A) abandons all structure when only some rules are blocking. (B) ignores legitimate urgency. (D) bypasses the team and damages trust precisely when the team needs to function under pressure.
