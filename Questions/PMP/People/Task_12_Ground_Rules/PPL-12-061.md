# Question

A PM is reviewing draft ground rules with the team. The draft includes "assume positive intent," "disagree and commit" and "no blame culture." A senior team member says these sound like corporate slogans and asks what they would actually look like day to day. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the aspirational wording because this is the expected tone for formal ground rules across most mature project management organisations today and in similar industries.
- **B**: Remove all three rules as too vague and stick to strictly operational rules like meeting times, response times and specific code-review requirements instead.
- **C**: Ask a communications function to redraft the three rules into more professional, polished language that sounds less like corporate slogans to the team.
- **D**: Work with the team to translate each aspirational rule into one or two observable behaviours that would show up day to day in team interactions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's approach to team norms is to make them observable and actionable — translating aspiration into specific behaviours makes them coachable and enforceable. (A) ignores the valid challenge from the senior member. (B) loses valuable cultural rules entirely rather than making them concrete. (C) addresses language rather than the underlying abstractness that is the actual problem.
