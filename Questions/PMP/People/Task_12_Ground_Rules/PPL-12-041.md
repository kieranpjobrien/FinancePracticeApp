# Question

The same ground-rule violation has been raised at three consecutive retrospectives despite the team agreeing each time to do better. The Scrum Master notices nothing is changing. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a root-cause exercise with the team to find why the rule keeps being broken.
- **B**: Stop discussing the rule because the team has already committed and the discussion is repetitive.
- **C**: Escalate the persistent violation to the team's functional management for enforcement.
- **D**: Introduce a consequence (such as a forfeit) for each future violation of the rule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Repeated commitments without behaviour change usually indicate a structural problem, not a willpower problem. (A) addresses the root cause and may reveal that the rule is unrealistic, conflicts with another priority, or depends on tooling the team lacks. (B) accepts the dysfunction. (C) escalates a team-norm issue out of the team's hands. (D) assumes motivation is the issue and risks shaming the team.
