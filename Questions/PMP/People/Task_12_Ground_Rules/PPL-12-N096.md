# Question

A Scrum Master coaching a fintech team wants its working agreement to hold only items that genuinely belong there — team behavioural norms the team governs itself — and to route everything else to its proper artefact. The team has thrown several statements onto the draft. Which statements genuinely belong in the working agreement? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: "We pause and address it as a team whenever our build stays red for more than thirty minutes."
- **B**: "All code is peer-reviewed and unit tests pass before a story is moved to Done."
- **C**: "We keep our cameras on during the daily stand-up and sprint planning."
- **D**: "We give feedback directly to the person concerned rather than discussing them with others."
- **E**: "The product increment must be deployed to the staging environment at the end of every sprint."

## Answer

A, C, D

## Explanation

The working agreement governs how the team behaves and interacts, and the team enforces it among itself; the test is whether a statement describes a team behaviour rather than a product-completeness criterion. (A), (C), and (D) all describe behavioural norms — how the team reacts to a broken build, conduct in meetings, and how members handle feedback — so they belong in the working agreement. (B) and (E) are product acceptance and release criteria: "peer-reviewed and tests pass before Done" and "deployed to staging at sprint end" define when an increment is complete, which is the province of the Definition of Done, not the working agreement. Filing acceptance criteria as behavioural norms blurs the boundary and is how done-ness criteria quietly stop being enforced at acceptance.
