# Question

A long-standing team ground rule requires cameras on during video meetings. A new team member from a different cultural background tells the project manager privately that this makes them uncomfortable. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain the existing rule because it was agreed by the team before this person joined.
- **B**: Quietly excuse this team member from the rule without changing the broader agreement.
- **C**: Bring the topic to the next team meeting so the team can revisit the rule's purpose together.
- **D**: Update the team charter to make camera use optional in all future meetings.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Ground rules are living agreements that should be revisited when circumstances change, including when new perspectives join the team. (C) returns the discussion to the group so they can re-examine the underlying purpose (engagement, connection) and adjust the rule together if needed. (A) prioritises precedent over inclusion. (B) creates a two-tier team and avoids the conversation. (D) makes a unilateral change without involving the team, which undermines team ownership of the rules.
