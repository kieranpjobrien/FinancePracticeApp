# Question

A project manager inherits a long-standing team that has no documented ground rules but operates effectively under a set of implicit norms everyone seems to share. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the organisation's standard ground-rules template and have the team adopt it for consistency.
- **B**: Observe how the team works, then propose making the most useful implicit norms explicit.
- **C**: Leave the team as it is, because what is working should not be changed.
- **D**: Run a formal team-charter workshop to replace implicit norms with new explicit ones.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Explicit norms help with onboarding, conflict, and stakeholder transparency, but a high-functioning team should not have effective practices replaced wholesale. (B) respects what works and adds clarity where it has the most value. (A) imposes external rules without understanding the team. (C) misses real benefits of explicit agreements when new members join. (D) discards proven norms in favour of brand new ones, risking disruption.
