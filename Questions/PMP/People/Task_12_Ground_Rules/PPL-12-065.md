# Question

During ground-rules co-design, the team quickly agrees to a set of positive rules but no one proposes a rule about how disagreement or pushback should be handled. The PM suspects this is the team avoiding the topic, not overlooking it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept what the team has produced and trust that behaviours around disagreement will emerge organically as the team matures over the coming months.
- **B**: Propose a standard "respectful disagreement" ground rule from the PMO template library and add it to the team's set once they broadly agree to adopt it.
- **C**: Rely on observing real disagreements as they emerge and intervene case-by-case, rather than trying to agree a rule for them before any have actually occurred.
- **D**: Name the gap, briefly describe why it matters, and facilitate a short discussion with the team to co-create a rule about disagreement and pushback.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leader facilitation role includes naming what the team is avoiding — rules about disagreement are among the most important to have explicit before they are needed. (A) relies on hope. (B) adds a template rule the team has not genuinely engaged with. (C) waits for a live conflict to teach a lesson the team could have agreed in advance.
