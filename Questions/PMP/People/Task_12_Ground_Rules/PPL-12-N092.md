# Question

A newly formed agile team developing a fitness-tracking app has just co-created its working agreement in a facilitated session. A junior member asks, "What is the actual point of these agreements — isn't this just a list of rules someone will police?" How should the project manager best describe the primary purpose of the team's ground rules?

## Options

- **A**: To set shared expectations for how the team works together, reducing misunderstandings and helping the team self-manage its own behaviour.
- **B**: To give the project manager a documented basis for holding individual members accountable when problems arise.
- **C**: To satisfy the organisation's requirement that every team produce a working agreement at the start of a project.
- **D**: To record the team's decisions so that they can be reported to stakeholders as evidence of team maturity.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ground rules exist to establish a shared understanding of how the team will collaborate, which prevents avoidable friction and enables the team to govern its own behaviour. (A) captures this purpose and directly answers the junior member's scepticism about policing. (B) frames the agreement as a top-down accountability tool for the project manager, which inverts the team-owned, self-managing intent. (C) reduces a valuable team practice to a compliance exercise, missing the point entirely. (D) treats the agreement as a reporting artefact for external audiences rather than a living guide for the team's own interactions.
