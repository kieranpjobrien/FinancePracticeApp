# Question

A team's ground rules require unanimous agreement on all decisions. As the project progresses, one team member frequently disagrees and decisions are stalling. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the decisions unilaterally to keep the schedule on track.
- **B**: Ask the dissenting member to step out of decision-making meetings.
- **C**: Facilitate a team conversation to revisit and adjust the decision-making rule.
- **D**: Escalate stuck decisions to the project sponsor whenever unanimity fails.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Ground rules exist to serve the team. When one is causing harm, the team should adapt it together. (C) opens the conversation about whether consensus ("can live with") might serve better than unanimity, or whether different decision types deserve different processes. (A) breaks the team's own agreement. (B) silences a perspective that may be valuable. (D) pushes routine decisions upward and removes team autonomy.
