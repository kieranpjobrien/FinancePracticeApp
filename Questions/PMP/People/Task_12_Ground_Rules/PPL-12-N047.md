# Question

A hybrid team has a ground rule that significant technical decisions are made by consensus. On a tight deadline, the team is deadlocked between two viable architectures; the consensus rule has produced a stalemate that has already cost two days, and a delivery milestone is now at risk. The team is aware of the problem. As the project manager, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team to agree, here and now, a fallback for when consensus stalls — for example, the technical lead decides after a timeboxed debate — and record it as an update to the ground rule.
- **B**: Facilitate a structured decision technique such as weighted scoring against agreed criteria so the team reaches consensus on the better architecture today.
- **C**: Propose the team timebox one more focused attempt at consensus, and if it fails, bring in a respected external architect to advise and break the tie.
- **D**: Suggest the team accept a deliberately reversible "good enough" choice now to protect the milestone, agreeing to revisit the architecture once the deadline passes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The consensus rule is the team's own, and the deeper problem is that it has no mechanism for deadlock — which will recur. (A) resolves the immediate stalemate and permanently strengthens the agreement by adding a consented fallback, all without the PM unilaterally overriding the team. (B) is a strong facilitation move and may well produce a decision today, but it treats only this instance; the rule remains deadlock-prone for the next hard call, so it is a weaker lasting fix than amending the agreement. (C) can break the tie but importing an outside architect to decide a team-owned technical choice cedes ownership and adds coordination delay the at-risk milestone can ill afford. (D) protects the date and reversibility is genuinely prudent, yet committing to a choice the team has not actually agreed on can entrench resentment and still leaves the consensus rule unfixed for next time.
