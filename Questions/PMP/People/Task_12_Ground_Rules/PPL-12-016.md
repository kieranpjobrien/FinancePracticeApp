# Question

At project kickoff, the project manager presents a draft set of ground rules based on previous projects and asks the team if they agree. Most members nod, though several appear hesitant and stay quiet. What should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Open the draft for the team to amend, remove, and add rules until they collectively own the final set.
- **B**: Treat the silence as tacit agreement and adopt the draft so the project can move into delivery quickly.
- **C**: Walk through each rule in detail to convince the hesitant members of its value and address concerns.
- **D**: Adopt the rules now and revisit them in the first retrospective so the team can refine them then.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ground rules are most effective when the team genuinely owns them, which requires meaningful participation, not just assent. (A) shifts the team from passive recipients to co-authors, surfacing the concerns hidden in the silence. (B) confuses politeness with buy-in. (C) is persuasion rather than collaboration and may produce compliance without commitment. (D) starts the project under rules nobody truly agreed to and defers a problem that will surface in the meantime.
