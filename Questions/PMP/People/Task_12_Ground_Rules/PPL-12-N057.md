# Question

A Scrum team is tidying up several agreements it made informally over its first few sprints, placing each where it will be owned and enforced correctly. Each statement below touches on quality, behaviour, or cadence, but belongs primarily in one artefact. Match each agreement (left) to the artefact it most properly belongs in (right).

## Items

- **1**: "We will not consider a backlog item complete for release until its automated test coverage and user documentation are in place."
- **2**: "If a stand-up starts running past fifteen minutes, whoever notices says so and we take the detail to a follow-up call."
- **3**: "Backlog refinement is held mid-sprint each cycle so items are ready before the next planning."

## Matches

- **A**: Team working agreement / ground rules
- **B**: Definition of Done
- **C**: Team's agreed cadence of events (ways of working schedule)

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Item 1 sets the completeness bar that every increment must meet before it is releasable — a quality criterion applied to the product, which is the Definition of Done (B), even though it reads like a general "rule." Item 2 looks like it could be a cadence item because it mentions timing, but it actually governs how members behave when a meeting drifts — a behavioural norm that lives in the working agreement (A). Item 3 fixes when a recurring event occurs, which belongs to the team's agreed event cadence (C); it is not a quality bar or a behaviour. The discrimination matters because the Definition of Done, behavioural agreements, and the event schedule are each owned, reviewed, and enforced through different mechanisms even though the team consents to all three.
