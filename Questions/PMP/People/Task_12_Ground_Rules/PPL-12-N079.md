# Question

A project manager joins an agile retail-analytics team that has operated for a year with no written working agreement. The team functions reasonably but onboarding new members is slow and disputes about "how we do things" recur. The team is sceptical, saying explicit rules feel bureaucratic and unnecessary. How should the project manager proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Invite the team to capture the unwritten norms it already follows, framing it as documenting what works rather than imposing new bureaucracy.
- **B**: Respect the team's preference and leave the norms implicit, since the team is functioning and resistant to formalising them.
- **C**: Introduce a concise working agreement template and ask the team to fill it in before the next sprint.
- **D**: Document the team's recurring disputes and propose rules to resolve each one for the team's approval.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team already has norms; the problems — slow onboarding and recurring disputes — come from those norms being implicit and contested, not from a lack of process. Framing the task as surfacing the team's own existing practices overcomes the "bureaucracy" objection while delivering the documentation that fixes both problems. (A) does this with full team ownership. (B) accepts the status quo and leaves the genuine, recurring costs unaddressed. (C) imports an external template the team did not author, which is exactly the bureaucratic imposition they are resisting and is likely to be completed half-heartedly. (D) positions the project manager as the rule-author proposing solutions for approval, which reduces ownership and frames the exercise around conflict rather than around the working practices the team already values.
