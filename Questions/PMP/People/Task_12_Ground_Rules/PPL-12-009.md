# Question

A team's original ground rule says "all decisions made by consensus." It worked well when the team was five people. The team has now grown to twelve and decisions are routinely getting stuck in long debates. What should the PM facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Reaffirm the consensus rule; the team agreed to it at start and should honour the original commitment they made.
- **B**: Replace the rule with majority voting so the team can move faster and avoid long debates on every decision.
- **C**: Split the team into two smaller decision-making groups so the original consensus model can still be preserved.
- **D**: Run a session where the team agrees a tailored approach — consensus, consent, or delegation by decision class.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle expects practices to be adapted as context changes; here, growth has changed the practical reality, and the team should agree a more nuanced model. (D) does this. (A) prioritises the original agreement over current effectiveness. (B) swings to the other extreme without the team's input and loses the collaborative spirit consensus was meant to protect. (C) restructures the team to fit a rule rather than the other way round, and creates new coordination cost.
