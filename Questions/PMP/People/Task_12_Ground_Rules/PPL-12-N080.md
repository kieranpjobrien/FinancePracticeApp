# Question

A hybrid biotech project has a long-standing ground rule: "cameras on for all meetings." A newly joined member raises, in a team channel, that keeping her camera on conflicts with a religious observance during certain meeting times, and a brief, awkward debate breaks out with some members defending the rule. The project manager wants to resolve this well. What should she do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team to revise the rule so it accommodates the member's needs while preserving the connection the rule was meant to provide.
- **B**: Grant the new member a personal exemption from the rule and leave the rule unchanged for everyone else.
- **C**: Suspend the cameras-on rule entirely and immediately, given that it is now contested.
- **D**: Move the discussion offline and decide with the member privately what accommodation to make, then inform the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A ground rule that cannot accommodate a legitimate individual need is no longer fit for purpose, and because the rule is team-owned, the team should adapt it together — preserving its original intent (connection) while removing the barrier. (A) resolves the substance and keeps ownership with the team, which also models inclusion for everyone watching. (B) solves the immediate case but treats it as a one-off exception, leaving an exclusionary rule intact and signalling the member as a special case rather than fixing the norm. (C) over-corrects by scrapping a rule the team values without exploring a revision that meets both needs. (D) handles the accommodation privately, which may be considerate, but it removes the team from revising its own agreement and misses the chance to make the norm inclusive by design; the rule itself is what needs to change, openly and with the team.
