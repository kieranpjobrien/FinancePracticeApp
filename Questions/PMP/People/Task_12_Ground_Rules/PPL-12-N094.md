# Question

A hybrid data-analytics team's working agreement includes "disagree and commit: once the team makes a decision, everyone supports it publicly, even if they argued against it." After a divisive choice of reporting tool, a member who lost the argument has begun telling stakeholders privately that "this was a mistake the team will regret." The behaviour is eroding stakeholder confidence. As the project manager, what is the most appropriate first step?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the breach in the next team meeting and ask the team to reaffirm the disagree-and-commit rule for everyone.
- **B**: Reopen the tool decision, since a member feeling strongly enough to undermine it suggests the decision lacked sufficient buy-in.
- **C**: Speak with the member privately to understand their continuing concern and remind them of the commitment they made under the agreement.
- **D**: Report the member's conduct to their functional manager so it can be addressed through the line-management chain.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The member has breached a specific agreement they personally committed to, and the conduct is targeted and individual, so the proportionate first response is a direct, private conversation — both to hear any unresolved concern and to hold them to the commitment. (C) does exactly this, respecting the person while addressing the behaviour at its source. (A) airs an individual's breach to the whole team prematurely, which can shame the member and stops short of the one-to-one conversation the situation calls for first. (B) rewards undermining by reopening a properly made decision, inviting any future loser of an argument to relitigate by going around the team. (D) escalates to line management before the project manager has even spoken to the person, an overreaction that bypasses the direct route and can damage the relationship.
