# Question

Your virtual team spans an organisation in Tokyo and a contractor team in Amsterdam. In early meetings the Amsterdam members challenge ideas openly while the Tokyo members rarely interrupt and seem reluctant to disagree in front of senior colleagues, so decisions feel one-sided. You are facilitating the team's ground-rules session. Which intervention is most effective at making participation genuinely balanced?

## Options

- **A**: Co-create ground rules that offer several routes to contribute — round-robin spoken input plus a written channel where ideas and dissent can be raised before and after meetings.
- **B**: Adopt a ground rule that proposals are circulated in writing 24 hours ahead and comments gathered async, so views are surfaced before anyone meets live.
- **C**: Introduce a rotating facilitator role drawn from both locations who is responsible for explicitly inviting quieter members into each discussion.
- **D**: Establish anonymous input for contentious decisions — for example, silent written voting — so members can dissent without challenging seniors in public.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The goal is to let every member contribute through a mode that suits them without forcing anyone to behave against their cultural norms, and to give the team ownership of how it will work. (A) is strongest because it combines spoken and written routes and is co-created, covering the widest range of comfort levels in one durable agreement. (B) is a genuinely useful async mechanism and would help, but on its own it addresses pre-meeting input while leaving live discussion — where the imbalance shows — unaddressed. (C) can draw out quieter voices and shares responsibility across locations, yet relying on a facilitator to invite people still puts members on the spot in the live setting some find uncomfortable. (D) protects dissent on the hardest decisions, but defaulting to anonymity for everything can mask who holds which view and weaken open collaboration, so it is better as one tool within a broader agreement than the primary fix.
