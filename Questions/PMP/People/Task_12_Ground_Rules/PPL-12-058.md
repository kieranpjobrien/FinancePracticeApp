# Question

A fully remote team is finalising ground rules. Debate stalls on response-time expectations for team chat. Some members want guaranteed one-hour responses during working hours; others want full async freedom with next-day responses acceptable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a middle-ground default of four-hour response times during working hours and treat this across the team as the binding single operational standard going forward.
- **B**: Adopt the strict one-hour standard so genuinely urgent issues move quickly, and accept that some team members will find the expectation interruptive in their daily workflow.
- **C**: Defer the rule altogether for now and let bilateral expectations form naturally between working pairs as the project moves through its first few sprints.
- **D**: Tier the rule by message type — defined urgent markers get fast responses, everything else is async — and agree the markers together as a team.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle says rules should match context — a tiered rule captures both legitimate needs by routing them to different channels, and is the PMI-aligned co-design. (A) is a compromise that serves neither use case well. (B) imposes a cost on the majority to serve the urgent minority. (C) leaves a foundational norm unresolved and invites inconsistent expectations.
