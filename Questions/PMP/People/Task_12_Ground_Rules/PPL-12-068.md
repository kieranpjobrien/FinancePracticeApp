# Question

A key external stakeholder regularly breaks the team's ground rule about minimum notice for change requests. The stakeholder is not bound by the team's rules, but their behaviour is causing the team to repeatedly break their own sprint goals. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Adjust the team's ground rules to remove the notice expectation, since it is unrealistic to hold when external stakeholders regularly submit requests at short notice.
- **B**: Tell the team to absorb the disruption and continue trying to meet sprint goals despite the short-notice requests, as stakeholder engagement must come first.
- **C**: Engage the stakeholder directly, share the cost of short notice with real data, and agree an intake mechanism that respects the team's planning rule.
- **D**: Escalate the stakeholder's behaviour to the sponsor and ask the sponsor to enforce the team's notice rule on the stakeholder in future interactions.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder-engagement lens is to engage directly with evidence and co-design an intake that meets both parties' real needs. (A) surrenders a rule the team had good reasons for. (B) preserves dysfunction and trains the team to break their own commitments. (D) skips a conversation the PM should have directly with the stakeholder first.
