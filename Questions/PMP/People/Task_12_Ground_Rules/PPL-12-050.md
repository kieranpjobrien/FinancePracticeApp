# Question

A twelve-month predictive construction project is eight months in. The ground rules agreed at kickoff feel stale, some are ignored in practice and others now conflict with how the team actually works. The PM wonders whether to refresh them or leave them alone. What is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the ground rules in place unchanged to avoid re-opening debates the team had already resolved at kickoff and expended energy on.
- **B**: Run a short working session with the team to review each ground rule, retire the dead ones, and refresh those that still matter in today's context.
- **C**: Delete the existing ground rules wholesale and let the team operate on trust and current working practices for the remaining four project months.
- **D**: Replace the old ground rules with a new set drafted by the PM based on what has been observed working best over the last eight months.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats ground rules as a living artefact — reviewing, retiring and refreshing them as the project matures is the PMI-aligned move. (A) keeps a document the team has already stopped following in practice, undermining trust in the artefact. (C) throws out shared agreement with no replacement. (D) bypasses the team whose ownership makes ground rules work.
