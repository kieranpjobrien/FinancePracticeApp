# Question

During a retrospective, an agile team maintaining a payments gateway proposes adding two items to its working agreement: "no story is accepted until it passes the automated security scan" and "we keep our stand-up to fifteen minutes." A respected senior developer argues both belong in the working agreement because "they are just things the team agrees to do." As the project manager supporting the team, how should you guide them?

## Options

- **A**: Help the team place the security-scan criterion in the Definition of Done and keep the stand-up timebox in the working agreement, explaining why each artefact owns a different kind of agreement.
- **B**: Agree with the senior developer and add both items to the working agreement, since the team genuinely agrees to do both and consolidation aids clarity.
- **C**: Put both items in the Definition of Done, because both describe standards the team commits to meeting before work is considered complete.
- **D**: Defer the decision to the team's vote, adding both wherever the majority prefers, to preserve the team's ownership of its own agreements.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Definition of Done specifies the completeness and quality criteria a product increment must meet to be releasable, whereas the working agreement governs how the team behaves together; the two items belong to different categories despite both being "things the team agrees to do." (A) routes the security-scan gate — a product acceptance criterion — to the Definition of Done and keeps the stand-up timebox, a behavioural norm, in the working agreement. (B) collapses a meaningful distinction; misfiling acceptance criteria in the working agreement is exactly how quality gates stop being enforced at acceptance. (C) wrongly treats a behavioural meeting norm as a product-completeness criterion. (D) preserves ownership but abdicates the project manager's facilitation duty to help the team understand which artefact each agreement properly belongs to, so the team may entrench a confusion that later costs it.
