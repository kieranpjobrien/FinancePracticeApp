# Question

On a regulated pharmaceutical validation project, company policy and regulatory requirements dictate strict documentation, sign-off and change-control standards. A team member argues that "ground rules should be things the team chose, not things imposed on us." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree and exclude the regulatory requirements from the ground rules, covering them only in the project management plan as external constraints.
- **B**: Agree to soften the regulatory requirements within the ground rules so the team feels less constrained about how they apply day-to-day.
- **C**: Explain that mandatory standards are non-negotiable and belong in ground rules precisely so expectations are explicit, and co-create everything discretionary.
- **D**: Separate ground rules entirely from mandatory standards and record only the team's freely chosen behaviours as the team's ground rules.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI says compliance is non-negotiable and ground rules should make expectations explicit — the PMI-aligned move is to include mandatory standards alongside discretionary team agreements. (A) hides compliance where it is less likely to be followed. (B) is not an option for regulatory requirements. (D) risks the team acting as if compliance is optional because it sits outside their agreement.
