# Question

The team is debating a proposed ground rule to limit work-in-progress to two items per developer. One vocal team member calls this micromanagement; most others want the rule. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the rule based on majority preference so the team can move on.
- **B**: Withdraw the proposal because there is significant disagreement.
- **C**: Facilitate a discussion of the underlying needs and craft a version that addresses the dissenter's concerns.
- **D**: Run a one-sprint trial of the rule and decide afterwards based on metrics.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Disagreement on a proposed norm is a chance to improve the norm, not just to vote on it. (C) explores why the rule matters to the majority (focus, flow) and why it concerns the dissenter (autonomy, trust), and looks for an option that meets both. (A) institutionalises a minority's grievance. (B) abandons a useful norm to avoid conflict. (D) is a reasonable later step but jumps ahead before the team has understood the disagreement.
