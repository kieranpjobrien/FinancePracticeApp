# Question

On a predictive infrastructure project, the team's agreed ground rule is that meeting owners start on time regardless of who is absent. A senior architect consistently joins ten minutes late and the PM has noticed junior members now arriving late too, citing the architect's behaviour. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the lateness pattern in the next team retrospective so the team can decide collectively what to do about the specific ground rule and its enforcement.
- **B**: Speak privately with the senior architect, reference the ground rule they agreed to, and ask them to model the behaviour the team signed up for.
- **C**: Quietly relax the ground rule across the team since the architect has made clear they will not follow it and enforcement is becoming awkward.
- **D**: Escalate the issue to the architect's line manager and ask them to remind the architect of their ground-rule commitment on this particular project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to address ground-rule breaches with the specific individual first and respectfully — senior people modelling the rule matters most, and a private 1:1 is the PMI-aligned first step. (A) forces a public conversation before a private one. (C) rewards the breach and erodes the team's agreement. (D) is premature escalation of something the PM can handle directly.
