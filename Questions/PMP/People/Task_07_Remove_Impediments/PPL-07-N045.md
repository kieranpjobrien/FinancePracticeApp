# Question

A mobile-banking team is repeatedly stalled because the product owner takes three to four days to answer clarifying questions on stories, so developers either build on assumptions that sometimes prove wrong or sit idle. The product owner is shared across three teams and is genuinely overloaded; she agrees the delay is a problem. As the Scrum Master, which TWO actions will most directly and durably address this impediment? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the product owner's leadership that one PO split across three teams is creating decision latency that is throttling delivery, and request additional product capacity or an empowered proxy.
- **B**: Agree a short daily fixed window with the product owner to clear outstanding questions, so the wait shrinks from days to hours.
- **C**: Have the team capture assumptions explicitly when an answer is pending, proceed on the most likely interpretation, and review with the PO at the next opportunity.
- **D**: Bring the recurring delays and their delivery cost to the next sprint retrospective so the team can decide collectively how to respond.
- **E**: Tighten backlog refinement so stories entering a sprint already carry enough detail to need far fewer mid-sprint clarifications.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The impediment has a structural cause (one PO across three teams) and a day-to-day flow cause (multi-day answer latency), so the two actions that most directly attack the impediment itself are escalating for more product capacity or a proxy (A) and installing a reliable daily clearing window (B). (E) better refinement genuinely reduces clarification volume and is a sound complementary practice, but it shrinks demand on the PO rather than fixing the constrained supply of decisions, so it is the weaker of the candidates. (C) assumption-logging keeps work moving but still manufactures rework and treats the symptom. (D) routing it to the retrospective defers action on a blocker that is hurting delivery now. The strongest pair removes both the bottleneck and the latency directly.
