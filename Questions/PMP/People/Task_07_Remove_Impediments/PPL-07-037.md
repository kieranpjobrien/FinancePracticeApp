# Question

A Scrum team raises in retrospective that they spend roughly 60% of their week in meetings, leaving little time for focused development. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel any meeting the team is not directly running for the next sprint.
- **B**: Audit the standing meetings the team attends and challenge the purpose, attendees, and cadence of each.
- **C**: Tell developers to decline meetings they consider non-essential at their own discretion.
- **D**: Raise the issue with the steering committee and request a meeting moratorium across the organisation.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's job is to remove impediments, and the disciplined first step is to understand the impediment before acting on it. Auditing each meeting's purpose and attendance produces the evidence needed to consolidate or remove specific meetings. (A) is a unilateral, blunt action that may break legitimate coordination. (C) pushes responsibility (and political risk) onto individual developers and creates inconsistent behaviour. (D) escalates to organisation-level governance before the Scrum Master has done the team-level analysis they own.
