# Question

For four sprints on a telecoms billing programme, the shared test environment has been unstable and the team loses roughly a day each sprint. The PM has already agreed with the platform team that a permanent rebuild is needed; the platform lead estimates it will take three weeks and has put it on their backlog behind other work, so it is not yet scheduled. The product owner is frustrated that velocity keeps dipping in the meantime. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the cumulative cost of the instability visible to the platform team's manager and the sponsor, and seek a committed start date and priority for the rebuild rather than leaving it unscheduled on the backlog.
- **B**: Coordinate a short-term mitigation with the platform team — such as a dedicated environment snapshot the team can reset themselves — to stem the daily losses until the rebuild is scheduled.
- **C**: Ask the platform lead to bring the rebuild forward ahead of their other committed work so the root cause is fixed as quickly as possible.
- **D**: Re-baseline the team's velocity expectation downward and explain to the product owner that the dip is unavoidable until the environment is rebuilt.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root-cause fix is already agreed but stuck because it has no priority or date; the PM's real lever is to surface the cumulative impact to those who own the platform backlog and the budget so the rebuild gets committed (A). (B) a self-service reset is a sensible interim measure and may well run in parallel, but on its own it once again only contains the symptom and leaves the permanent fix drifting. (C) simply asking the platform lead to jump the queue ignores that their other work may be equally or more important — that trade-off is not theirs to make unilaterally and belongs to the manager who owns both priorities. (D) lowering the velocity expectation rationalises the waste and removes the pressure that would get the rebuild scheduled. The fine margin is between escalating to get the agreed fix prioritised (A) and merely mitigating again (B): the question is recurring precisely because the fix keeps being deferred.
