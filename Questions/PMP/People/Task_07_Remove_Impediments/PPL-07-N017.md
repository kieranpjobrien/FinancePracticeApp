# Question

As servant leader on an edtech product team, you want to get ahead of obstacles rather than only reacting once work is already stuck. Which THREE practices best help you anticipate and clear impediments earlier? (Choose THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Keep a generous buffer in each sprint's capacity so that when obstacles do appear, the team has the slack to absorb them without missing the sprint goal.
- **B**: Listen for early signals in daily scrums and planning — emerging cross-team dependencies, access not yet granted, decisions drifting — and start working them before they harden into blockers.
- **C**: Maintain a risk register of potential obstacles and review it at each sprint boundary so emerging threats are formally tracked alongside delivery risks.
- **D**: Build standing relationships with the upstream and supporting teams you depend on, so cross-team dependencies can be resolved quickly the moment they surface.
- **E**: Review the upcoming backlog a sprint or two ahead with the team to spot dependencies, environments and approvals that will be needed, and line them up in advance.

## Answer

B, D, E

## Explanation

The discriminator is *anticipate and clear earlier* — actions that prevent or pre-empt obstacles — versus actions that merely cushion or record them. Reading early signals in ceremonies (B), having relationships with dependency partners already in place (D), and scanning the upcoming backlog to line up dependencies and access ahead of need (E) all act *before* work stalls and directly remove or forestall the obstacle. (A) is sound resilience and genuinely helps the team cope, but buffer absorbs the *impact* of impediments rather than anticipating or clearing them — it is mitigation, not prevention, so it does not fit "clear earlier". (C) is a legitimate and useful practice, but tracking potential obstacles in a register is about *visibility and monitoring*; review at sprint boundaries is also slower than catching signals live in (B), and a logged risk is not a cleared one. The three best answers are the ones that surface and resolve obstacles ahead of time, not those that buffer against them or merely record them.
