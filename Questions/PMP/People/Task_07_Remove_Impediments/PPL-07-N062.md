# Question

An agile team building a retail loyalty platform cannot complete its sprint goal because a downstream payments team has not delivered an API stub the loyalty team depends on. The payments team's Scrum Master says the work is in their next sprint, which starts in eight days. The loyalty team's current sprint ends in four days. As the loyalty team's Scrum Master, what is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Move the dependent stories out of the current sprint and pull forward independent backlog items so the team stays productive.
- **B**: Raise the cross-team dependency at the next scrum of scrums so both teams' sprint plans can be aligned going forward.
- **C**: Meet the payments Scrum Master to see whether a lightweight mock or partial stub can be provided within four days to unblock the loyalty team.
- **D**: Escalate to the release train engineer that the payments team's sequencing is jeopardising the loyalty sprint goal.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The impediment is a cross-team dependency with a four-day window, and the most direct removal is a Scrum-Master-to-Scrum-Master conversation exploring whether a cheap interim artefact — a mock or partial stub — can unblock the work in time (C). (A) re-planning around the blocker keeps the team busy but concedes the sprint goal without first trying to remove the obstacle. (B) the scrum of scrums fixes future sequencing but is too slow for a goal due in four days. (D) escalating to the release train engineer skips the peer negotiation that should be attempted first and spends political capital prematurely. The fine line is between actively negotiating a quick unblock (C) and either coping around it (A) or escalating before trying (D).
