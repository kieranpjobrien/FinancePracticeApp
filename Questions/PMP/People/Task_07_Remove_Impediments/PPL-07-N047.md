# Question

On a predictive ERP implementation, a key integration cannot proceed because the external software vendor has not delivered a configuration file promised under the contract, and it is now five days overdue. The vendor's account manager keeps saying it is "almost ready." This activity is on the critical path and the PM has no slack left. Who should the PM primarily work through to remove this blocker?

## Options

- **A**: The team's technical lead, asking the developers to reverse-engineer the configuration so the project does not stay dependent on the vendor.
- **B**: The contract or procurement administrator, to formally invoke the delivery obligation and any remedies in the agreement while keeping the sponsor informed.
- **C**: The vendor's account manager, by continuing to apply pressure for a date since they are the established point of contact.
- **D**: The project steering committee, by tabling the delay at the next scheduled monthly governance meeting for a decision.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This is a contractual non-delivery on the critical path, so the right lever is the procurement channel that can formally enforce the obligation, supported by sponsor visibility (B). (A) reverse-engineering a contracted deliverable wastes the team's effort and lets the vendor off a binding commitment. (C) the account manager has already failed to deliver and repeating informal pressure has not worked. (D) waiting for a monthly governance meeting is far too slow for a blocker that is already consuming the critical path.
