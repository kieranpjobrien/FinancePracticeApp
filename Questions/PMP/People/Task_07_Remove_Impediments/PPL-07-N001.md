# Question

During daily scrum on a mobile-banking team, a developer reports she is blocked because the shared test-environment database is being rebuilt by a separate platform team and will not be ready for two days. Two of the four committed stories depend on it; the sprint goal hinges on at least one of them shipping. The platform team sits in a different reporting line and the scrum master has no authority over their schedule. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Go to the platform team's lead now to understand the rebuild timeline and jointly explore an earlier cut-over or a temporary alternative environment, while the developer picks up unblocked work in the meantime.
- **B**: Ask the developer to swarm with teammates on the two unblocked stories to protect the sprint goal, and let the database dependency clear on its own two-day timeline.
- **C**: Bring the dependency to the product owner so they can decide whether to renegotiate the sprint scope with stakeholders given the two-day wait.
- **D**: Raise the cross-team dependency at the next scrum-of-scrums so it is visible to all teams and the platform team can prioritise it against their other commitments.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four are things a scrum master might do, but only A both attacks the blocker at its source and keeps the team productive *now*. The scrum master's primary duty with an external dependency is to actively pursue its removal, so engaging the owning team immediately to compress or bypass the two-day wait is the strongest first move (A). (B) protects the sprint by swarming but passively accepts the full two-day delay without any attempt to shorten it — acceptable only once removal has been tried. (C) prematurely hands the problem to the product owner as a scope-renegotiation before anyone has tried to clear it, conceding the impact too early. (D) is correct transparency but too slow — scrum-of-scrums may be days away and routes the issue through another forum when the scrum master can act on it directly today.
