# Question

On a hybrid hospital-EHR implementation, an agile delivery team is blocked because a separate, predictively managed data-migration workstream keeps missing the interface deliverables the team depends on, and the two workstreams report to different managers. The delivery team's lead has asked the migration lead repeatedly, with little change. As the project manager spanning both workstreams, which TWO actions best address this impediment? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Bring the two workstream leads together to agree concrete interface dates and a shared dependency plan with clear ownership.
- **B**: Direct the agile team to stub the missing interfaces and proceed, treating the migration deliverables as optional for now.
- **C**: Make the dependency and its delivery impact transparent to both managers and, if the leads cannot resolve it, escalate for a joint decision on resourcing or sequencing.
- **D**: Instruct the migration lead to reprioritise their work to meet the agile team's dates.
- **E**: Have the agile team absorb the migration tasks themselves so they no longer depend on the other workstream.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

A cross-workstream dependency that peer requests have failed to fix needs both alignment and a path to escalation: convene the two leads to agree firm interface dates and shared ownership (A), and make the impact transparent to both managers, escalating for a joint resourcing or sequencing decision if the leads still cannot resolve it (C). (B) permanently stubbing and treating the migration deliverables as optional misrepresents a genuine dependency and risks an unworkable integration. (D) unilaterally ordering the migration lead to reprioritise overrides the other manager's authority and is likely to breed resentment rather than a sustainable fix. (E) having the agile team absorb specialist migration work is usually infeasible and dodges the coordination problem. The right pair resolves the dependency through alignment first, with structured escalation held in reserve.
