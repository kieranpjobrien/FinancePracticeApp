# Question

During a sprint on an agile fintech team, a developer raises at the daily scrum that the team's local test data is stale, which is slowing several people down. Refreshing it sits within the team's own access and skill, though it would take a couple of people a few hours away from their committed stories. As the Scrum Master, what is the most appropriate action?

## Options

- **A**: Refresh the test data yourself so the developers can stay on their committed stories.
- **B**: Surface it to the team to decide how and when they will refresh it, and clear the way for whoever takes it on.
- **C**: Raise it with the product owner first so the sprint scope can be adjusted before the team diverts effort to the refresh.
- **D**: Ask the central data platform team to take on the refresh so the team's capacity stays on the sprint goal.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The impediment is within the team's own capability, and the trade-off is theirs to make — pulling a couple of people for a few hours touches their sprint commitment — so the Scrum Master's role is to put it to the team to decide how and when to handle it and then remove any friction for whoever picks it up (B), supporting self-organisation rather than pre-empting it. (A) doing it personally takes the decision and the work away from the team and builds dependency, even though it looks like protecting their focus. (C) routing it to the product owner to adjust scope treats a few hours of internal maintenance as a scope event and inserts an unnecessary gatekeeper into a call the team can make itself. (D) handing it to the platform team offloads work the team is equipped to do and is typically slower for a local refresh. The discrimination is between enabling the team's own decision (B) and options that take it over (A), over-formalise it (C), or outsource it (D).
