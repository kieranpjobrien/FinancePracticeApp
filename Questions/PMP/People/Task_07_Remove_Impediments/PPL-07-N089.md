# Question

During the daily scrum on an agile retail-analytics team, a developer says he is completely blocked: he needs read access to a production data extract to validate a report, and only the data-governance team can grant it. He raised a ticket through the standard access process yesterday but has heard nothing, and the work is stalled until access is granted. As the Scrum Master, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the developer pull a different sprint-backlog item so progress continues while the access request moves through the governance queue.
- **B**: Note the blocker on the impediment board and review it at tomorrow's daily scrum, giving the standard process a full cycle to respond.
- **C**: Ask the developer to chase his own ticket with data governance, since he understands exactly what access he needs.
- **D**: Reach out to the data-governance team yourself today to understand the hold-up and get the access request moving.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Clearing impediments that sit outside the team is a core Scrum Master responsibility, and here the blocker is total, the owner is known, and a day has already passed with no response, so the Scrum Master should engage data governance today (D). (A) is the most defensible alternative — keeping the developer productive is reasonable — but reshuffling work absorbs the impediment instead of removing it, and the report still cannot be validated. (B) letting the standard process run another full day accepts avoidable idle time on a total blocker. (C) pushing the follow-up back onto the developer leaves him to navigate an external team alone, which is the obstacle-removal the Scrum Master exists to take on. The margin is between actively clearing the external blocker now (D) and working around or waiting it out.
