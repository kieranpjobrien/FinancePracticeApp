# Question

A Scrum Master on an e-commerce team sees the same impediment recur across three consecutive sprints: a shared staging environment that another department reboots without warning, wiping the team's test data mid-sprint and costing hours of rework each time. The other department reboots on its own schedule for reasons unrelated to this team. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team automate a snapshot-and-restore of their test data so a surprise reboot costs minutes rather than hours.
- **B**: Take the recurring disruption and its measured delivery cost to the department that owns the environment and agree a coordinated reboot window so it stops affecting the team mid-sprint.
- **C**: Add a standing item to each daily scrum to check the environment's status so the team reacts to a reboot the moment it happens.
- **D**: Request a dedicated staging environment for the team through the next budget cycle to remove the shared dependency entirely.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The impediment originates outside the team — another department's reboot behaviour the team cannot control — so the durable fix is to engage the environment's owner and change the behaviour at its source (B). (A) automated restore and (C) faster reaction both genuinely reduce the team's pain, but they harden the team against a disruption that continues rather than stopping it, leaving the cost recurring every sprint. (D) a dedicated environment may be the long-term answer, but deferring to a future budget cycle leaves the team exposed for months when a coordination agreement could stop the disruption now. The margin is between resolving the impediment at its external source (B) and absorbing or out-waiting it within the team.
