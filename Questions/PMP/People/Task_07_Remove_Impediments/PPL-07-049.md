# Question

The single approver for design decisions on your project, a senior architect, has been called into a six-week confidential merger-planning activity and is largely unavailable. Design choices are stacking up and the team is beginning to stall on ambiguous stories. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Agree an interim delegation with the senior architect and his leadership so that named alternate approvers can clear specific decision categories during his absence.
- **B**: Freeze all design decisions and pause the affected backlog until the senior architect is available again in six weeks, absorbing the slip into the project schedule.
- **C**: Instruct the team to proceed with their own best design judgement and review every decision retrospectively once the senior architect returns from the merger work.
- **D**: Escalate directly to the CEO as a delivery-threatening impediment, since the merger priorities have blocked a senior architect from performing his normal project role.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI resolves approver absence with a proper interim delegation agreed by the approver and his leadership, keeping decision accountability clear. (A) does that. (B) absorbs a fully avoidable six-week slip. (C) lets the team make unapproved architectural decisions that are costly to unwind. (D) escalates above the right level and politicises an issue the architect's own leadership can resolve.
