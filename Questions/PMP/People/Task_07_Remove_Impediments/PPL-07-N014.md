# Question

On a predictive government case-management system, a key user group has gone quiet on requests to validate business rules, blocking sign-off of a major deliverable. They were engaged early on; the silence began a few weeks ago and you do not yet know why. They remain the only group with the domain knowledge to validate these rules correctly. What should you do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Sit down with the user group, openly acknowledge whatever concern is driving the disengagement, and work with the sponsor on addressing its impact so validation can resume on an honest footing.
- **B**: Escalate the stalled validation to the sponsor with the schedule impact and ask them to reinforce the group's commitment and unblock their participation.
- **C**: Offer to reduce the burden on the group — fewer, better-prepared sessions and pre-analysed rule summaries — to lower the cost of engaging and draw them back in.
- **D**: Meet the user group's lead in person to understand what has changed and why engagement has dropped, before deciding how to get the validation back on track.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

This is a fine-margin call where the trap is jumping to a solution before you know the cause. A, B and C are each a reasonable *response to a specific cause* — fear of job loss, a commitment problem, or engagement fatigue respectively — but the stem deliberately does not tell you which it is, and applying the wrong remedy can entrench the resistance. So the correct first move is to find out what actually changed by talking to them directly (D). (A) is exactly the right action *if* the driver turns out to be fear about role impact, but assuming that and "acknowledging their concern" before you have confirmed it can feel presumptuous or miss a different cause entirely. (B) is appropriate *if* it is a commitment or priority failure, but escalating to force participation before understanding a possibly legitimate or fixable reason risks hardening the block. (C) helps *if* the problem is workload, but lightening sessions does nothing if the real issue is fear or politics. The distinguishing factor is diagnosing the cause of the disengagement before selecting the intervention.
