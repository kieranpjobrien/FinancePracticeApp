# Question

New joiners on your data-platform project consistently report that missing or inconsistent documentation is their biggest impediment in the first four weeks. Experienced team members have been reluctant to stop to document, citing their own delivery pressure. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the documentation gap as an inevitable cost of working on a fast-moving data platform and focus the team's available time on delivery rather than any new documentation work.
- **B**: Mandate that every experienced team member spend two full days writing documentation before any further feature work is committed in the next sprint cycle, regardless of delivery pressure.
- **C**: Define the minimum viable runbook set, treat updates as part of the Definition of Done, and pair newcomers with experienced members to capture what they most need.
- **D**: Hire a technical writer whose only job is to document the data platform so that the delivery team can continue uninterrupted on their existing priorities and sprint commitments.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI addresses a recurring onboarding impediment by embedding documentation into the team's normal flow via the Definition of Done and newcomer pairing, not by ignoring, mandating bulk effort, or outsourcing. (C) does this. (A) normalises the impediment. (B) stops delivery for a big batch of docs that often miss what newcomers actually need. (D) adds a parallel resource without fixing the flow habit.
