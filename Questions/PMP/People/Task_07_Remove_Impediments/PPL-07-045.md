# Question

A partner organisation has missed two API delivery milestones, communication has gone quiet, and the PM has limited contractual leverage. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Re-baseline the project schedule to absorb the slip and update stakeholders accordingly.
- **B**: Engage the partner's account executive to surface the issue and re-establish a delivery commitment.
- **C**: Begin building the API functionality internally as a parallel workstream.
- **D**: Issue a formal breach notice citing the missed milestones in the partnership agreement.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Before re-baselining, escalating contractually, or duplicating work, PMI expects the PM to use relationship channels to restore communication and a credible plan from the partner. The account executive often has different leverage and visibility than the working-level contact who has gone quiet. (A) hides the problem in the schedule without addressing the dependency. (C) commits real cost and effort before exhausting the cheaper relationship-based fix. (D) is premature; formal breach action should follow, not precede, attempted resolution.
