# Question

The only person who knows how to fix your team's brittle build pipeline is on extended unplanned sick leave. Builds have failed three days this week and the team is losing hours per day. The sick person is not expected back for at least a fortnight. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pair two other engineers to learn the pipeline from the source and existing documents, engage DevOps colleagues, and stabilise the builds while capturing the knowledge for the future.
- **B**: Wait for the team member's return, ask the team to manually work around the broken pipeline, and accept the delivery slip that will result from the lost capacity in the meantime.
- **C**: Scrap the current pipeline and rebuild from scratch with the rest of the team using a completely different tool, since a fragile pipeline is clearly not fit for purpose anyway.
- **D**: Contact the team member on sick leave directly to ask for urgent help, on the basis that the business need is severe enough to justify interrupting their recovery.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI tackles a single-point-of-failure impediment by building redundancy in the team and engaging adjacent help, while capturing knowledge for the longer term. (A) does that. (B) accepts ongoing loss for a fortnight. (C) over-reacts by scrapping a working system and adds huge unplanned work. (D) intrudes on a sick colleague, which is both a welfare issue and often unlawful depending on jurisdiction.
