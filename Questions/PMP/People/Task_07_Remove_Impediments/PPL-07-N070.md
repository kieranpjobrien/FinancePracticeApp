# Question

On a predictive water-treatment construction project, the only commissioning specialist on the team has been signed off sick for three weeks, and the commissioning activities sit on the critical path, due to start in five days. The project manager has confirmed the absence with HR. Which TWO actions should the project manager take FIRST to address this impediment? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Request a qualified replacement commissioning specialist through the resource manager or an approved subcontractor to cover the activities.
- **B**: Re-sequence the schedule to bring forward other critical-path work that can proceed without the specialist while a replacement is arranged.
- **C**: Raise a change request to extend the project end date by three weeks to reflect the specialist's absence.
- **D**: Notify the sponsor and key stakeholders of the absence and the potential schedule impact before committing to a recovery approach.
- **E**: Have a senior engineer review whether some early commissioning prep can be safely done now and the qualified sign-off steps held for the specialist's return.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

A critical-path resource gap with a hard five-day horizon calls first for actions that actually close the gap and protect the schedule: secure a qualified replacement through the resource manager or an approved subcontractor (A), and re-sequence to advance other critical-path work that does not need the specialist while that replacement is arranged (B). The remaining options are defensible but not the first moves. (C) raising a change request to extend the end date concedes the slip before any recovery has been attempted — you pursue schedule change only once replacement and re-sequencing have failed to hold the date. (D) informing the sponsor and stakeholders is appropriate and will likely follow, but communicating the impact is not itself removing the impediment and should rest on a recovery plan you can present. (E) splitting prep from qualified sign-off is a sensible idea to explore, yet it only partially mitigates and still leaves the specialist-dependent steps blocked, so it cannot substitute for sourcing a replacement. The discrimination is between acting to fill and re-sequence now (A, B) and the slower change, communication, or partial-mitigation steps (C, D, E).
