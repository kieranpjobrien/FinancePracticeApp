# Question

Two newly hired developers cannot start real work because the team has run out of licences for the IDE and the database tool. Procurement can add licences in seven days. The new developers are idle and morale is dipping on their first week. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait out the seven days and ask the new developers to read documentation in the meantime, starting real work once the new licences have come through from procurement.
- **B**: Share existing licences on a rota with the current team so the new developers get half-days on tools, even though this impacts everyone's throughput for the week.
- **C**: Use your discretionary budget to purchase trial licences or a short-term workaround tool, request the permanent licences through procurement, and plan onboarding activities on safe tasks for the first few days.
- **D**: Remove one of the current developers from the licensed tooling for a week so the new developer can take his licence, on the assumption the current developer can find other things to do.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI keeps new starters productive through short-term mitigations and meaningful onboarding work, while the permanent fix is progressed through proper channels. (C) covers both. (A) wastes a week and dents morale on week one. (B) degrades the whole team's throughput for a fixable licensing issue. (D) shifts the impediment onto another individual without any legitimate reason.
