# Question

On a predictive railway-signalling upgrade, a test engineer reports that a calibration rig promised by an external supplier has slipped its delivery, and without it the next two weeks of acceptance testing cannot begin. The supplier relationship is active and the rig is on the critical path. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Contact the supplier to confirm a firm delivery date and recover the calibration rig before testing is due to start.
- **B**: Re-sequence the schedule to pull forward documentation and setup tasks so the team is not idle while the rig remains unavailable.
- **C**: Add the rig delay to the risk register and assign it a high probability and impact for the next review cycle.
- **D**: Notify the sponsor that acceptance testing is at risk so expectations about the milestone can be managed early.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The obstacle is concrete, time-bound, and sits with a supplier the project manager can reach directly, so the first move is to work the channel that actually recovers the missing rig (A). (B) re-sequencing other work is a sensible parallel mitigation but treats the symptom of idle time rather than the blocker itself, and should run alongside A rather than instead of it. (C) logging it as a risk mis-classifies an issue that has already occurred as a future uncertainty. (D) alerting the sponsor may follow, but raising alarm before the supplier has even been pressed for a date is premature. The margin is between acting on the impediment at its source now (A) and merely working around, reclassifying, or reporting it.
