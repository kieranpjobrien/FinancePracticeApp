# Question

In a one-to-one, a team member mentions that a low-level technical impediment has been slowing the team for months but was never logged, because the original lead dismissed it as "just the way the system is." Several other members quietly work around it. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Thank the team member, log the impediment themselves, and work a fix without raising it in the wider team in order to protect the individual who originally disclosed it.
- **B**: Raise the impediment sharply in the next team meeting as evidence the team has lost discipline in impediment management, and direct everyone to log issues properly.
- **C**: Treat the omission as a historical issue since the team has clearly been coping, and focus instead only on any brand-new impediments the team raises from this point.
- **D**: Use the next retrospective to explore what stopped the impediment being logged, re-establish psychological safety norms, and jointly decide on an approach to address it.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats under-logged impediments as a culture and psychological-safety signal that the team should address together, not in private or through blame. (D) does that. (A) fixes the symptom and keeps the culture pattern in place. (B) punishes the team publicly and discourages future disclosure. (C) ignores the root cause entirely and accepts the existing working-around behaviour.
