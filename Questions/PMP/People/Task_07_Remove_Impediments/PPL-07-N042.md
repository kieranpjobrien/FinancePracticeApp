# Question

On a predictive hospital-construction project, the structural-steel subcontractor cannot start because a specialised crane must be hired through the client's central procurement function, whose standard purchase-order lead time is eight weeks. There are six weeks of float before the crane shortage hits the critical path. The procurement team is following its normal process and has not flagged any problem. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Resequence the programme to pull non-steel activities forward, preserving float, and continue tracking the purchase order through the normal procurement reporting line.
- **B**: Escalate now to the project sponsor with the float position and a request to expedite the purchase order, so executive attention is applied while float still exists.
- **C**: Raise the procurement lead time as a risk, quantify the schedule and cost exposure, and agree an owner and trigger date with the procurement team for expediting if float erodes.
- **D**: Ask the procurement buyer directly whether the eight-week lead time can be compressed for this single critical item, and confirm the dates the order must be placed by.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The procurement process is functioning normally and float still covers the lead time, so the proportionate first move is to engage the owning function directly to test whether the standard timeline can be shortened for this one critical item and to pin down the must-order-by dates (D) — clarifying the real constraint before invoking heavier levers. (B) escalating to the sponsor over a process that is working to schedule, with weeks of float intact, spends executive capital prematurely and can read as crying wolf. (C) logging a risk with a trigger date is sound contingency planning but is a parallel safeguard, not the action that engages the constraint now. (A) resequencing is useful mitigation but reorders the team's work before anyone has even asked whether the lead time is genuinely fixed. The strongest sequence starts by confirming the constraint with the people who own it.
