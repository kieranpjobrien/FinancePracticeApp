# Question

A team member raises a small, recurring impediment that costs the team about two hours a week in workaround. They add: "It's not worth fixing, we've always worked around it." What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the cumulative cost over the project, compare to removal effort, and decide.
- **B**: Accept the established workaround since the team has confirmed they are comfortable with it.
- **C**: Prioritise fixing it immediately because impediments should always be removed when identified.
- **D**: Log the impediment and revisit it at a later retrospective if it becomes more painful.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI uses cost-benefit thinking on impediments rather than treating them as binary "remove or accept" decisions. (A) makes the decision deliberate and visible. (B) accepts cost without analysis. (C) ignores opportunity cost; not every impediment is worth fixing now. (D) defers a clearly tractable decision.
