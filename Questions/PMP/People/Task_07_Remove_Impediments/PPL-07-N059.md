# Question

A developer on a govtech scrum team hits a blocker mid-sprint: she needs a firewall rule changed by the network team, who have a published two-day turnaround. She raises it in the daily scrum. The Scrum Master knows the request usually clears within the stated time, and there is enough sprint left to absorb the two days. What is the most appropriate response?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the request has been correctly logged with the network team, note it on the impediment board, and let the published two-day process run — escalating only if it exceeds the turnaround.
- **B**: Contact the network team's manager now to ask that the change be expedited ahead of their queue, given the developer is blocked.
- **C**: Reorder the developer's work onto a story that does not need the firewall change so she stays productive, and pick the blocked story back up once access lands.
- **D**: Ask the network team's lead directly whether this specific rule could be turned around faster than the standard two days, to shorten the wait.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The owning process is working within its published service level and there is slack to absorb it, so the proportionate response is to confirm the request is in correctly, make it visible, and let the two-day turnaround run, holding escalation in reserve for an actual breach (A). (B) asking the manager to jump the queue spends goodwill on a request that is tracking normally and reserves a lever for which there is no cause yet. (C) reordering her work is a reasonable way to avoid idle time and may well be done alongside, but as the primary response it treats the symptom and is unnecessary when the dependency is on course to clear within the sprint. (D) pressing the lead to beat the standard turnaround applies pressure with no justification while the process is performing to its stated time. The fine margin is between letting a healthy process run with monitoring (A) and reaching prematurely for expediting or workaround moves (B, C, D) that the situation does not yet warrant.
