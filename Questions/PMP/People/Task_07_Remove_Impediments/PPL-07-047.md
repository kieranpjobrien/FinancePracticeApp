# Question

A niche vendor supplying a specialised component for your project has just filed for insolvency protection. Deliveries are paused and their creditors are circling. Your team is two months away from the integration milestone that depends on this component. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Wait and see how the insolvency process unfolds over the coming weeks, since many companies continue supplying through protection without major customer disruption in practice.
- **B**: Cancel the current contract with immediate effect and sue the vendor for damages on the basis of the missed delivery obligation to recover project costs.
- **C**: Switch internal effort to unrelated backlog items and postpone the integration milestone until such time as the insolvency proceedings are clearly resolved.
- **D**: Activate the contingency plan: confirm status with administrators, accelerate evaluation of the backup supplier, engage legal and procurement, and surface the risk to the sponsor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects a pre-planned risk response to be activated immediately when the triggering event occurs, in parallel with legal and governance engagement. (D) covers all four. (A) relies on hope when the risk is materialising. (B) jumps to legal action before the administrators' intent is known. (C) delays the milestone without resolving the supply issue.
