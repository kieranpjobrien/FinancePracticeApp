# Question

A partnership data-sharing agreement has been stuck in the partner's legal review for nine weeks, and integration work is paused waiting for it. Your legal team has resolved all queries they have received. The partner's legal department is silent despite regular follow-ups. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Pause all integration work indefinitely and treat the partner's legal review timeline as a fixed external constraint that the project has to accept without further pushback.
- **B**: Sign a non-binding memorandum of understanding with the partner's business owner instead of the legal agreement, so integration work can resume despite the legal review.
- **C**: Draft a back-up technical integration that routes around the data-sharing altogether so the project can ship without any agreement at all and revisit the partnership later.
- **D**: Engage the partner's business sponsor with a summary of the impact, request legal-to-legal joint working with a defined target date, and adjust scope transparently if resolution slips.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI escalates through the relationship, asks for legal-to-legal working with a date, and adjusts scope transparently rather than accepting silence, shortcutting the agreement, or routing around it. (D) does all three. (A) absorbs a preventable delay. (B) signs informally for a data-sharing arrangement that genuinely needs legal cover. (C) changes the scope materially to avoid a legal blockage that can still be resolved.
