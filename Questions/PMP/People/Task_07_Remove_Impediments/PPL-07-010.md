# Question

Three senior stakeholders are bypassing the Product Owner and dropping urgent requests directly on developers' desks. The developers feel they cannot say no. The team cites this as their biggest impediment. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish clear request channels with the stakeholders and redirect direct asks through the Product Owner.
- **B**: Coach the developers to firmly decline any request that does not come through the formal channels.
- **C**: Assign one developer as a rotating "stakeholder concierge" each sprint to absorb all interruptions.
- **D**: Accept stakeholder responsiveness as part of the developer role and adjust velocity expectations downward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI addresses recurring interruptions by setting structure and protecting the team while preserving stakeholder service. (A) does both. (B) puts the burden on individual developers in a power-imbalanced dynamic. (C) protects most of the team but burns out one person and does not change stakeholder behaviour. (D) accepts an avoidable productivity loss.
