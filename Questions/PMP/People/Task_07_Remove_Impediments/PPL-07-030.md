# Question

You have noticed that the same handful of impediments keeps recurring across your project and at least three other projects in the portfolio. Each project has been resolving them locally. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue resolving the issues within your own project and let other PMs handle theirs separately.
- **B**: Document the pattern internally but take no further action to avoid being seen as overstepping.
- **C**: Send a sharp note to the responsible support functions demanding they fix the underlying causes.
- **D**: Raise the pattern at the PMO with consolidated data so systemic causes can be addressed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI views recurring cross-project impediments as candidates for systemic improvement at the right governance level. (D) drives change at the level where the cause lives. (A) duplicates effort across the portfolio. (B) is passive on a known and avoidable loss. (C) is confrontational and is unlikely to produce sustainable change.
