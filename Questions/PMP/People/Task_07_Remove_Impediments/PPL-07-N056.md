# Question

On a predictive systems-integration project, a team member mentions that the test-data migration "might run into trouble" next month: the source-system owner has hinted at an upgrade that could change the data format, though no date or scope is confirmed. The migration is not due to start for several weeks. How should the project manager handle this right now?

## Options

- **A**: Record it as a risk, assess probability and impact, assign an owner, and proactively confirm the source system's upgrade timing and scope so it can be managed before it could become a blocker.
- **B**: Log it on the impediment list and flag it to the sponsor now so the dependency on the source-system owner is visible at the right level.
- **C**: Note it informally and revisit it at the next planning checkpoint, by which time the upgrade is more likely to be confirmed and can be acted on with real information.
- **D**: Design the migration defensively now so it tolerates a format change, removing the team's exposure regardless of whether the upgrade happens.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

This is an uncertain future event, not a current blocker, so it belongs in the risk process — assessed, owned, and responded to — and the most useful response is to confirm the upgrade's timing and scope so it can be managed before it ever bites (A). (B) putting it on the impediment list and escalating to the sponsor misclassifies an unconfirmed possibility as an active blocker and spends sponsor attention on something not yet real. (C) deferring to the next checkpoint is defensible but passive — it forfeits the chance to confirm the upgrade and shape it now, while waiting for the uncertainty to resolve itself. (D) building tolerance for a format change may be wise contingency, but committing engineering effort to it before the risk is even assessed could be premature investment against an event that may not occur. The fine margin is between managing the uncertainty properly through the risk process (A) and either over-reacting (B/D) or under-reacting (C).
