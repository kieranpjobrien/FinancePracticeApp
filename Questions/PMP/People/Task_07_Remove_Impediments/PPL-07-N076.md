# Question

An agile team integrating a third-party mapping service is blocked because the vendor's sandbox environment has been down for two days. The Scrum Master raised a support ticket yesterday and received an automated acknowledgement but no human response, and the team remains blocked. What should the Scrum Master do next?

## Options

- **A**: Wait another day for the vendor's standard support process to respond before taking further action.
- **B**: Escalate through the vendor's account or technical contact, citing the ticket and the delivery impact, to get the sandbox restored urgently.
- **C**: Have the team build against a locally stubbed version of the mapping service and integrate once the sandbox returns.
- **D**: Raise the outage at the next sprint review so stakeholders understand why the increment may slip.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A ticket that has produced only an automated reply while the team stays blocked has stalled, so the right next step is to escalate through the vendor's account or technical contact, referencing the ticket and the delivery impact, to drive an urgent fix (B) — actively pursuing the blocker rather than waiting on a process that is not moving. (A) waiting another day repeats what has already failed to help. (C) stubbing locally is a reasonable parallel tactic to keep some work flowing, but it does not restore the actual sandbox the integration ultimately needs and so is secondary to getting the vendor to act. (D) informing stakeholders at review is communication, not resolution, and is too late to help the current blocker. The margin is escalating to the vendor to remove the obstacle now (B) rather than waiting (A) or only coping (C).
