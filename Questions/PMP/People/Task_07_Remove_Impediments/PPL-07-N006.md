# Question

On a hybrid health-records project, the team is repeatedly stalled waiting on clinical decisions from a senior consultant who is genuinely available only one afternoon a week. Stories accumulate in a "waiting for clinical input" state and cycle time has roughly doubled. The product owner acknowledges the problem but says the consultant "is just very busy" and is reluctant to push. What is the best way for the project manager to address this impediment?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team prepare each pending decision as a tightly framed, pre-analysed options paper so the consultant can clear a week's worth of decisions quickly in the single weekly afternoon.
- **B**: Help the product owner make the case — using cycle-time data and the cost of the queue — for a delegated clinical proxy or named deputy who can make routine calls between the consultant's weekly sessions.
- **C**: Re-order upcoming work so that, wherever feasible, clinically dependent stories are batched and timed to land just before the weekly afternoon, reducing how long each one waits.
- **D**: Introduce a short daily decision triage with the consultant for only the highest-impact pending items, accepting that lower-priority decisions still wait for the weekly slot.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

None of these is a throwaway — each genuinely improves the situation, and several could be combined. But the constraint is structural: a single overloaded approver with one afternoon a week. A durable fix has to add decision capacity, which means securing a proxy or deputy — and because the product owner owns the relationship and is hesitant, the PM's real job is to equip and support them to make that case (B). (A) is excellent practice and will get more out of each session, but it optimises within the one-afternoon ceiling rather than lifting it, so the bottleneck remains. (C) is sound flow management and reduces wait time, yet it too works around the constraint instead of removing it. (D) adds capacity for top items but asks an already-stretched consultant for daily time, which the PO says is unrealistic, and it leaves everything else queued — a partial fix at best. The distinguishing move is the one that structurally increases decision throughput and works through the relationship owner.
