# Question

Your lead architect, who holds unique knowledge of the core platform design, has just handed in her resignation after being poached by a competitor. Her notice period is four weeks. The team is mid-build on the platform she designed. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Build a structured four-week handover with paired work, decision documentation, knowledge capture interviews, and a succession conversation with two credible internal candidates identified ahead of her departure.
- **B**: Make an aggressive counter-offer above the market rate to keep her in post, on the basis that her unique knowledge makes almost any retention cost financially justified.
- **C**: Pause the platform build entirely until a replacement architect is hired externally, on the basis that continuing without her unique knowledge is too risky.
- **D**: Escalate to the CEO to formally contest the competitor's recruitment approach as being out of line with the industry's informal no-poaching norms across rival firms.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI responds to key-person impediment with a structured handover, succession planning, and knowledge capture during the notice period, rather than reactive retention, a halt, or industry escalation. (A) does that. (B) addresses symptoms, often fails, and sets a damaging retention precedent. (C) halts work unnecessarily when four weeks of handover is available. (D) is outside the PM's remit and unlikely to reverse the decision.
