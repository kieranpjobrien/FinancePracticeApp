# Question

A regulator has issued guidance that could be read as either permitting or prohibiting a core capability of your platform. The team is uncertain how to proceed and has stopped implementation pending clarification. The regulator's published response window is up to ninety days. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue full implementation while waiting, assuming the most favourable regulatory interpretation and planning to rework if the guidance turns out to be adverse later on.
- **B**: Stop all related work entirely for ninety days and shift all team capacity to completely unrelated backlog items, then restart once written clarification eventually arrives.
- **C**: Choose the more conservative interpretation and build only against that reading, without engaging the regulator or seeking any formal confirmation of the chosen stance.
- **D**: Engage the compliance function and legal counsel, request expedited regulator clarification, and split the backlog so unaffected work proceeds while contentious items are paused.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI separates the compliant path from the ambiguous path, keeps unblocked work moving, and engages the regulator formally rather than guessing. (D) does all three. (A) builds risk into the backlog by assuming favourable interpretation. (B) halts far more work than needed and wastes team capacity. (C) picks a private interpretation without compliance or legal engagement.
