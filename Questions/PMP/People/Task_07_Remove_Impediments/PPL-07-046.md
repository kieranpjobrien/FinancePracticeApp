# Question

The shared legacy mainframe that hosts your test data is down intermittently due to unplanned patching by another department. Two of your test sprints have already been disrupted. Infrastructure says nothing can be done until next quarter's planning cycle completes. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage infrastructure leadership directly with the delivery impact quantified, propose short-term mitigations, and request a specific date for a stable environment window.
- **B**: Accept the disruption as the reality of shared infrastructure and absorb the lost sprint capacity into the project baseline through a straightforward schedule extension.
- **C**: Direct the team to run tests only during periods when patching is unlikely, even if this shifts work to irregular late-evening and weekend working hours.
- **D**: Request budget to provision a brand-new dedicated test mainframe environment exclusively for your project, separating from the shared platform entirely going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to surface live delivery impact with data and negotiate a specific resolution with the right owner, not to absorb, burn out the team, or over-spend. (A) does that. (B) silently absorbs a preventable loss. (C) shifts the impediment onto the team's working pattern. (D) is disproportionate capital spending when dialogue and scheduling changes may resolve the issue.
