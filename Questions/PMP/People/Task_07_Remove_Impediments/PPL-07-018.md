# Question

Approvals from another department routinely take three to four weeks. The team has raised it as their biggest recurring impediment. Past escalations have not moved the dial. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate again to the same executive and ask them to demand immediate action.
- **B**: Stop seeking approvals from the department where possible and rely on internal sign-off instead.
- **C**: Investigate the root cause of the delays and engage the department in joint improvement options.
- **D**: Add four-week buffers everywhere a dependency on that department exists in the plan.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI prefers root-cause work and joint problem-solving over repeated escalation, bypassing, or buffer padding. (C) addresses the cause and brings the other department in as a partner. (A) repeats a tactic that has already failed. (B) creates compliance and governance risk. (D) hides the cost rather than reducing it.
