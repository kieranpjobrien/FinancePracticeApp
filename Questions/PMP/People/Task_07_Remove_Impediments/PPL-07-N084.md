# Question

An agile insurance-claims platform team consistently finishes coding fast but stories then queue for days awaiting a single specialist who handles all security reviews; the cumulative-flow diagram shows work piling up at exactly that step. The specialist is conscientious but is the only person qualified, and the queue is the team's dominant source of cycle-time variability. As the Scrum Master, which TWO actions most directly and durably attack this impediment? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Arrange for the specialist to pair with and progressively upskill one or two team members so security review is no longer dependent on a single person.
- **B**: Impose a work-in-progress limit on the steps feeding the review so the team stops overloading the bottleneck and surfaces the constraint explicitly.
- **C**: Bring in a contract security reviewer for the next few sprints to share the load while the queue is at its worst.
- **D**: Reorder the workflow so security review runs in parallel with later coding, smoothing how work arrives at the specialist.
- **E**: Have developers self-certify low-risk changes against a checklist and route only higher-risk changes to the specialist.

## Answer

A, E

## Explanation

The constraint is a single-person bottleneck consuming all review demand, so the durable fixes either expand capacity at the constraint permanently (A, upskilling removes the single point of dependency) or reduce demand on it intelligently (E, routing only higher-risk work to the specialist). (B) a WIP limit makes the bottleneck visible and stops pile-up, but on its own it throttles the whole team to the specialist's pace without enlarging or relieving the constraint, so it manages rather than removes the impediment. (C) a contract reviewer relieves the queue only while funded and leaves the team back at a single point of dependency once the contract ends, so it is temporary rather than durable. (D) resequencing changes when work hits the specialist but not the total review capacity, so the queue re-forms. The strongest pair durably grows and offloads the constraint itself.
