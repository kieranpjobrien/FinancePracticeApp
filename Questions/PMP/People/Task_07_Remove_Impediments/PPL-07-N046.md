# Question

At the daily scrum on a logistics-software team, a developer says he has been stuck for a day waiting on a paid licence for a testing tool. He adds that a teammate has a spare seat on her licence he could borrow today, but he is unsure whether sharing it complies with the vendor's terms. The licence is needed for several upcoming stories, not just his current one. As Scrum Master, what is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Take ownership of getting his own licence provisioned through the proper channel today, and check whether borrowing the teammate's seat in the meantime is permitted before he relies on it.
- **B**: Have him borrow the teammate's spare seat straight after the stand-up so he is unblocked immediately, and sort out a permanent licence afterwards.
- **C**: Raise the licence request through the procurement channel and ask him to continue on other tasks until it comes through.
- **D**: Add the licence shortage to the impediment board and bring it to the next backlog refinement, since several upcoming stories will need it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The blocker affects multiple upcoming stories, so the durable fix is to get his own licence provisioned now — but the offered workaround carries a compliance question the Scrum Master should resolve before letting him depend on it, so A pairs the real fix with appropriate diligence. (B) reaches for the fastest unblock but commits him to a licence-sharing arrangement of uncertain legitimacy, which could create a vendor-compliance problem. (C) provisioning is right, but parking him on other tasks needlessly leaves him blocked when a legitimate bridge might be available. (D) deferring to refinement treats a current, recurring-impact blocker as a future planning item and leaves him idle today. The fine margin is between unblocking responsibly (A) and unblocking fastest at the cost of an unverified compliance shortcut (B).
