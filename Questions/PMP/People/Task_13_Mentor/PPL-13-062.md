# Question

A mentee has become increasingly reliant on the mentor, now pinging daily with small questions between formal sessions. The mentor's time is being consumed and the mentee is no longer building their own judgement on small decisions. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Answer as quickly as ever and absorb the time cost, since mentoring means being available and eventually the mentee will find their own feet.
- **B**: Reply to every ping with a redirecting coaching question rather than an answer so the mentee is repeatedly prompted to think things through themselves.
- **C**: Stop replying to between-session messages and direct the mentee to bring all topics to the scheduled sessions, re-establishing the original mentoring rhythm.
- **D**: Name the pattern with the mentee, co-design which decisions they own independently, and agree when direct contact between sessions is genuinely warranted.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's coaching-stance principle supports the mentee's growing independence by agreeing explicit boundaries and decision-ownership together — this builds judgement and protects both parties' time. (A) enables over-dependence. (B) coaches everything into coaching questions in a way that can feel withholding rather than developmental. (C) imposes a boundary without agreement or coaching.
