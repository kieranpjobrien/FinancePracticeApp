# Question

A PM is mentoring a team lead from a culture in which openly questioning a senior stakeholder is considered disrespectful. The team lead has a well-founded concern that a deadline the sponsor has just set is unachievable, but says they cannot raise it with the sponsor. The PM wants to help the team lead develop here while respecting how they see the situation. What is the best mentoring approach?

## Options

- **A**: Explore with the team lead ways of putting the concern that feel respectful to them — framing it as questions, or presenting options for the sponsor to weigh — and rehearse that approach together.
- **B**: Help the team lead route the concern through a channel their norms permit, such as briefing the sponsor's deputy or raising it in writing, so the deadline gets challenged without a direct confrontation they will not make.
- **C**: Work with the team lead to assemble the schedule evidence and put the unachievable deadline to the sponsor jointly, with the PM present, so the team lead sees the concern raised respectfully before doing it themselves.
- **D**: Focus the session on what the team lead can control — building a realistic schedule and surfacing the risk through the project's reporting — and develop their upward-challenge skill on a lower-stakes issue later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Each option respects the team lead's norms and gets the concern dealt with somehow, so none is a throwaway and the margin is genuinely fine. (A) develops the capability the team lead most needs — voicing a concern upward in a way that is both effective and authentic to them — and rehearsal makes a daunting step achievable, which is the heart of the mentoring task. (B) is pragmatic and culturally sensitive, but routing around the team lead builds a workaround rather than the skill, leaving the same block in place next time. (C) models the behaviour and gives a real-stakes example, yet doing it jointly keeps the team lead in the PM's shadow on the very exchange they need to own, risking dependence. (D) is reasonable risk management and sequences the stretch, but parking the upward challenge concedes the development point the situation is offering and leaves the live deadline unaddressed by the team lead. (A) is best because it builds the durable capability now, on the real issue, without overriding who the team lead is.
