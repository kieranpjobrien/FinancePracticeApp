# Question

A programme sponsor has asked the PM to help them prepare for a board presentation. The sponsor is new to the board, anxious, and wants the PM to review the deck and role-play questions. The PM has done this before but has never formally been in a mentoring relationship with the sponsor. What is BEST?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the support informally, since formalising the relationship would add bureaucracy and the sponsor's request is a one-off practical need.
- **B**: Decline on the grounds that mentoring upward to a senior stakeholder is not part of the PM's role and may cross authority boundaries.
- **C**: Hand the sponsor over to an executive coaching service, since this request is closer to coaching than the mentoring the PM would normally provide.
- **D**: Provide the support while being explicit about purpose, scope and confidentiality, so both parties are clear on what this engagement is and is not.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI-aligned mentoring is grounded in explicit purpose, scope and confidentiality regardless of formality — even an "informal" engagement benefits from the frame. (A) leaves expectations ambiguous and exposes both parties. (B) refuses a legitimate stakeholder request the PM can fulfil. (C) outsources something the PM can genuinely help with and slows the sponsor down.
