# Question

A PM is about to begin formally mentoring a high-potential analyst identified through a talent programme. The PM wants the relationship to be effective from the outset. Which of the following will most help establish it well in the first session or two? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: Agree clear development goals the mentee owns, and a way to revisit progress against them regularly.
- **B**: Establish confidentiality so the mentee can speak openly about struggles and uncertainties.
- **C**: Set a regular, protected cadence of sessions that both parties commit to keeping.
- **D**: Map out a full curriculum of topics and an end date now, so the relationship has a defined scope from day one.
- **E**: Agree that the PM will introduce the mentee to senior contacts in the PM's network early, to accelerate their visibility.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The foundations of effective mentoring are mentee-owned goals (A), psychological safety through confidentiality (B), and a reliable rhythm of contact (C) — together they give the relationship direction, openness, and consistency from the start. (D) sounds disciplined but fixing a complete curriculum and end date up front pre-empts the mentee's emerging needs and converts a developmental relationship into a syllabus; scope should follow the goals, not precede them. (E) describes sponsorship — a real and valuable thing — but front-loading network introductions before goals and trust exist can pressure an unready mentee and skips the developmental work; it is a later by-product of a good relationship, not a way to establish one. The trap is that D and E both look constructive.
