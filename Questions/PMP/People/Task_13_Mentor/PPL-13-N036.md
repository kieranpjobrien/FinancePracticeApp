# Question

A PM mentoring several first-time scrum masters notices recurring habits across them. The PM wants to coach the individuals toward growth rather than simply correcting tasks. For which of the following observed behaviours is a mentoring conversation that builds the scrum master's own insight the most appropriate primary response? (Select three.)

## Options

- **A**: A scrum master is solving every team impediment personally instead of helping the team build its own problem-solving muscle.
- **B**: A scrum master is acting as a status reporter in standups, turning them into a manager update rather than a team sync.
- **C**: A scrum master shipped a defect to production by skipping the agreed regulated sign-off step, breaching a compliance control.
- **D**: A scrum master avoids surfacing team conflict in retrospectives because they find confrontation uncomfortable.
- **E**: A scrum master has misconfigured access permissions in the team's tooling, exposing client data.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Behaviours rooted in mindset, habit, or skill — over-owning impediments (A), misframing standups (B), and avoiding conflict (D) — are precisely where mentoring that builds self-insight develops lasting capability. (C) is a compliance breach with regulatory consequences that requires immediate corrective and governance action first; coaching the underlying habit comes later, not as the primary response. (E) is a live data-exposure incident demanding prompt remediation, not a reflective conversation in the moment. Mentoring grows judgement, but it does not substitute for acting on control breaches and security incidents.
