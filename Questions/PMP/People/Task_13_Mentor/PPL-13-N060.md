# Question

A formal twelve-month mentoring relationship between a project manager and a now-experienced agile coach is reaching its agreed end, with the mentee's development goals met. As the PM brings the relationship to a healthy close, which actions are appropriate? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Review progress against the original goals together and acknowledge what the mentee has achieved.
- **B**: Discuss how the mentee will keep developing and where they might find support going forward.
- **C**: Keep the formal arrangement open indefinitely regardless of goals, so the mentee never feels cut off.
- **D**: Invite honest feedback on the mentoring itself so the PM can improve how they mentor others.
- **E**: Insist the relationship stop completely on the final day, with no further contact, to mark a clean break.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

A healthy closure reviews achievement against the agreed goals (A), plans the mentee's ongoing growth and sources of future support (B), and gathers feedback so the mentor improves for others (D). (C) is wrong because keeping a formal arrangement open indefinitely once goals are met fosters dependence and denies both parties a purposeful conclusion. (E) is wrong because a rigid, total cut-off is needlessly harsh; good closure usually allows the relationship to ease into peer or occasional contact rather than ending abruptly.
