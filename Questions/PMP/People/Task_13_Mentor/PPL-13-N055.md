# Question

A project manager has set aside thirty minutes for a mentoring session with a junior team member who has been struggling with a difficult stakeholder. As the session begins, the mentee starts explaining the situation. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Listen actively to understand the mentee's situation and how they see it before offering any guidance.
- **B**: Ask the mentee at the outset what they want from the session, so the limited time is aimed at the outcome they need.
- **C**: Share early that the PM has handled a similar stakeholder, to reassure the mentee the problem is solvable and that the PM understands it.
- **D**: Start by suggesting a couple of concrete tactics the mentee could try, then refine them as the mentee reacts.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Mentoring starts with genuinely understanding the mentee's situation and perspective, and active listening is what makes any later guidance relevant and trusted. (B) is a reasonable way to focus a short session, but setting the goal before hearing the situation risks aiming at the wrong outcome; understanding comes first. (C) is meant to reassure yet recentres the opening on the mentor's story before the mentee's own account is heard. (D) jumps to solutions before the real issue and the mentee's view of it are understood, which is exactly what listening first prevents.
