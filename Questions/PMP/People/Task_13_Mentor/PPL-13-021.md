# Question

A project manager has been informally mentoring a stakeholder from another department. The stakeholder asks the project manager to advocate for their promotion with their manager. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Advocate for the promotion since they have direct knowledge of the stakeholder's capabilities.
- **B**: Politely refuse involvement in another department's personnel decisions altogether.
- **C**: Tell the stakeholder honestly whether the project manager believes they are ready.
- **D**: Clarify the boundaries and offer a written reference or specific feedback if the stakeholder wants one.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Mentoring relationships need clear, honest boundaries. (D) gives meaningful support (a reference, structured feedback) without overstepping into political advocacy across departments. (A) presumes a level of advocacy the project manager may not legitimately have. (B) is unnecessarily cold; useful, bounded help exists. (C) is a personal judgement that may not be the project manager's place to make.
