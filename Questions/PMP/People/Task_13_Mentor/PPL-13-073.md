# Question

A PM mentors several stakeholders across an agile transformation programme. In each situation below, the mentee needs a different developmental response from the PM. Match each mentee situation (left) to the most appropriate mentoring response (right).

## Items

- **1**: A capable team lead keeps asking the PM to confirm decisions that are clearly within their authority and competence.
- **2**: A junior analyst is about to attempt a stakeholder presentation for the first time and is visibly anxious.
- **3**: A product owner insists their backlog-refinement approach is working, but the PM has clear evidence the team is repeatedly blocked by unclear stories.

## Matches

- **A**: Prepare and rehearse the task together beforehand to build readiness and confidence.
- **B**: Redirect the question back with "what are you leaning toward and why" to build independent judgement.
- **C**: Calmly present the evidence and ask them to view the pattern from the team's perspective so they reconsider it themselves.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Each mentee needs the response matched to whether the gap is dependence, readiness, or denial. Situation 1 is a capable person over-relying on the mentor, so (B) redirects them to their own reasoning to rebuild independence rather than feeding the dependency. Situation 2 is a first-time stretch where anxiety, not capability, is the barrier, so (A) preparation and rehearsal makes a daunting task achievable. Situation 3 is resistance to sound feedback, so (C) presents the evidence without escalating and shifts perspective so the product owner reaches the insight themselves — telling them harder would only deepen the defensiveness. Swapping any pairing misreads the mentee's actual need: rehearsing with the capable lead would patronise, and redirecting the anxious first-timer to "what would you do" offers no scaffold when they most need one.
