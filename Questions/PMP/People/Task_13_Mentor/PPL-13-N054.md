# Question

A project manager mentoring several stakeholders faces different situations in the same week. Match each situation to the mentoring response that best fits it.

## Items

- **1**: A capable mentee keeps asking the PM to confirm decisions they are clearly equipped to make alone.
- **2**: A mentee is about to make a choice the PM believes will backfire, but the decision is genuinely theirs to make.
- **3**: A mentee lacks a specific technical skill the PM has and has asked to learn it quickly.

## Matches

- **A**: Ask questions that surface the likely consequences so the mentee can reconsider before deciding.
- **B**: Demonstrate the skill and then have the mentee practise it with feedback.
- **C**: Hand the decision back and encourage them to act on their own judgement.

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Mentoring responses should fit the need: an over-reliant but capable mentee needs the decision handed back to build confidence (1-C); a mentee heading toward a poor but legitimate choice needs their thinking challenged through consequence-focused questions, not an override (2-A); and a genuine skill gap is best closed by demonstration plus guided practice (3-B). Matching the wrong response — for example coaching by questions when a concrete skill is needed — would slow the mentee's development.
