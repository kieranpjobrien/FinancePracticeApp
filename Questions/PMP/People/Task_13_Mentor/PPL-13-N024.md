# Question

A PM is mentoring a Scrum Master who, facing a tight commercial deadline, has begun assigning specific sprint tasks to named developers during planning to remove the time the team spends self-allocating. Two sprints in, the forecast looks stable but the developers have stopped raising alternatives in planning and one has privately told the Scrum Master the team feels managed. The Scrum Master tells the PM the approach is "working" and asks whether to keep it. How should the PM respond in this mentoring session?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm that pre-assigning tasks conflicts with the developers' accountability for self-organisation and advise the Scrum Master to return allocation to the team.
- **B**: Ask the Scrum Master to weigh the deadline gain against the developer's feedback and the drop in alternatives, and reason out with them what each signal is telling them.
- **C**: Suggest the Scrum Master keep the approach until this deadline passes, then revert to self-allocation once the commercial pressure is off.
- **D**: Encourage the Scrum Master to put the question to the team directly in the next retrospective and let them decide how planning should run.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Scrum Master has framed the situation around one signal — the forecast — while two others (the "feels managed" feedback, the loss of alternatives) point the other way; the mentoring move is to develop their ability to read competing signals rather than to hand them a verdict. (B) does that. (A) is defensible and may even be the eventual conclusion, but supplying it as a ruling skips the judgement the Scrum Master needs to build and treats a genuine trade-off as a rule violation. (C) endorses the short-term fix without testing the Scrum Master's reasoning, and "revert later" rarely happens once a habit sets. (D) is a sound facilitation step the Scrum Master could take, but outsourcing the decision to the team now lets the Scrum Master avoid forming their own read of the evidence in front of them, which is what this session is for.
