# Question

A PM uses the GROW model to mentor an agile delivery lead. The lead has set a clear goal — sprint reviews that genuinely engage stakeholders — and has talked at length and accurately about everything blocking it: stakeholders skim the demo, feedback comes late, attendance drifts. They have named three or four options they could try and seem to understand each. But the conversation keeps circling back to how difficult the obstacles are, and the session is nearly over. What should the mentor do now?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the lead which option they will commit to trying in the next sprint and by when, and what support they need to follow through.
- **B**: Help the lead probe the obstacles further to be sure they have fully understood what is driving the poor engagement.
- **C**: Revisit the goal to check that engaging stakeholders is genuinely the right outcome to be pursuing.
- **D**: Offer the lead a couple of additional options they may not have considered for lifting engagement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The lead has effectively completed Goal, Reality, and Options: the goal is clear, the situation is well understood, and several viable options are on the table. The session is stalling not for want of analysis but because it has not moved to commitment — the "Will" stage. Pushing for a specific, time-bound action and the support to deliver it (A) is what converts insight into change. (B) deepens Reality the lead has already explored thoroughly, which is what is causing the circling. (C) reopens a goal that is already clear and agreed, sending the conversation backward. (D) adds to Options when the lead is not short of options — more choices here risk decision paralysis. The skill being tested is reading where a coaching conversation has got to and moving it forward, not reciting the stages.
