# Question

A project manager is mentoring a mid-career engineer who has been passed over for promotion twice. In sessions, the mentee increasingly vents and complains about leadership rather than engaging with growth topics. The pattern has continued for three months. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Name the pattern kindly, reset the purpose of the relationship, and check whether the mentee wants mentoring or would be better served elsewhere.
- **B**: Continue to listen patiently on the basis that emotional processing is itself a valuable form of support for a mentee struggling in their career.
- **C**: Pivot the sessions into informal performance coaching so the mentee's underlying career issues are tackled through mentoring rather than their line manager.
- **D**: End the mentoring relationship immediately without explanation, on the grounds that it has become unproductive and both parties' time is being wasted.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring principles expect the mentor to name and reset when purpose drifts — this respects the mentee's agency and keeps both parties' time in service of real growth. (B) confuses sustained venting with support and leaves the mentee stuck. (C) steps over the line manager's role and blurs mentoring with performance management. (D) ends the relationship without the honest conversation that might have repaired it.
