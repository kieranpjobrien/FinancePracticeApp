# Question

In a mentoring session, a mentee casually describes a workaround on their own project that would let them miss a regulatory step to hit a tight deadline. They are not asking for advice — just venting about schedule pressure. The PM recognises the step is genuinely mandatory. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop the venting and directly address the compliance issue with the mentee immediately, making clear that skipping the regulatory step is not acceptable.
- **B**: Stay in listening mode in the session and address the compliance issue in a follow-up email once the mentee has finished processing their schedule frustration.
- **C**: Report the potential compliance breach to the mentee's project's compliance function without first raising it with the mentee, since the regulation is mandatory.
- **D**: Keep listening without comment, since the mentee is only venting and may not actually proceed with the workaround they briefly mentioned in session.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics treats compliance as non-negotiable — when a clear breach surfaces in any conversation, the PMI-aligned move is to address it immediately and clearly with the person. (B) delays a compliance intervention via email when an in-session conversation is available. (C) bypasses the mentee without first giving them the chance to course-correct themselves. (D) stays silent on a known regulatory breach.
