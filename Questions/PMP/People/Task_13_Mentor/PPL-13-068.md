# Question

A PM runs a quarterly mentoring cohort of six early-career PMs. Over three sessions, one cohort member has started dominating discussion while two others have contributed very little. The PM wants the cohort dynamic to serve everyone. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Redesign cohort sessions with structured turn-taking, breakouts and written reflections, and privately coach the dominant member on creating more space for others.
- **B**: Speak privately with the dominant member and ask them to create more space for others, then continue the existing cohort format otherwise unchanged for the group.
- **C**: Split the cohort into two smaller groups so the dominant member can no longer monopolise discussion and the quieter members have a simpler group to contribute in.
- **D**: Introduce the topic of airtime balance openly in the next cohort session so the group collectively agrees new norms about participation and contribution from everyone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI-aligned facilitation combines structural redesign with a private conversation — the format does most of the work, and coaching the dominant member handles what the format alone cannot. (B) relies on one conversation to fix a structural issue. (C) hides the issue by splitting the cohort. (D) opens a sensitive topic publicly before a private conversation and risks embarrassing the dominant member.
