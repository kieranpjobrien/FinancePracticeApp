# Question

A project manager has just begun a formal mentoring arrangement with a junior PM working on an agile delivery team. The PM wants the relationship to be genuinely developmental rather than directive. Which of the following actions support effective mentoring here? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Agree clear, mentee-owned development goals and revisit them periodically to check progress.
- **B**: Use open questions to help the mentee reach their own conclusions before offering the PM's own view.
- **C**: Make the mentee's key project decisions for them so their outcomes stay strong while they learn.
- **D**: Create a confidential space where the mentee can raise mistakes and uncertainties without fear of judgement.
- **E**: Report the content of each mentoring session to the mentee's line manager so progress is visible to the organisation.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Effective mentoring rests on mentee-owned goals (A), drawing out the mentee's own thinking through questions (B), and a confidential, psychologically safe relationship (D). (C) undermines development by removing the decisions the mentee should be learning to make. (E) breaks the confidentiality that makes honest disclosure possible and turns mentoring into surveillance, which corrodes trust.
