# Question

A PM mentors a capable product owner who can articulate the product's outcome metrics well in their sessions but, in front of a forceful business sponsor who pushes feature requests, keeps accepting items without testing them against those metrics. The product owner tells the PM they "freeze" in those meetings and know they should push back but cannot in the moment. What is the most useful focus for the next mentoring session?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner walk through how the last few accepted requests moved the metrics, so the gap between intent and outcome becomes visible in their own analysis.
- **B**: Work with the product owner on how to hold a value conversation under pressure — surfacing what they want to say, the words that stick, and rehearsing the exchange with the sponsor.
- **C**: Help the product owner prepare a short metrics-based brief to send the sponsor before each meeting, so the case is made in writing before the room gets difficult.
- **D**: Offer to attend the next sponsor meeting alongside the product owner to model how to redirect a feature push toward outcomes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The product owner already understands value and can name the metrics; the failure is specific and self-reported — they freeze under a forceful sponsor. The development need is the in-the-moment capability, so rehearsing the pressured conversation (B) targets exactly what is breaking down. (A) builds analytical insight the product owner has already demonstrated they possess, so it treats the wrong gap. (C) is a sensible tactic and may help, but it routes around the skill the product owner needs to build rather than developing it, and a written brief does not survive a forceful push in the room. (D) gives a single useful demonstration but risks the product owner leaning on the PM's presence; modelling helps, yet it develops the product owner less than equipping them to carry the conversation themselves.
