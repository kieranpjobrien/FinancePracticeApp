# Question

A newly certified Scrum Master on an agile delivery team brings the project manager a fourth team disagreement this fortnight, again asking the PM to settle it. The PM mentors this Scrum Master and has limited time before a stakeholder review. The current disagreement is genuinely minor. What should the PM do during this escalation?

## Options

- **A**: Quickly give the Scrum Master the call the PM would make and move on, on the basis that a minor item does not warrant a coaching session and the PM's time is short.
- **B**: Ask the Scrum Master what options they have weighed and which best serves the team, then have them decide and act on it themselves.
- **C**: Offer to co-facilitate the team's next discussion of the issue so the Scrum Master can see the PM model the approach and then take it over.
- **D**: Resolve this one but agree with the Scrum Master that, from now on, disagreements at this level are theirs to settle and will not be escalated.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The repeated pattern, not this single minor item, is the real issue, and the developmental move is to hand the thinking back so the Scrum Master builds the judgement to stop escalating. (A) is efficient for the moment but reinforces dependence, which is the actual cost. (C) is a defensible coaching device, but modelling keeps the PM as the source of resolution for an issue minor enough that the Scrum Master could already handle it; the larger lever is to make them own the decision now. (D) sets a sensible boundary but, on its own, tells them what to stop doing without growing the capability to resolve well, so the next disagreement still finds them unequipped.
