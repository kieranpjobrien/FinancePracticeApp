# Question

A PM is starting a formal six-month mentoring arrangement with an aspiring product owner at a fintech scale-up. To set the relationship up well from the outset, which actions should the PM and mentee agree together at the first session? (Choose 3)

## Options

- **A**: The development goals the mentee wants to work towards and how they will know progress is being made.
- **B**: How confidentiality will work and what, if anything, is shared with the mentee's line manager.
- **C**: The cadence and expected duration of sessions and how either party can raise that the arrangement is not working.
- **D**: A fixed rating the PM will give the mentee at the end to feed into the mentee's annual performance review.
- **E**: A detailed list of the technical certifications the mentee must obtain before the arrangement concludes.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Effective mentoring relationships are established with a lightweight working agreement covering goals and how progress is judged (A), the confidentiality boundary including what reaches the line manager (B), and the logistics plus a way to surface if it is not working (C). (D) is wrong because mentoring is developmental and confidential, not an appraisal input — tying it to a performance rating undermines the candour the relationship depends on. (E) is wrong because it imposes a rigid external deliverable rather than mentee-owned goals, and certifications are not what a mentoring agreement is for. The three correct items establish direction, trust, and a working rhythm without turning mentoring into assessment.
