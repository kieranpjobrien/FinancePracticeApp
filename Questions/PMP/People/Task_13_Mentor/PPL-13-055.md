# Question

A PM has been mentoring a talented but abrasive junior PM for six months. The mentee has just received 360-degree feedback highlighting their abrasiveness, but is dismissing the feedback as "political." Their next career move will depend on this shifting. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Validate the mentee's frustration with the 360 process before pushing on the content, so they remain emotionally open to working on the feedback together.
- **B**: Reinforce the feedback directly and specifically with examples the mentor themselves has observed, so the mentee cannot keep dismissing the 360 as political.
- **C**: Wait a few weeks for the emotional reaction to cool down before raising the 360 content, since attempts right now will be deflected rather than absorbed.
- **D**: Stay neutral on the content of the 360 and coach the mentee through how to respond professionally to their manager's interpretation of the results.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's feedback principle is specific, behavioural and timely — the mentor's own observations grounded in examples cut through the "it's political" defence the 360 alone cannot. (A) validates the deflection before addressing it. (C) waits out a window where concrete examples are still fresh and useful. (D) avoids the hard conversation that is precisely what mentoring is for.
