# Question

What is the PRIMARY benefit of a reverse mentoring arrangement?

## Options

- **A**: It is generally faster than a traditional mentoring relationship.
- **B**: It allows junior staff to share fresh perspectives or current skills with senior staff.
- **C**: It removes the need for formal mentoring programs in the organisation.
- **D**: It reduces the senior staff's workload by transferring tasks downward.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reverse mentoring exists to flow knowledge from junior to senior, not to replace traditional mentoring or to redistribute work. (B) captures the value: junior staff often have currency in technology, methods, or culture that benefits senior staff. (A) is irrelevant; speed is not the point. (C) misframes reverse mentoring as a substitute. (D) confuses mentoring with delegation.
