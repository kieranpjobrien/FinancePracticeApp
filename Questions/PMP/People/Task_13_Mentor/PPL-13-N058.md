# Question

A project manager is mentoring a competent team member who has plateaued and seems bored. In the next iteration the team needs someone to facilitate the retrospective, a role the mentee has never done and is slightly nervous about. How can the PM best use this as a mentoring opportunity?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the retrospective themselves and let the mentee observe, so the mentee is not exposed before they feel ready.
- **B**: Encourage the mentee to facilitate it, help them prepare, and agree to debrief afterward, framing it as a supported stretch.
- **C**: Assign the facilitation to the mentee without discussion, on the basis that being thrown in is the fastest way to grow.
- **D**: Wait for the mentee to volunteer for a stretch task on their own initiative rather than steering them toward one.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stretch assignment with preparation and a debrief is a classic, effective mentoring device: it pulls a plateaued mentee into growth while keeping the challenge supported and safe. (A) removes the very stretch that would re-engage the mentee. (C) provides the challenge but no support, raising the risk of a discouraging failure. (D) leaves a disengaged mentee to languish when an active nudge from the mentor is what is needed.
