# Question

In PMI's view, what is the PRIMARY difference between mentoring and coaching?

## Options

- **A**: Mentoring focuses on long-term growth and career; coaching focuses on specific skills or performance.
- **B**: Mentoring is provided by HR; coaching is provided by the project manager.
- **C**: Mentoring applies to senior staff; coaching applies to junior staff.
- **D**: Mentoring is informal; coaching follows a structured curriculum.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The two practices share intent (developing the person) but differ in scope. (A) is the standard PMI distinction. (B) wrongly maps the practices to specific functions. (C) wrongly maps them to seniority. (D) wrongly maps them to formality; in practice both can be either formal or informal.
