# Question

A senior project manager is mentoring a junior project manager who keeps repeating the same mistakes despite repeated feedback. The junior nods and agrees in the moment but reverts to old patterns. What should the senior project manager try?

## Options

- **A**: Conclude the junior is not ready for the role and recommend reassignment.
- **B**: Switch approach and try real-time observation, role-play, or a deeper exploration of why the pattern persists.
- **C**: Toughen the feedback and make the consequences of continuing more explicit.
- **D**: Take over the work the junior is fumbling so it does not affect the project.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When the same mentoring approach has not worked, the answer is to vary the approach, not to escalate or quit. (B) tries different learning modalities and looks for the underlying driver of the pattern. (A) gives up before alternatives have been exhausted. (C) is more of the same delivered louder. (D) creates dependency and prevents the learning the junior needs.
