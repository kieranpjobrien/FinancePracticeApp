# Question

A project manager is mentoring a team lead who responds defensively to almost all feedback, treating each suggestion as criticism. The mentoring is stalling because the team lead deflects rather than reflects. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Shift to asking the team lead to self-assess each situation first, offering the PM's observations only where the team lead's own account leaves a clear gap.
- **B**: Explore with the team lead how they prefer to receive feedback and what makes it land badly, then adjust the approach and reaffirm that the intent is their growth.
- **C**: Anchor future feedback in specific observed facts and their effect rather than judgements, so there is less for the team lead to read as personal criticism.
- **D**: Name the defensiveness directly with the team lead as the pattern blocking their progress, and ask them to work on receiving feedback differently.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When feedback is not landing, the mentor's first move is to understand and adapt to how this mentee receives it and to rebuild the safety that lets them reflect rather than defend. (A) is a sound technique that lowers the threat, but it works around the defensiveness without surfacing what actually drives it. (C) likewise improves delivery and is good practice, yet it still adjusts the feedback in the dark rather than learning from the mentee what makes it land badly. (D) confronts the pattern honestly but risks triggering the very defensiveness in play; understanding the cause with the mentee usually opens reflection more reliably than labelling the behaviour.
