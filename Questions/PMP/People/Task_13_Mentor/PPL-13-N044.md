# Question

A newly appointed product owner on a hybrid programme is mentored by the project manager. The PO has filled the backlog with detailed solution specifications for items planned six months out, and the team is struggling to give estimates that mean anything. A refinement session is tomorrow. What is the best thing for the PM to do?

## Options

- **A**: Sit with the PO before the session and rework the distant items together to a lighter level, so the artefact is fixed for tomorrow and the PO sees the target state.
- **B**: Ask the PO what the team can and cannot estimate at this distance and why, and have them decide how to reshape the backlog from that conversation.
- **C**: Suggest the PO leave the backlog as it is for tomorrow and instead ask the team to give rough size bands rather than firm estimates on the far-out items.
- **D**: Point the PO to the team's definition of ready and the cadence for refining items as they near the top, and let them apply it to the backlog.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PO's gap is understanding why detail and certainty change across the backlog horizon, and mentoring works best when it builds that reasoning so the PO can keep applying it. (A) fixes tomorrow's artefact and shows the target but transfers little of the underlying judgement, leaving the PO dependent. (C) is a reasonable estimation workaround but treats the symptom for one session without addressing why the backlog was over-specified. (D) supplies the right reference and is more developmental than (A), yet handing over a standard to apply still skips the conversation that makes the principle stick; drawing out the PO's own reasoning is what closes the gap.
