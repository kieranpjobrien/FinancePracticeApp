# Question

An agile coach is mentoring a development team toward self-organisation. The team has started bringing every prioritisation and dependency question to the coach for a ruling, and decisions stall whenever the coach is unavailable. The coach wants the team to own these decisions. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Continue giving rulings for now while the team builds trust in the coach, then gradually hand decisions back as they mature.
- **B**: Set up a clear decision framework and RACI so the team knows which decisions are theirs to make without consulting the coach.
- **C**: Make the coach deliberately less available for these questions so the team is forced to decide on their own.
- **D**: When the team brings a decision, ask what options they see and how they would decide, and hold back from ruling so they practise resolving it together.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Self-organisation develops when the team practises deciding together with the coach as facilitator rather than arbiter. (D) redirects each question back to the team's own reasoning in the moment, building their collective decision-making muscle while the coach stays present to facilitate, which is the most direct way to grow the capability. (B) gives helpful clarity on authority, but a framework alone does not build the team's confidence and skill in actually making contested calls — they may still hesitate within their own remit. (C) reaches the right goal by withdrawal, but simply becoming unavailable can feel like abandonment and leaves the team to flounder without the coaching that turns the struggle into learning. (A) entrenches the dependency it should be unwinding, and "gradually later" tends to drift while the team learns the coach will always rule.
