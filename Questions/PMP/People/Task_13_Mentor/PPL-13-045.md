# Question

A project manager has long mentored a team member who has now been promoted, is performing strongly, and rarely contacts the project manager any more. The project manager feels disappointed by the reduced contact. What does this situation BEST indicate?

## Options

- **A**: The mentee is being ungrateful and should keep the relationship active.
- **B**: The relationship has likely failed; the mentee no longer values the project manager's input.
- **C**: The mentoring largely succeeded; the mentee has internalised the learning and is operating independently.
- **D**: The project manager should reinstate regular sessions to ensure the mentee continues to develop.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The aim of mentoring is independence, not enduring dependence. (C) is the right interpretation: success often looks like the mentee no longer needing the same level of support. (A) imposes the mentor's emotional needs on the mentee. (B) misreads the outcome. (D) re-creates a structure for the mentor's benefit, not the mentee's.
