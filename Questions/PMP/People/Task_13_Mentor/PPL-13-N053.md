# Question

An organisation is scaling agile across eight teams. Several experienced Scrum Masters could help the many new ones, but the project manager has no authority to assign anyone to formal one-to-one mentoring across teams. The PM wants to spread good practice and grow the newer Scrum Masters anyway. What is the most effective approach?

## Options

- **A**: Personally mentor all the new Scrum Masters one-to-one, since the PM is the most experienced person available.
- **B**: Establish a Scrum Master community of practice where experienced and new practitioners share challenges and coach each other regularly.
- **C**: Write a detailed best-practice playbook and circulate it to every team for the new Scrum Masters to follow.
- **D**: Ask leadership to mandate that each new Scrum Master be paired with a senior one before any further coaching happens.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

At scale, a community of practice multiplies mentoring by enabling peer-to-peer learning among many practitioners, which fits the PM's limited authority and the number of people involved. (A) does not scale and makes the PM a bottleneck. (C) shares explicit knowledge but provides none of the relational coaching that develops judgement. (D) stalls progress waiting on a mandate when a community can start immediately and may achieve the pairing organically.
