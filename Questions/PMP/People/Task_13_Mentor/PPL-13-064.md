# Question

A project manager has agreed to mentor a junior coordinator who is keen to grow into a project management role. They have scheduled their first regular session. To set the relationship up well, what should the PM do at the start?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the coordinator what they want to develop, how often they will meet, and what each of them expects from the relationship.
- **B**: Assess the coordinator's current skills against a project management competency framework to identify the biggest gaps to work on.
- **C**: Share the PM's own career path and the lessons learned along the way, to give the coordinator a sense of what the journey looks like.
- **D**: Ask the coordinator's line manager what development the coordinator most needs, so the mentoring targets the organisation's priorities.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A productive mentoring relationship starts by jointly establishing its purpose, cadence and mutual expectations, which gives both parties a shared basis and the mentee ownership of their development. (B) is useful input but assessing gaps before agreeing what the mentee wants risks driving the agenda from the mentor's analysis rather than the mentee's goals. (C) can build rapport and perspective, yet leading with the mentor's story before understanding the mentee's aims centres the wrong person at the outset. (D) brings a valid stakeholder view, but letting the manager define the goals before the mentee does undermines the mentee's ownership of their own growth.
