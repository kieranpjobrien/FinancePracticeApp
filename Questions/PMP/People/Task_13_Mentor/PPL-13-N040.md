# Question

A six-month mentoring relationship is reaching its agreed end. The mentee has met most of their goals but tells the PM in the penultimate session that they still feel shaky on one of them — handling difficult stakeholders — and is anxious about losing the PM's support. The PM wants to close the relationship well. What is the best way to use the final session?

## Options

- **A**: Review progress across all the goals, then work with the mentee on a concrete plan for continuing to build the stakeholder skill independently — who they can turn to, what to practise, how to track it.
- **B**: Focus the final session on the stakeholder skill specifically, coaching the mentee through it as far as possible in the time left so the gap is as small as it can be.
- **C**: Offer to extend the mentoring informally until the mentee feels confident on the stakeholder skill, since ending while they feel shaky would undo the trust built.
- **D**: Recognise the mentee's overall growth and reassure them that feeling shaky on one skill is normal and they are more capable than they think.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Closing well means consolidating the whole relationship and equipping the mentee to keep developing without the mentor — so reviewing all the goals and building a concrete self-directed plan for the remaining gap (A) both honours the progress and directly answers the mentee's anxiety with structure rather than dependence. (B) is well-intentioned but pouring the final session into one skill skips the consolidation and tries to "finish" development that is meant to continue beyond the relationship. (C) responds to the anxiety by extending, which can be the right call occasionally but here defaults to prolonging dependence rather than preparing the mentee to stand alone, and open-ended extension undermines a clean close. (D) offers warmth and useful reassurance but no plan, leaving the mentee encouraged yet without a way forward on the gap they named. The margin is fine because B, C and D each contain something reasonable; A is best because it both closes and launches.
