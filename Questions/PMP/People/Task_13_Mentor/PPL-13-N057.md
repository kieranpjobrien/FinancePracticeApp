# Question

A project manager is mentoring a junior coordinator who works in a different time zone and whom the PM has never met in person. Early sessions feel stilted and the coordinator rarely opens up. Which actions would most improve this remote mentoring relationship? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Switch the sessions to video and keep a consistent regular cadence so the relationship can build over time.
- **B**: Move all mentoring to asynchronous written messages so the coordinator can reply whenever convenient.
- **C**: Invest early sessions in getting to know each other and building rapport before pushing into development topics.
- **D**: Schedule the sessions at a fixed time that suits the PM's working day, since the PM is giving up their time.
- **E**: Keep each session short and strictly task-focused to respect the coordinator's busy schedule.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

Remote mentoring relationships are strengthened by richer, consistent contact such as regular video sessions (A) and by deliberately investing in rapport before driving hard into development (C), which is what overcomes the stilted early dynamic. (B) strips out the real-time connection that builds trust. (D) imposes the burden of inconvenient timing on the mentee and signals the relationship is on the PM's terms. (E) keeps sessions transactional, which is precisely why the coordinator is not opening up.
