# Question

During a confidential mentoring session, a mentee tells the project manager they have been quietly cutting a required safety inspection step to keep a regulated construction deliverable on schedule, and asks the PM to keep it between them as they have always done. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Honour the confidentiality of the mentoring relationship and coach the mentee to correct the practice themselves over the coming weeks.
- **B**: Explain that confidentiality cannot cover a safety and compliance breach, and ensure the issue is reported and the inspection step restored.
- **C**: Keep it confidential but quietly arrange for someone else to re-inspect the affected work so no one need know the step was skipped.
- **D**: Treat it as a coaching opportunity and ask the mentee what they think the consequences could be, leaving the decision to act with them.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mentoring confidentiality has limits: it does not extend to concealing a safety or regulatory breach, and the PM's duty to protect people and uphold compliance overrides the request for secrecy. (A) and (D) keep a genuine safety risk live while relying on the mentee to act, which is not acceptable for a regulated inspection. (C) attempts a cover-up that compounds the breach. The mentor must act on the breach while still handling the mentee with care, but secrecy is not on the table.
