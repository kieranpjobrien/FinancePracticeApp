# Question

Demand for mentoring from early-career PMs has outstripped the senior PM's available time. Five people would like regular sessions but only two fit in the calendar. The senior PM does not want to turn anyone away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the existing two and politely decline the other three, explaining capacity constraints honestly and suggesting they seek other mentors internally from the same pool.
- **B**: Accept all five as individual mentees and shorten every session to twenty minutes each so the scheduling becomes feasible in the senior PM's busy week.
- **C**: Offer a hybrid of monthly 1:1s plus a group cohort, keeping personal relationships while using the cohort to scale shared learning across the five.
- **D**: Introduce rotating mentorship where all five mentees rotate through short individual sessions on a six-week cycle to share the senior PM's available time fairly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's approach to scaling development is to combine individual and cohort forms — each serves a different learning need and together they extend capacity without gutting quality. (A) is honest but leaves three people without support. (B) shortens sessions to the point they are unlikely to be useful. (D) over-distributes personal contact and loses the continuity of individual mentoring.
