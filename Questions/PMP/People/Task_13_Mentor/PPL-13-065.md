# Question

A PM acting as agile coach is mentoring a new Scrum Master to run the team's ceremonies. The coach wants the Scrum Master to build real facilitation capability over the next few sprints while the ceremonies still produce value for the team. Which approaches support developing the Scrum Master's facilitation? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Let the Scrum Master facilitate the ceremonies while the coach observes, then debrief together what worked and what to adjust.
- **B**: During a ceremony, give the Scrum Master a quiet just-in-time prompt they can use, so they lead the moment themselves rather than the coach taking over.
- **C**: Co-plan a difficult ceremony beforehand, walking through likely moments and how the Scrum Master might handle them.
- **D**: Have the coach facilitate the ceremonies whenever they are present, since the coach's experience produces better sessions for the team.
- **E**: Instruct the Scrum Master to follow a fixed ceremony script exactly, removing the need for them to make facilitation judgements.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Facilitation is built by doing it with feedback, supported in the moment, and prepared in advance. (A) gives the Scrum Master real ownership of the ceremonies with a debrief that converts experience into learning. (B) scaffolds them just-in-time while keeping them visibly in the lead, developing capability without undermining their authority. (C) prepares them for hard moments so a daunting ceremony becomes manageable. (D) removes the practice entirely whenever the coach is present, so the Scrum Master never develops and the team learns to rely on the coach. (E) substitutes a rigid script for judgement, which prevents the adaptive facilitation skill the role actually requires and produces hollow ceremonies.
