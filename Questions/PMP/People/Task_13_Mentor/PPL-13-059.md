# Question

A mentee has talked through a major career decision in multiple mentoring sessions and is now set on accepting an offer the mentor strongly believes is wrong for them. The mentee has considered the mentor's concerns and disagrees. What should the mentor do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the concerns to a colleague close to the mentee and ask them to add weight to the message the mentee has chosen to ignore.
- **B**: Respect the mentee's decision, ensure they have truly considered the trade-offs, and commit to supporting them with the move they have chosen.
- **C**: Continue to argue the mentor's view in the next session, on the basis that the mentee clearly has not yet grasped the full seriousness of the risks involved.
- **D**: End the mentoring relationship since the mentor cannot in good conscience support a decision they believe the mentee will regret over the long run.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's mentoring principle is the mentee's autonomy — once they have made an informed decision, the mentor supports them. (A) triangulates through others against the mentee's stated choice. (C) refuses to accept the mentee's agency. (D) punishes autonomy and withdraws support at exactly the moment the mentee may need it most.
