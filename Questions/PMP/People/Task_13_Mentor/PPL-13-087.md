# Question

A PM mentoring several agile practitioners encounters different situations in the same week. For each mentee situation, match the most appropriate mentoring response.

## Items

- **1**: A mentee keeps asking the mentor to make their decisions for them and is not building independent judgement.
- **2**: A mentee genuinely lacks specialised knowledge of a regulatory constraint that affects their backlog.
- **3**: A mentee has just delivered their first well-run sprint review after weeks of struggling.
- **4**: A mentee's request concerns a personal dispute with a colleague, outside the project-skills remit.

## Matches

- **A**: Provide the specific expert input directly, then check the mentee can apply it.
- **B**: Use coaching questions to draw out the mentee's own reasoning before offering any view.
- **C**: Give specific recognition of what the mentee did well to reinforce the behaviour.
- **D**: Acknowledge the request but signpost the appropriate channel rather than mentoring on it.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

A dependency pattern (1) calls for a coaching stance that builds the mentee's own judgement rather than supplying answers (B). A genuine knowledge gap on a regulatory constraint (2) is exactly where direct expert input adds value, followed by checking application (A). A breakthrough first success (3) warrants specific recognition to reinforce the new behaviour (C). A personal dispute (4) sits outside the mentoring remit and should be acknowledged but redirected to the right channel (D). The tension is between coaching and telling — knowledge gaps justify telling, while judgement and dependency call for coaching — and recognition and boundaries each have their own clear trigger.
