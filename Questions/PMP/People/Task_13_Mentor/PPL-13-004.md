# Question

A junior team member has been given their first significant stretch responsibility. They are making decisions within their authority but occasionally choose suboptimal approaches. How should the project manager mentor them?

## Options

- **A**: Step in to redirect them whenever a decision looks suboptimal.
- **B**: Let them make and recover from low-impact mistakes while remaining available for guidance.
- **C**: Require them to run every decision past the project manager before acting.
- **D**: Hold formal feedback until the end-of-project review and document concerns there.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real growth requires the right to be wrong on bounded decisions. (B) gives space for learning while keeping a safety net through availability. (A) signals lack of trust and prevents learning. (C) revokes the authority that was given and stalls the team. (D) delays feedback to the point where it is no longer useful for development.
