# Question

How should a mentoring relationship typically end?

## Options

- **A**: Automatically when the project that brought the parties together completes.
- **B**: When the mentor decides the mentee no longer needs ongoing support.
- **C**: By mutual agreement, when objectives have been met or circumstances change.
- **D**: Mentoring relationships should remain in place indefinitely once formed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Healthy mentoring relationships have natural endings shaped together. (C) is the PMI-aligned answer. (A) ties the relationship to a project boundary that may not match the mentee's needs. (B) makes it a unilateral decision. (D) ignores that the mentee's needs evolve and that other supports may serve them better in time.
