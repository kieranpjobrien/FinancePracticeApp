# Question

A project manager is mentoring a team member from another department, at that person's request, to develop their stakeholder-facing skills. Over several sessions the mentee begins disclosing serious frustrations with their own line manager and asks the PM to intervene with that manager on their behalf. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Speak privately with the mentee's line manager to relay the concerns, since the PM now understands the situation and is well placed to help resolve it.
- **B**: Acknowledge the frustration, clarify that the mentoring relationship does not extend to acting as the mentee's advocate with their manager, and help the mentee think through how they could raise it themselves.
- **C**: Continue the mentoring as before but avoid the topic, steering each session firmly back to stakeholder skills whenever the line-manager issue arises.
- **D**: Refer the mentee straight to HR and pause the mentoring until the line-management matter is formally resolved.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A mentor maintains the boundaries of the relationship while still supporting the mentee's growth, which here means helping them develop the capability to address the issue rather than carrying it for them. (A) oversteps the mentoring remit and could damage trust and the mentee's standing with their own manager. (C) dismisses a real concern and wastes the developmental moment of helping the mentee handle a difficult conversation. (D) is an over-escalation that abandons the mentoring relationship over something the mentee has not asked to formalise.
