# Question

A PM has mentored a high-potential junior for over a year. A prestigious cross-functional assignment has just opened up that the junior would be ideal for, but the role is not yet advertised and is being quietly scoped in leadership conversations. What is the BEST mentoring move?

## Options

- **A**: Say nothing until the role is officially posted to avoid any perception of unfair advantage being given to the PM's own mentee in the process.
- **B**: Tell the junior privately about the role now so they can prepare their approach and arrive ahead of other applicants when it is opened.
- **C**: Advocate for the junior to the leaders involved in scoping, and separately alert the junior once they can apply through the official process.
- **D**: Offer to draft the junior's application and prepare their interview materials personally, using the PM's knowledge of the leaders involved in hiring.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's sponsorship-beyond-mentoring view: advocate on the mentee's behalf where appropriate, and respect process integrity for the application itself. (A) withholds sponsorship, which is part of good mentoring for high-potential mentees. (B) gives private prior notice that can unfairly advantage the mentee over other candidates. (D) crosses a line by writing the mentee's own application.
