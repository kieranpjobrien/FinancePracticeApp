# Question

A PM has informally mentored a colleague for a year. That colleague has now applied for a senior role the PM is on the hiring panel for. The mentee has not mentioned it; the PM only noticed via the applicant list. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Stay on the panel and mentally discount the mentoring relationship during the scoring, since the PM believes they can remain genuinely impartial during the interview process itself.
- **B**: Recuse themselves from the panel and disclose the mentoring relationship to the hiring manager before any further selection steps proceed on this role.
- **C**: Stay on the panel but agree with the hiring manager to score strictly on evidence, and let the mentee know that their views will not affect the score.
- **D**: Continue without disclosure, finish the panel, and only flag the mentoring relationship afterward if the mentee is successful in securing the role from the process.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct requires proactive disclosure of conflicts of interest — recusal with disclosure is the clean move. (A) treats impartiality as a matter of intent when the conflict is structural. (C) keeps a known conflict in place. (D) delays disclosure until after the decision, which is exactly what the conflict rule exists to prevent.
