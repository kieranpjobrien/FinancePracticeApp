# Question

A project manager has been mentoring a team member on risk response planning. The team member is about to act on a decision the project manager believes is suboptimal but is within the team member's authority. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Use their authority to override the decision before it causes downstream issues.
- **B**: Stay out of it; the decision is the team member's to make and own.
- **C**: Share concerns and reasoning, then let the team member own the call and the outcome.
- **D**: Raise the concern with the team member's functional manager so they can intervene.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Mentoring respects autonomy while still adding value. (C) gives the team member full information and ownership; mentee learns either by being right or by recovering from being wrong. (A) revokes authority and prevents learning. (B) withholds insight that the mentee is entitled to. (D) escalates a normal coaching moment.
