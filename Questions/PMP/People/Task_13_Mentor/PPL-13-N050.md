# Question

A project manager is rolling off a long programme in two months and has been asked to mentor the colleague who will take over. The successor is capable but has no history with the programme's many stakeholders and political sensitivities. With limited time, where should the PM focus the mentoring?

## Options

- **A**: Produce a comprehensive written handover pack covering every process and document, so the successor has a complete reference after the PM leaves.
- **B**: Prioritise transferring the tacit knowledge — stakeholder relationships, sensitivities, and the reasoning behind key decisions — through joint meetings and discussion the successor cannot get from documents.
- **C**: Have the successor shadow the PM silently in all meetings for two months, then take over fully on the final day.
- **D**: Introduce the successor to the sponsor and leave the remaining relationships for them to build once they are in post.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The highest-value mentoring in a handover targets the tacit, hard-to-document knowledge — relationships, context, and the why behind decisions — which is exactly what is lost when an experienced PM leaves. (A) captures explicit process but misses the political and relational knowledge that matters most. (C) is passive and back-loads the transition into a single abrupt handover. (D) covers only the sponsor and leaves the successor to rebuild the wider stakeholder network from scratch with no transferred context.
