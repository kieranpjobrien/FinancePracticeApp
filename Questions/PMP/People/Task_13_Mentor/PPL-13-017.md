# Question

A senior stakeholder asks the project manager to mentor their entire department on agile because of the project manager's experience. The project manager is already at capacity on project work. What should they do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline politely; capacity does not allow another commitment of this size.
- **B**: Accept the full request to maintain a strong relationship with the senior stakeholder.
- **C**: Reply with a scoped proposal: defined topics, time per week, and shared facilitation with another agile coach.
- **D**: Hand the engagement to a more junior team member who has more spare time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The PMI approach to a stretch ask under capacity constraints is to negotiate scope rather than refuse or overcommit. (C) keeps the relationship intact, delivers real value, and protects the project. (A) is a missed opportunity that better scoping could capture. (B) jeopardises project delivery and risks burnout. (D) sends a less qualified mentor when the stakeholder specifically asked for the project manager's experience.
