# Question

The PM realises stakeholder expectations have drifted significantly from what the project will deliver. The gap is now sizeable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Surface the expectation gap transparently and align on what will and will not be delivered.
- **B**: Press on as planned and let the stakeholders adjust their expectations at delivery time.
- **C**: Quietly extend scope to match expectations and absorb the impact within the existing team.
- **D**: Frame the gap as the original requirements team's failure when raising it with stakeholders.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's transparency principle requires surfacing expectation gaps as soon as they are visible so a properly governed realignment can occur. (B) sets the project up for a delivery-day surprise that destroys trust. (C) takes on uncommitted scope and breaches change control. (D) deflects responsibility, which is unethical and does not solve the problem.
