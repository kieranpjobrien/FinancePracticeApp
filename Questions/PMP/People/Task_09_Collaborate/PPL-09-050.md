# Question

On an agile project, the real business customer is too senior and too busy to engage regularly, and a proxy product owner has been appointed. Decisions the proxy makes are repeatedly overturned when the real customer weighs in at reviews. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the sponsor to redefine the proxy's decision authority, establish a tight escalation path to the real customer for defined decisions, and brief both.
- **B**: Continue with the current arrangement and treat review-time reversals as a normal part of iterative stakeholder feedback on an evolving product over time.
- **C**: Ask the proxy to stop making substantive decisions and refer significant calls to the real customer through email for her written response before the team acts.
- **D**: Bypass the proxy and establish a direct working channel between the team and the real customer, so decisions are made by the person with actual authority.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's answer to proxy/real-customer misalignment is to redesign the decision rights — what the proxy can decide, what must escalate, what the cadence is — not to pick a single winner. (B) accepts a pattern that is burning team effort and eroding trust in the proxy. (C) neuters the proxy role and reintroduces the bottleneck the proxy was meant to solve. (D) removes the proxy without addressing why the role was created, and overloads the real customer.
