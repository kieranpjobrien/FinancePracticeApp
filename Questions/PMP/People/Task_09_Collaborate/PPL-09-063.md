# Question

A hybrid programme is rebuilding the checkout experience for an online retailer. The business has set up a "customer council" of a dozen loyal shoppers, but so far the council only receives a quarterly email showing what shipped. Council members have started to disengage, and the product manager wants their input to genuinely shape the roadmap rather than just hear about decisions afterwards. Which TWO actions would most improve genuine two-way collaboration with the council? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Bring selected council members into roadmap-shaping sessions before priorities are locked, so their input affects what gets built.
- **B**: Run early prototype walkthroughs with the council and feed what is learned straight back into the next iteration of the design.
- **C**: Increase the council update from quarterly to monthly so members see shipped changes much sooner after release.
- **D**: Publish a council satisfaction score on the programme dashboard each quarter to keep the team focused on the council's sentiment.
- **E**: Ask council members to vote on a shortlist of features the programme has already committed to, to confirm the team chose well.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Two-way collaboration means the council's input shapes decisions and design while they can still change the outcome. (A) gives them influence over priorities before they are fixed, and (B) puts real prototypes in front of them and loops their reactions back into the work, both of which are genuine collaboration. (C) shortens a one-way reporting cycle but the council is still only told what already happened. (D) measures sentiment without giving members any actual say. (E) is consultation theatre: voting on already-committed features lets them ratify but not influence, which is why such councils disengage.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- C, D, and E all look like "more engagement" but none transfer real influence; the margin is shaping decisions versus reacting to settled ones.
