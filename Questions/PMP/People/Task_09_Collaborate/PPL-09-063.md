# Question

Pilot customers have given consistent negative feedback on a specific feature after each of the last three demos, but the product owner keeps deferring the fix because other backlog items are ranked higher. Customers are starting to disengage. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the product owner's prioritisation decision and let the pilot run its course so that the team can learn from whatever customer adoption ultimately emerges.
- **B**: Surface the adoption risk to the product owner and sponsor with data, reframe the feedback as a business-value question, and push for a conscious prioritisation call.
- **C**: Escalate directly to the sponsor and request that the feedback be actioned regardless of the product owner's current view of the backlog priority sequence today.
- **D**: Ask the team to absorb the fix as part of engineering maintenance so that backlog ordering is preserved while customer concerns are addressed in parallel effort.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's principles of empirical process control and business-value orientation require the PM to reframe ignored signals as explicit trade-off conversations, not to override the product owner. (A) watches a solvable problem become a failed pilot. (C) undermines the product owner's role rather than influencing it with evidence. (D) sneaks work into the team's load, which breaks transparency and sustainable pace.
