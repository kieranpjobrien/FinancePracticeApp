# Question

A hybrid clean-energy grid project is delivered jointly by your utility and a partner distribution company, each contributing teams that report to their own management. The two sides hold separate stand-ups, maintain separate trackers, and surface integration problems only at formal monthly governance. Both organisations are committed to the project but operate as two projects bolted together. Which TWO actions would most directly build collaboration across the two organisations' teams? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Establish shared working practices the combined teams adopt together — a joint stand-up, a single shared backlog or tracker, and shared definitions of done at integration points.
- **B**: Create a joint integration working group of practitioners from both sides that meets frequently to resolve cross-organisation issues as they arise, rather than waiting for monthly governance.
- **C**: Add a fortnightly cross-organisation sync to the existing governance, where each side reports progress and raises integration issues between the monthly meetings.
- **D**: Appoint an integration lead in each organisation as the single channel for cross-team queries, so cross-organisation communication is coordinated and tracked.
- **E**: Have the two organisations align their separate trackers to a common field set and reconcile them weekly, so both can see each other's status without merging tools.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The teams operate as two silos with no shared cadence, tooling or fast issue resolution, so collaboration grows by making them work as one. Shared practices the combined teams adopt together (A) dissolve the separate-trackers, separate-stand-ups split, and a frequently-meeting joint integration group of practitioners (B) resolves cross-organisation issues directly instead of at distant governance. (C) a fortnightly governance sync is more frequent reporting, but it keeps the formal, report-and-raise rhythm rather than building joint working between the teams. (D) integration leads as single channels improve coordination and traceability, yet routing cross-team contact through two gatekeepers restricts the everyday interaction collaboration depends on. (E) reconciling aligned-but-separate trackers gives mutual visibility without the upheaval of merging tools — a pragmatic half-step — but it preserves two systems and two ways of working instead of one shared way. The two keys make the teams work as one; the distractors improve reporting, channel control, or visibility while leaving the two-projects structure in place.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: a more frequent governance sync, designated integration leads, and reconciled parallel trackers are all real integration tactics with genuine merits. Each is set aside for keeping the silos intact, not for being absurd.
