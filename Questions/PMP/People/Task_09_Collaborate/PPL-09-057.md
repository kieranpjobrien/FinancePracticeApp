# Question

A rumour has spread across the business that the new system will lead to significant redundancies. This is not the project's intent and no such plan exists. Engagement in change activities has fallen sharply in the last fortnight. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the rumour and continue with the planned communications calendar so that the official messaging is not perceived to be reacting to unfounded speculation.
- **B**: Issue a firmly worded internal memo from the PM denying the rumour and warning against any further speculation until the transformation programme is complete.
- **C**: Ask HR and line managers to reassure staff informally in one-to-ones while the project keeps its formal communications focused entirely on delivery progress updates.
- **D**: Coordinate with HR and the sponsor to issue a clear, transparent communication from senior leadership, and run question-and-answer sessions in each affected area.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats misinformation as a stakeholder-engagement risk requiring direct, credible, two-way communication from legitimate senior voices, not from the PM in isolation. (A) lets silence be interpreted as confirmation. (B) fights speculation with authority rather than dialogue and usually entrenches the rumour. (C) pushes the response into unrecorded one-to-ones that cannot be verified or scaled consistently.
