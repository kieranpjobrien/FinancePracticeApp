# Question

The PM's project depends on a data feed that another project is building. The other PM has repeatedly missed commitment dates and does not attend the PM's integration forum. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the portfolio manager and request that the other project be formally directed to meet its inter-project commitments to the PM's team.
- **B**: Meet the other PM directly to understand the constraints, jointly replan the integration points, and agree what visibility each side needs going forward.
- **C**: Design an interim workaround in the PM's own project so the team can progress independently of the other project's fluctuating delivery schedule.
- **D**: Raise the missed commitments formally in the next portfolio status report and let the wider transparency drive the other project to fix its problems.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats cross-project collaboration as peer-level first: understand the other side's reality, replan jointly, and only escalate once direct resolution has failed. (A) skips the peer conversation and damages the long-term relationship. (C) duplicates effort and masks a real dependency risk from the portfolio. (D) uses public reporting as an escalation tool, which is passive-aggressive and usually backfires.
