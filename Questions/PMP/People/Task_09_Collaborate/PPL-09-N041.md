# Question

On an agile insurance-claims platform, the product owner sends written requirements by email and attends refinement and review only occasionally; their calendar is dominated by other departmental duties. Requirements frequently arrive with gaps that surface mid-sprint and force rework, and the next sprint starts in three days. The team has asked the project manager to get them a more reliable source of answers. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner which one or two ceremonies they can commit to attending in person, and protect those slots so the team gets direct answers where gaps most often appear.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to nominate someone from their department to attend ceremonies and answer the team's questions when the product owner cannot.
- **C**: Have the team raise each gap the moment it appears and hold the affected story until the product owner responds, so incomplete requirements stop entering the sprint.
- **D**: Run a backlog-refinement deep-dive with the team to harden the next sprint's stories against the gaps they already know about before the product owner's input.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The gaps are resolved fastest when the accountable product owner is present, so the first move is to secure their direct involvement at the highest-leverage points rather than building a substitute around them. (A) gets the product owner personally into the ceremonies where requirements are clarified, addressing the cause. (B) a departmental proxy can answer some questions but rarely carries the product owner's decision authority and can reintroduce the second-hand-requirements problem. (C) gating stories on a response enforces quality but stalls flow while the absent product owner remains the bottleneck. (D) a team deep-dive sharpens known gaps but the team is guessing at the product owner's intent — the unknown gaps are exactly what keeps biting. A and B both keep work moving, but only A re-establishes the direct stakeholder link; B is the strongest distractor because delegation looks pragmatic when a calendar is full.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: proxy, story-gating, and team refinement are all real PM responses. The margin is securing the accountable stakeholder's direct presence versus substituting for it.
