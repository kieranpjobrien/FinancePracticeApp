# Question

Two months from go-live on a healthcare platform, the PM discovers a clinical-safety committee must sign off on the release, and it was never on the stakeholder register. Their next formal meeting is after go-live. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed to go-live on the planned schedule and present the platform at the committee's next regular meeting so the project maintains its committed date.
- **B**: Request an emergency extraordinary session of the committee and continue delivery planning on the assumption that the approval will be granted as is expected.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor for a decision on whether to proceed without formal clinical-safety sign-off, documenting the residual risk in the programme register clearly.
- **D**: Engage the committee chair immediately, understand their review requirements, confirm whether an out-of-cycle review is possible, and replan go-live around their needs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's first move when a material stakeholder surfaces late is direct engagement to understand real requirements, not to plan around assumptions. (A) goes live without a required sign-off, which is a compliance and safety issue in healthcare. (B) books a meeting before understanding what the committee even needs to see, and assumes the answer. (C) escalates before the direct engagement that the PM is accountable for has occurred.
