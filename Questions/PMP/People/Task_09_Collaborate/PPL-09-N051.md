# Question

A predictive nuclear-decommissioning project must keep the safety regulator engaged throughout. Contact with the regulator's inspector has so far happened mainly when approval packages are submitted, often close to the deadline, and the relationship is correct but distant. An important approval is needed in two months, and several more will follow over the project's life. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a regular engagement rhythm with the inspector — sharing progress, upcoming submissions and emerging issues ahead of time — so the relationship runs on continuity rather than deadline-driven asks.
- **B**: Submit the upcoming approval package well ahead of its deadline to give the inspector ample review time and demonstrate a more considerate way of working.
- **C**: Offer the inspector early sight of the technical basis for the upcoming approval and a pre-submission meeting to walk through it, smoothing the decision that matters most now.
- **D**: Map all the project's remaining regulatory submissions onto a forward schedule and share it with the inspector so they can plan their workload around your milestones.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The relationship is transactional and deadline-driven, so the remedy is sustained, forward-looking engagement across the whole project rather than a better single interaction. (A) builds an ongoing two-way rhythm that earns goodwill and surfaces issues early, addressing the pattern, not just the next package. (B) submitting early is considerate and reduces deadline pressure, but it improves one transaction while leaving the underlying transactional relationship intact. (C) a pre-submission walkthrough is strong practice and builds real engagement — but it is scoped to the imminent approval, so it stops short of the continuity the long regulatory relationship needs. (D) a forward submission schedule is genuinely useful and forward-looking, yet on its own it is one-way information about your milestones rather than a two-way relationship. The margin is a sustained two-way engagement rhythm versus a single improved submission, a one-off pre-meeting, or a one-way schedule.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: early submission, a pre-submission walkthrough, and a shared forward schedule are all sound, considerate moves. The discrimination is building continuous two-way engagement versus improving one interaction or pushing information one way.
