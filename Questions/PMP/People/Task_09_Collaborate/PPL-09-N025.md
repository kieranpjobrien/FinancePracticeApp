# Question

On a predictive aerospace component programme, the design, manufacturing, and quality functions communicate with one another only through the PM, forwarding interface issues for the PM to relay rather than resolving them directly. Cycle time is slipping because the PM is a bottleneck. The PM has proposed a weekly cross-functional working forum, but the three function leads are wary: each says the others raise issues without enough analysis, so talking directly would just generate noise and rework. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the three functions to begin resolving interface issues between themselves at the weekly forum, making clear the PM will no longer relay messages, so the new way of working takes hold.
- **B**: Keep relaying interface issues for now but copy all three functions on every exchange, so they gain visibility of the cross-function traffic without the PM stepping out yet.
- **C**: Work with the leads to agree what a well-formed interface issue must contain before it is raised, then stand up the forum on that basis so direct discussion is productive rather than noisy.
- **D**: Split the forum into separate design-manufacturing and manufacturing-quality sessions so each pair resolves its own interfaces without the third function adding noise.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The leads have named a real obstacle to collaborating directly, that issues arrive half-formed, so simply forcing them together will produce the rework they fear; the PM must remove that obstacle first. (C) agrees a shared standard for how issues are raised, which makes direct resolution viable and addresses the stated concern, then establishes the forum. (A) imposes the forum while ignoring the legitimate objection, likely confirming the leads' fear and souring them on direct working. (B) keeps the PM as the bottleneck and only adds visibility, leaving the underlying dependency unchanged. (D) reduces the noise by fragmenting the conversation, but quality and design interfaces still exist and now get resolved without each other present, trading one coordination gap for another.
