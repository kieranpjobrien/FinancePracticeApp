# Question

An agile data-analytics product has its development team in Manila and its product owner and main business stakeholders in Toronto, a 12-hour offset. The team complains they lose a full day every time they need a backlog clarification because questions sit unanswered overnight. Velocity is suffering. As the project lead, what is the most effective way to improve collaboration here?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner to relocate their working hours substantially to overlap with Manila so the team can reach them in real time each day.
- **B**: Work with the product owner to agree a clarified, example-rich definition of ready and a short daily overlap window for live questions, supplemented by a delegated proxy for urgent calls.
- **C**: Instruct the team to make their best assumption when blocked and proceed, recording the assumptions for the product owner to correct at the next review.
- **D**: Introduce a detailed written requirements document for each backlog item so the team has everything they need before a sprint starts and rarely needs to ask.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The distributed-team fix combines several levers proportionate to the offset: better-prepared backlog items reduce the need to ask, a small overlap window handles live questions, and a proxy covers urgent gaps — collaborative and realistic (B). (A) demanding a wholesale shift of the product owner's hours is unsustainable and merely moves the burden onto one person. (C) blanket assumption-making without a clarification path invites rework and silently transfers product decisions to developers. (D) heavyweight upfront requirements documents recreate the problem agile is meant to avoid and still cannot anticipate every question.
