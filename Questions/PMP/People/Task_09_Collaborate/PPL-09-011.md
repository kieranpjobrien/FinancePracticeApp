# Question

A technical team and business stakeholders frequently misunderstand each other: technical detail loses the business audience, and business requirements feel vague to engineering. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run joint working sessions with visual aids, concrete examples, and a shared glossary.
- **B**: Have the PM personally rewrite communication in both directions to ensure translation consistency.
- **C**: Keep the two groups apart and exchange only formal written specifications between them.
- **D**: Send the business stakeholders to a technical training course before the next design session.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI promotes building shared understanding directly between contributors rather than mediating it through a single bottleneck — visuals, examples, and glossaries make abstract concepts concrete. (B) puts the PM in the middle of every exchange and prevents real relationships forming. (C) reduces interaction when the problem requires more of it. (D) puts the burden of bridging on one side and ignores that the technical team also needs to learn business language.
