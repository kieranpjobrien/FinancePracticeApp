# Question

A PM on a public-sector digital transformation notices that a local community group with formal consultation rights has been classified as "low influence, low interest" and is barely engaged. The group has just hired a media adviser. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the stakeholder register using power, urgency and legitimacy, update the engagement strategy, and move the group to a more actively managed posture.
- **B**: Continue the current engagement pattern until the group formally raises an issue, since consultation rights do not automatically make them influential.
- **C**: Run a public information campaign targeted at the broader community to dilute any narrative the group's new adviser attempts to build around the project.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor's communications team to meet the group quietly and offer project access in exchange for holding off on any media commentary.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects stakeholder analysis to be continuous and to use multiple lenses — a media adviser changes salience by raising urgency and legitimacy even if positional power is unchanged. (B) treats the register as static and waits for a problem to present. (C) is a communications manoeuvre that bypasses direct stakeholder engagement and risks inflaming the group. (D) trades access for silence, which is an ethics issue and corrodes trust once discovered.
