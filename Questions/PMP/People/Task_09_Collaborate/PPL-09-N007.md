# Question

On a predictive water-treatment plant upgrade, the operations director wants the plant taken offline for six weeks for a clean installation, while the regional regulator and a community group insist supply must not be interrupted for more than 48 hours at a time. Both positions are firmly held and each has escalated. Your engineers have now confirmed a phased install is technically feasible: it holds supply within the 48-hour limit but adds cost and three months to the schedule. The operations director still prefers the shutdown. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Convene the operations director, the regulator and the community representative to examine the phased option together and converge on an agreed cost, time and supply-continuity envelope.
- **B**: Present the phased install to the sponsor as the recommended way forward, since it is the only feasible option that satisfies the regulatory and community supply constraint.
- **C**: Take the phased option and the shutdown option to the steering committee with a full trade-off analysis and ask the committee to select between them.
- **D**: Work the operations director's remaining objections to the phased plan first, then bring a single agreed proposal to the regulator and community for endorsement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The phased option has reframed a binary clash into a solvable trade-off, but its cost and schedule impact crosses all three parties' interests, so the collaborative move is to bring them together to weigh it jointly and own the resulting envelope between them (A) — this is what converts a feasible technical answer into a durable agreement none of them will later reopen. (B) recommending it to the sponsor reaches a likely-correct technical outcome but does so without the parties in the room, leaving the operations director disengaged and the cost/time impact unowned by those who must live with it. (C) committee selection is a governance backstop; reaching for it while a genuine integrative solution is available abandons the PM's facilitation role and risks a ruling the losing party resents. (D) squaring the operations director away first and then presenting a "single agreed proposal" to the regulator and community is shuttle diplomacy that excludes them from shaping the solution, which tends to surface fresh objections at the point you most need their endorsement.
