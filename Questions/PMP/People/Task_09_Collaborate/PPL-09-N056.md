# Question

On an agile banking-app modernisation, four stakeholders show different collaboration problems. For each behaviour on the left, match the collaboration response most likely to address its root cause on the right.

## Items

- **1**: A stakeholder agrees to decisions in reviews but quietly reverses them afterwards in side conversations with the team.
- **2**: A stakeholder withholds input in sessions, saying afterwards they did not feel their area was being asked about.
- **3**: A stakeholder over-engages, commenting on every backlog item including those far outside their remit, slowing decisions.
- **4**: A stakeholder engages well but only ever through their delegate, who cannot make commitments.

## Matches

- **A**: Reshape the session so their specific area is explicitly invited and visibly considered, drawing their contribution out at the right moment.
- **B**: Clarify and agree the scope of their input with them, focusing their attention where their expertise genuinely adds value.
- **C**: Agree with them how decisions are confirmed and that agreements reached together stand unless formally revisited, so reversals are surfaced openly.
- **D**: Establish directly with the principal which decisions require their personal commitment, while keeping the delegate involved for the rest.

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

Each response targets the cause of the behaviour rather than the symptom. The quiet reverser (1) needs an agreed decision-confirmation practice so reversals must come into the open (C). The silent stakeholder who feels unconsulted (2) needs their area explicitly invited in the session (A). The over-engaged commenter (3) needs their scope clarified so their input lands where it adds value (B). The principal who only acts through a non-committing delegate (4) needs a direct line for the decisions that require their personal commitment (D). Mismatching these — for example applying scope-clarification to the silent stakeholder or a decision protocol to the over-engager — would treat the wrong cause and leave the behaviour unchanged.
