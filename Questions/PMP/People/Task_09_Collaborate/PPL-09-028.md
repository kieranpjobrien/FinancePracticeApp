# Question

A key vendor has been responding slowly, missing deadlines, and getting defensive in meetings. The vendor is contractually locked in for the rest of the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin a formal paper trail of the vendor's failures in case of a future contractual dispute.
- **B**: Limit project interaction with the vendor to the contractual minimum going forward.
- **C**: Replace the vendor immediately and absorb the switching cost to secure delivery.
- **D**: Diagnose the root cause with the vendor's account lead and reset expectations and channels.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's first move on a struggling vendor relationship is diagnosis and joint reset, not paperwork or termination — most vendor performance issues are addressable through clearer expectations and the right people on the call. (A) builds a litigation file before trying to fix the relationship. (B) reduces interaction when more is needed to surface the cause. (C) is disproportionate and likely impractical mid-contract.
