# Question

On an agile programme building automated assembly cells for a car-parts manufacturer, a key components supplier delivers hardware that the team integrates each sprint. The supplier's account manager only ever sees a monthly written summary, so when integration problems appear the supplier hears about them weeks later and the fixes lag. The supplier has said they would value seeing progress sooner. The team is comfortable with outside observers in their events. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Invite the supplier's technical representative to attend the sprint reviews so they see working integrations and surface issues with the team in the same room.
- **B**: Move the supplier's written summary from monthly to weekly so integration problems reach them with far less delay than at present.
- **C**: Set up a shared defect tracker the supplier can read at any time, and ask them to monitor it for integration issues affecting their hardware.
- **D**: Schedule a standing fortnightly call between the project manager and the supplier's account manager to talk through any integration problems that have arisen.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Collaboration is strongest when the supplier engages directly with the working product and the team, which the sprint review is purpose-built to enable. (A) puts the supplier's technical person where issues are seen live and resolved face to face, closing the feedback lag at its source. (B) shortens the reporting cycle but keeps the relationship one-way and document-driven. (C) gives passive visibility but relies on the supplier to self-serve and still routes problems through a tool rather than a conversation. (D) adds a manager-to-manager channel that filters technical detail through two intermediaries rather than connecting the people doing the integration.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- All four improve information flow; the margin is direct collaboration with the working product versus faster or richer one-way reporting.
