# Question

A linear infrastructure project crosses land with significant cultural heritage to a local indigenous community. The statutory consultation has been completed, but community elders have raised concerns about the quality of that engagement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm that the statutory consultation was legally compliant and proceed with construction on the approved schedule to avoid further project delays to the programme.
- **B**: Ask the statutory body that ran the consultation to respond to the elders' concerns, since those concerns relate to their process, not the project team's own.
- **C**: Offer the community a financial package in recognition of the cultural sensitivity and in exchange for an agreement not to raise further concerns publicly later.
- **D**: Meet the elders directly, listen to the specific concerns, re-engage on mitigations that go beyond statutory minimums, and adjust the plan where practicable.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement principles, and the specific ethical weight of indigenous consultation, require direct engagement that goes beyond statutory minimums when legitimate concerns are raised. (A) treats legal minimum as adequate, which it demonstrably is not in the elders' view. (B) passes responsibility to an external body whose job ended, not the project's job now. (C) trades money for silence in a cultural heritage context, which is ethically and reputationally catastrophic.
