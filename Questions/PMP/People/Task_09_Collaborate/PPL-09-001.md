# Question

A key stakeholder has stopped attending project reviews and has not responded to the last three status updates. Her sign-off is required on upcoming milestones. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reach out personally to understand what has changed and what would make engagement easier.
- **B**: Escalate the non-participation to the stakeholder's line manager.
- **C**: Proceed with decisions and document her lack of response as tacit approval.
- **D**: Send more frequent, more detailed status updates to re-capture her attention.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects proactive direct engagement at the source before any escalation; a behaviour change usually signals a change in workload, priority, or perceived project value that a conversation will surface. (B) escalates before the PM has spoken to the stakeholder. (C) treats silence as consent, which creates a governance exposure and may misrepresent her real position. (D) assumes the issue is volume when it is more likely one of relevance or bandwidth.
