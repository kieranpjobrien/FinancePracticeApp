# Question

You have inherited a hybrid renewable-energy project where the engineering consultancy, the client's asset team and the EPC contractor have a history of mistrust, mostly communicating through formal letters and rarely speaking directly. Collaboration is brittle and small issues escalate quickly. As the new project manager, which actions would most directly rebuild collaborative working relationships *among the three parties*? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Establish a regular face-to-face or video forum where the three parties work through live issues together rather than through correspondence.
- **B**: Create shared, jointly owned project information so all three parties see the same data and decisions in real time.
- **C**: Agree a set of working ground rules with the three parties covering how they will raise issues, make decisions, and treat each other.
- **D**: Run a structured risk workshop with the three parties to build a single shared risk register they jointly own and review.
- **E**: Introduce a joint early-warning notification process so each party formally alerts the others as soon as it foresees a problem affecting them.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Rebuilding collaboration among parties stuck in defensive correspondence depends on increasing direct contact, shared visibility and agreed norms of behaviour: a live joint issue forum (A), jointly owned shared information (B), and co-created ground rules (C) move the three from adversarial letter-writing toward genuinely working together. (D) a shared risk register is a worthwhile collaborative artefact, but it is a narrower, single-purpose activity; relationship trust is rebuilt by the broader habits of meeting, sharing and agreed conduct rather than by one workshop's deliverable. (E) an early-warning process is good practice, yet formalising notifications still routes interaction through a defined procedure rather than building the direct, trusting dialogue that is missing — it manages the symptom of late surprises without warming the relationship.
