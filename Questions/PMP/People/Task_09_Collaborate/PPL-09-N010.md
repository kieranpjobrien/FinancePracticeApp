# Question

On a predictive pharmaceutical packaging-line project, the site quality manager has approved every deliverable promptly but consistently provides only summary confirmations, withholding the detailed line-validation data your engineers need. A recent delay was traced to a validation gap that could not be diagnosed because the underlying data was never shared, and a second similar deliverable is due from her next week. As project manager, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the quality manager to understand why the detailed data is not being shared, set out the diagnosis problem it caused, and agree a data-sharing arrangement that works for both before the next deliverable.
- **B**: Ask the quality manager specifically to include the full validation dataset with next week's deliverable, framing it as a one-off so the team can verify the fix without a wider conversation.
- **C**: Add a requirement to the deliverable acceptance criteria that supporting validation data accompany every quality sign-off, so the obligation is captured formally going forward.
- **D**: Escalate to the quality manager's director that critical validation data is being withheld and request that full data access for your team be mandated.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The withholding is a pattern with an unknown cause — it may stem from data-sensitivity rules, system-access limits, workload, or past misuse — so the move that both fixes it and preserves the relationship is a direct conversation to understand why, surface the demonstrated harm, and agree a workable arrangement before the next deliverable lands (A). (B) asking only for next week's dataset as a one-off treats the immediate instance but leaves the underlying pattern unaddressed, so the same gap returns on the deliverable after; it patches without understanding. (C) writing the requirement into acceptance criteria imposes a rule before anyone has asked why the data is being held, which can read as a unilateral demand and may simply meet resistance rather than cooperation. (D) escalating to mandate access before even asking the quality manager frames a colleague as obstructive and is likely to harden the very behaviour you need to change.
