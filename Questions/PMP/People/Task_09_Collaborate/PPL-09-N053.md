# Question

A predictive highway-bypass construction project affects several residential communities. Relations with community representatives have soured: they say the project tells them about disruptive works — night closures, diversions — too late to influence them. Works are planned several weeks ahead, and some are unavoidable for engineering reasons, but the timing, routing and mitigation of many are still open when planning begins. Which actions would most directly improve collaboration with the communities? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Set up a standing community liaison forum where upcoming works are shared while options are still open and representatives can flag concerns that shape the plan.
- **B**: Give representatives advance notice of planned disruptive works with the rationale and a route to raise issues before the works are locked in.
- **C**: Involve representatives in shaping the mitigation for unavoidable works — timing, signage, access — even where the works themselves cannot change.
- **D**: Publish a rolling works calendar with the rationale for each item on a community portal and notify representatives whenever it updates.
- **E**: Hold a quarterly open meeting where representatives can hear a project update and put questions to the project team.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The grievance is being informed too late to influence anything, so the fixes restore the communities' ability to shape decisions while they are still open. A standing forum at the open-options stage (A), advance notice with a route to raise issues before plans lock (B), and involving representatives in mitigation even for fixed works (C) all give timely, genuine influence. (D) a rolling calendar with rationale and update alerts is a real improvement on late notice and more transparent than most projects manage — but it is still one-way information that leaves communities reacting to decisions already taken. (E) a quarterly open meeting is two-way, yet its cadence is far too slow for works planned weeks ahead, so concerns arrive after the relevant decisions. The three correct actions create timely two-way influence; the distractors improve transparency or dialogue but at the wrong direction or the wrong tempo to let communities shape decisions in time.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: a rolling rationale-backed calendar and a quarterly open meeting are both genuine engagement practices. Each falls short on a specific dimension — one-way, or too slow — rather than being obviously wrong.
