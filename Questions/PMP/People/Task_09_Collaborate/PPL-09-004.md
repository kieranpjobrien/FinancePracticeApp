# Question

A key stakeholder consistently changes her mind about requirements after work is underway. Her changes are legitimate refinements as she sees the product take shape, but they drive rework. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree a scope freeze with the sponsor after each milestone so late changes are disallowed.
- **B**: Involve the stakeholder earlier with prototypes or incremental demos so refinements surface first.
- **C**: Accept the rework as the ongoing cost of keeping a key stakeholder engaged with the product.
- **D**: Apply change-request pricing to every refinement to shape the stakeholder's change behaviour.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI-aligned iterative engagement moves learning earlier in the cycle where refinements are cheap, which is exactly what prototypes and demos do. (A) treats legitimate discovery as a process violation and produces a product that meets spec but not need. (C) accepts waste indefinitely. (D) penalises a stakeholder for normal learning and damages the relationship the project depends on.
