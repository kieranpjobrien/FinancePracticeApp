# Question

A PM notices that some stakeholders receive early drafts of key documents informally while others see only final versions at the steering committee. The "early" group are more senior; the "late" group feel increasingly sidelined and are pushing back harder on every decision. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the current arrangement because senior stakeholders legitimately need earlier visibility and can usefully contribute to shaping the content before broader distribution occurs.
- **B**: Brief each stakeholder individually on why the pattern exists and ask them to trust the process, given the different roles each group plays in overall governance.
- **C**: Standardise the distribution policy — same version at the same time for the same role-based group, with transparent criteria for any pre-read or early invitations.
- **D**: Send all documents to all stakeholders at the same time going forward, regardless of their role in the project, to remove any appearance of information asymmetry.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's transparency principle does not require equal information to all stakeholders — it requires a defensible, documented basis for any differences. (A) preserves an asymmetry stakeholders have already detected and resented. (B) defends the pattern rather than examining its legitimacy. (D) overcorrects by flattening genuine role-based differences and may overwhelm stakeholders with information they cannot use.
