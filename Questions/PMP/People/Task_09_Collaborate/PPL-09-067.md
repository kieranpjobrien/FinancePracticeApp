# Question

On a predictive project integrating an avionics module into an aircraft, the airframe design team holds interface tolerances the avionics supplier needs to finalise their housing, but the airframe team is reluctant to share unreleased figures, fearing they will change and be blamed if the supplier builds to them. The supplier is now idling and the schedule is slipping. Both teams report to different functional heads. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both teams together to agree a way to share the current tolerances as provisional, with an explicit change protocol so the airframe team is not exposed if figures move.
- **B**: Formally request the airframe team release the tolerances through a change-controlled document, accepting that any later revision will be handled as a normal change.
- **C**: Escalate to both functional heads that the airframe team's reluctance is now causing schedule slip, and ask them to direct their team to release the data.
- **D**: Have the supplier proceed using their own assumed tolerances based on the previous aircraft variant, and reconcile against the airframe figures once those are released.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The blockage is a collaboration and trust problem between two interdependent stakeholders, so the durable fix addresses the airframe team's real fear while unblocking the supplier. (A) brokers a shared arrangement — provisional data plus a change protocol — that meets the airframe team's concern about being blamed and lets the supplier proceed, which is genuine collaboration. (B) forces release through process but leaves the underlying fear unaddressed, inviting foot-dragging or padded figures. (C) escalates straight to authority before attempting to resolve the relationship, which can harden positions and damage cooperation the project still needs. (D) works around the stakeholders entirely and bets on guessed tolerances, risking expensive rework if they differ.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- Forcing, escalating, and working around all bypass the trust issue; the margin is brokering an arrangement that resolves the actual fear.
