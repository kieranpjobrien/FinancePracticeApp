# Question

The project needs input from several internal SMEs who report to different functional managers and have competing priorities. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Send a formal written request to each functional manager listing the SMEs and time required for the work.
- **B**: Work with each SME's manager to negotiate time-boxed allocation and design lean outcome-focused sessions.
- **C**: Engage the SMEs directly and ask them to fit project work around their existing departmental commitments.
- **D**: Hire an external consultancy to provide equivalent expertise without any internal scheduling friction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's resource and engagement model uses the formal authority of the functional manager combined with respectful, well-designed sessions and visible value to secure ongoing SME participation. (A) is one component but is mechanical and skips the relationship work. (C) puts the burden on overcommitted SMEs and tends to fail. (D) is expensive, risks losing organisational tacit knowledge, and may breach internal sourcing rules.
