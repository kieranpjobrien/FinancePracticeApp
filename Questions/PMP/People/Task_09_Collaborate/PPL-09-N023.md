# Question

A hybrid payroll-migration project runs discovery in iterations but delivers in a fixed go-live window. The business analyst has already facilitated two joint sessions between the HR and finance leads on a set of contradictory requirements; each session ends in apparent agreement, but within days both groups send the BA fresh write-ups that again reflect only their own function's view. The BA is asking the PM how to proceed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sit in on the next reconciliation session to observe why the agreements are not holding, then adjust how the decision is reached based on what is actually blocking closure.
- **B**: Ask the HR and finance leads to nominate a single accountable owner for the disputed requirements area who can make the final call when the two functions cannot converge.
- **C**: Have the BA capture each agreement as a signed decision record at the end of the session, so subsequent contradictory write-ups can be referred back to what was agreed.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor that the two functions are unable to hold a joint agreement and ask for a ruling on the contested requirements.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Two facilitated sessions have already been run and keep failing, so simply convening the parties again is not the answer; the PM first needs to understand why agreements evaporate before choosing a remedy. (A) diagnoses the actual blocker, after which the right fix, whether a single owner, a decision record, or an escalation, can be applied deliberately. (B) may well be the eventual solution, but imposing an owner before understanding why consensus collapses is premature and could entrench one function. (C) treats the symptom by documenting agreements that the parties are clearly not honouring, without addressing why they revert. (D) escalates a problem the PM has not yet diagnosed and that the leads may still be able to resolve once the real obstacle is understood.
