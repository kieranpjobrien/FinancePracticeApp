# Question

A joint-venture project has two co-sponsors, one from each parent organisation. They regularly give the PM conflicting direction and expect the PM to reconcile it. Decisions are stalling across three workstreams. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both sponsors to a single facilitated session to align on decision rights, conflict-resolution protocol and a joint position on each stalled decision.
- **B**: Implement whichever sponsor's direction was given most recently on each conflict and document the choice transparently so both parties can see the logic applied.
- **C**: Route all decisions through the parent board of the JV so neither sponsor can unilaterally direct the PM in ways the other has not endorsed formally.
- **D**: Request a single accountable sponsor from the two parent organisations so the PM has a clear, unambiguous line of direction for the remainder of the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's answer to conflicting accountable authorities is to surface the conflict and design alignment explicitly — not to pick, escalate around, or restructure without trying that first. (B) is recency bias dressed up as a decision rule. (C) bypasses the sponsors whose buy-in the project needs and slows every decision. (D) restructures the JV before the lighter-weight step of a facilitated alignment has been attempted.
