# Question

A PM on an agile insurance-claims modernisation programme is calibrating how to collaborate with three stakeholders whose current engagement differs from what the project needs. Match each stakeholder situation to the most appropriate collaboration move.

## Items

- **1**: A regional claims head is openly resistant, lobbying peers against the new workflow because they fear job losses on their team.
- **2**: A data-protection officer is currently neutral and uninvolved, but their sign-off will be mandatory before any release handling personal data.
- **3**: An operations supervisor is already a supportive champion who runs informal demos for their team but is being overloaded by acting as an unofficial help desk.

## Matches

- **A**: Proactively bring them into release planning early so their mandatory requirements shape the increment rather than block it late.
- **B**: Hold a direct conversation to understand the underlying concern, then co-design how their team's role evolves so resistance is addressed at its source.
- **C**: Recognise and support their advocacy while redistributing the informal support burden so their enthusiasm is sustained, not exhausted.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Collaboration is tailored to each stakeholder's stance and what the project needs from them. The resistant claims head (1) needs their genuine fear surfaced and addressed by co-designing the role change, not persuasion alone (B). The neutral but gate-keeping data-protection officer (2) must be pulled into planning early so requirements shape rather than block the work (A). The overloaded champion (3) needs their advocacy protected by lifting the unofficial support load so their support endures (C). Mismatching these, for example treating the resistant head as a late approver, would leave the real issue unmanaged.
