# Question

End users are reluctant to participate in user acceptance testing because they "don't have time" alongside their day jobs. UAT is critical for go-live readiness. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with line managers to allocate UAT time and design tightly scoped sessions that respect it.
- **B**: Skip UAT in favour of additional internal QA cycles run by the project team and vendor.
- **C**: Have the project team perform UAT in the users' place to keep the schedule on track for go-live.
- **D**: Escalate to senior leadership to compel user participation in the UAT sessions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's engagement model says secure formal time through line managers, design lean sessions that respect that time, and reinforce participation by showing impact. (B) substitutes internal QA for the actual user perspective UAT exists to provide. (C) violates the very purpose of UAT and produces a false readiness signal. (D) escalates a workload issue and creates resentment that produces low-quality testing.
