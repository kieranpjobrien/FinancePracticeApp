# Question

A renewables project has secured all regulatory approvals, but a previously quiet community group is now vocal, citing visual amenity and concerns not covered by the formal assessments. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rely on the regulatory approvals as sufficient authority to proceed, and continue construction on the planned schedule without any further community engagement beyond that.
- **B**: Engage the community directly — listen, share accurate information, explore mitigations within engineering tolerances, and involve them in a continuing community liaison forum.
- **C**: Offer a one-off community benefit payment in exchange for a commitment that the group will not publicly object to the construction or operation phases.
- **D**: Refer the group back to the regulator and let the regulator determine whether any additional obligations apply to the project at this stage of delivery.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and responsible project delivery distinguish legal permission from social licence; the latter is earned through direct, continuing engagement and shared problem-solving. (A) treats regulatory compliance as the end of the stakeholder conversation, which routinely triggers delays and litigation. (C) trades cash for silence and is an ethical and reputational exposure. (D) outsources a stakeholder relationship the project owns to an authority whose scope does not cover the community's concern.
