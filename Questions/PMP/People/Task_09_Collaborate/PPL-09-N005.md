# Question

Midway through a public-sector grants-management project, a new programme director is appointed on the customer side. She was not involved in the original planning and at a town-hall has publicly questioned several decisions made before her arrival, including the chosen delivery approach. Your sponsor is keen for the project to keep moving and the next milestone is three weeks away. As project manager, what should you do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet her one-to-one to understand her expectations, concerns and priorities, and to walk her through the prior decisions and the rationale behind them.
- **B**: Prepare a concise onboarding pack covering the charter, the key decisions and their rationale, and send it ahead of a briefing so the meeting can focus on her questions.
- **C**: Invite her to the next steering committee, where the existing decisions and their basis are already documented, so she can raise her concerns in the proper governance forum.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor, who agreed the original decisions with her predecessor, to brief her on why they were made before you engage her on delivery detail.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A newly arrived senior stakeholder who is already challenging decisions in public needs a direct, two-way relationship built first — a one-to-one lets you hear her actual concerns and priorities and explain the rationale in dialogue, which is what defuses the unease and surfaces issues early (A). (B) is a reasonable supporting step, but leading with a document pack puts the material before the relationship and risks her reading prior decisions as a fait accompli she is being asked to accept; the pack is more useful after you understand what she actually objects to. (C) routing a stakeholder who is questioning the project's direction straight into a formal committee, before any private conversation, is likely to feel adversarial and can entrench positions in front of an audience. (D) having the sponsor brief her on your behalf outsources the very relationship the PM most needs to build and may read to her as being managed around rather than engaged with.
