# Question

The PM is delivering a regulatory compliance project where the regulator is a key stakeholder with limited availability. What is the most effective approach?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the regulator like any other stakeholder and invite them to all routine working sessions.
- **B**: Avoid contacting the regulator to keep the project beneath their radar and compliance attention.
- **C**: Interpret ambiguous regulatory requirements internally to avoid using up the regulator's scarce time.
- **D**: Prepare thoroughly for each interaction, clarify ambiguities at source, and build the relationship.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Regulatory stakeholders need targeted, well-prepared, low-frequency engagement, and PMI's compliance posture is to clarify ambiguity at source rather than guess. (A) wastes the regulator's scarce time and degrades the relationship. (B) increases the chance of non-compliance through ignorance. (C) may produce non-compliance through misinterpretation, which is exactly the risk PMI's compliance principle warns against.
