# Question

Stakeholder analysis identifies a director with high influence over a function the project will materially affect, but who is currently unaware of the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold off on contacting her until the project has demonstrable progress to share with her.
- **B**: Place her at "keep informed" with monthly status reports and standard distribution materials.
- **C**: Engage her proactively now and target a "supportive" engagement level before she hears elsewhere.
- **D**: Wait for the sponsor to introduce the project to her through their own channels and timing.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

High-influence, currently-unaware stakeholders are the textbook risk of a negative surprise; PMI says move them to a supportive engagement level proactively. (A) trades silence for the chance she discovers the project from a less favourable source. (B) reduces a major stakeholder to a passive recipient and misses the chance to build alignment. (D) defers an action the PM owns to a sponsor who may not act in time.
