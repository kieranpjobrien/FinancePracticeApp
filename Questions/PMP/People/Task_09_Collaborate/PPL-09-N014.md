# Question

On a predictive ERP rollout, your executive sponsor prefers a brief monthly summary and tells you he "trusts the team to get on with it". However, a material risk to the go-live date has just emerged that he will need to make a decision on within two weeks, well before the next scheduled summary. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the risk for the monthly summary as the sponsor requested, but flag in advance that the next report will contain an item needing his urgent decision.
- **B**: Make the go-live decision yourself within the project's delegated authority so the sponsor is not troubled between his preferred reporting points.
- **C**: Escalate the risk to the steering committee instead, since a decision affecting go-live is a governance matter rather than a single sponsor's call.
- **D**: Contact the sponsor now with a concise briefing on the risk and the decision required, while respecting his preference for brevity, and confirm how he wants to handle time-sensitive items in future.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A stakeholder's stated communication preference is a default, not a rule that overrides a time-critical decision they own — so the PM briefs the sponsor now, in the concise style he likes, and uses the moment to agree how urgent items will be handled (D). (A) waiting for the monthly summary on a decision due within two weeks subordinates the project's needs to a reporting cadence and risks missing the window. (B) taking a go-live decision that belongs to the sponsor exceeds the PM's role and removes the sponsor's accountability. (C) routing to the steering committee may be appropriate later, but bypassing the sponsor who owns the relationship and the decision is premature.
