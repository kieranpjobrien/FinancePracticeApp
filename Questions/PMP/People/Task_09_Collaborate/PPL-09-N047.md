# Question

You manage a hybrid public-transport ticketing programme with stakeholders who differ in influence, location and preferred ways of working. For each stakeholder situation on the left, match the most appropriate collaboration approach on the right.

## Items

- **1**: A time-poor executive sponsor who only wants to be involved when a decision genuinely needs them.
- **2**: A distributed group of regional station managers who must stay aligned on rollout but are spread across the network and rarely all available at once.
- **3**: A vocal passenger-advocacy group that feels unheard and has been criticising the programme publicly.

## Matches

- **A**: Establish an asynchronous shared space — a regularly updated board and recorded briefings — so they can stay aligned and contribute on their own schedule.
- **B**: Set up a recurring two-way forum where their concerns are heard directly, with visible follow-through on the points they raise.
- **C**: Provide concise decision-focused briefings and reserve their time for the specific choices that require their authority.

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Each approach fits the stakeholder's availability and the kind of engagement they need. The time-poor sponsor (1) is best served by decision-focused briefings that protect their time (C). The distributed, rarely-simultaneously-available station managers (2) need an asynchronous shared space to stay aligned without depending on everyone being free at once (A). The vocal, alienated advocacy group (3) needs to be genuinely heard through a two-way forum with visible follow-through (B), because their grievance is feeling unheard, not lacking information. Swapping any pairing — for example giving the sponsor the recurring forum or the advocacy group only async updates — misreads what each one actually requires.
