# Question

A PM is mobilising a predictive coastal flood-defence construction project with a wide stakeholder base: an environmental regulator, local residents' associations, a marine contractor, and the council finance team. The PM wants to set up collaboration well from the outset. Which actions are appropriate during early stakeholder engagement? (Choose all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Identify each stakeholder's interests, influence, and information needs, and use this to shape how and how often the PM engages each of them.
- **B**: Agree with key stakeholders the decisions they need to be involved in and the points at which their input is required.
- **C**: Defer engaging the residents' associations until the design is firm enough to present, so early consultation is based on something concrete rather than abstractions.
- **D**: Establish two-way channels so stakeholders can raise concerns early, not only receive project information.
- **E**: Route all engagement with the environmental regulator through the marine contractor, since the contractor deals with the regulator's technical conditions day to day.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Effective early collaboration is built on understanding stakeholders, agreeing their involvement, and enabling genuine two-way dialogue. (A) tailors engagement to each stakeholder's profile. (B) clarifies where their input is needed, preventing late surprises. (D) creates the feedback loops that surface concerns while they are cheap to address. (C) sounds reasonable but delays engaging an affected community until the design is largely set, which forecloses their ability to influence it and tends to harden opposition on a consented infrastructure project. (E) hands the PM's direct relationship with a critical regulator to a third party with its own commercial interests, weakening the PM's line to a make-or-break stakeholder rather than strengthening it.
