# Question

On an agile claims-automation team, a powerful operations director attends the daily stand-up and uses it to assign tasks directly to developers and challenge their estimates. The team has gone quiet and the scrum master has already spoken to the director once about the stand-up's purpose; the director was receptive in the moment but reverted to the same behaviour within two days. The director genuinely wants the project to succeed. As PM, what is your best response?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the director to understand what need is driving them into the stand-up, and agree a specific alternative forum that actually satisfies that need so they no longer rely on the stand-up.
- **B**: Reinforce the scrum master's earlier message by jointly restating to the director, in writing, the stand-up's purpose and the expectation that they observe rather than direct.
- **C**: Invite the director to a separate weekly session with the product owner to review priorities and progress, giving them a sanctioned channel for their input.
- **D**: Ask the product owner, who holds the priority relationship with the director, to be the one to address the stand-up behaviour with them.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A reminder about the stand-up's purpose has already been tried and failed, so repeating it will not work; the behaviour persists because an underlying need is unmet, and collaboration means finding and satisfying that need. (A) diagnoses what is pulling the director in, then designs a channel that genuinely replaces the stand-up for them, which is the durable fix. (B) repeats the approach that has already proven ineffective, just more formally. (C) offers a sensible alternative forum but assumes the director's need is priority review; if the real driver is something else, such as delivery anxiety or wanting visible control, it will not displace the behaviour, which is why understanding must come first. (D) changes who delivers the message but not the message that has already failed, and offloads rather than resolves the issue.
