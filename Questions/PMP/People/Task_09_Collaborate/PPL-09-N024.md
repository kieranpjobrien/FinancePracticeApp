# Question

A scaled agile programme delivering a retail banking app has three feature teams, a product owner per team, and a single sponsor who has stopped attending reviews because they find them too detailed. The sponsor now relies on a monthly RAG summary from a programme assistant and has twice made funding comments that contradict where the teams are actually heading. Which actions should the project manager take to rebuild effective collaboration with the sponsor? (Choose all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the sponsor a short, decision-focused forum at a cadence and altitude that suits them, replacing the detailed review attendance.
- **B**: Ask the product owners to prepare a concise outcomes-and-options view for that forum so the sponsor engages with value and trade-offs, not task detail.
- **C**: Keep the sponsor's engagement to the monthly RAG report but have the lead product owner add a short written narrative interpreting the status for them.
- **D**: Invite the sponsor to a rotating one feature team's detailed review each month so they see real progress without committing to all three.
- **E**: Surface the two contradictory funding comments with the sponsor directly to understand the expectations behind them and close the gap.

## Answer

A, B, E

## Explanation

The sponsor disengaged because the format mismatched their needs, so collaboration is restored by meeting them at the right altitude and addressing the misalignment directly. (A) and (B) reshape engagement around decisions and value, which is what a sponsor needs. (E) tackles the contradictory comments at source rather than letting the gap widen. (C) keeps the sponsor on the same one-way monthly report that already produced misaligned comments; a narrative layer does not restore two-way engagement or decision involvement. (D) returns the sponsor to exactly the detailed-review format they disengaged from, just one team at a time, which still pitches at the wrong altitude and gives a partial, rotating picture rather than a programme-level view.
