# Question

A joint delivery with a strategic vendor partner is struggling: their team and the PM's team point fingers when things go wrong and each blames the other's governance. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Tighten the PM's internal controls and require the vendor to match those controls by contract so that accountability moves clearly inside each organisation separately.
- **B**: Escalate to the vendor's account executive and the PM's sponsor, then let the two executives negotiate a revised governance structure for the ongoing partnership.
- **C**: Co-design a joint operating model — shared risks register, joint standups, single integrated plan, agreed escalation path — and commit both sides to it.
- **D**: Run parallel governance where each party manages its own risks and issues, and combine the reporting at a fortnightly joint steering committee review meeting.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats joint delivery as requiring a genuinely joint operating model — one backlog, one risk register, one plan — because separate artefacts preserve the silos that caused the dysfunction. (A) increases internal rigour but maintains the silos. (B) punts the design question to executives without the teams doing the joint work. (D) formalises the two-track dynamic that produced the blame, with only a late-stage combining point.
