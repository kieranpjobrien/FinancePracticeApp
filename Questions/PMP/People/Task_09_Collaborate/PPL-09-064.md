# Question

The PM inherits a programme where the previous PM over-promised and under-delivered for two years. Stakeholders are now cynical and turn up to forums mainly to critique. Commitments made by the new PM are met with visible scepticism. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Push back on the scepticism in forums and insist stakeholders give the new PM a clean slate based on her track record from past projects.
- **B**: Reduce the frequency of stakeholder forums so the team can build a delivery track record quietly and re-engage stakeholders once outcomes speak for themselves later.
- **C**: Acknowledge the history openly, make smaller explicit commitments the team can consistently meet, report honestly on progress, and rebuild trust through evidence steadily.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to publicly endorse the new PM at the next forum so stakeholders recognise the change in leadership and adjust their stance accordingly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's trust-building principle is that credibility is earned through small, consistent, transparent delivery — not asserted or sponsored. (A) argues with a feeling rather than changing the behaviour that causes it. (B) treats engagement as a reward for delivery rather than a means of rebuilding trust. (D) borrows the sponsor's authority to bypass work the new PM needs to do personally.
