# Question

The product owner on an agile online-education platform is a busy senior manager who attends sprint planning but is then largely unreachable during the sprint. The team frequently reaches points where only the product owner can clarify intent, and work stalls until the next planning event. The product owner is responsive and well-regarded when she is available — the issue is purely her time. What should the agile project lead do first?

## Options

- **A**: Discuss the impact with the product owner and agree a practical mechanism for timely answers during the sprint — for example a fixed daily availability window or a properly empowered delegate.
- **B**: Propose that the product owner formally delegate day-to-day clarification authority to a named proxy who sits with the team and can answer most questions in the moment.
- **C**: Strengthen backlog refinement so each story carries an example-rich definition of ready, cutting the number of mid-sprint clarifications the team needs to raise.
- **D**: Set up a single batched question channel the product owner answers at a guaranteed time each day, so the team's queries are handled predictably without needing her on demand.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The right first step is a direct conversation with the product owner that makes the cost of her unavailability visible and *jointly* agrees a mechanism fitting her constraints — which may turn out to be a window, a delegate, or both (A); choosing the mechanism with her is what makes it stick and keeps her ownership intact. (B) a delegated proxy is one valid mechanism, but committing to that specific solution before the conversation pre-empts her input and may fragment the single source of priorities if imposed rather than agreed. (C) richer refinement genuinely reduces clarifications and is worth doing, but it cannot eliminate the in-sprint questions that need a real decision, so on its own it leaves the stalling problem only partly addressed. (D) a batched daily-answer channel is a sensible option to put *on the table*, yet adopting it unilaterally still presumes the answer; the first move is to agree the approach with the person whose time is the constraint.
