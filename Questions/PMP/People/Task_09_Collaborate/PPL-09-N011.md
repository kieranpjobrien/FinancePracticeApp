# Question

At the end of a sprint on a retail loyalty-app project, the development team has demonstrated a working increment. The business stakeholders attending the review offer enthusiastic but vague reactions — "looks great", "love it" — and the session is about to wrap up with ten minutes to spare. The increment touches a checkout flow the stakeholders had previously argued about. As the agile project lead, what is the best thing to do?

## Options

- **A**: Use the remaining time to ask the stakeholders specific questions about whether the increment meets their intended outcomes and what, if anything, they would change.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to walk the stakeholders through the upcoming backlog items now, while they are engaged, to confirm the next sprint's priorities reflect their views.
- **C**: Note the positive reception, close the review on time, and ask the product owner to follow up individually with each stakeholder over the next few days for more detailed input.
- **D**: Invite the stakeholders to try the checkout flow hands-on after the demo and send any observations to the product owner by end of week.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The sprint review exists to generate feedback that shapes the backlog, and the right people are in the room reacting to a live increment that touches a previously contested area — so the lead should spend the available time actively drawing out specifics while context is fresh (A). (B) pivoting to next-sprint priorities is a legitimate part of the conversation, but it moves past the increment just demonstrated without ever testing whether it actually met the need, which is the review's primary job. (C) deferring to individual follow-ups loses the shared, real-time dialogue the live demo enables and risks the feedback never materialising once attention disperses. (D) hands-on trial is valuable, but pushing it to end-of-week email surrenders the immediacy of reacting together in the session and invites the same late, fragmented feedback the live review is meant to prevent.
