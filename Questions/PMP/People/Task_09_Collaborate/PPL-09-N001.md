# Question

On a hybrid programme delivering a hospital patient-portal, the clinical sponsor and the IT security lead each insist their feature set must lead the next two sprints. You have already run a joint session where both presented their cases against the agreed value criteria, but each left convinced their own work is the higher priority and neither moved. The product owner, who owns ordering, now says she is willing to set the sequence but is worried that whichever way she calls it, the losing party will escalate to the steering committee and reverse it. The next sprint starts in two days. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the product owner to make and publish the ordering decision with the value rationale attached, and brief both stakeholders beforehand that the sequence is the product owner's call within the agreed criteria.
- **B**: Run a second facilitated session with the same two stakeholders to keep working the priorities until they reach genuine consensus on the ordering themselves.
- **C**: Take the unresolved trade-off to the steering committee now with a recommended sequence, so the body that both parties would escalate to settles it before the sprint starts.
- **D**: Sequence the two sprints so the clinical and security feature sets each lead one sprint, splitting the contested capacity evenly to keep both parties engaged.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Collaboration has already been attempted and the parties are at a genuine impasse, so the next move is not more facilitation but to let the accountable role decide — the product owner owns backlog ordering, and the PM's job is to equip her to make the call defensibly and pre-frame it with both stakeholders so the decision holds. (B) repeating the same session that has already failed ignores that the parties are stuck and burns the two days before sprint start chasing a consensus that may never come. (C) escalating pre-empts the product owner's own accountability and trains both stakeholders to bypass her whenever they dislike an outcome; escalation is the fallback if she is overruled, not the first resort once facilitation stalls. (D) splitting capacity evenly is a compromise that ignores which feature set actually carries more value and may starve the higher-value work to keep the peace.
