# Question

A key stakeholder rarely attends meetings or replies to messages. His input is required for the next phase. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reach out directly to understand his barriers and adapt the engagement format.
- **B**: Remove him from the stakeholder register and reassign his decision rights to a deputy.
- **C**: Escalate his disengagement to his line manager for a formal response.
- **D**: Proceed on assumptions and capture them in an issue log for later review with him.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PMI-aligned first step is direct engagement to understand the cause; disengagement is a symptom and the cause (workload, perceived value, channel mismatch) usually points to a fixable change in approach. (B) removes a needed stakeholder rather than re-engaging him. (C) escalates before the PM has had the conversation. (D) generates assumption-based decisions in his name and creates rework when he eventually surfaces.
