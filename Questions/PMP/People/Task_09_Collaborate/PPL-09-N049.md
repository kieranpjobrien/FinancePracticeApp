# Question

A hybrid pharmaceutical-manufacturing systems project has a steering committee of five senior leaders who are individually supportive but collectively paralysing decisions. Members raise new objections after meetings, reverse positions agreed in the room, and increasingly send delegates who cannot commit on their behalf. The project charter names the committee as the decision body. Which TWO actions would most directly improve collaboration within the committee? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the committee which decision types require members' personal attendance and authority to commit, so delegates attend only where they can genuinely decide.
- **B**: Agree a decision-finality rule with the committee — decisions recorded in the meeting stand unless formally reopened — and circulate the record promptly for confirmation within a set window.
- **C**: Ask the executive sponsor to chair the committee and personally hold members to the decisions taken, lending authority the PM does not have.
- **D**: Pre-brief each member individually before meetings to understand their position, so surprises and reversals are anticipated and headed off in the room.
- **E**: Move detailed decision-shaping into a smaller working group of the most engaged members and bring the committee only ratification-ready recommendations.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The dysfunction has two procedural roots — no agreement on who must attend with authority to commit, and no shared point at which a decision becomes final — and the actions that work fix those at source, with the committee. Agreeing attendance-and-commitment expectations (A) addresses the non-committing delegates and the after-the-fact reopening of who decided; a decision-finality rule with a prompt confirm window (B) gives reversals a clear line they must cross openly. Both are co-adopted, so they build the committee's own discipline. (C) a sponsor-chair may help but borrows authority rather than building the committee's collaboration, and is not the PM's to arrange unilaterally. (D) individual pre-briefing to understand positions is sound practice, but framed as heading off objections in the room it manages around the dysfunction and can drive disagreement underground. (E) a smaller shaping group is a real governance pattern but narrows participation and leaves the full committee's commitment problem untouched — the same members can still reverse the ratification. The two keys attack distinct causes; the distractors borrow authority, work around the behaviour, or narrow the body rather than fixing how it decides together.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: a sponsor-chair, pre-meeting alignment, and a shaping sub-group are all genuine governance options. Each is set aside for a specific reason, not because it is absurd; the two correct actions target separate root causes rather than restating one idea.
