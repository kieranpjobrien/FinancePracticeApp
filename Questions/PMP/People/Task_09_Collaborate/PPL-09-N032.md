# Question

An agile e-commerce replatforming has a checkout team that depends on a payments team in a different value stream. The payments team keeps reprioritising away from the checkout team's needs, and the two product owners have a polite but unproductive standoff, each protecting their own backlog. Delivery of a committed feature is now threatened. Which actions best support effective collaboration across the dependency? (Choose all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Bring both product owners together to make the cross-team dependency and its business impact explicit, so prioritisation reflects the shared outcome rather than two separate backlogs.
- **B**: Establish a lightweight cross-team coordination forum so dependencies are visible and negotiated each iteration rather than discovered late.
- **C**: Have the checkout team build a permanent parallel payments capability of its own so it controls the full flow and is no longer dependent on the payments team.
- **D**: Escalate to both value-stream leads now to direct the payments backlog to be reordered in the checkout team's favour.
- **E**: Make the dependency and its risk visible in a shared, transparent view both teams and their stakeholders can see.

## Answer

A, B, E

## Explanation

Cross-team dependencies are managed through visibility and joint prioritisation around shared outcomes, not by duplication or premature escalation. (A) reframes two protective backlogs around the common goal. (B) creates the ongoing mechanism to negotiate dependencies. (E) makes the dependency transparent so it is managed rather than rediscovered. (C) duplicates a whole capability that belongs to another team, creating long-term cost, divergence, and maintenance burden to avoid collaborating. (D) goes to the value-stream leads to impose a reordering before the product owners have genuinely attempted joint prioritisation, which is premature and undercuts their ownership; escalation may follow only if collaboration is tried and fails.
