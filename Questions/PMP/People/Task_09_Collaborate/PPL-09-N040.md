# Question

Six iterations into a hybrid customer-loyalty platform, the PM realises the sponsor expects a fully featured launch matching the original business case, while the steering group has, through iteration decisions, steadily traded breadth for a smaller, higher-quality first release. Neither party has noticed the divergence, and the gap will become obvious at the launch-readiness review in four weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Prepare a detailed reconciliation pack for the launch-readiness review so the divergence is fully evidenced when the parties meet as scheduled.
- **B**: Brief the sponsor alone now on the reduced scope so they are not caught out, and leave the steering group's position unchanged.
- **C**: Steer the next iterations to add back the missing features so the release more closely matches the sponsor's original expectation before the review.
- **D**: Bring the sponsor and steering group together now to surface the divergence, align on what the first release will deliver, and agree how the remaining business case scope is handled.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A silent expectation gap between two key stakeholders is a collaboration failure waiting to surface; the PM should close it proactively and jointly while there is still time to act. (D) aligns both parties on a shared reality and a path for the remaining scope before the review. (A) lets the divergence persist until the review, turning a manageable conversation into a confrontation in a formal setting. (B) informs one side only, leaving the two parties still misaligned and the steering group blindsided when the gap emerges. (C) unilaterally reverses direction to paper over the gap, overriding the steering group's deliberate quality-over-breadth decisions without their agreement.
