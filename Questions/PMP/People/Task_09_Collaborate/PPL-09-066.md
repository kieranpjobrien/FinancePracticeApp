# Question

The PM runs a stakeholder satisfaction pulse each quarter. Scores have been steadily declining for two quarters, but individual stakeholders say they are "fine" when asked directly. The pattern in the data is clear; the individual conversations contradict it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Dig into the pulse data by segment — role, workstream, tenure, region — and run structured conversations with the segments showing the sharpest decline.
- **B**: Trust the individual conversations over the pulse results and adjust the pulse instrument, since face-to-face feedback is generally considered more reliable than survey data.
- **C**: Discontinue the pulse since it is producing conflicting signals and rely entirely on direct stakeholder conversations to inform engagement adjustments from now on.
- **D**: Share the pulse trend openly with all stakeholders and ask the group collectively what the scores are signalling about their experience of the project to date.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's data-driven engagement approach treats divergent signals as useful: both sources are telling the PM something, and the right response is targeted analysis, not either instrument alone. (B) discards data because it is inconvenient. (C) removes a useful trend signal rather than investigating it. (D) can be useful later, but without segmentation first, the group conversation is likely to surface only the loudest voices, not the actual pattern.
