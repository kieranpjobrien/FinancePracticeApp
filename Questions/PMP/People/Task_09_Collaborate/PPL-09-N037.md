# Question

On a predictive field-service mobile app project, requirements were gathered entirely from operations management. During a pilot, frontline technicians, who will be the daily users, report that the workflow does not match how jobs actually run in the field, and several refuse to adopt it. Management insists the requirements they signed off are correct. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the management-approved requirements as signed off, since they are the documented and authorised baseline, and manage technician adoption through training and change support.
- **B**: Rework the app to the technicians' description of the real workflow, since adoption by the actual end users determines whether the app delivers any value.
- **C**: Commission a neutral observation of technicians doing real jobs to produce evidence of the gaps, then decide what to change once the findings are in.
- **D**: Bring the technicians and management together to reconcile the signed-off requirements with the observed field reality, so both perspectives shape the workflow.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A key stakeholder group, the end users, was underrepresented, and adoption is failing; collaboration means reconciling management's intent with frontline reality together rather than siding with either. (D) brings both groups into the decision so the workflow reflects how work is actually done while respecting management's legitimate stake and governance. (A) enforces a baseline that is demonstrably failing its users, guaranteeing non-adoption whatever the training. (B) swings entirely to the technicians, bypassing management's authority and the agreed baseline, and may miss constraints management understands. (C) gathers more evidence on a gap the pilot has already exposed and would still leave the two groups unreconciled afterwards, delaying the conversation that has to happen.
