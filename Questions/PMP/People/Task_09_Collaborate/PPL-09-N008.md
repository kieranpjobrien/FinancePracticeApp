# Question

You are managing stakeholder engagement on a telecom network-migration programme. For each stakeholder situation on the left, match the most appropriate collaboration response on the right.

## Items

- **1**: A senior executive sponsor is currently neutral but their active backing would unlock funding for a critical phase.
- **2**: An operations team is resistant because the migration changes their daily tools, and their cooperation is essential for cut-over.
- **3**: A regulatory liaison is supportive and well-informed, and you simply need to keep them that way through to go-live.

## Matches

- **A**: Involve them early in designing the cut-over runbook so their concerns shape the approach and they gain ownership of the change.
- **B**: Schedule a focused briefing on the phase's business value and the specific decisions where their sponsorship is needed, to move them from neutral to supportive.
- **C**: Maintain a steady cadence of concise status updates and early notice of any change that could affect compliance, without over-burdening them.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Each response is matched to the stakeholder's current position and the engagement shift required. The neutral sponsor (1) needs a targeted value-and-asks briefing to move them to active support (B). The resistant but essential operations team (2) is best won over by being involved in co-designing the cut-over so they own it (A) — participation converts resistance more reliably than persuasion. The already-supportive, informed regulator (3) needs maintenance, not conversion, so a steady, proportionate update cadence (C) keeps them onside without wasting their time.
