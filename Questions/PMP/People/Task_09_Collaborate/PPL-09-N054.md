# Question

An agile team building an internal HR self-service tool holds sprint reviews attended only by the team and the product owner. The HR operations staff who will use the tool daily have not attended any, and the team's assumptions about their workflow keep proving wrong. The product owner knows HR policy well but not the day-to-day operational detail. What should the project manager encourage?

## Options

- **A**: Invite the HR operations staff to sprint reviews so they see increments and give direct feedback on how the tool fits their daily work.
- **B**: Have the product owner run a short workflow-validation session with the HR operations staff each sprint and bring their feedback into the review.
- **C**: Arrange for a couple of team members to sit with the HR operations staff for a half-day to observe their actual workflow before the next sprint.
- **D**: Schedule a detailed requirements workshop with the HR operations staff at the start of the next quarter to capture their workflow properly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The wrong assumptions persist because the actual users are absent from the feedback loop, so the direct fix is recurring, first-hand contact between the users and the increment. (A) brings the operations staff into reviews to correct misunderstandings sprint by sprint, which is exactly the ongoing engagement the situation lacks. (B) a product-owner-run validation session is better than nothing, but it filters user input through someone who themselves lacks the operational detail, so gaps can pass straight through. (C) a workflow observation is genuinely valuable and would surface real detail — its limit is that it is a one-off snapshot, whereas the tool keeps evolving and needs continued user contact. (D) a thorough quarterly workshop captures requirements well but is both slow and a single point in time when the need is iterative. All four engage the users more than today; the margin is direct, recurring user contact with the increment versus mediated or one-time engagement.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- No strawman: a PO-led validation session, a workflow-observation visit, and a requirements workshop are all real engagement techniques. The discrimination is recurring first-hand user contact with the increment versus mediated or one-off alternatives.
