# Question

A previously supportive stakeholder is now pressing for changes that would breach the project's quality standards. The PM senses she is herself under pressure from her own leadership. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the requests and remind her that quality is a non-negotiable baseline on the project.
- **B**: Implement the requested changes and capture them as a formal exception in the quality plan.
- **C**: Acknowledge the pressure, explore the underlying need, and find alternatives that preserve quality.
- **D**: Flag the change in stakeholder behaviour to the sponsor as a new project risk to track.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's relationship model says start with empathy and joint problem-solving — most quality-pressuring requests come from an unmet underlying need that can be met another way. (A) refuses without acknowledging her situation and damages an important alliance. (B) compromises quality through governance theatre and breaches PMI's stand on non-negotiable quality. (D) treats an ally as a threat and skips the direct conversation the PM should have first.
