# Question

On a hybrid manufacturing-automation project, you need to tailor how you collaborate with different stakeholders. Match each stakeholder communication need on the left to the most fitting approach on the right.

## Items

- **1**: The shop-floor supervisors need to flag and resolve day-to-day integration snags quickly and informally as they arise.
- **2**: The executive steering committee needs a concise, decision-oriented view of status, risks and key choices on a regular cadence.
- **3**: The plant-safety officer needs an auditable, traceable record of every change that could affect operational safety.

## Matches

- **A**: A formal written change log and sign-off trail that captures each safety-relevant decision.
- **B**: A short, structured periodic report focused on the decisions and risks the committee must act on.
- **C**: A quick, interactive channel such as a stand-up or team chat where issues can be raised and cleared on the spot.

## Answer

1-C, 2-B, 3-A

## Explanation

Effective collaboration tailors the channel to each stakeholder's actual need. The shop-floor supervisors (1) need speed and informality, best served by an interactive real-time channel (C). The steering committee (2) needs to make decisions efficiently, so a concise structured periodic report (B) fits. The safety officer (3) needs traceability and accountability, which a formal written change log with sign-offs (A) provides. Mismatching these — for example forcing supervisors through formal logs or giving executives raw chat threads — would degrade rather than aid collaboration.
