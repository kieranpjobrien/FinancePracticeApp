# Question

A capital project will spend AUD 14 million across 18 months. What financial governance design is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Tiered spending authority across PM, sponsor, and steering committee, with monthly variance reporting and a defined CCB.
- **B**: Allow the PM to approve all expenditures so the project can move quickly, with quarterly summaries to the steering committee for oversight.
- **C**: Require steering committee approval for every invoice over AUD 10,000 to maintain tight control over all project spending.
- **D**: Delegate financial control entirely to the finance business partner who tracks the project and reports spending to leadership.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's financial governance pattern is tiered authority plus regular reporting and a defined route for changes. (A) does all three. (B) gives the PM too much authority for a project of this size and reports too rarely for the spend rate. (C) over-controls to the point of paralysis. (D) confuses tracking with decision authority — finance reports, the project governs.
