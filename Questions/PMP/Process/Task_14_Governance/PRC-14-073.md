# Question

A hybrid project to roll out a national retailer's loyalty programme has a governance charter giving the project manager authority to manage within a cost tolerance of plus or minus 5 percent. A newly confirmed scope clarification will push forecast cost to 8 percent over baseline. The project manager is confident the work is justified and the sponsor is generally supportive. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the sponsor informally whether the overrun is acceptable and proceed on the basis of that conversation.
- **B**: Proceed with the work and inform the governance body at the next routine review, since the sponsor is supportive and the scope clarification is justified.
- **C**: Absorb the overrun by cutting lower-priority scope elsewhere to bring forecast cost back within 5 percent without raising it.
- **D**: Escalate the forecast breach to the governance body named in the charter, because exceeding the cost tolerance is the defined trigger for raising the matter above the project manager's authority.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tolerances mark the edge of delegated authority: inside them the project manager acts freely, but a forecast breach is the charter's own signal to escalate to the named body. An 8 percent forecast against a 5 percent tolerance has breached that boundary, so escalation is required now (D). (B) defers a breach to a routine review and relies on general goodwill rather than the defined trigger. (C) is legitimate only if cutting scope is a genuine, sponsor-endorsed decision, but unilaterally removing scope to stay under tolerance hides a breach and makes a scope decision the project manager may not own. (A) substitutes an informal chat for the governance process the charter specifies, leaving no proper record or authority for the decision.
