# Question

On a predictive project to migrate a council's records to a new document management system, recurring confusion arises about who must be consulted before configuration decisions and who ultimately signs them off. Decisions are being remade because the wrong people were involved or the right people were not. Which tool should the project manager use to remove this confusion at the governance level?

## Options

- **A**: A responsibility assignment matrix that names, for each decision or deliverable, who is accountable, who is responsible, who is consulted, and who is informed.
- **B**: A detailed Gantt chart showing the sequence and duration of all configuration activities.
- **C**: A stakeholder register listing every stakeholder's contact details and interest level.
- **D**: A communications plan setting out the frequency and format of project status updates.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The confusion is about roles in decision-making — who is accountable, who must be consulted, who merely informed — which is exactly what a responsibility assignment matrix (commonly a RACI chart) makes explicit for each decision or deliverable (A). (B) sequences work in time but says nothing about decision roles. (C) identifies who the stakeholders are and how interested they are, but not their authority or consultation role in specific decisions. (D) governs information flow rather than decision rights; knowing how often updates are sent does not establish who signs a configuration decision off. Only the responsibility matrix resolves the accountability ambiguity driving the rework.
