# Question

A predictive energy project is approaching a tollgate where the sponsor must authorise continued funding. The project is amber on schedule but the business case still looks sound. Some stakeholders want to skip the gate for speed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Skip the gate as stakeholders suggest to maintain schedule, since the business case is clearly still sound at this stage.
- **B**: Shorten the gate package to reduce time spent in the review and keep the tollgate formally in place as scheduled.
- **C**: Delay the gate by a month until the schedule recovers so the sponsor sees a green status and approves funding smoothly.
- **D**: Hold the gate as scheduled with an honest position on schedule, cost, and benefits so the sponsor can exercise authority properly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's governance principle treats tollgates as the sponsor's formal decision right and transparency as non-negotiable. (D) preserves both. (A) bypasses designed oversight. (B) tailors effort but signals that gate packages can be trimmed under pressure. (C) manipulates timing so the sponsor sees a managed picture rather than the real one.
