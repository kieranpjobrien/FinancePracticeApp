# Question

On an agile product-development project, the empowered product owner has set a clear backlog priority for the coming quarter. Outside the team's ceremonies, the executive sponsor repeatedly contacts individual developers directly and instructs them to drop their committed work to build small features the sponsor personally favours. The product owner is frustrated and the team is whipsawed between two sources of direction. The sponsor outranks everyone involved. What is the best action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the team to follow the sponsor's instructions whenever they arrive, because the sponsor holds the most senior position.
- **B**: Have a candid conversation with the sponsor about how the governance structure assigns backlog priority to the product owner, and agree that the sponsor's requests will flow through the product owner's prioritisation.
- **C**: Tell the developers to ignore the sponsor's direct requests and escalate any such contact to the project manager.
- **D**: Ask the product owner to insert each of the sponsor's pet features at the top of the backlog as they are raised, to keep the sponsor satisfied.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Seniority does not equate to a decision right the governance structure has assigned elsewhere; the sponsor's direct-to-developer reprioritisation bypasses the product owner's authority over the backlog and destabilises the team. The project manager's job is to protect the governance design by addressing it with the sponsor directly and agreeing that the sponsor's requests will be channelled through the product owner's prioritisation, where they can be weighed against everything else (B). Simply obeying the sponsor (A) ratifies the bypass and renders the product-owner role meaningless. Instructing developers to ignore or deflect the sponsor (C) puts junior staff in conflict with an executive and sidesteps the conversation that only the project manager can have. Auto-inserting every pet feature at the top (D) preserves the appearance of using the product owner while surrendering the prioritisation authority that role exists to exercise.
