# Question

A cross-departmental transformation programme is producing either gridlock or contradictory decisions because every department head insists on full authority over how the change lands in their area. What governance approach best resolves this?

## Options

- **A**: Delegate all change decisions to each department head for their own area to respect functional autonomy across the enterprise.
- **B**: Route every decision to the CEO so a single voice ensures consistency across departments and the wider leadership team.
- **C**: Stand up a change governance board with departmental representation, written decision criteria, and a local-versus-cross-cutting split.
- **D**: Defer the programme until every affected department head signs off on a unified operating model for the end state.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Programme-level change needs governance that balances local expertise against enterprise consistency. (C) gives each department a seat, defines what the board owns versus what stays local, and uses written criteria to keep decisions principled. (A) is exactly the structure that produced the inconsistency. (B) overloads the CEO and ignores departmental knowledge. (D) makes universal consensus a prerequisite and effectively halts the programme.
