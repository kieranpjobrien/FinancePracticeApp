# Question

A PM produces detailed weekly reports for steerco, monthly reports for executives, and daily dashboards for the team. The PM now spends nearly a full day each week on reporting alone. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the full reporting suite since each audience relies on its specific artifact, even though the effort is significant.
- **B**: Rationalise reporting by confirming each audience's real decision needs, consolidating sources, and automating where possible across channels.
- **C**: Drop the weekly steerco report and rely on verbal updates at steerco meetings themselves to save preparation time each week.
- **D**: Have a team member take over all reporting so the PM can focus fully on active delivery rather than reporting.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring applies to governance artifacts as much as process and expects reporting to serve decision needs. (B) tests real needs and consolidates. (A) preserves the workload without questioning whether each report is needed. (C) removes a governance artifact without a replacement that serves steerco. (D) transfers the workload but does not reduce its total size.
