# Question

On a predictive offshore wind-farm project, the steering committee is the named authority for decisions above the project manager's tolerances. It has lost quorum and cancelled two of its last three meetings, and several above-tolerance decisions are now blocking critical-path work. The project manager has noted the resulting delays in successive status reports, but nothing has changed. What is the most effective action for the project manager to take?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the sponsor directly about the committee's quorum failures and their critical-path impact, and work with the sponsor to fix how the governance body operates so decisions can flow again.
- **B**: Make the blocked above-tolerance decisions now to protect the critical path, documenting each one and tabling them for committee ratification once it is quorate again.
- **C**: Ask the committee chair to grant the project manager temporary authority to clear the backlog of above-tolerance decisions until the quorum problem is resolved.
- **D**: Propose that the committee delegate a standing quorum to a smaller subset of its members so that routine above-tolerance decisions can be taken without the full body.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When the governance body itself stops functioning, the remedy is to repair the governance, and the sponsor — the project's accountable champion — is the right person to engage to fix the committee's quorum, membership, or delegation so decisions can flow. Crucially, the fix to the structure should be made by the right authority, not improvised by the project manager. (B) is the strongest distractor because the critical-path pressure is real and "decide now, ratify later" feels responsible, but it has the project manager exercise an authority they do not hold; documenting and back-tabling does not supply the authorisation, and a later refusal would leave decisions taken without standing. (C) at least seeks a grant of authority, but a single chair handing the committee's collective decision rights to the project manager is not how the body's authority is legitimately reassigned, and it concentrates above-tolerance calls in one person. (D) names a sensible remedy — a delegated standing quorum — but routes it as the project manager's proposal to the malfunctioning committee that cannot even convene; such a change to the governance structure should be driven through the sponsor, which is what (A) does.
