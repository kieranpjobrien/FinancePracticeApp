# Question

A time-sensitive vendor decision needs sign-off from the technical review board, the business committee, and the executive steering group. Each meets monthly on different cycles, and the next time all three have already met would be six weeks away. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the standard meeting cycle and explain the resulting delay to the vendor as a normal cost of governance.
- **B**: Invoke the project's expedited approval mechanism, requesting out-of-session decisions or chair-delegated approvals from each body.
- **C**: Take the decision under your own authority and ask the three groups to ratify it at their next regular meetings.
- **D**: Combine the three approval requests into a single meeting hosted by the sponsor without first consulting the chairs of each body.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mature governance frameworks include expedited paths for time-critical decisions: out-of-session approvals, delegated authority to a chair, or emergency convenings. (B) uses governance as designed rather than around it. (A) accepts an avoidable six-week delay. (C) acts beyond the PM's authority and uses ratification as a fig leaf. (D) tries to short-circuit governance by reorganising meetings without the chairs' agreement, which is likely to backfire.
