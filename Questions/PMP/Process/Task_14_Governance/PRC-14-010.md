# Question

A project manager on a hybrid logistics-systems programme is finalising the governance structure and must route four recurring decision types to the body or role that holds the appropriate authority. Match each decision to the most appropriate decision-maker under a sound governance design.

## Items

- **1**: Reprioritising the order of features within the team's product backlog for the coming iteration.
- **2**: Approving a change that increases the approved programme budget by 15%.
- **3**: Deciding the day-to-day technical approach for building an agreed backlog item.
- **4**: Confirming that the programme's expected business benefits remain valid and worth pursuing.

## Matches

- **A**: The development team.
- **B**: The product owner.
- **C**: The programme sponsor or steering committee.
- **D**: The sponsoring business owner accountable for benefits.

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Sound governance places each decision with the role that legitimately owns it. Backlog prioritisation is the product owner's right, balancing value and business need (1-B). A 15% budget increase exceeds any reasonable delegated tolerance and is a funding decision for the sponsor or steering committee (2-C). How to build an agreed item technically is the self-organising team's call, not management's (3-A). Whether the expected benefits still justify the investment is the accountable business owner's judgement, since they own the benefits case (4-D). The traps are subtle: prioritisation (1) can look like a sponsor decision but is delegated to the product owner, while benefit validity (4) can look like a product-owner decision but rests with the benefit-accountable owner; keeping budget authority (2) and technical method (3) at the correct levels prevents both over-escalation and unauthorised commitment.
