# Question

Your project has tight internal governance with clear authority, but several external partners contribute resources and expect a say in decisions that affect them. The team is unsure which decisions need partner agreement and which can be made internally. How should governance be clarified?

## Options

- **A**: Run every significant decision past the partners to ensure the working relationships stay strong throughout delivery.
- **B**: Keep decisions internal and simply inform partners after the fact to preserve decision velocity and internal control.
- **C**: Create a duplicate governance structure for partner-touching work so there is no ambiguity about which rules apply where.
- **D**: Define decision categories in writing — internal-only, joint, and partner-owned — each with its own approval path.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Clarity is produced by explicitly categorising decisions and aligning authority with each category. (D) gives everyone a shared map of who decides what. (A) slows internal work and blurs accountability. (B) is likely to damage partner relationships and may breach agreements. (C) creates parallel structures for interconnected work, which typically multiplies coordination problems rather than solving them.
