# Question

A cross-functional initiative spans four departments. Each department head wants final authority over decisions that affect their area. How should the project manager structure governance?

## Options

- **A**: Centralise all decisions with the project sponsor so there is a single decision authority.
- **B**: Stand up a steering committee that meets weekly with all four department heads to vote on every decision.
- **C**: Define decision rights by topic in a RACI, with a steering committee handling only cross-departmental conflicts.
- **D**: Grant each department head full authority within their area, with no committee involvement.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects governance to clarify who decides what and to escalate only when necessary. (C) does both — operational decisions move quickly, conflicts go to the right forum. (A) overloads the sponsor. (B) is a guaranteed bottleneck. (D) creates silo decisions on a clearly cross-functional initiative.
