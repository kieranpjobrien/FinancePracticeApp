# Question

The steering committee has settled into a pattern where every recommendation is approved with no real discussion. The project manager believes governance is no longer adding value. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Continue running the meetings as they are, since unanimous approvals keep the project moving smoothly through each gate.
- **B**: Deliberately seed the next agenda with provocative items to force debate and re-energise the committee's engagement.
- **C**: Ask the committee chair to cancel the next two meetings to free up senior calendars for higher-value strategic work.
- **D**: Discuss the pattern with the sponsor and propose raising the threshold for committee items or restructuring the tiers.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Rubber-stamping usually means decisions are being made well below the committee or that members are disengaged. (D) raises the structural question with the sponsor, who owns governance design. (A) wastes senior time and erodes the committee's purpose. (B) manufactures conflict instead of diagnosing the root cause. (C) cancels meetings unilaterally and removes oversight without addressing why the meetings have lost value.
