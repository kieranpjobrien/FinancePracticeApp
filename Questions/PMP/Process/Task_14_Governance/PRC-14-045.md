# Question

Your project will hand a new system over to operations at go-live. The current governance structure does not include operations because they were not part of initiation, but they are now raising supportability and run-cost concerns. How should governance be adjusted?

## Options

- **A**: Keep operations out of project governance since they will pick up the system at handover and govern it under their own structures from then on.
- **B**: Add a representative from operations to project governance so their supportability and operational requirements are addressed before deployment.
- **C**: Create a parallel operations advisory group that meets separately and shares minutes with the steering committee.
- **D**: Pause deployment until operations confirms in writing that all of their concerns have been resolved to their satisfaction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operations is a legitimate stakeholder for a system they will own; bringing them inside governance addresses their concerns when changes are still cheap. (B) is the standard PMI move. (A) defers known concerns and risks a failed handover. (C) adds coordination overhead without giving operations real decision rights. (D) treats deployment as conditional on operations veto, which is disproportionate and should follow integration into governance, not replace it.
