# Question

A project manager on a hybrid programme has defined escalation rules so that each kind of situation is raised to the body that actually holds authority over it. For each situation below, match it to the destination the governance structure should send it to.

## Items

- **1**: The team is blocked by a dependency on another team within the same programme and cannot resolve it themselves
- **2**: The forecast cost has breached the project's agreed cost tolerance
- **3**: A defect within the team's own sprint that the team can fix without outside help
- **4**: A discovery that the project's underlying business case may no longer be viable

## Matches

- **A**: The team resolves it itself; no escalation
- **B**: The programme-level cross-team forum
- **C**: The steering committee
- **D**: The sponsor

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Effective escalation sends each matter to the lowest body that actually has authority to resolve it. A cross-team dependency the team cannot settle alone belongs to the programme-level forum where teams are coordinated (1-B). A cost-tolerance breach exceeds the project manager's delegated limit and is the steering committee's remit (2-C). A defect the team can fix within its own sprint requires no escalation at all and stays with the team (3-A). A threat to the viability of the business case strikes at what the sponsor owns and must go to the sponsor (4-D). The trap is over-escalating routine team matters or under-escalating a business-case threat; each item turns on whose authority the situation genuinely engages.
