# Question

A project manager is standing up a steering committee for a hybrid programme that will replace a business unit's core operating platform. The change sponsor proposes that the committee consist solely of the IT delivery director and two of the project manager's own delivery leads, on the grounds that they understand the work best. The project manager is uneasy about this composition. What is the most appropriate concern to raise?

## Options

- **A**: The committee is too small to reach a quorum if one member is unavailable for a decision.
- **B**: The delivery leads are too junior to sit alongside the IT delivery director on the same committee.
- **C**: The committee lacks representation from the business unit that will own and operate the outcome, so decisions about value and fitness for purpose would be made without the accountable beneficiary.
- **D**: The IT delivery director may dominate discussion and reduce the influence of the delivery leads.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A steering committee's job is to govern the project on behalf of those accountable for its outcome and benefits, which means it must include the business owner who will live with the result. As proposed, the committee is entirely delivery-side and contains people who report to the project manager, so it would decide on value and fitness for purpose with no voice from the accountable beneficiary — the most serious structural flaw (C). Quorum sizing (A) is a procedural detail that does not address the missing accountability. The seniority of the delivery leads (B) and the risk of the director dominating discussion (D) are minor interpersonal concerns next to the absence of business representation, and including the project manager's own reports also weakens the committee's independence from the delivery it is meant to oversee.
