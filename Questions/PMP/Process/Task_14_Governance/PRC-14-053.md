# Question

An agile programme modernising a city's parking-permit system has made several significant architectural and vendor calls across its first two quarters, taken in a mix of forums as they arose. A new portfolio director joins and asks the project manager to walk her through how those past decisions were reached, by whom, and on what basis, so she can understand the programme's trajectory. The project manager can piece some of it together but finds no consistent place where this is captured. Which action best strengthens the governance going forward?

## Options

- **A**: Stand up a maintained decision log that records, for each governance decision, the body or role that made it, the date, the options weighed, and the rationale.
- **B**: Have every governance forum keep and publish its own meeting minutes so each body's decisions are documented within that forum.
- **C**: Produce a thorough written summary of the decisions taken so far and circulate it to the portfolio director and key stakeholders.
- **D**: Route all significant future decisions to the steering committee so they are taken and recorded consistently in one body.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Transparent, auditable governance needs a single, maintained record of the decision trail — what was decided, by whom, when, and why — that anyone can consult, which is exactly what a standing decision log provides and what serves enquiries like the portfolio director's. (B) is the strongest distractor: per-forum minutes are good practice and do capture decisions, but they scatter the record across many documents with no consolidated, queryable view of the programme's decisions and their rationale, so the trajectory remains hard to follow. (C) answers the director's immediate request but is a one-off backfill that establishes no ongoing discipline, so the same gap recurs the moment the next decision is taken elsewhere. (D) funnels future decisions into one body to fix the record, but that distorts the decision rights — many calls legitimately belong to other forums — and centralising authority is the wrong tool for what is a documentation gap.
