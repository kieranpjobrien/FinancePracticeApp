# Question

A public-sector project is subject to freedom-of-information requests. The PM learns some team members use a personal messaging app for "quick chats" about project decisions that would otherwise be on the record. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the messaging app for speed but ask the team to keep any substantive decisions off it and on record.
- **B**: Ban the messaging app and ensure all project communication runs through approved and auditable official channels only from today.
- **C**: Continue as is since prohibiting informal communication tends to slow project progress across modern distributed delivery teams today.
- **D**: Log informal decisions retrospectively once a week into official records so nothing meaningful falls outside the record later.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats compliance with public-records obligations as non-negotiable and the channel itself is the control. (B) closes the exposure. (A) relies on individual judgement under speed pressure. (C) accepts a clear compliance breach for convenience. (D) treats the symptom while the exposed channel keeps operating.
