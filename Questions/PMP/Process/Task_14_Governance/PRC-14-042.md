# Question

On a predictive project to build a desalination plant, the governance charter lets the project manager absorb schedule and cost variance up to set limits and routes anything beyond them to the steering committee, which next meets in four weeks. A supplier failure has pushed the forecast completion date past the schedule limit; cost is unaffected. The project manager has begun drafting a recovery plan that, if it works, could pull the date back inside the limit before the committee ever meets — but its success is not yet certain. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Notify the steering committee of the position now, ahead of its scheduled meeting, and let it decide whether to wait for the recovery, intervene, or re-baseline.
- **B**: Press on with the recovery plan and, if the date is back within the limit by the meeting, report the excursion and its recovery as a single closed item at the routine sitting.
- **C**: Continue the recovery and escalate only if, once the plan is complete, the forecast still sits outside the limit.
- **D**: Brief the sponsor on the slippage and ask the sponsor to judge whether it warrants pulling the committee together early.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The forecast is already outside the band, so the matter has crossed out of the project manager's delegated authority and into the committee's — and the choice of how to respond (wait for the recovery, step in, re-baseline) is now the committee's to make, not the project manager's to pre-empt. (B) is the sharpest distractor because it looks efficient and may even avoid a formal breach on paper, but it lets the project manager decide that the committee need not hear about an out-of-tolerance forecast until it is conveniently resolved — substituting the project manager's judgement for the committee's right to be informed when the trigger fires. (C) makes the same bet later: if the recovery works the committee never learns authority was exceeded, and if it fails the escalation is simply delayed past critical time. (D) hands a decision the charter assigns to the committee to the sponsor informally, bypassing the named body.
