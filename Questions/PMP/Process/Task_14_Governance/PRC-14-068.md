# Question

A retailer is running five agile teams that together build a single e-commerce platform. Each team has its own product owner, and cross-team dependencies are frequently discovered late, causing teams to block one another near release. Leadership wants a governance addition that resolves competing priorities across the teams without dictating how any individual team works internally. What should the project manager establish?

## Options

- **A**: A single combined backlog owned by one lead product owner who sets priorities for all five teams.
- **B**: A cross-team governance forum where the product owners and key stakeholders align priorities, sequence shared dependencies, and resolve conflicts that no single team can settle.
- **C**: A weekly status meeting where each team reports progress so leadership can see where blocks are occurring.
- **D**: A rule that any cross-team dependency must be escalated to the sponsor for prioritisation as soon as it is found.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The problem is an absence of governance at the level where teams intersect: priorities and dependencies that span teams have no body empowered to resolve them. A cross-team forum of product owners and stakeholders supplies that decision-making layer while leaving each team's internal way of working intact (B). (A) over-centralises by collapsing five product-owner roles into one, removing the local ownership that makes each team effective and going beyond "without dictating how teams work". (C) creates visibility but no decision authority, so blocks are seen but not resolved. (D) routes every dependency to the sponsor, overloading a single senior individual with operational sequencing that the product owners are better placed to handle collectively.
