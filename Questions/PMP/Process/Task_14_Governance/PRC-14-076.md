# Question

Halfway through a hybrid programme to build a regional rail signalling upgrade, the sponsor wants confidence that the programme is genuinely being run the way the governance framework requires, not merely that the project manager reports it is. Which characteristics should the assurance arrangement have to give the sponsor that confidence? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: It is carried out by people who are independent of the programme's delivery line.
- **B**: It evaluates whether the programme is being managed in line with the agreed governance framework and reports findings to the sponsor.
- **C**: It replaces the project manager's regular status reporting to the steering committee.
- **D**: It is performed by the delivery teams themselves through a periodic self-assessment checklist.
- **E**: It takes over decision-making authority for the programme during each review period.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Assurance gives independent confidence precisely because it is conducted by people outside the delivery line (A) and because it tests the programme against the agreed governance framework and reports to the sponsor who commissioned it (B). (D) describes the delivery teams reporting on themselves, which is the very dependence on self-reporting the sponsor is trying to get beyond, so it cannot supply independent confidence. (C) confuses two distinct functions: assurance complements management reporting rather than replacing it, and removing the project manager's reporting would weaken, not strengthen, oversight. (E) misunderstands the role; assurance reviews and advises but does not assume decision-making authority, which remains with the governance bodies and the project manager.
