# Question

The project sponsor has attended only one of the last six steering committee meetings and delegates to a rotating deputy each time. Decisions taken with a deputy are regularly overturned later by the sponsor. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with the sponsor one-to-one to understand engagement constraints and reset governance expectations, decision authority, and deputy arrangements.
- **B**: Escalate over the sponsor's head to their line manager asking for a more engaged sponsor to be appointed to the project.
- **C**: Continue accepting deputies and build stronger written minutes so overturned decisions are at least well documented afterwards in future.
- **D**: Ask the steering committee to adopt a policy that deputy decisions are binding and cannot be overturned by the sponsor later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI places stakeholder engagement and governance clarity first on the PM's own shoulders. (A) addresses the root cause with the accountable person. (B) is premature escalation bypassing a direct conversation. (C) treats the symptom of overturning by improving paperwork only. (D) attempts to bind an executive through committee rules rather than addressing engagement.
