# Question

An agile team at a media company has been delivering smoothly, already tracking value through its own outcome metrics on a live dashboard. A newly formed enterprise PMO issues a governance standard requiring all projects to report value delivered against funded epics each quarter using a common benefits-tracking template. The team's existing metrics cover much of the same ground in a different shape, and the team is wary of duplicated reporting overhead. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the enterprise standard and integrate the quarterly value reporting into the team's existing cadence, mapping the team's current metrics onto the common template so the data is produced once.
- **B**: Propose to the PMO that the team's existing dashboard be accepted in place of the common template, since it already reports value and the team knows it.
- **C**: Begin reporting against funded epics on the common template, and retire the team's own outcome dashboard to avoid maintaining two views of value.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to approve a deferral so the team can continue as it is for now and take on the standard at the start of the next funding cycle.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Project governance must align with the organisation's governance, and an enterprise PMO standard is a legitimate exercise of organisational oversight to be operated within, not opted out of. The efficient way to comply is to adopt the standard and fold the quarterly reporting into the existing cadence, mapping the team's current metrics onto the common template so the underlying data is captured once and the overhead the team fears is minimised. (B) is a reasonable-sounding negotiation, but it asks the organisation to bend its common standard to one team's bespoke artefact; the comparable reporting need is met by mapping onto the standard, not by seeking to displace it, and a single team's preference is a weak basis for an exception. (C) complies but over-corrects by discarding the team's own outcome dashboard, throwing away delivery information the team uses to steer; aligning with the standard does not require abandoning useful local tooling. (D) defers a current, applicable requirement on grounds of timing convenience, leaving the project outside organisational governance in the interim when the requirement could simply be integrated now.
