# Question

A global programme has stakeholders in North America, EMEA, and APAC, each with different regulators and meeting expectations. Synchronous full-group meetings are difficult to schedule. How should governance be designed?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a single weekly governance meeting at HQ time, and produce detailed minutes for the other regions to read afterwards.
- **B**: Tier governance so regional decision-makers handle region-specific matters with async decisions across regions where possible.
- **C**: Rotate the meeting time week-to-week so each region is inconvenienced equally on a fair and transparent basis.
- **D**: Designate one region as the lead and have all decisions flow through that region's existing governance and approval chain.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring for distributed governance combines tiered authority with async-first decision flow. (B) does both. (A) is one-sided and risks missing regional regulatory differences. (C) addresses meeting time but not decision authority. (D) ignores legitimate regional autonomy and regulatory differences.
