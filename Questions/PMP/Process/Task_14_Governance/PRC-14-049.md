# Question

A PM discovers that one steering committee member holds shares in a vendor being evaluated for a major project contract. The member has not disclosed the interest and continues to participate in vendor discussions. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the potential conflict privately with the steering committee chair and the member so disclosure and recusal follow.
- **B**: Remove the member from vendor discussions discreetly without raising the conflict explicitly with anyone else at all afterwards.
- **C**: Say nothing since the PM lacks formal authority to challenge steering committee members about their personal financial interests directly.
- **D**: Report the member directly to external regulators for failing to disclose a material conflict of interest to them immediately.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's code of ethics requires the PM to raise conflicts transparently and through proper channels. (A) engages the chair and member appropriately. (B) handles the conflict informally and leaves no audit trail. (C) ignores an ethical duty the PM holds regardless of formal authority. (D) skips internal channels and escalates externally without first raising it internally.
