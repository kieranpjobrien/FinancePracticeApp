# Question

A scaled agile programme delivering a retail loyalty platform has five teams. The governance framework requires that any decision affecting more than one team be approved by a cross-team governance forum that meets fortnightly. Over three sprints, teams report that genuinely small cross-team decisions are queuing for the forum and stalling flow, while the forum spends most of its time waving through low-risk items. The teams are pressing the project manager to do something about the delays. What is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Increase the cross-team forum's frequency to weekly so decisions clear the queue faster.
- **B**: Propose tailoring the governance framework so that low-risk cross-team decisions are delegated to the teams under defined criteria, reserving the forum for higher-impact matters.
- **C**: Allow teams to proceed with small cross-team decisions immediately and seek the forum's ratification afterwards.
- **D**: Remove the cross-team forum requirement, since the teams have shown they can coordinate informally.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Governance should be right-sized to the level of risk, and the symptom here — a forum congested with low-risk items while flow stalls — points to a mismatch between the decision threshold and the actual stakes. Proposing to tailor the framework so low-risk cross-team decisions are delegated under clear criteria, while the forum keeps high-impact matters, fixes the threshold rather than just the throughput, preserving oversight where it counts. (A) makes the forum faster but still funnels trivial decisions through a governance body, treating a design problem as a scheduling problem. (C) decide-then-ratify erodes the forum's authority and risks decisions that are hard to unwind if later rejected. (D) over-corrects by deleting cross-team oversight entirely, exposing the programme to exactly the high-impact misalignments the forum was created to catch.
