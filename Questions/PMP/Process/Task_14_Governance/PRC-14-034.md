# Question

In a projectised organisational structure, who typically holds the most authority over assigned project resources?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, who directs the work, manages performance, and controls the project budget.
- **B**: The functional manager who owns the home department of each team member.
- **C**: The PMO director, who allocates staff across the portfolio and signs off timesheets.
- **D**: The HR business partner, who approves any reassignment of staff between projects.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a projectised structure, staff report into projects, and the PM holds line authority over their work, performance, and budget for the duration. (A) reflects that. (B) describes a functional or weak matrix structure. (C) describes a portfolio governance role rather than day-to-day authority. (D) confuses HR administration with project authority.
