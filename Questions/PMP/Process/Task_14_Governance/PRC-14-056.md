# Question

A newly agile organisation still has a traditional steering committee expecting monthly detailed plans and Gantt charts from each Scrum team. The teams feel the ceremony drains focus. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the steering committee with a product council focused on outcomes, investment, and risk, with demos replacing Gantt reviews.
- **B**: Keep the steering committee unchanged and ask each team to produce the Gantt charts the committee wants monthly.
- **C**: Keep the steering committee but reduce its frequency from monthly to quarterly so the teams have more time to deliver value.
- **D**: Remove the steering committee entirely and rely on sprint reviews to provide all the governance visibility the organisation needs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring asks governance to fit delivery, not the other way around. (A) redesigns the body to suit outcome-led delivery. (B) keeps ceremonial reporting that does not serve the agile approach. (C) reduces frequency without redesigning content or purpose. (D) removes governance rather than reshaping it for an agile context.
