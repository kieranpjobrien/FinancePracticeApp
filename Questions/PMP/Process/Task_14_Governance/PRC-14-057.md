# Question

A RACI established at initiation no longer reflects how work is actually being done. Several "accountable" people have left and new people are doing the work without formal accountability assigned. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the original RACI as the formal record and informally note who is really doing what so delivery continues smoothly today.
- **B**: Rewrite the RACI alone based on your current view and circulate it to the team afterwards for their information only.
- **C**: Facilitate a session with current role-holders and the sponsor to refresh the RACI and reconfirm specific accountabilities explicitly.
- **D**: Wait for the next project phase, when a formal replan is due anyway, and refresh the RACI at that point.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's governance principle keeps accountabilities explicit and collaboratively owned. (C) refreshes the structure with the people and sponsor. (A) keeps a fiction that will fail under audit. (B) imposes changes without the discussion that drives genuine buy-in. (D) defers clarity that is needed now to avoid accountability gaps.
