# Question

A programme with five workstreams has workstream leads resolving issues within their streams but rarely raising cross-stream impacts. Integration defects are appearing late and expensively. What should the programme manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a weekly integration forum for workstream leads with clear escalation triggers for cross-stream impacts and risks.
- **B**: Require every workstream lead to raise any issue, large or small, so programme-level oversight captures everything happening.
- **C**: Add a senior integration architect who overrides workstream leads when integration impacts are at risk of being missed.
- **D**: Escalate each integration defect to the sponsor as it appears so the sponsor can drive cross-workstream coordination.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's programme governance uses a standing integration forum to make cross-stream dependencies visible proactively. (A) matches that pattern. (B) floods governance with noise and hides the signals. (C) replaces collaboration with command. (D) relies on the sponsor to do integration work that sits with the programme manager.
