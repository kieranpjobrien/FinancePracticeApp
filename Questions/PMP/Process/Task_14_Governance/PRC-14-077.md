# Question

A project manager is launching an agile project to build a crowdfunding platform for a non-profit and wants to establish lightweight but effective governance from the outset. Which actions genuinely establish the governance structure rather than just the delivery plan? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Agreeing who sponsors the project and what decisions that sponsor is accountable for.
- **B**: Defining the tolerances within which the team may operate before a matter must be escalated.
- **C**: Estimating the story points for the first two sprints of the product backlog.
- **D**: Establishing how and to whom the team escalates impediments it cannot resolve itself.
- **E**: Selecting the team's preferred task-tracking tool and configuring its columns.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Establishing governance means defining authority, boundaries and escalation. Agreeing the sponsor's accountabilities (A), setting the tolerances that bound the team's independent action (B), and defining the impediment escalation path (D) are all acts of allocating decision rights and oversight. (C) is delivery estimation that belongs to sprint planning, not governance; knowing the size of upcoming work says nothing about who is authorised to decide what. (E) is team tooling logistics, useful but irrelevant to authority structures. The distinguishing test is whether the action determines who may decide and when matters move up — only A, B and D do.
