# Question

On an agile software team, developers repeatedly pause work mid-iteration to ask the project manager whether a particular feature interpretation matches what the business wants, and the project manager then has to track down a business representative. Iteration throughput is suffering from the back-and-forth. Which governance adjustment most directly resolves this?

## Options

- **A**: Empower a product owner with the authority to make scope and priority decisions on behalf of the business, available to the team throughout the iteration.
- **B**: Schedule a daily call with the business sponsor so feature questions can be answered within twenty-four hours.
- **C**: Require developers to log all feature questions and resolve them in a batch at the iteration review.
- **D**: Have the project manager document each business decision in the change log so interpretations are consistent over time.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The friction comes from no single empowered voice of the business being continuously accessible to the team, so decisions stall and route through the project manager. Agile governance vests product-scope and priority decision rights in an empowered product owner who is available to the team, which is precisely the role missing here (A). A daily sponsor call (B) still introduces up to a day's delay and burdens a senior stakeholder with decisions a product owner should own. Batching questions to the iteration review (C) defers answers until the increment is effectively finished, defeating the purpose of asking mid-iteration. Logging decisions in the change log (D) improves traceability but does nothing to provide a timely decision-maker, which is the actual bottleneck.
