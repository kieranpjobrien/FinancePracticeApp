# Question

A steering committee overseeing an agile data-migration project complains that it feels "in the dark" between its monthly meetings and only learns of problems when they have already become serious. The team works in two-week iterations and produces a working increment each time. The project manager wants to redesign how the committee receives information so governance is based on visible, current reality rather than periodic narrative. Which THREE practices best achieve this?

## Options

- **A**: Give the committee standing access to the team's information radiators — the board, burn-up, and impediment list — that the team already maintains.
- **B**: Invite committee members to observe iteration reviews, where they see the actual increment and the team's empirical progress.
- **C**: Replace the monthly meeting with a polished monthly slide pack prepared by the project manager summarising status in narrative form.
- **D**: Surface significant impediments and risks to the committee as they arise, rather than holding them for the monthly cycle.
- **E**: Restrict committee visibility to a single agreed end-of-quarter checkpoint to avoid distracting the team with frequent oversight.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

The committee's blind spots come from relying on month-old narrative, so the fix is to base governance on the same current, empirical evidence the team already generates. Standing access to live information radiators (A) and observing iteration reviews of the real increment (B) let the committee see actual progress rather than a curated account, and surfacing significant impediments as they arise (D) closes the gap where problems were festering between meetings. A polished narrative slide pack (C) is exactly the periodic, interpreted reporting that created the blind spots — it adds preparation effort without adding transparency. Restricting visibility to a single quarterly checkpoint (E) moves in the opposite direction, widening the interval in which problems can grow unseen, which is the very failure the committee is reporting.
