# Question

A PM inherits a programme where every operational decision reaches the steering committee, which meets monthly. Decisions take six weeks. Most items could clearly be resolved at PM or sponsor level. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Define and agree a decision-rights matrix with the sponsor and steerco that delegates operational decisions with clear thresholds.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor to take more decisions off the steerco agenda personally to free up committee time for strategic items.
- **C**: Continue taking all items to steerco and simply work to reduce preparation time so each decision arrives there faster.
- **D**: Make routine operational decisions yourself without steerco review and inform them after the fact at the next meeting.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI addresses decision-latency by clarifying authority at the right level through a formal governance structure. (A) is the structured solution. (B) fixes sponsor behaviour without establishing a durable structure. (C) accepts the six-week cycle as inevitable. (D) exceeds the PM's authority and erodes governance trust.
