# Question

During a governance review of a large predictive rail-signalling project, four oversight responsibilities are found to be sitting with the wrong parties, causing confusion and slow decisions. Match each responsibility to the role that should properly hold it under a sound governance structure.

## Items

- **1**: Championing the project at executive level, securing funding, and owning the business case and its benefits.
- **2**: Providing independent assurance to senior management that the project is being managed to standard and reporting findings objectively.
- **3**: Making collective directional decisions that exceed the project manager's authority and resolving cross-functional trade-offs.
- **4**: Managing day-to-day delivery within the tolerances and decision rights delegated by governance.

## Matches

- **A**: The project management office (PMO) assurance function.
- **B**: The executive sponsor.
- **C**: The steering committee.
- **D**: The project manager.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Each governance responsibility maps to a distinct accountability. Championing the project, securing funding, and owning the business case belong to the executive sponsor (1-B). Independent, objective assurance to senior management is the PMO assurance function's remit precisely because it is independent of delivery (2-A). Collective directional decisions above the project manager's authority and cross-functional trade-offs are the steering committee's role (3-C). Managing delivery within delegated tolerances is the project manager's job (4-D). The likely confusions are pairing assurance with the steering committee — but assurance must stay independent of the body it informs — and blurring the sponsor's single-point benefit accountability (1) with the committee's collective decision-making (3); keeping these separated is what makes the structure work.
