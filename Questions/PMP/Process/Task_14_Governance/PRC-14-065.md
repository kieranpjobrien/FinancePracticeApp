# Question

A pharmaceutical company is funding a digital health product through an agile delivery team. The finance director is uncomfortable releasing the full annual budget at once and wants the ability to stop the investment if early increments do not show value, while the team needs predictable funding to stay engaged across sprints. The project manager is asked to recommend a governance funding approach that satisfies both concerns. Which approach should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Release funding incrementally against agreed value-review points, where the steering body confirms continued investment after reviewing demonstrated outcomes at the end of each funding increment.
- **B**: Release the full annual budget up front so the team has stability, and rely on the project manager's status reports to alert finance if value fails to materialise.
- **C**: Fund the project one sprint at a time, with the steering body approving each sprint's budget based on the previous sprint's burndown chart.
- **D**: Defer all funding decisions to the finance director, who releases money monthly at their own discretion based on whatever evidence is available at the time.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Incremental funding tied to defined value-review points is the governance mechanism that reconciles the two concerns: the funder keeps the option to stop or redirect investment at known decision points, while the team receives a committed funding increment spanning several sprints rather than living hand-to-mouth. (B) gives stability but removes the funder's stop option entirely, which is the specific control finance requested, and a status report is not a funding decision point. (C) preserves the stop option but at a cadence so tight that it destroys the predictability the team needs and burdens the steering body with constant micro-approvals. (D) hands a governance decision to one individual on an undefined evidentiary basis, replacing a transparent gate with personal discretion.
