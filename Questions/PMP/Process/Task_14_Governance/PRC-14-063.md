# Question

A sponsor has delegated financial authority of up to $50,000 to the PM for non-scope items. The PM encounters a $48,000 purchase that solves a delivery risk but also sets a precedent the sponsor may dislike. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the purchase under delegated authority since it falls within the agreed financial threshold set by the sponsor today.
- **B**: Split the purchase across two invoices of $24,000 so the sponsor is not unnecessarily involved in operational decisions now.
- **C**: Inform the sponsor of the decision, the reasoning, and the precedent consideration, then act under your delegated authority.
- **D**: Escalate the full decision to the sponsor so they can approve or reject personally, even though it is below threshold.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's governance principle uses delegated authority with transparency, especially where precedents may matter to the sponsor. (C) exercises authority responsibly with disclosure. (A) acts correctly but hides the precedent consideration. (B) is invoice-splitting which breaches procurement ethics. (D) surrenders delegated authority and overloads the sponsor.
