# Question

A project manager is documenting a governance structure for a manufacturing automation programme and must make clear which body or role is accountable for each decision so that escalations land in the right place. Match each decision in the project to the governance role or body that holds the authority to make it.

## Items

- **1**: Approving a change that increases the project budget beyond the approved baseline
- **2**: Deciding whether the project still has a sound business case and should continue to be funded
- **3**: Reassigning a team member between work packages within the agreed schedule and cost tolerances
- **4**: Approving a change to a shared enterprise data standard used by other programmes

## Matches

- **A**: Project manager
- **B**: Steering committee
- **C**: Sponsor
- **D**: Enterprise architecture review board

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Each decision maps to the level whose authority it engages. A baseline budget increase exceeds the project manager's delegated limit and affects multiple stakeholder interests, so it belongs to the steering committee (1-B). Judging whether the business case remains sound and worth funding is the sponsor's defining accountability, as the sponsor owns the business case (2-C). Moving a person between work packages while staying inside agreed tolerances is routine delivery management within the project manager's delegated authority (3-A). A change to a shared enterprise data standard reaches beyond the project into other programmes, which is exactly why an enterprise architecture review board, not the project's own bodies, holds that decision right (4-D). The discriminator throughout is organisational reach and financial size, not merely how technical or urgent the decision feels.
