# Question

A growing project now has 32 team members across five locations. Governance designed for an original small team no longer scales: stand-ups run 45 minutes, decisions stall, and the PM is a bottleneck. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the current structure and simply enforce a stricter time limit on the daily stand-up to keep meetings short.
- **B**: Have the PM personally sit in on every sub-team decision so cross-team alignment stays strong and nothing is missed.
- **C**: Restructure into smaller pods with clear owners, local decision rights, and a slimmed-down cross-pod coordination forum running weekly.
- **D**: Escalate the scaling issue to the sponsor and ask for a second project manager to help share the coordination workload.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's governance scales by delegation and structure, not by extending existing routines. (C) redesigns for the new size. (A) treats a 45-minute stand-up as a time-management problem. (B) makes the PM bottleneck even more central. (D) requests more resource rather than redesigning the structure.
