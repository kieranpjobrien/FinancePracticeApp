# Question

A PM's onshore steering committee expects blunt challenge at meetings, while the offshore delivery lead's culture favours consensus before meetings and avoids disagreement in front of seniors. Decisions feel cosmetic in meetings. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let each location handle decisions in the style that fits its own culture, accepting some duplication in the process.
- **B**: Facilitate a governance discussion with both sides to design meeting norms and pre-alignment steps that respect both cultures.
- **C**: Ask the offshore lead to adopt the onshore style in meetings since the senior decision-makers sit onshore.
- **D**: Ask the onshore committee to soften its style so the offshore lead feels able to contribute openly during meetings.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement and cultural awareness principle designs governance norms jointly with those who must live under them. (B) does that. (A) formalises incoherence in decision-making. (C) imposes onshore norms on offshore participants. (D) imposes the opposite without addressing underlying pre-alignment practice.
