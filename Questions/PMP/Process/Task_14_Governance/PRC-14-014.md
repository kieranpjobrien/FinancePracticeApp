# Question

Three months into a predictive water-treatment project, a stakeholder challenges a key technical decision and asks the project manager to explain who authorised it and on what basis. The project manager finds that decisions have been made in meetings but never captured in a way that records who decided, when, and why. What governance practice should the project manager establish to prevent this in future?

## Options

- **A**: A weekly status report circulated to all stakeholders summarising progress against the schedule.
- **B**: A decision log that records each governance decision, the authority who made it, the date, and the rationale.
- **C**: A communications management plan defining how often each stakeholder receives updates.
- **D**: A lessons-learned register capturing what the team would do differently next time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The stakeholder's challenge exposes a missing audit trail for decisions — there is no record of who decided, when, or why. A decision log directly fills this gap, giving traceable accountability for each governance decision (B). A status report (A) communicates progress but does not record decision authority or rationale. A communications management plan (C) governs the flow of information to stakeholders, not the capture of decisions and the authority behind them. A lessons-learned register (D) captures improvement insights for the future and is not the contemporaneous record of who authorised a given decision that the situation calls for.
