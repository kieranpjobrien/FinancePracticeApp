# Question

The project sponsor is rarely available and decisions that require their approval are stacking up. The PM has tried to get time three times. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the pending decisions independently to keep the project moving, documenting each one for later ratification.
- **B**: Pause the project formally until the sponsor engages and the backlog of pending decisions is resolved.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor's manager so the manager can require the sponsor to engage on the backlog promptly.
- **D**: Meet the sponsor to agree delegated authority for routine decisions, name an alternate, and define a light touchpoint.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI puts direct conversation with the sponsor before escalation, and asks the PM to design a workable rhythm. (D) does both — and uses governance tools (delegated authority, alternate) rather than just asking for more meetings. (A) exceeds authority. (B) is a heavy-handed move that hurts the project. (C) escalates over the sponsor's head and damages trust before direct options have been exhausted.
