# Question

A new PMP is asked to explain the difference between a quality audit and a quality inspection to a junior team member. Which explanation is most accurate?

## Options

- **A**: Audits are always external and inspections are always internal.
- **B**: Audits are mandatory under regulation; inspections are optional internal practices.
- **C**: Audits review whether quality processes are being followed; inspections check whether deliverables meet requirements.
- **D**: Audits are performed at project closure; inspections are performed during execution.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI defines the distinction by focus: an audit (part of Manage Quality) examines processes and adherence; an inspection (part of Control Quality) examines the deliverable against requirements. (A) is wrong because either can be internal or external. (B) confuses regulatory triggers with the intrinsic difference. (D) imposes timing that does not reflect practice — both happen iteratively throughout a project.
