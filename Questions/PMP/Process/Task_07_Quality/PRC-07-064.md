# Question

User acceptance testing on a predictive HR system reveals users expect features that were never in the requirements. Business analysts argue the requirements document is authoritative and the new items are new scope. Users argue "you should have asked." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the new expectations as out of scope and proceed to production on the current requirements as agreed.
- **B**: Accept the new expectations into scope without change control to keep the users happy and the project relationship intact.
- **C**: Agree the items are new scope, capture them as change requests, and let governance decide which will be accepted into release.
- **D**: Run a facilitated session with users, analysts, and sponsor to agree what is in current scope versus future-release scope.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The immediate issue is a shared-understanding gap; a facilitated session aligns expectations before process steps are taken. (D) is correct. (A) is harsh and misses learning. (B) bypasses change control. (C) is correct in process but premature without the shared-understanding conversation to define what is genuinely new.
