# Question

A manufacturing project's first production run of a moulded enclosure shows a 9% reject rate against a 2% target. The reject tags record a mix of reasons — short shots, warping, surface blemishes, flash, and dimensional drift — logged across several machines, shifts, and material lots. The plant manager wants to add a second inspector to keep rejects from reaching the customer while the team works out what to do. Production continues next week. Which should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Add the second inspector now, then begin investigating, so the customer is protected while the analysis proceeds.
- **B**: Categorise the logged reject reasons and apply a Pareto analysis to find which few causes account for most of the rejects.
- **C**: Convene the line, machine, and materials leads to brainstorm the likely causes on a cause-and-effect diagram across people, machine, material, and method.
- **D**: Compare reject rates by machine, shift, and material lot to isolate whether the problem is concentrated in one source.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The rejects are already recorded against several distinct reasons spread across machines, shifts, and lots, so the data exist but are unprioritised; the first move is to find where the bulk of the loss is so improvement effort is aimed at the vital few rather than spread thin. A Pareto analysis does exactly this and is the natural first cut of categorical defect data (B). (C) is a legitimate next step, but brainstorming causes on a fishbone before the data have told you which defect dominates risks chasing minor categories; the Pareto focuses the fishbone that follows. (D) is also sound analysis, but stratifying by machine and lot is most powerful once Pareto has identified which defect type to stratify — doing it first across all reasons at once is unfocused. (A) is a containment action that protects the customer but does nothing to lower the 9% and, chosen first, spends effort on appraisal before the data have been read. The fine margin is among three genuine analysis moves; Pareto is the correct first because it sequences the others.
