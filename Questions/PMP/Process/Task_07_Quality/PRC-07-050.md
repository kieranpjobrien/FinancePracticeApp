# Question

A Scrum team has been marking stories "done" while deferring unit-test updates to a "cleanup sprint" at the end. Over 12 sprints the tech-debt backlog has grown to hundreds of items. A new team member flags it to the PM. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Dedicate two full sprints to paying down technical debt before any new feature work enters the Scrum backlog.
- **B**: Work with the team to strengthen the Definition of Done so unit tests are part of each story closure going forward.
- **C**: Add a technical-debt story to every sprint at fixed capacity of 10 percent to chip away at the backlog steadily.
- **D**: Hire a dedicated quality engineer to clear the debt backlog while the team continues its current feature pace.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The root cause is a weak Definition of Done that allowed debt to accumulate; fixing it stops the bleeding before addressing stock. (B) is correct. (A) tackles the symptom without changing the system, so debt will reaccumulate. (C) helps steady-state but leaves the DoD weak. (D) offshores a team responsibility and does not prevent future debt.
