# Question

A call-centre improvement project tracks average handle time on a control chart. The last seven plotted points all fall above the mean but remain inside the upper control limit. The team lead says no action is needed because nothing has breached a limit. What should the project manager conclude?

## Options

- **A**: The team lead is correct; with no points outside the control limits the process is stable and requires no investigation.
- **B**: The process should be considered out of control because a run of seven consecutive points on one side of the mean signals a non-random pattern worth investigating.
- **C**: The control limits should be recalculated wider so the recent points are comfortably contained.
- **D**: The specification limits, not the control limits, should be checked to decide whether the process is acceptable.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A control chart signals an out-of-control condition not only when a point breaches a limit but also when non-random patterns appear; the rule of seven (seven consecutive points on one side of the mean) indicates an assignable cause even though every point is inside the limits (B). (A) ignores the run rule. (C) hides the signal by loosening limits rather than investigating it. (D) confuses specification limits (product acceptability) with the process-stability question the chart is answering.
