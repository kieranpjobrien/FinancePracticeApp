# Question

A routine quality audit on a predictive construction project finds the site team is not following the approved inspection procedure: random spot checks replace the mandated 100 percent inspection. No defects have been reported. The site manager says the approach is faster. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the spot-check approach since no defects have been reported, then formally update the procedure to match practice.
- **B**: Reinstate the 100 percent inspection regime and review any work completed under the unauthorised spot-check approach.
- **C**: Log a minor non-conformance in the audit report and continue the current approach until the next regular audit cycle.
- **D**: Escalate the deviation to the sponsor and ask for direction on whether the procedure should be enforced or changed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An unauthorised deviation from the quality plan must be reinstated and prior work reviewed, even if no defects have surfaced yet; compliance is not optional. (B) is correct. (A) rewards circumvention by changing the standard to fit behaviour. (C) accepts known non-conformance. (D) is premature escalation before the PM corrects the known breach.
