# Question

A development team has a high defect rate this quarter. Defect analysis shows that 80% of bugs originated in three modules written by one new developer who joined the team last month. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign all of the new developer's modules to a more senior developer who has historically had a low defect rate on similar work.
- **B**: Add a dedicated QA test phase before each release to catch the additional defects before the customer sees them.
- **C**: Introduce mandatory peer code reviews for all merges, with a stronger focus on the new developer's pull requests, paired with onboarding and mentoring.
- **D**: Submit a change request to remove the new developer from the project and request an experienced replacement from the resource pool.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI prefers prevention over inspection and addressing root causes — here, an onboarding gap — rather than removing the person. (C) puts the prevention mechanism (code review) in place and supports the new developer with mentoring. (A) hides the developer from the work rather than developing them. (B) inspects defects in after they exist rather than preventing them — the more expensive position in the cost-of-quality curve. (D) skips development of a team member and treats an onboarding issue as a performance issue.
