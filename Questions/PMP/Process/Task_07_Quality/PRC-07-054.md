# Question

A statistical control chart for a chemical fill process shows seven consecutive points above the mean but all still within control limits. An operator asks whether to adjust the process. The PM is the quality-responsible party. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Investigate the run as a potential special-cause signal despite the points sitting inside the established control limits.
- **B**: Take no action because all points remain inside the control limits and no individual reading is out of control.
- **C**: Adjust the process by the average offset immediately to recentre the mean back toward its target value now.
- **D**: Tighten the control limits so the run of points above the mean exceeds the revised boundary and triggers an alarm.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A run of seven consecutive points on the same side of the mean is a classic out-of-control signal (rule of seven) even within limits. (A) follows SPC practice. (B) ignores a known signal. (C) over-adjusts without investigation, increasing variation (Deming's funnel experiment). (D) is arbitrary limit tampering, not analysis.
