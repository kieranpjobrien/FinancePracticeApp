# Question

Which statement BEST captures the difference between Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Control (QC)?

## Options

- **A**: QA is performed only by independent testers, while QC is performed only by the developers who produced the deliverable.
- **B**: QA is process-focused and aimed at preventing defects, while QC is product-focused and aimed at detecting defects in deliverables.
- **C**: QA happens during the planning phase, while QC happens after the project has formally closed and is in operations.
- **D**: QA produces the project's quality metrics, while QC produces the project's quality management plan and policies.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) captures the PMI distinction: QA improves processes to prevent defects (proactive), while QC inspects deliverables to detect defects (reactive). (A) is wrong about who performs the work — both can involve multiple roles. (C) is wrong about timing — both are continuous activities throughout execution. (D) reverses the artefact ownership; the quality management plan is a planning output, not a QA output, and metrics may come from either.
