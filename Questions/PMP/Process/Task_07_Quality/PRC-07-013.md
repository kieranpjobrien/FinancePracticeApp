# Question

A development team is delivering features that pass every functional test in the test plan, but end users complain the product is hard to learn and frustrating to use. The product owner is upset. Which quality concept is the team primarily failing to address?

## Options

- **A**: Grade — the product lacks higher-tier features that competitors offer in their premium editions.
- **B**: Gold plating — the team is adding unrequested features that distract from the core experience users want.
- **C**: Cost of conformance — the team is investing too much in QC and not enough in prevention activities upstream.
- **D**: Fitness for purpose — the deliverable conforms to its written requirements but does not satisfy actual user needs.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the right diagnosis: passing functional tests is conformance to specification, while quality also requires fitness for purpose — the product must satisfy the needs it was built to serve. (A) confuses quality with grade; grade is about feature category, not usability. (B) describes the opposite problem (extra unrequested features). (C) is a cost-of-quality term and does not name what is being missed.
