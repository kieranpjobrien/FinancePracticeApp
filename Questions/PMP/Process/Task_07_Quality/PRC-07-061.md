# Question

Your quality report shows all lagging indicators green: defect-escape rate is low and customer-satisfaction scores are high. Leading indicators are amber: test coverage is falling and review turnaround is slowing. The sponsor is happy with the green board and questions the leading-indicator focus. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop leading indicators from the board since lagging outcomes are currently green and the sponsor prefers a simpler view.
- **B**: Continue showing both and explain that amber leading indicators typically precede a fall in lagging outcomes within months.
- **C**: Average leading and lagging indicators into a single quality score so the board shows one colour the sponsor accepts.
- **D**: Review all indicators to see which can be retired and produce a shorter board focused on what the sponsor engages with.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leading indicators predict future performance; removing them when they are amber to preserve a green view is the worst time to drop them. (B) is correct. (A) blinds the sponsor to impending issues. (C) dilutes signal into a meaningless number. (D) is cosmetic and sacrifices analytical value.
