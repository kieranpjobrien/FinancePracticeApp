# Question

A construction project receives precast concrete panels from a supplier. The last two deliveries each contained several panels with surface cracks that were caught at goods-in inspection and returned, costing the schedule a week each time. The supplier insists their process is sound. What is the BEST step for the project manager to take next?

## Options

- **A**: Increase the rigour and sample size of goods-in inspection so no cracked panels reach the site.
- **B**: Arrange a joint review of the supplier's production and curing process to identify and correct the source of the cracking.
- **C**: Add a contingency buffer of spare panels to each order so rejected units do not delay installation.
- **D**: Issue a formal claim against the supplier under the contract's defect clause for the schedule impact.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Goods-in inspection is appraisal that keeps finding the same defects after they are made; the durable fix is to address the cause in the supplier's process, which a joint process review targets (B). (A) intensifies inspection without reducing the defect rate or schedule risk. (C) buys insurance against the symptom while the waste continues. (D) may be contractually appropriate later but does not stop the cracks recurring; quality is improved by preventing defects at source, not by inspecting them out or compensating for them.
