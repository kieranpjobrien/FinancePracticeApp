# Question

A control chart for a manufacturing line shows seven consecutive sample means above the centreline, all of which are within the upper and lower control limits. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Take no action because every point remains inside the control limits, which is the definition of a process being in control.
- **B**: Halt production and quarantine all output produced during the seven samples until full root-cause analysis is complete.
- **C**: Investigate the process for an assignable cause because seven consecutive points on one side of the mean signal a non-random pattern.
- **D**: Recalculate the upper and lower control limits using the seven new samples to recentre the chart on current performance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The "Rule of Seven" in statistical process control treats seven consecutive points on one side of the mean as a non-random pattern requiring investigation, even when the points remain within the control limits. (C) responds correctly. (A) ignores the pattern signal. (B) halts production without evidence of an out-of-control condition; the rule warns of a shift, not necessarily defective output. (D) re-centres the chart on a likely process shift and erases the very signal that needs investigating.
