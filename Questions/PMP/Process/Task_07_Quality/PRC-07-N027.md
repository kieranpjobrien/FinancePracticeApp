# Question

While building the quality management plan for an aerospace component project, the project manager wants to weight investment toward the cost-of-quality categories that prevent defects rather than those that find or fix them. Which of the following are conformance (cost-of-quality) activities the PM would fund to prevent defects? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Training assemblers on the new torque procedure before production begins.
- **B**: Conducting destructive testing on a sample of finished units to confirm they meet strength requirements.
- **C**: Documenting and piloting a standard work instruction so the process is performed consistently.
- **D**: Reworking units that failed final inspection and re-running them through the line.
- **E**: Building a poka-yoke fixture that physically prevents a part from being installed backwards.

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

Prevention costs are conformance costs spent to stop defects occurring: training (A), defining and piloting standard work (C), and mistake-proofing fixtures such as poka-yoke (E) all prevent defects. (B) is appraisal — money spent inspecting/testing to find defects, and being destructive it is also a cost incurred to assess conformance, not prevent it. (D) is an internal failure cost (rework), the most expensive category and the one prevention investment aims to avoid.
