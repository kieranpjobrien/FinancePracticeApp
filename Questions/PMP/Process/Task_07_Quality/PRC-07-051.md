# Question

A defect log for a telecoms release shows: authentication 45, billing 20, UI rendering 12, notifications 10, localisation 8, reports 5. The quality manager invokes the Pareto principle. Which is the correct interpretation to guide action?

## Options

- **A**: Authentication and billing together account for about 65 percent of defects, so they should receive priority root-cause attention.
- **B**: All six categories should receive proportional investigation since Pareto distributions rarely apply to software defect logs.
- **C**: Authentication alone represents 45 percent of defects, so it should be the sole focus until it is below the threshold.
- **D**: Address the smallest categories first because they are easiest to resolve and quickly shorten the defect backlog overall.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pareto says roughly 80 percent of effects come from roughly 20 percent of causes; here, authentication (45) + billing (20) = 65 of 100 defects, the two largest categories. Root-cause focus on these yields the biggest return. (B) denies a well-established pattern. (C) narrows too far and ignores the second-largest contributor. (D) is the anti-Pareto approach.
