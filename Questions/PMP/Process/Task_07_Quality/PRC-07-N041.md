# Question

A Scrum team's Definition of Done requires an accessibility check and a security scan on every story. At the sprint review the product owner accepts a completed feature as valuable, and a tester then mentions that both checks were quietly skipped on this item to make the demo. The same shortcut, it emerges, has been taken on a few earlier items across recent sprints without anyone flagging it. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Move the item back to in-progress for the outstanding checks, and have the team review the wider pattern of skipping the Definition of Done at the retrospective.
- **B**: Move the item back to in-progress for the outstanding checks, then add the earlier skipped items to the backlog so their checks are completed in the next sprint.
- **C**: Have the team complete the outstanding checks on this item before release, and raise the repeated skipping with the team straight away rather than waiting for the retrospective.
- **D**: Treat the item as not done and ask the team to agree, there and then, a lighter Definition of Done that the team can realistically meet every sprint under demo pressure.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The item plainly is not done — Definition of Done not met — so all four options correctly stop it being called done; the decision turns on how to handle the now-visible pattern of skipping it. (A) returns the item and routes the systemic issue to the retrospective, the team's standing forum for inspecting and adapting its own process, which keeps the response team-owned and addresses root cause. (B) fixes this item but treats the earlier skipped items as ordinary new backlog work for the product owner to rank — they are existing DoD breaches that should be remediated, not re-prioritised against features, and it still leaves the behaviour itself unaddressed. (C) is reasonable, but pulling the whole team into an immediate ad hoc discussion mid-review pre-empts the retrospective and reacts before the pattern is examined with the data; the recurring issue is exactly what the retrospective exists to handle. (D) jumps to weakening the quality bar in the heat of a review as the fix for people bypassing it, changing the standard before understanding why it was skipped.
