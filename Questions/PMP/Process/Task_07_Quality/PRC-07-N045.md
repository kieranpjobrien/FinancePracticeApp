# Question

During execution of a pharmaceutical packaging project, a quality audit finds the team has been running production from a superseded version of the labelling procedure; the approved updated version was issued several weeks ago. The labels produced so far are confirmed to conform to the requirement, and the team has already been switched to the current procedure for upcoming runs. The audit notes that two other controlled procedures on the project are accessed the same way. What should the project manager prioritise next?

## Options

- **A**: Investigate how the superseded procedure stayed in use and close the configuration-control weakness so an obsolete document cannot be picked up again.
- **B**: Verify the other two procedures accessed the same way to confirm whether any of them is also being used at a superseded version.
- **C**: Issue a corrective-action request to re-validate the labels already produced under the old procedure, as a documented precaution.
- **D**: Record the finding and the corrective switch in the audit log and brief the team that the labelling procedure has been updated.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Using a superseded procedure is a process non-conformance, and quality assurance fixes the process so the failure cannot recur, not just the instance the audit happened to catch. The immediate product issue is already contained — output conforms and the team is now on the right version — so the highest-leverage next action is to find and close the configuration-control weakness that let an obsolete document stay live. (A) does this, and because it addresses the access mechanism, it also protects the other procedures that share it. (B) is sound and worth doing, but checking the other two procedures detects further instances of the same flaw without removing the flaw; it is appraisal where prevention is available, and fixing the control supersedes screening each document for the symptom. (C) re-validates output already confirmed to conform, spending effort on a product check the evidence does not justify. (D) records and communicates but neither corrects the underlying process nor prevents recurrence.
