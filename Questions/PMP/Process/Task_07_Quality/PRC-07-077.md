# Question

On a hybrid corporate-website rebuild, the agreed requirement for this iteration was a working contact form. Reviewing the increment before the customer demo, the project manager finds the developer has also added animated page transitions and an auto-suggesting address field that were not in the requirements. They work and pass the developer's tests. The demo is tomorrow. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Surface the unrequested additions with the product owner before the demo, so the customer can decide whether they want the extras kept, parked, or dropped.
- **B**: Include the extras in the demo as delivered work, since they function correctly and show the customer added capability they did not have to ask for.
- **C**: Have the developer pull the extras out of the build before the demo, because work outside the agreed requirements should not be shown as if it were committed scope.
- **D**: Keep the extras and log them as an approved scope change so the additional effort is captured against the project's scope baseline.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Delivering more than the agreed requirements without authorisation is gold plating — it consumes effort, can add maintenance burden and new defects, and may not be wanted — but the additions are not automatically worthless either. The first step is to put the decision where it belongs: surface them with the product owner so the customer can choose, rather than the project manager unilaterally keeping or discarding them. (A) is correct. (B) assumes unrequested features equal value, the gold-plating fallacy, and pre-empts the customer's choice. (C) is also unilateral in the opposite direction — the extras might be welcome, so removing them without asking discards possible value and is still the project manager making the call alone. (D) records uncommissioned team work as an approved scope change before anyone has approved it, corrupting the baseline; a change is logged after the customer decides, not as a way of retaining the extras by default.
