# Question

Which of the following BEST distinguishes Quality Assurance (QA) from Quality Control (QC)?

## Options

- **A**: QA focuses on the processes used to produce deliverables (preventive); QC focuses on inspecting the deliverables themselves (detective).
- **B**: QA is performed by senior management; QC is performed by the project team.
- **C**: QA only takes place during the planning phase; QC only takes place during the execution phase.
- **D**: QA is optional in agile projects; QC is mandatory in predictive projects.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

(A) is the PMI distinction: QA is process-focused and preventive; QC is product-focused and detective. (B) confuses the activity with role assignment — both can involve various roles. (C) is wrong about timing; both run continuously. (D) is wrong on both counts — quality discipline applies in both predictive and agile contexts.
