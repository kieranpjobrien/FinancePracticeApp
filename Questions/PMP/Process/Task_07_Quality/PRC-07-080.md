# Question

A project manager on a satellite-ground-station build is allocating activities between quality assurance and quality control responsibilities so the team understands which is which. Which of the following are quality assurance activities, concerned with the adequacy of processes rather than the inspection of specific deliverables? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Conducting a process audit to confirm the team is following the agreed welding procedure across all assemblies.
- **B**: Performing root-cause analysis on a class of recurring defects and improving the process to prevent recurrence.
- **C**: Reviewing and improving the project's quality management processes against organisational standards as the project proceeds.
- **D**: Measuring a fabricated bracket against its dimensional tolerances and recording the result on the inspection sheet.
- **E**: Running the acceptance test on a completed antenna assembly to verify it meets its functional specification.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Quality assurance is process-focused: it asks whether the right processes are in place and being followed, and improves them. Auditing adherence to the welding procedure (A), root-cause analysis with process improvement to prevent recurrence (B), and reviewing and improving the quality processes against standards (C) are all assurance activities. By contrast, quality control is product-focused inspection: measuring a bracket against tolerances (D) and running an acceptance test on a finished assembly (E) examine specific deliverables for conformance, which is control, not assurance. The discrimination is between activities that evaluate or improve the process (A, B, C) and those that inspect a particular output (D, E); root-cause analysis (B) can look deceptively like control because it starts from defects, but its purpose here is to change the process, placing it in assurance.
