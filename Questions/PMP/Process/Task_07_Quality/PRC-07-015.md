# Question

A Scrum team has averaged 30 story points per sprint for the past six sprints. The product owner asks the team to commit to 40 story points next sprint to hit a marketing-driven launch date. What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Support the 40-point commitment because the team should make best efforts to align with business priorities.
- **B**: Approve the higher commitment but reduce the team's Definition of Done for the sprint to make 40 points achievable in the time available.
- **C**: Stand on sustainable pace, share the velocity data with the product owner, and explore scope reduction or a launch-date adjustment as the realistic options.
- **D**: Authorise overtime for the sprint as a one-off gesture so the team can hit the 40 points without changing the Definition of Done.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI agile principles treat sustainable pace as foundational and use historical velocity as the empirical basis for forecasting. (C) presents the data and the realistic levers — scope or date. (A) commits to a number the data does not support and risks burnout or quality failure. (B) trades quality for velocity, which is both anti-agile and dishonest about what is being delivered. (D) normalises overtime as a planning tool and does not address the underlying mismatch between scope and capacity.
