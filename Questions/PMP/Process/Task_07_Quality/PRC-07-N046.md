# Question

On an agile e-commerce team, a developer delivers a checkout story that meets all its acceptance criteria and, unprompted, also adds a currency-conversion widget that was never in any story. It works, but it touches the payment flow and has not been through the team's usual review for that area. At the review the product owner says the widget is actually interesting and might be worth keeping. As project manager, what should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner to extend the current story's acceptance criteria to include the widget so the delivered work can be accepted together as one increment.
- **B**: Have the team accept the checkout story against its criteria, and ask the product owner to create a new backlog item for the widget so it is sized, reviewed, and ordered like any other work.
- **C**: Accept the story but have the widget reverted before release, then let the product owner add it to the backlog if it is genuinely wanted.
- **D**: Treat the whole story as not done until the widget has been through the team's payment-flow review, then accept the story with the widget included.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The unrequested widget is gold-plating: capability added outside the agreed criteria and outside the team's review process for a sensitive area. The product owner's passing interest does not change how it should be handled — new value is decided and ordered through the backlog, not absorbed into a story after the fact. (B) accepts the story on its actual criteria and routes the widget through the normal flow, where it gets properly sized, reviewed, and prioritised. (A) retrofits the criteria to legitimise unplanned work, which distorts the backlog and ships an unreviewed change into the payment flow. (C) is not wrong in principle, but discarding working code outright pre-empts the product owner's decision and is heavier-handed than simply backlogging it. (D) penalises the conforming part of the story by blocking its acceptance on the merits of an addition that should not have been bundled with it in the first place.
