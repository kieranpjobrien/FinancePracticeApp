# Question

Late in a government web portal project, a stakeholder notes that WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility was assumed but never written into the quality acceptance criteria. The portal will serve the general public. The PM discovers several screens currently fail contrast and keyboard-navigation tests. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Release on time with a commitment to remediate accessibility gaps in a post-launch update within the first month.
- **B**: Pause release, treat accessibility as a quality defect, and remediate before production since the portal is public-facing.
- **C**: Raise a change request for accessibility as new scope, estimate impact, and let the sponsor decide release timing.
- **D**: Add accessibility to the quality plan, remediate critical failures before release, and schedule remaining fixes immediately after.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Public-facing government services have accessibility as an implicit requirement even if not written down; the balanced action is to fix critical failures before release and schedule the rest. (D) is correct. (A) ships a publicly accessible service with known exclusion. (B) is over-cautious without triage. (C) treats a quality baseline as discretionary new scope.
