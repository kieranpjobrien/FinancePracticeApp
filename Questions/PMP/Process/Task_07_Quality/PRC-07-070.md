# Question

On an agile e-commerce checkout project, a developer demonstrates a story for applying promotional codes. The product owner watches it calculate every discount case correctly and says she is happy to accept it. The developer agrees and moves to mark it done. The project manager knows the story carries no automated tests and is missing the audit logging the team adopted as standard for all checkout changes, but the product owner is keen to show progress to stakeholders this afternoon. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Point out before the story is marked done that, although the product owner is satisfied, the story has not met the standard the team applies to every checkout change, and let the team decide on that basis.
- **B**: Defer to the product owner's acceptance and mark the story done, since she owns the product and is satisfied it does what was asked.
- **C**: Mark the story done for today's stakeholder showcase and raise a follow-up item to add the tests and logging in the next sprint.
- **D**: Direct the developer to add the tests and logging now and hold the story out of the showcase until they are complete.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Product-owner satisfaction and the team's standing quality bar are two different gates, and both have a legitimate pull here — the product owner can accept the behaviour, but "done" on this team also means the standard adopted for every checkout change is met. The project manager's first move is not to rule but to make the gap visible at the decision point so the team applies its own agreed standard rather than quietly skipping it. (A) is correct. (B) treats the product owner's functional acceptance as sufficient and lets unfinished quality work pass under stakeholder pressure. (C) labels incomplete work done and defers the standard, the habit that erodes a shippable increment. (D) jumps to dictating the fix and the showcase decision; the call on the standard and the demo belongs to the team and product owner once the project manager has surfaced the gap, not to the project manager acting unilaterally.
