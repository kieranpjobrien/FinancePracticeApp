# Question

A project manager joins a 12-person agile team and quickly notices that team members each have different mental models of what "done" means: one developer counts a feature done after passing local tests, another counts it done only after deploy to staging, and a third only after product owner sign-off. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session for the team to agree and document a shared Definition of Done.
- **B**: Ask the most senior engineer to publish the canonical Definition of Done for the team to follow.
- **C**: Direct the team to use the strictest current interpretation to ensure highest quality.
- **D**: Delegate the decision to the product owner since they own acceptance.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Definition of Done is a team agreement; PMI and Scrum both place ownership with the whole team because shared understanding is what makes it stick. (B) imposes a standard from one person and skips the conversation that produces buy-in. (C) optimises for caution at the cost of throughput and ignores the team's input on what is reasonable. (D) confuses acceptance criteria (PO domain) with the team's quality standard for completeness.
