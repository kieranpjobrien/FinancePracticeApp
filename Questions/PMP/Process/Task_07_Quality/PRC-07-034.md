# Question

A development team proposes adopting test-driven development (TDD). Two senior stakeholders push back, saying writing tests before code "doubles the work." The PM is asked to settle the debate at the next steering meeting. What is the strongest framing the PM can offer?

## Options

- **A**: TDD is a widely adopted modern engineering practice that this team should follow to stay current.
- **B**: TDD makes requirements explicit upfront and embeds quality in the design rather than relying on later inspection.
- **C**: TDD removes the need for separate integration and acceptance testing by covering behaviour at the unit level.
- **D**: TDD primarily helps developers debug faster when issues are reported by customers after production deployment.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and Lean both treat prevention as cheaper than appraisal or failure. TDD's value is that the act of writing the test first forces requirement clarity and produces code that is testable by design — quality is built in. (A) appeals to authority and gives the stakeholders no reason to agree. (C) overstates TDD; unit tests do not replace integration, performance, or UAT. (D) understates the benefit and reframes a prevention practice as a debugging aid.
