# Question

A process has a mean of 100 mm and a standard deviation of 2 mm, and specification limits of 94 mm and 106 mm. The team asks whether the process is six-sigma capable. What does the capability analysis conclude?

## Options

- **A**: The process is six-sigma capable because the specification limits sit exactly three standard deviations from the process mean.
- **B**: The process is not six-sigma capable; the spec limits are three sigma from the mean, giving approximately Cpk of 1.0 only.
- **C**: The process is six-sigma capable because the mean sits between the upper and lower specification limits as required.
- **D**: The process is not six-sigma capable because the standard deviation should ideally be less than 1 mm for capability.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cpk measures how many standard deviations fit between the process mean and the nearest spec limit: (106-100)/(3×2) = 1.0, meaning 3 sigma fits, not 6. Six-sigma capability requires Cpk of 2.0. (A) confuses spec range with capability. (C) misinterprets capability as simply being within spec. (D) states an arbitrary threshold not grounded in Cpk maths.
