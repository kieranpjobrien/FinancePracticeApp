# Question

Which quality principle holds that it is more cost-effective to design and build quality into a product than to inspect deliverables for defects after they are produced?

## Options

- **A**: Continuous improvement (kaizen)
- **B**: Prevention over inspection
- **C**: Statistical process control
- **D**: Customer satisfaction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI principle "prevention over inspection," based on the cost-of-quality model: prevention costs are lower than failure costs incurred after defects exist. (A) is the broader improvement philosophy; it includes prevention but is not specifically about the prevention-vs-inspection trade-off. (C) is a measurement technique used in QC, not the principle itself. (D) is a separate quality principle about meeting stakeholder needs.
