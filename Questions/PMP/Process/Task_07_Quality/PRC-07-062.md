# Question

A quality audit on your predictive programme finds the team performs deliverable inspections well but documents them inconsistently. Evidence is scattered across emails and chat. The auditor flags the practice as a moderate finding. The team feels the finding is bureaucratic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Appeal the finding as bureaucratic on the grounds that the inspections themselves are being performed to standard already.
- **B**: Instruct the team to produce retrospective documentation for past inspections so the audit gap is closed immediately.
- **C**: Create a parallel evidence repository and have an admin assistant populate it from the existing emails and chats weekly.
- **D**: Work with the team to agree a lightweight evidence template that is produced at the time each inspection is performed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The root cause is lack of an easy, real-time evidence habit; co-designing a lightweight template addresses it sustainably. (D) is correct. (A) dismisses a legitimate finding. (B) papers over the gap without changing behaviour. (C) adds overhead and leaves the team disconnected from the evidence they produce.
