# Question

The sponsor has asked the project manager to "cut the testing budget in half" to bring forward delivery. Testing was sized using industry-standard ratios for the project's risk class. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce testing by 50% as instructed and update the cost and schedule baselines accordingly.
- **B**: Decline the request because reducing the standard testing investment would compromise quality.
- **C**: Brief the sponsor on the quality risks the cut would create and let them decide based on the information.
- **D**: Propose risk-based testing that concentrates effort on the highest-risk areas, quantify the residual risk, and review the proposal with the sponsor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to bring options, not a yes or a flat no. (D) does that — keep the highest-leverage testing, quantify what is given up, and let the sponsor decide informed. (A) implements the cut without analysis and removes coverage from areas that may need it most. (B) refuses without offering an alternative. (C) is informational only — the PM is in a better position than the sponsor to design the alternative, not just describe the risk.
