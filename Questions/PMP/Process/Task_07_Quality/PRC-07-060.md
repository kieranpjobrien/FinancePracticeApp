# Question

A hybrid team has held 12 retrospectives and generated 80 improvement ideas. Only four have been implemented. The team is starting to disengage from retrospectives, saying they are a waste of time. The PM wants to restore the value. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Cap each retrospective at two actionable items and make the team owner of completion by the next retrospective cycle.
- **B**: Replace the retrospective with a written survey each sprint to save time and still capture improvement ideas effectively.
- **C**: Skip retrospectives for three sprints to let the team reset and reintroduce them with fresh enthusiasm later overall.
- **D**: Schedule longer retrospectives with external facilitation so the conversation reaches deeper root causes across the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Retrospectives lose value when actions aren't completed; limiting scope and assigning ownership creates accountability and restores belief in the practice. (A) is correct. (B) removes the dialogue that drives empathy. (C) abandons a ceremony that is central to empirical improvement. (D) adds cost and still risks action-item bloat without the ownership fix.
