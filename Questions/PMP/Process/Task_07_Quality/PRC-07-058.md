# Question

A predictive aerospace project receives a batch of fasteners from a supplier. First-article inspection finds 3 percent fail dimensional tolerance; the contract allows up to 1 percent. The supplier asks to continue shipping and "sort at assembly." The PM faces schedule pressure. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the batch and sort at assembly to keep the schedule and absorb the inspection cost into the project budget.
- **B**: Reject the batch, invoke the contract remedy, and require the supplier to deliver compliant material as contracted.
- **C**: Accept the batch under a documented deviation and add the sorting cost to the supplier's invoice as recovery.
- **D**: Allow the batch into kitting while the supplier investigates the root cause in parallel with the ongoing assembly work.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Specification is a contractual commitment; accepting non-conforming material shifts risk and cost onto the project and erodes supplier accountability. (B) is correct. (A) absorbs cost that belongs to the supplier. (C) treats spec non-conformance as a deviation, which it is not. (D) exposes assembly to defect flow while root cause is still unknown.
