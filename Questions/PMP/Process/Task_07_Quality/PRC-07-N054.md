# Question

A quality engineer must pick the right tool for several closely related questions on a manufacturing project. The options overlap, so the choice turns on exactly what each question asks. Match each need to the most appropriate tool.

## Items

- **1**: Decide whether a stable-looking process is actually within statistical control or is exhibiting special-cause variation against calculated limits
- **2**: Show how the measured fill weights of produced units are distributed, to see whether they cluster near target or spread toward the spec edges
- **3**: Tell which few of many defect categories account for the majority of total defects, to decide where to focus effort

## Matches

- **A**: Histogram
- **B**: Control chart
- **C**: Pareto chart

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

A control chart plots a measure against statistically calculated control limits over time and is the only one of these tools that distinguishes common-cause from special-cause variation, answering (1) with B — a plain run chart or histogram cannot do this. A histogram shows the distribution of a single variable, revealing central tendency and spread relative to specification, so the fill-weight distribution (2) is A. A Pareto chart ranks categories by frequency to surface the vital few for prioritisation, answering (3) with C. The traps are confusing a histogram's distribution view with a control chart's stability test, and confusing a histogram's spread with a Pareto's ranked prioritisation.
