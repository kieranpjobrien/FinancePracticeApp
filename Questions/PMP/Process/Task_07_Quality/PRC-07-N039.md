# Question

A project manager is classifying quality-related expenditures on a medical-device project into cost-of-quality categories. Match each expenditure to its category.

## Items

- **1**: Recall and replacement of devices already shipped to hospitals after a fault is discovered in the field.
- **2**: Salaries of inspectors who test each device on the line before it is packed.
- **3**: A training programme for engineers on the updated design-control procedure.

## Matches

- **A**: Appraisal cost
- **B**: External failure cost
- **C**: Prevention cost

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

External failure costs are incurred when defects reach the customer; a field recall is the classic, most expensive example (1-B). Appraisal costs are spent assessing whether deliverables conform, such as line inspectors testing each unit (2-A). Prevention costs are spent to stop defects occurring, such as training on the design-control procedure (3-C). The categories form the cost-of-quality model, where investing in prevention reduces the far larger appraisal and failure costs downstream.
