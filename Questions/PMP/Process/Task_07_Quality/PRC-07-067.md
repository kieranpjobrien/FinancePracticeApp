# Question

A production lot of 1,000 items uses acceptance sampling with a sample size of 80 and an acceptance number of 2. In the sample, 3 defective items are found. What is the correct disposition of the lot and why?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the lot since 3 defectives in 80 represents 3.75 percent, which is within most industry tolerance limits broadly.
- **B**: Accept the lot after 100 percent sorting of the remaining 920 units, then release all non-defective units for despatch.
- **C**: Reject the lot because the number of defects exceeds the acceptance number; re-inspect or return per the plan procedures.
- **D**: Inspect a second, larger sample to confirm the finding before any decision to accept or reject the entire lot is made.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Acceptance sampling is a pre-defined rule: if defects exceed the acceptance number, the lot is rejected and processed per the sampling plan (often 100 percent inspection or return). (C) follows the plan. (A) substitutes a heuristic for the defined rule. (B) skips the rejection step. (D) invents an ad hoc second sample not in the plan.
