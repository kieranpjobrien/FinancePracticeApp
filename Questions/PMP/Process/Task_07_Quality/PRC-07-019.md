# Question

Your construction project requires acceptance inspection of 10,000 concrete masonry units. Inspecting every block would take three weeks and put the project two weeks behind plan. What is the BEST quality control approach?

## Options

- **A**: Skip receiving inspection because the supplier is well-regarded in the industry and certified to ISO 9001 standards.
- **B**: Apply a statistical sampling plan with a defined acceptance quality level so a representative subset is inspected for accept/reject decisions.
- **C**: Hire additional inspectors so that every block is inspected within the original schedule, accepting the additional inspection cost on the project.
- **D**: Accept the supplier's certificate of conformance as a substitute for project-side receiving inspection on the full delivered lot.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI lists statistical sampling as a primary QC tool precisely for situations where 100% inspection is impractical. (B) provides quantified confidence at a fraction of the time and cost. (A) accepts unverified material on reputation alone — not appropriate for structural items. (C) is workable but inefficient compared with sampling. (D) substitutes the supplier's quality system for the buyer's verification, which is acceptable in some contexts but not as a default for critical structural materials.
