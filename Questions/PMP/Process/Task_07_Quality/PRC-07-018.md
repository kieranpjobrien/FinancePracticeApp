# Question

What is the primary distinction between Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Control (QC)?

## Options

- **A**: QA is performed by the project team; QC is performed by external auditors or independent inspectors.
- **B**: QA captures defects in a defect log; QC reports them to stakeholders in the next status report.
- **C**: QA focuses on the processes used to produce deliverables; QC focuses on inspecting the deliverables themselves.
- **D**: QA is performed only during planning; QC is performed only during execution and closing.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI distinction: QA is process-focused (preventive); QC is product-focused (detective). (A) is wrong about who performs the work — both can involve a mix of internal and external roles. (B) confuses defect handling roles with QA/QC; both contribute to defect identification and response. (D) is wrong about timing — both are continuous through the project lifecycle.
