# Question

A predictive software release has defect density double the baseline in its final test cycle. The test lead wants to "fix forward" and ship on date, arguing most defects look cosmetic. The PM is unconvinced the team understands the underlying cause. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the fix-forward plan since most defects are cosmetic and the release date has significant business value attached.
- **B**: Delay the release automatically until defect density returns to baseline levels with no further investigation needed first.
- **C**: Facilitate a root-cause analysis on the defect cluster to determine whether the cosmetic pattern masks deeper issues.
- **D**: Transfer testing to a different team to obtain a second opinion on defect severity before any release decision.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A sharp rise in defect density is a quality signal; root-cause analysis determines whether the pattern is superficial or structural. (C) is correct. (A) risks shipping based on appearance, not analysis. (B) blocks release without diagnosis. (D) adds delay and second-guesses a competent team instead of diagnosing the defects.
