# Question

During sprint review, stakeholders claim a user story does not match what they wanted, despite the team meeting all written acceptance criteria. The product owner accepted the criteria at sprint planning. Stakeholders are now demanding rework at no cost. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a conversation about what was missed in the acceptance criteria, capture new stories, and let the PO prioritise them.
- **B**: Agree to free rework immediately to preserve the relationship with the stakeholders and keep confidence in the delivery.
- **C**: Reject the rework request outright since the team met the accepted criteria and the product owner signed them off.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to rule on whether unwritten stakeholder expectations can trigger rework at no additional cost.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Emergent feedback goes into the backlog as new stories the PO can prioritise; the team met the contract, and new understanding is welcome but not free rework. (A) respects both team and stakeholder. (B) caves and sets a precedent that accepted criteria don't matter. (C) is correct on process but poor on relationship and learning. (D) is premature escalation over a routine PO call.
