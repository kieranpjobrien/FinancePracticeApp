# Question

A PM has identified a risk with potential $1.5M impact. The PM's authority threshold allows them to manage risks up to $500K impact; above that, escalation is mandated. The sponsor is asking the PM to "handle it" without escalation to avoid programme noise. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the risk per the governance threshold, inform the sponsor, and propose joint management with the programme board.
- **B**: Split the risk into smaller sub-risks each below the threshold so the PM can manage them without escalation required.
- **C**: Handle the risk as requested by the sponsor and document their decision to bypass the escalation in the project log.
- **D**: Manage the immediate response within authority and escalate only if and when actual impact approaches the $500K threshold later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Risk thresholds exist to make escalation non-discretionary; they protect governance integrity and don't yield to sponsor preference. (A) respects the rule and keeps the sponsor informed. (B) splitting a risk to avoid thresholds is a governance workaround. (C) documenting a sponsor bypass still breaches the rule. (D) waiting for realised impact defeats the point of threshold-based escalation.
