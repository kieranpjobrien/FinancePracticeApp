# Question

Three iterations into an agile insurance-claims platform, a risk previously rated low (a niche reporting library being deprecated) has begun to materialise faster than expected after the vendor announced an accelerated end-of-support date. The existing accepted response no longer looks adequate. Several other risks were last reviewed at project start. What is the most appropriate action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the original accepted response immediately, since it was agreed by the team and changing it now would undermine the plan.
- **B**: Conduct a risk reassessment that re-scores this risk in light of the new information and revisits whether the planned response remains appropriate.
- **C**: Escalate the deprecation to the sponsor as an issue and request additional budget to replace the reporting library.
- **D**: Close the risk and raise it as an issue, because the accelerated end-of-support date means it has now occurred.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

New information that changes a risk's likelihood or timing should trigger reassessment, re-scoring the risk and checking that the chosen response is still fit for purpose; this is core risk monitoring and control in an adaptive setting. (A) executing a response now judged inadequate ignores the changed conditions the question stresses. (C) escalating for budget pre-empts the analysis and treats a still-emerging risk as a settled issue requiring funds. (D) the risk has not yet occurred; an announced future end-of-support date is a changed probability/proximity, not a realised issue, so closing it is premature. Reassessment is the disciplined first step when conditions shift.
