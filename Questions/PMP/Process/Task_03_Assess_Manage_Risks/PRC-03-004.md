# Question

A risk that was identified during planning, accepted, and assigned a documented contingency plan has now materialised. The actual impact is within the range that was estimated. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor because a risk has materialised into an issue.
- **B**: Convene the risk team to perform a fresh assessment of the now-materialised risk and confirm the response.
- **C**: Execute the documented contingency plan, draw down the contingency reserve, and update the risk register status.
- **D**: Submit a change request to add the impact to the project budget baseline.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The whole point of a contingency plan is that it is ready to execute when the risk occurs — no fresh decisions required if the impact is in range. (C) follows the plan. (A) escalates a planned and bounded event. (B) re-does work that was done during planning and delays action. (D) is wrong on two counts: contingency reserves are drawn down rather than added to the baseline, and change control isn't required for in-tolerance materialisation.
