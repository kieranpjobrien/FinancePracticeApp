# Question

A large approved change adds a new subsystem to an in-flight telecommunications project. The change has been costed and scheduled, and the baselines updated. As the change is integrated, what risk-related action should the project manager ensure is not overlooked?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct a risk reassessment focused on the new subsystem and its interactions, updating the register with any new or altered risks.
- **B**: Wait until the next periodic risk review, since risks are reassessed on a fixed cadence regardless of changes.
- **C**: Increase the management reserve proportionally to the size of the change to cover any risks the change may bring.
- **D**: Re-run the full quantitative risk model for the entire project before allowing any work on the subsystem to start.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A significant approved change is a standard trigger for reassessing risk; new scope can introduce or alter risks, especially at interfaces, so the register must be revisited for that change. (A) is the targeted, timely action. (B) deferring to a fixed cadence ignores that change is precisely an event-driven reason to reassess now. (C) padding management reserve without analysis substitutes money for understanding and may be wrong in either direction. (D) re-running the entire quantitative model before any subsystem work is disproportionate; a focused reassessment of the changed area is what is needed first.
