# Question

To mitigate the threat that a sole-supplier component arrives late, a project manager arranges a second supplier to produce the same component in parallel. During a risk review a team member points out that the second supplier has never made this component and could deliver an out-of-tolerance part. What should the project manager do first with this observation?

## Options

- **A**: Record it as a secondary risk arising from the chosen response and analyse it so an owner and any further response can be assigned.
- **B**: Abandon the dual-sourcing response because it has created a new risk rather than removing one.
- **C**: Accept the new risk, since a small chance of an out-of-tolerance part is the unavoidable price of protecting the schedule.
- **D**: Add an inspection step at goods-in and consider the matter closed once incoming parts are checked.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A risk that is created directly by implementing a risk response is a secondary risk, and the correct first step is to capture and analyse it in the register so it can be owned and, if warranted, responded to. (A) names and handles it properly. (B) overreacts: the original mitigation may still be sound and the new risk may be cheaply managed. (C) jumps to acceptance before the risk has even been analysed for probability and impact. (D) leaps to a specific response (inspection) without first assessing whether the residual exposure justifies it or whether more is needed.
