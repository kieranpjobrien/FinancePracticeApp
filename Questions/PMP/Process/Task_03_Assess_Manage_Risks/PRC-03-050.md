# Question

A Scrum team notices a pattern: the same integration test environment has caused flaky failures in three of the last four sprints. It's not yet a declared risk. The PO wants the team to work around it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise it with the platform team as an incident so they prioritise a stable fix without adding it to the project's own register entries.
- **B**: Accept the PO's workaround for now and reassess at the release-level review if the pattern continues to affect the team's delivery pace.
- **C**: Add it to the risk register with probability, impact and an owner, engage the platform team on a fix, and track until fully resolved.
- **D**: Treat it as a technical debt item in the team's backlog rather than formalising it as a risk entry in the register at this stage.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Emerging patterns are exactly what the risk register exists for; PMI treats risk management as continuous. (C) formalises and acts. (A) bypasses the register and loses the pattern view over time. (B) workaround-plus-watch accepts recurring impact without structure. (D) backlog items aren't a substitute for the risk register when probability and impact matter for planning.
