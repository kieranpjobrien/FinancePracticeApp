# Question

Which tool is most commonly used during qualitative risk analysis to prioritise risks?

## Options

- **A**: Monte Carlo simulation.
- **B**: Probability and impact matrix.
- **C**: Decision tree analysis.
- **D**: Sensitivity analysis (tornado diagram).

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The probability and impact matrix rates risks on two axes to produce a priority score — the standard qualitative analysis tool. (A), (C), and (D) are all quantitative techniques: Monte Carlo simulates outcome distributions, decision trees evaluate branching decisions under uncertainty, and tornado diagrams rank variables by sensitivity. All require numeric inputs beyond qualitative ratings.
