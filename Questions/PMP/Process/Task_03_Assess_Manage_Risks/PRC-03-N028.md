# Question

Two sprints into an eight-sprint release, a Scrum Master notices that the same three high-severity risks keep reappearing in sprint reviews without their exposure falling. The team logs risks but rarely revisits whether responses are working. What is the most useful step the Scrum Master can take to change this pattern?

## Options

- **A**: Move risk discussion out of sprint reviews and into a separate monthly risk meeting so it gets dedicated focus.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to add the three risks as backlog items so they are scheduled like any other work.
- **C**: Introduce a risk burndown chart and review it during retrospectives so the team can see whether exposure is actually decreasing and adjust.
- **D**: Escalate the three persistent risks to the sponsor since the team has been unable to reduce them.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A risk burndown chart makes total exposure visible over time, and reviewing it in the retrospective (the team's regular inspect-and-adapt point) turns logging into active management of whether responses are working. (C) targets the root cause: risks are recorded but not re-evaluated. (A) pulling risk into a separate monthly cadence reduces the frequent visibility that agile relies on. (B) turning every risk into a backlog item conflates response actions with the risks themselves and may not suit risks needing monitoring rather than a discrete task. (D) escalation is premature when the team has not yet established a routine to track and adjust its own responses.
