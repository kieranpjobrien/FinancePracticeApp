# Question

A realised risk has become an issue and the team has implemented a successful workaround. The PM is writing up the lessons learned for the closeout report. The workaround relied on informal favours from another team. What should the PM document?

## Options

- **A**: Record the workaround as a success and move on; informal favours are common and don't belong in formal closeout documentation at all.
- **B**: Record the workaround, the informal dependency it relied on, and a recommendation for a formal alternative approach for future projects.
- **C**: Record only the formal steps taken so the closeout document stays focused on reproducible process rather than informal individual action.
- **D**: Record the workaround anonymously without identifying the teams involved, to protect existing relationships and avoid political sensitivity around naming specific individuals.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lessons learned exist to inform future decisions; hiding informal dependencies robs future PMs of the full picture. (B) captures reality with an improvement recommendation. (A) silent success stories are the weakest kind of lesson. (C) sanitising the record misrepresents what actually worked. (D) anonymising enough to protect people is fine, but anonymising the dependency itself removes the learning.
