# Question

A hybrid programme's monthly risk review involves the PM and risk lead only. The delivery teams report they never see or discuss the register despite owning most of the technical risks. The PM wants risk management to feel more continuous to teams. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send the risk register to each team weekly so they have visibility of the risks currently tracked on their own deliveries.
- **B**: Invite team leads to the monthly risk review so they can represent their teams' perspectives on the register directly.
- **C**: Embed a brief risk discussion into sprint planning and retrospectives, with team-owned risks visible on the team's own board.
- **D**: Ask the risk lead to walk each team through the register quarterly in a dedicated meeting so context is properly shared.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI-aligned hybrid practice embeds risk in the team's existing cadence rather than bolting on separate meetings. (C) makes risk continuous and team-owned. (A) read-only visibility doesn't create discussion or action. (B) adding leads to a monthly PM meeting continues treating risk as a PM process. (D) quarterly walkthroughs are heavyweight and still decoupled from team flow.
