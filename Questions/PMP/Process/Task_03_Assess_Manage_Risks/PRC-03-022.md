# Question

A project manager is leading risk identification for a new software project. The team tends to anchor on the same few risks they've seen before. What technique is most effective to broaden the risk set?

## Options

- **A**: Interview each team member privately and combine their individual lists.
- **B**: Review the risk categories from the last three completed projects and copy applicable risks.
- **C**: Facilitate a Delphi technique session with anonymous expert input over multiple rounds.
- **D**: Use a pre-built industry risk register and have the team remove risks that don't apply.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Delphi uses anonymous expert input with iterative feedback to reduce groupthink and anchoring bias — exactly the problem described. (A) interviews capture breadth but don't challenge assumptions across participants. (B) copying past risks reinforces the very anchoring the PM is trying to overcome. (D) a pre-built register introduces the opposite problem — bias toward a generic list — and the team still has to evaluate each item.
