# Question

The team uses a risk-adjusted backlog: items that retire significant uncertainty are scheduled early. The product owner wants to push a technically risky feature to a later sprint because business stakeholders prefer to see business-facing features first. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the PO since backlog ordering is their decision and the Scrum Master should respect it.
- **B**: Escalate to the sponsor on technical grounds and ask them to override the PO's prioritisation directly.
- **C**: Negotiate a compromise by moving the risky feature one sprint later but no further than that.
- **D**: Coach the PO on late-discovery cost and propose a thin slice that retires the risk with business value.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The SM coaches with options, not by deferring or escalating. (D) addresses the underlying risk principle while finding a middle path that respects the PO's stakeholder pressure. (A) abandons the SM's coaching role. (B) goes around the PO, damaging the relationship. (C) is a compromise without addressing the underlying trade-off, so it doesn't change the dynamic.
