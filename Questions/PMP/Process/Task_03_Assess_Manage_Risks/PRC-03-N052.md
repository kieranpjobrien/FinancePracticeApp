# Question

A project manager is kicking off risk identification for a complex, first-of-its-kind water treatment plant with many unknowns and a team new to this technology. The manager wants techniques that will surface threats the team has not thought of, not just confirm those they already suspect. Which approaches are well suited to drawing out genuinely novel risks? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Facilitated brainstorming with the cross-functional team to generate a broad, unfiltered list of possible risks.
- **B**: Anonymous Delphi technique with external subject-matter experts to reduce groupthink and bias.
- **C**: Reviewing the risk register from the team's most recent, but very different, office-fit-out project.
- **D**: Structured assumptions and constraints analysis to expose where shaky assumptions hide threats.
- **E**: Asking the sponsor to provide the definitive list of risks they are most worried about.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

For novel, high-uncertainty work, you want techniques that broaden thinking and counter bias: brainstorming (A) generates breadth, Delphi (B) taps independent expert judgement while suppressing groupthink, and assumptions/constraints analysis (D) systematically uncovers hidden threats. (C) reuses a register from a dissimilar project, which anchors the team on irrelevant risks and reinforces existing thinking rather than expanding it. (E) narrows to the sponsor's pre-existing worries—useful input but the opposite of surfacing the unknown, and it makes one stakeholder the single source.
