# Question

A predictive infrastructure project has a risk register of 60 identified threats and opportunities. The sponsor complains that the team is "drowning in risks" and asks the project manager to focus effort where it matters. The team has already recorded probability and impact estimates for each item. What should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Perform quantitative risk analysis with a Monte Carlo simulation across all 60 risks to produce a defensible ranked list for the sponsor.
- **B**: Use the probability and impact data to score and rank the risks, then concentrate response planning on those in the high-priority zone of the matrix.
- **C**: Ask each risk owner to recommend which of their risks should be closed so the register is reduced to a manageable size.
- **D**: Combine related risks into broader categories so the register has fewer, higher-level entries for the sponsor to review.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

With probability and impact already estimated, the immediate value comes from qualitative analysis—scoring and prioritising so attention flows to the risks that most threaten objectives. (B) does exactly this. (A) jumps to expensive quantitative modelling that most projects apply only to the top risks, not all 60, and the sponsor's concern is focus, not numerical precision. (C) lets owners prune by preference rather than by assessed exposure, which can discard serious threats. (D) reduces visible count but obscures distinct response needs and does not actually prioritise by exposure.
