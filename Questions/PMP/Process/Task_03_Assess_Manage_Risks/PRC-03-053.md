# Question

A PM starts planning a new initiative for a sponsor who says they are "risk averse" but then requests aggressive delivery dates with no contingency. The behaviour suggests an unclear risk appetite. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Plan to the aggressive dates since sponsor-stated dates reflect their real appetite more than their stated aversion to risk.
- **B**: Plan cautiously to the stated aversion, adding contingency even if it delays the aggressive dates the sponsor requested.
- **C**: Draft a risk-appetite statement with examples and specific trade-offs, review it with the sponsor to align stated and revealed preferences.
- **D**: Escalate the inconsistency to the programme board for a ruling so delivery planning has an authoritative risk-appetite basis.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects explicit alignment on risk appetite before planning; stated and revealed preferences often differ and must be reconciled openly. (C) produces that alignment with the sponsor directly. (A) treats revealed behaviour as unchallenged reality. (B) treats stated aversion as unchallenged reality. (D) escalating a conversation the PM should lead skips the sponsor relationship.
