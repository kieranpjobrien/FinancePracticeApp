# Question

At a monthly governance review for a city-transport-ticketing project, the steering committee tells the project manager it has stopped reading the 90-line risk register and wants a single artefact that tells it both how much risk the project carries as a whole and which few risks matter most. The project manager already maintains all the standard risk documents. Which one should be put in front of the committee?

## Options

- **A**: The risk register, re-sorted by risk score so the highest-scoring rows appear at the top.
- **B**: A probability-and-impact matrix showing where each identified risk plots by severity.
- **C**: The risk report, giving the committee both the project's overall risk position and the standout individual risks.
- **D**: The issue log, listing the risks that have already materialised and how they are being resolved.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The risk report is the artefact that combines a view of overall project risk with summary information on the most significant individual risks, which is exactly the two-part, board-level read the committee asked for; (C) fits. (A) re-sorting the register still hands the committee the detailed line-by-line instrument it said it has stopped reading, and a sort conveys nothing about overall project risk. (B) a probability-and-impact matrix plots individual risks by severity but carries no statement of the project's overall risk position, so it answers only half the request. (D) the issue log looks backward at realised problems and does not describe forward-looking risk exposure.
