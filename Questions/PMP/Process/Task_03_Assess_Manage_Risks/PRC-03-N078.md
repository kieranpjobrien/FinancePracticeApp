# Question

While preparing the plan for a new e-learning platform, an agile team lists several assumptions: that a key API will remain free, that two senior developers will stay on the team, and that the client will provide content on time. The project manager wants to use this list to strengthen risk identification. What is the most useful way to treat these assumptions?

## Options

- **A**: Examine each assumption for the risk that it proves false, and record any credible threats it reveals in the risk register.
- **B**: Convert each assumption directly into a project constraint so the plan is built around it.
- **C**: Remove the assumptions from the documentation once the plan is approved, since they introduce uncertainty.
- **D**: Treat the assumptions as confirmed facts to keep planning simple and avoid over-analysis.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Assumption analysis examines the validity of assumptions and identifies risks arising from their inaccuracy or instability, feeding new threats into the register. (B) assumptions and constraints are distinct; assumptions are believed-true premises that may fail, not boundaries to build around, and converting them hides the risk they carry. (C) deleting assumptions removes valuable information and the very basis for identifying related risks. (D) treating unverified assumptions as facts is exactly the blind spot risk identification is meant to expose. Testing assumptions for what happens if they fail is the productive approach.
