# Question

A Scrum team building a telematics platform wants to manage risk in a way that fits its iterative cadence rather than relying solely on a periodic risk workshop. Which practices genuinely embed risk management into the team's regular agile rhythm? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Adding risk-reduction or spike items directly to the product backlog and ordering them by exposure alongside feature work.
- **B**: Reviewing emerging risks and the status of risk responses as a standing topic in iteration planning and retrospectives.
- **C**: Sequencing high-risk or high-uncertainty features into early iterations so threats are confronted while time to respond remains.
- **D**: Freezing the risk register at project initiation so the team has a stable baseline of risks for the whole release.
- **E**: Deferring all risk discussion to a separate monthly governance meeting to keep iteration ceremonies focused on delivery.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Agile manages risk continuously: representing risk responses as backlog items prioritised by exposure (A), making risk a recurring agenda item in planning and retrospectives (B), and front-loading uncertain work so failures surface early (C) all weave risk into the cadence. (D) freezing the register contradicts the empirical, adaptive nature of agile, where risks change every iteration. (E) pushing all risk talk to a separate monthly meeting removes it from the team's regular rhythm, which is the opposite of what was asked. The correct options keep risk visible and actionable each iteration.
