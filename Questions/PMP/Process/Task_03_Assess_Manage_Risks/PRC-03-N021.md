# Question

Midway through a hospital information-system rollout, three stakeholders separately email the project manager about distinct risks they have spotted. The risk register is already large and the next steering committee meeting is in two days. The project manager wants to make sure the committee's limited attention goes to the right items.

## Options

- **A**: Add all three new risks to the register and present every open risk to the committee so nothing is hidden from the decision-makers.
- **B**: Perform qualitative analysis on the three new risks, then re-rank the full register by probability and impact so the highest-priority risks surface for the committee.
- **C**: Commission a quantitative Monte Carlo analysis of cost and schedule so the committee sees numerical exposure across all risks before deciding.
- **D**: Ask the three stakeholders to agree among themselves which of their risks is most serious and bring only that one forward.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The immediate need is prioritisation under limited attention, which is exactly what qualitative analysis (probability-and-impact assessment and re-ranking) delivers quickly. (A) buries the committee in undifferentiated data and defeats the purpose of focusing attention. (C) quantitative analysis is heavier, slower than two days, and usually reserved for the high-priority risks that qualitative analysis first identifies. (D) outsources a project-management judgement to stakeholders who each see only their own risk and cannot weigh them against the whole register.
