# Question

Partway through a national broadband deployment, the project manager realises that a newly announced government spectrum policy could fundamentally undermine the project's business case. The matter is entirely outside the project's authority and would require a portfolio-level decision about whether the project should continue. How should the project manager handle this risk?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the risk to the sponsor or governing body, because responding to it lies beyond the project's authority and scope.
- **B**: Add a large contingency reserve to the project budget to cover the financial impact if the policy takes effect.
- **C**: Develop a project-level mitigation plan to lobby the government and reduce the probability of the policy proceeding.
- **D**: Accept the risk and continue as planned, monitoring the policy's progress through the risk register.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When a risk falls outside the project's scope or the project manager's authority and requires action at a higher level, the correct strategy is to escalate it so the appropriate body owns the response. (B) reserves cannot address a threat to the business case itself and the project lacks authority to commit to a strategic decision. (C) lobbying a government is well outside the project's mandate and resourcing, making it an inappropriate project-level response. (D) passively accepting a risk that may invalidate the whole project understates its significance and leaves a strategic decision unmade. Escalation is the defined response for risks beyond the project boundary.
