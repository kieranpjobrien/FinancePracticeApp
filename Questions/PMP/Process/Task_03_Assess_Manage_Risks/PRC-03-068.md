# Question

The PM is reviewing the risk register and notices that 18 of 40 risks are owned by the PM personally. Most of these are technical risks whose subject-matter experts are elsewhere on the team. The sponsor thinks concentrated ownership is efficient. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Retain PM ownership of the 18 risks since concentrated ownership makes escalation and reporting faster and more consistent for the sponsor.
- **B**: Rotate risk ownership each month so the whole team shares the load across the register over the course of the project.
- **C**: Push ownership of each risk down to the person nearest the work, with the PM retaining escalation ownership above the threshold.
- **D**: Transfer every PM-owned technical risk directly to the engineering lead so one senior technical person owns the full technical portfolio.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI-aligned risk ownership assigns each risk to the person best placed to monitor and respond; the PM owns the framework and escalation, not every risk. (C) distributes correctly. (A) concentration may feel efficient but misses the people closest to the risk's triggers. (B) rotation breaks continuity of attention on each risk. (D) concentrating all technical risk on one lead replaces one bottleneck with another.
