# Question

A hybrid energy-storage project must choose between two designs. Design X costs $1.0M and has a 70% chance of passing certification first time (no rework) and a 30% chance of needing $600K of rework. Design Y costs $1.3M and has a 90% chance of first-time pass and a 10% chance of $400K rework. The sponsor wants the lower expected total cost. Using a decision tree, which design should be selected and why?

## Options

- **A**: Design X, because its lower base cost of $1.0M makes it cheaper before any rework is considered.
- **B**: Design X, because its expected total cost of $1.18M is lower than Design Y's expected total cost.
- **C**: Design Y, because its expected total cost of $1.34M is lower than Design X's expected total cost.
- **D**: Design Y, because its higher certification probability reduces schedule risk regardless of expected cost.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Expected total cost for X = 1.0M + 0.30 × 0.6M = 1.0M + 0.18M = $1.18M. For Y = 1.3M + 0.10 × 0.4M = 1.3M + 0.04M = $1.34M. X has the lower expected total cost, so on the sponsor's stated criterion X is selected. (A) reaches the right design but on incomplete reasoning that ignores expected rework. (C) computes Y's figure correctly but wrongly claims it is lower. (D) introduces a schedule-risk rationale the sponsor did not ask for and contradicts the expected-cost basis. The decision turns on combining base cost with probability-weighted rework, where X wins by $0.16M.
