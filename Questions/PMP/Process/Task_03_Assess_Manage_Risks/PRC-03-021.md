# Question

What is a secondary risk?

## Options

- **A**: A risk with lower priority than primary risks in the risk register.
- **B**: A new risk that arises as a direct result of implementing a risk response.
- **C**: A risk that has a less than 50% probability of occurring.
- **D**: A risk identified during execution rather than during planning.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A secondary risk is a risk that arises as a direct consequence of implementing a risk response. For example, outsourcing to transfer a schedule risk creates a secondary vendor-management risk. (A) confuses secondary with lower priority; the terms are distinct. (C) invents a probability threshold. (D) describes an emergent risk identified during execution, which is different from a secondary risk caused by a response.
