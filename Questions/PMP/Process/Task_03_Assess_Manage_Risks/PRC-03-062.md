# Question

A PM has run a Monte Carlo schedule simulation showing the project finishing at P50 in 240 days and at P80 in 275 days. The sponsor wants a committed date. The PM's stated risk appetite allows modest schedule risk only. What commitment date should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the P50 date of 240 days, the most likely single outcome on the distribution curve generated by the simulation.
- **B**: Commit to 230 days to create a buffer visible to the delivery team and incentivise them to beat the curve aggressively.
- **C**: Commit to approximately the P80 date of 275 days, giving a higher confidence level consistent with low-appetite risk commitment.
- **D**: Commit to the mean of P50 and P80 at about 258 days to balance delivery ambition with the stated risk appetite.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A low-risk appetite maps to higher-confidence commitments (P80 or higher); P50 has a 50% chance of being missed. (C) aligns date choice to appetite. (A) P50 is an inappropriate external commitment under a low-risk appetite. (B) an unstated buffer inside a commitment is a form of dishonesty. (D) arithmetic averaging of percentiles has no sound statistical basis.
