# Question

A Monte Carlo schedule simulation for a refinery turnaround shows the deterministic critical-path finish at the P35 confidence level, while the sponsor has committed externally to a date corresponding to P50. The project manager must advise on next steps. What is the soundest interpretation and action?

## Options

- **A**: The plan is optimistic relative to the commitment; quantify the schedule contingency needed to reach the sponsor's P50 date and discuss adding it.
- **B**: The plan is conservative; the team can likely finish earlier than the deterministic date, so no contingency is required.
- **C**: The simulation is invalid because a deterministic finish should always fall at P50 by definition.
- **D**: Re-baseline the schedule to the deterministic P35 date and manage to it, since that is the calculated critical-path finish.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A deterministic finish landing at only P35 confidence means there is roughly a 65% chance of overrunning it, so the plan is optimistic relative to a P50 commitment, and the gap between P35 and P50 is the schedule contingency the project needs. (A) reads the percentiles correctly and proposes the right action. (B) inverts the meaning: a low percentile is optimistic, not conservative. (C) deterministic estimates frequently fall below P50 because they ignore variability; that is the whole reason Monte Carlo is run. (D) baselining to a date with only 35% confidence locks in a plan likely to slip and ignores the sponsor's actual commitment.
