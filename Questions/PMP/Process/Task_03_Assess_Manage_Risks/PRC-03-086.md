# Question

On a utility-scale solar-farm project, an early-connection incentive is paid only if grid connection is completed before a published cut-off date. The project manager responds to four opportunities the team has identified. Match each action to the positive-risk (opportunity) response strategy it represents.

## Items

- **1**: Reassigned the in-house design team and re-sequenced the schedule so the grid-connection design will be complete well before the incentive cut-off, removing the question of whether the team makes the date.
- **2**: Set up a joint venture with a specialist inverter manufacturer, with the efficiency gains from the partner's technology and the associated revenue split between the two firms under the agreement.
- **3**: Funded a second commissioning crew on standby so that, in a spell of good weather, more work can be completed per day than a single crew could manage.
- **4**: Recorded a possible relaxation of import tariffs that could lower panel costs, decided the saving did not justify any action to pursue it, and left it in the register to be welcomed if it happens.

## Matches

- **A**: Share
- **B**: Exploit
- **C**: Accept
- **D**: Enhance

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-D, 4-C

## Explanation

Exploit makes a desired outcome essentially certain: re-sequencing and re-staffing so the design finishes comfortably before the cut-off removes the uncertainty about earning the incentive (1-B). Share gives a partner who is better placed to realise the upside a stake in it; the joint venture with the inverter specialist, splitting the resulting gains, does this (2-A). Enhance raises the probability or scale of the upside without guaranteeing it; a second crew on standby increases what can be captured if good weather arrives but assures nothing (3-D). Accept takes no proactive step and simply leaves the project ready to benefit, as with the speculative tariff change (4-C). The closest pair is 1 versus 3: item 1 converts a maybe into a near-certainty by controlling the schedule, whereas item 3 only improves the odds of capturing more, contingent on weather outside the team's control. Share is set apart from both by transferring part of the upside, and its ownership, to a third party.
