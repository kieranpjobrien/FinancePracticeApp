# Question

Three risks have been quantified: Risk 1 (20% x $50,000 loss), Risk 2 (40% x $30,000 loss), and Risk 3 (10% x $80,000 gain). What contingency reserve should the project hold based on total EMV?

## Options

- **A**: $14,000
- **B**: $22,000
- **C**: $30,000
- **D**: $42,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

EMV sums: Risk 1 = 0.20 x (-50,000) = -$10,000; Risk 2 = 0.40 x (-30,000) = -$12,000; Risk 3 = 0.10 x (+80,000) = +$8,000. Net EMV = -$10,000 - $12,000 + $8,000 = -$14,000, so reserve = $14,000. (B) ignores the positive EMV of Risk 3. (C) sums only the two threat EMVs and rounds up. (D) sums absolute values, treating the opportunity as a threat.
