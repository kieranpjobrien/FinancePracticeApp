# Question

On a new medical device programme, two key stakeholders disagree sharply about acceptable risk: the regulatory lead wants near-zero tolerance for any compliance risk, while the commercial sponsor will accept more risk to hit a market window. Both pressures are legitimate, and the team has begun asking the project manager how cautious to be when planning responses. The project manager wants to put the team on a sound footing for the decisions ahead. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Work with both stakeholders to agree and document risk thresholds for each objective, so the team has sanctioned limits that reflect both the regulatory and the commercial constraints.
- **B**: Hold a joint workshop for the two stakeholders to air their concerns and reach a shared understanding before any thresholds are written down.
- **C**: Capture both stakeholders' differing risk attitudes in the stakeholder register and risk management plan, so the divergence is visible and can be managed through the programme.
- **D**: Map each objective's regulatory and commercial sensitivity so the eventual thresholds are grounded in where compliance and schedule risk actually bite, rather than in stated preferences alone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team needs something concrete to plan responses against now: agreed, documented risk thresholds per objective, set with the very stakeholders whose constraints they must reflect. (A) directly produces that sanctioned reference and is what resolves competing attitudes in practice. (B) is a reasonable means of building agreement, but a workshop that airs concerns without committing to thresholds leaves the team with no usable limit—dialogue is a step toward (A), not a substitute for it. (C) records the disagreement faithfully but documenting divergence is not the same as resolving it; the team still has no agreed limit to act on. (D) is useful analysis that can inform the thresholds, but it sequences the supporting study ahead of the decision the team is waiting on, and sensitivity mapping alone yields no agreed tolerance. Agreeing and recording the thresholds is the first action that actually gives the team sanctioned limits.
