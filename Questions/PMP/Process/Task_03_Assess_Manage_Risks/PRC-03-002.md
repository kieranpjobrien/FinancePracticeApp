# Question

What is the defining difference between a risk and an issue?

## Options

- **A**: A risk is an uncertain future event that may or may not occur; an issue is a problem that has already occurred.
- **B**: A risk is recorded in the risk register for tracking; an issue is recorded in the formal change log instead.
- **C**: A risk is something the project team owns directly; an issue is something that must be escalated to the sponsor.
- **D**: A risk has both probability and impact scores; an issue has only an impact, with no probability attached.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The defining attribute is uncertainty over time — risks are future possibilities, issues are realities. (A) captures this. (B) describes recording locations but not the underlying difference; issues live in the issue log, not the change log. (C) confuses ownership with type; both can be team-owned or escalated. (D) is partly true (issues don't have probability) but doesn't reach the underlying conceptual distinction.
