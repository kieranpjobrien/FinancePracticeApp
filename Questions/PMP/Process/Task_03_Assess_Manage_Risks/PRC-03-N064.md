# Question

A construction project manager identifies that a new modular fabrication method, if adopted, would almost certainly finish a critical building phase two weeks early and free the crew for other work. The team has the skills to adopt it. The project manager wants to make this beneficial outcome happen rather than leave it to chance. Which opportunity response strategy does this represent?

## Options

- **A**: Exploit, by taking direct action to ensure the opportunity is realised.
- **B**: Enhance, by increasing the probability that the opportunity might occur.
- **C**: Share, by allocating the opportunity to a partner better able to capture it.
- **D**: Accept, by taking no proactive action but being ready to benefit if it occurs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Exploit is used when the organisation wants to make certain a high-value opportunity is captured by taking direct action, such as adopting the fabrication method outright. (B) enhance only raises the probability or impact of an uncertain opportunity rather than securing it, but here the team can simply act to realise it. (C) share gives the opportunity to a third party better positioned to capture it, which is unnecessary when the team itself is capable. (D) accept is passive and would forgo a near-certain benefit the team can directly secure. The intent to guarantee the gain points to exploit.
