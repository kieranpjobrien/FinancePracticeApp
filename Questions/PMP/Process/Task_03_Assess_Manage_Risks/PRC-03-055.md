# Question

A risk logged six months ago — "key architect resignation" — was scored medium and accepted with a watch. The architect has just booked four weeks of unexpected leave, and the team knows they've been approached by a competitor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the risk's probability and impact immediately and activate contingency plans including knowledge capture and succession.
- **B**: Wait to see whether the architect resigns after returning from leave, since the trigger event hasn't formally occurred.
- **C**: Ask the architect directly about their intentions so the team knows whether to act on the contingency plan now.
- **D**: Accept the risk as is since the mitigation effort required isn't justified before a resignation is actually confirmed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Triggers and leading indicators warrant immediate reassessment and proactive response; PMI-aligned practice is continuous risk management. (A) treats observable signals as real signals. (B) waiting for the event to realise wastes the preparation window. (C) asking directly is intrusive, likely counterproductive, and not a substitute for acting on triggers. (D) accepting after clear indicators is behind the curve.
