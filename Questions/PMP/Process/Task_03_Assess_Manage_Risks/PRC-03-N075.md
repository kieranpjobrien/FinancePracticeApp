# Question

Before relying on a qualitative risk analysis for a museum-redevelopment project, the project manager wants to be confident the analysis is trustworthy. Which considerations legitimately affect the quality and credibility of the qualitative risk analysis? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: The degree of understanding of each risk and the reliability and accuracy of the data used to assess it.
- **B**: Whether the probability and impact definitions in the risk management plan were applied consistently across all risks.
- **C**: The extent to which assessor bias, such as optimism or anchoring, may have skewed probability and impact ratings.
- **D**: Whether each risk's expected monetary value was calculated to two decimal places of precision.
- **E**: The precise discount rate applied to convert future risk costs into present value during the assessment.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Qualitative analysis quality depends on risk data quality, namely how well each risk is understood and how reliable its data is (A); on consistent application of the agreed probability and impact scales (B); and on managing assessor bias that can distort subjective ratings (C). (D) EMV precision is a quantitative-analysis concern and decimal precision does not make a qualitative rating credible. (E) discount rates belong to quantitative cost modelling, not qualitative scoring. The defensible distractors describe real techniques but from quantitative analysis, which is not what governs qualitative credibility here.
