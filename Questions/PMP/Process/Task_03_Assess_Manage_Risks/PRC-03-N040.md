# Question

A previously identified threat on a wind-farm-cabling project has just occurred: a subsea survey has confirmed an unexpected rock outcrop on the cable route, exactly as one risk in the register anticipated. The project manager has minutes in a site call to decide what to set in motion. Which actions are appropriate now that the risk has materialised? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Trigger the contingency response that was pre-agreed for this risk and assign the actions to carry it out.
- **B**: Record the now-realised risk as an issue in the issue log and manage it through to resolution.
- **C**: Treat this as an unforeseen event and request management reserve to fund the response.
- **D**: Reassess related risks that the outcrop may affect, such as installation-vessel scheduling and burial-depth compliance.
- **E**: Remove the original risk entry without comment because the threat is no longer uncertain.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

When an identified risk occurs, you execute its pre-planned contingency response (A), convert the realised risk into a managed issue (B), and reassess knock-on risks the event may have changed (D). (C) is wrong because this was an identified ("known-unknown") risk, so it draws on contingency reserve, not management reserve, which is for genuinely unforeseen events. (E) deleting the entry destroys traceability; the register should reflect that the risk occurred and link to the resulting issue rather than be silently erased.
