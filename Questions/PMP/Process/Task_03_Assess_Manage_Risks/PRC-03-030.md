# Question

A project manager is building the risk management plan for a mid-sized agile project. The sponsor asks how often risks will be reviewed. What is the BEST answer?

## Options

- **A**: At the start and end of the project, plus on-demand reviews when issues actually arise.
- **B**: Quarterly, aligned with the organisation's portfolio review cadence and steering committee cycle.
- **C**: Continuously during daily work, with formal review at sprint events and reassessment on change.
- **D**: Monthly, via a dedicated risk review meeting with all stakeholders and relevant vendor representatives.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Agile projects embed risk review into existing events — daily work, sprint reviews, retrospectives — with formal reassessment when the environment changes. (A) two-point review is far too infrequent and misses the iterative nature of agile. (B) quarterly cadence doesn't match sprint timing and lets risks drift for too long. (D) a separate monthly meeting with all stakeholders is heavyweight and duplicates the conversations that should happen in sprint events.
