# Question

An agile software team responds to a risk that a third-party authentication service may be unreliable by transferring the function to a more robust managed provider under contract. The product owner asks the project manager what new risk exposure this response itself creates and what exposure remains. Which statements correctly characterise the situation after the response is implemented? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: A secondary risk arises that the new managed provider could impose pricing or contract changes that the original in-house option would not have.
- **B**: Residual risk remains that even the more robust provider could experience an outage, though at a lower likelihood than before.
- **C**: Transferring the function eliminates all authentication-related risk because contractual liability now rests with the provider.
- **D**: The response converts the threat into an opportunity, so it should now be tracked on the opportunity side of the register.
- **E**: A secondary risk emerges that integrating with the new provider's interface could introduce defects the original integration did not have.

## Answer

A, B, E

## Explanation

Secondary risks are new risks created directly by implementing a response: dependence on a new vendor's commercial terms (A) and integration defects from the new interface (E) both qualify. Residual risk is the exposure that remains after the response: the provider can still suffer outages, just less often (B). (C) is wrong because transferring liability does not remove the operational risk of an outage, which still affects the product. (D) is wrong because reducing a threat does not turn it into an opportunity; the underlying uncertainty is still a threat. Recognising that responses create and leave behind exposure is the core of this question.
