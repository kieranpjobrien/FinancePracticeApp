# Question

Quantitative analysis reveals the project has an 80% chance of finishing at least 10% over budget. The sponsor rejects this result, calling the analysis 'too pessimistic'. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Rerun the simulation with lower cost impact estimates to align with the sponsor's intuition.
- **B**: Accept the sponsor's view and present only the 50th-percentile estimate going forward.
- **C**: Escalate to the PMO to arbitrate the disagreement between PM and sponsor.
- **D**: Walk the sponsor through input assumptions and offer to adjust only where better data exists.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right response is transparency: analysis is only as good as inputs, and the sponsor may have data the PM doesn't. (D) addresses the sponsor's discomfort by inviting scrutiny of assumptions rather than just the conclusion. (A) changing inputs without justification is data manipulation. (B) presenting only the P50 hides the shape of uncertainty and misleads decision-makers. (C) escalating a technical disagreement the PM should resolve through dialogue first.
