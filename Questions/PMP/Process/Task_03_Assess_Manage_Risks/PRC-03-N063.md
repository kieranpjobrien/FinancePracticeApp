# Question

A pharmaceutical packaging project has a cost baseline that already includes a contingency reserve sized from quantitative risk analysis. Midway through execution, a previously unidentified regulatory requirement emerges that will cost an estimated $300K to address. The contingency reserve was not sized to cover risks of this kind. What is the most appropriate source of funds and the correct process to access them?

## Options

- **A**: Draw from the contingency reserve, since it exists to absorb in-project cost risks as they materialise during execution.
- **B**: Request management reserve through the organisation's change-control or governance process, because this is an unidentified (unknown-unknown) risk outside the baseline.
- **C**: Reallocate budget from contingency on lower-priority risks that have since closed, applying the freed funds to the regulatory work.
- **D**: Absorb the cost within existing work-package estimates by reducing scope elsewhere to keep the cost baseline unchanged.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contingency reserve is held within the cost baseline for identified risks (known-unknowns); a risk the reserve was never sized for is effectively an unknown-unknown, which is funded from management reserve outside the baseline and accessed via governance/change control. (A) misapplies contingency to a risk it was not provisioned for. (C) repurposing closed-risk contingency is not the sanctioned route for an out-of-baseline event and may leave remaining identified risks underfunded. (D) silently cutting scope distorts the baseline and hides a material change from governance. The defining clue is that this risk sits outside what the baseline anticipated.
