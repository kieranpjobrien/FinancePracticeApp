# Question

A community-facing infrastructure project has received social media criticism of its environmental approach. The project's environmental controls meet all regulations. The sponsor wants the PM to ignore "non-regulatory noise". The volume of criticism is growing weekly. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow the sponsor's direction and continue focusing on regulatory compliance since the project already meets all legal requirements fully.
- **B**: Respond publicly to each specific criticism with factual corrections so the project's true environmental posture is visible online.
- **C**: Raise a reputational risk in the register, engage community-relations and sponsor together, and develop a proactive community-engagement plan.
- **D**: Commission an independent environmental audit to publish results, demonstrating the project meets or exceeds the regulatory standards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Reputational risk is a legitimate project risk even when regulatory compliance is met; PMI-aligned practice logs it and engages proactively. (C) uses the right process. (A) ignoring the signal lets a tractable risk grow. (B) public point-by-point debate rarely reshapes sentiment and can escalate conflict. (D) an audit may be useful later but doesn't address the engagement gap driving sentiment.
