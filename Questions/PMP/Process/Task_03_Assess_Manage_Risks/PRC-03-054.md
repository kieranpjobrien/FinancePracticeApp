# Question

A PM can choose between two design approaches. Approach 1 costs $100K with a 40% chance of a $50K rework. Approach 2 costs $120K with a 15% chance of a $30K rework. Based on expected monetary value, which is preferred and by what amount?

## Options

- **A**: Approach 2 is preferred by about $4,500 on expected monetary value over Approach 1's higher total.
- **B**: Approach 1 is preferred by $20,000 on base cost alone, with rework probability within tolerance.
- **C**: Approach 2 is preferred by $15,000 because lower rework probability dominates base cost difference.
- **D**: Approach 1 is preferred by $4,500 on expected monetary value over Approach 2's higher total.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

EMV(1) = 100,000 + (0.40 × 50,000) = 120,000. EMV(2) = 120,000 + (0.15 × 30,000) = 124,500. Approach 1 is cheaper by $4,500. (D) applies EMV correctly. (A) reverses the preferred approach on otherwise correct arithmetic. (B) ignores expected rework entirely. (C) overweights probability and produces a fabricated difference.
