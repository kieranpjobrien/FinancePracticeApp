# Question

A PM has identified that an early supplier delivery could let the team accelerate testing and release two weeks early, unlocking $400,000 in additional revenue. The supplier's early delivery currently has a 30% probability. What response strategy and action best fits this opportunity?

## Options

- **A**: Enhance — fund the supplier's early-delivery incentive programme to actively increase the probability of the early delivery occurring.
- **B**: Exploit — sign a contract amendment committing the supplier to early delivery so the opportunity becomes certain to be captured.
- **C**: Share — bring a partner into the acceleration effort so the work of realising the opportunity is distributed more widely.
- **D**: Accept — monitor for early delivery and capture the benefit if it arises, without investing effort to change the probability.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Enhance increases the probability or impact of an opportunity; exploit makes it certain. At 30% probability with uncertainty still present, enhance is the right-sized response. (A) matches the concept. (B) exploit requires the PM's ability to make the event certain, which isn't available here without large concessions. (C) share is about allocating benefit and effort with a partner for mutual gain, not relevant to this specific context. (D) accept leaves potential value on the table when a cost-effective enhance action is feasible.
