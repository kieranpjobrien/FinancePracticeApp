# Question

What is a risk trigger?

## Options

- **A**: The root cause that ultimately produces the risk event in practice.
- **B**: A leading indicator that a risk is about to occur or has just occurred.
- **C**: The response action that automatically activates when a risk materialises.
- **D**: The person who first identifies a risk during the project identification process.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A risk trigger is a leading indicator — an early warning sign that a risk is starting to occur — allowing the response to be activated. (A) root cause is what creates the risk, not the signal that it's occurring. (C) describes the response itself, not the signal that activates it. (D) describes a risk identifier, unrelated to the trigger concept.
