# Question

A PM inherits a risk register with 80 entries, mostly unstructured and free-text. Sponsors complain they can't tell where the concentration of risk sits. The PM wants a structural view before mitigations. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team prioritise all 80 risks by probability-impact score and present the top 15 in the next steering committee report.
- **B**: Categorise all risks against a risk breakdown structure — technical, external, organisational, management — to reveal where risk concentration sits across the programme.
- **C**: Delete risks that haven't been updated in 60 days to reduce noise and focus only on recently active entries in reporting.
- **D**: Engage an external consultant to review the current register and reorganise it using industry-standard risk taxonomies fully for the programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A risk breakdown structure reveals concentration and systemic gaps that probability-impact scoring alone misses. (B) gives sponsors the structural view they're asking for. (A) scoring alone isn't structure and the concentration view remains hidden. (C) deleting stale entries loses institutional memory about dormant threats. (D) external consulting for a structural recategorisation is disproportionate when it's a PM task.
