# Question

A hybrid programme runs predictive infrastructure work alongside agile feature teams. The programme manager wants risk management to be effective across both without creating a heavyweight separate process. Which practices appropriately embed risk management into the existing flow? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Forbid the agile teams from logging risks so that all risk handling is centralised with the programme manager.
- **B**: Use retrospectives to inspect whether risk responses are working and to adapt the team's approach.
- **C**: Defer all risk discussion to a quarterly programme risk workshop to keep sprints free of overhead.
- **D**: Surface emerging risks and blockers in the daily stand-up so the team can raise and address them continuously.
- **E**: Maintain a programme-level risk register for cross-team and predictive-stream risks that span sprint boundaries.

## Answer

B, D, E

## Explanation

Embedding risk into existing events is the agile way: stand-ups surface emerging risks and impediments continuously (D), retrospectives inspect-and-adapt on whether responses work (B), and a programme-level register handles cross-team and longer-horizon predictive risks that no single sprint owns (E). (A) banning teams from logging risks removes the people closest to the work from identification and contradicts agile transparency. (C) confining risk to a quarterly workshop reintroduces exactly the slow, batched process the manager wants to avoid and lets risks fester between sessions.
