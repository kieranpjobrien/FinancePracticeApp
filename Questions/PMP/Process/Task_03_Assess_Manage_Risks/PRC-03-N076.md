# Question

A renewable-energy developer identifies an opportunity: an emerging government grant could fund 20% of a solar-farm project, but only if the project demonstrates a community-ownership component, which the team has not yet built in. The project manager arranges for the design to include community shareholding, increasing the chance the grant is awarded. Which opportunity response strategy is being used?

## Options

- **A**: Enhance, by increasing the probability that the opportunity will be realised.
- **B**: Exploit, by ensuring with certainty that the opportunity is captured.
- **C**: Share, by partnering with a community organisation that can better capture the benefit.
- **D**: Accept, by taking no action while remaining ready to benefit if the grant appears.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Enhance increases the probability or positive impact of an opportunity without guaranteeing it; adding the community-ownership feature raises the chance of grant approval but cannot make it certain, since the award still rests with the government. (B) exploit would apply only if the action made capture certain, which it does not here. (C) share would assign the opportunity to a partner better placed to capture it; involving a community body for shareholding is a design change to qualify, not a transfer of benefit ownership. (D) accept is passive and contradicts the deliberate design change made to improve the odds. Raising probability short of certainty is the hallmark of enhance.
