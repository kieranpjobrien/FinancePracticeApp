# Question

On a coastal-defence construction project, the project manager selects a response for each of three threats. Match each action the project manager took to the negative-risk response strategy it represents.

## Items

- **1**: Rerouted the works programme to avoid pouring any concrete during the storm season, eliminating exposure to weather-delay on that activity entirely.
- **2**: Purchased a delay-in-start-up insurance policy so that the financial loss from a late handover would be borne by the insurer.
- **3**: Scheduled additional ground-water pumps on standby so that, if flooding occurs, its impact on the excavation timeline is reduced.

## Matches

- **A**: Transfer
- **B**: Avoid
- **C**: Mitigate

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Avoid removes the threat or its cause entirely; rescheduling so the activity never coincides with storm season eliminates the weather exposure (1-B). Transfer shifts the financial impact and ownership to a third party; insurance moves the loss to the insurer (2-A). Mitigate reduces the probability or impact without removing it; standby pumps lessen the impact of flooding if it occurs (3-C). The two close calls are 2 versus 3: insurance shifts the loss to another party (transfer), whereas standby pumps keep the impact with the project but make it smaller (mitigate).
