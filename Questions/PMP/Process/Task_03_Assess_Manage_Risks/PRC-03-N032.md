# Question

After a mitigation lowers the probability of a data-migration failure from high to low, the project manager confirms that the remaining exposure now sits within the organisation's risk threshold. Which actions are appropriate for handling this remaining (residual) exposure proportionately? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Actively accept the residual risk and document the decision and its rationale in the register.
- **B**: Establish a contingency response and a trigger so the team can act quickly if the failure still occurs.
- **C**: Keep the risk under periodic review in case its probability or impact changes during execution.
- **D**: Apply further mitigations to drive the residual probability as near to zero as possible before proceeding.
- **E**: Delete the risk from the register now that mitigation has brought it below the threshold.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

When residual exposure is within tolerance, the proportionate handling is active acceptance with the decision documented (A), a contingency plan and trigger held in reserve (B), and continued periodic monitoring in case conditions change (C). (D) is disproportionate: spending effort to push an already-acceptable risk toward zero rarely justifies the cost. (E) is wrong because deleting the entry hides a live, if small, exposure and removes the trigger that would prompt the contingency response.
