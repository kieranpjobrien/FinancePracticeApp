# Question

On a hybrid product-development programme, analysis shows that if an early prototype passes the regulator's voluntary pre-review, the team could compress the certification phase and launch a quarter early into a seasonal sales window. The pre-review verdict is the regulator's alone to give. The programme manager is deciding how to respond to this opportunity. Which action best fits?

## Options

- **A**: Move the programme's strongest engineers onto the prototype and rework the plan around an early launch as the committed date.
- **B**: Run pre-review rehearsal sessions and tighten the submission to raise the chance the prototype passes, while keeping the baseline plan if it does not.
- **C**: Invite the certification body into a joint arrangement so it has a stake in the prototype clearing pre-review.
- **D**: Take no special action and capture the early launch only if the prototype happens to pass.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Because the verdict belongs solely to the regulator, the programme cannot make the early launch certain; the most it can do is improve the odds of an outcome that remains uncertain, which is the enhance strategy. (B) fits: rehearsals and a stronger submission lift the probability while the team keeps its fallback. (A) is an exploit move that treats the opportunity as if it can be made certain and commits the schedule to it, overstating control over an external gate the team does not hold. (C) resembles sharing, but a regulator is not a commercial partner that can be given a stake in approving a submission, and the arrangement would be improper. (D) is acceptance, which forgoes the achievable lift in probability the manager is trying to secure.
