# Question

An agile team delivering a mobile banking feature maintains a lightweight risk list reviewed each iteration. Two risks score identically on a probability-and-impact matrix. One could materialise within the current iteration; the other could only materialise after the public launch three months away. With limited capacity to address risks this iteration, how should the project manager use this difference?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the two risks as equal priority because their probability-and-impact scores are the same on the matrix.
- **B**: Prioritise the near-term risk for response this iteration because its proximity gives less time to react and shortens the available response window.
- **C**: Prioritise the post-launch risk because risks closer to a public release carry greater reputational consequences for the organisation.
- **D**: Defer both risks to a dedicated risk workshop after the iteration so they can be analysed with the full quantitative model.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk urgency or proximity is a recognised additional criterion in qualitative analysis; when scores tie, the risk that can occur sooner generally warrants earlier attention because there is less time to develop and deploy a response. (A) ignores proximity, which is precisely the distinguishing factor offered. (C) invents a reputational weighting that is not supported and the post-launch risk has more lead time to manage. (D) deferring both wastes the current capacity and delays response to a risk that could strike this iteration. Proximity is the deciding lens here.
