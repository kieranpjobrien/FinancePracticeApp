# Question

A hybrid hospital electronic-records implementation has a fixed go-live tied to a regulatory deadline, and the steering committee has asked the project manager to put a defensible figure on the schedule contingency. The project manager intends to run a quantitative risk analysis across the prioritised threats and opportunities. Which statements about this analysis are correct? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: It can express the combined effect of individual risks as a numerical estimate of the contingency needed to meet the deadline at a chosen confidence level.
- **B**: It typically draws on the highest-priority risks already filtered by qualitative analysis, rather than every risk in the register.
- **C**: Expected monetary value combines each risk's probability with its impact, and opportunities contribute as positive values offsetting threats.
- **D**: A risk's qualitative priority rating is, on its own, a sufficient numerical input to a Monte Carlo schedule simulation.
- **E**: Because the go-live date is fixed, quantitative analysis cannot inform contingency and the team should rely on qualitative ratings alone.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Quantitative analysis aggregates individual risks into an overall numerical estimate—such as the contingency required to hit a date at a given confidence level—which is exactly what the committee asked for (A). It is normally applied to the risks that qualitative analysis has already prioritised, not the whole register (B). Expected monetary value multiplies probability by impact, with opportunities entered as positive values that offset threats (C). (D) is wrong because a qualitative rating (e.g. "high") is not a usable numerical distribution; a Monte Carlo simulation needs quantitative ranges or estimates as inputs. (E) is wrong because a fixed deadline makes quantifying confidence and contingency more valuable, not less. The discriminators are that quantitative analysis builds on prioritised risks with real numerical inputs and remains useful—indeed pertinent—when a date is fixed.
