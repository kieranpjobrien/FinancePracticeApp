# Question

A project has accepted a residual risk after mitigation. The risk then occurs, with impact within the accepted range. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the sponsor to explain why the risk was not prevented.
- **B**: Update the risk register to show the risk as 'avoided' since acceptance was the planned response.
- **C**: Document the occurrence and actual impact, confirm reserve drawdown, and capture lessons learned.
- **D**: Reopen the risk for further analysis to determine why mitigation didn't fully eliminate it.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Acceptance is a legitimate strategy; when the accepted risk occurs within tolerance, the PM documents and learns from it — no surprise, no escalation. (A) escalation implies a problem, but the risk behaved exactly as planned. (B) 'avoided' misrepresents what happened; the risk occurred, and acceptance is the correct classification. (D) reopening implies mitigation was supposed to eliminate it, which misunderstands acceptance — residual risk after mitigation is expected, not a failure.
