# Question

An infrastructure programme has identified a promising opportunity: a government grant could fund an optional sustainability upgrade if the team meets specific criteria by a deadline. The project manager wants to actively pursue and increase the chance of capturing this opportunity. Which actions are consistent with exploiting and enhancing a positive risk? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Assign the organisation's most capable engineer to lead the grant submission to make capturing the grant as certain as possible.
- **B**: Reschedule preparatory work so the qualifying criteria are met comfortably ahead of the grant deadline.
- **C**: Take no action now and decide whether to pursue the grant only if time permits later in the programme.
- **D**: Partner with a third party purely so they absorb the consequences if the grant is not awarded.
- **E**: Remove the sustainability upgrade from scope to avoid the effort the grant application would require.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Exploit makes an opportunity certain to occur—assigning top talent to lock in the grant (A)—while enhance increases its probability or impact, such as reshaping the schedule to meet the criteria early (B). (C) is passive acceptance, the opposite of actively pursuing. (D) describes a sharing/transfer-style motive but is framed around offloading downside, not capturing the upside, and misuses partnering. (E) is avoidance applied to an opportunity, which forfeits it entirely.
