# Question

A PM identifies a risk that is only material because two sibling projects share the same vendor dependency. No single project exceeds their own threshold, but in aggregate the exposure is high. The PM's sponsor wants her to focus on her own project only. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the risk only on her own project's register and monitor it locally since the threshold hasn't been crossed at project level yet.
- **B**: Quietly coordinate with the other sibling PMs to watch the vendor together without formalising the aggregation in any shared risk register.
- **C**: Inform the sibling PMs and jointly rely on their own registers to reflect the vendor exposure for each independent project view going forward.
- **D**: Raise the aggregation explicitly with the programme manager and request a programme-level risk entry with shared ownership across the sibling projects.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Some risks only exist at the programme level by virtue of aggregation; PMI expects explicit escalation for these and programme-level ownership. (D) treats the aggregation properly. (A) project-only view misses the aggregation entirely. (B) informal coordination lacks accountability and governance visibility. (C) parallel registers at project level don't aggregate and don't assign shared ownership.
