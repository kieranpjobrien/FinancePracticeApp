# Question

During sprint planning, a development team cannot estimate a story that integrates a machine-learning library none of them has used, and the uncertainty is making the whole sprint commitment unreliable. What is the most appropriate way to reduce this uncertainty?

## Options

- **A**: Pad the story's estimate generously to cover the unknowns and commit to it as written.
- **B**: Time-box a spike to investigate the library so the team can produce a reliable estimate for the real story next sprint.
- **C**: Move the story to the bottom of the backlog and let it stay there until the uncertainty resolves itself.
- **D**: Ask the architect to estimate the story on the team's behalf based on prior experience.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A spike is a time-boxed investigation used specifically to reduce technical or estimation uncertainty so the real work can be planned with confidence. (B) directly addresses the unknown. (A) padding an estimate hides the uncertainty rather than reducing it and tends to produce an unreliable commitment. (C) deferring indefinitely leaves the risk unmanaged and the dependency unresolved. (D) a third party's estimate does not give the team the understanding it needs and undermines the team's ownership of its commitment.
