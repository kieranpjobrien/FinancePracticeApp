# Question

What is a secondary risk?

## Options

- **A**: A new risk that is created by, or arises as a direct consequence of, implementing a planned risk response.
- **B**: A risk that is rated as low priority during qualitative analysis and therefore monitored rather than actively managed.
- **C**: A risk identified after the project's risk register has been baselined and approved.
- **D**: A risk that affects only stakeholders considered secondary in the stakeholder register.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A secondary risk is one that emerges because of a response — for example, transferring a risk to a vendor introduces vendor performance risk. (A) is correct. (B) describes a low-priority or watch-list risk. (C) describes a newly identified risk, which can be primary or secondary. (D) confuses risk classification with stakeholder importance.
