# Question

A project manager must choose between two suppliers. Supplier X charges $400K and has a 10% chance of a $300K rework cost. Supplier Y charges $360K but has a 30% chance of a $300K rework cost. No other costs differ between them. On expected-monetary-value grounds, which decision is correct and why?

## Options

- **A**: Choose Supplier X; its expected total cost of $430K is lower than Supplier Y's $450K.
- **B**: Choose Supplier Y; its lower base price of $360K makes it cheaper before any rework is considered.
- **C**: Choose Supplier Y; its expected total cost of $390K is lower than Supplier X's $410K.
- **D**: Either is acceptable; the expected total costs are within rounding and the choice should be made on relationship factors.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Expected cost = base price + (probability × rework cost). Supplier X = 400 + 0.10×300 = $430K. Supplier Y = 360 + 0.30×300 = $450K. So X is cheaper on EMV by $20K. (A) computes both correctly. (B) ignores the expected rework, which is exactly what EMV exists to incorporate. (C) miscalculates both figures. (D) the $20K difference is a real expected gap, not a rounding artefact, so deferring to relationship factors misreads the analysis.
