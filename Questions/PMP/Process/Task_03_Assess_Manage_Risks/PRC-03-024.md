# Question

During monitoring, a project manager notices several low-probability risks have been added to the risk register in the last month, crowding out visibility of high-priority risks. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Apply a probability-impact threshold to move low-priority risks to a watch list and keep the register focused.
- **B**: Delete the low-probability risks entirely since they divert attention from the real project threats.
- **C**: Ask the team to stop identifying risks below a certain probability level to keep the register clean.
- **D**: Create a separate risk register for each work package to distribute the items across the team.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A watch list is the standard PMI mechanism for risks that are tracked but don't warrant active response — it preserves visibility without cluttering the primary register. (B) deleting risks loses identified information that may become relevant if conditions change. (C) suppresses risk identification, which is exactly the wrong behaviour to encourage. (D) fragmenting the register by work package hides cross-cutting risks and makes roll-up reporting harder.
