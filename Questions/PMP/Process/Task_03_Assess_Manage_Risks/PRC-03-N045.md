# Question

A project manager must choose between two designs using expected monetary value, comparing total expected cost (build cost plus expected rework). Design X costs $200,000 and carries a 30% chance of a $400,000 rework loss. Design Y costs $260,000 and carries a 10% chance of a $300,000 rework loss. Which design is preferable, and by how much?

## Options

- **A**: Design X, by $30,000.
- **B**: Design Y, by $30,000.
- **C**: Design X, by $60,000.
- **D**: Design Y, by $10,000.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Total expected cost for X = 200,000 + (0.30 × 400,000) = 320,000. For Y = 260,000 + (0.10 × 300,000) = 290,000. Design Y has the lower total expected cost, so it is preferable by 320,000 − 290,000 = 30,000, making (B) correct. (A) reverses the better design. (C) ignores Design Y's residual rework EMV (subtracting only build-cost difference). (D) miscomputes one of the EMV terms. The decision rule is to select the option with the lowest total expected cost.
