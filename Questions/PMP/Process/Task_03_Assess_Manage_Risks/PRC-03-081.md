# Question

A project manager has just taken over a predictive programme to re-tool an automotive components line. The handover pack includes a schedule, a budget, a stakeholder register, and a risk register holding four entries. The sponsor expects the line running within nine months, several specialist suppliers are engaged, and the outgoing project manager leaves the organisation at the end of the week. The project manager has time this week to take one substantive step on risk. Which should she take first?

## Options

- **A**: Run an identification workshop with the project team and key suppliers, working from the schedule, scope, and assumptions to build out the register.
- **B**: Sit down with the outgoing project manager to capture the reasoning and any unrecorded concerns behind the four entries before that person leaves.
- **C**: Rate the four logged risks for probability and impact so the most serious can be flagged to the sponsor against the nine-month commitment.
- **D**: Draft the risk management plan, fixing categories, thresholds, and roles, so all subsequent identification and analysis follow one agreed method.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

With four entries against a multi-supplier re-tool, the register is almost certainly incomplete, and the largest unmanaged exposure is the risks no one has named yet; a participative workshop drawing on the team and suppliers is the move that closes that gap (A). The competing options are each defensible, which is what makes the call turn on sequence. (B) is tempting because the source leaves on Friday, but one departing individual's recollection only deepens a single perspective and cannot substitute for broad identification; the knowledge worth capturing is narrow relative to the gap. (C) refines and escalates the four known risks while the bulk of the exposure stays invisible. (D) installs good method but produces no actual risk information, and method without a populated register does not protect the deadline. The discriminator is breadth of identification first versus capturing departing knowledge, analysing the known few, or formalising method.
