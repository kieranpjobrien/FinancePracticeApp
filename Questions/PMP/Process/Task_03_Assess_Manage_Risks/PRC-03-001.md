# Question

In a sprint retrospective, the team observes that for the last four sprints they've discovered significant defects only in the final two days, leading to rushed fixes and incomplete regression testing. Which risk response best fits this pattern?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the risk by allocating a permanent buffer of two days at the end of every sprint for defect remediation.
- **B**: Avoid the risk by extending sprint length from two weeks to three weeks so defects have more time to be addressed.
- **C**: Mitigate the risk by introducing earlier-feedback practices such as test-driven development, continuous integration, and pair-testing during the sprint.
- **D**: Transfer the risk by moving testing responsibility to a separate QA team that runs in parallel to the development team.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The pattern describes late discovery; the right response shifts feedback earlier so problems surface when there's still time to address them. (C) is mitigation aimed at the root cause — when defects are found, not the defects themselves. (A) accepts the symptom rather than fixing it. (B) gives the same broken process more runway. (D) isn't true risk transfer (no third party assumes the impact) and adding QA without changing the feedback loop won't move discovery earlier.
