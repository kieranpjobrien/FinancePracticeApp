# Question

A PM has decided to transfer a cybersecurity risk to an insurance provider with a $2M policy. During planning a team member points out that insurance introduces a policy-dispute delay risk, and that a residual $500K excess still sits with the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the residual excess quietly and note the dispute-delay exposure separately in the risk register for ongoing tracking over time.
- **B**: Choose a different response strategy because transfer leaves too many follow-on risks to manage effectively for the project over delivery.
- **C**: Record the dispute-delay as a secondary risk and the $500K excess as a residual risk, with owners and monitoring triggers assigned.
- **D**: Increase the insurance policy to cover the excess fully and negotiate faster payout terms to eliminate the delay exposure in the process.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI recognises secondary risks (created by a response) and residual risks (remaining after a response), both requiring explicit tracking. (C) applies both concepts correctly. (A) downplays residuals and keeps secondary risk out of the register. (B) discarding a sound response because it has follow-ons misunderstands risk response theory. (D) may not be available commercially and misses the process point about documenting what remains.
