# Question

A low-priority risk on a logistics-software project was placed on a watch list with no active response. During execution the project manager observes the early-warning condition the team had associated with that risk beginning to appear. Which actions are appropriate at this point? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Treat the warning condition as a trigger indicating the risk may be becoming more likely or imminent.
- **B**: Reassess the risk's probability and impact now that conditions have changed, and reprioritise it if warranted.
- **C**: Determine and, if justified, plan a response, since a watch-listed risk can move into active management once conditions shift.
- **D**: Immediately log the risk as an issue, because the appearance of a trigger means the risk has occurred.
- **E**: Leave the risk untouched on the watch list, as its priority was fixed when it was first assessed.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A trigger is an early-warning sign that a watch-listed risk may be intensifying, so the correct loop is to recognise the trigger (A), reassess the risk under the changed conditions (B), and move it into active management with a response if the reassessment justifies it (C). (D) is wrong because a trigger signals a risk may be approaching, not that it has occurred; only a realised risk becomes an issue. (E) is wrong because risk priorities are not fixed; monitoring exists precisely so that changed conditions prompt reassessment.
