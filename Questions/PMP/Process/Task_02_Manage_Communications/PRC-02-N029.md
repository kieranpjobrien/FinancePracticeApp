# Question

On an agile programme, a senior business sponsor approaches individual developers mid-sprint with new feature ideas, and several have quietly begun building them, so the product owner only discovers the additions at the sprint review after capacity has gone on unprioritised work. The sponsor's ideas are genuinely valuable and the sponsor, who moves fast and dislikes process, believes they are simply being responsive. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Explain to the sponsor why feature ideas must go through the product owner to be prioritised, so the team commits to one agreed set of work each sprint.
- **B**: Establish a fast, lightweight intake the sponsor can use the instant an idea strikes, which the product owner triages into the backlog, and explain that this replaces going to developers.
- **C**: Set up a shared idea board the sponsor posts to whenever they wish, which the product owner reviews and orders into the backlog each sprint.
- **D**: Agree a team working agreement that developers accept work only from the sprint backlog, and brief the sponsor on it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The breakdown is that requirements enter through a side channel because the sponsor values speed, so any fix that only tells them to route through the product owner has to give them an equally fast alternative or the bypass will return. (B) does both: it redirects the sponsor to the product owner and gives them an immediate outlet that satisfies the responsiveness they care about, removing the reason to grab a developer. (A) addresses the cause and relationship but offers no fast route, so a speed-oriented sponsor is likely to revert the next time an idea strikes. (C) provides a home for ideas, but a board the product owner reviews "each sprint" is too slow for this sponsor and still does not stop them walking up to a developer in the meantime. (D) protects the backlog from the developers' side but puts them in the position of rebuffing a senior sponsor and does nothing to redirect the sponsor's energy. The fine margin is between simply asserting the correct channel (A/C/D) and replacing the side channel with one at least as fast (B).
