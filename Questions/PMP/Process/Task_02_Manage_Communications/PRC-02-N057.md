# Question

A confirmed data-privacy breach is in active response on a project the PM leads. Legal, the data-protection officer (DPO), the sponsor, affected customers, and a supervisory regulator all have a stake, with different timing and content needs; the regulator has a statutory notification clock already running. The PM is coordinating communications during the response. Which THREE actions should the PM take? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Establish a single source of agreed, current facts that all outbound messages draw from, with each external statement authorised before release.
- **B**: Confirm with legal and the DPO what may be disclosed to each audience and on what timeline, before any external statement goes out.
- **C**: Track the regulator's statutory notification deadline as the binding constraint on external sequencing, and align other audience timings around meeting it.
- **D**: Notify affected customers immediately with the facts confirmed so far, ahead of the regulator, so customers are never the last to learn of the breach.
- **E**: Brief the sponsor in parallel with preparing external messaging, giving them the same agreed fact base rather than waiting until external statements are settled.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Coordinated communications in a regulated breach require one authoritative fact base feeding all messages (A), content and timing cleared by legal and the DPO before anything external is said (B), and recognition that the statutory regulator clock is the hard constraint that other timings must respect (C). (D) sounds principled but unilaterally sequencing customer notice ahead of a statutory regulator deadline, without legal clearance, can breach the very notification rules in play and pre-empts the timeline (B) and (C) exist to govern. (E) is genuinely good practice, keeping the sponsor informed early, but among these it is the less critical of the timing actions: it does not bear on the legally constrained external sequencing the way (A), (B), and (C) do, so it is the weaker selection in a "choose three" set.
