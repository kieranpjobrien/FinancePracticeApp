# Question

On a digital transformation programme, team members report they are fielding dozens of "where are we at?" questions from middle managers weekly. The questions are uncoordinated and frequently duplicated. The team's productive focus time is being eroded noticeably. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask team members to redirect every inbound question to the PM so the team is shielded from interruptions.
- **B**: Publish a self-service status page updated weekly and explicitly direct middle managers to it before asking the team.
- **C**: Raise the interruption pattern with the manager group's lead and ask them to queue questions through a single coordinator.
- **D**: Institute a weekly drop-in session for middle managers where any question can be asked and answered directly in person.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Push/pull communication design is a core PMI concept; repeatedly answering the same pull question is wasted. (B) moves information to pull where it belongs. (A) single-point funnelling to the PM creates a new bottleneck. (C) coordinating demand still leaves the inefficient channel in place. (D) a drop-in reduces interruptions but doesn't address duplication or give managers information on their own timeline.
