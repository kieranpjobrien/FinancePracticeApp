# Question

The current PM is rolling off in three weeks and a new PM has been named. The outgoing PM holds substantial undocumented context — informal stakeholder agreements, sensitivity around two specific stakeholders, and a couple of off-record commitments. What should be done?

## Options

- **A**: Compile the undocumented information into a confidential single-document brief and hand it to the new PM on day one.
- **B**: Have the outgoing PM record short voice memos summarising each undocumented item so the new PM can listen as needed.
- **C**: Hold a one-day handover workshop covering the formal artefacts and trust the new PM to surface the rest as needed in their first month.
- **D**: Run a structured handover with stakeholder introductions, written capture of informal agreements, and overlap time at key meetings.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PM handovers fail on tacit knowledge; the PMI move is to make tacit knowledge explicit and create overlap so the new PM is introduced into existing relationships rather than discovering them cold. (D) does this comprehensively. (A) captures information but skips the live introductions and meetings. (B) is informal and unstructured. (C) underweights the informal context and creates risk of dropped commitments.
