# Question

Two technical leads on a software programme disagree sharply about an architectural direction, and the choice blocks three teams from starting the next iteration. They have exchanged long, increasingly entrenched messages on the team channel for two days without converging. The decision needs to be made by tomorrow. Both leads are articulate in writing and currently in different offices. What is the best way for the PM to drive this to a decision?

## Options

- **A**: Ask each lead to write a one-page options paper with a recommendation, circulate both, and have the affected teams' input gathered before the PM decides.
- **B**: Convene a short facilitated working session with both leads to surface assumptions, weigh options in real time, and reach a decision both can commit to.
- **C**: Move the existing thread to a dedicated decision channel with a hard deadline, so the written debate is captured and concluded in one place by tomorrow.
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to the architecture authority with both positions attached and ask them to rule before the iteration starts.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A real-time disagreement that written exchange has only hardened needs interactive communication, where assumptions can be challenged and a jointly owned decision reached quickly, which is why a facilitated session is the right method here. (B) fits the urgency and the entrenchment. (A) is a sound technique for comparing options but keeps two dug-in leads in the written mode that has already failed to converge, and adds a round of input the deadline barely allows. (C) merely relocates the same asynchronous debate that produced the stalemate and trusts a deadline to break entrenchment it has not broken in two days. (D) may be appropriate if the leads genuinely cannot agree, but escalating before attempting a facilitated resolution removes ownership from the people closest to the work and is premature as a first move.
