# Question

A predictive infrastructure project has 40 stakeholders who all receive the same fortnightly 12-page status report. Over the last two cycles, a senior regulator has complained they cannot locate the two compliance metrics they are accountable for, and the delivery crews have confirmed they no longer open the report at all. The PM checks the communications management plan: the report's content and distribution were set as written. The project is otherwise on track and the next cycle is due in three days. How should the PM proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the communications requirements for each stakeholder group, then revise the plan so format, content, and frequency match each group, scheduling the changes from the next feasible cycle.
- **B**: Issue a regulator-facing one-pager carrying the two compliance metrics now, given the regulator's accountability, and reassess the wider report afterwards.
- **C**: Add a one-page executive summary to the front of the existing report so any reader can find the key items quickly.
- **D**: Convert the report to a self-service dashboard whose filtered views give each group only what it needs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Two distinct stakeholder needs have demonstrably failed, which is a signal to re-derive requirements across the audience rather than treat the two complaints as isolated, and there is no deadline forcing a same-cycle fix for either. (A) reassesses needs and corrects the artefact that drives every communication, preventing the next mismatch among the other 38; scheduling from the next feasible cycle is reasonable because nothing here is time-critical. (B) is genuinely defensible because a regulator's compliance metrics carry weight, but the regulator faces no stated deadline, so jumping to a point fix before reassessing risks repeating the pattern and still leaves the crews unserved. (C) helps time-poor readers but still buries the regulator's metrics in a long document and does nothing for crews who have stopped opening it. (D) presumes every group will pull information, which the crews have already shown they will not. The fine margin is between re-deriving requirements for the whole audience (A) and prioritising the most accountable single stakeholder first (B) when no deadline forces the latter.
