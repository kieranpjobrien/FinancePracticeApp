# Question

On a hybrid software programme, three of the eight Scrum team members work from a satellite office. The team already runs a shared digital board and a daily video stand-up. At the retrospective the satellite members raise that small design decisions the co-located five settle in passing keep reaching them only when their stories are rejected; rework on those stories is climbing. The team agrees this is the top issue and asks the Scrum Master to help them act on it before the next sprint. Several fixes are proposed in the room. Which should the Scrum Master back?

## Options

- **A**: A standing agreement that any design decision reached outside a team event is written to the shared board the moment it is made.
- **B**: A short, team-run working agreement that no design decision is considered settled until it is on the board, so unrecorded decisions carry no authority.
- **C**: A daily second check-in, timed for the satellite office, in which the co-located members recap decisions taken since stand-up.
- **D**: A buddy arrangement pairing each satellite member with a co-located member who passes on decisions relevant to their stories.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

All four target the same gap and all could plausibly be tried, so the discriminator is which one removes the incentive for decisions to stay unrecorded rather than relying on people to remember an extra step. (A) is the obvious "capture it on the board" move, but a duty to post the moment a decision is made depends entirely on busy people remembering in the moment, the exact behaviour that is already failing. (B) is finer: by making board entry the definition of "decided," it strips authority from any decision not recorded, so the satellite members can safely treat anything off-board as not yet binding and the co-located members have a reason to post or risk their decision not counting. (C) creates a durable relay but still lets decisions go unrecorded for hours and adds a daily meeting. (D) recreates person-to-person asymmetry and fails the day a buddy forgets. The fine margin is between asking people to remember to capture decisions (A/C/D) and changing what counts as a decision so capture is self-enforcing (B).
