# Question

A PM on a hybrid renewable-energy construction project faces four distinct communication situations in the same week. Match each situation to the communication method that best fits it. Each method is used once.

## Items

- **1**: Two suppliers and the PM hold conflicting assumptions about a shared interface, and several design options are still open; the PM needs them reconciled into one agreed approach by Friday.
- **2**: A reportable safety incident has occurred, and the PM must notify the named regulator within the statutory window and be able to show, if audited later, exactly when the notice went and that it was received.
- **3**: The site team occasionally needs to check one figure or tolerance from a 200-page specification set, but which detail any individual needs, and when, is unpredictable.
- **4**: The site lead wants today's safety stand-down count and progress against plan to register with everyone passing through the site office, without anyone having to ask or open anything.

## Matches

- **A**: A facilitated working session held with all parties at once
- **B**: A formal notification addressed to a specific recipient with delivery recorded
- **C**: A shared repository the audience accesses on its own initiative
- **D**: A status display kept current in a place people pass

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Each situation is defined by the constraint that decides the method, not by naming the method. Situation 1 has open options and conflicting assumptions that must converge by a deadline, which needs the live, multi-party negotiation of interactive communication, so (A). Situation 2 must reach one named party within a legal window and survive later audit, which requires a targeted notification with recorded delivery, the essence of push, so (B). Situation 3 has unpredictable, occasional, individual lookups against a large reference body, which is uneconomic to push to everyone and best served by letting people retrieve what they need when they need it, pull communication, so (C). Situation 4 wants current status to land on passers-by with no request or action on their part, which is an information radiator, so (D). The discriminators are direction and initiative: interactive converges many parties live, push targets a recipient and proves delivery, pull waits to be drawn on, and a radiator broadcasts continuously in place. The near-confusions are deliberate, situation 2 could be logged in a repository, but only a recorded push satisfies the audit duty, and situation 4's status could be emailed, but only a radiator reaches people without their asking.
