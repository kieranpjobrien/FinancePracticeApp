# Question

The PM has just learned that a key supplier will miss a milestone by four weeks, putting a major release at risk. The next steering committee is in eight days. The sponsor is travelling and has asked to be bothered only with "material issues" while away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Contact the sponsor today using the agreed urgent-contact channel with a brief summary and a recommended response path.
- **B**: Prepare a comprehensive impact paper and present it at the scheduled steering committee meeting in eight days as usual.
- **C**: Email the sponsor a short update tonight and follow up only if there's no acknowledgement received within forty-eight hours.
- **D**: Inform the sponsor's deputy today and ask them to decide whether this meets the sponsor's material-issues threshold.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A four-week slip to a major release clearly meets the "material" bar, and PMI expects timely, direct, well-framed communication to the sponsor. (A) uses the agreed channel with a recommendation, which is the PM's role. (B) waiting eight days on a material issue is a transparency failure. (C) passive follow-up invites the issue to linger unaddressed. (D) routing a material issue through a deputy abdicates the direct communication the sponsor is owed.
