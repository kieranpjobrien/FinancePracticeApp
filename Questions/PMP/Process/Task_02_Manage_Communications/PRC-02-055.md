# Question

A PM's status report shows the project is "amber" for the third week running due to an unresolved resourcing issue. The sponsor pushes back, saying an amber status makes them look bad to their peers. They suggest reporting "green with watchpoint" until the issue resolves. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the report to "green with watchpoint" as the sponsor requests, documenting their stated rationale in the project file.
- **B**: Keep the amber status and offer to brief the sponsor's peers directly on the specific issue and the response plan.
- **C**: Hold the amber status firm and explain to the sponsor that altering reporting labels for optics breaches PMI ethics and governance trust.
- **D**: Propose a joint briefing with the resourcing team and the sponsor to agree a response plan that genuinely returns status to green.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects honest reporting and constructive response — not just resisting pressure. (D) addresses both the sponsor's concern and the underlying issue by fixing the colour legitimately. (A) capitulates to pressure to misreport, breaching transparency. (B) preserves integrity but leaves the underlying issue unaddressed. (C) is ethically correct but confrontational without offering a path through — not the PMI-preferred first move.
