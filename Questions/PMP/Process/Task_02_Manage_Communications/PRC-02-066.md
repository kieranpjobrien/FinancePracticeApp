# Question

After most project meetings a junior coordinator circulates lengthy minutes capturing everything said. Three weeks later, team members dispute what was agreed about a scope item and cannot find a clear record amid the narrative. What is the most useful improvement to how meeting outcomes are recorded?

## Options

- **A**: Have the minutes reviewed and formally approved by all attendees before they are circulated.
- **B**: Record decisions made and action items with owners and dates as a distinct, easily found section, separate from the discussion narrative.
- **C**: Shorten the minutes to a brief paragraph summarising the meeting so the key points are not buried.
- **D**: Move to recording the meetings so the team can replay the relevant discussion when a dispute arises.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The failure is that commitments are lost inside discussion notes, so the fix is to capture decisions and actions, with owners and due dates, as a clearly delineated record that can be found and relied on. (B) does this directly. (C) reduces volume but a single summary paragraph can still obscure specific decisions and drops the action ownership the team needs. (A) adds rigour to the existing format but approving long narrative minutes does not make a specific agreement easy to locate later. (D) lets people relitigate by replaying audio, which is slower and still produces no authoritative statement of what was decided.
