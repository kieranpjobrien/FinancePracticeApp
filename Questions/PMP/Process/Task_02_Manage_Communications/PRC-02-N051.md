# Question

The PM must tell a long-standing vendor that their contract will not be renewed, owing to documented quality failures, and the supporting performance data is ready. Procurement will handle the formal contractual notice and its required wording. The vendor's account manager has a strong working relationship with the team and is likely to push back and ask for specifics. Reading the situation, how should the PM handle delivering this message?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a direct conversation with the account manager to deliver the decision and rationale and absorb the immediate reaction, then have procurement issue the formal written notice.
- **B**: Have procurement issue the formal written notice first so the position is precise and on record, then follow up with a conversation to discuss it with the account manager.
- **C**: Deliver the decision in a written message from the PM that sets out the performance data clearly, then offer a call if the account manager wants to discuss it.
- **D**: Raise the decision with the account manager at the next scheduled vendor review meeting, where the performance data is already on the agenda, so it is delivered in context.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A sensitive, relationship-laden decision is best delivered interactively, so tone and questions can be handled in real time, and the considerate sequence is for the person who owns the relationship to deliver it before the formal paperwork lands, not after. (A) puts the human conversation first and lets procurement formalise it. (B) is precise but lets a contractual notice reach a long-standing partner before any personal conversation, which reads as cold and procedural for news of this weight. (C) leads with one-way written delivery and makes the discussion optional, forgoing control of the reaction the PM knows is coming. (D) folds a serious, relationship-ending message into a routine review with others potentially present, diluting its gravity and removing the privacy such news warrants.
