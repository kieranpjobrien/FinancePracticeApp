# Question

The PM learns that a stakeholder has been describing the project to executives in much more pessimistic terms than the data supports. The executives have started questioning the project's value. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Send a written rebuttal to the executives correcting the record with the actual data.
- **B**: Have a private conversation with the stakeholder to understand what's driving their view, then address any underlying concerns.
- **C**: Escalate the misrepresentation to the project sponsor and ask them to handle the executive conversation.
- **D**: Ask the stakeholder's manager to coach them on factual representation of project status.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI default is to engage the source directly and try to understand the underlying concern before escalating or correcting publicly. (B) does this. (A) corrects the record but bypasses a stakeholder relationship that's clearly strained. (C) escalates before any direct attempt at resolution. (D) routes through the stakeholder's line manager, which is also escalation by another route and damages trust.
