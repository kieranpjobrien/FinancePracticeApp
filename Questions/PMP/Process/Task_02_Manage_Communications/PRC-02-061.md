# Question

A PM is asked by the sponsor to share detailed individual team performance data in the monthly stakeholder report, including names and productivity metrics per person. The PM's organisation has a clear policy against this and staff haven't consented. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Share the data the sponsor requested and flag the policy concern in a brief appendix note for the record only.
- **B**: Share aggregated team-level metrics without individual names, and explain the privacy policy constraint to the sponsor directly.
- **C**: Escalate the request to HR and legal for a ruling and await their written direction before responding to the sponsor.
- **D**: Ask each team member individually for consent and share only the data of those who explicitly agree to be named.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Compliance is non-negotiable; the PM shares what's permitted and explains what isn't, rather than escalating or breaching policy. (B) delivers usable information while respecting policy and dignity. (A) breaches policy under a sponsor request is still a breach. (C) escalating a clearly-defined policy question wastes governance attention. (D) selective consent-gathering creates unequal visibility and pressure on team members to opt in.
