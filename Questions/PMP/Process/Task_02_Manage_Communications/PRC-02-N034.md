# Question

A newly formed virtual team is struggling: messages get lost across several tools, some members feel excluded, and decisions are hard to trace later. The PM wants to put communication on a healthier footing. Which two actions address the underlying causes most directly? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team to agree which channel is used for what purpose and where decisions are recorded.
- **B**: Establish team communication norms covering responsiveness, meeting etiquette, and inclusion across time zones.
- **C**: Standardise the whole team onto a single collaboration platform to eliminate the spread of tools.
- **D**: Increase the frequency of synchronous video meetings so members interact more often and feel less excluded.
- **E**: Have the PM personally summarise and record the key decisions from each channel at the end of every day.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

A virtual team lacks the informal cues of co-location, so the durable fixes are deliberate, team-owned agreements about channels and behaviour. (A) gives each channel a clear purpose and a traceable home for decisions, addressing the lost-messages and untraceable-decisions problems at the root. (B) sets shared expectations on responsiveness and inclusion, which is what stops members feeling excluded. (C) is tempting and may help, but tooling alone does not tell people what to use a channel for or where decisions live, so confusion can persist even on one platform; it treats a symptom. (D) adds contact but more meetings without agreed norms can deepen exclusion across time zones rather than ease it. (E) makes decisions traceable yet relies on one person daily and does not build the team's own discipline, creating a single point of failure. The discrimination is between team-owned working agreements (A/B) and tool or effort fixes that leave the causes in place (C/D/E).
