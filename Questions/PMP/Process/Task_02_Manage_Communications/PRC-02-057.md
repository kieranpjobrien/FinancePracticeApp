# Question

A public-sector infrastructure project has just experienced a safety incident on site. No serious injury, but media attention is likely. The sponsor asks the PM to draft an external statement within the hour. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Draft a factual statement covering what happened and the remedial actions taken, and send it to the sponsor for review.
- **B**: Engage the organisation's communications and legal teams immediately and collaborate on any external statement rather than drafting solo.
- **C**: Interview the site supervisor and nearby workers for a detailed account before drafting any statement for external audiences.
- **D**: Prepare a holding response saying the organisation is investigating, and commit to a full public statement within twenty-four hours.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

External communications on regulated incidents are a specialist function; PMI expects the PM to engage the right discipline rather than draft solo. (B) is the correct first move. (A) drafting external statements without communications and legal input risks statutory and reputational exposure. (C) witness interviews are for investigations, not media statements. (D) a holding response is a reasonable output, but only after engaging the specialist teams.
