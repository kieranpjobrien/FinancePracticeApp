# Question

On an agile retail-systems programme, stakeholders keep emailing the PM and the Scrum Master throughout the day asking "where is feature X" and "is the team blocked on anything". Answering each query individually is consuming hours and the answers are already visible on the team's board. The team is co-located with the main stakeholder group in the same building. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Set up a shared dashboard that mirrors the board and brief stakeholders that current status now lives there for them to check.
- **B**: Introduce a rule that status questions are collected and answered in a single daily email so the team is not interrupted piecemeal.
- **C**: Invite the questioning stakeholders to attend the daily stand-up so they hear progress and blockers directly.
- **D**: Publish a more detailed written status report twice a week so stakeholders have richer information and ask fewer questions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The information already exists and is current on the board; the gap is access, so the fix is to make that live status self-serve through an information radiator the stakeholders can pull from at any time. (A) does exactly this and removes the interruption at source. (C) is plausible but the stand-up is the team's working session, and turning it into a stakeholder briefing risks diluting its purpose and inviting steering; it also only serves those who attend each morning. (B) still routes every question through people and batches rather than eliminates the load. (D) adds a heavier push artefact when the stakeholders want quick current status, not more prose, and reporting twice weekly will not answer an intraday "where is it now".
