# Question

A regulatory stakeholder is listed in the communications plan to receive the full weekly status report. The stakeholder has emailed saying the report is "too much, too often" and asks only to be told when compliance risks arise. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue sending the full report to preserve audit traceability and meet the regulator's documented obligation on this programme.
- **B**: Summarise the weekly report into a shorter exception-based update, reserving the full detail for monthly or on-demand requests.
- **C**: Update the communications plan so the stakeholder receives targeted compliance-risk notifications, with the full report on demand.
- **D**: Discuss the request with the sponsor before any change in communications approach to a regulatory stakeholder is considered.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Communications plans are living documents tailored to stated information needs; when a stakeholder clarifies theirs, the plan updates. (C) honours the stated need and preserves access to detail. (A) ignoring a stakeholder's own information need isn't "preserving traceability" — it's wasting their attention. (B) informal trimming without updating the plan creates ambiguity. (D) escalating a routine plan adjustment to the sponsor is over-governance.
