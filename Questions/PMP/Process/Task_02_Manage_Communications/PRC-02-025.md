# Question

A senior engineer's status updates to business stakeholders are dense with technical jargon. Stakeholders politely nod but later raise the same questions repeatedly, and decisions are being delayed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ban the engineer from speaking directly to business stakeholders and route all technical updates through the PM instead.
- **B**: Tell business stakeholders to email the engineer directly with questions about anything in the updates they don't understand.
- **C**: Continue current practice and absorb the question delays as normal communication friction on technical delivery projects.
- **D**: Coach the engineer on audience-tailored communication and review key updates before they're sent until the new style embeds.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM's job is to develop communication capability on the team, not to insulate stakeholders from technical contributors. (D) builds the engineer's skill and creates a temporary safety net. (A) removes a valuable communicator entirely. (B) shifts the burden to stakeholders rather than fixing the source. (C) accepts a known and worsening problem.
