# Question

A programme has grown to 60 people spread across five workstreams. Teams are using a mix of Slack, Teams, Confluence, email, and two task trackers. Information is scattered and new joiners can't find anything. The sponsor is pressing for better visibility. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate a single chat tool and single documentation tool across all workstreams to eliminate sprawl and make information discoverable for joiners.
- **B**: Hire a dedicated information-management coordinator for the programme who curates content across all existing tools and guides new joiners daily.
- **C**: Run a short discovery with each workstream on how they actually use each tool before choosing any rationalisation approach for the programme.
- **D**: Agree a minimal tool baseline — one hub, one chat, one work-item tracker — and migrate workstreams progressively over a defined period.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI-aligned information management balances standardisation with realistic migration effort. (D) defines the baseline, plans the move, and respects the work-in-flight. (A) sudden mandates disrupt productive work and usually fail. (B) a curator treats the symptom by papering over sprawl rather than fixing it. (C) discovery without a decision keeps the sponsor waiting for visibility.
