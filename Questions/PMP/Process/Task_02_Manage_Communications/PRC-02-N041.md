# Question

Midway through a predictive infrastructure programme, three stakeholders independently say the weekly status report is "too long and I can't find what matters to me." They share one report. The steering-committee chair wants schedule and budget exceptions; the operations lead wants upcoming outage windows; the regulator wants open compliance items. The three sets barely overlap. The report is produced manually each week by a part-time coordinator who is already stretched. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Produce role-specific views for each of the three stakeholders, generated from the single underlying dataset so the coordinator maintains one source, not three reports.
- **B**: Restructure the single report into clearly labelled per-stakeholder sections, so each reader goes straight to their part without the coordinator producing multiple outputs.
- **C**: Add a one-page front summary that pulls each stakeholder's key items to the top, leaving the detailed body unchanged behind it.
- **D**: Meet the three stakeholders together to agree a single trimmed report that keeps only what at least one of them needs, cutting the rest.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The needs are genuinely distinct and the complaint is about relevance, so role-specific views give each stakeholder only their content, and generating them from one dataset means the stretched coordinator still maintains a single source rather than three hand-built reports. (A) resolves the relevance problem without multiplying manual effort. (B) is the closest rival and keeps effort contained, but every reader still receives the others' sections, so the document stays long and the "too long" complaint largely persists. (C) improves navigation yet still delivers the full irrelevant body to all three, treating a relevance problem as a findability one. (D) trims length but, because the three needs barely overlap, the union of "what at least one needs" is nearly the whole report, so it removes little while forcing shared content nobody wanted in common.
