# Question

A co-located agile team building an insurance claims portal emails the sponsor a hand-built status note every Friday. The sponsor sits two floors up, visits the team area perhaps once a fortnight, and has told the PM the Friday figures are already stale by the time the leadership meeting opens on Monday. The team keeps a physical board and a live burndown on the wall that the sponsor rarely sees. The PM wants a sustainable fix. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a standing fifteen-minute briefing with the sponsor each Monday immediately before the leadership meeting, walking through current status.
- **B**: Stand up a live electronic dashboard that mirrors the team's board and burndown, and direct the sponsor to it to check status whenever needed.
- **C**: Move the status email from Friday to first thing Monday so the sponsor carries fresh figures into the leadership meeting.
- **D**: Invite the sponsor to the team's daily stand-up so they absorb status directly from the team rather than from a report.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team already generates current information on its radiators; the only gap is that the remote sponsor cannot see it, and every option that pushes a fresh snapshot still requires someone to keep pushing. (B) surfaces the live data the team already maintains as a self-service view, so the sponsor pulls current status on demand and the channel does not decay between sends or depend on anyone's availability. (A) gives the sponsor a fresh, interactive read but ties currency to a recurring meeting and the PM's diary, and leaves the sponsor blind the rest of the week. (C) re-times the same hand-built report so the figures are fresh for the meeting, but it preserves the manual effort and goes stale again immediately after. (D) gives direct, current exposure but conscripts a fortnightly visitor into a daily team ceremony built for the team's coordination, not executive oversight, and depends on the sponsor showing up. The discriminator is which channel stays current without continued manual effort or attendance.
