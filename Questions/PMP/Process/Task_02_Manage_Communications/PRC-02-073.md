# Question

A PM is classifying four communications on a hybrid programme so each is handled with appropriate formality and reach. Match each communication (left) to the dimension that most defines how it must be handled (right).

## Items

- **1**: A signed contract variation that must be issued to an external supplier and will be relied on in any later dispute.
- **2**: A quick "are you free to pair for an hour" message between two developers on the same team.
- **3**: A regulator's mandated quarterly compliance statement submitted outside the performing organisation.

## Matches

- **A**: Internal and informal: brief, immediate, between team members, with no record needed.
- **B**: External and formal: documented, controlled, and structured because it crosses the organisation's boundary and carries obligation.
- **C**: External and informal: outside the organisation but routine and conversational, such as a casual note to a known counterpart.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Communications are characterised along dimensions such as internal/external and formal/informal, and the handling follows from where each sits. The contract variation (1) and the regulatory statement (3) both cross the organisational boundary and carry legal or compliance weight, so both are external and formal, demanding documentation and control (1-B, 3-B). The developers' pairing request (2) is internal and informal, a fast exchange needing no record (2-A). The deliberate trap is option C: a tempting "external" label might pull item 3 toward it, but a mandated regulatory submission is anything but informal, so C is the unused distractor. The discrimination is recognising that "external" alone does not set the handling, the formality dimension does, and obligation-bearing external communications are always formal.
