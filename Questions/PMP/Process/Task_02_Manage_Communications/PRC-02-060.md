# Question

At each monthly steering committee the same three stakeholders dominate debate and the meeting regularly overruns without clearly documented decisions. Actions assigned verbally are often not followed through. What should the PM propose to the chair?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the current format but add a dedicated minute-taker to capture decisions and actions verbatim throughout each steering committee.
- **B**: Restructure the steering committee agenda around specific decisions with clear pre-reads, timeboxed discussion, and recorded decisions.
- **C**: Move the steering committee to a fortnightly cadence so fewer items per meeting reduce pressure and allow focused debate.
- **D**: Reduce the steering committee attendee list to only the decision-makers so the dominant three voices have less surrounding conflict.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Steering committees fail when they become discussion forums rather than decision forums; PMI-aligned governance is decision-oriented with pre-reads and timeboxing. (B) restructures around that purpose. (A) a minute-taker captures the chaos but doesn't fix it. (C) more meetings doesn't address lack of decision discipline. (D) trimming attendees may reduce dissent rather than improve decisions.
