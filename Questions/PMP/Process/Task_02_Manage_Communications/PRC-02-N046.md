# Question

On a hybrid project, the PM meets a senior executive who has been requesting a bespoke PowerPoint before each governance board. The conversation reveals the real need: the board asks the executive two recurring questions each cycle, "are we on track to the funding milestone, and what decisions need us this period," and the executive uses the deck to answer them. The product owner already publishes the increment, sprint-review outcomes, and a live roadmap, but none is framed around those two questions, and assembling the deck still costs a developer an afternoon each fortnight. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the executive to the sprint review and walk them to the live roadmap, so they can read the milestone position and pending decisions directly each fortnight.
- **B**: Agree a short standing extract, drawn automatically from the existing roadmap and review outputs, that answers the board's two recurring questions, and have a non-delivery role own its upkeep.
- **C**: Reframe the published roadmap itself so milestone-tracking and decisions-needed are surfaced prominently, removing the need for any separate executive artefact.
- **D**: Keep producing the bespoke deck for now but have the product owner, not a developer, assemble it, since the executive's governance need is genuine and the framing is bespoke.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The need is specific and recurring, two governance questions on a fixed cadence, so a small standing extract that targets exactly those questions and is maintained off the delivery team meets the need at the executive's altitude without draining capacity. (B) fits the cadence, content, and capacity together. (C) is attractive but bends a team-facing roadmap to carry board-level framing, which can clutter the artefact the delivery team relies on and still may not match the board's cadence. (A) routes a governance question into a delivery event whose altitude and timing serve the team, not a funding board, and depends on the executive interpreting raw artefacts themselves. (D) accepts the genuine need but simply moves the recurring cost onto the product owner rather than removing the manual rework the standing extract eliminates.
