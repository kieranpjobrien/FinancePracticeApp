# Question

On a transport programme, the executive sponsor has told the PM she wants "only exceptions and decisions that need me." A supplier delay has just put one of four parallel workstreams at risk of missing an internal integration date; the PM is confident the team can recover it within the next two weeks without sponsor action, and no funding, scope, or external commitment is affected yet. The monthly sponsor briefing is in three days. What should the PM do about communicating this to the sponsor?

## Options

- **A**: Send the sponsor a brief heads-up now noting the at-risk workstream and that the team is recovering it, so she is not blindsided if it later escalates.
- **B**: Hold the item for the monthly briefing and present it as a managed risk with the recovery plan, since it currently needs no sponsor decision and fits her exception-only preference.
- **C**: Omit the item entirely unless the recovery fails, because an exception-only sponsor should not receive risks the team is handling within its own authority.
- **D**: Escalate the supplier delay to the sponsor now and ask whether she wants to intervene with the supplier relationship, given the integration date is at risk.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The sponsor has defined her information need, exceptions and decisions requiring her, and this item is neither: it needs no decision, threatens nothing external yet, and has a credible recovery in train, so the disciplined choice is to surface it at the next scheduled touchpoint as a managed risk with its plan. (B) respects the agreed filter while still keeping her informed on cadence. (C) goes too far by hiding a live risk until it fails, which denies the sponsor the chance to weigh in if her tolerance differs and breaches transparency. (A) is defensible and tempting, but injecting an unscheduled heads-up for a risk the team is handling, three days before the briefing, edges back toward the noise the sponsor explicitly asked to be spared. (D) over-escalates by inviting sponsor intervention on an issue inside the team's authority, the opposite of an exception-only approach.
