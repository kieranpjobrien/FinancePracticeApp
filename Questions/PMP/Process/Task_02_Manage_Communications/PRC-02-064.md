# Question

A PM leads a multi-country team where English is most members' second language. Written English status updates are routinely misinterpreted and clarifying emails multiply. Team members report feeling reluctant to ask for clarification in front of the wider group. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask bilingual team members to translate key updates into local languages informally so less-fluent members have easier access.
- **B**: Simplify written English, add visual summaries and diagrams, and run short follow-up walkthroughs where people can ask freely.
- **C**: Move all status updates to voice calls so tone and live clarification reduce the misinterpretation of written written content.
- **D**: Mandate that all team members confirm understanding in writing after each update so gaps are explicit and visible.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI communications practice adapts form to audience and creates psychological safety for clarification. (B) attacks both problems — comprehension and the confidence to ask. (A) informal translation creates quality drift and puts unfair load on bilingual colleagues. (C) voice-only loses the written record that a distributed team needs. (D) forced confirmations surface gaps but pressure rather than help the less-confident members.
