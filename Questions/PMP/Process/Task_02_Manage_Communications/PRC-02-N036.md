# Question

At sprint reviews the most senior stakeholder consistently dominates the discussion while the quieter business representatives who actually use the product stay silent. The product owner, hearing mainly the senior voice in the room, has prioritised around it, and the last few releases missed what the broader user base needs. The senior stakeholder is engaged and well-intentioned, and the product owner has not realised the input is skewed. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the review so feedback is deliberately drawn from each stakeholder group in turn before open discussion begins, making the broader needs visible where prioritisation happens.
- **B**: Make the product owner aware that the review input is dominated by one voice and that recent releases reflect it, so they prioritise with that bias in mind.
- **C**: Privately ask the senior stakeholder to hold back until the user representatives have spoken, explaining that their input is being lost.
- **D**: Run a short pre-review with the user representatives to gather their feedback and bring it into the review on their behalf.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The product owner samples stakeholder opinion in the review and is getting a skewed signal, so the durable fix has to rebalance the input at the point where it is gathered, not merely warn about it or work around it. (A) restructures the facilitation so every group's view reaches the room before the loud voice can anchor the discussion, correcting the signal exactly where the product owner reads it. (B) is genuinely useful and may be a necessary companion, but telling the product owner about the bias does not produce the missing user input; they would still have to act on a still-skewed forum. (C) manages the dominant voice but does nothing to draw out people who are choosing not to speak, and may simply create silence. (D) gathers the quiet input but relays it secondhand, losing the live dialogue and keeping those representatives passive. The fine margin is between rebalancing participation in the room itself (A) and either alerting the product owner to the bias (B) or routing around the imbalance (C/D).
