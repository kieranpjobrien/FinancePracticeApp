# Question

Two senior engineers have been trading increasingly terse emails about a genuine, unresolved design choice, copying the whole team. The tone is hurting morale and other members are now taking sides in the replies. Both engineers are technically credible and the decision is theirs to reach. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Convene the two engineers in a real-time conversation, off the email thread, to work through the design question together.
- **B**: Ask the two engineers to take their disagreement off the all-team thread and continue it directly between themselves.
- **C**: Bring in a neutral senior technical authority to facilitate a decision meeting between the two engineers.
- **D**: Have the team's technical lead arbitrate the design choice and communicate the outcome to everyone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A relationship-laden disagreement is escalating in a lean, public channel that strips out tone and invites an audience, so the PM should move it to a rich, synchronous setting where the two can read each other and resolve the actual issue. (A) does this directly and is the proportionate first step. (B) correctly removes the audience but leaves the two still arguing asynchronously by email, the very medium amplifying the conflict. (C) adds a facilitator, which may help if the pair cannot converge, but reaching for a third party before they have even spoken live is heavier than the situation yet warrants. (D) takes the decision away from the credible engineers whose call it is and resolves the technical point without addressing their working relationship. The fine margin is between getting the pair into the right channel themselves (A) and immediately escalating to outside help or authority (C/D).
