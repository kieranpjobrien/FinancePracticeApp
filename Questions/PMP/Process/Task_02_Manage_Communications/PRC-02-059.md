# Question

A key stakeholder has replied to the PM's recent status email with a terse, publicly-cc'd message criticising the project's pace and questioning the PM's competence. The tone is unprofessional and several stakeholders have seen it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reply-all with a calm factual correction of the progress claims and the supporting data, without responding to the personal criticism.
- **B**: Request a one-to-one meeting with the stakeholder to understand their concerns and discuss the tone directly in private.
- **C**: Forward the email to the sponsor and ask them to address the stakeholder's unprofessional public conduct on the PM's behalf.
- **D**: Ignore the email and continue running the project, letting the stakeholder's tone speak for itself to the wider audience.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Public conflict is best de-escalated privately; PMI-aligned practice is to understand concerns and address behaviour directly but respectfully. (B) does both. (A) replying-all invites an ongoing public exchange. (C) escalating to the sponsor without attempting resolution skips a level and looks retaliatory. (D) ignoring leaves the public impression unchallenged and undermines the PM's credibility.
