# Question

A project's monthly progress report is published to a SharePoint site that all 30 stakeholders can browse. Next month's report must include early commercial figures from a confidential vendor negotiation that, per the sponsor, only the six-person steering committee may see; the rest of the report should remain broadly available. The site supports folder-level permissions. The PM wants to keep the convenience of the existing site. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the general report openly available on the site and place the commercial figures in a separate, permission-restricted area visible only to the steering committee.
- **B**: Keep the general report on the site and send the commercial figures to the six committee members as a separately controlled, targeted distribution.
- **C**: Publish the full report to the open site with the commercial section clearly marked "Confidential — Steering Committee Only".
- **D**: Continue the single open report but move the commercial figures into a sealed appendix released to the committee on a need-to-know basis.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The information should stay where stakeholders already pull it, and the platform itself can enforce who sees what, so the cleanest fit is to keep one site and let its permissions segregate the sensitive content. (A) preserves the convenient pull channel for the general report while applying an actual access control to the figures. (B) protects the figures but abandons the platform for that content and splits the report across two systems, adding maintenance and reducing the convenience the PM wants to keep. (C) relies on a label rather than a control on an openly browsable site, leaving the data exposed. (D) keeps a single document, which couples the open and restricted content together and makes consistent access control harder than separating them. The fine margin is between enforcing access within the existing site (A) and pushing the sensitive part out to a separate channel (B).
