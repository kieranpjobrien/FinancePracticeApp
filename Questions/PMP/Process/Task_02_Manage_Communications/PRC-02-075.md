# Question

An engaged product owner on an agile programme is copied on every team channel, automated build notification, and pull-request alert because she once asked to "stay close to the work". She now says she is drowning and is missing the few items that genuinely need her input among the constant noise. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the product owner to define which decisions and exceptions actually need her, and route only those to her directly.
- **B**: Create a daily digest that consolidates the day's notifications into a single summary she can read once.
- **C**: Remove her from the automated technical channels and rely on the Scrum Master to flag anything relevant to her.
- **D**: Suggest she set up filters and rules so the high-priority items are highlighted within the existing flow.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The product owner is suffering information overload, and the remedy is to filter at source by agreeing what genuinely requires her attention and sending only that, rather than continuing to pipe everything to her. (A) addresses the root cause by matching what she receives to what she needs to act on. (B) reduces interruption frequency but still delivers the full firehose once a day, so the signal she needs can still be lost in a long digest. (D) pushes the filtering burden onto her within the same overwhelming stream and depends on her configuring tooling correctly. (C) cuts her off and delegates her awareness to someone else's judgement, swinging from too much to too little and removing her agency over what she sees. Defining the relevant subset with her keeps her in control and ends the overload.
