# Question

A PM discovers through a casual conversation that two team members are being considered for redundancy post-project. HR has asked the PM to keep this confidential. One of the two team members confides that they're worried about their future and asks the PM directly if their role is safe. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm to the team member that their role is secure so they can focus fully on finishing the project without unnecessary worry.
- **B**: Tell the team member what the PM has heard so they can make informed decisions about their own career and financial situation.
- **C**: Refer the team member to HR for any questions about role security while maintaining the confidence the PM has been asked to keep.
- **D**: Deflect the question by saying the PM has no information about organisational decisions outside the immediate project's direct scope of work.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's ethics code requires honesty without breaching legitimate confidentiality; redirecting to the correct authority respects both. (C) is the honest, proper channel response. (A) lying to a team member breaches honesty. (B) breaches HR-requested confidence and may pre-empt a decision not yet made. (D) is a deflection bordering on dishonesty because the PM does hold relevant information.
