# Question

A PM has finished analysing communication needs for a product rollout across three regional offices and has the findings documented. East wants a weekly written summary; West wants a fortnightly call; Central wants only exception alerts when their region is affected. The three sets of needs overlap on roughly half the content but differ on cadence and format. The sponsor has asked the PM to keep the communications approach "as simple as we responsibly can." How should the PM structure the approach?

## Options

- **A**: Run all three communications on a single weekly cycle in a common format, since the content overlaps by half and one cadence is simpler to operate than three.
- **B**: Define one shared content set maintained centrally, and deliver it to each office in the format and cadence its analysis showed, drawing from the common content plus region-specific items.
- **C**: Maintain three fully independent communication streams, one per office, each owned and produced separately so every regional preference is honoured exactly.
- **D**: Deliver the overlapping half through one shared weekly report to all offices, and handle each office's distinct needs through separate ad hoc messages as they arise.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A single source of content delivered in each audience's analysed format and cadence honours the documented needs while keeping production simple, satisfying the sponsor's "as simple as responsibly possible" constraint. (B) balances tailoring against efficiency. (A) collapses three validated cadences into one for the producer's convenience, overriding the analysis and pushing West and Central into a rhythm they did not want. (C) honours preferences but triples the production effort and invites the shared content to drift out of sync across three streams, the opposite of simple. (D) splits each office's communication across a scheduled report plus unplanned ad hoc messages, which fragments their picture and makes the exception-only office field a weekly report it never requested.
