# Question

A PM on a large utilities transformation runs a detailed communications management plan and sends every artefact on schedule through the agreed channels. This quarter, several stakeholders made decisions that contradicted information the PM had distributed weeks earlier, and one said they had never grasped a key constraint that was in those artefacts. The distribution records show everything went out on time and to the right people. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Reinforce the key messages by repeating them more often and across more channels, so they are harder for stakeholders to miss or forget.
- **B**: Convert the dense written artefacts into briefer, plainer formats and walk the most affected stakeholders through them, so the content is easier to absorb.
- **C**: Add feedback mechanisms to the communications approach to check whether messages are being received and understood, then adjust the approach based on what they reveal.
- **D**: Review and re-segment the stakeholder analysis, on the theory that the wrong information went to the wrong people, and re-target the artefacts accordingly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The plan is being measured on activity, artefacts sent on time to the right people, but not on effectiveness, whether messages were received and understood, and decisions that contradict distributed information point to a comprehension gap the PM currently has no way to see. (C) installs the missing feedback so the PM can detect and correct misunderstanding generally and verify any later fix actually works, which is the core of monitoring communications. (A) may help retention but assumes the failure is exposure and could add noise without ever confirming anything landed. (B) is a reasonable improvement to clarity, but it is a single untested remedy applied to a problem whose cause is not yet known, and without feedback the PM still cannot tell whether it worked. (D) is plausible if the records were wrong, but they show the right people received the artefacts on time, so re-segmenting treats a targeting fault the evidence does not support. The discriminator is that A, B and D each push one fix blind, while C is what tells the PM what is actually failing and whether the fix took.
