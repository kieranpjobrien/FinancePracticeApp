# Question

Forty staff across several time zones must individually acknowledge that they have read and understood a revised data-handling procedure before a regulatory deadline, and the organisation must be able to evidence each person's acknowledgement to an auditor. The PM has the procedure ready. Comprehension matters: the changes are nuanced and staff are likely to have questions. Which approach best fits this need?

## Options

- **A**: Run an interactive briefing on the changes to ensure understanding, then issue the procedure through a system that records each person's individual acknowledgement and chase non-responders before the deadline.
- **B**: Issue the procedure through a system that records each individual's acknowledgement, chase non-responders, and offer an optional clinic for anyone with questions.
- **C**: Hold an all-hands briefing on the changes, then have each attendee sign an attendance-and-acknowledgement sheet at the end as the record.
- **D**: Issue the procedure and acknowledgement request to all 40 individually, with a clearly stated deadline and a reply address for questions, and report the acknowledgements collected.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The hard requirement is an auditable, per-person acknowledgement by a deadline; the nuanced content is a real but secondary need that an optional clinic can serve without making attendance a precondition for everyone. (B) guarantees the recorded acknowledgement from all 40 and offers help to those who want it. (A) is the strongest rival and adds a comprehension step, but making an interactive briefing the gating first action across many time zones is heavier than the obligation requires and risks delay; acknowledgement, not attendance, is what must be evidenced. (C) conflates attending a briefing with having read and accepted the procedure, and an attendance sheet evidences presence rather than individual acknowledgement of the document. (D) collects acknowledgements but a free-form reply-and-report process gives a weaker, less consistent audit trail than a system designed to record each confirmation.
