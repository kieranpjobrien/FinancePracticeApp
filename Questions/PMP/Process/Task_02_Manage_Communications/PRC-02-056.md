# Question

A rumour is spreading through the delivery team that the project will be cancelled in the next quarter. The PM has no information confirming or denying this. Productivity has started visibly dipping. Three team members have asked the PM directly whether they should look for other roles. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassure the team that the project is continuing and any cancellation would have to go through formal governance processes first.
- **B**: Tell team members you have no information and advise them not to make career decisions based on unverified workplace rumours.
- **C**: Raise the rumour and its productivity impact with the sponsor, request clarity, and commit to communicating back to the team promptly.
- **D**: Run a team retrospective focusing on team morale and avoid the specific rumour until clearer information becomes available to share.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Information voids get filled by rumour; the PM's role is to close the void by getting facts and reporting back transparently. (C) acts on root cause. (A) reassurance without facts is dishonest and risks the PM's credibility. (B) dismissing concerns without seeking clarity ignores a real team-welfare signal. (D) avoiding the topic leaves the void open and is perceived as dodging.
