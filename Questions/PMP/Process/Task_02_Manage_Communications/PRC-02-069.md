# Question

A new member joins an agile delivery team and is profoundly deaf, communicating through written text and a sign-language interpreter. The team's core communications are a daily stand-up held by voice over a conference bridge and rapid back-and-forth in a voice channel during pairing. The new member is keen to contribute fully. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange for live captioning and interpreter access on the synchronous sessions and confirm with the member that the arrangements meet their needs.
- **B**: Move the team's primary communications to text-based channels so the new member can participate on equal terms with everyone else.
- **C**: Assign a buddy to summarise each voice discussion in writing for the new member immediately afterwards.
- **D**: Ask the new member how they would prefer the team to adapt its communications and proceed on that basis.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The first move when accommodating a team member's communication needs is to involve that person in deciding what works for them, rather than imposing a solution the PM assumes is best. (D) does this and is the correct starting point. (A) is a strong, likely-correct measure, but committing to a specific mechanism before asking the person risks providing support that does not fit how they actually work; it is what the conversation may well decide, not what precedes it. (B) unilaterally restructures the whole team's communication on the member's behalf without consulting them and may not even be their preference. (C) relegates the member to delayed second-hand summaries, excluding them from the live participation they have asked to share in. Asking first respects the individual and produces an accommodation that actually works.
