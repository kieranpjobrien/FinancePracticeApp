# Question

A PM has thirty minutes with the executive committee to brief a major programme at its halfway mark. The PM has prepared a detailed 40-slide deck covering every workstream. A peer PM advises that the exec committee rarely reads full decks and prefers narrative. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the full deck ready as reference material and present just the executive summary slide in detail to the committee during the session.
- **B**: Send the full deck as a pre-read and ask for confirmation that committee members have reviewed it before the meeting.
- **C**: Skip the deck entirely and deliver a verbal narrative only, relying on prepared answers for any specific questions the committee raises.
- **D**: Restructure the brief around three to five key decisions or risks the committee needs to engage with, with backup detail on demand.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Executive communications are decision-oriented and short-form; PMI-aligned practice structures the brief around what the audience needs to decide. (D) does that. (A) a summary slide still forces linear reading rather than discussion. (B) pre-reads are fine but don't address the brief structure itself. (C) narrative without structure can drift and leave key decisions unaddressed.
