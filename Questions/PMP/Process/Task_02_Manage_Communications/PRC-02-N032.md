# Question

An agile release train's leadership wants continuous visibility of progress and blockers without pulling teams into extra status meetings, and asks the Scrum Masters to lean on information radiators. Which choices use information radiators in the way intended? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A burnup chart on a shared wall and dashboard that updates as work completes, open to anyone at any time.
- **B**: A Kanban board showing every work item and its current state, refreshed in real time and visible to all.
- **C**: An impediment board displaying open blockers and how long each has been open, on view to everyone.
- **D**: A dashboard with the same metrics that any stakeholder can open, but whose detailed views require a login granted on request.
- **E**: A daily automated email digest of the latest burndown and blocker figures pushed to the leadership distribution list.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

An information radiator makes project information continuously and openly visible so people can self-inform at a glance without interrupting the team. (A), (B), and (C) are textbook radiators: progress, flow, and ageing impediments, all live and open to all. (D) looks like a radiator but gates the real detail behind access granted on request, which reintroduces a pull-with-permission step and breaks the "visible to everyone, instantly" property. (E) takes radiator data but converts it into a pushed, periodic message to a fixed list, the opposite of information that simply radiates to whoever looks. The discrimination is between information that is openly and continuously on display (A/B/C) and information that is gated or pushed on a schedule (D/E).
