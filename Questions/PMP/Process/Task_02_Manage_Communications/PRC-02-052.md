# Question

Two senior team members have started communicating exclusively via written channels after a disagreement. Decisions that should take minutes are taking days. Their written tone is correct but cold. Other team members are noticing and adapting the same style. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send both a direct message instructing them to communicate verbally until the relationship is repaired for the team's sake.
- **B**: Raise the pattern in the next team retrospective so the group names the problem and agrees shared norms together.
- **C**: Meet each of the two separately to understand the underlying disagreement before bringing them together for resolution.
- **D**: Ignore the pattern unless outputs visibly suffer, since interpersonal conflict can often resolve itself without active intervention.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Servant leadership on interpersonal conflict starts with understanding, privately, before facilitating resolution. (C) respects dignity and builds the picture needed to help. (A) commanding behavioural change skips the substance. (B) surfacing the disagreement in a group retro without consent can publicly shame the two involved. (D) ignoring the pattern is already failing — outputs are suffering and the team is absorbing the norm.
