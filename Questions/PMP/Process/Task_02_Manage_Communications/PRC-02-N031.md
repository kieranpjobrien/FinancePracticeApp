# Question

A PM inherits a hybrid programme whose communications management plan has drifted into a catch-all: it now duplicates content that is maintained, and kept current, in other artefacts, so when those artefacts change the plan silently goes stale and teams act on the wrong version. The PM wants to strip the plan back to what genuinely belongs in it and reference the rest. Reviewing the current contents below, which entries should the PM keep in the communications management plan itself rather than move out and link to? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A statement, per stakeholder group, of what information they need and why each communication is sent to them.
- **B**: The method, format, and frequency chosen for each communication, matched to its audience.
- **C**: The named owner accountable for producing each communication and the person authorised to release it.
- **D**: Each stakeholder's current versus desired engagement level and the moves planned to close the gap.
- **E**: The live, continuously updated status of every open risk and issue that stakeholders may want to read.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The discriminator is not what is communication-related, all five are, but what the communications management plan should hold versus merely reference, given that the plan goes stale whenever it copies data another artefact owns. (A), (B), and (C) define the communication approach itself, who needs what and why, how and when it is delivered, and who owns and releases it, and they have no other home, so keeping them in the plan is correct. (D) is real and adjacent, but current-versus-desired engagement and the moves to shift it are maintained in the stakeholder engagement plan; copying them here is exactly the duplication that drifts out of date, so they should be referenced, not held. (E) is live register content that changes constantly; the plan may point to where it lives but must not embed a status that will be wrong by the next update. The fine margin is between the stable approach the plan defines (A/B/C) and live or engagement data the plan should link to rather than duplicate (D/E).
