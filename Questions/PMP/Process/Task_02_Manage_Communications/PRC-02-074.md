# Question

Late in a hybrid energy-grid project, the PM confirms a hardware defect will delay a major milestone by six weeks, affecting the client, the regulator, two downstream contractors, and the internal steering group. The PM has an hour before a previously scheduled client call. Which actions should the PM take before communicating the delay outward? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the facts, the cause, the revised date, and the recovery options, so the message is accurate and offers a way forward.
- **B**: Identify which stakeholders need to hear it first and through what channel, sequencing so none learns of it second-hand inappropriately.
- **C**: Draft consistent core messaging so different audiences receive the same facts, tailored in emphasis but not contradictory.
- **D**: Wait until a fully validated recovery plan is approved so the delay is only ever communicated alongside a committed solution.
- **E**: Quietly inform the two contractors first and ask them to hold the information until the client has been told.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Communicating bad news well requires getting the facts straight, planning who hears it and in what order, and ensuring a consistent core message across audiences. (A) ensures accuracy and a forward path; (B) sequences disclosure so no key party is blindsided; (C) prevents the contradictions that erode trust when stakeholders compare notes. (D) sounds responsible but withholding a material, already-confirmed delay until a recovery plan is fully approved leaves stakeholders, including a regulator, uninformed of something they need now; the delay should be communicated promptly with options, even if the final plan follows. (E) asks one set of external parties to sit on information the client has not yet received, creating an unfair and unstable position; the right control is sequencing the PM owns (B), not asking contractors to keep a secret. The margin is between disciplined preparation (A/B/C) and either undue delay (D) or improper selective disclosure (E).
