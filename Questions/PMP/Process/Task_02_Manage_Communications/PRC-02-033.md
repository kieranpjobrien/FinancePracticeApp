# Question

A senior stakeholder consistently interrupts others in steering meetings, and quieter members have stopped contributing. Discussion quality is dropping. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reseat the meeting so the interrupting stakeholder is positioned where they can be more easily managed.
- **B**: Ask the steering chair to address the interruption pattern at the start of the next meeting in front of all attendees.
- **C**: Remove the stakeholder from the steering committee distribution list to reduce their attendance.
- **D**: Introduce explicit facilitation techniques (round-robin, hand signals, timeboxes), agree them as ground rules, and have a private chat with the stakeholder about the impact.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PMI response combines structural facilitation changes with private feedback to the individual — neither alone is enough. (D) does both. (A) is symbolic and unlikely to change behaviour. (B) embarrasses the stakeholder publicly and damages the relationship. (C) removes a legitimate steering committee member rather than addressing the behaviour.
