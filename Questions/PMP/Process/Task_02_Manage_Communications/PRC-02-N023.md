# Question

Midway through an agile delivery, business stakeholders attend the sprint review, stay quiet, then email the product owner long change-request lists after each release. The product owner spends days reconciling these against the backlog and is frustrated. When the Scrum Master asks the stakeholders why, they say the review "isn't the place to get into detail" and that they would rather see and react to working features than sit through a slide walkthrough. The product owner is open to changing how the review runs. What should the Scrum Master do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the product owner to run the review around a live walkthrough of the increment, drawing stakeholder reactions in the room and folding them straight into visible backlog prioritisation.
- **B**: Facilitate the review personally so that each stakeholder group is invited to give feedback on the increment before discussion opens up.
- **C**: Coach the product owner to capture the emailed requests directly into the backlog and prioritise them transparently with the stakeholders each cycle.
- **D**: Establish a short backlog refinement session with the stakeholders between reviews to work through their emerging requests.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stakeholders have named the cause: the review gives them nothing to engage with, so they default to email, and reconciling product feedback into the backlog is the product owner's accountability, not the Scrum Master's to run. (A) puts the fix where it belongs by coaching the product owner to make the review a live inspect-and-adapt of the increment and to channel the reactions into transparent prioritisation, which is exactly what the stakeholders said would draw them in. (B) makes the review interactive too, but it has the Scrum Master own a forum whose product conversation belongs to the product owner, and facilitating turn-taking does not by itself give stakeholders the working features they want to react to. (C) keeps the backlog honest but accepts email as the channel rather than repairing the review the stakeholders are abandoning. (D) adds a separate forum that can pull engagement even further from the review. The fine margin is between coaching the accountable product owner to make the review worth attending (A) and the Scrum Master managing the forum or the escaped feedback themselves (B/C/D).
