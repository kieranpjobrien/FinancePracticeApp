# Question

On an international project, members from one regional office rarely voice objections in large group calls but email well-founded concerns to the PM afterwards, so risks surface late. The PM has confirmed with the office's members that this reflects how they prefer to contribute rather than disengagement, and wants the concerns to arrive earlier. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a round-robin in calls in which every member in turn is invited to raise any concern, normalising input from everyone.
- **B**: Speak with the office's team lead and ask them to surface their team's concerns on the team's behalf during the larger calls.
- **C**: Invite written input on the key agenda topics ahead of each call through the office's preferred channels, so members can raise issues earlier in a medium that suits them.
- **D**: Hold smaller breakout discussions with that office's members where they are more likely to speak, and feed the points into the main forum afterwards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The members have confirmed their preferred way of contributing, so the most effective move adapts the communication approach to that style while pulling the timing earlier, rather than pressing them to perform in the format that is causing the delay. (C) does exactly this: pre-call written input on their own channels lets concerns land before decisions are taken, fixing the lateness at its source without asking anyone to act against their preference. (A) is a reasonable facilitation idea, but inviting individuals to speak in turn in the large call still puts them on the spot in the very setting they avoid, so it works against the grain of what the PM just confirmed. (B) can work but funnels everything through one relay and gives the members themselves no earlier, comfortable route. (D) helps but adds a parallel forum and a secondhand hand-off rather than enabling earlier direct contribution in the existing flow. The fine margin is between adapting the medium and timing so members contribute directly and sooner (C) and arranging others, or a turn-taking ritual, to extract their input (A/B/D).
