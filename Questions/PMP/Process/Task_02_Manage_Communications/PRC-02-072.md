# Question

During a predictive manufacturing project, the PM learns at 4 pm that a safety inspection scheduled for 8 am tomorrow has been brought forward by the regulator to 6 am, and three named supervisors must be on site early to prepare the line. The information is on the shared project calendar, which the supervisors check irregularly. What is the most appropriate way to communicate the change?

## Options

- **A**: Update the shared project calendar with the new time so the change is recorded in the agreed single source of truth.
- **B**: Contact the three supervisors directly by phone or message to confirm each has received and understood the new time.
- **C**: Post an announcement in the project's team channel so the whole project is aware of the rescheduled inspection.
- **D**: Email all stakeholders the revised inspection time and ask the three supervisors to acknowledge receipt.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The message is time-critical and must reach three specific people who do not reliably monitor passive channels, so it calls for direct push communication with confirmed receipt. (B) targets exactly those people and verifies they know. (A) records the change on a channel the supervisors check irregularly, so it may not be seen in time. (C) broadcasts to everyone but a busy supervisor may not notice a channel post before 6 am, and it lacks confirmation. (D) reaches the right people but email plus an acknowledgement request is too slow and uncertain for a same-evening, must-act-by-dawn change. When urgency and a small known audience coincide, direct contact with confirmation beats any broadcast or repository.
