# Question

A PM maintains an open, well-used project wiki where all stakeholders can see plans, status, and risks, which has greatly improved trust. The sponsor now shares, in confidence, that a possible reorganisation may cut one of the partner teams, and asks the PM to factor the risk into planning but to keep the reorganisation itself confidential for now. The risk materially affects the schedule. How should the PM handle communicating this?

## Options

- **A**: Reflect the schedule risk in planning using a neutral description of its impact, and keep the confidential reorganisation rationale out of the open channels.
- **B**: Add the risk to the open register with its real cause so the affected partner team is not blindsided by a change that concerns them.
- **C**: Keep the risk out of all project communications entirely until the sponsor authorises disclosure, to avoid any breach of the confidence.
- **D**: Raise with the sponsor that the open-information culture makes the confidence untenable and ask to be released from planning around it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM must honour a legitimate confidence while still managing a real schedule risk, and these are reconcilable by communicating the impact without the sensitive cause. (A) records and plans for the risk in neutral terms and keeps the protected rationale out of open view, satisfying both duties. (B) breaches the confidence by publishing the cause, and "they deserve to know" does not override the sponsor's legitimate hold on unconfirmed reorganisation news. (C) over-corrects by suppressing a material schedule risk entirely, leaving the project exposed and stakeholders planning on a false basis. (D) is an overreaction, the confidence does not actually prevent the PM from planning, as (A) demonstrates, so there is no need to escalate or withdraw.
