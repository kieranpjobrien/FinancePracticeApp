# Question

A newly distributed agile team has been sharing all of its feedback about how the team is working through written comments in a shared document. Tensions are building because comments are read as blunter than intended, and disagreements escalate in the margins rather than getting resolved. What should the Scrum Master change?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a regular synchronous retrospective where the team discusses how it is working, face to face by video.
- **B**: Introduce a guideline that written feedback must be phrased constructively and assume good intent.
- **C**: Have the Scrum Master review comments and mediate disagreements before they escalate in the document.
- **D**: Move the feedback document to a tool that supports threaded replies so disagreements are easier to follow.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Feedback about team dynamics is sensitive and relational, exactly the kind of communication that needs the tone, nuance, and real-time clarification of interactive dialogue, which written margins strip away. (A) restores that channel through a synchronous retrospective. (B) helps soften wording but cannot supply the missing tone or resolve a live disagreement in a static document. (C) inserts the Scrum Master as a permanent intermediary, which does not scale and keeps the team out of direct dialogue. (D) makes written arguments easier to follow but still keeps a charged conversation in a low-bandwidth medium where intent is misread. The point is matching a relational, easily misunderstood topic to interactive communication.
