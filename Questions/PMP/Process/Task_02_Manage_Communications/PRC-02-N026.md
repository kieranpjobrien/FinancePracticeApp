# Question

A distributed agile team spans Sydney, Berlin, and San Francisco. The single daily stand-up suits Sydney and Berlin, so San Francisco joins at 23:00 and members there have begun skipping it; they then spend the next day chasing what they missed. Stand-up content is currently only spoken. The team wants to keep one synchronous stand-up. What is the most effective improvement?

## Options

- **A**: Have every member post a brief written update to a shared channel before the stand-up, and use the call to discuss only what needs discussion.
- **B**: Record the stand-up each day so the San Francisco members can watch it first thing in their morning.
- **C**: Rotate the stand-up time on a fixed cycle so each region in turn takes the awkward slot and no one is always disadvantaged.
- **D**: Keep stand-up unchanged but assign a Berlin member to message the San Francisco members a summary of anything relevant to them afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The fix must leave a durable, low-effort record that someone who cannot attend can absorb quickly, without abandoning the synchronous touchpoint the team values. (A) does this: written-ahead updates create a scannable record and shorten the call, so absence costs little and the stand-up still happens live. (B) also creates a record, but a video forces San Francisco to replay a whole meeting to extract a few relevant items, which is slow and rarely sustained. (C) shares the inconvenience fairly yet still leaves one region impaired each day and does nothing to capture the spoken content. (D) depends on one person reliably judging and relaying what matters, recreating a single point of failure. The fine margin is between a self-serve written record (A) and a replayable but heavyweight recording (B).
