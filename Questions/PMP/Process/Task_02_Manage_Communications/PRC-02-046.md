# Question

A sponsor has started cc-ing their peer executives on every weekly status email, and several of those peers are now replying with conflicting direction. The PM's core delivery decisions are being debated in reply-all threads. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reply-all to each thread with the delivery decision and the supporting rationale, inviting peers to raise formal objections.
- **B**: Move status conversations to a regular standing meeting with named decision-makers, keeping email for distribution only.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to stop cc-ing their peers on status updates to prevent peer executives from offering conflicting direction.
- **D**: Escalate the cc-and-reply behaviour to the PMO and request a communications governance intervention for this specific programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects communications channels to be designed for the decisions they support. (B) separates decision forums from distribution and reduces miscommunication structurally. (A) invites reply-all debates on every decision, exactly the problem. (C) asks the sponsor to limit their own transparency without offering a better channel. (D) escalation without first redesigning the channel is premature.
