# Question

A PM is planning communications for a project with 10 internal stakeholders and is about to bring on a further 5 external stakeholders. By how much will the number of potential communication channels increase with the additional stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Increase of 60, taking total channels from 45 to 105 using the n(n−1)/2 channel formula.
- **B**: Increase of 45, taking total channels from 45 to 90 using doubled-stakeholder channel counting.
- **C**: Increase of 75, taking total channels from 45 to 120 using the n×(n−1) directed-pair formula.
- **D**: Increase of 50, taking total channels from 45 to 95 using the n(n+1)/2 triangular formula.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Channels = n(n−1)/2. With 10 stakeholders: 10×9/2 = 45. With 15 stakeholders: 15×14/2 = 105. Increase = 60. (A) applies the formula correctly. (B) assumes simple doubling, ignoring combinatorial growth. (C) uses n(n−1), which counts directed pairs rather than undirected channels. (D) substitutes the triangular formula n(n+1)/2, a common formula-confusion distractor.
