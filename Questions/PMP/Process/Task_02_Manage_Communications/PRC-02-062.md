# Question

A PM has discovered that a mistake made early in planning will likely cost the project $120,000 in rework. The sponsor is currently presenting positive results to the executive committee. The PM will see the sponsor in person tomorrow. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the cost impact with the sponsor privately tomorrow, with a clear proposed response and a draft revised forecast.
- **B**: Send a detailed written note to the sponsor tonight so they have the information before any further executive presentations.
- **C**: Wait for the normal monthly cycle and include the rework cost in the next formal status report to avoid blindsiding.
- **D**: Ask the programme office to brief the sponsor on the rework cost so the PM can focus on planning the recovery actions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Bad news belongs with the sponsor directly, promptly, with a proposed path — not via a third party or delayed cycle. (A) does exactly this with appropriate timing. (B) a written note risks misreading of tone on a material issue better covered in conversation. (C) waiting for the cycle on a material issue is a transparency failure. (D) routing bad news through the PMO offloads the relationship duty the PM holds.
