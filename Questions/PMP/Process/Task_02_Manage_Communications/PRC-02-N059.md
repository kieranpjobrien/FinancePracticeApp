# Question

An agile programme is moving stakeholders from weekly written status reports to information radiators and a self-service dashboard, to cut reporting overhead while improving transparency. The dashboard is live, accurate, and accessible. The PM wants the change to genuinely serve stakeholders, not merely retire the report. Which TWO considerations should most shape the transition? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm that each stakeholder group can interpret the radiators and dashboard for the specific decisions they must make, not just access them.
- **B**: Retain a lightweight push summary for stakeholders whose governance role requires defined information delivered to them on a set cadence.
- **C**: Add concise narrative commentary alongside the dashboard each cycle to explain what the numbers mean and what changed.
- **D**: Run the old written report and the new dashboard in parallel for several cycles before retiring the report, to de-risk the change.
- **E**: Brief stakeholders on how to read the radiators and where to find each item, so the self-service model is adopted rather than ignored.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The two considerations that most determine whether the change serves stakeholders are whether each group can actually use the artefacts for their decisions (A) and whether stakeholders whose role requires scheduled delivery still receive a push channel, since pure self-service silently fails an audience that must be pushed to (B). (C), (D), and (E) are all sound supporting tactics, narrative aids comprehension, parallel running de-risks, and onboarding lifts adoption, but each addresses the smoothness or polish of the rollout rather than the two structural questions of whether the new model fits every stakeholder's decision needs and delivery obligations. The distinction is fine: (E) in particular overlaps with (A), but (E) concerns mechanics of finding information while (A) concerns whether the information, once found, supports the decision, which is the deeper test of fitness.
