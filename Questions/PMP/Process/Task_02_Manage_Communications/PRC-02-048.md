# Question

A distributed agile team spans Sydney, Singapore and London. Sydney engineers complain the London architects dominate design meetings, while London architects say Sydney team members rarely speak up and later raise concerns in written channels. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Move design discussions to written asynchronous channels so every voice has equal time and decisions are documented in writing for review.
- **B**: Split meetings by region so each location designs independently and shares outcomes afterward through the written channel for visibility.
- **C**: Facilitate a team-norms conversation covering participation styles and introduce structured turn-taking techniques into every synchronous design meeting for the team.
- **D**: Appoint senior Sydney engineers as regional spokespeople so Sydney voices carry equal weight in synchronous design discussions going forward.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cross-cultural and distributed dynamics often reflect participation norms more than intent; PMI-aligned facilitation surfaces the pattern and shifts behaviour together. (C) addresses the root cause. (A) async-only removes rich interaction and hides rather than resolves the dynamic. (B) splitting the team sacrifices shared design for convenience. (D) spokespeople concentrate voice rather than distribute it and can entrench hierarchy.
