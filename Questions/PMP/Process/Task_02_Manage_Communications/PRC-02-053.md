# Question

A PM notices that on virtual meetings, 80% of the airtime is held by four people, while twelve others participate silently with cameras off. Decisions made in those meetings are often revisited afterwards because absent voices surface concerns later. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate cameras on for all virtual meetings so facial cues surface quiet dissent and keep participants visibly engaged throughout.
- **B**: Split each meeting into small breakout rooms with rotating facilitation so every attendee speaks at least once during sessions.
- **C**: Introduce a structured facilitation approach including pre-reads, explicit turn-taking, and silent written input before group discussion.
- **D**: Reduce meeting frequency and make most decisions asynchronously so those uncomfortable speaking have a quiet written channel.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI communications practice treats participation as design, not luck; structured facilitation widens the contribution surface. (C) does that coherently. (A) cameras don't solve airtime imbalance and can feel coercive. (B) breakouts without pre-reads or facilitation craft often replicate the same dynamics at small scale. (D) async alone loses the real-time reconciliation needed for complex decisions.
