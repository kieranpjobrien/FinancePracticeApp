# Question

You want to introduce continuous knowledge sharing into an agile delivery team, rather than waiting for project close. Team members push back, saying they have no time on top of feature delivery. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate weekly knowledge-share sessions and track attendance to ensure compliance across the whole delivery team.
- **B**: Defer the initiative until velocity has stabilised and the team has more capacity for non-delivery overhead activities.
- **C**: Embed lightweight knowledge moments into existing Scrum ceremonies, like a five-minute share at demos and a retro item.
- **D**: Bring in a dedicated technical writer to interview engineers and produce the supporting documentation on the team's behalf.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) respects the team's delivery focus by adding small increments to ceremonies they already run, which is the standard hybrid PMI/agile pattern. (A) creates resistance and tends to produce low-quality output. (B) defers indefinitely; agile teams rarely have a quiet patch. (D) outsources knowledge capture and does not transfer knowledge between team members.
