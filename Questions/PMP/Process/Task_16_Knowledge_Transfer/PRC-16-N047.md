# Question

A predictive ERP implementation is moving from the build phase, run by a system-integration partner, into a hypercare-and-operations phase run by the client's internal IT team. The internal team will own incident resolution from go-live. The integration partner has deep knowledge of the custom configurations and known workarounds. What should the project manager ensure is in place before the phase transition?

## Options

- **A**: A structured handover covering configuration documentation, known issues and workarounds, plus a defined period of joint working or shadowing between the partner and the internal team.
- **B**: A signed acceptance from the internal IT team confirming they accept responsibility for operations from go-live.
- **C**: A comprehensive runbook authored by the integration partner and delivered to the internal team on the transition date.
- **D**: A support contract extension with the integration partner so they remain on call for the first six months of operations.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Effective phase transition transfers both explicit artefacts and the tacit operational know-how the receiving team needs to run the system. (A) combines documentation of configurations and workarounds with overlap working, which is how the internal team builds real capability. (B) records accountability but transfers no knowledge, so the team owns problems it cannot yet solve. (C) hands over an explicit artefact alone; a runbook delivered cold rarely conveys the judgement behind workarounds. (D) buys a safety net but lets the internal team stay dependent rather than absorbing the knowledge, which defeats the point of the transition.
