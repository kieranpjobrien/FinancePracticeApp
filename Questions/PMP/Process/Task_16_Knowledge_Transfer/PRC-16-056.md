# Question

During handover review on a hybrid utilities platform, the PM finds the documentation library is large but roughly a third of it is out of date or inconsistent with current system behaviour. The go-live date is firm. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Hand over all documentation as-is and let operations update entries gradually as they encounter inaccuracies during normal support activities post-launch.
- **B**: Delay go-live until every document has been reviewed, updated, and verified against current system behaviour across the full documentation corpus.
- **C**: Prioritise critical path documentation for immediate correction, clearly flag uncertain content, and plan a structured remediation programme post-handover with owners.
- **D**: Replace the existing library wholesale with a new set of documents generated from scratch by the team during the remaining time before go-live.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects risk-based tailoring at handover: fix what matters most, be transparent about the rest, and plan for follow-up. (C) protects operations from dangerous gaps while respecting the go-live constraint. (A) exposes operations to incorrect procedures. (B) delays go-live to achieve perfection that isn't required. (D) discards working content and creates new risk through rushed rewrites.
