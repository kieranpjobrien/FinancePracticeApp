# Question

A PM is preparing transfer materials for a hybrid government platform. Stakeholders include operations engineers, call centre staff, policy officers, and executives. The PM has only limited time to produce the package. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Build tailored content for each audience covering the depth and format they need, starting from a shared evidence base of decisions.
- **B**: Produce one comprehensive document covering every topic in full detail and let each stakeholder group extract what is relevant to their context.
- **C**: Prioritise the executive audience first, since their endorsement will unlock adoption among the other stakeholder groups across the wider organisation.
- **D**: Produce a single short executive summary plus a link to the full project repository where every stakeholder can locate information themselves now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI insists communication be tailored to audience need, and a shared factual base prevents conflicting versions of truth. (A) respects both tailoring and consistency under the time constraint. (B) creates a document no audience will read effectively. (C) ignores operational groups whose uptake actually determines success. (D) offloads the tailoring work onto stakeholders who lack project context.
