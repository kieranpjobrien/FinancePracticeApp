# Question

A predictive managed services contract is ending and a new vendor is taking over the support function for a regulated insurance platform. The incumbent has limited incentive to cooperate fully. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the incumbent vendor to brief the new vendor directly, step back, and let the two organisations manage the handover between themselves.
- **B**: Offer a small incentive payment to the incumbent to encourage a smoother handover than would otherwise occur under the existing contract.
- **C**: Invoke the contract's transition clauses, define handover deliverables with acceptance criteria, and actively broker the transfer between vendors involved.
- **D**: Extend the incumbent contract for three more months so the new vendor has extra time to come up to speed before cutover.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects vendor transitions to be governed by contract clauses and actively managed by the PM rather than left to the suppliers to negotiate. (C) uses proper contractual authority with clear deliverables. (A) leaves the handover to a conflicted party. (B) pays extra for what the contract likely already obliges. (D) extends time without improving the handover mechanism.
