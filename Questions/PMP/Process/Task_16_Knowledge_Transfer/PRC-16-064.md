# Question

A Scrum programme has run 15 teams over two years and each team has held retrospectives capturing valuable lessons. Other parts of the organisation keep repeating mistakes the programme already learned from. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Publish every retrospective output to the organisation's shared folder so anyone who needs the lessons can search for them and apply them locally.
- **B**: Restrict retrospective content to the originating team since lessons depend heavily on local context and may mislead other teams if shared widely.
- **C**: Synthesise patterns across retrospectives, share them through communities of practice, and actively connect other teams with those who lived the lessons.
- **D**: Escalate the recurring mistakes to senior leadership so a mandate can be issued that teams review the programme's retrospective outputs before starting.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's organisational knowledge perspective emphasises synthesis and active dissemination through human networks, not passive publication. (C) bridges the gap between capture and adoption. (A) relies on search that rarely happens in practice. (B) protects lessons from the teams that most need them. (D) uses top-down mandates where peer sharing works better.
