# Question

You are handing a product over to a different team that will continue development. The receiving team is anxious about taking ownership. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide the full documentation pack and a single half-day briefing, then formally transfer responsibility to the receiving team.
- **B**: Plan a structured transition with an overlap period, graduated handover, contextual documentation, and post-handover escalation paths.
- **C**: Retain ownership of the product on the original team's books for another quarter to give the receiving team time to settle in first.
- **D**: Have the receiving team shadow the original team for one day during a typical sprint cycle to observe how the work is run.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) addresses both the knowledge gap and the confidence gap with a structured, graduated transition, which is the standard PMI handover pattern. (A) is too thin to leave the receiving team capable. (C) defers the transition rather than executing it well. (D) provides almost no exposure for a meaningful transfer.
