# Question

A PM is closing a long hybrid platform programme with many architectural decisions taken over three years. The incoming enhancement team includes engineers who were not present for those decisions. What should the PM transfer to help them?

## Options

- **A**: The final state of the architecture in diagrams and code, since new engineers should focus on the current system rather than historical trade-offs.
- **B**: A chronological archive of every meeting minute, email thread, and decision conversation from the three years of programme architecture work in full.
- **C**: A curated architecture decision record set capturing each major decision, its context, alternatives considered, rationale, and the consequences observed afterwards.
- **D**: A list of senior architects who led each decision so the incoming team can contact them individually whenever historical context is needed later.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI favours curated decision records that compress context into usable form for future decisions. (C) gives the incoming team the why, the alternatives, and the consequences, protecting them from repeating bad trade-offs. (A) discards the rationale that prevents bad changes. (B) buries signal in volume. (D) creates a dependency on individuals who may leave.
