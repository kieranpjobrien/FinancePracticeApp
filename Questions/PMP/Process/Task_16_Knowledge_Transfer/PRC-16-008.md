# Question

A specialist holding critical integration knowledge is being promoted in four weeks; her replacement starts six weeks from now, leaving a two-week gap. How should the project manager plan knowledge transfer?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the two-week gap and have the replacement learn primarily from the documentation the specialist produces before leaving, supplementing with peer support.
- **B**: Stand up a transfer plan covering documentation, interim knowledge holders, limited specialist availability during the gap, and structured replacement onboarding.
- **C**: Ask the specialist's new manager to delay her promotion until the replacement has started, completed onboarding, and confirmed competence in the role.
- **D**: Outsource integration support to an external vendor for the gap period and resume internal ownership once the replacement is up to speed on the platform.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The gap must be actively managed across documentation, interim coverage, residual access to the specialist, and structured onboarding for the new hire. (B) covers all of those. (A) accepts the risk without mitigation. (C) is often outside the PM's authority and may not even be possible. (D) is slow to set up and replaces internal capability with an external dependency.
