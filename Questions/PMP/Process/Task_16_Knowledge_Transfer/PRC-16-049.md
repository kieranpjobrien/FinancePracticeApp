# Question

The sole subject matter expert on a predictive engineering project has given four weeks' notice and holds critical tacit knowledge about a safety-certified subsystem. The sponsor expects full continuity after departure. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the expert to prepare comprehensive written documentation of everything they know across the remaining four weeks before their final working day.
- **B**: Offer a retention bonus or contract extension so the expert remains available until a replacement is fully trained on the subsystem.
- **C**: Launch a recruitment effort for an equally experienced replacement and assume the departing expert can provide a short overlap handover.
- **D**: Identify the highest-risk knowledge areas, pair successors with the expert on real work, and capture decisions, rationale, and failure scenarios progressively.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tacit knowledge transfers best through paired real work, and risk-based prioritisation ensures the most critical content is captured first. (D) targets what matters and uses the right technique. (A) produces volume but misses tacit signal. (B) buys time without reducing the knowledge concentration. (C) gambles on hiring and a thin overlap during a safety-sensitive transfer.
