# Question

Six weeks after a predictive CRM rollout went live, the operations team reports that several configuration choices made during the project are causing daily friction, and says it would have pushed back had it understood the reasoning before go-live. Most of the project team has rolled off, but the lead and two members who made those choices are still reachable for a short while. Another rollout to a second business unit starts next quarter. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Convene a post-implementation review with the remaining project members and the operations team, transferring the rationale behind the configuration and capturing what the next rollout should do differently.
- **B**: Have the operations team raise the friction points as change requests so the configuration can be reworked to suit daily use.
- **C**: Ask the project lead to write up the reasoning behind the contested configuration choices and circulate it to operations.
- **D**: Log a lessons-learned entry that operations must be engaged before go-live on the next rollout, and brief the next project manager.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Two things are both live and both time-boxed by the project members' availability: operations needs the missing rationale now, and the organisation needs to carry the lesson into next quarter's rollout. (A) does both in one forum while the people who hold the reasoning are still reachable — the only option that closes the present gap and the recurrence risk together. (B) fixes today's friction operationally but skips the rationale transfer, so operations reworks blind and the next rollout repeats the pattern. (C) moves the reasoning across but one-way and on paper, with no dialogue to reach shared understanding and nothing captured for next time. (D) secures the forward lesson yet leaves operations still living with configuration it does not understand. Doing both at once beats any single partial step, especially while the window on the departing members is open.
