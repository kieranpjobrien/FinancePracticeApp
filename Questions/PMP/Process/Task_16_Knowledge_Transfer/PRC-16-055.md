# Question

A PM is planning knowledge transfer for a predictive data platform project. Some knowledge is procedural and well-understood; other knowledge is expert judgement built over years of incident response. What transfer approach best fits each?

## Options

- **A**: Use written documentation for procedural content and also for judgement content since all knowledge must ultimately be in writing.
- **B**: Document procedural knowledge in runbooks and transfer judgement knowledge through paired work, simulations, and mentorship over an overlap period.
- **C**: Have each expert record a single long video covering everything they know about the platform so successors can watch during onboarding.
- **D**: Transfer only procedural knowledge at handover and accept that judgement knowledge cannot be transferred, retaining retrieval contracts for emergencies.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes explicit knowledge, which transfers via documents, from tacit knowledge, which requires socialisation and practice. (B) applies the right medium to each type. (A) treats all knowledge as explicit and fails on judgement. (C) creates unusable reference material nobody rewatches in context. (D) gives up on transfer that is achievable with the right techniques.
