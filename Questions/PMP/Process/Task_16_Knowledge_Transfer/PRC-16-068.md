# Question

A predictive medical device project is closing and must hand over to a post-market surveillance team. Regulators expect complete traceability from requirements through design decisions to testing evidence. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Transfer the current design and test artefacts and let the surveillance team reconstruct the decision trail themselves from the delivered engineering documentation package.
- **B**: Ensure the handover pack contains the traceability chain with decision records, test evidence, and rationale linked to the regulatory requirements.
- **C**: Hand over summary documentation suitable for routine surveillance and defer full traceability artefacts for later retrieval during specific regulatory inspection that arises.
- **D**: Archive the full project documentation in the corporate records system and provide the surveillance team with retrieval instructions they can use as needed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulated handovers must transfer complete, linked traceability because the receiving team cannot reconstruct lost rationale during an inspection. (B) meets the regulatory bar and equips surveillance to operate. (A) shifts reconstruction to a team that lacks the context. (C) exposes the organisation to inspection gaps. (D) buries traceability behind retrieval friction that fails under inspection timelines.
