# Question

A project manager is beginning to plan a predictive office-relocation project and wants the plan to reflect what the organisation already knows from the several relocations it has run before, so the same problems are not repeated. Which source should the PM draw on first?

## Options

- **A**: The lessons-learned records and historical files from the organisation's previous relocation projects.
- **B**: The organisation's project management methodology and relocation-planning templates.
- **C**: Interviews with the facilities and business managers who will be affected by this move.
- **D**: A benchmark of how comparable organisations in the industry have run their relocations.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The discriminator is which source most directly carries *this organisation's own past experience of relocations* — exactly what the PM wants to reuse — and each option is a legitimate planning input. (A) lessons-learned and historical files are the organisational memory of what went wrong and right on prior moves, the direct route to not repeating mistakes. (B) methodology and templates capture how the organisation runs projects in general and give useful structure, but they encode process, not the specific pitfalls of past relocations. (C) stakeholder interviews surface current needs and constraints and are valuable, yet they gather present requirements rather than the record of earlier outcomes. (D) external benchmarking brings in others' practice, but it is not this organisation's own history and is a second step after mining what is already known internally. The fine margin is internal lessons (A) versus internal process assets (B): both are organisational, but only (A) holds the experience of previous relocations.
