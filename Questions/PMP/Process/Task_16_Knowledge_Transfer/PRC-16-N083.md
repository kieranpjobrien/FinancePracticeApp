# Question

A new project manager takes over a long-running predictive hospital-IT rollout after the previous PM left at short notice, with no handover meeting possible. The previous PM kept a full set of project documents — charter, baselined plans, registers, logs, and reports — all reasonably up to date. The new PM has one day before chairing the next steering committee and needs to walk in able to speak to where the project actually stands. Which of the documents should the new PM work through first?

## Options

- **A**: The project management plan and its baselines, to understand the approved scope, schedule, and cost the project is being measured against.
- **B**: The issue log and risk register alongside the two most recent status reports, to see what is currently open, being watched, and trending.
- **C**: The project charter and business case, to ground the project's objectives and success criteria before discussing progress.
- **D**: The stakeholder register and communications plan, to know who the steering committee members are and what they expect to hear.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Every option is a legitimate project document an incoming PM will eventually need; the discriminator is which one most directly conveys the *current state* the PM must speak to at a steering committee tomorrow. (B) is the live picture — open issues, watched risks, and recent trend — which is exactly what "where the project stands" means and what a steering committee interrogates. (A) gives the approved targets but not where reality sits against them today, so on its own it cannot tell the PM what is going wrong now. (C) explains why the project exists and how success is judged — valuable orientation, but it is background, not current status. (D) is genuinely useful for managing the meeting and knowing the audience, but it tells the PM about people, not about the state of the work; it supports the conversation rather than supplying its substance. The fine margin is plan-versus-baselines (A) against the live logs (B): both are about "the work", but only (B) reflects what is happening right now.
