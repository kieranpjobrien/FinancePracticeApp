# Question

Midway through an agile product development effort, the product owner who built the relationships with regulators and held the deep rationale for backlog prioritisation accepts a role at another company and gives two weeks' notice. A replacement product owner has been identified internally but has no history with this product or its stakeholders. The team is mid-release and cannot pause. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange for the outgoing and incoming product owners to work jointly through the current backlog and the key stakeholder relationships during the notice period, with the team observing where useful.
- **B**: Ask the outgoing product owner to record the prioritisation rationale for the top backlog items and document the stakeholder map before departure.
- **C**: Have the incoming product owner shadow the next two sprint reviews to learn the product before formally taking ownership.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to request that the outgoing product owner's departure be delayed until the release ships.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The scarce resource is the two-week overlap, and the highest-value knowledge — prioritisation rationale and stakeholder relationships — is relational and tacit, so direct joint working transfers it best. (A) uses the overlap deliberately to hand over both the backlog logic and the relationships while the outgoing PO is still accountable. (B) captures some explicit artefacts but loses the relationship transfer and the live reasoning that comes from working real decisions together. (C) is too slow — shadowing two sprint reviews stretches past the notice period, leaving a gap. (D) attempts to override a personal career decision the PM cannot control and delays the inevitable transfer rather than executing it well.
