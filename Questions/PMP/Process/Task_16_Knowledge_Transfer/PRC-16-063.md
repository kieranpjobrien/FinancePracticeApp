# Question

A hybrid insurance project has produced excellent materials in multiple repositories: a team wiki, a shared drive, and a ticketing system. Operations reports difficulty finding the right information when incidents occur. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Archive the older repositories and consolidate future content into a single chosen repository, accepting that historical material may be harder to retrieve.
- **B**: Curate a clear index into the operational knowledge base, cross-link related artefacts, and train operations on where to locate each content class.
- **C**: Leave the repositories as they are and add a mandatory central search log so operations can request help from the project team as needed.
- **D**: Migrate everything into a new knowledge management platform that the operations team uses routinely across their existing support tickets and workflows.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI values knowledge accessibility as much as knowledge existence, and curation usually beats migration. (B) improves findability without disrupting proven repositories. (A) loses historical context in a one-way archive. (C) makes operations dependent on the project team that is leaving. (D) risks a migration project inside a closure window.
