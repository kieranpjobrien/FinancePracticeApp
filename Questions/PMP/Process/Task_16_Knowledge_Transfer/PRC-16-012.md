# Question

Across multiple recent projects, lessons-learned sessions consistently surface the same recurring issues (scope creep, unclear requirements, resource thrash). New projects keep hitting the same problems. What is most likely failing?

## Options

- **A**: The format of the lessons-learned sessions is too informal to produce useful records for future reference.
- **B**: The wrong audience attends the sessions; the people who could prevent the issues are not in the room for them.
- **C**: The sessions should be discontinued because they clearly are not changing outcomes on subsequent projects.
- **D**: Capture is working, but the organisation lacks a mechanism to apply lessons during initiation and planning of the next project.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

This is a classic organisational learning failure: lessons are captured but never consulted when new projects start. (D) names that gap. (A) blames the format when the data is being captured. (B) may be a contributor but does not explain why already-captured lessons go unused. (C) abandons the upstream practice rather than fixing the downstream application.
