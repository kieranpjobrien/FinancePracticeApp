# Question

Your project has used a novel technology and the team has built deep expertise through trial and error. The organisation plans more projects on the same stack. How should this expertise be captured for organisational benefit?

## Options

- **A**: Document the technical implementation in detail and rely on future project teams to read the documentation thoroughly before starting work.
- **B**: Plan to staff every future project with at least one member of the original team to carry the accumulated knowledge with them.
- **C**: Assume future teams will follow a similar trial-and-error path and benefit from the same learning experience that this team had.
- **D**: Build layered artefacts: documentation, pitfalls and patterns, ADRs, training material, and an advisor pool from the original team.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) captures both explicit and tacit knowledge in forms suited to each, which is the right answer for organisational scale-up. (A) captures only the explicit layer. (B) is unsustainable as the technology spreads. (C) wastes the organisation's investment in learning.
