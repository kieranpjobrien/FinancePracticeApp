# Question

A hybrid programme runs across four time zones with barely an hour of shared working time a day. Knowledge lodges with whoever is online when a question lands, decisions taken in one region go unnoticed in another, and the corridor conversations co-located teams lean on do not happen. The project manager has one hour of overlap to spend and wants the changes that most durably improve knowledge flow. Which measures should the project manager adopt? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Stand up a shared, searchable record of decisions and their rationale that anyone can consult regardless of time zone.
- **B**: Make written, asynchronous updates the default for sharing progress and context, rather than relying on live conversation.
- **C**: Record key meetings and walkthroughs so members in other zones can take them in later.
- **D**: Spend the daily overlap hour on a single all-hands sync so the whole programme hears the same thing at once.
- **E**: Have each region nominate a liaison who gathers cross-region questions and chases down the answers for their colleagues.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

With almost no shared time, durable flow depends on knowledge that persists and can be pulled asynchronously, not on being in the room. (A), (B) and (C) each replace lost co-located sharing with something that survives the time gap — findable decisions, async-by-default updates, and replayable sessions — so knowledge no longer hinges on who was online. (D) looks reasonable but spends the one scarce overlap hour broadcasting, which suits some zones and excludes others, and a daily sync still leaves the async gaps untouched between meetings; the overlap is better saved for genuine real-time collaboration. (E) improves on knowledge being stuck with individuals, yet it routes flow through a few people who become bottlenecks and single points of failure, and it patches the symptom rather than making the knowledge itself directly accessible. Persistent, pull-based channels beat consuming the overlap or relaying through intermediaries.
