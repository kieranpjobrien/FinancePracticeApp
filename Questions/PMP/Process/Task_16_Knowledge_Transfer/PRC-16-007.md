# Question

Which statement best captures the difference between tacit and explicit knowledge?

## Options

- **A**: Tacit knowledge is owned by individuals and explicit knowledge is owned by the organisation, regardless of how it was created.
- **B**: Tacit knowledge is personal, experience-based, and hard to articulate; explicit knowledge is codified and can be shared through documents and procedures.
- **C**: Explicit knowledge belongs to experts; tacit knowledge belongs to beginners and degrades as they gain experience.
- **D**: Tacit and explicit knowledge are functionally interchangeable for project continuity purposes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the standard PMI/knowledge-management distinction. (A) confuses ownership with type. (C) reverses the relationship; experts hold significant tacit knowledge. (D) collapses the distinction the discipline depends on.
