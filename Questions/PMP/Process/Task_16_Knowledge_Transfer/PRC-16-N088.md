# Question

In an agile fintech team, the only person who fully understands the fraud-scoring model is a data scientist who has just given six weeks' notice. No successor has been identified and recruitment will take longer than six weeks. The model is business-critical and changes regularly as fraud patterns shift. The project manager has the data scientist's time for six weeks and a modest budget. Which TWO actions should the project manager prioritise during the notice period? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Nominate one or two existing team members to take on the model and have the data scientist start transferring the work to them now through joint hands-on sessions.
- **B**: Negotiate a defined paid on-call advisory arrangement with the data scientist for a fixed period after departure to cover questions the team cannot yet answer.
- **C**: Have the data scientist write thorough documentation of the model — its logic, assumptions, and tuning decisions — to anchor the transfer and serve the eventual hire.
- **D**: Bring forward recruitment and have the data scientist help define the role profile and screen candidates so the replacement is the right fit.
- **E**: Ask the data scientist to record walkthrough videos explaining how the model works for the team to consult after she leaves.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The discriminator is that the capability is critical, uniquely held, and the source disappears in six weeks with no replacement arriving in time — so the priorities are getting the knowledge into people who will actually be present and securing a bridge for what cannot fully transfer. (A) starts hands-on transfer to existing members immediately, the action that most directly builds in-house capability while the source is available — the irreplaceable window. (B) secures a defined post-departure advisory arrangement, the realistic safety net for residual gaps that six weeks cannot close. (C), (D), and (E) are all legitimate and worth doing, which is what makes this discriminate: documentation and recorded walkthroughs (C, E) preserve the explicit layer but leave it passive, and with the model changing regularly they cannot convey the live reasoning the way joint work does; bringing recruitment forward (D) is sensible but the hire still will not be productive within the window, so it does not protect continuity in the gap. The fine margin is choosing the two that maintain capability through the gap (A, B) over the artefacts and hiring activity that help mainly once someone is there to use them.
