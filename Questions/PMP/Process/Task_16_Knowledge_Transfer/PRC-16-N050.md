# Question

A project manager faces four distinct continuity situations on a hybrid programme. For each, choose the knowledge transfer method that fits it best. Each method is used once.

## Items

- **1**: An architect leaves in two weeks; her named successor must be able to maintain the design and, crucially, understand why its trade-offs were made.
- **2**: New joiners keep asking colleagues the same setup questions, and the answers given vary because nobody owns the canonical version.
- **3**: The same integration mistake has now been hit by three of eight separate agile teams, each unaware the others had already solved it.
- **4**: A capable junior analyst can follow procedures but consistently misjudges the calls that need seasoned domain feel, and there is a year to close that gap.

## Matches

- **A**: Pairing and recorded walkthroughs focused on reasoning
- **B**: A maintained repository with owned, reviewed content
- **C**: A community of practice across teams
- **D**: An ongoing mentoring relationship

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

The give-away each situation hides is the *shape* of the gap, which dictates the method. (1) is perishable, person-specific reasoning that one successor must absorb fast — pairing and recorded walkthroughs move that best (A). (2) is stable, reusable procedure whose real problem is the lack of a single owned source of truth — a maintained repository fixes it (B); pairing would not stop the answers diverging. (3) is the same lesson failing to travel *sideways* between peer teams, which is precisely what a community of practice exists to do (C). (4) is slow growth of judgement over a year, the long-horizon relationship that mentoring provides (D). The traps are mapping (1) to a repository (rationale rarely survives the write-up under time pressure) or (4) to walkthroughs (a one-off session cannot build judgement that needs months).
