# Question

A hybrid banking project has delivered training, runbooks, and a two-week overlap period to operations. The PM is about to sign the formal handover but wants evidence the transfer actually worked. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run a readiness assessment with scenario-based exercises, sample real work performed solo by operations, and confirmation from operations leadership that they accept ownership.
- **B**: Review the training attendance records, runbook acknowledgement signatures, and overlap period notes to confirm that every planned activity was completed.
- **C**: Collect a signed handover acceptance form from the operations manager stating the materials were delivered and the training sessions were completed as scheduled.
- **D**: Extend the overlap by a further week to give operations additional time regardless of their current readiness, then sign the handover afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects verification of outcomes, not of inputs: evidence that the receiving team can actually operate independently. (A) tests capability through practice and secures leadership acceptance. (B) confirms activities happened but not that learning landed. (C) gathers a signature that certifies delivery, not readiness. (D) adds time without testing whether time is the missing ingredient.
