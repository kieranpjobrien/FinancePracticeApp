# Question

On a hybrid defence logistics project, the PM is designing knowledge transfer to operations. Some material contains information classified above the clearance of certain operations staff who still need to run the system. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Transfer all materials to operations and trust receivers not to read beyond their clearance level during day-to-day support activities.
- **B**: Remove all classified content from transfer artefacts so everyone can access everything and no clearance disputes arise during later support operations.
- **C**: Escalate the classification mismatch to the sponsor and delay handover until all operations staff hold equivalent clearances across the platform environment.
- **D**: Segment materials by classification, transfer each only to appropriately cleared recipients, and brief the security officer on the tailored handover arrangement.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats compliance as non-negotiable and tailors artefact distribution to authorisation. (D) honours the classification regime while enabling operations to work. (A) breaches need-to-know principles. (B) strips essential content, impairing operations. (C) blocks handover over a mismatch that a proper segmentation resolves.
