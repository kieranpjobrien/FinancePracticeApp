# Question

A Scrum team of 11 developers is transferring a trading platform to an offshore support organisation across a six-hour time zone gap. The offshore team prefers written artefacts; the Scrum team prefers conversations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Insist the Scrum team convert all knowledge to written form before transfer to match the offshore team's documented preference for reference material.
- **B**: Design a blended transfer combining written artefacts, recorded walkthroughs, paired sessions at overlapping hours, and a defined support escalation path.
- **C**: Require the offshore team to adopt Scrum ceremonies so knowledge transfer mirrors the originating team's established collaborative working rhythm.
- **D**: Delay transfer until both teams agree on a single shared communication approach that works for every participant across both locations.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's knowledge transfer guidance tailors media to audience needs and combines explicit and tacit channels. (B) respects both preferences and covers handover, reinforcement, and safety net. (A) imposes one team's preference on the other. (C) requires a methodology change the offshore team has not agreed to. (D) delays transfer to chase consensus that is not required.
