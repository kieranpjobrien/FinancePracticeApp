# Question

An organisation runs eight Scrum teams building a shared insurance platform. Each team holds its own sprint retrospective and logs improvements locally, but the same defects and integration problems keep recurring across teams. A retrospective action that one team solved months ago resurfaces as a fresh problem on another. The programme is predictive at the portfolio level and agile at the team level. What is the best way to address this?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a regular cross-team forum (such as a scrum-of-scrums retrospective or community of practice) where teams share retrospective findings and proven improvements.
- **B**: Require each team to submit its retrospective actions to the programme manager, who consolidates them into a central lessons-learned register.
- **C**: Standardise the retrospective format across all eight teams so findings are recorded consistently and can be compared.
- **D**: Add a recurring agenda item to each team's retrospective asking whether other teams have faced the same issue.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The failure is that knowledge stays trapped inside individual teams and does not flow horizontally, so a mechanism for inter-team exchange is needed. (A) creates that channel directly, letting teams pull proven solutions rather than re-solving problems. (B) centralises collection but a register that the programme manager owns is a push artefact teams rarely read; it does not create the peer exchange that actually moves tacit fixes. (C) improves consistency of records but consistent records that nobody shares still leave the silo intact. (D) relies on teams knowing what other teams have encountered, which is precisely the information they lack.
