# Question

A predictive marketing-automation product is being handed to a different internal team that will operate and enhance it. The two specialists who built the recommendation engine leave for new assignments in nine days, and the receiving team has not yet been named beyond its manager. The project manager has that nine-day window and a closure deadline four weeks out. Which actions should the project manager prioritise within the nine days, before the specialists leave? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Have the departing specialists document the open defects and the workarounds currently in use, including the ones they have been carrying in their heads.
- **B**: Schedule paired walkthroughs of the recommendation engine between the specialists and whichever receiving-team members the manager can free up now.
- **C**: Have the specialists record the rationale behind the contested configuration choices that future enhancements will most likely revisit.
- **D**: Run the full lessons-learned workshop and publish it to the organisational repository before the specialists depart.
- **E**: Ask the specialists to build a complete operations runbook covering every routine and edge case before they go.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The discriminator is the nine-day window: prioritise what is lost when the specialists walk out the door over what can be done afterwards. (A), (B) and (C) all capture knowledge that exists only in the specialists' heads — undocumented defects and workarounds, tacit understanding of the engine, and the reasoning behind decisions enhancers will reopen — and each can be partly done even before the receiving team is fully staffed. (D) is genuine closure work, but the lessons-learned workshop does not depend on these two individuals and fits the four-week window; spending scarce overlap days on it trades away irreplaceable access. (E) is the trap: a complete runbook is desirable but cannot realistically be authored in nine days, and chasing exhaustive coverage burns the window that should go to the highest-risk, head-only knowledge — runbook gaps can be filled later from records, lost tacit knowledge cannot.
