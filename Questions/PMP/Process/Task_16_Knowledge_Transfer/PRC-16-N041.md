# Question

On a predictive grid-modernisation programme, the lead instrumentation engineer who designed the legacy SCADA integration is rolling off in three weeks to join another division. A named successor has been identified and will own the integration, which continues for another nine months after she leaves. The PM has a three-week window of her time and must decide how to use it. What should the project manager prioritise first?

## Options

- **A**: Have her work alongside the successor on live integration tasks, talking through the decisions as they go, with the sessions recorded.
- **B**: Have her produce a comprehensive written handover covering the integration's configuration and the rationale behind its key trade-offs before her last day.
- **C**: Have her run a series of group briefings to the whole integration team so the knowledge is spread across several people rather than one successor.
- **D**: Commission an independent review of the integration with her support, so the design is validated and gaps are documented while she is still available.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The give-away the stem withholds is which kind of knowledge is most at risk: the configuration is already captured in accurate design documents, so the scarce, perishable asset is her undocumented *reasoning* — and reasoning of that depth transfers far better through working real tasks together than through any artefact. (A) puts the limited window into hands-on pairing with the person who must own the work, capturing the thinking in context. (B) tries to convert deep tacit rationale into prose under time pressure, where it tends not to survive the conversion, and much of the explicit content already exists. (C) spreads exposure thinly across a group rather than building real depth in the one person accountable for the next nine months, and group briefings rarely convey design reasoning. (D) produces validation and a gap list but does not transfer the operating knowledge the successor needs to maintain and extend the integration.
