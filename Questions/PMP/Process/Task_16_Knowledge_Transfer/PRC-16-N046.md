# Question

A project manager is drafting the knowledge management approach for a hybrid satellite-payload project that will run across three vendors and two countries over four years, with predictable specialist turnover. The PM wants an approach that safeguards both explicit and tacit knowledge and stays workable across independent vendor organisations. Which THREE should the approach include? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Mechanisms for tacit knowledge sharing such as pairing, mentoring, and communities of practice across the vendor teams.
- **B**: A defined repository and taxonomy for explicit artefacts, with ownership and a review cadence so content stays current.
- **C**: Identification of which knowledge is critical to continuity and most at risk from the anticipated turnover.
- **D**: A contractual clause requiring each vendor to surrender full copies of all internal working knowledge to the programme on demand.
- **E**: A single comprehensive knowledge-capture exercise at the start, on the basis that documenting everything up front removes the need for ongoing transfer.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The give-away the question hides is that a good approach plans for *both* knowledge types, targets effort by continuity risk, and must work across organisations the PM does not control. (A) supplies the tacit-sharing channels essential across distributed vendors; (B) gives explicit artefacts a trusted home with ownership and review; (C) is the risk-based focus that says where to invest first. (D) is the trap — it sounds like prudent continuity insurance, but vendors will not (and often legally cannot) hand over all internal working knowledge, and much of the highest-value tacit knowledge cannot be "surrendered" as artefacts anyway. (E) misunderstands knowledge transfer as a one-off documentation event; over a four-year run with turnover, knowledge keeps being created and people keep leaving, so transfer has to be continuous.
