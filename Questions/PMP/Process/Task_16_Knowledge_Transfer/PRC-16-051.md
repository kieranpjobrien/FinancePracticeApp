# Question

A Scrum team of nine is disbanding at project close. Members return to different functional departments across the organisation. The product they built will continue to be enhanced by a separate team. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct a single farewell retrospective and let the departing team members naturally share insights with their new functional departments informally.
- **B**: Curate product-specific knowledge for the incoming team and, separately, capture transferable lessons for functional areas where former members are returning.
- **C**: Assign one departing member to stay embedded with the new team for three months so continuity of context is maintained during early enhancement.
- **D**: Rely on the existing product documentation for the incoming team and focus closure effort on the disbanding team's individual career outcomes instead.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI recognises that different audiences need different knowledge: the incoming team needs product continuity; the wider organisation benefits from transferable lessons. (B) serves both. (A) leaves transfer to chance conversations. (C) creates dependency on one person and delays the new team's ownership. (D) ignores the significant tacit knowledge the team holds beyond documents.
