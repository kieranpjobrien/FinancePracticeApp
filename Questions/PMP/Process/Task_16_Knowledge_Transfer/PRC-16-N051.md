# Question

A project manager is initiating a wastewater treatment upgrade that closely resembles three projects the organisation has delivered before. The PM wants to avoid repeating mistakes those earlier projects made. The organisation maintains a lessons-learned repository and historical project records. What should the PM do at the outset?

## Options

- **A**: Review the lessons learned and historical records from the comparable projects and incorporate the relevant insights into the planning of this project.
- **B**: Invite the project managers of the three earlier projects to a kickoff meeting to share their experiences with the new team.
- **C**: Schedule the first lessons-learned session for the end of this project's initiation phase to start building a record early.
- **D**: Benchmark the new project's planned cost and schedule against the actuals achieved by the three earlier projects.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Lessons learned and historical records are organisational process assets meant to inform new projects, and the start of planning is exactly when to draw on them. (A) puts prior knowledge to work where it changes outcomes. (B) is a useful supplement but relies on individuals' availability and memory rather than the captured record, and it is narrower than reviewing the documented lessons. (C) generates new knowledge but does nothing to reuse what already exists, which is the PM's stated aim. (D) is a worthwhile estimating check but addresses only cost and schedule, not the broader mistakes the lessons repository would reveal.
