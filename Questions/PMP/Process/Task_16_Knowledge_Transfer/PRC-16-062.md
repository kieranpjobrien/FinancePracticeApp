# Question

A predictive integration project is transferring systems knowledge from an acquired company to the acquiring organisation's support function. The acquired team feels displaced and is passively cooperating. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the displacement, involve the acquired team in co-designing the transfer, and recognise their expertise explicitly in the handover process.
- **B**: Use the contractual retention clauses to require full cooperation and track deliverables against those obligations across the remaining transfer period carefully.
- **C**: Bring in external consultants to extract the knowledge independently so the project does not depend on the acquired team's discretionary effort.
- **D**: Accept the passive cooperation as inevitable in mergers and rely on the documentation that has already been produced to carry the handover.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's stakeholder engagement approach addresses the emotional and identity dimensions of transfer, especially in mergers, because engagement cannot be compelled. (A) restores agency and unlocks tacit knowledge. (B) coerces compliance but not genuine sharing. (C) discards the richest knowledge source. (D) accepts a risk that has known interventions.
