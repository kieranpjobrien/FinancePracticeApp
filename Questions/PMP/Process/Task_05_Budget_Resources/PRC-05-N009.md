# Question

A predictive infrastructure project reports BAC = $1,000,000. Four monthly snapshots are shown below. For each snapshot, match the cost and schedule figures to the conclusion they jointly support.

## Items

- **1**: EV = $300,000, AC = $300,000, PV = $360,000
- **2**: EV = $400,000, AC = $360,000, PV = $360,000
- **3**: EV = $250,000, AC = $300,000, PV = $310,000
- **4**: EV = $500,000, AC = $550,000, PV = $480,000

## Matches

- **A**: On budget for the work done, but behind schedule
- **B**: Over budget, yet ahead of schedule
- **C**: Over budget and behind schedule
- **D**: Under budget and ahead of schedule

## Answer

1-A, 2-D, 3-C, 4-B

## Explanation

Snapshot 1: CV = EV − AC = 0 (on budget) and SV = EV − PV = −60,000 (behind schedule) → A. Snapshot 2: CV = +40,000 (under budget) and SV = +40,000 (ahead of schedule) → D. Snapshot 3: CV = 250,000 − 300,000 = −50,000 (over budget) and SV = 250,000 − 310,000 = −60,000 (behind schedule) → C. Snapshot 4: CV = 500,000 − 550,000 = −50,000 (over budget) and SV = 500,000 − 480,000 = +20,000 (ahead of schedule) → B. The discrimination point is that cost (CV = EV − AC) and schedule (SV = EV − PV) are independent signals: AC alone never tells you schedule, and PV alone never tells you cost — snapshots 3 and 4 have the same cost variance but opposite schedule positions.
