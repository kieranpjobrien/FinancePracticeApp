# Question

During execution, the PM discovers the approved budget doesn't include estimated cost for a minor scope item that is essential to delivery. The gap is 3% of baseline. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Absorb the cost quietly using contingency reserve without changing the baseline.
- **B**: Notify the sponsor informally and carry on without paperwork.
- **C**: Cut scope to match the available budget.
- **D**: Raise a change request through the change control board with cost impact and rationale.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Even a 3% gap affects the baseline and must go through change control. (D) is the disciplined path. (A) using contingency reserve for previously-unplanned scope — not a risk response — bypasses change control and misuses the reserve. (B) informal sponsor notification without documentation is unprofessional and creates audit problems. (C) unilateral scope cutting changes the product without stakeholder agreement.
