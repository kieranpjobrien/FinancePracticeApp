# Question

A renovation project reports BAC = $1,000,000, PV = $400,000, EV = $350,000, and AC = $420,000 at month 5 of 10. Leadership asks for cost variance and schedule variance in dollar terms and your interpretation. Which interpretation is correct?

## Options

- **A**: CV is -$70,000 and SV is -$50,000, meaning the project is over budget and behind schedule at this checkpoint.
- **B**: CV is +$70,000 and SV is +$50,000, meaning the project is under budget and ahead of schedule at this checkpoint.
- **C**: CV is -$50,000 and SV is -$70,000, meaning the project is over budget and behind schedule at this checkpoint.
- **D**: CV is -$20,000 and SV is -$70,000, meaning the project is over budget and behind schedule at this checkpoint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

CV = EV - AC = 350 - 420 = -70; SV = EV - PV = 350 - 400 = -50. Negative values indicate over budget and behind schedule. (B) flips signs. (C) swaps CV and SV values. (D) miscalculates CV.
