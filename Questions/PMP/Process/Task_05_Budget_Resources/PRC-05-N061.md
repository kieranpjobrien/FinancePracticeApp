# Question

On a predictive retrofit, the resource histogram shows the same specialist crew swinging between 60% and 130% utilisation across consecutive weeks, driving fatigue and overtime cost. The contractual finish date is firm. Investigation shows the peaks come partly from non-critical tasks that hold some float, but the two worst weeks are caused by two critical-path activities scheduled in parallel that together exceed the crew's capacity. The project manager wants to cut the overtime without breaching the deadline. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reschedule only the non-critical tasks within their available float, accept that the two overlapping critical activities still peak the crew, and cover those two weeks with planned overtime.
- **B**: Reschedule the non-critical tasks within float and, for the two overlapping critical activities, bring in supplementary hired resource for those weeks so neither the peak nor the finish date remains.
- **C**: Reschedule the non-critical tasks within float and resequence the two critical activities to run consecutively, accepting the resulting extension to the critical path.
- **D**: Cap the crew at 100% utilisation across the whole histogram and let the schedule reflow wherever that pushes activities, including the finish.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The float-only move resolves the non-critical peaks but cannot touch the two activities that are on the critical path and overlapping — there is no float there. Because the finish is firm, the only way to relieve those two weeks without slipping the date is to add capacity for that window. (B) treats both causes: float-based adjustment where float exists, and supplementary resource where it does not, preserving the deadline. (A) settles for leaving the worst two weeks unaddressed, which is the actual cost and fatigue problem the manager set out to fix. (C) and (D) both relieve the peak by letting the critical path and the finish date move, which the firm contractual date rules out. The discrimination is that pure within-float optimisation (smoothing) is insufficient when the peak sits on the critical path, so capacity must be added rather than work merely shifted.
