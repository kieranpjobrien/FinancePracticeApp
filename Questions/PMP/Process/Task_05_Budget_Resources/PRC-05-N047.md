# Question

On a predictive ERP rollout, the project manager has built the schedule and now needs to commit a named integration specialist to a four-week critical-path task that starts in six weeks. The specialist is the only person in the organisation qualified for the work, sits in a shared resource pool, and is currently assigned to another project. The project manager wants the commitment to hold once made. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the specialist is free across the exact task dates in the resource calendar, accounting for leave, holidays, and the competing assignment, before committing her to the schedule.
- **B**: Reserve the specialist with her functional manager for the four-week window so the pool does not reassign her once the date is set.
- **C**: Add an alternative resource as a backup on the same task so the critical path is protected if the specialist becomes unavailable.
- **D**: Re-sequence the task to use the specialist's earliest guaranteed free period, reducing the chance of a clash with her other work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A commitment is only credible once it reflects real availability, so the project manager first verifies the specialist is genuinely free on the precise dates — the resource calendar exposes leave, holidays, and the competing assignment that determine whether the window is even open. Only then do the other moves make sense. (B) reserving her with the functional manager is the right next step, but reserving a window before confirming she is actually free on those dates can lock in a conflict rather than prevent one. (C) a backup is prudent risk response, yet it presumes the primary assignment is sound; you confirm the primary's availability before provisioning around its failure. (D) re-sequencing to her free period is a valid option, but you cannot identify that period without first reading her availability — so it follows the calendar check, not precedes it.
