# Question

In a weak matrix organisation, a database administrator shared across three projects is repeatedly pulled onto higher-priority production incidents, and your project's tasks keep slipping as a result. You have already re-sequenced what you can. The functional manager controls the DBA's assignments. What is the most effective next step?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the schedule and cost impact of the DBA's unavailability and negotiate a committed allocation with the functional manager, escalating to the sponsor if no commitment is reached.
- **B**: Escalate directly to the sponsor to demand the DBA be reassigned exclusively to your project.
- **C**: Ask a developer on your team to take over the database administration tasks to keep the schedule moving.
- **D**: Log the slippage as an accepted risk and rebaseline the schedule around the DBA's actual availability.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a weak matrix the PM lacks authority over the DBA, so the effective move is to bring the quantified impact to the functional manager who owns the allocation and negotiate a firm commitment, escalating only if that fails. (B) jumping straight to the sponsor to demand exclusivity skips the accountable manager and is unlikely to stick across three competing projects. (C) handing specialist DBA work to an untrained developer trades a schedule problem for a quality and risk problem. (D) accepting the slippage and rebaselining surrenders without first attempting the negotiation that could recover the resource.
