# Question

A cost-saving proposal for your construction fit-out replaces compliant, locally sourced timber with cheaper imported timber that meets spec but comes from suppliers with disputed sustainability claims. The saving is $140,000. The sponsor is enthusiastic. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the saving since the material meets technical specification and sustainability is outside the project's direct scope.
- **B**: Negotiate a lower price on the compliant local timber and present that as a smaller saving without changing supplier source.
- **C**: Reject the proposal, document the ethical and brand risk, and present alternative savings options to the steering committee.
- **D**: Defer the decision to procurement and legal so they can determine whether the disputed claims create any formal liability.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Organisational commitments to sustainability are part of stakeholder expectations and ethical obligations; a saving that relies on disputed sourcing is the wrong trade-off. (C) aligns with PMI's ethics stance and proposes substitutes. (A) treats ethics as out of scope, which PMI rejects. (B) is a limited workaround that doesn't address the proposed substitution. (D) defers ownership of a decision the PM should champion first.
