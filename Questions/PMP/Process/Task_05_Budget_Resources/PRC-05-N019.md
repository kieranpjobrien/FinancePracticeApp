# Question

On a predictive project, four funding events occur in the same reporting period. For each event, match it to the funding source the project manager should draw on, given how each source is authorised and what it is meant to cover.

## Items

- **1**: A supplier price rise that was logged in the risk register during planning and quantified in the reserve analysis has now materialised, costing close to its assessed amount.
- **2**: A wholly unforeseen regulatory levy, never in the risk register, must be paid to keep the in-scope deliverable lawful; the amount exceeds what remains in contingency.
- **3**: A work package finishes under its approved figure, and the saved funds are no longer needed for that work.
- **4**: A change board has just approved an enhancement that adds scope, and funding must be attached to it before the baseline is updated.

## Matches

- **A**: Released from management reserve by the sponsor
- **B**: Drawn from contingency reserve within the baseline
- **C**: Returned to the cost baseline / reported as a favourable cost variance
- **D**: Funded from management reserve as part of authorising the approved change

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

A risk identified and quantified in planning is exactly what contingency reserve exists for, and it is drawn down within the baseline (1-B). A truly unforeseen item that also exceeds remaining contingency is beyond the PM's contingency authority and is escalated for the sponsor to release from management reserve (2-A). An underrun is not "spare cash" to redeploy — the unused budget stays in the baseline and shows as a favourable variance (3-C). An approved scope change is funded from management reserve and folded into an updated baseline as part of implementing the change (4-D). The discrimination point is authority and trigger: items 2 and 4 both touch management reserve, but one is unforeseen impact escalated by need and the other is approved scope change implemented through change control; and an underrun (3) is reported, not reallocated.
