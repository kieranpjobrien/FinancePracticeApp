# Question

It is the final month of the fiscal year and the finance director asks whether your project can accelerate $80,000 of discretionary spend so the funds are not lost in next year's allocation. The project has no current need for that spend and the work it would fund is not scheduled until Q2 next year. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Bring forward the Q2 work to consume the budget before year-end so the funds are not forfeited.
- **B**: Decline the request and explain that spending without a current project need would not represent value to the organisation.
- **C**: Spend the funds on prepaid licences or services that the project will eventually use anyway.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to authorise carrying the unused funds into the next fiscal year.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics requires PMs to act as responsible stewards of organisational resources, and business value drives spending decisions — not fiscal year accounting pressure. (B) refuses to waste resources for a non-project reason, which is the correct ethical and stewardship position. (A) compresses the schedule purely for budget reasons and risks quality and team strain. (C) is "creative" spending that disguises waste as legitimate procurement. (D) is not a PM decision to delegate upward — the PM should first decline the wasteful spend; whether funds carry forward is a finance/sponsor matter that does not require the PM to spend them.
