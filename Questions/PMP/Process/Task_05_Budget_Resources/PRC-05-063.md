# Question

A senior data engineer has given four weeks' notice midway through a nine-month predictive analytics project. She holds tacit knowledge about the data pipelines that nobody else shares. The replacement hire will only start three weeks after she leaves. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask HR to make a counter-offer to retain her through the critical pipeline phase, even if the cost exceeds market rate.
- **B**: Extend the schedule by three weeks to cover the gap and communicate the adjustment to the steering committee transparently.
- **C**: Assign a mid-level engineer to shadow her full-time to build pipeline competence before she departs the project.
- **D**: Facilitate a structured knowledge-transfer plan, including pairing, documentation, and handover to a named interim owner.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The central risk is loss of tacit knowledge; a structured transfer with a named owner is the PMI-aligned response. (D) addresses root cause. (A) treats retention as the only lever and ignores transfer. (B) accepts the delay without mitigating the knowledge loss. (C) is part of the right answer but is not a complete plan, lacking documentation, a named successor, and an exit checkpoint.
