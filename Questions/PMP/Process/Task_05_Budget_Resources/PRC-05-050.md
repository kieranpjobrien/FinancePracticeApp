# Question

A Scrum team of eight has averaged 42 story points per two-week sprint for the past six sprints. Two senior developers will be on leave for half of the next sprint, and a new junior joins on day one. The product owner is pressuring the team to commit to 45 points. What should the project manager recommend the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the requested 45 points to maintain stakeholder confidence and catch up using overtime on critical stories.
- **B**: Let the team forecast using their velocity data and the known capacity reduction, then agree the sprint goal accordingly.
- **C**: Commit to 42 points to remain at historical velocity and assume the junior will offset the seniors' reduced availability.
- **D**: Ask the product owner to reprioritise the backlog so that only low-complexity work enters the upcoming sprint plan.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sprint forecasting is the team's responsibility, based on empirical velocity adjusted for known capacity changes. (B) respects team self-management and empirical process control. (A) bypasses the team and invokes unsustainable pace. (C) assumes a new junior replaces two seniors' output — unrealistic. (D) is a unilateral backlog directive that is the PO's call, not the PM's.
