# Question

Resource histograms show your lead architect over-allocated by 50 percent across weeks 8-12 on a hybrid programme. The critical path runs through her assignments in this period. Adding another architect is not possible at short notice. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Apply resource smoothing by shifting non-critical work so the architect stays within capacity and the critical path holds.
- **B**: Pay overtime to the architect for the five-week period so the current schedule remains intact and committed dates are kept.
- **C**: Split the architect role by delegating design decisions to mid-level engineers and keep only reviews on the architect.
- **D**: Fast-track the downstream activities so earlier effort from the architect can be absorbed across a longer window.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Resource smoothing adjusts within existing float and does not delay the critical path, so it is the correct first action when the critical path must be preserved. (B) relies on unsustainable pace and does not solve over-allocation on paper. (C) risks quality and authority issues without analysis. (D) confuses fast-tracking with resource treatment and adds risk unnecessarily.
