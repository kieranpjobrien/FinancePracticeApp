# Question

A new regulatory framework will apply to a banking project midway through delivery. Three of your eight developers lack the specialist knowledge needed to comply. Vendor training costs $12,000 per developer and takes two weeks. The sponsor prefers to "learn on the job." What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Send all three developers to vendor training so the team is qualified before regulatory-sensitive work begins in earnest.
- **B**: Agree to on-the-job learning and pair each junior with a senior so the skill transfer happens during sprint delivery.
- **C**: Hire three contract specialists on a three-month engagement and release the existing developers to non-regulated streams.
- **D**: Defer the training decision until the regulator publishes the final rules so the team trains on the confirmed material.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a compliance-sensitive environment, building the required capability up front is the defensible choice; on-the-job learning courts regulatory rework and risk. (A) invests in competence before exposure. (B) risks non-conformance when stakes are highest. (C) offshores knowledge and leaves the team unqualified. (D) delays action when rules are already known to be coming.
