# Question

A project has BAC = $1,500,000, EV = $600,000, AC = $750,000. Assuming the cost variance pattern continues, what is the Variance at Completion, and what does it mean for the project?

## Options

- **A**: VAC of negative $375,000, meaning at completion the project is forecast to be that much over the approved budget.
- **B**: VAC of negative $150,000, meaning the current cost variance simply carries forward to the end of the project.
- **C**: VAC of positive $375,000, meaning the project is forecast to finish that amount under the approved budget at closure.
- **D**: VAC of zero, meaning the current variance will be recovered across the remaining work in the project baseline.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

CPI = 600/750 = 0.80. EAC (typical) = BAC/CPI = 1,500,000/0.80 = $1,875,000. VAC = BAC - EAC = 1,500,000 - 1,875,000 = -$375,000. Negative VAC means forecast overrun. (B) simply carries the current CV and ignores forecasting logic. (C) inverts the sign. (D) ignores the typical-variance assumption stated.
