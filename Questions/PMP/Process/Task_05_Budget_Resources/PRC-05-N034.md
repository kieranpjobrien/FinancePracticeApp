# Question

A start-up's agile product team is funded from a fixed cash pool. The project manager projects that at the current monthly burn the funding will run out two months before the planned market launch. Velocity is stable, the backlog is ordered by value, and the team is already at sustainable capacity. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Show the sponsor the burn projection against the value-ordered backlog and propose a minimum viable launch scope that the available runway can fund.
- **B**: Take the burn projection to the sponsor with a request to secure additional funding to extend the runway to the planned launch date.
- **C**: Reduce the monthly burn now by releasing the lowest-utilised team members, extending the runway toward the original launch.
- **D**: Re-order the backlog to pull the highest-value features earliest, so the most valuable product exists before the cash runs out.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

With a fixed runway, stable velocity, and a value-ordered backlog, the lever the project manager controls is scope, and the responsible first move is transparency plus a fundable plan: show the sponsor the gap and propose a minimum viable launch the runway can actually deliver. (B) asking for more money may follow, but leading with a funding request before presenting a runway-fit scope option puts the decision to the sponsor without the recommendation the PM should bring, and gambles on funds that may not come. (C) cutting the team lowers burn but also lowers throughput, typically pushing launch further out and shrinking delivered value rather than protecting it. (D) the backlog is already value-ordered, so re-ordering changes little and still does not address that the funded runway ends before the planned scope can be completed.
