# Question

A project shows BAC = $500,000, EV = $200,000, AC = $240,000. The PM believes the remainder will be delivered at planned rates because the overrun was caused by a now-resolved supplier issue. What is the Estimate to Complete on this atypical assumption?

## Options

- **A**: $300,000, calculated as BAC minus EV, which is the planned budgeted cost of the remaining work to do.
- **B**: $360,000, calculated as BAC minus EV divided by the cumulative cost performance index to date.
- **C**: $260,000, calculated as EAC minus AC using an atypical EAC derived from AC plus BAC minus EV.
- **D**: $336,000, calculated as BAC minus EV divided by both CPI and SPI to be conservative on remaining work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When future performance is expected at planned rates (atypical assumption), ETC = BAC - EV = 500 - 200 = $300,000. (B) applies the typical (CPI-based) ETC, which contradicts the stated assumption. (C) overcomplicates a simple calculation. (D) uses the combined CPI×SPI formula, applied only when both future cost and schedule will mirror the past.
