# Question

Halfway through a predictive programme the project is over budget for the work completed. The sponsor will not release more funds, so the original budget at completion is a hard ceiling. The project manager computes the to-complete performance index against that ceiling and finds it well above the cumulative cost performance index achieved so far. Which two statements are sound conclusions or actions in this situation? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: To finish within the ceiling, the remaining work must run more cost-efficiently than the team has managed in any period to date.
- **B**: The required future efficiency looks unrealistic, so the project manager should quantify the gap and bring de-scoping or other corrective options to the sponsor.
- **C**: The most likely cost at completion should now be forecast as the budget at completion, since the ceiling is fixed and the team will be directed to meet it.
- **D**: The gap between the required and achieved efficiency should be closed by reforecasting the estimate to complete at the team's current cost performance.
- **E**: Because the variance to date is sunk, the index should be recomputed against the estimate at completion so the remaining target becomes achievable.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

A TCPI well above the achieved CPI means every remaining unit of work must be performed more efficiently than the team ever has (A), and the responsible action is to expose that gap with numbers and offer corrective options such as de-scoping to the funder (B). (C) confuses a funding ceiling with a forecast: setting EAC equal to BAC because the sponsor refuses more money asserts an outcome the performance data contradicts, rather than forecasting honestly. (D) reforecasting the ETC at current efficiency produces a realistic EAC but does not by itself close the gap to the ceiling — it quantifies the overrun, it does not resolve it, so stated as the action it is incomplete. (E) recomputing TCPI against EAC instead of BAC is a legitimate variant, but choosing it specifically to make the target look achievable misuses the index to disguise the shortfall rather than confront it.
