# Question

A 12-week sprint-to-release push has left your team of 10 showing clear burnout signs: retrospectives are flat, two people have taken unplanned sick leave, and quality defects are climbing. The sponsor has requested one more "final push" of two weeks overtime. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to the two-week push and promise the team a recovery break immediately afterwards to retain sponsor goodwill.
- **B**: Decline the push outright and demand a full four-week recovery cycle before any further work is committed to by the team.
- **C**: Transfer critical work to a surge team drawn from other projects and let the existing team rest during the requested period.
- **D**: Present burnout data and defect trends to the sponsor, propose a sustainable plan, and jointly agree a realistic release date.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI and agile principles both call for sustainable pace; burnout data shows the "push" would harm quality and people. (D) uses evidence to renegotiate with the sponsor. (A) trades long-term harm for short-term goodwill. (B) is adversarial and dismissive of a legitimate business need. (C) is a surge-team fix that assumes availability and bypasses honest sponsor conversation.
