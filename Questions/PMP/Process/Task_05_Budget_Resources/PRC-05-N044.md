# Question

A modular data-centre build depends on long-lead switchgear with a 22-week manufacturing time, plus locally available cabling and racks. The installation window is fixed by a client cut-over date, and the switchgear design is stable but a minor revision is still under review. The project manager is planning how to manage the physical resources so the schedule is protected and capital is not tied up unnecessarily. Which THREE actions are appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Place the switchgear order against its lead time plus a delivery buffer so it lands before the installation window, managing the open design revision through change control.
- **B**: Schedule delivery of cabling and racks close to when they are needed rather than committing them all at project start.
- **C**: Hold the switchgear order open and procure it through an expedited supplier nearer the window, accepting the premium to keep the design fully flexible.
- **D**: Reflect the switchgear's lead time and any holding requirements in the resource calendar and procurement schedule.
- **E**: Order the switchgear now but defer the cabling and racks contract until the switchgear has been delivered and inspected.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Long-lead items are committed against their lead time plus a buffer so they arrive before they are needed, with the open minor revision handled deliberately through change control rather than used as a reason to wait (A); readily available materials are pulled just-in-time to avoid tying up cash and storage (B); and both decisions belong in the resource calendar and procurement schedule that drive the plan (D). (C) is defensible only if no credible 22-week supplier exists, but here a stable design and a fixed date make a normal committed order far cheaper and lower-risk than paying an expedite premium to preserve flexibility that change control already provides. (E) needlessly chains two independent procurements: the cabling and racks have short lead times and gain nothing from waiting on switchgear inspection, so deferring them risks the window without reducing cost.
