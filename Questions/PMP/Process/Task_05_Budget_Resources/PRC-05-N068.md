# Question

A vendor delivering a subsystem under a firm-fixed-price contract reports that their internal costs have run well over their bid and asks the project manager to absorb the difference to keep them whole. The deliverable, acceptance criteria, and scope are unchanged. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to share the overrun from the project's management reserve to protect the schedule and goodwill.
- **B**: Decline the cost transfer, since under firm-fixed-price the vendor bears the overrun on unchanged scope, while continuing to manage delivery and the relationship.
- **C**: Convert the remaining work to time-and-materials so the vendor's actual costs are covered going forward.
- **D**: Issue a change request to raise the contract value to the vendor's actual cost.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under a firm-fixed-price contract the seller carries the risk of its own cost overrun when scope has not changed, so the buyer is not obliged to fund it, and the project manager keeps delivery and the relationship on track without conceding money. (A) management reserve covers the buyer's own unknown risks, not a vendor's bid shortfall, and tapping it here is unjustified. (C) switching to time-and-materials transfers the vendor's cost risk onto the project for no scope benefit. (D) a change request is only warranted when scope or requirements change, which they have not.
