# Question

A construction fit-out has BAC = $800,000, EV = $300,000, AC = $360,000, and the sponsor insists the project must finish within the original BAC. What To-Complete Performance Index must the team achieve on the remaining work to hit BAC, and what does the value imply?

## Options

- **A**: TCPI of 1.14, meaning the remaining work must be delivered at $1.14 of earned value per actual dollar, which is aggressive.
- **B**: TCPI of 0.83, meaning the remaining work can be delivered more efficiently than planned and BAC is easily attainable.
- **C**: TCPI of 1.20, meaning the remaining work requires a 20 percent gain on the current cost performance index trend.
- **D**: TCPI of 1.00, meaning the team only needs to perform at the planned rate on remaining work to land on budget.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

TCPI-to-BAC = (BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC) = (800 - 300) / (800 - 360) = 500/440 = 1.1364, rounded to 1.14. A value above 1.0 means above-plan efficiency is required on remaining work. (B) inverts the ratio. (C) misreads the maths. (D) ignores the cost overrun already incurred.
