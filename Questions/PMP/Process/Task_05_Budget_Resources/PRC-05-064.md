# Question

Midway through a 10-month project, current CPI is 0.75 and unlikely to improve. Current EAC forecasts a 25 percent overrun. The sponsor has told you privately she "does not want bad news in the steering pack this month." Next steering is in three days. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Present an optimistic EAC that assumes recovery, and work quietly with the sponsor on a private recovery plan after steering.
- **B**: Omit the EAC from this month's pack and include a general "budget pressure" commentary without specific numbers or trends.
- **C**: Delay formal reporting of the overrun by one cycle to allow a recovery plan to be prepared and presented as a package.
- **D**: Report the forecast accurately in the pack, paired with an analysis of causes and the recovery options being considered.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics requires honesty and transparent reporting; suppressing or softening a forecast to please a sponsor is a violation. (D) maintains integrity and is still constructive by pairing bad news with options. (A), (B), and (C) each involve hiding or massaging information, which breaches the reporting-trust principle.
