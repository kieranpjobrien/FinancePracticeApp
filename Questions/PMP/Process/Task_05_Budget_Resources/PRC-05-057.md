# Question

Your construction project depends on imported steel arriving by week 14 to avoid idling a 30-person crew at $45,000 per week. The supplier has just warned of a potential two-week slip due to port congestion. The risk register lists this risk but no response was chosen. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Cancel the current order and place a higher-cost emergency order with a domestic supplier to guarantee the delivery date.
- **B**: Instruct the supplier to pay a penalty under the contract and keep planning for on-time delivery in internal reporting.
- **C**: Analyse response options, including alternative sources and resequencing, then choose a proportionate response with stakeholders.
- **D**: Release the crew for the likely idle period to reduce cost and recall them once the steel delivery date is confirmed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When a previously identified risk shows warning signs, the PM completes the risk response planning step with the team and stakeholders before acting. (C) is the correct first step. (A) jumps to the most expensive option without analysis. (B) hides risk and violates reporting trust. (D) makes a major resource decision on an unconfirmed event and risks losing the crew.
