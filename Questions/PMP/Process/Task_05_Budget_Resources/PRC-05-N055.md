# Question

A predictive infrastructure project is funded in fixed annual tranches that the funder has confirmed cannot be brought forward. The time-phased cost baseline shows planned expenditure exceeding next year's funding limit in two consecutive quarters, even though the total budget across the project is sufficient. Some of the work in those quarters sits on the critical path and some is discretionary work that carries available float. What is the most appropriate first action?

## Options

- **A**: Reschedule the discretionary activities within their available float to shift spend out of the over-limit quarters, checking the spend profile now fits the tranche without moving the finish date.
- **B**: Reschedule both the discretionary and the critical-path work so the entire spend profile sits comfortably under the limit with margin to spare, accepting a modest delay to the finish.
- **C**: Reduce the planned scope in the over-limit quarters to bring spend under the tranche, deferring the descoped work to a later year.
- **D**: Raise a change request to draw the projected over-limit amount from management reserve so the scheduled work can proceed without rescheduling.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The funding limit is a hard constraint and the total budget is adequate, so the move is to reshape the spend profile to fit the tranche while protecting the end date — reschedule the discretionary, float-bearing work first and confirm the limit is met without touching the critical path (A). (B) rescheduling the critical-path work too would relieve the limit but needlessly extends the finish when float on discretionary work can absorb the breach on its own; you do not delay the project until you have confirmed float cannot solve it. (C) cutting scope changes what the project delivers to fix a timing problem that is purely a cash-flow profile issue — the work is funded overall, it is only mis-phased. (D) management reserve funds unidentified risk events, not the smoothing of a known funding-limit breach that rescheduling within float resolves at no cost.
