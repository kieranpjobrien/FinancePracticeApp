# Question

A project needs a specialised testing capability for a six-month window. Building it in-house means hiring and training staff whose skills the organisation will not use after the project; a vendor can supply the same capability as a service for the window. A make-or-buy analysis shows the in-house all-in cost and the vendor fee are within a few per cent of each other. The capability is on the critical path, and the organisation has used this vendor reliably before. Which factor should most influence the project manager's recommendation?

## Options

- **A**: That the vendor route transfers delivery and staffing risk and gives a known, time-boxed commitment, since the skills have no lasting value and cost is effectively a tie.
- **B**: That building in-house keeps direct control over a critical-path capability rather than depending on an external party's delivery.
- **C**: That the small cost difference between the two options should be the deciding factor, choosing whichever total is marginally lower.
- **D**: That the in-house option avoids the procurement lead time and contracting effort needed to bring the vendor on board within the window.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When cost is effectively a tie, the decision turns on the non-cost factors, and here they favour buy: the skills have no post-project value, so carrying them adds no lasting capability, while a proven vendor offers a defined, time-boxed commitment and shifts staffing and delivery risk off the project. (B) raises a real consideration — control over a critical-path item — but a reliable prior relationship substantially mitigates the dependency risk, so it does not outweigh the absence of any enduring value from building. (C) on a near-tie, fixating on a few per cent ignores the risk, flexibility, and capability-retention factors that should dominate a marginal cost gap. (D) lead time and contracting effort are genuine but manageable for a known vendor and do not offset the structural waste of building a capability the organisation will discard in six months.
