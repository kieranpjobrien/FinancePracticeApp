# Question

A newly formed nine-person hybrid team is in the second month of a 12-month programme. Two senior specialists are openly disagreeing in standups, junior members are disengaging, and velocity has dropped after an encouraging first sprint. The programme sponsor is pressing for delivery visibility. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign one of the senior specialists to another programme to remove the friction and restore a calmer team dynamic.
- **B**: Facilitate a working-agreement and conflict-resolution workshop to help the team move through storming into norming.
- **C**: Report the slowdown to the sponsor with a request for two additional specialists to absorb the disrupted work.
- **D**: Introduce stricter standup rules and require the specialists to submit disagreements in writing to the PM for resolution.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team is in the storming stage of Tuckman's model; the PM's role is servant-leader facilitation, not removal or command. (B) addresses root cause through team development. (A) loses knowledge and avoids the real issue. (C) throws bodies at a dynamic problem. (D) substitutes the PM for the team's own conflict resolution capability.
