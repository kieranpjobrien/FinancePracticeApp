# Question

A specialist commissioning engineer is named in the project charter as pre-assigned. Building the resource management plan, the project manager finds the engineer's resource calendar shows availability only from week 10, but the commissioning work is scheduled to start in week 6. The commissioning work has free float that would let it start as late as week 9. The functional manager controls the engineer's calendar. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assess the schedule and cost effect of the four-week gap and the available float, then take a recommended option — re-sequencing, securing earlier availability, or an alternative — to the sponsor and functional manager.
- **B**: Re-sequence the commissioning work to start at week 9 using its free float, narrowing the conflict, and request that the functional manager free the engineer one week early to close the remaining gap.
- **C**: Ask the functional manager to bring the engineer's availability forward to week 6 so the commissioning work can proceed exactly as scheduled.
- **D**: Move the commissioning work to start at week 10 to align fully with the engineer's confirmed availability, absorbing the float and any knock-on to successors.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The gap is a planning problem whose size and options are not yet quantified; the sound move is to evaluate the schedule and cost impact against the available float and bring a recommended option to the people who can decide — the sponsor and the functional manager who owns the calendar. (B) commits to one specific blended solution before the impact is assessed, and assumes the functional manager will grant the early week without the analysis that would justify it. (C) asks the functional manager to solve the whole gap unilaterally without offering the float the project already holds, weakening the request. (D) defaults to the latest start and silently consumes all the float plus any successor impact, choosing the most schedule-costly option without weighing the cheaper blends first.
