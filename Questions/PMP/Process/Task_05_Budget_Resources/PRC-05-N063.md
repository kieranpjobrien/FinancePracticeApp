# Question

A geotechnical survey rig is on the critical path and is the only one of its kind the contractor owns. The project manager learns it is committed to another job and will not be free until two weeks after the planned survey start. The survey cannot begin without it. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Crash the survey activity by hiring a second rig externally so the planned start date holds regardless of cost.
- **B**: Update the resource calendar with the rig's true availability and assess the schedule and cost impact before deciding how to recover.
- **C**: Fast-track downstream activities so the two-week rig delay is absorbed elsewhere on the critical path.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor for authority to reprioritise the rig away from the other job.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The resource calendar is the artifact that records when a resource is genuinely available; correcting it and then quantifying the schedule and cost impact is the prerequisite for choosing any recovery action intelligently. (A) crashing by renting another rig may be the answer, but committing the cost before sizing the impact or checking cheaper options is premature. (C) fast-tracking presumes downstream work can start without the survey output, which the scenario does not support. (D) escalating to seize the rig is an organisational fight that may be unnecessary once the real impact and alternatives are understood.
