# Question

On a hybrid programme, one specialist skill is held by a single team member; sprint work repeatedly queues behind her and her approved leave is one quarter away. The product owner needs that skill's output to continue across the leave period and beyond, and the team has roughly three sprints before she goes. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Pair her with two team members now so the skill is partly transferred before her leave and the dependency is reduced for the long term.
- **B**: Contract an external specialist to start before her leave, overlap for handover, and continue cover while she is away.
- **C**: Have her record detailed runbooks and recorded walkthroughs the team can follow to perform the work in her absence.
- **D**: Re-sequence the backlog so the skill-dependent work is front-loaded and largely completed before her leave begins.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pairing builds genuine capability in others, which both relieves the day-to-day bottleneck and provides durable cover for the leave and beyond — and three sprints is enough runway to start the transfer. (B) external cover bridges the leave but the skill leaves with the contractor, so the single point of dependency returns afterwards. (C) documentation supports transfer but rarely conveys tacit specialist judgement on its own; followers without the underlying skill stall on anything the runbook did not anticipate. (D) front-loading is fragile — it distorts prioritisation, depends on nothing slipping, and leaves the team unable to do the work the moment new demand arrives after her departure.
