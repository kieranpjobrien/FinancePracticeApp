# Question

A project charter names a key engineer as pre-assigned because the bid promised her specific expertise. When the project starts, her functional manager has quietly allocated her to a competing initiative. The schedule depends on her. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate with the functional manager, referencing the charter and the project's dependency, to secure the agreed allocation.
- **B**: Escalate to the sponsor to invoke the charter and direct the functional manager to release the engineer.
- **C**: Identify an alternative engineer and update the resource plan to remove the dependency on her.
- **D**: Log a resource-availability risk and prepare a contingency plan before approaching the functional manager.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pre-assignment recorded in the charter gives the PM a strong basis to negotiate directly with the functional manager for the committed resource — the appropriate first step in a matrix environment. (B) escalation to the sponsor is the right next move if negotiation fails, but going over the functional manager's head before a direct conversation damages the working relationship the PM still depends on. (C) replacing a specifically promised, bid-critical expert abandons a commitment before any attempt to honour it, and may breach what the bid undertook. (D) raising a risk and building contingency is prudent in parallel, but doing it before even approaching the functional manager treats the loss as a given and delays the direct action most likely to resolve it.
