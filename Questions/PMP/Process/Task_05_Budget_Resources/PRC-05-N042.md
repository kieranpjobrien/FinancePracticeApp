# Question

A project manager on a predictive rail-signalling programme is determining the cost baseline. Work-package cost estimates have been completed for the next two phases, but the final phase is still defined only at a high level with no detailed packages yet. The sponsor wants a credible time-phased budget now. How should the project manager treat the final phase in the baseline?

## Options

- **A**: Spread an equal share of the remaining budget across the final phase's months so the time-phased curve is complete.
- **B**: Exclude the final phase from the baseline entirely until its work packages are estimated, and add it by change request later.
- **C**: Establish a planning package within the relevant control account, carrying a high-level cost estimate, to be decomposed into work packages later.
- **D**: Move an allowance for the final phase into management reserve until the detail is known.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A planning package holds known scope and a high-level estimate inside a control account above the work-package level, letting the cost baseline be complete and credible while detail is deferred to rolling-wave planning. (A) flat-spreading invents a time-phasing the work does not support and misleads cash-flow planning. (B) leaving the phase out understates the budget and forces avoidable change requests for already-known scope. (D) management reserve is for unidentified risk, not for known future scope awaiting decomposition.
