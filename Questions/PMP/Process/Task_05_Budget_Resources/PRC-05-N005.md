# Question

A capital project is funded in quarterly tranches with a strict ceiling each quarter; the total project budget is sufficient. Planned spend in quarter three exceeds that quarter's tranche. Some quarter-three activities have float that would let them slip into quarter four within the same tranche limits; the remaining excess sits on critical-path work with no float. The project manager wants to protect the go-live date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Reschedule the float-bearing activities into quarter four to reduce quarter-three spend, then escalate the residual critical-path overshoot to the sponsor for a funding decision.
- **B**: Reschedule all quarter-three activities, including critical-path work, into quarter four so spend fits the tranche without involving the sponsor.
- **C**: Escalate the whole quarter-three overshoot to the sponsor to release additional funding before adjusting any of the schedule.
- **D**: Hold the schedule and let quarter-three spend exceed the tranche, reconciling against the underspent quarter-four ceiling at year end.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Funding limit reconciliation uses available float first: slip the float-bearing activities into the next tranche to cut the overshoot at no schedule cost, then escalate only the residual that lands on critical-path work, where the date is genuinely at risk. (B) rescheduling critical-path activities to fit funding moves the go-live date the PM is protecting. (C) escalating the entire overshoot before using float asks the sponsor to solve a problem the PM can partly resolve, and the tranche ceilings are usually firm commitments, not freely topped up. (D) deliberately breaching a strict quarterly ceiling and reconciling later violates the funding agreement.
