# Question

On a predictive healthcare IT project, the sponsor asks for a weekly "traffic-light" cost report rather than the earned-value dashboard you provide. She says EV is too complex for the steering committee. You believe EV is essential to surface trends. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the EV dashboard with a simple traffic-light tile each week so the steering committee engages with the report.
- **B**: Continue with the full EV dashboard and ignore the request since PMI best practice supports earned-value reporting.
- **C**: Provide a concise traffic-light summary tile backed by the EV dashboard as a drill-down for those who want detail.
- **D**: Escalate the request to the PMO and request a ruling on which reporting format steering committees must receive.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Tailoring means meeting the audience where they are without sacrificing analytical rigour. (C) layers a digestible summary on top of EV so the trend signals remain available. (A) discards analytical value. (B) ignores a legitimate stakeholder preference and harms engagement. (D) is premature escalation over a routine tailoring decision.
