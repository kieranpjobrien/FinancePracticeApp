# Question

What does Variance at Completion (VAC) measure?

## Options

- **A**: The difference between Budget at Completion (BAC) and Estimate at Completion (EAC), indicating expected over- or under-spend.
- **B**: The difference between Earned Value (EV) and Actual Cost (AC) at the current reporting period end.
- **C**: The standard deviation of cost estimates produced during three-point estimating for the project's activities.
- **D**: The average monthly variance between planned and actual spend observed across the project lifecycle to date.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

VAC = BAC - EAC. (A) is the textbook definition — a positive VAC means an expected underrun, negative means expected overrun. (B) describes Cost Variance (CV), a current-period metric. (C) describes estimate uncertainty, not variance at completion. (D) is a fabricated metric that does not exist in EVM.
