# Question

At a control point on a predictive bridge-deck project, the project manager calculates SPI = 0.86 and CPI = 1.05, and the cost variance to date is favourable. The deck pour is on the critical path, and the sponsor wants the planned finish date held. The project manager has confirmed the slip stems from late delivery of a single specialist component, now resolved, with the remaining schedule otherwise sound. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply a portion of the favourable cost variance to fund a fast-tracking option on the remaining critical-path work, after confirming it recovers the date and analysing the new risk it introduces.
- **B**: Hold the current plan and let the recovered component delivery work through the schedule, since the underlying cause is resolved and the cost position is strong.
- **C**: Authorise overtime and an additional crew across all remaining work packages to lift SPI back toward 1.0 as quickly as possible.
- **D**: Reallocate the cost underrun into the contingency reserve to strengthen the budget, and report the schedule slip as recovered at the next review.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The cause is a one-off that is already resolved, so the remaining schedule will not self-recover the lost time on its own; with a favourable cost position and a date the sponsor wants held, the disciplined move is to buy back the critical-path slip with a targeted compression option, but only after confirming it actually recovers the date and analysing the risk that fast-tracking adds. (B) is tempting because the root cause is fixed, but resolving the cause does not return the days already lost on the critical path, so the date is not recovered by waiting. (C) blanket overtime and an extra crew on every package spends the cost advantage indiscriminately on non-critical work that does not move the finish date. (D) moving the underrun into contingency does nothing for the schedule, and reporting the slip as recovered when no recovery action has been taken misrepresents status.
