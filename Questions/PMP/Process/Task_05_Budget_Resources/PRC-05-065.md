# Question

Your cross-functional Scrum team of seven is being pulled into production support roughly 20 percent of each sprint. Velocity has dropped and the product owner is frustrated. Ops leadership says support must take priority. The team is becoming demoralised by the constant context switching. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the 20 percent support load as a fixed capacity drain and reforecast commitments on the remaining 80 percent.
- **B**: Request a dedicated support team so your Scrum team can recover focus on the product backlog during sprint cycles.
- **C**: Negotiate a structured arrangement with ops leadership, including a dedicated on-call rotation and a clear escalation path.
- **D**: Raise the issue formally at the next steering committee and request a prioritisation ruling from executive leadership.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The root cause is unmanaged interruption; a structured arrangement with ops protects focus while meeting legitimate support needs. (C) is a negotiated, sustainable solution. (A) accepts context-switching harm as permanent. (B) is a big-bang request that is unlikely to be granted without negotiation first. (D) is premature escalation over an issue the PM can resolve with a peer leader.
