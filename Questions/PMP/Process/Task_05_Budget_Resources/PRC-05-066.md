# Question

A 14-month predictive project needs a machine-learning component that sits outside the team's core skills. Building in-house adds 10 weeks to schedule; buying a vendor component costs $180,000 but includes a proprietary data-lock clause. The sponsor wants the quick option. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Buy the vendor component as the sponsor prefers so the project schedule is protected and delivery remains on track.
- **B**: Build the component in-house to avoid the data-lock clause, accepting the 10-week schedule impact as a trade-off.
- **C**: Issue a split procurement where vendor delivers the first version and the team replaces it with an in-house version later.
- **D**: Facilitate a make-or-buy analysis that weighs lifecycle cost, strategic fit, and lock-in, then present options to the sponsor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A make-or-buy decision is a structured analysis, not a sponsor preference; the PM's role is to present the trade-offs transparently. (D) follows PMI's procurement guidance. (A) skips analysis and ignores a material lock-in risk. (B) pre-selects one option without analysis. (C) proposes a costly rebuild that assumes an answer before analysis is done.
