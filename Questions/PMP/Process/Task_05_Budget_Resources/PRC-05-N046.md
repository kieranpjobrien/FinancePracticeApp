# Question

On a predictive pharmaceutical-plant fit-out, a regulator issues new guidance that adds genuinely new scope to the project. Resolving it will cost about 6% of the budget. The contingency reserve is nearly depleted by earlier identified risks, but the management reserve is untouched. The sponsor tells the PM to "just use the management reserve and keep moving". How should the project manager proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Draw directly on the management reserve as the sponsor instructed, since the sponsor controls that reserve and has approved the spend.
- **B**: Charge the new scope to the remaining contingency reserve and report the resulting baseline overrun afterwards.
- **C**: Proceed with the work now and document the management-reserve usage at the next monthly governance meeting.
- **D**: Raise a change request defining the new scope and its cost, and obtain authorisation to move the required funds from management reserve into the cost baseline before drawing on them.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

New scope plus a management-reserve draw is a baseline change: the PM defines it in a change request, and on approval the funds are transferred from management reserve into the cost baseline before being spent. (A) a verbal instruction is not the same as an authorised baseline change; the governance route still applies even when the sponsor agrees in principle. (B) contingency is for identified risks, not new regulatory scope, and "report afterwards" inverts the control. (C) acting first and documenting later removes traceability and bypasses the authorisation the reserve transfer requires.
