# Question

A new corporate time-tracking tool requires every team member to log effort in 15-minute blocks against a complex code structure. Your team complains that compliance is consuming roughly 45 minutes per person per day. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate full compliance and remind the team that corporate time-tracking policy is not a project-level decision.
- **B**: Streamline how the team tags work, and raise the productivity impact and proposed simplifications with the PMO.
- **C**: Have a delegate log everyone's time on their behalf based on a weekly timesheet emailed in by each person.
- **D**: Suspend time tracking on the project until the corporate tool is improved to reduce the daily overhead.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to balance organisational requirements with team wellbeing and productivity, and to advocate upward when a corporate process is harming delivery. (B) does both — it improves compliance practice locally and raises the issue with the PMO who can change the standard. (A) ignores legitimate productivity concerns and treats the team as enforcement targets. (C) produces inaccurate data and likely violates policy. (D) unilaterally opts out of a corporate control, which the PM does not have authority to do.
