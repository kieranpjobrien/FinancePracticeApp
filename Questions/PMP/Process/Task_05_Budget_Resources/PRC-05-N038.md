# Question

On a software migration staffed by two teams, work is being duplicated and several decisions have stalled because both teams have acted as though they own the same deliverables. The schedule shows the right sequence and durations, the resource plan lists everyone's role, and an org chart with reporting lines is already published. The project manager wants a durable fix rather than a one-off intervention. What will most directly resolve the situation?

## Options

- **A**: Build a responsibility assignment matrix against each deliverable, fixing for every deliverable a single owner accountable for its sign-off and naming who is responsible, consulted, and informed.
- **B**: Convene the two teams to talk through the overlaps and agree between them who handles what before any further work proceeds.
- **C**: Escalate to the functional managers of both teams and ask them to jointly direct which team owns each contested deliverable.
- **D**: Add owner and approver columns to the existing schedule, naming for each activity who performs it and who signs it off.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The breakdown is about overlapping ownership and unclear decision rights per deliverable, and a responsibility assignment matrix is the durable artefact built to settle exactly that — one accountable owner per deliverable plus the supporting roles — which the existing org chart and role list do not provide (A). (B) a working session can surface the disagreement, but talk alone yields nothing durable unless its conclusions are captured in an accountability artefact, so it is a step toward A rather than the fix. (C) manager escalation can break a single deadlock but is a one-off ruling that does not give the teams a standing reference for the next contested deliverable, and it bypasses the PM's own ownership of role clarity. (D) tagging owner and approver onto the schedule is close, but the schedule is organised by activity and timing rather than by deliverable accountability, and it omits the consulted and informed roles, so it only partly addresses the overlap.
