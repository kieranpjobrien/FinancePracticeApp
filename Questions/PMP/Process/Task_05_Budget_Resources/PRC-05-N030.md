# Question

On an agile delivery, a finance stakeholder asks the project manager for a week-by-week plan naming which individual will work on each backlog item for the next three sprints, so they can confirm the resourcing is adequate to hit the release. The team self-organises and pulls work each sprint. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Address the underlying question — is capacity adequate? — by sharing the team's velocity, available capacity per sprint, and a forecast of what the backlog implies for the release, while explaining why fixed named assignments are not how the team plans.
- **B**: Produce the named three-sprint assignment as a best-effort snapshot, clearly caveated that the team will re-plan each sprint and the detail will change.
- **C**: Provide a sprint-by-sprint plan of which backlog items are forecast to be completed, but leave individual assignment out since the team decides that at sprint planning.
- **D**: Bring the stakeholder into the next sprint planning and review sessions so they can see capacity and assignment decisions made live, rather than supplying a plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The stakeholder's real need is assurance that capacity meets the release, and the best response satisfies that need with the agile artefacts that actually carry the information — velocity, capacity, and a release forecast — while explaining why named multi-sprint assignments would be fiction. (B) producing the named plan, even caveated, hands over data the team does not commit to and invites the stakeholder to track against assignments that will be wrong within days. (C) a forecast of completed items per sprint is useful but stops short of the capacity-versus-demand picture that directly answers "is resourcing adequate", so it under-serves the question. (D) inviting the stakeholder into ceremonies is reasonable engagement but does not give them the capacity assurance they asked for now, and shifts the burden rather than answering it.
