# Question

A fixed-price vendor delivering a critical subsystem has just signalled a 20 percent cost overrun and is asking the client to absorb it "to keep the project on track." The contract places performance risk on the vendor. The PM's sponsor suggests paying to avoid delay. What should the PM recommend first?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to the additional payment informally so delivery is not disrupted, and formalise a contract amendment after go-live.
- **B**: Reject the request outright, enforce the contract, and let the vendor bear the overrun as the pricing structure intended.
- **C**: Engage procurement to analyse the claim, validate the cause, and determine contractual remedies before any payment commitment.
- **D**: Issue a change request to fund the vendor's overrun from management reserve and brief the steering committee afterwards.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Under a fixed-price contract, cost overruns sit with the vendor unless a legitimate change has occurred. The PM's first step is joint analysis with procurement to validate the claim and establish remedies. (A) bypasses procurement and change control. (B) is premature without understanding the cause. (D) commits funds before establishing whether the claim is valid.
