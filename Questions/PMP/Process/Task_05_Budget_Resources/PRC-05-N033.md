# Question

While building the budget for a medical-device documentation project, the project manager must decide how much to fund for reviews, inspections, and validation. A team member argues for funding only inspection and validation at the end — appraisal that catches defects before release — and minimising up-front review effort to keep the budget lean, on the basis that what matters is that defects do not reach the customer. How should the project manager frame the funding decision?

## Options

- **A**: Fund both prevention and appraisal as cost of quality, weighting prevention because catching a defect later in the cost-of-quality chain is more expensive than preventing it, and in a regulated context the failure cost is severe.
- **B**: Fund appraisal heavily as the team member suggests, since rigorous inspection and validation before release is what actually stops defects reaching the customer.
- **C**: Fund prevention and appraisal equally as a fixed proportion of the project budget, applying the organisation's standard quality-cost allocation.
- **D**: Fund prevention and appraisal to the level the regulator's documentation standard explicitly mandates, and no further, to keep the budget lean.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cost of quality says conformance cost (prevention plus appraisal) is generally far cheaper than non-conformance, and within conformance, prevention is cheaper than appraisal because defects caught earlier cost less to fix — so the budget should fund both and weight prevention, especially where failure cost is high. (B) the team member's appraisal-only stance ignores that defects found at end-stage validation are the most expensive to rework; preventing them up front is cheaper than inspecting them out. (C) a fixed equal split is an arbitrary rule that ignores this project's specific failure-cost profile, which should drive the prevention/appraisal balance. (D) funding only the regulator's explicit minimum treats compliance as the ceiling rather than the floor and can leave the economically optimal prevention investment unfunded.
