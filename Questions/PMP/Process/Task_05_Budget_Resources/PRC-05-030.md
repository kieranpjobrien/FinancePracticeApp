# Question

The project budget was approved last quarter using rough order-of-magnitude estimates. After completing detailed planning, your bottom-up estimate is 40% higher than the approved budget. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a budget change request through the change control board to update the cost baseline.
- **B**: Identify scope reductions that bring the bottom-up estimate back to the approved budget figure.
- **C**: Begin execution within the approved budget and re-baseline once variances become visible in earned value.
- **D**: Present the refined estimate and the reasons for the variance to the sponsor and discuss options.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A 40% variance from rough estimates to detailed planning is significant enough to threaten the business case, so PMI expects the PM to surface it to the sponsor before invoking formal change processes. (D) gives the sponsor the information needed to choose: more funding, scope reduction, or cancellation. (A) submits a change before the sponsor has weighed in on whether the project is still viable at the new cost. (B) prejudges the trade-off in favour of scope cuts when the sponsor may prefer a different option. (C) starts work knowing the budget is wrong, which wastes resources if the project is later cancelled.
