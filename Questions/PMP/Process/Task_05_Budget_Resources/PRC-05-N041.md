# Question

Midway through a predictive water-treatment upgrade, a hazard surfaces that resolving will cost roughly 4% of the budget. The cost baseline holds contingency reserve, sized risk-by-risk for the threats in the register, of which about 5% of the budget remains unspent; a separate management reserve sits above the baseline. Reviewing the register, the project manager finds no entry corresponding to this hazard and no prior analysis of it. Funds are needed to act. What is the appropriate source and route?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a change request to transfer management reserve into the cost baseline, on the basis that the contingency was never provisioned against this exposure.
- **B**: Fund the response from the remaining contingency reserve, because contingency exists to meet realised risks and the unspent balance is more than sufficient to cover this one.
- **C**: Fund it from contingency now to act without delay, then submit a change request to replenish the contingency from management reserve.
- **D**: Add the hazard to the risk register, allocate contingency to it as a now-identified risk, and draw that contingency to fund the response.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Contingency is provisioned, risk by risk, for the specific threats analysed in the register; an exposure that was never identified or assessed falls outside what contingency was sized to cover and is funded from management reserve, which enters the baseline only through change control (A). (B) is the strongest distractor — the contingency balance is large enough in pure cash terms — but that money was earmarked for specific identified risks, and spending it on an exposure it was never sized against leaves those risks under-funded and misreports what the reserve was held for. (C) the act-now-replenish-later instinct is reasonable, yet it still spends contingency on an exposure it was not provisioned for and presumes a management-reserve transfer that has not yet been authorised. (D) adding the hazard to the register after it has already occurred so it can pull contingency is a relabelling workaround that bypasses the management-reserve authorisation; contingency is for risks identified and analysed before they occur, not for retro-fitting funding to an event already in progress.
