# Question

As an agile delivery initiative closes and its product moves into long-term operation, the PM is clarifying who owns each post-closure responsibility so nothing falls through the gaps. Match each responsibility (left) to the party who owns it after closure (right).

## Items

- **1**: Tracking whether the promised business benefits are realised in operation
- **2**: Accepting that the delivered increments meet the product needs
- **3**: Running and supporting the live product day to day

## Matches

- **A**: Product owner
- **B**: Business or benefits owner
- **C**: Operations or support team

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

After closure, responsibilities split clearly across three parties. Benefits realisation is owned by the business or benefits owner, who tracks outcomes once the product is in use (1-B). Acceptance that the delivered functionality meets the need sits with the product owner, who represents the customer (2-A). Day-to-day operation and support of the live product belongs to the operations or support team (3-C). Confusing these, such as expecting the operations team to own benefits or the product owner to run live support, leaves accountability misplaced after the project team has gone.
