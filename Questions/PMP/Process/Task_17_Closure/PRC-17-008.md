# Question

At a hybrid pharmaceutical programme's phase gate, the development phase has met most exit criteria, but one regulatory validation report is incomplete because a third-party lab is two weeks late. The next phase's funded team is contracted to start on Monday. The sponsor asks the project manager for a recommendation at the gate review. What is the most defensible recommendation?

## Options

- **A**: Pass the gate unconditionally and treat the missing validation report as a minor administrative item to close later.
- **B**: Recommend a conditional pass that authorises only the next-phase work not dependent on the validation result, with the gate's regulatory criterion tracked to closure.
- **C**: Fail the gate and hold the entire next-phase team idle until the validation report arrives, regardless of cost.
- **D**: Quietly start the full next phase without convening the gate, to avoid drawing attention to the missing report.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A conditional (or partial) gate decision lets value-adding work that does not depend on the open item proceed while keeping governance honest about the unmet criterion. (B) protects the funded team's time, isolates the regulatory dependency, and tracks it transparently to closure. (A) waves through a genuine regulatory criterion as if trivial, which is unacceptable in a regulated context. (C) is overly rigid — idling the whole team and incurring full cost when much of the next-phase work is unaffected is poor stewardship. (D) bypasses governance entirely and conceals a known gap, which is both a control failure and an ethical breach.
