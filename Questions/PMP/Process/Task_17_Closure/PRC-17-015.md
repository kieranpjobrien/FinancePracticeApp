# Question

A PM is drafting the final project report for an executive audience. Which set of elements is MOST important to include?

## Options

- **A**: A summary of delivered versus planned scope, key performance metrics, major issues and resolutions, and recommendations for future work.
- **B**: A detailed activity-by-activity log of everything the project team completed during the engagement, grouped by work package.
- **C**: A summary of positive outcomes and successes, omitting the issues so the report tells a cohesive and positive delivery story.
- **D**: A list of contributing team members and stakeholders with their roles and the hours they billed against the project budget.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A final project report serves executive decision-making and organisational learning, so it should be a concise, balanced summary against the original objectives. (A) covers the four things executives actually use: what was delivered, how it performed, what was learned, and what to do next. (B) is too granular for the audience and obscures the signal. (C) is incomplete and undermines trust because mature stakeholders know real projects had issues. (D) is administrative detail that belongs in supporting documentation, not the executive report.
