# Question

During a closure review for a national census-processing project, a graduate project coordinator confuses several closure-related concepts. To help her, the project manager wants her to match each scenario (left) to the concept it best illustrates (right).

## Items

- **1**: The product met the agreed scope, schedule, cost, and quality targets at delivery
- **2**: Two quarters after go-live, the expected reduction in processing cost has been achieved in operations
- **3**: The project ended before completion because a legislative change removed the need for it
- **4**: A formal review captured what worked and what to improve, stored for future projects

## Matches

- **A**: Early termination
- **B**: Project success (delivery)
- **C**: Lessons learned
- **D**: Benefits realisation

## Answer

1-B, 2-D, 3-A, 4-C

## Explanation

These concepts are routinely confused but are distinct. Meeting scope, schedule, cost, and quality at delivery is project (delivery) success (1-B). Achieving the intended operational outcome later — here the cost reduction — is benefits realisation, owned by the business after closure (2-D). Ending a project before completion because its rationale disappeared is early termination (3-A), which still requires orderly closure. A structured review of what worked and what to improve, retained for reuse, is lessons learned (4-C). Distinguishing delivery success from benefits realisation is the most frequently tested of these distinctions.
