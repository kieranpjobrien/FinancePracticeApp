# Question

A predictive logistics project is at closure. The outputs are delivered, but the benefits in the business case will only accrue over the next 18 months. The sponsor wants the project closed now. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project formally, hand benefits tracking to a named owner in the business with a documented realisation plan, and transfer the benefits register accordingly.
- **B**: Keep the project open until all business benefits have been fully realised so the benefits register remains under project governance throughout the full realisation period.
- **C**: Close the project now and archive the benefits register, accepting that the organisation will revisit the business case separately whenever benefits data becomes available later.
- **D**: Hand the benefits register informally to the sponsor without a structured realisation plan so the sponsor retains discretion over how benefits are tracked afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI separates project closure from benefits realisation: a project ends when its outputs are delivered, and benefits tracking passes to a named business owner. (A) closes properly and protects benefits visibility. (B) keeps a project open for work it cannot perform. (C) lets benefits disappear from view after closure. (D) hands over without accountability or structure.
