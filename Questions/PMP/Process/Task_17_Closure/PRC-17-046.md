# Question

A telecommunications project is at final closure and the sponsor is ready to sign acceptance. Reconciling the delivered product against the approved scope baseline, the PM finds that one low-priority requirement in the baseline was never built. The sponsor says it is no longer needed and is happy to accept the project as delivered. How should the PM resolve the discrepancy?

## Options

- **A**: Record the sponsor's decision to accept without the requirement in the acceptance sign-off and the closure report, then proceed to close.
- **B**: Raise a change request to remove the requirement from the scope baseline, obtain approval, and then take final acceptance.
- **C**: Log the unbuilt requirement as a deviation in the lessons learned register and proceed to acceptance, since the sponsor has waived it.
- **D**: Have the sponsor sign acceptance now and open a small follow-on work order to deliver the requirement later if it is ever required.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

At closure the delivered scope must reconcile with the approved scope baseline, and any difference is resolved through integrated change control, not by capturing a decision in a sign-off or a register. (B) is correct because a formal change request approved through the change process is what legitimately removes the requirement from the baseline, giving a clean audit trail and a defensible acceptance. (A) records the sponsor's intent but leaves the baseline unchanged, so acceptance would certify against scope the project did not meet. (C) treats the lessons learned register as a place to dispose of a live scope gap, which documents but does not resolve the baseline discrepancy. (D) accepts against an unmet baseline and parks the gap in a follow-on order rather than correcting the baseline before sign-off.
