# Question

A logistics platform has gone live and ownership is moving to the operations team. Operations completed training, but in the first weeks they call project team members several times a day with questions, and the project team is being lined up for new work. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Tell operations to log all questions in a shared inbox and respond on a best-effort basis once the project team has rolled off.
- **B**: Establish a defined hypercare period with a fixed end date, named contacts, and an escalation path, after which operations owns the system.
- **C**: Keep the full project team allocated until the volume of operations questions naturally tails off to a manageable level over time.
- **D**: Reassign the most active responders to operations permanently so support knowledge stays in place for the longer term after go-live.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats transition as a planned phase with explicit duration, scope, and exit criteria, not an open-ended availability. (B) sets clear expectations on both sides and gives operations a safety net while protecting the project team's transition. (A) effectively cuts off support without a structured handover and pushes the burden onto operations too quickly. (C) leaves the project formally open and prevents resource release, which is itself a closure obligation. (D) reorganises the staffing model rather than completing the transition, and makes a structural change beyond the PM's authority.
