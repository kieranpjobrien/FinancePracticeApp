# Question

A digital banking project has just delivered. Some stakeholders are pleased with the product, others are critical of the budget overrun, and a third group is focused on the missed go-live date. The PM is preparing the closure presentation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Lead with the product success and place the budget and schedule data in an appendix so the closure conversation stays positive.
- **B**: Defer the closure presentation until stakeholder views have aligned and converged to avoid an adversarial and unproductive meeting.
- **C**: Present each stakeholder group's concern in the section of the deck most relevant to them so each audience hears what matters to them.
- **D**: Present a balanced report covering scope, schedule, cost, quality, and stakeholder satisfaction, with context behind any variances.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's closure reporting reflects the multi-dimensional definition of project success: scope, schedule, cost, quality, and stakeholder satisfaction, supported by context. (D) gives all stakeholder groups the same complete picture and trusts them to weigh it. (A) selectively presents to manage the room and undermines transparency. (B) postpones the PM's reporting obligation in pursuit of an alignment that closure itself is meant to drive. (C) tailors content per audience, which is appropriate for some communications but inappropriate for the formal closure report, where a single shared record is the point.
