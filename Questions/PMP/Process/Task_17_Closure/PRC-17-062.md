# Question

A Scrum product has passed from a build-phase team to a product-led continuous delivery team. The PM is closing the original project. The receiving team uses different tooling and cadence from the originating team. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Transition the product with tooling alignment discussions, a defined period of joint operation, and a clearly demarcated project closure milestone separate from product continuity.
- **B**: Require the receiving team to adopt the originating team's tooling and cadence to preserve continuity of approach across the product's continuing development lifecycle.
- **C**: Close the project immediately and let the receiving team decide how to operate the product from day one without any overlap with the originating team's practices.
- **D**: Keep the originating team attached to the product informally for another quarter to smooth the transition and preserve knowledge across the differing methodology preferences.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI separates project closure from product continuity and respects the receiving team's methodology autonomy. (A) achieves both through structured transition. (B) imposes tooling on an autonomous team. (C) closes without any handover overlap, risking capability loss. (D) blurs project closure by keeping the originating team informally attached.
