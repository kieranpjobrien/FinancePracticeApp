# Question

A municipal water-metering project has reached final acceptance and is closing. The project had a wide group of stakeholders, including council departments, a billing vendor, and field crews who were kept informed throughout delivery. As part of closure, what should the PM do regarding these stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Issue a formal completion notice to the stakeholder group confirming the project has ended and setting out the handover of outcomes and any ongoing support arrangements.
- **B**: Send a closure notice to the sponsor and senior responsible owner, who will cascade it to the wider council departments, vendor, and field crews through their own channels.
- **C**: Lodge the final report and acceptance certificate in the project repository and notify stakeholders that closure information is available there for any who need it.
- **D**: Brief only the parties with continuing involvement, namely the billing vendor and field crews, since other stakeholders have no further role once the project ends.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Closure includes formally informing stakeholders that the project has ended and confirming how outcomes and ongoing support will be handled, so there is a clean, shared understanding of the conclusion. (A) is correct because a deliberate completion notice to the stakeholder group leaves everyone clear on closure and any continuing arrangements. (B) relies on second-hand cascade through the sponsor to a broad and varied group, which risks gaps and inconsistent messages. (C) makes the information passively available rather than actively communicating closure, so stakeholders may never learn the project has ended. (D) narrows the audience to those with ongoing roles and leaves the rest of an informed stakeholder group without the closure message they were led to expect.
