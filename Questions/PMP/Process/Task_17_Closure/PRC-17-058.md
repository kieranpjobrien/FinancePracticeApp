# Question

A hybrid insurance platform has gone live and the PM is planning closure lessons learned. Input from external customers, internal operations, developers, and executive sponsors all differs substantially in what they consider a lesson. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Gather lessons from each stakeholder group using appropriate methods, synthesise cross-cutting patterns, and present both the themes and the source-specific lessons honestly.
- **B**: Prioritise developers' lessons because they have the deepest technical knowledge of what went right and wrong during the construction and release phases of the project.
- **C**: Focus on executive sponsor lessons since their view will most influence the organisational lessons repository and future governance decisions about similar initiatives.
- **D**: Aggregate all input into a single narrative that represents average perspectives, smoothing out disagreements so the lessons document reads as a unified account.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI values diverse perspectives and synthesises without smoothing away legitimate disagreements. (A) respects all voices and surfaces both patterns and specifics. (B) privileges one group's lens. (C) privileges another group's lens for political reasons. (D) obscures real divergence that itself often constitutes the most important lesson.
