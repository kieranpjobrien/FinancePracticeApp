# Question

A hybrid rail-signalling programme is closing its design phase and preparing to enter the construction phase, which carries a far larger budget and a stricter regulatory regime. The programme board will decide whether to authorise the next phase. Which THREE of the following are appropriate for the PM to complete as part of closing the design phase and enabling a controlled transition? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the design-phase deliverables are complete and accepted against their criteria.
- **B**: Update the business case and risk profile to reflect what the design phase revealed.
- **C**: Present the phase outcomes to the programme board to seek authorisation to proceed.
- **D**: Award the principal construction contracts now so mobilisation can begin the moment the board gives its approval.
- **E**: Re-baseline the remaining programme schedule and cost to the construction estimate so the board reviews figures that already reflect the next phase.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Closing a governed phase means verifying and accepting the phase's deliverables, refreshing the business case and risks with what the phase revealed, and seeking the board's authorisation to proceed. (A), (B), and (C) are the legitimate phase-closure and transition activities. (D) is wrong because committing to construction contracts ahead of the board's decision pre-empts the very authorisation the gate exists to give, exposing the organisation to obligations for a phase that may not be approved. (E) is wrong because re-baselining to the construction figures before authorisation overwrites the board's reference point with the outcome it is meant to decide on; the updated estimates are presented for the decision, not locked in as the baseline beforehand.
