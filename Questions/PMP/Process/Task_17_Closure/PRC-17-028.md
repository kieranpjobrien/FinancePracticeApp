# Question

A trading risk system is being transitioned from a London project team to a 24x5 support team based in Singapore. The PM is planning the handover. What is the MOST important thing to design into the transition plan?

## Options

- **A**: An overlap of daily live working hours, recorded sessions for async review, and defined escalation contacts on each side.
- **B**: A written request that the receiving team adopt the project's existing tools and ticketing conventions to maintain continuity with the build phase approach.
- **C**: A daily email update from the project team summarising open questions and answers, replacing live sessions entirely because of the significant time zone gap.
- **D**: A six-month embedded engineer from the London team based in Singapore to bridge the differences and coach the support team throughout the transition.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI tailors transition planning to the specific constraints of the situation. (A) targets the actual constraints of distance and time zones with overlap, asynchronous artefacts, and unambiguous escalation, all of which let the receiving team work effectively after the project closes. (B) tries to make the receiver adopt the sender's environment, which conflicts with their established practices and is rarely accepted. (C) eliminates live interaction at the moment when complex tacit knowledge needs to be transferred. (D) is heavyweight and expensive and is a fallback for failed transition planning, not the first design choice.
