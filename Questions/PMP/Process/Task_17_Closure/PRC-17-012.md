# Question

Two months after a system was handed over, the PM notices that operations is still calling former project team members daily, even though the project's hypercare period officially ended four weeks ago. The team members are torn between their new assignments and helping operations. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct the project team members to stop responding to operations and redirect all calls to the operations manager for triage.
- **B**: Meet with the operations manager to understand why questions are still arriving, then address whichever gap is driving them.
- **C**: Reopen the project and extend hypercare for another month so the calls can be handled formally through the project channel.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to enforce that operations stop contacting the project team now that hypercare has formally ended.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI starts with root cause analysis when a control breaks down. (B) treats the symptomatic calls as a signal that something in the transition was incomplete, then targets that gap, which is the only path to a stable steady state. (A) cuts off support without fixing the cause and damages the relationship with operations. (C) reopens a closed project before knowing whether more time would actually solve the problem; it could just defer it. (D) escalates before the PM has attempted to diagnose and resolve the issue at their level.
