# Question

A hybrid hospital electronic-records project is preparing to transition a live clinical system to the in-house IT and clinical-operations teams. The project manager is drafting the transition plan. Which elements are essential to include so the handover genuinely protects ongoing operations? (Choose THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Defined operational support model, including who responds to incidents and the agreed service levels after go-live.
- **B**: Knowledge-transfer activities and a list of known issues, limitations, and pending backlog items handed to the receiving owner.
- **C**: A clause forbidding the operations team from making any change to the system for its first year in production.
- **D**: Acceptance criteria and a sign-off step confirming the receiving teams agree they are ready to take responsibility.
- **E**: A directive that all original project documentation be deleted once handover completes, to keep the operational record uncluttered.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

A sound transition plan establishes how the product will be supported (A), transfers the knowledge and the honest backlog of known issues (B), and confirms through acceptance and sign-off that the receiving teams are genuinely ready (D). (C) is unrealistic and harmful — a live clinical system will inevitably require fixes and changes, so freezing it for a year endangers patients and operations. (E) destroys the very records operations and future projects rely on; documentation should be archived and made accessible, not deleted.
