# Question

An agile team delivered a minimum viable product for an online-grocery subscription and the sponsor has decided to close the funded initiative now that the MVP is live. The expected revenue benefits are designed to accrue over the following two quarters, well after the team disbands. During closure, what should the project manager do about benefits that will only materialise later?

## Options

- **A**: Declare the benefits realised at closure because the MVP that enables them has shipped successfully.
- **B**: Keep the project formally open until the revenue benefits are measured, so the project can be held accountable for them.
- **C**: Hand over a benefits-measurement and ownership plan to the business so the later benefits are tracked after the project closes.
- **D**: Exclude benefits from the closure scope entirely, since an agile project is concerned only with delivering working increments.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Many benefits are realised in operations after a project ends, so closure should transfer the means and ownership of measurement to the business rather than claim or abandon them. (C) does this, preserving accountability without keeping the project artificially alive. (A) overstates the position — shipping the enabler is not the same as benefits being realised. (B) keeps the project open for outcomes outside the team's control, which is not how benefits ownership works. (D) wrongly treats agile delivery as indifferent to value; agile is intensely value-focused, and ensuring benefits are tracked is part of responsible closure.
