# Question

An agile team is closing out the final release of an internal expenses application that will continue in production under a support team. As part of release closure, which activities are genuinely appropriate? (Choose THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Hold a release-level retrospective to capture learning across the whole release, not just the last sprint.
- **B**: Confirm the product owner has accepted the released increment and that residual backlog has an owner.
- **C**: Re-estimate every remaining backlog item in detail even though the funded initiative is ending and they will not be built.
- **D**: Complete the handover to the support team, including documentation, access, and known issues.
- **E**: Reopen the team's velocity targets and commit to a stretch of extra sprints that the sponsor has not funded.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Release closure looks wider than a single sprint: a release-level retrospective gathers broader learning (A), the product owner's acceptance and a clear owner for residual backlog confirm the increment is genuinely done with no orphaned work (B), and the handover transfers documentation, access, and known issues to support (D). (C) wastes effort detailing items that will never be built once funding ends. (E) commits the team to unfunded work, which is outside the project manager's authority and contradicts an orderly close.
