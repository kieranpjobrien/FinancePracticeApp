# Question

A predictive office-relocation project has been accepted by the client and the project manager is releasing the project team. To release people responsibly as part of closure, which actions are appropriate? (Choose THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm that each person's outstanding deliverables are complete or formally reassigned before releasing them.
- **B**: Provide performance feedback and update each team member's records so functional managers have current information.
- **C**: Release all specialists on the same day regardless of remaining work, to simplify the administrative paperwork.
- **D**: Acknowledge contributions and ensure the team's knowledge has been captured before individuals move on.
- **E**: Retain the entire team on the project payroll for a further month in case the client raises any question at all.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Responsible release combines completing or reassigning outstanding work (A), giving feedback and updating records so functional managers can deploy people well (B), and recognising contributions while capturing knowledge so nothing leaves with the individual (D). (C) prioritises paperwork over delivery and risks abandoning unfinished work simply to release everyone together. (E) is wasteful over-insurance — minor post-closure queries are handled through warranty or support arrangements, not by holding a whole team idle, and indefinite retention conflicts with orderly closure.
