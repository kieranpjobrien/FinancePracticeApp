# Question

An external consultancy has built a custom inventory module for a manufacturing client under a fixed-price contract. The module passes the consultancy's internal testing, and the PM believes it meets every requirement. The client has not yet run their own acceptance testing. The PM wants to close the project. What must happen first?

## Options

- **A**: Send the consultancy's internal test evidence to the client and request sign-off on that basis, since the results already demonstrate every requirement is met.
- **B**: Arrange for the client to run their own verification against the agreed acceptance criteria and obtain their formal acceptance before closing.
- **C**: Issue the deliverable to the client with a defined acceptance window after which it is deemed accepted if no defects are raised, then proceed to close.
- **D**: Confirm with the client's project sponsor that they are satisfied with the module and treat that confirmation as acceptance to enable closure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A project cannot close until the customer formally accepts the deliverable against the agreed acceptance criteria, and on an external contract that acceptance is the client's to give after their own verification. (B) is correct because formal client acceptance following the client's verification, not the vendor's self-assessment, is the gate to closure. (A) asks the client to sign off on the vendor's own testing, substituting the consultancy's evidence for the client's contractual right to verify. (C) imposes a deemed-acceptance mechanism the contract may not provide for, manufacturing acceptance from the client's silence rather than their verification. (D) relies on an informal expression of satisfaction from one person in place of the formal acceptance the agreement requires.
