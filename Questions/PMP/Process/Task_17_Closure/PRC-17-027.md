# Question

A project manager is releasing team members back to their functional managers at closure. What should the PM provide to those functional managers?

## Options

- **A**: A confidential list of any concerns about each team member, so the functional manager can address them privately.
- **B**: A summary of the hours each team member billed to the project for time-tracking accuracy.
- **C**: No information beyond the fact of release, so each team member can present their own contributions to their functional manager.
- **D**: Balanced feedback on each team member's performance, skills demonstrated or developed, and notable contributions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's people-first orientation expects PMs to support team members' careers, including providing useful, balanced feedback to their functional managers at release. (D) gives the functional manager the information they need for future assignments and recognises team contributions. (A) is one-sided and unhelpful, and bypassing the team member damages trust. (B) provides administrative data without the qualitative content the functional manager actually needs. (C) misses an opportunity to recognise contribution and to support development.
