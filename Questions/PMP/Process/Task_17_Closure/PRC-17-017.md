# Question

A hybrid smart-metering rollout is handing its central data platform to a managed-service provider. Acceptance criteria for the handover were agreed at planning. At the transition meeting the provider's lead reviews the platform, says it "looks fine," and offers a verbal go-ahead so the team can stand down on schedule. Two of the agreed criteria — a completed operational runbook walkthrough and a documented acceptance sign-off — have not yet been worked through. How should the project manager treat the transition?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the verbal go-ahead as completing the handover, since the receiving expert has reviewed the platform and is satisfied, and record the outstanding criteria as a courtesy follow-up.
- **B**: Complete the remaining agreed criteria, including the runbook walkthrough, and obtain documented sign-off before treating the transition as complete.
- **C**: Treat the handover as complete now to meet the schedule, and convert the two outstanding criteria into post-handover warranty obligations on the provider.
- **D**: Have the provider sign off now against the criteria already met, and schedule the runbook walkthrough as a separate operational-readiness activity after responsibility has transferred.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A transition is complete only when the receiving party has accepted the product against the pre-agreed criteria with documented sign-off; here two of those criteria, including a readiness step, remain open, so the handover is not finished (B). (A) substitutes an informal verbal nod for the agreed acceptance and demotes real criteria to a "follow-up," leaving accountability ambiguous if problems emerge. (C) is plausible-sounding but reclassifies unmet acceptance conditions as warranty items to hit a date, which quietly lowers the agreed bar and pushes readiness risk onto operations. (D) splits the handover so responsibility transfers before the runbook walkthrough that proves the receiver can actually operate the platform — accepting against a partial criteria set defeats the purpose of agreeing them. Meeting all the criteria and securing sign-off is what makes the transfer of responsibility safe and unambiguous.
