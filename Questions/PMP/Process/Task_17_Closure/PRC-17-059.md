# Question

A predictive pharmaceutical project requires a regulatory sign-off before closure can complete. The regulator has indicated likely approval but has not yet provided the formal letter. Business pressure to close is mounting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project pending the regulator's letter and raise the missing approval as an administrative item tracked outside the formal closure pack now.
- **B**: Withhold closure declaration until the regulator's letter actually arrives, document the delay transparently, and communicate revised expectations to business stakeholders.
- **C**: Issue a conditional closure and retain the right to reopen the project if the regulator raises issues, so pressure to redeploy is accommodated.
- **D**: Escalate the delayed letter to the sponsor as a blocker the PM cannot progress, and await sponsor direction before taking further closure action.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats regulatory sign-off as a non-negotiable gate and requires transparent communication when it slips. (B) holds the line on compliance and manages expectations honestly. (A) declares closure without the required approval. (C) creates a fictional closure state that lacks regulatory standing. (D) escalates something the PM should manage directly until the regulator responds.
