# Question

While closing a predictive port-terminal automation project, the PM is sorting outstanding closure items into the right closure stream so each is actioned correctly. Match each outstanding item (left) to the closure activity that resolves it (right).

## Items

- **1**: A subcontractor's final invoice and a notice that the contract is complete
- **2**: An open purchase order with unspent committed funds
- **3**: A deliverable awaiting the customer's formal sign-off
- **4**: Design rationale held only by an engineer leaving next week

## Matches

- **A**: Obtain customer acceptance against the agreed criteria
- **B**: Reconcile and close the cost commitment, releasing unused funds
- **C**: Close the contract and issue formal completion notice
- **D**: Conduct knowledge transfer before the person departs

## Answer

1-C, 2-B, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Each item belongs to a distinct closure stream. Item 1 is a procurement/contract-closure matter resolved by settling the contract and issuing a completion notice (C). Item 2 is financial closure, resolved by reconciling and closing the commitment and releasing funds (B). Item 3 is deliverable closure, resolved only by formal customer acceptance against the criteria (A). Item 4 is continuity, resolved by transferring tacit knowledge before the engineer leaves (D). Mismatching these, for example treating the open purchase order as a contract-completion notice or the unsigned deliverable as a financial item, would leave the real obligation unresolved at closure.
