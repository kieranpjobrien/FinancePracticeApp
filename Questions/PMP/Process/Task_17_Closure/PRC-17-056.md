# Question

At closure of a predictive energy project, the PM finds that the finance team has closed commitments against the project but two invoices from a vendor are still in dispute and unpaid. The PM wants to close the project. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project and leave the disputed invoices for the finance team to resolve on their own timeline after the project's formal closure date.
- **B**: Defer project closure indefinitely until both invoices are paid so the financial record is completely clean before the project is formally concluded.
- **C**: Drive the dispute to resolution through procurement and finance, ensure all financial commitments are settled or formally handed over, then close the project.
- **D**: Close the project, write off the disputed amounts from the project's financial record so closure can proceed without affecting the final reported variance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Financial closure requires all commitments resolved or formally transferred with accountability, and the PM drives this rather than deferring or writing off. (C) achieves both. (A) abandons accountability for unresolved financial items. (B) blocks closure for a resolution the PM can actively pursue. (D) conceals disputed amounts rather than resolving them.
