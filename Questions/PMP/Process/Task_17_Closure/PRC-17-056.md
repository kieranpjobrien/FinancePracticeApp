# Question

An agile team is releasing the final increment of a mobile insurance app into production and will then disband. The PM wants the release to constitute a genuine transition to live operation rather than just a code deployment. Which conditions should be satisfied for the release to be considered a complete transition? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: The increment meets the agreed definition of done, including the release-level criteria for going live.
- **B**: The operations and support function has the runbooks and access needed to run the service.
- **C**: Every nice-to-have item remaining in the product backlog has been delivered.
- **D**: The product owner has accepted the released functionality.
- **E**: The remaining backlog items have been re-estimated so the future product team has a fully sized backlog at handover.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

A complete transition to live operation requires that the work meets the agreed quality bar, that the people who will run it are equipped, and that the customer's representative has accepted it. (A), (B), and (D) together cover quality (definition of done), operational readiness (runbooks and access), and acceptance (product owner). (C) is wrong because remaining nice-to-have backlog items are lower-priority and need not be built for a valid release. (E) is wrong because re-estimating the residual backlog is useful housekeeping for the future product team but is not a condition for the release itself to be a complete operational transition; the service can be live and fully handed over with an unsized backlog.
