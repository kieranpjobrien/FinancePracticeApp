# Question

A logistics optimisation project in a matrix organisation has finished and the deliverable is accepted. The PM is releasing the team members back to their functional managers and updating the staffing records. As part of releasing the resources, what should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Provide each member's functional manager with the PM's performance feedback and recognise notable contributions at the point of release.
- **B**: Submit the team's contribution data into the organisation's annual performance-review system so feedback is delivered through the formal corporate cycle.
- **C**: Forward to each functional manager the self-assessments the team members prepared, so the appraisal reflects the individuals' own account of their work.
- **D**: Confirm the release through HR records and leave performance feedback to the functional managers, who hold the formal line-management relationship.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Releasing resources properly includes giving the functional managers timely feedback and recognising contributions while the work is fresh and the PM's first-hand observations are most accurate. (A) is correct because the PM directly observed the work and should pass that feedback and recognition to the managers as the team is released. (B) routes the input into a cycle that may be months away, by which time context is lost and the feedback carries less weight. (C) substitutes the individuals' self-assessments for the PM's own observations, which is what the functional managers actually value from the PM. (D) abdicates the PM's stewardship role, since the functional managers did not see the project work and rely on the PM's account.
