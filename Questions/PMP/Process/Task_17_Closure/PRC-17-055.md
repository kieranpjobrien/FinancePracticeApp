# Question

A predictive enterprise resource planning rollout has been accepted by the business, and the PM is completing administrative closure before the team is released. The PMO has asked the PM to confirm that closure is genuinely complete rather than just acceptance being signed. Which actions are essential to complete administrative closure? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Reconcile and close all open cost accounts and financial commitments.
- **B**: Archive project records and capture lessons learned into organisational assets.
- **C**: Re-baseline the schedule to align with the actual completion date for reporting.
- **D**: Confirm formal acceptance and transition of deliverables to the receiving group.
- **E**: Reopen the risk register to add risks that might affect the product in operation.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Administrative closure settles the project's accounts, preserves its records and knowledge, and confirms that deliverables are accepted and handed over. (A), (B), and (D) are each essential closure activities covering financial, knowledge, and acceptance/transition dimensions. (C) is wrong because re-baselining is a planning control action, not a closure step, and the baseline is recorded as-was for variance analysis rather than rewritten. (E) is wrong because product-in-operation risks belong to the operational owner after handover, and reopening the project risk register at closure confuses ongoing operational risk management with project closeout.
