# Question

At formal closure of a predictive manufacturing project, the PM finds 12 open risks, seven open issues, and four deferred change requests. Nothing has been decided about them. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close all open items at project closure because the project is ending and any residual items are effectively no longer active work to track anyway.
- **B**: Triage each item, assign a disposition such as transfer, defer, or accept, record the rationale, and obtain acceptance from receiving owners before closing the project.
- **C**: Leave all items open in the project's tracking systems so future reference is possible even though the project itself is being formally concluded administratively.
- **D**: Hand the complete set of items to the PMO and ask them to apply their standard disposition rules without further project manager involvement required at closure.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects every open item to receive a deliberate disposition at closure, with acceptance by a receiving owner where applicable. (B) closes the project cleanly and transfers live items with accountability. (A) closes items without decision, losing operational signal. (C) leaves items in a tracker no one monitors. (D) pushes disposition to the PMO without project context.
