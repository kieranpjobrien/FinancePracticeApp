# Question

A hybrid aerospace programme has completed its design phase. The steering committee must decide whether to proceed into build. The PM has mixed signals: design met most acceptance criteria but two critical risks remain open. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend proceeding into the build phase as scheduled because most acceptance criteria were met and the two open risks may resolve during early build activities.
- **B**: Present the phase completion pack honestly, including outstanding risks and their impact, and support the steering committee to make an informed go/no-go decision.
- **C**: Delay the phase gate meeting until the two remaining risks are fully resolved so the steering committee receives a cleaner and more decisive recommendation.
- **D**: Escalate to the programme director privately and ask for guidance on how to present the mixed signals before any steering committee meeting takes place publicly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Phase-gate closure requires honest, complete reporting so governance can decide, regardless of whether signals are mixed. (B) protects governance and leaves the decision with the body authorised to make it. (A) applies PM judgement beyond authority. (C) delays governance to manage appearances. (D) seeks private coaching that may influence a decision the committee should own.
