# Question

The sponsor has just announced that a partially-built CRM project is being cancelled because of a strategic pivot. About 40% of scope is complete and several vendor contracts are still active. The PM is asked to wind the project down. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Move team members to new assignments immediately and let procurement resolve the open contracts on their own timeline.
- **B**: Continue building the in-flight features so the partial system has a defensible level of completeness before shutdown.
- **C**: Archive the existing artefacts as-is and record the cancellation reason in the closure report.
- **D**: Run a full closure including formal cancellation acceptance, contract closeout, lessons learned, archiving of usable assets, and structured resource release.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI requires the same closure discipline whether a project ends successfully or is terminated early; the trigger differs but the obligations do not. (D) treats cancellation as a closure event that protects the organisation against contractual exposure, captures salvageable value, and learns from the cancellation. (A) leaves contractual loose ends and skips the PM's accountability for orderly shutdown. (B) ignores the sponsor's strategic decision and continues to spend on work that has been formally stopped. (C) is partial closure and omits contracts, resources, and acceptance of cancellation.
