# Question

An agile mobile-banking team reaches its planned final release. The product owner is satisfied that the most valuable features are live and the sponsor has confirmed funding will not be renewed. Roughly 40 items remain in the product backlog, most of them low-value or speculative. How should the project manager handle the remaining backlog as the project closes?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the team for two more sprints to finish the backlog so nothing is left incomplete.
- **B**: Review the remaining items with the product owner, record any with residual value for the operations or future-roadmap owner, and close the rest.
- **C**: Move every remaining backlog item into the operational support queue so the support team can deliver them later.
- **D**: Delete the backlog entirely once the final release ships, since an undelivered item has no value.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In agile delivery the backlog is deliberately never fully completed; closure means consciously deciding what to do with what remains, guided by value rather than by a wish to finish everything. (B) does this: the product owner triages residual value and hands those few items to the right future owner while formally retiring the rest. (A) spends scarce funding finishing low-value work the sponsor has chosen not to fund. (C) dumps planning decisions on a support team without an owner or prioritisation. (D) discards items that may still carry value and loses the record of decisions made.
