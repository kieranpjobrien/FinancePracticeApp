# Question

A predictive shipbuilding project is in closeout. The yard's main subcontractor has submitted a claim for additional payment, arguing that several client-instructed changes were never priced. The client disputes that the changes were instructed. The contract specifies a defined dispute-resolution procedure. The project manager wants to close the contract. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse the claim outright and close the contract, recording the subcontractor's submission as having no merit.
- **B**: Pay the disputed amount in full to keep the relationship intact and allow the contract to close on schedule.
- **C**: Follow the contract's dispute-resolution procedure to settle the claim, and treat the contract as closed only once the claim is resolved or formally recorded for resolution.
- **D**: Close the contract now and handle the unresolved claim afterwards through general litigation outside the contract terms.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Open claims and disputes must be settled, or at least formally captured for resolution under the agreed mechanism, before a contract can properly close. (C) follows the contractually defined procedure and conditions closure on resolution, which is the disciplined approach. (A) prejudges the claim and closes over an open dispute, exposing the project to later liability. (B) pays a disputed amount without verifying entitlement, breaching stewardship of funds. (D) bypasses the contract's own dispute mechanism in favour of external litigation, which is premature and usually contrary to the agreed terms.
