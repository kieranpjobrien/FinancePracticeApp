# Question

During closure, the PM realises that several deliverables were rolled out and have been in active use by end users for four months, but the designated accepting authority never signed off formally. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat the deliverables as accepted by use and record acceptance in the closure report on that basis.
- **B**: Ask end users to halt use of the deliverables until formal acceptance has been obtained.
- **C**: Document the delivered scope, confirm it meets the original acceptance criteria, and obtain formal sign-off from the designated authority retroactively.
- **D**: Note the gap as an open closure issue and let the operations team chase the sign-off after the project closes.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI requires formal acceptance against the agreed criteria, regardless of how the deliverable came to be in use. (C) preserves the formal record by completing acceptance properly, even after the fact. (A) substitutes informal use for formal acceptance and leaves the record incomplete, which has audit and contractual consequences. (B) is disruptive and unnecessary when the deliverables are working. (D) abdicates the PM's closure responsibility and leaves the project formally unfinished.
