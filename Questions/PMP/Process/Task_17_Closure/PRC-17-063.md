# Question

A predictive aerospace programme has been suspended for 12 months pending a funding decision. The PM is told to put the project into hibernation rather than close it. Team members are being reassigned. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed as if the project continues normally, retain the team on the books, and await the funding decision before making any material changes to project posture.
- **B**: Package the project in a resumable state: baseline artefacts, decisions to date, open items, knowledge capture, and a clear restart playbook before releasing the team.
- **C**: Close the project formally, archive everything, and treat any future restart as a fresh project with a new business case and newly assembled team on new terms.
- **D**: Keep one senior team member embedded on a retainer so that institutional memory is preserved for the full 12-month suspension period without packaging the artefacts fully.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI supports tailored closure forms including hibernation, which requires a complete resumable package rather than either carrying on or full closure. (B) matches the instruction and protects future restart. (A) ignores the suspension instruction. (C) exceeds the instruction and destroys resumability. (D) relies on one person's memory instead of a package.
