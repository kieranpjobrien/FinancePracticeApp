# Question

A hybrid e-commerce replatforming project is two days from go-live and the project manager is finalising the transition to operations. The build is accepted and the warranty terms are agreed, but the operations team has not yet been trained on the new incident-handling runbook and the on-call rota for week one is not confirmed. The sponsor is pressing to go live on schedule. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Go live as scheduled and arrange the runbook training and on-call rota at some point in the first month of operations.
- **B**: Treat operational readiness — runbook training and a confirmed week-one on-call rota — as a go-live prerequisite, and resolve it with the sponsor before cutover.
- **C**: Cancel the project's go-live entirely because the operations team is not fully trained.
- **D**: Hand responsibility for the gap to the operations manager and proceed, since support readiness is no longer the project's concern after build acceptance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A transition is not complete just because the product is built and accepted; the receiving team must be ready to operate and support it, which here means runbook training and a confirmed on-call rota for the critical first week. (B) makes readiness a cutover prerequisite and resolves it transparently with the sponsor. (A) goes live with no support cover, exposing customers to unmanaged incidents at the riskiest moment. (C) over-corrects — the gap is a few days of readiness work, not grounds to abandon go-live. (D) wrongly disowns the handover; ensuring operational readiness is part of the project manager's closure responsibility until the transition is genuinely complete.
