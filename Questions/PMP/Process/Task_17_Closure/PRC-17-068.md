# Question

The PMO requires every closing project to complete a 60-item closure checklist. The PM judges that roughly a third of the items are not applicable to the small agile pilot project now closing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Complete every checklist item regardless of applicability, marking each as complete to preserve smooth compliance with the PMO's published closure standard across items.
- **B**: Discuss applicability with the PMO, tailor the checklist with documented rationale for any items omitted, and complete the tailored set accordingly.
- **C**: Skip items the PM judges inapplicable without consulting the PMO, document the judgement briefly, and submit the checklist as the project's closure artefact.
- **D**: Refuse to use the checklist and submit a custom closure pack designed for the pilot, arguing standard artefacts are unsuitable for agile work overall.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats tailoring as a dialogue with governance, with documented rationale, not unilateral edit or blind compliance. (B) respects the PMO while adapting to context. (A) fakes completion of irrelevant items, a data integrity breach. (C) tailors unilaterally and bypasses governance. (D) rejects governance entirely rather than working with it.
