# Question

A retail analytics platform has been delivered and accepted, and the project is closing. The business case promised a measurable reduction in stock-outs once the platform is in use, but that outcome can only be observed several months after go-live. The sponsor asks how the organisation will know whether the promised benefit was achieved. As part of closure, what is the PM's most appropriate action?

## Options

- **A**: Capture the current stock-out baseline and hand a defined set of benefit measures and a tracking plan to the business owner, who will monitor realisation after closure.
- **B**: Schedule a post-implementation review several months out and keep the project formally open until that review confirms the stock-out reduction.
- **C**: State in the closure report the benefit forecast from the business case, noting that realisation occurs in operation and so sits beyond what the project can confirm.
- **D**: Agree with the sponsor that the project team will reconvene in six months to take the post-go-live measurements and report whether the benefit was achieved.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Benefits frequently accrue after a project closes, so the PM instruments the measurement and transfers ownership of benefits tracking to the accountable business owner rather than keeping the project open. (A) is correct because establishing the baseline and handing defined measures and a plan to the business owner lets realisation be tracked properly once the project has closed. (B) keeps a temporary project running with no remaining deliverable work simply to wait on an operational outcome, contradicting the temporary nature of projects. (C) restates the forecast but sets up no measurement or ownership, so the organisation still has no means to confirm the benefit. (D) recalls a disbanded team for ongoing operational measurement that belongs to the business owner, not the project.
