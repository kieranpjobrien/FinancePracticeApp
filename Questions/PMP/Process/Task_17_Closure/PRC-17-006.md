# Question

A vendor-built reporting system has met every contracted deliverable, but the customer is withholding sign-off and asking for two new dashboards that were never in scope. The project is past its planned close date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the two dashboards informally to secure sign-off and avoid escalating into a formal contract dispute with the customer.
- **B**: Demonstrate contracted deliverables meet acceptance criteria, request acceptance for original scope, and route new dashboards through change control.
- **C**: Escalate to the legal team to enforce the customer's acceptance obligations under the contract and compel sign-off on the original scope.
- **D**: Hold sign-off open and continue light engineering effort while the customer reviews further and clarifies the additional dashboard requirements.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI separates acceptance from change: the existing scope must be accepted on its own terms, and new requests go through change control or a new contract. (B) protects the baseline, closes the existing obligation, and offers the customer a legitimate path for the additional dashboards. (A) bypasses change control, sets a precedent for ongoing scope creep, and may breach the contract terms or budget. (C) escalates legally before the PM has attempted to close the original scope cleanly. (D) leaves the project in a costly open state and rewards the customer for withholding acceptance.
