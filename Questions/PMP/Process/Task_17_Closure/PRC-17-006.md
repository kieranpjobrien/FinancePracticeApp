# Question

An agile team has been running a customer-portal product for eight months and is about to disband as the funded initiative ends. The portal will continue in production, maintained by a separate operations team that has not been involved in development. The product owner will move to another initiative. What should the project manager prioritise to protect continuity?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a single demo for the operations team on the last day so they can see the product working before the team leaves.
- **B**: Plan a transition that transfers documentation, known issues, the prioritised remaining backlog, and access, with overlap time for the operations team to ask questions.
- **C**: Leave the operations team to learn the product from the source code and the user-facing help pages after the development team has gone.
- **D**: Keep the product owner permanently assigned to the portal so that the operations team never needs to build their own understanding.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Continuity depends on a deliberate transfer of explicit and tacit knowledge while the people who hold it are still available. (B) covers documentation, the issue list, residual backlog, and access, plus overlap so questions can be answered before the team disperses. (A) is far too thin — a one-hour demo cannot convey operational knowledge of a complex product. (C) leaves operations to reverse-engineer understanding, which is slow and error-prone. (D) is not a sustainable model and conflicts with the product owner moving on; the goal is to make operations self-sufficient, not to create a permanent dependency.
