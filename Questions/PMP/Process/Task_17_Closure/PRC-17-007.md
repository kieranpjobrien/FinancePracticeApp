# Question

A predictive plant-automation project is reaching completion and the project manager is closing out a fixed-price contract with the integration vendor. Before the contract can be formally closed, which actions are genuinely required? (Choose THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Verify that all contracted deliverables have been completed and formally accepted against the agreed acceptance criteria.
- **B**: Confirm that any outstanding claims, disputes, or change requests under the contract have been resolved or are recorded for resolution.
- **C**: Re-tender the same scope to a second vendor to benchmark whether the price paid was competitive.
- **D**: Settle final payments and obtain documentation that all financial obligations under the contract are discharged.
- **E**: Increase the contingency reserve to cover any defects that might be discovered after the vendor has been released.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Procurement closure confirms the contract has been fully performed and can be settled. (A) verifying completion and acceptance, (B) resolving open claims and changes, and (D) settling payments with documented discharge are the core required steps. (C) is irrelevant to closing this contract — benchmarking against a fresh tender is a sourcing activity, not a closeout requirement, and re-tendering completed work is wasteful. (E) misuses reserves: defects after release are handled through warranty or support terms in the contract, not by inflating contingency, which in any case is being closed out, not topped up.
