# Question

A hybrid data-platform project is approaching closure. Two engineers who built much of the platform will leave the organisation shortly after the project ends, while a competent support group that did not work on it will take over operation. The PM has a few weeks of overlap before the engineers depart and is deciding how to use it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run documented walkthroughs and joint working sessions in which the engineers operate the platform alongside the support group, capturing design rationale and operational quirks as they go.
- **B**: Have the two engineers write up the platform's design rationale and operational notes into the existing documentation set for the support group to work through after handover.
- **C**: Pair the support group with the engineers on a shadowing rota so the support group watches day-to-day operation during the overlap period and asks questions as issues arise.
- **D**: Retain the two engineers on a part-time advisory arrangement after they leave, so the support group can call on them when it hits problems it cannot resolve.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Continuity depends on actively transferring tacit knowledge, the undocumented design rationale and operational quirks, from departing key people while they are still available, and capturing it so it persists. (A) is correct because documented walkthroughs plus hands-on joint sessions move the knowledge into the support group and into a durable record before the engineers leave. (B) relies on the engineers documenting knowledge that is, by nature, hard to articulate in writing, and defers the support group's learning to after handover with no chance to question. (C) lets the support group observe but does not deliberately surface the rationale and edge cases, and captures nothing once the engineers have gone. (D) creates a dependency on people who have left rather than achieving the transfer during the overlap, and the advisers may be unreachable when most needed.
