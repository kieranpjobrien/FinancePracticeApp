# Question

Project closure has stalled because the two stakeholders required for acceptance keep cancelling meetings due to a major regulatory submission. The project team is being reassigned in two weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Offer flexible acceptance methods such as a 30-minute virtual review or written sign-off, while progressing non-blocked closure activities.
- **B**: Wait for the stakeholders to free up after the regulatory submission, holding the team in place to be ready when they are finally available.
- **C**: Submit the acceptance package by email and treat non-response within five working days as deemed acceptance against the acceptance criteria.
- **D**: Reassign the team on schedule and personally return to chase acceptance after the regulatory submission is delivered by the two stakeholders.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to remove blockers proactively, including making it easy for busy stakeholders to fulfil their role. (A) reduces friction with lighter-weight acceptance options and keeps non-blocked closure work moving in parallel. (B) wastes the rest of the team while waiting on two people. (C) invents a deemed-acceptance rule that has no contractual basis and risks contested closure. (D) abandons closure until later and leaves the project formally open with no clear restart timeline.
