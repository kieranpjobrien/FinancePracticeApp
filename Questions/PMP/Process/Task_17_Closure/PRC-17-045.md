# Question

An agile programme has incrementally delivered a new self-service portal that is already in production and meeting its adoption targets. The product will continue to evolve under a permanent product team funded from an operational budget, while the temporary delivery funding is ending. The sponsor questions why the project is closing when development will clearly continue. What is the project manager's best explanation?

## Options

- **A**: The project's funded objective and initial value have been delivered, so it closes now, while the product itself continues to evolve under permanent operational ownership.
- **B**: The project should remain open until the operational product team has demonstrated that the business benefits are being realised in production.
- **C**: The project should remain open through a defined transition period so the delivery group can keep building alongside the new product team until handover is complete.
- **D**: The project closes because its funding has been exhausted, and any further development will depend on the product team securing a fresh budget allocation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A project is a temporary endeavour that ends when its funded objective is met; the product it produces can continue indefinitely under operational ownership without the project staying open. (A) is correct because the initial value is delivered and ongoing evolution is now business-as-usual under a permanently funded product team. (B) ties closure to long-run benefits realisation, which by design accrues after the project ends and is owned by the business, so it would keep the project open indefinitely. (C) prolongs the temporary structure to run in parallel with the permanent team, blurring the ownership boundary that closure exists to establish. (D) frames closure as merely running out of money rather than completing the objective, and wrongly implies further evolution is contingent on new project funding when it is now operational work.
