# Question

A hybrid platform project is ready to close, but two downstream projects are still consuming its artefacts and expecting ongoing support. Their PMs expect the closing project's team to remain available. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the project open until both downstream projects complete, so support arrangements remain within the closing project's accountability until the entire dependency chain is finished.
- **B**: Close the project as planned and let the downstream PMs adapt their plans to the absence of ongoing support from the previously available closing project's team.
- **C**: Escalate the conflict to the portfolio office and ask them to arbitrate between the closing project and the downstream projects' expectations of continued support.
- **D**: Negotiate named support responsibilities, transfer them to an appropriate ongoing owner, document the arrangements clearly, and close the project on its planned date.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects closing PMs to broker clean handovers for downstream dependencies, not to keep projects open or leave others stranded. (D) solves the dependency without delaying closure. (A) keeps a project open for work outside its scope. (B) abandons downstream projects that depend on visible signals. (C) escalates a negotiation the PM should conduct directly.
