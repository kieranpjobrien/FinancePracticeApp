# Question

A hybrid financial services project produced extensive documentation over two years. The PM is deciding which records to retain and how long. The organisation's retention policy is generic; the industry has specific regulatory obligations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the stricter of organisational and regulatory retention requirements to each record class, document the rationale, and archive before project closure.
- **B**: Follow only the organisation's generic retention policy because internal governance determines record keeping for closed projects across all business domains uniformly.
- **C**: Retain all records indefinitely to eliminate any future regulatory risk, even if the storage cost exceeds what the retention policy otherwise dictates.
- **D**: Delegate retention decisions to the PMO since classification of regulatory requirements is outside the project manager's normal accountability at closure time now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects retention to honour the stricter of applicable obligations and to be documented for defensibility. (A) satisfies both regimes and creates an audit trail. (B) ignores regulatory requirements that supersede internal policy. (C) avoids one risk while creating cost, discovery, and privacy risks. (D) abdicates an accountability that closure explicitly gives the PM.
