# Question

A regional bank has completed a core-banking upgrade and the project is in administrative closure. The PM is dealing with the final project records: the signed acceptance certificate, design decisions, test results, and the issue and risk registers. This is a regulated environment with audit and records-retention obligations. What should the PM do with these records?

## Options

- **A**: Archive the complete set of project records in the organisation's designated repository in line with the records-retention policy.
- **B**: Keep the records in the project's collaboration workspace with read-only access granted to the operations team, so they stay where the team already filed them.
- **C**: Hand the full record set to the sponsor as the accountable owner, for the sponsor to retain and produce if it is ever requested.
- **D**: Archive the signed acceptance certificate and the key design decisions, and discard the working drafts, test logs, and registers as superseded once the system is live.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Project records must be archived in the organisation's designated repository under the records-retention policy so they remain governed and available for audits, future projects, and any later disputes. (A) is correct because a managed archive preserves the full record under defined retention rules and access controls. (B) leaves records in a project-team space that is unlikely to be governed or retained to policy after the team disbands, even with access granted. (C) shifts a records-management obligation to an individual who has no defined duty or system to retain them to the required standard. (D) prunes the record to a summary and discards working evidence that audits and investigations frequently require, so the retained set is incomplete.
