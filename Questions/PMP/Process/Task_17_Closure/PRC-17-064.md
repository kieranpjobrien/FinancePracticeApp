# Question

At closure of a hybrid public sector project, the PM wants to capture stakeholder satisfaction honestly. Some stakeholders are reluctant to give direct feedback because they work with the PM on other initiatives. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Run face-to-face feedback interviews personally so that nuance and tone can be captured directly and the PM can probe responses further as needed.
- **B**: Skip formal feedback because the reluctance suggests honest input is unlikely and the resulting data would be unreliable for any meaningful closure analysis.
- **C**: Use an independent facilitator or anonymous mechanism to collect feedback, share aggregated results broadly, and act visibly on the themes that emerge.
- **D**: Ask only the stakeholders who seem comfortable speaking directly, on the assumption that their views are representative of the wider stakeholder population views.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects honest feedback loops and accepts that mechanisms must respect power dynamics to yield truthful data. (C) produces reliable signal and closes the loop visibly. (A) dampens candour due to the existing relationship. (B) abandons a closure deliverable that has mechanisms to overcome the obstacle. (D) biases the sample toward already-comfortable voices.
