# Question

A Scrum product that has run for six years is being retired. The PM has been brought in to close the product and decommission the infrastructure. A small user base still depends on it daily. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the remaining users, understand their needs and alternatives, plan a migration path with them, and sequence decommissioning against a communicated timeline.
- **B**: Switch off the product on the announced retirement date and direct the remaining users to use the already-available replacement product that marketing has been promoting.
- **C**: Extend the product's runtime indefinitely until every remaining user has voluntarily migrated away, then decommission the infrastructure once usage has dropped to zero.
- **D**: Offer the remaining users a financial incentive to migrate by a fixed deadline and decommission immediately after that deadline regardless of their actual migration status.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats retirement as a transition managed with stakeholder engagement, planning, and communication, not a unilateral date. (A) respects users and sequences work realistically. (B) disregards legitimate users without a tested migration path. (C) removes all retirement discipline and delays indefinitely. (D) uses incentive without ensuring users have a viable alternative ready.
