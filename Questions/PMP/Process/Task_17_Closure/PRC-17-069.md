# Question

A predictive corporate finance system has gone live six months ago. The sponsor asks the PM, who is still on payroll, to run a post-implementation review. The original team has all been redeployed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Decline the review because the team that built the system has dispersed and any review findings would be impossible to validate with the original delivery participants.
- **B**: Conduct the review alone based on documentation, since the PM has sufficient knowledge of the project to produce a defensible post-implementation review report independently.
- **C**: Plan the review with appropriate stakeholders including current operations, business users, and available former team members, and focus on outcomes against business case.
- **D**: Delay the review until the original team can be temporarily reassembled for a workshop, ensuring that technical delivery context is fully represented in the findings.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects post-implementation reviews to focus on outcomes with the stakeholders who can actually observe them, not purely on delivery teams. (C) uses the right participants for a six-month-post-go-live review. (A) refuses a valid closure-adjacent activity. (B) produces a one-person review missing outcome perspectives. (D) delays for context that is less relevant at outcome-review stage.
