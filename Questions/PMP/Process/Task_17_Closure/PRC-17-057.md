# Question

A Scrum team is closing a customer self-service portal. Release went smoothly and the team delivered every committed feature. The sponsor's original success criterion was a 30 percent reduction in call volume. It is too early to measure. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Declare the project a success based on delivery of committed features and on-time go-live, noting that call volume trends are outside project scope from now on.
- **B**: Decline to close the project until call volume data confirms the 30 percent reduction so the success criterion can be measured before formal project closure occurs.
- **C**: Revise the success criterion at closure to focus only on feature delivery since the original measurement would have been beyond the project's formal end date anyway.
- **D**: Close the project, record delivery outcomes honestly, and hand measurement of the call-volume criterion to a named business owner with a defined assessment point.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI distinguishes output delivery from outcome achievement: the PM closes on outputs but transfers outcome tracking to the business. (D) handles both correctly. (A) declares success on a criterion that isn't the actual success criterion. (B) keeps the project open for work it cannot do. (C) redefines success to match what was delivered, a form of goal shifting.
