# Question

A hybrid biotech instrumentation project is cancelled at 40% complete when a partner withdraws funding. The PM has a short window to close it before the team is reassigned across the organisation. Leadership wants the closure to protect the organisation's interests as far as possible. Which actions should the PM take during this early-termination closure? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Document the reason for termination and the project's status at the point of cancellation.
- **B**: Identify and hand over any partially completed work or knowledge that retains value for other initiatives.
- **C**: Formally close out contracts and release resources in line with the relevant agreements.
- **D**: Bring the work already started to a clean technical stopping point by completing the in-progress modules before tools are downed.
- **E**: Defer archiving the interim records until the cancelled scope has been formally removed from the baseline, to avoid filing records against work that will not complete.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Closing a terminated project still requires recording why and where it stopped, preserving any residual value, and formally winding down contracts and resources. (A), (B), and (C) capture the status, salvage usable assets and knowledge, and close obligations cleanly. (D) is wrong because, while it sounds disciplined, spending further unfunded effort to finish modules on a cancelled project still commits resources to an objective that is no longer authorised; the status is captured as-is, not advanced. (E) is wrong because the interim records should be archived as part of closure to support audits, lessons, and any later disputes; withholding them pending a baseline change risks losing evidence of how funds were spent and is not a precondition for archiving.
