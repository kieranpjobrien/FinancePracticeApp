# Question

A Scrum team on a retail customer engagement has delivered every increment per the Definition of Done, but the customer's sign-off on final acceptance is still outstanding after two weeks. Invoicing cannot proceed without it. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Invoice the customer based on the documented Definition of Done compliance since completion has been demonstrated technically for every released increment.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor to intervene directly with the customer's executive and request they override the delay in the customer's formal acceptance process.
- **C**: Accept the delay and continue to wait until the customer is ready to provide formal sign-off through their established internal governance routes.
- **D**: Engage the customer to understand the blocker, address any concerns directly, and facilitate the formal acceptance process actively on an agreed timeline.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI treats acceptance as an engagement task, not a passive waiting game or an escalation-first problem. (D) addresses the actual blocker through stakeholder dialogue. (A) bills without formal acceptance, breaching contract norms. (B) escalates before understanding the delay. (C) waits indefinitely on a commercially sensitive step.
