# Question

A predictive airport-baggage-system project is in administrative closure. All works are physically complete and accepted, but the mechanical subcontractor has lodged a disputed claim for acceleration costs that remains unresolved under the contract's claims procedure. The PM is under pressure to declare the project closed so the team can demobilise this week. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Close the project administratively now, recording the disputed claim as an open item handed to the legal department to pursue under the contract after closure.
- **B**: Keep the affected contract open and progress the claim through the agreed dispute-resolution procedure before finalising procurement and project closure.
- **C**: Negotiate an interim settlement of the claim within the PM's delegated authority so the contract can be closed and the team demobilised on schedule.
- **D**: Close out every other contract and work package, leaving only the disputed contract's financial position provisionally accrued so closure can be reported this week.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A procurement cannot be formally closed while a claim against it is unresolved; the contract's claims and dispute-resolution process must run its course before that contract, and the project, can close. (B) is correct because the affected contract stays open and the claim is worked through the agreed mechanism, so closure rests on a settled record. (A) declares closure while a live contractual liability exists and detaches the claim from the project that holds the facts and evidence needed to resolve it. (C) trades a sound contractual outcome for schedule, settling under pressure within the PM's authority rather than letting the agreed procedure determine validity. (D) reports closure on an estimated accrual while the obligation is still genuinely open, presenting an unsettled contract as effectively closed.
