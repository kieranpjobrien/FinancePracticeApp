# Question

A newly formed Scrum team asks the Scrum Master to clarify which of the things they handle are the Scrum *artifacts* — the three that carry a transparency commitment — as distinct from the commitments themselves or supporting charts. Which three are the artifacts? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: The product backlog, kept ordered and refined as understanding evolves.
- **B**: The sprint backlog, reflecting the work selected and the plan to deliver the sprint goal.
- **C**: The increment, the sum of completed, usable work that meets the definition of done.
- **D**: The definition of done, the shared quality standard a piece of work must meet to be releasable.
- **E**: The sprint burndown, the chart tracking remaining work against time within the sprint.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The three Scrum artifacts are the product backlog (A), the sprint backlog (B), and the increment (C). The catch is that (D) and (E) are real, correctly described Scrum elements that the team genuinely uses — but neither is an artifact. The definition of done is the *commitment* attached to the increment, not an artifact in its own right; treating it as one is the classic Scrum-Guide confusion this question targets. The sprint burndown is an optional supporting *chart* for tracking progress, never one of the three artifacts. Knowing the descriptions is not enough; the discrimination is the artifact-versus-commitment-versus-chart distinction.
