# Question

A digital transformation programme maintains a detailed benefits register at initiation but has not revisited it since. The sponsor now asks whether projected savings are still realistic. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reassure the sponsor that the original benefits case remains valid unless something material has obviously changed since approval.
- **B**: Refresh the benefits register with current assumptions, validate each item with the benefit owners, and report any revised projections.
- **C**: Commission an external consultant to produce a fresh benefits study so the sponsor receives an independent second opinion.
- **D**: Wait until the next phase gate when benefits will be reviewed anyway and then raise any adjustments with the sponsor.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats the benefits register as a living artifact that informs continuing business justification. (B) updates it transparently with the accountable owners. (A) gives comfort without evidence, which PMI rejects. (C) outsources an accountability the PM and owners hold. (D) delays a question the sponsor has asked now.
