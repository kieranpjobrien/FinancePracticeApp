# Question

During an agile coaching session, a team is clarifying the Scrum artifacts and the commitment associated with each, so everyone understands what each artifact is meant to make transparent. Match each Scrum artifact to its associated commitment.

## Items

- **1**: Product backlog
- **2**: Sprint backlog
- **3**: Increment

## Matches

- **A**: Sprint goal
- **B**: Definition of done
- **C**: Product goal

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

In Scrum each artifact carries a commitment that gives it transparency and focus: the product backlog is committed to the product goal (1-C), the sprint backlog to the sprint goal (2-A), and the increment to the definition of done (3-B). Mixing these up is a frequent error: the sprint goal belongs to the sprint backlog, not the increment, and the definition of done is the quality commitment that makes an increment genuinely usable, not a property of the backlog.
