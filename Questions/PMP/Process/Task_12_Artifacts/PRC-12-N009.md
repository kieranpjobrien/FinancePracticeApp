# Question

Midway through a SaaS development effort, the engineering lead has started quietly reordering the product backlog in the tool to put technically interesting items at the top, without telling the product owner. The product owner discovers stakeholders' priority items have slipped down. As Scrum Master, what is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Lock the backlog so only the product owner has edit rights, removing the engineering lead's ability to reorder it.
- **B**: Reaffirm with the team that the product owner owns backlog ordering, and facilitate a conversation so the engineering lead can raise technical priorities as input the PO weighs into the order.
- **C**: Restore the product owner's original order and ask the engineering lead to stop editing the backlog.
- **D**: Escalate the engineering lead's behaviour to their line manager as a breach of the team's ways of working.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Backlog ordering is the product owner's accountability, and the durable fix is to reassert that ownership while giving the engineering lead a legitimate channel to feed technical considerations into the PO's prioritisation. (A) a hard tooling lock treats people as the enemy, suppresses valuable technical input, and damages collaboration. (C) restoring the order addresses this instance but neither establishes the principle nor channels the lead's genuine concerns, so it recurs. (D) escalating to a line manager is heavy-handed for a misunderstanding about ownership that the Scrum Master should resolve within the team first.
