# Question

Team members have started using a public generative AI tool to draft project documents, pasting excerpts from the charter and risk register into prompts. The organisation has no AI usage policy yet. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Let the team continue since productivity has improved and raise the policy gap to the PMO when time allows later.
- **B**: Ban all AI tool use on the project until an enterprise policy is published and signed off by legal and security.
- **C**: Direct the team to pause AI use with sensitive artifacts, clarify acceptable use with security, and define interim project rules.
- **D**: Ask each team member to redact sensitive content themselves before pasting anything into the public AI tool in future.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats information protection as non-negotiable, and tailoring applies interim rules where enterprise guidance is absent. (C) stops the leak, engages the right control owners, and sets defensible interim rules. (A) leaves a confidentiality breach active. (B) is disproportionate and blocks legitimate productivity. (D) relies on individual judgement where policy and tooling controls are needed.
