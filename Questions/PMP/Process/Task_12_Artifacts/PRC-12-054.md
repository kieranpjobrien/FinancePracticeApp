# Question

A compliance project's requirements traceability matrix has not been updated for three months, and testing has begun. QA cannot confirm that all regulatory requirements are covered by test cases. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pause testing and have the business analyst immediately bring the traceability matrix current against the latest approved requirements.
- **B**: Let QA proceed based on their test plan and reconcile gaps with the matrix after the first test cycle.
- **C**: Ask the auditors to accept QA's internal coverage report in place of the traceability matrix for this regulatory cycle.
- **D**: Reassign the business analyst to full-time test support so the matrix can be updated once testing has fully concluded.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI treats the traceability matrix as a living artifact that binds requirements, design, and verification, especially under compliance. (A) restores the control before testing produces gaps that are hard to close. (B) accepts a known control failure. (C) asks auditors to accept an alternative rather than meeting the actual requirement. (D) defers the update to a time when detecting gaps is too late.
