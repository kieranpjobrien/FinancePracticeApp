# Question

A Scrum team has a one-line Definition of Done: "works on my laptop". Production incidents have risen sharply since the last release. The team says a richer DoD would slow them down unnecessarily. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the DoD as is and focus retrospective attention on root-causing the production incidents themselves going forward.
- **B**: Impose a detailed DoD taken from another team's template so this team starts producing higher-quality increments immediately.
- **C**: Escalate to the product owner and ask them to mandate a stricter Definition of Done across the whole Scrum team.
- **D**: Facilitate the team to expand the DoD themselves using incident data, so quality improves with team ownership.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's servant-leader stance and Scrum both place DoD ownership with the team, informed by empirical data. (D) uses incidents as input while the team retains ownership. (A) ignores the link between DoD and quality outcomes. (B) imposes a template without team buy-in. (C) is premature escalation bypassing the team's own authority over its DoD.
