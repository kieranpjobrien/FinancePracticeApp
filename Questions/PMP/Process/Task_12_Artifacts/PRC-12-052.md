# Question

A Scrum team is told the PMO's 40-page project management plan template is mandatory for every project including theirs. The team says most sections do not apply to iterative delivery. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Complete the template fully, marking inapplicable sections "not used", so the PMO ticks its governance box and the team can proceed.
- **B**: Work with the PMO to tailor the template for agile delivery, retaining required governance content and dropping sections that add no value.
- **C**: Ignore the template and rely on the product backlog, sprint plans, and working agreements as equivalent artifacts for this project.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to grant a blanket exemption so the team does not have to use any PMO templates on this delivery.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle requires artifacts to fit the delivery approach while respecting governance intent. (B) achieves both and brings the PMO along. (A) wastes effort on ceremonial documentation. (C) unilaterally bypasses governance. (D) is premature escalation before attempting a tailoring conversation with the PMO.
