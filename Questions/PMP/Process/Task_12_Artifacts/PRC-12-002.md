# Question

The PM finds that several team members have been editing the master schedule directly outside the change control process. As a result, the team is now arguing about which dates are the actual baseline. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reconstruct the correct current schedule state, agree the working baseline with the team, and re-establish change control.
- **B**: Lock the schedule file so only the PM can edit going forward, and let the team work from read-only snapshots.
- **C**: Accept the current edited state as the de facto baseline and move on without re-establishing change control.
- **D**: Revert to the original baseline and require every change to be re-submitted formally before being reapplied to the plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team needs a known-good baseline to operate against, then the process gap that allowed direct edits must be closed. (B) addresses one symptom (edit access) without re-establishing a verified baseline. (C) ratifies uncontrolled changes without analysis. (D) is heavyweight and disruptive when many of the changes may be valid; the priority is restoring a clear baseline first.
