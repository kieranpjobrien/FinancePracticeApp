# Question

A senior architect announces resignation mid-project. Much of the design rationale lives in their head rather than in design documents. They leave in four weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Launch recruitment for a replacement architect immediately so the new hire can shadow the outgoing one before they finally leave.
- **B**: Prioritise capture of design decisions and rationale into formal artifacts with the architect, then address replacement recruitment afterwards.
- **C**: Ask the architect to record a series of video walkthroughs that the team can reference afterwards when needed.
- **D**: Escalate the resignation to the sponsor and request that the architect's notice period be formally extended beyond four weeks.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's knowledge-management principle prioritises converting tacit knowledge to explicit artifacts before the source leaves. (B) does that. (A) addresses succession but not the undocumented knowledge at risk. (C) captures narrative but skips the structured artifacts the team will maintain. (D) assumes extension is feasible and defers capture that should start immediately.
