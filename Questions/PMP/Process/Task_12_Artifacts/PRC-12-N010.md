# Question

A predictive pharmaceutical validation project is closing. The project manager is shutting down the team's collaboration site and is about to delete it to free up storage. Some of the project's quality records and approvals are subject to a regulatory retention requirement of several years. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Archive the project artifacts to the organisation's records repository in line with the retention and information management requirements before decommissioning the site.
- **B**: Export everything to a personal drive so the records are kept safe and retrievable if anyone needs them.
- **C**: Delete only the working drafts and chat history, leaving the final documents on the collaboration site indefinitely.
- **D**: Ask each team member to keep a copy of the documents they personally worked on before the site is closed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Closure includes archiving artifacts according to defined retention and records-management policy, which guarantees the regulated records survive in a governed, retrievable store. (B) a personal drive is not a controlled corporate record store, risks loss when the individual leaves, and may breach data-governance rules. (C) leaving documents on a collaboration site that is otherwise being wound down is not a managed archive and provides no retention guarantee. (D) distributing copies to individuals fragments the records, loses authoritative control, and fails the traceability a regulator expects.
