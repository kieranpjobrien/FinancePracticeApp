# Question

A PM discovers that decisions made in steering committee meetings have been recorded inconsistently, with some captured in emails, some in chat threads, and some not at all. A recent dispute over a prior decision cannot be resolved. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask each attendee to share their recollection so the decision record can be reconstructed from multiple independent viewpoints here.
- **B**: Accept the meeting chair's recollection as authoritative for the disputed item and move on to avoid further delay.
- **C**: Establish a standard decision log kept in one location, confirmed at the next meeting, and brief attendees on it.
- **D**: Escalate the record-keeping gap to the sponsor with a recommendation that an independent secretariat run all future meetings.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats decision records as primary governance artifacts that must be consistent, accessible, and confirmed. (C) institutes the control going forward and closes the gap at source. (A) produces a reconstruction not a reliable record. (B) privileges one perspective and sets a poor precedent. (D) is premature escalation before the PM has established basic record-keeping hygiene.
