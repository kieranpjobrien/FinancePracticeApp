# Question

A project manager on a defence contract discovers that the project's commercially sensitive cost model and supplier contracts are stored in a team folder that every member of the wider 200-person department can open. The information is meant to be restricted to the core project team and procurement. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Move the sensitive artifacts to an access-controlled repository and grant permissions only to the roles that need them, in line with the information management plan.
- **B**: Email the department asking colleagues not to open the project's cost and contract files.
- **C**: Encrypt the individual files and circulate the password to the core team and procurement.
- **D**: Raise the exposure as a risk in the risk register and schedule a review of folder permissions at the next governance meeting.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Artifact management includes controlling who can access information, so the correct response is to relocate the data to a properly permissioned store aligned to the information management plan, which closes the exposure now. (B) a request relies on goodwill and leaves the access wide open. (C) password-protecting files within an open folder is fragile, hard to maintain across revisions, and a circulated password is easily leaked. (D) logging it as a risk and deferring action leaves sensitive data exposed in the interim when an immediate, low-cost fix exists.
