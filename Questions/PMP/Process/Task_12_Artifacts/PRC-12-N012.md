# Question

A sponsor tells the project manager she has lost confidence in the agile delivery because she cannot see whether it is on track for the funded release date and the outcomes the business case promised. The team maintains a current digital board, product backlog, and release burndown, and the sponsor already has read access to all of them but says they do not tell her what she needs. What is the most appropriate response?

## Options

- **A**: Walk the sponsor through the team's board and burndown again so she can interpret the existing artifacts for herself.
- **B**: Agree with the sponsor the few delivery and outcome measures she cares about and surface them as a release-level view she can read on demand from the same live data.
- **C**: Begin sending the sponsor a narrative weekly status report that translates the team's metrics into business terms.
- **D**: Invite the sponsor to the sprint review each iteration so she sees working increments and can judge progress first-hand.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The sponsor already has the team's artifacts and still cannot answer her question, so the gap is not access but altitude: she needs release-level progress against the date and the promised outcomes, not a team's sprint board. Surfacing the handful of measures she cares about as an on-demand release view built from the same live data gives her self-serve transparency pitched at her level without standing up a parallel reporting stream (B). Re-explaining the board and burndown (A) assumes the problem is comprehension, but those artifacts genuinely do not express release-date confidence or benefit realisation, so she would be no better served. A narrative weekly report (C) does translate to business terms and is a reasonable supplement, yet it reintroduces manual, lagging, push-based reporting to convey what a tailored live view could show continuously. Attending sprint reviews (D) shows working increments and builds confidence in quality, but a single increment does not by itself tell her whether the whole release will land on time and deliver the case, and it ties her insight to a fixed cadence. The fine margin is recognising that the missing ingredient is information at the right level, which B provides through the existing data rather than through added overhead or a misread of what the sponsor actually lacks.
