# Question

A vendor's performance has deteriorated and the PM may need to exercise contract remedies. Reviewing project artifacts, the PM finds vendor deliverable acceptance records are incomplete and informal approvals sit in chat threads. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Contact procurement immediately to begin formal dispute action against the vendor before performance deteriorates further.
- **B**: Reconstruct a formal acceptance record from the chat threads and email history, validated with the deliverable reviewers.
- **C**: Waive prior informalities, set clear written acceptance rules from today, and measure the vendor forward against those rules.
- **D**: Ask the vendor to agree jointly on which previously informal approvals should count as formally accepted deliverables.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's contract-management artifacts must be complete and defensible before remedies are invoked, and evidence exists in chat and email. (B) builds the defensible record. (A) pursues remedy without the evidential basis to sustain it. (C) sacrifices rights already earned by prior non-performance. (D) gives the underperforming vendor a veto over its own record.
