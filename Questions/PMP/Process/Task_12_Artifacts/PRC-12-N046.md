# Question

A business analyst joining a predictive ERP project needs the current schedule, stakeholder register, and approved scope statement to start a task due in two days. Her repository access has been requested but the IT grant typically takes a day or two. The project manager has personal copies of all three documents to hand. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Send her the three current documents now to unblock the task, while chasing her repository access and orienting her to the repository once it is granted.
- **B**: Wait until her repository access is approved, then walk her through the folder structure and version conventions so she retrieves the documents herself.
- **C**: Send her the three documents now and ask her to treat whatever the PM forwards as authoritative until her access comes through.
- **D**: Grant her temporary access to the PM's own copies by sharing that folder, so she works from the same files the PM holds.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Two needs coexist: unblock a near-term task and make her self-sufficient on the controlled repository. Sending the current copies now *and* following through on access plus orientation serves both, so (A) is best. (B) honours the controlled source but leaves her blocked against a two-day deadline for no real benefit, since the PM can supply the current versions immediately. (C) unblocks her but tells her to rely on PM-forwarded files as authoritative, which entrenches the exact bypass of the controlled store that good artifact management avoids. (D) makes the PM's personal folder a quasi-source others depend on; it solves access expediently but normalises working from an uncontrolled location rather than the managed repository.
