# Question

A delivery manager asks the project manager to produce a weekly executive slide deck of each agile team's velocity, defect counts, and burndown — a manual job of several hours. The teams already maintain live dashboards showing the same metrics, and the executives could be given read-only access. The delivery manager says the deck has always been produced and the executives have never complained about it. What is the best course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Give the executives read-only access to the live dashboards and propose to the delivery manager that the weekly deck be retired in favour of that view plus a short narrative only when something needs explaining.
- **B**: Ask the executives directly what decisions they use these figures for and in what form they want them, then decide whether the dashboards, the deck, or a combination serves that.
- **C**: Replace the manual deck with an automatically generated weekly summary drawn straight from the dashboard data, preserving a curated format without the manual effort.
- **D**: Keep producing the deck but add direct links to the live dashboards so executives can drill into the current figures when the snapshot raises questions.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

"Never complained" is not the same as "needs it", and the only people who can say what the report must do are its consumers — so the value-driven move is to learn what decisions the executives make from these figures and in what form before building or dismantling any artifact (B). (A) is a strong, likely outcome and may well be where this lands once the need is understood; but committing to retire the deck first risks removing something the executives quietly rely on and presumes the answer to the very question that should be asked. (C) automates a duplicate, always-lagging artifact without first testing whether a separate report is needed at all. (D) layers links onto the manual deck yet keeps the redundant, effort-heavy snapshot in place and still leaves the executives' real requirement unconfirmed.
