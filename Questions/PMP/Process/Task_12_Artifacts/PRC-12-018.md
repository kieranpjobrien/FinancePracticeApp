# Question

A 12-person team works across four time zones and frequently overwrites each other's edits to shared Word documents on a network drive. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Set a daily editing window where only one regional team can edit documents at a time.
- **B**: Move the documents to a platform that supports real-time co-authoring and version history.
- **C**: Designate a single document owner per artifact who makes all edits on behalf of the team.
- **D**: Require team members to email proposed changes to the PM, who will merge them centrally.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The conflict is a tooling problem, not a behavioural one. (B) removes the conflict at the technical layer — concurrent edits merge automatically and version history protects against accidental overwrites. (A) penalises productivity to work around the tool. (C) creates a serial bottleneck and discourages collaboration. (D) makes the PM a manual merge engine, which does not scale and slows the team down.
