# Question

A Scrum team is told the PMO requires monthly progress reports, RAID logs, and a stakeholder communications matrix. The team complains this is bureaucracy. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Push back on the PMO and request a formal exemption based on the team's agile delivery model and cadence.
- **B**: Accept the PMO requirements as-is and add all the documents to the team's definition of done for each sprint.
- **C**: Meet with the PMO to map their information needs to existing agile artifacts and add new ones only where gaps exist.
- **D**: Ask the product owner to take on the full PMO documentation load outside the team's normal sprint capacity.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The conflict is usually about format and assumptions, not information. (C) is classic tailoring — find what the PMO actually needs and reuse what the team already produces. (A) treats governance as optional, which it is not. (B) blindly doubles the team's documentation load and validates the bureaucracy complaint. (D) shifts the burden onto the product owner without addressing whether the work is necessary.
