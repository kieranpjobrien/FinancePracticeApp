# Question

A cybersecurity incident exposes that the project's shared drive lacks multi-factor authentication and has broad guest access. The project contains sensitive commercial data. The sponsor wants the project to continue without interruption. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the project as directed and raise the security posture issue at the next monthly steering committee update.
- **B**: Halt the project until IT security completes a full penetration test and audit of every artifact stored on the drive.
- **C**: Move the artifacts to a personal encrypted laptop repository while IT investigates and considers longer-term controls next.
- **D**: Work with IT security to apply MFA and appropriate access controls now, while the project continues under tighter monitoring.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI requires the PM to act on compliance and security risks without stopping delivery unless necessary. (D) applies proportionate controls immediately and preserves progress. (A) defers a known control gap. (B) is disproportionate and penalises the project for an enterprise control failure. (C) creates custody and compliance risk on an uncontrolled device.
