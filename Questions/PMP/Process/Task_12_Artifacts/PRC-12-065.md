# Question

Two sibling projects in the same programme have created near-identical risk registers and stakeholder registers in isolation. The programme manager discovers this during a routine review. What should the programme manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow each project to keep its own artifacts since local ownership drives better quality than consolidated registers.
- **B**: Mandate immediate consolidation into single programme-level registers that replace the two project-level copies from today.
- **C**: Ask the PMO to audit both registers and decide centrally which entries are programme-level and which stay project-level.
- **D**: Work with both PMs to share common risks and stakeholders at programme level while preserving project-specific entries.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's programme governance shares what is genuinely shared while respecting project-level context. (D) balances both. (A) accepts duplication and missed shared risks. (B) forces consolidation that strips project-level nuance. (C) outsources a decision the programme manager and PMs are accountable for making together.
