# Question

A global project's stakeholder register contains personal contact details and sensitivity notes about several executives. A team lead asks for the whole register to help with comms planning. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Share the full register freely since effective communications planning depends on having all stakeholder context visible.
- **B**: Refuse the request and produce the entire comms plan yourself so the sensitive register never leaves your custody.
- **C**: Ask the team lead to sign a confidentiality agreement, then share the full register including executive sensitivity notes.
- **D**: Provide a filtered extract covering only the stakeholders relevant to the team lead's scope, excluding sensitive personal notes.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's information-management principle applies the need-to-know standard to project artifacts, especially those containing personal data. (D) gives the team lead what the role requires and nothing more. (A) overshares personal and sensitive material. (B) blocks a legitimate delegation and creates a bottleneck. (C) relies on a paper control where access control is the right answer.
