# Question

A newly remote Scrum team has a digital work-tracking board, but cards sit unmoved for days because members update them only when they remember, and the daily standup has become verbal status nobody can reconstruct afterwards. The team has already discussed the problem twice in retrospectives and agreed each time that "we should keep the board current", yet nothing changes and the product owner still interrupts people individually to learn where things stand. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate the team in agreeing a specific, observable working agreement — such as moving a card the moment its status changes — and how they will hold each other to it.
- **B**: Restructure the daily standup so it is run by walking each in-progress card on the board, letting the conversation drive every card to its true state.
- **C**: Add a definition-of-done check so a story cannot be called done until its board card and linked task states match reality.
- **D**: Have the team review board freshness at each retrospective and agree improvement actions until updating becomes habitual.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The team has already named the right behaviour twice; what is missing is a concrete, enforceable agreement and a way to make the standard stick, so a specific observable working agreement with peer accountability is the move that actually changes behaviour — (A). (B) walking the board at standup is a strong reinforcing habit and may well be part of the agreement, but on its own it only synchronises the board once a day, leaving it stale between standups exactly when the product owner needs it. (C) a definition-of-done gate catches stories at completion but does nothing for the in-flight cards that sit unmoved for days mid-sprint, which is the actual complaint. (D) routing this back through retrospectives repeats precisely the loop that has already failed twice — the team keeps agreeing in principle without a binding standard — so it is the least likely to break the pattern.
