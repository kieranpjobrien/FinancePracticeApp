# Question

A PM inherits a six-month-old aviation project. Reviewing artifacts, they find an assumptions log created at initiation that has never been updated, though several assumptions have clearly changed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Archive the old log as historical and start a fresh assumptions log going forward from today's baseline state.
- **B**: Review each assumption with the relevant owner, update or retire it, and raise any that have become risks or issues.
- **C**: Send the log to all team leads and ask them to flag any assumptions they believe are now wrong since initiation.
- **D**: Focus attention on current risks and issues since assumptions are generally most useful only during early planning stages.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats the assumptions log as a living artifact where invalidated assumptions often become active risks or issues. (B) validates each assumption with its accountable owner and converts appropriately. (A) breaks traceability to the original business case. (C) produces scattered feedback without structured conversion. (D) misunderstands the log's role across the full lifecycle.
