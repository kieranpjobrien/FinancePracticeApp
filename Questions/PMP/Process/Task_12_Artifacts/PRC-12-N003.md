# Question

Halfway through a hybrid software-and-hardware programme, the project manager realises the risk register has not been updated in six weeks. Several risks logged early have clearly materialised or expired, and two new regulatory risks have come up in conversation but were never recorded. A steering committee review is in three days. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reconstruct an accurate current risk register by reviewing recent issues, status reports, and team input, then re-rate and re-prioritise the risks before the review.
- **B**: Add the two new regulatory risks to the register so the most urgent gaps are captured before the committee meets.
- **C**: Present the existing register at the review and explain to the committee that it is out of date and will be refreshed afterwards.
- **D**: Replace the neglected register with a simpler top-ten risk list that the team can keep current more easily going forward.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The register is the project's authoritative risk artifact, and the immediate need is to make it reflect reality, so a full reconciliation of materialised, expired, and new risks restores its integrity before it informs a governance decision. (B) capturing only the two new risks leaves the stale and expired entries uncorrected, so the committee still sees a misleading picture. (C) knowingly presenting a register that is six weeks out of date risks decisions based on bad data and is a governance failure. (D) changing the register's format is a process decision for later; it does not address the urgent problem that the current content is wrong.
