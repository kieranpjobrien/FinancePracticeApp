# Question

A government-funded research project is closing. The funding agreement requires seven-year retention of all research artifacts, but the organisation's default policy deletes project repositories after two years to save storage costs. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Transfer the artifacts to the research lead's personal drive so they are retained outside the default deletion policy entirely.
- **B**: Apply the default two-year policy and rely on backups in case the artifacts are needed by the funding agency later.
- **C**: Arrange a formal retention exception with records management that meets the full seven-year funding obligation and document it.
- **D**: Email all team members asking each to keep their own copies of the research artifacts for the seven-year period.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats compliance obligations as non-negotiable and expects the PM to arrange proper retention through the organisation's records function. (C) does so and documents the exception for audit. (A) violates records-management policy and creates custody risk. (B) risks non-compliance by relying on unverified backups. (D) distributes custody to individuals with no retention obligation after they leave.
