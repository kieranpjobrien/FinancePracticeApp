# Question

Midway through a marketing-campaign project the project manager finds three team members each keeping their own copy of the risk register — "Risk_Register_final_v2_KO.xlsx", "risks_latest.xlsx", and "RR_working.xlsx" — in different folders, with entries that no longer agree. A risk raised yesterday appears in only one copy. Each of the three owns a distinct risk area and updates throughout the week. What should the project manager do to stop this recurring?

## Options

- **A**: Move all three owners onto one version-controlled register in the repository that each updates directly in their own area.
- **B**: Keep one master register and have the three owners submit their changes to a single nominated person who applies them.
- **C**: Consolidate the three copies into one now, confirm it holds every current entry, and store it as the authoritative register going forward.
- **D**: Split the register into three area-specific files, one per owner, so each maintains only their own without overwriting the others.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The recurrence is caused by multiple editable copies of the same artifact; a single version-controlled register that all three update directly removes the duplication while still letting each own their area, so (A) is the durable fix. (C) is a necessary step but only cleans up today's divergence — without a shared controlled register, parallel copies reappear. (B) gives one authoritative file but funnels every change through one person, creating a bottleneck and lag that invite owners to keep private working copies again. (D) ends the overwrites yet fragments the risk picture into three files no one sees whole, trading one artifact problem for another and complicating any consolidated risk review.
