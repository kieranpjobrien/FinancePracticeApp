# Question

A PM inherits a construction project where the RAID log shows only three entries for a nine-month-old programme. The team privately says many issues get handled informally in corridors. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the informal culture since the team clearly resolves things and formal logging would only slow work down.
- **B**: Quietly populate the RAID log yourself based on what you pick up in stand-ups without disturbing the team's rhythm.
- **C**: Coach the team on why transparent logging protects everyone, then agree a simple capture habit they can sustain.
- **D**: Report the underlogging to the sponsor and ask for a directive requiring all items to be logged immediately.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to build transparent information flow through coaching, not command or silent capture. (C) addresses the behaviour while respecting team autonomy. (A) normalises the opacity that creates audit and risk gaps. (B) produces a log no one trusts or maintains. (D) is premature escalation that damages trust before coaching is tried.
