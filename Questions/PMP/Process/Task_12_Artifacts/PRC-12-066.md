# Question

A predictive infrastructure project reports green monthly, but EVM artifacts show CPI trending steadily from 1.02 down to 0.88 over six months. The sponsor has not questioned the green status. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the report green since CPI remains within broadly tolerable industry norms for this type of infrastructure work.
- **B**: Commission a new cost baseline that reflects current run rates so CPI recovers to a green reading for the sponsor.
- **C**: Ask finance whether the CPI calculation method could be adjusted to better represent the recent project performance data.
- **D**: Change the report to amber, show the CPI trend honestly, and present forecast completion with recommended corrective actions.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's transparency principle requires reports to reflect reality regardless of prior colour convention. (D) restores accurate reporting and triggers real corrective planning. (A) hides a clear negative trend behind thresholds. (B) is a rebaseline to mask performance, not to reflect a real scope change. (C) seeks a measurement change to disguise the data.
