# Question

A project running a predictive lifecycle for a regulated finance migration is performing well against its baseline, but the project manager notices the team repeats the same avoidable mistakes from phase to phase because lessons are only captured at the very end. The manager wants to borrow a single agile practice that would help without disrupting the predictive governance. Which practice is the most suitable to introduce?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the phase gates with sprint reviews so progress is inspected more often.
- **B**: Hold regular retrospectives during the project to inspect and adapt the team's way of working as it goes.
- **C**: Convert the requirements into a prioritised product backlog managed by a product owner.
- **D**: Abandon the baseline and move the migration to continuous flow with WIP limits.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Borrowing a practice means adding one technique that fits the gap without dismantling the governance that works. The gap here is delayed learning, and regular retrospectives capture and apply lessons continuously while leaving the predictive lifecycle intact. (B) targets exactly that. (A) tears out the regulated phase gates the migration depends on, a far larger change than the problem warrants. (C) restructures requirements management wholesale to solve a learning-cadence issue. (D) discards the baseline and governance entirely, an overhaul where a single targeted practice would suffice.
