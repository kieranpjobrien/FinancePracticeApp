# Question

A Scrum team faces an upcoming story that depends on an unfamiliar third-party API. The PO wants it in the next sprint. The developers say they cannot confidently estimate it without research first. What should the scrum master suggest?

## Options

- **A**: Have the developers commit a best-guess estimate to protect the product owner's preferred sprint plan and proceed.
- **B**: Move the story to the sprint after next so developers can research the API during the current sprint's slack time.
- **C**: Add a timeboxed research spike in the next sprint to learn the API, then plan the implementation story afterwards confidently.
- **D**: Ask the product owner to find an alternative requirement that avoids the third-party API altogether for this release.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI and agile practice handle uncertainty by timeboxed investigation before commitment. (C) does exactly that. (A) pretends to confidence the team does not have. (B) relies on undefined slack to do real discovery work. (D) avoids the requirement rather than reducing uncertainty about it.
