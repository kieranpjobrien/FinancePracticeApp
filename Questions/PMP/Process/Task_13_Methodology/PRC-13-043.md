# Question

A consumer electronics firm is launching a project to develop firmware for a new wearable. The technology is novel, customer requirements are poorly understood, and early experiments are expected to fail and inform direction. However, the silicon chip the firmware runs on is locked, with a fixed tape-out date eight months out that cannot move. A steering group asks the project manager which single factor should weigh most heavily when choosing the firmware team's delivery approach. Which factor should it be?

## Options

- **A**: The fixed tape-out date, because a hard external deadline means the approach must be plan-driven to protect the milestone.
- **B**: The degree of requirements and technical uncertainty, because high uncertainty favours an adaptive, experiment-driven approach for the firmware itself.
- **C**: The organisation's existing process maturity, because teams deliver best using the methods they already know.
- **D**: The steering group's reporting preference, because the chosen approach must produce the status formats leadership expects.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Suitability assessment weighs project and product characteristics first, and for the firmware the dominant characteristic is high uncertainty in both requirements and technology, which points to an adaptive, experiment-led approach. (B) names that driver. (A) confuses a delivery constraint with a methodology driver: the fixed date is real, but it shapes the schedule and scope strategy, not whether the volatile firmware work is done predictively. (C) lets familiarity override fit, which produces the wrong method when the work genuinely differs from past projects. (D) elevates a reporting convenience above the nature of the work, which is a governance concern layered on top, not the deciding factor.
