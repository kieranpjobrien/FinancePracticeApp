# Question

A government agency with a strongly hierarchical culture, annual fixed budgets approved by a finance committee, and a procurement model that pays suppliers against signed milestone documents wants its new case-management system delivered with "more agility". Early discovery work has shown that the caseworkers cannot yet specify large parts of what they need, and several requirements have already changed twice. The supplier's project manager must propose an approach that can actually function inside this environment from day one. What is the BEST approach to propose?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt full Scrum immediately across all teams, since the product uncertainty is the dominant factor and the organisation asked for agility.
- **B**: Deliver predictively against milestone documents as the procurement model requires, and treat the agility request as satisfied by more frequent status reporting.
- **C**: Use a hybrid approach: iterate on the uncertain product internally, while mapping iteration outputs onto the milestone-based payment and governance points the organisation requires.
- **D**: Defer any agile practices until the agency reforms its budgeting and procurement to remove the milestone-payment and annual-funding constraints.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Methodology choice must fit both the product's needs and the organisation's enterprise environmental factors; here the product wants iteration but funding and procurement are milestone-bound, so the approach must bridge the two rather than ignore either. (C) lets the team work iteratively where uncertainty lives while translating outputs into the documented milestones the payment and governance model demands. (A) imposes full Scrum onto a milestone-payment, hierarchical structure that cannot release funds or sign off work iteration by iteration, so it stalls at the first invoice. (B) honours procurement but discards the real requirement volatility, relabelling unchanged plan-driven delivery as agility through reporting alone. (D) makes progress hostage to organisational reform the project manager does not control, indefinitely deferring value. The discriminator is reconciling genuine product uncertainty with hard funding and procurement constraints, not letting one side override the other.
