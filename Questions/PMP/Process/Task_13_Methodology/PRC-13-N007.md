# Question

A manufacturing company's IT department has always delivered projects predictively. Senior leadership has directed that a new e-commerce platform be delivered "in an agile way" to get to market faster. The team has no agile experience, the finance function still requires an approved fixed annual budget, and procurement contracts with vendors are fixed-price. As project manager, what is the most appropriate approach to determining the methodology?

## Options

- **A**: Implement full Scrum immediately as directed, and let finance and procurement adapt their processes to the team's new cadence.
- **B**: Design a hybrid approach that uses agile delivery practices the team can adopt now while working within the existing budget and contract constraints, and plan to mature the agile capability over time.
- **C**: Tell leadership that agile is not feasible because the surrounding finance and procurement processes are incompatible, and proceed predictively.
- **D**: Outsource the build to an experienced agile vendor so the in-house constraints do not interfere with the agile way of working.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Methodology selection must account for organisational readiness and surrounding constraints, not just the desired label — a pragmatic hybrid lets the team begin adopting agile practices while honouring fixed budgets and contracts. (B) balances the leadership intent with real constraints and treats capability as something to grow. (A) ignores team inexperience and the hard finance/procurement boundaries, setting up failure. (C) over-rotates to "impossible" when a hybrid clearly bridges the gap, and dismisses a legitimate directive. (D) sidesteps the directive's intent to build internal speed and creates dependency without addressing the home environment.
