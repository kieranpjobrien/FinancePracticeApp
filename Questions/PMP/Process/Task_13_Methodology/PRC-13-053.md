# Question

A facilities team is relocating a corporate data centre over a single weekend. Every step is known, the sequence is rehearsed, the scope cannot change once the move begins, and the outcome must match the plan exactly with no opportunity to iterate. Which delivery approach is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Adaptive, so the team can change the plan as it learns during the move.
- **B**: Predictive, planning the move in full and executing strictly against the plan.
- **C**: Incremental, releasing parts of the data centre to users as each is moved.
- **D**: Iterative, repeating the move several times to refine the approach.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A fully understood, rehearsed, fixed-scope event with one chance to execute correctly is a predictive situation. (B) plans completely up front and executes against that plan, which is what a one-shot cutover requires. (A) invites mid-move changes where there is no room to experiment and high risk in deviating. (C) misreads the work: a data-centre cutover is not delivered in user-facing increments over time. (D) is impossible here, since the move cannot be repeated to refine it; it must succeed once.
