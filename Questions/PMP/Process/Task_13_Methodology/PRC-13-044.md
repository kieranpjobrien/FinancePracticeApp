# Question

A project manager is assessing whether a new logistics-optimisation initiative should lean predictive or adaptive. Which characteristics of the work would point towards an adaptive (agile) approach being the better fit? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Requirements are expected to emerge and change frequently as the business learns from early releases.
- **B**: The customer can be available regularly to clarify priorities and review increments.
- **C**: The scope is fixed by a fixed-price contract with detailed acceptance criteria agreed before work starts.
- **D**: Delivering partial functionality early provides real business value and useful feedback.
- **E**: Regulatory sign-off requires the full solution to be documented and approved before any of it is released.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Adaptive approaches suit work where requirements are volatile (A), the customer is engaged enough to steer frequent increments (B), and incremental delivery yields early value and feedback (D). (C) describes fixed scope agreed up front under a fixed-price contract, which is a classic predictive condition that constrains the freedom agile relies on. (E) requires the whole solution before any release, which removes the incremental delivery that makes agile valuable and points firmly to a predictive, plan-driven path. The split turns on which conditions enable iterative learning versus which demand up-front commitment.
