# Question

A predictive construction project has firm near-term design but the fit-out phase twelve months out has many open variables. The sponsor wants a full detailed schedule and budget approved today. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Produce a fully detailed schedule and budget now using best-guess assumptions so the sponsor gets the approval package requested today.
- **B**: Reject the request as unrealistic and wait until the fit-out variables resolve before presenting any detailed plan to sponsor.
- **C**: Move the whole project to an agile approach so forward planning is no longer expected of the team by sponsor.
- **D**: Use rolling-wave planning: detailed near-term, summary far-term, with clear commitment to plan in detail as variables resolve.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's rolling-wave planning applies progressive elaboration where uncertainty varies across phases. (D) gives the sponsor commitment at appropriate granularity. (A) fabricates precision and locks the team to assumptions. (B) refuses to provide a plan the sponsor reasonably needs. (C) reframes the whole project to avoid a planning technique designed for this case.
