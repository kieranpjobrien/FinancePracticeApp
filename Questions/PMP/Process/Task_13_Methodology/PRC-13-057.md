# Question

A start-up project plans to deliver a comprehensive "version 1" product in eight months. The PM notes the market window may shift quickly and competitors are moving fast. The founder insists the full feature set is non-negotiable. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the founder's mandate and deliver the full product in eight months as originally scoped and planned out.
- **B**: Deliver the full scope but split into two parallel streams to reduce time to market by using more people concurrently.
- **C**: Adopt MVP thinking: define the minimum valuable release to test the market, then iterate based on real customer feedback.
- **D**: Switch from predictive to agile without changing scope, believing the method itself will speed delivery to market.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's value-delivery principle prioritises early validated learning when market uncertainty is high. (C) reduces risk and accelerates learning. (A) ignores market volatility the PM has identified. (B) buys time but not learning. (D) adopts a method without adopting the thinking that makes it work.
