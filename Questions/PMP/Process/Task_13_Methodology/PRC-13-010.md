# Question

What does "tailoring" mean in the context of selecting a delivery approach?

## Options

- **A**: Adapting processes, tools, and practices so the methodology fits the specific project context.
- **B**: Choosing one of agile, waterfall, or hybrid for the project at the outset.
- **C**: Designing a unique methodology from scratch for each new project.
- **D**: Applying the organisation's standard methodology consistently across every project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI defines tailoring as adapting an established methodology to project context, not invention or rigid application. (A) captures that. (B) is methodology selection, which is one input to tailoring but not tailoring itself. (C) is needlessly bespoke and ignores reuse. (D) is the opposite of tailoring.
