# Question

A project manager is deciding whether an adaptive (agile) approach is suitable for an upcoming initiative. Which of the following project characteristics genuinely favour an adaptive approach over a predictive one? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Requirements are expected to emerge and change frequently as the market is explored.
- **B**: The customer can make a knowledgeable representative available to the team on a continuous basis.
- **C**: A regulator requires the complete, final design to be documented and approved before any build begins.
- **D**: Early, frequent delivery of working increments is valued more highly than a single large handover.
- **E**: The deliverable is a physical asset whose components cannot be cheaply reworked once produced.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Adaptive approaches thrive where requirements are volatile (A), where an engaged customer representative can steer continuously (B), and where incremental value delivery is prized over a big-bang release (D) — these are core suitability signals. (C) describes a constraint demanding full up-front specification and approval, which pushes towards predictive delivery. (E) describes high cost of change in physical components, which also favours plan-driven work because you cannot iterate cheaply. Picking C or E would mean reading the wording as "agile-friendly" without weighing the cost-of-change and approval constraints they actually impose.
