# Question

A team has been running Scrum for six months. Velocity is flat and quality is poor. Retrospectives generate the same action items quarter after quarter, but few of them get done. What should the project manager focus on FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Bring in an external Scrum trainer to refresh the team on the official ceremonies and accountabilities.
- **B**: Coach the team to reduce retro actions and assign clear owners and dates so they get delivered.
- **C**: Switch to Kanban so the team has fewer ceremonies to maintain and lighter process overhead.
- **D**: Roll back to a predictive approach since Scrum clearly is not working for this team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The team has retrospectives but no follow-through, which is "mechanical Scrum." (B) addresses the root cause — improvement requires action, not just discussion. (A) treats it as a knowledge gap when the symptoms point to execution. (C) changes framework rather than fixing the practice that already exists. (D) abandons agile when the implementation is the issue, not the methodology.
