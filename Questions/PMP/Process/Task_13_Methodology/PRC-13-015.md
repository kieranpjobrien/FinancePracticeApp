# Question

A Scrum team consistently fails to complete stories within the two-week sprint because most stories are too large. What should the scrum master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Extend the sprint length to four weeks so the larger stories can fit.
- **B**: Allow stories to roll over for as long as they need, since velocity will average out.
- **C**: Coach the team on splitting techniques in backlog refinement so stories are sized to fit a sprint.
- **D**: Switch the team from Scrum to Kanban since flow does not require timeboxing.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The root cause is story size, not the framework. (C) addresses it by building the team's capability to slice work — vertical splits, spike-then-implement, etc. (A) treats the symptom and reduces feedback frequency. (B) abandons the sprint goal mechanism that drives focus. (D) is a major change to make before trying the simpler fix.
