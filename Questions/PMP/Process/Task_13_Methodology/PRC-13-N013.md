# Question

A distributed agile team of five spends roughly forty-five minutes in a daily stand-up that routinely turns into detailed problem-solving, leaving members frustrated and the meeting overrunning. The Scrum Master wants to tailor the team's events so the daily coordination is effective. Which adjustment is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the daily stand-up with a written status update in the team channel to eliminate the meeting overhead entirely.
- **B**: Extend the daily stand-up to a formal hour so the detailed discussions that are clearly needed have enough time to conclude.
- **C**: Reduce the stand-up to twice weekly so the team spends less total time in synchronisation meetings.
- **D**: Keep the daily stand-up time-boxed to its coordination purpose and move detailed problem-solving to follow-up conversations among only those involved.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The daily event is for coordination and surfacing impediments, not deep problem-solving; the correct tailoring preserves its purpose and time-box while routing detail to the right, smaller forum. (D) keeps the value of daily alignment without dragging the whole team into every issue. (A) removes the team's synchronisation rhythm for distributed members who especially benefit from a brief live touchpoint. (B) institutionalises the very anti-pattern causing the frustration. (C) cuts coordination frequency below what a distributed team usually needs, trading one problem for poorer alignment.
