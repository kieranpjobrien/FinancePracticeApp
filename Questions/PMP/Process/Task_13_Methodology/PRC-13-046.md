# Question

A charity is digitising thirty years of paper case files. The activity is well understood and repetitive: scan a batch, index it, quality-check it, then publish it. Stakeholders want usable batches of records released to staff as soon as each batch is finished rather than waiting for the entire archive. Which lifecycle best matches this need?

## Options

- **A**: An iterative lifecycle, refining the same set of records repeatedly until quality is acceptable.
- **B**: An incremental lifecycle, delivering completed batches of records to users as each is finished.
- **C**: A predictive lifecycle with a single delivery of the whole archive at the end.
- **D**: An adaptive lifecycle driven by frequently changing requirements for the records.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The work is well understood and the value comes from releasing finished pieces progressively, which is the definition of an incremental lifecycle. (B) delivers completed batches as they are done, matching the stakeholders' request. (A) implies repeatedly reworking the same items to improve them, but the records do not need refining once correctly processed, they need releasing. (C) withholds everything until the end, the opposite of what stakeholders asked for. (D) presumes volatile requirements, yet the process here is stable and repetitive, so adaptivity adds nothing.
