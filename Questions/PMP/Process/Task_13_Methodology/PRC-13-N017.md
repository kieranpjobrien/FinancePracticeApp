# Question

Midway through a year-long programme run by several interacting sub-teams under an established hybrid approach, the teams agree that a handful of their adopted practices add little value while a few painful gaps remain. The next phase boundary, where the programme already plans a formal re-baseline, is four months away. The project manager wants to evolve the way of working responsibly. What is the BEST way to adjust the methodology during execution?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the changes for the planned re-baseline in four months so they are introduced once, together, through the programme's existing change point rather than piecemeal.
- **B**: Ask each sub-team to drop the low-value practices and close the gaps in the way that best fits its own context, since the teams know their own pain best.
- **C**: Tackle the most painful gap first with a single substantial process change agreed across all teams now, then assess before touching anything else.
- **D**: Use the teams' regular retrospectives to inspect the practices, agree small targeted changes that hold across the interacting teams, and adapt incrementally while monitoring the effect.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tailoring is continuous: inspect-and-adapt through retrospectives lets the teams improve the process with small, evidence-based adjustments while the work is live, and crucially the changes are agreed to hold across the interacting teams so consistency is preserved. (D) evolves the approach responsibly, keeps each change observable, and protects the seams between teams. (A) is tempting because the re-baseline is a natural batching point, but it knowingly carries four more months of waste and unmet gaps when the changes are small and reversible enough to start now. (B) gives each team autonomy that sounds empowering, yet uncoordinated local divergence breaks the shared practices the interacting teams rely on. (C) front-loads one large change across all teams at once, importing more disruption and risk than small incremental moves, before any of it has been validated. The discriminator is not "improve the process" — every option does that — but doing it incrementally while holding the teams aligned.
