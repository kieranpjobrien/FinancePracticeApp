# Question

Six weeks into a predictive project to build a customer portal, the market shifts and the sponsor now wants frequent releases and the freedom to re-prioritise features every month based on uptake. The baseline scope is already approved and partly built. A team member argues the team should simply switch to Scrum next week. Before changing the approach, what is the MOST important thing for the project manager to do?

## Options

- **A**: Switch to Scrum immediately, because responding to change is more valuable than following a plan.
- **B**: Assess the impact of changing the approach on the existing baseline, governance, and contracts, and take the change through the agreed change process.
- **C**: Refuse the change, since the scope baseline is approved and a portal should be finished as planned.
- **D**: Keep the predictive plan but add a monthly status meeting where the sponsor can comment on progress.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Changing delivery approach mid-project is itself a significant change with consequences for the approved baseline, governance arrangements, and any contracts, so it must be assessed and run through change control rather than enacted unilaterally. (B) does this. (A) flips to Scrum overnight without considering the commitments already in place, mistaking an agile value for a licence to skip impact analysis. (C) ignores a genuine, justified market-driven need and rigidly defends a baseline that no longer serves the business. (D) adds a token feedback meeting that does not give the sponsor the re-prioritisation and frequent releases actually being requested, so it fails to meet the need.
