# Question

A pharmaceutical company is building a patient-facing mobile app that must integrate with a regulated, slow-changing clinical records backend. The app's features are expected to evolve rapidly based on user feedback, while the backend interface is fixed by a validated specification that cannot change without a lengthy revalidation. The sponsor has asked the project manager to recommend a delivery approach. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: A predictive approach for the whole programme, because the regulated backend dictates the constraints and a single governance model reduces audit complexity.
- **B**: A hybrid approach: iterative, incremental delivery for the app layer and a predictive, plan-driven approach for the backend integration.
- **C**: A fully agile approach across both components so the team maintains one cadence and avoids the overhead of running two methods.
- **D**: Defer the decision and run a two-sprint experiment on the app before committing to any methodology for either component.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The two components have genuinely different characteristics: the app has high requirements volatility (suited to iterative/incremental agile) while the backend is stable and regulation-bound (suited to predictive). (B) tailors the lifecycle to each, which is the essence of selecting fit-for-purpose methods. (A) forces a heavyweight model onto the volatile app layer and slows learning. (C) ignores that you cannot iterate freely against a validated, change-controlled interface. (D) is reasonable as a tactic but evades the recommendation the sponsor actually asked for, and the backend's nature is already well understood.
