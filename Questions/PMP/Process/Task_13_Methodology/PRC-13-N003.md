# Question

A project manager joins an organisation that mandates a stage-gate methodology with formal phase reviews for all projects above a cost threshold. Her project is a small data-pipeline build with stable requirements and a co-located team that could comfortably deliver in short increments. She believes a lighter approach would be faster. What should she do first?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt the lighter incremental approach and document the rationale, since tailoring to project characteristics is the project manager's responsibility.
- **B**: Review the organisation's tailoring guidelines and governance policy to understand what adaptation is permitted within the mandated methodology.
- **C**: Run the project under the mandated stage-gate methodology as written, because deviating from organisational standards creates compliance risk.
- **D**: Raise a change request to the PMO to have the methodology mandate waived for projects below a complexity threshold.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tailoring must respect the organisation's governance framework and tailoring guidelines, which often permit adaptation within bounds — so the first move is to learn what is allowed before acting. (B) gathers the constraints that determine which of the other actions is even legitimate. (A) presumes unilateral authority the PM may not have and risks a governance breach. (C) over-complies without checking whether tailoring is already sanctioned, potentially wasting effort. (D) jumps to a formal waiver before establishing whether the existing policy already accommodates a lighter cadence.
