# Question

A project manager is designing a hybrid approach for a programme that pairs an exploratory product build with a fixed hardware procurement. To create a coherent hybrid rather than a confused mix, which design choices are sound? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Apply adaptive, iteration-based delivery to the uncertain product build and plan-driven, milestone-based delivery to the fixed hardware procurement.
- **B**: Define clear interfaces and integration points where the agile and predictive streams must align, with agreed hand-off criteria.
- **C**: Force the hardware procurement into two-week sprints so both streams share an identical cadence regardless of the work's nature.
- **D**: Agree shared governance, reporting, and a combined risk view so leadership sees one coherent programme across both streams.
- **E**: Keep the two streams entirely independent with no synchronisation, so neither constrains the other in any way.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

A coherent hybrid assigns each stream the approach that fits its nature (A), explicitly manages the seams between streams with defined interfaces and hand-offs (B), and unifies governance and reporting so leadership sees one programme (D). (C) imposes an inappropriate sprint cadence on procurement work that does not benefit from it, which is mixing methods for appearance rather than fit. (E) ignores the real integration dependencies between the streams, and a hardware delivery date will inevitably constrain the build. Choosing C or E would mean mistaking "uniform" or "fully decoupled" for "well designed".
