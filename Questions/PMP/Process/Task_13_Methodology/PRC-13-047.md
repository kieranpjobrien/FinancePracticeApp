# Question

A support team runs Kanban with no WIP limits. Lead times have crept from three days to fifteen over six months and stakeholders are frustrated. The team says WIP limits would feel restrictive and unnecessary. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Measure lead time and cycle time, show the team the data, and coach them to set WIP limits to test effect.
- **B**: Set WIP limits yourself based on team size since the team is unlikely to adopt any limit voluntarily from a suggestion.
- **C**: Switch the team from Kanban to Scrum so sprint commitment naturally constrains how much work they can take on.
- **D**: Add more team members so the current throughput can keep up with the volume of work stakeholders are requesting.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's empirical and servant-leader principles use data to help teams discover the right constraint themselves. (A) coaches through evidence. (B) imposes a limit without ownership, which rarely holds. (C) changes the method rather than fixing the flow issue. (D) scales the team before fixing the underlying flow problem.
