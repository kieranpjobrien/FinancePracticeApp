# Question

An insurer is launching a programme with eight feature teams that must deliver one integrated claims platform on a shared cadence, with significant cross-team dependencies and a single combined release every quarter. Each team already runs Scrum well. Leadership wants the teams to keep their autonomy but needs coordinated planning and a unified backlog of cross-cutting work. Which approach is the BEST fit?

## Options

- **A**: Leave each team on independent Scrum and resolve cross-team dependencies through a weekly Scrum of Scrums only.
- **B**: Adopt a scaling framework that coordinates multiple teams around a shared cadence and an aligned backlog while preserving team-level Scrum.
- **C**: Merge the eight teams into one large Scrum team with a single product owner to remove the coordination problem.
- **D**: Switch the whole programme to a predictive plan so that the cross-team dependencies can be sequenced in one master schedule.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Eight interdependent teams delivering one product on a shared quarterly cadence is precisely the problem scaling frameworks are designed to solve, coordinating teams while leaving each team's Scrum intact. (B) matches the need for aligned planning and a shared backlog without stripping autonomy. (A) relies on a single lightweight forum to manage heavy, structural dependencies across eight teams, which will not scale. (C) ignores that a Scrum team has an effective size limit; one team of that size is unworkable. (D) abandons the agile delivery the teams already do well and that the product's nature still warrants, trading proven team practice for a master schedule.
