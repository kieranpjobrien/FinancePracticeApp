# Question

A project manager is assessing a new initiative to decide whether a predictive approach is the appropriate fit before committing the team to a way of working. Several features of the initiative are already known. Which TWO of them most strongly support a predictive approach rather than an adaptive one? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: The team has delivered three near-identical installations before and the steps, durations and acceptance tests are already known from those.
- **B**: A fixed go-live date is set, and the sponsor has asked for a detailed end-to-end schedule they can track progress against.
- **C**: Altering the deliverable after the authority's sign-off forces the whole approval to be re-run, so the design is locked before build starts.
- **D**: The customer is unsure what the end users actually need and expects priorities to shift as early versions are trialled.
- **E**: Funding is committed in full for the year, but the steering group reviews and may redirect scope at each quarterly gate.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

Predictive fits where the scope is stable and the path is genuinely known, so a baseline can be set up front and held. (A) is exactly that: proven, repeatable work whose steps and acceptance are already established, leaving little to discover. (C) is a locked, sign-off-bound design where change is deliberately expensive, which rewards detailed up-front planning over continual change. (B) is the strongest distractor — a fixed date and a desire to track a schedule feel predictive, but both adaptive and predictive work set dates and report progress; wanting a schedule does not make the work low-uncertainty, so this alone does not point to predictive. (D) is a clear adaptive signal: unknown user needs and shifting priorities demand feedback and iteration. (E) describes full funding but periodic redirection of scope, which actually favours an adaptive response to each gate, not a fixed baseline. The discriminator is that only stability of the work itself (A) and locked conformance (C) point to predictive; a wish for dates and schedules (B) is lifecycle-neutral and is the trap.
