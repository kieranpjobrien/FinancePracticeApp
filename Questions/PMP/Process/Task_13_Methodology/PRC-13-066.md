# Question

Eight weeks into a predictive project, a new sponsor wants to switch the delivery approach to Scrum because "agile is faster". The team has detailed plans, baselines, and a signed business case. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Change to Scrum immediately as the new sponsor has directed and re-plan the remaining work accordingly.
- **B**: Refuse the change on the basis that mid-project methodology switches always destroy predictive progress already made.
- **C**: Run a short assessment of work remaining, risks, benefits, and tailoring options, then present options to the sponsor.
- **D**: Keep working under the predictive plan until the project closes and implement agile on the next project instead.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects methodology decisions to follow structured analysis of context rather than directives. (C) gives the sponsor an informed choice. (A) obeys a directive without analysis and will disrupt work in flight. (B) reflexively refuses without assessing whether partial adoption is sensible. (D) defers a legitimate question the sponsor has raised for good reasons.
