# Question

What is rolling wave planning?

## Options

- **A**: Planning the near-term work in detail and elaborating future work progressively as more becomes known.
- **B**: Producing a fully detailed plan at project initiation that does not change.
- **C**: Planning a single iteration at a time without longer horizon visibility.
- **D**: Continuously replanning the whole project at the start of each phase.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Rolling wave is the textbook progressive elaboration technique — detailed plans for the next wave, higher-level plans for later waves. (A) is the definition. (B) is the opposite — comprehensive upfront planning. (C) loses the longer horizon. (D) describes wholesale replanning, not the staged elaboration rolling wave actually uses.
