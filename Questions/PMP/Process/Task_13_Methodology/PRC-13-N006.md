# Question

A contractor is delivering a distribution warehouse under a fixed-price contract with an approved design, issued permits, and a build sequence proven across many similar projects. One element is genuinely unsettled: the client has not finalised the automated racking and conveyor fit-out and wants to refine that part with the operations team as they observe how the space comes together. The project manager is setting the delivery approach. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Run the entire project predictively against the contract baseline and handle any racking refinements through the formal change control process as they arise.
- **B**: Plan the structural build predictively while delivering the racking and conveyor fit-out through short iterative cycles with the operations team, integrating the two streams at defined points.
- **C**: Keep the whole project predictive but defer baselining the racking scope until the structure is sufficiently complete for the operations team to walk the space.
- **D**: Move the whole project to an adaptive approach so the operations team can continuously reprioritise both the structure and the fit-out as they learn.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The project has two genuinely different parts: a stable, contractually fixed structural build (predictive) and a deliberately exploratory fit-out the client wants to refine through observation (iterative) — matching each part to a fit-for-purpose approach with managed integration points is the essence of tailoring. (B) does exactly that. (A) is defensible but forces every fit-out refinement through change control, treating planned discovery as exceptions and adding friction to work that is meant to evolve. (C) is a sensible rolling-wave tactic for the structure alone, yet it still plans the fit-out as a single late baseline rather than giving it the iterative collaboration the client explicitly wants. (D) applies adaptiveness to a structural build that is fixed by contract and proven by repetition, where it adds risk and no value.
