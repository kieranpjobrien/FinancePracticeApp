# Question

A regional water utility is replacing a pumping station's control system. The engineering specification is signed off, fixed by a safety case, and the scope is unlikely to change before commissioning. The regulator requires a documented plan against which delivery is audited. The newly assigned project manager is deciding how to run the work. Which delivery approach fits this situation BEST?

## Options

- **A**: An adaptive approach, releasing control logic in two-week increments so the operators can give feedback continuously.
- **B**: A predictive approach, planning the work in detail up front and managing delivery against a baseline.
- **C**: An iterative approach, repeatedly refining the control design through successive prototypes until the operators are satisfied.
- **D**: A hybrid approach, with the design predictive and the build delivered in adaptive sprints.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When requirements are stable, the scope is fixed by a safety case, and an auditor needs a baseline to measure against, a predictive approach is the natural fit. (B) plans up front and tracks against the baseline, exactly what the regulator wants. (A) and (C) introduce iteration and feedback loops that add no value when the specification is already fixed and cannot change without revalidating the safety case. (D) layers adaptive build onto stable, signed-off requirements, creating ceremony and change overhead without a corresponding benefit.
