# Question

A start-up is building a brand-new mobile game and has no clear idea yet which mechanics players will enjoy. The founders expect to test ideas, see what engages users, and change direction often based on what they learn. They want a delivery approach that embraces this constant change. Which approach fits best?

## Options

- **A**: A predictive approach with the full game scope defined and signed off before development starts.
- **B**: An adaptive (agile) approach that delivers in short iterations and adjusts based on player feedback.
- **C**: A single large release at the end so players experience the complete game at once.
- **D**: A fixed-scope contract with the development studio specifying every feature in advance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When the product is exploratory and the team expects to change direction frequently based on user feedback, an adaptive approach is the natural fit. (B) delivers in short iterations and adapts to what players reveal, matching the founders' stated way of working. (A) demands full scope sign-off up front, which is impossible when no one yet knows what will engage players. (C) withholds all feedback until the end, defeating the learn-as-you-go intent. (D) locks every feature into a contract before the learning has happened, the opposite of embracing change.
