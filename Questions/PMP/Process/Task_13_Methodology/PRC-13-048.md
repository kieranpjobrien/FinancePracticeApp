# Question

A portfolio director is coaching new project managers on selecting a development approach to match the work. For each project situation below, match it to the development approach that fits it most naturally.

## Items

- **1**: Construction of a bridge to a fixed engineering design, scope unlikely to change, delivered in one go at the end
- **2**: A user-interface concept that must be reworked through several rounds until the design is right, with one final release
- **3**: A loyalty platform whose features are delivered to customers in successive working releases as each is completed
- **4**: A research-driven product where requirements emerge from frequent customer feedback and change often

## Matches

- **A**: Iterative
- **B**: Incremental
- **C**: Predictive
- **D**: Adaptive (agile)

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

A fixed-design bridge delivered once with stable scope (1) is predictive (C). A single deliverable refined through repeated rounds to get the design right (2) is iterative (A), since iteration improves the same item rather than adding new ones. A platform whose finished features are released to customers progressively (3) is incremental (B), because each increment is usable on delivery. A product with emergent, frequently changing requirements driven by feedback (4) is adaptive/agile (D). The distinction hinges on whether the work refines one thing repeatedly, builds usable pieces successively, plans once up front, or responds continuously to change.
