# Question

A client insists on a fixed-price contract for software development with a deadline in eight months. Requirements are clearly volatile. The delivery team wants to work agile. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Accept fixed-price on fixed scope, since commercial stability matters more than flexibility during volatile requirements phases.
- **B**: Refuse fixed-price because agile methods are not commercially compatible with fixed-price contracts under any circumstances.
- **C**: Structure a fixed-price contract with a fixed budget and timebox, scope prioritised iteratively, and defined governance checkpoints.
- **D**: Accept the client's terms now and raise change requests for scope flexibility whenever requirements drift during delivery.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's tailoring principle adapts contracting to fit uncertainty while respecting commercial needs. (C) fixes cost and time but flexes scope, which is how agile and fixed-price co-exist. (A) assumes fixed scope works when requirements are known to be volatile. (B) treats contract structure as binary when hybrid structures exist. (D) accepts a contract destined for conflict and leans on change control to repair it.
