# Question

A Scrum team's stakeholders keep asking "when will feature X be done". The team currently plans only one sprint ahead and cannot answer. The scrum master wants to respect the agile principle of welcoming change. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain a prioritised backlog plus rolling release forecast using velocity, with explicit uncertainty ranges for each feature.
- **B**: Tell stakeholders that agile does not allow forward commitments and ask them to wait for each sprint review to see.
- **C**: Switch to waterfall so the team can provide stakeholders with the date-certain forecasts they are asking for confidently.
- **D**: Have the product owner pick one date per feature and treat it as a commitment regardless of emerging sprint realities.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI and modern agile practice forecast with explicit uncertainty rather than refusing to forecast. (A) does this and preserves adaptability. (B) misrepresents agile as anti-forecasting. (C) changes the method to solve a forecasting skill gap. (D) produces false precision the team cannot support.
