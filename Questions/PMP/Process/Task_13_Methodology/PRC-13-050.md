# Question

A Scrum team's retrospectives produce the same action items every sprint, and nothing changes. The scrum master has been documenting the repeats diligently for four months. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Stop running retrospectives until the team demonstrates genuine motivation to act on items they themselves raise.
- **B**: Escalate the stalled retrospectives to the product owner and ask them to drive accountability on action items.
- **C**: Facilitate a retrospective on the retrospective itself, exploring why actions do not land, and adjust the format.
- **D**: Replace the scrum master with someone more senior who can enforce action completion between sprints more effectively.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's empirical and servant-leader approach inspects the process itself when improvement stalls. (C) brings the team's own insight to the pattern. (A) removes the only mechanism the team has to improve. (B) outsources team ownership to the product owner. (D) personalises a systemic problem.
