# Question

A defence supplier has won a contract with two clauses: deliverables must pass formal milestone reviews tied to staged payments, and the customer wants to see and influence working software every month. The development work itself involves substantial technical uncertainty. The project manager must design an approach that honours both contractual obligations and the uncertain build. Which design BEST satisfies all three pressures?

## Options

- **A**: Run pure Scrum and report sprint outcomes at each milestone, treating the milestone reviews as a formality.
- **B**: Run a pure predictive plan with monthly demonstrations of whatever is complete against the baseline.
- **C**: Deliver the build in monthly iterations that produce working software, and map iteration outputs to the contractual milestone reviews and payment gates.
- **D**: Ask the customer to drop the milestone-and-payment clauses so the team can work in a single continuous flow.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The situation demands iterative working software (for uncertainty and monthly customer influence) wrapped in predictive governance (for milestone reviews and staged payments), which is a deliberate hybrid. (C) builds iteratively yet ties iteration outputs to the contractual gates, satisfying all three pressures coherently. (A) dismisses contractually binding reviews as a formality, exposing the supplier to payment and compliance risk. (B) honours the contract but loses the genuine monthly steering and the responsiveness the uncertain build needs. (D) tries to remove a fixed contractual constraint rather than designing within it, which is rarely available and sidesteps the actual design problem.
