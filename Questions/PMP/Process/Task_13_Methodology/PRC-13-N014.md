# Question

A project manager is determining whether a new initiative should be delivered predictively, with an adaptive approach, or as a hybrid. Which factors are sound, primary drivers of that decision? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The degree of uncertainty and expected volatility in the product requirements.
- **B**: The organisation's culture, governance constraints, and approved tailoring guidelines.
- **C**: The methodology used by the last project the organisation delivered successfully.
- **D**: The team's experience and the availability of an engaged customer or product owner.
- **E**: The delivery approach named in the sponsor's standard project status template.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Sound selection weighs the volatility of the requirements (A), the organisational and governance environment the project must operate within, including any sanctioned tailoring (B), and the team's capability together with how engaged the customer can be (D) — these directly determine which approach will fit. (C) is the availability heuristic: the last project's method may have suited a different problem, so copying it without testing fit is a bias, not a driver — it is informative context at best. (E) confuses a reporting artefact with a delivery decision; a status template can be adjusted to whatever approach is chosen and must not dictate it. Selecting C or E means anchoring on precedent or paperwork instead of the project's actual characteristics.
