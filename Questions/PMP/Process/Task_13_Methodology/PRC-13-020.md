# Question

Six Scrum teams at the same company work on a connected product family. Each team's velocity is healthy, but cross-team integration regularly slips and rework is mounting. What should leadership do?

## Options

- **A**: Combine the six teams into one large team to remove cross-team handoffs.
- **B**: Have each team narrow its scope so it has fewer dependencies on the others.
- **C**: Adopt a scaling framework (SAFe, LeSS, Nexus) that adds explicit coordination ceremonies and dependency management across teams.
- **D**: Move the integration work to a dedicated platform team and let the feature teams stay independent.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The problem is cross-team coordination, which is exactly what scaling frameworks exist to solve. (C) introduces shared planning and integration mechanics without breaking team-level agility. (A) creates an oversized team that loses small-team benefits. (B) treats integration as undesirable rather than as work to be coordinated. (D) creates a downstream bottleneck and hides cross-team dependencies in a queue.
