# Question

A newly forming Scrum team wants to start sprinting immediately. The PM notes they have no product backlog, no definition of done, no working agreements, and no shared understanding of the product vision. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Run a short enabling iteration to establish vision, backlog, DoD, and working agreements before the first value sprint.
- **B**: Start Sprint 1 now and develop backlog, DoD, and working agreements inside the first sprint alongside delivery work.
- **C**: Spend six weeks on detailed upfront requirements before any sprinting starts, so the team has full clarity from the outset.
- **D**: Have the scrum master write the DoD, vision, and working agreements alone so the team can begin sprinting uninterrupted tomorrow.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's agile guidance treats a short enabling iteration as legitimate preparation for value sprinting. (A) balances preparation with speed to delivery. (B) overloads the first sprint and produces weak foundations. (C) reverts to waterfall upfront analysis. (D) denies the team ownership of its own working norms.
