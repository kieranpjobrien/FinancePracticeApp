# Question

A Scrum team's product owner is a business executive available only two hours per fortnight. Backlog grooming stalls, priorities shift unpredictably, and sprint goals often miss the mark. What should the scrum master recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Have the scrum master act as the product owner for day-to-day decisions while the executive remains titular PO formally.
- **B**: Escalate to the executive asking for a dedicated or proxy product owner empowered to make backlog decisions daily now.
- **C**: Let the team choose priorities themselves between executive meetings and seek confirmation retrospectively each fortnight with the executive.
- **D**: Extend the sprint length to four weeks so the executive's limited availability lines up with a single sprint boundary.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and Scrum place backlog accountability with a truly available product owner or empowered proxy. (B) addresses the role gap directly. (A) creates a conflict of role for the scrum master. (C) lets priorities drift without authority and then rationalises them. (D) accommodates the scarcity rather than fixing it.
