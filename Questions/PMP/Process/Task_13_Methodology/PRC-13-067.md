# Question

A portfolio director is overseeing four projects, each with a different delivery profile. For each project situation, match it to the development approach that best fits its dominant characteristics.

## Items

- **1**: A pharmaceutical validation study whose scope and acceptance steps are fixed by regulation and signed off before work begins.
- **2**: A new consumer app whose feature set is uncertain and whose customer wants to inspect working software every two weeks and re-steer.
- **3**: An enterprise data-platform migration with a stable, fully specified target state that the business wants delivered in usable stages to spread disruption.
- **4**: A complex product where both the requirements and the technical solution are poorly understood and expected to be discovered through repeated trials.

## Matches

- **A**: Predictive
- **B**: Agile (iterative, feedback-driven)
- **C**: Incremental
- **D**: Iterative (refine through experimentation)

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Each profile is defined by a single dominant driver. (1) is fixed and pre-approved by regulation, the classic case for a predictive baseline (A). (2) pairs uncertain scope with frequent inspection and re-steering, the defining conditions for an agile, feedback-driven approach (B). (3) has a fully specified, stable target but wants delivery in usable stages to spread disruption, which is incremental delivery of known scope (C) — not agile, because the requirements are not uncertain. (4) has both requirements and solution unknown and expects discovery through repeated trials, the signature of an iterative refine-by-experiment approach (D). The trap is conflating incremental (staged delivery of known scope) with iterative (repeated refinement of unknown scope) and lumping both under "agile"; the match turns on whether uncertainty or staging is the real driver.
