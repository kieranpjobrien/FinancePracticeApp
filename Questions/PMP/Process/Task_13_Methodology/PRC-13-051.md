# Question

A project manager is tailoring the delivery approach for a new initiative and wants to base the decision on the right factors. Which considerations are legitimately part of tailoring the development approach and practices to the situation? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The size and complexity of the product and the rate at which its requirements are likely to change.
- **B**: The degree of regulatory oversight and the documentation that compliance demands.
- **C**: The personal preference of the project manager for whichever method they enjoy running most.
- **D**: The organisational culture, existing process assets, and the team's experience with candidate approaches.
- **E**: The desire to appear modern by adopting agile regardless of whether the work suits it.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Tailoring weighs the nature of the product and its requirements volatility (A), the regulatory and documentation environment (B), and organisational culture, process assets, and team capability (D). These are project, product, and organisational factors that genuinely shape fit. (C) substitutes the project manager's enjoyment for evidence about the work, which is not a tailoring factor. (E) chases the appearance of being modern rather than fit-for-purpose delivery, and adopting agile where the work does not suit it harms outcomes. Sound tailoring is driven by characteristics of the work and its environment, not by preference or fashion.
