# Question

A project manager on a municipal library construction project has drafted the scope, schedule, cost, quality, resource, communications, risk, procurement, and stakeholder plans, plus the three baselines. A junior coordinator asks what the integrated project management plan actually is, beyond this stack of documents. What is the most accurate description for the PM to give?

## Options

- **A**: It is the consolidated plan that brings the subsidiary plans and baselines together so they are consistent and describe one coherent approach to executing and controlling the project.
- **B**: It is the scope baseline plus the schedule, since those two define what will be built and when.
- **C**: It is whichever subsidiary plan the sponsor considers most important for this particular project.
- **D**: It is the project charter expanded with more detail in each knowledge area.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The integrated project management plan is the consolidated document that unifies the subsidiary plans and baselines into one consistent, coherent approach to executing and controlling the work. (A) captures this. (B) names only two components and omits the rest, mistaking a part for the whole. (C) reduces an integrated plan to a single favoured plan, which defeats integration. (D) confuses the charter — which authorises the project and stays high-level — with the detailed, integrated management plan that conforms to it.
