# Question

What is the relationship between the project charter and the project management plan?

## Options

- **A**: They are essentially the same document presented at different points in the project's life cycle.
- **B**: The charter authorises the project and PM authority; the plan defines how it will be executed and controlled.
- **C**: The charter is created by the PM whilst the project management plan is approved by the sponsor directly.
- **D**: In agile delivery, the project charter completely replaces the need for a project management plan.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The charter is the authorisation; the plan is the execution playbook. The charter is an input to plan development. (A) confuses two distinct artefacts. (C) inverts ownership — the sponsor authorises the charter; the PM owns the plan. (D) is wrong; agile projects still require both authorisation and an integrated plan, even if formats differ.
