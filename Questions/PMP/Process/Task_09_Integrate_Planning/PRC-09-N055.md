# Question

A project manager joining a regulated medical-device manufacturer is integrating the planning for a new infusion pump. The organisation has a mandated stage-gate process and a quality management system, while the engineering group strongly prefers iterative prototyping. The PM must produce one plan that satisfies both. What should the PM do when integrating the planning?

## Options

- **A**: Tailor the planning so iterative prototyping runs inside each mandated stage, with the quality system's evidence produced from the prototype cycles and reviewed at the existing gates.
- **B**: Tailor the planning so each prototyping iteration is treated as its own mini stage-gate, replacing the organisation's gate sequence with an iteration-driven one that the quality team reviews per iteration.
- **C**: Run the prototyping freely up to the first formal gate, then switch the project to a fully predictive, gate-controlled approach for the remaining design and verification.
- **D**: Keep the mandated gates and quality system as the plan of record and let the engineers prototype informally between gates, outside the controlled plan, to satisfy their preference.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tailoring integrates the two by fitting iterative prototyping *within* the organisation's existing gates and quality system, so the prototype cycles generate the controlled evidence the gates review — one plan honouring both the regulatory environment and the way the team works best (A). (B) tailors in the wrong direction by overwriting the organisation's mandated gate structure with an iteration cadence, which a regulated stage-gate regime does not permit the PM to discard. (C) confines iteration to an early sandbox and abandons it precisely where design still benefits, treating prototyping as a throwaway phase rather than an integrated practice. (D) lets prototyping happen outside the plan of record, so the team's real working method is uncontrolled and its outputs are not captured as quality evidence — coordination by exclusion, not integration.
