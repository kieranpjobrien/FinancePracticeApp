# Question

Functional managers have submitted resource estimates for the same project, but each used different assumptions — some assumed 100% allocation, others assumed shared resourcing at 40-60%. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Take the most conservative number from each functional manager to stay on the safe side.
- **B**: Average the submissions to balance the differing approaches across the functional managers.
- **C**: Accept the estimates and reconcile resource conflicts during execution as they arise on the project.
- **D**: Publish common planning assumptions (allocation, hours, calendar) and request re-estimation on that basis.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

You cannot meaningfully integrate estimates produced under different assumptions; the right step is to harmonise the assumptions and re-collect. (A) and (B) combine non-comparable inputs and make planning numbers worse, not better. (C) defers a planning problem into execution where it costs more.
