# Question

A hybrid utilities programme has its schedule plan built around senior engineer availability that the resource plan does not confirm. The resource manager has privately told the PM the engineers will not be available as assumed. The PM is finalising the integrated plan. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Build the resource plan around the schedule plan's assumed engineer availability regardless of reality.
- **B**: Reconcile schedule and resource plans together, adjusting the schedule to reflect actual resource availability.
- **C**: Keep the existing schedule and mark the resource risk as high in the risk register for later management.
- **D**: Escalate the conflict to the sponsor and let the sponsor direct which plan should govern the programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Integrated planning reconciles schedule with actual resource availability; a schedule built on unavailable resources is a known-to-fail plan. (A) fabricates availability that does not exist. (C) documents a problem while committing to a plan built on its unreality. (D) escalates a reconciliation the PM should have performed as part of integration.
