# Question

A project manager is performing a final integration review of the project management plan for a hospital records-system migration before the approval gate. Which checks are essential to confirm the plan is genuinely integrated rather than merely a bundle of separate documents? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Each subsidiary plan has been signed off independently by its own knowledge-area lead.
- **B**: The scope, schedule, and cost baselines are consistent with one another and with the WBS.
- **C**: Assumptions and constraints used in one subsidiary plan do not contradict those in another.
- **D**: The risk responses are reflected in the schedule reserves, cost contingency, and resource plans.
- **E**: The project charter has been re-approved by the sponsor to match the latest plan.

## Answer

B, C, D

## Explanation

Genuine integration means the baselines reconcile to the same WBS (B), cross-plan assumptions and constraints are coherent (C), and risk responses have been propagated into the schedule, cost, and resource plans (D) — these tests confirm the parts actually fit together. (A) confirms only that each part was approved in isolation, which is the opposite of an integration check. (E) is incorrect because the charter authorises the project and is not re-approved to track the evolving plan; the plan conforms to the charter, not the reverse.
