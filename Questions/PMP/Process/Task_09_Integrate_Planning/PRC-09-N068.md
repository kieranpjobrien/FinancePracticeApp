# Question

Midway through integrating the subsidiary plans for an ERP implementation, the finance stakeholder requests an additional reporting module. The scope, schedule, and cost plans are drafted but not yet baselined. A team member suggests the PM simply update the scope document to include the module and move on. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Assess the module's impact across scope, schedule, cost, and resources together, then reflect a consistent change in all affected plans before baselining.
- **B**: Add the module to the scope document only, because the integrated change control process does not apply until the baseline is set.
- **C**: Reject the request and tell the finance stakeholder to submit it as a change after the baseline is approved.
- **D**: Add the module to scope and schedule but hold the cost impact until the budget is finalised separately.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Even before baselining, integrating the plans means any accepted change must be carried through every affected subsidiary plan so they stay mutually consistent. (A) assesses cross-cutting impact and updates all affected plans coherently, which is the integration discipline. (B) updates one plan in isolation and leaves schedule and cost out of step — the very inconsistency integration exists to prevent — and misstates that nothing matters pre-baseline. (C) needlessly defers a reasonable pre-baseline refinement and treats planning as closed when it is still open. (D) partially propagates the change but deliberately omits the cost dimension, producing a budget that no longer matches the agreed scope.
