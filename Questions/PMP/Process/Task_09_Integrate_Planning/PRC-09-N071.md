# Question

On an agile SaaS analytics product, three feature teams maintain their own backlogs and a shared release plan targets a quarterly launch. During integrated release planning, Team A's backlog assumes a new data-pipeline service that Team B has scheduled to deliver, but Team B's most recent increment plan has deprioritised that service in favour of higher-value reporting work. Both teams' plans are individually justified by their product owners. What is the PM's most appropriate action?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a cross-team session to surface the dependency, expose the trade-off to both product owners, and have them re-sequence so the shared release plan stays achievable.
- **B**: Direct Team B to reinstate the data-pipeline service because Team A's roadmap depends on it and the release date is fixed.
- **C**: Accept both backlogs as the product owners set them and let the dependency resolve itself during the next increment.
- **D**: Remove the dependent feature from Team A's scope so the release plan no longer relies on Team B's service.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The integration failure is a cross-team dependency that two locally optimal plans have left inconsistent; the PM's role is to make the dependency and its trade-off visible so the product owners decide with full information. (A) does exactly this and keeps the release plan integrated without overriding ownership. (B) usurps Team B's prioritisation and assumes the pipeline must win without weighing the reporting value being displaced. (C) ignores a known dependency and gambles the quarterly release on it sorting itself out. (D) unilaterally cuts scope to dodge the conflict rather than letting the owners evaluate the higher-value option, which may be the wrong call for the product.
