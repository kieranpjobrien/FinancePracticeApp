# Question

A PM running a predictive retail rollout discovers late in planning that a sister project in the same programme depends on two of her deliverables completing by specific dates. Neither PM was told by the programme manager. The PM is drafting her schedule now. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Build the schedule without those dependency dates, since the other project should have raised them earlier.
- **B**: Schedule the two deliverables to the other project's preferred dates to avoid cross-project tension.
- **C**: Ask the programme manager to formally document and prioritise the cross-project dependency first.
- **D**: Confirm the dependency with the other PM, document it, and integrate it into the schedule plan.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Cross-project dependency confirmation and documentation are an integration responsibility; schedules must reflect confirmed dependencies. (A) ignores a known dependency, which will surface as an integration defect later. (B) commits dates without confirming feasibility against the PM's own constraints. (C) escalates before doing the basic confirmation conversation that the PM should run first.
