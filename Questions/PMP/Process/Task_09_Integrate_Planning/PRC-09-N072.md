# Question

A project manager on a pharmaceutical clinical-trial platform is integrating the subsidiary plans before the planning gate. The programme uses predictive planning for the validated manufacturing system and iterative delivery for the trial-data dashboards. Which actions genuinely advance integration of the planning activities, as opposed to merely collecting documents? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Confirm that risk responses with cost and time implications are reflected in the contingency reserves and schedule of the affected workstreams.
- **B**: Verify that the dependencies between the manufacturing milestones and the dashboard iterations are mapped and time-phased consistently.
- **C**: Collect each subsidiary plan into a single repository and confirm every required plan is present and version-stamped.
- **D**: Reconcile the assumptions log so that an assumption relied on in one plan is not contradicted in another.
- **E**: Obtain individual sign-off from each knowledge-area lead on their own plan in isolation.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Integration means making the parts mutually consistent: propagating risk responses into reserves and schedule (A), time-phasing cross-workstream dependencies (B), and reconciling shared assumptions (D) all test whether the plans actually fit together. (C) is necessary housekeeping but only assembles documents — completeness and version control do not establish consistency. (E) confirms each plan was approved alone, which is the opposite of integration and can leave cross-plan conflicts entirely unexamined.
