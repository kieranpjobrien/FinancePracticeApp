# Question

An agile media-streaming team is integrating its delivery plan with the organisation's quarterly objectives. The product owner's release plan commits to four major features this quarter. The team's measured velocity and the resource plan, however, support roughly three features once planned leave and a known platform-migration commitment are accounted for. The product owner is reluctant to drop a feature. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Make the capacity reality visible by reconciling velocity, leave, and the migration commitment against the release plan, so the product owner can re-prioritise within realistic capacity.
- **B**: Commit to all four features and plan to recover the shortfall through overtime if velocity falls behind.
- **C**: Accept the four-feature release plan as the product owner's prerogative and adjust expectations only if the team misses.
- **D**: Reduce the platform-migration commitment to free capacity for the fourth feature without consulting the migration's stakeholders.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integration requires the release plan to be consistent with the team's real capacity as reflected in velocity and the resource plan, including known commitments. (A) surfaces the true capacity so prioritisation happens with honest constraints, which is the integrating step. (B) plans on systematic overtime, an unsustainable response that hides an over-commitment rather than reconciling it. (C) defers the inevitable conflict to a missed release instead of aligning plan and capacity now. (D) raids another committed initiative without consulting its stakeholders, creating a new cross-plan conflict to mask the first.
