# Question

The triple constraint concept implies that a change to one of scope, schedule, or cost typically:

## Options

- **A**: Affects only the constraint that was changed.
- **B**: Has no project effect provided contingency reserves are available.
- **C**: Forces an adjustment to one or more of the other constraints.
- **D**: Improves overall project performance through focus.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The triple constraint exists precisely to express coupling: a scope expansion typically forces more time or money; compressing the schedule typically demands more resources or less scope. (A) denies the coupling. (B) misuses contingency, which exists for risk events, not baseline changes. (D) is unsupported and ignores the trade-off.
