# Question

The communications plan calls for daily synchronous standups at a fixed time. Several team members are based in time zones where this falls in the middle of the night and have already raised the issue. Which integration gap most directly explains this?

## Options

- **A**: The communications plan was developed without alignment to the resource management plan, which captures team locations and availability.
- **B**: Time zone risk should have been entered in the risk register and is therefore now an open risk to manage.
- **C**: The communications plan needs a stricter attendance policy so distributed members fully participate.
- **D**: The stakeholder engagement plan does not yet treat distributed team members as a distinct group.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The clearest integration failure is between communications and resource management: the cadence was set without referencing where the people actually are. (B) treats a known fact as a probabilistic risk. (C) compounds the harm by mandating bad-hours attendance. (D) is plausible but secondary; the immediate plan-to-plan misalignment sits between communications and resources.
