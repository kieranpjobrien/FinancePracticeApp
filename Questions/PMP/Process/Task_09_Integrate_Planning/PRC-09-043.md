# Question

A new PM inherits a project where the schedule baseline, scope baseline, and cost baseline were each set by different previous PMs and are visibly inconsistent — the schedule assumes a smaller scope than the scope baseline describes. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct a current-state assessment — what has actually been delivered, spent, and committed to stakeholders.
- **B**: Adopt the most recent version of each artefact as the new working baselines and proceed with execution.
- **C**: Discard the existing inconsistent plans and rebuild all three baselines from scratch with the team.
- **D**: Escalate the inconsistency to the sponsor and request a decision on which of the three baselines should win.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

You cannot reconcile inconsistent baselines without first knowing the truth on the ground; otherwise you produce a different wrong picture. The PM owes the sponsor analysis and options, not just the problem. (B) papers over inconsistency. (C) discards potentially useful work without understanding what is real. (D) escalates without the diagnostic the sponsor will need anyway.
