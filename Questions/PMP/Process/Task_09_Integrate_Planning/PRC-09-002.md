# Question

What is the primary purpose of integrated change control?

## Options

- **A**: To prevent any changes from being introduced after baselining.
- **B**: To ensure changes are evaluated holistically across scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, and risk before approval.
- **C**: To accelerate the change approval process so the team is not slowed down.
- **D**: To document stakeholder complaints in a central register.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Integrated change control exists because project elements are coupled — touching one usually moves others. The point is informed, holistic decisions, not prevention or speed. (A) is wrong; the purpose is to manage change, not block it. (C) is wrong; the priority is rigour, not velocity. (D) confuses change control with issue/complaint logging.
