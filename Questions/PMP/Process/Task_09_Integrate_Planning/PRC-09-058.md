# Question

During planning for a hospital equipment upgrade, the quality plan demands a test regime whose cost would breach the approved cost baseline. The sponsor is adamant both quality and cost targets must hold. The PM is integrating the subsidiary plans. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Present the trade-off analysis to the sponsor with options and let them choose an informed path.
- **B**: Reduce the test regime silently to fit cost, as quality plans are usually tailored in execution anyway.
- **C**: Breach the cost baseline to preserve quality, and explain the overrun in the next monthly report.
- **D**: Note the conflict in the risk register and continue finalising each plan independently of the other.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrated planning surfaces conflicts and brings informed options to the accountable decision-maker; transparency is the PMI-aligned action. (B) tailors quality without authority to satisfy cost, compromising safety-relevant outcomes. (C) blows a baseline without prior discussion and surfaces it after the fact. (D) records the problem without forcing the decision that planning is supposed to resolve now.
