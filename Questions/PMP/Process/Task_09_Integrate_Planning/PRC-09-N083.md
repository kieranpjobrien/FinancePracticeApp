# Question

A project manager on a national parcel-logistics hub project is performing a consistency review of the integrated plan before the approval gate. Which findings indicate a genuine integration defect the PM must resolve before proceeding? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The cost baseline totals less than the sum of the time-phased budgets in the schedule plus the contingency reserve.
- **B**: A milestone in the schedule depends on a deliverable the scope baseline does not include.
- **C**: The communications plan specifies a weekly status report, while the team prefers fortnightly.
- **D**: A risk requiring a contingency reserve has no corresponding reserve reflected in the cost baseline.
- **E**: The resource plan names a role title that differs slightly from the title used in the organisation chart.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Integration defects are inconsistencies between subsidiary plans and baselines: a cost baseline that does not reconcile to the time-phased budget and reserve (A), a schedule milestone depending on out-of-scope work (B), and a risk reserve missing from the cost baseline (D) all break cross-plan consistency and must be fixed. (C) is a stakeholder preference about cadence, not a contradiction between plans, and can be discussed without blocking the gate. (E) is a cosmetic naming mismatch with no impact on the consistency of scope, schedule, cost, or risk.
