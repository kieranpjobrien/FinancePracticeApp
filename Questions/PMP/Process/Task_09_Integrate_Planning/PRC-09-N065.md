# Question

An agile team building a payments API has a product backlog, a release plan, and a sprint cadence in place. During integration of the planning artefacts, the project manager finds that the quality approach specifies mandatory security penetration testing and the architecture approach mandates load testing, yet neither requirement appears in the team's definition of done or in any backlog item. The first sprint starts tomorrow. What is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the team and product owner to fold the security and performance criteria into the definition of done and create the backlog items needed to satisfy them.
- **B**: Add a hardening sprint at the end of the release to perform all security and load testing in one batch before go-live.
- **C**: Raise a risk that the quality and architecture requirements may not be met and review it at each sprint retrospective.
- **D**: Keep the testing requirements in the quality and architecture documents and have the PM verify them during release sign-off.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrating planning in an agile context means the quality, architecture and security expectations are reflected in the artefacts the team actually works from — primarily the definition of done and the backlog — so the work is built right from the first sprint. (A) makes the plans consistent at the point of execution and bakes quality in rather than inspecting it later. (B) defers all assurance to a single end-stage batch, accumulating risk and rework and contradicting continuous-quality intent; a hardening sprint can supplement but not replace embedded criteria. (C) merely documents the gap as a risk without closing it. (D) leaves the requirements stranded in documents the team does not consult, so they are likely to be discovered only at sign-off when remediation is most expensive.
