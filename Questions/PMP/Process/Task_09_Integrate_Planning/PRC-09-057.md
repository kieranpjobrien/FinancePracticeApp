# Question

A PM beginning planning for a defence systems integration project is under deadline pressure. The PM has not yet reviewed enterprise environmental factors or organisational process assets, and is tempted to start drafting the schedule straight away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Start the schedule now using the PM's past project experience as the primary planning reference.
- **B**: Outsource the EEF and OPA review to a junior team member while the PM drafts the schedule.
- **C**: Prepare the plan in isolation and retrofit EEFs and OPAs once the draft is already in place.
- **D**: Review EEFs and OPAs first so planning incorporates mandates, constraints, and reusable assets properly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

EEFs and OPAs are essential inputs to planning in defence contexts where regulatory mandates and organisational standards must be respected. (A) ignores context inputs that often drive constraints and standards. (B) delegates the review to someone unlikely to recognise what matters. (C) retrofits discoveries into a plan, producing rework and potential compliance gaps.
