# Question

A project manager integrating the plans for a hybrid smart-metering rollout is explaining to the sponsor how different cross-plan conflicts get resolved during integration. Match each integration issue on the left with the planning action that most directly resolves it.

## Items

- **1**: The schedule assumes a vendor delivery date that the procurement plan has not yet secured
- **2**: A risk response calls for extra testing not reflected in any plan's effort or duration
- **3**: Two workstreams claim the same specialist during the same window
- **4**: The agile workstream's release dates are not aligned with the predictive workstream's milestones

## Matches

- **A**: Incorporate the response activity into the affected schedule and cost estimates so reserves and durations reflect it
- **B**: Map and time-phase the cross-workstream dependencies so releases and milestones line up
- **C**: Reconcile the assumption against the procurement plan and align the dates before baselining
- **D**: Perform resource levelling or smoothing across the workstreams to remove the over-allocation

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-D, 4-B

## Explanation

A schedule date depending on an unsecured procurement (1) is resolved by reconciling the assumption with the procurement plan before baselining (C). A risk response absent from effort and duration (2) is integrated by reflecting it in schedule and cost estimates and reserves (A). Two workstreams contending for one specialist (3) is a resource over-allocation cleared by levelling or smoothing (D). Misaligned agile and predictive cadences (4) are reconciled by mapping and time-phasing the cross-workstream dependencies (B). Each conflict is a distinct integration mechanism, and matching the issue to the correct lever is the core of integrating planning activities.
