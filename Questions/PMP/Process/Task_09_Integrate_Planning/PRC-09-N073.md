# Question

An e-commerce replatforming effort runs as a single agile team delivering in two-week increments. The team has a strong product backlog, a release plan, and a working agreement, but the PM notices that the test, security, and accessibility expectations are each described differently across the quality plan, the release plan, and the team's stated definition of done. The team is about to start the first increment. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the team together to reconcile the quality, security, and accessibility expectations into a single coherent definition of done that the plans reference consistently.
- **B**: Proceed with the increment and capture any quality gaps as defects during the sprint review.
- **C**: Adopt the quality plan's wording as authoritative and treat the definition of done as informal team guidance.
- **D**: Ask each specialist to maintain their own checklist so the relevant expectations are not lost.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Inconsistent quality, security, and accessibility expectations across the plans and the definition of done is exactly the kind of cross-artifact incoherence integration is meant to resolve before work begins. (A) aligns the artifacts so the team builds to one agreed standard. (B) starts work on a contradictory basis and converts an avoidable planning conflict into rework. (C) privileges one document and downgrades the definition of done, the very mechanism the team uses to enforce quality each increment. (D) fragments the standard further into parallel checklists, deepening rather than resolving the inconsistency.
