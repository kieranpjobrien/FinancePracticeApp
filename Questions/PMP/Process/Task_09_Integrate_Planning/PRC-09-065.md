# Question

The PM on a predictive airport expansion has drafted a stakeholder engagement plan that identifies twenty-three stakeholders with specific engagement strategies. The communications plan lists only six regular recipients. The PM has not reconciled these numbers. What should the PM do before baselining the plan?

## Options

- **A**: Leave both plans as drafted, since engagement strategy and communications cadence do differ by design.
- **B**: Reduce the stakeholder engagement plan to six so it matches the communications plan and reporting capacity.
- **C**: Expand the communications plan to include all twenty-three stakeholders on the same detailed reporting cadence.
- **D**: Map stakeholder engagement needs to communications methods and cadences, producing two aligned plans.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The stakeholder engagement plan should drive the communications plan; mapping needs to methods integrates the two without collapsing them. (A) leaves a genuine disconnect rather than integrating the two plans. (B) cuts engagement to match reporting, inverting the logical dependency. (C) floods all stakeholders with the same content regardless of interest or influence.
