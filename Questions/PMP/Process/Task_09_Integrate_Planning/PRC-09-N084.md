# Question

A biotech firm's R&D portfolio office requires every project to submit a single integrated project management plan with fixed scope, schedule, and cost baselines at the outset. A project manager is leading a genuinely exploratory assay-development effort best suited to adaptive, iteration-based planning where scope emerges. The PMO insists the standard integrated plan template be used unchanged. What is the PM's most appropriate course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Propose a tailored integrated plan that keeps governance-required elements but expresses scope and schedule adaptively, and seek the PMO's agreement to the tailoring.
- **B**: Comply fully with the template and commit to fixed baselines, accepting frequent change requests as scope emerges.
- **C**: Bypass the PMO requirement and run the project on an informal adaptive plan without an integrated document.
- **D**: Abandon the adaptive approach and convert the exploratory work to a fully predictive plan to fit the template.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrating planning activities includes tailoring how the plan is expressed to suit the work while still satisfying governance, and that tailoring should be agreed with the body that owns the standard. (A) preserves required governance elements, fits the adaptive nature of the work, and secures PMO agreement. (B) forces fixed baselines onto emergent work, generating a stream of change requests that signals the plan was wrong by design. (C) discards governance and transparency entirely, which is not an acceptable way to resolve the tension. (D) abandons the approach best suited to the deliverable purely to fit a template, harming the work to satisfy a form.
