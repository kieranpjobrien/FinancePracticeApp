# Question

The risk management plan on a predictive aerospace project identifies a ninety-day schedule exposure if a critical supplier slips. The schedule baseline and cost baseline contain no reserve for this risk. The sponsor approves the plan as drafted. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed because the sponsor has approved the baselines as drafted, regardless of the risk exposure.
- **B**: Submit a revised baseline containing a reserve for the supplier risk and request separate sponsor approval.
- **C**: Raise a formal change request to align schedule and cost baselines with the identified risk exposure reserves.
- **D**: Update the risk register with a higher severity rating so the exposure is visible in steering reports.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated baselines must reflect planned risk responses; contingency reserves are established through integrated change control and documented approval. (A) sets the baselines up to fail on a known exposure. (B) submits revisions without using the formal change control path. (D) records severity without addressing the baseline integration gap.
