# Question

Late in planning, the PM realises that the requested scope cannot fit inside the fixed deadline with the budgeted resources. Which of the following best characterises this situation?

## Options

- **A**: A normal planning iteration that will resolve itself with one more refinement pass.
- **B**: A triple-constraint conflict that must be surfaced for a trade-off decision by governance.
- **C**: A risk that should be logged and monitored without further action being required right now.
- **D**: Evidence that the project is no longer viable and should be cancelled at this stage.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When scope, time, and cost cannot all be honoured, the PM owes governance an explicit choice between reducing scope, extending the deadline, adding cost, or accepting more risk. (A) underplays the issue. (C) defers a structural problem as if it were a probabilistic one. (D) is premature; constraint conflicts are very common and usually have at least one viable response.
