# Question

After the integrated project management plan for an offshore wind-farm cable installation is baselined, the procurement lead negotiates a faster vessel charter that shortens the installation window but raises cost by 8 per cent. The schedule lead wants to update the schedule baseline straight away to capture the earlier finish. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Route the change through integrated change control, assessing the cost, schedule, and risk impacts together before any baseline is altered.
- **B**: Update the schedule baseline immediately to reflect the earlier finish and adjust the cost baseline at the next reporting cycle.
- **C**: Allow the procurement lead to proceed with the charter and reconcile the plans afterwards once the actual impact is known.
- **D**: Reject the faster charter because the integrated baselines have already been approved and should remain fixed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Once baselined, changes that touch multiple subsidiary plans must pass through integrated change control so the cost, schedule, and risk effects are evaluated and applied coherently. (A) does this, keeping the baselines consistent with one another. (B) edits one baseline in isolation and lets cost lag, breaking the integration the change-control process exists to protect. (C) commits to the charter before assessing or approving its cross-plan impact, undermining baseline integrity. (D) treats baselines as immutable rather than controlled, and may forgo a genuinely beneficial trade-off without proper evaluation.
