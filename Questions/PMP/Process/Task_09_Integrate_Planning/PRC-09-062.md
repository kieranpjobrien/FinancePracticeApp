# Question

A PM supporting a Scrum team at a renewable energy startup finds the team has no release planning cadence, no PI planning, and no roadmap reviews. The product owner says ceremonies slow the team down. Delivery predictability is poor and stakeholders complain. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a lightweight cadenced planning rhythm proportionate to team size and stakeholder coordination needs.
- **B**: Leave the team alone, as the product owner's preference against ceremonies should be respected absolutely.
- **C**: Mandate SAFe PI planning every eight weeks to bring structural predictability to the delivery approach.
- **D**: Ask stakeholders to stop complaining and trust the team's velocity to stabilise over future sprints eventually.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Planning integration requires a cadence tailored to context; small teams need lightweight rhythms rather than nothing or heavyweight frameworks. (B) defers to preference rather than treating poor predictability as a symptom to address. (C) imposes a heavyweight framework not matched to a startup Scrum team. (D) dismisses stakeholder signals and hopes the underlying problem self-corrects.
