# Question

During integrated planning for a city-wide cycling-infrastructure rollout, the project manager finds the stakeholder engagement plan commits to monthly in-person community forums, but the communications management plan budgets only for a quarterly email newsletter, and the cost plan funds neither at the implied level. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm with the engagement lead which level of community contact the stakeholder analysis actually warrants, then reconcile the communications and cost plans to that agreed level before baselining.
- **B**: Fund the monthly in-person forums named in the engagement plan and update the communications and cost plans to match, since the engagement plan is the authoritative statement of stakeholder need.
- **C**: Set all three plans to the quarterly newsletter level the communications plan already assumes, and let the engagement lead escalate for more if community feedback later justifies it.
- **D**: Reconcile the engagement and communications plans to a consistent approach and raise the resulting shortfall to the sponsor as a funding change, leaving the cost baseline untouched for now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The three plans disagree on both the engagement level and its funding, so integration starts by establishing what the stakeholder analysis actually requires, then aligning the communications mechanisms and the budget to that agreed level before baselining (A). (B) reconciles the plans but anchors on the engagement plan's figure without testing whether monthly in-person forums are warranted, potentially over-funding contact the analysis does not support. (C) lets the budget assumption silently dictate the engagement level, subordinating stakeholder need to the cheapest existing line rather than integrating the plans. (D) reconciles two of the three plans but deliberately leaves the cost baseline inconsistent with them, which is precisely the kind of unresolved gap integration is meant to close before approval.
