# Question

A PM is starting a hybrid transformation with three delivery teams in Sydney and two in Manila. Each team runs slightly different cadences. Sponsors want consistent monthly portfolio reporting. What planning approach should the PM take to keep integration realistic?

## Options

- **A**: Force all five teams onto a single identical cadence so integration is mechanical and reporting is trivial.
- **B**: Allow each team its own cadence with no cross-team integration beyond monthly portfolio reporting dates.
- **C**: Define a shared integration rhythm and shared reporting artifacts while allowing team-level cadence variation.
- **D**: Ask each team lead to submit monthly narrative reports and have the PMO compile them into one pack.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integration at the programme level is achieved through shared rhythms and artifacts while respecting team-level tailoring; this is the scaled-agile mindset of minimum viable coordination. (A) imposes uniformity where tailoring is appropriate. (B) omits integration entirely, leaving sponsors without coherent insight. (D) compiles reports without the upstream integration rhythm that would make them coherent.
