# Question

A project depends on a deliverable from a sister project led by another PM. That sister project is slipping and the dependency now threatens this project's go-live. What integration activity is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the portfolio or programme layer with documented dependency, impact, and options for trade-offs.
- **B**: Quietly absorb the slip by extending this project's schedule and notifying stakeholders at the next update.
- **C**: Try to remove the dependency by re-architecting this project around the missing deliverable entirely.
- **D**: Wait for the other PM to provide an updated date before taking any action on this project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A cross-project dependency that neither PM controls is exactly what portfolio/programme governance exists to resolve. (B) hides a known issue and erodes trust. (C) may be valid but should not be the default — it could be far more expensive than waiting or rephasing. (D) is passive at the moment proactive escalation is needed.
