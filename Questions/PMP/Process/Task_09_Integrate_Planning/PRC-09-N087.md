# Question

An agile edtech team is about to start integrated release planning for a new assessment module. The PM is deciding who should participate so the resulting plan genuinely accounts for all the work. Who should the PM ensure is involved?

## Options

- **A**: The product owner, the delivery team, and representatives of the key dependencies, so priorities, capacity, and cross-team needs are reconciled in the plan.
- **B**: Only the PM and the product owner, who can set scope and priorities and brief the team afterwards.
- **C**: Only the delivery team, since they alone know what can be built each iteration.
- **D**: The sponsor and the PMO, who will sign off the release plan and therefore should shape it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrated release planning reconciles priorities, team capacity, and cross-team dependencies, so it needs the product owner, the team, and the relevant dependency representatives together. (A) brings exactly those perspectives, producing a plan that reflects real capacity and external needs. (B) and (C) each include only part of the picture, omitting either the people who build the work or the priorities and dependencies it must satisfy. (D) confuses approval authority with the planning participants; the sponsor and PMO endorse the plan but do not supply the capacity and dependency detail integration requires.
