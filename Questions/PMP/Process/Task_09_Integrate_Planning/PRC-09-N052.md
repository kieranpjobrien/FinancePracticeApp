# Question

A project manager is about to begin integrating the planning for a new corporate intranet. Several inputs are available at once. Which most fundamentally shapes how the subsidiary plans and baselines will be structured and integrated, so that settling it first lets the rest of the planning fall into place coherently?

## Options

- **A**: The development approach and project life cycle selected for the work.
- **B**: The high-level scope and success criteria recorded in the approved project charter.
- **C**: The organisation's mandated stage-gate governance and its required management-plan templates.
- **D**: The assumptions and constraints captured in the assumption log during initiation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The development approach and life cycle (predictive, iterative, incremental, or hybrid) determine how scope is defined, how the schedule and baselines are structured, how often planning is elaborated, and how the streams must be stitched together — settle it and the shape of every other plan follows, which is why it is the most fundamental integrating input (A). (B) bounds *what* the project must achieve and feeds scope planning, but it does not dictate how the plans are structured or how often they elaborate. (C) constrains the form planning artefacts take and the gates they pass, shaping process compliance rather than the integration logic of the plans themselves. (D) records important conditions that inform many plans, but assumptions are elaborated and tested within an approach — they do not set the structural backbone the way the chosen life cycle does.
