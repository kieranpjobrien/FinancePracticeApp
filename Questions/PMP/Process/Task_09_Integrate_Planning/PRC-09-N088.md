# Question

A hybrid defence-communications programme has just baselined its integrated plan. A subcontractor proposes a design change that would shorten the agile software workstream but extend the predictive hardware qualification by six weeks and alter the test sequence. As the PM processes this through integrated change control, which steps are essential before any baseline is updated? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Assess the change's combined impact across scope, schedule, cost, and risk for both workstreams together.
- **B**: Update the affected subsidiary plans consistently so they remain mutually coherent if the change is approved.
- **C**: Obtain the appropriate authority's decision on the change before altering any baseline.
- **D**: Implement the software acceleration immediately, since it is beneficial, and assess the hardware impact separately later.
- **E**: Let the subcontractor revise the test sequence directly in the integrated plan to save time.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Integrated change control requires evaluating cross-cutting impact together (A), propagating an approved change through all affected plans to keep them coherent (B), and securing the authorised decision before any baseline moves (C). (D) splits a single interdependent change and implements part of it without approval, breaking the very integration and control the process protects. (E) lets an external party edit the integrated baseline directly, bypassing impact assessment and change authority altogether.
