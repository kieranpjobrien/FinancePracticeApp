# Question

A project manager has assembled the integrated project management plan for a national payroll-system replacement and the sponsor has approved it. The functional managers who will supply staff, the lead vendor, and the operations team that will run the system were not part of assembling the plan. Before execution begins, what should the project manager do next to support successful integration?

## Options

- **A**: Hold a kick-off that walks all key parties through the integrated plan, confirms their commitments and interdependencies, and aligns them on how the pieces fit before work starts.
- **B**: Meet the functional managers, vendor, and operations lead individually to confirm each party's commitments and the dependencies that affect them, then circulate the consolidated confirmations.
- **C**: Distribute the approved plan to all parties with a written briefing on their roles and dependencies, and have them raise any concerns before execution begins.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to formally launch the project to the parties, since their authority secures the commitment the parties need to honour the plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A kick-off brings the parties who must execute together at once, so they confirm commitments and see how their interdependent parts mesh before work starts — the standard bridge from an approved plan to coordinated execution, and the only option that builds shared understanding across the parties simultaneously (A). (B) confirms the same commitments but one-to-one, so no party hears the others' dependencies or develops the joint picture that integration needs. (C) informs and invites questions but is one-way and easy to skim, securing neither active commitment nor a shared cross-party understanding. (D) usefully lends sponsor authority, yet a launch that secures buy-in without walking the parties through how their work integrates still leaves the coordination gaps unaddressed.
