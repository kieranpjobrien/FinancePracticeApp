# Question

A hybrid payments-platform programme runs a predictive workstream for regulatory reporting and an agile workstream for the customer-facing wallet. The PM is assembling a single integrated plan. The agile team plans in two-week increments with an evolving backlog, while the regulatory team has fixed milestones tied to a central bank deadline. A steering committee member argues the integrated plan is invalid because the two workstreams do not share a common planning horizon. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Integrate at the points where the workstreams interact — shared dependencies, releases, and milestones — while letting each workstream plan at the cadence suited to its work.
- **B**: Force both workstreams onto a fixed milestone schedule so the integrated plan presents a single consistent horizon to the committee.
- **C**: Convert the regulatory workstream to two-week increments so its cadence matches the wallet team and the plan becomes uniform.
- **D**: Maintain two entirely separate plans and present them side by side, since hybrid workstreams cannot be genuinely integrated.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integration in a hybrid setting means reconciling the workstreams at their interfaces — dependencies, integration points, releases, and shared milestones — not imposing a single uniform cadence. (A) preserves each approach's strengths while ensuring the combined plan is coherent where the parts meet. (B) and (C) destroy the fit between method and work, harming the very deliverables the cadence was chosen to suit. (D) abandons integration altogether, leaving cross-workstream dependencies and the regulatory deadline unmanaged.
