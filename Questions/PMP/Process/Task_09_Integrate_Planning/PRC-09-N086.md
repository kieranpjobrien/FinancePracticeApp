# Question

While integrating the plans for a hospital wing fit-out, a project manager must ensure each planning input feeds the right integrated output. Match each planning input on the left with the integrated output it most directly informs.

## Items

- **1**: Activity duration estimates and the schedule network
- **2**: Cost estimates aggregated and time-phased across the schedule
- **3**: Risk responses with funding implications
- **4**: Scope statement, WBS, and WBS dictionary

## Matches

- **A**: The cost baseline, including contingency reserves
- **B**: The contingency reserve component within the cost and schedule baselines
- **C**: The schedule baseline
- **D**: The scope baseline

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

Activity durations and the network logic (1) integrate into the schedule baseline (C). Time-phased, aggregated cost estimates (2) form the cost baseline including reserves (A). Risk responses with funding implications (3) feed the contingency reserve component sitting within the cost and schedule baselines (B). The scope statement, WBS, and WBS dictionary (4) constitute the scope baseline (D). The fine distinction is between the cost baseline as a whole (A) and the contingency reserve element within the baselines that risk responses specifically drive (B); conflating them is the common error this item tests.
