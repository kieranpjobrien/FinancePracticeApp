# Question

A project manager on a hybrid insurance claims-modernisation programme is integrating the subsidiary plans. The benefits management plan states that the primary value — a 30 per cent reduction in claim cycle time — depends on a new fraud-scoring service and the legacy decommissioning, both of which the integrated schedule places in the final release. The sponsor expects measurable value within the first two releases. The individual plans are each internally consistent. What is the PM's best action?

## Options

- **A**: Re-examine the integrated schedule and release plan against the benefits plan and propose sequencing an early value-delivering slice so benefits realisation aligns with the sponsor's expectation.
- **B**: Leave the sequence as planned and reset the sponsor's expectation to match the integrated schedule's final-release delivery.
- **C**: Pull the fraud-scoring service and decommissioning forward into release one regardless of dependencies to hit the early-value expectation.
- **D**: Treat benefits realisation as a post-project concern outside the integrated plan and proceed with the current sequence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Integrating planning activities includes reconciling the delivery sequence with the benefits plan so the schedule actually realises value when stakeholders expect it. (A) revisits the schedule and release plan against the benefits plan and seeks an early value-delivering slice, which is the integrating move. (B) accepts a misalignment between value and expectation without testing whether an earlier benefit is feasible. (C) forces work forward while ignoring the dependencies that determine feasibility, risking an unworkable plan. (D) wrongly excludes benefits from planning integration, when value delivery is a core driver the integrated plan must support.
