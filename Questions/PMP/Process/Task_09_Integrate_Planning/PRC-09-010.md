# Question

The team pushes back on time spent in planning, arguing the deadline is too tight to "waste" effort there. What is the most appropriate response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Begin work immediately on the highest-priority items and treat planning as a delay.
- **B**: Refuse any execution until a complete, fully detailed project management plan is formally approved.
- **C**: Adopt rolling-wave planning — plan near-term work in detail and elaborate later work progressively.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor to choose between thorough planning and fast execution upfront.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Rolling-wave planning is the PMI-endorsed answer to "we can't plan everything yet." Plan enough to start safely; elaborate as more is known. (A) executes blind and usually slows the project via rework. (B) imposes waterfall rigidity inappropriately. (D) ducks a methodological recommendation that the PM is qualified to make.
