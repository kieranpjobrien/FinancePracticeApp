# Question

A newly assigned project manager on a light-rail depot construction project has received approved subsidiary plans for scope, quality, procurement, and risk. The sponsor asks when the cost and schedule baselines can be set. The PM notes that the resource plan and the activity estimates are still being finalised by the engineering lead. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the resource and activity estimating work is complete, because the cost and schedule baselines depend on those inputs being integrated first.
- **B**: Set provisional cost and schedule baselines now using the approved plans and adjust them once the resource work arrives.
- **C**: Baseline cost immediately from the procurement plan and defer only the schedule baseline until estimating finishes.
- **D**: Ask the engineering lead to release the partial resource data so both baselines can be set on a best-effort basis.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cost and schedule baselines are downstream outputs that integrate resource quantities and activity estimates; setting them before those inputs are ready would create baselines the later data may contradict. (A) respects the dependency and keeps the integrated plan internally consistent. (B) and (D) baseline on incomplete inputs, guaranteeing rework once the resource data lands and undermining the value of a baseline. (C) splits cost from schedule even though both rely on the same unfinished estimating effort, producing a cost baseline that may not match the eventual schedule.
