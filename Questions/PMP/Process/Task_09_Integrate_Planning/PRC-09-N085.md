# Question

A hybrid automotive infotainment project has a predictive hardware workstream and an agile software workstream. Integrating the plans, the PM finds the software release plan schedules the first user-acceptance iteration in week 6, but the hardware plan delivers the first test bench only in week 9. Each workstream lead validated their own plan against their own constraints. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Map the cross-workstream dependency, then work with both leads to either bring the test bench forward or re-sequence the acceptance iteration so the integrated plan is feasible.
- **B**: Keep both plans as validated and let the software team begin acceptance on simulated hardware, accepting any rework when real hardware arrives.
- **C**: Push the entire software release plan back three weeks to align with hardware availability, regardless of other software dependencies.
- **D**: Instruct the hardware lead to commit to week 6 because the software acceptance schedule was finalised first.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The conflict is a cross-workstream dependency invisible until the plans are integrated, and the PM must reconcile it jointly with both leads. (A) maps the dependency and seeks the least-disruptive re-sequencing or acceleration, which is the integrating action. (B) lets acceptance proceed on a substitute that the plan never validated, courting rework and a false sense of progress. (C) shifts the whole software plan without checking its own internal dependencies, possibly breaking other commitments. (D) imposes an unrealistic date on hardware based on which plan was written first, not on feasibility.
