# Question

While integrating the subsidiary plans for a pharmaceutical packaging line, the project manager notices the risk register includes a high-probability supplier delay for a custom filler, yet neither the schedule reserve nor the cost contingency reflects it. The schedule and cost leads built their plans before the latest risk analysis. What is the most appropriate action?

## Options

- **A**: Size schedule and cost contingency reserves to the analysed supplier-delay exposure and fold them into both baselines, so the integrated plan reflects the risk consistently.
- **B**: Add a management reserve to the budget to cover the supplier delay and flag the schedule exposure in the risk register for monitoring, leaving the schedule baseline unchanged.
- **C**: Ask the schedule and cost leads to qualitatively re-screen the risk against their plans and adjust only if they judge it material, since they built the original estimates.
- **D**: Escalate the unreflected risk to the sponsor for a decision on additional reserve before changing either baseline.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A high-probability, already-analysed risk belongs in contingency reserve sized to its exposure, propagated into both the schedule and cost baselines so the integrated plan and the risk register tell the same story (A). (B) uses management reserve — meant for unknown-unknowns — for a known, quantified risk, and leaves the schedule baseline blind to a delay that is fundamentally a schedule threat. (C) hands the analysed risk back for an informal re-screen by the same leads whose pre-analysis estimates created the gap, risking it being talked down rather than planned for. (D) escalates a reserve-integration step the PM is equipped to perform; the sponsor's input is warranted only if the contingency breaches thresholds or funding limits, not as the first move.
