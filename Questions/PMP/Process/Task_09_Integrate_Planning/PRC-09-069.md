# Question

The PM inherits a predictive systems project where subsidiary plans live across email threads, three SharePoint sites, and individual laptops. No single system of record exists. The PM is about to enter execution and monthly steering reporting starts in two weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin execution with the current scattered documents and centralise artifacts during the second steering cycle.
- **B**: Ask each team member to keep their own artifacts current and collate them monthly for steering reports.
- **C**: Replace all existing artifacts with new ones created inside a new project management information system.
- **D**: Consolidate artifacts into a single project management information system as the authoritative source before execution.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A project management information system is the integration backbone; consolidating before execution protects configuration management and reporting integrity. (A) enters execution on known-fragmented artifacts, creating version drift. (B) leaves artifacts fragmented and compiles stale copies monthly. (C) discards current content unnecessarily and loses useful work.
