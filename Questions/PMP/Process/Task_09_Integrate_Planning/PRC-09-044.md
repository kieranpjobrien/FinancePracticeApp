# Question

A Kanban team supports a regulated platform with continuous flow and no fixed iterations. The product owner is being pressured by a steering committee to commit to a delivery date for an in-flight feature epic. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Use the team's historical throughput and cycle time to provide a probabilistic forecast range to the steering committee.
- **B**: Adopt two-week Scrum sprints so the team can give a date based on velocity at the next planning event.
- **C**: Ask each developer for a personal estimate on the remaining stories and roll them up into a committed delivery date.
- **D**: Tell the steering committee that Kanban teams operate on flow and that fixed delivery dates are not appropriate to provide.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to tailor forecasting to the methodology in use. Kanban teams use empirical flow metrics (throughput, cycle time, work-in-progress limits) to produce Monte Carlo or percentile-based forecasts that honestly reflect uncertainty. (B) abandons a working method to satisfy a reporting need. (C) reverts to bottom-up estimation, which loses the statistical reliability of flow data and pressures developers into commitments. (D) refuses a legitimate stakeholder need; flow-based forecasting absolutely can answer the question.
