# Question

A media company is delivering a subscriber platform using a hybrid approach: the mobile apps are built by two Scrum teams on two-week sprints, while the regulated billing component follows a predictive plan with fixed milestone gates. During integrated release planning, the product owner commits the apps to a feature set for a marketing launch date, but that date falls inside a billing milestone whose gate review has not yet been scheduled. What is the best action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Align the release plan by mapping the Scrum teams' sprint cadence and the billing milestone gates onto a single integrated roadmap and surfacing the dependency to both tracks.
- **B**: Ask the Scrum teams to extend their sprints to four weeks so the agile and predictive cadences match and the dependency disappears.
- **C**: Let the product owner own the launch commitment, since prioritisation of agile scope is the product owner's accountability, and manage billing separately.
- **D**: Move the regulated billing component onto the Scrum boards so the entire programme runs on one iterative cadence.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a hybrid delivery the PM's integration job is to make the iterative and predictive tracks visible to each other and reconcile their dependencies on a shared plan, not to force one cadence onto the other. (A) does exactly this — an integrated roadmap exposes the launch-versus-gate clash so it can be managed. (B) distorts the agile team's working rhythm to solve a coordination problem that integration should handle directly. (C) is a real responsibility boundary but it ignores the cross-track dependency the PM is accountable for integrating; the product owner's commitment is unsafe while the gate is unaligned. (D) discards the deliberate hybrid design — a regulated component with fixed gates is usually predictive for good reasons, and collapsing it into Scrum is an over-correction.
