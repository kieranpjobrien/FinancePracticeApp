# Question

A Scrum team building a fintech mobile app has a detailed sprint plan but no release plan or roadmap. Sponsors are asking for a twelve-month view of what value will be delivered and when. The product owner pushes back, saying roadmaps are not agile. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Agree with the product owner that a roadmap is predictive and unsuitable for this agile fintech context.
- **B**: Produce a rigid twelve-month feature roadmap with fixed dates to give sponsors the clarity they are asking for.
- **C**: Build a themes-based directional roadmap with progressively firmer commitments in the nearer horizons.
- **D**: Defer the roadmap conversation until the team has six more sprints of velocity data to draw from now.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Directional themes-based roadmaps with progressive commitment are the agile-aligned way to integrate planning with a sponsor value view. (A) treats agile as incompatible with forward planning, which misrepresents agile practice. (B) imposes predictive certainty on work that should be allowed to evolve. (D) withholds visibility from sponsors for an extended period with no benefit.
