# Question

A predictive offshore wind maintenance-scheduling project is six weeks from a baselined milestone. The PM reviews variance data and stakeholder requests and wants to prevent uncontrolled scope growth while still allowing genuinely needed changes. Which actions are appropriate for controlling scope in this situation? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Compare current work performance against the scope baseline to detect and quantify any variance.
- **B**: Route every requested change through integrated change control for impact assessment and a decision.
- **C**: Approve small requested additions directly at PM level to keep momentum, deferring larger ones to the board.
- **D**: Update the scope baseline and affected documents only after a change is formally approved.
- **E**: Pre-emptively widen the baseline to absorb likely future requests so fewer change requests are needed.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Controlling scope means measuring performance against the baseline (A), processing all changes through integrated change control (B), and updating the baseline only once a change is approved (D). (C) approving additions at PM discretion bypasses the change control authority and is exactly how scope creep takes hold, regardless of size. (E) inflating the baseline in advance to pre-absorb requests abandons the disciplined comparison point and legitimises future scope growth without assessment, which defeats the purpose of a baseline.
