# Question

A Kanban team supporting a trading platform must commit to a regulatory feature by a hard deadline set by legislation. The team's current cycle time data shows the feature is unlikely to fit in the available window. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the deadline and ask the team to work overtime to close the forecast gap as needed.
- **B**: Tell regulators the feature will be late and ask for an extension to the statutory deadline date.
- **C**: Swap in an alternative team with a faster historical cycle time to deliver the regulatory feature instead.
- **D**: Use cycle time data to model slicing options and present trade-offs to stakeholders for informed choice.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Empirical forecasting with cycle time data lets stakeholders choose which slices to deliver by the deadline; transparency and trade-off analysis drive the decision. (A) commits to a forecast the data contradicts and risks burnout. (B) seeks an extension to statutory obligation before exhausting delivery options. (C) assumes team swapping will preserve quality and context, ignoring ramp-up cost.
