# Question

A vendor delivers a sub-component that meets every documented requirement but does not integrate with the rest of the system because integration constraints were never specified in the SOW. Where does primary responsibility lie under PMI procurement principles?

## Options

- **A**: With the vendor, who should have anticipated integration even if it was not written in.
- **B**: With the buyer's project team, whose requirements documentation was incomplete.
- **C**: Equally with both parties, who should now jointly fund the rework.
- **D**: With the project manager personally, for failing to catch the gap.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A contract is bounded by what it specifies. The vendor met the contract; the omission is in the buyer's requirements process. The buyer's team — including the PM but not the PM alone — owns that gap and the lessons learned. (A) holds a vendor to unwritten obligations. (C) feels collaborative but unjustly shifts cost to a vendor that performed. (D) places sole blame on one role for a team artefact.
