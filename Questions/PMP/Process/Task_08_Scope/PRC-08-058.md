# Question

A Scrum team planning the next sprint cannot estimate a story because a critical integration pattern is unclear and the team has never worked with the third-party API before. The product owner is keen to have the story delivered this sprint. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Estimate the story using worst-case assumptions and commit to it in the current sprint.
- **B**: Split the story into smaller parts and commit to whatever portion the team can confidently size.
- **C**: Introduce a timeboxed spike this sprint to investigate, then size and plan the story next sprint.
- **D**: Defer the story until the third-party vendor supplies complete integration documentation and examples.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A timeboxed spike is the Scrum-aligned response to technical uncertainty that blocks estimation; it buys knowledge without committing to a fragile estimate. (A) commits to a padded estimate that damages forecasting and trust. (B) may produce slices that also cannot be reliably sized. (D) hands control to a vendor and blocks progress indefinitely.
