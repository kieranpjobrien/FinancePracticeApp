# Question

Two teams on a hybrid mining analytics programme are each building features that depend on the other's data model. Each team has defined their own scope independently and incompatibilities have surfaced. The PM must restore alignment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pick the stronger team's data model and direct the other team to conform to it going forward.
- **B**: Facilitate a joint scope working session to agree shared interfaces and update each team's scope.
- **C**: Escalate to portfolio governance and wait for a directive on which team's model should prevail.
- **D**: Ask both teams to continue independently and address incompatibilities during integration testing.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-team scope alignment is best done jointly at the interface boundary, with both teams' scope documents updated to reflect the agreed contract. (A) imposes one team's model without facilitating shared ownership of the interface. (C) escalates before attempting structured facilitation and delays the fix. (D) defers integration problems into the most expensive phase to find them.
