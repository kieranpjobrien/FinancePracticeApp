# Question

A project's approved scope statement explicitly excludes a particular feature. At 40% completion, a senior business stakeholder argues the exclusion was a mistake and the feature is now essential. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the feature immediately given the stakeholder's seniority and the urgency they have expressed.
- **B**: Maintain the exclusion because the scope statement was formally approved by all key stakeholders.
- **C**: Treat as a change, analyse cost, schedule, risk, and rework impact, and route through the CCB.
- **D**: Schedule a workshop with the original scope authors to determine why the feature was excluded originally.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Documented exclusions can absolutely be revisited; the discipline is doing it through change control rather than by influence. (A) bypasses governance. (B) treats baselines as immutable rather than as the artefacts change control updates. (D) gathers historical context — useful, but not what the situation needs first; it doesn't move toward a documented decision.
