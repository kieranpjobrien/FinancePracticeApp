# Question

During requirements workshops for an aged care booking system, the PM notices frontline carers are rarely heard while managers dominate sessions. The product will primarily be used by carers. What should the PM do to strengthen scope quality?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule dedicated elicitation sessions with carers using techniques that surface their day-to-day needs.
- **B**: Rely on managers to represent carer needs since managers understand the workflows that carers perform.
- **C**: Circulate a detailed written questionnaire to carers instead of running additional live workshops.
- **D**: Ask the carers to nominate a single representative to attend existing workshops on their behalf.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Requirements fidelity depends on engaging actual end users with appropriate elicitation techniques; managers are a proxy at best. (B) assumes managers can speak for daily users, which rarely holds. (C) surfaces surface-level answers without dialogue and misses tacit knowledge. (D) concentrates representation in one voice and loses the diversity of carer experience.
