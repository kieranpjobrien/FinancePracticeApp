# Question

In a cross-departmental discovery workshop, two stakeholder groups describe radically different visions for the same product — one wants a quick simple tool, the other a comprehensive platform. The budget cannot fund both visions. What should the project manager facilitate FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend cancelling the project until the business agrees internally on a single shared vision.
- **B**: Run a vision-and-MVP alignment workshop that produces a shared vision and minimum viable scope.
- **C**: Propose building the larger platform incrementally so that both groups eventually get what they want.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to choose between the two visions on behalf of the stakeholder groups.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM's first move with mismatched visions is facilitation, not escalation, capitulation, or solutioning. A shared vision plus an MVP frequently bridges fast-and-simple with comprehensive-over-time. (A) abandons the project rather than doing the alignment work the role is designed for. (C) proposes a solution before the parties have agreed on goals. (D) escalates a question the PM should help the stakeholders answer themselves.
