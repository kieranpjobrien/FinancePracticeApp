# Question

During requirements gathering for a customer portal, a senior business stakeholder demands a minimal interface for speed while a customer support lead demands rich features for power users. Both insist their need is non-negotiable. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the more senior stakeholder's preference for a simpler interface without further analysis.
- **B**: Build two parallel interfaces and let users opt in to whichever one of the two they prefer.
- **C**: Document both positions as conflicting requirements and ask the sponsor to choose between them.
- **D**: Facilitate a session to surface the underlying user goals each side is protecting and design a solution that serves both.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Conflicting "what" requirements often mask compatible "why" goals. The PM's job is to facilitate that exploration before forcing a binary trade-off. (A) substitutes seniority for analysis. (B) doubles the build cost without checking whether one design can serve both. (C) escalates without doing the facilitation work that would let the sponsor (or the parties themselves) decide on better information.
