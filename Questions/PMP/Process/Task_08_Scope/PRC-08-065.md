# Question

Six sprints into a Scrum ecommerce rebuild, the architect flags that emerging performance requirements will require a structural refactor not captured anywhere in the current scope or backlog. The product owner is unaware. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Let the architect quietly refactor within sprint capacity before the product owner is even notified.
- **B**: Add the refactor as a new story so the product owner can weigh its value against other backlog items.
- **C**: Escalate the issue directly to the steering committee so that executives can make the structural call.
- **D**: Defer the refactor until a dedicated tech debt sprint can be allocated in the following quarter.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Technical work with real business impact belongs in the backlog so the product owner can sequence it against value; transparency maintains trust. (A) hides significant work from the product owner and distorts velocity and forecasts. (C) escalates past the product owner before they have even had a chance to decide. (D) imposes a cadence on emergent design that may arrive too late.
