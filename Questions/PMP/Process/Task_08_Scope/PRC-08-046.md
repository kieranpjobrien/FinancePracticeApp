# Question

On a hybrid banking modernisation programme, two senior business stakeholders disagree on whether a fraud alert feature belongs in the current release scope. Each cites a different interview from the elicitation phase. The PM must resolve the ambiguity before the release planning workshop next week. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Trace the conflicting requirements back to source documentation and facilitate a joint session to reconcile intent.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to make the call since scope decisions ultimately rest with the product owner alone.
- **C**: Include the fraud alert feature in scope to avoid cutting capability that a senior stakeholder has asked for.
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to the steering committee so executives can formally decide what belongs in scope.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Requirements traceability and joint clarification resolve the conflict at source and preserve stakeholder relationships. (B) abdicates facilitation to a single role before the conflicting inputs are reconciled. (C) expands scope to avoid conflict, which breeds creep and sets a precedent. (D) escalates prematurely before the PM has attempted a structured reconciliation.
