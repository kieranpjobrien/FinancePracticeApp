# Question

Reviewing a requirements traceability matrix on a regulated medical device project, the PM notices several requirements have no linked test cases. The QA lead says those requirements are "obvious" and don't need explicit verification. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Defer to the QA lead's professional judgement and update the matrix to mark them out of scope for testing.
- **B**: Capture the gap as a documented assumption and proceed with the current verification plan.
- **C**: Direct that every requirement, including obvious ones, has a linked verification artefact before release.
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to the regulatory affairs lead for a binding ruling.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In regulated environments — and as PMI good practice generally — every requirement must be traceable to a verification artefact; "obvious" is not an acceptable defence under audit. (A) creates an unverifiable surface and weakens the safety case. (B) records a known gap rather than closing it; assumptions are for unknowns, not skipped steps. (D) prematurely escalates a question the PM can resolve via the project's own quality plan.
