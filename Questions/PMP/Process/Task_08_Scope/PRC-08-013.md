# Question

A product owner keeps adding small refinements to in-flight stories during the sprint, calling them "just clarifications." The team's velocity has dropped 30% over three sprints. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the additions because the PO owns the backlog and sits closest to user value in the product discovery loop.
- **B**: Lock requirements firmly at sprint start and route all subsequent input to future sprints regardless of its nature.
- **C**: Work with the PO to draw a line between clarifications and new acceptance criteria, routing the latter through refinement.
- **D**: Lengthen the sprint cycle to absorb the additional work and stabilise the team's commitment ratio over time.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

There is a real distinction between clarifying intent and adding new behaviour, and the cure is a shared rule the team and PO can apply consistently. (A) ignores the velocity damage and conflates backlog ownership with mid-sprint disruption. (B) over-corrects by treating legitimate clarifications as forbidden. (D) treats a symptom — the timebox — and entrenches the disruption pattern.
