# Question

Which of the following best defines scope creep?

## Options

- **A**: A formal process for negotiating and approving scope changes through the CCB.
- **B**: Uncontrolled expansion of project scope without corresponding adjustments to time, cost, or resources.
- **C**: Continuous backlog re-prioritisation by the product owner in agile delivery.
- **D**: Removing low-value features late in the project to protect the deadline.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Scope creep is unmanaged growth; the defining attribute is the absence of corresponding adjustments to baseline. (A) describes integrated change control, which is the antidote. (C) describes normal agile re-planning within an existing scope envelope. (D) is descoping, the inverse problem.
