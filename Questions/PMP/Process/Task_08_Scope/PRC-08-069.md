# Question

A PM is starting requirements work for a new public sector portal serving culturally diverse citizens. Past projects have relied almost entirely on workshops, with limited success reaching non-English-speaking users. What technique mix should the PM plan?

## Options

- **A**: Use large generic workshops as before because consistency of technique aids requirements comparability.
- **B**: Rely on secondary research and published demographic reports to stand in for direct user elicitation.
- **C**: Delegate elicitation entirely to community liaison officers who already serve these citizen groups.
- **D**: Tailor elicitation to include interviews, observation, and translated surveys alongside workshops.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tailoring elicitation techniques to stakeholder context is core PMI practice; mixing techniques reaches users that a single technique excludes. (A) repeats a technique that has demonstrably failed the target users. (B) substitutes reports for direct engagement with the affected citizens. (C) outsources the PM's stakeholder engagement accountability without oversight.
