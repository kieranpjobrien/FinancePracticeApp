# Question

Which of the following best defines gold plating in project scope management?

## Options

- **A**: Delivering precisely the features defined in the approved scope baseline.
- **B**: The team adding extra features or quality the customer did not request or approve.
- **C**: Substituting premium materials at the customer's request to lift product quality.
- **D**: Exceeding the approved cost baseline to deliver agreed scope.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gold plating is unilateral over-delivery by the team — features or polish beyond what was authorised. PMI treats it as poor practice because it consumes capacity without customer endorsement and bypasses change control. (A) describes proper conformance, not gold plating. (C) is a customer-driven scope change, not gold plating. (D) is a cost overrun, an unrelated baseline issue.
