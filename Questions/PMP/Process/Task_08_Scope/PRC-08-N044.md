# Question

On a predictive railway-signalling project, the customer is keen to start the formal acceptance walkthrough of a batch of survey deliverables and has asked the project manager to schedule it for tomorrow. The internal quality team has not yet finished inspecting the deliverables against their specifications, and one inspection result is still outstanding. The customer relationship is good and the team is confident the work is correct. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the acceptance walkthrough until the quality team has completed its inspections, then present only the deliverables confirmed to meet specification.
- **B**: Run the acceptance walkthrough tomorrow as the customer requested, and address any specification gaps if the customer happens to raise them.
- **C**: Run the walkthrough on the already-inspected deliverables tomorrow and schedule a second session for the one still under inspection.
- **D**: Send the customer the inspection records completed so far and ask them to grant conditional acceptance subject to the final result.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Formal acceptance (Validate Scope) should follow verification of correctness (Control Quality) so the team presents only work it has already confirmed conforms to specification; seeking sign-off on unverified deliverables risks the customer rejecting defective work and eroding confidence. (A) sequences the two correctly and protects the acceptance event. (C) is tempting because it satisfies the customer's date for most items, but it still surfaces deliverables to formal acceptance before verification is complete and fragments one batch into an uncontrolled sign-off. (B) seeks acceptance with a known-open inspection and relies on the customer to police quality, which is the PM's job. (D) invites conditional acceptance of work whose correctness is not yet established, blurring the acceptance record and the baseline.
