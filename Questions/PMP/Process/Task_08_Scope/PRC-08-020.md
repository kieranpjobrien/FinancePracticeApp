# Question

An agile team struggles to estimate user stories because they regularly arrive in sprint planning with little detail. The PO insists that "agile means lightweight stories." What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Adopt a team-agreed Definition of Ready setting minimum detail before a story can be pulled into a sprint.
- **B**: Require fully detailed specifications before any story is added to the product backlog for consideration.
- **C**: Accept vague stories and clarify them inside the sprint when the developer is ready to start work.
- **D**: Have the developers themselves draft the acceptance criteria for stories they will pick up next.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A Definition of Ready preserves agile lightness for backlog items that are far away while ensuring stories are fit to commit when sprint planning happens. (B) imposes waterfall-style upfront specification on the entire backlog. (C) accepts predictable sprint failure as the cost of vagueness. (D) cedes business intent to the implementers, which is the PO's accountability.
