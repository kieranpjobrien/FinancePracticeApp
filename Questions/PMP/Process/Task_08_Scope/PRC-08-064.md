# Question

A contractor on a predictive infrastructure refresh has been quietly performing tasks that sit outside the statement of work at the request of a friendly site manager. The contractor now asks the PM to approve an invoice that covers the extra work. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the invoice entirely and tell the contractor to absorb the cost of the unauthorised extra tasks.
- **B**: Pay the invoice because the contractor did the work in good faith at a stakeholder's direct request.
- **C**: Assess whether the work has value, pursue a change request to authorise payment retroactively if justified.
- **D**: Approve the invoice informally to preserve the contractor relationship and address the process lapse later.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Procurement governance and integrated change control require a formal assessment and a change mechanism; retroactive change requests are an available path when justified. (A) punishes work that may have added value without assessment. (B) pays outside the contract and sets a precedent that undermines procurement governance. (D) normalises informal approvals and breaks audit trails.
