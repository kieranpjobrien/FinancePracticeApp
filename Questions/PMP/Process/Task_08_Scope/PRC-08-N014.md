# Question

A predictive five-year urban rail signalling programme must give the sponsor a baseline now. Years one and two can be specified in detail, but the scope of years three to five genuinely depends on a technology selection that will not be made until year two. The sponsor wants a single approved baseline that covers the whole programme and against which performance can be measured from day one. How should the project manager baseline the scope?

## Options

- **A**: Baseline near-term phases at work-package detail and later phases at a higher planning-package level, refining those packages into detail as the technology decision firms up.
- **B**: Baseline only years one and two now and defer baselining years three to five until the technology decision is made, since their scope is not yet knowable.
- **C**: Baseline all five years at work-package detail now using the most likely technology assumption, and rebaseline through change control once the actual decision is made.
- **D**: Baseline near-term phases in detail and represent the later phases in the baseline as a scope reserve, to be drawn down and defined when the technology is chosen.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Rolling-wave planning lets the entire programme be baselined now while holding far-term work at a coarser planning-package level and elaborating it as information arrives, which satisfies both the sponsor's need for a whole-programme baseline and the reality that later scope is not yet knowable. (A) does exactly this. (B) gives the sponsor only a partial baseline and no programme-level measurement point, which the scenario rules out. (C) fabricating work-package detail on an assumption creates a precise-looking baseline that is mostly invention and will churn, and routine rebaselining of guessed detail is not how genuine uncertainty is handled. (D) "scope reserve" misapplies the reserve concept: reserves cover cost or schedule uncertainty within defined scope, not undefined future scope; later phases should be carried as higher-level planning packages, not parked as an undefined reserve.
