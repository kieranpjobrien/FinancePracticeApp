# Question

During an agile logistics-tracking project, a developer finished early and used the spare time to add an animated dashboard the product owner never requested. It works, demos well, and the developer is proud of it, but it was not tied to any story and will carry ongoing maintenance. At the review, the product owner is pleasantly surprised and inclined to keep it. What is the most appropriate response from the scrum master?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner to treat the dashboard as a new backlog item and prioritise it against existing work before the team commits to maintaining it.
- **B**: Since the product owner is happy to keep it, accept the dashboard into the increment now and create a backlog item afterwards to record its maintenance.
- **C**: Let the team keep the dashboard but agree that any future spare-time work must first be raised with the product owner.
- **D**: Recognise the developer's initiative but have the dashboard removed from this increment because work that did not come from an accepted story should not ship.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The behaviour is gold-plating, and the principle being protected is that the product owner decides what has enough value to build and maintain. Even though the product owner likes it, the disciplined response is to put the dashboard into the backlog and have it prioritised against everything else before the team takes on its ongoing cost (A); the owner may well choose to keep it, but as a deliberate, ordered decision. (B) accepting it first and documenting later ratifies unrequested scope and normalises building outside the backlog, with prioritisation reduced to a formality. (C) fixing the future process is sensible but sidesteps the immediate question of whether this feature should be carried, leaving an unprioritised item in the product. (D) automatically removing functioning, wanted work is heavier-handed than needed and wastes value the product owner may legitimately decide to keep; the call belongs to the owner via the backlog, not to a removal rule.
