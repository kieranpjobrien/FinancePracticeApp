# Question

A predictive ERP project is 60% complete. The sponsor asks for a fundamental architecture change driven by a new compliance regime; a significant amount of completed work would be discarded. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the team to begin reworking immediately because the sponsor has the authority to redirect the project at will.
- **B**: Conduct a full impact analysis across scope, cost, schedule, quality, and risk, and present options to the sponsor and CCB.
- **C**: Reject the request to protect the work already invested and defend the integrity of the agreed scope baseline.
- **D**: Propose a hybrid approach that reuses existing components without first analysing technical feasibility of the reuse.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A change of this magnitude calls for analysis and explicit governance before commitment. (A) implements without giving the sponsor the trade-off picture they need. (C) is the sunk cost fallacy — past spend should not drive forward decisions. (D) commits to a specific solution before establishing whether it is feasible. Only (B) puts the decision on a defensible footing.
