# Question

A predictive payments-platform project has an approved scope statement listing high-level deliverables, but two team leads are already arguing over whether a set of reconciliation reports falls inside the "settlement module" deliverable. The sponsor wants build work to start next week. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Decompose the deliverables into a WBS and write the WBS dictionary entries so each work package is explicitly bounded.
- **B**: Raise a change request to add the reconciliation reports so the team can begin without further argument.
- **C**: Ask the two leads to split the difference and each take half of the reports into their estimates.
- **D**: Convene the change control board to rule on whether the reports are in or out of scope.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The argument exists precisely because the scope statement is still high-level; decomposition into a WBS and dictionary is what converts deliverables into precisely bounded work packages and resolves "in or out" questions at source. (A) fixes the root cause and completes the scope baseline. (B) assumes the reports are out of scope and adds work that may already be covered, inviting gold-plating and cost. (C) lets convenience rather than scope definition decide what work exists. (D) escalates to change control before the baseline is even detailed enough to assess a change against.
