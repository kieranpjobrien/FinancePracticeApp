# Question

Mid-execution on a predictive infrastructure refresh, a sponsor asks the PM to quietly absorb a small additional site into the rollout, arguing it is too minor to justify a change request. The extra site adds roughly three percent effort. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Absorb the site since the effort is small and declining would damage the sponsor relationship.
- **B**: Log the request in lessons learned and proceed without amending the current baseline documents.
- **C**: Raise a formal change request so the impact is assessed and the baseline is updated if approved.
- **D**: Add the site to a running list of minor items and process them together at the next milestone.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated change control applies regardless of size; bypassing it normalises uncontrolled scope expansion and breaks baseline integrity. (A) accommodates the sponsor but violates change control discipline. (B) records without acting, leaving scope ambiguous. (D) batches requests in a way that still sidesteps the process and defers transparency.
