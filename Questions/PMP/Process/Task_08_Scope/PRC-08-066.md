# Question

A predictive education platform project has drifted thirty percent over the scope baseline over four months because small informal changes kept being absorbed. The sponsor asks the PM for a recovery approach. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Analyse absorbed changes, re-baseline with approved additions, and tighten change control to stop future drift.
- **B**: Re-baseline the scope to match the current reality and keep the existing change control process unchanged.
- **C**: Reset to the original baseline and reject the drifted scope items regardless of their current value.
- **D**: Continue absorbing further changes since the baseline is already invalid after four months of drift.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Scope recovery requires analysis of what drift added value, a formal re-baseline of approved content, and a tightened control process to prevent recurrence. (B) relabels the drift as the plan without fixing the control gap that caused it. (C) throws away value already delivered without assessment. (D) institutionalises the failure mode that produced the drift.
