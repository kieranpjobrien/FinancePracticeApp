# Question

A Scrum product owner on a logistics platform has been adding items to the backlog without refining or ordering them. The backlog now exceeds four hundred items and the team struggles to plan sprints because priorities are unclear. What should the PM coach the product owner to do?

## Options

- **A**: Freeze all new backlog additions until the existing four hundred items are groomed and ordered by value.
- **B**: Delegate backlog ordering to the development team so the product owner can focus only on stakeholder engagement.
- **C**: Archive items older than ninety days on the assumption that stale items rarely deliver real value when eventually built.
- **D**: Run regular refinement sessions, keep the top of the backlog ordered and ready, and let lower items remain rough.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A healthy product backlog is deep but only the top is refined and ordered; refinement is continuous and value-driven. (A) imposes a waterfall gate on a continuous activity. (B) shifts product ownership accountability away from the product owner. (C) discards items by age rather than value and may lose genuine needs.
