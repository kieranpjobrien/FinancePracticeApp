# Question

A hybrid digital transformation programme has collected over three hundred requirements from a six-week elicitation phase. Stakeholders all describe their items as "must have". The next phase starts in two weeks. What should the PM do to make scope tractable?

## Options

- **A**: Include every requirement in initial scope and rely on execution to reveal which ones truly matter.
- **B**: Apply a structured prioritisation technique such as MoSCoW with stakeholders to rank value and dependencies.
- **C**: Let the delivery team decide priority based on technical feasibility from an engineering perspective.
- **D**: Select the top fifty requirements by stakeholder seniority so senior sponsors feel properly represented.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Structured prioritisation with stakeholders creates a defensible ranking based on value and dependencies rather than political pressure. (A) guarantees scope overload and pushes hard trade-offs into crisis mode. (C) replaces business value with technical convenience. (D) maps priority to politics instead of value and alienates non-senior stakeholders.
