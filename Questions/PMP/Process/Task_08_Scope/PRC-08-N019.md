# Question

Three days into a two-week sprint on an agile pensions-portal product, the product owner learns of a regulatory change that makes an uncommitted compliance feature urgent, with a deadline two sprints away. The committed sprint goal is unrelated and partly delivered. The product owner and project manager must decide how to respond. Which two actions are appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Have the product owner assess with the team whether the current sprint goal is still the most valuable focus, since only the product owner can cancel a sprint.
- **B**: Order the compliance feature near the top of the product backlog and confirm at the next refinement that it is ready for the upcoming sprint.
- **C**: Add the compliance feature into the current sprint and have the team de-scope an equivalent committed story to keep the sprint goal achievable.
- **D**: Let the current sprint finish as planned and address the compliance feature in the next sprint, since its deadline is two sprints out and the goal is partly done.
- **E**: Raise a change request through the project's change control board to authorise adding the compliance feature to the sprint backlog.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

The judgement is whether the regulatory change makes the current sprint goal the wrong focus. The appropriate moves are to have the product owner assess the goal's continued validity with the team, which is the precondition for any sprint cancellation (A), and to order the feature high on the product backlog and confirm its readiness for the next sprint, which the two-sprint runway comfortably allows (B). (C) injecting the feature and swapping out a committed story changes the sprint scope mid-sprint and is not warranted when the deadline can be met next sprint without disrupting in-progress work. (D) "do nothing until next sprint" is too passive: even if the work waits, the product owner should still re-order and ready the item now rather than leave it unaddressed. (E) a change control board governs the predictive baseline, not the contents of a sprint backlog, which the product owner and team manage directly; routing sprint scope through a CCB misapplies the mechanism.
