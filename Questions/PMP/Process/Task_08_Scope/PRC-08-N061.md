# Question

On an agile mobile-banking team, the product owner refines stories thoroughly and the developers say the refined stories are clear and well written. Even so, the developers report they "can't see what's coming" and arrive at iteration planning unable to commit confidently, while refinement sessions keep overrunning. Reviewing the backlog, the scrum master sees the product owner refines several iterations' worth of stories in depth each session and works through them in roughly the order they were captured. The next iteration planning is in two days. What should the scrum master do first?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the product owner to keep the backlog ordered so the items most likely to be pulled next sit at the top, and to refine only a small buffer of those top items just ahead of planning.
- **B**: Timebox each refinement session and cap the number of stories refined per session so the sessions stop overrunning.
- **C**: Bring the developers into refinement so they help break down and clarify the upcoming stories alongside the product owner.
- **D**: Move detailed refinement out of the shared session and have the product owner refine asynchronously, circulating ready stories before planning.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The symptoms point to two coupled problems: refinement effort is spread across far more stories than the team will pull, and it is not concentrated on the items that are actually next. Coaching the product owner to keep the backlog ordered by what comes next and to refine only a thin buffer of the top items (A) fixes both at once: the team gains a clear, value-ordered view of the upcoming work and the sessions shrink because only near-term items are elaborated in depth. (B) timeboxing and capping volume controls the overrun but, if the cap is applied to the same loosely ordered list, the team may still refine items it will not pull next and arrive at planning without the right stories ready. (C) involving developers improves shared understanding and is good practice, yet it does not address that the wrong items are being refined and could lengthen sessions further. (D) async refinement changes the mechanics but not the underlying ordering and scoping of what gets refined, so the team can still be left without the next items ready. Ordering and limiting refinement to the near-term buffer is the root fix and must come first.
