# Question

A senior developer on a SaaS project has implemented a polished dashboard enhancement that was never requested by the product owner, arguing customers will love it. The work took about four days outside the sprint commitment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the enhancement quietly because the team member worked hard and meant well.
- **B**: Reverse the changes immediately and issue a formal warning about unauthorised work.
- **C**: Ship the dashboard enhancement since the effort is already sunk and users will benefit from it.
- **D**: Discuss with the developer why unrequested scope hurts the team, and route the idea through the product owner.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Gold-plating undermines scope discipline, velocity, and trust with the product owner; coaching plus redirecting the idea into the backlog is the PMI-aligned response. (A) tacitly endorses unsanctioned scope and invites repetition. (B) punishes without coaching and damages psychological safety. (C) rewards gold-plating with the sunk-cost fallacy.
