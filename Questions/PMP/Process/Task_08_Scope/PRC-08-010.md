# Question

A product owner repeatedly changes story priorities mid-sprint, leaving items half-finished as the team pivots. Velocity is declining and morale is dropping. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the changes to continue because the PO is the single voice for priority.
- **B**: Coach the PO on sprint stability and route new priorities into refinement for the next sprint planning.
- **C**: Escalate to the PO's manager so leadership can intervene.
- **D**: Privately ask the team to disregard the PO's mid-sprint changes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Scrum Master's first response to a process problem with the PO is coaching, not escalation or workaround. PO authority over priority does not extend to disrupting an in-flight sprint; that is what backlog refinement and the next planning event are for. (A) tolerates a behaviour that is harming delivery. (C) skips the coaching step. (D) undermines the PO and corrodes team trust.
