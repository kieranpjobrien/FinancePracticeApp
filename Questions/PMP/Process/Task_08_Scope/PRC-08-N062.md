# Question

Midway through a predictive hospital records-migration project, the PM discovers two extra interfaces that were never in the scope baseline. A senior analyst added them informally over several weeks in response to direct clinician requests; the team believes they are genuinely needed for patient safety, the interfaces are partly built, and clinicians have already woven them into their workflow planning. Cost and schedule are now tracking over baseline. The PM has confirmed what was built and why. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a change request for the two interfaces so their full cost, schedule, and risk impact is analysed and the additions are formally authorised or rejected.
- **B**: Direct the analyst to keep the interfaces but stop adding any further scope, and fold the work already done into the next baseline update.
- **C**: Have the analyst pause work on the interfaces and notify the clinicians not to rely on them until a decision is made.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor that unauthorised work has put the project over baseline and ask how they want it handled.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM has already established the facts, so the next move must put the uncontrolled additions back under the change-control process that decides whether scope changes. Submitting a change request (A) is what triggers impact analysis and a recorded authorise-or-reject decision; it is the action that actually adjudicates the scope change rather than reacting around it, and it is reversible if the change is declined. (B) treats the additions as already accepted and quietly absorbs them into the baseline, which ratifies scope creep and skips the impact analysis and authorisation the baseline change requires. (C) pausing and managing clinician expectations is a defensible protective step, but stopping work neither assesses the change nor decides it, and prematurely telling clinicians to abandon a possibly safety-critical capability can do harm before any decision is made. (D) the sponsor will likely be involved, but escalating before the impact is analysed hands them a problem with no data to decide on; the change request is what produces that data, so it comes first.
