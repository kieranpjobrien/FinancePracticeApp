# Question

On a predictive aerospace test-rig project, an influential stakeholder submits a change request to drop one functional requirement to save cost, and wants a decision by Friday. The requirement was originally introduced to satisfy a safety-certification clause, and the PM suspects several design components and test cases depend on it. The PM maintains a requirements traceability matrix. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Trace the requirement through the matrix to its linked design elements, test cases, and the certification clause, then bring the assessed impact to the change control board.
- **B**: Convene the engineering and test leads to walk the design and test suite and report what would break if the requirement were removed.
- **C**: Approve the removal provisionally to capture the saving, on condition that any dependent items surfaced later are reinstated through a follow-up change.
- **D**: Consult the compliance lead on whether the certification clause still applies before spending effort tracing internal design and test dependencies.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The PM already holds the artefact that maps this requirement forward and backward to design, tests, and its regulatory driver, so the fastest reliable way to scope the impact is to trace it in the matrix and feed an evidenced assessment to the board that owns the decision. (B) asking the leads to manually re-discover the dependencies duplicates what the maintained matrix already captures and is slower and more error-prone under a tight deadline. (C) provisionally approving a change before its impact is understood is exactly the uncontrolled-change behaviour scope control exists to prevent, and "reinstate later" may be impossible once design work has moved on. (D) the compliance question is real, but checking the certification clause in isolation answers only one branch of the impact; the requirement may still be load-bearing for design and test even if the clause changed, so a single trace that captures all linkages is the better first move.
