# Question

Midway through a hospital fit-out project, the contractor confirms that a custom nurse-call panel has arrived with the wrong wiring loom and cannot be installed as scheduled this week. The project manager has been tracking the late delivery of this panel on the risk register for a month. What should the project manager do now?

## Options

- **A**: Move the entry from the risk register into the issue log, assign an owner and a resolution date, and manage it to closure.
- **B**: Leave it on the risk register and increase its probability score to reflect that the wiring is now confirmed wrong.
- **C**: Submit a change request to revise the project schedule before recording anything about the wiring fault.
- **D**: Record it as a lesson learned so the procurement team avoids the same supplier on future fit-outs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A risk that has occurred is now a present problem, so it belongs in the issue log with an owner and a target date and must be worked to closure. (B) keeps a realised event on the register of things that *might* happen, which is the wrong artefact and stops it from being actively managed. (C) jumps to a schedule change before the issue is even logged or its true impact assessed. (D) is a closure-stage activity that does nothing to resolve the live installation problem.
