# Question

A project manager on a university campus-WiFi upgrade is reviewing a mixed list of newly raised items and wants the issue log to hold only items that belong there rather than elsewhere. Which TWO of the following should be recorded as issues? (Select TWO.)

## Options

- **A**: A network switch delivered this week is dead on arrival and cannot be installed.
- **B**: A planned supplier price rise next quarter might increase the cost of remaining access points.
- **C**: The contracted cabling crew failed to show up today, leaving an installation front idle.
- **D**: A possible future change to campus building-access policy could affect later phases.
- **E**: The sponsor may, at some point, ask to extend coverage to the sports grounds.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

The issue log holds conditions that have already materialised and need resolving now, whereas conditional future possibilities belong in the risk register. (A) is a delivered switch that is dead on arrival and blocking installation today — a present problem, so an issue. (C) is a crew that did not turn up, leaving work idle right now, which is likewise a current problem to log and resolve. (B), (D), and (E) are all phrased as things that "might", "could", or "may" happen — a future price rise, a possible policy change, and a potential sponsor request — none of which has occurred, so they sit in the risk register (or as potential future changes), not the issue log. The trap is letting the seriousness of a future possibility tempt you into logging it as an issue before it has actually happened.
