# Question

An issue raised in today's daily stand-up on a hybrid logistics project looks identical to a risk that was accepted three months ago during planning. The team assumes the old risk response still applies. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Treat it as a fresh issue, assign an owner, and decouple it from the older risk entry for administrative clarity at reporting.
- **B**: Retire the previous risk entry, copy its response across to issues, and keep both logs numerically aligned for reporting consistency.
- **C**: Link the issue to the risk that materialised, re-evaluate whether the response is still adequate, and update both logs.
- **D**: Close the original risk as obsolete and allow the issue log to drive all further management of the problem going forward.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI treats a realised risk as the transition point where risk and issue management integrate; the prior response is a starting hypothesis, not a guarantee. (C) preserves traceability and re-tests the response against current reality. (A) loses the planning history that informs response choice. (B) enforces cosmetic alignment without reassessment. (D) discards useful risk context prematurely.
