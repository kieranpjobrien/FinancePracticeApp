# Question

Two weeks before go-live on a hybrid retail platform, a medium-severity issue is raised that the team could fix but would consume testing capacity reserved for critical path scenarios. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Fix the issue immediately because every open issue at go-live is a risk to customer experience and brand reputation on launch.
- **B**: Evaluate deferral impact against the risk to critical path testing, consult stakeholders, and decide transparently whether to fix or defer.
- **C**: Defer the issue to the post-go-live hypercare period without analysis because critical path testing must remain fully protected always.
- **D**: Ask the testing team to work overtime so both the medium issue and critical path scenarios are completed before launch.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects late-stage decisions to be made with analysis, stakeholder input, and transparency, not by default rules in either direction. (B) weighs the genuine trade-off and documents the decision. (A) applies a blanket rule without assessing critical path consequences. (C) defers without analysis and may miss a genuine launch risk. (D) imposes unsustainable pace to avoid making a choice.
