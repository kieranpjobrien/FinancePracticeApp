# Question

Two senior engineers on a predictive aerospace project disagree strongly on the cause of a recurring integration issue and have stopped collaborating. The PM has logged the issue but resolution is stalled. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Assign the issue to the more experienced engineer and remove the other from the resolution path to restore momentum quickly.
- **B**: Escalate the disagreement to the engineering director and ask her to decide which engineer's theory the team should pursue.
- **C**: Temporarily reassign the issue to an external consultant who can deliver an unbiased technical verdict on the integration behaviour.
- **D**: Facilitate a structured problem-solving session using data and a shared root cause technique so both perspectives surface and converge.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's conflict approach favours collaborative problem-solving grounded in shared data, preserving team relationships and capability. (D) applies facilitation and evidence to resolve both the technical and interpersonal blockage. (A) sidelines expertise and rewards withdrawal. (B) escalates a dispute the PM should facilitate. (C) outsources knowledge the team already holds.
