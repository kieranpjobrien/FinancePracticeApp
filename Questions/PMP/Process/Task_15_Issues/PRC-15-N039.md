# Question

Midway through a sprint on an insurance-claims modernisation team, three separate impediments surface the same morning: a key API the team depends on is returning errors, a developer is off sick for the rest of the sprint, and the product owner has not yet clarified the acceptance criteria for the next story in the sprint backlog. The sprint goal is now at risk. Which actions are appropriate for the scrum master to take in managing these impediments? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Make the impediments and their impact on the sprint goal visible, and work the most threatening ones first.
- **B**: Engage the right people for each impediment — the API's owning team, the developers on capacity, and the product owner on the criteria.
- **C**: Help the team and product owner consider re-scoping the sprint backlog if the combined impact genuinely threatens the sprint goal.
- **D**: Take ownership of clearing all three impediments yourself so the developers can stay heads-down on delivery for the rest of the sprint.
- **E**: Convene the team to decide which story to drop, and have that decision communicated to stakeholders as the new sprint plan without involving the product owner, who is the source of one of the impediments.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Managing impediments means surfacing them and tackling the most damaging first (A), routing each to the people who can actually resolve it (B), and, if the sprint goal is truly threatened, helping the team and product owner re-scope transparently (C). (D) sounds protective but the scrum master cannot personally resolve a sick developer's capacity or clarify acceptance criteria that only the product owner owns; shielding the team here means cutting them off from the very people (B) who must be engaged. (E) re-scopes without the product owner — even though the product owner is causing one impediment, they own the backlog, so changing committed scope behind them breaches transparency and their accountability; the answer is to engage them on the criteria and on any re-scope, not to route around them.
