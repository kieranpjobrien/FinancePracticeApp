# Question

A project manager taking over a troubled hybrid rail-signalling project finds an issue log that is little more than a list of one-line problem descriptions. Some entries are months old with no apparent movement, and the sponsor complains she "never knows where anything stands." The project manager wants to make the log a genuine management tool that drives issues to closure and supports clear reporting. Which THREE attributes are most important to add to each open entry to achieve this? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A single named owner accountable for resolving the entry.
- **B**: A current status and a target resolution date.
- **C**: The estimated monetary value of the contract the issue relates to.
- **D**: A priority reflecting urgency and impact on objectives.
- **E**: The name of every team member who has ever commented on the entry.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

An issue log functions as a management tool when each entry has an accountable owner (A), a visible status with a target date that exposes whether it is moving or stalled (B), and a priority that lets the team and sponsor focus on what matters most (D); together these directly answer the sponsor's complaint that she never knows where anything stands and drive entries to closure. The contract's monetary value is not a property of the issue and does not help track or resolve it, so it adds noise rather than control (C). Listing everyone who has ever commented obscures accountability by diffusing it across many names, the opposite of assigning a single owner, and bloats the log without aiding resolution (E).
