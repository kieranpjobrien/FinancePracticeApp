# Question

An issue has been escalated three times. The sponsor says it is a team problem; the team says it is organisational; middle management says they lack the authority to act. The project keeps absorbing the impact. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document history, impact, escalation attempts, and a proposed action, then request a single decision meeting with all parties present.
- **B**: Continue escalating to ever higher levels until someone finally takes the decision off the table and resolves the deadlock.
- **C**: Build a permanent workaround into the project and accept the residual impact as an ongoing cost of doing business in this area.
- **D**: Close the issue in the log on the basis that no party has accepted ownership and treat it as formally out of scope now.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Escalation ping-pong is a symptom of unclear ownership. (A) breaks the cycle by consolidating evidence and forcing a single conversation with everyone present. (B) repeats the same pattern that has not worked. (C) bakes in damage rather than resolving it. (D) makes the log lie about a real, ongoing impact.
