# Question

An external enterprise customer on a Scrum engagement has started logging issues directly into the team's internal tracker, bypassing the product owner. The team is reacting to each one in real time. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Permit the customer's direct logging since they are the ultimate user and their feedback carries the most authentic signal about value.
- **B**: Block the customer from the tracker immediately and require all future feedback to be routed through formal contract communication channels.
- **C**: Work with the product owner to establish a feedback intake channel, triage customer input there, and feed validated items into the backlog properly.
- **D**: Keep the customer's tracker access but require them to tag items as "for review" so the team can defer most of them safely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects clear intake pathways that respect role accountabilities, and the product owner owns what enters the backlog. (C) preserves customer engagement while restoring ordering discipline. (A) lets direct input bypass the prioritisation role. (B) damages a valuable stakeholder relationship without offering an alternative channel. (D) leaves the anti-pattern in place with weaker labels.
