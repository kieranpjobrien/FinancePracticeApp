# Question

During testing on a predictive pharmaceutical system, a team member privately tells the PM about an issue that, if disclosed, could trigger a regulatory audit. The team member asks the PM to keep it off the log. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the issue off the log but monitor privately and raise it only if the situation worsens during later testing rounds.
- **B**: Ask the team member to investigate further and report back before any formal logging or disclosure decision is taken forward.
- **C**: Record the issue in the log using generic language that avoids triggering a regulatory disclosure threshold at this early stage.
- **D**: Log the issue fully, inform the compliance officer, and follow the organisation's regulatory disclosure procedures immediately and accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics treats compliance and honest reporting as non-negotiable, regardless of convenience. (D) upholds transparency and regulatory duty. (A) conceals a material compliance issue from governance. (C) disguises a reportable matter through careful wording. (B) uses further investigation as a stalling tactic to avoid required disclosure.
