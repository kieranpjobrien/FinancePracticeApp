# Question

A newly formed agile team for a mobile-banking app keeps raising blockers verbally during the day, but they are forgotten by the next standup and several have slipped without being addressed. The scrum master wants a lightweight way to make sure blockers are visible, tracked, and not lost between conversations. What should the scrum master do?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain a visible impediment board or list where each blocker is recorded with an owner and tracked to resolution.
- **B**: Ask each developer to email the scrum master whenever they hit a blocker.
- **C**: Add a recurring agenda item to the monthly steering meeting to review all blockers.
- **D**: Tell the team to raise blockers only at the daily scrum to keep them in one place.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A visible, shared impediment board or list gives the team a single transparent place where every blocker is captured with an owner and visibly tracked until cleared, which directly solves blockers being forgotten between conversations (A). Routing blockers through private emails to the scrum master hides them from the team and creates a bottleneck rather than shared visibility (B). A monthly steering review is far too slow for impediments that need clearing within a sprint, and most never warrant that level (C). Restricting blockers to the daily scrum delays surfacing urgent ones by up to a day and still relies on memory, which is what failed in the first place (D).
