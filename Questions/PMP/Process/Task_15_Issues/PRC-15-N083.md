# Question

A project manager on a hospital fit-out project is updating the project documentation after a planning call. A subcontractor reports that a specialist ceiling component is sourced from one overseas supplier, that a port-workers' ballot for strike action next month is under way, and that delivery would slip if the ballot passes and action follows. The component is on schedule today and installation is six weeks out. Where should the project manager primarily capture this?

## Options

- **A**: In the risk register, with a probability-and-impact assessment and a planned response.
- **B**: In the assumption log, recording the single-source supply as a planning dependency.
- **C**: In the issue log, with an owner tasked to secure the delivery date.
- **D**: In the change log, pending a schedule change request for the affected installation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Two facts are in play: a present condition (single-source supply) and a possible future event (a delivery slip that occurs only if the ballot passes and strike action follows). PMI classifies an uncertain future event with a potential effect as a risk, so the threat belongs in the risk register with an assessment and a planned response (A) — that is the artefact that captures probability, impact and a response. The assumption log (B) is the close distractor: the sole-source arrangement is a genuine planning condition worth recording there, but the *thing requiring management* is the strike-driven delay, which is an uncertainty, not an assumption taken as true. The issue log (C) is for problems that have already occurred and need resolving now; nothing has slipped, so logging it as an issue misclassifies a possibility as a present problem. The change log (D) is premature because no change has been raised or approved and there is nothing to change while delivery remains on schedule. The discriminator is separating the standing condition from the future uncertainty and filing the uncertainty where it can be assessed and responded to.
