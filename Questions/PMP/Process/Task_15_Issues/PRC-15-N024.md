# Question

On a hybrid payments-platform programme, the same category of defect — transactions duplicated under retry conditions — has been logged and closed three separate times over four months, each time with a targeted code fix. It has now reappeared a fourth time in production. The team is ready to apply another point fix to close it again. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the point fix again, since it has successfully closed this issue on each previous occasion and is the fastest route back to stability.
- **B**: Pause the point fix, treat the recurrence as a signal of an unresolved root cause, and direct a root-cause analysis before any further patch is applied.
- **C**: Accept the duplicates as a known limitation of the retry design and add a manual reconciliation step to net them out each day.
- **D**: Reopen the three previously closed issues and reassign them to the original owners to investigate why their fixes failed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A defect that recurs after three "fixes" indicates the team has been treating symptoms, not the cause, so the disciplined move is to stop patching and run a proper root-cause analysis. (A) repeats a pattern that has already failed three times and all but guarantees a fifth recurrence. (C) institutionalises a daily manual workaround for a defect in a payments system, accepting financial risk instead of fixing it. (D) creates churn by reopening closed records and blames prior owners rather than investigating the shared underlying cause that now needs ownership.
