# Question

A distributed team spread across four time zones raises issues during local working hours, but follow-up chats happen asynchronously and key context is lost between handoffs. The PM is seeing duplicated investigation work. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Mandate a single synchronous daily meeting that every team member must attend regardless of their local time zone differences globally.
- **B**: Assign each issue to a single owner from one time zone and require that person to drive it through to closure themselves.
- **C**: Restrict issue logging to two windows per day when multiple time zones overlap so live discussion is always available to everyone.
- **D**: Strengthen the log as single source of truth, require timestamped context before handoff, and add brief handover rituals daily.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Distributed teams rely on rich written artefacts and disciplined handoffs rather than synchronous heroics, and the issue log is the correct artefact. (D) upgrades the log to match the working model. (A) imposes unsustainable attendance on most of the team. (B) solves duplication by removing collaboration entirely. (C) restricts legitimate work to preserve a synchronous habit.
