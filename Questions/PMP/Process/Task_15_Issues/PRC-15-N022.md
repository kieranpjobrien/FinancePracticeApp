# Question

Two days into a two-week sprint, the team's continuous-integration pipeline starts rejecting all builds because a third-party licence the build server depends on has expired. No story can be merged until it is renewed, and procurement says a renewal normally takes five working days. The team is fully blocked. What is the scrum master's best first move?

## Options

- **A**: Log the impediment, then immediately work the renewal as an escalation with procurement and finance, pursuing an emergency or temporary licence to unblock the team fast.
- **B**: Cancel the sprint, since with the pipeline down the sprint goal can no longer be met within the timebox.
- **C**: Have developers keep coding locally and stack up un-merged branches until the licence is renewed in five days.
- **D**: Add a "renew CI licence" item to the product backlog for the product owner to prioritise in a future sprint.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A total build blockage is a live impediment the scrum master owns, and the priority is to clear it fast by escalating the renewal and chasing an emergency licence rather than accepting the standard five-day lead time. (B) is premature; the impediment may be cleared in hours, and cancelling a sprint is the product owner's call and a last resort. (C) lets work pile up unintegrated, creating a merge and quality risk and doing nothing to remove the block. (D) treats an urgent operational blocker as future product scope, leaving the team idle now.
