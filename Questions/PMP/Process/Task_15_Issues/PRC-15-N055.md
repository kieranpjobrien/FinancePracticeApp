# Question

During a daily scrum, a developer says she cannot progress her current story until she has a data extract that only another developer, also in the meeting, can produce. The two have not yet discussed how long it will take or how it fits against the second developer's own sprint commitments. The scrum master is facilitating and the standup is partway through its timebox. What is the most appropriate way to handle this?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the dependency in the standup and have the two developers meet immediately afterwards to agree the work and timing, with the scrum master following up that it is resolved.
- **B**: Pause the round-robin so the two developers can settle the sequencing and a target time then and there, while the rest of the team waits.
- **C**: Have the scrum master record it as an impediment on the impediment board and own driving it to closure on the team's behalf.
- **D**: Ask the product owner, if present, to confirm which of the two developers' items takes priority before the developers agree how to proceed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The give-away the question conceals is that this dependency sits entirely inside the team and between two people who are both in the room — so the standup's job is to surface it, and the developers themselves are best placed to self-organise the resolution straight afterwards, with the scrum master tracking that it actually closes. (B) converts the timeboxed standup into a working session and makes the whole team wait on a two-person conversation. (C) has the scrum master assume ownership of a problem the developers can resolve directly; impediments are escalated when the team cannot clear them itself, which is not yet established here. (D) reaches for the product owner to arbitrate a sequencing question the two developers can usually settle between themselves, and pre-empts the conversation they have not yet had.
