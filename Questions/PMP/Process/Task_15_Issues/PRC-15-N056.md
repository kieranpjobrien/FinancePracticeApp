# Question

A project manager is reviewing the issue log entry for a newly raised problem before tracking it. Several fields could be captured. The PM wants the entry to be genuinely actionable and ready for follow-up at the next log review, without front-loading detail that belongs to later analysis. Which THREE attributes most directly serve that aim? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A single accountable owner responsible for driving the issue to resolution.
- **B**: A target resolution date or review date by which progress is expected.
- **C**: The current status and the agreed next action.
- **D**: A confirmed root cause of the problem, established before the entry is logged.
- **E**: A full list of every stakeholder who could conceivably be affected.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

An actionable entry that can be picked up at the next review needs three things: a single accountable owner so it is clear who moves it (A), a target or review date that creates a follow-up cadence (B), and a current status with the agreed next action so the next reviewer knows what happens next (C). The give-away the question hides is the difference between what makes an issue *trackable now* and what is *the work of resolving it*: (D) is part of that later resolution work — demanding a confirmed root cause before logging would delay capture and pre-empt the investigation the owner is assigned to do. (E) substitutes breadth for usefulness; an exhaustive "could conceivably be affected" list is noise at logging, where naming the genuinely impacted parties (often implicit in the impact note) is enough.
