# Question

On a manufacturing-line commissioning project, the owner of an issue reports that the faulty sensor calibration causing rejected parts has been corrected and the line is now producing in tolerance. The owner asks the project manager to mark the issue closed in the log. What should the project manager do before closing it?

## Options

- **A**: Close the issue immediately on the owner's word, since the owner is accountable for that issue.
- **B**: Leave the issue open indefinitely in case the calibration drifts again at some point in the future.
- **C**: Delete the issue entry from the log entirely now that the underlying problem is fixed.
- **D**: Confirm the fix has actually resolved the problem and the agreed outcome is met, then record the resolution and close the issue.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Closing an issue requires verifying the resolution genuinely met the intended outcome and recording it, which preserves traceability and prevents premature closure. (A) closes on assertion alone with no verification, risking a reopen. (B) refuses to close a resolved issue, cluttering the log; any future drift is a new issue or a risk. (C) destroys the historical record, losing the resolution detail that informs audits and lessons learned.
