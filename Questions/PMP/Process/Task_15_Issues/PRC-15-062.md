# Question

On a hybrid utilities project, issues in the log have owners but many have sat untouched for weeks because owners claim they are busy with higher-priority work. The issues are genuinely blocking progress elsewhere. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet with each owner to understand competing priorities, renegotiate commitments with their line managers, and reset realistic target dates.
- **B**: Reassign all stalled issues to yourself and personally work through them over the next two weeks to restore progress.
- **C**: Escalate every stalled issue to the steering committee in a single bundle and request a formal resource intervention from above.
- **D**: Close any issue untouched for more than two weeks on the assumption it is no longer material to project delivery outcomes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI favours negotiated commitments grounded in visibility of competing demands, engaging line managers who control priorities. (A) restores realistic ownership and reveals resource constraints honestly. (B) creates a new bottleneck at the PM. (C) escalates before attempting local negotiation. (D) closes issues that remain real blockers simply because they are neglected.
