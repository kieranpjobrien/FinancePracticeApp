# Question

A risk that has been actively monitored on the risk register actually occurs. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Convert the risk to an issue, log it in the issue log, and manage it using the planned risk response.
- **B**: Leave the entry on the risk register and adjust its probability to 100% so the event history stays in one place.
- **C**: Remove the entry from the risk register entirely since risks that have already occurred no longer require active tracking.
- **D**: Escalate immediately to the project sponsor regardless of the impact level or response plan already in place.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A realised risk becomes an issue and moves from the risk register into the issue log. (A) is the textbook transition. (B) keeps the realised event misclassified as still uncertain. (C) loses tracking of an event that is now actively impacting the project. (D) prescribes blanket escalation regardless of materiality.
