# Question

On a hybrid retail-logistics programme, the project manager has logged an issue: two business units have given the delivery team directly contradictory acceptance criteria for the same warehouse module, and each insists its version is correct. The team has stopped on this module, unable to proceed without one agreed definition. The PM has already facilitated a joint working session; neither unit conceded, and neither unit's head reports to the PM. Both units' directors sit on the programme board chaired by the sponsor. What should the PM do next?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the programme sponsor, presenting both criteria sets, the delivery impact, and a recommended option for decision.
- **B**: Apply the criteria set that best aligns with the programme's documented business case, recording the rationale in the issue log.
- **C**: Re-convene the two units with a neutral facilitator and a deadline, attempting one more time to reach a binding agreement between them.
- **D**: Proceed on unblocked modules and hold this one open in the issue log while the two units continue to seek agreement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The blocker is a decision between two peer business units that are deadlocked, do not report to the PM, and have already failed PM-led facilitation — the textbook trigger for escalation to an authority who can adjudicate. The right move escalates to the sponsor, who chairs the board both units answer to, and crucially carries the conflicting criteria, the delivery impact, *and* a recommended option so the decision is informed rather than dumped upward (A). Deciding by reference to the business case (B) is tempting and at least has a rationale, but it has the PM making a binding business-acceptance call between two units who outrank the PM on this scope — overstepping authority even when the logic seems sound. One more facilitated attempt (C) is not unreasonable, but facilitation between these two parties has already failed and the work is stopped; repeating it risks further delay without any new mechanism to break a genuine deadlock. Continuing elsewhere (D) is a fine *parallel* action but is not by itself a path to resolving this issue — it lets a work-stopping deadlock drift with no force behind it. The discriminator is recognising the decision sits above the PM and that escalation should arrive with options, not just a problem.
