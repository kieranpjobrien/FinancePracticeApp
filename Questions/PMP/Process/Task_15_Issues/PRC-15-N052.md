# Question

A defect is found in a feature that was released to production two sprints ago. It affects a small number of users but does not breach any regulatory or safety requirement. The development team wants to fix it in the current sprint; the scrum master logs it. Who should decide whether and when the fix is scheduled, and on what basis?

## Options

- **A**: The development team, because they have the technical context and found the defect.
- **B**: The product owner, who weighs the defect's user and business impact against other backlog items to decide its priority.
- **C**: The scrum master, because managing issues and impediments is the scrum master's accountability.
- **D**: The project sponsor, because any production defect is a governance matter requiring sponsor sign-off.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Prioritising what the team works on, including production defects without an overriding compliance driver, is the product owner's call based on relative value against the rest of the backlog. (A) gives the team scheduling authority that belongs to the product owner, though their input on effort is valuable. (C) confuses removing impediments with deciding backlog priority. (D) escalates a low-impact, non-regulatory defect to the sponsor unnecessarily, which is disproportionate.
