# Question

On an airport baggage-system upgrade, an issue arises: a structural survey reveals that a planned conveyor mount cannot be fixed to the existing slab without reinforcement, blocking installation in that zone. The project manager has three candidate responses of differing cost, schedule, and risk profiles, and the decision sits within the project manager's delegated authority. The team is waiting and pressing for a quick call. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Briefly evaluate the options against cost, schedule, and risk, then decide and direct the team accordingly.
- **B**: Pick the cheapest option immediately to keep the cost baseline intact and move on.
- **C**: Escalate the choice to the steering committee despite holding the authority to decide.
- **D**: Let the installation team choose whichever option they consider easiest to execute.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Resolving an issue within one's authority still calls for a deliberate, if rapid, comparison of the realistic options against cost, schedule, and risk so the decision is defensible, after which the project manager decides and directs (A). Defaulting to the cheapest option ignores schedule and risk trade-offs and may prove a false economy if it is slower or riskier (B). Escalating a decision the project manager is empowered to make abdicates the role, slows resolution, and burdens governance unnecessarily (C). Delegating the choice to the installation team on the basis of execution ease alone disregards cost, schedule, and risk considerations the project manager is accountable for (D).
