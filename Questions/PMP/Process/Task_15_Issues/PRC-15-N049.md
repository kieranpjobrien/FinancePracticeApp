# Question

A project manager discovers that a defect found during user-acceptance testing will require rework affecting the launch date. The PM has not yet analysed the full schedule or cost impact. A senior stakeholder, hearing a rumour, demands an immediate commitment on the new launch date in a corridor conversation. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Give the stakeholder a confident best-guess date on the spot to demonstrate the project is in control.
- **B**: Tell the stakeholder the issue is logged and being assessed, and commit to providing the impact analysis and revised date by a specific near-term time.
- **C**: Decline to discuss the matter until the issue is fully resolved and the rework is complete.
- **D**: Reassure the stakeholder that the original launch date still stands while the team works to absorb the impact quietly.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The professional move is to acknowledge the issue, confirm it is being assessed, and commit to a firm time for a substantiated impact and date, which manages the stakeholder without fabricating numbers. (A) commits to a date with no analysis, creating a false expectation likely to be missed. (C) stonewalls a legitimate stakeholder concern and damages trust. (D) conceals a known schedule threat and misrepresents project status, which breaches transparency and will rebound badly when the date slips.
