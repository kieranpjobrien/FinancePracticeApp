# Question

A project manager on a regional water-treatment upgrade has eleven open issues in the log. The sponsor asks why a particular issue, raised three weeks ago, still has no resolution date while a newer one raised yesterday already has an owner actively working it. Both were logged correctly, and the newer issue threatens an imminent commissioning milestone while the older one has, so far, no schedule or cost consequence. What is the best basis on which the PM should be sequencing the two?

## Options

- **A**: Work issues strictly in the order they were raised, so the three-week-old issue takes precedence and the newer one waits its turn for fairness and auditability.
- **B**: Work the issue with the nearer external deadline and the larger threat to project objectives first, regardless of when each was raised.
- **C**: Work whichever issue currently has a willing, available owner, since an issue with no one free to take it cannot be progressed anyway.
- **D**: Work the older issue first specifically to clear the ageing backlog, because the longer an issue stays open the more its impact compounds.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The issue log is a prioritised tool: issues are sequenced by their impact and urgency to project objectives, so a high-impact item with an imminent deadline rightly outranks a lower-impact one raised weeks earlier. (B) states exactly that basis. (A) sounds fair and audit-friendly but a strict first-in-first-out order would let a serious, time-critical issue wait behind a benign older one — order of arrival is not impact. (C) describes a real practical constraint, but availability of an owner is a resourcing problem to solve, not the principle that should set priority; letting "who happens to be free" drive sequencing lets a critical issue stall because its owner is busy. (D) is the most tempting trap: impact can compound with age, yet here the facts say the older issue still has no schedule or cost consequence while the newer one threatens a milestone now, so age alone does not justify jumping it ahead. The discriminator is impact and urgency, not arrival order, owner availability, or age.
