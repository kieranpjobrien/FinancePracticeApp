# Question

A predictive aerospace programme contains four interdependent projects. One project's issue log shows an item that will, if unresolved, cascade into the other three. The PM owns only one project. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Inform the programme manager with cascade analysis, collaborate with the other PMs, and plan a coordinated cross-project response under governance.
- **B**: Resolve the issue on the owning project first and allow the other PMs to respond to any cascade as it reaches them later.
- **C**: Take ownership across all four projects temporarily to ensure consistent handling of the cascading dependency across the programme going forward.
- **D**: Delay resolution on your project until the other three PMs have formally agreed to accept the cascade on their own timelines.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cross-project cascades require programme-level visibility and coordinated response, and the PM's first duty is to surface and collaborate. (A) engages the correct authority and peers with the right data. (B) lets a foreseeable cascade happen by inaction. (C) assumes authority the PM does not hold. (D) delays necessary resolution for negotiation no one has requested.
