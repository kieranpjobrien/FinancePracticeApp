# Question

Why does PMI guidance recommend assigning a single owner to each entry in the issue log?

## Options

- **A**: To create a clear record of who is to blame if the issue is not resolved on time.
- **B**: To establish unambiguous accountability for driving the issue to resolution, even when others contribute.
- **C**: To restrict the work on the issue to one person so changes do not collide.
- **D**: To free the project manager from involvement in resolving the issue.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI uses single ownership to make accountability unambiguous, not to assign blame or restrict collaboration. (B) captures that. (A) frames ownership as blame, which is the opposite of healthy issue culture. (C) misreads ownership as exclusive access. (D) implies the PM steps away from issue management, which is incorrect; the PM oversees the log.
