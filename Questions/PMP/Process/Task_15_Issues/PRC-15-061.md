# Question

During a Scrum retrospective on a media platform, the team notes that the same three categories of issue have recurred across five sprints. Previous action items were logged but never implemented. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Record the three categories again as action items and ask the team to commit to working on them during the next sprint.
- **B**: Examine why past actions were not implemented, address those barriers, and tie resulting actions to owners and review points.
- **C**: Raise the recurring categories with senior management so resources can be assigned to tackle them outside the team's sprint workload.
- **D**: Accept the categories as structural and update the project's risk register so the team stops raising them in future retrospectives.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI treats retrospectives as genuine continuous improvement, so unimplemented actions are themselves a pattern to inspect. (B) addresses the meta-problem of stalled improvement and creates accountability. (A) repeats the same failed pattern. (C) escalates before the team investigates its own barriers. (D) normalises the recurrence rather than solving it.
