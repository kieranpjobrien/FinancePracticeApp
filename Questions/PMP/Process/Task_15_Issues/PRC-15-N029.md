# Question

An issue on a telecoms tower-rollout project is owned by a subcontractor's site lead, who has missed two agreed resolution dates and stopped responding to the project manager's emails. The unresolved issue is now days away from delaying a scheduled crew mobilisation. The PM has no direct authority over the subcontractor's staff. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reassign the issue to a member of the PM's own team and have them resolve it directly on the subcontractor's behalf.
- **B**: Escalate through the subcontractor's management or the contract's defined point of contact, stating the missed dates and the mobilisation impact, and ask for action by a firm deadline.
- **C**: Remove the issue from the log since the assigned owner is unresponsive and continuing to chase it is unproductive.
- **D**: Wait one more cycle to see whether the site lead resumes responding before taking any further step.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The owner has failed and gone silent on a contractually delivered issue, so the PM escalates through the subcontractor's management chain or the agreed contact, making the impact and deadline explicit. (A) has the PM's team perform another firm's contracted work, blurring responsibility and possibly breaching the contract. (C) deletes a live, impact-bearing issue to avoid the discomfort of chasing it. (D) gives more time to an owner who has already missed two dates while a hard deadline approaches.
