# Question

During construction of a data-centre fit-out, the client's representative walks the site and tells the project manager that the agreed cable-tray routing "won't work for us" and asks for it to be rerouted along a different wall. The current routing fully meets the approved design and specification. The project manager records the conversation. To handle this correctly, what should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Raise it through the change control process as a requested scope change, with cost and schedule impact assessed.
- **B**: Log it as an issue and assign an owner to reroute the cable trays promptly.
- **C**: Instruct the crew to reroute the trays immediately to keep the client satisfied.
- **D**: Decline the request because the installed routing already conforms to the approved design.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The installed work conforms to the approved design, so the client is asking for something different from the baseline — a scope change — which must go through change control where its cost and schedule impact are assessed and a decision authorised (A). Treating it as an issue and rerouting implies a problem with conforming work and bypasses the change process, exposing the project to unrecovered cost and uncontrolled scope (B). Acting immediately commits resources to unapproved rework and undermines change control and the agreed baseline (C). Flatly declining ignores a legitimate stakeholder need that change control exists to evaluate; the request may well be worth approving once its impact is known (D).
