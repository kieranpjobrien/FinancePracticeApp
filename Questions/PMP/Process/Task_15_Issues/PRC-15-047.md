# Question

A senior stakeholder on a predictive banking programme asks the PM to remove a politically sensitive issue from the log before the steering committee sees it. The issue is genuine and affects delivery. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Remove the entry and track the issue informally offline so the steering committee receives a less contentious status pack from delivery.
- **B**: Keep the entry but soften the wording and the impact rating so the issue does not attract unwanted attention at the committee.
- **C**: Retain the entry in the log with accurate wording, discuss the concern privately, and report it honestly to the committee.
- **D**: Defer the issue to the next reporting cycle to give the sponsor time to resolve the political sensitivity behind the scenes.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics requires honest reporting and prohibits misleading stakeholders, regardless of internal pressure. (C) preserves integrity while acknowledging the political concern through dialogue. (A) conceals material information from a governance body. (B) engages in the same deception using softer language. (D) uses timing to avoid disclosure that should happen now.
