# Question

On a hybrid insurance modernisation, the PM performs quarterly analysis and discovers that 70 percent of issues originated in integration points between two specific legacy systems. The team has been closing each one individually. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue closing issues individually because each one has unique contextual details that resist generalised treatment across the portfolio.
- **B**: Commission a targeted architectural review of the integration layer, propose a structural remediation, and present options with impact data to governance.
- **C**: Add more testers to accelerate individual issue closure so the integration-related backlog stops expanding month over month ahead.
- **D**: Report the pattern in the status update and rely on the steering committee to decide whether structural remediation is warranted now.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to act on systemic findings with analysis and options, not merely to report the pattern or scale symptom-fixing. (B) drives toward root cause correction with evidence. (A) ignores a pattern visible across many individual items. (C) increases throughput of symptom fixes. (D) delegates the analysis the PM should perform before escalating.
