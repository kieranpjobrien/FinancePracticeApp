# Question

The team has spent two days investigating a complex production issue and is no closer to root cause. What should the project manager suggest?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the most likely fix from the team's notes and close the investigation when the symptom disappears from production.
- **B**: Time-box further investigation, bring in additional expertise, and reassess whether to apply an interim mitigation now.
- **C**: Continue the investigation with the current team for as long as it takes since they have the most context and background.
- **D**: Escalate the lack of progress to engineering leadership as a team performance concern that needs management attention.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) reflects PMI's posture on investigation: bound the work, escalate the expertise, and decouple mitigation from root cause where impact is ongoing. (A) treats symptom disappearance as resolution. (C) lets investigation run open-ended at the cost of other work. (D) reframes a hard problem as a performance issue, which damages the team and rarely helps.
