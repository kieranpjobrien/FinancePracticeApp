# Question

A project manager on a hybrid pharmaceutical packaging project is triaging four newly surfaced situations. Each calls for a different first response. Match each situation to the most appropriate one.

## Items

- **1**: A regulatory submission was due to the authority yesterday and was not sent; the line cannot ship product until it is filed, and the PM's team can prepare and submit it.
- **2**: A validation step has failed the same way in two of the last three batches; each time the team re-ran it on the affected line, and now it has failed on a third line.
- **3**: The team has logged an issue that the approved label artwork conflicts with a new market's mandatory wording; resolving it requires the marketing director to authorise a wording change, and it has sat unanswered.
- **4**: An auditor has noted that a guideline revision under consultation, were it adopted next year, would require a change to the current sterilisation record format.

## Matches

- **A**: Escalate to the stakeholder with authority to decide, supplying options and impact.
- **B**: Log it as an issue, assign an owner, and drive it to closure now.
- **C**: Investigate the common root cause behind the repeated failures and fix it at source.
- **D**: Record it in the risk register and plan a response.

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

Each item turns on two hidden tests: has it already occurred, and who has the authority to resolve it. (1) is a present, materialised problem squarely within the team's power to fix — the missed submission blocks shipping and the team can file it — so it is logged and driven to closure as an issue (B). (2) is not three separate issues but one failure mode resurfacing across batches and now a third line, the hallmark of an unaddressed root cause to investigate and eliminate rather than re-run again (C). (3) is already an issue, but it has stalled on a decision only the marketing director can make, so it must be escalated to that authority with options and impact (A). (4) is an uncertain future event contingent on a guideline that may be adopted next year — the defining mark of a risk to record and plan for (D). The traps are mapping (1) and (3) both to plain issue-working without noticing (3)'s blocker sits above the PM, and reading (2)'s repeated symptom as a string of independent issues rather than one root cause.
