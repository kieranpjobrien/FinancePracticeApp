# Question

A project manager on a hybrid clinical-trial system is deciding which of several open issues to escalate to the project's governance board this week and which to keep managing at team level. Which factors should genuinely drive the decision to escalate an issue? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The resolution requires a decision or resources beyond the project manager's delegated authority.
- **B**: The issue has remained unresolved past its target date despite the team's reasonable efforts, and its impact is growing.
- **C**: The issue, if unresolved, will breach a regulatory obligation or cross-project dependency that governance must adjudicate.
- **D**: The issue was raised by a vocal stakeholder who has asked to be kept informed of progress.
- **E**: The issue is technically detailed and the project manager would prefer not to write up the explanation.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Escalation is warranted when a matter exceeds the PM's authority (A), when it has stalled beyond its target with rising impact despite genuine effort (B), or when its consequences reach regulatory or cross-project levels that only governance can resolve (C). (D) describes a communication need, not an escalation trigger; the stakeholder can be updated without involving the board. (E) escalates to dodge effort, which abuses the governance route and erodes the PM's credibility.
