# Question

On a hybrid telecommunications-rollout programme, a scrum master faces four blockers, each resolvable at a different level of the organisation. Match each blocker to the most appropriate party to resolve or escalate it to.

## Items

- **1**: Two developers on the same team disagree on a coding approach and have not yet talked it through.
- **2**: A shared staging environment owned by another delivery team is misconfigured and that team has not responded to repeated requests.
- **3**: A funding shortfall means a needed third-party licence cannot be purchased without additional budget approval.
- **4**: A dependency on another team's deliverable needs resequencing across several teams in the programme.

## Matches

- **A**: The two developers themselves, facilitated by the scrum master.
- **B**: The other delivery team, via their scrum master.
- **C**: The programme sponsor or steering body.
- **D**: The scrum-of-scrums / cross-team coordination forum.

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Each blocker should be resolved at the lowest level with the authority and reach to clear it. (1) is an internal team disagreement the developers can resolve themselves with the scrum master facilitating, so it needs no escalation (A). (2) sits with another single team that owns the environment, making that team — reached through their scrum master — the right party (B). (3) requires additional budget, a funding decision that only the sponsor or steering body can authorise (C). (4) spans several teams and is about resequencing dependencies, which is precisely the scrum-of-scrums' remit (D). The traps are over-escalating (1) to a forum or sponsor, or routing the budget decision in (3) to the cross-team forum, which has no authority to release funds.
