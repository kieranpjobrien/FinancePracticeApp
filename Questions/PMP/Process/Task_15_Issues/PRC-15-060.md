# Question

An issue on a predictive manufacturing project has been open for three weeks because resolution requires a decision outside the PM's authority. The owning functional manager keeps promising a response next week but has not delivered. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue waiting for the functional manager's decision since repeated escalation may damage the working relationship between the two functions.
- **B**: Take the decision yourself based on best available information so the project is not held hostage to another manager's schedule moving forward.
- **C**: Reclassify the issue as low priority so the overdue status no longer appears on the weekly status dashboard under aged items.
- **D**: Document the impact of continued delay, escalate to the sponsor with a requested date, and include the functional manager transparently.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI sanctions formal escalation with impact data when a decision stalls beyond reasonable time, and transparency to the stalled party is essential. (D) gets the decision made without bypassing relationships. (A) lets inaction damage the project indefinitely. (B) takes a decision the PM lacks authority for. (C) hides delay through cosmetic reclassification.
