# Question

A sponsor on a predictive government project instructs the PM to downgrade a Critical issue to Medium because it makes the monthly report look bad. The severity rating reflects genuine impact per the project's documented criteria. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Retain the Critical rating, explain the impact criteria supporting it, and offer to co-author clear mitigation narrative for the report.
- **B**: Downgrade the rating as the sponsor directed, since the sponsor holds ultimate authority over external programme reporting decisions.
- **C**: Compromise at a High rating so the sponsor sees movement and the report carries a slightly less alarming signal this month.
- **D**: Escalate the sponsor's request to the steering committee as a governance breach before taking any further action yourself.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI requires that severity reflect reality and that the PM push back respectfully when asked to misrepresent status. (A) keeps the record honest and helps the sponsor communicate well. (B) falsifies governance data to manage appearances. (C) splits the difference on an ethical matter. (D) escalates what the PM should first attempt to resolve directly with the sponsor.
