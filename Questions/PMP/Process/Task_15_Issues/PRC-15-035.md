# Question

An issue was closed two weeks ago after what looked like a successful fix. The same symptoms have now returned. What should the project manager ensure happens?

## Options

- **A**: Reapply the original fix immediately and re-close the issue once the symptoms disappear again.
- **B**: Open a new issue under a new ID so the resolution status of the original closed item is preserved.
- **C**: Reopen the original issue, investigate why the fix did not hold, and ensure the actual root cause is identified and addressed.
- **D**: Accept the recurrence as a known limitation of the system and add a permanent workaround to operational runbooks.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Returning symptoms after a fix mean the root cause was not addressed. (C) preserves history and forces a deeper investigation. (A) treats symptom suppression as resolution. (B) loses the link between the original investigation and the recurrence. (D) institutionalises the failure rather than fixing it.
