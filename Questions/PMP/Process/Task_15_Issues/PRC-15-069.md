# Question

A predictive construction PM faces a recurring site safety issue reported four times this month. Operations has been applying the documented corrective fix each time. The fix works briefly and the issue returns. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Request additional safety inspections each week so the next occurrence is detected earlier than previous instances of the issue.
- **B**: Rotate the corrective fix between documented options so different solutions are tested against the recurring pattern over a period.
- **C**: Document the recurrence, escalate to the health and safety committee, and defer to their recommended structural intervention plan.
- **D**: Lead a five whys analysis with frontline operators, identify the deeper cause, and redesign the corrective action durably.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI promotes root cause techniques like five whys when documented fixes fail repeatedly, and frontline input is essential. (D) targets the underlying cause with the right participants. (A) improves detection of a problem already well-detected. (B) experiments blindly among fixes already known not to last. (C) escalates before performing the analysis the PM should lead first.
