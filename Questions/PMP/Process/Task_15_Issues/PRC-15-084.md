# Question

A project manager on an agile health-records product is reviewing the issue log before a delivery review and finds several entries that read only as one-line problem statements with no further detail. To make each entry genuinely manageable and auditable, which attributes must the project manager ensure are present? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A single named owner who is accountable for driving the issue to resolution.
- **B**: A target resolution date against which progress and overdue status can be measured.
- **C**: A current status that shows whether the issue is open, in progress, or resolved.
- **D**: The name of the team member who originally raised the issue in stand-up.
- **E**: A monetary contingency reserve drawn down specifically to fund the issue's resolution.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A manageable issue log entry needs an accountable owner (A), a target date that lets you judge whether it is on track or overdue (B), and a current status that shows where it sits (C); without these three you cannot actually track or report the issue. (D) is sometimes recorded for context, but who first mentioned it does not drive resolution and is not what makes the entry manageable. (E) confuses issue management with contingency reserves; issues are resolved by assigned action and, where needed, escalation, not by an earmarked reserve attached to each log line.
