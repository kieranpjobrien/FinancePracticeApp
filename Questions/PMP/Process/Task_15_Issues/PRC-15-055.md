# Question

On a predictive construction programme, monthly issue counts have risen steadily from 12 to 34 over six months while the team and scope have stayed constant. The PM has been closing each issue as it appears. What should the project manager do now?

## Options

- **A**: Increase resourcing on issue triage to keep up with the higher monthly volume and restore prompt closure rates for items.
- **B**: Report the rising trend in the next status update and ask the sponsor whether additional triage funding should be approved now.
- **C**: Analyse the trend to identify systemic causes driving the growth, then address those causes rather than accelerating individual closures.
- **D**: Tighten the issue intake criteria so only higher-severity items enter the log and the reported monthly count stabilises downward.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A sustained upward trend with stable inputs signals a systemic cause that PMI expects the PM to investigate, not just a throughput problem. (C) is a root cause response to the trend itself. (A) scales the workaround while the underlying cause grows. (B) escalates without doing the analysis first. (D) manipulates the measurement rather than addressing reality.
