# Question

In a sprint retrospective, the team raises that handoffs to the operations group are consistently late, causing rework. One developer wants this captured as an issue and escalated today; another argues it belongs in the retrospective's improvement actions. As the team's servant leader, how should you handle it?

## Options

- **A**: Log the late handoffs as a current issue with an owner and next action, and also record the systemic fix as a retrospective improvement to be actioned.
- **B**: Record it only as a retrospective improvement action, since anything raised in a retrospective is by definition a process improvement, not an issue.
- **C**: Escalate it as an issue and drop it from the retrospective notes to avoid duplicating the same item in two places.
- **D**: Defer any action until the team can quantify exactly how much rework the late handoffs have caused.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The late handoffs are a present, recurring problem (an issue needing an owner now) and also a systemic process weakness (a retrospective improvement); the two are complementary, not mutually exclusive. (B) collapses an active problem into a process tweak and loses the near-term ownership. (C) forces a false choice and throws away the systemic improvement the retrospective is meant to capture. (D) delays both responses behind data gathering when the existence and impact of the problem are already agreed.
