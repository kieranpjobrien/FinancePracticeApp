# Question

On a predictive commercial-construction project, the issue log holds eleven open items. Two threaten the critical path and a regulatory inspection next week; the rest are minor snags with no schedule impact. At the weekly issue review, the team tends to work down the list in the order issues were logged. What should the project manager do to manage the log most effectively?

## Options

- **A**: Work strictly in logged order so that the oldest items, which have waited longest, are cleared before newer ones.
- **B**: Prioritise the issues by impact and urgency so attention and effort go to the two that threaten the critical path and the inspection first.
- **C**: Close the nine minor snags immediately to shrink the log to the two items that genuinely matter.
- **D**: Split the minor snags off into the risk register so the issue log only ever shows critical-path items.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An issue log is worked by impact and urgency, so the two items threatening the critical path and a regulatory deadline must be addressed ahead of cosmetic snags. (A) lets logging order trump consequence, which can leave a high-impact issue waiting behind trivial ones. (C) closes issues that are still open and unresolved purely to tidy the list, which loses tracking of real, if minor, problems. (D) misuses the risk register, which is for future uncertainties; the snags are present problems and belong in the issue log, just at lower priority.
