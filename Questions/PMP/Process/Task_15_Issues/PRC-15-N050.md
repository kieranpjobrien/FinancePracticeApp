# Question

A newly formed agile team has no consistent way of surfacing problems; impediments are raised verbally and forgotten, then resurface weeks later. The scrum master wants to improve how the team handles emerging problems. Which action best addresses this?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a visible, shared impediment or issue board that the team updates and reviews regularly, with owners and status.
- **B**: Ask each team member to keep a personal log of any problems they encounter during the sprint.
- **C**: Add a standing agenda item to the monthly steering meeting where the team reports problems to management.
- **D**: Require that all impediments be emailed to the scrum master, who will remember and follow up on each one.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A visible, shared board with owners and status makes problems transparent to the whole team and is reviewed routinely, which directly fixes the "raised and forgotten" failure. (B) fragments visibility into private logs no one else sees. (C) sends near-term operational problems to a monthly management forum, far too slow for impediments. (D) creates a single human bottleneck and relies on memory, the very weakness being addressed.
