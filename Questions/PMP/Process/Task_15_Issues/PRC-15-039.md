# Question

A current issue traces back to a design decision made early in the project. Reversing that decision would require significant rework. Some stakeholders are reluctant to acknowledge the original call may have been wrong. How should the PM approach the conversation?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid linking the current issue to the original decision so stakeholders are not put on the defensive during discussion.
- **B**: Name the people responsible for the original decision so accountability is clear before any discussion of options proceeds.
- **C**: Insist that the original decision be reversed regardless of rework cost since it is the root source of the current problem.
- **D**: Present the issue, its linkage to the original decision, and realistic options with costs, then facilitate a stakeholder decision.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM's job is to make the trade-off decidable, not to score points or avoid hard truths. (D) does that. (A) hides information stakeholders need. (B) replaces analysis with blame. (C) ignores cost and removes the trade-off entirely.
