# Question

On a hybrid hospital information-system rollout, an issue has been open for three weeks: the clinical and finance directorates disagree on which patient-identifier standard the new module must use, and each insists the other must change. The team cannot build the module until the question is settled, both directors outrank the project manager, and previous attempts by the project manager to mediate a compromise have not moved either party. The project manager has documented options and the impact of further delay. What should the project manager do now?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the issue to the steering committee with the options and impact, requesting a binding decision.
- **B**: Choose the identifier standard the project manager judges most defensible and instruct the team to proceed.
- **C**: Continue facilitating between the two directors until one of them concedes.
- **D**: Record the disagreement as an assumption and build the module to be reconfigurable later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The issue has stalled on a decision that two senior stakeholders refuse to make and that exceeds the project manager's authority, which is precisely the condition for escalation to a higher governance level with options and impact already prepared (A). Unilaterally selecting a standard oversteps the project manager's authority on a clinical and financial matter and risks a worse dispute and rework if overturned (B). Further facilitation has already failed repeatedly, so persisting simply extends the delay the project manager has documented (C). Reframing a live, build-blocking disagreement as an assumption and building for later reconfiguration defers rather than resolves the issue, adds cost, and leaves the core decision outstanding (D).
