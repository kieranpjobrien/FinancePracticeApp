# Question

In a scaled-agile programme, one feature team for a retail-analytics platform is blocked because an API it needs will not be delivered until another team finishes work two sprints away. The blocked team's scrum master has confirmed the dependency is real and that neither team can compress the other's plan on its own. What is the most appropriate next step for the scrum master?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the cross-team dependency at the scrum-of-scrums so the teams and programme can coordinate a resolution.
- **B**: Instruct the blocked team to build a throwaway stub and proceed as if the API existed.
- **C**: Ask the blocked team to wait the two sprints and pick up other backlog items in the meantime.
- **D**: Escalate immediately to the programme sponsor to demand the other team be reprioritised.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The blocker is a genuine cross-team dependency that neither team can resolve alone, which is exactly what the scrum-of-scrums exists to coordinate, so raising it there lets the teams and programme agree sequencing or reprioritisation together (A). Building a throwaway stub may sometimes help, but adopting it unilaterally as the answer ignores the coordination needed and risks integration rework if assumptions about the API are wrong (B). Simply waiting two sprints and pulling other work accepts the delay without attempting to coordinate a faster path and may stall dependent value unnecessarily (C). Escalating straight to the sponsor to demand reprioritisation skips the established cross-team coordination forum and is premature before the teams have tried to resolve it together (D).
