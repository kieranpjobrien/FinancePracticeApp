# Question

For three consecutive sprints, a mobile-banking team has logged the same impediment: the QA environment is refreshed with stale test data overnight, forcing testers to recreate accounts each morning and losing roughly half a day per sprint. Each sprint the team works around it manually. At the upcoming retrospective, what is the most valuable action the scrum master can drive?

## Options

- **A**: Reassure the team that the manual workaround is acceptable since they have absorbed the lost time three sprints running.
- **B**: Increase the sprint's testing capacity estimate to formally account for the half-day loss going forward.
- **C**: Raise the data-refresh problem to the steering committee as a programme-level risk.
- **D**: Treat the recurring impediment as an improvement item, agree a concrete action with an owner to fix the overnight refresh, and track it like any commitment.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A retrospective exists to convert recurring pain into concrete improvement, so the team should commit a specific action with an owner to fix the refresh job rather than keep paying the cost. (A) normalises a fixable waste and abandons the team's improvement mandate. (B) bakes the inefficiency permanently into estimates instead of removing it. (C) escalates prematurely to governance for something the team and its support functions can almost certainly resolve themselves first.
