# Question

A Scrum team has stopped raising issues in retrospectives after a senior manager publicly criticised someone for a defect last month. The PM has noticed the chilling effect but the manager is not directly in the chain. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the behaviour since the senior manager sits outside the project team's direct reporting and organisational control structure currently.
- **B**: Privately counsel individual team members to keep raising issues and assure them management criticism will not recur in future retrospectives.
- **C**: Engage the manager about the impact on team openness, coach both sides on safe reporting, and reset psychological safety.
- **D**: Switch retrospectives to anonymous written submissions so issues surface without requiring anyone to speak up during the meeting openly.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Servant leadership requires addressing psychological safety directly, including influencing those outside the direct reporting line. (C) treats the root cause and rebuilds team openness. (A) abandons the team to a deteriorating environment. (B) offers reassurance the PM cannot guarantee. (D) works around the culture problem rather than fixing it.
