# Question

Midway through a sprint, a developer on a payments-platform team finds that a shared test environment has been reconfigured by another department and now fails the team's integration tests. Two committed stories depend on that environment. The developer has exhausted the usual fixes, the cause sits in the other department's configuration, and that department has not answered the team's chat message. The other department's manager is reachable, and the team also has a partly-built local mock it could finish to test against in the meantime. The scrum master hears of this at the daily scrum. What should the scrum master do next?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the other department's manager directly to get the shared environment restored, since the root cause and the fix both sit with that department.
- **B**: Have the team finish the local mock and test against that, so work continues without depending on the other department.
- **C**: Take the two affected stories to the product owner now to renegotiate the sprint scope around the blockage.
- **D**: Add the recurring environment-ownership problem to the retrospective agenda and ask the developer to keep probing the configuration meanwhile.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The blocker's cause and its only durable fix both sit with the other department, which owns the environment, so the scrum master clears it where it actually lives — by engaging that department's manager — which is the action that restores the real environment the committed stories depend on (A). Finishing the local mock is a reasonable parallel mitigation, but adopting it as the primary response lets the team test against a substitute rather than the environment it must ultimately integrate with, risking false confidence and rework (B). Renegotiating scope with the product owner concedes sprint value before the impediment has even been pushed at the level that can remove it, making it a fallback rather than the next step (C). The retrospective addresses prevention later and keeping the developer probing a cause that is external repeats what has already failed, so neither unblocks the team now (D).
