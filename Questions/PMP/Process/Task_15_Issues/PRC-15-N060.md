# Question

A project manager is handling four situations on a hybrid project. Each needs a different response. Match each situation to the most appropriate next action.

## Items

- **1**: A vendor delivered a build last night that corrupts data on import; the team can roll back and patch it themselves but delivery is blocked until they do.
- **2**: A defect the team has "closed" three times in three sprints has reappeared again, each time in a different module but with the same underlying symptom.
- **3**: The team logged a contract ambiguity weeks ago; it cannot proceed without a legal interpretation that only the sponsor's office can authorise, and it has stalled there.
- **4**: A competitor is rumoured to be lobbying for a standards change that, if it happened next year, could affect the product's compliance.

## Matches

- **A**: Escalate to the appropriate governance level with options and impact.
- **B**: Log and work it as an issue, assign an owner, and drive it to closure.
- **C**: Investigate and address the common root cause behind the recurrences.
- **D**: Record it in the risk register and plan a response.

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A, 4-D

## Explanation

The give-away each item hides is the combination of *has it occurred* and *who can resolve it*. (1) is a present, materialised problem the team can fix within its own authority — log it as an issue and drive it to closure (B). (2) is not a fresh issue each time but the same fault resurfacing under different guises, which signals an unaddressed root cause to investigate and eliminate at source rather than patch again (C). (3) is already an issue, but it has stalled on a decision that only the sponsor's office can make, so it must be escalated to that authority with options and impact (A). (4) is an uncertain future event that has not occurred, the defining mark of a risk to record and plan for (D). The trap is mapping (1) or (3) both to plain issue-working without noticing that (3)'s blocker sits above the PM, and mistaking (2)'s repeated symptom for a series of separate issues.
