# Question

A Scrum Master on a fintech product reports that the impediment list has grown to 23 open items across three sprints, and older entries are going stale. The team feels overwhelmed and unsure where to start. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add impediment resolution as a fixed reserved capacity slot on every sprint board going forward across the delivery team.
- **B**: Facilitate a session to classify impediments by blocking impact, close stale entries, and focus on items constraining flow.
- **C**: Delegate the full impediment list to a designated team member and have them resolve items in the order originally logged.
- **D**: Escalate all 23 impediments to the sponsor so organisational resources can be mobilised against the complete backlog at once.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Empirical process control requires regular inspection and adaptation of the impediment list, concentrating effort where flow is most blocked. (B) applies Pareto thinking and recovers focus. (A) reserves capacity without fixing the prioritisation problem. (C) treats impediments as FIFO queue ignoring impact variance. (D) over-escalates items the PM and team should triage first.
