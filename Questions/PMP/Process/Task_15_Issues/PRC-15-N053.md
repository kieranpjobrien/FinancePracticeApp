# Question

On a hybrid programme, your team's delivery depends on a component owned by another project under a different project manager. The component is two weeks late and your team is now idle. You have logged the dependency on your issue log and spoken to the other PM, who is apologetic and again forecasts delivery "next week". You are not certain whether that PM is under-resourcing the work or genuinely blocked upstream. The programme manager runs a fortnightly dependency forum, with the next session in four days. What is the most effective next action?

## Options

- **A**: Agree a firm date and interim checkpoint directly with the other PM in writing, with the understanding that you will take it to the programme forum if that date slips.
- **B**: Raise the dependency now at the programme dependency forum so the programme manager can weigh both projects' priorities and reallocate where needed.
- **C**: Reforecast your own schedule around the latest "next week" estimate and redeploy your idle team to other ready work in the meantime.
- **D**: Ask the other PM to walk you through their blockers so you can jointly diagnose whether the slip is resourcing or an upstream dependency.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The give-away here is that informal, bilateral handling has already cycled once without a reliable commitment, the cause is unclear, and your team is now bearing idle cost — the dependency needs an authority that sees both projects' priorities, which is exactly what the programme forum provides, and it sits only four days away. (A) keeps resolution at the same peer level that has already failed to produce a kept commitment and defers any cross-project re-prioritisation. (D) is a sound diagnostic step but, on its own, still leaves two stalled-and-idle weeks unaddressed and assumes the fix lies within the other PM's gift. (C) manages your own symptom and absorbs the slip rather than getting the dependency itself moved. Taking it to the forum does not preclude continuing to work with the other PM, but it puts the decision where the trade-off between the two projects can actually be made.
