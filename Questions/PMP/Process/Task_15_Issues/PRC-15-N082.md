# Question

During a Scrum team's daily standup for a mobile-banking product, a developer reports she has been waiting two days on a test-environment credential that only a separate platform team can issue, and the iteration goal is now at risk. The developer has already raised one ticket with the platform team; the Scrum Master has a standing working relationship with that team's lead. Two other backlog items are ready and unblocked. What should the Scrum Master do first?

## Options

- **A**: Personally contact the platform team's lead now to get the specific credential expedited, then track it to issue.
- **B**: Reorder the iteration so the developer starts a ready, unblocked item while the existing ticket runs its course.
- **C**: Help the developer escalate her own ticket through the platform team's intake so she carries the request to closure.
- **D**: Raise the recurring cross-team dependency on environment access with management as a systemic impediment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four are legitimate servant-leadership responses; the question is which one serves *this* goal *first*. The iteration goal is at risk now and the constraint is a single credential the team cannot self-issue, so the Scrum Master uses the asset that resolves it fastest — the existing relationship with the platform lead — to expedite that specific credential and track it to issue (A). Reordering to an unblocked item (B) protects the developer's flow but works *around* the impediment rather than removing it; the credential is still needed and the original goal still slips. Routing the developer through standard intake (C) builds her self-sufficiency but repeats the channel that has already stalled for two days, trading the live goal for a future capability. Raising the systemic dependency with management (D) is the right move for the *pattern* but is too slow for today's goal and addresses recurrence, not this instance. The margin is removing the specific blocker now versus accommodating it, building capability, or fixing the system — only (A) clears what is at risk in the time available.
