# Question

A change request to add a second language to a predictive e-learning platform has been logged and recorded. The sponsor is keen and asks, "What happens next?" The project manager wants to follow integrated change control correctly. What should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Analyse the change's impact across scope, schedule, cost, quality and risk so the decision-makers have what they need to approve or reject it.
- **B**: Estimate the translation effort with the development team and add that estimate to the change request before circulating it for a decision.
- **C**: Convene the change control board so members can discuss the request and reach a decision in the meeting.
- **D**: Confirm to the sponsor that the request is now in the queue and will be evaluated at the next scheduled change review.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Once a change is logged, the next step in integrated change control is a full impact analysis across the relevant constraints, because no decision-maker can responsibly approve or reject without understanding the consequences. (A) is that step. (B) gathers one input, a development effort estimate, but stops short of assessing the full picture of schedule, cost, quality and risk; a partial estimate is not the impact analysis the decision requires. (C) moves to a board decision before the analysis that should inform it, so the board would be deciding without consequences in front of it. (D) is a reasonable holding reply to the sponsor but defers the actual work rather than advancing the change; the impact still has to be assessed. Assessment precedes the decision.
