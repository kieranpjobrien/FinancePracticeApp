# Question

Midway through a sprint, an influential stakeholder goes to the development team and asks them to add a high-value feature before the sprint ends, calling it urgent. One developer is keen and starts looking into it. The product owner is at their desk nearby and has not been part of the conversation. The scrum master notices and intervenes. What is the most appropriate thing for the scrum master to do first?

## Options

- **A**: Have the developer write the request up so the team can present it to the product owner at the next daily scrum to be sized and ranked.
- **B**: Ask the product owner to step into the conversation now so the stakeholder's request is taken up directly by the person who owns what the team builds.
- **C**: Tell the stakeholder the team cannot take the feature mid-sprint and ask them to raise it at the next sprint planning.
- **D**: Suggest the team and product owner look at swapping the new feature in for lower-priority work still left in the sprint.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stakeholder is steering work straight to the team, and the immediate need is to put the request in front of the product owner while it is live, since the product owner owns priorities and can then choose whatever response fits. (B) does that without delay and keeps the decision where it belongs. (A) is reasonable but defers the handover to the next daily scrum and quietly lets the stakeholder's direct-to-team routing stand in the meantime, with a keen developer already drifting toward the work. (C) has the scrum master refuse and reschedule on the team's behalf, pre-empting a call the product owner might make differently, perhaps to renegotiate the sprint for genuinely urgent value. (D) jumps to one specific accommodation, reordering the sprint, before the product owner has even judged whether the request warrants disturbing the sprint at all. The margin is that (A), (B) and (D) all eventually involve the product owner, but only (B) brings the request to them now and unconditionally, while (A) delays and (D) presupposes the outcome.
