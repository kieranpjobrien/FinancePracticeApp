# Question

The PM notices that five of the last eight approved change requests on a predictive telco programme originated from a single business unit rediscovering requirements mid-execution. The CCB keeps approving them. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the business unit to understand the root cause of late requirements and adjust elicitation going forward.
- **B**: Continue approving the changes because each has been individually justified to the CCB on merits.
- **C**: Reject future change requests from that business unit until they submit a formal apology and plan.
- **D**: Raise the pattern in lessons learned at project closure so future programmes can avoid the same issue.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Change control works best when patterns are detected and the process that feeds changes is improved; root cause analysis reduces future churn. (B) approves symptoms without addressing the cause. (C) punishes rather than coaches and damages the stakeholder relationship. (D) defers learning until after the cost has been paid, missing the chance to stop the flow.
