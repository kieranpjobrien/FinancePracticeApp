# Question

A vendor delivers a hardware module that the team's engineers say does not meet the contract specifications. The vendor responds that the specs are ambiguous and that their interpretation is valid. Rework would delay the project four weeks. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Notify procurement and legal so formal contract enforcement action can begin against the vendor quickly.
- **B**: Direct the team to integrate the delivered module to protect the schedule and log the deviation formally.
- **C**: Initiate termination of the vendor and brief procurement on alternate suppliers identified during the selection process.
- **D**: Convene a joint technical review to walk through the specs and identify whether it is ambiguity or non-conformance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to establish facts before invoking contractual or commercial remedies. A joint technical review surfaces whether the language is genuinely ambiguous (negotiation territory) or clearly violated (enforcement territory) and informs every option that follows. (A) escalates to legal before establishing facts, which is adversarial and expensive. (B) accepts non-conforming work without understanding the implications, creating downstream quality risk. (C) jumps to termination, the most expensive remedy, without attempting resolution.
