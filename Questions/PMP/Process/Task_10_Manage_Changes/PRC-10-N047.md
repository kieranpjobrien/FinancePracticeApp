# Question

On a predictive satellite ground-station project, an approved change to upgrade the antenna controller was implemented two weeks ago. The integration team now reports that the new firmware broke compatibility with the tracking software, a dependency that was not flagged in the original change's impact assessment. A forward fix and a rollback both appear feasible. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Reassess the change's full impact across the affected components to scope both the forward fix and the rollback, then bring the options and their cost and schedule impacts back through change control for a disposition.
- **B**: Raise a new change request for the forward fix to restore tracking-software compatibility, since the upgrade itself was already approved and is worth keeping.
- **C**: Raise a change request to roll the antenna controller back to its previous firmware, restoring the last known good integration before any further work proceeds.
- **D**: Treat the break as the realisation of an integration risk, invoke the risk response, and have the technical team restore compatibility under the existing approved plan.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

An approved change that reveals an unassessed cross-component impact sends the work back into integrated change control, but the first move is to re-scope the actual footprint so the board can choose between remedies on assessed impact, not to pre-select one remedy. (A) does that and keeps both viable options open for a proper disposition. (B) commits to the forward fix before the full impact is understood and before anyone has weighed it against rollback, pre-judging the cheaper-looking path. (C) commits to rollback just as prematurely, discarding an upgrade that may still be wanted, again before the comparative assessment exists. (D) mislabels remediation of an already-realised break as routine risk-response work under the existing plan, side-stepping the change control that a baseline-affecting fix now requires; calling it "risk" does not exempt it from re-assessment and board disposition. The margin is that (A), (B) and (C) all correctly recognise this needs change control, but only (A) assesses before choosing, while (B) and (C) each lock in a direction first.
