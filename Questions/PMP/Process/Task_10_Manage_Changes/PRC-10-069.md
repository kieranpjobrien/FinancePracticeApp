# Question

An approved change was implemented three weeks ago on a predictive pharma project. The change log shows implementation complete, but no one has verified that the change actually delivered the intended outcome. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Mark the change as closed in the log since implementation has been completed and reported by the team.
- **B**: Ask the requester to self-certify effectiveness, which is simpler than independent verification at this stage.
- **C**: Wait until the next audit cycle to verify the change, since audit will test effectiveness as part of its review.
- **D**: Verify the change achieved its intended outcome before closing it in the change log and updating stakeholders.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Change closure requires verification that the intended outcome was achieved, not just that implementation occurred; effectiveness verification is part of integrated change control. (A) conflates completion with effectiveness. (B) outsources verification to the requester, removing independence. (C) defers verification and leaves ambiguity about whether the change actually worked.
