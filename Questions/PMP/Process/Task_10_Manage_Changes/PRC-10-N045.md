# Question

A hybrid programme runs an agile build team inside a predictive governance wrapper with a quarterly funded scope baseline. Mid-quarter, the product owner reprioritises the backlog and drops two committed features in favour of two newly urgent ones of similar size and cost. The agile team is comfortable; the steering committee approved the quarter's scope at a feature level. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm whether the swap stays within the approved quarterly scope envelope; if it changes the committed feature set, raise a change request to the governance board while letting the team continue ordering within approved scope.
- **B**: Let the product owner make the swap freely, since prioritising the backlog is the product owner's role in any agile context.
- **C**: Freeze the backlog at the features the steering committee approved and require a change request for any reordering the team does.
- **D**: Make the swap and inform the steering committee retrospectively in the next quarterly review.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a hybrid setup the product owner orders work freely within the approved envelope, but a change to the committed feature set that governance funded at feature level crosses the line into integrated change control. (A) distinguishes ordering inside scope (team's right) from changing what scope was committed (governance's decision). (B) ignores that governance approved a specific feature set, not a blank cheque. (C) over-controls by requiring change requests for ordinary backlog ordering the team should own. (D) makes a governance-level scope change first and tells the funders afterwards, undermining the control the wrapper exists to provide.
