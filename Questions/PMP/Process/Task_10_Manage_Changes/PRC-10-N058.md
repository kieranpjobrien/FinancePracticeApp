# Question

On a hybrid programme, the agile team works to a quarterly scope envelope that the steering committee funded as a set of outcomes, not detailed features. The product owner now wants to replace a planned "export to PDF" capability with "export to spreadsheet", judging it more valuable. The two are similar in size and cost and both serve the same funded outcome of "let users take their data offline." The team is ready to proceed. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Support the product owner making the swap as backlog prioritisation, since both options serve the same funded outcome and the scope envelope is defined at the outcome level.
- **B**: Raise a change request to the steering committee, because swapping one named capability for another always alters committed scope.
- **C**: Let the swap proceed but log it as a change in the change log for the committee to ratify retrospectively at quarter end.
- **D**: Hold the swap until the next quarterly funding cycle, since the current quarter's plan named the PDF capability.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The decisive fact is the level at which scope was committed: the steering committee funded an outcome ("take data offline"), not a specific feature, so choosing the export format that best delivers that outcome is the product owner's prioritisation, not a scope change. (A) reads the envelope correctly and keeps the team agile. (B) applies feature-level change control to a programme that deliberately committed at outcome level, over-controlling and contradicting the funding basis. (C) treats a within-scope decision as a change needing ratification, creating governance theatre and implying the PO acted outside authority. (D) defers a legitimate within-scope improvement to a future cycle for no reason other than the old plan happened to name PDF. Had the committee funded "PDF export" specifically, the answer would flip to a change request, which is the fine margin the question turns on.
