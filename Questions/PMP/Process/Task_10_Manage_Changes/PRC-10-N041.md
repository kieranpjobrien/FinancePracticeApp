# Question

On a predictive hospital fit-out, the clinical sponsor emails the project manager directly asking to relocate two operating theatres. The change is large, would shift the cost and schedule baselines, and the sponsor says "treat this as approved, I've signed off." What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Log the request and put it through the integrated change control process so the impacts are assessed before any baseline moves, even though the sponsor sits on the change control board.
- **B**: Treat the sponsor's email as the approval and have the team begin design for the relocation, since the sponsor holds change authority and has explicitly signed off.
- **C**: Acknowledge the sponsor's authority but tell them no baseline change can be actioned until the full board convenes, and place the request on hold until the next scheduled meeting.
- **D**: Commission the impact assessment straight away and update the cost and schedule baselines with the results, then present the revised plan to the board for endorsement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A change that crosses the cost and schedule baselines must go through integrated change control, where the impacts are assessed and the board formally disposes of the request, regardless of who raised it. (A) starts that process now without pre-judging the outcome, and the sponsor being a board member does not let one person stand in for the board's collective assessment. (B) converts a single member's email into board approval and commits effort before any analysis. (C) is half-right that the sponsor cannot act alone, but needlessly idles the request rather than progressing the assessment that the board will need, and most plans allow disposition without waiting for a full quarterly meeting. (D) does the analysis but then moves the baselines on the strength of the assessment alone, ahead of the board's decision, reducing the board to rubber-stamping an already-changed plan.
