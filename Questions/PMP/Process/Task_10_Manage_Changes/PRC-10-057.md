# Question

A sponsor overrules a CCB rejection and directs the PM to implement the change anyway. The PM has concerns about ethics and baseline integrity. The sponsor cites their authority as the project's sponsor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change as directed, since sponsor authority overrides change control board decisions formally.
- **B**: Refuse the direction entirely and escalate directly to the sponsor's line manager without further conversation.
- **C**: Resign from the PM role on ethical grounds before the sponsor's direction affects the project's integrity.
- **D**: Document the direction, engage the sponsor on governance implications, and escalate if not resolved.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Sponsors have authority but the PM's duty is to surface governance risk, document transparently, and escalate through the proper channel if unresolved. (A) treats authority as a licence to bypass documented governance entirely. (B) skips the dialogue step and goes straight to escalation. (C) abandons the project without exhausting proper escalation paths first.
