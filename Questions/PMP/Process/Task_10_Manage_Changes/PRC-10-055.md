# Question

On a predictive infrastructure project, a vendor proposes a design change that would improve performance but requires a contract change order. The vendor has offered to proceed at risk and invoice once the change is formally approved later. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the vendor's offer because the vendor carries the risk if the change is ultimately not approved.
- **B**: Refuse and halt the change process entirely, as any vendor proposing at-risk work demonstrates weak governance.
- **C**: Decline at-risk work and run the change through formal contract change control before work begins.
- **D**: Allow preparatory work at risk but stop short of anything that would affect the actual delivered product.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Contract changes are authorised through formal change control; starting work before approval undermines governance and sets a precedent for future at-risk commitments. (A) accepts scope commitment outside the contract regardless of who carries the risk. (B) overreacts to a reasonable vendor offer by terminating rather than governing. (D) opens a partial bypass that is still a bypass.
