# Question

The CCB rejects a change request from a business stakeholder. The stakeholder is frustrated and tells the PM they will go directly to the CEO. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Reverse the CCB rejection so the stakeholder is satisfied and avoids the executive escalation entirely.
- **B**: Meet with the stakeholder to walk through the CCB's reasoning and offer a path to resubmit with new data.
- **C**: Inform the sponsor that an executive escalation to the CEO is imminent and request appropriate air cover.
- **D**: Update the change log with the formal rejection and consider the matter fully closed once properly recorded.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI emphasises engaging stakeholders directly when concerns arise. The PM should ensure the requester understands the rationale and offer a constructive path (resubmission with additional information) before escalation paths are triggered. (A) undermines the CCB and rewards pressure. (C) escalates before attempting direct resolution at the PM's level. (D) is administratively correct but ignores the stakeholder relationship the PM owns.
