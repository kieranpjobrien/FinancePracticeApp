# Question

The PM reviewing monthly trends notices that each individual approved change is small, but cumulatively scope has grown by eight percent in a quarter. The CCB is unaware of the trend. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue processing changes individually since each is already approved through proper CCB channels.
- **B**: Suspend new change approvals for a quarter to give the cumulative impact time to settle before further work.
- **C**: Report the cumulative trend to the CCB and sponsor for strategic review of cumulative project impact.
- **D**: Convert the cumulative growth to a new baseline silently so future trend analysis starts from the new level.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated change control requires visibility into cumulative impact, not just individual change decisions; the CCB needs the aggregated trend to govern strategic scope health. (A) treats each change as if it stands alone, missing accumulation. (B) imposes a blanket halt instead of informing the decision-makers. (D) hides the trend by re-baselining quietly and removes the history governance needs.
