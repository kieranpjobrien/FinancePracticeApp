# Question

Midway through a sprint, the product owner of a health-records team asks that the team's definition of done be tightened to require accessibility testing on every story, effective immediately for the work already underway. The change is sensible but would mean reworking stories the team considered nearly complete. What should the Scrum Master facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the request to a change control board for formal approval before the definition of done is altered.
- **B**: Apply the strengthened definition of done to all in-progress stories now, so no story is accepted below the new accessibility bar.
- **C**: Convene the team to adopt the stronger definition of done for new work and to decide together how to treat the stories already in progress.
- **D**: Adopt the stronger definition of done immediately for new work, and leave the in-progress stories under the bar that applied when they were started.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The definition of done is a team working agreement; strengthening it is reasonable, but how it applies to work already in flight should be settled by the team, not imposed mid-sprint or decided by the project manager alone. (C) adopts the improvement and lets the team choose how to handle in-progress stories together. (B) unilaterally rebases the acceptance bar on committed work mid-sprint, disrupting the sprint without team agreement and potentially breaking the commitment. (A) routes a team-owned agreement through predictive governance that does not own it. (D) makes a unilateral cut-off decision for the in-progress stories rather than letting the team weigh whether to retrofit them, which is a judgement the team should make given the accessibility concern; it pre-decides the very question that should be discussed. Letting the team set the boundary respects both the quality goal and team ownership.
