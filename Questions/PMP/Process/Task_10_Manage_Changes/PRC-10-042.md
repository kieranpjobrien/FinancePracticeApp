# Question

On a programme with four parallel work streams, an approved change in stream 1 forces dependent changes in streams 2, 3, and 4. The PM of stream 3 refuses to accept the cascading change. What should the stream 1 PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change only in streams 1, 2, and 4 and accept the inconsistency in stream 3.
- **B**: Withdraw the original change because it cannot be implemented end-to-end.
- **C**: Override the stream 3 PM by directing the stream 3 team to make the dependent change.
- **D**: Escalate to programme management with the cross-stream impact analysis so coordination and decision-making happen at the right level.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Cross-stream changes require programme-level governance; no individual stream PM has authority over the others. The stream 1 PM brings the analysis to the programme level for coordinated decision and arbitration. (A) creates an inconsistent product. (B) abandons a potentially valuable change without escalation. (C) assumes authority that does not exist and damages programme relationships.
