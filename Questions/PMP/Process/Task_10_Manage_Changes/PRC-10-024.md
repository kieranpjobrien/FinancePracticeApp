# Question

A tester finds a defect in a deliverable that has already passed quality inspection. The fix will require modifying the approved design as well as the affected component. What change request type is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: A defect repair only, since fixing the failing deliverable is the immediate operational need.
- **B**: A preventive action to keep similar defects from occurring in future deliverables across the project.
- **C**: A combined request covering defect repair for the component and a scope change for the design update.
- **D**: No change request at all, because defect remediation is considered part of normal team delivery work.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The defect needs repair, and altering the approved design is a scope change that must go through integrated change control with its own impact assessment. (A) would log the repair but skip the design change governance. (B) addresses future defects but not the current situation. (D) treats a baseline change as routine work and bypasses the process.
