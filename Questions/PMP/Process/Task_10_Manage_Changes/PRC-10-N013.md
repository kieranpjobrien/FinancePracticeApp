# Question

During execution of a predictive medical-device project, a test engineer discovers that a safety interlock required by the certification standard is missing from the approved design. She is confident the device will fail certification without it. The next change control board is six days away, the build of the affected subassembly is scheduled to start tomorrow, and the change procedure has no emergency provision. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the design correction immediately so it is in place before the subassembly build starts, and submit the change request to the board retrospectively.
- **B**: Have the engineer raise a change request now, and ask the change control board chair whether the board can convene early or assess it out of cycle given the certification and build timing.
- **C**: Hold the affected subassembly build and raise the change request for the board's next scheduled meeting, accepting the short delay so the change follows the standard route.
- **D**: Ask the engineer to confirm the interlock is genuinely mandatory with the certification body first, then raise a change request once that is established.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The defect is real and the design is baselined, so the correction must go through change control; the live questions are sequencing and speed, and the right move is to log the change now and use the board's own discretion to expedite its assessment without bypassing it. (B) does both. (A) corrects an approved design with no authorisation at all and only documents it afterwards, which is ungoverned even when the engineer is right and is especially unacceptable absent an emergency provision. (C) follows the standard route but lets the subassembly either proceed to a known-defective design or stall for the full cycle when expediting the board is available; it treats the schedule as fixed when the board's cadence is the flexible part. (D) inserts an external confirmation step that delays raising the change while the build looms, when the proper response to a credible certification gap is to enter it into change control immediately and let assessment confirm the detail. Raising it now and seeking an expedited board decision protects both governance and the schedule.
