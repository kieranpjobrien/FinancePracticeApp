# Question

On a Scrum team rebuilding a logistics portal, the team has been expanding stories mid-sprint based on emerging technical discoveries, not through the product owner. Velocity looks inflated and stakeholder trust is eroding. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Coach the team to surface expansions to the product owner for backlog decisions rather than absorbing them.
- **B**: Ask the Scrum Master to block technical discoveries from reaching the product owner during the sprint timebox.
- **C**: Let the team continue expanding stories, as emergent design is a legitimate part of agile delivery anyway.
- **D**: Transfer all expansions into the risk register so the trend is visible to sponsors for oversight purposes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Scope decisions belong to the product owner even for technical discoveries; coaching the team to surface rather than absorb preserves transparency and trust. (B) blocks information the product owner needs and worsens transparency. (C) misapplies "emergent design" to excuse unmanaged scope expansion. (D) captures the trend without correcting the root behaviour.
