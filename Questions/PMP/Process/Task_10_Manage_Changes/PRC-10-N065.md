# Question

On a hybrid satellite-ground-station project, the change control board has just approved a significant change to the antenna control software. The project manager now moves into putting the approved change into effect. Which THREE actions belong to carrying an approved change through to completion? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Update the affected baselines and project documents to reflect the approved change.
- **B**: Communicate the approved change and its effects to the stakeholders and team members it touches.
- **C**: Re-run the impact analysis on scope, cost, and schedule to confirm the board's figures before committing the work.
- **D**: Ensure the change is incorporated into the work, for example by adding the resulting items to the team's backlog or work plan.
- **E**: Hold the work until the customer countersigns the board's decision, since the change is significant.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Implementing an approved change means updating the baselines and documents it affects (A), communicating it to the people it impacts (B), and getting the work executed, such as by feeding the resulting items into the backlog or plan (D). Re-running the impact analysis (C) repeats an input the board already used to decide; the assessment precedes the decision, and redoing it after approval second-guesses the board and stalls implementation rather than enacting it. Holding for a customer countersignature (E) inserts an approval step the board's authority has already satisfied; unless the change control procedure names the customer as a required approver, the decision is made and the work should proceed. The discriminator is that the three correct actions move an approved change into effect, whereas C reopens the assessment phase and E reopens the approval phase.
