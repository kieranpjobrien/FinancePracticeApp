# Question

A PM running a predictive logistics project is unsure whether a proposed change falls within her own delegated authority or requires CCB escalation. The change management plan is silent on the specific threshold involved. The sponsor is travelling. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change using PM authority, since the change management plan does not explicitly forbid it.
- **B**: Reject the change on the grounds that silence in the plan means the change is not allowed at all.
- **C**: Confirm authority thresholds with the sponsor or governance lead before acting, and update the plan.
- **D**: Route the change to the CCB to be safe, regardless of what thresholds will ultimately be defined later.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Governance ambiguity is resolved by confirming authority with the accountable role and updating the change management plan so the ambiguity does not recur. (A) assumes silence equals permission. (B) assumes silence equals prohibition. (D) is a reasonable fallback but sidesteps the clarification the PM should make for future cases.
