# Question

An approved change on a predictive construction project will consume the entire contingency reserve. The sponsor is uncomfortable because future unknowns will have no reserve left at all. What should the PM recommend as next steps?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with contingency release as planned, since the change has been formally approved by the CCB.
- **B**: Request access to management reserve through the sponsor's authority with justification and risk impact analysis.
- **C**: Reduce scope elsewhere in the project to free contingency that can then be held back for future unknowns.
- **D**: Approve the change but only partially fund it from contingency, with the remainder coming from sponsor discretion.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contingency reserve covers known risks within PM authority; management reserve covers unknown-unknowns and requires sponsor approval with justification. (A) ignores the risk of zero remaining reserve for unknowns. (C) unilaterally cuts scope rather than surfacing the reserve question. (D) mixes funding sources without a formal request for management reserve.
