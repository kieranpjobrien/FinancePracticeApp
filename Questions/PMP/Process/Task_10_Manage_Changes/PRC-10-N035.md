# Question

A change request to add an integration with an external payment provider has been logged on a predictive project. The project manager has already costed it and confirmed the schedule slip is two days with no critical-path impact, and the sponsor has confirmed funding. As the project manager finalises the impact assessment for the change control board, which addition would most strengthen the board's ability to decide?

## Options

- **A**: A breakdown of the additional testing effort and the new licensing fees the integration introduces.
- **B**: An assessment of the new operational and security risks created by depending on a third-party provider, with proposed responses.
- **C**: A revised resource histogram showing the two extra days of developer time the integration requires.
- **D**: Confirmation from the requesting department that the integration aligns with their reporting needs.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The assessment already covers cost and a quantified, non-critical schedule impact with funding confirmed, so the dimension still missing and most decision-relevant is the risk a new external dependency introduces, together with how those risks would be handled. (B) gives the board the one input that could change its decision even though the numbers look acceptable. (A) is largely redundant: cost has already been established, and listing the testing and licensing components restates rather than extends it. (C) refines a schedule impact already shown to be immaterial to the critical path, adding little to the decision. (D) confirms desirability, which the funded, sponsor-backed request has effectively settled; it does not surface a new consequence of approving. Because integrated change control weighs the full ripple of a change, the unexamined risk dimension is what most strengthens the board's basis to decide.
