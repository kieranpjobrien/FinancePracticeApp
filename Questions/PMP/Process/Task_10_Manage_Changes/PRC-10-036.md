# Question

A fast-tracked project is generating change requests faster than the weekly CCB can evaluate them; the backlog is two weeks deep and growing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin approving low-impact changes directly to clear the queue and restore the delivery tempo quickly.
- **B**: Auto-reject any change request older than 14 days on the backlog to keep the queue manageable going forward.
- **C**: Filter the queue and only present the changes the PM judges most important to the CCB each week.
- **D**: Propose adjusting governance: increase CCB cadence and define delegated authority bands to match tempo.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The change process must scale with the project's tempo; the right intervention is structural. Higher cadence and defined delegation thresholds keep governance intact while clearing the bottleneck. (A) introduces ad-hoc bypass without authority. (B) discards potentially valuable changes for procedural reasons. (C) puts the PM in the role of gatekeeper to the CCB, which they are not authorised to be.
