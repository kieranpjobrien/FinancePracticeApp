# Question

During a code review, the PM learns that a developer pushed an "obvious improvement" to the deliverable last sprint without raising a change request. The change is harmless but bypassed the team's defined change control. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the change retroactively in the change log and coach the developer on the process.
- **B**: Have the developer revert the change and resubmit it through proper channels before any further work.
- **C**: Accept the change because it improves the product and avoid creating unnecessary friction with the developer.
- **D**: Raise the developer's behaviour with their functional manager for it to be reflected in performance review.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to restore traceability and use the moment to reinforce the process. Logging the change preserves the audit trail and configuration record, and coaching addresses the behaviour at the appropriate level. (B) is disproportionate for a benign change and wastes effort. (C) normalises bypassing change control and erodes discipline. (D) escalates a coaching moment unnecessarily and skips the PM's own responsibility to address it directly.
