# Question

A change request to re-route a section of a fibre-network project has just been approved by the change control board. The project manager now needs to put the approved change into effect properly. Which three of the following are appropriate actions as part of implementing this approved change? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Update the affected components of the project management plan and the relevant baselines to reflect the approved re-route.
- **B**: Re-run the cost-benefit analysis the board used, to confirm independently that the board reached the right conclusion before proceeding.
- **C**: Communicate the approved change and its implications to the affected stakeholders, including the field crews and the customer.
- **D**: Direct the work to begin under the approved change while the plan and baseline updates are completed in parallel afterwards.
- **E**: Update the change log to record the approval and the change's disposition.

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

Implementing an approved change means propagating it into the plan and baselines (A), communicating it to those affected (C) and recording its disposition in the change log (E); together these enact the decision and keep the project's artifacts consistent. (B) re-performs analysis the board has already used to decide, second-guessing the authorised decision and adding no value once approval is given. (D) is tempting because it feels efficient, but starting work before the baselines and plan are updated means the team executes against stale documents and progress is measured against an out-of-date baseline; the updates should be in place so work proceeds against the current plan. Approved changes are propagated, communicated and recorded, then executed against the updated baseline, not re-analysed or run ahead of the plan update.
