# Question

The sponsor of a predictive reporting project phones the project manager directly and, during a friendly call, asks her to "just slip in" two extra report formats before next week's demo. He is the project's main funder and has authority over scope. The work is genuinely small and the project manager believes it can be absorbed. What is the best response?

## Options

- **A**: Acknowledge the request on the call, then capture it and run it through the project's change process so the impact is assessed and the decision recorded before any work starts.
- **B**: Confirm on the call that the team will do it, since the sponsor holds scope authority and explicitly directed the addition.
- **C**: Add the two formats to the team's work for the demo and report the change to the sponsor in the next status update for the record.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to put the request in an email so there is a written instruction, then have the team begin the formats.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A sponsor can authorise scope, but the mechanism still matters: the request is a change to the agreed baseline and must enter change control so its impact (even if small) is assessed and the decision is documented, protecting both parties later. (A) honours the sponsor's authority and the process. (B) acts on a verbal corridor instruction with no impact analysis and no controlled record. (C) executes first and documents afterwards, inverting assess-then-decide. (D) obtains a written instruction, which feels rigorous, but a directive email is not the same as assessing impact and processing the change; work still begins before any evaluation. Capturing and assessing through the change process is the controlled path even when the requester is the sponsor.
