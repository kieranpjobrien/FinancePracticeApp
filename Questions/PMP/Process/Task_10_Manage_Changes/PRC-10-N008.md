# Question

A hybrid programme builds a customer portal using Scrum teams, but the overall budget, vendor contracts and the regulatory scope are governed at the programme level under a change control board. The programme manager is deciding which decisions belong to the teams and which to the board. Which of the following should be routed to the programme change control board rather than handled by the Scrum teams? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Increasing the total programme budget to fund an additional integration.
- **B**: Reordering the next sprint's backlog after new user feedback.
- **C**: A change to the regulatory scope the programme is contractually obliged to deliver.
- **D**: Modifying a fixed-price vendor contract to add a new deliverable.
- **E**: Splitting a large user story into two smaller ones during refinement.

## Answer

A, C, D

## Explanation

Budget increases (A), regulatory scope obligations (C) and contractual vendor changes (D) cross the boundary of team self-management and affect baselines, contracts and compliance, so they belong to programme-level change control. Reordering a backlog (B) and splitting a story in refinement (E) are ordinary agile activities the product owner and team own, requiring no board. The principle is that empowered teams manage their own work within agreed guardrails, while changes to money, contracts and compliance escalate to formal governance.
