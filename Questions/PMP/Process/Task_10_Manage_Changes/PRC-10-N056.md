# Question

A change control board is about to convene to dispose of a request to switch a predictive data-centre project from one cooling technology to another. As project manager, which inputs should you ensure are in front of the board so it can make a sound disposition? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: A documented impact assessment covering scope, schedule, cost, quality and risk for the proposed switch.
- **B**: The change management plan defining the board's authority and the criteria it applies to decisions.
- **C**: The current scope, schedule and cost baselines the change would affect, for reference.
- **D**: A detailed technical specification of the new cooling units, so the board can validate the engineering design itself.
- **E**: The full design-stage trade study that originally selected the current cooling technology.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A sound disposition needs the impact assessment across all dimensions (A), the change management plan that frames the board's authority and decision criteria (B), and the affected baselines the change would move (C). (D) asks the board to re-do engineering validation, which is the project team's job to assure, not the board's; the board weighs project impact, not component design. (E) is historical justification for the earlier decision and does not tell the board the impact of switching now; reopening the original trade study is a distraction from disposing of the present request. The board decides on assessed impact against governance criteria, not by re-engineering the solution or relitigating past choices.
