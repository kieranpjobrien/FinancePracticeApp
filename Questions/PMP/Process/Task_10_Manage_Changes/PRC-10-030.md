# Question

The PM has delegated authority to approve changes under $5,000. A change request lands with an estimated cost of $4,500, but the PM, based on similar past work, expects the real cost will land between $6,000 and $7,500. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change because the documented estimate is below the PM's threshold.
- **B**: Refine the estimate using analogous or parametric techniques and decide on the more reliable number.
- **C**: Route the change to the CCB regardless of the estimate to be safe.
- **D**: Approve the change and add a clause that the team will stop work if costs exceed $5,000.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Approval thresholds depend on accurate cost estimates. The PM should improve the estimate before deciding whether the change falls within delegated authority. (A) hides behind a number the PM already believes is wrong. (C) is overcautious without first improving the data; routing every uncertain change to the CCB undermines delegation. (D) approves on weak data and pushes the consequences onto the team mid-implementation.
