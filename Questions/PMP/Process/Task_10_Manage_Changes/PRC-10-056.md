# Question

Midway through a two-year programme, the PM realises the change log has not been updated in six weeks. Twelve approved changes are undocumented. An internal audit is scheduled in three weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Defer change log updates until after the audit so the audit reviews the current formal record as it stands.
- **B**: Quietly record the backlog of twelve changes during the week before audit without telling anyone about it.
- **C**: Reconstruct the change log from source artifacts and communicate the gap to the audit team before audit.
- **D**: Ask audit to focus on process improvement rather than historical log accuracy during the upcoming review.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Transparency and integrity with audit require prompt reconstruction and disclosure rather than concealment or deferral. (A) lets the audit run against an incomplete record, increasing findings. (B) reconstructs quietly in a way that looks like concealment. (D) asks audit to narrow scope to avoid exposure of the gap.
