# Question

The change control board has approved a change that increases the project budget by 4%. The project manager's delegated authority allows cost changes up to 5% without sponsor sign-off. However, the change also pushes the delivery date past a contractual milestone tied to a customer payment. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Re-submit the change to the CCB and ask them to reconsider given the milestone slip.
- **B**: Implement the change, as the cost increase is within the 5% delegated authority.
- **C**: Escalate to the sponsor before implementing, because the milestone impact exceeds what the cost-based delegation covers.
- **D**: Implement the change but notify the customer that the contractual milestone will be missed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Delegated authority defined in cost terms does not extend to a consequence the delegation never contemplated, here a contractual milestone slip with payment implications, so the project manager escalates to the sponsor who owns that contractual relationship. (B) reads the delegation too literally and ignores the contractual dimension. (A) is unnecessary because the CCB already approved the change; the issue is authority to accept the contractual impact, not the technical merit. (D) notifies the wrong party first and commits to the slip before the sponsor has decided. Escalation respects the limits of the project manager's authority.
