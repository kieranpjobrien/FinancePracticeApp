# Question

A predictive safety upgrade project faces an urgent regulator-imposed deadline that appeared overnight. The sponsor instructs the PM to skip the change control board and act immediately, promising to backfill the paperwork later. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Follow change control by convening an emergency CCB the same day, then act per the decision.
- **B**: Act on the sponsor's direction now and create change documentation retrospectively after the deadline.
- **C**: Delay the regulator's action until a regularly scheduled CCB meeting is due to review the change.
- **D**: Split the response into emergency steps now and formal change control for any remaining work later.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Change control can be urgent but cannot be skipped; an emergency CCB preserves governance while matching urgency. (B) skips governance and normalises the bypass. (C) ignores the regulator's timeline and harms compliance standing. (D) institutionalises two standards, one of which bypasses integrated change control.
