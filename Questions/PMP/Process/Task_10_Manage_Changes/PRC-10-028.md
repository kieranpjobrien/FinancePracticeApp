# Question

A regulated project keeps its change log in a shared spreadsheet that any team member can open and edit. An auditor flags this as a concern. What is the primary risk this arrangement creates?

## Options

- **A**: The spreadsheet may grow too large for the team to navigate efficiently across many sprints.
- **B**: Concurrent edits and lack of an immutable audit trail compromise the integrity of the change record.
- **C**: Stakeholders outside the core team may not have direct access to the shared file at any given time.
- **D**: The spreadsheet format itself may not be acceptable to certain auditors in some regulated industries.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Audit trails for regulated projects must guarantee that records have not been altered. A shared, freely editable spreadsheet allows silent overwrites, lost edits, and no reliable history of who changed what when, undermining the integrity of the change log. (A) is an administrative inconvenience, not the primary audit risk. (C) is a distribution issue. (D) is possible but secondary to the integrity problem.
