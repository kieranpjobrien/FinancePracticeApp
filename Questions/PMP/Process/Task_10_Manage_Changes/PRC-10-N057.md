# Question

Midway through a release, an agile team and product owner agree to tighten the definition of done to include automated accessibility checks, after a stakeholder raised legal exposure. The team asks whether stories already accepted under the old definition need to be revisited. What should the Scrum Master guide them to do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the new definition of done only to stories completed from now on and leave previously accepted stories untouched.
- **B**: Have the product owner decide whether the accessibility gap in already-accepted work warrants new backlog items to bring it up to the tightened standard, then prioritise that work.
- **C**: Immediately rework all previously accepted stories to meet the new definition before any new work proceeds.
- **D**: Keep the old definition for the whole release to avoid inconsistency, and apply the new one only next release.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A tightened definition of done applies to future work, but any gap in already-accepted stories that now matters (here, legal accessibility exposure) becomes new backlog items the product owner prioritises against everything else. (B) handles both the going-forward standard and the residual gap through normal backlog economics. (A) ignores a real legal exposure in existing work by simply moving on. (C) forces blanket immediate rework regardless of value or risk, displacing higher-priority work. (D) defers a standard the team and product owner already agreed is needed now, leaving the legal exposure unaddressed for the whole release.
