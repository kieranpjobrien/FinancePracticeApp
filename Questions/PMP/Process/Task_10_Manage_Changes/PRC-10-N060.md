# Question

A predictive project receives a change request to upgrade the database platform. The sponsor wants it in front of the change control board at tomorrow's meeting and the database lead is confident the upgrade is straightforward. The project manager has logged the request but has not yet worked out its effect on the data-migration schedule, the interfaces, or the support contract. The board meets only monthly. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Complete the cross-impact analysis on schedule, interfaces and contract first, even if that means the change goes to the following month's board rather than tomorrow's.
- **B**: Take the request to tomorrow's board with the database lead's confidence noted, so the decision is not delayed a month, and analyse the details once it is approved.
- **C**: Ask the board to approve the upgrade in principle tomorrow, conditional on the impact analysis being completed before build starts.
- **D**: Since the lead says it is straightforward, update the schedule baseline for the upgrade now so the board sees a ready plan tomorrow.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The board can only make a sound disposition once the change's impact across the affected areas is assessed, so the analysis must precede the decision even at the cost of waiting for the next monthly board. (A) preserves the integrity of the decision over the convenience of the calendar. (B) brings an unassessed change to the board on one person's optimism, the exact gap integrated change control exists to close, and defers analysis until after a decision that should have depended on it. (C) "approval in principle" still commits the board to a change whose schedule, interface and contract impacts are unknown, and conditions are routinely overtaken once a change is greenlit. (D) updates a baseline before any approval or analysis, inverting the process on the strength of an informal assurance.
