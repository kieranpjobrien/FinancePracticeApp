# Question

Midway through a hospital records migration, the PM reviews the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix. The head of nursing was recorded as "supportive" at planning but has missed the last three steering meetings and now responds to emails only after several days. Clinical sign-off for the next phase depends on her. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the matrix to reclassify her current engagement level and tailor a renewed approach to move her back towards supportive.
- **B**: Speak with her directly to understand what has changed and what she now needs from the project before updating any artifact.
- **C**: Route the clinical sign-off through her deputy, who has been attending and remains engaged with the project.
- **D**: Flag the slipping engagement to the sponsor as a risk to the next phase and request help re-engaging her.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The matrix is a planning aid, but a changed engagement signal warrants understanding the cause before adjusting the artifact or the approach — talking to her first (B) gives you the why, which drives everything else. (A) updates the record and picks an approach, but choosing tactics without knowing what changed risks the wrong remedy. (C) bypasses the accountable clinical authority and may breach governance for sign-off. (D) escalates prematurely; you have not yet attempted the direct, low-cost conversation that may resolve it and that the sponsor would expect you to try first.
