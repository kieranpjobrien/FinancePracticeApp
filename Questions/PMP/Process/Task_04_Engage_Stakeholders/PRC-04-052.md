# Question

A PM is refining the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix. A stakeholder is currently "resistant" and the desired state is "supportive". Previous attempts at direct engagement have produced polite agreement that hasn't translated into behaviour change. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue direct engagement with the stakeholder since persistent effort usually moves a "polite agreement" state toward genuine support over time.
- **B**: Identify the people who influence this stakeholder and engage them so supportive behaviour reaches the stakeholder from trusted sources.
- **C**: Downgrade the desired state to "neutral" since "supportive" doesn't appear achievable given the stakeholder's current pattern and response.
- **D**: Escalate the pattern to the sponsor and ask them to intervene with the resistant stakeholder personally on the programme's behalf.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When direct engagement plateaus, PMI-aligned practice uses the stakeholder's influence network; salience theory recognises indirect influence is often decisive. (B) widens the approach. (A) more of what isn't working isn't a strategy. (C) lowering the target accepts an avoidable problem and may not serve programme outcomes. (D) escalating without trying influence-network engagement skips a useful option.
