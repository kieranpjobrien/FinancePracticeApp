# Question

A PM inherits a predictive pharmaceutical-plant commissioning project a year into delivery. Engagement is patchy, and in the last two months several issues have surfaced from people the project should have been talking to but was not — a regulator's new requirement, a changed delivery date from a key supplier. The stakeholder register exists but the PM has not yet worked through how current it is. What should the PM prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Work through the stakeholder register to bring it up to date, then realign the engagement approach to the corrected picture.
- **B**: Move straight to re-engaging the stakeholders behind the recent surprises, since those relationships are the ones actively causing problems.
- **C**: Stand up a recurring monthly forum and invite the current stakeholder list, so communication becomes regular and no one is missed.
- **D**: Concentrate first on the highest-impact stakeholders for the commissioning phase and engage the rest as time allows.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The surprises are a symptom of an engagement picture the PM cannot yet trust, so the foundational move is to refresh the register — who exists now, who has changed, who was never really contacted — and then realign engagement to that corrected picture (A); acting on an unverified stakeholder list risks chasing the wrong or absent people. (B) addresses the loudest problems and feels urgent, but firefighting individual surprises without correcting the underlying list leaves the same blind spots generating the next surprise. (C) bolts a regular forum onto a stakeholder set of unknown accuracy, so the wrong people may be invited and genuinely material ones still missed. (D) prioritising by impact is sound practice, yet you cannot reliably judge who is highest-impact until the register reflects reality. The margin is between correcting the stakeholder picture first (A) and jumping to engage, convene, or prioritise on top of an unverified one (B, C, D).
