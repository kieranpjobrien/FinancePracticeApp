# Question

An agile team is two sprints into building a mobile banking feature. The product owner has been representing "the business" alone, but during a sprint review a compliance officer appears for the first time and states that several already-completed stories breach a mandatory disclosure rule. The team is frustrated; the product owner is embarrassed. The release is in four sprints. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Add the compliance officer to the backlog refinement and review cadence so the breached stories and all future stories are checked against disclosure rules.
- **B**: Work with the product owner to revisit how stakeholders were identified for this product, so compliance and any other overlooked voices are represented going forward.
- **C**: Convene the compliance officer and product owner to agree the remediation of the breached stories and fold the disclosure rule into the team's definition of done.
- **D**: Raise the breach with the sponsor and ask the compliance function to nominate a representative to work with the product owner for the rest of the release.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A whole class of stakeholder — compliance — was never surfaced during identification, and the same blind spot may be hiding others (legal, security, operations). The first move is to fix the identification process with the product owner (B), because that is what prevents the next unseen stakeholder from re-emerging at a future review; remediating this specific breach and tightening the definition of done follow once you know who else was missed. (A) and (C) both correctly engage compliance and harden quality going forward, but they treat compliance as the single gap to plug rather than testing whether identification itself failed — so a different omitted voice can repeat the surprise. (D) is a reasonable escalation given a mandatory breach, but routing in one nominated representative again fixes only the compliance gap and defers the broader re-identification that is the actual root cause.
