# Question

Late in planning a hospital wing refurbishment, the project manager notices that a respected senior consultant surgeon, who holds no formal project role, is privately shaping the opinions of several department heads who are formal stakeholders. Their feedback increasingly echoes his unstated preferences. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the consultant surgeon directly, acknowledge his influence, and bring his perspective into the formal stakeholder process.
- **B**: Continue working through the formal stakeholders, since the consultant holds no defined role and decisions should follow the governance structure.
- **C**: Raise with the department heads that their feedback appears externally influenced and ask them to base it on the project's own information.
- **D**: Add a note to the risk register about informal influence and monitor whether it begins to distort formal decisions.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

An influential informal stakeholder who is already shaping formal stakeholders' views is a real force on the project, and the engaging response is to recognise his influence and draw him into the process rather than ignore or resist it. (A) converts hidden influence into transparent, manageable engagement. (B) sticking strictly to formal roles ignores how the project's stakeholder reality actually operates and lets his influence run unmanaged. (C) confronting the department heads about being influenced is likely to feel accusatory and damages those relationships without engaging the source. (D) logging and monitoring is passive when the influence is already affecting feedback and a constructive engagement option is available now.
