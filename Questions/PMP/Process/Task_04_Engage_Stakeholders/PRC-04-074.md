# Question

A construction project's monthly steering pack runs to forty pages of schedule detail, cost tables, and risk logs. The executive sponsor has twice asked "so are we on track or not?" during reviews and recently forwarded the pack unread. The project manager wants the sponsor to stay engaged and able to act on issues. What should the PM change?

## Options

- **A**: Open the pack with a one-page summary stating overall status, the two or three decisions needed, and key risks, keeping the detail as an appendix.
- **B**: Keep the full pack but walk the sponsor through it live each month so nothing important is missed in the volume of detail.
- **C**: Reduce the pack to a short status email and drop the detailed sections the sponsor does not read.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor which sections they want and remove the rest, tailoring the pack strictly to stated preferences.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The sponsor needs decision-relevant information at the right altitude, and the engaging move is to lead with a concise summary and decisions while preserving detail for those who need it. (A) tailors the message to the sponsor without discarding information other stakeholders or governance may require. (B) more live walkthrough time does not fix that the format buries the signal the sponsor wants. (C) cutting the detail entirely may remove content needed for the record or for other readers of the pack. (D) asking the sponsor's section preferences risks stripping governance content and still may not surface the headline status they actually want.
