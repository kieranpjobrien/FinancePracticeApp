# Question

An agile team developing a patient-appointment system demonstrates a completed booking workflow at the sprint review. A clinical stakeholder, seeing it working for the first time, realises the workflow omits a triage step that is essential in practice. Adding it is significant work not in the current release plan. The product owner looks to the PM for guidance. How should the PM advise proceeding?

## Options

- **A**: Capture the triage requirement in the backlog and let the product owner prioritise it against existing items for upcoming sprints.
- **B**: Treat the omission as a defect in the completed workflow and have the team fix it within the current sprint.
- **C**: Raise a change request through the change control board before the team does any further work on the triage step.
- **D**: Add the triage step immediately, since a clinical stakeholder has confirmed it is essential to the workflow.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sprint reviews exist precisely to surface emerging needs, and the correct agile response is to add the requirement to the backlog for the product owner to prioritise — (A) — which honours both the new value and the team's current commitments. (B) framing it as a defect is wrong; the team built what was agreed, and disrupting the in-flight sprint violates the sprint commitment. (C) a formal change-control board is a predictive mechanism that the backlog already replaces in this agile context, adding needless bureaucracy. (D) inserting it immediately bypasses prioritisation and lets a single stakeholder reorder the work, undermining the product owner's accountability for value.
