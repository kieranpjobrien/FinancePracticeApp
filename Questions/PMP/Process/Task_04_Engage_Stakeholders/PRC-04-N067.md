# Question

On an agile public-health data platform, the epidemiology lead wants advanced outbreak-modelling analytics while the operations lead wants basic data-entry reliability fixed first. Both hold legitimate authority, both are escalating to the programme board, and the product owner now reorders the backlog after whichever conversation happened last. Both leads are available this week. The next sprint planning is in two days. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Convene the two leads and the product owner to agree explicit value criteria for ranking the backlog, then prioritise against those criteria.
- **B**: Ask the sponsor to rule which lead's priorities take precedence, so the product owner has a clear authority to point to and can stop reordering.
- **C**: Have the product owner publish the current ordering with the rationale for each item and commit to changing it only through an agreed change path.
- **D**: For the next sprint only, allocate capacity across both leads' top items so planning can proceed, and resolve the prioritisation conflict afterwards.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The dysfunction is that there is no shared basis for deciding value, so each stakeholder's last conversation wins; engaging the stakeholders to agree how priority will be determined attacks that cause, and (A) does so while producing an ordering the product owner can defend transparently. (B) would resolve the immediate jam and a sponsor does have the authority, but a winner-takes-all ruling leaves the losing lead disengaged and the underlying conflict unaddressed, so escalations recur. (C) rightly stops the thrash by making the ordering and its rationale visible, yet without an agreed value basis the published rationale is still the PM's view, and the two leads have no reason to stop escalating against it. (D) is a pragmatic way to make the two-day deadline, but spreading capacity to avoid the trade-off defers the decision the project must eventually make and can leave both leads' top items half-finished. The margin is that (A) is the only option that builds the shared criteria the others quietly assume already exist.
