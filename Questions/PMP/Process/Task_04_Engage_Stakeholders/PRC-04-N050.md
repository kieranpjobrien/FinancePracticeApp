# Question

A project manager is planning communications for an enterprise CRM rollout. The executive sponsor has said she wants to stay informed but has "no time for detail". The PM is deciding how to keep her engaged. What is the most appropriate approach?

## Options

- **A**: Provide a brief monthly dashboard highlighting status, key risks, and decisions needed, with detail available on request.
- **B**: Forward the full weekly project status report so the sponsor has complete information whenever she chooses to read it.
- **C**: Schedule a standing weekly meeting to walk the sponsor through progress in person.
- **D**: Update the sponsor only when a decision or escalation requires her direct involvement.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Tailoring communication to a stakeholder's stated needs is core to engagement; a concise dashboard surfacing status, risks, and required decisions matches a time-poor executive who still wants oversight (A). (B) sending the full report ignores her explicit preference and pushes the filtering burden onto her. (C) a weekly in-person walkthrough contradicts "no time for detail" and over-consumes scarce executive time. (D) updating only on escalation leaves her out of the loop between decisions, which is not the "stay informed" engagement she asked for.
