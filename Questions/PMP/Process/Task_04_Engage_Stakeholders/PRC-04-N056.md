# Question

A PM has just been assigned to an office-relocation project. A stakeholder register already exists, naming the facilities lead, the affected department heads, the landlord, and IT. The PM has a kick-off to run next week and wants the relocation's stakeholders to back it from the outset. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Assess each listed stakeholder's interest, influence, and likely attitude to the move so engagement can be tailored to each.
- **B**: Send every listed stakeholder the approved charter and the draft relocation timeline ahead of the kick-off.
- **C**: Draft a communications management plan setting out the channel and frequency of updates for each stakeholder.
- **D**: Meet the department heads first, since the staff they manage are the ones the relocation most directly disrupts.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The register tells the PM who the stakeholders are but nothing about how each regards the move, and a relocation typically meets very different dispositions — a landlord chasing a signed lease, department heads braced for disruption, IT weighing an outage. Assessing interest, influence, and attitude (A) is what lets every later step — the kick-off framing, the comms plan, who to meet first — be tailored rather than generic, so it precedes them. (C) the communications plan is a sound next artifact, but its channel-and-frequency choices should follow the disposition analysis, not pre-empt it. (B) pushing the charter and timeline to everyone is undifferentiated broadcast before the PM knows who needs persuading versus merely informing. (D) prioritising the department heads is a reasonable judgement, but committing to that sequence before assessing the others risks under-weighting a landlord or IT stakeholder who could derail the date; the assessment is what justifies any such ordering.
