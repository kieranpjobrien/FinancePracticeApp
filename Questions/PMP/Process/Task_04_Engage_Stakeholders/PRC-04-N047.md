# Question

During an agile project to build a logistics tracking app, sprint reviews are well attended by stakeholders, but the same senior operations director dominates every session with detailed feature requests, while warehouse supervisors who will be the primary users stay silent. The product backlog is increasingly skewed towards the director's preferences. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Restructure the review so each user group, including the supervisors, gives structured feedback before open discussion begins.
- **B**: Hold separate feedback sessions with the warehouse supervisors so their needs are captured without the director present.
- **C**: Privately ask the operations director to allow more space for others to contribute during the reviews.
- **D**: Have the product owner reweight the backlog to better balance the director's requests against the supervisors' needs.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The structural problem is that one voice crowds out the actual users in the feedback forum, so changing the forum's structure to guarantee every group is heard (A) treats the cause at the point it occurs. (B) a side session captures the supervisors' input but concedes the review itself to the director and fragments feedback. (C) a quiet word may help but relies on the director self-moderating and does not actively draw out the silent users. (D) reweighting the backlog corrects today's symptom while the skew regenerates at the next review because the feedback dynamic is unchanged.
