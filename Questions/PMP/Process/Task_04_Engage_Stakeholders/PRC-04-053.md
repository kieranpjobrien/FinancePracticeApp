# Question

A project sponsor has been unavailable for four consecutive monthly steering committees due to competing priorities. Their deputy attends but doesn't have decision authority. Decisions are being deferred and momentum is slowing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue working with the deputy and escalate only the most critical decisions by email directly to the sponsor when needed.
- **B**: Request the sponsor delegates full decision authority to the deputy in writing so steering committee decisions can proceed effectively.
- **C**: Meet with the sponsor one-to-one to raise the impact of the absences and agree either renewed attendance or a fully empowered delegate.
- **D**: Ask the PMO to reassign the project to a more available sponsor so governance decisions can proceed without continued delays.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Sponsor engagement is a relationship, not a role seat; PMI expects the PM to engage the sponsor directly on sponsorship issues. (C) raises the problem and proposes solutions. (A) email-only escalation is unlikely to recover sponsor engagement. (B) demanding written delegation may not be culturally or organisationally appropriate without a conversation first. (D) requesting sponsor reassignment is extreme and damages the sponsor relationship unnecessarily.
