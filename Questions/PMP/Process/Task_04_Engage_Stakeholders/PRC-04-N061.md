# Question

On an agile programme replatforming a retail bank's mortgage origination system, a regional operations head was assessed at kickoff as neutral and low-influence. Over the last two releases he has begun lobbying two steering-committee members and has secured a vote in the upcoming funding decision. The product owner, unaware of this, continues to rank the operations head's backlog items near the bottom. The next steering committee is in a week, and the project manager has just learned all of this from a passing comment. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Speak with the operations head to learn what outcomes he is pursuing and why he is advancing them through the committee rather than the product owner.
- **B**: Update the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix to reflect his increased influence, then design a tailored engagement approach for the new classification.
- **C**: Give the product owner a heads-up that the operations head now has a funding vote, so the backlog ranking can account for that before the committee meets.
- **D**: Raise the shift discreetly with the sponsor and agree how much weight the operations head's position should carry in the funding decision.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Every downstream move — reclassifying him, briefing the product owner, aligning with the sponsor — depends on knowing what the operations head actually wants and why he bypassed the product owner, and only (A) gets that. (B) is the correct artifact to update and a tailored approach is needed, but rebuilding the engagement strategy from observed behaviour alone, before understanding his motivation, risks tailoring to the wrong interest. (C) is reasonable and will likely follow, but prompting the product owner to factor in his vote nudges ranking toward influence ahead of validated value, and is premature before the PM knows what he is even asking for. (D) is a legitimate governance conversation, but pre-agreeing how much weight he carries before the PM has spoken to him lets the sponsor frame the relationship the PM has not yet established. The fine margin is sequencing: understand the stakeholder, then update the matrix, brief the team, and align governance.
