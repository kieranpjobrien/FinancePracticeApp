# Question

An agile team has a proxy product owner who represents customers but rarely talks with actual customers. The team is building features based on assumptions the proxy PO relays. Customer feedback from recent releases suggests meaningful drift from real needs. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the proxy product owner with someone who has real customer access and decision authority for the product's direction across releases.
- **B**: Establish a direct customer feedback loop — interviews, usage data, advisory board — feeding both the team and the proxy PO regularly.
- **C**: Ask the proxy product owner to speak with customers directly at least monthly so assumptions are informed by actual customer voices each cycle.
- **D**: Run a customer-focused retrospective so the team understands where their own assumptions diverge from observed actual customer behaviours in the product over time.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI and agile practice prescribe a direct feedback loop to the actual customer; a proxy plus research channels closes the gap without a disruptive role change. (B) creates that loop. (A) replacing the PO is disruptive and may not be feasible. (C) asking the proxy to speak with customers monthly is a partial step and doesn't inform the team directly. (D) retrospectives surface the drift but don't build the ongoing input channel.
