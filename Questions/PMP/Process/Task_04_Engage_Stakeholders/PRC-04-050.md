# Question

A mobile app team is building a feature for call-centre agents. The PO represents the business but has never worked in a call centre. Early demos have received polite positive feedback from the PO but the team suspects agents will struggle with the design. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange direct user-research sessions with actual call-centre agents to validate the design before further development investment is made.
- **B**: Continue with the PO's feedback since the PO carries legitimate business authority for the feature's scope and design decisions.
- **C**: Add a UX researcher to the team permanently to ensure end-user perspectives are represented across all features built going forward.
- **D**: Schedule a showcase demo with call-centre leadership so their concerns and sign-off are clearly captured before release readiness.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI-aligned engagement prioritises the actual end-user voice on usability-sensitive features; proxy feedback isn't enough. (A) does that directly. (B) PO authority doesn't substitute for end-user validation on usability. (C) a permanent UX hire is disproportionate for validating one feature. (D) leadership feedback isn't a substitute for agent feedback on a tool agents will use daily.
