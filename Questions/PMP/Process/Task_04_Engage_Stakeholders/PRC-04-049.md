# Question

Eight months into a twelve-month programme, a new regulatory body publishes guidance that affects the programme's deliverables. The PM hadn't identified this regulator as a stakeholder. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Update the stakeholder register with the regulator and run an analysis on their power, interest and likely engagement needs for the programme.
- **B**: Arrange an immediate meeting with the regulator to understand their concerns and expected programme-level interactions over the remaining months.
- **C**: Consult the legal and compliance teams on the guidance and plan the regulator engagement with their specialist input and ongoing support.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor how the organisation wants the regulator handled, since the sponsor owns external regulatory relationships broadly at this level.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Engaging regulators without internal legal and compliance input is high-risk; PMI-aligned practice uses specialist support before direct engagement. (C) gets the right expertise first. (A) a register update is part of the response but isn't the first move with a regulator. (B) reaching out to a regulator before briefing internal specialists can create commitments or exposure. (D) asking the sponsor before engaging specialists skips the expertise that shapes that conversation.
