# Question

On a predictive offshore wind-farm construction project, a small coastal fishing cooperative — logged at project start as low power, low interest and given no targeted engagement — has started running local press coverage and lobbying the council that issues the project's marine works permit. The permit renewal is in three months and a refusal would halt construction. What is the most appropriate first response?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the cooperative directly to understand the concerns behind its campaign and look for ways to address them.
- **B**: Brief the permitting council with evidence the project meets every marine works condition, ahead of the renewal decision.
- **C**: Work with the communications team on a media-response plan to correct inaccurate claims in the local coverage quickly.
- **D**: Commission an updated assessment of the cooperative's influence and revise the engagement plan to match its new salience.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The cooperative has acquired real leverage over a project-critical permit, and the grievance driving its campaign is something only direct engagement can surface and address; meeting them to understand and, where possible, resolve their concerns attacks the cause and is the move most likely to defuse both the lobbying and the media (A). (B) protecting the permit relationship with the council is legitimate and probably needed, but reassuring the authority while never speaking to the aggrieved party can read as going over their heads and leaves the underlying concern alive. (C) managing the narrative addresses the symptom — coverage — not the people generating it, and rebuttals often escalate a dispute rather than settle it. (D) is the correct analytical step and must happen, but commissioning an assessment and rewriting the plan is documentation about the stakeholder; with three months to a halting decision, engaging them comes before re-papering the matrix. The margin is between engaging the source first (A) and the defensible instincts to secure the permit, manage the media, or reassess on paper first (B, C, D).
