# Question

On a hybrid hospital records system, the chief medical officer wants clinicians to enter structured data on every patient contact to improve reporting, while the head of nursing says this will add unacceptable time at the bedside and reduce care quality. Both are senior and influential. The next planning increment cannot be finalised until the data-entry approach is set, and that increment is committed for two weeks' time. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both leaders together to surface the needs behind each position and seek a data-entry approach that serves reporting without overloading clinicians.
- **B**: Run a short structured-data trial on one ward so a measured time impact, rather than two opinions, informs the decision.
- **C**: Confirm who actually holds decision authority over clinical data standards, then take the disagreement to that owner to rule.
- **D**: Frame two or three options with their reporting-versus-time trade-offs and circulate them to both leaders for comment before the increment.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The conflict is between two influential stakeholders whose stated positions hide underlying interests — reliable reporting and protected bedside time — that are not necessarily mutually exclusive; the first engagement move is to get them into the same conversation to expose those interests and look for an approach both can back (A). (B) would produce useful evidence, but launching a trial before the parties have discussed their needs frames the issue as a contest one side must lose and can entrench positions. (C) respects governance and may be needed later, but routing to a decision-owner to adjudicate forecloses a solution the two could reach together and treats an alignment problem as an authority one. (D) is genuinely collaborative and close to right, yet circulating pre-built options asynchronously substitutes the PM's framing for a real dialogue and can harden each leader around the option that favours them. The margin is between facilitating a joint conversation first (A) and the defensible alternatives of letting data, authority, or written options drive it (B, C, D).
