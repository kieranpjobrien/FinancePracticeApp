# Question

An agile team building an e-commerce checkout redesign takes all of its customer direction from the product owner. Velocity is healthy and the product owner is decisive and trusted, but three consecutive releases have under-delivered on conversion, and decisions made on the product owner's read of shopper behaviour keep being contradicted by production data. What should the project manager encourage?

## Options

- **A**: Open direct channels for the team to observe and hear from real customers, alongside the product owner's input.
- **B**: Have the product owner pressure-test each major assumption against production analytics and prior A/B results before it enters a sprint.
- **C**: Introduce a regular customer-discovery cadence the product owner runs, so insight is gathered systematically rather than ad hoc.
- **D**: Add a conversion target as an acceptance criterion on checkout stories so outcomes, not output, drive what the team builds.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The releases keep missing because the team's understanding of customers is filtered through a single person whose reads are proving wrong; the most effective lever is to give the team its own first-hand exposure to customers so insight no longer depends on one interpreter (A), while keeping the trusted product owner's role intact. (B) is genuinely useful and tightens rigour, but checking assumptions against internal data still keeps the team one step removed from customers and relies on the same person choosing what to test. (C) is also sound and improves on ad-hoc guessing, yet routing all discovery through the product owner preserves the very bottleneck that is failing — better cadence, same single channel. (D) sharpens outcome accountability but holds the team to a result they cannot reliably hit without the customer insight that is actually missing. The margin is between widening who hears from customers (A) and improving how the existing single conduit works (B, C).
