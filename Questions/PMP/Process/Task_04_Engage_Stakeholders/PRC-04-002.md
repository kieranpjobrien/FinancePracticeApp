# Question

A stakeholder has shifted from 'supportive' to 'resistant' based on the current engagement assessment matrix. The PM's attempts to meet with them have been declined twice. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue current communication and wait for the stakeholder to re-engage on their own timeline.
- **B**: Escalate to the sponsor immediately since the stakeholder is not responding to the PM.
- **C**: Send a detailed written briefing addressing what the PM assumes are the stakeholder's concerns.
- **D**: Seek a trusted peer of the stakeholder to understand what changed and help facilitate a conversation.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When direct channels fail, PMI expects the PM to find an indirect path through the stakeholder's network rather than guess or escalate. (A) waiting lets resistance harden into active opposition. (B) escalation to the sponsor is premature; the PM hasn't exhausted options at their level. (C) writing a briefing based on assumed concerns is a cold message that often reinforces the resistance by missing the actual issue.
