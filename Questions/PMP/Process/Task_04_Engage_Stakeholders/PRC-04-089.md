# Question

On a predictive water-treatment upgrade, a regional health regulator signed off the design eight months ago and has been silent since; the PM's last assessment placed the regulator at high power, low interest. Detailed construction starts next month and will move several discharge points away from the locations the regulator originally approved. The PM has limited time before the team mobilises. What is the most appropriate first action?

## Options

- **A**: Update the regulator's position on the engagement assessment matrix and adjust the planned approach to reflect the changed discharge points.
- **B**: Meet the regulator now to walk through the revised discharge points and understand what they will need before construction proceeds.
- **C**: Confirm with the project's permitting adviser whether the original sign-off still covers the revised discharge points before approaching the regulator.
- **D**: Issue the revised discharge design to the regulator as a formal notification and continue mobilising unless they raise an objection.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The project is about to change the very thing this high-power stakeholder approved, and the gap that matters is whether their current engagement still holds once they see the revised points; a direct conversation to walk them through the change and learn what they now need is the engagement move that de-risks mobilisation (B). (A) is necessary housekeeping, but updating the matrix is recording a judgement about the regulator without yet talking to them — it documents the change rather than engaging on it. (C) is a sound governance check and may run in parallel, but confirming the permit's legal coverage is a compliance question; even if the old sign-off technically stands, an unbriefed regulator discovering an unannounced change is the engagement risk, so verifying paperwork first does not address it. (D) treats a statutory body's silence as consent and proceeds on a one-way alert, exactly the passive stance that lets a powerful approver's concern surface too late.
