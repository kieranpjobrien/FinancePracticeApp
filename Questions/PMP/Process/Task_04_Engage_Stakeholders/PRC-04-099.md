# Question

On a predictive ERP migration, the executive sponsor has begun giving two developers direct instructions to prioritise a report he wants for an upcoming board presentation. The work is pulling them off committed milestone deliverables, and the developers feel unable to refuse him. The PM discovers the diversion at the weekly stand-up. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the sponsor to understand the board need behind the requests and agree how such priorities will flow through the project going forward.
- **B**: Capture the sponsor's report as a change request so its effort and schedule impact on the milestone become visible and can be decided properly.
- **C**: Re-confirm with the team which work is committed this period and ask them to route any new requests to the PM, so the milestone is protected while the situation is sorted out.
- **D**: Raise the milestone risk created by the diversion with the project board so the delivery impact is on the record.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The sponsor is a powerful stakeholder acting on a real but unmanaged need, and the bypassing will recur until that need is understood and given a proper route; engaging him directly to learn the board pressure and agree how priorities will be handled addresses the cause and protects both the relationship and the plan (A). (B) is the correct mechanism for the schedule impact and will likely follow, but raising a change request before speaking to the sponsor processes his request as paperwork without addressing why he went around governance. (C) sensibly shields the team and the milestone, yet redirecting the developers before the PM has talked to the sponsor can place them between two authorities and treats a senior stakeholder's legitimate need as mere interference. (D) puts the impact on record but escalates over the very stakeholder the PM should engage first, which is disproportionate before a direct conversation. The margin is between engaging the sponsor first (A) and the defensible instincts to formalise, contain, or escalate first (B, C, D).
