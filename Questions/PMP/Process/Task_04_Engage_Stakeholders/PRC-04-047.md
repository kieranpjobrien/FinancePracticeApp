# Question

A new ERP rollout is running into resistance from a division head whose team's work will be reshaped. They've attended every steering meeting but haven't publicly opposed anything. However, their middle managers are quietly slowing adoption. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the division head directly at the next steering meeting about the pattern of behaviour visible in their team.
- **B**: Meet with the division head privately to surface the adoption signal, understand their concerns, and co-design a path forward.
- **C**: Escalate the adoption issue to the sponsor so leadership pressure is applied to the division head's team across the organisation.
- **D**: Run an engagement survey across the division to quantify the adoption concerns before any direct conversation takes place.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Passive resistance is common on large change; PMI and change-management practice prescribes surfacing the substance privately first. (B) respects the relationship and addresses root cause. (A) public confrontation is unlikely to yield the honest conversation needed. (C) escalating without a direct conversation skips a level and damages trust. (D) a survey delays the conversation and generates heat without the direct engagement that moves the stakeholder.
