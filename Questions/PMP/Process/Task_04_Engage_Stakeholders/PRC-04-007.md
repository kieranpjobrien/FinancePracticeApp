# Question

Late in execution, the PM discovers a regulatory stakeholder who should have been consulted during design was never added to the stakeholder register. Their input now could cause rework. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the regulator immediately, share current design, and seek their input before further build progresses.
- **B**: Complete the build as planned and address regulator concerns only if they arise during launch.
- **C**: Update the stakeholder register and communications plan, then continue as scheduled.
- **D**: Escalate the oversight to the sponsor so they can inform the regulator formally.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Regulators cannot be handled defensively — an un-consulted regulator is a compliance risk that grows the longer it's left. (A) opens the channel immediately, trading short-term schedule impact for avoided rework and compliance risk. (B) betting that the regulator won't raise concerns is the opposite of PMI's compliance-first stance. (C) documentation without engagement doesn't solve the actual gap. (D) escalating before the PM has done the direct outreach wastes the sponsor's time on something the PM can handle.
