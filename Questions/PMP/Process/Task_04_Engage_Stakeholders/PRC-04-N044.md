# Question

A newly formed agile team is delivering a customer-portal redesign. Several business stakeholders keep emailing the PM individually to ask how the current sprint is progressing, consuming a lot of the PM's time on repetitive status updates. The work itself is on track. What is the most effective way to engage these stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Set up a shared information radiator — a visible board showing sprint goal, progress, and impediments — and point stakeholders to it.
- **B**: Compile a weekly written status report covering progress against the sprint goal and circulate it to all the business stakeholders.
- **C**: Invite the business stakeholders to attend the sprint review so they can see the increment and ask questions there.
- **D**: Ask each stakeholder to route progress questions through their single nominated representative to reduce the volume of enquiries.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Agile favours transparency through information radiators that give stakeholders self-service, real-time visibility and cut the PM out of the manual relay role, which is exactly the problem here. (B) a weekly report is a pull-to-push improvement but still snapshots stale information and keeps the PM producing it. (C) the sprint review is essential but happens only at sprint end, so it does not answer mid-sprint questions. (D) funnelling through a representative reduces volume but adds a bottleneck and still relies on someone pushing updates rather than radiating them.
