# Question

Three sprints into a retail inventory project, the team discovers a technical constraint that will delay a feature a major store-operations stakeholder is expecting at the next release. The stakeholder has been openly enthusiastic and has told their own leadership the feature is coming. The next sprint review is two weeks away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Inform the stakeholder now, explaining the constraint, the revised forecast, and the options being explored.
- **B**: Wait until the sprint review to present the delay alongside the working increment so the message is balanced.
- **C**: Have the team investigate workarounds first and only raise the delay with the stakeholder if no workaround is found.
- **D**: Reprioritise the backlog to deliver as much of the feature as technically possible by the original date before saying anything.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Agile engagement runs on transparency and trust, and a stakeholder who has publicly committed to a date needs early, honest notice so they can manage their own leadership — disclosing now with the forecast and options (A) respects that. (B) holding the news until the review delays it purely for presentational convenience and risks the stakeholder being blindsided in front of others. (C) exploring workarounds is sensible in parallel, but withholding the delay contingent on the outcome leaves the stakeholder exposed if the workaround fails. (D) salvaging scope before communicating again defers the conversation and could lock in a partial solution the stakeholder may not even want.
