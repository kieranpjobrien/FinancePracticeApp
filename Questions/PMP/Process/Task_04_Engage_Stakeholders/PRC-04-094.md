# Question

Midway through a predictive rail-signalling project, a corporate reorganisation moves the project under a new operating division whose head now holds the budget. She was not previously involved, has not been briefed, and has begun questioning the project's priority in passing remarks to her staff. The PM learns of this informally and the former sponsor has now stepped away. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the new division head to understand her expectations for the project and agree how she wants to be engaged going forward.
- **B**: Prepare a concise briefing pack — charter, business case, current status — and send it to her so she can form a view from the facts.
- **C**: Update the stakeholder register and communications plan to reflect the new division head and the departed sponsor before engaging her.
- **D**: Confirm with the PMO whether the project's funding and mandate transfer intact under the reorganisation before approaching the new head.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A newly powerful stakeholder has appeared mid-flight and is already forming a negative view with no input from the project; the move that both informs her and surfaces her expectations is a direct conversation about what she needs and how she wants to be engaged (A). (B) is a reasonable supporting step, but sending documents to a senior leader already sceptical assumes a pack will shape her opinion as well as a dialogue would — and risks her reading it through the doubt she has already voiced. (C) is correct housekeeping that must happen, yet reclassifying the register before speaking to her means encoding guesses about her interest; the artefact should follow the conversation, not precede it. (D) is a sensible governance check and may run in parallel, but confirming the mandate transfer is a continuity question, not an engagement one, and does nothing about a budget-holder disparaging the project today. The margin is between engaging her first (A) and the defensible instincts to brief, document, or verify continuity first (B, C, D).
