# Question

Three iterations into an agile insurance-claims modernisation effort, a newly appointed chief operating officer takes ownership of the affected business unit. She was not part of the original stakeholder analysis and has views on the product direction that have not yet been heard. Which actions should the project manager take to engage her effectively? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Add the COO to the stakeholder register and assess her interest, influence, and engagement needs.
- **B**: Arrange an early conversation to understand her expectations and concerns about the product direction.
- **C**: Reassure her that the backlog was agreed with her predecessor and is therefore settled for the current release.
- **D**: Route all future communication about the product through the existing product owner to avoid confusing the chain.
- **E**: Pause delivery until the COO has formally ratified the original product vision and roadmap.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

A new senior stakeholder entering mid-flight requires both updating the stakeholder analysis and proactively surfacing her expectations, which is exactly what continuous engagement and re-analysis demand. (A) and (B) together identify her and open the dialogue. (C) telling her the direction is settled dismisses a high-authority stakeholder's input and invites a later, harder reset. (D) funnelling everything through the product owner without first engaging her directly risks her feeling shut out and is not the PM's call to make for her. (E) pausing all delivery is disproportionate; engagement can run in parallel with continued work, and demanding formal ratification before any conversation is heavy-handed.
