# Question

A community stakeholder group is actively lobbying against a planned infrastructure project. Their concerns are substantive though the project has all required approvals. The sponsor wants to proceed while ignoring the group to avoid "legitimising" their position. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the community group in structured dialogue, acknowledge concerns, and explore mitigations that might reduce substantive opposition.
- **B**: Proceed with delivery per sponsor direction and document the opposition as a reputational risk in the project risk register entry.
- **C**: Meet the community group in public forums to demonstrate the project's willingness to explain decisions to affected community members.
- **D**: Commission independent expert reviews of the concerns so the response can be grounded in objective assessments rather than dialogue.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Ignoring substantive community opposition is the exact opposite of proactive stakeholder engagement; PMI-aligned practice seeks mitigations even without formal power. (A) does that. (B) logging a risk while ignoring the substance is process theatre. (C) public-only engagement isn't dialogue and often escalates conflict. (D) expert reviews have their place but don't replace direct engagement with affected stakeholders.
