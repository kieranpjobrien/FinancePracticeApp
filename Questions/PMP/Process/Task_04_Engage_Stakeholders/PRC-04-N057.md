# Question

On a public-sector grants-management system, the operations director wants the system optimised for processing speed, while the audit and risk director wants extensive validation and approval checkpoints that will slow processing. Both have signed off the business case, both have escalation power, and their requirements are now in direct tension as detailed design begins. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Bring both directors together to clarify the underlying business objectives their conflicting requirements are trying to serve.
- **B**: Analyse the trade-off between processing speed and control rigour and recommend a balanced design option to both directors.
- **C**: Escalate the conflict to the sponsor, since two equally powerful stakeholders cannot resolve it between themselves.
- **D**: Sequence the requirements so the validation checkpoints are added in a later phase after the fast processing path is delivered.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Conflicting positions usually mask reconcilable interests, so the first step is to expose the underlying objectives — fraud prevention versus citizen service levels, say — because a solution often satisfies both once the real goals are visible (A). (B) proposing a balanced design before you understand each side's actual objective risks recommending a compromise that genuinely serves neither. (C) escalating immediately abdicates the PM's facilitation role and removes the directors' ownership of a decision they are best placed to shape. (D) phasing the requirements is a possible outcome, but committing to it now pre-empts the conversation and may breach the audit director's non-negotiable controls.
