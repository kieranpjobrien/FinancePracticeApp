# Question

A project manager on a hybrid e-commerce replatforming initiative needs the head of fulfilment to move from "neutral" to "supportive", because the warehouse process changes depend on her teams. She is data-driven, time-poor, and sceptical of IT projects after a failed rollout last year that her teams blame for months of disruption. Which three actions are most likely to move her toward the desired engagement level? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Share concise, metrics-led updates showing how the changes cut fulfilment errors and handling time, in a format she can scan in minutes.
- **B**: Invite her to a sprint review where her own teams demonstrate an early increment, so she sees working functionality rather than promises.
- **C**: Acknowledge the previous failed rollout directly and walk her through the specific differences in approach that address what went wrong.
- **D**: Schedule a recurring weekly one-hour steering meeting with her to review the full project plan and discuss progress in depth.
- **E**: Have the sponsor formally direct her to support the initiative, citing the dependency on her warehouse teams.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Moving a neutral, sceptical, data-driven stakeholder means tailoring engagement to her motivations, her time constraints, and the specific trust deficit. (A) matches her data orientation and limited time; (B) gives tangible evidence that counters scepticism and lets her teams see ownership; (C) confronts the real barrier — the prior failure — head-on. (D) is a defensible engagement mechanism in general, but a standing weekly hour reviewing the whole plan is exactly the time burden a time-poor stakeholder resents, and depth of process does not address her trust concern. (E) may secure compliance on paper, but a sponsor mandate imposed on an already-sceptical stakeholder typically converts quiet doubt into resentment and does not produce genuine support. The trap is that (D) and (E) both look like reasonable engagement, but neither fits this stakeholder's profile.
