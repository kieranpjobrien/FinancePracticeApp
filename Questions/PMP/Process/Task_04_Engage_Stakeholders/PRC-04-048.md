# Question

Two business unit leaders disagree sharply on a feature's design. Each holds legitimate authority over different parts of the affected process. Their teams are pausing work awaiting resolution. The PM has tried separate conversations and the positions have not moved. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the design decision to the sponsor and ask for a ruling that both business unit leaders are required to accept.
- **B**: Propose a joint working session with both leaders and their key team members to converge on agreed decision criteria.
- **C**: Choose the design position that better fits the original business case and proceed, accepting one leader will be unhappy.
- **D**: Pause the feature and move the team to other backlog items until both business unit leaders agree the design direction.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When separate conversations haven't moved positions, PMI-aligned practice brings the parties together with criteria rather than escalating or stalling. (B) facilitates genuine convergence. (A) escalating a decision both leaders have authority over damages both relationships. (C) unilateral PM decision over business-authority-holders oversteps role. (D) pausing work without a resolution path burns value and leaves the conflict untouched.
