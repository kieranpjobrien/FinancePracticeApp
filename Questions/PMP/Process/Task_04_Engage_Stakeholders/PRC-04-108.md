# Question

A hybrid municipal smart-metering programme must engage a wide external community: residents who will have meters installed, a consumer-rights group sceptical of data privacy, the electricity distributor, and the city council that owns the mandate. Early engagement has been ad hoc, and the consumer-rights group has begun framing the programme negatively in local media. The PM is setting the engagement strategy for delivery. Which actions are most appropriate to strengthen engagement with this external community? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Engage the consumer-rights group directly and early to understand their privacy concerns and incorporate reasonable assurances into the programme.
- **B**: Differentiate the engagement approach by group, recognising residents, the rights group, the distributor, and the council have distinct interests and information needs.
- **C**: Set up a two-way channel for residents to ask questions and report installation issues, and feed recurring themes back into delivery.
- **D**: Route programme messaging on the privacy issue through the city council, so communications carry the authority of the mandate owner.
- **E**: Concentrate engagement on the council and distributor, who hold the mandate and the network, and address residents mainly through them.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

For a diverse, partly hostile external community, the strong moves are engaging the sceptical group directly and early to address the real concern (A), tailoring the approach to each group's distinct interests (B), and giving residents a genuine two-way channel whose feedback shapes delivery (C). (D) is tempting because borrowing the council's authority sounds credible, but funnelling privacy messaging through the mandate owner makes the programme look like it is hiding behind authority rather than answering the concern, and it sidelines the direct engagement with the rights group that actually defuses the issue. (E) reflects a real power-interest instinct — focus on the powerful — but engaging residents only via the council and distributor treats the people whose homes are affected as a downstream audience, forfeiting the direct two-way trust-building that (C) provides and that hostile coverage makes essential.
