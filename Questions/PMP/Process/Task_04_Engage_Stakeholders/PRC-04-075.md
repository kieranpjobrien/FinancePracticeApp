# Question

A distributed agile team delivers fortnightly, but a key business stakeholder repeatedly emails the project manager asking what the team is working on and when specific features will land. The team already maintains a live Kanban board and a forecast burnup in its tooling. The stakeholder has access but says she "never has time to dig through the tool." How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Set up a short, regular cadence with the stakeholder to walk the board and forecast together and confirm the format and frequency that works for her.
- **B**: Export the board and forecast to a PDF each fortnight and email it, so the information reaches the stakeholder without her opening the tool.
- **C**: Continue answering her emails individually, since responsive replies maintain the relationship and the information she needs is specific each time.
- **D**: Ask her manager to encourage her to use the existing radiators, since the transparency she wants is already published and accessible.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The information exists but is not reaching the stakeholder in a form she will consume, so the engaging response is to meet her where she is and agree a pull-and-push cadence that fits her, rather than blaming the tool or her. (A) confirms a working format and builds a relationship, which sustains engagement. (B) a PDF push may help but assumes the format works without checking, and a static export loses the live context she may need to interpret. (C) ad hoc email replies are reactive, do not scale, and leave her dependent on the PM. (D) pushing her to use a tool she has already rejected ignores the real barrier and is likely to fail.
