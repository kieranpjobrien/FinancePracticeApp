# Question

On a predictive ERP consolidation, the finance controller of an acquired subsidiary keeps requesting "just one more analysis" before approving the data-migration design, delaying a milestone by six weeks. Her team privately tells the project manager she fears the new system will reveal that her local processes were non-compliant. The CFO, who has formal authority over the sign-off, asks the PM why migration approval is stalled. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the controller privately to surface her underlying concern and explore how the migration design can proceed without exposing her to blame for past practices.
- **B**: Give the CFO a factual account of the repeated analysis requests and the six-week impact, and ask the CFO how to proceed on the sign-off.
- **C**: Walk the controller through the migration's data-handling design in detail to demonstrate that it will not surface or judge historical process compliance.
- **D**: Run a workshop with all subsidiary controllers to agree standard migration acceptance criteria, so approval no longer rests on one controller's open-ended discretion.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The analysis requests are a symptom; the driver is fear of exposure, and engaging the stakeholder means addressing that interest directly with the person who holds it. (A) does this and can unlock the approval by decoupling the migration from blame for past practices. (B) is a legitimate, honest update to the authority who asked — and the PM does owe the CFO transparency — but reporting the impasse before attempting to resolve it with the controller invites a top-down ruling that hardens her resistance and the acquired entity's. (C) sounds reassuring and may form part of the conversation, but a technical walkthrough answers a question she has not openly asked and, without first acknowledging the fear, lands as the project talking past her concern. (D) is a sound structural improvement for future approvals, yet it bypasses the specific relationship blocking this milestone and could be read as engineering around her. The fine margin is that the unblocking lever is the controller's private concern, which only (A) engages.
