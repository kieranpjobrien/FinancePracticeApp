# Question

On an agile platform project, the assigned product owner is a junior analyst who frequently cannot answer the team's questions about priority and acceptance, instead deferring to a busy senior business manager who is rarely available. Velocity is suffering from blocked clarifications. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the sponsor to ensure the product owner role is held by someone empowered to make timely product decisions, or that the senior manager delegates real authority to the analyst.
- **B**: Have the team make reasonable assumptions on blocked items and proceed, validating later when the senior manager is available.
- **C**: Escalate each blocked clarification to the senior business manager directly so decisions are made by the person with authority.
- **D**: Ask the junior analyst to compile questions into a weekly batch for the senior manager to answer in one sitting.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The core engagement failure is that the empowered decision-maker is not effectively present in the product owner role, and the durable fix is to ensure that role is filled with real authority, whether by empowering the analyst or replacing the arrangement. (A) addresses the structural cause through the sponsor. (B) assumption-driven work creates rework risk and treats the symptom. (C) routing every clarification to the senior manager bypasses the product owner and depends on a person who is rarely available, so it will not scale. (D) weekly batching slows the fast feedback agile relies on and still hinges on a disengaged authority, leaving the team blocked between cycles.
