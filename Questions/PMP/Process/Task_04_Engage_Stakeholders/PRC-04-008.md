# Question

The sponsor asks the PM to stop providing status reports to a specific mid-level stakeholder the sponsor finds 'too demanding'. The stakeholder has legitimate project interest. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Comply with the sponsor's request and cut off status reports to the stakeholder.
- **B**: Discuss with the sponsor the stakeholder's legitimate interest and propose an adjusted reporting format.
- **C**: Continue sending reports to the stakeholder but exclude the sponsor from knowing about it.
- **D**: Ask the stakeholder to take their request directly to the sponsor to resolve it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM owes the sponsor respect and also owes legitimate stakeholders appropriate communication. (B) handles the tension through conversation and compromise — possibly a reduced report rather than none. (A) blindly cutting off a legitimate stakeholder may damage delivery. (C) deceiving the sponsor violates PMI ethics. (D) pushing the stakeholder at the sponsor escalates a conflict the PM should help resolve.
