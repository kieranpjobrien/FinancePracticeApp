# Question

On a predictive airport baggage-system upgrade, the airline tenants' representative — whose acceptance is needed at go-live — was engaged and responsive early in planning. Lately the PM has noticed the representative opening status updates without replying and declining the last two optional design walkthroughs without explanation. Everything is nominally on track. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reach out to the representative directly to check where they stand and surface any concerns before they harden.
- **B**: Treat the change as a possible bandwidth issue and make the walkthroughs lighter and easier to join before reading anything into it.
- **C**: Note the reduced responsiveness in the stakeholder engagement assessment and watch the next update or two for a clearer signal before acting.
- **D**: Use the next scheduled status meeting to ask the representative, in front of the group, whether they are comfortable with the current direction.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A previously engaged stakeholder whose acceptance gates go-live has quietly pulled back from optional contact — a weak but real signal of cooling engagement that is cheapest to address early; a direct, low-key approach to check their view and surface any concern is the proportionate move (A). (B) is a charitable reading that may even be right, but redesigning the walkthroughs on an assumed cause acts before asking the person and could miss a substantive concern entirely. (C) recording the drift and waiting for a clearer signal is defensible monitoring, yet on a go-live-critical acceptance it risks letting discontent set before the PM engages — the cost of asking now is low. (D) raising it in front of the group puts the representative on the spot and is likely to get a polite deflection rather than the candour a private approach invites. The margin is between probing directly now (A) and the plausible instincts to reinterpret, watch, or ask publicly (B, C, D).
