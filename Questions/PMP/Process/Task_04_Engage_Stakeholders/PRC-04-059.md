# Question

A business-wide process change is being delivered by a PM's programme. Adoption surveys show pockets of strong uptake in some regions and poor uptake in others. The PM has limited direct access to the poorly-performing regions. What should the PM propose?

## Options

- **A**: Ask regional leaders in poorly-performing areas to mandate the process change within their regions so adoption becomes compulsory.
- **B**: Identify and mobilise peer change champions from the high-adoption regions to partner with teams in the lower-adoption areas.
- **C**: Schedule direct PM visits to the poorly-performing regions regardless of travel and time costs, since ownership sits with the PM.
- **D**: Send more detailed communications to the poorly-performing regions explaining the benefits and expectations of the new process fully.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Change-champion networks are the evidence-based PMI-aligned approach for scaling adoption, especially where direct access is limited. (B) leverages successful peers. (A) mandate-first approaches generate compliance without commitment and often rebound. (C) direct PM visits don't scale and miss peer-credibility effects. (D) more communication rarely fixes adoption where the gap is trust, not information.
