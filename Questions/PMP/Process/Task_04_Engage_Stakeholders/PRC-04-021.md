# Question

A new senior stakeholder joins the organisation and replaces an incumbent who was supportive of the project. The new stakeholder has publicly questioned similar initiatives in the past. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Send them the full archive of project documentation and wait for them to respond with questions.
- **B**: Plan the project as if their support is guaranteed since the sponsor has already approved the project.
- **C**: Ask the outgoing stakeholder to vouch for the project in their handover notes to the successor.
- **D**: Request an early introductory meeting, listen to their perspective, and tailor the engagement plan accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A new senior stakeholder is a change event for the project. (D) is proactive engagement — listen before telling, then tailor. (A) dumping documentation is a one-way information push that doesn't address relationship or concerns. (B) assuming support is guaranteed ignores a well-known signal of potential opposition. (C) relying on the outgoing stakeholder's endorsement is weak; the new stakeholder will form their own view quickly.
