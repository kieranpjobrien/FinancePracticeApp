# Question

A hybrid programme has workstream leads across four countries. A key leader has been disengaging — fewer contributions in meetings, late on commitments, no direct concerns raised. The PM suspects personal or contextual pressures but doesn't know. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Confront the disengagement pattern directly in the next programme meeting so concerns are aired with the leadership group.
- **B**: Ask peer workstream leads whether they know anything about what's going on with the disengaged leader behind the scenes.
- **C**: Arrange a one-to-one in a relaxed format, open with observations without judgement, and invite the leader to share context privately.
- **D**: Escalate the engagement concern to the leader's line manager so the right HR support can be brought in if needed.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI-aligned practice on disengagement treats it as a person first — private, curious, non-judgmental. (C) models that. (A) public confrontation is unlikely to surface honest context and risks humiliation. (B) gossip-style fact-finding is disrespectful and unreliable. (D) escalating to a line manager without a private conversation with the person first is a trust breach.
