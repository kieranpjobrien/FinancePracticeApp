# Question

A PM completes a power-interest grid and places a senior executive in the "manage closely" quadrant. During the first interaction, the executive says they're too busy to engage regularly and will rely on the PM's judgement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept their preference, reduce the engagement frequency, and focus attention on other high-power stakeholders who want closer involvement.
- **B**: Agree a minimum engagement — short monthly decisions-and-risks brief in a format the executive prefers — and confirm escalation triggers.
- **C**: Escalate the mismatch to the sponsor and ask them to reinforce the importance of active executive engagement for this project.
- **D**: Continue engaging at the "manage closely" cadence because the power-interest analysis indicates this level of engagement is needed.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Engagement intensity should be tailored to a stakeholder's actual decision role, not held rigidly to a matrix output. (B) matches engagement to their capacity and information needs. (A) passive acceptance abandons legitimate engagement needs. (C) escalating over a workable accommodation damages trust unnecessarily. (D) holding the matrix-indicated cadence against stated preference is disrespectful and unproductive.
