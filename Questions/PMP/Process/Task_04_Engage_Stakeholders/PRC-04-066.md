# Question

A PM learns another project in the portfolio is planning a release that will land in the same month as hers, targeting the same user population with competing change messages. Neither sponsor is aware of the collision. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with her own release as scheduled, since scheduling collisions are the portfolio manager's issue to resolve rather than project PMs.
- **B**: Delay her release by a month to avoid the collision with the other project's planned release in the same timeframe.
- **C**: Raise the collision with the other PM and agree an approach before jointly briefing both sponsors with a recommended sequencing.
- **D**: Coordinate directly with communications and change teams to align messages, ignoring the scheduling dimension of the collision entirely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cross-project user-population collisions need coordinated PM-to-PM engagement before engaging sponsors; PMI-aligned practice is to propose, not just raise. (C) handles both correctly. (A) leaving coordination to the portfolio manager when peer-to-peer resolution is available is passive. (B) unilateral delay without coordination is wasteful and may not be needed. (D) message coordination without addressing the scheduling collision treats only half the problem.
