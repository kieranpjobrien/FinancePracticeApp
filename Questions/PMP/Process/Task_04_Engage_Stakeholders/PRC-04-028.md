# Question

The PM discovers that a stakeholder's power in the organisation has grown significantly following a reorganisation. The stakeholder was previously classified as 'keep informed'. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the classification unchanged to avoid appearing politically reactive to the reorganisation.
- **B**: Upgrade the classification automatically to 'manage closely' without further analysis or review.
- **C**: Reassess the stakeholder's current power and interest, update the register, and adjust engagement accordingly.
- **D**: Raise the change at the next steering committee to seek formal approval for the reclassification.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Stakeholder classification is dynamic; organisational changes should trigger reassessment. (C) follows the PMI process. (A) ignoring a material power shift leads to misaligned engagement. (B) auto-upgrading to 'manage closely' skips the check on whether interest actually warrants it (power alone isn't enough). (D) reclassifying is PM-level tailoring and doesn't need steering committee approval.
