# Question

On an agile product development effort, two business sponsors each fund part of the work and each push the product owner for their priorities to lead the backlog. The product owner keeps reordering the backlog after each sponsor conversation, and the team is now whipsawed between half-finished items. The project manager is asked to help. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a joint session with both sponsors and the product owner to agree a shared prioritisation basis and a single decision path for backlog ordering.
- **B**: Advise the product owner to lock the backlog for the upcoming iterations so the team can finish committed work without further reordering.
- **C**: Recommend escalating to the sponsors' common executive to rule on whose priorities take precedence going forward.
- **D**: Propose splitting the team into two streams, one per sponsor, so each sponsor's priorities progress in parallel without contention.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The root problem is two engaged but competing stakeholders lacking a shared, transparent basis for prioritisation, and the engaging fix is to bring them together to align rather than to insulate the team or impose an answer from above. (A) builds shared understanding and a durable decision mechanism, addressing the cause. (B) locking the backlog protects the current iteration but does nothing about the recurring conflict and sidelines legitimate sponsor input. (C) executive escalation is premature before the sponsors have been given a structured chance to align, and a ruling may damage one relationship. (D) splitting the team fragments capacity and ignores that a single product needs one coherent priority order.
