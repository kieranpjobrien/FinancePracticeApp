# Question

A project manager on a hybrid logistics platform is updating the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix. For each stakeholder behaviour observed this quarter, match it to the engagement level it best demonstrates. (Engagement levels follow the standard unaware–resistant–neutral–supportive–leading scale.)

## Items

- **1**: A warehouse union representative attends every steering meeting, actively promotes the change to members, and volunteers to pilot the new picking workflow.
- **2**: A finance controller knows about the project, raises no objections, but offers no help and abstains from decisions affecting it.
- **3**: A regional depot manager has heard nothing of the project and is unaware it will change his depot's operating hours next month.
- **4**: A long-serving dispatcher is aware of the project and openly argues against it in team meetings, encouraging others to delay adopting the new system.

## Matches

- **A**: Unaware
- **B**: Resistant
- **C**: Neutral
- **D**: Leading

## Answer

1-D, 2-C, 3-A, 4-B

## Explanation

The union representative not only supports the change but actively drives it and volunteers to lead the pilot, which is the defining behaviour of "leading" rather than merely "supportive" (1-D). The finance controller is informed and neither helps nor opposes, the textbook "neutral" position (2-C). The depot manager has no knowledge of the project at all, so he is "unaware" despite being materially affected (3-A). The dispatcher knows about the project and actively works against its adoption, which is "resistant" (4-B). The fine margins are between leading and supportive (active promotion and volunteering to pilot tips item 1 past mere support) and between neutral and unaware (the controller's awareness rules out unaware for item 2).
