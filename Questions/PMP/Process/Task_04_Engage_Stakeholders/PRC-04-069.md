# Question

A project is approaching closure and handover to the operations team. The operations manager has attended most steering meetings but their team has had little direct involvement with the solution. Handover is scheduled in three weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the scheduled handover since the operations manager has attended meetings and represents their team adequately at that forum.
- **B**: Ask the operations manager to brief their team prior to handover so they have the context needed to take operational ownership.
- **C**: Extend the handover window by three more weeks to give the operations team more time to familiarise themselves with the solution.
- **D**: Engage the operations team directly now through joint sessions, shadowing and runbook co-creation so they genuinely own the solution before the handover date.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A handover isn't a document transfer; it's a transfer of ownership that requires hands-on engagement with the receiving team. (D) builds that ownership. (A) meeting attendance by the manager isn't team readiness. (B) a cascaded briefing passes context through a filter and isn't hands-on. (C) a longer window without different engagement just delays the same problem.
