# Question

A scaled agile programme delivering a citizen-facing tax portal has a sprawling stakeholder community: ministers who care only about launch dates, a data-protection authority that wants assurance on privacy, call-centre managers who will absorb the support load, and accessibility advocacy groups. The PM is designing the engagement approach for the next two quarters. Which actions would most strengthen stakeholder engagement across this community? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Tailor the message, channel, and frequency for each stakeholder group to what that group actually values and can act on.
- **B**: Establish a feedback route from the call-centre managers and advocacy groups into backlog refinement so their input shapes upcoming work.
- **C**: Lock the engagement plan for the two quarters once it is agreed, so groups receive a stable, predictable cadence they can rely on.
- **D**: Reassess each group's engagement level each quarter and adjust the approach where the gap between current and desired engagement persists.
- **E**: Prioritise engagement effort on the ministers and the data-protection authority first, as the highest-power groups, before extending to the rest.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Strong engagement across a diverse community rests on tailoring communication to each group's interests (A), giving operational and advocacy stakeholders a genuine channel to influence the work rather than merely receive news (B), and periodically reassessing and closing engagement gaps as the programme evolves (D). (C) sounds responsible — predictability has value — but freezing the plan for two quarters contradicts (D): engagement needs shift as a launch nears and a regulator's assurance demands evolve, so a locked cadence becomes stale. (E) is a real and tempting heuristic, but engaging only the high-power groups first sidelines call-centre managers and accessibility advocates whose early input prevents downstream rework and whose neglect carries its own delivery and reputational risk; power-interest should shape *how* each group is engaged, not gate *whether* lower-power groups are engaged at all.
