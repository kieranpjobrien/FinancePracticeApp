# Question

On a factory automation project, a senior plant director rarely opens the weekly written status report and then complains in steering meetings that she is "kept in the dark." Other stakeholders are content with the current report. The PM has a steering meeting with the director next week. What is the best way to address her complaint?

## Options

- **A**: Use the meeting to find out how and how often the director wants to receive updates, and record the agreed approach in the stakeholder register.
- **B**: Offer the director a short verbal briefing from the PM ahead of each steering meeting so she is across the key points.
- **C**: Add the director's personal assistant to the distribution list and flag each report as high importance to improve the odds it is read.
- **D**: Move all stakeholders to a single live status dashboard so the director can check progress whenever she chooses.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The complaint signals a mismatch between how this stakeholder is being communicated with and how she will actually take in information; the engagement move is to learn her preferred channel and cadence and capture it so the approach is deliberately tailored (A). (B) is a reasonable guess at a fix and may well end up being the answer, but offering a specific format before asking assumes the PM already knows her preference — it skips the step of agreeing it with her. (C) chases delivery of an unwanted format rather than changing it; a read receipt does not make the report useful to her. (D) imposes a new channel on everyone, including stakeholders the current report already satisfies, and presumes self-serve suits a director who says she feels left out. The margin is between agreeing her needs with her (A) and jumping to a plausible but unconfirmed format (B).
