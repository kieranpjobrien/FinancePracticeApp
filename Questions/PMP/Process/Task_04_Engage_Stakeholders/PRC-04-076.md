# Question

A pharmaceutical packaging project is in execution. The project manager completes a stakeholder engagement assessment matrix and finds a senior regulatory affairs manager whose desired engagement level is "supportive" but whose current level is assessed as "neutral." This stakeholder must sign off a critical validation gate in six weeks. The PM has competing demands on her time this period. What should the PM prioritise?

## Options

- **A**: Develop and act on a targeted plan to move the regulatory manager from neutral to supportive before the validation gate.
- **B**: Confirm the assessment is accurate by gathering more evidence of the regulatory manager's current stance before investing engagement effort.
- **C**: Prepare a strong validation package so the gate can be passed on technical merit regardless of the regulatory manager's engagement level.
- **D**: Maintain current engagement, since "neutral" is not "resistant" and the manager is likely to approve a compliant submission anyway.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The engagement assessment matrix exists precisely to surface gaps between desired and current engagement so the PM can close them, and a neutral stakeholder controlling an imminent critical gate is a clear priority for a tailored engagement plan. (A) acts on the gap the matrix was built to reveal. (B) re-validating the assessment burns scarce time before the gate when the matrix already gives an actionable read. (C) a strong package helps but does not substitute for the relationship and trust that smooth a sign-off, and a neutral gatekeeper can still stall a technically sound submission. (D) treating neutral as good enough ignores the explicit desired-state gap and gambles the critical gate on assumption.
