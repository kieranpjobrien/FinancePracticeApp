# Question

An agile team building a logistics tracking app has been getting written acceptance from the customer on completed stories but has not held a live demonstration in several iterations. The customer recently expressed surprise at how a feature actually behaves, despite having signed off the story. What should the project manager encourage the team to do?

## Options

- **A**: Reinstate regular working-software demonstrations so the customer experiences increments directly and can give grounded feedback.
- **B**: Add more acceptance criteria detail to each story so written sign-off captures behaviour more precisely.
- **C**: Record screen-capture videos of completed features and attach them to each story for the customer to review.
- **D**: Ask the customer to nominate a technical representative who can interpret the stories more accurately before sign-off.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The gap between written sign-off and the customer's lived expectation is the classic reason agile favours demonstrating working software, which provides the richest, most honest feedback loop. (A) restores that direct engagement. (B) more detailed criteria still rely on the customer correctly imagining behaviour from text, which is what failed. (C) videos are better than text but remain passive and one-way compared with an interactive demo where the customer can probe. (D) a technical proxy may read stories better but distances the actual customer from the product, weakening engagement rather than strengthening it.
