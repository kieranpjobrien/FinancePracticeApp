# Question

A major stakeholder has publicly voiced doubts about the project at a company town hall, citing an outdated risk figure from a status report three months ago. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Post a public correction on the company intranet with the current figures.
- **B**: Raise a formal complaint to the stakeholder's manager about public misstatement.
- **C**: Update the engagement plan to require approval before senior stakeholders speak publicly about the project.
- **D**: Request a one-on-one meeting with the stakeholder to understand their concerns and share current information.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The root cause is likely that the stakeholder hasn't been briefed with current data. (D) addresses that directly and rebuilds the relationship. (A) public correction embarrasses the stakeholder and hardens opposition. (B) complaining to their manager is escalation without first attempting direct resolution. (C) policy-making after the fact doesn't address the current concern and feels punitive.
