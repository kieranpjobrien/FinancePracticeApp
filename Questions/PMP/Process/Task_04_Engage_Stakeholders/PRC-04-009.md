# Question

A stakeholder holds strong opinions that are technically incorrect and is influencing the team. Direct correction in meetings has caused embarrassment and worsened relations. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue correcting the stakeholder in meetings so the team has the right information.
- **B**: Let the stakeholder's opinions stand in meetings to preserve the relationship.
- **C**: Meet the stakeholder privately to walk through the evidence and agree how corrections are handled in future.
- **D**: Ask the team's senior engineer to handle the technical pushback on the PM's behalf.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Technical accuracy matters, but so does the relationship. (C) preserves both by handling the correction privately and creating a process the stakeholder won't feel ambushed by. (A) public correction has already been shown to worsen relations — repeating it is doubling down on a failed approach. (B) letting incorrect information stand is a disservice to the team and ultimately to the stakeholder. (D) offloading the correction to an engineer doesn't repair the underlying relationship and may create a new conflict.
