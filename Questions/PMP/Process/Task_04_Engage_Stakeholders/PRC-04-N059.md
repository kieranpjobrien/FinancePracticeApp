# Question

Halfway through a manufacturing-plant automation project, the PM senses that overall stakeholder engagement has weakened: attendance at reviews is down, feedback is thinner, and two previously supportive managers now seem indifferent. The PM wants to diagnose and respond before it affects delivery. Which actions are appropriate? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Reassess current engagement levels against the desired levels in the engagement assessment matrix to locate the specific gaps.
- **B**: Hold one-to-one conversations with the managers whose engagement has dropped to understand what has changed for them.
- **C**: Review whether the engagement approaches in the plan are still appropriate given how the project and stakeholders have evolved.
- **D**: Mandate attendance at all project reviews and report absentees to their line managers to restore participation.
- **E**: Reduce the frequency of stakeholder communications to avoid overloading people who already seem disengaged.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Diagnosing waning engagement means measuring the gap (reassess current vs desired levels, A), finding the cause directly (one-to-one conversations, B), and checking the plan still fits a changed reality (review the approaches, C) — a coherent observe-then-adjust loop. (D) compelling attendance treats the symptom coercively and damages goodwill, often deepening disengagement. (E) cutting communication assumes overload is the cause without evidence and may further isolate stakeholders; if anything, the diagnosis in (B) should drive whether frequency goes up, down, or changes channel.
