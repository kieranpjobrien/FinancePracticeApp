# Question

A hybrid project is rolling out a new warehouse management system across twelve distribution centres. The site manager at the largest centre has been openly resistant in meetings, arguing the system will slow his team during peak season. His centre's adoption is critical to the rollout's success metrics. What is the most effective engagement approach?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange a one-to-one with the site manager to understand his peak-season concerns and explore how the rollout schedule and configuration could accommodate his operation.
- **B**: Present benchmark data from early-adopter centres showing productivity gains, to demonstrate objectively that his concerns are not borne out elsewhere.
- **C**: Ask the operations director to reinforce that adoption is mandatory and that the site manager is expected to support the corporate rollout.
- **D**: Schedule his centre last in the sequence so he can observe other sites succeed before his own go-live reduces his resistance.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A resistant stakeholder whose buy-in is critical needs his specific concerns surfaced and addressed, because resistance rooted in a genuine operational worry is best converted through dialogue and accommodation rather than data, authority, or avoidance. (A) moves him along the engagement spectrum by listening and adapting, which is the PM's core engagement tool. (B) data may be dismissed as not reflecting his peak-season context and can feel like being argued with. (C) coercion through the director may force compliance but not commitment, and can harden resistance. (D) resequencing defers the conflict without resolving the underlying concern and gambles on social proof working.
