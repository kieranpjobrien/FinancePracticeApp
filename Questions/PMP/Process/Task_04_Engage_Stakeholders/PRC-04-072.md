# Question

A project manager is two weeks into initiating a hospital records digitisation project. A clinical lead mentions in passing that the night-shift nursing staff will be heavy users of the new system but have not been consulted and are not listed anywhere in the project documentation. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the night-shift nursing staff to the stakeholder register and assess their interest, influence, and engagement needs.
- **B**: Note the gap and raise it at the next steering committee so leadership can decide whether the group needs formal inclusion.
- **C**: Ask the clinical lead to act as the proxy voice for night-shift staff, since the lead already understands their working patterns.
- **D**: Defer engaging this group until the requirements phase, when their input on system use will be most relevant.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Discovering an unidentified group that will be a primary user is a direct trigger to update the stakeholder register and analyse their needs, because the register is a living artifact maintained throughout the project. (A) captures the group and assesses how to engage them. (B) escalating a routine register update to the steering committee is unnecessary and slow. (C) a proxy may be reasonable later, but it does not replace identifying and assessing the actual stakeholders first. (D) waiting until requirements risks the group being engaged too late to shape decisions that affect them, and identification should not be deferred.
