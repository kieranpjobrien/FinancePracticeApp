# Question

On a retail e-commerce replatforming project, the marketing lead wants a flexible, frequently changing promotions engine, while the IT security lead wants tightly controlled, infrequent changes to reduce attack surface. Both are influential, both escalate to the same director, and their disagreement is stalling a key design decision. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session where both leads articulate the underlying needs behind their positions and jointly explore options that address security and agility.
- **B**: Document both positions and the impasse, then ask the shared director to make the design call so the schedule is not held up further.
- **C**: Adopt the security lead's position, since reducing risk to the platform should take precedence over marketing's convenience.
- **D**: Adopt the marketing lead's position, since the project's business case is built on promotional agility driving revenue.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The leads have stated positions, but the engaging path is to move from positions to underlying interests and look for an option that serves both, because security and agility are not inherently mutually exclusive. (A) facilitates that interest-based exploration and is the PM's role in stakeholder conflict. (B) escalating before the parties have genuinely tried to reconcile abdicates the PM's facilitation role and may impose a worse compromise. (C) and (D) each pick a winner on the PM's own judgement of priority, which damages one relationship and forecloses a possible integrated solution the experts might design together.
