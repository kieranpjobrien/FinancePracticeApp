# Question

A project manager taking over a stalled hybrid programme finds the stakeholder register lists names, job titles, contact details, and a one-line note on each stakeholder's role in the project. Engagement has been faltering, and the PM has limited time to enrich the register before the next phase. Which additions would most improve its value for tailoring engagement in the coming phases? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Each stakeholder's assessed level of power and interest in the project.
- **B**: The current versus desired engagement level for each stakeholder.
- **C**: The department and reporting line each stakeholder sits within on the organisation chart.
- **D**: The communication needs and preferred channel for each stakeholder.
- **E**: The risks and issues currently owned by each stakeholder across the programme.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Tailoring engagement turns on knowing who matters and why (power/interest, A), the gap between where each stakeholder is and where you need them (current vs desired engagement, B), and how to reach each one (communication needs and preferred channel, D) — these three feed directly into an engagement strategy. (C) the department and reporting line is useful organisational context and can inform influence paths, but on its own it tells you nothing about a stakeholder's attitude or the engagement gap to close, so it ranks below the three that do. (E) the risks and issues a stakeholder owns belong in the risk and issue registers; cross-referencing them is sound governance but it describes their workload, not how to engage them, and duplicating that data here adds maintenance burden without shaping the engagement approach.
