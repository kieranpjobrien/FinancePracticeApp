# Question

A stakeholder has told the PM they've "lost trust" in project reporting after a previous PM over-promised and under-delivered. The current PM inherited the project three months ago. Status reports have been accurate but the stakeholder remains skeptical. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue accurate reporting and let consistency over time rebuild the stakeholder's trust in the project's reporting integrity.
- **B**: Invite the stakeholder into delivery reviews to see progress directly rather than relying only on the written reports.
- **C**: Add independent verification from QA or the PMO to reported metrics so the stakeholder has a trusted second source.
- **D**: Meet with the stakeholder to acknowledge the history, share what's changed, and co-design what trustworthy reporting looks like.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Rebuilding trust requires explicit acknowledgement and co-designed new commitments, not just performing better in silence. (D) does the acknowledgement and the joint design. (A) time alone rebuilds trust slowly and may never fully close the gap. (B) witnessing progress helps but doesn't restore confidence in reporting as a channel. (C) third-party verification is useful but doesn't address the relationship or the stakeholder's role in defining "trustworthy".
