# Question

An agile team is rolling out a new claims-processing tool to replace a legacy system that a long-serving operations supervisor built and still champions. The supervisor attends sprint reviews and praises the work; two analysts have separately told the PM that afterwards she tells her team the old system is safer and to keep using it. Adoption metrics are flat. The product owner manages the supervisor's day-to-day input and is unaware of the side conversations. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Speak with the supervisor directly to understand her concerns and what would let her back the tool with her team.
- **B**: Brief the product owner, who owns the relationship, so they can raise the adoption gap with the supervisor through the channel already in place.
- **C**: Bring the contradiction into the open at the next sprint review by reviewing the flat adoption data with the group present.
- **D**: Validate the analysts' accounts by quietly checking actual usage logs before deciding whether the supervisor's behaviour is really the cause.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The flat adoption traces to an influential stakeholder's unaddressed concern, and the most direct route to changing her behaviour is to engage her yourself and find her conditions for genuine support (A); a candid, private conversation owns the issue rather than routing around it. (B) is tempting because the product owner holds the formal relationship, but handing off a delicate engagement problem the PM has the information to address can dilute it and delay the conversation that matters — and the PM, not the PO, is accountable for adoption here. (D) reflects sound caution about acting on second-hand reports, yet usage logs already show flat adoption; further evidence-gathering before any dialogue mostly defers engagement. (C) airing the gap publicly is likely to humiliate a long-serving stakeholder and harden her resistance. The margin is between engaging directly now (A) and the defensible instinct to delegate or verify first (B, D).
