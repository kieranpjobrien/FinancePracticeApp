# Question

A globally distributed agile team is delivering a payments platform with stakeholders across three continents and several time zones. The product owner is in one region, key compliance stakeholders in another, and the team split across two. Engagement is uneven: some stakeholders feel out of the loop and decisions are being revisited. Which actions would most improve stakeholder engagement across this distributed setting? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Establish a shared digital workspace where backlog, decisions, and increments are visible asynchronously to all stakeholders regardless of time zone.
- **B**: Rotate the timing of key ceremonies so the inconvenience of off-hours meetings is shared rather than always falling on the same region.
- **C**: Record all decisions in a single source of truth but restrict edit access to the product owner to keep it authoritative.
- **D**: Schedule all live ceremonies during the product owner's working hours so the most critical stakeholder is always present.
- **E**: Reduce the number of stakeholders involved in decisions to those in the product owner's time zone to speed agreement.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Distributed engagement depends on asynchronous transparency so no one is disadvantaged by time zone (A), and on equitable scheduling so the burden of inconvenient hours rotates rather than always penalising the same group (B) — both build inclusion and reduce the "out of the loop" effect driving decision rework. (D) optimising solely around the PO's hours entrenches the very imbalance that is excluding others. (E) shrinking the decision group to one time zone disenfranchises legitimate stakeholders such as compliance and invites later reversals. (C) confuses authoritative records with restricted access; a single source of truth should be widely visible, and locking edits to one person is a side issue that does not address uneven engagement.
