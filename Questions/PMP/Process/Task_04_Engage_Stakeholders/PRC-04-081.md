# Question

A project manager is recording stakeholder positions in the engagement assessment matrix. For each stakeholder behaviour described, match it to the engagement level it best represents.

## Items

- **1**: A department head actively promotes the project to peers and volunteers her team's time to pilot the new process.
- **2**: A finance controller is aware the project exists and what it aims to deliver, but neither helps nor hinders it.
- **3**: A regional manager is unaware of the project, its purpose, and its potential impact on his operation.

## Matches

- **A**: Unaware
- **B**: Neutral
- **C**: Leading

## Answer

1-C, 2-B, 3-A

## Explanation

The standard engagement levels run unaware, resistant, neutral, supportive, and leading, distinguished by both awareness and disposition toward the project. The department head who promotes the project and mobilises resources is "leading," the most engaged level (1-C). The finance controller who knows the project but neither aids nor obstructs sits at "neutral" (2-B). The regional manager who does not know the project exists is "unaware" (3-A). Confusing neutral with unaware is the common error: a neutral stakeholder is informed but uncommitted, whereas an unaware stakeholder lacks the knowledge entirely, which calls for very different engagement actions.
