# Question

A wind-farm construction project faces growing concern from a nearby rural community about noise, traffic, and visual impact. A small but organised group has begun lobbying the local council, and a public consultation is required before the next consent. The project manager wants to engage the community constructively before positions harden. Which actions best support effective engagement? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Hold open community sessions to listen to concerns, explain mitigations, and identify issues the project can address.
- **B**: Establish a single point of contact and a transparent channel for the community to raise and track concerns through delivery.
- **C**: Delay any community contact until the consent is approved, to avoid giving the lobby group a platform.
- **D**: Issue a press release emphasising the regional economic benefits to shift public sentiment in the project's favour.
- **E**: Direct all community queries to the council, since the consent process is the proper forum for objections.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

Constructive engagement with an affected community depends on genuine two-way dialogue and a reliable, transparent channel that lets the project hear and respond to concerns before they escalate. (A) opens that dialogue and (B) sustains it through delivery. (C) delaying contact concedes the narrative to the lobby group and signals avoidance, hardening opposition. (D) a benefits-focused press release is one-way persuasion that ignores the actual concerns and can read as spin. (E) deflecting to the council abdicates the project's own stakeholder relationship and leaves grievances unmanaged in the forum most likely to threaten consent.
