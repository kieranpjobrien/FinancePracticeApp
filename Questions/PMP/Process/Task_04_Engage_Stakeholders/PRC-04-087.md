# Question

A globally distributed software project holds its only stakeholder review at a time that suits the headquarters timezone. Stakeholders in two other regions either join at unsociable hours or skip it and read the minutes. Those regions are starting to feel their input does not shape decisions. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Rotate the review time across regions or add a second session so all stakeholder groups can participate live on fair terms.
- **B**: Improve the minutes with more detail and a clear decision log so absent regions stay fully informed of outcomes.
- **C**: Ask each region to nominate a representative in the headquarters timezone to attend on the wider group's behalf.
- **D**: Record the session and circulate the recording so stakeholders in other regions can watch at a convenient time.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The problem is exclusion from the live decision-making forum, not merely access to information, so the engaging fix is to share the inconvenience fairly and let every region participate in the conversation. (A) rotating or adding sessions restores genuine inclusion. (B) richer minutes still deliver decisions after the fact and reinforce that some regions only observe. (C) a proxy distances the actual stakeholders and can dilute or misrepresent their input. (D) a recording is passive and one-way, doing nothing to let those regions influence decisions as they are made.
