# Question

A hybrid programme runs an agile delivery team inside an organisation with a traditional, stage-gate PMO. The PMO director demands fixed scope, dates, and cost locked at each gate, while the business product owner expects scope to flex sprint by sprint based on emerging value. Both are powerful, both are unhappy, and the team is caught between conflicting expectations. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Facilitate a working session with the PMO director and product owner to agree how agile delivery will be governed and reported within the stage-gate framework.
- **B**: Adopt the PMO's stage-gate controls for reporting while preserving sprint-level flexibility for the team internally.
- **C**: Educate the PMO director on agile principles so they understand why fixed scope at each gate is incompatible with the chosen approach.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to decide whether the programme will follow agile or stage-gate governance and direct both parties accordingly.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The conflict is a genuine governance mismatch between two legitimate, powerful stakeholders, and the durable resolution is a jointly agreed model — for example fixing budget and dates while flexing scope, with gate reviews fed by sprint outcomes — which only (A) builds with their ownership. (B) quietly reconciling the two in your reporting may look pragmatic but commits to an approach neither party has agreed and invites a credibility crisis when they discover it. (C) one-sided education frames the PMO as the problem and breeds defensiveness. (D) forcing a binary sponsor ruling discards the hybrid middle ground that usually serves both, and is a fallback only if facilitation genuinely fails.
