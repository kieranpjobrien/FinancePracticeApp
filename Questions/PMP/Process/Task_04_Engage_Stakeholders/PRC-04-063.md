# Question

A senior stakeholder assessed as "high power, high interest" has provided minimal input for two months despite being invited to every relevant meeting and review. The PM has been accepting this as busy-executive behaviour. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue inviting the stakeholder to meetings and reviews, and accept their pattern as a reflection of their genuine capacity.
- **B**: Reduce the formal cadence for this stakeholder and rely on occasional summary updates since their active engagement appears limited.
- **C**: Meet with the stakeholder to check whether the engagement approach fits their working style and adjust if it doesn't suit.
- **D**: Assume tacit approval on decisions made while the stakeholder was silent, since they chose not to raise concerns in meetings.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Silence from a high-power stakeholder is a signal, not a free pass; PMI-aligned engagement asks whether the channel is serving them. (C) finds out. (A) passive continuation risks a late, loud reaction from a disengaged stakeholder. (B) reducing cadence unilaterally assumes the cause without checking. (D) treating silence as approval is a trap that blows up at governance decisions.
