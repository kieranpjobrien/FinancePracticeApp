# Question

A business-process redesign project will change roles for 200 unionised staff. The sponsor asks the PM to minimise union involvement "until we have something concrete to share". The PM knows the enterprise agreement requires early consultation for role changes. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Discuss the consultation requirement with the sponsor, explain the enterprise agreement obligation, and plan consultation into the project timeline.
- **B**: Proceed per the sponsor's direction and rely on HR to handle consultation when the design is concrete and ready for review.
- **C**: Escalate directly to HR and legal so they can bring the sponsor into line with the consultation requirement formally and quickly.
- **D**: Follow the sponsor's direction while logging a compliance concern in the risk register for later visibility if questions arise.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Enterprise-agreement consultation is a legal obligation; the PM's first move is to engage the sponsor with the requirement, not comply with a non-compliant direction. (A) does this properly. (B) delaying consultation breaches the agreement. (C) going around the sponsor without first engaging them damages trust. (D) logging compliance concerns while breaching the requirement is ethics-bypass theatre.
