# Question

An agile team building an insurance-quoting engine is blocked twice a sprint because the product owner, who also holds a demanding operational role, is frequently unavailable to clarify acceptance criteria. Stories carry over and the team's confidence in commitments is dropping. The PM has raised the time pressure with the PO before, with no lasting change. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the PO's line manager to formally protect a defined portion of the PO's time for the project.
- **B**: Work with the PO and sponsor to clarify the PO role's expected commitment and, if it cannot be met, secure a dedicated or proxy product owner.
- **C**: Ask the team to draft their own acceptance criteria when the PO is unavailable and confirm them retrospectively.
- **D**: Move clarifications to a single scheduled weekly slot with the PO to batch the team's questions efficiently.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A repeatedly unavailable PO is a structural staffing problem, not a scheduling glitch, so the fix is to confront the role's feasibility with the sponsor and resolve the capacity gap — dedicate the role or appoint a proper proxy (B). (A) escalating to the line manager may carve out hours but treats it as a calendar issue and skips the sponsor's accountability for the role's design. (C) the team writing criteria the PO later rubber-stamps erodes the PO's ownership of value and invites rework. (D) a weekly batch slot is incompatible with agile's need for just-in-time clarification and would stall stories mid-sprint.
