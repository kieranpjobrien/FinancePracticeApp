# Question

A hybrid digital-transformation programme has a steering committee that meets quarterly, but several committee members complain they learn of major scope and budget decisions only after they have been made by the programme's working group. They feel reduced to rubber-stamping. The project manager wants to restore meaningful committee engagement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Establish a lightweight interim touchpoint and a clear decision-rights agreement so the committee is consulted on major decisions before they are finalised.
- **B**: Increase the depth of the quarterly pack so members have complete information to review decisions already taken.
- **C**: Move the steering committee to monthly meetings so decisions are reviewed closer to when they are made.
- **D**: Invite committee members to observe the working group's sessions so they can follow how decisions are formed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The committee's complaint is about timing and decision rights, not information volume, so the engaging fix is to involve them before decisions are locked and to clarify what they actually decide versus what the working group decides. (A) addresses both the cadence gap and the ambiguity of authority. (B) a thicker pack still presents fait accompli decisions and worsens the rubber-stamp feeling. (C) monthly meetings narrow the gap but do not guarantee involvement before decisions are made, and may overload a governance body. (D) observing the working group adds burden without granting the decision involvement they are asking for.
