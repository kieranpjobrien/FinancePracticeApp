# Question

On a hybrid retail point-of-sale rollout, a powerful regional operations director was told informally during early conversations that self-service kiosks would be included. The kiosks were later removed from scope through an approved change that funded a mandatory tax-compliance feature, but the director was never told. She is now publicly calling the project "watered down" and withholding her stores from the pilot. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Meet the director to explain the change and why it was made, and rebuild a shared understanding of what the project will deliver.
- **B**: Send her the approved change request and updated scope statement so she can see the kiosks were removed properly and with sign-off.
- **C**: Find out how the original commitment was made and never communicated, so the conversation with her is grounded and the gap does not recur.
- **D**: Put a proposal to the change board to reinstate the kiosks in a later release, then take that to her to regain her cooperation.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The harm is an expectation gap created by a communication failure: she was promised something, never told it changed, and is reacting to being blindsided rather than to the decision itself; the first move is a direct conversation to explain the change and re-establish a shared understanding of the deliverables (A). (B) is technically accurate but cold — sending a signed change request to an aggrieved senior stakeholder defends the paperwork while ignoring the relationship that actually needs repair. (C) is sensible due diligence and worth doing, but reconstructing the internal failure before speaking to her delays the engagement she is waiting on; she does not need the post-mortem, she needs the conversation. (D) jumps to reopening a closed, funded decision to buy cooperation before her position is even understood, which can undercut the mandatory compliance feature. The margin is between repairing the relationship directly first (A) and defending, investigating, or bargaining first (B, C, D).
