# Question

Three activities on the critical path can be crashed. Activity M: crash 2 days for $4,000. Activity N: crash 3 days for $9,000. Activity P: crash 1 day for $1,500. You need to crash exactly 4 days at minimum cost. What is the optimal combination?

## Options

- **A**: Crash P by 1 day and M by 2 days and N by 1 day, totalling $8,500, combining the cheapest available options.
- **B**: Crash N by 3 days and P by 1 day, totalling $10,500, using the longest compression available from one activity.
- **C**: Crash M by 2 days and N by 2 days, totalling $10,000, splitting the crash between two middle-cost activities.
- **D**: Crash P by 1 day and N by 3 days, totalling $10,500, because N's daily rate is lower than M's daily rate.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cost per day: P = $1,500/day, M = $2,000/day, N = $3,000/day. Crashing cheapest first: P(1 day, $1,500) + M(2 days, $4,000) + N(1 day, $3,000) = 4 days, $8,500. (B) at $10,500 is more expensive. (C) at $10,000 skips the cheapest per-day option (P). (D) confuses N's total with per-day cost; N at $3,000/day is the most expensive, not the cheapest.
