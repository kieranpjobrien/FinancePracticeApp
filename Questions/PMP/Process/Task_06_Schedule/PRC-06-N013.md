# Question

A sponsor asks, "What is the probability we finish on or before 30 September?" The deterministic critical path lands on 28 September. Reviewing the network, the project manager notes that two other chains feed the same final milestone and both finish within a day of the critical path, and that several activities across all three chains have been estimated with three-point ranges that are wide and skewed. The project manager wants to give the sponsor a defensible probability rather than a single date. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply PERT to the activities along the deterministic critical path to get an expected duration and standard deviation, then read the probability of 30 September off the normal curve.
- **B**: Build optimistic, most likely, and pessimistic end-to-end schedules and tell the sponsor which of the three finishes on or before 30 September.
- **C**: Report the 28 September date together with the schedule reserve already held, and state confidence that the reserve covers the margin to 30 September.
- **D**: Run a Monte Carlo simulation sampling the duration distributions across the whole network and report the share of runs finishing on or before 30 September.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The detail that matters is that two other chains land within a day of the critical path: under variation any of the three can finish last, so a probability built on the deterministic critical path alone will be too optimistic. (D) Monte Carlo samples the duration distributions across all three chains and captures the merge bias at the shared milestone, producing a genuine probability for the target date. (A) is the tempting answer because PERT does yield a probability from a normal curve, but applying it only to the deterministic critical path ignores the near-critical chains that frequently become the longest under variation, so it understates the merge risk the scenario is built around. (B) three discrete end-to-end schedules give a crude range, not a probability, and the all-pessimistic case is itself highly improbable. (C) a date plus reserve states a single outcome and a judgement, not a calculated confidence level. When several chains converge with little margin between them, only whole-network sampling gives a defensible figure.
