# Question

Activity D has finish-to-start dependencies on Activities A, B, and C — all three must finish before D can begin. Activity A finished two days early, Activity B finished on its planned date, and Activity C finished five days late. By how much is the start of Activity D affected?

## Options

- **A**: Activity D is delayed by 5 days because, with finish-to-start logic, the latest predecessor finish governs the start.
- **B**: Activity D starts on its planned date because Activity B finished on plan and most predecessors were on or ahead of schedule.
- **C**: Activity D can start two days early because Activity A's early finish is passed forward to the successor.
- **D**: Activity D's delay cannot be determined without knowing the float on each predecessor path.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When an activity has multiple finish-to-start predecessors, its earliest possible start is the latest finish among them. With C running five days late, D is delayed by exactly five days. (A) is correct. (B) ignores that D depends on C as well as B. (C) confuses one predecessor's early finish with the latest predecessor's finish. (D) overcomplicates: regardless of float on A or B, D cannot start until C finishes.
