# Question

A Scrum team has delivered the following velocities over the last four sprints: 32, 28, 35, and 30 story points. The remaining product backlog is 150 story points. How should the project manager forecast the completion to stakeholders?

## Options

- **A**: Five sprints, because the average velocity rounds neatly to 30 story points per sprint.
- **B**: Approximately five sprints, expressed as a range of four to six sprints to reflect velocity variability across the recent history.
- **C**: Six sprints, by dividing 150 by the lowest historical velocity (28) to give a conservative date.
- **D**: Four sprints, by dividing 150 by the highest historical velocity (35) to give an optimistic stretch goal.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's empirical forecasting guidance is to use historical velocity but to communicate the result as a range that reflects observed variation. Average velocity is 31.25, so 150 / 31.25 ~ 4.8 sprints; given the 28-35 range a 4-6 sprint forecast is honest. (B) does that. (A) uses the average as a precise number with no expression of uncertainty. (C) and (D) each anchor on a single extreme of the velocity series and miscommunicate the central forecast.
