# Question

A hybrid infrastructure programme runs the software workstream in two-week sprints while the hardware workstream follows a predictive schedule with fixed installation milestones. The "Network hardware live" milestone — a hard dependency for the software team's integration testing — has just slipped three weeks. Roughly a third of the remaining software backlog is integration-dependent; the rest is not. The hardware lead is separately assessing whether the milestone can be recovered. What is the project manager's best next step for the software workstream?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the product owner to re-sequence the backlog so the affected sprints pull forward integration-independent stories, deferring the integration-dependent work until the milestone lands.
- **B**: Hold the software team's sprint plan as-is and wait for the hardware lead's recovery assessment, so the team replans only once against a confirmed milestone date rather than twice.
- **C**: Move the integration-dependent stories into a hardening sprint scheduled for after the new milestone date, and run the intervening sprints on whatever stories the team selects.
- **D**: Stand up a hardware emulator for the integration-dependent stories so testing proceeds on the original dates independent of the physical milestone.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The slip is known now and roughly two-thirds of the backlog is unaffected, so the highest-value move is to keep the team delivering by re-ordering toward dependency-free stories while integration work waits — the agile workstream's core strength in a hybrid programme. (A) acts immediately on what is certain without betting on the hardware outcome. (B) is defensible reasoning about replanning churn, but idling or freezing a productive team for up to three weeks on the chance of a single replan sacrifices real delivery for tidiness. (C) defers integration work appropriately but parks the intervening sprints on unprioritised "whatever" stories, abandoning value-based ordering the PO should still drive. (D) may be worthwhile, yet an emulator is a non-trivial build whose cost and fidelity are unverified, and it is a parallel investment rather than the immediate step to keep the team productive.
