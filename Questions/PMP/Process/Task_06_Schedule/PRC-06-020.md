# Question

A vendor deliverable sits on your project's critical path. The vendor will only commit to a date range of "between week 14 and week 18" and refuses a hard date. How should the PM handle this in the project schedule?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule the dependency at week 14, the vendor's optimistic date, to keep commercial pressure on early delivery.
- **B**: Schedule the dependency at week 18, the vendor's pessimistic date, and pad every downstream activity by four weeks.
- **C**: Remove the dependency entirely from the project schedule until the vendor formally commits to a firm delivery date.
- **D**: Schedule against the most likely date, hold contingency in the schedule reserve, and record the risk.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI handles uncertain external dependencies by using a realistic point estimate, holding contingency reserve at the project level (not padding every activity), and capturing the risk explicitly. (D) does all three. (A) plans to the optimistic date with no reserve. (B) double-counts conservatism by both using the pessimistic date and padding downstream work. (C) hides a real dependency from the schedule, which prevents impact analysis.
