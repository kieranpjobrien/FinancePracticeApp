# Question

Three sprints into an eight-sprint release, a Scrum team's velocity has fallen from 30 to 18 points. The cause is clear: the team repeatedly waits on a shared platform team to provision test environments, and the platform team reports to a different manager with its own priorities. The project manager has already raised the dependency at the scrum of scrums for two sprints running with no improvement, and the product owner now fears the release scope is unachievable. What should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate the unresolved provisioning dependency to the managers who own both teams, quantifying the lost velocity and release-scope impact and requesting a committed provisioning service level.
- **B**: Reforecast the release using the depressed 18-point velocity and work with the product owner to cut scope to what that velocity supports.
- **C**: Have the team build and maintain its own disposable test environments so it stops depending on the platform team altogether.
- **D**: Keep the impediment on the scrum-of-scrums log and give the coordination forum another sprint or two to resolve it before taking further action.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The standard coordination forum has already been tried for two sprints without effect, so the right next step is to escalate the organisational impediment to the level that can actually reprioritise the platform team, backed by the quantified impact. (A) escalates at the right time with the right evidence and seeks a durable service-level commitment. (B) may eventually be needed, but baking a fixable external constraint into the forecast and cutting scope before exhausting impediment removal gives away release value prematurely. (C) self-provisioning can be a sound longer-term fix, but standing up and owning environments mid-release is a significant new scope and skill commitment that itself consumes velocity and may breach platform standards, so it is not the immediate move. (D) repeating a forum that has already failed twice just lets the velocity loss compound, which is the passive option the situation has outgrown.
