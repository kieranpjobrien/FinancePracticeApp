# Question

A sprint burndown shows work is not coming down at the expected rate at day 6 of 10. A team member suggests updating the story estimates upward to make the chart look less alarming for the sponsor's sprint review. The PM is the team's scrum master. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to revise the estimates so the chart reflects a more accurate picture of the team's current progress overall.
- **B**: Decline to change estimates, explain that charts are diagnostic signals, and use the review to discuss what they reveal.
- **C**: Hide the burndown from the sponsor this sprint and restore it next sprint after the team has recovered its rhythm again.
- **D**: Revise estimates and hold the burndown constant so stakeholders see a steady decline until the sprint end completes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Burndowns are empirical signals; altering estimates to flatter the chart violates transparency and hides information that should drive adjustment. (B) is correct. (A), (C), and (D) each involve hiding, falsifying, or suppressing information, breaching PMI's transparency principle and undermining empirical process control.
