# Question

A pharmaceutical clinical-trials project uses critical-chain scheduling with a 15-day project buffer and resource-specific feeder buffers. At day 90, the project buffer is 80 percent consumed but only 60 percent of the work is complete. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply fast-tracking across the remaining work to recover the buffer before any downstream activities are further affected.
- **B**: Continue execution and wait until the project buffer is fully consumed before taking any recovery action at all.
- **C**: Replenish the project buffer from management reserve so the project can absorb further variation without disruption.
- **D**: Investigate causes of buffer consumption, then apply targeted responses focused on the constraint driving the drain.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Critical-chain management uses buffer consumption as a signal to investigate causes and act on the constraint, not to apply blanket compression or wait. (D) is the correct approach. (A) is action without diagnosis. (B) ignores a clear amber signal. (C) hides the problem by spending reserve without addressing root cause.
