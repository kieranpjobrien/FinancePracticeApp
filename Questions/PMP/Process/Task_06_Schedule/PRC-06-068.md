# Question

The programme board is about to approve a schedule baseline that the PM believes is unrealistic: key assumptions are untested and one vendor date is unconfirmed. The PM is under pressure from the sponsor to "just get it approved" so execution can start. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the baseline as drafted so execution can begin, and plan to rebaseline quickly as real data emerges in execution.
- **B**: Delay approval until every assumption is tested, even if it delays execution start, to protect baseline credibility long-term.
- **C**: Present the risks and unconfirmed items clearly to the board and recommend conditional approval with defined resolution dates.
- **D**: Recommend the board reject the baseline and send the team back to rebuild the plan from scratch with firmer inputs overall.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A PM's duty to the board is transparent disclosure of risks in the proposed baseline, allowing conditional or phased approval. (C) preserves both pace and integrity. (A) approves a flawed baseline to please the sponsor. (B) halts momentum unnecessarily. (D) overcorrects and discards valid work already done.
