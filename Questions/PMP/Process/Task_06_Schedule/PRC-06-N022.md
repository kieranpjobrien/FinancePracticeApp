# Question

In a programme of five Scrum teams building a single banking platform, Team Payments has a story that needs an API change owned by Team Core. Team Core agrees the change is necessary but has it lower in its own backlog than work it considers more urgent, and both teams answer to their own product owners. The shared release is eight sprints away, so there is time, but Team Payments wants to avoid stalling. As the programme's project manager, what is the most effective action?

## Options

- **A**: Bring the dependency to the scrum of scrums and have it logged and made visible, so the teams' representatives track it to resolution sprint by sprint.
- **B**: Facilitate a conversation between the two product owners to agree, against the shared release goal, which sprint Team Core will deliver the API so Team Payments can plan its dependent story accordingly.
- **C**: Help Team Payments re-sequence its own backlog to pull forward other valuable stories now and schedule the dependent story for after Team Core's API is expected.
- **D**: Have Team Payments build a thin temporary stub for the API so it can demonstrate its story this sprint, replacing the stub once Team Core delivers the real change.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The dependency is a sequencing trade-off between two backlogs owned by two product owners, so the durable fix is to get those product owners to agree, against the shared release goal, a sprint in which Team Core will deliver the API, which then lets Team Payments plan reliably. (B) puts the decision with the people who own prioritisation and produces a committed sequencing both teams plan around. (A) raising and tracking the dependency at the scrum of scrums is necessary and correct, but visibility and a log do not by themselves resolve the priority conflict; the product owners still have to agree the sequencing, so it is a supporting step rather than the effective one. (C) re-sequencing Team Payments' own backlog keeps that team productive and is wise, but on its own it leaves the API delivery date unagreed and merely defers the same collision. (D) a stub can unblock a demo, but with eight sprints in hand, building and later ripping out throwaway integration code is avoidable rework that does not address when the real API arrives.
