# Question

The published schedule shows a 20% schedule reserve held at the end of the project. A senior stakeholder asks why you cannot just bring the delivery date forward by 20% by removing the reserve. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to remove the reserve and commit to the earlier date because schedule reserve represents avoidable delay in the published plan.
- **B**: Decline and explain that the reserve is needed but provide no further detail because reserve management is an internal matter for the PM.
- **C**: Reduce the reserve from 20% to 10% as a compromise so the stakeholder gets at least some of the time saving they have asked for today.
- **D**: Explain that the reserve sizes the risk identified in the register and that removing it would make the project finish probability-weighted later, not earlier.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI sizes schedule reserve to the residual risk in the schedule, and reserves are visible to stakeholders so they understand what protects the date. (D) explains the link between reserve and risk and reframes the conversation accurately. (A) commits to a date the project cannot probabilistically meet. (B) refuses to engage with a legitimate stakeholder question. (C) splits the difference without analysis and leaves the project under-protected.
