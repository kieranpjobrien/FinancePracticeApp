# Question

You inherit a project mid-execution. The schedule shows on-track delivery, but several team members tell you in one-on-ones that the dates are unrealistic and they have always been "playing catch-up." What should the new project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Trust the inherited schedule because it was approved before you arrived and the current variance reports all show the project is on track to deliver.
- **B**: Inform the sponsor immediately that the schedule is unrealistic and that the project is now at serious risk of slipping against the commitment date.
- **C**: Reset the team's expectations by reminding them that the approved schedule is the current commitment regardless of personal opinions about the estimates.
- **D**: Validate the schedule with the team by reviewing assumptions and estimates, then surface any confirmed gaps to the sponsor with options.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects a new PM to verify before either trusting or reporting alarm. (D) gathers evidence first — comparing inherited estimates with the people doing the work — then takes action with options. (A) trusts without verifying. (B) escalates without first checking whether the team's concern is supported by analysis. (C) silences the most informed source of risk information on the project.
