# Question

A hybrid programme has an agile software team feeding a predictive hardware-integration team. The predictive team must order long-lead components in month three and so needs the parts of the interface specification that affect those components frozen by then; the rest of the interface will keep evolving as the software team learns from early sprints. The project manager must reconcile the two schedules. What is the best approach?

## Options

- **A**: Identify exactly which interface elements drive the long-lead components, baseline only those by month three as the last responsible moment, and let the agile team keep iterating on the remaining elements.
- **B**: Freeze the entire interface specification at month three so the predictive team has full certainty for procurement, and have the agile team adapt its later sprints to the frozen spec.
- **C**: Keep the whole interface fluid and ask the predictive team to place the long-lead orders against the current best-guess interface, re-ordering later if the design changes.
- **D**: Split the long-lead components into two orders, placing half against today's interface and deferring the other half, so the agile team gets more time before anything is fixed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Hybrid interfaces are managed by deferring commitment to the last responsible moment and freezing only what the constrained workstream actually needs, here just the elements that drive the long-lead parts. (A) gives procurement the certainty it needs while preserving iteration everywhere else. (B) freezing the whole interface to serve a subset of components discards the value of iteration on elements that have no bearing on the long-lead order. (C) ordering long-lead parts against a best-guess interface invites expensive re-orders and scrapped components precisely on the items where a wrong call is most costly. (D) splitting the order does not change that long-lead parts still need their driving interface frozen; it just leaves half the procurement exposed to the same late-change risk.
