# Question

A team member has finished a critical-path activity three days ahead of schedule. The successor activity has free float and a different team-member assignment. What is the BEST action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the early finish in reserve and report the activity as completed on its planned end date so the project's published schedule is not disrupted.
- **B**: Assign the team member to additional in-scope work so the saved capacity is fully consumed before the successor activity begins.
- **C**: Update the schedule to reflect the early finish and instruct the team member to start a new task from the next phase.
- **D**: Update the schedule to reflect the early finish and notify the successor's owner so they can pull in their start and capture the time saving.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects PMs to capture early finishes on the critical path because they may compress the overall project. (D) does that — update the schedule honestly and offer the time to the next activity owner. (A) hides the saving, which is dishonest reporting. (B) defaults to filling time with new work without considering whether the project benefits more from pulling the successor in. (C) jumps the team member into a new phase without checking dependencies, sequencing, or readiness.
