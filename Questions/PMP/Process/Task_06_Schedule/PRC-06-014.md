# Question

In schedule network analysis, what does "free float" mean for a given activity?

## Options

- **A**: The amount of time the activity can be delayed without delaying the project's overall finish date.
- **B**: The amount of contingency time held in reserve for the activity by the project manager or sponsor.
- **C**: The amount of time the activity can be delayed without delaying the early start of any successor.
- **D**: The amount of time scheduled for non-work events such as breaks, public holidays, or training.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

(C) is the PMI definition of free float — slack against the next activity. (A) describes total float, which is slack against the project finish date. (B) describes contingency reserve, a separate risk-based concept. (D) confuses calendar non-working time with schedule float.
