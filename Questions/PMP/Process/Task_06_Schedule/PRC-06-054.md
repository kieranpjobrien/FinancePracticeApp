# Question

During a hybrid transformation, a key stakeholder informally asks a developer to add a small feature "while you're in there." The developer spends two days on it and it's now in the build. The PM learns of this at the sprint review. The change was never through CCB. What should the PM do first?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the feature since it is already built and the stakeholder is happy with the outcome delivered to the team.
- **B**: Remove the feature from the build and revert the code before the next release to enforce the change-control baseline.
- **C**: Log the change retrospectively, assess impacts, and route it through change control for formal approval or rejection.
- **D**: Coach the developer, informally update the backlog, and continue with the feature in the build without escalating.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Integrated change control applies even to small changes made informally; the right step is to surface them and process them formally. (C) preserves baseline integrity. (A) rewards process circumvention. (B) is premature disposition before analysis. (D) keeps the breach hidden and sets precedent that bypasses are tolerated.
