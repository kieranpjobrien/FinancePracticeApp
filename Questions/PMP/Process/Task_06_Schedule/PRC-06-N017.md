# Question

A predictive manufacturing-line installation is a week behind, and the project manager is looking for sequence overlaps that can recover time. Four finish-to-start links sit on the critical path: (1) electrical inspection comes before walls are closed, as the local building authority signs off concealed wiring before it is covered; (2) the pilot cell is built before the full line, a sequence the engineering team set so it could validate the cell design before committing to the rest; (3) commissioning comes after the incoming supply is energised, which the regional utility schedules and performs; (4) machine bases are grouted before alignment, with the grout left to reach the strength the base supplier's manual states before alignment loads are applied. Which link is the most appropriate candidate to compress by overlapping its two activities?

## Options

- **A**: The inspection-before-wall-closure link, closing walls in already-inspected zones while later zones still await sign-off.
- **B**: The grout-before-alignment link, starting alignment once the grout has had part of the cure time the manual states.
- **C**: The pilot-cell-before-full-line link, starting full-line build before the pilot cell has finished proving out.
- **D**: The commissioning-after-energisation link, commissioning the internally powered sections before the utility energises the incoming supply.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The task is to classify each link as mandatory or discretionary from its substance, because only a discretionary dependency can be re-sequenced without breaching a hard constraint, and each option is framed to look locally overlappable. (C) the pilot-cell-first ordering exists because the team chose to validate the design before committing to the rest; nothing physical, legal, or external forces it, so it is discretionary and the team may legitimately overlap or drop it to recover time. (A) the inspection link is mandatory by code: a building authority signs off concealed wiring before it is covered, so closing any wall before its own sign-off is a compliance breach, and "already-inspected zones" hides that the link itself cannot move. (B) the grout strength requirement is a mandatory engineering constraint; applying alignment loads before the grout reaches the stated strength risks the exact failure the cure prevents. (D) energisation is an external dependency the utility controls; commissioning "internally powered sections" does not remove the dependence on the incoming supply that this link represents. The discretionary link is the only one whose compression violates no constraint.
