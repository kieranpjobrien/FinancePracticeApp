# Question

A hybrid product launch pairs an agile marketing-website team, which can flex scope freely, with a predictive manufacturing line locked to a fixed "first units off the line" date. Manufacturing has confirmed it will hit its date, but a regulatory-label approval that several website product pages depend on is now forecast two weeks after launch. Most of the website (overview, pricing, support, ordering) does not depend on the label. The project manager must hold the overall launch date. What is the best course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the regulator whether a conditional or partial label approval can be issued in time to unblock the dependent pages by launch.
- **B**: Launch the full website behind a feature flag, switching on the label-dependent pages automatically the moment approval is granted two weeks later.
- **C**: Re-sequence the website backlog to ship all label-independent pages at launch and gate only the label-dependent pages behind the approval, releasing them when it lands.
- **D**: Escalate to expedite the regulatory review so the approval is pulled forward to the launch date and nothing has to be deferred.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The agile workstream's flexibility is the lever the project manager actually controls, and the launch can succeed with most of the site live. (C) ships everything that is genuinely ready and defers only the truly blocked pages, protecting the fixed date while respecting the regulatory constraint, entirely within the project's control. (A) seeking conditional approval is worth pursuing in parallel but depends on the regulator's discretion, so the plan cannot be built on it; it is a hopeful enabler, not the decision. (B) a feature flag is a sound delivery mechanism, but flagging the dependent pages still leaves them unpublished at launch, so it is just a technical means to the same deferral as (C) without re-sequencing the backlog; on its own it does not decide what ships. (D) expediting the review again relies on an external party agreeing, and treating the date as recoverable risks the launch if it is not. Acting on the controllable agile scope is the most reliable course.
