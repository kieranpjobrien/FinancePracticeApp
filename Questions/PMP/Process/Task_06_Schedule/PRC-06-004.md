# Question

Which of the following BEST describes "crashing" as a schedule compression technique?

## Options

- **A**: Overlapping activities that were planned to be done in sequence to shorten the overall schedule.
- **B**: Reducing scope or quality on critical-path deliverables so they take less time to complete.
- **C**: Cutting non-critical activities from the schedule to focus the team on critical-path work.
- **D**: Adding resources to critical-path activities to shorten their duration, typically at higher cost.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

(D) is the PMI definition of crashing — buying time with money by adding resources, overtime, or capacity to critical-path activities. (A) describes fast-tracking, the other compression technique. (B) describes scope or quality reduction, which is not a compression technique under PMI definitions. (C) describes scope or schedule pruning, also not crashing.
