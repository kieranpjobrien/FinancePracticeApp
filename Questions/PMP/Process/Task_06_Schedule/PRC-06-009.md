# Question

Your Scrum team has averaged 30 story points per sprint over the last six sprints. The product backlog currently holds 180 story points. The steering committee asks for a project completion date for next month's portfolio review. What should the project manager communicate?

## Options

- **A**: A forecast of approximately six sprints, communicated as a range that reflects velocity variability and possible backlog change.
- **B**: A firm completion date six sprints from now so the portfolio review has a definite milestone to plan against.
- **C**: A completion date eight sprints out to add a safety buffer in case the team's velocity slows.
- **D**: That an agile project cannot commit to a long-range completion date, so no forecast can be given.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's agile guidance is to forecast empirically using velocity but to communicate the result honestly as a forecast with a range, not as a fixed commitment. (A) does that: ~6 sprints with a confidence range. (B) overstates certainty by treating an empirical forecast as a fixed date. (C) hides uncertainty inside a padded number rather than communicating it. (D) misrepresents agile — empirical forecasting is one of agile's strengths.
