# Question

On a hybrid product launch, the agile team will be ready to integrate a third party's API two sprints earlier than planned, but the contract sets a fixed delivery milestone with no acceleration clause, and the vendor confirms it cannot deliver sooner. The team is frustrated and wants the schedule changed. What is the project manager's best move?

## Options

- **A**: Re-order the team's upcoming sprints to pull forward independent, demonstrable backlog items and schedule the API-dependent integration for the contracted delivery date.
- **B**: Build a lightweight stub or mock of the API so the team can develop and test the integration now and swap in the real service when it arrives.
- **C**: Open a conversation with the vendor about an incentive or change to the contract to bring the milestone forward, since both sides may benefit from earlier delivery.
- **D**: Hold the two ready sprints' integration work as planned and reallocate the freed capacity to hardening, test automation, and technical-debt reduction.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

All four are legitimate and a strong PM might combine them; the question is the best primary move for the schedule given a ready, productive team and a firm external constraint. (A) directly maximises delivered value now by re-sequencing the team's own independent work around the fixed dependency — the highest-leverage action fully within the PM's control. (B) is excellent complementary practice and may let integration work start, but a stub does not remove the need for the real API and carries rework risk; it supports (A) rather than replacing the re-sequencing decision. (C) is worth exploring, yet it depends entirely on the vendor's goodwill and a contract change the other party need not accept, so it cannot be relied on as the primary plan. (D) keeps the team usefully occupied but forgoes the available throughput gain from delivering ready backlog value, making it a weaker choice than actively re-sequencing to deliver more now.
