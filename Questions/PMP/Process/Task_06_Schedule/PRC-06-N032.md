# Question

On an agile project, the same developer has reported at three consecutive daily standups that they are "still blocked waiting on test data" from a separate data team that the scrum master has not yet contacted about it. The story is the highest-value item in the sprint and another upcoming story needs the same data. As scrum master, the project manager wants to get this story moving and stop the blocker recurring. What is the best action?

## Options

- **A**: Straight after the standup, engage the data team directly to get this story's data provided, and use that contact to understand and fix the underlying cause so the next story is not blocked the same way.
- **B**: During the standup, work with the team to re-slice the blocked story so the parts that do not need the test data can be built now while the data is chased separately.
- **C**: Ask the product owner to reorder the sprint so the team pulls the next-highest ready story while the data team is given more time to deliver the data on its own schedule.
- **D**: Raise the recurring test-data dependency with the data team's lead to agree a standing provisioning arrangement that covers this story and future ones.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Removing an impediment the team cannot clear itself is a core scrum master responsibility, and a blocker that has recurred across three standups on the highest-value item, with the data team not yet even contacted, calls for acting on both the immediate story and the root cause now. (A) unblocks the current work by going to the source and uses the same contact to address why it keeps happening, covering both the story and the next one. (B) re-slicing wins some partial progress and is a reasonable parallel tactic, but it leaves the actual blocker, the missing data, unresolved for both the current story and the upcoming one, so the impediment still bites. (C) keeps the team busy on other value and is defensible flow management, but it works around the blocker rather than removing it and quietly accepts that the highest-value story stays stuck. (D) agreeing a standing arrangement is the right kind of root-cause fix, but negotiating a future provisioning process does not get this sprint's data moving now and puts the durable arrangement ahead of unblocking the live, highest-value story.
