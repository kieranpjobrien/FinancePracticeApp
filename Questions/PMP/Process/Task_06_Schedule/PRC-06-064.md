# Question

Your Scrum team consistently spills work to the next sprint. Analysis of the last six sprints shows stories enter sprint planning with unclear acceptance criteria and unresolved dependencies. The product owner resists spending time on refinement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce a strict Definition of Ready at sprint planning and block any story that does not meet it from entering the sprint.
- **B**: Coach the team and product owner on backlog refinement value, using spill data as the evidence for adopting the practice.
- **C**: Reduce sprint scope by 20 percent so the team always completes commitments, even when refinement practices remain weak.
- **D**: Introduce a mandatory two-hour refinement ceremony each sprint as a formal agenda item on the Scrum calendar.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Servant leadership uses evidence to coach, not to enforce rules unilaterally; the PO's buy-in is essential for refinement to stick. (B) addresses the root cause collaboratively. (A) imposes a gate without shared understanding. (C) masks the problem by shrinking scope. (D) imposes a ceremony without agreement, which often breeds compliance without quality.
