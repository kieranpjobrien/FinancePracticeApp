# Question

To hit a release date, your test lead proposes skipping the regression suite for low-risk modules on a predictive banking project. The lead believes this saves five days on the critical path. The quality plan requires full regression. The PM has release pressure from the sponsor. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Permit the skip for the low-risk modules only and document the decision as a calculated project-risk trade-off.
- **B**: Submit a formal change to the quality plan, analyse the risk, and route the proposal through change control for decision.
- **C**: Reject the proposal and instruct the test lead to run the full regression suite exactly as the quality plan requires.
- **D**: Escalate the dilemma to the sponsor and let the sponsor decide whether the schedule or quality commitment takes priority.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quality plan deviations are not unilateral PM decisions; they are handled through formal change control with a documented risk analysis. (B) is correct. (A) skips change control "because it's urgent," which violates integrated change control. (C) is valid but premature if a formal change can transparently weigh the trade-off. (D) dumps analysis onto the sponsor without the PM's recommendation.
