# Question

On a predictive plant-upgrade project, the civil works are finishing 10 days early. The next activity, mechanical installation, cannot be accelerated because the lead equipment has a fixed delivery date. A team member suggests the project "just finished a milestone early." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the schedule to show the civil works finish and keep the mechanical start on its planned delivery-dependent date.
- **B**: Communicate the early finish as a gain to the sponsor and hold the planned end date to create a float buffer instead.
- **C**: Reassign the civil works crew to early preparation tasks to get value out of the 10 days of extra capacity available.
- **D**: Request an earlier equipment delivery from the vendor to capture the 10-day gain on the overall project schedule.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Early finish on one activity does not accelerate a successor bound by a fixed delivery date; the correct action is a factual schedule update reflecting actual finish and unchanged successor start. (A) is correct. (B) overstates a gain that does not flow to the end date. (C) risks scope and resource decisions without authorisation. (D) assumes vendor flexibility and is premature without analysis.
