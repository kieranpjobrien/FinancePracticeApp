# Question

Your 12-person Kanban team's cycle-time trend is rising, the cumulative-flow diagram shows a growing "in progress" band, and WIP limits are frequently breached. Delivery is slipping. The product owner wants to "push harder." What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Lower WIP limits and enforce them strictly to reduce work in flight and return cycle time to its earlier level.
- **B**: Raise WIP limits temporarily so the team can clear the backlog of in-flight items and return to normal flow.
- **C**: Add two more people to the team to lift throughput so the team can clear the growing in-progress work steadily.
- **D**: Facilitate a flow analysis with the team and product owner, address bottlenecks, and agree sustainable WIP limits together.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Rising cycle time and growing WIP point to bottleneck and limit discipline issues, best diagnosed and resolved collaboratively by the team. (D) reflects servant-leader facilitation and flow-theory practice. (A) applies medicine without diagnosis. (B) makes the symptom worse. (C) adds people, which typically worsens flow in the short term (Brooks's law).
