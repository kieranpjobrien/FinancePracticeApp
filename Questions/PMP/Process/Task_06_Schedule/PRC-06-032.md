# Question

Your project schedule was developed using rolling-wave planning. The next phase has detailed activities, while subsequent phases hold only milestones and high-level estimates. A senior stakeholder demands a fully detailed schedule for the entire 18-month project today. How should the PM respond?

## Options

- **A**: Produce a fully detailed schedule using assumptions for the unplanned phases so the stakeholder has a complete picture by the deadline.
- **B**: Refuse the request because rolling-wave planning is the agreed methodology and producing detailed schedules would breach it.
- **C**: Explain how rolling-wave planning works, share the detailed near-term schedule and the milestone-level long-term plan, and commit to elaborating each phase as it approaches.
- **D**: Provide the detailed near-term schedule only and decline to share the long-term milestone plan because it is not yet detailed enough.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI's progressive elaboration principle is to plan in detail only what can be reasonably known. (C) educates the stakeholder, gives them what is known, and commits to elaboration — meeting their need without creating false precision. (A) fabricates detail that will not survive contact with reality and will mislead decisions. (B) rejects a legitimate stakeholder need without offering an alternative. (D) withholds information the stakeholder reasonably needs to plan downstream activities.
