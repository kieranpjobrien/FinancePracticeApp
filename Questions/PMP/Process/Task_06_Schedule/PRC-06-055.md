# Question

A Monte Carlo schedule simulation indicates a P80 finish date 18 days later than the baseline date. The sponsor accepts only the P50 date and questions the point of running the simulation at all. The PM wants the contingency recognised. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the P50 date and absorb the risk quietly, hoping favourable variance during execution restores the buffer later.
- **B**: Insist on the P80 date as the commitment and refuse to publish any other date to avoid creating expectation mismatch.
- **C**: Present the P-curve, explain the probability interpretation, and negotiate a confidence level that reflects risk appetite.
- **D**: Run a second simulation with more optimistic inputs so the P80 aligns to the sponsor's preferred commitment date.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Monte Carlo outputs are probabilistic; the PM's role is to educate on the curve so the sponsor's choice is informed by risk, not convenience. (C) is correct. (A) hides risk. (B) is adversarial and ignores sponsor prerogative. (D) is analytical manipulation and breaches reporting integrity.
