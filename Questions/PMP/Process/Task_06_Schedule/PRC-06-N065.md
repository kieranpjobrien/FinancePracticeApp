# Question

A predictive pharmaceutical validation project used rolling-wave planning and carried its later commissioning phase as a summary placeholder of 15 working days in the approved, schedule-baselined plan. The detailed-planning window has now opened. The PM and the commissioning lead have decomposed the work and built a bottom-up schedule of 25 days — 10 of which fall beyond the project's committed completion date. They have also modelled that fast-tracking two sub-phases would recover about 6 of the 10 days, leaving a 4-day overrun at some added integration risk, and the project's schedule reserve could cover those 4 days. The change request to integrated change control is already being drafted. What should it recommend?

## Options

- **A**: That the board authorise the fast-tracking and the use of 4 days of schedule reserve to hold the committed completion date, with the added integration risk logged and owned.
- **B**: That the committed completion date be extended by 10 days to match the bottom-up estimate, since rolling-wave planning expects placeholder durations to be replaced by detail as it emerges.
- **C**: That the board authorise fast-tracking the two sub-phases, and that the residual 4 days be addressed by tightening durations elsewhere on the commissioning path rather than drawing on reserve.
- **D**: That the commissioning lead's bottom-up schedule be re-examined to bring it back toward 15 days before any date change is requested, since a placeholder this far below the detailed figure suggests the decomposition is over-conservative.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The change request is correctly being raised — the issue breaches a committed completion date, so it must go to integrated change control. The substance is *what the CR should recommend*, and the schedule reserve exists precisely to absorb estimating variance of this kind without moving the committed date, so the soundest recommendation pairs the recovery action with a defined draw on reserve and an owned risk (A). (B) is the trap that mistakes progressive elaboration for licence to relax a committed constraint: rolling-wave does expect detail to replace placeholders, but it never authorises a 10-day slip when 6 days are recoverable and reserve covers the rest — it concedes float that doesn't need conceding. (C) keeps the date but refuses the reserve that was set aside for exactly this, instead manufacturing further compression on an already fast-tracked path, stacking risk to avoid using a buffer that is there to be used. (D) inverts the evidence: a validated bottom-up estimate outranks a rough placeholder, so pressing the lead to shrink a defensible schedule back toward the guess is wishful re-estimation, not analysis. The fine margin is between using reserve as designed (A) and either over-conceding the date (B), over-compressing to dodge the buffer (C), or discrediting sound detail (D).
