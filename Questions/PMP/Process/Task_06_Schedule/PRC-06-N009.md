# Question

A Kanban support-and-enhancement team is breaching its service-level expectation: items take far longer end to end than committed. The cumulative flow diagram shows the "in testing" band widening steadily over the past month while every other band stays roughly constant. The team has one part-time tester and several developers. What should the project manager do first to shorten delivery times?

## Options

- **A**: Lower the work-in-progress limit on the development column so fewer items pile up waiting to enter testing.
- **B**: Reduce the overall arrival rate by pulling fewer new requests into the system until the queue drains.
- **C**: Redeploy the developers who have capacity to help test, clearing items out of the testing column.
- **D**: Add a second tester to the team so testing has more throughput.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The widening "in testing" band locates the bottleneck precisely: testing capacity cannot keep up. The fastest relief uses capacity the team already has. (C) swarming idle developers onto testing directly increases throughput at the constrained stage today, the classic response to a visible bottleneck. (A) lowering the development WIP limit stops the queue growing but does not drain the work already stuck in testing, so cycle time stays long. (B) throttling intake also only slows the growth and starves upstream stages without relieving the constraint. (D) adding a tester is a sound medium-term fix but requires hiring or reassignment lead time, so it is not the first action. Subordinating the rest of the system to the constraint and exploiting existing capacity comes first.
