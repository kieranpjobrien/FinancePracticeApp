# Question

A Scrum team's recent velocities are 24, 26 and 25 points. For the upcoming sprint, two of the six developers will each be away for half the sprint, and the team is also expected to spend roughly a day supporting a production release. The product owner wants to know how many points to plan for. As scrum master, what should the project manager guide the team to do?

## Options

- **A**: Have the team forecast this sprint from its actual available capacity, accounting for the leave and release support, and pull in the amount of backlog that capacity supports.
- **B**: Plan to the 25-point average and treat anything not finished because of the leave as carry-over into the next sprint.
- **C**: Plan to the 25-point average but pull in two extra ready stories as a buffer the team can drop if the capacity loss bites.
- **D**: Calculate the exact lost developer-hours, convert them to points using the team's hours-per-point ratio, and set the commitment to that precise figure.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Velocity is an average for typical capacity; with known leave and a planned interruption this sprint has materially less capacity, so the team should forecast from what is genuinely available and pull backlog to suit. (A) sets a realistic, team-owned forecast that preserves predictability. (B) plans to full average knowing capacity is down, manufacturing carry-over and eroding the sprint goal's meaning. (C) over-commits to the average and relies on dropping stories mid-sprint, which is just planned failure dressed as a buffer. (D) treating story points as convertible to exact hours misuses a relative-sizing measure and gives false precision; capacity adjustment is a forecast, not an hour-for-hour calculation.
