# Question

In a scaled agile programme, the payments team has pulled a story into the coming sprint that cannot be integrated or tested until an API the platform team is also building this sprint exists. Both teams have already finished their own sprint planning separately and are confident. The project manager wants to prevent a mid-sprint block. What is the most effective action?

## Options

- **A**: Stand up a recurring scrum-of-scrums so cross-team dependencies like this surface and are coordinated each iteration.
- **B**: Get the two teams to jointly agree the API will be delivered and validated early enough in the sprint that the payments work can consume it, and make that ordering explicit in both plans.
- **C**: Add the dependency to the programme risk register with an owner and a mitigation, and review it at the next programme sync.
- **D**: Have the payments team move its dependent story to the following sprint so it starts only once the API is confirmed done.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The dependency already exists in this sprint, so the most effective move acts on this sprint, not just on future process. (B) sequences the two pieces of work and bakes the ordering into both teams' plans, which is what actually prevents the block now. (A) a scrum-of-scrums is the right standing mechanism and would have caught this earlier, but on its own it surfaces dependencies rather than resolving this committed one; it is the systemic fix, not the immediate one. (C) logging a risk documents the exposure without removing it. (D) deferring the story guarantees no block but forfeits a sprint of value when same-sprint delivery is achievable with light coordination. Concrete cross-team sequencing beats deferral or mere visibility here.
