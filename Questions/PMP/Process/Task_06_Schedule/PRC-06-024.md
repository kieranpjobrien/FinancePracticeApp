# Question

Several team members are split across three concurrent projects, and context-switching is causing slippage on yours and on the others. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the context-switching cost and present the data to resource owners and other PMs to coordinate focus.
- **B**: Insist that the team members be assigned to your project full-time because it has the highest business priority.
- **C**: Adjust your own schedule to account for the multitasking and stop raising the issue across the wider portfolio.
- **D**: Escalate the resource conflict to the sponsor and ask them to reassign team members to a single project each.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to address the root cause with data and to engage peers and resource owners before escalating. (A) does that — measure the impact, then negotiate a coordinated solution. (B) makes a unilateral demand based on a self-serving priority claim and damages relationships with other PMs. (C) accepts the inefficiency and lets the symptom continue. (D) escalates before peer-level resolution has been attempted, which is PMI's default order of operations.
