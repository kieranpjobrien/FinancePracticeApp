# Question

A Kanban support team's lead times are worsening. The cumulative-flow diagram shows the "In review" band widening steadily while every other stage stays thin — items pile up waiting for review. The two senior engineers who do all reviews are also the team's busiest developers. The project manager wants to improve flow. Which actions are sound responses to this bottleneck? (Choose all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Lower the WIP limit on the stages feeding review so upstream work cannot accumulate faster than review can clear it.
- **B**: Have the team agree explicit review-capacity policies — such as reserving daily reviewer time or broadening who can review — to lift the constraint stage's throughput.
- **C**: Raise the WIP limit on the "In review" column so more items can be worked there at once and the visible queue shrinks.
- **D**: Track cycle time per stage and monitor the cumulative-flow diagram so the effect of any change on the bottleneck is measured rather than assumed.
- **E**: Pull the senior engineers off development to focus on clearing the review backlog until the "In review" queue is empty.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

The constraint is review *capacity*, so the sound moves protect and lift that stage while measuring the effect. (A) limiting upstream WIP stops flooding the bottleneck — core Kanban practice that subordinates the rest of the system to the constraint. (B) raising the constraint stage's actual throughput (reserved review time, more qualified reviewers) attacks the root cause. (D) per-stage cycle time and the CFD make the bottleneck and any improvement visible. The two wrong options are plausible, not bad faith: (C) raising the review WIP limit lets more items sit *in progress* at the bottleneck but adds no review capacity — it makes the queue look smaller while lengthening cycle time, the opposite of flow improvement and a common WIP-limit misunderstanding. (E) temporarily clears the queue but starves development, simply moving the bottleneck rather than balancing flow, and the backlog returns the moment they resume coding; it treats the symptom (a full column) instead of sizing review capacity to demand.
