# Question

In project scheduling, what does a "lag" represent?

## Options

- **A**: An acceleration of a successor activity that allows it to begin before the predecessor finishes.
- **B**: A required waiting period or delay imposed between two dependent activities.
- **C**: The amount of time a non-critical activity can slip without affecting the project end date.
- **D**: A schedule baseline that has been formally approved through change control.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

(B) is the PMI definition — a lag inserts mandatory waiting time between two activities (e.g., a 3-day cure time between concrete pour and form removal). (A) describes a lead, the opposite. (C) describes total float. (D) describes the schedule baseline.
