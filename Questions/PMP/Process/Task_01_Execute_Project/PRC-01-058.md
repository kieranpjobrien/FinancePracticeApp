# Question

Two senior engineers on a high-visibility delivery team publicly disagree about the architectural approach during sprint reviews. Delivery is slowing because the rest of the team waits for direction. The disagreement isn't resolving itself. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the sponsor to make an architectural ruling and direct both engineers to accept the decision without further debate.
- **B**: Move one of the two engineers to a different workstream so the remaining one can lead the architectural direction alone.
- **C**: Have the PO decide the architectural path through backlog prioritisation rather than addressing the relationship between them.
- **D**: Facilitate a focused working session with both engineers to surface assumptions, agree decision criteria, and produce a written choice.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI-aligned conflict resolution is collaborate/problem-solve — surface the substance, agree criteria, and converge. (D) addresses root cause and leaves a durable record. (A) escalation avoids developing the team and can entrench positions. (B) forcing/withdrawing removes a capable voice and penalises legitimate technical debate. (C) routing an architectural call through the backlog is the wrong tool and lets the interpersonal issue fester.
