# Question

A finance application team is ready to release at end-of-sprint. Regression testing found twelve cosmetic defects and two functional defects in non-core flows. The PO wants to ship. The QA lead wants to hold. What should the PM suggest the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Ship now with a clear public release note listing the known defects and committed fix dates for each outstanding issue.
- **B**: Hold the release until every identified defect is fully resolved so the team protects the application's quality reputation.
- **C**: Convene PO, QA lead, and a business representative to assess impact per defect and make an evidence-based go/no-go decision.
- **D**: Fix the two functional defects only, ship, and carry the twelve cosmetic items as technical debt into the next planned sprint.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Release decisions with mixed defects are business decisions informed by technical input; PMI-aligned practice is to bring the right voices together rather than picking a side. (C) produces a decision the team can stand behind. (A) decides on behalf of the business without consulting them. (B) zero-defect purism ignores legitimate business trade-offs. (D) makes a technical call that still leaves a business impact question unanswered.
