# Question

A predictive project has just exited the design phase and entered build. A new regulation has been gazetted that affects several requirements signed off two months ago. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the build to the original baseline and address regulatory differences in a remediation phase after go-live.
- **B**: Pause the build, document the regulatory delta as a change request, and route it through the change control board.
- **C**: Update the requirements directly and brief the team in stand-up so build can continue without losing momentum.
- **D**: Escalate the issue to the sponsor and wait for direction before involving the team.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulatory change is a textbook trigger for formal change control — the impact on baselines must be assessed and approved before work continues against potentially obsolete requirements. (B) follows the process. (A) ships a knowingly non-compliant product, the worst option. (C) bypasses change control on a regulatory item and creates audit exposure. (D) is unnecessary escalation when the PM owns running the change process and can present a recommendation to the CCB.
