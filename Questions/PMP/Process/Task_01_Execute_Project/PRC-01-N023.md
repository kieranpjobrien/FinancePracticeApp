# Question

An agritech startup is running a pilot of a soil-sensor dashboard across forty farms, and the central open question for the business is whether farmers will actually change irrigation decisions once they can see the data. The team could spend three more sprints adding forecasting, alerting, and export before any farmer touches the product, or release a basic live-readings view next week. Forecasting is the feature most likely to drive a decision, but only the live-readings view is ready now. What is the project manager's best move?

## Options

- **A**: Release the live-readings view next week and watch whether irrigation behaviour shifts, then let what farmers do steer which of the heavier features to build first.
- **B**: Hold the release until forecasting is ready, since that is the capability most likely to change a decision and a readings-only view may show no signal and discredit the pilot.
- **C**: Release the live-readings view next week but instrument it heavily and run structured interviews, so the first increment is designed to extract the decision signal rather than just expose data.
- **D**: Release the live-readings view to ten farms next week and hold the other thirty until forecasting ships, so the thin and full versions can be compared.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The pilot exists to find out what farmers do, and the only way to get that signal is to put something usable in their hands and observe, then let the result direct the build. (A) ships the ready increment now and treats real behaviour as the input that orders the remaining features, which is the fastest honest path to the answer. (B) is a genuine bet that the thin slice will show nothing, but it gambles three sprints on an assumption the readings view could have tested cheaply, and "no signal" is itself a finding. (C) is attractive and not wrong to instrument, but loading the first release with interviews and analytics adds scope and delay for a signal that basic usage already reveals. (D) splits the pilot to compare versions, yet the question is whether behaviour changes at all, so withholding the ready view from thirty farms slows learning without sharpening it.
