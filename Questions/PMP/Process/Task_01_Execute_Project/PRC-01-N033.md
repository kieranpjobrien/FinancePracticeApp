# Question

A hybrid energy-trading platform project has a sponsor who keeps asking the team to "wait until it's complete" before any release, fearing that a partial product will reflect badly on the business. The team has a robust slice ready that would let traders place and settle basic trades safely, with advanced analytics to follow. Competitors are already live. What is the project manager's best move?

## Options

- **A**: Respect the sponsor's preference and continue building until the full platform is complete before releasing.
- **B**: Show the sponsor how an early, robust slice delivers usable value and competitive position now, with a roadmap for the remaining capability, and seek agreement to release incrementally.
- **C**: Release the slice without the sponsor's agreement, since incremental delivery is the correct approach and the date pressure is real.
- **D**: Build a polished prototype of the full platform to reassure the sponsor, then release everything together later.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Urgency to deliver value depends on the sponsor understanding the case for incremental release, so the right move is to bring them along with evidence, not to wait indefinitely or act unilaterally. (B) reframes a robust slice as real value and competitive advantage and secures agreement to ship. (A) defers all value and cedes ground to live competitors. (C) bypasses the accountable sponsor on a significant business decision, risking trust even if the delivery logic is sound. (D) invests in a non-shippable prototype and still delays actual value.
