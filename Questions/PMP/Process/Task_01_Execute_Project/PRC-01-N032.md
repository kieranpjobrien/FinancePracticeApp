# Question

A project manager is coaching a delivery team on executing with urgency to deliver value. Match each situation the team faces to the most appropriate response.

## Items

- **1**: The team has finished a small, working slice that customers could use, but more features are planned for the epic.
- **2**: Six stories are in progress at once and none are reaching "done".
- **3**: A high-value story is blocked by another team's unfinished dependency, while independent stories are ready.

## Matches

- **A**: Limit work in progress and swarm to drive started items to completion.
- **B**: Release the working slice now and continue layering value afterwards.
- **C**: Deliver the ready independent stories now while actively managing the dependency.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Each response is the disciplined way to keep value flowing under the relevant constraint. A finished, usable slice should be released immediately rather than batched (1-B). Too much started-but-unfinished work calls for WIP limits and swarming to restore flow (2-A). When the top item is externally blocked, urgency is served by shipping the next-best ready value while managing the dependency (3-C). Swapping any pairing would either withhold deliverable value or fail to address the actual constraint.
