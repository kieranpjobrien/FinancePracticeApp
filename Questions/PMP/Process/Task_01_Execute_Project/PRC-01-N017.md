# Question

An aerospace supplier's agile team is two days from the sprint review. The sprint's highest-value story is 80% done but cannot be fully completed and tested in time. The product owner reckons the unfinished 80% could instead be split: a coherent, independently valuable sub-slice of it could be finished and properly tested in the two days, leaving the remainder to carry over. Separately, an unrelated smaller story could also be finished and tested in the time. Capacity allows only one of these to be brought to "done". The product owner is available. What should the project manager facilitate?

## Options

- **A**: Finish and test the coherent sub-slice of the high-value story, since partial delivery of the top-priority item is always worth more than completing a lower-priority one.
- **B**: Finish and test the unrelated smaller story, because a fully independent completed item is cleaner to demonstrate than a slice carved out of a larger one.
- **C**: Have the product owner decide between finishing the tested sub-slice of the high-value story and finishing the unrelated smaller story, based on which delivers more value done this sprint, and carry the rest over with progress visible.
- **D**: Keep the team on the full high-value story to maximise how much of it is progressed, and show that progress at the review even though it will not be done.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Two genuinely done-able options exist and capacity forces a choice, so the value trade-off belongs to the product owner with the facts laid out. (C) frames the real decision — tested sub-slice of the top story versus the unrelated completed story — and lets the value owner weigh them, which is both urgent and correctly placed. (A) pre-empts that decision with a false absolute: a fragment of the highest-priority story is not automatically more valuable than a complete lower-priority one, since a coherent finished item may deliver more usable value than an isolated slice. (B) likewise pre-decides on "cleanliness of demo", an aesthetic, rather than on value. (D) chases maximum progress on the full story but delivers nothing done and shows unfinished work as if it were an increment, breaching the definition of done in a safety-critical domain.
