# Question

A predictive enterprise-resource-planning rollout will take a year to complete fully, but the finance module alone, deliverable in four months, would let the organisation retire an expensive legacy system whose licence renews in five months at a steep cost. The original plan schedules all modules to go live together at the end. The project manager realises the timing creates an opportunity. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Propose a phased go-live that delivers the finance module before the licence renewal, allowing the legacy system to be retired and the renewal cost avoided, then continue with the remaining modules.
- **B**: Keep the single big-bang go-live at year-end as planned, since changing the delivery approach mid-project adds integration complexity.
- **C**: Accelerate the entire ERP rollout to complete all modules before the five-month licence renewal to avoid the cost.
- **D**: Renew the legacy licence for another term so the project can keep its original year-end go-live without time pressure.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Recognising that an early finance-module go-live avoids a steep licence cost is value-driven execution; proposing a phased release captures that benefit while the rest of the rollout proceeds (A). (B) clings to the original plan and forgoes a clear, quantifiable saving. (C) tries to compress a year of work into five months, which is unrealistic and high-risk. (D) spends money to preserve a plan rather than re-sequencing to realise available value, the inferior financial choice.
