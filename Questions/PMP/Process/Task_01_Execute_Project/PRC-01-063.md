# Question

A project with BAC $500,000 has EV $200,000 and AC $250,000 to date. The PM believes remaining work can be completed at the originally planned cost rate. What is the appropriate EAC?

## Options

- **A**: $550,000 — computed as actual cost incurred plus remaining planned value at the original planned rate.
- **B**: $625,000 — computed as the original budget divided by the current cost performance index ratio.
- **C**: $500,000 — the original budget amount, since future work will proceed at the originally planned cost rate.
- **D**: $700,000 — computed using combined cost and schedule indices applied over the remaining planned work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When future performance is expected to match the original plan, EAC = AC + (BAC − EV) = 250,000 + (500,000 − 200,000) = 550,000. (A) applies the atypical-variances formula correctly. (B) is EAC = BAC/CPI, the typical-variances formula — wrong assumption for this question. (C) ignores the sunk overrun entirely. (D) is the combined formula appropriate when schedule pressure affects future cost, not this scenario.
