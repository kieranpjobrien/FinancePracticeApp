# Question

On a predictive water-treatment construction project, a completed pump-station deliverable is ready for client acceptance, but the formal acceptance review is not scheduled for another three weeks because the client's reviewer is overseas. The station could be commissioned and start delivering treated water now if accepted. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait for the scheduled review in three weeks, since the reviewer's sign-off is the agreed gate and the sequence should hold.
- **B**: Commission the station now under a provisional handover and obtain the reviewer's formal acceptance retrospectively when they return.
- **C**: Propose to the client an earlier acceptance review conducted remotely or by a delegated authorised reviewer, so commissioning can proceed against a recorded sign-off.
- **D**: Use the three weeks to complete additional verification and snagging on the station so it is in the strongest possible state for the scheduled review.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The only thing holding back a working asset is reviewer availability, not readiness, so the urgent and disciplined move is to find a legitimate faster route to a recorded acceptance rather than to wait, skip, or merely fill the time. (C) keeps acceptance formal while compressing the delay by three weeks through a remote or delegated review. (A) accepts an avoidable three-week delay on benefits for a scheduling constraint that can be worked around. (B) starts the asset earning but commissions ahead of acceptance, leaving both parties exposed if a defect is later disputed without a recorded sign-off. (D) is reasonable use of the gap and not harmful, but it assumes the delay is fixed and improves an already-complete deliverable instead of attacking the actual cause of the wait.
