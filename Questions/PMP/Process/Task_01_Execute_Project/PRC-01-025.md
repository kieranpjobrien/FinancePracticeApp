# Question

What is the principal advantage of iterative delivery over single-release "big bang" delivery?

## Options

- **A**: Total project effort is reduced because rework is eliminated.
- **B**: The need for documentation, testing, and user training is largely removed.
- **C**: Earlier value realisation and continuous feedback that lets the team correct course before failures compound.
- **D**: Stakeholders are required to engage less because they only review the final release.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Iterative delivery's value is in early outcomes and short feedback loops — that's the whole point. (C) captures it. (A) is wrong; iterative delivery often involves more total effort, just with much higher value capture. (B) is false — testing, training, and documentation still happen, just incrementally. (D) is the opposite of reality; iteration requires more, not less, stakeholder engagement.
