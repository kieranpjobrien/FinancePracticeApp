# Question

A clinical-trial data portal must pass a partner audit in six weeks. The audit-trail and e-signature modules are mandatory for it and are running behind; the reporting dashboard and user-personalisation modules are ahead but are not assessed in this audit. In a regulated system, the mandatory modules must also be formally validated (test evidence documented) before they count as audit-ready. The team has enough capacity to finish the two mandatory modules but not to also validate them and complete the rest. How should the project manager direct the work?

## Options

- **A**: Reprioritise to get all four modules to feature-complete, then run a single validation pass across everything in the final week before the audit.
- **B**: Reprioritise capacity to build *and* formally validate the audit-trail and e-signature modules, deferring personalisation and freezing the dashboard at its current state.
- **C**: Reprioritise to build the two mandatory modules quickly and start their validation, while keeping a small effort polishing the already-ahead dashboard so the portal looks complete to the auditors.
- **D**: Reprioritise onto the two mandatory modules and rely on the development team's own test results as evidence, deferring formal validation documentation until after the audit.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Value here is a *passed* audit, and in a regulated system an unvalidated module is not audit-ready however finished it looks, so capacity must cover both build and validation of exactly the assessed scope. (B) concentrates the team on completing and validating the two mandatory modules and parks everything not under assessment — the disciplined match of effort to what the high-value event actually requires. (A) chases feature-completeness across all four and gambles validation into the last week; validation is the binding constraint, and a single end-of-line pass on twice the scope is the likeliest thing to slip and fail the audit. (C) spends scarce capacity making non-assessed modules look polished, cosmetic value that does nothing for the audit and risks the validation budget for the modules that matter. (D) gets the right modules built but substitutes informal dev test results for the formal validation evidence the audit demands, so the portal would not actually pass.
