# Question

At daily stand-up on a hospitality-booking platform, a developer mentions almost in passing that they have been stuck for a day and a half waiting on a database credential from the infrastructure team, and have been doing low-priority cleanup in the meantime. The credential is needed for the sprint's top-priority story. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Go to the infrastructure team straight after stand-up to get the credential issued now, and keep the top-priority story moving once it lands.
- **B**: Coach the developer to raise and chase their own blockers in future, then have them escalate this one to infrastructure themselves.
- **C**: Reassign the top-priority story to another developer who already has the access needed, so work resumes without waiting on the credential.
- **D**: Have the developer keep going on lower-priority items that are not blocked, and pick the story back up as soon as infrastructure responds.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The sprint's most valuable story has sat idle for a day and a half on a fixable external blocker, so the project manager removes that impediment now rather than delegating, reshuffling, or accepting the delay. (A) goes straight to infrastructure to clear the credential and restores flow on the highest-value work. (B) builds good long-term habits but, used as the response here, leaves the top-priority story blocked while the developer learns to self-escalate. (C) gets work moving but hands off the priority story without clearing the blocker, so the credential gap remains and the new developer's own work is displaced. (D) keeps the developer busy on filler while the most valuable item stays stalled, treating a removable blocker as something to wait out.
