# Question

A team has shipped on time for six consecutive sprints, but usage analytics show fewer than 10% of end users adopt the new features. The product owner insists the roadmap reflects what users said they wanted in interviews 18 months ago. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Pause new feature development for one sprint while a discovery team runs fresh user research to rebuild the roadmap.
- **B**: Continue the current roadmap but invest in user training and change management to drive adoption.
- **C**: Propose a lightweight validation loop — usability tests and prototype reviews — alongside ongoing delivery to confirm assumptions before each release.
- **D**: Trust the product owner's prior research and treat low adoption as a marketing problem to escalate to the business unit.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When delivered features aren't being used, the issue is direction rather than pace, but stopping work entirely is rarely needed. (C) keeps delivery moving while introducing the feedback loop that's clearly missing — running validation in parallel is faster, cheaper, and respects the PO's role. (A) halts value creation when validation can run alongside development. (B) treats adoption as the problem when stale assumptions are the more likely cause. (D) abdicates the PM's responsibility to surface evidence the PO may not have seen, and 18-month-old research is suspect on its own.
