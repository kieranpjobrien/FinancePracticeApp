# Question

A gaming studio's team has shipped value steadily for six sprints. To hit an external launch tied to a marketing campaign, the producer proposes four straight weeks of evening and weekend crunch. The team can technically deliver the full launch scope this way, but two senior engineers have privately told the project manager they are burning out and considering leaving. The launch date itself is firm. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the crunch, since the date is externally committed and the team has confirmed the full scope is achievable within it.
- **B**: Work with the product owner to identify a reduced launch scope that could still carry the campaign, then put that option and the explicit attrition risk to the producer and sponsor as the recommended path over sustained crunch.
- **C**: Tell the producer that four weeks of crunch is off the table on sustainable-pace grounds and that the scope will have to give, leaving the producer to determine what ships.
- **D**: Approve a single two-week crunch as a compromise between the date pressure and the team's wellbeing, on the expectation that morale recovers afterwards.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The date is fixed but the scope is the real variable, and crunch that pushes out senior engineers costs more future value than one launch is worth, so the PM's job is to find and propose the trade that protects both the campaign and the team. (B) shapes a viable reduced scope with the product owner and surfaces the attrition risk to the decision-makers, which is the disciplined way to act with urgency. (A) buys the launch by spending the team's sustainability and likely triggering the resignations that would set delivery back for months. (C) has the right instinct on pace but stops half-way, declining the crunch without doing the work to define what a campaign-viable reduced scope looks like. (D) softens the crunch without removing its cause, so it still risks burnout and possible departures for scope that could instead be trimmed.
