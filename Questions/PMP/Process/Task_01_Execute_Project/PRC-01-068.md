# Question

A team is about to deploy a major platform upgrade affecting 50,000 internal users. Testing is complete. Infrastructure suggests a full cut-over; architecture suggests canary rollout. The PO wants the fastest path to production. What should the PM recommend to the change advisory board?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend a full production cut-over with a strong rollback plan prepared and the infrastructure team placed on standby.
- **B**: Defer to whichever approach the CAB prefers, since rollout strategy for this upgrade is ultimately a governance call.
- **C**: Ask the PO to decide between full cut-over and canary since the PO owns user value and experience decisions.
- **D**: Recommend a canary rollout to a small user cohort first, with defined health metrics and explicit promote-or-rollback gates.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A canary rollout is the controlled, risk-reducing default for large deployments where a rollback of a full cut-over is expensive. (D) matches strategy to impact. (A) full cut-over accepts all-or-nothing risk when a lower-risk path exists. (B) deferring to CAB avoids the PM's advisory responsibility. (C) rollout strategy is a risk and delivery decision, not a pure product decision for the PO alone.
