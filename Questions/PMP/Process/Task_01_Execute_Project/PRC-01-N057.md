# Question

An agile team committed to deliver a recommendation-engine feature this release, but during the first sprint they discover the underlying machine-learning approach is far more uncertain than expected and could consume the whole release with no guarantee of a usable result. The business still wants visible value from this release. What should the product owner and team do?

## Options

- **A**: Time-box a research spike to reduce the technical uncertainty, while continuing to deliver other ready, valuable backlog items so the release still produces visible value.
- **B**: Commit the whole team to the recommendation engine for the release, accepting the risk that nothing else ships, because it is the headline feature.
- **C**: Drop the recommendation engine entirely from the roadmap since it is too uncertain, and replace it with safer features.
- **D**: Push the team to deliver a rough version of the recommendation engine by the deadline regardless of whether its outputs are reliable.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A time-boxed spike contains the uncertainty without betting the release on it, and continuing to deliver other ready items keeps visible value flowing (A). (B) gambles the entire release on an uncertain outcome, risking zero delivered value. (C) over-reacts by abandoning a potentially valuable feature instead of first reducing its uncertainty. (D) ships an unreliable recommendation engine, which can mislead users and damage trust — delivering negative value rather than real value.
