# Question

A Scrum team building a retail loyalty app is two sprints from a launch date that marketing has already advertised. At sprint review, the product owner sees that three of the eight planned features are polished and demonstrable, three are functional but rough, and two have not started. Marketing insists the launch date cannot move. What should the product owner do?

## Options

- **A**: Reduce the launch scope to the three demonstrable features plus whichever rough features can be hardened in the remaining sprints, and release that as the first increment.
- **B**: Keep all eight features in scope and ask the development team to work overtime across both remaining sprints to finish everything by the date.
- **C**: Move the two unstarted features to a fast-follow release and ship the other six regardless of their readiness so the advertised count is nearly met.
- **D**: Escalate to the sponsor that the date is unrealistic and request a six-week extension to deliver the full feature set.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Delivering value with urgency means shipping a coherent, working increment by the committed date rather than chasing a feature count. Releasing the demonstrable features plus any that can be genuinely hardened protects quality and the advertised launch (A). (B) relies on sustained overtime, which erodes quality and sustainable pace and still may not finish two unstarted features. (C) ships rough, unfinished features just to approach the original count, risking a poor first impression on a public launch. (D) abandons the fixed date that marketing has committed to externally before exploring the scope flex that agile expressly enables.
