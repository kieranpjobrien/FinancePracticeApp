# Question

An ERP rollout is delivering modules on schedule, but business adoption is lagging because change management, training, and process redesign workstreams are weeks behind. Even released modules are barely used. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue delivering modules on the original cadence; adoption is the change team's responsibility, not the project's delivery remit.
- **B**: Halt all module deliveries until the change and training teams catch up to the most recent release and the business adopts it.
- **C**: Raise value-realisation as a programme risk, coordinate with the change lead, and propose pacing releases to match adoption.
- **D**: Add the change-management activities into the development team's backlog so a single team owns end-to-end delivery and adoption.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Value isn't delivered when features ship — it's delivered when they're used. (C) treats the integrated outcome as the PM's accountability and proposes coordination, not control. (A) is a narrow definition of project success that fails the business case. (B) is too binary; partial pacing is better than full halt. (D) overloads the development team with skills they don't have and conflates two distinct workstreams.
