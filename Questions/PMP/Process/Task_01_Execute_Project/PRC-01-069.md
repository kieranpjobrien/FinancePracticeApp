# Question

A severity-1 production incident is active. The on-call engineer identifies a fix and wants to push it immediately. Organisational process requires a change advisory board review for all production changes. The business impact is growing by the minute. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise the engineer to deploy the fix immediately because severity-1 production incidents clearly override standard change-advisory-board review requirements here.
- **B**: Hold the fix and convene the full change advisory board since no exception exists to the standard production change process right now.
- **C**: Ask the sponsor to verbally approve the deployment and document that approval in the change log shortly after the engineer deploys it.
- **D**: Invoke the organisation's emergency change procedure, obtain the required approver, deploy the fix, and document the full review retrospectively.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Mature change-control frameworks include an emergency path for exactly this situation — speed with an auditable trail. (D) uses the right lane in the right way. (A) bypassing process without invoking the emergency procedure creates a compliance gap. (B) full CAB convening during an active sev-1 prolongs business impact unnecessarily. (C) verbal sponsor approval doesn't satisfy change-control requirements if a defined emergency process exists.
