# Question

A retail programme is delivering a peak-readiness release two weeks before Black Friday. A severity-2 defect is found in the payment path during user acceptance testing. Two team members argue for a patch release; two argue for a full regression cycle. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the patch path since the two-week window prohibits any time-consuming full regression of the payment system.
- **B**: Facilitate a focused triage with QA lead and architect to scope a risk-based regression around the payment path specifically.
- **C**: Defer the decision to the release board because payment defects near peak season require formal governance approval.
- **D**: Extend user acceptance testing by a week to allow a full regression and still hit the pre-peak release window.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Urgency doesn't remove the obligation to think; PMI expects a risk-based, targeted response rather than a binary choice. (B) gets the right experts to size the right test envelope. (A) picks a side under pressure without the engineering input. (C) escalating a technical scoping decision to a board is premature and slow. (D) adds schedule without choosing what to test, addressing duration rather than risk.
