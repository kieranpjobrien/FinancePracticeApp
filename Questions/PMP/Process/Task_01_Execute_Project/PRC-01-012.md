# Question

A predictive infrastructure project is three weeks behind. The PM has confirmed several requested features are "nice to have" while a smaller subset is essential to the original business case. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Submit a change request to defer the lower-value features and protect the essential scope, then take it through the CCB.
- **B**: Compress the schedule by adding contractors to the most-behind workstreams to recover all scope.
- **C**: Cut every workstream's scope by an equal percentage so no single area carries the burden of the slip.
- **D**: Inform stakeholders the deadline will move and rebaseline the schedule before further work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

When time is pressured, scope is the appropriate lever — but baseline scope changes flow through change control. (A) protects the business case and uses formal governance, which is what PMI expects. (B) commits cost and risks Brooks' law without first checking whether scope flexibility could solve the problem more cheaply. (C) treats unequal items equally and erodes value rather than preserving it. (D) jumps straight to schedule rebaselining without considering that scope reduction may be a better trade.
