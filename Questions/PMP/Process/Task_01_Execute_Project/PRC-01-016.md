# Question

A team is building a complex feature that will take three sprints. The steering committee wants tangible evidence of progress in two weeks. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule a detailed walkthrough of the technical design and architecture diagrams at the two-week mark.
- **B**: Slice the feature into thin end-to-end increments so a working partial capability can be demonstrated in the first sprint.
- **C**: Provide a written status report with percentage-complete metrics for each component.
- **D**: Ask the steering committee to wait until sprint three when the full feature is ready to demo.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Steering committees trust working software far more than artefacts about software. (B) addresses the request directly and produces feedback the team can act on. (A) shows intent but not progress and is a poor proxy for value. (C) gives a number but no demonstrable capability — the steering committee specifically asked for evidence. (D) refuses the legitimate ask when vertical slicing would solve it.
