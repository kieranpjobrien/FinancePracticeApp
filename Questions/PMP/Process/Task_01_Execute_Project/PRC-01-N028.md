# Question

An insurance-claims automation team consistently starts many stories but finishes few, and the time from "started" to "released" keeps growing. Leadership wants faster delivery of value without simply demanding longer hours. Which actions would most directly improve the flow of completed value? (Select two.)

## Options

- **A**: Introduce work-in-progress limits so the team finishes started items before pulling new ones.
- **B**: Increase the sprint commitment so more stories are planned into each iteration.
- **C**: Swarm the team on the oldest in-progress items to drive them to "done" before starting new work.
- **D**: Add a second daily stand-up so progress is reported more frequently to leadership.
- **E**: Lengthen the sprint from two weeks to four to reduce planning overhead.

## Answer

A, C

## Explanation

Flow of value improves by finishing work, not starting more or reporting more. WIP limits (A) stop the team overcommitting and force completion, and swarming on the oldest items (C) directly reduces cycle time and releases value sooner. (B) increases the very overcommitment causing the backlog of unfinished work. (D) adds reporting ceremony without changing throughput. (E) lengthens the feedback loop and delays value, the opposite of acting with urgency.
