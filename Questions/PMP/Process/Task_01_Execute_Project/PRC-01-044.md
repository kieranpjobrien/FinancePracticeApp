# Question

A Kanban team's WIP limit is full when a Sev-1 production incident lands that requires immediate development support. What should the team do?

## Options

- **A**: Pull the Sev-1 into the next available WIP slot once one of the in-flight items finishes naturally in the normal flow.
- **B**: Use the team's expedite swim lane for the Sev-1, hold new pulls until WIP drops, and review expedite usage for patterns.
- **C**: Raise the WIP limit by one to accommodate the Sev-1 without disrupting the team's existing flow of in-flight items.
- **D**: Abandon the Sev-1 to a separate hot-fix team so that the Kanban board's flow remains completely undisturbed by it.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mature Kanban systems include an expedite lane precisely for Sev-1s; using it intentionally beats either ignoring the policy or watering it down. (B) uses the lane, protects the rest of the system, and feeds learning back into the process. (A) waits when the incident requires immediate action. (C) treats WIP limits as adjustable based on demand, which defeats their purpose. (D) avoids the issue but assumes a hot-fix team exists; even when one does, expediting through Kanban is the right approach when the same team owns the work.
