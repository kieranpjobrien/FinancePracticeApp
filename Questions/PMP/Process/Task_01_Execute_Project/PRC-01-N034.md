# Question

During execution of a predictive automotive-plant retooling project, the sponsor requests that one production line be brought online two weeks early to capture an unexpected supply contract. Pulling that line forward is feasible but would divert the specialist commissioning crew from another line, delaying it by about two weeks. The downstream impact of the second line's delay on other customer commitments is not yet clear, and commissioning in this plant is safety-critical. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Assess the resequencing for cost, the knock-on effect of the second line's delay, and the safety implications, then take it through change control for an owned, documented decision.
- **B**: Expedite the requested line now and manage the second line's delay as it arises, since the sponsor has identified concrete new revenue and speed is the point.
- **C**: Decline the resequencing, since it disrupts an agreed commissioning order and pushes out a line that other customers are already counting on.
- **D**: Bring the urgency to the commissioning crew lead and let them decide the safest order to take the two lines, as the people closest to the work.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The expedite is a real plan change with an unquantified knock-on cost and safety-critical commissioning, so the disciplined first step is to assess the full trade-off and route it through change control rather than to accept or refuse it blind. (A) gets the impact and safety picture in front of the accountable decision-makers and produces an owned, documented call. (B) is tempting because the revenue is concrete, but it commits before the second line's downstream effect or the safety implications are understood. (C) protects existing commitments yet rejects potentially greater value on the basis of a sequence whose true cost has not been evaluated. (D) rightly respects the crew's expertise but hands a decision with cost and customer-commitment consequences to a technical lead, who owns the safe method, not the business trade-off.
