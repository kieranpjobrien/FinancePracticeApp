# Question

The critical path on a predictive data-centre migration shows a two-week slip against a hard regulatory deadline. Fast-tracking design and build was evaluated. The architect flags that fast-tracking introduces material rework risk if the design isn't stable. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with fast-tracking the design and build overlap since meeting the regulatory deadline outweighs potential rework cost.
- **B**: Crash by adding contract resources to the most constrained design activities rather than overlapping stages with uncertain dependencies.
- **C**: Request a deadline extension from the regulator immediately because design instability makes schedule compression genuinely unsafe.
- **D**: Split the design into stable and unstable components, fast-track only the stable parts, and crash the remainder with experts.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PMI expects compression techniques to be applied surgically with awareness of their risk profiles. (D) matches the technique to the component's stability and is the mature response. (A) accepts blanket rework risk without decomposing the problem. (B) crashing alone misses the identified overlap opportunities on stable work. (C) regulator extension should be a last resort after internal options are exhausted.
