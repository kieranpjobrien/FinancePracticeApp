# Question

At the end of month four on a twelve-month predictive project, PV is $400,000, EV is $360,000, AC is $380,000, and BAC is $1,200,000. What is the Schedule Performance Index (SPI) and what does it tell the PM about delivery pace?

## Options

- **A**: SPI is 0.90, meaning the project is behind schedule and earning ninety cents of planned work per dollar scheduled.
- **B**: SPI is 0.95, meaning the project is modestly behind cost baseline but tracking acceptably on schedule overall.
- **C**: SPI is 1.06, meaning the project is slightly ahead of schedule and should recover any small cost overruns.
- **D**: SPI is 1.11, meaning the project is meaningfully ahead of schedule with headroom to absorb later risks.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

SPI = EV/PV = 360,000 / 400,000 = 0.90, which means the project has earned 90% of the value it planned to at this point. (A) applies the formula correctly. (B) 0.95 is the rough CPI (EV/AC = 0.947), a classic SPI/CPI confusion. (C) 1.06 inverts the ratio to PV/EV. (D) 1.11 reflects another inversion; both C and D are common arithmetic-flip distractors.
