# Question

An online travel platform has built a fare-comparison engine. To let a customer actually book and generate revenue, payments and customer-support readiness must also be in place — both are a few sprints away. The comparison engine on its own, however, would draw search traffic and let the team observe real query patterns, though it cannot complete a booking. The product owner proposes releasing the engine now as a public, standalone feature. The board's stated objective for this quarter is revenue from completed bookings. What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Release the engine now only as an instrumented limited trial to learn from real query patterns, and hold the public revenue-bearing launch until payments and support complete the bookable increment.
- **B**: Release the engine now as a full public feature, since the search traffic and early visibility are real value the team should capture immediately.
- **C**: Hold the engine entirely until payments and support are ready, then release the complete bookable increment in one go so customers never meet a feature they cannot transact on.
- **D**: Release the engine publicly now and add a waitlist sign-up where interested customers leave details, converting the traffic into a marketing pipeline ahead of the bookable launch.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The engine alone carries genuine *learning* value but cannot meet the quarter's stated goal — revenue from completed bookings — so the skill is capturing the learning without misrepresenting progress against that goal. (A) takes the early value that actually exists (real query data) through an instrumented trial while reserving the public, revenue-framed launch for the increment that can truly transact — value realised honestly at each step. (B) ships a full public feature that cannot book; visibility has some worth, but a prominent "compare fares" front door that dead-ends drives abandonment and frames non-bookable output as the headline outcome the board is measuring. (C) is defensible but discards the cheap, real learning the engine could yield now, delaying all value and feedback for purity of experience. (D) is plausible, yet a public dead-end with only a waitlist still exposes every visitor to a broken journey and bets the quarter's revenue goal on speculative pipeline rather than the bookable increment.
