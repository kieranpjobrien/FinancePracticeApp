# Question

A project manager under schedule pressure is choosing how to recover time on a predictive project. Match each described situation to the schedule technique that best fits it.

## Items

- **1**: Two normally sequential design activities could be performed with partial overlap, accepting some rework risk, to recover time at no extra cost.
- **2**: A critical-path activity can be shortened by adding more skilled resources, which costs more but compresses the duration.
- **3**: The forecast shows the project finishing late, and the manager wants to update the remaining schedule based on actual progress and current reality.

## Matches

- **A**: Crashing
- **B**: Fast-tracking
- **C**: Schedule re-baselining / re-forecasting

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Fast-tracking overlaps activities normally done in sequence to save time at the cost of added risk, matching item 1. Crashing shortens duration by adding resources at additional cost, matching item 2. Re-forecasting or re-baselining updates the remaining plan to reflect actual progress and reality, matching item 3. Distinguishing crashing (cost, more resources) from fast-tracking (risk, overlap) is the crux, and neither is the same as re-projecting the schedule against actuals.
