# Question

A delivery team consistently ships features each sprint, but business stakeholders complain that the items they consider most valuable keep slipping into later releases. The PM has already confirmed velocity is stable. What should the PM examine NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Whether the team is selecting work in priority order or pulling easier items first regardless of rank.
- **B**: Whether the team's story-point estimates have drifted higher over time, effectively hiding underlying capacity problems.
- **C**: Whether stakeholders are submitting their priority requests through the formal intake process at the right point.
- **D**: Whether additional senior engineers are needed on the team to handle the more complex high-value backlog items.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The symptom — features ship, but the wrong ones — points to a gap between stated priority and what the team actually pulls. (A) directly investigates that gap and is the most likely root cause. (B) looks at estimation drift, which would show up as slowing velocity rather than misordered delivery. (C) shifts blame to stakeholders when the issue is internal execution, and intake process doesn't explain why ranked items aren't being worked. (D) jumps to a resource solution before the cause is even diagnosed.
