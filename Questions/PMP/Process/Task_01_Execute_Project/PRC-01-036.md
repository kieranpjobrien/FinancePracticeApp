# Question

A team's velocity has swung between 30 and 50 points across the last six sprints. Stakeholders complain delivery is unpredictable. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Set a fixed velocity target of 40 points per sprint and hold the team accountable to hitting it each time.
- **B**: Stop reporting velocity externally to stakeholders and switch instead to using story-count metrics only going forward.
- **C**: Use the rolling average to forecast and explain to stakeholders that this level of variance is normal in agile delivery.
- **D**: Investigate the variance with the team — estimation, story sizing, interruptions, story types — and address the likely root causes.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Wide velocity variance has causes the team can usually identify and address; the PM's job is to investigate, not to mask or rationalise it. (D) goes after root cause. (A) creates false precision and pressures the team to game estimates. (B) hides the data instead of fixing the underlying issue. (C) is partially correct that some variance is normal, but the magnitude here suggests something fixable, not just statistical noise.
