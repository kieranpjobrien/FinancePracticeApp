# Question

A product owner for an insurance-claims platform must order the next release's backlog. Four epics are candidates: a fraud-detection rule (high revenue protection, six weeks effort), a self-service portal (high customer value, four weeks, depends on nothing), a mobile redesign (moderate value, three weeks), and a compliance reporting update (mandated by a regulator, hard deadline in five weeks, two weeks effort). Capacity allows roughly nine weeks of work this release. How should the product owner order the work to deliver the most value?

## Options

- **A**: Schedule the compliance update to finish before its regulatory deadline, then the self-service portal, then begin the fraud-detection rule with any remaining capacity.
- **B**: Order strictly by business-value score: fraud-detection rule, self-service portal, then compliance update if time allows.
- **C**: Start with the quickest items to maximise the number of epics completed: compliance update, mobile redesign, then self-service portal.
- **D**: Begin the fraud-detection rule first because it protects the most revenue, fitting the others around it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A regulatory mandate with a hard deadline is a constraint that must be satisfied first or the organisation incurs penalties, so sequencing it to land before the deadline and then taking the next-highest-value independent item maximises realised value (A). (B) ranks purely by value score and risks missing the regulatory deadline, exposing the business to fines that outweigh other gains. (C) optimises for epic count rather than value, pulling in the moderate mobile redesign ahead of the high-value portal. (D) leads with revenue protection but ignores the binding compliance deadline that should be honoured first.
