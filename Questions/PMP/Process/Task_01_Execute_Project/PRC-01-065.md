# Question

A PM leads one of four projects in a digital programme. Her team's API is a dependency for two other projects. Those projects keep requesting urgent, undocumented API changes directly from her developers, slowing her own delivery. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct her developers to decline all dependency requests until they are documented and formally scheduled by the PM.
- **B**: Route all dependency requests through her directly as the single point of contact so she can prioritise them personally.
- **C**: Propose a programme-level dependency-management protocol and lightweight intake, agreed with the programme manager and peer PMs.
- **D**: Raise the pattern as a formal interdependency risk and let programme governance decide on a coordination mechanism.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PMI expects proactive coordination across dependent projects, not bilateral workarounds or passive risk reporting. (C) fixes the system with peers, not alone. (A) unilateral refusal preserves her project but damages the programme. (B) becoming a single bottleneck doesn't scale across four projects. (D) logging a risk without proposing a solution abdicates the coordination role the PM should lead.
