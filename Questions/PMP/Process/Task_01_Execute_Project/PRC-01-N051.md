# Question

A predictive construction-fit-out project is one week behind because a custom joinery delivery slipped. The joinery is on the critical path for the handover date, which a tenant has contractually committed to occupy. The supplier offers to expedite production for an additional fee that is within the project's management reserve. The project manager confirms the expedite would recover the full week. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Confirm the expedite recovers the critical-path slip, validate the cost against the reserve and the cost of a late handover, and proceed with the expedited order through the proper change process.
- **B**: Accept the supplier's expedite offer immediately to recover the week, since the cost is within reserve and time is critical.
- **C**: Decline the expedite to protect the management reserve and absorb the one-week delay, renegotiating the handover with the tenant.
- **D**: Look for non-critical activities to fast-track elsewhere so the overall schedule recovers without paying the expedite fee.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The expedite directly recovers the critical-path slip, so the right move is to weigh its cost against the cost and contractual exposure of a late handover and process the decision properly (A). (B) reaches the same action but skips validating the trade-off and applying change control, which the reserve usage warrants. (C) protects money at the expense of a contractual handover, likely a worse outcome than spending available reserve. (D) fast-tracks non-critical work, which cannot recover a delay sitting on the critical path.
