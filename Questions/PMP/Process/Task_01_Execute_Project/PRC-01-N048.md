# Question

A predictive project to relocate a manufacturing line has a fixed go-live driven by an equipment supplier's installation window that cannot be rescheduled for six months if missed. A specialist electrical engineer needed on the critical path next week has just been pulled by a functional manager onto a higher-priority corporate initiative. Losing the engineer for that week would delay go-live past the supplier window. A qualified external contractor is available at a premium, and there is some reserve to cover it. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the cost and consequence of missing the supplier window and take it to the functional manager and sponsor to negotiate retaining the engineer for the critical week or agreeing a replacement.
- **B**: Engage the external contractor for the critical week straight away, since the funds are available and acting now removes the threat to the go-live.
- **C**: Ask the engineer's team to brief the contractor and split the critical-path task between them so neither resource is fully relied upon.
- **D**: Re-sequence the schedule to pull non-critical activities forward, buying time in case the engineer is freed before the critical task starts.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

This is a priority conflict between two initiatives competing for one resource, and the irreversible six-month consequence is exactly what the functional manager and sponsor need quantified so they can re-balance priorities; that negotiation is the disciplined first move and may simply return the engineer (A). (B) jumps to spending premium reserve and locks in a less-familiar contractor before the cheaper route of negotiating the engineer back has been tried, and bypasses the functional manager. (C) splitting a specialist critical-path task between two people adds handover risk and coordination on the very activity that can least absorb it. (D) reordering non-critical work does nothing to free the critical-path resource and merely hopes the conflict resolves itself.
