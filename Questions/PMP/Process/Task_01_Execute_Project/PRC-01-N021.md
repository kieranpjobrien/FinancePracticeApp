# Question

A fintech squad delivering a payments-reconciliation engine has six items in progress, and the sponsor is asking why nothing has shipped in nine days despite the team being visibly busy. Each item is queuing for time in a single integration environment that another team controls, and the wait is what is stalling completions. The squad could pursue several responses at once. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Have the squad converge on the two items closest to done and push them through the environment first, so something completes while the contention is addressed.
- **B**: Engage the controlling team to secure a dedicated window in the integration environment, since the queue for that shared resource is what is preventing any item from finishing.
- **C**: Run a quick value-stream mapping exercise with the squad to confirm where the time is actually being lost before committing to a fix.
- **D**: Spin up a second, lightweight integration environment the squad can control, removing the dependency on the other team entirely.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Nine days with nothing completing points to one external resource as the active bottleneck, and the fastest route to flow is to relieve that specific constraint at its source. (B) goes to the team that controls the environment and secures throughput where it is actually blocked, so completions resume now. (A) drives a couple of items out but leaves the constraint intact, so the same queue re-forms behind them next time. (C) is sound diagnostics, but the cause is already visible, so mapping mainly delays acting on a known impediment. (D) removes the dependency over time but stands up new infrastructure the squad must build and trust, which is slower than negotiating access this week and may not match the controlled environment's fidelity.
