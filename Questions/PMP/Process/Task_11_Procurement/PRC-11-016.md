# Question

What primarily distinguishes a Request for Proposal (RFP) from a Request for Quotation (RFQ)?

## Options

- **A**: An RFP is strictly for government procurement; an RFQ is for commercial procurement contracts.
- **B**: An RFP solicits proposals on approach and price; an RFQ solicits price quotes for well-defined items.
- **C**: An RFP creates a legally binding offer on submission; an RFQ never creates binding commitment.
- **D**: An RFP is always used for large-value buys; an RFQ is reserved for small-value buys.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The substantive difference is what the buyer needs from the seller: an RFP asks how the seller would do the work and at what price; an RFQ asks for pricing on items already well-specified. (A) confuses sector convention with document type. (C) is incorrect; binding effect depends on the document language and jurisdiction, not the type. (D) is a generalisation; either may be used at any size.
