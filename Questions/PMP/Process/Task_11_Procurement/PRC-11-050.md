# Question

During a competitive procurement, a senior executive suggests the PM score a particular vendor's proposal more favourably because "they have always been reliable for us". The evaluation criteria were agreed and published to all bidders. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the published evaluation criteria consistently across all proposals and document the scoring rationale.
- **B**: Follow the senior executive's suggestion because past vendor reliability is a legitimate evaluation factor.
- **C**: Rewrite the evaluation criteria now to include past reliability so the favoured vendor can score highly too.
- **D**: Score the proposal however the PM privately judges right and leave the documentation deliberately vague.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Procurement ethics and fairness require consistent application of published criteria; favouring a vendor outside criteria breaches PMI ethics and creates legal exposure. (B) accepts an ethical breach on an authority cue. (C) changes criteria mid-process after bids have been submitted. (D) hides the favoritism in vague documentation, which is still unethical and audit-fragile.
