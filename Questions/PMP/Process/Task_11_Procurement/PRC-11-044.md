# Question

The PM discovers the prime vendor has subcontracted 40% of the work to a company that was not disclosed in the proposal. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the subcontracting arrangement provided the deliverables continue to meet specification and quality standards.
- **B**: Direct the prime vendor to terminate the subcontractor and complete the remaining work using only their own staff.
- **C**: Issue a termination-for-cause notice against the prime based on material misrepresentation in the original proposal submission.
- **D**: Review the subcontracting and disclosure clauses to determine if the non-disclosure is a breach, then respond proportionately.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The right response depends on what the contract says about subcontracting, disclosure, and consent; the PM must read the clauses before taking action. (A) ignores potential breach. (B) may not be enforceable if subcontracting is permitted. (C) is disproportionate before the contract analysis confirms a material breach justifies termination.
