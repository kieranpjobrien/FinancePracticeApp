# Question

A Fixed Price with Economic Price Adjustment (FP-EPA) contract is most appropriate when which condition is present?

## Options

- **A**: The scope is poorly defined and is expected to change throughout the contract.
- **B**: The contract spans a multi-year period and is exposed to inflation, currency, or commodity price movements.
- **C**: The buyer wishes to incentivise the seller to reduce cost below a target.
- **D**: The work is high-risk and the buyer is willing to bear cost overruns.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FP-EPA preserves a fixed-price structure while adjusting for defined economic indices over long contract periods, fairly handling external price movements neither party controls. (A) describes a situation calling for T&M or cost-reimbursable. (C) describes incentive-fee structures. (D) describes cost-reimbursable contracts where the buyer absorbs cost risk.
