# Question

A seller has delivered the final working component under a fixed-price contract and requests final payment and formal procurement closure. The product meets every functional requirement and operations has begun using it. The project manager finds that an operations-and-maintenance manual, explicitly listed as a deliverable in the statement of work, has not been provided. The seller says the manual is low priority and offers to send it later as a goodwill gesture. The sponsor wants the procurement closed this quarter. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Grant conditional acceptance now and release a retained portion of the final payment only once the manual is delivered.
- **B**: Withhold formal acceptance and final closure until the seller delivers the manual required by the statement of work.
- **C**: Accept and close the procurement, recording the outstanding manual on a punch list with an agreed delivery date the seller commits to in writing.
- **D**: Accept and close on the functional product, and raise the missing manual as a lessons-learned item to strengthen future SOWs.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Formal acceptance and closure require all contractually specified deliverables, and the manual is an explicit SOW item the buyer has paid for, so the correct position is to withhold acceptance until it is delivered (B). Conditional acceptance with payment retention (A) is a real contracting tool, but it presupposes a contractual right to retain and formally accepts a deliverable set that is still incomplete, conceding more than the situation requires when the seller can simply finish the obligation. Closing against a punch-list commitment (C) trades a firm contractual obligation for a promise and weakens the buyer's leverage to obtain a document it is entitled to; goodwill is no substitute for completion. Treating it only as a lesson learned and closing on the functional product (D) waives the obligation entirely and forgoes records the buyer has already bought. The fine margin is between holding acceptance for full performance (B) and the various ways of accepting-now-and-chasing-later (A, C, D), all of which surrender leverage the buyer does not need to give up.
