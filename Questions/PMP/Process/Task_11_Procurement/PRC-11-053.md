# Question

A time and materials contract for a hybrid analytics programme has a not-to-exceed ceiling set at the total project estimate. The vendor reports that at current burn rate, the ceiling will be reached two months before scope completion. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue under the current ceiling and let the vendor absorb the overrun beyond the not-to-exceed amount.
- **B**: Waive the ceiling informally by accepting invoices that exceed it, since the project still needs vendor work.
- **C**: Ask the vendor to slow down so the ceiling holds for the full original schedule regardless of quality impact.
- **D**: Assess scope and burn rate and raise a formal contract change to revise the ceiling or descope work.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Not-to-exceed ceilings are governance signals; breaching them requires a contract change addressing either scope or cost, not informal practice. (A) assumes the vendor will work unpaid, which they will not. (B) waives contract control without authority or record. (C) degrades quality to hold a number rather than addressing the underlying trade-off transparently.
