# Question

During an RFP process for a multi-million-dollar ERP implementation, one bidder has privately asked the PM a technical question by email. The PM knows the answer. Other bidders are unaware of the question. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Share the question and the answer with all bidders through the formal bidders' communication channel.
- **B**: Reply to the asking bidder only so they receive the information they specifically took the time to request.
- **C**: Ignore the email entirely so that no unfair information asymmetry is created between bidders anywhere.
- **D**: Reply through the formal channel but only with the answer, omitting which bidder originally asked.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Procurement fairness requires all bidders have access to the same information; sharing both question and answer through the formal channel preserves a level playing field. (B) creates information asymmetry. (C) leaves a reasonable question unanswered, which harms bid quality. (D) omits context that may matter for other bidders to frame their own questions.
