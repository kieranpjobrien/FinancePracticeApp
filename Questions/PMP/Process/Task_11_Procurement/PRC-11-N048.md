# Question

Six months into a cost-reimbursable contract, a seller's monthly invoices are rising faster than the visible progress on the deliverable. The seller insists the work is on track and that early-phase costs are front-loaded. The contract grants the buyer audit rights and requires monthly performance reporting, but the seller's reports show cost only, not progress. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Exercise the contract's audit right and commission an independent inspection of the seller's books and work in progress.
- **B**: Hold a procurement performance review with the seller to compare claimed costs against measured deliverable progress and the agreed reporting requirements.
- **C**: Require the seller to add earned-value or progress metrics to its monthly reports so cost and progress can be compared going forward.
- **D**: Reforecast the estimate at completion using the current cost trend and alert the sponsor to the likely overrun.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The core problem is that cost is visible but progress is not, and the seller disputes the inference, so the first step is the joint procurement performance review: bring the seller to the table, lay claimed costs against whatever measured progress exists, and test the explanation against the contract's reporting requirements (B). Exercising the audit right (A) is a valid escalation but is heavier-handed and adversarial before a straightforward review has even established whether a real problem exists. Tightening future reporting (C) is a sensible corrective that may well come out of the review, but on its own it addresses only the next periods and leaves the present discrepancy uninvestigated. Reforecasting and alerting the sponsor (D) projects from an unverified cost trend and risks raising an alarm before the facts are confirmed; the review should establish the position first. The discriminator is the collaborative, evidence-gathering review preceding both the contractual audit and any forecast or reporting change.
