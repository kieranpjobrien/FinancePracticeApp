# Question

A vendor is needed on site within two weeks, but the standard procurement cycle for new contracts is six weeks. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage procurement to identify approved expedited paths such as letter contracts or existing master agreements.
- **B**: Bring the vendor on under verbal terms now and paper the formal contract later when procurement catches up.
- **C**: Bypass procurement entirely and authorise the spend directly using the PM's delegated signing authority threshold.
- **D**: Slip the project schedule by four weeks so the full standard procurement cycle can run without shortcuts.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Most procurement functions have legitimate expedited paths for genuine urgency; the PM partners with procurement rather than working around them. (B) creates legal, financial, and ethics risk by performing work without a contract. (C) bypasses controls and exposes both the PM and the organisation. (D) is acceptable only when no legitimate expedited path exists and is not the first move.
