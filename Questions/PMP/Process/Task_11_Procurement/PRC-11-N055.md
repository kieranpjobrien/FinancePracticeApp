# Question

A project manager is finalising the weighted source selection criteria for a complex, high-risk software procurement before the RFP goes out. The evaluation panel has proposed five candidate criteria and must weight those that will most reliably distinguish bidders likely to deliver this engagement well. Which THREE should be carried into the scored criteria? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: Demonstrated technical capability of the staff the bidder will actually assign to this project.
- **B**: The bidder's past performance and verifiable references on comparable work.
- **C**: The bidder's total annual revenue as a proxy for financial stability and staying power.
- **D**: The credibility of the bidder's proposed approach to the project's specific key risks.
- **E**: The number of relevant industry certifications the bidder's organisation holds overall.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Criteria that predict success on this project focus on the capability of the people who will be assigned to it (A), proven performance on comparable work (B), and a credible approach to the project's specific risks (D), all of which bear directly on delivery and can be weighted and defended. Total annual revenue (C) touches financial stability but is a crude proxy that rewards size; if financial viability matters, it should be assessed through targeted financial-health checks, not headline revenue, so as a weighted selection criterion it predicts little about delivery. Organisation-wide certification counts (E) describe the firm in aggregate rather than the team and approach proposed for this engagement, so they are weak predictors of project-specific success. The discriminator is project-specific, performance-relevant evidence (A, B, D) versus organisation-level size and credential proxies (C, E) that look relevant but do not forecast delivery here.
