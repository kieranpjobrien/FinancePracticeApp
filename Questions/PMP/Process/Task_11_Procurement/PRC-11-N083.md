# Question

A project manager on a hybrid construction-and-systems programme is drafting the procurement management plan. Several stakeholders have suggested content for it, and the project manager must decide which items belong in this plan as opposed to other artefacts. Which THREE of the following belong in the procurement management plan itself? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: The types of contracts to be used and the criteria for choosing among them for each procurement.
- **B**: How procurement activities will be coordinated with the project schedule and other knowledge areas.
- **C**: The constraints and assumptions that could affect planned procurements, such as long-lead supplier markets.
- **D**: The negotiated final pricing and payment milestones for each awarded contract.
- **E**: The detailed technical specification each supplier must build to, drawn from the design authority.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The procurement management plan records *how* procurement will be conducted: the contract types and selection criteria to be applied (A), how procurement is coordinated with the schedule and other areas (B), and the constraints and assumptions shaping what can be procured and when (C). Negotiated final pricing and payment milestones (D) are the result of running an individual procurement and live in the agreement or contract, not in the plan that governs the approach; the plan precedes award. The detailed technical specification each supplier must build to (E) belongs in the procurement statement of work for that package, derived from the requirements and design, not in the management plan, which stays method-level. The trap is that D and E are unmistakably procurement-related, but they are *outputs of executing* procurements or inputs to specific solicitations, whereas the management plan sets the rules of engagement.
