# Question

Three vendors bid on regulated equipment. Vendor A is cheapest, Vendor B has the strongest quality record, Vendor C offers the fastest delivery. The deadline is tight and quality is required for compliance. How should the PM structure the recommendation?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend Vendor A and use the cost savings to fund extra inspections and quality assurance activities.
- **B**: Recommend Vendor C because the deadline is the binding constraint driving the delivery decision.
- **C**: Recommend Vendor B because compliance-grade quality is non-negotiable and trumps the other evaluation criteria.
- **D**: Build a weighted-criteria scoring model that reflects compliance quality, delivery speed, and cost, then evaluate each vendor.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Multi-factor procurement decisions should use a documented weighted scoring model so the recommendation is defensible and traceable. The weights reflect the project's actual priorities (compliance quality high, delivery high, cost as a constraint). (A) prioritises cost and tries to compensate for risk. (B) ignores the compliance quality requirement. (C) is right about non-negotiable compliance but skips the structured comparison the PMI process expects.
