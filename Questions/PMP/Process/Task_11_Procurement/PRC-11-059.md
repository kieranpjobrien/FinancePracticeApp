# Question

A cost plus incentive fee contract is closing on a predictive pharma project. Final cost is below target, so the vendor is owed a share of underrun per the sharing ratio. Final documentation has not yet been independently verified. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the incentive now based on the vendor's own cost reporting and trust the numbers to be correct.
- **B**: Verify cost data, confirm deliverables are accepted, calculate the incentive per contract, then pay and close.
- **C**: Withhold the incentive indefinitely until an external audit is scheduled next fiscal year to verify amounts.
- **D**: Pay a conservative partial amount now and reconcile any remainder in the following quarter after further review.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contract closure requires verification of cost data, confirmation of deliverable acceptance, and incentive calculation per contract formula before payment and formal closure. (A) pays without verification on a cost-reimbursable contract. (C) delays properly earned payment on speculative audit timing. (D) pays a made-up partial amount unsupported by the contract's sharing formula.
