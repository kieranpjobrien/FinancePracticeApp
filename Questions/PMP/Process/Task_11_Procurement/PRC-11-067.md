# Question

The PM is assembling an evaluation panel for a vendor selection. One proposed panel member previously worked for one of the bidders three years ago and has maintained social ties with that bidder's leadership. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the panel member to participate because the prior employment ended three years ago and is now immaterial.
- **B**: Require the panel member to disclose the relationship in writing and recuse them from evaluating that bidder.
- **C**: Require the panel member to score the related bidder more harshly as a compensatory fairness measure overall.
- **D**: Exclude the panel member from the panel entirely to avoid any perception of conflict or bias whatsoever.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI ethics require disclosure of potential conflicts and recusal from the affected portion while preserving the person's contribution on unaffected portions. (A) ignores a real perception-of-conflict issue. (C) applies a compensatory bias that is itself a breach of fairness. (D) excludes entirely rather than using the standard disclose-and-recuse mechanism.
