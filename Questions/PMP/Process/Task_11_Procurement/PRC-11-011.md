# Question

A project needs custom software where requirements are intentionally evolving and will be elaborated during delivery. The PM is choosing a contract type for the build vendor. Which is most appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: Firm Fixed Price with a comprehensive SOW that the vendor must meet exactly.
- **B**: Cost Plus Percentage of Cost so the vendor is paid for whatever the work needs.
- **C**: Cost Plus Fixed Fee with no spend cap and quarterly true-ups.
- **D**: Time and Materials with a not-to-exceed cap and short statements of work per iteration.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

T&M with a not-to-exceed clause balances the flexibility evolving scope demands with budget protection for the buyer. (A) FFP requires defined scope; without it, the vendor must price heavy risk premiums or fail to deliver. (B) CPPC creates an incentive for the seller to grow cost. (C) CPFF is plausible but typically less appropriate when iteration-by-iteration scope adjustment is the norm; without a cap, budget control suffers.
