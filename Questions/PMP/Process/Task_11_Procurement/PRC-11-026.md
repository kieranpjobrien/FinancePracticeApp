# Question

What is the PRIMARY difference between a purchase order and a formal contract in procurement?

## Options

- **A**: Purchase orders are never legally binding on either party, while signed contracts always are.
- **B**: A purchase order is a simpler instrument for routine transactions, while a contract covers complex or custom arrangements.
- **C**: Purchase orders are used only for physical goods, while contracts are used exclusively for services and labour.
- **D**: Purchase orders bypass internal approval workflows that more formal contracts are required to go through.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A PO is a legally binding but lightweight instrument used for routine, defined transactions; a contract is a fuller agreement appropriate for complex deliverables, risk allocation, and custom terms. (A) is wrong because POs are binding once accepted. (C) is wrong; both can be used for goods or services. (D) is wrong; both follow internal approval processes.
