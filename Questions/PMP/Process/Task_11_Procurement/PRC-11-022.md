# Question

A vendor on a cost-reimbursable contract is invoicing significant overtime that has pushed actuals 18% above estimate. The contract permits "reasonable overtime with buyer approval" but the approval workflow has not been used. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Reject the overtime portion of the invoice entirely on the basis that prior buyer approval was not obtained.
- **B**: Pay the invoices in full to maintain the vendor relationship and address the broken approval process going forward.
- **C**: Verify whether the overtime was reasonable, formalise the approval workflow, and pay justified hours only.
- **D**: Convert the agreement to a firm fixed-price contract retroactively in order to cap any further cost growth.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cost-reimbursable contracts require active oversight. The PM's job is to verify the work, fix the broken approval mechanism, and pay legitimate effort while contesting any unjustified hours. (A) may breach the contract if the overtime was implicitly tolerated. (B) abdicates oversight. (D) cannot be done unilaterally and would not apply retroactively to work already performed.
