# Question

A project faces significant uncertainty in the ground conditions for a tunnelling package. The buyer would prefer the seller to carry the risk of unforeseen conditions, but seasoned contractors warn that any seller asked to price that risk blind will load the bid heavily or refuse to bid. What is the most balanced procurement response?

## Options

- **A**: Insist on firm fixed price and accept whatever risk premium the market builds into the bids.
- **B**: Use pure cost-reimbursable terms so the buyer carries all the ground-condition risk and bids stay low.
- **C**: Commission a geotechnical survey to reduce the uncertainty before tendering, then allocate the residual risk in the contract on an informed basis.
- **D**: Add a generous contingency to the buyer's budget and proceed to tender without further investigation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The most balanced response reduces the underlying uncertainty through investigation before allocating risk, so the contract assigns residual risk on an informed basis and avoids the heavy blind risk premium (C). Insisting on fixed price and accepting the premium (A) wastes money pricing risk that a survey could have retired. Pure cost-reimbursable terms (B) swing all the risk onto the buyer unnecessarily. Padding the buyer's contingency without investigating (D) does nothing to reduce the actual uncertainty and still leaves bidders guessing. Sound risk allocation follows reducing what can be known.
