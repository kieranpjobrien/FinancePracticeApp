# Question

Two finalist vendors are tied on technical fit. Vendor A bids $480,000 with no relevant prior delivery experience. Vendor B bids $580,000 and has delivered three similar projects on time. The procurement evaluation criteria weight cost and past performance roughly equally. What should weigh most heavily for the PM's recommendation?

## Options

- **A**: Vendor A, because price savings of $100,000 directly reduce project cost against a fixed budget.
- **B**: Vendor B, because demonstrated past performance reduces execution risk that could exceed the price difference.
- **C**: Vendor A, because picking the lowest compliant bid is a standard and defensible procurement practice.
- **D**: Both, by splitting the work between the two vendors to hedge delivery risk across the programme.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When evaluation criteria weight performance equally with price, demonstrated past performance is a strong proxy for execution risk; an inexperienced vendor often costs more in delays, oversight, and rework than the headline price difference. (A) and (C) treat lowest cost as sufficient and ignore the equally-weighted performance criterion. (D) introduces coordination overhead and is not a standard risk response for vendor selection.
