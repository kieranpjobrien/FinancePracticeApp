# Question

A hybrid project engages a contractor under a time-and-materials arrangement to augment an agile team for an uncertain period. The sponsor values the staffing flexibility but has set a hard funding limit for the engagement and wants assurance the spend cannot quietly exceed it, while still letting the team direct the contractor's effort sprint by sprint. Which addition to the contract best fits both needs?

## Options

- **A**: A monthly spend cap that resets each month, keeping any single month within budget.
- **B**: A not-to-exceed ceiling on cumulative billings, with effort reprioritised from the backlog each sprint within that ceiling.
- **C**: A retainer for a fixed number of contractor hours per sprint, billed whether or not all hours are used.
- **D**: A requirement that the contractor commit, at the start, to a fixed set of features for the whole engagement at the agreed rates.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The sponsor's hard limit is on the total engagement, and a not-to-exceed ceiling on cumulative billings caps exactly that, while sprint-by-sprint reprioritisation from the backlog preserves the day-to-day flexibility the team needs (B). A monthly cap that resets (A) controls the rate of spend but not the cumulative total, so the engagement could still exceed the overall funding limit over time, missing the sponsor's actual concern. A fixed-hours retainer billed regardless of use (C) makes spend predictable but converts flexible capacity into a standing cost and can pay for hours the team does not need, which is the opposite of efficient flexibility. Committing the contractor to a fixed feature set up front (D) removes the very adaptability the time-and-materials form was chosen to provide and recreates a fixed-scope arrangement. The discriminator is that only a cumulative ceiling protects the total budget while leaving iteration-level direction intact.
