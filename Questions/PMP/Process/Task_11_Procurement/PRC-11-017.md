# Question

A vendor delivered a milestone two weeks ago. The contract specifies "net 30 days from acceptance" payment, but the buyer's team has not started acceptance testing. The vendor is asking for payment. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Authorise payment because the deliverable was submitted and delays are on the buyer's side.
- **B**: Tell the vendor that payment cannot be released until acceptance is complete and continue at the team's pace.
- **C**: Reprioritise team capacity to complete acceptance testing promptly so the contractual payment clock can start.
- **D**: Renegotiate the contract to change payment terms to "net 30 from delivery" so the dispute does not recur.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The buyer is contractually obliged to test in good faith; allowing the team's backlog to indefinitely delay a vendor payment is unfair and damages the relationship without any benefit. The PM removes the impediment by reprioritising. (A) pays without verifying acceptance, which the contract specifically guards against. (B) is technically defensible but uses the contract to mask a buyer-side scheduling problem. (D) changes terms rather than fixing the team behaviour that created the issue.
