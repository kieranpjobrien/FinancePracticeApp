# Question

A hybrid programme's T&M vendor has quietly expanded what they work on because team members keep asking them to help with adjacent tasks. The vendor is billing the hours legitimately. The PM reviews the invoices and sees the drift. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Pay the invoices because each hour was asked for by a legitimate team member and was worked diligently.
- **B**: Establish clear work authorisation rules, communicate them, and require written tasking before vendor work begins.
- **C**: Refuse further payments until the vendor formally confirms they will stop accepting adjacent tasking requests.
- **D**: Convert the T&M contract to FFP immediately so that scope drift stops by contract type changing itself.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T&M contracts require active work authorisation control by the buyer; without it, adjacent tasking is structural. The root cause is control, not vendor behaviour. (A) pays without addressing the missing control. (C) punishes the vendor for the buyer's control gap. (D) changes contract type without negotiation and does not fix tasking discipline.
