# Question

A programme manager is selecting a contract type for each of four procurements. Each situation favours a different incentive or risk structure. Match each situation (left) to the contract type whose risk and incentive profile fits it best (right).

## Items

- **1**: Scope and target cost are reasonably definable; the buyer wants the seller to share in both savings and overruns but insists on a price the seller can never exceed.
- **2**: Scope is uncertain and cost is hard to predict; the buyer will reimburse costs and wants a formula-based incentive for the seller to keep the final cost near a target.
- **3**: A multi-year fixed-scope commodity supply where the main worry is volatile raw-material and labour indices over the term, not the seller's efficiency.
- **4**: Defined scope where the buyer simply wants the seller to carry all cost-overrun risk for the agreed deliverable, with no incentive formula to administer.

## Matches

- **A**: Cost plus incentive fee
- **B**: Firm fixed price
- **C**: Fixed price incentive fee
- **D**: Fixed price with economic price adjustment

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-D, 4-B

## Explanation

Fixed price incentive fee combines a target cost, a sharing ratio, and a ceiling price the seller cannot exceed, which matches a definable scope where the buyer wants shared savings and overruns but a hard cap (1-C). Cost plus incentive fee reimburses costs and adds a formula-based fee tied to the target/actual variance, fitting genuinely uncertain scope where the buyer still wants a cost-control incentive (2-A). Fixed price with economic price adjustment fixes the price but indexes it to defined economic factors, which is designed precisely for long-term, fixed-scope buys exposed to volatile material and labour costs (3-D). Firm fixed price places all overrun risk on the seller with no incentive formula to administer, suiting defined scope where simplicity and seller-borne risk are the goals (4-B). The trap is that situations 1 and 2 both mention sharing and incentives, so the discriminator is the ceiling price (fixed-price family) versus cost reimbursement; and situation 3 is fixed-scope yet needs FP-EPA, not plain FFP, because the risk is external indices rather than seller efficiency.
