# Question

A project manager is drafting a master service agreement to bring an external development vendor into an ongoing agile programme. The intent is to preserve the ability to adjust scope each iteration while keeping the commercial relationship fair to both parties. Which provisions support an agile-friendly contract? (Select THREE.)

## Options

- **A**: A baseline scope fixed for the full term, with all variations handled solely through formal change requests priced individually.
- **B**: A change-for-free mechanism allowing scope swaps of equivalent size without repricing.
- **C**: A money-for-nothing early-termination clause letting the buyer exit and pay a fraction of remaining value once enough has been delivered.
- **D**: Prioritised, capacity-based work pulled from a shared backlog rather than a fixed deliverable list.
- **E**: Acceptance and payment milestones tied to delivery of the complete specification at a single final hand-off.

## Answer

B, C, D

## Explanation

Agile contracting keeps scope flexible while protecting both sides: a change-for-free mechanism lets equivalent scope be swapped without renegotiation (B); a money-for-nothing clause lets the buyer stop once enough value is delivered while fairly compensating the seller (C); and pulling prioritised work from a shared backlog by capacity reflects iterative delivery (D). A baseline scope fixed for the full term with every variation routed through individually priced change requests (A) recreates predictive change control and adds friction to exactly the per-iteration adjustment agile depends on. Acceptance and payment tied to one final hand-off of the complete specification (E) defers all value and feedback to the end and entrenches a fixed plan, the opposite of incremental delivery and acceptance.
