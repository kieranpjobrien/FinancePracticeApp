# Question

During an open tender with a published Q&A deadline still days away, one prospective bidder emails the project manager directly with a genuine clarification question about a technical requirement. A clear, accurate answer would materially help any bidder, and the requirement is the same for all. The buyer's procurement policy requires equal treatment of bidders. How should the project manager handle the question?

## Options

- **A**: Anonymise the question and publish it with the answer to all prospective bidders through the formal tender channel before the Q&A deadline.
- **B**: Reply to the asking bidder through the formal channel and copy the answer to all other prospective bidders at the same time.
- **C**: Hold the question and answer it together with all other received questions in a single consolidated Q&A release after the deadline.
- **D**: Acknowledge the question, then issue a formal addendum amending the requirement so the clarification is built into the tender itself.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Fairness requires that every bidder receives the same information without revealing who asked, so anonymising the question and publishing it with the answer to all bidders through the formal channel, in time to use before the Q&A deadline, is the cleanest equal-treatment move (A). Replying to the asker and copying everyone (B) discloses which bidder raised the issue, which can chill participation and hint at a bidder's thinking, so it is inferior to anonymised publication even though it does reach everyone. Holding all answers until after the Q&A deadline (C) is a legitimate batching practice but here it needlessly delays a clarification that bidders need while preparing, and may push it past the point of usefulness. Issuing a formal addendum that amends the requirement (D) is disproportionate when the requirement is sound and only needs clarifying, not changing, and amending it could introduce new ambiguity. The fine margin is between anonymised publication (A) and attributed copy-all (B).
