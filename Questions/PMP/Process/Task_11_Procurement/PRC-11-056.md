# Question

A vendor delivering under an FFP contract claims a specific deliverable is out of scope; the client's SME insists it was always implied. The statement of work describes it only at high level. The PM is refereeing. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Direct the vendor to deliver because the client's SME is the recognised domain authority on scope.
- **B**: Accept the vendor's narrower reading because ambiguous SOW language should be read in favour of the vendor.
- **C**: Facilitate joint interpretation, capture the agreement in writing, and raise a contract change if warranted.
- **D**: Escalate the dispute directly to legal counsel so contract lawyers can decide the correct interpretation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

SOW ambiguity is resolved through joint interpretation with written confirmation; formal contract change follows if the agreed interpretation expands scope. (A) imposes the client's reading unilaterally. (B) applies a legal construction rule without attempting resolution. (D) escalates before the PM has attempted facilitated resolution.
