# Question

A PM planning delivery for a specialised analytics component finds the organisation has two internal engineers with relevant expertise but no spare capacity, and three external vendors that quote reasonable prices. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Conduct formal make-or-buy analysis comparing cost, schedule, risk, and capability impact across both paths.
- **B**: Buy from the least expensive external vendor because internal capacity is already committed to other work.
- **C**: Make internally by reassigning one engineer from another project to free the capacity needed for this component.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor to decide make-or-buy since resource constraints make this a political rather than technical decision.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Make-or-buy analysis is a structured decision tool considering cost, schedule, risk, capability, and strategic alignment; doing the analysis first produces the evidence to decide. (B) picks cheapest without full analysis. (C) reassigns a scarce resource without comparing alternatives. (D) escalates a decision the PM is expected to analyse and recommend.
