# Question

A vendor on an FFP construction contract invokes force majeure due to regional flooding and claims a three-month schedule extension with no cost impact. The PM is unsure whether the flooding meets the contract's force majeure definition. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the claim at face value, as challenging a vendor's force majeure assertion invites costly commercial disputes.
- **B**: Reject the claim immediately because force majeure is often misused by vendors to cover up performance issues.
- **C**: Grant a one-month extension as a goodwill compromise without reviewing the contract language at all.
- **D**: Review the contract's force majeure clause, request supporting evidence, and assess the claim against its criteria.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Force majeure claims are adjudicated against the specific contract clause and supporting evidence; PMI expects procurement claims to be handled through the contract's prescribed process. (A) accepts without assessment and may pay for non-qualifying events. (B) rejects without assessment. (C) invents a compromise that has no contractual basis.
