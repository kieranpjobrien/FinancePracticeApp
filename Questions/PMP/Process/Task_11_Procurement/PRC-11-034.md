# Question

What is the primary use of an independent cost estimate prepared by the buyer before bids are received?

## Options

- **A**: To set a hard cap on the price the buyer will accept from any vendor.
- **B**: To provide a benchmark against which received proposals can be evaluated for reasonableness.
- **C**: To determine which qualified vendor will be invited to negotiate first.
- **D**: To finalise the project budget before issuing the RFP.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The independent estimate is a sanity check: if all bids are far above or below it, that signals scope misunderstanding, market shifts, or lowballing that must be investigated. (A) overstates the role; the estimate informs reasonableness, not a hard cap. (C) confuses the estimate with selection criteria. (D) is a budgeting activity that may use the estimate but is not its primary purpose.
