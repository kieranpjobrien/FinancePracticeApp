# Question

An FFP prime contractor on a rail project has subcontracted critical testing work to a third party the PM has not met. Quality defects are surfacing in the subcontractor's output. The prime insists all issues are their concern only. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the prime on subcontractor oversight, review quality metrics, and escalate through prime if unresolved.
- **B**: Bypass the prime and engage the subcontractor directly to push for faster quality improvement at the source.
- **C**: Accept the prime's position because the contract is with them and subcontractor management is their domain entirely.
- **D**: Terminate the prime contract so a new prime without the current subcontractor can be brought onto the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Privity of contract is with the prime, but the buyer still has legitimate interest in outcomes; working through the prime on oversight respects the structure while addressing quality. (B) breaches privity and confuses accountability. (C) accepts passive acceptance of visible defects. (D) terminates before working through the prime.
