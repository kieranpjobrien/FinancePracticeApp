# Question

A PM is planning procurement for a complex R&D effort where scope is uncertain and evolving. A firm fixed price contract template is the organisation's default. The PM is drafting the procurement management plan now. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Use FFP with generous assumptions built in so the vendor has margin to absorb scope uncertainty anyway.
- **B**: Use FFP to protect the buyer from cost overrun, and manage scope uncertainty through change orders.
- **C**: Issue a short FFP for discovery work first and then a separate FFP for main delivery afterwards.
- **D**: Use a cost-reimbursable contract type that fits scope uncertainty, with incentives for cost and schedule.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Contract type should follow scope certainty; cost-reimbursable with incentives fits R&D uncertainty far better than FFP, which transfers risk the vendor cannot reasonably price. (A) assumes vendors will price generous assumptions, which creates disputes. (B) sets up frequent change orders that consume governance capacity. (C) repeats FFP's unsuitability across two contracts instead of one.
