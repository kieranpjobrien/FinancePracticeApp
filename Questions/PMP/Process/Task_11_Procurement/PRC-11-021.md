# Question

In a Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF) contract, what mechanism motivates the seller to control costs?

## Options

- **A**: A pre-agreed sharing ratio that splits cost savings and overruns between the parties relative to target cost.
- **B**: A flat profit fee that the seller receives regardless of the final actual cost on delivery.
- **C**: The buyer's unilateral right to terminate the contract if costs exceed a defined threshold value.
- **D**: Mandatory third-party audits of the seller's books and records performed on every invoice cycle.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

CPIF aligns the seller's economic incentive with the buyer's by sharing cost savings and overruns according to a defined ratio relative to the target cost. (B) describes CPFF, where there is no cost-control incentive beyond the cap. (C) is a control, not an incentive, and termination clauses sit in many contract types. (D) is an oversight mechanism, not an incentive structure.
