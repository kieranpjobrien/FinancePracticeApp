# Question

A procurement lead is at different stages with four separate purchases. For each situation, match it to the procurement document or instrument that best fits that stage and need.

## Items

- **1**: The buyer does not yet know what solutions exist and wants to survey the market before deciding how to specify the requirement.
- **2**: The requirement is a defined commodity bought to a fixed specification, and price is the only meaningful differentiator.
- **3**: The requirement is complex and outcome-based, and the buyer wants to evaluate differing technical approaches alongside price.
- **4**: The buyer expects a long-running relationship with one supplier and wants an umbrella agreement under which individual pieces of work can be ordered over time.

## Matches

- **A**: Request for quotation
- **B**: Request for information
- **C**: Master services agreement
- **D**: Request for proposal

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-D, 4-C

## Explanation

A request for information is used to survey the market and learn what solutions exist before the requirement is specified, fitting the buyer who does not yet know how to specify (1-B). A request for quotation solicits a price against a settled specification for a defined commodity where price is the differentiator (2-A). A request for proposal is built for complex, outcome-based needs where differing technical approaches are evaluated alongside price (3-D). A master services agreement is an umbrella contract under which individual statements of work or orders are placed over a continuing relationship, matching the long-running single-supplier arrangement (4-C). The traps are that situations 2 and 3 both solicit offers, so the discriminator is commodity-and-price-only (RFQ) versus complex-approach-evaluated (RFP); and situation 1 gathers information rather than soliciting an award, while situation 4 establishes a framework rather than buying a single deliverable.
