# Question

A long-running T&M vendor on a programme has been late on several deliverables but the vendor's account manager has built a close relationship with the PM. The programme sponsor questions whether the PM is being too lenient in enforcement. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the current approach because the relationship has helped resolve several issues informally already.
- **B**: Transfer vendor relationship management to a different PM so independence can be restored for the programme.
- **C**: Terminate the vendor relationship so that personal ties no longer cloud contract management judgement here.
- **D**: Apply contract performance terms consistently, document enforcement, and separate relationship from governance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Professional conduct requires separating relationship from governance; PMI ethics emphasise impartiality and consistent application of contract terms. (A) continues a pattern that the sponsor has already flagged. (B) offloads the responsibility rather than correcting the PM's own conduct. (C) overreacts by terminating rather than enforcing.
