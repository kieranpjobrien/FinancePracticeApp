# Question

Under a firm fixed price contract, a vendor claims to have completed a milestone and has invoiced accordingly. The PM's inspection shows three acceptance criteria have not been met. The vendor disputes the criteria interpretation. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve partial payment to acknowledge progress and avoid damaging the vendor relationship further.
- **B**: Withhold payment per contract terms, document the acceptance criteria gap, and convene a joint review.
- **C**: Pay in full and escalate the dispute to the contracting officer for later recovery from the vendor.
- **D**: Reject the milestone claim outright and terminate the contract for cause based on the gap.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FFP milestones link payment to acceptance; the contract governs. Documenting the gap and convening a joint review preserves both governance and the path to resolution. (A) pays outside acceptance criteria and undermines the contract. (C) pays first and tries to recover later, which is commercially weak. (D) overreacts to a dispute that has not been worked through jointly.
