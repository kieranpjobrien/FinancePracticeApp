# Question

A PM is procuring a vendor to augment a Scrum team on a financial services product. The procurement group recommends FFP with a detailed upfront specification. The team is delivering through empirical iteration with evolving scope. What should the PM recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Use a T&M or capacity-based contract model that fits iterative delivery and evolving scope naturally.
- **B**: Keep FFP and write an exhaustive upfront specification so the vendor can bid with appropriate confidence.
- **C**: Bring the vendor in as internal staff on loan to avoid contract type selection entirely for this engagement.
- **D**: Defer vendor procurement until all product requirements are fully known and stable for a proper FFP bid.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Agile augmentation fits capacity-based or T&M contract models where scope emerges; forcing FFP onto iterative work creates perpetual change orders. (B) writes a specification the team has deliberately chosen not to write. (C) circumvents procurement governance to dodge the contract type question. (D) blocks team scaling until an impossible scope stability is reached.
