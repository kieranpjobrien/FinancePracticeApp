# Question

A supplier has delivered the final increment of an outsourced software component and is asking the buyer to sign off so the contract can be closed. The project manager wants to close the procurement properly. What must happen before the contract is formally closed?

## Options

- **A**: Release the supplier's final payment immediately, since prompt payment is the buyer's main remaining obligation.
- **B**: Archive the procurement correspondence and update the lessons-learned register, then notify the supplier the contract is closed.
- **C**: Verify the delivered work against the contract requirements and acceptance criteria, then formally accept it and complete any administrative closure activities.
- **D**: Obtain the sponsor's signature on the project closure report, which serves as acceptance of all supplier deliverables.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Procurement closure requires that the deliverable first be verified against the contract requirements and acceptance criteria and then formally accepted, after which administrative closure proceeds (C). Releasing final payment (A) follows acceptance, not precedes it. Archiving records and capturing lessons (B) are part of closure but come after the work has been verified and accepted, not instead of it. The sponsor's project closure report (D) operates at the project level and does not substitute for contractual acceptance of the specific deliverable. Verification and formal acceptance gate the closure.
