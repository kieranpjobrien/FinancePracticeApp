# Question

An FFP vendor is delivering the final acceptance package for a construction project. The warranty period begins on formal acceptance. The PM notices two minor punch list items remain. The vendor wants acceptance signed today. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sign acceptance today and trust the vendor to fix the punch list items during the warranty period anyway.
- **B**: Refuse any acceptance until every single punch list item is fully closed, regardless of how minor each is.
- **C**: Sign conditional acceptance that lists the open items and the remedy path before the warranty period begins.
- **D**: Delay the acceptance decision for one month without a defined path to give the vendor time to complete items.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Conditional acceptance with a recorded remedy path is a standard procurement practice that protects buyer rights while respecting the vendor's progress. (A) signs clean acceptance while known gaps exist. (B) holds acceptance hostage to trivial items regardless of impact. (D) delays without structure, leaving ambiguity over obligations and warranty start.
