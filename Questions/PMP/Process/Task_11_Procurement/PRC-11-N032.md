# Question

A project manager is finalising the source-selection criteria for an RFP to engage a managed-services provider for a five-year support contract. The buyer wants the award to reflect long-term value, not just headline price, and wants every criterion to discriminate fairly between bidders on what predicts sustained performance. Which of the following belong in the weighted source-selection criteria? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: The total life-cycle cost of the service across the five-year term, including renewals and exit, rather than the first-year price.
- **B**: The provider's financial stability and demonstrated capacity to sustain the service for the full term.
- **C**: The provider's proposed transition and continuity approach, evidenced by how it has run comparable services.
- **D**: Whether the provider already holds an account relationship elsewhere in the buyer's parent group.
- **E**: The headline day rate of the provider's consultants taken on its own.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Criteria that predict sustained value over a five-year term are whole-life cost rather than first-year price (A), the provider's financial stability and capacity to last the term (B), and an evidenced transition and continuity approach (C). An existing relationship elsewhere in the parent group (D) is tempting as a proxy for trust but it is not an objective predictor of performance on this service and can bias the award toward an incumbent rather than the best provider, so it does not belong as a scored criterion. The headline day rate on its own (E) is a real number but, isolated from volume and total cost, it misrepresents value and pulls the evaluation back toward exactly the headline-price thinking the buyer wants to avoid. The valid criteria each tie objectively to long-term performance.
