# Question

Under a firm fixed-price contract, who bears the risk of cost overruns on the defined scope?

## Options

- **A**: The seller
- **B**: The buyer
- **C**: Risk is shared equally between buyer and seller
- **D**: The project sponsor

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In a firm fixed-price contract the seller commits to deliver the defined scope for a set price; if their costs exceed that price, they absorb the loss. (B) describes cost-reimbursable contracts where the buyer reimburses actuals. (C) describes incentive-fee structures with sharing ratios, not pure FFP. (D) the sponsor is not a contract party and bears no contractual cost risk.
