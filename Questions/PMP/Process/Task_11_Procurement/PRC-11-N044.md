# Question

Four compliant bids have arrived for a systems integration package. The published RFP set out weighted source selection criteria covering price, technical capability, and risk approach, and stated that award would be on a best-value basis. The evaluation panel has independently scored all four bids against those criteria and the weighted totals are complete. The lowest-priced bid scored poorly on capability and risk; the second-lowest scored highest overall. The sponsor, seeing only the prices, is pressing the project manager to award to the cheapest compliant bidder. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Award to the highest-scoring bid under the project manager's delegated authority and inform the sponsor of the rationale afterward.
- **B**: Reconvene the panel to revisit the weightings so price carries more influence, then rescore and recommend on that basis.
- **C**: Present the completed weighted scoring against the published criteria to the sponsor and recommend award to the highest-scoring bid.
- **D**: Recommend the lowest-priced compliant bid, since it satisfies the mandatory requirements and aligns with the sponsor's stated preference.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The structured evaluation is already done and points to the second-lowest bid, so the project manager's job is to make the basis of the decision transparent to the decision-maker: present the weighted results against the criteria the bidders were told would govern the award, and recommend accordingly (C). Awarding unilaterally and informing the sponsor afterward (A) sidelines a sponsor who has a legitimate stake and invites a governance dispute, even if the underlying choice is correct. Revisiting the weightings after bids are open and scored (B) is a serious integrity breach: changing the rules post-submission to reach a predetermined price outcome would expose the buyer to a protest. Recommending the cheapest bid to satisfy the sponsor (D) abandons the agreed best-value basis and the capability and risk factors the criteria were built to weigh. The fine margin is between informing and persuading the sponsor with the evidence (C) versus bypassing the sponsor (A).
