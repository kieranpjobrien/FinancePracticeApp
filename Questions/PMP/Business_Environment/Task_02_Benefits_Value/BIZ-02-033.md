# Question

Halfway through a 12-month transformation programme, the PM realises the agreed KPIs do not actually measure the customer outcome the business case promised. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the existing KPIs to keep reporting consistent and revisit them at programme close.
- **B**: Work with the benefits owner to redefine the KPIs and route the change through governance.
- **C**: Add the missing customer-outcome KPI alongside the original KPIs without changing the existing ones.
- **D**: Replace the KPIs unilaterally now and inform the benefits owner of the change.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

KPIs are tied to the business case and have an owner; redefining them is a governance action, not a unilateral one (B). (A) keeps measuring the wrong thing. (C) avoids the harder conversation and clutters reporting. (D) bypasses the benefits owner who is accountable.
