# Question

Two projects in a programme each promise £1M of cost savings from automating overlapping processes. The PM realises the savings cannot both be claimed without double-counting. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Engage the programme manager and benefits owners to apportion the savings between the projects.
- **B**: Allow each project to claim the full £1M and let finance reconcile later.
- **C**: Average the savings and assign £500k to each project.
- **D**: Reduce the programme's total claimed savings to a single £1M without attributing it.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Overlapping benefits need a deliberate apportionment with the programme manager and owners (A). (B) creates known double-counting in the books. (C) is arithmetically tidy but may not reflect each project's contribution. (D) hides the issue rather than resolving attribution.
