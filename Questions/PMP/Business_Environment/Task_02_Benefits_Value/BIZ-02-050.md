# Question

An agile team developing a patient-appointment app for a private hospital group has, after four sprints, a working product that lets patients book and reschedule appointments online — the two highest-value capabilities in the roadmap. Remaining roadmap items (in-app payment, insurance pre-authorisation, loyalty rewards) are valuable but lower priority. The product owner wants to release now to start capturing value; the sponsor worries a partial product will look unfinished. What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the release until payment and pre-authorisation are also complete, so patients experience an end-to-end journey and the product does not appear partial.
- **B**: Release the current increment to begin realising booking and rescheduling value now, while the remaining items continue to be developed and released incrementally.
- **C**: Release now but to a small pilot group only, deferring broad value capture until the full roadmap is delivered and the product is judged complete.
- **D**: Re-sequence the backlog to bring loyalty rewards forward, since visible patient-facing perks will make the early release feel more finished.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The point of incremental delivery is to start realising value as soon as a usable increment exists, rather than deferring all benefit until a notional "complete" product — booking and rescheduling are the two highest-value capabilities and are ready, so releasing them now begins benefit realisation while later items follow (B). A withholds realisable value to satisfy a perception of completeness, which is the classic trade of real benefit for cosmetic wholeness. C concedes the product is usable yet still postpones the bulk of value capture behind a full-roadmap gate, defeating the purpose of incremental release. D reorders the backlog away from value toward appearance, prioritising "feels finished" over the prioritised benefit the roadmap already reflects.
