# Question

A logistics-optimisation project has closed and handed over. Several of its benefits will only emerge over the next eighteen months. The PM wants to set up the handover so realisation is genuinely managed after the team disbands. Which actions appropriately support continued benefit realisation beyond closure? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Transfer the benefits register and tracking responsibility to a named operational owner.
- **B**: Schedule post-implementation benefit reviews at agreed points after go-live.
- **C**: Keep the project formally open and the full team on standby until every benefit lands.
- **D**: Define the leading and lagging indicators the owner will monitor over the realisation period.
- **E**: Mark all forecast benefits as realised in the closure report to finalise the business case.

## Answer

A, B, D

## Explanation

Handover of the register to an accountable owner (A), scheduled post-implementation reviews (B), and agreed indicators to monitor (D) together let realisation continue after the project ends. Keeping the project open with the team on standby (C) misuses temporary project structures and wastes resources for work that is now operational. Declaring forecast benefits realised at closure (E) records projections as outcomes that have not yet happened, defeating genuine tracking.
