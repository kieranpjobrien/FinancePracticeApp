# Question

Halfway through a 14-month consumer product build, fresh research shows customer needs have shifted enough that the planned product will likely underperform. Significant investment has already been made. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the current plan; the team should not waste the investment already made.
- **B**: Cancel the project because the original business case no longer holds.
- **C**: Bring the research and pivot options to stakeholders and recommend re-aligning to current customer needs.
- **D**: Finish the current product as scoped and start a follow-on project for the new needs.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Past spend is sunk; future value is what matters (C). The PM's job is to put the new evidence and credible options in front of decision-makers. (A) is the textbook sunk-cost trap. (B) jumps to cancellation without exploring a salvageable pivot. (D) keeps spending on a product the market no longer wants.
