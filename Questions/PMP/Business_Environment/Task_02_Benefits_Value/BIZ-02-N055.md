# Question

A PM is briefing a new sponsor on how benefits are managed across the life of an initiative. To make the conversation concrete, the PM wants to map each activity to the point in the benefits lifecycle where it primarily belongs. Match each activity to the lifecycle stage it most directly supports.

## Items

- **1**: Quantifying the expected benefits and defining how each will be measured
- **2**: Building the deliverable that will enable the benefit
- **3**: Confirming, after go-live, that the forecast benefit actually occurred and feeding the result back

## Matches

- **A**: Benefits identification and planning
- **B**: Benefits delivery (enabling work during execution)
- **C**: Benefits realisation and review (post-delivery)

## Answer

1-A, 2-B, 3-C

## Explanation

Quantifying benefits and defining their measures is the planning activity that sets the baseline and targets, so it belongs to identification and planning (1-A). Constructing the deliverable that *enables* the benefit is execution work — the project produces the capability from which value will later flow (2-B). Confirming after go-live that the benefit materialised, and feeding that back, is realisation and review, which extends beyond delivery into operation (3-C). The discipline being tested is that delivering the output (stage B) is distinct from realising the outcome (stage C): a project can complete its build yet still need a separate, later step to verify and harvest the value, which is why 2 and 3 map to different stages.
