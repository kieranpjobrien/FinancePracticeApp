# Question

Three sprints into a 14-sprint programme, the leading indicator agreed in the benefits plan (weekly active users in the beta cohort) is trending 35% below expectation. The lagging revenue target is still nine months away. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue delivery as planned because the lagging revenue metric remains the true measure of realised value.
- **B**: Remove the leading indicator from reporting so governance is not distracted by noisy early-stage user data.
- **C**: Treat the indicator as an early signal, investigate drivers with the product owner, and adjust scope or enablement.
- **D**: Defer any decisions until at least three more sprints have passed to rule out normal user adoption variance.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Leading indicators exist to trigger early correction; 35% below plan is a signal to investigate drivers and adjust (C). (A) ignores the very indicator the benefits plan said to watch. (B) hides the signal instead of responding to it. (D) waits when action is what the metric is designed to prompt.
