# Question

On a hybrid programme delivering a new online citizenship-application service for a government agency, the project manager is clarifying who is accountable for what across the benefits lifecycle, because confusion has been causing decisions to stall. Match each responsibility to the role that most appropriately holds it.

## Items

- **1**: Owning the realisation of the target benefits after the service goes live and is in operational use.
- **2**: Ensuring the project delivers the outputs that enable the benefits, within its constraints.
- **3**: Championing the business case and securing the funding and organisational backing for the investment.
- **4**: Deciding, at the product level, how to sequence work to deliver the most value each iteration.

## Matches

- **A**: Project manager
- **B**: Sponsor
- **C**: Benefits owner (operational business owner)
- **D**: Product owner

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

Realisation of benefits after go-live sits with the operational benefits owner, who lives with the service in use and is accountable for the outcomes (1-C), while the project manager is responsible for delivering the enabling outputs within the project's constraints (2-A). The sponsor champions the business case and secures funding and organisational support for the investment (3-B), and the product owner decides how to sequence work to maximise value each iteration (4-D). The fine distinctions are sponsor versus benefits owner — both are senior business figures, but one secures the investment up front while the other is accountable for realising value in operation — and project manager versus product owner, who divide responsibility for delivering the outputs (PM) and prioritising what gets built for value (PO).
