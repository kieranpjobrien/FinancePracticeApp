# Question

A hospital is implementing a new electronic rostering system to cut unplanned overtime. The project will deliver the system, but the overtime savings will accrue gradually as ward managers change how they schedule staff. As the PM drafts the benefits management plan, the sponsor asks who should be accountable for the overtime savings once the project closes. Who is the most appropriate accountable owner for that benefit?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, who understands the system best and ran the implementation.
- **B**: The director of nursing operations, whose function the savings depend on and who will run the rostering after handover.
- **C**: The IT service desk, which will maintain the rostering system in live service.
- **D**: The project sponsor, who approved the funding and will report the result upwards.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Benefit accountability belongs with the operational leader whose area realises the value and who controls the behaviour the benefit depends on — here the director of nursing operations, since the savings come from how wards schedule staff after handover (B). The PM (A) is a temporary role that ends at closure and does not control ward scheduling, so cannot be accountable for a benefit that emerges over months of operation. The IT service desk (C) keeps the system running but has no influence over rostering decisions or overtime. The sponsor (D) champions and reports the benefit and may hold the owner to account, but accountability for realisation sits with the function that must change its practice, not with the person reporting upward.
