# Question

A hybrid programme is delivering two linked products for an energy retailer: a smart-meter data platform (Product 1) and a customer-facing usage app (Product 2). The app's entire benefit — reduced consumption and lower churn — depends on the platform feeding it accurate data. The app team is ahead and ready to release; the platform's data-quality work is running two iterations behind. Marketing is pressing to launch the app now to hit a seasonal campaign. The project manager must advise on sequencing. What is the soundest basis for the recommendation?

## Options

- **A**: Release the app now to capture the campaign window, since a live app begins building the user base that the benefits depend on.
- **B**: Release the app now with a visible disclaimer that data may be inaccurate, transferring the judgement to users.
- **C**: Cancel the seasonal campaign and re-plan both products onto a single, later combined release for simplicity.
- **D**: Sequence the app's release to follow the platform's data-quality readiness, because the app's benefits cannot be realised, and may be undermined, on inaccurate data.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The app's benefits are wholly dependent on accurate data from the platform, so releasing it before that dependency is ready cannot realise the intended value and risks actively eroding trust and churn if users see wrong figures — the release should follow data-quality readiness (D). A privileges a campaign date over the precondition for any benefit at all; a larger user base on a misfiring app accelerates harm rather than value. B ships known-bad data behind a disclaimer, which offloads the firm's responsibility onto users and is likely to damage the very churn and trust outcomes the app exists to improve. C overcorrects by scrapping the campaign and bundling both products into a later release, sacrificing the platform's independently realisable value and the campaign opportunity when only the app's timing needs to bend to the dependency.
