# Question

Halfway through the build, two features remain: feature X (high cost, modest benefit) and feature Y (low cost, high benefit). The plan funds both but the budget is now at risk. The sponsor refuses to choose. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Drop feature X unilaterally because the cost-benefit maths clearly favours completing feature Y alone.
- **B**: Show the sponsor a simple cost-to-benefit comparison, the risk-adjusted options, and request a governed decision.
- **C**: Split each feature into thinner slices and deliver half of each so both appear at launch.
- **D**: Proceed with both features as planned, absorb the overrun, and report the variance at the project close.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PM's job is to equip the sponsor with the evidence and options to decide, not to make the call unilaterally (B). (A) takes a scope decision that is not the PM's to take. (C) produces two half-features and probably no value from either. (D) tolerates budget breach rather than addressing the trade-off.
