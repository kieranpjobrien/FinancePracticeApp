# Question

A project delivered a self-service returns portal for a retailer. The target benefit — a 25% drop in call-centre returns enquiries — is expected to build over the six months after go-live as customers adopt the portal. The project is closing and the team is being released. The benefits realisation plan already names the head of customer operations as the benefit owner and sets the realisation window at six months. The sponsor asks the PM how realisation will actually be confirmed once the project is gone. What should the PM do before closing?

## Options

- **A**: Record the pre-launch enquiry baseline and the agreed measurement method in the benefits realisation plan, and confirm with the benefit owner how and when they will report the metric during the realisation window.
- **B**: Hand the benefits realisation plan to the head of customer operations and have them formally accept accountability for tracking and reporting the benefit from go-live.
- **C**: Schedule a post-implementation review six months out, owned by the PMO, to assess whether the 25% reduction was achieved and capture lessons for similar projects.
- **D**: Add a benefit-tracking line to the steering committee's standing report so the metric stays visible to governance until the realisation window closes.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The benefit owner and window are already set, so the gap the PM must close before the team disbands is the *measurability* of the benefit: without a recorded baseline and an agreed measurement method, a 25% reduction cannot be evidenced later, and only the closing project still holds that pre-launch data and context (A). Confirming the owner's acceptance (B) matters, but accountability over an unmeasurable target is hollow — handing over the plan does not make the benefit provable. Booking a post-implementation review (C) and keeping the metric on the governance report (D) are both legitimate realisation practices, but each *reads* a measure that A is responsible for making exist; scheduled too soon or without a baseline they confirm nothing. The fine margin is that A is the prerequisite the others depend on, and it is time-critical because the baseline and knowledge leave with the team.
