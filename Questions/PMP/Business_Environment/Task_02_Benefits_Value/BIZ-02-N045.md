# Question

A digital-banking squad is two sprints into a programme whose headline benefit is a 15% lift in current-account openings, a number that will only become readable months after launch as cohorts mature. At the sprint review the sponsor wants comfort that the benefit is on track now, well before the headline figure can move. The squad already captured an openings baseline and has a benefits realisation plan with a named owner. What is the most useful thing for the PM to put in front of the sponsor at this point?

## Options

- **A**: A leading indicator already trending in the data — such as completed online application starts per week — chosen because it reliably precedes account openings, with the caveat that it signals direction, not the final result.
- **B**: The current month's actual account-openings figure against the 15% target, so the sponsor sees real benefit data rather than a proxy.
- **C**: A refreshed ROI calculation for the programme using the latest cost and openings forecast, to show the investment still pays back.
- **D**: A burn-up of delivered versus remaining backlog for the openings-related features, showing the squad is on pace to ship what the benefit depends on.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The sponsor wants an early read on benefit *before the lagging outcome can move*, which is precisely what a leading indicator provides — a metric that causally precedes account openings and is already observable two sprints in (A). Reporting this month's openings against target (B) is the lagging benefit itself; this early it is statistically meaningless and would mislead, because cohorts have not matured. A refreshed ROI (C) restates the investment case but is a forecast built on the same not-yet-realised benefit, so it offers no genuine early evidence. A feature burn-up (D) shows delivery is on pace but tracks output, not whether the outcome is trending — shipping the features is necessary, not proof the benefit is landing. The fine margin is between A and D: both are available "now", but only A speaks to benefit trajectory rather than delivery progress.
