# Question

A PM is selecting the right measure to evaluate value in four different situations across the portfolio. Match each situation to the measure that most appropriately answers it.

## Items

- **1**: Comparing two investments whose benefits arrive over very different multi-year timescales
- **2**: Showing how quickly an initiative recovers its initial outlay for a cash-constrained sponsor
- **3**: Demonstrating early, ongoing value to stakeholders during an agile delivery before final outcomes are known
- **4**: Expressing the net return of an initiative as a ratio of benefit to cost for a go decision

## Matches

- **A**: Payback period
- **B**: Net present value
- **C**: Increment-level value demonstrated at reviews
- **D**: Return on investment

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C, 4-D

## Explanation

Net present value compares benefits arriving over different timescales by discounting them to today (1-B). Payback period answers how fast the outlay is recovered, the cash-constrained sponsor's concern (2-A). Demonstrating value at reviews suits showing ongoing worth during agile delivery before final outcomes exist (3-C). Return on investment expresses net return relative to cost for a go decision (4-D). Each measure is matched to the specific question it is designed to answer rather than a generic "best" metric.
