# Question

Midway through an agile data-platform programme, new information surfaces that casts doubt on whether the originally projected benefits are still achievable. The PM wants to bring a credible recommendation to the steering committee on whether and how to continue. Which inputs should the PM gather to reassess the value case? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: The remaining cost to complete versus the benefits still realistically achievable.
- **B**: Any change in market or organisational conditions affecting the value of the outcome.
- **C**: Evidence of value or learning already delivered by increments shipped so far.
- **D**: The amount of budget already spent, to justify pressing on and protect that investment.
- **E**: How disappointed the original sponsor would be if the programme were stopped now.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

A sound reassessment weighs future cost against still-achievable benefits (A), accounts for changed conditions affecting value (B), and recognises value or learning already banked (C). Citing sunk budget to justify continuing (D) is the sunk-cost fallacy and biases the decision. Anticipated sponsor disappointment (E) is a stakeholder-management consideration, not evidence about whether continued investment will pay off.
