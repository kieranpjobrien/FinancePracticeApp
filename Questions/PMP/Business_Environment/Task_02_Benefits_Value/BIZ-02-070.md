# Question

Your project's full benefits depend on a sister programme that has just slipped by six months. Your delivery is on track. If you go live on time, the benefits cannot yet be realised. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Hold the go-live by six months to align exactly with the sister programme's new revised delivery schedule.
- **B**: Go live on time and carry the capability unused until the sister programme's delivery catches up.
- **C**: Model the options, including partial benefits and staged enablement, and present trade-offs to joint governance.
- **D**: Escalate urgently to the CFO to pressure the sister programme back onto its originally agreed schedule.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Interdependent benefits deserve a modelled trade-off across both programmes, reviewed by joint governance (C). (A) delays without testing whether partial benefits are achievable. (B) operates unused capability at cost. (D) tries to force another programme's schedule rather than plan around the reality.
