# Question

A manufacturing automation project delivered 110% of projected cost savings, but post-implementation data shows a 15% rise in defect rates — a metric that was never in the business case. What is the right lessons-learned entry?

## Options

- **A**: Document the project as a strong success because the primary savings target was comfortably exceeded.
- **B**: Classify the defect increases as an operational matter that falls outside the project's accountability.
- **C**: Capture that business cases must track a fuller set of metrics; one-sided wins are not full success.
- **D**: Record that cost savings and quality are inherent trade-offs and should be accepted as part of automation.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The real lesson is definitional: success metrics must cover likely side-effects, not only the headline benefit (C). (A) presents an incomplete picture. (B) deflects accountability. (D) baselines the trade-off without stakeholder agreement.
