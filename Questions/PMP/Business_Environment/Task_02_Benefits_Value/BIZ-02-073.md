# Question

Your new digital channel is forecast to deliver $5M in revenue, but analysis shows $3M will be cannibalised from an existing channel in the same portfolio. The business case currently shows $5M. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate to the portfolio manager so cannibalisation is treated consistently across the portfolio's business cases.
- **B**: Keep the $5M figure because cannibalisation is a portfolio issue, not a project business case concern.
- **C**: Restate the project's net benefit at $2M in the business case, quietly and without further portfolio discussion.
- **D**: Exclude the new channel's revenue entirely until the existing channel's revenue decline can be properly observed.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cannibalisation is a genuine net-benefit issue but spans the portfolio; the PM escalates for consistent treatment (A). (B) inflates project benefit by ignoring offsets. (C) restates unilaterally without portfolio alignment, creating inconsistency. (D) erases the new benefit entirely rather than accounting for it net.
