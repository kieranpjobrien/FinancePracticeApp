# Question

The business case projected $2M annual savings. At the halfway point, market changes mean the realistic estimate is $500K. The project remains on plan for scope, schedule, and budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue executing because all delivery KPIs are green.
- **B**: Quietly revise internal forecasts to avoid alarming the sponsor mid-flight.
- **C**: Compress the schedule to capture savings sooner and offset the gap.
- **D**: Present the revised benefits analysis to the sponsor and request a go/continue/pivot decision.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

When the basis of the investment changes, the sponsor must be given the chance to decide (D). (A) ignores that the rationale for the spend has fundamentally shifted. (B) is a serious ethics breach. (C) treats the symptom; faster delivery does not bring the value back.
