# Question

Six months post-go-live, benefits show 120% of target. While preparing the report you discover the original baseline was understated, which inflates the apparent gain. What should you do?

## Options

- **A**: Disclose the baseline error, restate benefits using a corrected baseline, and capture the lesson.
- **B**: Submit the 120% number because that is what the data calculation shows.
- **C**: Keep the issue private to protect confidence in the benefits-tracking discipline.
- **D**: Attribute the over-performance to strong execution and recommend the team be recognised.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI's code of ethics and benefits-management discipline both demand accuracy (A): correct the record, report the true gain, and learn. (B) ships a misleading number. (C) trades transparency for image. (D) misattributes a measurement artefact to team performance.
