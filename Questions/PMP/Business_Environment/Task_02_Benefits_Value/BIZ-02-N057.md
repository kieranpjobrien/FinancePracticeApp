# Question

A PM is part-way through a project to migrate a logistics firm's scheduling onto a new platform. New information emerges that the expected efficiency benefit has roughly halved, while the remaining cost to complete is unchanged. The PM wants to test whether the project still makes sense to continue. Which document should the PM revisit first to make that judgement?

## Options

- **A**: The business case, which sets out the justification linking cost to expected benefit.
- **B**: The communications management plan, which defines how stakeholders are kept informed.
- **C**: The project schedule, which shows the remaining sequence of activities.
- **D**: The risk register, which catalogues threats and opportunities to the project.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The business case is the artefact that justifies the investment by relating cost to expected benefit, so when the benefit forecast changes it is the first place to test whether continued investment is still warranted (A). The communications plan (B) governs stakeholder reporting and says nothing about whether the cost-benefit balance still holds. The schedule (C) shows the sequence and timing of remaining work but not its economic justification. The risk register (D) tracks uncertainties that might affect the project, but the change here is a realised shift in expected value, which is precisely what the business case exists to evaluate, not a prospective risk.
