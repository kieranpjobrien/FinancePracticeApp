# Question

Your project has delivered 80% of planned features and exhausted its budget. Modelling shows the delivered scope will achieve about 95% of projected benefits. The sponsor asks whether to fund an extension or close. What should the project manager recommend?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend closing now and proposing the remaining 20% of features as a separate, value-justified initiative.
- **B**: Recommend funding the extension because the project should deliver everything that was originally promised.
- **C**: Recommend a minimum-viable extension to finish the features at reduced quality and within tight cost limits.
- **D**: Recommend extending only if the sponsor can secure unspent contingency from elsewhere in the portfolio.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Diminishing returns make closure the value-driven recommendation (A); any further investment should be re-justified on its own merits. (B) chases scope rather than benefit. (C) trades quality for completeness, often eroding the very benefits already captured. (D) hunts for funding without first questioning whether the spend is warranted.
