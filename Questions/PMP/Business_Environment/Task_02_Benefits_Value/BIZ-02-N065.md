# Question

A PM is setting up benefit tracking for a programme delivering an automated warehouse and wants the governance dashboard to use the right kind of measure for each purpose. Match each described measure to the category it represents.

## Items

- **1**: Picks completed per labour-hour, the operational outcome the programme is meant to improve
- **2**: Percentage of warehouse staff who have completed training on the new system, watched as an early signal
- **3**: The reduction in annual operating cost the improved productivity is expected to convert into

## Matches

- **A**: Leading indicator
- **B**: Lagging benefit measure (operational KPI)
- **C**: Financial benefit

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

Picks per labour-hour is the operational outcome the programme targets and is observed after the capability is in use, making it a lagging benefit measure or operational KPI (1-B). The proportion of staff trained is an early, predictive signal of whether the productivity gain will materialise, so it is a leading indicator (2-A). The annual operating-cost reduction is the monetary value the productivity improvement is expected to convert into, which is the financial benefit (3-C). The distinction being tested is that the operational KPI (B) and the financial benefit (C) are different things — productivity improving does not automatically equal money saved until it is translated into cost — while the training measure (A) leads both.
