# Question

A pilot of the change in one department reveals significant issues that the full rollout would also encounter. The launch date for the wider rollout is fixed. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the wider rollout on schedule and address all identified issues after go-live.
- **B**: Cancel the rollout entirely and write off the investment made so far in the change.
- **C**: Hide the pilot findings from the sponsor to avoid creating doubt before the fixed launch date.
- **D**: Brief the sponsor on the findings, propose remediation, and revisit the launch date if needed.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Pilots exist precisely to surface issues before scale; the PM presents the findings and the options to the sponsor (D). (A) ignores the pilot's purpose. (B) is disproportionate. (C) is dishonest and exposes the wider rollout to known issues.
