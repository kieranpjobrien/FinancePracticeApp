# Question

The process improvement project will make 40 roles redundant. HR plans to announce after go-live to minimise productivity dip during final testing. Rumours are already circulating and morale is deteriorating. What should the PM advise?

## Options

- **A**: Maintain the HR plan because productivity through final testing is essential to the overall project success.
- **B**: Announce immediately yourself in a project all-hands to restore transparency directly and stop any further rumours.
- **C**: Engage HR and the sponsor to bring the announcement forward with proper support before rumours worsen.
- **D**: Ignore the rumours and focus on technical delivery because the people issues are outside the project's scope.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

People impacts deserve transparent, supported communication through the right channels, ideally earlier than late (C). (A) prioritises short-term productivity over trust and duty of care. (B) announces outside the HR and sponsor process. (D) treats the human dimension as out of scope when it is central to change.
