# Question

The executive sponsor of your transformation programme is publicly supportive but privately avoids confronting a peer who is actively blocking the change. Impact is now visible. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Raise the behaviour privately with the sponsor, share evidence of impact, and agree a specific coalition-building step.
- **B**: Go around the sponsor and approach the peer executive directly to ask them to stop blocking the change.
- **C**: Escalate to the CEO that the sponsor is failing in their role and request a replacement sponsor immediately.
- **D**: Accept the situation and continue delivery, trusting that the blocker will eventually lose interest in blocking.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sponsor effectiveness is developed through private, evidence-based coaching and concrete next steps (A). (B) skips the sponsor and takes on a peer-executive conflict the PM has no mandate to resolve. (C) escalates over the sponsor without first giving them the chance to act. (D) hopes the problem goes away.
