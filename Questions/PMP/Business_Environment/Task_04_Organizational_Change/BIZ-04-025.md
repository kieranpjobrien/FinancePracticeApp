# Question

You're planning training for a new system. The current plan is a single classroom session two weeks before go-live. Experienced team members say this approach has failed in past rollouts. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed because classroom training is the organisation's long-standing approach to new rollouts.
- **B**: Extend the classroom block to give people more practice time before the cutover date.
- **C**: Replace the plan with just-in-time training, job aids, super-users, and post-go-live reinforcement.
- **D**: Cancel the training because the experienced team members are sceptical about this specific format.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Training works when it matches how people learn and apply skills (C); the cited failure pattern is well known. (A) ignores credible feedback. (B) does more of what hasn't worked. (D) drops training entirely without an alternative.
