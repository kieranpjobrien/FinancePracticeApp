# Question

Two weeks before a hard-dated go-live, the change readiness dashboard shows three of eight business units below threshold on ability and confidence. The sponsor wants to proceed regardless to hold the committed date. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed on the committed date and accept degraded ability because the schedule is externally fixed and visible.
- **B**: Cancel the go-live outright because proceeding with any business unit below threshold represents an unsafe launch.
- **C**: Swap all three units' staff with employees from the compliant units so readiness is technically satisfied by launch.
- **D**: Present the sponsor targeted remediation options for the three units, including phased go-live, and recommend a decision.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Readiness gaps inform a sponsor decision framed as options, not a binary proceed-or-cancel (D). (A) proceeds on schedule while ignoring the dashboard's very purpose. (B) is an all-or-nothing overreaction. (C) gaming the metric by reshuffling people does not create real readiness.
