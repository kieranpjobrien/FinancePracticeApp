# Question

In pilot testing of a new workflow tool, users say it feels slower than the old one, although end-to-end metrics show better throughput. What approach should the project manager take?

## Options

- **A**: Show users the throughput data to prove the new tool is faster overall and move on.
- **B**: Acknowledge the friction, explain the learning curve, and reinforce long-term benefits.
- **C**: Mandate use of the new tool and disable the old one to prevent any backsliding.
- **D**: Revert to the old tool until the new one feels faster to the pilot users involved.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Perceived speed during a learning curve is real to users; the change response addresses both feelings and competence (B). (A) dismisses lived experience with charts. (C) forces compliance and breeds workarounds. (D) discards a real improvement at the first sign of friction.
