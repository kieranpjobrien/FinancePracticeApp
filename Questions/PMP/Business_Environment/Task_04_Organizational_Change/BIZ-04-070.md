# Question

Post-training assessments show 90% pass rates, yet on-the-job error rates for the new process are 15%. The training team insists the numbers show success. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the training team's view and treat on-the-job errors as separate from the training programme's performance.
- **B**: Repeat the same training for all staff in the hope that repetition will close the error gap.
- **C**: Replace the training team with a new vendor because their current design clearly fails to produce effective performance.
- **D**: Treat transfer as the measure of training, investigate the gap with observation and coaching, and redesign accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Training effectiveness is measured by on-the-job transfer, not classroom pass rates; the PM investigates and redesigns (D). (A) accepts a vanity metric as proof of effectiveness. (B) repeats a design that did not transfer. (C) replaces the team before diagnosing the transfer gap.
