# Question

Midway through a complex change programme, the executive sponsor departs suddenly and the interim sponsor is unfamiliar with the programme. The next steering committee is in three weeks. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Arrange a structured sponsor onboarding covering context, decisions to date, risks, and the next pivotal choices ahead.
- **B**: Continue business as usual and let the interim sponsor absorb the programme through the regular governance meetings.
- **C**: Escalate to the CEO that the interim sponsor is inadequate and formally request a permanent replacement be assigned.
- **D**: Pause the programme until a permanent sponsor is appointed to avoid decisions being made without continuity of sponsorship.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A new sponsor needs a structured handover so upcoming decisions are made with context, not from cold (A). (B) hopes context will accrue while decisions pile up. (C) is premature and undermines the appointed interim. (D) halts momentum when the PM can equip the interim to lead.
