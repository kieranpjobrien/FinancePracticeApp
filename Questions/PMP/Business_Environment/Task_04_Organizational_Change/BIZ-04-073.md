# Question

The change team measures adoption by login counts; the benefits owner measures value by error-rate reduction. Both report green, yet the overall business case is not yet realising. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Align measurement to the chain from behaviour to outcome so adoption and benefit metrics trace the same value story.
- **B**: Keep both measures as they are because they independently validate different aspects of the broader change programme.
- **C**: Drop login counts in favour of error-rate reduction alone because error-rate is closer to the realised benefit value.
- **D**: Add more activity metrics to the change dashboard so the gap between adoption and benefit is less visible to stakeholders.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Measurement should trace a behaviour-to-outcome chain; disconnected green dashboards mask the actual problem (A). (B) accepts two green views that together do not explain the non-realisation. (C) strips a useful leading indicator without diagnosing. (D) hides the gap instead of addressing it.
