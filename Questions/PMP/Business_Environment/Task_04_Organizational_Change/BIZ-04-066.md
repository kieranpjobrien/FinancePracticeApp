# Question

Your programme aligns to Kotter's eight-step model. After three months, most employees still see the change as "another initiative." The guiding coalition is formed but low-profile. Which Kotter step needs attention?

## Options

- **A**: Empower broad-based action, because the coalition cannot act if employees themselves feel disempowered to participate fully.
- **B**: Consolidate gains, because the change programme needs to show momentum by stacking one visible success on another.
- **C**: Anchor the change in the culture, because new behaviours only persist when they are embedded in organisational norms.
- **D**: Establish a sense of urgency, because without a felt why, every other Kotter step below runs on empty.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Kotter step 1 is urgency; without it the coalition and communication land on flat ground (D). (A) empowerment matters but is step 5 and assumes urgency and vision exist. (B) consolidation is late-stage and requires earlier steps to have landed. (C) anchoring is the final step and presumes urgency has been set.
