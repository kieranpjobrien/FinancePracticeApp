# Question

An agile programme shifted a software company's support teams to a new ticketing workflow. Adoption metrics hit target during the launch quarter and the change team was stood down. Six months on, teams are drifting back to informal channels, workarounds are creeping in, and the workflow is used inconsistently. The benefits case assumed sustained use. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Re-run the original launch training and communications at full intensity to lift usage back to the levels seen at launch.
- **B**: Find out what is driving the drift, then build the missing ongoing reinforcement — coaching, routines, and recognition — to hold the new behaviour in place.
- **C**: Examine the workarounds to learn where the workflow is failing people, and feed changes to the tool into the backlog so it fits their work better.
- **D**: Add the workflow's key steps to team performance metrics so managers can hold staff accountable for consistent use.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A spike at launch that fades by month six is the signature of a change that was introduced but never reinforced — sponsorship, coaching, and routines were withdrawn when the change team stood down, and behaviour reverted. The first move is to understand the drift and put durable reinforcement in place (B). Re-running the launch (A) treats the symptom and is the classic trap: it can manufacture another temporary spike but, because nothing sustains the behaviour afterwards, it will decay again. Examining the workarounds and improving the tool (C) is worth doing if the workflow is genuinely deficient, but workarounds appearing only after six months of successful use point to fading reinforcement rather than a design flaw that was somehow tolerable at launch — so tool changes address the wrong cause. Baking steps into performance metrics (D) can support accountability, but imposed measurement without first understanding why people are reverting tends to drive box-ticking rather than the genuine, sustained adoption the benefits case needs.
