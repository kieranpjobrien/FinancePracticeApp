# Question

After launching a new performance-management system, HR reports that some managers are quietly continuing to run the old evaluation process in parallel. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Report the parallel use to senior leadership and request firm enforcement action.
- **B**: Investigate why managers prefer the legacy process and partner with HR on the transition.
- **C**: Permit dual use indefinitely so managers can choose whichever process works for them.
- **D**: Schedule additional mandatory training sessions on the new performance management system.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quiet parallel use is a symptom; the cause is in the manager experience or value proposition of the new system (B). (A) escalates without diagnosis. (C) defeats the project's purpose. (D) assumes a knowledge problem when the issue is usually willingness or design.
