# Question

A project manager has launched a new field-service mobile app for engineers and wants to know whether the organisational change is actually taking hold, not just whether the app was delivered. Leadership keeps asking "are we there yet?" Which THREE measures best indicate genuine adoption and embedding of the change? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: The number of features delivered against the original product backlog.
- **B**: The proportion of engineers using the app for their daily jobs versus reverting to paper or phone calls.
- **C**: The total spend consumed against the project budget to date.
- **D**: Trends in the business outcome the change targeted, such as reduction in repeat visits or faster job closure.
- **E**: Qualitative feedback and proficiency signals showing engineers are confident and competent with the new way of working.

## Answer

B, D, E

## Explanation

Genuine adoption is measured by behaviour, outcomes, and proficiency. Usage versus reversion (B) shows whether people have actually switched. Movement in the targeted business outcome (D) shows the change is producing value, which is the whole point. Confidence and competence signals (E) indicate the new way of working is embedding, not just being tolerated. Features delivered (A) measures output, not adoption — you can ship everything and have no one use it. Budget consumed (C) is a cost metric with no bearing on whether people have changed. The trap is treating delivery and spend as proxies for change.
