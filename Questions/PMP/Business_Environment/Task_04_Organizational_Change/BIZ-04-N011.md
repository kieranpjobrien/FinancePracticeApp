# Question

A project manager is supporting adoption of a new clinical documentation system and is using a structured change model to diagnose where individuals are stuck. For each observed symptom on the left, match the change element that is most clearly deficient on the right.

## Items

- **1**: Clinicians say they have heard about the system but do not understand why the hospital is replacing the current one.
- **2**: Clinicians understand and accept the change but repeatedly make errors because they have never been shown how to use the new screens.
- **3**: Clinicians were proficient at launch but, months later, have quietly reverted to the old habits where they can.

## Matches

- **A**: Knowledge and ability to use the new system
- **B**: Reinforcement to sustain the new behaviour
- **C**: Awareness of the need for change

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

Symptom 1 is a pure awareness gap — people do not know why the change is happening — so it maps to awareness (C). Symptom 2 describes accepting people who lack the know-how and skill to perform, which is a knowledge-and-ability deficiency (A). Symptom 3 is post-launch decay of an established behaviour, the classic signal that reinforcement is missing (B). Distinguishing these elements lets the project manager target the right intervention rather than, for example, retraining people who actually need a stronger reason to change.
