# Question

A global rollout of a performance management system is technically complete, but one region reports that the feedback norms conflict with local cultural expectations. Sentiment is poor and adoption stalls. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Enforce consistent global application of the tool in all regions to preserve comparable performance data everywhere.
- **B**: Abandon the rollout in the affected region because the cultural conflict cannot be resolved within project timelines.
- **C**: Centralise all feedback conversations to HR staff so line managers in the region are removed from the process entirely.
- **D**: Partner with local HR and leaders to tailor norms and practices within the global framework, then re-communicate.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Global frameworks with local tailoring preserve comparability while respecting cultural context (D). (A) prioritises data consistency over adoption and creates passive resistance. (B) abandons a region rather than adapting. (C) removes managers from their role rather than enabling them.
