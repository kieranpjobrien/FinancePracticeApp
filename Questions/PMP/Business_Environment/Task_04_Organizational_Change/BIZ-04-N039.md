# Question

A construction firm is rolling out a digital site-inspection process that replaces paper checklists used by site foremen. Adoption is poor. The project manager wants to act on the actual drivers rather than assume. Which actions are sound ways to understand and address the resistance? (Select all that apply.)

## Options

- **A**: Talk with foremen on site to learn the specific reasons they are not using the new process.
- **B**: Examine usage and inspection data to see where and how the new process is being avoided.
- **C**: Involve respected foremen in refining the process and championing it to their peers.
- **D**: Announce that paper checklists will no longer be accepted from next week, regardless of the reasons for resistance.
- **E**: Conclude the resistance is simply discomfort with technology and respond by re-running the same training.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Understanding resistance starts by hearing it directly from the foremen (A) and corroborating it with where and how the process is actually being bypassed (B), then engaging credible peers to refine and advocate the change (C). Banning paper checklists before the drivers are understood (D) suppresses the workaround without removing the cause and tends to harden resistance on a busy site. Assuming the cause is technophobia and repeating the same training (E) presumes a conclusion the firm has not verified and is likely to miss the real obstacle, whether trust, workload, or a genuine gap in the process.
