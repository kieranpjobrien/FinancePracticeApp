# Question

Middle managers across three departments are quietly undermining the new process by exempting their teams "just this once" repeatedly. Executive messaging has been clear but behaviour has not shifted. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Escalate each exception to the executive team to enforce compliance directly on the undermining middle managers.
- **B**: Remove the option to grant exemptions from the process so middle managers can no longer exempt their teams.
- **C**: Run tailored sessions with middle managers to surface their concerns, co-design adjustments, and rebuild commitment.
- **D**: Require middle managers to publicly commit to the change at an all-hands meeting so peer pressure drives alignment.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Middle-manager resistance is addressed by understanding what is driving it and rebuilding ownership (C). (A) polices symptoms without addressing the underlying resistance. (B) removes latitude without changing beliefs, inviting workarounds. (D) uses public pressure to extract a commitment that will not survive contact with daily work.
