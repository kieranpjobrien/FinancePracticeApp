# Question

An agile team is delivering a new digital onboarding journey for a telco's customer-service staff, releasing it call-centre by call-centre. After the first site goes live, staff satisfaction is mixed and a few are struggling, though most are coping and the journey is functioning. The product owner wants to pause the rollout and redesign the whole journey before any further sites go live. The next site is due to start in a fortnight. How should the project manager respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree to halt further rollout until a redesign is finished and re-approved, so no more staff are exposed to a journey that is causing difficulty.
- **B**: Treat the first site as a learning increment — gather specific feedback, fold the achievable fixes into the next release, and continue the rollout while improving.
- **C**: Proceed to the next site on schedule without changes, since most staff are coping and one site's mixed reaction is too small a sample to act on.
- **D**: Keep the rollout running but split the team, dedicating part of it to a parallel full redesign while the rest continues deploying the current journey unchanged.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The first site has surfaced real but contained friction, and the incremental model exists precisely so each release can be improved by what the last one taught — so the project manager should capture the feedback, apply the fixes that fit, and keep going (B). Halting for a full redesign (A) is the most tempting alternative because protecting staff sounds responsible, but it discards the core advantage of incremental delivery and treats early, expected teething problems as grounds to stop, when most staff are in fact coping. Pressing on with no changes (C) goes too far the other way, ignoring genuine signal that could be acted on cheaply before the next site. Splitting the team for a parallel full redesign (D) is disproportionate to mixed-but-workable feedback and starves the live rollout of capacity to chase a wholesale rebuild that the evidence does not yet justify.
