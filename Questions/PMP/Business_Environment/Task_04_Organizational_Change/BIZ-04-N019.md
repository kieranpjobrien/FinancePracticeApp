# Question

A predictive project has delivered a new warehouse management system, and the operations team will own it once the project closes. The project manager wants the organisational change to stick after the team disbands. The deployment was clean and users are trained. What should the project manager ensure is in place before closing the project?

## Options

- **A**: A signed-off operational support model, so that when staff hit problems with the system there is a help desk and escalation path to resolve them.
- **B**: A refresher-training schedule and an updated set of user guides, so staff can keep their skills current after the project team has gone.
- **C**: Named operational owners accountable for ongoing adoption and reinforcement, with their responsibilities and the measures of sustained use agreed and handed over.
- **D**: An adoption dashboard that continues to report usage to leadership after closure, so any decline in use becomes visible.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Change decays when no one in the business is accountable for keeping it alive, so the decisive pre-closure step is to hand ownership of ongoing adoption and reinforcement to named operational roles, with responsibilities and success measures agreed (C). A support model (A) is genuinely necessary and the strongest distractor, but it keeps the system working rather than keeping the new behaviour embedded — staff can have a functioning help desk and still drift back to old habits. Refresher training and guides (B) sustain capability, which matters, but capability without an owner to drive and reinforce use does not by itself prevent reversion. An adoption dashboard (D) makes decline visible but only measures the problem; without someone accountable to act on what it shows, visibility alone changes nothing. The element that actually sustains the change is clear operational ownership.
