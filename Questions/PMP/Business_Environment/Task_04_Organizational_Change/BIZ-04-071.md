# Question

Your change project lands into an organisation that has absorbed four major changes in the last 12 months. Employees describe themselves as "change fatigued." A survey confirms it. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the standard change plan because fatigue is a subjective feeling rather than a tangible impact on delivery.
- **B**: Raise change saturation with the portfolio and sponsor, then tailor pacing, messaging, and support to the current state.
- **C**: Compress the change quickly into a single intense month so the organisation finishes and moves past the fatigue sooner.
- **D**: Delay the project indefinitely until the organisation naturally recovers from its accumulated recent change fatigue levels.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Change saturation is a portfolio-level signal requiring tailoring, not a reason to proceed unchanged or cancel (B). (A) dismisses a measured condition. (C) compresses intensity and worsens fatigue. (D) defers indefinitely when tailoring could proceed.
