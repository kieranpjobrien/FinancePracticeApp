# Question

A hybrid programme is building a subscription streaming service, four weeks from launch. A well-capitalised new entrant launches an ad-funded free tier that undercuts the market's pricing assumptions. The product owner can adapt features within the remaining iterations, but the revenue model and business case assumed paid subscriptions only. The sponsor already knows about the entrant and has asked the PM for a recommendation on how to proceed. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend launching on schedule to establish a market presence, then iterate the monetisation model once real adoption data is available.
- **B**: Recommend adding an ad-supported tier before launch so the product can compete with the entrant's free offering from day one.
- **C**: Bring the product owner, business owner and finance together to test the business case under revised pricing and monetisation scenarios, then base the recommendation on what that shows.
- **D**: Recommend a short delay to the launch to give the organisation time to rethink the commercial model before going to market.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The sponsor wants a recommendation, but a sound recommendation on a threat to the business case must be grounded in analysis, so the PM first convenes the people who own the revenue model and finances to test the scenarios, then advises from evidence (C). Recommending launch-then-iterate (A), adding an ad tier (B), or delaying to rethink (D) are each plausible outcomes, but advancing any of them now means handing the sponsor a recommendation before the commercial impact has actually been assessed, when the right answer turns on numbers no one has yet run.
