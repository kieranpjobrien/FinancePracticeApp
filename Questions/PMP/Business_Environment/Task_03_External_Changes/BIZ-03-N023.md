# Question

A construction project sources a critical turbine from an overseas supplier, priced in a foreign currency, with delivery in eight months. That currency has appreciated 12% against the project's home currency in three weeks, and analysts expect continued volatility either way. The fixed-price contract with the client carries no allowance for exchange movements, but the project holds a contingency reserve sized for identified risks. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Engage finance and procurement to evaluate hedging or a fixed-rate purchase agreement, and quantify the residual exposure that would remain.
- **B**: Draw on the contingency reserve to cover the forecast cost increase at the current exchange rate.
- **C**: Negotiate with the supplier to convert the order to the project's home currency or fix the price in writing.
- **D**: Update the cost forecast and risk register to reflect the movement, then report the exposure to the sponsor for direction.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Currency exposure on a future, volatile payment is a financial risk best shaped before it is funded or reported: finance and procurement own the instruments (hedging, forward contracts) that can cap it, and quantifying the residual exposure is what makes any reserve or reporting decision meaningful (A). Drawing contingency now (B) funds a number that is still moving and may prove too high or too low before delivery. Negotiating the currency or a fixed price with the supplier (C) is one specific mitigation the broader evaluation in A would consider, but jumping to a single counterparty tactic skips comparison with hedging and may forfeit a better option. Updating forecast and register and reporting to the sponsor (D) is correct housekeeping and will follow, but reporting an unmanaged, un-quantified exposure asks the sponsor to decide before the response options have been worked. The margin is that A both engages the right owners and produces the quantified residual the other three actions depend on.
