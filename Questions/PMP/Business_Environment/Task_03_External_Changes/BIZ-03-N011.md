# Question

Your co-located agile team is building an events booking platform. A sudden public-health directive bans large in-person gatherings in your region for at least three months, forcing the office to close and casting doubt on customers' near-term need for the product. Which three actions are most appropriate for the project manager to take? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Move the team to a fully remote way of working and re-establish the team's ceremonies in a virtual format.
- **B**: Reallocate the team to another in-flight product until in-person events resume and demand returns.
- **C**: With the product owner, revisit the backlog to emphasise features that serve virtual or hybrid events.
- **D**: Have the team keep delivering the agreed backlog remotely while you alone work out the demand implications.
- **E**: Engage stakeholders to reassess the product's near-term value and direction given the changed demand.

## Answer

A, C, E

## Explanation

The directive changes both how the team works and what customers need, so the PM re-establishes a remote operating model (A), works with the product owner to shift the backlog toward virtual or hybrid capability (C), and engages stakeholders to reassess the product's near-term value and direction (E). Reallocating the team elsewhere (B) writes off the product on the assumption demand will simply return, abandoning the chance to pivot toward features the new environment now demands. Continuing the existing backlog while the PM privately works out the implications (D) keeps the team building possibly obsolete features and excludes the product owner and stakeholders from the value reassessment that should drive what comes next.
