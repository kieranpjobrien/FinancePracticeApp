# Question

An economic downturn prompts your organisation to cut discretionary budgets across the portfolio. Your agile product, building toward a full feature set over four more quarters, is told to "show value sooner or face funding cuts." The team is delivering steadily increment by increment, but nothing has yet been put in front of customers and benefits realisation is still a year out. The product owner has a prioritised backlog. What should the project manager and product owner do?

## Options

- **A**: Work with the product owner to define a minimum viable product from the existing backlog and resequence work to release it as early as possible.
- **B**: Demonstrate the latest working increment to the sponsor at the next review to evidence steady progress and protect funding.
- **C**: Re-forecast the remaining roadmap into smaller, frequent releases so value is delivered incrementally rather than at the end.
- **D**: Meet with the sponsor to clarify what "value" means to them so the team optimises the backlog for the outcomes they will fund.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The ask is to convert steady internal progress into value the business can see and fund, and the most direct way is to define an MVP and resequence the backlog so a usable increment reaches customers soonest (A). Demonstrating the latest increment to the sponsor (B) shows activity but an internal demo of unreleased work is not the same as customer-realised value, which is what is under threat. Re-forecasting into smaller releases (C) is a strong supporting move and may well follow, but cadence planning across four quarters is slower than what the funding pressure demands now—the first releasable slice matters most. Clarifying the sponsor's definition of value (D) is sensible and could sharpen the MVP, yet the core problem is that nothing has shipped; even a clarified definition still requires defining and releasing that first increment. The margin is that A produces the earliest external, fundable proof of value, which the others support but do not deliver.
