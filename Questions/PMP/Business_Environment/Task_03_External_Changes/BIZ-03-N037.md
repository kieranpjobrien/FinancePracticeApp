# Question

Your client is acquired by a larger group midway through a predictive ERP implementation. The new parent runs a different ERP as its global standard, but no direction about your project has reached the team and the integration could take many months to settle. The work is on track. Rumours of consolidation are unsettling the team and a few members are quietly job-hunting. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Seek direction from the sponsor and steering committee on the project's status and strategic fit under the new ownership, and plan team communication around what comes back.
- **B**: Brief the team candidly that the project continues until told otherwise, acknowledge the uncertainty, and address the retention risk now.
- **C**: Continue executing to the current baseline and the agreed governance cadence, raising the acquisition as a risk to be monitored until formal direction arrives.
- **D**: Prepare an impact assessment of what a switch to the parent's ERP standard would mean for scope, schedule and cost, ready for when governance asks.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A change of ownership can redefine a project's mandate, and the people who can resolve that—sponsor and steering committee—must be engaged first, because their answer shapes everything else including what the team can honestly be told (A). Reassuring the team now (B) addresses a real and pressing retention risk, but messaging "we continue until told otherwise" before confirming status with governance can prove hollow if direction then changes, damaging the PM's credibility; the communication should follow the clarification it depends on. Continuing to baseline and merely monitoring the acquisition as a risk (C) is too passive for a development that may already have changed the project's justification—waiting for direction to arrive forfeits the chance to seek it. Preparing the platform-switch impact assessment (D) is prudent contingency and may well be requested, but analysing one particular outcome before governance has even indicated direction commits effort to a scenario that may not occur. The margin is that A goes to the only parties who can resolve the core uncertainty, and the team message, the steady-state execution and any impact analysis all hinge on what they say.
