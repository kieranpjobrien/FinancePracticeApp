# Question

A public-health emergency declaration restricts non-essential travel for the next 90 days. Your project relies on an onshore vendor team that was due to arrive next month for a 60-day integration workshop. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Push the vendor visit to after the restriction lifts and adjust the schedule by 90 days.
- **B**: Insist the vendor travel anyway, classifying the integration work as essential to avoid schedule slippage.
- **C**: Cancel the integration workshop entirely and retender the remaining work to a domestic vendor without travel risk.
- **D**: Redesign the integration as a distributed workshop with tooling, and revise schedule and resourcing accordingly.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The PM adapts how the work is done rather than waiting or forcing the old model (D). (A) is passive rescheduling that ignores remote alternatives. (B) tries to override a public-health restriction. (C) is a disproportionate retendering before redesign has been attempted.
