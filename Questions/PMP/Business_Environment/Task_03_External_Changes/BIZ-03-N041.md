# Question

Your team is three sprints into building a paid mobile budgeting app on an agile plan with a fixed launch date the sponsor has committed to publicly. A well-funded competitor releases a free product carrying two features your backlog had ordered for late delivery, and analytics show some early adopters trialling the rival. The product owner has already drafted a re-ordered backlog that pulls those two features forward and is asking you to confirm the next sprint against it. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the product owner's re-ordered backlog and plan the next sprint from it, since responding to the competitor quickly is what matters.
- **B**: Have the product owner validate the assumed value shift with real signals before the team commits the re-ordered backlog to the next sprint.
- **C**: Keep the current order for the next sprint and let the product owner re-prioritise from the following sprint once the competitor's impact is clearer.
- **D**: Ask the sponsor whether the public launch date can move so the pulled-forward features can be built to the same depth as planned.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The competitor is a genuine market signal, but re-ordering the whole next sprint rests on an assumed value shift the product owner has not yet tested; the right move is to ground that prioritisation in real evidence (churn, trial-to-paid, feedback) before the team commits, which is still fast and is the product owner's decision to make well (B). Accepting the drafted re-order outright (A) treats the product owner's hunch as fact and may chase a feature set that is not actually where value moved. Holding the current order and deferring a sprint (C) is defensible but cedes a sprint of responsiveness when the data could be gathered now within the same cadence. Moving the committed launch date (D) is a real lever but a disproportionate first step that escalates to the sponsor before the value question is even tested, and agile would normally flex scope within the date rather than the date itself.
