# Question

A draft regulation under public consultation could impose new accessibility requirements on your client's public-facing web platform, which your team is building in an agile delivery. The draft is detailed, but the final text may be amended before it becomes binding in roughly six months. Which actions are appropriate for the project manager to take now? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Record the potential regulatory change as a risk and assess its likelihood and impact on the product.
- **B**: Engage stakeholders, including legal, to interpret the draft and monitor the consultation outcome.
- **C**: Identify low-regret design and architecture choices that hold up regardless of how the final text lands.
- **D**: Have the product owner raise accessibility stories to the top of the backlog and build fully to the draft text this sprint.
- **E**: Submit a change request now to add the draft requirements to the scope baseline so the work is authorised early.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

Because the final text may still change, the sound moves are to log and assess the change as a risk (A), engage legal and stakeholders to interpret and track it (B), and steer design toward low-regret, reversible choices that survive either outcome (C). Building fully to the draft this sprint (D) commits delivery effort to text that may be amended, inviting rework on a requirement that is not yet binding. Raising a change request to baseline the draft now (E) is the correct mechanism only once a requirement is confirmed—formalising un-enacted text into the baseline locks in scope that consultation could still alter. Both D and E are real PM tools, but each commits prematurely; the distinction is acting decisively to *prepare for* an uncertain rule versus *committing to* it before it is final.
