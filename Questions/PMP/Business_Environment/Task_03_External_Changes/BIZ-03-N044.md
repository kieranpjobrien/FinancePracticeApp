# Question

Your agile programme delivers a SaaS analytics platform used across several countries. A government in one of your largest markets enacts a data-residency law, now in force, requiring citizens' personal data to be stored and processed in-country; your architecture currently holds all data in a single overseas region. The product owner and sponsor are engaged and want a grounded plan. Which three actions should the project manager take in the immediate response? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Log the compliance gap as a high-priority issue and bring it into the next backlog refinement for the team to work.
- **B**: Engage legal and security to confirm the law's scope, including which data, users and processing activities it actually covers.
- **C**: Assess delivery options such as an in-region data store, with their effect on roadmap, cost and other markets, for the product owner to prioritise.
- **D**: Issue a customer notice committing to a compliance date now, so the largest accounts in that market are reassured while the work proceeds.
- **E**: Submit a change request to add the residency work to the programme scope and update the cost and schedule baselines.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The law is in force and the architecture does not meet it, so the immediate response is to log the confirmed gap as a high-priority issue and pull it into refinement (A), have legal and security pin down exactly what the law covers (B), and assess the delivery options and their roadmap, cost and market impacts for the product owner to prioritise (C); together these establish scope, feasibility and value before anyone commits. Committing a public compliance date to customers (D) is a real action but premature, exposing the organisation to a deadline it has not yet shown is achievable. A change request to re-baseline (E) is the right governance step once the option and its cost and schedule are known, but raising it before B and C have sized the work commits a baseline change ahead of the analysis that should shape it. The distinction is between confirming and scoping the obligation now versus committing externally or to a baseline before that work is done.
