# Question

Mid-execution, geopolitical tension threatens shipping routes for a critical hardware component with a 14-week lead time. No disruption has yet occurred. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Order the full remaining quantity immediately to de-risk availability before any disruption actually materialises.
- **B**: Wait for a disruption to occur so that stock is not tied up with inventory cost.
- **C**: Update the risk register, trigger supplier diversification analysis, and stage orders based on early warning indicators.
- **D**: Switch suppliers now to a domestic vendor regardless of cost to fully eliminate the geopolitical exposure risk.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Emerging external risks are managed with updated risk response and staged actions linked to indicators (C). (A) over-commits capital without graduated triggers. (B) waits for the event instead of preparing for it. (D) eliminates exposure at potentially material cost without testing whether the risk warrants it.
