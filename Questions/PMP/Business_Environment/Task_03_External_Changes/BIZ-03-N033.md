# Question

Usage analytics spanning the last three quarters show customer demand for your agile e-commerce product steadily shifting toward mobile, while the roadmap—agreed a year ago—remains weighted toward desktop features. Which two actions are the most appropriate immediate responses for the product owner and project manager? (Choose two.)

## Options

- **A**: Bring the analytics to the sponsor and stakeholders to revisit the product vision and benefit priorities.
- **B**: Have the product owner reprioritise the backlog to weight mobile capabilities more heavily.
- **C**: Add a discovery spike to validate which mobile capabilities customers value most before reprioritising at scale.
- **D**: Re-plan the release roadmap around mobile and communicate revised dates to stakeholders.
- **E**: Commission market research to confirm the trend will persist beyond the current data.

## Answer

A, B

## Explanation

A confirmed, sustained market shift should prompt the PM to raise it with sponsor and stakeholders to realign the vision and benefits (A), while the product owner adjusts backlog priority to follow the value (B)—the two moves that respond directly and at the right level. A discovery spike (C) is useful refinement and may follow, but inserting a validation step before any reprioritisation delays response to a trend the data already confirms. Re-planning the roadmap and reissuing dates (D) is downstream work that should come *after* the vision is realigned and the backlog reordered, not as a first move. Commissioning fresh market research (E) re-validates a trend the analytics have already established, spending time to confirm what is known. C, D and E are all real activities, but each is either premature or redundant given that the shift is validated; A and B act on it now at the value and priority levels.
