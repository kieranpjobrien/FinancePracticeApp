# Question

A newly elected government signals a policy reversal that would reduce demand for your project's output by approximately 30%. Formal legislation is expected in six months; your launch is in three. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Launch on the current schedule and let operations handle the demand decline after the policy legislation passes.
- **B**: Cancel the project immediately because the policy-driven demand decline will render its outputs economically unviable.
- **C**: Reshape scope toward the narrower demand segment, accelerate learning cycles, and brief the sponsor on revised value.
- **D**: Freeze the project until the legislation passes and the final reduction in demand is known with real certainty.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A credible policy signal shapes the product sooner rather than later; the PM reshapes scope and informs governance (C). (A) delivers to a shrinking market without adaptation. (B) cancels on a signal before material reshape is attempted. (D) freezes when reshaping can continue to add value.
