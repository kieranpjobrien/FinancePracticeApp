# Question

A project manager monitors several external indicators relevant to an in-flight commercial programme. Match each external development (Items) to the project dimension it most directly puts at risk (Matches).

## Items

- **1**: The base lending rate rises steeply across two consecutive quarters.
- **2**: A new occupational safety regulation becomes mandatory for the work being performed.
- **3**: A key skill becomes scarce as a rival hiring surge drains the local labour market.

## Matches

- **A**: Compliance of the deliverables and the methods used to produce them.
- **B**: The financial business case and the project's continued justification.
- **C**: Resource availability and the schedule that depends on it.

## Answer

1-B, 2-A, 3-C

## Explanation

A sustained rate rise increases the cost of capital and erodes returns, directly threatening the financial business case (1-B). A mandatory safety regulation bears on whether the deliverables and working methods are lawful, a compliance concern (2-A). A hiring surge that drains scarce skills primarily endangers resource availability and the schedule built on it (3-C). Mapping any indicator to the wrong dimension, such as treating a rate rise as a compliance issue, would point the PM's response at the wrong risk.
