# Question

Your capital project's NPV was calculated at an 8% discount rate. Central bank rate rises push the enterprise discount rate to 12% mid-execution. The base IRR was 14%. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to report against the original 8% rate to preserve baseline comparability across reporting periods.
- **B**: Re-cancel the project immediately because a 2% IRR margin is too thin against current macro volatility.
- **C**: Ignore the rate change because the project has already committed capital and is operationally underway.
- **D**: Recalculate NPV at 12%, engage finance on revised hurdles, and present a refreshed case to governance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Material macro changes require a refreshed business case with updated rates and governance review (D). (A) reports against a stale rate, misleading decision-makers. (B) cancels before analysis, skipping the refresh. (C) treats committed capital as if the future decision surface no longer exists.
