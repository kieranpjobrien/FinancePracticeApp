# Question

A predictive medical-device project is 60% complete when an international standards body releases a revised interoperability standard that the client's hospital customers will mandate within 18 months. Conforming means reworking the communications module, already built and tested. You are the supplier's project manager; the contract scope references the older standard, and your sponsor and the client have jointly reviewed the trade-off and agreed to conform now and absorb the rework. The change adds cost and four weeks to the schedule. What should the project manager do next?

## Options

- **A**: Raise a change request to adopt the new standard and route it through integrated change control before the rework starts.
- **B**: Direct the engineering team to begin the communications-module rework immediately, since the client has agreed and delay only adds risk.
- **C**: Update the schedule and cost baselines to reflect the four-week extension and added cost, then inform the team of the new plan.
- **D**: Re-confirm with the client in writing that they accept the added cost and schedule before committing any rework effort.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A decision to conform has been made, but it changes contracted scope, cost and schedule, so the next step is to put it through integrated change control via a change request—the mechanism that formally authorises the change and updates the baselines under proper approval (A). Starting the rework immediately (B) acts on a strategic agreement before it is authorised through the change process; on a contracted medical-device project, beginning out-of-baseline work without an approved change exposes both parties and bypasses the control that protects scope. Updating the baselines directly (C) is part of what must happen, but baselines are revised as an *output* of approved change control, not edited unilaterally ahead of it. Re-confirming acceptance in writing (D) duplicates a decision the sponsor and client have already taken jointly; seeking the same agreement again adds a step without adding control. The margin is that A is the correct governance vehicle to convert an agreed scope change into authorised work and revised baselines, where B and C skip the authorisation and D re-does a settled decision.
