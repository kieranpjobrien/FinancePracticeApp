# Question

A predictive construction project is two-thirds complete when the relevant standards body revises the building code; the revision becomes mandatory for works not yet certified. Part of your structure is already certified, but several upcoming elements are not. Which three actions are most appropriate for the project manager to take? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Apply the revised code to all remaining elements, including those whose certification is imminent, to keep the build uniform.
- **B**: Determine precisely which uncertified elements the revised code affects, with the design authority.
- **C**: Assess the cost, schedule and design impact on the affected elements and raise a change request.
- **D**: Pause work on the affected uncertified elements until the change request is approved, while continuing unaffected work.
- **E**: Inform the sponsor and relevant stakeholders of the compliance change and its likely consequences.

## Answer

B, C, E

## Explanation

The PM first scopes exactly which uncertified elements are caught by the revision, using the design authority (B), assesses the impact and formalises it through change control (C), and keeps the sponsor and stakeholders informed of the compliance change (E). Blanket-applying the revised code to all remaining elements (A) sounds prudent but pre-empts the scoping in B and may force needless redesign on elements that, once their imminent certification lands, fall outside the mandate. Pausing the affected elements pending approval (D) is a reasonable-sounding safeguard, but stopping work before the impact is even assessed prematurely hits the schedule; whether and what to pause should follow the assessment, not precede it.
