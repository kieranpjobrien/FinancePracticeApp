# Question

A union representing 40% of your field installation workforce announces a two-week industrial action beginning in three weeks. Your deployment schedule is already tight. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Publicly pressure the union to end the dispute quickly by framing the project's delay as a community harm.
- **B**: Engage HR and industrial relations to understand the scope and negotiating landscape before committing to any response.
- **C**: Hire contractors to replace the unionised workers for the duration of the action so schedule pressure is relieved.
- **D**: Accelerate this week's deployment work to maximise throughput before the industrial action begins in three weeks.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Industrial action is an HR and legal matter; the PM engages specialists to understand before acting (B). (A) escalates publicly with no standing to resolve the dispute. (C) risks severe escalation and potential legal breach if done reactively. (D) pushes unsustainable pace without engaging the people process.
