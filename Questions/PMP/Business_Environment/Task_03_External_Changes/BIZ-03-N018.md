# Question

Your agile team builds compliance software sold to insurers. The insurance regulator announces a rule change, effective in four months, that alters the reports your customers must file. Several customers have flagged they will need your product updated before the deadline. The team is two days into a two-week sprint on unrelated features, and the regulatory work is estimated at roughly one sprint. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Ask the product owner to call an immediate sprint review and replan so the team can pivot to the regulatory work without delay.
- **B**: Have a couple of developers begin scoping the regulatory changes now while the rest of the team continues the current sprint.
- **C**: Confirm with the customers and a compliance specialist exactly which reporting changes the rule requires before involving the product owner.
- **D**: Bring the regulatory deadline to the product owner so the reporting changes can be brought into backlog refinement and prioritised for upcoming sprints.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The change clearly alters backlog priority, and with four months of runway it does not require disrupting the current sprint, so the PM routes it to the product owner to prioritise through normal refinement for upcoming sprints (D). Calling an immediate review to pivot the whole team (A) overreacts to a deadline that comfortably allows the current sprint to finish and undermines the sprint commitment. Quietly diverting a couple of developers mid-sprint (B) erodes the sprint goal and bypasses the product owner's prioritisation. Nailing down the exact requirements with customers and compliance (C) will be necessary, but it sits within refining the work the product owner prioritises, not ahead of even making them aware, and treating it as a precondition needlessly delays getting the item onto the backlog.
