# Question

A project manager is reviewing several external developments affecting an in-flight programme. Match each external change (Items) to the most appropriate first project response (Matches).

## Items

- **1**: A new safety standard becomes legally binding and applies to the product under development.
- **2**: A competitor launches a rival product, shifting which planned features now matter most.
- **3**: A supplier's home country imposes export controls that may delay a critical component.

## Matches

- **A**: Re-prioritise the backlog with the product owner so the most valuable differentiators are built first.
- **B**: Log the delay threat as a risk and work with procurement to assess alternative sources and lead times.
- **C**: Assess the compliance gap and route the required changes through change control.

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B

## Explanation

A binding safety standard is a compliance obligation, so the gap is assessed and the changes are formalised through change control (1-C). A competitor launch is a market shift that alters relative value, handled by re-prioritising the backlog with the product owner (2-A). Export controls threaten supply, so the appropriate response is to treat it as a risk and explore alternative sourcing with procurement (3-B). Matching each change to the response aimed at the wrong driver, such as routing a supply risk through change control, would delay the correct action.
