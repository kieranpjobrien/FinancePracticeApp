# Question

Your company announces the acquisition of a competitor. Your in-flight CRM project overlaps significantly with the competitor's CRM implementation. The integration team will be formed next quarter. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Pause the CRM project entirely until the integration team reaches a final future-state architectural decision.
- **B**: Proceed exactly as planned because the integration will not formally start work for another quarter.
- **C**: Engage the integration sponsor, surface the overlap, and propose decision points that minimise irreversible investment.
- **D**: Accelerate delivery so your CRM is entrenched before the integration team begins looking at the two systems.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Overlapping investment is managed by early engagement and decision points that limit irreversible spend (C). (A) pauses when analysis and preparation can continue usefully. (B) keeps spending on potentially redundant scope. (D) races to create a fait accompli, wasting money if the future state is the other platform.
