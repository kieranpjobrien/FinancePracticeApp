# Question

A government unexpectedly raises an import duty applying to several materials in your predictive project's procurement plan, pushing forecast costs beyond the authorised management reserve. Which three actions are appropriate project manager responses? (Choose three.)

## Options

- **A**: Quantify the cost increase across the affected procurements and update the cost forecast.
- **B**: Review the design and procurement plan for substitutions or sourcing that reduce duty exposure.
- **C**: Escalate the funding shortfall to the sponsor with options and a recommendation.
- **D**: Reallocate contingency reserve from unaffected work packages to cover the duty increase.
- **E**: Defer the affected procurements to a later phase to push the cost into a future budget period.

## Answer

A, B, C

## Explanation

The PM should size the impact and refresh the forecast (A), look for design or sourcing changes that lower duty exposure (B), and escalate the reserve shortfall with options and a recommendation (C)—the analyse-mitigate-escalate sequence a funding gap calls for. Reallocating contingency from unaffected packages (D) raids reserve set aside for those packages' identified risks, leaving them exposed, and the shortfall already exceeds the reserve, so this masks rather than solves the gap. Deferring the procurements to a later period (E) is schedule manipulation that shifts the cost without reducing it and may disrupt the delivery sequence and dependent work. D and E look like cost-management moves but each merely hides or relocates the overrun; the sound responses quantify it, try to genuinely reduce it, and put a funded decision to the sponsor.
