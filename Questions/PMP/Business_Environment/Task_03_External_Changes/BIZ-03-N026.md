# Question

A hybrid hardware project depends on a single fabrication plant in a region where political tension has just escalated, raising the prospect of export restrictions. The component is 40% of the bill of materials and has a six-month lead time; the project needs it in nine months. The team has recorded the threat in the risk register and rated it high probability and high impact. A qualified dual source exists but charges 20% more and would itself need three months to qualify. The sponsor has delegated risk-response decisions within the project budget to the project manager. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Begin qualifying the dual source now, in parallel with the primary, so a proven alternative is ready before the lead-time window closes.
- **B**: Place a single forward order with the primary supplier for the full quantity to lock in supply ahead of any restriction.
- **C**: Hold the primary source and set a trigger—first sign of restriction—at which the dual-source qualification would begin.
- **D**: Re-rate the risk with the procurement team to confirm the high-high assessment before authorising any spend on a response.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The risk is already assessed as high-high and the PM holds delegated authority to respond, so the decision is now which response fits the timing: because the alternative needs three months to qualify and the component needs nine, starting parallel qualification now buys a ready fallback while the window is still open (A). Placing a full forward order with the single primary (B) doubles down on the very concentration that creates the exposure—if restrictions hit, a paid order in a constrained region may not ship. Waiting for a trigger before qualifying the dual source (C) looks prudent, but the first sign of restriction plus three months to qualify could blow past the nine-month need date; a trigger-based response only works when the lead time to act is shorter than the warning time, which it is not here. Re-rating the risk first (D) re-opens an analysis the team has already completed to a clear conclusion, delaying action on a high-high risk whose timing is the binding constraint. The margin is that A matches the response to the lead times the scenario specifies, whereas B increases exposure, C reacts too late, and D stalls on analysis that is already done.
