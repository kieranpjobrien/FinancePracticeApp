# Question

A well-funded startup enters the same market with a radically different business model that undercuts your project's value proposition. The sponsor demands to "out-innovate them" with no analysis yet done. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Begin immediate scope expansion to add features that could match the startup's differentiated model rapidly.
- **B**: Present a rapid assessment covering the threat, response options, and impact on the current project's value.
- **C**: Reject the sponsor's demand as premature and continue executing the approved scope on its original schedule.
- **D**: Cancel the project and propose a new project scoped specifically to counter the startup's model head-on.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A rapid, structured assessment gives the sponsor real options rather than a reflex (B). (A) expands scope before analysis and likely invents the wrong response. (C) ignores a credible external threat. (D) is an all-in pivot without understanding whether the threat warrants cancellation.
