# Question

During a predictive CRM implementation, the privacy regulator issues new binding guidance clarifying how long customer marketing data may be retained. The data model is due to be baselined at a design review in three days, and the planned retention rules appear on the PM's reading to fall within what the guidance allows. Re-opening the model after baseline would mean a formal change and rework. What is the most appropriate first action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the baseline as planned and add the new guidance to the items the project tracks, revisiting the retention rules only if a later review surfaces a gap.
- **B**: Ask the data privacy officer to check the guidance against the planned retention rules and confirm the position before the design review.
- **C**: Raise a change request now to tighten the retention rules to the most conservative reading of the guidance, removing any compliance doubt ahead of the baseline.
- **D**: Hold the design review and rebaseline the data model to allow time for a full legal interpretation of the guidance across the whole CRM scope.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binding guidance has landed three days before the model is frozen, and although it looks compliant, that is the PM's own non-expert read — so the proportionate first move is a quick confirmation from the accountable privacy stakeholder while the window to adjust is still open (B). Baselining now and merely tracking the guidance (A) is tempting because the rules appear to comply and the schedule is tight, but it freezes a design against binding requirements on an unverified reading — cheap to check now, expensive to unwind later. Raising a change request to the most conservative interpretation (C) presumes a change is needed before anyone has established whether the model already complies, risking real rework and over-restrictive retention that the guidance may not demand. Halting the review for a full legal interpretation of the entire CRM scope (D) is disproportionate to a targeted retention question and forfeits schedule the situation does not require. The fine margin is A versus B: both could end with the model unchanged, but only B verifies that before the baseline locks it in, rather than after.
