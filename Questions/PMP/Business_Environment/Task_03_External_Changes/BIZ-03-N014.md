# Question

Your agile fintech team is building a payments product. The financial regulator unexpectedly opens a time-limited regulatory sandbox that would let the firm trial a feature currently blocked by existing rules. Applications close in three weeks, the application itself requires significant compliance and legal input, and acceptance would reshape the next two quarters of work. The sponsor is keen and has asked the team to pursue it. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Convene compliance and legal at once to begin preparing the application, given the three-week window and the work it involves.
- **B**: Work with the product owner, compliance and legal to assess what the sandbox would require and how it reshapes the roadmap, so the sponsor's decision to apply is an informed one.
- **C**: Have the product owner provisionally re-plan the next two quarters around the sandbox feature so the team can start as soon as acceptance comes.
- **D**: Add a spike to the current sprint for the team to prototype the blocked feature, so a working demo strengthens the sandbox application.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Even with the sponsor keen, "pursue it" still needs to be grounded in what the sandbox actually demands and how it affects the roadmap, so the PM first assembles that assessment with the product owner, compliance and legal to inform the commitment (B). Starting application drafting immediately (A) is tempting given the deadline but commits scarce compliance and legal effort before the opportunity and its roadmap cost are understood. Provisionally re-planning two quarters (C) reshapes committed work around an outcome not yet decided. Prototyping via a spike (D) may help the application but spends team capacity ahead of the go/no-go assessment, so it follows the evaluation rather than leading it.
