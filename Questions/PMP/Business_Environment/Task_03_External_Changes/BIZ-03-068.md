# Question

An industry standards body publishes a revised interoperability specification two months before your go-live. Adopting it is optional but will soon become market-expected. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until customers or partners explicitly demand the new specification before committing to any adoption investment.
- **B**: Adopt the revised specification before go-live at any cost to ensure the project is future-proofed on launch.
- **C**: Ignore the revision until the next major release cycle, regardless of market expectations around the specification.
- **D**: Analyse adoption effort, market timing, and partner impact, and propose a phased adoption plan to governance.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Optional-but-expected changes are addressed with phased adoption analysis and a governed decision (D). (A) waits for customer pain to force action when the PM can act earlier. (B) commits at any cost without testing proportionality. (C) dismisses a known market signal.
