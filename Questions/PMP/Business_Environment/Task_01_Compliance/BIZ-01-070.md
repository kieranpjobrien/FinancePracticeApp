# Question

A hybrid programme delivering a customer-loyalty platform relies on a third-party analytics vendor that will process personal data on the project's behalf. The data-protection regulation in force requires the data controller to ensure processors meet defined security obligations. The project manager has a signed master services agreement with the vendor but no specific data-processing terms. The first data feed to the vendor is scheduled for next sprint. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the feed to proceed under the master services agreement and add data-processing terms at the next contract renewal.
- **B**: Halt the personal-data feed until a compliant data-processing agreement is executed with the vendor.
- **C**: Anonymise the first feed so personal data is not transferred, then negotiate the data-processing terms in parallel.
- **D**: Ask the vendor to confirm in an email that they comply with the regulation, and proceed on that basis.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The regulation places a non-delegable duty on the controller to bind the processor through specific terms before personal data is shared, and the generic master agreement does not satisfy that duty, so transferring personal data now would breach it regardless of the schedule (B). Proceeding under the existing agreement (A) knowingly transfers data without the required terms and defers the obligation past the point it bites. An email confirmation (D) is not the contractual instrument the regulation requires and provides no enforceable protection. Anonymising the feed (C) is plausible and would remove the personal-data transfer, but it changes the product's data flow and analytics value to accommodate a contracting gap, and only works if true anonymisation is achievable for this dataset — the direct, certain compliance route is to execute the processing agreement, not to re-engineer the deliverable.
