# Question

Final verification testing for a Class II medical device uncovers an edge case that, in rare conditions, could cause patient harm. Remediation will push launch back three months. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Document the edge case as a known limitation in the user manual and ship.
- **B**: Hold the release, fix the safety defect, and accept the three-month delay.
- **C**: Release on schedule and ship a software patch within 90 days of launch.
- **D**: Quantify the probability of the edge case and release if it falls below a defined threshold.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

For regulated medical devices, known harm potential cannot be released (B). (A) shifts responsibility to users and does not satisfy regulators. (C) is unlawful for known safety defects in most medical device regimes. (D) misuses risk-based reasoning; "rare enough" is not a defence to the FDA, MDR, or TGA.
