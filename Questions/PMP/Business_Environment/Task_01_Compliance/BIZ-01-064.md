# Question

A Scrum team building a banking app learns mid-sprint that a new APRA standard changes how customer consent must be captured. Three user stories in progress are now non-compliant. What should the PM advise the product owner?

## Options

- **A**: Finish the sprint as committed, then rework the consent flow in the next sprint cycle.
- **B**: Abandon the sprint immediately and reconvene sprint planning around the new compliance requirement as a baseline.
- **C**: Reprioritise the backlog with the PO, pull the affected stories back, and respin them with compliant criteria.
- **D**: Ask the compliance team to approve an exception allowing the three stories to ship as originally specified.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In Scrum, the PO reprioritises in response to new information, and stories are respun with compliant acceptance criteria (C). (A) knowingly produces non-compliant output. (B) cancels the sprint, an overreaction when scope can be adjusted instead. (D) seeks an exception to a regulation, which compliance cannot grant.
