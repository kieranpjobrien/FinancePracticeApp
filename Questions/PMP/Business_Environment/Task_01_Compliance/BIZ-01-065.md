# Question

A critical SaaS subprocessor in your project's data path is acquired by a firm headquartered in a jurisdiction subject to new data sovereignty rules. The acquisition closes in 45 days. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Trigger a third-party risk reassessment with privacy, legal, and security before the acquisition legally closes.
- **B**: Switch to a new subprocessor immediately because any foreign ownership creates unacceptable residual data risk.
- **C**: Notify customers of the ownership change and ask each to reconfirm their data processing consent.
- **D**: Wait for the deal to close and then reassess whether the new parent entity changes anything materially.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Reassess the third-party risk now, before the control change completes, so mitigation decisions are made with time in hand (A). (B) jumps to replacement without weighing risk or cost. (C) involves customers before the PM has even scoped the issue internally. (D) forfeits the 45-day window when action is possible.
