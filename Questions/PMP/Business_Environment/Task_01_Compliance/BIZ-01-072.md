# Question

Your cross-border engineering team discovers the tax treatment of a hybrid cloud deployment may trigger a permanent establishment in a new jurisdiction. Finance, legal, and tax have differing views. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed with the current deployment plan because no authority has formally told the PM to stop.
- **B**: Defer the deployment indefinitely until finance, legal, and tax independently agree on a single interpretation.
- **C**: Relocate the workloads to a different region chosen by the engineering lead to sidestep the tax question entirely.
- **D**: Escalate the tax risk for a binding position from the tax director, and pause only the jurisdiction-specific elements.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tax matters require a binding interpretation; the PM escalates for a decision and pauses only what is affected (D). (A) treats silence as approval when the exposure is material. (B) halts everything when only one scope slice is implicated. (C) lets engineering make a tax decision that is not theirs to make.
