# Question

The same engineer writes code, deploys to production, and signs off on the deployment. The internal control framework requires segregation of duties for production changes. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Allow the practice to continue because the engineer is highly trusted.
- **B**: Add a peer reviewer and a separate deployer to restore segregation of duties.
- **C**: Document the gap as a known risk and revisit at the next audit.
- **D**: Move all deployments to a managed service so the question becomes moot.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The control objective is segregation; restore it by splitting the duties across people (B). (A) ignores the control framework based on trust, which is exactly what the control exists to remove. (C) accepts a known control failure indefinitely. (D) is a larger change that may be appropriate later but does not fix the immediate gap.
