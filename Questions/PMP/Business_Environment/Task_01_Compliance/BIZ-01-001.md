# Question

Three months into a 12-month healthcare data platform build, your compliance officer informs you that a new state data privacy law takes effect before your planned go-live. The current architecture does not satisfy the new requirements. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Assess the compliance gap with the compliance officer and architect to size the impact on scope, schedule, and cost.
- **B**: Submit a change request to add the missing controls and update the budget and schedule baselines.
- **C**: Pause development on affected components until legal counsel issues formal guidance.
- **D**: Inform the sponsor that the project is now at risk and request additional funding.

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PMI expects the PM to understand a problem before acting on it. Sizing the compliance gap (A) produces the impact data needed for any change request, escalation, or stakeholder conversation. (B) jumps to change control without an impact assessment, so the request would be incomplete. (C) halts work prematurely; assessment can begin in parallel with legal input. (D) escalates before the PM has done the analysis the sponsor will inevitably ask for.
