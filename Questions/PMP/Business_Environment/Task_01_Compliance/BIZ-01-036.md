# Question

A new feature on your e-commerce project will collect biometric login data from EU customers. Privacy counsel flags this as a high-risk processing activity under GDPR. What should the PM include in the next planning iteration?

## Options

- **A**: A note in the risk register that biometric data may require future review.
- **B**: A communication plan describing the feature to customers post-launch.
- **C**: A budget line item for potential GDPR fines should the feature be challenged.
- **D**: A data protection impact assessment as a formal deliverable before development begins.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A DPIA is the prescribed control for high-risk processing under GDPR and must precede development to influence design (D). (A) acknowledges the risk but does not act on it. (C) treats compliance as a financial trade-off rather than an obligation. (B) is downstream of the privacy decision and does not satisfy the requirement.
