# Question

Your sponsor on a regulated medical device project asks you to sign off on a compliance test result he says is "basically passed" despite two non-conformances logged by QA. He cites an urgent market window. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Sign off as requested because the sponsor has authority over the project's final release decisions.
- **B**: Decline to sign, document the non-conformances, and escalate through the quality and compliance governance path.
- **C**: Sign off with a written caveat that the sponsor directed the decision over the PM's formal objection.
- **D**: Defer the sign-off conversation until after the market window closes and pressure naturally subsides.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PMI's Code of Ethics is explicit: the PM does not sign off on falsely passed compliance outcomes regardless of authority pressure (B). (A) surrenders independent oversight to authority. (C) still certifies a false pass, with a note that does not undo the regulatory exposure. (D) stalls rather than addresses the ethical issue directly.
