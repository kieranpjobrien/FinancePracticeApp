# Question

A junior developer quietly tells you a senior architect directed them to bypass a logging control "because audit never checks that module." The control is mandated by policy. What should the PM do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Speak with the architect directly and ask him to restore the control before raising it further.
- **B**: Protect the junior developer's identity and report the control bypass through the compliance reporting channel.
- **C**: Restore the control yourself quietly and avoid creating interpersonal conflict within the project team.
- **D**: Gather additional independent evidence of the bypass before deciding whether any report is warranted.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A known control bypass is reported through the proper channel, and the reporter is protected (B). (A) tips off the subject and risks retaliation against the junior developer. (C) hides the breach and protects the architect without any corrective record. (D) delays action when the bypass is already admitted to the PM.
