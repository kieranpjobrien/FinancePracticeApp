# Question

In compliance terms, what is a "control"?

## Options

- **A**: A penalty applied when a regulation is violated.
- **B**: A measure put in place to satisfy a requirement and produce evidence.
- **C**: A status report sent to the regulator on a fixed cadence.
- **D**: An auditor's opinion on the effectiveness of project governance.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A control is the implemented mechanism — process, policy, or technical measure — that meets a requirement and generates audit evidence (B). (A) describes a sanction. (C) is a reporting activity, which may itself be a control but is not the definition. (D) describes an audit finding, not a control.
