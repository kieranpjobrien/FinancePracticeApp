# Question

A generative AI feature in your product is nearly complete. The EU AI Act's obligations for your risk classification take effect in six months, but legal has not yet finalised internal guidance. The go-live date is in two months. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Launch on the planned date because formal internal guidance does not yet exist to constrain scope.
- **B**: Hold the launch until the internal guidance is fully finalised and formally issued by legal.
- **C**: Engage legal on interim guidance and design the feature to meet published Act obligations proactively.
- **D**: Launch with an internal disclaimer that the feature will be reworked once guidance is issued later.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The regulation exists even if internal guidance lags, so the PM engages legal and designs to the published obligations (C). (A) relies on an internal gap to justify ignoring an external regulation. (B) waits passively when the PM can drive the guidance conversation. (D) ships to an unknown standard and treats rework as planning.
