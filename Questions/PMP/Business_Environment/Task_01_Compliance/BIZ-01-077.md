# Question

An agile team is building a hiring-analytics tool to be deployed in two regions. One region's anti-discrimination law requires the project to retain candidate demographic data to audit for adverse impact. The other region's privacy law prohibits processing that demographic data without explicit consent the recruiters cannot reliably obtain. The same data field is mandated in one jurisdiction and restricted in the other. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the stricter privacy rule everywhere and drop the demographic field from the product entirely.
- **B**: Bring the conflicting obligations to the legal and compliance authorities to determine a jurisdiction-specific compliant design.
- **C**: Build to whichever region launches first and address the other region's requirement in a later release.
- **D**: Configure the product to collect the demographic data globally, on the basis that the audit obligation protects candidates.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Two binding legal obligations genuinely conflict, and resolving that conflict requires authoritative legal interpretation the project manager cannot make alone — the first step is to put the conflict to legal and compliance so a defensible, jurisdiction-specific design can be defined (B). Dropping the field everywhere (A) prematurely sacrifices a mandatory control in the region that requires it, potentially breaching anti-discrimination law to satisfy privacy law. Building for the first region and deferring the other (C) ignores a known legal requirement for a market in scope and stores up non-compliance. Collecting the data globally (D) unilaterally overrides the prohibiting jurisdiction's privacy law on the project manager's own reasoning; the design must be split per jurisdiction, and only legal can confirm how.
