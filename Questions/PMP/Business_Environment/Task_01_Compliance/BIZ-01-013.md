# Question

A SaaS rollout must comply with data residency rules in three jurisdictions. Country A requires customer data to remain on-shore; Country B prohibits the encryption library Country A mandates. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the headquarters jurisdiction's rules globally for consistency and simpler audit.
- **B**: Adopt the strictest available combination across all three regulations.
- **C**: Engage legal and compliance experts to design jurisdiction-specific implementations that meet each rule.
- **D**: Pursue formal exemptions from the conflicting rules in Country A and Country B.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When jurisdictional rules genuinely conflict, the answer is usually localised implementations co-designed with legal and compliance specialists (C). (A) ignores the actual rules in the other countries. (B) is incoherent here — the rules aren't merely different in strength, they conflict. (D) treats exemptions as routinely available; in practice they are rare and slow.
