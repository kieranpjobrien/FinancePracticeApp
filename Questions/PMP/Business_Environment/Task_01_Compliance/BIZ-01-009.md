# Question

A construction project's legal team says a proposed waste disposal method is "probably acceptable" under ambiguous environmental rules. The fully compliant alternative adds 8% to project cost. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Use the cheaper method and rely on the legal opinion if it is later questioned.
- **B**: Default to the compliant alternative and absorb the cost increase to remove all risk.
- **C**: Present both options to the sponsor with the legal opinion, cost delta, and reputational risk.
- **D**: Ask the regulator directly for a binding written ruling before doing anything else.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Regulatory ambiguity is a risk-appetite decision the sponsor must make with full information (C). (A) accepts unquantified legal exposure on the PM's authority. (B) is conservative but removes the sponsor's choice over a meaningful cost trade-off. (D) is sometimes a useful follow-up but typically too slow to be the first action and may not yield a definitive answer in time.
