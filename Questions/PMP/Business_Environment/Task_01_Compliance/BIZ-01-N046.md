# Question

A project manager leading a pharmaceutical packaging-line upgrade is triaging three quality findings raised in the same week. Each involves a batch already produced. Match each finding to the most proportionate immediate response.

## Items

- **1**: A batch record shows the validated cleaning step was performed and electronically time-stamped, but the operator's confirming signature was not captured before the record was locked. The batch is still in quarantine.
- **2**: Three batches were run against a work instruction that, it now emerges, was never updated to reflect a control parameter tightened in the master validation protocol six weeks ago; the recorded readings for all three sit within the older, wider limit but outside the newer one.
- **3**: A batch was produced and released to the warehouse under a work instruction whose pressure tolerance had been transcribed one decimal place too wide; some units may have been sealed outside the validated range.

## Matches

- **A**: Open an investigation with containment and a recall assessment for product that has left the controlled area.
- **B**: Raise a documentation corrective action to capture the missing record entry and reinforce the record-completion control.
- **C**: Open a CAPA to correct the work instruction to the validated requirement and assess the conformance status of the affected batches.

## Answer

1-B, 2-C, 3-A

## Explanation

All three findings touch both paperwork and produced batches, so the match turns on what is actually at risk, not on surface cues. Finding 1 is a pure record-completion gap on a step that was performed and independently time-stamped, with the batch still contained in quarantine — a documentation corrective action is proportionate (1-B). Finding 2 is a systemic procedure defect: the instruction lagged the validated protocol, and the readings are conforming to the wrong limit, so a CAPA to fix the instruction and assess the three affected batches fits — there is a control-system gap to remediate but no indication product has escaped containment (2-C). Finding 3 is the only one where potentially non-conforming product has already left the controlled area, making investigation, containment and a recall assessment the proportionate response (3-A). The trap is that 2 and 3 both stem from a faulty work instruction; what separates them is that 3's product is released and possibly out of validated range, escalating it from CAPA to investigation-and-recall.
