# Question

While planning a new release, you realise the organisation has been applying an incorrect interpretation of an industry regulation across several projects over multiple years. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Apply the corrected interpretation on your project only, without flagging the wider historical gap.
- **B**: Keep the existing interpretation to avoid disrupting other teams, contracts, and ongoing work.
- **C**: Report the finding to compliance and legal so they can assess exposure and plan a response.
- **D**: Wait for the next regulator audit cycle to raise the issue and then respond reactively.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A systemic regulatory exposure is beyond the PM's authority and must be routed to the functions that own organisational risk (C). (A) fixes one project but leaves systemic breach unaddressed — and creates an uncomfortable inconsistency. (B) knowingly continues non-compliance. (D) forgoes the substantial leniency many regulators give for voluntary self-disclosure.
