# Question

Internal audit issues three "management action" findings on your project mid-execution, each with target close dates before go-live. The team views audit as a distraction. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Negotiate softer findings with the auditor informally so the team is not distracted from delivery activities.
- **B**: Close the findings administratively at the target date regardless of whether the underlying actions have fully completed.
- **C**: Assign the findings to the PMO to handle centrally so the core project team can focus exclusively on delivery.
- **D**: Own each finding, assign single accountable owners, track remediation in the risk register, and report progress.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Audit findings are project work and are managed with explicit ownership, tracking, and reporting (D). (A) seeks to dilute findings rather than address them. (B) is closure by paperwork and is itself an audit issue waiting to happen. (C) pushes accountability away from the team that owns the issue.
