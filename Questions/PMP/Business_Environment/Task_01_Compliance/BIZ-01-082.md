# Question

At the start of a project to build a community childcare centre, the project manager wants to make sure no compliance obligation is missed before design begins. Which action best supports identifying the obligations that will apply?

## Options

- **A**: Begin design and address each regulation as inspectors raise it during construction.
- **B**: Consult the relevant regulators, legal advisers, and codes to identify the compliance requirements applicable to the facility.
- **C**: Reuse the compliance checklist from the organisation's last office-fit-out project unchanged.
- **D**: Assume standard building regulations apply and proceed on that basis.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Identifying applicable obligations up front means going to the authoritative sources — regulators, legal advisers, and the governing codes — for the specific facility type before design locks in decisions (B). Addressing regulations only as inspectors raise them (A) is reactive and invites costly rework and enforcement during construction. Reusing an office-fit-out checklist unchanged (C) ignores that a childcare facility carries distinct safety, ratio, and safeguarding requirements an office does not. Assuming only standard building regulations apply (D) is precisely the gap the planning step exists to close, as childcare attracts additional licensing and safety obligations.
