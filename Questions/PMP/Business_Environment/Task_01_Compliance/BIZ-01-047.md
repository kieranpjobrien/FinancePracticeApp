# Question

A major customer contract requires you to notify them within 24 hours of any "material" cybersecurity incident. The team has just discovered a contained intrusion that did not affect customer data. Engineering argues notification is unnecessary. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Withhold notification, as the contained intrusion did not affect any customer data.
- **B**: Document the incident internally and brief the customer at the next scheduled quarterly review.
- **C**: Notify the customer immediately as a precaution, regardless of the materiality assessment.
- **D**: Convene legal and security to assess whether the incident is material and act within the window.

## Answer

D

## Explanation

"Material" is contractually defined and the PM should not interpret it alone; legal and security should make the call within the notification window (D). (A) interprets the contract unilaterally based on a favourable view of the facts. (C) over-notifies without analysis and may breach other clauses or precedents. (B) bypasses the 24-hour contractual obligation entirely.
