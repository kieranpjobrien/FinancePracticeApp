# Question

Your team wants to adopt a promising new technology on a highly regulated project. The regulator has not explicitly approved or disallowed it. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Avoid the technology entirely until the regulator publishes explicit written approval for its use.
- **B**: Adopt the technology now and deal with any regulator questions or challenges if they arise later.
- **C**: Engage compliance and legal to assess the technology and seek regulator guidance if needed.
- **D**: Adopt the technology and log a thorough decision record as mitigation against future challenge.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Regulatory silence is not permission; it needs expert assessment and, when material, proactive engagement (C). (A) may forgo real benefit without needing to. (B) is "forgiveness-not-permission" which is risky in regulated sectors. (D) documents the choice but skips the required analysis.
