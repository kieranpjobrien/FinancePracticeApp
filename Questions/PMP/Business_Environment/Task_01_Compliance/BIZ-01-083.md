# Question

An agile team has been releasing a tax-filing product every two weeks. The product owner, under pressure from the business, has for three consecutive sprints deferred a mandated change to a tax-calculation rule that took legal effect last month, choosing revenue features instead. Each release since the rule changed has therefore computed some filings incorrectly. The project manager has raised it informally without effect. What should the project manager do now?

## Options

- **A**: Continue raising the deferral at each sprint review and trust the product owner to prioritise it eventually.
- **B**: Escalate formally to the governance body that the product is shipping non-compliant calculations in live releases.
- **C**: Instruct the team to implement the rule change without the product owner's agreement.
- **D**: Add a prominent disclaimer to the product stating that some calculations may be affected by recent rule changes.

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The product is already in breach in production, repeated informal raises have failed, and the consequence is incorrect statutory filings, so the project manager must escalate formally to governance with the facts so an accountable authority can compel correction (B). Continuing to raise it gently and trusting eventual prioritisation (A) has demonstrably not worked and lets known non-compliance keep shipping. Overriding the product owner and directing the team (C) usurps the prioritisation role and creates a governance breach of its own. A disclaimer (D) neither makes the calculations compliant nor discharges the legal obligation, and arguably advertises a known defect while leaving it unfixed; the situation has crossed from a backlog conversation into a compliance escalation.
