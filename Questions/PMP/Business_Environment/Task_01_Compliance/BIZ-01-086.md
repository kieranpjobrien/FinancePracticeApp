# Question

A project manager is classifying the compliance obligations on a fintech project by their source, so the right owner and verification route can be assigned to each. Match each obligation to the category that best describes its source.

## Items

- **1**: A national law requiring customer funds to be held in segregated accounts.
- **2**: An internal corporate policy mandating two-person approval for production deployments.
- **3**: A payment-card industry standard the product must certify against to process card payments.
- **4**: A clause in the client contract requiring monthly third-party penetration testing.

## Matches

- **A**: Organisational (internal) requirement.
- **B**: Industry or sector standard.
- **C**: Legal or regulatory requirement.
- **D**: Contractual obligation.

## Answer

1-C, 2-A, 3-B, 4-D

## Explanation

A national law on fund segregation is a statutory obligation, so item 1 is legal or regulatory (C). A two-person deployment rule set by the company itself is an internally imposed control, so item 2 is organisational (A). A payment-card standard the product must certify against is set by an industry body, so item 3 is an industry or sector standard (B). A penetration-testing requirement written into the client contract binds the project through the agreement, so item 4 is contractual (D). Classifying by source matters because each category carries a different owner, enforcement body, and consequence of breach, so confusing, say, a contractual term with a legal one would misdirect both the verification route and the escalation path.
