# Question

A change request adds a feature that will bring the product into the scope of a new financial regulation that did not previously apply. The business owner says the feature is "only" a small addition. What should the PM do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change because the addition is small and the business owner has authority.
- **B**: Approve the change subject to a separate compliance project starting after launch.
- **C**: Update the change request with the full compliance impact and route through the change control board.
- **D**: Deny the change to avoid the regulatory exposure entirely.

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The change request must reflect its true cost — including new compliance scope — and be decided by the CCB with full information (C). (A) underestimates impact and bypasses governance. (B) splits the obligation across an artificial boundary, leaving go-live exposed. (D) is overreach; the CCB may well approve the change with the compliance work scoped in.
