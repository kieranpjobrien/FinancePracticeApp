# Question

During a hospital IT rollout, an executive on the project board phones the project manager almost daily to approve minor scheduling decisions that sit well within the agreed stage tolerances. What is the BEST response in line with manage by exception?

## Options

- **A**: Continue seeking the executive's approval for each minor decision to keep them comfortable
- **B**: Make the in-tolerance decisions using delegated authority and report progress through the agreed reports
- **C**: Escalate every scheduling decision as an exception to be safe
- **D**: Ask the executive to take over day-to-day management of the stage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Manage by exception delegates authority to the project manager to act freely while forecasts stay within tolerance, which also protects efficient use of senior management time. (A) Daily approvals defeat the principle and waste the executive's time. (C) Raising in-tolerance items as exceptions misuses the escalation route. (D) Day-to-day management is the project manager's role, not the executive's.
