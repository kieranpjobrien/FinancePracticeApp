# Question

A supplier on a solar-farm construction project has delivered the grid connection design, but the customer rejects it, saying it is 'not what we wanted'. On checking, the project manager finds the product description for the design contained no quality criteria. What should the project manager do to prevent this recurring on future products?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the design anyway to maintain the relationship with the supplier
- **B**: Ask the customer to write the criteria after each product is delivered
- **C**: Remove product descriptions from the process to speed up delivery
- **D**: Ensure each product description defines clear quality criteria agreed before the product is built

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The dispute arose because no measurable quality criteria were agreed in advance, contrary to the 'focus on products' principle; defining them up front gives an objective basis for acceptance. (A) Accepting an unsuitable product does not address the cause. (B) Writing criteria after delivery repeats the very problem. (C) Removing product descriptions would worsen scope and acceptance disputes.
