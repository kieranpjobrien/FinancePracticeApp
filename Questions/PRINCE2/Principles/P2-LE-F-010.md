# Question

How does the 'learn from experience' principle apply during the closing of a PRINCE2 project?

## Options

- **A**: All lessons logged during the project are discarded once the project closes
- **B**: Only positive lessons are retained; problems are excluded to protect the team
- **C**: Lessons that could benefit future projects are identified and passed on
- **D**: Lessons are forwarded solely to the project's suppliers, not to the host organisation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

As a project closes, the principle requires that lessons useful to future projects are reviewed and passed on, typically via a lessons report or the end project report. (A) discarding lessons defeats the purpose of capturing them. (B) both what went well and what went badly are valuable lessons. (D) lessons are passed to those who can use them, including the host organisation, not just suppliers.
