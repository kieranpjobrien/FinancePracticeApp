# Question

A project manager produces every PRINCE2 management product in full for a very small, low-risk project, simply because the templates exist. Which tailoring concept does this MOST clearly fail to apply?

## Options

- **A**: Continued business justification
- **B**: Manage by exception
- **C**: Avoiding mechanical, robotic use of the method
- **D**: Learn from experience

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Tailoring exists to prevent mechanical, robotic application of PRINCE2; producing every product in full regardless of need is exactly the bureaucratic box-ticking that tailoring is meant to avoid. (A), (B) and (D) are genuine PRINCE2 principles but none specifically addresses applying the method thoughtlessly by rote; the failure here is treating PRINCE2 as a checklist rather than tailoring it to the project.
