# Question

The project board of a banking compliance programme receives an exception report showing the deviation cannot be contained within the overall project tolerances set at authorisation. What should the project board do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve an exception plan and continue without informing anyone above the board
- **B**: Direct the project manager to re-baseline the project on its own authority
- **C**: Escalate the forecast breach to corporate, programme or customer management for a decision
- **D**: Instruct the project manager to reduce scope until the numbers fit, without further reference

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When a deviation cannot be kept within project tolerance, the project board has reached the limit of its delegated authority and must escalate to corporate, programme or customer management. (A) and (B) Approving an exception plan or re-baselining only applies to deviations within the board's authority, i.e. stage-level exceptions inside project tolerance. (D) Cutting scope to disguise a project-level breach still requires the level above to agree, as benefits and the business case are affected.
