# Question

What does the PRINCE2 principle 'focus on products' require a project to define before any planning of activities takes place?

## Options

- **A**: The activities and tasks the team will perform
- **B**: The detailed project budget and cash-flow forecast
- **C**: The products to be delivered and their quality criteria
- **D**: The resource calendar for every team member

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The 'focus on products' principle states that a PRINCE2 project agrees and defines its products, including their quality criteria, before deciding the activities needed to produce them. (A) Activities are derived from the products, not the other way round. (B) and (D) are inputs to planning but are not what the principle prioritises; the principle is fundamentally about products, not money or people schedules.
