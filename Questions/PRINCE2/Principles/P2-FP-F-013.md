# Question

Which statement correctly describes the order of work implied by the 'focus on products' principle?

## Options

- **A**: Agree the activities first, then infer which products result from them
- **B**: Agree the budget first, then choose products that fit within it
- **C**: Agree the project schedule first, then define products to fill the time
- **D**: Agree the products and their quality criteria first, then plan the activities to produce them

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The principle requires products and their quality criteria to be agreed before the activities needed to produce them are planned, so the plan is driven by required outputs. (A) Inferring products from activities reverses the correct sequence. (B) and (C) let cost or time drive the project rather than the products it must deliver.
