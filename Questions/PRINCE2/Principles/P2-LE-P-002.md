# Question

Midway through a bridge refurbishment project, a team member discovers that a particular supplier's lead times are consistently double what was assumed, and this is delaying work. What is the BEST response in line with PRINCE2?

## Options

- **A**: Record the lesson in the lessons log and use it to adjust planning for the remaining work
- **B**: Keep the observation informal, as only the project manager may capture lessons
- **C**: Hold the lesson back until the end project report is written
- **D**: Treat it solely as a risk and make no entry about the lesson learned

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Everyone is responsible for seeking, recording and acting on lessons, so the team member's insight should be logged and applied to improve the rest of the project. (B) restricting lesson capture to the project manager contradicts the principle that everyone contributes. (C) delaying to closure wastes the chance to act now. (D) while a risk entry may also be appropriate, ignoring the lesson itself fails the principle.
