# Question

Besides reviewing the next stage plan, what else does the project board assess at a stage boundary before giving authorisation to proceed?

## Options

- **A**: The individual performance appraisals of every team member
- **B**: Whether the project manager should be replaced
- **C**: The continued business justification, confirming the project is still viable, desirable and achievable
- **D**: The procurement contracts of unrelated projects

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A stage boundary is the natural point to confirm continued business justification, so the board reviews the updated business case to check the project remains viable, desirable and achievable before committing to the next stage. (A) Personnel appraisals are not part of the stage gate decision. (B) Replacing the project manager is not a routine boundary activity. (D) Other projects' contracts are outside the scope of this project's decision.
