# Question

A retail bank must run a project to meet a new regulatory reporting deadline. The sponsor argues that because compliance is compulsory, there is no point preparing a Business Case. As project manager, how should you respond?

## Options

- **A**: Agree, since mandatory projects do not require a justification
- **B**: Prepare the Business Case only if time allows after the project is delivered
- **C**: Replace the Business Case with the text of the regulation itself
- **D**: Explain that a justification is still required, documenting the chosen option and the consequences of non-compliance

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Even compulsory projects need a justification: the Business Case records why this is the right response, the option chosen and the impact of not complying, supporting decisions on scope and spend. (A) repeats the misconception the principle corrects. (B) leaves the project unjustified during delivery, when decisions are actually made. (C) confuses the external trigger with the project's own documented justification.
