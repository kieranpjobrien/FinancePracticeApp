# Question

Midway through a renewable-energy installation project, two project board members disagree about whether to fund an expensive design change, and they cannot reach consensus. Who should make the final decision?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, since they manage the project day to day
- **B**: The senior supplier, because the change concerns the design they will build
- **C**: The executive, who holds single-point accountability for the project
- **D**: A majority vote of all project board members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PRINCE2 establishes single-point accountability, so when the board cannot reach consensus the executive, who is ultimately accountable, makes the final decision in the interest of the business case. (A) The project manager directs delivery, not board-level investment decisions. (B) The senior supplier represents one interest only and cannot decide alone. (D) The board operates under the executive's accountability rather than by majority vote.
