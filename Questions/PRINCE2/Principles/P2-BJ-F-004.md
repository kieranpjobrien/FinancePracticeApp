# Question

A project is being undertaken purely to comply with new legislation. What does the "Ensure continued business justification" principle say about its justification?

## Options

- **A**: A justification is still required, even though there may be no choice about whether to do the project
- **B**: Mandatory projects are exempt from needing a documented justification
- **C**: A justification is only required if the project also delivers a financial return
- **D**: The justification can be replaced by a copy of the relevant legislation

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Even compulsory or mandatory projects require a justification; the principle applies to all projects. The justification simply documents why the project is the chosen response (for example, the consequences of non-compliance and the option selected). (B) is the common misconception the principle corrects. (C) wrongly limits justification to financial return. (D) confuses the trigger for the project with its documented justification.
