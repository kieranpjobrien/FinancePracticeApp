# Question

In PRINCE2, how does a management stage differ from a technical stage (sometimes called a delivery step)?

## Options

- **A**: They are two names for exactly the same thing
- **B**: A management stage is always shorter than a technical stage
- **C**: A management stage is a section of the project the project board controls; a technical stage is a set of specialist techniques or skills
- **D**: A technical stage can only ever contain one management stage

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A management stage is the unit of control the project board uses, bounded by go/no-go decision points, whereas a technical stage groups work by the specialist skills or techniques used (for example, design or build). (A) They are distinct concepts that may or may not align. (B) There is no fixed rule that a management stage is shorter. (D) Several technical stages may sit within one management stage, or a single technical stage may span several management stages.
