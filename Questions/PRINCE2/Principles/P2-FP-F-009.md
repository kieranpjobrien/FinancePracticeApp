# Question

What is the difference between a product breakdown structure and a product flow diagram?

## Options

- **A**: The breakdown structure shows the sequence of creation; the flow diagram shows a hierarchy
- **B**: The breakdown structure shows a hierarchy of products; the flow diagram shows the sequence and dependencies of their creation
- **C**: Both show only the final product and nothing else
- **D**: The breakdown structure lists costs; the flow diagram lists risks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A product breakdown structure presents the products in a hierarchy, while a product flow diagram shows the sequence in which they must be created and their dependencies. (A) Reverses the two. (C) Both cover all the project's products, not just the final one. (D) Neither is primarily a cost or risk register.
