# Question

During a university e-learning project, the project manager notices the approved Business Case has not been looked at since initiation, even though two stages have passed. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave the Business Case alone, as it only needs approval once at the start
- **B**: Archive the Business Case because it is no longer needed once delivery is underway
- **C**: Review and update the Business Case and ensure the executive confirms continued justification at the stage boundary
- **D**: Ask the team manager to revise the Business Case in place of the executive

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The Business Case is a living product; the project manager maintains it and the executive, who owns it, confirms ongoing justification at each stage boundary. (A) treats justification as a one-off, breaching the principle. (B) discards the very product used to confirm justification during delivery. (D) misassigns ownership — the executive, not a team manager, is accountable for the justification.
