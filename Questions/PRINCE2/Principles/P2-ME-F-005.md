# Question

What benefit does "manage by exception" provide to senior management?

## Options

- **A**: It guarantees the project will finish ahead of schedule
- **B**: It makes efficient use of senior management time by removing the need for routine progress meetings
- **C**: It transfers all project risk to the team managers
- **D**: It removes the project board's accountability for the project

## Answer

B

## Explanation

By delegating authority within tolerances, "manage by exception" makes efficient use of senior management time — they are only involved when an exception arises rather than attending frequent progress meetings. (A) The principle controls deviation; it offers no guarantee of an early finish. (C) Risk accountability is not transferred wholesale to team managers. (D) The project board remains accountable throughout; only day-to-day authority is delegated.
