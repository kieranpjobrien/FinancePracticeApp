# Question

A software migration project needs a specialist database engineer for one stage only, but no such role has been defined in the project management team structure. What is the BEST response by the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Carry out the database work personally to avoid changing the team structure
- **B**: Leave the role undefined and ask the team to cover the work informally
- **C**: Escalate to corporate management and pause the stage until a permanent hire is made
- **D**: Define the role and responsibilities for the specialist and engage someone with the right competence for that work

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The principle requires engaging the right people with clearly defined responsibilities, so the project manager should define the specialist role and bring in someone competent for the work. (A) Doing the work personally ignores the need for the right competence and overloads the project manager. (B) Leaving roles undefined creates gaps in responsibility, which the principle exists to prevent. (C) A short-term specialist need does not warrant a permanent hire or halting the stage.
