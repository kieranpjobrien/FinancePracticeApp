# Question

At the boundary of the penultimate stage of a defence logistics project, the end stage report shows the stage delivered on plan, but the updated business case now forecasts negative net benefit because a policy change has removed the main beneficiary. The next stage plan is complete. What should the project manager do at the boundary?

## Options

- **A**: Recommend proceeding because the stage itself met its plan and the next stage plan is ready
- **B**: Present the end stage report and the revised business case and recommend the board consider stopping the project early
- **C**: Authorise the final stage to finish what was started, then close normally
- **D**: Re-baseline the business case to show a positive benefit so the project can continue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stage boundary tests continued business justification, and a project that no longer pays back should be stopped even if the current stage went well; the project manager presents the facts and recommends the board consider premature closure. (A) and (C) Good stage delivery does not justify continuing a project with negative benefit — that ignores continued business justification. (D) Manipulating the business case to force continuation is dishonest and breaches PRINCE2's principles.
