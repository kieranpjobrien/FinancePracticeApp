# Question

During quality review on a railway signalling software project, a delivered module is found to miss a stated quality criterion in its product description, but the customer now says the original criterion was too strict and the module is actually fine as built. What is the BEST response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Silently update the product description to match what was delivered and close the matter
- **B**: Record an off-specification and, because the customer wants to relax the criterion, also handle the requested baseline change through change control
- **C**: Reject the module outright as an off-specification and require it to be rebuilt to the original criterion
- **D**: Accept the module immediately, since the customer is satisfied with it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The shortfall against an agreed criterion is an off-specification, and the customer's wish to relax that baselined criterion is a request for change; both must go through proper change control rather than informal agreement. (A) Quietly altering a baseline bypasses governance. (C) Rebuilding ignores that the customer wants to change the criterion. (D) Accepting without amending the baseline leaves the product description inconsistent with what was agreed and approved.
