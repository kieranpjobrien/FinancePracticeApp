# Question

According to product-based planning, how should the activities in a plan be determined?

## Options

- **A**: They are derived from the products that need to be created
- **B**: They are copied from a previous project of similar duration
- **C**: They are set by the team members based on their availability
- **D**: They are dictated solely by the agreed time tolerance

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In product-based planning, once products are identified and defined, the activities needed to create, change or deliver those products are derived from them. (B) Reusing another project's activity list ignores this project's specific products. (C) Availability affects scheduling, not which activities exist. (D) Time tolerance constrains a plan but does not generate its activities.
