# Question

Why are quality criteria recorded in a product description rather than agreed informally once the product is built?

## Options

- **A**: So that the producer can decide later what 'good enough' means
- **B**: So that the project board can avoid reviewing the product
- **C**: So that the cost of the product can be calculated precisely
- **D**: So that the measurements a product must meet to be accepted are agreed in advance

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Recording quality criteria in advance gives an objective, agreed standard a product must meet to be accepted, supporting fair quality reviews and acceptance. (A) Leaving the standard to the producer's later judgement invites disputes, which is exactly what the principle avoids. (B) The board still has assurance responsibilities. (C) Quality criteria define acceptability, not cost.
