# Question

To save time on a tight pharmaceutical compliance project, a sponsor suggests dropping the business case entirely and tailoring it out, arguing the project "obviously has to happen". What is the BEST response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Agree, because a regulatory project does not need a business case
- **B**: Replace the business case with a verbal agreement recorded in the daily log
- **C**: Explain that continued business justification is a principle and cannot be tailored away, though the business case document itself may be kept proportionate
- **D**: Escalate to corporate management to overrule the principle for this project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Continued business justification is one of the seven principles, so it can never be tailored out; however, the business case document can be made proportionate to the project. (A) and (B) both remove the justification a principle requires; (D) is wrong because no level of management can authorise omitting a principle, as principles are universal to all PRINCE2 projects.
