# Question

On a logistics-tracking project, the senior user requests an attractive new feature. It adds clear value but increases cost enough that the overall benefits no longer outweigh the costs and risks. The change is within the project manager's delegated change budget. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the change because it falls within the delegated change budget
- **B**: Approve it because the senior user, who represents the users, has requested it
- **C**: Refer the decision to the project board because the change undermines the project's continued business justification
- **D**: Reject the change outright without referring it, as it harms the Business Case

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A change that destroys the project's viability is no longer a routine change-budget decision; it threatens continued business justification, so it must go to the project board even though funds are technically available. (A) is the near-miss — delegated authority covers cost, not the loss of justification. (B) treats user enthusiasm as overriding viability. (D) pre-empts a decision that rightly belongs to the board, which owns the Business Case.
