# Question

A cross-border logistics project brings together teams from three companies who have clearly defined roles in the PID but are working in silos, communicating poorly and missing handovers. Applying PRINCE2 7, what should the project manager focus on?

## Options

- **A**: Rewrite everyone's role descriptions in more detail, assuming the definitions are the root cause
- **B**: Escalate to the project board to replace the under-performing supplier teams
- **C**: Accept the silos as normal for multi-company projects and adjust the schedule to absorb the delays
- **D**: Actively manage the relationships and collaboration between the teams, recognising that defined roles alone do not guarantee effective working

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PRINCE2 7's People element makes clear that success depends on people and the relationships between them, so where roles are already defined the project manager should focus on improving collaboration, communication and handovers. (A) is the tempting near-miss: the roles are clear, so adding detail addresses the wrong problem. (B) Replacing teams is disproportionate before relationships have been addressed. (C) Tolerating silos and simply absorbing delay neglects the project manager's responsibility to lead and integrate people.
