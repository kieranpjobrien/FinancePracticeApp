# Question

On an airport baggage-system upgrade, the executive resigns suddenly with two stages remaining, and the senior user offers to "cover both roles" until the project ends so work can continue without interruption. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the offer, since the senior user already sits on the board and knows the project
- **B**: Ensure a new executive is appointed so single-point accountability for the business case is preserved and the interests stay balanced
- **C**: Let the project manager temporarily assume the executive role to keep delivery moving
- **D**: Continue with the board carrying the executive's responsibilities collectively until a replacement is found

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The executive provides single-point accountability and owns the business case, so the correct action is to appoint a replacement executive rather than dilute or merge that accountability. (A) is the tempting near-miss: although the senior user is already on the board, combining the roles collapses the business and user interests and removes independent ownership of the business case. (C) The project manager is accountable to the board and should not occupy a board role. (D) Spreading the executive's accountability across the board contradicts the single-point-of-accountability requirement.
