# Question

On an offshore wind project, three separate risks are each individually within the stage's risk tolerance, but the project manager forecasts that, taken together, their combined effect will tip the stage beyond its agreed risk tolerance. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Take no action, because each individual risk sits within tolerance
- **B**: Produce an exception report, because the aggregate forecast exceeds the stage risk tolerance
- **C**: Update the risk register and continue, deferring any decision to the next stage boundary
- **D**: Quietly raise the stage risk tolerance to accommodate the combined exposure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Manage by exception responds to a forecast that a tolerance will be exceeded, and tolerance applies to the aggregate position, so the combined exposure breaching stage risk tolerance triggers an exception report. (A) is the tempting near-miss: judging each risk in isolation overlooks that the stage tolerance applies to the overall forecast. (C) Logging and waiting ignores an active forecast breach. (D) The project manager has no authority to relax a tolerance the project board set.
