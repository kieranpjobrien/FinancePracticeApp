# Question

In the context of defining roles, what is the difference between being "accountable" and being "responsible" for project work?

## Options

- **A**: The accountable person is ultimately answerable for the outcome, while responsible people carry out the work
- **B**: There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable in PRINCE2
- **C**: Responsible people are answerable to corporate management, while accountable people do the hands-on work
- **D**: Accountability applies only to suppliers and responsibility only to users

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PRINCE2 distinguishes accountability — being ultimately answerable for an outcome and unable to delegate that answerability — from responsibility, which is carrying out the work and can be shared. (B) The terms are deliberately distinct. (C) reverses the meanings. (D) Both concepts apply across all interests and roles, not to particular parties only.
