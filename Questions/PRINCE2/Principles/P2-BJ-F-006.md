# Question

PRINCE2 states that a viable project should be desirable, viable and achievable. Which of the following best describes what "viable" means in this context?

## Options

- **A**: The balance of costs, benefits and risks makes the project worth doing
- **B**: The project is capable of delivering the products and outcomes
- **C**: The project has a sufficiently positive balance of benefits over the disadvantages and costs of doing it
- **D**: The project's products will be wanted and used once delivered

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In PRINCE2 a justifiable project is desirable, viable and achievable: "viable" means there is a sufficiently favourable balance of benefits against the costs and risks of carrying out the project. (B) describes "achievable" — the capability to actually deliver. (D) leans towards "desirable" — that the outcome is wanted. (A) blends the ideas but does not isolate the specific definition of viable as cleanly as (C).
