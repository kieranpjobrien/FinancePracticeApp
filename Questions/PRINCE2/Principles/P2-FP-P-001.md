# Question

On a hospital records digitisation project, the project manager is keen to show momentum and has begun building a detailed activity schedule and assigning team members, but the products to be delivered have not yet been identified or described. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue with the activity schedule, as it demonstrates progress to stakeholders
- **B**: Ask the team to define their own products as they carry out the activities
- **C**: Pause and first identify and define the required products, then derive the activities from them
- **D**: Escalate to the project board to request more time before any planning starts

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The 'focus on products' principle and product-based planning require products and their quality criteria to be defined before activities are derived; the project manager has the sequence reversed. (A) Visible momentum on the wrong foundation risks scope creep. (B) Letting the team invent products during delivery undermines agreed scope. (D) No board escalation is needed simply to plan correctly.
