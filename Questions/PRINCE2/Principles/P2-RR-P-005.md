# Question

For a council waste-management project, the proposed senior supplier is the same external company that will profit most from selling the equipment, and it has also been suggested they take the senior user role to "save time". What should the project manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Advise that the user interest be represented by the council's operational staff, keeping the interests appropriately separate
- **B**: Accept the arrangement, since fewer board members will speed up decisions
- **C**: Remove the senior supplier role and let the supplier act only as a team manager
- **D**: Ask the executive to also take the senior user role on top of the business interest

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The three interests must be genuinely represented, and the user interest should be voiced by those who will operate the service — the council's staff — not by the supplier whose commercial interest would conflict. (B) Speed does not justify collapsing distinct interests into a conflicted role. (C) The supplier still legitimately holds the senior supplier role; the issue is the user representation. (D) Loading the user interest onto the executive blurs the business and user perspectives the board is meant to balance.
