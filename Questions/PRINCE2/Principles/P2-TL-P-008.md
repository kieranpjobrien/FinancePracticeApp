# Question

On a small local-government project, the same individual is asked to act as both project manager and team manager, and another person will combine the executive and senior user roles. A project manager worries this is not "proper PRINCE2". What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse all role combination, as every PRINCE2 role must be held by a separate person
- **B**: Combine the executive and supplier interests into one role to simplify the board further
- **C**: Drop the senior user role entirely because the executive can represent everyone
- **D**: Allow appropriate combining of roles as legitimate tailoring, provided accountabilities are not compromised and the executive role remains clearly accountable

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Tailoring permits combining roles on smaller projects as long as the responsibilities are still discharged and no conflicts of interest are introduced, with a single executive remaining accountable. (A) is the tempting near-miss: PRINCE2 does allow some roles to be combined, so an outright ban is wrong; (B) creates a conflict of interest by merging the opposing supplier and business or user interests; (C) breaches the defined roles principle by leaving the user perspective unrepresented on the board.
