# Question

A project will deliver its product using an agile delivery approach with short iterations and a self-organising team. The project board asks whether PRINCE2 can still be used. What is the BEST response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: PRINCE2 cannot be used because it is incompatible with agile delivery
- **B**: PRINCE2 can be used only if the agile approach is abandoned in favour of fixed stages and detailed up-front plans
- **C**: PRINCE2 can be tailored to work with agile, keeping all principles while adapting practices, controls and management products to the agile way of working
- **D**: PRINCE2 can be used by keeping the directing and managing layers fully formal and exempting the delivery team from any PRINCE2 governance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

PRINCE2 is designed to be tailored to the delivery method, including agile, retaining all seven principles while adapting practices, controls and management products to suit short iterations and a self-organising team. (A) wrongly treats the two as incompatible; (B) is the tempting near-miss: it keeps PRINCE2 but sacrifices the agile delivery the project actually needs, which is the wrong trade-off; (D) severs the delivery team from governance such as work packages and tolerances, breaking the link the manage by exception principle relies on.
