# Question

While preparing the outline business case during start-up of an airport baggage-system upgrade, the executive recalls that two earlier upgrades across the industry overran badly because of integration testing. How should this experience BEST be used?

## Options

- **A**: Record the lesson and reflect it when shaping the approach and estimates for this project
- **B**: Disregard it, because lessons belong only in the lessons log at project closure
- **C**: Note it privately, since the executive is not responsible for recording lessons
- **D**: Raise it only if the project later overruns, to explain the delay

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Lessons from previous and external projects should be sought at the start and acted upon when shaping the project's approach, plans and estimates. (B) lessons are sought and applied throughout, not reserved for closure. (C) everyone, including the executive, is responsible for seeking, recording and acting on lessons. (D) acting only after an overrun ignores the chance to prevent it.
