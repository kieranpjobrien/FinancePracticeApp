# Question

Which statement correctly distinguishes the project's outputs from the basis of its continued business justification?

## Options

- **A**: The justification rests on the outputs delivered, regardless of whether any benefits follow
- **B**: Outputs and benefits are the same thing in PRINCE2, so the distinction does not matter
- **C**: The justification rests on the expected benefits and outcomes, not merely on producing the outputs
- **D**: Benefits are relevant only after the project closes and so cannot affect justification

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Continued justification depends on the outcomes and benefits the outputs are expected to enable, not on the act of producing outputs alone. (A) is wrong because delivering outputs that yield no benefit destroys the justification. (B) conflates outputs (what is built) with benefits (the measurable improvement they enable). (D) is incorrect — expected benefits underpin the justification throughout, even though many are realised after closure.
