# Question

Under "manage by exception", when should a level of management escalate to the level above it?

## Options

- **A**: At the end of every management stage regardless of progress
- **B**: When a tolerance is forecast to be exceeded
- **C**: Whenever an issue of any kind is raised
- **D**: Only after a tolerance has actually been breached

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Escalation occurs when a tolerance is forecast to be exceeded, so the higher level can decide on corrective action before control is lost. (A) Stage boundaries are a separate control and do not in themselves require an exception. (C) Routine issues are handled within tolerance, not escalated. (D) PRINCE2 acts on a forecast breach, not after the event, which would defeat the purpose of early warning.
