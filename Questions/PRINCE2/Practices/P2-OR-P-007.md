# Question

On a small charity website project, the same person has been proposed as both Executive and Senior Supplier because the charity is also building the site in-house. The Project Manager is concerned about a conflict of interest. What is the BEST course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the single Executive but ensure the supplier interest is represented separately and that Project Assurance is set up to safeguard objectivity in board decisions
- **B**: Allow it, since combining roles is normal on small projects and saves time
- **C**: Remove the Executive role and let the Senior Supplier run the project board
- **D**: Appoint the Project Manager as the Senior Supplier to separate the interests

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PRINCE2 allows roles to be combined on small projects but warns that the Executive and Senior Supplier interests can conflict, so the supplier interest should be represented and assurance arranged to protect objective board decisions. (B) ignores the genuine conflict between owning the business case and supplying the work; (C) leaves the project without its single accountable Executive; (D) breaches the principle that the Project Manager should not also hold a board role, importing a fresh conflict.
