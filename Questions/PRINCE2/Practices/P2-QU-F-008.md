# Question

Within a product description, what is the relationship between quality criteria and quality tolerances?

## Options

- **A**: Quality criteria define the acceptable range of variation; quality tolerances define the target measure
- **B**: Quality criteria and quality tolerances are interchangeable terms for the same attribute
- **C**: Quality criteria apply to the project as a whole; quality tolerances apply only to the final product
- **D**: Quality criteria specify the measures a product must meet; quality tolerances define the permissible range around those measures

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Quality criteria are the measurable characteristics a product must possess, and quality tolerances define the acceptable range of variation permitted around those criteria. (A) reverses the definitions; (B) is wrong because they are distinct concepts; (C) is wrong because both are defined per product within its product description.
