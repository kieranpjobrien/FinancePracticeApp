# Question

On a vehicle-fleet telematics project, final testing shows a tracking unit will not meet its agreed battery-life quality criterion, and no tolerance remains. The project manager believes the shortfall is acceptable to the business but it breaches the baseline specification. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly accept the unit, since the project manager judges the shortfall acceptable to the business
- **B**: Raise an off-specification and escalate it to the project board for a decision on whether to grant a concession
- **C**: Reject the unit outright and instruct the supplier to rebuild it before reporting anything
- **D**: Record the shortfall as a lesson and move on, as battery life is only a minor attribute

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A product that fails its agreed quality criteria with no tolerance left is an off-specification, which is beyond the project manager's authority to accept; it must be escalated to the project board, who may grant a concession. (A) the project manager cannot accept a baseline breach alone; (C) rebuilding without board involvement removes the board's decision rights and may waste effort; (D) recording a lesson does not resolve the unaccepted product.
