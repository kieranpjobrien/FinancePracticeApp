# Question

Which of the following is an example of an in-process quality method rather than an appraisal method?

## Options

- **A**: A quality review meeting held after a document is drafted
- **B**: Building quality into a product as it is created, for example through pair programming or following a coding standard
- **C**: An independent inspection of a finished component against its quality criteria
- **D**: A formal test of the completed product before acceptance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In-process methods build quality into a product as it is being developed, such as adhering to standards or using paired working, whereas appraisal methods check a product against its criteria after it has been produced. (A), (C) and (D) are all appraisal methods because they assess a product once it already exists.
