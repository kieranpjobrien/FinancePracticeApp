# Question

During initiation of a university campus redevelopment, the Project Manager realises that local residents, the students' union and a heritage body all have a strong interest but were not considered when drafting communications. What should the Project Manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until a complaint arises before engaging these groups
- **B**: Revisit stakeholder identification and analysis, then update the communication management approach to define how and when each group will be engaged
- **C**: Add the heritage body to the project board as a Senior User
- **D**: Delegate all external communication to the Senior Supplier

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective organising depends on identifying all stakeholders and capturing their needs in the communication management approach, which should be revisited when new stakeholders emerge. (A) is reactive and risks damaging engagement; (C) wrongly elevates an external interest group to a directing role; (D) misassigns stakeholder engagement to the supplier interest rather than handling it through the agreed communication approach.
