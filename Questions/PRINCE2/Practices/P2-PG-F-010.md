# Question

How do management stages support the control of progress in PRINCE2?

## Options

- **A**: They allow the project board to delegate day-to-day delivery and review the project at defined control points before committing further resources
- **B**: They remove the need for the project board to set tolerances
- **C**: They guarantee that the project will be delivered within its original cost target
- **D**: They are technical phases defined solely by the specialist work being undertaken

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Management stages give the project board key decision points where it reviews the end stage report and decides whether to authorise the next stage, reflecting the principle of managing by stages. (B) is wrong because the board still sets stage tolerances; (C) overstates the effect; (D) confuses management stages with technical or delivery stages, which can overlap and are not the basis for board control.
