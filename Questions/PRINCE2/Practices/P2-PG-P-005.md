# Question

On a rail signalling project, a stakeholder verbally agrees a small change to a baselined product during a corridor conversation and asks the project manager to "just get on with it". What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Implement the change immediately because the stakeholder is senior
- **B**: Update the product baseline silently to reflect the new requirement
- **C**: Add a note to the daily log and wait for the stakeholder to raise it formally
- **D**: Capture the change as an issue in the issue register and handle it through the agreed change-control procedure

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A change to a baselined product is an issue and must be recorded in the issue register and assessed through change control before any action is taken, preserving baseline integrity. (A) acting on a verbal instruction skips assessment and authority; (B) altering a baseline without control undermines the reference point for measuring progress; (C) relegating a live change to an informal log fails to manage it through the proper procedure.
