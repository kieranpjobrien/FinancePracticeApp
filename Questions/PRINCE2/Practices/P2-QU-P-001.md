# Question

On a hospital IT migration project, a team manager completes a quality review of a data-mapping document and the product passes. The team manager is unsure where the outcome should be recorded. What should happen next?

## Options

- **A**: The result is recorded in the quality register, updating the entry for that quality activity
- **B**: The result is recorded only in the team manager's checkpoint report to the project manager
- **C**: The result is added to the issue register because a quality check has taken place
- **D**: The result is held informally until the stage end, when all quality outcomes are reported together

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The quality register is the management product that records the planned and actual results of all quality activities, so the passed review should be logged there. (B) the checkpoint report communicates progress but is not the system of record for quality results; (C) the issue register is for issues, not routine quality outcomes; (D) delaying recording breaks the audit trail the register is designed to provide.
