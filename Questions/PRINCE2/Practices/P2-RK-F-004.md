# Question

In assessing a risk, what does "proximity" refer to?

## Options

- **A**: How close the risk is, in distance, to the project team
- **B**: The likelihood that the risk will occur
- **C**: When the risk is likely to occur or have its impact
- **D**: The financial size of the risk's potential impact

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Proximity describes when a risk is likely to materialise, or when its impact would be felt, helping to prioritise responses by timing. (B) describes probability and (D) describes impact, which are separate assessment factors. (A) is a literal misreading; proximity in PRINCE2 is about time, not physical distance.
