# Question

A start-up is being asked to launch a new mobile payments product. At the very start, before any detailed planning, the project board wants just enough justification to decide whether to commit funds to initiation. Which management product should the Project Manager prepare to meet this need?

## Options

- **A**: The detailed business case, fully costed to two decimal places
- **B**: The benefits management approach with confirmed post-project review dates
- **C**: The outline business case, giving proportionate justification to authorise initiation
- **D**: The end project report summarising achieved benefits

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In Starting Up a Project the outline business case provides proportionate, just-enough justification for the board to decide whether initiation is worthwhile. (A) a fully detailed case belongs to Initiating, once more is known; (B) the benefits management approach is developed later and is not the vehicle for this go/no-go decision; (D) an end project report is produced at closure. Producing an outline first avoids wasting effort on a project that may never be authorised.
