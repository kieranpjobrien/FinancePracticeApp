# Question

A twelve-month CRM-replacement project is closing on schedule, but its headline benefit, a 15% rise in customer retention, can only be measured after a full year of live operation. As the Project Manager prepares to close the project, what should they ensure happens regarding this benefit?

## Options

- **A**: Delay project closure until the retention benefit has been measured a year from now
- **B**: Treat the benefit as realised because the CRM has gone live successfully
- **C**: Estimate the likely retention figure now and report it in the end project report as confirmed
- **D**: Record in the benefits management approach that the retention benefit will be reviewed post-project, and hand responsibility for that review to the appropriate body

## Answer

D

## Explanation

PRINCE2 expects benefits that fall due after closure to be scheduled in the benefits management approach, with responsibility for the post-project review passing to corporate, programme management or the customer. (A) is wrong because projects are not held open simply to await benefit measurement; (B) go-live delivers the output, but the benefit is the later measurable improvement, not the deployment itself; (C) reporting an estimate as a confirmed result is misleading and not a genuine confirmation. The distinction between delivering a product and confirming its benefit is the crux here.
