# Question

A university is initiating a campus-wide energy-efficiency programme. The outline business case from start-up listed expected carbon and cost savings only as broad estimates. During Initiating a Project, what must the Project Manager do to develop the business case appropriately?

## Options

- **A**: Carry the broad estimates straight into delivery, since they were already approved at start-up
- **B**: Replace the business case with the benefits management approach, as the two cover the same ground
- **C**: Refine the business case into the detailed version and ensure the benefits, with their measurement defined in the benefits management approach, are robust enough to commit to delivery
- **D**: Defer all benefit detail until closure, when the actual savings can finally be measured

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Developing the business case in Initiating means refining the outline into a detailed, robust case, while the benefits management approach defines how each benefit will be measured, both being prepared before committing to delivery. (A) is the near-miss: start-up approval was only to authorise initiation, not to lock in unrefined estimates for the whole project; (B) the detailed business case and the benefits management approach are distinct, complementary products, not substitutes; (D) deferring all benefit detail leaves the board unable to verify justification before delivery. The detailed case and a credible measurement plan together support the decision to proceed.
