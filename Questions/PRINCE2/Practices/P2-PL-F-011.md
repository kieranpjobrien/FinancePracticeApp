# Question

In scheduling, what is an external dependency?

## Options

- **A**: A dependency between two activities both within the plan being prepared
- **B**: A reliance of an activity in the plan on something delivered or provided from outside the plan, such as another project
- **C**: A dependency that exists only between products in the product breakdown structure
- **D**: A tolerance set by the project board on the duration of an activity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An external dependency is a reliance of the plan's activities on a product, decision or action provided from outside the plan, for example a deliverable from another project. (A) describes an internal dependency. (C) the product breakdown structure shows hierarchy, not dependencies. (D) a tolerance is a permissible deviation, not a dependency.
