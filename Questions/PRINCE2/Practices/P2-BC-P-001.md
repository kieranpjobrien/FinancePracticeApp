# Question

Midway through a hospital records-digitisation project, a new government grant suddenly halves the customer's expected running costs after go-live, materially improving the forecast benefits. What should the Project Manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Update the business case to reflect the revised benefits and bring it to the project board's attention
- **B**: Continue unchanged, since the business case is fixed once the project has been authorised
- **C**: Quietly increase the project's scope to consume the improved margin
- **D**: Ask the Senior Supplier to revise the benefits figures

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Maintaining the business case requires the Project Manager to keep it current as circumstances change and to surface significant changes for the board, who own the justification. (B) is wrong because the business case is a living product, not frozen; (C) expanding scope without board approval breaches tolerance and the manage-by-exception principle; (D) benefits are the Senior User's and Executive's concern, not the Senior Supplier's. Better-than-expected news still triggers a maintain-and-verify cycle.
