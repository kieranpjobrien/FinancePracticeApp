# Question

Which statement correctly distinguishes quality assurance from project assurance?

## Options

- **A**: Quality assurance is an independent corporate function, whereas project assurance acts on behalf of the project board
- **B**: Quality assurance is performed by the project manager, whereas project assurance is performed by team managers
- **C**: Quality assurance checks individual products, whereas project assurance only reviews the business case
- **D**: Quality assurance and project assurance are the same activity carried out at different times

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Quality assurance is provided independently of the project, typically by a corporate, programme or organisational function, while project assurance is the project board's own responsibility for monitoring all aspects of the project's performance and products. (B) is wrong because project assurance belongs to the board, not team managers; (C) understates project assurance, which covers the whole project; (D) is wrong because they are distinct in both source and scope.
