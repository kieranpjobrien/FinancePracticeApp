# Question

The Organizing practice describes three project interests that must be represented on the project board. What are they?

## Options

- **A**: Time, cost and quality
- **B**: Sponsor, customer and contractor
- **C**: Business, user and supplier
- **D**: Directing, managing and delivering

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The Organizing practice defines the three project interests as business, user and supplier; each is represented on the project board by the Executive (business), Senior User(s) (user) and Senior Supplier(s) (supplier). (A) lists performance targets, not interests; (B) and (D) are not PRINCE2 terms for the project interests — (D) actually describes the three levels of the management team.
