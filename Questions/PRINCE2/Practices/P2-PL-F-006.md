# Question

Which statement best distinguishes a product flow diagram from a product breakdown structure?

## Options

- **A**: The product flow diagram shows the hierarchy of products, whereas the product breakdown structure shows their sequence
- **B**: Both show the same information but the product flow diagram is used only at project level
- **C**: The product flow diagram lists acceptance criteria, whereas the product breakdown structure lists quality methods
- **D**: The product flow diagram shows the sequence in which products are created and their dependencies, whereas the product breakdown structure shows their hierarchy

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The product breakdown structure is a hierarchy of all products, while the product flow diagram arranges those products into the sequence in which they must be created, showing dependencies between them. (A) reverses the two. (B) they convey different information and both can be used at any plan level. (C) neither diagram lists acceptance criteria or quality methods; those belong in product descriptions.
