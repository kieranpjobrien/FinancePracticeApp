# Question

Which activity is part of quality planning rather than quality control?

## Options

- **A**: Carrying out a quality review of a completed product
- **B**: Recording the result of a test in the quality register
- **C**: Defining the quality criteria and quality methods for a product
- **D**: Gaining acceptance of a product that has passed its quality checks

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Quality planning establishes what quality means for the project and its products, including defining quality criteria, methods, tolerances and responsibilities before work begins. (A), (B) and (D) are all quality control activities, which involve carrying out the checks, recording the outcomes and confirming that products meet their defined criteria.
