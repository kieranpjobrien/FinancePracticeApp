# Question

How does the detailed business case produced in Initiating a Project differ from the outline business case?

## Options

- **A**: The detailed business case removes the cost and benefit figures so the board can focus on scope
- **B**: The detailed business case is a fuller, refined version used to confirm the project remains justified before committing to delivery
- **C**: The detailed business case is owned by the Project Manager rather than the Executive
- **D**: The detailed business case replaces the project brief as the project's mandate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The outline created in Starting Up is refined and expanded in Initiating into the detailed business case, giving the board a robust basis to authorise the project. (A) is wrong because costs and benefits are made more complete, not removed; (C) ownership stays with the Executive throughout; (D) the mandate precedes the project and is not replaced by the business case. The progression matches PRINCE2's principle of proportionate, just-enough planning.
