# Question

What is a product breakdown structure?

## Options

- **A**: A hierarchy of all the products to be created by a plan
- **B**: A diagram showing the sequence and dependencies of the products to be created
- **C**: A description of the project's final product and its acceptance criteria
- **D**: A list of the activities required to create each product

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A product breakdown structure is a hierarchical diagram that breaks down all the products a plan will deliver into their constituent sub-products. (B) describes the product flow diagram, which shows sequence and dependencies. (C) describes the project product description. (D) refers to activity identification, which follows product-based planning rather than forming part of the breakdown structure.
