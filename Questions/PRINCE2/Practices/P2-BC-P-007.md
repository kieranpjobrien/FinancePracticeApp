# Question

On a rail-signalling upgrade, the Senior User proposes adding a sizeable new feature that would lift benefits but also raise costs beyond the project's cost tolerance. The Senior User insists the change is clearly worthwhile and asks the Project Manager to revise and approve the updated business case directly. What should the Project Manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Approve the revised business case, since the Senior User has confirmed the benefits outweigh the costs
- **B**: Refer the proposed change and the revised justification to the Executive, who owns the business case, via the project board
- **C**: Implement the feature first and update the business case afterwards to keep momentum
- **D**: Reject the proposal outright because the business case has already been baselined

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Although the Senior User specifies benefits, the Executive owns the business case and the project board must decide on a change that breaches cost tolerance, so the matter is escalated rather than settled by the Project Manager. (A) is the tempting near-miss: the Senior User's view on benefits does not give them or the Project Manager authority to approve the case or sanction a tolerance breach; (C) acting before approval bypasses governance; (D) a worthwhile change should be assessed, not dismissed merely because a baseline exists. Ownership of the case, not enthusiasm for the benefit, decides who approves it.
