# Question

What is a key benefit of using product-based planning before identifying activities?

## Options

- **A**: It removes the need to estimate the effort required for the work
- **B**: It focuses the plan on the products to be delivered and their quality, providing a sound basis for then identifying the activities needed to create them
- **C**: It allows the project board to take over day-to-day control of the stage
- **D**: It guarantees the project will be delivered within its original cost tolerance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Product-based planning ensures the plan is built around clearly defined, quality-specified products, which then form the basis for identifying the activities and dependencies needed to produce them. (A) estimating is still required after products and activities are identified. (C) the project board does not take over day-to-day control. (D) no technique can guarantee delivery within tolerance.
