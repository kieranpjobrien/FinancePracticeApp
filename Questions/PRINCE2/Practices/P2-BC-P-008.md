# Question

At an end-stage assessment for a banking app project, all products for the stage are complete and within quality criteria, and the stage finished on budget. However, a regulatory change now means one of the three core benefits can no longer be realised. The Project Manager is preparing advice for the project board. What is the most appropriate basis for the board's decision to proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Re-verify the business case in light of the lost benefit, and let the board judge whether the remaining justification still warrants continuing
- **B**: Proceed, because the stage delivered its products on time, to cost and to quality
- **C**: Proceed, because two of the three benefits remain and a majority is sufficient
- **D**: Close the project immediately, since any loss of a benefit removes justification

## Answer

A

## Explanation

End-stage assessment is precisely when the business case is verified, and a lost benefit means the board must reassess whether the project remains desirable, viable and achievable before authorising the next stage. (B) is the near-miss: meeting the stage's delivery targets does not prove the project is still justified, since justification rests on benefits, not output alone; (C) PRINCE2 weighs the value of remaining benefits against costs and risks, it does not count benefits as a majority vote; (D) loss of one benefit does not automatically end justification, that is the board's judgement after re-verification. The point is that delivery success and business justification are assessed separately.
