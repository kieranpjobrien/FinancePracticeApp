# Question

At the end of a museum exhibition fit-out project, the supplier reports every product has passed its individual quality checks and proposes the project close immediately. Before recommending closure, what must the project manager confirm?

## Options

- **A**: That each product description's quality criteria were met, which alone is sufficient to close
- **B**: That the project's output meets the acceptance criteria in the project product description and the required acceptance has been obtained from those identified to give it
- **C**: That the quality register contains no outstanding entries, which constitutes formal acceptance
- **D**: That the senior supplier has signed off all products, since acceptance is a supplier responsibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Passing individual product checks is necessary but not sufficient; closure requires confirming the overall output satisfies the acceptance criteria in the project product description and that formal acceptance has been gained from the parties identified to provide it. (A) product-level conformance does not by itself constitute customer acceptance of the whole; (C) a clean quality register is not the same as formal acceptance; (D) acceptance is given by the customer or user side, not granted by the senior supplier.
