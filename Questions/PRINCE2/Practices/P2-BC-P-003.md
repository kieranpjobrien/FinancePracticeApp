# Question

On a renewable-energy installation project, a competitor launches an identical service and a key forecast benefit, market share, now looks unachievable. The current management stage is still within all its tolerances. What is the BEST response by the Project Manager?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the current stage finishes naturally before mentioning the change, since the stage is in tolerance
- **B**: Reduce the project budget to protect the margin and carry on
- **C**: Cancel the project immediately on their own authority
- **D**: Escalate the threat to the project board now, as the continued business justification may no longer hold

## Answer

D

## Explanation

A loss of business justification is a project-level concern that must be escalated to the project board straight away, even if the stage remains within tolerance, because the principle of continued business justification governs the whole project. (A) waiting risks investing further in an unviable project; (B) cutting the budget does not restore the lost benefit; (C) only the board, not the Project Manager, can decide to prematurely close a project. The board may then re-verify the business case and decide whether to continue.
