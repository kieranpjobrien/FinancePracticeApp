# Question

Many of a project's benefits are realised only after the project has closed. Under PRINCE2 7, how is the confirmation of these post-project benefits handled?

## Options

- **A**: They are abandoned, because PRINCE2 responsibility ends at project closure
- **B**: They are confirmed by the Project Manager, who remains accountable indefinitely
- **C**: They are confirmed by reopening the project board for a further management stage
- **D**: They are confirmed through activities scheduled in the benefits management approach, often carried out by corporate, programme or the customer

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The benefits management approach schedules benefit reviews that may fall after closure, with responsibility passing to corporate, programme management or the customer organisation. (A) is wrong because PRINCE2 deliberately plans for post-project measurement; (B) the Project Manager's role ends at closure and the Senior User is accountable for realising benefits; (C) projects are not reopened simply to measure benefits. This ensures value is checked even when it accrues long after delivery.
