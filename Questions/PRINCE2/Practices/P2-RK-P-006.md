# Question

To respond to a threat that a key contractor might go out of business, a project manager arranges a second standby contractor. She then realises the standby firm has limited experience, which could itself introduce quality problems. What should she do about this newly recognised quality concern?

## Options

- **A**: Record it as a secondary risk in the risk register and assess and plan a response for it
- **B**: Ignore it, because it arises from an approved response and is therefore already covered
- **C**: Cancel the standby arrangement, since any response that creates a new risk is invalid
- **D**: Treat it as an issue and raise it directly with the project board

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A risk that arises as a direct consequence of implementing a response is a secondary risk, and PRINCE2 requires it to be identified, recorded and assessed like any other risk. (B) wrongly assumes responses cannot generate new exposure. (C) overreacts, as creating a manageable secondary risk does not invalidate an otherwise sound response, and (D) is premature escalation before the new risk has even been assessed against tolerance.
