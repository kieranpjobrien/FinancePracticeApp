# Question

A project manager preparing a stage plan for a data-centre relocation finds that one specialist migration activity is highly uncertain and could take anywhere between two and six weeks. The board wants realistic timescales. What is the BEST approach?

## Options

- **A**: Refine the estimate using the planning information available, document the assumptions and uncertainty, and reflect it when agreeing tolerances with the board
- **B**: Use the most optimistic estimate of two weeks to keep the board satisfied
- **C**: Leave the activity out of the stage plan until the duration is known with certainty
- **D**: Ask the team manager to commit to a fixed two-week figure regardless of the uncertainty

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Estimating in PRINCE2 acknowledges that estimates are uncertain; the project manager should produce the best estimate possible, record the assumptions and degree of uncertainty, and use this when agreeing appropriate tolerances. (B) and (D) force an unrealistic optimistic figure and hide the risk. (C) omitting a known activity produces an incomplete and misleading plan; the uncertainty should be managed, not avoided.
