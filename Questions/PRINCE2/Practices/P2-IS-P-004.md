# Question

Midway through a museum refurbishment project, a key subcontractor warns the project manager that a shortage of skilled conservators may delay later work, though nothing has been missed yet and no baseline has changed. How should the project manager classify and handle this?

## Options

- **A**: As a request for change, recorded and sent to the change authority
- **B**: As an off-specification, because a product may not be delivered
- **C**: As a problem/concern, captured and examined like any formal issue
- **D**: As a risk only, with no entry needed in the issue register

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A reported event that has happened and needs management action, but is neither a change to a baseline nor a current shortfall against specification, is a problem/concern. (A) is wrong because nothing proposes altering an agreed baseline. (B) is wrong because no product yet fails its specification — the warning is about a future possibility. (D) misreads it: although the resourcing shortage has a risk dimension, the warning itself is a present issue that should be captured and examined.
