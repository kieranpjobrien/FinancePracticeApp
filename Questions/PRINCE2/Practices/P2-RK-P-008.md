# Question

A pharmaceutical project's risk register shows a data-breach threat with a high inherent assessment. After encryption and access controls are implemented, the project manager updates the register. To give the project board an accurate basis for its decision, which figure should she ensure is recorded against the threat?

## Options

- **A**: Only the inherent risk, as it reflects the worst case
- **B**: An average of the inherent and residual assessments
- **C**: The residual risk after the responses, so the board sees the true remaining exposure
- **D**: Neither, because the responses have removed the threat completely

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Once responses are in place, the residual risk is the level that actually remains, and recording it gives the project board an accurate picture for decision-making. (A) the inherent figure alone overstates the live exposure now that controls exist. (B) averaging the two is not a PRINCE2 concept, and (D) is wrong because controls such as encryption reduce but do not necessarily eliminate the threat.
