# Question

Midway through a residential housing development, the customer tells the project manager that the finished show home "must simply look impressive to buyers." The project manager is concerned this cannot be objectively confirmed at acceptance. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the statement as a customer quality expectation and proceed without further definition
- **B**: Record the statement as a risk in the risk register because it is too vague to deliver
- **C**: Ask the supplier to define suitable acceptance criteria on the customer's behalf
- **D**: Work with the customer to translate the expectation into prioritised, measurable acceptance criteria in the project product description

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Customer quality expectations are deliberately high level and must be refined into measurable, prioritised acceptance criteria so the product can be objectively accepted; that refinement belongs in the project product description and is agreed with the customer. (A) leaves acceptance unverifiable; (B) misclassifies a definition gap as a risk; (C) wrongly transfers the customer's role for setting acceptance criteria to the supplier.
