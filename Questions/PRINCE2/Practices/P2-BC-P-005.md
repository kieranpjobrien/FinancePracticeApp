# Question

A council is running a project to launch an online permit-application portal whose main aim is to cut average processing time from ten days to two. The Senior User wants assurance this improvement will actually be measured. Which action best addresses this?

## Options

- **A**: Add the target to the project plan's milestone list and consider it covered
- **B**: Capture how, when and by whom the processing-time improvement will be measured in the benefits management approach, including the current baseline
- **C**: Rely on the end project report alone, produced at closure, to record the improvement
- **D**: Leave measurement to corporate management to arrange after closure without documenting it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The benefits management approach is the product that defines how each benefit will be measured, when, by whom and against what baseline, which is exactly what the Senior User needs. (A) a milestone records delivery of the portal, not realisation of the benefit; (C) the end project report may note expected benefits but cannot capture improvements realised after closure; (D) leaving it undocumented gives no assurance the measurement will happen. A clear baseline of ten days is essential, since improvement cannot be proven without it.
