# Question

On a bridge refurbishment project, the project manager has identified a threat that adverse weather could damage exposed steelwork. A site engineer is best placed to fit protective covering when needed, but a senior user has the authority and accountability to oversee this risk. How should the project manager assign responsibility?

## Options

- **A**: Make the senior user the risk owner and the site engineer the risk actionee
- **B**: Make the site engineer the risk owner and the senior user the risk actionee
- **C**: Make the project manager both the risk owner and the risk actionee
- **D**: Leave the risk unassigned until the weather forecast worsens

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The risk owner should be the person best placed to manage and monitor the risk — here the senior user with the authority and accountability — while the risk actionee carries out the response, which the site engineer is best placed to do. (B) reverses these roles. (C) needlessly concentrates both roles on the project manager when better-placed individuals exist, and (D) leaves a known threat unmanaged contrary to continual risk management.
