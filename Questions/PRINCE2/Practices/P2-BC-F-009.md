# Question

In the PRINCE2 7 path from project work to value, how is a benefit correctly distinguished from an output and an outcome?

## Options

- **A**: A benefit is the specialist product the project delivers, while the outcome is its later use
- **B**: A benefit is the change in working practice that the output makes possible
- **C**: A benefit is the measurable improvement resulting from an outcome that is regarded as an advantage by stakeholders
- **D**: A benefit and an output are the same thing expressed in financial terms

## Answer

C

## Explanation

In PRINCE2, outputs enable outcomes (changes), and benefits are the measurable improvements arising from those outcomes that stakeholders regard as advantages. (A) confuses a benefit with an output; (B) describes an outcome, not a benefit; (D) wrongly equates outputs and benefits. Keeping these distinct lets the project board judge whether the value, not just the deliverable, justifies the investment.
