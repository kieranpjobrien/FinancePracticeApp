# Question

On a hospital IT modernisation project, the proposed project board lists two Executives — the Director of Finance and the Chief Medical Officer — so that both funding and clinical interests are equally accountable. What should the Project Manager advise?

## Options

- **A**: Accept the arrangement, as shared accountability strengthens decision-making
- **B**: Recommend a single Executive, with the other senior individual taking the Senior User role to represent the clinical interest
- **C**: Ask both Executives to alternate the role month by month
- **D**: Escalate to corporate management to create a third tie-breaking Executive

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PRINCE2 requires a single Executive who is ultimately accountable, so one individual should hold that role while the clinical interest is represented as Senior User. (A) and (C) breach the single-Executive rule and blur accountability; (D) compounds the error by adding more Executives rather than resolving the duplication.
