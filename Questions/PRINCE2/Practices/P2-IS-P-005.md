# Question

A software product owner sits as the delegated change authority on an insurance claims project, with a defined change budget. A request for change is assessed and its cost falls comfortably within that budget. What is the correct PRINCE2 action?

## Options

- **A**: The change authority may approve the change and authorise its funding from the change budget
- **B**: The project board must still ratify every change before it proceeds
- **C**: The project manager should approve it because it is within budget
- **D**: The request should be deferred until the next stage boundary regardless of urgency

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The project board delegates approval of changes within an agreed change budget to the change authority, so a change costing within budget can be approved and funded by that authority without returning to the board. (B) defeats the purpose of delegation and the change budget. (C) has the wrong role deciding — approval rests with the change authority, not the project manager. (D) imposes an artificial delay PRINCE2 does not require for in-budget changes.
