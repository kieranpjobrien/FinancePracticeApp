# Question

How do customer quality expectations differ from acceptance criteria?

## Options

- **A**: Customer quality expectations are measurable, whereas acceptance criteria are general statements of intent
- **B**: Customer quality expectations apply to individual products, whereas acceptance criteria apply only to the management stage
- **C**: Customer quality expectations are general statements about the quality required, whereas acceptance criteria are a prioritised, measurable list the product must meet to be accepted
- **D**: Customer quality expectations are defined by the supplier, whereas acceptance criteria are defined by the project manager

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Customer quality expectations are high-level statements describing the quality the customer wants from the final product, and these are then refined into acceptance criteria, a prioritised and measurable list of attributes the product must satisfy to be accepted. (A) reverses the two; (B) misallocates them, as both relate to the project product; (D) is wrong because both originate from the customer side, not the supplier or project manager.
