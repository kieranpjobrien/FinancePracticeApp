# Question

A project manager is assembling the team for a new airport baggage-handling upgrade. Several specialists are joining from different organisations and are unsure what is expected of them. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Leave each specialist to work out their own remit so they feel empowered
- **B**: Give every member identical responsibilities to keep things simple
- **C**: Wait until problems arise before clarifying who does what
- **D**: Agree clear role descriptions setting out each member's responsibilities, accountabilities and required skills

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Building an effective team starts with clear role descriptions so that each member understands their responsibilities, accountabilities and the skills expected, especially when people come from different organisations. (A) leaving remits undefined invites gaps and overlaps; (B) identical responsibilities ignore the complementary skills the project needs; (C) deferring clarity until problems emerge is reactive and avoidable.
