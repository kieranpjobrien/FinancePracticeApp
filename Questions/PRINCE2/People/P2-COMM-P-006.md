# Question

On an offshore wind project, an external regulator requires a formal monthly compliance update in a prescribed format. The project's standard highlight report does not meet this format. The communication management approach is silent on the regulator. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Record the regulator's specific information needs, format and frequency in the communication management approach and provide the update accordingly
- **B**: Send the regulator the standard highlight report, since it already summarises progress
- **C**: Treat the regulatory update as a separate piece of correspondence and leave the communication management approach unchanged
- **D**: Ask the project board to take over all contact with the regulator so the project manager is not involved

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The communication management approach should capture each stakeholder's specific needs, including a regulator's prescribed format and cadence, so the obligation is planned and met consistently. (B) forcing the standard report onto a regulator ignores their defined requirements. (C) handling it ad hoc and leaving the approach silent is the tempting near-miss, but it means the requirement is undocumented and may be missed if the project manager changes. (D) the board represents stakeholder interests but day-to-day regulatory reporting still needs to be planned in the approach, not simply offloaded.
