# Question

A renewable-energy project runs with engineers spread across three countries who have never met in person. The project manager notices misunderstandings and a growing sense of disconnection among the team. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Insist that all team members relocate to a single office for the duration of the project
- **B**: Reduce communication to written status reports only, to avoid further confusion
- **C**: Ask the project board to replace the overseas engineers with local staff
- **D**: Establish clear communication norms and regular structured contact suited to a virtual team to build trust and shared understanding

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Virtual teams need deliberate communication structures and regular contact to build the trust and shared understanding that proximity would otherwise provide, so establishing these norms is the right first step. (A) forced relocation is disproportionate and often impractical; (B) cutting back contact worsens disconnection rather than fixing it; (C) replacing capable staff is an extreme response that ignores the real issue of communication.
