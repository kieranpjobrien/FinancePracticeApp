# Question

A project manager on a museum refurbishment is overseeing a junior team member who is enthusiastic but new to the type of task and unsure how to proceed. Applying situational leadership, what should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Delegate the task fully and step back, trusting the member's enthusiasm
- **B**: Reassign the task to the most senior available person and give the junior nothing to do
- **C**: Provide clear direction and close guidance on how to carry out the task, while encouraging their enthusiasm
- **D**: Leave the member to learn entirely by trial and error to build resilience

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Situational leadership matches style to development level; an eager but inexperienced person needs direction and guidance on the 'how', combined with encouragement. (A) full delegation suits a competent, committed person, not a novice. (B) sidelining the junior wastes their willingness and offers no development. (D) abandoning them to trial and error risks errors and disengagement without the support the situation calls for.
