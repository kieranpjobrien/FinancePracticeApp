# Question

What distinguishes active stakeholder engagement from simply keeping stakeholders informed?

## Options

- **A**: Active engagement involves only the project board, while informing covers everyone else
- **B**: Active engagement seeks to understand, involve and influence stakeholders through two-way dialogue, not just push information at them
- **C**: Active engagement means sending reports more frequently than usual
- **D**: Active engagement replaces the need for the project to manage stakeholder expectations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Active engagement is a two-way effort to understand stakeholders, involve them and influence their attitudes, whereas informing is a one-way push of information. (A) engagement is not limited to the board; it spans the relevant stakeholder population. (C) increasing report frequency is still one-directional informing. (D) managing expectations is part of active engagement, not something it removes.
