# Question

Midway through a software integration project, two specialists from different supplier firms repeatedly clash, and the friction is spreading and harming team morale. What is the BEST action for the project manager as a leader?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the friction and hope it resolves once the technical work is finished
- **B**: Remove both specialists from the project to protect morale
- **C**: Report both to their employers and request replacements before investigating
- **D**: Address the conflict openly, facilitate a constructive discussion, and refocus both specialists on the shared project purpose

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Building a high-performing culture relies on trust, open communication and shared purpose, so the project manager should surface the conflict and realign the parties around common goals. (A) ignoring conflict lets it erode the culture further. (B) removing both is disproportionate and loses needed expertise. (C) escalating to employers before any attempt to resolve it skips the leadership response and damages relationships.
