# Question

On a retail logistics project, the project manager needs analytical work from a data analyst who reports to the finance department, not to the project. The analyst is busy and has not started the agreed task. What is the BEST first response?

## Options

- **A**: Instruct the analyst directly to drop their finance work and prioritise the project
- **B**: Build rapport with the analyst, explain why the work matters to the project's benefits, and agree a realistic commitment
- **C**: Raise an exception to the project board immediately
- **D**: Reassign the task to a project team member who lacks the required analytical skills

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The project manager has no line authority over the analyst, so PRINCE2 7's guidance on influencing without formal authority points to building a relationship and explaining the value to gain genuine commitment. (A) issuing instructions exceeds the project manager's authority over another department's staff. (C) escalation is premature before attempting to influence. (D) reassigning to an unqualified person trades a relationship problem for a quality risk.
