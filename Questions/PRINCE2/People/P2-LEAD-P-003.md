# Question

A council is rolling out a new digital permitting system. During the final stage, frontline staff who will use the system voice anxiety and quiet resistance about the change. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Press ahead with go-live as planned and address complaints afterwards
- **B**: Engage the affected staff, listen to their concerns, explain the benefits, and arrange support to help them adopt the new system
- **C**: Escalate the resistance to the senior supplier to resolve the staffing issue
- **D**: Treat the resistance as a risk to be closed by mandating compliance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leading the adoption of change means engaging affected people, hearing their concerns and supporting the transition so outputs become used outcomes that deliver benefits. (A) ignoring resistance until after go-live jeopardises adoption and the benefits. (C) the senior supplier is accountable for supplier-side delivery, not for end-user adoption. (D) mandating compliance addresses symptoms, not the engagement that genuine adoption requires.
