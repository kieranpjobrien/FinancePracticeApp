# Question

A software delivery project has slipped and will miss a milestone that a key business stakeholder is counting on. The slippage is within stage tolerance. What should the project manager do regarding communication?

## Options

- **A**: Say nothing until the milestone date passes, to avoid alarming the stakeholder prematurely
- **B**: Proactively and honestly inform the affected stakeholder of the slippage and its impact, in line with the communication management approach
- **C**: Escalate an exception to the project board even though tolerance has not been breached
- **D**: Report the milestone as on track and quietly try to recover the time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Transparency requires timely, honest communication of impacts to affected stakeholders, even when the variance is within tolerance and no escalation is needed. (A) staying silent until the date passes denies the stakeholder time to react. (C) raising a formal exception is inappropriate when tolerance is intact. (D) misreporting status as on track is dishonest and destroys trust once discovered.
