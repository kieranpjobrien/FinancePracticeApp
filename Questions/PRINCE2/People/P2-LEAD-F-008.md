# Question

In PRINCE2 7, why is leading the adoption of change considered essential rather than optional for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Because adoption is the responsibility of project assurance, who require regular leadership briefings
- **B**: Because a project's outputs only become outcomes and benefits when people actually adopt and use them
- **C**: Because adoption is a performance target that must be reported alongside cost
- **D**: Because PRINCE2 forbids closing a project until everyone has been retrained

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PRINCE2 7 links leadership of change to benefits realisation: outputs deliver value only once people change their behaviour and adopt them as outcomes, so guiding that adoption is core leadership work. (A) misattributes the responsibility to project assurance. (C) the targets are time, cost, quality, scope, benefits, risk and sustainability; adoption is not itself a target. (D) overstates a rule that PRINCE2 does not impose.
