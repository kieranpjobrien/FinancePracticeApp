# Question

A team member is highly skilled and experienced in the task but has recently become demotivated. Applying situational leadership as described in PRINCE2 7, which approach is MOST appropriate?

## Options

- **A**: A highly directive approach, giving detailed step-by-step instructions on how to do the task
- **B**: Withdrawing entirely and leaving the person to resolve their own motivation
- **C**: A supportive approach, listening, encouraging and rebuilding commitment rather than directing the technical work
- **D**: Removing the person from the team to protect the schedule

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Situational leadership matches style to competence and commitment; a competent but demotivated person needs support and encouragement, not technical direction. (A) directing the 'how' is redundant for someone already skilled and ignores the real issue of commitment. (B) full withdrawal abandons the leader's duty to engage. (D) removal is a disproportionate management reaction that skips the leadership response.
