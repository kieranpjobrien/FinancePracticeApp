# Question

Three weeks into a hospital records-digitisation project, the newly assembled team is arguing over priorities and individuals are challenging each other's approach. Delivery has not yet suffered. What is the BEST response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Replace the most vocal team members to restore harmony quickly
- **B**: Escalate to the project board as a team that is failing to perform
- **C**: Recognise this as a normal storming stage, facilitate open discussion and help the team agree ways of working
- **D**: Suspend all collaboration and have members work in isolation until the project ends

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The behaviour described is typical of the storming stage of team development, and the project manager's role is to facilitate the team through it towards agreed norms rather than treat it as failure. (A) removing people destroys capability and ignores a normal phase; (B) premature escalation misrepresents a developing team as a failing one; (D) isolating members prevents the very collaboration the team needs to mature.
