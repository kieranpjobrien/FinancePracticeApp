# Question

A pharmaceutical project's virtual team has reached the performing stage and works well remotely. A new phase brings in five additional members from a newly acquired company. The project manager wants to keep the team effective. What is the BEST course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Assume the established team's high performance will automatically carry the new members along
- **B**: Keep the new members in a separate sub-team with no contact with the existing members to avoid disruption
- **C**: Recognise that adding members effectively reforms the team, and deliberately invest in integrating them through clear roles, induction and communication
- **D**: Insist the whole enlarged team becomes co-located, since virtual teams cannot absorb new members

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Adding people changes the group's composition, so the team in effect returns to an earlier development stage and needs deliberate integration — clear roles, induction and strong communication — to regain effectiveness. (A) wrongly assumes performance is automatic and self-sustaining through a change in membership; (B) isolating the newcomers prevents integration and shared understanding; (D) is false, as virtual teams can absorb new members with the right support, and forced co-location is unjustified.
