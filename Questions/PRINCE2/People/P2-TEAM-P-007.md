# Question

On a defence software project, a persistent personal conflict between two senior engineers is now affecting morale and slowing delivery, and the project manager's facilitation attempts have not resolved it. The conflict sits within stage tolerances but threatens the team's effectiveness. What should the project manager do NEXT?

## Options

- **A**: Continue facilitating in the same way and wait for the engineers to settle their differences in time
- **B**: Take further appropriate action to manage the conflict, drawing on others such as line management or HR as needed, while keeping responsibility for the team's effectiveness
- **C**: Raise an exception to the project board, since any morale problem is by definition beyond the project manager's authority
- **D**: Reassign one engineer off the project immediately without further discussion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Resolving interpersonal issues is part of the project manager's responsibility for an effective team, and when initial facilitation fails the manager should escalate the approach — for example involving line management or HR — rather than abandon the issue. (A) repeating an unsuccessful tactic ignores that it has not worked; (C) is wrong because the matter is within tolerance, so it does not warrant a board exception, and morale is not automatically beyond the manager's remit; (D) is a disproportionate first resort taken without proper handling.
