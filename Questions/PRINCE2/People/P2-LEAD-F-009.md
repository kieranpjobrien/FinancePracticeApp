# Question

Which of the following is the clearest example of a project manager influencing through personal rather than positional power?

## Options

- **A**: Reminding a contractor of the penalty clause in their contract
- **B**: Citing their job grade to require a junior to comply
- **C**: Asking the project board to instruct a resisting team
- **D**: Drawing on their reputation for sound judgement and a trusted relationship to persuade a sceptical specialist to help

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Personal power derives from credibility, expertise and relationships, exactly what is used when reputation and trust persuade the specialist. (A) and (B) rely on contractual or hierarchical (positional) power. (C) substitutes the board's formal authority for the project manager's own influence, the opposite of influencing without authority.
