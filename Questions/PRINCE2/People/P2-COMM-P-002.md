# Question

On a road-bridge construction project, residents near the site have complained that they only hear about disruptive works through rumour. The communication management approach did not specify a channel for the local community. What is the BEST response from the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Refer all residents to the project board so they can field the queries directly
- **B**: Tell residents that construction schedules are confidential project information
- **C**: Issue a one-off apology letter and continue with the existing approach unchanged
- **D**: Update the communication management approach to include the community as a stakeholder group with a defined channel and frequency, such as regular local notices

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The fix is to recognise the community as a stakeholder group and define an appropriate channel and cadence in the communication management approach so information flows reliably. (A) routing residents to the board sidesteps the missing channel and overloads governance. (B) withholding routine schedule information breaches the expectation of transparency. (C) a single apology without changing the approach leaves the same gap that caused the problem.
