# Question

A senior stakeholder who controls a critical shared testing environment is unconvinced of an aerospace project's priority and keeps deprioritising the team's access. The stakeholder outranks the project manager and sits outside the project's authority. The project manager has already explained the timeline once. What is the BEST next action?

## Options

- **A**: Invoke the project manager's authority and formally direct the stakeholder to grant access
- **B**: Understand the stakeholder's own priorities and pressures, frame the project's needs in terms of their interests to build a persuasive case, and seek a workable agreement
- **C**: Immediately ask the executive to overrule the stakeholder
- **D**: Quietly arrange unofficial out-of-hours access without telling the stakeholder

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Influencing without formal authority means understanding the other party's motivations and framing requests in terms of their interests to win cooperation, which is the right next step before any escalation. (A) the project manager has no authority over a more senior, external stakeholder, so a formal direction is not available. (C) is the tempting near-miss: escalating to the executive may sometimes be needed, but jumping straight to it after a single explanation skips genuine influencing and can damage the relationship. (D) covert workarounds breach openness and create governance and trust risks.
