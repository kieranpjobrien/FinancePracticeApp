# Question

A manufacturing change project sends out detailed weekly bulletins, but the project manager has no idea whether the shop-floor teams actually read or understand them. What should the project manager do to improve the communication?

## Options

- **A**: Introduce a feedback mechanism, such as short briefings or a return channel, to confirm messages are understood and capture concerns
- **B**: Increase the length and frequency of the bulletins to be thorough
- **C**: Stop the bulletins, since unread communication is wasted effort
- **D**: Delegate all shop-floor communication to the supplier's account manager

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Effective communication is two-way, so adding a feedback loop confirms that messages are received and understood and surfaces concerns the bulletins alone would miss. (B) more one-way information does not establish whether it lands. (C) abandoning communication leaves the teams uninformed. (D) handing the relationship to a supplier contact does not address the missing feedback and may dilute ownership.
