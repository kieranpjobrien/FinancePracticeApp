# Question

In PRINCE2 7, what is the difference between being accountable and being responsible for an activity?

## Options

- **A**: Accountability can be shared among several people, but responsibility rests with only one person
- **B**: The person who is accountable answers for the outcome and cannot delegate that answerability, whereas responsibility for carrying out the work can be delegated
- **C**: Accountability and responsibility mean the same thing and are used interchangeably
- **D**: Responsibility sits with the project board, while accountability sits with the delivery teams

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In PRINCE2 7 a single person is accountable and answers for the result, and that answerability cannot be passed on, while the responsibility for actually performing the work may be delegated to others. (A) reverses the rule — accountability rests with one person and is not shared. (C) is wrong because the terms are deliberately distinct. (D) misallocates both terms, since accountability ultimately rises to the project board and Executive, not the delivery teams.
