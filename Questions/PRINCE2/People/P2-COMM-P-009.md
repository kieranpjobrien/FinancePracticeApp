# Question

A rail-upgrade project discovers that a planned approach will produce significantly more waste than forecast, worsening the project's sustainability performance target. An influential community group has specifically asked to be kept informed on environmental impacts. What should the project manager do FIRST?

## Options

- **A**: Resolve the waste issue internally and inform the community group only once a remedy is confirmed
- **B**: Omit the waste figures from external communication because they could attract negative publicity
- **C**: Capture the sustainability impact as an issue, and in line with the communication management approach and transparency, inform the community group of the impact and the steps being taken
- **D**: Refer the community group to the supplier responsible for the waste so the project stays neutral

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Transparency and the agreed communication approach require that a stakeholder who asked to be kept informed on environmental impacts is told promptly about the sustainability issue and the response, with the matter also captured as an issue for management. (A) waiting for a confirmed remedy is the tempting near-miss, but it withholds information the group explicitly requested and breaches transparency. (B) suppressing unfavourable data to avoid publicity is dishonest. (D) deflecting to the supplier evades the project's own accountability for stakeholder communication and its sustainability target.
