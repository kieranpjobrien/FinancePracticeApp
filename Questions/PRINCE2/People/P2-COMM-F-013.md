# Question

Managing stakeholder expectations is a key aim of communication in PRINCE2 7. Which statement best reflects how this is achieved?

## Options

- **A**: By promising stakeholders whatever outcome will keep them supportive
- **B**: By avoiding communication until the project has a confirmed result to report
- **C**: By setting realistic, evidence-based messages about scope, timing and benefits, and updating them as the project progresses
- **D**: By delegating all expectation management to a separate marketing function

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Expectations are managed by communicating realistic, fact-based information about what the project will and will not deliver, and by keeping stakeholders updated as circumstances change. (A) over-promising damages credibility and stores up disappointment. (B) silence breeds uncertainty and rumour, the opposite of managing expectations. (D) expectation management is integral to the project's own communication approach, not outsourced wholesale.
