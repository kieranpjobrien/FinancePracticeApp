# Question

Midway through a hospital IT system rollout, the project manager discovers that the pharmacy department, a significant user of the new system, was never identified as a stakeholder and has received no communications. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Wait until the next highlight report to mention the omission to the project board
- **B**: Ignore the gap, since the system will be delivered to all departments at go-live anyway
- **C**: Add the pharmacy department to the stakeholder analysis, assess their interest and influence, and update the communication management approach
- **D**: Cancel the affected work package until the pharmacy can be consulted in full

## Answer

C

## Explanation

When a missing stakeholder is found, the correct response is to bring them into the stakeholder analysis, assess their position, and revise the communication management approach so their needs are met. (A) delaying for a routine report leaves an important user disengaged longer than necessary. (B) assuming delivery alone equals engagement risks resistance and unmet requirements. (D) cancelling a work package is a disproportionate response to a communication gap that can be corrected directly.
