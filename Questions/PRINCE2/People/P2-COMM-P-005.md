# Question

A charity's fundraising-platform project has an influential trustee who keeps publicly stating the launch will include features that are actually out of scope. What is the BEST action for the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly expand the scope to match what the trustee has promised
- **B**: Engage the trustee directly with clear, evidence-based information on the agreed scope to realign expectations, escalating to the executive if needed
- **C**: Issue a public correction contradicting the trustee without speaking to them first
- **D**: Allow the misunderstanding to continue and deal with complaints after launch

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Managing expectations means engaging the stakeholder directly with accurate information about the agreed scope and, where their influence warrants it, involving the executive who owns the business case. (A) silently changing scope to fit a promise bypasses change control and tolerances. (C) a public contradiction without dialogue damages the relationship and the project's reputation. (D) ignoring the gap guarantees disappointment and complaints at launch.
