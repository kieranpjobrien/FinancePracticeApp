# Question

Towards the end of starting up a university campus refurbishment project, the Executive is keen to move quickly and asks the Project Manager to begin the building works straight after the Project Brief is approved. What should the Project Manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Start the building works immediately, since the Executive has authority to direct the project
- **B**: Produce the full Project Plan and begin the first delivery stage in parallel
- **C**: Produce a Stage Plan for the initiation stage and seek the project board's authorisation to initiate
- **D**: Skip initiation to save time and proceed directly into delivery

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Start-up concludes by producing the Stage Plan for the initiation stage so the board can authorise initiation; delivery cannot begin until the project has been properly initiated and authorised, honouring "manage by stages". (A) Even the Executive cannot leapfrog initiation; the board authorises initiation as a defined decision point. (B) Building the full Project Plan is initiation work and starting delivery in parallel bypasses the board's authorisation. (D) Skipping initiation discards the planning and justification that make delivery controllable and is contrary to PRINCE2.
