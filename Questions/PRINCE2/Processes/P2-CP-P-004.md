# Question

A retailer's new self-checkout rollout is complete, but the expected reduction in queue times can only be measured over the following six months. As the project closes, the project manager wonders how those benefits will be tracked. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Add the six-month benefit measurement to the project plan and keep the project open until it is done
- **B**: Update the benefits review plan so post-project benefits are measured after closure, with responsibility assigned to a standing authority
- **C**: Record in the end project report that the benefits have already been achieved
- **D**: Leave benefits tracking out of scope, as the project's job was only to deliver the checkouts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Benefits that materialise after the products are in use are handled through the benefits review plan, which is updated at closure to define post-project measurement and to assign it to corporate, programme management or the customer. (A) Keeping the project open for six months wastes the project organisation when delivery is complete. (C) Claiming benefits are already achieved would be untrue, as they cannot yet be measured. (D) Ignoring benefits realisation defeats the project's justification under the continued business justification principle.
