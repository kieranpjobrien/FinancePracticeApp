# Question

Why is the detailed business case refined after the project plan has been created during Initiating a Project?

## Options

- **A**: Because the project plan supplies the costs and timescales needed to confirm the project's justification
- **B**: Because the business case cannot exist until the project plan is approved by the board
- **C**: Because the project plan replaces the benefits management approach as evidence of value
- **D**: Because the business case is only finalised once delivery has begun

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The project plan provides the overall cost and timescale estimates that the detailed business case needs in order to confirm whether the project remains a worthwhile and viable investment. (B) An outline business case already exists from Starting Up; refinement does not depend on board approval of the plan. (C) The benefits management approach addresses benefit measurement, a separate concern. (D) The business case is refined within initiation, before delivery is authorised.
