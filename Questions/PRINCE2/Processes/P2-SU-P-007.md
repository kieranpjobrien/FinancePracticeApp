# Question

While preparing the outline Business Case during start-up of a renewable heating retrofit project, the Executive concludes the expected benefits will not justify the likely costs and that the project is not viable. What is the BEST course of action?

## Options

- **A**: Continue assembling the Project Brief and let the project board discover the problem at initiation
- **B**: Reduce the cost estimates in the outline Business Case so the numbers justify proceeding
- **C**: Proceed to Initiating a Project, where a stronger Business Case can be constructed
- **D**: Recommend through the Project Brief and the request to the board that the project should not proceed to initiation

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Start-up answers "is this a worthwhile and viable project?"; if the honest conclusion is no, the outputs and the request to the board should make clear that the project should not be initiated, applying "continued business justification" from the outset. (A) Concealing the finding to surface later wastes effort and misleads the board. (B) Manipulating estimates to force a positive case is dishonest and undermines the justification. (C) Pressing into initiation in the hope the case improves invests further in a project already judged unviable, contrary to PRINCE2.
