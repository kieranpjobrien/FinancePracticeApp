# Question

An aerospace manufacturer's corporate policy already mandates a detailed organisational quality assurance regime with prescribed audits and standards. While drafting the quality management approach during initiation, what should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Ignore the corporate regime and design a wholly independent project quality system
- **B**: Align the quality management approach with the organisation's existing quality standards and assurance requirements
- **C**: Omit the quality management approach entirely, relying solely on corporate policy
- **D**: Copy another project's quality management approach without reviewing the corporate standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The quality management approach should reflect and align with the host organisation's standards and quality assurance requirements, tailoring the project's quality regime to its environment. (A) Designing an independent system ignores mandatory corporate standards and the tailoring principle. (C) The approach is still required to describe how quality will be managed on this project, even where corporate policy exists. (D) Reusing another project's approach unexamined is the tempting near-miss, but it fails to account for the relevant standards and project context.
