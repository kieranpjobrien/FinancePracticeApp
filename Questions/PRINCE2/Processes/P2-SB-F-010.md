# Question

Managing a Stage Boundary chiefly supports which type of project board decision?

## Options

- **A**: A go/no-go decision on whether to proceed to the next stage
- **B**: A decision on which team member should perform a specific task
- **C**: A decision on the wording of an individual product description
- **D**: A decision on the daily schedule of checkpoint meetings

## Answer

A

## Explanation

By assembling the end stage report, the updated business case and the next stage plan, Managing a Stage Boundary gives the board the basis for a go/no-go (continue or stop) decision at each stage boundary. (B), (C) and (D) are operational details handled by the project manager or team manager, not strategic stage-gate decisions.
