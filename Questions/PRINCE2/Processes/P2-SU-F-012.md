# Question

Treating Starting Up a Project as a short, lightweight pre-project process — rather than fully planning the entire project up front — most directly supports which PRINCE2 principle?

## Options

- **A**: Continued business justification
- **B**: Focus on products
- **C**: Manage by stages
- **D**: Tailor to suit the project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Keeping start-up short and deferring full project planning until initiation reflects "manage by stages": PRINCE2 plans, delegates and controls in manageable chunks rather than detailing the whole project before it is even justified. (A) Continued business justification is served by the outline Business Case, but the staged, do-just-enough approach is the principle being illustrated here. (B) Focus on products concerns defining products and quality, not the timing of planning. (D) Tailoring adapts PRINCE2 to context, but it is not what the staged, minimal start-up specifically demonstrates.
