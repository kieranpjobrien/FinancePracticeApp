# Question

When accepting a work package, what should the team manager do before confirming agreement with the project manager?

## Options

- **A**: Produce a highlight report summarising expected progress
- **B**: Produce a team plan (where needed) and confirm the work can be done within the agreed tolerances and constraints
- **C**: Update the business case to reflect the cost of the work
- **D**: Authorise the work package on behalf of the project board

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In accept a work package the team manager reviews the requirements, produces a team plan if one is needed, and agrees that the work is achievable within the tolerances and constraints in the work package. (A) checkpoint reports, not highlight reports, are the team manager's output and come during execution. (C) the business case is owned by the executive, not the team manager. (D) authorising work packages is the project manager's responsibility.
