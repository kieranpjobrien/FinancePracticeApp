# Question

Midway through a regional rail-signalling upgrade, the customer withdraws funding and the project board instructs the project manager to stop the project. Eager to be helpful, the project manager begins archiving records and tells the team to down tools immediately. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Continue shutting everything down at once; speed minimises wasted cost
- **B**: Follow Closing a Project for a premature closure, including checking what has been produced and recommending how to salvage or make safe work in progress
- **C**: Ignore the instruction and finish the current stage first to preserve the products
- **D**: Hand the shutdown entirely to the team manager and step away

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A board instruction to stop early triggers Closing a Project as a premature closure, where the project manager must still confirm the status of products and advise on salvaging or making safe any partially completed work rather than abandoning it. (A) Down-tooling instantly risks leaving products unsafe and value unrecovered. (C) Overriding the board's decision to keep working breaches the board's authority. (D) Closure is the project manager's responsibility to coordinate, not something to delegate wholesale.
