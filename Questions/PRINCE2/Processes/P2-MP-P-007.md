# Question

During execution of a work package to install solar panels, the team manager discovers that the specified mounting brackets cannot bear the wind load required by the approved design, so a product cannot meet one of its quality criteria. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Quietly substitute stronger brackets and update the team plan, treating it as a minor delivery detail
- **B**: Capture the shortfall as an issue and raise it to the project manager, since the product cannot meet its agreed quality criteria
- **C**: Stop all work on the project until the project board convenes
- **D**: Deliver the work package as complete and flag the problem only in the closing checkpoint report

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A product that cannot meet its agreed quality criteria is an off-specification; the team manager must record it as an issue and raise it to the project manager rather than resolving it outside the controlled interface. (A) silently substituting components bypasses change control and the project manager's authority over the baseline. (C) halting the whole project is a disproportionate over-reaction at work-package level. (D) concealing a known off-specification until closure defeats the purpose of quality control and timely escalation.
