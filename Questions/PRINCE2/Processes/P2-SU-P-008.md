# Question

A Project Manager is assembling the Project Brief for an airport baggage-system upgrade. A stakeholder insists the brief should already specify every detailed product description, full quality tolerances and a complete work breakdown so nothing changes later. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Comply and produce all detailed product descriptions and the full work breakdown within the Project Brief
- **B**: Postpone assembling the Project Brief until every product can be fully specified
- **C**: Replace the Project Brief with the Project Initiation Documentation to capture the requested detail now
- **D**: Keep the Project Brief at the level needed for the initiation decision, leaving detailed product descriptions and planning for Initiating a Project

## Answer

D

## Explanation

The Project Brief should give a firm, full basis for the decision to initiate without descending into detailed planning; detailed product descriptions, quality tolerances and the work breakdown are developed during Initiating a Project under "manage by stages". (A) Loading the brief with full delivery detail is premature, wasteful and blurs the start-up and initiation boundary. (B) Delaying the brief until everything is specified stalls the very decision start-up exists to enable. (C) The PID is produced during initiation, not in place of the Project Brief during start-up.
