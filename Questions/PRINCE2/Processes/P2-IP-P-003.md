# Question

A retailer is initiating a project to launch a customer loyalty app expected to increase repeat purchases. The Executive asks how the organisation will know, after the app goes live, whether those benefits actually materialise. Which action best addresses this?

## Options

- **A**: Record the expected benefits in the risk management approach
- **B**: Add the benefits as acceptance criteria in the project product description
- **C**: Create the benefits management approach defining how and when benefit realisation will be measured
- **D**: Defer the question to the operations team to handle after closure

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The benefits management approach, created during Initiating a Project, defines how and when each expected benefit will be measured and reviewed, often after the product is in operational use. (A) The risk management approach concerns uncertainty, not benefit measurement. (B) Acceptance criteria confirm the product is fit for purpose, not whether downstream benefits are realised. (D) Simply deferring to operations leaves no defined plan; the approach is what assigns and schedules those measurements.
