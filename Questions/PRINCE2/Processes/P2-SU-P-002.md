# Question

A newly appointed Project Manager at a publishing house is starting up a project to launch a digital subscription platform. The organisation ran a similar, troubled e-commerce project two years ago. What is the BEST action during Starting Up a Project?

## Options

- **A**: Disregard the earlier project, as each project is unique and its issues will not recur
- **B**: Wait until Initiating a Project to review lessons, when the detailed plans are being written
- **C**: Seek out and capture relevant lessons from the earlier project to inform how this one is set up and approached
- **D**: Open the earlier project's risk register and copy every risk straight into the new project

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Capturing previous lessons in start-up applies "learn from experience": relevant lessons from the troubled earlier project should be sought and used to shape the team, approach and plans of the new project. (A) Ignoring prior experience wastes valuable insight and risks repeating known mistakes. (B) Lessons are deliberately reviewed during start-up so they can influence how the project is set up, not deferred. (D) Blindly copying every old risk ignores this project's context; lessons inform, they are not mechanically transplanted.
