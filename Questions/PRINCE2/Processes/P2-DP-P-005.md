# Question

Midway through a marketing-automation project, a competitor releases an identical free tool and the business case is no longer viable. The project manager believes the project should stop. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Halt all work and disband the team immediately on the project manager's own authority
- **B**: Raise the issue to the project board so it can decide, through ad hoc direction, whether to authorise premature closure
- **C**: Continue to the next stage boundary and let the board decide then, regardless of the wasted spend
- **D**: Quietly redirect the team to a different, unapproved objective that still seems worthwhile

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deciding to stop a project is a board responsibility; the project manager escalates the changed business justification so the board can give ad hoc direction and, if appropriate, instruct premature closure. (A) is outside the project manager's authority. (C) wastes resources by ignoring the loss of justification until a routine boundary. (D) abandons the agreed objectives and bypasses the board entirely, breaching business justification and the board's directing role.
