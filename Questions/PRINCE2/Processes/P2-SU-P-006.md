# Question

A logistics firm's project mandate names two co-sponsors who both wish to act as Executive for a fleet electrification project, each representing a different business division. As Project Manager assembling the team during start-up, what is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Appoint both as joint Executives so each division feels represented
- **B**: Ensure a single Executive is appointed, with the other senior stakeholder taking an appropriate project board role such as Senior User
- **C**: Appoint yourself as Executive to remain neutral between the divisions
- **D**: Leave the Executive role vacant and let the project board decide collectively as work proceeds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PRINCE2 requires a single Executive who is accountable for the project, supporting clear, single-point accountability; the other senior stakeholder should take a fitting project board role, often Senior User, to represent their division's interests. (A) Joint Executives split accountability and breach the principle of one accountable individual. (C) The Project Manager cannot also be the Executive; the roles and their accountabilities are distinct. (D) Leaving the Executive role unfilled removes the very accountability start-up is meant to establish.
