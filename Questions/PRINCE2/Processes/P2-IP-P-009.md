# Question

During initiation of a national charity's fundraising-platform project, the Executive informally tells the project manager to "just lock in" a fixed end date the board has not yet seen, before the project plan is finished. The project manager knows the plan may show this date is unachievable. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the fixed end date now to satisfy the Executive
- **B**: Complete the project plan, then present the realistic timescales in the PID for the board to consider when authorising the project
- **C**: Adjust the project plan's estimates downward so they match the Executive's date
- **D**: Escalate the disagreement to corporate management above the project board

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The project manager completes the project plan on a realistic basis and presents the resulting timescales within the PID, so the project board makes an informed authorisation decision rather than committing to an unverified date. (A) Locking in an unachievable date abandons business justification and pre-empts the plan. (C) Manipulating estimates to fit a desired date produces a dishonest plan. (D) Going over the board's head is premature; the proper route is to give the board accurate information through the PID, and the board owns the decision.
