# Question

Within the benefits review plan, who is typically made responsible for reviewing benefits that can only be measured after the project has closed?

## Options

- **A**: The project manager, who is retained on a part-time basis after closure
- **B**: Corporate, programme management or the customer, because the project organisation is disbanded at closure
- **C**: The team manager who delivered the related products
- **D**: Project assurance, acting on behalf of the supplier

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Because the project's temporary organisation is dissolved at closure, responsibility for post-project benefit measurement is handed to a standing authority — corporate, programme management or the customer — and recorded in the benefits review plan. (A) The project manager's role ends and is not retained for benefits work. (C) The team manager's responsibilities end with product delivery. (D) Project assurance advises but does not own ongoing benefits measurement.
