# Question

An airport's new baggage-tracking system is complete except for one reporting feature that the customer agreed to accept as an off-specification with a concession. At closure, the operations team is ready to receive the system. How should the project manager proceed?

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to recommend closure until the missing reporting feature is built, regardless of the agreed concession
- **B**: Confirm acceptance reflecting the agreed concession, record the outstanding feature as a follow-on action recommendation, and proceed with handover and the closure recommendation
- **C**: Recommend closure but conceal the missing feature so acceptance looks complete
- **D**: Treat the concession as an exception and escalate the whole project to corporate management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Where the project board has accepted an off-specification under a concession, acceptance can be confirmed on that basis, with the outstanding item carried forward as a follow-on action recommendation, allowing handover and the closure recommendation to proceed. (A) Insisting on building the feature ignores the board's valid concession and the receiving team's readiness — the tempting near-miss. (C) Concealing the gap falsifies acceptance. (D) An agreed concession is not an exception requiring escalation of the entire project to corporate management.
