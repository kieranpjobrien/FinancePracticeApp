# Question

Midway through a retail loyalty-app programme, a competitor launches a near-identical free service, sharply reducing the expected benefits. The current stage will finish on time next week. What is the BEST action for the project manager at the stage boundary?

## Options

- **A**: Continue to the next stage without comment, as the stage met its tolerances
- **B**: Update the business case to reflect the reduced benefits and present it to the project board for the continuation decision
- **C**: Cancel the project immediately on their own authority
- **D**: Remove the benefits from scope so the business case still looks positive

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Managing a Stage Boundary requires the project manager to update the business case so the board's go/no-go decision reflects current viability — here, the eroded benefits. (A) hides material information from the board; (C) usurps the board's authority to stop the project; (D) is a dishonest manipulation that undermines continued business justification.
