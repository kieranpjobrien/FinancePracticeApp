# Question

What is the purpose of the benefits management approach created during Initiating a Project?

## Options

- **A**: To list the products the project will deliver and their quality criteria
- **B**: To define how and when the project's expected benefits will be measured and reviewed
- **C**: To record the costs and timescales that justify the project's investment
- **D**: To describe how risks to the realisation of benefits will be escalated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The benefits management approach defines how and when a measurement of the project's benefits, expected in the business case, can be made — often extending beyond project closure into operations. (A) describes the product descriptions and project plan. (C) describes the business case itself. (D) is a narrower risk concern, not the purpose of the approach, which is about measuring and reviewing benefit realisation.
