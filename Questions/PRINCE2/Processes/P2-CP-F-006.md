# Question

Why must the benefits review plan be reviewed and, if necessary, updated during Closing a Project?

## Options

- **A**: Because most of a project's benefits are only realised after the project has closed and someone must be left responsible for measuring them
- **B**: Because the project board cannot authorise closure until all benefits have already been achieved
- **C**: Because the project manager will continue to measure the benefits personally after closure
- **D**: Because benefits realised during the project must be re-baselined into the business case

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Many benefits accrue after the products are in use, so the benefits review plan is updated at closure to cover post-project benefit measurement and to confirm who (usually within corporate, programme management or the customer) will carry it out once the project no longer exists. (B) Closure does not require all benefits to be realised first; that is often impossible. (C) The project manager's role ends at closure, so they cannot be the post-project measurer. (D) Re-baselining benefits into the business case is not the reason the plan is reviewed.
