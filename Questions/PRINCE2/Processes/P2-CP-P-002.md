# Question

A council's new waste-collection scheduling system has been delivered and accepted, but three minor enhancement requests and one low-priority risk remain open in the registers at closure. What is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Keep the project open until the three enhancements have been built
- **B**: Close the issues and risk as rejected so the registers are clean for closure
- **C**: Record the open items as follow-on action recommendations and pass them to the group that will support the system
- **D**: Escalate the open items to corporate management as a project exception

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Outstanding work, open issues and unaddressed risks at closure are captured as follow-on action recommendations and handed to whoever will operate and maintain the product, so they are not lost. (A) Holding the project open for minor enhancements wastes the temporary organisation when the product is already accepted. (B) Rejecting valid items just to tidy the registers loses genuine information. (D) These routine open items are not an exception requiring escalation to corporate management.
