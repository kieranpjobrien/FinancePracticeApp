# Question

What is the main purpose of the "authorise closure" activity in Directing a Project?

## Options

- **A**: To confirm the project has delivered what was expected and to formally bring it to a controlled end
- **B**: To approve the plan for the final management stage of the project
- **C**: To verify that each work package has met its quality criteria before acceptance
- **D**: To decide whether the project should be prematurely terminated during a stage

## Answer

A

## Explanation

"Authorise closure" is the board's confirmation, on receipt of the end project report and recommendation to close, that the project has achieved what was expected and can be formally and controllably shut down. (B) is part of "authorise a stage or exception plan". (C) is delivery-level quality checking, not a closure decision. (D) Premature termination is handled through ad hoc direction during a stage, separate from authorising the planned closure.
