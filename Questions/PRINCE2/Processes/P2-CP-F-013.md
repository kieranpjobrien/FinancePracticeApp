# Question

As part of closing the project, what does the project manager do with the project's records, registers and logs?

## Options

- **A**: Deletes them, because the project is finished and they are no longer needed
- **B**: Ensures they are secured, archived and the project's management products are brought up to date so the project can be evaluated and audited
- **C**: Hands them to the team manager to continue updating during operations
- **D**: Transfers ownership of them to project assurance for ongoing change control

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Closing a Project includes tidying up — updating and archiving the project's records, registers and logs so the project can be properly evaluated and later audited, and so lessons and follow-on items are preserved. (A) Records are retained, not deleted. (C) The team manager's role ends; they do not continue updating project records. (D) Project assurance does not take over change control after closure.
