# Question

A hotel chain's new property-management system is functionally complete, but the operations team that will run it day to day has not yet confirmed it is ready to take the system on. The project manager is preparing to recommend closure. What should the project manager do first?

## Options

- **A**: Obtain acceptance and confirm the operational and maintenance environment is ready to receive the product before handover
- **B**: Recommend closure immediately, as the system is technically finished
- **C**: Ask the supplier to sign off acceptance on the operations team's behalf
- **D**: Transfer the system into live use and sort out readiness afterwards

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Before products are handed over, Closing a Project requires that acceptance is obtained and that those who will operate and maintain the product confirm they are ready to receive it. (B) Recommending closure while handover readiness is unconfirmed risks an unsupported product. (C) The supplier cannot grant acceptance on behalf of the receiving operations team. (D) Going live before readiness is confirmed reverses the proper sequence and endangers service.
