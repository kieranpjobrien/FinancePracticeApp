# Question

During Initiating a Project, what happens to the business case that was outlined earlier?

## Options

- **A**: It is approved and closed, having served its purpose in Starting Up
- **B**: It is refined from the outline produced in Starting Up into the detailed business case
- **C**: It is created for the first time, since no business case exists before initiation
- **D**: It is replaced by the benefits management approach as the justification document

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The outline business case prepared in Starting Up a Project is refined during Initiating a Project into a detailed business case, drawing on the now-available project plan and cost information. (A) The business case is a living product maintained throughout the project, not closed. (C) An outline business case already exists from Starting Up, so it is refined, not created from scratch. (D) The benefits management approach complements, but does not replace, the business case.
