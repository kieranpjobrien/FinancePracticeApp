# Question

While designing the project management team during start-up of a museum exhibition project, the Project Manager identifies an ideal candidate for Senior Supplier, but that person's manager has not confirmed they can be released. What should the Project Manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Record the candidate as appointed and assume their availability will be arranged later
- **B**: Leave the Senior Supplier role unassigned and proceed to assemble the Project Brief without it
- **C**: Appoint a more junior, available person regardless of whether they have the required supplier authority
- **D**: Confirm the candidate's availability and obtain their acceptance of the role before treating the appointment as made

## Answer

D

## Explanation

Designing and appointing the team includes confirming that each proposed person is available and accepts the role, reflecting the central People element; an appointment is not real until availability and agreement are secured. (A) Assuming availability risks building the team around someone who may never be released. (B) The Senior Supplier represents those delivering the products and should be in place to give a complete project board for the brief. (C) Filling the role with someone lacking the necessary supplier authority undermines the role's purpose; suitability matters, not just availability.
