# Question

What does selecting the project approach during Starting Up a Project involve?

## Options

- **A**: Deciding the sequence and number of management stages for the project
- **B**: Deciding how the work of the project will be delivered, for example whether by in-house build, off-the-shelf purchase or a contracted supplier
- **C**: Choosing which PRINCE2 practices will be tailored out of the project
- **D**: Defining the detailed acceptance criteria and quality tolerances for each product

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The project approach defines how the project's work will be delivered — for instance built in-house, bought off the shelf, or contracted out — taking account of the operational environment the products must fit into. (A) Deciding the number and sequence of management stages is part of planning, not the project approach. (C) Tailoring decisions are recorded in the PID during initiation, not as the project approach. (D) Detailed acceptance criteria and quality tolerances belong to the Quality practice and product descriptions, developed beyond start-up.
