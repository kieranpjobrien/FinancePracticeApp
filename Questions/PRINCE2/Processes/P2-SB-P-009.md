# Question

An offshore data-centre project is at a stage boundary. New analysis shows the next stage's chosen cooling design will materially worsen the project's agreed sustainability performance target, although time and cost remain within tolerance. What should the project manager do?

## Options

- **A**: Proceed, since only time and cost tolerances govern whether the board needs to be involved
- **B**: Reflect the sustainability impact in the updated business case and next stage plan, and bring it to the project board for the authorisation decision
- **C**: Quietly switch to a greener cooling design in the next stage plan without informing the board
- **D**: Record it solely as a risk and take no further action at the boundary

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sustainability is one of PRINCE2 7's seven performance targets, so a forecast adverse impact must be surfaced in the updated business case and next stage plan for the board's go/no-go decision at the boundary. (A) wrongly ignores sustainability as a managed target; (C) is the tempting near-miss — changing the design may be sensible, but doing so unilaterally bypasses the board's authority; (D) under-responds by logging a material design issue as a passive risk.
