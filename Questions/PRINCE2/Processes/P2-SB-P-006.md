# Question

A pharmaceutical packaging project has finished its current stage. The next stage plan, updated project plan, business case and end stage report are all ready, but the project board's review meeting is not for another five days. A supplier offers a steep discount if the project commits to the next stage's materials today. What is the BEST action?

## Options

- **A**: Commit to the materials today to capture the discount, then inform the board afterwards
- **B**: Submit the stage boundary products and request that the project board hold an earlier review to authorise the next stage before committing
- **C**: Ask the team manager to start the next stage so the discount is not lost
- **D**: Treat the discount as a benefit and add it to the business case without board input

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stage may only begin once the project board authorises it; the correct response is to give the board the completed stage boundary products and request an expedited decision, respecting manage-by-stages. (A) and (C) start work without authorisation, breaching the principle; (D) is the tempting near-miss — but unilaterally amending the business case and committing spend still bypasses the board's go/no-go decision.
