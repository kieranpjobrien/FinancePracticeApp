# Question

What does the Project Manager do with the Project Brief and initiation Stage Plan at the end of Starting Up a Project?

## Options

- **A**: Requests the project board, through Directing a Project, to authorise initiation
- **B**: Begins the initiation stage immediately, since start-up authorises the next steps
- **C**: Submits them to corporate management to authorise the whole project
- **D**: Archives them and waits for the first delivery stage to be approved

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Start-up ends with a request to the project board, via the Directing a Project activity "authorise initiation", to decide whether to commit resources to initiate the project. (B) The Project Manager cannot self-authorise; that breaches the "manage by stages" principle and the board's authority. (C) Corporate or programme management commission the project but it is the project board that authorises initiation. (D) The documents drive the next decision rather than being shelved until a delivery stage.
