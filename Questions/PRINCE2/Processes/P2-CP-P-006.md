# Question

A pharmaceutical firm halts a clinical-trials data project early after a regulatory change makes the planned product unviable. Two of the five deliverables are complete, accepted and genuinely useful to another department. The project manager is closing the project prematurely. Which action is the BEST response?

## Options

- **A**: Discard the benefits review plan, since the project failed to deliver its full scope
- **B**: Update the benefits review plan to cover only the benefits still achievable from the two accepted deliverables, and assign their post-project review accordingly
- **C**: Report in the end project report that no benefits were realised because the project was stopped early
- **D**: Leave the benefits review plan unchanged so the original targets remain on record

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Even in a premature closure, any benefits still attainable from accepted products should be captured, so the benefits review plan is revised to reflect what is now realistically achievable and to assign post-project measurement. (A) Discarding the plan abandons real benefits the firm can still gain. (C) Claiming zero benefits is inaccurate when two deliverables are accepted and useful — a tempting but wrong simplification. (D) Leaving the plan unchanged ignores that most of the original scope will never be delivered.
