# Question

Predatory pricing involves a firm:

## Options

- **A**: Charging high prices due to monopoly power
- **B**: Temporarily pricing below cost to drive out competitors, then raising prices
- **C**: Matching competitor prices exactly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Predatory pricing is a strategy where a firm prices below cost (often below average variable cost) to force competitors out of the market. Once competition is eliminated, the predator raises prices to recoup losses. This practice is illegal under antitrust law, though difficult to prove—courts must distinguish predation from legitimate competition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes monopoly pricing, not predatory pricing
- Option C describes competitive pricing, not predation
