# Question

Dumping in international trade refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Exporting goods at a price below their normal value or cost of production
- **B**: Imposing high tariffs on all imports
- **C**: A country abandoning its currency peg

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Dumping occurs when a company exports products at prices below what they charge domestically or below production costs. This may be predatory (to drive out competitors), cyclical (to clear excess inventory), or structural (cross-subsidized by domestic profits). Countries may impose anti-dumping duties to protect domestic industries from unfairly priced imports, subject to WTO rules.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes protectionist tariff policy, not dumping
- Option C describes exchange rate policy, not trade practices
