# Question

The convergence hypothesis suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Developed economies will always grow faster than developing economies
- **B**: Poorer economies tend to grow faster than richer economies
- **C**: All economies will eventually have identical growth rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The convergence (or catch-up) hypothesis predicts that poorer economies can grow faster than richer ones by adopting existing technologies and practices. With less capital per worker, the marginal return to investment is higher. However, convergence depends on appropriate policies, institutions, and human capital—conditional convergence rather than automatic catch-up.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the convergence hypothesis
- Option C overstates—convergence refers to income levels, not identical growth rates
