# Question

Subsidies can improve economic efficiency when they:

## Options

- **A**: Protect domestic industries from foreign competition
- **B**: Correct for positive externalities that markets underprovide
- **C**: Maintain artificially low prices for all consumer goods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Subsidies are efficient when they address market failures. Positive externalities (education, R&D, vaccines) are underprovided by markets because producers don't capture all benefits. Subsidies align private incentives with social benefits, increasing production to the socially optimal level. Subsidies without market failure justification create inefficiency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes protectionism, which typically reduces efficiency
- Option C would create widespread market distortions and shortages
