# Question

The primary budget balance differs from the overall balance in that the primary balance:

## Options

- **A**: Includes all government revenues and expenditures
- **B**: Excludes interest payments on existing debt
- **C**: Only counts capital expenditures

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The primary balance equals revenues minus expenditures excluding interest payments on outstanding debt. It measures the government's current fiscal stance without the legacy of past borrowing decisions. A country with high debt but a primary surplus is generating enough revenue to cover non-interest spending and could reduce debt over time. The primary balance better reflects current fiscal policy choices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the overall budget balance
- Option C conflates capital budgeting with debt service concepts
