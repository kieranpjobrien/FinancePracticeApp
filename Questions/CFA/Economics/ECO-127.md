# Question

Consumer confidence indices are considered leading indicators because:

## Options

- **A**: They report on past economic conditions
- **B**: Higher confidence typically precedes increased consumer spending
- **C**: They measure current unemployment levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Consumer confidence measures how optimistic households are about their financial prospects and the economy. When confidence is high, consumers are more likely to make major purchases and less likely to increase precautionary savings. Changes in confidence often precede changes in actual spending, making it useful for forecasting economic activity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes lagging indicators
- Option C describes current conditions, and unemployment is itself a lagging indicator
