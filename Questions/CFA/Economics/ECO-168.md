# Question

Intra-industry trade refers to countries:

## Options

- **A**: Only exporting goods they don't consume domestically
- **B**: Simultaneously importing and exporting goods within the same industry
- **C**: Trading only with countries at similar development levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Intra-industry trade describes simultaneous imports and exports of similar products—for example, Germany exporting BMWs while importing Japanese cars. This pattern, common between developed countries, is explained by product differentiation, economies of scale, and consumer preference for variety. Traditional comparative advantage theory better explains inter-industry trade (different products between countries).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes specialized production without variety
- Option C describes a pattern but not the definition of intra-industry trade
