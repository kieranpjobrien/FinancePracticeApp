# Question

Economies of scale occur when:

## Options

- **A**: Average costs increase as output increases
- **B**: Average costs decrease as output increases
- **C**: Total costs remain constant regardless of output

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Economies of scale exist when a firm's average cost per unit decreases as production volume increases. Sources include spreading fixed costs over more units, bulk purchasing discounts, specialized equipment, and learning effects. Economies of scale can create barriers to entry and are common in industries with high fixed costs like manufacturing, utilities, and technology.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes diseconomies of scale
- Option C ignores the relationship between output and costs
