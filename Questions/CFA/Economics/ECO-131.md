# Question

The Laffer curve suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Higher tax rates always generate more tax revenue
- **B**: At some point, higher tax rates may reduce tax revenue
- **C**: Tax rates have no effect on economic behavior

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Laffer curve illustrates that tax revenue is zero at both 0% and 100% tax rates. Between these extremes, some rate maximizes revenue. If rates are already above this optimal level, further increases may reduce revenue as people work less, avoid taxes, or move activity underground. The curve's exact shape and optimal rate are debated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores behavioral responses to taxation
- Option C contradicts abundant evidence that taxes affect incentives
