# Question

The neutral interest rate (r*) is the rate at which:

## Options

- **A**: Inflation is always zero
- **B**: Monetary policy is neither stimulative nor restrictive
- **C**: All borrowing stops

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The neutral rate is the real interest rate consistent with stable inflation and full employment—where monetary policy neither stimulates nor restrains the economy. When policy rates are below neutral, policy is accommodative; above neutral, it's restrictive. The neutral rate isn't directly observable and is estimated based on economic models. It varies over time with productivity, demographics, and global factors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses the neutral rate with zero inflation conditions
- Option C describes an unrealistic extreme
