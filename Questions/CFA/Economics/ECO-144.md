# Question

The balance of payments must always:

## Options

- **A**: Show a surplus in the current account
- **B**: Balance to zero when all accounts are included
- **C**: Show a surplus in the financial account

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The balance of payments is an accounting identity: current account + capital account + financial account + statistical discrepancy = 0. A current account deficit must be offset by net capital/financial inflows. The accounts balance by definition because every international transaction has two sides. Individual accounts can show surpluses or deficits, but the total sums to zero.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that many countries run persistent current account deficits
- Option C ignores that surpluses and deficits depend on economic conditions
