# Question

Deadweight loss from monopoly arises because:

## Options

- **A**: The monopolist produces the socially optimal quantity
- **B**: The monopolist restricts output and charges higher prices than under competition
- **C**: Consumers gain surplus at the expense of producers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A monopolist maximizes profit by producing where marginal revenue equals marginal cost, which is less than the competitive output (where price equals marginal cost). This output restriction and higher price creates deadweight loss—a reduction in total surplus because some mutually beneficial transactions don't occur. The lost consumer and producer surplus represents economic inefficiency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—monopolists produce below the social optimum
- Option C reverses the transfer—monopolists gain at consumers' expense
