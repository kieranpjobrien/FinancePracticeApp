# Question

A country has a trade deficit when:

## Options

- **A**: The value of exports exceeds the value of imports
- **B**: The value of imports exceeds the value of exports
- **C**: Exports and imports are equal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A trade deficit (or trade gap) occurs when a country imports more goods and services than it exports. The trade balance equals exports minus imports, so a deficit represents a negative balance. While often viewed negatively, trade deficits can reflect strong domestic demand and investment financed by foreign capital. Persistent deficits may be sustainable depending on the country's circumstances.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a trade surplus
- Option C describes balanced trade
