# Question

Fiscal austerity measures typically include:

## Options

- **A**: Increasing government spending to stimulate growth
- **B**: Spending cuts and/or tax increases to reduce budget deficits
- **C**: Central bank purchases of government bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Austerity refers to fiscal policies aimed at reducing government budget deficits, typically through spending cuts, tax increases, or both. While austerity can improve fiscal sustainability, it may reduce aggregate demand and slow growth in the short term, particularly during recessions. The effectiveness and timing of austerity are heavily debated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes fiscal stimulus, the opposite of austerity
- Option C describes quantitative easing, a monetary policy
