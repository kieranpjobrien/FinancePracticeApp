# Question

A "sudden stop" in economics refers to:

## Options

- **A**: A gradual decline in export growth
- **B**: An abrupt reversal or cessation of capital inflows to an economy
- **C**: A central bank halting interest rate changes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A sudden stop describes an abrupt halt or reversal of capital inflows to an economy that had been receiving significant foreign investment. This forces a sharp current account adjustment (imports must fall), often triggers currency depreciation and financial crisis. Sudden stops have caused severe recessions in emerging markets (Mexico 1994, Asia 1997, Argentina 2001).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes gradual trade changes, not capital flow reversals
- Option C describes a monetary policy pause, not a capital flow phenomenon
