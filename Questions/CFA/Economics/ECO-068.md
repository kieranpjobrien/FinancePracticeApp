# Question

A liquidity trap occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Banks have excess reserves but economic growth is too strong
- **B**: Interest rates are near zero and monetary policy becomes ineffective at stimulating demand
- **C**: The central bank raises rates too quickly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A liquidity trap exists when interest rates approach zero and conventional monetary policy loses effectiveness. People and banks prefer holding cash rather than lending or investing because expected returns are too low. In this situation, increasing money supply doesn't stimulate spending—"pushing on a string." Central banks may resort to unconventional policies like quantitative easing or negative rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite problem—an overheating economy
- Option C describes policy error, not a liquidity trap
