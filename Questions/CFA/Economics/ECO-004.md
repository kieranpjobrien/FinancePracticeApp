# Question

A natural monopoly is most likely to occur when:

## Options

- **A**: The government grants exclusive production rights to one firm
- **B**: Economies of scale are so large that one firm can serve the market at lower cost than multiple firms
- **C**: A firm owns all sources of a key natural resource

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A natural monopoly arises when economies of scale are so significant that having multiple firms would be inefficient. The industry's long-run average cost curve is still declining at the quantity demanded by the entire market. Examples include utilities (electricity, water, natural gas) where fixed costs are high and marginal costs are low. Regulation often addresses the pricing power that results.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a government-granted monopoly (legal monopoly)
- Option C describes resource monopoly, which is different from natural monopoly
