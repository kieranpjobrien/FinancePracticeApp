# Question

Cap-and-trade systems reduce pollution by:

## Options

- **A**: Banning all emissions above a set threshold for each firm
- **B**: Setting a total emissions cap and allowing firms to trade permits
- **C**: Imposing a fixed tax per unit of emissions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cap-and-trade sets an overall emissions cap, allocates or auctions permits, and allows trading. Firms that can reduce emissions cheaply sell permits to those facing higher abatement costs. This achieves the emissions target at minimum total cost, as reductions occur where they're cheapest. The EU Emissions Trading System is a major example.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes command-and-control, not market-based regulation
- Option C describes a carbon tax, a different market-based approach
