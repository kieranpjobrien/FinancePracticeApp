# Question

A government's debt-to-GDP ratio will tend to decline if:

## Options

- **A**: The interest rate on debt exceeds the GDP growth rate
- **B**: Nominal GDP growth exceeds the average interest rate on debt, assuming a primary balance
- **C**: The government consistently runs large primary deficits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt sustainability depends on the relationship between GDP growth (g), interest rates (r), and the primary balance. If g > r, a country can stabilize or reduce its debt ratio even with a modest primary deficit. If r > g, primary surpluses are needed to prevent debt from spiraling upward. This framework is crucial for assessing sovereign credit risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conditions that increase the debt ratio
- Option C describes conditions that add to debt regardless of growth
