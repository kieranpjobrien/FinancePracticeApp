# Question

A reserve currency is best described as a currency:

## Options

- **A**: That is only used domestically
- **B**: Held in significant quantities by central banks as part of foreign exchange reserves
- **C**: That has been demonetized and removed from circulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reserve currencies are held by central banks worldwide as part of their foreign exchange reserves, used for international transactions, and serve as a store of value. The US dollar is the dominant reserve currency (~60% of global reserves), followed by the euro, yen, and pound. Reserve currency status provides benefits (seigniorage, low borrowing costs) but also responsibilities (maintaining stability).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the international nature of reserve currencies
- Option C describes the opposite of a functioning currency
