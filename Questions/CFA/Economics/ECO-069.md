# Question

Forward guidance as a monetary policy tool works by:

## Options

- **A**: Immediately changing the current policy rate
- **B**: Influencing expectations about future policy to affect current financial conditions
- **C**: Directly setting long-term interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward guidance involves central bank communication about the likely future path of policy. By signaling that rates will remain low (or high) for an extended period, central banks influence market expectations, which affects current long-term rates, asset prices, and economic decisions. This is particularly important at the zero lower bound, where current rate cuts are impossible but expectations can still be shifted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes current policy changes, not forward guidance
- Option C overstates central bank control over long-term rates
