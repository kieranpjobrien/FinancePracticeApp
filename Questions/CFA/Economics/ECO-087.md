# Question

In long-run equilibrium under perfect competition, firms earn:

## Options

- **A**: Positive economic profits
- **B**: Zero economic profit (normal profit only)
- **C**: Negative economic profits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under perfect competition with free entry and exit, economic profits attract new entrants that increase supply and drive prices down until profits disappear. Losses cause firms to exit, reducing supply and raising prices. In long-run equilibrium, price equals minimum average total cost, and firms earn only normal profit (the opportunity cost of capital). This is the theoretical benchmark for competitive markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the impact of new entry on profits
- Option C would cause firms to exit the market
