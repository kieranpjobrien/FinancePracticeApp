# Question

A perfectly competitive firm faces a demand curve that is:

## Options

- **A**: Downward sloping
- **B**: Perfectly horizontal at the market price
- **C**: Upward sloping

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In perfect competition, each firm is so small relative to the market that it cannot influence the market price—it is a "price taker." The firm can sell any quantity at the market price but nothing above it, so its demand curve is perfectly horizontal (elastic). The market demand curve is downward sloping, but individual firms face horizontal demand.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the market demand curve or a monopolist's demand
- Option C describes supply, not demand
