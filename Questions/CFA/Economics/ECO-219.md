# Question

Public goods are characterized by being:

## Options

- **A**: Excludable and rival in consumption
- **B**: Non-excludable and non-rival in consumption
- **C**: Produced only by government agencies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Public goods are non-excludable (cannot prevent non-payers from benefiting) and non-rival (one person's use doesn't diminish availability to others). Examples include national defense, clean air, and street lighting. Markets underprovide public goods because of free-riding—people benefit without paying. This justifies government provision or subsidy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes private goods
- Option C is incorrect—public goods can be privately produced; the definition is about consumption characteristics
