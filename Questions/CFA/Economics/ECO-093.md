# Question

The Phillips curve describes the historical inverse relationship between:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates and bond prices
- **B**: Inflation and unemployment
- **C**: Exchange rates and trade balances

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Phillips curve shows that historically, lower unemployment has been associated with higher inflation and vice versa. When the economy is strong and unemployment is low, workers have more bargaining power, pushing up wages and prices. This trade-off is important for monetary policy, though the relationship has been less stable in recent decades, particularly during stagflation periods.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a different financial relationship
- Option C describes yet another economic relationship unrelated to the Phillips curve
