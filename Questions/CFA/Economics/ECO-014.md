# Question

Ricardian equivalence suggests that government deficit spending financed by borrowing:

## Options

- **A**: Always stimulates consumption because households feel wealthier
- **B**: May not stimulate demand if households save more anticipating future taxes
- **C**: Is more effective than spending financed by current taxes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ricardian equivalence theory proposes that rational, forward-looking households recognize that current deficits mean higher future taxes to repay government debt. In response, they increase current saving to pay those future taxes, offsetting the stimulative effect of deficit spending. While strict Ricardian equivalence rarely holds perfectly in practice, partial effects may reduce fiscal multipliers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores forward-looking household behavior
- Option C contradicts the theory's prediction that financing method doesn't matter
