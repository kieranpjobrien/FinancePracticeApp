# Question

A natural monopoly exists when:

## Options

- **A**: The government grants exclusive operating rights to one firm
- **B**: One firm can serve the entire market at lower cost than multiple firms
- **C**: A firm owns all sources of a critical raw material

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A natural monopoly occurs when economies of scale are so large relative to market demand that one firm can produce at lower average cost than multiple competing firms. Common examples include utilities (electricity transmission, water systems) with high fixed costs and low marginal costs. Regulators often permit these monopolies but control pricing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a government-granted (legal) monopoly
- Option C describes a resource-based monopoly, not a natural monopoly
