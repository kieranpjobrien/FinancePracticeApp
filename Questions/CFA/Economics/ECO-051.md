# Question

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) is used to measure:

## Options

- **A**: The inflation rate in an economy
- **B**: Market concentration based on the sum of squared market shares
- **C**: The unemployment rate by industry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The HHI measures market concentration by summing the squares of each firm's market share percentage. For example, a market with four firms holding 40%, 30%, 20%, and 10% has HHI = 40² + 30² + 20² + 10² = 3,000. Higher HHI indicates greater concentration: below 1,500 is considered unconcentrated, 1,500-2,500 moderately concentrated, and above 2,500 highly concentrated. Regulators use HHI to evaluate merger impacts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses market analysis with macroeconomic indicators
- Option C is unrelated to market structure measurement
