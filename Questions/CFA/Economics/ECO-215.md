# Question

Cost-benefit analysis of regulations should consider:

## Options

- **A**: Only the direct compliance costs to regulated firms
- **B**: Both the costs of compliance and the benefits of reduced harm
- **C**: Only the benefits to protected parties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proper regulatory cost-benefit analysis weighs both sides: compliance costs (direct costs, administrative burden, reduced innovation) against benefits (reduced pollution, improved safety, consumer protection). Regulations are economically justified when benefits exceed costs. Ignoring either side leads to under-regulation or over-regulation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the benefits regulations provide
- Option C ignores the costs regulations impose
