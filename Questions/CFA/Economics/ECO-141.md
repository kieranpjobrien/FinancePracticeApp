# Question

The Stolper-Samuelson theorem predicts that international trade will:

## Options

- **A**: Benefit all factors of production equally
- **B**: Benefit the abundant factor and harm the scarce factor in each country
- **C**: Have no effect on domestic factor prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Building on Heckscher-Ohlin, Stolper-Samuelson shows that trade increases returns to the abundant factor (e.g., labor in labor-abundant countries) while reducing returns to the scarce factor. This helps explain political opposition to trade from groups who lose (e.g., unskilled workers in capital-abundant countries) even as overall national welfare increases.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores distributional effects of trade
- Option C contradicts the theorem's central prediction
