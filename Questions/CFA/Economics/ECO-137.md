# Question

Countries heavily dependent on commodity exports face increased risk because:

## Options

- **A**: Commodity prices are typically very stable
- **B**: Commodity prices are volatile, creating boom-bust economic cycles
- **C**: Commodities always trade at premium prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity-dependent economies face "Dutch disease" risks: volatile commodity prices create boom-bust cycles, resource wealth can crowd out other sectors, and price collapses strain government finances. Oil exporters, for example, suffer when oil prices fall sharply. Diversification away from commodity dependence is often a policy priority for resource-rich developing economies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the well-documented volatility of commodity markets
- Option C ignores the cyclical nature of commodity prices
