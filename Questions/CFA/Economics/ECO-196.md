# Question

The long-run aggregate supply curve is vertical because:

## Options

- **A**: Prices are sticky in the long run
- **B**: Output is determined by real factors, not the price level
- **C**: Monetary policy is ineffective in all time periods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In the long run, aggregate supply is determined by real factors: labor, capital, technology, and natural resources—not the price level. The economy produces at potential GDP regardless of whether prices are high or low. While the short-run AS curve slopes upward (due to sticky wages/prices), the long-run AS curve is vertical at potential output.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is backwards—prices are sticky in the short run, flexible in the long run
- Option C overstates—monetary policy affects output in the short run
