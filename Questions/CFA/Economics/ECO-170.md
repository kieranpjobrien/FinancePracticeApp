# Question

Remittances are:

## Options

- **A**: Payments made by governments to other governments
- **B**: Money transfers sent by migrant workers to their home countries
- **C**: Interest payments on international bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Remittances are funds that migrants send to family members in their home countries. They represent a significant source of income for many developing economies—often exceeding foreign aid. Remittances tend to be counter-cyclical (rising when home country conditions worsen) and go directly to households, supporting consumption and reducing poverty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes foreign aid or inter-government transfers
- Option C describes debt service payments
