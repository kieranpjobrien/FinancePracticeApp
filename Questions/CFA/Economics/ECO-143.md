# Question

In international finance, "original sin" refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Countries that default on their first bond issuance
- **B**: The inability of emerging markets to borrow internationally in their own currency
- **C**: Excessive lending by developed country banks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Original sin describes emerging markets' historical inability to borrow abroad in their own currency, forcing them to borrow in dollars or euros. This creates a currency mismatch—revenues are in domestic currency while debts are in foreign currency. Currency depreciation then increases the debt burden in local terms, potentially triggering debt crises.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misinterprets the term
- Option C describes a different phenomenon (perhaps excessive capital flows)
