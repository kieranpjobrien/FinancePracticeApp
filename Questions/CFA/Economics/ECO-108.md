# Question

Sovereign default occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A corporation files for bankruptcy
- **B**: A government fails to meet scheduled debt payments
- **C**: A central bank runs out of foreign exchange reserves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sovereign default happens when a government fails to pay principal or interest on its debt obligations. This can involve missing payments, restructuring debt at unfavorable terms to creditors, or repudiating debt entirely. Sovereign defaults are significant events that typically lead to loss of market access, currency depreciation, and economic disruption. Examples include Argentina and Greece.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes corporate default, not sovereign
- Option C describes reserve depletion, which may precede but isn't the same as default
