# Question

The Phillips curve suggests an inverse relationship between:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates and investment
- **B**: Inflation and unemployment
- **C**: Government spending and tax revenue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Phillips curve posits a tradeoff between inflation and unemployment—lower unemployment is associated with higher inflation and vice versa. When unemployment is low, workers have bargaining power to demand wage increases, which pushes up prices. The short-run Phillips curve is downward sloping; however, the long-run relationship is debated, with many economists believing no permanent tradeoff exists.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a different economic relationship
- Option C relates to fiscal policy, not the Phillips curve
