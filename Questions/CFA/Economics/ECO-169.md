# Question

The gravity model of trade predicts that bilateral trade flows:

## Options

- **A**: Are unrelated to the size of trading economies
- **B**: Increase with economic size and decrease with distance
- **C**: Depend only on relative factor endowments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The gravity model, borrowing from physics, predicts trade flows are proportional to the product of countries' economic sizes (GDP) and inversely proportional to distance (a proxy for trade costs). Like gravitational attraction, larger economies trade more, and closer countries trade more. The model has strong empirical support and is widely used to estimate trade potential and effects of trade agreements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the strong size relationship in gravity models
- Option C describes Heckscher-Ohlin theory, not the gravity model
