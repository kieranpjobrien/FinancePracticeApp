# Question

The spot exchange rate represents:

## Options

- **A**: The rate for currency exchange at a future date
- **B**: The rate for immediate or near-term currency exchange
- **C**: An average rate over the past year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The spot rate is the current market price for immediate exchange of currencies, typically settled within two business days (T+2). It reflects current supply and demand for currencies. The forward rate, in contrast, is agreed today for exchange at a specified future date. Spot rates are continuously quoted in forex markets during trading hours.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the forward rate
- Option C describes a historical average, not a current market rate
