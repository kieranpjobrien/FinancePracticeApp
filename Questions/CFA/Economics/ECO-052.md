# Question

High barriers to entry in an industry are most likely to result in:

## Options

- **A**: Lower prices for consumers in the long run
- **B**: Higher economic profits for incumbent firms
- **C**: More firms entering the market

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High barriers to entry (such as large capital requirements, patents, regulatory licenses, or network effects) prevent or discourage new competitors from entering. This protects incumbent firms from competition, allowing them to maintain economic profits in the long run. In contrast, industries with low barriers see profits competed away as new entrants are attracted by profitable opportunities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A occurs with competition, not barriers to entry
- Option C is the opposite of what barriers cause
