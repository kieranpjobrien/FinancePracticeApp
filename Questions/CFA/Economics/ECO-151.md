# Question

The GDP deflator measures:

## Options

- **A**: Only the price of consumer goods
- **B**: The overall price level of all goods and services produced domestically
- **C**: The exchange rate between currencies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The GDP deflator is a broad price index covering all domestically produced goods and services (consumption, investment, government, and net exports). It equals (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100. Unlike the CPI, which uses a fixed basket, the GDP deflator reflects current production patterns and excludes imports. It's useful for converting nominal to real GDP.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the Consumer Price Index scope
- Option C describes forex markets, not price measurement
