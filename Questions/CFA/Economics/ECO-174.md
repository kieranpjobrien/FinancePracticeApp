# Question

A US exporter expecting to receive €1 million in 90 days can hedge currency risk by:

## Options

- **A**: Buying euros forward
- **B**: Selling euros forward
- **C**: Only by not doing business internationally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

To hedge the currency risk of receiving euros in the future, the US exporter should sell euros forward—locking in today's forward rate to convert future euro receipts to dollars. This eliminates uncertainty about the USD value of the payment. If the exporter bought euros forward, they would be doubling rather than hedging their euro exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would increase euro exposure, not hedge it
- Option C is impractical and ignores available hedging tools
