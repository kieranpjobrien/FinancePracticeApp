# Question

A country's terms of trade improve when:

## Options

- **A**: Export prices rise relative to import prices
- **B**: Import prices rise relative to export prices
- **C**: Both export and import volumes increase equally

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Terms of trade measure the ratio of export prices to import prices. When this ratio rises (exports become relatively more expensive), the country can obtain more imports for each unit of exports—an improvement. Commodity exporters see terms of trade improve when commodity prices rise; they deteriorate when commodity prices fall relative to manufactured goods prices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes deteriorating terms of trade
- Option C describes trade volume changes, not price ratio changes
