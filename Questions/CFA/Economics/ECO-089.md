# Question

A cartel is an agreement among competitors to:

## Options

- **A**: Compete more aggressively on price
- **B**: Coordinate on pricing, output, or market division to increase profits
- **C**: Share technology openly without restriction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A cartel is a formal or informal agreement among competing firms to act collectively like a monopoly—setting prices, limiting output, or dividing markets to earn economic profits. OPEC is a famous cartel of oil-producing countries. Cartels are generally illegal in most jurisdictions as they harm consumers through higher prices and reduced output.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of cartel behavior
- Option C describes open-source collaboration, not cartel activity
