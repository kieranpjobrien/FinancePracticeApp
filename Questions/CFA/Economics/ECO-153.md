# Question

The Federal Reserve prefers the Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index over CPI because PCE:

## Options

- **A**: Only measures food and energy prices
- **B**: Uses a chain-weighted formula that better accounts for substitution
- **C**: Is more volatile and easier to forecast

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PCE deflator uses a chain-weighted (Fisher) formula that updates weights more frequently, better capturing substitution effects as consumers adjust to price changes. PCE also has broader coverage (including items paid by employers like health insurance) and different weighting. The Fed targets 2% PCE inflation, which typically runs slightly lower than CPI inflation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a very narrow index
- Option C is backwards—the Fed prefers less volatile measures
