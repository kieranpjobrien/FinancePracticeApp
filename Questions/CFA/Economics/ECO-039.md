# Question

If the USD/EUR exchange rate moves from 1.10 to 1.20, the:

## Options

- **A**: US dollar has appreciated against the euro
- **B**: Euro has appreciated against the US dollar
- **C**: Exchange rate has not changed in real terms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The USD/EUR rate represents how many US dollars are needed to buy one euro. When this rate rises from 1.10 to 1.20, it takes more dollars to buy a euro, meaning the euro has strengthened (appreciated) and the dollar has weakened (depreciated). Understanding quote conventions is essential for interpreting exchange rate movements correctly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the interpretation—the dollar has depreciated
- Option C ignores the clear nominal change
