# Question

A currency board differs from a conventional fixed exchange rate in that a currency board:

## Options

- **A**: Allows unlimited monetary policy discretion
- **B**: Requires domestic currency to be fully backed by foreign reserves
- **C**: Sets the exchange rate daily based on market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A currency board is a strict form of fixed exchange rate where domestic currency issuance is legally backed 100% by foreign reserves. The central bank has no discretion to create money beyond what reserves support, essentially importing the monetary policy of the anchor currency country. This provides credibility but removes monetary policy flexibility. Hong Kong's dollar peg operates like a currency board.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite—currency boards eliminate discretion
- Option C describes a managed float, not a currency board
