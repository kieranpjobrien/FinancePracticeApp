# Question

The inventory cycle (Kitchin cycle) typically lasts:

## Options

- **A**: 2-4 years
- **B**: 7-11 years
- **C**: 15-25 years

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Kitchin cycle is a short-term business cycle lasting 2-4 years, driven by inventory adjustments. When businesses accumulate excess inventory, they cut production until inventories normalize, then increase production again. The Juglar cycle (7-11 years) relates to fixed investment, and the Kuznets cycle (15-25 years) relates to infrastructure investment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes the Juglar investment cycle
- Option C describes longer structural cycles
