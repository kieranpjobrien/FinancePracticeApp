# Question

Monetary policy effectiveness is limited by lags, which include:

## Options

- **A**: Recognition lag, decision lag, and impact lag
- **B**: Only the time between policy announcement and implementation
- **C**: Lags that are shorter for monetary policy than fiscal policy

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Monetary policy faces three types of lags: recognition lag (time to identify economic conditions requiring action), decision lag (time to formulate and announce policy response), and impact lag (time for policy changes to affect the economy). For monetary policy, decision lags are typically shorter than fiscal policy (no legislative process), but impact lags can be 12-18 months or longer.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B captures only part of the lag structure
- Option C is partially true for decision lags but impact lags can be substantial
