# Question

If nominal GDP is $22 trillion and the GDP deflator is 110, real GDP is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $20.0 trillion
- **B**: $22.0 trillion
- **C**: $24.2 trillion

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Real GDP = Nominal GDP / (GDP Deflator / 100) = $22 trillion / 1.10 = $20.0 trillion. The GDP deflator of 110 means prices are 10% higher than in the base year. Real GDP removes the effect of price changes to show actual output. Real GDP is always expressed in base-year prices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B confuses nominal with real GDP
- Option C multiplies instead of divides by the deflator
