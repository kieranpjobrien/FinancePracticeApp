# Question

Government regulation of pollution is most justified by:

## Options

- **A**: The desire to increase tax revenue
- **B**: Negative externalities not reflected in market prices
- **C**: The need to support domestic industries

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pollution creates negative externalities—costs imposed on third parties not involved in the transaction. Without regulation, polluters don't bear the full social cost of their activities, leading to excessive pollution. Regulation (emissions limits, carbon taxes, cap-and-trade) aims to internalize these external costs, aligning private incentives with social welfare.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses regulatory purpose with fiscal outcomes
- Option C describes protectionism, not environmental regulation
