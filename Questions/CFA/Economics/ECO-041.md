# Question

Capital controls are government policies that:

## Options

- **A**: Encourage unrestricted international capital flows
- **B**: Restrict the flow of money into or out of a country
- **C**: Fix exchange rates to a commodity standard

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital controls are measures that restrict the movement of capital across borders. These can include limits on foreign investment, restrictions on repatriation of profits, taxes on capital flows, or requirements for central bank approval. Countries use them to prevent capital flight, maintain monetary policy independence, or protect against speculative attacks. Their effectiveness and economic impact are debated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes capital account liberalization, the opposite of controls
- Option C describes a monetary policy choice, not capital flow restrictions
