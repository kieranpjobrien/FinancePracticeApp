# Question

Automatic stabilizers help moderate business cycles by:

## Options

- **A**: Requiring legislative action to change tax rates
- **B**: Automatically increasing government spending during expansions
- **C**: Increasing transfer payments during recessions without new legislation

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Automatic stabilizers are fiscal mechanisms that cushion economic fluctuations without requiring new legislation. During recessions, unemployment benefits rise automatically, and tax revenues fall as incomes drop—both supporting spending. During expansions, the opposite occurs. Examples include progressive income taxes, unemployment insurance, and means-tested welfare programs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes discretionary fiscal policy, not automatic stabilizers
- Option B is backwards—automatic stabilizers reduce government spending during expansions
