# Question

A country with a persistent current account deficit must finance it through:

## Options

- **A**: Printing more domestic currency
- **B**: Net capital inflows or drawing down reserves
- **C**: Increasing exports immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The balance of payments identity requires that current account deficits be financed by capital/financial account surpluses (net capital inflows) or by depleting foreign exchange reserves. A country importing more than it exports must borrow from abroad, attract foreign investment, or spend its reserves. This financing constraint is why unsustainable current account deficits can trigger crises.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't provide foreign currency to pay for imports
- Option C would reduce the deficit, not finance it
