# Question

If the price elasticity of demand for a good is -0.5, a 10% increase in price will result in a change in quantity demanded of:

## Options

- **A**: -5%
- **B**: -20%
- **C**: +5%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Price elasticity of demand = % change in quantity demanded / % change in price

Rearranging: % change in quantity demanded = Elasticity × % change in price
% change in quantity demanded = -0.5 × 10% = -5%

The negative sign indicates that quantity demanded decreases when price increases, which is consistent with the law of demand.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B incorrectly divides instead of multiplies (10% / 0.5 = 20%)
- Option C incorrectly uses a positive sign, which would violate the law of demand
