# Question

The World Trade Organization (WTO) primarily functions to:

## Options

- **A**: Set tariff rates for all member countries
- **B**: Provide a framework for trade negotiations and dispute resolution
- **C**: Control global monetary policy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The WTO provides a rules-based multilateral trading system, facilitates trade negotiations to reduce barriers, and offers a dispute settlement mechanism when countries believe trading partners violate agreements. While it promotes trade liberalization, countries retain sovereignty over their tariff policies within negotiated limits. The WTO doesn't control monetary policy—that's the domain of central banks and institutions like the IMF.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates WTO authority—countries set their own tariffs within agreed bounds
- Option C confuses trade policy with monetary policy institutions
