# Question

The primary objectives of most central banks typically include:

## Options

- **A**: Maximizing government tax revenue
- **B**: Price stability and sustainable economic growth
- **C**: Minimizing the national debt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Central banks typically have mandates focused on price stability (controlling inflation) and promoting sustainable economic growth or maximum employment. The US Federal Reserve has a "dual mandate" of price stability and maximum employment. Many other central banks prioritize price stability as their primary objective, with secondary goals around financial stability and economic growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses central bank objectives with fiscal policy goals
- Option C relates to fiscal policy, not monetary policy objectives
