# Question

During a period of high inflation, a central bank is most likely to:

## Options

- **A**: Lower the policy rate and purchase government securities
- **B**: Raise the policy rate and sell government securities
- **C**: Lower the reserve requirement and purchase government securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

To combat high inflation, central banks implement contractionary monetary policy, which includes:
- Raising the policy rate (increases borrowing costs, reduces spending)
- Selling government securities (removes money from circulation via open market operations)

Both actions reduce the money supply and aggregate demand, helping to lower inflation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes expansionary policy, which would worsen inflation
- Option C also describes expansionary measures that would increase inflation
