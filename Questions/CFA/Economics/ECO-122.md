# Question

For a monopolist, marginal revenue is less than price because:

## Options

- **A**: The monopolist can charge any price it wants
- **B**: To sell an additional unit, the monopolist must lower the price on all units
- **C**: Monopolists always have constant returns to scale

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A monopolist faces the market's downward-sloping demand curve. To sell more units, it must lower the price. The marginal revenue from an additional unit equals the price of that unit minus the revenue lost from selling all other units at the lower price. This makes MR < P for a monopolist, unlike perfect competition where MR = P.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is true but doesn't explain why MR < P
- Option C relates to production costs, not the revenue relationship
