# Question

Frictional unemployment results from:

## Options

- **A**: Permanent elimination of jobs in declining industries
- **B**: Normal job search time as workers transition between positions
- **C**: Economic downturns and weak aggregate demand

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Frictional unemployment is the temporary unemployment that occurs as workers search for jobs that match their skills and preferences. It exists even in healthy economies because job matching takes time—new graduates seeking first jobs, people relocating, or workers seeking better opportunities. Frictional unemployment is generally considered voluntary and non-problematic.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes structural unemployment
- Option C describes cyclical unemployment
