# Question

In the Cournot duopoly model, firms compete by choosing:

## Options

- **A**: Prices simultaneously
- **B**: Output quantities simultaneously
- **C**: Prices sequentially, with one firm moving first

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In Cournot competition, duopolists simultaneously choose production quantities, taking the other's output as given. The market price then adjusts based on total supply. The Cournot equilibrium results in output above monopoly but below perfect competition levels. Bertrand competition, alternatively, involves simultaneous price competition, while Stackelberg involves sequential quantity choices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Bertrand competition
- Option C describes a sequential game, not the simultaneous Cournot model
