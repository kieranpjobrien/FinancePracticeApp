# Question

A sinking fund provision typically:

## Options

- **A**: Increases bondholder risk by forcing early redemption at potentially unfavorable prices
- **B**: Reduces credit risk by ensuring gradual debt retirement
- **C**: Guarantees bondholders will receive par value at maturity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sinking fund: requires issuer to retire portion of bonds periodically. Reduces credit risk—less debt outstanding at maturity. Methods: open market purchases or lottery at par. May disadvantage bondholders if rates fall (redeemed at par when trading above).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes potential disadvantage but misses primary credit benefit
- Option C ignores that some bonds retired early via sinking fund
