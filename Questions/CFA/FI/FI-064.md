# Question

A sinking fund provision requires the issuer to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase coupon payments over time
- **B**: Retire a portion of the bond issue periodically before maturity
- **C**: Maintain a minimum credit rating

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sinking fund: issuer retires bonds gradually—either purchasing in market or calling at par (usually by lottery). Reduces refinancing risk for issuer and credit risk for investors. However, creates call-like risk for investors if bonds called at par in low-rate environment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes step-up coupons
- Option C describes a maintenance covenant, not sinking fund
