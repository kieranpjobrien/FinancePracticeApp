# Question

A callable bond gives the issuer the right to:

## Options

- **A**: Extend the maturity date
- **B**: Redeem the bond before maturity at a specified price
- **C**: Convert the bond to equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Callable bond: issuer can repurchase (call) bond before maturity, usually at par plus call premium. Issuers call when rates fall—refinance at lower rates. Call provision benefits issuer, not bondholder. Callable bonds offer higher yields to compensate for call risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes extendible bonds
- Option C describes convertible bonds
