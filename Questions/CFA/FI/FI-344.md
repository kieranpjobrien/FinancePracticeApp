# Question

All else equal, a lower-coupon bond has:

## Options

- **A**: Lower duration than a higher-coupon bond
- **B**: Higher duration than a higher-coupon bond
- **C**: The same duration as a higher-coupon bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lower coupon = higher duration. With smaller coupons, larger proportion of value comes from distant principal repayment. Cash flows are more back-loaded, increasing duration. Extreme case: zero-coupon bond has duration equal to maturity (highest for its term).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—reverses the relationship
- Option C—coupon rate affects duration
