# Question

Credit analysis of banks differs from non-financial corporates primarily because:

## Options

- **A**: Banks have no credit risk
- **B**: Bank balance sheets are highly leveraged with deposit funding and regulated capital requirements
- **C**: Banks only lend to governments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bank credit analysis: high leverage (10-20x), deposit funding, regulatory capital constraints. Focus on capital adequacy (CET1, tier 1), asset quality (NPLs), liquidity, earnings. Different metrics than industrial companies. Systemic importance and too-big-to-fail considerations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—banks have significant credit risk
- Option C—banks lend to various borrowers
