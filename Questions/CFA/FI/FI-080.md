# Question

A negotiable certificate of deposit (CD) differs from a regular bank CD in that it:

## Options

- **A**: Pays higher interest rates
- **B**: Can be traded in the secondary market
- **C**: Is insured by the government for higher amounts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negotiable CDs: large-denomination ($100,000+) time deposits that can be traded in secondary market. Issued by banks. Regular CDs have early withdrawal penalties; negotiable CDs provide liquidity through trading instead.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—rates depend on market conditions, not negotiability
- Option C—insurance limits same regardless of negotiability
