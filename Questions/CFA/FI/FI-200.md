# Question

Sustainability-linked bonds differ from green bonds in that:

## Options

- **A**: Proceeds must fund specific green projects
- **B**: Coupon rate adjusts based on issuer achieving sustainability performance targets
- **C**: They can only be issued by governments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sustainability-linked bonds (SLBs): coupon tied to KPI targets (emissions reduction, renewable energy use). If target missed, coupon steps up. Proceeds unrestricted—incentivizes overall company sustainability. Different from use-of-proceeds bonds (green/social).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes green bonds
- Option C—corporates issue most SLBs
