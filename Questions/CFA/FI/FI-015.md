# Question

US Treasury securities are considered to have:

## Options

- **A**: High credit risk due to government debt levels
- **B**: Virtually no credit risk, serving as the risk-free benchmark
- **C**: Higher yields than corporate bonds of similar maturity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Treasury securities: backed by full faith and credit of US government. Considered default-free—benchmark for risk-free rate. Other bonds priced as spread over Treasuries. Lower yields than corporate bonds reflect absence of credit risk premium.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—Treasuries considered credit risk-free despite debt levels
- Option C reverses relationship—Treasuries yield less due to safety
