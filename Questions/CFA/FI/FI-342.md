# Question

In a competitive Treasury auction:

## Options

- **A**: All bidders pay the same price
- **B**: Bidders specify both quantity and yield, winning at their bid price
- **C**: Only retail investors may participate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Competitive bid: specify amount and yield. Bids ranked from lowest to highest yield. Awards fill from lowest yield up until issue sold. Single-price auction: all pay lowest accepted price. Multiple-price: each pays own bid. US Treasuries use single-price.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes non-competitive or single-price result
- Option C—institutions dominate competitive bidding
