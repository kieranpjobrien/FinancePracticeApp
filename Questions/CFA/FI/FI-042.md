# Question

US Treasury bonds use which day count convention?

## Options

- **A**: 30/360
- **B**: Actual/Actual
- **C**: Actual/360

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Day count conventions: Treasuries use Actual/Actual (actual days in period/actual days in year). Corporate bonds typically 30/360 (assume 30-day months, 360-day year). Money market often Actual/360. Convention affects accrued interest calculations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is convention for corporate bonds
- Option C is common for money market instruments
