# Question

Treasury bills (T-bills) are:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term bonds with semi-annual coupon payments
- **B**: Short-term discount securities with maturities up to one year
- **C**: Inflation-protected securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T-bills: short-term Treasury securities (4, 8, 13, 17, 26, 52 weeks). Zero-coupon—issued at discount, mature at par. Sold at weekly auctions. Highly liquid money market instruments. Quoted on discount basis. Used for cash management and as risk-free rate proxy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Treasury bonds
- Option C describes TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities)
