# Question

A government issuing bonds in its own currency has:

## Options

- **A**: Higher default risk than issuing in foreign currency
- **B**: Lower default risk because it can print money to service debt
- **C**: The same default risk regardless of currency

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Local currency debt: government can monetize (print money), reducing outright default risk. Trade-off: inflation risk instead. Foreign currency debt can't be monetized—must earn/borrow foreign currency to repay. Argentina, Russia defaulted on foreign currency debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores currency's impact on default risk
