# Question

A revenue bond is backed by:

## Options

- **A**: The municipality's general taxing power
- **B**: Income from a specific project or enterprise
- **C**: State government guarantee

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Revenue bonds: backed by specific revenue stream (tolls, utility fees, hospital revenue, airport fees). No recourse to general taxes. Credit depends on project's ability to generate revenue. Often higher yield than GO bonds due to narrower backing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes general obligation bonds
- Option C—typically no state guarantee
