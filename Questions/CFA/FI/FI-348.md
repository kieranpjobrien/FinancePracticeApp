# Question

An amortizing bond:

## Options

- **A**: Pays all principal at maturity
- **B**: Repays principal gradually over the bond's life
- **C**: Never pays principal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Amortizing: principal repaid in installments, not bullet at maturity. Each payment includes interest and principal. Common for mortgages, auto loans. Duration lower than bullet bond of same maturity. Reduces refinancing risk for issuer; reinvestment considerations for investor.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes bullet bond
- Option C—all bonds eventually repay principal
