# Question

Covered bonds differ from asset-backed securities in that covered bonds:

## Options

- **A**: Have no recourse to the issuing bank
- **B**: Provide dual recourse to both the cover pool and the issuing bank
- **C**: Are always backed by auto loans

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered bonds: dual recourse—claim on issuer (bank) and segregated cover pool (usually mortgages). Assets stay on bank's balance sheet. If bank defaults, bondholders have priority claim on cover pool. Common in Europe; lower risk than ABS.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—covered bonds have MORE recourse than ABS
- Option C—typically backed by mortgages or public sector loans
