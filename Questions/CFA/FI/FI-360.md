# Question

An inverted yield curve typically signals:

## Options

- **A**: Strong economic growth ahead
- **B**: Expected economic slowdown or recession with lower future rates
- **C**: Rising inflation expectations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inverted curve: short rates exceed long rates. Often precedes recessions. Markets expect Fed to cut rates as economy weakens. Long rates low due to lower growth/inflation expectations. Historically reliable recession predictor. Reflects pessimistic economic outlook.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—normal/steep curve associated with growth
- Option C—rising inflation usually steepens curve
