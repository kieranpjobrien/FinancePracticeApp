# Question

A deferred coupon bond:

## Options

- **A**: Pays no coupons for an initial period, then pays regular coupons
- **B**: Pays all coupons at maturity
- **C**: Has coupons that decrease over time

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Deferred coupon: no cash interest for initial years (deferred period), then regular coupons begin. Interest accrues during deferral. Common for project finance—matches cash flow to project revenue timing. Higher reinvestment risk than regular bonds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes accumulated coupon payment, not deferred structure
- Option C describes step-down bonds
