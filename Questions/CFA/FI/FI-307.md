# Question

A credit default swap (CDS) spread represents:

## Options

- **A**: The premium paid by the protection buyer to the protection seller, expressed in basis points annually
- **B**: The difference between two corporate bond yields
- **C**: The bid-ask spread in corporate bond trading

## Answer

A

## Explanation

CDS spread: annual premium (in bps) for credit protection. Protection buyer pays spread, receives par if reference entity defaults. Higher spread = higher perceived default risk. Market-implied credit risk measure. Traded independently of underlying bonds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—describes credit spread between bonds
- Option C—describes trading liquidity spread
