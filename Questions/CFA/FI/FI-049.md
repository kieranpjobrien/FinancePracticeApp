# Question

Reinvestment risk is the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: Bond prices will fall when rates rise
- **B**: Coupon payments will be reinvested at lower rates than expected
- **C**: The issuer will default on payments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reinvestment risk: coupons and principal may be reinvested at rates different from original YTM. Higher for: high coupon bonds (more cash to reinvest), longer maturities, declining rate environments. Zero-coupon bonds have no reinvestment risk—no interim cash flows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes price risk/market risk
- Option C describes credit/default risk
