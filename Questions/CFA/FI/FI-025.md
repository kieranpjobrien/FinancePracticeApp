# Question

A putable bond benefits investors because:

## Options

- **A**: The issuer can call the bond at any time
- **B**: Investors can sell the bond back to the issuer at a specified price
- **C**: It pays higher coupons than comparable bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Putable bond: investor has right to sell bond back to issuer at par (usually) on specified dates. Valuable when rates rise—can reinvest at higher rates. Put option benefits investor, so putable bonds have lower yields than otherwise similar non-putable bonds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes callable bonds (issuer benefit)
- Option C is opposite—putables have lower yields due to investor benefit
