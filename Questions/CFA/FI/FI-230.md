# Question

Money duration (dollar duration) equals:

## Options

- **A**: Modified duration × Portfolio market value
- **B**: Modified duration ÷ Portfolio market value
- **C**: Macaulay duration × Yield

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Money duration = Modified duration × Market value. Measures dollar change in value for 1% yield change. DV01 = Money duration / 10,000 (dollar change per basis point). Useful for hedging and risk management in dollar terms.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B inverts the relationship
- Option C describes different duration relationship
