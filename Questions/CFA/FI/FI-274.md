# Question

A callable bond has a price of 102 when yields are 5%. If yields rise 50 bps to 5.5%, the price falls to 99. If yields fall 50 bps to 4.5%, the price rises to 103. The effective duration is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 3.9
- **B**: 4.0
- **C**: 8.0

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Effective duration = (P₋ - P₊) / (2 × P₀ × Δy) = (103 - 99) / (2 × 102 × 0.005) = 4 / 1.02 = 3.92 ≈ 3.9. Note asymmetric price response due to call option: price rise limited (103 vs 102 = +1) but price fall larger (99 vs 102 = -3). Call caps upside.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—slight rounding difference
- Option C—forgets to divide by 2 in formula
