# Question

Credit spreads typically widen during:

## Options

- **A**: Economic expansions with low default rates
- **B**: Economic recessions when default risk increases
- **C**: Periods of low interest rate volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit spread behavior: widens during recessions, market stress, rising defaults—"flight to quality" pushes Treasury prices up and corporate prices down. Tightens during expansions with low defaults and risk appetite. Spreads are countercyclical to economic growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—spreads tighten during expansions
- Option C—volatility typically causes spread widening
