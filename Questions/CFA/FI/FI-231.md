# Question

Treasury securities are considered benchmark bonds because they:

## Options

- **A**: Have the highest yields in the market
- **B**: Are considered default-free and highly liquid
- **C**: Are issued only in small amounts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Treasuries as benchmark: no credit risk (backed by US government), highly liquid (deep markets), full yield curve available. Other bonds priced as spread to Treasuries. On-the-run Treasuries most commonly used as benchmarks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—Treasuries have lowest yields (no credit risk)
- Option C—Treasuries issued in large amounts (adds liquidity)
