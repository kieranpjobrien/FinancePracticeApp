# Question

The Fisher equation relates:

## Options

- **A**: Spot rates to forward rates
- **B**: Nominal interest rates to real rates and expected inflation
- **C**: Credit spreads to default probabilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fisher equation: Nominal rate ≈ Real rate + Expected inflation. More precisely: (1 + nominal) = (1 + real) × (1 + expected inflation). Breaks down nominal rate into real return and inflation compensation. Foundation for understanding TIPS and inflation-linked bonds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes forward rate relationship
- Option C—describes credit risk decomposition
