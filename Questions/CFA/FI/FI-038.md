# Question

Bond covenants are provisions that:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee the bond will never default
- **B**: Restrict issuer actions to protect bondholder interests
- **C**: Allow issuers unlimited flexibility in operations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covenants: legal restrictions in bond indenture. Affirmative covenants: actions issuer must take (maintain insurance, pay taxes). Negative covenants: actions prohibited (limit additional debt, asset sales, dividends). Protect bondholders from risk-increasing behavior. Covenant-lite has fewer protections.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates—covenants reduce but don't eliminate risk
- Option C is opposite—covenants restrict, not expand flexibility
