# Question

During the expansion phase of the credit cycle:

## Options

- **A**: Default rates typically rise and spreads widen
- **B**: Corporate profitability improves, leverage may increase as companies borrow, spreads narrow
- **C**: All new issuance stops

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Expansion: profits up, credit metrics initially improve. Risk appetite increases, spreads narrow. Late expansion: leverage rises as companies/investors become complacent. Sets stage for next downturn. Cycles repeat—expansion, peak, contraction, trough.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes contraction phase
- Option C—issuance typically robust in expansion
