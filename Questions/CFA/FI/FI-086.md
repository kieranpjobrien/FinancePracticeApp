# Question

Compared to a fixed-rate bond, a floating-rate note has:

## Options

- **A**: Higher interest rate risk
- **B**: Lower interest rate risk
- **C**: The same interest rate risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FRN interest rate risk limited to time until next reset. Coupon adjusts to market, so price stays near par. Duration approximately equals time to next reset. Fixed-rate bonds have duration equal to weighted average of all cash flows—much higher rate sensitivity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores reset mechanism's impact on duration
