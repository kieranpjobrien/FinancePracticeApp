# Question

Why might a specific bond issue have a different rating than the issuer's corporate credit rating?

## Options

- **A**: Bond ratings are always the same as issuer ratings
- **B**: The bond may have features like security or subordination affecting its expected recovery
- **C**: Rating agencies always rate issues higher than issuers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Issue rating adjusts for seniority, security, guarantees. Secured bond may rate higher than issuer (better recovery). Subordinated bond rates lower. Notching adjusts from issuer rating. Recovery prospects drive issue-level rating differences.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores structural features affecting issue ratings
- Option C—direction of notching depends on structure
