# Question

High-yield bonds are also known as:

## Options

- **A**: Treasury bonds
- **B**: Junk bonds or speculative-grade bonds
- **C**: Investment-grade bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High-yield (junk, speculative-grade): rated below Baa3/BBB-. Higher default risk compensated by higher yields. Two origins: "fallen angels" (downgraded from investment grade) and "original issue high-yield" (issued as speculative). Different investor base than investment grade.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes government bonds
- Option C is the opposite category
