# Question

Duration is most accurate for estimating price changes when:

## Options

- **A**: Yield curve changes are large and non-parallel
- **B**: Yield curve shifts are small and parallel
- **C**: Only long-term rates change

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Duration assumes parallel shift and small changes. For large moves, convexity adjustment needed. Non-parallel shifts require key rate durations. Duration is first-order approximation—works well for small parallel shifts. Real curves often shift non-parallel.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—duration less accurate for large, non-parallel moves
- Option C—duration measures sensitivity to entire curve, assuming parallel
