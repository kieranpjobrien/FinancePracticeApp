# Question

A catastrophe bond (cat bond) is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Principal and interest payments that depend on insurance-related events
- **B**: Government guarantee against natural disasters
- **C**: Fixed payments regardless of any events

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cat bonds: transfer insurance risk to capital markets. Principal/coupon at risk if specified catastrophe occurs (hurricane, earthquake). High yields compensate for loss risk. Uncorrelated with financial markets. Issued by insurers/reinsurers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—cat bonds are private, not government-guaranteed
- Option C—payments contingent on triggering events
