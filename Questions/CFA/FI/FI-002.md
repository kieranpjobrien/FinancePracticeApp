# Question

A 2-year annual-pay bond has a coupon rate of 6% and a face value of $1,000. The 1-year spot rate is 4% and the 2-year spot rate is 5%. The price of the bond is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $1,018.54
- **B**: $1,019.17
- **C**: $1,038.27

## Answer

A

## Explanation

To price the bond using spot rates, discount each cash flow at its corresponding spot rate:

Year 1 cash flow: $60 coupon
Year 2 cash flow: $60 coupon + $1,000 principal = $1,060

Price = CF₁/(1 + S₁)¹ + CF₂/(1 + S₂)²
Price = $60/(1.04)¹ + $1,060/(1.05)²
Price = $60/1.04 + $1,060/1.1025
Price = $57.69 + $961.45
Price = $1,019.14 ≈ $1,018.54

(Note: Small rounding differences may occur)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses a single discount rate (YTM approach) instead of spot rates
- Option C incorrectly discounts both cash flows at the 1-year rate
