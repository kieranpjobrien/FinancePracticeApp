# Question

A higher debt-to-EBITDA ratio generally indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Lower financial risk and stronger credit quality
- **B**: Higher financial leverage and potentially weaker credit quality
- **C**: Better ability to service debt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt/EBITDA: measures leverage—years of EBITDA needed to repay debt. Higher ratio = more leverage, higher risk. Industry-specific thresholds apply. 2-3x often considered moderate; 5-6x+ high. Used with coverage ratios for complete credit picture.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses relationship—higher leverage means more risk
- Option C is opposite—higher ratio means harder to service debt
