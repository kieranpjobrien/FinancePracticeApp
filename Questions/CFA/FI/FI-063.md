# Question

The G-spread (government spread) is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Bond yield minus the yield on a government bond of similar maturity
- **B**: The difference between high-yield and investment-grade yields
- **C**: The spread between two corporate bonds

## Answer

A

## Explanation

G-spread: yield difference between corporate bond and government benchmark of similar maturity. Simple, commonly used spread measure. Nominal spread—doesn't account for shape of yield curve. For US corporates, typically spread over on-the-run Treasury.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes credit sector spread
- Option C describes spread between two corporates, not over government
