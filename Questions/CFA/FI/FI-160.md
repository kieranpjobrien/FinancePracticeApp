# Question

Monte Carlo simulation is preferred over binomial trees for valuing:

## Options

- **A**: Simple callable bonds
- **B**: Mortgage-backed securities with path-dependent cash flows
- **C**: Zero-coupon Treasury bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monte Carlo: simulates thousands of rate paths, averages values. Essential for path-dependent instruments like MBS—prepayments depend on rate history, not just current rate. Binomial works for path-independent options but not prepayment modeling.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—binomial sufficient for simple callables
- Option C—no optionality, basic DCF works
