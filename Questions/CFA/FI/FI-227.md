# Question

The nominal spread (G-spread) is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Bond yield minus the yield of a similar-maturity Treasury
- **B**: Bond yield minus LIBOR
- **C**: Bond yield minus the risk-free spot rate

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Nominal spread (G-spread): yield difference between corporate bond and Treasury of same maturity. Simple measure—doesn't account for term structure shape. G for "government." Compare to Z-spread (accounts for curve) and OAS (accounts for options).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes spread to LIBOR/SOFR (used for floaters)
- Option C describes portion of Z-spread calculation
