# Question

A collateralized loan obligation (CLO) is backed by:

## Options

- **A**: Residential mortgages
- **B**: A portfolio of leveraged loans (bank loans to below-investment-grade companies)
- **C**: Government bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CLO: securitized leveraged loans. Pool of syndicated bank loans to speculative-grade companies. Tranched like other ABS. Floating-rate loans, so less interest rate risk. Actively managed—manager can trade loans within guidelines. Credit risk is key concern.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes MBS
- Option C describes no credit risk, opposite of CLO
