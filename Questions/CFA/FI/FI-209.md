# Question

A serial bond issue has:

## Options

- **A**: A single maturity date for all bonds
- **B**: Bonds maturing on different dates according to a schedule
- **C**: Callable provisions on all bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Serial bonds: portions mature on different dates (e.g., $10M per year for 10 years). Common for municipal bonds matching project revenue. Provides predictable debt service. Contrast with term bonds (single maturity) or sinking fund.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes term bonds
- Option C describes call features, not maturity structure
