# Question

A special purpose vehicle (SPV) in securitization is designed to be:

## Options

- **A**: Part of the originator's consolidated balance sheet
- **B**: Bankruptcy-remote from the originator
- **C**: Subject to all of the originator's liabilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

SPV: legally separate entity holding securitized assets. Bankruptcy-remote: if originator fails, SPV assets protected from originator's creditors. Investors have claim on SPV assets only. True sale of assets to SPV achieves separation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—SPV designed to be OFF balance sheet
- Option C—SPV isolated from originator's other liabilities
