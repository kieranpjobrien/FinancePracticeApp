# Question

A bond's par value represents:

## Options

- **A**: The price paid by the investor at purchase
- **B**: The principal amount repaid at maturity
- **C**: The current market price of the bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Par value (face value): principal amount repaid at maturity. Typically $1,000 for corporate bonds, $100 for government bonds. Coupon payments calculated as percentage of par. Market price may differ from par (premium/discount) based on interest rates and credit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—purchase price can differ from par
- Option C—market price fluctuates based on rates and credit
