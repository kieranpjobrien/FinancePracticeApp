# Question

Non-conforming mortgages are those that:

## Options

- **A**: Meet all GSE (Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac) underwriting standards
- **B**: Do not meet GSE standards due to size, credit, or documentation issues
- **C**: Are guaranteed by the federal government

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Non-conforming: mortgages that don't meet GSE criteria. May be jumbo (exceeds loan limits), alt-A (limited documentation), or subprime (weak credit). Cannot be securitized into agency MBS. Securitized as private-label (non-agency) MBS with credit risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes conforming loans
- Option C—only FHA/VA loans have government guarantee
