# Question

Collateralized mortgage obligations (CMOs) differ from pass-throughs by:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminating prepayment risk entirely
- **B**: Redistributing prepayment risk among tranches with different priorities
- **C**: Providing government guarantees on all tranches

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CMO: redistributes pass-through cash flows into tranches. Sequential-pay: first tranche receives all principal until retired. PAC tranches have more predictable cash flows; support tranches absorb prepayment variability. Doesn't eliminate risk—redirects it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—prepayment risk redistributed, not eliminated
- Option C—guarantee depends on underlying mortgages, not CMO structure
