# Question

An issuer credit rating differs from an issue credit rating because:

## Options

- **A**: Issue ratings consider specific bond features like seniority and collateral
- **B**: Issuer ratings are always lower than issue ratings
- **C**: Issue ratings only apply to investment-grade bonds

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Issuer rating: overall creditworthiness of company. Issue rating: specific bond considering seniority, security, guarantees. Secured bonds may rate higher than issuer; subordinated debt rates lower. Notching adjusts from issuer rating based on structural features.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—secured debt can rate higher than issuer
- Option C—issue ratings apply to all rated bonds
