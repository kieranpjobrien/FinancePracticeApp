# Question

Effective duration is preferred over modified duration for bonds with embedded options because:

## Options

- **A**: It ignores interest rate sensitivity
- **B**: It accounts for how cash flows change when interest rates change
- **C**: It only works for zero-coupon bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Effective duration: measures price sensitivity accounting for option-adjusted cash flows. Callable bonds: cash flows shortened if rates fall (call likely). Modified duration assumes fixed cash flows, inaccurate for optioned bonds. Effective duration = (P- - P+) / (2 × P0 × Δy).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—effective duration measures sensitivity
- Option C—effective duration works for any bond with options
