# Question

Duration times spread (DTS) measures:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rate risk only
- **B**: A bond's sensitivity to changes in credit spreads
- **C**: The time to maturity multiplied by coupon rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DTS = Duration × Credit spread. Measures credit risk exposure. Higher DTS = more spread duration risk. Useful for comparing credit risk across bonds of different durations and spreads. Analogous to interest rate duration but for spread changes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—DTS specifically addresses spread risk
- Option C—unrelated calculation
