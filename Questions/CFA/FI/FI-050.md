# Question

Price risk and reinvestment risk have what relationship?

## Options

- **A**: They move in the same direction
- **B**: They partially offset each other—when one increases, the other decreases
- **C**: They are completely unrelated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Offsetting risks: when rates rise, prices fall (bad) but reinvestment rates rise (good). When rates fall, prices rise (good) but reinvestment rates fall (bad). Immunization strategies exploit this offset. Duration matching can minimize combined effect over investment horizon.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—they actually offset
- Option C ignores the complementary nature of these risks
