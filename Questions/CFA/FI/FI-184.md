# Question

Contingent convertible bonds (CoCos) are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Optional conversion at the bondholder's discretion
- **B**: Automatic conversion or write-down triggered by capital ratio falling below threshold
- **C**: Guaranteed principal protection

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CoCos: hybrid bank capital. Automatically convert to equity or write down principal if bank's capital ratio drops below trigger. Provides loss absorption in distress. Higher yield due to write-down risk. Used for regulatory capital (AT1/Tier 2).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes traditional convertibles
- Option C—CoCos have principal loss risk by design
