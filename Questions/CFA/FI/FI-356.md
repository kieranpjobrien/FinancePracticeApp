# Question

A serial bond issue:

## Options

- **A**: Has all bonds maturing on the same date
- **B**: Has bonds maturing on a series of different dates
- **C**: Pays variable interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Serial bond: portions mature at regular intervals (annually, for example). Common for municipal issuers matching revenue streams. Different series may have different rates. Reduces refinancing risk. Contrast with term bond (single maturity date).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes term bond
- Option C—describes floating-rate, unrelated to maturity structure
