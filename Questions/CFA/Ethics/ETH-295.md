# Question

The key element that distinguishes market manipulation from legitimate trading is:

## Options

- **A**: The size of the trade
- **B**: The intent to deceive or artificially affect prices
- **C**: Whether the trade is profitable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market manipulation under Standard II(B) is defined by intent to deceive or artificially affect prices, not by trade size or profitability. Legitimate large trades or unprofitable manipulation attempts are still judged by intent.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses market impact with manipulation
- Option C ties violation to outcome rather than intent
