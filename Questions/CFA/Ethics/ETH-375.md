# Question

A member receives a valid subpoena requesting confidential client information. The member should:

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to provide any information to protect client confidentiality
- **B**: Comply with the legal requirement while notifying the client if permitted
- **C**: Provide only partial information to balance legal and ethical duties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(E) allows disclosure of confidential information when required by law. A valid subpoena represents such a legal requirement. Members should comply while protecting client interests to the extent possible, including notification if legally permitted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that legal requirements override confidentiality
- Option C could constitute obstruction of a legal process
