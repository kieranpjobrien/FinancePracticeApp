# Question

Which activity would most likely constitute market manipulation under Standard II(B)?

## Options

- **A**: Executing a large order that moves the market price
- **B**: Placing trades intended to artificially inflate a security's price
- **C**: Recommending a stock that subsequently declines in value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard II(B) prohibits practices that distort prices or artificially inflate trading volume with the intent to mislead market participants. Trading with the intent to manipulate prices is prohibited, while legitimate large trades that happen to move prices are not.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes legitimate trading that may have market impact
- Option C involves a failed recommendation, not manipulation
