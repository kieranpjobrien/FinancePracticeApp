# Question

A sell-side analyst is invited by a company to tour its overseas manufacturing facility, with all travel expenses paid by the company. Under Standard I(B):

## Options

- **A**: The analyst must decline as any company-paid travel is prohibited
- **B**: The analyst may accept if the trip has a legitimate business purpose and is disclosed
- **C**: The analyst may accept without disclosure if the trip is brief

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(B) permits accepting company-paid travel for legitimate business purposes such as site visits, provided the arrangement is disclosed to the employer. The key is transparency and ensuring the travel serves research purposes rather than being lavish entertainment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; legitimate business travel is acceptable
- Option C ignores the disclosure requirement
