# Question

The primary purpose of disclosing conflicts of interest is to:

## Options

- **A**: Protect the professional from liability for biased recommendations
- **B**: Allow clients to evaluate whether conflicts may have influenced the professional's judgment
- **C**: Satisfy regulatory requirements without affecting investment decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Disclosing conflicts of interest enables clients to assess whether those conflicts may have influenced the professional's recommendations or actions. With this information, clients can appropriately weigh advice and make more informed decisions. Disclosure promotes transparency and informed decision-making.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A focuses on liability protection rather than client benefit
- Option C treats disclosure as a formality rather than substantive client protection
