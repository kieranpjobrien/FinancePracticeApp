# Question

New information emerges that contradicts an analyst's recent buy recommendation. Under Standard V(A):

## Options

- **A**: The analyst should maintain the recommendation to avoid appearing indecisive
- **B**: The analyst should promptly reassess the recommendation based on the new information
- **C**: Only annual reviews of recommendations are required

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard V(A) requires ongoing diligence. When material new information emerges, analysts must reassess their conclusions. Maintaining a recommendation despite contradictory evidence violates the duty to have a reasonable, current basis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes appearance over analytical integrity
- Option C understates the requirement for ongoing diligence
