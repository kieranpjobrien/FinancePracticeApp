# Question

Independence and objectivity in investment analysis is most likely threatened when an analyst:

## Options

- **A**: Uses publicly available research from multiple sources to form opinions
- **B**: Accepts expensive gifts from companies she covers
- **C**: Disagrees with the consensus view on a stock's valuation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accepting expensive gifts from covered companies creates a conflict of interest that can compromise an analyst's independence and objectivity. The analyst may consciously or unconsciously produce more favorable research to maintain the relationship and continue receiving benefits.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes appropriate research practice that supports independent analysis
- Option C actually demonstrates independence—willingness to hold a different view from the crowd
