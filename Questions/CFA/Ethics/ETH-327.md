# Question

A client offers to fund the education of a portfolio manager's child. Under Standard IV(B):

## Options

- **A**: Educational funding is not considered compensation
- **B**: This substantial benefit should be disclosed and approved by the employer
- **C**: Only direct cash payments require disclosure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(B) covers all forms of compensation that could create conflicts, including substantial non-cash benefits like education funding. Such arrangements must be disclosed to and approved by the employer.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the value and potential influence of such benefits
- Option C too narrowly defines covered compensation
