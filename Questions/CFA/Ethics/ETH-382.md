# Question

An analyst owns shares in a company she covers and is about to issue a buy recommendation. Standard VI(A) requires:

## Options

- **A**: No disclosure if the position is small relative to the market
- **B**: Disclosure of the ownership interest to clients and employer
- **C**: Immediate sale of the position before issuing any recommendation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(A) requires disclosure of ownership interests regardless of size. The potential conflict exists regardless of position size. Disclosure allows clients to evaluate the recommendation with knowledge of the analyst's personal interest.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A creates an undefined materiality threshold
- Option C imposes a requirement beyond what the Standard mandates
