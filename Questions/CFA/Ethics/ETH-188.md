# Question

Which characteristic is essential for an occupation to be considered a profession?

## Options

- **A**: High barriers to entry through costly education
- **B**: A duty to serve clients' interests ahead of one's own
- **C**: Government-mandated licensing requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A defining characteristic of a profession is the duty to place client interests ahead of personal gain. While education requirements and licensing may exist, the ethical duty to clients is what distinguishes professions from other occupations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an entry barrier but not a defining professional characteristic
- Option C describes a regulatory feature that exists across many occupations
