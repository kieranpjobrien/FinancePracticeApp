# Question

An analyst owns shares in a company she covers and is about to issue a buy recommendation. Under Standard VI(A), she must:

## Options

- **A**: Sell her shares before issuing the recommendation
- **B**: Disclose her ownership in the research report
- **C**: Refrain from covering companies she owns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(A) requires disclosure of beneficial ownership in securities being recommended. The analyst need not sell her shares or stop coverage, but must disclose the ownership so readers can evaluate potential bias.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is not required; disclosure is the remedy, not divestment
- Option C is overly restrictive; coverage with disclosure is permitted
