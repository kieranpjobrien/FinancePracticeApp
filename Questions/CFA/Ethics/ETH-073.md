# Question

The requirement to have a "reasonable basis" for investment recommendations means that recommendations should be supported by:

## Options

- **A**: At least three independent analyst opinions agreeing on the recommendation
- **B**: Appropriate research and investigation proportionate to the complexity of the investment
- **C**: A guarantee that the investment will achieve its expected returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A reasonable basis requires that recommendations be supported by adequate research and analysis appropriate to the investment's complexity. The depth of research should match the nature of the investment—complex or novel investments require more thorough investigation. There is no requirement for multiple opinions or performance guarantees.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets an arbitrary threshold unrelated to reasonable basis requirements
- Option C demands guarantees that are impossible in investing; reasonable basis concerns the quality of analysis, not outcomes
