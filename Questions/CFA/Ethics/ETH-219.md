# Question

A portfolio manager claims credit for strong returns that were primarily due to a colleague's stock picks. Under Standard I(C):

## Options

- **A**: This is acceptable since the manager oversees the portfolio
- **B**: Misrepresenting the source of performance violates the Standard
- **C**: Internal attribution of credit is not covered by the Standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(C) prohibits misrepresentation, including false claims about performance attribution. Taking credit for work done by others misrepresents professional qualifications and achievements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses oversight with personal contribution
- Option C incorrectly limits the scope of misrepresentation
