# Question

An analyst publishes a research report containing false positive information about a stock she owns, intending to profit from the resulting price increase. This action most directly violates standards concerning:

## Options

- **A**: Fair dealing with clients
- **B**: Market manipulation through information dissemination
- **C**: Suitability of investment recommendations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Disseminating false or misleading information with the intent to artificially influence a security's price constitutes market manipulation. This information-based manipulation undermines market integrity by causing prices to reflect false information rather than true supply and demand. The analyst's intent to profit from the price movement she causes makes the violation clear.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relates to treating clients equitably, which is secondary to the manipulation issue
- Option C addresses whether recommendations match client needs, not the truthfulness of the underlying information
