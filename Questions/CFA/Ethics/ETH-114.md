# Question

How can overconfidence bias affect ethical decision-making in investment management?

## Options

- **A**: It helps professionals make faster, more decisive ethical choices
- **B**: It may cause professionals to underestimate the likelihood of their actions causing harm
- **C**: It has no effect on ethical decisions, only on investment decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Overconfidence bias can lead investment professionals to underestimate risks associated with their actions, including ethical risks. They may believe they can "handle" situations that actually pose significant ethical dangers or assume negative consequences won't materialize.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses speed with quality in decision-making
- Option C incorrectly limits the scope of overconfidence bias to purely technical decisions
