# Question

The "reasonable investor" test for materiality asks whether:

## Options

- **A**: Any investor would want to know the information
- **B**: The information would be important to a reasonable investor's decision
- **C**: The information guarantees profitable trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Materiality is assessed by whether a reasonable investor would consider the information important in making an investment decision. This is a practical test focusing on likely impact, not universal interest or guaranteed outcomes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets too broad a standard
- Option C confuses materiality with profitability
