# Question

Why is trust considered essential to the investment management profession?

## Options

- **A**: Trust reduces the need for regulatory oversight
- **B**: Clients cannot fully evaluate investment advice quality without specialized knowledge
- **C**: Trust allows managers to charge higher fees

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trust is essential because investment management involves significant information asymmetry. Clients typically lack the specialized knowledge to fully evaluate the quality of investment advice and must rely on the integrity and competence of their advisors. This makes trust fundamental to the client-advisor relationship.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect because trust does not eliminate the need for regulation; both work together
- Option C is incorrect as trust is about client protection, not fee justification
