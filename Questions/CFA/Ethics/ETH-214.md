# Question

Information asymmetry between investment managers and clients creates:

## Options

- **A**: An advantage that managers should exploit
- **B**: A heightened need for ethical conduct and trust
- **C**: An opportunity for regulatory arbitrage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When clients cannot fully evaluate the quality of services due to information asymmetry, they must trust their managers to act ethically. This imbalance makes ethical conduct even more important in the investment profession.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A suggests exploiting a position of trust
- Option C misunderstands the ethical implications of information imbalance
