# Question

An analyst incorporates statistical data and analysis from a third-party research provider into her own report without attribution. This action most likely violates professional standards concerning:

## Options

- **A**: Fair dealing with clients
- **B**: Misrepresentation through plagiarism
- **C**: Duties to employers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Using another's work without proper attribution constitutes plagiarism, which is a form of misrepresentation. By presenting others' analysis as her own, the analyst misrepresents the source and originality of the work. Professional standards require giving credit to the original sources of ideas, analysis, and data.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relates to treating clients equitably, not the attribution of work
- Option C could be relevant if the analyst violated employer policies, but the primary violation is misrepresentation
