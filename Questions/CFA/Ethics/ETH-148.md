# Question

An analyst relies on a third-party research report to make a recommendation without reviewing the underlying methodology. This approach:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable since the third party is responsible for accuracy
- **B**: May violate Standard V(A) if the analyst cannot establish reasonable basis for the recommendation
- **C**: Is always prohibited under the Standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard V(A) requires analysts to have a reasonable basis for recommendations, even when using third-party research. Members must evaluate the methodology and assess whether the research provides adequate support. Blind reliance on third-party work may not satisfy this requirement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly shifts responsibility for having a reasonable basis
- Option C is too absolute; third-party research can be used with proper evaluation
