# Question

A trader places numerous small buy orders throughout the day to create the appearance of sustained demand, then sells a large position at the artificially elevated price. This constitutes:

## Options

- **A**: Legitimate market making activity
- **B**: A violation of Standard II(B) Market Manipulation
- **C**: Acceptable if no false statements are made

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This describes "painting the tape" - a form of transaction-based manipulation that artificially inflates prices through deceptive trading patterns. Standard II(B) prohibits actions designed to mislead market participants about true supply and demand.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses manipulation with legitimate market making
- Option C ignores that manipulation can occur through transactions, not just statements
