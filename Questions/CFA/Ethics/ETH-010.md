# Question

A portfolio manager learns that her firm will be issuing a strong buy recommendation on a small-cap stock tomorrow morning. Before the market opens, she places a personal order to buy shares of the stock. She plans to hold the shares for her retirement account. According to Standard VI(B) Priority of Transactions, this action:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable because personal retirement accounts are treated differently than other personal trading accounts
- **B**: Violates the standard because client transactions must have priority over personal transactions in securities being recommended
- **C**: Is acceptable if she discloses the trade to her compliance department after execution

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(B) requires that investment transactions for clients and employers must have priority over transactions in which a member is the beneficial owner. Trading ahead of a pending recommendation (front-running) is a clear violation, regardless of the purpose of the personal account. The manager knew the recommendation would likely move the stock price and positioned herself to benefit before clients could act.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly suggests retirement accounts have different treatment—all personal beneficial ownership is subject to the same rules
- Option C is incorrect because disclosure after the fact does not cure a front-running violation; the violation occurs at the time of the trade
