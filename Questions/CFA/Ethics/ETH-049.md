# Question

Which of the following trading activities is most likely to be considered legitimate rather than market manipulation?

## Options

- **A**: Executing trades at the close to artificially influence settlement prices
- **B**: Spreading false rumors to create trading opportunities
- **C**: Executing large trades that may temporarily impact prices due to order size

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Large trades may legitimately impact prices simply due to their size relative to normal trading volume. This price impact is a natural market consequence, not manipulation, as long as the trades are executed for legitimate investment purposes. The key distinction is intent—legitimate trading to establish or exit positions differs from trading designed to artificially influence prices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes "marking the close," a manipulative practice designed to artificially influence closing prices
- Option B describes information-based manipulation through disseminating false information
