# Question

A trader places large orders with the intent to cancel them before execution to influence prices. This practice, known as "spoofing":

## Options

- **A**: Is a legitimate trading strategy
- **B**: Violates Standard II(B) as market manipulation
- **C**: Is only illegal if profits are made

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Spoofing—placing orders intended to be cancelled to create false impressions of supply or demand—is market manipulation prohibited by Standard II(B). The intent to mislead, not profitability, determines whether conduct is manipulative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly legitimizes deceptive practices
- Option C ties violation to profit rather than intent
