# Question

An advisor discovers that a client's investment funds appear to originate from money laundering. Under Standard III(E), the advisor:

## Options

- **A**: Must maintain confidentiality regardless of the source of funds
- **B**: May disclose to appropriate authorities as illegal activity is an exception to confidentiality
- **C**: Should confront the client and demand an explanation before any action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(E) includes an exception for illegal activity. When members have reason to believe client funds are derived from illegal activity, they may (and under AML laws, may be required to) report to appropriate authorities without violating confidentiality requirements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly treats confidentiality as absolute
- Option C could jeopardize an investigation and is not required
