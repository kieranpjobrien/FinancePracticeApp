# Question

A trader spreads false rumors about a company's financial difficulties, then profits by short selling the stock as its price falls. This violates Standard II(B) as:

## Options

- **A**: Transaction-based manipulation
- **B**: Information-based manipulation
- **C**: Insider trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Spreading false or misleading information to affect security prices constitutes information-based manipulation under Standard II(B). The trader's intent to profit from the resulting price movement makes this a clear violation, distinct from transaction-based manipulation which involves trading patterns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A involves manipulative trading patterns, not false information
- Option C involves trading on material nonpublic information, not spreading false information
