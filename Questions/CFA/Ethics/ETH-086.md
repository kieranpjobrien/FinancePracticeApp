# Question

Investment professionals should disclose to clients the general nature of their compensation arrangements because:

## Options

- **A**: Clients have a legal right to know exact salary information
- **B**: Compensation structure may create incentives that could bias recommendations
- **C**: Industry regulations require public disclosure of all compensation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

How a professional is compensated can create incentives that might influence recommendations. For example, commission-based compensation might incentivize recommending more transactions, while asset-based fees might favor holding assets. Clients should understand these potential biases to appropriately evaluate advice they receive.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates what must be disclosed; general nature is required, not exact amounts
- Option C mischaracterizes disclosure requirements; they are typically to clients, not public
