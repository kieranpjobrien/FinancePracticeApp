# Question

An analyst plans to recommend a small-cap stock in tomorrow's research report. She purchases shares in her personal account today, before the report is published. This action:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable if she discloses her ownership in the report
- **B**: Violates priority of transactions rules by trading ahead of clients
- **C**: Is permissible since personal investing is allowed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trading ahead of clients or the firm based on pending recommendations (front-running) violates the priority of transactions standard. Client interests must come first, meaning clients should have the opportunity to act on recommendations before the professional trades for her own account. Even with disclosure, the timing violation remains.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly suggests disclosure cures the front-running violation
- Option C ignores the specific prohibition on trading ahead of client recommendations
