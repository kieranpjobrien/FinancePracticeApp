# Question

An analyst who owns shares in a company she covers should:

## Options

- **A**: Sell the shares before publishing any research on the company
- **B**: Disclose the ownership interest in research reports about the company
- **C**: Only disclose ownership if it exceeds 5% of the company's outstanding shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Personal ownership in covered securities creates a potential conflict that should be disclosed to readers. Disclosure allows clients to factor the potential bias into their evaluation of the research. There is no requirement to sell, and disclosure is appropriate regardless of the size of the position.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A imposes an unnecessarily restrictive requirement; disclosure is sufficient
- Option C sets an arbitrary threshold when any material ownership should be disclosed
