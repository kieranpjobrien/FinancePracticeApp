# Question

When referencing a professional designation in communications, members should:

## Options

- **A**: Use the designation as frequently as possible to maximize marketing impact
- **B**: Use the designation accurately and not overstate or misrepresent their qualifications
- **C**: Only reference the designation in formal legal documents

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professional designations should be used accurately and appropriately. Members must not exaggerate the meaning of a designation or imply qualifications they do not possess. While designations can be referenced in marketing and communications, the usage must be truthful and not misleading.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes marketing over accurate representation
- Option C is unnecessarily restrictive; appropriate use extends beyond legal documents
