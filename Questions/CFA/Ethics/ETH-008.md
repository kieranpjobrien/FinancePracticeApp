# Question

According to Standard IV(A) Loyalty, members must act for the benefit of their employer and not deprive their employer of the advantage of their skills and abilities. This duty of loyalty exists:

## Options

- **A**: Only while the member is physically at the workplace during business hours
- **B**: Until the employment relationship is terminated
- **C**: For a period of one year after leaving the employer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) Loyalty requires members to act in their employer's interest during the employment relationship. This duty continues until the relationship is terminated. Members should not engage in activities that compete with their employer or take actions that would harm the employer's interests while employed.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too narrow—the duty extends beyond just business hours and workplace presence
- Option C is incorrect—while some obligations (like confidentiality) may extend beyond employment, the general duty of loyalty ends when employment terminates
