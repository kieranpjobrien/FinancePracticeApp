# Question

When a code of ethics requires members to "act with integrity," this most likely means members should:

## Options

- **A**: Follow all applicable laws and regulations precisely as written
- **B**: Be honest and forthright in all professional dealings and communications
- **C**: Prioritize employer profitability to maintain financial stability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Acting with integrity means being honest, truthful, and forthright in all professional dealings. It requires consistency between one's words and actions, transparency in communications, and honoring commitments. Integrity goes beyond mere legal compliance to encompass ethical behavior in all situations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes legal compliance, which is necessary but not sufficient for integrity; one can follow laws while still acting without integrity
- Option C confuses business objectives with ethical conduct; integrity is about honesty and trustworthiness, not profitability
