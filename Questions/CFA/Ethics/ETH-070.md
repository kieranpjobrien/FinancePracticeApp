# Question

Before undertaking independent practice that could compete with the employer, an employee must:

## Options

- **A**: Wait until after resignation to discuss any plans with the employer
- **B**: Notify the employer and obtain consent before beginning the competing activity
- **C**: Only notify the employer if the competing practice will generate significant revenue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Employees must notify their employer and receive consent before engaging in independent practice that could compete with the employer or create conflicts of interest. This allows the employer to evaluate potential conflicts and decide whether to permit the activity. The notification should occur before the activity begins, not after.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A delays notification until it's too late for the employer to address conflicts
- Option C incorrectly suggests revenue size determines whether notification is required
