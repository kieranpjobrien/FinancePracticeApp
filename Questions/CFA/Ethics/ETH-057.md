# Question

Client confidentiality may be breached without violating professional standards when:

## Options

- **A**: Sharing client information helps the advisor generate more business referrals
- **B**: Disclosure is required by applicable law or regulation
- **C**: The information would be useful for other clients' investment decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Client information must be kept confidential except when disclosure is legally required. Legal or regulatory requirements—such as court orders, subpoenas, or mandatory regulatory reporting—override the general duty of confidentiality. In such cases, disclosure does not violate professional standards.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes the advisor's business interests over client confidentiality, which is inappropriate
- Option C would violate confidentiality even if the information could benefit other clients
