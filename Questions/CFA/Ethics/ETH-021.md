# Question

When using an ethical decision-making framework, an investment professional facing a complex situation should first:

## Options

- **A**: Consult with legal counsel to determine which actions are legally permissible
- **B**: Identify the relevant facts, stakeholders, and ethical principles involved in the situation
- **C**: Consider the potential consequences for their career and reputation before taking action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An ethical decision-making framework begins with identifying and understanding the situation: gathering relevant facts, identifying all stakeholders affected, recognizing the ethical principles at stake, and considering applicable laws and standards. This foundational analysis must precede the evaluation of alternatives and decision-making. Starting with legal advice or personal consequences puts secondary considerations before the essential first step of understanding the ethical dimensions of the situation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be appropriate later in the process, but legal analysis alone is insufficient for ethical decision-making; ethics may require more than legal compliance
- Option C prioritizes self-interest, which contradicts the ethical principle of placing client and public interests first; career concerns should not drive ethical decisions
