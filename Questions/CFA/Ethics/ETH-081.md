# Question

A research report is prepared by a team of analysts, with each contributing specific sections. When the report is published under one analyst's name, the named analyst:

## Options

- **A**: Is solely responsible for the entire report including sections she did not write
- **B**: Bears responsibility for sections she wrote and should acknowledge other contributors
- **C**: Has no responsibility for sections prepared by other team members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When publishing group research, analysts should acknowledge all contributors and are primarily responsible for sections they personally prepared. However, the named analyst should also ensure the overall report is coherent and that she has reasonable confidence in the quality of all sections. Proper attribution of contributions maintains transparency and accountability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may overstate individual responsibility for others' work
- Option C understates the named analyst's obligation to ensure overall report quality
