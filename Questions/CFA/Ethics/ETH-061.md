# Question

A portfolio manager directs client brokerage to a broker-dealer that charges slightly higher commissions but provides valuable research services. This arrangement:

## Options

- **A**: Violates the duty of loyalty because the manager is not obtaining the lowest possible trading costs
- **B**: May be acceptable if the research benefits clients and is properly disclosed
- **C**: Is only permissible if the research services are used exclusively for the accounts paying the higher commissions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Soft dollar arrangements, where clients pay higher commissions in exchange for research or services that benefit them, can be consistent with fiduciary duties if the research provides value to clients and the arrangement is properly disclosed. The key is that clients receive benefit commensurate with the additional cost and are informed of the practice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly assumes lowest cost is always required; the duty is to obtain best execution, which considers factors beyond price alone
- Option C is too restrictive; research obtained through soft dollars may benefit all clients, not just those whose trades generated the credits
