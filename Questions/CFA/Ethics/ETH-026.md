# Question

An investment analyst discovers that her firm's marketing materials contain statements about portfolio performance that, while technically accurate, are presented in a way that could mislead less sophisticated investors. The statements do not violate any securities laws. According to a professional code of ethics, the analyst should most appropriately:

## Options

- **A**: Take no action since the materials comply with legal requirements
- **B**: Raise concerns with appropriate parties about the potentially misleading presentation
- **C**: Report the firm to regulators for securities fraud

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professional codes of ethics require members to act with integrity and not engage in conduct that deceives or misleads clients or prospective clients. Even if materials are technically legal, presenting information in a misleading manner violates ethical standards. The appropriate response is to raise concerns internally through proper channels to have the materials revised.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly equates legal compliance with ethical behavior; ethics requires going beyond legal minimums
- Option C is disproportionate since no law has been violated; internal escalation is the appropriate first step for ethical concerns
