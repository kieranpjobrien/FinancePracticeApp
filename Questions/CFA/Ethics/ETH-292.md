# Question

An analyst consults with industry experts through an expert network service. Under Standard I(B):

## Options

- **A**: Expert networks are always prohibited
- **B**: The analyst must ensure information obtained is not material nonpublic information
- **C**: Expert consultations eliminate the need for independent research

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Expert networks can provide valuable insights, but Standard I(B) and II(A) require that analysts verify they are not receiving material nonpublic information. Due diligence on information sources is essential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; expert networks can be used appropriately
- Option C suggests expert input replaces independent analysis
