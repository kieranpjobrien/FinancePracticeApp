# Question

Under GIPS, composites are defined as:

## Options

- **A**: A grouping of all portfolios managed by a single portfolio manager
- **B**: An aggregation of portfolios managed according to a similar investment strategy or objective
- **C**: The firm's largest accounts that represent its best performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS requires firms to construct composites by grouping portfolios managed with similar strategies, objectives, or mandates. This ensures that performance presentations reflect the firm's actual results for a given strategy rather than cherry-picked accounts. Composite construction is based on investment approach, not manager or account size.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A groups by manager rather than investment strategy
- Option C describes cherry-picking, which GIPS specifically prohibits
