# Question

A firm's compensation structure heavily incentivizes product sales regardless of client suitability. A member working at this firm should:

## Options

- **A**: Follow the incentive structure as designed by the firm
- **B**: Raise concerns about the ethical implications and continue placing client interests first
- **C**: Leave the firm immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Members must place client interests first regardless of compensation incentives. When firm practices create ethical conflicts, members should raise concerns through appropriate channels while continuing to fulfill their ethical obligations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes firm incentives over client welfare
- Option C may be premature before attempting to address the issue
