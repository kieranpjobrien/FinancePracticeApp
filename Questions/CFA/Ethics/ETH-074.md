# Question

When communicating investment recommendations, professionals must disclose:

## Options

- **A**: Only the expected returns and not risk factors to avoid discouraging clients
- **B**: The basic format and principles of the investment process and any significant limitations
- **C**: Every assumption and calculation used in the analysis regardless of relevance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Communications should include disclosure of the investment process's basic approach and any significant limitations or risks. Clients need enough information to understand how recommendations are developed and what factors could affect outcomes. This does not require exhaustive disclosure of every detail, but material information must be communicated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A omits material risk information that clients need for informed decisions
- Option C imposes an impractical standard; disclosure should be material and relevant, not exhaustive
