# Question

The duty of loyalty to an employer most directly requires an employee to:

## Options

- **A**: Never disagree with management decisions or policies
- **B**: Act for the benefit of the employer and not cause harm to the employer
- **C**: Work exclusively for one employer and not have any outside business interests

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Loyalty to an employer means acting in the employer's interest and not taking actions that would harm the employer. This includes protecting confidential information, not competing with the employer, and acting in good faith. However, loyalty does not require blind obedience or prohibit all outside activities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses loyalty with unquestioning compliance; employees can and should raise legitimate concerns
- Option C is too broad; outside activities are permissible if they don't conflict with employer interests
