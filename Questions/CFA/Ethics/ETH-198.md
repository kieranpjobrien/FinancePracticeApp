# Question

A client's risk tolerance should be assessed by evaluating:

## Options

- **A**: Only the client's stated preferences
- **B**: Both the client's willingness to take risk and ability to bear risk
- **C**: Only the client's current portfolio holdings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(C) requires understanding both components of risk tolerance: willingness (psychological comfort with risk) and ability (financial capacity to bear losses). These may differ, and the lower of the two typically constrains appropriate risk levels.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the objective assessment of risk capacity
- Option C uses current holdings as a proxy rather than direct assessment
