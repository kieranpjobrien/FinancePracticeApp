# Question

An analyst leaving a firm wants to take copies of research she authored while employed. Under Standard V(C):

## Options

- **A**: The analyst owns her work product and may take copies
- **B**: Records created during employment generally belong to the employer
- **C**: Record ownership depends solely on the analyst's employment contract

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Records created during employment are typically considered property of the employer, not the individual analyst. While employment contracts may specify arrangements, the default presumption under Standard V(C) is that work product belongs to the firm.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly assumes employee ownership of work product
- Option C overstates the role of contracts; absent specific provisions, records belong to employer
