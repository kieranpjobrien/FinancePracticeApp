# Question

An analyst at a large investment firm notices that colleagues routinely share confidential client information during informal conversations. The analyst has never been explicitly told this is acceptable, but observes that senior managers participate in these discussions without objection. This situation best illustrates which challenge to ethical behavior?

## Options

- **A**: Overconfidence bias leading to excessive risk-taking in investment decisions
- **B**: Situational influences where observed group behavior normalizes unethical conduct
- **C**: Conflicts of interest between the analyst's personal trading and client recommendations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This scenario illustrates how situational influences can challenge ethical behavior. When unethical conduct is observed among peers and supervisors without consequence, it becomes normalized within the organization's culture. Individuals may rationalize participation because "everyone does it" or assume the behavior must be acceptable since leaders engage in it. Recognizing these situational pressures is essential for maintaining ethical conduct.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a cognitive bias related to investment decisions, not the social/organizational pressure described in the scenario
- Option C describes a different type of ethical issue (personal conflicts); the scenario specifically addresses information sharing norms within the firm culture
