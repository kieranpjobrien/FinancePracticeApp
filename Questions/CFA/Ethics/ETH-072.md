# Question

An analyst leaving for a competitor copies the firm's proprietary stock screening model to her personal device, reasoning that she helped develop it. This action:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable since she contributed to the model's development
- **B**: Violates duties to the employer by misappropriating confidential property
- **C**: Is permissible if she only uses it for personal, non-commercial purposes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proprietary models and other intellectual property developed during employment belong to the employer, regardless of the employee's contribution to their creation. Taking such materials when departing violates the duty of loyalty and misappropriates confidential employer property. The employee may take general knowledge and skills but not specific proprietary materials.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses contribution with ownership; employer owns work product
- Option C incorrectly suggests intended use determines whether taking the materials is permitted
