# Question

The primary purpose of maintaining records supporting investment recommendations is to:

## Options

- **A**: Provide marketing materials for prospective clients
- **B**: Document the basis for recommendations in case of later questions or disputes
- **C**: Satisfy employer record-keeping preferences

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Records should be maintained to demonstrate that recommendations had a reasonable basis at the time they were made. This documentation protects both the professional and the client by providing evidence of the analysis performed. If questions arise later about why a recommendation was made, records can substantiate the decision-making process.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses research records with marketing materials; they serve different purposes
- Option C focuses on employer preference rather than the professional responsibility to document analysis
