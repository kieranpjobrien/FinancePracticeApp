# Question

In a sale-leaseback transaction:

## Options

- **A**: A company purchases property to occupy
- **B**: An owner sells property and simultaneously leases it back from the buyer
- **C**: A lease is terminated early without penalty

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sale-leaseback structure:

**How it works:**
- Company owns property
- Sells to investor
- Leases back from buyer
- Continues occupying

**Seller benefits:**
- Unlocks capital
- Retains use of property
- Off-balance sheet (sometimes)
- Tax advantages possible

**Buyer benefits:**
- Stable tenant
- Long-term lease
- Strong credit (often)
- Predictable income

**Common situations:**
- Corporate restructuring
- Capital redeployment
- Debt reduction
- Focus on core business

**Lease terms:**
- Long duration (10-25 years)
- Triple net common
- Renewal options
- Rent escalations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes acquisition, not sale-leaseback
- Option C: No early termination involved
