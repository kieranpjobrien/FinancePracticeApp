# Question

Exchange-traded commodities (ETCs) differ from commodity ETFs primarily in:

## Options

- **A**: ETCs never track commodity prices
- **B**: ETCs are debt securities backed by physical commodities or derivatives
- **C**: ETCs are only available to institutional investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ETC structure:

**ETC characteristics:**
- Debt instrument structure
- Issued by special purpose vehicle
- Backed by physical or derivatives
- Trade like stocks on exchange

**ETF comparison:**
- ETFs: Fund structure
- ETCs: Debt notes
- Both exchange-traded
- Similar investor experience

**Physical ETCs:**
- Backed by actual commodity
- Stored in vaults (metals)
- Direct ownership exposure
- No roll yield

**Synthetic ETCs:**
- Use derivatives
- Swap agreements
- Counterparty risk
- Track futures

**Why structure matters:**
- Regulatory treatment
- Counterparty exposure
- Tax implications
- Investor protections

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: ETCs do track commodities
- Option C: Available to retail investors
