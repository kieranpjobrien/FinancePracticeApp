# Question

A long/short equity hedge fund strategy involves:

## Options

- **A**: Only buying undervalued stocks
- **B**: Buying stocks expected to rise and shorting stocks expected to fall
- **C**: Indexing to the S&P 500

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Long/short equity explained:

**Core approach:**
- Long positions: Expected to appreciate
- Short positions: Expected to decline
- Profit from both directions
- Reduce market exposure

**Net exposure:**
- Gross long - Gross short
- Can be net long, short, or neutral
- Varies by manager
- Typical: 20-60% net long

**Example:**
- $100M AUM
- $80M long positions
- $40M short positions
- Net exposure: 40%
- Gross exposure: 120%

**Alpha sources:**
- Long stock selection
- Short stock selection
- Sector/factor allocation
- Timing

**Risk reduction:**
- Hedge market risk
- Lower volatility than long-only
- Less directional dependence

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Only long is traditional investing
- Option C: Active, not passive strategy
