# Question

In a leveraged buyout (LBO), value is primarily created through:

## Options

- **A**: Investing only equity capital with no debt
- **B**: Using significant debt financing and operational improvements
- **C**: Holding companies indefinitely without exit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LBO value creation:

**Leverage component:**
- Significant debt (60-80% of purchase)
- Debt magnifies equity returns
- Interest tax deductible
- Cash flows service debt

**Operational improvements:**
- Cost reduction initiatives
- Revenue enhancement
- Working capital optimization
- Management incentives

**Multiple expansion:**
- Buy at lower multiple
- Sell at higher multiple
- Industry consolidation
- Strategic positioning

**Governance improvements:**
- Aligned management incentives
- Active board involvement
- Performance monitoring
- Strategic focus

**Exit strategies:**
- IPO
- Strategic sale
- Secondary buyout
- Dividend recapitalization

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Leverage is defining feature
- Option C: LBOs have planned exits (3-7 years)
