# Question

Hedge funds often target "absolute returns" meaning:

## Options

- **A**: Returns that exactly match a benchmark index
- **B**: Positive returns regardless of market direction
- **C**: The highest possible returns with unlimited risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Absolute return objective:

**Definition:**
- Positive returns in all conditions
- Not relative to benchmark
- Regardless of market direction
- Risk-adjusted focus

**Contrast with relative returns:**
- Relative: Beat the benchmark
- Can "outperform" with negative return
- Absolute: Positive in absolute terms

**How hedge funds pursue this:**
- Long and short positions
- Market hedging
- Uncorrelated strategies
- Risk management focus

**Reality check:**
- Not always achievable
- Drawdowns still occur
- Lower beta, not zero beta
- Risk-adjusted goal

**Appropriate expectations:**
- Lower volatility than markets
- Positive skew desired
- Consistent returns
- Crisis protection

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not benchmark-focused
- Option C: Risk-adjusted, not unlimited risk
