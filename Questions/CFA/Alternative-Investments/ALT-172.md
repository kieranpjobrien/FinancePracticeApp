# Question

Commitment pacing in alternative investment portfolios refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The speed of fund manager decision-making
- **B**: Scheduling new fund commitments to maintain target allocations over time
- **C**: How quickly investors can redeem their investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commitment pacing:

**Purpose:**
- Maintain target allocation
- Smooth vintage year exposure
- Manage cash flows
- Avoid denominator effects

**The challenge:**
- Commitments ≠ invested capital
- Capital called over time
- Distributions return capital
- Allocation drifts

**Pacing models consider:**
- Target allocation
- Expected capital calls
- Distribution projections
- Fund life assumptions

**Common approaches:**
- Overcommit strategy
- Commitment as % of target
- Net asset value targeting
- Cash flow matching

**Example:**
- Target: 10% of portfolio in PE
- May commit 15% to achieve 10%
- Accounts for uncalled capital
- J-curve effect

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Investor planning, not manager speed
- Option C: Redemption is different from pacing
