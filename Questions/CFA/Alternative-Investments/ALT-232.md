# Question

Weather derivatives in commodity markets are used to:

## Options

- **A**: Predict weather with perfect accuracy
- **B**: Hedge against financial losses from adverse weather conditions
- **C**: Control actual weather patterns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Weather derivatives:

**Purpose:**
- Transfer weather risk
- Financial protection
- Volume hedging
- Revenue stabilization

**Who uses them:**
- Energy companies
- Agricultural firms
- Ski resorts
- Outdoor events

**Common structures:**
- Heating degree days (HDD)
- Cooling degree days (CDD)
- Precipitation levels
- Temperature triggers

**Example - utility:**
- Warm winter reduces heating demand
- Lower gas sales revenue
- Weather derivative pays off
- Hedges volume risk

**Difference from insurance:**
- Parametric payout
- Based on index, not loss
- Faster settlement
- No proof of loss needed

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Hedges impact, doesn't predict
- Option C: Financial, not physical impact
