# Question

Mezzanine financing in private equity:

## Options

- **A**: Has priority over all other debt in the capital structure
- **B**: Is subordinated debt that often includes equity participation rights
- **C**: Represents the safest layer of the capital structure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mezzanine financing explained:

**Position in capital structure:**
- Below senior debt
- Above common equity
- Subordinated claims
- Higher risk than senior debt

**Typical features:**
- Higher interest rates (12-20%)
- Equity kickers (warrants, conversion)
- Payment-in-kind (PIK) options
- Less covenants than senior

**Equity participation:**
- Warrants to buy equity
- Conversion rights
- Equity co-invest options
- Upside participation

**Use in buyouts:**
- Bridge gap between senior debt and equity
- Reduces equity needed
- Enhances returns if successful
- Absorbs losses before equity

**Return sources:**
- Cash interest payments
- PIK interest accumulation
- Equity upside participation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Mezzanine is subordinated, not senior
- Option C: Mezzanine is riskier than senior debt
