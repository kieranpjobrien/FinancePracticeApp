# Question

Fixed income arbitrage hedge fund strategies typically:

## Options

- **A**: Only buy government bonds and hold to maturity
- **B**: Exploit pricing inefficiencies between related fixed income securities
- **C**: Avoid all forms of leverage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fixed income arbitrage:

**Core strategy:**
- Identify mispriced bonds
- Relative value focus
- Long/short positions
- Convergence trades

**Common trades:**
- On-the-run/off-the-run
- Yield curve arbitrage
- Interest rate swaps
- Credit spreads
- Mortgage prepayment

**Characteristics:**
- Low returns per trade
- High leverage amplifies
- Spread compression focus
- Interest rate exposure hedged

**Leverage use:**
- Essential for strategy
- 10-30x common
- Magnifies small spreads
- Increases risk

**Risks:**
- Spread widening
- Liquidity crisis
- Leverage amplification
- Basis risk

**LTCM lesson:**
- Extreme leverage (25x+)
- Near-collapse 1998
- Spreads widened dramatically

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Active trading, not buy-and-hold
- Option C: Highly leveraged typically
