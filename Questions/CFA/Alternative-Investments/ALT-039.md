# Question

Private debt investments differ from public bonds primarily because:

## Options

- **A**: Private debt is always unsecured
- **B**: Private debt is not traded on public exchanges
- **C**: Private debt has lower yields than public bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Private debt characteristics:

**Key distinction:**
- Not publicly traded
- Bilateral or club negotiations
- Limited secondary market
- Relationship-driven

**Types of private debt:**
- Direct lending
- Mezzanine
- Distressed debt
- Special situations
- Infrastructure debt

**Yield premium:**
- Higher yields than public bonds
- Illiquidity premium
- Complexity premium
- Smaller borrower premium

**Benefits:**
- Higher income
- Floating rate options
- Covenant protections
- Relationship value

**Risks:**
- Illiquidity
- Credit risk
- Concentration risk
- Valuation challenges

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Private debt can be secured or unsecured
- Option C: Private debt typically has higher yields
