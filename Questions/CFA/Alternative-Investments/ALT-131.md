# Question

Mortgage REITs differ from equity REITs in that mortgage REITs primarily:

## Options

- **A**: Own and operate physical properties
- **B**: Invest in real estate loans and mortgage-backed securities
- **C**: Only invest in residential properties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

REIT types compared:

**Equity REITs:**
- Own physical properties
- Collect rental income
- Property appreciation exposure
- ~90% of REIT market

**Mortgage REITs:**
- Own real estate loans
- Invest in MBS
- Interest income focus
- Interest rate sensitive

**Mortgage REIT income:**
- Net interest margin
- Spread between borrowing and lending
- Leverage-dependent
- Rate sensitive

**Risk differences:**
- Equity REITs: Property/market risk
- Mortgage REITs: Interest rate/credit risk
- mREITs typically more volatile

**Dividend yields:**
- mREITs often higher yields
- More variable payouts
- Interest rate driven

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes equity REITs
- Option C: Both can focus on residential or commercial
