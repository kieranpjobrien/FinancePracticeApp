# Question

Private company valuations typically rely on:

## Options

- **A**: Daily market prices from stock exchanges
- **B**: Methods such as comparable company analysis, precedent transactions, and DCF
- **C**: Only book value of assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Private company valuation methods:

**Comparable company analysis:**
- Public company multiples
- Applied to private company metrics
- EV/EBITDA, P/E common
- Discount for illiquidity

**Precedent transactions:**
- Similar M&A deals
- Transaction multiples
- Control premium included
- Recent is better

**Discounted cash flow:**
- Project future cash flows
- Discount to present value
- Terminal value calculation
- Requires assumptions

**Other methods:**
- Leveraged buyout analysis
- Asset-based valuation
- Recent financing rounds
- Rule of thumb multiples

**Challenges:**
- Less information available
- Subjectivity in assumptions
- Illiquidity discount
- Control premium questions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No public market prices
- Option C: Multiple methods typically used
