# Question

Funds from Operations (FFO) is used for REITs because:

## Options

- **A**: It increases the importance of depreciation
- **B**: It adds back depreciation to better reflect cash-generating ability
- **C**: It is identical to GAAP net income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FFO for REITs:

**Why FFO needed:**
- GAAP earnings include depreciation
- Real estate doesn't depreciate like equipment
- Depreciation distorts cash earnings
- FFO better shows cash generation

**FFO calculation:**
Net Income
+ Depreciation/amortization
- Gains on property sales
= FFO

**Why add back depreciation:**
- Real estate often appreciates
- Depreciation is accounting, not economic
- Better cash flow proxy
- Industry standard metric

**AFFO (Adjusted FFO):**
- FFO minus capital expenditures
- Even more conservative
- Accounts for maintenance capex
- "True" cash flow

**Valuation use:**
- Price/FFO ratio
- Compare REITs
- Dividend coverage
- Trend analysis

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: FFO removes depreciation impact
- Option C: FFO differs from net income
