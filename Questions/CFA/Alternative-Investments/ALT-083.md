# Question

An equity market neutral strategy aims to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize exposure to overall market movements
- **B**: Eliminate market risk while profiting from individual stock selection
- **C**: Only invest in the largest market cap stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market neutral approach:

**Objective:**
- Zero (or near-zero) market beta
- Returns from stock selection only
- Independent of market direction

**How it works:**
- Equal dollar long and short
- Or beta-matched positions
- Market moves cancel out
- Alpha from stock picking

**Example:**
- $50M long, $50M short
- Market rises 10%
- Longs gain $5M, shorts lose $5M
- Net return from selection skill

**Advantages:**
- Lower volatility
- Uncorrelated returns
- Consistent in all markets
- Pure alpha exposure

**Challenges:**
- Requires strong stock selection
- Shorting costs
- Leverage needed for returns
- Crowded trades

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Eliminates, not maximizes market exposure
- Option C: Strategy, not stock selection criterion
