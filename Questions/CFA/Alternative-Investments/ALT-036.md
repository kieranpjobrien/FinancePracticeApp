# Question

Growth equity investments differ from venture capital in that growth equity:

## Options

- **A**: Targets earlier stage companies with no revenue
- **B**: Invests in established companies with proven business models
- **C**: Uses significantly more leverage than buyouts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Growth equity characteristics:

**Target companies:**
- Established but still growing
- Proven revenue model
- Often profitable or near-profitable
- Seeking expansion capital

**Key differences from VC:**
- More mature companies
- Lower failure rates
- Less technology focus
- Shorter path to profitability

**Key differences from buyouts:**
- Typically minority stakes
- Less or no leverage
- Focus on growth, not restructuring
- Less operational involvement

**Use of proceeds:**
- Geographic expansion
- New product development
- Acquisitions
- Capacity expansion

**Risk-return profile:**
- Lower risk than VC
- Higher risk than buyouts
- Returns between VC and LBO

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: VC targets earlier stage
- Option C: Growth equity uses minimal leverage
