# Question

When evaluating alternative investment performance, the difference between gross and net returns primarily reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Tax implications for the investor
- **B**: Management fees and performance fees
- **C**: Currency hedging costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gross vs net returns:

**Gross returns:**
- Before all fees deducted
- Investment performance only
- Measures manager skill
- Used for attribution analysis

**Net returns:**
- After management and performance fees
- What investor actually receives
- Relevant for investor decisions
- Comparable across managers

**Fee impact example:**
- Gross return: 15%
- Management fee: 2%
- Performance fee: 20% of gain
- Net return: 15% - 2% - (13% × 20%) = 10.4%

**Why difference matters:**
- Can be 3-5%+ annually
- Compounds significantly over time
- Higher fees need higher gross returns
- Net returns for decision-making

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Taxes are separate calculation
- Option C: Hedging costs usually small vs fees
