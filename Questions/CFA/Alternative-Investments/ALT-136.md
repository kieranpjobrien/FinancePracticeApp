# Question

The size of the illiquidity premium in alternative investments depends on:

## Options

- **A**: Only the current stock market level
- **B**: Lock-up period length, asset complexity, and market conditions
- **C**: The fund manager's reputation exclusively

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Illiquidity premium determinants:

**Lock-up period:**
- Longer lock-up = higher premium
- 10-year PE vs 2-year hedge fund
- Capital tied up longer
- More compensation required

**Asset complexity:**
- Harder to value = higher premium
- Less liquid secondary market
- Specialized knowledge needed

**Market conditions:**
- Premium expands in stress
- Contracts in calm markets
- Cyclical variation
- Risk appetite driven

**Other factors:**
- Fund size
- Manager quality
- Strategy type
- Exit uncertainty

**Estimated premiums:**
- Private equity: 2-4%
- Real estate: 1-3%
- Venture capital: 3-5%
- Varies significantly

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Multiple factors beyond stock market
- Option C: Manager reputation is one factor of many
