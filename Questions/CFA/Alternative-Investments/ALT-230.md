# Question

Mezzanine debt in real estate capital structures:

## Options

- **A**: Has first priority claim on the property
- **B**: Sits between senior debt and equity with higher yields
- **C**: Is identical to common equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate mezzanine debt:

**Position:**
- Subordinate to senior debt
- Senior to equity
- Middle of capital stack
- Gap financing role

**Characteristics:**
- Higher interest rates (10-15%+)
- May have equity participation
- Second lien or unsecured
- Intercreditor agreements

**Common uses:**
- Bridge financing gaps
- Acquisition financing
- Development funding
- Refinancing situations

**Return sources:**
- Cash interest
- PIK (payment-in-kind) interest
- Exit fees
- Equity kickers

**Risk profile:**
- Higher risk than senior
- Lower risk than equity
- First loss after equity
- Upside participation possible

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Senior debt has first priority
- Option C: Debt instrument, not equity
