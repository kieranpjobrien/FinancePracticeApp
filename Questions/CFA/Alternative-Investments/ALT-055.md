# Question

Real estate is often considered an inflation hedge because:

## Options

- **A**: Property values always increase faster than inflation
- **B**: Rents and property values tend to rise with inflation over time
- **C**: Real estate investments are guaranteed against loss

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate and inflation:

**Inflation protection mechanisms:**
- Replacement cost increases
- Rents adjust over time
- Property values follow
- Real asset characteristics

**Lease structures matter:**
- Short-term leases: Quicker adjustment
- Long-term leases: Slower response
- CPI-linked escalations
- Percentage rent clauses

**Property type differences:**
- Multifamily: Annual lease renewals
- Retail: Percentage of sales
- Office: 3-10 year leases
- Industrial: Varies

**Limitations:**
- Short-term correlation weak
- Long-term relationship stronger
- Property-specific factors
- Market conditions vary

**Historical evidence:**
- Long-term positive correlation
- Not perfect hedge
- Better than bonds typically

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Values don't always beat inflation
- Option C: No investment is guaranteed
