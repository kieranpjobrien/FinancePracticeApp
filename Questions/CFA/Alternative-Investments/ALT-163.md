# Question

Dedicated short bias hedge fund strategies primarily:

## Options

- **A**: Only take long positions in stocks
- **B**: Maintain net short exposure to profit from declining markets
- **C**: Invest only in fixed income securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dedicated short bias:

**Strategy approach:**
- Net short equity exposure
- May have some longs
- Profit from market declines
- Contrarian positioning

**Typical exposure:**
- Net short 30-60%
- Some long positions
- Less than 100% short
- Hedged short

**When it works:**
- Bear markets
- Market corrections
- Overvalued markets
- Bubble bursting

**When it struggles:**
- Bull markets
- Rising equity markets
- Extended rallies
- Multiple expansion

**Portfolio role:**
- Tail risk hedge
- Diversification
- Crisis protection
- Reduce portfolio beta

**Challenges:**
- Long-term negative drift (markets rise)
- Short squeezes
- Borrowing costs
- Difficult to sustain

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Opposite - primarily short
- Option C: Equity-focused strategy
