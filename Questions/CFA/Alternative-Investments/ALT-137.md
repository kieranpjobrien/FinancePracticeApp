# Question

A "bolt-on" or "add-on" acquisition in private equity refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The initial acquisition of a platform company
- **B**: Acquiring smaller companies to add to an existing portfolio company
- **C**: Selling portions of a portfolio company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bolt-on acquisitions:

**Definition:**
- Smaller company acquired
- Added to existing platform
- Synergy creation
- Growth strategy

**Strategic rationale:**
- Geographic expansion
- Product line addition
- Customer base growth
- Capability acquisition

**Value creation:**
- Revenue synergies
- Cost synergies
- Multiple arbitrage
- Scale benefits

**Example:**
- PE owns regional distributor (platform)
- Acquires smaller local competitors
- Consolidates operations
- Expands market share

**Execution:**
- Platform company makes acquisition
- PE provides guidance/capital
- Integration is key
- Multiple bolt-ons possible

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Platform is initial, bolt-on is subsequent
- Option C: This describes divestiture
