# Question

In oil and gas investing, "proved reserves" refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Any hydrocarbons that might exist underground
- **B**: Quantities with reasonable certainty to be commercially recoverable
- **C**: Oil that has already been extracted

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reserve classifications:

**Proved reserves:**
- High confidence (90%+)
- Commercially recoverable
- Current technology
- Existing economic conditions

**Categories:**
- Proved developed: Wells drilled
- Proved undeveloped: Planned wells

**Probable reserves:**
- Less certain (50%+ likely)
- May be recoverable
- Additional drilling needed

**Possible reserves:**
- Least certain
- Speculative
- May never be developed

**Why classification matters:**
- Valuation input
- Reserve-based lending
- SEC reporting requirements
- Investor disclosure

**Reserve life:**
- Reserves / Production rate
- Measures sustainability

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes resources, not reserves
- Option C: This is production, not reserves
