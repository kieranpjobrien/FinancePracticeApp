# Question

Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchain consensus differs from Proof-of-Work (PoW) by:

## Options

- **A**: Requiring more energy consumption
- **B**: Selecting validators based on staked tokens rather than computational power
- **C**: Being less secure than Proof-of-Work

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PoS vs PoW comparison:

**Proof-of-Work (Bitcoin):**
- Miners solve puzzles
- Computational power determines odds
- High energy consumption
- Hardware investment required

**Proof-of-Stake (Ethereum 2.0):**
- Validators stake tokens
- Stake size influences selection
- Much lower energy use
- Capital at risk as security

**PoS advantages:**
- Energy efficient (99%+ reduction)
- Lower barrier to participate
- Environmentally friendly
- Scalability potential

**PoS mechanics:**
- Lock up tokens as collateral
- Selected to validate blocks
- Earn rewards for validation
- Slashing for misbehavior

**Staking returns:**
- Yield for participating
- Varies by network
- New income source for holders

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: PoS uses far less energy
- Option C: Different security model, not inherently less secure
