# Question

A capital commitment to a private equity fund represents:

## Options

- **A**: Capital immediately transferred to the fund
- **B**: A promise to provide capital when called by the GP
- **C**: The expected return on investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital commitment explained:

**Definition:**
- Legally binding promise
- To invest specified amount
- When GP makes capital calls
- Over the investment period

**How it works:**
- LP commits $10M to fund
- Capital not transferred immediately
- GP calls capital as needed
- Typically over 3-5 years

**Investment period:**
- First 3-5 years of fund life
- Capital called for new investments
- Follow-on investments may continue
- "Commitment period"

**LP considerations:**
- Must have liquidity available
- Uncertain timing of calls
- Penalties for failing to fund
- Cash management planning

**Unfunded commitment:**
- Difference between commitment and called
- Future obligation
- Must track across multiple funds

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Capital called gradually, not immediately
- Option C: Commitment is capital, not return
