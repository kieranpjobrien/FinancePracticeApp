# Question

Capital calls in private investment funds refer to:

## Options

- **A**: The fund returning capital to investors
- **B**: Requests for investors to fund their committed capital
- **C**: Emergency fundraising from new investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital calls explained:

**Process:**
1. Investor commits capital amount
2. Capital not transferred immediately
3. GP calls capital as needed
4. Investor must fund within notice period

**Typical structure:**
- Commitment period: 3-5 years
- Capital called as investments made
- Notice period: 10-30 days
- Penalty for failure to fund

**Why this approach:**
- Efficient capital deployment
- No idle cash in fund
- Better IRR for investors
- Flexibility for manager

**Investor considerations:**
- Must maintain liquidity reserves
- Uncertain timing of calls
- Multiple fund commitments compound
- Cash management planning needed

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes distributions
- Option C: Capital comes from existing committed LPs
