# Question

A clawback provision in private equity protects LPs by:

## Options

- **A**: Allowing them to withdraw capital at any time
- **B**: Requiring the GP to return excess carried interest if final returns fall short
- **C**: Guaranteeing a minimum return on investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Clawback provisions:

**Purpose:**
- Protects LPs from overpayment of carry
- Ensures carry reflects total fund performance
- Addresses timing of distributions

**How it works:**
- Early exits may generate carry
- Later investments may lose money
- Final fund return may not justify carry paid
- GP must return excess carry

**Example:**
- Early deals: 25% returns, carry paid
- Later deals: Losses occur
- Final fund IRR: 6% (below hurdle)
- GP owes back carried interest

**Practical considerations:**
- Usually at fund termination
- GP may need to escrow funds
- Tax complications for GP
- Collectability concerns

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Lock-ups prevent early withdrawal
- Option C: No return guarantees exist
