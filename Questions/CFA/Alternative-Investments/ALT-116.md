# Question

Triple net (NNN) leases in commercial real estate require tenants to pay:

## Options

- **A**: Only base rent with landlord covering all expenses
- **B**: Base rent plus property taxes, insurance, and maintenance
- **C**: A lower rent with no additional charges

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Triple net lease structure:

**Tenant responsibilities (3 nets):**
1. Property taxes
2. Insurance
3. Maintenance/CAM

**Landlord receives:**
- Base rent (relatively predictable)
- Pass-through of operating expenses
- Limited expense volatility

**Benefits for landlord:**
- Stable net income
- Reduced expense risk
- Simpler management
- Lower operating burden

**Benefits for tenant:**
- Control over property costs
- Direct expense management
- Often lower base rent
- Incentive for efficiency

**Common in:**
- Single-tenant retail
- Industrial properties
- Freestanding buildings
- Net lease REITs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes gross lease
- Option C: Total occupancy cost can be higher
