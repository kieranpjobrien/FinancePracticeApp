# Question

The total expense ratio for alternative investments:

## Options

- **A**: Is typically lower than for passive index funds
- **B**: Can be significantly higher due to management fees, performance fees, and other costs
- **C**: Is fixed by regulation for all funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Alternative investment costs:

**Fee components:**
- Management fee (1-2%)
- Performance fee (15-20%)
- Administrative costs
- Trading costs
- Fund expenses

**Comparison to traditional:**
- Index funds: 0.03-0.20%
- Active mutual: 0.50-1.50%
- Hedge funds: 1.5-2% + 20%
- Private equity: 2% + 20%

**Why higher:**
- Active management intensive
- Specialized strategies
- Limited competition
- Illiquidity premium capture

**Total cost example:**
- 2% management fee
- 20% of 10% gain = 2%
- Fund expenses: 0.5%
- Total: 4.5% of assets

**Impact on returns:**
- Significant over time
- Must generate alpha to justify
- Net returns matter

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Much higher than passive
- Option C: No regulatory fee limits
