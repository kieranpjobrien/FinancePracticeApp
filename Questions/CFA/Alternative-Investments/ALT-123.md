# Question

A dividend recapitalization in private equity involves:

## Options

- **A**: Selling the portfolio company to a strategic buyer
- **B**: Increasing debt to pay a dividend to equity holders
- **C**: Reducing the company's debt burden

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividend recapitalization:

**The transaction:**
- Company takes on new debt
- Proceeds paid as dividend to PE sponsor
- Partial return of capital
- No change in ownership

**Why sponsors do this:**
- Return capital to LPs
- Improve IRR (earlier cash flows)
- Retain upside exposure
- Fund life management

**Example:**
- PE owns 100% of company
- Company borrows $50M
- Dividend paid to PE sponsor
- Debt on company's balance sheet

**Considerations:**
- Increases company leverage
- Interest burden rises
- May limit future flexibility
- Debt service must be manageable

**Criticism:**
- Financial engineering focus
- Increased bankruptcy risk
- Less for operational improvement
- Moral hazard concerns

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No sale of company occurs
- Option C: Increases, not reduces debt
