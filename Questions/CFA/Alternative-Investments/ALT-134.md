# Question

Offshore hedge funds are typically domiciled outside the manager's country to:

## Options

- **A**: Avoid all regulations and oversight
- **B**: Provide tax efficiency for certain non-US and tax-exempt investors
- **C**: Generate higher investment returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Offshore fund structures:

**Why offshore:**
- Tax efficiency for non-US investors
- Tax-exempt investor accommodation
- Regulatory flexibility
- Global investor access

**Common jurisdictions:**
- Cayman Islands
- British Virgin Islands
- Ireland
- Luxembourg

**Who benefits:**
- Non-US investors (no US tax)
- US tax-exempt entities (UBTI avoidance)
- International investors

**Parallel structures:**
- Onshore: US taxable investors
- Offshore: Non-US/tax-exempt
- Same strategy, different vehicles

**Still regulated:**
- Offshore jurisdictions have rules
- SEC registration may apply
- Investor suitability requirements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Still subject to regulations
- Option C: Structure doesn't affect returns directly
