# Question

When conducting due diligence on a private equity fund, the most important factor is typically:

## Options

- **A**: The fund's stated target return
- **B**: The quality and stability of the investment team
- **C**: The fund's marketing materials

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PE due diligence priorities:

**Team quality indicators:**
- Track record attribution
- Team stability over time
- Key person experience
- Incentive alignment

**Why team matters most:**
- PE is skill-intensive
- Manager dispersion is wide
- Past success may predict future
- Relationships drive deal flow

**Key due diligence areas:**
1. Investment team and track record
2. Investment strategy and process
3. Operational capabilities
4. Fund terms and alignment
5. References and reputation

**Track record analysis:**
- Attribution to current team
- Consistent strategy
- Performance across cycles
- Deal-level returns

**Red flags:**
- Key departures
- Strategy drift
- Unexplained poor deals
- Misaligned incentives

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Target returns are just projections
- Option C: Marketing materials aren't due diligence
