# Question

Core real estate investments typically feature:

## Options

- **A**: Development projects with no current income
- **B**: Stabilized properties with high occupancy and predictable income
- **C**: Distressed properties requiring significant renovation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Core real estate:

**Characteristics:**
- Fully leased or nearly so
- High-quality tenants
- Prime locations
- Strong credit tenants

**Return profile:**
- Lower risk
- Lower returns (6-9% target)
- Income-focused
- Stable cash flows

**Property types:**
- Class A office
- Institutional retail
- Stabilized multifamily
- Industrial in strong markets

**Investor profile:**
- Income seekers
- Conservative allocation
- Long-term holders
- Pension funds

**Leverage:**
- Moderate (40-50% LTV)
- Conservative approach
- Investment grade focus

**Contrast with opportunistic:**
- Less development
- Less turnaround
- Less leverage
- More certainty

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is opportunistic/development
- Option C: This is value-add or opportunistic
