# Question

Compared to long-only equity managers, hedge funds typically:

## Options

- **A**: Have more restrictions on investment strategies
- **B**: Have greater flexibility to short sell, use leverage, and employ derivatives
- **C**: Charge only management fees with no performance fees

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hedge fund vs long-only:

**Hedge fund flexibility:**
- Short selling allowed
- Leverage use
- Derivatives trading
- Concentrated positions
- Cross-asset strategies

**Long-only constraints:**
- No shorting (typically)
- Limited leverage
- Benchmark relative
- Diversification requirements
- Regulatory limits

**Fee differences:**
- Hedge funds: 2 and 20 typical
- Long-only: 0.5-1% management
- Performance fees rare in long-only

**Risk management:**
- Hedge funds: Absolute return focus
- Long-only: Relative to benchmark
- Different risk metrics

**Liquidity:**
- Hedge funds: Lock-ups, gates
- Long-only: Often daily

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Hedge funds have fewer restrictions
- Option C: Performance fees are typical for HFs
