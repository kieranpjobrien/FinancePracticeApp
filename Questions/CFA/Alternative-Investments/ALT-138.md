# Question

A gross or full-service lease in commercial real estate:

## Options

- **A**: Requires tenants to pay all operating expenses directly
- **B**: Includes most operating expenses in the base rent paid by tenant
- **C**: Has no base rent, only expense reimbursements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gross lease structure:

**Tenant pays:**
- Base rent (includes expenses)
- Simplified payment
- Predictable occupancy cost

**Landlord covers:**
- Property taxes
- Insurance
- Common area maintenance
- Utilities (often)

**Where common:**
- Office buildings
- Multi-tenant properties
- Full-service environments

**Landlord considerations:**
- Expense risk retained
- Must estimate costs accurately
- Inflation exposure
- Operating efficiency incentive

**Comparison to NNN:**
- Gross: Landlord bears expense risk
- NNN: Tenant bears expense risk
- Net income more variable for gross

**Expense stops:**
- Base year expense level set
- Tenant pays increases above base
- Modified gross structure

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes NNN lease
- Option C: Base rent is the primary payment
