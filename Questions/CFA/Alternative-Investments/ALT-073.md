# Question

When commodity futures are in contango:

## Options

- **A**: Futures prices are below spot prices
- **B**: Futures prices are above spot prices, creating negative roll yield
- **C**: There is no relationship between spot and futures prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contango explained:

**Definition:**
- Futures price > Spot price
- Upward sloping futures curve
- Common in many markets

**Why contango occurs:**
- Storage costs (carry)
- Insurance costs
- Financing costs
- Convenience yield low

**Roll yield impact:**
- Sell expiring (lower price)
- Buy next month (higher price)
- Negative roll yield
- Reduces total return

**Example:**
- Spot oil: $70
- 1-month future: $71
- Sell at $71, buy next at $72
- $1 loss on roll

**Backwardation (opposite):**
- Futures < Spot
- Positive roll yield
- Often during shortages

**Investment implications:**
- Contango erodes returns
- Backwardation enhances returns
- Consider roll strategy

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes backwardation
- Option C: Clear mathematical relationship
