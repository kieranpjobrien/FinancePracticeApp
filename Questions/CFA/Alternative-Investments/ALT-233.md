# Question

A long-biased equity hedge fund:

## Options

- **A**: Maintains zero equity market exposure at all times
- **B**: Generally has more long positions than short, resulting in net positive market exposure
- **C**: Only takes short positions in stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Long-biased strategies:

**Characteristics:**
- Net long equity exposure
- Some short positions
- Positive market beta
- Participate in up markets

**Typical exposure:**
- 50-80% net long
- Varies by manager
- May adjust tactically
- Less hedged than market neutral

**How it differs:**
- From long-only: Has some shorts
- From market neutral: Net positive exposure
- From short-bias: Opposite direction

**Return drivers:**
- Market direction (beta)
- Stock selection (alpha)
- Sector allocation
- Short selection

**Portfolio role:**
- Equity exposure with hedge
- Lower beta than index
- Alpha potential
- Modest downside protection

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Has positive market exposure
- Option C: Describes dedicated short
