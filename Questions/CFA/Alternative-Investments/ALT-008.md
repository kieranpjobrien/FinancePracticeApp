# Question

The use of leverage in alternative investments:

## Options

- **A**: Reduces overall portfolio risk through diversification
- **B**: Amplifies both gains and losses
- **C**: Is prohibited by most regulatory frameworks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leverage in alternatives:

**How leverage works:**
- Borrow to increase investment size
- Magnifies returns (positive and negative)
- Common in hedge funds, PE, real estate

**Example:**
- 2:1 leverage on $100 investment
- $200 total exposure
- 10% gain → 20% return on equity
- 10% loss → 20% loss on equity

**Leverage sources:**
- Bank borrowing
- Prime brokerage margin
- Derivatives (embedded leverage)
- Fund-level debt

**Risks amplified:**
- Market risk
- Liquidity risk (margin calls)
- Counterparty risk
- Interest rate risk on borrowing

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Leverage increases, not reduces risk
- Option C: Leverage is widely used, not prohibited
