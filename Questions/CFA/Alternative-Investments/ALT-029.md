# Question

Benchmarking alternative investments is challenging because:

## Options

- **A**: Too many standardized benchmarks exist
- **B**: Most alternatives lack representative, investable benchmarks
- **C**: All alternative investments have the same return patterns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Benchmarking challenges:

**Issues with alternative benchmarks:**
- Voluntary reporting to databases
- Survivorship and backfill biases
- Not investable indices
- Different strategy definitions

**Comparison to traditional:**
- Stocks: S&P 500 easily tracked
- Bonds: Barclays Aggregate investable
- Alternatives: No equivalent

**Specific challenges by type:**
- Private equity: Vintage year matters
- Hedge funds: Strategy-specific needed
- Real estate: Property type/location varies

**What practitioners use:**
- Peer group comparisons
- Public market equivalents
- Custom benchmarks
- Absolute return targets

**Best practices:**
- Multiple comparison approaches
- Understand benchmark limitations
- Focus on risk-adjusted metrics
- Long-term evaluation periods

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Too few good benchmarks
- Option C: Wide variation in return patterns
