# Question

The internal rate of return (IRR) is commonly used for alternative investments because:

## Options

- **A**: It ignores the timing of cash flows
- **B**: It accounts for the timing and magnitude of cash flows
- **C**: It is easier to calculate than simple returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IRR for alternative investments:

**Why IRR is appropriate:**
- Cash flows occur at irregular intervals
- Capital called over time
- Distributions at various points
- Time-weighted measures not suitable

**IRR calculation:**
- Discount rate where NPV = 0
- Considers timing of each cash flow
- Reflects actual investor experience

**Example:**
- Year 0: -$100 (capital call)
- Year 3: -$50 (second call)
- Year 5: +$200 (distribution)
- Year 7: +$150 (final distribution)
- IRR: ~18%

**Limitations:**
- Assumes reinvestment at IRR
- Multiple IRRs possible
- Sensitive to timing
- Can be manipulated

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: IRR specifically accounts for timing
- Option C: IRR calculation is more complex
