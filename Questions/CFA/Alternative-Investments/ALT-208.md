# Question

Fund life extensions in private equity:

## Options

- **A**: Are never permitted under any circumstances
- **B**: May be granted with LP consent to allow time for remaining exits
- **C**: Are automatically applied to all funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fund extensions:

**Purpose:**
- Additional time for exits
- Market conditions unfavorable
- Value maximization
- Orderly liquidation

**Typical terms:**
- 10-year initial life
- 1-2 year extension options
- LP consent required
- Often in LPA provisions

**Process:**
- GP requests extension
- Rationale provided
- LP or LPAC approval
- Modified fee terms possible

**Reasons sought:**
- Remaining portfolio companies
- Poor exit market
- Maximize value vs fire sale
- Specific situations

**LP considerations:**
- Ties up capital longer
- Reduced management fees often
- Exit timeline uncertainty
- Continuation fund alternative

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Common with consent
- Option C: Must be requested and approved
