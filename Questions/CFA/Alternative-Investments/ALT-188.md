# Question

The "promote" or "carried interest" in real estate joint ventures represents:

## Options

- **A**: The tenant's share of profits
- **B**: The sponsor's disproportionate share of profits after investor hurdles are met
- **C**: The debt service payments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Promote in real estate:

**Definition:**
- Sponsor's incentive compensation
- Above their capital contribution %
- After investor return hurdles
- Performance-based

**Typical structure:**
- Investor contributes 90% capital
- Sponsor contributes 10%
- After 8% pref to investors
- Split changes (80/20 or 70/30)

**Tiered promote example:**
- 0-8% IRR: 90/10 split
- 8-12% IRR: 80/20 split
- 12%+ IRR: 70/30 split
- Sponsor upside increases

**Purpose:**
- Align interests
- Motivate performance
- Reward success
- Attract talent

**Similar to PE carry:**
- Performance-based
- After hurdle return
- Creates incentive
- Disproportionate share

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Sponsor, not tenant receives
- Option C: Promote is equity, not debt
