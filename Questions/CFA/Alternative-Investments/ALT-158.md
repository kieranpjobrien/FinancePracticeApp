# Question

Capacity constraints in alternative investments refer to:

## Options

- **A**: Physical storage limitations for documents
- **B**: Limits on how much capital a strategy can effectively manage
- **C**: The number of investors allowed in a fund

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capacity constraints:

**What limits capacity:**
- Market impact of trades
- Opportunity scarcity
- Liquidity availability
- Strategy alpha decay

**Strategy-specific:**
- Small-cap equity: Limited liquidity
- Merger arbitrage: Deal volume
- Distressed: Opportunity set
- Quant: Signal crowding

**Implications:**
- Fund may soft close
- Returns may decline with size
- Smaller funds may outperform
- Manager discipline important

**Soft close:**
- No new investors
- Existing may add
- Protect performance
- Responsible management

**Hard close:**
- No new capital at all
- Even existing investors
- Rare but occurs

**Investor consideration:**
- Access to capacity
- Track record before size
- Manager incentives

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Investment capacity, not storage
- Option C: Investor limits are separate (legal)
