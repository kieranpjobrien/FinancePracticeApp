# Question

A lock-up period in alternative investments refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The time managers must hold their own investment
- **B**: A period during which investors cannot redeem their capital
- **C**: The time between investment decision and capital deployment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lock-up periods explained:

**Definition:**
- Period when investor cannot withdraw capital
- Mandatory holding period
- Ranges from months to years

**Purpose:**
- Allows manager to execute strategy
- Prevents disruptive redemptions
- Matches liabilities to illiquid assets
- Provides investment stability

**Typical lock-ups:**
- Hedge funds: 1-2 years initial
- Private equity: 7-10 years (fund life)
- Real estate funds: 5-10 years

**After lock-up (hedge funds):**
- Redemption notice periods
- Gates limiting withdrawals
- Quarterly/annual redemption windows

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes co-investment requirements
- Option C: This is the investment period or ramp-up
