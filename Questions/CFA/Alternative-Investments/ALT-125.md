# Question

A concession agreement in infrastructure investments typically specifies:

## Options

- **A**: Only the initial construction requirements
- **B**: Rights, obligations, risk allocation, and asset transfer provisions
- **C**: Government ownership throughout the project life

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Concession agreements:

**Key elements:**
- Rights granted to operator
- Operating period length
- Performance standards
- Revenue/pricing mechanisms
- Risk allocation

**Common provisions:**
- Handback conditions
- Termination clauses
- Change provisions
- Dispute resolution
- Force majeure

**Asset transfer:**
- Typically returns to government
- At end of concession
- May require certain condition
- Handback reserves

**Risk allocation examples:**
- Construction: Usually private
- Demand: Varies by contract
- Regulatory: Shared or public
- Force majeure: Shared

**Duration:**
- 20-99 years
- Varies by asset type
- Allows investment recovery

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Covers full project life
- Option C: Private ownership during concession
