# Question

An earnout in private equity transactions:

## Options

- **A**: Requires immediate full payment of purchase price
- **B**: Makes part of the purchase price contingent on future performance
- **C**: Eliminates all seller risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Earnout provisions:

**Definition:**
- Contingent consideration
- Based on future performance
- Bridges valuation gap
- Additional payment if targets met

**How it works:**
- Base price paid at close
- Additional if metrics achieved
- Time period specified
- Clear measurement criteria

**Common metrics:**
- Revenue targets
- EBITDA thresholds
- Customer retention
- Product milestones

**When used:**
- Valuation disagreement
- Uncertain growth trajectory
- Key person retention
- Seller confidence signal

**Structure considerations:**
- Duration (1-3 years typical)
- Measurement methodology
- Control after sale
- Dispute resolution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Part is contingent, not all upfront
- Option C: Seller bears performance risk
