# Question

Anti-dilution provisions in venture capital protect investors from:

## Options

- **A**: Any future investment rounds occurring
- **B**: Share value decline when new shares are issued at lower valuations
- **C**: Having to invest additional capital

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Anti-dilution protection:

**What it protects:**
- Downround scenarios
- Lower valuation than prior round
- Existing share value dilution
- Investor's ownership percentage

**Types:**
- Full ratchet: Complete adjustment
- Weighted average: Partial adjustment
- Broad-based vs narrow-based

**Full ratchet:**
- Conversion price adjusts fully
- To new lower price
- Most investor-friendly
- Harsh on founders

**Weighted average:**
- Considers amount raised
- More balanced protection
- Industry standard
- Less founder dilution

**Example:**
- Series A at $10/share
- Series B at $5/share (down round)
- Anti-dilution adjusts Series A conversion

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Doesn't prevent future rounds
- Option C: Capital calls are separate issue
