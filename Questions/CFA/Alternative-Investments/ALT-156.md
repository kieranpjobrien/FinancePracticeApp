# Question

Convertible arbitrage strategies typically:

## Options

- **A**: Only invest in government bonds
- **B**: Buy convertible bonds and hedge by shorting the underlying stock
- **C**: Focus exclusively on currency trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convertible arbitrage:

**Basic strategy:**
- Long convertible bond
- Short underlying stock
- Capture mispricing
- Delta-hedged position

**Why it works:**
- Convertibles often undervalued
- Embedded optionality
- Market inefficiencies
- Complexity premium

**Profit sources:**
- Bond income (coupon)
- Volatility capture
- Gamma trading
- Credit spread moves

**Hedging mechanics:**
- Delta determines hedge ratio
- Adjust as stock moves
- Dynamic rebalancing

**Risks:**
- Credit deterioration
- Liquidity drying up
- Borrow costs spike
- Correlation breakdown

**2008 impact:**
- Strategy struggled
- Forced selling
- Liquidity crisis

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Corporate convertibles, not government
- Option C: Equity/credit focus, not currency
