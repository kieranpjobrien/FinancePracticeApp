# Question

Distressed debt investing involves:

## Options

- **A**: Purchasing debt of financially healthy companies
- **B**: Acquiring debt of troubled companies at discounted prices
- **C**: Only investing in government-guaranteed securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Distressed debt investing:

**Target investments:**
- Companies in financial distress
- Debt trading at deep discounts
- Bankruptcy or near-bankruptcy
- High yield bonds in default

**Investment thesis:**
- Buy debt below par value
- Potential recovery exceeds purchase price
- May convert to equity ownership
- Operational turnaround potential

**Strategies:**
- Active: Drive restructuring process
- Passive: Wait for recovery
- Loan-to-own: Convert debt to equity
- Trading: Short-term value capture

**Required expertise:**
- Credit analysis
- Bankruptcy law knowledge
- Restructuring experience
- Operational improvement skills

**Returns and risks:**
- High potential returns
- Significant loss potential
- Long, uncertain timeline
- Legal complexity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Distressed means troubled companies
- Option C: Not limited to government securities
