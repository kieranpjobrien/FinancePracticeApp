# Question

An endowment fund that uses a spending rule of 5% of a 3-year moving average of assets is primarily trying to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize short-term returns
- **B**: Smooth spending while preserving real purchasing power
- **C**: Minimize investment management costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Endowment spending rules balance competing objectives:

**Smoothing mechanisms (like 3-year average):**
- Reduce year-to-year spending volatility
- Prevent dramatic cuts during market downturns
- Provide predictable funding for beneficiaries
- Decouple spending from short-term market fluctuations

**Typical spending rates (4-5%):**
- Allow for inflation adjustment
- Aim to preserve real value over time
- Based on expected real returns

**Perpetual time horizon:**
- Endowments seek to exist indefinitely
- Must balance current spending with intergenerational equity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Endowments focus on long-term sustainability, not short-term maximization
- Option C: Spending rules address spending policy, not investment costs
