# Question

An Investment Policy Statement (IPS) should include the investor's:

## Options

- **A**: Specific security selections and trade timing
- **B**: Return objectives and risk tolerance
- **C**: Predictions for market performance next year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An Investment Policy Statement (IPS) is a written document that outlines:

**Objectives:**
- Return requirements (income, growth, total return)
- Risk tolerance (ability and willingness to take risk)

**Constraints (RRTTLLU):**
- Risk tolerance
- Return requirements
- Time horizon
- Taxes
- Liquidity needs
- Legal/regulatory
- Unique circumstances

The IPS serves as a long-term guide for investment decisions and benchmark for performance evaluation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Specific securities and trade timing are implementation details, not policy elements
- Option C: Market predictions are short-term views; the IPS establishes long-term policy independent of forecasts
