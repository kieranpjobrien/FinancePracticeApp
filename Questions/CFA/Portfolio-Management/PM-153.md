# Question

A risk parity approach to portfolio construction allocates capital such that:

## Options

- **A**: Each asset class has equal dollar weights
- **B**: Each asset class contributes equally to total portfolio risk
- **C**: The highest-returning asset class receives the largest allocation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk parity:

**Definition:**
- Allocate so each asset contributes equal risk
- Not equal dollar weights
- Focus on risk contribution, not capital allocation

**Implementation:**
- Lower volatility assets (bonds) get larger weights
- Higher volatility assets (stocks) get smaller weights
- Often uses leverage to achieve target return

**Example:**
- Stocks: 20% volatility → smaller allocation
- Bonds: 5% volatility → larger allocation
- Result: Equal risk contribution from each

**Benefits:**
- Diversification across risk sources
- Less dependent on equity risk premium
- May reduce drawdowns

**Criticisms:**
- Requires leverage
- Interest rate sensitive
- Historical correlations may not persist

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Equal weight ≠ equal risk contribution
- Option C: Risk parity focuses on risk, not returns
