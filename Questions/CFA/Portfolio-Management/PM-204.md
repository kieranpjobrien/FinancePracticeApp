# Question

Correlation risk in portfolio management refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The risk that expected returns will not be achieved
- **B**: The risk that correlations between assets change unexpectedly, affecting diversification
- **C**: The risk of making calculation errors in portfolio models

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Correlation risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk of unexpected changes in correlations
- Affects diversification benefits
- Particularly dangerous during market stress

**Sources:**
- Regime changes
- Crisis contagion
- Structural market changes
- Model misspecification

**Consequences:**
- Diversification fails
- VaR underestimates risk
- Unexpected portfolio losses
- Hedge effectiveness deteriorates

**Management approaches:**
- Stress testing with elevated correlations
- Scenario analysis
- Tail risk hedging
- Dynamic correlation models
- Margin of safety in estimates

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is return risk, not correlation risk
- Option C: This is operational/model risk
