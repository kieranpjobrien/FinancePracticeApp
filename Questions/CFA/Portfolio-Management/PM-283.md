# Question

The endowment effect causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Undervalue assets they already own
- **B**: Overvalue assets they already own
- **C**: Ignore assets they don't own

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Endowment effect:

**Definition:**
- Emotional bias
- Owned assets valued more highly
- Reluctance to sell at fair value
- "What's mine is worth more"

**Psychology:**
- Ownership creates attachment
- Loss aversion component
- Status quo bias related

**Investment impact:**
- Hold concentrated positions
- Demand premium to sell
- Resist portfolio changes
- Inherited stock syndrome

**Examples:**
- Inherited family shares
- Company stock from employer
- Original investments held too long

**Mitigation:**
- Regular portfolio review
- Ask "Would I buy this today?"
- Professional advice

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Endowment effect causes overvaluation
- Option C: Not related to ignored assets
