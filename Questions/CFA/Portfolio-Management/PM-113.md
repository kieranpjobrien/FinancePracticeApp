# Question

Position limits in a portfolio are typically set to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize returns by concentrating in best ideas
- **B**: Prevent excessive concentration in any single security or sector
- **C**: Ensure all positions are equally weighted

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Position limits:

**Purpose:**
- Prevent concentration risk
- Ensure adequate diversification
- Limit impact of single-name events
- Enforce risk management discipline

**Common types:**
- Individual security limit: e.g., max 5% in any stock
- Sector/industry limit: e.g., max 25% in any sector
- Issuer limit: e.g., max exposure to single counterparty
- Country/region limits for international portfolios

**Setting limits:**
- Based on liquidity, volatility, correlation
- Aligned with investment policy
- May vary by asset class

**Monitoring:**
- Regular compliance checks
- Breach escalation procedures
- Forced selling if exceeded

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Concentration increases risk; limits control it
- Option C: Limits set maximums, not equal weights
