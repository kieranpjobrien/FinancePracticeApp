# Question

A U.S. defined benefit pension plan manager is considering investing plan assets in the stock of the sponsoring company. This investment would most likely be:

## Options

- **A**: Permitted without restriction as the manager has discretion
- **B**: Limited to 10% of plan assets under ERISA regulations
- **C**: Prohibited entirely under the prudent investor rule

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act), defined benefit pension plans face specific legal constraints on investing in employer securities:

- Employer stock and real property: Limited to 10% of plan assets combined
- Purpose: Protect employees from concentration risk in their employer
- Rationale: If the company fails, employees could lose both jobs AND retirement savings

This represents a legal/regulatory constraint in the IPS framework.

Key ERISA requirements:
- Fiduciary duties (loyalty and prudence)
- Diversification requirement
- Prohibited transactions rules
- Employer securities limits

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: ERISA specifically restricts employer securities; managers don't have unlimited discretion
- Option C: Employer securities aren't entirely prohibited; they're limited to 10%
