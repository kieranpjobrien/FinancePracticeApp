# Question

An investor who frequently checks and trades her portfolio believing that active involvement improves returns is likely exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Illusion of control
- **B**: Loss aversion
- **C**: Regret aversion

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Illusion of control:

**Definition:**
- Believing one can influence random or uncontrollable outcomes
- Overestimating effect of personal actions on results
- Related to overconfidence

**Manifestations in investing:**
- Frequent trading believing activity adds value
- Excessive portfolio monitoring
- Believing personal analysis beats market
- "Lucky" rituals or behaviors

**Evidence against:**
- Studies show frequent traders underperform
- Transaction costs erode returns
- Much market movement is random/unforeseeable

**Mitigation:**
- Recognize limits of influence
- Focus on controllable factors (costs, diversification)
- Reduce monitoring frequency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Loss aversion): Fear of realizing losses
- Option C (Regret aversion): Avoiding decisions that might cause regret
