# Question

An investor who consistently spends more than planned and struggles to save for retirement is most likely exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Confirmation bias
- **B**: Self-control bias
- **C**: Illusion of control

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Self-control bias:

**Definition:**
- Difficulty in deferring current consumption for future benefit
- Conflict between short-term desires and long-term goals
- Present bias: Overweighting immediate gratification

**Manifestations:**
- Insufficient retirement savings
- Excessive spending
- Failure to follow through on financial plans
- Procrastination on important financial decisions

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Automatic enrollment and escalation in retirement plans
- Payroll deductions before money is accessible
- Mental accounting can help (designated "savings" accounts)
- Financial commitment devices

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Confirmation): Seeking confirming information, not related to self-control
- Option C (Illusion of control): Believing one can influence random outcomes
