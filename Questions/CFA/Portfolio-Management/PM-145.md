# Question

An investor who predicts continued bull markets primarily because markets have risen recently is exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Recency bias
- **B**: Confirmation bias
- **C**: Anchoring bias

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Recency bias:

**Definition:**
- Overweighting recent events in forming expectations
- Extrapolating recent trends into the future
- Subset of availability bias

**Manifestations:**
- Expecting bull market to continue after run-up
- Expecting bear market to continue after decline
- Chasing recent performance

**Examples:**
- "Markets have been up 5 years, they'll keep rising"
- Buying hot funds based on recent performance
- Avoiding asset classes after recent losses

**Contrast with mean reversion:**
- Rational analysis may suggest reversion
- Recency bias leads to trend extrapolation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Confirmation): Seeking confirming information
- Option C (Anchoring): Fixating on reference points
