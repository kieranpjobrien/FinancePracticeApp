# Question

Classifying investors by behavioral investor type (BIT) helps advisors:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee investment success
- **B**: Tailor communication and recommendations to client tendencies
- **C**: Eliminate all client biases completely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Behavioral investor types:

**Pompian's classification:**
1. **Passive Preserver:** Risk-averse, emotional
2. **Friendly Follower:** Passive, follows advice
3. **Independent Individualist:** Active, cognitive
4. **Active Accumulator:** Active, emotional

**Uses:**
- Predict likely biases
- Tailor communication style
- Anticipate reactions to markets
- Customize advice delivery

**Benefits:**
- Better client relationships
- More effective communication
- Improved compliance with recommendations
- Reduced behavioral mistakes

**Limitations:**
- Categories are generalizations
- Individuals may not fit neatly
- Behavior can change over time

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Classification doesn't guarantee success
- Option C: Biases cannot be completely eliminated
