# Question

An IPS should most likely be reviewed and potentially revised when:

## Options

- **A**: Daily market fluctuations cause portfolio value to change
- **B**: The investor experiences a significant life event such as retirement
- **C**: Quarterly performance reports show slight underperformance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IPS revision triggers:

**When to review/revise IPS:**
- Major life events (marriage, divorce, birth, death)
- Significant wealth changes (inheritance, lottery, job loss)
- Changed investment objectives or risk tolerance
- Retirement or career change
- Health changes affecting time horizon
- Changes in tax laws or regulations
- Fundamental shift in capital market expectations

**When NOT to revise IPS:**
- Normal market fluctuations
- Short-term underperformance
- Minor portfolio drift
- Routine rebalancing needs

The IPS is a long-term policy document; frequent revision defeats its purpose.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Daily fluctuations are normal; IPS addresses long-term policy
- Option C: Slight underperformance requires performance review, not IPS revision
