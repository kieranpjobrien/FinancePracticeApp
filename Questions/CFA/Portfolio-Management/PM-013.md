# Question

The first step in the portfolio management process is to:

## Options

- **A**: Construct the portfolio by selecting specific securities
- **B**: Develop an investment policy statement
- **C**: Analyze current market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The portfolio management process follows a logical sequence:

1. **Planning** - Develop the Investment Policy Statement (IPS)
   - Understand client objectives (return, risk)
   - Identify constraints (liquidity, time horizon, taxes, legal, unique)
   - Create the IPS document

2. **Execution** - Construct and implement the portfolio
   - Asset allocation
   - Security selection

3. **Feedback** - Monitor and rebalance
   - Performance evaluation
   - Rebalancing as needed

The IPS must come first because it guides all subsequent investment decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Security selection comes after the IPS establishes the framework and asset allocation
- Option C: Market analysis informs decisions but doesn't precede understanding client needs
