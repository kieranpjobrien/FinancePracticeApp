# Question

Myopic loss aversion theory suggests that the equity risk premium can be explained by:

## Options

- **A**: Investors' preference for stocks over bonds
- **B**: Investors evaluating portfolios frequently, experiencing more interim losses
- **C**: Investors' ability to accurately forecast returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Myopic loss aversion:

**Theory (Benartzi & Thaler):**
- Combines loss aversion + frequent evaluation
- More frequent evaluation = more observed losses
- Losses feel worse than equivalent gains

**How it explains equity premium:**
- Stocks volatile in short term
- Frequent evaluation shows many small losses
- Loss-averse investors demand premium
- Would accept lower return if evaluated less often

**Experimental evidence:**
- Investors shown annual returns → more stocks
- Investors shown monthly returns → fewer stocks
- Same investments, different framing

**Implications:**
- Long-term investors should evaluate less
- Reduces emotional impact of volatility
- May improve asset allocation decisions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Investors actually show reluctance toward stocks
- Option C: Related to overconfidence, not myopic loss aversion
