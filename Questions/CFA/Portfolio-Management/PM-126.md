# Question

An investor's IPS specifies a 60/40 stock/bond allocation, but the manager currently holds 70/30 due to a bullish equity outlook. The 60/40 represents the:

## Options

- **A**: Tactical asset allocation
- **B**: Strategic asset allocation
- **C**: Security selection decision

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strategic vs. Tactical allocation:

**Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA):**
- Long-term policy allocation
- Based on investor's objectives and constraints
- "Neutral" or "normal" portfolio
- In this case: 60% stocks, 40% bonds

**Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA):**
- Short-term deviation from strategic weights
- Based on manager's market views
- In this case: 70% stocks (10% overweight)
- Active decision to exploit perceived opportunity

**Key distinction:**
- SAA = what investor should hold long-term
- TAA = temporary deviation based on views
- TAA should revert to SAA when views normalize

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: 70/30 is the tactical position, not the IPS target
- Option C: This is about asset class weights, not individual securities
