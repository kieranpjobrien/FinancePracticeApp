# Question

Information overload can lead investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Make better decisions with more data
- **B**: Make poorer decisions or avoid decisions altogether
- **C**: Always increase portfolio diversification

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information overload:

**Effects:**
- Decision paralysis
- Poorer decision quality
- Reliance on shortcuts (heuristics)
- Stress and confusion

**Causes:**
- Abundant financial data
- 24/7 news cycle
- Multiple information sources
- Complex products

**Consequences:**
- Analysis paralysis (no action)
- Overtrading (reacting to noise)
- Focus on recent/salient information
- Ignoring important factors

**Mitigation:**
- Filter information sources
- Focus on key metrics
- Systematic decision process
- Limit portfolio checking frequency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: More information can worsen decisions
- Option C: Overload affects behavior, not directly allocation
