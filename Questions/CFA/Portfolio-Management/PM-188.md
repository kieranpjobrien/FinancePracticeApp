# Question

On the Capital Market Line, portfolios above the tangency point represent:

## Options

- **A**: Lending portfolios with part invested in risk-free asset
- **B**: Borrowing portfolios with leverage applied to the market portfolio
- **C**: Portfolios composed only of bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CML and leverage:

**Below tangency point (lending):**
- Invest part in risk-free asset
- Part in market portfolio
- Lower risk than market

**At tangency point:**
- 100% in market portfolio
- No borrowing or lending

**Above tangency point (borrowing):**
- Borrow at risk-free rate
- Invest more than 100% in market portfolio
- Leverage amplifies both risk and expected return

**Assumptions:**
- Can borrow at risk-free rate
- Unlimited borrowing available
- No margin constraints

**Reality:**
- Borrowing rate > lending rate
- Borrowing has limits
- CML kinks at tangency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes points below tangency
- Option C: CML involves market portfolio, not bonds specifically
