# Question

An investor with risk aversion coefficient of 4 evaluates a portfolio with expected return of 12% and standard deviation of 20%. The utility is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.04
- **B**: 0.08
- **C**: 0.12

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Utility function calculation:

**Formula:**
U = E(r) - ½ × A × σ²

**Given:**
- E(r) = 12% = 0.12
- σ = 20% = 0.20
- A = 4

**Calculation:**
U = 0.12 - ½ × 4 × (0.20)²
U = 0.12 - 2 × 0.04
U = 0.12 - 0.08
U = 0.04

**Interpretation:**
- Risk penalty = 0.08
- Utility = 0.04 (4%)
- Higher A = more risk penalty
- Lower utility for same risk

**Risk aversion levels:**
- A = 0: Risk neutral
- A > 0: Risk averse
- Higher A: More conservative

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Calculated risk penalty only
- Option C: Used expected return without penalty
