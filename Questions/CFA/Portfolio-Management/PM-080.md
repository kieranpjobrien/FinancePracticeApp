# Question

In the CAPM framework, the only factor that explains differences in expected returns across securities is:

## Options

- **A**: Total risk as measured by standard deviation
- **B**: Systematic risk as measured by beta
- **C**: Unsystematic risk specific to each company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CAPM as a single-factor model:

**CAPM equation:**
E(Ri) = Rf + βi[E(Rm) - Rf]

**Key implications:**
- Only systematic risk (beta) is priced
- Higher beta = higher expected return
- No compensation for unsystematic risk (it's diversifiable)

**Why only beta matters:**
- Unsystematic risk can be eliminated through diversification
- Rational investors won't pay for risk they can avoid
- Market only compensates for non-diversifiable risk

**Security Market Line (SML):**
- Plots expected return vs. beta
- All fairly priced securities lie on the SML

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Total risk includes diversifiable risk, which isn't compensated
- Option C: Firm-specific risk can be diversified away
