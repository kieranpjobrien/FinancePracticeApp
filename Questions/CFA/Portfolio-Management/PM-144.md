# Question

Liability-relative asset allocation differs from asset-only optimization by:

## Options

- **A**: Ignoring the investor's liabilities in the optimization
- **B**: Explicitly considering the present value and characteristics of future liabilities
- **C**: Focusing only on maximizing absolute returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liability-relative vs. asset-only:

**Asset-only approach:**
- Maximizes risk-adjusted return on assets
- Ignores liability characteristics
- Traditional mean-variance optimization

**Liability-relative approach:**
- Considers assets relative to liabilities
- Objective: Fund liabilities or minimize surplus volatility
- Common for pension funds, insurance companies

**Key concepts:**
- Surplus = Assets - Liabilities
- Liability duration matching
- Hedging liability interest rate sensitivity
- Surplus optimization

**When to use liability-relative:**
- Defined benefit pension plans
- Insurance companies
- Any investor with specific future obligations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes asset-only, opposite of liability-relative
- Option C: Liability-relative focuses on surplus/funding status, not just returns
