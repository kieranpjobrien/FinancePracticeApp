# Question

Tail risk refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The most likely investment outcomes
- **B**: Extreme events in the tails of a return distribution
- **C**: Average returns over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tail risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk of extreme outcomes
- Events in distribution tails
- Low probability, high impact
- "Black swan" events

**Characteristics:**
- Beyond normal expectations
- Large potential losses
- Often underestimated
- Fat tails in reality

**Normal distribution issue:**
- Assumes thin tails
- Underestimates extremes
- Real markets have fat tails
- Kurtosis > 3 (leptokurtic)

**Examples:**
- 2008 financial crisis
- 1987 crash
- Currency crises
- Pandemic market impact

**Management:**
- Stress testing
- Scenario analysis
- Tail risk hedging
- Conservative assumptions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Tail risk is about unlikely extremes
- Option C: Not about average outcomes
