# Question

A pension fund manager is considering investing 15% of the fund in venture capital. The expected return is high but so is the risk. Under the prudent investor rule, this investment would most likely be considered:

## Options

- **A**: Imprudent because venture capital is inherently too risky
- **B**: Prudent if appropriate within the context of the total portfolio
- **C**: Imprudent because pension funds must avoid illiquid investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The modern prudent investor rule evaluates investments in the context of the overall portfolio, not in isolation:

Key principles:
- No investment is inherently imprudent
- Risk is evaluated at the portfolio level
- Diversification is a duty
- Delegation of investment decisions is permitted

For the venture capital allocation:
- 15% is a reasonable allocation if suitable for the fund's objectives
- Must consider the total portfolio's risk/return profile
- Illiquidity is acceptable if matched with long time horizon
- Higher expected returns may justify higher risk within limits

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: The prudent investor rule rejects the "legal list" approach that banned entire asset classes
- Option C: While liquidity is important, pension funds typically have long time horizons that can accommodate some illiquidity
