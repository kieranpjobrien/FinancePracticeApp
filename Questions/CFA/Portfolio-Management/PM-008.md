# Question

A stock with a beta of 1.0 is expected to:

## Options

- **A**: Have no systematic risk
- **B**: Move in line with the overall market
- **C**: Outperform the market in all conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Beta measures a stock's sensitivity to market movements (systematic risk). A beta of 1.0 means:

- The stock is expected to move in the same direction and magnitude as the market
- If the market rises 10%, the stock is expected to rise approximately 10%
- If the market falls 5%, the stock is expected to fall approximately 5%

Beta = 1.0 indicates average systematic risk, equal to that of the market portfolio.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: A beta of 1.0 indicates market-level systematic risk, not zero risk. A beta of 0 would indicate no systematic risk
- Option C: Beta indicates direction and magnitude of movement relative to the market, not guaranteed outperformance
