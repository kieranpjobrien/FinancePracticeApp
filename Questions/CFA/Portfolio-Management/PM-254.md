# Question

Ambiguity aversion causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Embrace investments with unknown probability distributions
- **B**: Prefer known risks over unknown uncertainties
- **C**: Take more risk when outcomes are unclear

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ambiguity aversion (Ellsberg paradox):

**Definition:**
- Preference for known risks over unknown risks
- Discomfort with unmeasurable uncertainty
- "Known unknowns" vs. "unknown unknowns"

**Investment implications:**
- Avoid unfamiliar asset classes
- Prefer domestic over foreign (familiarity)
- Underweight emerging markets
- Prefer established companies

**Examples:**
- Preferring lottery with known odds
- Avoiding new investment types
- Home bias partially explained

**Distinction from risk aversion:**
- Risk aversion: Known probability, prefer certainty
- Ambiguity aversion: Unknown probability, prefer known

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ambiguity aversion avoids unknown distributions
- Option C: Uncertainty leads to avoidance, not more risk
