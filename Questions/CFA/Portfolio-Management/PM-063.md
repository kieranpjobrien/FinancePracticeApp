# Question

An investor purchased a stock at $100 and refuses to sell it at $80, saying "I'll sell when it gets back to what I paid." This is an example of:

## Options

- **A**: Availability bias
- **B**: Anchoring bias
- **C**: Hindsight bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Anchoring bias:

**Definition:**
- Fixating on a reference point when making decisions
- Insufficiently adjusting from the anchor despite new information
- The anchor may be irrelevant to the decision

**In this example:**
- The $100 purchase price is the anchor
- Current fundamental value is irrelevant to the investor
- Investment decision should be forward-looking, not based on historical cost

**Investment consequences:**
- Holding losing positions too long
- Missing better opportunities
- Ignoring current valuation signals

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Availability): Overweighting easily recalled information
- Option C (Hindsight): Believing past events were predictable
