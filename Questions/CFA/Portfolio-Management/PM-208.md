# Question

A market-neutral portfolio has:

## Options

- **A**: A beta close to zero
- **B**: Maximum exposure to market movements
- **C**: Only long positions

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Market-neutral strategy:

**Definition:**
- Portfolio designed to have zero (or near-zero) beta
- Returns independent of market direction
- Pure alpha strategy

**Implementation:**
- Long undervalued securities
- Short overvalued securities
- Balance to neutralize market exposure

**Characteristics:**
- Beta ≈ 0
- Returns from security selection only
- No systematic market risk
- May use leverage

**Return sources:**
- Long/short spread
- Dividends (net)
- Interest on short proceeds (rebate)

**Uses:**
- Hedge funds
- Portable alpha strategies
- Low-correlation investments

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Maximum exposure is opposite of neutral
- Option C: Market-neutral typically requires shorts
