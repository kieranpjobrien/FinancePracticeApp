# Question

Monte Carlo simulation in retirement planning is used primarily to:

## Options

- **A**: Predict exact future portfolio values
- **B**: Model probability distributions of outcomes under uncertainty
- **C**: Eliminate all investment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monte Carlo simulation:

**Purpose:**
- Model uncertainty
- Generate probability distributions
- Assess range of outcomes
- Stress test plans

**How it works:**
- Random sampling from distributions
- Thousands of scenarios run
- Probability of success calculated
- Sensitivity analysis possible

**Applications:**
- Retirement income planning
- Spending rate analysis
- Goal feasibility assessment
- Risk tolerance evaluation

**Outputs:**
- Success probability
- Wealth distribution
- Shortfall risk
- Confidence intervals

**Limitations:**
- Assumptions matter greatly
- Not prediction of future
- Garbage in, garbage out

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Provides probabilities, not predictions
- Option C: Cannot eliminate risk, only model it
