# Question

The risk that a borrower will fail to make promised payments is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Market risk
- **B**: Liquidity risk
- **C**: Credit risk

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Credit risk (also called default risk) is the risk that a borrower will fail to fulfill their contractual obligations:

- Missing interest or principal payments
- Defaulting on debt obligations
- Bankruptcy of the counterparty

Types of credit risk:
- Default risk: Complete failure to pay
- Downgrade risk: Credit rating deterioration
- Credit spread risk: Widening of credit spreads

Credit risk affects:
- Bonds and fixed-income securities
- Loans and receivables
- Counterparty obligations in derivatives

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Market risk): Risk of losses due to market price movements (interest rates, equity prices, FX rates)
- Option B (Liquidity risk): Risk of being unable to sell an asset quickly without significant price concession
