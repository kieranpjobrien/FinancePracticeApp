# Question

When estimating beta from historical data, using a longer time period:

## Options

- **A**: Always produces more accurate beta estimates
- **B**: May capture outdated information if the company has changed
- **C**: Has no effect on the beta estimate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Beta estimation considerations:

**Longer estimation period:**
- More data points (statistical precision)
- But company may have changed
- May include irrelevant past

**Shorter estimation period:**
- More recent/relevant
- But less statistical precision
- More noise

**Trade-off:**
- Stability vs. relevance
- Typical: 2-5 years, monthly data

**Other factors:**
- Data frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Market proxy choice
- Adjustment methods

**Common practice:**
- Bloomberg: 2 years weekly
- Adjust raw beta toward 1.0

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: More data isn't always better if company changed
- Option C: Estimation period significantly affects estimate
