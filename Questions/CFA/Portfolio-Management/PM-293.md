# Question

Illusion of control bias leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Believe they can influence outcomes that are actually random
- **B**: Avoid making any investment decisions
- **C**: Rely too heavily on professional advice

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Illusion of control:

**Definition:**
- Cognitive bias
- Belief in ability to control uncontrollable events
- Overestimate influence on outcomes
- Related to overconfidence

**Manifestations:**
- Stock picking confidence
- Market timing attempts
- Believing research guarantees success
- Trading more than warranted

**Examples:**
- "I researched thoroughly, so it will work"
- Active trading despite evidence
- Ignoring market randomness
- Rejecting indexing

**Investment impact:**
- Excessive trading
- Underdiversification
- Overconfidence in predictions
- Higher costs and lower returns

**Mitigation:**
- Recognize randomness
- Track prediction accuracy
- Use systematic approaches

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Leads to more action, not inaction
- Option C: Often leads to rejecting advice
