# Question

Stock A has a standard deviation of 30% and Stock B has a standard deviation of 20%. If the correlation between them is -1.0, what weight in Stock A would create a zero-variance portfolio?

## Options

- **A**: 30%
- **B**: 40%
- **C**: 60%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When correlation = -1.0, a zero-variance portfolio is possible. The weight in Asset A is:

wA = σB / (σA + σB)

Where:
- σA = 30% = 0.30
- σB = 20% = 0.20

wA = 0.20 / (0.30 + 0.20)
wA = 0.20 / 0.50
wA = 0.40 = 40%

With 40% in Stock A and 60% in Stock B, the portfolio has zero variance when correlation is perfectly negative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May confuse the formula or use σA in numerator
- Option C: Uses the weight for Stock B, not Stock A
