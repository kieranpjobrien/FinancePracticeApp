# Question

Which phase of the portfolio management process involves selecting specific securities?

## Options

- **A**: Planning
- **B**: Execution
- **C**: Feedback

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The three phases of portfolio management:

**1. Planning:**
- Analyze investor objectives and constraints
- Develop Investment Policy Statement (IPS)
- Form capital market expectations
- Determine strategic asset allocation

**2. Execution:**
- Asset allocation implementation
- **Security selection**
- Portfolio construction
- Trade execution

**3. Feedback:**
- Monitor investor circumstances
- Rebalance portfolio
- Evaluate performance
- Revise IPS as needed

Security selection is the choice of specific investments within each asset class during execution.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Planning establishes the framework; execution implements it
- Option C: Feedback involves monitoring and adjusting, not initial selection
