# Question

In a risk parity portfolio with two assets, if Asset A has volatility of 10% and Asset B has volatility of 20%, and ignoring correlations, the weight in Asset A should be approximately:

## Options

- **A**: 33%
- **B**: 50%
- **C**: 67%

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Risk parity weights (simplified):

**Concept:**
Equal risk contribution from each asset.

**For two uncorrelated assets:**
Weight inversely proportional to volatility:
wA = σB / (σA + σB)
wB = σA / (σA + σB)

**Calculation:**
wA = 20% / (10% + 20%) = 20/30 = 67%
wB = 10% / (10% + 20%) = 10/30 = 33%

**Verification:**
- Risk from A: 67% × 10% = 6.7%
- Risk from B: 33% × 20% = 6.6%
- Approximately equal risk contributions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is weight for higher-volatility asset
- Option B: Equal weight doesn't give equal risk
