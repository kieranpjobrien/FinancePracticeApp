# Question

Compared to mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs) typically offer:

## Options

- **A**: Higher expense ratios
- **B**: Intraday trading and greater tax efficiency
- **C**: Active management only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ETF vs. Mutual Fund:

**ETF advantages:**
- Intraday trading (priced continuously)
- Generally lower expense ratios
- Tax efficiency (in-kind redemptions)
- Transparency (holdings disclosed daily)
- No minimum investment

**Mutual fund features:**
- End-of-day pricing (NAV)
- Automatic dividend reinvestment
- May offer active strategies
- No bid-ask spread

**ETF tax efficiency:**
- Authorized participants redeem in-kind
- No capital gains from redemptions
- Shareholders don't bear others' tax burden

**Considerations:**
- ETFs have bid-ask spreads
- Commission costs (though often zero now)
- Premium/discount to NAV possible

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: ETFs typically have lower expenses
- Option C: Many ETFs are passive; active ETFs exist too
