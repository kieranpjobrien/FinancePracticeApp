# Question

After a market crash, an investor says "I knew the market was going to collapse; it was so obvious." This statement most likely reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Representativeness bias
- **B**: Hindsight bias
- **C**: Regret aversion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hindsight bias (the "I knew it all along" phenomenon):

**Characteristics:**
- Believing past events were more predictable than they actually were
- Selective memory of correct predictions
- Forgetting incorrect predictions and uncertainty
- Overconfidence in ability to predict future events

**Consequences:**
- Excessive confidence in forecasting ability
- Unfair evaluation of investment managers
- Failure to learn from mistakes
- Taking on excessive risk based on false pattern recognition

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Representativeness): Judging probability based on stereotypes
- Option C (Regret aversion): Avoiding decisions that might later cause regret
