# Question

Total risk can be decomposed into systematic and unsystematic risk. As more securities are added to a portfolio:

## Options

- **A**: Both types of risk decrease
- **B**: Only systematic risk decreases
- **C**: Only unsystematic risk decreases

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Risk decomposition:

**Total risk:**
Total Risk = Systematic + Unsystematic

**Systematic risk:**
- Market risk, non-diversifiable
- Affects all securities
- Cannot be eliminated
- Compensated by market

**Unsystematic risk:**
- Firm-specific, diversifiable
- Unique to individual securities
- CAN be eliminated
- Not compensated (no premium)

**Diversification effect:**
- Adding securities reduces unsystematic risk
- Approaches zero with ~30+ stocks
- Systematic risk remains
- Cannot diversify below market risk

**Practical implication:**
- Diversify to eliminate unsystematic
- Only systematic risk matters for pricing
- Beta measures systematic exposure

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Systematic risk cannot be diversified away
- Option B: Diversification reduces unsystematic, not systematic
