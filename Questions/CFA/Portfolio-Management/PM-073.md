# Question

Conditional VaR (CVaR), also known as Expected Shortfall, represents:

## Options

- **A**: The probability that losses will exceed VaR
- **B**: The expected loss given that losses exceed the VaR threshold
- **C**: The maximum loss under any market condition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conditional VaR (CVaR) / Expected Shortfall:

**Definition:**
- The expected (average) loss when losses exceed VaR
- Answers: "When things go bad, how bad do they get?"
- Also called Expected Tail Loss (ETL)

**Example:**
- 95% VaR = $1 million
- 95% CVaR = $1.5 million
- Interpretation: When losses exceed $1M (5% of time), average loss is $1.5M

**Advantages over VaR:**
- Captures severity of tail losses
- Coherent risk measure (satisfies subadditivity)
- Better for portfolios with significant tail risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Probability of exceeding VaR is the confidence level (e.g., 5%)
- Option C: No measure can predict maximum possible loss
