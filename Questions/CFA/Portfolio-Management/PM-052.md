# Question

The portfolio perspective in investment management emphasizes that:

## Options

- **A**: Each security should be evaluated independently for risk and return
- **B**: Risk and return should be evaluated at the portfolio level rather than security level
- **C**: Higher-risk securities should always be excluded from portfolios

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The portfolio perspective (Markowitz approach):

Key insights:
- Individual security risk is less important than contribution to portfolio risk
- Diversification can reduce risk without proportionally reducing return
- An asset's correlation with other holdings matters more than its standalone risk
- A "risky" asset may reduce overall portfolio risk if it has low correlation

Implications:
- Evaluate investments based on portfolio impact
- Consider covariance/correlation, not just variance
- An individually risky asset may be appropriate in a diversified portfolio

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Pre-Markowitz view; ignores diversification benefits
- Option C: High-risk securities may improve portfolio efficiency if correlations are favorable
