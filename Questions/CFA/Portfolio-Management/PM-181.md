# Question

The sunk cost fallacy occurs when an investor:

## Options

- **A**: Ignores past costs when making future investment decisions
- **B**: Continues investing because of money already spent, regardless of future prospects
- **C**: Properly writes off losses as non-recoverable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sunk cost fallacy:

**Definition:**
- Letting irrecoverable past costs influence future decisions
- "I've already invested so much, I can't stop now"
- Rational decisions should be based on future prospects only

**Examples:**
- Holding losing stock because of amount already invested
- Continuing failed project due to prior investment
- Throwing good money after bad

**Why it occurs:**
- Loss aversion: Don't want to "waste" prior investment
- Regret avoidance: Quitting feels like admitting mistake
- Mental accounting: Tracking past investments

**Proper approach:**
- Consider only future costs and benefits
- Ignore sunk costs in decisions
- Ask: "Would I invest now if starting fresh?"

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is correct behavior, not the fallacy
- Option C: This is rational, not the fallacy
