# Question

An investor with a longer investment time horizon can typically:

## Options

- **A**: Only invest in risk-free securities
- **B**: Accept more short-term volatility for potentially higher returns
- **C**: Ignore asset allocation decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Time horizon and risk:

**Longer horizon advantages:**
- More time to recover from downturns
- Can ride out short-term volatility
- Benefit from equity risk premium
- Less need for near-term liquidity

**Risk capacity implications:**
- Higher allocation to growth assets
- Less concern about short-term fluctuations
- Can invest in less liquid assets

**Historical evidence:**
- Equity outperformance more reliable over long periods
- Short-term stock returns highly variable
- Bonds provide stability but lower long-term returns

**Cautions:**
- Time diversification debate
- No guarantee of recovery
- Sequence risk still matters (distributions)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Long horizon allows, not requires, risk-taking
- Option C: Asset allocation remains crucial regardless of horizon
