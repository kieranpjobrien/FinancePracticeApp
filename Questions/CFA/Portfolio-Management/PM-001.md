# Question

An investor holds a portfolio with 60% in Stock A (standard deviation 20%) and 40% in Stock B (standard deviation 30%). If the correlation between the stocks is 0.5, the portfolio standard deviation is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 19.7%
- **B**: 24.0%
- **C**: 25.0%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Portfolio variance formula:
σ²p = w²A × σ²A + w²B × σ²B + 2 × wA × wB × σA × σB × ρAB

Where:
- wA = 0.60, wB = 0.40
- σA = 0.20, σB = 0.30
- ρAB = 0.50

σ²p = (0.60)² × (0.20)² + (0.40)² × (0.30)² + 2 × 0.60 × 0.40 × 0.20 × 0.30 × 0.50
σ²p = 0.36 × 0.04 + 0.16 × 0.09 + 2 × 0.60 × 0.40 × 0.20 × 0.30 × 0.50
σ²p = 0.0144 + 0.0144 + 0.0144
σ²p = 0.0432

σp = √0.0432 = 0.197 = 19.7%

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B calculates the weighted average of standard deviations (ignores diversification benefit)
- Option C uses incorrect correlation or weighting
