# Question

The risk-free rate is typically represented by:

## Options

- **A**: The average return on the stock market
- **B**: Short-term government securities like Treasury bills
- **C**: High-yield corporate bond yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk-free rate:

**Definition:**
- Return on an investment with zero default risk
- Guaranteed return with certainty

**Proxies used:**
- U.S. Treasury bills (3-month)
- Government bonds of stable countries
- Short-term to avoid interest rate risk

**Characteristics:**
- No default risk (government backing)
- No reinvestment risk (short maturity)
- Highly liquid

**Uses:**
- CAPM calculations
- Sharpe ratio denominator
- Risk premium calculations
- Discounting cash flows

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Stock market has significant risk
- Option C: Corporate bonds have credit risk
