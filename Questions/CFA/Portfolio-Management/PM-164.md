# Question

An investor who evaluates each investment independently rather than considering total portfolio impact is exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Broad framing
- **B**: Narrow framing
- **C**: Temporal framing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Narrow vs. broad framing:

**Narrow framing:**
- Evaluating decisions in isolation
- Ignoring relationship to other holdings
- Each bet considered separately

**Consequences:**
- Excessive loss aversion on individual positions
- Missing diversification benefits
- Suboptimal portfolio construction
- Rejecting risky bets that would benefit portfolio

**Broad framing:**
- Evaluating decisions in portfolio context
- Considering correlations and total impact
- Modern portfolio theory approach

**Example:**
- Reject stock A in isolation (too volatile)
- But A reduces portfolio risk (low correlation)
- Narrow framing leads to wrong decision

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Broad framing is the opposite (considers total portfolio)
- Option C: Temporal relates to time horizon framing
