# Question

An investor believes that after five consecutive down days, the market is "due" for an up day. This reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Gambler's fallacy
- **B**: Confirmation bias
- **C**: Loss aversion

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Gambler's fallacy:

**Definition:**
- Incorrectly believing that past independent events affect future probabilities
- Expecting a "correction" or "reversion" in random sequences
- Misunderstanding of the law of large numbers

**Example:**
- Coin flip: After 5 heads, expecting tails is "due"
- Reality: Each flip is independent, still 50/50
- Market: Past returns don't make future returns more likely

**Related concept - Hot hand fallacy:**
- Opposite error: Believing streaks will continue
- Both involve misunderstanding of probability

**Investment implications:**
- May exit or enter positions based on false pattern recognition
- Ignores fundamental analysis

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Confirmation): Seeking confirming evidence
- Option C (Loss aversion): Fear of losses exceeding desire for gains
