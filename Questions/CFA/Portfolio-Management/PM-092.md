# Question

A U.S. investor holding European stocks faces currency risk because:

## Options

- **A**: European interest rates may change
- **B**: The euro/dollar exchange rate may change
- **C**: European stocks may underperform U.S. stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Currency risk (exchange rate risk):

**Definition:**
- Risk that exchange rate movements will affect investment returns
- Even if foreign asset performs well, currency depreciation can reduce returns

**Example:**
- U.S. investor buys European stock at €100 when EUR/USD = 1.10
- Stock rises to €110 (+10%)
- But if EUR/USD falls to 1.00, dollar return is less than 10%
- Extreme case: Currency loss can exceed asset gain

**Hedging options:**
- Forward contracts
- Currency futures
- Currency options
- Currency ETFs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Interest rate changes are interest rate risk, not currency risk
- Option C: This is equity market risk, not currency risk
