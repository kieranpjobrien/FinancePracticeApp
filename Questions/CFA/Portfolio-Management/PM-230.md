# Question

A momentum investment strategy involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying recent losers and selling recent winners
- **B**: Buying recent winners and selling recent losers
- **C**: Maintaining equal weights in all securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Momentum investing:

**Strategy:**
- Buy recent outperformers
- Sell recent underperformers
- Trend-following approach

**Evidence:**
- Documented anomaly (Jegadeesh & Titman)
- Works over 3-12 month horizons
- Persists across markets globally

**Potential explanations:**
- Behavioral: Underreaction to information
- Gradual information diffusion
- Herding behavior
- Risk-based: Momentum as risk factor

**Risks:**
- Occasional severe reversals
- "Momentum crashes"
- Transaction costs from high turnover
- Tax inefficiency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes contrarian/mean reversion strategy
- Option C: Equal weighting is different from momentum
