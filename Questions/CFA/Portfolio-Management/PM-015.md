# Question

Strategic asset allocation differs from tactical asset allocation in that strategic allocation:

## Options

- **A**: Is adjusted frequently based on short-term market views
- **B**: Establishes long-term target weights based on investor objectives
- **C**: Focuses primarily on security selection within asset classes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA):
- Sets long-term target weights for each asset class
- Based on investor's risk tolerance, return objectives, and constraints
- Reflects the investor's "policy portfolio"
- Changes infrequently (major life events, significant objective changes)
- Determined by capital market expectations over full market cycles

Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA):
- Short-term deviations from strategic weights
- Attempts to exploit market inefficiencies or mispricing
- Active management approach
- More frequent adjustments based on market conditions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes tactical, not strategic, asset allocation
- Option C: Security selection is a separate step; asset allocation determines weights across asset classes, not individual securities
