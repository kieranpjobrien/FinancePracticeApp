# Question

A core-satellite investment approach combines:

## Options

- **A**: Only small-cap stocks in the core with large-cap in satellite
- **B**: Passive index investments in the core with active strategies in satellites
- **C**: Domestic assets in core with international assets in satellites only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Core-satellite strategy:

**Core portfolio (typically 60-80%):**
- Passively managed, low-cost index funds
- Provides broad market exposure
- Minimizes fees and tracking error
- Represents strategic asset allocation

**Satellite positions (typically 20-40%):**
- Actively managed strategies
- Alternative investments
- Tactical tilts
- Seeks alpha/outperformance

**Benefits:**
- Lower overall costs than full active management
- Maintains market exposure while seeking alpha
- Allows for tactical adjustments
- Risk budget focused on high-conviction ideas

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Core-satellite is about passive/active, not cap size
- Option C: Geographic allocation is separate from core-satellite structure
