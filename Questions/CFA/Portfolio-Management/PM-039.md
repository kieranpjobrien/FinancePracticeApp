# Question

As the number of securities in a portfolio increases, the portfolio's total risk:

## Options

- **A**: Approaches zero
- **B**: Approaches the level of systematic risk
- **C**: Remains unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

As diversification increases:

Total Risk = Systematic Risk + Unsystematic Risk

- Unsystematic (diversifiable) risk decreases as more securities are added
- Systematic (non-diversifiable) risk remains constant
- Total risk approaches systematic risk as the portfolio becomes fully diversified

Research shows most diversification benefits are achieved with 20-30 stocks, but some unsystematic risk remains unless the portfolio includes all securities in the market.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Total risk cannot reach zero; systematic risk always remains
- Option C: Total risk decreases with diversification as unsystematic risk is eliminated
