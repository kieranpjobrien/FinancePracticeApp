# Question

Goals-based investing differs from traditional asset allocation by:

## Options

- **A**: Ignoring the investor's risk tolerance entirely
- **B**: Linking specific portfolio segments to specific financial goals
- **C**: Focusing exclusively on maximizing returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Goals-based investing:

**Traditional approach:**
- Single portfolio with overall risk/return objective
- Asset allocation based on aggregate risk tolerance
- Mean-variance optimization

**Goals-based approach:**
- Multiple sub-portfolios ("mental accounts")
- Each linked to specific goal (retirement, education, emergency)
- Different time horizons and risk levels per goal
- Priority ranking of goals

**Benefits:**
- Better aligns with how people think about money
- Clearer connection between investments and purposes
- May improve investor behavior (less panic selling)
- Helps prioritize competing goals

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Goals-based still considers risk tolerance for each goal
- Option C: Risk is tailored to each goal; not purely return-focused
