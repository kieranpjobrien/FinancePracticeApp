# Question

Self-control bias leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Favor immediate consumption over long-term saving
- **B**: Overweight recent information
- **C**: Hold losing positions too long

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Self-control bias:

**Definition:**
- Emotional bias
- Failure to delay gratification
- Present consumption preferred over future

**Manifestations:**
- Undersaving for retirement
- Excessive spending
- Insufficient emergency funds
- Short-term focus

**Consequences:**
- Inadequate retirement assets
- Asset allocation too aggressive (need higher returns)
- Taking excess risk

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Automatic savings plans
- Forced contribution rules
- Mental accounting for goals
- Budget discipline

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: This is recency bias
- Option C: This is disposition effect/loss aversion
