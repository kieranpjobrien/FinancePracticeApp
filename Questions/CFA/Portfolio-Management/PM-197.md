# Question

Historical correlations between asset classes tend to:

## Options

- **A**: Remain perfectly stable over time
- **B**: Change over time and increase during market stress
- **C**: Always equal zero in efficient markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Correlation dynamics:

**Time variation:**
- Correlations change over market cycles
- Economic conditions affect relationships
- Globalization has increased correlations

**Crisis behavior:**
- Correlations spike during market stress
- Diversification fails when needed most
- "Flight to quality" changes patterns

**Implications:**
- Don't rely solely on historical correlations
- Stress test with elevated correlations
- Consider regime changes

**Research findings:**
- Stock-bond correlation varies
- International correlations increasing
- Alternative assets may decorrelate

**Risk management:**
- Use conditional correlations
- Scenario analysis
- Tail risk hedging

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Correlations are not stable; they're dynamic
- Option C: Correlations vary; no requirement for zero
