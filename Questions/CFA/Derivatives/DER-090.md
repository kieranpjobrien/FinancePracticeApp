# Question

A basis swap involves exchanging:

## Options

- **A**: A fixed interest rate for a floating rate
- **B**: Two different floating interest rates
- **C**: Principal amounts in different currencies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Basis swap structure:

**Definition:**
- Exchange of two floating rates
- Different reference rates
- Same currency typically

**Examples:**
- SOFR vs. Prime rate
- 3-month SOFR vs. 6-month SOFR
- SOFR vs. Fed Funds

**Why use basis swaps:**
- Assets tied to one rate
- Liabilities tied to another
- Convert between rate bases
- Hedge basis risk

**Spread in basis swaps:**
- Rates may include spread adjustment
- SOFR + X bps vs. Prime
- Spread reflects market conditions

**Valuation:**
- At inception: Value = 0
- Spread set so PV of both legs equal
- Value changes as rates move

**Compared to plain vanilla:**
- Plain vanilla: Fixed vs. floating
- Basis swap: Floating vs. floating

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is plain vanilla swap
- Option C: This is currency swap
