# Question

In a commodity swap, a company that wants to lock in its fuel costs would most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Pay floating and receive fixed commodity prices
- **B**: Pay fixed and receive floating commodity prices
- **C**: Pay and receive floating commodity prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity swap for cost hedging:

**Company situation:**
- Must buy fuel regularly
- Exposed to price increases
- Wants predictable costs

**Swap solution:**
- Pay fixed price in swap
- Receive floating market price

**How it works:**
- Physical market: Buy fuel at spot price
- Swap: Pay fixed, receive spot price
- Net cost = Fixed price

**Cash flows:**
- Pay spot for physical fuel
- Receive spot from swap (offsets)
- Pay fixed in swap (net cost)

**Example:**
- Swap fixed = $80/barrel
- Spot = $90/barrel
- Receive $90, pay $80 in swap (+$10)
- Pay $90 for physical (-$90)
- Net cost = $80

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This increases exposure to price changes
- Option C: Basis swap, doesn't fix price
