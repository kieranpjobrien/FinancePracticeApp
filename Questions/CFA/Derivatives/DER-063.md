# Question

Which is NOT an assumption of the Black-Scholes-Merton option pricing model?

## Options

- **A**: The underlying asset price follows a lognormal distribution
- **B**: Volatility remains constant over the option's life
- **C**: The underlying asset can only move to two prices

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Black-Scholes assumptions:

**Key assumptions:**
- Continuous price movements (not discrete)
- Lognormal distribution of returns
- Constant volatility (σ)
- Constant risk-free rate
- No dividends (basic model)
- European exercise only
- No transaction costs or taxes
- Continuous trading possible

**Binomial vs. Black-Scholes:**
- Binomial: Discrete (two outcomes)
- BSM: Continuous movements
- Binomial converges to BSM as steps → ∞

**Limitations in practice:**
- Volatility changes (volatility smile)
- Jumps in prices occur
- Trading not truly continuous
- Transaction costs exist

**Despite limitations:**
- Foundation of option pricing
- Starting point for modifications
- Widely used in practice

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This IS an assumption
- Option B: This IS an assumption (though unrealistic)
