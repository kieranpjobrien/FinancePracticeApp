# Question

In a currency swap, principal amounts are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Never exchanged, only used for calculation
- **B**: Exchanged at the beginning and re-exchanged at the end
- **C**: Exchanged only at the end of the swap

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Currency swap structure:

**Principal exchanges:**
- Initial exchange: At start of swap
- Final exchange: At maturity
- Same amounts, original exchange rates

**Example:**
- Party A gives $10 million
- Party B gives €9 million
- Throughout: A pays € interest, B pays $ interest
- At maturity: Amounts re-exchanged

**Why exchange principal:**
- Different currencies involved
- Need currency to invest/use
- Creates exposure to exchange rates
- Different from interest rate swaps

**Interest payments:**
- Each party pays interest in currency received
- May be fixed or floating
- Based on notional in that currency

**Risk:**
- Exchange rate exposure
- Credit risk on principal
- Interest rate risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: True for IR swaps, not currency swaps
- Option C: Initial exchange also occurs
