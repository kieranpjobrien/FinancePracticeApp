# Question

Cash settlement of a derivative contract means:

## Options

- **A**: The contract must be paid for in cash upfront
- **B**: Only the net difference is exchanged rather than the underlying asset
- **C**: The contract can only be traded for cash

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash vs. physical settlement:

**Cash settlement:**
- No physical delivery
- Calculate difference between price and spot
- Loser pays winner the difference
- Simpler execution

**Example:**
- Futures price = $100
- Spot at expiration = $105
- Long receives $5 cash
- No actual asset changes hands

**Physical settlement:**
- Actual delivery of underlying
- Buyer pays full price, receives asset
- Common in commodities
- More logistically complex

**When cash settlement preferred:**
- Index products (can't deliver "index")
- Financial derivatives
- When delivery impractical
- Non-deliverable forwards (NDFs)

**Most stock index futures:**
- Cash settled
- Can't deliver basket of stocks
- Practical necessity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Settlement type, not how contract is purchased
- Option C: Refers to settlement, not trading
