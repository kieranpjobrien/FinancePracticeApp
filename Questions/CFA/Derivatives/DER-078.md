# Question

The cheapest-to-deliver (CTD) bond in Treasury futures is:

## Options

- **A**: The bond with the longest maturity
- **B**: The bond that minimizes the cost of fulfilling delivery obligations
- **C**: The bond with the highest coupon rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cheapest-to-deliver concept:

**Multiple deliverable bonds:**
- Treasury futures allow several bonds
- Different coupons and maturities
- Seller chooses which to deliver

**Conversion factors:**
- Adjust for bond differences
- Standardize to hypothetical 6% bond
- Invoice price = Futures × CF + Accrued

**CTD determination:**
- Seller chooses bond minimizing net cost
- CTD = Lowest (Bond Price - Futures × CF)
- Changes with yield curve

**CTD characteristics:**
- Often highest duration when rates high
- Often lowest duration when rates low
- Depends on yield relative to 6%

**Why it matters:**
- Futures track CTD most closely
- Basis reflects CTD relationship
- Hedging considerations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Maturity alone doesn't determine CTD
- Option C: Coupon alone doesn't determine CTD
