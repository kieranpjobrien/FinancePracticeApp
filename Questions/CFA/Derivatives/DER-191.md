# Question

The principle that a derivative's fair price equals the cost of replicating its payoff is based on:

## Options

- **A**: Historical pricing patterns
- **B**: The no-arbitrage condition
- **C**: Supply and demand equilibrium

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Replication and no-arbitrage:

**Core principle:**
- Identical payoffs must have identical prices
- If not, arbitrage exists
- Market forces correct mispricings

**Replication logic:**
1. Construct portfolio matching derivative payoff
2. Cost of portfolio = Fair derivative price
3. Any difference = Arbitrage opportunity

**How it works:**
- If derivative > replication cost: Sell derivative, buy replication
- If derivative < replication cost: Buy derivative, sell replication
- Lock in risk-free profit

**Examples:**
- Forward = Spot + Financing
- Call = Delta × Stock - Borrowing
- Swap = Series of forwards

**Key requirement:**
- Can actually replicate payoff
- Tradeable instruments exist
- Low transaction costs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Based on theory, not history
- Option C: Arbitrage enforcement stronger than supply/demand
