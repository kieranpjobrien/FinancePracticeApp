# Question

The forward exchange rate is determined by:

## Options

- **A**: The difference in inflation rates between two countries
- **B**: The difference in interest rates between two currencies
- **C**: The expected change in central bank policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Currency forward pricing:

**Covered Interest Rate Parity:**
F/S = (1 + r_d) / (1 + r_f)

**Relationship:**
- Higher interest rate currency trades at forward discount
- Lower interest rate currency trades at forward premium
- Prevents risk-free arbitrage

**Example:**
- USD rate: 5%
- EUR rate: 2%
- USD will depreciate in forward market
- Forward USD/EUR < Spot USD/EUR

**Why it works:**
- Borrow low-rate currency
- Convert to high-rate currency
- Invest at high rate
- Hedge with forward
- Arbitrage if parity violated

**Note:**
- Different from uncovered parity (expected spot)
- Covered parity is no-arbitrage condition
- Works for all currency pairs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Inflation affects long-term spot, not forward
- Option C: Policy expectations affect spot, arbitrage sets forward
