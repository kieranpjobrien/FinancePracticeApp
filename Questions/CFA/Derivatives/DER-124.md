# Question

A bull call spread involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying a call with a lower strike and selling a call with a higher strike
- **B**: Buying calls at two different strikes
- **C**: Selling a call with a lower strike and buying a call with a higher strike

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Bull call spread:

**Structure:**
- Buy call at lower strike (K1)
- Sell call at higher strike (K2)
- Same expiration
- K1 < K2

**Characteristics:**
- Net debit (pay premium)
- Bullish position
- Limited profit, limited loss

**Maximum profit:**
- Achieved when stock ≥ K2
- = K2 - K1 - Net premium
- Capped by short call

**Maximum loss:**
- When stock ≤ K1
- = Net premium paid
- Both options expire worthless

**Breakeven:**
= K1 + Net premium

**Why use:**
- Reduce cost of long call
- Accept limited upside
- Defined risk profile

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Must sell one call to create spread
- Option C: This is a bear call spread (credit spread)
