# Question

The mark-to-market value of a receive-fixed swap increases when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates increase
- **B**: Interest rates decrease
- **C**: The credit quality of the counterparty improves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Receive-fixed swap value:

**When rates decrease:**
- Fixed receipts more valuable
- PV of fixed payments increases
- Floating payments expected lower
- Swap value increases

**Bond analogy:**
- Receive-fixed = Long fixed bond + Short FRN
- Falling rates → Bond prices rise
- Fixed bond gains value
- FRN stays near par

**Example:**
- Receiving 5% fixed
- Rates fall from 5% to 4%
- Fixed receipts now above market
- Swap has positive value

**Opposite for pay-fixed:**
- Value increases when rates rise
- Paying below-market fixed rate
- Benefit from rate increase

**Duration:**
- Receive-fixed: Positive duration
- Pay-fixed: Negative duration
- Like bond exposure

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Rising rates hurt receive-fixed
- Option C: Credit quality affects value differently
