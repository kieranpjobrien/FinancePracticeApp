# Question

As the number of steps in a binomial model increases:

## Options

- **A**: The model becomes less accurate
- **B**: The option price converges to the Black-Scholes price
- **C**: The risk-neutral probability approaches 1

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binomial model convergence:

**Key relationship:**
- Binomial is discrete approximation
- Black-Scholes is continuous limit
- More steps → Better approximation

**Mathematical basis:**
- As n → ∞
- Binomial → Continuous
- Price → Black-Scholes price

**Practical implications:**
- 30+ steps usually sufficient
- Oscillation may occur
- Even vs. odd steps

**Parameter adjustment:**
- Time step Δt = T/n
- Up/down factors adjust with n
- u = e^(σ√Δt)

**Advantages of binomial:**
- Can handle American options
- More intuitive
- Flexible for dividends

**When convergence exact:**
- European options
- No dividends
- Constant volatility

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: More steps increase accuracy
- Option C: Risk-neutral probability depends on u, d, r
