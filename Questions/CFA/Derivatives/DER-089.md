# Question

Regulatory arbitrage using derivatives refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Exploiting price differences between derivative markets
- **B**: Using derivatives to circumvent regulations or reduce regulatory burdens
- **C**: Trading derivatives only in unregulated markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulatory arbitrage:

**Definition:**
- Structuring transactions to reduce regulatory impact
- Using derivatives to achieve outcomes regulations restrict
- Exploiting differences across jurisdictions

**Examples:**
- Capital requirements avoidance
- Off-balance sheet financing
- Tax treatment optimization
- Accounting classification benefits

**Common techniques:**
- Credit derivatives to transfer risk
- Total return swaps for exposure
- Synthetic positions vs. cash
- Cross-border structures

**Concerns:**
- Undermines regulatory intent
- Shifts risk to less regulated areas
- Potential systemic implications
- Cat and mouse with regulators

**Legitimate uses:**
- Efficient risk management
- Tax efficiency within law
- Optimal capital allocation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is standard price arbitrage
- Option C: Derivatives trade in regulated markets too
