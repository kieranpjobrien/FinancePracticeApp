# Question

The law of one price states that:

## Options

- **A**: All derivatives must trade at par value
- **B**: Identical assets must sell for the same price in all markets
- **C**: Prices must increase over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Law of one price:

**Principle:**
- Same asset, same price everywhere
- Regardless of market or location
- Foundation of arbitrage pricing

**Enforcement mechanism:**
- Arbitrageurs exploit price differences
- Buy low, sell high simultaneously
- Prices converge through trading

**Example:**
- Stock trades at $50 in New York
- Same stock at $50.10 in London
- Arbitrageur buys NY, sells London
- Profit = $0.10 (minus costs)
- Prices converge

**Derivative application:**
- Derivative must equal replication cost
- If not, arbitrage opportunity exists
- Markets enforce fair pricing

**Practical limitations:**
- Transaction costs
- Liquidity constraints
- Regulatory barriers
- Execution risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not about par value
- Option C: Not about price trends over time
