# Question

In an interest rate swap, the floating rate is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Set at the end of each payment period based on a reference rate
- **B**: Set at the beginning of each period and paid at the end
- **C**: Fixed for the entire life of the swap

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Floating rate mechanics:

**Standard convention:**
- Rate SET at period start
- Payment MADE at period end
- Called "set in advance, paid in arrears"

**Timeline example (quarterly):**
- Day 0: Observe 3-month SOFR
- Day 90: Pay interest based on Day 0 rate
- Day 90: Observe new rate for next period
- Day 180: Pay based on Day 90 rate

**Why this convention:**
- Known payment amount before due
- Reduces uncertainty
- Standard market practice

**Floating rate references:**
- SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate)
- EURIBOR
- Formerly LIBOR

**Reset frequency:**
- Monthly, quarterly, semi-annually
- Matches payment frequency typically

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: "Paid in arrears" means rate set at beginning
- Option C: This describes fixed rate, not floating
