# Question

Compared to a straddle, a long strangle:

## Options

- **A**: Costs more but has higher profit potential
- **B**: Costs less but requires a larger price move to profit
- **C**: Has unlimited risk on both sides

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strangle vs. straddle:

**Straddle:**
- Buy call and put at SAME strike (ATM)
- Higher premium (both ATM)
- Smaller breakeven range
- More expensive

**Strangle:**
- Buy call and put at DIFFERENT strikes
- OTM call (higher strike)
- OTM put (lower strike)
- Lower premium
- Wider breakeven range

**Trade-off:**
- Strangle: Cheaper entry
- Strangle: Needs larger move to profit
- Straddle: Profits from smaller moves
- Straddle: More expensive

**Example:**
- Stock at $50
- Straddle: Buy $50 call and $50 put
- Strangle: Buy $55 call and $45 put

**When to use strangle:**
- Expect very large move
- Want cheaper entry
- Accept wider breakeven

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Strangle costs less, not more
- Option C: Both strategies have limited risk (premium paid)
