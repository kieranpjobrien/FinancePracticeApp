# Question

An interest rate swap is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: An exchange of principal amounts in different currencies
- **B**: An agreement to exchange interest payments based on a notional principal
- **C**: A contract to buy or sell an asset at a future date

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest rate swap structure:

**Basic mechanics:**
- Two parties exchange interest payments
- Based on notional principal (not exchanged)
- One pays fixed rate
- One pays floating rate

**Plain vanilla swap:**
- Fixed-for-floating exchange
- Same currency
- Same notional amount
- Periodic settlements

**Example:**
- Notional: $10 million
- Party A pays: 5% fixed
- Party B pays: SOFR + spread
- Net payment exchanged each period

**Uses:**
- Convert fixed debt to floating
- Convert floating debt to fixed
- Hedge interest rate exposure
- Speculate on rate movements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes currency swap, not interest rate swap
- Option C: Describes a forward or futures contract
