# Question

Convenience yield represents:

## Options

- **A**: The interest earned on funds used to purchase commodities
- **B**: The benefit of physically holding a commodity versus holding a futures contract
- **C**: The storage costs of holding inventory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convenience yield:

**Definition:**
- Non-monetary benefit of holding physical
- Arises from ability to use/sell immediately
- Reduces effective cost of carry

**When convenience yield is high:**
- Supply shortages
- High immediate demand
- Production needs
- Strategic inventory value

**Impact on pricing:**
F = S × e^((r + c - y) × T)

Where:
- c = storage costs
- y = convenience yield
- Net carry = r + c - y

**Backwardation connection:**
- High convenience yield
- May exceed r + c
- Creates backwardation (F < S)

**Examples:**
- Oil during supply crisis
- Agricultural products pre-harvest
- Industrial metals in shortage

**Key insight:**
- Futures holder doesn't get convenience yield
- Only physical holder benefits
- Explains why F can be < S

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is interest/financing cost
- Option C: Storage cost is separate from convenience yield
