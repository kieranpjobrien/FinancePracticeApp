# Question

In the one-period binomial model, the replicating portfolio for a call option consists of:

## Options

- **A**: Lending money and shorting the underlying stock
- **B**: Buying a fractional share of stock and borrowing money
- **C**: Buying the stock and buying a put option

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Replicating portfolio for calls:

**Components:**
- Long Δ shares of stock
- Borrow B dollars (at risk-free rate)

**Determining Δ and B:**
Must match option payoffs in both states

**Up state:**
Δ × Su - B × (1+r) = Cu

**Down state:**
Δ × Sd - B × (1+r) = Cd

**Solving for delta:**
Δ = (Cu - Cd) / (Su - Sd)

**Solving for borrowing:**
B = (Δ × Sd - Cd) / (1+r)

**Call option value:**
C = Δ × S - B

**Interpretation:**
- Long fractional share captures upside
- Borrowing finances the position
- Net cost = Call option value

**For puts:**
- Short stock + Lending money
- Opposite of calls

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This replicates short call or long put
- Option C: This creates different exposure
