# Question

Swap dealers typically earn profits by:

## Options

- **A**: Speculating on interest rate movements
- **B**: Charging a bid-ask spread between paying and receiving fixed rates
- **C**: Guaranteeing that swaps will be profitable for clients

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Swap dealer business model:

**Bid-ask spread:**
- Pay fixed at lower rate (bid)
- Receive fixed at higher rate (ask)
- Difference = Dealer profit

**Example:**
- Bid: Pay 4.95% fixed
- Ask: Receive 5.05% fixed
- Spread: 10 basis points

**How it works:**
- Client A pays 5.05% to dealer
- Dealer pays 4.95% to Client B
- Dealer earns 0.10% on notional

**Risk management:**
- Dealers hedge their positions
- Try to match offsetting trades
- Manage residual risk

**Sources of income:**
- Bid-ask spreads
- Fees on complex structures
- Portfolio management

**Dealer role:**
- Market maker
- Provides liquidity
- Assumes counterparty risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Dealers hedge, not speculate (primarily)
- Option C: No guarantee of profitability for clients
