# Question

The minimum variance hedge ratio is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: The ratio of futures price to spot price
- **B**: The covariance between spot and futures divided by the variance of futures
- **C**: The correlation between spot and futures prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Optimal hedge ratio:

**Formula:**
h* = Cov(ΔS, ΔF) / Var(ΔF)

**Alternatively:**
h* = ρ × (σS / σF)

Where:
- ρ = Correlation between spot and futures
- σS = Standard deviation of spot changes
- σF = Standard deviation of futures changes

**Interpretation:**
- Number of futures per unit of exposure
- Minimizes variance of hedged position
- Regression coefficient

**Example:**
- Cov = 0.0012
- Var(F) = 0.0016
- h* = 0.0012/0.0016 = 0.75
- Hedge 0.75 futures per unit spot

**When h* = 1:**
- Perfect correlation
- Equal volatility
- Spot and futures move identically

**Cross-hedging:**
- Different underlying
- h* likely ≠ 1
- Adjust for basis risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Price ratio isn't hedge ratio
- Option C: Correlation is one component, not full ratio
