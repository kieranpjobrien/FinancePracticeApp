# Question

A calendar spread (time spread) typically profits when:

## Options

- **A**: The underlying price moves dramatically in either direction
- **B**: The underlying price remains relatively stable and volatility increases
- **C**: Interest rates increase significantly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Calendar spread characteristics:

**Structure:**
- Sell near-term option
- Buy longer-term option
- Same strike price
- Same type (calls or puts)

**Profit drivers:**
- Price stability (near strike)
- Volatility increase
- Time decay differential

**Why stability helps:**
- Near-term option decays faster
- Time decay benefit on short leg
- Long-term option retains value

**Volatility effect:**
- Long option gains from vol increase
- Vega of long > Vega of short
- Net long vega position

**Maximum profit:**
- Near expiration of short option
- Price at or near strike
- Time decay maximized

**Loss scenarios:**
- Large price moves either direction
- Volatility decrease
- Away from strike

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This hurts calendar spreads
- Option C: Interest rates have minor effect
