# Question

A derivative instrument derives its value from:

## Options

- **A**: The credit rating of the issuer
- **B**: An underlying asset, rate, or index
- **C**: The exchange where it is traded

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Derivative fundamentals:

**Definition:**
- Value derived from something else
- The "something else" is the underlying
- No intrinsic value of its own

**Common underlyings:**
- Stocks and stock indexes
- Bonds and interest rates
- Currencies
- Commodities
- Credit events

**Key relationship:**
- Derivative price moves with underlying
- Mathematical relationship exists
- Can be used for hedging or speculation

**Examples:**
- Stock option: derives from stock price
- Interest rate swap: derives from rates
- Currency forward: derives from exchange rates

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Issuer credit may matter but isn't the value source
- Option C: Trading venue doesn't determine derivative value
