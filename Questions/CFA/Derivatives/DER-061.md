# Question

The tenor of a swap refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The frequency of interest payments
- **B**: The total life or maturity of the swap
- **C**: The credit quality of the counterparties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Swap terminology:

**Tenor:**
- Total length of swap contract
- Time from start to final payment
- Common tenors: 2, 5, 10, 30 years

**Payment frequency (different):**
- How often payments occur
- Quarterly, semi-annual, annual
- Can differ for fixed and floating

**Example swap:**
- 5-year tenor
- Fixed leg: semi-annual payments
- Floating leg: quarterly payments
- 10 fixed payments, 20 floating

**Other important terms:**

*Notional principal:*
- Amount used for calculations
- Not exchanged in IR swaps

*Effective date:*
- When swap begins
- Payment calculations start

*Termination date:*
- When swap ends
- Final payment date

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is payment frequency
- Option C: Credit quality is separate consideration
