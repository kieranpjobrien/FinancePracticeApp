# Question

Gamma measures the:

## Options

- **A**: Sensitivity of option price to time decay
- **B**: Rate of change of delta with respect to the underlying price
- **C**: Sensitivity of option price to volatility changes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gamma explained:

**Definition:**
- Second derivative of option price
- Change in delta per unit change in underlying
- Measures delta's instability

**Formula:**
Gamma = ∂Delta / ∂S = ∂²C / ∂S²

**Characteristics:**
- Highest for ATM options
- Lower for ITM and OTM
- Peaks near expiration for ATM
- Always positive for long options

**Why gamma matters:**
- Delta changes as stock moves
- Rebalancing frequency
- Curvature of option payoff

**Gamma risk:**
- Large gamma = delta changes quickly
- Harder to hedge
- More rebalancing needed

**Position implications:**
- Long gamma: Buy options (convexity)
- Short gamma: Sell options (risk)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is theta
- Option C: This is vega
