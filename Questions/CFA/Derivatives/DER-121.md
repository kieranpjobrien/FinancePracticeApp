# Question

Standardized terms in futures contracts typically include all of the following EXCEPT:

## Options

- **A**: Contract size and expiration dates
- **B**: The price agreed upon between buyer and seller
- **C**: Quality specifications and delivery locations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures standardization:

**Standardized terms:**
- Contract size (quantity)
- Expiration dates (cycles)
- Quality specifications
- Delivery locations
- Trading hours
- Tick size

**NOT standardized:**
- Price (determined by trading)
- Each trade negotiates price
- Supply and demand determine price

**Why standardize:**
- Increases liquidity
- Reduces complexity
- Enables fungibility
- Facilitates clearing

**Example (crude oil):**
- Size: 1,000 barrels
- Quality: Light sweet crude
- Delivery: Cushing, OK
- Price: Varies by trade

**Contrast with forwards:**
- Forwards can customize everything
- Less liquid but flexible
- OTC negotiated terms

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: These are standardized
- Option C: These are standardized
