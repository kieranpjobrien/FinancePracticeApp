# Question

Rolling a futures position involves:

## Options

- **A**: Exercising the futures contract early
- **B**: Closing the expiring contract and opening a new contract in a later month
- **C**: Converting a futures position into an options position

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures roll process:

**Rolling defined:**
- Close near-month contract
- Open far-month contract
- Maintain continuous exposure
- Avoid physical delivery

**Why roll:**
- Don't want delivery
- Need ongoing hedge
- Index funds require continuous exposure
- Maintain speculation position

**Roll yield:**
- Difference between contracts
- Contango: Negative roll yield (sell low, buy high)
- Backwardation: Positive roll yield (sell high, buy low)

**Roll timing:**
- Typically before expiration week
- Active trading in "roll period"
- Liquidity considerations

**Example:**
- Long June crude futures
- Roll to September before June expiry
- Sell June, buy September

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Futures don't have early exercise like options
- Option C: Rolling maintains futures position
