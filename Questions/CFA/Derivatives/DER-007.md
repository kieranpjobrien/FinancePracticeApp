# Question

A put option gives the holder the right to:

## Options

- **A**: Buy the underlying at the strike price
- **B**: Sell the underlying at the strike price
- **C**: Exchange one asset for another

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Put option fundamentals:

**Put option holder (long put):**
- Right to SELL at strike price
- Pays premium for this right
- Benefits from price decreases
- Protective when owning the asset

**When to exercise:**
- Exercise when market price < strike
- Sell at higher strike, buy cheaper in market
- Profit = Strike - Market Price - Premium

**Protective put example:**
- Own stock at $100
- Buy put with $95 strike
- If stock falls to $80, sell at $95
- Limits downside loss

**Put seller (writer):**
- Obligation to buy if exercised
- Receives premium
- Wants price to stay above strike

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes a call option
- Option C: This describes a swap
