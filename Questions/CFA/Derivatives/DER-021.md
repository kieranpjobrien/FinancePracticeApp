# Question

The no-arbitrage principle in derivative pricing implies that:

## Options

- **A**: All investors will earn the risk-free rate
- **B**: Equivalent assets or portfolios must have the same price
- **C**: Derivatives are always fairly priced

## Answer

B

## Explanation

No-arbitrage principle:

**Core concept:**
- Two portfolios with identical payoffs
- Must have the same current price
- Otherwise, arbitrage opportunity exists

**Arbitrage defined:**
- Risk-free profit
- No net investment
- Guaranteed positive return
- "Free lunch"

**How it works:**
- Buy underpriced asset
- Sell overpriced equivalent
- Lock in profit
- Market forces eliminate mispricing

**Application to derivatives:**
- Derivative price derived from replication
- Replicate payoff with underlying + financing
- Derivative price = cost of replication

**Market efficiency:**
- Arbitrageurs eliminate mispricings
- Prices converge to no-arbitrage values
- Foundation of derivative pricing

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not about earning risk-free rate
- Option C: Mispricings can exist temporarily
