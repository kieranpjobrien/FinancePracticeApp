# Question

Risk-neutral pricing in derivatives valuation assumes that:

## Options

- **A**: All investors are indifferent to risk
- **B**: Expected returns on all assets equal the risk-free rate
- **C**: Volatility is zero

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk-neutral pricing:

**Key assumption:**
- Price as if all assets earn risk-free rate
- Discount expected payoffs at risk-free rate
- No risk premium required

**Why it works:**
- Based on no-arbitrage
- Replication eliminates risk
- Risk preferences don't matter for pricing
- Mathematical convenience

**Important clarification:**
- Investors aren't actually risk-neutral
- It's a pricing technique
- Gives same answer as real-world with risk adjustment

**Application:**
- Option pricing (Black-Scholes)
- Binomial models
- Forward pricing
- Swap valuation

**Risk-neutral probability:**
- Adjusted probabilities for pricing
- Different from actual probabilities
- Incorporate risk adjustment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Assumption for pricing, not investor behavior
- Option C: Volatility is still modeled, not assumed zero
