# Question

The writer of an option:

## Options

- **A**: Has the right to exercise the option
- **B**: Receives the premium and has an obligation
- **C**: Pays the premium for optionality

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Option writer (seller):

**Writer's position:**
- Receives premium upfront
- Takes on obligation
- Called "short" the option
- Hopes option expires worthless

**Call writer obligations:**
- Must sell underlying if exercised
- At the strike price
- When buyer chooses

**Put writer obligations:**
- Must buy underlying if exercised
- At the strike price
- When buyer chooses

**Risk profile:**

*Call writer:*
- Limited profit (premium)
- Potentially unlimited loss
- Risk if price rises sharply

*Put writer:*
- Limited profit (premium)
- Large but limited loss
- Risk if price falls sharply

**Why write options:**
- Collect premium income
- Enhance portfolio yield
- Covered calls strategy

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Buyer has rights, not writer
- Option C: Buyer pays premium, not writer
