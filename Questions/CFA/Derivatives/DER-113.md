# Question

In the binomial model, the up factor (u) is typically calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: 1 plus the risk-free rate
- **B**: e raised to the power of volatility times the square root of time
- **C**: The historical average return

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binomial parameter calibration:

**Up factor formula:**
u = e^(σ√Δt)

**Down factor:**
d = 1/u = e^(-σ√Δt)

**Parameters:**
- σ = Volatility (annualized)
- Δt = Time step length
- e = Euler's number (2.718...)

**Example:**
- σ = 20% annual volatility
- Monthly steps: Δt = 1/12
- u = e^(0.20 × √(1/12))
- u = e^(0.0577)
- u ≈ 1.059

**Why this formula:**
- Matches Black-Scholes in limit
- Calibrates to actual volatility
- As steps increase, converges to continuous

**Key relationship:**
- Higher volatility → Higher u
- Larger time steps → Higher u
- u × d = 1 (recombining tree)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Risk-free rate is separate parameter
- Option C: Uses forward-looking volatility, not historical returns
