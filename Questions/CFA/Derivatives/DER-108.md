# Question

An amortizing swap is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: A notional principal that increases over time
- **B**: A notional principal that decreases over time
- **C**: A floating rate that is fixed at inception

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Amortizing swap structure:

**Definition:**
- Notional principal declines over life
- Matches amortizing loan payments
- Decreasing exposure over time

**Common use:**
- Hedge amortizing debt
- Mortgage portfolios
- Equipment financing

**Example:**
- $100M notional initially
- Declines by $10M each year
- Final year: $10M notional

**Payment calculation:**
- Interest based on current notional
- Changes each period
- Mirrors underlying loan

**Comparison:**

*Bullet swap:*
- Constant notional
- Standard swap structure

*Accreting swap:*
- Notional increases over time
- Matches construction loans

*Index amortizing:*
- Notional based on index (rates)
- Prepayment-linked

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes accreting swap
- Option C: Floating rate still resets in amortizing swaps
