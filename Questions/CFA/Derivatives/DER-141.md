# Question

A total return swap differs from an interest rate swap in that a total return swap:

## Options

- **A**: Only exchanges interest payments
- **B**: Includes both the income and capital gains/losses of the reference asset
- **C**: Has no credit risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Total return swap structure:

**TRS payments:**
- Total return payer: Pays all returns (income + price change)
- Total return receiver: Pays financing rate (SOFR + spread)

**Components of total return:**
- Interest or dividends
- Capital appreciation
- Or capital depreciation (if negative)

**vs. Interest rate swap:**
- IRS: Only interest payments exchanged
- TRS: Full economic exposure transferred
- TRS: Includes principal/price movements

**Uses:**
- Synthetic asset exposure
- Balance sheet management
- Leverage
- Avoiding ownership costs

**Credit aspect:**
- Reference asset credit risk transferred
- Receiver takes credit exposure
- Used extensively in structured credit

**Example:**
- TRS on corporate bond
- Receiver gets: Coupons + Price changes
- Receiver pays: SOFR + 50 bps

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes interest rate swaps
- Option C: TRS has significant credit risk on reference asset
