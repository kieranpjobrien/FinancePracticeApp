# Question

Exchange-traded derivatives differ from OTC derivatives primarily in that exchange-traded contracts are:

## Options

- **A**: Customized to meet specific client needs
- **B**: Standardized and cleared through a central counterparty
- **C**: Subject to less regulatory oversight

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Exchange-traded vs. OTC derivatives:

**Exchange-traded characteristics:**
- Standardized contract terms
- Central clearinghouse (CCP)
- Reduced counterparty risk
- Greater liquidity
- Transparent pricing
- Margin requirements

**OTC characteristics:**
- Customized terms
- Bilateral agreements
- Direct counterparty risk
- Less transparency
- Flexible structures
- Negotiated terms

**Clearinghouse function:**
- Becomes counterparty to both sides
- Guarantees performance
- Manages margin
- Reduces systemic risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: OTC derivatives are customized, not exchange-traded
- Option C: Exchange-traded typically have MORE regulatory oversight
