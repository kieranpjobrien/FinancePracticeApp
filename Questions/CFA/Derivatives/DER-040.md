# Question

An increase in the volatility of the underlying asset will cause option prices to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase for calls and decrease for puts
- **B**: Decrease for both calls and puts
- **C**: Increase for both calls and puts

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Volatility and option prices:

**Why volatility increases option values:**
- Greater chance of large moves
- Asymmetric payoff structure
- Upside captured, downside limited

**Call options:**
- Benefit from large upward moves
- Downside limited to premium
- Higher volatility = more valuable

**Put options:**
- Benefit from large downward moves
- Downside limited to premium
- Higher volatility = more valuable

**The key insight:**
- Options have unlimited upside potential
- Limited downside (can't lose more than premium)
- Volatility increases probability of big moves
- Option holder benefits either way

**Vega:**
- Measures volatility sensitivity
- Positive for both calls and puts
- Longer-dated options have higher vega

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Both types benefit from volatility
- Option B: Both increase, not decrease
