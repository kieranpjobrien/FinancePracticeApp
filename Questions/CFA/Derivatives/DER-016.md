# Question

The primary purpose of using derivatives for hedging is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize potential returns on investments
- **B**: Reduce or transfer unwanted risk exposures
- **C**: Increase leverage in a portfolio

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hedging with derivatives:

**Purpose:**
- Reduce risk exposure
- Transfer risk to willing parties
- Protect against adverse movements
- Stabilize cash flows

**How it works:**
- Take offsetting position
- Gain on hedge offsets loss on underlying
- Reduces both upside and downside

**Common hedging examples:**
- Farmer sells futures to lock in crop price
- Importer buys currency forward
- Borrower uses interest rate swap
- Investor buys put options

**Hedging vs. speculation:**
- Hedging: Risk reduction
- Speculation: Risk taking for profit

**Trade-off:**
- Reduces risk but also limits gains
- Cost of hedging (premiums, spreads)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Hedging limits returns to reduce risk
- Option C: Hedging reduces leverage/risk, doesn't increase it
