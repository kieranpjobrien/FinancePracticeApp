# Question

The maturity or expiration date of a derivative contract specifies:

## Options

- **A**: When the contract was first traded
- **B**: When the contract obligations must be fulfilled or the contract terminates
- **C**: The date when margin requirements begin

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Maturity/expiration date:

**Definition:**
- End date of the contract
- When obligations are settled
- Contract ceases to exist

**For different derivatives:**

*Options:*
- Last day to exercise
- Expires worthless if OTM
- Settlement if exercised

*Futures:*
- Delivery or cash settlement
- Position must be closed or delivered
- Last trading day matters

*Forwards:*
- Settlement date
- Exchange of asset and payment
- Single settlement

*Swaps:*
- Final payment date
- Last exchange of cash flows
- Contract terminates

**Time value connection:**
- Options lose time value approaching expiration
- Futures converge to spot
- Forward value approaches settlement value

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is the trade date, not maturity
- Option C: Margin applies throughout, not starting at maturity
