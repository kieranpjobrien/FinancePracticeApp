# Question

A synthetic long forward position can be created by:

## Options

- **A**: Buying the underlying and borrowing at the risk-free rate
- **B**: Buying a call and selling a put with the same strike and expiration
- **C**: Selling the underlying and lending at the risk-free rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Synthetic forward creation:

**Put-call parity relationship:**
C - P = S - PV(K)

Rearranging:
C - P = Forward position

**Synthetic long forward:**
- Buy call (right to buy)
- Sell put (obligation to buy if exercised)
- Same strike (K) and expiration
- Net effect: Obligation to buy at K

**Payoff comparison:**

*Synthetic (Long call + Short put):*
- If S > K: Exercise call, buy at K
- If S < K: Put exercised, must buy at K
- Result: Always buy at K

*Actual forward:*
- Obligated to buy at forward price
- Same payoff structure

**Practical uses:**
- Create forward when not available
- Arbitrage if prices misaligned
- Regulatory or portfolio constraints

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Creates leveraged long position, not forward
- Option C: Creates synthetic short forward
