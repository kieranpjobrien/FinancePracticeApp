# Question

A bear put spread involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying a put with a lower strike and selling a put with a higher strike
- **B**: Buying a put with a higher strike and selling a put with a lower strike
- **C**: Buying two puts at different strikes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bear put spread:

**Structure:**
- Buy put at higher strike (K2)
- Sell put at lower strike (K1)
- Same expiration
- K2 > K1

**Characteristics:**
- Net debit (pay premium)
- Bearish position
- Limited profit and loss
- Profits when stock falls

**Maximum profit:**
- K2 - K1 - Net premium
- Achieved when stock ≤ K1
- Both puts ITM

**Maximum loss:**
- Net premium paid
- When stock ≥ K2
- Both puts expire worthless

**Breakeven:**
= K2 - Net premium

**Why use:**
- Reduce cost of long put
- Accept capped profit
- Defined risk bearish bet

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is a bull put spread (credit)
- Option C: Must sell one put to create spread
