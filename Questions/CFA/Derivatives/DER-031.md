# Question

Futures prices may differ from forward prices primarily due to:

## Options

- **A**: The underlying asset being different
- **B**: The correlation between futures prices and interest rates
- **C**: Exchange trading fees

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures vs. forward price differences:

**Key difference: Daily settlement**
- Futures: Daily mark-to-market
- Forwards: Single payment at expiration
- Reinvestment of daily gains/losses matters

**When prices differ:**

*Positive correlation (futures > interest rates):*
- When prices rise, rates often rise
- Gains reinvested at higher rates
- Futures more valuable than forwards

*Negative correlation:*
- When prices rise, rates fall
- Gains reinvested at lower rates
- Futures less valuable than forwards

*Zero correlation:*
- Futures ≈ Forward prices
- Reinvestment effects cancel

**Practical impact:**
- Difference usually small
- Matters more for interest rate derivatives
- Often ignored for equity/commodity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Same underlying for both
- Option C: Trading fees are minor factor
