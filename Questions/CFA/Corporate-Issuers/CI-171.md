# Question

Cross-shareholding arrangements between companies can:

## Options

- **A**: Enhance takeover vulnerability
- **B**: Create potential conflicts of interest and entrench management
- **C**: Improve transparency and accountability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-shareholding: companies hold shares in each other. Potential issues: reduces takeover threats (friendly shareholders won't tender), management entrenchment, voting decisions may prioritize relationship over value. Common in some markets (e.g., Japan's keiretsu). Raises governance concerns about independence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—cross-shareholding typically reduces vulnerability
- Option C is contrary—arrangements often reduce transparency
