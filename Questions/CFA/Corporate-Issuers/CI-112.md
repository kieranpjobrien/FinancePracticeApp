# Question

Under Modigliani-Miller without taxes, the cost of equity:

## Options

- **A**: Is constant regardless of leverage
- **B**: Increases linearly with the debt-to-equity ratio
- **C**: Decreases as debt increases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

MM Proposition II (no taxes): Re = Ra + (Ra - Rd) × (D/E). As leverage increases, equity becomes riskier, requiring higher returns. The cost of equity increases, but WACC remains constant because cheaper debt exactly offsets more expensive equity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores leverage's effect on equity risk
- Option C reverses the relationship
