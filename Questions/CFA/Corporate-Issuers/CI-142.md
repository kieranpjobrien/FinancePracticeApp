# Question

According to the life-cycle theory of dividends:

## Options

- **A**: Young, high-growth companies typically pay high dividends
- **B**: Mature companies with limited investment opportunities pay higher dividends
- **C**: Dividend policy is constant throughout a company's life

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Life-cycle theory: young/growing companies retain earnings for investment (low/no dividends). As companies mature with fewer growth opportunities, returning cash to shareholders becomes optimal (higher dividends). Dividend policy evolves with the company's investment opportunity set.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the typical pattern
- Option C ignores changing circumstances over the life cycle
