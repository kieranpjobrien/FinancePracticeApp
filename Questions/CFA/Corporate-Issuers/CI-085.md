# Question

Using a single company-wide hurdle rate for all projects:

## Options

- **A**: Always leads to optimal capital allocation
- **B**: May accept too many high-risk projects and reject good low-risk projects
- **C**: Is required by accounting standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A single hurdle rate ignores project-specific risk. High-risk projects compared to a moderate WACC appear attractive (returns exceed hurdle) even if they destroy value. Low-risk projects may be rejected despite positive NPV. Risk-adjusted or divisional hurdle rates improve capital allocation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores misallocation from ignoring risk differences
- Option C—WACC usage is a financial decision, not accounting requirement
