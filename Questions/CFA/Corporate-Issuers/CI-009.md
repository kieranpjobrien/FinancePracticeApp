# Question

The internal rate of return (IRR) is:

## Options

- **A**: The rate that makes NPV equal to zero
- **B**: The company's weighted average cost of capital
- **C**: The arithmetic average of annual project returns

## Answer

A

## Explanation

IRR is the discount rate that makes NPV = 0. If IRR > cost of capital, accept (positive NPV). IRR is intuitive but has limitations: assumes reinvestment at IRR, may have multiple solutions, and can rank projects differently than NPV for mutually exclusive projects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes WACC, which is compared to IRR
- Option C is not how IRR is calculated
