# Question

Dividend signaling theory suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Dividend changes convey management's view of future earnings prospects
- **B**: All dividend increases are positive signals
- **C**: Dividends have no information content

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Signaling theory: dividend changes reveal management's private information about future earnings. Increases signal confidence; cuts signal trouble. Because dividend policy has commitment characteristics (cuts are costly), signals are credible. Market reactions to dividend announcements support signaling effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B overstates—some increases may signal lack of investment opportunities
- Option C describes MM irrelevance, ignoring information asymmetry
