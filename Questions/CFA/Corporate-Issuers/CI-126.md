# Question

If international capital markets are segmented rather than integrated:

## Options

- **A**: A single global cost of capital applies to all companies
- **B**: Country-specific factors affect the cost of capital
- **C**: Currency risk is irrelevant

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Segmented markets: barriers to international investment (capital controls, taxes, information asymmetry) mean local factors dominate pricing. Different required returns exist across markets. In integrated markets, global factors matter more and a global cost of capital is more appropriate.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to integrated markets
- Option C ignores an important international risk factor
