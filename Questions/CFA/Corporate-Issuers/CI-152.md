# Question

Factoring receivables differs from pledging receivables in that factoring:

## Options

- **A**: Uses receivables as collateral while retaining ownership
- **B**: Involves selling receivables outright to a third party
- **C**: Always results in lower financing costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Factoring: selling receivables to a factor who assumes collection responsibility. Pledging: using receivables as collateral for a loan while retaining ownership. Factoring removes receivables from the balance sheet; pledging keeps them with a corresponding liability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes pledging, not factoring
- Option C is not necessarily true—factoring can be more expensive
