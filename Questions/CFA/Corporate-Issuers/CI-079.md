# Question

Related party transactions require scrutiny because:

## Options

- **A**: They always benefit shareholders
- **B**: They may not be conducted at arm's length terms
- **C**: They are always illegal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Related party transactions (with insiders, affiliates, family members) may not reflect fair market terms—potentially transferring value from the company to related parties. Proper disclosure, independent approval, and fair valuation are essential governance safeguards. Not all related party transactions are harmful.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores potential for abuse
- Option C overstates—they're legal if properly disclosed and managed
