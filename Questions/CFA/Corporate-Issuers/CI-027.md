# Question

The "bird-in-hand" theory of dividends suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Investors are indifferent between dividends and capital gains
- **B**: Investors prefer dividends because they are less risky than future capital gains
- **C**: Companies should never pay dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bird-in-hand theory: investors value near-term dividends more than uncertain future capital gains. "A bird in hand is worth two in the bush." Investors perceive dividends as less risky, so dividend-paying stocks require lower returns. This contradicts MM's irrelevance argument.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes MM irrelevance
- Option C describes the opposite recommendation
