# Question

A credit rating downgrade typically results in:

## Options

- **A**: Lower borrowing costs
- **B**: Higher borrowing costs and potential covenant triggers
- **C**: No impact on the company's financing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit downgrades signal increased default risk, raising the yield investors require. This increases borrowing costs for new debt. Some covenants trigger at rating thresholds (rating triggers), potentially accelerating debt repayment. Stock prices often decline on downgrades.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores the significant market impact
