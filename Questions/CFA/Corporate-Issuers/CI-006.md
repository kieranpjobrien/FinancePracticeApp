# Question

The audit committee of the board is primarily responsible for:

## Options

- **A**: Setting executive compensation
- **B**: Overseeing financial reporting and internal controls
- **C**: Nominating new board members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The audit committee oversees: financial reporting integrity, internal control systems, internal and external auditors, risk management, and compliance. It ensures financial statements fairly represent the company's position. The compensation committee handles executive pay; the nominating committee handles board appointments.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the compensation committee's role
- Option C is the nominating/governance committee's role
