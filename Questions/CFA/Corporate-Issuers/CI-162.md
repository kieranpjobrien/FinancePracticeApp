# Question

When estimating cost of equity for a project in an emerging market, analysts often:

## Options

- **A**: Use only the domestic risk-free rate
- **B**: Add a country risk premium to account for additional political and economic risks
- **C**: Apply the same WACC as domestic projects

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emerging market projects face additional risks: political instability, currency volatility, less developed legal systems, expropriation risk. Country risk premium can be estimated from sovereign bond spreads or country risk ratings. Adjusts cost of equity upward to compensate for these risks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores significant emerging market risks
- Option C doesn't adjust for materially different risk environment
