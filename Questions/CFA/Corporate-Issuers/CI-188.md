# Question

Under the dividend irrelevance theory, investors who prefer higher cash payments can:

## Options

- **A**: Only invest in high-dividend stocks
- **B**: Create "homemade dividends" by selling shares
- **C**: Force companies to increase dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Homemade dividends: under MM assumptions, investors can replicate any desired payout by selling shares. If dividend is too low, sell shares. If too high, reinvest dividends. This flexibility means dividend policy doesn't affect value—investors can adjust to their preferences. Assumes no taxes or transaction costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A limits investment choices unnecessarily
- Option C overstates shareholder power on dividend policy
