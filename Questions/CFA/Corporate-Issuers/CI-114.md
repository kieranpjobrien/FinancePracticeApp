# Question

Companies tend to smooth dividends because:

## Options

- **A**: Volatile dividends attract more investors
- **B**: Dividend cuts send negative signals and upset income-dependent investors
- **C**: Regulations require stable dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividend smoothing: managers are reluctant to cut dividends (negative signal, upsets clientele) and cautious about increases they can't maintain. Dividends follow earnings with a lag, adjusting gradually toward target payout. This creates sticky, predictable dividend patterns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts investor preferences for stability
- Option C overstates regulatory requirements
