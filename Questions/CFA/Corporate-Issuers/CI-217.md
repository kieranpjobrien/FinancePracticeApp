# Question

An increase in net working capital during a project's life:

## Options

- **A**: Is a source of cash
- **B**: Represents a use of cash that reduces project cash flows
- **C**: Has no effect on project valuation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Working capital increase: more cash tied up in inventory, receivables (net of payables). Represents cash outflow reducing available cash flows. At project end, working capital is typically released (recovered), creating cash inflow. Working capital changes must be included in project cash flow analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the direction—increases use cash
- Option C ignores real cash flow impact
