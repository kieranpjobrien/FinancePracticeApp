# Question

A company's debt capacity is most influenced by:

## Options

- **A**: Historical dividend payments only
- **B**: Stability of cash flows, asset tangibility, and growth opportunities
- **C**: The number of shares outstanding

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt capacity determinants: stable, predictable cash flows support higher debt; tangible assets provide collateral value; high growth firms may prefer less debt (preserve flexibility). Industry characteristics, business risk, and market conditions also matter. Determines how much debt lenders will provide.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is not a primary debt capacity factor
- Option C affects equity structure, not debt capacity
