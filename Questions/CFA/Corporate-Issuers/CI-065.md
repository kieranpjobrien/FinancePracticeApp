# Question

When investing in emerging markets, the cost of capital should be adjusted for:

## Options

- **A**: Lower expected returns
- **B**: Country risk premium reflecting political and economic risks
- **C**: Lower discount rates due to growth potential

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emerging markets have additional risks: political instability, currency volatility, expropriation, regulatory uncertainty. A country risk premium is added to the discount rate to compensate for these risks. Higher risk requires higher expected returns, not lower discount rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses higher risk with lower returns
- Option C reverses the relationship—higher risk means higher discount rates
