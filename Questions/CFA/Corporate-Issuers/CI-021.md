# Question

A shorter cash conversion cycle indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Greater need for external financing
- **B**: More efficient working capital management
- **C**: Higher inventory levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash conversion cycle = DSO + DOH - DPO. Shorter cycles mean faster conversion of inventory/receivables to cash relative to paying suppliers—less capital tied up, better efficiency. Negative cycles (cash received before paying suppliers) are ideal. Longer cycles require more working capital financing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes longer cycles needing financing
- Option C would lengthen, not shorten, the cycle
