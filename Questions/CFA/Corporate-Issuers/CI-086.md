# Question

Debt overhang leads to underinvestment because:

## Options

- **A**: Shareholders bear all costs but share benefits with bondholders
- **B**: Bondholders refuse to allow any new investments
- **C**: Debt always increases investment incentives

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Debt overhang: when a company has significant debt, new equity investment benefits bondholders first (reducing default risk). Shareholders fund investments but share upside with creditors. This creates underinvestment in positive NPV projects—an agency cost of debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B overstates bondholder control
- Option C reverses the actual effect
