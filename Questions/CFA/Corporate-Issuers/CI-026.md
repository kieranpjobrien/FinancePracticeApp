# Question

According to Modigliani-Miller's dividend irrelevance theory:

## Options

- **A**: Dividends always increase share value
- **B**: Dividend policy does not affect firm value in perfect markets
- **C**: Companies should always reinvest earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In perfect markets (no taxes, transaction costs, or information asymmetry), dividend policy is irrelevant—investors can create "homemade dividends" by selling shares. Firm value depends on investment decisions, not how earnings are distributed. Real-world frictions make dividends matter in practice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the irrelevance proposition
- Option C states a universal preference, which doesn't exist
