# Question

Factoring accounts receivable:

## Options

- **A**: Increases the collection period
- **B**: Provides immediate cash by selling receivables to a third party
- **C**: Eliminates the need for credit sales

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Factoring sells receivables to a factor (financial institution) at a discount, providing immediate liquidity. The company receives cash minus the factor's fee, transferring collection responsibility and credit risk (if non-recourse). It's useful when cash is needed quickly but has costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite effect
- Option C doesn't eliminate credit sales—just accelerates collection
