# Question

According to market timing theory, companies:

## Options

- **A**: Issue securities randomly without regard to market conditions
- **B**: Issue equity when shares are overvalued and debt when interest rates are low
- **C**: Maintain a constant capital structure regardless of market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market timing: companies exploit temporary mispricings. Issue equity when stock is overvalued (high prices, favorable sentiment), issue debt when rates are low. Implies no optimal capital structure—structure reflects cumulative timing decisions. Explains observed capital structure patterns and issuance clustering.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores strategic financing decisions
- Option C contradicts observed financing behavior
