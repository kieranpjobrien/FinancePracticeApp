# Question

Institutional investor engagement with portfolio companies typically involves:

## Options

- **A**: Selling shares immediately when disagreeing with management
- **B**: Private dialogue on governance, strategy, and ESG matters before public action
- **C**: Ignoring corporate governance issues

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Engagement: institutional investors increasingly engage privately with management on governance, strategy, capital allocation, and ESG. This may include meetings, letters, and voting. Public campaigns or proxy fights are usually last resorts when private engagement fails.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes "exit" rather than "voice" strategy
- Option C ignores fiduciary duty to monitor investments
