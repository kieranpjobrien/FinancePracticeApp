# Question

When evaluating a project in a different industry, a company should use:

## Options

- **A**: Its own company-wide WACC
- **B**: A project-specific discount rate reflecting the new industry's risk
- **C**: The risk-free rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Projects with different risks than the company average should use project-specific discount rates. Use pure-play beta: find public companies in the target industry, unlever their betas, relever for project capital structure, and calculate the appropriate required return. Using company WACC misprices risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores different risk profiles
- Option C ignores risk premiums entirely
