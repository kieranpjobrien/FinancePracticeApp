# Question

Modified IRR (MIRR) addresses IRR's weaknesses by:

## Options

- **A**: Ignoring the time value of money
- **B**: Using realistic reinvestment and financing rate assumptions
- **C**: Always equaling NPV

## Answer

B

## Explanation

MIRR assumes reinvestment at the cost of capital (more realistic than IRR's assumption of reinvestment at IRR) and financing of negative cash flows at the financing cost. This produces a unique rate and more realistic performance measure than traditional IRR.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is backward—MIRR uses time value
- Option C is incorrect—MIRR and NPV measure different things
