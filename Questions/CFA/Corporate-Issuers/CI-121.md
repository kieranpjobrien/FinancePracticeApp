# Question

Whistle-blower protections are important for corporate governance because they:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminate the need for external auditors
- **B**: Encourage employees to report misconduct without fear of retaliation
- **C**: Guarantee that all fraud will be detected

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Whistle-blower protections: legal safeguards encouraging reporting of fraud, violations, or unethical conduct. Without protection, employees fear job loss or retaliation. Effective programs include anonymous hotlines, anti-retaliation policies, and rewards for reporting. They complement other controls.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates whistle-blowers' role
- Option C is unrealistic—no system detects all fraud
