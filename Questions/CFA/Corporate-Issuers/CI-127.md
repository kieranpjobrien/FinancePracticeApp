# Question

Convertible bonds are considered hybrid securities because:

## Options

- **A**: They can only be issued by financial institutions
- **B**: They combine debt features with an equity conversion option
- **C**: They have no maturity date

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convertible bonds: fixed-income securities that can be converted to equity at the holder's option. They combine bond characteristics (coupon, maturity, seniority) with equity upside (conversion feature). This hybrid nature affects cost of capital calculations and financial leverage analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is false—any company can issue convertibles
- Option C describes perpetual securities, not convertibles
