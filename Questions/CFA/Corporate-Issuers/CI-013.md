# Question

The weighted average cost of capital (WACC) represents:

## Options

- **A**: The cost of equity only
- **B**: The minimum return required to satisfy all capital providers
- **C**: The historical average cost of funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

WACC weights the costs of equity and debt (after-tax) by their proportions in the target capital structure. It represents the opportunity cost of capital and minimum required return to maintain firm value. Projects earning WACC create zero NPV; exceeding WACC creates value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores debt in the capital structure
- Option C uses historical rates instead of current market-based costs
