# Question

The clientele effect in dividend policy suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: All investors prefer the same dividend policy
- **B**: Investors are attracted to companies matching their dividend preferences
- **C**: Companies should frequently change dividend policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Clientele effect: investors self-select into stocks matching their preferences. Tax-sensitive investors prefer low dividends; income-seeking investors prefer high dividends. Once a clientele forms, changing policy may drive away current investors. The effect supports dividend stability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores investor heterogeneity
- Option C would upset the established clientele
