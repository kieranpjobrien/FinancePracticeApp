# Question

Having a non-executive independent chairman of the board:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminates the need for other independent directors
- **B**: Provides oversight of the CEO and reduces potential conflicts of interest
- **C**: Is legally required in all jurisdictions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Independent chair: separates board leadership from management, enhancing oversight. Can challenge CEO more effectively than combined CEO/chair. Improves governance by ensuring board serves shareholder interests independently. Alternative: lead independent director when CEO also chairs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A understates need for broader board independence
- Option C is incorrect—requirements vary by jurisdiction
