# Question

"Tunneling" in corporate governance refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Creating underground facilities for operations
- **B**: Transferring resources out of a company to benefit controlling shareholders at minority expense
- **C**: Conducting due diligence investigations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tunneling: controlling shareholders extract value through self-dealing transactions, transfer pricing manipulation, excessive compensation, asset stripping, or favorable related-party deals. Common in weak governance environments. Minority shareholders bear losses. Legal protections, disclosure requirements, and independent oversight help prevent tunneling.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A takes the term literally
- Option C describes legitimate investigation activity
