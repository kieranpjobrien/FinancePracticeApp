# Question

The principal-agent problem in corporations arises because:

## Options

- **A**: Shareholders always have more information than managers
- **B**: Managers may pursue their own interests rather than shareholders' interests
- **C**: The board of directors has no authority over management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The agency problem occurs when agents (managers) have incentives that diverge from principals (shareholders). Managers might prefer empire-building, job security, or excessive perks over shareholder value maximization. Solutions include performance-based compensation, monitoring, and strong governance structures.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the information asymmetry—managers typically have more information
- Option C is incorrect—boards can hire/fire management and set compensation
