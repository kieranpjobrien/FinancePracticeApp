# Question

Stand-alone risk of a project refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The project's risk when viewed in isolation, ignoring diversification
- **B**: The project's contribution to the company's overall risk
- **C**: The project's systematic risk only

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Stand-alone risk: project's total risk viewed independently, measured by variability of expected returns. Ignores how project fits with company's existing operations or investor portfolios. Useful for initial project screening but not complete—corporate and market risk perspectives also matter.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes corporate risk, not stand-alone risk
- Option C ignores unsystematic risk components
