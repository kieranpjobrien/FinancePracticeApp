# Question

A company has total debt of $400 million, EBITDA of $100 million, and interest expense of $25 million. The debt-to-EBITDA ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 4.0x
- **B**: 16.0x
- **C**: 0.25x

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Debt-to-EBITDA = Total debt / EBITDA = $400M / $100M = 4.0x. This leverage metric shows how many years of EBITDA would be needed to repay debt. Higher ratios indicate greater leverage risk. Interest coverage = EBITDA / Interest = $100M / $25M = 4.0x.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B divides debt by interest expense
- Option C inverts the ratio
