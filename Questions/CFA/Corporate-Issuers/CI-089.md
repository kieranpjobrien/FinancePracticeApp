# Question

Market timing theory of capital structure suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Companies maintain constant capital structure regardless of market conditions
- **B**: Companies issue equity when valuations are high and debt when rates are low
- **C**: Markets are perfectly efficient in pricing securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market timing: managers exploit perceived market mispricing—issuing equity when stock is overvalued, issuing debt when interest rates are low, repurchasing shares when undervalued. Capital structure reflects historical timing decisions rather than targeting an optimal structure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes target capital structure approach
- Option C contradicts the premise of market timing
