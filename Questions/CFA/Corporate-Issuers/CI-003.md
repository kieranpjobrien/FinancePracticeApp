# Question

The primary fiduciary duty of a corporate board of directors is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize short-term profits
- **B**: Act in the best interests of shareholders
- **C**: Satisfy all stakeholder demands equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The board's primary fiduciary duty is to shareholders—the residual owners of the company. This includes duties of care (informed decision-making) and loyalty (avoiding conflicts). While stakeholder interests matter, shareholders' interests take precedence in the traditional corporate governance framework.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes short-term over long-term value creation
- Option C overstates obligations to non-shareholder stakeholders
