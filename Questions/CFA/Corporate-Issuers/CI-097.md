# Question

In bankruptcy, secured creditors:

## Options

- **A**: Are paid after all other creditors
- **B**: Have priority claims on specific assets pledged as collateral
- **C**: Receive the same treatment as equity holders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Secured creditors have claims on pledged collateral—they're paid first from those specific assets. The priority typically: secured creditors (to extent of collateral), then administrative claims, then unsecured creditors, then subordinated debt, finally equity. Recovery rates vary by seniority.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the priority order
- Option C ignores the seniority hierarchy
