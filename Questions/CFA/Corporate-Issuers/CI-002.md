# Question

The agency problem arises when:

## Options

- **A**: Shareholders and managers have perfectly aligned interests
- **B**: Managers (agents) may act in their own interests rather than shareholders' (principals') interests
- **C**: The company has no debt in its capital structure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Agency problem: separation of ownership (shareholders) and control (managers) creates potential conflicts. Managers may pursue personal goals—empire building, excessive compensation, job security—rather than maximizing shareholder wealth. Governance mechanisms (board oversight, incentive compensation, monitoring) help mitigate agency costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the ideal situation with no agency problem
- Option C is unrelated to agency conflicts
