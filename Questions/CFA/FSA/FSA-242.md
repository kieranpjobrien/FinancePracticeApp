# Question

Under IFRS, a "true and fair override" allows companies to:

## Options

- **A**: Choose any accounting method preferred by management
- **B**: Depart from a standard in rare cases when compliance would be misleading
- **C**: Defer adoption of new standards indefinitely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS permits departure from a standard (in extremely rare circumstances) if compliance would conflict with the objective of fair presentation. Full disclosure of the departure and its effects is required. US GAAP does not have a similar override provision—compliance with GAAP is presumed to achieve fair presentation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates management discretion
- Option C confuses override with transition provisions
