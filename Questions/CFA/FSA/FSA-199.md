# Question

"Channel stuffing" is a practice that:

## Options

- **A**: Delays revenue recognition to future periods
- **B**: Accelerates revenue by inducing customers to purchase more than needed
- **C**: Improves inventory management efficiency

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Channel stuffing involves offering incentives (discounts, extended payment terms) to push excess inventory onto distributors or retailers, accelerating revenue recognition. Warning signs include high receivables, customer inventory build-up, and revenue reversals in subsequent periods. It borrows from future sales, creating unsustainable revenue growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conservative accounting, not manipulation
- Option C confuses revenue acceleration with operational improvement
