# Question

A company has revenue of $1,000,000, gross profit of $400,000, and operating income of $150,000. The operating margin is:

## Options

- **A**: 15%
- **B**: 37.5%
- **C**: 40%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Operating margin = Operating income / Revenue = $150,000 / $1,000,000 = 15%. This measures profitability from core operations after all operating costs. Gross margin (40%) measures production efficiency; operating margin adds selling, administrative, and other operating costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B divides operating income by gross profit
- Option C is gross margin, not operating margin
