# Question

Under the indirect method, an increase in inventory is:

## Options

- **A**: Added to net income to calculate operating cash flow
- **B**: Subtracted from net income to calculate operating cash flow
- **C**: Reported in investing activities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An increase in inventory means cash was used to purchase goods not yet sold—a use of cash not reflected in net income. Therefore, inventory increases are subtracted when converting net income to operating cash flow. Conversely, inventory decreases are added (cash previously spent is now recovered through sales).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the adjustment
- Option C misclassifies inventory as an investing item
