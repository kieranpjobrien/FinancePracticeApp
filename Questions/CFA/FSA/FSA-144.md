# Question

Assets classified as held for sale are:

## Options

- **A**: Depreciated at an accelerated rate
- **B**: Measured at the lower of carrying amount and fair value less costs to sell
- **C**: Reported at estimated selling price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When assets meet held-for-sale criteria (available for immediate sale, management committed, sale probable within one year), they're measured at the lower of carrying amount or fair value less costs to sell. Depreciation ceases. They're presented separately as current assets (or in discontinued operations if applicable).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—depreciation stops for held-for-sale assets
- Option C omits the "lower of" test
