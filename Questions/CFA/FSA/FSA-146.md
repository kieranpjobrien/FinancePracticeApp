# Question

The units-of-production depreciation method is most appropriate for assets whose:

## Options

- **A**: Value declines evenly over time
- **B**: Useful life is best measured by output or usage
- **C**: Salvage value is difficult to estimate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Units-of-production depreciation bases expense on actual usage (miles driven, units produced, hours operated) rather than time. It matches expense to the periods when the asset generates revenue. It's appropriate for vehicles, machinery, and equipment where wear is usage-dependent rather than time-dependent.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes straight-line method
- Option C is unrelated to the choice of method
