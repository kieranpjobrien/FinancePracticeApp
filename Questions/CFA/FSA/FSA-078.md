# Question

Treasury stock is reported on the balance sheet as:

## Options

- **A**: An asset
- **B**: A reduction of shareholders' equity
- **C**: A liability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Treasury stock represents shares repurchased by the company and held in its treasury. It reduces shareholders' equity (a contra-equity account) because repurchases return capital to selling shareholders. Treasury shares have no voting rights, receive no dividends, and are not included in shares outstanding for EPS calculations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly treats repurchased shares as an asset
- Option C incorrectly treats it as an obligation
