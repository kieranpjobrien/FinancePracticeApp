# Question

Under IFRS, biological assets (living animals or plants) are typically measured at:

## Options

- **A**: Historical cost less accumulated depreciation
- **B**: Fair value less costs to sell
- **C**: Lower of cost or net realizable value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IAS 41 requires biological assets (cattle, timber, crops) to be measured at fair value less costs to sell, with changes recognized in profit or loss. This reflects the biological transformation (growth, degeneration, production) that changes asset value. Agricultural produce is also measured at fair value at harvest.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to standard PPE, not biological assets
- Option C applies to inventory, not biological assets
