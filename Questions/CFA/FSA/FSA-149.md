# Question

Under IFRS, a convertible bond is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Recorded entirely as a liability
- **B**: Split between a liability component and an equity component
- **C**: Recorded entirely as equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS requires bifurcation of convertible bonds: the liability component (present value of future cash flows at a comparable non-convertible rate) goes to liabilities, and the residual (conversion option) goes to equity. US GAAP generally records the entire amount as a liability unless the conversion option is separated under specific conditions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes typical US GAAP treatment
- Option C misclassifies the entire instrument
