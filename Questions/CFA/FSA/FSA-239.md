# Question

Conservative accounting choices typically result in:

## Options

- **A**: Higher current earnings and higher assets
- **B**: Lower current earnings and lower assets
- **C**: No effect on financial statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conservative accounting recognizes expenses and losses quickly, defers revenue recognition, and uses lower asset valuations (e.g., accelerated depreciation, LIFO in rising prices, aggressive allowances). This understates current income and assets but may overstate future periods. It builds "hidden reserves" that can be released later.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes aggressive accounting
- Option C ignores the impact of accounting choices
