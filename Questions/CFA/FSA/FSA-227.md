# Question

In the indirect method, an increase in accounts receivable is:

## Options

- **A**: Added to net income
- **B**: Subtracted from net income
- **C**: Not adjusted

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Increased receivables mean revenue exceeded cash collected—sales were made on credit. To convert accrual income to cash basis, this difference is subtracted. Working capital increases (receivables, inventory, prepaids) reduce CFO; working capital decreases in assets increase CFO. The opposite applies to liabilities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the adjustment direction
- Option C ignores the accrual-to-cash conversion requirement
