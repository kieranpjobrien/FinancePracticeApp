# Question

A company's stock trades at $60 per share with earnings per share of $4. Its price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 6.7%
- **B**: 15.0
- **C**: 0.067

## Answer

B

## Explanation

P/E ratio = Price per share / Earnings per share = $60 / $4 = 15.0. This means investors pay $15 for every $1 of earnings. Higher P/E ratios generally indicate higher growth expectations. The P/E is a multiple, not a percentage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly expresses the inverse as a percentage (earnings yield)
- Option C inverts the ratio (E/P)
