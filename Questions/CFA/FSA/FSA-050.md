# Question

Under IFRS, the recoverable amount of an asset for impairment testing is:

## Options

- **A**: The higher of fair value less costs to sell and value in use
- **B**: The undiscounted expected future cash flows
- **C**: The lower of carrying amount and fair value

## Answer

A

## Explanation

IFRS defines recoverable amount as the higher of: (1) fair value less costs of disposal, and (2) value in use (present value of expected cash flows). If carrying amount exceeds recoverable amount, the asset is impaired. US GAAP uses a two-step test with undiscounted cash flows first.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes the first step of US GAAP impairment testing
- Option C describes the lower-of-cost-or-market inventory rule
