# Question

A company acquires equipment by issuing common stock. This transaction is:

## Options

- **A**: Reported in the investing activities section
- **B**: Reported in the financing activities section
- **C**: Disclosed as a significant non-cash transaction

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Non-cash investing and financing activities (asset acquisitions via stock issuance, debt-to-equity conversions, lease capitalizations) don't appear in the cash flow statement body because no cash changes hands. They're disclosed in supplemental schedules or notes to maintain transparency about significant transactions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would require cash outflow, which didn't occur
- Option B would require cash inflow, which didn't occur
