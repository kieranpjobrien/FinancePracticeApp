# Question

EBITDA is useful because it:

## Options

- **A**: Is a GAAP-defined measure required on financial statements
- **B**: Approximates operating cash flow before working capital changes
- **C**: Includes the effects of capital structure and tax rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization) approximates cash generated from operations before capital expenditures and working capital changes. By excluding D&A, interest, and taxes, it facilitates comparison across companies with different capital structures and tax situations. It's not a GAAP measure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—EBITDA is a non-GAAP measure
- Option C describes what EBITDA excludes, not includes
