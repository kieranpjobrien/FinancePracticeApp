# Question

Under US GAAP, costs of internally developed software for sale are:

## Options

- **A**: Expensed entirely as incurred
- **B**: Capitalized after technological feasibility is established
- **C**: Capitalized from project inception

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Software development costs are expensed through technological feasibility, then capitalized until general release. Technological feasibility is established upon completing a detailed design or working model. Capitalized costs are amortized over the product's economic life. This differs from internal-use software, which has different capitalization criteria.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is overly conservative—post-feasibility costs are capitalized
- Option C is too aggressive—pre-feasibility costs must be expensed
