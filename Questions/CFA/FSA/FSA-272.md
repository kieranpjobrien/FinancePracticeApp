# Question

Goods held on consignment should be included in the inventory of:

## Options

- **A**: The consignee who holds the goods
- **B**: The consignor who owns the goods
- **C**: Both parties, pro-rata

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Consignment goods remain the property of the consignor (shipper) until sold by the consignee (holder/agent). The consignor includes them in inventory; the consignee does not (they're agent, not owner). Revenue recognition occurs only when the consignee sells to the final customer. Title and risk determine inventory ownership.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses physical possession with ownership
- Option C misunderstands ownership—only one party owns
