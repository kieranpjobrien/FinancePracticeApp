# Question

Under the equity method, the investor's share of investee earnings is:

## Options

- **A**: Recorded only when dividends are received
- **B**: Recorded as investment income, increasing the investment account
- **C**: Recorded directly in other comprehensive income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The equity method records the investor's proportionate share of investee net income as investment income on the investor's income statement. The investment account on the balance sheet increases. When dividends are received, the investment account decreases (no additional income). This reflects the investor's underlying economic interest.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the cost method, not equity method
- Option C is incorrect—equity method earnings flow through net income
