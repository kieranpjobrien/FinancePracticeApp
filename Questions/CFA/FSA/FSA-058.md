# Question

A valuation allowance against a deferred tax asset is required when:

## Options

- **A**: The tax rate is expected to decrease
- **B**: It is more likely than not that some portion will not be realized
- **C**: The deferred tax asset exceeds the deferred tax liability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A valuation allowance reduces a DTA when it's more likely than not (>50% probability) that some or all of it won't be realized. This occurs when future taxable income may be insufficient to utilize the tax benefit. Analysts view valuation allowances as signals about management's expectations for future profitability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A affects DTA value but not the need for allowance
- Option C doesn't determine realizability
