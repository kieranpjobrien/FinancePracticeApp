# Question

Interim (quarterly) financial reports differ from annual reports in that:

## Options

- **A**: They are not audited and may use more estimates
- **B**: They require more detailed disclosures
- **C**: They must use different accounting policies

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Interim reports are unaudited (though may be reviewed) and rely more heavily on estimates and allocations (tax rate, seasonal revenues). The integral view treats the quarter as part of the annual period; the discrete view treats it independently. Notes are condensed. Seasonality disclosure is required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is reversed—interim disclosures are condensed
- Option C is incorrect—same accounting policies apply
