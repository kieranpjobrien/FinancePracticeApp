# Question

A capital expenditure ratio (capex/depreciation) consistently below 1.0 suggests:

## Options

- **A**: The company is aggressively expanding
- **B**: The company may be underinvesting in asset replacement
- **C**: Depreciation expense is understated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capex/depreciation below 1.0 means capital spending is less than depreciation—the asset base is shrinking in real terms. This may indicate: mature business with declining needs, cost cutting, underinvestment, or shift to asset-light model. Sustained underinvestment can impair future competitiveness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would show ratio well above 1.0
- Option C would increase the ratio, not explain low capex
