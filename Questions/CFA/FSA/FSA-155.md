# Question

Which cash flow is classified as operating activities under US GAAP?

## Options

- **A**: Dividends paid to shareholders
- **B**: Interest paid to bondholders
- **C**: Proceeds from stock issuance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under US GAAP, interest paid is classified as an operating activity (despite being a financing cost) because it's deducted in calculating net income. Dividends paid are financing activities (return to shareholders). Stock issuance proceeds are financing activities. IFRS allows more flexibility in classifying interest.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is a financing activity
- Option C is a financing activity
