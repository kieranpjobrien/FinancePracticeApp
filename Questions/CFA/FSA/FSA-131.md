# Question

IFRS standards are developed by the:

## Options

- **A**: Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB)
- **B**: International Accounting Standards Board (IASB)
- **C**: Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The IASB (International Accounting Standards Board) develops and issues IFRS. It's an independent, private-sector body based in London. FASB sets US GAAP; the SEC is a US regulator that enforces GAAP but doesn't write standards. The IASB works toward global accounting convergence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the US standard-setter
- Option C is a regulator, not a standard-setter
