# Question

The quick ratio differs from the current ratio by excluding:

## Options

- **A**: Cash and cash equivalents
- **B**: Inventory and prepaid expenses
- **C**: Accounts receivable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quick ratio = (Current assets - Inventory - Prepaids) / Current liabilities, or equivalently (Cash + Marketable securities + Receivables) / Current liabilities. It excludes inventory (may not sell quickly) and prepaids (can't be converted to cash). The quick ratio is a more stringent liquidity measure than the current ratio.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would exclude the most liquid asset
- Option C would exclude a relatively liquid current asset
