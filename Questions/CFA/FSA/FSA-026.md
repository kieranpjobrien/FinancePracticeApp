# Question

Under the indirect method, an increase in accounts payable is:

## Options

- **A**: Added to net income to calculate operating cash flow
- **B**: Subtracted from net income to calculate operating cash flow
- **C**: Reported in financing activities

## Answer

A

## Explanation

An increase in accounts payable means the company received goods/services but hasn't paid cash yet—expenses were recorded but cash was conserved. This represents a source of cash not reflected in net income, so payable increases are added. The company effectively "borrowed" from suppliers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B reverses the adjustment
- Option C misclassifies payables as financing
