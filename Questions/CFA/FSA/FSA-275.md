# Question

Non-deductible expenses in the tax reconciliation:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the effective tax rate above statutory rate
- **B**: Decrease the effective tax rate below statutory rate
- **C**: Create deferred tax assets

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Non-deductible expenses (fines, penalties, certain entertainment, goodwill amortization) reduce accounting income but aren't tax deductible. Taxable income exceeds pretax book income, resulting in higher taxes relative to book income and an effective rate above statutory. These are permanent differences with no deferred tax effect.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes tax-exempt income or credits
- Option C confuses permanent differences with temporary differences
