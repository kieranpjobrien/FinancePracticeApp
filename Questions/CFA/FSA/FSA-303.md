# Question

A company should report revenue on a gross basis (as principal) rather than net (as agent) when it:

## Options

- **A**: Acts only as an intermediary passing goods to customers
- **B**: Controls the goods or services before transfer to the customer
- **C**: Never takes title to inventory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Principal vs. agent determination depends on control before transfer. Principals control the goods/services and recognize gross revenue and COGS. Agents facilitate transactions, recognizing only commission revenue (net). Indicators include inventory risk, pricing discretion, and credit risk. This significantly affects revenue and margins.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes agent characteristics warranting net reporting
- Option C suggests agent status, though title isn't the only factor
