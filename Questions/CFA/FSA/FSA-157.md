# Question

The current portion of long-term debt is classified as a current liability because:

## Options

- **A**: The interest rate is variable
- **B**: The principal amount is due within one year
- **C**: The debt is secured by current assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The portion of long-term debt principal due within one year (or operating cycle) is reclassified from non-current to current liabilities. This reflects the near-term cash outflow required to service the debt. The remaining principal due after one year stays in non-current liabilities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relates to interest rate risk, not classification
- Option C relates to security/collateral, not maturity timing
