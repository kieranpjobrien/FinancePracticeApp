# Question

When comparing a company to its peers, an analyst should adjust for differences in:

## Options

- **A**: Stock price only
- **B**: Accounting methods, business models, and capital structure
- **C**: Management compensation only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Peer comparison requires adjusting for: accounting differences (lease treatment, depreciation methods, revenue recognition), business model variations (product mix, geographic exposure), and capital structure (leverage levels). Without adjustments, ratios may not be comparable. Stock price and compensation are less relevant for operational comparisons.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is relevant for valuation, not operational analysis
- Option C is too narrow—many factors affect comparability
