# Question

An intangible asset with an indefinite useful life is:

## Options

- **A**: Amortized over 40 years
- **B**: Not amortized but tested annually for impairment
- **C**: Written off immediately as an expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Indefinite-life intangibles (like certain trademarks and brands) are not amortized because their useful life cannot be determined. Instead, they are tested for impairment at least annually. Finite-life intangibles are amortized over their useful lives. Goodwill is also not amortized but tested for impairment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A was an old rule before current standards
- Option C would only apply if there's no future economic benefit
