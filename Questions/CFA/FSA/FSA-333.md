# Question

An exchange of assets with commercial substance is recorded at:

## Options

- **A**: Book value of asset given up
- **B**: Fair value, with gain or loss recognized
- **C**: No value until cash is received

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commercial substance means the exchange changes future cash flows. The new asset is recorded at fair value (usually of asset given up), with gain/loss recognized on the disposed asset. Without commercial substance (similar productive assets), book value is used and no gain is recognized.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to exchanges lacking commercial substance
- Option C ignores that asset exchanges are recognizable transactions
