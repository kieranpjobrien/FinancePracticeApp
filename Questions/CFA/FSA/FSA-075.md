# Question

An unqualified (clean) audit opinion indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: The auditor found material errors in the financial statements
- **B**: The financial statements present fairly in all material respects
- **C**: The company will definitely be profitable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An unqualified opinion is the standard "clean" opinion indicating financial statements are presented fairly in accordance with the applicable accounting framework (GAAP or IFRS). It provides reasonable (not absolute) assurance. A qualified opinion indicates exceptions; adverse opinion indicates material misstatement; disclaimer indicates inability to opine.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would result in a qualified or adverse opinion
- Option C misunderstands—audits verify presentation, not predict performance
