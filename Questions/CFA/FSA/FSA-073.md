# Question

Channel stuffing refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Delaying shipments to smooth revenue across periods
- **B**: Inducing customers to purchase more than needed to inflate current sales
- **C**: Recognizing revenue on consignment sales prematurely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Channel stuffing pushes excess inventory to customers or distributors (often with incentives or extended terms) to boost current-period revenue. The practice borrows from future sales, creating unsustainable patterns. Signs include spikes in quarter-end sales, rising receivables, and subsequent returns or writeoffs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes revenue smoothing in the opposite direction
- Option C describes a different revenue recognition issue
