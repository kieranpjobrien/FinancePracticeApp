# Question

A "big bath" accounting strategy involves:

## Options

- **A**: Spreading losses evenly across multiple periods
- **B**: Taking excessive charges in one period to improve future earnings
- **C**: Capitalizing expenses to avoid current-period losses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Big bath accounting concentrates charges (write-offs, restructuring costs, impairments) into one "bad" period, often during management changes or already-poor quarters. This creates reserves that can be released later to boost future earnings. While individual charges may be legitimate, the magnitude and timing may be manipulated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes smoothing, the opposite strategy
- Option C describes aggressive capitalization
