# Question

A tax loss carryforward creates:

## Options

- **A**: A deferred tax liability
- **B**: A deferred tax asset
- **C**: Immediate cash tax refund

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tax losses that can be carried forward to offset future taxable income create deferred tax assets. The benefit is realized when future profits generate tax savings. A valuation allowance may be required if realization is uncertain (more likely than not that some portion won't be realized).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses assets and liabilities
- Option C applies only to carrybacks (where available)
