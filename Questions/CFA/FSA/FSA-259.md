# Question

"Cookie jar reserves" refer to:

## Options

- **A**: Cash reserves held for emergencies
- **B**: Excessive accruals created in good years to boost earnings in bad years
- **C**: Legal reserves required by regulators

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cookie jar reserves involve overstating expenses or reserves in strong quarters (creating hidden reserves) and reversing them in weak quarters to smooth or boost earnings. Examples: excessive bad debt allowances, inflated restructuring reserves. This violates the matching principle and distorts economic reality.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses with liquidity management
- Option C confuses with regulatory capital requirements
