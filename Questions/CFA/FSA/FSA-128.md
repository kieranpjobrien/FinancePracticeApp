# Question

When analyzing earnings quality, restructuring charges should be:

## Options

- **A**: Always added back to earnings as they are non-recurring
- **B**: Evaluated for frequency—if recurring, treated as operating expense
- **C**: Ignored as they have no cash flow impact

## Answer

B

## Explanation

While restructuring charges are often presented as one-time, many companies report them repeatedly. Analysts should examine the frequency and nature of such charges. If "non-recurring" items appear regularly, they may represent ongoing operating costs and should be included in normalized earnings. Persistent restructuring suggests continuous operational issues.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that some companies restructure continuously
- Option C is incorrect—restructuring often involves cash costs
