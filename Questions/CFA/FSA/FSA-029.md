# Question

Under IFRS, interest paid may be classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating or financing activities
- **B**: Operating activities only
- **C**: Financing activities only

## Answer

A

## Explanation

IFRS allows flexibility: interest paid can be classified as operating (relates to expenses) or financing (relates to debt). Similarly, interest and dividends received can be operating or investing. US GAAP is more restrictive: interest paid and received must be operating activities. This IFRS flexibility can affect cash flow comparability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes US GAAP treatment
- Option C is too restrictive under IFRS
