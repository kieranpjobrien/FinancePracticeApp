# Question

An asset retirement obligation (ARO) is recorded when:

## Options

- **A**: An asset is fully depreciated
- **B**: A legal obligation exists to restore a site after asset retirement
- **C**: Management decides to dispose of an asset

## Answer

B

## Explanation

AROs are recognized when a legal or constructive obligation exists to dismantle, remove, or restore a long-lived asset at the end of its useful life. Common examples include oil well decommissioning, mine reclamation, and nuclear plant retirement. The liability is recorded at present value when the asset is acquired.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes depreciation completion, not obligation recognition
- Option C describes disposal intent, not legal obligation
