# Question

Reclassifying operating expenses as non-operating items would:

## Options

- **A**: Increase operating income without affecting net income
- **B**: Decrease operating income without affecting net income
- **C**: Have no effect on any income measures

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Moving expenses from operating to non-operating (e.g., treating recurring costs as "one-time") inflates operating income and operating margin, making core operations appear more profitable. Net income is unchanged since the expense is still recorded. Analysts should scrutinize such reclassifications for potential manipulation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B reverses the effect
- Option C ignores the impact on subtotals
