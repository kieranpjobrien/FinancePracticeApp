# Question

A LIFO liquidation occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A company switches from LIFO to FIFO accounting
- **B**: Old, low-cost inventory layers are sold, reducing COGS
- **C**: Inventory is written down due to obsolescence

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LIFO liquidation happens when a company sells more units than it purchases, dipping into older, lower-cost inventory layers. This artificially reduces COGS and inflates gross profit—a one-time boost not reflecting current economics. Analysts view LIFO liquidation profits as lower quality earnings because they're non-recurring and don't reflect current replacement costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an accounting method change
- Option C describes inventory impairment
