# Question

Cash paid to acquire a subsidiary is classified on the cash flow statement as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating activity
- **B**: Investing activity
- **C**: Financing activity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Acquiring a subsidiary is an investing activity—it's a long-term investment in productive capacity. The cash paid (net of any cash acquired) appears in CFI. Operating activities involve the company's core business. Financing activities involve raising capital from investors and creditors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses acquisitions with operational purchases
- Option C confuses investment with capital structure changes
