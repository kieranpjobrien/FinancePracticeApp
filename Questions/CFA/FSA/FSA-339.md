# Question

Which pattern is a warning sign for potential revenue manipulation?

## Options

- **A**: Revenue growth matching GDP growth
- **B**: Large fourth-quarter revenue concentration with subsequent reversals
- **C**: Declining revenue in a declining industry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Revenue spikes in Q4 (to meet annual targets) followed by Q1 reversals or adjustments suggest premature recognition or channel stuffing. Analysts should examine revenue patterns for seasonality, compare to industry norms, and investigate unusual quarterly distributions. This pattern indicates potential earnings management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A indicates normal, sustainable growth
- Option C reflects economic reality, not manipulation
