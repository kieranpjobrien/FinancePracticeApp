# Question

A company has revenue of $2 million, COGS of $1.2 million, and operating expenses of $400,000. Its operating profit margin is:

## Options

- **A**: 20%
- **B**: 40%
- **C**: 60%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Operating profit = Revenue - COGS - Operating expenses = $2M - $1.2M - $0.4M = $0.4M. Operating margin = Operating profit / Revenue = $0.4M / $2M = 20%. This measures the percentage of revenue remaining after all operating costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is the gross margin ($0.8M / $2M)
- Option C calculates COGS/Revenue incorrectly
