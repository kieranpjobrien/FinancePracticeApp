# Question

For a lessor, an operating lease results in:

## Options

- **A**: Removing the leased asset from the balance sheet
- **B**: Continued recognition of the asset with rental income over the lease term
- **C**: Recording a lease receivable and unearned income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under an operating lease, the lessor retains the asset on its balance sheet, continues depreciating it, and recognizes rental income (usually straight-line) over the lease term. This differs from finance/sales-type leases where the lessor derecognizes the asset and records a receivable and potentially a profit at inception.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes finance/sales-type lease treatment
- Option C describes finance lease lessor accounting
