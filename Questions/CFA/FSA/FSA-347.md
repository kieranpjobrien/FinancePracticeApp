# Question

A healthy, growing company typically shows:

## Options

- **A**: Negative CFO and positive CFI
- **B**: Positive CFO and negative CFI
- **C**: Negative CFO and negative CFI

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Healthy growth means generating cash from operations (positive CFO) and investing in expansion (negative CFI—cash outflows for capex, acquisitions). Mature companies may have positive CFI (divestitures). Negative CFO with negative CFI (burning cash while investing) is concerning unless early-stage with strong funding.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A suggests operational problems with asset sales
- Option C indicates cash burn on both fronts—unsustainable
