# Question

Which of the following is least likely a warning sign of aggressive accounting?

## Options

- **A**: Frequent changes in accounting policies or estimates
- **B**: Cash flow from operations consistently exceeding net income
- **C**: Fourth-quarter adjustments that boost annual earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash flow exceeding net income indicates high-quality, cash-backed earnings—the opposite of a warning sign. Warning signs include: policy changes that boost income, large quarter-end adjustments, growing receivables outpacing sales, unusual related-party transactions, and frequent "one-time" charges.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is a warning sign (possible manipulation)
- Option C is a warning sign (earnings management)
