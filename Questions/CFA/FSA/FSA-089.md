# Question

An investment in equity securities classified as "fair value through profit or loss" is:

## Options

- **A**: Reported at amortized cost with no fair value adjustments
- **B**: Reported at fair value with unrealized gains/losses in net income
- **C**: Reported at fair value with unrealized gains/losses in OCI

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Securities at fair value through profit or loss (FVPL, or "trading securities" under US GAAP) are marked to fair value each period, with unrealized gains and losses recognized in net income. This treatment applies to securities held for active trading or designated at FVPL upon acquisition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes amortized cost securities (certain debt)
- Option C describes fair value through OCI (available-for-sale)
