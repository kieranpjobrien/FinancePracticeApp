# Question

For a long-term construction contract where revenue is recognized over time, the percentage of completion is typically determined by:

## Options

- **A**: The ratio of contract billings to total contract price
- **B**: The ratio of costs incurred to estimated total costs
- **C**: Management's subjective judgment each period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The input method uses costs incurred to date divided by estimated total costs to measure progress. This percentage is applied to total expected revenue to determine revenue recognized. Output methods (units delivered, milestones achieved) are alternatives. Reliable estimates of progress are essential for over-time recognition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses billings, which may not reflect actual progress
- Option C lacks the objective basis required for recognition
