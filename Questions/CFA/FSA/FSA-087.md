# Question

Stock-based compensation expense is recognized:

## Options

- **A**: Only when options are exercised
- **B**: Over the vesting period based on fair value at grant date
- **C**: As a reduction of cash flow from operations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock compensation is expensed over the service (vesting) period at the grant-date fair value. This matches the expense to the period employees provide service. The expense is non-cash, so it's added back when reconciling net income to operating cash flow. It reduces net income and increases equity (additional paid-in capital).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes old intrinsic value method (no longer permitted for expense)
- Option C misunderstands—it's a non-cash expense added back to cash flow
