# Question

A company has current assets of $800,000 and current liabilities of $500,000. Its working capital is:

## Options

- **A**: $300,000
- **B**: $500,000
- **C**: $1,300,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Working capital = Current assets - Current liabilities = $800,000 - $500,000 = $300,000. Positive working capital indicates the company can cover short-term obligations with short-term assets. Working capital is a key measure of short-term liquidity and operational efficiency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses only current liabilities
- Option C adds instead of subtracts
