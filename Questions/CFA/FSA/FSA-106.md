# Question

The notes to financial statements are important because they:

## Options

- **A**: Replace the primary financial statements
- **B**: Provide additional detail and explain accounting policies
- **C**: Are optional disclosures not required by standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Notes are an integral part of financial statements, providing: accounting policies used, details behind line items, contingent liabilities, subsequent events, related party transactions, and other required disclosures. They supplement—not replace—the face statements and are mandatory under both IFRS and US GAAP.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—notes supplement, not replace
- Option C is incorrect—notes are required disclosures
