# Question

When calculating diluted EPS, stock options are included using the:

## Options

- **A**: If-converted method
- **B**: Treasury stock method
- **C**: Fair value method

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The treasury stock method assumes options are exercised, proceeds are used to repurchase shares at average market price, and only the net incremental shares are added to the denominator. In-the-money options (exercise price < market price) are dilutive. The if-converted method applies to convertible securities, not options.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to convertible bonds and preferred stock
- Option C is used for valuing options for compensation expense, not EPS
