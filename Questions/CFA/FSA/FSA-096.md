# Question

Under the current rate method for translating foreign subsidiary statements, a cumulative translation adjustment (CTA) arises and is reported in:

## Options

- **A**: Net income on the income statement
- **B**: Other comprehensive income (accumulated in equity)
- **C**: Cash flow from financing activities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The current rate method translates assets/liabilities at period-end rates and income/expenses at average rates. Because equity uses historical rates, a translation adjustment arises to balance the accounting equation. This CTA is reported in OCI and accumulated in equity until the subsidiary is sold, when it's recycled to net income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to gains/losses under the temporal method
- Option C confuses translation with cash flow
