# Question

Goodwill impairment testing compares:

## Options

- **A**: Goodwill carrying amount to its fair value directly
- **B**: Reporting unit carrying amount to its fair value
- **C**: Purchase price to current market capitalization

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Goodwill is tested at the reporting unit level (operating segment or component). If carrying amount > fair value, impairment equals the excess, limited to goodwill balance. Prior two-step approach (comparing implied goodwill fair value) was simplified. Testing is required annually and when triggering events occur.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A was never the approach—goodwill isn't tested in isolation
- Option C is too high-level and doesn't match accounting requirements
