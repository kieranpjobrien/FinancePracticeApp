# Question

A capital-intensive industry typically has:

## Options

- **A**: High asset turnover and low fixed asset ratio
- **B**: Low asset turnover and high fixed asset ratio
- **C**: Minimal depreciation expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital-intensive industries (utilities, manufacturing, airlines) require large fixed asset investments relative to sales, resulting in high fixed assets as percentage of total assets and lower asset turnover. They also have high depreciation expense and operating leverage. Service industries tend toward the opposite.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes asset-light businesses
- Option C contradicts high fixed assets—depreciation would be substantial
