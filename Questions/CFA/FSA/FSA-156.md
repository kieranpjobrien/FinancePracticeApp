# Question

A ratio of operating cash flow to net income significantly greater than 1.0 generally indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Aggressive revenue recognition
- **B**: High-quality, cash-backed earnings
- **C**: Excessive working capital investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CFO/Net income > 1.0 means cash generated exceeds accrual earnings—typically a sign of high-quality earnings. This can occur with large non-cash expenses (depreciation), declining receivables, or decreasing inventory. Conversely, ratio < 1.0 may signal accrual-heavy earnings that aren't backed by cash.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would typically produce ratio < 1.0
- Option C would typically produce ratio < 1.0
