# Question

Under US GAAP, a company may not recognize a deferred tax liability for undistributed foreign earnings when:

## Options

- **A**: Earnings will be repatriated within one year
- **B**: The company asserts indefinite reinvestment of the earnings abroad
- **C**: Foreign tax rates exceed domestic rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The indefinite reversal exception allows companies to avoid recognizing DTLs on undistributed foreign earnings if they demonstrate intent and ability to indefinitely reinvest earnings abroad. This can significantly understate tax liabilities. Changes in assertion (repatriation plans) require recognizing the previously unrecognized DTL.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would require DTL recognition since reversal is expected
- Option C doesn't create an exception—foreign tax credits are a separate issue
