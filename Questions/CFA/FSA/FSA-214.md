# Question

Assets held for sale are:

## Options

- **A**: Depreciated at an accelerated rate
- **B**: Not depreciated and carried at lower of carrying amount or fair value less costs to sell
- **C**: Reclassified as inventory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When management commits to sell an asset and sale is probable within one year, the asset is classified as held for sale. Depreciation stops, and it's measured at the lower of carrying amount or fair value less selling costs. Any impairment is recognized. The asset is presented separately on the balance sheet.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—depreciation ceases
- Option C confuses disposal of assets with operating inventory
