# Question

A cost flow assumption is needed because:

## Options

- **A**: All inventory items have different physical characteristics
- **B**: Identical units may have been purchased at different costs
- **C**: Inventory is always valued at selling price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When identical goods are purchased at different times and prices, a cost flow assumption determines which costs attach to COGS versus ending inventory. FIFO, LIFO, and weighted average are assumptions—physical flow may differ. Specific identification tracks actual costs for unique items.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes heterogeneous inventory using specific ID
- Option C is incorrect—inventory is valued at cost (or lower)
