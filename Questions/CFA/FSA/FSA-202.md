# Question

The going concern assumption means that:

## Options

- **A**: Assets should be valued at liquidation value
- **B**: The company is expected to continue operating for the foreseeable future
- **C**: All liabilities will be paid within one year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Going concern assumes the entity will continue operations indefinitely, justifying valuation methods like historical cost and depreciation. Without this assumption, assets might be valued at liquidation values. Auditors must assess going concern and express doubt if significant uncertainty exists about the entity's ability to continue.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the approach when going concern is NOT appropriate
- Option C confuses going concern with liquidity
