# Question

Return on invested capital (ROIC) is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Net income divided by total equity
- **B**: NOPAT divided by invested capital
- **C**: EBIT divided by total assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ROIC = NOPAT (Net Operating Profit After Tax) / Invested Capital. Invested capital = Total debt + Equity - Cash (or Net assets). ROIC measures returns on capital from all providers, removing the effects of capital structure. It's useful for comparing companies with different leverage levels.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes ROE
- Option C is closer to operating ROA but doesn't precisely match ROIC
