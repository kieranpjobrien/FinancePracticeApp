# Question

A company has sales of $1,000,000, beginning accounts receivable of $80,000, and ending accounts receivable of $100,000. Cash collected from customers is:

## Options

- **A**: $980,000
- **B**: $1,000,000
- **C**: $1,020,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cash collected = Sales - Increase in receivables = $1,000,000 - ($100,000 - $80,000) = $1,000,000 - $20,000 = $980,000. The receivables increase means $20,000 of sales haven't been collected yet. Alternatively: Beginning AR + Sales - Cash collected = Ending AR.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B ignores the change in receivables
- Option C adds the receivable increase instead of subtracting
