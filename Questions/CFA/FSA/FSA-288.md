# Question

When analyzing a company that made significant acquisitions, an analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Exclude all acquisition effects from the analysis
- **B**: Adjust for acquired working capital and consider organic vs. acquired growth
- **C**: Only examine post-acquisition financial statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Acquisitions distort trend analysis and ratios. Analysts should: separate organic from acquired growth, adjust working capital comparisons for acquired balances, consider integration costs, and evaluate whether acquisition-related revenue is sustainable. Simply excluding acquisitions or only using post-acquisition data loses valuable context.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A loses important information about the business
- Option C ignores historical comparability issues
