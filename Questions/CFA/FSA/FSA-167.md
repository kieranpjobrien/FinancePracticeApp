# Question

A company has CFO of $80 million, capital expenditures of $25 million, and net borrowings of $15 million. Free cash flow to the firm (FCFF) using CFO is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $40 million
- **B**: $55 million
- **C**: $70 million

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FCFF from CFO = CFO + Interest expense × (1 - Tax rate) - Fixed capital investment. Without interest adjustment given, FCFF ≈ CFO - CapEx = $80M - $25M = $55M. Net borrowings are financing activities and don't reduce FCFF. FCFF represents cash available to all capital providers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly subtracts net borrowings
- Option C ignores capital expenditures
