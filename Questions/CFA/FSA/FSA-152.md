# Question

Activity ratios primarily measure:

## Options

- **A**: A company's ability to meet short-term obligations
- **B**: How efficiently a company uses its assets
- **C**: The proportion of debt in the capital structure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Activity (turnover/efficiency) ratios measure how effectively a company uses its assets to generate revenue. Examples include inventory turnover, receivables turnover, asset turnover, and working capital turnover. Higher turnover generally indicates more efficient asset use.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes liquidity ratios
- Option C describes leverage/solvency ratios
