# Question

Other comprehensive income (OCI) includes:

## Options

- **A**: Operating expenses not included in cost of goods sold
- **B**: Items that affect equity but bypass the income statement
- **C**: Cash flows from financing activities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

OCI captures gains and losses that affect shareholders' equity but are excluded from net income. Examples include: unrealized gains/losses on available-for-sale securities, foreign currency translation adjustments, pension liability adjustments, and cash flow hedge gains/losses. OCI plus net income equals comprehensive income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes operating expenses, which are on the income statement
- Option C describes cash flow statement items
