# Question

In a common-size income statement, all items are expressed as a percentage of:

## Options

- **A**: Total assets
- **B**: Revenue (sales)
- **C**: Net income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Common-size income statements express each line item as a percentage of revenue, enabling comparison across companies of different sizes and over time. For example, if COGS is 60% of revenue, it shows that 60 cents of every sales dollar goes to direct costs. Common-size balance sheets use total assets as the base.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the base for common-size balance sheets
- Option C would make the statement less useful for analysis
