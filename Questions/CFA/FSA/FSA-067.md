# Question

In a defined benefit pension plan, the investment risk is borne by:

## Options

- **A**: The employees
- **B**: The employer
- **C**: A third-party insurance company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Defined benefit plans promise specific retirement benefits based on salary and service years. The employer bears the investment risk—if plan assets underperform, the company must contribute more. In defined contribution plans (like 401k), employees bear investment risk as their benefits depend on investment returns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes defined contribution plans
- Option C may be true for insured plans but isn't the general case
