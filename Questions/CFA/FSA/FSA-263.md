# Question

Under current accounting standards, unusual or infrequent items are:

## Options

- **A**: Reported net of tax below income from continuing operations
- **B**: Reported separately within continuing operations with disclosure
- **C**: Excluded from the income statement entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

After eliminating the "extraordinary items" category, unusual or infrequent items are reported as part of continuing operations but may be shown separately or disclosed. This prevents companies from burying unfavorable items while maintaining a cleaner income statement structure. Analysts must identify and assess these items.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes discontinued operations, not unusual items
- Option C would hide important information from users
