# Question

The direct method of presenting operating cash flows:

## Options

- **A**: Starts with net income and adjusts for non-cash items
- **B**: Shows major classes of gross cash receipts and payments
- **C**: Is required under both IFRS and US GAAP

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The direct method presents operating cash flows by showing cash received from customers, cash paid to suppliers, cash paid for wages, etc.—the actual cash transactions. While more informative, it's less commonly used. The indirect method starts with net income and adjusts for non-cash items. Both standards encourage but don't require the direct method.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the indirect method
- Option C is incorrect—the direct method is encouraged but not required
