# Question

A company should report revenue on a gross basis (principal) rather than net (agent) when it:

## Options

- **A**: Has no inventory risk and receives commission
- **B**: Controls the goods or services before transfer to the customer
- **C**: Acts purely as an intermediary between buyer and seller

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Companies report gross revenue when they control the goods/services before transfer—bearing inventory risk, setting prices, and having primary fulfillment responsibility. Agents (who don't control goods) report only commission as revenue. Gross vs. net significantly affects revenue but not profit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an agent (net reporting)
- Option C describes an agent (net reporting)
