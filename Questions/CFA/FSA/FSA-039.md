# Question

The cash conversion cycle is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: DSO + DIO - DPO
- **B**: DSO + DIO + DPO
- **C**: DSO - DIO + DPO

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cash conversion cycle = Days sales outstanding + Days inventory outstanding - Days payables outstanding. It measures the time between paying suppliers and receiving cash from customers. A shorter cycle means less working capital financing is needed. DPO is subtracted because supplier credit reduces the financing period.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B adds DPO instead of subtracting (payables reduce the cycle)
- Option C has the signs reversed
