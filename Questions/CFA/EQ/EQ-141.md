# Question

In the decline stage of the industry lifecycle, companies typically focus on:

## Options

- **A**: Aggressive expansion and market share growth
- **B**: Cost management, harvesting cash flows, and potential consolidation or exit
- **C**: Heavy R&D investment to develop new products

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Decline stage: shrinking demand due to substitution, technology changes, or shifting preferences. Strategies: cost cutting, harvesting remaining profits, consolidating (buying distressed competitors), or exiting via sale/liquidation. Limited investment makes sense given declining future prospects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is inappropriate when market is shrinking
- Option C rarely justified given declining demand
