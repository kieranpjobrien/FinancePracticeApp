# Question

The price-to-sales (P/S) ratio is useful because:

## Options

- **A**: Sales are always positive, unlike earnings
- **B**: Sales are never affected by accounting choices
- **C**: It captures the company's profitability directly

## Answer

A

## Explanation

P/S = Price / Sales per share. Advantage: sales are almost always positive, available even when earnings are negative. Useful for unprofitable or cyclical companies. Limitation: ignores profitability—high sales mean little if company can't profit. Sales also harder to manipulate than earnings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B overstates—revenue recognition choices affect sales
- Option C is opposite—P/S ignores profitability
