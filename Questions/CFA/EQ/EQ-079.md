# Question

A Level III sponsored ADR differs from Level I in that Level III:

## Options

- **A**: Has fewer disclosure requirements
- **B**: Can raise capital in U.S. markets and requires full SEC registration
- **C**: Trades only on OTC markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ADR levels: Level I—OTC trading, minimal SEC requirements, no capital raising. Level II—exchange listed, requires SEC registration but no capital raising. Level III—exchange listed, full SEC registration, can issue new shares and raise capital in U.S. Highest disclosure but greatest access to capital.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Level I
- Option C also describes Level I
