# Question

Fundamentally-weighted indices weight constituents based on:

## Options

- **A**: Stock price alone
- **B**: Fundamental measures like revenue, earnings, book value, or dividends
- **C**: Equal weights for all stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fundamental weighting: uses company fundamentals rather than market cap. Common factors: sales, earnings, book value, dividends, cash flow. Avoids market-cap weighting's tendency to overweight overvalued stocks. Part of "smart beta" strategies. Examples: RAFI indices. Requires periodic rebalancing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes price-weighted indices (like Dow Jones)
- Option C describes equal-weighted indices
