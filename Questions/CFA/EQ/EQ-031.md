# Question

During the growth stage of the industry lifecycle:

## Options

- **A**: Industry sales are declining
- **B**: Sales growth accelerates, new entrants appear, and product features improve
- **C**: There are very few competitors and minimal revenue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Growth stage: demand expands rapidly, products gain acceptance, new competitors enter. Companies invest heavily in capacity and marketing. Profitability may vary as competition intensifies. Follows embryonic stage; precedes shakeout. Characterized by increasing revenues and product refinement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes decline stage
- Option C describes embryonic/pioneer stage
