# Question

Forward P/E multiples are often preferred over trailing P/E because:

## Options

- **A**: Historical earnings perfectly predict future performance
- **B**: Investors are buying future earnings, and forward P/E reflects expected performance
- **C**: Trailing P/E is always unavailable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward P/E: uses expected next 12 months earnings. More forward-looking, relevant for pricing future cash flows. Trailing P/E useful when earnings stable or forecasts unreliable. Most institutional investors focus on forward multiples. Analyst consensus provides forward estimates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—history doesn't perfectly predict future
- Option C—trailing P/E is always calculable from past earnings
