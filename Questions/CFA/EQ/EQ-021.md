# Question

A multi-stage dividend discount model is most appropriate for valuing:

## Options

- **A**: Mature companies with stable dividend growth
- **B**: Companies expected to have different growth rates in different periods
- **C**: Companies that don't pay dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Multi-stage DDM: accommodates varying growth rates—typically high growth initially, transitioning to stable growth. Common: two-stage (high growth then stable) or three-stage (growth, transition, mature). More realistic for growth companies. Requires estimating when growth transitions occur.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A can use simpler Gordon Growth Model
- Option C cannot use any dividend model directly
