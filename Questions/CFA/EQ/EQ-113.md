# Question

A high industry concentration ratio (e.g., four-firm concentration of 80%) suggests:

## Options

- **A**: Perfect competition with many small players
- **B**: An oligopolistic structure where few firms dominate the market
- **C**: Easy entry for new competitors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Concentration ratio: market share held by largest firms. High concentration (oligopoly): few dominant players, potential for coordination, barriers to entry. May have pricing power. Low concentration: fragmented market, intense competition. Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) provides more nuanced concentration measure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes low concentration industries
- Option C contradicts typical oligopoly characteristics
