# Question

Securities regulations primarily aim to:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee investment returns
- **B**: Protect investors through disclosure requirements and fair market practices
- **C**: Set stock prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Securities regulation goals: investor protection through disclosure (inform investors), prevention of fraud and manipulation, fair and orderly markets, capital formation. SEC in US, FCA in UK, etc. Regulations cover: registration, disclosure, insider trading, market manipulation, broker-dealer conduct.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—regulations don't guarantee returns
- Option C—markets set prices, not regulators
