# Question

Extensive related-party transactions may concern analysts because:

## Options

- **A**: They always represent illegal activity
- **B**: They may not be conducted at arm's-length terms and could benefit insiders at shareholder expense
- **C**: All public companies are prohibited from related-party transactions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Related-party concerns: transactions with insiders, affiliated entities may not reflect fair market terms. Potential for self-dealing, tunneling value from public shareholders. Requires disclosure in financial statements. Analysts should scrutinize for fairness and necessity. Not always problematic but warrants attention.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates—related party doesn't mean illegal
- Option C is wrong—they're allowed but must be disclosed
