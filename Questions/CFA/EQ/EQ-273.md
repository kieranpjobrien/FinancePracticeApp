# Question

Channel stuffing is a questionable practice where companies:

## Options

- **A**: Underreport sales to minimize taxes
- **B**: Induce distributors to purchase excess inventory, artificially boosting current sales
- **C**: Delay revenue recognition inappropriately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Channel stuffing: pushing excess inventory to distribution channels (often with incentives or extended payment terms) to inflate current period sales. Revenue recognized now, but future sales cannibalized when channel inventory normalizes. Red flags: rising DSO, inventory at distributors, quarter-end loading.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes underreporting, not inflation
- Option C describes delayed recognition, not accelerated
