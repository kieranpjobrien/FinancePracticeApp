# Question

Loss aversion in prospect theory suggests that investors:

## Options

- **A**: Feel gains and losses equally
- **B**: Feel the pain of losses more intensely than the pleasure of equivalent gains
- **C**: Prefer risky investments to safe ones

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Loss aversion: from prospect theory (Kahneman & Tversky). Losses hurt roughly twice as much as gains feel good. Leads to: holding losers too long (hoping to break even), selling winners too quickly (locking in gains), and taking inappropriate risks to avoid realizing losses.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts loss aversion
- Option C oversimplifies—loss aversion affects risk-taking in nuanced ways
