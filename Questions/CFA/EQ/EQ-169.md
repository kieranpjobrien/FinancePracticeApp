# Question

Segment reporting is most useful for analysts because it:

## Options

- **A**: Simplifies analysis by combining all business units
- **B**: Reveals profitability and growth characteristics of different business lines
- **C**: Eliminates the need to analyze consolidated statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Segment reporting: disaggregates financial data by business line or geography. Reveals different margins, growth rates, capital intensity across segments. Helps analysts value diversified companies using sum-of-parts, identify cross-subsidization, understand risk exposure. Required disclosures include revenue, profit, and assets by segment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—segment reporting disaggregates, not combines
- Option C—segment data supplements, doesn't replace consolidated analysis
