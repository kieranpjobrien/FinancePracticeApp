# Question

Underfunded pension obligations represent a risk because:

## Options

- **A**: They indicate the company has excess cash
- **B**: They are debt-like obligations that may require future cash contributions
- **C**: They always improve company valuation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pension underfunding: plan assets < projected benefit obligation. Company must make cash contributions to close gap. Affects: future cash flows, credit quality, enterprise value. Analysts often add unfunded pension to debt for leverage ratios. Market assumptions (discount rate, returns) affect calculations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—underfunding means deficit, not surplus
- Option C—underfunding is a negative, reduces value
