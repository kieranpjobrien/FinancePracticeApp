# Question

A stop-loss order to sell at $45:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantees execution at exactly $45
- **B**: Becomes a market order when the price falls to $45 or below
- **C**: Will only execute if the price rises above $45

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stop-loss sell order: triggered when price falls to or below stop price, then becomes market order. Used to limit losses or protect gains. No price guarantee—execution price may differ from stop price, especially in fast markets. Stop-limit variant specifies maximum/minimum acceptable execution price.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores potential slippage with market orders
- Option C describes the opposite—stop sells trigger on price declines
