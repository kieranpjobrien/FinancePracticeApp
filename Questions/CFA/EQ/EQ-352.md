# Question

Stock delisting from an exchange typically occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: The company's stock price increases significantly
- **B**: The company fails to meet exchange listing requirements or chooses to go private
- **C**: Trading volume is extremely high

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Delisting causes: failing minimum price requirements, market cap threshold, financial reporting violations, shareholder count minimums, or voluntary privatization (go-private transaction). Consequences: reduced liquidity, OTC trading only, investor exit difficulty. Regulatory requirements protect listed company standards.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—high price doesn't cause delisting (though splits may follow)
- Option C—high volume is positive for listing maintenance
