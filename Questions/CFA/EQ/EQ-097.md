# Question

Callable preferred stock allows:

## Options

- **A**: The holder to force redemption at any time
- **B**: The issuer to redeem shares at a specified price after a certain date
- **C**: Automatic conversion to common stock

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Callable preferred: issuer has right (not obligation) to redeem at call price after call protection period. Issuers call when interest rates fall—can refinance at lower cost. Call feature benefits issuer, so callable preferred typically offers higher yield to compensate investors for call risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes puttable preferred
- Option C describes convertible preferred features
