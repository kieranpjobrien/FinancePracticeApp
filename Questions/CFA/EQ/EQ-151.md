# Question

Cumulative voting rights benefit:

## Options

- **A**: Majority shareholders seeking to control all board seats
- **B**: Minority shareholders seeking board representation
- **C**: Preferred shareholders over common shareholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cumulative voting: shareholders can allocate all their votes (shares × seats) to one candidate. Helps minority shareholders concentrate votes to elect at least one director. Statutory voting (one vote per share per seat) favors majority shareholders who can win all seats. Cumulative voting enhances minority protection.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—cumulative voting limits majority control
- Option C confuses voting rights (common stock) with preferred stock features
