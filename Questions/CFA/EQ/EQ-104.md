# Question

Stocks in a value index are typically characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: High price-to-earnings ratios and rapid earnings growth
- **B**: Low price-to-book ratios, high dividend yields, and lower growth expectations
- **C**: No dividend payments and high capital appreciation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Value stocks: trade at low multiples relative to fundamentals (earnings, book value, cash flow). Often mature companies with stable operations. Higher dividend yields. May be temporarily out of favor or facing challenges. Value investing seeks undervalued securities relative to intrinsic worth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes growth stocks
- Option C also describes growth stock characteristics
