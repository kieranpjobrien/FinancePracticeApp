# Question

A key assumption of the Gordon Growth Model is that:

## Options

- **A**: Dividends fluctuate unpredictably year to year
- **B**: The dividend growth rate is constant and less than the required return
- **C**: The company will eventually stop paying dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gordon Growth Model assumptions: constant perpetual dividend growth rate (g), required return (r) greater than growth (r > g), company continues indefinitely. If g ≥ r, formula produces negative or infinite values. Model works best for stable, mature companies with predictable dividend policies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates constant growth assumption
- Option C contradicts the perpetual dividend assumption
