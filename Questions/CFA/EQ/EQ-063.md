# Question

Trailing P/E differs from forward P/E in that trailing P/E uses:

## Options

- **A**: Expected future earnings
- **B**: Actual past 12 months' earnings
- **C**: Projected 5-year earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trailing P/E: price / last 12 months' actual EPS. Forward P/E: price / expected next 12 months' EPS. Trailing is backward-looking but based on actual results. Forward is more relevant for valuation but relies on estimates. Analysts typically use forward P/E for valuation comparisons.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes forward P/E
- Option C is too long a horizon for standard P/E
