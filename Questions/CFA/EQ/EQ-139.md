# Question

Companies typically execute reverse stock splits to:

## Options

- **A**: Make shares more affordable for retail investors
- **B**: Increase the share price to meet exchange listing requirements or improve perception
- **C**: Increase total shares outstanding for greater liquidity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reverse split: reduces shares outstanding, increases share price proportionally. Common reasons: avoid delisting (exchanges have minimum price requirements), improve institutional investor perception (some avoid "penny stocks"), reduce transaction costs. Often viewed negatively as sign of struggling company.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is a reason for regular splits, not reverse splits
- Option C describes regular splits—reverse splits decrease shares
