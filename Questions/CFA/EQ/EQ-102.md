# Question

Standard settlement for equity trades in most developed markets is:

## Options

- **A**: Same day (T+0)
- **B**: Trade date plus two business days (T+2)
- **C**: Trade date plus one week

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T+2 settlement: standard in most developed markets. Buyer must deliver payment; seller must deliver securities within two business days after trade. Moving from T+3 improved efficiency. Some markets moving toward T+1 or even T+0. Settlement risk exists during the period.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is faster than current standard (though some markets are moving this direction)
- Option C is too long—older standard was T+5 or T+3
