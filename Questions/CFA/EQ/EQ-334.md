# Question

Regret aversion can lead investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Take appropriate risks based on expected returns
- **B**: Avoid making decisions or hold onto losing investments to avoid realizing regret
- **C**: Sell all investments immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regret aversion: fear of feeling regret from wrong decisions. Leads to inaction (regret of commission > regret of omission), holding losers (no regret until sold), herding (shared regret less painful). Conventional choices feel safer. Can cause suboptimal portfolio decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational decision-making
- Option C describes panic selling, different bias
