# Question

The two main components of DCF valuation are:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue and expenses
- **B**: Projected cash flows and the discount rate
- **C**: Assets and liabilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DCF fundamentals: (1) forecast future cash flows (FCFF, FCFE, or dividends), (2) discount at appropriate rate (WACC for FCFF, cost of equity for FCFE/dividends). Terminal value captures cash flows beyond explicit forecast period. Present value of all flows equals intrinsic value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes income statement items, not DCF inputs
- Option C describes balance sheet, not cash flow valuation
