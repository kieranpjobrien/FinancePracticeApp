# Question

Recurring restructuring charges over multiple years may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: Highly effective management executing a turnaround
- **B**: Ongoing operational problems or manipulation to exclude costs from "core" earnings
- **C**: Strong and stable business performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Recurring "non-recurring" charges: red flag. True restructuring should be one-time. Repeated charges suggest: chronic operating issues, overly optimistic initial estimates, or manipulation to shift normal costs to "special items" and inflate adjusted earnings. Analysts should add back to operating costs if persistent.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A gives too much credit—recurring charges question execution
- Option C contradicts the implication of ongoing restructuring
