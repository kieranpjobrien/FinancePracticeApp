# Question

Company earnings guidance serves to:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee future earnings performance
- **B**: Communicate management's expectations to reduce information asymmetry
- **C**: Legally bind management to specific financial targets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Earnings guidance: management's projection of future results. Reduces information gap between insiders and investors. Can include revenue, EPS, margins, capital expenditure. Forward-looking, not guarantees. Companies may lower guidance to manage expectations. Some choose not to guide to avoid short-termism.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—guidance is projection, not guarantee
- Option C—guidance is informational, not legally binding commitment
