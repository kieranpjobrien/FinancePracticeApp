# Question

A private placement differs from a public offering in that a private placement:

## Options

- **A**: Must be registered with securities regulators
- **B**: Is sold directly to a limited number of qualified investors without public registration
- **C**: Can only involve debt securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Private placement: securities sold directly to institutional or accredited investors. Avoids costly public registration and disclosure. Faster execution, lower costs. Limited resale market. Rule 144A permits trading among qualified institutional buyers (QIBs). Common for debt but also used for equity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—private placements avoid registration
- Option C is incorrect—private placements can include equity
