# Question

The momentum effect in stock returns refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Stocks with poor past performance tend to outperform in the future
- **B**: Stocks with strong past performance tend to continue outperforming over intermediate horizons
- **C**: Stock returns are completely random and unpredictable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Momentum: tendency for past winners to continue winning (and losers to continue losing) over 3-12 month periods. Jegadeesh and Titman documented this anomaly. Explanations include underreaction to news, investor herding, and slow information diffusion. Contradicts weak-form efficiency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes mean reversion or contrarian effect
- Option C describes random walk consistent with efficiency
