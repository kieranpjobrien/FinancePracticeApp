# Question

Loss aversion in behavioral finance suggests that investors:

## Options

- **A**: Experience equal psychological impact from gains and losses of the same magnitude
- **B**: Feel the pain of losses more intensely than the pleasure of equivalent gains
- **C**: Always sell losing positions immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Loss aversion (Kahneman & Tversky): losses loom larger than gains—typically 2-2.5x psychological impact. Leads to disposition effect (holding losers too long, selling winners too soon), excessive risk aversion, and reluctance to realize losses. Foundation of prospect theory.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational expected utility theory
- Option C describes opposite behavior—loss aversion causes holding losers
