# Question

Availability bias causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Seek out all relevant information before deciding
- **B**: Overweight easily recalled or recently observed information in decision-making
- **C**: Ignore all media coverage of investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Availability bias: judging probability based on ease of recall rather than actual frequency. Recent events, vivid stories, personal experience overweighted. Examples: fearing plane crashes after news coverage, buying stocks with recent media attention. Leads to trend-chasing, neglecting base rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes thorough, unbiased research
- Option C describes ignoring information, different issue
