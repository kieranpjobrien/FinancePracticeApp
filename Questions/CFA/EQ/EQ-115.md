# Question

Operating margin measures:

## Options

- **A**: Net income as a percentage of revenue
- **B**: Operating profit as a percentage of revenue
- **C**: Gross profit as a percentage of total assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operating margin = Operating income (EBIT) / Revenue. Measures profitability from core operations before interest and taxes. Higher margin indicates better operational efficiency and pricing power. Compare across competitors and over time. Excludes financing decisions and tax effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes net profit margin
- Option C mixes income statement and balance sheet items incorrectly
