# Question

Mental accounting can lead to suboptimal investment decisions because investors:

## Options

- **A**: Treat all money as completely fungible regardless of source
- **B**: Treat money differently based on its source or intended use, ignoring fungibility
- **C**: Always make perfectly rational portfolio decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mental accounting: treating money differently based on subjective categories (salary vs. bonus, retirement vs. fun money). Violates fungibility principle—all dollars have equal value. Examples: taking more risk with "found money," not consolidating accounts, treating paper gains differently than realized gains.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational view—money is fungible
- Option C contradicts behavioral finance evidence
