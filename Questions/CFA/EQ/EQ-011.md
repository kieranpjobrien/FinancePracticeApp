# Question

Fundamental-weighted indices differ from market-cap weighted indices by using:

## Options

- **A**: Stock prices as weights
- **B**: Fundamental factors like earnings, dividends, or book value as weights
- **C**: Trading volume as weights

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fundamental weighting: weights based on economic measures (sales, earnings, dividends, book value, cash flow) rather than market prices. Avoids overweighting overvalued stocks (a criticism of cap-weighting). Creates automatic contrarian rebalancing. Examples: FTSE RAFI indices. May have higher turnover than cap-weighted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes price-weighted indices
- Option C is not a standard weighting method
