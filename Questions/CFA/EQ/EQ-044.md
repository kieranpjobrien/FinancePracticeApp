# Question

In a firm commitment underwriting, the investment bank:

## Options

- **A**: Only uses best efforts to sell shares without guaranteeing proceeds
- **B**: Purchases the entire issue from the issuer and resells to the public at a higher price
- **C**: Acts only as an advisor without capital commitment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Firm commitment: underwriter buys entire issue at agreed price (less spread), bears risk of unsold shares. Spread is compensation for risk and distribution. Issuer gets guaranteed proceeds. Best efforts alternative: underwriter only commits to try selling, issuer bears unsold share risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes best efforts underwriting
- Option C understates the underwriter's role
