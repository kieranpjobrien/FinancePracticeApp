# Question

Central clearing counterparties (CCPs) reduce risk in securities trading by:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminating all market risk from trades
- **B**: Becoming the counterparty to both sides, guaranteeing trade settlement
- **C**: Setting prices for all securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CCPs: interpose themselves between buyer and seller through novation. Each party faces CCP, not each other. CCP manages counterparty risk through: margin requirements, member qualifications, default funds. Reduces systemic risk but concentrates risk at CCP. Examples: DTCC, LCH, CME Clearing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—CCPs address counterparty risk, not market risk
- Option C—CCPs handle clearing/settlement, not price discovery
