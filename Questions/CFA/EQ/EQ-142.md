# Question

The bid-ask spread represents:

## Options

- **A**: The difference between a stock's high and low price for the day
- **B**: The difference between the highest price a buyer will pay and the lowest price a seller will accept
- **C**: The commission charged by brokers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bid-ask spread: difference between bid (highest buy price) and ask/offer (lowest sell price). Represents transaction cost and market maker's compensation. Narrower spreads indicate greater liquidity. Factors affecting spread: trading volume, volatility, number of market makers, stock price level.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes daily price range, not bid-ask spread
- Option C is a separate transaction cost component
