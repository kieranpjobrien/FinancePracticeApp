# Question

The "January effect" is a market anomaly suggesting that:

## Options

- **A**: Stock returns tend to be higher in January, particularly for small-cap stocks
- **B**: Trading volume is lowest in January
- **C**: Large-cap stocks significantly outperform in January

## Answer

A

## Explanation

January effect: tendency for stock returns, especially small-cap stocks, to be abnormally high in January. Explanations include tax-loss selling in December followed by repurchases, window dressing by fund managers, and new year cash inflows. Evidence has weakened as anomaly became known.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B addresses volume, not returns
- Option C is wrong—the effect is more pronounced for small-caps
