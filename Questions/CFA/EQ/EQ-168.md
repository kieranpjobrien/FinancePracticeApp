# Question

A stock warrant differs from a call option primarily because warrants:

## Options

- **A**: Give the right to sell shares
- **B**: Are issued by the company itself and create dilution when exercised
- **C**: Have shorter maturities than options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Warrants: company-issued rights to buy stock at specified price. When exercised, company issues new shares—dilutes existing shareholders. Options are contracts between investors, don't create new shares. Warrants typically have longer terms (years vs. months for options) and are often attached to bonds or preferred stock.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes puts, not warrants
- Option C is opposite—warrants typically have longer terms
