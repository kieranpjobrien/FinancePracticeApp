# Question

Hindsight bias can harm investment decisions because investors:

## Options

- **A**: Accurately predict future events
- **B**: Believe they "knew it all along" after events occur, leading to overconfidence
- **C**: Underweight past information

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hindsight bias: tendency to believe, after an event, that one would have predicted it. "I knew the market would crash." Leads to overconfidence in forecasting ability, insufficient learning from mistakes, unfair evaluation of past decisions with benefit of hindsight.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes accurate forecasting, not bias
- Option C describes other biases, not hindsight specifically
