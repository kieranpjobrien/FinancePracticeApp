# Question

Value investing emphasizes:

## Options

- **A**: Paying premium prices for high-growth companies
- **B**: Buying stocks trading below their intrinsic value, often with low P/E or P/B ratios
- **C**: Ignoring fundamental analysis entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Value investing: seeks mispriced securities trading below intrinsic value. Characteristics: low P/E, low P/B, high dividend yield. Graham and Buffett practitioners. Requires patience—market may take time to recognize value. Margin of safety important. Contrasts with growth investing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes growth investing approach
- Option C contradicts value investing's analytical foundation
