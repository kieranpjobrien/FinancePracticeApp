# Question

Economic Value Added (EVA) is positive when a company:

## Options

- **A**: Has positive accounting earnings
- **B**: Earns returns above its weighted average cost of capital
- **C**: Pays dividends to shareholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EVA = NOPAT - (WACC × Invested Capital). Positive EVA means operating returns exceed the cost of all capital employed. Unlike accounting profit, EVA accounts for full cost of capital including equity. A company can have positive net income but negative EVA if returns don't cover capital costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—positive accounting earnings can still destroy value if below WACC
- Option C—dividends are a use of value, not creation of it
