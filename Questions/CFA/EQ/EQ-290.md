# Question

High R&D intensity (R&D/Sales) is typical of industries where:

## Options

- **A**: Products are mature commodities with little innovation
- **B**: Competitive advantage depends on continuous innovation and new product development
- **C**: Fixed assets drive competitive position

## Answer

B

## Explanation

R&D intensive industries: pharmaceuticals, technology, biotechnology, aerospace. Innovation-driven competition requires sustained investment. Expensed immediately (GAAP) creating earnings volatility. Analysts evaluate R&D efficiency (revenue per R&D dollar) and pipeline value. Lower R&D in mature/commodity industries.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes low R&D industries
- Option C describes capital-intensive but not R&D-intensive industries
