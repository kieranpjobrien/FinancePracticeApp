# Question

Two events are independent if and only if:

## Options

- **A**: P(A|B) = P(A)
- **B**: P(A and B) = P(A) + P(B)
- **C**: P(A or B) = P(A) × P(B)

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Events A and B are independent if the occurrence of one does not affect the probability of the other. Mathematically, this means P(A|B) = P(A) and P(B|A) = P(B). Equivalently, for independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B). If these conditions hold, knowing that B occurred provides no information about A.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B shows the addition rule for mutually exclusive events, not the condition for independence
- Option C reverses multiplication and addition; P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) for independence
