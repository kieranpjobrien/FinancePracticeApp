# Question

A discrete probability distribution differs from a continuous distribution in that:

## Options

- **A**: It can only take on a countable number of distinct values
- **B**: It can take any value within a range
- **C**: It always has higher variance

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A discrete distribution has outcomes that can be listed or counted—such as the number of defaults (0, 1, 2, ...) or stock price movements (up or down). A continuous distribution can take any value within a range (like returns between -100% and +∞). The binomial and Poisson are discrete; normal and lognormal are continuous.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes a continuous distribution
- Option C is incorrect; variance depends on the distribution parameters, not whether it is discrete or continuous
