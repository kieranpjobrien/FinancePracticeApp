# Question

A return distribution with positive skewness has:

## Options

- **A**: A longer left tail and mean less than median
- **B**: A longer right tail and mean greater than median
- **C**: Equal tails and mean equal to median

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Positive skewness indicates asymmetry with a longer right tail (more extreme positive values). The mean is pulled toward the longer tail, so mean > median > mode. Investment returns often exhibit positive skewness when there are occasional large gains but limited downside due to the zero-return floor.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes negative skewness
- Option C describes a symmetric (normal) distribution
