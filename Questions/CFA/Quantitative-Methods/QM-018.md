# Question

A screening model correctly identifies 90% of stocks that will outperform (true positive rate) and correctly identifies 80% of stocks that will underperform (true negative rate). If 30% of stocks actually outperform, what is the probability that a stock identified as "outperform" will actually outperform?

## Options

- **A**: 54%
- **B**: 66%
- **C**: 90%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Using Bayes' Theorem:
P(Outperform|Identified as Outperform) = P(ID as OP|OP) × P(OP) / P(ID as OP)

P(ID as OP) = P(ID as OP|OP)×P(OP) + P(ID as OP|Under)×P(Under)
P(ID as OP) = (0.90 × 0.30) + (0.20 × 0.70) = 0.27 + 0.14 = 0.41

P(OP|ID as OP) = 0.27 / 0.41 = 0.659 ≈ 66%

This demonstrates that even a good screening model can have lower precision than its sensitivity due to base rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may result from calculation errors
- Option C incorrectly assumes the true positive rate (sensitivity) equals the precision (predictive value)
