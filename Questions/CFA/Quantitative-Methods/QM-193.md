# Question

Convenience sampling is problematic because it:

## Options

- **A**: Requires too much time and resources
- **B**: May not be representative of the population
- **C**: Can only be used with small populations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convenience sampling selects easily accessible subjects (e.g., surveying nearby people, using readily available data). While efficient, it often produces samples that systematically differ from the target population, leading to biased estimates. Results may not generalize beyond the specific convenience sample. Probability sampling methods produce more reliable, representative results.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite; convenience sampling is quick and inexpensive
- Option C is incorrect; convenience sampling can be used with any population size
