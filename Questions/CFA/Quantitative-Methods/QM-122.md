# Question

Asset prices are often modeled using a lognormal distribution rather than a normal distribution because:

## Options

- **A**: Lognormal distributions allow for negative values, which is realistic for prices
- **B**: Lognormal distributions are bounded below by zero and exhibit positive skewness
- **C**: Lognormal distributions have thinner tails than normal distributions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lognormal distributions are appropriate for asset prices because: (1) prices cannot be negative, and lognormal is bounded at zero; (2) the distribution is positively skewed, reflecting that prices can rise substantially but can only fall to zero; (3) it implies that continuously compounded returns are normally distributed. If returns are normally distributed, then the price level follows a lognormal distribution.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of correct; lognormal distributions cannot have negative values
- Option C is incorrect; lognormal distributions have fatter right tails than normal distributions
