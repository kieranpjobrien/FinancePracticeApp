# Question

A stated annual rate of 6% with continuous compounding is equivalent to an effective annual rate closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 6.00%
- **B**: 6.09%
- **C**: 6.18%

## Answer

C

## Explanation

For continuous compounding:
EAR = e^r - 1
EAR = e^0.06 - 1
EAR = 1.0618 - 1 = 0.0618 = 6.18%

Continuous compounding produces the highest effective rate for a given stated rate because compounding occurs infinitely often.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the compounding effect entirely
- Option B may result from using semi-annual or quarterly compounding instead of continuous
