# Question

For a dataset with extreme outliers on the high end, the most appropriate measure of central tendency is typically:

## Options

- **A**: The arithmetic mean
- **B**: The median
- **C**: The mode

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The median is resistant to outliers because it depends only on the middle value(s) of the ordered data, not on the magnitude of extreme values. The arithmetic mean is pulled toward outliers, making it less representative of the typical value when extreme observations exist. In positively skewed distributions with high outliers, the mean exceeds the median.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is sensitive to outliers and will be distorted upward by extreme high values
- Option C identifies the most frequent value but doesn't necessarily represent central tendency and may not exist in some datasets
