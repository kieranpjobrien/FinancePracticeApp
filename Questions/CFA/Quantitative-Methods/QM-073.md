# Question

An investment returns 20% in Year 1 and -10% in Year 2. The geometric mean annual return is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 3.92%
- **B**: 5.00%
- **C**: 10.00%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The geometric mean return is calculated as:

G = [(1 + R₁) × (1 + R₂)]^(1/n) - 1
G = [(1.20) × (0.90)]^(1/2) - 1
G = [1.08]^(0.5) - 1
G = 1.0392 - 1
G = 0.0392 = 3.92%

The geometric mean accounts for compounding and is always less than or equal to the arithmetic mean.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B calculates the arithmetic mean ((20% + (-10%))/2 = 5%), which overstates the actual compound growth
- Option C averages only the absolute values, ignoring the sign of returns
