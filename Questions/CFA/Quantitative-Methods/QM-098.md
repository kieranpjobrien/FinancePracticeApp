# Question

A limitation of neural networks compared to simpler models is that:

## Options

- **A**: They cannot capture nonlinear relationships
- **B**: They are "black boxes" with limited interpretability of how predictions are made
- **C**: They require smaller datasets to train effectively

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Neural networks are powerful at capturing complex patterns but are difficult to interpret. Unlike linear regression where coefficients have clear meanings, neural network weights across multiple layers do not provide intuitive explanations for predictions. This "black box" nature can be problematic when transparency or regulatory explanation is required. Neural networks also typically need large datasets and significant computational resources.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of true; neural networks excel at capturing nonlinear relationships
- Option C is incorrect; neural networks generally require larger datasets to train effectively
