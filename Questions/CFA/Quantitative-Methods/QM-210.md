# Question

When testing a hypothesis about a population mean using a sample of 25 observations, the degrees of freedom for the t-test are:

## Options

- **A**: 24
- **B**: 25
- **C**: 26

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Degrees of freedom = n - 1 = 25 - 1 = 24

One degree of freedom is lost because we estimate the population mean using the sample mean. The degrees of freedom affect the shape of the t-distribution and the critical values used in hypothesis testing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses n instead of n-1
- Option C adds instead of subtracts one
