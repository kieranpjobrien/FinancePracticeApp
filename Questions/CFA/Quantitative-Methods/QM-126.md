# Question

Unsystematic risk (also called diversifiable risk) can be reduced by:

## Options

- **A**: Investing in a single high-quality stock
- **B**: Holding a well-diversified portfolio of many securities
- **C**: Investing only in government bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unsystematic risk is company-specific or industry-specific risk that can be reduced through diversification. By holding many different securities, the positive surprises in some holdings tend to offset negative surprises in others. Research suggests that holding approximately 20-30 uncorrelated stocks eliminates most unsystematic risk. Systematic (market) risk, however, cannot be diversified away.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A concentrates risk rather than reducing it
- Option C reduces total risk by changing asset class but doesn't specifically address diversification of equity risk
