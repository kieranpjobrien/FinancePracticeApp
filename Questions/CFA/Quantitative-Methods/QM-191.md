# Question

In the presence of heteroskedasticity, the OLS coefficient estimates are:

## Options

- **A**: Biased and inconsistent
- **B**: Unbiased but inefficient, with unreliable standard errors
- **C**: Both biased and have correct standard errors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Heteroskedasticity (non-constant error variance) does not bias OLS coefficient estimates—they remain unbiased and consistent. However, the estimates are no longer efficient (minimum variance), and the conventional standard error formulas are incorrect. This leads to invalid t-tests and confidence intervals. Robust (White) standard errors or weighted least squares can address this issue.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly states coefficients are biased; they remain unbiased
- Option C is incorrect on both counts; coefficients are unbiased but standard errors are unreliable
