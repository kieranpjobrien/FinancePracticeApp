# Question

When evaluating portfolio manager performance, the time-weighted rate of return is preferred over the money-weighted rate of return because:

## Options

- **A**: It produces higher returns, making the manager look better
- **B**: It is not affected by cash flows the manager does not control
- **C**: It requires fewer calculations and is simpler to compute

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The time-weighted rate of return (TWRR) removes the impact of cash flows (deposits and withdrawals) that are typically controlled by the client, not the manager. This makes TWRR appropriate for comparing investment manager performance since all managers are evaluated on the same basis—the returns generated from investment decisions. MWRR would unfairly penalize or reward managers for client-directed cash flow timing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; TWRR may be higher or lower than MWRR depending on the timing of cash flows
- Option C is incorrect; TWRR is actually more complex, requiring return calculations for each sub-period between cash flows
