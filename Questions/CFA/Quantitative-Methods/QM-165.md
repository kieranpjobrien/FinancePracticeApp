# Question

A stated annual interest rate of 12% with quarterly compounding means:

## Options

- **A**: The rate earned each quarter is 12%
- **B**: The rate earned each quarter is 3%
- **C**: The effective annual rate is exactly 12%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stated (nominal) annual rate is divided by the number of compounding periods per year to get the periodic rate:

Quarterly rate = Annual rate / 4 = 12% / 4 = 3%

The effective annual rate would be (1.03)⁴ - 1 = 12.55%, which exceeds the stated rate due to compounding.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses annual rate with quarterly rate
- Option C ignores the compounding effect that makes EAR exceed the stated rate
