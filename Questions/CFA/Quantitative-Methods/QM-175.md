# Question

Holding other factors constant, the present value of a future cash flow increases when:

## Options

- **A**: The discount rate increases
- **B**: The time until payment increases
- **C**: The discount rate decreases

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Present value is inversely related to the discount rate. A lower discount rate means less discounting of future cash flows, resulting in a higher present value. The relationship is:

PV = FV / (1 + r)^n

As r decreases, (1 + r)^n decreases, and PV increases. This is fundamental to understanding bond prices (which rise when interest rates fall) and investment valuation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite; higher discount rates reduce present value
- Option B increases time, which reduces present value (more discounting)
