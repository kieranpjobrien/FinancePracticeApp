# Question

An ordinary annuity of $1,000 per year begins at the end of year 3 and continues for 4 years (payments at end of years 3, 4, 5, and 6). If the discount rate is 8%, the present value today is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $2,631
- **B**: $2,839
- **C**: $3,312

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Step 1: Calculate PV of the 4-year ordinary annuity as of end of year 2

Using the annuity formula with first payment at end of year 3:
PV₂ = $1,000 × [(1 - (1.08)⁻⁴) / 0.08]
PV₂ = $1,000 × 3.3121 = $3,312.13

Step 2: Discount back to today (2 years)

PV₀ = $3,312.13 / (1.08)²
PV₀ = $3,312.13 / 1.1664 = $2,839

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly discounts for 3 years instead of 2
- Option C fails to discount for the deferral period
