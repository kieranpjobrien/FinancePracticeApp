# Question

Two independent events each have a 60% probability of occurring. The probability that both events occur is:

## Options

- **A**: 36%
- **B**: 60%
- **C**: 120%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For independent events, the probability of both occurring is the product of their individual probabilities:

P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
P(A and B) = 0.60 × 0.60
P(A and B) = 0.36 = 36%

Independence means the occurrence of one event does not affect the probability of the other.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B assumes the probability remains 60%, which would only be true for a single event
- Option C adds the probabilities, which is incorrect and produces an impossible probability greater than 100%
