# Question

When calculating the average price paid per share under dollar-cost averaging (investing equal dollar amounts at different prices), the appropriate measure is the:

## Options

- **A**: Arithmetic mean of the prices
- **B**: Geometric mean of the prices
- **C**: Harmonic mean of the prices

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The harmonic mean is appropriate when averaging ratios or rates, such as prices per share when equal dollar amounts are invested. With dollar-cost averaging, the same dollar amount buys more shares at lower prices and fewer shares at higher prices. The harmonic mean accounts for this weighting effect, giving more weight to lower prices. It is calculated as n divided by the sum of the reciprocals.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (arithmetic mean) would overstate the average price paid because it doesn't account for buying more shares at lower prices
- Option B (geometric mean) is used for compounding rates of return, not for averaging prices in this context
