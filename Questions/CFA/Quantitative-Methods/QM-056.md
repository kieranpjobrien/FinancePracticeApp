# Question

A bank offers a nominal annual interest rate of 12% with monthly compounding. The effective annual rate (EAR) is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 12.00%
- **B**: 12.55%
- **C**: 12.68%

## Answer

C

## Explanation

EAR = (1 + Periodic Rate)^m - 1

Where periodic rate = 12%/12 = 1% = 0.01, and m = 12 periods per year

EAR = (1 + 0.01)^12 - 1
EAR = (1.01)^12 - 1
EAR = 1.1268 - 1
EAR = 0.1268 = 12.68%

The effective annual rate exceeds the nominal rate due to the compounding effect.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A assumes no compounding effect (EAR equals nominal rate only with annual compounding)
- Option B results from calculation errors or using the wrong compounding formula
