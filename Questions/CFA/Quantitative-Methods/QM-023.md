# Question

If the covariance between two assets is 0.006 and their standard deviations are 10% and 20% respectively, the correlation coefficient is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.30
- **B**: 0.60
- **C**: 0.03

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Correlation (ρ) = Covariance / (σ₁ × σ₂)

ρ = 0.006 / (0.10 × 0.20)
ρ = 0.006 / 0.02
ρ = 0.30

The correlation coefficient is 0.30, indicating a moderately positive relationship between the two assets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B results from incorrectly using only one standard deviation in the denominator
- Option C results from multiplying instead of dividing by the product of standard deviations
