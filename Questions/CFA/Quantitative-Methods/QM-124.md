# Question

In regression analysis, Total Sum of Squares (TSS) equals:

## Options

- **A**: Explained Sum of Squares minus Residual Sum of Squares
- **B**: Explained Sum of Squares plus Residual Sum of Squares
- **C**: Residual Sum of Squares divided by degrees of freedom

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The fundamental decomposition in regression is:

TSS = ESS + RSS

Total variation in Y (TSS) = Variation explained by the model (ESS) + Unexplained variation (RSS)

This relationship shows how the total variability in the dependent variable is partitioned between what the regression explains and what remains unexplained. R² = ESS/TSS measures the proportion of total variation explained.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C describes mean squared error (MSE), not TSS
