# Question

Diversification benefits are maximized when the correlation between portfolio assets is:

## Options

- **A**: +1.0 (perfect positive correlation)
- **B**: 0 (no correlation)
- **C**: -1.0 (perfect negative correlation)

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Diversification reduces portfolio risk most effectively when assets are negatively correlated. With perfect negative correlation (-1.0), it is theoretically possible to construct a zero-risk portfolio from two risky assets. As correlation increases toward +1.0, diversification benefits diminish until, at perfect positive correlation, the portfolio risk equals the weighted average of the individual risks with no diversification benefit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A provides no diversification benefit
- Option B provides diversification benefits, but not the maximum possible
