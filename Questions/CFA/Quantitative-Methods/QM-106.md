# Question

Stress testing differs from Monte Carlo simulation in that stress testing:

## Options

- **A**: Uses random sampling from probability distributions
- **B**: Examines specific extreme scenarios rather than a range of random outcomes
- **C**: Is only applicable to individual securities, not portfolios

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stress testing evaluates portfolio performance under specific, predefined extreme scenarios (e.g., "what if interest rates rise 300 basis points and stocks fall 30%?"). Unlike Monte Carlo simulation, which generates many random scenarios based on assumed distributions, stress tests focus on particular adverse conditions chosen by the analyst. Stress tests are useful for understanding tail risks and preparing for market crises.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Monte Carlo simulation, not stress testing
- Option C is incorrect; stress testing is widely applied to portfolios, not just individual securities
