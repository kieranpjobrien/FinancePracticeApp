# Question

Serial correlation (autocorrelation) in regression residuals most likely leads to:

## Options

- **A**: Biased coefficient estimates
- **B**: Unreliable standard errors and invalid hypothesis tests
- **C**: A decrease in R-squared

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Serial correlation occurs when regression residuals are correlated with each other over time. While coefficient estimates remain unbiased, the standard errors of those estimates become unreliable—typically understated for positive serial correlation. This makes t-statistics appear larger than they should be, leading to false conclusions of statistical significance. Serial correlation is especially common in time-series data.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; serial correlation does not bias coefficients (OLS remains unbiased), but affects standard errors
- Option C is incorrect; serial correlation does not directly affect R-squared, which measures explained variation
