# Question

A sample of 36 observations has a mean of 52 and standard deviation of 12. Testing whether the population mean equals 50, the t-statistic is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.17
- **B**: 1.00
- **C**: 6.00

## Answer

B

## Explanation

t = (Sample mean - Hypothesized mean) / Standard error

Standard error = s / √n = 12 / √36 = 12 / 6 = 2

t = (52 - 50) / 2 = 2 / 2 = 1.00

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A divides by the sample standard deviation (12) instead of the standard error (2)
- Option C multiplies instead of dividing: (52-50) × 3 = 6
