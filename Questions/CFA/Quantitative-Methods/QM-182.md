# Question

The Poisson distribution is most appropriate for modeling:

## Options

- **A**: The outcome of a single yes/no trial
- **B**: The number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space
- **C**: The continuous probability of stock prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Poisson distribution models the count of events in a fixed interval when: (1) events occur independently, (2) the average rate is constant, and (3) events don't occur simultaneously. Financial applications include: number of trades per hour, credit defaults per year, or arrivals of customer orders. The distribution is characterized by a single parameter λ (average rate).

P(X = k) = (λ^k × e^(-λ)) / k!

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a Bernoulli trial (success/failure)
- Option C describes a continuous distribution like lognormal
