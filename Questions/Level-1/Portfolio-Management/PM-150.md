# Question

Marginal VaR measures:

## Options

- **A**: The total VaR of a portfolio
- **B**: The change in portfolio VaR from adding a small amount of a position
- **C**: The historical average VaR over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Marginal VaR:

**Definition:**
- Sensitivity of portfolio VaR to small change in position
- ∂VaR / ∂wi (partial derivative)
- Shows risk contribution at the margin

**Uses:**
- Identify which positions add most risk
- Guide position sizing
- Risk allocation decisions
- Optimize risk budget

**Related concepts:**
- Component VaR: Marginal VaR × Position size
- Sum of component VaRs = Total VaR
- Incremental VaR: Change from adding entire position

**Interpretation:**
- High marginal VaR = position adds more risk
- Consider reducing high marginal VaR positions
- Diversifying positions often have lower marginal VaR

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Total VaR is different from marginal
- Option C: Historical average is not marginal VaR
