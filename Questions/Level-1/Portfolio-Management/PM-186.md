# Question

In risk budgeting, a position's risk contribution is equal to its:

## Options

- **A**: Weight in the portfolio times the total portfolio volatility
- **B**: Marginal contribution to risk times the position size
- **C**: Individual volatility regardless of correlation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk contribution:

**Formula:**
Risk Contribution = Position Size × Marginal Contribution to Risk

Or equivalently:
RCi = wi × MCTRi

**Key relationships:**
- Sum of all risk contributions = Total portfolio risk
- MCTR = ∂σp/∂wi (marginal change in portfolio risk)
- Accounts for correlations with other holdings

**Uses:**
- Identify largest risk contributors
- Optimize risk allocation
- Guide position sizing

**Risk parity:**
- Equal risk contributions from all positions
- Not equal dollar weights

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignores correlations and marginal effect
- Option C: Individual volatility ignores portfolio context
