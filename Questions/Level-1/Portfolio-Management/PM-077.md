# Question

Modern Portfolio Theory, developed by Harry Markowitz, introduced the concept that:

## Options

- **A**: Portfolio risk equals the weighted average of individual asset risks
- **B**: Portfolio risk depends on asset correlations, not just individual risks
- **C**: Diversification cannot reduce portfolio risk below market risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Markowitz's Modern Portfolio Theory (1952):

**Key insight:**
- Portfolio risk is NOT the weighted average of individual risks
- Correlations between assets determine diversification benefit
- Low/negative correlations reduce portfolio risk

**Portfolio variance:**
σ²p = Σwi²σi² + ΣΣwiwjσiσjρij

The second term (covariance term) captures the correlation effect.

**Implications:**
- "Don't put all eggs in one basket" is mathematically formalized
- Optimal portfolios lie on the efficient frontier
- Rational investors choose based on risk-return trade-off

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This ignores the correlation effect; true only if ρ = +1
- Option C: Diversification can reduce total risk to systematic risk level
