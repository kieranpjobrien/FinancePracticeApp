# Question

The risk-free rate is 3% and the expected market return is 11% with a standard deviation of 16%. The slope of the Capital Market Line is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.19
- **B**: 0.50
- **C**: 0.69

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital Market Line slope:

**CML equation:**
E(Rp) = Rf + [(E(Rm) - Rf) / σm] × σp

**Slope of CML:**
Slope = (E(Rm) - Rf) / σm

This equals the Sharpe ratio of the market portfolio.

**Calculation:**
Slope = (11% - 3%) / 16%
Slope = 8% / 16%
Slope = 0.50

**Interpretation:**
- For each 1% increase in risk (standard deviation)
- Expected return increases by 0.50%
- Market's risk-return trade-off

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May divide by wrong denominator
- Option C: May use different values or calculation approach
