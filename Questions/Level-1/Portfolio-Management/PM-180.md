# Question

Mean reversion in asset returns suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Asset returns will continue in their current trend indefinitely
- **B**: Periods of above-average returns tend to be followed by below-average returns
- **C**: All assets will eventually have identical returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mean reversion:

**Concept:**
- Returns tend to revert to long-term average
- Extreme returns (high or low) are temporary
- Over time, returns converge toward mean

**Implications for investing:**
- Contrarian strategies may benefit
- Past winners may underperform going forward
- Valuation matters over long term

**Evidence:**
- P/E ratios historically mean-revert
- Style (value/growth) cycles
- Some commodity prices

**Caveats:**
- Time frame uncertain
- Structural changes can shift mean
- Short-term momentum may persist
- Not applicable to all assets

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes momentum, opposite of mean reversion
- Option C: Mean reversion to own average, not identical returns
