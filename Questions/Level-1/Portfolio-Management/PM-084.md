# Question

When an investor's ability to take risk exceeds their willingness to take risk, the portfolio manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Invest according to the higher ability level
- **B**: Invest according to the lower willingness level
- **C**: Split the difference between ability and willingness

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Resolving ability vs. willingness conflicts:

**General rule:**
- Use the more conservative of the two assessments
- Client comfort and behavioral consistency are important
- Forcing unwilling clients into risky portfolios leads to poor outcomes

**When willingness < ability:**
- Respect the lower willingness
- Client may panic and sell at wrong times if uncomfortable
- Educate client about missed opportunities over time
- May gradually increase risk as client becomes more comfortable

**When ability < willingness:**
- Definitely constrain to ability level
- Protect client from financial harm
- Client wants more risk than they can afford

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignoring willingness leads to behavioral problems
- Option C: No specific averaging method; generally use lower level
