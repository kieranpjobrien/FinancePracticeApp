# Question

An investor who believes markets are efficient would most likely choose:

## Options

- **A**: Active management to exploit mispricings
- **B**: Passive management tracking a market index
- **C**: Concentrated positions in undervalued securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) states that security prices reflect all available information. An investor who believes markets are efficient would conclude:

- Securities are fairly priced
- Mispricings are rare and quickly corrected
- Active management costs reduce returns without adding value
- Passive/index investing is optimal

Passive management:
- Tracks a market index
- Minimizes transaction costs and management fees
- Accepts market returns rather than trying to beat the market

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Active management assumes markets are inefficient and mispricings can be exploited
- Option C: Concentrated positions assume the investor can identify undervalued securities, contradicting market efficiency
