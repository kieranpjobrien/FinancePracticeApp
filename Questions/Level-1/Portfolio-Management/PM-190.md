# Question

A positive alpha indicates that a portfolio:

## Options

- **A**: Has higher risk than the benchmark
- **B**: Outperformed its risk-adjusted expected return
- **C**: Is perfectly correlated with the market

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Alpha interpretation:

**Definition:**
α = Actual return - Expected return (CAPM)
α = Rp - [Rf + β(Rm - Rf)]

**Positive alpha:**
- Outperformed CAPM expectation
- Earned more than justified by beta
- May indicate manager skill
- Plots above Security Market Line

**Negative alpha:**
- Underperformed expectation
- Earned less than justified by beta
- May indicate poor management
- Plots below SML

**Caveats:**
- May be due to luck
- May reflect unmeasured risk factors
- Statistical significance matters
- Past alpha may not persist

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Alpha measures return, not risk level
- Option C: Correlation doesn't determine alpha
