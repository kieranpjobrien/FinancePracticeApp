# Question

The permitted deviation from strategic allocation for tactical shifts should be:

## Options

- **A**: Unlimited to maximize returns
- **B**: Specified in the IPS with clear ranges and governance
- **C**: Left entirely to the manager's discretion without limits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tactical allocation governance:

**Best practices:**
- IPS specifies tactical ranges
- Clear limits on deviations
- Rebalancing triggers defined
- Approval levels for larger shifts

**Example:**
- Strategic: 60% equity
- Tactical range: 55-65%
- Beyond range requires approval

**Benefits of limits:**
- Risk control
- Manager accountability
- Prevents style drift
- Maintains intended risk profile

**Too much flexibility risks:**
- Excessive market timing
- Strategy creep
- Unintended risks

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Unlimited deviation creates uncontrolled risk
- Option C: Discretion without limits is poor governance
