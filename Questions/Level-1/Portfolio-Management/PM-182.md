# Question

An employee who holds 50% of their retirement portfolio in employer stock is likely exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Optimal diversification strategy
- **B**: Familiarity bias leading to concentration risk
- **C**: Mean-variance optimization

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Familiarity bias:

**Definition:**
- Preference for investments you know
- Overconfidence in familiar companies
- Related to home bias

**Employer stock concentration:**
- Common in retirement plans
- Employees feel they "know" the company
- Often encouraged by matching programs

**Risks:**
- Double jeopardy: Job + retirement tied to same company
- Enron, Lehman examples
- Underdiversified portfolio

**Why it happens:**
- False sense of information advantage
- Loyalty to employer
- Anchoring on past performance
- Mental accounting (company = safe)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Concentration in single stock is suboptimal
- Option C: MVO would diversify away firm-specific risk
