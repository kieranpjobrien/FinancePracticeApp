# Question

An investor who ignores historical failure rates of startups when evaluating a specific startup investment is exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Base rate neglect
- **B**: Confirmation bias
- **C**: Endowment effect

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Base rate neglect:

**Definition:**
- Ignoring underlying probability (base rate)
- Overweighting specific case information
- Underweighting statistical evidence

**Example:**
- Startup failure rate: ~90%
- Investor focuses on specific company's story
- Ignores base rate of failure

**Investment implications:**
- Overconfidence in specific picks
- Underestimating probability of failure
- Poor probability assessment

**Related to:**
- Representativeness bias
- Sample size neglect
- Overconfidence

**Mitigation:**
- Always consider base rates
- Bayesian updating
- Statistical thinking

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Confirmation): Seeking confirming info
- Option C (Endowment): Overvaluing owned assets
