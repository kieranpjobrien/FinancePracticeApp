# Question

A stock has a total variance of 0.16, a beta of 1.5, and the market variance is 0.04. The systematic variance is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.04
- **B**: 0.09
- **C**: 0.16

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk decomposition:

**Total variance = Systematic variance + Unsystematic variance**

**Systematic variance formula:**
σ²(systematic) = β² × σ²(market)

**Calculation:**
σ²(systematic) = (1.5)² × 0.04
σ²(systematic) = 2.25 × 0.04
σ²(systematic) = 0.09

**Unsystematic variance:**
σ²(unsystematic) = Total - Systematic
σ²(unsystematic) = 0.16 - 0.09 = 0.07

**Verification:**
- Total variance: 0.16
- Systematic: 0.09 (56%)
- Unsystematic: 0.07 (44%)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is market variance, not stock's systematic variance
- Option C: This is total variance, not just systematic
