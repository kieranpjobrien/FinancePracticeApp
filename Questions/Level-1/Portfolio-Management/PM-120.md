# Question

A stock has a historical (raw) beta of 1.4. Using the standard Bloomberg adjustment formula, the adjusted beta is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 1.13
- **B**: 1.27
- **C**: 1.40

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Adjusted beta (Blume adjustment):

**Formula:**
Adjusted Beta = (2/3 × Raw Beta) + (1/3 × 1.0)

**Rationale:**
- Betas tend to revert toward 1.0 over time
- High betas tend to decrease, low betas tend to increase
- Adjustment improves forecast accuracy

**Calculation:**
Adjusted Beta = (2/3 × 1.4) + (1/3 × 1.0)
Adjusted Beta = 0.933 + 0.333
Adjusted Beta = 1.27

**Bloomberg approach:**
- Uses 2/3 weight on raw beta
- 1/3 weight on market beta (1.0)
- Simple but effective adjustment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May use different weights or calculation errors
- Option C: This is the unadjusted raw beta
