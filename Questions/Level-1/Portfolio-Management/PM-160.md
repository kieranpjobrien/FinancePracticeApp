# Question

An emergency fund should typically be invested in:

## Options

- **A**: High-growth stocks for maximum appreciation
- **B**: Safe, liquid assets like money market funds or savings accounts
- **C**: Long-term bonds for higher yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emergency fund characteristics:

**Purpose:**
- Cover unexpected expenses
- Job loss, medical emergencies, major repairs
- Prevent need to sell investments at bad times

**Investment requirements:**
- High liquidity (accessible immediately)
- Capital preservation (no risk of loss)
- Stable value

**Recommended vehicles:**
- Money market funds
- Savings/checking accounts
- Short-term CDs (with penalty consideration)
- Treasury bills

**Size guidelines:**
- Typically 3-6 months of expenses
- More if income volatile or single earner
- Less if dual income with stable jobs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Stocks can decline when money needed most
- Option C: Long bonds have interest rate risk and less liquidity
