# Question

A portfolio has an alpha of 3% and residual (unsystematic) risk of 12%. The appraisal ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.25
- **B**: 0.36
- **C**: 4.00

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Appraisal ratio:

**Formula:**
Appraisal Ratio = Alpha / σ(ε)

Where:
- Alpha = 3% = 0.03
- σ(ε) = Residual (unsystematic) risk = 12% = 0.12

**Calculation:**
Appraisal Ratio = 0.03 / 0.12 = 0.25

**Interpretation:**
- Measures alpha per unit of unsystematic risk
- Relevant for actively managed portion of portfolio
- Higher is better
- Used in Treynor-Black model

**Comparison to other ratios:**
- Sharpe: Total risk in denominator
- Treynor: Systematic risk (beta) in denominator
- Information ratio: Tracking error in denominator

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: May use incorrect formula or values
- Option C: Inverts the ratio
