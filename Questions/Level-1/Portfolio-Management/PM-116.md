# Question

An investor who is more concerned about downside risk than upside volatility would likely prefer which risk measure?

## Options

- **A**: Standard deviation
- **B**: Semi-variance
- **C**: Beta

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Semi-variance and downside risk measures:

**Semi-variance:**
- Only considers returns below the mean (or target)
- Ignores positive deviations
- Focuses on harmful volatility

**Why preferred for downside-focused investors:**
- Standard deviation penalizes both up and down moves
- Investors typically don't mind upside volatility
- Semi-variance captures the risk investors actually care about

**Related measures:**
- Sortino ratio: Uses downside deviation in denominator
- Target semi-variance: Uses target return instead of mean
- VaR and CVaR: Focus on tail losses

**Limitations:**
- Requires more data for stability
- Less mathematically tractable

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Standard deviation treats upside and downside equally
- Option C: Beta measures systematic risk, not asymmetric risk
