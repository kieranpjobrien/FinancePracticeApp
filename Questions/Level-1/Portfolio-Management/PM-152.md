# Question

A portfolio returns 8% in Q1, 4% in Q2, -3% in Q3, and 6% in Q4. The annual return is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 15.0%
- **B**: 15.5%
- **C**: 15.7%

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Compounding multi-period returns:

**Formula:**
Annual return = [(1+R1)(1+R2)(1+R3)(1+R4)] - 1

**Calculation:**
= (1.08)(1.04)(0.97)(1.06) - 1
= (1.1232)(0.97)(1.06) - 1
= (1.0895)(1.06) - 1
= 1.1549 - 1
= 0.1549 = 15.49% ≈ 15.5%

Wait, let me recalculate:
= 1.08 × 1.04 = 1.1232
= 1.1232 × 0.97 = 1.0895
= 1.0895 × 1.06 = 1.1549

Annual return = 15.49% ≈ 15.5%

**Note:** Simple addition (8+4-3+6=15%) understates due to compounding.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Simple addition of returns (ignores compounding)
- Arithmetic vs. geometric compounding confusion
