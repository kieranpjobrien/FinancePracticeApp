# Question

Counterparty risk is the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: An investment's price will decline due to market factors
- **B**: The other party to a transaction will fail to fulfill their obligation
- **C**: An investor cannot find a buyer for their securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Counterparty risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk that the other party in a financial contract fails to perform
- Type of credit risk specific to bilateral transactions
- Particularly relevant for OTC derivatives

**Examples:**
- Forward contract: Counterparty fails to deliver
- Swap agreement: Counterparty misses payment
- Securities lending: Borrower doesn't return securities

**Mitigation methods:**
- Collateral/margin requirements
- Central clearing (CCPs)
- Netting agreements
- Credit monitoring

**Post-2008 reforms:**
- Increased central clearing
- Margin requirements for non-cleared derivatives
- Enhanced counterparty credit risk management

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes market risk
- Option C: This describes liquidity risk
