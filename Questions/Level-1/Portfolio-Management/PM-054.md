# Question

Compared to a defined contribution pension plan, a defined benefit pension plan:

## Options

- **A**: Places investment risk primarily on the employee
- **B**: Promises a specific retirement benefit based on salary and years of service
- **C**: Allows employees complete control over investment choices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Defined Benefit (DB) vs. Defined Contribution (DC):

**Defined Benefit Plan:**
- Employer promises specific benefit at retirement
- Benefit formula typically based on salary × years of service
- Investment risk borne by employer
- Employer responsible for funding shortfalls
- Less common today due to liability concerns

**Defined Contribution Plan:**
- Employer/employee contribute specified amounts
- Final benefit depends on investment performance
- Investment risk borne by employee
- Employee often chooses investments (401(k), IRA)
- Predominant plan type today

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes defined contribution plans
- Option C: This also describes defined contribution plans
