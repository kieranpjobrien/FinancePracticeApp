# Question

Monte Carlo simulation in financial planning is used to:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee specific investment outcomes
- **B**: Model a range of possible outcomes based on probability distributions
- **C**: Eliminate all investment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monte Carlo simulation:

**Definition:**
- Computational technique using random sampling
- Generates thousands of possible scenarios
- Each scenario uses randomly drawn returns from specified distributions

**Applications in financial planning:**
- Retirement planning: Probability of not outliving assets
- Goal funding: Likelihood of meeting future needs
- Risk assessment: Distribution of possible outcomes

**Output:**
- Probability distribution of outcomes
- Confidence intervals (e.g., 90% chance of success)
- Sensitivity to assumptions

**Benefits:**
- Captures uncertainty and variability
- More realistic than single-point estimates
- Illustrates range of possibilities

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Monte Carlo shows possibilities, not guarantees
- Option C: Shows risk range; doesn't eliminate risk
