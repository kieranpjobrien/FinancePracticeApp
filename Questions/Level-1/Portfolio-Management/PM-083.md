# Question

A bank's investment portfolio typically serves primarily to:

## Options

- **A**: Generate trading profits through active speculation
- **B**: Provide liquidity and manage interest rate risk
- **C**: Achieve maximum capital appreciation over the long term

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bank investment portfolios:

**Primary purposes:**
- Liquidity reserve (meet depositor withdrawals)
- Interest rate risk management (balance sheet hedging)
- Regulatory compliance (capital and liquidity ratios)
- Generate income on excess funds

**Typical characteristics:**
- High-quality, liquid securities
- Government and agency bonds
- Moderate duration (balance yield vs. interest rate risk)
- Conservative approach

**Asset-Liability Management (ALM):**
- Match asset duration to liability duration
- Manage net interest margin
- Control interest rate sensitivity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Trading is separate from investment portfolio; banks are generally conservative
- Option C: Capital appreciation is secondary to liquidity and risk management
