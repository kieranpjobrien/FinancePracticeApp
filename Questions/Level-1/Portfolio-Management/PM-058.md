# Question

An investor who refuses to invest in tobacco or gambling companies is expressing:

## Options

- **A**: A tax constraint
- **B**: A liquidity constraint
- **C**: A unique circumstance

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Unique circumstances in the IPS (the "U" in RRTTLLU):

**Examples of unique circumstances:**
- Ethical or ESG restrictions (tobacco, weapons, gambling)
- Religious requirements (Sharia-compliant investing)
- Concentrated stock holdings (employer stock)
- Pending inheritance or litigation
- Non-financial assets (real estate, business ownership)
- Special family situations

**Socially Responsible Investing (SRI):**
- Negative screening: Exclude certain industries
- Positive screening: Include companies meeting ESG criteria
- Impact investing: Target specific social outcomes

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Tax constraints relate to minimizing tax liability
- Option B: Liquidity constraints relate to cash needs and marketability
