# Question

Which of the following is most likely classified as an institutional investor?

## Options

- **A**: A high-net-worth individual with $10 million in assets
- **B**: A corporate pension fund
- **C**: A family office managing one family's wealth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Institutional investors are organizations that invest on behalf of others or for institutional purposes. Key examples include:

- Pension funds (defined benefit and defined contribution)
- Endowments and foundations
- Insurance companies
- Sovereign wealth funds
- Banks and investment companies

A corporate pension fund is a classic institutional investor with:
- Fiduciary duty to beneficiaries
- Long time horizon
- Professional management
- Regulatory oversight

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: High-net-worth individuals are classified as individual investors regardless of wealth level
- Option C: Family offices, while sophisticated, typically serve individual families and are generally classified as individual investors
