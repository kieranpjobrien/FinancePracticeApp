# Question

Stress testing differs from Value at Risk (VaR) in that stress testing:

## Options

- **A**: Focuses on normal market conditions
- **B**: Examines portfolio behavior under extreme hypothetical scenarios
- **C**: Provides a single probability-based number

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stress testing vs. VaR:

**Stress testing:**
- Extreme scenario analysis
- "What if" questions
- Historical or hypothetical events
- Beyond normal probabilities
- Multiple factor movements

**VaR characteristics:**
- Probability-based measure
- Normal/historical conditions
- Specific confidence level
- Single summary number
- Limited in extreme events

**Stress test types:**
- Historical (replaying past crises)
- Hypothetical (designed scenarios)
- Factor push (extreme factor moves)
- Maximum loss scenarios

**Advantages of stress testing:**
- Captures tail risks
- Not limited by history
- Explores extreme correlations
- Identifies vulnerabilities

**Complementary use:**
- Use both together
- VaR for normal conditions
- Stress tests for extremes

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Stress testing focuses on extreme conditions
- Option C: VaR provides single probability-based number
