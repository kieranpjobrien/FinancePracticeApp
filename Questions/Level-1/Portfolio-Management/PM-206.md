# Question

A portfolio has a monthly standard deviation of 4%. The annualized standard deviation is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 13.9%
- **B**: 48.0%
- **C**: 16.0%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Annualizing volatility:

**Formula:**
σ(annual) = σ(periodic) × √(number of periods per year)

**For monthly to annual:**
σ(annual) = σ(monthly) × √12

**Calculation:**
σ(annual) = 4% × √12
σ(annual) = 4% × 3.464
σ(annual) = 13.86% ≈ 13.9%

**Key insight:**
- Volatility scales with square root of time
- Returns scale linearly with time
- Daily → Annual: multiply by √252

**Common periods:**
- Monthly: √12 ≈ 3.46
- Weekly: √52 ≈ 7.21
- Daily: √252 ≈ 15.87 (trading days)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Multiplies by 12 (scaling linearly, incorrect)
- Option C: May use wrong time factor
