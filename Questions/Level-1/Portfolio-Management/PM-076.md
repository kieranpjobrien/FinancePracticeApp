# Question

During financial crises, diversification often fails to provide expected protection because:

## Options

- **A**: Asset returns become more negatively correlated
- **B**: Correlations tend to increase during market stress
- **C**: Investors become more rational during crises

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Correlation breakdown during crises:

**Normal markets:**
- Assets have varying correlations
- Diversification reduces portfolio risk
- Some assets zig while others zag

**Crisis periods:**
- Correlations spike toward +1.0
- "All correlations go to 1" phenomenon
- Flight to quality/liquidity
- Diversification benefits diminish precisely when needed most

**Implications:**
- Historical correlations may understate crisis risk
- Stress testing with elevated correlations
- Consider tail risk hedging
- Truly uncorrelated assets are rare in crises

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Correlations typically increase (become more positive), not more negative
- Option C: Behavioral biases intensify during crises (panic, herding)
