# Question

Immunization is a fixed-income strategy that:

## Options

- **A**: Maximizes yield regardless of risk
- **B**: Matches portfolio duration to liability duration to protect against interest rate changes
- **C**: Invests only in Treasury bills

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Immunization:

**Purpose:**
- Protect against interest rate risk
- Ensure liability is funded regardless of rate changes
- Lock in rate of return

**Mechanism:**
- Match portfolio duration to liability duration
- Price risk and reinvestment risk offset
- If rates rise: bond price falls, reinvestment improves
- If rates fall: bond price rises, reinvestment suffers

**Requirements:**
- Duration match
- Present value match
- Convexity considerations (portfolio ≥ liability)
- Periodic rebalancing

**Types:**
- Classical: Single liability
- Multiple liability: Multiple payment dates
- Contingent: Active management within bounds

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Immunization focuses on risk management, not yield
- Option C: T-bills don't provide duration matching
