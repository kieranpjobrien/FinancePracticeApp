# Question

An investor who overweights recent news events when making investment decisions is exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Availability bias
- **B**: Conservatism bias
- **C**: Endowment effect

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Availability bias:

**Definition:**
- Overweighting information that is easily recalled
- Recent, vivid, or emotional events have outsized influence
- Probability estimates based on ease of memory retrieval

**Manifestations:**
- Recent events seem more likely (recency effect)
- Dramatic events overweighted (plane crashes vs. car accidents)
- Personal experience overweighted vs. statistics

**Investment implications:**
- Overreacting to recent market news
- Avoiding sectors with recent high-profile failures
- Chasing recent winners (performance chasing)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Conservatism): Slow to update beliefs; opposite of overreacting to news
- Option C (Endowment effect): Overvaluing assets you own
