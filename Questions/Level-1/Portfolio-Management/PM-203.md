# Question

Reinvestment risk is the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: A bond issuer will default on payments
- **B**: Cash flows from investments will be reinvested at lower rates
- **C**: Bond prices will fall when interest rates rise

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reinvestment risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk that interim cash flows reinvested at lower rates
- Actual return differs from yield-to-maturity assumption
- Applies to coupon payments and maturing principal

**When it occurs:**
- Interest rates decline
- Coupon received, reinvested at new lower rate
- Bond matures during low-rate environment

**Most affected:**
- High-coupon bonds (more interim cash flows)
- Long time horizons
- Callable bonds (forced reinvestment if called)

**Mitigation:**
- Zero-coupon bonds (no interim cash flows)
- Immunization strategies
- Laddered portfolios
- Duration matching

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes credit/default risk
- Option C: This describes interest rate (price) risk
