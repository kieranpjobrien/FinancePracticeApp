# Question

Choice architecture and nudges can improve investment behavior by:

## Options

- **A**: Removing all investment choices from individuals
- **B**: Structuring choices to guide people toward better decisions without restricting options
- **C**: Mandating specific asset allocations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Choice architecture:

**Definition:**
- Designing how choices are presented
- Influencing behavior through context
- "Nudges" toward better outcomes

**Examples in investing:**
- Default enrollment in retirement plans
- Default contribution rates (auto-escalation)
- Target-date funds as default option
- Simplified fund menus

**Principles:**
- Maintain freedom of choice
- Make good options easy
- Provide helpful defaults
- Reduce complexity

**Evidence:**
- Auto-enrollment dramatically increases participation
- Default rates stick (status quo bias used positively)
- Simpler choices reduce paralysis

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Nudges preserve choice, don't eliminate it
- Option C: Mandates restrict choice; nudges don't
