# Question

Dollar-cost averaging involves:

## Options

- **A**: Investing a lump sum at the market's lowest point
- **B**: Investing a fixed dollar amount at regular intervals
- **C**: Adjusting investment amounts based on market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dollar-cost averaging (DCA):

**Definition:**
- Investing fixed dollar amount at regular intervals
- Regardless of price level
- Systematic, disciplined approach

**Mechanics:**
- Buy more shares when prices are low
- Buy fewer shares when prices are high
- Average cost per share is lowered over time

**Benefits:**
- Removes market timing decisions
- Behavioral discipline
- Reduces regret from poor timing
- Matches income timing (paycheck investing)

**Considerations:**
- Lump sum historically outperforms (on average)
- DCA may reduce risk of poor entry point
- Good for investors with regular income

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Market timing is opposite of DCA philosophy
- Option C: DCA maintains fixed amounts regardless of conditions
