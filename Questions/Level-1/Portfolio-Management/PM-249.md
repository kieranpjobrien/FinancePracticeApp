# Question

Strategy capacity refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The intelligence of the portfolio manager
- **B**: The amount of assets a strategy can manage before performance degrades
- **C**: The number of securities in the portfolio

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strategy capacity:

**Definition:**
- Maximum assets strategy can manage effectively
- Beyond capacity, returns diminish
- "Alpha decay" from size

**Causes of capacity limits:**
- Market impact of large trades
- Limited opportunities in niche strategies
- Wider spreads when accumulating positions
- Faster signal decay

**Affected strategies:**
- Small-cap investing
- Concentrated portfolios
- High-frequency strategies
- Illiquid markets

**Less affected:**
- Large-cap indexing
- Diversified strategies
- Liquid markets

**Implications:**
- Successful strategies may close to new investors
- Returns often decline as AUM grows

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Capacity is about assets, not skill
- Option C: Number of securities is different concept
