# Question

Research on portfolio return determinants has shown that asset allocation:

## Options

- **A**: Has minimal impact compared to security selection
- **B**: Explains the majority of variation in portfolio returns over time
- **C**: Is only important for short-term investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset allocation importance:

**Key research (Brinson et al.):**
- Asset allocation explains ~90% of return variation over time
- Security selection and market timing less impactful
- Policy allocation is primary determinant

**Important distinctions:**
- Variation over time (for single fund) vs.
- Variation across funds (at point in time)

**Implications:**
- Get asset allocation right first
- Security selection secondary
- Don't obsess over stock picking

**Practical application:**
- Strategic allocation primary decision
- Tactical adjustments secondary
- Security selection within asset classes

**Debate:**
- Some argue about interpretation
- But consensus: allocation matters greatly

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Security selection is secondary
- Option C: Allocation important for all horizons
