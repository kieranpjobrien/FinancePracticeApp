# Question

According to prospect theory, the value function:

## Options

- **A**: Is linear and symmetric for gains and losses
- **B**: Is steeper for losses than for gains, and concave in gains, convex in losses
- **C**: Shows constant marginal utility across all wealth levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Prospect theory value function (Kahneman & Tversky):

**Key features:**
1. **Reference dependence:** Evaluated relative to reference point, not absolute wealth
2. **Loss aversion:** Steeper slope in loss region (losses hurt ~2x more than equivalent gains please)
3. **Diminishing sensitivity:**
   - Concave for gains (diminishing pleasure)
   - Convex for losses (diminishing pain)

**Shape:**
- S-shaped curve
- Kink at reference point
- Losses loom larger than gains

**Implications:**
- Risk-seeking in loss domain (gamble to break even)
- Risk-averse in gain domain (lock in sure gain)
- Framing effects matter

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Expected utility theory is linear in probabilities; prospect theory is not
- Option C: Diminishing sensitivity contradicts constant marginal utility
