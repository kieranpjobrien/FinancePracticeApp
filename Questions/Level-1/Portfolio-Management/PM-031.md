# Question

A portfolio manager rebalances the portfolio back to target weights whenever any asset class drifts more than 5% from its strategic allocation. This approach is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Calendar rebalancing
- **B**: Percentage-of-portfolio rebalancing
- **C**: Tactical rebalancing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rebalancing approaches:

**Percentage-of-portfolio (threshold) rebalancing:**
- Rebalances when allocations deviate beyond specified thresholds
- Example: 5% corridor around target weights
- Pros: Responds to market movements, cost-effective
- Cons: May trigger during volatile periods

**Calendar rebalancing:**
- Rebalances at fixed time intervals (monthly, quarterly, annually)
- Ignores intermediate deviations
- Pros: Simple, predictable
- Cons: May miss optimal rebalancing points

**Tactical rebalancing:**
- Combines strategic targets with tactical views
- Intentionally deviates from strategic weights

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Calendar rebalancing occurs at fixed time intervals, not when thresholds are breached
- Option C: Tactical rebalancing involves intentional deviations to exploit market opportunities
