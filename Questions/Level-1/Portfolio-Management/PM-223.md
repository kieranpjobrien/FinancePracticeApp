# Question

A risk-averse investor:

## Options

- **A**: Prefers higher risk regardless of return
- **B**: Requires higher expected return to accept higher risk
- **C**: Is indifferent between risky and risk-free investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk aversion:

**Definition:**
- Prefer certainty to equivalent expected value gamble
- Require risk premium to take risk
- Concave utility function

**Investment implications:**
- Demand higher return for higher risk
- Prefer less volatile to more volatile investments
- Will pay for insurance/hedging

**Utility function:**
- Diminishing marginal utility of wealth
- Risk premium = Certainty equivalent - Expected value

**Degree of risk aversion:**
- Varies among investors
- Affects asset allocation
- Higher aversion = more conservative portfolio

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes risk-seeking behavior
- Option C: This describes risk-neutral behavior
