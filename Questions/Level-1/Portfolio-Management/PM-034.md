# Question

When the correlation between two assets equals +1.0, the portfolio standard deviation is:

## Options

- **A**: Zero
- **B**: The weighted average of the individual standard deviations
- **C**: Less than the weighted average of the individual standard deviations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When correlation (ρ) = +1.0, there is no diversification benefit:

σp = √[w₁²σ₁² + w₂²σ₂² + 2w₁w₂σ₁σ₂ρ]

When ρ = +1:
σp = √[(w₁σ₁ + w₂σ₂)²]
σp = w₁σ₁ + w₂σ₂

This is exactly the weighted average of standard deviations - no risk reduction from diversification.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Zero standard deviation only occurs when ρ = -1 and weights are optimally chosen
- Option C: Describes cases where correlation is less than +1 (diversification benefit exists)
