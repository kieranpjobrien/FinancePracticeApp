# Question

An effective risk governance framework should include:

## Options

- **A**: Risk limits only for traders, not senior management
- **B**: Clear risk policies, measurement, monitoring, and escalation procedures
- **C**: Reliance on a single risk measure to simplify decision-making

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk governance framework components:

**Risk policies:**
- Board-approved risk appetite statement
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Documentation of approved activities

**Risk measurement:**
- Multiple risk measures (VaR, stress tests, limits)
- Both quantitative and qualitative assessment
- Regular model validation

**Risk monitoring:**
- Real-time position tracking
- Limit monitoring and breach reporting
- Exception management

**Escalation procedures:**
- Clear escalation paths
- Authority levels for decisions
- Crisis management protocols

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: All levels need appropriate risk limits and accountability
- Option C: Multiple measures needed; no single measure captures all risks
