# Question

The affect heuristic causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Carefully analyze all available financial data
- **B**: Make judgments based on current emotions or feelings about an investment
- **C**: Only invest in assets with low volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Affect heuristic:

**Definition:**
- Making decisions based on feelings/emotions
- Using affect (good/bad feeling) as shortcut
- Positive affect = lower perceived risk, higher perceived return

**Mechanism:**
- "I like this company" → "Must be good investment"
- Positive feeling → underweight risks
- Negative feeling → overweight risks

**Examples:**
- Investing in companies you like as customer
- Avoiding "evil" industries regardless of value
- Following gut instinct over analysis

**Investment implications:**
- Correlation between perceived risk and return (shouldn't exist)
- Emotional attachment to holdings
- Difficulty selling "favorite" stocks

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is rational analysis, not affect heuristic
- Option C: Risk preference may vary; affect is about emotional shortcut
