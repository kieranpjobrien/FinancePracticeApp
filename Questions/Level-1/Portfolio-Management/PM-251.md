# Question

The correlation between stocks and bonds:

## Options

- **A**: Is always strongly positive
- **B**: Varies over time and can be positive, negative, or near zero
- **C**: Is always exactly negative

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock-bond correlation dynamics:

**Historical patterns:**
- Varied significantly over decades
- Was positive in 1970s-1990s
- More negative since 2000s
- Changes with economic regime

**Factors affecting correlation:**
- Inflation expectations
- Monetary policy
- Risk sentiment
- Economic growth outlook

**Current environment:**
- Often negative (flight to quality)
- Bonds rise when stocks fall
- Diversification benefit

**Implications:**
- Don't assume stable correlation
- Stress test with various scenarios
- Portfolio risk changes with correlation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Correlation is time-varying
- Option C: Not always negative
