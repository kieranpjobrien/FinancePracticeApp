# Question

A bucket strategy for retirement planning involves:

## Options

- **A**: Investing all assets in a single diversified portfolio
- **B**: Segmenting assets into time-based buckets with different risk profiles
- **C**: Holding only fixed-income investments in retirement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bucket strategy:

**Structure:**
- Divide portfolio into time-based segments
- Each bucket has appropriate risk level

**Typical buckets:**
1. **Short-term (1-3 years):** Cash, money markets
   - Covers immediate income needs
   - Provides peace of mind in market downturns

2. **Medium-term (3-10 years):** Bonds, balanced funds
   - Refills short-term bucket
   - Moderate growth with stability

3. **Long-term (10+ years):** Stocks, growth investments
   - Maximum growth potential
   - Time to recover from volatility

**Benefits:**
- Behavioral: Reduces panic selling
- Mental accounting: Matches goals to investments
- Systematic refilling process

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Bucket strategy specifically segments assets
- Option C: Still includes equities for long-term growth
