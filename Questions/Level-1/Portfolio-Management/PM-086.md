# Question

A young professional with stable employment in a government job should most likely have a portfolio:

## Options

- **A**: Heavily weighted toward bonds due to career uncertainty
- **B**: Heavily weighted toward equities to complement bond-like human capital
- **C**: Equally weighted between stocks and bonds regardless of career

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Human capital in portfolio construction:

**Human capital concept:**
- Present value of future labor income
- Typically largest asset for young professionals
- Declines over career as income is earned

**Characteristics of this investor:**
- Young: Long investment horizon
- Government job: Stable, bond-like income stream
- Low correlation between job and stock market

**Portfolio implications:**
- Human capital is bond-like (stable, predictable)
- Financial portfolio should be equity-heavy for diversification
- Total wealth (human + financial) is then balanced

**Contrast with stockbroker:**
- Stockbroker's income correlates with markets
- Should hold MORE bonds to diversify human capital

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Bond-heavy would over-concentrate in fixed income when including human capital
- Option C: Ignores the role of human capital in total wealth
