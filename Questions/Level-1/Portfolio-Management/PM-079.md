# Question

The two-fund separation theorem states that:

## Options

- **A**: Investors should divide their portfolios equally between stocks and bonds
- **B**: All investors should hold the same optimal risky portfolio, adjusting risk via the risk-free asset
- **C**: Active and passive investments should be kept in separate accounts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Two-fund separation (Tobin):

**Theorem:**
- All investors, regardless of risk preference, hold the same risky portfolio
- Risk tolerance is expressed by the allocation between this portfolio and Rf
- Only two "funds" needed: risk-free asset and optimal risky portfolio

**Investment decision separated into two steps:**
1. Determine optimal risky portfolio (same for all investors)
2. Allocate between optimal risky portfolio and Rf (varies by risk tolerance)

**Implications:**
- Simplifies investment decision
- Foundation for CAPM
- Passive investing justification (hold the market)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No specific split is mandated; allocation depends on individual risk tolerance
- Option C: This describes account structure, not portfolio theory
