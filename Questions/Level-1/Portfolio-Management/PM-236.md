# Question

When dealing with investor biases, advisors should generally:

## Options

- **A**: Ignore all biases since they cannot be changed
- **B**: Adapt to emotional biases but educate to correct cognitive biases
- **C**: Force clients to change all behaviors immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bias management approach:

**Cognitive biases:**
- Stem from faulty reasoning
- More amenable to correction
- Education and information help
- Example: Anchoring, confirmation bias

**Approach:** Educate, inform, correct

**Emotional biases:**
- Deeply rooted in psychology
- Harder to change
- May need to accommodate
- Example: Loss aversion, overconfidence

**Approach:** Adapt portfolio, work around

**Practical application:**
- Identify bias type
- Assess severity
- Choose adapt vs. correct
- Consider client wealth (more wealth = more adapt)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Many biases can be reduced
- Option C: Forcing change may cause client to leave
