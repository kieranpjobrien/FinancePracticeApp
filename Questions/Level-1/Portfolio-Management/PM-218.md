# Question

Social proof as a behavioral influence leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Conduct independent research before investing
- **B**: Follow the investment choices of others as a guide
- **C**: Avoid all risky investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Social proof:

**Definition:**
- Looking to others' behavior to guide decisions
- "If others are doing it, it must be right"
- Related to herd behavior

**Examples:**
- Buying popular stocks
- Following celebrity investors
- Investing in what friends recommend
- Trendy investment themes

**Why it occurs:**
- Uncertainty → look for guidance
- Assumption others have information
- Desire to belong
- Fear of missing out (FOMO)

**Consequences:**
- Bubbles and crashes
- Overvalued popular stocks
- Undervalued unpopular stocks

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Social proof replaces independent research
- Option C: May lead to either risk-seeking or avoiding
