# Question

Decision fatigue in investing leads to:

## Options

- **A**: Better decision quality over time
- **B**: Preference for default options and simplified choices as mental energy depletes
- **C**: More thorough analysis of each investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Decision fatigue:

**Definition:**
- Declining quality of decisions over time
- Mental energy is finite
- Leads to avoidance or shortcuts

**Manifestations:**
- Accept defaults
- Avoid decisions entirely
- Make impulsive choices
- Rely on simple heuristics

**Investment implications:**
- Default allocations stick
- End-of-day decisions worse
- Complex menus cause paralysis
- Simplified options preferred

**Mitigation:**
- Make important decisions when fresh
- Limit number of choices
- Use defaults wisely (choice architecture)
- Pre-commit to strategies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Decision quality declines with fatigue
- Option C: Analysis becomes superficial
