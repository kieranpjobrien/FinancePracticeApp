# Question

Value at Risk (VaR) is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: The maximum loss a portfolio can experience
- **B**: The minimum loss expected to occur over a given time period at a specified probability
- **C**: The expected loss on a portfolio under normal market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Value at Risk (VaR) measures the minimum loss expected at a given confidence level over a specified time period.

For example, a 1-day 95% VaR of $1 million means:
- There is a 5% probability of losing at least $1 million in one day
- Alternatively: 95% confident that losses will not exceed $1 million
- The loss could be larger; VaR only states the threshold

Key VaR parameters:
- Confidence level (typically 95% or 99%)
- Time horizon (1-day, 10-day, etc.)
- Currency/value units

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: VaR is NOT the maximum possible loss; extreme losses beyond VaR are possible (tail risk)
- Option C: VaR focuses on tail losses at a specific probability, not expected losses under normal conditions
