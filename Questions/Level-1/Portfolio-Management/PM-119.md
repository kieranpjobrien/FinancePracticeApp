# Question

A stock has a covariance with the market of 0.027. The market variance is 0.018. The stock's beta is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.67
- **B**: 1.50
- **C**: 2.25

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Beta calculation:

**Formula:**
β = Cov(Ri, Rm) / Var(Rm)

Or equivalently:
β = Cov(Ri, Rm) / σ²m

**Calculation:**
β = 0.027 / 0.018
β = 1.50

**Interpretation:**
- Beta of 1.50 means stock is 50% more volatile than market
- If market rises 10%, stock expected to rise 15%
- Higher systematic risk than market

**Alternative formula:**
β = ρ × (σi / σm)

Where ρ is correlation between stock and market.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May have inverted the ratio (0.018/0.027)
- Option C: May have made calculation errors
