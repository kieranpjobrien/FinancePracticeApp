# Question

A return distribution with excess kurtosis (fat tails) would cause a standard VaR model to:

## Options

- **A**: Overestimate the probability of extreme losses
- **B**: Underestimate the probability of extreme losses
- **C**: Have no effect on loss estimates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fat tails and VaR:

**Normal distribution assumption:**
- Standard parametric VaR assumes normality
- Normal distribution has kurtosis of 3

**Fat-tailed distributions:**
- Higher probability of extreme events
- More "weight" in the tails than normal
- Common in financial returns

**Impact on VaR:**
- Normal-based VaR underestimates tail risk
- Extreme events more likely than model predicts
- Historical VaR may capture this better

**Solutions:**
- Historical simulation VaR
- t-distribution or other fat-tailed distributions
- Extreme Value Theory (EVT)
- Stress testing

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Normal assumption underestimates, not overestimates, tail risk
- Option C: Distribution shape significantly impacts VaR accuracy
