# Question

The Capital Market Line (CML) represents portfolios that combine:

## Options

- **A**: Any two risky assets in varying proportions
- **B**: The risk-free asset and the optimal risky portfolio
- **C**: The minimum variance portfolio and the maximum return portfolio

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Capital Market Line (CML):

- Represents all efficient portfolios combining the risk-free asset and the market portfolio (optimal risky portfolio)
- Starts at the risk-free rate on the y-axis
- Is tangent to the efficient frontier at the market portfolio
- Has slope = (E(Rm) - Rf) / σm (Sharpe ratio of market portfolio)

CML equation: E(Rp) = Rf + [(E(Rm) - Rf) / σm] × σp

All points on the CML are efficient portfolios:
- Below tangent point: Lending (investing some in risk-free asset)
- Above tangent point: Borrowing (leveraging the market portfolio)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes combinations of risky assets, not the CML
- Option C: These are points on the efficient frontier, not the CML
