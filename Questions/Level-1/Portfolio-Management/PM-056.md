# Question

A sovereign wealth fund established to save petroleum revenues for future generations would most likely have:

## Options

- **A**: A short time horizon and high liquidity needs
- **B**: A long time horizon and above-average risk tolerance
- **C**: A limited investment universe restricted to domestic securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sovereign Wealth Funds (SWFs) for intergenerational savings:

**Characteristics:**
- Very long/perpetual time horizon
- Funded by commodity revenues (oil, gas, minerals)
- Goal: Preserve wealth for future generations
- Low immediate liquidity needs

**Investment implications:**
- Higher risk tolerance due to long horizon
- Ability to invest in illiquid assets
- Global diversification (often away from domestic economy)
- Equity-oriented allocation

Examples: Norway's Government Pension Fund Global, Abu Dhabi Investment Authority

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Long-term savings SWFs have extended horizons and low liquidity needs
- Option C: Most diversify globally to reduce dependence on domestic (often resource-dependent) economy
