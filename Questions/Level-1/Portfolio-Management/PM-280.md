# Question

Liquidity risk refers to the risk of:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates rising
- **B**: Not being able to sell an asset quickly at fair value
- **C**: Currency fluctuations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity risk:

**Definition:**
- Inability to transact quickly
- Must accept price concession
- Wide bid-ask spreads
- Market impact costs

**Types:**
- Market liquidity risk: Asset hard to sell
- Funding liquidity risk: Cannot meet obligations

**Characteristics of illiquid assets:**
- Low trading volume
- Wide bid-ask spreads
- Large price impact
- Longer time to sell

**Examples:**
- Real estate
- Private equity
- Small-cap stocks
- Exotic derivatives

**Management:**
- Liquidity buffers
- Diversification
- Liquidity risk premium required

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is interest rate risk
- Option C: This is currency/exchange rate risk
