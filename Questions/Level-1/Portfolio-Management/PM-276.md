# Question

Compared to calendar rebalancing, percentage-of-portfolio rebalancing:

## Options

- **A**: Ignores market movements between rebalancing dates
- **B**: Triggers more frequent trades in volatile markets
- **C**: Is simpler to implement and monitor

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rebalancing approaches:

**Calendar rebalancing:**
- Fixed time intervals (monthly, quarterly)
- Simple, predictable
- May miss significant drift
- Lower monitoring costs

**Percentage-of-portfolio rebalancing:**
- Triggered by deviation from targets
- Corridor/threshold based
- More responsive to markets
- Higher monitoring costs

**Volatile markets impact:**
- Percentage approach triggers more trades
- More frequent rebalancing
- Better risk control
- Higher transaction costs

**Trade-off:**
- Transaction costs vs. risk control
- Monitoring costs vs. drift tolerance
- Tax efficiency considerations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Calendar approach ignores interim movements
- Option C: Calendar approach is simpler
