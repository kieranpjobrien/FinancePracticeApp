# Question

Risk budgeting is the process of:

## Options

- **A**: Minimizing all portfolio risks regardless of return
- **B**: Allocating portfolio risk across different sources to maximize return per unit of risk
- **C**: Setting a fixed maximum loss for each trading day

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk budgeting:

**Definition:**
- Allocating a total risk budget across different risk sources
- Optimizing risk-adjusted returns
- Treating risk as a scarce resource

**Process:**
1. Determine overall risk tolerance (risk budget)
2. Identify sources of active risk
3. Allocate risk to sources with highest expected return per unit of risk
4. Monitor risk consumption vs. budget

**Applications:**
- Asset allocation decisions
- Manager selection
- Active vs. passive decisions
- Factor exposure management

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Risk budgeting balances risk and return, not just minimizing risk
- Option C: This describes a stop-loss policy, not risk budgeting
