# Question

A significance level of 1% compared to 5% means:

## Options

- **A**: Less evidence is required to reject the null hypothesis
- **B**: The test is more stringent and requires stronger evidence to reject the null hypothesis
- **C**: The probability of a Type II error decreases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The significance level (α) is the probability of rejecting the null hypothesis when it is true (Type I error). A lower significance level (1% vs 5%) means the analyst is less willing to make a Type I error and requires stronger evidence (a smaller p-value) to reject the null hypothesis. This makes the test more conservative or stringent.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of correct; lower α requires more evidence, not less
- Option C is incorrect; holding sample size constant, a lower α actually increases the probability of Type II error (they trade off against each other)
