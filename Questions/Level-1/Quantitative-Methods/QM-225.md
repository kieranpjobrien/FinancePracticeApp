# Question

The risk-return tradeoff principle states that:

## Options

- **A**: Lower-risk investments always have higher returns
- **B**: Investors require higher expected returns to accept higher risk
- **C**: Risk and return are unrelated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The risk-return tradeoff is fundamental to finance: investors must be compensated for bearing risk. Higher-risk investments must offer higher expected returns to attract investors; otherwise, rational investors would choose lower-risk alternatives. This principle underlies asset pricing models like CAPM and explains why equity returns historically exceed bond returns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores the fundamental relationship between risk and expected return
