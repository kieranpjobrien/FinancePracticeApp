# Question

The money-weighted rate of return is most appropriately described as:

## Options

- **A**: The internal rate of return that equates the present value of cash inflows to cash outflows
- **B**: The geometric mean of holding period returns
- **C**: The return earned per dollar invested regardless of timing

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The money-weighted rate of return (MWRR) is the internal rate of return (IRR) of an investment, considering the timing and magnitude of all cash flows. It equates the present value of all cash outflows (investments) to the present value of all cash inflows (withdrawals plus ending value). The MWRR is sensitive to the timing of cash flows, making it suitable for evaluating individual investor performance but less suitable for comparing investment managers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes the time-weighted rate of return, not money-weighted
- Option C describes an approach that ignores timing, which is the opposite of MWRR's sensitivity to timing
