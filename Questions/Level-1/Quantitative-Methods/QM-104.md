# Question

When comparing the variances of two populations, the appropriate test statistic follows:

## Options

- **A**: The t-distribution
- **B**: The chi-square distribution
- **C**: The F-distribution

## Answer

C

## Explanation

The F-test is used to compare two population variances. The test statistic is the ratio of the two sample variances (F = s₁²/s₂²), which follows an F-distribution with degrees of freedom (n₁-1, n₂-1). The F-distribution is right-skewed and bounded at zero, appropriate for ratios of positive quantities like variances.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (t-distribution) is used for comparing means, not variances
- Option B (chi-square) is used for testing a single variance against a hypothesized value, not comparing two variances
