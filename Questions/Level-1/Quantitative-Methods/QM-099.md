# Question

A portfolio consisting of a risk-free asset and a risky portfolio will have a standard deviation that:

## Options

- **A**: Is always higher than the risky portfolio's standard deviation
- **B**: Equals the weight in the risky portfolio multiplied by its standard deviation
- **C**: Is undefined because risk-free assets cannot be combined with risky assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When combining a risk-free asset (σ = 0) with a risky portfolio, the combined portfolio's standard deviation is:

σp = wrisky × σrisky

Since the risk-free asset has zero standard deviation and zero correlation with any asset, only the risky portion contributes to portfolio risk. The relationship is linear along the Capital Allocation Line (CAL).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; adding the risk-free asset reduces overall portfolio risk
- Option C is incorrect; combining risk-free and risky assets is fundamental to portfolio theory
