# Question

When the NPV and IRR methods give conflicting rankings for mutually exclusive projects, the most appropriate decision criterion is:

## Options

- **A**: IRR, because it shows the actual return on investment
- **B**: NPV, because it measures the absolute increase in shareholder wealth
- **C**: Accept both projects regardless of the conflict

## Answer

B

## Explanation

For mutually exclusive projects (where only one can be chosen), NPV is the superior criterion because it directly measures the dollar increase in firm value. IRR conflicts can arise due to differences in project scale, timing of cash flows, or reinvestment rate assumptions. NPV assumes reinvestment at the cost of capital, which is generally more realistic than IRR's assumption of reinvestment at the IRR itself.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overlooks NPV's focus on wealth maximization and IRR's reinvestment assumption problems
- Option C is not possible by definition—mutually exclusive projects require choosing one
