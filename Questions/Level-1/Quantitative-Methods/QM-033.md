# Question

When constructing a confidence interval for the population mean with an unknown population variance and a small sample, an analyst should use:

## Options

- **A**: The z-distribution because it is always more accurate
- **B**: The t-distribution with n-1 degrees of freedom
- **C**: The chi-square distribution with n degrees of freedom

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When the population variance is unknown and must be estimated from the sample, the t-distribution is appropriate. The t-distribution has heavier tails than the normal distribution, reflecting additional uncertainty from estimating the variance. The degrees of freedom equal n-1, where n is the sample size. As sample size increases, the t-distribution approaches the z-distribution, but for small samples the difference is significant.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect because the z-distribution assumes known population variance; using it when variance is estimated understates uncertainty
- Option C is incorrect because the chi-square distribution is used for variance estimation and hypothesis tests, not for confidence intervals of means
