# Question

A time series is considered stationary if:

## Options

- **A**: Its values are always positive
- **B**: Its mean, variance, and autocovariances do not change over time
- **C**: It exhibits a clear upward or downward trend

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A stationary time series has statistical properties (mean, variance, and covariance structure) that do not depend on when the series is observed. Weak stationarity requires constant mean, constant variance, and autocovariances that depend only on the lag between observations, not on time. Many statistical techniques require stationarity; non-stationary series often need transformation (differencing, detrending) before analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses stationarity with a constraint on values
- Option C describes a trending series, which is the opposite of stationary
