# Question

A distribution has a mean of 50 and standard deviation of 10. The z-score for an observation of 70 is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.5
- **B**: 2.0
- **C**: 7.0

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Z-score = (Observation - Mean) / Standard Deviation

Z = (70 - 50) / 10
Z = 20 / 10
Z = 2.0

A z-score of 2.0 means the observation is 2 standard deviations above the mean.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A divides the difference by the wrong value
- Option C divides the observation by standard deviation without subtracting the mean first
