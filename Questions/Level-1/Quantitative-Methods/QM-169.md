# Question

The Capital Market Line (CML) shows the relationship between:

## Options

- **A**: Expected return and beta for individual securities
- **B**: Expected return and total risk for efficient portfolios
- **C**: Historical return and volatility for all portfolios

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The CML shows expected return as a function of total risk (standard deviation) for efficient portfolios—those composed of the risk-free asset and the market portfolio. The CML passes through the risk-free rate and the market portfolio, with slope equal to the Sharpe ratio of the market portfolio.

CML: E(Rp) = Rf + [(E(Rm) - Rf) / σm] × σp

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the Security Market Line (SML), which uses beta
- Option C describes historical observation, not the theoretical CML
