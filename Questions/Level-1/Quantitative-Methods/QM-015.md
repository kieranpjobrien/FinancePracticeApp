# Question

Compared to a normal distribution, a distribution with excess kurtosis greater than zero (leptokurtic) will have:

## Options

- **A**: Thinner tails and a flatter peak
- **B**: Fatter tails and a more peaked center
- **C**: The same tail thickness but different symmetry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leptokurtic distributions (positive excess kurtosis) have fatter tails and more peaked centers than the normal distribution. This means more observations near the mean AND more extreme observations in the tails. For investors, this implies a higher probability of extreme returns (both positive and negative) than a normal distribution would suggest.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a platykurtic distribution (negative excess kurtosis)
- Option C confuses kurtosis with skewness
