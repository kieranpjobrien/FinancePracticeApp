# Question

In a portfolio, the probability of a credit downgrade is 15% and the probability of an interest rate increase is 30%. If these events are independent, the probability that at least one occurs is:

## Options

- **A**: 40.5%
- **B**: 45.0%
- **C**: 45.5%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For the probability that at least one event occurs:

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

For independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) = 0.15 × 0.30 = 0.045

P(A or B) = 0.15 + 0.30 - 0.045
P(A or B) = 0.405 = 40.5%

Alternatively: P(at least one) = 1 - P(neither) = 1 - (0.85 × 0.70) = 1 - 0.595 = 0.405

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B simply adds the probabilities without subtracting the joint probability
- Option C results from calculation errors in the joint probability
