# Question

A sample of 100 returns has a mean of 8% and a standard deviation of 20%. The 95% confidence interval for the population mean is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 4.08% to 11.92%
- **B**: 6.04% to 9.96%
- **C**: -31.2% to 47.2%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The confidence interval is calculated as: Sample mean ± (z-score × Standard error)

Standard error = σ/√n = 20%/√100 = 20%/10 = 2%

For a 95% confidence interval, z = 1.96

Confidence interval = 8% ± (1.96 × 2%)
= 8% ± 3.92%
= 4.08% to 11.92%

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses z = 1.0 instead of 1.96, resulting in a narrower interval
- Option C uses the raw standard deviation (20%) instead of the standard error (2%), which incorrectly applies the variability of individual observations rather than the precision of the sample mean
