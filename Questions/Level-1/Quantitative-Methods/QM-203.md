# Question

The binomial distribution approaches the normal distribution when:

## Options

- **A**: The number of trials is small
- **B**: The number of trials is large and the probability of success is not extreme
- **C**: The probability of success equals exactly 0.5

## Answer

B

## Explanation

By the de Moivre-Laplace theorem, as n (number of trials) increases, the binomial distribution approaches a normal distribution. This approximation works best when n is large and p (probability of success) is not too close to 0 or 1. A common rule of thumb is that np ≥ 5 and n(1-p) ≥ 5 for the normal approximation to be reasonable.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; small n produces a clearly discrete distribution
- Option C is not required; p can differ from 0.5 as long as n is large enough
