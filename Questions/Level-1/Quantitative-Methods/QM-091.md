# Question

A 90-day Treasury bill with a face value of $10,000 is quoted at a bank discount yield of 4%. The purchase price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $9,600
- **B**: $9,900
- **C**: $9,901

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bank discount yield calculates the discount as a percentage of face value:

Discount = Face Value × (Bank Discount Yield) × (Days to Maturity / 360)

Discount = $10,000 × 0.04 × (90/360)
Discount = $10,000 × 0.04 × 0.25
Discount = $100

Purchase Price = Face Value - Discount
Purchase Price = $10,000 - $100 = $9,900

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses a full year's discount instead of the 90-day fraction
- Option C might result from using 365 days or calculation errors
