# Question

A regression of bond prices on interest rates yields a negative slope coefficient. This indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: Higher interest rates are associated with higher bond prices
- **B**: Higher interest rates are associated with lower bond prices
- **C**: There is no relationship between interest rates and bond prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A negative slope indicates an inverse relationship: as the independent variable (interest rates) increases, the dependent variable (bond prices) decreases. This is consistent with fundamental bond pricing—when rates rise, the present value of fixed future cash flows falls, reducing bond prices. The negative sign confirms this inverse relationship.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a positive relationship, which would have a positive slope
- Option C is incorrect when there is a statistically significant slope coefficient
