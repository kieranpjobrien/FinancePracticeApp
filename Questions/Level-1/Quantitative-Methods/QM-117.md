# Question

The interquartile range (IQR) is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Maximum value minus minimum value
- **B**: 75th percentile minus 25th percentile
- **C**: Mean plus or minus one standard deviation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The interquartile range (IQR) is the difference between the third quartile (Q3, 75th percentile) and the first quartile (Q1, 25th percentile). It represents the range of the middle 50% of observations. The IQR is resistant to outliers, making it a useful measure of dispersion when extreme values are present.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the range, not the interquartile range
- Option C describes a different concept related to the normal distribution
