# Question

A high R-squared in a regression:

## Options

- **A**: Proves that X causes Y
- **B**: Indicates strong association but does not establish causation
- **C**: Guarantees accurate predictions for all future values

## Answer

B

## Explanation

R-squared measures the strength of the linear relationship between variables but cannot establish causation. A high R-squared indicates that X and Y move together, but this could be because: (1) X causes Y, (2) Y causes X, (3) both are caused by a third variable, or (4) the relationship is coincidental. Establishing causation requires additional evidence beyond statistical association.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses correlation with causation
- Option C ignores that models can fail on new data, especially if conditions change
