# Question

A size premium is added to the cost of equity for smaller companies because:

## Options

- **A**: Larger companies have higher systematic risk
- **B**: Smaller companies have higher total risk and less liquidity
- **C**: Size has no relationship to required returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Small-cap premium: smaller firms historically earn higher returns (size effect). Explanations include higher business risk, less liquidity, less analyst coverage, and higher information asymmetry. In cost of capital estimation, a size premium compensates for these additional risks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores empirical evidence of size effects
