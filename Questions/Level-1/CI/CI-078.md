# Question

Dual-class share structures:

## Options

- **A**: Give all shareholders equal voting rights
- **B**: Provide different voting rights to different share classes
- **C**: Are prohibited in all markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dual-class structures have shares with different voting rights (e.g., Class A with 10 votes per share, Class B with 1 vote). This allows founders/insiders to maintain control with minority ownership. Critics argue it weakens shareholder democracy; supporters cite long-term focus benefits.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes single-class structures
- Option C is incorrect—many markets allow dual-class shares
