# Question

A special (extra) dividend is typically:

## Options

- **A**: A commitment to ongoing higher dividend payments
- **B**: A one-time payment not expected to be repeated regularly
- **C**: Required by law when profits exceed a threshold

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Special dividend: one-time distribution, often from unusual profits, asset sales, or accumulated cash. Signals no commitment to higher regular dividends. Allows returning excess cash without raising regular dividend expectations. Common after significant transactions or exceptional earnings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes regular dividend increases
- Option C is not a legal requirement in most jurisdictions
