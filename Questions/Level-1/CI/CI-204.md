# Question

The underinvestment problem (debt overhang) occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Companies invest in too many projects
- **B**: Highly leveraged firms forgo positive NPV projects because benefits would largely go to creditors
- **C**: Bondholders invest directly in company projects

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt overhang: when company is near or in distress, shareholders may reject positive NPV projects. Reason: investment benefits accrue primarily to creditors (reducing default probability), while shareholders bear costs. Wealth transfer discourages value-creating investment. Agency cost of debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes overinvestment, opposite problem
- Option C misidentifies how investment decisions are made
