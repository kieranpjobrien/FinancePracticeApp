# Question

An expansion project differs from a replacement project in that:

## Options

- **A**: Expansion projects have no incremental cash flows
- **B**: Expansion projects typically increase capacity and revenue, while replacement maintains current operations
- **C**: Replacement projects always have positive NPV

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Expansion projects: add new capacity, products, or markets—generating incremental revenue. Replacement projects: replace worn assets to maintain current operations—focus on cost savings and efficiency. Expansion typically involves more uncertainty and requires revenue forecasting.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is false—expansion projects have significant incremental flows
- Option C is not guaranteed for any project type
