# Question

A banker's acceptance is:

## Options

- **A**: A bank's rejection of a loan application
- **B**: A time draft guaranteed by a bank, used to finance international trade
- **C**: A type of equity security

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Banker's acceptances: short-term debt instruments where a bank guarantees payment. Commonly used in international trade—importers get goods before payment; exporters get bank-guaranteed payment. The bank's guarantee makes them low-risk and negotiable in secondary markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misinterprets the term
- Option C misclassifies the security type
