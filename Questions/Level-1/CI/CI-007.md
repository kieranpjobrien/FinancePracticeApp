# Question

Separating the roles of CEO and board chairman is considered good governance because:

## Options

- **A**: It reduces the number of executives needed
- **B**: It provides independent oversight of management
- **C**: It eliminates the need for independent directors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Separating CEO (manages company) and chairman (leads board) reduces concentration of power and ensures independent board oversight. The chairman can objectively evaluate CEO performance and represent shareholder interests. Combined roles create potential conflicts and weaken board independence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands—the roles require different skills, not fewer people
- Option C is incorrect—independent directors remain essential regardless of structure
