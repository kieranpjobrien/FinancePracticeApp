# Question

A break point in the marginal cost of capital schedule occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: The company decides to stop investing
- **B**: A low-cost source of capital is exhausted, causing marginal cost to increase
- **C**: Interest rates decline in the market

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Break point: capital level where cost of a component changes. Example: retained earnings exhausted, requiring more expensive new equity. Break point = Amount of lower-cost capital available / Weight of that component. Beyond break point, marginal cost rises, affecting investment decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't define a break point
- Option C would shift entire cost curve, not create break point
