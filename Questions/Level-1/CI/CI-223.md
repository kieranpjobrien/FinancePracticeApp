# Question

A pure-play method for estimating a project's beta involves:

## Options

- **A**: Using the company's overall beta for all projects
- **B**: Finding comparable companies focused on similar businesses and adjusting their betas for leverage differences
- **C**: Assuming all projects have beta of 1.0

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pure-play method: find publicly traded companies whose sole business matches the project. Unlever their betas to remove financing effects, then relever using the project's target leverage. Provides project-specific risk estimate when project differs significantly from company's existing operations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores project-specific risk
- Option C is arbitrary and ignores actual risk differences
