# Question

Using an industry average beta rather than company-specific beta may be appropriate when:

## Options

- **A**: The company has a very stable stock price history
- **B**: The company's historical beta is unreliable due to limited trading history
- **C**: The company operates in multiple unrelated industries

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Industry beta: when company beta is unreliable (new public company, thin trading, major changes), industry average beta may better represent systematic risk. Assumption: the company has similar operating risk to industry peers. Adjust for leverage differences when comparing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A suggests reliable company beta is available
- Option C complicates industry beta usage—no single industry applies
