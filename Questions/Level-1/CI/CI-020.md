# Question

Financial leverage increases:

## Options

- **A**: Operating risk
- **B**: The variability of returns to equity holders
- **C**: The company's asset base

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Financial leverage (using debt) magnifies returns to equity—both gains and losses. Fixed interest payments mean equity absorbs more variability. High leverage increases equity beta and required return. Operating risk relates to business operations, not capital structure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses operating and financial risk
- Option C describes asset purchases, not leverage effects
