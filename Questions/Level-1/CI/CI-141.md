# Question

Dividend reinvestment plans (DRIPs) allow shareholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Defer taxes on dividends indefinitely
- **B**: Automatically use dividends to purchase additional shares
- **C**: Receive higher dividend rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DRIPs: shareholders elect to reinvest dividends in additional company shares, often at a small discount and without commissions. This provides automatic compounding and savings. Dividends are still taxable when received, even if reinvested.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—dividends are taxed when paid, even if reinvested
- Option C—DRIPs don't increase dividend rates
