# Question

Carrying costs of inventory include:

## Options

- **A**: Ordering and shipping costs only
- **B**: Storage, insurance, obsolescence risk, and opportunity cost of capital
- **C**: Cost of goods sold

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Carrying costs are expenses of holding inventory: storage/warehousing, insurance, spoilage/obsolescence, theft, and opportunity cost (capital tied up). Higher inventory means higher carrying costs. Order costs and COGS are separate considerations. Optimal inventory balances carrying costs against stockout costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes ordering costs, not carrying costs
- Option C is an income statement item, not holding cost
