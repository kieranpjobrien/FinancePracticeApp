# Question

Financial flexibility in capital structure refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Having maximum debt at all times
- **B**: Maintaining reserve borrowing capacity for future opportunities or needs
- **C**: Never using debt financing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Financial flexibility: ability to raise capital when needed at reasonable cost. Companies may maintain below-optimal leverage to preserve spare debt capacity for acquisitions, economic downturns, or unexpected opportunities. This option value offsets some tax benefit of higher debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A eliminates future borrowing capacity
- Option C sacrifices tax benefits unnecessarily
