# Question

ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) factors are increasingly considered by:

## Options

- **A**: Only activist investors
- **B**: Mainstream investors as part of risk and opportunity assessment
- **C**: Regulators, but not investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ESG integration considers environmental (climate, pollution), social (labor, community), and governance (board, compensation) factors in investment analysis. Evidence suggests ESG factors affect long-term performance and risk. Mainstream investors increasingly incorporate ESG into fundamental analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A understates ESG's broad adoption
- Option C ignores growing investor demand for ESG analysis
