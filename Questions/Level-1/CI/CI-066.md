# Question

High operating leverage combined with high financial leverage results in:

## Options

- **A**: Very stable earnings
- **B**: Very volatile equity returns
- **C**: Reduced total risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operating leverage (fixed operating costs) and financial leverage (fixed financing costs) compound: both magnify earnings variability. High operating leverage + high financial leverage = very high total risk. Small sales changes create large swings in equity returns—"leveraging leverage."

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the effect
- Option C ignores the compounding of risks
