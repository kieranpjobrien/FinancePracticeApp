# Question

Activist investors typically:

## Options

- **A**: Hold diversified portfolios without engaging with companies
- **B**: Take significant stakes and push for strategic or governance changes
- **C**: Only invest in government bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Activist investors acquire meaningful stakes and advocate for changes: strategic shifts (divestitures, M&A), governance improvements (board composition), financial restructuring (dividends, buybacks), or operational changes. They may seek board seats and use public campaigns to pressure management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes passive investors
- Option C describes fixed-income, not activist equity investing
