# Question

The primary objective of cash management is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize cash holdings at all times
- **B**: Maintain sufficient liquidity while investing excess cash
- **C**: Eliminate all cash balances

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash management balances liquidity needs against the opportunity cost of holding idle cash. Too little cash risks inability to meet obligations; too much forgoes investment returns. The goal is optimal cash levels—sufficient for operations and contingencies while investing excess.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores opportunity costs of idle cash
- Option C is impractical—some cash is needed for operations
