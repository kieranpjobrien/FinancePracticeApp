# Question

Trade credit as a source of short-term financing:

## Options

- **A**: Requires formal loan documentation
- **B**: Is often the most convenient and widely used source
- **C**: Is only available to large corporations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trade credit (accounts payable) is financing provided by suppliers through delayed payment terms. It's informal, doesn't require separate loan applications, and is available to most businesses. It's a significant source of short-term financing, especially for smaller firms. Early payment discounts affect its true cost.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes formal borrowing, not trade credit
- Option C ignores that trade credit is available to all sizes
