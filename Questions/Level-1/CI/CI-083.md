# Question

When analyzing projects with inflation:

## Options

- **A**: Always use real cash flows with nominal discount rates
- **B**: Nominal cash flows should be discounted at nominal rates; real cash flows at real rates
- **C**: Inflation effects should be ignored

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Consistency is key: nominal cash flows (including inflation) use nominal discount rates; real cash flows (inflation-adjusted) use real rates. Mixing them produces errors. Both approaches give the same NPV if applied correctly. Depreciation tax shields are fixed in nominal terms.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A mixes real and nominal incorrectly
- Option C ignores real economic effects of inflation
