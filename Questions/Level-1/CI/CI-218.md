# Question

A committed line of credit differs from an uncommitted line in that a committed line:

## Options

- **A**: Has no borrowing limit
- **B**: Legally obligates the bank to lend up to the specified amount
- **C**: Has lower fees than uncommitted facilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Committed line of credit: bank contractually obligated to lend up to limit for the term (typically one year). Provides financing certainty. Uncommitted line: bank can refuse to lend. Committed lines have commitment fees but offer reliability—important for liquidity planning.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—both have limits
- Option C is backwards—committed lines typically cost more due to guarantee
