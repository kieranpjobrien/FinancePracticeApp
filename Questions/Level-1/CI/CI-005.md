# Question

An independent board director is one who:

## Options

- **A**: Is also a senior executive of the company
- **B**: Has no material relationship with the company
- **C**: Owns significant shares in the company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Independent directors have no material business, family, or financial relationship with the company that could compromise objectivity. They provide unbiased oversight of management, chair key committees (audit, compensation, nominating), and represent shareholder interests. Stock ownership alone doesn't disqualify independence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an inside/executive director
- Option C describes a significant shareholder, which may actually enhance alignment
