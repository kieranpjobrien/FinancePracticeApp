# Question

A company has net income of $10 million and pays $3 million in dividends. Its dividend payout ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 30%
- **B**: 70%
- **C**: 33%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Dividend payout ratio = Dividends / Net income = $3 million / $10 million = 30%. This means 30% of earnings are distributed as dividends; 70% is retained (retention ratio). Payout ratio indicates management's dividend policy and reinvestment strategy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is the retention ratio (1 - payout ratio)
- Option C incorrectly calculates the ratio
