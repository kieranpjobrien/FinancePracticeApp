# Question

Share repurchases return cash to shareholders by:

## Options

- **A**: Issuing new shares
- **B**: Buying back outstanding shares, increasing ownership percentage of remaining shareholders
- **C**: Paying special dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Share repurchases (buybacks) use company cash to purchase outstanding shares in the open market or through tender offers. This reduces shares outstanding, increases EPS (same earnings, fewer shares), and gives remaining shareholders a larger ownership stake. It's an alternative to dividends for returning cash.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite—increasing, not reducing shares
- Option C describes dividends, a different cash distribution method
