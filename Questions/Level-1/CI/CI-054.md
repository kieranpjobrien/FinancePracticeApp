# Question

In a one-tier (unitary) board structure:

## Options

- **A**: Supervisory and management boards are separate
- **B**: Executive and non-executive directors serve on a single board
- **C**: Only shareholders can serve as directors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

One-tier boards (common in US/UK) have both executive directors (company managers) and non-executive/independent directors on a single board. Two-tier boards (Germany, Netherlands) separate the supervisory board from the management board. One-tier boards allow closer interaction but potential independence concerns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the two-tier system
- Option C is not a requirement in either system
