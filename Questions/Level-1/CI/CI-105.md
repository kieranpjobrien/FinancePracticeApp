# Question

The business judgment rule protects directors who:

## Options

- **A**: Make decisions resulting in losses, regardless of process
- **B**: Make informed, good-faith decisions without conflicts of interest
- **C**: Follow management recommendations without question

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The business judgment rule presumes directors acted on an informed basis, in good faith, and honestly believing the action was in the company's best interest. It protects against liability for honest mistakes but not for negligence, self-dealing, or uninformed decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the requirement for proper process
- Option C describes rubber-stamping, not informed judgment
