# Question

A company with a current ratio of 2.0 and quick ratio of 0.5 likely has:

## Options

- **A**: High cash balances
- **B**: Large inventory relative to other current assets
- **C**: High accounts receivable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The difference between current ratio (includes inventory) and quick ratio (excludes inventory) reveals inventory's weight in current assets. A current ratio of 2.0 with quick ratio of 0.5 means inventory comprises most current assets—potentially concerning for liquidity if inventory is slow-moving.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would increase both ratios similarly
- Option C would increase quick ratio, not decrease it
