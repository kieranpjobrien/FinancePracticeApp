# Question

The marginal cost of capital is:

## Options

- **A**: The average cost of all previously issued capital
- **B**: The cost of raising an additional dollar of capital
- **C**: The cost of the cheapest source of financing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Marginal cost of capital is the cost of raising one more dollar of capital—the relevant rate for new investment decisions. As companies raise more capital, costs may increase (moving up the supply curve). The investment opportunity schedule intersects marginal cost to determine optimal capital budget.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes historical/average cost
- Option C ignores that capital structure optimizes total cost
