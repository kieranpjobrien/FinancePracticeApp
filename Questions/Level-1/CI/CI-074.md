# Question

A share repurchase financed with excess cash will:

## Options

- **A**: Always increase earnings per share
- **B**: Increase EPS if the P/E ratio exceeds the after-tax interest rate on cash
- **C**: Always decrease earnings per share

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Repurchase effect on EPS depends on cash yield foregone vs. shares reduced. If cash earns less than earnings yield (E/P = 1/P/E), reducing shares increases EPS. If company P/E is 20 (E/P = 5%), excess cash earning 3% after-tax should be used for buybacks—net accretive.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the opportunity cost of cash
- Option C ignores the earnings yield comparison
