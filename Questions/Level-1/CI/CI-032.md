# Question

A proxy fight occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Shareholders vote on routine matters
- **B**: Dissident shareholders solicit votes to replace current board members
- **C**: The company issues new shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proxy fights: activist shareholders or rival groups solicit proxies (voting rights) from other shareholders to vote against management's proposals or elect their own board candidates. It's a mechanism for shareholder activism and corporate control contests when negotiations fail.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes normal proxy voting
- Option C describes equity issuance, unrelated to proxy contests
