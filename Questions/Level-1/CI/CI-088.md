# Question

Debt covenants are used to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the cost of debt
- **B**: Protect bondholders by restricting borrower actions
- **C**: Guarantee debt repayment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covenants are contractual restrictions protecting bondholders: financial covenants (minimum coverage, maximum leverage), negative covenants (restrictions on dividends, additional debt, asset sales), and affirmative covenants (reporting requirements). They limit agency costs and reduce default risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is a consequence, not purpose—covenants may reduce cost by reducing risk
- Option C overstates protection—covenants don't guarantee repayment
