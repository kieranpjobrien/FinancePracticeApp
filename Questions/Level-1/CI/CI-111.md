# Question

A company has $50 million in debt at 6% interest and a 25% tax rate. The annual tax shield is:

## Options

- **A**: $750,000
- **B**: $3,000,000
- **C**: $12,500,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Interest expense = $50M × 6% = $3M. Tax shield = Interest × Tax rate = $3M × 25% = $750,000. This represents annual tax savings from deducting interest. The present value of perpetual tax shields at the cost of debt is T × D.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is the interest expense, not the tax shield
- Option C multiplies debt by tax rate incorrectly
