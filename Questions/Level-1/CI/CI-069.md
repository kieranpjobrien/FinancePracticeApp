# Question

According to pecking order theory, issuing equity signals that:

## Options

- **A**: The company has excellent investment opportunities
- **B**: Management believes the stock may be overvalued
- **C**: The company has strong internal cash flows

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pecking order: if management (with superior information) issues equity, markets infer overvaluation—why sell ownership cheaply if undervalued? Stock prices often decline on equity issuance announcements. Debt issuance is less information-sensitive since debt value is less affected by firm value uncertainty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes investment opportunity signaling
- Option C would reduce need for external financing
