# Question

On the ex-dividend date:

## Options

- **A**: The company's stock price typically increases by the dividend amount
- **B**: Buyers no longer receive the upcoming dividend, and the price typically drops by approximately the dividend amount
- **C**: The dividend is paid to shareholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ex-dividend date: first day stock trades without right to declared dividend. Buyers on or after this date don't receive dividend. Stock price typically opens lower by roughly the dividend amount, reflecting that new buyers miss the payment. Record date follows ex-date; payment date is later.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—price drops, not increases
- Option C confuses ex-date with payment date
