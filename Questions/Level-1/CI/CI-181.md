# Question

The bond yield plus risk premium approach estimates cost of equity as:

## Options

- **A**: Risk-free rate plus beta times market risk premium
- **B**: Company's bond yield plus an equity risk premium
- **C**: Dividend yield plus expected growth rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bond yield plus risk premium: Cost of equity = YTM on company's long-term debt + Risk premium (typically 3-5%). Logic: equity is riskier than debt, so add premium to observed debt cost. Simple approach when bond yields available but beta unreliable. Risk premium based on historical relationships.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the CAPM approach
- Option C describes the dividend discount model approach
