# Question

The correct way to incorporate flotation costs in capital budgeting is to:

## Options

- **A**: Add flotation costs to the discount rate
- **B**: Increase the initial cash outflow by the flotation costs
- **C**: Ignore flotation costs as they are immaterial

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Flotation costs should be treated as part of the project's initial investment, not added to the discount rate. Adding to WACC would overstate the cost across all future periods. Correct treatment: add flotation costs to Year 0 outflow, keeping discount rate pure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates the ongoing cost of capital
- Option C ignores real costs that can be significant for equity issues
