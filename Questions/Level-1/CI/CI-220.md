# Question

Cumulative voting for board elections:

## Options

- **A**: Requires shareholders to vote for all nominees equally
- **B**: Allows shareholders to concentrate votes on fewer candidates, improving minority representation
- **C**: Gives management extra voting power

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cumulative voting: shareholders receive votes equal to shares × number of positions. Can allocate all votes to one candidate or spread across several. Enables minority shareholders to elect at least one director. Contrast with straight voting where majority controls all seats.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes straight/statutory voting
- Option C is opposite—cumulative voting helps minority shareholders
