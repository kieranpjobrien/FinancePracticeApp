# Question

Using a single company-wide hurdle rate for all projects:

## Options

- **A**: Is always the optimal approach
- **B**: May lead to accepting too many high-risk projects and rejecting good low-risk projects
- **C**: Eliminates the need for project-specific analysis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Single hurdle rate problem: if WACC used for all projects, high-risk projects (which should require higher returns) may be accepted because their IRR exceeds average WACC. Low-risk projects (deserving lower hurdle) may be rejected. Result: suboptimal project selection biasing toward risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores risk variation across projects
- Option C understates needed project-level analysis
