# Question

A change in the estimated useful life of a depreciable asset is:

## Options

- **A**: Treated retrospectively with restatement of prior periods
- **B**: Applied prospectively from the date of change
- **C**: Reported as an error correction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Changes in accounting estimates (useful life, salvage value, bad debt percentages) are applied prospectively—the new estimate affects current and future periods, not prior periods. The remaining book value is depreciated over the revised remaining useful life. This differs from changes in accounting policy (retrospective).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to accounting policy changes, not estimates
- Option C applies to errors, not revised estimates
