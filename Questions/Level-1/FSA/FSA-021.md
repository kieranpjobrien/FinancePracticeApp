# Question

A deferred tax liability arises when:

## Options

- **A**: Tax expense exceeds taxes payable in the current period
- **B**: Taxes payable exceed tax expense in the current period
- **C**: A company has unused tax loss carryforwards

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Deferred tax liabilities (DTL) arise when accounting income exceeds taxable income—creating a temporary difference that will reverse in the future. Common causes include accelerated depreciation for tax purposes or revenue recognized for books before it's taxable. The company will owe more taxes in future periods as these differences reverse.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes creation of a deferred tax asset
- Option C describes a deferred tax asset from operating losses
