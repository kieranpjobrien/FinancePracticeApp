# Question

Information is considered material if:

## Options

- **A**: It exceeds a fixed dollar threshold
- **B**: Its omission or misstatement could influence users' decisions
- **C**: It relates to related party transactions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Materiality is judged by whether information would influence reasonable users' economic decisions. It's entity-specific and considers both quantitative (size) and qualitative (nature) factors. There's no universal fixed threshold—what's material depends on context. Related party transactions may be material but aren't automatically so.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that materiality is relative to entity size and context
- Option C describes a disclosure category, not the definition of materiality
