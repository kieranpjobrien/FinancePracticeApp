# Question

A contract asset differs from a receivable in that:

## Options

- **A**: Contract assets are owed by related parties only
- **B**: The right to payment is conditional on something other than passage of time
- **C**: Contract assets are non-current while receivables are current

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under IFRS 15/ASC 606, a contract asset exists when a company has satisfied a performance obligation but payment is conditional on future performance. A receivable exists when the right to payment is unconditional (only passage of time required). Contract assets involve more uncertainty about collection.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands the distinction
- Option C is incorrect—both can be current or non-current
