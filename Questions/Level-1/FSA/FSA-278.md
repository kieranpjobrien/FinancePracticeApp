# Question

A contingent asset is recognized when:

## Options

- **A**: The inflow of economic benefits is probable
- **B**: The inflow of economic benefits is virtually certain
- **C**: Any possibility of benefit exists

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unlike liabilities (recognized when probable), contingent assets require virtual certainty for recognition—a higher threshold reflecting conservatism. When probable but not virtually certain, disclosure is required. When only possible, no disclosure is needed. This asymmetry prevents overstating assets and income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the threshold for contingent liabilities
- Option C is too low a threshold for any disclosure
