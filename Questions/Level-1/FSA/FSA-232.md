# Question

The specific identification inventory method is most appropriate for:

## Options

- **A**: Commodity products with high turnover
- **B**: Unique, high-value items
- **C**: Standardized manufactured goods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Specific identification tracks the actual cost of each individual item, matching specific costs to specific sales. It's practical only for distinguishable, high-value items (automobiles, jewelry, real estate). For homogeneous products, cost flow assumptions (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average) are used since individual tracking is impractical.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would use FIFO or weighted average
- Option C typically uses standard cost flow assumptions
