# Question

Converting from indirect to direct method CFO presentation requires:

## Options

- **A**: Only rearranging existing cash flow data
- **B**: Additional information about gross cash receipts and payments
- **C**: Auditor approval

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The indirect method shows net adjustments, not gross cash flows. Converting to direct requires breaking out: cash collected from customers (revenue ± receivables change), cash paid to suppliers (COGS ± inventory ± payables), etc. This detail isn't available from indirect presentation alone.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A underestimates the information required
- Option C is not a requirement for presentation method
