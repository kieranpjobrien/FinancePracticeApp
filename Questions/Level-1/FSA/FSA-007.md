# Question

Under accrual accounting, revenue is recognized when:

## Options

- **A**: Cash is received from customers
- **B**: The performance obligation is satisfied
- **C**: An invoice is sent to the customer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accrual accounting recognizes revenue when earned (performance obligation satisfied), regardless of cash timing. Under IFRS 15/ASC 606, revenue is recognized when control of goods or services transfers to the customer. This provides a better measure of economic activity than cash-basis accounting, which only records transactions when cash changes hands.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cash-basis accounting
- Option C is too early—invoicing doesn't mean the obligation is satisfied
