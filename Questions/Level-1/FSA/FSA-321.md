# Question

Notes to financial statements:

## Options

- **A**: Are optional supplementary information
- **B**: Are an integral part of the financial statements
- **C**: Only summarize the main financial statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Notes are an essential component of financial statements, providing details not shown in the face of statements: accounting policies, disaggregated data, commitments, contingencies, and significant estimates. The auditor's opinion covers both statements and notes. Without notes, statements would be incomplete for analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A understates their importance—notes are required
- Option C ignores that notes provide additional information
