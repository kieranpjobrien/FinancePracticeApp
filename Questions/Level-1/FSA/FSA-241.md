# Question

The IASB (International Accounting Standards Board) is responsible for:

## Options

- **A**: Enforcing compliance with IFRS
- **B**: Developing and issuing IFRS
- **C**: Auditing multinational corporations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The IASB develops and issues International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). Enforcement is the responsibility of securities regulators in each jurisdiction. The IASB operates under the IFRS Foundation and works toward global accounting standard convergence. Auditing is performed by independent auditors, not standard setters.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the role of securities regulators
- Option C describes the role of audit firms
