# Question

A company has market capitalization of $500 million and book value of equity of $200 million. The price-to-book ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.4
- **B**: 2.5
- **C**: 4.0

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price-to-book ratio = Market capitalization / Book value of equity = $500M / $200M = 2.5. This indicates the market values the company at 2.5 times its accounting equity. Ratios above 1 suggest market sees value beyond book value (intangibles, growth). Financial firms often trade near book value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is book-to-price (inverse)
- Option C results from calculation error
