# Question

Key Audit Matters (KAMs) disclosed in the audit report:

## Options

- **A**: Replace the audit opinion
- **B**: Highlight areas requiring significant auditor judgment or attention
- **C**: Indicate material misstatements were found

## Answer

B

## Explanation

KAMs (Critical Audit Matters under US PCAOB standards) identify areas of significant auditor judgment, high estimation uncertainty, or significant risk. They provide transparency about challenging audit areas without modifying the opinion. KAMs help users understand where financial statements require careful analysis, not that errors exist.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—KAMs supplement, not replace, the opinion
- Option C misinterprets KAMs—they indicate complexity, not misstatement
