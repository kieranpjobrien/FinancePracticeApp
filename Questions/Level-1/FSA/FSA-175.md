# Question

A valuation allowance against a deferred tax asset is required when:

## Options

- **A**: The company has positive taxable income
- **B**: It is more likely than not that some portion of the DTA will not be realized
- **C**: The tax rate is expected to decrease

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A valuation allowance reduces DTAs when realization is uncertain (>50% probability of non-realization). Factors include history of losses, expiring carryforwards, and lack of future taxable income. The allowance is a contra-asset that reduces net DTAs, signaling concerns about future profitability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A suggests high probability of realization, reducing need for allowance
- Option C affects DTA value but doesn't trigger valuation allowance
