# Question

A contingent liability is recognized on the balance sheet when:

## Options

- **A**: The outcome is possible but not probable
- **B**: The obligation is probable and the amount can be reasonably estimated
- **C**: Any potential obligation exists regardless of probability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A contingent liability is recognized (recorded as a provision) when the obligation is probable (>50% likely under IFRS, "likely" under US GAAP) and reasonably estimable. If only possible or not estimable, disclosure is required but no recognition. Remote contingencies require neither recognition nor disclosure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A requires disclosure only, not recognition
- Option C overstates the recognition threshold
