# Question

Non-controlling interest (minority interest) in consolidated statements represents:

## Options

- **A**: The parent's ownership share of subsidiary equity
- **B**: The portion of subsidiary equity not owned by the parent
- **C**: Intercompany receivables between parent and subsidiary

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Non-controlling interest (NCI) represents outside shareholders' claim on subsidiary equity. If a parent owns 80% of a subsidiary, NCI is 20%. NCI is shown in the equity section of the consolidated balance sheet and receives its share of subsidiary net income on the income statement. Full subsidiary assets/liabilities are consolidated despite partial ownership.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the parent's interest, not NCI
- Option C describes intercompany items, which are eliminated
