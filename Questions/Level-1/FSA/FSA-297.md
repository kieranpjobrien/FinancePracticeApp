# Question

In a sale-leaseback transaction where the sale qualifies as a sale:

## Options

- **A**: The seller-lessee removes the asset and recognizes a gain or loss
- **B**: The seller-lessee keeps the asset on its balance sheet
- **C**: No accounting entries are required until lease payments begin

## Answer

A

## Explanation

In qualifying sale-leasebacks, the seller-lessee derecognizes the asset, recognizes sale proceeds, and records a right-of-use asset and lease liability for the leaseback. Any gain/loss is recognized only for the portion of rights transferred (not retained through the leaseback). This differs from failed sales, which are treated as financing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B applies when the transaction fails to qualify as a sale
- Option C ignores the sale accounting required at transaction date
