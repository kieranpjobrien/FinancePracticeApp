# Question

In a sale-leaseback transaction where the leaseback is a finance lease, the seller-lessee most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Removes the asset from the balance sheet permanently
- **B**: Continues to report a right-of-use asset and lease liability
- **C**: Recognizes immediate gain on the entire sale

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under current lease standards, a sale-leaseback with a finance lease results in the seller-lessee recognizing a right-of-use asset and lease liability. Any gain is generally deferred and recognized over the lease term. The transaction is essentially secured financing—the asset remains economically with the seller-lessee.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the leaseback portion
- Option C is generally prohibited—gains are usually deferred
