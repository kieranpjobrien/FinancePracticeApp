# Question

A tax loss carryforward generates:

## Options

- **A**: A deferred tax liability
- **B**: A deferred tax asset
- **C**: An increase in current tax expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tax loss carryforwards allow companies to offset future taxable income with current losses. This creates a DTA representing future tax savings. However, the DTA is only recognized if it's more likely than not that future taxable income will be sufficient to realize the benefit. A valuation allowance may be required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses assets and liabilities
- Option C is incorrect—losses reduce tax expense
