# Question

Operating income is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue minus all expenses including interest and taxes
- **B**: Gross profit minus operating expenses
- **C**: Net income plus interest expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operating income (EBIT) equals gross profit minus operating expenses (SG&A, R&D, depreciation). It measures profitability from core business operations before financing costs (interest) and taxes. This metric isolates operational efficiency from capital structure decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes net income, not operating income
- Option C works backward from net income but ignores taxes
