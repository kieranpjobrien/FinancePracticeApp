# Question

Analysts should be concerned when a company's effective tax rate:

## Options

- **A**: Equals the statutory rate
- **B**: Varies significantly from year to year without clear explanation
- **C**: Is slightly above the statutory rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Significant unexplained volatility in effective tax rates may indicate earnings management (through reserve releases, valuation allowance changes, or one-time items), aggressive tax positions, or changing business mix. Stable rates close to statutory suggest straightforward tax positions. Disclosure analysis helps explain variations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is expected and unremarkable
- Option C may reflect permanent differences—not necessarily concerning
