# Question

Treasury stock is reported on the balance sheet as:

## Options

- **A**: An asset
- **B**: A reduction of shareholders' equity
- **C**: A liability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Treasury stock represents a company's own shares that have been repurchased. It is recorded as a contra-equity account, reducing total shareholders' equity. These shares have no voting rights and receive no dividends. The cost method records treasury stock at acquisition cost.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—shares are not assets to the issuing company
- Option C is incorrect—no obligation exists to repay
