# Question

A company has EBIT of $120 million and interest expense of $30 million. Its interest coverage ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.25
- **B**: 4.0
- **C**: 25%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest coverage ratio = EBIT / Interest expense = $120 million / $30 million = 4.0 times. This means earnings cover interest payments 4 times over. Higher coverage indicates greater ability to service debt. A ratio below 1.0 means the company cannot cover interest from operating earnings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A inverts the ratio
- Option C expresses the ratio as a percentage rather than times coverage
