# Question

Under accrual accounting, revenue is recognized when:

## Options

- **A**: Cash is received from customers
- **B**: Performance obligations are satisfied
- **C**: A sales contract is signed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accrual accounting recognizes revenue when earned (performance obligations satisfied per IFRS 15/ASC 606), regardless of cash receipt timing. This provides a better measure of economic performance than cash-basis accounting. Signing a contract creates an obligation but revenue recognition awaits performance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cash-basis accounting
- Option C is too early—revenue requires actual performance
