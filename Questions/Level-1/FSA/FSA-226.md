# Question

Deferred revenue represents:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue that has been earned but not collected
- **B**: Cash received for goods or services not yet delivered
- **C**: Expected future revenue from signed contracts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred revenue (unearned revenue) is a liability representing advance payments from customers for goods/services to be provided later. When performance obligations are satisfied, it's recognized as revenue. Common examples: subscription payments, prepaid services, gift cards. It's a source of financing from customers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes accounts receivable
- Option C overstates—only cash received creates deferred revenue
