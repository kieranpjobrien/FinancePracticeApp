# Question

Retained earnings increases when:

## Options

- **A**: A company issues new common stock
- **B**: Net income exceeds dividends declared
- **C**: A company repurchases its own shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Retained earnings = Beginning retained earnings + Net income - Dividends declared. It represents cumulative undistributed profits. When net income exceeds dividends, retained earnings grows. Stock issuance affects contributed capital, not retained earnings. Share repurchases reduce treasury stock (a contra-equity account), not retained earnings directly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A increases contributed capital, not retained earnings
- Option C reduces equity through treasury stock, not retained earnings
