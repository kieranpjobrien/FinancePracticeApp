# Question

Capitalized interest is reported in the cash flow statement as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating cash outflow only
- **B**: Investing cash outflow (as part of capitalized costs)
- **C**: Financing cash outflow

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capitalized interest becomes part of the asset cost and flows through investing activities as part of capital expenditures. This differs from expensed interest, which is operating under US GAAP. The difference affects CFO comparisons—companies with more capitalized interest show higher CFO.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to expensed interest under US GAAP
- Option C misclassifies interest payments
