# Question

If a company revises its estimate of an asset's useful life:

## Options

- **A**: Prior period financial statements must be restated
- **B**: The change is applied prospectively to current and future periods
- **C**: The asset is immediately written off

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Changes in accounting estimates (like useful life, salvage value, or depreciation method) are applied prospectively—affecting current and future depreciation but not requiring restatement of prior periods. The remaining book value is depreciated over the revised remaining life. This differs from changes in accounting policies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to changes in policy or error corrections
- Option C misunderstands—the asset continues to be depreciated
