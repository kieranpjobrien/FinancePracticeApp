# Question

Under IFRS, reversal of an impairment loss on equipment is:

## Options

- **A**: Prohibited under all circumstances
- **B**: Permitted if conditions that caused impairment have improved
- **C**: Permitted only for goodwill impairment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS allows reversal of impairment losses on non-goodwill assets if conditions improve. The reversal is limited to the carrying amount that would have existed (net of depreciation) had no impairment occurred. Goodwill impairment is never reversed. US GAAP prohibits all impairment reversals on long-lived assets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the US GAAP treatment
- Option C reverses the rule—goodwill reversals are prohibited, not other assets
