# Question

EBITDA is useful because it:

## Options

- **A**: Represents actual cash flow from operations
- **B**: Approximates operating cash flow before working capital changes
- **C**: Is a standardized measure under GAAP

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization) approximates cash generation from operations by adding back major non-cash charges to operating income. However, it ignores working capital changes, capex, and taxes. It's not a GAAP measure and definitions vary. Useful for comparing companies with different capital structures.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates EBITDA's relationship to cash flow
- Option C is incorrect—EBITDA is a non-GAAP measure
