# Question

A contingent liability is recognized on the balance sheet when:

## Options

- **A**: It is possible that an outflow will occur
- **B**: It is probable that an outflow will occur and the amount can be reasonably estimated
- **C**: Any uncertainty exists about the outcome

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contingent liabilities are recognized (accrued) when: (1) a past event created a present obligation, (2) an outflow of resources is probable, and (3) a reliable estimate can be made. If probable but not estimable, or possible but not probable, disclosure is required but no accrual is made.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A requires only disclosure, not recognition
- Option C would lead to over-accrual of remote contingencies
