# Question

For financial information to be relevant, it must:

## Options

- **A**: Be verifiable by independent parties
- **B**: Be capable of influencing users' decisions
- **C**: Be presented in a consistent format over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Relevance means information can make a difference in users' decisions by having predictive value, confirmatory value, or both. Information is relevant if it helps users predict future outcomes or confirm/correct prior expectations. Relevance and faithful representation are the two fundamental qualitative characteristics of useful financial information.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes verifiability, an enhancing characteristic
- Option C describes consistency, also an enhancing characteristic
