# Question

A high degree of operating leverage indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: The company has high fixed costs relative to variable costs
- **B**: The company has high debt relative to equity
- **C**: The company has highly liquid assets

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Degree of operating leverage (DOL) = % change in operating income / % change in sales. High DOL means small sales changes create large profit swings, typically from high fixed costs (depreciation, rent). Above break-even, high DOL amplifies profits; below break-even, it amplifies losses. Financial leverage involves debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes financial leverage, not operating leverage
- Option C describes liquidity, unrelated to leverage
