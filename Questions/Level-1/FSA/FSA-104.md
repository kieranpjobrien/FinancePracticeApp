# Question

A company retires bonds with a carrying value of $950,000 by paying $980,000 in cash. The result is:

## Options

- **A**: A gain of $30,000
- **B**: A loss of $30,000
- **C**: No gain or loss

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gain/loss on extinguishment = Carrying value - Cash paid = $950,000 - $980,000 = -$30,000 (loss). The company paid more than book value to retire the debt, resulting in a loss. This occurs when market interest rates have fallen since issuance, making the old bonds more valuable.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the calculation
- Option C ignores the difference between carrying value and payment
