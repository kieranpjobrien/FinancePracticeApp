# Question

A qualified audit opinion indicates:

## Options

- **A**: The financial statements are fairly presented with no exceptions
- **B**: Material misstatement or scope limitation exists, but overall statements are fairly presented
- **C**: The auditor cannot form an opinion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A qualified opinion ("except for") indicates material but not pervasive issues—either misstatement (disagreement with management) or scope limitation (couldn't obtain evidence). Unqualified means clean; adverse means pervasive misstatement; disclaimer means severe scope limitation preventing any opinion.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an unqualified (clean) opinion
- Option C describes a disclaimer of opinion
