# Question

Unearned revenue is classified as a liability because it represents:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue that the company has earned but not collected
- **B**: Cash received for goods or services not yet provided
- **C**: Expenses that will be paid in future periods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unearned revenue (deferred revenue) is a liability because the company has received cash but owes goods or services to the customer. It's an obligation to perform in the future. Common examples include subscription payments received in advance, prepaid gift cards, and deposits. Revenue is recognized when earned.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes accounts receivable
- Option C describes accrued expenses
