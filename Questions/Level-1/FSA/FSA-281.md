# Question

Neutrality in financial reporting means:

## Options

- **A**: Avoiding complex accounting treatments
- **B**: Information is free from bias intended to influence users' decisions
- **C**: Presenting only positive news about the company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Neutrality means financial information is unbiased—not slanted, emphasized, or de-emphasized to influence behavior. Neutral information doesn't mean no impact; it means the selection and presentation isn't manipulated to achieve a predetermined outcome. Neutrality is a component of faithful representation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses neutrality with simplicity
- Option C describes biased, not neutral, reporting
