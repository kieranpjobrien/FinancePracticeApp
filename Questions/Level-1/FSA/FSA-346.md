# Question

Additional paid-in capital (APIC) increases when:

## Options

- **A**: The company declares a cash dividend
- **B**: Stock is issued at a price above par value
- **C**: Net income is reported

## Answer

B

## Explanation

APIC (share premium) represents amounts received from shareholders above par value. When shares are issued at $15 per share with $1 par, $1 goes to common stock and $14 to APIC. Stock-based compensation also increases APIC. Net income increases retained earnings; dividends reduce retained earnings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reduces retained earnings
- Option C increases retained earnings, not APIC
