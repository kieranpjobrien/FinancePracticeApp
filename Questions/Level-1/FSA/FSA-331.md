# Question

A large inventory obsolescence write-down would:

## Options

- **A**: Increase current period gross profit
- **B**: Decrease current period COGS and gross profit
- **C**: Increase current period COGS and decrease gross profit

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Inventory write-downs are charged to COGS, increasing COGS and reducing gross profit and net income. The balance sheet shows lower inventory. Future COGS will be lower (already written down), potentially improving future margins. Large write-downs may signal demand problems or poor inventory management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the effect
- Option B confuses the direction—write-downs increase, not decrease, COGS
