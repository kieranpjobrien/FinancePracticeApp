# Question

When a company issues bonds, the proceeds are classified on the cash flow statement as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating activities
- **B**: Investing activities
- **C**: Financing activities

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Bond issuance is a financing activity because it involves obtaining capital from creditors. Similarly, repayment of bond principal is a financing outflow. However, interest payments are classified as operating activities under US GAAP (IFRS allows operating or financing classification).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses with interest payment classification
- Option B confuses with use of proceeds for investments
