# Question

Free cash flow to the firm (FCFF) represents cash available to:

## Options

- **A**: Common shareholders only
- **B**: All providers of capital (debt and equity holders)
- **C**: Reinvestment in the business only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FCFF = Operating cash flow - Capital expenditures (or EBIT(1-t) + Depreciation - CapEx - ΔWorking Capital). It's the cash available to pay all capital providers—bondholders and shareholders—after maintaining and expanding the asset base. FCFE (free cash flow to equity) is the portion available to shareholders after debt service.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes FCFE, not FCFF
- Option C misunderstands—FCF is what's available after necessary reinvestment
