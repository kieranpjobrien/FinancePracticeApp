# Question

When receivables are sold (factored) without recourse, the cash received is classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating cash inflow
- **B**: Financing cash inflow
- **C**: Investing cash inflow

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Non-recourse factoring (true sale) where receivables are derecognized generates operating cash from accelerated collection. With recourse (seller retains risk), it may be financing since it's effectively a secured borrowing. Classification depends on whether the transaction qualifies as a sale or financing under accounting standards.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B applies to recourse factoring (borrowing character)
- Option C is not used for receivables transactions
