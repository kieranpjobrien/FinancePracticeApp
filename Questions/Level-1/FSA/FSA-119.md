# Question

If a company increases its discount rate assumption for pension obligations, the most likely immediate effect is:

## Options

- **A**: Higher projected benefit obligation and higher pension expense
- **B**: Lower projected benefit obligation and lower pension expense
- **C**: No effect on PBO but higher pension expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A higher discount rate reduces the present value of future pension obligations (PBO). This typically reduces the interest cost component of pension expense (though the rate is applied to a lower liability). Companies may opportunistically adjust discount rates to manage reported obligations and expenses.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the discount rate effect
- Option C ignores the PV relationship
