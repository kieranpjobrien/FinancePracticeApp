# Question

Segment profit margin analysis is valuable because:

## Options

- **A**: Segment profits always equal consolidated net income
- **B**: It reveals which business units drive or drag overall profitability
- **C**: Segment data uses more conservative accounting than consolidated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Segment analysis decomposes performance by business line or geography, revealing which segments generate profits and which struggle. This helps assess management effectiveness, predict future performance, and understand business mix changes. Segment totals may not equal consolidated amounts due to unallocated items and intersegment eliminations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores unallocated corporate costs and eliminations
- Option C is unfounded—segment accounting follows the same GAAP
