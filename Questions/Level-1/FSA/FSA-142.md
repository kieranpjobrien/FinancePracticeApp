# Question

Subsequent events that provide evidence about conditions existing at the balance sheet date:

## Options

- **A**: Are disclosed but do not affect recorded amounts
- **B**: Require adjustment to the financial statements
- **C**: Are not required to be reported

## Answer

B

## Explanation

"Adjusting" subsequent events provide additional evidence about conditions existing at the balance sheet date (like settling a lawsuit that was accrued, or collecting on a disputed receivable). These require adjustment to financial statement amounts. "Non-adjusting" events arising after the date are disclosed but don't change recorded amounts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes non-adjusting subsequent events
- Option C is incorrect—both types require action
