# Question

IFRS is generally considered to be more:

## Options

- **A**: Rules-based than US GAAP
- **B**: Principles-based than US GAAP
- **C**: Detailed in specific guidance than US GAAP

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS is characterized as principles-based, providing broad principles for preparers to apply using professional judgment. US GAAP is more rules-based, with detailed, specific guidance for numerous situations. Principles-based standards offer flexibility but may reduce comparability; rules-based standards are more prescriptive but may invite structuring to technically comply while violating the spirit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the characterization
- Option C is incorrect—US GAAP typically has more detailed guidance
