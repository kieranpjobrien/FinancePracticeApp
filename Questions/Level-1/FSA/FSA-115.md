# Question

The tax base of an asset is:

## Options

- **A**: Its fair market value for tax purposes
- **B**: The amount deductible for tax purposes in future periods
- **C**: The purchase price originally paid for the asset

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An asset's tax base is the amount that will be deductible for tax purposes as the asset is used or sold. The difference between carrying value and tax base determines temporary differences and deferred taxes. For depreciable assets, tax base equals cost minus accumulated tax depreciation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses tax base with fair value
- Option C is the original cost, but tax base reflects remaining deductions
