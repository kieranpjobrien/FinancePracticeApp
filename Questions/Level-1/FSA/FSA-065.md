# Question

Under current lease accounting standards (IFRS 16/ASC 842), a lessee generally recognizes:

## Options

- **A**: No assets or liabilities for operating leases
- **B**: A right-of-use asset and lease liability for most leases
- **C**: Only rental expense on a straight-line basis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Current standards require lessees to recognize most leases on the balance sheet. A right-of-use (ROU) asset and corresponding lease liability are recorded at the present value of lease payments. This eliminates the previous distinction where operating leases were off-balance-sheet. Short-term and low-value leases may be exempted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes pre-2019 operating lease treatment
- Option C describes the old operating lease income statement treatment
