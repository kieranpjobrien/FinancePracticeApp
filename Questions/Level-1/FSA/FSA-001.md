# Question

A company uses LIFO for inventory valuation. During a period of rising prices, if the company switched to FIFO, which of the following would most likely increase?

## Options

- **A**: Cost of goods sold and net income
- **B**: Ending inventory and net income
- **C**: Cost of goods sold and ending inventory

## Answer

B

## Explanation

During rising prices:
- **LIFO**: Recent (higher-cost) inventory is expensed first → Higher COGS, Lower ending inventory
- **FIFO**: Older (lower-cost) inventory is expensed first → Lower COGS, Higher ending inventory

Switching from LIFO to FIFO during rising prices:
- Ending inventory increases (valued at recent higher costs)
- COGS decreases (older lower costs are expensed)
- Net income increases (due to lower COGS)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect because COGS would decrease, not increase
- Option C is incorrect because COGS would decrease, not increase
