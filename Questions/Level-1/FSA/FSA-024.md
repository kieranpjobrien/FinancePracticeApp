# Question

Under the indirect method, operating cash flow starts with net income and:

## Options

- **A**: Subtracts depreciation expense
- **B**: Adds back non-cash expenses like depreciation
- **C**: Adds increases in accounts receivable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The indirect method reconciles net income to operating cash flow. Depreciation is a non-cash expense that reduced net income, so it's added back. Other adjustments include adding back losses (subtracting gains) on asset sales, and adjusting for working capital changes. Increases in receivables are subtracted (cash not yet collected).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the adjustment—depreciation is added, not subtracted
- Option C reverses the adjustment—receivable increases reduce cash flow
