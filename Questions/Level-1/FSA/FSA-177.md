# Question

Under the effective interest method, interest expense on a bond issued at a discount:

## Options

- **A**: Remains constant over the bond's life
- **B**: Decreases over the bond's life
- **C**: Increases over the bond's life

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Interest expense = Carrying amount × Market rate at issuance. For discount bonds, carrying amount increases toward par as the discount amortizes. Since interest expense is calculated on a growing carrying amount, expense increases each period. Premium bonds show decreasing expense as carrying value declines.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the straight-line method, not effective interest
- Option B describes premium bonds under effective interest
