# Question

A LIFO liquidation occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Inventory purchases exceed sales quantities
- **B**: Sales quantities exceed purchases, dipping into old inventory layers
- **C**: Inventory prices are falling

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LIFO liquidation happens when a company sells more units than it purchases, forcing it to dip into older, lower-cost inventory layers. This temporarily inflates gross profit (lower COGS from old costs) and increases taxes. It may signal operational issues (supply chain problems, declining demand) or deliberate profit management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would build inventory, not liquidate
- Option C describes price environment, not liquidation
