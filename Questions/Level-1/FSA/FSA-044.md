# Question

Under IFRS, inventory is measured at the lower of:

## Options

- **A**: Cost or market value
- **B**: Cost or net realizable value
- **C**: Cost or replacement cost

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS requires inventory to be measured at the lower of cost or net realizable value (NRV = expected selling price - costs to complete and sell). If NRV falls below cost, inventory is written down and a loss recognized. US GAAP uses lower of cost or market (with market being replacement cost bounded by NRV ceiling and NRV-profit floor).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses US GAAP terminology
- Option C is one component of the US GAAP market definition
