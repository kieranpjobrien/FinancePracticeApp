# Question

Acquired patents are generally:

## Options

- **A**: Expensed immediately as research costs
- **B**: Capitalized and amortized over their useful life
- **C**: Recorded at fair value each reporting period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Acquired intangible assets (patents, trademarks, customer lists) are capitalized at cost and amortized over their estimated useful life (or legal life if shorter). Internally developed patents have some costs capitalized, some expensed. Intangibles are tested for impairment but not routinely revalued to fair value (except under IFRS revaluation model).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to research costs, not acquired patents
- Option C describes trading securities, not patents
