# Question

A company reports revenue of $800,000, COGS of $480,000, and operating expenses of $200,000. Gross profit is:

## Options

- **A**: $120,000
- **B**: $320,000
- **C**: $520,000

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gross profit = Revenue - COGS = $800,000 - $480,000 = $320,000. Gross profit measures profitability from core production activities before selling, general, and administrative expenses. Operating income would be $320,000 - $200,000 = $120,000, subtracting operating expenses from gross profit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is operating income (gross profit minus operating expenses)
- Option C adds instead of subtracts COGS
