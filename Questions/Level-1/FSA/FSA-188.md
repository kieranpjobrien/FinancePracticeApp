# Question

Under US GAAP, dividends paid to shareholders are classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating activity
- **B**: Investing activity
- **C**: Financing activity

## Answer

C

## Explanation

US GAAP classifies dividends paid as a financing activity—it's a distribution to capital providers (shareholders). IFRS allows choice: either operating or financing. Interest paid is operating under US GAAP, financing or operating under IFRS. These classification differences affect CFO comparability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the IFRS alternative, not required US GAAP treatment
- Option B confuses dividends with investment transactions
