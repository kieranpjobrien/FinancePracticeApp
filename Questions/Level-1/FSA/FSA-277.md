# Question

A deferred compensation arrangement creates:

## Options

- **A**: An asset on the employer's balance sheet
- **B**: A liability as the employee earns the compensation
- **C**: Equity when employees are granted stock options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred compensation (earned now, paid later) creates a liability as employees perform services. The expense is recognized as services are provided (matching principle), with corresponding liability growth. When paid, the liability is reduced. This differs from share-based compensation, which may affect equity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship—employer has obligation, not asset
- Option C describes stock options, not cash deferred compensation
