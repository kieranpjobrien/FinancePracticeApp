# Question

Stock options granted to employees affect the financial statements by:

## Options

- **A**: Increasing assets when granted
- **B**: Creating expense over the vesting period
- **C**: Reducing liabilities when exercised

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock-based compensation is expensed over the vesting (service) period based on grant-date fair value. The expense reduces net income; the credit increases equity (APIC). When options are exercised, cash increases, common stock increases, and APIC is adjusted. There's no asset or liability recognition at grant.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—no asset is created by granting options
- Option C—options don't create liabilities
