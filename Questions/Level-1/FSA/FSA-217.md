# Question

When a company violates a debt covenant, the related debt is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Written off immediately
- **B**: Reclassified as a current liability unless a waiver is obtained
- **C**: Reported at fair value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covenant violation typically triggers acceleration provisions, making debt immediately due. The debt must be reclassified to current liabilities since it's payable on demand. A waiver from lenders before the balance sheet date can allow continued classification as non-current. Disclosure of the violation is required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses covenant violation with debt forgiveness
- Option C is unrelated to covenant violations
