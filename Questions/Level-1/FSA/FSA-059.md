# Question

Permanent differences between taxable and accounting income:

## Options

- **A**: Create deferred tax assets or liabilities
- **B**: Never reverse and affect the effective tax rate
- **C**: Will reverse in future periods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Permanent differences never reverse—they represent items treated differently for tax and book purposes forever. Examples include tax-exempt municipal bond interest, non-deductible fines, and certain meals/entertainment. They affect the effective tax rate (tax expense/pretax income) but don't create deferred taxes. Temporary differences create DTAs/DTLs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes temporary differences
- Option C describes temporary differences
