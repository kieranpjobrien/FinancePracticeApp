# Question

A company has total debt of $600,000, total equity of $400,000, and total assets of $1,000,000. The debt-to-equity ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.6
- **B**: 1.5
- **C**: 2.5

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt-to-equity ratio = Total debt / Total equity = $600,000 / $400,000 = 1.5. This solvency ratio measures financial leverage—higher ratios indicate greater reliance on debt financing. A ratio of 1.5 means $1.50 of debt for every $1 of equity. The debt-to-assets ratio is 0.6 ($600,000 / $1,000,000).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the debt-to-assets ratio
- Option C reverses equity and debt in the calculation
