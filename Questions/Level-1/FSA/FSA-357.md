# Question

A higher discount rate used to calculate pension obligations results in:

## Options

- **A**: Higher PBO and higher pension expense
- **B**: Lower PBO and lower pension expense
- **C**: No effect on PBO

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Higher discount rate reduces the present value of future benefit payments, lowering PBO. Lower PBO means lower interest cost and potentially lower service cost (since benefits are valued at higher discount rate). Companies may have incentive to select higher discount rates to reduce reported obligations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores that PBO is a present value calculation
