# Question

Transfer pricing concerns arise when:

## Options

- **A**: A company sells products to unrelated third parties
- **B**: Related entities transact at non-arm's length prices to shift profits
- **C**: Inventory is transferred between warehouses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Transfer pricing involves pricing intercompany transactions (goods, services, intellectual property) between related entities. Non-arm's length pricing can shift profits to lower-tax jurisdictions. Tax authorities scrutinize these transactions, and adjustments can result in significant tax liabilities. Companies must document their pricing methodologies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A involves arm's length transactions, no transfer pricing issue
- Option C is an internal movement, not a tax-significant transaction
