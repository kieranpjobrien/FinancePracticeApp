# Question

The specific identification inventory method is most appropriate for:

## Options

- **A**: Commodity products with high volume and low unit cost
- **B**: Unique, high-value items like automobiles or jewelry
- **C**: Perishable goods requiring rapid turnover

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Specific identification tracks the actual cost of each individual item sold. It's practical only for distinguishable, high-value, low-volume goods like cars, art, and custom equipment. For homogeneous, high-volume items, cost flow assumptions (FIFO, weighted average) are more practical and cost-effective.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would use FIFO or weighted average due to volume
- Option C would typically use FIFO to ensure oldest items sell first
