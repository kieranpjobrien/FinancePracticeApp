# Question

Under the equity method for investments in associates, the investor's share of the investee's net income:

## Options

- **A**: Is recognized as dividend income when received
- **B**: Increases the carrying value of the investment
- **C**: Is reported in other comprehensive income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The equity method (typically 20-50% ownership with significant influence) records the investor's share of investee net income as investment income, increasing the investment asset. Dividends received reduce the investment carrying value (return of capital, not income). This differs from passive investments where dividends are income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the cost method or passive investment treatment
- Option C is incorrect—equity income goes to net income
