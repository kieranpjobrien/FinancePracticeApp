# Question

Structural subordination occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Bond terms explicitly state lower priority than other debt
- **B**: Parent company bondholders are behind subsidiary creditors in claim on subsidiary assets
- **C**: Bonds mature after other obligations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Structural subordination: holding company creditors have claim only on holding company assets (including subsidiary equity). Subsidiary creditors paid first from subsidiary assets. Even if holding company debt is "senior," structurally subordinate to subsidiary debt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes contractual subordination
- Option C describes maturity, not subordination
