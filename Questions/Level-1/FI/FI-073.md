# Question

The implied forward rate represents:

## Options

- **A**: The guaranteed future interest rate
- **B**: The rate that makes investing for a longer period equivalent to rolling over shorter investments
- **C**: The average of current spot rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Implied forward rate: breakeven rate that equates longer-term investment to sequential shorter investments. Derived from spot rate curve. Not a forecast—reflects current pricing. (1+s₂)² = (1+s₁)(1+f₁,₁) solves for 1-year rate, 1 year forward.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that forward rates are implied, not guaranteed
- Option C describes simple average, not forward rate derivation
