# Question

A surety bond as credit enhancement is considered:

## Options

- **A**: Internal credit enhancement
- **B**: External credit enhancement
- **C**: Structural credit enhancement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

External credit enhancement: third-party guarantee. Surety bonds, letters of credit, corporate guarantees. Depends on guarantor's credit—if guarantor downgraded, ABS rating may fall. Internal enhancements (subordination, overcollateralization) don't have this risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—surety bonds are external, provided by third party
- Option C—structural enhancement is type of internal enhancement
