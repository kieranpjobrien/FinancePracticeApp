# Question

A change of control put provision allows bondholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Acquire equity shares in the company
- **B**: Sell bonds back to the issuer if the company is acquired
- **C**: Block any proposed merger or acquisition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Change of control put: if company acquired, bondholders can put bonds at par (or premium). Protects against credit deterioration from LBO/acquisition. Limits acquirer's ability to lever up target at bondholders' expense.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes convertible bond feature
- Option C—bondholders can't block deals, only put bonds
