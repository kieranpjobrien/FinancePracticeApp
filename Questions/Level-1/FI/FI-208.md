# Question

A distressed exchange offer allows a company to:

## Options

- **A**: Issue new stock to existing shareholders
- **B**: Offer bondholders new securities (often with lower principal) to avoid bankruptcy
- **C**: Force all creditors to accept reduced payments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Distressed exchange: company offers new bonds (lower par, longer maturity, lower coupon) or equity in exchange for existing debt. Voluntary—bondholders can refuse. Alternative to bankruptcy. May still be rated as default by agencies if impairs value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rights offering to equity
- Option C describes cram-down in bankruptcy, not voluntary exchange
