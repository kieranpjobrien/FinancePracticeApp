# Question

A TIPS bond has a real coupon rate of 2% and par value of $1,000. If the inflation-adjusted principal after one year is $1,030, the coupon payment is:

## Options

- **A**: $20.00
- **B**: $20.60
- **C**: $30.00

## Answer

B

## Explanation

TIPS coupon = Real coupon rate × Inflation-adjusted principal = 2% × $1,030 = $20.60. The coupon rate is fixed, but applied to the inflation-adjusted principal, so coupon payments grow with inflation. This protects real income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—applies coupon to original par, ignoring adjustment
- Option C—confuses principal increase with coupon
