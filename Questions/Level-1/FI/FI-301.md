# Question

Recovery rates in default are typically highest for:

## Options

- **A**: Subordinated unsecured bonds
- **B**: Senior secured bonds
- **C**: Equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Recovery priority: Senior secured > Senior unsecured > Subordinated > Equity. Secured bonds have collateral claims, recovering 50-70%+ on average. Subordinated may recover 30-40%. Equity often receives nothing. Seniority crucial for credit analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—subordinated has lower priority
- Option C—equity is last in line, often wiped out
