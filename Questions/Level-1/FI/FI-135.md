# Question

The par curve shows:

## Options

- **A**: Yields on zero-coupon bonds across maturities
- **B**: Coupon rates at which bonds would trade at par across maturities
- **C**: Forward rates for future periods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Par curve: yields (coupon rates) at which bonds price at par for each maturity. Derived from spot curve. Used to price new issues. If 5-year par rate is 4%, a 5-year 4% coupon bond prices at 100.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes spot/zero curve
- Option C describes forward curve
