# Question

The relationship between bond prices and interest rates is:

## Options

- **A**: Direct—prices rise when rates rise
- **B**: Inverse—prices fall when rates rise
- **C**: Unrelated—prices don't respond to rate changes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inverse relationship: when market rates rise, existing bond prices fall (and vice versa). Fixed coupons become less attractive relative to new higher-rate bonds. Present value of cash flows decreases with higher discount rate. Fundamental fixed-income principle.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores basic bond math
