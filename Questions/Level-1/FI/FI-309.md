# Question

A bear flattening of the yield curve occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Short-term rates fall more than long-term rates
- **B**: Short-term rates rise more than long-term rates, flattening the curve
- **C**: All rates decline equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bear flattener: rates rise (bear market), short end rises more than long end. Often during monetary tightening—Fed raises short rates. Long rates constrained by growth/inflation expectations. Curve flattens as spread between short and long rates narrows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes bull flattener (rates falling)
- Option C—parallel shift, not flattening
