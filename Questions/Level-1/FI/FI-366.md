# Question

Price risk for a bondholder refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The risk that the issuer will not pay coupons
- **B**: The risk of capital loss if interest rates rise before selling
- **C**: The risk of inflation eroding purchasing power

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price risk (market risk): rising rates cause bond prices to fall. Affects investors who may sell before maturity. Longer duration = more price risk. Holding to maturity eliminates price risk but exposes to reinvestment risk. Key risk for bond portfolios.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes credit/default risk
- Option C—describes inflation risk
