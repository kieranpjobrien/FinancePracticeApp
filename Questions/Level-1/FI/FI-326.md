# Question

Monte Carlo simulation is preferred over binomial trees for valuing:

## Options

- **A**: European-style options on bonds
- **B**: Path-dependent securities like mortgage-backed securities
- **C**: Simple fixed-coupon bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monte Carlo: generates thousands of interest rate paths, averages present values. Essential for path-dependent securities (MBS) where cash flows depend on rate history (prepayment patterns). Binomial trees assume path independence. MC captures burnout and other path effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—binomial trees work fine for European options
- Option C—simple bonds don't need simulation
