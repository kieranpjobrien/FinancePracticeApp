# Question

The segmented markets theory suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Investors freely move between maturities based on relative value
- **B**: Supply and demand within each maturity segment determines rates independently
- **C**: Forward rates predict future spot rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Segmented markets: different investor types prefer specific maturities (pension funds want long; banks want short). Each segment's supply/demand sets rates independently. Explains why curve shape may not reflect rate expectations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes arbitrage-free/preferred habitat view
- Option C describes pure expectations theory
