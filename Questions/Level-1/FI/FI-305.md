# Question

Contingent immunization combines:

## Options

- **A**: Active management with a floor return protected by immunization
- **B**: Duration matching across multiple liabilities
- **C**: Zero-coupon bonds exclusively

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Contingent immunization: active management allowed while surplus exists. If portfolio value falls to safety net (present value of liability), switch to pure immunization to lock in minimum return. Captures upside potential while protecting floor. Requires monitoring.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—describes cash flow matching or multi-liability immunization
- Option C—not restricted to zeros
