# Question

A bond with a 6% annual coupon has a flat (clean) price of 98.50. If 90 days have elapsed since the last coupon (180-day period), the full (dirty) price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 98.50
- **B**: 100.00
- **C**: 100.00

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Accrued interest = (90/180) × 6% = 0.5 × 6 = 3.00 per 100 face. Full price = Flat price + Accrued interest = 98.50 + 1.50 = 100.00. Wait—recalculate: 6% coupon = 6 per year, so accrued = (90/180) × 6 = 3. But that's for annual coupon paid once. If semiannual: coupon = 3 per period, accrued = (90/180) × 3 = 1.50. Full price = 98.50 + 1.50 = 100.00.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—ignores accrued interest
- Option B and C appear same—note full price includes accrued
