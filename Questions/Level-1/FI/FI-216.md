# Question

The price-yield relationship for a bond is:

## Options

- **A**: Linear and positively sloped
- **B**: Convex and negatively sloped
- **C**: Concave and negatively sloped

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price-yield curve is convex (bowed toward origin) and downward sloping. Higher yield = lower price. Convexity means price rises more for rate decrease than it falls for equal rate increase. Positive convexity benefits bondholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—relationship is inverse, not positive
- Option C—curve is convex, not concave
