# Question

A company's debt capacity is primarily determined by:

## Options

- **A**: Its desired capital structure only
- **B**: Its ability to generate stable cash flows to service debt obligations
- **C**: Current interest rate levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt capacity: maximum debt a company can sustain given cash flows, asset base, and business risk. Stable, predictable cash flows support more debt. Cyclical businesses, startups have lower capacity. Credit ratios indicate capacity utilization.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores cash flow constraints
- Option C affects cost, not capacity
