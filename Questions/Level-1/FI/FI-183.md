# Question

A "busted convertible" is one where:

## Options

- **A**: The stock price has risen far above the conversion price
- **B**: The stock price has fallen far below conversion price, so it trades like a straight bond
- **C**: The issuer has called the bond for redemption

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Busted convertible: stock price well below conversion price, conversion option has little value. Bond trades near straight bond value, driven by credit risk and rates. Equity-like exposure gone. May recover if stock rallies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes deep in-the-money convertible
- Option C describes call exercise, different situation
