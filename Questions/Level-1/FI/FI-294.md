# Question

Corporate bonds in the US typically settle:

## Options

- **A**: Same day as trade (T+0)
- **B**: Two business days after trade (T+2)
- **C**: One month after trade

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corporate bonds: T+2 settlement (trade date plus two business days). Government bonds often T+1. Settlement date determines when cash and securities exchange. Accrued interest calculated to settlement date. Different markets may have different conventions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—same-day settlement rare for bonds
- Option C—one month much too long
