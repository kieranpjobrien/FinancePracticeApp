# Question

"Distressed debt" typically refers to bonds trading at:

## Options

- **A**: Par value
- **B**: Yields significantly above comparable bonds, often below 70 cents on the dollar
- **C**: A premium to par

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Distressed debt: trading at high yields (often 1000+ bps over Treasuries) or deep discount (below 70-80% of par). Implies market expects default or restructuring. Distressed investors specialize in bankruptcy/workout situations. High risk, potentially high return.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes healthy credits
- Option C describes low-yield premium bonds
