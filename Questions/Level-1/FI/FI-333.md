# Question

A Eurobond is:

## Options

- **A**: A bond denominated in euros only
- **B**: A bond issued outside the jurisdiction of any single country, typically by an international syndicate
- **C**: A bond guaranteed by the European Central Bank

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Eurobond: issued in international market, outside any single country's regulations. Can be denominated in any currency. "Euro" prefix refers to external/international, not the euro currency. Typically bearer bonds, less regulation, syndicated globally.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—Eurobonds can be in any currency (USD, GBP, JPY, etc.)
- Option C—no government/central bank guarantee
