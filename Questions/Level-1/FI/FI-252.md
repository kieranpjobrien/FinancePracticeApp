# Question

A cash reserve fund in a securitization provides:

## Options

- **A**: External credit enhancement from a third party
- **B**: Internal credit enhancement through set-aside cash to cover shortfalls
- **C**: Insurance against interest rate movements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash reserve fund: cash set aside at closing to cover potential shortfalls (delinquencies, losses). Internal enhancement—no external guarantor. Often funded from excess spread. Provides liquidity and credit support. Similar to overcollateralization in function.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes external enhancement
- Option C describes interest rate hedge
