# Question

A wider bid-ask spread on a bond typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Higher credit quality
- **B**: Lower liquidity
- **C**: Higher expected returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bid-ask spread: dealer's compensation for making market. Wider spread = less liquid (harder to trade, more inventory risk for dealer). Treasury spreads tight (liquid); corporate high-yield spreads wider. Spread is transaction cost for investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—credit quality affects price level, not necessarily spread
- Option C—wider spreads indicate cost, not expected return
