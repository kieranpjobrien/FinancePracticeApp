# Question

A secured bond differs from an unsecured bond in that:

## Options

- **A**: Secured bonds have higher yields
- **B**: Secured bonds have specific assets pledged as collateral
- **C**: Secured bonds cannot default

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Secured bonds: specific collateral pledged (real estate, equipment, receivables). If default, bondholders have claim on collateral. Higher recovery rates. Unsecured (debentures) have general claim only. Secured bonds typically lower yield due to lower risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—secured bonds have LOWER yields due to collateral protection
- Option C—secured bonds can default, just have better recovery
