# Question

Treasury bills are typically quoted on a:

## Options

- **A**: Price basis as percentage of par
- **B**: Bank discount yield basis
- **C**: Yield to maturity basis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T-bills quoted on bank discount yield basis: annualized discount as percentage of face value, 360-day year. Discount yield = (Face - Price)/Face × 360/days. Not true yield—understates actual return. Bond equivalent yield converts to comparable basis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes how bonds trade, not T-bill convention
- Option C describes yield measure but not quoting convention
