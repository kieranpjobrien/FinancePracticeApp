# Question

Wider bid-ask spreads in bond markets typically indicate:

## Options

- **A**: Higher liquidity and easier trading
- **B**: Lower liquidity and higher transaction costs
- **C**: Stronger credit quality

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bid-ask spread: dealer compensation for market-making. Wider spreads = less liquid, harder to trade, higher costs. Factors: issue size, credit quality, market conditions, complexity. Investment-grade typically tighter than high-yield. Spreads widen during market stress.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses relationship—wide spreads mean low liquidity
- Option C—credit quality affects spreads but isn't directly indicated
