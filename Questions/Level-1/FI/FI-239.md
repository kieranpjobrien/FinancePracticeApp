# Question

Going "long credit" means:

## Options

- **A**: Expecting credit spreads to widen
- **B**: Expecting credit spreads to tighten (narrow)
- **C**: Having no exposure to credit risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Long credit = benefit from spread tightening. Buy corporate bonds expecting spreads to narrow (prices rise). Short credit = benefit from spread widening. Credit longs outperform when economy strengthens, defaults decrease, risk appetite increases.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes short credit position
- Option C describes no credit exposure
