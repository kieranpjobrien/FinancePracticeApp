# Question

Corporate bonds typically offer:

## Options

- **A**: Lower yields than Treasury securities of similar maturity
- **B**: Higher yields than Treasury securities to compensate for credit risk
- **C**: No credit risk premium

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corporate bond yields: Treasury yield + credit spread. Spread compensates for: default risk, liquidity risk, tax differences. Higher credit risk = wider spread. Investment-grade spreads tighter than high-yield. Spreads widen during economic stress.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores fundamental credit risk compensation
