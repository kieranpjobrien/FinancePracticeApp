# Question

A bull steepening of the yield curve occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Short-term rates fall more than long-term rates
- **B**: Long-term rates rise more than short-term rates
- **C**: All rates rise equally

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Bull steepener: rates fall (bull market), short end falls more. Often at start of easing cycle—Fed cuts short rates. Long rates decline less due to growth expectations. Curve steepens as spread between short and long rates widens. Favorable for long-duration positions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—describes bear steepener (rates rising)
- Option C—parallel shift, not steepening
