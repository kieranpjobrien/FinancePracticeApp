# Question

A normal (upward-sloping) yield curve indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Short-term rates higher than long-term rates
- **B**: Long-term rates higher than short-term rates
- **C**: All rates are equal across maturities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Normal yield curve: slopes upward—longer maturities have higher yields. Reflects: term premium for duration risk, expectations of future rate increases, and/or inflation expectations. Most common shape historically. Inverted and flat curves occur but less frequently.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes inverted yield curve
- Option C describes flat yield curve
