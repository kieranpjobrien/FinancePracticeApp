# Question

During an economic recession, high-yield bond default rates typically:

## Options

- **A**: Decrease as companies become more conservative
- **B**: Increase as weaker companies struggle to service debt
- **C**: Remain unchanged due to diversification

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Default rates cyclical: rise in recessions (revenue falls, leverage rises, refinancing harder), fall in expansions. Historical high-yield defaults range 1-2% in good years to 10%+ in recessions. Credit spreads widen before defaults peak.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—recession stress increases defaults
- Option C—diversification doesn't prevent cycle impact
