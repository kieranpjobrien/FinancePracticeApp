# Question

Callable bonds exhibit negative convexity when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates are very high
- **B**: Interest rates are low and the call option is likely to be exercised
- **C**: The bond is trading at a discount

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative convexity: when rates fall, callable bond price rises less than non-callable (call limits upside). Call option becomes valuable as rates drop below coupon. Price-yield curve bends toward the call price ceiling. High rates = positive convexity (call unlikely).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—high rates mean call unlikely, positive convexity
- Option C—discount means rates above coupon, call unlikely
