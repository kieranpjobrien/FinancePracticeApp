# Question

Compared to a bullet portfolio of the same duration, a barbell portfolio has:

## Options

- **A**: Lower convexity and performs worse in volatile rate environments
- **B**: Higher convexity and performs better when rates move significantly
- **C**: Identical performance regardless of yield curve changes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Barbell higher convexity than bullet of same duration. Convexity helps in large rate moves—price rises more when rates fall, falls less when rates rise. Trade-off: bullet outperforms for small parallel shifts if yield curve steepens.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the convexity relationship
- Option C ignores structural differences
