# Question

In Moody's rating scale, "Aaa" represents:

## Options

- **A**: High yield/speculative grade
- **B**: Highest quality with minimal credit risk
- **C**: Below investment grade

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Moody's Aaa: highest rating, minimal credit risk. Scale descends: Aaa, Aa, A, Baa (investment grade) then Ba, B, Caa, Ca, C (speculative/high yield). Numeric modifiers (1,2,3) refine within categories. S&P equivalent: AAA.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Ba and below
- Option C describes speculative grades (Ba and below)
