# Question

A credit spread represents:

## Options

- **A**: The difference between a bond's yield and a risk-free benchmark yield
- **B**: The difference between bid and ask prices
- **C**: The change in yield over time

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Credit spread = Corporate bond yield - Risk-free benchmark yield (e.g., Treasury). Compensates for credit risk, liquidity risk, and other factors. Widens in stress, narrows in calm markets. Key measure of credit risk premium.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—describes bid-ask spread, a liquidity measure
- Option C—describes yield change, not spread
