# Question

A "long duration" position profits when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates rise
- **B**: Interest rates fall
- **C**: Credit spreads widen

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Long duration = benefit from falling rates. Higher duration means greater price increase for rate decline. Go long duration if expecting rates to fall. Short duration if expecting rates to rise. Duration positioning is key active management decision.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—rising rates hurt long duration positions
- Option C describes spread position, not duration
