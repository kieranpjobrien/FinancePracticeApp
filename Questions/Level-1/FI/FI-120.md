# Question

Prepayment risk for mortgage-backed securities increases when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates rise significantly
- **B**: Interest rates fall significantly
- **C**: Interest rates remain stable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Prepayment risk: borrowers refinance when rates fall, returning principal early. Investors reinvest at lower rates. MBS holders short a call option—borrowers have refinancing option. Prepayment speeds measured by CPR (conditional prepayment rate) or PSA.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—rising rates reduce prepayments (extension risk instead)
- Option C—stable rates mean predictable prepayments
