# Question

All else equal, a longer-maturity bond has:

## Options

- **A**: Lower interest rate sensitivity
- **B**: Higher interest rate sensitivity
- **C**: The same interest rate sensitivity as a shorter-maturity bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Longer maturity = higher duration = greater interest rate sensitivity. More distant cash flows are more affected by rate changes. A 30-year bond's price moves more than a 5-year bond's for the same yield change. Fundamental duration relationship.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—reverses the relationship
- Option C—maturity directly affects sensitivity
