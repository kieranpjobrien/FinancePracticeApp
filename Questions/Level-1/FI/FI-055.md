# Question

Extension risk in MBS refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Risk that investors will extend holdings beyond original plan
- **B**: Risk that prepayments slow when rates rise, extending effective maturity
- **C**: Risk of bond maturity being extended by the issuer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Extension risk: when rates rise, prepayments slow (less refinancing). MBS duration extends, holding longer than expected in rising rate environment. Opposite of contraction risk (fast prepayments when rates fall). Both create reinvestment challenges.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands "extension" in MBS context
- Option C describes extendible bonds, not MBS extension risk
