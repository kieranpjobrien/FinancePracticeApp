# Question

An event of default in a bond indenture typically allows bondholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the coupon rate
- **B**: Accelerate repayment, demanding immediate payment of principal
- **C**: Convert bonds to equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Events of default: failure to pay interest/principal, covenant breach, bankruptcy filing. Acceleration: upon default, bondholders can declare entire principal immediately due. Provides leverage in negotiations. Trustee typically acts on behalf of bondholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—default doesn't typically change coupon (step-ups excepted)
- Option C describes convertible bond feature, not default remedy
