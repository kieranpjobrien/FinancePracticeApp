# Question

High-yield bond spreads typically:

## Options

- **A**: Remain constant through economic cycles
- **B**: Widen during recessions and narrow during expansions
- **C**: Move inversely with Treasury yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High-yield spreads are procyclical to credit conditions: widen in recessions (higher default risk, reduced risk appetite) and narrow in expansions (improving credit metrics, risk-on sentiment). Spread volatility exceeds investment grade. Key indicator of credit market health.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—spreads are highly cyclical
- Option C—spread movement related to credit conditions, not just rate levels
