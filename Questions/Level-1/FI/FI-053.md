# Question

Mortgage-backed securities (MBS) are created by:

## Options

- **A**: Issuing unsecured corporate bonds
- **B**: Pooling mortgage loans and selling securities backed by the cash flows
- **C**: Buying Treasury securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

MBS: securities backed by pools of residential or commercial mortgages. Cash flows (principal and interest) from underlying mortgages pass through to investors. Issuers: government agencies (Ginnie Mae), GSEs (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac), private issuers. Prepayment risk is key consideration.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes unsecured corporate bonds
- Option C describes Treasury purchases, not securitization
