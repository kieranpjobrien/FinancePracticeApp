# Question

A bond portfolio has a market value of $10 million and a modified duration of 5.5. The price value of a basis point (PVBP) is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $550
- **B**: $5,500
- **C**: $55,000

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PVBP = Portfolio value × Modified duration × 0.0001 = $10,000,000 × 5.5 × 0.0001 = $5,500. One basis point change causes approximately $5,500 change in portfolio value. Also called DV01 (dollar value of 01). Used for hedging calculations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—uses 0.00001 instead of 0.0001
- Option C—uses 0.001 instead of 0.0001
