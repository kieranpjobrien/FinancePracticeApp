# Question

Agency MBS (Ginnie Mae, Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac) have:

## Options

- **A**: No credit risk because they are government-guaranteed or government-sponsored
- **B**: Higher credit risk than corporate bonds
- **C**: No prepayment risk

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Agency MBS: Ginnie Mae has explicit US government guarantee. Fannie/Freddie have implicit (now explicit after conservatorship) support. Minimal credit risk on timely payment. Still have prepayment and extension risk. Trade at tighter spreads than non-agency MBS.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—agency MBS have less credit risk than corporates
- Option C—all MBS have prepayment risk
