# Question

A payment-in-kind (PIK) bond allows the issuer to:

## Options

- **A**: Pay coupons in additional bonds instead of cash
- **B**: Skip coupon payments entirely
- **C**: Pay coupons in equity shares

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PIK bonds: issuer can pay interest by issuing more bonds instead of cash. Increases principal owed. Common in leveraged buyouts when cash flow is constrained. Higher risk—debt grows if PIK option exercised.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes missed payments/default
- Option C describes convertible features
