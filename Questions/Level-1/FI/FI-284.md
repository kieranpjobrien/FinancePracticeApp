# Question

An "on-the-run" Treasury security is:

## Options

- **A**: A Treasury that has been called by the issuer
- **B**: The most recently issued Treasury of a given maturity
- **C**: A Treasury trading at a significant discount

## Answer

B

## Explanation

On-the-run: most recently auctioned Treasury for each maturity (2-year, 5-year, 10-year, etc.). Most liquid, used as benchmark. Off-the-run: previously issued, less liquid, may trade at slight discount (liquidity premium). On-the-run serves as reference rate.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—Treasuries are not callable
- Option C—on-the-run refers to issuance, not price
