# Question

According to pure expectations theory, an upward-sloping yield curve implies:

## Options

- **A**: Investors expect short-term rates to rise in the future
- **B**: Investors require a term premium for longer maturities
- **C**: Long-term bonds are riskier than short-term bonds

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pure expectations: yield curve shape reflects only expected future short rates. Upward slope = expected rate increases. No term premium. Forward rates are unbiased predictors of future spot rates. Ignores risk preferences.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes liquidity preference theory
- Option C describes risk relationship but not expectations theory
