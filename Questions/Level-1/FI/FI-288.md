# Question

A zero-coupon bond:

## Options

- **A**: Pays interest annually at a fixed rate
- **B**: Is issued at a discount and matures at par with no periodic payments
- **C**: Has variable interest payments linked to a reference rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Zero-coupon: no periodic interest. Issued at discount, matures at par. Return = price appreciation. Examples: Treasury STRIPS, savings bonds. All duration equals maturity (no reinvestment risk). Price more sensitive to rates than coupon bonds of same maturity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes fixed-coupon bond
- Option C—describes floating-rate note
