# Question

A credit rating agency's "negative outlook" indicates:

## Options

- **A**: The rating will definitely be downgraded
- **B**: There is a heightened probability of a downgrade over the medium term
- **C**: The rating has already been downgraded

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative outlook: rating may be lowered if adverse trends continue. Not guaranteed downgrade—depends on developments. "Credit watch" is more urgent (typically 90 days). Outlooks extend over 1-2 years. Positive outlook suggests potential upgrade.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—outlook indicates possibility, not certainty
- Option C—outlook precedes potential action
