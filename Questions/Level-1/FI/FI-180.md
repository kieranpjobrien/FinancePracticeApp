# Question

Putable bonds exhibit increased positive convexity when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates are very low
- **B**: Interest rates rise significantly, making the put option valuable
- **C**: The issuer's credit quality improves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Putable bond: holder can sell back at put price. When rates rise, put becomes valuable—limits downside. Price falls less than non-putable. Enhanced positive convexity from put protection. Floor on price regardless of rate rises.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—low rates mean put unlikely, less convexity benefit
- Option C—credit improvement helps but not why putable adds convexity
