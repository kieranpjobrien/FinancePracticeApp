# Question

A structured note's return is typically linked to:

## Options

- **A**: Only the issuer's credit quality
- **B**: The performance of an underlying asset, index, or formula
- **C**: Treasury bond yields exclusively

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Structured notes: combine bond with derivative. Returns linked to equity indexes, commodities, currencies, interest rates, or formulas. Principal protection possible but not guaranteed. Credit risk to issuer plus market risk from underlying exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes plain corporate bonds
- Option C too narrow—can link to many underlyings
