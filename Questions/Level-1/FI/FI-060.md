# Question

Covered bonds differ from asset-backed securities because covered bonds:

## Options

- **A**: Have no underlying collateral
- **B**: Provide dual recourse to both the issuer and a segregated cover pool of assets
- **C**: Are only issued by governments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered bonds: dual recourse—claim against issuer AND segregated collateral pool. If issuer defaults, pool remains for bondholders. Cover pool typically mortgages or public sector loans. Popular in Europe. Assets stay on issuer's balance sheet. Higher credit quality due to dual protection.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—covered bonds have collateral (cover pool)
- Option C—covered bonds are issued by financial institutions
