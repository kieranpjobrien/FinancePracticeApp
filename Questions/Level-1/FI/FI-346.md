# Question

Interest rate swaps are commonly used in liability-driven investing to:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminate all interest rate exposure
- **B**: Extend portfolio duration to match long-dated liabilities without buying long-term bonds
- **C**: Increase credit risk exposure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Swaps for LDI: receive fixed, pay floating extends duration. Allows matching long-dated pension liabilities without concentrated long-bond positions. Capital-efficient (no upfront cash). Synthetic duration adjustment. Overlays common for pension funds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—swaps transform, don't eliminate rate exposure
- Option C—interest rate swaps primarily affect rate risk, not credit
