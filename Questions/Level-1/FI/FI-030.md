# Question

An inverted yield curve is often interpreted as:

## Options

- **A**: A signal of strong economic growth ahead
- **B**: A potential indicator of economic recession
- **C**: Having no economic significance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inverted curve: short-term rates exceed long-term rates. Historically preceded recessions. Interpretation: market expects rate cuts due to economic weakness. Fed tightening pushes short rates up while long rates reflect lower growth/inflation expectations. Not perfect predictor but notable signal.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—inversion suggests weakness
- Option C ignores well-documented predictive relationship
