# Question

A mortgage interest-only (IO) strip:

## Options

- **A**: Benefits from faster prepayments
- **B**: Loses value when prepayments accelerate
- **C**: Has no prepayment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IO strip: receives only interest payments from mortgage pool. Faster prepayments = smaller principal base = less interest. IO loses value when rates fall (prepayments rise). Negative duration—gains when rates rise. PO strip is opposite.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes PO (principal-only) strip behavior
- Option C—IOs have significant prepayment exposure
