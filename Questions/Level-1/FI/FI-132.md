# Question

Liquidity preference theory suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Investors prefer long-term bonds for their higher yields
- **B**: Investors require a premium to hold longer-term bonds due to greater price risk
- **C**: Short-term and long-term bonds are perfect substitutes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity preference: investors prefer shorter maturities (more liquid, less price risk). Require term premium for longer bonds. Yield curve biased upward even if rates expected flat. Combines expectations with risk premium.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A states preference but misses the risk premium concept
- Option C describes pure expectations theory
