# Question

Agency bonds issued by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac:

## Options

- **A**: Carry the explicit full faith and credit guarantee of the US government
- **B**: Have an implicit government backing but are not explicitly guaranteed
- **C**: Have no relationship to the government

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GSE (Government-Sponsored Enterprise) bonds: Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac—implicit guarantee, not explicit. Government conservatorship since 2008 reinforced support perception. Ginnie Mae is different—explicitly government-guaranteed. GSE bonds yield more than Treasuries but less than corporates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Ginnie Mae or Treasury securities
- Option C ignores GSE government sponsorship and conservatorship
