# Question

Key rate duration measures:

## Options

- **A**: Sensitivity to changes in the overall level of interest rates
- **B**: Sensitivity to changes in rates at specific maturities along the yield curve
- **C**: The average time to receive bond cash flows

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Key rate duration: price sensitivity to change in single point on yield curve (e.g., 2-year, 5-year, 10-year). Sum of key rate durations equals effective duration. Useful for non-parallel shift analysis. Shows where on curve bond has most exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes effective/modified duration
- Option C describes Macaulay duration concept
