# Question

In a company's capital structure, senior secured debt has:

## Options

- **A**: The lowest priority claim in bankruptcy
- **B**: Higher priority and typically higher recovery rates than unsecured debt
- **C**: No collateral backing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Seniority hierarchy: senior secured > senior unsecured > subordinated > junior subordinated > preferred > equity. Secured debt backed by collateral (assets pledged). Higher seniority = higher recovery in default = lower yield required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses priority—senior is highest, not lowest
- Option C contradicts "secured" which means collateral-backed
