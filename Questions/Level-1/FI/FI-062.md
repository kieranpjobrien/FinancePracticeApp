# Question

The Z-spread (zero-volatility spread) is:

## Options

- **A**: The spread that makes a bond's price equal its par value
- **B**: The constant spread added to each spot rate to equate present value of cash flows to price
- **C**: The difference between two bond's yields to maturity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Z-spread: single spread over entire spot curve that discounts bond's cash flows to market price. More precise than nominal spread (over single benchmark yield). Doesn't account for option effects—use OAS for optioned bonds. Shows spread across term structure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a par yield concept
- Option C describes yield spread between bonds
