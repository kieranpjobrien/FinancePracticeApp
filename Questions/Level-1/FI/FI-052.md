# Question

SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate) has replaced LIBOR because:

## Options

- **A**: SOFR is a higher rate than LIBOR
- **B**: SOFR is based on actual transactions in the Treasury repo market, making it more robust
- **C**: LIBOR was too stable and predictable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

SOFR: risk-free rate based on overnight Treasury repo transactions. Replaced LIBOR due to manipulation scandals and thin underlying market. SOFR is backward-looking, transaction-based, secured. Transition completed in 2023. Key benchmark for derivatives and floating-rate instruments.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—SOFR level relative to LIBOR varied
- Option C—LIBOR issues were manipulation and lack of transactions, not stability
