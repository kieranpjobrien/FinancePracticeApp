# Question

A bond trading at a premium:

## Options

- **A**: Has a coupon rate lower than the market yield
- **B**: Has a coupon rate higher than the market yield
- **C**: Always has embedded options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Premium bond: price > par. Occurs when coupon rate > market yield. Investors pay extra for above-market coupons. As maturity approaches, premium amortizes toward par. Discount bond: coupon < yield, price < par. Price reflects present value of cash flows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes discount bond
- Option C—premium/discount unrelated to options
