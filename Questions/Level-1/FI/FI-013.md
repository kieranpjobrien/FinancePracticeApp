# Question

Modified duration estimates:

## Options

- **A**: The percentage price change for a 1% change in yield
- **B**: The bond's credit spread over Treasury
- **C**: The time remaining until maturity

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Modified duration = Macaulay duration / (1 + yield/n). Measures price sensitivity to yield changes. If modified duration is 5, a 1% yield increase causes approximately 5% price decrease. Linear approximation—convexity needed for large yield changes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes credit spread analysis
- Option C describes remaining maturity, not duration
