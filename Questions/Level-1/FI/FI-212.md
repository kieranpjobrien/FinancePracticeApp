# Question

When a central bank raises its policy rate, bond prices typically:

## Options

- **A**: Rise across all maturities
- **B**: Fall, especially at the short end of the yield curve
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Policy rate increase: directly raises short-term rates, prices fall. Long end may move less (already pricing in tightening, or expecting slower growth). Yield curve often flattens with rate hikes. "Bear flattening" if curve flattens with rising rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—higher rates mean lower prices
- Option C—policy rates directly impact bond markets
