# Question

A collateralized debt obligation (CDO) differs from a typical ABS because CDOs:

## Options

- **A**: Have no credit risk
- **B**: Are backed by a portfolio of bonds, loans, or other debt instruments rather than consumer loans
- **C**: Do not use tranching

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CDO: securitizes corporate bonds, loans, or other debt (not consumer assets like mortgages/auto loans). Creates tranched exposure to credit portfolio. Types: CLO (leveraged loans), CBO (bonds). Active management possible. Credit derivatives may be used.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—CDOs have significant credit risk
- Option C—CDOs heavily use tranching for credit enhancement
