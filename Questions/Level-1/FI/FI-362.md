# Question

The G-spread is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: The spread over the swap curve
- **B**: The spread over an interpolated government bond yield of the same maturity
- **C**: The option-adjusted spread

## Answer

B

## Explanation

G-spread: yield spread over government (Treasury) benchmark. Uses interpolated government yield for exact maturity match. Simple measure but ignores term structure shape. Quoted for most corporate bonds. Different from I-spread (swap curve) or Z-spread (zero curve).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes I-spread (swap curve)
- Option C—OAS adjusts for embedded options
