# Question

A make-whole call provision:

## Options

- **A**: Allows the issuer to call at par at any time
- **B**: Requires the issuer to pay the present value of remaining cash flows discounted at a spread over Treasuries
- **C**: Protects the issuer from declining interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Make-whole call: call price = PV of remaining cash flows at Treasury + fixed spread. Expensive for issuer—no economic incentive to call when rates fall. Provides call flexibility for M&A but minimal cost to investors. Effectively bondholder-friendly call provision.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes par call, not make-whole
- Option C—make-whole protects bondholders, not issuer
