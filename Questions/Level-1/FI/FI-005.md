# Question

A zero-coupon bond:

## Options

- **A**: Pays interest annually at a fixed rate
- **B**: Pays no periodic interest and is sold at a discount to par value
- **C**: Has no maturity date

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Zero-coupon bonds: no periodic interest payments. Issued at discount, matures at par. Return comes entirely from price appreciation. Examples: Treasury STRIPS, savings bonds. Accretion of discount recognized as interest income for tax purposes even without cash receipt.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes coupon-paying bonds
- Option C describes perpetual bonds (consols)
