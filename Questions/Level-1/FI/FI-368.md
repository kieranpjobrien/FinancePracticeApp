# Question

A wider bid-ask spread on a bond typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Higher liquidity
- **B**: Lower liquidity and higher transaction costs
- **C**: Better credit quality

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Wide bid-ask = less liquid. Dealers charge more to compensate for inventory risk. Off-the-run bonds, smaller issues, high-yield typically wider spreads. Liquidity costs matter for portfolios with turnover. On-the-run Treasuries have tightest spreads.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—wider spread means LESS liquidity
- Option C—credit quality affects spread level, not bid-ask width directly
