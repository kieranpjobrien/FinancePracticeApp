# Question

A firm receives a recommendation change but some clients have limit orders that prevent immediate execution. Under Standard III(B):

## Options

- **A**: The firm should cancel clients' limit orders to ensure fair execution
- **B**: The firm should notify clients of the recommendation change and respect their limit orders
- **C**: Limit orders exempt clients from fair dealing requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(B) requires fair opportunity to act on recommendations. For clients with limit orders, this means notifying them of the recommendation change so they can adjust if desired, while respecting their standing instructions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates client instructions
- Option C incorrectly suggests limit orders remove fair dealing obligations
