# Question

A buy-side analyst receives a call from a sell-side analyst recommending a stock with a "strong buy" rating. To maintain independence and objectivity, the buy-side analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Automatically adopt the recommendation since sell-side analysts have more resources
- **B**: Conduct her own independent analysis before making any investment decision
- **C**: Ignore all sell-side research to avoid any possibility of bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Maintaining independence and objectivity requires conducting one's own analysis rather than simply adopting others' recommendations. Sell-side research can be a useful input, but the buy-side analyst must form her own independent opinion based on her own analysis and judgment of the investment merits.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A surrenders independent judgment by blindly following external recommendations
- Option C is unnecessarily extreme; using external research as an input is fine, but independent judgment must be applied
