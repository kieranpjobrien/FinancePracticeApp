# Question

A grateful client gives a portfolio manager an expensive watch as a gift after excellent performance. Under Standard IV(B), the manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Accept it graciously without disclosure since it came from a client, not a third party
- **B**: Disclose the gift to the employer and obtain consent before accepting
- **C**: Accept it privately to avoid embarrassing the client

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(B) requires disclosure and consent for compensation or benefits received from parties other than the employer. Client gifts represent additional compensation that could create loyalty conflicts and must be disclosed.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misreads the Standard as only applying to non-client parties
- Option C prioritizes social comfort over ethical requirements
