# Question

A research analyst is approached by a small-cap company offering to pay $50,000 for the analyst to write a research report on the company. The analyst's firm does not currently cover this company. To maintain ethical standards, the analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Decline because issuer-paid research is always prohibited
- **B**: Accept only if the payment and potential conflicts are disclosed in the research report
- **C**: Accept the payment but not disclose it to avoid biasing readers against the research

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Issuer-paid research is permissible but requires full disclosure of the arrangement and any potential conflicts of interest. Readers must be informed that the subject company paid for the research so they can appropriately assess potential bias. The disclosure should be prominent and clearly explain the nature of the compensation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; issuer-paid research is allowed with proper disclosure
- Option C violates disclosure requirements and could constitute misrepresentation
