# Question

Client information protected under Standard III(E) includes:

## Options

- **A**: Only financial account information
- **B**: All information communicated by the client in the professional relationship
- **C**: Only information marked as confidential

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(E) broadly protects information obtained through the client relationship, including personal matters, investment preferences, and any other information shared in confidence—not just financial data.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A too narrowly defines protected information
- Option C requires explicit marking that isn't necessary
