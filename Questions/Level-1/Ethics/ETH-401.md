# Question

A manager presents backtested strategy results to prospective clients without clearly labeling them as simulated. This:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable if the backtest methodology is sound
- **B**: Violates Standard III(D) because simulated results must be clearly identified
- **C**: Is permitted if actual results are also shown

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(D) requires clear identification of simulated or backtested results. Prospective clients must understand they are not viewing actual performance. Failing to label simulated results creates a misleading impression.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the labeling requirement
- Option C does not excuse the failure to identify simulated results
