# Question

Under Standard II(A), information is considered "material" if its disclosure would most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Cause any change in the security's price
- **B**: Affect the price of a security or influence a reasonable investor's decision
- **C**: Result in regulatory investigation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information is material if a reasonable investor would consider it important when making an investment decision, or if it would likely affect the security's price. The standard uses a reasonable investor test, not just any price movement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets too low a threshold; trivial price changes don't indicate materiality
- Option C focuses on regulatory consequences rather than investment relevance
