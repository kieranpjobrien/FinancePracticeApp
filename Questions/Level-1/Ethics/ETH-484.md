# Question

After leaving a firm, a member may contact former clients to solicit their business:

## Options

- **A**: Never, as this violates loyalty to the former employer
- **B**: After leaving, using publicly available contact information
- **C**: Only if given written permission by the former employer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) applies during employment. After leaving, members may compete for business using publicly available information. They cannot take proprietary client lists but may contact clients through public channels.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A extends loyalty obligations beyond employment
- Option C imposes an unnecessary permission requirement
