# Question

The Code of Ethics recognizes duties to:

## Options

- **A**: Only the member's direct clients
- **B**: Clients, prospective clients, employers, colleagues, and the investment profession
- **C**: Only regulators and employers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Code of Ethics establishes broad duties extending beyond immediate client relationships. Members must consider the impact of their actions on the profession, colleagues, and the functioning of capital markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A too narrowly limits the scope of ethical duties
- Option C ignores duties to clients and the profession
