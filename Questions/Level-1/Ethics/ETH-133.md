# Question

Standard III(A) Loyalty, Prudence, and Care requires members to act for the benefit of:

## Options

- **A**: The employer above all other parties
- **B**: Clients, placing their interests ahead of the member's or employer's interests
- **C**: The investment profession generally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(A) establishes a duty of loyalty to clients, requiring members to place client interests above their own interests and the interests of their employer. This is a fundamental fiduciary principle in investment management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the priority; client interests come before employer interests
- Option C describes a Code of Ethics obligation but not the specific focus of Standard III(A)
