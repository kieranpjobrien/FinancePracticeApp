# Question

An investment advisor plans to leave her current firm to start her own practice. Before resigning, she:

## Options

- **A**: May begin soliciting the firm's clients to move their accounts to her new practice
- **B**: Should not solicit the firm's clients until after she has left and is no longer an employee
- **C**: Must sign a non-compete agreement before being allowed to contact any clients

## Answer

B

## Explanation

While employed, an investment professional owes a duty of loyalty to the employer and should not solicit the employer's clients for a competing business. After departing, the former employee may generally contact clients (subject to any valid contractual restrictions) since the employment relationship has ended. The timing of when solicitation begins is the critical factor.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates the duty of loyalty owed while still employed
- Option C overstates the requirement; non-competes may exist but are not universally required for ethical conduct
