# Question

A client requests a highly leveraged investment strategy. The portfolio manager believes this exceeds the client's risk capacity. Under Standard III(C):

## Options

- **A**: The manager must implement the strategy as requested
- **B**: The manager should discuss concerns and may refuse if the strategy is clearly unsuitable
- **C**: Leverage decisions are always the client's responsibility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(C) requires assessing suitability based on the client's full financial situation. If leverage would be clearly unsuitable given risk capacity, the manager should explain concerns and may refuse to implement, even if the client requests it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the manager's suitability responsibility
- Option C abdicates professional judgment
