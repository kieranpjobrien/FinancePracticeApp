# Question

An analyst receives conflicting information from two credible sources. Under Standard V(A):

## Options

- **A**: The analyst should use whichever source supports her thesis
- **B**: The analyst should investigate further to resolve the discrepancy
- **C**: Conflicting sources mean no recommendation can be made

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard V(A) requires diligent investigation to establish reasonable basis. When sources conflict, further investigation is needed to resolve discrepancies before reaching conclusions. Cherry-picking supportive sources or abandoning analysis entirely is inappropriate.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A introduces confirmation bias
- Option C is unnecessarily restrictive
