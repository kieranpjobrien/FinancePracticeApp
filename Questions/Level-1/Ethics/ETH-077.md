# Question

When presenting investment analysis, professionals should distinguish between:

## Options

- **A**: Short-term and long-term recommendations only
- **B**: Facts and opinions in their communications
- **C**: Domestic and international investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Clear communication requires distinguishing facts from opinions. Clients should understand which statements represent verified information versus the analyst's interpretation, judgment, or forecast. This distinction helps clients appropriately weigh different elements of the analysis and form their own views.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A addresses time horizon but not the fundamental clarity requirement
- Option C relates to investment categorization, not communication standards
