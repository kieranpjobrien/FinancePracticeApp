# Question

A portfolio manager's parents have an account at her firm. When allocating trades, she should:

## Options

- **A**: Ensure her parents receive favorable allocations as a family benefit
- **B**: Treat her parents' account the same as any other client account
- **C**: Exclude her parents from allocations to avoid any appearance of impropriety

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(B) requires fair treatment of all clients. Family accounts should receive neither preferential nor disadvantaged treatment. Members must apply the same allocation policies to family members as to all other clients.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A provides preferential treatment to family
- Option C discriminates against family accounts unfairly
