# Question

Which of the following best describes the primary purpose of a professional code of ethics for investment professionals?

## Options

- **A**: To provide legal protection for firms against client lawsuits
- **B**: To establish a framework for ethical decision-making and professional conduct
- **C**: To replace regulatory requirements with industry self-governance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A professional code of ethics serves as a framework for ethical decision-making and establishes standards of professional conduct that members are expected to uphold. It provides guidance on how to handle situations that may not be explicitly covered by laws or regulations, promoting integrity and trust in the profession.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect because codes of ethics are not primarily designed for legal protection; they set higher standards than mere legal compliance
- Option C is incorrect because codes of ethics complement rather than replace regulatory requirements
