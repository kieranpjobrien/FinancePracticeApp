# Question

While at a social event, an analyst overhears two executives from a publicly traded company discussing a merger that has not been announced. The analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Immediately trade on the information since she did not seek it out intentionally
- **B**: Refrain from trading and not communicate the information until it becomes public
- **C**: Share the information with her firm's compliance department who can decide how to proceed with trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The prohibition on trading on material nonpublic information applies regardless of how the information was obtained. Even accidentally overheard information cannot be acted upon if it is both material and nonpublic. The analyst must refrain from trading and from communicating the information to others who might trade on it. The information should be kept confidential until the company makes a public announcement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly suggests intent matters; the prohibition applies regardless of how information is obtained
- Option C incorrectly suggests compliance can authorize trading on MNPI; they cannot, and sharing the information risks spreading the violation
