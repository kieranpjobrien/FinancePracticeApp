# Question

A portfolio manager has been managing a client's aggressive growth portfolio for 5 years. The client recently retired and requests that the portfolio be shifted to income-generating securities. The manager believes growth stocks will outperform over the next year. According to Standard III(C) Suitability, the manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Maintain the current aggressive allocation since the manager has superior market insight
- **B**: Update the investment policy statement and adjust the portfolio to reflect the client's new circumstances
- **C**: Recommend the client maintain the current allocation but reduce position sizes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(C) Suitability requires that members and candidates make reasonable inquiry into a client's investment experience, risk and return objectives, and financial constraints, and must regularly update this information. When a client's circumstances change (such as retirement), the IPS must be updated and the portfolio adjusted accordingly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates suitability requirements by prioritizing the manager's market views over the client's stated needs and changed circumstances
- Option C is a half-measure that doesn't properly address the fundamental change in the client's risk tolerance and income needs
