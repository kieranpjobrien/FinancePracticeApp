# Question

A portfolio manager is arrested for disorderly conduct at a sporting event. This incident:

## Options

- **A**: Automatically violates Standard I(D)
- **B**: May or may not violate Standard I(D) depending on severity and circumstances
- **C**: Cannot be a violation because it occurred in a personal setting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(D) violations depend on whether conduct reflects adversely on professional integrity, competence, or reputation. Minor personal incidents may not rise to this level, while more serious misconduct might. Context and severity matter.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies the Standard too broadly to any personal incident
- Option C incorrectly exempts all personal conduct from scrutiny
