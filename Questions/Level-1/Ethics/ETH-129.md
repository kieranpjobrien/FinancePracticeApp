# Question

An analyst combines publicly available industry data with insights from management meetings where no material nonpublic information was shared. Her investment conclusion differs from consensus. This approach:

## Options

- **A**: Violates Standard II(A) because it uses non-public information
- **B**: Is permitted under the mosaic theory
- **C**: Requires disclosure to clients before acting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The mosaic theory permits analysts to combine public information with non-material nonpublic information gathered through research to form investment conclusions. As long as no material nonpublic information was used, the analyst's unique conclusion is the result of legitimate analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly categorizes non-material information as prohibited
- Option C is unnecessary since no MNPI was used
