# Question

Information becomes "public" for purposes of trading restrictions when it:

## Options

- **A**: Is shared with a select group of institutional investors at a private meeting
- **B**: Is disseminated broadly so that market participants have access to it
- **C**: Has been known by company management for at least 24 hours

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information is considered public when it has been broadly disseminated to the marketplace and investors have had an opportunity to react to it. The key test is whether the information is accessible to the general investing public through established distribution channels. A reasonable time must also pass after dissemination for the market to absorb the information.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes selective disclosure, which does not make information public for trading purposes
- Option C focuses on an arbitrary time period rather than the manner of dissemination to the public
