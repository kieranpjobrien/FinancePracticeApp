# Question

A compliance officer discovers that a junior analyst has been making personal trades ahead of client trades. The compliance officer informed the analyst's supervisor, who took no action. The compliance officer should:

## Options

- **A**: Take no further action since the matter has been referred to the appropriate supervisor
- **B**: Escalate the matter to senior management or take other steps to ensure proper handling
- **C**: Report the supervisor to regulators for failing to act on the violation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Supervisors have a responsibility to ensure that violations are properly addressed. When a supervisor fails to act, those with compliance responsibilities must escalate the matter to ensure the violation is remediated. Simply referring a matter to a supervisor who then ignores it does not fulfill supervisory and compliance obligations. Further escalation within the organization is required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly suggests that referral alone satisfies compliance duties
- Option C may be premature; internal escalation should typically precede external reporting
