# Question

A CFA charterholder is convicted of tax fraud unrelated to his investment work. Under Standard I(D):

## Options

- **A**: This is not a violation because it occurred outside the investment profession
- **B**: This may constitute misconduct as it reflects adversely on professional integrity
- **C**: Only investment-related crimes can trigger Standard I(D)

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(D) covers conduct reflecting adversely on professional reputation, integrity, or competence regardless of whether it involves investment activities. Tax fraud, though not investment-related, reflects on the member's honesty and integrity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly limits misconduct to professional activities
- Option C too narrowly defines the scope of misconduct
