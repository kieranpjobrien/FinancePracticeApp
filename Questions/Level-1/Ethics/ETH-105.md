# Question

A portfolio manager joins a new firm and wants to bring her historical track record from her previous employer. Under GIPS, this performance may be portable to the new firm if:

## Options

- **A**: The manager provides written documentation of her past performance
- **B**: Substantially all the decision-making personnel join the new firm and records can properly document the track record
- **C**: The new firm simply discloses that the performance was earned at a prior employer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS has specific requirements for performance portability. The track record may be linked to the new firm if substantially all the investment decision-makers come to the new firm, the decision-making process remains substantially intact, and proper records exist to support the performance history. Mere disclosure or documentation by the manager is insufficient.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A lacks the substantive requirements for portability
- Option C treats disclosure as sufficient when specific conditions must be met
