# Question

Research models and analytical tools developed by an analyst during employment are generally considered:

## Options

- **A**: The personal property of the analyst who created them
- **B**: The property of the employer who paid for the analyst's time
- **C**: Public domain materials that either party may use freely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Work product created during employment typically belongs to the employer. This includes research models, proprietary methods, client lists, and analytical tools developed using the employer's resources and time. Employees should not take such materials when leaving or use them for competing purposes without authorization.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands employment relationships; work created on company time generally belongs to the company
- Option C incorrectly suggests no ownership rights exist; employers have clear rights to work product
