# Question

An analyst who possesses material nonpublic information is prohibited from:

## Options

- **A**: Continuing to follow the security as part of normal research activities
- **B**: Recommending that clients trade in the security based on that information
- **C**: Maintaining the information in secure files until it becomes public

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professionals who possess material nonpublic information are prohibited from acting on it themselves or causing others to act on it. This includes making recommendations that are based on the MNPI, even if the information itself is not explicitly shared. "Tipping" others to trade, whether directly or through recommendations, violates the same principles as direct trading.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is permitted—analysts can continue normal research activities as long as they don't trade or recommend trades based on the MNPI
- Option C is appropriate record-keeping; maintaining confidential information securely is expected
