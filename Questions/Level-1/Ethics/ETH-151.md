# Question

An analyst recommends a high-yield bond without mentioning its credit risk. This most likely violates Standard V(B) by:

## Options

- **A**: Failing to disclose significant factors that could affect the investment's value
- **B**: Recommending an unsuitable investment
- **C**: Using material nonpublic information

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Standard V(B) requires disclosure of significant risks and limitations affecting an investment recommendation. Credit risk is a fundamental consideration for bonds, and failing to mention it omits information important to the investment decision.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B addresses suitability (Standard III(C)), a separate issue from communication
- Option C relates to insider trading, which is not indicated here
