# Question

A portfolio manager manages accounts under a written investment policy statement (IPS) that specifies the account should hold only investment-grade bonds. The manager identifies a high-yield bond that she believes offers exceptional risk-adjusted returns. The manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Purchase the bond since her professional judgment supports the investment decision
- **B**: Not purchase the bond without first obtaining client approval to modify the IPS
- **C**: Purchase a small position as a test before recommending changes to the IPS

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An investment policy statement represents the client's agreed-upon parameters for managing the account. The manager must operate within those constraints unless they are formally modified with client consent. Even if the manager believes an investment outside the IPS parameters would benefit the client, she cannot deviate without authorization. The proper course is to discuss with the client and, if appropriate, update the IPS.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes the manager's judgment over the client's documented preferences and the agreed IPS
- Option C still violates the IPS, even if only for a small position; any deviation requires authorization
