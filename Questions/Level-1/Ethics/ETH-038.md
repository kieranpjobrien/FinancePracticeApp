# Question

A portfolio manager is convicted of drunk driving, an offense unrelated to his investment activities. Under professional standards related to misconduct, which statement is most accurate?

## Options

- **A**: The conviction has no relevance to his professional standing since it occurred outside work
- **B**: The conviction may reflect on his integrity and could constitute a violation of professional standards
- **C**: Only securities-related legal violations can trigger professional conduct reviews

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professional misconduct standards extend beyond securities-related activities to include any conduct that reflects adversely on a member's integrity, competence, or professional reputation. A criminal conviction, even for an unrelated offense, may call into question a member's judgment and trustworthiness, potentially constituting a violation of standards governing professional conduct.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly assumes professional standards only apply to work-related activities
- Option C is too narrow; standards address overall professional reputation and integrity
