# Question

A firm implements a blackout period preventing employee trades around recommendation changes. This policy:

## Options

- **A**: Unnecessarily restricts employee investment freedom
- **B**: Helps ensure compliance with Standard VI(B) by preventing front-running
- **C**: Is only required for large-cap stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Blackout periods around recommendation changes help firms comply with Standard VI(B) by preventing employees from trading ahead of clients. This is a reasonable procedure to ensure client transactions receive priority.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores legitimate compliance purposes
- Option C incorrectly limits the policy by market cap
