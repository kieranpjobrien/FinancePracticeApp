# Question

A portfolio manager discovers a legal tax strategy that would significantly reduce a client's tax liability. However, the strategy involves complex offshore structures that, while legal, could damage the client's reputation if publicly disclosed. Recommending this strategy would most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Be acceptable since the strategy is legal and provides clear financial benefit to the client
- **B**: Require the manager to consider whether the strategy aligns with ethical standards even though it is legal
- **C**: Violate the manager's fiduciary duty because tax minimization strategies are inherently unethical

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This scenario illustrates that legal compliance alone does not guarantee ethical behavior. While the strategy is legal, the portfolio manager must consider broader ethical implications including the client's full interests (including reputational risk), transparency, and whether the strategy aligns with the spirit of professional conduct. Ethical analysis should accompany legal analysis in professional decision-making.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A focuses solely on legality and ignores the ethical dimension and potential reputational harm to the client
- Option C goes too far—tax minimization strategies are not inherently unethical; the issue is whether this specific strategy serves the client's overall interests and meets ethical standards
