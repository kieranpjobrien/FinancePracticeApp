# Question

The duty of loyalty to clients requires investment professionals to:

## Options

- **A**: Always recommend the investments with the highest potential returns
- **B**: Place client interests ahead of their own and their employer's interests
- **C**: Maintain exclusive relationships with each client they serve

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The duty of loyalty requires that investment professionals always act in the best interests of their clients, placing those interests ahead of personal interests and, where appropriate, ahead of their employer's interests. This fundamental duty ensures that clients can trust that their advisor is working for their benefit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses loyalty with return maximization; loyalty involves acting in the client's best interest, which includes considering risk and suitability
- Option C misunderstands loyalty; professionals can serve multiple clients as long as they are loyal to each
