# Question

A portfolio manager receives a hot IPO allocation of 10,000 shares that is insufficient to fill all client orders. The manager has orders for 50,000 shares across five discretionary accounts. One account belongs to a high-net-worth individual who generates significant fee revenue, while the others are smaller retirement accounts. According to Standard III(B) Fair Dealing, the manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Allocate all shares to the high-net-worth client since they generate more revenue and can better utilize the investment
- **B**: Allocate shares pro rata across all accounts that placed orders, regardless of account size or fee revenue
- **C**: Allocate shares based on each account's investment objectives, giving priority to accounts where the IPO best fits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(B) Fair Dealing requires members to deal fairly with all clients when taking investment action. For IPO allocations that are oversubscribed, the recommended procedure is pro rata allocation across accounts that have expressed interest and for which the investment is suitable. The size of the account or fee revenue generated should not influence allocation decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates fair dealing by favoring larger fee-generating clients, which would discriminate against smaller clients
- Option C sounds reasonable but could be used to justify preferential treatment; pro rata allocation is the clearest way to ensure fairness when demand exceeds supply
