# Question

A firm names itself "CFA Investment Advisors" even though not all employees are charterholders. Under Standard VII(B):

## Options

- **A**: This is acceptable if at least one employee is a charterholder
- **B**: This may misrepresent the firm's credentials by implying all staff hold the designation
- **C**: Firm names are not covered by the Standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Using CFA in a firm name may misrepresent the firm's credentials if it implies that all professionals hold the designation. Standard VII(B) prohibits such misrepresentation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets too low a threshold
- Option C incorrectly exempts firm naming
