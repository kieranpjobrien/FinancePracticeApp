# Question

An analyst planning to leave her firm to start a competing advisory practice begins preparing marketing materials using general skills learned at work, without using proprietary client lists or trade secrets. This is:

## Options

- **A**: A violation because she cannot prepare for a competing business while employed
- **B**: Permissible preparation as long as she does not use confidential employer information
- **C**: Only acceptable after formal resignation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) permits reasonable preparation for departure, including developing general marketing materials. The key distinction is between using general skills and knowledge (permissible) versus taking confidential information or client lists (prohibited).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overly restricts reasonable career planning
- Option C incorrectly requires resignation before any preparation
