# Question

Which of the following actions would most likely constitute misrepresentation under professional standards?

## Options

- **A**: Omitting disclosure of a professional designation that is currently inactive
- **B**: Claiming to guarantee investment returns in marketing materials
- **C**: Providing a range of possible outcomes rather than a single point estimate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Guaranteeing investment returns is a clear misrepresentation because investment outcomes are inherently uncertain. Such claims mislead clients about the nature of investing and the risks involved. Professional standards prohibit making statements that are false or misleading, including guarantees of future performance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be questionable but is less severe than an outright false claim about investment outcomes
- Option C is actually good practice—providing ranges acknowledges uncertainty rather than making misleading point predictions
