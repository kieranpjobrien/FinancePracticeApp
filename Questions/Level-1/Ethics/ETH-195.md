# Question

A trader executes numerous small trades near market close to push up a stock's closing price. This practice, known as "painting the tape," violates Standard II(B) because:

## Options

- **A**: Trading near market close is prohibited
- **B**: The transactions are intended to distort the price and mislead market participants
- **C**: Small trades are less liquid than large trades

## Answer

B

## Explanation

"Painting the tape" manipulates closing prices through trades intended to distort market prices rather than execute legitimate investment decisions. Standard II(B) prohibits practices that artificially affect prices to mislead other investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overgeneralizes; trading near close is not inherently wrong
- Option C is factually incorrect and irrelevant to manipulation
