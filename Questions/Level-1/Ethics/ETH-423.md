# Question

A CEO selectively tells analysts at a conference that upcoming earnings will be below estimates. An analyst attending:

## Options

- **A**: May trade since the information was disclosed at a public conference
- **B**: Should not trade until the information is broadly disseminated to the public
- **C**: May share the information with preferred clients before trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Selective disclosure at a conference does not constitute broad public dissemination. Standard II(A) prohibits trading on material nonpublic information. The analyst must wait until the information is widely available through proper channels.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses selective disclosure with public dissemination
- Option C compounds the violation by tipping others
