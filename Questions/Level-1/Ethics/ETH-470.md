# Question

A firm creates carve-outs from larger portfolios to show equity-only performance. GIPS requires:

## Options

- **A**: No special requirements for carve-outs
- **B**: Cash must be allocated to carve-outs in a documented, systematic manner
- **C**: Carve-outs are prohibited under GIPS

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS permits carve-outs but requires systematic cash allocation. Without proper cash treatment, carve-out performance can be misleading. Firms must document and consistently apply their cash allocation methodology.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores special carve-out requirements
- Option C incorrectly prohibits a permissible practice
