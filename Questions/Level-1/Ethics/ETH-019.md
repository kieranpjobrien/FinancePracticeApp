# Question

The relationship between ethical standards and legal standards is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Identical—any action that is legal is also ethical, and any action that is illegal is also unethical
- **B**: Overlapping but not identical—some actions may be legal but unethical, and ethical standards often exceed legal requirements
- **C**: Completely separate—ethical standards apply to personal behavior while legal standards apply only to business conduct

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ethical and legal standards overlap but are not identical. Laws establish minimum acceptable behavior enforced by government authority, while ethics represent higher standards based on moral principles. An action can be legal yet unethical (e.g., exploiting a client's lack of knowledge within legal bounds) or illegal in one jurisdiction but ethical (e.g., some historical civil rights violations of unjust laws). Investment professionals are expected to meet ethical standards that typically exceed mere legal compliance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly assumes perfect alignment; many legal activities can still be unethical, and professionals are expected to go beyond legal minimums
- Option C incorrectly separates the domains; both ethical and legal standards apply to business and personal conduct, and they frequently address the same situations
