# Question

An analyst is under pressure from her supervisor to issue a buy recommendation on a stock by the end of the day. She has completed only preliminary research and has significant concerns about the company's accounting practices. According to Standard V(A) Diligence and Reasonable Basis, the analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Issue the buy recommendation since her supervisor has approved it and she can complete the research later
- **B**: Request additional time to complete her analysis or decline to issue a recommendation without adequate support
- **C**: Issue a hold recommendation as a compromise until she can complete her research

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard V(A) requires that members have a reasonable and adequate basis, supported by appropriate research and investigation, for any investment recommendation. Issuing any recommendation without completing adequate due diligence violates this standard. The analyst should communicate her concerns to her supervisor and request additional time or refuse to issue a recommendation that lacks proper support.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A violates the standard by prioritizing supervisor pressure over the requirement for reasonable basis
- Option C is still a violation—any recommendation (including hold) must be supported by adequate research; issuing a hold without proper analysis is also problematic
