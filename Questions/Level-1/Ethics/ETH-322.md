# Question

Cross-trading securities between client accounts managed by the same firm:

## Options

- **A**: Is always prohibited as a conflict of interest
- **B**: May be acceptable if both clients benefit and proper procedures are followed
- **C**: Requires no special procedures since both are clients

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-trades between client accounts can be beneficial (saving transaction costs) but require careful handling to ensure fair pricing and that both clients benefit. Proper policies and procedures must be in place.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive when cross-trades can benefit both parties
- Option C ignores the need for fairness protections
