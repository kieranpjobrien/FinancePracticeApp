# Question

Professionalism in investment management is best demonstrated when a practitioner:

## Options

- **A**: Maximizes returns for clients regardless of the methods used to achieve those returns
- **B**: Maintains competence, acts with integrity, and places client interests above personal gain
- **C**: Follows all legal requirements while pursuing the most profitable opportunities for their firm

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professionalism in investment management requires maintaining and developing competence through ongoing education, acting with integrity in all professional dealings, and consistently placing client interests ahead of personal or firm interests. This goes beyond legal compliance to encompass ethical behavior and service orientation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the ethical constraints on how returns should be achieved; "regardless of methods" could include unethical or illegal practices
- Option C describes minimum legal compliance but misses the ethical dimension—following laws while maximizing firm profits could still involve conflicts of interest that harm clients
