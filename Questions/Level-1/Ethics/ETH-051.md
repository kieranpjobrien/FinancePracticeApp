# Question

An investor purchases a large position in a thinly traded stock, then posts numerous positive messages about the company on social media forums using multiple anonymous accounts, and sells after the price increases. This scheme violates standards concerning market manipulation because:

## Options

- **A**: The investor failed to disclose her position when posting the messages
- **B**: The investor used anonymous accounts rather than posting under her real name
- **C**: The investor's intent was to artificially inflate the price to sell at a profit

## Answer

C

## Explanation

This describes a "pump and dump" scheme, which is market manipulation regardless of disclosure. The core violation is the intent to artificially inflate prices through misleading communications in order to profit from the subsequent sale. The manipulative intent—buying, promoting to increase prices, then selling—is what makes this a violation, not merely the failure to disclose or the anonymity of the posts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A addresses disclosure but even with full disclosure, the manipulative intent would still be a violation
- Option B focuses on anonymity, but the same conduct under a real name would still be manipulation
