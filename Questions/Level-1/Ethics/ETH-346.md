# Question

A trader executes small trades throughout the day to create an appearance of active trading. Under Standard II(B):

## Options

- **A**: Small trades cannot constitute manipulation
- **B**: Creating artificial appearance of volume may be manipulative
- **C**: Only large trades can manipulate markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard II(B) prohibits practices intended to mislead market participants, including artificially inflating trading volume through unnecessary transactions. Trade size doesn't determine whether conduct is manipulative—intent does.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly ties manipulation to trade size
- Option C underestimates how smaller trades can deceive
