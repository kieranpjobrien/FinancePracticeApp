# Question

An analyst serves on the board of directors of a company she covers in her research. To comply with conflict of interest standards, she should:

## Options

- **A**: Resign from the board to eliminate the conflict
- **B**: Disclose the directorship prominently in all research reports on the company
- **C**: Only write positive research to avoid accusations of self-dealing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Board membership creates a significant conflict of interest that must be disclosed to readers of the analyst's research. Disclosure allows clients to evaluate whether the relationship might bias the analysis. While resignation could eliminate the conflict, disclosure is the minimum requirement if the analyst continues to cover the company.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be prudent but is not strictly required if proper disclosure is made
- Option C compounds the problem by allowing the conflict to actually bias the research
