# Question

Information is considered "material" for purposes of trading restrictions when it:

## Options

- **A**: Comes from a company insider such as an executive or board member
- **B**: Would likely affect the price of a security or influence an investor's decision
- **C**: Has been discussed in private meetings between analysts and management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Materiality is determined by whether the information would likely affect a security's price or would be considered important by a reasonable investor in making an investment decision. The source of the information is irrelevant to determining materiality—what matters is the potential impact on price or investment decisions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses the source of information with its materiality; insider information may or may not be material
- Option C describes the setting where information is received, not its materiality
