# Question

A code of ethics typically requires members to maintain and improve their professional competence. This requirement most likely reflects which underlying principle?

## Options

- **A**: Clients deserve advisors who stay current with industry developments and best practices
- **B**: Competence requirements help reduce competition in the profession
- **C**: Regulatory bodies require annual continuing education hours

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The competence requirement in professional codes of ethics reflects the principle that clients deserve to work with advisors who maintain current knowledge and skills. As financial markets, products, and regulations evolve, professionals must continuously develop their expertise to serve clients effectively and provide sound advice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B incorrectly suggests an anticompetitive motive; competence requirements actually raise professional standards for client benefit
- Option C confuses the ethical principle with regulatory compliance; while regulations may mandate CE, the ethical principle is about client service
