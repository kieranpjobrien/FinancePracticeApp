# Question

Under the prudent investor rule, the appropriateness of an investment should be evaluated:

## Options

- **A**: Based solely on the individual investment's risk characteristics
- **B**: In the context of the client's total portfolio and investment objectives
- **C**: By comparing its historical returns to a benchmark index

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The modern prudent investor rule evaluates investments in the context of the entire portfolio rather than in isolation. An investment that might appear risky on its own could be appropriate if it provides diversification benefits within the client's overall portfolio. The focus is on whether the investment serves the client's objectives given their complete financial situation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reflects the outdated "prudent man" rule that evaluated investments individually
- Option C focuses on historical performance rather than suitability for the client's situation
