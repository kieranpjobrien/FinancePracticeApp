# Question

When allocating shares in an oversubscribed IPO, Standard VI(B) prohibits:

## Options

- **A**: Allocating any shares to employee accounts
- **B**: Allocating shares to personal or proprietary accounts ahead of clients
- **C**: Using any allocation methodology

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(B) establishes that client interests take priority. In an oversubscribed IPO, allocating shares to personal or proprietary accounts before satisfying client interest violates transaction priority requirements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; employees can participate after client needs are met
- Option C misunderstands the Standard; a fair methodology is required, not prohibited
