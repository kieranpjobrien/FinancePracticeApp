# Question

A CFA member files for personal bankruptcy after accumulating excessive personal debt. This:

## Options

- **A**: Automatically violates Standard I(D)
- **B**: Does not necessarily violate the Standard unless involving fraud or dishonesty
- **C**: Requires immediate surrender of the CFA charter

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Personal bankruptcy alone does not violate Standard I(D). The Standard focuses on conduct reflecting adversely on professional integrity, such as fraud or dishonesty. Financial misfortune without misconduct does not trigger a violation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A equates financial difficulty with professional misconduct
- Option C imposes an automatic sanction not required by the Standard
