# Question

The asset substitution problem in agency theory refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Bondholders replacing management
- **B**: Shareholders incentive to invest in riskier projects after debt is issued
- **C**: Managers substituting dividends for salaries

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset substitution: after issuing debt at rates reflecting current risk, shareholders may prefer riskier projects. Upside goes to equity; downside shared with bondholders. This wealth transfer harms bondholders. Protective covenants and monitoring help mitigate this agency problem between shareholders and bondholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misidentifies the parties involved
- Option C describes a different type of agency issue
