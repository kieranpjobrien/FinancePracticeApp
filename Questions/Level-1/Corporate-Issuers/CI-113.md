# Question

Miller's model with personal taxes suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Personal taxes on interest income reduce the advantage of debt
- **B**: Personal taxes always increase the value of debt financing
- **C**: Corporate and personal taxes have no interaction

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Miller's model: value of leverage depends on corporate tax rate, personal tax on equity, and personal tax on interest income. If personal tax on interest is high enough, it can offset or eliminate the corporate tax advantage of debt. The net benefit depends on all three rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B ignores the offsetting effect of personal taxes on interest
- Option C ignores the interaction between tax systems
