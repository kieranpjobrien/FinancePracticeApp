# Question

According to signaling theory, a company's choice to issue equity rather than debt may signal:

## Options

- **A**: Management believes the stock is overvalued
- **B**: Management believes the stock is undervalued
- **C**: The company has no debt capacity

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Signaling theory: managers have inside information about firm value. If they believe shares are overvalued, they prefer equity issuance (selling expensive shares). If undervalued, they prefer debt (avoid diluting cheap shares). Equity issuance often viewed negatively by markets for this reason.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is opposite—undervalued stock leads to debt preference
- Option C may be true but isn't the primary signaling interpretation
