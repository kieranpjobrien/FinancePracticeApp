# Question

A lockbox system improves cash management by:

## Options

- **A**: Increasing the time to deposit customer payments
- **B**: Reducing collection float through geographically dispersed collection points
- **C**: Delaying payments to suppliers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lockboxes: banks in strategic locations collect and process payments, reducing mail and processing float. Customers send payments to nearby lockbox; bank deposits immediately. This accelerates cash availability. Benefits depend on payment volumes and float reduction.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite effect
- Option C describes payables management, not collections
