# Question

A project that is negatively correlated with a company's existing operations would:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the company's overall risk
- **B**: Potentially reduce the company's overall risk through diversification
- **C**: Have no effect on corporate risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corporate risk considers how a project affects total firm risk. A project negatively correlated with existing operations provides diversification benefit—when one area underperforms, the other may perform well. This stabilizes overall company cash flows and may reduce cost of capital.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—negative correlation reduces total risk
- Option C ignores correlation effects on portfolio risk
