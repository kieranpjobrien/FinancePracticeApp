# Question

A committed line of credit differs from an uncommitted line in that:

## Options

- **A**: It requires no fees or costs
- **B**: The bank is contractually obligated to lend up to the limit
- **C**: It can only be used for capital expenditures

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Committed lines are binding agreements—banks must lend if conditions are met. They typically require commitment fees on unused portions. Uncommitted lines are informal; banks can refuse to lend. Committed lines provide financing certainty but at higher cost.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores commitment fees required for committed lines
- Option C places no such restriction on credit line use
