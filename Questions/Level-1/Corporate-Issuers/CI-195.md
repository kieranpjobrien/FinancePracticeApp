# Question

Stakeholder theory differs from shareholder theory in that stakeholder theory:

## Options

- **A**: Focuses exclusively on maximizing shareholder wealth
- **B**: Considers the interests of all parties affected by corporate decisions
- **C**: Ignores financial performance metrics

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stakeholder theory: corporations should balance interests of all stakeholders—shareholders, employees, customers, suppliers, communities, environment. Contrasts with shareholder primacy (maximize shareholder wealth). Proponents argue long-term value creation requires considering all stakeholders. Critics note potential conflicts and accountability issues.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes shareholder theory
- Option C is too extreme—financial performance still matters
