# Question

Dividends can reduce agency costs by:

## Options

- **A**: Increasing management's control over corporate resources
- **B**: Forcing companies to seek external financing, increasing monitoring
- **C**: Eliminating the need for governance oversight

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividends reduce free cash flow available for wasteful spending (empire building, pet projects). When companies return cash, they must access capital markets for new investments, subjecting them to external scrutiny. This discipline aligns management with shareholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the agency problem, not the solution
- Option C overstates dividends' role—governance remains important
