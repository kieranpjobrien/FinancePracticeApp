# Question

An activist investor strategy typically involves:

## Options

- **A**: Passive long-term holding without engagement
- **B**: Acquiring a significant stake and pushing for changes to unlock value
- **C**: Short-selling to profit from declining prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Activist investors: acquire meaningful ownership positions, then advocate for changes—strategic shifts, cost cutting, management changes, spin-offs, dividends, or buybacks. Use various tactics: private engagement, public campaigns, proxy contests, board nominations. Aim to close gap between current price and perceived intrinsic value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes passive investing
- Option C describes short-seller strategy, not activism
