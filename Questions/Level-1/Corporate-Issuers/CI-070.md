# Question

A company has earnings per share of $4 and pays dividends of $1.20 per share. The dividend payout ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 30%
- **B**: 70%
- **C**: 333%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Dividend payout ratio = Dividends per share / Earnings per share = $1.20 / $4.00 = 30%. The retention ratio is 70% (1 - 30%). Higher payout means less retained for reinvestment; lower payout means more retained for growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is the retention ratio
- Option C reverses the ratio
