# Question

In a Dutch auction share repurchase, the company:

## Options

- **A**: Buys shares at a fixed price from all willing sellers
- **B**: Solicits offers from shareholders and selects the lowest price that allows desired share acquisition
- **C**: Purchases shares in the open market over an extended period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dutch auction: shareholders specify quantities and minimum prices they'll accept. Company determines lowest price that acquires target shares, paying that price to all successful tenders. More price discovery than fixed-price tender; less market impact than open market purchases.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a fixed-price tender offer
- Option C describes open market repurchases
