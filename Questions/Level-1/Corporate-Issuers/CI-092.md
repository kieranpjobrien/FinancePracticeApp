# Question

In a Dutch auction share repurchase:

## Options

- **A**: The company sets a fixed price for all shares
- **B**: Shareholders specify prices at which they're willing to sell, and a clearing price is determined
- **C**: Only institutional investors can participate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dutch auctions: company specifies a price range; shareholders tender shares indicating their minimum acceptable price. Company finds the lowest price to acquire the desired number (clearing price), paying that price to all tendering at or below it. This discovers the market-clearing price efficiently.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes fixed-price tender offers
- Option C—all shareholders can typically participate
