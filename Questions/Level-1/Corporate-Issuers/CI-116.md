# Question

The ex-dividend date is significant because:

## Options

- **A**: It's when dividends are paid to shareholders
- **B**: Buyers after this date do not receive the upcoming dividend
- **C**: It's when the board declares the dividend

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ex-dividend date: the first day shares trade without the dividend. Buyers on or after this date don't receive the pending dividend; sellers do. Stock prices typically drop by approximately the dividend amount on the ex-date. Declaration date is when announced; payment date is when paid.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the payment date
- Option C describes the declaration date
