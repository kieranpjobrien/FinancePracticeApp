# Question

A company has EBIT of $500,000 and interest expense of $100,000. The degree of financial leverage (DFL) is:

## Options

- **A**: 1.0
- **B**: 1.25
- **C**: 5.0

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DFL = EBIT / (EBIT - Interest) = $500,000 / ($500,000 - $100,000) = $500,000 / $400,000 = 1.25. This means a 1% change in EBIT produces a 1.25% change in earnings per share. Higher interest expense increases DFL.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would require zero interest expense
- Option C divides EBIT by interest
