# Question

A company's weighted average cost of capital (WACC) is most accurately described as the:

## Options

- **A**: Cost of the company's most recent financing
- **B**: Rate the company must earn on its investments to satisfy all capital providers
- **C**: Cost of equity multiplied by the proportion of equity in the capital structure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

WACC represents the minimum rate of return a company must earn on its investments to satisfy all capital providers (both debt holders and equity holders) and maintain the firm's value. It is calculated as the weighted average of the after-tax cost of debt and the cost of equity.

WACC = (E/V × Re) + (D/V × Rd × (1-T))

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the marginal cost of the latest financing, not the weighted average
- Option C only describes part of the WACC formula (the equity component) and ignores the debt component
