# Question

Flotation costs should be treated by:

## Options

- **A**: Adding them to the cost of capital
- **B**: Including them as part of the project's initial investment outlay
- **C**: Ignoring them since they are tax-deductible

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Flotation costs (underwriting fees, legal costs for issuing securities) should be treated as part of initial project cost, not by adjusting the discount rate. Adding to cost of capital creates errors if projects have different lives. The correct approach increases cash outflow at time zero.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A creates compounding errors over project life
- Option C—while sometimes deductible, they're still a real cost
