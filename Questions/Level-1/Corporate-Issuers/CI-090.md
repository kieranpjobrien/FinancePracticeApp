# Question

A special (extra) dividend is typically:

## Options

- **A**: A permanent increase to the regular dividend
- **B**: A one-time payment not expected to recur
- **C**: Smaller than the regular dividend

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Special dividends are one-time distributions—often from asset sales, excess cash accumulation, or windfall earnings. They signal the payment isn't expected to continue, avoiding implied commitment of regular dividend increases. Regular dividends signal ongoing capacity; specials signal temporary surplus.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses special with permanent increase
- Option C is often false—specials are frequently substantial
