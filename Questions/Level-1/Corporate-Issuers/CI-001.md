# Question

The primary goal of corporate governance is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize short-term profits regardless of risk
- **B**: Align the interests of managers with those of shareholders and other stakeholders
- **C**: Minimize the company's tax burden

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corporate governance establishes systems and processes to direct and control corporations. The primary goal is aligning management's interests with shareholders' (principals') interests, reducing agency conflicts. Good governance also considers other stakeholders—employees, creditors, customers, and society.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores long-term value creation and risk management
- Option C is a narrow financial objective, not the purpose of governance
