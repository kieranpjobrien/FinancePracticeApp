# Question

Under majority voting for directors:

## Options

- **A**: Directors are elected if they receive the most votes among candidates
- **B**: Directors must receive more than 50% of votes cast to be elected
- **C**: Only majority shareholders can vote

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Majority voting: directors must receive more votes "for" than "against" to be elected. This gives shareholders meaningful power—they can reject poorly performing directors. Plurality voting (Option A) only requires the most votes, even if fewer than 50%.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes plurality voting
- Option C describes voting eligibility, not the voting standard
