# Question

A controlling shareholder structure creates potential conflicts between:

## Options

- **A**: Only management and the board
- **B**: The controlling shareholder and minority shareholders
- **C**: Employees and customers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Controlling shareholder: owns enough shares to control decisions. Potential conflicts: related-party transactions at unfavorable terms, tunneling assets, pursuing private benefits at minority expense, resisting beneficial takeovers. Governance focus shifts from management oversight to protecting minority shareholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the shareholder-level conflict
- Option C describes stakeholder issues, not governance conflicts
