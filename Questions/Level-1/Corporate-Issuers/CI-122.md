# Question

Supermajority voting requirements:

## Options

- **A**: Make it easier to approve major transactions
- **B**: Require more than a simple majority for certain decisions
- **C**: Apply only to director elections

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Supermajority provisions require higher approval thresholds (e.g., 2/3 or 80%) for significant actions: mergers, charter amendments, removal of takeover defenses. This protects against hasty decisions but can entrench management and block value-enhancing transactions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the effect
- Option C understates the scope—applies to various major decisions
