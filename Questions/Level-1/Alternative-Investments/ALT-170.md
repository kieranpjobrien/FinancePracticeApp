# Question

In the US, hedge fund managers with significant assets typically must:

## Options

- **A**: Register as commercial banks
- **B**: Register with the SEC as investment advisers
- **C**: Avoid all regulatory oversight

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hedge fund regulation:

**SEC registration:**
- Required above $150M AUM
- Investment Advisers Act of 1940
- Form ADV filing
- Ongoing compliance

**What registration requires:**
- Disclosure of business practices
- Compliance programs
- Books and records
- SEC examinations

**Exemptions:**
- Smaller managers (under threshold)
- Certain foreign advisers
- Venture capital advisers
- Private fund adviser exemption (limited)

**Post-Dodd-Frank:**
- More oversight
- Systemic risk monitoring
- Form PF reporting
- Increased transparency

**Fund vs manager distinction:**
- Manager registers
- Fund may not register (3(c)(7))
- Fund exempt from '40 Act

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not banks, investment advisers
- Option C: Significant regulation exists
