# Question

Private equity investments are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Ownership stakes in publicly traded companies
- **B**: Equity investments in companies not listed on public exchanges
- **C**: Short-term trading of distressed securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Private equity definition:

**Core characteristic:**
- Equity ownership in private companies
- Not traded on public exchanges
- Direct ownership stakes
- Active involvement typical

**Types of private equity:**
- Leveraged buyouts (LBO)
- Venture capital
- Growth equity
- Distressed/turnaround
- Mezzanine financing

**Key features:**
- Illiquid investments
- Long holding periods (3-7 years)
- Active value creation
- Control or significant influence

**How it differs from public equity:**
- No daily market prices
- Limited liquidity
- More information asymmetry
- Higher return expectations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: "Private" means not publicly traded
- Option C: Distressed PE holds long-term, not trades
