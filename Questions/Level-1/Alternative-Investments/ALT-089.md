# Question

Short selling allows hedge funds to:

## Options

- **A**: Only buy additional shares of stocks they already own
- **B**: Profit from declining stock prices
- **C**: Avoid all investment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Short selling explained:

**How it works:**
1. Borrow shares from broker
2. Sell borrowed shares
3. Hope price declines
4. Buy back at lower price
5. Return shares, keep difference

**Example:**
- Borrow and sell at $100
- Price falls to $80
- Buy back at $80
- Profit: $20 (minus costs)

**Uses in hedge funds:**
- Profit from overvalued stocks
- Hedge long positions
- Reduce market exposure
- Alpha generation

**Risks:**
- Unlimited loss potential
- Short squeeze risk
- Borrowing costs
- Dividend payments
- Recall risk

**Costs:**
- Borrow fee
- Margin requirements
- Dividend obligations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Opposite of buying more
- Option C: Short selling has significant risks
