# Question

Minimum investment sizes for alternative investments are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Lower than for mutual funds to encourage broad access
- **B**: Higher than traditional investments, often $100,000 to $10 million or more
- **C**: Fixed at exactly $1,000 for all funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Alternative investment minimums:

**Typical minimums:**
- Hedge funds: $100K-$5M
- Private equity: $250K-$10M
- Venture capital: $250K-$5M
- Private real estate: $50K-$5M

**Why high minimums:**
- Accredited investor targeting
- Administrative efficiency
- LP base management
- Regulatory exemptions

**Access expanding:**
- Fund-of-funds lower minimums
- Interval funds
- Registered alternatives
- Digital platforms

**Implications:**
- Limits retail access
- Institutional focus
- Wealth concentration
- Due diligence expected

**Emerging options:**
- Feeder funds
- Aggregation platforms
- Tokenization
- Democratization trends

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Much higher than mutual funds
- Option C: Varies widely by fund
