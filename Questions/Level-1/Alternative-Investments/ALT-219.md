# Question

Emerging hedge fund managers are often defined as:

## Options

- **A**: The largest and most established funds in the industry
- **B**: Newer managers typically with smaller assets under management
- **C**: Funds exclusively investing in emerging markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emerging managers:

**Typical characteristics:**
- Track record < 3-5 years
- AUM < $500M-$1B
- Newer firm
- Building infrastructure

**Potential advantages:**
- More nimble/agile
- Higher alpha potential
- Capacity available
- Hungry to perform

**Potential risks:**
- Limited track record
- Operational concerns
- Key person risk
- Business viability

**Investor considerations:**
- Enhanced due diligence needed
- Operational DD critical
- Often better terms
- First-mover advantage

**Supporting programs:**
- Seeding arrangements
- Emerging manager allocators
- Incubators
- Diversity initiatives

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Opposite of established
- Option C: Manager stage, not geographic focus
