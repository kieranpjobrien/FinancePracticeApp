# Question

Demand or volume risk in toll road investments:

## Options

- **A**: Can be completely eliminated through contract terms
- **B**: Depends on traffic levels which are influenced by economic conditions and competition
- **C**: Is always borne entirely by the government

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Toll road demand risk:

**Traffic determinants:**
- Economic activity
- Employment levels
- Fuel prices
- Competing routes
- Development patterns

**Economic sensitivity:**
- Recession: Traffic declines
- Growth: Traffic increases
- Commercial traffic
- Leisure travel

**Risk allocation options:**
- User-pays: Full demand risk to private
- Availability: No demand risk
- Minimum revenue guarantees: Shared

**Traffic projections:**
- Critical for valuation
- Often overly optimistic
- Ramp-up period
- Historical accuracy varies

**Mitigation:**
- Long concessions smooth cycles
- Diversified networks
- Guaranteed minimums
- Toll escalation clauses

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Cannot eliminate in user-pays model
- Option C: Risk allocation varies by contract
