# Question

An offtake agreement in infrastructure investment:

## Options

- **A**: Covers the removal of waste products only
- **B**: Commits a buyer to purchase output from the project at specified terms
- **C**: Guarantees government ownership of the asset

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Offtake agreements:

**Definition:**
- Contract to purchase output
- From infrastructure project
- Pre-agreed terms
- Long-term commitment

**Common in:**
- Power plants (PPA)
- LNG facilities
- Mining projects
- Water treatment

**Key terms:**
- Volume commitments
- Pricing mechanism
- Duration
- Take-or-pay provisions

**Why important:**
- Revenue certainty
- Bankability
- Credit support
- Risk allocation

**Power Purchase Agreement (PPA):**
- Specific to electricity
- Fixed or indexed prices
- 15-25+ year terms
- Utility/corporate buyers

**Investor benefits:**
- Contracted cash flows
- Reduced demand risk
- Credit quality of offtaker
- Project financing enabled

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Offtake is output purchase
- Option C: Private ownership with contracts
