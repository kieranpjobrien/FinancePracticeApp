# Question

The spread between cap rates and Treasury yields in real estate:

## Options

- **A**: Is always constant over time
- **B**: Reflects the risk premium investors require for real estate versus risk-free assets
- **C**: Has no relationship to property valuations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cap rate spread:

**Definition:**
- Cap rate minus Treasury yield
- Risk premium measure
- Compensation for real estate risk
- Relative value indicator

**Historical average:**
- Typically 150-300 basis points
- Varies by property type
- Changes with conditions

**When spread is wide:**
- Real estate relatively cheap
- Risk aversion high
- More compensation for risk
- Buying opportunity potential

**When spread is narrow:**
- Real estate relatively expensive
- Risk appetite high
- Less risk premium
- Caution warranted

**Factors affecting spread:**
- Economic conditions
- Credit availability
- Real estate fundamentals
- Investor sentiment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Spread varies significantly
- Option C: Direct relationship to values
