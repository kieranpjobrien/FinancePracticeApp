# Question

Master Limited Partnerships (MLPs) in the energy sector typically:

## Options

- **A**: Are taxed as corporations with double taxation
- **B**: Pass through income to unitholders and avoid entity-level taxation
- **C**: Only invest in renewable energy assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

MLP structure:

**Tax treatment:**
- Pass-through entity
- No entity-level tax
- Income flows to unitholders
- K-1 tax reporting

**Typical assets:**
- Pipelines
- Storage facilities
- Processing plants
- Gathering systems

**Income characteristics:**
- High distribution yields
- Fee-based revenue
- Inflation escalators
- Stable cash flows

**Requirements:**
- 90%+ qualifying income
- Energy/natural resources focus
- Publicly traded
- Complex tax reporting

**Investor considerations:**
- High current income
- Tax-deferred portion
- UBTI for tax-exempt
- K-1 complexity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: MLPs avoid double taxation
- Option C: Primarily midstream oil/gas assets
