# Question

Political and regulatory risk in infrastructure investments refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Only risks in developing countries
- **B**: The risk that government actions may adversely affect investment returns
- **C**: Risk that can be completely eliminated through contracts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Political/regulatory risk:

**Types of risk:**
- Changes in regulatory framework
- Tariff/price controls
- Contract renegotiation
- Nationalization/expropriation
- Tax changes

**Examples:**
- Utility rate cuts
- Toll road concession changes
- Renewable subsidy reduction
- New environmental rules

**Risk factors:**
- Election cycles
- Policy changes
- Public opinion shifts
- Fiscal pressures

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Investment-grade jurisdictions
- Strong contract terms
- Political risk insurance
- Local partnerships
- Diversification

**Developed vs emerging markets:**
- Both face political risk
- Emerging: More extreme outcomes
- Developed: More subtle changes

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Exists in all countries
- Option C: Cannot be completely eliminated
