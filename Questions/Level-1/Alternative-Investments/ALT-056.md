# Question

The income approach to real estate valuation:

## Options

- **A**: Values property based on recent comparable sales only
- **B**: Estimates value by capitalizing expected income or discounting cash flows
- **C**: Calculates replacement cost of improvements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate valuation approaches:

**Income approach:**
- Direct capitalization: NOI / Cap Rate
- Discounted cash flow (DCF)
- Based on income-producing ability
- Most common for commercial

**Direct capitalization:**
- Single year NOI
- Divided by market cap rate
- Quick estimate
- Assumes stable income

**DCF method:**
- Project future cash flows
- Include terminal value
- Discount to present
- More detailed analysis

**Other approaches:**
- Sales comparison: Comparable sales
- Cost approach: Replacement cost
- Used for different situations

**When to use income approach:**
- Income-producing properties
- Investment analysis
- Most commercial real estate

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes sales comparison approach
- Option C: Describes cost approach
