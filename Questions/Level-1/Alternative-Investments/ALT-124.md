# Question

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) in real estate measures:

## Options

- **A**: The total amount of debt on a property
- **B**: Net operating income divided by debt service obligations
- **C**: The property's appreciation potential

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DSCR explained:

**Formula:**
DSCR = NOI / Debt Service

**Debt service includes:**
- Principal payments
- Interest payments
- Annual total obligation

**Interpretation:**
- DSCR > 1.0: NOI covers debt
- DSCR < 1.0: Shortfall exists
- Higher is safer

**Lender requirements:**
- Minimum 1.20-1.35x typical
- Higher for riskier properties
- Key underwriting metric

**Example:**
- NOI: $1,200,000
- Debt service: $1,000,000
- DSCR: 1.20x

**Why it matters:**
- Loan sizing constraint
- Default risk indicator
- Cash flow cushion
- Covenant in loan docs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: DSCR is ratio, not absolute amount
- Option C: Measures income coverage, not appreciation
