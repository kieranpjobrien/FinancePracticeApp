# Question

Subscription line facilities in private equity funds:

## Options

- **A**: Are used only for permanent leverage
- **B**: Provide short-term borrowing secured by LP capital commitments
- **C**: Replace capital calls entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Subscription lines:

**Definition:**
- Fund-level credit facility
- Secured by LP commitments
- Short-term borrowing
- Bridges capital calls

**Purpose:**
- Smooth capital calls
- Speed of execution
- Reduce call frequency
- Investment flexibility

**Mechanics:**
- Fund borrows from bank
- Makes investments
- Later calls LP capital
- Repays facility

**Impact on IRR:**
- Can boost IRR
- Delays capital call timing
- Same MOIC typically
- Timing differences

**LP considerations:**
- Less frequent calls
- IRR may be inflated
- True return unchanged
- Transparency important

**Controversy:**
- IRR enhancement debate
- Disclosure requirements
- Investor education
- Industry evolution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Temporary, not permanent leverage
- Option C: Supplements, doesn't replace calls
