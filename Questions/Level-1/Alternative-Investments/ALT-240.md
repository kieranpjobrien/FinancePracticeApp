# Question

Hedge funds typically report performance:

## Options

- **A**: Only at fund termination
- **B**: Monthly, with NAV provided to investors
- **C**: Every five years

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hedge fund performance reporting:

**Standard frequency:**
- Monthly NAV
- Monthly returns
- Quarterly letters
- Annual audited statements

**Monthly reports include:**
- Net return
- NAV per share
- AUM update
- Brief commentary

**Quarterly materials:**
- Detailed commentary
- Portfolio positioning
- Market outlook
- Performance attribution

**Annual requirements:**
- Audited financials
- K-1 tax documents
- Annual review

**Timing:**
- Monthly: 10-15 business days
- Quarterly: 30-45 days
- Annual: 90-120 days

**Comparison to PE:**
- More frequent than PE
- More liquid strategies
- Easier to value
- Investor expectations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Regular ongoing reporting
- Option C: Much more frequent
