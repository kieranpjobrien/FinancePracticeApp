# Question

A "unicorn" in venture capital terminology refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Any privately held company
- **B**: A private company valued at $1 billion or more
- **C**: A company that has completed an IPO

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unicorn definition:

**Criteria:**
- Private company
- Valuation of $1 billion+
- Pre-IPO stage
- Venture-backed typically

**Origin:**
- Term coined 2013
- Aileen Lee, Cowboy Ventures
- Rare like mythical unicorn
- (Now more common)

**Notable unicorns:**
- Uber (before IPO)
- Airbnb (before IPO)
- SpaceX
- Stripe

**Related terms:**
- Decacorn: $10 billion+
- Hectocorn: $100 billion+
- Centaur: $100 million+

**Investment implications:**
- Late-stage opportunities
- High valuations
- IPO path
- Significant capital raised

**Challenges:**
- Valuation uncertainty
- Mark-to-model
- Liquidity limited
- Down-round risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Must meet valuation threshold
- Option C: Public companies not unicorns
