# Question

Stranded asset risk in infrastructure investing refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Assets that are geographically isolated
- **B**: Assets that lose value due to technological or regulatory changes before end of useful life
- **C**: Assets that require additional maintenance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stranded asset risk:

**Definition:**
- Premature obsolescence
- Value impairment before expected
- Often climate/technology related
- Regulatory driver common

**Examples:**
- Coal power plants (climate policy)
- Fossil fuel reserves
- Gas pipelines (energy transition)
- Combustion engine infrastructure

**Causes:**
- Regulatory changes
- Climate commitments
- Technology disruption
- Market shifts

**Impact:**
- Asset write-downs
- Earlier-than-planned retirement
- Reduced returns
- Capital losses

**Mitigation:**
- ESG integration
- Scenario analysis
- Transition alignment
- Diversification

**Growing importance:**
- Net-zero commitments
- Climate regulations
- Energy transition acceleration

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Geographic location unrelated
- Option C: Maintenance is operational, not stranding
