# Question

Automated Market Makers (AMMs) in decentralized exchanges:

## Options

- **A**: Require traditional order books with bid and ask prices
- **B**: Use mathematical formulas to price assets based on liquidity pool ratios
- **C**: Only work with stablecoins

## Answer

B

## Explanation

AMM mechanics:

**How AMMs work:**
- Liquidity pools of token pairs
- Mathematical pricing formula
- x × y = k (constant product)
- No order book needed

**Key innovation:**
- Continuous liquidity
- Permissionless trading
- Algorithmic pricing
- 24/7 availability

**Liquidity providers:**
- Deposit token pairs
- Earn trading fees
- Face impermanent loss
- Passive market making

**Pricing example (x*y=k):**
- Pool: 100 ETH, 100,000 USDC
- k = 10,000,000
- Price adjusts with trades
- Larger trades = more slippage

**Impermanent loss:**
- Value differs from holding
- Occurs when prices diverge
- May become permanent
- Fee income may offset

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No order book, formula-based
- Option C: Work with any token pairs
