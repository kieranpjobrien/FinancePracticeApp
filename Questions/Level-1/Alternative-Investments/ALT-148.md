# Question

Strategy drift in hedge funds refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The fund performing exactly as described in marketing materials
- **B**: The fund deviating from its stated investment strategy
- **C**: The fund's returns drifting higher over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strategy drift:

**Definition:**
- Deviation from stated strategy
- May be gradual or sudden
- Changes risk profile
- Investor expectations violated

**Why it matters:**
- Investors allocated for specific strategy
- Risk management assumptions change
- Portfolio diversification affected
- Due diligence mismatch

**Examples:**
- Equity fund trading options
- Market neutral becoming directional
- Long-only becoming long/short
- Taking on more leverage

**Causes:**
- Seeking higher returns
- Chasing opportunities
- Manager skill in new areas
- Style creep

**Detection:**
- Returns correlation changes
- Factor exposure analysis
- Position monitoring
- Communication with manager

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Drift means deviation, not adherence
- Option C: Drift refers to strategy, not return trend
