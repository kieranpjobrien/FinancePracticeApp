# Question

A ground lease in real estate involves:

## Options

- **A**: Leasing both the land and building together
- **B**: Separating land ownership from building improvements, with building reverting at lease end
- **C**: Short-term rental agreements only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ground lease structure:

**Basic arrangement:**
- Landowner retains land
- Tenant builds improvements
- Long-term lease (50-99 years)
- Improvements revert at expiration

**Landowner benefits:**
- Retains land ownership
- Receives ground rent
- Gets improvements at end
- Often inflation-linked rent

**Tenant benefits:**
- Lower initial investment
- Tax benefits (rent deductible)
- Control during term
- Financing available

**Common situations:**
- Prime urban locations
- Institutional landowners
- Church/university land
- Ground lease REITs

**Financing considerations:**
- Leasehold financing available
- Requires lender consent
- Remaining term matters
- Reversion value consideration

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Specifically separates land and building
- Option C: Very long-term agreements
