# Question

The convenience yield in commodity markets represents:

## Options

- **A**: The interest earned on commodity investments
- **B**: The benefit of holding physical inventory rather than futures contracts
- **C**: The storage cost of commodities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convenience yield:

**Definition:**
- Benefit of physical ownership
- Non-monetary value
- Ability to use/sell immediately
- Production continuity

**Why it exists:**
- Avoid stockouts
- Production needs
- Immediate availability
- Supply security

**When high:**
- Tight supply conditions
- Strong near-term demand
- Inventory shortages
- Backwardation driver

**When low:**
- Abundant supply
- High inventories
- Weak demand
- Contango environment

**Relationship to futures curve:**
- High convenience yield → backwardation
- Low convenience yield → contango
- Part of cost-of-carry model

**Examples:**
- Oil refinery needs crude
- Manufacturer needs metals
- Agricultural processor needs grain

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not interest, but holding benefit
- Option C: Convenience yield offsets storage cost
