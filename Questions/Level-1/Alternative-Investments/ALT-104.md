# Question

Stablecoins are designed to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize volatility for trading profits
- **B**: Maintain a stable value, typically pegged to a fiat currency
- **C**: Replace all traditional cryptocurrencies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stablecoins explained:

**Purpose:**
- Price stability
- Usually pegged to $1 USD
- Bridge between crypto and fiat
- Reduce volatility

**Types:**
- Fiat-backed (USDC, USDT)
- Crypto-collateralized (DAI)
- Algorithmic (various)

**Fiat-backed:**
- Reserves held in dollars
- 1:1 backing
- Centralized issuers
- Most common type

**Use cases:**
- Trading pairs
- DeFi collateral
- Payments
- Remittances

**Risks:**
- Reserve transparency
- Regulatory scrutiny
- De-pegging events
- Counterparty risk

**Major stablecoins:**
- Tether (USDT)
- USD Coin (USDC)
- DAI

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Designed for stability, not volatility
- Option C: Complement, not replace other cryptos
