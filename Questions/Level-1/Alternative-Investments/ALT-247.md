# Question

When a hedge fund "soft closes":

## Options

- **A**: The fund immediately liquidates all positions
- **B**: New investor subscriptions are limited while existing investors may add capital
- **C**: Performance fees are eliminated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Soft close explained:

**What happens:**
- No new investors accepted
- Existing investors may add
- Capacity management
- Performance protection

**Why soft close:**
- Strategy capacity limits
- Protect existing investors
- Market impact concerns
- Alpha preservation

**Hard close comparison:**
- Hard close: No new capital at all
- Soft close: Limited additions
- Hard is more restrictive

**Manager considerations:**
- Fee revenue vs performance
- Investor relationships
- Long-term reputation
- Strategy integrity

**Investor implications:**
- Existing relationship value
- Access advantage
- May still have limits
- Good sign of discipline

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Continue operating, not liquidating
- Option C: Fee structure unchanged
