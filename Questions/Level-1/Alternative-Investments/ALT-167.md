# Question

Tenant improvement (TI) allowances in commercial real estate:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the landlord's cash flow immediately
- **B**: Are upfront costs to customize space for tenants, often amortized through rent
- **C**: Are paid by tenants before lease signing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tenant improvements:

**Definition:**
- Landlord contribution
- Customize space for tenant
- Build-out costs
- Upfront investment

**Typical amounts:**
- Office: $30-100+ per sq ft
- Retail: Varies widely
- Industrial: Lower usually
- Market-dependent

**How it works:**
- Landlord provides TI allowance
- Tenant uses for improvements
- Landlord recoups through rent
- Amortized over lease term

**Negotiation factors:**
- Tenant credit quality
- Lease term length
- Market conditions
- Space condition

**Accounting treatment:**
- Capitalize as asset
- Depreciate over lease term
- Affects underwriting

**Impact on returns:**
- Reduces initial cash flow
- Higher rent compensates
- Leasing cost consideration

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Upfront cost, not immediate income
- Option C: Landlord typically pays
