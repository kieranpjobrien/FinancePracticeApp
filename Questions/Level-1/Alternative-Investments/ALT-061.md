# Question

Infrastructure investments typically include:

## Options

- **A**: Only technology and software companies
- **B**: Essential physical assets like utilities, transportation, and communication networks
- **C**: Primarily retail and hospitality properties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Infrastructure defined:

**Core infrastructure sectors:**
- Utilities: Electric, gas, water
- Transportation: Toll roads, airports, ports
- Communications: Towers, fiber, data centers
- Social: Hospitals, schools, prisons

**Key characteristics:**
- Essential services
- Long-lived assets (30-50+ years)
- High barriers to entry
- Often regulated or contracted

**Economic infrastructure:**
- Enables economic activity
- Transportation networks
- Energy distribution
- Communications

**Social infrastructure:**
- Serves community needs
- Healthcare facilities
- Educational institutions
- Government buildings

**Investment attributes:**
- Stable cash flows
- Inflation linkage
- Low correlation with stocks
- Long duration

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Tech companies aren't infrastructure
- Option C: Retail/hospitality is real estate
