# Question

Side letters in alternative investments:

## Options

- **A**: Are available to all investors automatically
- **B**: Provide modified terms to specific investors, often larger LPs
- **C**: Replace the main fund documents entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Side letter provisions:

**Definition:**
- Separate agreements
- Between fund and specific LP
- Modified terms from LPA
- Negotiated individually

**Common provisions:**
- Reduced fees
- Capacity rights
- Co-investment priority
- Enhanced reporting
- MFN (most favored nation)

**Who gets side letters:**
- Largest LPs typically
- Early closers sometimes
- Strategic investors
- Anchor investors

**MFN clause:**
- Access to other side letter terms
- "Most favored nation" treatment
- Important for medium LPs
- Levels playing field

**Transparency:**
- ILPA recommends disclosure
- Fund should share provisions
- Fair treatment concerns
- Industry evolution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Negotiated for specific investors
- Option C: Supplements, not replaces LPA
