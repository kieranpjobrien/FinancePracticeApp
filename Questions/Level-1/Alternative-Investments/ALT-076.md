# Question

Timber investments have a unique return characteristic because:

## Options

- **A**: Trees stop growing once planted
- **B**: Biological growth adds value regardless of market conditions
- **C**: Timber prices are fixed by government regulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Timber return components:

**Biological growth:**
- Trees grow 2-8% annually by volume
- Physical asset appreciation
- Independent of markets
- "Natural factory"

**Price appreciation:**
- Timber prices fluctuate
- Supply/demand dynamics
- Construction activity
- Export demand

**Harvest flexibility:**
- Delay harvest if prices low
- Trees continue growing
- "Timber on the stump"
- Option value in timing

**Total return sources:**
1. Biological growth
2. Timber price changes
3. Land appreciation
4. Carbon credits (emerging)

**Investment characteristics:**
- Low correlation with stocks
- Inflation protection
- Long investment horizon
- Sustainable/ESG benefits

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Trees continue growing for decades
- Option C: Timber is a market-priced commodity
