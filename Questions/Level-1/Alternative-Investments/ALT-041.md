# Question

A secondary buyout (SBO) occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A company is sold to its management team
- **B**: One private equity firm sells a portfolio company to another PE firm
- **C**: Investors sell their LP interests in the secondary market

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Secondary buyout explained:

**Definition:**
- PE firm sells to another PE firm
- Portfolio company changes PE sponsors
- New LBO transaction structure

**Why SBOs occur:**
- Fund life ending, need exit
- Different value creation approach
- Larger firm can scale further
- Market conditions favor PE buyers

**Considerations:**
- Value creation potential remains?
- Entry multiple for buyer
- Additional leverage capacity
- New operational improvements

**Concerns:**
- "Passing the parcel"
- Diminishing returns each round
- Fee layering
- Already "picked over"

**For selling firm:**
- Realized exit
- Return of capital
- May not maximize value

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is management buyout (MBO)
- Option C: This is secondary market LP sale
