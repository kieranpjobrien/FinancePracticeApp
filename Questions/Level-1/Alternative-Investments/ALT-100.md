# Question

Credit-focused hedge fund strategies include:

## Options

- **A**: Only investing in government bonds
- **B**: Long/short credit, distressed debt, and structured credit
- **C**: Exclusively equity investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit hedge fund strategies:

**Long/short credit:**
- Long undervalued bonds
- Short overvalued or at-risk credits
- CDS for hedging/expression
- Relative value focus

**Distressed debt:**
- Buy debt of troubled companies
- Deep discounts
- Turnaround/restructuring
- May convert to equity

**Structured credit:**
- RMBS, CMBS, ABS
- CLO tranches
- Complexity premium
- Relative value opportunities

**Return drivers:**
- Credit selection
- Spread movements
- Default/recovery outcomes
- Restructuring profits

**Risk factors:**
- Default risk
- Spread widening
- Liquidity risk
- Recovery uncertainty

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Corporate/credit focus, not government
- Option C: Fixed income/credit strategies
