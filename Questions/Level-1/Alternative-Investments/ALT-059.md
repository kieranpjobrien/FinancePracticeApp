# Question

Ground-up real estate development carries higher risk than acquiring existing properties primarily because:

## Options

- **A**: Development projects have lower return potential
- **B**: There is no income during construction and significant execution risk
- **C**: Existing properties never need capital improvements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Development risk factors:

**No income during construction:**
- Negative cash flow period
- Interest carry costs
- No offsetting revenue
- Must fund from equity/debt

**Execution risks:**
- Construction delays
- Cost overruns
- Design/permitting issues
- Contractor problems

**Market timing risk:**
- Long development timeline
- Market may change
- Leasing conditions uncertain
- Exit environment unknown

**Development timeline:**
- Entitlement: 6-24 months
- Construction: 12-36 months
- Lease-up: 6-24 months
- Total: 2-6+ years

**Compensation:**
- Higher target returns
- Development profit margin
- Typically 15-25%+ returns

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Development has higher returns for higher risk
- Option C: All properties need capital improvements
