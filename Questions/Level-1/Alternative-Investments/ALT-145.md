# Question

Depreciation in real estate provides investors with:

## Options

- **A**: An actual decline in property value
- **B**: A non-cash deduction that reduces taxable income
- **C**: Additional rental income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate depreciation:

**Tax treatment:**
- Non-cash expense deduction
- Reduces taxable income
- Paper loss, not cash loss
- Shields income from tax

**Depreciation schedules:**
- Residential: 27.5 years
- Commercial: 39 years
- Straight-line method
- Land not depreciable

**Example:**
- Building value: $1M
- Annual depreciation: ~$25,600 (39 years)
- Reduces taxable income
- Tax savings = deduction × tax rate

**Benefits:**
- Tax-deferred cash flow
- Higher after-tax returns
- Accelerated depreciation options

**Recapture:**
- Upon sale, depreciation recaptured
- Taxed at 25% (US)
- Deferred, not eliminated

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Tax concept, not actual value decline
- Option C: Reduces taxes, doesn't increase rent
