# Question

Distributions to Paid-In (DPI) measures:

## Options

- **A**: The total value including unrealized investments
- **B**: The cash returned to investors relative to capital invested
- **C**: The fund's net asset value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DPI multiple explained:

**Formula:**
DPI = Cumulative Distributions / Paid-In Capital

**What it shows:**
- Cash actually returned
- Realized returns only
- No valuation subjectivity
- "Cash-on-cash" return

**Interpretation:**
- DPI < 1.0: Haven't returned all capital
- DPI = 1.0: Capital returned
- DPI > 1.0: Profit distributions

**Evolution over fund life:**
- Early years: DPI near 0
- Middle years: DPI increases
- Mature fund: DPI approaches TVPI

**Why investors focus on DPI:**
- Most reliable measure
- No valuation uncertainty
- Actual cash received
- "You can't eat IRR"

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes TVPI
- Option C: NAV is remaining value, not distributions
