# Question

A private equity fund charges "2 and 20." This fee structure most likely means:

## Options

- **A**: 2% of committed capital and 20% of total returns
- **B**: 2% annual management fee on assets and 20% of profits above a hurdle rate
- **C**: 20% management fee and 2% performance fee

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The "2 and 20" fee structure is standard in private equity and hedge funds:

- **2%**: Annual management fee, typically calculated on committed capital or net asset value
- **20%**: Performance fee (carried interest), typically calculated on profits above a hurdle rate (often 8%)

The hurdle rate ensures the GP only receives carried interest after delivering minimum returns to LPs. This aligns GP and LP interests.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't mention the hurdle rate, which is a key feature
- Option C reverses the percentages entirely
