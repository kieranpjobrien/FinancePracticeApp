# Question

Agricultural commodity prices often exhibit seasonality due to:

## Options

- **A**: Random market speculation only
- **B**: Harvest cycles, planting seasons, and weather patterns
- **C**: Constant year-round demand and supply

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Agricultural seasonality:

**Supply-side factors:**
- Harvest timing
- Planting decisions
- Growing seasons
- Weather events

**Seasonal patterns:**
- Pre-harvest: Often higher prices
- Post-harvest: Supply abundance, lower prices
- Storage over winter
- New crop vs old crop

**Examples:**
- Corn/Soybeans: Fall harvest pressure
- Wheat: Winter and spring varieties
- Coffee: Harvest timing varies by region

**Demand factors:**
- Holiday consumption
- Livestock feeding patterns
- Export timing
- Processing schedules

**Trading implications:**
- Calendar spreads
- Seasonal strategies
- Historical patterns
- Weather premium

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Fundamental supply/demand, not just speculation
- Option C: Supply/demand vary by season
