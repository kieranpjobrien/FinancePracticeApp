# Question

Gold is often considered a "safe haven" investment because:

## Options

- **A**: Gold prices always increase during all market conditions
- **B**: It tends to hold value during market stress and currency devaluation
- **C**: It generates significant dividend income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gold as safe haven:

**Historical behavior:**
- Retains value during crises
- Currency devaluation hedge
- Geopolitical risk protection
- Store of value for millennia

**Why investors hold gold:**
- Portfolio insurance
- Currency diversification
- Inflation hedge (long-term)
- Negative correlation in stress

**Characteristics:**
- No cash flow or yield
- No counterparty risk
- Limited industrial uses
- Sentiment-driven prices

**When gold performs well:**
- Market stress/crashes
- High inflation
- Currency weakness
- Geopolitical uncertainty

**Limitations:**
- No income generation
- Volatile in normal times
- Opportunity cost
- Storage costs (physical)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Prices can decline in many conditions
- Option C: Gold produces no income
