# Question

The illiquidity premium in alternative investments compensates investors for:

## Options

- **A**: Higher management fees
- **B**: The inability to quickly convert investments to cash
- **C**: Lack of diversification benefits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Illiquidity premium explained:

**Definition:**
- Additional return for accepting illiquidity
- Compensation for lock-up periods
- Reward for limited exit options

**Why it exists:**
- Capital is tied up for years
- Cannot respond to market changes
- Opportunity cost of locked capital
- Uncertainty about exit timing

**Magnitude:**
- Typically 2-4% additional return
- Varies by asset class
- Higher for longer lock-ups

**Examples:**
- Private equity: 7-10 year fund life
- Real estate: Transaction takes months
- Infrastructure: Very long horizons

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Fees are separate from illiquidity premium
- Option C: Alternatives can provide diversification
