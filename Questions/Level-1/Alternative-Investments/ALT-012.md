# Question

Alternative investments are typically restricted to accredited or qualified investors because:

## Options

- **A**: These investors pay lower tax rates
- **B**: They are presumed to have the sophistication and resources to bear the risks
- **C**: Retail investors are not interested in alternative investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Investor qualification requirements:

**Accredited investor criteria (US):**
- Income: $200K+ individual, $300K+ joint
- Net worth: $1M+ excluding primary residence
- Institutional investors
- Certain professional credentials

**Rationale for restrictions:**
- Complex strategies require understanding
- Illiquidity requires financial resilience
- Higher losses possible
- Less regulatory protection

**Presumed sophistication:**
- Can evaluate risks independently
- Access to professional advisors
- Diversified wealth base
- Can afford total loss

**Regulatory framework:**
- SEC Regulation D exemptions
- Reduced disclosure requirements
- Private placement rules

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Tax treatment unrelated to qualification
- Option C: Many retail investors would invest if allowed
