# Question

A royalty interest in oil and gas investments:

## Options

- **A**: Requires the owner to pay for operating expenses
- **B**: Entitles the owner to a share of production revenue without operating costs
- **C**: Gives full control over drilling decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Royalty interests:

**Definition:**
- Share of production revenue
- No operating costs
- Passive income stream
- Percentage of gross

**Key features:**
- No capital calls for drilling
- No operating expense burden
- Revenue-based, not profit
- Lower risk than working interest

**Typical royalty rates:**
- 12.5% to 25% common
- Negotiated with mineral owner
- Varies by region/deal

**Comparison to working interest:**
- Working interest: Costs and revenues
- Royalty: Revenue only
- Working interest: Active decisions
- Royalty: Passive

**Investment thesis:**
- Commodity exposure
- No operating risk
- Predictable percentage
- Inflation hedge

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Working interest pays costs
- Option C: No operational control
