# Question

The "basis" in commodity markets refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The fundamental value of a commodity
- **B**: The difference between spot and futures prices
- **C**: The minimum price floor for commodities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity basis explained:

**Definition:**
Basis = Spot Price - Futures Price

**Positive basis (backwardation):**
- Spot > Futures
- Tight near-term supply
- Convenience yield high

**Negative basis (contango):**
- Spot < Futures
- Adequate supply
- Storage costs dominant

**Why basis matters:**
- Hedging effectiveness
- Roll yield implications
- Supply/demand signal
- Arbitrage relationships

**Basis risk:**
- Basis changes unpredictably
- Imperfect hedges
- Location differences
- Quality variations

**For investors:**
- Affects total return
- Roll yield component
- Storage economics
- Market structure insight

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Basis is price relationship, not value
- Option C: No minimum price floor concept
