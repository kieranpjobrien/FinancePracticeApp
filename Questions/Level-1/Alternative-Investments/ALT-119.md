# Question

Investing in oil and gas exploration (upstream) differs from pipelines (midstream) primarily in:

## Options

- **A**: Both having identical risk profiles
- **B**: Exploration having higher commodity price exposure and geological risk
- **C**: Pipelines having higher commodity price risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Upstream vs midstream:

**Upstream characteristics:**
- Exploration and production
- High commodity price exposure
- Geological/finding risk
- Reserve depletion
- Capital intensive drilling

**Midstream characteristics:**
- Transportation and storage
- Fee-based revenue
- Volume-dependent (less than price)
- Infrastructure assets
- More stable cash flows

**Risk comparison:**
- Upstream: High risk, high reward
- Midstream: Lower risk, stable returns
- Different volatility profiles
- Correlation with oil prices differs

**Upstream risks:**
- Dry holes
- Reserve estimates wrong
- Price collapse
- Environmental costs

**Midstream advantages:**
- Long-term contracts
- Inflation escalators
- Essential infrastructure
- Regulated assets

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Very different risk profiles
- Option C: Pipelines have lower price exposure
