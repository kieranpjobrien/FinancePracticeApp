# Question

Commodity futures differ from physical commodity ownership because futures:

## Options

- **A**: Require taking physical delivery of commodities
- **B**: Allow price exposure without storage and handling costs
- **C**: Have no connection to commodity prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures vs physical commodities:

**Futures advantages:**
- No storage costs
- No insurance needed
- No deterioration risk
- Easy to trade
- Leverage possible

**Physical ownership challenges:**
- Storage facilities required
- Insurance costs
- Quality degradation
- Transportation logistics
- Large capital needed

**Futures mechanics:**
- Contract for future delivery
- Typically closed before expiration
- Cash settlement common
- Roll to next contract

**Roll yield impact:**
- Contango: Negative roll yield
- Backwardation: Positive roll yield
- Affects total return

**When physical makes sense:**
- Strategic stockpiles
- Producer hedging
- Consumer hedging
- Very long-term holding

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Most futures closed before delivery
- Option C: Futures prices track physical closely
