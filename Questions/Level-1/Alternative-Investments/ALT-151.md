# Question

Quartile rankings in private equity fund performance:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee future fund performance
- **B**: Compare fund returns to peers within the same vintage year
- **C**: Are based only on management fees charged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quartile rankings:

**How they work:**
- Compare to same vintage peers
- Divide into four groups
- Top quartile: Top 25%
- Bottom quartile: Bottom 25%

**Why vintage matters:**
- Market conditions differ
- Entry valuations vary
- Exit environments differ
- Fair comparison requires similar timing

**Interpretation:**
- First quartile: Excellent relative performance
- Second quartile: Above median
- Third quartile: Below median
- Fourth quartile: Poor relative performance

**Importance:**
- Manager selection tool
- Track record evaluation
- LP due diligence
- Industry benchmarking

**Limitations:**
- Sample size in vintage
- Self-reported data
- Survivorship bias
- Vintage year definition varies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Past performance no guarantee
- Option C: Based on returns, not fees
