# Question

Bitcoin's "halving" event refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The price of Bitcoin being cut in half
- **B**: The reduction of new Bitcoin mining rewards by 50%
- **C**: The total supply of Bitcoin being reduced

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bitcoin halving explained:

**What happens:**
- Mining reward cut 50%
- Approximately every 4 years
- Built into Bitcoin protocol
- Programmatic scarcity

**Historical halvings:**
- 2012: 50 → 25 BTC
- 2016: 25 → 12.5 BTC
- 2020: 12.5 → 6.25 BTC
- 2024: 6.25 → 3.125 BTC

**Purpose:**
- Control inflation rate
- Approach 21M cap gradually
- Predictable monetary policy
- Scarcity mechanism

**Market implications:**
- Supply shock potential
- Historical price increases
- Miner economics change
- Market anticipation

**Why it matters:**
- Core to Bitcoin thesis
- Differentiates from fiat
- Supply-side event

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Mining reward, not price affected
- Option C: Existing supply unchanged, new issuance reduced
