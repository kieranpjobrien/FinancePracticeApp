# Question

In merger arbitrage, the "spread" represents:

## Options

- **A**: The difference between buy and sell prices in the market
- **B**: The difference between current stock price and the announced deal price
- **C**: The hedge fund's management fee

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Merger arbitrage spread:

**The spread:**
- Deal price minus current price
- Example: Deal at $50, trading at $47
- Spread = $3 (or 6.4%)

**Why spread exists:**
- Time value of money
- Deal completion uncertainty
- Regulatory approval risk
- Financing contingencies

**Profit opportunity:**
- Buy at $47, receive $50 at close
- Annualized if deal closes in 6 months
- Spreads widen with risk perception

**Risks:**
- Deal breaks: Major loss
- Timing delays: Lower returns
- Bid changes: Up or down
- Regulatory blocks

**Return characteristics:**
- Small gains when deals close
- Larger losses when deals fail
- Asymmetric payoff profile

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Market bid-ask is different
- Option C: Fees are separate from strategy
