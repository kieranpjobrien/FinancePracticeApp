# Question

Revenue cap regulation in utilities:

## Options

- **A**: Allows unlimited revenue regardless of performance
- **B**: Limits total revenues while incentivizing efficiency gains
- **C**: Only applies to competitive industries

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Revenue cap regulation:

**How it works:**
- Regulator sets maximum revenue
- Adjusted for inflation
- Efficiency factor (X-factor)
- Formula: Revenue cap × (1 + CPI - X)

**Incentive properties:**
- Cost savings kept by utility
- Efficiency rewarded
- Until next regulatory reset
- Profit from outperformance

**Comparison to cost-plus:**
- Cost-plus: All costs passed through
- Revenue cap: Incentive to reduce costs
- Different risk allocation
- UK model widely adopted

**Regulatory reset:**
- Periodic reviews (5 years typical)
- Rebasing of revenues
- Reset efficiency assumptions
- Renegotiation risk

**Investor implications:**
- Predictable revenues (mostly)
- Upside from efficiency
- Regulatory risk at reset
- Inflation protection

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Caps limit revenues
- Option C: Applied to regulated monopolies
