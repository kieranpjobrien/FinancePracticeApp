# Question

Carried interest in private equity represents:

## Options

- **A**: The interest rate on fund-level debt
- **B**: The GP's share of fund profits above a hurdle rate
- **C**: Management fees accumulated over the fund life

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Carried interest explained:

**Definition:**
- GP's performance-based compensation
- Share of profits (typically 20%)
- Earned after hurdle rate met
- Aligns GP and LP interests

**How it works:**
- LPs first receive hurdle return (e.g., 8%)
- Then often GP "catch-up" provision
- Then 80/20 split of remaining profits
- Significant wealth creation for GPs

**Calculation example:**
- $100M fund, 2.0x return = $200M
- Hurdle at 8% compound = $147M
- Profit above hurdle: $53M
- Carry (20%): $10.6M to GP

**Waterfall structures:**
- Deal-by-deal carry
- Whole-fund carry
- European vs American style

**Importance:**
- Primary GP incentive
- Can be millions per partner
- Aligns long-term interests

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Carry isn't interest on debt
- Option C: Management fees are separate
