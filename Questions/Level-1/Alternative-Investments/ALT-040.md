# Question

Which exit strategy typically provides the highest valuations for private equity investments?

## Options

- **A**: Dividend recapitalization
- **B**: Strategic sale to a corporate acquirer
- **C**: Liquidation of company assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Exit strategies compared:

**Strategic sale:**
- Sale to corporate buyer
- Synergy value captured
- Control premium paid
- Often highest valuations

**Why strategics pay more:**
- Revenue synergies
- Cost synergies
- Strategic value
- Competitive dynamics

**Other exits:**
- IPO: Public market valuation
- Secondary buyout: Sale to another PE firm
- Dividend recap: Partial exit via borrowing
- Liquidation: Last resort, lowest value

**Valuation hierarchy (typical):**
1. Strategic sale (highest)
2. IPO (market dependent)
3. Secondary buyout
4. Dividend recapitalization
5. Liquidation (lowest)

**Considerations:**
- Market conditions matter
- Company characteristics
- Buyer universe
- Timing flexibility

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Recap isn't true exit, often lower value
- Option C: Liquidation destroys value
