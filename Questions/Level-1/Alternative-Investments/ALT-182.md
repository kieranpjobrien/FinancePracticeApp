# Question

Early termination provisions in infrastructure concessions typically address:

## Options

- **A**: Only the private sector's ability to exit
- **B**: Compensation for termination by either party and government default scenarios
- **C**: Automatic termination without compensation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Termination provisions:

**Termination triggers:**
- Government default
- Concessionaire default
- Force majeure
- Voluntary buyback
- Public interest termination

**Compensation varies by cause:**
- Concessionaire default: Reduced or no compensation
- Government default: Full compensation
- Force majeure: Shared treatment
- Public interest: Market value or more

**Key considerations:**
- Debt to be repaid
- Equity return expectations
- Sunk costs
- Future cash flows

**Lender protection:**
- Step-in rights
- Cure periods
- Assignment rights
- Direct agreements

**Investor perspective:**
- Downside protection
- Recovery mechanisms
- Dispute resolution
- Political risk mitigation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Both parties can trigger termination
- Option C: Compensation usually provided
