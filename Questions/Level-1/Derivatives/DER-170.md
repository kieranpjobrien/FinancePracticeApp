# Question

Reverse cash-and-carry arbitrage is executed when:

## Options

- **A**: The forward price is too high relative to the spot price
- **B**: The forward price is too low relative to the spot price
- **C**: Spot and forward prices are equal

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reverse cash-and-carry:

**When to execute:**
- Forward price < Theoretical price
- Forward is underpriced
- F < S(1 + r)^T for no-dividend stock

**Strategy:**
1. Short sell the underlying asset
2. Invest short sale proceeds at risk-free rate
3. Buy forward contract
4. Lock in profit

**At expiration:**
- Take delivery via forward
- Return stock to close short
- Keep interest earned minus forward price

**Profit:**
= S(1 + r) - F > 0

**Example:**
- S = $100, r = 5%, T = 1 year
- Fair forward = $105
- Actual forward = $102 (underpriced)
- Profit = $105 - $102 = $3

**Opposite of cash-and-carry:**
- Cash-carry: Forward overpriced
- Reverse: Forward underpriced

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is regular cash-and-carry
- Option C: Equal prices mean no arbitrage
