# Question

A collar strategy consists of:

## Options

- **A**: Long stock, long put, and short call
- **B**: Long stock, short put, and long call
- **C**: Short stock, long put, and long call

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Collar strategy:

**Components:**
- Long stock position
- Long put (protection)
- Short call (pays for put)

**Purpose:**
- Protect downside
- Finance protection by selling upside
- Range-bound payoff

**Cash flow:**
- Put premium paid
- Call premium received
- Often structured as zero-cost collar

**Payoff profile:**
- Below put strike: Protected (sell at put strike)
- Between strikes: Keep stock exposure
- Above call strike: Capped (sell at call strike)

**Example:**
- Own stock at $50
- Buy $45 put
- Sell $55 call
- Outcome: Profits between $45-$55

**Uses:**
- Corporate hedging
- Executive stock protection
- Risk reduction

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: This creates different exposure
- Option C: Collar requires long stock
