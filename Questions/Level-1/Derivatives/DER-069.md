# Question

An investor entered a 1-year forward to buy stock at $50. After 6 months, the stock is at $55 and the risk-free rate is 6%. The value of the long forward position is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $3.54
- **B**: $5.00
- **C**: $6.46

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Forward value after inception:

**Formula for long forward value:**
V_t = S_t - PV(F)

**Given:**
- Original forward price F = $50
- Current spot S_t = $55
- Time remaining = 0.5 years
- Risk-free rate = 6%

**Calculate PV of forward price:**
PV(F) = F / (1 + r)^T
PV(F) = $50 / (1.06)^0.5
PV(F) = $50 / 1.0296
PV(F) = $48.56

**Calculate forward value:**
V = S_t - PV(F)
V = $55 - $48.56
V = $6.44 ≈ $6.46

**Interpretation:**
- Long position has gained value
- Stock rose, forward locked in lower price
- Value = Benefit of buying at $50 vs. market

**Short position:**
- Value = -$6.46
- Obligated to sell at $50, stock worth $55

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Used wrong discounting
- Option B: Simple difference without PV adjustment
