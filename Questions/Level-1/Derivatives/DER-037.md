# Question

After inception, if interest rates rise, the value of a receive-fixed, pay-floating swap will most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Increase
- **B**: Decrease
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Swap value after rate changes:

**Receive-fixed position:**
- Receives fixed rate payments
- Pays floating rate payments
- Like being long a fixed-rate bond

**When rates rise:**
- Fixed payments become less valuable
- PV of fixed receipts declines
- Floating payments increase
- Net value decreases

**Think of it as bond equivalent:**
- Receive-fixed = Long fixed bond + Short floating bond
- Fixed bond loses value when rates rise
- Floating bond stays near par
- Net position loses value

**Pay-fixed (opposite):**
- Benefits from rising rates
- Fixed obligations become less valuable (good)
- Like being short a bond

**Key insight:**
- Duration of fixed leg matters
- Longer swaps more sensitive
- Value moves inversely with rates for receiver

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This applies to pay-fixed position
- Option C: Swap values change with rates
