# Question

A short futures position profits when:

## Options

- **A**: The futures price increases
- **B**: The futures price decreases
- **C**: The futures price remains unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Short futures profit:

**Short position:**
- Agree to SELL at futures price
- Benefit from price decreases
- "Sell high, buy back low"

**Profit calculation:**
Profit = Entry price - Exit price
Profit = F_initial - F_final

**Example:**
- Short at $100
- Price falls to $90
- Profit = $100 - $90 = $10

**If price rises:**
- Short at $100
- Price rises to $110
- Loss = $100 - $110 = -$10

**Who goes short:**
- Hedgers expecting to sell (farmers)
- Speculators expecting price decline
- Basis traders

**Opposite of long:**
- Long profits when prices rise
- Short profits when prices fall
- Zero-sum game

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Price increases hurt short positions
- Option C: Unchanged price means no profit/loss
