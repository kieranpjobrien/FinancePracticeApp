# Question

A stock trades at $100 and will pay a $2 dividend in 3 months. The risk-free rate is 6% annually. The 6-month forward price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $100.97
- **B**: $101.00
- **C**: $103.00

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Forward price with discrete dividend:

**Formula:**
F = (S - PV(D)) × (1 + r)^T

**Step 1: Present value of dividend**
PV(D) = $2 / (1.06)^0.25
PV(D) = $2 / 1.0147
PV(D) = $1.97

**Step 2: Adjusted spot price**
Adjusted S = $100 - $1.97 = $98.03

**Step 3: Forward price**
F = $98.03 × (1.06)^0.5
F = $98.03 × 1.0296
F = $100.93 ≈ $100.97

**Interpretation:**
- Dividend reduces forward price
- Must subtract PV of dividend from spot
- Then compound to forward date

**Why dividend matters:**
- Forward holder doesn't receive dividend
- Spot holder does
- Forward price adjusted downward

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Roughly correct but rounding errors
- Option C: Ignored dividend entirely
