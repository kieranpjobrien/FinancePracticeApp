# Question

The forward price of an asset is typically determined by:

## Options

- **A**: Supply and demand for the forward contract only
- **B**: The spot price plus the cost of carry
- **C**: The expected future spot price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward price determination:

**Basic formula:**
F = S × e^(rT) or F = S(1 + r)^T

**Cost of carry model:**
- Start with spot price
- Add financing cost (interest)
- Add storage costs (if applicable)
- Subtract benefits (dividends, convenience yield)

**Components:**
- S = Current spot price
- r = Risk-free rate
- T = Time to expiration
- Forward price = Spot + Net carry costs

**Why not expected future spot?**
- Arbitrage enforces relationship
- Can replicate forward with spot + financing
- No-arbitrage, not forecasting

**Example:**
- Spot = $100, Rate = 5%, T = 1 year
- Forward = $100 × 1.05 = $105

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Arbitrage links forward to spot
- Option C: Forward price ≠ expected future spot
