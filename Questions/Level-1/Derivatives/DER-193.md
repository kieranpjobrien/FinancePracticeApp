# Question

Unlike options, forward contracts involve:

## Options

- **A**: Rights for one party and obligations for the other
- **B**: Obligations for both parties
- **C**: Rights for both parties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward vs. option obligations:

**Forward contracts:**
- Long: Obligated to buy
- Short: Obligated to sell
- Both must perform
- No choice at expiration

**Options:**
- Buyer: Has right (can exercise or not)
- Seller: Has obligation (if exercised)
- Asymmetric structure

**Consequence for forwards:**
- Both parties locked in
- No upfront premium
- Both face potential losses

**Consequence for options:**
- Buyer pays for flexibility
- Premium is the cost
- Limited loss for buyer

**Forward example:**
- Agree to buy at $100
- Price goes to $80
- Must still buy at $100
- No choice to walk away

**Option example:**
- Buy call strike $100
- Price goes to $80
- Let option expire
- Only lose premium

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes options
- Option C: Rights come with optionality
