# Question

Delta hedging requires:

## Options

- **A**: A one-time hedge that remains fixed until expiration
- **B**: Continuous rebalancing as the underlying price changes
- **C**: Only hedging at-the-money options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Delta hedging dynamics:

**Why rebalancing needed:**
- Delta changes as stock moves (gamma)
- Hedge ratio becomes stale
- Must adjust to maintain hedge

**Example:**
- Delta = 0.5, own 100 calls
- Hedge: Short 50 shares
- Stock rises, delta → 0.6
- Need to short 10 more shares

**Gamma effect:**
- Gamma = Rate of delta change
- Higher gamma = More rebalancing
- ATM options have highest gamma

**Practical considerations:**
- Transaction costs
- Discrete rebalancing
- Hedging error
- Cannot be truly continuous

**Cost of hedging:**
- Frequent rebalancing expensive
- Trade-off: Accuracy vs. cost
- Rebalance at intervals or thresholds

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Static hedge doesn't work due to gamma
- Option C: All options can be delta hedged
