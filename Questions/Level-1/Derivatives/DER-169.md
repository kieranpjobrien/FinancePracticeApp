# Question

Derivatives on stock indexes are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Physically settled with delivery of all stocks in the index
- **B**: Cash settled based on the index value
- **C**: Converted to individual stock options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Index derivative settlement:

**Cash settlement:**
- Calculate gain/loss in cash
- Based on index level
- No physical stocks exchanged
- Most practical method

**Why cash settlement:**
- Impractical to deliver all stocks
- Index may have hundreds of stocks
- Fractional shares issues
- Transaction costs would be huge

**Settlement calculation:**
- Compare contract price to settlement index
- Multiply by contract multiplier
- Pay cash difference

**Example (S&P 500 futures):**
- Multiplier = $250
- Entry: 4,500
- Settlement: 4,520
- Cash = 20 × $250 = $5,000

**Single stock derivatives:**
- Usually allow physical settlement
- One stock to deliver
- Options often physically settled

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Physical delivery impractical for indexes
- Option C: Remain as index derivatives
