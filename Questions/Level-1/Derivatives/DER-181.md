# Question

The settlement date for a derivative is:

## Options

- **A**: The date when the contract is first traded
- **B**: The date when funds and/or assets are actually exchanged
- **C**: The last date to modify the contract terms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Settlement date:

**Definition:**
- When actual exchange occurs
- Transfer of funds/assets
- Fulfillment of obligations

**For different derivatives:**

*Futures:*
- Daily settlement (mark-to-market)
- Final settlement at expiration

*Options:*
- T+1 for equity options (usually)
- Funds and shares exchange

*Forwards:*
- Single settlement at maturity
- Exchange of asset and payment

*Swaps:*
- Periodic settlement dates
- Net payment exchanged

**vs. Trade date:**
- Trade date: Agreement made
- Settlement: Actual exchange
- May differ by days

**Settlement mechanics:**
- Clearinghouse facilitates
- Ensures both parties perform
- Manages timing

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is the trade date
- Option C: Terms fixed at trade, not modified later
