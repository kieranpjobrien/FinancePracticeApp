# Question

Liquidity risk in derivative markets refers to the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates will change unfavorably
- **B**: A position cannot be closed or adjusted quickly at a fair price
- **C**: The underlying asset will default

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Derivative liquidity risk:

**Definition:**
- Inability to trade at fair prices
- Wide bid-ask spreads
- Market impact costs
- Delayed execution

**Causes of illiquidity:**
- Customized OTC contracts
- Unusual terms or maturities
- Low trading volume
- Market stress conditions

**Consequences:**
- Forced to hold unwanted positions
- Sell at significant discount
- Unable to hedge effectively
- Mark-to-model vs. mark-to-market

**Liquid vs. illiquid contracts:**
- Liquid: Standard futures, at-the-money options
- Illiquid: Exotic options, long-dated OTC swaps

**Managing liquidity risk:**
- Use standardized contracts
- Trade liquid underlyings
- Maintain position limits
- Have backup liquidity sources

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is interest rate risk
- Option C: This is credit/default risk
