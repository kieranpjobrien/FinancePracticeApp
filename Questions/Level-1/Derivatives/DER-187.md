# Question

Derivatives markets may increase information asymmetry risk because:

## Options

- **A**: All derivatives are traded on transparent exchanges
- **B**: Complex products may be difficult for some parties to fully understand and value
- **C**: Derivatives always eliminate information differences

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information asymmetry in derivatives:

**Sources of asymmetry:**
- Complex product structures
- Valuation expertise differences
- Hidden assumptions
- Counterparty knowledge gaps

**Sophisticated vs. less sophisticated:**
- Dealers understand products well
- End users may not fully grasp risks
- Information advantage to sellers

**Product complexity:**
- Exotic options
- Structured products
- Embedded derivatives
- Multi-factor instruments

**Consequences:**
- Mispricing by one party
- Inadequate hedging
- Unexpected losses
- Disputes

**Mitigation:**
- Disclosure requirements
- Suitability rules
- Independent valuation
- Education and transparency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Many derivatives trade OTC with less transparency
- Option C: Derivatives can increase information gaps
