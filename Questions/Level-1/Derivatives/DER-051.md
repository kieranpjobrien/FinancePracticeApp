# Question

A contingent claim is a derivative in which the payoff:

## Options

- **A**: Is fixed and known at the start of the contract
- **B**: Depends on the outcome of an uncertain future event
- **C**: Is always zero regardless of market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contingent claims:

**Definition:**
- Payoff depends on future event
- Uncertainty about whether payoff occurs
- "Contingent" = conditional on outcome

**Options as contingent claims:**
- Call pays only if S > K
- Put pays only if S < K
- Payoff contingent on price level

**Contrast with forward commitments:**
- Forwards/futures: Both parties obligated
- Payoff occurs regardless of price
- Not contingent on specific outcome

**Examples of contingent claims:**
- Call and put options
- Credit default swaps
- Insurance contracts
- Caps and floors

**Key characteristic:**
- Asymmetric payoff profile
- Buyer has optionality
- May or may not pay off

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes fixed payments, not contingent
- Option C: Payoffs can be substantial when triggered
