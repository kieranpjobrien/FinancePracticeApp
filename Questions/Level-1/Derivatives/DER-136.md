# Question

A forward contract is described as a "zero-sum game" because:

## Options

- **A**: The contract has zero value at inception
- **B**: One party's gain equals the other party's loss
- **C**: No money is exchanged during the contract life

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Zero-sum nature of forwards:

**Definition:**
- Total gains + Total losses = 0
- Winner's profit = Loser's loss
- No net value creation

**At expiration:**
- Long profit = S_T - F
- Short profit = F - S_T
- Sum = 0

**Example:**
- Forward price = $100
- Spot at expiration = $110
- Long gains $10
- Short loses $10
- Net = $0

**Implication:**
- Derivatives don't create wealth
- They transfer risk and returns
- Redistribution between parties

**Contrast with positive-sum:**
- Stocks can gain for all holders
- Bonds pay interest to all
- Economy can grow overall

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: True but not why it's zero-sum
- Option C: Settlement involves money exchange
