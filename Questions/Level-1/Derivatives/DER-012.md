# Question

Initial margin for a futures position is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: A loan from the broker to leverage the position
- **B**: A performance bond or good faith deposit
- **C**: The maximum loss that can occur on the position

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures margin system:

**Initial margin:**
- Deposit required to open position
- Performance guarantee
- NOT a down payment
- Typically 3-12% of contract value

**Purpose:**
- Ensures ability to cover losses
- Protects clearinghouse
- Reduces counterparty risk
- Creates leveraged exposure

**Maintenance margin:**
- Minimum balance required
- Usually 70-80% of initial
- Triggers margin call if breached

**Margin call process:**
- Account falls below maintenance
- Must deposit to initial level
- Failure leads to liquidation

**Key distinction:**
- Not a loan (no interest)
- Your money held as collateral
- Returned when position closed

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Margin is a deposit, not a loan
- Option C: Losses can exceed margin (leverage risk)
