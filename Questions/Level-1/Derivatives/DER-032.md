# Question

As a futures contract approaches expiration, the futures price:

## Options

- **A**: Diverges further from the spot price
- **B**: Converges to the spot price
- **C**: Becomes fixed at the original contract price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures price convergence:

**Convergence principle:**
- Futures price → Spot price at expiration
- Basis (Futures - Spot) → 0
- Must occur by arbitrage

**Why convergence happens:**
- At expiration, futures = delivery of asset
- Must equal spot price
- If not, arbitrage opportunity

**Arbitrage enforcement:**

*If Futures > Spot at expiration:*
- Sell futures, buy spot
- Deliver spot against futures
- Risk-free profit

*If Futures < Spot at expiration:*
- Buy futures, sell spot
- Take delivery, cover short
- Risk-free profit

**Basis behavior:**
- Starts positive or negative
- Gradually approaches zero
- Equals zero at expiration

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Prices converge, not diverge
- Option C: Price changes until expiration
