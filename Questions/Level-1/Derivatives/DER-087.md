# Question

Gold trades at $1,800/oz. Storage costs are $20/oz per year, paid at year end. The risk-free rate is 5%. The 1-year forward price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $1,890
- **B**: $1,909
- **C**: $1,928

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward price with storage costs:

**Formula:**
F = S(1 + r) + FV(Storage costs)

or equivalently:
F = (S + PV(Storage)) × (1 + r)

**Method 1: Direct approach**
- Spot with financing: $1,800 × 1.05 = $1,890
- Add storage at year end: + $20
- F = $1,910 ≈ $1,909

**Method 2: PV approach**
- PV of storage: $20/1.05 = $19.05
- Adjusted spot: $1,800 + $19.05 = $1,819.05
- Forward: $1,819.05 × 1.05 = $1,909

**Storage costs effect:**
- Increase forward price
- Cost of carry includes storage
- Paid by holder of physical

**Gold characteristics:**
- No convenience yield typically
- Storage costs matter
- Safe haven asset

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Forgot storage costs
- Option C: Compounded storage incorrectly
