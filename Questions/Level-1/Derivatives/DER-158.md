# Question

The daily settlement price for futures is used to:

## Options

- **A**: Determine the initial margin requirements
- **B**: Calculate daily gains and losses for mark-to-market
- **C**: Set the price for new contracts only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Daily settlement price:

**Purpose:**
- Mark-to-market calculation
- Determine daily P&L
- Credit/debit margin accounts

**Determination:**
- Closing price or weighted average
- Exchange-determined
- End of trading day

**Mark-to-market process:**
1. Compare today's settlement to yesterday's
2. Calculate price change
3. Multiply by contract size
4. Credit winners, debit losers

**Example:**
- Yesterday settlement: $100
- Today settlement: $102
- Long position gains: $2 × contract size
- Short position loses: $2 × contract size

**Why important:**
- Realizes gains/losses daily
- Prevents buildup of large positions
- Ensures ability to pay
- Reduces credit risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Initial margin based on contract specs, not settlement
- Option C: Affects all open positions, not just new ones
