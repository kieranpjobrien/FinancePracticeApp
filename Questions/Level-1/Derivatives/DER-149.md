# Question

Hedge effectiveness in using futures is reduced when:

## Options

- **A**: The futures contract and underlying asset are identical
- **B**: There is a significant basis risk between the futures and the hedged item
- **C**: The hedge ratio is exactly matched to the exposure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Factors affecting hedge effectiveness:

**Basis risk reduces effectiveness:**
- Asset mismatch (cross-hedging)
- Maturity mismatch
- Location/quality differences
- Timing differences

**Basis defined:**
Basis = Spot price - Futures price

**Basis risk:**
- Basis changes unpredictably
- Hedge doesn't perfectly offset
- Residual risk remains

**Examples of basis risk:**

*Cross-hedge:*
- Hedging jet fuel with crude oil futures
- Correlation < 1
- Spread varies

*Timing mismatch:*
- Need hedge for July 15
- Futures expire July 31
- Must close early

**Improving effectiveness:**
- Match contract closely
- Choose nearby expiration
- Monitor basis
- Adjust hedge ratio

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Identical contracts minimize basis risk
- Option C: Proper hedge ratio improves effectiveness
