# Question

Derivatives provide leverage because:

## Options

- **A**: They require borrowing money from a broker
- **B**: A small initial investment can control a large underlying position
- **C**: They eliminate all risk from an investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Derivative leverage:

**How leverage works:**
- Small capital outlay (margin or premium)
- Control large notional value
- Amplifies gains AND losses
- Higher return potential with higher risk

**Examples of leverage:**

*Futures:*
- 5% margin controls 100% of contract
- 20:1 leverage ratio
- 1% price move = 20% gain/loss on margin

*Options:*
- Premium is fraction of stock price
- Control 100 shares per contract
- Percentage gains can be very large

**Risks of leverage:**
- Losses can exceed initial investment (futures)
- Can lose entire premium (options)
- Margin calls
- Forced liquidation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Leverage from structure, not borrowing
- Option C: Leverage increases risk, doesn't eliminate it
