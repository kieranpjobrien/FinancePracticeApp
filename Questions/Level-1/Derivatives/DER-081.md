# Question

An American call option on a dividend-paying stock may be exercised early:

## Options

- **A**: Never, because calls should always be held to expiration
- **B**: Just before an ex-dividend date if the dividend exceeds remaining time value
- **C**: Only when the stock price equals the strike price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Early exercise of American calls:

**General rule:**
- American calls on non-dividend stocks: No early exercise
- Time value always positive
- Better to sell than exercise

**With dividends:**
- Stock price drops by dividend on ex-date
- Option holder misses dividend
- May be optimal to exercise early

**Early exercise criteria:**
- Usually just before ex-dividend date
- When dividend > Remaining time value
- Capture dividend by owning stock

**Analysis:**
- Compare: Dividend received
- Versus: Time value lost by exercising
- If Dividend > Time value → Exercise

**Deep ITM calls:**
- Low time value
- High intrinsic value
- More likely early exercise candidates

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignores dividend consideration
- Option C: ATM isn't the trigger for early exercise
