# Question

The buyer of a call option has:

## Options

- **A**: The obligation to buy the underlying asset
- **B**: The right, but not the obligation, to buy the underlying asset
- **C**: The right, but not the obligation, to sell the underlying asset

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Call option mechanics:

**Call option buyer (long call):**
- Pays premium upfront
- Has the RIGHT to buy
- No obligation to exercise
- Benefits from price increases
- Maximum loss = premium paid

**Call option seller (short call):**
- Receives premium
- Has OBLIGATION to sell if exercised
- Benefits from price decreases or stability
- Potentially unlimited loss

**Exercise decision:**
- Exercise if underlying > strike price
- Let expire if underlying < strike price
- Right gives flexibility

**Key distinction:**
- Buyer: pays premium, gets rights
- Seller: receives premium, has obligations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Buyer has rights, not obligations
- Option C: This describes a put option buyer
