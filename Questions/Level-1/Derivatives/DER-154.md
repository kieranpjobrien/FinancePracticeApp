# Question

Most futures contracts are closed out:

## Options

- **A**: Through physical delivery of the underlying asset
- **B**: By offsetting the position before expiration
- **C**: Automatically by the exchange at the original price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures contract closure:

**Offset statistics:**
- 97%+ of positions closed before expiry
- Offsetting trade cancels obligation
- No delivery required

**How offsetting works:**
- Long position: Sell identical contract
- Short position: Buy identical contract
- Net position becomes zero
- Realize profit/loss

**Why offset preferred:**
- Avoid delivery logistics
- No need for physical asset
- Lower transaction costs
- Simpler settlement

**Physical delivery:**
- Small percentage actually deliver
- Commercial hedgers may want delivery
- Specific delivery procedures
- Location and quality matter

**Cash settlement:**
- Some contracts settle in cash
- Final settlement price used
- No physical delivery possible
- Stock index futures, etc.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Delivery is rare exception
- Option C: Exchange doesn't automatically close positions
