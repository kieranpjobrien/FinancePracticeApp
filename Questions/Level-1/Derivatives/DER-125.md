# Question

When pricing a forward contract on a coupon-paying bond, coupon payments during the forward period:

## Options

- **A**: Are ignored because they don't affect forward prices
- **B**: Reduce the forward price relative to a zero-coupon bond
- **C**: Increase the forward price relative to a zero-coupon bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bond forward pricing:

**Formula:**
F = (B - PV(Coupons)) × (1 + r)^T

**Why coupons reduce forward:**
- Forward holder doesn't receive coupons
- Bond holder does receive coupons
- Must adjust price for this benefit

**Analogy to stocks:**
- Like dividend adjustment
- Holder of underlying gets cash flows
- Forward holder doesn't

**Example:**
- Bond price = $1,000
- PV of coupons during forward period = $30
- Adjusted bond = $970
- Forward = $970 × (1 + r)^T

**Comparison:**
- Zero-coupon: F = B × (1 + r)^T
- Coupon-paying: F < above
- Coupon reduces net cost of carry

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Coupons materially affect forward prices
- Option C: Coupons reduce, not increase forward price
