# Question

When futures prices are higher for longer-dated contracts, the market is said to be in:

## Options

- **A**: Backwardation
- **B**: Contango
- **C**: Convergence

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures term structure:

**Contango:**
- Futures price > Spot price
- Longer-dated > Shorter-dated
- Upward sloping futures curve
- Normal for storable commodities

**Causes of contango:**
- Cost of carry (storage + financing)
- No supply shortage
- Normal market conditions

**Backwardation:**
- Futures price < Spot price
- Longer-dated < Shorter-dated
- Downward sloping futures curve

**Causes of backwardation:**
- Supply shortages
- High convenience yield
- Strong immediate demand

**Convergence:**
- Futures price → Spot price at expiration
- Always occurs at maturity
- Arbitrage ensures convergence

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Backwardation is when futures < spot
- Option C: Convergence is at expiration, not term structure
