# Question

According to put-call parity, if a call option is overpriced relative to the put:

## Options

- **A**: Both options are fairly valued
- **B**: An arbitrage opportunity exists
- **C**: The stock must also be overpriced

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Put-call parity violations:

**Parity relationship:**
C + PV(K) = P + S

**If violated:**
- Arbitrage opportunity exists
- Risk-free profit possible
- Market forces should correct

**Call overpriced scenario:**
C + PV(K) > P + S

**Arbitrage strategy:**
- Sell overpriced: Sell call
- Buy underpriced: Buy put + stock + borrow PV(K)
- Lock in profit = C + PV(K) - P - S

**Execution:**
1. Sell call, receive premium
2. Buy put, pay premium
3. Buy stock
4. Borrow PV(K)
5. Net positive cash flow = profit

**Why it works:**
- Guaranteed payoff regardless of outcome
- No net investment
- Risk-free profit

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Mispricing creates opportunity
- Option C: Stock price is separate from option mispricing
