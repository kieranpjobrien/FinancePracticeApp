# Question

An investor buys a put option with a $60 strike for a $4 premium. The breakeven price at expiration is:

## Options

- **A**: $56
- **B**: $60
- **C**: $64

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Put option breakeven:

**Formula:**
Breakeven = Strike - Premium

**Calculation:**
Breakeven = $60 - $4 = $56

**At breakeven ($56):**
- Exercise put, sell at $60
- Stock worth $56, gain $4
- Less premium paid $4
- Net profit = $0

**Profit analysis:**
- Above $60: Put expires worthless, lose $4
- At $60: Put at-the-money, lose $4
- At $56: Breakeven, zero profit
- Below $56: Profit = $60 - Stock - $4

**Example at $50:**
- Exercise put at $60
- Stock only worth $50
- Intrinsic = $10
- Net profit = $10 - $4 = $6

**Contrast with call:**
- Call breakeven = Strike + Premium
- Put breakeven = Strike - Premium

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Strike alone ignores premium
- Option C: This would be call breakeven formula
