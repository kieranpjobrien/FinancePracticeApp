# Question

Holding all else equal, an increase in the underlying stock price will cause the value of a call option to:

## Options

- **A**: Decrease
- **B**: Increase
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock price and call value:

**Direct relationship:**
- Higher stock → Higher call value
- Positive delta
- Most intuitive relationship

**Why calls increase:**
- Right to buy at fixed strike
- Higher stock = more valuable right
- Greater intrinsic value potential

**Example:**
- Strike = $50
- Stock at $45: Call OTM
- Stock at $55: Call ITM with $5 intrinsic

**Delta measures this:**
- Change in call per $1 stock change
- Positive for calls (0 to 1)
- ATM call: delta ≈ 0.5

**Opposite for puts:**
- Higher stock → Lower put value
- Negative delta
- Right to sell becomes less valuable

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This applies to put options
- Option C: Stock price is key driver of option value
