# Question

Price limit rules in futures markets are designed to:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantee profits for long positions
- **B**: Provide a cooling-off period during extreme price volatility
- **C**: Prevent all price movements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Futures price limits:

**Purpose:**
- Prevent panic trading
- Allow information processing
- Reduce extreme volatility
- Protect market integrity

**How they work:**
- Maximum daily price change
- Trading halted if limit reached
- "Limit up" or "Limit down"
- May expand after initial halt

**Types of limits:**
- Daily price limits
- Circuit breakers (dynamic)
- Trading halts

**Example:**
- Limit = $3 per bushel
- Settlement yesterday = $10
- Today's range: $7 to $13
- Trading stops outside range

**Criticisms:**
- Can trap traders
- Delays price discovery
- May increase volatility later

**Modern approaches:**
- Dynamic limits
- Expanded limits after halt
- Circuit breakers by percentage

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Limits don't guarantee profits
- Option C: Normal price movement allowed within limits
