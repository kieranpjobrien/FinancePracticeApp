# Question

A stock trades at $100 with no dividends. The 6-month futures price is $105, and the 6-month risk-free rate is 4% (annualized). The arbitrage profit per share is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $3.00
- **B**: $2.96
- **C**: $5.00

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Futures arbitrage calculation:

**Fair futures price:**
F* = S × (1 + r)^T
F* = $100 × (1.04)^0.5
F* = $100 × 1.0198
F* = $101.98

**Actual futures:** $105

**Mispricing:**
Futures overpriced by $105 - $101.98 = $3.02

**Arbitrage strategy (cash-and-carry):**
1. Borrow $100 at 4%
2. Buy stock at $100
3. Sell futures at $105

**At expiration:**
- Deliver stock, receive $105
- Repay loan: $100 × 1.0198 = $101.98
- Profit = $105 - $101.98 = $3.02

**Approximately $3.00**

**Why it works:**
- Futures too expensive relative to spot
- Sell expensive, buy cheap
- Lock in difference

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Minor calculation difference
- Option C: Simple difference without considering financing
