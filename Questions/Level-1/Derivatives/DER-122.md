# Question

Operational risk in derivatives trading includes:

## Options

- **A**: Risk of losses from adverse price movements
- **B**: Risk of losses from inadequate systems, processes, or human error
- **C**: Risk of counterparty default

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operational risk in derivatives:

**Definition:**
- Failed internal processes
- Human errors
- System breakdowns
- External events

**Examples:**
- Incorrect trade entry
- Settlement failures
- Model errors
- Technology failures
- Fraud or unauthorized trading

**Famous cases:**
- Barings Bank (Leeson's unauthorized trades)
- Knight Capital (algorithmic error)
- London Whale (risk management failure)

**Key distinction:**
- Market risk: Price movements
- Credit risk: Counterparty default
- Operational risk: Internal failures

**Management:**
- Strong internal controls
- Trade confirmation processes
- System redundancy
- Segregation of duties
- Compliance monitoring

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is market risk
- Option C: This is credit/counterparty risk
