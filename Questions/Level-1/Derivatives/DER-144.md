# Question

A long butterfly spread using calls is constructed by:

## Options

- **A**: Buying 1 low strike call, selling 2 middle strike calls, buying 1 high strike call
- **B**: Selling 1 low strike call, buying 2 middle strike calls, selling 1 high strike call
- **C**: Buying calls at three different strikes in equal amounts

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Butterfly spread construction:

**Long butterfly (calls):**
- Buy 1 call at K1 (low strike)
- Sell 2 calls at K2 (middle strike)
- Buy 1 call at K3 (high strike)
- K2 - K1 = K3 - K2 (equidistant)

**Characteristics:**
- Net debit (costs money)
- Limited profit and loss
- Profits if stock stays near K2
- Low volatility bet

**Maximum profit:**
- At K2 (middle strike) at expiration
- = K2 - K1 - Net premium

**Maximum loss:**
- = Net premium paid
- If stock below K1 or above K3

**Payoff profile:**
- Tent-shaped
- Peak at middle strike
- Wings limit both gains and losses

**When to use:**
- Expect low volatility
- Stock to stay in range
- Neutral view on direction

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: This is a short butterfly
- Option C: Not equal amounts (1-2-1 ratio)
