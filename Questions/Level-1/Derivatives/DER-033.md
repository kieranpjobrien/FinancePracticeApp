# Question

Basis risk in futures hedging arises when:

## Options

- **A**: The futures contract expires on a different date than the hedge is needed
- **B**: The underlying asset and futures contract are identical
- **C**: The hedge ratio is exactly 1.0

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Basis risk explained:

**Basis definition:**
Basis = Spot price - Futures price

**Sources of basis risk:**
- Maturity mismatch
- Asset mismatch (cross-hedge)
- Location differences
- Quality differences

**Maturity mismatch:**
- Hedge needed June 15
- Nearest futures expires June 30
- Must close position early
- Basis at closeout uncertain

**Cross-hedge basis risk:**
- Hedging jet fuel with crude oil futures
- Prices don't move perfectly together
- Imperfect correlation

**Impact:**
- Hedge won't be perfect
- Some residual risk remains
- Basis can widen or narrow

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Identical underlying reduces basis risk
- Option C: 1.0 ratio doesn't eliminate basis risk
