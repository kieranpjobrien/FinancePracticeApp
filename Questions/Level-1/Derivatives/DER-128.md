# Question

In a standard interest rate swap, payment netting means:

## Options

- **A**: Both parties make full payments to each other
- **B**: Only the net difference between payments is exchanged
- **C**: Payments are accumulated until swap termination

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Payment netting in swaps:

**How it works:**
- Calculate each party's payment
- Compare amounts
- Party owing more pays difference
- Single net payment

**Example:**
- Fixed payment = $125,000
- Floating payment = $110,000
- Net payment = $15,000 from fixed payer

**Benefits of netting:**
- Reduces settlement risk
- Lowers operational complexity
- Decreases credit exposure
- Single cash flow

**Without netting:**
- Fixed payer sends $125,000
- Floating payer sends $110,000
- Two separate transactions
- Higher operational burden

**ISDA master agreement:**
- Standard industry documentation
- Enables bilateral netting
- Close-out netting on default

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Gross settlement is rarely used for swaps
- Option C: Payments made periodically, not at end
