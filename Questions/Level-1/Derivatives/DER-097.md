# Question

The notional principal of a derivative contract:

## Options

- **A**: Must always be physically exchanged between parties
- **B**: Is used as a reference for calculating payments
- **C**: Represents the maximum possible loss

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Notional principal explained:

**Definition:**
- Reference amount for calculations
- Basis for payment determination
- May or may not be exchanged

**Uses:**
- Swap payment calculations
- Option contract size
- Exposure measurement

**Examples:**

*Interest rate swap:*
- Notional = $10 million
- Fixed payment = 10M × 5% × (days/360)
- Notional NOT exchanged

*Equity option:*
- Contract covers 100 shares
- 100 shares × price = notional

*Currency swap:*
- Notional IS exchanged
- Different currencies involved

**Risk vs. notional:**
- Actual exposure often less than notional
- Netting reduces exposure
- Mark-to-market risk matters

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Often not exchanged (IR swaps)
- Option C: Loss can exceed or be less than notional
