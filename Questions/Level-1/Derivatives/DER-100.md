# Question

Using derivatives for speculation means:

## Options

- **A**: Reducing existing risk exposures
- **B**: Taking positions to profit from expected price movements
- **C**: Ensuring compliance with regulations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Speculation with derivatives:

**Definition:**
- Taking risk for profit
- Betting on price direction
- No underlying exposure to hedge

**Speculator characteristics:**
- Forms view on future prices
- Willingly takes risk
- Seeks leveraged returns
- Provides market liquidity

**Examples:**
- Buy calls expecting stock to rise
- Sell futures expecting prices to fall
- Pay fixed in swap expecting rates to rise

**Benefits to markets:**
- Increased liquidity
- Tighter bid-ask spreads
- Price discovery
- Absorbs hedger risk

**Risks:**
- Losses can be substantial
- Leverage amplifies losses
- Requires accurate forecasting

**vs. Hedging:**
- Speculation: Take risk
- Hedging: Reduce risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is hedging, not speculation
- Option C: Unrelated to regulatory compliance
