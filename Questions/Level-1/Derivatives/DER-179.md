# Question

Position limits in futures markets are designed to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase market volatility for trading opportunities
- **B**: Prevent excessive speculation and market manipulation
- **C**: Guarantee profits for hedgers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Position limits purpose:

**Objectives:**
- Prevent concentration of positions
- Reduce manipulation potential
- Maintain orderly markets
- Protect market integrity

**How they work:**
- Maximum position size allowed
- Per speculator/entity
- Spot month limits stricter
- Non-spot month limits

**Exempt positions:**
- Bona fide hedgers
- Commercial users
- Must demonstrate hedge need
- Apply for exemption

**Reportable positions:**
- Large trader reporting
- Daily position reports
- Transparency for regulators

**Consequences of violations:**
- Forced liquidation
- Fines and penalties
- Trading restrictions

**Related concepts:**
- Accountability levels
- Price limits
- Circuit breakers

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Limits reduce excess speculation
- Option C: Limits don't guarantee profits
