# Question

An aging population most directly affects potential GDP growth through:

## Options

- **A**: Reduced labor force growth
- **B**: Increased inflation expectations
- **C**: Higher government interest rates

## Answer

A

## Explanation

As populations age, the working-age population grows more slowly or shrinks, reducing one key input to potential GDP. Countries with aging demographics (Japan, much of Europe) face structural headwinds to growth. This can be partially offset by higher labor force participation, immigration, or productivity gains, but demographic trends significantly constrain growth potential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is not a direct effect of aging—inflation effects are ambiguous
- Option C conflates demographic effects with fiscal outcomes
