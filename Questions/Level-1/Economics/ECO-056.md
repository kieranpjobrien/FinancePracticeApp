# Question

A Nash equilibrium in oligopoly occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: All firms cooperate to maximize joint profits
- **B**: No firm can improve its outcome by unilaterally changing its strategy
- **C**: The market reaches perfect competition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A Nash equilibrium exists when each firm's strategy is optimal given the strategies of all other firms—no one can benefit by changing only their own strategy. In the classic Cournot model, firms choose quantities simultaneously, reaching an equilibrium between monopoly (joint profit maximization) and perfect competition. Understanding Nash equilibrium helps predict oligopoly behavior without explicit collusion.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes collusion, which is typically unstable and illegal
- Option C is a limiting case, not the typical Nash equilibrium in oligopoly
