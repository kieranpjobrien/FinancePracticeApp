# Question

The credit cycle typically:

## Options

- **A**: Moves independently of the business cycle
- **B**: Amplifies business cycle fluctuations through pro-cyclical lending
- **C**: Stabilizes the economy by providing counter-cyclical credit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The credit cycle tends to amplify business cycle swings. During expansions, banks loosen lending standards as optimism grows and asset values rise, providing more credit that fuels further growth. During contractions, banks tighten lending as defaults rise and collateral values fall, restricting credit when the economy most needs it. This pro-cyclical behavior intensifies both booms and busts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the strong correlation between credit and business cycles
- Option C describes what counter-cyclical policy aims to do, not typical bank behavior
