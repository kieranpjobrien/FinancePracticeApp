# Question

A depression differs from a recession in that a depression:

## Options

- **A**: Is any decline in GDP lasting one quarter
- **B**: Is a severe and prolonged downturn with significant declines in output and employment
- **C**: Only affects the financial sector

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A depression is a severe, prolonged economic downturn characterized by steep declines in GDP (often 10%+), very high unemployment, extended duration (years rather than months), and widespread business failures. There is no precise technical definition, but depressions are far more severe than typical recessions. The Great Depression of the 1930s remains the defining example.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a minor or technical slowdown
- Option C ignores the economy-wide impact of depressions
