# Question

A negative output gap indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: Actual GDP exceeds potential GDP
- **B**: Actual GDP is below potential GDP
- **C**: Real GDP growth is negative

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The output gap is actual GDP minus potential GDP. A negative output gap means the economy is operating below its potential, with underutilized resources—unemployment above the natural rate and excess productive capacity. This typically occurs during recessions. A positive output gap (economy above potential) creates inflationary pressure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a positive output gap
- Option C conflates GDP levels with growth rates
