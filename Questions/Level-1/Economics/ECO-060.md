# Question

A positive output gap indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: Actual GDP is below potential GDP
- **B**: Actual GDP exceeds potential GDP, creating inflationary pressure
- **C**: The economy is in recession

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The output gap measures the difference between actual and potential GDP. A positive gap means the economy is operating above its sustainable capacity—unemployment is below the natural rate, and resources are stretched. This creates inflationary pressure as demand exceeds supply. Central banks typically tighten policy to close positive output gaps and prevent overheating.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a negative output gap (recessionary gap)
- Option C is typically associated with negative output gaps
