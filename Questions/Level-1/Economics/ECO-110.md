# Question

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) primarily provides:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term development loans for infrastructure projects
- **B**: Emergency lending to countries facing balance of payments crises
- **C**: Direct investment in private sector companies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The IMF provides temporary financial assistance to countries experiencing balance of payments difficulties, helping them stabilize currencies, restore conditions for growth, and regain market access. IMF programs typically come with policy conditions (structural adjustment). The World Bank focuses on long-term development lending, while the IMF addresses short-term financial crises.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the World Bank's primary function
- Option C describes private equity or IFC activities
