# Question

Regulatory capture occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Regulators effectively serve the interests of the industries they regulate
- **B**: Multiple agencies have overlapping regulatory authority
- **C**: Regulations are updated too frequently for firms to comply

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Regulatory capture describes situations where regulatory agencies, established to protect public interest, instead serve the interests of the regulated industry. This can occur through lobbying, revolving door employment, information asymmetries, or concentrated industry interests overwhelming diffuse public interests. Captured regulators may approve higher prices, weaken safety standards, or block competition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes regulatory fragmentation, not capture
- Option C describes regulatory uncertainty, not capture
