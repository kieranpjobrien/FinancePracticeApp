# Question

Strong institutions contribute to economic growth by:

## Options

- **A**: Directly increasing the money supply
- **B**: Protecting property rights and enforcing contracts
- **C**: Reducing international trade

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Institutions—legal systems, property rights, contract enforcement, regulatory frameworks—create the stable environment needed for investment and innovation. When investors trust that their assets are protected and contracts will be honored, they're more willing to invest. Weak institutions lead to corruption, uncertainty, and underinvestment, hindering growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes monetary policy, not institutional quality
- Option C would harm growth by reducing specialization benefits
