# Question

In response to geopolitical risks, companies are increasingly pursuing supply chain strategies that include:

## Options

- **A**: Concentrating production in the lowest-cost country regardless of political risk
- **B**: Nearshoring, reshoring, and diversifying supplier bases across multiple regions
- **C**: Eliminating all international suppliers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Geopolitical tensions, trade wars, and supply chain disruptions have led companies to reassess just-in-time and single-source strategies. Nearshoring (moving production closer to home markets), reshoring (returning production domestically), and supplier diversification reduce dependence on any single country or region. These strategies increase resilience but may raise costs, requiring careful trade-off analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the risk management considerations increasingly driving decisions
- Option C is impractical for most companies and ignores benefits of global trade
