# Question

An export subsidy is a government payment that:

## Options

- **A**: Encourages imports by lowering their cost
- **B**: Helps domestic producers compete in foreign markets by reducing their costs
- **C**: Taxes exports to raise revenue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Export subsidies are payments by governments to domestic producers that lower production costs or prices, making exports more competitive internationally. They can take forms like direct payments, tax breaks, or subsidized credit. Export subsidies are often challenged under WTO rules as unfair trade practices because they distort international competition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an import subsidy
- Option C describes an export tax, the opposite of a subsidy
