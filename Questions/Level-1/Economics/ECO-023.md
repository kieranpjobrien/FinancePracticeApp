# Question

Through the exchange rate channel, a central bank rate increase typically:

## Options

- **A**: Weakens the domestic currency and boosts exports
- **B**: Strengthens the domestic currency and reduces net exports
- **C**: Has no effect on the exchange rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Higher interest rates attract foreign capital seeking better returns, increasing demand for the domestic currency. This appreciates the exchange rate, making exports more expensive for foreign buyers and imports cheaper for domestic consumers. The result is reduced net exports, which has a contractionary effect on the economy. This exchange rate channel reinforces the direct interest rate effects of monetary tightening.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship between rates and currency strength
- Option C ignores the well-documented link between interest rates and exchange rates
