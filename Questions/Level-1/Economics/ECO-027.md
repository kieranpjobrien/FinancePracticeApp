# Question

Resource nationalism refers to the tendency of governments to:

## Options

- **A**: Encourage foreign ownership of natural resources
- **B**: Assert greater control over natural resources, often at the expense of foreign investors
- **C**: Reduce taxes on resource extraction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Resource nationalism involves governments taking actions to increase state control over natural resources such as oil, gas, minerals, and agricultural land. This can include nationalization, increased taxation, renegotiation of contracts, export restrictions, or requirements for local ownership and processing. It typically increases risks for foreign investors in resource-rich countries and can affect global commodity supply.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of resource nationalism
- Option C contradicts the typical policy direction of resource nationalism
