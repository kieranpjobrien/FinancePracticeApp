# Question

The Non-Accelerating Inflation Rate of Unemployment (NAIRU) represents:

## Options

- **A**: Zero percent unemployment
- **B**: The unemployment rate consistent with stable inflation
- **C**: The highest sustainable employment rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

NAIRU is the unemployment rate at which inflation neither accelerates nor decelerates. Below NAIRU, tight labor markets create wage pressure that accelerates inflation; above NAIRU, slack labor markets allow inflation to decelerate. Central banks try to keep unemployment near NAIRU to achieve stable inflation. NAIRU varies by country and over time based on structural factors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that some unemployment (frictional, structural) is natural
- Option C conflates unemployment rate with employment rate
