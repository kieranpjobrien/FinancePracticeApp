# Question

At the peak of a business cycle, which condition is most likely present?

## Options

- **A**: Rising unemployment and falling inflation
- **B**: Full employment and upward pressure on wages
- **C**: Excess capacity and weak consumer demand

## Answer

B

## Explanation

At the business cycle peak, the economy is operating at or above potential. Labor markets are tight with unemployment at or below the natural rate, creating upward wage pressure. Capacity utilization is high, inflation may be accelerating, and the economy is vulnerable to overheating. The subsequent phase is contraction.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conditions during a recession
- Option C describes conditions at the trough of a cycle
