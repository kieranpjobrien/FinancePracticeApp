# Question

The spot USD/EUR rate is 1.10, US interest rates are 5%, and Eurozone rates are 3%. According to covered interest rate parity, the 1-year forward USD/EUR rate should be closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 1.08
- **B**: 1.12
- **C**: 1.10

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered interest rate parity: F = S × (1 + r_domestic) / (1 + r_foreign)

Where USD is domestic and EUR is foreign:
F = 1.10 × (1.05) / (1.03)
F = 1.10 × 1.0194
F = 1.121 ≈ 1.12

The higher US rates mean the dollar should depreciate forward (more dollars per euro), compensating for the interest rate differential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies the ratio incorrectly
- Option C assumes rates don't affect forward rates
