# Question

A shift in the aggregate demand curve to the right would result from:

## Options

- **A**: An increase in taxes with no change in spending
- **B**: An increase in consumer confidence leading to higher consumption
- **C**: A decrease in the money supply

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Aggregate demand (AD) shifts right when total spending in the economy increases at each price level. Increased consumer confidence boosts consumption spending (C), shifting AD right. Other AD boosters include: increased investment (I), government spending (G), net exports (NX), or monetary expansion. Tax increases and monetary contraction shift AD left.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would reduce disposable income and shift AD left
- Option C is contractionary monetary policy, shifting AD left
