# Question

Deposit insurance may create moral hazard by:

## Options

- **A**: Encouraging depositors to monitor bank risk more carefully
- **B**: Reducing banks' incentives to manage risk prudently
- **C**: Eliminating the possibility of bank runs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deposit insurance protects depositors from bank failures, but this safety net can encourage excessive risk-taking. Banks may pursue risky loans knowing depositors won't flee, and depositors have less incentive to monitor bank behavior. This moral hazard justifies prudential regulation—capital requirements, supervision, and risk limits—to counterbalance insurance-induced risk-taking.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is backwards—insurance reduces depositor monitoring incentives
- Option C overstates—deposit insurance reduces but doesn't eliminate run risk
