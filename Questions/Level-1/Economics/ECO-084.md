# Question

Uncovered interest rate parity (UIP) differs from covered interest rate parity (CIP) in that UIP:

## Options

- **A**: Uses forward contracts to eliminate exchange rate risk
- **B**: Relies on expected future spot rates without hedging
- **C**: Only applies to short-term interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered interest rate parity uses forward contracts to lock in future exchange rates, creating a risk-free arbitrage relationship. Uncovered interest rate parity assumes expected future spot rates will adjust to offset interest differentials—the high-rate currency is expected to depreciate. UIP doesn't hold as consistently as CIP because exchange rate expectations may be wrong and risk premiums exist.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes covered interest rate parity
- Option C is not a distinguishing factor between CIP and UIP
