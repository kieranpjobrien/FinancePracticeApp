# Question

The principle of comparative advantage suggests that countries should:

## Options

- **A**: Only trade if they can produce everything more efficiently than trading partners
- **B**: Specialize in producing goods where they have the lowest opportunity cost
- **C**: Avoid trade with countries that have lower wages

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Comparative advantage states that countries benefit from specializing in goods where they have the lowest opportunity cost of production, even if they don't have an absolute advantage in any good. By specializing and trading, both countries can consume beyond their production possibilities. This principle is foundational to understanding gains from international trade.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes absolute advantage thinking, which misses the benefits of trade
- Option C ignores that wage differences can reflect productivity differences
