# Question

Cost-push inflation differs from demand-pull inflation in that cost-push inflation:

## Options

- **A**: Results from excessive aggregate demand
- **B**: Results from supply shocks that raise production costs
- **C**: Only occurs during economic expansions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cost-push inflation originates from the supply side—rising input costs (oil, wages, materials) push production costs higher, which firms pass on as higher prices. Demand-pull inflation comes from excess demand relative to supply. Cost-push can create stagflation (inflation plus recession), while demand-pull typically accompanies strong growth. Policy responses differ.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes demand-pull inflation
- Option C ignores that cost-push can occur during recessions (stagflation)
