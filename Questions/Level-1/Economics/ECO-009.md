# Question

Fiscal policy refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Central bank decisions about interest rates and money supply
- **B**: Government decisions about spending and taxation
- **C**: Exchange rate interventions by the central bank

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fiscal policy involves government decisions on spending and taxation to influence the economy. Expansionary fiscal policy (increased spending or reduced taxes) stimulates aggregate demand. Contractionary fiscal policy (reduced spending or higher taxes) slows the economy. Fiscal policy is implemented by the government, typically through the legislative process.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes monetary policy
- Option C describes foreign exchange intervention, part of monetary/exchange rate policy
