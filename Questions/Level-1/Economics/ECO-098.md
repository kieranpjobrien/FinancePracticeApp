# Question

Transfer payments, such as unemployment benefits and social security, differ from government purchases in that transfers:

## Options

- **A**: Directly purchase goods and services
- **B**: Redistribute income without directly purchasing output
- **C**: Always increase during economic expansions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Government purchases (G) involve direct spending on goods and services (roads, defense, education) and add directly to GDP. Transfer payments redistribute income from taxpayers to recipients (unemployed, retirees, disabled) without purchasing output. Transfers affect consumption indirectly as recipients spend the money. In national accounts, transfers are not included in G.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes government purchases
- Option C is backwards—transfers typically increase during recessions (automatic stabilizers)
