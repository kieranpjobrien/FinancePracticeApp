# Question

The quantity theory of money equation (MV = PY) suggests that if velocity (V) and real output (Y) are stable:

## Options

- **A**: Changes in money supply have no effect on prices
- **B**: Changes in money supply lead to proportional changes in the price level
- **C**: Prices are determined solely by production costs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The equation of exchange states: Money supply (M) × Velocity (V) = Price level (P) × Real output (Y). If V and Y are constant, any increase in M must result in a proportional increase in P. This classical view suggests that in the long run, monetary expansion leads to inflation. While the assumptions are debatable, the relationship has empirical support over long periods.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the equation's implications
- Option C ignores the monetary side of price determination
