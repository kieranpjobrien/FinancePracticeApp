# Question

Financial contagion refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The spread of beneficial financial innovations across countries
- **B**: The transmission of financial crises from one country or market to others
- **C**: The gradual development of financial markets in emerging economies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Financial contagion describes how financial crises spread across borders through various channels: trade linkages (crisis reduces imports from partners), financial linkages (bank exposures, portfolio rebalancing), and informational channels (investors reassess similar countries). Examples include the 1997 Asian crisis spreading across the region and the 2008 global financial crisis. Contagion risk is a key consideration for international investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes financial innovation diffusion
- Option C describes financial development, not crisis transmission
