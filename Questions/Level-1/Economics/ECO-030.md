# Question

A tariff is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: A tax on imported goods
- **B**: A limit on the quantity of imports
- **C**: A subsidy to domestic producers

## Answer

A

## Explanation

A tariff is a tax levied on imported goods, making them more expensive relative to domestic alternatives. Tariffs generate government revenue and provide protection for domestic industries. However, they raise prices for consumers and can lead to retaliatory measures from trading partners. Tariffs are one of the most common forms of trade restriction.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes an import quota, not a tariff
- Option C describes a subsidy, which is a different form of industrial policy
