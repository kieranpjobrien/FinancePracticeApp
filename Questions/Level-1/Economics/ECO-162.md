# Question

The expectations channel of monetary policy works through:

## Options

- **A**: Direct central bank lending to businesses
- **B**: Influencing private sector expectations about future inflation and economic conditions
- **C**: Changing tax rates on investment income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The expectations channel recognizes that monetary policy affects the economy partly through changing expectations. If businesses expect future growth, they invest more today; if households expect stable prices, wage demands moderate. Central bank communication (forward guidance, credibility) shapes these expectations. This is why central bank credibility and clear communication are crucial for policy effectiveness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes direct credit allocation
- Option C describes fiscal policy
