# Question

The debt-to-GDP ratio is considered an important measure because it:

## Options

- **A**: Shows only the absolute level of government debt
- **B**: Indicates the debt burden relative to the economy's ability to service it
- **C**: Measures the annual budget deficit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The debt-to-GDP ratio measures total government debt as a percentage of GDP, indicating whether debt is sustainable relative to economic output. A country with higher GDP can support more debt. Rising ratios suggest debt is growing faster than the economy, potentially threatening sustainability. While there's no universal "safe" level, ratios above 100% raise concerns about fiscal sustainability in many contexts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misses the comparative nature of the ratio
- Option C confuses stock (debt) with flow (annual deficit)
