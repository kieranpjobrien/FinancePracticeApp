# Question

Macroprudential policy differs from traditional monetary policy in that it focuses on:

## Options

- **A**: Setting short-term interest rates
- **B**: Reducing systemic risk in the financial system as a whole
- **C**: Controlling the money supply through open market operations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Macroprudential policy addresses systemic risks to financial stability—risks that could trigger a financial crisis affecting the whole economy. Tools include countercyclical capital buffers, loan-to-value limits, and stress testing requirements. While monetary policy focuses on price stability and output, macroprudential policy targets the resilience of the financial system itself.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conventional monetary policy
- Option C describes a monetary policy tool
