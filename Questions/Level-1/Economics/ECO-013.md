# Question

A structural budget deficit differs from a cyclical deficit in that a structural deficit:

## Options

- **A**: Only occurs during recessions
- **B**: Would exist even if the economy were at full employment
- **C**: Is caused entirely by automatic stabilizers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A structural deficit reflects underlying fiscal imbalances that persist regardless of economic conditions—it would exist even at full employment. A cyclical deficit results from temporary revenue shortfalls and spending increases during recessions due to automatic stabilizers. Economists decompose actual deficits into structural and cyclical components to assess the true fiscal stance and long-term sustainability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cyclical deficits
- Option C confuses the cyclical component with structural issues
