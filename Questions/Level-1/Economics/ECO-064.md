# Question

A government budget deficit occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Government revenues exceed expenditures
- **B**: Government expenditures exceed revenues
- **C**: The national debt decreases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A budget deficit occurs when government spending exceeds tax revenues in a given period (typically a fiscal year). Deficits must be financed by borrowing, which adds to the national debt. Conversely, a surplus occurs when revenues exceed spending, allowing debt reduction. Most developed economies run deficits during recessions and ideally surpluses during expansions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a budget surplus
- Option C would require a surplus, not a deficit
