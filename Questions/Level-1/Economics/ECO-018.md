# Question

If a central bank lowers the reserve requirement ratio, banks can:

## Options

- **A**: Hold fewer reserves and lend more, increasing money supply
- **B**: Hold more reserves, reducing lending capacity
- **C**: Only lend to the government

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The reserve requirement specifies the minimum percentage of deposits banks must hold as reserves. When this ratio is lowered, banks can hold a smaller fraction of deposits as reserves and lend out a larger fraction. This increases the money multiplier and allows the banking system to create more money through lending. Lowering reserve requirements is an expansionary monetary policy tool.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B reverses the effect of lowering reserve requirements
- Option C misunderstands the nature of bank lending restrictions
