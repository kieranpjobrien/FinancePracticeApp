# Question

The real exchange rate differs from the nominal exchange rate because the real rate:

## Options

- **A**: Ignores inflation differentials between countries
- **B**: Adjusts for relative price levels between countries
- **C**: Is always equal to the nominal rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The real exchange rate adjusts the nominal rate for differences in price levels between countries. It measures the purchasing power of one currency versus another:

Real rate = Nominal rate × (Foreign price level / Domestic price level)

A country with higher inflation sees its real exchange rate appreciate even if the nominal rate is unchanged, reducing its competitiveness. Real exchange rates are crucial for analyzing trade competitiveness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the nominal rate
- Option C ignores the fundamental difference between real and nominal rates
