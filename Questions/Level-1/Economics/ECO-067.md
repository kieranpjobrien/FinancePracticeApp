# Question

Contractionary monetary policy involves:

## Options

- **A**: Lowering interest rates and increasing money supply
- **B**: Raising interest rates and reducing money supply growth
- **C**: Increasing government spending

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contractionary monetary policy aims to slow economic activity and reduce inflation by raising interest rates and restricting money supply growth. Higher rates discourage borrowing and spending, cooling demand. Central banks use contractionary policy when inflation is above target or the economy is overheating. The opposite—expansionary policy—lowers rates and increases money supply to stimulate growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes expansionary monetary policy
- Option C describes expansionary fiscal policy
