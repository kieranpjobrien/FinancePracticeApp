# Question

The GDP deflator measures:

## Options

- **A**: The change in prices of a fixed basket of consumer goods
- **B**: The ratio of nominal GDP to real GDP
- **C**: The unemployment rate adjusted for inflation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The GDP deflator = (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100. It measures the overall price level of all domestically produced goods and services, not just consumer goods. Unlike the CPI, which uses a fixed basket, the GDP deflator's basket changes with actual production patterns. It's a broader measure of inflation in the economy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the Consumer Price Index (CPI)
- Option C conflates different economic indicators
