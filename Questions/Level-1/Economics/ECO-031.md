# Question

An import quota differs from a tariff in that a quota:

## Options

- **A**: Generates revenue for the importing government
- **B**: Sets a maximum quantity of imports rather than raising their price through taxation
- **C**: Has no effect on domestic prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

While tariffs tax imports, quotas directly limit the quantity that can be imported. Both raise domestic prices by restricting supply, but quotas don't generate government revenue—instead, the price premium (quota rent) may accrue to importers or foreign exporters depending on how quotas are allocated. Quotas provide more certain quantity control but less price predictability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a tariff benefit, not a quota characteristic
- Option C is incorrect—quotas do raise prices by restricting supply
