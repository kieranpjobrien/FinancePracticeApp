# Question

Political risk for international investments includes:

## Options

- **A**: Only the risk of war or terrorism
- **B**: Risks from government policy changes, expropriation, contract repudiation, and civil unrest
- **C**: Only currency exchange rate fluctuations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Political risk encompasses a broad range of government and political actions that could harm investments: policy changes (taxes, regulations), expropriation or nationalization, contract breach, currency inconvertibility, transfer restrictions, war, civil unrest, and bureaucratic obstacles. Investors assess political risk when making cross-border investments and may require higher returns or use political risk insurance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too narrow—political risk extends beyond violent conflict
- Option C describes currency risk, which is separate from political risk
