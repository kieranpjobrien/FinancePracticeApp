# Question

In the United States, recessions are officially dated by:

## Options

- **A**: The Federal Reserve
- **B**: The National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER)
- **C**: The Bureau of Labor Statistics

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The NBER's Business Cycle Dating Committee officially declares US recession start and end dates. They define a recession as "a significant decline in economic activity spread across the economy, lasting more than a few months." The committee examines multiple indicators including employment, income, spending, and industrial production—not just two quarters of GDP decline.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the central bank, focused on monetary policy
- Option C produces employment statistics but doesn't officially date recessions
