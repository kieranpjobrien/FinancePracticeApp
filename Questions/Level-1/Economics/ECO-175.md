# Question

According to covered interest rate parity, a currency with a higher interest rate will trade at a:

## Options

- **A**: Forward premium
- **B**: Forward discount
- **C**: The same rate as spot

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered interest rate parity ensures no arbitrage between investing domestically versus abroad with a hedge. A higher-rate currency must trade at a forward discount (forward rate below spot) to offset the interest advantage. Otherwise, investors could profit risk-free by borrowing in the low-rate currency and investing in the high-rate currency while hedging.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C ignores the no-arbitrage condition
