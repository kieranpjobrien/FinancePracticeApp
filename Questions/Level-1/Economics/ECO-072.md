# Question

Currency manipulation allegations typically involve claims that a country:

## Options

- **A**: Allows its currency to float freely
- **B**: Artificially weakens its currency to gain export competitiveness
- **C**: Maintains strict capital controls without affecting the exchange rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Currency manipulation refers to government intervention to artificially depress a currency's value, making exports cheaper and more competitive internationally while making imports more expensive. This is seen as an unfair trade practice because it provides a subsidy to exporters at trading partners' expense. The US Treasury monitors major trading partners for potential currency manipulation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite of manipulation
- Option C focuses on capital controls without addressing exchange rate effects
