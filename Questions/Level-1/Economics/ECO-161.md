# Question

Yield curve control (YCC) is a monetary policy where the central bank:

## Options

- **A**: Allows market forces to determine all interest rates
- **B**: Targets specific yields on government bonds by buying or selling as needed
- **C**: Only controls overnight interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under yield curve control, the central bank commits to keeping certain bond yields at target levels by purchasing or selling whatever quantity is needed. The Bank of Japan pioneered this approach, targeting 10-year yields near zero. YCC differs from QE (which targets purchase quantities) by targeting price/yield instead. It can keep borrowing costs low but may require unlimited balance sheet expansion.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes pure market determination without intervention
- Option C describes conventional policy, not YCC
