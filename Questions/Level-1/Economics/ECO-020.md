# Question

Inflation targeting as a monetary policy framework involves:

## Options

- **A**: Setting interest rates to achieve a specific exchange rate target
- **B**: Publicly committing to an explicit inflation target and adjusting policy to achieve it
- **C**: Focusing exclusively on money supply growth targets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inflation targeting involves a central bank publicly announcing a specific inflation target (commonly around 2%) and committing to adjust monetary policy to achieve that target over time. This framework emphasizes transparency, accountability, and communication. The explicit target helps anchor inflation expectations, which can make the policy more effective. Many major central banks now use inflation targeting.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes exchange rate targeting, a different framework
- Option C describes monetary targeting, a largely abandoned approach
