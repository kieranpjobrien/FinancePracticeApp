# Question

In contestable markets theory, even a monopolist may behave competitively if:

## Options

- **A**: The government regulates prices directly
- **B**: Potential entrants can enter and exit with minimal sunk costs
- **C**: The monopolist faces elastic demand at all price levels

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Contestable markets theory argues that the threat of entry, not actual competition, disciplines firm behavior. If potential competitors can enter without significant sunk costs (which would be lost upon exit), even a monopolist must price near competitive levels or face entry. Low barriers to entry and exit, not the number of existing firms, determine market performance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes direct regulation, not market discipline through potential competition
- Option C relates to demand elasticity, not contestability
