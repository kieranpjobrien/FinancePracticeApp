# Question

Deflation is particularly problematic for an economy because it:

## Options

- **A**: Reduces the real burden of existing debt
- **B**: Encourages consumers to delay purchases expecting lower prices
- **C**: Causes rapid currency depreciation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deflation creates a deflationary spiral: consumers delay spending anticipating lower future prices, which reduces demand, causing further price drops. This stalls economic activity. Additionally, deflation increases the real value of debt (opposite of option A), making it harder for borrowers to repay. Japan's "lost decades" illustrate deflation's damaging persistence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is backwards—deflation increases the real debt burden
- Option C is incorrect—deflation typically causes currency appreciation, not depreciation
