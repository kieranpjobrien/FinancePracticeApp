# Question

The infant industry argument for trade protection suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: All industries should be permanently protected from foreign competition
- **B**: New industries may need temporary protection to develop before competing internationally
- **C**: Only agricultural industries require protection

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The infant industry argument holds that newly developing industries may need temporary protection from established foreign competitors until they achieve economies of scale and learning curve efficiencies. Once mature, protection should be removed. Critics argue governments are poor at picking winners, protection often becomes permanent, and market mechanisms should determine industry viability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A advocates permanent protection, not the temporary support the argument proposes
- Option C arbitrarily limits the argument to one sector
