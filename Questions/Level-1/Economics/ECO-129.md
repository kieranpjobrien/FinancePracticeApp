# Question

Okun's law describes the relationship between:

## Options

- **A**: Inflation and unemployment
- **B**: Unemployment and GDP growth
- **C**: Interest rates and investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Okun's law is an empirical relationship stating that for every 1% increase in unemployment above its natural rate, GDP falls approximately 2-3% below potential (the exact coefficient varies by country and time). This relationship helps policymakers estimate the output cost of unemployment or conversely, how much growth is needed to reduce unemployment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the Phillips curve
- Option C describes investment theory, not Okun's law
