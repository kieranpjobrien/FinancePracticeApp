# Question

Which of the following is most likely a leading economic indicator?

## Options

- **A**: GDP growth rate
- **B**: Stock market index returns
- **C**: Unemployment rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leading indicators change before the economy changes direction, helping forecast future activity. Stock prices are leading indicators because markets are forward-looking—investors buy/sell based on expectations. Building permits, new orders, and yield curve shape are also leading indicators. GDP and unemployment are coincident and lagging indicators respectively.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (GDP) is a coincident indicator—it moves with the economy
- Option C (unemployment) is a lagging indicator—it changes after the economy turns
