# Question

Keynesian economics recommends that during a recession, governments should:

## Options

- **A**: Reduce spending to balance the budget
- **B**: Increase spending or cut taxes to boost aggregate demand
- **C**: Raise interest rates to encourage saving

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Keynesian economics argues that during recessions, private sector spending is insufficient to maintain full employment. Government should fill this demand gap through increased spending or tax cuts (fiscal stimulus), even if it means running deficits. The multiplier effect amplifies the stimulus impact. Once recovery takes hold, policy can normalize.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes austerity, which Keynesians argue worsens recessions
- Option C describes contractionary monetary policy
