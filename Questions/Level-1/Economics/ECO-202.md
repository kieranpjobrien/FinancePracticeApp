# Question

Total factor productivity (TFP) growth represents:

## Options

- **A**: The increase in output due to additional labor and capital inputs
- **B**: Output growth not explained by increases in labor and capital
- **C**: The growth rate of the labor force participation rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

TFP (also called the Solow residual) measures efficiency gains—how much more output can be produced with the same inputs. It captures technological progress, organizational improvements, and better resource allocation. Growth accounting decomposes GDP growth into contributions from labor, capital, and TFP. TFP is often the largest driver of long-run growth in developed economies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes factor accumulation, the opposite of TFP
- Option C is a labor market metric, not a productivity measure
