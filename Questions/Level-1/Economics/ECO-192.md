# Question

A recession is typically defined as:

## Options

- **A**: Any quarter with negative GDP growth
- **B**: Two or more consecutive quarters of declining real GDP
- **C**: Unemployment rising above 5%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The traditional rule-of-thumb defines a recession as two consecutive quarters of negative real GDP growth. However, official recession dating (by bodies like the NBER in the US) considers broader factors including employment, income, industrial production, and sales. Recessions feature declining output, rising unemployment, and falling business activity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too narrow—a single quarter of negative growth isn't typically a recession
- Option C uses an arbitrary unemployment threshold that varies by country
