# Question

Arguments favoring deregulation typically emphasize:

## Options

- **A**: The need for more government control over industry
- **B**: Improved efficiency through competition and innovation
- **C**: Higher barriers to entry for new firms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deregulation proponents argue that removing or reducing regulations increases efficiency through greater competition, lower costs, more innovation, and better resource allocation. Successful deregulation examples include airlines and telecommunications in many countries. However, deregulation can also lead to market failures if underlying market power or externality problems persist.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of deregulation
- Option C contradicts deregulation's goal of reducing barriers
