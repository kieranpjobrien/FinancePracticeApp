# Question

An asset price bubble is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Prices that reflect fundamental value
- **B**: Prices that rise far above fundamental values, often followed by a sharp decline
- **C**: Steadily falling asset prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset bubbles occur when prices rise substantially above levels justified by fundamentals (earnings, rents, or intrinsic value), often driven by speculation, easy credit, and the belief prices will continue rising. When sentiment shifts, bubbles typically burst, causing sharp price declines that can trigger financial crises. Historical examples include tulip mania, dot-com, and housing bubbles.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes efficient markets without bubbles
- Option C describes a bear market, not a bubble
