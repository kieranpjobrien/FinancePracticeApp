# Question

If the forward rate is higher than the spot rate, the base currency is trading at a:

## Options

- **A**: Forward discount
- **B**: Forward premium
- **C**: Interest rate parity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When the forward rate exceeds the spot rate, the base currency (denominator) is at a forward premium—it is expected to strengthen or is more expensive for future delivery. This typically reflects lower interest rates in the base currency country relative to the quote currency country. Conversely, a forward discount indicates the base currency is cheaper for forward delivery.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C is a theory that explains forward premiums/discounts, not the premium itself
