# Question

The inventory cycle is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term cycles driven by technological innovation
- **B**: Short-term fluctuations as businesses adjust inventory levels
- **C**: Medium-term cycles driven by capital investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The inventory cycle (also called the Kitchin cycle) lasts roughly 3-5 years and reflects short-term business adjustments. When demand rises, firms first deplete inventories, then increase production to rebuild stocks—amplifying the upturn. When demand falls, excess inventory leads to production cuts beyond the demand decline—amplifying the downturn.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the Kondratieff long wave (40-60 years)
- Option C describes the Juglar investment cycle (7-11 years)
