# Question

Economic profit differs from accounting profit in that economic profit:

## Options

- **A**: Only includes explicit costs
- **B**: Includes both explicit costs and opportunity costs of resources
- **C**: Is always higher than accounting profit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Economic profit subtracts all costs, including the opportunity cost of capital and owner's time (implicit costs), while accounting profit only subtracts explicit out-of-pocket costs. A firm earning zero economic profit is earning normal returns—enough to cover all opportunity costs. Positive economic profit indicates returns exceeding what resources could earn in their next best use.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes accounting profit
- Option C is backwards—economic profit is typically lower due to implicit costs
