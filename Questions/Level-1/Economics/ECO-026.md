# Question

Economic sanctions imposed by one country on another typically:

## Options

- **A**: Always benefit both economies through reduced competition
- **B**: Restrict trade, investment, or financial transactions to achieve political objectives
- **C**: Have no economic effects on the sanctioning country

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Economic sanctions are diplomatic tools that restrict economic activity with targeted countries. They can include trade embargoes, asset freezes, travel bans, and restrictions on financial transactions. Sanctions aim to pressure countries to change policies but often have costs for both the targeted economy and the sanctioning countries. Effectiveness depends on enforcement, scope, and the targeted country's alternatives.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the deliberately punitive nature of sanctions
- Option C overlooks that sanctions often reduce trade opportunities for the sanctioning country too
