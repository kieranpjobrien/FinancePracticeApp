# Question

Geopolitical risk refers to the risk associated with:

## Options

- **A**: Changes in a company's management team
- **B**: Political events and tensions between nations that affect investments
- **C**: Fluctuations in daily stock prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Geopolitical risk encompasses the potential negative impact on investments from political events such as wars, territorial disputes, trade conflicts, sanctions, terrorism, and political instability. These risks can affect entire regions or specific countries and may impact supply chains, trade relationships, commodity prices, and investor sentiment. Managing geopolitical risk is increasingly important in global portfolio management.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes corporate governance risk
- Option C describes market volatility, not geopolitical risk specifically
