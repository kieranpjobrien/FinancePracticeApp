# Question

Cyclical unemployment is most closely associated with:

## Options

- **A**: Workers voluntarily searching for better opportunities
- **B**: Decline in aggregate demand during economic contractions
- **C**: Technological changes eliminating certain job categories

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cyclical unemployment rises during recessions when aggregate demand falls and businesses reduce output and layoffs increase. It's directly tied to the business cycle—unemployment rises in contractions and falls during expansions. This type is considered involuntary and is the primary target of counter-cyclical fiscal and monetary policy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes frictional unemployment
- Option C describes structural unemployment
