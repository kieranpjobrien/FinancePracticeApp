# Question

Hyperinflation is generally defined as inflation exceeding:

## Options

- **A**: 10% per year
- **B**: 50% per month
- **C**: 100% total over any period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

While definitions vary, hyperinflation is commonly defined as monthly inflation exceeding 50% (equivalent to annual rates above 13,000%). Under hyperinflation, money loses value so rapidly that people rush to spend it, prices change daily or hourly, and the monetary system may collapse. Historical examples include Weimar Germany, Zimbabwe, and Venezuela.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes high but not hyper inflation
- Option C is too vague a criterion
