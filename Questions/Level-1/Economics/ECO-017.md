# Question

When a central bank conducts open market purchases of government securities, it:

## Options

- **A**: Decreases the money supply and raises interest rates
- **B**: Increases the money supply and lowers interest rates
- **C**: Has no effect on bank reserves

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Open market purchases involve the central bank buying government securities from banks and other financial institutions, paying with newly created reserves. This injects liquidity into the banking system, increasing the money supply. The increased supply of reserves puts downward pressure on short-term interest rates. This is an expansionary monetary policy tool.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes open market sales, not purchases
- Option C ignores the fundamental mechanism of how purchases add to bank reserves
