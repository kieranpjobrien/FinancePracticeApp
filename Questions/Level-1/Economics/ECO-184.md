# Question

Cost-push inflation is most likely caused by:

## Options

- **A**: Excessive growth in the money supply
- **B**: Strong consumer spending and credit growth
- **C**: Rising input costs such as wages or raw materials

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Cost-push inflation occurs when production costs rise, pushing up prices from the supply side. Common causes include rising wages, energy price shocks (like oil crises), raw material shortages, or supply chain disruptions. Unlike demand-pull inflation, cost-push inflation can occur even when demand is weak, creating stagflation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a monetary cause of demand-pull inflation
- Option B describes demand-side pressures
