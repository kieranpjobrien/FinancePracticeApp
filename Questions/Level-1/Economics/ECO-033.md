# Question

Trade diversion occurs when a regional trade agreement causes:

## Options

- **A**: Imports to shift from a more efficient non-member producer to a less efficient member producer
- **B**: Trade to increase between all countries globally
- **C**: Exports to increase from the most efficient global producers

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Trade diversion is a potential cost of regional trade agreements. When tariffs are eliminated within a bloc, imports may shift from efficient non-member producers (who still face tariffs) to less efficient member producers (who don't). This reduces overall economic efficiency, unlike trade creation, where the agreement leads to imports from more efficient member producers replacing less efficient domestic production.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes an overly optimistic view that ignores trade diversion effects
- Option C describes trade creation, not diversion
