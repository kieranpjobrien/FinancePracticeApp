# Question

A positive cross-price elasticity of demand indicates the goods are:

## Options

- **A**: Complements
- **B**: Substitutes
- **C**: Unrelated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-price elasticity measures how demand for one good responds to price changes in another. Positive cross-elasticity means when Good B's price rises, demand for Good A increases—they're substitutes (Coke and Pepsi). Negative cross-elasticity indicates complements (cars and gasoline)—when one's price rises, demand for both falls.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes goods with negative cross-elasticity
- Option C would show zero cross-elasticity
