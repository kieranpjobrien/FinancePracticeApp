# Question

Stagflation is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: High growth, low unemployment, and rising inflation
- **B**: Slow growth, high unemployment, and high inflation
- **C**: Negative growth, high unemployment, and falling prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stagflation combines economic stagnation (slow/negative growth, high unemployment) with inflation—a problematic combination for policymakers. Traditional policy faces a dilemma: stimulating demand to reduce unemployment worsens inflation, while fighting inflation worsens unemployment. The 1970s oil shocks caused notable stagflation episodes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an overheating economy
- Option C describes deflationary recession/depression
