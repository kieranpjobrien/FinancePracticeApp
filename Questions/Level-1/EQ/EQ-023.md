# Question

According to the Gordon Growth Model, a justified P/E ratio is higher when:

## Options

- **A**: The required return is higher
- **B**: The expected growth rate is higher
- **C**: The dividend payout ratio is lower

## Answer

B

## Explanation

From DDM: P₀/E₁ = (D₁/E₁) / (r - g) = payout ratio / (r - g). Higher growth (g) narrows the denominator, increasing justified P/E. Higher required return (r) widens denominator, lowering P/E. Higher payout ratio also increases P/E (larger numerator).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—higher r lowers justified P/E
- Option C is opposite—lower payout lowers justified P/E
