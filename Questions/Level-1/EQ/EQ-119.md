# Question

Treasury stock represents:

## Options

- **A**: Shares held by the government treasury
- **B**: Shares repurchased by the company and held in its own treasury
- **C**: Newly authorized but unissued shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Treasury stock: shares previously issued but repurchased by the company. Reduces shares outstanding and shareholders' equity. Can be retired or reissued later (for compensation, acquisitions). Treasury shares don't receive dividends or have voting rights. Shown as contra-equity on balance sheet.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misinterprets "treasury"
- Option C describes authorized but unissued shares
