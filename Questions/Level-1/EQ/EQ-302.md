# Question

Monopolistic competition is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: A single seller with complete market control
- **B**: Many firms selling differentiated products with relatively easy market entry
- **C**: Perfectly homogeneous products across all sellers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monopolistic competition: many firms, differentiated products (brand, features, location), some pricing power, low barriers to entry. Examples: restaurants, retail, consumer products. Short-run profits possible, but entry erodes advantages over time. Most consumer-facing industries fall here.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes pure monopoly
- Option C describes perfect competition with identical products
