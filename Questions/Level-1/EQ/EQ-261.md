# Question

Goodwill impairment signals to analysts that:

## Options

- **A**: The acquisition continues to perform as expected
- **B**: Management acknowledges that an acquisition has not met expectations
- **C**: The company is growing rapidly through acquisitions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Goodwill impairment: write-down when acquired business value falls below carrying amount. Indicates acquisition overpayment, synergies not realized, or deteriorating business. Non-cash charge but signals management admission of value destruction. Analysts scrutinize acquisition track record when evaluating future deals.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—impairment indicates underperformance
- Option C describes acquisition activity, not impairment
