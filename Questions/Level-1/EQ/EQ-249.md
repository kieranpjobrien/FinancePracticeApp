# Question

The framing effect in behavioral finance describes how:

## Options

- **A**: Investors always interpret information objectively
- **B**: The way information is presented influences decisions, even if substance is unchanged
- **C**: All investment frames are equally valid

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Framing effect: choices depend on how options are presented. Same information framed as gain vs. loss produces different decisions. Examples: "90% survival rate" vs. "10% mortality rate" affect medical choices similarly in investing. Violates rational utility theory assumption of consistent preferences.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational behavior without framing effects
- Option C misunderstands framing as a valid analytical approach
