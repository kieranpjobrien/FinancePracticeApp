# Question

The random walk hypothesis suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Stock prices follow predictable patterns
- **B**: Future price changes are independent of past changes and cannot be predicted
- **C**: All stocks move in the same direction

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Random walk: price changes are random and unpredictable based on past information. Consistent with market efficiency—if prices fully reflect available information, only new (unpredictable) information moves prices. Past price movements provide no guidance for future movements. Supports weak-form efficiency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the random walk concept
- Option C misunderstands stock price behavior
