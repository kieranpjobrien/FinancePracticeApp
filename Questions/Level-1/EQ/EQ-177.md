# Question

A stop-loss order to sell at $45 on a stock currently trading at $50:

## Options

- **A**: Will execute immediately at $45
- **B**: Becomes a market order to sell when the price drops to $45 or below
- **C**: Guarantees the stock will be sold at exactly $45

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stop order: dormant until trigger price reached, then becomes market order. Stop-loss sell: triggers when price falls to stop level, then executes at next available price (may be below $45 in fast market). Used to limit downside loss. Stop-limit adds price floor but risks non-execution.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—stop doesn't execute until triggered
- Option C—once triggered, executes as market order at prevailing prices
