# Question

Under statutory voting, a shareholder owning 100 shares voting for 5 board seats can cast a maximum of:

## Options

- **A**: 500 votes for a single candidate
- **B**: 100 votes per candidate, for a total of 500 votes
- **C**: 100 votes total across all candidates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Statutory voting: one vote per share for each seat being filled. With 100 shares and 5 seats: 100 votes per position, cannot concentrate votes on fewer candidates. Total votes = shares × seats = 500, but distributed 100 per seat. Contrasts with cumulative voting where votes can be pooled.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cumulative voting (100 × 5 = 500 for one candidate)
- Option C underestimates—statutory allows votes for each seat
