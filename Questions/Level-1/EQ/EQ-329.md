# Question

A large and growing order backlog typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Weak demand for the company's products
- **B**: Strong demand and revenue visibility, though potentially capacity constraints
- **C**: The company should immediately cut production

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Backlog: unfilled orders representing future revenue. Growing backlog: positive demand signal, visibility into future periods. However, very large backlog may indicate production constraints limiting growth. Backlog-to-revenue ratio indicates months of visibility. Common in aerospace, construction, capital goods.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the demand signal
- Option C contradicts the implication of strong demand
