# Question

A trailing P/E ratio uses earnings from:

## Options

- **A**: The next 12 months (forecast)
- **B**: The past 12 months (actual)
- **C**: The average of past and future earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Trailing P/E: price divided by earnings per share from the most recent 12 months (TTM - trailing twelve months). Uses actual, reported earnings. Leading (forward) P/E uses forecast earnings for next 12 months. Trailing is based on known data; forward incorporates analyst expectations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes leading/forward P/E
- Option C is not a standard P/E calculation method
