# Question

Return on invested capital (ROIC) measures:

## Options

- **A**: Returns to equity holders only
- **B**: How efficiently a company generates profits from all invested capital (debt and equity)
- **C**: The dividend yield paid to shareholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ROIC = NOPAT / Invested Capital = Operating profit after tax / (Debt + Equity - Cash). Measures efficiency of all capital regardless of financing. Compare to WACC: ROIC > WACC means value creation. Better than ROE for comparing companies with different leverage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes ROE (equity only)
- Option C describes dividend yield, not return on capital
