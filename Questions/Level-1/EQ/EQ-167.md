# Question

In a rights offering, existing shareholders:

## Options

- **A**: Are required to purchase additional shares
- **B**: Are given the option to purchase new shares at a discount before the general public
- **C**: Must sell their current shares back to the company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rights offering: existing shareholders receive rights to buy new shares, typically at discount to market price, proportional to current holdings. Protects against dilution. Rights are usually transferable—can be sold if shareholder doesn't want to exercise. Not mandatory to purchase.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—rights can be exercised or sold, not mandatory
- Option C describes share buyback, not rights offering
