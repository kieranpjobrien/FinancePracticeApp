# Question

The random walk hypothesis suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Stock prices follow predictable patterns
- **B**: Future price changes are independent of past prices and cannot be predicted
- **C**: Stocks always increase in value over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Random walk: price changes are random, unpredictable based on past data. Each price change independent of previous. Implies technical analysis cannot generate abnormal returns. Related to weak-form efficiency. Doesn't mean prices are random—they reflect information—but changes are unpredictable.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts random walk
- Option C confuses randomness with long-term trend
