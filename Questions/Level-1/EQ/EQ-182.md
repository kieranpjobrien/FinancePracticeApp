# Question

Strong-form market efficiency implies that:

## Options

- **A**: Only historical prices are reflected in stock prices
- **B**: All information, including insider information, is reflected in prices
- **C**: Fundamental analysis can consistently generate abnormal returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strong-form efficiency: prices reflect all information—public and private. Even insider trading cannot generate abnormal returns. Most extreme form of EMH. Empirically rejected—insider trading regulations exist because insiders can profit. Useful as theoretical benchmark, not practical description.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes weak-form only
- Option C contradicts efficiency—implies exploitable information
