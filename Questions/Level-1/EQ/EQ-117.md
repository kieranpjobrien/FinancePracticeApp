# Question

A high accrual ratio (accruals relative to assets or earnings) may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: High-quality earnings backed by cash
- **B**: Lower earnings quality and potential earnings manipulation
- **C**: Strong operating cash flow generation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High accruals: earnings significantly exceed operating cash flow. May signal aggressive accounting—recognizing revenue early, deferring expenses, underprovisioning. Accrual-based earnings less persistent than cash-based. Analysts use accrual ratios as earnings quality red flag. Research shows high accruals predict lower future returns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—low accruals indicate quality
- Option C contradicts high accruals definition
