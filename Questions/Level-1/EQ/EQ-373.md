# Question

"Adjusted EBITDA" reported by companies may concern analysts because:

## Options

- **A**: It always understates profitability
- **B**: Companies may exclude legitimate recurring expenses to inflate the metric
- **C**: It is identical to GAAP net income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Adjusted EBITDA concerns: management discretion in adjustments. May exclude: stock-based compensation, restructuring costs, acquisition costs, litigation. Some adjustments reasonable; others remove real, recurring costs. Analysts should examine adjustments carefully, calculate their own adjusted metrics.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—adjusted EBITDA typically overstates, not understates
- Option C—Adjusted EBITDA differs significantly from GAAP income
