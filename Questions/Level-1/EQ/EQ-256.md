# Question

A dealer differs from a broker because a dealer:

## Options

- **A**: Only executes orders for clients
- **B**: Trades from their own inventory and takes principal risk
- **C**: Never quotes prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dealer: trades as principal from own account. Maintains inventory, quotes bid/ask prices, earns spread. Takes market risk on inventory. Provides liquidity by standing ready to buy or sell. Market makers are dealers. Broker-dealers can act in both capacities but must disclose.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes broker role
- Option C is opposite—dealers quote prices continuously
