# Question

In an order-driven market:

## Options

- **A**: Dealers set all prices
- **B**: Buy and sell orders are matched directly based on price and time priority
- **C**: Only institutional investors can participate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Order-driven market: orders from buyers and sellers collected in order book, matched by price-time priority (best price first, then earliest order). Electronic exchanges typically order-driven. May have designated market makers for liquidity. Examples: most modern stock exchanges.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes quote-driven markets
- Option C is incorrect—both retail and institutional can participate
