# Question

Convertible preferred stock gives holders the right to:

## Options

- **A**: Receive additional dividend payments
- **B**: Exchange preferred shares for common shares at a predetermined ratio
- **C**: Force the company to repurchase shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convertible preferred: can be converted to common stock at holder's option at specified conversion ratio. Combines preferred dividend stability with common stock upside potential. Conversion usually occurs when common stock price makes conversion valuable. Conversion is typically irrevocable.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cumulative or participating features
- Option C describes redeemable (puttable) preferred
