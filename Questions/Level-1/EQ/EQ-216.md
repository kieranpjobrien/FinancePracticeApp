# Question

A broad market index is designed to:

## Options

- **A**: Track only the largest 10 stocks in a market
- **B**: Represent the performance of an entire market or major market segment
- **C**: Measure performance of a single industry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Broad market indices: represent overall market or major segment (all large-cap, all domestic stocks). Examples: S&P 500 (US large-cap), Russell 3000 (US total market), MSCI World (global developed). Used as benchmarks, economic indicators, and index fund targets. Contrast with narrow or sector indices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a narrow, concentrated index
- Option C describes a sector or industry index
