# Question

A significant and persistent gap where net income exceeds operating cash flow may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: High-quality, sustainable earnings
- **B**: Aggressive revenue recognition or earnings management
- **C**: Conservative accounting policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash flow divergence: when NI consistently exceeds CFO, question arises—where's the cash? Possible issues: aggressive revenue recognition (booking sales before cash collection), capitalizing expenses, inventory buildup, receivables growth. Healthy companies should have CFO ≥ NI over time.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—quality earnings are cash-backed
- Option C would typically show CFO exceeding NI
