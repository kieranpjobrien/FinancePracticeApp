# Question

Large-cap stocks are typically characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Higher volatility and growth potential than small-caps
- **B**: Greater liquidity, more analyst coverage, and lower volatility than small-caps
- **C**: Higher expected returns due to lower risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Large-cap characteristics: high market value, greater liquidity (easier to trade large positions), more analyst coverage, institutional ownership, lower volatility. Trade-off: often lower growth potential than small-caps. Historically, small-cap premium compensates for higher risk/lower liquidity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes small-caps better
- Option C—lower risk typically means lower expected returns
