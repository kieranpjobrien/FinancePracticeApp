# Question

Cross-sectional regression analysis in equity valuation:

## Options

- **A**: Uses only historical data from a single company
- **B**: Relates valuation multiples to fundamental factors across many companies at a point in time
- **C**: Predicts exact future stock prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-sectional regression: analyzes relationship between multiples (P/E, P/B) and drivers (growth, risk, profitability) across a sample of companies. Estimates are: P/E = a + b(growth) + c(ROE) + d(risk). Produces justified multiple based on fundamental characteristics. Complements traditional comparables analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes time-series analysis of one firm
- Option C overstates precision—provides estimates, not predictions
