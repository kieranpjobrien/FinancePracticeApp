# Question

High customer concentration (one customer representing >10% of revenue) is a risk because:

## Options

- **A**: It guarantees stable, predictable revenue
- **B**: Loss of that customer could significantly impact the company's financial performance
- **C**: It indicates the company has too many customers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Customer concentration risk: dependence on few large customers creates vulnerability. Customer loss, pricing pressure, or payment issues severely impact results. Disclosure required for >10% customers. Diversified customer base provides stability. Evaluate customer relationships, contract terms, switching costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the risk of customer loss
- Option C is opposite—concentration means fewer, not more customers
