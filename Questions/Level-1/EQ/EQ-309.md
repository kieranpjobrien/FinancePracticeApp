# Question

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) measures:

## Options

- **A**: Individual company profitability
- **B**: Industry concentration based on market share distribution
- **C**: Stock price volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

HHI: sum of squared market shares of all firms in industry. Ranges from near 0 (fragmented) to 10,000 (monopoly with 100% share). Used by regulators to assess M&A competitive impact. HHI < 1,500 = unconcentrated, 1,500-2,500 = moderately concentrated, > 2,500 = highly concentrated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—HHI is industry-level, not company-specific
- Option C—HHI measures concentration, not volatility
