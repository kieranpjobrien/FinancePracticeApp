# Question

The PEG ratio is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Price-to-earnings ratio divided by expected earnings growth rate
- **B**: Price-to-book ratio divided by dividend growth rate
- **C**: Earnings per share divided by stock price

## Answer

A

## Explanation

PEG ratio: P/E ratio divided by expected earnings growth rate (typically 5-year growth). Attempts to adjust P/E for growth differences. PEG of 1 often considered fair value. Lower PEG suggests undervaluation relative to growth. Limitations: assumes linear P/E-growth relationship, sensitive to growth estimate accuracy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B confuses P/B ratio with P/E and uses wrong growth metric
- Option C describes earnings yield (inverse of P/E)
