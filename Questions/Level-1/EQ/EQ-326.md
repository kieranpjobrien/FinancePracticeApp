# Question

Industries with high economic sensitivity typically have:

## Options

- **A**: Lower beta and less volatile earnings
- **B**: Higher beta and more volatile earnings that move with economic cycles
- **C**: No correlation with GDP growth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Economic sensitivity: high beta industries—consumer discretionary, industrials, financials—amplify economic moves. Earnings volatile across cycles. Low sensitivity industries—utilities, healthcare, staples—more stable. Portfolio construction uses sector allocation to manage economic exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes low-sensitivity industries
- Option C ignores clear economic relationships
