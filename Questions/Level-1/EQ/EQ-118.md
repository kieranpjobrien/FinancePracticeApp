# Question

Dual-class share structures:

## Options

- **A**: Give all shareholders equal voting rights
- **B**: Create different classes with different voting rights, often allowing founders to maintain control
- **C**: Are prohibited in all major markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dual-class shares: different share classes with unequal voting rights. Typically founders/insiders hold high-vote shares; public holds low-vote shares. Allows raising capital while maintaining control. Concerns: reduced accountability, entrenchment. Some indices exclude dual-class companies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes single-class structure
- Option C is incorrect—many markets allow dual-class
