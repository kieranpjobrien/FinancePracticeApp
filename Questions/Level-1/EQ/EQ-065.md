# Question

Dividend yield is most useful for valuing:

## Options

- **A**: High-growth technology companies
- **B**: Mature, stable companies with consistent dividend payments
- **C**: Pre-revenue startups

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividend yield = Annual dividend / Current price. Most meaningful for dividend-paying stocks—utilities, REITs, mature companies with stable payouts. Less useful for growth companies reinvesting earnings or non-dividend payers. Income-focused investors prioritize dividend yield.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A typically reinvests earnings, low/no dividends
- Option C has no earnings or dividends
