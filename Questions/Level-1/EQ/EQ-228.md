# Question

If an analyst estimates a stock's intrinsic value is $50 and it currently trades at $40:

## Options

- **A**: The stock is overvalued by $10
- **B**: The stock is undervalued by $10 and may be a buy opportunity
- **C**: The analyst and market are always in perfect agreement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Intrinsic value vs. market price: undervaluation when intrinsic > market, overvaluation when intrinsic < market. Margin of safety: difference between intrinsic value and purchase price. Analyst believes $10 upside if valuation is correct. Investment decision depends on confidence in analysis and margin of safety required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship
- Option C contradicts the premise of active valuation
