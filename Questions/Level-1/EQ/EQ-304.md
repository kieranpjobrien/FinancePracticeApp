# Question

High institutional ownership of a stock typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: The stock is unsuitable for investment
- **B**: Professional investors have analyzed and approved the investment thesis
- **C**: The stock will definitely outperform

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Institutional ownership: holdings by mutual funds, pension funds, hedge funds. High institutional ownership suggests professional due diligence, meets investment criteria (size, liquidity, quality). Can increase liquidity, analyst coverage. However, crowded trades can reverse sharply. Monitor ownership changes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A implies negative signal—opposite typically true
- Option C—institutional approval doesn't guarantee performance
