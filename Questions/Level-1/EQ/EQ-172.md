# Question

A price-to-book ratio significantly below 1.0 may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: The market values the company's assets above their recorded book value
- **B**: The market believes book value overstates the true value, or the company is distressed
- **C**: The company has no tangible assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

P/B < 1: market price below book value. Possible reasons: assets overvalued on books (impairment needed), poor earnings prospects, financial distress, or market undervaluation. May signal value opportunity or value trap. Compare to peers and historical levels. Asset-heavy industries typically have lower P/B.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes P/B > 1
- Option C—would still have some book value from working capital
