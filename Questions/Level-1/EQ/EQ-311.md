# Question

Proxy voting allows shareholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Sell their shares at a premium
- **B**: Vote on corporate matters without attending shareholder meetings
- **C**: Override management on all decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proxy: shareholder authorizes another party to vote shares on their behalf. Enables participation without physical attendance. Matters voted: board elections, auditor ratification, executive compensation, shareholder proposals. Institutional investors increasingly use proxy voting for corporate governance engagement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a sale transaction, not voting
- Option C overstates—shareholders vote on specific matters, not daily management
