# Question

Reverse DCF analysis is useful for determining:

## Options

- **A**: The historical dividend payments of a company
- **B**: The growth rate or other assumptions implied by the current market price
- **C**: The exact future stock price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reverse DCF: instead of inputting assumptions to calculate value, uses current price to solve for implied assumptions. What growth rate justifies this price? What margin is embedded? Helps assess whether market expectations are reasonable. Compares implied expectations to analyst's own forecasts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is backward-looking, not what reverse DCF does
- Option C—no method can determine exact future prices
