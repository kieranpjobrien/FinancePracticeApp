# Question

The embryonic (pioneer) stage of the industry lifecycle is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Stable profits and predictable cash flows
- **B**: Slow initial growth, high uncertainty, and significant investment in product development
- **C**: Consolidation among existing players

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Embryonic stage: new industry, products still developing, customer acceptance uncertain. High failure rates, negative cash flows common. Focus on R&D and market development. Few competitors initially. Revenue growth may be slow as market develops. High risk but potentially high reward.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes mature stage
- Option C describes shakeout or maturity
