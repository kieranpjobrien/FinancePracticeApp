# Question

Common stockholders are considered residual claimants because:

## Options

- **A**: They are first to receive payments in bankruptcy
- **B**: They receive what remains after all other obligations are satisfied
- **C**: They have no claim to company assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Residual claim: common shareholders are last in priority—after creditors, bondholders, and preferred shareholders. In liquidation, receive remaining assets (if any) after all others paid. In operations, entitled to residual profits after interest, taxes, preferred dividends. Higher risk, higher potential return.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses priority—common is last, not first
- Option C—common shareholders do have claims, just lowest priority
