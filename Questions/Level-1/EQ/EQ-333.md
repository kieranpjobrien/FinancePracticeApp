# Question

Self-attribution bias causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Attribute all outcomes to luck
- **B**: Credit successes to their own skill while blaming failures on external factors
- **C**: Accurately assess their investment performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Self-attribution: taking credit for good outcomes (skill) while blaming bad outcomes on luck, market conditions, or others. Hinders learning from mistakes. Leads to overconfidence. Common in investing where randomness makes causation difficult to assess. Related to illusion of control.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite pattern
- Option C describes unbiased self-assessment
