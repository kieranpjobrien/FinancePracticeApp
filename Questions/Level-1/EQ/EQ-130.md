# Question

A company's cash conversion cycle is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Days inventory outstanding + Days sales outstanding - Days payable outstanding
- **B**: Days inventory outstanding - Days sales outstanding + Days payable outstanding
- **C**: Days payable outstanding - Days inventory outstanding - Days sales outstanding

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cash conversion cycle (CCC): DIO + DSO - DPO. Measures time between paying for inventory and collecting cash from sales. Lower/negative CCC means faster cash generation. Negative CCC possible when company collects from customers before paying suppliers (e.g., some retailers).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B incorrectly subtracts DSO and adds DPO
- Option C reverses all components
