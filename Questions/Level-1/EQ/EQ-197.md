# Question

In a price-weighted index, the stock with the highest influence is the one with:

## Options

- **A**: The largest market capitalization
- **B**: The highest stock price
- **C**: The highest trading volume

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price-weighted index: simple average of stock prices divided by divisor. Higher-priced stocks have more influence regardless of company size. Example: $200 stock moves index twice as much as $100 stock. Dow Jones Industrial Average is price-weighted. Stock splits affect weights.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market-cap weighted indices
- Option C—trading volume doesn't affect price-weighted calculation
