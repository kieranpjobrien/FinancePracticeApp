# Question

Preemptive rights protect existing shareholders by:

## Options

- **A**: Guaranteeing a minimum dividend
- **B**: Giving them the right to purchase new shares proportionally before public offering
- **C**: Preventing the company from issuing any new shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Preemptive rights: existing shareholders can maintain proportional ownership when new shares issued. Protects against dilution of ownership and voting power. Rights offerings implement this. Not universal—some jurisdictions require, others make optional. Common in Europe, less so in U.S.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes dividend rights, not preemptive rights
- Option C is too restrictive—doesn't prevent issuance, just protects proportion
