# Question

Free cash flow to the firm (FCFF) represents cash available to:

## Options

- **A**: Only common shareholders after all obligations
- **B**: All capital providers, including debt and equity holders
- **C**: Only bondholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FCFF: cash generated by operations available to all capital providers (debt holders, preferred shareholders, common shareholders). FCFF = EBIT(1-t) + Depreciation - FCInv - WCInv. Discounted at WACC to value entire firm. FCFE is after debt obligations, for equity only.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes FCFE (Free Cash Flow to Equity)
- Option C ignores equity holders who also have claims on FCFF
