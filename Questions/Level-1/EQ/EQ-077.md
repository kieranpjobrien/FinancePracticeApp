# Question

A reverse stock split is typically used by companies to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the number of shares outstanding
- **B**: Raise the share price to meet exchange listing requirements or improve perception
- **C**: Distribute additional shares to existing shareholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reverse split: reduces shares outstanding, increases price per share proportionally. 1-for-10: every 10 shares become 1 share at 10x the price. Used to avoid delisting (minimum price requirements) or improve institutional investor perception. Often seen as negative signal about company prospects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes regular stock splits
- Option C describes stock dividends
