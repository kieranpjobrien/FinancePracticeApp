# Question

The primary market refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Where existing securities are traded between investors
- **B**: Where new securities are first issued and sold to investors
- **C**: The largest stock exchange in a country

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Primary market: where issuers sell new securities directly to investors, raising capital. Includes IPOs (initial public offerings) and seasoned offerings. Proceeds go to issuing company. Secondary market is where existing securities trade between investors—issuer receives no proceeds from secondary trades.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes secondary market
- Option C confuses "primary" with "main" or "largest"
