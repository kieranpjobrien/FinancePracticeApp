# Question

Cumulative preferred stock protects investors because:

## Options

- **A**: It guarantees dividend payments every quarter
- **B**: Missed dividends accumulate and must be paid before common dividends can be paid
- **C**: It cannot be called by the issuer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cumulative feature: if preferred dividends are missed, they accumulate as "dividends in arrears." All arrears must be paid before any common dividends. Non-cumulative preferred loses missed dividends permanently. Cumulative provides better creditor-like protection for preferred shareholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—no dividend is truly guaranteed, even preferred
- Option C describes non-callable feature, separate from cumulative
