# Question

Using CAPM, a stock with beta of 1.3, risk-free rate of 3%, and market risk premium of 6% has a required return of:

## Options

- **A**: 9.0%
- **B**: 10.8%
- **C**: 7.8%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CAPM: r = Rf + β(Rm - Rf) = 3% + 1.3(6%) = 3% + 7.8% = 10.8%. Beta measures systematic risk exposure. Higher beta means higher required return. Market risk premium = expected market return minus risk-free rate. Used as discount rate in equity valuation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A forgets to add risk-free rate to risk premium (just 3% + 6%)
- Option C only calculates the beta-adjusted premium without Rf
