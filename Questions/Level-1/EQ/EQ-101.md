# Question

Crossing networks differ from traditional exchanges in that they:

## Options

- **A**: Provide continuous price discovery
- **B**: Match orders at prices determined elsewhere, typically at specified times
- **C**: Always offer better prices than exchanges

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Crossing networks: match buy and sell orders internally, typically at midpoint of exchange bid-ask or closing price. No price discovery—use external reference prices. Execute at set times (not continuous). Reduce market impact for large orders. Examples: internal broker crosses, institutional networks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—crossing networks don't discover prices
- Option C is not guaranteed—prices depend on external markets
