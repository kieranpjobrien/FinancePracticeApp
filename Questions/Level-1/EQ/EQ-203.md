# Question

The disposition effect causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Hold winning stocks and sell losing stocks appropriately
- **B**: Sell winning stocks too early and hold losing stocks too long
- **C**: Buy more stocks during market downturns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Disposition effect: tendency to sell winners prematurely (locking in gains) while holding losers (avoiding regret of realizing loss). Driven by loss aversion and mental accounting. Tax-inefficient—should do opposite (harvest losses, let winners run). Well-documented in trading data studies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational tax-aware behavior
- Option C describes contrarian investing, not disposition effect
