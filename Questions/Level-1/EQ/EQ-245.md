# Question

Enterprise value to invested capital (EV/IC) ratio is most useful for:

## Options

- **A**: Comparing companies with similar capital intensity
- **B**: Assessing whether markets are valuing invested capital above or below replacement cost
- **C**: Calculating dividend yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EV/IC ratio: compares market value of enterprise to book value of invested capital. EV/IC > 1 means market values company above its capital investment—implies positive NPV projects, competitive advantage. Similar to market-to-book for firm value. Useful for capital-intensive industries.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—more relevant for absolute comparisons
- Option C is unrelated to EV/IC ratio
