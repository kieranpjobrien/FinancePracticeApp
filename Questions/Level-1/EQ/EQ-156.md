# Question

Float-adjusted market capitalization weighting excludes shares that are:

## Options

- **A**: Traded on foreign exchanges
- **B**: Closely held and not available for public trading
- **C**: Recently issued through secondary offerings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Float-adjusted cap weighting: uses only freely tradable shares, excluding strategic holdings (insiders, governments, cross-holdings). More representative of investable market. Reduces weight of companies with low float. Most major indices (S&P 500, MSCI) now use float-adjusted methodology.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—foreign-listed shares may be included if freely tradable
- Option C—new issues are typically included if publicly available
