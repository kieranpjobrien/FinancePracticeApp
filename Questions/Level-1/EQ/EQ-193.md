# Question

In a short sale, an investor:

## Options

- **A**: Buys shares expecting the price to rise
- **B**: Borrows shares to sell, hoping to repurchase at a lower price
- **C**: Holds shares for an extended period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Short selling: borrow shares from broker, sell immediately, buy back later to return shares. Profit if price falls (sell high, buy low). Risks: unlimited loss potential (price can rise indefinitely), borrowing costs, dividend payments to lender, short squeeze risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a long position
- Option C describes buy-and-hold investing
