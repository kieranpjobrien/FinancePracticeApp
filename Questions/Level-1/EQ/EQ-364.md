# Question

Opening and closing auctions at stock exchanges:

## Options

- **A**: Have no impact on price discovery
- **B**: Aggregate orders to determine a single opening/closing price with maximum volume execution
- **C**: Only allow limit orders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Call auctions: collect orders over period, then execute at single price maximizing matched volume. Opening auction sets day's first price; closing sets final (benchmark) price. Important for: index funds (track closing prices), derivatives settlement, benchmark-sensitive trades.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores important price discovery function
- Option C—both market and limit orders participate
