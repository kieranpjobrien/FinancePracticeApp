# Question

Asset-based valuation is most appropriate for:

## Options

- **A**: High-growth technology companies
- **B**: Companies being liquidated or with significant tangible assets relative to earnings
- **C**: Service companies with minimal physical assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset-based valuation: values company based on assets minus liabilities. Most relevant for: liquidation scenarios, asset-heavy companies (real estate, natural resources), investment companies, or when earnings don't reflect asset values. Less useful when value comes from intangibles or growth potential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A has value in growth, not assets
- Option C lacks tangible assets to value
