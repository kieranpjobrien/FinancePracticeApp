# Question

Overconfidence bias leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Underestimate their knowledge and trade too infrequently
- **B**: Overestimate their ability to pick stocks and trade excessively
- **C**: Always follow professional advice

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Overconfidence: investors believe their information or analysis is superior. Results in excessive trading, underdiversification (concentrated bets), and underestimation of risk. Studies show overconfident investors underperform. Men typically more overconfident than women in investing contexts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—overconfidence leads to more trading
- Option C contradicts overconfident self-reliance
