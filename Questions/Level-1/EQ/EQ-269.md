# Question

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors are increasingly relevant to equity analysis because:

## Options

- **A**: They guarantee higher investment returns
- **B**: They can impact long-term value through risks and opportunities not captured in traditional analysis
- **C**: Regulations require all investors to use ESG criteria

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ESG integration: considers environmental risks (climate, pollution), social factors (labor, community), governance (board, ethics) alongside financial analysis. Can identify risks (regulatory, reputational) and opportunities (clean tech, stakeholder loyalty). Growing investor demand, but no guaranteed outperformance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates—ESG doesn't guarantee returns
- Option C—ESG use growing but not universally mandated
