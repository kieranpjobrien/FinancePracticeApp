# Question

The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio measures:

## Options

- **A**: The price investors pay per dollar of earnings
- **B**: The company's total revenue
- **C**: The dividend payout ratio

## Answer

A

## Explanation

P/E ratio = Stock price / Earnings per share. Shows how much investors pay for each dollar of earnings. Higher P/E may indicate growth expectations or overvaluation; lower P/E may suggest value or concerns. Most widely used valuation multiple. Can use trailing or forward earnings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes revenue, not a valuation ratio
- Option C is dividends/earnings, a different ratio
