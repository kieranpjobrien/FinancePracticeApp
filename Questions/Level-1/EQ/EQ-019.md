# Question

The Gordon Growth Model (constant growth DDM) assumes:

## Options

- **A**: Dividends will fluctuate randomly
- **B**: Dividends grow at a constant rate forever, with growth rate less than required return
- **C**: The company will never pay dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Gordon Growth Model: V₀ = D₁ / (r - g), where D₁ is next year's dividend, r is required return, g is constant growth rate. Requires g < r for formula to work. Assumes stable, mature company with predictable dividend growth. Simple but sensitive to input assumptions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the constant growth assumption
- Option C makes the model inapplicable
