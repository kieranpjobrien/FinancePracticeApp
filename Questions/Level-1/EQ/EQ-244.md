# Question

If a company's capex consistently equals depreciation, this suggests:

## Options

- **A**: The company is investing heavily for growth
- **B**: The company is maintaining but not growing its asset base
- **C**: The company is neglecting maintenance of its assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capex = Depreciation: replacement of assets as they wear out. Asset base remains constant. Capex > Depreciation indicates growth investment. Capex < Depreciation (over extended period) may indicate underinvestment, asset base decline, or shift to asset-light model.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would require Capex significantly exceeding depreciation
- Option C would show Capex persistently below depreciation
