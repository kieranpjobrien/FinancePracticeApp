# Question

A company has ROE of 15%, pays out 40% of earnings as dividends. Its sustainable growth rate is:

## Options

- **A**: 6%
- **B**: 9%
- **C**: 15%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sustainable growth rate = ROE × Retention ratio = ROE × (1 - Payout ratio) = 15% × (1 - 0.40) = 15% × 0.60 = 9%. This represents the maximum growth rate achievable without external equity financing, assuming constant ROE and capital structure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses payout ratio instead of retention ratio (15% × 0.40)
- Option C ignores retention—would require 100% earnings retention
