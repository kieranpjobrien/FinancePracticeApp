# Question

Sell-side analyst research reports typically:

## Options

- **A**: Are produced by institutional investors for internal use
- **B**: Are produced by brokerage firms and provided to investing clients
- **C**: Are confidential and never shared externally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sell-side research: produced by broker-dealers, investment banks. Distributed to buy-side clients (mutual funds, hedge funds) as value-added service. Includes company analysis, earnings estimates, price targets, recommendations. Potential conflicts (banking relationships) require disclosure. Contrast with buy-side (internal) research.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes buy-side research
- Option C is opposite—sell-side research is distributed widely
