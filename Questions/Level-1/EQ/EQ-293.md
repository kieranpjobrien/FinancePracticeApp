# Question

Diluted EPS is lower than basic EPS when:

## Options

- **A**: The company has no dilutive securities
- **B**: In-the-money stock options or convertible securities would increase shares outstanding
- **C**: The company repurchases shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Diluted EPS: includes impact of all potentially dilutive securities—stock options, convertibles, warrants—if exercised/converted. When in-the-money, these increase share count, reducing EPS. Treasury stock method for options, if-converted method for convertibles. Shows potential worst-case dilution.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—no dilutive securities means diluted = basic EPS
- Option C—repurchases reduce shares, increasing EPS
