# Question

Order internalization occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: All orders are routed to public exchanges
- **B**: A broker-dealer fills customer orders from its own inventory instead of routing to exchanges
- **C**: Orders are cancelled before execution

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Internalization: broker executes customer order against own inventory or matching with other customer flow. Doesn't go to public exchange. Potential benefits: price improvement, speed. Concerns: less public price discovery, potential conflicts. Regulated to ensure best execution maintained.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite of internalization
- Option C describes order cancellation, not internalization
