# Question

The Russell 2000 Index primarily represents:

## Options

- **A**: The 2000 largest US companies
- **B**: US small-cap stocks (bottom 2000 of the Russell 3000)
- **C**: International emerging market stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Russell 2000: measures small-cap US equity performance. Comprises smallest 2000 stocks in Russell 3000 Index. Widely used small-cap benchmark. Russell 1000 covers large-cap (top 1000). Together, Russell 1000 + Russell 2000 = Russell 3000 (broad US market). Reconstituted annually.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would include large and mid-cap stocks
- Option C describes indices like MSCI EM
