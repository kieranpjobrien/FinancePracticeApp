# Question

In real estate capital structures, the equity investor:

## Options

- **A**: Has first claim on property cash flows and sale proceeds
- **B**: Receives residual cash flows after debt service and has last claim
- **C**: Never participates in property appreciation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate capital structure:

**Debt characteristics:**
- First claim on cash flows
- Contractual interest payments
- Secured by property
- Priority in liquidation

**Equity characteristics:**
- Residual claimant
- After debt payments
- Last in liquidation
- All upside participation

**Cash flow waterfall:**
1. Operating expenses
2. Debt service (interest + principal)
3. Equity distributions
4. Remaining to equity

**On sale:**
1. Selling costs
2. Outstanding debt payoff
3. Remaining to equity

**Equity benefits:**
- All appreciation upside
- Tax benefits (depreciation)
- Control over property

**Equity risks:**
- First to absorb losses
- No guaranteed return
- Illiquid investment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Debt has first claim, not equity
- Option C: Equity gets all appreciation
