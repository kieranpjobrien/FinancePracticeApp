# Question

The typical fee structure for alternative investments includes:

## Options

- **A**: Only a flat management fee
- **B**: A management fee plus a performance fee
- **C**: No fees, as returns compensate managers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Alternative investment fee structures:

**Two-part fee structure:**
1. Management fee: Annual % of assets
2. Performance fee: % of profits above hurdle

**Common structures:**
- Private equity: "2 and 20" (2% management, 20% carried interest)
- Hedge funds: Similar 2 and 20 structure
- Real estate: Varies, often lower base

**Fee components explained:**
- Management fee: Covers operations, salaries
- Performance fee: Aligns manager/investor interests
- Hurdle rate: Minimum return before performance fee

**Why higher fees?**
- Active management intensive
- Specialized expertise required
- Limited competition for capital

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Performance fees are standard
- Option C: Alternatives have highest fees in industry
