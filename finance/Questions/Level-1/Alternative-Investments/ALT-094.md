# Question

Hedge funds typically use leverage to:

## Options

- **A**: Reduce portfolio risk and volatility
- **B**: Amplify returns from their investment strategies
- **C**: Comply with regulatory requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leverage in hedge funds:

**Purpose of leverage:**
- Amplify returns
- Increase capital efficiency
- Access more opportunities
- Enhance small edge strategies

**Sources of leverage:**
- Prime broker margin
- Derivatives (embedded)
- Repo financing
- Bank credit lines

**Strategy variation:**
- Macro: 3-10x common
- Relative value: 5-15x+
- Long/short equity: 1-2x
- Managed futures: Varies

**Risk amplification:**
- Gains magnified
- Losses magnified equally
- Margin calls possible
- Forced selling risk

**LTCM example:**
- 25x+ leverage
- Near-collapse 1998
- Systemic risk concerns

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Leverage increases, not reduces risk
- Option C: Leverage is choice, not requirement
