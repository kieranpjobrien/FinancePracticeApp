# Question

In private equity distribution waterfalls, the order of payments typically prioritizes:

## Options

- **A**: Performance fees before any return of capital
- **B**: Return of capital to LPs, then preferred return, then profit sharing
- **C**: Equal splits from the first dollar of distributions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Distribution waterfall:

**Typical order:**
1. Return of capital to LPs
2. Preferred return to LPs (hurdle)
3. GP catch-up (if applicable)
4. Carried interest split (80/20)

**Return of capital:**
- LPs get back invested capital first
- Before any profit sharing
- Principal protection priority

**Preferred return:**
- Typically 8% compounded
- LPs receive before GP carry
- Hurdle rate concept

**GP catch-up:**
- GP receives larger share
- Until proportional with carry %
- Not in all structures

**Profit sharing:**
- After hurdle/catch-up
- Typically 80% LP / 20% GP
- Carried interest to GP

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Capital return comes first
- Option C: Waterfall is sequential, not equal
