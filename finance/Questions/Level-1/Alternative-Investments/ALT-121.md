# Question

Transparency in alternative investments is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Greater than for publicly traded securities
- **B**: Limited compared to public markets due to less regulatory disclosure
- **C**: Identical across all investment types

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Alternative investment transparency:

**Why less transparent:**
- Private placements exempt
- No SEC/public filing requirements
- Proprietary strategies protected
- Infrequent valuations

**What investors may receive:**
- Quarterly/annual reports
- Capital account statements
- Audited financials (annual)
- Manager letters

**What's often not disclosed:**
- Individual position holdings
- Real-time NAV
- Detailed strategy mechanics
- Counterparty exposures

**Investor challenges:**
- Verify performance claims
- Assess true risk
- Aggregate across managers
- Portfolio monitoring

**Improving trends:**
- Institutional investor demands
- Better technology
- Risk reporting standards
- Manager competition

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Public markets more transparent
- Option C: Wide variation in transparency
