# Question

Global macro hedge fund strategies are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Bottom-up analysis of individual securities
- **B**: Top-down views on economies, currencies, and interest rates
- **C**: Exclusive focus on equity markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Global macro strategy:

**Approach:**
- Top-down economic analysis
- Macroeconomic forecasting
- Cross-asset class trading
- Directional bets

**Instruments traded:**
- Currencies
- Interest rates/bonds
- Equity indices
- Commodities

**Types of positions:**
- Currency appreciation/depreciation
- Yield curve trades
- Country equity allocation
- Commodity trends

**Famous examples:**
- Soros: British pound (1992)
- Paulson: Subprime crisis
- Major economic event positioning

**Characteristics:**
- Highly liquid positions
- Concentrated bets
- High conviction trades
- Can have large gains/losses

**Risk profile:**
- Can be volatile
- Dependent on macro calls
- Tail risk both directions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is bottom-up, not macro
- Option C: Multi-asset, not just equities
