# Question

Commodity inventory levels are important market indicators because:

## Options

- **A**: They have no relationship to prices
- **B**: High inventories suggest oversupply and potential price weakness
- **C**: Inventories never change from week to week

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inventory analysis:

**Supply indicator:**
- High inventories = oversupply
- Low inventories = tightness
- Trend matters
- Regional variations

**Price relationship:**
- Rising inventories → price pressure
- Falling inventories → price support
- Not always immediate
- Market expectations matter

**Key inventory reports:**
- EIA (energy)
- USDA (agriculture)
- LME (metals)
- Weekly releases

**Interpretation:**
- Compare to historical
- Seasonally adjust
- Consider demand trends
- Supply disruption context

**Market reaction:**
- Surprise element matters
- Expectations vs actual
- Immediate price moves
- Futures curve impact

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Strong price relationship
- Option C: Inventories change constantly
