# Question

Cash-on-cash return in real estate measures:

## Options

- **A**: Total property value appreciation
- **B**: Annual cash flow divided by total cash invested
- **C**: The property's cap rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash-on-cash return:

**Formula:**
Cash-on-Cash = Annual Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested

**Cash flow used:**
- After debt service
- Net cash to equity investor
- Before taxes typically

**Example:**
- Equity invested: $200,000
- Annual cash flow: $16,000
- Cash-on-cash: 8%

**What it measures:**
- Current income yield
- On equity invested
- Levered return
- Actual cash return

**Relationship to leverage:**
- More leverage can increase CoC
- If debt cost < property return
- Risk also increases

**Limitations:**
- Ignores appreciation
- Ignores principal paydown
- Point-in-time measure

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is capital appreciation
- Option C: Cap rate is unlevered, different metric
