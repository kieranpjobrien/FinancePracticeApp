# Question

Infrastructure investments may provide inflation protection through:

## Options

- **A**: Fixed long-term pricing with no adjustment mechanisms
- **B**: Revenue contracts or regulations that include inflation-linked escalators
- **C**: Avoiding any connection to economic conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Infrastructure inflation linkage:

**Explicit mechanisms:**
- CPI-linked toll increases
- Inflation-adjusted tariffs
- Cost pass-through provisions
- Regulatory rate adjustments

**Examples:**
- Toll roads: CPI + 1% escalation
- Utilities: Periodic rate reviews
- Power contracts: Index-linked pricing

**Implicit protection:**
- Replacement cost rises with inflation
- Asset values follow
- Pricing power increases

**Regulated utilities:**
- Allowed returns on rate base
- Rate base grows with capex
- Inflation increases costs
- Rates adjusted accordingly

**Why investors value this:**
- Real return preservation
- Long asset lives match
- Liability matching for pensions
- Diversification benefit

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Fixed pricing doesn't protect
- Option C: Economic linkage is the protection
