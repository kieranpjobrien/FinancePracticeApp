# Question

Concentration risk in alternative investment portfolios refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Having too many different investments
- **B**: Overexposure to a single manager, strategy, or sector
- **C**: Investing only in liquid securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Concentration risk:

**Forms of concentration:**
- Single manager exposure
- Strategy concentration
- Sector/industry focus
- Vintage year clustering
- Geographic concentration

**Why it matters:**
- Amplified losses if wrong
- Correlation increases
- Less diversification benefit
- Manager-specific risk

**Mitigation approaches:**
- Manager diversification
- Strategy diversification
- Vintage year spreading
- Geographic allocation

**Practical limits:**
- Minimum investment sizes
- Access constraints
- Due diligence capacity
- Relationship management

**Guidelines:**
- Limit per manager (5-10%)
- Limit per strategy
- Monitor aggregate exposure
- Stress test scenarios

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Concentration is too few, not too many
- Option C: Describes liquidity, not concentration
