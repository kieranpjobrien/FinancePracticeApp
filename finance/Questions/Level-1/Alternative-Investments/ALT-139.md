# Question

ESG considerations in infrastructure investing are particularly relevant because:

## Options

- **A**: ESG factors have no impact on infrastructure investments
- **B**: Infrastructure assets have long lives and significant environmental/social impacts
- **C**: All infrastructure investments are automatically ESG-compliant

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ESG in infrastructure:

**Why particularly relevant:**
- 30-50+ year asset lives
- Large environmental footprint
- Community impact
- Regulatory exposure

**Environmental factors:**
- Emissions and pollution
- Climate resilience
- Resource usage
- Transition risk (fossil fuels)

**Social factors:**
- Community relations
- Labor practices
- Safety records
- Access and affordability

**Governance factors:**
- Regulatory compliance
- Anti-corruption
- Stakeholder engagement
- Transparency

**Impact on value:**
- Regulatory risk mitigation
- Social license to operate
- Climate resilience
- Long-term sustainability

**Growing investor focus:**
- Climate commitments
- Net-zero targets
- Sustainable infrastructure funds

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: ESG highly relevant for infrastructure
- Option C: ESG compliance must be evaluated
