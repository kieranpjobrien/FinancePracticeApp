# Question

Geopolitical risk in commodity markets can lead to:

## Options

- **A**: Permanently stable prices
- **B**: Supply disruptions and price volatility
- **C**: Elimination of all commodity demand

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Geopolitical impacts:

**Supply disruption sources:**
- Wars and conflicts
- Sanctions
- Trade disputes
- Political instability
- Export restrictions

**Historical examples:**
- Oil embargoes
- Russia-Ukraine conflict
- OPEC decisions
- Trade wars

**Affected commodities:**
- Oil and gas
- Metals (rare earths)
- Agricultural products
- Critical minerals

**Price impacts:**
- Immediate spikes
- Volatility increase
- Risk premium
- Supply fears

**Risk premium:**
- Priced into commodities
- Varies by security situation
- Geopolitical events add premium
- Uncertainty premium

**Investor implications:**
- Portfolio diversification
- Risk management
- Opportunity identification
- Scenario planning

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Causes instability
- Option C: Demand persists, supply affected
