# Question

Merchant power plants face revenue risk because:

## Options

- **A**: All electricity is sold through long-term fixed-price contracts
- **B**: Power is sold at fluctuating spot market prices without contracted offtake
- **C**: The government guarantees minimum electricity prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Merchant power risk:

**Merchant vs contracted:**
- Merchant: Spot market sales
- Contracted: Fixed price PPAs
- Merchant = More volatile revenue
- Higher risk, higher potential return

**Spot price drivers:**
- Supply/demand balance
- Fuel prices
- Weather conditions
- Grid constraints
- Time of day/season

**Risk factors:**
- Price volatility
- New generation competition
- Fuel cost exposure
- Capacity factor uncertainty

**Valuation impact:**
- Higher discount rates
- More conservative projections
- Scenario analysis important
- Terminal value sensitivity

**Mitigation:**
- Partial hedging
- Virtual PPAs
- Financial hedges
- Fuel cost pass-through

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No contracts = merchant
- Option C: No price guarantees typically
