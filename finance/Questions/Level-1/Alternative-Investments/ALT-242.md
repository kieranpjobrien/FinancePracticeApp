# Question

The largest institutional investors in alternative investments include:

## Options

- **A**: Only retail individual investors
- **B**: Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, endowments, and insurance companies
- **C**: Exclusively commercial banks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Institutional investors:

**Pension funds:**
- Long time horizons
- Large capital pools
- Illiquidity tolerance
- Major PE/real estate allocators

**Sovereign wealth funds:**
- Government capital
- Very long-term
- Significant size
- Global reach

**Endowments/foundations:**
- Perpetual capital
- Yale model influence
- High alternatives allocation
- Return requirements

**Insurance companies:**
- Asset-liability matching
- Long-dated liabilities
- Credit/real estate focus
- Regulatory constraints

**Family offices:**
- High net worth
- Flexible mandates
- Long-term view
- Growing allocators

**Allocation levels:**
- Can exceed 50% for some
- Growing over time
- Varies by institution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Primarily institutional
- Option C: Banks are intermediaries
