# Question

Availability-based infrastructure contracts provide revenue:

## Options

- **A**: Based on actual user volumes and traffic
- **B**: For keeping the asset available regardless of usage levels
- **C**: Only when the asset generates maximum capacity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Availability-based payments:

**How it works:**
- Fixed payments for availability
- Asset must meet standards
- Not dependent on usage
- Government/authority pays

**Key features:**
- Predictable revenue stream
- Volume risk transferred to authority
- Performance standards required
- Deductions for unavailability

**Common examples:**
- Shadow toll roads
- Hospital PPPs
- School buildings
- Government buildings

**Contrast with demand-based:**
- Demand-based: User-pays (toll roads)
- Availability-based: Authority pays
- Different risk profiles

**Investor implications:**
- Lower revenue risk
- More predictable cash flows
- Counterparty credit matters
- Inflation protection often included

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes demand/volume-based
- Option C: Available at standard capacity required
