# Question

For private equity funds, since-inception IRR measures:

## Options

- **A**: Returns only in the most recent quarter
- **B**: The annualized return from fund inception to the measurement date
- **C**: Expected future returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Since-inception IRR:

**Definition:**
- From first capital call to present
- Includes all cash flows
- Annualized return rate
- Standard PE metric

**Calculation includes:**
- All capital calls (negative)
- All distributions (positive)
- Current NAV (positive)
- Time-weighted by timing

**Why used:**
- Captures full fund history
- Accounts for cash flow timing
- Comparable across funds
- Industry standard

**Interpretation:**
- Higher is better
- Compare to vintage peers
- Consider fund age
- Unrealized portion uncertain

**Limitations:**
- NAV is estimated
- Early years misleading
- Assumes reinvestment at IRR

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Full fund life, not just recent
- Option C: Measures historical, not expected
