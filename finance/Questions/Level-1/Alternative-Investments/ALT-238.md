# Question

In infrastructure valuation, the weighted average cost of capital (WACC) typically:

## Options

- **A**: Is higher than for comparable corporate investments
- **B**: Is lower due to stable cash flows, contracted revenues, and lower business risk
- **C**: Is identical to equity returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Infrastructure WACC:

**Why lower WACC:**
- Stable, predictable cash flows
- Long-term contracts
- Regulated returns
- Lower business risk
- Essential service demand

**Components:**
- Cost of equity (lower beta)
- Cost of debt (investment grade)
- Tax shield
- Capital structure

**Typical ranges:**
- Core infrastructure: 6-8%
- Value-add: 8-10%
- Opportunistic: 10-12%+
- Varies by risk profile

**Lower beta factors:**
- Inelastic demand
- Contracted revenues
- Regulated returns
- Low correlation with economy

**Debt advantages:**
- Long-dated available
- Investment grade ratings
- Low spreads
- Matches asset life

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Lower, not higher than corporate
- Option C: WACC blends debt and equity
