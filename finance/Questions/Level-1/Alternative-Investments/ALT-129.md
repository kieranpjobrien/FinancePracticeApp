# Question

During market crises, correlations between alternative investments and traditional assets:

## Options

- **A**: Always decrease, providing perfect diversification
- **B**: Often increase, reducing diversification benefits when most needed
- **C**: Remain perfectly stable and predictable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Crisis correlation behavior:

**The phenomenon:**
- Correlations increase in stress
- "All correlations go to 1"
- Diversification fails when needed
- Flight to quality/liquidity

**Why it happens:**
- Forced selling across assets
- Liquidity withdrawal
- Risk-off behavior
- Margin calls

**Alternative investment examples:**
- 2008: Many hedge funds correlated
- Real estate declined with stocks
- Commodity crash
- Credit spreads widened

**Exceptions:**
- Managed futures (sometimes)
- Tail risk strategies
- True market neutral
- Treasuries

**Implications:**
- Don't rely on historical correlations
- Stress test portfolios
- Liquidity planning essential
- Crisis alpha strategies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Correlations increase, not decrease
- Option C: Correlations are unstable
