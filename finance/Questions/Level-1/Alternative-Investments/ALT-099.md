# Question

Quantitative or systematic hedge fund strategies primarily rely on:

## Options

- **A**: Subjective judgment of portfolio managers
- **B**: Computer models and algorithms to make investment decisions
- **C**: Only fundamental analysis of company financials

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quantitative strategies:

**Characteristics:**
- Model-driven decisions
- Algorithmic execution
- Data-intensive
- Systematic rules

**Types of quant strategies:**
- Statistical arbitrage
- Factor investing
- High-frequency trading
- Machine learning/AI

**Data inputs:**
- Price and volume data
- Fundamental data
- Alternative data
- Sentiment indicators

**Advantages:**
- Removes emotional bias
- Consistent execution
- Scalable across markets
- Backtesting possible

**Challenges:**
- Model risk
- Crowded trades
- Regime changes
- Data quality issues

**Key players:**
- Renaissance Technologies
- Two Sigma
- DE Shaw
- Citadel (systematic)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Systematic minimizes subjective judgment
- Option C: Uses quantitative, not just fundamental data
