# Question

In a limited partnership structure for alternative investments, the general partner (GP):

## Options

- **A**: Provides most of the capital but has no management role
- **B**: Manages the fund and has unlimited liability
- **C**: Can only lose their initial investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GP/LP structure in alternatives:

**General Partner (GP):**
- Manages the fund
- Makes investment decisions
- Has unlimited personal liability
- Typically invests 1-5% of capital

**Limited Partners (LPs):**
- Provide majority of capital (95-99%)
- Passive investors
- Liability limited to investment
- No management role

**GP responsibilities:**
- Source deals
- Conduct due diligence
- Monitor investments
- Execute exits
- Report to LPs

**Why this structure?**
- Separates management from capital
- Aligns incentives (GP co-invest)
- Limits LP exposure
- Tax efficiency (pass-through)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes LPs, not GP
- Option C: Also describes LPs (limited liability)
