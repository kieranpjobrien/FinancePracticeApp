# Question

Base metals like copper and aluminum are considered economically sensitive because:

## Options

- **A**: Their prices never change with economic conditions
- **B**: Demand is closely tied to industrial production and construction activity
- **C**: They are primarily used for jewelry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Base metal economics:

**Key uses:**
- Construction (copper wiring)
- Manufacturing (aluminum)
- Infrastructure (steel)
- Electronics (copper)

**Demand drivers:**
- Industrial production
- Construction activity
- Manufacturing output
- Infrastructure investment

**Cyclical nature:**
- Expansion: Rising demand/prices
- Recession: Falling demand/prices
- Leading indicators
- China demand important

**Specific examples:**
- Copper: "Dr. Copper" economic indicator
- Aluminum: Packaging, transportation
- Zinc: Galvanizing, alloys
- Nickel: Stainless steel, batteries

**Investment implications:**
- Economic cycle exposure
- Inflation correlation
- Global growth beta
- Emerging market development

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Highly cyclical prices
- Option C: Industrial, not jewelry (precious metals)
