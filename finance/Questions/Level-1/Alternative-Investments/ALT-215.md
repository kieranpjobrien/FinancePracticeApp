# Question

A fund's "final close" in private equity refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The first investor commitment to the fund
- **B**: The deadline after which no new investor commitments are accepted
- **C**: The date the fund terminates and returns all capital

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fund closings explained:

**First close:**
- Initial investor commitments
- Fund begins investing
- Minimum capital raised
- Often anchor investors

**Subsequent closes:**
- Additional investors join
- Same terms (usually)
- Catch up on fees
- Interim closings

**Final close:**
- Last opportunity to commit
- No new investors after
- Fund size finalized
- Typically 12-18 months from first

**Why multiple closings:**
- Begin investing sooner
- Continued fundraising
- Build investor base
- Momentum preservation

**After final close:**
- Investment period continues
- No new LP additions
- Existing LPs funded
- Focus on deploying capital

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is first close
- Option C: This is fund termination
