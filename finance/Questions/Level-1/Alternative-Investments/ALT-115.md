# Question

An IPO exit for private equity requires:

## Options

- **A**: The company to immediately become fully liquid for all shareholders
- **B**: The portfolio company to be ready for public market scrutiny with gradual share sales
- **C**: All PE investors to sell on the first day of trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IPO exit considerations:

**Readiness requirements:**
- Audited financials
- Strong management team
- Growth story
- Corporate governance
- Regulatory compliance

**Lock-up provisions:**
- PE shares typically locked
- 90-180 days common
- Prevent immediate dumping
- Orderly selling

**Gradual exit process:**
- IPO creates liquidity
- But PE sells over time
- Secondary offerings
- Block trades

**Advantages of IPO:**
- Market valuations
- Continued upside participation
- Prestigious exit
- Public currency for M&A

**Challenges:**
- Market timing risk
- Lock-up delays full exit
- Ongoing public company costs
- Price volatility

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Significant lock-up restrictions
- Option C: Lock-ups prevent immediate sales
