# Question

Event-driven hedge fund strategies seek to profit from:

## Options

- **A**: General market movements
- **B**: Corporate events such as mergers, bankruptcies, or restructurings
- **C**: Long-term buy-and-hold investing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Event-driven strategies:

**Target events:**
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Spinoffs
- Bankruptcies/restructurings
- Share buybacks
- Earnings surprises

**Sub-strategies:**
- Merger arbitrage
- Distressed securities
- Activist investing
- Special situations

**Merger arbitrage example:**
- Target at $45, deal at $50
- Buy target at $47
- Spread narrows as deal closes
- Profit: $3 per share

**Risk factors:**
- Deal break risk
- Timing uncertainty
- Regulatory approval
- Financing conditions

**Return characteristics:**
- Lower correlation with markets
- Event-specific returns
- Tail risk from deal failures
- Steady income when successful

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Event-specific, not market-driven
- Option C: Catalyst-driven, not passive
