# Question

Infrastructure investments often provide stable cash flows because:

## Options

- **A**: They face intense competition from many providers
- **B**: They often operate as monopolies or have long-term contracts
- **C**: Users can easily switch to alternative providers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Infrastructure cash flow stability:

**Monopoly characteristics:**
- Natural monopolies common
- High barriers to entry
- Essential services
- Limited competition

**Long-term contracts:**
- Power purchase agreements
- Availability payments
- Toll concessions
- Take-or-pay contracts

**Demand characteristics:**
- Essential services
- Inelastic demand
- Consistent usage
- Economic resilience

**Revenue predictability:**
- Contracted revenues
- Regulated tariffs
- Inflation escalators
- Long contract terms

**Examples:**
- Utility: Regulated returns
- Toll road: Usage + inflation escalation
- Airport: Long-term concession

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Limited competition is key feature
- Option C: High switching costs, few alternatives
