# Question

Pension funds often invest in alternative investments for:

## Options

- **A**: Maximum liquidity needs
- **B**: Long-term returns, diversification, and liability matching
- **C**: Short-term trading profits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pension fund alternatives rationale:

**Return enhancement:**
- Higher expected returns
- Illiquidity premium capture
- Outperform public markets
- Close funding gaps

**Diversification:**
- Low correlation benefits
- Risk reduction
- Smoother returns
- Different return drivers

**Liability matching:**
- Infrastructure: Long cash flows
- Real estate: Inflation hedge
- Duration matching possible
- Predictable income streams

**Suitable alternatives:**
- Infrastructure (long-dated)
- Private equity (return enhancement)
- Real estate (inflation, income)
- Private credit (yield)

**Considerations:**
- Long investment horizon fits
- Can accept illiquidity
- Sufficient scale for access
- Governance requirements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Can accept illiquidity
- Option C: Long-term investors
