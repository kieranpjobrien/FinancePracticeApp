# Question

Commodities may provide portfolio diversification because:

## Options

- **A**: They always move in the same direction as stocks
- **B**: Their returns are often driven by different factors than stocks and bonds
- **C**: They generate the same cash flows as equities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity diversification:

**Different return drivers:**
- Supply disruptions
- Weather events
- Geopolitical factors
- Currency movements

**Low correlation sources:**
- Not driven by earnings
- Physical supply/demand
- Inflation sensitivity
- Different economic exposures

**Historical correlation:**
- Low to moderate with stocks
- Often negative with bonds
- Varies by commodity
- Higher in crises sometimes

**Diversification benefits:**
- Portfolio risk reduction
- Different cycle timing
- Inflation hedge
- Global growth exposure

**Considerations:**
- No yield or income
- Storage costs (physical)
- Roll yield impact (futures)
- Volatility can be high

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Low, not high correlation with stocks
- Option C: Commodities don't generate cash flows
