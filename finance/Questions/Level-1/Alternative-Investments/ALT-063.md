# Question

Regulated infrastructure assets typically have:

## Options

- **A**: Unlimited pricing power to maximize returns
- **B**: Regulated returns but high cash flow predictability
- **C**: No government oversight or involvement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Regulated infrastructure:

**How regulation works:**
- Government sets allowed returns
- Cost-of-service model
- Rate base × allowed ROE
- Periodic rate reviews

**Regulated sectors:**
- Electric utilities
- Gas distribution
- Water utilities
- Some telecommunications

**Benefits for investors:**
- Predictable returns
- Stable cash flows
- Natural monopoly position
- Low volume risk

**Limitations:**
- Capped upside
- Regulatory risk
- Political exposure
- Capital intensity

**Contracted vs regulated:**
- Regulated: Government-set returns
- Contracted: Long-term purchase agreements
- Both provide stability

**Investment thesis:**
- Lower risk, stable returns
- Bond-like characteristics
- Inflation protection often built in

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Returns are capped, not unlimited
- Option C: Strong government involvement
