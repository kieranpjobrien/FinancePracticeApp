# Question

Public-private partnerships (PPPs) in infrastructure involve:

## Options

- **A**: Complete government ownership and operation of assets
- **B**: Private sector participation in financing, building, or operating public infrastructure
- **C**: Privatization with no ongoing government involvement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PPP structures:

**Definition:**
- Collaboration between public and private sectors
- Private capital and expertise
- Public service delivery
- Risk sharing arrangements

**Common PPP models:**
- Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT)
- Build-Own-Operate (BOO)
- Design-Build-Finance-Operate (DBFO)
- Concession agreements

**Private sector role:**
- Financing
- Construction
- Operation
- Maintenance

**Public sector role:**
- Project oversight
- Regulatory framework
- Risk allocation
- Performance monitoring

**Benefits of PPPs:**
- Access to private capital
- Efficiency gains
- Risk transfer
- Innovation

**Risks for private investors:**
- Political/regulatory risk
- Demand risk (some models)
- Construction risk
- Long time horizons

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Private sector involved by definition
- Option C: Government remains involved throughout
