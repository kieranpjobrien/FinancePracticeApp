# Question

A hedge fund's redemption notice period requires investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Receive immediate cash upon request
- **B**: Provide advance notice before receiving their redemption proceeds
- **C**: Stay invested for the full fund life

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Redemption notice periods:

**Definition:**
- Advance notice required
- Before redemption processed
- Typically 30-90 days
- Varies by fund

**Purpose:**
- Allow orderly liquidation
- Manage cash needs
- Match with portfolio liquidity
- Prevent runs on fund

**Common structures:**
- 30 days notice
- 45 days notice
- 60-90 days for less liquid
- Quarterly/annual windows

**After notice:**
- Redemption processed at next NAV
- Usually 30 days after period end
- Total time: Notice + processing

**Example:**
- 45-day notice
- Quarterly redemptions
- Miss deadline = next quarter
- Could be 3+ months

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Notice delays access to cash
- Option C: Open-end funds allow exit after terms
