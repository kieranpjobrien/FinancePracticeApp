# Question

Venture capital returns typically follow a "power law" distribution, meaning:

## Options

- **A**: Returns are normally distributed around the mean
- **B**: A small number of investments generate most of the returns
- **C**: All investments contribute equally to fund performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Power law in venture capital:

**Pattern:**
- Few big winners drive returns
- Many investments fail or return little
- Top 10-20% of deals drive 80%+ of returns
- Extreme outcome concentration

**Typical VC portfolio:**
- 10-20 investments per fund
- 30-50% complete losses
- 20-30% modest returns
- 10-20% big winners (10x+)

**Implications for strategy:**
- Must invest in potential "home runs"
- Cannot play it safe
- Portfolio approach essential
- One winner can make the fund

**Manager skill differentiation:**
- Access to best deals
- Picking future winners
- Supporting portfolio growth
- Avoiding complete losses

**Contrast with buyouts:**
- Buyouts more normally distributed
- Fewer complete failures
- Fewer 10x+ winners

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not normal distribution
- Option C: Extreme concentration, not equal
