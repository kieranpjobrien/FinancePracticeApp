# Question

Venture capital investments typically focus on:

## Options

- **A**: Mature companies with stable cash flows
- **B**: Early-stage companies with high growth potential
- **C**: Distressed companies requiring turnaround

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Venture capital characteristics:

**Target companies:**
- Early-stage or startup
- High growth potential
- Often technology-focused
- Pre-profit or early profit

**Investment stages:**
- Seed: Concept/prototype
- Series A: Product development
- Series B: Scaling
- Series C+: Expansion

**Risk-return profile:**
- Highest risk in private equity
- Many investments fail (50%+)
- Winners must compensate for losers
- "Power law" returns

**VC value-add:**
- Capital for growth
- Strategic guidance
- Network connections
- Governance support

**Differences from LBO:**
- Less leverage used
- Minority stakes common
- Longer holding periods
- Higher failure rates

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes buyout targets
- Option C: Describes distressed PE/turnaround
