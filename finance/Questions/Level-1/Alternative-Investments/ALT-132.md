# Question

In infrastructure investing, "core" assets are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: High development risk and unproven demand
- **B**: Stable, operational assets with contracted or regulated revenue
- **C**: Maximum leverage and speculative returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Infrastructure strategy spectrum:

**Core characteristics:**
- Operational assets
- Contracted/regulated revenue
- Stable cash flows
- Lower leverage
- Target returns: 6-10%

**Core-plus:**
- Predominantly core features
- Some enhancement potential
- Moderate value-add elements
- Target returns: 8-12%

**Value-add:**
- Improvement opportunities
- Expansion potential
- Higher operating risk
- Target returns: 10-15%

**Opportunistic:**
- Development/greenfield
- Turnaround situations
- Highest risk
- Target returns: 15%+

**Core examples:**
- Regulated utilities
- Long-term contracted pipelines
- Established toll roads
- Operating renewable assets

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes opportunistic
- Option C: Core uses moderate leverage
