# Question

Key person risk in alternative investments refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The risk that too many employees will join the fund
- **B**: The risk that departure of key individuals will negatively impact fund performance
- **C**: The requirement for investors to be key decision makers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Key person risk:

**The risk:**
- Critical individuals leave
- Performance depends on few people
- Continuity concerns
- Succession uncertainty

**Why prevalent in alternatives:**
- Manager skill is paramount
- Small, specialized teams
- Relationships matter
- Track record tied to individuals

**Mitigation in LPA:**
- Key person clauses
- Investment period suspension
- LP consent for new hires
- Replacement requirements

**Key person events:**
- Death or disability
- Departure from firm
- Reduced time commitment
- Criminal or regulatory issues

**Investor protections:**
- Suspension of investing
- Potential for termination
- Right to approve successors
- Enhanced monitoring

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Risk of departure, not joining
- Option C: Describes investor involvement
