# Question

A commodity "supercycle" refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Normal daily price fluctuations
- **B**: Extended periods (10-35 years) of above-trend prices driven by structural demand shifts
- **C**: Government-mandated price controls

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity supercycles:

**Definition:**
- Multi-decade price cycles
- Above long-term trend
- Driven by structural factors
- Supply/demand imbalances

**Historical supercycles:**
- 1890s-early 1900s: US industrialization
- 1930s-1950s: Post-WWII rebuilding
- 1970s: Oil shocks, inflation
- 2000s-2010s: China growth

**Demand drivers:**
- Industrialization waves
- Urbanization
- Infrastructure buildout
- Population growth

**Supply constraints:**
- Underinvestment
- Resource depletion
- Long lead times
- Geopolitical disruption

**Investment thesis:**
- Multi-year trend following
- Structural positioning
- Long-term allocation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Multi-decade, not daily
- Option C: Market-driven, not mandated
