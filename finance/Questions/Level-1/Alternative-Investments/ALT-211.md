# Question

Rebalancing in commodity index investing can provide:

## Options

- **A**: No impact on returns
- **B**: A diversification return from systematically selling outperformers and buying underperformers
- **C**: Only transaction costs with no benefits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity rebalancing return:

**How it works:**
- Sell winners (above target weight)
- Buy losers (below target weight)
- Systematic mean reversion capture
- "Buy low, sell high" effect

**Diversification return:**
- Unique to volatile assets
- With low/negative correlation
- Rebalancing harvests volatility
- Additional return source

**Mathematical basis:**
- Variance drag reduction
- Geometric vs arithmetic
- Portfolio effect > sum of parts
- Works in mean-reverting environments

**Index-specific impact:**
- Rebalancing frequency matters
- Monthly/quarterly common
- Transaction costs offset
- Net benefit varies

**Conditions needed:**
- Low correlations
- High volatility
- Mean reversion tendency
- Regular rebalancing

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Can meaningfully impact returns
- Option C: Benefits often exceed costs
