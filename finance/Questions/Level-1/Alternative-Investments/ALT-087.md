# Question

Relative value hedge fund strategies attempt to profit from:

## Options

- **A**: Outright directional bets on market movements
- **B**: Pricing discrepancies between related securities
- **C**: Only investing in undervalued assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Relative value strategies:

**Core concept:**
- Identify mispriced pairs
- Long undervalued, short overvalued
- Profit from convergence
- Market-neutral focus

**Sub-strategies:**
- Fixed income arbitrage
- Convertible arbitrage
- Statistical arbitrage
- Capital structure arbitrage

**Fixed income example:**
- On-the-run vs off-the-run Treasuries
- Same credit, different liquidity
- Spread should converge

**Convertible example:**
- Long convertible bond
- Short underlying stock
- Capture mispricing

**Characteristics:**
- Low market correlation
- Leverage typically needed
- Spread compression profit
- Small but consistent returns

**Risks:**
- Spreads can widen
- Leverage amplifies losses
- Liquidity crises

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Relative, not directional
- Option C: Both sides of trade matter
