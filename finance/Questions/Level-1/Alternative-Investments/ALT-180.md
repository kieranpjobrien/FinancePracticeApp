# Question

Pro-rata rights in venture capital give investors:

## Options

- **A**: The obligation to invest in all future rounds
- **B**: The option to maintain their ownership percentage in future financing rounds
- **C**: Control over company management decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pro-rata rights:

**Definition:**
- Right (not obligation) to participate
- Maintain ownership percentage
- In future financing rounds
- Prevents dilution

**How it works:**
- Investor owns 10%
- New round raises $10M
- Right to invest $1M (10% of new)
- Maintains 10% ownership

**Value of pro-rata:**
- Protect stake in winners
- Avoid dilution in success
- Double down on conviction
- Valuable for early investors

**Negotiation:**
- Often given to Series A+
- May have limits (super pro-rata)
- Sometimes fight for allocation
- In hot rounds, may be contested

**Exercise decision:**
- Evaluate company progress
- Capital allocation decision
- Portfolio construction
- Not always exercised

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Right, not obligation
- Option C: Separate from governance rights
