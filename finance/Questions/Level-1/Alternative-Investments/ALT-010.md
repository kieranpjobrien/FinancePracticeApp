# Question

Valuation of alternative investments is challenging primarily because:

## Options

- **A**: They trade on public exchanges with high frequency
- **B**: Many assets lack observable market prices
- **C**: Regulatory bodies set standardized valuations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Valuation challenges in alternatives:

**Why valuation is difficult:**
- No public market prices
- Infrequent transactions
- Unique asset characteristics
- Appraisal-based valuations

**Valuation methods used:**
- Comparable transactions
- Discounted cash flow analysis
- Appraisals (real estate)
- Mark-to-model approaches

**Problems with appraisal-based values:**
- Smoothed returns (understates volatility)
- Stale pricing
- Subjective assumptions
- Potential conflicts of interest

**Impact on reported statistics:**
- Correlation appears lower than reality
- Volatility understated
- Sharpe ratios overstated
- Diversification benefits exaggerated

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Alternatives don't trade on exchanges
- Option C: No standardized regulatory valuations
