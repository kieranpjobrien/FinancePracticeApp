# Question

Occupancy rate in real estate measures:

## Options

- **A**: The building's total square footage
- **B**: The percentage of rentable space currently leased
- **C**: The building's construction quality

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Occupancy rate:

**Formula:**
Occupancy = Leased Space / Total Rentable Space × 100

**Example:**
- Building: 100,000 sq ft rentable
- Leased: 92,000 sq ft
- Occupancy: 92%

**Why important:**
- Revenue indicator
- Property performance
- Market demand signal
- Valuation input

**Physical vs economic:**
- Physical: Space occupied
- Economic: Rent-paying occupancy
- Economic may differ (concessions)

**Benchmarks vary:**
- By property type
- By market
- By economic cycle
- 90%+ often considered strong

**Related metrics:**
- Vacancy rate = 1 - Occupancy
- Absorption rate
- Leasing velocity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is square footage, not occupancy
- Option C: This is property quality, not occupancy
