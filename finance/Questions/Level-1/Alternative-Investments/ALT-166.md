# Question

A Limited Partner Advisory Committee (LPAC) in private equity:

## Options

- **A**: Makes all investment decisions for the fund
- **B**: Advises on conflicts of interest and certain fund matters
- **C**: Only includes the general partner

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LPAC function:

**Primary roles:**
- Review conflicts of interest
- Approve related-party transactions
- Consent to certain actions
- Represent LP interests

**Common matters reviewed:**
- Valuation issues
- Extension requests
- Co-investment allocation
- Fee waivers

**Composition:**
- Selected LPs (usually largest)
- Fiduciary duty to all LPs
- Typically 5-10 members
- Rotating membership possible

**What LPAC doesn't do:**
- Make investment decisions
- Override GP on strategy
- Day-to-day management
- Replace GP judgment

**Benefits:**
- Governance mechanism
- LP voice
- Conflict resolution
- Transparency enhancement

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: GP makes investment decisions
- Option C: Comprised of LPs, not GP
