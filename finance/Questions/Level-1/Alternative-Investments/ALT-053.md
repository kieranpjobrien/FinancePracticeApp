# Question

In real estate, the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio measures:

## Options

- **A**: The property's income relative to expenses
- **B**: The amount of debt financing relative to property value
- **C**: The cap rate compared to interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LTV ratio explained:

**Formula:**
LTV = Loan Amount / Property Value × 100

**Example:**
- Property value: $10 million
- Mortgage: $6 million
- LTV: 60%

**Importance:**
- Measures leverage level
- Lender risk assessment
- Borrower equity cushion

**Typical LTV ranges:**
- Core properties: 50-65%
- Value-add: 60-75%
- Development: 50-65% of cost

**Higher LTV implications:**
- More leverage
- Higher equity returns if positive
- Greater loss risk
- Higher interest rates

**Lender perspective:**
- Lower LTV = safer loan
- More equity cushion
- Better terms for borrower

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes operating metrics
- Option C: Describes spread analysis
