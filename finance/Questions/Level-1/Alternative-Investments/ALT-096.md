# Question

Side pockets in hedge funds are used to:

## Options

- **A**: Store excess cash for future investments
- **B**: Segregate illiquid investments from the main portfolio
- **C**: Hold short positions separately from long positions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Side pockets explained:

**Purpose:**
- Separate illiquid holdings
- Fair treatment of investors
- Proper valuation handling
- Protect existing investors

**How they work:**
- Illiquid asset moved to side pocket
- Valued separately
- Not included in main NAV
- Redeemers don't get access

**When used:**
- Position becomes illiquid
- Hard-to-value situations
- Restructuring situations
- Private investments

**Investor implications:**
- Cannot redeem side pocket portion
- Must wait for liquidity event
- Separate accounting
- May take years to realize

**Concerns:**
- Manager discretion issues
- Valuation challenges
- Fee calculations
- Transparency needs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not a cash reserve
- Option C: Long/short positions treated together
