# Question

In real estate, the equity multiple measures:

## Options

- **A**: The annual yield on the property
- **B**: Total cash returned divided by total cash invested
- **C**: The cap rate of the property

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Equity multiple:

**Formula:**
Equity Multiple = Total Distributions / Total Invested Capital

**What it shows:**
- Wealth creation
- Total return in dollars
- Simple to understand
- Ignores timing

**Example:**
- Invest: $100,000
- Total distributions: $200,000
- Equity multiple: 2.0x

**Relationship to IRR:**
- Multiple ignores time
- 2.0x in 3 years vs 7 years
- IRR very different
- Use together

**Interpretation:**
- 1.0x: Break-even
- 2.0x: Doubled money
- 3.0x: Tripled money

**Limitations:**
- No time value
- Doesn't measure efficiency
- Different for different holding periods
- Use with IRR

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is yield/cash-on-cash
- Option C: Cap rate is income-based metric
