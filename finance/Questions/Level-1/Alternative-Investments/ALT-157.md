# Question

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) operates through:

## Options

- **A**: Traditional corporate hierarchy with a CEO and board
- **B**: Smart contracts and token-holder voting for governance decisions
- **C**: Government oversight and regulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DAO structure:

**Core characteristics:**
- Rules encoded in smart contracts
- Token-based governance
- No central authority
- Community decisions

**How DAOs work:**
- Token holders vote
- Proposals submitted
- Voting thresholds met
- Smart contracts execute

**Use cases:**
- Protocol governance
- Investment DAOs
- Social/community organizations
- Treasury management

**Governance mechanisms:**
- One token, one vote (common)
- Quadratic voting
- Delegation
- Time-weighted voting

**Advantages:**
- Transparent rules
- Global participation
- Reduced agency costs
- Permissionless

**Challenges:**
- Legal uncertainty
- Security vulnerabilities
- Voter apathy
- Coordination difficulties

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No traditional hierarchy
- Option C: Largely unregulated currently
