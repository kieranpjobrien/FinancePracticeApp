# Question

Commodity ETFs provide investors with:

## Options

- **A**: Direct physical ownership of all commodities
- **B**: Liquid, exchange-traded exposure to commodity prices
- **C**: Guaranteed returns linked to commodity prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity ETFs:

**Key benefits:**
- Daily liquidity
- Exchange-traded
- Low minimums
- Easy access

**Implementation methods:**
- Futures-based (most common)
- Physical-backed (gold, silver)
- Equity-based (resource stocks)

**Futures-based ETFs:**
- Roll futures contracts
- Subject to contango/backwardation
- May diverge from spot
- Roll yield impact

**Physical-backed:**
- Actually hold commodity
- Common for precious metals
- No roll yield
- Storage costs embedded

**Considerations:**
- Tracking error
- Expense ratios
- Tax treatment (varies)
- Contango drag

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Most use futures, not physical
- Option C: No guaranteed returns
