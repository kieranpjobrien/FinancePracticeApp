# Question

Typical reporting frequency for private equity fund performance:

## Options

- **A**: Daily, like mutual funds
- **B**: Quarterly, with annual audited statements
- **C**: Only at fund termination

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PE reporting standards:

**Quarterly reports include:**
- Capital account statements
- Fund performance (IRR, TVPI)
- Portfolio company updates
- Cash flow summary

**Annual materials:**
- Audited financial statements
- K-1 tax documents
- Annual letter from GP
- Detailed portfolio review

**Key metrics reported:**
- IRR (since inception)
- TVPI, DPI, RVPI
- Capital called/distributed
- NAV

**Industry standards:**
- ILPA guidelines
- Institutional requirements
- Investor expectations
- Manager competition

**Lag in reporting:**
- Quarterly reports: 30-60 days
- Audited: 90-120 days
- Private company valuations take time

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Illiquid assets, not daily
- Option C: Regular reporting throughout fund life
