# Question

Maximum drawdown as a risk measure represents:

## Options

- **A**: The average decline in portfolio value
- **B**: The largest peak-to-trough decline over a period
- **C**: The standard deviation of negative returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Maximum drawdown explained:

**Definition:**
- Largest cumulative loss
- From peak to subsequent trough
- Before new peak established
- Measures worst-case scenario

**Why useful for alternatives:**
- Captures tail risk
- Relevant for illiquid investments
- Investors care about worst losses
- Not dependent on distribution assumptions

**Example:**
- Portfolio peaks at $100
- Drops to $60 (40% decline)
- Recovers to $80, drops to $55
- Max drawdown: 45% ($100 to $55)

**Related metrics:**
- Time to recovery
- Drawdown duration
- Ulcer index

**Hedge fund application:**
- Often reported in fact sheets
- Risk management trigger levels
- Redemption decision input

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Max drawdown is largest, not average
- Option C: Standard deviation is different measure
