# Question

A minority stake in private equity investments typically provides investors with:

## Options

- **A**: Complete operational control of the company
- **B**: Governance rights and board representation without full control
- **C**: No rights or influence over company decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Minority stake characteristics:

**What minority investors get:**
- Board seat(s)
- Information rights
- Protective provisions
- Pro-rata participation rights

**Protective provisions may include:**
- Veto on major decisions
- Anti-dilution protection
- Approval of budgets
- Change of control rights

**Without full control:**
- Cannot force sale
- Cannot replace management unilaterally
- Cannot change strategy alone
- Depend on majority cooperation

**Common in:**
- Growth equity
- Venture capital (early stages)
- Family business investments
- Pre-IPO positions

**Minority discount:**
- Less control = less value
- Discount to control value
- Negotiated protections mitigate

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Majority needed for full control
- Option C: Minority investors negotiate meaningful rights
