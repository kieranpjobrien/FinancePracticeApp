# Question

Brownfield infrastructure investments refer to:

## Options

- **A**: New construction of infrastructure assets from scratch
- **B**: Existing operational assets with established cash flows
- **C**: Environmental cleanup projects only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Brownfield vs greenfield:

**Brownfield characteristics:**
- Existing operational assets
- Established revenue streams
- Known performance history
- Lower risk profile

**Examples:**
- Operating toll roads
- Existing power plants
- Functional airports
- Active utility systems

**Greenfield characteristics:**
- New construction projects
- No operating history
- Development/construction risk
- Higher return potential

**Risk-return comparison:**
- Brownfield: Lower risk, lower return
- Greenfield: Higher risk, higher return

**Brownfield advantages:**
- Proven demand
- Known costs
- Existing permits/approvals
- Immediate cash flow

**Greenfield challenges:**
- Construction risk
- Demand uncertainty
- Regulatory approvals needed
- Longer time to cash flow

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes greenfield
- Option C: Environmental term, different meaning here
