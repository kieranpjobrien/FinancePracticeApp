# Question

The difference between "hot" and "cold" cryptocurrency wallets relates to:

## Options

- **A**: The temperature of the storage facility
- **B**: Whether the wallet is connected to the internet
- **C**: The type of cryptocurrency stored

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hot vs cold wallets:

**Hot wallets:**
- Connected to internet
- Convenient for transactions
- Higher hacking risk
- Exchange wallets, mobile apps

**Cold wallets:**
- Offline storage
- Hardware devices or paper
- Maximum security
- Less convenient

**Security trade-off:**
- Hot: Convenient but vulnerable
- Cold: Secure but less accessible
- Balance based on needs

**Cold storage methods:**
- Hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor)
- Paper wallets
- Air-gapped computers
- Multi-signature setups

**Best practices:**
- Large holdings in cold storage
- Small amounts in hot for trading
- Multiple backups
- Test recovery process

**Institutional approach:**
- Majority in cold storage
- Insured custody solutions
- Multi-signature requirements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Internet connectivity, not temperature
- Option C: Any crypto can use either
