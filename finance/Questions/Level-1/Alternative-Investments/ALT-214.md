# Question

Separately managed accounts (SMAs) in alternatives provide:

## Options

- **A**: Less transparency than commingled funds
- **B**: Customized portfolios with direct ownership of securities
- **C**: Lower minimum investments than pooled funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

SMA characteristics:

**Key features:**
- Direct ownership
- Customization available
- Individual account
- Tailored guidelines

**Benefits:**
- Transparency (full holdings)
- Tax efficiency (loss harvesting)
- Custom restrictions
- ESG overlays possible

**Compared to funds:**
- More transparency
- Greater control
- Higher minimums usually
- Administrative complexity

**Common customizations:**
- Exclude specific securities
- Sector restrictions
- ESG screening
- Risk parameters

**Who uses SMAs:**
- Large institutions
- Family offices
- Sophisticated investors
- Those needing customization

**Trade-offs:**
- Higher operational burden
- Minimum size requirements
- May miss diversification

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: More transparent than funds
- Option C: Typically higher minimums
