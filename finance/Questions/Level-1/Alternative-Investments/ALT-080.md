# Question

Commodity indices differ from equity indices primarily in their:

## Options

- **A**: Use of market capitalization weighting
- **B**: Use of production or liquidity-based weighting and futures contracts
- **C**: Exclusive focus on precious metals

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Commodity index construction:

**Weighting approaches:**
- Production-weighted
- Liquidity-weighted
- Equal-weighted
- Combinations

**Unlike equity indices:**
- No market cap weighting
- Based on futures, not spot
- Roll methodology matters
- Rebalancing frequency varies

**Major indices:**
- S&P GSCI: Production-weighted
- Bloomberg Commodity: Diversified
- DBIQ: Optimized roll
- Rogers: Broad coverage

**Index differences impact:**
- Sector allocations vary
- Energy weight varies widely
- Roll yields differ
- Returns can diverge significantly

**Implementation:**
- Futures-based exposure
- Collateral investment
- Roll schedule

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Commodities don't use market cap
- Option C: Include energy, agriculture, metals
