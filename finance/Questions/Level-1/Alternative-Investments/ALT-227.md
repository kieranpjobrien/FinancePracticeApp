# Question

Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) differ from cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin in that CBDCs:

## Options

- **A**: Are decentralized with no central authority
- **B**: Are issued and controlled by central banks as legal tender
- **C**: Have a fixed maximum supply like Bitcoin

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CBDC characteristics:

**Key features:**
- Central bank issued
- Legal tender status
- Government controlled
- Digital fiat currency

**Differences from crypto:**
- Centralized, not decentralized
- Central bank controls supply
- Regulatory compliance built-in
- No mining or staking

**Potential benefits:**
- Payment efficiency
- Financial inclusion
- Monetary policy tools
- Reduced cash handling

**Concerns:**
- Privacy implications
- Financial surveillance
- Bank disintermediation
- Technology risks

**Global development:**
- China: Digital yuan advanced
- ECB: Digital euro exploring
- Fed: CBDC research ongoing
- Many countries piloting

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Centralized by definition
- Option C: Supply controlled by central bank
