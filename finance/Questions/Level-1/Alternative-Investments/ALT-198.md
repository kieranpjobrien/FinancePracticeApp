# Question

Risk parity strategies aim to:

## Options

- **A**: Concentrate all risk in the highest returning asset class
- **B**: Allocate portfolio risk equally across asset classes rather than capital
- **C**: Avoid all investment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk parity approach:

**Core concept:**
- Equal risk contribution
- Not equal dollar allocation
- Volatility-weighted
- Diversify risk, not just capital

**Traditional 60/40 issue:**
- 60% stocks, 40% bonds
- But ~90% of risk from stocks
- Not truly diversified risk
- Bonds contribute little

**Risk parity solution:**
- Lower stock allocation
- Lever up bonds
- Equal risk from each
- True diversification

**Implementation:**
- Calculate asset volatilities
- Allocate inversely to volatility
- Adjust for correlation
- Use leverage for return

**Example:**
- Stocks: 20% of capital, 50% risk
- Bonds: 80% of capital (levered), 50% risk
- Equal risk contribution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Equal risk, not concentrated
- Option C: Still takes risk, just diversified
