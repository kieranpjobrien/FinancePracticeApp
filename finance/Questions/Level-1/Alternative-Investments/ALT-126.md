# Question

Carbon credits as an investment represent:

## Options

- **A**: Ownership of coal mining operations
- **B**: Tradeable permits representing the right to emit greenhouse gases
- **C**: Guaranteed returns from renewable energy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Carbon credits explained:

**What they are:**
- Permits for emission allowance
- One credit = one tonne CO2
- Tradeable instruments
- Price set by market

**Market types:**
- Compliance markets (regulated)
- Voluntary markets
- Cap-and-trade systems
- Carbon taxes (related)

**Investment thesis:**
- Climate regulation increasing
- Carbon prices rising long-term
- Supply constraints
- Demand from emitters

**Investment vehicles:**
- Direct carbon futures
- Carbon-focused funds
- Company exposure
- ETFs

**Risks:**
- Policy changes
- Market volatility
- Regulatory uncertainty
- Oversupply risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Carbon credits oppose fossil fuels
- Option C: Market-priced, no guarantee
