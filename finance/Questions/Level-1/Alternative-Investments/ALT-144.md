# Question

In venture capital, a "Series A" funding round typically:

## Options

- **A**: Is the final round before IPO
- **B**: Is an early institutional round following seed funding
- **C**: Only involves debt financing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Venture funding stages:

**Pre-seed/Seed:**
- Earliest stage
- Concept/prototype
- Friends, family, angels
- Smallest amounts

**Series A:**
- First major VC round
- Product-market fit stage
- Institutional VCs lead
- $5-15M typical

**Series B:**
- Scaling stage
- Proven model
- Larger amounts
- Growth acceleration

**Series C and beyond:**
- Expansion capital
- Often growth equity
- Pre-IPO rounds
- Largest amounts

**Progression:**
- Each round higher valuation (usually)
- More capital raised
- Different investor types
- Increasing maturity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Later rounds precede IPO
- Option C: VC is equity financing
