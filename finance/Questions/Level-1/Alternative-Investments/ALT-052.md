# Question

A "value-add" real estate strategy typically involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying stabilized properties with long-term tenants for steady income
- **B**: Acquiring properties requiring improvements to increase value and income
- **C**: Investing only in ground-up development projects

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real estate strategy spectrum:

**Core (lowest risk):**
- Fully leased properties
- High-quality tenants
- Prime locations
- Stable income focus
- Target returns: 6-9%

**Value-add (moderate risk):**
- Properties needing improvement
- Lease-up opportunities
- Renovation potential
- Moderate leverage
- Target returns: 10-14%

**Opportunistic (highest risk):**
- Ground-up development
- Major repositioning
- Distressed situations
- High leverage
- Target returns: 15%+

**Value-add improvements:**
- Physical renovations
- Tenant improvements
- Lease renegotiations
- Management improvements
- Repositioning

**Risk-return trade-off:**
- Higher potential returns
- More execution risk
- Active management required

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes core strategy
- Option C: Development is opportunistic
