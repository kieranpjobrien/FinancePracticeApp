# Question

Proxy voting allows shareholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Directly manage company operations
- **B**: Authorize another party to vote their shares at shareholder meetings
- **C**: Override board decisions without a meeting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proxy: written authorization allowing someone to vote shares on shareholder's behalf. Essential for widely-held companies where shareholders can't attend meetings. Proxy statements disclose meeting agenda, board nominees, and management proposals. Proxy contests occur when competing parties solicit votes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes management's role, not shareholder voting
- Option C misrepresents the proxy process
