# Question

A conventional cash flow pattern is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Multiple sign changes in cash flows over the project life
- **B**: An initial outflow followed by a series of inflows
- **C**: Cash inflows only with no initial investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conventional cash flow: one sign change—initial negative cash flow (investment) followed by positive cash flows (returns). IRR rule works well with conventional flows. Non-conventional flows (multiple sign changes) can produce multiple IRRs, making interpretation difficult.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes non-conventional cash flows
- Option C is unrealistic—projects require investment
