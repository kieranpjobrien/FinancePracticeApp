# Question

The equivalent annual annuity (EAA) method is used when:

## Options

- **A**: Projects are independent with equal lives
- **B**: Mutually exclusive projects have unequal lives
- **C**: Projects have the same initial investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

EAA converts project NPVs into equivalent annual amounts for comparison when mutually exclusive projects have different lives. It assumes projects can be repeated at similar returns. Higher EAA is preferred. Alternative methods: replacement chain (extending to common lives) or least common multiple approach.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't require EAA—NPV works for independent projects
- Option C is irrelevant to the life-difference problem
