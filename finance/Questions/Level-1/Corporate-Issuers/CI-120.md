# Question

Cross-shareholding arrangements can create governance concerns because:

## Options

- **A**: They always improve corporate performance
- **B**: Management may become entrenched as friendly shareholders support each other
- **C**: They are prohibited in all jurisdictions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cross-shareholdings: companies own stakes in each other. This can entrench management (allied shareholders support incumbents), reduce takeover threats, and create conflicts (voting on matters affecting business partners). Common in some Asian and European markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores potential governance weaknesses
- Option C is incorrect—they're legal in many jurisdictions
