# Question

The "bird in the hand" argument suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Investors are indifferent between dividends and capital gains
- **B**: Investors prefer certain current dividends over uncertain future capital gains
- **C**: Companies should never pay dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bird in the hand: theory that investors value dividends more than equivalent capital gains because dividends are certain while future gains are risky. Suggests higher payout increases value by reducing investor uncertainty. Criticized: dividend policy doesn't change underlying business risk; investors can create their own certainty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes dividend irrelevance theory
- Option C is opposite—this theory supports dividends
