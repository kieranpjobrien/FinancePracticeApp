# Question

Agency costs of debt arise because:

## Options

- **A**: Bondholders may take excessive risks with the company's assets
- **B**: Shareholders may take actions that benefit themselves at bondholders' expense
- **C**: Management always acts in bondholders' best interests

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt agency costs: shareholders (through management) may pursue risky projects (asset substitution), pay excessive dividends (claim dilution), or underinvest (debt overhang). Bondholders protect themselves through covenants, security, and higher yields. These costs limit optimal leverage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses—shareholders, not bondholders, control the company
- Option C is incorrect—management may exploit bondholders
