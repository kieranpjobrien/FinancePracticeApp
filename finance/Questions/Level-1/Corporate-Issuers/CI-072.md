# Question

Tax preference theory suggests that investors may prefer:

## Options

- **A**: High dividend payouts for income
- **B**: Capital gains over dividends due to tax treatment differences
- **C**: No preference between dividends and capital gains

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tax preference: capital gains are often taxed at lower rates than dividends and are deferred until realization. Investors in higher tax brackets may prefer companies that retain earnings (capital gains) rather than pay dividends. This argues against high dividend payouts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores tax disadvantage of dividends
- Option C describes dividend irrelevance, ignoring taxes
