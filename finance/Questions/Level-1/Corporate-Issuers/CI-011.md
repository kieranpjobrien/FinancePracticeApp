# Question

The payback period method's main weakness is that it:

## Options

- **A**: Is too complex to calculate
- **B**: Ignores the time value of money and cash flows after payback
- **C**: Always gives the same result as NPV

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Payback calculates time to recover initial investment without discounting cash flows (ignoring time value) and ignores cash flows occurring after payback. It favors liquidity over profitability and may reject value-creating projects. Discounted payback addresses time value but still ignores post-payback flows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—payback is simple to calculate
- Option C is incorrect—payback often conflicts with NPV rankings
