# Question

Equipment costs $100,000 with 5-year straight-line depreciation. If the tax rate is 30%, the annual depreciation tax shield is:

## Options

- **A**: $6,000
- **B**: $14,000
- **C**: $20,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Annual depreciation = $100,000 / 5 = $20,000. Tax shield = Depreciation × Tax rate = $20,000 × 0.30 = $6,000 per year. Depreciation reduces taxable income, generating cash savings equal to the tax rate times depreciation expense.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B subtracts tax shield from depreciation
- Option C is depreciation, not the tax shield
