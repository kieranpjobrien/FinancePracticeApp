# Question

The cost of capital represents:

## Options

- **A**: The accounting cost of funds
- **B**: The opportunity cost of capital providers
- **C**: The historical average borrowing rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cost of capital is the return required by investors for providing capital—their opportunity cost of investing elsewhere at similar risk. It's forward-looking and market-based, not historical or accounting-based. Projects must earn at least the cost of capital to create value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses economic and accounting concepts
- Option C uses historical, not required future returns
