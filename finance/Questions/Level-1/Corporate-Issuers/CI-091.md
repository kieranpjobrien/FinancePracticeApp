# Question

A fixed-price tender offer to repurchase shares:

## Options

- **A**: Buys shares at current market price
- **B**: Offers a premium to market price for a specified number of shares
- **C**: Requires all shareholders to sell

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fixed-price tender offers: company offers to buy a specific number of shares at a stated price, usually above market (premium). Shareholders choose whether to participate. If oversubscribed, shares are purchased pro-rata. This method is faster than open-market buybacks and signals confidence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes open-market repurchases
- Option C is incorrect—participation is voluntary
