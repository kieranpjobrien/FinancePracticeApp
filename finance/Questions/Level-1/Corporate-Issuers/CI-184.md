# Question

Indirect costs of financial distress include:

## Options

- **A**: Legal and accounting fees in bankruptcy
- **B**: Lost sales, reduced productivity, and difficulty attracting talent
- **C**: Interest payments on debt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Indirect distress costs: harder to quantify but often larger than direct costs. Include: customers avoiding distressed firms, suppliers demanding cash payment, employees leaving, management distraction, foregone investment opportunities. Can begin well before formal bankruptcy and persist afterward.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes direct bankruptcy costs
- Option C is a normal financing cost, not distress-related
