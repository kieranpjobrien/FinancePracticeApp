# Question

In calculating the cost of equity, only systematic risk is considered because:

## Options

- **A**: Unsystematic risk cannot be measured
- **B**: Investors can eliminate unsystematic risk through diversification
- **C**: Systematic risk is always larger than unsystematic risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In CAPM, diversified investors eliminate company-specific (unsystematic) risk through portfolio diversification. Only market-related (systematic) risk remains, so only systematic risk earns a risk premium. Beta measures systematic risk, determining the cost of equity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—unsystematic risk can be measured, but isn't compensated
- Option C isn't necessarily true and misses the point about diversification
