# Question

A company with a quick ratio of 0.6 and a current ratio of 1.8 likely has:

## Options

- **A**: High proportion of inventory in current assets
- **B**: Very liquid current assets
- **C**: Negative working capital

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Quick ratio excludes inventory from current assets. Large gap between current ratio (1.8) and quick ratio (0.6) indicates inventory is a significant portion of current assets. This may signal liquidity concerns if inventory is slow-moving or difficult to liquidate quickly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is contradicted by the low quick ratio
- Option C is incorrect—current ratio above 1 means positive working capital
