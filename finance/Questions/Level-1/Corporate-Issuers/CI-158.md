# Question

The profitability index (PI) is most useful when:

## Options

- **A**: Projects are independent with unlimited capital
- **B**: Capital is rationed and projects must be ranked for selection
- **C**: All projects have the same initial investment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Profitability index = PV of future cash flows / Initial investment. PI > 1 indicates positive NPV. Under capital rationing, PI helps rank projects by efficiency of capital use—highest value per dollar invested. Select projects with highest PI until capital exhausted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't require ranking—accept all positive NPV projects
- Option C allows direct NPV comparison without PI
