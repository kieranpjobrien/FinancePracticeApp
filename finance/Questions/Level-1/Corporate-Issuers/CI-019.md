# Question

According to pecking order theory, firms prefer:

## Options

- **A**: Debt financing over internal funds due to tax benefits
- **B**: Internal financing, then debt, then equity as last resort
- **C**: Equity financing to minimize financial risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pecking order theory (based on information asymmetry): firms prefer internal funds first (no adverse selection problem), then debt (less information-sensitive), then equity (most information-sensitive, highest adverse selection). This explains why profitable firms may have less debt (can fund internally).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores information asymmetry costs
- Option C ignores that equity signals overvaluation to markets
