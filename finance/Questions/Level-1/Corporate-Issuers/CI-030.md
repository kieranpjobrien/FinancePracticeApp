# Question

A stock split differs from a cash dividend in that:

## Options

- **A**: It transfers cash to shareholders
- **B**: It increases shares outstanding without changing shareholders' wealth
- **C**: It requires shareholder approval

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock splits (and stock dividends) increase shares outstanding proportionally for all shareholders—no wealth transfer occurs. A 2-for-1 split doubles shares but halves price, leaving market cap unchanged. Motivations include improving liquidity by lowering share price to a "normal" trading range.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cash dividends
- Option C may apply to both in some jurisdictions
