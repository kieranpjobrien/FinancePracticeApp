# Question

Cumulative voting allows shareholders to:

## Options

- **A**: Vote only on dividend proposals
- **B**: Concentrate all their votes on fewer director candidates
- **C**: Proxy their votes multiple times

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cumulative voting: shareholders receive votes equal to shares times directors to be elected, and can allocate all votes to one candidate. This helps minority shareholders elect at least one board member. Straight voting (one vote per share per seat) favors majority control.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands what cumulative voting applies to
- Option C describes proxy multiplication, which isn't how cumulative voting works
