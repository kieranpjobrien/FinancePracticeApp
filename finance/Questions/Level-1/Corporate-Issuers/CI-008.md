# Question

A positive NPV project:

## Options

- **A**: Has returns equal to the cost of capital
- **B**: Increases shareholder wealth
- **C**: Should be rejected if IRR is below 15%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Positive NPV means the present value of cash inflows exceeds outflows at the required rate—the project returns more than the cost of capital and creates value. Accept all positive NPV projects when capital isn't constrained. NPV is the most reliable capital budgeting criterion for wealth maximization.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes zero NPV (break-even with cost of capital)
- Option C uses an arbitrary hurdle—NPV decision rule is definitive
