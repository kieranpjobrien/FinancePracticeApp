# Question

The Anglo-American governance model differs from the continental European model primarily in its:

## Options

- **A**: Emphasis on stakeholder interests and two-tier board structures
- **B**: Focus on shareholder primacy, dispersed ownership, and single-tier boards
- **C**: Requirement for government ownership of major companies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Anglo-American model: shareholder-focused, dispersed ownership, active markets for corporate control, single-tier boards mixing executive and non-executive directors. Continental European: often two-tier boards (supervisory and management), stakeholder orientation, concentrated ownership, stronger employee representation. Each reflects different legal and cultural traditions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the continental European model
- Option C is not characteristic of either model
