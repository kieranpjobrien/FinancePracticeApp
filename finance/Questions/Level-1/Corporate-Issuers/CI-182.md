# Question

A company's preferred stock pays a $5 annual dividend and trades at $50 per share. The cost of preferred equity is:

## Options

- **A**: 5%
- **B**: 10%
- **C**: 15%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cost of preferred stock = Annual dividend / Current price = $5 / $50 = 10%. Preferred stock is a perpetuity—constant dividend with no maturity. Unlike debt, preferred dividends are not tax-deductible, so no tax adjustment needed. Cost lies between debt and common equity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may confuse dividend with percentage
- Option C may incorrectly add a premium
