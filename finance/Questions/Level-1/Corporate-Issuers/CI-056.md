# Question

"Say on pay" provisions give shareholders:

## Options

- **A**: The power to set executive salaries directly
- **B**: An advisory vote on executive compensation
- **C**: Veto power over all compensation decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Say on pay allows shareholders to vote (typically non-binding/advisory) on executive compensation packages. While usually not mandatory, negative votes pressure boards to modify pay practices. Some jurisdictions require say on pay votes periodically. It enhances shareholder voice on compensation matters.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates shareholder authority—votes are typically advisory
- Option C overstates binding authority
