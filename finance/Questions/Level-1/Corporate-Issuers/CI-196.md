# Question

ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) considerations in corporate governance:

## Options

- **A**: Are only relevant to socially responsible investors
- **B**: Can affect long-term value creation and risk management
- **C**: Have no impact on financial performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ESG factors: increasingly recognized as material to long-term value. Environmental risks (climate, resources), social factors (labor, community relations), and governance quality affect sustainability. Investors increasingly integrate ESG; companies with strong ESG may have lower cost of capital and better risk profiles.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A understates broad investor interest in ESG
- Option C ignores growing evidence of ESG-performance linkages
