# Question

When setting credit policy, a company should consider:

## Options

- **A**: Only the potential increase in sales
- **B**: The tradeoff between increased sales and the costs of credit extension
- **C**: Exclusively the industry standard terms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit policy optimization: balance higher sales from easier credit against costs—bad debts, collection expenses, carrying costs of receivables, opportunity cost of delayed cash. Optimal policy maximizes net benefit. Must consider customer creditworthiness, competitive environment, and company's cost of capital.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores significant costs of credit
- Option C doesn't optimize for company-specific factors
