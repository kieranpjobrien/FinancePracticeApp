# Question

Just-in-time (JIT) inventory management:

## Options

- **A**: Maintains large safety stock levels
- **B**: Minimizes inventory by receiving materials only as needed
- **C**: Is only applicable to service companies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

JIT minimizes inventory holding by coordinating deliveries precisely with production needs. This reduces carrying costs, frees working capital, and highlights production problems (no buffer inventory). Requires reliable suppliers, quality inputs, and smooth production. Risk: supply disruption vulnerability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts JIT's zero-inventory goal
- Option C—JIT was developed for manufacturing (Toyota)
