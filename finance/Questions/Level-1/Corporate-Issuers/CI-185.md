# Question

A company has EBIT of $100 million and interest expense of $20 million. Its interest coverage ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 20%
- **B**: 5.0x
- **C**: 0.2x

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest coverage ratio = EBIT / Interest expense = $100 million / $20 million = 5.0x. Measures ability to meet interest obligations from operating earnings. Higher ratio indicates greater debt service capacity. Credit rating agencies use this ratio to assess creditworthiness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A calculates interest as percentage of EBIT
- Option C inverts the ratio
