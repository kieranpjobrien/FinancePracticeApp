# Question

An aging schedule for accounts receivable shows:

## Options

- **A**: The historical sales growth rate
- **B**: The distribution of receivables by age/due date
- **C**: Customer credit scores

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Aging schedules categorize receivables by time outstanding: current, 30-60 days, 60-90 days, over 90 days. This reveals collection patterns, identifies potentially uncollectible accounts, and assesses credit policy effectiveness. Rising older categories signals collection problems.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes sales analysis
- Option C describes credit evaluation, not receivables status
