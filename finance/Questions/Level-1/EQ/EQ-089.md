# Question

Sum-of-the-parts valuation is most appropriate when:

## Options

- **A**: A company operates a single, focused business
- **B**: A company has distinct business segments with different risk and growth characteristics
- **C**: All business segments have identical multiples

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sum-of-the-parts: value each business segment separately using appropriate methods/multiples, then sum to get total enterprise value. Useful for conglomerates or diversified companies where segments differ significantly. May reveal "conglomerate discount" if market values company below sum of parts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't require segment-by-segment valuation
- Option C makes SOTP unnecessary—single multiple would suffice
