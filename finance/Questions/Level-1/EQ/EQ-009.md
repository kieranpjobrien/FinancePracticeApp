# Question

A market-capitalization weighted index:

## Options

- **A**: Gives equal weight to all constituent stocks
- **B**: Weights stocks by their price multiplied by shares outstanding
- **C**: Requires daily rebalancing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market-cap weighted index: weight = (Price × Shares outstanding) / Total market cap of index. Larger companies have greater influence. Self-rebalancing—no trading needed as prices change. Examples: S&P 500, MSCI indices. Float-adjusted versions use only publicly tradable shares.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes equal-weighted indices
- Option C is incorrect—cap-weighted indices self-adjust
