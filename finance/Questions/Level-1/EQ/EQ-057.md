# Question

Herding behavior in financial markets:

## Options

- **A**: Always leads to correctly priced securities
- **B**: Can amplify price movements and contribute to bubbles and crashes
- **C**: Is illegal in most jurisdictions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Herding: investors follow others rather than analyzing independently. Can create self-reinforcing price movements—buying begets more buying (bubbles), selling begets more selling (crashes). Professional managers may herd to avoid career risk of underperforming peers. Can push prices away from fundamentals.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—herding can cause mispricing
- Option C is incorrect—herding itself isn't illegal
