# Question

Earnings yield (E/P ratio) is useful because it:

## Options

- **A**: Is always higher than the P/E ratio
- **B**: Can be compared directly to bond yields and is defined even when earnings are positive
- **C**: Measures dividend payments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Earnings yield = EPS / Price = 1 / P/E. Expressed as percentage, enabling direct comparison to interest rates and bond yields. Higher earnings yield suggests cheaper valuation. Useful in equity risk premium calculations. Works when earnings positive; P/E undefined when earnings negative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses the relationship—E/P is inverse of P/E
- Option C describes dividend yield, not earnings yield
