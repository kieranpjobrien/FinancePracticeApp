# Question

A book-to-bill ratio above 1.0 indicates:

## Options

- **A**: The company is shipping more than it is receiving in orders
- **B**: New orders exceed shipments, suggesting growing demand
- **C**: The company should be concerned about declining sales

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Book-to-bill: new orders (bookings) divided by shipments (billings). Ratio > 1: orders growing faster than shipments, backlog building, demand strength. Ratio < 1: shipments exceeding orders, backlog declining. Important metric for semiconductor, capital goods, and other long-cycle industries.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes book-to-bill < 1
- Option C is opposite of what ratio > 1 suggests
