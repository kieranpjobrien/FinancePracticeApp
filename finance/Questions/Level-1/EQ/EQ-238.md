# Question

Payment for order flow (PFOF) involves:

## Options

- **A**: Investors paying brokers to execute trades faster
- **B**: Market makers paying brokers for the right to execute retail customer orders
- **C**: Exchanges paying companies to list their shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PFOF: market makers pay brokers for routing retail orders to them. Enables commission-free trading. Controversy: potential conflict of interest—broker incentivized to route for payment rather than best execution. Market makers profit from spread on retail flow. Regulatory scrutiny ongoing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the payment direction
- Option C describes listing fees, unrelated to PFOF
