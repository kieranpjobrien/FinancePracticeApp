# Question

Behavioral finance challenges market efficiency by identifying:

## Options

- **A**: That all investors are always rational
- **B**: Systematic investor biases and cognitive errors that can cause mispricings
- **C**: That transaction costs don't exist

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Behavioral finance: documents systematic psychological biases—overconfidence, loss aversion, anchoring, herding, mental accounting. These can cause prices to deviate from fundamental values. Explains anomalies and bubbles. Challenges assumption of fully rational investors underlying efficient markets theory.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite—behavioral finance argues against this
- Option C is not a behavioral finance argument
