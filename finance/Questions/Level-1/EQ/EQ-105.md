# Question

Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Stock prices fully adjusting immediately to earnings news
- **B**: Stock prices continuing to move in the direction of earnings surprises for weeks after announcements
- **C**: Earnings having no impact on stock prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PEAD: prices don't fully adjust immediately to earnings surprises. Positive surprises followed by continued positive returns; negative surprises followed by continued negative returns. Anomaly challenging semi-strong efficiency. Possible explanations: investor underreaction, transaction costs preventing arbitrage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes efficient market prediction
- Option C contradicts observed earnings impact
