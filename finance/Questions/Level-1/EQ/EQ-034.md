# Question

When analyzing a company's revenue, an analyst should consider:

## Options

- **A**: Only the total revenue number
- **B**: Volume growth, pricing power, product mix, and geographic segmentation
- **C**: Only year-over-year percentage changes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Revenue analysis requires decomposition: volume vs. price, organic vs. acquisition-driven, by product/segment/geography. Understanding drivers reveals sustainability and quality of growth. Pricing power indicates competitive position; volume trends show market demand. Single revenue figure obscures important dynamics.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A lacks analytical depth
- Option C ignores composition and drivers
