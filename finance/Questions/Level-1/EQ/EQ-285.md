# Question

The Present Value of Growth Opportunities (PVGO) represents:

## Options

- **A**: The value of a company if it never grows
- **B**: The portion of stock value attributable to future growth investments
- **C**: The historical growth rate of dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PVGO = Stock Price - (E₁/r). Decomposes value into no-growth value (earnings capitalized at required return) and growth component. High PVGO/Price ratio indicates market expects significant growth. Useful for understanding how much of current price depends on growth materializing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the no-growth component (E₁/r)
- Option C is backward-looking, not a value component
