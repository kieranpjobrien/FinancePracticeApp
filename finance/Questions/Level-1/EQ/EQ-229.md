# Question

The margin of safety concept in value investing refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The maximum amount of debt a company should carry
- **B**: Buying securities at a sufficient discount to intrinsic value to protect against errors
- **C**: Stop-loss orders to limit downside

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Margin of safety (Benjamin Graham): buy below estimated intrinsic value to provide cushion for estimation errors, unforeseen events, and market volatility. Larger margin = greater protection. Conservative approach recognizes uncertainty in valuation. Central concept in value investing philosophy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relates to debt capacity, not valuation
- Option C describes trading technique, not valuation concept
