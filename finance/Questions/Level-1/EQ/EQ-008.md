# Question

In a price-weighted index, stocks are weighted by their:

## Options

- **A**: Market capitalization
- **B**: Share price
- **C**: Trading volume

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price-weighted index: each stock's weight equals its price divided by sum of all prices. Higher-priced stocks have greater influence regardless of company size. Example: Dow Jones Industrial Average. Stock splits require divisor adjustments. Simple but criticized because price alone doesn't reflect economic significance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market-cap weighted indices
- Option C is not used for standard index weighting
