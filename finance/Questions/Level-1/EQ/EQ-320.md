# Question

Volume-weighted average price (VWAP) is used as:

## Options

- **A**: A method to set opening prices for stocks
- **B**: A benchmark for measuring execution quality of trades over a period
- **C**: A regulatory requirement for all trades

## Answer

B

## Explanation

VWAP: average price weighted by volume throughout trading period. Benchmark for institutional execution—trading at or below VWAP considered good buy execution. VWAP algorithms break large orders to track intraday volume pattern. Doesn't account for market impact of the order being measured.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes opening auction process
- Option C—VWAP is voluntary benchmark, not requirement
