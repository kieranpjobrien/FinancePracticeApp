# Question

The representativeness bias causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Carefully calculate statistical probabilities before making decisions
- **B**: Classify situations based on superficial similarities rather than underlying probabilities
- **C**: Underweight recent information in their analysis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Representativeness: judging likelihood based on how similar something is to a stereotype, ignoring base rates and sample size. Examples: assuming small sample performance will continue, classifying "hot" stock as good investment based on recent returns. Leads to overreaction and trend-chasing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational statistical analysis
- Option C describes conservatism bias, not representativeness
