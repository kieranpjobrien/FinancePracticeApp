# Question

A company with high operating leverage will experience:

## Options

- **A**: Stable earnings regardless of revenue changes
- **B**: Amplified profit changes relative to revenue changes
- **C**: Lower fixed costs than competitors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operating leverage: degree to which costs are fixed. High fixed costs mean contribution margin flows to profit (or loss) as revenue changes. DOL = % change in EBIT / % change in Sales. High operating leverage: greater profit sensitivity to sales, higher break-even point, more earnings volatility.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes low operating leverage scenario
- Option C is opposite—high leverage means high fixed costs
