# Question

Anchoring bias causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Constantly update their views based on new information
- **B**: Give disproportionate weight to initial or reference information when making decisions
- **C**: Ignore all historical data

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Anchoring: over-reliance on initial piece of information (the anchor). Subsequent adjustments from anchor are typically insufficient. Examples: anchoring on purchase price, analyst targets, or historical highs. Can cause slow adjustment to new information and contribute to market inefficiencies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational updating, not anchoring
- Option C is opposite—anchoring means clinging to reference points
