# Question

The difference between a broker and a dealer is that:

## Options

- **A**: Brokers trade for their own account while dealers trade on behalf of clients
- **B**: Brokers act as agents for clients while dealers trade as principals for their own account
- **C**: There is no difference between brokers and dealers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Broker: agent who executes trades on behalf of clients, earning commissions. Dealer: principal who trades for their own account, profiting from bid-ask spreads. Broker-dealers combine both functions. Market makers are dealers who provide liquidity by continuously quoting bid and ask prices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the definitions
- Option C ignores the distinct roles
