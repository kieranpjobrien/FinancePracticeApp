# Question

An appropriate benchmark index should be:

## Options

- **A**: Unrelated to the investment strategy being measured
- **B**: Investable, representative, and specified in advance
- **C**: Changed frequently to show favorable performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Good benchmark characteristics: relevant (matches strategy), investable (can be replicated), measurable, unambiguous, specified in advance (not cherry-picked ex post), reflective of manager's investment universe. Common benchmarks: S&P 500 for US large-cap, Russell 2000 for small-cap, MSCI EAFE for international.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would make benchmark meaningless for evaluation
- Option C introduces cherry-picking bias, invalidating comparison
