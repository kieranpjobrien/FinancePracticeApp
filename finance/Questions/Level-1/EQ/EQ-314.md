# Question

Deferred revenue (unearned revenue) on a balance sheet represents:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue that will never be earned
- **B**: Cash received for services not yet delivered, a liability until earned
- **C**: Accounts receivable from customers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred revenue: liability representing obligation to deliver goods/services for payment already received. Examples: subscription payments, prepaid services, advance deposits. Converted to revenue as delivery occurs. Growing deferred revenue often positive—indicates future revenue backlog.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—deferred revenue will typically be earned
- Option C—receivables are assets (opposite of deferred revenue)
