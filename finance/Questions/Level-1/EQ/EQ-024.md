# Question

The price-to-book (P/B) ratio is most useful for valuing:

## Options

- **A**: High-growth technology companies with minimal tangible assets
- **B**: Financial institutions and companies with significant tangible assets on the balance sheet
- **C**: Start-up companies with negative earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

P/B = Price / Book value per share. Most relevant when book value meaningfully reflects economic value—financial institutions (assets at market), asset-heavy companies. Less useful when intangibles dominate (tech) or book value is distorted. Banks often valued relative to book value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A has intangible-heavy assets where book is less meaningful
- Option C may have minimal or negative book value
