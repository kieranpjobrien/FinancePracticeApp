# Question

A three-stage dividend discount model is most appropriate for a company that:

## Options

- **A**: Has stable, constant growth expected indefinitely
- **B**: Is expected to have high initial growth, followed by transition to mature stable growth
- **C**: Will never pay dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Three-stage DDM: Stage 1 = high growth, Stage 2 = transition (declining growth), Stage 3 = mature stable growth (terminal value). Appropriate for growth companies expected to mature. More realistic than constant growth for firms not yet in steady state. Requires estimates for duration and growth rates of each stage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A only needs single-stage Gordon model
- Option C requires FCFE or other models, not DDM
