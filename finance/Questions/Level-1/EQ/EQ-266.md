# Question

Dividend yield is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Stock price divided by annual dividend
- **B**: Annual dividend per share divided by stock price
- **C**: Total dividends paid divided by net income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividend yield = Annual DPS / Stock Price. Measures income return component of total return. Higher yields typically from mature companies with limited growth. Can use trailing (past year) or forward (next year's expected) dividends. Important for income-focused investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is inverted
- Option C describes payout ratio, not dividend yield
