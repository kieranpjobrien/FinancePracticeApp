# Question

In a DCF valuation, terminal value typically represents:

## Options

- **A**: A small portion of total value for mature companies
- **B**: A significant portion of total value, especially for growth companies
- **C**: Exactly 50% of total value in all cases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Terminal value: captures value beyond explicit forecast period. Often represents 60-80% or more of total value, especially for growth companies where much value comes from distant cash flows. Sensitivity to terminal growth rate and exit multiple assumptions is high—small changes significantly impact valuation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A understates terminal value importance
- Option C is arbitrary—percentage varies by company
