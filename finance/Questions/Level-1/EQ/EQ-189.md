# Question

A company with a high dividend payout ratio likely:

## Options

- **A**: Has significant reinvestment opportunities requiring retained earnings
- **B**: Is mature with limited growth opportunities
- **C**: Is in the early growth stage of its lifecycle

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High payout ratio: distributes large share of earnings as dividends. Typical of mature, stable companies with limited profitable reinvestment options. Growth companies retain earnings to fund expansion. Payout policy reflects growth opportunities, capital needs, and shareholder preferences.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would have low payout to fund investments
- Option C describes companies that typically retain earnings for growth
