# Question

The overreaction hypothesis suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Markets correctly price all available information immediately
- **B**: Investors tend to overweight recent information, causing prices to overshoot fair value before correcting
- **C**: Stock prices never deviate from fundamental value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Overreaction: behavioral phenomenon where investors overweight recent news or trends. Leads to price movements beyond justified levels, followed by reversals. Basis for contrarian strategies—buying past losers, selling past winners. DeBondt and Thaler documented long-term reversals supporting this hypothesis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes efficient market hypothesis
- Option C claims perfect pricing, contradicted by overreaction evidence
