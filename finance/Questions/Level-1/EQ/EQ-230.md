# Question

Herding behavior in financial markets can contribute to:

## Options

- **A**: Prices that always reflect fundamental value
- **B**: Bubbles and crashes as investors follow the crowd rather than fundamentals
- **C**: More efficient price discovery

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Herding: following others' actions rather than independent analysis. Causes: reputation concerns (career risk of being wrong alone), information cascades, social proof, FOMO. Leads to: momentum, bubbles, crashes, excess volatility. Prices deviate from fundamentals as crowd psychology dominates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts herding effects on prices
- Option C—herding impedes, not improves, efficiency
