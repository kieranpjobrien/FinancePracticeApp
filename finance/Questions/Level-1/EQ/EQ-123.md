# Question

American Depositary Receipts (ADRs) allow:

## Options

- **A**: US companies to list on foreign exchanges
- **B**: US investors to invest in foreign companies through securities traded on US exchanges
- **C**: Foreign investors to avoid all US regulations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ADRs: negotiable certificates issued by US banks representing shares in foreign companies. Benefits for US investors: trade in US dollars, on US exchanges, during US market hours, with US settlement. Types: sponsored (company involvement) vs. unsponsored; Level I, II, III with varying disclosure requirements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the direction—ADRs bring foreign stocks to US
- Option C is wrong—ADRs must comply with applicable SEC regulations
