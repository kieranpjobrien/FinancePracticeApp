# Question

Index reconstitution involves:

## Options

- **A**: Adjusting weights back to target allocations
- **B**: Adding or removing securities to maintain index representativeness
- **C**: Calculating total returns including dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Reconstitution: periodic review of index membership. Companies may be added (meet criteria, IPOs, spin-offs) or removed (fail to meet criteria, mergers, delistings, bankruptcies). Ensures index remains representative of its target market or strategy. Usually performed quarterly or annually.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rebalancing, not reconstitution
- Option C describes return calculation methodology
