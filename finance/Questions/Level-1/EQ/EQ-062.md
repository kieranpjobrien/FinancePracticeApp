# Question

The PEG ratio (P/E to growth) is useful for:

## Options

- **A**: Comparing companies with the same growth rates
- **B**: Comparing valuations of companies with different growth rates
- **C**: Measuring dividend yield

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PEG = P/E ratio / Expected earnings growth rate. Adjusts P/E for growth expectations. PEG < 1 often considered undervalued (low P/E relative to growth); PEG > 1 may be overvalued. Enables comparison across growth rates. Limitations: assumes linear relationship between P/E and growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't require PEG—P/E comparison works
- Option C is unrelated to PEG calculation
