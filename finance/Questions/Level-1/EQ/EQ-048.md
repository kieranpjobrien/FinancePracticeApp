# Question

A margin call occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: An investor wants to increase their position
- **B**: The equity in a margin account falls below the maintenance margin requirement
- **C**: The broker wants to close the account

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Margin call: when account equity drops below maintenance margin (typically 25-30% of position value), broker demands additional funds. Investor must deposit cash or securities to restore equity. Failure to meet call allows broker to liquidate positions. Protects broker from default on margin loan.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes adding to position, not a margin call
- Option C is too vague—margin calls have specific triggers
