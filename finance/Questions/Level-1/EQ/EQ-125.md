# Question

Using the Gordon Growth Model, the justified leading P/E ratio equals:

## Options

- **A**: (1 - b) / (r - g)
- **B**: D₁ / (r - g)
- **C**: EPS₀ × (1 + g) / r

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Justified leading P/E from Gordon model: P₀/E₁ = (D₁/E₁) / (r - g) = (1 - b) / (r - g), where b is retention ratio, (1-b) is payout ratio. Higher payout, lower required return, or higher growth increases justified P/E. Connects dividend valuation to relative valuation multiples.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is the DDM formula for price, not P/E ratio
- Option C incorrectly structures the relationship
