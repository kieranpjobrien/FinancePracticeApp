# Question

T+1 settlement means:

## Options

- **A**: Trades are settled immediately
- **B**: Settlement occurs one business day after the trade date
- **C**: Trades are never settled

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T+1: trade date plus one business day for settlement (exchange of cash and securities). US moved to T+1 in 2024 (from T+2). Reduces counterparty risk and capital requirements. Some markets still T+2 or longer. "T" refers to trade execution date.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes same-day settlement
- Option C contradicts the settlement concept
