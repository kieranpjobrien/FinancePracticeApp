# Question

Growth investing focuses on:

## Options

- **A**: Companies trading at low multiples relative to earnings
- **B**: Companies with above-average expected earnings or revenue growth
- **C**: Companies with the highest dividend yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Growth investing: seeks companies with superior growth potential—revenue, earnings, market expansion. Willing to pay higher multiples for faster growth. Less emphasis on current valuation, more on future potential. Tech, healthcare often growth-oriented. Contrasts with value investing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes value investing
- Option C describes income/dividend investing
