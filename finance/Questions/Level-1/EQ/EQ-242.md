# Question

A Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC) is:

## Options

- **A**: A traditional operating company going public
- **B**: A shell company that raises IPO funds to acquire a private company, taking it public
- **C**: A government-sponsored investment vehicle

## Answer

B

## Explanation

SPAC: "blank check" company that IPOs with no operations, seeking acquisition target. Sponsors identify target, shareholders vote to approve. Alternative to traditional IPO—faster, price certainty, less regulatory scrutiny. Risks: sponsor incentives, dilution from warrants, limited due diligence time.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes traditional IPO
- Option C describes government entities like GSEs
