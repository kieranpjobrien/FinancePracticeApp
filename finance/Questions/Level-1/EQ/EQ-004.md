# Question

In a short sale, the investor:

## Options

- **A**: Buys shares expecting the price to rise
- **B**: Borrows shares and sells them, expecting to buy them back at a lower price
- **C**: Sells shares they already own

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Short selling: borrow shares from broker, sell immediately, hope to buy back (cover) at lower price. Profit = sale price - repurchase price - borrowing costs. Risk: unlimited loss potential if price rises. Requires margin account and paying any dividends to lender during short period.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a long position
- Option C describes a regular sale, not a short sale
