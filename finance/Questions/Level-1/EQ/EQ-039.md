# Question

American Depository Receipts (ADRs) allow U.S. investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Only invest in U.S. companies
- **B**: Invest in foreign companies' shares through U.S. dollar-denominated securities
- **C**: Avoid all currency risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ADRs: negotiable certificates representing shares in foreign companies, traded on U.S. exchanges in dollars. Depository bank holds underlying foreign shares, issues receipts. Provides access to foreign stocks without direct foreign market trading. Currency risk remains—underlying shares denominated in foreign currency.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—ADRs provide foreign exposure
- Option C is incorrect—currency risk remains as underlying is foreign
