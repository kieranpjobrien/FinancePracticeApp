# Question

Price-to-cash-flow ratios are sometimes preferred over P/E ratios because:

## Options

- **A**: Cash flow is always higher than earnings
- **B**: Cash flow is less subject to accounting manipulation than earnings
- **C**: Cash flow ignores operating performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash flow advantages: harder to manipulate than accrual earnings, less affected by accounting choices (depreciation methods, revenue recognition). Different cash flow measures: CFO, FCF, EBITDA. Useful when earnings quality is questionable or for capital-intensive businesses with high non-cash charges.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is not always true—depends on working capital and other items
- Option C is opposite—cash flow reflects operations
