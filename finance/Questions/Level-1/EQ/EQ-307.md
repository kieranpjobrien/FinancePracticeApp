# Question

A highly liquid stock is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Wide bid-ask spreads and low trading volume
- **B**: Narrow bid-ask spreads and high trading volume, enabling easy execution
- **C**: Infrequent trading and price volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity indicators: tight spreads (low transaction cost), high volume, market depth (large orders without impact), continuous trading. Allows entry/exit with minimal price impact. Large-cap stocks typically more liquid. Illiquid stocks require liquidity premium in valuation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes illiquid characteristics
- Option C describes illiquid conditions
