# Question

Cumulative preferred stock provides that:

## Options

- **A**: Dividends can never be skipped
- **B**: Any missed dividends accumulate and must be paid before common dividends
- **C**: The dividend rate increases each year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cumulative preferred: if company skips preferred dividends, they accumulate as "dividends in arrears." All accumulated preferred dividends must be paid before any common dividends can be distributed. Protects preferred holders from being permanently disadvantaged by dividend omissions. Most preferred stock is cumulative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—dividends can be skipped, they just accumulate
- Option C describes a different feature (escalating dividends)
