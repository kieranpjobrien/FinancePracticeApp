# Question

Stock-based compensation (SBC) should be considered a real expense because:

## Options

- **A**: It has no economic value
- **B**: It represents value transferred from existing shareholders to employees
- **C**: It never affects cash flows

## Answer

B

## Explanation

SBC as expense: represents dilution—value transfers from shareholders to employees. Non-cash expense but real economic cost. Many tech companies exclude from "adjusted" metrics but analysts should include. High SBC relative to revenue may signal excessive dilution or reliance on equity compensation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—SBC has significant economic value
- Option C—SBC does affect cash taxes and indirectly impacts cash
