# Question

An industry with a few dominant firms and high barriers to entry is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Perfect competition
- **B**: Oligopoly
- **C**: Monopolistic competition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Oligopoly: few large firms, high entry barriers, interdependent pricing/strategy. Actions by one firm affect others. Examples: airlines, telecommunications, automobiles. Can maintain above-normal profits if firms avoid destructive competition. Opposite of perfect competition (many firms, easy entry, no pricing power).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes many firms, no barriers, price-taking
- Option C describes many firms with differentiated products
