# Question

When analyzing a company's credit risk, a declining interest coverage ratio most likely indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Improved ability to service debt obligations
- **B**: Deteriorating capacity to meet interest payments from operating earnings
- **C**: Reduced financial leverage

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest coverage ratio: EBIT / Interest expense. Measures ability to pay interest from operating earnings. Declining ratio indicates either falling operating income or rising interest costs (or both). Red flag for credit risk—may signal approaching financial distress if trend continues.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—declining coverage means reduced, not improved, capacity
- Option C is wrong—coverage and leverage are different concepts
