# Question

The H-model for dividend valuation assumes:

## Options

- **A**: Constant growth forever
- **B**: High initial growth that declines linearly to a stable long-term rate
- **C**: No dividends paid

## Answer

B

## Explanation

H-model: approximates multi-stage growth with linear decline from high initial growth to long-term sustainable rate. Formula: V₀ = [D₀(1+gL) + D₀H(gS-gL)] / (r-gL), where H is half the high-growth period length. Simpler than explicit multi-stage but captures growth transition.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Gordon Growth Model
- Option C makes dividend models inapplicable
