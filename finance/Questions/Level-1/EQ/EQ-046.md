# Question

Exchange-traded markets differ from over-the-counter (OTC) markets in that exchanges:

## Options

- **A**: Have no listing requirements
- **B**: Provide centralized trading with standardized rules and greater transparency
- **C**: Only handle bond transactions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Exchanges: centralized venues with listing requirements, standardized trading rules, transparency, and regulatory oversight. Examples: NYSE, NASDAQ. OTC markets: decentralized dealer networks, less regulation, less transparency. Exchanges generally offer better price discovery and liquidity for listed securities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—exchanges have strict listing requirements
- Option C is incorrect—exchanges primarily trade equities
