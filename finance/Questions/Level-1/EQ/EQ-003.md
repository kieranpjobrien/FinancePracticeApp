# Question

A limit order to buy a stock differs from a market order in that a limit order:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantees immediate execution
- **B**: Specifies a maximum price the buyer is willing to pay
- **C**: Must be executed at the current market price

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Limit order: specifies maximum buy price (or minimum sell price). Provides price certainty but may not execute if market doesn't reach the limit price. Market order: executes immediately at best available price, guaranteeing execution but not price. Limit orders offer price control; market orders offer execution certainty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market orders
- Option C also describes market order behavior
