# Question

An initial public offering (IPO) occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A public company issues additional shares
- **B**: A private company offers its shares to the public for the first time
- **C**: Existing shareholders sell their shares on the exchange

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IPO: first sale of shares to the public, transforming private company to public. Provides capital for growth and liquidity for existing shareholders. Underwritten by investment banks who set price and distribute shares. Post-IPO, shares trade on secondary markets. Regulated process with disclosure requirements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a seasoned/secondary offering
- Option C describes secondary market trading
