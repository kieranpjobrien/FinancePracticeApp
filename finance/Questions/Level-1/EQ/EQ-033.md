# Question

Cyclical industries are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Stable demand regardless of economic conditions
- **B**: Revenue and profits that fluctuate significantly with the business cycle
- **C**: Government-regulated pricing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cyclical industries: demand tied to economic activity—expand in booms, contract in recessions. Examples: autos, housing, luxury goods, travel. High operating leverage amplifies earnings swings. Investors may underweight cyclicals late in expansions. Contrast with defensive/non-cyclical industries (utilities, consumer staples).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes defensive industries
- Option C describes regulated industries, which can be cyclical or not
