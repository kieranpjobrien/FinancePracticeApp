# Question

Global Depository Receipts (GDRs) differ from ADRs in that GDRs:

## Options

- **A**: Can only be traded in the United States
- **B**: Are typically traded on exchanges outside the United States, often in Europe
- **C**: Represent only European companies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GDRs: similar to ADRs but trade on non-U.S. exchanges (commonly London, Luxembourg). Allow companies to access international capital without listing in home country or U.S. May be denominated in euros or dollars. ADRs specifically for U.S. markets; GDRs for international markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes ADRs, not GDRs
- Option C is incorrect—GDRs can represent companies from any country
