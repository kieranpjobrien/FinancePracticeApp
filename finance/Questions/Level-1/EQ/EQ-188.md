# Question

Declining inventory turnover ratio may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: Improved efficiency in inventory management
- **B**: Slowing sales or inventory obsolescence
- **C**: Better supplier relationships

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inventory turnover = COGS / Average inventory. Declining turnover means inventory growing faster than sales. Concerns: demand weakness, obsolescence risk, storage costs, potential write-downs. Compare to industry peers and historical trend. Some buildup may be intentional (anticipating demand), but persistent decline is concerning.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—improved efficiency would increase turnover
- Option C doesn't directly affect inventory turnover calculation
