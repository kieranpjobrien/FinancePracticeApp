# Question

The explicit forecast period in DCF valuation typically:

## Options

- **A**: Extends indefinitely into the future
- **B**: Covers 5-10 years until the company reaches steady state
- **C**: Only includes the current year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Explicit forecast period: years with detailed projections before terminal value. Length depends on business visibility—typically 5-10 years. Should extend until company reaches competitive equilibrium/steady state. Detailed revenue, margin, investment assumptions for each year. Terminal value captures value beyond.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—explicit forecasts are finite; terminal value covers perpetuity
- Option C is too short for meaningful DCF
