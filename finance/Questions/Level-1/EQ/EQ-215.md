# Question

Overconfidence bias typically causes investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Underestimate their ability to pick winning investments
- **B**: Trade excessively and underestimate risks
- **C**: Invest too conservatively

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Overconfidence: overestimating one's knowledge, ability to predict, and precision of estimates. Leads to: excessive trading (hurts returns through costs), underdiversification (concentrated bets), underestimating risks. Studies show overconfident traders earn lower returns. More prevalent in men.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes lack of confidence
- Option C describes risk-averse behavior opposite to overconfidence effects
