# Question

High insider ownership (management owning significant company stock) can be positive because:

## Options

- **A**: Insiders can easily manipulate stock prices
- **B**: Management interests are aligned with shareholders
- **C**: It guarantees company success

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Insider ownership alignment: managers who own stock benefit from share price appreciation like other shareholders. Reduces agency costs—incentive to maximize value, not personal perks. However, excessive insider control can marginalize minority shareholders. Balance important. Monitor insider buying/selling for signals.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes manipulation concern, not benefit
- Option C—ownership aligns incentives but doesn't guarantee outcomes
