# Question

The quick ratio (acid-test ratio) differs from the current ratio by excluding:

## Options

- **A**: Accounts receivable
- **B**: Inventory and prepaid expenses
- **C**: Cash and marketable securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quick ratio = (Current assets - Inventory - Prepaids) / Current liabilities. More conservative than current ratio—excludes less liquid current assets. Focuses on assets quickly convertible to cash. Useful when inventory is slow-moving or obsolescence risk exists.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—receivables are included in quick ratio
- Option C—cash is the most liquid asset, definitely included
