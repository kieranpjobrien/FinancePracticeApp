# Question

An earnings surprise occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A company reports earnings exactly in line with analyst expectations
- **B**: Reported earnings differ significantly from analyst consensus estimates
- **C**: A company announces it will stop reporting earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Earnings surprise: actual EPS differs from analyst consensus. Positive surprise: actual > expected. Negative surprise: actual < expected. Market reaction typically reflects surprise magnitude and direction. Post-earnings announcement drift suggests market doesn't fully incorporate surprise immediately.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes earnings "in line"—no surprise
- Option C describes disclosure change, not earnings surprise
