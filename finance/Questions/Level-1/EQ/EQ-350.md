# Question

Under current accounting standards, operating leases:

## Options

- **A**: Are not recorded on the balance sheet
- **B**: Are capitalized as right-of-use assets with corresponding lease liabilities
- **C**: Are treated identically to cash purchases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Lease accounting (ASC 842/IFRS 16): operating leases now on balance sheet as right-of-use assets and lease liabilities. Improves comparability between companies that lease vs. buy. Increases reported debt/leverage. Expense pattern differs between operating and finance leases.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A described old rules before ASC 842/IFRS 16
- Option C—leases still treated differently from purchases
