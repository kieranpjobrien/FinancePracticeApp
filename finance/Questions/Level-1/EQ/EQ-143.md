# Question

Market depth refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The number of different stocks listed on an exchange
- **B**: The ability to execute large orders without significantly affecting the price
- **C**: The time it takes for orders to be executed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market depth: measure of liquidity indicating volume available at various price levels. Deep markets can absorb large orders with minimal price impact. Shown in order books with quantities at each price level. Important for institutional investors executing large trades. Related to but distinct from bid-ask spread.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes breadth of exchange listings
- Option C describes execution speed, a different aspect of market quality
