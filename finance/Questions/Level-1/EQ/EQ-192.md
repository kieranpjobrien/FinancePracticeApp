# Question

A control premium in acquisition valuation represents:

## Options

- **A**: A discount applied because the target has too much control over its operations
- **B**: The additional amount paid above market price to gain control of a company
- **C**: The value of minority shares in a company

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Control premium: amount above trading price acquirer pays to gain majority control. Reflects value of: changing management, strategic decisions, accessing cash flows, synergies, eliminating minority shareholders. Typically 20-40% above unaffected stock price. Opposite concept is minority discount.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the concept
- Option C describes minority interest, not control premium
