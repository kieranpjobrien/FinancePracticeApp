# Question

Home bias in investing refers to investors':

## Options

- **A**: Preference for real estate investments
- **B**: Tendency to overweight domestic securities despite diversification benefits of international exposure
- **C**: Requirement to invest only in their home country

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Home bias: investors overallocate to domestic markets despite global diversification benefits. Causes: familiarity, information costs, currency risk aversion, behavioral comfort. Results in suboptimal portfolios. Studies show home bias exists across most countries. International allocation typically recommended for diversification.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses "home" with real estate
- Option C suggests requirement, but it's a behavioral preference
