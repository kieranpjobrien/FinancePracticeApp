# Question

Over-the-counter (OTC) markets differ from exchanges in that OTC markets:

## Options

- **A**: Have a central physical trading floor
- **B**: Involve dealers who make markets and negotiate prices directly with customers
- **C**: Only trade large-cap, highly liquid stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

OTC markets: decentralized, dealer-based trading. Dealers quote bid/ask, hold inventory, trade from own accounts. No central order book or physical location. Includes bonds, derivatives, smaller stocks, ADRs. Less transparent than exchanges, more negotiated pricing. Examples: NASDAQ (historically), bond markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes traditional exchange structure
- Option C is opposite—OTC often includes smaller, less liquid securities
