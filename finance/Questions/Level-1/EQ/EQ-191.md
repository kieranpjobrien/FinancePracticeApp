# Question

A conglomerate discount refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The premium investors pay for diversified companies
- **B**: The tendency for diversified companies to trade below the sum of their parts' values
- **C**: Discounts offered to institutional investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conglomerate discount: market value less than sum of individual segments valued separately. Reasons: complexity, lack of focus, cross-subsidization of underperforming units, agency costs, difficulty for analysts to value. Activist investors often push for spinoffs to unlock this value.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite (a premium)
- Option C refers to transaction discounts, not valuation concept
