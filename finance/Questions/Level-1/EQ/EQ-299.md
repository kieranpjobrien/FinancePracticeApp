# Question

Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) differ from traditional mutual funds in that ETFs:

## Options

- **A**: Can only be purchased directly from the fund company
- **B**: Trade on exchanges throughout the day like stocks
- **C**: Never track indices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ETF advantages: intraday trading (price changes continuously), typically lower expense ratios, tax efficiency (in-kind redemptions), transparency. Mutual funds: end-of-day NAV pricing, can only transact with fund. Both can be passive (index) or active. ETF structure increasingly popular for various strategies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes mutual fund purchase process
- Option C—most ETFs do track indices
