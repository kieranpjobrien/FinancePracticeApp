# Question

Market fragmentation in equity trading refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Trading concentrated on a single exchange
- **B**: Order flow spread across multiple venues including exchanges, dark pools, and ATSs
- **C**: Only institutional investors trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fragmentation: trading occurs across many venues—NYSE, NASDAQ, BATS, IEX, dark pools, wholesalers. Increases competition but complicates best execution. Smart order routers needed to find best prices across venues. Reg NMS ensures interconnection between markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes consolidated trading (opposite)
- Option C is about participants, not venue structure
