# Question

Best execution in securities trading refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Always getting the lowest possible stock price
- **B**: Achieving the most favorable terms reasonably available under the circumstances
- **C**: Executing all trades at the market open

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Best execution: duty to seek most favorable terms for client trades considering price, costs, speed, likelihood of execution, settlement. Not just lowest price—includes timing, market impact, broker quality. Regulatory requirement for fiduciaries. Balances multiple factors based on circumstances.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A oversimplifies—lowest price isn't always best execution
- Option C is about timing preference, not execution quality
