# Question

When valuing companies in emerging markets, analysts often adjust for country risk by:

## Options

- **A**: Using the same discount rate as developed market companies
- **B**: Adding a country risk premium to the required return
- **C**: Ignoring political and economic instability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Country risk premium: additional return required to compensate for political risk, economic instability, currency risk, weaker property rights, regulatory uncertainty. Methods: sovereign spread approach, country credit rating approach, relative volatility adjustment. Increases discount rate, reducing valuation for given cash flows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores additional risks of EM investing
- Option C would underestimate risk and overvalue assets
