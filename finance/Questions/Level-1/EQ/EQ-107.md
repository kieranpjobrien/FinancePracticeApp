# Question

Confirmation bias leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Seek out information that contradicts their existing beliefs
- **B**: Favor information confirming their existing views while dismissing contradictory evidence
- **C**: Make completely random investment decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Confirmation bias: tendency to search for, interpret, and remember information confirming pre-existing beliefs. Investors may ignore negative news about holdings or seek only supporting analyst opinions. Leads to overconfidence and poor diversification. Awareness and structured analysis help mitigate.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes rational, unbiased information gathering
- Option C ignores systematic nature of the bias
