# Question

Book value per share represents:

## Options

- **A**: The market price of a company's stock
- **B**: Total shareholders' equity divided by shares outstanding
- **C**: The company's total assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Book value per share = (Total Shareholders' Equity) / Shares Outstanding. Represents accounting value of net assets per share. Often differs from market price—P/B ratio compares the two. Book value based on historical cost accounting, may not reflect current asset values or intangibles.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market price, not book value
- Option C ignores liabilities and doesn't calculate per share
