# Question

A growth stock index typically includes companies with:

## Options

- **A**: High dividend yields and low P/E ratios
- **B**: High earnings growth rates and higher valuation multiples
- **C**: Declining revenues and market share

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Growth indices: stocks with above-average earnings growth, higher P/E, P/B, and lower dividend yields. Value indices: opposite characteristics—lower multiples, higher yields. Style classification helps portfolio construction and performance attribution. Some stocks classified as blend (neither pure growth nor value).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes value stock characteristics
- Option C describes distressed or declining companies
