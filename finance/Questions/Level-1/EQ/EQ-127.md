# Question

High barriers to entry in an industry tend to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase competitive rivalry and reduce profitability
- **B**: Protect existing firms and allow higher profitability
- **C**: Have no effect on industry profitability

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Barriers to entry: factors that make it difficult for new competitors to enter. Examples: economies of scale, capital requirements, patents, brand loyalty, regulatory licenses. High barriers protect incumbents from new competition, allowing them to maintain pricing power and higher profitability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses barriers to entry with rivalry among existing firms
- Option C ignores the fundamental impact on competitive dynamics
