# Question

The main difference between ADRs and Global Depositary Receipts (GDRs) is that GDRs:

## Options

- **A**: Can only be traded in the company's home country
- **B**: Are typically listed on exchanges outside the US, often London or Luxembourg
- **C**: Do not represent actual underlying shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GDRs: similar to ADRs but typically listed on non-US exchanges (London Stock Exchange, Luxembourg). Allow companies to access international capital markets. Both ADRs and GDRs represent underlying shares held by a depositary bank. GDRs often used by emerging market companies seeking European investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—GDRs are for trading outside home country
- Option C is wrong—both ADRs and GDRs represent underlying shares
