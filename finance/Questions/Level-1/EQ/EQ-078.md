# Question

In a rights offering, existing shareholders:

## Options

- **A**: Must sell their shares back to the company
- **B**: Receive rights to purchase additional shares at a discount, proportional to their holdings
- **C**: Are required to buy more shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rights offering: existing shareholders receive rights to buy new shares at a discount to market price, proportional to current holdings. Preserves proportional ownership if exercised. Rights are transferable—can be sold if shareholder doesn't want to buy. Less dilutive to existing shareholders than public offering.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes share repurchase
- Option C is incorrect—exercise is optional
