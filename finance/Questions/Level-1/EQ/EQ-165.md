# Question

An information cascade occurs when investors:

## Options

- **A**: Conduct thorough independent analysis before each investment decision
- **B**: Follow the actions of others rather than relying on their own private information
- **C**: Sell all holdings during market downturns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information cascades: investors observe others' actions and follow them, ignoring their own information. Rational individually if you believe others have superior information. Can lead to herding and price bubbles/crashes. Once cascade begins, private information is not incorporated into prices efficiently.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is opposite—cascades involve ignoring independent analysis
- Option C describes selling behavior, not the cascade mechanism
