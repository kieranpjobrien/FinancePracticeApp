# Question

Preferred stock differs from common stock in that preferred stockholders:

## Options

- **A**: Always have voting rights
- **B**: Receive dividends before common shareholders and have priority in liquidation
- **C**: Participate in all company profits without limit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Preferred stock: fixed dividend claim with priority over common. Usually no voting rights. Paid before common dividends; priority in bankruptcy over common (but after debt). May be cumulative (missed dividends accumulate), callable, convertible. Hybrid security with debt-like features.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is typically opposite—preferred usually lacks voting rights
- Option C describes common stock's unlimited upside
