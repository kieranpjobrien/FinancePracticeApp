# Question

An equity carve-out differs from a spin-off in that:

## Options

- **A**: No new shares are created
- **B**: A subsidiary is sold to the public via IPO, raising cash for the parent
- **C**: The parent company ceases to exist

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Carve-out: parent sells minority stake (typically) in subsidiary through IPO. Raises cash for parent. Parent usually retains control. May be precursor to full spin-off or sale. Provides market valuation for subsidiary. Existing shareholders don't automatically receive subsidiary shares—must buy in IPO.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—new shares are created and sold
- Option C—parent continues operating
