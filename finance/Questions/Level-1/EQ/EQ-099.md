# Question

A quote-driven market is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Orders matched directly between buyers and sellers
- **B**: Dealers providing liquidity by continuously quoting bid and ask prices
- **C**: No intermediaries in the trading process

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Quote-driven (dealer) market: dealers quote two-way prices (bid and ask) and trade as counterparties. Provide immediacy and liquidity. Examples: NASDAQ (historically), OTC bond markets, foreign exchange. Contrast with order-driven markets where orders match directly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes order-driven markets
- Option C also describes pure order-driven markets
