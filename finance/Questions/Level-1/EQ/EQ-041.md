# Question

Convertible preferred stock gives holders the right to:

## Options

- **A**: Convert their shares into bonds
- **B**: Convert their preferred shares into common shares at a specified ratio
- **C**: Force the company to redeem shares at any time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Convertible preferred: can be exchanged for common stock at predetermined conversion ratio. Conversion typically at holder's option. Provides downside protection (preferred's priority and fixed dividend) with upside participation (conversion when common appreciates). Conversion ratio sets the exchange terms.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conversion to debt, not typical
- Option C describes puttable or redeemable features
