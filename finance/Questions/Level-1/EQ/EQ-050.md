# Question

Float-adjusted market capitalization weighting uses:

## Options

- **A**: Total shares outstanding regardless of availability
- **B**: Only shares available for public trading, excluding closely held shares
- **C**: Equal weights for all stocks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Float-adjusted weighting: uses publicly available shares, excluding shares held by insiders, governments, or strategic investors unlikely to trade. More accurately represents investable opportunity. Standard practice for major indices (S&P 500, MSCI). Prevents overweighting stocks with limited actual liquidity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes full market-cap weighting
- Option C describes equal weighting
