# Question

During employment, an analyst develops a proprietary trading algorithm. After leaving, she uses the algorithm at a new firm. Under Standard IV(A):

## Options

- **A**: Her intellectual contributions belong to her personally
- **B**: Work product developed during employment generally belongs to the employer
- **C**: Algorithms cannot be protected as employer property

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) generally treats work product created during employment as employer property. Proprietary algorithms developed on employer time and resources typically belong to the employer and cannot be taken to a new firm.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores employment relationship implications
- Option C incorrectly suggests algorithms are exempt
