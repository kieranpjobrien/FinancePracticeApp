# Question

A portfolio manager's spouse maintains an investment account at a competing firm. When the manager changes her recommendation on a stock, she should treat her spouse's account:

## Options

- **A**: The same as any other client account in terms of timing and priority
- **B**: As a personal account subject to trading restrictions and disclosure requirements
- **C**: With no special considerations since it is held at a different firm

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accounts of immediate family members are typically treated as personal accounts under conflicts of interest standards. The manager should not provide preferential treatment to her spouse's account and must comply with trading restrictions and disclosure requirements applicable to personal trading. The location of the account does not eliminate the conflict.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A fails to recognize the heightened scrutiny for family accounts
- Option C incorrectly assumes the different firm location eliminates the conflict
