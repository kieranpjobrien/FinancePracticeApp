# Question

An analyst recommends a stock based primarily on third-party research without conducting her own analysis. To comply with reasonable basis requirements, the analyst should have:

## Options

- **A**: Verified that the third-party research was prepared by a reputable firm
- **B**: Evaluated the methodology and assumptions of the third-party research for reasonableness
- **C**: Simply cited the source of the research in her recommendation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When relying on third-party research, analysts must evaluate the soundness of the research rather than accepting it at face value. This includes reviewing the methodology, checking key assumptions, and assessing whether the conclusions are reasonable. Mere citation or reliance on firm reputation is insufficient to establish a reasonable basis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relies on reputation rather than substantive evaluation
- Option C addresses attribution but not the analytical requirement to verify the research quality
