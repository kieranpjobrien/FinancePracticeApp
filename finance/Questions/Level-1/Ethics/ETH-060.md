# Question

When a portfolio manager receives more shares of an oversubscribed IPO than can be allocated to all interested clients, fair dealing requires that:

## Options

- **A**: Shares be allocated to the largest clients who generate the most revenue for the firm
- **B**: Shares be allocated on a pro-rata basis or another fair method
- **C**: Shares be allocated to clients whose accounts most recently purchased similar securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fair dealing requires that investment opportunities be allocated equitably among clients. When supply is limited, allocation methods such as pro-rata (proportional to account size or order size), rotation, or random selection ensure that no clients are systematically favored. The method should be objective, consistently applied, and disclosed.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A favors larger clients over smaller ones, violating fair dealing principles
- Option C uses an arbitrary criterion that doesn't ensure equitable distribution
