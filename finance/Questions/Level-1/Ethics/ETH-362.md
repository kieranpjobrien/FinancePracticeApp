# Question

Under GIPS, "discretionary" portfolios are those where:

## Options

- **A**: The firm charges higher fees
- **B**: The firm has authority to implement the investment strategy
- **C**: The client makes all investment decisions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS defines discretionary portfolios as those where the firm has authority to implement the intended investment strategy. Non-discretionary portfolios where clients control decisions are excluded from composites.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A relates fees to discretion incorrectly
- Option C describes non-discretionary accounts
