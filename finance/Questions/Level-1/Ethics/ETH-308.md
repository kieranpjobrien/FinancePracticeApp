# Question

A firm establishes blackout periods prohibiting personal trading around recommendation changes. This practice:

## Options

- **A**: Exceeds Standard VI(B) requirements and is unnecessary
- **B**: Is a reasonable procedure to implement the Standard's priority requirements
- **C**: Violates employee trading rights

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Blackout periods are a reasonable implementation of Standard VI(B)'s priority requirements, preventing employees from personally benefiting from advance knowledge of recommendations. Such procedures help ensure client priority.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A undervalues proactive compliance measures
- Option C mischaracterizes trading restrictions as rights violations
