# Question

An analyst uses a chart from a third-party research report in her own presentation without attribution. This violates Standard I(C) because:

## Options

- **A**: Charts cannot be used across different reports
- **B**: Using another's work without acknowledgment constitutes plagiarism
- **C**: Third-party research is inherently unreliable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(C) Misrepresentation includes plagiarism—using another's work (including charts, graphs, or data) without proper attribution. Members must acknowledge the sources of information and analysis they use.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; materials can be used with proper attribution
- Option C incorrectly dismisses third-party research quality
