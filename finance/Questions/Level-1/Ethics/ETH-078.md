# Question

An analyst uses a complex quantitative model to generate investment signals. She does not fully understand how the model works but it has produced strong backtested results. Using this model to make recommendations:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable if the backtested performance is statistically significant
- **B**: May violate diligence requirements if the analyst cannot evaluate the model's methodology
- **C**: Is required to stay competitive with other quantitative strategies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Diligence and reasonable basis requirements extend to understanding the tools used in analysis. Using a model without understanding its methodology, assumptions, and limitations makes it impossible to evaluate when the model might fail or be inappropriate. The analyst should understand her tools well enough to assess their reliability before relying on them for recommendations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overweights backtested results without considering model understanding
- Option C prioritizes competition over analytical rigor
