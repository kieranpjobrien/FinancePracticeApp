# Question

A trader engages in "painting the tape" by executing a series of transactions among related accounts to create the appearance of active trading interest in a security. This practice is prohibited because it:

## Options

- **A**: Increases transaction costs for the trader's employer
- **B**: Misleads other market participants about genuine supply and demand
- **C**: Violates exchange rules about minimum trade sizes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

"Painting the tape" is a form of transaction-based market manipulation that creates a misleading appearance of trading activity. Other market participants may be deceived into believing there is genuine interest in the security and make trading decisions based on this false signal. Such manipulation undermines market integrity by causing prices to reflect artificial rather than genuine supply and demand.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A focuses on costs rather than the deceptive nature of the practice
- Option C references specific exchange rules that are not the primary ethical concern
