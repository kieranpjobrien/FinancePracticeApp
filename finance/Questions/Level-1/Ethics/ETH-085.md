# Question

An investment advisor receives referral fees for directing clients to a particular estate planning attorney. This arrangement:

## Options

- **A**: Must be prohibited because it creates an inherent conflict of interest
- **B**: May be permitted if disclosed to clients before the referral is made
- **C**: Is only a concern if the referral fee exceeds a specified dollar amount

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Referral fee arrangements create conflicts of interest because the advisor may be motivated by the fee rather than client benefit. However, such arrangements are permissible if fully disclosed before any referral is made. Disclosure allows clients to assess whether the referral is in their interest and to seek alternatives if desired.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A takes an overly restrictive view; disclosure can adequately address the conflict
- Option C incorrectly suggests size determines disclosure requirements
