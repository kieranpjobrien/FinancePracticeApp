# Question

When facing pressure to compromise ethical standards for short-term gains, a member should:

## Options

- **A**: Comply if the compromise is small
- **B**: Maintain ethical standards recognizing that long-term reputation and trust are more valuable
- **C**: Follow market practice if others are doing it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ethical conduct builds long-term value through trust and reputation. Short-term compromises, even small ones, erode this foundation. Members should maintain standards regardless of immediate pressure, understanding that integrity is an enduring asset.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A allows incremental erosion of standards
- Option C uses others' behavior to justify ethical lapses
