# Question

When a portfolio manager votes proxies for securities held in client accounts, Standard III(A) requires:

## Options

- **A**: Always voting with management recommendations
- **B**: Voting in the best interest of clients in accordance with their investment objectives
- **C**: Abstaining from voting to avoid potential conflicts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Proxy voting is part of fiduciary duty under Standard III(A). Managers must vote proxies in clients' best interests, which requires evaluating each proposal on its merits rather than automatically following management or abstaining.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A abdicates responsibility to evaluate proposals
- Option C fails the duty to act in client interests
