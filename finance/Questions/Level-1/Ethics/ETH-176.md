# Question

For a portfolio managed to a benchmark, a speculative small-cap stock representing 2% of the portfolio is:

## Options

- **A**: Always unsuitable because speculative investments are prohibited
- **B**: Potentially suitable if it fits the overall portfolio strategy and risk parameters
- **C**: Unsuitable unless the client specifically requested it

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(C) evaluates suitability in the context of the total portfolio, not individual securities in isolation. A speculative position may be suitable as a small allocation within a diversified portfolio that overall meets client objectives and risk parameters.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A judges securities in isolation rather than portfolio context
- Option C requires specific client direction for every holding, which is impractical
