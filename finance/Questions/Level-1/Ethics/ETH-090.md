# Question

An investment banker offers a portfolio manager personal shares in an oversubscribed IPO as thanks for directing significant brokerage business. Accepting this allocation would most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Be acceptable if the manager discloses the allocation to her employer
- **B**: Violate conflict of interest standards as personal benefit from professional relationships
- **C**: Be permitted since IPO allocations are a normal part of the investment industry

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accepting valuable personal benefits as a result of professional relationships creates serious conflicts of interest. The IPO allocation could be viewed as compensation for directing business, which might influence future allocation decisions in ways that don't serve clients. Such personal benefits tied to professional activities are generally prohibited, not merely a disclosure matter.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A suggests disclosure is sufficient when the practice itself is problematic
- Option C normalizes conduct that creates improper incentives
