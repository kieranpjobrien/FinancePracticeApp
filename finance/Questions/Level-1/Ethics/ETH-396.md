# Question

During a meeting with company management, an analyst learns that quarterly earnings will significantly exceed estimates but this has not been publicly announced. The analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Trade immediately to benefit clients before the information becomes public
- **B**: Refrain from trading and not share the information until it is publicly disseminated
- **C**: Share the information only with the firm's largest clients

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard II(A) prohibits trading or causing others to trade on material nonpublic information. The earnings information is material (likely to affect stock price) and nonpublic. The analyst must wait until public dissemination before acting.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A directly violates the prohibition on trading on MNPI
- Option C constitutes tipping, which also violates the Standard
