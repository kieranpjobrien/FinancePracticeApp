# Question

A client informs her financial advisor that she intends to use funds in her account to finance what the advisor suspects may be money laundering activities. The advisor should:

## Options

- **A**: Maintain client confidentiality since there is no confirmed illegal activity
- **B**: Report the suspicious activity to appropriate authorities as required by anti-money laundering laws
- **C**: Quietly close the client's account to avoid involvement without reporting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

While client confidentiality is important, it does not protect illegal activities. Anti-money laundering (AML) laws typically require financial professionals to report suspicious activities to appropriate authorities. Legal requirements to report suspicious activity override the general duty of confidentiality. Failing to report when required could make the advisor complicit in the illegal activity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly prioritizes confidentiality over legal reporting requirements
- Option C attempts to avoid the issue without fulfilling legal reporting obligations, which itself may violate regulations
