# Question

An analyst's report projects 15% annual returns for a speculative growth stock but does not mention the significant risks that could lead to substantial losses. This report most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Complies with standards if the return projection is based on sound analysis
- **B**: Violates communication standards by failing to disclose material risks
- **C**: Is acceptable for sophisticated investors who understand growth stock risks

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Investment communications must present a balanced view that includes both potential returns and material risks. Projecting returns without disclosing significant risks creates a misleading impression and prevents clients from making informed decisions. All material factors that could affect the investment outcome should be communicated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A focuses only on return analysis while ignoring the communication requirement
- Option C incorrectly assumes investor sophistication eliminates disclosure obligations
