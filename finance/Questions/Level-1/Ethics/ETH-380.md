# Question

When communicating investment analysis, members must:

## Options

- **A**: Present all opinions as certain facts to instill confidence
- **B**: Distinguish between facts and opinions in their communications
- **C**: Avoid expressing opinions entirely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard V(B) requires clear distinction between facts and opinions. Presenting opinions as facts misleads clients, while avoiding opinions entirely would fail to provide valuable analytical judgment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A conflates opinions with facts
- Option C unnecessarily limits the analyst's value-added perspective
