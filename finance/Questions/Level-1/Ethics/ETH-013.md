# Question

Ethics is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: A set of rules established by regulatory authorities that must be followed to avoid legal penalties
- **B**: A set of moral principles or rules of conduct that provide guidance for behavior
- **C**: A formal code adopted by professional organizations that applies only to their members

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ethics refers to a set of moral principles or rules of conduct that provide guidance for our behavior. Ethics encompasses beliefs about what is good, acceptable, or responsible behavior, and what is bad, unacceptable, or irresponsible behavior. Ethics goes beyond mere legal compliance—it involves making decisions based on what is right, not just what is legally required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses ethics with legal regulations; while laws and ethics may overlap, ethics is broader and not limited to avoiding legal penalties
- Option C is too narrow—ethics applies to all individuals, not just members of professional organizations, and extends beyond formal codes
