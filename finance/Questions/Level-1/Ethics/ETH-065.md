# Question

An analyst receives an offer from a client to provide additional consulting services for a separate fee, outside of her regular employment. Before accepting, the analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Decline since any outside compensation creates a conflict of interest
- **B**: Obtain written consent from her employer before accepting the arrangement
- **C**: Accept as long as the work is performed outside of regular business hours

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standards regarding additional compensation require that employees obtain written consent from their employer before accepting compensation or benefits from third parties that could create a conflict with the employer's interests. This allows the employer to evaluate potential conflicts and make an informed decision about whether to permit the arrangement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; outside arrangements may be permissible with proper consent
- Option C focuses on timing rather than the need for employer approval; when the work is done is less important than obtaining consent
