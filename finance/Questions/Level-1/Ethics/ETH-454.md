# Question

In determining suitability, risk capacity differs from risk tolerance in that:

## Options

- **A**: They are the same concept
- **B**: Risk capacity is the ability to bear losses; risk tolerance is willingness to accept volatility
- **C**: Risk capacity only applies to institutional investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(C) recognizes that suitability requires understanding both concepts. Risk capacity relates to financial ability to sustain losses without compromising goals. Risk tolerance reflects psychological comfort with uncertainty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A conflates two distinct concepts
- Option C incorrectly limits risk capacity to certain investor types
