# Question

The Code of Ethics' requirement to promote the integrity and viability of global capital markets primarily benefits:

## Options

- **A**: Only institutional investors
- **B**: All market participants and society broadly
- **C**: Only CFA charterholders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Promoting capital market integrity benefits all participants—retail investors, institutional investors, issuers, and society at large—by fostering efficient allocation of capital and economic growth. This is a broad public interest goal, not limited to any single group.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores retail investors and broader societal benefits
- Option C incorrectly limits benefits to CFA charterholders
