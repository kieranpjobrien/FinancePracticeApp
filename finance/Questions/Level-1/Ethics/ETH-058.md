# Question

When presenting investment performance to clients or prospects, professionals must ensure that:

## Options

- **A**: Only the best-performing time periods are highlighted to attract new clients
- **B**: Performance is presented in a fair, accurate, and complete manner
- **C**: Benchmark comparisons are avoided since they may confuse unsophisticated investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Performance presentations must be fair, accurate, and complete to give clients and prospects an honest picture of results. This means including all relevant time periods, using appropriate benchmarks, and not cherry-picking data to create a misleading impression of success. Complete information enables informed decision-making.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes selective presentation that misleads clients, violating standards
- Option C is backwards—benchmark comparisons provide valuable context for evaluating performance
