# Question

Under GIPS, a firm combines two strategies with different investment objectives into one composite because both have performed well. This practice:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable if both strategies outperformed their benchmarks
- **B**: Violates GIPS because composites must include only portfolios with similar investment mandates
- **C**: Is permitted if disclosed to prospective clients

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS requires composites to include portfolios managed according to similar investment mandates, objectives, or strategies. Combining dissimilar strategies based on performance creates misleading composite results and violates the fundamental composite construction rules.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses performance rather than strategy as the grouping criterion
- Option C suggests disclosure cures improper composite construction
