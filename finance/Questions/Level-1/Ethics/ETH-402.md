# Question

A member suspects a client is engaged in money laundering based on transaction patterns. Under Standard III(E), the member should:

## Options

- **A**: Maintain confidentiality because there is no legal requirement to report suspicions
- **B**: Report to appropriate authorities as required by anti-money laundering laws
- **C**: Confront the client directly before taking any action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(E) allows disclosure when required by law. Anti-money laundering regulations typically require reporting suspicious activities. Legal requirements override the confidentiality obligation, and members should follow proper reporting procedures.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores legal reporting requirements
- Option C could compromise an investigation and may violate law
