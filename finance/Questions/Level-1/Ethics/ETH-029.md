# Question

A portfolio manager's code of ethics requires placing client interests first. The manager is offered an all-expenses-paid trip to an exclusive resort by a broker-dealer, ostensibly for an "investment conference" that consists of two hours of presentations and three days of leisure activities. According to ethical principles, the manager should most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Accept the trip because educational conferences are a legitimate business expense
- **B**: Decline the trip because it could compromise objectivity in future broker selection
- **C**: Accept the trip but disclose it to compliance after returning

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This gift creates a potential conflict of interest that could compromise the manager's objectivity when selecting broker-dealers for client transactions. The disproportionate entertainment value relative to educational content suggests the trip is designed to influence behavior rather than provide genuine professional development. Ethical conduct requires avoiding situations that could impair independent judgment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A mischaracterizes the trip; the minimal educational content does not justify the lavish entertainment
- Option C is insufficient; disclosure after the fact does not address the underlying conflict of interest that accepting creates
