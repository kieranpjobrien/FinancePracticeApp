# Question

A firm's algorithm is designed to detect and exploit other algorithms' trading patterns in ways that cause them to trade at disadvantageous prices. Under Standard II(B):

## Options

- **A**: Algorithmic trading is exempt from manipulation rules
- **B**: This may constitute manipulation if designed to distort prices or disadvantage others
- **C**: Only human traders can commit manipulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard II(B) applies regardless of whether manipulation is conducted manually or algorithmically. Trading strategies designed to exploit others by distorting prices can constitute manipulation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly exempts technology-based trading
- Option C ignores that algorithmic actions are still attributable to humans
