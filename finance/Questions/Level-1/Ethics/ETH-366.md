# Question

When the law is unclear on a particular issue, a member should:

## Options

- **A**: Assume the most permissive interpretation to maximize client returns
- **B**: Seek guidance and err on the side of caution until the matter is clarified
- **C**: Ignore the unclear law until explicitly required to comply

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When facing legal ambiguity under Standard I(A), members should seek appropriate guidance and generally adopt a conservative approach. Taking the most permissive interpretation or ignoring unclear requirements risks violations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A risks violating the law through aggressive interpretation
- Option C ignores the member's duty to comply even with unclear requirements
