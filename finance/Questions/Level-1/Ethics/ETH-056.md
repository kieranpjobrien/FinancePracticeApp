# Question

A client instructs her portfolio manager to invest 80% of her retirement portfolio in a single speculative biotechnology stock. The manager believes this concentration is unsuitable given the client's financial situation and goals. The manager should most appropriately:

## Options

- **A**: Follow the client's instruction since the client has ultimate authority over her own assets
- **B**: Explain the risks and document the client's decision if she still wishes to proceed
- **C**: Refuse to execute the trade and terminate the advisory relationship

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When a client requests an action the manager believes is unsuitable, the manager should educate the client about the risks and document any discussion. If the client, after being fully informed, still wishes to proceed, the manager may execute the trade but should document that the client was advised of the risks and made an informed decision. This approach respects client autonomy while fulfilling the duty to provide suitable advice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A fails to fulfill the manager's duty to provide guidance and ensure informed decision-making
- Option C may be appropriate in extreme cases but is generally too drastic when education and documentation can address the concern
