# Question

An employee discovers that her employer is systematically overcharging clients through hidden fees that violate securities regulations. After internal reporting produces no response, the employee's duty of loyalty to the employer:

## Options

- **A**: Requires her to remain silent to protect the employer's reputation
- **B**: Does not extend to covering up illegal conduct and external reporting may be appropriate
- **C**: Obligates her to resign quietly without disclosing the violations to anyone

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The duty of loyalty to employers has limits—it does not require employees to participate in or conceal illegal activities. When internal channels fail to address regulatory violations, external reporting to appropriate authorities may be necessary and appropriate. Loyalty to an employer cannot override legal and ethical obligations to clients and the market.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly extends loyalty to covering up illegal conduct
- Option C fails to address the ongoing harm to clients and potential legal obligations to report
