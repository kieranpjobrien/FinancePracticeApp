# Question

An employee of a mutual fund company purchases shares in the same fund they manage. Standard VI(B):

## Options

- **A**: Prohibits employees from investing in funds they manage
- **B**: Generally permits this as employees invest alongside clients, not ahead of them
- **C**: Requires trading only at month-end

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(B) addresses front-running and priority issues. Mutual fund purchases at NAV mean employees invest at the same price as clients, eliminating the priority concern. This differs from securities where trade timing creates advantages.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prohibits an alignment of interests
- Option C imposes arbitrary timing restrictions
