# Question

An analyst discovers that her firm is systematically front-running client orders. After reporting to compliance without result, she should:

## Options

- **A**: Continue working but document her concerns
- **B**: Dissociate from the activity and consider whether to remain with the firm
- **C**: Immediately contact regulatory authorities without notifying the firm

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When internal reporting fails to address illegal activity, Standard I(A) requires members to dissociate from the violation. This may include refusing to participate in the activity and ultimately leaving the firm if the violations continue. While regulatory reporting may be appropriate, dissociation is the required first step.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A allows continued association with known violations
- Option C may be appropriate eventually but skips the dissociation requirement
