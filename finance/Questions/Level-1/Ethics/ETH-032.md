# Question

An investment advisor recently began receiving soft dollar credits from a brokerage firm in exchange for directing client trades to that firm. The advisor uses the credits to pay for research services that benefit all clients. Which action is most consistent with the advisor's ethical obligations?

## Options

- **A**: Continue the arrangement without disclosure since all clients benefit from the research
- **B**: Disclose the arrangement to clients and obtain their consent where required
- **C**: Discontinue the arrangement because soft dollar arrangements are inherently unethical

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Soft dollar arrangements create potential conflicts of interest because the advisor may be motivated to direct trades based on credits received rather than best execution for clients. Ethical obligations require disclosure of such conflicts so clients can evaluate whether the arrangement serves their interests. Obtaining consent where required ensures clients understand and accept the practice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A fails to recognize the conflict of interest that requires disclosure, regardless of whether clients receive some benefit
- Option C overstates the ethical issue; soft dollar arrangements are permitted but require transparency and must benefit clients
