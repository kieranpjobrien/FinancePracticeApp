# Question

The mosaic theory in investment research allows analysts to:

## Options

- **A**: Trade on material nonpublic information if obtained through diligent research
- **B**: Combine nonmaterial, nonpublic information with public information to reach investment conclusions
- **C**: Share material nonpublic information with clients as long as the source is disclosed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The mosaic theory holds that an analyst may combine multiple pieces of individually nonmaterial, nonpublic information with public information to form an investment conclusion that may constitute material insight. The key distinction is that each individual piece of nonpublic information must be nonmaterial on its own. This legitimate research process is distinguished from trading on material nonpublic information.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect because material nonpublic information cannot be traded on regardless of how it was obtained
- Option C is incorrect because disclosure does not cure the prohibition on communicating material nonpublic information
