# Question

A manager routes trades to a broker offering higher commissions but superior execution. This practice:

## Options

- **A**: Violates the duty to minimize costs
- **B**: May be appropriate if overall transaction quality benefits clients
- **C**: Is prohibited regardless of execution quality

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(A) focuses on client benefit, not simply minimizing costs. If superior execution quality more than offsets higher commissions, the total transaction cost to clients may be lower. Best execution considers the complete picture.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A oversimplifies the duty as cost minimization only
- Option C ignores the broader concept of best execution
