# Question

A CFA charterholder's business card reads "John Smith, CFA, Expert Investment Manager." This:

## Options

- **A**: Violates Standard VII(B) because the CFA designation cannot appear on business cards
- **B**: May violate Standard VII(B) if it implies the CFA designation certifies expertise beyond what it represents
- **C**: Is proper use since the individual has earned the designation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VII(B) requires accurate representation of the CFA designation. Combining "CFA" with "Expert" could imply the CFA Program certifies expertise in a way that overstates or misrepresents what the designation signifies.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; the CFA designation may appear on business cards
- Option C ignores potential misrepresentation through adjacent language
