# Question

A firm's advertisement claims "100% of our advisors are CFA charterholders" when only 80% actually hold the designation. This:

## Options

- **A**: Is acceptable marketing enthusiasm
- **B**: Violates Standard VII(B) and potentially Standard I(C) Misrepresentation
- **C**: Is only wrong if clients rely on the claim

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VII(B) prohibits misleading claims about CFA designation. Overstating the percentage of charterholders is both a misuse of the designation and a material misrepresentation under Standard I(C).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A accepts false advertising
- Option C makes violations contingent on client reliance
