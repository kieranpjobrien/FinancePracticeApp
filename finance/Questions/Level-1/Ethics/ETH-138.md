# Question

A client insists on buying a speculative stock that the portfolio manager believes is unsuitable for the client's conservative objectives. The manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to execute the trade under any circumstances
- **B**: Document the unsolicited nature of the request and client's insistence, then consider executing
- **C**: Execute without comment to maintain client relationship

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When clients request unsuitable investments, the manager should explain the concerns and document the discussion. If the client still insists and understands the risks, an unsolicited trade may be executed with proper documentation. The manager cannot force the client to accept advice.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be appropriate in extreme cases but is too rigid for most situations
- Option C fails the duty to advise clients on suitability
