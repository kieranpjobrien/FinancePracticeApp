# Question

Which of the following characteristics most distinguishes a profession from other occupations?

## Options

- **A**: Higher average compensation and greater job security
- **B**: Specialized knowledge combined with a commitment to service beyond self-interest
- **C**: Government licensing requirements and mandatory continuing education

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Professions are distinguished by specialized expert knowledge and skills, combined with a commitment to serve clients and society with integrity, placing client interests ahead of self-interest. This service orientation and ethical commitment, not compensation or licensing alone, establishes the trust relationship that defines a profession. Investment management qualifies as a profession because practitioners possess specialized knowledge and are expected to act in clients' best interests.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A focuses on economic outcomes rather than defining characteristics; many non-professions also offer high pay
- Option C describes regulatory features that may apply to professions but are not the essential distinguishing characteristic; the commitment to ethical service is more fundamental
