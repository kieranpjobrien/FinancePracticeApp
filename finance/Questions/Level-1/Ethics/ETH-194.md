# Question

Information becomes "public" for purposes of Standard II(A) when:

## Options

- **A**: Any single person outside the company knows it
- **B**: It has been disseminated broadly enough to affect the security's price
- **C**: It appears in any media outlet

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information becomes public when it has been disseminated to the marketplace in a manner sufficient to be reflected in the security's price. Limited disclosure to a few individuals or even a single media mention may be insufficient if investors generally cannot access it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets too low a threshold for public status
- Option C doesn't address whether the dissemination was broad enough
