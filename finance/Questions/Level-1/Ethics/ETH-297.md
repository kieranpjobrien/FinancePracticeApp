# Question

When investment opportunities are oversubscribed and must be rationed among clients, Standard III(B) requires:

## Options

- **A**: Allocation to the first clients who expressed interest
- **B**: Fair allocation using documented, equitable methods
- **C**: Priority to accounts paying the highest fees

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(B) requires fair dealing through documented, equitable allocation methods. While first-come or pro-rata methods may be acceptable if fairly applied, favoring high-fee accounts or arbitrary allocation violates fair dealing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may not be equitable if first responders are systematically advantaged
- Option C creates unfair bias toward profitable accounts
