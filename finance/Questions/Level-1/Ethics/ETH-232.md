# Question

A research analyst's firm makes a market in securities the analyst covers. Under Standard VI(A):

## Options

- **A**: The analyst must stop covering securities where the firm is a market maker
- **B**: The market-making relationship should be disclosed in research reports
- **C**: Market-making creates no conflict requiring disclosure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market-making creates potential conflicts of interest that must be disclosed under Standard VI(A). The firm has economic interests in trading activity that could influence research objectivity. Disclosure allows readers to evaluate this potential bias.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too restrictive; disclosure manages the conflict
- Option C ignores the inherent conflict in market-making relationships
