# Question

An analyst learns from a company CFO during a routine meeting that the company will cut its dividend next quarter. This information:

## Options

- **A**: Is non-material since dividends are discretionary
- **B**: Is likely material nonpublic information that cannot be traded upon
- **C**: Can be used since it came from a legitimate business meeting

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dividend changes are typically material to investors. Information about future dividend cuts received before public announcement is material nonpublic information under Standard II(A), regardless of how it was obtained.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A underestimates dividend materiality to investors
- Option C confuses legitimate meeting with permission to trade
