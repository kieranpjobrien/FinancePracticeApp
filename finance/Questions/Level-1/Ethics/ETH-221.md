# Question

"Tipping" in the context of Standard II(A) refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Providing gratuities to service providers
- **B**: Communicating material nonpublic information to others who may trade on it
- **C**: Giving investment advice to friends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Tipping means passing material nonpublic information to someone who may use it for trading. This violates Standard II(A) regardless of whether the tipper personally trades, because it enables others to trade on unfair information.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses the colloquial meaning of "tip"
- Option C describes advice-giving, not improper information sharing
