# Question

A supervisor notices unusual trading patterns in a subordinate's personal account but takes no action. Later, the subordinate is found to have engaged in insider trading. Under Standard IV(C):

## Options

- **A**: The supervisor is not liable because they did not personally trade
- **B**: The supervisor may have violated supervisory duties by failing to investigate red flags
- **C**: Supervisors are only responsible for firm accounts, not personal accounts

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(C) requires supervisors to make reasonable efforts to detect violations. Ignoring red flags such as unusual personal trading patterns constitutes a failure of supervisory responsibility, even though the supervisor did not participate in the violation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses personal liability with supervisory duty
- Option C incorrectly limits the scope of supervisory responsibility
