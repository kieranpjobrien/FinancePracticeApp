# Question

A supervisor fails to establish any monitoring procedures and an employee commits fraud that could have been detected. Under Standard IV(C):

## Options

- **A**: The supervisor is not responsible because she did not commit the fraud
- **B**: The supervisor may have violated the Standard by failing to implement reasonable procedures
- **C**: Only the employee who committed fraud is liable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(C) requires supervisors to make reasonable efforts to detect violations. Failing to establish any monitoring procedures demonstrates inadequate supervision, which is itself a violation regardless of who committed the underlying fraud.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the supervisor's independent duty to implement controls
- Option C overlooks that supervisory failure is a separate violation
