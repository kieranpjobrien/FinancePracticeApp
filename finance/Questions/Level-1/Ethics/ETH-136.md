# Question

A firm receives a limited allocation of a "hot" IPO. According to Standard III(B), the shares should be:

## Options

- **A**: Allocated to accounts that have expressed prior interest on a pro-rata or other equitable basis
- **B**: Given entirely to the firm's most profitable clients
- **C**: Distributed based on portfolio manager discretion without documented criteria

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Hot IPOs with limited availability must be allocated fairly among clients who have expressed interest. A pro-rata allocation based on account size or other documented, equitable methodology complies with Standard III(B). Favoritism toward profitable clients or undocumented allocations violate fair dealing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B creates unfair advantage for certain clients
- Option C lacks transparency and invites abuse
