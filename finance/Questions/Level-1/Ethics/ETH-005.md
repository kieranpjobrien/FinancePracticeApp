# Question

An equity analyst receives an invitation from a company she covers to attend an all-expenses-paid conference at a luxury resort. The conference includes educational content about the company's industry. According to Standard I(B) Independence and Objectivity, the analyst should most appropriately:

## Options

- **A**: Accept the invitation since the conference has legitimate educational content
- **B**: Decline the invitation because it could reasonably be expected to compromise her objectivity
- **C**: Accept only if she discloses the gift to her employer and in her research reports

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(B) requires members to use reasonable care to maintain independence and objectivity. Gifts that could reasonably be expected to influence the member's objectivity must be declined. An all-expenses-paid trip to a luxury resort from a covered company creates a clear appearance of a potential conflict, regardless of the educational content included.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly suggests that educational content justifies accepting lavish gifts from covered companies
- Option C misunderstands the standard—disclosure does not cure the problem when the gift could compromise objectivity; such gifts should be declined outright
