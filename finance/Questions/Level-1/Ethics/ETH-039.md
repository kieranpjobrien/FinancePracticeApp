# Question

An analyst discovers that her supervisor is directing client trades to a broker-dealer in exchange for personal benefits, which violates securities regulations. After raising concerns internally without success, the analyst's most appropriate next step is to:

## Options

- **A**: Continue following supervisor instructions to avoid career repercussions
- **B**: Dissociate from the activity and consider whether to report externally
- **C**: Immediately resign and file a complaint with regulators

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When internal reporting fails to address illegal activity, professionals must dissociate from the conduct—meaning they should not participate in or facilitate the violation. The member should consider whether external reporting to regulators is appropriate or required. Dissociation may involve escalating to higher levels internally, refusing to participate, or ultimately leaving the firm.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would make the analyst complicit in the violation
- Option C may be premature; dissociation and documentation should precede resignation, and external reporting decisions require careful consideration
