# Question

An analyst planning to leave her firm begins downloading client lists and proprietary research to take to her new employer. This violates Standard IV(A) because:

## Options

- **A**: Employees can never take any information when leaving
- **B**: Client lists and proprietary research are employer property
- **C**: The analyst should have resigned before taking any action

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) prohibits misappropriating employer property, including client lists and proprietary research developed during employment. While employees may take general knowledge and skills, specific client information and proprietary work product belong to the employer.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too broad; employees can take general knowledge and skills
- Option C focuses on timing rather than the substantive violation
