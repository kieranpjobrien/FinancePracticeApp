# Question

Work product created during employment generally belongs to:

## Options

- **A**: The employee who created it
- **B**: The employer, as specified in Standard IV(A)
- **C**: Both employer and employee equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(A) establishes that work product created during employment belongs to the employer. This includes research, models, and client lists. Members may not take proprietary work when departing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores employer ownership of work product
- Option C creates an unworkable shared ownership concept
