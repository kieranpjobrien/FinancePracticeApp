# Question

A supervisor discovers that the firm's compliance procedures are inadequate to detect potential violations. The supervisor should:

## Options

- **A**: Continue using existing procedures since they were approved by management
- **B**: Work to implement improved procedures and decline supervisory responsibility if improvements are not made
- **C**: Ignore the deficiency unless an actual violation occurs

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(C) requires supervisors to make reasonable efforts to ensure adequate compliance procedures. When procedures are inadequate, supervisors must work to improve them. If the firm refuses improvements, declining supervisory duties may be necessary.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A accepts inadequate procedures passively
- Option C waits for violations rather than preventing them
