# Question

An advisor receives a court subpoena for client records. Under Standard III(E):

## Options

- **A**: The advisor must refuse disclosure to protect confidentiality
- **B**: Legal process requiring disclosure is an exception to confidentiality
- **C**: The advisor should warn the client before any response

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard III(E) permits disclosure when required by law. A valid court subpoena creates a legal obligation to produce records. While the advisor might inform the client as appropriate, legal requirements override confidentiality.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A could result in contempt of court
- Option C is often appropriate but is not always required before compliance
