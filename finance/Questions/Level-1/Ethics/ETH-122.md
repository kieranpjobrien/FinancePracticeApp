# Question

A client offers an analyst an expensive vacation package after the analyst recommended a successful investment. According to Standard I(B) Independence and Objectivity, the analyst should:

## Options

- **A**: Accept the gift since it was given after the recommendation
- **B**: Decline or disclose the gift and obtain employer approval before accepting
- **C**: Accept but not disclose to avoid embarrassing the client

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(B) requires members to decline gifts that could reasonably be expected to compromise their independence and objectivity. If gifts are modest enough to accept, they must be disclosed to the employer. Even post-recommendation gifts can create expectations for future favorable treatment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores how gifts create future obligations
- Option C violates disclosure requirements
