# Question

A firm's investment banking division is advising on a merger transaction. The firm also has an equity research department that covers the target company. To maintain compliance with standards on material nonpublic information, the firm should most appropriately:

## Options

- **A**: Require the research analyst to discontinue coverage until the deal is announced
- **B**: Establish information barriers ("firewalls") between the investment banking and research divisions
- **C**: Have the research analyst issue a "sell" recommendation to avoid any appearance of impropriety

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Information barriers (firewalls) are the primary control mechanism for preventing the flow of material nonpublic information between different areas of a firm. These barriers allow different divisions to continue their normal activities while preventing conflicts and improper information sharing. Properly implemented firewalls enable the research department to continue coverage independently.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be unnecessarily restrictive if proper information barriers are in place
- Option C would itself be a violation—changing recommendations based on MNPI rather than fundamental analysis
