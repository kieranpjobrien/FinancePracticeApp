# Question

An investment manager based in Country A manages accounts for clients in Country B. Country A prohibits accepting gifts over $100 from clients, while Country B has no such restriction. The manager receives a $500 gift from a Country B client. According to professional standards regarding knowledge of the law, the manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Accept the gift since the client is in Country B where it is permitted
- **B**: Decline the gift or return it to comply with Country A's stricter rules
- **C**: Accept the gift but not report it to avoid unnecessary complications

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When operating across multiple jurisdictions, members must identify and comply with all applicable laws and regulations. When conflicts exist, the stricter law or regulation should be followed. Since Country A (where the manager is based) has the stricter rule, the manager should comply with that restriction regardless of the client's location.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly applies only the client's jurisdiction, ignoring the manager's home jurisdiction requirements
- Option C compounds the violation by adding a failure to disclose
