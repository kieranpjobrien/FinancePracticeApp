# Question

When an analyst changes a recommendation from "buy" to "sell," she should:

## Options

- **A**: Wait until her original clients have had time to sell before publishing the change
- **B**: Communicate the change and the reasons for it to all affected clients promptly
- **C**: Only issue the new recommendation without explaining what changed

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Recommendation changes should be communicated promptly and fairly to all clients, with an explanation of the factors that led to the change. This transparency helps clients understand the analyst's reasoning and make informed decisions. There is no justification for delaying communication or omitting the rationale.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A creates unfair tiered access to information
- Option C fails to provide the transparency needed for informed decision-making
