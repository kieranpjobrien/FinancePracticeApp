# Question

An analyst is asked by their supervisor to change a research report conclusion from "sell" to "hold" because the company is a major investment banking client of the firm. This situation primarily represents a challenge to ethical behavior arising from:

## Options

- **A**: The analyst's lack of technical competence in valuation methodology
- **B**: Conflicts between the analyst's duty to provide objective research and pressure from firm relationships
- **C**: Ambiguity in the applicable laws regarding research report disclosures

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This scenario illustrates a classic conflict of interest challenge: pressure to compromise objective analysis due to firm business relationships. The analyst faces competing loyalties—professional duty to provide independent, objective research versus pressure from within the firm to protect a lucrative client relationship. Recognizing such conflicts is essential because they can subtly influence judgment even when the professional believes they are being objective.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misidentifies the issue; the problem is not competence but external pressure to change a competently reached conclusion
- Option C deflects to legal ambiguity when the ethical issue is clear—the analyst is being asked to compromise objectivity regardless of disclosure rules
