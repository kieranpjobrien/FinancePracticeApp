# Question

When a professional body initiates an inquiry into a member's conduct, the member should:

## Options

- **A**: Refuse to respond to protect against self-incrimination
- **B**: Cooperate with the investigation and provide requested information
- **C**: Only respond through legal counsel to avoid making admissions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Members generally have an obligation to cooperate with professional bodies investigating potential violations. This includes responding to inquiries and providing relevant information. Failure to cooperate can itself be a violation of professional standards and may result in additional sanctions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A conflates professional inquiries with criminal proceedings; different rules apply
- Option C may be appropriate in some circumstances but does not eliminate the duty to cooperate
