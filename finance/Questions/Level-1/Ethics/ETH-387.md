# Question

Under GIPS, a client restricts their portfolio to exclude tobacco stocks, significantly affecting performance relative to the benchmark. This portfolio:

## Options

- **A**: Must be included in the composite because it follows the general strategy
- **B**: May be excluded from the composite if the restriction materially affects implementation
- **C**: Should be reported in a separate composite for restricted accounts only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

GIPS allows firms to exclude portfolios from composites when client-imposed restrictions materially affect implementation of the investment strategy. The exclusion must be documented and the reason must be related to strategy implementation, not performance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that material restrictions can warrant exclusion
- Option C creates an unnecessary separate composite rather than exclusion
