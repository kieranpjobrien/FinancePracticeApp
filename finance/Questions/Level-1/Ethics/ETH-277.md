# Question

A manager discovers that her subordinate violated securities law last month. The manager had no prior knowledge. Under Standard IV(C):

## Options

- **A**: The manager violated the Standard by failing to prevent the violation
- **B**: The manager should promptly address the violation and review supervisory procedures
- **C**: Past violations are not the manager's concern

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard IV(C) requires reasonable efforts to prevent and detect violations. A supervisor who discovers a past violation should address it appropriately and review whether supervisory procedures need improvement, but is not automatically liable for unknown past violations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A holds supervisors responsible for undetectable violations
- Option C ignores the duty to respond and improve procedures
