# Question

An investment manager is convicted of tax fraud related to his personal finances, unconnected to his professional activities. Under Standard I(D) Misconduct:

## Options

- **A**: This is not relevant since it is a personal matter
- **B**: This reflects adversely on professional reputation and integrity
- **C**: Only securities-related crimes are covered by Standard I(D)

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard I(D) covers conduct that reflects adversely on a member's professional reputation, integrity, or competence—including acts outside of professional activities. Dishonesty in personal matters, such as tax fraud, raises questions about overall integrity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly separates personal conduct from professional standards
- Option C too narrowly limits the scope of misconduct
