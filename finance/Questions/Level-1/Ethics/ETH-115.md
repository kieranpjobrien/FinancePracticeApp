# Question

The CFA Institute Code of Ethics requires members and candidates to place the integrity of which of the following above their own interests?

## Options

- **A**: Only client interests
- **B**: The investment profession and the interests of clients
- **C**: Employer interests and regulatory bodies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Code of Ethics requires members to place the integrity of the investment profession and the interests of clients above their own personal interests. This dual focus protects both individual clients and the broader financial system.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incomplete; the profession's integrity is also paramount
- Option C incorrectly prioritizes employer interests, which should not supersede client interests
