# Question

A portfolio manager learns from a friend who works at a pharmaceutical company that the FDA will approve the company's new drug tomorrow. This information has not been publicly announced. According to Standard II(A) Material Nonpublic Information, the manager should:

## Options

- **A**: Trade on the information quickly before it becomes public to benefit clients
- **B**: Not trade or cause others to trade on this information until it is publicly disseminated
- **C**: Share the information with the firm's research department for verification before trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard II(A) prohibits members from trading or causing others to trade on material nonpublic information. FDA approval of a new drug is clearly material information that would affect the company's stock price. Until this information is publicly disseminated, the manager cannot trade on it or share it with others who might trade on it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A directly violates the prohibition on insider trading, regardless of the intent to benefit clients
- Option C would cause others to potentially trade on material nonpublic information, which is also prohibited
