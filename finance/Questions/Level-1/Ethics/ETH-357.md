# Question

Determining which conflicts require disclosure under Standard VI(A):

## Options

- **A**: Depends on a formal list of disclosed conflicts
- **B**: Requires judgment about what could reasonably be expected to impair objectivity
- **C**: Is limited to conflicts involving direct financial interests

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard VI(A) requires disclosure of matters that could "reasonably be expected" to impair objectivity. This requires judgment rather than mechanical checklist compliance and extends beyond direct financial interests.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A substitutes checklists for judgment
- Option C too narrowly defines relevant conflicts
