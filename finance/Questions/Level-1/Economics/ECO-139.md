# Question

Deglobalization refers to trends where:

## Options

- **A**: Global trade and investment integration continue to accelerate
- **B**: International economic integration slows or reverses due to policy and geopolitical factors
- **C**: All countries adopt identical economic policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deglobalization describes the slowdown or reversal of economic integration that characterized the late 20th century. Drivers include rising protectionism, geopolitical tensions, supply chain reshoring, and skepticism about globalization's benefits. Indicators include slowing trade-to-GDP ratios, trade barriers, and more regionalized supply chains. This trend has implications for inflation, growth, and investment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes continued globalization
- Option C describes policy convergence, not deglobalization
