# Question

According to relative purchasing power parity (PPP), if Country A has 5% inflation and Country B has 2% inflation, Country A's currency should:

## Options

- **A**: Appreciate by approximately 3% against Country B's currency
- **B**: Depreciate by approximately 3% against Country B's currency
- **C**: Remain unchanged relative to Country B's currency

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Relative PPP states that exchange rate changes should offset inflation differentials to maintain constant purchasing power. If Country A has higher inflation (5% vs 2%), its currency should depreciate by the inflation differential (approximately 3%) to maintain PPP. This keeps the real exchange rate stable and preserves competitive positions in trade.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the direction—higher inflation leads to depreciation
- Option C ignores the PPP relationship between inflation and exchange rates
