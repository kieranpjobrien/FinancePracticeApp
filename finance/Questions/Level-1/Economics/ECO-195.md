# Question

Aggregate demand is the sum of:

## Options

- **A**: Consumption, investment, government spending, and net exports
- **B**: Wages, profits, rents, and interest
- **C**: Consumer goods, capital goods, and intermediate goods

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Aggregate demand (AD) represents total spending on goods and services: AD = C + I + G + (X - M), where C = consumption, I = investment, G = government spending, and (X - M) = net exports. This is the expenditure approach to GDP. Changes in any component shift the AD curve.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes the income approach to GDP (factor payments)
- Option C mixes production categories, not demand components
