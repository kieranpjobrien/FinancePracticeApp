# Question

Secondary sanctions are sanctions that:

## Options

- **A**: Only apply to a country's exports
- **B**: Penalize third parties that do business with the primary sanctions target
- **C**: Are less severe than primary sanctions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Secondary sanctions extend the reach of primary sanctions by threatening penalties on third-country entities that conduct business with the sanctioned target. For example, a non-US bank might face exclusion from the US financial system if it transacts with a sanctioned country. This extraterritorial application is controversial but amplifies sanctions effectiveness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a narrow type of primary sanction
- Option C misunderstands the relative impact—secondary sanctions can be very powerful
