# Question

During a recession, which of the following typically occurs?

## Options

- **A**: Rising employment and increasing consumer spending
- **B**: Declining GDP, rising unemployment, and falling consumer confidence
- **C**: Accelerating inflation and asset price bubbles

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Recessions are characterized by declining economic activity across multiple indicators: falling real GDP, rising unemployment, reduced consumer and business spending, declining industrial production, and falling consumer confidence. Recessions are technically defined as two consecutive quarters of GDP decline, though official dating considers broader factors including employment, income, and sales.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an expansion phase
- Option C describes an overheating economy, typically before a recession
