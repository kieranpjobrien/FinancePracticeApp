# Question

Protectionism refers to government policies that:

## Options

- **A**: Encourage free trade by removing all barriers
- **B**: Shield domestic industries from foreign competition through trade barriers
- **C**: Protect consumers from high prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Protectionism involves government policies designed to shield domestic producers from foreign competition. Common tools include tariffs, quotas, subsidies to domestic firms, and regulatory barriers. While protectionism may preserve domestic jobs in the short run, economists generally argue it reduces overall welfare by raising prices, reducing choice, and inviting retaliation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes free trade policy
- Option C misinterprets "protection"—protectionism typically raises consumer prices
