# Question

The fiscal multiplier effect suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: An increase in government spending leads to a smaller increase in GDP
- **B**: An increase in government spending can lead to a larger increase in GDP
- **C**: Government spending has no impact on GDP

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The fiscal multiplier captures how initial government spending creates additional income that is then spent, creating further income and spending. If the multiplier is 2, a $100 increase in government spending could increase GDP by $200. The multiplier depends on marginal propensities to consume, import, and tax rates. In reality, crowding out effects may reduce the multiplier.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a multiplier less than 1, which can occur but is not the typical effect
- Option C ignores the economic transmission mechanisms
