# Question

The crowding out effect occurs when increased government borrowing:

## Options

- **A**: Lowers interest rates, stimulating private investment
- **B**: Raises interest rates, reducing private investment
- **C**: Has no effect on private sector activity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When governments borrow heavily to finance deficits, they compete with private borrowers for available funds. This increased demand for loanable funds pushes interest rates higher. Higher rates make private investment more expensive, "crowding out" some private sector borrowing and investment that would otherwise occur. This partially offsets the stimulative effect of fiscal expansion.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the interest rate effect
- Option C ignores the fundamental loanable funds market mechanism
