# Question

Emerging market economies typically differ from developed economies in having:

## Options

- **A**: Lower growth rates and more stable political systems
- **B**: Higher growth potential but greater political and economic risks
- **C**: Lower inflation and stronger institutions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Emerging markets are economies transitioning from developing to developed status. They typically offer higher growth potential due to demographic advantages, catch-up growth, and underutilized resources. However, they also carry higher risks: political instability, weaker institutions, currency volatility, less developed financial markets, and greater susceptibility to external shocks. Examples include China, India, Brazil, and Indonesia.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes characteristics more typical of developed markets
- Option C reverses typical emerging market characteristics
