# Question

The deadweight loss from a tax is minimized when:

## Options

- **A**: The tax is applied to goods with inelastic demand or supply
- **B**: The tax is applied to goods with elastic demand and supply
- **C**: The tax revenue is maximized

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Deadweight loss arises because taxes distort behavior—people produce and consume less of the taxed good. When demand or supply is inelastic, quantity responds less to price changes, so the tax causes smaller quantity distortions. This is why taxes on necessities, land, or addictive goods create less deadweight loss than taxes on luxury goods with many substitutes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B maximizes deadweight loss, not minimizes it
- Option C is unrelated—revenue maximization doesn't minimize deadweight loss
