# Question

The law of diminishing marginal returns to capital suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Adding more capital always increases output proportionally
- **B**: Each additional unit of capital adds less to output than the previous unit
- **C**: Capital investment has no effect on long-run growth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Diminishing marginal returns mean that holding other inputs constant, each additional unit of capital produces progressively smaller increases in output. A factory with 10 machines gains more from adding an 11th machine than a factory with 100 machines gains from adding a 101st. This limits growth from capital accumulation alone—sustained growth requires technological progress.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores diminishing returns
- Option C understates—capital investment does contribute to growth, just with diminishing returns
