# Question

"Hot money" in international finance refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term foreign direct investment in manufacturing
- **B**: Short-term capital flows seeking higher returns that can quickly reverse
- **C**: Counterfeit currency in circulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hot money describes short-term capital flows that move quickly between countries seeking the highest returns. These flows respond rapidly to interest rate differentials, exchange rate expectations, and risk perceptions. While they can provide liquidity, hot money can also cause financial instability when it suddenly reverses (capital flight), contributing to currency crises and financial market volatility.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes stable, long-term investment
- Option C is a literal misinterpretation of the term
