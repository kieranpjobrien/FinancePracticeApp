# Question

The discount rate in central banking refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The rate at which banks lend to each other overnight
- **B**: The rate at which banks can borrow directly from the central bank
- **C**: The rate of inflation adjustment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The discount rate (called the "bank rate" in some countries) is the interest rate at which eligible financial institutions can borrow from the central bank's lending facility. It typically serves as a ceiling for short-term rates since banks won't pay more in interbank markets than they could pay at the discount window. Central banks adjust the discount rate as a policy tool.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the federal funds rate or interbank rate
- Option C misinterprets the term "discount"
