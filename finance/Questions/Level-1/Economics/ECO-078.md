# Question

A country has absolute advantage in producing a good when it:

## Options

- **A**: Can produce the good with fewer resources than other countries
- **B**: Has the lowest opportunity cost of production
- **C**: Imports more of the good than it exports

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Absolute advantage exists when a country can produce a good using fewer resources (or more efficiently) than another country. For example, if Country A can produce a car using 100 labor hours while Country B requires 150 hours, Country A has absolute advantage in car production. Note that comparative advantage (lowest opportunity cost) determines trade patterns, not absolute advantage.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes comparative advantage
- Option C describes a net importer, unrelated to production efficiency
