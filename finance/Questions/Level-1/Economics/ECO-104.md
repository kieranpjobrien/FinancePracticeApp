# Question

Deflation refers to:

## Options

- **A**: A decline in the rate of inflation
- **B**: A sustained decrease in the general price level
- **C**: A decrease in the money supply only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deflation is a sustained decline in the overall price level—the opposite of inflation. During deflation, money gains purchasing power over time. While this sounds beneficial, deflation can be harmful: it increases the real burden of debt, may cause consumers to delay purchases expecting lower prices, and can lead to a deflationary spiral. Central banks generally aim to avoid deflation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes disinflation (slowing inflation but still positive)
- Option C describes one potential cause of deflation, not deflation itself
