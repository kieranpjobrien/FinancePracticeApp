# Question

The unemployment rate is classified as a:

## Options

- **A**: Leading indicator
- **B**: Coincident indicator
- **C**: Lagging indicator

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Unemployment is a lagging indicator because it typically peaks after a recession has ended and troughs after an expansion is well underway. Employers are slow to hire during recovery (waiting for sustained demand) and slow to fire during downturns (due to hiring/training costs). Leading indicators (stock prices, building permits) move before the cycle; coincident indicators (GDP, industrial production) move with it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses unemployment with forward-looking indicators
- Option B is incorrect—unemployment changes follow GDP changes
