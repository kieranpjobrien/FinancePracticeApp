# Question

Non-tariff barriers to trade include:

## Options

- **A**: Import taxes based on value or quantity
- **B**: Quotas, subsidies, technical standards, and bureaucratic procedures
- **C**: Only embargoes during wartime

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Non-tariff barriers (NTBs) are trade restrictions other than tariffs. They include: quotas (quantity limits), subsidies (giving domestic producers cost advantages), technical standards and regulations (safety/environmental requirements that may disadvantage imports), licensing requirements, and customs procedures. NTBs can be harder to identify and challenge than tariffs but equally restrictive.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes tariffs
- Option C is too narrow—NTBs are widely used in peacetime
