# Question

The balanced budget multiplier suggests that an equal increase in government spending and taxes will:

## Options

- **A**: Have no effect on GDP
- **B**: Increase GDP by approximately the amount of the spending increase
- **C**: Decrease GDP

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The balanced budget multiplier shows that equal increases in government spending and taxes (maintaining budget balance) still boost GDP. Government spending adds directly to demand with a multiplier effect, while higher taxes reduce consumer spending by less (since some tax increase comes from savings). The net effect is approximately 1—GDP rises by the amount of the spending increase.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the asymmetry between spending and tax multipliers
- Option C reverses the net effect
