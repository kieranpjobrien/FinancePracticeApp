# Question

The "impossible trinity" or monetary policy trilemma states that a country cannot simultaneously maintain:

## Options

- **A**: A fixed exchange rate, free capital mobility, and independent monetary policy
- **B**: A trade surplus, low inflation, and high growth
- **C**: A central bank, commercial banks, and shadow banks

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The impossible trinity holds that countries can achieve only two of three policy goals: fixed exchange rates, free capital flows, and independent monetary policy. For example, with fixed rates and free capital mobility, a country must match the anchor country's monetary policy. Most developed countries choose floating rates and free capital to retain monetary independence, while some emerging markets use capital controls to maintain fixed rates with some monetary autonomy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes economic outcomes, not the policy trade-off
- Option C describes financial system structure, not the policy trilemma
