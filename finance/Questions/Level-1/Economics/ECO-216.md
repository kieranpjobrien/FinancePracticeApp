# Question

A binding price ceiling set below the market equilibrium price will most likely result in:

## Options

- **A**: A surplus of the good
- **B**: A shortage of the good
- **C**: An increase in producer surplus

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A price ceiling below equilibrium keeps prices artificially low. At this lower price, quantity demanded exceeds quantity supplied, creating a shortage. Examples include rent control (housing shortages) and fuel price caps during crises (gas lines). Price ceilings also create deadweight loss and may lead to black markets or quality deterioration.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the effect of a price floor, not a ceiling
- Option C is incorrect—producer surplus decreases with price ceilings
