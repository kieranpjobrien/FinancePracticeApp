# Question

Foreign direct investment (FDI) differs from portfolio investment in that FDI:

## Options

- **A**: Involves only the purchase of financial assets like stocks and bonds
- **B**: Involves acquiring a controlling interest or significant influence in a foreign enterprise
- **C**: Is more liquid and easily reversible

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FDI involves acquiring a lasting management interest (typically 10% or more ownership) in a foreign enterprise, including building new facilities (greenfield) or acquiring existing companies (brownfield). Portfolio investment involves purchasing foreign stocks and bonds without controlling interest. FDI is generally more stable and brings management expertise and technology transfer, while portfolio investment is more liquid and mobile.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes portfolio investment
- Option C is a characteristic of portfolio investment, not FDI
