# Question

Triangular arbitrage opportunities exist when:

## Options

- **A**: All exchange rates are in equilibrium
- **B**: Cross rates calculated from bilateral rates differ from quoted cross rates
- **C**: Interest rates are the same across all countries

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Triangular arbitrage exploits inconsistencies between exchange rates. If the implied cross rate (calculated from two bilateral rates) differs from the market-quoted cross rate, a trader can profit by trading through three currencies. For example: USD→EUR→GBP→USD yielding more dollars than started. Such opportunities are typically small and short-lived due to active arbitrageurs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a market without arbitrage opportunities
- Option C relates to interest rate parity, not triangular arbitrage
