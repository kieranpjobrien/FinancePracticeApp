# Question

First-degree price discrimination occurs when a seller:

## Options

- **A**: Charges different prices in different geographic markets
- **B**: Charges each customer their maximum willingness to pay
- **C**: Offers quantity discounts to all buyers equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

First-degree (perfect) price discrimination involves charging each customer exactly their maximum willingness to pay, capturing all consumer surplus. This is rare in practice as it requires knowing each customer's demand curve. Second-degree discrimination involves pricing by quantity (bulk discounts), and third-degree involves different prices for different market segments (student discounts, regional pricing).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes third-degree price discrimination
- Option C describes second-degree price discrimination
