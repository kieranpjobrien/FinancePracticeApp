# Question

Mercantilism is an economic philosophy that emphasizes:

## Options

- **A**: Free trade and comparative advantage
- **B**: Accumulating trade surpluses and restricting imports
- **C**: International economic cooperation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Mercantilism, dominant in the 16th-18th centuries, viewed trade as zero-sum: nations should maximize exports and minimize imports to accumulate wealth (gold/silver). This led to protectionist policies. Adam Smith and David Ricardo later showed trade benefits both parties. Neo-mercantilist policies still appear when countries pursue persistent surpluses through currency intervention or trade barriers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes classical liberal trade theory
- Option C describes modern multilateral approaches
