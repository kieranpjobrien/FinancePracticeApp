# Question

An inverted yield curve has historically been associated with:

## Options

- **A**: Strong economic growth ahead
- **B**: An increased probability of recession within 12-18 months
- **C**: Rising inflation expectations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Yield curve inversion (when short-term rates exceed long-term rates) has historically preceded recessions. Inversion suggests markets expect the central bank to cut rates in the future due to economic weakness. While not every inversion leads to recession, and timing is uncertain, the inverted yield curve has a strong track record as a recession predictor, particularly the 2-year to 10-year spread.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is contrary to the signal an inverted curve provides
- Option C is typically associated with a steepening curve, not inversion
