# Question

Long-term economic growth is primarily driven by:

## Options

- **A**: Increases in aggregate demand
- **B**: Expansionary monetary policy
- **C**: Improvements in labor productivity

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Long-term sustainable growth comes from supply-side factors, particularly productivity improvements—output per hour worked. Productivity grows through capital accumulation, technological progress, improved education/skills, and better management practices. Demand-side factors (monetary/fiscal policy) affect short-term fluctuations but cannot permanently raise growth rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A affects short-run output, not long-run growth potential
- Option B stimulates demand but doesn't improve productive capacity
