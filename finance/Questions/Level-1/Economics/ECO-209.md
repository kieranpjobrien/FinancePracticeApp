# Question

Endogenous growth theory differs from neoclassical growth theory primarily by:

## Options

- **A**: Assuming constant returns to capital throughout the economy
- **B**: Treating technological progress as an outcome of economic decisions
- **C**: Ignoring the role of savings in economic growth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Endogenous growth models explain technological progress as resulting from deliberate investment in R&D, education, and knowledge creation—rather than treating it as an unexplained external factor. Knowledge spillovers and increasing returns to ideas can sustain growth without diminishing returns. This makes policy choices about education and innovation directly relevant to long-run growth rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is imprecise—endogenous models emphasize increasing returns to knowledge, not constant returns to all capital
- Option C is incorrect—savings still matter in endogenous growth models
