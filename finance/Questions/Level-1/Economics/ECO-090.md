# Question

The kinked demand curve model of oligopoly explains:

## Options

- **A**: Why oligopoly prices are always the lowest in the market
- **B**: Why prices may be sticky even when costs change
- **C**: How firms always cooperate to maximize joint profits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The kinked demand curve model suggests oligopolists face asymmetric competitor responses: rivals match price cuts (so demand is elastic below current price) but don't match price increases (so demand is inelastic above current price). This creates a "kink" at the current price and a discontinuity in marginal revenue, making prices sticky even when costs change within a range.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts oligopoly pricing patterns
- Option C describes cartel behavior, not the kinked demand explanation
