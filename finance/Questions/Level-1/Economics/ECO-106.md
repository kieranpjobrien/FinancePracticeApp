# Question

Negative interest rate policies (NIRP) implemented by some central banks:

## Options

- **A**: Pay banks to hold excess reserves
- **B**: Charge banks for holding excess reserves to encourage lending
- **C**: Automatically increase the money supply

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative interest rates charge banks for parking excess reserves at the central bank, creating an incentive to lend money rather than hold it. This unconventional policy has been used by the ECB, Bank of Japan, and others when conventional rate cuts are exhausted. NIRP aims to stimulate lending and economic activity but has limits (as holding physical cash becomes attractive) and potential unintended consequences for bank profitability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the opposite of negative rates
- Option C conflates rate policy with quantitative easing
