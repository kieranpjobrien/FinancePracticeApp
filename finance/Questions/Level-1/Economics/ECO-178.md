# Question

An income elasticity of demand greater than 1 indicates the good is:

## Options

- **A**: An inferior good
- **B**: A necessity
- **C**: A luxury (superior good)

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Income elasticity measures demand responsiveness to income changes. Elasticity > 1 means demand rises proportionally more than income—characteristic of luxury goods (fine dining, luxury cars). Necessities have positive but less than 1 elasticity (demand rises slower than income). Inferior goods have negative elasticity (demand falls as income rises).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes goods with negative income elasticity
- Option B describes goods with elasticity between 0 and 1
