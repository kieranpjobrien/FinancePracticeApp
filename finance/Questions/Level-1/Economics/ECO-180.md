# Question

Structural unemployment is most likely caused by:

## Options

- **A**: Temporary slowdowns in economic activity
- **B**: Mismatches between workers' skills and available jobs
- **C**: Workers taking time to find suitable positions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Structural unemployment occurs when workers' skills, locations, or industries don't match available jobs—often due to technological change, globalization, or shifts in consumer demand. Former factory workers in declining industries or workers in areas where industries have moved away face structural unemployment. It's more persistent than cyclical unemployment and requires retraining or relocation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cyclical unemployment
- Option C describes frictional unemployment
