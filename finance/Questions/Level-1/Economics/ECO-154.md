# Question

Core inflation excludes food and energy prices because:

## Options

- **A**: Food and energy are not important to consumers
- **B**: Food and energy prices are volatile and may not reflect underlying inflation trends
- **C**: Food and energy prices never change

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Food and energy prices are subject to supply shocks (weather, geopolitics) that cause temporary volatility unrelated to underlying monetary conditions. Core inflation (excluding these components) provides a better signal of persistent inflation trends that monetary policy can address. Central banks monitor both headline and core measures but often focus on core for policy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that food and energy are essential expenditures
- Option C contradicts reality—these prices are notably volatile
