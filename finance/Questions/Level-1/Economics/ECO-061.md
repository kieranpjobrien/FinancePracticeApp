# Question

In the business cycle, a trough represents:

## Options

- **A**: The peak of economic activity before decline begins
- **B**: The lowest point of economic activity before recovery begins
- **C**: A period of stable economic growth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A trough marks the lowest point of the business cycle—the end of a contraction/recession and the beginning of an expansion. At the trough, unemployment is typically at its highest, GDP at its lowest for the cycle, and consumer confidence at its weakest. From the trough, economic indicators begin improving as the recovery phase starts.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a peak, not a trough
- Option C describes expansion, not a turning point
