# Question

In a perfectly competitive market, individual firms are:

## Options

- **A**: Price setters who determine market prices
- **B**: Price takers who accept the market-determined price
- **C**: Able to differentiate their products from competitors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In perfect competition, many small firms sell identical (homogeneous) products. No single firm is large enough to influence the market price, so each firm must accept the prevailing market price as given. The firm's demand curve is perfectly elastic (horizontal) at the market price. Examples approaching perfect competition include agricultural commodities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes monopoly or firms with market power
- Option C describes monopolistic competition, where products are differentiated
