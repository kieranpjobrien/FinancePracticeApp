# Question

Net exports in GDP calculations equal:

## Options

- **A**: Total exports only
- **B**: Exports minus imports
- **C**: Imports minus exports

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Net exports (NX) equal the value of a country's exports minus its imports. In the GDP equation (GDP = C + I + G + NX), net exports can be positive (trade surplus) or negative (trade deficit). Exports add to GDP because they represent domestic production, while imports are subtracted because they represent foreign production consumed domestically.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores imports in the calculation
- Option C reverses the formula
