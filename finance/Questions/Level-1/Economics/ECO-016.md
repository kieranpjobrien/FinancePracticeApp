# Question

When a central bank raises its policy interest rate, the most likely immediate effect is:

## Options

- **A**: Lower borrowing costs for businesses and consumers
- **B**: Higher borrowing costs throughout the economy
- **C**: Increased money supply

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The central bank's policy rate (such as the federal funds rate in the US) serves as a benchmark for other interest rates. When the policy rate rises, commercial banks face higher costs for short-term borrowing, which they pass on to customers through higher lending rates for mortgages, business loans, and consumer credit. This transmission mechanism is central to monetary policy effectiveness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship between policy rates and borrowing costs
- Option C contradicts the contractionary nature of rate increases
