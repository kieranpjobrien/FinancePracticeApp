# Question

If demand is price elastic and a firm raises prices, total revenue will:

## Options

- **A**: Increase
- **B**: Decrease
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When demand is elastic (elasticity > 1), the percentage decrease in quantity demanded exceeds the percentage price increase. The volume drop more than offsets the higher price, reducing total revenue. Firms facing elastic demand should not raise prices; instead, they benefit from price cuts that expand volume more than proportionally.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is true for inelastic demand
- Option C is true for unit elastic demand
