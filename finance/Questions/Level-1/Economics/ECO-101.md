# Question

M2 money supply includes:

## Options

- **A**: Only physical currency in circulation
- **B**: M1 plus savings deposits, small time deposits, and money market funds
- **C**: Only large certificates of deposit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

M2 is a broader money measure that includes M1 (currency, demand deposits, and other checkable deposits) plus savings deposits, small time deposits (under $100,000), and retail money market funds. M2 captures money that is slightly less liquid but still readily convertible to spending. Central banks monitor M2 as an indicator of money available for spending and inflation potential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a subset of M1
- Option C describes components of M3 or broader aggregates
