# Question

Adverse selection in insurance markets occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: Insurers charge different premiums based on risk characteristics
- **B**: Higher-risk individuals are more likely to purchase insurance
- **C**: Insurance companies refuse to pay legitimate claims

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Adverse selection arises from information asymmetry—individuals know their risk better than insurers. Higher-risk people have stronger incentives to buy insurance, while low-risk people may opt out if premiums reflect average risk. This can cause a "death spiral" of rising premiums and shrinking pools. Remedies include mandatory coverage, risk-based pricing, and disclosure requirements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes risk-based pricing, a solution to adverse selection
- Option C describes claims disputes, not adverse selection
