# Question

A customs union differs from a free trade area in that a customs union:

## Options

- **A**: Has no internal tariffs but members set their own external tariffs
- **B**: Eliminates internal tariffs and adopts a common external tariff
- **C**: Includes free movement of labor

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A customs union goes beyond a free trade area by having members adopt a common external tariff on imports from non-members. This prevents trade deflection (importing through the lowest-tariff member) and requires deeper policy coordination. The EU started as a customs union before evolving into a single market and economic union with additional freedoms.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a free trade area, not a customs union
- Option C describes aspects of a common market or economic union
