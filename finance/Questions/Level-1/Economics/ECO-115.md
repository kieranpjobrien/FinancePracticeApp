# Question

A country's financial account records:

## Options

- **A**: Trade in goods and services only
- **B**: Cross-border investment flows including FDI, portfolio investment, and other investments
- **C**: Transfer payments and remittances

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The financial account (part of the balance of payments) records cross-border investment flows: foreign direct investment (FDI), portfolio investment (stocks and bonds), other investment (loans, deposits), and reserve assets. The current account records trade and income flows. A current account deficit must be financed by a financial account surplus (net capital inflows).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the trade component of the current account
- Option C describes the secondary income component of the current account
