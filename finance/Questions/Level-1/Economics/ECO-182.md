# Question

The natural rate of unemployment includes:

## Options

- **A**: Cyclical unemployment only
- **B**: Frictional and structural unemployment only
- **C**: Frictional, structural, and cyclical unemployment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The natural rate of unemployment (also called the non-accelerating inflation rate of unemployment or NAIRU) consists of frictional and structural unemployment—the unemployment that exists even when the economy is at "full employment." Cyclical unemployment is excluded because it represents deviations from the natural rate due to business cycle fluctuations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses cyclical with natural unemployment
- Option C incorrectly includes cyclical unemployment
