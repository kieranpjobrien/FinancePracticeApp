# Question

The Heckscher-Ohlin model predicts that countries will export goods that:

## Options

- **A**: They can produce most efficiently in absolute terms
- **B**: Use their relatively abundant factors of production intensively
- **C**: Have the highest domestic demand

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Heckscher-Ohlin model extends comparative advantage theory by explaining it in terms of factor endowments. Countries export goods that intensively use their relatively abundant factors (labor, capital, land). Labor-abundant countries export labor-intensive goods; capital-abundant countries export capital-intensive goods. This framework helps explain trade patterns and the effects of trade on factor prices.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes absolute advantage
- Option C ignores that exports depend on production advantages, not domestic demand
