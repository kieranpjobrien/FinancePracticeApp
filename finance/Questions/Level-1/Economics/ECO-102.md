# Question

If the reserve requirement is 10%, the simple money multiplier is:

## Options

- **A**: 5
- **B**: 10
- **C**: 0.1

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The simple money multiplier equals 1 divided by the reserve requirement ratio:

Money multiplier = 1 / Reserve requirement = 1 / 0.10 = 10

This means each dollar of bank reserves can support up to $10 in deposits through the lending process. In practice, the actual multiplier is lower due to excess reserves and currency holdings by the public.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses the wrong formula (perhaps 1/0.20)
- Option C is the reserve requirement itself, not the multiplier
