# Question

The "twin deficits" hypothesis suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Trade deficits cause budget surpluses
- **B**: Government budget deficits can lead to current account deficits
- **C**: Inflation and unemployment move together

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The twin deficits hypothesis argues that government budget deficits lead to current account deficits. The mechanism: budget deficits raise interest rates, attracting foreign capital that strengthens the currency, reducing exports and increasing imports. Additionally, fiscal stimulus increases domestic demand, some of which is met by imports. The relationship isn't always observed but has theoretical support.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the typical relationship
- Option C describes the breakdown of the Phillips curve, not twin deficits
