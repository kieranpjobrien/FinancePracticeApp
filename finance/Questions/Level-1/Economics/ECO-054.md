# Question

A profit-maximizing firm should produce where:

## Options

- **A**: Total revenue is maximized
- **B**: Marginal revenue equals marginal cost
- **C**: Average cost is minimized

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The profit-maximizing output level is where marginal revenue (MR) equals marginal cost (MC). At this point, the revenue from the last unit equals its cost, and any deviation reduces profit. If MR > MC, producing more adds to profit; if MR < MC, producing less would increase profit. This rule applies to all market structures, though the MR curve differs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores costs—maximum revenue doesn't mean maximum profit
- Option C focuses on efficiency rather than profit maximization
