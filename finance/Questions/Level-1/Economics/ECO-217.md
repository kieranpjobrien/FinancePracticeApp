# Question

A binding minimum wage set above the market equilibrium wage will most likely result in:

## Options

- **A**: A shortage of workers
- **B**: An increase in employment
- **C**: A surplus of labor (unemployment)

## Answer

C

## Explanation

A minimum wage above equilibrium is a price floor in the labor market. At this higher wage, quantity of labor supplied (workers seeking jobs) exceeds quantity demanded (employers' hiring), creating unemployment. The magnitude depends on labor demand elasticity—low-skill workers in competitive markets are most affected. Some studies suggest moderate minimums have small employment effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a labor shortage, which occurs when wages are below equilibrium
- Option B contradicts basic price floor theory
