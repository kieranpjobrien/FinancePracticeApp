# Question

A Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) reading above 50 typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Economic contraction in the manufacturing sector
- **B**: Economic expansion in the manufacturing sector
- **C**: No change in economic conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The PMI surveys purchasing managers about business conditions including new orders, production, and employment. The index is scaled so 50 represents no change from the prior month. Readings above 50 indicate expansion (conditions improving), while below 50 indicates contraction. PMI is a leading indicator because purchasing decisions precede actual production and sales.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the interpretation
- Option C describes exactly 50, not above 50
