# Question

In neoclassical growth theory, the sustainable long-run growth rate of per capita income depends primarily on:

## Options

- **A**: The savings rate
- **B**: The rate of technological progress
- **C**: The population growth rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In the Solow model, the steady-state growth rate of per capita income equals the rate of technological progress. While higher savings increases the level of output per capita, it doesn't permanently raise the growth rate due to diminishing returns to capital. Only continuous technological improvement drives sustained per capita income growth in the long run.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A affects the income level, not the long-run growth rate
- Option C affects total GDP growth but not per capita growth
