# Question

In oligopoly markets, firms often use game theory because:

## Options

- **A**: They can ignore competitors' actions when making decisions
- **B**: Each firm's optimal strategy depends on the expected actions of rival firms
- **C**: There are too many competitors to monitor individually

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Oligopoly features few firms with significant market power, where each firm's decisions affect and are affected by competitors' responses. Game theory helps analyze strategic interaction—firms consider how rivals will react to price changes, output decisions, or new product launches. This interdependence distinguishes oligopoly from other market structures and leads to complex competitive dynamics.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes competitive markets where firms don't affect each other
- Option C describes competitive markets with many small firms
