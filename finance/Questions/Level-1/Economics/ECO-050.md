# Question

Monopolistic competition is characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: A single seller of a unique product
- **B**: Many sellers offering differentiated products with some pricing power
- **C**: Many sellers of identical products with no pricing power

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Monopolistic competition features many firms selling differentiated products (through branding, quality, features, or location). Firms have some pricing power due to product differentiation but face competition from close substitutes. Entry is relatively free, so economic profits attract new entrants and are competed away in the long run. Examples include restaurants, clothing retailers, and service businesses.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a monopoly
- Option C describes perfect competition
