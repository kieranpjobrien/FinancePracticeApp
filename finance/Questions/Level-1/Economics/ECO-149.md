# Question

Diseconomies of scale occur when:

## Options

- **A**: Average costs decrease as output increases
- **B**: Average costs increase as output increases beyond an optimal level
- **C**: Total costs remain constant as output increases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Diseconomies of scale arise when a firm grows so large that average costs begin rising. Causes include management coordination problems, bureaucratic inefficiencies, communication breakdowns, and worker alienation. Beyond some optimal size, additional scale harms efficiency. The long-run average cost curve is U-shaped: decreasing (economies), flat (constant returns), then increasing (diseconomies).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes economies of scale
- Option C ignores the relationship between output and costs
