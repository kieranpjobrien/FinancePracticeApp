# Question

A currency crisis is most likely to occur when:

## Options

- **A**: A country has large foreign exchange reserves and balanced trade
- **B**: A fixed exchange rate becomes unsustainable due to economic fundamentals
- **C**: A country has floating exchange rates and strong capital inflows

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Currency crises typically occur when fixed or managed exchange rates become unsustainable. Signs of vulnerability include overvalued currencies, large current account deficits, declining foreign reserves, and weak economic fundamentals. Speculative attacks can force abandonment of the peg when speculators bet reserves will be exhausted. Famous examples include the 1997 Asian crisis and 1992 ERM crisis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes conditions that reduce crisis risk
- Option C describes conditions that typically strengthen a currency
