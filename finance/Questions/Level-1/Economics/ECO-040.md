# Question

Under a floating exchange rate regime:

## Options

- **A**: The central bank sets the exchange rate by decree
- **B**: The exchange rate is determined by supply and demand in the foreign exchange market
- **C**: The currency is fixed to gold at a set rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

In a floating (flexible) exchange rate regime, currency values are determined by market forces—supply and demand for the currency in foreign exchange markets. These flows depend on trade, investment, interest rate differentials, and expectations. Most major currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY) float, though central banks may occasionally intervene to smooth volatility.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a fixed or managed exchange rate
- Option C describes the gold standard, which is no longer used
