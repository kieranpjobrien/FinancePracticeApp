# Question

Minimum efficient scale (MES) is the:

## Options

- **A**: Largest output at which a firm can operate profitably
- **B**: Smallest output at which a firm achieves minimum average cost
- **C**: Output level that maximizes total revenue

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Minimum efficient scale is the smallest production level at which a firm fully exploits economies of scale, reaching the lowest point on its long-run average cost curve. MES is important for market structure: if MES is large relative to market demand, only a few firms can operate efficiently, creating natural oligopoly conditions. Industries with high MES tend to be more concentrated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses profitability with cost minimization
- Option C describes revenue maximization, not cost efficiency
