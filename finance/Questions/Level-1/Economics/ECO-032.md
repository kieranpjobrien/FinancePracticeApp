# Question

A free trade agreement (FTA) between countries typically:

## Options

- **A**: Increases tariffs on goods traded between member countries
- **B**: Reduces or eliminates tariffs and trade barriers between member countries
- **C**: Requires all members to adopt a common external tariff

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Free trade agreements reduce or eliminate tariffs and other trade barriers between signatory countries while each country maintains its own trade policy with non-members. Examples include USMCA (US-Mexico-Canada) and various bilateral agreements. FTAs aim to increase trade flows and economic integration while allowing countries to retain sovereignty over external trade policy.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite of what FTAs do
- Option C describes a customs union, which is a deeper form of integration
