# Question

Demand-pull inflation is most likely caused by:

## Options

- **A**: Rising production costs pushing up prices
- **B**: Aggregate demand growing faster than aggregate supply
- **C**: Supply chain disruptions reducing available goods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Demand-pull inflation occurs when aggregate demand exceeds aggregate supply at current prices—"too much money chasing too few goods." Common causes include expansionary monetary policy, fiscal stimulus, or strong consumer/investment spending. The excess demand pulls prices upward as buyers compete for limited goods.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cost-push inflation
- Option C describes a supply shock leading to cost-push inflation
