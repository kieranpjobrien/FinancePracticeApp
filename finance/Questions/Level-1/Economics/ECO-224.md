# Question

Command-and-control regulation refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Market-based incentives like carbon taxes
- **B**: Direct government mandates specifying required actions
- **C**: Voluntary industry self-regulation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Command-and-control regulation directly mandates specific behaviors: emissions limits, technology requirements, safety standards. Regulators specify what firms must do rather than using prices or incentives. While straightforward to implement, this approach may be less cost-effective than market-based alternatives because it doesn't allow firms to find the cheapest compliance methods.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market-based regulation, the alternative to command-and-control
- Option C describes voluntary action, not regulation
