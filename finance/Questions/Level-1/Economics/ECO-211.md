# Question

The primary purpose of antitrust regulation is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize government tax revenue from corporations
- **B**: Prevent monopolistic practices and promote competition
- **C**: Ensure all firms earn equal profits

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Antitrust (competition) policy aims to prevent market power abuse, maintain competitive markets, and protect consumer welfare. It addresses monopolization, price-fixing, market allocation, and anticompetitive mergers. Competitive markets generally produce better outcomes—lower prices, more innovation, and efficient resource allocation—than monopolized ones.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes tax policy, not competition policy
- Option C misunderstands the goal—competition policy promotes market efficiency, not profit equality
