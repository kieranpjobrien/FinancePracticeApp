# Question

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) may overstate inflation because of:

## Options

- **A**: Constantly updating the basket of goods
- **B**: Substitution bias, quality improvements, and new product introduction
- **C**: Using current-year weights for all calculations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CPI uses a fixed basket of goods that doesn't account for: substitution (consumers switch to cheaper alternatives when prices rise), quality improvements (a more expensive computer may offer more value), and new products (delayed basket updates miss innovations). These biases lead to overstating inflation and understating real income growth.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes what would reduce bias, not cause it
- Option C describes a different weighting approach
