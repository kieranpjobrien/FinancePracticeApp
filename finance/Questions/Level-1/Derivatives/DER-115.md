# Question

If the underlying stock price increases, the value of a put option will:

## Options

- **A**: Increase
- **B**: Decrease
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stock price effect on puts:

**Inverse relationship:**
- Higher stock → Lower put value
- Negative delta
- Opposite to calls

**Why puts decrease:**
- Put = Right to sell at strike
- Higher stock = Less valuable to sell at fixed strike
- Intrinsic value decreases

**Example:**
- Strike = $50
- Stock at $45: Put ITM by $5
- Stock rises to $55: Put OTM
- Put loses intrinsic value

**Put delta:**
- Ranges from -1 to 0
- Negative sensitivity
- Relationship: Put delta = Call delta - 1

**Moneyness changes:**
- As stock rises, put moves toward OTM
- ITM put becomes ATM becomes OTM
- Value decreases throughout

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This applies to call options
- Option C: Stock price significantly affects put value
