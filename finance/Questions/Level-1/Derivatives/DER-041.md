# Question

An increase in the risk-free interest rate will most likely cause:

## Options

- **A**: Call option values to increase and put option values to decrease
- **B**: Both call and put option values to increase
- **C**: Both call and put option values to decrease

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Interest rate effect on options:

**Call options (value increases):**
- Higher rate = higher cost of carry
- Forward price increases
- More likely to be in-the-money
- Present value of strike decreases

**Put options (value decreases):**
- Higher rate = lower PV of strike received
- Forward price increases (bad for puts)
- Less likely to be in-the-money

**Intuition (call):**
- Call is like deferred purchase
- Higher rates = more benefit from delay
- Save more interest on strike price

**Intuition (put):**
- Put is like deferred sale
- Higher rates = less valuable to wait
- PV of future strike receipt lower

**Rho:**
- Measures interest rate sensitivity
- Positive for calls
- Negative for puts

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Puts decrease with higher rates
- Option C: Calls increase with higher rates
