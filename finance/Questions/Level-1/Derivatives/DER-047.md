# Question

The binomial option pricing model assumes that the underlying asset price can:

## Options

- **A**: Move continuously in small increments
- **B**: Move to one of two possible values in each period
- **C**: Only move upward over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binomial model basics:

**Key assumption:**
- Discrete time periods
- Two possible outcomes per period
- Price moves up (u) or down (d)
- Known magnitudes

**Model structure:**
- Start at current price S
- After one period: Su or Sd
- Tree expands at each step
- Multiple paths to expiration

**Parameters:**
- u = up factor (u > 1)
- d = down factor (d < 1)
- Usually: d = 1/u
- Risk-free rate r

**Advantages:**
- Intuitive and flexible
- Handles American options
- Easy to implement
- Converges to Black-Scholes

**Example:**
- S = $100, u = 1.1, d = 0.9
- Up state: $110
- Down state: $90

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes Black-Scholes (continuous)
- Option C: Model includes down movements
