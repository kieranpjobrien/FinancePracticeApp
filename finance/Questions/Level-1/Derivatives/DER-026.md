# Question

A stock trades at $80 with no dividends expected. If the risk-free rate is 4% annually, the 6-month forward price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $80.00
- **B**: $81.58
- **C**: $83.20

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward price calculation:

**Formula (discrete compounding):**
F = S × (1 + r)^T

**Given:**
- Spot price (S) = $80
- Risk-free rate (r) = 4% = 0.04
- Time (T) = 6 months = 0.5 years

**Calculation:**
F = $80 × (1 + 0.04)^0.5
F = $80 × (1.04)^0.5
F = $80 × 1.0198
F = $81.58

**Alternative (continuous):**
F = S × e^(rT)
F = $80 × e^(0.04 × 0.5)
F = $80 × e^0.02
F = $80 × 1.0202
F = $81.62

**Interpretation:**
- Forward price > Spot price
- Difference = cost of financing
- No dividend offset

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignored time value of money
- Option C: Used full year instead of 6 months
