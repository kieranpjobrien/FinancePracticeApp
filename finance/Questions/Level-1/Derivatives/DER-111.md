# Question

A long straddle position profits when:

## Options

- **A**: The underlying price remains stable
- **B**: The underlying price moves significantly in either direction
- **C**: Implied volatility decreases

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Long straddle strategy:

**Components:**
- Buy call at strike K
- Buy put at strike K
- Same expiration
- ATM typically

**Profit scenario:**
- Large move in either direction
- Volatility play
- Don't know direction, expect movement

**Breakeven points:**
- Upper: K + Total premium
- Lower: K - Total premium
- Profit outside this range

**Example:**
- Stock at $50
- Buy $50 call for $3
- Buy $50 put for $2.50
- Total cost: $5.50
- Breakeven: $44.50 and $55.50

**When to use:**
- Expecting big move (earnings, news)
- Volatility expected to increase
- Uncertain on direction

**Risk:**
- Lose both premiums if price stable
- Time decay hurts both legs
- High premium cost

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Stability causes maximum loss
- Option C: Volatility decrease hurts long straddles
