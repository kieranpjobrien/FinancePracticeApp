# Question

In the binomial model, the actual (real-world) probability of an up move:

## Options

- **A**: Is always equal to 0.5
- **B**: Is not needed for risk-neutral option pricing
- **C**: Must be estimated from historical data for pricing

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Real-world vs. risk-neutral probabilities:

**Risk-neutral pricing:**
- Uses risk-neutral probability (π)
- Not actual probability (p)
- Based on no-arbitrage

**Why actual probability not needed:**
- Replication eliminates risk
- Same price regardless of beliefs
- Arbitrage enforces pricing

**Risk-neutral probability:**
π = (1 + r - d) / (u - d)

**Based on:**
- Up/down factors (u, d)
- Risk-free rate (r)
- NOT actual probabilities

**Actual probability matters for:**
- Risk management
- Expected returns
- Portfolio decisions

**Key insight:**
- Different investors may have different p
- All get same option price
- Market mechanism enforces consistency

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not always 0.5, depends on market
- Option C: Historical data used for volatility, not pricing probability
