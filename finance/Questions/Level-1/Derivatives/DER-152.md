# Question

Day count conventions in swaps determine:

## Options

- **A**: The currency in which payments are made
- **B**: How interest is calculated based on the number of days in each period
- **C**: The credit rating of the counterparties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Day count conventions:

**Purpose:**
- Calculate interest for periods
- Standardize accrual methods
- Determine payment amounts

**Common conventions:**

*Actual/360:*
- Actual days in period
- Divide by 360
- Money market standard

*Actual/365 (Fixed):*
- Actual days in period
- Divide by 365
- Common in UK

*30/360:*
- Assumes 30-day months
- Divide by 360
- Bond market standard

**Impact:**
- Different conventions give different payments
- Must match convention to market
- Specified in swap documentation

**Example (90 days, 5% rate, $1M):**
- Actual/360: 1M × 0.05 × (90/360) = $12,500
- Actual/365: 1M × 0.05 × (90/365) = $12,329
- 30/360: 1M × 0.05 × (90/360) = $12,500

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Currency is separate from day count
- Option C: Credit rating is unrelated
