# Question

Derivatives can contribute to systemic risk through:

## Options

- **A**: Reducing leverage in the financial system
- **B**: Interconnecting financial institutions through counterparty exposures
- **C**: Decreasing the speed of price movements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Derivatives and systemic risk:

**Interconnection risk:**
- Large OTC derivative positions
- Exposure between major banks
- Failure cascades through system
- "Too interconnected to fail"

**2008 financial crisis example:**
- AIG's massive CDS exposure
- Lehman counterparty failures
- Contagion through derivative links

**Sources of systemic risk:**
- Counterparty credit risk
- Leverage amplification
- Liquidity spirals
- Procyclical margining

**Risk transmission:**
- Margin calls force selling
- Hedging creates feedback loops
- Fire sales depress prices
- Spreads across institutions

**Post-crisis reforms:**
- Central clearing mandates
- Margin requirements
- Trade reporting
- Capital requirements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Derivatives often increase leverage
- Option C: Can accelerate price movements
