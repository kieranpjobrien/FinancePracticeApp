# Question

A prepaid forward contract differs from a standard forward in that:

## Options

- **A**: Settlement occurs at the beginning rather than at maturity
- **B**: The buyer pays the present value of the forward price at initiation
- **C**: No payment is ever made by the buyer

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Prepaid forward structure:

**Standard forward:**
- No payment at initiation
- Pay forward price F at maturity
- Receive asset at maturity

**Prepaid forward:**
- Pay discounted amount now
- Receive asset at maturity
- Like buying now, taking delivery later

**Prepaid forward price:**
F^P = S × e^(-qT) = S - PV(dividends)

**For non-dividend stock:**
F^P = S (just the spot price)

**Relationship:**
Regular forward: F = F^P × e^(rT)

**Why use prepaid:**
- Monetize asset without selling
- Tax or regulatory reasons
- Estate planning
- Lock in price with prepayment

**Example:**
- Stock at $100, no dividends
- 1-year prepaid forward = $100
- Regular forward = $100 × e^(0.05) = $105

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Buyer pays now, receives asset later
- Option C: Buyer pays full discounted price upfront
