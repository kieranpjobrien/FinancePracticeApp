# Question

In derivative markets, risk is primarily transferred from:

## Options

- **A**: Speculators to hedgers
- **B**: Hedgers to speculators
- **C**: Exchanges to individual traders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk transfer in derivatives:

**Hedgers:**
- Have underlying exposure
- Want to reduce risk
- Pay to transfer risk
- Accept reduced upside

**Speculators:**
- No underlying exposure
- Willing to take risk
- Seek profit opportunity
- Provide liquidity

**How transfer works:**
- Farmer sells wheat futures (hedges)
- Speculator buys futures (takes risk)
- Farmer locks in price
- Speculator hopes price rises

**Both parties benefit:**
- Hedger: Risk reduction
- Speculator: Profit potential
- Market: Liquidity and efficiency

**Insurance analogy:**
- Hedger = Insured party
- Speculator = Insurance company
- Premium flows to risk-taker

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Risk flows TO speculators, not from
- Option C: Exchanges intermediate, don't take positions
