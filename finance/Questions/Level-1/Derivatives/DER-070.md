# Question

A perfect hedge using derivatives:

## Options

- **A**: Is easily achieved in most market conditions
- **B**: Eliminates all risk from a position
- **C**: Is rare because of basis risk and other factors

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Perfect vs. imperfect hedging:

**Perfect hedge (theoretical):**
- Completely eliminates price risk
- Gain/loss on hedge = Loss/gain on position
- Net change = 0

**Why perfect hedges are rare:**

*Basis risk:*
- Asset and hedge instrument differ
- Timing mismatches
- Location/quality differences

*Other imperfections:*
- Contract size doesn't match exposure
- Margin requirements
- Transaction costs
- Liquidity constraints

**Cross-hedging:**
- Hedge with related but different asset
- Jet fuel hedged with crude oil
- Correlation < 1

**Practical hedging:**
- Reduces but doesn't eliminate risk
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Acceptable risk reduction

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Perfect hedges are rare, not common
- Option B: Some residual risk typically remains
