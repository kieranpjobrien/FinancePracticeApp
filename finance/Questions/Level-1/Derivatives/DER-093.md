# Question

Vega is highest for options that are:

## Options

- **A**: Deep in the money with short time to expiration
- **B**: At the money with long time to expiration
- **C**: Deep out of the money with short time to expiration

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Vega characteristics:

**Vega defined:**
- Sensitivity to volatility changes
- Change in option price per 1% change in volatility
- Always positive for long options

**Maximum vega:**
- At-the-money options
- Longer time to expiration
- More uncertainty about outcome

**Why ATM has highest vega:**
- Most uncertain whether ITM or OTM at expiration
- Volatility most impactful
- Could swing either way

**Why more time = higher vega:**
- More time for volatility to affect price
- Greater potential for large moves
- Time amplifies uncertainty

**Deep ITM or OTM:**
- Outcome more certain
- Less affected by volatility
- Lower vega

**Trading implications:**
- Volatility traders focus on ATM
- Long vega = long volatility
- Short vega = short volatility

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Deep ITM has low vega
- Option C: Short time reduces vega
