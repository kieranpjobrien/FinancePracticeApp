# Question

Model risk in derivatives arises from:

## Options

- **A**: Using too many traders to manage positions
- **B**: Errors or inappropriate assumptions in pricing or risk models
- **C**: Trading exclusively on exchanges

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Model risk in derivatives:

**Sources of model risk:**
- Wrong model specification
- Incorrect assumptions
- Parameter estimation errors
- Implementation mistakes

**Examples:**

*Volatility assumptions:*
- Constant volatility assumed
- Reality: Volatility changes
- Mispricing results

*Distribution assumptions:*
- Normal distribution assumed
- Fat tails in reality
- Underestimated tail risk

*Correlation estimates:*
- Historical correlation used
- Correlations change in stress
- 2008 crisis example

**Consequences:**
- Mispriced derivatives
- Unexpected losses
- Inadequate hedges
- Systemic issues

**Mitigation:**
- Model validation
- Stress testing
- Multiple models
- Expert oversight

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Trader count isn't model risk
- Option C: Exchange trading doesn't cause model risk
