# Question

Implied volatility is:

## Options

- **A**: Calculated from historical price data
- **B**: The volatility that makes the model price equal the market price
- **C**: Always constant across different strike prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Implied volatility explained:

**Definition:**
- Backed out from option prices
- Volatility input that gives market price
- Forward-looking measure

**Calculation process:**
1. Observe market option price
2. Use pricing model (Black-Scholes)
3. Solve for volatility that matches price
4. This is implied volatility

**vs. Historical volatility:**
- Historical: Based on past prices
- Implied: Based on current option prices
- Often different values

**VIX (Volatility Index):**
- Based on S&P 500 option prices
- Implied volatility of market
- "Fear gauge"

**Volatility smile/skew:**
- Implied vol varies by strike
- Not constant (violates BSM assumption)
- Higher for OTM puts typically

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is historical volatility
- Option C: Volatility smile shows it varies
