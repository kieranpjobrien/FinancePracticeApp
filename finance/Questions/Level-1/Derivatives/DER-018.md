# Question

Counterparty risk in derivatives is most significant for:

## Options

- **A**: Exchange-traded futures with central clearing
- **B**: Over-the-counter (OTC) derivatives
- **C**: Options traded on organized exchanges

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Counterparty risk in derivatives:

**OTC derivatives (highest risk):**
- Bilateral agreements
- Direct exposure to counterparty
- No clearinghouse guarantee
- Default can cause significant losses

**Exchange-traded (lower risk):**
- Clearinghouse intermediates
- Becomes counterparty to both sides
- Margin requirements
- Daily settlement reduces exposure

**Mitigating OTC counterparty risk:**
- Collateral/margin agreements
- Netting arrangements
- Credit limits
- Central clearing (post-2008 reforms)

**2008 financial crisis:**
- OTC derivatives amplified systemic risk
- Lehman Brothers default
- Led to clearing mandates

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Central clearing greatly reduces counterparty risk
- Option C: Exchange clearing reduces this risk
