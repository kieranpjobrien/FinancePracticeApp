# Question

The fixed rate in an interest rate swap is primarily determined by:

## Options

- **A**: Historical average of floating rates
- **B**: Forward interest rates derived from the current yield curve
- **C**: The credit rating of the swap counterparties

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Swap fixed rate determination:

**Based on forward rates:**
- Implied by yield curve
- Expected future floating rates
- Discount factors from curve

**Calculation approach:**
1. Bootstrap forward rates from yield curve
2. Calculate PV of expected floating payments
3. Find fixed rate where PV(fixed) = PV(floating)
4. This is the par swap rate

**Why forward rates:**
- Reflect market expectations
- No-arbitrage pricing
- Fair value at inception

**Yield curve shape matters:**
- Upward sloping: Fixed > Current floating
- Downward sloping: Fixed < Current floating
- Flat: Fixed ≈ Current floating

**Credit adjustment:**
- May adjust for credit risk
- But primary driver is rate curve
- CVA/DVA adjustments

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Historical rates don't determine forward prices
- Option C: Credit affects spread, not base rate calculation
