# Question

The strike price of an option is:

## Options

- **A**: The price at which the underlying was trading when the option was purchased
- **B**: The price at which the option holder can buy or sell the underlying
- **C**: The premium paid for the option

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strike price fundamentals:

**Definition:**
- Exercise price of the option
- Predetermined price in contract
- Fixed throughout option's life

**For call options:**
- Right to BUY at strike price
- Exercise when spot > strike
- Profit = Spot - Strike - Premium

**For put options:**
- Right to SELL at strike price
- Exercise when spot < strike
- Profit = Strike - Spot - Premium

**Strike selection:**
- Multiple strikes available
- ITM, ATM, OTM options
- Choice affects premium and probability

**Example:**
- Stock at $50
- Call with $55 strike
- Right to buy at $55
- Profitable if stock exceeds $55

**Common terminology:**
- Strike price = Exercise price
- K in formulas

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Strike is predetermined, not entry price
- Option C: Premium is the price paid for the option
