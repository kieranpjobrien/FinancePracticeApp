# Question

Credit risk in a forward contract is borne by:

## Options

- **A**: Only the party in the long position
- **B**: The party with positive contract value (in-the-money)
- **C**: Only the party in the short position

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward contract credit risk:

**Who bears risk:**
- Party with positive value
- "In the money" party
- Could lose if counterparty defaults

**Changes over time:**
- At inception: No credit risk (value = 0)
- As prices move: One party gains value
- Winner has credit exposure to loser

**Example:**
- Forward price = $100
- Spot rises to $110
- Long has positive value ($10)
- Long bears credit risk on short

**If spot falls:**
- Short has positive value
- Short bears credit risk on long
- Positions can reverse

**Mitigation:**
- Collateral agreements
- Margin requirements
- Central clearing
- Netting arrangements

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Either party can have credit exposure
- Option C: Either party can have credit exposure
