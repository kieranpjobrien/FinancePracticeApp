# Question

A stock index is at 2,500 with a continuous dividend yield of 2%. The risk-free rate is 5% (continuous). The 6-month forward price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 2,463
- **B**: 2,538
- **C**: 2,563

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Forward price with continuous dividend yield:

**Formula:**
F = S × e^((r - q) × T)

**Given:**
- S = 2,500
- r = 5% (continuous)
- q = 2% (dividend yield)
- T = 0.5 years

**Calculation:**
F = 2,500 × e^((0.05 - 0.02) × 0.5)
F = 2,500 × e^(0.015)
F = 2,500 × 1.01511
F = 2,537.78 ≈ 2,538

**Interpretation:**
- Net cost of carry = r - q = 3%
- Forward > Spot because r > q
- Dividend yield reduces forward price

**If q > r:**
- Forward would be below spot
- Negative cost of carry

**Alternative formula:**
F = S × e^(rT) × e^(-qT)
= Financing cost × Dividend adjustment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Used q - r instead of r - q
- Option C: Used r only, ignored dividend yield
