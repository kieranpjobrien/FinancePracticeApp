# Question

In a bullet swap, the notional principal:

## Options

- **A**: Declines over the life of the swap
- **B**: Increases over the life of the swap
- **C**: Remains constant throughout the life of the swap

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Bullet swap structure:

**Definition:**
- Fixed notional principal
- Constant throughout life
- Most common swap type

**Payment calculation:**
- Same notional each period
- Interest = Notional × Rate × Time
- Consistent exposure

**Example (5-year bullet swap):**
- Notional: $100 million
- Year 1: $100M notional
- Year 3: $100M notional
- Year 5: $100M notional

**Contrast with amortizing:**
- Notional declines over time
- Matches loan amortization
- Decreasing payments

**Contrast with accreting:**
- Notional increases over time
- Matches construction financing
- Growing payments

**Why most common:**
- Simple structure
- Matches many hedging needs
- Easy to understand

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes amortizing swap
- Option B: This describes accreting swap
