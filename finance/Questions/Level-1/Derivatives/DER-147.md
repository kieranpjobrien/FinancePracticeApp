# Question

Covered interest rate parity ensures that:

## Options

- **A**: Exchange rates remain constant over time
- **B**: The return from hedged foreign investment equals the domestic risk-free rate
- **C**: Higher interest rate currencies always appreciate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered interest rate parity:

**The relationship:**
F/S = (1 + r_d) / (1 + r_f)

**What it means:**
- Invest domestic at r_d
- OR: Convert to foreign, invest at r_f, hedge back
- Both strategies earn same return

**If violated:**
- Arbitrage opportunity
- Borrow in one currency
- Lend in another
- Hedge currency risk
- Risk-free profit

**Example:**
- USD rate = 5%, EUR rate = 2%
- If parity holds, no arbitrage
- Forward rate adjusts for rate differential

**"Covered" meaning:**
- Currency risk is hedged
- Forward contract locks in exchange rate
- No FX exposure

**Contrast with uncovered:**
- Uncovered: No hedge
- Based on expected spot rate
- Risky return

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Exchange rates move based on rates
- Option C: Higher rate currencies depreciate forward
