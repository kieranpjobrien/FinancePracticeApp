# Question

Options have asymmetric payoff profiles because:

## Options

- **A**: Both parties have the same potential gains and losses
- **B**: The buyer has limited downside but unlimited or large upside potential
- **C**: The seller always makes a profit

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asymmetric option payoffs:

**Option buyer:**
- Maximum loss = Premium paid
- Potential gain = Large/unlimited
- Asymmetric risk-reward

**Option seller:**
- Maximum gain = Premium received
- Potential loss = Large/unlimited
- Opposite asymmetry

**Call buyer example:**
- Pay $3 premium
- Max loss = $3
- If stock goes to $100: Huge profit
- Asymmetric payoff

**Contrast with forwards:**
- Symmetric payoffs
- Gains and losses mirror each other
- No limited downside

**Why asymmetry exists:**
- Premium buys protection
- Seller compensated for risk
- Optionality has value

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes forwards/futures
- Option C: Seller can have large losses
