# Question

Open interest in futures markets represents:

## Options

- **A**: The total number of contracts traded during the day
- **B**: The total number of outstanding contracts not yet closed
- **C**: The interest rate used to price futures

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Open interest explained:

**Definition:**
- Outstanding contracts at end of day
- One long + one short = 1 open interest
- Measures market participation

**Open interest changes:**

*Increases when:*
- New buyer + New seller
- Both entering new positions
- Creates new contracts

*Decreases when:*
- Existing long closes + Existing short closes
- Both exiting positions
- Contracts eliminated

*Unchanged when:*
- Existing trader transfers to new trader
- One closes, one opens

**vs. Volume:**
- Volume = Total contracts traded
- Can include opening and closing
- Resets to zero each day

**Interpretation:**
- High OI = Liquid market
- Rising OI = New money entering
- Falling OI = Positions being closed

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is volume, not open interest
- Option C: Unrelated to interest rates
