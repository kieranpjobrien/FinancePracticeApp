# Question

A ratio call spread involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying and selling an equal number of calls at different strikes
- **B**: Buying fewer calls at a lower strike and selling more calls at a higher strike
- **C**: Buying more calls at a lower strike than selling at a higher strike

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Ratio call spread:

**Typical structure (1:2):**
- Buy 1 call at lower strike
- Sell 2 calls at higher strike
- Net credit often possible

**Characteristics:**
- Moderately bullish
- Limited downside risk
- Unlimited upside risk
- Profits if stock rises modestly

**Maximum profit:**
- At short strike at expiration
- Long call ITM
- Short calls expire worthless

**Risk:**
- Large upside move hurts
- Extra short call exposed
- Losses above upper breakeven

**Profit zone:**
- Lower strike to upper breakeven
- Beyond that: Losses accumulate

**When to use:**
- Expect modest rise
- Want to reduce cost
- Willing to cap large gains

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is a regular vertical spread
- Option C: Ratio is more short than long
