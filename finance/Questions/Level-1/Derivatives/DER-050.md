# Question

In the binomial model, the hedge ratio (delta) represents:

## Options

- **A**: The number of options needed to hedge one share of stock
- **B**: The number of shares needed to hedge one option
- **C**: The probability of the option expiring in the money

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binomial hedge ratio:

**Delta formula:**
Δ = (Cu - Cd) / (Su - Sd)

**Interpretation:**
- Shares of stock per option
- Creates risk-free portfolio
- Changes as price moves

**Replicating portfolio:**
- Long Δ shares of stock
- Borrow appropriate amount
- Payoff equals option payoff

**Example:**
- Su = $120, Sd = $85
- Cu = $20, Cd = $0
- Δ = (20 - 0) / (120 - 85)
- Δ = 20 / 35 = 0.571

**Meaning:**
- Need 0.571 shares to hedge 1 call
- Or 1.75 calls to hedge 1 share
- Rebalance as price changes

**Properties:**
- Call delta: 0 to 1
- Put delta: -1 to 0
- ATM options: delta ≈ 0.5

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Inverse of correct interpretation
- Option C: Delta ≠ probability (though related)
