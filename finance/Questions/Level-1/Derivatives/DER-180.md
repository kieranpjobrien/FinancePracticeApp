# Question

An iron condor strategy profits most when:

## Options

- **A**: The underlying price moves significantly in either direction
- **B**: The underlying price stays within a defined range
- **C**: Volatility increases dramatically

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Iron condor strategy:

**Structure:**
- Sell OTM put (K1)
- Buy further OTM put (K2, K2 < K1)
- Sell OTM call (K3)
- Buy further OTM call (K4, K4 > K3)

**Alternatively:**
- Bull put spread + Bear call spread
- Credit spread on both sides

**Profit zone:**
- Between K1 and K3
- Stock stays in range
- All options expire worthless
- Keep full credit

**Maximum profit:**
- Net premium received
- When stock between K1 and K3

**Maximum loss:**
- Difference between strikes - Premium
- If stock moves outside wings

**When to use:**
- Low volatility expectation
- Range-bound market
- Defined risk neutral strategy

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Large moves hurt iron condors
- Option C: Short volatility position
