# Question

In the binomial model, as the number of time steps increases while holding total time constant:

## Options

- **A**: Each up and down movement becomes larger
- **B**: Each up and down movement becomes smaller
- **C**: The movements remain the same size

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Binomial step size and time:

**Relationship:**
u = e^(σ√Δt)
Δt = T/n

**As n increases:**
- Δt decreases
- √Δt decreases
- u approaches 1
- Smaller movements per step

**Example:**
- T = 1 year, σ = 20%
- n = 1: Δt = 1, u = e^(0.20) = 1.22
- n = 12: Δt = 1/12, u = e^(0.20×0.29) = 1.06
- n = 252: Δt = 1/252, u = e^(0.20×0.063) = 1.013

**Why this matters:**
- Many small steps approximate continuous
- Converges to Black-Scholes
- Better approximation of reality

**Same total volatility:**
- Cumulative movement same
- Just divided into smaller steps
- Law of large numbers applies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Movements get smaller, not larger
- Option C: Time step affects movement size
