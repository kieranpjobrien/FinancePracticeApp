# Question

A speculator who expects interest rates to rise would most likely:

## Options

- **A**: Enter a receive-fixed, pay-floating interest rate swap
- **B**: Enter a pay-fixed, receive-floating interest rate swap
- **C**: Buy interest rate call options

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Speculating on rising rates:

**Pay-fixed, receive-floating:**
- Pay fixed rate (locked in)
- Receive floating rate (increases with rates)
- Net gain as rates rise

**Position analysis:**
- If rates rise above fixed rate:
  - Floating receipts > Fixed payments
  - Swap becomes valuable
  - Mark-to-market gain

**Alternative interpretation:**
- Like being short a fixed-rate bond
- Bonds lose value when rates rise
- Short position profits

**Other strategies for rising rates:**
- Short bond futures
- Buy put options on bonds
- Pay fixed in FRAs

**Receive-fixed (opposite):**
- Benefits from falling rates
- Fixed receipts more valuable
- Loses value if rates rise

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Benefits from falling rates
- Option C: Interest rate calls typically benefit receivers
