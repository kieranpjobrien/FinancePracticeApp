# Question

A margin call on a futures position is triggered when:

## Options

- **A**: The account balance exceeds the initial margin
- **B**: The account balance falls below the maintenance margin
- **C**: The futures contract is about to expire

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Margin call mechanics:

**Margin levels:**
- Initial margin: Required to open position
- Maintenance margin: Minimum balance allowed
- Typically: Maintenance = 70-80% of initial

**Margin call trigger:**
- Daily mark-to-market losses
- Account falls below maintenance
- Must restore to initial margin
- Usually same day (variation margin)

**Example:**
- Initial margin: $10,000
- Maintenance margin: $7,500
- After losses, account = $7,000
- Below $7,500 → Margin call
- Must deposit $3,000 (back to $10,000)

**Failure to meet margin call:**
- Broker liquidates position
- At current market price
- Investor realizes losses

**Purpose:**
- Ensures ability to pay
- Limits counterparty risk
- Daily settlement of gains/losses

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Excess margin is fine
- Option C: Expiration unrelated to margin calls
