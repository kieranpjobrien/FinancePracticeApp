# Question

The lower bound for a European call option on a non-dividend paying stock is:

## Options

- **A**: Max(0, S - K)
- **B**: Max(0, S - PV(K))
- **C**: S - K

## Answer

B

## Explanation

European call lower bound:

**Formula:**
C ≥ Max(0, S - PV(K))

**Derivation:**
- Compare call to stock minus borrowing
- Can't be worth less than intrinsic in PV terms
- No arbitrage enforcement

**Why PV(K) not K:**
- European can only exercise at maturity
- Strike paid in future
- Must discount to today

**Arbitrage if violated:**
- If C < S - PV(K)
- Buy call, short stock, lend PV(K)
- Risk-free profit

**American call:**
- C ≥ Max(0, S - K)
- Can exercise anytime
- No need to discount K

**Example:**
- S = $50, K = $45, r = 5%, T = 1 year
- PV(K) = $45/1.05 = $42.86
- Lower bound = $50 - $42.86 = $7.14

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is American call lower bound
- Option C: Could be negative, need Max with 0
