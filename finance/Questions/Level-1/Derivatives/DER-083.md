# Question

To close out a long futures position before expiration, a trader must:

## Options

- **A**: Take physical delivery of the underlying asset
- **B**: Sell an identical futures contract
- **C**: Let the contract expire automatically

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Closing futures positions:

**Offsetting trade:**
- Enter opposite position
- Same contract specifications
- Cancels original position
- Net position becomes zero

**Long position close:**
- Originally bought futures
- Sell identical contract to close
- Profit/loss realized

**Short position close:**
- Originally sold futures
- Buy identical contract to close
- Profit/loss realized

**Most common exit:**
- 97%+ of futures closed before expiration
- Avoids delivery logistics
- Realizes economic gain/loss

**Alternative exits:**
- Physical delivery (rare)
- Cash settlement (for some contracts)
- Exchange for physical (EFP)

**Clearinghouse role:**
- Matches offsetting trades
- Nets positions to zero
- Returns margin

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Delivery is rare, offsetting is standard
- Option C: Position persists until closed or expired
