# Question

A non-deliverable forward (NDF) is used primarily for:

## Options

- **A**: Currencies that can be freely converted and delivered
- **B**: Currencies with restricted convertibility or capital controls
- **C**: Equity index forwards only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Non-deliverable forwards:

**Definition:**
- Cash-settled forward contract
- No actual currency delivery
- Settlement in convertible currency (usually USD)

**Why NDFs exist:**
- Currency controls prevent delivery
- Restricted convertibility
- Offshore hedging/speculation

**Common NDF currencies:**
- Chinese yuan (CNY)
- Indian rupee (INR)
- Korean won (KRW)
- Brazilian real (BRL)
- Others with restrictions

**Settlement:**
- Calculate difference between NDF rate and fixing
- Pay in USD (or other hard currency)
- Based on official fixing rate

**Example:**
- NDF rate: 7.00 CNY/USD
- Fixing rate: 7.10 CNY/USD
- Long USD position profits
- Settlement in USD

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Freely convertible currencies use regular forwards
- Option C: NDFs are for currencies, not equity indexes
