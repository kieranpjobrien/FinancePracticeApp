# Question

A cash-and-carry arbitrage involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying the underlying and selling a forward when the forward is overpriced
- **B**: Selling the underlying and buying a forward when the forward is underpriced
- **C**: Buying both the underlying and a forward simultaneously

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cash-and-carry arbitrage:

**When to execute:**
- Forward price > Theoretical fair price
- Forward is overpriced

**Strategy:**
1. Buy (carry) the underlying asset
2. Borrow to finance the purchase
3. Sell (short) the forward contract

**At expiration:**
- Deliver underlying against forward
- Receive forward price
- Repay loan
- Profit = Forward price - (Spot + Carry costs)

**"Cash":** Borrowed money to buy
**"Carry":** Hold the asset until delivery

**Reverse cash-and-carry:**
- Forward underpriced
- Short the underlying
- Invest proceeds
- Buy forward

**Arbitrage ensures:**
- F ≤ S × (1 + r)^T + Carry costs
- Otherwise profit opportunity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Describes reverse cash-and-carry
- Option C: Not an arbitrage strategy
