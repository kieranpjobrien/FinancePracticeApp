# Question

An American-style option differs from a European-style option in that an American option:

## Options

- **A**: Can only be traded on American exchanges
- **B**: Can be exercised at any time up to and including expiration
- **C**: Is always more valuable due to geographic factors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Exercise style comparison:

**American-style options:**
- Exercise any time before/at expiration
- Greater flexibility
- Generally more valuable
- Early exercise sometimes optimal

**European-style options:**
- Exercise only at expiration
- Less flexible
- Often easier to price
- No early exercise decisions

**Value implications:**
- American ≥ European (same terms)
- Extra value = early exercise premium
- Difference depends on dividends, rates

**When early exercise matters:**
- Deep in-the-money calls on dividend stocks
- Deep in-the-money puts
- Interest rate considerations

**Note:**
- Names are historical, not geographic
- Both types traded globally

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Names don't indicate trading location
- Option C: More valuable due to flexibility, not geography
