# Question

Which of the following can serve as an underlying for a derivative?

## Options

- **A**: Only physical commodities like gold and oil
- **B**: Stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, interest rates, and indexes
- **C**: Only exchange-traded securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Underlying assets in derivatives:

**Wide range of underlyings:**

*Financial assets:*
- Stocks and stock indexes
- Bonds and interest rates
- Currencies
- Credit (CDS)

*Physical assets:*
- Commodities (oil, gold, wheat)
- Metals
- Energy products

*Other:*
- Volatility (VIX)
- Weather (temperature, rainfall)
- Real estate indexes
- Cryptocurrency

**Non-asset underlyings:**
- Interest rates (not an asset)
- Credit events
- Economic data
- Even election outcomes

**Key point:**
- Anything with measurable value
- That can be contractually defined
- With settlement mechanism

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Many non-commodity underlyings exist
- Option C: OTC derivatives can have any underlying
