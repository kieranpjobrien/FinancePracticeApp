# Question

In a multi-period binomial model, the replicating portfolio's stock position must be:

## Options

- **A**: Fixed at the initial delta throughout the option's life
- **B**: Adjusted at each node as the option's delta changes
- **C**: Equal to one share of stock at all times

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dynamic replication:

**Why rebalancing needed:**
- Delta changes at each node
- Stock price moves change moneyness
- Hedge ratio updates required

**At each node:**
- Calculate new delta
- Δ = (Cu - Cd) / (Su - Sd)
- Different for each node
- Adjust stock position

**Example (2-period):**
- Initial Δ = 0.6: Hold 0.6 shares
- After up move: New Δ = 0.8
- Rebalance: Buy 0.2 more shares
- After down move: New Δ = 0.3
- Rebalance: Sell 0.3 shares

**Self-financing:**
- Rebalancing financed by borrowing
- No additional capital needed
- Theoretical construct

**Practical implications:**
- Continuous rebalancing impossible
- Transaction costs matter
- Discrete rebalancing errors

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Delta changes, must adjust
- Option C: Delta rarely equals 1 exactly
