# Question

In a bilateral OTC swap, each party is exposed to:

## Options

- **A**: No credit risk due to exchange guarantees
- **B**: Credit risk of the other party
- **C**: Credit risk of the exchange clearinghouse only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bilateral swap credit risk:

**OTC (over-the-counter):**
- Direct agreement between parties
- No exchange intermediary
- Each faces the other

**Credit exposure:**
- If counterparty defaults
- May lose value of contract
- Replacement cost risk

**When exposure exists:**
- Contract has positive value
- "In the money" party has risk
- Could lose if other defaults

**Mitigation methods:**
- Collateral agreements (CSA)
- Netting arrangements
- Credit limits
- CVA/DVA adjustments

**Contrast with exchange-traded:**
- Clearinghouse guarantees
- Counterparty is CCP
- Reduced credit risk

**Post-2008 reforms:**
- More central clearing mandated
- But many swaps still bilateral
- Margin requirements increased

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: OTC swaps have no exchange guarantee
- Option C: Bilateral means no clearinghouse involved
