# Question

The theory of normal backwardation suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Futures prices are unbiased predictors of future spot prices
- **B**: Futures prices are below expected future spot prices, compensating speculators
- **C**: Futures prices always exceed spot prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Normal backwardation theory:

**Keynes' theory:**
- Hedgers are predominantly sellers (short)
- Speculators must be induced to go long
- Futures price < Expected spot price
- Risk premium for speculators

**Hedger motivation:**
- Producers want to lock in prices
- Willing to accept lower futures price
- Pay "insurance premium"

**Speculator reward:**
- Takes on price risk
- Earns risk premium
- F < E(S_T)
- Expects to buy low, sell high at expiration

**Formula:**
E(S_T) - F = Risk premium to long speculators

**Contrast with contango:**
- Contango: F > S (about current spot)
- Backwardation: F < E(S_T) (about expected spot)
- Different concepts

**Modern view:**
- May not always hold
- Depends on hedger balance
- Varies by market

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is unbiased expectations hypothesis
- Option C: This describes contango in current terms
