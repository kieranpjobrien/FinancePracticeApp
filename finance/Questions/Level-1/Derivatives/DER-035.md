# Question

In a plain vanilla interest rate swap, the notional principal is:

## Options

- **A**: Exchanged at the beginning of the swap
- **B**: Used only to calculate interest payments
- **C**: Exchanged at both the beginning and end

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Notional principal in swaps:

**Plain vanilla interest rate swap:**
- Notional is NEVER exchanged
- Only used to calculate payments
- Both parties use same notional
- Same currency

**Why no exchange needed:**
- Both pay in same currency
- Principal payments would offset
- Net payment is zero
- Simplifies the transaction

**Calculation example:**
- Notional = $10 million
- Fixed rate = 5%
- Fixed payment = $10M × 5% × (period)
- Floating payment = $10M × SOFR × (period)

**Currency swaps (different):**
- Principal IS exchanged
- At beginning and end
- Different currencies involved

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: No exchange at beginning for IR swaps
- Option C: Describes currency swaps, not interest rate swaps
