# Question

A "naked" or uncovered option position refers to:

## Options

- **A**: An option buyer who hasn't paid the premium
- **B**: An option seller without an offsetting position in the underlying
- **C**: An option that has expired worthless

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Naked option positions:

**Definition:**
- Short option without offsetting hedge
- No stock position to cover
- Exposed to unlimited/large risk

**Naked call:**
- Sold call without owning stock
- If exercised, must buy stock at market
- Potentially unlimited loss

**Naked put:**
- Sold put without short stock
- If exercised, must buy stock
- Large loss if stock falls to zero

**Contrast with covered:**
- Covered call: Short call + Long stock
- Stock covers delivery obligation
- Limited risk

**Margin requirements:**
- Higher for naked positions
- Reflects greater risk
- Broker protection

**Who uses naked options:**
- Experienced traders
- Market makers
- Speculators on stability

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Premium always required from buyer
- Option C: Expiration status unrelated to coverage
