# Question

A true arbitrage opportunity has which characteristic?

## Options

- **A**: Small but certain profit with some risk
- **B**: Risk-free profit with no net investment
- **C**: Large potential profit with moderate risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

True arbitrage characteristics:

**Definition:**
- Risk-free profit
- No net investment
- Guaranteed positive return
- "Free lunch"

**Requirements:**
- Simultaneous transactions
- Offsetting positions
- No capital required (or financed)
- Certain outcome

**Example:**
- Same stock trades at $100 in NY
- Same stock trades at $101 in London
- Buy NY, sell London
- Risk-free $1 profit

**Why rare in practice:**
- Markets are efficient
- Arbitrageurs eliminate mispricings
- Transaction costs may exceed profit
- Execution risk

**Quasi-arbitrage:**
- Near risk-free
- Small residual risks
- Most "arbitrage" strategies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: True arbitrage has NO risk
- Option C: This is speculation, not arbitrage
