# Question

The primary role of a clearinghouse in futures trading is to:

## Options

- **A**: Set futures prices based on supply and demand
- **B**: Guarantee contract performance by becoming counterparty to all trades
- **C**: Provide investment advice to traders

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Clearinghouse functions:

**Primary role:**
- Central counterparty (CCP)
- Guarantees all trades
- Buyer to every seller
- Seller to every buyer

**How it works:**
- Trade executed on exchange
- Clearinghouse steps in
- Original counterparty relationship severed
- Each party faces clearinghouse

**Benefits:**
- Eliminates bilateral credit risk
- Standardizes risk management
- Enables netting
- Increases market confidence

**Other functions:**
- Margin collection and management
- Daily settlement processing
- Default management
- Position monitoring

**Funding:**
- Margin from participants
- Default fund contributions
- Own capital

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Exchange determines prices through trading
- Option C: Clearinghouse doesn't advise traders
