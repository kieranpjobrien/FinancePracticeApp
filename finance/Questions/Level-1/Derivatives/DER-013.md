# Question

A trader goes long one S&P 500 futures contract at 4,500. The contract multiplier is $250. If the futures price settles at 4,480 at the end of day one, the trader's daily settlement is:

## Options

- **A**: Loss of $5,000
- **B**: Gain of $5,000
- **C**: Loss of $20

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Daily settlement calculation:

**Given:**
- Entry price: 4,500
- Settlement price: 4,480
- Multiplier: $250
- Position: Long

**Calculation:**
Daily P&L = (Settlement - Entry) × Multiplier
Daily P&L = (4,480 - 4,500) × $250
Daily P&L = -20 × $250
Daily P&L = -$5,000

**Interpretation:**
- Price fell by 20 points
- Each point = $250
- Long position loses when price falls
- $5,000 debited from margin account

**Mark-to-market effect:**
- Loss realized immediately
- Must maintain margin level
- May trigger margin call

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: This would be gain if price rose to 4,520
- Option C: Forgot to multiply by contract size
