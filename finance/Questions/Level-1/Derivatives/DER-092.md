# Question

In commodity futures, the concept of "deliverable grades" refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The credit quality of futures counterparties
- **B**: Specific quality standards the underlying commodity must meet for delivery
- **C**: The different expiration months available

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deliverable grades in commodity futures:

**Definition:**
- Quality specifications for delivery
- Commodity must meet standards
- Ensures contract fungibility

**Examples:**

*Crude oil futures:*
- Specific gravity range
- Sulfur content limits
- Delivery location (Cushing, OK)

*Wheat futures:*
- Grade (No. 1, No. 2)
- Moisture content
- Foreign material limits

*Gold futures:*
- 995 fineness (purity)
- Approved refiners
- Bar weight specifications

**Why standards matter:**
- Ensures consistent quality
- Enables price comparison
- Facilitates delivery process
- Reduces disputes

**Price adjustments:**
- Premium for higher quality
- Discount for lower quality
- Based on contract specifications

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Grade refers to commodity, not counterparty
- Option C: This describes contract months
