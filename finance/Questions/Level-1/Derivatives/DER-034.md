# Question

An equity index is at 3,000 with a continuous dividend yield of 2% and risk-free rate of 5%. The 3-month futures price is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 2,978
- **B**: 3,022
- **C**: 3,037

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Equity index futures pricing:

**Formula (continuous yields):**
F = S × e^((r - q) × T)

**Given:**
- S = 3,000
- r = 5% = 0.05
- q (dividend yield) = 2% = 0.02
- T = 3 months = 0.25

**Calculation:**
F = 3,000 × e^((0.05 - 0.02) × 0.25)
F = 3,000 × e^(0.03 × 0.25)
F = 3,000 × e^0.0075
F = 3,000 × 1.00753
F = 3,022.58

**Interpretation:**
- Net cost of carry = r - q = 3%
- Futures > Spot because r > q
- If r < q, futures would be below spot

**Cost of carry components:**
- Financing cost: +5%
- Dividend yield: -2%
- Net: +3%

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Used q - r instead of r - q
- Option C: Used r only, ignored dividends
