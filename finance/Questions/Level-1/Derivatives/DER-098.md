# Question

Holding all else equal, an increase in the strike price will cause the value of a put option to:

## Options

- **A**: Decrease
- **B**: Increase
- **C**: Remain unchanged

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Strike price effect on puts:

**Put value increases with strike because:**
- Put = Right to sell at strike
- Higher strike = Sell at higher price
- More valuable right

**Example:**
- Stock at $50
- $45 put: Right to sell at $45 (OTM)
- $55 put: Right to sell at $55 (ITM)
- $55 put more valuable

**Intrinsic value:**
- Put intrinsic = Max(0, K - S)
- Higher K increases intrinsic value
- All else equal

**Time value impact:**
- Higher strike puts also have more time value
- Greater chance of being ITM
- More protection value

**Opposite for calls:**
- Call value decreases with higher strike
- Pay more to exercise = less valuable

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This applies to call options
- Option C: Strike price is key determinant
