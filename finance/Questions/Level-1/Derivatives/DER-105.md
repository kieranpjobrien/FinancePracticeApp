# Question

A conversion arbitrage involves:

## Options

- **A**: Converting bonds into equity
- **B**: Long stock, long put, and short call at the same strike
- **C**: Buying calls and selling puts at different strikes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Conversion arbitrage:

**Conversion components:**
- Long stock (+S)
- Long put (+P)
- Short call (-C)
- Same strike and expiration

**Why it's risk-free:**
- Synthetic short forward created by options
- Offset by long stock
- Locked-in payoff = Strike

**Payoff at expiration:**

*If S_T > K:*
- Stock worth S_T
- Put expires worthless
- Call exercised: Sell at K
- Net: K

*If S_T < K:*
- Stock worth S_T
- Exercise put: Sell at K
- Call expires worthless
- Net: K

**Profit if mispriced:**
- If S + P - C < PV(K)
- Buy conversion (long stock, long put, short call)
- Arbitrage profit

**Reversal (opposite):**
- Short stock, short put, long call
- Same logic, opposite positions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not about convertible bonds
- Option C: Must be same strike for conversion
