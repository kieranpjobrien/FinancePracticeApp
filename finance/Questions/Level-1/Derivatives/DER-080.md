# Question

A covered call strategy involves:

## Options

- **A**: Buying a call option without owning the underlying
- **B**: Owning the underlying stock and selling a call option
- **C**: Buying both the underlying stock and a call option

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Covered call strategy:

**Components:**
- Long stock position
- Short (sell) call option
- "Covered" because stock covers obligation

**Motivation:**
- Generate income from premium
- Willing to sell stock at strike
- Reduce cost basis
- Modest bullish to neutral view

**Payoff profile:**
- Limited upside (capped at strike)
- Downside risk reduced by premium
- Premium income always kept

**Example:**
- Own stock at $50
- Sell $55 call for $2
- If stock stays below $55: Keep stock + $2
- If stock rises to $60: Sell at $55, keep $2

**Maximum profit:**
= Strike - Stock price + Premium

**Risk:**
- Stock could fall significantly
- Premium provides limited cushion

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is a naked (uncovered) call
- Option C: This doubles exposure, not covered
