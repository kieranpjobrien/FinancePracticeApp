# Question

Rho for a long put option is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Positive
- **B**: Negative
- **C**: Zero

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rho for put options:

**Definition:**
- Sensitivity to interest rate changes
- Change in option value per 1% rate change

**Put rho is negative because:**
- Higher rates = Lower PV of strike
- Put holder receives strike if exercised
- Less valuable to receive future payment
- Put value decreases

**Call rho (opposite):**
- Positive
- Higher rates benefit call holders
- Defer payment of strike price

**Intuition:**
- Put = Right to sell at K in future
- Higher rates discount future receipt
- PV(K) decreases
- Put less valuable

**Magnitude:**
- Longer expiration = Higher |rho|
- More time for rates to matter
- Usually small effect on option prices

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Positive rho applies to calls
- Option C: Rho is non-zero for most options
