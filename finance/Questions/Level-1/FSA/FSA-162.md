# Question

The correction of a prior period error in financial statements requires:

## Options

- **A**: Prospective adjustment with disclosure
- **B**: Retrospective restatement of prior period financial statements
- **C**: Recognition in the current period income statement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Errors in prior period financial statements (such as mathematical mistakes, misapplication of accounting policies, or oversight of facts) are corrected by restating comparative prior period figures. This maintains the integrity of trend analysis. The nature of the error and impact on prior periods must be disclosed.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to changes in estimates, not errors
- Option C would distort current period performance
