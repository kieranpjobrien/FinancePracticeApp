# Question

The fraud triangle includes which three elements?

## Options

- **A**: Opportunity, pressure, and rationalization
- **B**: Greed, means, and motive
- **C**: Collusion, concealment, and conversion

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The fraud triangle identifies conditions for fraud: (1) Pressure/incentive—financial distress, bonus targets, analyst expectations; (2) Opportunity—weak controls, complex transactions, management override; (3) Rationalization—"everyone does it," "just borrowing," "company owes me." Presence of all three increases fraud risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses informal terms, not the established framework
- Option C describes fraud elements, not risk factors
