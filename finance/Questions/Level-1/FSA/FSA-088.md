# Question

Level 1 inputs in the fair value hierarchy are:

## Options

- **A**: Unobservable inputs based on company assumptions
- **B**: Quoted prices in active markets for identical assets
- **C**: Observable inputs other than quoted prices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The fair value hierarchy prioritizes inputs: Level 1 = quoted prices in active markets for identical assets (most reliable). Level 2 = observable inputs other than Level 1 (comparable transactions, interest rates). Level 3 = unobservable inputs (company models, assumptions). Higher levels indicate greater measurement reliability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Level 3 inputs
- Option C describes Level 2 inputs
