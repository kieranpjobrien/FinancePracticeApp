# Question

A company's cash taxes paid differs from income tax expense due to:

## Options

- **A**: Accrual accounting adjustments only
- **B**: Changes in deferred taxes and taxes payable
- **C**: Non-deductible expenses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Cash taxes = Tax expense ± Change in deferred taxes ± Change in taxes payable. Increasing DTLs mean cash taxes < expense (future taxes deferred). Changes in taxes payable reflect timing of payments. Analyzing cash taxes helps assess actual tax burden and sustainability of reported tax rates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is vague—deferred taxes are the specific accrual item
- Option C affects expense level, not cash-expense difference
