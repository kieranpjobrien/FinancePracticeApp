# Question

The allowance for doubtful accounts is:

## Options

- **A**: An expense account on the income statement
- **B**: A contra-asset account reducing gross receivables
- **C**: A liability for future credit losses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The allowance for doubtful accounts is a contra-asset that reduces gross accounts receivable to net realizable value. Bad debt expense appears on the income statement; the allowance is on the balance sheet. When specific accounts are written off, both receivables and the allowance decrease.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses the expense with the balance sheet allowance
- Option C misclassifies it as a liability
