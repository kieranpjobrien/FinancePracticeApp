# Question

A company with high net income but negative operating cash flow likely has:

## Options

- **A**: High-quality earnings
- **B**: Aggressive revenue recognition or poor collections
- **C**: Conservative accounting policies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When net income significantly exceeds CFO, accruals are high. This divergence often signals aggressive accounting (premature revenue recognition, delayed expense recognition) or operational issues (poor receivables collection, inventory build-up). High-quality earnings are characterized by CFO exceeding or closely tracking net income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite—this pattern signals low-quality earnings
- Option C would produce CFO exceeding net income
