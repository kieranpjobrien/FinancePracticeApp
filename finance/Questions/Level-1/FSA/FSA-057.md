# Question

A deferred tax asset arises when:

## Options

- **A**: Taxable income exceeds accounting income
- **B**: Taxes payable exceed tax expense in the current period
- **C**: A company has permanently lower tax rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred tax assets (DTA) arise when taxes payable exceed tax expense—meaning the company paid more tax now than the books suggest, creating a future tax benefit. Common sources include tax loss carryforwards, warranty expense recognition, and bad debt allowances recognized for books before tax deduction.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes creation of a deferred tax liability
- Option C describes a permanent difference, not a DTA
