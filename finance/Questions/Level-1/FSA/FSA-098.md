# Question

A company has total assets of $500 million and shareholders' equity of $200 million. Its financial leverage ratio (equity multiplier) is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.4
- **B**: 1.5
- **C**: 2.5

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Financial leverage (equity multiplier) = Total assets / Shareholders' equity = $500M / $200M = 2.5. This means the company has $2.50 of assets for every $1 of equity, with the difference ($1.50) financed by debt. Higher leverage amplifies both returns and risks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is equity/assets (the inverse)
- Option B uses debt/equity relationship incorrectly
