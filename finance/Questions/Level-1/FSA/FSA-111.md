# Question

Interest costs incurred during the construction of a qualifying asset:

## Options

- **A**: Must always be expensed as incurred
- **B**: May be capitalized as part of the asset's cost
- **C**: Are reported as financing activities on the cash flow statement

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest incurred during construction of assets that take substantial time to prepare for use (like buildings, ships, or major equipment) can be capitalized as part of asset cost. This matches the interest cost with the asset's useful life through depreciation. Both IFRS and US GAAP permit capitalization of borrowing costs.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—capitalization is permitted during construction
- Option C describes cash flow classification, not accounting treatment
