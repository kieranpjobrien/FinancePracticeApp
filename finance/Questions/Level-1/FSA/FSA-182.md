# Question

Under IFRS, when a company uses the revaluation model for property, plant, and equipment, increases in fair value are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Recognized in profit or loss
- **B**: Recognized in other comprehensive income (revaluation surplus)
- **C**: Recorded as a reduction of accumulated depreciation only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS allows the revaluation model where PPE is carried at fair value. Increases go to OCI (revaluation surplus in equity), unless reversing a previous decrease recognized in profit or loss. Decreases go to profit or loss unless reversing a prior revaluation surplus. US GAAP doesn't permit revaluation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is only correct for reversals of prior impairments
- Option C is incomplete—revaluation affects carrying value, not just depreciation
