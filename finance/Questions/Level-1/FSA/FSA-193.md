# Question

An asset retirement obligation (ARO) is initially measured at:

## Options

- **A**: The undiscounted expected future cost
- **B**: The present value of expected future costs
- **C**: The historical cost of similar retirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

AROs (legal obligations to dismantle, remove, or restore assets at end of life) are recorded at fair value, typically the present value of expected future costs using a credit-adjusted risk-free rate. The liability increases over time (accretion expense) as the payment date approaches. The corresponding asset is capitalized and depreciated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates the initial liability by ignoring time value
- Option C isn't a valid measurement approach
