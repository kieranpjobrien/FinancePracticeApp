# Question

The Sloan accrual ratio measures:

## Options

- **A**: The proportion of earnings from cash versus accruals
- **B**: The reliability of management earnings guidance
- **C**: The percentage of revenue recognized on credit

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Sloan ratio = (Net income - CFO) / Average total assets, measuring accrual intensity. High accruals relative to cash flows suggest lower earnings quality—more estimates and judgments, greater manipulation potential. Research shows high-accrual firms tend to have lower future returns and earnings persistence.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B measures forecast accuracy, unrelated to accruals
- Option C describes a component of working capital management
