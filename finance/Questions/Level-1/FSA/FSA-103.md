# Question

When a bond is issued at a premium, interest expense over the life of the bond will be:

## Options

- **A**: Equal to the cash coupon payments
- **B**: Less than the cash coupon payments
- **C**: Greater than the cash coupon payments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Premium bonds have coupon rates above market rates. Interest expense = Carrying value × Market rate at issuance. As the premium amortizes, carrying value decreases toward face value. The premium amortization reduces interest expense below the cash coupon payment, with the difference representing return of premium to investors.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores premium amortization
- Option C describes discount bonds
