# Question

Significant accounting estimates should be scrutinized when:

## Options

- **A**: They have been consistent for many years
- **B**: They change frequently or differ significantly from peers
- **C**: They result in lower reported earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Frequent estimate changes or estimates differing from industry norms warrant scrutiny—they may indicate manipulation or genuinely different circumstances. Consistent estimates may still be wrong. Lower earnings alone don't indicate problems. Analysts should understand the basis for estimates and evaluate their reasonableness.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A assumes consistency indicates accuracy—not necessarily true
- Option C assumes lower earnings signal problems—conservative estimates can be appropriate
