# Question

A company with net income significantly exceeding operating cash flow may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: High-quality, sustainable earnings
- **B**: Aggressive accrual accounting practices
- **C**: Conservative revenue recognition

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When net income greatly exceeds operating cash flow, earnings are "accrual heavy"—based on estimates and judgments rather than cash. This can signal aggressive accounting: premature revenue recognition, understated expenses, or insufficient reserves. High-quality earnings should be supported by corresponding cash flows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the opposite—cash-backed earnings are higher quality
- Option C would produce lower, not higher, net income relative to cash
