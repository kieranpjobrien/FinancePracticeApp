# Question

Information is considered material if:

## Options

- **A**: It represents more than 5% of net income
- **B**: Omitting it could influence users' decisions
- **C**: It involves amounts greater than $1 million

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Materiality is determined by whether omission or misstatement could influence economic decisions of users. It's a matter of professional judgment considering both size and nature of the item in context. There are no fixed percentage or dollar thresholds—a $1,000 error might be material for a small company but immaterial for a large one.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A and C apply arbitrary thresholds—materiality is judgmental
