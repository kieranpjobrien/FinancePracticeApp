# Question

Under US GAAP, the recoverability test for long-lived assets involves:

## Options

- **A**: Comparing carrying amount to fair value
- **B**: Comparing carrying amount to undiscounted future cash flows
- **C**: Comparing carrying amount to discounted future cash flows

## Answer

B

## Explanation

US GAAP uses a two-step approach: (1) Recoverability test—compare carrying amount to undiscounted future cash flows. If carrying amount > undiscounted cash flows, impairment exists. (2) Measurement—write down to fair value. IFRS uses a one-step test comparing directly to recoverable amount (higher of value in use or fair value less costs to sell).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes step 2 (measurement), not step 1 (recoverability)
- Option C is more like the IFRS approach
