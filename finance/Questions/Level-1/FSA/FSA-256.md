# Question

A warranty liability creates a temporary difference because:

## Options

- **A**: Warranty expense is never tax deductible
- **B**: Warranty expense is recognized for books when sold but deducted for tax when paid
- **C**: Warranty costs are capitalized for books but expensed for tax

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Companies record warranty expense and liability when products are sold (matching principle), but tax deductions occur when warranty costs are actually paid. This creates a deductible temporary difference and DTA—the company has prepaid its book expense and will receive a future tax deduction.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—warranty costs are tax deductible when paid
- Option C reverses the book/tax treatment
