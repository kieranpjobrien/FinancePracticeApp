# Question

If tax rates are expected to increase, existing deferred tax liabilities will:

## Options

- **A**: Decrease in value
- **B**: Increase in value
- **C**: Remain unchanged under current standards

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred taxes are measured at enacted future rates. If rates increase, DTLs increase (higher future taxes owed) and DTAs increase (higher future tax savings). The adjustment affects income tax expense in the period of the rate change. Higher rates mean larger temporary difference reversals.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses the direction of the effect
- Option C ignores that enacted rate changes require remeasurement
