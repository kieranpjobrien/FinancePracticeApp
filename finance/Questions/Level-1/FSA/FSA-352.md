# Question

The retail inventory method estimates ending inventory by:

## Options

- **A**: Counting every item at fiscal year-end
- **B**: Applying cost-to-retail ratio to ending inventory at retail prices
- **C**: Using replacement cost for all items

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The retail method calculates cost-to-retail ratio from available goods, then applies it to ending inventory at retail prices to estimate cost. It's efficient for retailers with large item counts and frequent turnover. Physical counts verify accuracy but aren't required for each valuation period.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes physical count method
- Option C describes replacement cost, not the retail method
