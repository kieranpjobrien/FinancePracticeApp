# Question

Interest expense is $100,000, beginning interest payable is $15,000, and ending interest payable is $20,000. Cash paid for interest is:

## Options

- **A**: $95,000
- **B**: $100,000
- **C**: $105,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Cash paid = Interest expense + Beginning payable - Ending payable = $100,000 + $15,000 - $20,000 = $95,000. The increase in payable ($5,000) means less cash was paid than expensed—some interest was accrued but not yet paid. Alternatively: Expense + Decrease in payable or - Increase in payable.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B uses expense only, ignoring accrual changes
- Option C reverses the payable adjustment
