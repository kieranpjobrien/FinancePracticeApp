# Question

A company that consistently reports CFO exceeding net income most likely has:

## Options

- **A**: Aggressive revenue recognition
- **B**: High depreciation relative to capital expenditures
- **C**: Increasing accounts receivable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

When CFO > net income, non-cash expenses (depreciation, amortization) exceed non-cash revenues. High depreciation (a non-cash expense added back in CFO) relative to capex suggests mature assets being depreciated without proportional reinvestment. This can signal quality earnings or underinvestment. Aggressive recognition and rising AR reduce CFO.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would create net income > CFO
- Option C would reduce CFO relative to net income
