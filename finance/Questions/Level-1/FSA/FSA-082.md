# Question

A price-to-book ratio below 1.0 may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: The stock is definitely undervalued
- **B**: The market values the company below its accounting net worth
- **C**: The company has no debt

## Answer

B

## Explanation

P/B < 1 means market capitalization is less than book value of equity—the market values the company at less than its accounting net worth. This may indicate undervaluation, but could also reflect poor expected returns, hidden liabilities, or overstated book assets. Further analysis is needed.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is too definitive—low P/B can reflect fundamental problems
- Option C is unrelated to P/B ratio
