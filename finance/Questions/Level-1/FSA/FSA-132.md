# Question

The primary objective of financial reporting is to provide information useful for:

## Options

- **A**: Calculating taxable income
- **B**: Making economic decisions by existing and potential investors and creditors
- **C**: Maximizing reported earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The conceptual framework's primary objective is providing financial information useful to investors, lenders, and other creditors in making decisions about providing resources to the entity. This includes decisions about buying, selling, or holding equity and debt. Tax reporting has different objectives.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the objective of tax accounting
- Option C describes aggressive accounting, not the framework's objective
