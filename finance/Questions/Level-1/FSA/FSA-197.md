# Question

Under IFRS 16, a lessee recognizes for virtually all leases:

## Options

- **A**: Rent expense only, no balance sheet impact
- **B**: A right-of-use asset and a lease liability
- **C**: An owned asset and a loan payable

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS 16 requires lessees to recognize a right-of-use (ROU) asset and corresponding lease liability for nearly all leases (except short-term and low-value). This eliminates the distinction between operating and finance leases for lessees. Expense recognition differs: interest on liability plus ROU depreciation, front-loaded compared to straight-line rent.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A was the old operating lease treatment, no longer permitted
- Option C overstates the nature of lease rights—ROU is not full ownership
