# Question

Taxable income is determined by:

## Options

- **A**: Generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP)
- **B**: Tax laws and regulations
- **C**: Management's discretion

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Taxable income is calculated according to tax code rules, which often differ from GAAP/IFRS used for financial reporting. These differences cause deferred taxes. Examples include different depreciation methods (MACRS vs. straight-line), timing of expense recognition, and non-deductible items like fines.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A determines accounting income, not taxable income
- Option C ignores that tax rules are prescriptive
