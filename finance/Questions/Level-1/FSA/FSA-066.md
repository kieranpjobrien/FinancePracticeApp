# Question

For a finance lease, the lessee's total expense over the lease term consists of:

## Options

- **A**: Rental payments only
- **B**: Depreciation of the ROU asset plus interest on the lease liability
- **C**: Interest expense only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Finance lease expense has two components: (1) depreciation of the right-of-use asset (typically straight-line), and (2) interest expense on the lease liability (front-loaded as liability decreases). Total expense over the lease term equals total cash payments, but the pattern differs from operating lease straight-line expense.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes cash payments, not expense
- Option C ignores depreciation expense
