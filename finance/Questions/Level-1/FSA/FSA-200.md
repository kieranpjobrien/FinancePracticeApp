# Question

A "big bath" accounting strategy typically occurs when:

## Options

- **A**: A company wants to maximize current period earnings
- **B**: New management takes over or during an already poor quarter
- **C**: A company has exceeded analyst expectations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Big bath involves taking excessive write-offs and charges in a period that's already bad (or during management transition), creating reserves that can be released to boost future earnings. New CEOs often "clean house" in their first quarter. This strategy manages earnings trajectory rather than a single period.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes income-increasing manipulation
- Option C would motivate income-decreasing tactics to "save" earnings for future periods
