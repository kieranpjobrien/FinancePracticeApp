# Question

During a period of rising prices, compared to LIFO, FIFO will result in:

## Options

- **A**: Lower ending inventory and higher cost of goods sold
- **B**: Higher ending inventory and lower cost of goods sold
- **C**: Lower ending inventory and lower cost of goods sold

## Answer

B

## Explanation

With rising prices, FIFO (first-in, first-out) assigns older, lower costs to COGS and newer, higher costs to ending inventory. This results in higher inventory values, lower COGS, and higher gross profit compared to LIFO. LIFO does the opposite—matching recent higher costs to revenue.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes LIFO effects
- Option C mixes up the effects incorrectly
