# Question

The fundamental accounting equation states that:

## Options

- **A**: Assets = Liabilities - Equity
- **B**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- **C**: Assets + Liabilities = Equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Assets = Liabilities + Equity is the foundational equation of double-entry bookkeeping. Assets are resources controlled by the entity; liabilities are obligations to others; equity is the residual claim of owners (assets minus liabilities). Every transaction maintains this equality.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A has the wrong sign for equity
- Option C incorrectly adds liabilities to assets
