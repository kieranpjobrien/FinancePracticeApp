# Question

The going concern assumption implies that:

## Options

- **A**: A company will liquidate its assets in the near future
- **B**: A company will continue operating for the foreseeable future
- **C**: A company's assets are valued at their liquidation values

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The going concern assumption presumes the entity will continue operating long enough to realize its assets and satisfy its liabilities in the normal course of business. This justifies historical cost accounting and asset classification as current/non-current. If going concern is doubtful, assets may need revaluation to liquidation values and additional disclosures are required.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes liquidation, the opposite of going concern
- Option C would apply only if going concern is not appropriate
