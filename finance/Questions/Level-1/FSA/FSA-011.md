# Question

Operating income is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Revenue minus all expenses including interest and taxes
- **B**: Gross profit minus operating expenses
- **C**: Net income plus interest expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Operating income (also called operating profit or EBIT) = Gross profit - Operating expenses. It measures profit from core business operations before financing costs and taxes. Operating expenses include SG&A, R&D, and depreciation. Operating income excludes interest expense, interest income, and income taxes, which are non-operating items.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes net income
- Option C works backward from net income but ignores tax effects
