# Question

Under IFRS, provisions are measured at:

## Options

- **A**: The maximum possible outflow
- **B**: The best estimate of expenditure required to settle the obligation
- **C**: The minimum possible outflow

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS requires provisions be measured at management's best estimate of expenditure to settle the obligation at balance sheet date. For a large population of items, this is the expected value. For single obligations, the most likely outcome may be best estimate. Subsequent changes adjust the provision through profit or loss.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates the liability (conservatism doesn't mean worst-case)
- Option C understates the liability inappropriately
