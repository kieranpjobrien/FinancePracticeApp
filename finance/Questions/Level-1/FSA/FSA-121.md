# Question

A company has net income of $100 million and pays dividends of $30 million. Its retention ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 30%
- **B**: 70%
- **C**: 130%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Retention ratio = (Net income - Dividends) / Net income = ($100M - $30M) / $100M = 70%. Alternatively: 1 - Payout ratio = 1 - ($30M / $100M) = 1 - 30% = 70%. The retention ratio shows the portion of earnings reinvested in the business.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the payout ratio, not retention ratio
- Option C adds instead of subtracting
