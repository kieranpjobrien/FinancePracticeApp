# Question

Which of the following is classified as a current liability?

## Options

- **A**: Bonds payable due in 5 years
- **B**: Accounts payable to suppliers
- **C**: Mortgage payable due in 15 years

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Current liabilities are obligations due within one year or the operating cycle. Accounts payable (amounts owed to suppliers for goods/services received) are typically settled within 30-90 days. Long-term debt (bonds, mortgages) due beyond one year is classified as non-current, though the current portion is separated.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is a non-current liability (5-year maturity)
- Option C is a non-current liability (15-year maturity)
