# Question

Under US GAAP, income taxes paid are classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Operating activities
- **B**: Financing activities
- **C**: Allocated among operating, investing, and financing based on source

## Answer

A

## Explanation

US GAAP classifies all income taxes paid as operating activities for simplicity, even though taxes relate to all types of income. IFRS allows classification based on the nature of the underlying transaction but most entities classify as operating. This is a potential comparability issue between standards.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B confuses taxes with debt payments
- Option C is an IFRS option but not standard US GAAP treatment
