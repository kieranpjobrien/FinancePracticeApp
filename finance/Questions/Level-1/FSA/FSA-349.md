# Question

Declining gross profit margins over time may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: Improved operational efficiency
- **B**: Increasing pricing pressure or rising input costs
- **C**: Better inventory management

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Declining gross margins suggest either: selling prices falling faster than cost reductions (competitive pressure, commoditization), or input costs rising faster than price increases. Analyzing margin trends reveals pricing power and cost management effectiveness. Industry comparison provides context for normal fluctuations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would typically improve margins
- Option C would reduce COGS, improving margins
