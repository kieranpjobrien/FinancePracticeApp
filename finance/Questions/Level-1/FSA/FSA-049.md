# Question

Compared to expensing a cost, capitalizing that cost will result in:

## Options

- **A**: Lower assets and higher current-period net income
- **B**: Higher assets and higher current-period net income
- **C**: Higher assets and lower current-period net income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capitalizing records the expenditure as an asset rather than an immediate expense. This increases total assets and defers expense recognition to future periods through depreciation/amortization. Current-period net income is higher because the full cost isn't expensed immediately. Over time, total expense is the same.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes expensing, not capitalizing
- Option C confuses the income effect
