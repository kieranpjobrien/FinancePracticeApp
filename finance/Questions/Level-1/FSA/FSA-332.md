# Question

Under standard costing, production cost variances are:

## Options

- **A**: Always capitalized in inventory
- **B**: Allocated between inventory and COGS if material
- **C**: Always expensed immediately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Standard costing uses predetermined costs for inventory valuation. Variances (differences from actual costs) may be: expensed if immaterial, or allocated between ending inventory and COGS if material. This ensures inventory reflects actual costs for material variances. Most companies expense immaterial variances for simplicity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would overstate inventory with unfavorable variances
- Option C ignores the allocation requirement for material variances
