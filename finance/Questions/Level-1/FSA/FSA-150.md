# Question

A warranty provision is recognized when:

## Options

- **A**: A customer makes a warranty claim
- **B**: A product is sold and future warranty costs are probable and estimable
- **C**: The warranty period expires

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under the matching principle, warranty expense is recognized at the time of sale, with a corresponding liability for estimated future claims. The provision is based on historical claim rates and expected costs. As claims are made, the liability decreases and costs are charged against it.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would understate expense in the sale period
- Option C is when the liability is reversed if unused
