# Question

Prepaid expenses are classified as current assets because they:

## Options

- **A**: Will be converted to cash within one year
- **B**: Represent future economic benefits to be consumed within one year
- **C**: Are owed by customers within one year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Prepaid expenses (insurance, rent, etc.) are current assets because they represent services or benefits that will be consumed, typically within one year or operating cycle. They won't be converted to cash—rather, they'll become expenses as consumed. They differ from receivables, which are amounts owed by others.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A misunderstands—prepaids don't convert to cash
- Option C describes accounts receivable
