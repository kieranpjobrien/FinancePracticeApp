# Question

A company has revenue of $800 million and average total assets of $400 million. Its asset turnover ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.5
- **B**: 2.0
- **C**: 3.2

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset turnover = Revenue / Average total assets = $800 million / $400 million = 2.0. This means the company generates $2 of revenue for every $1 of assets. Higher asset turnover indicates more efficient use of assets. The ratio varies significantly by industry—retailers have high turnover, utilities have low turnover.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A inverts the ratio (assets/revenue)
- Option C results from calculation error
