# Question

Debt that is callable by the lender at any time should be classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Non-current liability
- **B**: Current liability
- **C**: Equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Callable-on-demand debt is classified as current because the lender can require immediate repayment. Even if management doesn't expect the call to be exercised, the legal right makes the liability due within one year. Long-term classification requires ability and intent to refinance on a non-current basis before the balance sheet date.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the immediate payment possibility
- Option C misclassifies debt as equity
