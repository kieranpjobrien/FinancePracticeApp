# Question

Retained earnings increase when a company:

## Options

- **A**: Issues new shares at a premium
- **B**: Reports net income greater than dividends declared
- **C**: Receives cash from customers for future services

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Retained earnings = Beginning retained earnings + Net income - Dividends. When net income exceeds dividends declared, retained earnings increase. Share issuances affect contributed capital, not retained earnings. Customer prepayments create liabilities, not equity increases.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A increases share capital/APIC, not retained earnings
- Option C creates unearned revenue (a liability)
