# Question

Interest costs during construction of a qualifying asset are:

## Options

- **A**: Expensed as incurred
- **B**: Capitalized as part of the asset cost
- **C**: Reported as financing cash outflow

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Both IFRS (IAS 23) and US GAAP require capitalization of borrowing costs directly attributable to acquiring, constructing, or producing qualifying assets (assets requiring substantial time to prepare). Capitalization begins when expenditures occur, borrowing costs are incurred, and construction activities are underway.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the capitalization requirement
- Option C describes cash flow classification, not measurement
