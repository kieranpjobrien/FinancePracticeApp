# Question

Free cash flow to equity (FCFE) can be calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: CFO - CapEx
- **B**: CFO - CapEx + Net borrowing
- **C**: Net income + Depreciation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FCFE = CFO - CapEx + Net borrowing. It represents cash available to equity holders after maintaining/expanding assets and after debt service. Net borrowing adds proceeds from new debt and subtracts repayments. FCFE is used in equity valuation as the cash that could be paid to shareholders.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is closer to FCFF before considering debt changes
- Option C ignores capital expenditures and working capital
