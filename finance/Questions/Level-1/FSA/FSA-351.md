# Question

Inventory growing faster than sales may indicate:

## Options

- **A**: Improved supply chain management
- **B**: Potential obsolescence or demand weakness
- **C**: Increased pricing power

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inventory outpacing sales suggests: products not selling as expected (demand weakness), production exceeding sales (deliberate build-up or poor planning), or obsolescence risk. This ties up cash and may lead to future write-downs. Comparing inventory turnover trends and days inventory confirms the concern.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A would typically show inventory declining relative to sales
- Option C is unrelated to inventory levels
