# Question

A financial statement restatement typically signals:

## Options

- **A**: Improved accounting quality going forward
- **B**: Past misstatement from error or fraud requiring correction
- **C**: A voluntary change in accounting method

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Restatements correct material errors in previously issued statements—from mistakes, misapplication of GAAP, or fraud. They're retrospective, affecting prior period comparatives. Restatements damage credibility, often lead to stock declines, and may trigger regulatory scrutiny. Voluntary method changes typically don't require restatement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may be true, but the immediate signal is past problems
- Option C uses prospective treatment, not restatement
