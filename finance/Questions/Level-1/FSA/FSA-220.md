# Question

Related party transactions are of concern to analysts because they:

## Options

- **A**: Are always conducted at fair market value
- **B**: May not reflect arm's length terms
- **C**: Must be eliminated in consolidated statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Related party transactions (with subsidiaries, affiliates, key management, major shareholders) may not be at market terms, potentially distorting financial performance. Transactions might be at inflated or deflated prices benefiting the related party. Disclosure is required including nature, amounts, and terms. Elimination applies to intercompany transactions in consolidation.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A assumes proper pricing, which is the concern
- Option C applies to intercompany transactions specifically, not all related parties
