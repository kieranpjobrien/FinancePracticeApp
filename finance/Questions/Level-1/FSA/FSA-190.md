# Question

A company has EBIT of $450,000 and interest expense of $90,000. The interest coverage ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 4.0 times
- **B**: 5.0 times
- **C**: 6.0 times

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest coverage ratio = EBIT / Interest expense = $450,000 / $90,000 = 5.0 times. This solvency ratio indicates how many times a company can cover its interest obligations from operating earnings. Higher ratios suggest lower default risk. Creditors and rating agencies closely monitor this metric.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A results from calculation error
- Option C incorrectly uses a different numerator
