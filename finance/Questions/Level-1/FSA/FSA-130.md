# Question

The treasury stock method for stock options assumes:

## Options

- **A**: All options are exercised at the end of the period
- **B**: Proceeds from exercise are used to repurchase shares at average market price
- **C**: Options are only included if in-the-money at year end

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The treasury stock method calculates dilution by assuming options are exercised, then proceeds (exercise price × options) are used to buy back shares at average market price. Net new shares = Options exercised - Shares repurchased. This understates dilution if the stock price rises significantly after exercise.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A doesn't describe the repurchase assumption
- Option C is partially true but doesn't describe the method
