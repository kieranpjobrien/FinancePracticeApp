# Question

The current portion of long-term debt represents:

## Options

- **A**: Interest payments due within one year
- **B**: Principal payments due within one year
- **C**: All debt with original maturity under one year

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The current portion of long-term debt is the principal amount of existing long-term debt due within the next year. It's reclassified from non-current to current liabilities. Interest payable is a separate current liability. Short-term debt (original maturity <1 year) was always current, not reclassified.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes interest payable, a separate liability
- Option C describes short-term borrowings, not reclassified long-term debt
