# Question

A company has revenue of $500,000 and cost of goods sold of $300,000. Its gross profit margin is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 40%
- **B**: 60%
- **C**: 67%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Gross profit = Revenue - COGS = $500,000 - $300,000 = $200,000. Gross profit margin = Gross profit / Revenue = $200,000 / $500,000 = 40%. This measures the percentage of revenue remaining after direct production costs to cover operating expenses and generate profit.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B incorrectly calculates COGS/Revenue (60%)
- Option C incorrectly calculates COGS/Gross profit
