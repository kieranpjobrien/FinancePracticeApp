# Question

A variable interest entity (VIE) must be consolidated by:

## Options

- **A**: The entity with the largest ownership percentage
- **B**: The primary beneficiary that has power and receives benefits/losses
- **C**: All investors proportionally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

VIEs are entities where voting rights don't determine control. The primary beneficiary (party with power to direct significant activities and obligation to absorb losses or right to benefits) consolidates the VIE. This prevents off-balance-sheet structures from hiding risk. Voting percentage may not indicate economic exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses voting control model, not applicable to VIEs
- Option C is proportionate consolidation, not used for VIEs
