# Question

Current service cost in pension accounting represents:

## Options

- **A**: Interest on the projected benefit obligation
- **B**: Present value of benefits earned by employees in the current period
- **C**: Actuarial gains and losses

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Current service cost is the present value of pension benefits employees earned from current-period service. It increases the PBO and is expensed as part of pension cost. Past service cost (from plan amendments) and interest cost (time value increase in PBO) are separate components.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes net interest cost
- Option C describes remeasurements reported in OCI
