# Question

A company's DTL has been growing steadily for years due to accelerated tax depreciation. If the company stops growing:

## Options

- **A**: DTL will continue to grow
- **B**: DTL will begin to reverse as older differences reverse faster than new ones form
- **C**: DTL will remain stable indefinitely

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Growing companies continuously create new depreciation timing differences, perpetually deferring taxes. When growth stops, older assets' differences reverse while fewer new differences form, causing net DTL reduction and higher cash taxes. This is why some argue perpetually growing DTLs resemble permanent tax savings.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A requires continued growth for new differences
- Option C ignores the life cycle of timing differences
