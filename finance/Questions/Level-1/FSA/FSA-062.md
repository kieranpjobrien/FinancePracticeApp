# Question

A bond is issued at a discount when:

## Options

- **A**: The coupon rate exceeds the market rate
- **B**: The coupon rate is less than the market rate
- **C**: The coupon rate equals the market rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A bond sells at a discount when its coupon rate is below the market (yield) rate—investors demand compensation for the below-market interest payments. The discount is the difference between face value and issue price. Over time, the discount is amortized, increasing interest expense above the coupon payment.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a premium bond
- Option C describes a par bond
