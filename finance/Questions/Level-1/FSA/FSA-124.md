# Question

Accrued expenses represent:

## Options

- **A**: Cash paid for future services
- **B**: Expenses incurred but not yet paid
- **C**: Revenue earned but not yet collected

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Accrued expenses (accrued liabilities) are obligations for expenses already incurred but not yet paid—like wages earned by employees, utilities consumed, or interest accrued. They represent the flip side of accrual accounting: expense is recognized when incurred, even if cash payment comes later.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes prepaid expenses
- Option C describes accrued revenue (accounts receivable)
