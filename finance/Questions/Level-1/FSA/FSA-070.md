# Question

High-quality earnings are characterized by:

## Options

- **A**: Maximum reported profits regardless of sustainability
- **B**: Sustainable, repeatable results that reflect economic reality
- **C**: Aggressive revenue recognition and expense deferral

## Answer

B

## Explanation

High-quality earnings are sustainable (likely to recur), reflect economic reality (not accounting manipulation), and are backed by cash flows. Analysts prefer earnings from core operations over one-time gains, and earnings supported by operating cash flow over accrual-heavy results. Earnings quality affects valuation reliability.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A prioritizes magnitude over quality
- Option C describes techniques that reduce earnings quality
