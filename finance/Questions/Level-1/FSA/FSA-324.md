# Question

Interest income earned on investments is typically reported:

## Options

- **A**: As part of operating income for all companies
- **B**: As non-operating income for non-financial companies
- **C**: As a reduction of interest expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

For non-financial companies, interest income on cash and investments is non-operating—not core business activity. For banks and financial institutions, interest income is operating since lending is their primary business. Proper classification helps analysts assess core operating performance separately from investment returns.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores the operating/non-operating distinction for non-financials
- Option C is incorrect—interest income is reported separately
