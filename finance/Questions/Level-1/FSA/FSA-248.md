# Question

The direct method of presenting operating cash flows reports:

## Options

- **A**: Net income adjusted for non-cash items and working capital changes
- **B**: Major classes of gross cash receipts and payments
- **C**: Only cash transactions with customers

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The direct method shows gross operating cash flows: cash from customers, cash paid to suppliers, cash paid to employees, cash paid for interest and taxes. This provides better insight into actual cash movements. The indirect method (Option A) is more common but provides less detail about cash sources and uses.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the indirect method
- Option C is incomplete—includes all operating cash flows, not just customer receipts
