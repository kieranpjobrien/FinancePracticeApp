# Question

Under US GAAP, an uncertain tax position is recognized when:

## Options

- **A**: Any possibility of sustaining the position exists
- **B**: It is more likely than not the position will be sustained
- **C**: The position is virtually certain to be sustained

## Answer

B

## Explanation

US GAAP uses a two-step approach: (1) Recognition—is it more likely than not (>50%) the position will be sustained on audit? If yes, (2) Measurement—recognize the largest amount with >50% likelihood of realization. Positions not meeting the threshold are not recognized. IFRS uses similar principles under IAS 12.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A sets too low a threshold
- Option C sets too high a threshold
