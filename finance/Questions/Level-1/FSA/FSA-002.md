# Question

A company reports net income of $500,000. During the year, depreciation expense was $80,000, accounts receivable increased by $30,000, inventory decreased by $20,000, and accounts payable decreased by $15,000. Using the indirect method, cash flow from operations is:

## Options

- **A**: $555,000
- **B**: $535,000
- **C**: $525,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Starting with net income, adjust for non-cash items and changes in working capital:

Net Income: $500,000
+ Depreciation (non-cash expense): +$80,000
- Increase in A/R (cash not collected): -$30,000
+ Decrease in Inventory (cash preserved): +$20,000
- Decrease in A/P (cash paid out): -$15,000

CFO = $500,000 + $80,000 - $30,000 + $20,000 - $15,000 = $555,000

**Key rules:**
- Add back non-cash expenses (depreciation)
- Increases in current assets = cash outflow (subtract)
- Decreases in current assets = cash inflow (add)
- Increases in current liabilities = cash inflow (add)
- Decreases in current liabilities = cash outflow (subtract)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B incorrectly treats the A/P decrease as an inflow
- Option C incorrectly treats the inventory decrease as an outflow
