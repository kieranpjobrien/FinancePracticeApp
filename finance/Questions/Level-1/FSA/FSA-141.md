# Question

Related party transactions require special attention because they:

## Options

- **A**: Are prohibited under both IFRS and US GAAP
- **B**: May not be conducted at arm's length prices
- **C**: Are always immaterial to financial statements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Related party transactions (with officers, directors, major shareholders, affiliates) may not occur at fair market value, potentially distorting financial results. While not prohibited, they require disclosure of the nature, amounts, and terms. Analysts should assess whether terms differ from independent transactions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—they're allowed but must be disclosed
- Option C is incorrect—related party transactions can be highly material
