# Question

Segment reporting is most useful to analysts because it:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminates the need for consolidated financial statements
- **B**: Provides insight into different business lines' performance and risk
- **C**: Guarantees consistent accounting across all segments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Segment reporting disaggregates consolidated results by operating segment, revealing which businesses drive revenue and profit. This helps analysts understand risk exposure (geographic, industry), growth drivers, and margin differences. Consolidated totals can obscure underperforming or high-risk segments.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—segment data supplements, doesn't replace, consolidated statements
- Option C is incorrect—some accounting differences may exist across segments
