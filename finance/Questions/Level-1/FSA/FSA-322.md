# Question

A subsequent event that provides evidence about conditions existing at the balance sheet date:

## Options

- **A**: Requires adjustment to the financial statements
- **B**: Requires disclosure only
- **C**: Is ignored in the current period statements

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Subsequent events are divided into two types: (1) Adjusting events—evidence of conditions existing at balance sheet date (e.g., customer bankruptcy confirming receivable uncollectibility), requiring statement adjustment. (2) Non-adjusting events—new conditions arising after (e.g., fire, acquisition), requiring disclosure only.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B applies to non-adjusting events
- Option C ignores relevant information about existing conditions
