# Question

When evaluating non-GAAP measures reported by management, analysts should:

## Options

- **A**: Accept them as more accurate than GAAP measures
- **B**: Understand the adjustments and assess their appropriateness
- **C**: Ignore them entirely in favor of GAAP-only analysis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Non-GAAP measures can provide useful insights but may be misleading if adjustments are inappropriate or inconsistent. Analysts should: understand what's adjusted, evaluate if adjustments are reasonable, check for consistency over time, and compare to GAAP measures. Neither blind acceptance nor complete rejection is appropriate.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A assumes non-GAAP is always better—often it's self-serving
- Option C loses potentially valuable information
