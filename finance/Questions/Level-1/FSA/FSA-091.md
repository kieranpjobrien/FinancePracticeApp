# Question

Consolidated financial statements are required when a parent company:

## Options

- **A**: Owns exactly 50% of another company
- **B**: Has control over another company (typically >50% voting rights)
- **C**: Holds any investment in another company's equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Consolidation is required when a parent controls a subsidiary—typically through owning more than 50% of voting shares, but control can exist with less if there are contractual rights or other arrangements. Consolidated statements combine parent and subsidiary as one economic entity, eliminating intercompany transactions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (50%) typically means joint control or significant influence, not consolidation
- Option C is too broad—passive investments don't require consolidation
