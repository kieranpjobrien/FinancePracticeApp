# Question

Operating cash flow is considered more reliable than net income because:

## Options

- **A**: It uses more sophisticated accounting estimates
- **B**: It is less subject to manipulation through accruals and estimates
- **C**: It includes more comprehensive income items

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CFO is actual cash generated, less affected by accounting judgments (depreciation methods, revenue recognition timing, allowances). Net income relies heavily on accruals and estimates that can be manipulated. Sustainable CFO confirms earnings quality. However, CFO can still be managed through timing of payments/collections.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the relationship—net income uses more estimates
- Option C confuses CFO with comprehensive income
