# Question

Normalized earnings are best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Earnings reported under conservative accounting policies
- **B**: Earnings adjusted for non-recurring items and accounting distortions
- **C**: The arithmetic average of past five years' earnings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Normalized earnings remove one-time items (restructuring, litigation, asset sales), adjust for non-operating gains/losses, and correct for unusual accounting choices to reveal sustainable earning power. Analysts normalize to assess core profitability and forecast future performance. Historical averaging alone doesn't address non-recurring items.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes accounting policy choice, not normalization
- Option C is a simplistic approach that doesn't address the nature of items
