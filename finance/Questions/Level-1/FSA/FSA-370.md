# Question

The Altman Z-score is used to:

## Options

- **A**: Value equity securities
- **B**: Predict bankruptcy probability
- **C**: Calculate optimal capital structure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Altman Z-score combines five financial ratios (working capital/assets, retained earnings/assets, EBIT/assets, market equity/liabilities, sales/assets) to predict financial distress. Scores below 1.81 indicate high bankruptcy risk; above 2.99 indicates safety. It's a credit screening tool, not a valuation model.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes DCF or multiple analysis
- Option C describes capital structure optimization
