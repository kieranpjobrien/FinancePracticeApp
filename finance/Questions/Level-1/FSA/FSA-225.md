# Question

A company has total assets of $5,000,000 and total liabilities of $3,200,000. Book value of equity is:

## Options

- **A**: $1,800,000
- **B**: $3,200,000
- **C**: $8,200,000

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Book value of equity = Total assets - Total liabilities = $5,000,000 - $3,200,000 = $1,800,000. This represents the accounting value of shareholders' claims on net assets. Book value may differ significantly from market value due to unrecorded intangibles, historical cost basis, and market expectations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is total liabilities, not equity
- Option C incorrectly adds assets and liabilities
