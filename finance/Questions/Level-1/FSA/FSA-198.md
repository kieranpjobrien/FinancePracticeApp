# Question

Under IFRS, a convertible bond with a fixed conversion ratio is classified as:

## Options

- **A**: Entirely as a liability
- **B**: Part liability, part equity
- **C**: Entirely as equity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

IFRS requires bifurcation of convertible bonds with fixed conversion ratios into liability (present value of future cash flows) and equity (residual) components. The liability is subsequently measured at amortized cost. US GAAP generally treats the entire instrument as debt unless specific conditions for beneficial conversion features are met.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is the typical US GAAP treatment
- Option C ignores the contractual obligation component
