# Question

Under the effective interest method, interest expense on a bond issued at a discount:

## Options

- **A**: Remains constant each period
- **B**: Decreases each period
- **C**: Increases each period

## Answer

C

## Explanation

For discount bonds, interest expense = Carrying value × Market rate at issuance. As the discount amortizes, carrying value increases toward face value, so interest expense increases each period. The difference between interest expense and cash coupon payment is the discount amortization.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes straight-line amortization
- Option B describes premium bonds under effective interest method
