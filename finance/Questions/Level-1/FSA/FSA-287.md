# Question

The distinction between maintenance capital expenditures and growth capital expenditures is important because:

## Options

- **A**: Only growth capex appears on the cash flow statement
- **B**: Maintenance capex is required to sustain current operations while growth capex expands capacity
- **C**: Maintenance capex is expensed while growth capex is capitalized

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Maintenance capex replaces worn assets to maintain current capacity—essentially mandatory. Growth capex expands or upgrades capacity—discretionary. This distinction helps assess sustainable free cash flow (FCF minus maintenance capex) and evaluate management's investment decisions. Both are capitalized and appear in CFI.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect—both appear in investing activities
- Option C is incorrect—both are capitalized
