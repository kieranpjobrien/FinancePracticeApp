# Question

Deferred tax assets and liabilities are offset on the balance sheet when:

## Options

- **A**: Always, to show net deferred tax position
- **B**: There is a legal right to offset and they relate to the same tax authority
- **C**: Never, they must always be shown separately

## Answer

B

## Explanation

DTAs and DTLs can only be offset when the entity has a legally enforceable right to offset current taxes and they relate to income taxes levied by the same authority on the same taxable entity. This prevents misleading presentation while allowing simplification when appropriate. Different jurisdictions' balances cannot be offset.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A overstates netting requirements
- Option C ignores the permissible offsetting criteria
