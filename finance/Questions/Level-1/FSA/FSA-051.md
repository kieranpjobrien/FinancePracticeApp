# Question

Under IFRS, if a company uses the revaluation model for property, plant, and equipment, revaluation gains are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Recognized in profit or loss
- **B**: Recognized in other comprehensive income
- **C**: Recognized as a reduction of liabilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Under IFRS revaluation model, increases in asset value are generally recognized in other comprehensive income (OCI) and accumulated in revaluation surplus within equity. However, a revaluation increase is recognized in profit or loss to the extent it reverses a previous impairment loss. US GAAP does not permit revaluation above historical cost.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies only when reversing prior impairment
- Option C misunderstands the accounting treatment
