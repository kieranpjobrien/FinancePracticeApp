# Question

A contract liability arises when:

## Options

- **A**: A customer fails to pay an invoice
- **B**: A company receives payment before satisfying performance obligations
- **C**: A company owes a supplier for goods received

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A contract liability (similar to unearned/deferred revenue) exists when a company has received payment but hasn't yet satisfied the related performance obligation. The company owes the customer goods or services. As performance occurs, the contract liability decreases and revenue is recognized.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes an allowance for doubtful accounts situation
- Option C describes accounts payable to suppliers
