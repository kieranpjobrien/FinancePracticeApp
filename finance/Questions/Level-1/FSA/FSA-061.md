# Question

If enacted tax rates increase, the effect on existing deferred tax liabilities is:

## Options

- **A**: DTL decreases, increasing net income
- **B**: DTL increases, decreasing net income
- **C**: No effect until the temporary differences reverse

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Deferred taxes are measured at enacted rates expected when differences reverse. If rates increase, DTLs grow because future tax payments will be larger. The adjustment increases deferred tax expense, reducing current net income. The opposite occurs for DTAs (they increase in value). Rate changes are recognized immediately.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the effect
- Option C ignores that rate changes are recognized when enacted
