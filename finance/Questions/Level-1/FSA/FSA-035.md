# Question

A company has total debt of $200 million and total equity of $400 million. Its debt-to-equity ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.33
- **B**: 0.50
- **C**: 2.00

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt-to-equity ratio = Total debt / Total equity = $200 million / $400 million = 0.50. This means the company has $0.50 of debt for every $1 of equity. The D/E ratio measures financial leverage—higher ratios indicate greater reliance on debt financing and higher financial risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A may result from dividing debt by total capital
- Option C inverts the ratio (equity/debt)
