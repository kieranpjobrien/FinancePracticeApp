# Question

A change in accounting policy generally requires:

## Options

- **A**: Prospective application only
- **B**: Retrospective application with restatement of prior periods
- **C**: Immediate recognition of cumulative effect in current income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Changes in accounting policy (like switching from LIFO to FIFO) are applied retrospectively—prior period financials are restated as if the new policy had always been used. This enhances comparability across periods. Changes in estimates are applied prospectively. Error corrections are also retrospective.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A applies to changes in estimates
- Option C was the old US GAAP treatment, now replaced
