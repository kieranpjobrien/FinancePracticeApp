# Question

In a period of rising prices, switching from FIFO to LIFO would result in:

## Options

- **A**: Higher ending inventory and higher COGS
- **B**: Lower ending inventory and higher COGS
- **C**: Lower ending inventory and lower COGS

## Answer

B

## Explanation

LIFO assigns recent (higher) costs to COGS and older (lower) costs to ending inventory. In rising prices: LIFO COGS > FIFO COGS (reducing income) and LIFO inventory < FIFO inventory. This provides tax benefits but understates inventory on the balance sheet. Note: LIFO is prohibited under IFRS.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses LIFO and FIFO effects
- Option C describes falling price environments
