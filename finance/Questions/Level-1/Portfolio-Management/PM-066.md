# Question

An investor maintains the same asset allocation for 15 years despite significant changes in age, income, and market conditions. This behavior most likely reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Status quo bias
- **B**: Conservatism bias
- **C**: Illusion of control

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Status quo bias:

**Definition:**
- Preference for current state of affairs
- Emotional preference for maintaining existing positions
- Inertia in decision-making

**Causes:**
- Regret avoidance (if changes lead to losses)
- Cognitive effort required to make changes
- Endowment effect (overvaluing what you own)
- Loss aversion related to giving up current position

**Consequences:**
- Failure to adapt to changing circumstances
- Inappropriate asset allocation over time
- Missing rebalancing opportunities

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Conservatism): Slow to update beliefs given new information, but still may act
- Option C (Illusion of control): Believing one can influence random outcomes
