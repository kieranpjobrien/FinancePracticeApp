# Question

A separately managed account (SMA) differs from a mutual fund primarily in that:

## Options

- **A**: SMAs cannot hold stocks or bonds
- **B**: The investor directly owns the underlying securities
- **C**: SMAs have lower minimum investments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Separately managed accounts:

**Key difference:**
- Direct ownership of securities
- Not commingled with others
- Individual account structure

**Advantages:**
- Tax-loss harvesting flexibility
- Customization possible
- Direct control
- No embedded gains from others

**Mutual fund structure:**
- Pooled investment vehicle
- Own shares of fund
- Indirect ownership
- Subject to others' redemptions

**SMA characteristics:**
- Higher minimums (typically $100K+)
- More transparency
- Personalized portfolios
- Direct relationship with manager

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: SMAs invest in traditional securities
- Option C: SMAs typically have HIGHER minimums
