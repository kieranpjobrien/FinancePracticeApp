# Question

Cognitive biases differ from emotional biases in that cognitive biases:

## Options

- **A**: Are more difficult to correct through education
- **B**: Stem from faulty reasoning or information processing
- **C**: Are based on feelings rather than analysis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Behavioral biases are classified into two categories:

**Cognitive Biases:**
- Stem from faulty reasoning, memory errors, or information processing mistakes
- Examples: anchoring, confirmation bias, hindsight bias, mental accounting
- Easier to correct through education and awareness
- Based on how we think and process information

**Emotional Biases:**
- Stem from feelings, impulses, or intuition
- Examples: loss aversion, overconfidence, regret aversion, status quo bias
- More difficult to correct as they are deeply rooted in psychology
- Based on how we feel

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Cognitive biases are actually easier to correct; emotional biases are harder to change
- Option C: This describes emotional biases, not cognitive biases
