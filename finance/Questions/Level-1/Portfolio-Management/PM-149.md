# Question

A stop-loss order is a risk management tool that:

## Options

- **A**: Guarantees a maximum loss on a position
- **B**: Triggers a sale when price falls to a specified level
- **C**: Prevents an investor from ever losing money

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Stop-loss orders:

**Definition:**
- Order to sell when price drops to specified level
- Converts to market order when triggered
- Automates loss-limiting discipline

**Example:**
- Buy stock at $100
- Set stop-loss at $90
- If price hits $90, sell order triggered

**Limitations:**
- No guarantee of execution price
- Gaps can result in worse prices
- May be triggered by temporary volatility
- Locks in loss (prevents recovery participation)

**Variations:**
- Stop-limit orders
- Trailing stops
- Mental stops vs. actual orders

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Execution price not guaranteed (gaps, illiquidity)
- Option C: Stop-loss limits but doesn't prevent losses
