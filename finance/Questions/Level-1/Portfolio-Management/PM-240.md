# Question

Volatility clustering in financial markets means that:

## Options

- **A**: Volatility is constant over time
- **B**: Periods of high volatility tend to be followed by high volatility
- **C**: All assets have the same volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Volatility clustering:

**Definition:**
- High volatility periods followed by high volatility
- Low volatility periods followed by low volatility
- Autocorrelation in volatility

**Implications:**
- Volatility is predictable to some extent
- Historical volatility informs future volatility
- Time-varying risk models needed

**GARCH models:**
- Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity
- Captures volatility clustering
- Used in VaR, option pricing

**Investment implications:**
- Risk management should be dynamic
- VaR may need adjustment
- Position sizing based on current volatility
- Volatility forecasting valuable

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Volatility is clearly time-varying
- Option C: Different assets have different volatilities
