# Question

Style drift occurs when a portfolio manager:

## Options

- **A**: Maintains consistent investment style over time
- **B**: Deviates from their stated investment style or mandate
- **C**: Follows a passive indexing strategy

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Style drift:

**Definition:**
- Manager moves away from stated strategy
- Holdings no longer match mandate
- Unintentional or intentional deviation

**Examples:**
- Value manager buying growth stocks
- Small-cap fund holding large-caps
- Domestic fund adding international

**Consequences:**
- Unintended risk exposures
- Distorted asset allocation
- Performance attribution difficulty
- Potential benchmark mismatch

**Monitoring:**
- Regular holdings analysis
- Style analysis (returns-based)
- Peer group comparison

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Consistency is the opposite of drift
- Option C: Passive strategies minimize drift
