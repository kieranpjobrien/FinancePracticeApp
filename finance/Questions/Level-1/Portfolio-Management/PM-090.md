# Question

An investor attributes successful investments to their own skill but blames unsuccessful investments on bad luck or external factors. This is an example of:

## Options

- **A**: Self-attribution bias
- **B**: Outcome bias
- **C**: Hindsight bias

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Self-attribution bias:

**Definition:**
- Taking credit for successes (skill)
- Blaming failures on external factors (luck, market conditions)
- Asymmetric attribution of outcomes

**Two components:**
1. Self-enhancing: Claiming credit for positive outcomes
2. Self-protecting: Denying responsibility for negative outcomes

**Consequences:**
- Overconfidence in investment abilities
- Failure to learn from mistakes
- Repeated poor decision-making
- Excessive trading

**Related to overconfidence:**
- Self-attribution feeds overconfidence over time
- Success reinforces false belief in skill

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Outcome bias): Judging decision quality solely by outcome
- Option C (Hindsight bias): Believing past events were predictable
