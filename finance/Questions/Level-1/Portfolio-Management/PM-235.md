# Question

A primary way to mitigate behavioral biases in investment decisions is to:

## Options

- **A**: Make decisions quickly without analysis
- **B**: Follow a systematic, rules-based investment process
- **C**: Rely entirely on intuition and gut feelings

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debiasing strategies:

**Systematic process:**
- Written investment policy
- Pre-commitment to rules
- Checklists for decisions
- Removes emotional decision-making

**Other mitigation techniques:**
- Awareness and education
- Devil's advocate analysis
- Seeking disconfirming evidence
- Delayed decision-making
- Multiple perspectives

**Benefits of rules-based approach:**
- Reduces emotional reactions
- Enforces discipline
- Consistent execution
- Easier to evaluate and improve

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Quick decisions increase bias influence
- Option C: Intuition reflects biases
