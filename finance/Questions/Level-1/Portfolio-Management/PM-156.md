# Question

The Fama-French three-factor model extends CAPM by adding:

## Options

- **A**: Momentum and volatility factors
- **B**: Size (SMB) and value (HML) factors
- **C**: Interest rate and credit spread factors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fama-French three-factor model:

**Factors:**
1. Market factor (same as CAPM)
2. SMB (Small Minus Big): Size premium
3. HML (High Minus Low): Value premium

**Model:**
E(Ri) = Rf + βi,mkt(Rm-Rf) + βi,SMB(SMB) + βi,HML(HML)

**Rationale:**
- Small stocks historically outperform large
- Value stocks (high B/M) outperform growth
- CAPM alone doesn't explain these patterns

**Extensions:**
- Four-factor (adds momentum)
- Five-factor (adds profitability, investment)

**Implications:**
- Alpha in CAPM may be factor exposure
- More complete risk attribution

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Momentum is added in four-factor model
- Option C: These are fixed income factors, not Fama-French
