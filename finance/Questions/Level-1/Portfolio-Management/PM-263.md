# Question

A company's equity beta will typically increase when the company:

## Options

- **A**: Pays down debt and reduces leverage
- **B**: Takes on more debt and increases leverage
- **C**: Issues more equity shares

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Leverage and beta:

**Relationship:**
- More leverage → higher equity beta
- Debt amplifies equity risk
- Fixed obligations increase volatility

**Formula:**
βEquity = βAsset × [1 + (1-t) × D/E]

Where:
- βAsset = unlevered (asset) beta
- t = tax rate
- D/E = debt-to-equity ratio

**Intuition:**
- Debt holders have priority claim
- Equity absorbs all volatility
- Fixed costs create operating leverage effect

**Implications:**
- Compare betas at same leverage
- Unlever before comparing companies
- Relever for valuation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Reducing leverage lowers beta
- Option C: More equity dilutes but doesn't raise beta
