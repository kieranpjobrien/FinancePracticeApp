# Question

An investor avoids making investment decisions independently and instead follows the crowd, reasoning that "if everyone else is doing it, at least I won't be the only one who's wrong." This reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Regret aversion combined with herding behavior
- **B**: Overconfidence combined with self-attribution
- **C**: Anchoring combined with confirmation bias

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Regret aversion and herding:

**Regret aversion:**
- Avoiding actions that might cause regret
- Fear of being singled out for bad decisions
- Preference for errors of omission over commission
- "Safety in numbers" mentality

**Herding behavior:**
- Following the crowd despite individual analysis
- Ignoring private information in favor of public sentiment
- Can create or amplify market bubbles and crashes

**Combined effect:**
- Investor follows others to avoid personal regret
- If wrong, "everyone was wrong" provides psychological cover
- Leads to suboptimal individual decisions

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Overconfidence leads to independent (often excessive) action
- Option C: These biases affect information processing, not crowd-following
