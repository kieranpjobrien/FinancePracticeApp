# Question

For an n-asset equally-weighted portfolio, as n increases:

## Options

- **A**: Portfolio variance approaches zero
- **B**: Portfolio variance approaches the average covariance
- **C**: Portfolio variance remains constant

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Large portfolio variance:

**Formula for equal weights:**
σ²p = (1/n)σ̄² + [(n-1)/n]Cov̄

Where:
- σ̄² = average variance
- Cov̄ = average covariance

**As n → ∞:**
- First term → 0
- Second term → Cov̄
- σ²p → Average covariance

**Interpretation:**
- Diversification eliminates unique risk
- Covariance (systematic) risk remains
- Cannot diversify below market risk

**Implications:**
- ~20-30 stocks capture most benefit
- Marginal benefit decreases
- Correlation determines floor

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Variance approaches covariance, not zero
- Option C: Variance decreases toward limit
