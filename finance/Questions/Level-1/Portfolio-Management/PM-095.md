# Question

An investor uses 2:1 leverage to purchase a portfolio. If the unlevered portfolio has an expected return of 10% and volatility of 15%, the levered portfolio's volatility is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 15%
- **B**: 20%
- **C**: 30%

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Leverage and risk:

**Leverage ratio 2:1:**
- $1 of equity controls $2 of assets
- Gains and losses are doubled

**Effect on return and risk:**
- Levered return = Leverage × Unlevered return - Borrowing costs
- Levered volatility = Leverage × Unlevered volatility

**Calculation:**
Levered volatility = 2 × 15% = 30%

**Key insight:**
- Leverage amplifies both returns AND risks proportionally
- A 10% loss becomes 20% loss with 2:1 leverage
- Leverage adds borrowing costs, reducing expected return

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignores the leverage effect on risk
- Option B: May assume leverage only partially affects risk
