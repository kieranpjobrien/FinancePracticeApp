# Question

Risk appetite differs from risk capacity in that risk appetite:

## Options

- **A**: Is an objective financial measure
- **B**: Represents the amount of risk an organization chooses to accept in pursuit of objectives
- **C**: Is always greater than risk capacity

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk appetite vs. capacity:

**Risk capacity:**
- Maximum risk organization can absorb
- Based on financial resources
- Objective measure
- "How much risk CAN we take?"

**Risk appetite:**
- Amount of risk willing to accept
- Based on strategic objectives
- Deliberate choice
- "How much risk DO WE WANT to take?"

**Relationship:**
- Appetite should be ≤ capacity
- Appetite is strategic decision
- Capacity is constraint

**Governance:**
- Board approves risk appetite
- Management operates within appetite
- Regular review and adjustment

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Appetite is subjective/strategic; capacity is objective
- Option C: Appetite typically less than capacity
