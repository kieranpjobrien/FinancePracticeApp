# Question

The dispersion of portfolio returns is typically measured by:

## Options

- **A**: Expected return
- **B**: Standard deviation or variance
- **C**: Beta coefficient

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Measures of dispersion:

**Standard deviation:**
- Square root of variance
- Same units as returns
- Most common risk measure

**Variance:**
- Average squared deviation from mean
- Squared units
- Used in portfolio calculations

**Range:**
- Maximum - minimum
- Simple but limited

**What dispersion captures:**
- Variability around expected return
- Uncertainty of outcomes
- "Width" of distribution

**What it doesn't capture:**
- Systematic vs. unsystematic risk
- Downside vs. upside volatility
- Tail risk (unless used with other measures)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Expected return is central tendency, not dispersion
- Option C: Beta measures systematic risk relative to market
