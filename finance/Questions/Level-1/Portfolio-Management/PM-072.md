# Question

The historical simulation method for calculating VaR:

## Options

- **A**: Assumes returns are normally distributed
- **B**: Uses actual historical returns to estimate the distribution
- **C**: Requires estimation of volatility and correlation parameters

## Answer

B

## Explanation

VaR calculation methods:

**Historical simulation:**
- Uses actual historical returns
- No distribution assumptions
- Ranks historical returns and finds percentile
- Advantage: Captures fat tails and non-normality
- Disadvantage: Limited by historical data; past may not predict future

**Parametric (variance-covariance):**
- Assumes normal distribution
- Requires volatility and correlation estimates
- Computationally simple
- May underestimate tail risk

**Monte Carlo simulation:**
- Generates random scenarios from assumed distribution
- Flexible for complex portfolios
- Computationally intensive

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Historical simulation makes no distributional assumptions; parametric method assumes normality
- Option C: Parameter estimation is for parametric method, not historical simulation
