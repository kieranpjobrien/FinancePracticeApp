# Question

Hedging is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminating all portfolio risk to achieve risk-free returns
- **B**: Taking a position to offset potential losses from another position
- **C**: Increasing portfolio exposure to capture higher returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Hedging:

**Definition:**
- Taking offsetting positions to reduce risk
- Trading off some upside potential to protect against downside
- Risk transfer, not risk elimination

**Common hedging instruments:**
- Futures and forwards
- Options (puts for downside protection)
- Swaps
- Short selling

**Examples:**
- Buying put options on stock holdings
- Currency forwards for international investments
- Interest rate swaps for bond portfolios

**Key points:**
- Hedging has costs (premiums, spreads, opportunity cost)
- Perfect hedges are rare
- Reduces both upside and downside typically

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Hedging reduces specific risks but doesn't eliminate all risk
- Option C: This describes leveraging or speculating, not hedging
