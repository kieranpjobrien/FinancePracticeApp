# Question

Fundamental indexing differs from market-cap weighted indexing by:

## Options

- **A**: Using market capitalization to determine weights
- **B**: Weighting stocks by fundamental factors like sales, earnings, or book value
- **C**: Only including growth stocks in the index

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fundamental indexing:

**Market-cap weighted:**
- Weight by market value
- Larger companies = larger weights
- Implicitly overweights overvalued stocks

**Fundamental weighted:**
- Weight by fundamental metrics
- Sales, earnings, book value, dividends, employees
- Breaks link between price and weight

**Advantages claimed:**
- Avoids overweighting overvalued stocks
- Systematic value tilt
- May outperform cap-weighted

**Criticisms:**
- Essentially a value strategy
- Higher turnover and costs
- May underperform in growth markets

**Examples:**
- RAFI (Research Affiliates)
- Various "smart beta" strategies

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes market-cap weighting
- Option C: Fundamental indexing isn't limited by style
