# Question

A portfolio has returns of +20% in Year 1 and -20% in Year 2. The geometric mean return is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: -2.0%
- **B**: 0.0%
- **C**: +2.0%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Geometric mean return:

**Formula:**
Geometric return = [(1 + R₁)(1 + R₂)...(1 + Rn)]^(1/n) - 1

**Calculation:**
= [(1.20)(0.80)]^(1/2) - 1
= [0.96]^0.5 - 1
= 0.9798 - 1
= -0.0202 = -2.0%

**Verification:**
- $100 × 1.20 = $120 after Year 1
- $120 × 0.80 = $96 after Year 2
- Net loss of $4 or 4% over 2 years
- Annualized: approximately -2% per year

**Arithmetic mean:** (20% + (-20%)) / 2 = 0%

**Key insight:** Geometric mean is always ≤ arithmetic mean; they're equal only when all returns are identical.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: This is the arithmetic mean, not geometric
- Option C: Incorrectly assumes symmetry
