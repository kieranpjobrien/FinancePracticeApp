# Question

The legal constraint in an IPS most commonly addresses:

## Options

- **A**: The investor's personal investment preferences
- **B**: Regulatory requirements and fiduciary standards
- **C**: Expected inflation and real return targets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Legal and regulatory constraints:

**Common legal constraints:**
- ERISA requirements for pension plans
- Prudent investor rule/prudent person standard
- Investment restrictions in trust documents
- Insurance company investment regulations
- Foundation and endowment spending requirements
- Securities regulations (insider trading, reporting)

**Fiduciary standards:**
- Duty of loyalty (act in beneficiary's interest)
- Duty of care (prudent decision-making)
- Diversification requirements
- Documentation and monitoring obligations

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Personal preferences are unique circumstances, not legal constraints
- Option C: Inflation and return targets are part of objectives, not legal constraints
