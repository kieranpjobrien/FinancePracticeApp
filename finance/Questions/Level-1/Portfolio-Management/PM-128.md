# Question

Judging an investment decision solely by its outcome rather than the quality of the decision process is an example of:

## Options

- **A**: Hindsight bias
- **B**: Outcome bias
- **C**: Self-attribution bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Outcome bias:

**Definition:**
- Evaluating decisions based on results, not process
- Good outcome = good decision (false logic)
- Bad outcome = bad decision (false logic)

**Problem:**
- Good decisions can have bad outcomes (bad luck)
- Bad decisions can have good outcomes (good luck)
- Randomness plays a role in investment outcomes

**Examples:**
- Criticizing a diversified investor who underperformed concentrated portfolio
- Praising a reckless bet that happened to pay off

**Proper evaluation:**
- Assess decision-making process
- Consider information available at decision time
- Evaluate risk management and reasoning

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Hindsight): Believing past was predictable; related but different
- Option C (Self-attribution): Taking credit for successes, blaming failures on others
