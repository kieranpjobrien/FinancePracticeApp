# Question

Availability bias causes investors to overweight:

## Options

- **A**: Events that are statistically most likely
- **B**: Events that are easily recalled or recently experienced
- **C**: Events recommended by financial advisors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Availability bias:

**Definition:**
- Cognitive bias
- Easily recalled = perceived as likely
- Recent or vivid events overweighted
- Probability estimation error

**Causes of easy recall:**
- Recency (just happened)
- Vividness (dramatic events)
- Personal experience
- Media coverage

**Investment impact:**
- Overweight recent market events
- Fear after crashes
- Euphoria after rallies
- Chase recent winners

**Examples:**
- Avoid stocks after 2008 crash
- Buy tech after 1990s bubble
- Fear airline stocks after crash

**Mitigation:**
- Use base rate statistics
- Long-term historical data
- Systematic decision processes

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Availability distorts probability assessment
- Option C: Not related to advisor recommendations
