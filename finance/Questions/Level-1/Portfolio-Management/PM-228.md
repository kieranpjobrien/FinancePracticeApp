# Question

Roll's critique of CAPM testing argues that:

## Options

- **A**: CAPM has been definitively proven true
- **B**: The true market portfolio is unobservable, making CAPM untestable
- **C**: Beta is always exactly 1.0 for all securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Roll's critique:

**Key argument:**
- True market portfolio includes all risky assets
- Stocks, bonds, real estate, human capital, etc.
- This portfolio is unobservable in practice

**Implications:**
- Using proxies (S&P 500) may not represent true market
- Results depend on proxy chosen
- Cannot truly test CAPM
- Joint hypothesis problem

**Practical considerations:**
- Tests of CAPM are really tests of proxy efficiency
- Different proxies give different results
- Model may be "true" but untestable

**Response:**
- Use multiple market proxies
- Acknowledge limitations
- Consider alternative models (APT)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Roll argues CAPM cannot be definitively tested
- Option C: Beta varies by security relative to market
