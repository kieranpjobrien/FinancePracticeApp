# Question

In liability-driven investing (LDI), the primary focus is on:

## Options

- **A**: Maximizing absolute returns regardless of liabilities
- **B**: Managing the surplus between assets and liabilities
- **C**: Minimizing portfolio volatility

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liability-driven investing:

**Objective:**
- Fund future liabilities
- Manage surplus risk
- Ensure obligations are met

**Surplus defined:**
Surplus = Assets - Liabilities

**Key considerations:**
- Liability characteristics (duration, inflation sensitivity)
- Asset-liability matching
- Interest rate hedging
- Funded status volatility

**Common applications:**
- Defined benefit pensions
- Insurance companies
- Endowments with spending rules

**Strategies:**
- Duration matching
- Cash flow matching
- Immunization
- Glide paths

**Risk focus:**
- Surplus volatility, not asset volatility
- Liability hedging
- Tracking error vs. liabilities

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Must consider liabilities
- Option C: Volatility relative to liabilities matters most
