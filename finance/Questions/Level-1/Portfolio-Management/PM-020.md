# Question

An investor has a stable high income, no debt, a 30-year time horizon, and substantial emergency savings. However, she states she "cannot sleep at night" if her portfolio declines more than 10%. Her risk tolerance is best described as:

## Options

- **A**: High ability and high willingness to take risk
- **B**: High ability but low willingness to take risk
- **C**: Low ability and low willingness to take risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk tolerance has two components:

**Ability to take risk** (objective factors):
- Stable high income: High ability
- No debt: High ability
- 30-year time horizon: High ability
- Substantial emergency savings: High ability
→ Overall high ability

**Willingness to take risk** (subjective/psychological):
- Cannot tolerate 10%+ declines: Low willingness
- Emotional response to volatility
→ Low willingness

When ability and willingness conflict, the more conservative assessment (willingness) typically governs. However, education may help align them over time.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignores the expressed psychological discomfort with losses
- Option C: The objective factors clearly indicate high ability to take risk
