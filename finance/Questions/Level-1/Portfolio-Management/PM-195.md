# Question

Closet indexing refers to actively managed funds that:

## Options

- **A**: Take concentrated bets that differ significantly from the benchmark
- **B**: Charge active management fees while closely tracking a benchmark
- **C**: Invest only in index funds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Closet indexing:

**Definition:**
- Fund claims active management
- Actually tracks benchmark closely
- Charges higher active fees for near-index returns

**How to detect:**
- Low active share (< 60%)
- High R-squared with benchmark
- Low tracking error
- Holdings similar to index

**Active share:**
- Measures % of portfolio different from benchmark
- 0% = identical to benchmark
- 100% = no overlap

**Problems:**
- Investors pay high fees for index-like returns
- Unlikely to outperform after fees
- Misrepresentation of strategy

**Solution:**
- Examine active share
- Compare to low-cost index
- Evaluate fee vs. differentiation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes truly active management
- Option C: This is index fund investing, not closet indexing
