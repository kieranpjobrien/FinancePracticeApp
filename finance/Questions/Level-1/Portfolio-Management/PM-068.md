# Question

An investor is more willing to take risk when told an investment has "70% chance of success" than when told it has "30% chance of failure." This illustrates:

## Options

- **A**: Availability bias
- **B**: Framing bias
- **C**: Conservatism bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Framing bias:

**Definition:**
- Decisions influenced by how information is presented
- Same information, different presentation = different decision
- Violates rational economic theory (information should matter, not presentation)

**In this example:**
- "70% success" and "30% failure" are mathematically identical
- Positive frame (success) leads to more risk-taking
- Negative frame (failure) leads to more risk aversion

**Forms of framing:**
- Gain/loss framing
- Narrow vs. broad framing
- Temporal framing (short vs. long term)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Availability): Based on ease of recall, not presentation
- Option C (Conservatism): Slow to update beliefs with new information
