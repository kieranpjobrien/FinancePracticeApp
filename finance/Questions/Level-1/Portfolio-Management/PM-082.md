# Question

Life insurance companies typically have investment portfolios that emphasize:

## Options

- **A**: Short-term, highly liquid securities due to unpredictable claims
- **B**: Long-duration fixed-income securities to match long-term liabilities
- **C**: Aggressive equity investments to maximize returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Life insurance company characteristics:

**Liabilities:**
- Long-term and relatively predictable (actuarial tables)
- Death benefits, annuity payments
- Duration can be 10-30+ years

**Investment approach:**
- Asset-liability matching
- Long-duration bonds to match liability duration
- Emphasis on fixed income (typically 70-80% of portfolio)
- Some equities and alternatives for return enhancement
- Regulatory constraints on investment types

**Key contrast with P&C insurers:**
- P&C has shorter, less predictable liabilities
- P&C needs more liquidity

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes property & casualty insurers better than life insurers
- Option C: Insurance regulations limit equity exposure; liability matching is priority
