# Question

Which of the following correlation coefficients between two assets would provide the greatest diversification benefit in a portfolio?

## Options

- **A**: +0.8
- **B**: +0.2
- **C**: -0.3

## Answer

C

## Explanation

Diversification benefit is maximized when the correlation between assets is as low as possible. A correlation of -0.3 provides the greatest diversification benefit among the options because:

- Negative correlation means the assets tend to move in opposite directions
- When one asset declines, the other tends to rise, reducing overall portfolio volatility
- The lower the correlation, the greater the reduction in portfolio risk relative to the weighted average of individual asset risks

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (+0.8): High positive correlation provides minimal diversification benefit as assets move together
- Option B (+0.2): While better than +0.8, positive correlation still means assets generally move in the same direction
