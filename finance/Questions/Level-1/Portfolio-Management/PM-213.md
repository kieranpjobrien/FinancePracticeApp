# Question

Qualitative factors in manager due diligence include:

## Options

- **A**: Only the manager's historical returns
- **B**: Investment philosophy, process, organization, and personnel
- **C**: Sharpe ratio and information ratio only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Manager due diligence:

**Qualitative factors:**
- Investment philosophy and process
- Team experience and stability
- Organizational structure
- Compliance and controls
- Research capabilities
- Decision-making process

**Quantitative factors:**
- Historical returns
- Risk measures (volatility, drawdown)
- Risk-adjusted ratios (Sharpe, Information)
- Style analysis
- Holdings analysis

**"Four Ps" framework:**
1. Philosophy: Investment beliefs
2. Process: How decisions are made
3. People: Team quality and stability
4. Performance: Historical track record

**Balance:**
- Both qualitative and quantitative important
- Past performance alone insufficient

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Returns are quantitative, not qualitative
- Option C: These are quantitative measures
