# Question

An investor holds both taxable and tax-deferred accounts. To maximize after-tax returns, high-yield bonds should generally be held in:

## Options

- **A**: Taxable accounts to benefit from lower tax rates
- **B**: Tax-deferred accounts to shelter ordinary income
- **C**: Either account, as taxes don't affect bond returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Asset location strategy:

**Tax-deferred accounts (IRA, 401(k))** - Best for:
- High-yield bonds (taxed as ordinary income)
- REITs (distributions taxed as ordinary income)
- Actively traded strategies (frequent capital gains)
- Taxable bonds

**Taxable accounts** - Best for:
- Municipal bonds (tax-exempt interest)
- Stocks held long-term (preferential capital gains rates)
- Tax-efficient index funds
- Assets with losses (for tax-loss harvesting)

High-yield bond interest is taxed at ordinary income rates, so sheltering in tax-deferred accounts preserves more after-tax return.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: High-yield bond interest gets no preferential tax treatment
- Option C: Tax treatment significantly impacts after-tax returns
