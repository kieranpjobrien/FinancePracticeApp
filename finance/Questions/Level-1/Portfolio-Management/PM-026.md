# Question

An investor who trades frequently because she believes she can consistently outperform the market, despite evidence that her trades have underperformed a simple index strategy, is most likely exhibiting:

## Options

- **A**: Hindsight bias
- **B**: Overconfidence bias
- **C**: Self-control bias

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Overconfidence bias is an emotional bias characterized by:

- Unwarranted faith in one's own abilities, reasoning, and judgment
- Overestimating knowledge and ability to predict outcomes
- Underestimating risks
- Excessive trading that generates transaction costs without adding value

Manifestations include:
- Illusion of knowledge: Believing more information leads to better predictions
- Illusion of control: Believing one can influence random outcomes
- Prediction overconfidence: Underestimating uncertainty ranges

Studies show overconfident investors trade more and earn lower returns on average.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (Hindsight bias): Believing past events were predictable ("I knew it all along")
- Option C (Self-control bias): Difficulty deferring consumption or sticking to long-term plans
