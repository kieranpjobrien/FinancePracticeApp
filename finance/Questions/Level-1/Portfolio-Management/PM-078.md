# Question

Introducing a risk-free asset to the investment opportunity set allows investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Achieve portfolios only on the efficient frontier of risky assets
- **B**: Achieve portfolios on a straight line connecting the risk-free rate to the optimal risky portfolio
- **C**: Eliminate all systematic risk from their portfolios

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Adding the risk-free asset:

**Before (Markowitz):**
- Efficient frontier is curved
- All optimal portfolios are risky asset combinations

**After (Tobin's Separation):**
- New efficient set is the Capital Market Line (CML)
- CML is a straight line from Rf to the tangency portfolio
- All investors hold combinations of Rf and the optimal risky portfolio

**Portfolio choices on CML:**
- Lending portfolios: Part in Rf, part in risky portfolio (below tangency point)
- Tangency portfolio: 100% in optimal risky portfolio
- Borrowing portfolios: Borrow at Rf to leverage risky portfolio (above tangency)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: The CML dominates the efficient frontier
- Option C: Cannot eliminate systematic risk; only unsystematic risk is diversifiable
