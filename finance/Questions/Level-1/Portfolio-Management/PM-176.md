# Question

A stock has a bid price of $49.80 and an ask price of $50.20. The bid-ask spread as a percentage of the midpoint is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.40%
- **B**: 0.80%
- **C**: 2.00%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bid-ask spread calculation:

**Absolute spread:**
Spread = Ask - Bid = $50.20 - $49.80 = $0.40

**Midpoint:**
Midpoint = (Bid + Ask) / 2 = ($49.80 + $50.20) / 2 = $50.00

**Percentage spread:**
% Spread = Absolute Spread / Midpoint × 100
% Spread = $0.40 / $50.00 × 100 = 0.80%

**Interpretation:**
- Round-trip cost approximately 0.80%
- Wider spreads = less liquid
- Implicit trading cost

**Factors affecting spread:**
- Trading volume
- Price volatility
- Market maker competition
- Stock price level

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May use ask price as denominator
- Option C: May use percentage points incorrectly
