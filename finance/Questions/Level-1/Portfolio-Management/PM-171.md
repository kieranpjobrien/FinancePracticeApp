# Question

Corner portfolios on the efficient frontier are portfolios where:

## Options

- **A**: All assets have exactly equal weights
- **B**: An asset weight changes from zero to positive or positive to zero
- **C**: The expected return equals the risk-free rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corner portfolios:

**Definition:**
- Points on efficient frontier where asset composition changes
- An asset either enters or exits the portfolio
- Occurs at specific risk-return combinations

**Characteristics:**
- Any efficient portfolio is a combination of adjacent corner portfolios
- Identified during quadratic optimization
- Key points for understanding portfolio evolution

**Uses:**
- Simplify efficient frontier description
- Understand how allocation changes with risk tolerance
- Linear interpolation between corners gives efficient portfolios

**Example:**
- At low risk: Only bonds in portfolio
- Corner portfolio: Small-cap stocks enter at certain risk level
- At higher risk: Small-cap weight increases

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Equal weights are naive diversification, not corner
- Option C: Risk-free rate isn't typically on risky frontier
