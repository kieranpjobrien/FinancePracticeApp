# Question

Compared to a fixed withdrawal rate, a dynamic withdrawal strategy:

## Options

- **A**: Maintains constant inflation-adjusted withdrawals regardless of portfolio performance
- **B**: Adjusts withdrawals based on portfolio performance, reducing sequence risk
- **C**: Only allows withdrawals from dividend and interest income

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Dynamic vs. fixed withdrawal:

**Fixed withdrawal (e.g., 4% rule):**
- Constant real (inflation-adjusted) amount
- Ignores current portfolio value
- Risk of depletion if early losses

**Dynamic withdrawal:**
- Adjusts based on portfolio performance
- Reduces spending after poor years
- Increases (or maintains) after good years
- Reduces sequence-of-returns risk

**Dynamic approaches:**
- Guardrails: Adjust within bands
- Percentage of portfolio: Fluctuates with value
- Floor-ceiling: Set minimum and maximum
- Actuarial methods: Based on remaining time

**Trade-off:**
- More sustainable vs. less predictable income

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes fixed withdrawal strategy
- Option C: This is an income-only approach, not dynamic
