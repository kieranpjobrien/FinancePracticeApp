# Question

A target-date fund's "glide path" refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The fund's historical performance trajectory
- **B**: The gradual shift from stocks to bonds as target date approaches
- **C**: The fund's expense ratio declining over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Target-date fund glide path:

**Definition:**
- Planned asset allocation changes
- Shifts from aggressive to conservative
- Based on years to target date

**Typical progression:**
- Early: 90% stocks / 10% bonds
- Mid-career: 70% stocks / 30% bonds
- Near retirement: 50% stocks / 50% bonds
- In retirement: 30% stocks / 70% bonds

**Rationale:**
- Young investors: Long horizon, can take risk
- Near retirement: Preserve capital
- Automatic rebalancing

**Variations:**
- "To" retirement: Reaches conservative at target
- "Through" retirement: Continues adjusting after

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Not about historical returns
- Option C: Expenses are separate from glide path
