# Question

A total return approach to investing:

## Options

- **A**: Focuses exclusively on dividend and interest income
- **B**: Considers both income and capital appreciation as sources of return
- **C**: Invests only in growth stocks with no dividends

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Total return approach:

**Definition:**
- Considers all sources of return: dividends, interest, capital gains
- Spending can come from any source
- Optimizes risk-adjusted total return rather than just income

**Advantages:**
- Greater investment flexibility
- Better diversification (not limited to income-producing assets)
- Tax efficiency (capital gains often taxed favorably)
- Higher expected long-term wealth

**Contrast with income-only approach:**
- Income approach: Only spend dividends/interest
- May force suboptimal portfolio to generate income
- May lead to insufficient diversification

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes an income-only approach
- Option C: Total return includes dividend-paying stocks as part of the mix
