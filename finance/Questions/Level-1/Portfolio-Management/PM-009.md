# Question

Which type of risk can be eliminated through diversification?

## Options

- **A**: Systematic risk
- **B**: Unsystematic risk
- **C**: Total risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Unsystematic risk (also called diversifiable, firm-specific, or idiosyncratic risk) can be eliminated through diversification because:

- It relates to factors specific to individual companies (management decisions, labor strikes, product recalls)
- These company-specific events tend to be uncorrelated across different firms
- As more securities are added to a portfolio, firm-specific risks offset each other

Systematic risk (market risk) cannot be diversified away as it affects all securities in the market (interest rates, inflation, recessions).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Systematic risk affects the entire market and cannot be eliminated through diversification
- Option C: Total risk = Systematic + Unsystematic; only the unsystematic portion can be diversified away
