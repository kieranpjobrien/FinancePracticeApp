# Question

The disposition effect refers to investors' tendency to:

## Options

- **A**: Diversify across too many asset classes
- **B**: Sell winners too early and hold losers too long
- **C**: Follow the recommendations of financial advisors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Disposition effect:

**Definition:**
- Tendency to realize gains quickly while reluctantly realizing losses
- Related to prospect theory and loss aversion
- One of the most robust findings in behavioral finance

**Psychology:**
- Selling winner provides pleasure of "locking in" gain
- Holding loser avoids pain of admitting mistake
- Reference point (purchase price) drives behavior

**Consequences:**
- Suboptimal tax management (should harvest losses)
- Holding poor investments hoping to break even
- Selling good investments too early

**Evidence:**
- Studies show winners sold at 1.5x rate of losers
- Reduces after-tax returns

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes naive diversification
- Option C: Unrelated to disposition effect
