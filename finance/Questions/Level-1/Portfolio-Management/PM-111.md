# Question

An analyst maintains her earnings estimate despite several quarters of results that contradict her model, updating her forecast only slightly. This most likely reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Conservatism bias
- **B**: Availability bias
- **C**: Representativeness bias

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Conservatism bias:

**Definition:**
- Slow to update beliefs when presented with new information
- Overweight prior views, underweight new evidence
- Rational updating requires appropriate weight on new data (Bayesian)

**In this example:**
- Multiple quarters of contrary evidence
- Only slight forecast adjustments
- Clinging to original model despite data

**Contrasted with anchoring:**
- Both lead to insufficient adjustment
- Anchoring: Focus on irrelevant reference point
- Conservatism: Slow to incorporate new relevant information

**Investment implications:**
- Delayed reaction to earnings surprises
- Post-earnings announcement drift
- Slow portfolio adjustments

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Availability): Based on ease of recall, not updating speed
- Option C (Representativeness): Based on stereotypes, not belief updating
