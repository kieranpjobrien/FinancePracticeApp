# Question

A stock has a standard deviation of 30% and a correlation with the market of 0.6. If the market standard deviation is 18%, the stock's beta is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.36
- **B**: 1.00
- **C**: 1.00

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Beta using correlation:

**Formula:**
β = ρ × (σstock / σmarket)

Where:
- ρ = correlation = 0.6
- σstock = 30% = 0.30
- σmarket = 18% = 0.18

**Calculation:**
β = 0.6 × (0.30 / 0.18)
β = 0.6 × 1.667
β = 1.00

**Interpretation:**
- Despite higher volatility, correlation of 0.6 moderates beta
- Stock moves 1:1 with market on average
- Higher individual volatility offset by imperfect correlation

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May only multiply correlation by ratio without proper calculation
- May confuse correlation with beta
