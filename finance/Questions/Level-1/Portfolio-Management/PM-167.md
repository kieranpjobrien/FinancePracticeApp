# Question

The costs of portfolio rebalancing include:

## Options

- **A**: Only brokerage commissions
- **B**: Transaction costs, market impact, and potential tax consequences
- **C**: Management fees only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Rebalancing costs:

**Transaction costs:**
- Brokerage commissions
- Bid-ask spreads
- Market impact (large trades)

**Tax consequences:**
- Realized capital gains
- Taxable in non-retirement accounts
- May reduce after-tax returns

**Opportunity costs:**
- Selling winners that may continue rising
- Buying losers that may continue falling
- Giving up momentum effect

**Cost-benefit analysis:**
- More frequent rebalancing = higher costs
- Less frequent = more drift from targets
- Threshold-based often more efficient than calendar

**Tax management:**
- Use new contributions to rebalance
- Rebalance in tax-advantaged accounts first
- Consider tax-loss harvesting

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Ignores taxes and market impact
- Option C: Management fees are ongoing, not rebalancing-specific
