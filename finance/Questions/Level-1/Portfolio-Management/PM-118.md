# Question

The market risk premium is defined as:

## Options

- **A**: The expected return on the market portfolio
- **B**: The expected return on the market minus the risk-free rate
- **C**: The risk-free rate minus the expected market return

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Market risk premium:

**Definition:**
- Excess return expected from investing in market vs. risk-free asset
- Compensation for bearing systematic (market) risk
- Key input in CAPM and many valuation models

**Formula:**
Market Risk Premium = E(Rm) - Rf

**Historical estimates:**
- Long-term U.S. equity premium: approximately 5-7%
- Varies by time period and methodology
- Subject of ongoing academic debate

**Uses:**
- CAPM: E(Ri) = Rf + βi × (Market Risk Premium)
- Cost of equity estimation
- Valuation models

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is expected market return, not the premium
- Option C: This would be negative; the premium is positive
