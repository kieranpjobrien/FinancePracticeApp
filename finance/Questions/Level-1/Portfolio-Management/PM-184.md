# Question

Sovereign risk refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The risk of corporate bond defaults
- **B**: The risk that a government will default on its obligations or impose restrictions
- **C**: The risk of currency fluctuations

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sovereign risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk that government will fail to honor obligations
- Includes default on bonds
- May include expropriation, capital controls

**Components:**
- Default risk on government bonds
- Convertibility risk (currency controls)
- Transfer risk (inability to repatriate)
- Expropriation risk

**Factors affecting:**
- Economic stability
- Political environment
- Debt levels
- Foreign reserves
- History of defaults

**Management:**
- Country analysis
- Diversification across countries
- Credit default swaps on sovereigns
- Investment limits by country rating

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Corporate default is credit risk, not sovereign
- Option C: Currency risk is separate from sovereign risk
