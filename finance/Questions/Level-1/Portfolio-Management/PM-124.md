# Question

During the accumulation phase of the investment life cycle, an investor is typically:

## Options

- **A**: Withdrawing funds to support retirement expenses
- **B**: Building wealth through saving and investment for future goals
- **C**: Focused primarily on preserving existing wealth

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Investment life cycle phases:

**Accumulation phase:**
- Typically early to mid-career
- Building wealth for future goals
- Relatively high risk tolerance (long horizon)
- Focus on growth investments
- Regular contributions to portfolio

**Consolidation phase:**
- Peak earning years
- Continued saving but may begin reducing risk
- Balance growth and preservation

**Distribution/Spending phase:**
- Retirement years
- Drawing down accumulated wealth
- Focus on income and preservation
- Lower risk tolerance

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes the distribution phase
- Option C: This describes consolidation/distribution phases
