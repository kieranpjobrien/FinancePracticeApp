# Question

According to CAPM, the expected return on a zero-beta portfolio equals:

## Options

- **A**: Zero
- **B**: The risk-free rate
- **C**: The market risk premium

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Zero-beta portfolio:

**CAPM equation:**
E(Ri) = Rf + βi[E(Rm) - Rf]

**With β = 0:**
E(Ri) = Rf + 0 × [E(Rm) - Rf]
E(Ri) = Rf

**Interpretation:**
- No systematic risk
- No market risk exposure
- Earns only risk-free rate
- All risk is unsystematic (not compensated)

**Characteristics:**
- Uncorrelated with market
- May still have volatility
- No expected return for bearing unsystematic risk

**Black's Zero-Beta CAPM:**
- Uses zero-beta portfolio instead of Rf
- Doesn't require risk-free borrowing/lending

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Zero beta means zero market risk, not zero return
- Option C: Risk premium requires bearing systematic risk
