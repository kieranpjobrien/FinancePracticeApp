# Question

Which of the following represents a liquidity constraint for an investor?

## Options

- **A**: The investor is 25 years old and plans to retire at 65
- **B**: The investor needs $50,000 in 6 months for a home down payment
- **C**: The investor is in the highest marginal tax bracket

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity constraints refer to the need for cash or easily convertible assets to meet anticipated or unanticipated expenditures:

- Known liquidity needs: Down payments, tuition, planned purchases
- Emergency reserves: 3-6 months of expenses typically recommended
- Ongoing cash flow needs: Living expenses, debt payments

The $50,000 needed in 6 months is a specific, near-term liquidity requirement that must be invested in liquid, low-risk assets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes the time horizon constraint (40 years until retirement)
- Option C: This describes the tax constraint, which affects after-tax returns and asset location decisions
