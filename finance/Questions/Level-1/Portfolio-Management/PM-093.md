# Question

A portfolio manager who needs to sell a large position quickly and accepts a lower price than expected is experiencing:

## Options

- **A**: Market risk
- **B**: Liquidity risk
- **C**: Settlement risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk of being unable to sell an asset quickly without significant price concession
- Two components:
  1. Market liquidity: Ability to transact without moving price
  2. Funding liquidity: Ability to meet cash obligations

**Causes of illiquidity:**
- Large position relative to market
- Thinly traded securities
- Market stress periods
- Unique/complex securities

**Manifestations:**
- Wide bid-ask spreads
- Market impact (price moves against you)
- Inability to exit positions
- Forced selling at distressed prices

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Market risk is price change due to market factors, not transaction difficulty
- Option C: Settlement risk is failure to deliver/receive after trade execution
