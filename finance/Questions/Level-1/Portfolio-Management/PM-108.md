# Question

Sequence-of-returns risk is particularly important for:

## Options

- **A**: Young investors in the accumulation phase
- **B**: Retirees making regular withdrawals from their portfolio
- **C**: Institutional investors with perpetual time horizons

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Sequence-of-returns risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk that timing of poor returns adversely affects outcomes
- Order of returns matters when money is added or withdrawn
- Same average return, different sequence = different ending wealth

**Critical for retirees because:**
- Withdrawals during down markets deplete capital faster
- Fewer shares remain to recover when markets rebound
- Early retirement losses are particularly damaging
- Can lead to premature portfolio depletion

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Cash buffer/bucket strategy
- Flexible withdrawal rates
- Guaranteed income sources
- Reduced equity in early retirement years

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: During accumulation, regular contributions benefit from down markets (dollar-cost averaging)
- Option C: Perpetual investors have long horizons to recover from poor sequences
