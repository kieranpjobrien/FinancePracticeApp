# Question

Home bias refers to investors' tendency to:

## Options

- **A**: Prefer fixed-income investments over equities
- **B**: Overweight domestic securities relative to global market weights
- **C**: Invest primarily in real estate assets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Home bias:

**Definition:**
- Overweighting domestic assets in portfolios
- Underweighting international assets
- Deviation from global market capitalization weights

**Evidence:**
- U.S. investors hold ~70-80% domestic equity
- U.S. represents ~40-50% of global market cap
- Similar pattern observed in most countries

**Potential causes:**
- Familiarity (know domestic companies better)
- Currency risk aversion
- Transaction costs (historically higher for foreign)
- Information asymmetry
- Regulatory/tax factors

**Consequences:**
- Suboptimal diversification
- Concentrated country risk
- Missed opportunities in foreign markets

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes asset class preference, not home bias
- Option C: This describes real estate preference, not home bias
