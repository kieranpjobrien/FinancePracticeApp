# Question

Inflation risk is the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates will rise significantly
- **B**: Investment returns will not keep pace with rising prices
- **C**: Currency exchange rates will fluctuate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inflation risk:

**Definition:**
- Risk that purchasing power declines
- Returns insufficient to offset price increases
- Real returns negative or inadequate

**Most affected:**
- Fixed-income investments
- Cash holdings
- Nominal annuities
- Long-term fixed contracts

**Less affected:**
- Equities (long-term)
- Real assets (real estate, commodities)
- TIPS
- Floating-rate instruments

**Measurement:**
- Compare nominal return to inflation
- Real return = Nominal - Inflation

**Protection strategies:**
- TIPS
- Equities
- Real assets
- Commodities

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Interest rate risk is separate (though related)
- Option C: Currency risk is different from inflation risk
