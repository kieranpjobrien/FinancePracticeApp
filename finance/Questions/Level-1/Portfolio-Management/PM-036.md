# Question

An investor with a higher risk aversion coefficient would have indifference curves that are:

## Options

- **A**: Flatter, requiring less additional return for taking on more risk
- **B**: Steeper, requiring more additional return for taking on more risk
- **C**: Identical to those of a less risk-averse investor

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The utility function is: U = E(R) - ½Aσ²

Where A = risk aversion coefficient.

Higher risk aversion (higher A) means:
- Greater penalty for taking risk
- Steeper indifference curves
- More additional expected return required to accept additional risk
- Investor demands higher risk premium

Graphically:
- Y-axis: Expected return
- X-axis: Risk (standard deviation)
- Steeper curves = more risk-averse investors

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Flatter curves indicate lower risk aversion, not higher
- Option C: Risk aversion directly affects the slope of indifference curves
