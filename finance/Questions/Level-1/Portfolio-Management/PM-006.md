# Question

A portfolio consists of three equally weighted assets. Each asset has a standard deviation of 20% and each pair of assets has a correlation of 0.25. The portfolio standard deviation is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 13.9%
- **B**: 16.3%
- **C**: 20.0%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

For a three-asset equally weighted portfolio:

σ²p = Σwi²σi² + ΣΣwiwjσiσjρij (for i≠j)

With equal weights (w = 1/3) and equal standard deviations (σ = 20%) and equal correlations (ρ = 0.25):

σ²p = 3 × (1/3)² × (0.20)² + 3 × 2 × (1/3) × (1/3) × (0.20) × (0.20) × 0.25

σ²p = 3 × (1/9) × 0.04 + 6 × (1/9) × 0.04 × 0.25
σ²p = 0.04/3 + 0.06/9
σ²p = 0.0133 + 0.0067
σ²p = 0.0193

σp = √0.0193 = 0.139 = 13.9%

Note: The portfolio standard deviation (13.9%) is less than any individual asset's standard deviation (20%) due to diversification benefits.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: Uses incorrect weights or correlation adjustments
- Option C: Ignores diversification entirely and uses individual asset standard deviation
