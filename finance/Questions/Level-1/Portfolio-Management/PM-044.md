# Question

A portfolio has 50% in Stock A (beta 1.2), 30% in Stock B (beta 0.8), and 20% in Stock C (beta 1.5). The portfolio beta is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 1.10
- **B**: 1.14
- **C**: 1.17

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Portfolio beta is the weighted average of individual betas:

βp = Σ(wi × βi)

βp = (0.50 × 1.2) + (0.30 × 0.8) + (0.20 × 1.5)
βp = 0.60 + 0.24 + 0.30
βp = 1.14

The portfolio has slightly more systematic risk than the market (β > 1.0).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: May use incorrect weights or arithmetic errors
- Option C: Simple average of betas (1.2 + 0.8 + 1.5)/3 = 1.17, ignoring weights
