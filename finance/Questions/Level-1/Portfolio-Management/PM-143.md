# Question

The "4% rule" for retirement withdrawal suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Retirees should hold exactly 4% in cash at all times
- **B**: Withdrawing 4% of initial portfolio annually (inflation-adjusted) is sustainable for 30 years
- **C**: Portfolio returns must exceed 4% annually

## Answer

B

## Explanation

4% withdrawal rule:

**Origin:**
- William Bengen's 1994 research
- Based on historical U.S. market data
- 30-year retirement horizon

**The rule:**
- Withdraw 4% of initial portfolio in Year 1
- Increase withdrawal by inflation each subsequent year
- High probability of portfolio lasting 30 years

**Example:**
- $1,000,000 portfolio
- Year 1: $40,000 withdrawal
- Year 2: $40,000 × (1 + inflation)

**Caveats:**
- Based on historical data (may not repeat)
- Assumes balanced portfolio (50/50 to 75/25 stocks/bonds)
- Current low yields may require lower rate
- Individual circumstances vary

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Rule is about withdrawals, not cash allocation
- Option C: Returns can vary; rule is about withdrawal rate
