# Question

Utility stocks typically have betas that are:

## Options

- **A**: Greater than 1.0 because they are essential services
- **B**: Less than 1.0 because they have stable, regulated cash flows
- **C**: Equal to 1.0 because they are publicly traded

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Industry betas:

**Defensive sectors (beta < 1.0):**
- Utilities: Regulated, stable demand
- Consumer staples: Essential products
- Healthcare: Consistent demand

**Cyclical sectors (beta > 1.0):**
- Technology: Discretionary spending
- Consumer discretionary: Economic sensitive
- Financials: Interest rate/economy sensitive

**Why utilities have low beta:**
- Regulated returns
- Inelastic demand (electricity needed regardless of economy)
- Stable cash flows
- Dividend-focused investors

**Investment implications:**
- Low beta = less market sensitivity
- Defensive in downturns
- May underperform in strong bull markets

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Essential nature reduces volatility, not increases it
- Option C: Being publicly traded doesn't determine beta
