# Question

An investor's risk capacity is primarily determined by:

## Options

- **A**: Their emotional comfort with market volatility
- **B**: Their financial ability to withstand losses
- **C**: Their desire to achieve high returns

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk capacity vs. risk tolerance:

**Risk capacity (ability):**
- Objective, financial factors
- Determined by:
  - Wealth and income
  - Investment horizon
  - Liquidity needs
  - Job stability
  - Other resources
- "Can afford to take risk"

**Risk tolerance (willingness):**
- Subjective, psychological factors
- Emotional comfort with volatility
- Behavioral responses to losses
- "Comfortable taking risk"

**Overall risk tolerance:**
- Consider both capacity and tolerance
- Usually constrained by lower of the two
- Education may align willingness with ability

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes willingness, not capacity
- Option C: This describes desired returns, not risk capacity
