# Question

The difference between "required return" and "desired return" in the IPS is that:

## Options

- **A**: Required return is always higher than desired return
- **B**: Required return is the minimum needed to meet goals; desired return is aspirational
- **C**: There is no meaningful difference between the two terms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Required vs. desired return:

**Required return:**
- Minimum return necessary to meet financial obligations
- Based on essential goals and needs
- Spending requirements, inflation protection
- Must be achieved to avoid financial hardship

**Desired return:**
- Return investor would like to achieve
- Based on aspirational goals
- May include lifestyle upgrades, larger legacy
- "Nice to have" rather than "must have"

**Importance of distinction:**
- Required return sets minimum risk needed
- If required exceeds what's prudent, goals must be adjusted
- Desired return may require excessive risk

**Example:**
- Required: 5% to maintain living standard
- Desired: 8% to leave large inheritance

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Desired can exceed required (aspirational goals)
- Option C: The distinction has important planning implications
