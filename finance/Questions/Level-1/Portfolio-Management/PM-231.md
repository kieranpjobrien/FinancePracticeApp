# Question

Smart beta strategies differ from traditional passive indexing by:

## Options

- **A**: Always holding the exact market portfolio
- **B**: Using alternative weighting schemes based on factors rather than market capitalization
- **C**: Attempting to time the market through active trading

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Smart beta:

**Definition:**
- Alternative to market-cap weighting
- Rules-based factor exposure
- Between passive and active

**Common smart beta approaches:**
- Equal weighting
- Fundamental weighting
- Minimum volatility
- Value tilt
- Momentum tilt
- Quality tilt
- Multi-factor combinations

**Characteristics:**
- Transparent methodology
- Rules-based (not discretionary)
- Lower fees than active
- Systematic factor exposure

**Considerations:**
- May have value/size tilts implicitly
- Higher turnover than cap-weighted
- Factor timing risk

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Smart beta deviates from market portfolio
- Option C: Smart beta is rules-based, not market timing
