# Question

An investor with a multi-stage time horizon typically:

## Options

- **A**: Has a single investment objective throughout life
- **B**: Has different return and risk objectives at different life stages
- **C**: Should avoid making any changes to asset allocation

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Multi-stage time horizon:

**Life-cycle perspective:**
- Accumulation phase (working years): Higher risk tolerance, growth focus
- Pre-retirement transition: Moderate risk, begin shifting to income
- Retirement/distribution phase: Lower risk, income focus, capital preservation

**Key considerations:**
- Risk capacity changes with life stage
- Liquidity needs evolve (college, home purchase, retirement income)
- Different stages may require different strategic allocations

**Implications:**
- IPS should address multiple stages
- Asset allocation glide paths (like target-date funds)
- Regular reviews as circumstances change

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Objectives typically evolve over an investor's lifetime
- Option C: Asset allocation should adapt to changing circumstances
