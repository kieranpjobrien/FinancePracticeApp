# Question

Unlike mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs) can be:

## Options

- **A**: Traded only at end-of-day NAV
- **B**: Traded throughout the day at market prices
- **C**: Purchased only through fund companies

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ETF characteristics:

**Intraday trading:**
- Trade on exchanges like stocks
- Continuous pricing
- Buy/sell anytime during market hours
- Market, limit, stop orders available

**Mutual fund trading:**
- Once daily at NAV
- Orders executed at closing price
- No intraday trading

**Other ETF advantages:**
- Lower expense ratios typically
- Tax efficiency (creation/redemption)
- Transparency of holdings
- Short selling possible

**Considerations:**
- Bid-ask spreads
- Premium/discount to NAV
- Commission costs

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes mutual funds, not ETFs
- Option C: ETFs trade on exchanges through brokers
