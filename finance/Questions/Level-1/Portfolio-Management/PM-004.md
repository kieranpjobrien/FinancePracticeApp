# Question

Asset X has a standard deviation of 15% and Asset Y has a standard deviation of 25%. If the correlation between X and Y is 0.4, the covariance between the two assets is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 0.0015
- **B**: 0.0150
- **C**: 0.1000

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The covariance between two assets is calculated as:

Cov(X,Y) = ρXY × σX × σY

Where:
- ρXY = correlation = 0.4
- σX = standard deviation of X = 0.15
- σY = standard deviation of Y = 0.25

Cov(X,Y) = 0.4 × 0.15 × 0.25
Cov(X,Y) = 0.015

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Incorrectly squares or divides the result
- Option C: Multiplies only the standard deviations without the correlation, or uses percentage values incorrectly
