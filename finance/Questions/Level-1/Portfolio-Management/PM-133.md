# Question

The fundamental risk-return trade-off in investing states that:

## Options

- **A**: Lower risk always leads to higher returns
- **B**: Higher expected returns generally require accepting higher risk
- **C**: Risk and return are unrelated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Risk-return trade-off:

**Fundamental principle:**
- Higher expected returns require bearing more risk
- Investors demand compensation for taking risk
- Risk premium = additional return for bearing risk

**Why this exists:**
- Risk-averse investors prefer certainty
- Must be compensated to accept uncertainty
- Market equilibrium requires risk-return relationship

**Important nuances:**
- Trade-off is for expected, not realized returns
- Some risks are diversifiable (not compensated)
- Only systematic risk is priced in CAPM

**Efficient frontier:**
- Visualizes optimal risk-return combinations
- Cannot get higher return without more risk (efficiently)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Lower risk means lower expected (not higher) returns
- Option C: Risk and return are positively related in equilibrium
