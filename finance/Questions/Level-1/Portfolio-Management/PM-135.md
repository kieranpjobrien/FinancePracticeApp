# Question

A return distribution with negative skewness would have:

## Options

- **A**: A longer tail on the right side with more extreme positive returns
- **B**: A longer tail on the left side with more extreme negative returns
- **C**: Perfect symmetry around the mean

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Skewness:

**Negative skewness (left-skewed):**
- Longer left tail
- More extreme negative returns
- Mean < Median < Mode
- Common in equity returns

**Positive skewness (right-skewed):**
- Longer right tail
- More extreme positive returns
- Mode < Median < Mean
- Common in lottery-like investments

**Implications for investors:**
- Negative skewness = larger potential for extreme losses
- Risk measures based on symmetry may understate risk
- Investors generally dislike negative skewness

**Preference:**
- Risk-averse investors prefer positive skewness
- Willing to accept lower mean return for positive skew

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This describes positive skewness
- Option C: This describes a symmetric distribution (zero skewness)
