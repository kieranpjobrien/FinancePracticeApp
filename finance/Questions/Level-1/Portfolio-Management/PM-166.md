# Question

The primary purpose of diversification is to:

## Options

- **A**: Maximize expected returns
- **B**: Reduce unsystematic risk through asset combination
- **C**: Eliminate all portfolio risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Diversification purpose:

**Core benefit:**
- Reduces unsystematic (firm-specific) risk
- Combines assets with imperfect correlation
- "Don't put all eggs in one basket"

**How it works:**
- Company-specific events offset each other
- Good news for some, bad news for others
- Portfolio volatility less than average asset volatility

**What diversification cannot do:**
- Eliminate systematic (market) risk
- Guarantee positive returns
- Remove all volatility

**Practical considerations:**
- Most benefit from 20-30 stocks
- Diminishing returns beyond that
- International diversification adds benefits

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Diversification reduces risk, not necessarily maximize return
- Option C: Systematic risk remains even with perfect diversification
