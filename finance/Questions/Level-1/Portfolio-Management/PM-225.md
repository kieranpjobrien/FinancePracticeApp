# Question

A risk-averse investor with a risk aversion coefficient of 3 is evaluating an investment with expected return of 15% and variance of 0.04. The certainty equivalent return is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 9%
- **B**: 12%
- **C**: 15%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Certainty equivalent:

**Formula:**
CE = E(R) - ½ × A × σ²

Where:
- E(R) = Expected return = 15%
- A = Risk aversion coefficient = 3
- σ² = Variance = 0.04

**Calculation:**
CE = 0.15 - ½ × 3 × 0.04
CE = 0.15 - 1.5 × 0.04
CE = 0.15 - 0.06
CE = 0.09 = 9%

**Interpretation:**
- Investor indifferent between risky 15% and certain 9%
- Risk premium = 15% - 9% = 6%
- Higher A = lower CE (more risk averse)

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B: May use incorrect formula or values
- Option C: Ignores risk penalty entirely
