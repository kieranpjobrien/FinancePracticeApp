# Question

According to the efficient market hypothesis, security prices:

## Options

- **A**: Always reflect intrinsic value exactly
- **B**: Fully reflect all available information
- **C**: Never deviate from fundamental value

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH):

**Core principle:**
- Prices reflect available information
- Adjust rapidly to new information
- Cannot consistently beat market using known information

**Forms:**
1. Weak: Prices reflect past price data
2. Semi-strong: Prices reflect all public information
3. Strong: Prices reflect all information (public + private)

**Implications:**
- Active management unlikely to add value
- New information causes price changes
- Diversification is rational strategy

**Nuances:**
- Efficiency is relative, not absolute
- Anomalies may exist but are hard to exploit
- Transaction costs matter

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Prices may not equal "true" value, just reflect information
- Option C: Prices can deviate but unpredictably
