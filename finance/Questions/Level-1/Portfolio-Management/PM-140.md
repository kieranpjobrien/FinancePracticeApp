# Question

Capital market expectations in portfolio management refer to:

## Options

- **A**: Historical returns of major asset classes
- **B**: Forward-looking estimates of risk, return, and correlations
- **C**: The current market prices of securities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Capital market expectations (CME):

**Definition:**
- Forward-looking estimates for asset class behavior
- Key inputs for strategic asset allocation

**Components:**
1. Expected returns by asset class
2. Expected volatilities (standard deviations)
3. Expected correlations between asset classes

**Approaches:**
- Historical extrapolation (with adjustments)
- Economic analysis (growth, inflation, yields)
- Survey-based estimates
- Equilibrium models (risk premium based)

**Time horizon:**
- Typically long-term (5-10+ years)
- Strategic, not tactical

**Challenges:**
- Uncertainty in forecasting
- Regime changes
- Time-varying relationships

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: CME are forward-looking, not historical
- Option C: Current prices inform expectations but aren't CME
