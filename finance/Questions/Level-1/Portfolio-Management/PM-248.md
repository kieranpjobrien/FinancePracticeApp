# Question

Higher portfolio turnover typically results in:

## Options

- **A**: Lower transaction costs and taxes
- **B**: Higher transaction costs and potential tax drag
- **C**: No impact on portfolio performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Portfolio turnover impact:

**Costs of high turnover:**
- Transaction costs (commissions, spreads)
- Market impact costs
- Short-term capital gains taxes
- Opportunity cost

**Turnover calculation:**
Lesser of (buys or sells) / Average assets

**Typical turnover:**
- Index funds: 5-10%
- Active funds: 50-100%+
- Some strategies: >200%

**Tax implications:**
- High turnover → short-term gains
- Short-term taxed as ordinary income
- Reduces after-tax returns

**Implications:**
- Active strategies must overcome costs
- Tax efficiency matters in taxable accounts

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Higher turnover increases costs
- Option C: Turnover significantly impacts returns
