# Question

Portfolio A has a Sharpe ratio of 0.8 and a Treynor ratio of 0.12. Portfolio B has a Sharpe ratio of 0.7 and a Treynor ratio of 0.15. For a well-diversified investor adding one of these portfolios to an existing diversified portfolio, which is more appropriate and why?

## Options

- **A**: Portfolio A, because the Sharpe ratio is higher
- **B**: Portfolio B, because the Treynor ratio is higher
- **C**: Portfolio A, because total risk matters more than systematic risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

For a well-diversified investor, the Treynor ratio is more appropriate:

- The Treynor ratio uses beta (systematic risk) as the risk measure
- A diversified investor can eliminate unsystematic risk through diversification
- Only systematic risk matters for assets added to a diversified portfolio
- Portfolio B's higher Treynor ratio (0.15 vs 0.12) indicates better compensation per unit of systematic risk

The Sharpe ratio uses total risk (standard deviation), which is more relevant for:
- Undiversified investors
- Evaluating standalone investments
- Investors whose entire wealth is in one portfolio

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Sharpe ratio is appropriate for undiversified portfolios, not for adding to existing diversified holdings
- Option C: For diversified investors, unsystematic risk is irrelevant; only systematic risk (beta) matters
