# Question

Bounded rationality suggests that investors:

## Options

- **A**: Always make optimal decisions with complete information
- **B**: Make satisfactory rather than optimal decisions due to cognitive limitations
- **C**: Are completely irrational and make random choices

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bounded rationality:

**Concept (Herbert Simon):**
- Cognitive limitations exist
- Information processing is imperfect
- Time and attention are limited
- Optimal decisions often impossible

**Satisficing:**
- Seeking "good enough" solutions
- Not maximizing utility
- Practical decision-making
- Stopping when acceptable found

**Causes:**
- Limited cognitive capacity
- Information costs
- Time constraints
- Complexity of problems

**Implications:**
- Use heuristics and shortcuts
- May miss better alternatives
- Suboptimal but practical outcomes

**Investment impact:**
- Simplify complex choices
- Use rules of thumb
- May not find optimal portfolio

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Describes traditional rational economic agent
- Option C: Investors are boundedly rational, not irrational
