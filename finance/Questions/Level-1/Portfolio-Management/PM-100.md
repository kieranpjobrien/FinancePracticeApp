# Question

R-squared in a regression of a stock's returns against market returns represents:

## Options

- **A**: The stock's beta coefficient
- **B**: The proportion of total risk explained by market movements
- **C**: The stock's expected return

## Answer

B

## Explanation

R-squared (coefficient of determination):

**Definition:**
- Proportion of variance in dependent variable explained by independent variable
- In CAPM context: % of stock's return variance explained by market

**Interpretation:**
- R² = 0.70 means 70% of stock's volatility is due to market movements
- Remaining 30% is firm-specific (unsystematic) risk

**Relationship to diversification:**
- High R²: Stock moves closely with market
- Low R²: Much firm-specific risk remains
- Diversified portfolio has high R² (unsystematic risk diversified away)

**Formula connection:**
- R² = Systematic variance / Total variance
- R² = β² × σ²market / σ²stock

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Beta is the slope coefficient, not R²
- Option C: Expected return is not measured by R²
