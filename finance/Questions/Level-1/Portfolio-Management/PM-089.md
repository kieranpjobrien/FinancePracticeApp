# Question

An investor inherited shares of her late father's company and refuses to sell despite poor fundamentals, saying "these shares are special to me." This most likely reflects:

## Options

- **A**: Endowment effect combined with emotional attachment
- **B**: Representativeness bias
- **C**: Gambler's fallacy

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Endowment effect:

**Definition:**
- Valuing an asset more simply because you own it
- Asking price exceeds what you would pay to acquire it
- Related to loss aversion (selling feels like a loss)

**Combined with emotional attachment:**
- Inherited assets often carry sentimental value
- Family connection amplifies reluctance to sell
- Non-financial factors override rational analysis

**Investment consequences:**
- Concentrated, undiversified positions
- Holding inappropriate assets
- Ignoring fundamental analysis

**Mitigation:**
- Frame as "would I buy this today?"
- Consider tax-efficient ways to diversify
- Acknowledge emotional factors separately from investment decision

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (Representativeness): Judging based on stereotypes
- Option C (Gambler's fallacy): Believing past events affect future probabilities
