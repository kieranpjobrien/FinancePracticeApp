# Question

Optimism bias leads investors to:

## Options

- **A**: Underestimate potential gains
- **B**: Overestimate the likelihood of positive outcomes
- **C**: Always sell at the right time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Optimism bias:

**Definition:**
- Tendency to expect positive outcomes
- Underweight negative possibilities
- "It won't happen to me" mentality

**Investment manifestations:**
- Underestimating downside risks
- Overconfident forecasts
- Inadequate emergency planning
- Concentrated positions

**Examples:**
- "The market always recovers"
- "My stock picks will outperform"
- "I'll sell before it crashes"

**Consequences:**
- Insufficient diversification
- Taking excessive risk
- Surprised by adverse events

**Mitigation:**
- Consider base rates
- Seek disconfirming evidence
- Stress test portfolios

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Optimism overestimates gains
- Option C: Optimism doesn't improve timing
