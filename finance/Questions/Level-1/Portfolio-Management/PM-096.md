# Question

Factor-based risk analysis decomposes portfolio risk by:

## Options

- **A**: Individual security weights only
- **B**: Exposure to systematic risk factors like value, size, and momentum
- **C**: Historical calendar periods

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Factor-based risk analysis:

**Common risk factors:**
- Market (beta)
- Size (small vs. large cap)
- Value (value vs. growth)
- Momentum (winners vs. losers)
- Quality (profitable vs. unprofitable)
- Volatility (low vs. high vol)

**Benefits:**
- Identifies true risk exposures
- Explains performance attribution
- Reveals hidden concentrations
- Enables factor-based hedging

**Example:**
- Portfolio may appear diversified by sector
- But factor analysis reveals heavy value tilt
- Risk concentrated in "value factor" regardless of sectors

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: Security-level analysis misses common factor exposures
- Option C: Time-period analysis is different from risk decomposition
