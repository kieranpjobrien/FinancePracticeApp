# Question

Herd behavior in investing describes:

## Options

- **A**: Independently analyzing each investment decision
- **B**: Following the investment actions of others rather than independent analysis
- **C**: Diversifying across multiple asset classes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Herd behavior:

**Definition:**
- Following crowd's investment decisions
- Ignoring own analysis or information
- "Safety in numbers" mentality

**Causes:**
- Assumption others know something
- Career risk (being wrong alone is worse)
- Regret avoidance
- Information cascade

**Consequences:**
- Asset bubbles
- Market crashes
- Momentum effects
- Overreaction to news

**Examples:**
- Tech bubble (1999-2000)
- Housing bubble
- Meme stock phenomena

**Mitigation:**
- Independent analysis
- Contrarian discipline
- Long-term perspective

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A: This is independent thinking, opposite of herding
- Option C: Diversification is prudent, not herding
