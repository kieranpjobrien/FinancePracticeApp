# Question

The "three Vs" commonly used to characterize big data are:

## Options

- **A**: Value, Validity, and Verification
- **B**: Volume, Velocity, and Variety
- **C**: Variables, Vectors, and Variance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Big data is characterized by three Vs: Volume (massive amounts of data), Velocity (speed at which data is generated and processed), and Variety (different types and formats of data including structured, semi-structured, and unstructured). Some frameworks add additional Vs such as Veracity (data quality/accuracy) and Value (business utility), but the original three Vs are Volume, Velocity, and Variety.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A includes terms related to data quality and validation but not the standard big data framework
- Option C includes statistical terms unrelated to the big data characterization framework
