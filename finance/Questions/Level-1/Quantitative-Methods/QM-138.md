# Question

Two mutually exclusive events have probabilities of 0.3 and 0.4. The probability that at least one occurs is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.12
- **B**: 0.58
- **C**: 0.70

## Answer

C

## Explanation

For mutually exclusive events (events that cannot both occur), the addition rule simplifies to:

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)

P(A or B) = 0.3 + 0.4 = 0.70

Since the events cannot occur together, there is no overlap to subtract.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A multiplies the probabilities (which would apply to independent events for both occurring)
- Option B subtracts a non-existent joint probability
