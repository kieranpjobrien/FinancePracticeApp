# Question

The money-weighted rate of return (MWRR) differs from the time-weighted rate of return (TWRR) because the MWRR:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminates the impact of external cash flows on performance measurement
- **B**: Is affected by the timing and size of external cash flows
- **C**: Always produces a higher return than TWRR

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The money-weighted rate of return (internal rate of return) is influenced by both the timing and magnitude of cash flows. If an investor adds money before good performance, MWRR exceeds TWRR; if money is added before poor performance, MWRR is lower. TWRR neutralizes cash flow timing effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes TWRR, not MWRR
- Option C is incorrect; MWRR can be higher or lower than TWRR depending on cash flow timing
