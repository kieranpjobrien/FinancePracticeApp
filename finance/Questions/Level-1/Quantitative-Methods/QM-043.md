# Question

An R-squared value of 0.64 in a simple linear regression indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: 64% of the variation in Y is explained by the linear relationship with X
- **B**: The correlation between X and Y is 0.64
- **C**: The slope coefficient equals 0.64

## Answer

A

## Explanation

R-squared (coefficient of determination) measures the proportion of total variation in the dependent variable (Y) that is explained by the independent variable (X) through the regression model. An R² of 0.64 means 64% of Y's variability is accounted for by its linear relationship with X, while 36% remains unexplained. In simple linear regression, R² equals the square of the correlation coefficient.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B confuses R² with correlation; the correlation would be √0.64 = 0.80 (or -0.80)
- Option C confuses R² with the slope coefficient; these are different measures
