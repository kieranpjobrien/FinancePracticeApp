# Question

Total risk of a security can be decomposed into:

## Options

- **A**: Credit risk and liquidity risk
- **B**: Systematic risk and unsystematic risk
- **C**: Duration risk and convexity risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Total risk (as measured by variance or standard deviation) equals the sum of systematic (market) risk and unsystematic (idiosyncratic) risk:

Total variance = β²σ²market + σ²unsystematic

Systematic risk is related to market movements and cannot be diversified away. Unsystematic risk is firm-specific and can be eliminated through diversification.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes types of investment risks, not the variance decomposition
- Option C describes fixed-income interest rate risk components
