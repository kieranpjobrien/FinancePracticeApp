# Question

A test comparing the means of two independent samples is:

## Options

- **A**: A chi-square test
- **B**: An independent samples t-test
- **C**: A paired t-test

## Answer

B

## Explanation

An independent samples t-test compares the means of two groups that are not related or matched. Examples include comparing returns of growth stocks vs value stocks, or performance of two different strategies on different portfolios. The test assumes the two groups are independently drawn from their respective populations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A (chi-square) is used for categorical data or variance tests, not comparing two means
- Option C (paired t-test) is used when observations are matched or from the same subjects
