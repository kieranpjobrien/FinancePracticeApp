# Question

The law of large numbers states that as sample size increases:

## Options

- **A**: The sample variance approaches zero
- **B**: The sample mean converges to the population mean
- **C**: All sample observations become identical

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The law of large numbers guarantees that as the sample size grows, the sample mean (and other sample statistics) converge in probability to the true population parameter. This is the theoretical justification for using sample statistics to estimate population parameters—larger samples produce more reliable estimates.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; variance measures dispersion in the population, which doesn't change
- Option C is incorrect; observations retain their variability, but their average converges
