# Question

A portfolio has a monthly mean return of 1% and standard deviation of 5%. Assuming returns are normally distributed, the 5% monthly Value at Risk (VaR) is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: -7.2%
- **B**: -8.2%
- **C**: 7.2%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

VaR at the 5% level represents the return at the 5th percentile—the loss that would only be exceeded 5% of the time.

For normal distribution, the 5th percentile is at z = -1.645

VaR = Mean - (z × Standard Deviation)
VaR = 1% - (1.645 × 5%)
VaR = 1% - 8.225%
VaR = -7.225% ≈ -7.2%

The 5% VaR indicates a 5% probability of losing more than 7.2% in a month.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B adds instead of subtracting the z-score adjustment from the mean
- Option C reports the absolute value without the negative sign
