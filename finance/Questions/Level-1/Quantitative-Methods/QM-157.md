# Question

The bias-variance tradeoff in machine learning means that:

## Options

- **A**: Highly complex models always perform better
- **B**: Reducing model complexity reduces variance but may increase bias
- **C**: Bias and variance are unrelated to model performance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The bias-variance tradeoff is fundamental to machine learning:
- High bias (underfitting): Simple models that don't capture the true pattern
- High variance (overfitting): Complex models that are too sensitive to training data

Simpler models have lower variance but higher bias; complex models have lower bias but higher variance. The goal is to find the optimal complexity that minimizes total error (bias² + variance).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that complex models can overfit
- Option C contradicts the fundamental relationship between bias, variance, and prediction error
