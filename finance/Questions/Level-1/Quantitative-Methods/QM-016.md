# Question

The probability that a company's earnings will beat expectations is 60%. If earnings beat expectations, the probability the stock price rises is 80%. The probability that earnings beat expectations AND the stock price rises is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 48%
- **B**: 70%
- **C**: 80%

## Answer

A

## Explanation

P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A)
P(Beat AND Rise) = P(Beat) × P(Rise|Beat)
P(Beat AND Rise) = 0.60 × 0.80 = 0.48 = 48%

This is the multiplication rule for dependent events.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B (70%) might result from averaging the two probabilities
- Option C ignores the conditioning and uses only P(Rise|Beat)
