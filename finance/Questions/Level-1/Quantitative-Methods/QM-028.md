# Question

Compared to Monte Carlo simulation, historical simulation (bootstrapping) for risk analysis:

## Options

- **A**: Requires specifying probability distributions for input variables
- **B**: Uses actual past observations and may underestimate risk if the historical period was unusually calm
- **C**: Always produces more accurate results because it uses real data

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Historical simulation (bootstrapping) randomly samples from actual historical data rather than from assumed probability distributions. While this has the advantage of not requiring distribution assumptions, it assumes the future will resemble the past. If the historical period used lacks extreme events or was unusually calm, the simulation may underestimate potential risks. Conversely, if it includes unusual crises, it may overestimate typical risks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes Monte Carlo simulation, not historical simulation; historical simulation avoids distribution assumptions
- Option C is incorrect because historical simulation is only as representative as the historical period chosen, which may not reflect future conditions
