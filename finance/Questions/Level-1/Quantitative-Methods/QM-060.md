# Question

Investment A has a mean return of 10% with a standard deviation of 15%. Investment B has a mean return of 8% with a standard deviation of 10%. Which investment has lower risk per unit of return?

## Options

- **A**: Investment A (CV = 1.50)
- **B**: Investment B (CV = 1.25)
- **C**: Both have equal risk per unit of return

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The coefficient of variation (CV) measures risk per unit of return:
CV = Standard Deviation / Mean Return

Investment A: CV = 15% / 10% = 1.50
Investment B: CV = 10% / 8% = 1.25

A lower CV indicates less risk per unit of return. Investment B has lower risk relative to its expected return, making it more efficient from a risk-return perspective.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A correctly calculates CV for Investment A but incorrectly concludes it has lower risk per unit return
- Option C ignores the different CV values
