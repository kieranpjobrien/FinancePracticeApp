# Question

The discount rate used in present value calculations should reflect:

## Options

- **A**: The historical return on the investment
- **B**: The opportunity cost of capital given the investment's risk
- **C**: The inflation rate only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The discount rate represents the required rate of return or opportunity cost of capital—what could be earned on an alternative investment of similar risk. A riskier investment requires a higher discount rate to compensate for the additional uncertainty. The discount rate typically incorporates the risk-free rate plus a risk premium appropriate for the investment's characteristics.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A uses past returns, which may not reflect current required returns
- Option C ignores the risk premium; the discount rate should incorporate both real return and inflation, plus a risk premium
