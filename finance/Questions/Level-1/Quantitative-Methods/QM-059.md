# Question

A return distribution with negative skewness and excess kurtosis greater than zero (leptokurtic) indicates:

## Options

- **A**: A higher probability of extreme positive returns than a normal distribution
- **B**: A higher probability of extreme negative returns and fatter tails than a normal distribution
- **C**: Returns that are more normally distributed than the standard bell curve

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative skewness means the distribution has a longer left tail, indicating a higher probability of large negative returns relative to a normal distribution. Positive excess kurtosis (leptokurtic) means fatter tails and a higher peak than normal, indicating more probability mass in the tails (both extreme gains and losses). Combined, negative skewness with leptokurtosis creates elevated downside risk—particularly dangerous for investors as extreme losses are more likely.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes positive skewness, not negative skewness
- Option C is incorrect; excess kurtosis indicates departure from normality, not conformity to it
