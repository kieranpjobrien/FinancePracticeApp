# Question

When using historical simulation to calculate Value at Risk (VaR), the method assumes that:

## Options

- **A**: Future returns will be normally distributed
- **B**: The historical period is representative of future possibilities
- **C**: Correlations between assets are constant over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Historical simulation for VaR uses actual past returns without assuming a specific distribution. The key assumption is that the historical sample captures the range of possible future outcomes. If the historical period was unusually calm or turbulent, the VaR estimate may understate or overstate future risk. The method is distribution-free but relies heavily on having a representative historical sample.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A is incorrect; historical simulation is nonparametric and does not assume normality
- Option C describes an assumption of parametric VaR methods, not historical simulation
