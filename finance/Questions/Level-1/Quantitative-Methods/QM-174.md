# Question

A histogram displays:

## Options

- **A**: The relationship between two variables
- **B**: The frequency distribution of a single variable
- **C**: Changes in a variable over time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

A histogram shows the frequency (count or relative frequency) of observations falling within specified intervals (bins) for a single variable. The x-axis shows the variable's range divided into bins, and the y-axis shows the count or proportion in each bin. Histograms help visualize the shape of a distribution, including skewness and potential outliers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a scatter plot
- Option C describes a time series chart
