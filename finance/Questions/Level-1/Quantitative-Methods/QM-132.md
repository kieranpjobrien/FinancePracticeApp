# Question

Using the Rule of 72, approximately how many years will it take for an investment to double at an 8% annual rate of return?

## Options

- **A**: 6 years
- **B**: 9 years
- **C**: 12 years

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The Rule of 72 provides a quick approximation for doubling time:

Doubling time ≈ 72 / Interest rate

Doubling time ≈ 72 / 8 = 9 years

This approximation works reasonably well for rates between 4% and 16%. The exact answer using the formula ln(2)/ln(1.08) is 9.01 years.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A results from an incorrect calculation
- Option C might result from using Rule of 72 incorrectly or dividing the rate into 72 wrong
