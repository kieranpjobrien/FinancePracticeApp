# Question

A statistically significant result:

## Options

- **A**: Is always economically meaningful and actionable
- **B**: May not be economically meaningful if the effect size is small
- **C**: Guarantees the null hypothesis is false

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Statistical significance indicates that an observed effect is unlikely to have occurred by chance. However, a statistically significant result may have little practical importance if the magnitude of the effect is small. With large samples, even tiny effects become statistically significant. Analysts should consider both statistical significance (is the effect real?) and economic significance (is the effect large enough to matter in practice?).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores that statistical significance depends on sample size, not just effect magnitude
- Option C is incorrect; statistical tests calculate probabilities, they do not prove hypotheses definitively
