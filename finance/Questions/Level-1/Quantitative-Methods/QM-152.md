# Question

In a regression of stock returns (Y) on market returns (X), a positive intercept (alpha) indicates that:

## Options

- **A**: The stock has more systematic risk than the market
- **B**: The stock has earned excess returns beyond what is explained by market exposure
- **C**: The regression has a low R-squared

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The intercept (alpha) in a returns regression represents the average return when the independent variable equals zero. A positive alpha indicates the stock earned returns above what its market beta would predict—suggesting positive abnormal performance or mispricing. This is the foundation of Jensen's alpha as a performance measure.

Y = α + βX + ε
If α > 0, the stock outperformed given its systematic risk exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes beta (slope), not alpha (intercept)
- Option C is unrelated to the sign of the intercept
