# Question

In hypothesis testing, the null hypothesis typically represents:

## Options

- **A**: The condition that the researcher hopes to prove
- **B**: The default assumption that is tested and potentially rejected
- **C**: The most likely outcome based on preliminary analysis

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The null hypothesis (H₀) represents the status quo or default assumption—typically that no effect exists, no difference exists, or a parameter equals a specific value. It is the hypothesis that is tested and potentially rejected based on sample evidence. The alternative hypothesis (Hₐ) represents what the researcher suspects or hopes to demonstrate. Statistical tests determine whether evidence is sufficient to reject the null hypothesis in favor of the alternative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes the alternative hypothesis, not the null hypothesis
- Option C incorrectly characterizes the null; it is a testable assumption, not necessarily the most likely outcome
