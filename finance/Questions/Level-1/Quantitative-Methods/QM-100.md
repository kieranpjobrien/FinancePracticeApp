# Question

A $100,000 loan at 6% annual interest is to be repaid in equal annual payments over 5 years. The annual payment is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: $20,000
- **B**: $23,740
- **C**: $26,000

## Answer

B

## Explanation

For an amortizing loan, the payment is calculated using the present value of an annuity formula:

PV = PMT × [(1 - (1 + r)^(-n)) / r]

Solving for PMT:
PMT = PV × [r / (1 - (1 + r)^(-n))]
PMT = $100,000 × [0.06 / (1 - (1.06)^(-5))]
PMT = $100,000 × [0.06 / (1 - 0.7473)]
PMT = $100,000 × [0.06 / 0.2527]
PMT = $100,000 × 0.2374
PMT = $23,740

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A simply divides the loan by 5 years, ignoring interest
- Option C adds 6% to the simple payment ($20,000 + $6,000 = $26,000), which is incorrect
