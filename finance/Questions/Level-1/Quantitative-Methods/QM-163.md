# Question

As degrees of freedom increase, the t-distribution:

## Options

- **A**: Becomes more peaked with thinner tails
- **B**: Approaches the standard normal distribution
- **C**: Becomes flatter with fatter tails

## Answer

B

## Explanation

The t-distribution has heavier tails than the normal distribution because it accounts for uncertainty in estimating the population variance. As degrees of freedom increase (larger sample sizes), this uncertainty decreases and the t-distribution converges to the standard normal (z) distribution. With df > 30, the t-distribution is very similar to normal; at infinite df, they are identical.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes normal distribution approaching t, not t approaching normal
- Option C describes what happens as degrees of freedom decrease, not increase
