# Question

If a stock has a 60% probability of rising in any given period and movements are independent, the probability of the stock rising in exactly 2 out of 3 periods is closest to:

## Options

- **A**: 36%
- **B**: 43%
- **C**: 65%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

This is a binomial probability problem.

P(X = k) = C(n,k) × p^k × (1-p)^(n-k)

P(X = 2) = C(3,2) × (0.60)² × (0.40)¹
P(X = 2) = 3 × 0.36 × 0.40
P(X = 2) = 3 × 0.144
P(X = 2) = 0.432 = 43.2%

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A calculates (0.60)² = 0.36 but forgets to account for the different arrangements and the probability of one decline
- Option C calculates the probability of at least 2 rises instead of exactly 2
