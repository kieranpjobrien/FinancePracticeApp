# Question

Simple random sampling ensures that:

## Options

- **A**: Every member of the population has an equal probability of being selected
- **B**: The sample contains equal numbers from each subgroup
- **C**: The largest members of the population are selected first

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Simple random sampling gives every member of the population an equal chance of being selected into the sample. This ensures the sample is unbiased and representative of the population. It is the most basic probability sampling method and forms the foundation for statistical inference.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes stratified sampling with proportional or equal allocation
- Option C describes a biased, non-random selection process
