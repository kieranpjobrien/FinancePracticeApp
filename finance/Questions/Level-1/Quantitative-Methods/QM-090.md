# Question

A stock with a beta of 1.5 is expected to:

## Options

- **A**: Move 50% less than the market
- **B**: Move 50% more than the market in the same direction
- **C**: Move in the opposite direction to the market

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Beta measures systematic risk—the sensitivity of an asset's returns to market returns. A beta of 1.5 means the stock is expected to move 1.5 times as much as the market in the same direction. If the market rises 10%, the stock is expected to rise 15%. If the market falls 10%, the stock is expected to fall 15%. Beta greater than 1 indicates higher-than-market volatility.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes a beta less than 1, not greater than 1
- Option C describes a negative beta, which would mean inverse movement to the market
