# Question

When an analyst "fails to reject" the null hypothesis, the appropriate conclusion is:

## Options

- **A**: The null hypothesis is proven to be true
- **B**: There is insufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis
- **C**: The alternative hypothesis is proven to be false

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Failing to reject the null hypothesis does not prove it true; it means the sample evidence was not strong enough to reject it at the chosen significance level. The null hypothesis may still be false—we simply lack sufficient evidence to demonstrate this. This is why we say "fail to reject" rather than "accept" the null hypothesis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A incorrectly assumes hypothesis tests can prove hypotheses; they can only reject or fail to reject
- Option C also assumes proof is possible; we cannot prove the alternative false
