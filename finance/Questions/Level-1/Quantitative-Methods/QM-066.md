# Question

According to the Central Limit Theorem, as sample size increases, the sampling distribution of the sample mean:

## Options

- **A**: Approaches a normal distribution regardless of the population distribution
- **B**: Approaches the same distribution as the population
- **C**: Becomes more skewed

## Answer

A

## Explanation

The Central Limit Theorem states that for sufficiently large sample sizes, the sampling distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution, regardless of the shape of the underlying population distribution. This powerful result allows the use of normal distribution-based inference even when the population is not normally distributed, provided samples are large enough (typically n ≥ 30).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B is incorrect; the sampling distribution becomes normal, not similar to the population distribution
- Option C is the opposite of what occurs; larger samples produce sampling distributions that are more symmetric (normal), not more skewed
