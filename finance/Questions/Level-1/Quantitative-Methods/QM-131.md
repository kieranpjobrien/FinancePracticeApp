# Question

Time-period bias in sampling can occur when:

## Options

- **A**: The sampling method favors larger companies
- **B**: The time period studied includes unusual market conditions not representative of the long run
- **C**: Researchers only include companies that still exist at the end of the sample period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Time-period bias occurs when the sample covers a period that is not representative of typical conditions. For example, studying equity returns only during a bull market would overstate expected returns. Similarly, analyzing bond risk only during a period of stable interest rates would understate volatility. The solution is to use longer time periods that include various market conditions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes size bias or selection bias based on company characteristics
- Option C describes survivorship bias, a different type of sample selection problem
