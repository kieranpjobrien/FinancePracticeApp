# Question

A debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 5x indicates:

## Options

- **A**: The company generates $5 in EBITDA for every $1 of debt
- **B**: Total debt equals 5 years of EBITDA
- **C**: Interest expense is 5 times EBITDA

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt/EBITDA: leverage metric. 5x means debt would take 5 years of EBITDA to repay (ignoring interest, taxes, capex). Higher ratio = more leveraged. Investment grade typically <3x; high yield often >5x. Key LBO and credit metric.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A inverts the ratio
- Option C confuses with interest coverage
