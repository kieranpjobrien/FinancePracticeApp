# Question

When analyzing credit, analysts often adjust EBITDA to:

## Options

- **A**: Increase the appearance of profitability
- **B**: Remove non-recurring items and normalize for sustainable earnings
- **C**: Comply with GAAP requirements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Adjusted EBITDA: normalize for one-time charges, restructuring, stock comp, and other non-cash items. Goal: reflect sustainable cash-generating ability. Credit analysts skeptical of "adjustments" that always increase EBITDA. Compare to cash flow statement.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes manipulation, not legitimate adjustment
- Option C—adjustments are analytical, not GAAP requirement
