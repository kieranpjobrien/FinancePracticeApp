# Question

The par value (face value) of a bond represents:

## Options

- **A**: The current market price of the bond
- **B**: The principal amount to be repaid at maturity
- **C**: The annual interest payment

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Par value (face value): the principal amount the issuer promises to repay at maturity. Typically $1,000 for corporate bonds. Coupon payments calculated as percentage of par. Market price can differ from par based on interest rates and credit conditions.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A confuses par with market price—they differ based on rates
- Option C describes coupon payment, not principal
