# Question

Basis risk in bond hedging refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The risk that the hedge instrument and portfolio don't move together perfectly
- **B**: The risk of interest rates rising
- **C**: The risk of issuer default

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Basis risk: imperfect correlation between hedged position and hedge instrument. Example: hedging corporate bonds with Treasury futures—if spreads change, hedge imperfect. Caused by: different durations, different yield curves, spread changes, timing mismatches.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes general interest rate risk
- Option C describes credit risk
