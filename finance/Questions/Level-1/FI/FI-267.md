# Question

A company has total debt of $500 million and EBITDA of $125 million. Its debt-to-EBITDA ratio is:

## Options

- **A**: 0.25x
- **B**: 4.0x
- **C**: 25.0x

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Debt-to-EBITDA = Total Debt / EBITDA = $500M / $125M = 4.0x. Higher ratios indicate greater leverage and credit risk. Typical investment grade: below 3-4x. High yield: often 4-6x or higher. Key metric for credit analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—this inverts the ratio (EBITDA/Debt)
- Option C—calculation error
