# Question

"Notching" in credit ratings refers to:

## Options

- **A**: The process of initially assigning a credit rating
- **B**: Adjusting an issue rating up or down from the issuer rating based on structural features
- **C**: Small changes in spread due to market movements

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Notching: adjusting issue rating from issuer rating. Secured/senior debt may be notched up; subordinated debt notched down. Reflects different recovery expectations. Typical: 1-2 notches for investment grade, more for high yield due to greater recovery variance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes initial rating process, not adjustment
- Option C describes spread movements, not rating methodology
