# Question

A corporate bond's credit spread compensates investors for:

## Options

- **A**: Expected loss only
- **B**: Expected loss plus a risk premium for uncertainty
- **C**: The risk-free rate only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit spread = expected loss + credit risk premium. Expected loss = probability of default × loss given default. Risk premium compensates for uncertainty around expected loss and liquidity. Even if expected loss is zero, spread exists for bearing uncertainty.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A ignores risk premium component
- Option C describes base rate, not spread
