# Question

In liability-driven investing (LDI), surplus risk refers to:

## Options

- **A**: Risk that assets will exceed liabilities by too much
- **B**: Volatility of the difference between asset and liability values
- **C**: Risk of having too many liabilities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Surplus = Assets - Liabilities. Surplus risk: volatility of this difference. If assets and liabilities have different durations, interest rate changes create surplus volatility. LDI goal: minimize surplus risk through duration matching, often using derivatives.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—excess assets is not a "risk" in traditional sense
- Option C—confuses liability level with surplus volatility
