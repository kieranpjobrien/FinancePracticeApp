# Question

Interest coverage ratio is calculated as:

## Options

- **A**: Total debt divided by total assets
- **B**: EBIT divided by interest expense
- **C**: Net income divided by interest expense

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Interest coverage = EBIT / Interest expense. Measures ability to pay interest from operating earnings. Higher ratio = more cushion, lower default risk. Ratio below 1 means earnings don't cover interest. Credit analysts use various coverage metrics (EBITDA, fixed charge).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes debt-to-assets (leverage ratio)
- Option C uses net income which is after interest
