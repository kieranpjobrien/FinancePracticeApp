# Question

Macaulay duration measures:

## Options

- **A**: The time until a bond's first coupon payment
- **B**: The weighted average time to receive the bond's cash flows
- **C**: The bond's credit risk exposure

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Macaulay duration: weighted average time to receive cash flows, where weights are present values of each cash flow. Expressed in years. Related to interest rate sensitivity. Zero-coupon bond duration equals maturity. Coupon bonds have duration less than maturity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes time to first payment only
- Option C confuses duration with credit analysis
