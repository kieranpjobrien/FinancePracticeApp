# Question

When a bond is placed on "credit watch negative," it typically indicates:

## Options

- **A**: A downgrade is being reviewed and likely within 90 days
- **B**: The rating has been confirmed as stable
- **C**: A long-term outlook change

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Credit watch (rating watch): near-term rating review triggered by specific event. Typically resolved in 90 days. More urgent than outlook change. Events: M&A, major debt issuance, legal issues, sudden earnings decline. "Developing" watch means direction uncertain.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—watch indicates potential change, not stability
- Option C—outlook is longer-term; watch is short-term
