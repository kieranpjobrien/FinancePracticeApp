# Question

The boundary between investment-grade and non-investment-grade (high-yield) ratings is:

## Options

- **A**: A/Baa (Moody's) or A/BBB (S&P)
- **B**: Baa3/Ba1 (Moody's) or BBB-/BB+ (S&P)
- **C**: Aa/A (Moody's) or AA/A (S&P)

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Investment grade: Baa3 (Moody's) / BBB- (S&P/Fitch) and above. High yield (speculative): Ba1 / BB+ and below. This distinction matters for regulations—many institutional investors restricted to investment grade. "Fallen angel" describes downgrade across boundary.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A places boundary too high in rating scale
- Option C confuses with high-quality distinctions within investment grade
