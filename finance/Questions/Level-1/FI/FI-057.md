# Question

Collateralized mortgage obligations (CMOs) redistribute prepayment risk by:

## Options

- **A**: Eliminating prepayment risk entirely
- **B**: Creating tranches with different prepayment exposures and average lives
- **C**: Investing only in non-callable bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CMOs: structure MBS cash flows into tranches. Sequential-pay: early tranches receive principal first, absorbing prepayment risk. PAC tranches have more predictable cash flows; support tranches absorb variability. Redistributes—doesn't eliminate—prepayment risk among investor preferences.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—prepayment risk is redistributed, not eliminated
- Option C—CMOs are backed by mortgages, which are prepayable
