# Question

Asset-backed securities (ABS) differ from mortgage-backed securities in that ABS:

## Options

- **A**: Are always government-guaranteed
- **B**: Are backed by non-mortgage assets like auto loans, credit cards, or student loans
- **C**: Have no prepayment risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ABS: securitized non-mortgage receivables. Auto loans, credit cards, equipment leases, student loans. Structure similar to MBS—SPV issues securities backed by asset pool. Prepayment characteristics vary by asset type (autos prepay; credit cards revolve).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—most ABS are not government-guaranteed
- Option C—many ABS have prepayment risk (especially auto loans)
