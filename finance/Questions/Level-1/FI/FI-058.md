# Question

Credit enhancement in securitizations protects senior tranches by:

## Options

- **A**: Guaranteeing no losses will ever occur
- **B**: Providing loss absorption through subordination, overcollateralization, or external guarantees
- **C**: Increasing the interest rate paid to investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit enhancement: improves credit quality of senior tranches. Internal: subordination (junior tranches absorb losses first), excess spread, overcollateralization. External: third-party guarantees, letters of credit. Allows senior tranches to achieve higher ratings than underlying collateral.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—enhancement improves but doesn't guarantee against all losses
- Option C—enhancement affects credit risk, not coupon rates directly
