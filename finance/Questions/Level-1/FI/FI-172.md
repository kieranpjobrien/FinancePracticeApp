# Question

Yield curve risk refers to the risk that:

## Options

- **A**: The overall level of rates rises
- **B**: The shape of the yield curve changes (steepening, flattening, twisting)
- **C**: Credit spreads widen

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Yield curve risk: exposure to non-parallel shifts—steepening, flattening, butterfly (curvature). Even if portfolio duration-matched for parallel shifts, can gain/lose from curve shape changes. Key rate durations help measure exposure.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes parallel shift risk (level risk)
- Option C describes credit spread risk
