# Question

A Yankee bond is:

## Options

- **A**: Any bond denominated in US dollars
- **B**: A bond issued in the US by a non-US issuer, denominated in US dollars
- **C**: A US Treasury bond held by foreign investors

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Yankee bond: issued in US market by foreign issuer in USD. Subject to SEC registration. Other foreign bonds: Samurai (Japan, yen), Bulldog (UK, sterling), Maple (Canada, CAD), Kangaroo (Australia, AUD). Named for market where issued.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A too broad—includes all USD bonds
- Option C describes foreign holdings of US debt
