# Question

Most corporate bonds trade in the secondary market:

## Options

- **A**: On centralized exchanges like the NYSE
- **B**: Over-the-counter (OTC) through dealer networks
- **C**: Through automated matching systems only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bond secondary market primarily OTC: dealers quote prices, trade bilaterally with customers. Less transparent than equities. Electronic platforms growing but dealer market still dominates. Government bonds also trade OTC among dealers.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—unlike stocks, most bonds don't trade on exchanges
- Option C—electronic platforms exist but dealers still central
