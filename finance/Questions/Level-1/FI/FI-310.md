# Question

The inverse relationship between bond prices and yields means:

## Options

- **A**: When interest rates rise, bond prices rise
- **B**: When interest rates rise, bond prices fall
- **C**: Bond prices and yields move independently

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fundamental bond relationship: prices and yields move inversely. Higher required yield means lower present value of fixed cash flows. When rates rise, existing bonds' fixed coupons become less attractive—prices fall. Most basic concept in fixed income.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—reverses the relationship
- Option C—prices and yields are directly linked
