# Question

Free cash flow to the firm (FCFF) is most important for credit analysis because it measures:

## Options

- **A**: Accounting profits available to shareholders
- **B**: Cash available to service debt and fund operations after capital expenditures
- **C**: Total revenue generated during the period

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FCFF = Cash from operations - Capital expenditures. Represents cash available to pay debtholders and shareholders. Critical for credit: company needs sufficient free cash to service debt. Unlike accounting earnings, captures actual cash generation capacity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes net income, an accounting measure
- Option C—revenue is top-line, not cash flow
