# Question

ESG factors are increasingly relevant to credit analysis because:

## Options

- **A**: They have no impact on credit risk
- **B**: Environmental, social, and governance risks can materially affect long-term creditworthiness
- **C**: Regulators require all bonds to be ESG-rated

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ESG credit impact: environmental risks (climate, pollution), social risks (labor, community), governance risks (board, ethics) can affect cash flows and credit quality. Stranded assets, regulatory fines, reputational damage. Growing integration in credit analysis.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—ESG factors are financially material
- Option C—ESG integration voluntary, though growing
