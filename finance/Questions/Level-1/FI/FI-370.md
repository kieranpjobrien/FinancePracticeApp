# Question

In commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS), a lockout period:

## Options

- **A**: Prevents any principal payments to investors
- **B**: Prohibits borrower prepayment for a specified time, protecting investors from reinvestment risk
- **C**: Extends the maturity of the loan

## Answer

B

## Explanation

CMBS lockout: no prepayment allowed for initial period (often 2-5 years). After lockout, prepayment may require defeasance or yield maintenance. Protects CMBS investors from call risk. Commercial loans have stronger prepayment protection than residential.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—scheduled principal still flows
- Option C—lockout restricts prepayment, doesn't extend maturity
