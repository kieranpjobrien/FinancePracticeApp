# Question

Quantitative easing (QE) by central banks typically:

## Options

- **A**: Raises short-term interest rates
- **B**: Lowers long-term yields through large-scale asset purchases
- **C**: Has no effect on bond markets

## Answer

B

## Explanation

QE: central bank purchases long-term bonds, lowering yields and pushing investors into riskier assets. Expands central bank balance sheet. Used when policy rates at zero (ZLB). Transmission: portfolio rebalancing, signaling, wealth effects.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—QE is easing, not tightening
- Option C—QE directly affects bond supply/demand
