# Question

A butterfly spread trade profits when:

## Options

- **A**: The entire yield curve shifts in parallel
- **B**: The yield curve becomes more humped or less humped (curvature changes)
- **C**: Only short-term rates change

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Butterfly: long/short position at three maturities (e.g., long 2s and 10s, short 5s). Profits from curvature changes, not parallel shifts. If 5-year yield falls relative to 2s and 10s average (less humped), short wing profits. Expresses view on curve shape.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—parallel shifts don't affect butterfly if duration-neutral
- Option C—butterfly involves three points, not just short end
