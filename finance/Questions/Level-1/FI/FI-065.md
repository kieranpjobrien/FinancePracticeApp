# Question

A step-up coupon bond:

## Options

- **A**: Has a coupon that decreases over time
- **B**: Has a coupon that increases according to a predetermined schedule or credit trigger
- **C**: Always trades at a premium

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Step-up bonds: coupon increases at specified dates or if credit rating is downgraded. Provides compensation for extended holding or credit deterioration. Common in bank capital instruments. Credit-linked step-ups protect investors if issuer quality declines.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes step-down coupon structure
- Option C—step-up doesn't guarantee premium pricing
