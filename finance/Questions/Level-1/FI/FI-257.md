# Question

Effective convexity for a callable bond near the call price is:

## Options

- **A**: Always positive and high
- **B**: May be negative due to the call option limiting price increases
- **C**: Zero

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative convexity: callable bond's price-yield curve bends away from investor as rates fall (price capped near call price). Effective convexity captures this. When rates high, convexity positive (call unlikely). Near call price, convexity turns negative.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes option-free bonds
- Option C—convexity exists but changes sign
