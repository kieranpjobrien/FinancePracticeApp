# Question

A bullet bond structure means:

## Options

- **A**: Principal is repaid gradually over the bond's life
- **B**: All principal is repaid at maturity in one payment
- **C**: The bond can be called at any time

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bullet: entire principal due at maturity. Most corporate bonds are bullets. Contrast with amortizing (gradual repayment) or sinking fund (partial repayments). Bullet has higher duration than amortizing bond of same maturity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes amortizing structure
- Option C describes call feature, not principal payment structure
