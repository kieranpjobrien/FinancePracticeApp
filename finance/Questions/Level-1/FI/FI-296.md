# Question

A bond has key rate durations of: 2-year = 0.5, 5-year = 2.0, 10-year = 3.5. If the 5-year rate rises 25 bps while other rates unchanged, the approximate price change is:

## Options

- **A**: -0.125%
- **B**: -0.50%
- **C**: -1.50%

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Price change = -Key rate duration × Rate change = -2.0 × 0.25% = -0.50%. Key rate durations isolate sensitivity to specific maturity points. Only the 5-year rate changed, so only 5-year KRD matters. Sum of all KRDs ≈ effective duration.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—uses wrong rate change or KRD
- Option C—uses total duration instead of specific KRD
