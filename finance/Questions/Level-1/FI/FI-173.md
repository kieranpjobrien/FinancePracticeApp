# Question

A "bear flattener" describes a yield curve move where:

## Options

- **A**: Short rates fall more than long rates
- **B**: Short rates rise more than long rates, flattening the curve
- **C**: All rates fall equally

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bear flattener: curve flattens with rates rising (bearish for bonds). Short rates up more than long. Often during Fed tightening—short end responds to policy, long end anchored by inflation expectations. Opposite: bull steepener (rates fall, curve steepens).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes bull steepener
- Option C describes parallel shift, not flattening
