# Question

Asset-backed securities (ABS) differ from MBS in that ABS are backed by:

## Options

- **A**: Only residential mortgages
- **B**: Non-mortgage assets like auto loans, credit cards, or student loans
- **C**: Government guarantees exclusively

## Answer

B

## Explanation

ABS: securitized pools of non-mortgage receivables. Examples: auto loans, credit card receivables, student loans, equipment leases. Similar structure to MBS—pooling and tranching. Prepayment and credit characteristics differ by asset type. No implicit government support.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes residential MBS specifically
- Option C—most ABS lack government guarantees
