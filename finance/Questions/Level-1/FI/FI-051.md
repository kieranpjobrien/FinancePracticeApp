# Question

Floating-rate notes (FRNs) have lower interest rate risk than fixed-rate bonds because:

## Options

- **A**: They always trade at par value
- **B**: Their coupon rates reset periodically, adjusting to market rates
- **C**: They have no credit risk

## Answer

B

## Explanation

FRNs: coupon = reference rate (SOFR, etc.) + spread. Resets periodically (quarterly, monthly). Price stays near par as coupon adjusts to market. Lower duration than fixed-rate bonds. Still have credit risk and spread risk if issuer quality changes.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—FRNs can deviate from par if credit spread changes
- Option C—FRNs have credit risk like any bond
