# Question

The PSA (Public Securities Association) prepayment model assumes:

## Options

- **A**: Constant prepayment rate throughout mortgage life
- **B**: Prepayment rate ramps up from 0.2% CPR to 6% CPR over 30 months, then levels off
- **C**: No prepayments ever occur

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PSA standard: 0.2% CPR month 1, increases 0.2% monthly until month 30 (6% CPR), constant thereafter. 100% PSA = this benchmark. 200% PSA = double the rate. Captures seasoning effect—new mortgages prepay slower. Standard for comparing MBS speeds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes constant CPR, not PSA
- Option C—prepayments always occur in mortgages
