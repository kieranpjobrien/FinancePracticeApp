# Question

"Riding the yield curve" strategy works best when:

## Options

- **A**: The yield curve is upward sloping and expected to remain stable
- **B**: The yield curve is inverted and expected to steepen
- **C**: Interest rates are expected to rise significantly

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Riding the yield curve: buy longer-maturity bonds and sell before maturity as they "roll down" the curve. Works with upward-sloping stable curve: bond's yield falls (price rises) as maturity shortens. Captures rolldown return. Fails if curve shifts up.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—inverted curve means short rates higher, no rolldown benefit
- Option C—rising rates cause capital losses
