# Question

Key rate duration measures:

## Options

- **A**: Overall portfolio sensitivity to parallel yield curve shifts
- **B**: Sensitivity to changes in specific maturity points on the yield curve
- **C**: Credit spread duration only

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Key rate duration: sensitivity to rate changes at specific maturities (e.g., 2-year, 5-year, 10-year). Captures non-parallel yield curve shifts (steepening, flattening). Sum of key rate durations ≈ effective duration. Important for managing curve risk, not just level risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes effective or modified duration
- Option C describes spread duration, different concept
