# Question

In a credit card ABS, an early amortization trigger:

## Options

- **A**: Accelerates payments to investors if pool performance deteriorates beyond thresholds
- **B**: Extends the revolving period indefinitely
- **C**: Increases the credit limit on underlying cards

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Early amortization: protection mechanism. If excess spread falls below threshold, charge-offs rise, or other adverse events occur, revolving period ends immediately. Principal paid out to investors. Protects senior tranches from deteriorating pool. Critical structural feature.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—opposite: triggers end the revolving period early
- Option C—securitization structure doesn't affect card limits
