# Question

In bankruptcy, the priority of claims typically ranks (highest to lowest):

## Options

- **A**: Subordinated debt, senior unsecured, senior secured
- **B**: Senior secured, senior unsecured, subordinated debt
- **C**: All bondholders rank equally regardless of terms

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Bankruptcy priority: secured claims first (from collateral), then senior unsecured, then subordinated, then preferred stock, then common equity. Absolute priority rule. Higher seniority = higher recovery rate = lower yield.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the priority order
- Option C ignores contractual subordination
