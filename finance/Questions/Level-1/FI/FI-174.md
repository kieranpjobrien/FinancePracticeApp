# Question

A positive butterfly shift in the yield curve means:

## Options

- **A**: Short and long rates rise while intermediate rates fall
- **B**: Intermediate rates rise relative to short and long rates (less curvature)
- **C**: All rates move by the same amount

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Butterfly shift: changes in curvature. Positive butterfly: intermediate rates rise vs. wings (short and long)—curve becomes less curved/humped. Negative butterfly: intermediate falls vs. wings—more curvature. Barbell outperforms bullet in negative butterfly.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes negative butterfly
- Option C describes parallel shift
