# Question

A step-up bond has coupons that:

## Options

- **A**: Decrease over time
- **B**: Increase according to a predetermined schedule
- **C**: Vary randomly with market conditions

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Step-up bonds: coupon increases at scheduled dates (e.g., 4% years 1-3, then 5% years 4-7). Often combined with call provisions—issuer calls if rates fall below step-up rate. Compensates investor for taking call risk.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes step-down structure
- Option C describes floating-rate notes
