# Question

Credit ratings from Moody's, S&P, and Fitch primarily assess:

## Options

- **A**: The liquidity of a bond in secondary markets
- **B**: The probability of default and expected loss given default
- **C**: The interest rate sensitivity of a bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Credit ratings assess creditworthiness—probability of timely payment of interest and principal. Consider both default probability and recovery (loss given default). Ratings are opinions, not recommendations. Rating agencies also assess relative ranking within credit spectrum.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes market liquidity, not credit risk
- Option C describes interest rate risk (duration)
