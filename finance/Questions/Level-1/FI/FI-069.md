# Question

A make-whole call provision:

## Options

- **A**: Allows the issuer to call bonds at par at any time
- **B**: Requires the issuer to pay a premium based on discounting remaining cash flows
- **C**: Prevents the issuer from ever calling the bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Make-whole call: call price = present value of remaining cash flows discounted at Treasury + spread. Compensates investor for lost future payments. Call price typically above market—expensive for issuer, protective for investor. Differs from traditional calls at fixed price.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes par call provision
- Option C describes non-callable bonds
