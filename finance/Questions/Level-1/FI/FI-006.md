# Question

In the primary bond market:

## Options

- **A**: Existing bonds are traded between investors
- **B**: New bonds are issued and sold to initial investors
- **C**: Only government bonds are traded

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Primary market: where issuers sell new bonds directly to investors. Proceeds go to issuer. Methods: underwritten offerings, auctions (government), private placements. Secondary market is where existing bonds trade between investors after issuance.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes secondary market
- Option C incorrectly limits to government—all issuers use primary market
