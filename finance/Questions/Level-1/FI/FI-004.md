# Question

A bond's coupon rate is:

## Options

- **A**: The yield an investor earns if holding to maturity
- **B**: The annual interest rate paid on the bond's par value
- **C**: The current market interest rate

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Coupon rate: stated annual interest rate as percentage of par value. Fixed at issuance for fixed-rate bonds. A 5% coupon on $1,000 par = $50 annual interest. Differs from yield to maturity which reflects current market pricing.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes yield to maturity
- Option C describes market rates, which affect price but aren't the coupon
