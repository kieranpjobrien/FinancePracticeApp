# Question

The liquidity preference theory adds to expectations theory by suggesting:

## Options

- **A**: Investors require no compensation for interest rate risk
- **B**: Investors require a premium for holding longer-term bonds
- **C**: Short-term bonds always offer higher yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Liquidity preference: investors prefer shorter maturities (more liquid, less price risk). Long-term bonds require term premium to induce holding. Forward rates = expected spot rates + liquidity premium. Explains why yield curve often upward sloping even without rate increase expectations.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A contradicts the theory's core premise
- Option C reverses the yield relationship implied by the premium
