# Question

In a repurchase agreement (repo), the "repo rate" represents:

## Options

- **A**: The interest rate the borrower pays to use the cash
- **B**: The market yield on the underlying securities
- **C**: The discount from par at which securities are sold

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Repo rate: interest rate on cash borrowed via repurchase agreement. Borrower sells securities, agrees to repurchase at higher price. Difference is repo interest. Typically lower than unsecured rates due to collateral. Term repos have fixed maturity; overnight repos roll daily.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes yield on collateral, not repo financing cost
- Option C describes initial margin/haircut, not interest rate
