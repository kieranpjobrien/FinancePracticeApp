# Question

A mortgage pass-through security exhibits negative convexity when:

## Options

- **A**: Interest rates rise sharply
- **B**: Interest rates fall, increasing prepayment likelihood and limiting price appreciation
- **C**: Credit quality improves significantly

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Negative convexity: price appreciation limited when rates fall. Mortgagors prepay (refinance) when rates drop, returning principal at par. Price ceiling near par. When rates rise, no prepayment incentive—price falls fully. Asymmetric price response = negative convexity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—negative convexity appears with rate declines
- Option C—credit changes don't cause negative convexity
