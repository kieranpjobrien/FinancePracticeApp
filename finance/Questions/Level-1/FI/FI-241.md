# Question

Non-agency (private-label) MBS differ from agency MBS in that they:

## Options

- **A**: Have government guarantees
- **B**: Have credit risk in addition to prepayment risk
- **C**: Cannot be tranched into different classes

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Private-label MBS: no government guarantee. Credit risk depends on underlying loans and credit enhancement. Often backed by non-conforming loans (jumbo, subprime). Require credit analysis. Use subordination, overcollateralization for credit protection.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—agency MBS have government support, not private-label
- Option C—private-label MBS often heavily tranched
