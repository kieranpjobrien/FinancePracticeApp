# Question

A dual-currency bond:

## Options

- **A**: Pays coupons in one currency and principal in another
- **B**: Has two different coupon rates
- **C**: Can only be traded in two markets

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Dual-currency bond: coupon in one currency (often issuer's home), principal in another (often investor's preference). Embeds currency forward. Investor takes currency risk on principal. Popular in some Asian markets.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B describes step-up or variable coupon
- Option C describes listing/trading, not currency structure
