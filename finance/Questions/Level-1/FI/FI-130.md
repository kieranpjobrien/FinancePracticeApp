# Question

An inverted yield curve indicates:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term rates are higher than short-term rates
- **B**: Short-term rates are higher than long-term rates
- **C**: All maturities have identical yields

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Inverted (downward-sloping) yield curve: short rates > long rates. Often signals expected recession—Fed tightening, markets expect future rate cuts. Normal curve is upward-sloping. Flat curve has similar rates across maturities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes normal (upward-sloping) curve
- Option C describes flat yield curve
