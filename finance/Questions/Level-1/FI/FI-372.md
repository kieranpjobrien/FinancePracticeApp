# Question

A perpetual bond:

## Options

- **A**: Has a very short maturity
- **B**: Has no stated maturity date, paying coupons indefinitely
- **C**: Pays no interest

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Perpetual: no maturity—interest payments continue indefinitely. Value = Coupon / Yield (perpetuity formula). Examples: some bank capital instruments, UK consols (historical). Duration can approach 1/yield. May have call provisions allowing eventual redemption.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—perpetual means infinite, not short
- Option C—perpetuals do pay coupons
