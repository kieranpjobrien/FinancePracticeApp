# Question

A perpetual bond (perpetuity):

## Options

- **A**: Matures in exactly 30 years
- **B**: Has no stated maturity date and pays coupons indefinitely
- **C**: Automatically converts to equity after 10 years

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Perpetual bonds: no maturity, pay coupons forever. Value = Coupon / yield. Examples: UK consols (historical), AT1 bank capital. Often have call options allowing issuer to redeem. Duration approximately 1/yield for pure perpetuity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes long-dated bond with maturity
- Option C describes convertible feature
