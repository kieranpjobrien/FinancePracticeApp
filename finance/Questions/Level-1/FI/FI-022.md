# Question

Municipal bonds in the US typically offer:

## Options

- **A**: Taxable interest at all levels of government
- **B**: Interest exempt from federal income tax, and often state/local tax for residents
- **C**: Higher pre-tax yields than corporate bonds

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Municipal bonds (munis): interest generally exempt from federal tax. Often exempt from state/local tax for residents of issuing state ("triple tax-free"). Lower pre-tax yields but competitive after-tax yields for high-bracket investors. General obligation vs. revenue bonds.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes taxable bonds
- Option C—munis have lower pre-tax yields due to tax exemption
