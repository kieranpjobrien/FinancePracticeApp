# Question

A "fallen angel" bond is:

## Options

- **A**: A newly issued high-yield bond
- **B**: A bond downgraded from investment grade to high yield
- **C**: A defaulted bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fallen angel: was investment grade, downgraded to high yield (below BBB-/Baa3). Often due to industry/company deterioration. Forced selling by IG-only investors creates technical pressure. May be value opportunity if spread overreacts. Contrast with "rising star" (HY upgraded to IG).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes original-issue high yield
- Option C—fallen angel means downgrade, not default
