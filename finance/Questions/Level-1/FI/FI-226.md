# Question

A loan-to-value (LTV) ratio of 80% means:

## Options

- **A**: The loan equals 80% of the property or asset value
- **B**: The borrower has 80% equity in the property
- **C**: The interest rate is 80% of market rate

## Answer

A

## Explanation

LTV = Loan amount / Asset value. 80% LTV = 20% equity cushion for lender. Lower LTV = more protection. LTV increases if property values fall—can trigger covenant breach. Key metric for mortgages, real estate, and secured lending.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—80% LTV means 20% equity
- Option C—LTV is leverage metric, not pricing
