# Question

A pure bond indexing strategy:

## Options

- **A**: Holds every bond in the index in exact proportions
- **B**: Uses a sample of bonds to match index characteristics
- **C**: Actively selects undervalued bonds

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Pure indexing: hold all index bonds in exact weights. Perfect replication, zero expected tracking error. But impractical for large indexes (thousands of bonds, illiquidity). Stratified sampling more common: match key risk factors (duration, sectors, credit quality).

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—describes stratified sampling/enhanced indexing
- Option C—describes active management
