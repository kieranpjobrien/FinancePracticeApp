# Question

A "fallen angel" in fixed income refers to:

## Options

- **A**: A bond that defaults completely
- **B**: A bond downgraded from investment grade to high yield
- **C**: A new high-yield bond issuance

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Fallen angel: bond originally issued as investment grade but downgraded to high yield (below BBB-/Baa3). Often causes forced selling (some investors mandated to hold only IG). Opposite: "rising star"—upgraded from HY to IG. Transition effects can cause significant price movements.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes default, not rating transition
- Option C describes original-issue high yield, not fallen angels
