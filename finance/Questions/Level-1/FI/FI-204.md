# Question

An amortizing bond differs from a bullet bond in that:

## Options

- **A**: It pays no coupons during its life
- **B**: Principal is repaid gradually over the bond's life rather than at maturity
- **C**: It has variable interest rates

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Amortizing: principal paid down over time (like mortgage payments). Each payment includes interest and principal. Outstanding balance declines. Bullet: entire principal at maturity. Amortizing has lower duration than bullet of same maturity.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes zero-coupon bonds
- Option C describes floating-rate bonds
