# Question

Private activity bonds (PABs) differ from regular municipal bonds in that:

## Options

- **A**: They are always taxable at the federal level
- **B**: Interest may be subject to the alternative minimum tax (AMT)
- **C**: They cannot be issued by municipalities

## Answer

B

## Explanation

PABs: finance private-use projects (airports, housing). Interest federally tax-exempt but may be AMT preference item. Investors subject to AMT may owe tax on this interest. Regular munis fully exempt from AMT. PAB yields typically slightly higher.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—PABs are generally tax-exempt, just AMT-subject
- Option C—municipalities can issue PABs
