# Question

A bond's call protection period is:

## Options

- **A**: The time after which the bond cannot be called
- **B**: The period during which the issuer cannot call the bond
- **C**: The time during which investors can sell the bond

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Call protection (lockout period): period after issuance when call option cannot be exercised. Common: 5-10 years for investment grade. Protects investor from immediate call if rates fall. After protection period, callable at schedule of declining prices typically approaching par.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A reverses the concept
- Option C describes secondary market liquidity, not call protection
