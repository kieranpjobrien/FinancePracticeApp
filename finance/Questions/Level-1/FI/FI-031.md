# Question

The pure expectations theory of the yield curve suggests that:

## Options

- **A**: Long-term rates include a premium for bearing duration risk
- **B**: Forward rates are unbiased predictors of future spot rates
- **C**: Investors prefer short-term bonds regardless of yield

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pure expectations theory: forward rates equal expected future spot rates. Yield curve shape reflects only rate expectations, no term premium. Upward slope = expected rate increases. Criticized for ignoring risk—investors may require compensation for holding longer maturities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes liquidity preference theory addition
- Option C describes preferred habitat or liquidity preference
