# Question

The primary difference between Treasury notes and Treasury bonds is:

## Options

- **A**: Notes are discount securities while bonds pay coupons
- **B**: Notes have maturities of 2-10 years while bonds have maturities of 20-30 years
- **C**: Only bonds are backed by the US government

## Answer

B

## Explanation

T-notes: 2, 3, 5, 7, 10-year maturities; semi-annual coupons. T-bonds: 20 and 30-year maturities; semi-annual coupons. Both pay fixed coupons and are backed by US government. Distinction is purely maturity-based.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—both notes and bonds pay semi-annual coupons
- Option C—both are equally backed by US government
