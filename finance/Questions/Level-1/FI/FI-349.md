# Question

A synthetic CDO differs from a cash CDO because:

## Options

- **A**: It holds no physical assets—credit exposure obtained through credit default swaps
- **B**: It only invests in investment-grade bonds
- **C**: It has no tranching structure

## Answer

A

## Explanation

Synthetic CDO: credit exposure via CDS, not physical bonds. SPV sells protection on reference portfolio. Collateral invested in safe assets. Allows exposure without buying bonds. Enables shorting credit. Used for hedging or speculation. Complex counterparty risks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option B—can reference any credit quality
- Option C—synthetic CDOs still use tranching
