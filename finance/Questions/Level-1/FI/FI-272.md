# Question

Most corporate bonds trade in:

## Options

- **A**: Centralized exchanges with continuous auction matching
- **B**: Over-the-counter (OTC) dealer markets
- **C**: Electronic order books with full transparency

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Corporate bonds primarily trade OTC through dealer networks. Unlike stocks, no centralized exchange. Dealers quote bid-ask spreads, negotiate trades. Less transparent than equity markets. Bond market fragmentation due to many issues outstanding.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—describes equity exchange trading
- Option C—bond markets have limited pre-trade transparency
