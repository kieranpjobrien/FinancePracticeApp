# Question

Under the pure expectations theory, forward rates:

## Options

- **A**: Always exceed spot rates
- **B**: Are unbiased predictors of future spot rates
- **C**: Include a liquidity premium

## Answer

B

## Explanation

Pure expectations: forward rates equal expected future spot rates. No risk premium. Upward-sloping curve means markets expect rates to rise. Empirically weak—term premiums exist. Liquidity preference theory adds premium for longer maturities.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A—forwards can be above or below spots
- Option C—pure expectations excludes premiums
