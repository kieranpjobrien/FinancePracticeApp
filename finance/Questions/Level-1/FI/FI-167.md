# Question

Medium-term notes (MTNs) are typically:

## Options

- **A**: Issued in large single offerings like regular corporate bonds
- **B**: Offered continuously under a shelf registration program
- **C**: Always floating-rate instruments

## Answer

B

## Explanation

MTNs: issued incrementally from shelf registration. Maturities 9 months to 30+ years (not just "medium"). Issuer sells as needed, matching investor demand. More flexible than discrete bond offerings. Can be fixed or floating, plain or structured.

**Common mistakes:**
- Option A describes traditional bond issuance
- Option C—MTNs can be fixed or floating rate
